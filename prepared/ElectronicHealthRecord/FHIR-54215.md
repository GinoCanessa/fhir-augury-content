## Description

The submitter raises concerns about the "How was it recorded" subcategory under the **Process** observability factor in the AI Transparency IG. Specifically:

1. **Vagueness**: "How was it recorded" is too vague as a subcategory of the Process observability factor and needs better definition.
2. **Misplaced example**: The example provided (MCP usage to redirect to a controlled terminology corpus) seems more related to the **Context** category than to Process.
3. **Bias vs. accuracy**: The current description ties this subcategory to "Bias reduction strategies," but the submitter argues the MCP example is more about improving accuracy of inference than reducing bias.

**Submitter's suggestion**: Remove this subcategory entirely, or better define it with clearer scope and appropriate examples.

## Analysis

This ticket identifies a real clarity gap in the AI Transparency IG's observability factor taxonomy. The "How was it recorded" subcategory under Process conflates several concepts:

- **Process recording** (how the AI interaction was captured) — appropriate under Process
- **Bias reduction via controlled terminology** (the MCP example) — arguably belongs under Context, which covers data sources and inputs
- **Provenance linkage** — a cross-cutting concern that spans multiple observability factors

The submitter's observation that the MCP example relates more to accuracy than bias reduction is technically sound. Using a controlled terminology corpus improves consistency and accuracy of inputs, which is distinct from bias reduction strategies (which typically involve fairness-aware model design, representative training data, or outcome monitoring).

This aligns with a pattern seen across multiple tickets from this reporter (FHIR-54211, FHIR-54235, FHIR-54181) where observability factor subcategories lack clear definitions, examples are misaligned with their parent categories, and naming is inconsistent.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive with Modification — Redefine the Subcategory

**Action**: Keep the "How was it recorded" subcategory but substantially revise it:
- Rename to a more specific term such as "Recording Method" or "Capture Mechanism"
- Provide a clear definition scoped to how the AI interaction process itself was documented (e.g., audit logging, provenance chains, interaction recording)
- Move the MCP/controlled terminology example to the Context observability factor where it better fits
- Replace with Process-appropriate examples such as: structured audit trails, Provenance resources documenting AI agent actions, or logging of human-AI decision points

**Rationale**: The concept of documenting how the AI process was captured is valuable for transparency. The issue is not the subcategory's existence but its vague definition and misplaced examples. This approach preserves the intent while fixing the identified problems.

### Disposition B: Persuasive — Remove the Subcategory

**Action**: Remove the "How was it recorded" subcategory from the Process observability factor entirely. Relocate the bias reduction / controlled terminology content to the Context factor if appropriate, and ensure Provenance-related guidance is covered elsewhere in the IG.

**Rationale**: If the concept cannot be clearly distinguished from other Process subcategories or from Context, removing it simplifies the taxonomy and avoids confusion. The submitter explicitly suggests this as an option.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive — Clarify in Place with Minor Edits

**Action**: Keep the subcategory and examples as-is but add a brief clarifying sentence explaining how MCP usage for controlled terminology relates to process recording and bias reduction. Add a cross-reference to the Context factor noting the overlap.

**Rationale**: The existing content may be intentionally broad to accommodate diverse implementations. A minimal clarification could suffice without restructuring the taxonomy.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Persuasive with Modification — Redefine the Subcategory**

This is the strongest option because it addresses all three of the submitter's concerns (vagueness, misplaced example, bias vs. accuracy confusion) while preserving the legitimate transparency concept of documenting how AI processes are captured. The pattern of similar clarity issues across related tickets (FHIR-54181 resolved as Persuasive for naming inconsistencies, FHIR-54236 resolved as Persuasive with Modification for unclear descriptions) supports a substantive revision approach rather than outright removal. The workgroup has already demonstrated willingness to make these kinds of clarifying changes.
