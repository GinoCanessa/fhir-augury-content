## Issue Description

The element `meta.security:AItags` in the AI Transparency IG has a **required** binding to a ValueSet named **"Recommended provenance codes."** The reporter identifies a semantic conflict: the binding strength "required" implies that only codes from this ValueSet may be used, while the word "Recommended" in the ValueSet name implies the codes are suggestions rather than mandates.

The reporter proposes two resolution paths:
1. Change the binding strength from "required" to "extensible" to align with the "Recommended" terminology.
2. Rename the ValueSet to remove "Recommended" from the name, preserving the required binding strength.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive — Change Binding Strength to Extensible

**Action:** Change the binding strength of `meta.security:AItags` from "required" to "extensible."

**Rationale:** If the ValueSet was intentionally named "Recommended provenance codes" because the codes are indeed recommendations (not an exhaustive closed set), then the binding strength should reflect that intent. An extensible binding allows implementers to use codes from the ValueSet when applicable but permits additional codes where the ValueSet is insufficient. This aligns with the ValueSet's own naming and is consistent with FHIR best practices for IGs that may encounter provenance scenarios not yet captured in the initial code set.

**Risk:** Loosening the binding reduces interoperability guarantees. Systems may use arbitrary codes, making it harder to consistently identify AI-generated content — which is the core purpose of the IG.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification — Rename the ValueSet

**Action:** Keep the required binding strength. Rename the ValueSet from "Recommended provenance codes" to a name that does not include "Recommended" (e.g., "AI Provenance Codes" or "AI Transparency Security Codes").

**Rationale:** If the workgroup's intent was for the codes to be a closed, mandatory set, the binding strength of "required" is correct and should be preserved. The semantic conflict exists only in the ValueSet name, which can be corrected without affecting profile behavior. Renaming the ValueSet resolves the confusion without weakening interoperability. This is the lower-risk change and preserves the original design intent that all AI-tagged resources use a consistent, known code set.

**Risk:** Minimal. The ValueSet name is metadata; changing it does not alter the binding mechanics or break existing implementations.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive

**Action:** No change. Leave the binding and ValueSet name as-is.

**Rationale:** The binding strength and the ValueSet name serve different purposes. "Required" describes the constraint applied to implementers; "Recommended" in the ValueSet name describes the nature of the codes (they are recommended codes for provenance tagging). These are not inherently contradictory — a required binding to a set of recommended codes means "you must use one of these recommended codes." The semantic tension is understandable but not technically incorrect.

**Risk:** Continued confusion for implementers and ballot reviewers. The apparent contradiction may generate further ballot comments if left unaddressed.

## Recommendation

**Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification — Rename the ValueSet.**

The semantic conflict is real and will confuse implementers. However, the safer resolution is to fix the naming rather than loosen the binding. A required binding ensures consistent, interoperable AI provenance tagging — the IG's primary purpose. Renaming the ValueSet (e.g., to "AI Provenance Codes") eliminates the confusion with a minimal, non-breaking change. This approach also aligns with the pattern seen in FHIR-54723, where the same IG's binding approach is being tightened rather than relaxed.
