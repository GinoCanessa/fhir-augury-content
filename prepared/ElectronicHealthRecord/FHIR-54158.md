## Description

The reporter identifies that in the AI Transparency IG's scope statement, the word **"users"** is ambiguous when describing who benefits from data transparency. The specific passage reads:

> "is to enable observability for the use of AI algorithms in the production or manipulation of health data, thus enabling transparency for **users** of the data to determine the relevance, validity, applicability, and suitability of the data."

The suggestion is to replace **"users"** with **"consumers"** to clarify the role being described — i.e., those who receive and rely on the data, rather than the more generic "users" which could refer to clinicians, patients, system operators, or others.

## Analysis

This is a straightforward **wording change** in the IG's scope/purpose statement. The core question is whether "consumers" is a more precise and appropriate term than "users" in the context of data transparency for AI-generated or AI-manipulated health data.

**Arguments for the change:**
- "Users" is indeed ambiguous — it could refer to clinicians using an EHR, patients viewing their records, data analysts, or system administrators.
- "Consumers" more clearly identifies the downstream recipients of data who need to assess its provenance and quality.
- This aligns with common FHIR terminology where "data consumer" is a recognized actor pattern.

**Arguments for caution:**
- "Consumers" may also be ambiguous in some contexts (e.g., could imply patients as healthcare consumers).
- The workgroup may prefer a different term entirely (e.g., "recipients," "downstream actors," or "data recipients").
- FHIR-54113 (already resolved as Persuasive) is modifying the same paragraph — changes should be coordinated.

**Note:** FHIR-54113, which addresses the same paragraph, has already been resolved as **Persuasive**, meaning the scope statement text is already being edited. Any wording change from FHIR-54158 should be applied in coordination with FHIR-54113's resolution.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive
**Action:** Accept the change — replace "users" with "consumers" in the scope statement.

Rationale: "Consumers" is a more precise term that identifies the downstream recipients of AI-touched health data. This is a low-risk wording improvement consistent with FHIR's data consumer actor pattern. Coordinate the text change with the resolution of FHIR-54113, which is already modifying the same paragraph.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification
**Action:** Accept the intent but use "data consumers" (or another specific term such as "recipients" or "downstream actors") instead of the bare word "consumers."

Rationale: While the reporter's concern about ambiguity is valid, "consumers" alone could still be misinterpreted (e.g., as healthcare consumers/patients). Using the more explicit phrase "data consumers" or "recipients of the data" eliminates ambiguity entirely. This should be coordinated with FHIR-54113's resolution of the same paragraph.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive
**Action:** Retain "users" as-is.

Rationale: "Users of the data" is sufficiently clear in context — the phrase already scopes "users" to those interacting with the data. Changing to "consumers" introduces a different kind of ambiguity without a clear improvement. The term "users" is broadly understood and does not require correction.

## Recommendation

**Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification** — The reporter's concern about ambiguity is valid and well-founded, but "consumers" alone is only a marginal improvement. Using the more explicit phrase **"data consumers"** (or **"recipients of the data"**) fully resolves the ambiguity. Since FHIR-54113 is already modifying the same paragraph (resolved as Persuasive), the workgroup should coordinate both changes in a single edit pass to ensure consistency.
