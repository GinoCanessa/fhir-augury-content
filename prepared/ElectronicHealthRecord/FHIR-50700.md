## Summary

The submitter requests that function CP.1.6#02 be enhanced to include additional immunization-related data elements. Specifically, the comment proposes adding vaccine record type (indicating whether the vaccine was currently administered or historically reported), body route, body site, and Vaccine Information Statement (VIS) information. These additions would improve the completeness of immunization data captured by the EHRS Functional Model.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested
#### Proposal
Add the four requested data elements — vaccine record type (current vs. historical), body route, body site, and Vaccine Information Statement (VIS) information — to the conformance criteria or data elements associated with function CP.1.6#02 in the EHRS Functional Model.
#### Justification
These data elements are well-established in immunization information systems and are already represented in standards such as HL7 FHIR Immunization resource and CDC immunization messaging guides. Vaccine record type distinguishes between administered and historical records, which is critical for clinical decision support. Body route and body site are standard clinical documentation requirements for medication and immunization administration. VIS information is legally required in the United States under the National Childhood Vaccine Injury Act. Including these elements improves interoperability and regulatory compliance.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach
#### Proposal
Accept the request in principle but add the data elements as optional conformance criteria rather than mandatory, and reference existing standard vocabularies (e.g., CVX, NCI route of administration, SNOMED CT body site) for each element. Additionally, consider grouping these under a broader immunization data completeness criterion rather than listing them individually under CP.1.6#02.
#### Justification
While the requested data elements are clinically valuable, not all EHR systems may capture all of them in every deployment context (e.g., body site may not always be documented for routine injections). Making them optional with vocabulary references ensures the functional model guides implementers without imposing overly prescriptive requirements. Grouping under a broader criterion allows flexibility for future expansion without repeated amendments to CP.1.6#02.

---

### Disposition C: Decline
#### Proposal
Decline the request on the basis that CP.1.6#02 is intended to describe high-level functional capabilities rather than enumerate specific data elements, and that the level of detail requested is more appropriate for implementation guides or data exchange specifications.
#### Justification
The EHRS Functional Model is designed to describe what an EHR system should be able to do at a functional level, not to prescribe specific data fields. Enumerating individual data elements such as body route, body site, and VIS information risks scope creep within the functional model and may create maintenance burden as standards evolve. These details are better addressed in FHIR Implementation Guides or CDC immunization profiles that already define these elements precisely.

---

### Recommendation
**Recommended disposition:** A

The requested data elements — vaccine record type, body route, body site, and VIS information — are well-established, clinically important, and in several cases legally mandated components of immunization documentation. They are already broadly supported in existing interoperability standards including the HL7 FHIR Immunization resource. Adding them to CP.1.6#02 would improve the completeness and clinical utility of the EHRS Functional Model's immunization requirements without introducing undue complexity. The request is straightforward, well-scoped, and aligns with current industry practice. Accepting as requested is the most direct path to improving the specification.
