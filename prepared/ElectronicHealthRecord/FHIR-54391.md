## Description

The submitter requests that the AI Transparency IG provide a more explicit position on AI model card formats. The IG currently states:

> "The AI community is defining standards for describing an AI model. There are two Model-Card formats shown in this Guide, although other formats may be used as well."

The submitter asks the IG to clarify:

1. Whether the IG endorses any specific model card format, and if so, the rationale for that choice.
2. What the differences are between the Hugging Face (HF) and CHAI model card formats.
3. Whether other standard model card formats exist beyond those two.
4. The maturity level and industry adoption of each format.

This applies to the general introduction and section 2.6.2 specifically.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive with Modification ⭐ RECOMMENDED

**Resolution:** Accept the spirit of the request but scope the response appropriately.

**Rationale:** The submitter raises a valid concern — implementers need enough guidance to select and produce a model card. However, the IG is intentionally format-agnostic because model card standards are still evolving in the AI industry. A full endorsement of one format would be premature.

**Proposed Changes:**
1. In the introductory model card text and in section 2.6.2, add a brief comparison paragraph explaining the Hugging Face model card (widely adopted in the ML community for open-source models, structured metadata format) and the CHAI model card (focused on applied/clinical AI, includes risk and governance fields).
2. Mention additional emerging formats such as RedHat's AI System Card (per FHIR-53622) and note that the landscape continues to evolve.
3. State explicitly that the IG does not endorse a single format, and explain the rationale: the IG's role is to define how model card content is referenced and exchanged via FHIR (as a DocumentReference attached to Device), not to standardize the card format itself.
4. Coordinate with the resolutions of FHIR-54735 (defining model card better) and FHIR-54421 (clarifying model card semantics) to ensure consistency.

**Impact:** Editorial — adds informative content to the IG without changing profiles or conformance requirements. Aligns with existing resolutions on FHIR-54735 and FHIR-54421.

### Disposition B: Persuasive

**Resolution:** Fully accept the request and provide a comprehensive model card comparison.

**Rationale:** Implementers need detailed guidance to make informed choices. The IG should include a dedicated subsection comparing HF, CHAI, RedHat System Card, and any other known formats, covering maturity, adoption, field coverage, and recommended use cases.

**Proposed Changes:**
1. Add a new subsection (e.g., "Model Card Format Landscape") with a comparison table of known formats including fields covered, maturity, industry adoption, and clinical relevance.
2. Provide a recommendation for which format(s) are most suitable for healthcare use cases, with rationale.
3. Update section 2.6.2 to reference this new subsection.

**Impact:** Significant editorial addition. Risk of the content becoming outdated quickly as AI model card standards evolve. Would require ongoing maintenance and may overcommit the IG to positions on external specifications.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive

**Resolution:** The current text is sufficient.

**Rationale:** The IG intentionally avoids endorsing a specific model card format because the AI community has not converged on a single standard. The statement that "other formats may be used as well" preserves implementer flexibility. Detailed comparison of external formats is outside the scope of this FHIR IG and belongs in AI industry publications. The IG's responsibility is to define the FHIR integration pattern (DocumentReference attached to Device), not to evaluate AI community standards.

**Impact:** No change. The submitter's questions remain unanswered in the IG, which may reduce implementer confidence in choosing a format.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Persuasive with Modification** is recommended.

The submitter's core concern is valid: the IG references two model card formats without explaining what they are, how they differ, or why they were chosen. This leaves implementers without enough context to make informed decisions. However, fully endorsing one format (Disposition B) risks premature commitment to a still-evolving landscape and adds maintenance burden. A brief, informative comparison that explicitly states the IG's format-agnostic posture (Disposition A) gives implementers the guidance they need while preserving flexibility. This approach also aligns well with the already-accepted resolutions on FHIR-54735 (define Model Card better) and FHIR-54421 (clarify Model-Card semantics), enabling a coordinated set of editorial improvements.
