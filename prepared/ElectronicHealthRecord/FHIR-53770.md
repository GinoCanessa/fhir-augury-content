## Details

The ballot commenter (Yunwei Wang) notes that rows in the General Guidance page of the AI Transparency IG are unclear in what they represent. The commenter believes each row corresponds to one of the "4 levels" mentioned elsewhere on the page but requests that this be explicitly clarified.

John Moehrke re-classified the ticket from a technical correction to a change request (2026-01-26), acknowledging that the fix requires more than a minor editorial correction.

**Resolution Description:** The page was restructured to remove the ambiguous rows and instead detail them as named "factors." The work was implemented in [HL7/aitransparency-ig#22](https://github.com/HL7/aitransparency-ig/pull/22) — a rewrite of the General Guidance page that reorganized and harmonized the language around observability factors. That PR was authored by Sam Schifman, merged 2026-03-30, with +90/−129 lines changed in a single file (`general_guidance.md`).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept (Persuasive with Modification)

Accept the existing resolution as stated. The page was restructured to replace the ambiguous rows with clearly labeled "factors," directly addressing the commenter's concern about what each row represents. The implementation is already merged via PR #22 in the aitransparency-ig repository.

**Rationale:** The commenter's core concern — that the row-based layout was unclear — has been fully addressed by the rewrite. The restructuring goes beyond a simple label fix; it redesigns the section for clarity, which constitutes a persuasive-with-modification outcome.

### Disposition B: Accept with Additional Clarification

Accept the resolution but request that the work group add a brief introductory sentence or note on the General Guidance page explicitly defining what the "factors" represent and how they relate to the levels of AI transparency, ensuring new readers can orient themselves.

**Rationale:** While the restructuring removes the ambiguous rows, the relationship between the new "factors" and the original "levels" concept may still benefit from a connecting sentence for readers who encounter the IG for the first time.

### Disposition C: Decline (Not Persuasive)

Decline the change request on the grounds that the original table/row format was intentional and a brief caption or footnote would have sufficed without requiring a full page rewrite.

**Rationale:** This disposition would only be appropriate if the work group felt the original structure was clear enough and the rewrite introduced unnecessary churn. However, since the rewrite is already merged and addresses multiple related tickets, this option is not recommended.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Accept (Persuasive with Modification)**

The commenter's concern is valid and has already been substantively addressed. PR #22 restructured the General Guidance page to replace ambiguous rows with clearly defined factors, fully resolving the clarity issue. The change is merged, aligns with the broader ballot reconciliation effort, and improves the IG for all readers. No further action is required beyond accepting the existing resolution.
