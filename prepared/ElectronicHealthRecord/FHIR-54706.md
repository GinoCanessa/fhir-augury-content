## Analysis

This ticket raises a general quality concern about the sparseness of use case narratives (specifically use cases 2–4) in the AI Transparency IG. The resolution ("Persuasive with Modification") acknowledges the concern is valid but notes the submitter did not supply concrete improvement text or specific recommendations. The resolution explicitly contrasts this with other tickets (notably FHIR-54278) that did provide actionable improvement details, and those will be followed instead.

The closely related FHIR-54278 ("Use case improvement") asks for the same kind of elaboration but goes further by requesting FHIR resource/element-level examples and a concrete walkthrough of Use Case 4 specifically. If FHIR-54278's recommendations are applied, the substance of FHIR-54706 would largely be addressed.

The typo cleanup request in FHIR-54706 is minor and can be handled as an editorial pass.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive with Modification (Affirm Existing Resolution)
**Action:** Accept the existing "Persuasive with Modification" resolution as-is. Acknowledge the concern about sparse use case narratives and commit to improving use cases 2–4 as part of editorial refinement, but note that specific improvement text will come from other tickets (especially FHIR-54278) that provided concrete recommendations. Fix typos as a separate editorial correction.

**Rationale:** The resolution already validates the commenter's concern while honestly noting the absence of specific suggestions. Improvements will be driven by related tickets that supplied actionable content. This avoids duplicating work and keeps the editorial response coherent across the group of related use-case tickets.

### Disposition B: Not Persuasive
**Action:** Decline the ticket. The concern is too general to act on without specific improvement text, and the overlapping ticket FHIR-54278 already covers the same ground with concrete recommendations.

**Rationale:** "Expand and elaborate" without further detail is not actionable. The work group could consolidate this concern under FHIR-54278 and close FHIR-54706 as a duplicate in substance. However, this may frustrate the submitter since the concern is clearly legitimate.

### Disposition C: Persuasive
**Action:** Fully accept the request and commit to substantively elaborating use cases 2–4 with additional context, clearer narratives, and corrected typos, treating this ticket as an independent action item.

**Rationale:** This would treat the request as a standalone commitment. However, without specific improvement recommendations from this submitter, the work group would need to author the elaborations independently, which is effort already covered by other tickets providing concrete guidance.

---

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Persuasive with Modification (Affirm Existing Resolution)**

The existing resolution is well-reasoned. It validates the legitimate concern about sparse narratives while correctly noting that the submitter did not provide specific improvement text. The overlapping ticket FHIR-54278 provides the concrete FHIR resource-level examples and detailed walkthrough guidance that will actually drive the editorial improvements. Affirming "Persuasive with Modification" respects the ballot comment, avoids duplicated effort, and aligns the resolution with the broader set of use-case improvement tickets being addressed together. Typo fixes can be handled editorially.
