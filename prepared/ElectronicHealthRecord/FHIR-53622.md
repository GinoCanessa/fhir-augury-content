## Details

The submitter (John Moehrke) notes that RedHat is developing a specification beyond the traditional model card concept, called the **"AI System Card."** The ticket references a [RedHat blog post](https://www.redhat.com/en/blog/security-beyond-model-introducing-ai-system-cards) that introduces the System Card concept, which extends the model card analogy to encompass the broader AI system—including the infrastructure, data pipelines, and processes used to train and augment AI models.

The workgroup's resolution comment (2026-03-02) states that the AI Transparency IG already includes **CHAI and HuggingFace** as two examples of different approaches to Model Cards. The group is not familiar with the RedHat System Card example but welcomes the added specification. However, they have decided to keep to the **current scope of the IG** and limit the number of examples documented, resulting in a resolution of **Not Persuasive**.

This ticket was categorized under "Technical Errors & Links" in the ballot triage (alongside FHIR-53970) in the Zulip discussion for the January 2026 ballot cycle.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept — Not Persuasive (Affirm Current Resolution)

Affirm the existing **Not Persuasive** resolution. The AI Transparency IG already documents CHAI and HuggingFace as representative examples of Model Card approaches. Adding the RedHat System Card, while informative, is beyond the current defined scope of the IG. The workgroup has deliberately chosen to limit the number of external examples to maintain focus. The RedHat System Card addresses broader system-level concerns (infrastructure, data pipelines) that go beyond the model-level transparency the IG currently targets. The submitter can re-raise this if the IG scope expands in a future ballot cycle.

**Rationale:** The IG scope is intentionally bounded. Including every emerging specification would dilute focus and delay publication. The existing examples (CHAI, HuggingFace) are sufficient to illustrate diverse Model Card approaches.

### Disposition B: Accept — Persuasive with Modification (Add Informative Reference Only)

Resolve as **Persuasive with Modification**. Rather than fully documenting the RedHat System Card as an additional example, add a brief **informative note or reference** in the IG's background or related work section acknowledging the RedHat System Card concept. This would note that system-level transparency specifications (beyond model-level cards) are an emerging area, with a link to the RedHat blog post for interested readers.

**Rationale:** This acknowledges the valid contribution without expanding the IG's scope. It signals awareness of the evolving landscape while keeping the IG focused on its current deliverables. A single sentence or footnote is low-effort and adds value.

### Disposition C: Decline — Not Persuasive (Strict Scope)

Affirm **Not Persuasive** with stronger justification. The RedHat System Card is a fundamentally different concept from a Model Card—it encompasses the entire AI system rather than a single model. Including it, even as a reference, could create confusion about the IG's scope boundaries. The IG should remain strictly focused on model-level transparency as currently defined, and system-level transparency should be addressed by a separate future effort if needed.

**Rationale:** Clean scope boundaries make the IG easier to implement and ballot. The System Card concept may evolve significantly before stabilizing, making premature reference risky.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Accept — Not Persuasive (Affirm Current Resolution)**

The workgroup's existing resolution is well-reasoned. The IG already includes two representative Model Card examples (CHAI and HuggingFace), and the RedHat System Card addresses a broader system-level scope that extends beyond the IG's current model-level transparency focus. The workgroup explicitly acknowledged the value of the submission while appropriately limiting scope. Affirming the current Not Persuasive resolution maintains the IG's focus and is consistent with the workgroup's stated approach to managing examples.
