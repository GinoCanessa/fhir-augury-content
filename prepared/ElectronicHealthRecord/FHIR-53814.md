## Description

The submitter (John Moehrke) recommends an improved use-case for the AI Transparency IG. The ticket links to a blog post authored by Moehrke describing an **AI-Assisted Patient Appointment** scenario. The proposed use-case illustrates how AI can be integrated into routine patient care and how AI Transparency (Provenance, Audit, and Accountability) should be tracked in that context.

### Blog Post Content Summary

The [referenced blog post](https://healthcaresecprivacy.blogspot.com/2025/12/ai-assisted-patient-appointment.html) presents a detailed use-case with the following flow:

1. **Patient provides lab specimens** prior to a scheduled routine check-up.
2. **AI analyzes lab results** in context of patient history, current conditions, medications, and family medical history.
3. **AI generates a summary report** highlighting abnormalities, areas of concern, and recommended actions with rationale and citations to medical guidelines.
4. **Healthcare provider reviews the AI report** with the patient during the appointment, discusses concerns, and considers AI recommendations.
5. **Encounter documentation** may also be AI-assisted, with separate Provenance attribution.
6. **Patient receives a summary** via their patient portal.

The blog post emphasizes three transparency pillars:
- **AI Provenance**: Recording the AI model version, data sources, parameters, and settings used in analysis. The Provenance would reference a Device resource representing the AI system.
- **Audit Trail**: Logging input data, search requests against patient records, and analysis steps — distinct from Provenance in that it captures what the AI accessed (including data deemed not relevant), not just what it used.
- **Accountability**: Enabling retrospective identification of all outputs influenced by a specific AI model or prompt, allowing remediation if defective AI behavior is discovered.

The post also discusses scenarios for introducing new AI software, models, or prompts and tracking their adoption via Device resources and Provenance records.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive with Modification
**Action:** Accept the proposed use-case concept but adapt it for the IG format. Incorporate the AI-Assisted Patient Appointment scenario into the IG's use-cases section, with appropriate simplification and alignment to existing IG structure. Include the key transparency aspects (Provenance, Audit, Accountability) as described. Reference or coordinate with FHIR-54278 which also requests deeper use-case content with concrete FHIR resource examples.

**Rationale:** The blog post provides a well-thought-out, realistic clinical scenario that demonstrates AI transparency needs across multiple dimensions (provenance, audit, accountability). It fills a gap in the current use-cases. However, the blog post format needs adaptation to IG conventions, and the scope should be coordinated with FHIR-54278 to avoid duplication. Accepting with modification gives the editors flexibility to adapt the content appropriately.

### Disposition B: Persuasive
**Action:** Accept the recommendation and incorporate the AI-Assisted Patient Appointment use-case as described in the blog post into the IG. Add it as a new use-case with the full workflow (lab analysis → AI report → provider review → documentation → patient summary) and the transparency tracking details (Provenance, Audit, Accountability).

**Rationale:** The submitter is a known domain expert and active contributor to the AI Transparency IG. The proposed use-case is concrete, clinically realistic, and directly demonstrates the IG's core value proposition. Accepting as-is signals that the community values detailed, well-constructed use-case contributions.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive
**Action:** Decline the request. The current use-cases in the IG are sufficient, and adding a new elaborate use-case at this stage of the ballot process would expand scope significantly. The blog post content is externally hosted and would need substantial rework to meet IG standards.

**Rationale:** The ticket provides only a link to an external blog post without specific proposed text changes to the IG. The blog post, while informative, is lengthy and introduces concepts (Audit trails, new Device resources for AI models) that may overlap with or duplicate other ballot feedback. The IG may benefit from keeping use-cases concise at this maturity level.
