## Summary

The reporter (Mitra Rocca) requests that the AI Transparency IG provide complete, self-contained examples that clearly demonstrate the Provenance-based discovery model for identifying AI-generated or AI-assisted content. Currently, examples show only partial patterns — clinical resources appear without visible AI indicators, or Provenance resources are shown separately — forcing readers to infer the relationship. The IG's narrative guidance does not clearly explain that consumers must either search for Provenance by target (`_revinclude=Provenance:target`) or rely on co-bundling of resources and Provenance in the same Bundle. This gap may lead reviewers to incorrectly expect an explicit "AI-generated" marker embedded within the clinical resource itself.

## Details

### Description

The examples require readers to infer that they must either: search for Provenance by target, or rely on co-bundling of resources and Provenance in the same Bundle. This discovery model is not stated clearly in the narrative guidance, and many examples show only parts of the overall pattern (e.g., clinical resources without visible AI indicators, or Provenance shown separately). As a result, reviewers may reasonably expect to see an explicit "AI-generated" indicator within the resource and conclude — incorrectly — that such marking is missing.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept (Persuasive)

**Resolution:** Accept the request and add complete, self-contained examples to the AI Transparency IG that demonstrate the full Provenance discovery workflow.

**Specifics:**
- Add at least two complete examples showing end-to-end patterns:
  1. A **Bundle example** that co-bundles a clinical resource (e.g., Observation) with its AI Provenance resource, showing how they relate via `Provenance.target`.
  2. A **search-based discovery example** demonstrating the `_revinclude=Provenance:target` query pattern to retrieve a clinical resource alongside its AI Provenance.
- Add narrative text in the examples section explaining that AI attribution is asserted via Provenance (not embedded in clinical resources) and that consumers discover it through search or co-bundling.
- Cross-reference the related guidance requested in FHIR-54279 and FHIR-54280 to ensure consistency.

**Rationale:** Complete examples are the most effective way to eliminate the confusion described. The commenter (john_moehrke) acknowledged the need for "better explanation." Adding examples that illustrate both discovery patterns directly addresses the ballot feedback without turning the IG into a general Provenance tutorial.

### Disposition B: Partially Accept (Persuasive with Modification)

**Resolution:** Add narrative guidance explaining the Provenance discovery model, with a link to FHIR core Provenance documentation, but add only one illustrative example rather than multiple complete examples.

**Specifics:**
- Add a brief section (1–2 paragraphs) in the IG's guidance explaining that Provenance is discovered via `_revinclude=Provenance:target` or co-bundling, and that AI indicators are not embedded in clinical resources.
- Add one complete Bundle example that shows a clinical resource alongside its AI Provenance.
- For the search-based pattern, reference FHIR core documentation rather than duplicating Provenance discovery guidance.
- Coordinate with disposition of FHIR-54279 and FHIR-54280 since they address overlapping concerns.

**Rationale:** This balances the commenter's concern (john_moehrke) about not becoming "an IG that explains all about Provenance discovery" while still providing enough concrete guidance to prevent the misunderstanding the reporter identified.

### Disposition C: Decline (Not Persuasive)

**Resolution:** Decline the request. The existing examples and FHIR core Provenance documentation are sufficient; adding redundant Provenance discovery guidance would expand the IG's scope unnecessarily.

**Specifics:**
- Note that Provenance discovery is a general FHIR capability documented in the FHIR core specification and US Core.
- The IG should remain focused on AI-specific transparency patterns rather than re-teaching fundamental FHIR search mechanisms.
- Recommend that readers consult FHIR core documentation for Provenance discovery patterns.

**Rationale:** This aligns with the commenter's concern about scope creep. However, this risks leaving the specific confusion identified by the reporter unaddressed — ballot reviewers may continue to expect embedded AI indicators when the IG's design intentionally uses Provenance instead.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Accept (Persuasive)** — The request is well-founded and addresses a genuine usability gap. The reporter identified a concrete problem: reviewers are misunderstanding the IG's design because examples are fragmented and the discovery model is implicit. Complete examples are the standard remedy in FHIR IGs for this type of confusion. The commenter (john_moehrke) acknowledged the need to "provide better explanation," and the fact that this ticket is categorized alongside 9 other Provenance & Tagging issues from the same ballot (including FHIR-54279 and FHIR-54280 from the same reporter) indicates a systemic documentation gap. Adding complete examples does not require the IG to become a Provenance tutorial — it simply requires showing the full picture in context. This should be coordinated with FHIR-54279 (explicit AI attribution guidance) and FHIR-54280 (security label clarification) for a cohesive resolution.
