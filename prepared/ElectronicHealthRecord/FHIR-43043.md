## Summary
John Moehrke raises a question about whether the Archive record lifecycle event in the EHRS Functional Model should be modeled as a Create operation in FHIR. The ticket was filed in the context of a broader community discussion about purging, archiving, and deleting resources, and how these distinct lifecycle concepts map to FHIR operations. The concern centers on the semantic alignment between EHRS-RLE lifecycle events (Archive, Delete, Purge) and their FHIR counterparts.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested
#### Proposal
Clarify in the EHRS Functional Model - Record Lifecycle Events (FHIR) specification that Archive is semantically distinct from Create, and update the mapping documentation to explicitly describe how Archive events should be represented in FHIR interactions. Add normative language that an Archive lifecycle event does not equate to a FHIR Create operation, and provide implementation guidance on the appropriate FHIR interaction patterns for archival workflows (e.g., using a status change or a dedicated operation rather than a Create).
#### Justification
The question raised is valid and reflects a genuine ambiguity in the current specification. Clarifying the distinction between Archive and Create would prevent implementers from incorrectly modeling archival workflows as resource creation, which could lead to data integrity issues and misrepresentation of the record lifecycle. The Zulip discussion demonstrates that experienced implementers find the current mapping unclear.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach
#### Proposal
Rather than solely clarifying Archive versus Create, conduct a broader review of all EHRS-RLE lifecycle event mappings to FHIR interactions (including Archive, Delete, and Purge) and publish a comprehensive mapping table in the specification. This would address not only the Archive-as-Create question but also the related confusion around Purge versus Delete that surfaced in the community discussion.
#### Justification
The Zulip discussion reveals that the ambiguity extends beyond just the Archive event — participants also debated the semantics of Purge versus Delete and whether purging should produce audit events. A comprehensive review would provide a more complete and internally consistent solution, reducing the likelihood of follow-up change requests for each individual lifecycle event. This approach addresses the root cause rather than a single symptom.

---

### Disposition C: Decline
#### Proposal
Decline the change request on the basis that the ticket lacks a concrete description, specific proposed changes, or clear evidence of implementation harm. Request that the submitter provide a detailed description of the problem, the specific specification text that is ambiguous, and a concrete proposed resolution before re-submitting.
#### Justification
The ticket contains only a title question ("Archive is a Create?") with no description body and a single comment linking to a Zulip discussion. While the underlying question may have merit, a change request should include sufficient detail for the work group to evaluate and act upon. Declining with a request for more information ensures that any future change is well-scoped and actionable. The specification may already address this implicitly, and without a clear problem statement, the work group cannot determine what change is needed.

---

### Recommendation
**Recommended disposition:** B (Alternative Approach)

The Zulip discussion clearly demonstrates that the confusion about lifecycle event semantics extends beyond just the Archive event. Multiple experienced community members — including Grahame Grieve, Brian Postlethwaite, and Alexander Kiel — engaged in a substantive debate about how Archive, Delete, and Purge should be distinguished and mapped to FHIR operations. Addressing only the Archive-as-Create question in isolation would likely result in follow-up tickets for the other lifecycle events. A comprehensive mapping review would provide lasting clarity, reduce future ambiguity, and align the EHRS-RLE FHIR specification with the nuanced lifecycle semantics that the community expects. While the ticket itself is thinly documented (which supports elements of Disposition C), the underlying issue is real and well-evidenced in the community discussion, making it worth addressing proactively with a broader scope.
