Elliot Silver reports that the FHIR spec's [required binding section](https://hl7.org/fhir/terminologies.html#required) discusses the `CodeableConcept` and `code` datatypes but omits the `Coding` datatype (except as a component of CodeableConcept). The ticket raises three related issues:

1. **Missing Coding coverage:** The required binding rules do not address `Coding` as a standalone datatype.
2. **Ambiguous cardinality statement:** The statement _"If a required binding is applied to an element with maximum cardinality > 1, the binding applies to all the elements"_ is unclear about whether it applies only to CodeableConcept or also to code/Coding, and whether "all the elements" refers to repetitions of the element or to repeated Coding elements within a CodeableConcept.
3. **Unclear system/code/version requirements:** It is not specified whether both `system` and `code` must be present to satisfy the binding, or whether `version` is sometimes required. This ambiguity exists across binding strengths.

The reporter also notes (in comments) that the section should clarify that required bindings cannot be satisfied solely by `CodeableConcept.text`.

## Resolution Description

> Replace / enhance the content in the required binding section with something similar to what is in the extensible binding section where each datatype is a separate entry and there are specifics provided.
>
> TI tracker call attendees agree that the information in this section could be improved.

[Thread link](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/terminology/topic/Codeable%20Concept%20Required%20Binding%20and%20Text%20element)

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Affirm Persuasive — Restructure Required Binding Section Per-Datatype

Accept the current resolution as stated. Restructure the required binding section to mirror the extensible binding section's per-datatype format, with separate entries for `code`, `Coding`, and `CodeableConcept`. Each entry should explicitly state:

- What constitutes a valid value (e.g., must `system` + `code` both be present?)
- How the binding interacts with element cardinality (max > 1)
- That `CodeableConcept.text` alone does not satisfy the binding

This directly addresses all three issues raised, is consistent with how FHIR-40249 restructured the preferred binding section, and aligns with what TI tracker call attendees agreed to on 2026-03-23.

**Pros:** Targeted change; matches existing pattern in other binding sections; already has TI consensus.
**Cons:** Limited to the required binding section; does not address the same gaps in other binding strength sections.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification — Extend Consistency Review Across All Binding Strengths

Accept the ticket but broaden the scope: restructure **all** binding strength sections (Required, Extensible, Preferred, Example) to use a consistent per-datatype format covering `code`, `Coding`, and `CodeableConcept`. Additionally, explicitly address the `system`/`code`/`version` question across all binding strengths, as the reporter notes this ambiguity is not limited to required bindings.

**Pros:** Achieves full consistency across the spec; prevents future tickets about the same gaps in other sections; the reporter explicitly asked for review of "other bindings."
**Cons:** Larger scope of work; may delay completion; some sections (e.g., Preferred per FHIR-40249) may already be adequate.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive — Current Spec Is Adequate

Reject the ticket. The required binding section already states that "at least one Coding element SHALL be present" and "one of the Coding values SHALL be from the specified value set," which implicitly covers the `Coding` datatype. The cardinality language is standard spec phrasing. The `system`/`code`/`version` question is a separate conformance concern already addressed by the datatypes page.

**Pros:** No spec changes required; avoids scope creep.
**Cons:** Ignores documented community confusion (Zulip threads, FHIR-51975); leaves the structural inconsistency between binding sections unaddressed; the "implicit" coverage of Coding is exactly the gap the reporter identified.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Affirm Persuasive** is recommended.

The resolution is well-defined, has TI workgroup consensus from the 2026-03-23 tracker call, and follows a proven pattern already applied to the preferred binding section (FHIR-40249). The per-datatype restructuring directly addresses all three issues in the ticket. FHIR-51975 can be coordinated alongside this work since both target the same section. While Disposition B's broader scope has merit, it risks delaying implementation of an already-agreed change; a separate ticket could address consistency across other binding strengths if needed.
