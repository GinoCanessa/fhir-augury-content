## Summary

The reporter asks for clarification on the semantics of `AuditEvent.outcome` within the Scalable Consent Management IG. Specifically:

1. **What does "success" mean in a consent enforcement context?** When an AuditEvent records whether information was shared or withheld based on a Consent decision, does "success" indicate that data was successfully shared, or that privacy was successfully protected (data was withheld)?
2. **What do the failure grades mean?** The required value set includes grades of failure (minor, serious, major). The reporter asks how these map to consent enforcement scenarios—e.g., does a "minor failure" mean partial sharing, an authorization error, or something else?

The core issue is that the AuditEvent.outcome value set was designed for general-purpose audit logging (originating from DICOM/SYSLOG conventions for HTTP-style operations), and its meaning becomes ambiguous when applied to consent enforcement decisions where both "shared data" and "withheld data" could be considered successful outcomes depending on perspective.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Option A: Persuasive
**Action:** Add clarifying text to the Scalable Consent Management IG explaining the intended semantics of `AuditEvent.outcome` in the ConsentAuditEvent profile.

Specifically, the IG should clarify that:
- **"Success"** means the consent enforcement decision was successfully executed—the system correctly applied the consent rules, regardless of whether the result was sharing data or withholding data.
- **Failure grades** indicate problems in executing the consent decision (e.g., a system error prevented the consent from being evaluated), not a judgment about the privacy outcome:
  - *Minor failure*: The consent evaluation encountered a recoverable issue but the operation may have proceeded with degraded accuracy.
  - *Serious failure*: The consent evaluation could not complete, and a fallback policy was applied.
  - *Major failure*: The consent enforcement mechanism failed entirely, potentially resulting in an uncontrolled disclosure or improper withholding.
- The distinction between "data was shared" vs. "data was withheld" should be conveyed through other AuditEvent elements (e.g., `AuditEvent.action`, sub-type codes, or entity details), not through the outcome code.

### Option B: Persuasive with Modification
**Action:** In addition to adding clarifying text (as in Option A), introduce a more explicit mechanism in the ConsentAuditEvent profile to record the consent enforcement result.

This could include:
- Defining specific sub-type codes or outcome detail codes for the ConsentAuditEvent profile that explicitly distinguish "consent permitted disclosure" from "consent denied disclosure."
- Adding guidance on using `AuditEvent.outcome.detail` (available in R5's CodeableConcept-based outcome) to carry consent-specific outcome information.
- Providing example instances showing how to record both a successful share and a successful withhold, making the distinction concrete for implementers.

### Option C: Not Persuasive
**Action:** No change. The existing `AuditEvent.outcome` semantics are well-established from DICOM/IHE standards and are consistently applied across FHIR. "Success" universally means the requested operation completed successfully; "failure" means it did not. The consent enforcement context does not change these foundational definitions. Implementers should understand "success" as "the system did what it was supposed to do" and use other AuditEvent elements to determine the specific action taken.

## Recommendation

**Option A: Persuasive**

The concern is valid and well-articulated. In a consent enforcement audit context, the semantics of "success" are genuinely ambiguous to implementers who are not steeped in DICOM/IHE audit traditions. A consent enforcement operation that successfully withholds data and one that successfully shares data are both "successful" from a system perspective, but an implementer unfamiliar with this convention could reasonably interpret "success" as meaning "data was shared." The companion tickets FHIR-53755 and FHIR-53756 (both from the same reporter reviewing this spec) reinforce that the ConsentAuditEvent profile needs additional implementer guidance. Adding clarifying text is a straightforward change that addresses the ambiguity without requiring structural modifications to the profile. The historical discussion around FHIR-25287 and the Zulip threads confirm that AuditEvent.outcome semantics have been a recurring source of implementer confusion, lending further support to the need for explicit guidance in this IG.
