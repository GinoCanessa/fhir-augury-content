## Summary

This change request proposes several improvements to the use cases page of the Scalable Consent Management Implementation Guide. The reporter identifies five areas of concern: (1) the lack of descriptions for the "administrator" and "consenter" actors in the use case diagram, suggesting links to the existing persona definitions (payer, provider, etc.); (2) the potentially confusing use of the term "administrator," which typically implies system administration rather than the intended role of a Consent Initiator or Consent Requestor; (3) incorrect associations between the ConsentSystem actor and certain use case activities, arguing that ConsentSystem should only be linked to "Propagate audit event" and "Disclosure Audit" rather than activities initiated by the Consenter; (4) a question about whether a separate ConsentSystem administrator actor with a super user role is warranted; and (5) a recommendation to harmonize activity naming by using verb-based labels (e.g., "Consent Provenance" and "Disclosure Audit" do not begin with verbs). The resolution indicates the work group will add actor descriptions and take the other suggestions under advisement as they revise the use case diagrams.

## Details

**Description:**

1. Adding a description of what the administrator and a consenter is will be helpful to understand the diagram and the use cases, maybe with a link to [the use cases persona section](https://hl7.org/fhir/us/consent-management/2026Jan/index.html#use-cases) in the diagram (payer, provider, etc.).

2. "administrator" keyword may be confusing, as it is usually used for the system administration activities. Here it seems more to be a Consent Initiator, or a Consent Requestor actor.

3. The ConsentSystem has a link to "Consent Provenance", "Revoke consent", and "Sign and fill consent". Usually an actor in use case diagrams is mapped to activities that it performs and — most of the time — initiates. The ConsentSystem is only participating on these three activities, which are initiated by the Consenter. It feels more relevant to link ConsentSystem to only "Propagate audit event" (which should be "propagate consent events") and "Disclosure Audit".

4. Question: is it relevant to have another actor — the administrator of the ConsentSystem with super user role?

5. Usually activities in use cases start by a verb, which is not the case for "Consent Provenance" and "Disclosure Audit". Harmonizing them by using a verb would be better.

**Resolution Description:**

The work group will be adding descriptions of all of the actors and ensure that all actors represented in the use cases are listed. The other items will be taken under advisement as the use case diagrams are revised.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested
#### Proposal
Accept the ticket and implement all five suggested improvements to the Scalable Consent Management IG use cases page: (1) add descriptive text and persona links for the "administrator" and "consenter" actors; (2) rename the "administrator" actor to "Consent Initiator" or "Consent Requestor" to avoid confusion with system administration; (3) reassign the ConsentSystem actor's associations so it links only to "Propagate consent events" and "Perform disclosure audit"; (4) evaluate whether a separate ConsentSystem super-user administrator actor is needed and add one if appropriate; and (5) rename "Consent Provenance" and "Disclosure Audit" activities to verb-based labels (e.g., "Track consent provenance" and "Perform disclosure audit").
#### Justification
All five suggestions align with standard UML use case modeling conventions and would improve the clarity and correctness of the use cases page. The reporter provides well-reasoned rationale for each point, and the changes are relatively low-risk editorial improvements that strengthen the specification's usability and consistency.

---

### Disposition B: Partial Accept — Add Actor Descriptions and Rename Activities
#### Proposal
Accept items 1, 2, and 5 in full: add actor descriptions with persona links, rename "administrator" to a less ambiguous term such as "Consent Initiator," and harmonize activity labels to begin with verbs. Defer items 3 (ConsentSystem associations) and 4 (super-user actor) for further analysis in a future ballot cycle, as these involve structural changes to the use case model that require additional work group discussion.
#### Justification
The editorial improvements (descriptions, naming) are non-controversial and can be implemented immediately. The structural changes to actor–activity associations and the introduction of a new actor role require deeper analysis of the consent workflow model and may have downstream implications for other parts of the IG. Deferring these items allows the work group to address them with appropriate deliberation while still delivering meaningful improvements now. This aligns with the resolution description, which committed to actor descriptions and noted the other items would be "taken under advisement."

---

### Disposition C: Decline
#### Proposal
Decline the request as out of scope for the current ballot cycle.
#### Justification
The use cases page is intended as an informative overview rather than a normative specification element. While the suggestions are reasonable, the current diagrams are functional and understandable. Investing effort in UML diagram refinement may not be the highest priority relative to normative content changes. The work group could note these suggestions for a future revision without committing to specific changes at this time.

---

### Recommendation
**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B (Partial Accept) is the most pragmatic approach and aligns closely with the resolution already recorded on the ticket. The work group has committed to adding actor descriptions and reviewing the other items — this disposition formalizes that commitment by accepting the clear editorial improvements (actor descriptions, terminology clarification, and verb-based activity naming) while appropriately deferring the more structural questions about ConsentSystem associations and a potential super-user actor role. The editorial changes are straightforward, non-controversial, and will meaningfully improve the readability and standards-compliance of the use cases page. The deferred items can be tracked for the next ballot cycle, ensuring they receive the deeper analysis they warrant without blocking the current improvements.
