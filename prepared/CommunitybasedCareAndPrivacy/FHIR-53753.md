## Description

The submitter notes that `actor` is listed as a "Must Have" requirement in the Scalable Consent Management IG, but `provision.actor` has a cardinality of `0..*`. Since the element is optional by cardinality, the submitter asks whether it should be classified as "Must Support" rather than "Must Have."

## Resolution Description

> Yes, actor should be in the Must Support and not in the Must Have section.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept as Persuasive (Move actor to Must Support)

Accept the existing resolution as stated. Move `actor` (i.e., `provision.actor`) from the "Must Have" section to the "Must Support" section of the Scalable Consent Management IG conformance requirements.

**Rationale:** The cardinality of `provision.actor` is `0..*`, meaning it is structurally optional. Listing an optional element as "Must Have" creates a contradiction — "Must Have" implies the element must be present in conformant instances, but the cardinality allows it to be absent. Moving it to "Must Support" correctly signals that conformant systems must be capable of handling the element when present, without requiring it in every instance. This aligns with standard FHIR conformance semantics and resolves the inconsistency.

**Impact:** Low. Requires an editorial change to the conformance requirements table in the IG. No structural or profile changes are needed.

### Disposition B: Accept as Persuasive with Modification (Move actor to Must Support and audit full Must Have list)

Accept the resolution to move `actor` to Must Support, and additionally audit the entire Must Have list for similar cardinality mismatches, coordinating with FHIR-53756 which raises related issues (period ambiguity, missing .source).

**Rationale:** This ticket and FHIR-53756 (from the same reporter, same ballot cycle) both identify problems with the Must Have list. A holistic review would ensure consistency across all conformance requirements rather than fixing items one at a time.

**Impact:** Moderate. Requires a broader review of the conformance requirements section, but results in a more robust and internally consistent specification.

### Disposition C: Reject (Keep actor as Must Have with clarification)

Reject the change and instead clarify that "Must Have" in this IG context means the system must support the element and populate it when data is available, not that every instance must contain it.

**Rationale:** If the IG authors intended a custom interpretation of "Must Have" that differs from strict cardinality enforcement, the solution is better documentation rather than reclassification. This preserves the original design intent that actor information is considered critical to consent management.

**Impact:** Low editorial change, but risks ongoing confusion if the custom terminology diverges from standard FHIR conformance conventions.

---

## Recommendation

**Disposition B: Accept as Persuasive with Modification** is recommended. The resolution is straightforward and correct — `provision.actor` with `0..*` cardinality should not be in the "Must Have" list. However, coordinating with FHIR-53756 to perform a broader audit of the Must Have / Must Support lists will produce a more consistent result and avoid repeated one-off corrections. The existing resolution text ("Yes, actor should be in the Must Support and not in the Must Have section") already supports this direction; the modification simply extends the scope to ensure completeness.
