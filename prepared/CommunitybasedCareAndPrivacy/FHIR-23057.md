## Details

The original submitter (Chirag Bhatt) raises a concern about how the eLTSS IG maps the "Service Rate Per Unit" and "Total Cost of Service" data elements. The current mapping approach uses `CarePlan -> activity -> reference` to reference a `ServiceRequest`, with `supportingInfo` referencing a `Claim`.

**The core objection:** The submitter argues that a Claim is the result of completing a service, whereas a ServiceRequest exists at an earlier workflow stage — when the service plan is being prepared, a Claim should not yet exist. The submitter suggests that unit price should be captured at the service level, ideally by linking ServiceRequest to HealthcareService, where service definitions (including unit, rate/price) could be specified. However, rate is not currently included in HealthcareService and may require an extension.

**Previous resolution (reverted):** This ticket was previously resolved as "Considered for Future Use" in July 2020 (vote: 12-0-1) with the rationale that HealthcareService seemed the best fit, and an extension for cost should be added. The recommendation was to consult with FM (Claim), O&O (ServiceRequest), and Patient Administration (HealthcareService) to determine where cost fits best.

**Post-STU2 re-opening:** In May 2024 (lynn_laakso), the previous resolution was reverted and the ticket was re-opened for consideration after STU2 publication.

**Counterargument from bheale (Dec 2024):** Multiple comments argue against the submitter's premise:
1. The Claim resource is not limited to post-service use — per the [R4 Claim scope](https://hl7.org/fhir/r4/claim.html#scope), it supports "preauthorization" and "predetermination" via `Claim.use`.
2. An extension on HealthcareService is unnecessary; the proper approach is to use Claim and ClaimResponse resources.
3. In eLTSS STU2, guidance was added for using Claim with `Claim.referral` → ServiceRequest, `Claim.procedure.procedureReference` → Procedure, and `Claim.supportingInfo` → Task.
4. `ClaimResponse.net` and `Claim.net` capture total cost; combined with ServiceRequest frequency/quantity, a system can derive the per-unit rate.

**Related predecessor ticket:** [FHIR-15726](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-15726) ("add unit and total cost to ServiceRequest") was resolved as **Not Persuasive** by Orders & Observations, meaning the core FHIR spec declined to add cost elements directly to ServiceRequest.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept — Adopt Claim/ClaimResponse-Based Costing Guidance (Persuasive)

Resolve as **Persuasive** by formally endorsing the approach already documented in eLTSS STU2: use the Claim resource (with `Claim.use` set to "preauthorization" or "predetermination") to capture service rate and cost information, connected to ServiceRequest via `Claim.referral`. Update the IG to:

1. Clarify that Claim is not limited to post-service scenarios and can represent preauthorization costs.
2. Document that `Claim.net` and `ClaimResponse.net` capture total cost, and per-unit rate can be derived from frequency/quantity on the ServiceRequest.
3. Remove or deprecate any suggestion that an extension on HealthcareService is needed for cost.

**Rationale:** The Claim resource already supports pre-service cost scenarios per its scope definition. The STU2 IG has incorporated this guidance. This aligns with the O&O decision in FHIR-15726 that cost should not be added to ServiceRequest directly. Formalizing this approach resolves the original concern while using standard FHIR resources without extensions.

### Disposition B: Alternative — Add Explicit Cost Extension to ServiceRequest (Persuasive with Modification)

Resolve as **Persuasive with Modification** by acknowledging the workflow concern but taking a different approach: define a simple eLTSS-specific extension on ServiceRequest to carry unit cost and total cost directly, rather than requiring a Claim resource at the planning stage.

1. Create an eLTSS extension `eltss-service-cost` on ServiceRequest with sub-elements for unit cost (Money) and total cost (Money).
2. Make the Claim-based approach optional for implementations that do use preauthorization workflows.
3. Provide mapping guidance for both patterns.

**Rationale:** While Claim technically supports preauthorization, requiring implementers to create a Claim resource solely to carry cost information during care planning adds complexity. A lightweight extension on ServiceRequest may be simpler for implementations that don't have full financial workflows. However, this contradicts FHIR-15726's resolution and would need O&O/FM coordination.

### Disposition C: Decline — Close as Not Persuasive

Resolve as **Not Persuasive**, noting that the concern has been addressed in eLTSS STU2. The Claim resource's scope explicitly includes preauthorization and predetermination use cases, meaning it is appropriate for capturing cost at the planning stage. The STU2 IG already includes guidance on using Claim/ClaimResponse for payment workflows tied to ServiceRequest. The original premise — that Claim is only for post-service use — is factually incorrect per the FHIR R4 specification.

**Rationale:** The comments from bheale demonstrate that the original concern was based on a misunderstanding of the Claim resource's scope. The STU2 has already incorporated appropriate guidance. No further changes are needed.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Accept — Adopt Claim/ClaimResponse-Based Costing Guidance (Persuasive)**

This is recommended because:

1. **The technical argument is sound.** The Claim resource explicitly supports preauthorization and predetermination, addressing the submitter's core workflow concern.
2. **Precedent exists.** FHIR-15726 was resolved as Not Persuasive by O&O, confirming that cost should not be added to ServiceRequest core. Using Claim aligns with this decision.
3. **STU2 already implements this approach.** The guidance is already in the published IG; formalizing this resolution ensures consistency and closes a long-open ticket (since 2019).
4. **Avoids unnecessary extensions.** Using standard Claim/ClaimResponse resources is preferred over IG-specific extensions that could fragment interoperability.

Choosing Disposition A over C (Not Persuasive) is preferred because it acknowledges the submitter's valid concern about workflow timing while showing it has been addressed, rather than simply dismissing it. This promotes constructive engagement with ballot commenters.
