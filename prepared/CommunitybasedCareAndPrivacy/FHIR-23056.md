## Summary

This ticket requests improved guidance (and potentially structural changes) for representing complex service quantity and timing scenarios in the eLTSS ServiceRequest profile. The original submitter notes that while `quantityQuantity` handles simple quantities (e.g., "1 installation") and `quantityRatio` handles rate-based quantities (e.g., "8 hours a day"), real-world LTSS home services often involve more complex scheduling such as "2 hours twice a day, one AM and one PM," "4 hours each afternoon," or "Mon and Thu of each week." The submitter suggests that `occurrenceTiming` (with `timeOfDay`, `dayOfWeek`, etc.) may be more appropriate for these cases, but cautions that combining `quantityRatio` and `occurrenceTiming` to represent a single concept introduces complexity.

The ticket was originally resolved as "Considered for Future Use" in 2020 (vote 12-0-1) with a note that it depends on the outcome of related tickets FHIR-23054 and FHIR-23055 regarding `occurrence` cardinality. It was reopened post-STU2 publication in May 2024. A December 2024 comment from bheale argues that the two elements serve complementary roles — `quantityRatio` for precise coded units of service quantity (e.g., dollar per day) and `occurrenceTiming` for scheduling (e.g., specific days and times) — and points to existing IG guidance that already illustrates combined usage.

---

## Details

### Core Issue

The eLTSS IG maps service quantities to `ServiceRequest.quantity[x]` using either `quantityQuantity` (simple) or `quantityRatio` (rate). For complex LTSS scenarios involving day-of-week, time-of-day, or multi-occurrence patterns, the submitter questions whether `quantityRatio` is appropriate or whether `occurrenceTiming` should be used instead (or exclusively).

### Current IG Approach (STU2)

The eLTSS STU2 IG adopted the following pattern:
- **`occurrenceTiming.boundsPeriod`** — service start/end dates
- **`occurrenceTiming.dayOfWeek` / `timeOfDay`** — scheduling specifics
- **`quantity` or `quantityRatio`** — the amount of service delivered per unit of time

The IG provides a composite example: *"Give ServicePlan.quantity 5 with unit=[USD]/day with the occurrenceTiming.boundsPeriod of May 31, 2023 to June 23, 2023 on occurrenceTiming.dayOfWeek = Mon and Wed at occurrenceTiming.timeOfDay = 3 pm."*

### Discussion Points

1. **Complementary vs. redundant:** bheale argues that `quantityRatio` and `occurrenceTiming` serve distinct roles — quantity/unit of service vs. scheduling — and should be used together for full precision.
2. **Complexity concern:** The original submitter's concern remains valid that implementers may find combined usage confusing without strong guidance.
3. **Dependency on related tickets:** The 2020 resolution noted dependence on FHIR-23054 (whether to change `occurrence` cardinality from 0..1 to 0..*) and FHIR-23055 (use of `occurrencePeriod` vs. `occurrenceTiming`). FHIR-23054 remains Triaged; FHIR-23055 was resolved as "Question answered."

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept — Enhance IG Guidance on Combined quantityRatio + occurrenceTiming Usage

**Action:** Add detailed normative or best-practice guidance to the eLTSS IG's implementation section explaining when and how to use `quantityRatio` and `occurrenceTiming` together for complex LTSS service scheduling scenarios. Include worked examples covering the submitter's cases:
- "2 hours twice a day, one AM and one PM" → `quantityRatio` (2 hours / 1 session), `occurrenceTiming.frequency` = 2/day, `timeOfDay` = [AM, PM]
- "Mon and Thu of each week" → `occurrenceTiming.dayOfWeek` = [mon, thu]
- "4 hours each afternoon" → `quantityRatio` (4 hours / 1 session), `occurrenceTiming.timeOfDay` = PM

**Rationale:** The structural elements already exist in the profile (as bheale notes); the gap is implementer guidance, not a modeling deficiency. Concrete examples directly address the submitter's concerns without requiring profile changes.

### Disposition B: Alternative — Recommend occurrenceTiming-Only Pattern for Complex Scheduling

**Action:** Add guidance recommending that for complex multi-occurrence scenarios, implementers use `occurrenceTiming` exclusively (encoding duration, frequency, dayOfWeek, timeOfDay, and boundsPeriod) without `quantityRatio`. Reserve `quantityRatio` for straightforward rate-based quantities only.

**Rationale:** Aligns with the original submitter's suggestion and reduces implementer confusion by avoiding the need to coordinate two elements for a single concept. However, this sacrifices the coded-unit precision that `quantityRatio` provides (e.g., coded dollar amounts vs. unitless values).

### Disposition C: Decline — No Change Required

**Action:** Close as no change needed. The existing IG already includes guidance and examples for combined `quantityRatio`/`occurrenceTiming` usage in the [Quantities, UCUM, and the Timing Data Type](https://hl7.org/fhir/us/eltss/2024Jan/implementation.html#quantities-ucum-and-the-timing-data-type) section.

**Rationale:** The STU2 IG published in January 2024 already addresses the concern with worked examples. Additional guidance may be helpful but is not a change-request-level issue.

---

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Accept — Enhance IG Guidance on Combined quantityRatio + occurrenceTiming Usage**

The ticket raises a legitimate implementer concern about complex LTSS scheduling that, while partially addressed by STU2, would benefit from more thorough, scenario-specific examples. The structural elements are already in place — what's needed is expanded guidance covering the real-world patterns the submitter identifies. This approach preserves the precision of `quantityRatio` while making the combined pattern accessible. Given the ticket has been open since 2019, was deferred in 2020, and was deliberately reopened post-STU2, the work group should address it definitively with enhanced documentation. Additionally, this should be coordinated with FHIR-23054 (which remains Triaged) since both involve the eLTSS ServiceRequest timing/quantity modeling.
