# Ticket Review: FHIR-40040

**Title:** Aligning Contacts with HSDS
**Status:** Triaged **Priority:** Highest **Type:** Change Request
**Work Group:** Human and Social Services **Specification:** Human Services Directory (FHIR)
**Reporter:** Greg Bloom **Assignee:** Unassigned
**Created:** 2023-01-10 **Updated:** 2023-10-04 **Labels:** Alignment

---

## Summary

Greg Bloom requests improved alignment between the FHIR Human Services Directory IG and the Human Services Data Specification (HSDS) regarding contact information at the service level. In HSDS, multiple contacts can be associated with a service or organization, each with their own phone and address details. The FHIR HealthcareService resource currently lacks a contact element (it only has telecom), so full contact information (name, title, email, address) cannot be represented at the service level. The Organization resource already supports contacts. The reporter considers this one of the most significant alignment gaps between HSDS and the IG, though not critical for immediate release. The ticket was originally resolved as "Considered for Future Use" but was reopened post-STU1 publication.

## Details

**Description:**

In HSDS, multiple Contacts can be associated with a service or organization, each with their own Location and Phone details. Within this IG, a Service can have multiple locations and phone details but you can not specify the contact to which they relate to. Meanwhile, Organization does have contact information, however does not have the full Location information, like Contact does in HSDS.

Adding a contact section to the Service would improve alignment. The reporter states this is not a critical issue that absolutely needs to be addressed in this release, but it is one of the most significant limitations in alignment between the two data models.

**chirag_bhatt — 2023-03-02:** Chirag Bhatt provided a detailed analysis clarifying that references to "Location" in the ballot comment likely refer to address (physical and/or mailing) rather than a location entity. In HSDS, the contact entity is linked with service, organization, or service_at_location entities but not directly to a location entity. The FHIR Organization resource does have a contact element with purpose, name, telecom, and address. However, the Location and HealthcareService resources do not have a contact element, only telecom. Supporting contact information at the service level would require an extension added to the HealthcareService resource profile. This would be optional since HSDS does not require contacts for all service records. Chirag recommended addressing this by adding an extension in the subsequent version of the IG.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested
#### Proposal
Add a contact extension to the HealthcareService profile in the Human Services Directory IG. The extension should support multiple contacts, each with name, purpose, telecom (phone, email), and address elements, aligning with the HSDS contact entity model. The extension should be optional (0..*) to reflect that HSDS does not mandate contacts on every service record.
#### Justification
This directly addresses the identified alignment gap between HSDS and the FHIR IG. The FHIR Organization resource already supports a contact element; extending HealthcareService to do the same creates consistency across the IG and faithfully represents the HSDS data model. The ticket has the highest priority, was reopened after STU1 publication specifically for future work, and the previous workgroup vote (5-0-1) acknowledged the validity of the request. Chirag Bhatt's analysis confirmed the technical feasibility and recommended this approach for the next version.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach
#### Proposal
Rather than creating a custom extension, propose a change request to the base FHIR HealthcareService resource to add a native contact backbone element (similar to Organization.contact), which would include name, purpose, telecom, and address. In the interim, define a local extension in the IG with a clear migration path to the core element once adopted.
#### Justification
A core resource change would benefit all implementers who need contact information on services, not just the HSDS community. This approach avoids proliferation of IG-specific extensions and ensures long-term interoperability. However, core resource changes have a longer timeline and require broader consensus, so an interim extension ensures the IG can progress without waiting for the core specification change.

---

### Disposition C: Decline
#### Proposal
Decline the request and retain the current approach where contact information for services is managed through associated Organization resources. Document this design decision and provide implementation guidance for mapping HSDS contacts to Organization.contact with appropriate references from HealthcareService.
#### Justification
The FHIR Organization resource already supports full contact information. Implementers can associate contacts with the organization providing the service rather than duplicating contact structures on HealthcareService. This avoids adding extensions and keeps the IG simpler. However, this does not fully represent the HSDS model where contacts are independently linked to services, and it would leave the alignment gap identified by the reporter unaddressed.

---

### Recommendation
**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A is recommended because the alignment gap is well-documented, technically feasible, and has already received workgroup consensus. The previous vote (5-0-1) acknowledged the need, and the ticket was specifically reopened post-STU1 to be addressed in a future version. Chirag Bhatt's detailed analysis confirmed that an optional extension on the HealthcareService profile is the appropriate mechanism, since the core FHIR resource does not natively support contacts at the service level. The extension aligns the IG with HSDS's contact entity model without imposing mandatory requirements on implementers. Given the ticket's highest priority rating and the reopening for post-STU1 work, this is the natural time to implement the change.
