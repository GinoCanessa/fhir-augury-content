## Summary

This ticket requests that ownership of the OIDs page in the FHIR specification be transferred from Implementable Technology Specifications (ITS) to the FHIR Management Group (FMG). The reporter (Lloyd McKenzie) believes the OIDs page is a governance-level concern rather than a technology specification concern, making FMG the more appropriate owner.

The ticket has no description body beyond the title, no comments, and has remained in Triaged status since its creation in October 2022. The request is purely an administrative/governance change regarding page ownership—it does not propose changes to the content of the OIDs page itself.

---

## Details

The OIDs page in the FHIR specification documents Object Identifier (OID) assignments relevant to the FHIR standard. OIDs are globally unique identifiers historically used in healthcare standards (HL7 v2, v3, CDA) and still referenced in FHIR for backward compatibility with systems that rely on OID-based identification.

Page ownership determines which work group is responsible for reviewing tracker items filed against a page, managing its content, and approving changes. The request implies that the OIDs page content is more about governance of identifier assignments (an FMG responsibility) than about implementation technology formats (an ITS responsibility).

This is consistent with previous work on FHIR page ownership. FHIR-8353 (resolved as "Persuasive with Modification," now Published) established the practice of identifying which work group owns each page in the spec. The current request is a follow-up refinement to that governance model.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept (Persuasive)

**Action:** Transfer ownership of the OIDs page from ITS to FMG.

**Rationale:** The OIDs page is fundamentally about identifier governance—which OIDs are assigned, how they map to URLs, and how they are managed across FHIR versions. This is a governance activity that aligns with FMG's responsibilities for managing cross-cutting specification concerns, not an implementation technology concern. The reporter (Lloyd McKenzie, a FHIR core editor) is well-positioned to identify the appropriate ownership. OID discrepancy resolution efforts (e.g., FHIR-49641) have historically involved FMG, further supporting this reassignment.

### Disposition B: Alternative – Reassign to Terminology Infrastructure (Persuasive with Modification)

**Action:** Transfer ownership of the OIDs page to Terminology Infrastructure (Vocab/TSMG) instead of FMG.

**Rationale:** OIDs are primarily used as identifiers for terminology resources (code systems, value sets). The Terminology Services Management Group (TSMG) has been deeply involved in OID discrepancy resolution (e.g., FHIR-49641, the December 2022 OID resolution effort). Terminology Infrastructure may be a more natural home for ongoing OID maintenance than either ITS or FMG. This would consolidate OID-related governance under the group most actively working with OIDs.

### Disposition C: Decline (Not Persuasive)

**Action:** Keep the OIDs page under ITS ownership.

**Rationale:** The OIDs page describes technical identifier assignments that have direct implications for implementation formats (XML, JSON, RDF). ITS is responsible for how identifiers are used in wire formats, and OIDs are a core part of that concern. Reassigning to FMG would fragment identifier governance across multiple groups. The current ownership has not caused documented problems, and no evidence of mismanagement or neglect has been presented.

---

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Accept (Persuasive)**

The request comes from a FHIR core editor who understands the governance model well. The OIDs page is primarily about identifier governance and assignment policy, not about implementation technology. FMG is the natural owner for cross-cutting governance pages. The change is low-risk (purely administrative) and aligns with the intent of FHIR-8353, which established page ownership to help implementers understand governance responsibility. ITS should concur with the reassignment unless it identifies specific content on the page that requires ongoing ITS stewardship.
