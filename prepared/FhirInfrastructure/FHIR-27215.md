## Summary

Bas van den Heuvel submitted a change request proposing that FHIR Shorthand (FSH) include support for OperationDefinition resources when addressing CapabilityStatements. The ticket was originally resolved as "Considered for Future Use" during the June 2020 ballot cycle with a unanimous vote (14-0-0, Kramer/Rhodes), acknowledging the suggestion and adding OperationDefinition to the future development shopping list. The ticket was subsequently re-opened after publication of STU1 in October 2020 by Lynn Laakso and re-triaged, where it has remained since. The existing specification text notes that FSH does not yet help populate CapabilityStatement instances and acknowledges that creative approaches could be employed for other conformance resources such as SearchParameter; the submitter requests that OperationDefinition be explicitly included in that scope.

## Details

**Description:**

When looking at CapabilityStatements also OperationDefinitions should be included.

**Existing Wording:**

FSH does nothing to help populate that instance. There may be more interesting approaches that create CapabilityStatements more directly from requirements. Creative approaches and purpose-specific syntax could also be employed for other conformance resources such as SearchParameter.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested
#### Proposal
Add OperationDefinition to the list of conformance resources that FSH aims to support with purpose-specific syntax or creative authoring approaches, alongside CapabilityStatement and SearchParameter. Update the specification text to explicitly mention OperationDefinition as a target for future FSH support.
#### Justification
The original ballot vote was unanimously in favor (14-0-0) of adding OperationDefinition to the future development list. OperationDefinition is a core conformance resource frequently used alongside CapabilityStatements, and its omission from the FSH roadmap text is a reasonable gap to address. Including it aligns with the original ballot resolution and the community's expressed intent.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach
#### Proposal
Rather than singling out OperationDefinition, broaden the specification text to reference all conformance resources (including OperationDefinition, CompartmentDefinition, ImplementationGuide, and others) as candidates for future FSH authoring support, replacing the current narrow mention of SearchParameter alone.
#### Justification
This approach addresses the submitter's concern while also future-proofing the specification text against similar requests for other conformance resource types. It avoids the need to enumerate each resource type individually and provides a more comprehensive vision for FSH's conformance authoring capabilities.

---

### Disposition C: Decline
#### Proposal
Close the ticket without making changes, noting that the existing specification text already acknowledges future possibilities for conformance resources generally, and that OperationDefinition is implicitly covered by the phrase "other conformance resources."
#### Justification
The specification text already uses inclusive language ("other conformance resources such as SearchParameter") that can be read as encompassing OperationDefinition. The "such as" phrasing is illustrative rather than exhaustive. Adding explicit mentions of individual resource types to informative text creates a maintenance burden without materially changing the specification's normative content or FSH's actual capabilities.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B best serves both the submitter's intent and the broader community interest. The original ballot resolution unanimously acknowledged the value of supporting OperationDefinition, so declining outright would disregard that consensus. However, simply adding OperationDefinition to the existing list (Disposition A) addresses only the immediate request without considering the broader pattern — other conformance resources like CompartmentDefinition face the same gap. Broadening the language to reference all conformance resources provides a more forward-looking and maintainable solution. It satisfies the submitter's core concern, honors the original ballot consensus, and reduces the likelihood of future tickets requesting similar additions for other resource types.
