## Summary

This ticket reports a spelling error in the FHIR specification. The word **"managable"** (incorrect) is used where the correct spelling **"manageable"** should appear. The affected text is the standard extension governance boilerplate that appears throughout the specification:

> "To make the use of extensions safe and **managable**, there is strict governance applied to the definition and use of extensions. Although any implementer can define and use extensions, there is a set of requirements that must be met as part of their use and definition."

This is a straightforward typographical correction with no impact on semantics or functionality.

## Details

### Description
The reporter identified that the word "managable" is a misspelling of "manageable" in the extension governance boilerplate text. This text appears across multiple resource and extension pages in the FHIR specification. Because this is standard boilerplate, the typo may appear in many locations throughout the spec and should be corrected in the template or generation source rather than on individual pages.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept as Technical Correction (Persuasive)
**Action:** Correct the spelling of "managable" to "manageable" in the extension governance boilerplate text across the FHIR specification.

**Rationale:** This is a clear, unambiguous spelling error. The correct English spelling is "manageable." Since this text appears in a boilerplate template used across many pages, the fix should be applied to the template source so that all instances are corrected. This is a textual correction with zero impact on conformance, interoperability, or implementation. It falls squarely within the "Technical Correction" category and requires no ballot or substantive review.

**Scope of Change:** Fix the spelling in the boilerplate template source (e.g., in the FHIR publisher tooling or spec source files). All generated pages will then reflect the correct spelling.

### Disposition B: Accept with Broader Typo Review (Persuasive with Modification)
**Action:** Correct the spelling of "managable" to "manageable" and perform a broader review of the extension governance boilerplate for any additional typographical or grammatical issues.

**Rationale:** While the reported typo is the immediate issue, accepting it provides an opportunity to do a quick editorial pass on the boilerplate text to catch any other minor issues. This would reduce the likelihood of future low-priority tickets for similar corrections in the same text block.

**Scope of Change:** Same as Disposition A, plus a brief editorial review of the surrounding boilerplate text.

### Disposition C: Decline (Not Persuasive)
**Action:** Close without action.

**Rationale:** While the spelling error exists, it does not affect the meaning, conformance, or usability of the specification. Very low priority corrections consume workgroup review time that could be spent on substantive issues. The misspelling is understandable in context and does not cause confusion.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Accept as Technical Correction** is recommended. This is an obvious, unambiguous spelling error that should be corrected. The fix is trivial (a single word change in a boilerplate template), carries no risk, and improves the professional quality of the specification. There is no reason to leave a known misspelling in place, and the cost of correction is negligible compared to the cost of leaving it unresolved.
