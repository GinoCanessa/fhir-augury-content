## Description

The submitter questions why the FSH specification includes a sentence stating that FSH supports FHIR R5 datatypes `integer64` and `CodeableReference` "on a trial use basis." The argument is that FSH as a language does not depend on these specific datatype elements — all datatypes are simply expressed as strings — so the sentence is unnecessary and should be removed.

The quoted text from the spec:

> FSH supports new pre-release FHIR R5 datatypes integer64 and CodeableReference on a trial use basis.

## Ticket History

## Analysis

### Core Issue
The submitter's argument is that FSH language syntax treats all datatypes uniformly (as strings), so singling out `integer64` and `CodeableReference` with a TU caveat is misleading and unnecessary. The spec sentence implies these datatypes have special or limited support, when in fact FSH handles them the same as any other datatype.

### Prior Resolution Context
The work group previously resolved this as "Considered for Future Use" (16–0–1 vote), acknowledging the submitter's point for `integer64` but noting that:
1. **CodeableReference** does have dedicated FSH language constructs (Section 3.6.3.7), justifying TU marking for those sections.
2. Removing TU markings would be a **substantive change** requiring another ballot cycle.
3. The plan was to advance TU sections to Normative in a later ballot when appropriate.

That resolution was reverted to re-open the issue after the Normative Shorthand spec was published, suggesting the work group intended to revisit this once the spec matured further.

### FHIR-42305 Precedent
The closely related FHIR-42305 (filed for FSH 3.0.0) proposed identical wording changes and was resolved as "No Change / Question Answered," indicating the work group decided the current wording remains appropriate at that time.

### Current State
Now that FHIR R5 has been published and `integer64`/`CodeableReference` are part of the normative FHIR spec, the rationale for marking them as TU in FSH may no longer hold. The question is whether the FSH spec should:
- Remove the TU qualifier entirely (as both datatypes are now normative in R5)
- Keep the sentence but update it to reflect current status
- Remove the sentence altogether as FSH doesn't need to single out specific datatypes

---

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive — Remove the sentence entirely
**Action:** Remove the sentence "FSH supports new FHIR R5 datatypes integer64 and CodeableReference on a trial use basis" from the specification.

**Rationale:** The submitter's core point is valid — FSH as a language treats all FHIR datatypes uniformly and does not need to single out specific datatypes. With FHIR R5 now published and these datatypes normative in FHIR itself, the TU qualifier is outdated. Where FSH has specific language constructs for CodeableReference (Section 3.6.3.7), the TU/Normative marking should be handled at the section level, not via a blanket sentence about datatype support. This aligns with the original plan to advance TU sections to Normative in a later ballot.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification — Update the sentence
**Action:** Replace the sentence with: "FSH supports FHIR R5 datatypes integer64 and CodeableReference." (Remove "on a trial use basis" but retain the mention of these datatypes for informational purposes.)

**Rationale:** This is the approach proposed by FHIR-42305. It preserves the note about these R5 datatypes for readers who may be coming from R4, while removing the now-outdated TU qualifier. Sections with CodeableReference-specific language constructs can retain their own maturity markings independently.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive — Retain as-is
**Action:** Keep the current wording unchanged.

**Rationale:** The work group has previously voted (16–0–1) and again in FHIR-42305 to maintain the current approach. The TU marking reflects the maturity of the FSH spec sections that deal with these datatypes (particularly CodeableReference constructs), not the maturity of the FHIR datatypes themselves. Changing this would be a substantive modification requiring ballot review.

---

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Persuasive — Remove the sentence entirely.**

The original rationale for the TU marking was twofold: CodeableReference had new FSH constructs, and removing TU was a substantive change during that ballot cycle. Now that FHIR R5 is published (making `integer64` and `CodeableReference` normative in FHIR), and the Normative Shorthand spec has gone through additional ballots, the deferral reason no longer applies. The submitter correctly identifies that FSH language syntax is datatype-agnostic — singling out specific datatypes creates confusion. Section-level maturity markings (e.g., on Section 3.6.3.7 for CodeableReference constructs) already handle the TU/Normative distinction appropriately. Removing the sentence is the cleanest resolution and fulfills the work group's original intent to revisit this in a later ballot.
