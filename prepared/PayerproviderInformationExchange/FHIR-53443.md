## Analysis

### Context
The CARIN Digital Insurance Card (C4DIC) IG uses `Coverage.class` to represent pharmacy benefit identifiers commonly found on physical insurance cards. The industry has been transitioning from the legacy term "BIN" (Bank Identification Number) to "IIN" (Issuer Identification Number) since ISO/IEC 7812-1 retired the BIN term in 2017. FHIR-34480 (now Published) previously updated the C4DIC IG to acknowledge this transition. This ticket takes the next step by adding specific **display guidance** for how RxIIN should be presented on digital insurance cards, based on agreed-upon text from the C4DIC work group's shared planning document.

### Key Considerations
1. **Industry alignment:** The NCPDP ID Card Standard has already replaced BIN with IIN. Adding display guidance ensures the IG stays aligned with industry standards.
2. **Source material:** The guidance originates from a shared Google document that the work group has collectively authored. The resolution confirms this content has been agreed upon.
3. **Scope:** The change is additive — it adds display guidance to the Coverage Class ValueSet section, not a structural change to profiles or extensions.
4. **Predecessor:** FHIR-34480 laid the groundwork by updating terminology; this ticket completes the effort with display-level guidance.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept as Persuasive (Recommended)
**Action:** Accept the ticket as Persuasive and incorporate the RxIIN display guidance from the linked Google document into the C4DIC IG's Coverage Class ValueSet section.

**Rationale:** The guidance aligns with the industry-wide BIN→IIN transition already acknowledged in FHIR-34480. The content has been collaboratively developed by the work group in their shared planning document and the resolution is already marked Persuasive. Adding display guidance is a straightforward, additive change that improves implementer clarity without altering profiles or extensions.

### Disposition B: Accept as Persuasive with Modification
**Action:** Accept the request but require the editor to also add a brief note cross-referencing the terminology change history (i.e., that RxIIN was formerly RxBIN per ISO/IEC 7812-1 and NCPDP updates), ensuring implementers who may still encounter the older term understand the equivalence.

**Rationale:** While the core guidance should be added as requested, explicitly noting the BIN→IIN history within the guidance text (not just in FHIR-34480's resolution) would help implementers who encounter legacy systems still using the BIN terminology.

### Disposition C: Defer for Additional Detail
**Action:** Request that the submitter extract and include the specific guidance text directly in the Jira ticket rather than referencing an external Google document, then re-evaluate.

**Rationale:** The ticket currently depends on an external Google document for the actual guidance content. If the document becomes unavailable or changes, the ticket's intent may be unclear to future reviewers. However, since the work group has already agreed on the content and the resolution is Persuasive, this may introduce unnecessary delay.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Accept as Persuasive.** The request is well-founded, aligns with prior work (FHIR-34480), reflects agreed work group content, and addresses a real industry terminology transition. The change is additive display guidance that improves the IG without structural impact.
