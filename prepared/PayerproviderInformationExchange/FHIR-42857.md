## Description

The submitter requests revisions to Scenario 2 in the Background section (§3.4) of the US Dental Data Exchange IG. Several aspects of the scenario are out of alignment with current clinical practice guidelines:

1. **Incorrect antibiotic** — The antibiotic prescribed in the scenario is no longer accurate per current ADA Clinical Practice Guidelines.
2. **Incorrect activity order** — The order of activities within the scenario does not reflect actual clinical workflow (e.g., the dental care team would know about a penicillin allergy well before scheduling the next appointment).
3. **Non-standard oral location nomenclature** — Needs updating to match ISO/ANSI/ADA Standard 3950 (Designation System for Teeth and Areas of the Oral Cavity: arch first, side, then tooth).
4. **Confusing terminology** — Should use "second molar" instead of "back molar" when referencing tooth #18 to avoid confusion with tooth #17.

A Word document attachment provides the recommended revised wording and example table.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive (Reaffirm Previous Vote)

**Rationale:** This ticket already received a unanimous Persuasive vote (21-0-0) on 2024-01-29 with Russell Ott and Greg Zeller as movers. The resolution was reverted only because the change was not applied to the IG, not because of any disagreement with the substance. The companion ticket FHIR-42957 (Scenario 3 corrections by the same subject matter expert) was voted Persuasive and successfully applied. Reaffirming the original disposition and ensuring the change is applied this time is the most straightforward path.

**Action:** Reaffirm as Persuasive. Verify the attached Word document ("Change to Background item 3.4 in IG-1.docx") is still current. Apply the revised wording to Scenario 2 in the Background section (§3.4) of the US Dental Data Exchange IG. Assign to an editor to confirm application.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification

**Rationale:** While the original vote was valid, nearly two years have passed since the attachment was last updated (January 2024). ADA clinical practice guidelines or antibiotic recommendations may have been further updated. Accept the change request but require the submitter (Kyle Pelkey / Russell Ott) to confirm or refresh the attachment against current 2025 ADA guidelines before applying.

**Action:** Resolve as Persuasive with Modification. Request the submitter verify the attachment reflects the latest ADA Clinical Practice Guidelines, then apply the verified changes to the IG.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive

**Rationale:** The scenario is illustrative and intended to demonstrate a dental data exchange workflow pattern, not to serve as clinical guidance. Clinical accuracy in the narrative, while desirable, is not essential for the IG's technical purpose of specifying data exchange patterns. The risk of the scenario becoming outdated again with future guideline changes argues for keeping it simple rather than tracking evolving clinical recommendations.

**Action:** Resolve as Not Persuasive. Optionally add a disclaimer noting that scenario details are illustrative and may not reflect current clinical guidelines.
