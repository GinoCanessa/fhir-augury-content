## Summary

This ticket requests two changes to the CARIN Digital Insurance Card (DIC) IG:

1. **Modify the "Additional Insurance Card Information" extension** to enable a reference to an `SBCInsurancePlan` resource.
2. **Add a new experimental `SummaryOfBenefits` profile** based on InsurancePlan to enable a move towards codified benefits information.

The proposed resolution is to add a new profile for Summary of Benefits based on the InsurancePlan resource.

## Analysis

### Strengths
- Addresses a real gap: there is currently no standardized way to represent codified Summary of Benefits information in FHIR within the insurance card context.
- Building on InsurancePlan is architecturally sound — InsurancePlan is the FHIR resource specifically designed to describe plan-level benefit structures, cost-sharing, and coverage details.
- Marking the profile as **experimental** appropriately signals maturity level and invites implementer feedback without prematurely locking in the design.
- Extending the "Additional Insurance Card Information" extension to reference SBCInsurancePlan creates a natural link between the insurance card (Coverage) and the plan's benefit details (InsurancePlan).

### Considerations
- The relationship between this new SummaryOfBenefits profile and existing InsurancePlan work (FHIR-47649, FHIR-47650, FHIR-18213) should be clearly documented to avoid overlap or confusion about resource boundaries.
- The scope of "codified benefits" needs clarity — does this cover cost-sharing tiers, formulary-related benefits, covered services, or all of the above?
- Coordination with Financial Management WG may be needed since InsurancePlan-based benefit representation overlaps with their domain (PCT AEOB Summary, ExplanationOfBenefit benefit balance).
- As an experimental profile, implementer uptake and Connectathon testing will be important to validate the design before moving to normative or STU status.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept as Persuasive (Recommended)
**Accept the ticket as Persuasive.** Add the experimental SummaryOfBenefits profile based on InsurancePlan and extend the Additional Insurance Card Information extension to reference SBCInsurancePlan. The experimental maturity label provides appropriate guardrails. Include narrative guidance explaining the profile's relationship to existing InsurancePlan resources and its intended scope (codified plan-level benefit summaries associated with an insurance card). This fills a genuine gap in the DIC IG and aligns with real-world implementer interest in richer benefit data (as demonstrated by the Flexpa RTPBC integration work).

### Disposition B: Accept as Persuasive with Modification
**Accept as Persuasive with Modification** requiring additional documentation and scope constraints. Specifically: (1) add a "Boundaries and Relationships" section to the new profile page explaining how SummaryOfBenefits relates to base InsurancePlan and to benefit representations in other IGs (PCT AEOB, CARIN BB); (2) define an explicit minimum set of benefit categories for the experimental profile; (3) coordinate with Financial Management WG on InsurancePlan-based benefit representation to avoid divergence. This provides a more cautious path while still advancing the work.

### Disposition C: Defer to Future Ballot
**Defer to a future ballot cycle.** While the concept is valuable, the lack of community discussion (no Zulip threads or cross-references) suggests this may not be mature enough for even experimental inclusion. Request that the submitter socialize the proposal through Connectathon testing and Zulip discussion first, then bring it back with implementer feedback and a more detailed profile design. This avoids introducing an under-specified experimental profile that may need significant revision.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Accept as Persuasive.** The proposal is well-scoped — it adds an experimental profile and extends an existing extension, both of which are low-risk given the experimental label. InsurancePlan is the correct base resource for codified benefit information. The CARIN DIC IG is the logical home for linking insurance card data to plan-level benefit summaries. The experimental designation gives the community room to iterate on the design through future Connectathons and implementer feedback without blocking the current ballot.
