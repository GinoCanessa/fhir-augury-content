## Description

SMART Health Cards and SMART Health Links have been published as official HL7 FHIR IGs. The submitter requests that the CARIN Digital Insurance Card IG be updated to:

1. **Add the SHC/SHL IG as a formal dependency** in the IG configuration.
2. **Update links in multiple sections** to point to the official HL7 IG ([SMART Health Cards and Links IG v1.0.0](https://hl7.org/fhir/uv/smart-health-cards-and-links/)) instead of the pre-IG specification sites (`spec.smarthealth.cards` and `docs.smarthealthit.org`):
   - **Section 1.1 Introduction** — Add link to the SMART Health Cards and Links IG.
   - **Section 2.1 Introduction** — Add link to the SMART Health Cards and Links IG.
   - **Section 3.3 Use Case – Consumer Access and Exchange** — Replace external `spec.smarthealth.cards` and `docs.smarthealthit.org` links with references to the official IG.
   - **Section 4.0.7 Payer SHALL/SHOULD requirements** — Update all conformance statement links (JWS creation, revocation, SHL specification, file download format) from external spec sites to the corresponding pages in the official IG.

The resolution description states: *"Update IG as recommended."*

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive (Accept as Submitted)

**Action:** Accept the ticket as-is and update the CARIN Digital Insurance Card IG exactly as the submitter describes.

**Rationale:** The SMART Health Cards and Links IG (v1.0.0) is now a published HL7 FHIR IG. The DIC IG currently references pre-publication external specification sites (`spec.smarthealth.cards`, `docs.smarthealthit.org`) which are not the normative HL7 sources. Updating to the official IG links and adding the dependency is a straightforward, uncontroversial maintenance task. The submitter has provided specific, section-by-section guidance. The PIE work group has already met with the reporter (per the 2026-01-29 comment) and is actively working on the changes. The resolution is already marked "Persuasive."

**Changes required:**
- Add `hl7.fhir.uv.smart-health-cards-and-links` as a dependency in the DIC IG's `sushi-config.yaml` or `ig.ini`.
- Update Sections 1.1, 2.1, 3.3, and 4.0.7 to replace external links with references to the official IG.
- Verify all conformance statement links (SHALL/SHOULD/SHALL NOT) resolve correctly to the published IG pages.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification

**Action:** Accept the intent of the ticket but expand scope to also review and update the conformance language in Section 4.0.7 to align with the published SHC/SHL IG's normative requirements.

**Rationale:** Simply swapping links may not be sufficient if the published IG has modified or refined the conformance requirements compared to the pre-IG specifications (e.g., revocation requirements, JWS encoding details, passcode handling). Given that FHIR-46914 raised concerns about the appropriateness of verifiability for insurance cards (resolved as "Not Persuasive with Modification"), the work group should ensure the conformance language in Section 4.0.7 is still consistent with the published IG and any modifications made in response to ballot feedback.

**Changes required:**
- All changes from Disposition A.
- Review Section 4.0.7 conformance statements against the published SHC/SHL IG for any normative differences.
- Update conformance language if the published IG has changed requirements from the original external specs.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive

**Action:** Decline the change request.

**Rationale:** This disposition would only be appropriate if the work group determined that the CARIN DIC IG should not take a dependency on the SHC/SHL IG at this time — for example, if there were concerns about version stability or if the DIC IG intentionally references the pre-IG specification for backward compatibility reasons. However, given that the SHC/SHL IG is published and the external spec sites are not the normative HL7 source, this disposition is difficult to justify.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Persuasive (Accept as Submitted)**

The request is well-defined, low-risk, and aligns with standard IG maintenance practices. The SMART Health Cards and Links IG is officially published, making the current external links in the DIC IG outdated. The PIE work group has already engaged with the reporter and is actively incorporating the changes. The submitter provided clear, section-by-section instructions, and the resolution is already marked "Persuasive." Accepting as submitted is the most efficient path forward.
