## Summary

This ticket requests that the Da Vinci Member Attribution List (ATR) specification implement notifications so that providers are automatically informed when attribution changes occur, particularly when new members are attributed to them. The reporter argues that this is important enough to address from the start rather than defer. The ticket was resolved as a Duplicate of FHIR-25915, which itself duplicates FHIR-40128. FHIR-40128 ("Subscription functionality") has since been resolved as Persuasive with Modification and published, incorporating subscription-based notification functionality into the ATR specification using the Subscription backport.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Confirm Duplicate Resolution
#### Proposal
Confirm the existing Duplicate resolution. FHIR-25898 requests notification functionality for attribution changes, which is the same concern addressed by FHIR-25915 and ultimately resolved through FHIR-40128 ("Subscription functionality"). FHIR-40128 was resolved as Persuasive with Modification and has been published, incorporating subscription-based notifications into the ATR specification. No further action is required on this ticket.
#### Justification
The underlying concern — that providers should be automatically notified of attribution changes — has been fully addressed through the duplicate chain. FHIR-40128 integrated the Subscription backport into the ATR specification, enabling producers to push change notifications to consumers. Since that ticket is published, the requested functionality is now part of the specification.

---

### Disposition B: Reopen for Clarification of Scope
#### Proposal
Reopen the ticket to verify whether the notification requirements in FHIR-25898 are fully satisfied by the changes published under FHIR-40128. Specifically, confirm that the published subscription functionality covers the scenario of notifying providers when new members are attributed to them (not just changes to existing members). If gaps are identified, file a new targeted tracker.
#### Justification
While FHIR-40128 addresses subscription-based notifications broadly, the original request in FHIR-25898 specifically emphasizes notification of new member attribution. It is possible that the published changes focus on changes to existing attribution lists rather than the addition of entirely new members. A brief review would ensure no gap remains.

---

### Disposition C: Decline
#### Proposal
Close the ticket without further action, noting that the duplicate resolution stands and the concern has been superseded by published specification changes.
#### Justification
The ticket is over five years old, has been through multiple rounds of review, and the underlying functionality has been addressed through FHIR-40128. Reopening at this stage would create unnecessary rework. If any gaps exist in the published notification functionality, they should be addressed through a new, focused ticket with current context rather than revisiting this aging tracker.

---

### Recommendation
**Recommended disposition:** A — Confirm Duplicate Resolution

The duplicate chain from FHIR-25898 to FHIR-25915 to FHIR-40128 is well-documented and coherent. All three tickets address the same fundamental concern: that providers should be automatically notified of changes to Member Attribution Lists rather than having to poll for updates. FHIR-40128 resolved this by integrating the Subscription backport into the ATR specification, and that resolution has been published. The comment from nageshbashyam on 2024-02-05 confirms the resolution was intentionally set before marking as duplicate, and the PDex Community Call review on 2026-03-27 further validates the disposition. Confirming the duplicate resolution is the cleanest path forward and accurately reflects the history of this issue.
