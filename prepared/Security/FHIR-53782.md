## Description

The ticket requests that corrected data should have a profiled Provenance resource so that future consumers of the data can clearly determine that the data was corrected. The reporter proposes:

1. **A Profile on Provenance** for corrections, recording both the reference and business identifiers (important downstream for matching, especially for data removal):
   - Provenance indicates that a given Reference and Identifier are to be **deleted**
   - Provenance indicates that a given Reference and Identifier are to be **replaced** by Target
2. **Provenance.activity** populated with codes indicating **patient-requested correction**
3. **Provenance.entity** pointing at the records corrected communication, with `entity.role = #instantiates`

A draft IG has been published by the reporter at:
http://build.fhir.org/ig/JohnMoehrke/correction/branches/main/index.html

## Zulip Discussions

### 1. [implementers > Provenance.target when only recording a removal](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/implementers/topic/Provenance.target%20when%20only%20recording%20a%20removal) (Jan 2026)

Directly related discussion initiated by John Moehrke alongside FHIR-53917. Key points:
- **John Moehrke** argues that the current model is inconsistent: when replacing a resource, the removed resource goes in `.entity`, but when only removing (no replacement), the removed resource goes in `.target`. He proposes relaxing `.target` to 0..* and using `.entity.role` of removal consistently.
- **Grahame Grieve** disagrees, preferring `.target` to reference the deleted resource for consistency with other usages.
- **Daniel Venton** and **Lloyd McKenzie** agree with Grahame — `.target` should list the affected resources.
- **Lloyd McKenzie** suggests two separate Provenance instances for replacement (one for old, one for new).
- **John Moehrke** counters that replacement is a single activity and two Provenances loses the relationship between removed and new resources.

This thread provides important context on the unresolved tension around how removal/replacement should be modeled in Provenance — directly relevant to the correction profile proposed in FHIR-53782.

## Analysis

This ticket is part of a cluster of related change requests by John Moehrke aimed at establishing comprehensive Provenance patterns for patient-requested data corrections. The companion ticket FHIR-53783 (example of correction Provenance) has already been **Applied as Persuasive**, and FHIR-53917 (Provenance of removal) was **Applied as Not Persuasive with Modification**. FHIR-56165 (merge Provenance) was resolved as **Persuasive**.

The reporter has developed a draft IG (http://build.fhir.org/ig/JohnMoehrke/correction/branches/main/index.html) that demonstrates the proposed profile. The community discussion shows broad agreement that Provenance should document corrections, though there is active debate about the specific modeling pattern (particularly around `.target` vs `.entity` for removals).

The proposal intersects with an area of growing international interest (EHDS patient rights) and addresses a real gap: there is currently no standard Provenance profile for patient-requested corrections.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive

**Rationale:** The request clearly identifies a gap — corrected data lacks a standardized Provenance profile for corrections, making it difficult for downstream consumers to determine that data was corrected. The reporter has demonstrated feasibility through a draft IG. The companion ticket FHIR-53783 was already accepted as Persuasive, establishing precedent that the community agrees correction provenance needs to be addressed. A profiled Provenance with defined activity codes and entity usage for corrections would provide implementers clear, consistent guidance.

**Action:** Add a Provenance profile for patient-requested corrections to the Patient Corrections specification, using the draft IG as the basis. Define activity codes for patient-requested correction and establish entity usage for linking to correction request documentation.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification

**Rationale:** The concept of a Provenance profile for corrections is sound and needed, but the specific proposal should be aligned with the resolution of companion tickets (particularly FHIR-53917 on removal modeling and FHIR-53783 on examples). The ticket proposes a profile but the modeling details for removal vs. replacement scenarios remain under active debate. Rather than mandating a specific profile now, provide guidance and examples in the Patient Corrections specification, incorporating the patterns agreed upon in the companion tickets. The profile itself may be better maintained in the reporter's IG until the removal/replacement modeling questions are fully resolved.

**Action:** Accept the need for correction Provenance documentation. Add guidance to the Patient Corrections specification describing how Provenance should be used for corrections, referencing the draft IG and incorporating the agreed patterns from FHIR-53783 and FHIR-53917. Defer mandating a specific formal profile until the entity/target modeling for removal is fully resolved.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive

**Rationale:** The draft IG already provides the proposed profile and can evolve independently of the core specification. The companion ticket FHIR-53783 (already Applied) addresses the need for examples. Adding a mandated profile to the Patient Corrections specification may be premature given unresolved design questions about Provenance.target cardinality (FHIR-53917) and removal modeling. Implementers can follow the draft IG guidance without changes to the spec.

**Action:** No change to the specification. Implementers may reference the draft IG for correction Provenance patterns.

## Recommendation

**Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification**

The need for standardized correction Provenance is well-established and the community has shown clear support through the acceptance of companion tickets. However, this ticket proposes a specific _profile_ while the underlying modeling patterns (especially removal and replacement in Provenance) are still being refined across FHIR-53917 and related work. The most pragmatic path is to accept the concept, add guidance and examples to the Patient Corrections specification now, and formalize the profile once the companion modeling questions are resolved. This avoids creating a profile that may need immediate revision while still advancing the work.
