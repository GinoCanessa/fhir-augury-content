## Problem Statement

The FHIR security page (`security.html`) contains a **SHALL** statement requiring that any Access Control decision to deny an activity must be recorded (audited). The submitter argues this conformance requirement is too strong for all use cases—some implementations (e.g., test servers) may legitimately not maintain audit logs, and mandating this would prevent them from claiming FHIR conformance. The submitter requests changing **SHALL** to **SHOULD**, or making the language conditional on environment/capabilities.

### Quoted Language Under Dispute

> "Audit of the Access Control Decision: Any Access Control decision to deny an activity **SHALL** be recorded, and Access Control decisions to permit access **SHOULD** be recorded. The record **SHOULD** use the FHIR AuditEvent Resource."

## Discussion Summary

Three comments capture a substantive debate:

1. **John Moehrke (2026-02-19)** — Defended the **SHALL**, noting that recording access control denials is critical security practice: "It is these that show you where the edges of your policy are being probed, and where people are trying to go beyond their scope."

2. **Lloyd McKenzie (2026-03-23)** — Supported the change, arguing it is unreasonable to declare systems non-conformant for lacking audit logging. Cited test servers as legitimate use cases where audit is unnecessary and noted "it's not HL7's place to set expectations for audit in general."

3. **John Moehrke (2026-03-30)** — Reported that Elliot Silver agreed to the proposed change on a call, and asked Lloyd McKenzie if changing to **SHOULD** would be acceptable.

The resolution description states: **"Will change the SHALL to a SHOULD."**

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive — Change SHALL to SHOULD (as proposed)

**Action:** Change the security.html text from "Any Access Control decision to deny an activity **SHALL** be recorded" to "Any Access Control decision to deny an activity **SHOULD** be recorded."

**Rationale:** The current SHALL creates a conformance gate that is inappropriate for all FHIR implementations. Not every system maintains an audit log (e.g., test servers, lightweight embedded systems), and it is not HL7's role to mandate audit policy universally. Changing to SHOULD preserves the strong recommendation while allowing legitimate implementations that lack audit capability to remain conformant. This aligns with the principle in FHIR-28908 that policy positions should not be encoded as testable conformance statements. The submitter, the reporter of the original SHALL language (John Moehrke), and Lloyd McKenzie all agreed to this change on the 2026-03-30 call.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification — Conditional SHALL

**Action:** Instead of a blanket change to SHOULD, add a conditional: "In production environments handling protected health information, Access Control decisions to deny an activity **SHALL** be recorded. In other environments, recording deny decisions is **RECOMMENDED**."

**Rationale:** This preserves the security intent for production healthcare systems while acknowledging that test servers and non-PHI environments need not be bound by audit requirements. However, this adds complexity and may be difficult to enforce, since FHIR cannot reliably distinguish "production" from "non-production" at the specification level.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive — Retain SHALL

**Action:** Keep the current SHALL language unchanged.

**Rationale:** Recording access control denials is a fundamental security practice. Removing the SHALL weakens the security posture of the specification. Systems that cannot meet this requirement can document their deviation. However, this position was not supported in the workgroup discussion—both Lloyd McKenzie and ultimately John Moehrke accepted the change to SHOULD.

---

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Persuasive — Change SHALL to SHOULD.**

This is the resolution already agreed upon by the participants (submitter Elliot Silver, Security WG member John Moehrke, and Lloyd McKenzie) during the 2026-03-30 call. It is the simplest, cleanest change, aligns with the documented resolution ("Will change the SHALL to a SHOULD"), and is consistent with the broader FHIR principle that conformance statements should be testable and appropriate for all implementation contexts. The SHOULD still communicates strong guidance that audit of deny decisions is best practice.
