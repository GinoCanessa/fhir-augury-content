## Description

This ticket was split from [FHIR-43418](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43418) (a Technical Correction for typos in the "Using CQL With FHIR" IG). The submitter requests adding a new section titled "Considerations re precision and uncertainty" to the CQL documentation. The goal is to highlight issues and implications of precision and uncertainty that may be apparent to those with a technical computing background but less so to those from a medical or facility environment, since both perspectives are required for optimal measure design. The submitter specifically calls out examples such as comparing timing elements at minutes versus milliseconds precision.

## Analysis

This is a documentation enhancement request for the CQL specification. The core ask is straightforward: add guidance to help measure authors who may not have deep technical computing backgrounds understand the implications of precision and uncertainty when writing CQL expressions, particularly around date/time comparisons and duration calculations.

**Strength of the request:**
- The "CQL Uncertainties" Zulip thread (Dec 2023) demonstrates that precision and uncertainty are genuinely confusing even for experienced implementers and engine developers (Evan Machusak notes that authors with 20-30 measures each "almost never get the answer right").
- FHIR-48482 (already resolved as Persuasive) shows the community has recognized the need for precision clarifications in specific areas, but a broader overview section is still missing.
- The request was explicitly encouraged by Bryn Rhodes in FHIR-43418 comments, suggesting WG receptivity.

**Considerations:**
- The scope is somewhat open-ended — "considerations re precision and uncertainty" could range from a short paragraph to a comprehensive appendix.
- The CQL spec already has technical content on this topic (e.g., [§5 Language Semantics — Implementing Precision-Based Timing with Uncertainty](https://cql.hl7.org/05-languagesemantics.html#implementing-precision-based-timing-with-uncertainty)), but it is written for implementers, not measure authors.
- Broader community discussion about whether uncertainty should become a first-class CQL concept is ongoing and could affect how such a section is framed.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive
**Resolution:** Accept the request and add a new section to the CQL Author's Guide (or the "Using CQL with FHIR" IG) titled "Considerations for Precision and Uncertainty."

**Rationale:** The community discussion strongly supports the need for author-facing guidance on precision and uncertainty. Experienced implementers and WG members have acknowledged this is a significant pain point for measure authors. A dedicated section would serve as a practical reference covering: (1) how date/time precision affects comparisons and duration calculations; (2) common pitfalls such as Measurement Period parameters without day-level precision; (3) examples showing when uncertainty intervals arise vs. scalar results; (4) guidance on how authors can ensure precision alignment in their expressions. The specific scope and placement should be determined by the editors, but the need is well-established.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification
**Resolution:** Accept the intent but narrow the initial scope to a focused subsection within the existing Author's Guide content on Date and Time intervals, rather than a standalone section.

**Rationale:** While the need is real, the full scope of "precision and uncertainty" touches many aspects of CQL (date/time arithmetic, comparisons, duration/difference calculations, type implications). Creating a comprehensive standalone section may be premature given ongoing discussions about whether uncertainty should become a first-class CQL concept. Instead, augment the existing Date and Time intervals discussion in the Author's Guide with practical guidance, common pitfalls, and concrete examples (e.g., why `Interval[2023, 2024]` as a Measurement Period breaks measures, why `years between DateTime(2005) and DateTime(2010)` returns `[4,5]`). This delivers immediate value while keeping the door open for a broader treatment as the uncertainty model matures.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive
**Resolution:** Decline the request. Existing documentation is sufficient.

**Rationale:** The CQL specification already covers precision and uncertainty in §5 Language Semantics, including a dedicated section on "Implementing Precision-Based Timing with Uncertainty." The Author's Guide also discusses Date and Time intervals. Additional clarifications have been addressed through FHIR-48482 (seconds/milliseconds precision). Adding another section risks duplicating existing content or creating maintenance burden. Authors can be directed to existing documentation and community resources (e.g., the "CQL Uncertainties" Zulip thread).

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Persuasive** — The evidence from community discussions, the explicit encouragement from a WG member (Bryn Rhodes) to file this as a change request, and the real-world experience of measure authors all support adding dedicated author-facing guidance on precision and uncertainty. The existing spec content on this topic is implementer-oriented and does not adequately serve the measure author audience. The specific scope and editorial placement should be left to the editors, but the request itself is well-motivated and should be accepted.
