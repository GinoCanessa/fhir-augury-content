## Summary

The submitter requests a wording change in the **appointment-book** hook description within the CDS Hooks Library specification. The current text reads:

> "This hook enables CDS Services to **intervene** in the decision of when future appointments should be scheduled, where they should be scheduled, what services should be booked, to identify actions that need to occur prior to scheduled appointments, etc."

The reporter finds the word **"intervene"** too aggressive and suggests replacing it with softer language such as **"provide guidance"**, **"guide"**, or **"help"**.

This is a straightforward editorial/wording change request with no structural or technical implications.

## Analysis

### Nature of the Change
This is a **purely editorial change** — replacing a single word ("intervene") in the appointment-book hook's descriptive text. There is no impact on hook semantics, context fields, prefetch templates, or technical behavior.

### Submitter's Rationale
The word "intervene" carries connotations of interference or interruption that may not align with the collaborative, supportive role CDS Services are intended to play. The submitter suggests alternatives that better reflect the advisory nature of CDS: "provide guidance", "guide", or "help".

### Context
- The reporter (Celine Lefebvre) has a pattern of submitting editorial/wording change requests during ballots (see FHIR-36508, FHIR-36521), indicating careful attention to specification language.
- The appointment-book hook has been the subject of several other change requests during the same period (FHIR-42045 on maturity, FHIR-22236 on workflow description), but none address this specific wording.
- The word "intervene" appears only in the hook description and does not affect any normative or conformance language.

---

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive
**Action:** Accept the change. Replace "intervene" with "provide guidance" in the appointment-book hook description.

**Rationale:** The word "intervene" implies forceful interference, which mischaracterizes the advisory role of CDS Services. Replacing it with "provide guidance" accurately reflects that CDS Services offer decision support rather than override clinician decisions. This is a low-risk editorial improvement that aligns the language with the intent of the specification.

**Proposed revised text:**
> "This hook enables CDS Services to **provide guidance on** the decision of when future appointments should be scheduled, where they should be scheduled, what services should be booked, to identify actions that need to occur prior to scheduled appointments, etc."

---

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification
**Action:** Accept the intent but use a broader rewrite that improves the entire sentence's clarity and tone.

**Rationale:** While the submitter correctly identifies "intervene" as problematic, the sentence could benefit from a more comprehensive rewrite for readability. A modified version avoids "intervene" while also improving the sentence structure.

**Proposed revised text:**
> "This hook enables CDS Services to **support decisions about** when and where future appointments should be scheduled, what services should be booked, and what actions need to occur prior to scheduled appointments."

---

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive
**Action:** Decline the change; retain the current wording.

**Rationale:** The word "intervene" in context means "to become involved in a process so as to alter or improve it" and does not inherently carry negative connotations in a technical specification. CDS Services do, by design, interpose themselves in clinical workflows to surface relevant information. Changing the word to something softer could understate the active role CDS Services play.

---

## Recommendation

**Disposition B — Persuasive with Modification**

The submitter's concern is valid: "intervene" carries unnecessarily adversarial connotations for what is fundamentally a supportive, advisory function. However, rather than a simple word swap, a light rewrite of the full sentence (Disposition B) produces clearer, more readable text while addressing the concern. The revised phrasing — "support decisions about" — accurately reflects the CDS role without overstating or understating it, and the restructured sentence is more concise. This is a low-risk editorial improvement with no technical impact.
