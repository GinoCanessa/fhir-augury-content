## Summary

This ticket requests the inclusion of EpisodeOfCare as an optional context element in the CDS Hooks Library, specifically for hooks such as order-sign. The submitter suggests that either an Encounter ID or an EpisodeOfCare ID should be available as context, noting that the existing wording only references the FHIR Encounter.id of the current encounter. The ticket was originally resolved as "Considered for Future Use" in April 2020, with a unanimous vote (19-0-0) noting that EpisodeOfCare was not yet mature enough for inclusion as a standard prefetch context. It was subsequently reopened in June 2021 after publication of STU1. Discussion has centered on the low implementation maturity of EpisodeOfCare, the fact that EpisodeOfCare is typically discoverable via the Encounter resource, and whether there is sufficient real-world usage to justify a dedicated context parameter. As of the most recent comments (June 2025), the work group has requested further justification from the submitter regarding the level of implementation support for EpisodeOfCare.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested
#### Proposal
Add `episodeOfCareId` as an optional context field to relevant CDS Hooks (e.g., order-sign, patient-view) in the CDS Hooks Library specification. The field would accept a FHIR EpisodeOfCare resource ID, enabling CDS services to access episode-level context when available. The existing `encounterId` field would remain unchanged.
#### Justification
EpisodeOfCare provides a longitudinal grouping of care activities that is distinct from individual encounters. For CDS services that need to reason across a care episode — such as chronic disease management, care coordination, or transitional care protocols — direct access to the EpisodeOfCare context can simplify logic and reduce the need for additional FHIR queries. Making it optional avoids imposing burden on systems that do not support EpisodeOfCare. The ticket has been open since 2020 and was reopened after STU1, suggesting persistent interest.

---

### Disposition B: Defer Pending Maturity Evidence
#### Proposal
Keep the ticket open but defer action until there is demonstrated implementation maturity of the EpisodeOfCare resource in CDS-relevant contexts. Request that the submitter or interested parties provide concrete evidence of EpisodeOfCare implementations being used in clinical decision support workflows, including use cases where the EpisodeOfCare is not discoverable via the Encounter resource.
#### Justification
The work group has consistently noted (2020, 2025) that EpisodeOfCare lacks sufficient implementation experience to warrant standardization as a hook context parameter. Since EpisodeOfCare is typically linked from Encounter and therefore discoverable without a dedicated context field, adding it prematurely could introduce complexity without clear benefit. Deferral allows the specification to evolve based on real-world evidence rather than theoretical use cases, while keeping the door open for future inclusion.

---

### Disposition C: Decline
#### Proposal
Decline the request on the basis that EpisodeOfCare context is already accessible through the existing Encounter context. Close the ticket as "Won't Fix."
#### Justification
As noted by Isaac Vetter and Bryn Rhodes, EpisodeOfCare is typically linked from the Encounter resource and is therefore always discoverable by CDS services that have access to the encounter context. Adding a dedicated `episodeOfCareId` context field introduces specification surface area and implementation burden without a demonstrated use case where the Encounter-based discovery path is insufficient. The resource's low implementation maturity, acknowledged repeatedly over the ticket's five-year history, further weakens the case for standardization.

---

### Recommendation
**Recommended disposition:** B (Defer Pending Maturity Evidence)

The ticket raises a valid concern — there are legitimate CDS scenarios where EpisodeOfCare context could be valuable, particularly for longitudinal care coordination. However, the work group's own repeated assessment over five years has been that EpisodeOfCare implementation maturity is insufficient to justify a standard context parameter. The most recent comment (June 2025) explicitly asks the submitter for evidence of implementation support, which has not yet been provided. Deferral is the most balanced path: it respects the submitter's concern, avoids premature standardization of a parameter with uncertain adoption, and establishes a clear criterion (demonstrated implementation maturity) for future acceptance. This also aligns with the work group's stated plan to revisit Hook Library issues after base spec updates are complete.
