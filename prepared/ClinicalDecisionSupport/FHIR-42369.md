## Description

The reporter (Bas van den Heuvel) raises two concerns about the `encounter-start` CDS Hook (STU1, maturity level 1):

1. **Sub-encounter ambiguity:** The hook definition does not clarify whether it fires for "sub-encounters" within an admission — e.g., when an inpatient has a face-to-face meeting with a clinician or moves between departments.
2. **Unnecessary inpatient/outpatient distinction:** The current spec distinguishes between inpatient (admission) and outpatient (check-in) triggering contexts. The reporter argues this adds confusion and that the hook should simply fire at the beginning of *any* Encounter, regardless of type.

The current spec language reads:
> "In an inpatient setting, this would be the time of admission. In an outpatient/community environment, this would be the time of patient-check-in for a face-to-face or equivalent for a virtual/telephone encounter."

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive — Simplify and Clarify Hook Trigger

**Action:** Revise the `encounter-start` hook definition to:
1. Remove the inpatient/outpatient distinction from the trigger description.
2. State that the hook fires at the beginning of any Encounter resource creation/activation, regardless of encounter type or class.
3. Add explicit guidance that sub-encounters (e.g., departmental transfers, specialist consultations within an admission) are also encounters and the hook fires for them too.

**Rationale:** The reporter's request aligns with the principle that CDS Hooks should be tied to workflow events, not encounter classifications. The WG's own discussion (Bob Dolin, Floyd Eisenberg) converged on "arrival/check-in" as the meaningful trigger rather than encounter class. Simplification reduces implementer confusion and broadens the hook's utility. However, this requires the WG to have resolved the outstanding workflow questions from the 2023 implementer callout.

**Risk:** May increase hook firing frequency significantly in inpatient settings (multiple sub-encounters per hospitalization), potentially causing CDS fatigue. Implementers need guidance on managing volume.

### Disposition B: Considered for Future Use — Defer Pending Implementer Feedback

**Action:** Maintain the current "Considered for Future Use" resolution. Keep the implementer callout active and defer substantive changes until sufficient real-world feedback is collected on encounter workflow patterns.

**Rationale:** The 2023 WG vote (12-0-0) explicitly acknowledged that unanswered workflow questions prevent accurately describing when this hook should fire. The callout questions remain largely unanswered: When does an encounter start? For which users? How many encounters per hospitalization? Without this data, changes risk creating new ambiguities. The ticket was reopened post-publication but no new implementer feedback has been recorded.

**Risk:** The hook remains underspecified, potentially leading to inconsistent implementations across vendors. The "Highest" priority and age of the ticket (since 2023) suggest the community expects progress.

### Disposition C: Persuasive with Modification — Clarify Without Simplifying

**Action:** Keep the inpatient/outpatient distinction but add clarifications:
1. Explicitly state that the hook fires once per Encounter resource, including sub-encounters.
2. Define "encounter start" as the point of patient arrival/check-in (per WG consensus from 2023-10-25 discussion).
3. Add an informative note listing encounter types/scenarios where the hook fires (ED arrival, inpatient admission, ambulatory check-in, telehealth session start, departmental transfer).
4. Retain the implementer feedback callout for further refinement.

**Rationale:** This addresses the reporter's primary concern (sub-encounter ambiguity) and incorporates the WG's "arrival at location" consensus without the risk of removing the inpatient/outpatient context that some implementers may rely on. It is an incremental improvement that doesn't require full resolution of all workflow questions.

**Risk:** Retaining the inpatient/outpatient distinction may perpetuate some confusion, and the enumerated scenarios may not cover all implementations.

## Recommendation

**Disposition C: Persuasive with Modification** is recommended.

This disposition is the most pragmatic path forward because:

1. **It addresses the core ask** — sub-encounter firing is clarified, which is the reporter's primary concern.
2. **It incorporates existing WG consensus** — the "arrival at location" trigger definition from the 2023-10-25 discussion (Bob Dolin, Floyd Eisenberg, Juliet Rubini) is already substantively agreed upon.
3. **It avoids premature simplification** — removing the inpatient/outpatient distinction entirely (Disposition A) is a larger change that the WG explicitly deferred due to unanswered workflow questions. Implementer feedback has not materialized to resolve those questions.
4. **It makes concrete progress** — unlike Disposition B (further deferral), this moves the specification forward after two years of the ticket being open, respecting its "Highest" priority.
5. **It is compatible with the companion ticket** — FHIR-42368 (appointment-book workflow) can be addressed in parallel with consistent principles about hook trigger timing.

The WG should also actively pursue the implementer feedback questions through Da Vinci workgroup calls, as Lloyd McKenzie suggested, to inform future iterations.
