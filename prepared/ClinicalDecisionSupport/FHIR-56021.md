## Description

The submitter identifies an ambiguity in the "Passing DTR QuestionnaireResponses to Downstream Processes" section of the Da Vinci DTR IG. The existing spec text requires that a DTR Client SHALL place a QuestionnaireResponse in a collection Bundle (complying with the `questionnaireResponseBundle` profile), and that the Bundle SHALL include resources referenced by `answerReference` that are not already contained within the QuestionnaireResponse.

However, the spec is silent on whether *other* referenced resources (e.g., `subject`, `author`, `encounter`) must also be included. The submitter argues this absence is ambiguous and proposes adding an explicit statement: **"No other resources referenced in the QuestionnaireResponse need to be included in the bundle."**

## Resolution: Persuasive with Modification

The committee agreed with the concern but modified the proposed wording. The approved addition to the spec is:

> *"Resources referenced elsewhere in the QuestionnaireResponse, such as subject or author, do not need to be included in the bundle."*

This provides the requested clarification while giving concrete examples (`subject`, `author`) of the types of references that are excluded from the Bundle inclusion requirement, making the intent clearer than the submitter's original blanket statement.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept as Persuasive with Modification (Recommended)

**Action:** Accept the resolution as stated — add the clarifying sentence: *"Resources referenced elsewhere in the QuestionnaireResponse, such as subject or author, do not need to be included in the bundle."*

**Rationale:** The existing spec text creates genuine ambiguity about whether non-answer references must be included in the Bundle. The approved wording resolves this clearly and concisely, with concrete examples to aid implementers. The change is scoped narrowly, has already been reviewed and approved on the BR call (2026-03-25), and aligns with the original submitter's intent. Including specific examples like `subject` and `author` is an improvement over the submitter's blanket statement, as it helps implementers understand the scope without having to reason abstractly about which references qualify.

### Disposition B: Accept as Persuasive (Original Wording)

**Action:** Use the submitter's original proposed text verbatim: *"No other resources referenced in the QuestionnaireResponse need to be included in the bundle."*

**Rationale:** The submitter's wording is more comprehensive and covers all non-answer references without enumerating specific examples. This avoids the risk that the modified wording's examples (`subject`, `author`) could be misread as an exhaustive list, potentially causing confusion about other reference types (e.g., `encounter`, `source`). However, the modified wording already uses "such as" (implying a non-exhaustive list), and the committee has already reached consensus on the modified version, making this change unnecessary and potentially disruptive.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive

**Action:** Reject the change request and leave the spec text as-is.

**Rationale:** One could argue that the current spec text is sufficiently clear: by stating that only `answerReference` resources SHALL be included, the implication is that other references need not be. Explicit negative requirements ("you do NOT need to do X") are not typically added for every possible action a spec does not require. However, this position is weak given the submitter's real-world confusion, and adding a brief clarification carries minimal risk while improving implementer experience.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A — Accept as Persuasive with Modification.** The resolution wording is a clear, low-risk improvement that resolves a genuine ambiguity. It has already been reviewed and approved on the BR call, and the use of "such as" prevents the examples from being read as exhaustive. There is no reason to deviate from the committee's consensus.
