## Problem Statement

The submitter (Bas van den Heuvel) reports that the language describing when the `appointment-book` CDS Hook fires is ambiguous. Specifically, the current specification does not make clear:

1. Whether the hook is called once or multiple times during the appointment scheduling workflow.
2. If called once, at which moment it fires (at the start of scheduling, when appointment details change, or just before saving).
3. Which firing moments are required vs. optional for the EHR to support.

The submitter also notes that even if the hook fires just before saving, it should fire again when a change is accepted after a CDS recommendation.

## Resolution Description

**Persuasive with Modification:** Add an implementer callout asking for feedback on how the `appt-book` hook does and should work in practice.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept as Persuasive with Modification (Confirm Current Resolution)

**Action:** Affirm the current resolution. The implementer feedback callout was already applied via PR #649 (merged January 2024). The ticket was reopened post-publication to allow the resolution to be re-applied under the current ballot cycle. Accept the resolution as-is: the callout solicits real-world implementer input on when and how the hook should fire, which is the prerequisite before the spec can prescribe specific behavior.

**Rationale:** The work group previously voted 17-0-0 for the original approach (Considered for Future Use + callout). The callout is already merged and live. Sufficient implementer feedback has not yet been gathered to dictate specific workflow behavior. This resolution acknowledges the problem while avoiding premature normative constraints.

### Disposition B: Persuasive — Prescribe Specific Workflow Guidance

**Action:** Go beyond the callout and add normative or recommended guidance to the `appointment-book` hook specification. Based on Bob's feedback (captured in comments) that the intent is analogous to `order-sign` — fire when all appointment details are specified, not at each step — codify this as the recommended firing behavior. Specify that the hook SHOULD fire when the appointment is being finalized/confirmed, and MAY fire again if appointment details change after CDS feedback is accepted.

**Rationale:** The original concern was ambiguity in the spec. While the callout gathers feedback, the work group already has directional consensus (fire once when details are complete, similar to order-sign). Two years have passed since the callout was added. If sufficient feedback has been received, this would resolve the ambiguity directly rather than leaving it open-ended.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive — Close Without Further Action

**Action:** Close the ticket without additional changes. The implementer callout has already been applied. The ambiguity is acknowledged but considered inherent to the early maturity of the hook. Let implementers decide workflow behavior organically until the hook has broader adoption.

**Rationale:** The hook is still evolving and has limited real-world deployment data. Prescribing specific workflow behavior prematurely could constrain implementations. The callout serves its purpose. Further changes should be driven by concrete implementer feedback submitted through the callout, tracked as separate tickets.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Accept as Persuasive with Modification (Confirm Current Resolution).**

This is the most pragmatic path forward. The implementer callout was already applied (PR #649, merged January 2024) and the work group voted 17-0-0 for this approach. The ticket was reopened post-publication for procedural reasons. The callout appropriately defers normative workflow constraints until sufficient real-world feedback is collected. The work group should confirm the resolution and monitor for incoming implementer feedback that could drive a future ticket with specific workflow guidance.
