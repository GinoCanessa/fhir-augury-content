## Description

The reporter requests that the Clinical Practice Guidelines (CPG) Implementation Guide acknowledge and reference "knowledge coupling" clinical decision support tools developed by Dr. Lawrence L. Weed, who originated problem lists, SOAP notes, and other components of problem-oriented health records (POHRs).

Specifically, Section 1.5 of the CPG IG states that "this effort is a concerted attempt to build on, learn from, contribute to, and work with existing approaches, initiatives, and efforts in guideline development, translation, and implementation." The reporter argues that knowledge coupling tools represent a distinct and relevant approach not currently referenced in the IG. Knowledge coupling tools are described as differing in crucial ways from the approaches currently acknowledged.

The reporter references:
- The HL7 EHR WG POHR Project Confluence page ([page 120752354](https://confluence.hl7.org/pages/viewpage.action?pageId=120752354))
- Sections 3.3.1 (pp. 44-46), Chapter 8 (pp. 103-135), and Section 9.3 (pp. 156-160) of the book *Ending Medicine's Chronic Dysfunction: Tools and Standards for Medical Decision Making* (Springer, 2021)
- A script for a Zoom talk on clinical decision support tools and POHRs (March 15, 2023)

## Analysis

This ticket requests that the CPG IG's background section (Section 1.5) acknowledge knowledge coupling tools as a distinct CDS approach. The reporter, Lincoln Weed, shares a surname with Dr. Lawrence L. Weed, the originator of the referenced knowledge coupling approach.

**Key considerations:**

1. **Scope of request:** The ticket asks for the CPG IG to reference an alternative methodology—knowledge coupling tools and problem-oriented health records—in its survey of existing CDS approaches. This is primarily a content/reference addition to the background section rather than a technical or structural change.

2. **Historical significance:** Dr. Lawrence Weed's contributions to medical informatics (problem lists, SOAP notes, POHRs) are well-recognized in the field. Knowledge coupling tools represent a historically important approach to clinical decision support.

3. **CPG IG context:** The CPG IG already addresses knowledge representation levels, guideline lifecycle processes, and various CDS approaches. Related tickets (FHIR-24242, FHIR-23980, FHIR-24179) targeting the same sections were generally resolved as Persuasive or Persuasive with Modification, showing willingness to refine the background content.

4. **Technical alignment:** Knowledge coupling tools represent a conceptual approach to CDS that predates and differs from the FHIR-based computable guideline approach. While historically relevant, the degree of technical overlap with the CPG IG's FHIR-based approach needs workgroup assessment.

5. **Priority vs. impact:** The ticket is marked "Highest" priority but the requested change is an addition of references/acknowledgment in the background section, not a change to technical content or FHIR resources.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive
**Action:** Add a reference to knowledge coupling tools and the POHR approach in Section 1.5 of the CPG IG, acknowledging Dr. Lawrence Weed's knowledge coupling tools as a significant prior approach to clinical decision support that informed the broader CDS landscape. Include a reference to the HL7 EHR WG POHR Project Confluence page and the Springer 2021 book.

**Rationale:** Section 1.5 explicitly states the intent to build on and learn from existing CDS approaches. Knowledge coupling tools represent a historically significant and conceptually distinct approach to CDS that merits acknowledgment alongside other referenced efforts.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification
**Action:** Add a brief mention of knowledge coupling tools and problem-oriented health records in Section 1.5 as part of the broader landscape of CDS approaches, with a reference link to the HL7 EHR WG POHR Project Confluence page. Do not incorporate the full scope of references and detailed discussion suggested by the reporter; instead, keep the addition proportional to other referenced approaches in the section.

**Rationale:** While knowledge coupling tools are a valid part of the CDS history, Section 1.5 is a high-level overview and the addition should be consistent in scope and depth with how other approaches are referenced. A brief acknowledgment with a pointer to more detailed resources balances completeness with conciseness.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive
**Action:** No change to the CPG IG. The workgroup may optionally note this input for future consideration.

**Rationale:** The CPG IG's Section 1.5 is a general statement of intent to learn from existing approaches, not an exhaustive catalog of all CDS methodologies. Knowledge coupling tools, while historically significant, represent a different paradigm from the FHIR-based computable guideline approach that the IG specifies. The referenced materials are primarily published works rather than active standards-track efforts that the CPG IG is designed to interoperate with. Adding specific methodological references risks expanding the background section beyond its intended scope.

## Recommendation

**Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification**

The CPG IG's Section 1.5 does claim broad intent to learn from existing approaches, so a brief acknowledgment of knowledge coupling tools is reasonable. However, the addition should be proportional to how other approaches are referenced in the same section—a sentence or two with a link to the Confluence page, rather than detailed discussion of the Weed methodology. This is consistent with how the workgroup resolved similar background-section tickets (FHIR-24242, FHIR-23980) with measured modifications rather than wholesale additions. The "Highest" priority rating appears disproportionate to what is essentially a reference addition in a background section, and the workgroup may wish to adjust priority accordingly.
