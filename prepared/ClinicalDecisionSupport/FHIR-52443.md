## Summary

This ticket requests that the CQL specification provide guidance on how to handle UCUM [arbitrary units](https://ucum.org/ucum#section-Arbitrary-Units) in clinical logic. UCUM arbitrary units (e.g., `[IU]`, `[iU]`, `[arb'U]`) are context-dependent and generally cannot participate in algebraic operations or comparisons because their magnitude is defined only within a specific assay or reference preparation—not on an absolute scale.

The proposal allows comparison of quantities involving arbitrary units under two strict conditions: (1) the units are exactly the same (case-sensitively), and (2) one or both operands to the comparison are literal values. Authoring systems should also provide a warning that arbitrary units differ based on context and care must be taken to ensure comparisons are appropriate.

## Details

### Description

UCUM defines arbitrary units as units whose magnitude depends on a specific biological or chemical context rather than a universal physical measurement. In general, operations on arbitrary units are not supported. However, clinical logic frequently involves quantities expressed in arbitrary units (e.g., lab results reported in `[IU]/mL`), and reasonable comparisons should be permitted when the context is appropriate.

The ticket proposes the following guidance for the CQL specification:

- Systems that support UCUM units **SHOULD** support comparison of quantities involving arbitrary units when:
  1. The units are exactly the same (case-sensitive match)
  2. One or both operands to the comparison are literal values
- Authoring systems **SHOULD** provide a warning to authors that arbitrary units differ based on context, and care should be taken to ensure comparisons are appropriate

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept (Persuasive)

**Action:** Accept the proposed guidance as written and add it to the CQL specification.

Add a new section or subsection to the CQL specification (likely within the Quantities or Operations section) that:

1. Acknowledges UCUM arbitrary units and their context-dependent nature
2. States that systems supporting UCUM units **SHOULD** support comparison of quantities with arbitrary units when the units are exactly the same (case-sensitive) and one or both operands are literals
3. Requires authoring systems to produce a warning when authors write comparisons involving arbitrary units, alerting them that these units are context-dependent

This approach is straightforward, addresses a real clinical need (e.g., comparing lab results in `[IU]/mL` against thresholds), and follows the precedent set by FHIR-41343 which provided explicit guidance for another non-standard UCUM unit category ("special" units). The two-condition guard (same unit + literal operand) is conservative and limits the risk of inappropriate comparisons.

### Disposition B: Coordinate with FHIRPath quantity rework (Persuasive with Modification)

**Action:** Accept the intent but defer the specific wording to be incorporated into the broader FHIRPath quantity handling clarifications (FHIR-56190).

Rather than adding isolated guidance to CQL, coordinate with the extensive FHIRPath quantity rework in FHIR-56190 to ensure arbitrary unit handling is addressed consistently across both FHIRPath and CQL. The modification would:

1. Accept the two conditions (same unit case-sensitively, literal operand) as the basis for the guidance
2. Extend the scope to explicitly cover equality, equivalence, and comparison operations (not just "comparison" generically)
3. Align the terminology and structure with the clarified quantity handling in FHIRPath
4. Clarify that arithmetic operations (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division) on arbitrary units should return empty, consistent with the FHIR-41343 resolution for special units

This ensures consistency across specifications and avoids conflicting guidance.

### Disposition C: Decline (Not Persuasive)

**Action:** Decline the proposal on the grounds that arbitrary units are intentionally excluded from operations by the UCUM standard, and adding guidance to support comparisons—even with guards—may encourage unsafe clinical logic.

The UCUM specification explicitly states that arbitrary units "are not 'real' units" and "do not represent any specific amount of any specific substance." Permitting comparisons, even with the proposed guards, could lead authors to write CQL that compares quantities whose magnitudes are not truly comparable across different assay contexts. The `SHOULD`-level conformance language means implementations may silently differ in behavior, creating portability risks for clinical decision support artifacts. Authors needing to compare such values can use workarounds (e.g., converting to coded observations or using string-based unit checks with explicit documentation).

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Accept (Persuasive)** is recommended.

The proposal is well-scoped with conservative guards (exact case-sensitive unit match + literal operand), addresses a genuine clinical need for CQL authors working with lab results in arbitrary units, and follows established precedent from FHIR-41343. The reporter (Bryn Rhodes) is a primary CQL specification author with deep domain expertise. While coordination with FHIR-56190 (Disposition B) has merit, that ticket focuses on FHIRPath-level quantity handling and is under a different work group (ITS); the CQL-specific guidance proposed here is complementary rather than duplicative. The warning requirement for authoring systems provides an appropriate safety net without blocking valid clinical use cases.
