## Description

The submitter requests an update to the Electronic Case Reporting (eCR) FHIR IG to provide guidance on what the conformance keyword "SHOULD" means within the context of the IG. The ticket title includes the qualifier "(if needed)", suggesting the submitter is flagging a potential gap in the IG's documentation of conformance language semantics rather than a confirmed deficiency.

The conformance keywords (SHALL, SHOULD, MAY, etc.) are defined by [RFC 2119](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/rfc2119) and are used extensively in FHIR IGs to express varying levels of obligation. However, implementers sometimes struggle to understand the practical implications of "SHOULD" — particularly whether data marked as SHOULD is expected to be present when available, or whether omission requires justification. Clarifying this within the eCR IG could improve implementation consistency.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition 1: Persuasive — Add a conformance language guidance section to the eCR IG

**Rationale:** Add a dedicated section (or subsection in the IG's introduction/general guidance page) explaining the meaning and practical implications of RFC 2119 conformance keywords (SHALL, SHOULD, MAY, SHALL NOT, SHOULD NOT) within the eCR IG context. This follows the pattern used by other mature IGs such as US Core, which include a "Conformance Expectations" page. The section should clarify that "SHOULD" means the element is recommended and expected when the data is available, but implementers may omit it with justification. This provides clear implementer guidance without changing any structural conformance requirements.

**Pros:**
- Directly addresses implementer confusion about conformance expectations
- Follows established patterns from other published IGs (e.g., US Core)
- Low-risk change — adds documentation only, no profile or structural changes
- Aligns with the reporter's prior pattern of improving IG clarity (see FHIR-46585)

**Cons:**
- The "(if needed)" qualifier in the title suggests even the submitter is uncertain whether this gap exists
- May be redundant if implementers are expected to already know RFC 2119
- Requires effort to draft and review the text

### Disposition 2: Not Persuasive — Conformance keyword semantics are already defined by RFC 2119 and FHIR core

**Rationale:** The meaning of SHOULD (and other conformance keywords) is authoritatively defined by RFC 2119, which is referenced by the FHIR core specification. Repeating or paraphrasing this definition in the eCR IG risks introducing inconsistency or dilution of the standard semantics. Implementers are expected to understand these keywords through their foundational FHIR knowledge. No specific instance of confusion or misapplication in the eCR IG has been cited in this ticket.

**Pros:**
- Avoids redundant documentation that could drift from the authoritative source
- The ticket does not cite a specific problem instance — it is speculative ("if needed")
- No effort required

**Cons:**
- Does not address potential real-world implementer confusion
- Other IGs have found value in restating conformance expectations in their own context

### Disposition 3: Persuasive with Modification — Add a brief note referencing RFC 2119 with an eCR-specific example

**Rationale:** Rather than a full conformance language section, add a brief note in the IG's introduction or general guidance page that references RFC 2119 for conformance keyword definitions and provides one or two eCR-specific examples of what "SHOULD" means in practice (e.g., "The eICR Composition SHOULD include an Encounter Diagnosis section when diagnosis data is available; omission should only occur when no diagnosis has been recorded"). This balances clarity with brevity and avoids duplicating RFC 2119.

**Pros:**
- Lightweight change that provides practical value
- Contextualizes the general RFC 2119 definitions within eCR workflows
- Minimal maintenance burden
- Addresses the spirit of the request without over-engineering

**Cons:**
- Limited in scope — may not cover all implementer questions
- Selecting the "right" examples requires care to avoid implying unstated requirements

---

## Recommendation

**Disposition 3: Persuasive with Modification** is recommended. The ticket's own "(if needed)" qualifier acknowledges uncertainty about whether a full conformance guidance section is warranted. A lightweight addition — a brief note referencing RFC 2119 with one or two eCR-specific examples — provides practical value to implementers without duplicating authoritative standards or requiring significant drafting effort. This approach is consistent with the reporter's track record of improving eCR IG clarity (FHIR-46585) while remaining proportionate to the undefined scope of the request.
