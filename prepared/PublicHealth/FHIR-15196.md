This ballot comment identifies a gap in the Electronic Case Reporting (eCR) Implementation Guide: the guide lacks documentation on how EHRs interact with clinical decision support (CDS) to evaluate reportability. The commenter notes that trigger codes initiate data summarization and evaluation by a decision support engine—not direct reporting to public health. The eICR serves as a patient data summary that must be processed by decision support before being forwarded. The comment requests guidance on CDS Hooks or other mechanisms for accessing decision support, whether provided as an external service or integrated into the EHR's decision support system using CQL rules.

## Details

### Existing Wording (from ballot)

The guide lists "Profiles defined as part of this Guide" covering:
- Trigger Code Transaction
- eICR Transaction
- Reportability Response Transaction

### Commenter's Concern

The guide is missing information related to interacting with decision support. Specifically:

1. **Trigger codes** should identify events that cause patient data to be summarized and processed by a decision support engine to evaluate for reportable conditions.
2. **eICR as data summary**: The eICR is being used as the patient data summary, but it is not ready to be forwarded to public health until it has been evaluated by decision support.
3. **CDS integration needed**: There will need to be CDS Hooks or some other method for accessing decision support—either provided as an external service or integrated into the EHR's DSS (populated with CQL rules).

### Resolution Context

The resolution is **Persuasive with Modification**, indicating the workgroup agreed with the substance of the comment but applied changes differently than proposed. The specification this applies to (Electronic Case Reporting) is now marked as deprecated, suggesting the content has been superseded by a newer IG version.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept (Confirm Persuasive with Modification)

**Action:** Confirm the existing "Persuasive with Modification" resolution. Acknowledge that the concern about documenting CDS integration in the eCR workflow was valid and that modifications were applied to subsequent versions of the eCR IG. Note that the specification is now deprecated and the successor IG (eCR FHIR IG on hl7.org) addresses decision support integration with CDS Hooks and CQL-based rule evaluation.

**Rationale:** The comment correctly identified a significant gap. The resolution was appropriate—the guide needed to document how trigger codes initiate decision support evaluation rather than direct public health reporting. Subsequent versions of the eCR IG have incorporated this guidance.

### Disposition B: Accept with Additional Note

**Action:** Confirm "Persuasive with Modification" and add an explicit cross-reference note pointing to the current (non-deprecated) eCR IG where CDS Hooks integration and CQL-based decision support evaluation are documented. Recommend closing related tickets (FHIR-19207, FHIR-19234) as a group if they have not already been addressed.

**Rationale:** While the original resolution was sound, providing a clear linkage to the successor IG helps reviewers and implementers understand where the decision support guidance now lives. Grouping related eCR decision support tickets ensures consistent disposition.

### Disposition C: Decline (Not Persuasive)

**Action:** Mark as "Not Persuasive" on the basis that the deprecated specification is no longer maintained and the comment lacks a specific proposed wording change (the commenter provided no summary or concrete text).

**Rationale:** The comment raises a valid architectural concern but does not propose specific profile changes or wording. The specification is deprecated, making further modification moot. However, this disposition risks dismissing a substantive concern that influenced the evolution of the eCR IG.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Accept (Confirm Persuasive with Modification)**

The existing resolution is appropriate. The commenter correctly identified that the eCR guide needed to document the role of decision support in evaluating reportability before forwarding to public health. The "Persuasive with Modification" resolution acknowledged this gap, and the deprecated status of the specification indicates the content has been superseded by a more complete IG that addresses these concerns. Confirming the resolution closes this ticket cleanly without requiring further action on a deprecated specification.
