## Description Summary

This ticket, submitted by NIOSH-NPPTL, proposes the development of a PPE (Personal Protective Equipment) inventory data standard at the granularity of manufacturer, model number, and (if available) NIOSH approval number for respirators. The rationale stems from a 2020 pilot study with the Center for Medical Interoperability (C4MI) in which 78 hospitals reported near-daily PPE inventories for 15 weeks. Analysis revealed significant data quality issues: only 74.2% of PPE model numbers could be verified (60% for N95 respirators, 40% for face shields, 84% for surgical masks), and only about one-third of manufacturers were correct due to private labeling. The submitter argues this granularity is essential for identifying counterfeit and substandard respiratory protection and for enabling automated PPE surveillance systems.

**Existing Wording:** None

**Proposed Wording:** Develop a PPE inventory data standard at the granularity of the manufacturer, model number, and, if available, the NIOSH approval number for respirators.

## Analysis

### Context
This ticket is one of a batch of NIOSH/NPPTL-originated PPE-focused ballot comments submitted during the SANER STU1 ballot cycle. The core ask — a detailed PPE data standard with manufacturer, model number, and NIOSH approval number granularity — reflects real operational challenges documented during the COVID-19 pandemic PPE tracking pilot.

### Key Considerations
1. **Partial overlap with accepted work:** FHIR-29872 (Persuasive with Modification) already addressed PPE inventory granularity at the manufacturer/model/approval level. FHIR-29869 (Not Persuasive with Modification) added vocabulary guidance for national models including value sets. This means the core concern of FHIR-29867 has been **partially addressed** through sibling tickets.
2. **Scope determination:** The final resolution ("Not within scope of this or Helios work") indicates the workgroup determined that creating a full PPE data standard goes beyond what SANER is designed to do. SANER focuses on situational awareness measures, not detailed supply chain data models.
3. **History of reconsideration:** The ticket was originally resolved as "Considered for Future Use" with unanimous support (22-0-0), then reopened after STU1 publication, and ultimately resolved as "Not Persuasive" — suggesting the workgroup gave it thorough consideration over multiple cycles.
4. **Status inconsistency:** The ticket shows status "Triaged" but resolution "Not Persuasive," which may indicate an incomplete workflow state that should be verified.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Affirm "Not Persuasive"
**Rationale:** The core concern about PPE inventory granularity has been partially addressed through FHIR-29872 (accepted) and FHIR-29869 (modified). Creating a comprehensive PPE data standard with manufacturer/model/NIOSH-approval-number granularity extends beyond SANER's scope as a situational awareness IG. The workgroup already considered this over multiple ballot cycles (2021–2024) and appropriately determined it is out of scope. A dedicated PPE data standard would be better pursued through a separate project proposal or a different IG.

**Suggested Action:** Close as "Not Persuasive" and ensure status is updated from "Triaged" to "Resolved - No Change" for consistency with the resolution.

### Disposition B: Change to "Considered for Future Use"
**Rationale:** The original 22-0-0 vote for "Considered for Future Use" reflected genuine interest in this work. While it is out of scope for the current SANER IG, PPE supply chain data standardization remains a valid public health need. Reverting to "Considered for Future Use" would acknowledge the merit of the proposal while recognizing it requires a separate specification effort — potentially under a new project scope or as an extension to SANER in a future cycle.

**Suggested Action:** Change resolution to "Considered for Future Use" with a note that a separate project proposal would be the appropriate vehicle for this work. Update status to "Resolved - No Change."

### Disposition C: Persuasive with Modification (Add Informative Guidance)
**Rationale:** Rather than dismissing the proposal entirely, the IG could include a brief informative section acknowledging the need for PPE data standards at manufacturer/model granularity and pointing implementers toward relevant work (e.g., NIOSH approval number registries, UDI standards for PPE). This would preserve the educational value of the NIOSH pilot findings without requiring normative changes to the IG.

**Suggested Action:** Add an informative paragraph in the PPE-related measures section referencing NIOSH approval numbers and the importance of manufacturer/model-level granularity for PPE surveillance, with links to external NIOSH resources.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Affirm "Not Persuasive"**

The workgroup has already thoroughly considered this request over multiple ballot cycles, and the core concern has been partially addressed by accepted sibling tickets (FHIR-29872, FHIR-29869). The full scope of a PPE data standard — encompassing manufacturer, model number, and NIOSH approval number — extends beyond SANER's remit as a situational awareness IG. The "Not Persuasive" resolution with rationale "Not within scope of this or Helios work" is appropriate and well-founded. The status should be corrected from "Triaged" to "Resolved - No Change" to match the resolution.
