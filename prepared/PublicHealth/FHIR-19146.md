## Description

This ticket was submitted as ballot feedback (ODH #18) during the 2018 ballot cycle. The commenter quotes the ODH home page text that describes the US vs. international design intent of the specification:

> "While this profile is specified for the US Realm, the design is intended to support international needs. Three of the referenced value sets supporting Occupation, Industry, and Supervisory Level are necessarily US specific, and the remaining three supporting Work Schedule, Employment Status, and Work Classification are defined to support international concepts."

The commenter asks whether the ODH model has considered incorporating **PTO (Personal Time Off)** as a data element, noting that the gap in available PTO between the US and some European countries is significant. The question was triggered by the statement about international support and raises whether PTO availability is relevant to occupational health.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Not Persuasive
**Rationale:** PTO (Personal Time Off) is outside the scope of the Occupational Data for Health model. ODH is designed to capture core occupational factors relevant to health — occupation, industry, employment status, work schedule, work classification, and supervisory level. PTO is a human resources / employer-specific benefit that varies widely even within a single country and is not a standardized occupational health concept captured by international classification systems (e.g., ILO, SOC, NAICS). The original PHWG vote (20-0-6) to resolve as "Considered for Future Use" with the rationale "This is not part of the considerations for the current model" supports this position. Regarding the broader international perspective question, the specification already acknowledges that three value sets are US-specific while three are internationally applicable — this is an intentional design choice documented in the IG.

**Applied To:** No specification change needed.

### Disposition B: Considered for Future Use
**Rationale:** The question about PTO is a valid observation about a potential gap in occupational health data, particularly in an international context where employment benefits differ significantly. However, PTO is not currently modeled in ODH and adding it would require significant scope expansion, new value sets, and international consensus on how to represent employment benefits. This topic should be documented for consideration in a future ODH release if there is demonstrated demand from implementers or public health use cases that require PTO data. This mirrors the original 2023-01-16 resolution before it was reverted.

**Applied To:** No immediate specification change. Document as a future consideration item for the ODH work group.

### Disposition C: Persuasive with Modification
**Rationale:** While adding PTO as a data element is out of scope, the specification could be enhanced with a brief clarification acknowledging that employment benefits (such as PTO) are intentionally excluded from the current model but may be relevant to international occupational health assessments. This would directly address the commenter's question and improve the IG's documentation of scope boundaries.

**Applied To:** Add a clarifying note to the ODH IG home page or scope section stating that employment benefits (e.g., PTO, leave policies) are not part of the current ODH model scope.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Not Persuasive**

The PTO concept is clearly outside the defined scope of the ODH model, which focuses on standardized occupational classification data relevant to clinical and public health use cases. The original PHWG vote (20-0-6) already reflected strong consensus that this is not part of the current model's considerations. Since the ticket was re-opened post-publication for administrative reasons rather than due to new information or arguments, the same reasoning applies. No specification change is warranted.
