## Analysis

### Context
This ticket was filed in September 2020 during active development of the Military Service History IG, which sought to reuse ODH profiles. The core idea is to establish a shared abstract "Employment History Episode" profile based on the Observation resource (using coded components for extensibility) that serves as the common ancestor for:
- **ODH Past or Present Job** profile
- **Military Service Episode** profile
- Potentially other employment-related profiles

### Key Concerns
1. **Stale / Aging Ticket:** Filed in 2020, last updated April 2021 with a comment that it would be addressed in "the next full release." Over 4 years have passed with no resolution.
2. **Conflicting Basis Already Identified:** FHIR-33434 (resolved as Persuasive) identified that the Military Service Episode profile is not actually based on Employment History Episode despite the documentation claiming so. This suggests the reuse intent from FHIR-28533 was never fully implemented.
3. **Tooling Issues Noted:** FHIR-33439 mentions that "due to tooling issues this profile uses the abstract, base profile" — indicating practical obstacles to the proposed reuse pattern.
4. **Military Service IG Has Progressed:** Multiple related tickets (FHIR-33434, FHIR-33435, FHIR-33439) have been resolved as Persuasive and Published in the Military Service IG, suggesting the IG has evolved since this request was filed.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive — Formalize the Shared Abstract Profile
**Action:** Create or update the Employment History Episode as a shared abstract profile in ODH, and ensure the Military Service IG's Military Service Episode actually derives from it (resolving the discrepancy noted in FHIR-33434).
- **Rationale:** This aligns with the original design intent described in Zulip and the stated goal of cross-IG interoperability. The community discussion confirms the Observation-with-components pattern is the agreed-upon approach.
- **Risk:** Requires coordination between the ODH and Military Service IG teams, and may require resolving tooling issues flagged in FHIR-33439.

### Disposition B: Not Persuasive — Superseded by Military Service IG Ballot Resolutions
**Action:** Close this ticket as not persuasive, noting that the Military Service IG has undergone ballot reconciliation (FHIR-33434, FHIR-33435, FHIR-33439 all resolved Persuasive/Published) and the relationship between the profiles has been clarified through those subsequent tickets.
- **Rationale:** The specific changes requested may have been overtaken by the ballot process. The conflicting-basis issue (FHIR-33434) was resolved separately, and the IG has been published.
- **Risk:** If the abstract profile was never actually formalized, leaving this unresolved could perpetuate misalignment between ODH and Military Service IG.

### Disposition C: Deferred — Pending Next ODH Release Coordination
**Action:** Defer this ticket until the next planned ODH IG update cycle, when cross-IG profile alignment can be addressed holistically.
- **Rationale:** The last comment (April 2021) indicated this was planned for "the next full release." Deferring acknowledges the intent while recognizing that coordination between work groups (Public Health and Cross-Group Projects) is needed.
- **Risk:** Further delay on an already 4+ year old ticket; the requirements may have shifted.

## Recommendation

**Disposition B: Not Persuasive — Superseded by Military Service IG Ballot Resolutions.**

The strongest evidence supports this disposition. The core issue — aligning the Employment History Episode profile across ODH and Military Service IG — has been substantially addressed through the Military Service IG ballot cycle. FHIR-33434 directly tackled the conflicting basis between Employment History Episode and Military Service Episode and was resolved as Persuasive. The typo and tooling issues (FHIR-33435, FHIR-33439) around the same profile were also resolved. While the original enhancement request envisioned a more formal shared abstraction, the practical alignment has been achieved through these subsequent tickets. If further cross-IG coordination is still needed, it should be filed as a new, more specific ticket reflecting the current state of both IGs rather than reopening this 2020-era request.
