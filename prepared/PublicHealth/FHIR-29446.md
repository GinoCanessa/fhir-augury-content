## Description

The submitter requests incorporating a ConceptMap resource from HAVBED (Hospital Available Beds for Emergencies and Disasters) terminology to the CDC Vocabulary for Bed Location. The ticket also suggests adding related content about mapping from HavBed to SANER (Situation Awareness for Novel Epidemic Response) and back.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Not Persuasive (Affirm Current Resolution)

**Rationale:** The current resolution of Not Persuasive is well-supported. The HAVBED standard has not gained community traction within the FHIR ecosystem, and no stakeholders have expressed interest in maintaining HAVBED code systems or ConceptMaps since the ticket was first filed in 2020. The ticket was already deferred once (2021) and re-opened, yet no community interest materialized in the subsequent two years before final resolution. The SANER specification has evolved independently without requiring HAVBED mappings, and the successor US SAFR specification (FHIR-50364) focuses on DEQM alignment rather than HAVBED compatibility.

**Wording:** "Not Persuasive. No community interest in HAVBED code systems or mappings has materialized since this ticket was originally filed. The SANER and US SAFR specifications have evolved without requiring HAVBED ConceptMaps. The current resolution stands."

### Disposition B: Considered for Future Use

**Rationale:** While there is currently no active demand for HAVBED mappings, the underlying use case — standardized bed availability reporting terminology — remains relevant for emergency preparedness. If future regulatory or interoperability requirements arise that reference HAVBED, having this ticket available as a deferred item would facilitate re-engagement. This disposition acknowledges the potential value without committing current resources.

**Wording:** "Considered for Future Use. The HAVBED-to-CDC bed location ConceptMap is not needed at this time due to lack of community interest, but the use case for standardized bed terminology mapping may become relevant in future emergency preparedness efforts. Defer for reconsideration if demand arises."

### Disposition C: Persuasive with Modification

**Rationale:** Rather than a full HAVBED-to-CDC ConceptMap, the specification could include a brief informative section or appendix noting the existence of HAVBED terminology and its relationship to the bed location concepts used in SANER/SAFR. This would provide implementers with context about legacy systems without the overhead of maintaining a formal ConceptMap resource.

**Wording:** "Persuasive with Modification. Add an informative note in the SANER specification acknowledging HAVBED terminology and its relationship to CDC bed location vocabulary, without creating a formal ConceptMap resource. This provides implementer guidance for organizations transitioning from HAVBED-based systems."

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Not Persuasive (Affirm Current Resolution)**

This is the strongest disposition. The ticket has been through two resolution cycles over four years (2020–2024), including an explicit deferral and re-opening. Despite this extended consideration period, no community stakeholders have championed HAVBED integration. The resolution description — "No interest has been shown for the HAVBED requirements or codesystems" — accurately reflects the state of the community. The SANER and US SAFR specifications have continued to evolve without HAVBED dependency. Affirming the Not Persuasive resolution avoids reopening work that has no demonstrated demand and allows the workgroup to focus on active priorities like FHIR-50364 (SANER/SAFR alignment).
