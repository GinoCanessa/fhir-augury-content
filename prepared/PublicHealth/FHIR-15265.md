## Description

The commenter questions the meaning of "reside" in the eCR IG text:

> **Existing Wording:** "This means processing rules for both the location that care is being provided and for the location where the patient resides - wherever in the country that may be."

> **Comment:** By reside, does the IG mean where the patient actually lives, or where the case is reported?

The commenter is asking for clarification of the term "resides" — specifically whether it refers to the patient's physical home address or the jurisdiction where the case is reported. These can differ (e.g., a patient receiving care in a different state from where they live, or a case reported in a jurisdiction different from the patient's residence).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive — Clarify "resides" in the IG text

**Action:** Revise the IG wording to explicitly define "resides" as the patient's physical home address (as opposed to the reporting jurisdiction). Suggested revised wording:

> "This means processing rules for both the location where care is being provided and for the location where the patient physically lives (their home address) — wherever in the country that may be."

**Rationale:** The commenter raises a valid ambiguity. In public health case reporting, the patient's residence address and the reporting jurisdiction can differ significantly. Clarifying this term eliminates confusion for implementers and aligns with the intent of jurisdiction-based reporting rules. Related tickets FHIR-15228 and FHIR-15226 (both resolved as Persuasive) addressed similar location-clarity issues in the same eCR ballot, suggesting the work group recognized location ambiguity as a real problem.

**Risk:** Low. This is a documentation/wording clarification with no structural impact.

### Disposition B: Not Persuasive — Existing wording is sufficient

**Action:** No change. Close the ticket with a comment explaining that "resides" in common usage means the patient's home address, and that implementers should interpret it accordingly.

**Rationale:** The word "resides" has a commonly understood meaning (where a person lives). The IG already distinguishes between "the location that care is being provided" and "the location where the patient resides," which implicitly differentiates care location from home address. Adding further explanation may be unnecessary.

**Risk:** Some implementers may still be confused, particularly in cross-jurisdictional scenarios where residence, care location, and reporting jurisdiction all differ.

### Disposition C: Considered for Future Use — Defer to successor IG

**Action:** Retain the current resolution of "Considered for Future Use." The Electronic Case Reporting specification referenced here is now deprecated, and any clarification should be addressed in the successor eCR IG (US Public Health Profiles / eCR FHIR IG R2+).

**Rationale:** Since the specification is deprecated, making changes to it is impractical. The concern is valid but should be carried forward to the active version of the eCR IG where the wording can be addressed in context.

**Risk:** The ambiguity persists until the successor IG explicitly addresses it, but this is mitigated by the deprecated status of the original spec.

## Recommendation

**Disposition C: Considered for Future Use** — This is the most pragmatic choice. The ticket's existing resolution already reflects this. The underlying specification is deprecated, so applying a wording change to it would have no practical effect. The clarification concern is valid (as evidenced by related tickets FHIR-15228 and FHIR-15226 being resolved as Persuasive for similar location-ambiguity issues), but the appropriate venue for the fix is the successor eCR IG, not the deprecated version. The work group should confirm this resolution and ensure the concern is tracked for the active specification.
