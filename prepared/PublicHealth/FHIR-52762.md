## Summary

This ticket requests that the US Public Health Profiles Library (USPHPL) adopt CRMI (Canonical Resource Management Infrastructure) MessageDefinition profiles instead of maintaining its own. The USPHPL currently defines a `us-ph-messagedefinition` profile. The companion ticket [FHIR-52761](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-52761) — owned by Clinical Decision Support — was resolved as **Persuasive** to create Shareable and Publishable MessageDefinition profiles in CRMI, but those profiles have not yet been created.

This ticket was previously resolved as **"Considered for Future Use"** (vote: 17-0-4, Sarah Gaunt / Ravi Kafle) on 2025-09-25 with the rationale that the CRMI profiles were not yet available. It was re-opened post-STU2 publication by Lynn Laakso on 2026-02-17 to revisit the alignment.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Re-affirm "Considered for Future Use" (Accept deferred approach)

**Resolution:** Considered for Future Use

Re-affirm the original 2025-09-25 resolution. The CRMI MessageDefinition profiles (Shareable and Publishable) approved under FHIR-52761 have not yet been created. Until CRMI publishes these profiles, the USPHPL cannot adopt them. Retain the existing `us-ph-messagedefinition` profile for now and revisit once CRMI delivers the MessageDefinition profiles. Track FHIR-52761 implementation progress as a trigger for re-evaluation.

### Disposition B: Accept with conditional timeline (Persuasive with Modification)

**Resolution:** Persuasive with Modification

Accept the request to adopt CRMI MessageDefinition profiles, but make adoption conditional on CRMI publishing the Shareable and Publishable MessageDefinition profiles. Add a tracker item in the USPHPL backlog so that when CRMI delivers these profiles (per FHIR-52761), the USPHPL will replace `us-ph-messagedefinition` with a profile derived from or aligned to the CRMI profiles in the next STU cycle. This creates an explicit commitment with a clear trigger rather than an open-ended deferral.

### Disposition C: Decline (Not Persuasive)

**Resolution:** Not Persuasive

Decline the request. The USPHPL `us-ph-messagedefinition` profile is already published and in use. Migrating to CRMI profiles would introduce a breaking change for implementers who have adopted the current profile. The CRMI profiles do not yet exist and their design may not fully align with public health requirements (e.g., event code bindings, response pattern constraints). The USPHPL should maintain its own MessageDefinition profile and independently evaluate CRMI alignment only if and when CRMI profiles materialize and demonstrate clear value.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Re-affirm "Considered for Future Use"**

This is the most pragmatic path. The blocking dependency — CRMI MessageDefinition profiles from FHIR-52761 — remains undelivered despite the Persuasive resolution. The previous workgroup vote (17-0-4) already endorsed this approach, and the underlying conditions have not changed. Re-affirming the deferral keeps the door open for CRMI alignment while avoiding premature commitments or disruption to the existing USPHPL profile. The ticket should remain open and be revisited when CRMI publishes the relevant profiles.
