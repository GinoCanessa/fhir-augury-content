Key points relevant to this ticket:
- **Vassil Peytchev** (the reporter of FHIR-21649) explains that `ServiceRequest.supportingInfo` can reference any resource and is the appropriate place for information essential to the referral authorization decision.
- **Lloyd McKenzie** distinguishes between `ServiceRequest.supportingInfo` (information tied to the authorization, independent of who fulfills it) and `Task.input` (information added during dispatch to a specific fulfiller).
- The discussion confirms the BSeR IG's pattern of using `ServiceRequest` + `Task` for referral workflows, with `supportingInfo` playing a key role in conveying referral context.

## Analysis

This ticket was filed over six years ago (May 2019) as a lightweight tracker with no substantive description or proposed design. The BSeR IG has evolved significantly since then — multiple ballot cycles have occurred, and subsequent tickets (e.g., FHIR-42223, FHIR-42156) have addressed ServiceRequest profiling concerns in more specific and actionable ways.

The core question — whether to profile `ServiceRequest.supportingInfo` with slices to distinguish different categories of supporting information — is a valid design concern. However:

1. **The ticket lacks specificity.** There is no proposed set of slices, no discriminator strategy, and no use-case justification beyond "following this topic."
2. **The BSeR IG has matured.** The current BSeR IG already defines how supporting information is bundled and referenced. Newer tickets address specific slice-related concerns (e.g., FHIR-42223 argues for *removing* over-constrained slices on `ServiceRequest.identifier`).
3. **No community engagement.** There are no comments, no cross-references, and no Zulip threads specifically discussing this ticket.
