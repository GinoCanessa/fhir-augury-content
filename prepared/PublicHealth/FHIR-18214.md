## Description

The submitter identifies two inconsistencies across observation profiles (citing Body Height vs. Blood Pressure as examples):

1. **Observation.status** — Some profiles (e.g., Body Height) restrict `Observation.status` to a fixed value of "Complete" while others (e.g., Blood Pressure) do not. The submitter asks why and suggests they should be consistent.

2. **Observation.code** — Some profiles define `Observation.code` as a CodeableConcept (e.g., Blood Pressure) while others do not (e.g., Body Height). Again, the submitter requests consistency.

## Analysis

### Context
This ticket was filed in September 2018 and has remained in "Triaged" status with no resolution for over six years. The BSeR IG's observation profiles inherited or referenced FHIR Core vital signs profiles, which have historically varied in how tightly they constrain `Observation.status` and how they structure `Observation.code`. The submitter's core question — "why are these inconsistent?" — is a design concern rather than a defect.

### Landscape Since Filing
Since 2018, the broader FHIR community has engaged extensively with the vital signs profile design, particularly around:
- **Status constraints**: FHIR-45120 (AU Core) successfully argued that fixing `Observation.status` to "final" is a safety concern because it excludes amended/corrected observations. This was resolved as Persuasive with Modification.
- **Code semantics**: FHIR-43538, FHIR-45617, and FHIR-49953 have all grappled with how `Observation.code` should work across vital signs profiles. The community is actively evolving this design area in FHIR R6.
- **Same reporter**: Craig Newman filed FHIR-29415 with essentially the same `.status` question for PACIO Cognitive Status, which was resolved as Persuasive.

### Key Considerations
- The inconsistencies the submitter identified are real and have been validated by subsequent community discussion.
- The vital signs profile design has significantly evolved since 2018; many of these inconsistencies are being addressed through other tickets in FHIR Core and US Core.
- The BSeR specification should align with whatever the FHIR Core vital signs profiles decide, rather than independently constraining these elements.
- The ticket is six years old with no comments, no assignee, and no activity since February 2019.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive — Direct BSeR to align observation profiles with FHIR Core vital signs
**Action**: Accept the request and update BSeR observation profiles to be consistent with each other and with FHIR Core vital signs profile constraints for `Observation.status` and `Observation.code`. Specifically, do not fix `Observation.status` to a single value unless there is a clear BSeR-specific reason, and ensure `Observation.code` usage follows the same pattern across all BSeR observation profiles.
**Rationale**: The inconsistencies are real, and subsequent community resolutions (FHIR-45120, FHIR-29415) confirm that fixing status to a single value is problematic. BSeR profiles should be internally consistent and aligned with base FHIR Core profiles.

### Disposition B: Not Persuasive — The inconsistencies reflect intentional profile-specific design
**Action**: Reject the request, noting that each observation profile may intentionally constrain elements differently based on the clinical context of the measurement being profiled.
**Rationale**: Profile-level constraints may reflect legitimate differences (e.g., Body Height is typically only reported as final, while Blood Pressure may go through preliminary/amended states). The submitter has not demonstrated that the inconsistency causes implementation harm.

### Disposition C: Considered — Defer to FHIR Core vital signs profile evolution
**Action**: Close the ticket as Considered, noting that the underlying design questions are being actively addressed in FHIR Core (FHIR-45617, FHIR-49953) and that BSeR should inherit the updated constraints once FHIR Core resolves them.
**Rationale**: The concerns are valid but are better addressed at the FHIR Core level. BSeR profiles derive from the FHIR Core vital signs profiles, and the community is actively resolving how `Observation.status` and `Observation.code` should be constrained. Resolving this independently in BSeR risks divergence.

## Recommendation

**Disposition C: Considered — Defer to FHIR Core vital signs profile evolution**

This is the most pragmatic path. The submitter's concerns are legitimate and have been validated by years of subsequent community discussion. However, the root cause is in the FHIR Core vital signs profile design, not in BSeR specifically. FHIR-45120, FHIR-45617, FHIR-49953, and related tickets are actively resolving these design questions at the appropriate level. BSeR should track those outcomes and update its profiles to align with the resolved FHIR Core approach rather than making independent changes that may conflict with upcoming FHIR Core decisions. The workgroup should note the related tickets and plan a follow-up alignment once FHIR Core vital signs stabilize for R6.
