## Summary

Lloyd McKenzie requests that standardized codes be defined for `Task.input.type` and `Task.output.type` so that senders and receivers can interoperate when exchanging referral information via BSeR. The ticket asserts that `Task.input` should be the mechanism to point to 'requester package' content, and `Task.output` should point to the response package content. Without standard codes, implementers cannot reliably identify the purpose or nature of Task inputs and outputs, undermining interoperability.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive (Accept)

**Action:** Define a standard CodeSystem (e.g., `http://hl7.org/fhir/us/bser/CodeSystem/bser-task-input-output-type`) with codes for the various requester package and response package content types used in BSeR Task exchanges. Bind this CodeSystem to `Task.input.type` and `Task.output.type` via a required or extensible ValueSet in the BSeR IG.

**Rationale:** Without standardized codes, senders and receivers cannot programmatically identify the purpose of each Task input/output entry. This is fundamental to interoperability in the referral workflow. The BSeR IG already defines distinct requester and response package bundles; assigning codes to each package type is a logical and necessary step. FHIR-42523 (Persuasive) already established that Task is the proper vehicle for referral status; this ticket extends that principle to referral content exchange.

**Impact:** Requires creating a new CodeSystem and ValueSet in BSeR IG, updating Task profiles to bind `input.type` and `output.type`, and updating examples. May affect existing implementers who use ad-hoc or extension-based identification of input/output types.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification

**Action:** Rather than a BSeR-specific CodeSystem, propose standardized codes at the FHIR core or US Core level for common Task input/output types, supplemented by BSeR-specific codes where needed. Use an extensible binding so other IGs can reuse the same pattern.

**Rationale:** The need for standard Task input/output codes is not unique to BSeR — other referral and care coordination IGs face the same challenge. Defining codes at a broader level would promote reuse and consistency across IGs. However, this approach requires cross-workgroup coordination and may delay resolution.

**Impact:** Broader scope than Disposition A. Requires coordination with Patient Care, Orders & Observations, and potentially other workgroups. Longer timeline but greater cross-IG interoperability benefit.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive (Decline)

**Action:** No change. Leave `Task.input.type` and `Task.output.type` without BSeR-specific standardized codes.

**Rationale:** Implementers can use local or extension-based coding to identify input/output types. The BSeR IG documentation and structural profiling (e.g., fixed slicing on input/output) may be sufficient to distinguish content without additional codes.

**Risk:** This maintains the current interoperability gap. Different implementations may use incompatible approaches to identify Task inputs and outputs, leading to integration failures. Given that the reporter (Lloyd McKenzie) and commenter (minigrrl) both agree on the need, declining would go against stated community consensus.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Persuasive** is recommended. The request is straightforward, well-scoped, and addresses a clear interoperability gap. The reporter and commenter agree on the specific elements needing codes (`Task.input.type` and `Task.output.type`). Related ticket FHIR-42523 was already resolved as Persuasive, establishing the precedent that Task is the correct resource for managing referral workflows in BSeR. Defining standardized codes for input/output types is a natural and necessary complement to that decision. The BSeR IG already distinguishes requester and response packages structurally; codifying that distinction in `Task.input.type` / `Task.output.type` is the minimum needed for machine-processable interoperability.
