## Summary

The reporter (Nick Radov) identified that the `Contract.term.offer.topic` Requirements section contains extraneous text that appears to have been accidentally copied from a structure definition. Specifically, the text "Answer: The Contract.topic, which may be described with more detail in the [0..*] Contract.valuedItem @ [0..1] Contract.valuedItem.entity[x] Definition: Specific type of Contract Valued Item that may be priced. CodeableConcept|Reference(Any) Contract.topic" was likely intended to be a hyperlink reference to the `Contract.valuedItem` element, but instead the full structure definition metadata (cardinality, type information, etc.) was pasted in as raw text.

A 2021 comment from pknapp notes this was "being reviewed in light of recent changes," but the ticket remains unresolved and triaged as of 2025-02-24.

## Details

- **What is affected:** The Requirements text on the `Contract.term.offer.topic` element in the FHIR specification.
- **Nature of the issue:** A block of structure definition metadata (cardinalities `[0..*]`, `[0..1]`, type information `CodeableConcept|Reference(Any)`, and element definitions) was inadvertently included as plain text in the Requirements narrative. This was likely meant to be a hyperlink or short cross-reference to `Contract.valuedItem`.
- **Impact:** The garbled text is confusing for implementers reading the specification. It does not affect machine-readable content (the StructureDefinition itself), but degrades the readability and quality of the human-facing documentation.
- **Age:** Filed over 6 years ago (January 2019). Last substantive comment in March 2021.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive (Accept — Remove Extraneous Text)

**Resolution:** Remove the erroneously copied structure definition text from the `Contract.term.offer.topic` Requirements section and replace it with a proper cross-reference link to `Contract.valuedItem`.

**Rationale:** The reporter correctly identified that raw structure definition metadata was accidentally pasted into the Requirements narrative. This is a clear editorial/documentation error — the intended content was a reference link, not the full element definition. The sister tickets FHIR-20156 and FHIR-20161 (both filed by the same reporter on the same day with similar editorial fixes) were both resolved as Persuasive. The fix is low-risk, improves specification readability, and does not change any normative or computable content.

**Change required:** In the `Contract.term.offer.topic` Requirements text, replace the raw structure definition text block with a proper hyperlink reference to `Contract.valuedItem` (e.g., "Answer: The Contract.topic, which may be described with more detail in Contract.valuedItem").

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification (Accept — Clean Up Requirements Section More Broadly)

**Resolution:** Remove the extraneous text as in Disposition A, and additionally review all `Contract.term.offer.*` element Requirements/Comments for similar copy-paste errors, applying corrections as needed.

**Rationale:** Given that FHIR-20156 identified a similar editorial issue on `Contract.topic` (comments mixed with requirements) and the 2021 comment from pknapp noted this was being "reviewed in light of recent changes," it may be worthwhile to do a broader editorial pass on the Contract resource's Requirements and Comments text. This would catch any remaining similar issues and avoid future tickets of this nature. However, this expands scope beyond what the reporter requested.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive (Decline)

**Resolution:** Close the ticket without changes.

**Rationale:** While the text is admittedly confusing, the Requirements section does contain the correct substantive information about the relationship between `Contract.term.offer.topic` and `Contract.valuedItem`. The extraneous metadata, while ugly, is not technically incorrect — it accurately describes the `Contract.valuedItem` element's cardinality and type. Given the ticket has been open for over 6 years and no implementer has reported being blocked by this, the priority may be too low to warrant action at this time. However, this disposition is difficult to justify given the clearly accidental nature of the error and the ease of the fix.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Persuasive** — This is a straightforward editorial fix for an obvious copy-paste error. The companion tickets (FHIR-20156, FHIR-20161) from the same reporter and date were both accepted as Persuasive, establishing a clear precedent. The fix is low-risk, requires minimal effort (removing extraneous text and inserting a proper link), and improves specification quality for implementers. The ticket's 6+ year age argues for resolution rather than continued deferral.
