## Summary

The reporter identified that the description text for `Contract.contentDerivative` is truncated mid-sentence. The current text reads:

> "and includes the signatures as *The Contract Resource may function"

The sentence appears to be cut off, leaving the definition incomplete. The reporter suspects the text was intended to reference `Contract.signer.signature` but is uncertain of the original intent. This is an editorial/documentation defect in the Contract resource specification.

A single comment from **pknapp** (2021-03-04) notes the issue is "Being reviewed in light of recent changes."

---

## Details

- The defect is in the narrative requirements text for the `Contract.contentDerivative` element, which describes a legally executed contract.
- The truncation makes it impossible for implementers to understand the full requirements for `contentDerivative` when it represents a legally binding document with signatures.
- The likely intended meaning is that a legally executed contract derivative includes the signatures (from `Contract.signer.signature`), and that the Contract Resource may function in some capacity related to that — but the exact wording is unknown.
- The ticket has been open since January 2019 and remains unresolved. The label `FM_R5_Contract` indicates it is tracked for R5 Contract resource work.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept — Fix the truncated text (Persuasive)

**Action:** Complete the truncated sentence in the `Contract.contentDerivative` definition for the legally-executed-contract usage. The corrected text should clarify that a legally executed contract derivative includes the signatures (via `Contract.signer.signature`) and describe how the Contract Resource functions as a record of that executed agreement.

**Rationale:** This is a clear editorial defect — a sentence was cut off mid-word, leaving the specification incomplete. The fix requires reconstructing the intended text from the surrounding context and the related `Contract.signer` definitions (as revised in FHIR-9567). The current text is unambiguously broken and confusing to implementers.

**Impact:** Low risk. This is a documentation/narrative fix only — no structural or data model changes required.

### Disposition B: Accept with broader review — Fix text and review all contentDerivative descriptions (Persuasive with Modification)

**Action:** Fix the truncated sentence as in Disposition A, and additionally review all `Contract.contentDerivative` value descriptions for consistency and completeness, particularly in light of changes made to `Contract.signer` (FHIR-9567) and the Signature datatype since the text was originally written.

**Rationale:** Given that the text has been broken since at least 2019 and that pknapp noted it was "being reviewed in light of recent changes" in 2021, a broader review of `contentDerivative` descriptions may uncover other inconsistencies introduced by structural changes to the Contract resource.

**Impact:** Moderate editorial effort but no structural changes. May improve overall quality of the Contract resource documentation.

### Disposition C: Decline — Not Persuasive

**Action:** Close without change.

**Rationale:** The truncated text could be considered a minor cosmetic issue. Implementers can infer the intended meaning from context, and the `Contract.contentDerivative` element is not widely implemented. However, this rationale is weak given that the text is objectively broken.

**Impact:** None, but leaves a known editorial defect in the specification.

---

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Accept — Fix the truncated text (Persuasive)**

This is an unambiguous editorial defect: a sentence is visibly truncated mid-word. The fix is low-risk and straightforward — complete the sentence to describe that a legally executed contract derivative includes signatures and clarify the Contract Resource's function in that context. The ticket has been open for over six years with no structural controversy, only a note that it was being reviewed. Fixing the broken text directly serves implementer clarity with minimal effort.
