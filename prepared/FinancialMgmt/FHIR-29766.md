## Description Summary

Peter Bomberg submitted this change request as part of a Health Canada (HC) review of FHIR resources used to support **licensing and inspections**. The request proposes changes to the **Invoice** resource to support regulated authorizations and non-clinical invoicing use cases (e.g., provider license fees). The specific changes are detailed in an attached Word document ("Invoice related wording changes.v1.docx"). The submitter noted that while leveraging invoices in R4B would be nice, it was not critical.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Not Persuasive (Affirm Current Resolution)

**Rationale:** The FM Working Group's scope covers healthcare-related financial information exchange. The Invoice resource is designed for billing of healthcare products and services. Extending it to cover regulatory licensing fees, inspection costs, and other non-clinical government administrative charges falls outside the defined scope of both HL7 and the FM WG. Other standards bodies or jurisdictional implementations are better suited to address these non-healthcare invoicing needs. The submitter was given multiple opportunities (2021, 2024, 2025) to attend FM calls and advocate for the change but did not successfully present the case.

**Proposed Wording:** No changes to the specification.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification

**Rationale:** While the full scope of regulated authorization invoicing is outside FM's purview, the Invoice resource description could be clarified to explicitly state that its intended use is limited to healthcare products and services. This would provide clearer guidance for implementers like Health Canada who may attempt to reuse the resource for non-clinical billing scenarios.

**Proposed Wording:** Add a clarifying note to the Invoice resource scope statement indicating it is designed for healthcare-related billing and is not intended for regulatory/administrative fee invoicing outside the healthcare delivery context.

### Disposition C: Deferred — Refer to a Future Ballot or Cross-WG Initiative

**Rationale:** The use case of invoicing for regulatory licensing and inspections (e.g., Health Canada provider licensing fees) represents a legitimate government health administration need. Rather than rejecting outright, the topic could be deferred to a future cross-workgroup initiative that addresses administrative/regulatory financial workflows, potentially involving the Public Health or Regulated Activities workgroups.

**Proposed Wording:** Defer for consideration in a future ballot cycle, with a recommendation to explore cross-WG collaboration on regulatory administrative billing use cases.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Not Persuasive** — Affirm the current resolution.

The FM WG has correctly identified that invoicing for non-clinical products and services (such as provider license fees and inspection costs) falls outside HL7's healthcare information exchange scope. The Invoice resource is purpose-built for healthcare billing. The submitter was given multiple opportunities over four years (2021–2025) to present the case at FM calls but did not successfully engage. The resolution description is clear and well-reasoned. No community discussion or cross-references indicate broader support for this change.
