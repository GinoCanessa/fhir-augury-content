## Summary

The submitter requests clarification of terminology used in the Contract Term Subtype Codes ValueSet. Specifically, the phrases "go to the very root of a contract" and "go to the root of a contract" are used without explanation. The submitter argues that these phrases are ambiguous and should either be clarified with explanatory text or replaced with self-explanatory terms. The submitter also asks what the distinction is between "going to the root" versus "going to the very root" of a contract, and whether these phrases mean that a falsehood would void the contract.

## Details

**Description:**

The terms "go to the very root of a contract" and "go to the root of a contract" are used here without any explanation of what going to the root means. Unless this is clarified in related text when the value set is viewed as part of the complete FHIR document the term should be explained or replaced with a self-explanatory term.

For example, does going to the root mean something that, if it is not true, voids the contact? What is the difference between going to the root and going to the *very* root?

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested
#### Proposal
Add clarifying definitions or explanatory text to the Contract Term Subtype Codes ValueSet entries that use the phrases "go to the root of a contract" and "go to the very root of a contract." The definitions should explain that these are legal concepts related to the severity of a breach: a term that "goes to the root" is one whose breach entitles the injured party to terminate the contract, while "goes to the very root" indicates the most fundamental terms whose breach renders the entire contract void. Update the display text or definition fields of the affected codes accordingly.
#### Justification
The phrases originate from common law contract terminology but are not self-evident to a healthcare informatics audience. FHIR ValueSets should be understandable without requiring specialized legal knowledge. Providing inline definitions improves interoperability by ensuring consistent interpretation across implementers. The change is low-risk and editorial in nature.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach
#### Proposal
Replace the ambiguous legal phrases with self-explanatory terminology. For example, rename or redefine the codes to use terms such as "fundamental term" (breach voids the contract entirely), "material term" (breach entitles termination), and "minor term" (breach entitles damages only). Retain the original legal terms as synonyms or alternate designations for traceability.
#### Justification
Rather than merely adding definitions, replacing the phrases with clearer terminology addresses the root cause of the confusion. Self-explanatory code descriptions reduce the learning curve for implementers unfamiliar with common law contract concepts. Retaining the original terms as synonyms preserves backward compatibility and allows legal specialists to map the codes to their established terminology.

---

### Disposition C: Decline
#### Proposal
Decline the change request on the basis that the terms "go to the root" and "go to the very root" of a contract are established common law legal concepts with well-understood meanings in their domain of use. No changes to the ValueSet are needed.
#### Justification
The Contract resource and its associated ValueSets are designed for use by those with domain expertise in contracts and legal agreements. The terms in question are standard legal terminology derived from common law jurisprudence (e.g., the distinction between conditions and warranties). Implementers working with Contract resources are expected to have or consult with domain expertise. Adding layperson definitions to legal terms risks oversimplification or inaccuracy.

---

### Recommendation
**Recommended disposition:** A

The request to clarify terminology is reasonable and low-effort. The phrases "go to the root" and "go to the very root" of a contract, while familiar in legal contexts, are opaque to the healthcare informatics audience that forms the primary FHIR user base. Adding concise definitions to the ValueSet code entries is a small editorial change that significantly improves clarity without altering semantics or breaking compatibility. This ticket has been open since 2017, and accepting it addresses a longstanding gap in documentation quality. Disposition A is preferred over B because it is less disruptive — it preserves existing code names and display values while adding explanatory text, whereas Disposition B would require renaming codes and potentially breaking existing implementations.
