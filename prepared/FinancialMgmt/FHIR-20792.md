# FHIR-20792: Contract has duplicated other resources codesystem and valueset

## Summary

**Ticket:** [FHIR-20792](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-20792)
**Status:** Triaged | **Priority:** Medium | **Type:** Change Request
**Specification:** FHIR Core (FHIR)
**Work Group:** Financial Mgmt
**Reporter:** John Moehrke | **Assignee:** Unassigned
**Labels:** FM_R5_Contract
**Created:** 2019-04-10 | **Updated:** 2025-02-24

The Contract resource folder contains duplicate copies of CodeSystems and ValueSets that are originally owned by other resources (Provenance, Consent). This causes build-order-dependent resolution and misleading references showing CodeSystems used by two identical ValueSets. The reporter identified the following duplicates in the Contract folder:

- `codesystem-provenance-agent-type`
- `codesystem-provenance-agent-role`
- `valueset-provenance-agent-role`
- `valueset-provenance-agent-type`
- `valueset-consent-content-class`
- `valueset-consent-content-code`

As of the most recent comment (2021-12-08), the provenance-related duplicates had been addressed, leaving only `valueset-consent-content-class` and `valueset-consent-content-code` as remaining duplicates in the Contract folder.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive — Remove remaining duplicate ValueSets from Contract folder

**Action:** Remove `valueset-consent-content-class` and `valueset-consent-content-code` from the Contract resource folder, ensuring the authoritative definitions in the Consent resource folder are the sole source. Verify no Contract bindings reference these ValueSets by a Contract-specific path.

**Rationale:** The principle that ValueSet and CodeSystem definitions should exist in only one place is well-established. The provenance-related duplicates were already removed, and the same cleanup should be completed for the remaining consent ValueSets. FHIR-24909 was resolved specifically noting this ticket as the remaining work. FHIR-48780 (Applied/Persuasive) has already promoted these consent ValueSets to active/non-experimental in their authoritative location, confirming they are properly maintained elsewhere.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification — Remove duplicates and audit for additional duplicates

**Action:** Same as Disposition A (remove `valueset-consent-content-class` and `valueset-consent-content-code` from Contract), but additionally perform a comprehensive audit of the Contract folder for any other remaining duplicated terminology resources. The original ticket noted "there may be others that I am not an authority on."

**Rationale:** The original report acknowledged that the list of duplicates might be incomplete. A broader audit would prevent this class of issue from recurring and align with the broader effort to eliminate terminology duplication (as seen in FHIR-47270 for the Extensions Pack). This provides a more thorough resolution given the ticket has been open since 2019.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive — Close with no action

**Action:** Close the ticket with no further changes. Note that the critical provenance duplicates have already been addressed and the two remaining consent ValueSets may be harmless if not actively referenced by Contract.

**Rationale:** The provenance-related duplicates were the most impactful issue and have been resolved. If the remaining consent ValueSet files in the Contract folder are not referenced by any Contract bindings, they may be inert artifacts that don't affect the build. However, this does leave dead files that could confuse future maintainers.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Persuasive — Remove remaining duplicate ValueSets from Contract folder.**

This ticket has been open since 2019 and represents clearly identified cleanup work. The sibling ticket FHIR-24909 was resolved in 2021 specifically pointing to this ticket as the remaining action item. Comment history confirms only two ValueSets remain (`valueset-consent-content-class` and `valueset-consent-content-code`), and the authoritative Consent copies are already being promoted to active status via FHIR-48780. Removing the duplicates is a straightforward, low-risk change that follows the established FHIR principle of single-source terminology definitions and completes work that is largely already done.
