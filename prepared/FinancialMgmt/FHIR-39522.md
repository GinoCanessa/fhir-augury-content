## Summary

This ticket requests that the Da Vinci ATR (Member Attribution List) Coverage profile (`atr-coverage`) be reconciled with the Da Vinci HREx (Health Record Exchange) Coverage profile (`hrex-coverage`). Currently, `atr-coverage` does not derive from `hrex-coverage`, leading to profile divergence within the Da Vinci ecosystem. The ticket was originally filed post-ballot in November 2022 and was previously resolved as "Considered for Future Use" (voted 10-0-0 by Nagesh Bashyam/Mark Scrimshire on 2023-10-03) with the rationale that it would be considered based on HREx updates and analysis. It was subsequently re-opened in January 2024 post-STU2 publication for further consideration.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive (Accept and Implement Reconciliation)

**Action:** Accept the request. Update the `atr-coverage` profile in the Da Vinci ATR IG to derive from `hrex-coverage` as its base profile. Ensure all ATR-specific constraints are layered on top of the HREx base, and verify compatibility with US Core Coverage when ATR updates its US Core dependency.

**Rationale:** Profile harmonization within the Da Vinci ecosystem has been a longstanding goal (see FHIR-25631, FHIR-39519 item 6). Having `atr-coverage` derive from `hrex-coverage` reduces profile proliferation, ensures consistent coverage data exchange across Da Vinci IGs, and aligns with the FM WG's own prior feedback. The previous 10-0-0 vote acknowledged the value of this work pending HREx readiness. With STU2 published and the ticket re-opened, the time is appropriate to act.

**Impact:** Requires analysis of structural differences between `atr-coverage` and `hrex-coverage` to ensure no breaking changes for existing ATR implementers. May require coordination with the HREx project team to confirm the base profile can accommodate ATR needs.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification (Partial Reconciliation with Phased Approach)

**Action:** Accept in principle but implement reconciliation in phases. First, perform a gap analysis between `atr-coverage` and `hrex-coverage` to identify constraints that conflict. In the next ballot cycle, align overlapping constraints and document a migration path. Defer full derivation until both profiles have stabilized further.

**Rationale:** Immediate full reconciliation may introduce breaking changes for implementers who have already adopted `atr-coverage`. A phased approach allows the community to identify and resolve conflicts between the two profiles without disrupting existing implementations. This mirrors the cautious approach taken in the original 2023 resolution.

**Impact:** Lower immediate implementation burden, but extends the timeline for full harmonization. Requires a documented plan and continued tracking across ballot cycles.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive (Decline)

**Action:** Decline the request. Maintain `atr-coverage` as an independent profile that does not derive from `hrex-coverage`.

**Rationale:** The two profiles may serve sufficiently different use cases that forced derivation could over-constrain one or both. ATR has already obtained a US Core variance for its Coverage profile (FHIR-43113), and implementers have built around the current independent profile. Reconciliation could introduce unnecessary complexity and breaking changes without clear implementer demand beyond the original submitter.

**Impact:** No change to current specifications. Profile divergence within Da Vinci continues, potentially increasing implementer burden for systems that must support both ATR and HREx.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Persuasive** — The reconciliation should be accepted and implemented. Profile harmonization within Da Vinci has been a consistent theme across multiple related tickets (FHIR-25631, FHIR-39519), and the FM WG itself previously recommended considering HREx Coverage for ATR (FHIR-39519, item 6). The prior 10-0-0 vote in favor of future consideration, combined with the explicit re-opening post-STU2, signals strong workgroup alignment. Having `atr-coverage` derive from `hrex-coverage` will reduce profile proliferation, improve cross-IG interoperability, and simplify the path toward US Core alignment when ATR updates its base dependency. A thorough gap analysis should accompany the change to protect existing implementers.
