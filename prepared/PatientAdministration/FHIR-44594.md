## Summary

This ticket requests the creation of a new Conformance tab within the Interoperable Digital Identity and Patient Matching Implementation Guide (IG). The proposed tab would clearly define what it means to be "in conformance" with the IG and would compartmentalize conformance requirements by implementer use case, such as business-to-business (B2B), business-to-consumer (B2C), and identity provider (IdP) scenarios. The goal is to help implementers identify the specific technical requirements relevant to their context.

## Details

**Description:**

We should create a Conformance tab to inform the reader of the IG how to be considered "in conformance" of this IG. This will help implementers determine the specific technical requirements that they should focus on including in their work. We should also compartment the requirements based on the use case of the implementer (B2B, B2C, IdP, etc.).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested
#### Proposal
Create a new Conformance tab/page in the Interoperable Digital Identity and Patient Matching IG that defines conformance criteria organized by implementer use case (B2B, B2C, IdP, and others as identified). The tab should enumerate the specific technical requirements — including required profiles, operations, security expectations, and identity verification levels — that each category of implementer must satisfy to be considered conformant.
#### Justification
A dedicated Conformance tab is a well-established pattern in FHIR IGs (e.g., US Core's "General Requirements" page) and directly improves implementer experience. Compartmentalizing requirements by use case reduces ambiguity, ensures implementers only focus on what is relevant to their role, and strengthens the IG's testability and certification readiness. This is consistent with the IG's resolution of "Considered for Future Use," indicating the work group sees merit in the request.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach
#### Proposal
Rather than adding a standalone Conformance tab, enhance the existing IG pages by adding conformance statements inline with each capability or profile, using CapabilityStatements and conformance verbs (SHALL, SHOULD, MAY) per use-case role. Add a summary matrix or table to the IG's home page or an appendix that maps each requirement to the B2B, B2C, and IdP roles.
#### Justification
An inline approach with a summary matrix avoids creating a separate page that could diverge from the detailed specification content over time. It keeps conformance language co-located with the relevant technical detail, reducing the maintenance burden of keeping two locations synchronized. A summary matrix still provides the at-a-glance use-case-based view that implementers need.

---

### Disposition C: Decline
#### Proposal
Decline the request as out of scope for the current ballot cycle; conformance requirements are already implicitly communicated through the existing profiles, CapabilityStatements, and narrative in the IG.
#### Justification
The IG already contains profiles and conformance expectations within its resource definitions. Adding a separate Conformance tab introduces ongoing maintenance overhead and risk of contradicting normative content elsewhere in the IG. The resolution "Considered for Future Use" suggests the work group may wish to defer this to a later release when the IG's scope and implementer base are more mature, allowing conformance requirements to be informed by real-world implementation experience.

---

### Recommendation
**Recommended disposition:** A

A dedicated Conformance tab directly addresses a genuine implementer need: clarity on what constitutes conformance and which requirements apply to which use-case role. The Interoperable Digital Identity and Patient Matching IG spans multiple actor types (B2B systems, B2C applications, identity providers), and without explicit conformance guidance organized by role, implementers risk either over-implementing or missing critical requirements. The pattern of a dedicated conformance page is well-proven across other HL7 IGs and increases the specification's usability for certification and testing programs. While the ticket's current resolution of "Considered for Future Use" indicates deferral may be appropriate for timing reasons, the request itself is sound and should be accepted for inclusion in the next IG development cycle.
