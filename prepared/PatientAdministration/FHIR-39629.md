## Description

The submitter raises a concern about patient matching when a person's identity is verified after they move, but only a former address is on file in the system from which a match is requested. The ticket calls for the IG to clarify two points:

1. **Identity verification should capture all home addresses** during the time frame a record search may cover — accounting for cases where a patient stopped receiving care while at a former address, so the health system only has the old address on file.
2. **Multiple verified addresses** (and other attributes that may change over time, such as name, telephone number, and email address) should be included in the match request if they were verified at some point in time.

The submitter recommends a short addition in both the identity verification section and the match section of the IG.

## Resolution Description

The approved resolution adds two changes:

1. **New sentence in the identity verification section** (fourth line break after the first sentence):
   > "Historical demographics, such as a former address, are held to the same verification requirements and guidance as current demographics."

2. **Revised weighting table rule** to recognize the value of multiple street addresses:
   > "max weight of five for inclusion of two or more different attributes from this row OR more than one street address"

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive (Accept as Proposed)

**Action:** Accept the resolution as written — add the sentence about historical demographics being held to the same verification requirements, and update the weighting table to give credit for more than one street address.

**Rationale:** The resolution directly addresses the submitter's concern with minimal, targeted text changes. The new sentence makes explicit what was previously implicit: historical demographics (former addresses, names, phone numbers) must meet the same verification standards as current demographics. The weighting table revision appropriately rewards match requests that include multiple verified street addresses, improving match quality for patients who have moved. The workgroup discussed this over multiple meetings and the resolution reflects consensus from the comment trail.

**Risk:** Low. The changes are additive clarifications and do not alter existing normative requirements.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification

**Action:** Accept the resolution but expand the scope slightly:
- Add the historical demographics sentence as proposed.
- Revise the weighting table rule as proposed.
- Additionally, add a brief worked example or note in Section 4.5 showing how a second verified address contributes to the match input weight (per the April 2024 comment from Julie Maas suggesting this for STU2).

**Rationale:** The commenter's April 2024 suggestion to demonstrate the effect of a second address in the weighting rubric and example patient would make the guidance more implementable. A concrete example would help implementers understand how to apply the revised weighting rule in practice. This aligns with the spirit of the original request and the workgroup's direction for STU2.

**Risk:** Moderate. Requires additional editorial work to craft an example; could delay publication if not already drafted.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive

**Action:** Reject the change on the grounds that the existing IG text and general verification principles already cover historical demographics implicitly.

**Rationale:** One could argue that verification requirements naturally extend to all demographics — historical or current — and that the existing text does not need an explicit call-out. The weighting table change may be premature without broader industry validation of how multiple addresses affect match rates.

**Risk:** Leaves an implicit expectation that implementers may overlook, particularly in TEFCA queries where historical records are common. The comment from JIRAUSER24103 ("Historical demographics are held to the same requirements") suggests this is already the intended interpretation but is not clearly stated.

---

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Persuasive (Accept as Proposed)**

The resolution is well-scoped, directly addresses the gap identified by the submitter, and reflects workgroup consensus from multiple discussions. The two changes — making the historical demographics requirement explicit and updating the weighting table for multiple street addresses — are clear, minimal, and low-risk. The additional example patient work suggested in Disposition B, while valuable, can be tracked as a separate enhancement for STU2 rather than blocking this change.
