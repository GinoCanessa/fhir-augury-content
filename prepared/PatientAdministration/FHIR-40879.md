## Description

The submitter reports that the RTLS IG specification page ([http://hl7.org/fhir/uv/rtls/2023May/spec.html](http://hl7.org/fhir/uv/rtls/2023May/spec.html)) lacks sufficient detail about transactions and interactions, making the IG difficult to implement. Specific suggestions include:

1. **Sequence diagrams** showing actors and interactions.
2. **Resource mapping** — indication of which FHIR resources are/may be used in each interaction.
3. **Narrative examples** connected to the transactions (e.g., "Patient John was admitted in Hospital Good Life. He received a tag… When he moved to the medication room, the RTLS sends a notification to the EMR…").

## Resolution Description

> This would be useful, but since we are focusing on Staff Duress initially, we want to defer this until after the 2.0 ballot.

The resolution acknowledges the value of the request but defers it as **Considered for Future Use**, citing the current priority focus on the Staff Duress use case and the 2.0 ballot timeline.

## Analysis

### Context
This ticket was filed during the STU1 ballot cycle (May 2023) for the RTLS IG. The IG defines interactions for tag enrollment, unenrollment, and location update notifications in healthcare settings, but the specification page at the time lacked the level of detail implementers expected — particularly around interaction flows, actor roles, and worked examples.

### Pattern of Feedback
FHIR-40879 is part of a cluster of STU1 ballot comments requesting better implementation guidance for the RTLS IG. FHIR-41167 questions the fundamental interaction pattern (messaging vs. RESTful), FHIR-40965 asks for more profiles and examples, and FHIR-52436 (submitted later) requests formal MessageDefinitions and CapabilityStatements. Together, these suggest a consistent implementer concern: the IG's transactions are under-specified.

### Current State
The resolution ("Considered for Future Use") indicates the WG acknowledged the value but prioritized the Staff Duress use case for the 2.0 ballot. Several newer tickets (FHIR-52433, FHIR-52436) indicate that the IG is evolving and some of this guidance may be addressed through resource narrative updates and formalized definitions.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Affirm — Considered for Future Use
**Rationale:** The WG's decision to defer this enhancement is reasonable given the strategic focus on Staff Duress for the 2.0 ballot. The request is acknowledged as valuable but is not blocking. Newer tickets (FHIR-52433, FHIR-52436) are addressing parts of this concern through formalized message definitions and narrative updates. Deferring a comprehensive sequence-diagram and examples overhaul to a future cycle allows the WG to incorporate lessons learned from the 2.0 ballot.

### Disposition B: Change to Persuasive with Modification — Add Minimal Interaction Overview
**Rationale:** Rather than a full deferral, the WG could add a lightweight interaction overview (e.g., a single summary table mapping each transaction to its actors, trigger, and primary FHIR resources) without committing to full sequence diagrams. This would address the core implementability concern at low cost and could be done alongside the 2.0 ballot work.

### Disposition C: Change to Persuasive — Full Implementation Guidance
**Rationale:** The consistent feedback across multiple ballot comments (FHIR-40879, FHIR-41167, FHIR-40965) demonstrates a significant implementability gap. Sequence diagrams, actor definitions, and worked examples are standard IG content. Prioritizing this would strengthen the IG for the 2.0 ballot and reduce future comment volume on the same topic.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Affirm — Considered for Future Use.** The deferral is well-reasoned. The WG has correctly prioritized Staff Duress for 2.0, and newer tickets (FHIR-52433, FHIR-52436) are incrementally addressing the underlying concerns about interaction formalization. A comprehensive transaction documentation effort is best undertaken after the 2.0 ballot when the IG's scope and interaction patterns have stabilized.
