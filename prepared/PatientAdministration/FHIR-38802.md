# FHIR-38802: Split out the concepts of patient and user

> **Prepared for:** Patient Administration Work Group Review
> **Ticket:** [FHIR-38802](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-38802)
> **Specification:** Interoperable Digital Identity and Patient Matching (FHIR)

> **Resolution:** Persuasive
> **Reporter:** Cooper Thompson | **Assignee:** Unassigned
> **Labels:** block-vote

## 1. Summary

The submitter (Cooper Thompson) argues that the Interoperable Digital Identity and Patient Matching IG blurs the line between "patient" (the subject of a data query) and "user" (the person initiating the request). This distinction matters for pediatric use cases, adult caregivers/proxies, and authorized representatives who interact with data on behalf of patients who may be unable to authenticate themselves (e.g., a two-month-old infant cannot perform identity proofing at IAL-n).

The ticket requests that the IG:
1. Clearly separate "user" (authenticated individual with OpenID tokens, capable of IAL verification) from "patient" (the data subject, who may not be the same person as the user).
2. Cover both **User matching** (B2C / patient-mediated exchange) and **Patient matching** (B2B).
3. Consider adding a RelatedPerson profile for weighted matching.
4. Clarify where IAL-n references apply to the user rather than the patient.

The existing resolution text proposes concrete IG changes: defining Patient and User as distinct actors, introducing the "Authorized Representative" concept, and adding guidance for B2B and B2C proxy-user scenarios.

## 2. Resolution Detail

**Resolution:** Persuasive (voted 2024-06-20)

The resolution text proposes the following changes:

> Two primary actors are defined:
> - **Patient** – the subject of the data query in a `$match` request (or equivalent)
> - **User** – the individual authenticated to initiate a `$match` request (or equivalent)
>
> The Patient and User may not be the same individual. An **Authorized Representative** is an individual authorized to access another individual's health data. Examples include:
> - **B2B Use Cases** – A physician accessing a patient's health data through an HIE/QHIN query via their EMR
> - **B2C Proxy User Use Case** – An authorized representative accessing a patient's health record (e.g., a parent accessing their child's records)
>
> An authorized representative's identity SHALL be verified according to guidance and sufficient demographics SHALL be collected if matching on the representative's identity is performed, at IAL 1.8 or higher if the match will authorize data access. Authorization determination is outside the scope of this IG.

The IG will also reference the HL7 SMART App Launch IG for the B2C workflow with credentials at the responding organization.

## 5. Zulip Discussions

### [argonaut > CLEAR Identities](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/argonaut/topic/CLEAR%20Identities) (Jul 2025)

Cooper Thompson (the ticket reporter) is actively advocating for the same patient/user distinction in the context of CLEAR identities. Key points from the thread:

- **Cooper Thompson** strongly argues that CLEAR is about *user/person identity*, not *patient identity*, and that linking an identity to a patient chart is a separate step.
- Caregiver/proxy access should be the target design because patient-self access is just a special case — designing only for self-access won't expand to caregivers.
- A CLEAR identity alone cannot determine RelatedPerson — there is always an additional step to link identity to patient charts.
- Not all patients will have CLEAR IDs (e.g., children, patients who don't use self-access).

This thread strongly reinforces the rationale behind FHIR-38802.

## 8. Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept as Persuasive (Implement Resolution as Written)

Accept the existing Persuasive resolution and implement the proposed changes:
- Add explicit Patient/User actor definitions throughout the IG
- Add Authorized Representative concept with B2B and B2C proxy scenarios
- Add identity verification requirements for authorized representatives (IAL 1.8+ for data access authorization)
- Reference SMART App Launch IG for B2C credential workflows

**Rationale:** The resolution text is detailed and actionable. It has been through a prior vote cycle and re-evaluation. The patient/user distinction is a foundational concern raised consistently across multiple related tickets (FHIR-37168, FHIR-37054, FHIR-40355, FHIR-45199) and remains actively discussed in the community (CLEAR Identities thread). The proposed changes align with TEFCA workflows and real-world implementation needs for pediatric and proxy use cases.

**Risk:** Low. The resolution text is well-defined and consistent with community consensus.

### Disposition B: Accept as Persuasive with Modification — Expand RelatedPerson Guidance

Accept the resolution but additionally require:
- A RelatedPerson profile for weighted matching (as originally suggested by the submitter)
- Explicit guidance on how RelatedPerson relates to the Authorized Representative concept
- Guidance on linking user identity to patient charts as a distinct step

**Rationale:** The original ticket and the 2024-07-11 comment both call for RelatedPerson and Authorized Representative integration into requirements. The CLEAR Identities Zulip thread (Jul 2025) demonstrates ongoing confusion about how identity links to RelatedPerson. Adding this guidance would address the full scope of the original request rather than just the actor naming portion.

**Risk:** Medium. Expanding scope may delay implementation. RelatedPerson matching profiles may need coordination with other work groups or IGs.

### Disposition C: Defer Implementation — Request Detailed Use Case Specifications First

Acknowledge the ticket as persuasive in principle but defer implementation until TEFCA-related workflows and RelatedPerson/Authorized Representative requirements are fully documented on the Use Cases page (as committed to in the 2024-07-11 comment).

**Rationale:** The 2024-07-11 comment indicates work is planned to integrate TEFCA workflows and describe how RelatedPerson and Authorized Representatives fit into requirements. Implementing actor naming changes before that work may result in incomplete or inconsistent guidance that needs further revision.

**Risk:** Further delays a ticket that has already been deferred once (2023) and re-opened. The distinction is fundamental enough that it should not be deferred again.

## 9. Recommendation

**→ Disposition A: Accept as Persuasive (Implement Resolution as Written)**

The resolution text provides clear, actionable changes that address the core concern. This ticket has already been through one deferral cycle and re-opening; the community has consistently raised the patient/user distinction as a critical gap across at least four other related tickets and active Zulip discussions. The proposed actor definitions (Patient, User, Authorized Representative) with B2B/B2C scenario guidance are well-formulated and ready for implementation. The RelatedPerson expansion (Disposition B) can be pursued as a follow-up ticket once the foundational actor definitions are in place.
