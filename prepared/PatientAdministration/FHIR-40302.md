## Description

This ticket proposes updated introductory text for V2 of the Digital Identity and Patient Matching IG explaining why identity is important to the IG. The proposed text covers three key points:

1. **Authentication in Patient-Directed Exchange** — When the authenticated identity of a person is known with high confidence, it can be used in access decisions (e.g., Patient-Directed Exchange), replacing probabilistic matching with direct authentication as federated identity services grow.
2. **Digital credentials in B2B matching** — High-confidence digital credentials with globally unique identifiers can emulate perfect matching even in Business-to-Business scenarios where the individual is not present, making unique identifier matching preferable to demographic-based matching.
3. **Identity proofing improves match quality** — Completed identity proofing makes demographic attributes more meaningful and improves probabilistic match quality when probabilistic matching is still required.

## Resolution Details

**Resolution: Persuasive with Modification**

The approved resolution text includes the original proposed content with two specific modifications (shown in blue in the Jira comments):

1. **Added qualifier**: "as enumerated in this implementation guide" added after "known with high confidence" to scope the statement to methods described in the IG.
2. **Kept "Patient-Directed Exchange"**: The final resolution retains "Patient-Directed Exchange" rather than changing to "Person-Directed Exchange" as was explored in intermediate drafts. The word "patient" was changed to "individual" in the B2B paragraph ("where the **individual** is not authenticated").

The full resolved text:

> Identity is important to this IG because:
>
> When the identity of the person who is authenticated in a transaction is known with high confidence, **as enumerated in this implementation guide,** this information can be used in an access decision, e.g., **Patient**-Directed Exchange. This allows implementers to rely on user authentication in this workflow instead of probabilistic matching, which becomes increasingly convenient as federated identity services proliferate. This also reduces the number of credentials an individual needs to maintain.
>
> As a secondary effect, digital credentials with high confidence identity assurance and a globally unique identifier associated with the individual, even in Business to Business matching (where the **individual** is not authenticated as being present in the transaction), emulate the perfect matching described in the paragraph above. Attempting to match on such a unique identifier is a preferred best practice over matching with a combination of demographics.
>
> When identity proofing has been completed for an individual, the process of verifying that demographic attributes are consistent with a unique individual in the real world makes the attributes more meaningful in match requests and improves match quality when probabilistic matching must be used.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive with Modification (Accept the resolution as stated)

**Rationale:** The resolved text is well-crafted and addresses the original request to add explanatory identity text. The modifications from the original submission are appropriate: adding "as enumerated in this implementation guide" properly scopes the high-confidence identity claim, retaining "Patient-Directed Exchange" maintains consistency with established terminology (per the workgroup's prior decision), and changing "patient" to "individual" in the B2B paragraph correctly broadens scope where the person is not directly authenticated. The text clearly articulates three distinct value propositions for identity in the IG.

**Suggested vote text:** "Accept the resolved text as written, incorporating the phrase 'as enumerated in this implementation guide,' retaining 'Patient-Directed Exchange,' and using 'individual' in the B2B context."

### Disposition B: Not Persuasive

**Rationale:** As noted in the most recent comment by Julie Maas (2025-04-15), the workgroup previously resolved that changing "Patient-Directed" to "Person-Directed" was unnecessary and that existing use case description updates already clarify that authorized users are not excluded. If the workgroup considers the current IG text adequate and the proposed additions redundant with existing content or other resolved tickets (e.g., FHIR-40355 on IAL levels), this change may not be needed. The introductory nature of the text means it may add narrative without normative value.

**Suggested vote text:** "The existing IG text and prior resolutions (including FHIR-40355) adequately cover identity's importance; no additional introductory text is needed."

### Disposition C: Persuasive with Modification (Revise to use "Person-Directed Exchange")

**Rationale:** The intermediate drafts explored using "Person-Directed Exchange" instead of "Patient-Directed Exchange" to better encompass authorized representatives and non-patient individuals. If the workgroup has reconsidered the scope of the IG to include broader person-directed workflows, updating the terminology here would align with that direction. This would also address the concern raised in JIRAUSER24103's December 2023 comment about cautious use of the word "Patient."

**Suggested vote text:** "Accept the resolved text but replace 'Patient-Directed Exchange' with 'Person-Directed Exchange' throughout to align with broadened scope for authorized representatives."

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Persuasive with Modification (Accept the resolution as stated)**

The resolved text is substantive, well-scoped, and reflects deliberate workgroup decisions. The key modifications — scoping the confidence statement to methods in the IG, retaining "Patient-Directed Exchange" per prior workgroup consensus, and using "individual" in the B2B context — represent a balanced outcome. The workgroup explicitly decided against changing to "Person-Directed Exchange" (as confirmed by Julie Maas's April 2025 comment), making the resolution internally consistent with prior decisions. The text adds meaningful introductory context explaining why identity matters to the IG, covering authentication, digital credentials, and identity proofing — all core topics. Accepting this resolution as-is avoids reopening settled terminology debates while improving the IG's explanatory content.
