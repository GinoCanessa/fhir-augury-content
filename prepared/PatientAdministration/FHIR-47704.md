## Summary

This ticket requests the addition of an example to the Interoperable Digital Identity and Patient Matching IG showing how a Digital Identifier is represented within a SMART Health Card. The reporter (Aaron Nusstein) is asking for a concrete illustration of how the Digital Identifier concept defined in the IG would appear when encoded in a SMART Health Card credential.

The ticket was originally submitted during the STU2 ballot cycle. It was previously resolved as **"Considered for Future Use"** on 2025-06-17 with a unanimous vote (8-0-0), with the Identity chairs indicating it would be considered for a future version. However, it was **re-opened** on 2025-12-10 by lynn_laakso post STU2 publication for reconsideration.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept (Persuasive)

**Action:** Accept the request and create an example showing how a Digital Identifier is encoded within a SMART Health Card JWS payload.

**Rationale:** The Interoperable Digital Identity and Patient Matching IG defines the Digital Identifier concept but does not currently provide a concrete example of its use in a SMART Health Card. Adding such an example would improve implementer understanding and reduce ambiguity. SMART Health Cards are now an official HL7 IG (SMART Health Cards and Links IG v1.0.0), making this integration point more relevant. Julie Maas already provided a mock-up that could serve as a starting point. The ticket was re-opened post STU2 publication specifically for reconsideration, and the original deferral was procedural rather than substantive.

**Implementation:** Add a new example section (or subsection within an existing section) to the IG that includes:
- A sample FHIR Patient resource with a Digital Identifier in `Patient.identifier`
- The corresponding SMART Health Card JWS payload showing how that identifier is carried
- Brief narrative explaining the encoding

### Disposition B: Defer with Specific Target (Considered for Future Use)

**Action:** Defer to a future ballot cycle, but specify the target version and assign an owner to draft the example.

**Rationale:** While the example is valuable, the IG was just published as STU2 and adding new examples may require broader consensus on the exact representation format. The original 8-0-0 vote to defer was unanimous, suggesting the work group agreed it was not urgent for STU2. Deferring with a concrete plan ensures it does not get lost again while allowing more time to align with the now-official SMART Health Cards and Links IG.

**Implementation:** Re-resolve as "Considered for Future Use" with a note specifying the target version (e.g., STU3) and assigning an author to draft the example content prior to the next ballot cycle.

### Disposition C: Decline (Not Persuasive)

**Action:** Decline the request on the grounds that the SMART Health Card encoding is defined by the SMART Health Cards and Links IG, not the Identity Matching IG.

**Rationale:** The SMART Health Cards and Links IG (now an official HL7 publication) defines how FHIR resources are encoded in health cards. Adding a SMART Health Card example to the Identity Matching IG could create a maintenance burden if the SHC encoding evolves, and could be seen as duplicating guidance that belongs in the SHC IG. The Identity Matching IG's scope is defining the Digital Identifier itself, not every possible transport or credential format it might appear in.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Accept (Persuasive)** is recommended.

The ticket was re-opened post STU2 publication specifically for reconsideration, indicating ongoing interest from the Identity chairs. The request is well-scoped — a single concrete example — and addresses a real implementer need: understanding how the abstract Digital Identifier concept maps to a widely-adopted credential format. SMART Health Cards are now an official HL7 IG, making this a stable integration point rather than a moving target. Julie Maas's existing mock-up provides a head start on content. The original deferral was procedural ("considered for a future version"), and with STU2 now published, the next version cycle is the appropriate time to add this content. Accepting now ensures the example is developed and ready for the next ballot.
