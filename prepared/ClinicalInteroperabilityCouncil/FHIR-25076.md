## Description

The ballot comment identifies broken hyperlinks in the **"Where to Focus"** section of the US Breast Cancer Radiology Reporting IG. Specifically:

- The **"logical models"** link navigates to an empty page
- The **"reference model"** link returns a **404 error**

The submitter requests that these links be corrected to point to the actual logical models and reference model content within the IG.

## Resolution Statement

> "We agree, and will improve the links and description of the links in the next ballot."

The resolution is **Persuasive** — the submitter's concern was accepted in full. The project team committed to fixing the broken links and also improving the link descriptions in the next ballot cycle.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept as Persuasive (Confirm Existing Resolution)
Confirm the existing **Persuasive** resolution. The broken links in the "Where to Focus" section are a clear editorial defect. The project team has already agreed to fix both the null links and improve the surrounding link descriptions. Mark as ready to apply and verify that the links have been corrected in subsequent ballot versions.

- **Pros:** Straightforward technical correction; already agreed upon by the project team; aligns with multiple related tickets identifying the same class of issue.
- **Cons:** None significant. The fix may already have been applied given the age of the ticket — verification is needed.

### Disposition B: Upgrade to Persuasive with Modification — Coordinate with Related Tickets
Retain the Persuasive resolution but add a modification noting that this fix should be coordinated with FHIR-25075 (same section, overlapping link issues) and FHIR-25132 (missing logical models page). A single pass to audit and repair all links in the "Where to Focus" section would be more effective than addressing each ticket independently.

- **Pros:** Avoids piecemeal fixes; addresses the root cause (incomplete link infrastructure) holistically; reduces risk of regression.
- **Cons:** Slightly increases scope; may delay closure if dependent tickets aren't also ready.

### Disposition C: Mark as Not Persuasive with Modification — Request Verification
If the IG has been significantly restructured since the 2019 ballot (the spec has been updated through 2021), the specific links referenced may no longer exist in their original form. Change the resolution to Not Persuasive with Modification, noting that the page structure has changed and the original broken links are no longer applicable, but that a general link audit should be performed.

- **Pros:** Acknowledges that the IG may have evolved past the original issue.
- **Cons:** Dismisses a valid ballot comment; unclear whether the underlying issue was actually resolved.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Accept as Persuasive (Confirm Existing Resolution)**

This is the most appropriate disposition. The ballot comment identified a genuine defect — broken links that prevent readers from navigating to key content. The resolution statement clearly acknowledges the problem and commits to fixing it. The ticket is a straightforward technical correction with no controversy. The workgroup should confirm the Persuasive resolution and verify that the links were corrected in subsequent IG publications.
