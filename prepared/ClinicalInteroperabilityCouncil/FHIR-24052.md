## Description

The submitter (Mark Kramer) reports that the narrative text throughout the US Breast Cancer Radiology Reporting IG contains numerous mistakes and omissions requiring a thorough editorial pass. A specific example is cited: an incomplete sentence reading *"While nothing being done here is specifically forbidden within FHIR, we feel that taken together this strategy has."* — which trails off without completing the thought.

## Resolution Description

The resolution is **Persuasive**: *"We agree. The next ballot will be more thoroughly edited."*

The work group acknowledged the editorial quality issues and committed to a more thorough editorial review for the subsequent ballot cycle.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Apply as Persuasive — Verify Editorial Corrections Made
Accept the existing Persuasive resolution. Confirm that the editorial corrections committed to in the resolution ("the next ballot will be more thoroughly edited") have been applied in subsequent ballot versions. If corrections are verified, close the ticket as applied.

**Rationale:** The work group already agreed with the submitter. The resolution acknowledged the problem and promised action. If the IG has undergone subsequent ballots with improved narrative quality, this ticket should be marked complete.

### Disposition B: Apply as Persuasive with Modification — Request Specific Editorial Review Checklist
Accept the Persuasive resolution but add a modification requesting that the work group document specific editorial issues identified and provide a checklist or tracking mechanism to ensure all narrative deficiencies (incomplete sentences, grammatical errors, omissions) are systematically addressed before the next publication.

**Rationale:** The original resolution was vague ("more thoroughly edited"). A more structured approach would ensure completeness and prevent recurrence of editorial issues in future ballots.

### Disposition C: Defer Pending Current IG Status Review
Defer action until the current publication status of the US Breast Cancer Radiology Reporting IG is confirmed. If the IG has been withdrawn, is inactive, or has not progressed to a new ballot since 2019, the editorial corrections may be moot. Re-evaluate once the IG's future direction is determined.

**Rationale:** The ticket is from 2019 and the IG has had several "Resolved - No Change" tickets in 2025 (FHIR-38775, FHIR-38778, FHIR-38774, FHIR-38773, FHIR-38777), which may indicate the IG's status is uncertain. Editorial fixes are only meaningful if the IG will proceed to a future ballot.

## Recommendation

**Disposition C: Defer Pending Current IG Status Review**

This ticket dates from September 2019 with a Persuasive resolution from November 2019. However, the ticket remains in "Triaged" status over five years later, suggesting the promised editorial improvements may not have been applied. More importantly, several recent tickets against this specification (2025) were resolved as "No Change," which may indicate the IG is not actively progressing. Before investing effort in verifying or enforcing editorial corrections, the work group should first clarify the current status and trajectory of the US Breast Cancer Radiology Reporting IG. If the IG is active and approaching a new ballot, the editorial pass should be confirmed; if not, deferral is the practical choice.
