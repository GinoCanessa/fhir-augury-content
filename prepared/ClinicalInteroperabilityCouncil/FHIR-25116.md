## Description

The commenter recommends reviewing and including the CDISC Breast Cancer Therapeutic Area User Guide (TAUG) as a source for the US Breast Cancer Radiology Reporting FHIR Implementation Guide. The TAUG was co-developed by CDISC with TransCelerate Biopharma, C-Path, FDA, and NCI, and represents a significant clinical data standard in the breast cancer domain.

## Resolution Description

> We agree. We have recently had conversations with CDISC about future collaboration.

The workgroup found this persuasive and indicated agreement, noting that discussions with CDISC about collaboration were already underway.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive (Affirm Existing Resolution)

**Rationale:** The CDISC Breast Cancer Therapeutic Area User Guide is a well-established clinical data standard developed in collaboration with FDA, NCI, TransCelerate Biopharma, and C-Path. It contains domain-specific terminology and data models directly relevant to breast cancer clinical data. The workgroup has already agreed and indicated that CDISC collaboration discussions were underway. Affirming the persuasive resolution allows the IG authors to incorporate relevant TAUG concepts (e.g., standard breast cancer data elements, terminology alignments) into future versions of the IG. This is consistent with the related FHIR-24501 request for the mCODE specification, demonstrating a broader community interest in CDISC alignment.

**Action:** Confirm the existing "Persuasive" resolution. The IG editors should review the CDISC Breast Cancer TAUG and incorporate relevant elements into the specification as appropriate in a future publication cycle.

### Disposition B: Not Persuasive

**Rationale:** The CDISC Breast Cancer TAUG is primarily oriented toward clinical research and regulatory data collection (CDASH/SDTM domains), which differs in scope from the radiology reporting focus of this IG. The IG is specifically about structured breast cancer radiology reports using DiagnosticReport, Observation, and related FHIR resources, whereas the TAUG addresses broader clinical trial data elements. Adding an external non-FHIR standard as a source could introduce scope creep and complexity without clear benefit to the radiology reporting use case.

**Action:** Close the ticket as Not Persuasive, noting that the TAUG's clinical research focus does not directly align with the radiology reporting scope of this IG.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive with Modification

**Rationale:** While the full CDISC TAUG covers a broader scope than radiology reporting, specific sections of the TAUG related to breast imaging findings, tumor characteristics, and staging terminology may be directly relevant. Rather than a wholesale review of the entire TAUG, a targeted review of sections pertaining to imaging and diagnostic data elements would be more appropriate and manageable for the IG editors.

**Action:** Close as Not Persuasive with Modification, requesting that the IG editors conduct a targeted review of TAUG sections relevant to breast radiology reporting terminology (e.g., imaging findings, tumor characteristics) rather than a full TAUG review, and document any adopted concepts in the IG's references.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Persuasive (Affirm Existing Resolution)**

The workgroup already found this persuasive and the resolution description confirms agreement, including active CDISC collaboration conversations. The same reporter (Mitra Rocca) made a parallel request for the mCODE specification (FHIR-24501), demonstrating a consistent cross-specification interest in CDISC alignment. The CDISC Breast Cancer TAUG, co-developed with FDA and NCI, is a credible and authoritative source that can inform breast cancer data elements. Affirming the existing persuasive resolution is the most appropriate path, allowing the IG editors flexibility to incorporate relevant TAUG content at their discretion in future updates.
