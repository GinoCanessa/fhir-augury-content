## Summary
This ticket requests the creation of profile examples for the US Breast Cancer Radiology Reporting FHIR Implementation Guide. Profile examples are essential conformance artifacts that demonstrate how to correctly populate resources according to the IG's structural and terminological constraints, and their absence is a gap in the specification's usability and testability.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested
#### Proposal
Accept the request and create at least one conformant example instance for each profile defined in the US Breast Cancer Radiology Reporting IG. Each example should demonstrate required elements, representative coded values from the IG's required value sets, and realistic clinical data for breast cancer radiology reporting scenarios (e.g., a screening mammography report, a diagnostic ultrasound report). Examples should be included in the IG package and validated against their respective profiles.
#### Justification
Profile examples are a best practice recommended by the FHIR IG Publisher guidance and HL7 IG quality expectations. They serve as concrete reference implementations for developers, aid in testing and validation, and improve the overall usability and adoptability of the specification. The absence of examples is a meaningful gap, and providing them is a standard part of IG development.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach
#### Proposal
Accept the request but limit the initial scope to creating examples only for the most critical or complex profiles in the IG (e.g., the primary DiagnosticReport and Observation profiles for breast radiology findings), rather than all profiles. Defer examples for simpler or less commonly used profiles to a subsequent ballot cycle. Prioritize examples that illustrate the most challenging conformance requirements such as slicing, extensions, and required terminology bindings.
#### Justification
If the IG contains a large number of profiles, creating examples for all of them may require significant effort. A phased approach ensures that the most impactful examples are delivered promptly while keeping the work manageable. This still addresses the core need of the ticket while allowing the work group to spread the effort across cycles.

---

### Disposition C: Decline
#### Proposal
Decline the request as out-of-scope for the current ballot cycle due to resource constraints, with a recommendation that example creation be tracked as a future enhancement.
#### Justification
While profile examples are valuable, the ticket description is minimal and does not specify which profiles need examples or what clinical scenarios should be covered. Additionally, the ticket has been in "Triaged" status since 2022 with no substantive activity (the only comment is a placeholder). If the work group lacks bandwidth or if the IG is undergoing significant structural changes, deferring example creation until the profiles are more stable could avoid rework.

---

### Recommendation
**Recommended disposition:** A (Accept As Requested)
Creating profile examples is a fundamental part of IG development and directly supports implementer adoption. The FHIR community consistently identifies the absence of examples as a barrier to correct implementation. While the ticket description is brief, the request itself is clear and well-understood — every profile in a published IG should have at least one conformant example. Given that this ticket has been open since October 2022 without resolution, accepting and completing it would close a known quality gap. If resource constraints are a concern, the work group could follow Disposition B's phased strategy, but the overall direction should be acceptance.
