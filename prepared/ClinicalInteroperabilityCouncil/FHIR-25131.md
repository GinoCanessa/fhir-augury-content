## Issue Description

The ballot commenter (Mitra Rocca, BC #82) identified an incomplete sentence in the US Breast Cancer Radiology Reporting IG:

> **Existing Wording:** "While nothing being done here is specifically forbidden within FHIR, we feel that taken together this strategy has."

The sentence ends abruptly after "has." without completing the thought. The commenter requests that the sentence be rephrased to be grammatically complete and convey its intended meaning.

## Resolution Description

> "We agree. We will improve this in the next ballot."

The work group agreed the sentence is incomplete and committed to fixing it in the next ballot cycle.

## Analysis

This is a straightforward editorial correction. The incomplete sentence ("...we feel that taken together this strategy has.") is clearly a drafting error — the thought was never finished. Multiple independent ballot commenters (FHIR-25082, FHIR-24052) flagged the exact same sentence, reinforcing that this is an obvious textual defect rather than a subjective concern.

The resolution of "Persuasive" is appropriate. The fix is purely editorial with no technical or structural impact on the IG's profiles, value sets, or conformance requirements. The work group acknowledged the error and committed to correcting it.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Apply as Persuasive — Complete the Sentence
- **Action:** Accept the "Persuasive" resolution. Complete the incomplete sentence to express its full intended meaning (e.g., "...we feel that taken together this strategy has significant benefits for interoperability" or similar wording consistent with the IG's modeling rationale).
- **Rationale:** The sentence is clearly incomplete. The work group already agreed. This is a simple editorial fix that should be applied to the next published version.
- **Impact:** Minimal — narrative text correction only. No impact on profiles, value sets, or conformance.

### Disposition B: Apply as Persuasive with Comment — Remove or Rewrite the Paragraph
- **Action:** Accept the "Persuasive" resolution, but instead of simply completing the sentence, remove or substantially rewrite the surrounding paragraph for clarity, as suggested by the broader editorial concerns in FHIR-24052.
- **Rationale:** If the sentence was left incomplete, the surrounding text may also need editorial attention. A more thorough rewrite could address multiple editorial issues at once.
- **Impact:** Low — still limited to narrative text. May require additional editorial review.

### Disposition C: Defer — Consolidate with FHIR-24052
- **Action:** Mark as duplicate or defer in favor of FHIR-24052, which calls for a comprehensive editorial pass covering this and other textual issues.
- **Rationale:** Since FHIR-24052 explicitly calls out this same sentence and requests a broader editorial review, consolidating the work could be more efficient.
- **Impact:** None immediately. The fix would be addressed as part of the broader editorial effort under FHIR-24052.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Apply as Persuasive — Complete the Sentence.**

This is the most direct and appropriate response. The defect is clear, the resolution is already agreed upon, and the fix is trivial. While FHIR-24052 covers broader editorial concerns, this specific ticket should be resolved on its own merits with a completed sentence. Deferring unnecessarily delays closing a simple, well-understood issue.
