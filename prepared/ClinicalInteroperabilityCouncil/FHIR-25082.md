## Summary

The ballot commenter (Keith Salzman) identified an incomplete sentence in the US Breast Cancer Radiology Reporting IG. The sentence reads: *"While nothing being done here is specifically forbidden within FHIR, we feel that taken together this strategy has."* — the sentence trails off without completing the thought. The commenter proposed that the sentence be completed. Additionally, the commenter noted several typos throughout the ballot and provided broader commentary on the challenge of transitioning from analog/stand-alone processes in the Breast/Radiology community to a digital, interoperable process that supports uniform data collection and improved breast cancer care outcomes.

**Resolution Description (Persuasive):**

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept as Persuasive (Implement as Resolved)

Accept the existing Persuasive resolution. The incomplete sentence must be completed to convey the intended meaning. The resolution acknowledged agreement and committed to improving content, structure, and clarity in the next ballot cycle. **Action:** Ensure the sentence beginning *"While nothing being done here is specifically forbidden within FHIR, we feel that taken together this strategy has"* is completed with an appropriate clause (e.g., describing what the strategy achieves or the concerns it raises). Also review the surrounding text for the noted typos and correct them.

### Disposition B: Accept as Persuasive with Modification — Request Specific Replacement Text

Accept as Persuasive but request that the editorial team provide specific replacement wording for the incomplete sentence rather than leaving it open-ended. The commenter did not propose exact replacement text, only "complete the thought." The work group should draft the completed sentence to ensure it accurately reflects the IG's intent and then apply the fix. This avoids ambiguity about what "completing the thought" means.

### Disposition C: Decline — Not Persuasive

Decline on the basis that this ballot comment was filed in 2019 and resolved as Persuasive in November 2019 with a general commitment to improve the next ballot. If the subsequent ballot iteration has already addressed these issues, the ticket may be considered moot and can be closed without further action.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Accept as Persuasive (Implement as Resolved)**

This is the most straightforward path. The comment clearly identifies a genuine defect — an incomplete sentence — and the resolution already acknowledges agreement. The fix is editorial in nature (complete the sentence, correct typos) and aligns with the pattern of similar ballot comments (FHIR-25131, FHIR-25064, FHIR-25081) that collectively called for improved content quality in the US Breast Cancer Radiology Reporting IG. Implementing the agreed resolution honors the ballot feedback process and ensures the IG text is coherent.
