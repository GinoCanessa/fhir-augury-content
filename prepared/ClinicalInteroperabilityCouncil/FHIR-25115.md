## Summary

The submitter recommends that the Biomedical Research and Regulation (BR&R) work group be included as a co-sponsor work group for the US Breast Cancer Radiology Reporting (FHIR) implementation guide. This is a governance/organizational request rather than a technical change to the specification content.

## Details

**Ballot Comment:** The commenter (Mitra Rocca) suggests that BR&R should be added as a co-sponsoring work group for the Breast Cancer Radiology Reporting IG, given the relevance of the specification to biomedical research and regulatory domains.

**Resolution (Persuasive):** The project team agreed with the recommendation and stated they would contact the Biomedical Research and Regulation (BR&R) work group to inquire about their interest in co-sponsorship.

> "We agree. We will contact Biomedical Research and Regulation (BR&R) and inquire of their interest."

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept (Apply as Persuasive)

Accept the resolution as stated. The project team agreed to contact BR&R about co-sponsorship. Confirm that this outreach was completed (or is still planned) and formally add BR&R as a co-sponsor work group if they expressed interest. Mark the ticket as Applied once the co-sponsorship status is confirmed.

**Rationale:** The resolution was already marked Persuasive, indicating agreement. The Breast Cancer Radiology Reporting IG has clear relevance to the BR&R domain (regulatory oversight of diagnostic imaging and cancer-related research). Adding BR&R as a co-sponsor strengthens cross-work-group alignment and clinical-regulatory interoperability.

### Disposition B: Accept with Follow-Up Action Required

Accept the recommendation as Persuasive but note that the ticket remains in Triaged status despite being resolved in 2019. Request a status update on whether BR&R was actually contacted and whether they agreed to co-sponsor. If BR&R declined or did not respond, document that outcome and close the ticket as Resolved - No Change with an explanatory note.

**Rationale:** Over five years have passed since the resolution was recorded, yet the ticket has not been moved to Applied. This suggests the follow-up action may not have been completed. A definitive status update would bring closure to the ballot comment.

### Disposition C: Decline (No Change)

Decline the change on the grounds that co-sponsorship decisions are organizational matters handled outside the ballot reconciliation process. Note the recommendation for the project leadership to consider but close the ticket without requiring a specification change.

**Rationale:** Co-sponsorship of an IG is a governance decision that does not directly affect the technical content of the specification. The ballot reconciliation process is primarily for addressing technical and content feedback, and organizational sponsorship changes may be better tracked through HL7 project management channels rather than Jira ballot tickets.

## Recommendation

**Disposition B: Accept with Follow-Up Action Required.**

The original resolution (Persuasive) is appropriate — the suggestion to include BR&R as a co-sponsor is reasonable and was accepted by the project team. However, given that over five years have elapsed with the ticket still in Triaged status, the work group should request a concrete status update on whether BR&R was contacted and what the outcome was. This ensures the ballot comment is properly closed with a documented outcome rather than left indefinitely open.
