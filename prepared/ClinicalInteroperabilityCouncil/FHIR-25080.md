## Description

The commenter (Claude Nanjo) argues that **Composition** is the right choice for representing breast radiology reports and that **DiagnosticReport** should either be eliminated as a resource or made a profile on Composition. Consequently, all resources that currently reference DiagnosticReport should instead reference the correct profile on Composition.

The title is somewhat contradictory to the body: while the summary says "Diagnostic Reports should be a reference, not composition," the body content argues in favor of Composition over DiagnosticReport. This appears to be a ballot comment (BC #57) on the US Breast Cancer Radiology Reporting IG.

## Resolution Description

**Not Persuasive with Modification:** The team agreed that with appropriate changes to referring resources and the addition of some elements in DiagnosticReport, Composition would be a better solution. However, this was not possible within the timing of the next ballot cycle. Per Graham Grieve's recommendations, the team intended to use DiagnosticReport as the root and Observations as the section headers. Based on feedback from subsequent ballots they planned to revisit this. The team noted they were not confident this was a complete or sufficient solution and expected to continue evolving it over several ballot cycles.

## Context & Analysis

This ticket is part of a large cluster of ballot comments (BC #1, #9, #12, #28, #43, #57, #76, #91, and others) from the US Breast Cancer Radiology Reporting IG ballot cycle (2019). The central architectural debate is:

1. **The problem:** The Breast Radiology IG needed to represent structured reports with sections and sub-sections. DiagnosticReport lacked native section support, so the IG used a hybrid approach: DiagnosticReport as the root with a reference to a Composition resource that held the sectioned content.

2. **The commenter's position (FHIR-25080):** Claude Nanjo argued Composition is the right choice and DiagnosticReport should be eliminated or made a profile on Composition.

3. **The majority position (from related tickets):** Most ballot commenters took the opposite view—DiagnosticReport should be the top-level anchor and should be enhanced with section capabilities, rather than relying on Composition. Key arguments included:
   - Many clinical resources (CarePlan, Procedure, ServiceRequest, etc.) can reference DiagnosticReport but not Composition (FHIR-25064)
   - Switching to Composition would encourage text over discrete values (FHIR-23933)
   - Adding sections to DiagnosticReport would benefit all diagnostic reporting, not just breast radiology (FHIR-24049)

4. **The resolution direction:** Per Graham Grieve's recommendation, the team adopted DiagnosticReport as root with Observations as section headers, acknowledging this was not a complete solution and would evolve over several ballot cycles.

This ticket was resolved as "Not Persuasive with Modification" because while the team agreed Composition had advantages, the practical direction was to keep DiagnosticReport as the anchor, which is the opposite of what this specific commenter requested.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Affirm Existing Resolution (Not Persuasive with Modification)
**Rationale:** The existing resolution correctly acknowledges the commenter's point that Composition has structural advantages while explaining why the practical direction favors DiagnosticReport as the anchor. The overwhelming majority of related ballot comments (FHIR-25061, FHIR-25062, FHIR-25064, FHIR-25085, FHIR-23933, FHIR-24049) argued for keeping DiagnosticReport and enhancing it. The resolution aligns with Graham Grieve's recommendation and the direction of subsequent IG development. The IG has since evolved to use DiagnosticReport with Observations as section headers, which is consistent with this resolution. **No change needed.**

### Disposition B: Change to Not Persuasive
**Rationale:** The commenter's core proposal—eliminating DiagnosticReport or making it a profile on Composition—was fundamentally rejected. The "with Modification" qualifier in the current resolution may overstate the degree to which the commenter's suggestion was adopted. The modifications actually made (using DiagnosticReport as root with Observation-based sections) followed the opposite architectural direction from what this commenter proposed. A straight "Not Persuasive" would more accurately reflect that the comment's recommendation was not followed. This would also be more consistent with the "Persuasive" resolutions given to the related tickets (FHIR-25061, FHIR-25062, etc.) that argued for keeping DiagnosticReport.

### Disposition C: Change to Persuasive with Modification
**Rationale:** The commenter raised a legitimate architectural concern about the limitations of DiagnosticReport for structured reports. The IG team themselves acknowledged this was an ongoing issue. The modifications to subsequent ballot cycles (restructuring around DiagnosticReport with Observations) were in part motivated by the same underlying problem the commenter identified, even if the specific solution differed. Upgrading to "Persuasive with Modification" would acknowledge that the comment contributed to the architectural evolution, with the modification being that DiagnosticReport was enhanced rather than replaced by Composition.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Affirm Existing Resolution (Not Persuasive with Modification)**

The existing resolution strikes the right balance. It acknowledges the commenter's valid observation that Composition has structural advantages for sectioned reports, while correctly noting the practical and interoperability reasons for keeping DiagnosticReport as the anchor resource. The resolution's candid acknowledgment that the solution was evolving was appropriate given the state of discussion at the time. The cluster of related ballot comments overwhelmingly supported DiagnosticReport, and the IG has since continued in that direction. No change to the resolution is warranted.
