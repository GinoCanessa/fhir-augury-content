## Description

The submitter (Susan Matney) reports that the ImagingStudy resource does not include fields for **result**, **impression**, or a place to add a **coded result, impression, or insertion**. The request is for the Breast Cancer Radiology Reporting IG to address the absence of these clinical reporting elements within ImagingStudy.

## Resolution Statement

> We intend to include all results/impressions and codes in the findings section and/or impressions section. We do not intend to try to use ImagingStudy as a place for them.
>
> We will discuss this.

The resolution rationale is that ImagingStudy is an imaging metadata resource, not a clinical results resource. Clinical findings, impressions, and coded results belong in Observation, DiagnosticReport, and Composition sections — not in ImagingStudy itself.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Affirm "Not Persuasive" (Recommended)

**Rationale:** The original resolution correctly identifies that ImagingStudy is a resource for imaging metadata (study-level information such as modality, series, and instances), not for clinical interpretations. In FHIR's architecture, clinical results, impressions, and coded findings are conveyed through Observation resources referenced from DiagnosticReport (via `result`) or organized within Composition sections. The Breast Cancer Radiology Reporting IG design appropriately places findings and impressions in dedicated sections of the Composition/DiagnosticReport rather than overloading ImagingStudy. Related tickets (FHIR-25165, FHIR-25168) separately addressed the linking between ImagingStudy and the diagnostic report, which is the appropriate mechanism for connecting imaging metadata with clinical findings.

**Action:** No specification change required. Confirm the existing "Not Persuasive" resolution.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification — Add Guidance

**Rationale:** While the resolution is technically correct that ImagingStudy should not carry clinical findings, the ticket reveals a usability gap: implementers may not understand where to place coded results and impressions when working with ImagingStudy-centric workflows. The IG could be enhanced with explicit guidance or an informative section explaining the relationship between ImagingStudy (imaging metadata) and the findings/impressions sections (clinical interpretations), including how to link them.

**Action:** Add an informative section to the IG clarifying the separation of concerns between ImagingStudy and clinical result resources, with examples of how to reference ImagingStudy from DiagnosticReport/Composition.

### Disposition C: Persuasive — Add Extension or Element

**Rationale:** Some implementers working in tightly integrated imaging/reporting workflows may benefit from a lightweight mechanism on ImagingStudy to reference associated clinical findings or impressions, even if the full clinical content lives elsewhere. This could be an extension on ImagingStudy pointing to the relevant DiagnosticReport or Composition.

**Action:** Define an optional extension on ImagingStudy (e.g., `associatedReport`) that references DiagnosticReport or Composition containing the clinical findings and impressions for the study.

---

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Affirm "Not Persuasive"**

The FHIR resource model intentionally separates imaging metadata (ImagingStudy) from clinical interpretations (Observation, DiagnosticReport, Composition). Adding result/impression fields to ImagingStudy would violate this separation of concerns and duplicate functionality already available in purpose-built resources. The related tickets (FHIR-25165, FHIR-25168) addressed the legitimate linking concern separately. The original resolution is sound and aligns with FHIR's architectural principles.
