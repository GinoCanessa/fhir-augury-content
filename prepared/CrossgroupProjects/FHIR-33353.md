## Description

The submitter requests that the US Military Service IG address clinical conditions representing health risks tied to military career exposures. Specific examples cited include Post Traumatic Stress, asbestos exposure, burn pit exposure, and depleted uranium exposure. The request asks that either:

1. Profiles and extensions to capture these exposure-related conditions be included in the IG, or
2. The IG explicitly document how such information is captured and acknowledge the need in a "deferred for future" section.

The existing IG text notes that "the clinician may be working with a Veteran in the community and must assess the health risks associated with military service," but the submitter argues there are no corresponding profiles, extensions, or coded value sets to actually capture these risk conditions.

## Analysis

### Key Considerations

1. **Recurrent theme**: Multiple ballot commenters (FHIR-33353, FHIR-33578, FHIR-33359) independently raised the absence of exposure-risk profiling, signaling strong community demand.
2. **Prior deferral was unanimous**: The original 11-0-1 vote to defer was reasonable at the STU1 stage when the IG focused on service history documentation. Re-opening post-STU1 implies the workgroup is now ready to consider broader scope.
3. **Scope implications**: Adding exposure-risk profiles would require new Condition/Observation profiles, value sets for exposure types (burn pit, depleted uranium, asbestos, noise, etc.), and possibly extensions linking exposures to service episodes.
4. **Cross-specification dependencies**: Occupational Data for Health (ODH) and SDOH Clinical Care IGs already model some occupational hazard concepts; alignment or reuse should be evaluated.
5. **PACT Act relevance**: The 2022 PACT Act expanded VA healthcare for veterans with toxic exposures, increasing the policy and clinical urgency for structured exposure data.

---

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive with Modification — Define an Exposure Risk Profile

**Action**: Accept the request but scope it to a defined set of exposure-risk profiles for the next ballot cycle. Create a new Condition or Observation profile (e.g., `MilitaryExposureCondition`) with a required binding to a value set of military-specific exposure types (burn pit, depleted uranium, asbestos, noise, chemical agents). Link the exposure to the relevant `MilitaryServiceEpisode` via a reference extension. Coordinate with FHIR-33578 to resolve both tickets together.

**Pros**: Directly addresses the submitter's request and the parallel request in FHIR-33578; aligns with PACT Act policy drivers; delivers concrete new capability.

**Cons**: Significant scope expansion requiring new profiles, value sets, and possibly new extensions; may delay the next ballot if resources are constrained.

### Disposition B: Considered for Future Use — Defer with Explicit Roadmap

**Action**: Maintain the deferral but add an explicit "Future Scope" section to the IG that (a) acknowledges the need for exposure-risk capture, (b) lists the specific exposure categories identified by commenters, and (c) references the PACT Act and ODH IG as context. Commit to addressing this in a named future version.

**Pros**: Low implementation cost; acknowledges the need formally; sets expectations for implementers; buys time to coordinate with ODH and SDOH IGs.

**Cons**: Does not deliver any new interoperability capability; same commenters may re-raise the issue in the next ballot cycle.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive — Out of Scope for This IG

**Action**: Close as not persuasive on the grounds that exposure-risk conditions are clinical data better addressed by condition-specific IGs (e.g., respiratory, oncology) or by ODH, and the US Military Service IG should remain focused on service history documentation.

**Pros**: Keeps the IG tightly scoped; avoids duplication with other IGs.

**Cons**: Ignores strong community demand (multiple independent commenters); the connection between military service and specific exposure risks is unique and unlikely to be addressed by generic clinical IGs; risks losing stakeholder engagement.

---

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Persuasive with Modification** is recommended. The re-opening of this ticket (and the closely related FHIR-33578) post-STU1 signals that the workgroup recognizes this gap must be addressed. The PACT Act has created tangible clinical demand for structured military exposure data. A scoped approach — defining an initial `MilitaryExposureCondition` profile with a starter value set of the most commonly cited exposures — can be delivered incrementally without requiring the IG to model all possible conditions. This ticket should be coordinated with FHIR-33578 and FHIR-33362 to ensure a cohesive approach to exposure types, occupational codes, and risk documentation.
