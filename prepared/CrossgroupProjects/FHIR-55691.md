## Summary

This ticket serves as a placeholder tracker for updated guidance and terminology for the US Core DocumentReference profile. The content was developed by the Argonaut Enhanced DocumentReference Access project, which ran from February–March 2026. The project surveyed implementers and proposed a three-category scheme for DocumentReference categorization: retaining the existing "clinical-note" category and adding "structured-document" and "unstructured-document." However, the group identified too many unresolved issues (overlap with format codes, mismatch between the clinical-note definition and actual system behavior, unaddressed questions around search/filtering/SMART scopes) to proceed with a full US Core 9.0.0 update. The agreed resolution is to add an STU Note to the US Core 9.0.0 Clinical Notes Guidance page signaling future intent, and to carry remaining open issues into the Argonaut FHIR Write – DocRef project. The resolution is "Persuasive with Modification" and the STU Note has been pre-applied to the US Core CI Build.

## Details

**Description:**

Placeholder tracker for updated guidance and terminology for US Core DocumentReference. The content is expected to be provided by [The Argonaut Enhanced DocumentReference Access](https://confluence.hl7.org/spaces/AP/pages/413054461/Enhanced+DocumentReference+Access) project.

**Resolution Description:**

The Argonaut Enhanced DocumentReference Access project set out to address gaps in the [US Core's Clinical Notes Guidance](https://hl7.org/fhir/us/core/clinical-notes.html) page and [US Core DocumentReference Profile](https://hl7.org/fhir/us/core/StructureDefinition-us-core-documentreference.html). The FHIR DocumentReference resource covers virtually any patient-associated content — from clinical notes to scanned insurance cards to structured CDA documents — yet the only required category in US Core is "clinical-note". Over three working sessions (February–March 2026), the group surveyed implementers and confirmed that real-world systems use several other local categories. A three-category scheme based on system architecture was proposed:

1. **clinical-note** (existing): *Part of a health record where healthcare professionals record details to document a patient's clinical status or achievements during the course of a hospitalization or over the course of outpatient care.*
2. **structured-document** (new): *Documents with predefined schemas (such as CDA or FHIR Composition) that allow for machine-readable headers and/or entries. These documents are either system-generated internally by the EHR or received from an external source.*
3. **unstructured-document** (new): *Unstructured content captured via digital upload, digital or traditional scanning, and stored and managed in a document repository (in other words, a DMS system). These documents are not natively authored in the EHR, lack discrete data elements, and are primarily in image or PDF format.*

Despite general consensus that these categories make operational sense and do not require customer mapping, the group identified too many unresolved issues to proceed with a US Core 9.0.0 update — including overlap with existing format codes, a mismatch between the current clinical-note definition and actual system behavior, and unaddressed questions around search, clinical content filtering, and SMART scopes. **The agreed outcome is to add an STU Note to US Core Version 9.0.0 Clinical Notes Guidance page to signal future intent**, and to carry the remaining open issues into the Argonaut FHIR Write – DocRef project and a potential future initiative.

The STU Note text reads:

> The current US Core DocumentReference category value set contains a single concept, "clinical-note", but the FHIR DocumentReference resource covers a broad range of patient-associated content — including structured CDA documents, FHIR Compositions, scanned uploads, insurance cards, and more. The *2026 Argonaut Enhance DocumentReference Access Project* proposed the addition of two new categories:
>
> 1. **structured-document**: Documents with predefined schemas, such as CDA or FHIR Composition, that allow for machine-readable headers and/or entries.
> 2. **unstructured-document**: Unstructured content captured via digital upload or scanning, stored in a document management system, and not natively authored in the EHR.
>
> and updating the "clinical-note" definition. The addition of a complementary set of clinical content categories to enable client access by clinical domain was also discussed.
>
> Further work is planned in the *2026 Argonaut FHIR Write – DocRef Project*. Based on these efforts, future versions of US Core may expand the DocumentReference category value set.

Pre-applied here: [Clinical Notes Guidance](https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/US-Core/clinical-notes.html#fhir-resources-to-exchange-clinical-notes) and [US Core DocumentReference Profile](https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/US-Core/StructureDefinition-us-core-documentreference.html).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Resolved (Persuasive with Modification)

#### Proposal

Accept the resolution as applied: add an STU Note to the US Core 9.0.0 Clinical Notes Guidance page and the US Core DocumentReference profile page signaling the proposed expansion of the DocumentReference category value set with "structured-document" and "unstructured-document" categories. Defer the actual value set expansion and remaining open issues (format code overlap, clinical-note definition mismatch, search/filtering/SMART scopes) to the Argonaut FHIR Write – DocRef project.

#### Justification

This resolution reflects the consensus reached across three Argonaut working sessions and was approved by the CGP workgroup on March 26, 2026. The STU Note approach is appropriately conservative — it signals future intent without introducing normative changes that still have unresolved dependencies. The change has already been pre-applied to the CI Build, and it avoids scope creep in the US Core 9.0.0 release while keeping the community informed of the planned direction.

---

### Disposition B: Expand the STU Note with Additional Detail

#### Proposal

Accept the resolution with modification: retain the STU Note but expand it to include explicit mention of the specific unresolved issues (format code overlap, clinical-note definition mismatch, search behavior, clinical content filtering, and SMART scopes) so that implementers are aware of the full scope of future work. Additionally, add a reference to the Confluence page for the Argonaut Enhanced DocumentReference Access project for traceability.

#### Justification

While the current STU Note communicates future intent, it omits the specific blockers that prevented adoption in this cycle. Including these details would provide implementers with a clearer picture of what to expect and help ballot commenters in future cycles provide targeted feedback on the remaining issues. Adding the Confluence reference improves traceability between the specification and the project that produced the guidance.

---

### Disposition C: Decline — Defer Entirely

#### Proposal

Decline the change and remove the STU Note, deferring all DocumentReference category work entirely to the Argonaut FHIR Write – DocRef project. The ticket would remain open and be re-evaluated when concrete proposals are ready for normative inclusion.

#### Justification

An STU Note that signals future intent without a concrete timeline or commitment could create confusion among implementers who may prematurely begin implementing the proposed categories. Since the group explicitly acknowledged that too many issues remain unresolved, it may be cleaner to defer entirely rather than add speculative guidance to the specification. This avoids setting expectations that may not be met in the next ballot cycle.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The STU Note approach represents a well-considered consensus from the Argonaut Enhanced DocumentReference Access project and the CGP workgroup. It strikes the right balance between informing the community of planned improvements and avoiding premature normative changes. The three-category scheme was validated through implementer surveys and multi-session deliberation, so the future direction is well-grounded even though specific details remain to be worked out. The change has already been pre-applied and approved by the workgroup, and deferring entirely (Disposition C) would discard useful directional guidance, while expanding the note (Disposition B), though reasonable, risks overloading an STU Note with implementation-level detail better suited for the specification itself once the issues are resolved. Accepting as resolved keeps the US Core 9.0.0 release on track while preserving the community's work for the next phase.
