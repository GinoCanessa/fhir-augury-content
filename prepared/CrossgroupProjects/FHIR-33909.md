## Description

The submitter requests adding slicing to the US Military Service IG to include the ODH (Occupational Data for Health) vocabulary for Industry — specifically the Industry NAICS Detail (ODH) value set ([OID 2.16.840.1.114222.4.11.7900](https://phinvads.cdc.gov/vads/ViewValueSet.action?oid=2.16.840.1.114222.4.11.7900)). The rationale is that ODH has a different and more complete vocabulary for industry than what is currently specified.

## Resolution Description

> Modified inheritance - OBE

The resolution indicates this request was deemed "Overtaken By Events" (OBE) due to modified inheritance in the IG structure, making the specific slicing request no longer necessary.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Affirm Not Persuasive (OBE)

**Rationale:** The resolution correctly identifies that inheritance changes in the US Military Service IG rendered this request moot. The IG now inherits its vocabulary bindings from upstream profiles (likely ODH), so adding explicit slicing for NAICS Industry Detail at this level is unnecessary. The "OBE" designation is appropriate — the underlying need may have been addressed through the structural change, not by rejecting the concept outright.

**Action:** No change to specification. Confirm the Not Persuasive resolution and close.

### Disposition B: Reconsider as Persuasive with Modification

**Rationale:** The twin ticket FHIR-33908 (Occupation vocabulary) was resolved as **Persuasive**, creating an inconsistency: Occupation got ODH slicing but Industry did not. If the inheritance change truly makes NAICS Industry Detail available through the profile hierarchy, this should be documented explicitly. If it does not, the asymmetry between Occupation and Industry treatment should be reconciled. The disposition would add clarifying documentation or verify that NAICS Industry Detail is actually inherited.

**Action:** Verify that NAICS Industry Detail vocabulary is available through the modified inheritance. If yes, add a note to the IG explaining this. If no, add the slicing as originally requested.

### Disposition C: Defer Pending FHIR-33362 Resolution

**Rationale:** FHIR-33362 raises a fundamental question about whether NAICS codes are meaningful in a military context. Until that ticket is resolved, it is premature to either add or definitively reject NAICS Industry vocabulary slicing for US Military Service. The two tickets are tightly coupled — NAICS applicability to military occupations should be settled first.

**Action:** Defer this ticket until FHIR-33362 is resolved. Re-evaluate based on that outcome.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Affirm Not Persuasive (OBE)** is recommended.

The resolution is well-supported: Sean Muir's comment confirms the inheritance was deliberately modified, and OBE is the correct characterization. The twin ticket FHIR-33908 (Occupation) being Persuasive does not create a true inconsistency — the inheritance change may have addressed the Industry vocabulary through the profile hierarchy in a way that made explicit slicing redundant. Additionally, FHIR-33362's unresolved questions about NAICS applicability in military contexts further supports not adding NAICS-specific slicing at this level. The workgroup should confirm the inheritance chain provides the expected vocabulary coverage, but the resolution itself is sound.
