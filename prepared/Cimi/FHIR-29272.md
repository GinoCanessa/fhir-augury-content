## Summary

This change request asks the CIMI work group to add percentile and z-score observations to the vital signs panels within the US Vital Signs with Qualifying Extensions specification. In pediatric clinical practice, vital sign measurements (weight, height, BMI, head circumference) are routinely interpreted relative to age- and sex-matched reference populations, producing percentile ranks and z-scores. The ticket specifically calls for careful selection of LOINC codes that accurately reflect the WHO or CDC growth chart method used to derive each percentile. The request was originally deferred as "Considered for Future Use" in January 2021 (vote 19-0-8, Susan Matney/Nathan Davis), then reopened post-STU1 publication in September 2022 because the missing percentile observations prevented meeting USCDI requirements. It was reopened again in February 2024 after being incorrectly deferred as part of a batch deferral. The ticket remains unresolved and awaiting CIMI disposition.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested
#### Proposal
Add new Observation profiles to the US Vital Signs with Qualifying Extensions specification for pediatric percentile and z-score observations covering body weight, body height, weight-for-length, and BMI-for-age, analogous to the existing pediatric head circumference percentile profile. Each profile should bind to specific LOINC codes that distinguish the reference method (CDC vs. WHO growth charts) and include guidance on recording the reference population in Observation.note or Observation.component. Z-score values should be supported as optional components.
#### Justification
Percentile and z-score observations are standard clinical practice in pediatric care and are required to meet USCDI data element expectations. The existing US Core specification already includes profiles for pediatric BMI-for-age and head circumference percentiles, demonstrating precedent. Extending this pattern to cover the remaining vital sign measurements (weight, height, weight-for-length) would close a known gap, improve interoperability for pediatric EHR systems, and ensure consistency with USCDI requirements. The ticket has highest priority and has been reopened multiple times over four years, indicating sustained community demand.

---

### Disposition B: Partial Acceptance — Profiles Without Z-Scores
#### Proposal
Add new Observation profiles for percentile observations (body weight percentile, body height percentile, weight-for-length percentile) with appropriate LOINC bindings and CDC/WHO method documentation guidance, but defer z-score support to a future iteration. Focus the initial work on percentile profiles that align directly with USCDI requirements and existing US Core patterns.
#### Justification
Percentile values are the most commonly used clinical metric in pediatric growth assessment and directly address USCDI requirements. Z-scores, while clinically useful (particularly in research and international contexts), add complexity in terms of LOINC mapping and may not yet have broad implementer demand. A phased approach delivers immediate value for the most pressing use case while allowing z-score support to be informed by implementer feedback and LOINC terminology maturation.

---

### Disposition C: Decline
#### Proposal
Decline on the basis that percentile profiles belong in US Core or jurisdiction-specific implementation guides rather than the base US Vital Signs with Qualifying Extensions specification.
#### Justification
Eric Haas noted in comments that "these are part of US Core," suggesting the appropriate home for these profiles may be the US Core Implementation Guide rather than the Vital Signs extensions specification. Pediatric percentile observations are already partially addressed in US Core (pediatric BMI-for-age, head circumference percentile). Adding them to the Vital Signs extensions specification could create duplication or conflict with US Core. The work group could recommend that the US Realm Steering Committee and Cross-Group Projects address this in US Core instead.

---

### Recommendation
**Recommended disposition:** A

The request to add percentile and z-score profiles has been open for over four years, has highest priority, and has been reopened multiple times — including once specifically because the missing profiles prevent meeting USCDI requirements. The Zulip discussion confirms active implementer demand and real-world gaps (e.g., no standard profiles for weight or height percentiles). While the concern about overlap with US Core is valid, the US Vital Signs with Qualifying Extensions specification is the natural home for vital-sign-related observation profiles that extend the FHIR core specification, and coordination with US Core can ensure alignment rather than duplication. Accepting the request in full, including z-scores, provides a comprehensive solution that serves both domestic and international implementers and resolves a long-standing gap.
