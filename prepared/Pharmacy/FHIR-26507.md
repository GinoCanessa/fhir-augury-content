**Request:** The submitter requests an option to return what is paid by the PBM (Pharmacy Benefit Manager) so that patients can understand how much of their coverage has been used and what remains before reaching the maximum benefit limit.

**Existing Wording Referenced:** The `rtpbc-patient-pay-type` value set currently contains 10 concepts defining patient pay types. The request implies adding PBM-paid amounts or accumulator/balance information to the response, which is not currently represented in this code system.

**Prior Resolution History:** This ticket was previously resolved as "Considered for Future Use" on 2020-04-13 with a vote of 12-0-0 (Frank McKinney / Daniel Zhang). The resolution noted: *"Consider including information in a future version that informs the patient of accumulator / balance information that could impact their cost in this or future fills of the medication, or contribution of this fill toward a balance that could impact future costs."* The ticket was re-opened by Lynn Laakso on 2020-08-27 after publication of STU1.

## Details

The consumer RTPBC specification provides patients with cost and coverage information for their prescribed medications. Currently, the `rtpbc-patient-pay-type` CodeSystem/ValueSet defines the types of patient-owed costs (e.g., copay, coinsurance, deductible amounts). The submitter is requesting an enhancement to also convey what the PBM pays on behalf of the patient, enabling patients to:

1. Understand the total benefit cost breakdown (not just their out-of-pocket portion)
2. Track remaining coverage before hitting PBM maximum limits
3. Make informed decisions about medication fills based on accumulator/balance status

This aligns with the broader RTPBC goal of transparency, extending it beyond patient cost to include plan contribution visibility. The prior 12-0-0 ballot vote acknowledged the value of this enhancement and deferred it for a future version.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive (Accept)

**Action:** Add new concepts to the `rtpbc-patient-pay-type` CodeSystem and ValueSet to represent PBM-paid amounts and accumulator/balance information. Define new codes such as `plan-paid` (amount paid by the PBM/plan) and `accumulator-balance` (remaining benefit balance toward coverage maximum). Update the RTPBC response profile to include optional elements conveying these values alongside patient cost information.

**Rationale:** The prior ballot unanimously acknowledged the value of this information (12-0-0 vote). Including PBM-paid amounts and accumulator data directly addresses the transparency goals of consumer RTPBC and helps patients make informed decisions about medication fills and cost planning. The feature was explicitly deferred to a future version, and sufficient time has passed since STU1 publication.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification (Accept with Scope Adjustment)

**Action:** Rather than adding PBM-paid amounts to the `rtpbc-patient-pay-type` value set, add a separate informational section or extension to the RTPBC response that conveys accumulator/benefit-balance summary data (e.g., deductible remaining, out-of-pocket maximum remaining). Do not include the exact PBM-paid amount per claim, as this may exceed the intended scope of patient-facing cost information and could introduce complexity with plan-specific adjudication details.

**Rationale:** The core patient need is understanding how close they are to benefit limits, not the exact dollar amount the PBM paid on a given fill. A benefit-balance summary is simpler to implement, avoids exposing proprietary adjudication internals, and aligns with the consumer RTPBC principle of showing only information "useful and meaningful to patients." This is consistent with the prior resolution's language about "accumulator / balance information."

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive (Decline)

**Action:** Retain the current `rtpbc-patient-pay-type` value set as-is. Do not add PBM-paid or accumulator concepts at this time.

**Rationale:** The consumer RTPBC specification intentionally limits content to information that is "useful and meaningful to patients," excluding detailed adjudication data present in the provider-facing NCPDP RTPB. PBM-paid amounts are adjudication details better suited for provider-facing transactions. Accumulator and benefit-balance data involves complex plan structures (deductible accumulators, MOOP tracking) that vary significantly by plan and may be difficult to convey meaningfully in a consumer context. Additionally, this information may already be available through other channels (e.g., insurer portals, EOB data). The ticket can remain deferred until implementer demand and plan data availability mature.

## Recommendation

**Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification** is recommended. The 12-0-0 prior vote confirms workgroup consensus that accumulator/balance information has patient value. However, the original request to return "what is paid by the PBM" conflates two concepts: (1) plan payment amounts per claim and (2) remaining benefit balance. Focusing on benefit-balance/accumulator summaries (Disposition B) delivers the patient-meaningful transparency the submitter seeks while avoiding the complexity and potential confidentiality concerns of exposing per-claim PBM adjudication amounts. This approach is consistent with the prior resolution's specific mention of "accumulator / balance information" and the RTPBC's design principle of including only patient-relevant content.
