## Summary

This ticket requests that `MedicationStatement.effective[x]` and `MedicationStatement.assertedDate` be marked as **must-support** in the SMP MedicationStatement profile. Currently, these date elements are optional. The submitter argues that must-support designation is needed to establish a medication timeframe, which is essential for clinical use of medication statements. The resolution is **Persuasive**, indicating the workgroup agrees with the proposed change.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept (Persuasive)

**Action:** Mark both `MedicationStatement.effective[x]` and `MedicationStatement.assertedDate` as must-support (MS) in the SMP MedicationStatement profile.

**Rationale:** Medication date information is clinically essential for establishing when a medication was taken or is being taken, and when the statement was asserted. Without must-support, systems may not populate these fields, undermining the ability to construct a meaningful medication timeline. AU Core (FHIR-49954) has independently reached the same conclusion by flagging both `effective[x]` and `dateAsserted` as MS in their new MedicationStatement profile. This change does not alter cardinality (the elements remain 0..1), so it does not impose a data-availability burden — it only requires that systems be capable of handling these elements when present.

### Disposition B: Accept with Modification — Mark only effective[x] as must-support

**Action:** Mark `MedicationStatement.effective[x]` as must-support but leave `assertedDate` without the MS flag.

**Rationale:** `effective[x]` directly represents the medication timeframe (when the medication was/is being taken) and is the most clinically impactful element for medication timeline construction. `assertedDate` (when the statement was recorded) is more of an administrative/provenance concern and may not always be available, particularly for historical medication data. QI-Core (FHIR-43010) specifically relaxed `dateAsserted` cardinality, noting it is only essential for quality measure logic. A narrower change reduces implementer burden while addressing the core clinical need.

### Disposition C: Decline (Not Persuasive)

**Action:** Leave both elements as optional without must-support.

**Rationale:** The SMP profile is designed to be broadly implementable. Adding must-support increases the conformance burden on systems, which must demonstrate they can store, retrieve, and display these elements. Some medication data sources (e.g., historical records, patient-reported medications) may not reliably include dates. The current optional approach allows flexibility while still permitting systems to support these elements voluntarily. However, this disposition contradicts the workgroup's existing Persuasive resolution.

---

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Accept (Persuasive)** is recommended.

The workgroup has already resolved this as Persuasive, and the change is well-justified. Medication dates are fundamental to clinical utility — knowing *when* a medication was taken and *when* it was reported are both important for medication reconciliation, clinical decision support, and care coordination. The must-support designation (without changing cardinality) is the appropriate mechanism: it requires systems to handle date data when available without mandating that every record contain dates. This aligns with the approach taken by AU Core (FHIR-49954) and maintains consistency with the broader FHIR ecosystem's treatment of MedicationStatement date elements.
