## Summary

This ticket requests that the PDMP (Prescription Drug Monitoring Program) FHIR IG should **not mandate** Social Security Number (SSN) as a required patient identifier in the response. The submitter argues that requiring SSN should be left to individual states' discretion, not mandated by the standard. Currently, SSN is marked with an asterisk (*) in the response mapping table, indicating it is required. The proposed change is to remove the required indicator from SSN.

The prior resolution (marked **Persuasive**) agreed that the response should not require the patient's SSN. The resolution notes that the PDMP Patient profile already requires at least one identifier (SSN, DL, MRN, or other), and the mapping table should be updated to reflect this flexibility.

This ticket was originally resolved as "Considered for Future Use" in January 2023 (vote: John Hatem / Tim McNeil, 4-0-0) because the IG had been withdrawn. It was reopened in November 2023 when the PDMP on FHIR project (PSS-2225) was relaunched.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept (Apply as Persuasive)

**Action:** Accept the existing Persuasive resolution. Update the PDMP response mapping tables (PMIX, NCPDP, ASAP 2.1A) to remove the required indicator (*) from SSN. Confirm that the PDMP Patient profile's existing constraint — requiring at least one identifier from (SSN, Driver's License, MRN, other) — remains the governing rule.

**Rationale:** The resolution was already agreed upon (Persuasive). SSN requirements vary by state, and mandating SSN transmission raises both privacy concerns and interoperability barriers for states that do not require it. The PDMP Patient profile already ensures at least one identifier is present, which is sufficient for patient matching. This change aligns with privacy best practices and gives states flexibility.

### Disposition B: Accept with Broader Identifier Guidance

**Action:** Accept the change to remove the SSN requirement, and additionally add a note in the IG clarifying that the preferred patient identifiers for PDMP queries/responses are state-determined. Reference the existing PDMP Patient profile constraint (at least one of SSN, DL, MRN, or other) in the mapping tables directly, so implementers can see the full identifier options in context. Coordinate with FHIR-29497, FHIR-29498, and FHIR-29499 to address all patient identifier mapping table issues together.

**Rationale:** The three related tickets (FHIR-29497/29498/29499) all concern the same mapping tables and patient identifier representation. Addressing them as a group would ensure consistency across the PMIX, NCPDP, and ASAP 2.1A mapping tables and avoid piecemeal updates.

### Disposition C: Decline (Not Persuasive)

**Action:** Reject the change. Keep SSN as required (*) in the response mapping tables.

**Rationale:** Some states rely on SSN as the primary patient identifier for PDMP matching. Removing the requirement could reduce match accuracy in those jurisdictions. However, this disposition conflicts with the existing Persuasive resolution and the prior 4-0-0 vote, and does not address the privacy concerns raised.

---

## Recommendation

**Disposition B: Accept with Broader Identifier Guidance**

The core change (removing SSN as required) is well-justified and was previously accepted as Persuasive with unanimous support. However, since three closely related tickets (FHIR-29497, FHIR-29498, FHIR-29499) all address patient identifier representation in the same mapping tables, the most efficient approach is to handle them together. This ensures all PDMP mapping tables (PMIX, NCPDP, ASAP 2.1A) are updated consistently to reflect the PDMP Patient profile's flexible identifier constraint, avoiding redundant rework and ensuring implementers have clear guidance.
