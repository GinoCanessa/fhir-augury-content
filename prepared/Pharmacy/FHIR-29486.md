## Summary

This ticket requests the simplification or removal of diagrams in figures 5A and 5B of the US Prescription Drug Monitoring Program (PDMP) FHIR Implementation Guide. The reporter observes that the diagrams are redundant and confusing because, in each case, there do not appear to be actual options for the entity acting as a requester or a responder. The reporter suggests it might be clearer to replace the diagrams with a few sentences noting that an EHR may or may not be involved, information which the reporter believes is already stated elsewhere in the text. The ticket was originally resolved as "Considered for Future Use" in January 2023 (vote: John Hatem / Tim McNeil, 4-0-0) when the IG was withdrawn, but was subsequently reopened in November 2023 when the PDMP on FHIR project (PSS-2225) was relaunched.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested
#### Proposal
Remove or consolidate the redundant diagrams in figures 5A and 5B of the PDMP FHIR IG. Replace them with concise narrative text explaining that the requester role may or may not involve an EHR, and similarly for the responder. Retain any unique information from the diagrams in textual form to ensure no content is lost.
#### Justification
The reporter's observation is reasonable: if the diagrams do not convey distinct architectural options beyond the presence or absence of an EHR intermediary, they add visual complexity without informational value. Simplifying the content improves readability and reduces maintenance burden on the IG. The original ballot comment was accepted by implied consensus before the IG was withdrawn, and the concern remains valid now that the project has been relaunched.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach
#### Proposal
Rather than removing the diagrams entirely, consolidate figures 5A and 5B into a single simplified diagram that clearly shows the optional presence of an EHR in the request/response flow. Annotate the diagram with a brief legend or note explaining the optional components. Update surrounding text to reference the single consolidated figure.
#### Justification
Diagrams can be valuable for implementers who benefit from visual representations of workflows. A single, well-designed diagram that clearly marks optional components (e.g., using dashed lines or annotations for the EHR) would address the redundancy concern while preserving the visual aid. This approach balances the reporter's feedback about confusion with the potential value of retaining a graphical overview.

---

### Disposition C: Decline
#### Proposal
Decline the request as not persuasive. The diagrams in figures 5A and 5B serve to illustrate distinct deployment scenarios and should be retained as-is.
#### Justification
Architectural diagrams, even when depicting similar flows, can help implementers understand the intended deployment topologies. Different figures may be referenced independently in different contexts. However, this justification is weakened by the reporter's specific observation that the diagrams do not actually depict meaningfully different options, and the ticket carries a "Highest" priority designation. The previous 4-0-0 vote to defer (not reject) also suggests the workgroup saw merit in the concern.

---

### Recommendation
**Recommended disposition:** B — Alternative Approach

Consolidating the diagrams into a single clear figure best addresses the reporter's concern while preserving the value of a visual workflow representation. The reporter's core complaint is about redundancy and confusion, not about the existence of diagrams per se. A single well-annotated diagram would eliminate the redundancy, reduce confusion, and still serve implementers who benefit from graphical depictions of the PDMP request/response architecture. This approach also aligns with the spirit of the original feedback — that the information could be conveyed more simply — without discarding the diagrammatic format entirely. Given that the PDMP IG has been relaunched under PSS-2225, this is an appropriate time to make such improvements to the specification's clarity.
