## Description

The submitter reports that the formatting in the PDMP Responder requirements section is broken, rendering the text unclear. The existing wording is a run-on sentence where what should be a bulleted list of required data elements has collapsed into a single paragraph:

> The PDMP Responder SHALL collect the following information about each PDMP Rqquestor which will be used for both auditing and for identifying the requestor uniquely. \*\* Requestor Identity namely name \*\* Requestor Role \*\* Requestor DEA, NPI Number \*\* Requestor Facility information

The `**` markers were likely intended as bullet points (or bold formatting delimiters) but were not properly rendered, making the four required data elements (Requestor Identity/name, Requestor Role, Requestor DEA/NPI Number, Requestor Facility information) difficult to distinguish from the surrounding prose.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive

**Rationale:** The formatting issue is clear and undeniable — the bulleted list has collapsed into an unreadable run-on sentence. Reformat the paragraph so that the four required requestor data elements are presented as a proper bulleted or numbered list. This should be resolved in conjunction with the overlapping tickets (FHIR-29477 for removing "namely", FHIR-29456/FHIR-29491 for the "Rqquestor" typo) to produce a clean, corrected paragraph such as:

> The PDMP Responder SHALL collect the following information about each PDMP Requestor, which will be used for both auditing and for identifying the requestor uniquely:
> - Requestor Identity (name)
> - Requestor Role
> - Requestor DEA, NPI Number
> - Requestor Facility information

**Pros:** Directly fixes the reported problem; improves readability; aligns with already-applied typo corrections (FHIR-29456, FHIR-29491).
**Cons:** The proposed wording above is illustrative — the workgroup should confirm the exact list items and whether "namely name" should be retained or removed (see FHIR-29477).

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification

**Rationale:** Accept the formatting fix but expand the scope to address the broader editorial concerns raised in FHIR-29492 (copy editing) and FHIR-29477 ("namely name" removal). The workgroup would consolidate FHIR-29475, FHIR-29477, and FHIR-29492 into a single editorial pass over the PDMP Responder requirements section, ensuring the list is properly formatted, the "Rqquestor" typo is corrected, the "namely name" wording is clarified, and the overall section reads clearly.

**Pros:** Comprehensive fix addressing all overlapping issues at once; avoids piecemeal edits to the same paragraph.
**Cons:** Broader scope may delay resolution; requires coordination with multiple ticket dispositions.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive

**Rationale:** The IG was previously withdrawn and the formatting issues stem from original ballot comment import artifacts. If the PDMP on FHIR IG is being substantially rewritten under PSS-2225, all formatting will be redone as part of the new publication, making this specific fix unnecessary.

**Pros:** Avoids spending workgroup time on formatting that will be superseded by a new IG version.
**Cons:** The ticket was explicitly reopened when PSS-2225 reactivated the IG, suggesting the workgroup intends to address legacy ballot comments; dismissing it may leave the formatting broken in interim publications.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Persuasive** — The formatting defect is straightforward and self-evident. The bulleted list lost its structure and became unreadable. This should be fixed by reformatting the four requestor data elements as a proper list. The overlapping content issues (typo "Rqquestor" from FHIR-29456/FHIR-29491, "namely name" from FHIR-29477) have either already been resolved or should be addressed in their own tickets. Accepting this as Persuasive provides a clean resolution for the formatting concern without overloading this ticket's scope.
