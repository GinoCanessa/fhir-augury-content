## Description

Josh Mandel requests that the scope description of the PDMP FHIR IG be generalized. Currently the IG states it defines how clinical systems can access a Patient's controlled substance prescription history "from the State PDMP systems." The suggestion is to describe this as accessing data from **any** PDMP system, rather than tying the scope to State-specific infrastructure. The rationale is that over time, different infrastructure may evolve (e.g., federal, multi-state, or private PDMP systems), and the IG should remain applicable regardless of the underlying PDMP architecture.

## Analysis

This ticket is one of several legacy ballot comments (labeled `PDMP-old-issues`) originally filed against the **US Medication FHIR Implementation Guide** during its 2020 ballot. The IG was subsequently withdrawn and these issues were deferred. In late 2023, the PDMP on FHIR project (PSS-2225) was re-established, and these tickets were reopened for reconsideration.

**Key context:** Since this ticket was filed, the PDMP effort has evolved into the separate **US Prescription Drug Monitoring Program (PDMP) FHIR IG** (now published as STU1 as of October 2024). The newer PDMP IG (specification: "US Prescription Drug Monitoring Program (PDMP) (FHIR)") has already gone through ballot and publication. The scope language in the published IG may have already been revised as part of that process — particularly given that FHIR-46046 (Persuasive with Modification, Published) directly addressed how the IG fits into the broader PDMP ecosystem.

The original specification this ticket targets ("US Medication FHIR Implementation Guide") appears to be the withdrawn predecessor. The concern raised — generalizing "State PDMP systems" to "any PDMP system" — remains conceptually valid, but the applicable specification and its scope language have likely changed substantially.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive — Apply to Current PDMP IG
**Recommendation:** Accept the comment and update the scope description in the current US PDMP FHIR IG to use technology-neutral language such as "any PDMP system" rather than "State PDMP systems." This future-proofs the IG against evolving PDMP infrastructure (multi-state hubs, federal systems, private aggregators) while remaining accurate for the current state-centric landscape.

**Proposed wording change:** Replace references to "State PDMP systems" in scope/introductory text with "PDMP systems" or "PDMP data sources" — removing the State-specific qualifier while retaining the US Realm focus of the IG.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification — Already Addressed in Published PDMP IG
**Recommendation:** Mark as Persuasive with Modification, noting that the published US PDMP FHIR IG (STU1, October 2024) has already addressed the spirit of this request through its revised scope language and the disposition of FHIR-46046. Verify that the current published text no longer ties scope exclusively to "State PDMP systems." If confirmed, close with a reference to the updated IG text.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive — State-Specific Scope is Intentional
**Recommendation:** Reject on the grounds that the US PDMP ecosystem is currently State-operated by design, and the IG accurately describes this reality. Generalizing to "any PDMP system" could introduce ambiguity about the intended deployment context. The IG should describe the current landscape; future infrastructure changes can be addressed in future versions.

## Recommendation

**Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification — Already Addressed in Published PDMP IG**

This is recommended because: (1) the ticket was filed in 2020 against a since-withdrawn IG; (2) the PDMP FHIR IG has been completely reworked and published as STU1 in October 2024; (3) the closely related FHIR-46046 (which directly addressed the IG's fit within the PDMP ecosystem) was resolved as Persuasive with Modification and is Published; and (4) the scope language has likely already been updated as part of the new IG development. The work group should verify that the published STU1 text no longer uses "State PDMP systems" as the sole scope qualifier, and if so, close this ticket with a reference to the updated language.
