## Summary

The ticket requests that the UDI Domain Analysis Model (DAM) and UDI Pattern Implementation Guide (IG) be referenced in the "Relationship to Other Projects & Guides" section of the Point of Care Devices (PoCD) FHIR Implementation Guide. The submitter recommends that these UDI-related documents, as well as any future related IG documents, be formally referenced. The ticket was originally accepted in 2018 with a 9-0-0 vote, then adjusted to "Not Persuasive" and deferred in 2019. In March–April 2026, the workgroup voted 13-0-1 to reopen the ticket, reverting the previous "Not Persuasive" resolution for further discussion. A commenter has noted this ticket may be a duplicate of FHIR-15359.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested
#### Proposal
Add explicit references to the UDI Domain Analysis Model (DAM) and UDI Pattern Implementation Guide in the "Relationship to Other Projects & Guides" section of the PoCD FHIR IG home page. Include a note that future related IG documents should also be referenced as they become available.
#### Justification
The original 2018 vote (9-0-0) accepted this change, and UDI fields have since been added to the Device resource post-STU3, making the reference even more relevant. The 2026 reopening vote (13-0-1) confirms continued workgroup interest. Adding cross-references to related UDI documents improves discoverability and helps implementers understand the broader UDI ecosystem.

---

### Disposition B: Consolidate with FHIR-15359 as Duplicate
#### Proposal
Mark FHIR-15358 as a duplicate of FHIR-15359 (or vice versa), consolidate discussion into a single ticket, and address both requests together. The surviving ticket should determine whether and where UDI DAM and UDI Pattern IG references should appear — whether in the Related Projects section, in mapping pages, or both.
#### Justification
Both tickets were filed by the same reporter on the same date, request essentially the same change, and have followed the same resolution history. Erik Moll has already flagged FHIR-15358 as a duplicate of FHIR-15359. Consolidating avoids redundant discussion and ensures a single, coherent resolution. Additionally, mcros's comment on FHIR-15359 suggests a broader approach (covering UDI mapping in DIM and v2 mapping pages) that could encompass both requests.

---

### Disposition C: Decline
#### Proposal
Close the ticket as not persuasive. The UDI DAM is a separate project and does not need to be referenced in the PoCD IG's Related Projects section.
#### Justification
As mcros noted, there is "no need to refer to the UDI DAM project under Related Project on home page." UDI support fields have already been added to the Device resource, and the PoCD IG's scope is point-of-care device communication, not UDI regulatory compliance. Adding references to every tangentially related IG creates maintenance burden. Implementers needing UDI information can find it through the UDI-specific IGs directly.

---

### Recommendation
**Recommended disposition:** B — Consolidate with FHIR-15359 as Duplicate

FHIR-15358 and FHIR-15359 are nearly identical requests filed by the same reporter on the same day, and they have followed parallel resolution paths through two cycles of acceptance, deferral, and reopening. Erik Moll has already identified the duplication. Consolidating into a single ticket will focus the workgroup's discussion and avoid the risk of contradictory resolutions. The surviving ticket should incorporate mcros's suggestion from FHIR-15359 to consider broader UDI mapping coverage (in DIM and v2 mapping pages) rather than limiting the change to just the Related Projects section on the home page. This approach respects the workgroup's clear intent to address UDI references (demonstrated by the 13-0-1 reopening vote) while streamlining the resolution process.
