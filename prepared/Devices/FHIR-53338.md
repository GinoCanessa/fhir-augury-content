## Description

The reporter identifies an inaccuracy in **Section 3.2** of the PHD IG. The current text states:

> "In some sense, the IEEE 11073-10206 ACOM objects are analogous to FHIR resources, and the attributes are analogous to FHIR resource elements"

The reporter argues this characterization is incorrect — Devices on FHIR was actually designed on the models of **IEEE 11073-10201**, and therefore there is a **direct equivalency** (not merely an analogy) between ACOM objects/attributes and FHIR resources/elements.

## Analysis

The core issue is whether Section 3.2's characterization of the relationship between IEEE 11073-10206 ACOM objects and FHIR resources is accurate. The current text uses hedging language ("in some sense… analogous") whereas the reporter asserts there is a **direct equivalency** because Devices on FHIR was designed based on the IEEE 11073-10201 models.

This is a factual/technical correction about the design lineage of the PHD IG. The distinction matters because:

1. **"Analogous" implies loose similarity** — suggesting implementers should not expect exact mappings.
2. **"Direct equivalency" based on 10201** — implies intentional design alignment, giving implementers stronger confidence in mapping fidelity.

The reporter (MalcolmClarke) has submitted a cluster of related tickets (FHIR-53325 through FHIR-53365) all targeting Section 3 of the PHD IG, suggesting deep expertise with the IEEE 11073 family of standards. Previously resolved FHIR-48140 (Persuasive) already corrected ACOM terminology in the same section, establishing a pattern of accepting clarifications to this part of the IG.

The ticket was originally deferred to the next PHD IG revision, then re-opened post STU2 publication for fresh consideration.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive
**Action:** Accept the correction. Revise Section 3.2 to replace the "analogous" language with text that accurately describes the direct design lineage from IEEE 11073-10201 models, clarifying that ACOM objects and attributes have a direct equivalency to FHIR resources and elements by design intent.

**Rationale:** The reporter's assertion about the design history is a factual claim about how Devices on FHIR was built. If the PHD IG was indeed designed on the 10201 models, then "analogous" is an understatement that could mislead implementers. This aligns with the precedent set by FHIR-48140 (accepted ACOM terminology corrections in the same section) and the broader cluster of Section 3 clarifications being submitted.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification
**Action:** Accept the intent of the correction but modify the proposed wording. Rather than asserting "direct equivalency," use more precise language such as: *"The Devices on FHIR mapping was designed based on the IEEE 11073-10201 data model. As a result, ACOM objects map directly to FHIR resources, and ACOM attributes map directly to FHIR resource elements."* This acknowledges the design lineage while being specific about the nature of the relationship.

**Rationale:** While the reporter's point about design lineage is valid, "direct equivalency" may overstate the relationship in cases where FHIR-specific constraints or extensions create divergence. A more nuanced statement that references the 10201 basis while describing the directness of the mapping is more technically precise and avoids implying a 1:1 mathematical equivalence.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive
**Action:** Reject the change. Retain the current "analogous" language in Section 3.2.

**Rationale:** The current language is intentionally imprecise as an introductory framing for readers unfamiliar with IEEE 11073. While the design was informed by 10201, the mapping between ACOM and FHIR is not a strict equivalence — FHIR resources have constraints, extensions, and structural differences that make "analogous" a safer characterization for a general audience. Changing to "direct equivalency" could create false expectations about mapping completeness.

## Recommendation

**Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification**

The reporter raises a valid point about the design lineage of the PHD IG, and the current "in some sense… analogous" hedging understates the intentional design relationship. However, claiming "direct equivalency" without qualification risks overpromising mapping fidelity. The best outcome is to acknowledge the IEEE 11073-10201 design basis explicitly while describing the mapping as "direct" rather than merely "analogous" — but with enough precision to avoid implying perfect 1:1 equivalence. This approach is consistent with the accepted clarifications in FHIR-48140 and the broader set of Section 3 improvements being tracked.
