## Summary

The reporter identifies an inconsistency in Section 3.3 of the Personal Healthcare Devices (PHD) Implementation Guide regarding the handling of relative time. The ticket argues that relative time is intended to be independent of external time references and exists to enable accurate determination of intervals between events. Mapping relative time to UTC may cause a loss of precision, and relative time should instead be represented simply as a quantity (e.g., microseconds). The current guidance to map relative time to UTC contradicts later sections of the IG that treat relative time differently. The ticket was previously deferred to the next revision of the PHD IG and has since been re-opened post STU2 publication for consideration.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested
#### Proposal
Revise Section 3.3 of the PHD IG to remove the instruction to map relative time values to UTC. Instead, represent relative time as a simple quantity in microseconds (using Observation.valueQuantity with UCUM unit "us" for microseconds), consistent with how relative time is treated in later sections of the IG (e.g., Section 2.8). Update any mapping guidance to clearly distinguish between absolute timestamps (which map to dateTime) and relative timestamps (which map to Quantity). Harmonize the guidance across all sections (3.3, 2.8, 2.9.7) so that relative time is consistently treated as an external-time-independent interval measurement.
#### Justification
Relative time in IEEE 11073 PHD is fundamentally different from absolute time — it represents a device-internal counter (at 125 µs or 1 µs resolution) for measuring intervals between events, not a wall-clock timestamp. Mapping it to UTC introduces potential accuracy loss and is semantically incorrect. The reporter correctly identifies that the IG already treats relative time as a quantity in other sections, making Section 3.3's UTC mapping guidance internally inconsistent. This aligns with the related cluster of tickets (FHIR-53349, FHIR-53353) that all point to the same fundamental inconsistency, and with the resolution of FHIR-48183 which clarified tick resolution for relative time.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — Clarify Section 3.3 While Preserving UTC Conversion as Optional
#### Proposal
Revise Section 3.3 to clarify that relative time SHOULD primarily be represented as a Quantity in microseconds. Add guidance that when a PHG can determine the relationship between the device's relative time clock and UTC (e.g., via a coincident timestamp), it MAY optionally provide a derived UTC timestamp as a secondary representation. Make it explicit that the primary representation is the raw relative time value and that any UTC conversion is approximate and for convenience only. Add a note explaining the accuracy implications of UTC conversion.
#### Justification
Some implementers may find it useful to have an approximate UTC timestamp derived from relative time for display or ordering purposes, even if it is less precise than the raw value. By making UTC conversion optional rather than required, the IG preserves implementer flexibility while addressing the accuracy concern. This approach also provides a migration path for existing implementations that may already rely on UTC-mapped relative times, while steering new implementations toward the more accurate quantity representation.

---

### Disposition C: Decline
#### Proposal
Decline this request on the basis that the current guidance, while imperfect, provides a workable approach and that the broader inconsistencies across sections should be addressed holistically in a future ballot cycle rather than through piecemeal corrections.
#### Justification
The ticket identifies a real inconsistency, but the scope of changes needed to fully harmonize relative time handling across Sections 2.8, 2.9.7, and 3.3 is significant and touches multiple related tickets (FHIR-53349, FHIR-53353). Addressing only Section 3.3 in isolation could introduce new inconsistencies. However, this rationale is weakened by the fact that the ticket was already deferred once (from STU2) and re-opened specifically for the next revision, suggesting the work group has acknowledged the need to address it.

---

### Recommendation
**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A is recommended because the technical argument is sound and well-supported by both the IEEE 11073 standard's intent for relative time and the IG's own internal inconsistencies. Relative time is a device-internal counter for measuring intervals — it has no inherent relationship to wall-clock time, and forcing a UTC mapping both misrepresents the data and risks precision loss. The IG already treats relative time as a quantity in later sections (as the reporter notes), so accepting this correction would make Section 3.3 consistent with the rest of the document. Additionally, this ticket is part of a coherent cluster of related corrections from the same reporter (FHIR-53349, FHIR-53353) that all address the same fundamental issue of inconsistent relative time handling. The work group has already reviewed this once and deferred it to the next PHD IG revision, which is now underway. Accepting the correction would resolve a known inconsistency cleanly and align the entire IG around a single, technically correct approach to relative time representation.
