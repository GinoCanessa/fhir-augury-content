## Description

The reporter requests that Section 3.4 of the PHD IG give greater emphasis to the support for proprietary devices, noting these constitute the majority of the market. The suggestion is to better promote the intermediate role of the gateway in mapping proprietary device data to consistent, compliant messages using IEEE 11073-10206 and IEEE 11073-10101. The reporter proposes enhancing the existing diagram showing proprietary devices feeding the gateway to illustrate this convergence, and notes personal experience mapping 10 proprietary devices (including Bluetooth profiles and GHS) to the architecture.

## Analysis

This ticket is one of a cluster of related items (FHIR-53326, FHIR-53328, FHIR-53338, FHIR-53340, FHIR-53341) filed by MalcolmClarke on the same date, all targeting Section 3 of the PHD IG. They collectively argue that the IG underrepresents the real-world dominance of proprietary devices and should better articulate the gateway's role in achieving standards compliance.

FHIR-53326 specifically focuses on the gateway convergence concept — the idea that proprietary devices feed into a gateway that maps their data to IEEE 11073-10206 ACOM-compliant objects, which then produce FHIR resources. The reporter has practical experience mapping 10 proprietary devices this way and wants a diagram to illustrate this flow.

The ticket was previously resolved as "Considered for Future Use" after the 2025-11-12 Devices WG call, but was re-opened by lynn_laakso on 2026-03-31 post STU2 publication for reconsideration in the next revision.

The closely related FHIR-53328 proposes specific replacement wording for Section 3.4, suggesting a more positive framing: "Proprietary devices are supported by this IG by mapping the observations, clock, and power to the objects of IEEE 11073-10206."

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive with Modification
**Action:** Accept the intent of the request but scope the change to adding a brief clarifying paragraph and an enhanced diagram in Section 3.4 that illustrates the gateway convergence pattern for proprietary devices. Coordinate with FHIR-53328 which proposes specific replacement wording for the same section.

**Rationale:** The reporter raises a valid point — proprietary devices are the majority of the market, and the IG should better communicate that the gateway architecture supports them. However, the change should be carefully scoped to avoid overpromising interoperability for arbitrary proprietary devices. Combining this with FHIR-53328's proposed wording provides a complete editorial improvement. The diagram enhancement adds practical value for implementers understanding the architecture.

### Disposition B: Persuasive
**Action:** Accept the request as submitted — enhance Section 3.4 with greater emphasis on proprietary device support and add/improve the gateway convergence diagram.

**Rationale:** The reporter has demonstrated real-world experience mapping proprietary devices to the architecture. The request is straightforward editorial improvement that would benefit implementers without changing normative content. The diagram addition is a low-risk, high-value enhancement.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive
**Action:** Decline the change. The current text in Section 3.4 already acknowledges proprietary device support, and adding greater emphasis risks implying a level of guaranteed interoperability that the IG cannot ensure for arbitrary proprietary devices.

**Rationale:** The existing text in Section 3.4 does mention proprietary devices and SPP/Bluetooth SIG device mapping. Over-emphasizing this support could set incorrect expectations. The architecture diagram is already present; further enhancement may be better handled through implementation guides or supplementary materials rather than the core IG.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Persuasive with Modification** — This ticket should be accepted with modification and coordinated with FHIR-53328. The reporter's core point is valid: proprietary devices dominate the market and the IG should better communicate gateway-based mapping support. However, the changes should be carefully scoped — updating the Section 3.4 language (using FHIR-53328's proposed wording as a starting point) and enhancing the gateway convergence diagram, while avoiding language that implies guaranteed interoperability for all proprietary devices. The fact that this ticket was re-opened post-STU2 publication indicates the WG recognizes its value for the next revision.
