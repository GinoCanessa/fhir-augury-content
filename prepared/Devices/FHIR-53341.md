## Description

In Section 3.2 of the PHD IG, the text states:

> "In the IEEE 11073-10206 Abstract Information Content Model (ACOM), IEEE 11073-10101 nomenclature codes are used to indicate what the items are."

The reporter questions what "items" refers to — attributes? objects? The reporter also notes that protocols such as GHS, BLE, and proprietary protocols do not use these codes directly. The suggestion is to replace "items" with **"coded values"** such as type and unit, which more precisely describes what the nomenclature codes identify.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive (Recommended)

**Action:** Replace the ambiguous term "items" with "coded values" in Section 3.2.

**Rationale:** The term "items" is vague in this context and could refer to attributes, objects, or other constructs. The reporter correctly identifies that "coded values" (specifically type and unit codes) is what the IEEE 11073-10101 nomenclature codes actually identify. This is a straightforward terminology clarification that improves readability and precision without changing the technical meaning. The prior resolution of FHIR-48140 (Persuasive) demonstrates the WG's willingness to improve ACOM-related terminology. The ticket was previously deferred to the next PHD IG revision and has now been re-opened post STU2 publication, making it timely to address.

**Proposed Change:** In Section 3.2, replace:
> "IEEE 11073-10101 nomenclature codes are used to indicate what the items are"

with:
> "IEEE 11073-10101 nomenclature codes are used to identify coded values such as type and unit"

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification

**Action:** Accept the clarification but use broader replacement text that also addresses protocol context.

**Rationale:** The reporter notes that protocols such as GHS, BLE, and proprietary do not use these codes directly. A more complete fix could acknowledge that the nomenclature codes define the semantic meaning at the ACOM level, regardless of the transport protocol. This would address both the ambiguity of "items" and the protocol concern in a single edit. However, this broader scope may overlap with related tickets (FHIR-53340, FHIR-53339) and should be coordinated.

**Proposed Change:** Revise the sentence to clarify both the terminology and the protocol-agnostic nature, e.g.:
> "In the IEEE 11073-10206 ACOM, IEEE 11073-10101 nomenclature codes identify coded values such as measurement type and unit, independent of the underlying transport protocol."

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive

**Action:** Reject the change, retaining the current wording.

**Rationale:** The term "items" could be considered sufficiently generic in context and readers familiar with IEEE 11073-10206 would understand the reference. Changing it might create inconsistency with other parts of the specification or with the IEEE 11073 standards themselves if "items" is used as a recognized term there. However, this rationale is weak given that the reporter (who is familiar with the IEEE standards) finds the term confusing.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Persuasive** — The change is a clear, low-risk terminology improvement. Replacing "items" with "coded values such as type and unit" removes genuine ambiguity and aligns with the reporter's domain expertise. This ticket is part of a cluster of Section 3.2 clarifications from the same reporter (FHIR-53340, FHIR-53338, FHIR-53339) that the WG may wish to address together, but the fix for this ticket stands independently. The successful precedent of FHIR-48140 (Persuasive) for similar ACOM clarifications supports acceptance.
