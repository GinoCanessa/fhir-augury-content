## Description

The reporter identifies confusing language in **Section 3.4** of the PHD IG. The specific text reads:

> "In general, if a PHD provides timestamps with its measurements, it needs to provide a means for the PHG to get its sense of current time. If it does, one can likely use this implementation guide with that device."

The reporter states this is:
1. **Confusing** — the phrasing is unclear and awkward ("one can likely use this implementation guide").
2. **Contradictory** — it conflicts with earlier statements in the section.
3. **Not required** — the capability described should not be stated as a hard requirement.

The reporter proposes either **removing the sentence entirely** or softening it to use "should provide" instead of "needs to provide."

## Analysis

This ticket addresses editorial clarity in the PHD IG's Section 3.4. The problematic sentence uses prescriptive language ("needs to provide") for a capability that the reporter argues should be recommended rather than required. The sentence also includes vague phrasing ("one can likely use this implementation guide") that undermines its own authority.

This ticket is closely related to **FHIR-53327**, which addresses the **preceding sentence** in the same section with the same concern — changing "requires" to "recommends." The two tickets together form a coherent request to soften the requirement language around PHDs reporting their sense of current time.

The ticket was previously deferred to the next PHD IG revision during the 2025-11-12 Devices call but has been re-opened post STU2 publication by lynn_laakso, indicating it is now in scope for action.

Historical precedent exists: **FHIR-23748** was resolved as "Persuasive with Modification" for a similar wording improvement in the same PHD IG area.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive — Remove the Sentence

**Action:** Remove the problematic sentence entirely from Section 3.4.

**Rationale:** The sentence is redundant with the preceding statement (addressed in FHIR-53327) and adds confusion rather than clarity. The earlier text in the section already conveys the relationship between PHD timestamps and PHG time synchronization. Removing this sentence eliminates both the contradictory language and the vague "one can likely" phrasing. This aligns with the reporter's primary suggestion.

**Impact:** Minimal — removes one sentence of editorial text; no structural or normative change.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification — Soften to "Should"

**Action:** Replace the sentence with softened, clearer language, e.g.:
> "In general, if a PHD provides timestamps with its measurements, it should provide a means for the PHG to obtain its sense of current time, enabling accurate time translation."

**Rationale:** Retains the guidance value of the sentence while removing the prescriptive "needs to" and the confusing "one can likely" phrasing. Uses normative "should" to indicate recommendation without mandate. This preserves the intent of the original text.

**Impact:** Minor editorial change — one sentence reworded. No structural impact.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive — Keep Current Text

**Action:** Retain the sentence as-is.

**Rationale:** The existing text, while imperfect in phrasing, conveys an important point about PHD capability expectations. The requirement for PHDs to provide time access is foundational to the IG's timestamp correction mechanism. Softening or removing it could lead implementers to omit this capability.

**Impact:** None — no change.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Persuasive — Remove the Sentence.**

The sentence is genuinely confusing and partially redundant with the preceding text in Section 3.4. Removing it is the cleanest editorial fix. The closely related **FHIR-53327** should be handled in conjunction, as it addresses the preceding sentence's requirement language. Together, these two tickets can produce a coherent revision of Section 3.4's requirement/recommendation posture. The historical precedent of FHIR-23748 being accepted as "Persuasive with Modification" for similar wording improvements in the same IG supports making this change. The re-opening post STU2 publication confirms the WG considers this in scope.
