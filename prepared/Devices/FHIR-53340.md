## Description

The reporter identifies that in **Section 3.2** of the Personal Healthcare Devices (PHD) IG, the text states that "all PHDs based upon the IEEE 11073-10206 model … can be handled by the PHG without requiring the PHG to view the values of the attributes." The reporter argues that the greater point is that the **objects created in the PHG** (Personal Healthcare Gateway) can be mapped to FHIR, **irrespective of the source of the PHD** (Personal Healthcare Device). In actuality, IEEE 11073-10206 defines the objects and nomenclature **in the PHG**, rather than necessarily defining the PHD itself. The terminology should be corrected to refer to PHG where appropriate.

## Analysis

This ticket is part of a **cluster of related Technical Corrections** (FHIR-53328, 53339, 53340, 53341, 53342) filed by MalcolmClarke on the same date, all targeting Section 3 of the PHD IG and all focused on improving the precision and clarity of IEEE 11073-10206 ACOM terminology. The core argument here is technically sound: IEEE 11073-10206 defines the Abstract Content Model (ACOM) for the gateway (PHG) side, and the section text should reflect that the objects and nomenclature are defined in the PHG context rather than attributing them solely to the PHD.

The ticket was previously deferred to the next revision after Devices WG review in November 2025, but has since been **re-opened post STU2 publication** by lynn_laakso, signaling that the WG is now ready to address it in the next cycle.

The related ticket FHIR-48236 (Persuasive with Modification, now Published) demonstrates that the WG has previously accepted substantive corrections to PHD/PHG profiling, setting a precedent for this type of terminology refinement.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive
**Action:** Accept the correction as-is. Update Section 3.2 of the PHD IG to replace references to "PHD" with "PHG" where the text is describing objects and nomenclature defined by IEEE 11073-10206 ACOM in the gateway context. Specifically, revise the sentence to clarify that the ACOM objects created in the PHG can be mapped to FHIR irrespective of the PHD source, and that 10206 defines the PHG-side model.

**Rationale:** The reporter's technical point is accurate — IEEE 11073-10206 defines the abstract content model for the gateway, not the device. Correcting the terminology improves precision and aligns the IG text with the actual IEEE standard semantics. This is a straightforward technical correction consistent with the ticket type.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification
**Action:** Accept the intent but broaden the scope to address this terminology correction alongside the related cluster of tickets (FHIR-53339, 53341, 53342) that all target Section 3.2–3.3 terminology. Perform a holistic review of Section 3 to ensure consistent and accurate use of PHG vs PHD, objects, attributes, observations, and nomenclature throughout.

**Rationale:** The reporter filed five closely related tickets on the same day, all addressing overlapping terminology issues in Section 3. Addressing them individually risks piecemeal edits that may introduce new inconsistencies. A consolidated pass through Section 3 would be more efficient and yield a more coherent result.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive
**Action:** Reject the change. The current text, while imprecise, is sufficiently understood by the target audience (PHD IG implementers familiar with IEEE 11073-10206), and the distinction between PHG and PHD in this context does not cause implementation errors.

**Rationale:** The existing wording has been in place since earlier STU versions without reported implementation issues. The PHG/PHD distinction in this sentence is a matter of editorial precision rather than technical correctness that would affect implementers.

## Recommendation

**Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification** is recommended. The reporter's correction is technically valid and should be accepted. However, given that this ticket is one of five closely related terminology corrections targeting the same sections, the workgroup should coordinate the editorial changes across FHIR-53339, 53340, 53341, 53342 (and potentially 53328) to ensure a consistent and coherent rewrite of Section 3.2–3.3 in the next PHD IG revision. This approach avoids redundant review cycles and ensures the terminology improvements are applied holistically.
