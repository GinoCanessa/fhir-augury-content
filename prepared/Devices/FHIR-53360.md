## Description

The reporter identifies a grammar error in **Section 2.10** of the Personal Healthcare Devices (PHD) Implementation Guide. The sentence currently reads:

> "The PHG Device Resource**,** can, in theory have"

The proposed correction is:

> "The PHG Device Resource can**,** in theory**,** have"

The fix removes a misplaced comma after "Resource" and adds a missing comma after "theory" to properly set off the parenthetical phrase "in theory."

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Applied — Technical Correction (Recommended ✅)

**Resolution:** Apply the grammar fix as submitted.

**Rationale:** This is a straightforward, unambiguous editorial correction. The existing text has two misplaced commas that disrupt readability. The proposed change is grammatically correct and does not alter any technical meaning. Both erikmoll (in comments) and lynn_laakso (by re-opening post-STU2) support the fix. As a Technical Correction, it requires no ballot and carries zero risk of breaking changes. There is no reason to defer further — the fix is trivial and should be applied immediately.

**Action:** In Section 2.10 of the PHD IG, change: `"The PHG Device Resource, can, in theory have"` → `"The PHG Device Resource can, in theory, have"`
