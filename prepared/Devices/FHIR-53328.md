## Description

The submitter requests a change to Section 3.4 of the PHD IG. The current text describes proprietary medical devices in a way the submitter considers "too negative" and includes an irrelevant final sentence about the guide writers' personal experience with SPP and Bluetooth SIG PHD devices.

**Current text (Section 3.4):**
> "In addition to PHDs implementing GHS or another Bluetooth PHD profile, there are numerous proprietary medical devices on the market. It may be possible to map the measurements coming from these devices to the PHD profiles from this IG, but that mapping would require that the resulting FHIR resources be created and populated as if they had come from an IEEE 11073-10206 ACOM compliant device. The writers of this guide have performed this task for a number of proprietary Serial Port Protocol (SPP) and Bluetooth SIG PHD devices."

**Proposed replacement:**
> "Proprietary devices are supported by this IG by mapping the observations, clock, and power to the objects of IEEE 11073-10206."

The change reframes proprietary device support positively rather than tentatively, and removes the self-referential sentence about the guide writers' experience.

## Analysis

### Context
This ticket is one of a batch of editorial corrections submitted by MalcolmClarke on 2025-11-05, all targeting the PHD IG's introductory sections (3.2–3.4). The ticket was initially resolved as "Considered for Future Use" and deferred to the next PHD IG revision per the Devices WG review on 2025-11-12. It was subsequently re-opened post STU2 publication by lynn_laakso on 2026-03-31.

### Core Issue
The existing Section 3.4 text uses hedging language ("It may be possible…", "but that mapping would require…") that understates proprietary device support. The submitter argues this is important because proprietary devices constitute the majority of the market. The proposed replacement text is direct and affirmative, clearly stating that proprietary devices are supported via mapping to IEEE 11073-10206 objects.

### Relationship to FHIR-53326
FHIR-53326 is closely related—it also targets Section 3.4 and requests greater emphasis on proprietary device support with a focus on the gateway's convergence role and a supporting diagram. The two tickets should be reviewed together; FHIR-53326 expands on the same theme with additional diagram and gateway emphasis.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Applied — Accept Proposed Replacement Text
**Action:** Replace the current Section 3.4 paragraph with the submitter's proposed text: "Proprietary devices are supported by this IG by mapping the observations, clock, and power to the objects of IEEE 11073-10206."

**Rationale:** The proposed text is concise, affirmative, and technically accurate. It removes the hedging language and the irrelevant self-referential sentence. This is a straightforward technical correction that improves clarity. The text should be reviewed alongside FHIR-53326 to ensure consistency with any additional diagram or gateway emphasis added.

**Impact:** Low — editorial change to a single paragraph in Section 3.4 with no structural or normative impact.

### Disposition B: Applied with Modification — Accept Intent but Expand Slightly
**Action:** Accept the spirit of the change but use slightly expanded replacement text that preserves the mapping context, e.g.: "Proprietary devices are also supported by this IG. Measurements from such devices can be mapped to FHIR resources by modeling their observations, clock, and power data as IEEE 11073-10206 ACOM objects within a Personal Health Gateway (PHG)."

**Rationale:** The proposed single-sentence replacement may be too terse for readers unfamiliar with the architecture. A slightly expanded version retains the positive framing while providing enough context about how the mapping works and where it occurs (the PHG). This also aligns with the related request in FHIR-53326 to emphasize the gateway's convergence role.

**Impact:** Low — editorial change to Section 3.4, slightly more text than Disposition A but still a single-paragraph correction.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive — Retain Current Text
**Action:** Leave the current Section 3.4 text unchanged.

**Rationale:** The existing text, while cautious in tone, is technically accurate. The hedging language ("It may be possible…") reflects genuine complexity in mapping proprietary devices. The sentence about the guide writers' experience provides practical validation. However, this would conflict with the WG's decision to re-open the ticket post STU2 for the next revision.

**Impact:** None.

## Recommendation

**Disposition B — Applied with Modification** is recommended. The submitter's core point is valid: the current text is unnecessarily negative about proprietary device support, and the self-referential sentence should be removed. However, the proposed single-sentence replacement is quite terse and drops useful context about the mapping mechanism and the PHG role. A slightly expanded version preserves the positive reframing while remaining informative. This should be coordinated with FHIR-53326, which requests additional emphasis and a diagram for the same section. The WG's decision to re-open this ticket post STU2 indicates intent to address it in the next revision.
