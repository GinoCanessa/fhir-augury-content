## Description

The reporter identifies that Section 3.3 of the PHD IG ("Observation objects in ACOM") uses imprecise language regarding the relationship between "observation objects" in a Personal Health Device (PHD) and their corresponding objects in the Personal Health Gateway (PHG). Specifically:

- A PHD generally contains one "observation object" that generates "observations."
- These observations must be mapped to objects in the PHG, which may be instantiated transiently or permanently depending on whether attribute values are repeated across observations.
- The current text does not clearly distinguish between the PHD-side observation objects and the PHG-side mapped objects, nor does it adequately explain the transient vs. permanent instantiation choice.

## Analysis

### Context
This ticket targets Section 3.3 of the Personal Healthcare Devices (PHD) IG, which describes how IEEE 11073-10206 ACOM observation objects relate to FHIR resources. The current text conflates PHD-side concepts (observation objects that generate measurement data) with PHG-side concepts (mapped objects that may persist across observations). This is part of a broader pattern of terminological imprecision in Section 3 identified by the reporter across multiple tickets.

### History
The ticket was originally filed 2025-11-05 and was reviewed by the Devices WG on 2025-11-12, where it was resolved as "Considered for Future Use" and deferred to the next PHD IG revision. On 2026-03-31, lynn_laakso reverted that resolution and re-opened the ticket post STU2 publication, indicating the WG now intends to address it.

### Impact
- **Scope**: Narrow — affects only Section 3.3 narrative text in the PHD IG.
- **Technical Risk**: Low — this is a documentation/terminology clarification, not a structural or conformance change.
- **Interdependencies**: Should be considered alongside the related cluster of Section 3 clarity tickets (FHIR-53339, 53340, 53341) as they share overlapping terminology concerns and could benefit from a coordinated editorial pass.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Applied — Clarify with targeted editorial revision
**Action**: Revise Section 3.3 to explicitly distinguish between (1) the observation object in the PHD that generates measurement data, and (2) the corresponding mapped object instantiated in the PHG. Clarify that PHG-side objects may be transient (created per observation) or persistent (retained across observations when attribute values are not repeated). Use consistent terminology aligned with IEEE 11073-10206 ACOM.

**Rationale**: The request is a straightforward technical correction to improve clarity. The text currently conflates PHD-side and PHG-side concepts in a way that could confuse implementers. The WG has already reviewed this once and has now reopened it for action post STU2.

### Disposition B: Applied — Coordinate with related Section 3 tickets
**Action**: Accept this ticket as part of a coordinated editorial revision of Section 3 (along with FHIR-53339, 53340, 53341, and potentially others). Perform a comprehensive terminology pass across Sections 3.2–3.3 to consistently distinguish "observation objects" (PHD), "mapped objects" (PHG), "attributes," "types," and "coded values." Address all related tickets in a single editorial change.

**Rationale**: The cluster of related tickets all stem from the same underlying problem — inconsistent use of ACOM terminology in Section 3. A coordinated fix would be more efficient and produce a more coherent result than individual patches.

### Disposition C: Deferred — Wait for next PHD IG ballot cycle
**Action**: Defer to the next PHD IG ballot cycle, keeping the ticket triaged for future action.

**Rationale**: While the clarification is valid, it is editorial in nature and does not affect conformance or interoperability. If the WG has higher-priority normative changes to address first, this could be deferred. However, given that the ticket was already deferred once and then re-opened, further deferral may frustrate the reporter.

## Recommendation

**Disposition B: Applied — Coordinate with related Section 3 tickets.**

This ticket is one of several closely related clarity issues in Section 3 of the PHD IG, all filed by the same reporter and all targeting imprecise ACOM terminology. Addressing them together in a coordinated editorial pass will produce a more coherent and consistent result. The WG has already reviewed and deferred this once; re-opening it post STU2 signals intent to act. A combined resolution is the most efficient path forward.
