## Summary

This ticket requests two text corrections in the QI-Core Encounter profile narrative that describes how QI-Core transitioned from using `Encounter.diagnosis` to the `Claim` profile for principal diagnosis, principal procedure, and present-on-admission (POA) determination. The changes were identified during the 3/6/2026 CQI call:

1. **Typo fix:** Change "differentiates" ŌåÆ "differentiate" (subjectŌĆōverb agreement).
2. **Remove "adjudicated":** Replace "the adjudicated determination" ŌåÆ "the determination" ŌĆö medical record coders provide the determination (not an adjudicated one) of principal diagnosis, principal procedure, and POA.

The ticket supplies both the current wording and the proposed updated wording in full.

## Details

### Change 1 ŌĆö Typo Correction

**Current text:**
> ŌĆ”Encounter.diagnosis.usevalue helped to **differentiates** if the diagnosis or procedure roleŌĆ”

**Proposed text:**
> ŌĆ”Encounter.diagnosis.usevalue helped to **differentiate** if the diagnosis or procedure roleŌĆ”

### Change 2 ŌĆö Remove "adjudicated"

**Current text:**
> Medical record coders review documentation and work with physicians to provide the **adjudicated** determination of what represents a *principal diagnosis*ŌĆ”

**Proposed text:**
> Medical record coders review documentation and work with physicians to provide **the determination** of what represents a *principal diagnosis*ŌĆ”

### Context

The paragraph being corrected explains why QI-Core STU7 no longer includes `Encounter.diagnosis` in the Key Element Table. Instead, QI-Core now relies on:
- `Claim.diagnosis.sequence`, `Claim.diagnosis.type`, `Claim.diagnosis.diagnosis[x]` for principal diagnosis
- `Claim.diagnosis.onAdmission` + `Claim.diagnosis.diagnosis[x]` for present-on-admission
- `Claim.procedure.sequence` + `Claim.procedure.procedure[x]` for principal procedure
- `Encounter.reasonCode` and `Encounter.reasonReference` (aligned with US Core 7.0.0) for encounter-managed diagnoses/procedures

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept (Persuasive)

Accept the two corrections as proposed:
1. Fix "differentiates" ŌåÆ "differentiate" (grammar).
2. Remove "adjudicated" and replace with "the determination."

**Rationale:** Both changes are clearly correct. The verb "differentiates" does not agree with the infinitive construction "helped toŌĆ”". The word "adjudicated" is inaccurate in context ŌĆö medical record coders provide a *coding* determination, not an adjudication (which implies a payer/claims processing step). The proposed wording was discussed and agreed on the 3/6/2026 CQI call. This is a straightforward technical correction with no semantic or structural impact on the profile itself.

### Disposition B: Accept with Broader Cleanup

Accept the two proposed corrections **and** direct the editors to review the same narrative block for any additional minor issues (e.g., "does not includes" ŌåÆ "does not include" in the final paragraph, or consistent use of linking text). This would clean up the entire section in one pass.

**Rationale:** Since the paragraph is already being edited, it is efficient to address any other minor grammatical issues in the same editorial pass. The phrase "QI-Core STU7 does not includes" in the same block also contains a subjectŌĆōverb agreement error. Batching corrections reduces future technical-correction tickets for the same text.

### Disposition C: Decline (Not Persuasive)

Decline the change on the grounds that "adjudicated" is an acceptable clinical/administrative term and "differentiates" is a tolerable variant.

**Rationale:** This disposition is difficult to justify. "Differentiates" is grammatically incorrect in the "helped toŌĆ”" construction, and "adjudicated" is misleading in context (coders determine coding, they do not adjudicate claims). The CQI call reached consensus on the correction. Declining would leave known errors in the published specification.

## Recommendation

**Disposition B: Accept with Broader Cleanup.** The two proposed changes are clearly correct and should be applied. However, the same paragraph also contains "does not includes" which is the same type of grammatical error being fixed. Addressing all minor text issues in a single editorial pass is more efficient than creating additional technical-correction tickets.
