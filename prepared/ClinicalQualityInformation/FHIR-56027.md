## Summary

The submitter asks for guidance on how to handle discrepancies between Encounter diagnosis lists sourced from EHR data versus Claims/billing data when submitting data for quality measures. Specifically:

- Claims data captures only billing codes and is the agreed-upon source for the Present on Admission (POA) indicator.
- EHRs may contain additional diagnosis codes associated with the encounter that are not present in the billing claim.
- The question is whether submitters should: (a) submit only billing-sourced diagnosis codes, or (b) submit all EHR encounter diagnoses and assign a missing/unknown POA value (e.g., "W") for diagnoses not found in the claim data.

This is a data-submission guidance question relevant to implementers of QI-Core measures that use Encounter diagnoses and POA indicators.

## Details

### Background

QI-Core STU7 shifted the modeling for principal diagnosis, principal procedure, and present on admission (POA) from `Encounter.diagnosis` to the `Claim` profile. This decision—documented in the QI-Core IG and discussed in companion ticket [FHIR-56026](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-56026)—was based on implementer feedback that these concepts are more accurately captured via billing/claim data where medical record coders make final determinations.

However, this creates a practical problem: EHR systems may record more encounter diagnoses than what appears on the claim. When quality measures require both the full encounter diagnosis list and POA status, implementers need clear guidance on how to reconcile the two sources.

### Related Prior Work

The QI-Core community has addressed related POA modeling issues several times:

- **[FHIR-23790](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-23790)** (Published, Persuasive) — Added the `presentOnAdmissionIndicator` extension to QI-Core Encounter.diagnosis, originating from QDM 5.5 requirements.
- **[FHIR-38181](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-38181)** (Published, Persuasive) — Addressed how to capture POA for claim data following UB-04 guidelines, specifically the exempt-from-POA code "1" not included in the original value set.
- **[FHIR-42054](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-42054)** (Published, Persuasive) — Updated the `onAdmission` required binding to use the QICore Present On Admission value set (which includes code "1") for QICore v6.0.
- **[FHIR-31764](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-31764)** (Published, Persuasive) — Made `diagnosisPresentOnAdmission` conditionally required only for inpatient encounters, since POA is not applicable for outpatient settings.
- **[FHIR-56026](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-56026)** (Triaged, Persuasive) — Companion ticket from the same cycle requesting removal of "adjudicated" and a typo fix in the QI-Core narrative explaining the shift of POA and principal diagnosis to the Claim profile.

### Core Issue

The fundamental question is one of data-submission policy rather than a FHIR modeling deficiency. The FHIR model supports both `Encounter.reasonCode`/`Encounter.reasonReference` (for clinical encounter diagnoses from the EHR) and `Claim.diagnosis` with `Claim.diagnosis.onAdmission` (for billing-sourced diagnoses with POA). The gap is the lack of explicit implementer guidance on what to do when the EHR diagnosis list is broader than the claim diagnosis list—and what POA value to assign to the non-billed diagnoses.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Provide Guidance in QI-Core IG (Recommended)

**Resolution:** Persuasive with Modification

Add explicit implementer guidance to the QI-Core IG (in the Encounter or Claim profile usage notes) addressing the EHR-vs-claims diagnosis discrepancy. The guidance should state:

1. For quality measure data submissions requiring POA, the `Claim.diagnosis` with `Claim.diagnosis.onAdmission` is the authoritative source for billing diagnoses and their POA indicators.
2. EHR-sourced encounter diagnoses (via `Encounter.reasonCode` / `Encounter.reasonReference`) serve the clinical record and may include diagnoses not present on the claim.
3. When a measure requires both the full encounter diagnosis list and POA status, implementers should submit all encounter diagnoses but assign POA value "W" (not applicable/unknown) or "1" (exempt from POA reporting) to diagnoses that do not appear on the claim, depending on the clinical context and reporting requirements.
4. Measure developers should clearly specify whether their measure intent is to evaluate billing-sourced diagnoses only or the broader clinical encounter diagnosis list.

**Rationale:** This directly answers the submitter's question with actionable guidance. The QI-Core IG already documents the shift to Claim-based POA but lacks guidance for the discrepancy scenario. Adding this guidance fills a real implementer need without requiring structural changes.

### Disposition B: Answer as Question Only

**Resolution:** Not Persuasive with Modification

Respond to the question directly in the ticket comments without modifying the IG. Clarify that:

- QI-Core STU7 intentionally separates clinical encounter diagnoses (`Encounter.reasonCode`/`Encounter.reasonReference`) from billing diagnoses (`Claim.diagnosis`).
- The POA indicator applies only to diagnoses on the claim (`Claim.diagnosis.onAdmission`).
- EHR diagnoses not on the claim do not need a POA value because they are not modeled in the Claim resource.
- If a measure specifically needs POA, it should query `Claim.diagnosis`, not `Encounter.diagnosis`.

**Rationale:** The existing modeling already addresses this concern implicitly—POA lives on Claim, clinical diagnoses live on Encounter. Framing this as a question-answered avoids IG churn while acknowledging the submitter's valid concern. However, this leaves the guidance implicit rather than explicit.

### Disposition C: Decline — Out of Scope

**Resolution:** Not Persuasive

Decline on the basis that this is a data-submission policy question outside the scope of FHIR/QI-Core specification. The question of whether to submit all EHR diagnoses or only billing diagnoses is a program-level decision (e.g., CMS eCQM reporting rules) rather than a FHIR modeling concern. Direct the submitter to the relevant quality reporting program guidance.

**Rationale:** FHIR specifications define the structure and semantics of data exchange, not submission policies. However, this disposition risks leaving implementers without practical guidance in the specification where they are most likely to look for it.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Provide Guidance in QI-Core IG** is recommended. The submitter raises a genuine implementer pain point that has been circling the QI-Core community for several years (as evidenced by the Zulip discussion from 2023 and the chain of related tickets from the same reporter). The QI-Core IG already made the architectural decision to shift POA to the Claim profile but did not document how to handle the resulting EHR-vs-claims diagnosis gap. Adding concise implementer guidance is a low-cost, high-value improvement that prevents each implementer from having to independently resolve this ambiguity. Framing the resolution as "Persuasive with Modification" acknowledges that the question is valid and results in a concrete IG improvement.
