## Description

The submitter asks whether the MeasureReport resource captures the outcome of a clinical trial. This is framed as a question rather than a specific change request to the specification.

## Resolution Description

> The answer depends on how much you report from the clinical trial. If the clinical trial information includes a measure, the MeasureReport could report that information. If reporting for the clinical trial extends beyond simple reporting the measure results, a MeasureReport resource may not be sufficient.

## Analysis

This ticket is a **question** rather than a concrete change request. The submitter asks whether MeasureReport can capture clinical trial outcomes. The CQI work group engaged with the submitter, attempted to schedule a meeting to understand the nuance, and ultimately resolved the ticket as **Not Persuasive** with a substantive answer:

- MeasureReport *can* capture clinical trial outcomes **if** the trial is modeled as a measure.
- MeasureReport is *not sufficient* if the clinical trial reporting extends beyond simple measure results.

The resolution is reasonable: the question was answered, and no specification change was identified as necessary. The work group made a good-faith effort to engage the submitter (three comments, two meeting invitations).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Agree with "Not Persuasive"
**Rationale:** The ticket is a question, not a change request. The work group answered the question thoroughly in the resolution description, explaining that MeasureReport can capture clinical trial outcomes when the trial is modeled as a measure, but is insufficient for broader clinical trial reporting. No specification change is warranted. The work group also made repeated attempts to engage the submitter for deeper discussion.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification — Add Guidance Note
**Rationale:** While the question was answered, the answer reveals a gap in the specification's guidance. A brief note in the MeasureReport resource documentation explaining its applicability (and limitations) for clinical trial outcome reporting could help future users with the same question. This aligns with FHIR-49824's goal of improving MeasureReport documentation.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive with Note to Submitter
**Rationale:** Maintain the Not Persuasive resolution but add an explicit note directing the submitter to the Biomedical Research & Regulation (BR&R) work group, which handles clinical research use cases. The question may be better addressed in the context of clinical research IGs (e.g., Vulcan Real World Data) rather than FHIR Core.
