## Description

The ticket identifies an inconsistency between two conformance requirements in the US Quality Measures IG (STU5):

- **Conformance Requirement 3.16** states that when specifying multiple populations and/or multiple population groups, a specific naming scheme **SHALL** be used.
- **Conformance Requirement 3.9** states that the name of an expression specifying a population criteria within a measure **SHOULD** always be the name of the criteria type.

The reporter notes it is odd that CR 3.16 uses a mandatory **SHALL** conformance verb while CR 3.9 — which covers essentially the same naming concern for population criteria — uses only a **SHOULD**. This creates a contradiction: CR 3.9 says naming is recommended but not required, while CR 3.16 says naming is mandatory for measures with multiple populations.

The reporter comments that this tracker will be incorporated into STU5 should an errata be published in the future.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive (Align CR 3.16 with CR 3.9 by changing SHALL to SHOULD)
- **Action:** Change the conformance verb in Conformance Requirement 3.16 from **SHALL** to **SHOULD** for the naming scheme when specifying multiple populations and/or multiple population groups in the US Quality Measures IG STU5.
- **Rationale:** This aligns CR 3.16 with CR 3.9, eliminates the contradiction, and is consistent with the resolution already applied to the UV IG via FHIR-49978 and FHIR-51698. The naming requirement was a SHOULD in US QM IG STU4 and was inadvertently escalated to SHALL in STU5. The population structure in the Measure resource already makes the population type identifiable without mandating a specific naming convention.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification (Strengthen both to SHALL for consistency)
- **Action:** Change Conformance Requirement 3.9 from **SHOULD** to **SHALL** so both CR 3.9 and CR 3.16 mandate the naming convention consistently.
- **Rationale:** If the workgroup believes a standardized naming scheme is important for interoperability, then both requirements should be mandatory. This resolves the contradiction by strengthening CR 3.9 rather than weakening CR 3.16. However, this would diverge from the UV IG resolution (which chose SHOULD) and would impose a stricter requirement than previously existed in STU4.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive (Maintain current wording)
- **Action:** No change. Keep CR 3.16 as SHALL and CR 3.9 as SHOULD.
- **Rationale:** CR 3.9 applies to general single-population naming while CR 3.16 specifically addresses the more complex scenario of multiple populations/groups where standardized naming is arguably more critical for disambiguation. The different conformance levels could be justified as intentional given the different scopes. However, this disposition contradicts the consensus established by FHIR-49978 and FHIR-51698 in the UV IG.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Persuasive** — Change CR 3.16 from SHALL to SHOULD in the US QM IG STU5 errata.

This is the strongest disposition because:
1. The identical issue has already been resolved twice in the UV IG (FHIR-49978 as "Persuasive with Modification" and FHIR-51698 as "Persuasive"), establishing clear community consensus.
2. The fix has already been implemented and merged in [HL7/fhir-cqm#25](https://github.com/HL7/fhir-cqm/pull/25).
3. The requirement was SHOULD in US QM IG STU4, meaning the escalation to SHALL in STU5 was unintentional.
4. Keeping the US IG aligned with the UV IG avoids divergence between the two specifications.
5. The reporter (jrubini) explicitly created this ticket as the STU5 counterpart, expecting the same resolution to be applied.
