## Summary

This ticket reports two misspellings in the HL7 FHIR® Implementation Guide: Quality Measures, Edition 1 – US Realm. The first is on the Home page in Section 1.8 (IP Statements), where "Further" is missing the letter "r" (rendered as "Futher" or similar). The second is in the Composites section 5.3 (Subject-level Linear Combination Scoring), in the last sentence, where "indicated" is misspelled as "indicates" (missing a "d"). The reporter requests these typographical errors be corrected.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested
#### Proposal
Correct the two identified misspellings in the Quality Measures IG: fix "Futher" to "Further" in Section 1.8 (IP Statements) on the Home page, and fix "indicates" to "indicated" in the last sentence of Section 5.3 (Subject-level Linear Combination Scoring) under Composites.
#### Justification
These are clear-cut typographical errors that reduce the professional quality of the specification. Correcting them requires minimal effort, carries no risk of changing normative meaning, and improves readability. Technical corrections of this nature are routinely accepted.

---

### Disposition B: Accept with Broader Review
#### Proposal
Correct the two reported misspellings and additionally perform a broader spell-check and proofreading pass across the Quality Measures IG to identify and fix any other typographical errors that may exist.
#### Justification
If two misspellings were found, there may be others. A broader review would improve the overall quality of the IG and reduce future tickets of this type. However, this expands scope beyond the original request and requires additional editorial effort.

---

### Disposition C: Decline
#### Proposal
Decline the request as out-of-scope for the current ballot cycle, deferring to a future editorial pass.
#### Justification
While the misspellings are real, they do not affect the technical accuracy or interoperability of the specification. Declining is defensible if editorial bandwidth is constrained. However, given the trivial nature of the fix, this disposition is difficult to justify.

---

### Recommendation
**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A is the clear choice for this ticket. The two misspellings are specific, verifiable, and trivially correctable. Technical corrections like these carry no risk of altering the meaning or normative content of the specification. Accepting and applying the fixes promptly maintains the professional quality of the IG and addresses the reporter's concern with minimal effort. There is no compelling reason to defer or decline such a straightforward editorial fix.
