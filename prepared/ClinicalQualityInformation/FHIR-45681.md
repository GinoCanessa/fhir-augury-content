## Summary

The submitter requests that the short descriptions for the `Measure.relatedArtifact:predecessor` and `Measure.relatedArtifact:citation` slices in the CQFM Publishable Measure profile (section 17.53.1.1) be revised. The current short descriptions are reported as not clear or descriptive of the data elements, despite being somewhat intuitive. The proposal is to rework these short descriptions to be more helpful and informative to the reader.

No specific replacement text is proposed in the ticket.

## Analysis

**Scope:** This is a documentation/profile clarity improvement. The request focuses solely on the `short` (display) descriptions of two `relatedArtifact` slices — `:predecessor` and `:citation` — in the CQFM Publishable Measure profile. No structural or conformance changes are involved.

**Context:**
- This ticket is part of a batch of five similar tickets (FHIR-45681 through FHIR-45686) submitted by the same reporter, all requesting short description improvements across different CQFM measure profiles.
- FHIR-45682 appears to be an accidental clone/duplicate of this ticket with identical content.
- FHIR-49783 (Applied, Persuasive with Modification) proposes deprecating `relatedArtifact` on Measure entirely and replacing it with discrete elements. If that change proceeds in the Quality Measures IG, the `:predecessor` and `:citation` slices addressed by this ticket may no longer exist in future versions, potentially making this change moot.

**Gap:** The submitter identifies the problem but does not propose specific replacement text for the short descriptions. The workgroup would need to draft appropriate wording.

---

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive
**Rationale:** Accept the request as-is. The workgroup acknowledges that clearer short descriptions improve usability of the profile and commits to revising the short descriptions for `Measure.relatedArtifact:predecessor` and `Measure.relatedArtifact:citation` in the CQFM Publishable Measure profile. The workgroup will draft improved wording during the update cycle. FHIR-45682 should be closed as a duplicate.

**Considerations:** This is a low-risk editorial improvement. However, the workgroup will need to define the replacement text since none was proposed. Should be coordinated with sibling tickets FHIR-45683 through FHIR-45686 for consistency.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification
**Rationale:** Accept the intent of the request but defer action pending the outcome of FHIR-49783 (which proposes replacing `relatedArtifact` with discrete elements). If `relatedArtifact` slices are deprecated and replaced, revising their short descriptions would be unnecessary. Instead, ensure that the new discrete elements (`citation`, `replaces`, etc.) have clear, descriptive short descriptions from the outset. Close FHIR-45682 as duplicate.

**Considerations:** This avoids wasted effort if the structural changes from FHIR-49783 are adopted. However, if FHIR-49783 is delayed or not applied to the Quality Measures IG, the unclear descriptions would persist.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive
**Rationale:** The existing short descriptions, while concise, are sufficiently intuitive (as the submitter acknowledges). Short descriptions are intentionally brief; detailed explanations belong in the element definitions and profile documentation. No change is needed.

**Considerations:** This risks leaving known usability issues unaddressed for implementers who rely on short descriptions for quick orientation in the profile.

---

## Recommendation

**Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification** — Given that FHIR-49783 (Applied) is actively restructuring `relatedArtifact` usage on Measure, the most pragmatic approach is to accept the concern but align the resolution with that ongoing structural change. The workgroup should ensure the replacement elements have clear short descriptions, and only revise the current `relatedArtifact` slice descriptions if those slices will persist in the next release. FHIR-45682 should be resolved as a duplicate of this ticket.
