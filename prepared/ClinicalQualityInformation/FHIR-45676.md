## Summary

The reporter asks whether users will have access to a Confluence page linked from section 1.3.2 ("Quality Measurement Standards Landscape") of the HL7 FHIR® Implementation Guide: Quality Measures, Edition 1 – US Realm. The linked page describes the Clinical Quality Framework and is hosted on HL7 Confluence, which may require authentication or HL7 membership to access. This raises a usability concern: if external readers of the published IG cannot reach the linked content, the reference is effectively broken for them.

## Details

The ticket's full description:

> HL7 FHIR® Implementation Guide: Quality Measures, Edition 1- US Realm
>
> 1.3.2 Quality Measurement Standards Landscape: The link to Clinical Quality Framework goes to a confluence page. Will users have access to this?

This is a ballot comment submitted as a question. The reporter is concerned about link accessibility in a published IG. HL7 Confluence pages are generally accessible to the public, though some spaces may have restricted permissions. The question is whether the specific Clinical Quality Framework Confluence page is publicly accessible, and if not, whether the IG should instead link to a publicly available resource or reproduce the relevant content directly.

No comments have been recorded on this ticket.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Not Persuasive – Question Answered (Recommended)

**Resolution:** Considered – Question answered / Not Persuasive with Modification

Answer the reporter's question: HL7 Confluence pages under the main HL7 wiki are generally publicly accessible and do not require HL7 membership to view. The Clinical Quality Framework Confluence page should be accessible to all users. If the specific page is found to be restricted, the work group will update the link to a publicly available equivalent.

**Rationale:** This is a question, not a change request. The nearly identical ticket FHIR-47542 (same reporter, same concern about a Confluence link in a different IG) was resolved as "Considered – Question answered" with no change. HL7 Confluence is broadly public-facing, so no action is required unless the specific link is confirmed to be restricted. This disposition is consistent with prior precedent.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification – Replace Link

**Resolution:** Persuasive with Modification

Replace the Confluence link to the Clinical Quality Framework with a direct link to a publicly accessible resource, such as the Clinical Quality Framework page on HL7's main website or the relevant published specification page. This ensures all IG readers can access the content regardless of Confluence access policies.

**Rationale:** Even if the current Confluence page is publicly accessible today, Confluence access policies could change. Linking to a more stable, public-facing URL (e.g., an HL7 product page or a published specification) would improve long-term usability and avoid future accessibility concerns.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive – No Change

**Resolution:** Not Persuasive

Decline the request. Confluence pages referenced in HL7 IGs are expected to be accessible to the public. The IG does not need modification.

**Rationale:** HL7 Confluence is a public wiki. The burden is on the reader to access standard HL7 resources. No evidence has been presented that the specific page is actually inaccessible.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Not Persuasive – Question Answered** is recommended. This aligns with the resolution of the nearly identical ticket FHIR-47542, submitted by the same reporter with the same concern about a Confluence link. That ticket was resolved with "Considered – Question answered" and no change. HL7 Confluence pages are generally publicly accessible, so the straightforward answer addresses the reporter's concern. If the work group wishes to go further, Disposition B offers a proactive improvement, but it is not strictly necessary.
