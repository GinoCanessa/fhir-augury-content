## Description

The submitter requests that the **Measure Reporting** section of the DEQM IG be expanded with clearer, more detailed guidance on how MeasureReport resources are constructed from quality measure calculation results. Currently, this section refers to the Quality Measure IG's calculation descriptions but does not provide step-by-step illustrations.

Specifically, the ticket requests:

1. **Proportion measure walkthroughs** — Simple worked examples for both subject-based and non-subject-based proportion measures, showing how the calculation process maps to MeasureReport construction.

2. **Null result interpretation** — Explicit guidance on how null expression results are handled, consistent with the companion tracker [FHIR-56278](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-56278) (Quality Measure IG):
   - For subject-based measures, a null result is interpreted as **false**.
   - For non-subject-based measures, a null result is interpreted as an **empty list**, and null elements within the resulting list do not contribute to the result.

3. **Additional walkthroughs** — Worked examples for calculating:
   - Supplemental data
   - Stratifiers
   - Value-based stratifiers
   - Continuous-variable values

4. **Cross-links to Quality Measure IG** — Each walkthrough area should link back to the corresponding section of the Quality Measure IG where the semantics are defined.

## Analysis

This is a **documentation enhancement** request for the DEQM IG's Measure Reporting section. The current guidance is abstract, pointing readers to the Quality Measure IG without providing concrete worked examples. The submitter (Bryn Rhodes) is a well-known contributor to the quality measurement space and has filed a companion tracker (FHIR-56278) against the Quality Measure IG itself to tighten the normative language around null-handling semantics.

**Key considerations:**

- **Scope**: The request is broad — it asks for walkthroughs covering proportion measures, supplemental data, stratifiers, value-based stratifiers, and continuous-variable values. This is a significant authoring effort.
- **Dependency**: The null-handling guidance in this ticket depends on the resolution of FHIR-56278 in the Quality Measure IG. Ideally, FHIR-56278 should be resolved first (or concurrently) so that the DEQM examples align with the normative semantics.
- **Impact**: This is purely additive guidance — no structural or breaking changes to existing profiles or conformance requirements. It improves implementer understanding and consistency.
- **Value**: Worked examples for MeasureReport construction are frequently requested by implementers, making this a high-value documentation improvement.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive (Accept as-is)
Accept the request in full. Expand the DEQM Measure Reporting section with walkthroughs for proportion measures (subject-based and non-subject-based), supplemental data, stratifiers, value-based stratifiers, and continuous-variable values. Include the null-result interpretation guidance consistent with FHIR-56278, and add cross-links to the Quality Measure IG. Coordinate with the resolution of FHIR-56278 to ensure alignment.

**Pros:** Addresses a well-documented gap; improves implementer experience; aligns DEQM guidance with Quality Measure IG normative semantics.
**Cons:** Significant authoring effort; broad scope may delay completion.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification (Phase the work)
Accept the request but scope the initial work to proportion measure walkthroughs (subject-based and non-subject-based) and the null-result interpretation guidance. Defer supplemental data, stratifier, and continuous-variable walkthroughs to a follow-up ticket, allowing incremental delivery.

**Pros:** Delivers the most impactful content first; reduces risk of the ticket stalling due to scope; aligns with the immediate need highlighted by FHIR-56278.
**Cons:** Defers some requested content; requires tracking a follow-up ticket.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive (Defer to Quality Measure IG)
Decline the request, noting that the Quality Measure IG (via FHIR-56278) is the appropriate place for normative semantics, and that the DEQM IG should remain a concise reference rather than duplicating walkthrough content. Suggest that examples be maintained externally (e.g., in a Connectathon track or implementation guide supplement).

**Pros:** Keeps the DEQM IG lean; avoids potential content drift between two IGs.
**Cons:** Does not address the documented implementer confusion; walkthroughs in the DEQM IG are specifically about MeasureReport construction, which is DEQM's domain, not duplication.

## Recommendation

**Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification** — Accept the request with phased delivery. The proportion measure walkthroughs and null-result interpretation guidance are the most immediately impactful and directly tied to the companion FHIR-56278 tracker. Delivering these first provides concrete value to implementers while allowing the broader walkthrough content (supplemental data, stratifiers, continuous-variable) to be developed in a follow-up ticket. This approach manages scope while still committing to the full vision of the request.
