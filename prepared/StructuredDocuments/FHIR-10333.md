## Summary

This ticket requests that four complex C-CDA R2.1 conformance constraints (CONF:1198-30613 through CONF:1198-30616) be expressed in the US C-CDA on FHIR Implementation Guide, specifically for the **History and Physical Composition** profile. These constraints enforce conditional section inclusion and mutual exclusivity rules that were present in C-CDA but not carried forward into the FHIR representation.

The constraints in question are:

1. **CONF:1198-30613** — The document SHALL contain a Chief Complaint and Reason for Visit Section (2.16.840.1.113883.10.20.22.2.13) **or** a Chief Complaint Section (1.3.6.1.4.1.19376.1.5.3.1.1.13.2.1) **or** a Reason for Visit Section (2.16.840.1.113883.10.20.22.2.12).
2. **CONF:1198-30614** — The document SHALL contain an Assessment and Plan Section (V2) (2.16.840.1.113883.10.20.22.2.9:2014-06-09), **or** both an Assessment Section (2.16.840.1.113883.10.20.22.2.8) **and** a Plan of Treatment Section (V2) (2.16.840.1.113883.10.20.22.2.10:2014-06-09).
3. **CONF:1198-30615** — The document SHALL NOT contain an Assessment and Plan Section (V2) when either an Assessment Section or a Plan of Treatment Section (V2) is present (mutual exclusivity).
4. **CONF:1198-30616** — The document SHALL NOT contain a Chief Complaint and Reason for Visit Section when either a Chief Complaint Section or a Reason for Visit Section is present (mutual exclusivity).

The ticket was originally filed in July 2016 and was reopened post-STU1.2 publication by Lynn Laakso in March 2024, indicating these constraints remain unaddressed.

## Details

The original related URL is the History and Physical Composition page: [http://build.fhir.org/ccda/ccda-history-and-physical-composition.html](http://build.fhir.org/ccda/ccda-history-and-physical-composition.html)

These constraints represent "OR" and "SHALL NOT coexist" patterns that are straightforward in CDA (where they are expressed in Schematron) but require FHIR-specific mechanisms to express. In FHIR, these could be implemented via:

- **FHIRPath invariants** on the Composition profile (e.g., `section.where(code.coding.where(system='http://loinc.org' and code='...')).exists()` patterns with `implies` or `xor` logic)
- **Composition.section slicing** with conditional `min`/`max` cardinalities and constraints

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept — Add FHIRPath Invariants to the History and Physical Composition Profile

Add FHIRPath invariants to the `USRealmHeaderforHistoryandPhysicalCompositions` profile (or equivalent H&P profile in the current C-CDA on FHIR IG) that enforce the four constraints:

1. An invariant requiring at least one of Chief Complaint and Reason for Visit Section, Chief Complaint Section, or Reason for Visit Section.
2. An invariant requiring either an Assessment and Plan Section (V2) or both an Assessment Section and a Plan of Treatment Section (V2).
3. An invariant prohibiting Assessment and Plan Section (V2) when either Assessment or Plan of Treatment Section is present.
4. An invariant prohibiting Chief Complaint and Reason for Visit Section when either Chief Complaint or Reason for Visit Section is present.

This directly translates the C-CDA conformance statements into equivalent FHIR constraints and ensures data quality parity between C-CDA and C-CDA on FHIR documents.

**Rationale:** These are SHALL-level constraints in C-CDA R2.1. Omitting them from the FHIR IG creates a gap where documents claiming C-CDA on FHIR conformance may not meet the structural requirements of the original C-CDA specification. FHIRPath is the standard mechanism for expressing such cross-element constraints in FHIR profiles.

### Disposition B: Accept with Modification — Consolidate with Sibling Tickets and Address Holistically

Accept this ticket as part of a broader effort to address all unimplemented complex C-CDA constraints across document types. Consolidate FHIR-10333, FHIR-10331, and FHIR-10335 into a single work item that audits all C-CDA on FHIR Composition profiles for missing conditional section constraints and mutual exclusivity rules, then implements them uniformly.

This would produce a consistent approach across all document types (Consultation Note, Discharge Summary, etc.) rather than addressing one document type at a time.

**Rationale:** The sibling tickets indicate this is a systemic gap, not isolated to the History and Physical. A consolidated approach avoids piecemeal changes and ensures consistent constraint expression across the IG.

### Disposition C: Decline — Defer to Implementation Guidance

Decline on the basis that these mutual exclusivity and conditional inclusion constraints are better handled through implementation guidance or validation tooling rather than formal profile constraints. FHIRPath invariants for complex section-level OR/XOR logic can be difficult to author correctly, may not be well-supported by all validators, and add significant complexity to the profile.

Instead, add a "Conformance Notes" section to the IG documentation explaining these constraints in plain text and recommending that validators check for them.

**Rationale:** FHIR profiling has known limitations in expressing complex conditional constraints. Adding brittle FHIRPath invariants that may not be uniformly validated could create a false sense of conformance assurance. The constraints are already documented in C-CDA R2.1, and implementers familiar with C-CDA know these rules.

## Recommendation

**Disposition B: Accept with Modification — Consolidate with Sibling Tickets and Address Holistically**

This ticket has been open since 2016 and was deliberately reopened post-STU1.2, indicating persistent need. The constraints are unambiguous SHALL-level requirements from C-CDA R2.1 and should be formally expressed in the FHIR IG. However, given that FHIR-10331 and FHIR-10335 address the same systemic gap across other document types, a consolidated approach will produce better, more consistent results. The work group should review all remaining sibling tickets together, define a standard pattern for expressing section mutual exclusivity and conditional inclusion via FHIRPath, and apply it uniformly across all C-CDA on FHIR Composition profiles.
