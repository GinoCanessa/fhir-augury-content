## Analysis

### Context

This ticket was filed in 2016 during early development of the C-CDA on FHIR Implementation Guide. At the time, the Social History Section did not include a reference to the DAF (now US Core) Smoking Status profile as an entry type. The reporter (Corey Spears) noted that DAF already defined a smoking status profile and that it should be referenced from the Social History Section, potentially requiring sub-slicing of the section entries.

Three duplicate tickets (FHIR-10328, FHIR-10329, FHIR-10330) were filed at the same time and have been marked as duplicates, confirming this ticket as the canonical tracker item.

### Evolving Landscape

Since this ticket was originally filed, significant work has been done in this area:

1. **FHIR-11883** (Published, Persuasive) addressed the inverse concern — that the Social History section was *too restricted* to only smoking status, whereas C-CDA allows other types of observations. This was resolved persuasively.
2. **FHIR-28436** (Published, Persuasive with Modification) added a required category to the US Core Smoking Status profile to enable consistent search behavior.
3. **FHIR-40804** (Published, Persuasive) broadened the discussion further, noting that US Core and other national IGs have loosened bindings and that the social history profile should be more permissive, with options including generalized Social History observation profiles.

The ticket was re-opened by lynn_laakso in March 2024 "post STU1.2 publication," suggesting it may need fresh review in the context of the current specification state.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive (Apply as Submitted)
**Action:** Add a smoking history / smoking status entry reference (e.g., to the US Core Smoking Status Observation profile) to the Social History Section in C-CDA on FHIR, using sub-slicing as needed.

**Rationale:** The original request is valid — the Social History Section should explicitly reference the smoking status profile as one of its allowed entry types. This aligns with how C-CDA itself structures the Social History section and would make the FHIR representation more faithful to the original C-CDA intent.

**Risk:** Low technical risk, but may need coordination with the current profile structure, especially if FHIR-11883's resolution already addressed the entry constraints.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification (Subsume into Broader Social History Alignment)
**Action:** Rather than adding only a smoking history entry, conduct a broader review of the Social History Section entries to ensure all relevant observation types (smoking status, alcohol use, social determinants, etc.) are properly referenced. Close this ticket as addressed by the broader alignment work.

**Rationale:** Given that FHIR-11883 was already resolved persuasively (addressing restricted Social History entries) and FHIR-40804 pushed toward more permissive social history profiles, a narrow fix to add only smoking status may be insufficient. A holistic update to the Social History Section entries, consistent with the current US Core and IPS approaches, would be more effective.

**Risk:** Larger scope of work; may delay resolution but produces a more complete outcome.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive (Already Addressed)
**Action:** Close as not persuasive, noting that the underlying concern has been substantially addressed by FHIR-11883 (which broadened Social History entries beyond just smoking status) and subsequent specification updates.

**Rationale:** The core issue — that the Social History Section did not properly reference smoking status — was part of a broader problem that FHIR-11883 resolved. If the current specification already includes smoking status as a valid entry in the Social History Section, this ticket's request has been effectively fulfilled.

**Risk:** If the current specification still does not include a smoking status entry reference, this disposition would leave a gap.

## Recommendation

**Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification** is recommended.

The original request is valid, but the landscape has evolved significantly since 2016. FHIR-11883 addressed the broadest form of this concern (Social History entries restricted to only smoking status), and FHIR-40804 further advanced the discussion toward more flexible social history profiling. Rather than a narrow fix to add just the smoking history entry, the workgroup should verify the current state of the Social History Section entries in C-CDA on FHIR and ensure they align with the modern US Core and IPS approaches to social history observations. If smoking status is already included as a result of FHIR-11883's resolution, the modification should document that and close the loop. If not, it should be added as part of a broader entry alignment.
