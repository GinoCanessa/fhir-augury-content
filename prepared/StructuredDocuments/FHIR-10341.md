## Description

The submitter requests that the Composition slicing discriminator rules in the C-CDA on FHIR profiles (specifically referenced for the Transfer Summary Composition at `http://build.fhir.org/ccda/ccda-transfer-summary-composition.html`) be changed from `openAtEnd` to `open`.

The `openAtEnd` rule requires that any additional content (sections not matching defined slices) must appear after all defined slices. The `open` rule allows additional content to appear in any position. For `Composition.section` in clinical document profiles, the ordering constraint imposed by `openAtEnd` may be unnecessarily restrictive since document sections typically have no meaningful ordering requirement.

The ticket was re-opened post-STU1.2 publication by Lynn Laakso (2024-03-12), indicating it remains relevant for future versions.

## Analysis

This is a longstanding ticket (filed 2016, re-opened 2024) requesting a straightforward change to slicing rules in the C-CDA on FHIR Composition profiles. The core question is whether `openAtEnd` is the appropriate slicing rule for `Composition.section` or whether `open` is more correct.

**Key considerations:**

1. **Semantic appropriateness:** `openAtEnd` enforces that additional sections not matching defined slices must appear after all defined slices. For clinical document sections, there is generally no meaningful ordering requirement — sections are identified by code, not by position.

2. **Precedent from related tickets:** FHIR-13461 (Lloyd McKenzie) explicitly noted that `openAtEnd` should raise warnings when `orderMeaning` is null, since "forcing elements to appear in a particular order when order has no meaning breaks interoperability unnecessarily." This directly supports changing to `open`.

3. **Validator implications:** Changing from `openAtEnd` to `open` is a relaxation of constraints, meaning existing valid instances remain valid. No breaking change for implementers.

4. **Scope:** The ticket targets the US C-CDA on FHIR specification. All C-CDA Composition profiles using `openAtEnd` on `Composition.section` should be reviewed for consistency.

5. **Longevity:** The ticket has been open for 8+ years and was explicitly re-opened post-STU1.2 publication, suggesting ongoing relevance.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive (Recommended)

**Action:** Change the slicing discriminator rules from `openAtEnd` to `open` on `Composition.section` in the C-CDA on FHIR Composition profiles (including the Transfer Summary and any other affected compositions).

**Rationale:** The `openAtEnd` rule imposes an ordering constraint that has no semantic meaning for document sections. Sections are identified by their code, not their position. As noted in FHIR-13461, forcing ordering when `orderMeaning` is null breaks interoperability unnecessarily. Changing to `open` is a non-breaking relaxation that aligns with the general principle of not over-constraining profiles. This is a simple, well-justified change.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification

**Action:** Change the slicing rules from `openAtEnd` to `open`, but additionally audit all C-CDA on FHIR Composition profiles (not just the Transfer Summary) to ensure consistent use of `open` slicing rules on `Composition.section` across the specification.

**Rationale:** The original ticket references the Transfer Summary specifically, but the same issue likely applies to other C-CDA Composition profiles (e.g., Discharge Summary, Consultation Note, etc.). A broader fix ensures consistency and avoids piecemeal corrections.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive

**Action:** Retain `openAtEnd` as the slicing rule.

**Rationale:** If the C-CDA on FHIR specification intentionally requires that additional sections appear after defined sections (e.g., to maintain a predictable document structure for clinical readers), then `openAtEnd` is the correct rule. However, no evidence in the ticket history or comments supports an intentional design choice for ordering.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Persuasive** — The change from `openAtEnd` to `open` is well-justified, non-breaking, and aligned with community consensus that ordering constraints should not be imposed without semantic meaning. The work group should additionally consider Disposition B's broader audit if time permits, but the core change is straightforward and should proceed.
