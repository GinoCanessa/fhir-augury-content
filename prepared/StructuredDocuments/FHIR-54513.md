## Summary

The submitter identifies that the introductory text on the FHIR [Documents page](https://build.fhir.org/documents.html) discusses the relationship between FHIR document bundles (using Composition) and the DocumentReference resource, but does not mention DiagnosticReport or the role it plays in relation to documents. The request is to add text clarifying how DiagnosticReport fits into the documents ecosystem alongside Composition and DocumentReference.

## Details

### Description

The current introductory text on the Documents page reads:

> Note that FHIR defines both this document format (a document bundle containing a Composition resource and potentially other resources) and a DocumentReference resource. FHIR documents are used for documents that are authored and assembled in FHIR, while the DocumentReference resource is for general references to documents (which may include FHIR documents as well as PDFs, CDAs, etc.).

The submitter requests that DiagnosticReport also be mentioned in this introduction and that its role in the document landscape be explained.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive (Accept as Submitted)

Add a sentence to the Documents page introduction mentioning DiagnosticReport and its role. For example, after the existing text about DocumentReference, add:

> "Additionally, the DiagnosticReport resource can be used to represent clinical reports (such as laboratory or imaging results) that may overlap with documents in scope. DiagnosticReport includes a 'composition' element that references a Composition for structured report content. See the DiagnosticReport resource for more information."

**Rationale:** The request is straightforward editorial guidance. The three resources (Composition, DocumentReference, DiagnosticReport) are frequently confused by implementers, and the Documents page is the natural place to clarify how they relate. This is a low-risk, high-value clarification.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification

Accept the intent of mentioning DiagnosticReport but coordinate the wording with the outcome of [FHIR-55368](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-55368) (which proposes allowing DiagnosticReport as a FHIR Document base). Rather than describing DiagnosticReport's current-state role only, draft broader text that:

1. Mentions DiagnosticReport and its current `composition` element link to Composition.
2. Notes that the relationship between DiagnosticReport and documents is under active discussion.
3. Points readers to the DiagnosticReport resource page for details.

This avoids writing text that may need immediate revision if FHIR-55368 is accepted.

**Rationale:** Since FHIR-55368 could significantly change DiagnosticReport's role in documents, the wording should be future-aware. A more cautious phrasing avoids creating contradictory guidance.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive (Decline)

Decline the request on the grounds that the Documents page is specifically about the FHIR document Bundle paradigm (Composition-based documents), and DiagnosticReport is a distinct resource with its own page. Mentioning DiagnosticReport in the Documents introduction could blur the boundaries between these concepts and add confusion rather than reduce it.

**Rationale:** The Documents page scope is intentionally focused on Composition-based document bundles. DiagnosticReport guidance belongs on the DiagnosticReport resource page itself.

## Recommendation

**Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification** is recommended. The submitter's request is valid—implementers regularly struggle to understand how Composition, DocumentReference, and DiagnosticReport relate in the context of documents, and the Documents page is the first place many look for guidance. However, given the active and closely related ticket [FHIR-55368](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-55368) (which proposes allowing DiagnosticReport as a FHIR Document bundle base), the added text should be drafted carefully to remain accurate regardless of that ticket's outcome. A brief mention of DiagnosticReport with a forward reference to its resource page, coordinated with FHIR-55368's resolution, provides the best balance of immediate clarity and future stability.
