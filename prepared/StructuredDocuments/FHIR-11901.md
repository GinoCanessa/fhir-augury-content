## Description

The commenter requests that computable (machine-processable) mappings be added from C-CDA to CDA on FHIR. This would enable automated transformation between C-CDA documents and their FHIR-based equivalents defined in the US C-CDA on FHIR Implementation Guide, using FHIR artifacts such as StructureMaps or ConceptMaps rather than narrative-only documentation.

## Analysis

This is a long-standing enhancement request (filed 2016, deferred, re-opened 2024) to add computable mappings—likely FHIR StructureMap and/or ConceptMap resources—that formally define how C-CDA document structures map to CDA on FHIR profiles. The original deferral acknowledged the value but cited resource constraints and scope limitations.

**Key considerations:**

1. **Scope of work:** Creating comprehensive computable mappings from C-CDA to CDA on FHIR would be a substantial effort, covering the CCD, Discharge Summary, Referral Note, Consultation Note, Progress Note, Transfer Summary, and other document types, plus their nested section and entry-level templates.

2. **Community ecosystem:** Since 2016, the ecosystem has matured. The FHIR Mapping Language is more widely implemented (e.g., matchbox.health), and the Swiss HL7 community has demonstrated the approach for their CDA profiles (hl7ch/cda-fhir-maps). However, no C-CDA-specific computable mappings have been published.

3. **Implementer demand:** Multiple Zulip threads (2019–2022) show ongoing implementer interest in CDA-to-FHIR mapping, but the work remains fragmented across individual implementers without a canonical reference.

4. **Re-opening context:** The ticket was deliberately re-opened post STU1.2.0 alongside other deferred ballot items, suggesting the Structured Documents WG is conducting a systematic review and may now be ready to scope future work.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive with Modification

**Action:** Accept the request in a scoped form. Add a set of initial computable StructureMap resources to the C-CDA on FHIR IG covering the highest-priority document type(s) (e.g., CCD/US Realm Header) as a starting point, with a roadmap to expand coverage in subsequent releases.

**Rationale:** The re-opening post STU1.2.0 signals readiness to address this gap. Starting with a scoped set of mappings (header-level elements, one or two document types) makes the work tractable. The FHIR Mapping Language and tooling (matchbox.health) are now mature enough to support this. The Swiss cda-fhir-maps project demonstrates a viable pattern.

**Risks:** Significant authoring and testing effort even for scoped mappings. Requires volunteers or funded contributors with expertise in both C-CDA templates and the FHIR Mapping Language.

### Disposition B: Considered for Future Use

**Action:** Defer again, noting the growing ecosystem and tooling availability. Encourage community development of computable mappings outside the IG, with the intent to evaluate incorporation when mature artifacts exist.

**Rationale:** The original deferral reasoning still holds—comprehensive mappings require substantial resources that the WG may not currently have. While the ecosystem has matured since 2016, no C-CDA-specific computable mappings have been published yet (per Oliver Egger, 2022). Deferring allows the WG to benefit from community efforts (such as the hl7ch/cda-fhir-maps pattern) before committing to maintaining mappings within the IG.

**Risks:** Continued lack of canonical mapping resources may perpetuate fragmented implementer efforts. The ticket has now been open for 8+ years without resolution.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive with Modification

**Action:** Do not add computable mappings to the IG itself, but add a documentation section referencing available community mapping tools and resources (matchbox.health, hl7ch/cda-fhir-maps pattern, CCDA / FHIR mapping stream on Zulip) to guide implementers.

**Rationale:** Computable mappings are better maintained as separate, independently versioned artifacts rather than embedded in the IG. The IG should focus on profile definitions and conformance requirements, while pointing implementers to external mapping resources. This addresses the underlying need (implementer guidance for transformation) without the maintenance burden of in-IG StructureMaps.

**Risks:** Does not fully address the request for computable, normative mappings. External resources may become stale or diverge from the IG profiles.

---

## Recommendation

**Disposition B: Considered for Future Use** is recommended.

The request for computable C-CDA to CDA-on-FHIR mappings remains valid and increasingly relevant as the ecosystem matures. However, no C-CDA-specific computable mappings exist in the community yet, and the effort to create and maintain them is substantial. The WG should explicitly encourage community-driven development of StructureMap resources (following the pattern established by hl7ch/cda-fhir-maps) and plan to evaluate incorporation once viable artifacts emerge. This avoids premature commitment while keeping the door open for a future ballot cycle when resources and artifacts are available.
