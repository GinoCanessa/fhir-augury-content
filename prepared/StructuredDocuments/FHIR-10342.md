## Description

The submitter requests removal of the Related Person section from the C-CDA on FHIR composition profiles (originally referencing the Transfer Summary Composition at `http://build.fhir.org/ccda/ccda-transfer-summary-composition.html`). The rationale is that related persons have been determined to appear in the composition header via an extension (the `ccda-participant-extension`), making a dedicated Related Person *section* in the differential redundant.

A comment from Lynn Laakso (2024-03-12) indicates this ticket was re-opened post STU1.2 publication, suggesting the issue was not fully addressed in that release cycle.

## Analysis

This is a long-standing ticket (filed 2016, re-opened 2024) requesting a structural change to the C-CDA on FHIR composition profiles. The core question is straightforward: if related persons are represented via the `ccda-participant-extension` in the composition header, then a dedicated Related Person section in the composition differential is redundant and potentially confusing for implementers.

Key considerations:
1. **Age of ticket**: Filed nearly 9 years ago, re-opened after STU1.2 — suggests this has been discussed before but not resolved.
2. **Header vs. section modeling**: The C-CDA specification models related persons as participants via a header extension. Having them also appear as a section creates ambiguity about the canonical location for this information.
3. **Related ticket FHIR-12165**: Confirms that the `ccda-participant-extension` is the intended mechanism for representing related persons (participants with function codes like next-of-kin, caregiver, etc.) in the composition header.
4. **Broader RelatedPerson cleanup**: Tickets FHIR-49768, FHIR-49775, and FHIR-49767 show ongoing community discussion about appropriate use of RelatedPerson across C-CDA on FHIR profiles, suggesting this is part of a larger modeling concern.
5. **Impact**: Removing a section from a composition profile is a breaking change for any implementer currently populating or consuming a Related Person section. However, if the section was never widely implemented (because the header extension was the agreed-upon approach), impact may be minimal.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Applied — Remove the Related Person Section
**Action:** Remove the Related Person section from the affected C-CDA on FHIR composition profile differentials (e.g., Transfer Summary). Document that related persons should be represented using the `ccda-participant-extension` in the composition header.

**Rationale:** The community already determined that related persons belong in the header as participants via the extension. Retaining a redundant section creates ambiguity and contradicts the agreed-upon modeling approach. The comment from Lynn Laakso confirms this remains an open issue post STU1.2 that should be resolved. This aligns with the original submitter's request and the design intent evidenced by FHIR-12165.

**Risk:** Low-to-moderate. If any implementers currently use the section, they would need to migrate to the header extension. A migration note in the IG should mitigate this.

### Disposition B: Deferred — Defer Pending Broader RelatedPerson Review
**Action:** Defer this ticket and bundle it with the broader RelatedPerson cleanup effort reflected in FHIR-49768, FHIR-49775, and FHIR-49767.

**Rationale:** Multiple recent tickets address RelatedPerson usage across C-CDA on FHIR profiles. Addressing FHIR-10342 in isolation risks inconsistency; a coordinated review of all RelatedPerson modeling decisions would be more coherent. However, those tickets are owned by Cross-Group Projects and are already deferred, which could further delay resolution.

**Risk:** Continued ambiguity for implementers during the deferral period. The ticket is already nearly 9 years old.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive — Retain the Related Person Section
**Action:** Keep the Related Person section in the composition differential and close the ticket.

**Rationale:** Some implementers may find it useful to have a dedicated section for related person information, separate from the header extension. The section could carry narrative content or additional context not suited for the header extension. Removing it would be a breaking change.

**Risk:** Maintains the current ambiguity about where related person information should be placed, and contradicts the stated design intent that related persons appear in the header.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Applied — Remove the Related Person Section.**

The original request is well-founded and supported by the existing design of the `ccda-participant-extension`. The ticket has been open for nearly 9 years with no substantive opposition. The re-opening post STU1.2 indicates the work group acknowledges this remains unresolved. Removing the redundant section and documenting the header extension as the canonical approach will reduce implementer confusion and align the specification with its stated design intent.
