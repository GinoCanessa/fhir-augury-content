## Summary

The heading/section numbers on the EHR-S RLE AuditEvent profile page (`http://hl7.org/fhir/ehrsrle/auditevent-ehrsrle.html`) are rendered as question marks instead of proper numeric values. The reporter checked other profile pages and found they displayed correctly, suggesting the issue may be isolated to this specific page (or a small subset of pages). Grahame Grieve directed the reporter to file this as a Publishing work group task.

---

## Details

- **Affected Page:** `http://hl7.org/fhir/ehrsrle/auditevent-ehrsrle.html` — an EHR-S Functional Model Record Lifecycle Events (RLE) profile of AuditEvent in FHIR Core.
- **Symptom:** Section/heading numbers render as question marks (`?`) rather than proper numeric identifiers.
- **Scope:** Reporter noted other profile pages appear correct; the issue may be limited to this page or the EHR-S RLE module pages.
- **Root Cause (likely):** A character encoding or template rendering issue in the FHIR publisher when generating heading numbers for this particular IG module.
- **Age:** Filed in March 2020 and has remained unresolved for over 5 years.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive (Accept — Fix the rendering)
- **Resolution:** Persuasive
- **Action:** Investigate and fix the heading number rendering on the EHR-S RLE AuditEvent profile page so that section numbers display correctly instead of question marks. This likely involves fixing a character encoding or template data issue in the FHIR publisher build for the `ehrsrle` module. Verify that all other EHR-S RLE module pages are also rendering correctly.
- **Rationale:** The defect is clearly reproducible, the fix is straightforward (a publishing/rendering bug), and similar issues in other IGs (FHIR-40854, FHIR-40517) have been resolved as Persuasive. Correct section numbering is important for readability and navigation.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Broader Scope
- **Resolution:** Persuasive
- **Action:** Fix the heading number rendering on the affected page and also audit all EHR-S RLE module pages for similar rendering issues. Consider adding a build-time validation check to the FHIR publisher that detects question marks or invalid characters in heading numbers.
- **Rationale:** Given that multiple similar tickets have been filed across different IGs (FHIR-40854, FHIR-40517, FHIR-35749, FHIR-52445), a systemic check would prevent recurrence. However, this broader scope may exceed the intent of the original ticket.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive (Decline)
- **Resolution:** Not Persuasive
- **Action:** Close as not persuasive. The EHR-S RLE module is a low-traffic area of the specification, and the issue is cosmetic. Publisher tooling has evolved significantly since 2020, and the issue may have self-resolved with subsequent publisher updates.
- **Rationale:** The ticket is over 5 years old, low priority, and the page may have been regenerated with newer publisher versions that no longer exhibit this bug. However, declining without verification is not ideal practice.

---

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Persuasive** — This is a legitimate, clearly described rendering bug. The fix is low-risk and aligns with how similar section numbering issues have been resolved across other IGs (FHIR-40854, FHIR-40517, FHIR-52445 were all resolved as Persuasive). Before applying a fix, verify whether the issue still exists on the current published page, as publisher tooling updates since 2020 may have already resolved it. If the issue has self-resolved, resolve as Persuasive with a note that no change is needed.
