## Description

The reporter finds the overall organization and content of the Personal Health Record (PHR) IG to be disorganized. Specific concerns include:

1. **Longitudinal Records page placement** — The Longitudinal Records page (page 23) appears only at the end of the document with no clear rationale for that ordering.
2. **Functional model references not grouped** — References to the functional model are scattered throughout the IG rather than being logically grouped together.
3. **Suggestion to break into multiple IGs** — The IG contains substantial content (e.g., Apple HealthKit tools) that might be better as standalone artifacts.
4. **Extraneous content** — Material about Algorithms, Physiological Domains, and Annotations appears extraneous to the stated purpose of defining the SPHR data exchange mechanism and might be better as a separate informative guide for implementing PHR software.

## Resolution Description

> Persuasive. There have been multiple rounds of editing and organizational change, as we incorporate JIRA tickets. We broke out the Apple HealthKit content and sent to LOINC instead; and have merged in the PGHD guide contents. Many structural changes, including new pages, etc. This is an ongoing area of refinement; but we substantially agree.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept as Persuasive with Modification (Affirm Current Resolution)
Accept the existing "Persuasive with Modification" resolution. The work group has already acknowledged the concerns and taken action: Apple HealthKit content has been broken out and sent to LOINC, PGHD guide content has been merged in, and multiple structural changes have been made. The resolution appropriately scopes the response — the work group agrees with the organizational critique but frames it as an ongoing area of refinement rather than a single discrete fix. This allows continued iterative improvement without over-committing to a specific reorganization plan.

**Pros:** Honors the substantial work already done; aligns with the comment history showing active engagement; avoids reopening a broad organizational issue that is being addressed incrementally.
**Cons:** Does not provide a concrete checklist of remaining organizational changes; the reporter's suggestion to break into multiple IGs is not explicitly addressed.

### Disposition B: Persuasive — Request Concrete Reorganization Plan
Upgrade to "Persuasive" and request that the work group produce a documented reorganization plan addressing each specific concern: (1) placement of Longitudinal Records content, (2) grouping of functional model references, (3) whether Algorithms/Physiological Domains/Annotations content should be separated into an informative companion guide, and (4) a reading guide or navigation aid for implementers (complementing FHIR-49244). This would provide a trackable commitment beyond the general acknowledgment.

**Pros:** Creates accountability for each specific concern raised; provides a clearer path to resolution; coordinates with FHIR-49244's request for a reading guide.
**Cons:** May be overly prescriptive for ballot reconciliation; the work group has already demonstrated active reorganization; could create duplicate work given overlapping tickets.

### Disposition C: Persuasive with Modification — Defer Final Reorganization
Accept as "Persuasive with Modification" but explicitly note that final IG reorganization should occur after all other ballot reconciliation tickets (especially FHIR-49244 and FHIR-49491) are resolved, consistent with the awatson1978 comment from August 2025. Add a condition that the work group should produce a summary of organizational changes made in response to this and related tickets before the next ballot cycle.

**Pros:** Pragmatic — avoids reorganizing before other content changes land; ensures a post-reconciliation review; creates a record of what was done.
**Cons:** Defers accountability; risks the reorganization being deprioritized if not explicitly tracked.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Accept as Persuasive with Modification (Affirm Current Resolution)**

The work group has already taken concrete action on the core concerns — Apple HealthKit content was separated, PGHD content was merged, and structural changes are ongoing. The comment history shows genuine engagement with the feedback, and the resolution description accurately captures the scope of work done. Given that FHIR-49244 separately tracks the need for scope clarity and reader guidance, and FHIR-49491 separately tracks the Apple HealthKit terminology issue, the organizational concerns raised here are being addressed through multiple coordinated tickets. Affirming the current resolution acknowledges the work done while leaving room for continued refinement.
