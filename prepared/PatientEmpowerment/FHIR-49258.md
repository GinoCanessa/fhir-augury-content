## Description

The reporter asks how the profiles listed under "Structures: Logical Models" in the Personal Health Record (PHR) IG are connected to the rest of the IG. Specifically, there are many profiles derived from Apple HealthKit, and the reporter requests that:

1. The relationship/connection between these logical models and the IG be clearly stated in the IG narrative.
2. Guidance be provided on **when** and **how** these logical models should be used by implementers.

## Analysis

This ticket raises a legitimate documentation gap in the PHR IG. The "Structures: Logical Models" section contains numerous Apple HealthKit-derived profiles, but the IG does not clearly explain:

- **Why** these logical models exist in the IG (their purpose and relationship to the PHR specification).
- **When** implementers should use them (use cases, scenarios).
- **How** they connect to the FHIR resource profiles defined elsewhere in the IG (mapping, transformation, or reference patterns).

This is part of a broader pattern of feedback about the PHR IG's organization (see FHIR-49075), where reviewers have noted that the HealthKit content is substantial but poorly contextualized. The comment from awatson1978 indicates the editorial team plans to address this by adding documentation.

The fix is primarily editorial/narrative — adding explanatory text to the IG that describes the role of logical models, their relationship to Apple HealthKit, and guidance for implementers on when and how to use them.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive
Accept the ticket as-is. The IG editors add clear narrative documentation explaining:
- The purpose and scope of the Apple HealthKit logical models within the PHR IG.
- How these logical models relate to the FHIR resource profiles in the IG.
- When and how implementers should use these logical models (e.g., for mapping HealthKit data to FHIR resources).

**Rationale:** The request is straightforward and aligns with existing editorial plans (per awatson1978's comment). The documentation gap is real and affects implementer adoption.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification
Accept with the modification that, in addition to documenting the logical models, the IG editors also add a clear mapping or linkage table showing which logical models correspond to which FHIR resource profiles, and consider adding an introductory section under "Structures: Logical Models" that orients readers before they encounter the individual profiles. This goes beyond narrative text to provide structured implementer guidance.

**Rationale:** A mapping table or structured linkage would be more actionable for implementers than narrative alone, and addresses the broader organizational concerns raised in FHIR-49075.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive
Reject the ticket on the grounds that logical models are a standard FHIR artifact type and their presence in an IG is self-explanatory; implementers familiar with FHIR should understand their role without additional documentation.

**Rationale:** This position is weak — the sheer volume of Apple HealthKit logical models and their domain-specific nature means even experienced FHIR implementers need contextual guidance.

## Recommendation

**Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification**

The reporter's concern is valid and reflects broader feedback about PHR IG organization (FHIR-49075). Simply adding narrative text (Disposition A) may not fully address the gap — a structured mapping or linkage between the HealthKit logical models and the IG's FHIR resource profiles would give implementers actionable guidance. The editorial team has already signaled intent to address this (awatson1978's comment), so the modification adds specificity to ensure the fix is comprehensive. This also aligns with the "Persuasive with Modification" resolution already applied to the closely related FHIR-49075.
