## Summary

Craig Newman identified nine typos and textual issues across multiple sections of the Personal Health Record (FHIR) IG. The issues range from simple spelling mistakes to a grammatical error and an incomplete sentence. The ticket was marked Persuasive with a comment from awatson1978 confirming "Persuasive. Will fix."

## Detailed Issues Reported

1. **Section 1.4** — "psychaitry" → "psychiatry"; "relavent" → "relevant"
2. **Section 2** — "espcially" → "especially"
3. **Section 2.3.1** — "The process flows below is based" — plural/singular mismatch (should be "process flow … is" or "process flows … are")
4. **Section 2.3.1** — "sign then with a FHIR Signature" → "sign them with a FHIR Signature"
5. **Section 5** — "inlcluded" → "included"
6. **Annotations Page** — Section numbers are incorrect / don't consistently increment
7. **Section 7.3** — "hesitate to pass on a patient summary containing information" — sentence appears incomplete (all summaries contain information; likely missing qualifier such as "sensitive" or "confidential")
8. **Section 9** — "assachusetts" → "Massachusetts" (missing "M")
9. **Section 9** — "lead to the" → "led to the" (past tense)

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept as Persuasive (Apply All Corrections)

Accept the ticket as Persuasive and apply all nine corrections as reported:

1. Fix "psychaitry" → "psychiatry" and "relavent" → "relevant" in Section 1.4
2. Fix "espcially" → "especially" in Section 2
3. Fix plural/singular mismatch in Section 2.3.1 (change to "The process flow below is based" or "The process flows below are based")
4. Fix "then" → "them" in Section 2.3.1
5. Fix "inlcluded" → "included" in Section 5
6. Correct annotation page section numbering to increment consistently
7. Clarify incomplete sentence in Section 7.3 (e.g., "containing *sensitive* information" or "containing *confidential* information")
8. Fix "assachusetts" → "Massachusetts" in Section 9
9. Fix "lead" → "led" in Section 9

**Rationale:** These are straightforward corrections that improve readability and professionalism of the IG. The resolution description already notes "Typo fixed" and a commenter confirmed "Persuasive. Will fix."

### Disposition B: Accept as Persuasive with Partial Scope

Accept the eight clear-cut spelling/grammar fixes (items 1–6, 8–9) as Persuasive. Defer item 7 (Section 7.3 incomplete sentence) to a separate ticket for editorial review, as it may require subject-matter input to determine the intended meaning (e.g., "sensitive information," "confidential information," or "stigmatizing information").

**Rationale:** The straightforward typos can be fixed immediately, while the incomplete sentence in Section 7.3 may need workgroup discussion on the intended qualifier.

### Disposition C: Decline as Not Persuasive

Decline the ticket on the grounds that the reported issues are cosmetic and do not affect the normative or technical content of the IG. Request that the reporter resubmit individual corrections as separate errata items if needed.

**Rationale:** This is not recommended. Typos undermine credibility and readability. The workgroup has already acknowledged these as valid corrections.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Accept as Persuasive (Apply All Corrections)**

The ticket has already been marked Persuasive with a confirming comment from awatson1978. All nine issues are clearly identified with specific section references and proposed corrections. The fixes are low-risk, non-controversial, and improve the quality of the IG. For the incomplete sentence in Section 7.3, the most likely intended meaning is "sensitive information" or "confidential information" given the privacy context of that section — the editor can select the appropriate qualifier during implementation.
