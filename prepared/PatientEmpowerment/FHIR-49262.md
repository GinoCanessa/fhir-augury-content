## Summary

Ricardo Quintano questions the relevance of the "Operating Systems" page in the Personal Health Record (PHR) Implementation Guide. The reporter does not understand why the IG includes guidance on implementing FHIR across different operating systems, or how the information on that page relates to FHIR implementation. A workgroup member (awatson1978) acknowledged an internal debate about the IG's intended audience—professional developers versus patients and patient advocates with less programming experience—and noted the page was aimed at novice implementors who may need additional help getting started. However, the commenter also acknowledged the page is disorganized ("a hodge-podge and scrap yard of notes") and would need significant work if retained. In a more recent comment, the same member agreed the OS-specific content is unnecessary and difficult to maintain going forward.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Remove the Operating Systems page from the Personal Health Record Implementation Guide entirely. The content does not directly pertain to FHIR implementation and is outside the expected scope of the IG.

#### Justification

The reporter's concern is well-founded: OS-specific guidance is not a core part of a FHIR IG. Even the workgroup member most familiar with the page has agreed that the guide does not need to cover operating systems and that such content would be difficult to maintain. Removing the page simplifies the IG and keeps its focus on FHIR-relevant content.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Replace the Operating Systems page with a brief "Getting Started" or "Prerequisites" section that provides links to external resources (e.g., official FHIR developer documentation, tooling installation guides) rather than maintaining OS-specific instructions within the IG itself.

#### Justification

If the IG's intended audience includes novice implementors, some onboarding guidance may still be valuable. However, rather than maintaining OS-specific content that quickly becomes outdated, a curated set of external links would serve the same purpose with much lower maintenance burden. This preserves the intent of the page while addressing the reporter's concern about relevance and the commenter's concern about maintainability.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Retain the Operating Systems page in the IG, with a note that it is intended for novice implementors and will be revised for clarity and accuracy in a future cycle.

#### Justification

The page was originally created to serve an audience of patients and patient advocates with limited programming experience. If the workgroup determines that this audience remains a primary target, some form of OS-level guidance could be justified. Declining the change request would preserve the content while acknowledging the need for future improvement.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A (Accept As Requested)

The workgroup's own internal discussion has converged on the view that OS-specific content is out of scope for this IG and unsustainable to maintain. The most recent comment from awatson1978 (August 2025) explicitly states the guide does not need to cover operating systems. Removing the page aligns with both the reporter's feedback and the workgroup member's latest position, eliminates a maintenance burden, and sharpens the IG's focus on FHIR-specific content. If onboarding guidance for novice users is desired in the future, it can be reintroduced in a more targeted form (as described in Disposition B) without retaining the current disorganized page.
