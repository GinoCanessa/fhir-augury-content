## Issue Description

The reporter identified an inconsistency in the Personal Health Record (PHR) FHIR Implementation Guide. The IG's introductory text states:

> *"The purpose of this implementation guide is to help the reader implement a **Patient Health Record** (in a programming language of their choice)."*

However, the IG itself is titled the **Personal** Health Record. The reporter asks: is it a "Patient Health Record" or a "Personal Health Record"? What is the difference?

The resolution description simply states: "Typo fixed."

## Analysis

This is a straightforward terminology inconsistency. The IG is titled "Personal Health Record" but the introductory text uses "Patient Health Record." The workgroup has confirmed they are standardizing on **"Personal Health Record"** as the canonical term. The resolution description confirms the typo has been conceptually addressed ("Typo fixed"). This is consistent with FHIR-20170's earlier resolution of the same Patient/Personal confusion in FHIR Core.

The broader naming confusion is also being addressed by FHIR-50763, which recommends distinguishing between "Personal Health Record" (the concept/system) and ".phr/.sphr" (the file format), and FHIR-49073 which captures another instance of "Patient Health Record" in Section 1.4.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept as Persuasive (Recommended)
**Action:** Accept the existing resolution of **Persuasive** and ensure all instances of "Patient Health Record" in the IG are replaced with "Personal Health Record."

**Rationale:** The reporter correctly identified an inconsistency. The workgroup has confirmed alignment on "Personal Health Record" as the standard term. The resolution note "Typo fixed" indicates the specific instance has been corrected. This is a clear-cut terminology fix with no design impact. Coordinate with FHIR-49073 to ensure all remaining "Patient Health Record" references are caught in a single pass.

### Disposition B: Accept as Persuasive with Modification — Broader Audit
**Action:** Accept as **Persuasive with Modification**, expanding the scope to include a full audit of the IG for all occurrences of "Patient Health Record" and any other naming inconsistencies (e.g., "SPHR standard" vs. "PHR FHIR IG").

**Rationale:** The single typo fix may not be sufficient — awatson1978's comment explicitly identified the need for a broader audit. FHIR-50763 demonstrates that naming ambiguity is a recurring issue throughout the IG. A modification to broaden the fix would proactively prevent future confusion.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive — Already Addressed Elsewhere
**Action:** Mark as **Not Persuasive** on the grounds that the naming issue is already being comprehensively addressed by FHIR-50763 and FHIR-49073.

**Rationale:** The specific typo instance has been fixed, and the broader naming cleanup is covered by related tickets with higher scope. Accepting this separately risks duplicate or conflicting changes. However, this approach risks the specific instance falling through the cracks if the related tickets are delayed.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Accept as Persuasive.** The ticket correctly identifies a straightforward terminology error. The resolution ("Typo fixed") is appropriate for the narrow scope of this Technical Correction. The broader audit concern is legitimately raised but is better tracked under FHIR-50763 (which has "Highest" priority and broader scope). Accepting as-is keeps the resolution clean and avoids scope creep on a simple typo fix.
