## Description

The reporter states that Section 1.5 of the PHR FHIR IG does not actually describe use cases — instead it describes concepts. The reporter argues it is important to include a set of common use cases to help readers understand the aim of the IG, and points to the IHE Scheduling IG ([volume-1.html §1.55.4.2](https://profiles.ihe.net/ITI/Scheduling/volume-1.html#15542-use-cases)) as a good example of how use cases should be presented.

## Comments Summary

1. **awatson1978 (2025-01-30):** Marked as Persuasive. Noted the workgroup can expand on use cases. Raised a todo to discuss with the workgroup whether to separate use cases to their own page.

2. **awatson1978 (2025-03-13):** Posted a detailed draft of expanded use case content covering 12 scenarios:
   - **Longitudinal Health Records** — assembly of records over decades across healthcare systems
   - **Longitudinal Studies** — clinical studies following patients 20+ years (e.g., childhood asthma research)
   - **Snowbirds** — patients with seasonal multi-location care (fragmented records)
   - **Symptom Tracking** — evolving symptom monitoring (e.g., MS symptom tracking with environmental data)
   - **Long COVID** — multi-institution research registry tracking persistent symptoms
   - **Multiple Chronic Conditions** — reconciling records across multiple specialists
   - **Differential Diagnoses** — unified timeline for complex diagnostic workups across systems
   - **Alternative Care** — tracking complementary therapies not captured in traditional EHRs
   - **Bring Your Own Device** — consumer medical device integration (CGMs, smart pumps, fitness trackers)
   - **Foster Care System** — tracking health records across custody/jurisdiction changes
   - **Migrants/Immigrants** — integrating fragmented international records
   - **Climate Refugees** — maintaining record continuity after forced relocation

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive (Accept as-is)

**Action:** Accept the ticket as Persuasive and incorporate the 12 draft use cases from awatson1978's 2025-03-13 comment into Section 1.5 (or a new dedicated use cases page), replacing or supplementing the current concept-focused content.

**Rationale:** The workgroup has already acknowledged the feedback is valid (marked Persuasive on 2025-01-30) and produced substantial draft content covering 12 detailed use case narratives. The reporter's concern is well-founded — use cases help implementers understand the practical scope of the IG. The draft content is comprehensive and covers diverse real-world scenarios (longitudinal records, chronic conditions, foster care, immigrants, climate refugees, device integration). This aligns with how other IGs (e.g., IHE Scheduling) present use cases. Accepting allows the workgroup to finalize and integrate the existing draft.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification — Separate use cases to a dedicated page

**Action:** Accept the ticket as Persuasive with Modification. Create a new dedicated "Use Cases" page in the IG (separate from Section 1.5), incorporate the 12 draft use cases, and keep Section 1.5 focused on high-level concepts with a cross-reference link to the new page.

**Rationale:** The awatson1978 comment on 2025-01-30 already raised the question of whether to "separate out to its own page." Given the volume of use case content (12 detailed narratives), embedding all of it in Section 1.5 could overwhelm that section. A separate page would improve IG organization — which is a concern raised in related tickets FHIR-49075 and FHIR-49244. The new use cases page could also serve as a natural home for additional use cases being proposed in FHIR-53514 ("Healthy Living") and FHIR-53509 ("lifelog"). Section 1.5 would retain a brief summary and link.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive — Defer pending IG reorganization

**Action:** Defer this ticket until the broader IG reorganization (FHIR-49075) is resolved, as the location and structure of use case content depends on the overall IG architecture.

**Rationale:** FHIR-49075 proposes significant restructuring of the entire IG, potentially breaking it into multiple IGs. Adding extensive use case content to the current structure may need to be redone if the IG is reorganized. However, this risks indefinite delay on a valid and already-drafted improvement.

---

## Recommendation

**Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification — Separate use cases to a dedicated page**

This disposition best addresses the reporter's concern while also aligning with the workgroup's own suggestion (awatson1978's todo to discuss separating to its own page) and the related IG organization tickets. The 12 draft use cases are already well-developed and ready for editorial review. Creating a dedicated use cases page keeps Section 1.5 clean while providing implementers with the practical guidance the reporter requested. It also creates a natural landing page for the additional use cases being proposed in FHIR-53514 and FHIR-53509.
