## Summary

The reporter identifies significant overlap and duplication of content across multiple patient persona pages in the Personal Health Record (FHIR) specification. Specifically, the **Patient Personas** (page 11), **William Sim** (page 12), and **Personas Index** (page 19) pages contain redundant or repeated information. The request asks that the content be consolidated so that information is presented only once.

## Analysis

This is an **editorial/structural change request** focused on content organization rather than technical or normative content. The reporter's concern is straightforward: three distinct pages in the Personal Health Record IG (Patient Personas, William Sim individual persona, and Personas Index) contain overlapping material that should be deduplicated.

**Key considerations:**

1. **Content organization precedent:** In other IGs (e.g., US SDOH Clinical Care), persona pages have been scrutinized for relevance and organization (FHIR-30363, FHIR-31142). The SDOH IG resolved similar concerns by either refining or removing persona pages.

2. **Active persona work in this spec:** FHIR-50703 asks for more context in persona/scope sections, and FHIR-53517 proposes adding IPS-specific persona content. Resolving duplication now would provide a cleaner foundation before adding new persona material.

3. **No controversy:** There are no Zulip discussions or comments suggesting disagreement with this request. The concern is non-controversial — it's asking for better editorial hygiene.

4. **Scope is well-defined:** The reporter identifies the specific pages (11, 12, 19) where overlap occurs, making the editorial task concrete and actionable.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive
**Action:** Accept the request and consolidate persona content so that each piece of information appears only once across the Patient Personas, William Sim, and Personas Index pages. Establish a clear structure where the Personas Index serves as the navigational hub, individual persona pages (e.g., William Sim) contain detailed persona-specific content, and the Patient Personas overview page provides context without repeating details found on sub-pages.

**Rationale:** The overlap is clearly identified, the fix is editorial in nature, and deduplication improves readability and maintainability. This also creates a clean foundation for planned persona additions (FHIR-53517).

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification
**Action:** Accept the concern but broaden the scope to restructure the entire persona section holistically, considering FHIR-50703's request for additional context and FHIR-53517's IPS persona additions. Deduplicate content as requested, and simultaneously reorganize the section to clearly define the purpose and scope of each persona page.

**Rationale:** Addressing this ticket in isolation may result in rework when related tickets are processed. A coordinated restructuring of the persona section would be more efficient and produce a more coherent result.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive
**Action:** Decline the change. Acknowledge the overlap but note that some degree of repetition across persona pages may be intentional to allow each page to stand alone as a self-contained reference.

**Rationale:** Some IGs intentionally include redundant introductory content on sub-pages so that readers arriving via deep links have sufficient context. However, the reporter's specific examples suggest the overlap goes beyond helpful repetition.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Persuasive** — The overlap is clearly identified with specific page references, the fix is editorial and low-risk, and cleaning up the duplication now will benefit the ongoing persona-related work in FHIR-50703 and FHIR-53517. The work group should consolidate the content so each piece of information appears exactly once, with appropriate cross-references between the overview, index, and individual persona pages.
