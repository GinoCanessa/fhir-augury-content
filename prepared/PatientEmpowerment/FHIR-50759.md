# FHIR-50759: Several statements regarding record lifecycle section appear incorrect

## Summary

**Ticket:** [FHIR-50759](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50759)
**Status:** Triaged | **Priority:** Highest | **Type:** Change Request
**Specification:** Personal Health Record (FHIR)
**Work Group:** Patient Empowerment
**Reporter:** Virginia Lorenzi
**Created:** 2025-05-06 | **Updated:** 2025-05-12

This change request raises two concerns about the Record Lifecycle section of the Personal Health Record FHIR IG:

1. **Inappropriate vendor-specific reference:** The IG text includes a note referencing the [Patient Data Receipt](https://open-health-manager.github.io/patient-data-receipt-ig/) implementation guide and calls it a "standard." The reporter notes this IG is vendor-owned (by MITRE/Open Health Manager) and is not an HL7 standard. Referring to a single vendor's guide as a "standard" within an HL7 specification is considered inappropriate and potentially misleading.

2. **Incorrect characterization of EHR as central repository:** The IG text states "The EHR system acts as the central 'master' repository, serving as the authoritative source of truth for the patient's health data. Updates and changes originate from the EHR." The reporter argues this is factually incorrect from the PHR perspective — patients who receive care at multiple facilities, clinics, and doctors have no single EHR that serves as a central repository. The PHR, not any single EHR, should be recognized as the patient's central record.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive — Remove vendor reference and rewrite EHR-centric language

**Resolution:** Persuasive
**Action:**
1. Remove the specific reference to the Patient Data Receipt IG and replace with a general statement describing the desired capability (e.g., "We envision these atomic updates as eventually including standardized data receipt mechanisms that provide patients with verifiable records of data exchanges.").
2. Rewrite the EHR-as-central-repository paragraph to acknowledge that patients often have records across multiple EHR systems and that the PHR serves as the patient's unified, longitudinal health record.

**Rationale:** Both concerns are well-founded. Referencing a vendor-owned IG as a "standard" is inappropriate in an HL7 specification, and characterizing a single EHR as the central repository contradicts the fundamental purpose of a PHR. These changes align with the IG's own stated goals and with the use cases accepted in FHIR-49225 (e.g., snowbirds, immigrants, multiple chronic conditions), all of which position the PHR — not any single EHR — as the central record.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification — Partial changes with context retained

**Resolution:** Persuasive with Modification
**Action:**
1. Retain a reference to the Patient Data Receipt concept but remove the specific URL and label it as an "emerging approach" rather than a "standard" (e.g., "We envision these atomic updates as eventually incorporating patient data receipt patterns, such as those being explored in related implementation guides.").
2. Revise the EHR paragraph to acknowledge multiple EHR systems but frame it as a common architectural pattern rather than removing the concept entirely (e.g., "In many care settings, an EHR system may serve as a local authoritative source for data originating from that organization. However, for patients receiving care across multiple organizations, the PHR provides the unified longitudinal view.").

**Rationale:** The Patient Data Receipt concept is relevant to the IG's goals even if the specific IG is vendor-owned; a softened reference preserves traceability to related work. The EHR language can be corrected without entirely dismissing the EHR's role in its own domain.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive — Maintain current text

**Resolution:** Not Persuasive
**Action:** No change. Add a non-normative clarifying note acknowledging the Patient Data Receipt IG is external, and that the EHR statement applies in single-provider contexts.

**Rationale:** The existing text could be read as aspirational (for the Patient Data Receipt reference) and as describing a common deployment scenario (for the EHR statement). Adding clarifying notes may be sufficient.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Persuasive** is recommended.

Both issues raised are substantive and well-supported:
- The Patient Data Receipt IG is not an HL7 standard and should not be presented as one in an HL7 specification. Removing the specific reference and describing the capability generically is the safest and most appropriate approach. If the Patient Data Receipt work is later brought into HL7, it can be referenced at that time.
- The EHR-as-central-repository language directly contradicts the patient-centric philosophy of the PHR IG. The use cases already accepted in FHIR-49225 (longitudinal records, snowbirds, migrants, multiple chronic conditions) all assume patients have data across multiple EHR systems. The IG should consistently reflect this reality. Related tickets from the same ballot cycle (FHIR-50756, FHIR-50763, FHIR-50734) form a cohesive set of feedback requesting clearer, more accurate language throughout the IG, and this ticket fits squarely within that pattern.
