## Description

The submitter observes that the Personal Health Record (PHR) IG, despite being published in the **universal (UV) realm**, has a regulatory and conformance scope that is heavily US-centric. Two specific concerns are raised:

1. **Section 1.3 – "Relevant Law"**: The list of relevant regulations omits non-US legislation. The submitter requests at minimum adding the **European Health Data Space (EHDS) Regulation** and suggests surveying other jurisdictions for additional relevant laws.

2. **Section 3 – "Conformance Recommendations"**: The conformance section references US Core profiles but does not mention equivalent base/core IGs from other regions. The submitter points to the **HL7 Europe Base Profiles** (currently under development at [build.fhir.org/ig/hl7-eu/base/](https://build.fhir.org/ig/hl7-eu/base/)) as an example of work being done to meet EHDS requirements, and calls for international coordination to include such profiles.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive

**Rationale:** The submitter's observation is valid — a UV-realm IG should not be exclusively scoped to US regulatory and conformance frameworks. The PHR IG's "Relevant Law" section should be expanded to include the EHDS Regulation and other major international health data regulations. The "Conformance Recommendations" section should be updated to acknowledge and reference base/core IGs from other jurisdictions (such as the HL7 Europe Base Profiles and IPA) alongside US Core. This aligns with HL7's stated goal of universal interoperability and is consistent with the approach taken in FHIR-49123 (which was resolved as Persuasive with Modification for US Core's relationship with IPA).

**Actions required:**
- Add the EHDS Regulation to Section 1.3 "Relevant Law."
- Survey and add other major international health data regulations (e.g., Australia's My Health Record Act, Canada's provincial health information acts).
- Update Section 3 "Conformance Recommendations" to reference IPA and regional base/core IGs alongside US Core.
- Add guidance for implementers on how to select and layer jurisdiction-specific profiles.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification

**Rationale:** The core concern is valid, but the scope of changes should be limited to what is immediately actionable. Rather than attempting a comprehensive international regulatory survey, the IG should add the EHDS Regulation to Section 1.3 and add a general statement in Section 3 acknowledging that UV-realm implementations should consider jurisdiction-specific base profiles (with IPA and EU Base as named examples). A comprehensive international expansion can be pursued in a future ballot cycle as more regional base profiles mature.

**Actions required:**
- Add the EHDS Regulation to Section 1.3 "Relevant Law."
- Add a paragraph to Section 3 noting that implementers should consider jurisdiction-appropriate base/core IGs (citing IPA, EU Base, AU Core as examples).
- Defer comprehensive international regulatory cataloging to a future cycle.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive

**Rationale:** While the observation has merit, the PHR IG in its current form is an initial publication focused on establishing core PHR concepts. The US-specific references reflect the maturity of US Core relative to other regional profiles. The HL7 Europe Base Profiles are still under development (pre-ballot), and adding references to immature specifications could create maintenance burden and misleading conformance expectations. The IG already uses the UV realm for its base resource profiles, and jurisdiction-specific layering is expected to happen via derived IGs rather than within the UV-realm IG itself.

## Recommendation

**Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification** is recommended.

The submitter raises a legitimate concern — a UV-realm IG should not be exclusively US-focused in its regulatory and conformance framing. However, a full international regulatory survey is premature given the varying maturity of regional base profiles. The practical path forward is to:

1. Immediately add the EHDS Regulation to Section 1.3, as it is a major enacted regulation with direct relevance to PHR data exchange.
2. Add a general acknowledgment in Section 3 that UV-realm conformance should consider jurisdiction-appropriate profiles, naming IPA, EU Base, and AU Core as concrete examples.
3. Defer comprehensive expansion to a future ballot cycle when more regional profiles have reached publication maturity.

This approach addresses the submitter's core concern without overcommitting to references that may change, and it aligns with the direction established by FHIR-49123 (US Core's relationship with IPA) and FHIR-51383 (EU Base alignment with IPA).
