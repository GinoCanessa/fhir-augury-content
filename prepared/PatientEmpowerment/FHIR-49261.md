## Description

The submitter notes that the PHR IG uses "EMR" in the first paragraph but "EHR" later in the sentence: *"What it does provide, is guidance on how to successfully exchange data with other PHR and **EHR** apps."* The request is to use these terms consistently throughout the specification, or at least provide clear definitions of each.

## Resolution Description

Typo fixed. The terminology has been harmonized to be consistent.

## Comments

- **awatson1978** (2025-01-30): Acknowledged that there was no particular reason for the inconsistency. Committed to harmonizing and making the usage consistent.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept as Persuasive (Apply as Resolved)

Accept the existing resolution. The submitter is correct that "EHR" and "EMR" were used inconsistently. The resolution description states the typo has been fixed and terminology harmonized. This is a straightforward technical correction with no normative impact.

- **Pros:** Simple, non-controversial fix. Aligns with precedent set by FHIR-18116 and FHIR-36285, both of which were resolved by harmonizing EHR/EMR terminology. The assignee (awatson1978) has already acknowledged and committed to the fix.
- **Cons:** None significant. The work group may wish to verify which term (EHR or EMR) was chosen for consistency.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification — Add Definitions

Accept the correction and additionally require a brief definitions section or glossary entry distinguishing EHR from EMR (per ONC definitions) and PHR, to prevent future confusion.

- **Pros:** Addresses the submitter's secondary request for clear definitions. Provides lasting clarity for readers unfamiliar with the distinction. Follows the approach suggested in FHIR-18116 (referencing ONC definitions).
- **Cons:** Scope creep beyond a simple typo fix. The PHR IG may not need to define industry-standard terms. Could be addressed in a separate ticket if desired.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive

Reject, arguing that EHR and EMR are commonly understood as interchangeable in many contexts, and the distinction is not material to the PHR specification's guidance.

- **Pros:** No editorial work required.
- **Cons:** Ignores a legitimate inconsistency. Contradicts the prior HL7 precedent of harmonizing these terms (FHIR-18116, FHIR-36285). The assignee has already agreed the fix is warranted.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Accept as Persuasive (Apply as Resolved).**

This is a clear-cut technical correction. The inconsistent use of "EHR" vs. "EMR" in the PHR IG was acknowledged by the assignee, and the resolution states the typo has been fixed. HL7 has a strong precedent of resolving identical issues as Persuasive (FHIR-18116, FHIR-36285). No further modification is needed — if the work group wants formal definitions added, that can be tracked as a separate enhancement ticket.
