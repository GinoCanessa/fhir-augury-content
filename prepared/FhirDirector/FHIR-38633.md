## Summary

Gay Dolin requests a new element or extension to capture whether a patient (or other subject) is aware of a clinical finding, condition, or result. The concept originates from CDA, which supports an `awarenessCode` element using the HL7 TargetAwareness value set (OID 2.16.840.1.113883.5.137). The reporter argues this capability is broadly applicable across all Clinical and Diagnostic Resources. The ticket was originally motivated by NIH requirements for the Multiple Chronic Care IG. After extensive cross-work-group discussion—including OO on FHIR, Patient Empowerment, Security, and FHIR-I—the ticket was assigned to FHIR Director at the January 2026 WGM. FHIR-I has agreed the concept should be represented in FHIR but requires further design refinement before determining the best representation (extension, new resource, or existing resource).

## Details

**Description:**

The reporter notes that CDA has the ability to capture a patient's awareness of clinical information via the `awarenessCode` element, using the TargetAwareness value set (https://terminology.hl7.org/2.1.0/ValueSet-v3-TargetAwareness.html) from code system TargetAwareness (2.16.840.1.113883.5.137). At least one vendor uses this in practice for results. FHIR currently has no equivalent element or extension on Condition, Observation, or other clinical resources. The reporter proposes this would be a useful element on all Clinical and Diagnostic Resources.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Create a complex extension applicable to DomainResource that captures target awareness information, including: an awareness status code (using the existing TargetAwareness value set), the target actor (patient, caregiver, related person), a relationship type, and a timestamp. Publish this as an STU extension in the FHIR Extensions Pack, making it usable on any clinical or diagnostic resource.

#### Justification

The concept has an established precedent in CDA with the `awarenessCode` element and an existing HL7 value set. Multiple work groups (OO on FHIR, FHIR-I, Patient Empowerment) have agreed the concept is valid and should be representable in FHIR. An extension is the least disruptive approach, can be published incrementally via the Extensions Pack, and allows implementers to adopt it where needed without waiting for a full specification cycle. The OO on FHIR group already proposed a reasonable backbone structure in January 2023.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Defer implementation and assign to Patient Care (with Patient Empowerment input) for a formal clinical design analysis. The analysis should address the open questions raised by FHIR-I: scope of target actors, temporal aspects, granularity of understanding levels, and whether this belongs as an extension, a new resource (e.g., a PatientAwareness or PatientEmpowerment resource), or tracked within existing resources such as Communication or Observation. Return the refined proposal to FHIR Director for final disposition once the design is complete.

#### Justification

FHIR-I explicitly stated at the May 2025 WGM that the concept needs more refinement before the appropriate representation can be determined. Multiple commenters raised fundamental design questions—John Moehrke suggested a standalone resource, Floyd Eisenberg proposed a PatientEmpowerment resource, and Ralf Herzog questioned whether this is communication- or workflow-related. Proceeding without answering these questions risks creating an extension that is later superseded or conflicts with a broader patient empowerment model. A structured design phase would produce a more durable and well-considered solution.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request on the basis that existing FHIR resources and mechanisms—Communication for documenting patient disclosures, Resource.meta.security for access restrictions, and Observation for recording patient-reported understanding—already provide sufficient representational capability for target awareness scenarios without requiring a new extension or element.

#### Justification

Lloyd McKenzie noted that Communication can already document information sharing with patients, and Resource.meta.security can convey "do not tell patient." John Moehrke questioned whether Encounter or EpisodeOfCare already cover this need. There is limited demonstrated implementation demand in FHIR (one known CDA vendor), and the concept has been open since 2022 without producing a concrete, implementer-validated design. Declining would avoid adding underspecified surface area to the specification while leaving implementers free to create local extensions as needed.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B — Alternative Approach

The broad consensus across work groups is that target awareness is a valid and important concept that should be representable in FHIR. However, FHIR-I's assessment at the May 2025 WGM is clear: the design is not yet refined enough to commit to a specific representation. The open questions are substantive—they affect whether this becomes an extension, a new resource, or a pattern using existing resources, and the answer has significant implications for long-term specification coherence. Assigning to Patient Care for clinical design work (with Patient Empowerment input, as FHIR-I recommended) gives the concept the domain expertise it needs to produce a well-specified proposal. This avoids premature commitment to an extension structure that may not be the right fit, while still affirming the validity of the request and keeping it on a concrete path toward resolution. Once Patient Care delivers a refined design, FHIR Director can expedite final approval.
