## Summary

Giorgio Cangioli requests a rewording of a sentence in the **Design Choices** section (§1.3) of the Laboratory Report IG. The current text contains a redundant fragment ("This guide proposes a scalable approach This is done by supporting a scalable approach…") and awkward phrasing. The proposed replacement streamlines the sentence into clear, grammatically correct language:

> **Current text:**
> "This guide proposes a scalable approach This is done by supporting a scalable approach allowing the different jurisdictions to select the solution that better fits with their requirements; while assuring the capability for everyone to retrieve laboratory report data by searching per DiagnosticReport."

> **Proposed text:**
> "This guide presents a scalable approach that enables different jurisdictions to select the solution that best fits their requirements, while ensuring the ability for all users to retrieve laboratory report data through DiagnosticReport searches."

The change is purely editorial — it corrects the redundant fragment, improves grammar ("better fits" → "best fits"), replaces the semicolon with a comma, and modernizes phrasing ("assuring the capability for everyone" → "ensuring the ability for all users").

## Details

This ticket targets a well-known problematic sentence in the Design Choices section that has drawn multiple overlapping ballot comments. The same redundant "proposes a scalable approach This is done by supporting a scalable approach" fragment is called out independently by **FHIR-48296** (which was resolved as Persuasive) and the semicolon issue is also raised by **FHIR-47569** (also resolved as Persuasive).

More broadly, the entire Design Choices section is undergoing significant rework via several resolved tickets:
- **FHIR-46865** (Persuasive with Modification) rewrites the paragraph to describe *three* design choices rather than two, and also touches the same surrounding text.
- **FHIR-46858** and **FHIR-46852** (both Persuasive with Modification) rename/clarify the section.
- **FHIR-47204** (Persuasive with Modification) improves the overall layout of the page.

Given that FHIR-48296 and FHIR-46865 have already been resolved and target the same sentence, this ticket's proposed change may be partially or fully superseded. The workgroup should verify whether the accumulated resolved changes already address the concerns raised here.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive (Accept the proposed wording)

Accept the submitter's proposed replacement text as-is. The revised sentence is clearer, grammatically correct, and eliminates the redundant fragment. Apply the change to the Design Choices section, coordinating with the resolved changes from FHIR-48296, FHIR-47569, and FHIR-46865 to ensure consistency.

**Pros:** Directly addresses the reported issue; the proposed wording is well-crafted.
**Cons:** May conflict with or duplicate changes already applied via FHIR-48296 and FHIR-46865.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification (Subsume into existing resolved changes)

Acknowledge the submitter's concern as valid but note that the redundant wording and grammar issues have already been addressed by the resolved tickets FHIR-48296 (redundant fragment), FHIR-47569 (semicolon), and FHIR-46865 (paragraph rewrite for three design choices). Mark as Persuasive with Modification, with the modification being that the specific replacement text will be aligned with the already-approved changes rather than adopting FHIR-47202's exact wording verbatim.

**Pros:** Avoids conflicting edits; leverages work already done; ensures the paragraph is internally consistent with the three-choice model from FHIR-46865.
**Cons:** The submitter's exact wording is not used, though the intent is fully honored.

### Disposition C: Duplicate (of FHIR-48296)

Resolve as Duplicate of FHIR-48296, which targets the same redundant sentence fragment and has already been resolved as Persuasive. The grammar and semicolon fixes are also covered by FHIR-47569. Combined with FHIR-46865's paragraph rewrite, all concerns in FHIR-47202 are already addressed.

**Pros:** Clean resolution; no additional editorial work required.
**Cons:** Does not give specific credit to the slightly different replacement wording proposed by Cangioli.

---

## Recommendation

**Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification** is recommended. The submitter's concern is clearly valid — the redundant fragment and awkward phrasing are genuine issues. However, multiple overlapping tickets (FHIR-48296, FHIR-47569, FHIR-46865) have already been resolved and address the same sentence. The most practical path is to acknowledge the submitter's contribution, confirm that the underlying issues are being fixed, and align the final wording with the broader Design Choices section rework (especially FHIR-46865's three-choice model) rather than adopting this ticket's exact replacement text in isolation.
