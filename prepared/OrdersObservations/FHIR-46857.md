## Description

The submitter reports that **Figure 3** in the Laboratory Report (FHIR) IG does not correctly reflect the relationships between the FHIR Document Bundle, Composition, and DiagnosticReport resources.

Key points from the submitter:
- The Bundle does include both Composition and DiagnosticReport, but the diagram does not show how DiagnosticReport relates to Composition.
- DiagnosticReport would relate to **at least one** Composition — the Composition required in a Bundle to make it a FHIR Document.
- A **second relationship MAY exist** when there are multiple DiagnosticReports and a separate Composition is needed to represent the organization of an individual DiagnosticReport.

## Analysis

This ticket addresses a **documentation accuracy issue** in Figure 3 of the Laboratory Report (FHIR) IG. The figure currently shows Bundle containing both Composition and DiagnosticReport but fails to depict the structural relationships between them — specifically:

1. **Required relationship**: Every FHIR Document Bundle must have a Composition as its first entry; DiagnosticReport is referenced from that Composition.
2. **Optional relationship**: When multiple DiagnosticReports exist, a per-report Composition MAY be used to organize the content of an individual DiagnosticReport.

The broader context from related tickets shows the workgroup has already accepted changes to the design choices narrative (FHIR-46865, FHIR-46858) that acknowledge three approaches to laboratory reporting including Composition-organized DiagnosticReport content. However, the related ticket FHIR-46871 (requesting the R5 `.composition` extension) was resolved as Not Persuasive, and FHIR-48059 (opposing combining Composition with DiagnosticReport) was also Not Persuasive — meaning the current design intent supports both resources coexisting. Figure 3 should be updated to reflect this accepted design accurately.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive
**Action:** Update Figure 3 to correctly depict the relationships between Bundle, Composition, and DiagnosticReport as described by the submitter.

- Add an arrow from DiagnosticReport to the document-level Composition (mandatory relationship in a FHIR Document).
- Add an optional arrow showing a per-DiagnosticReport Composition that organizes the content of an individual report.
- Include a legend or annotation clarifying when the second relationship applies (multiple DiagnosticReports scenario).
- Ensure the updated figure aligns with the three design choices accepted in FHIR-46865.

**Rationale:** The figure is factually inaccurate per the IG's own structural rules and the accepted design direction. Correcting it improves implementer understanding and is consistent with already-resolved companion tickets.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification
**Action:** Correct Figure 3 to show the mandatory Composition–DiagnosticReport relationship, but defer depiction of the optional per-report Composition until FHIR-46719 (multiple DiagnosticReports cardinality) is resolved.

- Update Figure 3 to show DiagnosticReport referencing the document-level Composition.
- Add a note that additional Composition relationships may apply when the multiple-DiagnosticReport pattern is finalized.
- Revisit the figure when FHIR-46719 and related structural tickets are resolved.

**Rationale:** The mandatory relationship is unambiguous and should be fixed now. The optional per-report Composition depends on design decisions (e.g., DiagnosticReport cardinality) that are still open.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive
**Action:** No change to Figure 3.

**Rationale:** Figure 3 is a simplified overview diagram intended to show containment within a Bundle, not detailed resource relationships. Detailed relationships are already described in the IG text. Adding relationship arrows to a high-level figure could add complexity without proportional benefit; implementers should refer to the profiles and narrative for precise semantics.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Persuasive** — The figure is objectively incorrect in that it omits the structural relationship between DiagnosticReport and Composition that the IG's own design requires. The workgroup has already accepted the three-design-choice framing (FHIR-46865), and the IG text describes relationships that Figure 3 does not show. Correcting the figure is straightforward, improves clarity for implementers, and aligns with accepted companion changes. There is no dependency on unresolved cardinality questions for the mandatory relationship.
