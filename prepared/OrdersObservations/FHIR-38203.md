## Details

This change request asks the At-Home In-Vitro Test Report IG to expand beyond its current public health reporting scope to also cover **Personal Health Record (PHR)** and **Electronic Health Record (EHR)** use cases. The submitter envisions a within-the-smart-device transaction—where an at-home test app transmits results to another app (PHR or provider EHR) for ingestion—rather than only over-the-network public health reporting.

### History

- **2023-01-05**: Initially resolved as "Considered for Future Use" by vote (Sarah Gaunt / Dan Rutz, 13-0-1) with the rationale that it was an interesting idea but out of scope for the current project.
- **2023-03-26**: Re-opened post STU1 publication. A note was added to the IG directing users to alternative methods (e.g., patient portals) until this use case is formally addressed.
- **2023-01-06** (apitkus comment): Emphasized the need for consumer test results to be ingested by provider EHRs for treatment (including time-sensitive and eCR-reportable scenarios), and suggested the scope should not be limited to COVID but also cover HIV, influenza, drug screening, urinalysis, CBC, etc.
- **2025-12-05**: Hans Buitendijk and Daniel Rutz discussed scheduling in-vitro time at the WGM or a Monday call to coordinate with Sarah Gaunt.
- **2026-01-12**: Daniel Rutz reported discussion on the O&O HCP call. Noted similarities to the CGM (Continuous Glucose Monitoring) IG data flow but highlighted distinct differences (CGM has continuous streaming; at-home testing is sparse data upload). **Currently there are no stakeholders ready to work out the details.**

### Key Considerations

- The IG is currently scoped to public health use cases only.
- A PHR/EHR use case introduces device-to-app data transfer patterns not currently specified.
- Similarities exist with the CGM IG but data patterns differ significantly.
- Extending to non-COVID consumer lab tests broadens scope considerably.
- eCR reporting obligations apply when providers ingest public health reportable results.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Deferred — Retain "Triaged" Status Pending Stakeholder Engagement

**Rationale:** The most recent comment (Jan 2026) explicitly states there are no stakeholders ready to work out the details. The request remains valid and was re-opened post-STU1 with clear interest, but proceeding without committed implementers risks producing under-informed specifications. Keep the ticket triaged and labeled "Ready-to-Discuss" for future WGM or HCP call sessions when stakeholders emerge.

**Action:** No change in status. Add a comment documenting that the workgroup acknowledges the request and will revisit when stakeholder engagement materializes. Optionally add a target date for re-evaluation (e.g., next ballot cycle).

### Disposition B: Accept as Persuasive — Scope an Initial PHR/EHR Use Case Section

**Rationale:** The request has been open since 2022 and was re-opened after STU1 with broad support (13-0-1 vote originally). Even without full implementation details, the workgroup could add an informative section to the IG outlining the PHR/EHR use case, expected data flows, and known gaps (identity, eCR reporting, non-COVID test types). This acknowledges the request, provides implementer guidance, and sets the stage for normative content in a future ballot.

**Action:** Resolve as Persuasive. Task the assignee (Sarah Gaunt) with drafting an informative use case section covering: (1) device-to-PHR/EHR data flow, (2) scope beyond COVID to other consumer tests, (3) eCR obligations, and (4) relationship to CGM IG patterns. Mark as requiring connectathon or pilot validation before normative inclusion.

### Disposition C: Decline — Considered for Future Use

**Rationale:** The original 2023 vote already deemed this out of scope. The intervening years have not produced committed stakeholders, and the CGM IG comparison suggests this may be better addressed by a broader consumer-device-to-EHR initiative rather than within the at-home in-vitro IG specifically. Close the ticket to keep the backlog focused.

**Action:** Resolve as "Considered for Future Use" with a note that the concept may be better pursued as a cross-IG initiative or within the PHR IG (FHIR-49225, FHIR-50631) once those specifications mature.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Deferred** is recommended. The Jan 2026 O&O HCP call discussion confirms ongoing interest but explicitly acknowledges no stakeholders are ready to develop the details. Premature acceptance risks producing incomplete specifications, while declining would dismiss a request with demonstrated community support (13-0-1 vote). Maintaining the current "Triaged / Ready-to-Discuss" status with a documented re-evaluation checkpoint preserves the request and signals workgroup intent to address it when resources align.
