## Summary

The submitter requests a review of the "Navigating the profiles" section in the Laboratory Report (FHIR) IG, which was originally taken from the EU guide. The question is whether this section is still useful in the current context of the IG, and if so, the included diagrams should be updated to reflect current content.

The resolved disposition is **Persuasive with Modification**: remove the "Navigating the profiles" section and its included diagrams entirely.

---

## Details

The "Navigating the profiles" section was carried over from the European Laboratory Report guide and provides visual diagrams to help readers understand the relationships between profiles in the IG. The submitter (Giorgio Cangioli, a key Laboratory Report contributor) questions whether this section remains relevant in the HL7 International version.

The resolution to remove the section rather than update it suggests the workgroup determined that:

1. The section added complexity without proportional benefit in the HL7 International context.
2. Maintaining accurate diagrams is an ongoing burden, especially as the profile structure evolves.
3. The information may be better communicated through other existing IG sections (e.g., the Design Choices/Approach pages that are being reworked in parallel tickets).

This ticket is closely related to several other Laboratory Report IG restructuring and documentation improvement tickets being addressed in the same ballot cycle.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept (Persuasive with Modification — Remove the Section)

**Action:** Accept the resolution as stated — remove the "Navigating the profiles" section and its included diagrams from the Laboratory Report IG.

**Rationale:** The section was inherited from the EU guide and has generated multiple ballot comments about inaccuracies (FHIR-46857) and missing optionality (FHIR-46856). Rather than investing effort in correcting and maintaining complex diagrams that may quickly become outdated, removing the section eliminates the maintenance burden and resolves at least two other open tickets. The IG's Design Approach pages (being improved via FHIR-47204, FHIR-46858, FHIR-46852) can serve as the primary mechanism for explaining profile relationships.

**Impact:** Removal of one IG section and its associated diagram images. FHIR-46856 and FHIR-46857 can be closed as addressed by this change.

### Disposition B: Alternative — Update Instead of Remove

**Action:** Retain the "Navigating the profiles" section but update the diagrams to accurately reflect current profile relationships, including optionality annotations as requested in FHIR-46856.

**Rationale:** The section was described as "very helpful" by Hans Buitendijk (FHIR-46856). Visual diagrams showing profile relationships can significantly aid implementers navigating the IG. The issues raised are about accuracy, not the concept itself. Fixing the diagrams may be preferable to losing a valuable navigation aid.

**Impact:** Requires effort to redraw diagrams with correct relationships and optionality. Ongoing maintenance commitment. FHIR-46856 and FHIR-46857 would need separate resolution.

### Disposition C: Decline (Not Persuasive)

**Action:** Decline the change request; keep the section as-is.

**Rationale:** The section provides useful visual guidance for implementers, and removing it without replacement reduces the accessibility of the IG. The issues with the diagrams should be addressed through their own tickets (FHIR-46856, FHIR-46857) rather than by removing the entire section.

**Impact:** No change. Diagram accuracy issues remain unresolved until addressed by other tickets.

---

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Accept (Remove the Section)**

The resolution to remove the "Navigating the profiles" section is the most pragmatic choice. The section has accumulated multiple accuracy complaints, was inherited from a different context (EU guide), and maintaining complex diagrams is an ongoing burden. Removing it also effectively resolves FHIR-46856 and FHIR-46857, consolidating the resolution of three tickets. The parallel work on Design Approach pages (FHIR-47204, FHIR-46858, FHIR-46852) provides an opportunity to incorporate any truly essential profile relationship guidance in a more maintainable format. The submitter (who is a primary contributor to the IG) proposed this change, lending confidence that the removal will not leave a critical gap.
