## Description

The submitter requests that [Occupational Data for Health (ODH)](http://hl7.org/fhir/us/odh/) be included in Section 3 of the Retrieval of Real World Data (RWD) for Clinical Research IG. ODH is a framework of information about a person's work. While ODH is not formally included in the International Patient Summary (IPS), the submitter argues it is important for clinical research and should be part of the RWD IG.

It was then re-opened by lynn_laakso in May 2023 specifically for post-STU publication reconsideration, indicating the work group acknowledged this should be revisited in the next cycle.

2. **Strong cross-IG precedent:** Multiple other IGs have successfully incorporated ODH references (US Central Cancer Registry, QI Core), all resolved as "Persuasive with Modification." The pattern is well-established.

3. **Companion ticket FHIR-40126:** The same reporter also filed a companion request to add a work-related use case. The two tickets are explicitly linked and should be considered together — including ODH data (FHIR-40098) is most meaningful when accompanied by a use case that motivates it (FHIR-40126).

4. **Reporter engagement:** Genevieve Luensman (the reporter) has been actively advocating for ODH inclusion across multiple IGs. The December 2023 comment adding Amanda Deering and Lori Fourquet suggests continued organizational interest from NIOSH/CDC.

5. **Maturity of ODH IG:** ODH has progressed through multiple STU releases (STU 1 through 1.3), demonstrating specification maturity and implementer adoption.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive with Modification
**Action:** Include ODH in Section 3 of the RWD IG by adding a reference to the ODH IG (http://hl7.org/fhir/us/odh/) with a brief description of relevant ODH profiles (e.g., PastOrPresentJob, UsualWork, EmploymentStatus). Scope the inclusion to specific ODH profiles relevant to clinical research rather than importing the entire ODH framework. Coordinate with FHIR-40126 to ensure a supporting use case is also added.

**Rationale:** The original deferral was based on absence of use cases in the current iteration. The ticket was re-opened explicitly for the next cycle, and the reporter has committed to providing a work-related use case (FHIR-40126). Multiple other IGs have already incorporated ODH references. A scoped inclusion with specific profile references (rather than blanket inclusion) keeps the IG focused while addressing the request.

### Disposition B: Persuasive
**Action:** Include ODH in Section 3 of the RWD IG as requested, adding a link and description of the ODH IG.

**Rationale:** The request is straightforward and aligned with precedent from other IGs. ODH is a mature, published specification directly relevant to real-world clinical research data. Accept as submitted and let the editors determine the appropriate level of detail.

### Disposition C: Considered for Future Use
**Action:** Defer ODH inclusion until a concrete work-related use case (FHIR-40126) is fully developed and accepted by the work group.

**Rationale:** The original 7-0-0 vote deferred this because no use cases required ODH data. While the ticket was re-opened post-STU, the fundamental prerequisite — a use case driving ODH inclusion — may not yet be fully developed. Defer until FHIR-40126 provides the motivating use case, then include both together.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Persuasive with Modification.** The re-opening of this ticket post-STU publication was an explicit acknowledgment that ODH inclusion should be addressed in the next cycle. Cross-IG precedent strongly supports adding ODH references. Scoping to specific relevant profiles and coordinating with FHIR-40126 ensures the inclusion is well-motivated and not over-broad. The modification adds the discipline of specifying exactly which ODH profiles are relevant to the RWD IG's clinical research context.
