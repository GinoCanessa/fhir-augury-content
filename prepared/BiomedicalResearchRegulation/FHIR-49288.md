## Description

The reporter identified three typographical errors in the figure within Section 1.3.1 ("FHIR Resources and Profiles") of the PQ-CMC IG Overview page:

1. **Orange box label**: "Substance Defintion" → should be **"SubstanceDefinition"** (missing letter 'i' in "Definition")
2. **Orange box label**: "ManufacturedItemDefintion" → should be **"ManufacturedItemDefinition"** (missing letter 'i' in "Definition")
3. **Connector labels (×2)**: "Test Ste" → should be **"Test Site"** (missing letter 'i' in "Site")

All three are straightforward spelling corrections in a diagram; no structural or semantic changes are proposed.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive (Accept as Technical Correction)

**Rationale:** The three items are unambiguous spelling errors in a diagram. "Defintion" is clearly a misspelling of "Definition," and "Ste" is a truncation of "Site." The corrections align the figure text with the actual FHIR resource type names (`SubstanceDefinition`, `ManufacturedItemDefinition`) and fix a truncated connector label. No design judgment or technical debate is needed. The precedent set by FHIR-49251 (same spec, Persuasive) and FHIR-50290 (typo batch, Persuasive) supports straightforward acceptance.

**Suggested resolution text:** "Correct the three typos in the Section 1.3.1 figure: change 'Substance Defintion' to 'SubstanceDefinition', 'ManufacturedItemDefintion' to 'ManufacturedItemDefinition', and 'Test Ste' (×2) to 'Test Site'."

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification (Accept with Broader Figure Review)

**Rationale:** While the three typos should be fixed, the work group may want to take the opportunity to do a broader review of all labels in the Section 1.3.1 figure for consistency (e.g., verifying all resource type names match their canonical FHIR names, checking connector labels for completeness). This avoids follow-up tickets for additional figure corrections.

**Suggested resolution text:** "Accept the three corrections as stated, and additionally review all labels and connectors in the Section 1.3.1 figure for accuracy and consistency with canonical FHIR resource type names before publishing."

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive (Decline)

**Rationale:** There is no reasonable basis to decline these corrections — the typos are objectively wrong. This disposition is included for completeness but is not recommended.

**Suggested resolution text:** "No action taken."

## Recommendation

**→ Disposition A: Persuasive** is recommended.

The corrections are clear-cut typographical fixes with no ambiguity or design implications. The reporter has precisely identified the errors and the correct text. Accepting as-is is the most efficient path and is consistent with how the work group has handled similar corrections in FHIR-49251 and other typo tickets. A broader figure review (Disposition B) could be undertaken independently if the work group desires, but should not block acceptance of these specific corrections.
