## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive (Accept as Submitted)
**Rationale:** Accept the change request as described. The sIRB questionnaires are highly specialized, and the reporter (Diane Rodden) has a track record of successful sIRB improvements (FHIR-36310, 36311, 36312 were all Persuasive). Hidden fields for external data are a recognized pattern in SDC-based questionnaires to support pre-population and system integration. Technical corrections improve overall IG quality.

**Risk:** Accepting without detailed documentation of specific changes may make review and validation difficult. The work group would need to request or verify a detailed change list before implementation.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification (Accept with Additional Detail Required)
**Rationale:** The intent is valid — hidden fields for external data and technical corrections are reasonable — but the ticket as filed lacks sufficient detail for review. Request that the reporter provide: (1) a specific list of questionnaire items affected, (2) which fields are being hidden and why, (3) what "external data" is being accommodated, and (4) a detailed enumeration of the technical corrections. This is consistent with how FHIR-36312 included an attached spreadsheet of changes.

**Risk:** May delay resolution, but ensures the work group can make an informed decision and implementers can understand the scope of changes.

### Disposition C: Deferred (Defer Pending More Information)
**Rationale:** The ticket has been Triaged since January 2023 with no progress, no assignee, and no community engagement. The description is too vague for actionable review. Defer until the reporter provides detailed specifications of the proposed changes, similar to the spreadsheet approach used in FHIR-36312. This also allows coordination with the companion tickets (FHIR-40057, FHIR-40059) so all three can be reviewed as a coherent package.

**Risk:** Further delays an already stale ticket. However, deferral with a clear ask for detail is preferable to accepting an under-specified change.

## Recommendation

**Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification** is recommended.

The underlying request is reasonable and consistent with the sIRB IG's history of questionnaire refinements. However, the ticket's description is insufficient for meaningful review — it does not enumerate which questionnaire items are affected, what hidden fields are being added or modified, or what the specific technical corrections are. Requesting additional detail (as was done with FHIR-36312's attached spreadsheet) ensures the work group can evaluate the full scope of changes. The work group should also coordinate review of this ticket alongside FHIR-40057 and FHIR-40059, as they form a cohesive set of sIRB updates.
