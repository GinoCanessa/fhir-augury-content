## Summary

This ticket raises a concern that the PCORnet Common Data Model (CDM) `patid` field is mapped to `Patient.identifier` in the CDMH FHIR Implementation Guide, which may be inappropriate. The `patid` field in PCORnet is intended to be a pseudonymous identifier—not a direct patient identifier such as a Medical Record Number (MRN) or Social Security Number (SSN)—whereas `Patient.identifier` in FHIR typically carries real-world identifiers. The resolution added guidance in the Mapping Conventions section noting that identifiers and IDs must be de-identified if required during translation and loading of CDM tables, and that the data should support re-linking and re-identification where necessary.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested
#### Proposal
Revise the CDMH mapping so that the PCORnet CDM `patid` field is no longer mapped directly to `Patient.identifier`. Instead, map `patid` to a separate extension or to `Patient.identifier` with a specific identifier type (e.g., a custom coding indicating a pseudonymous research identifier), clearly distinguishing it from clinical identifiers such as MRNs or SSNs.
#### Justification
The commenter correctly identifies a semantic mismatch: `patid` is designed as a pseudonymous, research-context identifier, whereas `Patient.identifier` conventionally carries real-world clinical identifiers. Mapping them directly without qualification conflates two different identifier domains and could lead to incorrect assumptions about the nature of the identifier by downstream consumers. A distinct identifier type or extension would preserve the pseudonymous semantics.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach
#### Proposal
Retain the mapping of PCORnet CDM `patid` to `Patient.identifier` but add explicit guidance in the Mapping Conventions section requiring that (1) `patid` values must be carried with an `Identifier.type` or `Identifier.system` that clearly marks them as pseudonymous research identifiers, and (2) identifiers must be de-identified during loading if required, with the ability to re-link and re-identify where necessary. This aligns with the resolution already applied by the commenter.
#### Justification
This approach preserves the existing mapping structure while addressing the semantic concern through metadata. The `Patient.identifier` element in FHIR is flexible enough to accommodate pseudonymous identifiers when properly typed. Adding de-identification guidance (as was done in the resolution) mitigates the risk of exposing real patient identifiers while maintaining mapping simplicity. This is the approach already implemented in the resolution comment.

---

### Disposition C: Decline
#### Proposal
Decline the request on the grounds that `Patient.identifier` is a general-purpose element that can carry any type of identifier, including pseudonymous ones, and no mapping change is needed.
#### Justification
The FHIR `Patient.identifier` element does not mandate that values be real-world clinical identifiers. Its `Identifier` datatype supports `system` and `type` fields that can distinguish pseudonymous research identifiers from MRNs or SSNs. Implementers are expected to use these fields appropriately. However, this disposition ignores the practical risk that consumers unfamiliar with the CDM context may misinterpret `patid` values as clinical identifiers, which weakens this position.

---

### Recommendation
**Recommended disposition:** B
The alternative approach (Disposition B) is the most pragmatic path forward and aligns with the resolution already applied to the ticket. Retaining the `Patient.identifier` mapping avoids disruptive structural changes to the CDMH IG, while adding explicit guidance about pseudonymous identifier typing and de-identification requirements directly addresses the commenter's concern. The resolution comment by nageshbashyam confirms that a note was already added to the Mapping Conventions requiring de-identification during loading and supporting re-linking. Formalizing and strengthening this guidance—particularly by recommending a specific `Identifier.type` or `Identifier.system` for pseudonymous research identifiers—would fully resolve the semantic mismatch without requiring a mapping restructure.
