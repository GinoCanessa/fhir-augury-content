## Summary

This ticket requests a review and potential reorganization of the domain model diagram on the Domain Overview page of the Pharmaceutical Quality (Industry) FHIR IG. The submitter notes that some subdomains may be incorrectly categorized — for example, "Microbiological attributes" may be placed under the wrong domain.

The ticket was previously resolved as "Considered for Future Use" on 2024-02-21 with a 15-0-0 vote (Diana Wright / Rob Hausam), but was subsequently re-opened post STU1 publication by Lynn Laakso on 2024-05-08 for further consideration.

## Details

The core concern is that the Domain Overview diagram does not accurately reflect the logical grouping of pharmaceutical quality subdomains. Specific reorganization examples discussed in the comments include:

- **"Reference standards"** — move from Composition to Identification
- **"Microbiological attributes"** — move from Identification to Composition
- **"Excipients"** — move from Identification to Composition

The original resolution committed to reviewing these reclassifications with industry stakeholders to assess the benefit of reorganization. The re-opening after STU1 publication indicates this review is still pending and the work group intends to address it in a future cycle.

This is a structural/informational change to the IG's conceptual domain model and diagram — it does not directly alter FHIR profiles or resource definitions but affects how users understand the organization of pharmaceutical quality concepts within the IG.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive (Accept as Requested)
**Action:** Accept the request and reorganize the domain model diagram on the Domain Overview page, implementing the specific reclassifications identified in the comments:
- Move "Reference standards" from Composition to Identification
- Move "Microbiological attributes" from Identification to Composition
- Move "Excipients" from Identification to Composition

**Rationale:** These reclassifications were already identified during the initial ballot resolution with a unanimous 15-0-0 vote and deferred for stakeholder review. Re-opening post STU1 publication signals the work group's intent to address this. The proposed reorganization improves conceptual accuracy — for example, microbiological attributes are compositional properties of a drug product, and excipients are ingredients that belong under composition. Accepting now allows the work to proceed in the next ballot cycle.

**Impact:** Non-breaking informational change. Updates to the Domain Overview page diagram and associated narrative text. No profile or resource definition changes required. Should be coordinated with FHIR-44038 (PQ Test Categories modeling) to ensure consistency.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification
**Action:** Accept the intent to reorganize the domain diagram, but require the work group to first complete the industry stakeholder review committed to in the original resolution before finalizing specific subdomain movements. The outcome of the stakeholder review should be documented in this ticket before changes are applied.

**Rationale:** The original 15-0-0 resolution explicitly conditioned the reorganization on industry stakeholder input. While the suggested moves appear logical, the stakeholder review has not yet been documented. Requiring it ensures broader community alignment and may identify additional reclassifications beyond the three examples listed.

**Impact:** Defers the diagram change until stakeholder feedback is captured. Adds a process step but ensures the final reorganization is comprehensive and well-supported. May result in additional or different subdomain moves than those currently proposed.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive (Decline)
**Action:** Decline the request and retain the current domain model diagram layout as-is.

**Rationale:** The current diagram was designed and published as part of STU1 and represents the consensus at the time of publication. Reorganizing subdomains introduces risk of confusion for implementers already familiar with the existing structure. The practical impact of subdomain placement in a conceptual diagram is limited — it does not affect the underlying FHIR profiles or data exchange. The work group's resources may be better spent on higher-priority Triaged tickets (FHIR-44038, FHIR-44037, FHIR-44041 are all rated Highest priority).

**Impact:** No changes to the IG. Preserves current structure and avoids churn on an informational diagram.

## Recommendation

**Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification** is recommended. The proposed reorganization is logically sound and has prior unanimous support, but the original resolution explicitly committed to industry stakeholder review before making changes. Completing that review step ensures the reorganization is comprehensive, well-justified, and supported by the broader PQ community. This approach respects the prior ballot outcome while keeping the ticket active for resolution in the next cycle.
