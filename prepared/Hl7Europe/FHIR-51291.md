## Description

The submitter requests that the EU Imaging Study Report IG require endpoints supporting WADO-URI in addition to WADO-RS. The rationale is that WADO-URI support would benefit simple viewers and patient-facing applications. The request originates from discussions at the IHE Vienna Connectathon.

## Resolution Description

> Support for WADO-URI is not required.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Not Persuasive (Affirm Current Resolution)

**Rationale:** The work group's resolution is sound. WADO-RS is the modern, RESTful DICOMweb standard and is the clear direction for the EU Imaging IG. Requiring WADO-URI would add implementation burden to every conformant endpoint without proportionate benefit. Related ticket FHIR-51338 explicitly argues *against* WADO-URI use in favor of WADO-RS, and FHIR-51296 recommends WADO-RS as the preferred endpoint type. The broader community trend is toward WADO-RS for programmatic imaging access. Simple viewers and patient-facing apps can work with WADO-RS rendered resources or thumbnails (as explored in FHIR-51334). Making WADO-URI *required* is an excessive obligation.

**Action:** No specification change needed.

### Disposition B: Persuasive — Require WADO-URI Support

**Rationale:** WADO-URI is simpler to implement for basic viewers and patient-facing apps — a single URL retrieves a rendered image without the complexity of multipart WADO-RS responses. The request stems from real-world interoperability testing at the IHE Vienna Connectathon, suggesting a genuine implementation gap. Patient access to imaging data is a key EHDS use case. Requiring WADO-URI alongside WADO-RS would lower the barrier for lightweight consumers.

**Action:** Update the EU Imaging Study Report IG to require Endpoint resources with `connectionType` of `dicom-wado-uri` alongside the existing WADO-RS requirement.

### Disposition C: Persuasive with Modification — Add Optional WADO-URI Guidance

**Rationale:** A middle ground: don't *require* WADO-URI but add normative guidance encouraging its support for simple viewers and patient-facing apps. This acknowledges the legitimate use case from the Connectathon discussions without imposing a mandatory obligation. It provides implementers with a clear signal that WADO-URI is a recognized option while keeping WADO-RS as the primary required mechanism.

**Action:** Add a recommendation (SHOULD or MAY) for WADO-URI endpoint support in the IG, with narrative explaining its utility for simple viewers and patient-facing applications.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Not Persuasive (Affirm Current Resolution)**

The "Not Persuasive" resolution should be upheld. The community direction strongly favors WADO-RS as the primary imaging access mechanism, as evidenced by multiple related tickets (FHIR-51338, FHIR-51296, FHIR-51334) that advocate for WADO-RS and seek to clarify its role. Requiring WADO-URI would contradict the emerging consensus and add implementation burden. The specification already permits WADO-URI endpoints without mandating them, which is the appropriate balance. The later ticket FHIR-56147 (requesting a WADO-URI example endpoint) suggests the community may address WADO-URI as optional guidance in future iterations without requiring it.
