## Description

The submitter requests that the Manifest DocumentReference in the EU Imaging Study Report IG require **both** a FHIR attachment and a DICOM KOS (Key Object Selection) attachment. This would allow clients to choose which format to receive. The request originated from discussions and agreement at the IHE Connectathon in Vienna (June 2025).

## Analysis

### Core Issue

The ticket asks the EU Imaging Study Report IG to mandate that the Manifest DocumentReference carry **both** a DICOM KOS and a FHIR (ImagingStudy-based) attachment. This enables interoperability for DICOM-native consumers (e.g., PACS workstations) and FHIR-native consumers (e.g., patient apps, exchange gateways, clinician apps) from the same document.

### Context from Related Discussions

- **FHIR-51295** (Applied, Persuasive) already established that the manifest specification will be split into a separate spec and that KOS and FHIR encodings will be aligned with lossless bidirectional mappings. This work was initiated within HL7-EU with intent to transfer to IHE-RAD.
- **FHIR-51315** presents the counterargument: requiring both formats burdens source systems; allowing either burdens consumers. The discussion converged on "server produces both, client picks" as the least burdensome overall approach. The IHE MADO profile is defining both encodings with this as an open issue.
- Comments on this ticket explicitly state the requirement will be addressed by the **IHE MADO (Manifest-Access to DICOM Objects)** profile, which defines both encodings and their bidirectional mappings.

### Key Considerations

1. There is broad agreement (from both this ticket and FHIR-51315 discussions) that supporting both formats is desirable.
2. The MADO profile is the designated vehicle for specifying dual-format support, and it has an open issue tracking exactly this question.
3. The EU Imaging Study Report IG may not be the right place to impose the dual-format requirement directly, since the manifest is being split into a separate specification (per FHIR-51295).
4. Mandating both formats in this IG before MADO reaches Trial Implementation could create conflicting or premature constraints.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive

**Action:** Accept the request and add a requirement in the EU Imaging Study Report IG that the Manifest DocumentReference SHALL include both a DICOM KOS attachment and a FHIR ImagingStudy-based attachment.

**Rationale:** The IHE Connectathon agreement and community discussion (FHIR-51315) support the "server produces both, client picks" model. Codifying this in the IG ensures interoperability across DICOM-native and FHIR-native consumers from the outset.

**Risk:** May create premature constraints before the MADO profile finalizes its approach; could conflict with MADO's eventual specification.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification

**Action:** Acknowledge the requirement as valid but defer the normative dual-format mandate to the IHE MADO profile. In the EU Imaging Study Report IG, add informative text noting that: (a) the manifest is expected to support both KOS and FHIR encodings, (b) the IHE MADO profile will define the normative requirements for dual-format support and bidirectional mappings, and (c) implementers SHOULD anticipate supporting both formats.

**Rationale:** This aligns with the already-accepted decision to split the manifest into a separate spec (FHIR-51295) and the ongoing MADO work. It avoids duplicating or conflicting with MADO while signaling the direction to implementers. The comments on this ticket explicitly state MADO will address this.

**Risk:** Implementers may not adopt both formats until MADO reaches Trial Implementation, delaying interoperability in the interim.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive

**Action:** Do not add a dual-format requirement. The EU Imaging Study Report IG should remain agnostic on the number of attachment formats required, leaving this entirely to the IHE MADO profile.

**Rationale:** The manifest specification is being moved to a separate IG and then to IHE-RAD governance (per FHIR-51295). Adding requirements in the EU Imaging Study Report IG for content that is migrating out of scope would be premature and could create maintenance burden.

**Risk:** Lack of any guidance in this IG may leave implementers without direction during the transition period.

## Recommendation

**Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification**

The community clearly agrees that dual-format support (KOS + FHIR) is the right direction, and both ticket comments confirm that the IHE MADO profile is the intended vehicle for this requirement. Since the manifest is already being split into a separate specification (FHIR-51295, Applied/Persuasive), imposing the normative constraint directly in the EU Imaging Study Report IG would be premature and risk conflict with MADO. The best approach is to acknowledge the intent, add informative guidance in this IG pointing to MADO, and let the normative requirement land in the appropriate specification.
