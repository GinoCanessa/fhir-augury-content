## Summary

The submitter asks how the **XD-Lab Notification Organizer** should be modelled using the EU Laboratory Report IG. In the IHE XD-Lab (Cross-Enterprise Sharing of Laboratory Reports) specification, a Notification Organizer is a CDA construct that groups three related concepts relevant to public health reporting:

1. **Notifiable Condition** — the reportable disease or condition
2. **Case Identification** — identifier linking the report to a public health case
3. **Outbreak Identification** — identifier linking the report to a recognized outbreak

The question is essentially: what FHIR resources and/or profiles in the EU Lab Report IG should be used to convey this public health notification information that was previously represented as a CDA organizer in XD-Lab?

---

## Details

The XD-Lab Notification Organizer is a CDA R2 construct defined in the IHE XD-Lab profile. It groups notification-related entries (notifiable condition, case ID, outbreak ID) under a single organizer act. FHIR does not have a direct equivalent of the CDA "Organizer" act; similar grouping patterns in FHIR are typically handled via:

- **Observation** resources (with `hasMember` references for grouping)
- **Composition** sections (for document-level grouping)
- **DiagnosticReport** (with `result` references)
- **Condition** resource (for the notifiable condition itself)
- **Extensions** on resources to carry case/outbreak identifiers

The EU Laboratory Report IG at the time of filing did not explicitly address the public health notification use case from XD-Lab. This ticket has remained in **Triaged** status with no comments or resolution since November 2023.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept — Provide Guidance on Mapping

**Resolution: Persuasive with Modification**

Accept the question and provide explicit guidance in the EU Laboratory Report IG on how to represent the XD-Lab Notification Organizer in FHIR. The recommended mapping would be:

- **Notifiable Condition** → `Condition` resource (profiled for the EU Lab context), referenced from the `Composition` or `DiagnosticReport`.
- **Case Identification** → An `identifier` on the `Condition` or a dedicated extension carrying the public health case identifier.
- **Outbreak Identification** → An `identifier` on the `Condition` or a dedicated extension carrying the outbreak identifier.
- **Grouping** → A `Composition.section` dedicated to public health notification data, or an Observation group with `hasMember` references if the IG uses that pattern.

Add a new section or informative page in the IG explaining the CDA-to-FHIR mapping for XD-Lab notification constructs.

### Disposition B: Defer to a Future Ballot Cycle

**Resolution: Considered – No action required (for now)**

Acknowledge the question as valid but note that the EU Laboratory Report IG is focused on the core laboratory result use case. Public health notification is a specialized extension of the lab report workflow that may be better addressed in a dedicated public health reporting IG or a future ballot cycle of the EU Lab Report IG. Document the question for future consideration and add a tracker item for the public health notification scope.

### Disposition C: Decline — Out of Scope

**Resolution: Not Persuasive**

Decline the question on the basis that the XD-Lab Notification Organizer represents a public health reporting concern that is out of scope for the EU Laboratory Report IG. The IG is designed for conveying laboratory results, not for public health case management. Public health notification workflows should be addressed by dedicated public health IGs (e.g., electronic case reporting profiles). The submitter should be directed to the appropriate work group (e.g., Public Health or Clinical Interoperability Council) for guidance on FHIR-based notifiable disease reporting.

---

## Recommendation

**Disposition B: Defer to a Future Ballot Cycle** is recommended. The question is valid and identifies a real gap in CDA-to-FHIR mapping guidance for the EU Laboratory Report IG. However, the Notification Organizer represents a specialized public health reporting concern that adds significant scope to the IG. Deferring allows the work group to properly scope the public health notification use case, coordinate with relevant work groups (Public Health, Orders & Observations), and address it in a future cycle with adequate design work. In the interim, the IG could add a brief informative note acknowledging that XD-Lab notification constructs are not yet in scope.
