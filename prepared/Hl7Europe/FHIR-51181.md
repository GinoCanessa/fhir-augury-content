## Summary

Giorgio Cangioli filed this change request to address five specific issues found on the **Design Consideration** page of the EU Imaging Study Report IG:

1. **Incorrect links** — Links to published EU guides point to `build.fhir.org` instead of the official site at `hl7.eu/fhir`.
2. **Inconsistent Composition description** — The page states the Composition "often just a single section," but the current Imaging Report requires **eight sections**, which is inconsistent.
3. **Open Issues → STU Notes** — If the questions listed under "Open Issues" are still relevant, they should be converted into STU Notes per HL7 publication guidelines.
4. **Actor scope and obligations** — Questions whether actors are limited to providers, or could also include content-focused actors (e.g., author, signer) or content-independent actors (e.g., transport service, intermediary).
5. **Unexplained acronym** — The acronym **MCWG** is used but never expanded or defined.

---

## Details

This ticket targets the Design Consideration page, which was re-added to the ballot candidate specification (see Zulip discussion from 2025-05-02 by Bas van den Heuvel). The issues raised are largely editorial and structural, but the actor scope question (#4) has substantive design implications.

The link correction issue (#1) is straightforward — published HL7 Europe IGs should use `hl7.eu/fhir` as the canonical URL base, not `build.fhir.org` which is the CI build location.

The Composition section inconsistency (#2) connects to broader discussions in FHIR-51290 (focusing on a core dataset) and FHIR-51390 (where unstructured report text should go), both of which question the current eight-section Composition structure.

The actor question (#4) touches on topics raised in FHIR-51283, which discusses Report Providers (RIS) and Manifest Providers (PACS) — content-specific actors that go beyond simple "provider" categorization. The MCWG acronym (#5) likely refers to the **Multi-Country Working Group** on Imaging Information Sharing (referenced in David Wattien's Zulip link to ihe-europe.net).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive (Accept All Five Points)

Accept all five points as filed. Specifically:

1. **Update links** from `build.fhir.org` to `hl7.eu/fhir` for all references to published EU guides.
2. **Correct or remove** the "often just a single section" statement in the Composition description to accurately reflect the eight-section structure.
3. **Convert remaining Open Issues** into STU Notes, consistent with HL7 publication guidelines.
4. **Expand the actor definitions** to include content-focused actors (author, signer) and content-independent actors (transport, intermediary), or clarify why the scope is limited to providers.
5. **Expand the MCWG acronym** on first use (Multi-Country Working Group) and add it to a glossary if one exists.

**Rationale:** All five points are straightforward editorial/structural improvements that enhance clarity, accuracy, and conformance with HL7 conventions. None require significant design changes.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification (Accept Editorial, Defer Actor Scope)

Accept points 1, 2, 3, and 5 as editorial fixes. For point 4 (actor scope), defer to a separate ticket or future ballot cycle, since actor definitions have broader implications that intersect with FHIR-51283 (search parameters by actor role) and the ongoing work on Report Provider / Manifest Provider actor definitions.

**Rationale:** The editorial items are unambiguous. However, expanding actor scope beyond providers could affect obligations, conformance statements, and potentially the entire actor model. This deserves dedicated discussion rather than being bundled as one bullet in a design-consideration review.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive (Decline)

Decline on the basis that the design consideration page is intentionally a high-level overview and not a normative artifact. The link issue will be resolved when the next version is published. The Composition statement is a general description, not binding. Open Issues are a legitimate tool during ballot. Actor definitions are addressed elsewhere. MCWG is known to the target audience.

**Rationale:** The page serves as informal guidance. The raised points, while valid observations, do not represent errors that affect implementability or conformance.

---

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Persuasive** — All five points are well-founded, clearly articulated, and individually low-risk. The link correction (#1) and acronym expansion (#5) are simple editorial fixes. The Composition statement correction (#2) removes a factual inaccuracy. Converting Open Issues to STU Notes (#3) aligns with established HL7 practice. The actor scope question (#4) is the most substantive, but the design consideration page is the right place to clarify the intended actor model — even if the full actor definitions are elaborated elsewhere. Accepting all points strengthens the IG's editorial quality and consistency ahead of the next ballot cycle.
