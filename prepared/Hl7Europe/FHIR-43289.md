## Description

The reporter notes that narrative text generated for the EU Laboratory Report specification contains the phrase "See above" with a directional arrow, but the referenced observation is actually **below** the current position in the rendered output. The question is whether this is an IG Publisher artifact or something that can be corrected in the specification's narrative. The reporter requests that the text be changed from "See above" to "See below" to point readers in the correct direction.

A screenshot is attached to the ticket showing the confusing narrative text in context (an Anaerobic Culture observation referencing another observation).

## Analysis

This ticket raises a valid usability concern about auto-generated or manually authored narrative text in the EU Laboratory Report IG. The core issue is directional language ("See above") that is incorrect relative to the rendered output — the referenced observation appears below, not above.

**Key considerations:**
1. **Publisher vs. IG content:** If the narrative is auto-generated by the IG Publisher, fixing it may require changes in the FHIR tooling (publisher), which is outside the HL7 Europe WG's direct control. The precedent from FHIR-31920 (resolved as "Not Persuasive") suggests narrative rendering bugs in the publisher may not be prioritized.
2. **If the narrative is authored in the IG:** The fix is straightforward — change "See above" to "See below" (or use a direction-neutral phrase such as "See referenced observation") in the relevant example or profile narrative.
3. **Ticket age:** Filed in November 2023 with no subsequent comments or resolution, suggesting it has not been actively worked.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive — Fix the narrative text
- **Resolution:** Persuasive
- **Action:** Identify whether the "See above" text originates from authored narrative in the IG or from auto-generated publisher output. If authored, change it to "See below" or a direction-neutral alternative (e.g., "See referenced observation"). If publisher-generated, file a follow-up tracker against the IG Publisher tooling.
- **Rationale:** The directional mismatch is objectively incorrect and can confuse implementers reading the rendered specification. A targeted text correction is low-risk and high-value for readability.

### Disposition B: Not Persuasive — Publisher behavior, not an IG issue
- **Resolution:** Not Persuasive
- **Action:** Close with a note explaining that the "See above/below" text is generated by the IG Publisher and is outside the scope of the EU Laboratory Report specification. Recommend the reporter file a separate issue against the FHIR IG Publisher tooling.
- **Rationale:** If the text is auto-generated by the publisher, the EU Laboratory Report IG cannot directly control it. Precedent from FHIR-31920 shows similar publisher narrative issues have been declined as "Not Persuasive."

### Disposition C: Deferred — Investigate and address in next cycle
- **Resolution:** Deferred
- **Action:** Defer investigation to the next ballot cycle. During that cycle, determine the source of the narrative text and apply the appropriate fix (either in the IG content or by engaging FHIR Infrastructure for a publisher fix).
- **Rationale:** The issue is valid but low-priority and non-blocking. Deferring allows the WG to prioritize higher-impact items while keeping the ticket open for future resolution.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Persuasive — Fix the narrative text.** The directional error ("See above" when the content is below) is a clear, verifiable mistake that harms readability. The fix is minimal — either correct the authored narrative or use direction-neutral language. If investigation reveals the text is publisher-generated, a follow-up issue against the publisher tooling should be filed at that time, but the first step is to confirm the source and apply the simplest available fix within the IG.
