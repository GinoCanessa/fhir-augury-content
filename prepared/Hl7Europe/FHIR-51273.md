## Description Summary

The submitter requests that the EU Imaging Study Report IG provide explicit guidance on how access to imaging endpoints (IID and WADO-RS) is controlled in cross-border scenarios involving National Contact Points (NCPs). The ticket raises several key questions:

1. **Proxy vs. direct access** — Should all queries be proxied through the NCP, with the NCP providing endpoints that resolve references to record holders? Or is direct access to imaging endpoints permitted?
2. **NCP viewer support** — Would NCPs themselves support IID viewing?
3. **Viewer hosting and localization** — Who hosts the viewer (source, intermediary, local, or all), and is there an expectation to localize viewers for the destination country?
4. **Leveraging existing standards** — Should the IG leverage concepts from XC-WADO, SMART Imaging Access, or SMART Multi Auth?
5. **Sequence diagrams** — The submitter proposes creating end-to-end sequence diagrams for key workflows, including pre-coordination steps, authentication/authorization flows, and endpoint discovery behind NCPs.

The submitter argues that such documentation would reduce implementation cost and time, ensure consistent understanding of envisioned flows during further balloting, and complement the [Transactions page](https://build.fhir.org/ig/hl7-eu/imaging/branches/qa-fixes-ballot1/transactions.html) under development.

## Comments Summary

Two comments provide substantive responses:

- **JIRAUSER30266 (2025-12-16):** Points to the IHE XC-WADO profile as the solution, noting it can be grouped with the IHE MADO Profile to gain access to retrieve URLs via the imaging study manifest (MADO) and format them for cross-border retrieval per XC-WADO.

- **JIRAUSER28578 (2025-12-17):** Agrees that XC-WADO + MADO handle manifest representation and image link conveyance (via MHD/XCA/XDS transport), but identifies **authorization to the WADO-RS endpoint** as the missing piece. Specifically: when a consumer has a manifest and builds a WADO-RS URL, what happens when they launch it? How is the endpoint authorized? Is it assumed to go through the same auth and NCP pipes (which would route heavy DICOM bits through many hops), or does authorization need to be separately negotiated?

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive with Modification
**Action:** Add a new informative section to the IG (alongside the Transactions page) that provides:
1. End-to-end sequence diagrams for the primary cross-border imaging access workflows (manifest retrieval → endpoint resolution → image access).
2. Narrative text clarifying that WADO-RS retrieval is supported cross-border via IHE MADO/XC-WADO profiles, while IID viewing is currently intra-community only.
3. A statement that authorization to WADO-RS endpoints in cross-border scenarios is under active development (referencing XC-WADO) and is expected to be addressed in future versions or through IHE profiling.
4. A note acknowledging the open question about NCP-proxied vs. direct endpoint access, deferring the binding decision to national infrastructure while documenting the trade-offs.

**Rationale:** The request is valid — implementers need clarity on endpoint access patterns. However, some questions (e.g., exact NCP authorization model) are outside the IG's normative scope and depend on IHE profiles still evolving. Informative guidance with sequence diagrams provides substantial value without over-committing on undecided architectural choices.

### Disposition B: Persuasive
**Action:** Fully accept the request: add normative or strongly recommended guidance specifying the expected endpoint access model, including a recommended approach (WADO-RS preferred, NCP-proxied, with XC-WADO profile referenced), along with complete sequence diagrams for all key workflows.

**Rationale:** The submitter's request is well-articulated and the community comments confirm that the gap is real. Providing definitive guidance now, even if some aspects evolve later, would accelerate implementation and reduce fragmentation. However, this risks over-specifying in areas where IHE profiling (XC-WADO, MADO) is still in progress.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive
**Action:** Decline the request, noting that the endpoint access model and authorization flows are addressed by IHE profiles (MADO, XC-WADO) and are outside the scope of this HL7 FHIR IG.

**Rationale:** The IG focuses on the FHIR data model for imaging reports and manifests. Transport, access control, and endpoint resolution are IHE profiling concerns. Adding this content would expand scope beyond the IG's charter and could conflict with IHE work in progress.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Persuasive with Modification** is recommended.

The comments demonstrate that IHE profiles (MADO, XC-WADO) partially address the submitter's concerns, but a significant gap remains around endpoint authorization in cross-border scenarios — as JIRAUSER28578's comment highlights. The IG should provide informative sequence diagrams and clarifying text that explain how MADO/XC-WADO address endpoint access, document the known open question of WADO-RS endpoint authorization, and point implementers to the relevant IHE profiles. This delivers the implementer value the submitter requests without the IG taking on normative obligations that belong to IHE profiling work still in progress. The closely related tickets FHIR-51296 and FHIR-51334 should be resolved in coordination, as they address overlapping questions about IID vs. WADO-RS expectations and WADO-RS compliance scope.
