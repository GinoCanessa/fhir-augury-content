## Description

The submitter requests that obligation profiles and structural profiles within the EU Imaging Study Report specification use IDs and names that make it easier to distinguish between them. The concern is that without clear naming conventions, implementers may confuse obligation profiles (which define functional expectations such as "SHALL support" or "SHALL send") with structural profiles (which constrain the shape of the data).

## Resolution Description

> We will take this into account when obligations are re-added.

The resolution is **Considered - No action required**, indicating the workgroup acknowledges the feedback but determined no immediate change is needed. The response suggests obligations were removed or are being reworked, and the naming concern will be addressed when obligations are reintroduced.

## Context & Analysis

This ticket reflects a usability concern common across FHIR implementation guides that use obligations: when both structural profiles and obligation profiles exist for the same resource, their IDs and display names should clearly indicate which is which. Without this distinction, tooling and human reviewers may misinterpret the intent of a profile.

The resolution ("Considered - No action required") combined with the note "we will take this into account when obligations are re-added" suggests that obligations were temporarily removed from the EU Imaging Study Report IG during the ballot cycle. The workgroup committed to applying better naming when obligations are reintroduced, making this a deferred improvement rather than a rejection.

FHIR-44634 (Applied, Persuasive) is an infrastructure-level change that added a `name` element to the obligations extension, which directly supports the kind of labeling this ticket requests. When obligations are re-added to the EU Imaging Study Report, the `name` element from FHIR-44634 can be leveraged to provide the distinguishability the submitter is asking for.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Confirm "Considered - No action required"
**Rationale:** The workgroup has acknowledged the feedback and committed to applying clearer naming when obligations are reintroduced. No immediate specification change is needed because obligations are not currently present in the IG. The infrastructure-level fix (FHIR-44634, adding `name` to obligations) has already been applied, providing the tooling needed to address this concern when the time comes. This disposition correctly reflects deferred action on a valid suggestion.

### Disposition B: Change to "Persuasive - Deferred"
**Rationale:** While the feedback is valid and acknowledged, "Considered - No action required" could imply the concern was dismissed. Changing to "Persuasive - Deferred" more accurately reflects that the workgroup agrees with the suggestion but is deferring implementation until obligations are re-added. This provides clearer tracking and signals to the submitter that the feedback will be acted upon.

### Disposition C: Change to "Persuasive" and add naming guidelines now
**Rationale:** Even before obligations are re-added, the workgroup could establish and document naming conventions (e.g., `[Resource]-obligations-[actor]` vs `[Resource]-[context]` for structural profiles) in the IG's design notes. This proactive approach ensures consistency when obligations are reintroduced and provides guidance for other HL7 Europe IGs adopting obligations (cf. FHIR-55389).

---

## Recommendation

**Disposition B: Change to "Persuasive - Deferred"**

This best reflects the workgroup's actual intent. The resolution description explicitly states the feedback will be taken into account when obligations are re-added, which aligns with "Persuasive - Deferred" rather than "No action required." It provides accurate tracking, signals respect for the submitter's feedback, and creates a clear record that this should be revisited in the next cycle when obligations return to the EU Imaging Study Report IG.
