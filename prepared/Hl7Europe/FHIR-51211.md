## Summary

Giorgio Cangioli raised a question about Group 6 in the ConceptMap mappings within the EU Imaging Study Report implementation guide. The reporter notes that Group 6 maps from the Imaging Report model to the ImagingStudy Manifest, but finds this unclear because the context is the report space—where one would expect a mapping to the report bundle rather than the manifest bundle. A follow-up comment from JIRAUSER28670 requests clarification on which specific resource or ConceptMap is being referenced, noting that the three existing ImagingReport ConceptMaps (EHDSImagingReportBodyMap, EHDSImagingReportHeaderMap, and EHDSImagingReportMap) do not contain a Group 6 that maps to the ImagingStudy Manifest. The ticket remains triaged and unresolved. This ticket is part of a series of ConceptMap mapping review comments filed by the same reporter (FHIR-51151, FHIR-51152, FHIR-51210, FHIR-51215). Critically, per FHIR-51152, the workgroup has since decided to abandon ConceptMaps in favor of markdown table–based mappings, which fundamentally changes the context of this ticket.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested
#### Proposal
Acknowledge the reporter's concern that report-context elements should map to the report bundle, not the manifest bundle. Ensure the new markdown table–based mappings (which replaced ConceptMaps per FHIR-51152) correctly distinguish between report and manifest mapping targets, and that no report-model elements are erroneously directed to the manifest bundle.
#### Justification
The underlying concern about correct mapping targets is valid regardless of the mapping representation format. The reporter raises a reasonable conceptual point: mappings within the report space should logically target report-related FHIR resources. The workgroup should verify that the transition from ConceptMaps to markdown tables addressed this separation correctly.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach
#### Proposal
Close as superseded by the workgroup's decision (documented in FHIR-51152) to replace ConceptMaps with markdown table–based mappings. The specific ConceptMap Group 6 artifact this comment addresses no longer exists. Add a note confirming that the new markdown-based mappings correctly handle the report-versus-manifest distinction, validating that report-model elements target the report bundle and not the manifest bundle.
#### Justification
The ConceptMap that contained the questioned Group 6 mapping has been deprecated. The responder (JIRAUSER28670) was already unable to locate the described mapping in the current ConceptMaps, suggesting the content had evolved. The broader architectural change to markdown tables resolves this specific concern. However, formalizing the verification of report-vs-manifest mapping correctness in the new format ensures the reporter's underlying point is addressed.

---

### Disposition C: Decline
#### Proposal
Close the ticket as not persuasive. The responder could not reproduce the described Group 6 mapping to the ImagingStudy Manifest in the current ConceptMaps. The submitter did not provide a follow-up clarification despite being asked (since 2025-07-09). Without a specific reference to the artifact in question, there is insufficient information to act on.
#### Justification
The ticket lacks specificity. The existing ConceptMaps do not appear to contain the mapping described. The reporter has not responded to the clarification request for an extended period, and the workgroup has since moved away from ConceptMaps entirely (per FHIR-51152).

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B — Alternative Approach

The workgroup's decision to move from ConceptMaps to markdown table–based mappings (per FHIR-51152, confirmed by bvdh on 2026-03-03) eliminates the specific artifact Giorgio questioned. The responder's inability to locate the described Group 6 mapping in the current ConceptMaps further supports that the content has evolved since the ballot comment was filed. Disposition B is the most appropriate because it formally acknowledges that the concern is superseded by the architectural change while also committing to verify that the new mapping format correctly handles the report-versus-manifest distinction—honoring the valid principle behind the reporter's concern. This avoids dismissing a reasonable conceptual point while recognizing that no separate corrective action is required for the specific artifact originally cited.
