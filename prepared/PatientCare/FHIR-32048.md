## Summary

This change request asks that the Condition resource's FHIR Maturity Level be elevated to Normative status. The ticket was filed in April 2021 as part of a coordinated set of requests (alongside similar tickets for AllergyIntolerance and Procedure) to advance key Patient Care resources toward normative status. It has remained in "Triaged" status for several years. A March 2026 comment from jlyle notes that the workgroup needs a consistent approach for handling these normative promotion tickets and that the matter may be deferred to the R6 ballot cycle.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested
#### Proposal
Advance the Condition resource to Normative status beginning with the next available ballot cycle (R6). This would involve completing the normative readiness review for Condition, including identification of any data elements that should remain STU-only, and including Condition in the R6 normative ballot package.
#### Justification
Condition is one of the most widely implemented and depended-upon clinical resources in FHIR. The Patient Care workgroup previously voted to advance it to normative, and the companion ticket for Procedure (FHIR-32049) has already been resolved as Persuasive. Condition's broad adoption across EHR systems, US Core, International Patient Summary, and other implementation guides demonstrates the stability expected of a normative resource. Promoting it would reduce uncertainty for implementers who already treat it as de facto normative.

---

### Disposition B: Defer to R6 With Coordinated Approach
#### Proposal
Keep this ticket open but explicitly defer resolution until a coordinated normative promotion strategy is established for the R6 ballot, as suggested by jlyle's comment. The workgroup should develop a unified plan that addresses Condition, AllergyIntolerance (FHIR-32050), and any other candidate resources together, ensuring consistent criteria and process for all normative promotions.
#### Justification
jlyle's March 2026 comment indicates the workgroup recognizes the need for a consistent approach across all normative promotion tickets. Handling these individually risks inconsistent decisions. A coordinated R6 strategy would allow the workgroup to define clear normative readiness criteria, review all candidate resources against those criteria simultaneously, and present a coherent normative package to the ballot. This avoids piecemeal normative advancement and ensures the Condition resource's promotion is part of a well-planned process.

---

### Disposition C: Decline
#### Proposal
Close this ticket without advancing Condition to normative status, on the basis that the resource's maturity level should continue to be managed through the standard FHIR maturity model process rather than by individual change request.
#### Justification
The FHIR maturity model is designed to be evidence-based, with resources advancing through defined levels as implementation experience accumulates. Normative status carries significant implications—it constrains future changes and commits the community to long-term backward compatibility. While Condition is widely used, the ticket provides no detailed analysis of normative readiness, no review of which elements should remain STU-only, and no assessment of outstanding issues that might need resolution before normative locking. The five-year period this ticket has remained open without resolution suggests that the community has not yet reached the consensus needed for this promotion.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B — Defer to R6 With Coordinated Approach

The most pragmatic path forward aligns with jlyle's March 2026 comment: the workgroup should establish a consistent approach for all pending normative promotion tickets before resolving any of them individually. The companion ticket for Procedure (FHIR-32049) has already been resolved as Persuasive, demonstrating that the workgroup does see merit in advancing Patient Care resources to normative. However, resolving Condition in isolation—without the same coordinated review applied to AllergyIntolerance and other candidates—risks inconsistency. Deferring to R6 with an explicit plan allows the workgroup to define normative readiness criteria, review Condition alongside its sibling resources, identify any elements that should remain STU-only, and present a coherent normative package. This approach respects the original intent of the request while ensuring the promotion is done methodically and with community consensus.
