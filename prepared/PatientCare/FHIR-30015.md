## Description Summary

The reporter (Daniel Vreeman) requests that the PACIO Functional Status IG expand its narrative to properly describe the IG's scope. Three core issues are raised:

1. **Domain definition and anchoring framework:** The IG does not define what "functional status" means or what anchoring framework/terminology it uses. The reporter recommends adopting the WHO's International Classification of Functioning, Disability and Health (ICF) as the conceptual framework, noting it will be part of ICD-11 and provides a globally recognized structure for complex functioning domains.

2. **"Status" is too narrow a term:** The term "status" implies a single axis of assessment, but functioning involves multiple dimensions (current performance, capability, goals) influenced by physiological, personal, and environmental factors. The reporter proposes using the umbrella term "functioning" instead, with ICF-based subdomain classification. This is linked to a companion proposal to merge the Functional Status and Cognitive Status IGs (FHIR-30014).

3. **Scope of resource types:** The IG heavily focuses on Observations from CMS-required assessment instruments, but it is unclear whether Conditions, Goals, Medications, Interventions, or other resource types are in scope now or in the future.

## Context & Evolution

Since this ticket was filed in December 2020, several developments have occurred:

- **STU1 publication and reopening:** The ticket was initially resolved as "Considered for Future Use" in August 2021 (vote 11-0-0), then re-opened in November 2021 for post-STU1 consideration.
- **ICF adoption:** Per the comment history, PACIO did adopt the ICF conceptual framework and added definitions for functional status, partially addressing the first concern.
- **IG evolution:** The Functional Status and Cognitive Status IGs have since been succeeded by the **US PACIO Personal Functioning and Engagement (PFE)** IG, which effectively merges the two IGs as FHIR-30014 proposed. The PFE IG uses the ICF framework as its conceptual anchor.
- **Ongoing concerns:** Even after the PFE IG was created, later tickets (FHIR-38054, FHIR-38618) show that ICF mapping detail and title/scope clarity remain active concerns, suggesting the narrative expansion requested here has not been fully resolved.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive (Apply to Successor IG)

**Rationale:** The core request — to expand the IG narrative with a clear definition of "functional status," adopt the ICF framework as the conceptual anchor, and clarify the scope of resource types — is well-founded and has been partially validated by subsequent developments. The Functional Status IG has been superseded by the Personal Functioning and Engagement (PFE) IG, which adopted ICF and merged the two IGs. However, persistent follow-on tickets (FHIR-38054, FHIR-38618) demonstrate that the narrative scope clarification has not been fully achieved. Accepting this as persuasive signals commitment to completing the narrative expansion in the PFE IG.

**Action:** Add comprehensive narrative sections to the PFE IG that (a) define the functioning domains covered using ICF categories, (b) explain the multi-dimensional nature of functioning assessments (performance, capability, goals), and (c) explicitly state which FHIR resource types (Observation, Condition, Goal, etc.) are in scope now versus planned for the future.

### Disposition B: Considered for Future Use

**Rationale:** Much of this request has already been partially addressed through the evolution from two separate IGs to the merged PFE IG, the adoption of the ICF framework, and the addition of functional status definitions (per FHIR-30036's resolution). The remaining gaps — detailed ICF mappings and explicit resource type scoping — are being tracked by more recent, targeted tickets (FHIR-38054). Deferring allows the PFE IG to mature further before committing to specific narrative content.

**Action:** Acknowledge the progress made since the original filing. Close this ticket as deferred, noting that FHIR-38054 and related PFE IG tickets are tracking the remaining scope clarification work.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive (Superseded)

**Rationale:** The original ticket was filed against the Functional Status IG, which no longer exists as a standalone specification. The IG has been merged into the PFE IG (addressing the FHIR-30014 companion proposal), the ICF framework has been adopted, and a functional status definition has been added (per FHIR-30036). The specific concerns about the "status" terminology and IG merger have been overtaken by events. Remaining narrative gaps are better addressed through the successor tickets filed directly against the PFE IG.

**Action:** Close as not persuasive, citing that the original specification no longer exists and the substantive concerns have been addressed or are being tracked by FHIR-38054 and FHIR-38618 against the PFE IG.

## Recommendation

**Disposition C: Not Persuasive (Superseded)** is recommended.

The original Functional Status IG targeted by this ticket has been superseded by the Personal Functioning and Engagement (PFE) IG, which already addresses the two most significant concerns raised: (1) adopting the ICF as the conceptual framework, and (2) merging the Functional Status and Cognitive Status IGs. The definition of functional status was added per FHIR-30036 (resolved Persuasive). The remaining open concerns about ICF mapping detail and title clarity are actively tracked by more specific, current tickets (FHIR-38054, FHIR-38618) filed directly against the PFE IG. Closing this ticket as superseded avoids duplicate tracking while acknowledging that the substantive work continues under the successor specification.
