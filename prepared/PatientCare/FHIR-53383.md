## Summary

The submitter argues that the current definition of "Community Based Organization" (CBO) in the SDOH Clinical Care IG is too broad. The existing definition — "An organization that provides specific services to the community or to a targeted population within the community" — could equally apply to healthcare organizations such as Federally Qualified Health Centers (FQHCs). The key differentiator, per the submitter, is that CBOs provide **social care** services rather than healthcare services. The submitter also proposes that the Gravity Project introduce the term "socialcare" (one word, no hyphen) as a counterpart to "healthcare," and that refining the CBO definition would be an ideal place to debut this term.

The resolution marks the ticket as **Persuasive** and states: "The Gravity Project will work on a more precise definition of CBO and socialcare in the Glossary, as part of our Glossary revisions."

---

## Details

### Description

The submitter identifies a specific ambiguity in the CBO definition: the current wording could encompass any organization serving a community, including healthcare providers like FQHCs. The proposed fix is to clarify that CBOs provide *social care* services specifically, distinguishing them from organizations providing healthcare services.

Additionally, the submitter advocates for the Gravity Project to formalize the term "socialcare" (as a single word) to parallel "healthcare." The rationale is that the Gravity Project is uniquely positioned to introduce this vocabulary given its charter to create terminology for the SDOH space.

### Resolution Description

> The Gravity Project will work on a more precise definition of CBO and socialcare in the Glossary, as part of our Glossary revisions.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept (Persuasive)

**Action:** Accept the resolution as stated. The Gravity Project will revise the CBO definition in the Glossary to clarify that CBOs provide social care services (as opposed to healthcare services) and will incorporate the term "socialcare" into the Glossary.

**Rationale:** The current CBO definition is demonstrably over-broad — it cannot distinguish a CBO from an FQHC or any other community-serving healthcare organization. Tightening the definition to emphasize social care services resolves this ambiguity. The Gravity Project's charter to develop SDOH vocabulary makes it the appropriate venue for introducing "socialcare" as a formal term. The resolution aligns with the submitter's request and does not require structural changes to profiles or value sets — only a Glossary update.

**Impact:** Low technical impact. The change is limited to Glossary text. Downstream effects may include clearer guidance for implementers on which Organization types qualify as CBOs when using SDOH-related profiles, but no profile or conformance changes are needed.

### Disposition B: Accept with Modification (Persuasive with Modification)

**Action:** Accept the Glossary revision for CBO definition, but defer the introduction of "socialcare" as a formal term. Instead, use the established phrase "social care" (two words) in the CBO definition to avoid confusion and vocabulary adoption barriers.

**Rationale:** The core issue — that the CBO definition needs to distinguish social care from healthcare — is valid and should be addressed. However, coining "socialcare" as a single word is a broader vocabulary initiative that may face resistance or require broader consensus beyond the Gravity Project. The CBO definition can be improved immediately using existing language (e.g., "An organization that provides social care services…") without waiting for or depending on the adoption of a neologism.

**Impact:** Same low technical impact as Disposition A, but avoids potential controversy around a new term. The Glossary revision proceeds independently of the "socialcare" terminology question, which can be addressed in a separate effort.

### Disposition C: Decline (Not Persuasive)

**Action:** Decline the change. Retain the current CBO definition as-is.

**Rationale:** The current definition, while broad, provides flexibility for implementers to apply it across jurisdictions and contexts where the boundary between healthcare and social care may vary. Adding "social care" as a qualifier could inadvertently exclude organizations that straddle the healthcare/social-care boundary (e.g., organizations providing both behavioral health and social services). The definition's breadth may be intentional to accommodate diverse implementation contexts.

**Impact:** No changes required. However, the ambiguity identified by the submitter remains, and implementers may continue to interpret "CBO" inconsistently.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Accept (Persuasive)**

The resolution as stated is appropriate. The ambiguity in the current CBO definition is a real problem — it fails to distinguish CBOs from healthcare organizations that also serve communities. The submitter's analysis is sound, and the resolution commits to a concrete corrective action (Glossary revision) through the right venue (Gravity Project). The introduction of "socialcare" in the Glossary is a low-risk, high-clarity improvement that the Gravity Project is well-positioned to steward. The fact that this is a Glossary-only change minimizes disruption while improving definitional precision for all downstream implementers of the SDOH Clinical Care IG.
