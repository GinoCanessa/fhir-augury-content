## Description

The FHIR specification has multiple resources relevant to representing clinical "Concerns" — Condition, Observation, Linkage, List, and EpisodeOfCare — but lacks a single place that explains how these pieces fit together and how they should be used in concert. The ticket also requests guidance on how these FHIR constructs map to C-CDA's notion of "Concern" (the Health Concern / Problem Concern Act pattern).

## Related Zulip Conversations

### 1. [cda to fhir] CCDA Health Concerns to FHIR
- **URL:** https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/cda%20to%20fhir/topic/CCDA%20Health%20Concerns%20to%20FHIR
- **Date:** 2018-08-28 to 2020-03-07
- **Participants:** Michael Allange, Lloyd McKenzie, Michelle (Moseman) Miller, Lisa Nelson
- **Summary:** Michael Allange asked how to map C-CDA Health Concern entries to FHIR. Lloyd explained that Concern in FHIR is expressed by including entries in a practitioner's or patient's problem List, using Linkage to tie multiple representations together, and using EpisodeOfCare for grouping. Michelle noted that "Concern" is overloaded — US Core uses Condition to represent "what" the concern event was (which may or may not appear on a problem list), while Linkage represents the aggregated concern. This conversation directly prompted the creation of FHIR-17736.

## Analysis

### Core Issue
This ticket has been open since August 2018 — over six years — and remains in "Triaged" status with no assignee. The fundamental ask is straightforward: the FHIR spec needs a section explaining how Condition, Observation, List, Linkage, EpisodeOfCare, ClinicalImpression, and CarePlan work together to represent the clinical concept of a "Concern," and how this maps to C-CDA's Concern pattern.

### Progress Made
Significant draft content already exists:
- **Jay Lyle's draft (2021/2023):** A well-developed narrative covering five resources (Observation, ClinicalImpression, Condition, List, CarePlan) and how they support a problem-oriented view. The draft was reviewed at multiple PC FHIR calls in 2023.
- **Confluence page:** A discussion of the Concern vs. Condition boundary exists at https://confluence.hl7.org/pages/viewpage.action?pageId=46892408
- **Lloyd McKenzie's feedback:** Endorsed the draft and recommended also including EpisodeOfCare.

### Open Questions from the Draft
1. Should ClinicalImpression's 'investigation' and 'prognosis' be moved to Condition? (No evidence of implementation for ClinicalImpression)
2. How to connect discrete pieces to visualize clinical patterns over time
3. Where in the spec the guidance should live (Condition notes section vs. Clinical Summary module vs. a standalone page)

### Blocking Impact
Michelle Miller noted in 2018 that this ticket was a factor in holding Condition back from FMM 4 advancement.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive — Add Concern Guidance Section to FHIR Spec
**Action:** Accept the request. Finalize Jay Lyle's existing draft (incorporating Lloyd McKenzie's EpisodeOfCare feedback), resolve the open editorial questions, and add the guidance as a new section in the Clinical Summary module or as a standalone page referenced from Condition, List, and EpisodeOfCare. Include a subsection mapping the FHIR pattern to C-CDA's Health Concern / Problem Concern Act.

**Rationale:** The draft is substantially complete and has been reviewed at multiple WG calls. The need for this guidance is well-established — it was cited as blocking Condition's FMM advancement and is a recurring question from implementers (as evidenced by the C-CDA Zulip thread). Six years without resolution is too long for a common implementation question.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification — Address via Confluence and Spec Cross-References
**Action:** Accept the request in a modified form. Rather than a full new spec section, formalize and expand the existing Confluence page (pageId=46892408) as an implementer-facing guide, and add brief cross-reference notes in the Condition, List, and EpisodeOfCare resource pages pointing to the guidance. This lighter approach avoids adding significant new normative content while still addressing the guidance gap.

**Rationale:** A non-normative Confluence guide is quicker to finalize and publish, and can be updated independently of the FHIR release cycle. The spec itself would only need small editorial additions to reference it.

### Disposition C: Deferred — Reassign and Target for Next Ballot Cycle
**Action:** Defer the ticket. Assign to a specific owner (e.g., Jay Lyle, who has been drafting content), set a concrete deadline for a next-cycle ballot, and ensure it is tracked as a priority item for Patient Care WG.

**Rationale:** While the need is real, the draft still has unresolved editorial questions (ClinicalImpression scope, placement, temporal visualization) and has stalled since September 2023 without final approval. Deferring with a named assignee and milestone prevents another multi-year lapse.

---

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Persuasive — Add Concern Guidance Section to FHIR Spec**

This ticket is over six years old, has a substantially complete draft, has been reviewed at multiple WG calls, and addresses a common implementer question that was explicitly cited as blocking Condition's maturity advancement. The remaining open questions are editorial, not fundamental. The WG should finalize the draft content (adding EpisodeOfCare, resolving placement), assign an owner to shepherd it through, and include it in the next FHIR build. Continued deferral risks another multi-year stall on an issue that directly impacts both implementer experience and resource maturity.
