## Description

The submitter suggests adding nutrition as an optional section in the International Patient Summary (IPS). The rationale is that certain diets—such as enteral feeding and dysphagia diets—would be important to know for unplanned care because they could present a patient safety issue.

## Analysis

### Context
This ticket is one of three near-duplicate ballot comments (FHIR-24999, FHIR-25021, FHIR-25022) from the IPS ballot cycle, all requesting nutrition as an optional IPS section. All three were originally resolved as "Considered for Future Use" in February 2020 with a strong 12-0-1 vote, then re-opened in November 2022 post STU update publication.

Since the original disposition, the landscape has evolved:
- **FHIR-46923** (2024, Highest priority) specifically proposes NutritionIntake in the IPS Social History section, and its December 2024 discussion indicates the approach is to first coordinate with ISO 27269 before proceeding.
- The Australian Sparked initiative ranked food/nutrition as the top priority for SDOH data in patient summaries.
- The FHIR Core Nutrition Module has seen active development (FHIR-50383 applied).

The original disposition to coordinate with ISO 27269 remains the active strategy, as confirmed by the FHIR-46923 discussion thread.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Defer — Coordinate with ISO 27269 and Consolidate with FHIR-46923
**Action:** Resolve as **Deferred / Considered for Future Use**. Consolidate this ticket with FHIR-24999, FHIR-25022, and FHIR-46923 under a single tracking effort. The workgroup should first coordinate with the ISO 27269 (IPS Dataset) team on whether nutrition belongs in the international dataset definition before adding it to the FHIR IPS IG.

**Rationale:** This continues the established approach. FHIR-46923 is already pursuing coordination with ISO 27269 as discussed on the December 2024 IPS call. Adding a nutrition section to the FHIR IG before the ISO standard addresses it risks divergence. The three ballot comments (FHIR-24999, -25021, -25022) should be consolidated to avoid redundant tracking.

**Suggested vote text:** "Defer. Consolidate with FHIR-24999, FHIR-25022, and FHIR-46923. Coordinate with ISO 27269 IPS Dataset team on nutrition content inclusion before adding to the FHIR IPS IG."

### Disposition B: Apply — Add Nutrition as an Optional IPS Section
**Action:** Resolve as **Persuasive**. Add a nutrition section (using NutritionOrder and/or NutritionIntake resources) as an optional section in the IPS Composition profile.

**Rationale:** There is strong consensus (12-0-1 in 2020) supporting the clinical value of nutrition data for patient safety. Multiple independent ballot commenters raised the same concern. International implementers (Australia) rank nutrition as the #1 SDOH priority. The FHIR Nutrition Module is maturing. Since the section would be optional, it poses no conformance burden on implementations that don't need it. Waiting for ISO alignment has already delayed this for 5+ years.

**Suggested vote text:** "Persuasive. Add nutrition as an optional section in the IPS Composition using NutritionOrder/NutritionIntake. Resolve FHIR-24999 and FHIR-25022 as duplicates."

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive — Nutrition Is Out of IPS Scope
**Action:** Resolve as **Not Persuasive**.

**Rationale:** The IPS should remain a focused summary for unplanned cross-border care. Nutrition data, while clinically important, expands the IPS scope beyond its original intent. National derivations can add nutrition sections as needed without changing the international specification. As Jose Costa Teixeira noted in Zulip discussions, IPS "is not meant to be the representation of the EHR" and should remain "an agreed subset."

**Suggested vote text:** "Not Persuasive. Nutrition data is important but is best addressed through national IPS derivations rather than the core international specification."

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Defer — Coordinate with ISO 27269 and Consolidate with FHIR-46923**

This is the recommended disposition because:
1. **Active coordination is already underway** — FHIR-46923 is pursuing the ISO 27269 coordination path as discussed on the December 2024 IPS call, making this the natural vehicle for the work.
2. **Consolidation reduces overhead** — Three near-duplicate tickets plus FHIR-46923 should be tracked as one effort.
3. **ISO alignment matters** — The IPS is based on ISO 27269; adding nutrition to the FHIR IG without ISO alignment risks specification divergence.
4. **The request is not rejected** — Deferral acknowledges the clinical validity (12-0-1 vote, international implementer support) while ensuring proper coordination before implementation.

The workgroup should explicitly link FHIR-25021, FHIR-24999, and FHIR-25022 to FHIR-46923 and ensure the ISO 27269 feedback loop is actively tracked.
