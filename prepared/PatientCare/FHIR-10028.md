# Ticket Review: FHIR-10028

**Title:** Careplan: Provide ability to specify patient and/or provider preferences
**Status:** Triaged **Priority:** Medium **Type:** Change Request
**Work Group:** Patient Care **Specification:** FHIR Core (FHIR)
**Reporter:** Emma Jones **Assignee:** Unassigned
**Created:** 2016-05-13 **Updated:** 2025-07-17 **Labels:** None

## Summary

This long-standing change request, originating from Clinicians on FHIR at Montreal 2016, asks for a standardized mechanism to capture patient and/or provider preferences within FHIR, initially scoped to CarePlan but recognized as cross-resource in nature. Over nine years of discussion, the work group has considered multiple approaches: profiling Observation, creating a new resource, using extensions on CarePlan and Request resources, or leveraging existing resources such as NutritionOrder. The most recent activity (June–July 2025) indicates a logical model for preferences has been drafted and largely agreed upon, with the group now exploring an extension-based approach on CarePlan and all Request resources. Cross-work-group coordination with Orders & Observations, Patient Empowerment, IPS, and PACIO/MCC is planned.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept — Define Standard Extension on CarePlan and Request Resources
#### Proposal
Accept the change request and pursue the approach currently being developed by the work group: define a standard extension (or set of extensions) on CarePlan and all Request resources to capture patient and/or provider preferences. The extension should support the preference content, preference category (nutrition, medication, care, end-of-life), priority (high/normal/delayed), expressor, and recorder. This aligns with the logical model drafted in July 2025 and Emma Jones's stated preference for an extension-based approach. The work should be coordinated with Orders & Observations, Patient Empowerment, IPS, and PACIO/MCC as planned in the June 2025 next steps.
#### Justification
This approach has the most recent momentum and working group agreement. The July 2025 call confirmed a logical model has been largely agreed upon. An extension on CarePlan and Request resources enables reuse across multiple resource types without requiring changes to Observation's scope. It addresses the concern that preferences are persistent statements rather than point-in-time observations, and it keeps the modeling within Patient Care's purview while enabling cross-resource applicability.

---

### Disposition B: Accept — Profile Observation for Preferences
#### Proposal
Accept the change request by creating an Observation profile for patient/provider preferences, as originally voted in 2016 (5-0-0). The profile would use Observation.code to indicate the preference category, Observation.value to capture the preference content, and extensions for expressor, recorder, and priority. This aligns with the ADI/PACIO consensus (Sept 2024 WGM) that person-authored preference concepts use the Observation resource, and with US Core Treatment Preferences which are also modeled as observations. Ownership could be shared with or transferred to Orders & Observations.
#### Justification
The Observation-based approach has historical precedent from the original 2016 vote, alignment with ADI/PACIO consensus for advance directive preferences, and consistency with US Core Treatment Preferences. Observation already has infrastructure for coded values, categories, and references. However, this approach was questioned in recent 2025 discussions — some members noted that Observation represents point-in-time snapshots rather than persistent preferences, and the June 2025 call shifted direction toward extensions.

---

### Disposition C: Defer Pending Logical Model Completion
#### Proposal
Defer this ticket until the logical model for preferences is finalized and cross-work-group coordination (with OO, PE, IPS, PACIO/MCC) is completed. The July 2025 call confirmed the logical model is not yet ready to discuss in the context of CarePlan. A specification change should not be made until the joint meeting planned for the September 2025 WGM has occurred and broader consensus is achieved.
#### Justification
The ticket has been open since 2016 and has been deferred multiple times across release cycles. The most recent comment (July 2025) explicitly states the work is not ready to be applied to CarePlan. Making a specification change before the logical model is mature and cross-WG consensus is achieved risks premature standardization that may need to be revised. Deferral allows the current active work to reach maturity before committing to a specific modeling approach.

---

### Recommendation
**Recommended disposition:** A

The extension-based approach on CarePlan and Request resources represents the current direction endorsed by the Patient Care work group, with a logical model already drafted and largely agreed upon as of July 2025. This approach addresses the core requirement — enabling reusable, persistent preference capture across multiple resource types — while avoiding the semantic concerns raised about Observation being a point-in-time resource. The work group has clear next steps: finalize the extension in CarePlan calls, then coordinate with OO, PE, IPS, and PACIO/MCC before the September 2025 WGM. After nine years of discussion and multiple approach considerations, the extension path offers the best balance of technical fitness, cross-resource reusability, and alignment with the group's most recent consensus. Accepting the request now formalizes the active work and provides a clear mandate to proceed.
