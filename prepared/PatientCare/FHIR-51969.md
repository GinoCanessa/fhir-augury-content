## Summary

This ticket requests adding MustSupport (MS) flags and obligations (`SHALL:populate-if-known`, `SHALL:handle`, `SHOULD:display`) to the value elements in two IPS social history observation profiles: `valueQuantity` in **ObservationAlcoholUseUvIps** and `valueCodeableConcept` in **ObservationTobaccoUseUvIps**. The reporter notes an inconsistency: the pregnancy history profiles (`ObservationPregnancyEddUvIps`, `ObservationPregnancyOutcomeUvIps`, `ObservationPregnancyStatusUvIps`) already have these MS flags and obligations on their `value[x]` slices, but the social history profiles do not. A PR was submitted ([HL7/fhir-ips#85](https://github.com/HL7/fhir-ips/pull/85)) implementing the change.

The IPS team discussed this on the 8/27/25 weekly call and resolved it as "Considered for Future Use" — the omission was deliberate because ISO 27269 has fewer requirements around social history than pregnancy profiles. The PR was closed without merging, with a note that this will be considered after IPS 2.0 publication.

## Details

### Description

The pregnancy history (PregnancyHx) entry profiles have their respective `value[x]` slices flagged as MS with obligations of `SHALL:populate-if-known`, `SHALL:handle`, and `SHOULD:display`. However, the social history entry profiles `ObservationAlcoholUseUvIps` and `ObservationTobaccoUseUvIps` do not follow this pattern — their `valueQuantity` and `valueCodeableConcept` elements respectively are not flagged as MS with obligations.

The reporter sees no reason why these social history profiles should not follow the same pattern as pregnancy profiles and recommends adding the MS flags and obligations.

### Comments

**jdamore (2025-08-27):** Discussed on 8/27/25 IPS weekly call. The omission was deliberate since ISO 27269 does not have as much requirements around social history (e.g. alcohol and tobacco) as it does for pregnancy profiles.

**Resolution Description:** Reviewed on 8/27 IPS call and will consider after 2.0 publication.

## Proposed Dispositions

### A: Accept — Apply After IPS 2.0 (Persuasive with Modification)

**Disposition:** Persuasive with Modification

Add MS flags and obligations (`SHALL:populate-if-known`, `SHALL:handle`, `SHOULD:display`) to `valueQuantity` in `ObservationAlcoholUseUvIps` and `valueCodeableConcept` in `ObservationTobaccoUseUvIps`, scheduled for the next release after IPS 2.0 publication. This aligns social history profiles with the existing pregnancy history pattern and improves interoperability consistency. The modification is to defer the change to post-2.0, respecting the current publication timeline while acknowledging the validity of the request.

**Rationale:** The inconsistency is real and affects implementers who expect uniform obligation patterns across IPS observation profiles. While ISO 27269 has fewer requirements for social history, the FHIR IG can exceed the ISO baseline to improve practical interoperability. The PR already exists and can be reapplied to the next release branch.

### B: Decline — Maintain ISO 27269 Alignment (Not Persuasive)

**Disposition:** Not Persuasive

Decline the change. The current design is intentional: ISO 27269 has weaker requirements around social history observations compared to pregnancy profiles. Adding MS and obligations beyond what ISO 27269 requires would create obligations on implementers that are not grounded in the underlying standard. The IPS IG should stay aligned with ISO 27269 to avoid scope creep and unnecessary implementation burden.

**Rationale:** The IPS team deliberately omitted these obligations based on ISO 27269 alignment. Adding them creates a precedent where every observation profile must have uniform obligations regardless of clinical priority or standards requirements.

### C: Accept as Submitted — Apply Immediately (Persuasive)

**Disposition:** Persuasive

Accept the change as proposed and apply it to the current release. The value elements in observation profiles are fundamental to the observation's clinical meaning. Not marking them as MS with obligations suggests that the value of an alcohol or tobacco use observation is optional for interoperability, which undermines the purpose of including these profiles in the IPS. Consistency across all observation profiles improves implementer experience and reduces confusion.

**Rationale:** If the social history observations are important enough to include as named profiles in the IPS composition, their value elements should be important enough to be MustSupport with obligations. The change is small (2 files, 15 lines) and low risk.

## Recommendation

**Recommended Disposition: A — Accept, Apply After IPS 2.0 (Persuasive with Modification)**

The inconsistency identified by the reporter is valid and affects implementer expectations. The value element is the clinical core of any observation — if a profile exists for alcohol/tobacco use in the IPS, the value should carry MS and obligations. However, the IPS team's decision to defer past 2.0 publication is pragmatic and avoids last-minute changes to a release in progress. Disposition A respects both the technical merit of the request and the project's release timeline. This aligns with the existing resolution ("Considered for Future Use") and the PR comment indicating post-2.0 consideration. The closely related tickets (FHIR-51934, FHIR-51932) suggest a broader social history profile improvement effort that could be coordinated together in the next release cycle.
