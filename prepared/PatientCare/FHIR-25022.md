## Description

The submitter affirms with a comment on patient safety related to dysphagia and enteral feeding, and suggests that nutrition be added as an optional section of the IPS Composition. The concern is that patients on dysphagia diets or requiring enteral feeding instructions could face patient safety risks if this information is not available in an unplanned care scenario.

## Analysis

This ticket is one of **three companion ballot comments** (FHIR-24999, FHIR-25021, FHIR-25022) from the same 2019 IPS ballot cycle, all requesting that nutrition be added as an optional section of the IPS Composition. All three cite the patient safety rationale: patients on dysphagia diets, fluid restrictions, or enteral feeding could face safety risks in unplanned care if nutrition information is absent.

All three were originally resolved as "Considered for Future Use" in February 2020 with a strong vote (12-0-1), with instructions to share the comment with the ISO 27269 (IPS Dataset) team. All were re-opened in November 2022 post STU update publication.

The more recent FHIR-46923 (2024) took a concrete approach by proposing a NutritionIntake slice in the Social History section, but was also deferred with the recommendation to first send feedback to ISO 27269. This suggests a consistent pattern: the workgroup acknowledges the clinical value of nutrition information in IPS but wants ISO 27269 alignment before making changes to the FHIR IG.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Defer — Considered for Future Use (Recommended)
**Resolution:** Considered for Future Use
**Action:** Defer this ticket pending alignment with ISO 27269 on nutrition data in the IPS dataset. The workgroup should track the outcome of the ISO 27269 outreach initiated for FHIR-46923 and revisit these companion tickets together once ISO provides guidance.
**Rationale:** This follows the established precedent for this ticket and its companions. The workgroup has consistently recognized the clinical merit (12-0-1 vote) but determined that IPS structural changes should align with the ISO 27269 dataset definition. FHIR-46923 already initiated outreach to ISO 27269 (per the Dec 2024 IPS call discussion). Deferring avoids duplicating that effort while keeping the ticket active for future consideration. The three companion tickets (FHIR-24999, FHIR-25021, FHIR-25022) should be resolved together.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification — Add Nutrition as Optional Section
**Resolution:** Persuasive with Modification
**Action:** Add a nutrition section as an optional (0..1) slice in the IPS Composition resource, referencing NutritionOrder and/or NutritionIntake. Apply the change and resolve FHIR-24999 and FHIR-25021 as duplicates of this ticket.
**Rationale:** After five years and multiple ballot cycles raising the same concern, the patient safety argument is well-established. The workgroup could proceed independently of ISO 27269 alignment, since the FHIR IG can include optional sections that go beyond the ISO dataset. This would address a long-standing patient safety gap, and making it optional minimizes impact on existing implementations.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive — Close Without Action
**Resolution:** Not Persuasive
**Action:** Close this ticket. Nutrition information can already be conveyed through existing IPS mechanisms (e.g., as part of Plan of Care or through extensions). Direct requesters to FHIR-46923 for future nutrition-related enhancements.
**Rationale:** The IPS intentionally maintains a minimal section structure. Nutrition-specific content may be better addressed through existing optional sections (e.g., Plan of Care) or through implementation-specific extensions rather than a new top-level section. The ISO 27269 dataset does not currently include a nutrition section, and diverging from the ISO standard could create interoperability challenges.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Defer — Considered for Future Use** is recommended. This is consistent with the prior resolution (12-0-1 vote), respects the ongoing ISO 27269 coordination initiated through FHIR-46923, and avoids redundant work. The workgroup should consolidate FHIR-24999, FHIR-25021, and FHIR-25022 for joint resolution once ISO 27269 provides direction on nutrition data in the IPS dataset.
