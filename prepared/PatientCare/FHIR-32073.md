## Summary

This ticket reports an inconsistency in the International Patient Summary (IPS) Implementation Guide regarding how profile-specific references are applied across related elements. Specifically, the `performer` element on IPS resources is constrained to reference IPS-profiled resources (e.g., IPS Practitioner, IPS Organization), while the `resultsInterpreter` element—which is not constrained out—still permits references to base (non-profiled) Practitioner, Organization, etc. The reporter notes this pattern appears in multiple places throughout the IG and asks whether it is intentional. The ticket was previously resolved as "Considered for Future Use" via a 6-0-0 Patient Care vote on 2022-10-10, with the acknowledgment that the inconsistency was by design but should be revisited in a future version for consistency. It was subsequently reopened post-STU update publication and remains triaged.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested
#### Proposal
Update the IPS profiles so that all reference elements pointing to Practitioner, Organization, PractitionerRole, and similar resources consistently use IPS-profiled target references. Specifically, constrain `resultsInterpreter` (and any other unconstrained reference elements identified) on resources such as DiagnosticReport-IPS and Observation-IPS to reference IPS Practitioner, IPS PractitionerRole, and IPS Organization, matching the pattern already applied to `performer`.
#### Justification
Consistency in reference target profiles reduces implementer confusion and ensures that all referenced resources carry the minimum data expectations defined by IPS. The prior vote acknowledged this inconsistency was by design but recognized that future alignment was desirable. Since the ticket was reopened specifically to address this in a subsequent version, now is the appropriate time to harmonize these references.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach
#### Proposal
Rather than tightening `resultsInterpreter` and similar elements to require IPS-profiled references, add non-normative guidance (e.g., in the IG narrative or a "Must Support" note) recommending that implementers use IPS-profiled references where available, while keeping the formal constraints relaxed. This preserves flexibility for systems that may not produce fully IPS-profiled practitioner or organization data for all roles.
#### Justification
Some source systems may populate `resultsInterpreter` from different data sources than `performer`, where full IPS-profile compliance is harder to guarantee. Adding guidance rather than hard constraints avoids breaking existing conformant implementations while still encouraging best practice. This approach acknowledges the practical reality that not all referenced actors may meet IPS minimum data requirements.

---

### Disposition C: Decline
#### Proposal
Decline the request on the basis that the current design is intentional and does not create interoperability issues. Close as "Won't Fix."
#### Justification
The prior Patient Care vote (6-0-0) confirmed the inconsistency was by design. Elements like `resultsInterpreter` are less commonly populated than `performer`, and requiring IPS-profiled references could create unnecessary conformance burdens. Implementers are already free to use IPS-profiled resources in these references without a formal constraint. Reopening this ticket does not necessarily indicate the design rationale has changed.

---

### Recommendation
**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket was reopened after STU publication specifically to revisit this inconsistency, indicating the work group intended to address it in a future cycle. The original vote acknowledged the inconsistency and expressed a desire to "make the references as consistent as possible" in a future version. Aligning `resultsInterpreter` and similar elements with the profiling pattern already applied to `performer` is a straightforward change that improves internal consistency of the IG, reduces implementer confusion, and upholds the design principle that IPS resources should reference other IPS-profiled resources. The reporter (Elliot Silver) raised this as a quality concern across multiple areas of the IG, and addressing it now fulfills the commitment made during the prior resolution.
