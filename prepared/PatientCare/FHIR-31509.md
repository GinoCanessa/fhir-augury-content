## Description

The submitter recommends that the PACIO Cognitive Status IG include differentiation between **self-reported measures** (patient-reported outcomes) and **performance-based measures**.

Key points from the submission:
- Findings from a self-report measure and a performance-based measure for the same activity may differ in the same individual.
- **Self-report measures** relay information about the individual's *perception* of how impaired body function or structure limits activities and participation.
- **Performance-based measures** provide *data* to the clinician about the level of impairment in body structure or functions.
- The distinction reveals the difference between perceived/self-measured ability versus clinically measured performance of a task or activity.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive
**Action:** Accept the comment and add narrative guidance to the PACIO Cognitive Status IG (or its successor PFE IG) that explicitly differentiates self-report measures from performance-based measures, including definitions and clinical significance of each type.

**Rationale:** The distinction between self-reported and performance-based measures is clinically meaningful and well-established in rehabilitation and functioning assessment literature. The WHO ICF framework (already referenced in FHIR-29978) supports this differentiation. Adding this guidance would improve implementer understanding of how to correctly categorize and interpret observation data. It aligns with the broader effort to clarify the IG's scope and conceptual foundations.

### Disposition B: Not Persuasive with Modification
**Action:** Acknowledge the clinical validity of the distinction but defer structural changes. Instead, add a brief informative note in the IG explaining that self-report and performance-based measures may yield different results for the same activity, and reference external classification frameworks (e.g., ICF, LOINC panel types) for further detail.

**Rationale:** The IG's primary focus is on data exchange profiles, not clinical methodology. A lightweight informative note would address the commenter's concern without expanding the IG's scope into clinical assessment methodology. The LOINC classification of assessment instruments already distinguishes patient-reported vs. clinician-administered tools, so the IG could simply reference these existing classifications.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive
**Action:** Decline the request, noting that the differentiation between measure types is a clinical concern outside the IG's scope and is already handled by the underlying terminology (LOINC codes distinguish assessment instrument types).

**Rationale:** The IG defines FHIR profiles for exchanging cognitive status observations, not clinical assessment methodology. The choice of self-report vs. performance-based instrument is a clinical workflow decision captured in the coding of the observation (e.g., specific LOINC codes). Adding this content could broaden the IG's scope beyond its intended purpose.

---

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Persuasive** is recommended.

The commenter raises a clinically important distinction that directly impacts how observation data should be interpreted and categorized. Given that the PACIO project has since evolved toward the Personal Functioning and Engagement (PFE) IG—which adopted the ICF framework as recommended in related ticket FHIR-29978—incorporating guidance on self-report vs. performance-based measures aligns with that direction. The distinction between perceived ability and measured performance is fundamental to functioning assessment and would materially improve the IG's utility for implementers. The related open tickets (FHIR-29978 scope clarification, FHIR-29977 IG merger) suggest this area is already under active consideration, making this an appropriate time to incorporate the guidance.
