## Description

The submitter suggests adding **Nutrition** as an optional section in the IPS Composition resource. The rationale is patient safety: while a general healthful diet may not be critical for unplanned care encounters, specific nutritional requirements such as **dysphagia diets**, **fluid restrictions**, or **enteral feeding instructions** can present patient safety risks if not communicated. Adding Nutrition as an optional (not required) section would allow IPS documents to convey this information when relevant.

## Analysis

### Context
This ticket is one of **three nearly identical ballot comments** (FHIR-24999, FHIR-25021, FHIR-25022) submitted during the same ballot period in October 2019, all requesting the addition of a Nutrition section to the IPS Composition as an optional section. All three were originally resolved as "Considered for Future Use" with a 12-0-1 vote on 2020-02-04, with a note to share the feedback with the ISO IPS (ISO 27269) team. All three were re-opened by lynn_laakso in November 2022 following STU update publication.

### Key Considerations
- **Patient Safety Rationale**: All three submitters emphasize patient safety scenarios (dysphagia diets, enteral feeding, fluid restrictions) where missing nutrition information during unplanned care could cause harm.
- **ISO 27269 Alignment**: The original 2020 disposition explicitly deferred to the ISO IPS Dataset team. Any resolution should consider whether ISO 27269 has since addressed nutrition in its dataset definition.
- **FHIR NutritionOrder Resource**: FHIR already has the `NutritionOrder` resource which could serve as the entry type for an optional Nutrition section, making implementation technically straightforward.
- **Three Duplicate Tickets**: FHIR-25021 and FHIR-25022 should be resolved consistently with FHIR-24999, ideally as duplicates or with identical dispositions.
- **Elapsed Time**: This request has been open since 2019. The IPS specification has evolved, and the community expectation for nutrition information may have changed.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive with Modification
**Action**: Add an optional Nutrition section to the IPS Composition profile, with `NutritionOrder` as the entry reference type, and coordinate with ISO 27269 on alignment.

**Rationale**: The patient safety argument is compelling — dysphagia diets, enteral feeding instructions, and fluid restrictions are clinically important in unplanned care settings. Three independent ballot commenters raised the same concern, indicating community demand. The FHIR `NutritionOrder` resource already exists and is a natural fit. Making it optional means no breaking change and no additional burden on implementers who don't need it. The original deferral to ISO was reasonable, but after 5+ years the FHIR IPS IG can lead by including it and work with ISO on alignment iteratively.

**Resolution for siblings**: Resolve FHIR-25021 and FHIR-25022 as duplicates of FHIR-24999.

### Disposition B: Considered for Future Use (Re-affirm)
**Action**: Re-affirm the original 2020 disposition. Defer to a future ballot cycle pending ISO 27269 alignment and broader community review of which optional sections to add.

**Rationale**: The original 12-0-1 vote to defer was not a rejection of the idea but a sequencing decision. Adding new optional sections to IPS should be coordinated with ISO 27269 to avoid divergence between the FHIR IPS IG and the international standard. Without confirmation that ISO has added nutrition to its dataset, proceeding unilaterally could create inconsistency. The ticket can remain open for the next ballot cycle.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive
**Action**: Close as not persuasive. Nutrition information can already be conveyed through existing IPS mechanisms (e.g., allergy/intolerance section for food-related restrictions, or as part of plan of care).

**Rationale**: The IPS is intentionally a minimal dataset for unplanned care. Adding sections, even optional ones, increases implementation complexity and testing surface. Dysphagia and enteral feeding concerns could be captured under existing sections (e.g., Plan of Treatment, or as coded conditions in the Problems section). The bar for adding new sections should be high, and nutrition may not meet that threshold for a cross-border summary focused on acute safety.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Persuasive with Modification** is recommended.

The patient safety rationale is well-supported by three independent commenters highlighting the same clinical scenarios. Five years have passed since the original deferral, and the IPS specification has matured sufficiently to accommodate an optional section without disrupting existing implementations. The `NutritionOrder` resource provides a ready-made entry type. Coordinating with ISO 27269 can happen in parallel rather than as a prerequisite. This resolves three long-standing tickets and addresses a genuine clinical safety gap in the IPS.
