## Summary

The reporter identifies ambiguity in the International Birth And Child Model IG regarding whether a child immediately after birth falls within the IG's scope. The "In scope" section's opening paragraph implies the child post-birth is out of scope, yet section 5.1.1 includes content about the child after birth. The reporter requests clarification on: (1) whether only the fetus or also the child after birth is in scope, (2) if the child is in scope, for how long after birth, and (3) explicit definition of the terms used (fetus vs. child vs. newborn). The reporter also noted this should have been filed as a Change Request rather than a Technical Correction.

## Details

### Description

The first paragraph of the "In scope" section suggests that the child after birth is out of scope of the IG. However it is part of it (for example, in section 5.1.1). The reporter suggests clarifying throughout the spec whether only fetus or also child after birth (till when?) is in scope, and making explicit which terms are used for these.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive (Accept)

**Action:** Accept the request and add explicit clarifying language to the IG.

- Revise the "In scope" section to explicitly state that the IG covers the continuum from fetal life through a defined post-birth period (e.g., the immediate neonatal period or the first 28 days of life).
- Define key terms (fetus, newborn, infant, child) in a glossary or in section 5.1.1 and use them consistently throughout the document.
- Resolve the contradiction between the introductory scope statement and the content in section 5.1.1 by aligning the scope statement with the actual coverage.

**Rationale:** The ambiguity is clearly demonstrable (the scope text contradicts section 5.1.1). Multiple other tickets from different reporters (FHIR-41853, FHIR-42530, FHIR-42061) independently identified the same confusion, confirming this is a genuine clarity issue rather than a single reader's misunderstanding.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification (Accept with Narrower Scope)

**Action:** Accept the clarity concern but limit the change to correcting the "In scope" section text without defining a specific post-birth duration.

- Update the "In scope" section to acknowledge that the IG includes data collection in the immediate post-birth period as already shown in section 5.1.1.
- Add a note that the precise boundary between this IG's scope and standard pediatric care documentation is implementation-dependent and may vary by jurisdiction.
- Reclassify the ticket type from "Technical Correction" to "Change Request" per the reporter's comment.

**Rationale:** This addresses the contradiction without committing to a specific neonatal time boundary, which could require additional clinical consensus. The related ticket FHIR-41853 already asks for the duration question specifically, so that detail can be addressed there.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive (Decline)

**Action:** Decline the request; the current text is considered sufficiently clear in context.

- The "In scope" section focuses on the primary audience (prenatal/fetal data modeling) and section 5.1.1 naturally extends this to the perinatal transition.
- Readers are expected to interpret the full document together rather than in isolation.
- The terminology and scope questions are partially addressed by the dispositions already applied to FHIR-41822 (Not Persuasive with Modification) and FHIR-42530 (Persuasive).

**Rationale:** Some of the overlapping tickets have already been applied, and modifications flowing from those dispositions may implicitly resolve the ambiguity raised here.

## Recommendation

**Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification** is recommended.

The contradiction between the "In scope" paragraph and section 5.1.1 is a genuine documentation defect that warrants correction. However, the closely related ticket FHIR-41853 specifically addresses the duration question ("how long is that period?"), so this ticket should focus narrowly on resolving the scope statement contradiction and deferring the duration boundary to FHIR-41853. Additionally, the ticket should be reclassified as a Change Request per the reporter's note. The applied resolutions on FHIR-41822 and FHIR-42530 should be reviewed to avoid duplicative changes, but they do not fully resolve the specific scope contradiction identified here.
