## Description Summary

This change request asks AU Patient Summary to adopt the IPS approach for representing "no known medications" using SNOMED CT code **787481004 |No known medications (situation)|**. The submitter recommends:

1. **AU Base:** Create a new NCTS value set combining the AMT value set + 787481004 |No known medications|, bound as preferred in AU Base MedicationStatement and MedicationRequest codeable concepts.
2. **AU Core:** Inherit AU Base terminology; add implementation-specific guidance to MedicationStatement (use negation code), Medication (not for "no known" use), and MedicationRequest (prefer MedicationStatement); add a "no known medications" example.
3. **AU PS:** No additional change required; inherits AU Core guidance and profiles.

The ticket also raises the question of the appropriate `MedicationStatement.status` value when recording no known medications, and notes that AU Base changes could be deferred to a subsequent release.

## Resolution History

This ticket has a complex resolution history:

1. **2025-10-31:** Originally resolved as **Persuasive with Modification** (vote: Heath Frankel / Kim Drever: 12-1-5). The agreed approach was to use the **Australian-specific** concept **1234391000168107 |No known current medicines|** in a MedicationStatement, with `SHOULD` for MedicationStatement use, `SHALL NOT` for MedicationRequest/Medication, and `SHOULD NOT` for Composition emptyReason.
2. **2026-02-20:** Motion to **revisit** passed (22-0-3) due to improved modelling of 787481004 |No known medications| and its reactivation in SNOMED CT-AU (Feb 2026 release). The previous resolution was reverted.
3. **2026-02-26:** A **new proposal** was posted recommending use of the **IPS preferred concept 787481004 |No known medications|** instead of the Australian-specific 1234391000168107 code. The rest of the originally agreed approach (MedicationStatement SHOULD, MedicationRequest/Medication SHALL NOT, etc.) would be retained.
4. **2026-03-05:** Proposal scheduled for joint discussion at the AU Core TDG Main Call.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive with Modification — Adopt IPS concept 787481004
**Resolution:** Persuasive with Modification
**Impact:** Non-compatible | Enhancement

Accept the revised proposal to use **787481004 |No known medications|** (now reactivated in SNOMED CT-AU) as the preferred concept for representing no known medications in AU Patient Summary. Retain the originally agreed structural approach:
- AU PS MedicationStatement **SHOULD** be used with `medicationCodeableConcept` = 787481004, `effectiveDateTime` = date/time current, `status` = active.
- AU PS MedicationRequest and AU PS Medication **SHALL NOT** be used for this purpose.
- AU PS Composition `section:sectionMedications.emptyReason` **SHOULD NOT** be used.
- Add note on differences when receiving international IPS that may use legacy codes.
- Coordinate with FHIR-53469 (AU Base binding) and FHIR-52286 (AU Core guidance).

**Rationale:** The reactivation of 787481004 in SNOMED CT-AU (Feb 2026) removes the original blocker. Using the IPS-preferred code aligns with international practice, avoids maintaining a local-only concept, and was endorsed by the AU Core TDG in strawman vote. The motion to revisit passed 22-0-3, indicating strong community support for reconsideration.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification — Retain Australian-specific concept 1234391000168107
**Resolution:** Persuasive with Modification
**Impact:** Non-compatible | Enhancement

Re-affirm the original 2025-10-31 resolution to use **1234391000168107 |No known current medicines|** as the preferred concept, retaining the same structural guidance (MedicationStatement SHOULD, MedicationRequest/Medication SHALL NOT, emptyReason SHOULD NOT). Add interoperability note for mapping to/from 787481004 when exchanging with international IPS systems.

**Rationale:** The Australian-specific concept is already established in local terminology and was agreed by the workgroup. The semantic difference ("no known current medicines" vs. "no known medications") may be clinically meaningful in some contexts. Avoids churn from changing a previously resolved decision.

### Disposition C: Defer — Await outcome of AU Core TDG joint discussion
**Resolution:** Deferred
**Impact:** None

Defer pending the outcome of the AU Core TDG joint discussion (scheduled 5 March 2026) to ensure AU Patient Summary, AU Core, and AU Base all align on the same approach before committing to a specific concept code.

**Rationale:** The ticket was reopened for reconsideration and a joint cross-IG discussion is planned. Deferring avoids making a decision that may conflict with the AU Core and AU Base resolutions.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Persuasive with Modification — Adopt IPS concept 787481004** is recommended.

The strongest signal from the community supports switching to the IPS-preferred concept. The motion to revisit passed with overwhelming support (22-0-3). The AU Core TDG endorsed the proposal in strawman vote. The key technical blocker — 787481004 being inactive in SNOMED CT-AU — has been resolved with the Feb 2026 release reactivation. Adopting the international concept aligns AU PS with IPS, reduces maintenance burden of a local-only code, and supports interoperability. The structural approach (MedicationStatement as carrier, SHALL NOT for MedicationRequest/Medication) is already agreed and would be retained. Coordination with FHIR-53469 (AU Base binding) and FHIR-52286 (AU Core guidance) should proceed in parallel.
