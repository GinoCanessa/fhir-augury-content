## Summary

This change request asks for **guidance material to be added to AU Base** describing what is appropriate modelling for domain-specific profiles that appear in AU Base. The submitter notes that domain-specific profiles may live in AU Base when there is no other suitable HL7 AU FHIR IG to host them, but unlike the general-purpose "localisation-only" profiles, these domain-specific profiles carry more specific use cases and tighter constraints (reference target types, terminology bindings/strength, etc.). The request is for formal policy and management content that distinguishes these two categories and sets expectations for their modelling.

A commenter (JIRAUSER25123) expanded the scope: the guidance must also address AU Base **non-domain (agnostic)** profiles — those that only define local concepts — so that both profile categories are clearly described. Brett Esler noted this ticket was triggered by related work.

## Details

### Problem

AU Base currently hosts two kinds of profiles without explicit written policy differentiating them:

1. **Agnostic / non-domain profiles** — General-purpose localisations of FHIR resources for the Australian context (e.g., adding Australian identifiers, local terminology bindings at a broad level).
2. **Domain-specific profiles** — Profiles constrained for particular clinical or business use cases (e.g., AU Base Diagnostic Report, AU Base Pathology Report) that carry tighter modelling (specific reference targets, stronger terminology bindings, narrower scope descriptions).

Without guidance, implementers and profile authors lack clarity on:
- When it is appropriate to create a domain-specific profile in AU Base vs. a separate IG.
- What level of constraint, use-case description, and terminology binding is expected.
- How domain-specific profiles relate to the broader, agnostic profiles.

### Scope of Change

The ticket asks for **new narrative/guidance content** in the AU Base IG (likely in the General Guidance or a new Policy section) covering:
- Criteria for hosting a domain-specific profile in AU Base.
- Expected modelling conventions for domain-specific profiles (use-case descriptions, reference target types, terminology binding strength).
- Corresponding description of the agnostic/non-domain profile approach.
- Policy on when profiles should instead be published in a separate HL7 AU FHIR IG.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept (Persuasive)

**Resolution:** Persuasive

Accept the ticket as submitted and draft guidance content for AU Base that covers both domain-specific and agnostic profiles. The new content should be added as a dedicated subsection (e.g., "Profile Categories and Modelling Policy") in the AU Base General Guidance page and should include:

1. **Definition of profile categories** — Clearly distinguish agnostic/non-domain profiles (local concept localisations) from domain-specific profiles (constrained for particular use cases).
2. **Criteria for hosting domain profiles in AU Base** — Specify that domain-specific profiles may be published in AU Base only when no other suitable HL7 AU FHIR IG exists to host them.
3. **Modelling expectations** — For domain-specific profiles: require a clearly stated purpose/scope, specific reference target types where appropriate, and stronger terminology bindings. For agnostic profiles: limit to broad Australian localisations without domain-specific constraints.
4. **Referencing policy** — Align with the policy established by FHIR-46999 regarding when constrained AU Base profiles may be referenced from other AU Base profiles.

This directly addresses the submitter's request and the commenter's expansion, and builds on policy groundwork already laid by the applied FHIR-46999.

### Disposition B: Accept with Modification (Persuasive with Modification)

**Resolution:** Persuasive with Modification

Accept the intent but modify the scope to first establish a formal governance framework before drafting specific guidance. Specifically:

1. Create a new "AU Base Governance" or "Publication Policy" page in AU Base (rather than embedding in General Guidance) to serve as the single authoritative location for all policy content — including this guidance, the FHIR-46999 reference policy, and future policy from tickets like FHIR-47166.
2. Within that page, include the profile category definitions and modelling expectations described in Disposition A.
3. Add a decision tree or checklist that profile authors can use to determine whether a new profile belongs in AU Base vs. a separate IG, and whether it should be agnostic or domain-specific.

This modification provides a more structured, maintainable home for the growing body of AU Base policy content.

### Disposition C: Decline (Not Persuasive)

**Resolution:** Not Persuasive

Decline on the basis that AU Base already addresses domain-specific profile policy implicitly through individual profile descriptions (e.g., FHIR-46934 clarified Diagnostic Report scope, FHIR-46999 added referencing commentary). Adding a separate overarching guidance section risks becoming stale or inconsistent with the actual profiles. The existing approach of addressing policy in-context through individual tickets and profile-level descriptions is sufficient, and the community can continue to handle these on a case-by-case basis.

---

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Accept (Persuasive)** is recommended.

The need for this guidance is well-evidenced by the cluster of related tickets (FHIR-46999, FHIR-46934, FHIR-47166) that all grapple with the lack of explicit policy on AU Base profile categorisation, referencing, and modelling conventions. The confusion that prompted these tickets demonstrates that the current implicit approach is inadequate. Adding clear, concise guidance distinguishing domain-specific from agnostic profiles — with explicit modelling expectations for each — would reduce ballot cycle friction, help profile authors make consistent design decisions, and improve implementer understanding. The scope is well-defined and builds on policy already applied via FHIR-46999, making it a natural next step rather than a large new initiative.
