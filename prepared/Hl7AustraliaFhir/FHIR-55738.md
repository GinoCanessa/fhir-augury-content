## Summary

The ticket requests the removal of constraint `au-core-org-01` from the AU Core Organization profile. This constraint currently restricts identifiers with type code NOI (National Organisation Identifier) to only HPI-O and PAI-O identifiers. However, the NOI code definition in AU Base's IdentifierType AU code system accommodates additional national organisation identifiers, including HSP-O (Healthcare Support Service Provider for Organisation) and HAE (Healthcare Administration Entity Organisation), which are being introduced via companion tickets FHIR-54923 and FHIR-54928. The constraint is therefore too restrictive and should be removed to allow AU Core Organization to accept the full range of national organisation identifiers that AU Base supports.

## Details

**Description:**

The definition of NOI in [IdentifierType AU - AU Base Implementation Guide v6.0.1-ci-build](https://build.fhir.org/ig/hl7au/au-fhir-base/CodeSystem-au-v2-0203.html) allows it to include other national identifiers, such as [HSP-O](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54923) and [HAE](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54928).

The constraint on AU Core Organization [au-core-org-01](https://build.fhir.org/ig/hl7au/au-fhir-core/StructureDefinition-au-core-organization.html#constraints-2) limits the use of NOI to HPI-O and PAI-O.

That is too strict. Remove the constraint.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested
#### Proposal
Remove the `au-core-org-01` constraint from the AU Core Organization profile (`StructureDefinition-au-core-organization`). This constraint currently enforces that when `Organization.identifier.type` is NOI, the identifier must be an HPI-O or PAI-O. Removing it allows AU Core Organization to accept any national organisation identifier that is valid under the AU Base IdentifierType AU code system, including the forthcoming HSP-O and HAE identifier types.
#### Justification
The constraint is inconsistent with the AU Base definition of NOI, which encompasses a broader set of national organisation identifiers beyond HPI-O and PAI-O. With FHIR-54923 (HSP-O) and FHIR-54928 (HAE) introducing new national identifier profiles in AU Base, the constraint would block valid national identifiers from being used in AU Core Organization. A pull request (hl7au/au-fhir-core#411) has already been raised implementing this change. Removing the constraint aligns AU Core with AU Base and ensures forward compatibility as new national identifiers are introduced.

---

### Disposition B: Broaden the Constraint Rather Than Remove It
#### Proposal
Instead of removing `au-core-org-01` entirely, update the constraint expression to explicitly include HSP-O and HAE alongside HPI-O and PAI-O. This would maintain a validation guard ensuring that NOI-typed identifiers on AU Core Organization are drawn from a known, curated set of national organisation identifier types.
#### Justification
Retaining a constraint (albeit broadened) preserves a data quality check that prevents arbitrary identifiers from being assigned the NOI type code. This approach would require updating the constraint each time a new national identifier type is introduced, but it provides stronger validation guarantees. It may be preferable if the workgroup wants to control exactly which national identifier types are permitted at the AU Core level, independent of what AU Base allows.

---

### Disposition C: Decline
#### Proposal
Decline the request. Keep the `au-core-org-01` constraint as-is, limiting NOI-typed identifiers to HPI-O and PAI-O on AU Core Organization.
#### Justification
The constraint could be seen as intentional — AU Core profiles are meant to represent a narrower, more opinionated conformance layer than AU Base. If HSP-O and HAE are not yet widely adopted in production systems, it may be premature to open the constraint. Systems that need these newer identifier types could use AU Base Organization directly. However, this position becomes increasingly difficult to defend as HSP-O and HAE gain adoption and AU Base profiles are created for them.

---

### Recommendation
**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A is the most appropriate course of action. The `au-core-org-01` constraint is clearly misaligned with the AU Base definition of the NOI identifier type code, which was designed to encompass all national organisation identifiers — not just HPI-O and PAI-O. The companion tickets (FHIR-54923 for HSP-O and FHIR-54928 for HAE) demonstrate that new national identifier types are actively being standardised, and the constraint would prevent their valid use in AU Core Organization profiles. Removing the constraint entirely (rather than broadening it per Disposition B) is the cleaner approach because it avoids creating a maintenance burden of updating the constraint each time a new national identifier type is introduced. The reporter has already implemented the change in PR #411, and the approach is straightforward with minimal risk of unintended side effects.
