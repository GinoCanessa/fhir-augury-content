## Problem Statement

The AU eRequesting IG references several AU Core profiles (e.g., AU Core Patient, Practitioner, PractitionerRole, Organization, Encounter), but does **not** inherit AU Core's obligations because the actors in eRequesting are different from those in AU Core. As a result, the meaning of *Must Support* on elements within these referenced AU Core profiles is undefined for eRequesting actors (AU eRequesting Placer, Filler, Server, Patient). This ambiguity creates implementation uncertainty — developers cannot determine what obligations they have for Must Support elements in AU Core profiles when operating within the eRequesting context.

The ticket proposes defining, in narrative form, the default meaning of Must Support for referenced AU Core profiles for each eRequesting actor. The long-term roadmap envisions progressing from narrative definitions to explicit obligation-only profiles.

This issue arose from the AU eRequesting co-chairs/facilitators meeting on 10 October 2025, in direct response to discussions on [FHIR-47008](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-47008) (Mandatory Patient Class for diagnostic requests).

## Analysis

This ticket identifies a genuine conformance gap: when the AU eRequesting IG references AU Core profiles but has different actors, the meaning of Must Support becomes undefined. This is not an academic concern — without clear obligation definitions, implementers must guess at their responsibilities, leading to inconsistent implementations.

**Significant subsequent progress has been made on this issue through later tickets:**

1. **FHIR-51636** (Applied/Persuasive) established an agreed proposal for default obligations on Must Support elements per actor, with a [Confluence page](https://confluence.hl7.org/spaces/HAFWG/pages/307304603/Proposal+for+Default+Obligations+on+Must+Support+Elements+and+Actor+Conformance+Requirements) detailing the approach.
2. **FHIR-51637** (Applied/Persuasive with Modification) extended this with default conformance for supported profiles and search parameters.
3. **FHIR-51874** (Applied/Persuasive) went beyond narrative — it creates AU eRequesting–specific profiles derived from AU Core and applies actor-specific obligations directly, which was identified as the long-term roadmap item in FHIR-48663.
4. **FHIR-52498** (Applied/Persuasive) refined the conformance language to correctly state that Must Support elements are implemented "in accordance with the specified obligations."

The work proposed in FHIR-48663 (narrative definitions of Must Support for referenced AU Core profiles, per actor) has been substantially addressed and even exceeded by these subsequent tickets, which moved from narrative to explicit obligation profiles.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Persuasive (Applied by Subsequent Work)
**Rationale:** The issue raised is valid and has been substantively addressed by the subsequent chain of tickets (FHIR-51636, FHIR-51637, FHIR-51874, FHIR-52498). The original proposal for narrative definitions has been superseded by the stronger approach of creating dedicated AU eRequesting profiles with explicit actor-specific obligations. Mark as persuasive, noting the resolution was achieved through the later work items.

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification
**Rationale:** Accept the ticket as valid but note that the resolution went beyond the original narrative-only proposal. The IG now includes both default obligation definitions (per FHIR-51636) and dedicated AU eRequesting profiles derived from AU Core (per FHIR-51874). The modification acknowledges that the solution was more comprehensive than originally proposed — explicit obligation profiles rather than narrative-only definitions.

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive (Already Resolved)
**Rationale:** While the concern was legitimate when raised, the work has since been completed through FHIR-51636, FHIR-51637, FHIR-51874, and FHIR-52498. Since the resolution was not directly applied under this ticket but through independent subsequent work, the ticket could be closed as the problem no longer exists. This risks undervaluing the original insight that catalysed the subsequent work.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Persuasive** is recommended. The issue identified a real conformance gap that was clearly articulated and well-motivated. The subsequent work (FHIR-51636, FHIR-51874, FHIR-52498) directly addressed and exceeded the proposal. Acknowledging the ticket as persuasive correctly credits the insight and documents the resolution path, while the "applied by subsequent work" framing accurately reflects how the changes were implemented.
