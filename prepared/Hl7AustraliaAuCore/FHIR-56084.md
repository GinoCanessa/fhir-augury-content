# Ticket Review: FHIR-56084

**Title:** Remove paragraphs regarding future releases
**Status:** Triaged **Priority:** Medium **Type:** Change Request
**Work Group:** HL7 Australia AU Core **Specification:** AU Patient Summary (FHIR)
**Reporter:** RicardoInacio **Assignee:** Unassigned
**Created:** 2026-03-16 **Updated:** 2026-03-30 **Labels:** None

---

## Summary

The reporter requests removal of informative paragraphs in the AU Patient Summary specification that describe potential methods for future releases, including Document Bundle Interactions, Document Bundle and DocumentReference Interactions (referencing IHE sIPS), and Encrypted File Exchange. The reporter argues that these forward-looking statements leave the specification in a "fluid state" since it is unclear whether the scope of supported methods will actually expand. The ticket was resolved as Not Persuasive, with the working group noting that the content is informative, does not introduce new requirements, and reflects approaches previously discussed and agreed upon by the working group.

## Details

**Description:**

The reporter suggests removing paragraphs that describe methods that may be supported in future releases of AU PS (or IPS). The specific content proposed for removal is:

> "In addition to the options described below, future releases of AU PS (or IPS) may describe alternative methods and recommendations such as:
> - Document Bundle Interactions - FHIR REST API interactions on Bundle resource type to create, update, search and read a patient summary document Bundle. A named query search approach could be used to provide more controlled and advanced search requirements.
> - Document Bundle and DocumentReference Interactions - Integrating the Healthcare Enterprise (IHE) has published guidance on the IHE Sharing of IPS (sIPS), which has been implemented in a number of jurisdictional implementations of IPS.
> - Encrypted File Exchange - in cases where patient summary document exchange is performed using insecure transport media such as email, USB or DVD."

The reporter believes that because it is not clear whether the scope of supported methods will increase, the content should be removed to avoid leaving the specification in a fluid state.

**Resolution Description (Not Persuasive):**

The working group resolved this as Not Persuasive, stating that the content is informative and does not introduce any new requirements or change the scope of the specification. It does not define supported methods or imply future commitments. The content reflects approaches discussed and agreed by the working group at the [AU Patient Summary Hot Topics Breakout 2025-12](https://confluence.hl7.org/spaces/HA/pages/413043304/AU+Patient+Summary+Hot+Topics+Breakout+2025-12) and the [2025-07-25 AU Patient Summary FHIR IG Agenda/Minutes](https://confluence.hl7.org/spaces/HAAUCOREWG/pages/358879233/2025-07-25+AU+Patient+Summary+FHIR+IG+Agenda+Minutes). Removing it would reduce context without improving clarity or conformance.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Remove the paragraphs describing potential future methods (Document Bundle Interactions, Document Bundle and DocumentReference Interactions, and Encrypted File Exchange) from the AU Patient Summary specification. This would eliminate all forward-looking statements about methods that may be supported in future releases.

#### Justification

The reporter raises a valid editorial concern: speculative content about future capabilities can create ambiguity about the current scope of the specification. Removing these paragraphs would make the specification more definitive about what is currently supported, avoiding any reader confusion about whether these methods are planned, committed, or merely aspirational. Specifications are generally stronger when they focus on current normative and informative content rather than speculating about future directions.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Retain the content but revise the language to make it clearly informative and non-committal. For example, rephrase "future releases of AU PS (or IPS) may describe alternative methods" to "other approaches that have been discussed in the community include" or "the following approaches are known to exist in other contexts." Remove the phrase "future releases" to avoid implying a roadmap commitment while preserving the informative context.

#### Justification

This approach addresses the reporter's concern about the specification appearing "fluid" while retaining the valuable context the working group intended to provide. The issue is not the content itself but the phrasing that implies future commitments. By reframing the language as purely informative context rather than a forward-looking statement, the specification remains stable and definitive while still guiding implementers about related approaches in the broader ecosystem.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request and retain the existing text as-is, consistent with the working group's existing resolution of Not Persuasive.

#### Justification

The existing content is explicitly informative and does not introduce normative requirements or change the specification's scope. It uses the word "may," which is appropriately non-committal. The content was deliberately included based on working group discussions (AU Patient Summary Hot Topics Breakout 2025-12 and the 2025-07-25 meeting minutes) and provides useful context for implementers about related approaches in the IPS ecosystem. Removing it would reduce the informative value of the specification without any corresponding improvement in clarity or conformance. The specification already clearly distinguishes between what is currently supported and what is mentioned for context.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** C (Decline)

The working group's existing resolution of Not Persuasive is well-supported. The paragraphs in question are clearly informative, use appropriately non-committal language ("may"), and do not introduce normative requirements or change the specification's scope. The content was included based on deliberate working group decisions documented in meeting minutes and breakout sessions. The reporter's concern about the specification being in a "fluid state" is understandable but does not hold when the content is read as informative context rather than a roadmap commitment. Removing the paragraphs would reduce valuable context for implementers without any corresponding benefit to specification clarity or conformance. The existing resolution is defensible and should be upheld.
