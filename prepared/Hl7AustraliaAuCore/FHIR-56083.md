## Summary

The submitter requests examples of computer-generated Patient Summary (PS) documents within the AU Patient Summary (FHIR) specification. The ticket asks for the specification to include or reference sample instances that demonstrate how a Patient Summary can be produced through automated or computer-driven processes, rather than relying solely on manually authored examples. The resolution has been marked as "Not Persuasive with Modification."

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add one or more example Patient Summary instances to the AU Patient Summary specification that demonstrate computer-generated output. These examples would illustrate how conformant PS documents can be assembled programmatically from source clinical data, complementing any existing manually-authored examples.

#### Justification

Providing computer-generated examples would give implementers concrete reference material for building automated PS generation pipelines. Examples are a low-cost addition to a specification and directly support adoption by showing what compliant output looks like in practice. This aligns with the general FHIR community practice of including diverse examples in implementation guides.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — Not Persuasive with Modification

#### Proposal

Decline to add full computer-generated example instances directly in the specification, but instead add guidance text or an informative section describing how implementers can approach automated Patient Summary generation. This could include references to existing tooling, transformation patterns, or links to external example repositories where computer-generated instances are maintained.

#### Justification

Maintaining example instances inside a specification carries an ongoing maintenance burden — each time profiles change, examples must be updated and revalidated. Providing guidance and external references achieves the submitter's goal of supporting computer generation while keeping the specification itself leaner and easier to maintain. This approach addresses the underlying need without committing the work group to perpetual example maintenance.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request on the basis that the AU Patient Summary specification already defines the profiles, constraints, and conformance expectations necessary for any implementer — whether generating Patient Summaries manually or programmatically — and that adding examples specific to computer generation does not materially improve the specification.

#### Justification

FHIR specifications are intentionally implementation-agnostic; the profiles and constraints apply equally regardless of how an instance is produced. Computer-generated and manually-authored instances that conform to the same profile are structurally identical, so distinguishing between them in examples provides limited additional value. Implementers building automated pipelines can validate their output against the published profiles using standard FHIR validation tooling.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B (Alternative Approach — Not Persuasive with Modification)

This aligns with the existing resolution of "Not Persuasive with Modification" already recorded on the ticket, suggesting the work group has previously leaned toward acknowledging the request's merit while finding the specific ask impractical as stated. Adding informative guidance on computer generation — without committing to maintaining in-specification example instances — strikes the right balance between supporting implementers and keeping the specification maintainable. It acknowledges the submitter's valid interest in automated PS generation while redirecting the effort toward a more sustainable deliverable. The work group can point implementers to validation tooling and community repositories for concrete examples, keeping the specification focused on normative content.
