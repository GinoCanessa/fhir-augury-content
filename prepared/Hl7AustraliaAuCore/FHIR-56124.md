## Details

The reporter identifies that **Section 3.3 (Localisation of the IPS)** on the [AU PS landing page](https://hl7.org.au/fhir/ps/1.0.0-ballot/the-aups.html) includes **Figure 3: The AU PS Passport** without any accompanying description or context. Specifically, the reporter notes there is no explanation of:

1. The concept of the AU PS "Passport"
2. How the Passport relates to AU PS or AU PS Actors (Producer/Consumer)

The reporter suggests two alternatives:
- **Option A:** Add context and descriptions in Section 3.3 to explain the relationship of the AU PS Passport to AU PS and AU PS Actors.
- **Option B:** Remove Figure 3 entirely.

### Resolution Description (from Jira)

The resolution is **Not Persuasive with Modification**. The committee found:
- It is **not persuasive** to remove Figure 3 or add additional contextual description — the figure is supported by surrounding text and represents the relationship between IPS and AU PS.
- The **modification** is to update the figure caption to: *"Figure 3: The AU PS - a valid AU PS document is a valid IPS document."*
- The image size will also be increased for readability.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept the Resolution (Not Persuasive with Modification)

**Action:** Agree with the proposed resolution — retain Figure 3 but update its caption to *"Figure 3: The AU PS - a valid AU PS document is a valid IPS document"* and increase the image size for readability.

**Rationale:** The figure illustrates a core architectural principle (AU PS as a valid IPS document) and is supported by surrounding text in Section 3.3. Renaming the caption from "The AU PS Passport" to something more descriptive addresses the reporter's concern about the unclear "Passport" concept without requiring additional prose. Increasing the image size improves accessibility. This is a proportionate response that addresses the readability concern while preserving useful visual content.

### Disposition B: Accept with Additional Context (Persuasive with Modification)

**Action:** Retain Figure 3 with the updated caption as proposed, but also add a brief explanatory sentence in Section 3.3 (e.g., *"Figure 3 illustrates that any conformant AU PS document is inherently a valid IPS document, enabling international interoperability."*).

**Rationale:** While the caption update helps, the reporter raised a valid point that the "Passport" concept is introduced without explanation. A single clarifying sentence would provide context for implementers who may not be familiar with the IPS relationship, without over-documenting. This aligns with the precedent set by FHIR-52283 (Applied/Persuasive) where similar requests for figure context were accepted. The additional effort is minimal and improves the specification's clarity.

### Disposition C: Decline (Not Persuasive)

**Action:** Reject the change request entirely — retain Figure 3 with its original caption and size unchanged.

**Rationale:** The surrounding text in Section 3.3 already provides sufficient context for the figure. The "Passport" concept is a visual metaphor that does not require formal definition. Adding descriptions or modifying captions risks over-documenting a simple diagram. However, this disposition ignores the legitimate readability concern about image size and the confusing "Passport" label.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Accept the Resolution (Not Persuasive with Modification)** is recommended.

The proposed resolution strikes the right balance: the updated caption (*"The AU PS - a valid AU PS document is a valid IPS document"*) directly addresses the core confusion by replacing the undefined "Passport" term with a clear, descriptive statement about IPS conformance. The image size increase addresses the readability concern. The surrounding text in Section 3.3 provides adequate context for the figure's purpose, making additional prose unnecessary. This resolution aligns with the workgroup's established approach of making targeted, proportionate changes to the IG documentation.
