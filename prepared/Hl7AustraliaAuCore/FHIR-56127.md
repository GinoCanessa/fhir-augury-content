## Summary

The reporter identifies an inaccuracy in Section 5.2.1 (Referral to Specialist and Allied Health Use Case), specifically in Figure 1, Step 5. The diagram states that the "endocrinologist also updates a patient summary" for Joyce to include the increase to her insulin. The reporter argues this is conceptually incorrect because a patient summary (in the context of IPS and AU PS) is a **point-in-time clinical summary** and, per the FHIR Clinical Document IG (Section 1.2), a clinical document must persist in an **unaltered state**. Therefore, a patient summary should not be "updated" — rather, a new patient summary should be generated and uploaded when new clinical data arises. The reporter requests removing the "updates a patient summary" statement from Figure 1.

## Details

### Reporter's Argument
- AU PS Composition (Section 7.1.5.1.1) states the patient summary document "may be constructed by a human or an automated process."
- The FHIR Clinical Document IG (Section 1.2) defines the persistence characteristic: "A clinical document continues to exist in an **unaltered state**."
- Reference: [FHIR Clinical Document IG](https://hl7.org/fhir/uv/fhir-clinical-document/en/index.html)
- A patient summary is a point-in-time artifact — it should not be mutated after creation.
- The suggested change is to remove the "updates a patient summary" wording from Figure 1 under Section 5.2.1.

### Resolution Description (Not Persuasive with Modification)
The resolution states:
1. **Not persuasive** in that editing or lifecycle management of patient summary documents is **out of scope** for AU PS R1.
2. The Referral to Specialist and Allied Health Use Case page **will be updated** to clarify that the focus is on **Step 4 (Endocrinologist consultation)** — e.g., by moving the use case statement above the diagram.
3. The [AU PS consumer journeys](https://sparked.csiro.au/index.php/products-resources/au-ps-consumer-journeys/), including this diagram, were developed by the **Sparked AU Patient Summary Clinical Focus Group (AU PS CFG)**. Informative feedback will be provided to the CFG regarding:
   - The wording used in Step 5
   - The broader question of whether patient summaries are "updated" or "newly generated"

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept as Not Persuasive with Modification (Agree with Current Resolution)

**Action:** Accept the current resolution. Clarify that the use case page focuses on Step 4 (Endocrinologist consultation) by repositioning the use case statement above the diagram. Provide informative feedback to the AU PS CFG regarding the "updates a patient summary" wording in Step 5.

**Rationale:** The reporter raises a valid conceptual concern about clinical document persistence semantics, but the diagram is sourced from the Sparked AU PS Clinical Focus Group and is informative content. The scope of AU PS R1 does not extend to lifecycle management of patient summary documents. The proposed modification appropriately shifts focus away from Step 5 while deferring the diagram wording change to the CFG. This balances conceptual accuracy with scope management.

### Disposition B: Accept as Persuasive (Modify the Diagram)

**Action:** Directly update Figure 1 under Section 5.2.1 to change the Step 5 wording from "endocrinologist also updates a patient summary" to language such as "a new patient summary is generated" or remove the patient summary reference from Step 5 entirely.

**Rationale:** The reporter's argument is technically correct — a patient summary is a point-in-time document that should not be "updated" per FHIR Clinical Document persistence requirements. Leaving misleading language in the IG, even in informative content, risks setting incorrect implementation expectations. FHIR-52396 already identified the same concern about AU PS being misinterpreted as an updatable portable EHR. Correcting the wording now would reinforce the correct document lifecycle model and prevent confusion for implementers.

### Disposition C: Decline as Not Persuasive

**Action:** Close with no change. The use case diagram is informative content from the Sparked CFG and does not define normative behavior. The term "updates" in a use case narrative is understood colloquially and does not imply mutation of a persisted clinical document.

**Rationale:** The use case section describes a clinical workflow, not a technical specification. In clinical workflows, practitioners commonly speak of "updating" patient information without intending to imply mutation of a specific FHIR document instance. The diagram is externally sourced informative content and changing it could create misalignment with the CFG's published consumer journeys. AU PS R1 should focus on normative content rather than refining colloquial language in informative use case diagrams.

---

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Accept as Not Persuasive with Modification** is recommended. The current resolution strikes the right balance: it acknowledges the valid concern about document persistence semantics without expanding AU PS R1 scope into lifecycle management. Repositioning the use case focus to Step 4 reduces the prominence of the problematic wording, while feeding the concern back to the CFG ensures it will be considered for future revisions of the consumer journey diagrams. This approach is consistent with how FHIR-52396 was handled (also Not Persuasive with Modification on a related concern about AU PS update semantics).
