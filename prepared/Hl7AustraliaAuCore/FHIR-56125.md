## Details

The submitter observes that in the AU Patient Summary (AU PS) IG 1.0.0-ballot, Section 4.6.1 (Profile comparison) states **"No equivalent profile for comparison"** for AU PS Composition against AU Core 2.0.0. While technically correct (there is no AU Core Composition profile), this statement can confuse readers because:

1. **Section 4.1.1.4.2 (Profiling approach)** clearly documents that AU PS Composition is derived from **AU Base Composition**.
2. **Section 7.1.5.1.2** shows `Composition 0..* AUBaseComposition`, confirming the derivation relationship.
3. Other AU PS profiles (e.g., AU PS Immunization, AU PS Medication) include compliance assertions against AU Core 2.0.2 profiles, making the Composition entry look like an outlier.

The submitter requests that **qualifying notes be added** to the comparison table to clarify that AU PS Composition is derived from / based on the AUBaseComposition profile, even though there is no equivalent AU Core profile.

**Proposed resolution (Not Persuasive):** The work group resolved that the Comparison with Other National and International IGs page is intended for profile compatibility comparison, not derivation documentation. The statement that AU PS Composition has no equivalent AU Core profile is correct, and adding derivation information would be inconsistent with the scope of that table.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept (Not Persuasive)

**Recommendation: Accept the work group's "Not Persuasive" resolution as-is.**

**Rationale:** The comparison table in Section 4.6.1 is specifically scoped to profile compatibility assessment between AU PS and AU Core profiles. The statement "No equivalent profile for comparison" is factually correct — there is no AU Core Composition profile. The derivation relationship (AU PS Composition → AU Base Composition) is already documented in Sections 4.1.1.4.2 and 7.1.5.1.2, so the information is available to readers who look for it. Adding derivation details to the comparison table would conflate two different concerns (compatibility vs. derivation) and set a precedent requiring similar annotations for every profile entry. The resolution is well-reasoned and maintains the clear scope of the comparison page.

### Disposition B: Alternative — Accept with Friendly Amendment

**Recommendation: Accept with a minor editorial addition — add a general introductory note to the comparison table clarifying its scope.**

**Rationale:** While the resolution logic is sound, the reader confusion is legitimate given that other sections of the IG describe the derivation relationship. Rather than adding per-profile derivation annotations (which would bloat the table), a brief introductory paragraph could be added to Section 4.6.1 stating something like: *"This table compares AU PS profiles against their AU Core equivalents for compatibility purposes. Where a profile has no equivalent AU Core profile, it does not imply the absence of a derivation relationship — see Section 4.1.1.4.2 for profiling approach details."* This addresses the confusion without changing the table structure and is consistent with the accepted resolution of FHIR-56128 (which added explanatory guidance text for similar reader confusion).

### Disposition C: Decline (Persuasive)

**Recommendation: Decline the "Not Persuasive" resolution and mark as Persuasive — add a qualifying note to the comparison table entry for AU PS Composition.**

**Rationale:** The submitter's request is narrow and reasonable: add a brief note (e.g., "Derived from AU Base Composition; no AU Core equivalent exists") to the specific Composition row. This does not fundamentally change the table's purpose but prevents a common misreading. Given that this is a "Highest" priority item and the same reporter has filed multiple related tickets about reader confusion in this area (FHIR-56128, FHIR-52333), there is a pattern suggesting the IG's current presentation genuinely causes confusion. A targeted annotation would improve clarity at minimal cost.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Accept (Not Persuasive)**

The work group's resolution is well-reasoned. The comparison table has a clearly defined scope (profile compatibility, not derivation), and the statement is factually correct. The derivation information is already available elsewhere in the IG (Sections 4.1.1.4.2 and 7.1.5.1.2). Adding derivation details would be scope creep for this table and would set a precedent requiring similar annotations across all profile entries. The related ticket FHIR-56128 (resolved Persuasive) already addresses the broader reader confusion issue by adding explanatory guidance text, which indirectly helps readers understand profile relationships including derivation. Accepting the resolution maintains consistency and clear separation of concerns within the IG.
