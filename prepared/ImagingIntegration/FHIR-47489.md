## Summary

Ana Kostadinovska has submitted a change request for the DICOM SR to FHIR Resource Mapping IG, suggesting that the word "resource" be appended after each FHIR resource name in subsection headings throughout the IG. The rationale is that doing so would disambiguate references to FHIR concepts from DICOM concepts, improving clarity for readers who work across both standards.

## Details

**Description:**

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update all subsection headings in the DICOM SR to FHIR Resource Mapping IG so that each FHIR resource name is followed by the word "Resource" (e.g., change "Patient" to "Patient Resource", "Observation" to "Observation Resource", etc.). Apply this consistently throughout the IG wherever a subsection is named after a FHIR resource type.

#### Justification

The DICOM SR to FHIR Resource Mapping IG inherently deals with two overlapping terminologies — DICOM SR concepts and FHIR resources. Readers familiar with one domain but not the other may be confused when a subsection heading like "Patient" or "Observation" does not clearly indicate whether it refers to a DICOM construct or a FHIR resource. Appending "Resource" is a low-effort, high-clarity editorial improvement that reduces ambiguity and improves the readability of the IG, particularly for implementers who are new to one of the two standards.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than appending "Resource" to every FHIR resource name in subsection headings, adopt a consistent prefix convention such as "FHIR:" (e.g., "FHIR: Patient", "FHIR: Observation") and "DICOM:" for DICOM concepts. Alternatively, add a brief introductory note at the beginning of each mapping section that clarifies the naming convention, stating that bare resource names refer to FHIR resources unless otherwise indicated.

#### Justification

While the reporter's concern about ambiguity is valid, appending "Resource" to every heading may become verbose and may not fully resolve the ambiguity in contexts where DICOM structures share similar names. A prefix-based convention or an explicit naming note would provide clearer disambiguation while keeping headings concise. This approach also scales better if the IG later introduces additional DICOM-side section headings.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change request on the grounds that the current naming convention is sufficiently clear in context and that the IG's structure already distinguishes FHIR resources from DICOM concepts through the mapping tables and surrounding narrative text.

#### Justification

The IG is structured around mapping relationships, and each subsection's context — including its position within the document hierarchy, the mapping tables it contains, and the descriptive text — makes it clear whether a heading refers to a FHIR resource or a DICOM concept. Adding "Resource" to every heading introduces editorial overhead for a marginal clarity gain and could set a precedent for similar verbose naming across other IGs. Experienced readers of FHIR IGs are accustomed to resource names appearing as bare nouns in section headings.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter raises a legitimate and practical concern: in a document that inherently bridges two terminologies (DICOM SR and FHIR), bare resource names in subsection headings can create unnecessary ambiguity. Appending "Resource" after each FHIR resource name is a minimal, non-breaking editorial change that immediately clarifies the intent of each subsection for all readers, regardless of their background. The change is consistent with good technical writing practice — being explicit when two domains overlap — and carries no risk of altering the technical content or structure of the IG. Given the low effort required and the clear improvement to readability, accepting the request as stated is the most straightforward path forward.
