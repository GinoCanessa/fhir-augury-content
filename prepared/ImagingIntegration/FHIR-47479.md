## Summary

The reporter identifies that the DICOM SR to FHIR Resource Mapping IG does not provide a way to capture the `$ModType` and `$Derivation` concept modifier values from DICOM TID 300 (Measurement). TID 300 defines four concept modifiers—`$ModType`, `$Method`, `$Derivation`, and `$TargetSite`—that allow post-coordination of a measurement concept name. While `$Method` and `$TargetSite` have existing mappings, the IG currently lacks corresponding extensions or mappings for `$ModType` and `$Derivation`. This results in loss of clinically relevant information needed for full post-coordination of measurement concepts when converting DICOM SR content to FHIR Observations.

The reporter requests that the IG provide standard Observation extensions to capture these values.

## Details

DICOM TID 300 is a widely-used template for structured measurements in DICOM Structured Reports. The four concept modifiers (`$ModType`, `$Method`, `$Derivation`, `$TargetSite`) are critical for precisely qualifying what a measurement represents. For example, `$Derivation` can specify whether a measurement is a mean, maximum, minimum, etc., and `$ModType` can further qualify the measurement type.

Without a standard mapping for these modifiers, implementers either lose the information during conversion or resort to ad-hoc extensions, leading to interoperability problems. The closely related ticket [FHIR-48643](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48643) (filed by Jonathan Whitby on behalf of Harald Zachmann) raises the broader question of how to map concept modifiers from DICOM SR to FHIR Observations and suggests using the `Observation.component` array for this purpose.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept — Define Extensions for `$ModType` and `$Derivation` on Observation

**Resolution:** Persuasive

Accept the change request and define new Observation extensions in the DICOM SR to FHIR Resource Mapping IG for `$ModType` and `$Derivation`. These extensions would be added to the Imaging Measurement Observation profile (`StructureDefinition-imaging-measurement`) to enable lossless round-tripping of TID 300 concept modifiers.

**Rationale:** The gap is clearly documented—`$Method` and `$TargetSite` have mappings but `$ModType` and `$Derivation` do not. Without these, post-coordination information is lost, reducing the clinical utility of the converted FHIR resource. This aligns with the IG's goal of faithful DICOM SR to FHIR mapping.

### Disposition B: Alternative — Use `Observation.component` Instead of Extensions

**Resolution:** Persuasive with Modification

Rather than defining new extensions, map `$ModType` and `$Derivation` values as additional entries in the `Observation.component` array, using appropriate DICOM-defined coded concepts as the `component.code`. This approach is consistent with the suggestion in [FHIR-48643](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48643) and addresses concept modifiers more broadly without requiring custom extensions.

**Rationale:** Using `Observation.component` leverages an existing FHIR pattern for qualifying measurements, avoids proliferating extensions, and provides a consistent mechanism for all TID 300 concept modifiers. This should be coordinated with the resolution of FHIR-48643 to ensure a unified approach.

### Disposition C: Decline — Defer Until Broader Concept Modifier Strategy Is Resolved

**Resolution:** Not Persuasive

Decline this specific request, noting that FHIR-48643 addresses the broader question of concept modifier mapping. The workgroup should first establish a general strategy for all concept modifiers (not just `$ModType` and `$Derivation`) before making piecemeal additions to the IG.

**Rationale:** Acting on this ticket in isolation risks creating an inconsistent mapping approach. However, this leaves a known gap in the IG and risks continued ad-hoc implementations.

---

## Recommendation

**Disposition B (Persuasive with Modification)** is recommended. The `Observation.component` approach provides a general-purpose mechanism for mapping all TID 300 concept modifiers—not just `$ModType` and `$Derivation`—and aligns with the proposal in the closely related FHIR-48643. Resolving both tickets together with a unified `component`-based mapping strategy avoids extension proliferation and gives implementers a single, consistent pattern. The workgroup should coordinate this disposition with FHIR-48643 to ensure alignment.
