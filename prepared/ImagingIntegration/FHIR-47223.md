## Details

The reporter identifies that the Example section of the DICOM SR to FHIR Resource Mapping IG conflates normative mapping guidance with illustrative examples. Specifically, the section contains both mapping concepts (which should be normative/prescriptive content) and specific DICOM/FHIR resource instances (which are purely illustrative). The reporter suggests restructuring this section so that the mapping concepts are presented as normative guidelines, separate from the concrete examples.

This ticket is part of a cluster of related ballot feedback (all labeled `II_SEP_2024_SR_Example`) from the same reporter targeting the Example section of the SR Mapping IG. Closely related issues include:
- **FHIR-47673** — Identifies misalignment between the Mapping section and Example section (e.g., the Mapping section states Patient/ImagingStudy/ServiceRequest resources must already exist, while the Example section provides guidance on handling missing resources).
- **FHIR-47487** — Unclear column semantics in example subsection tables.
- **FHIR-47510** — Section naming inconsistency within the examples.
- **FHIR-47525** — Only DICOM representations shown; FHIR representations missing from examples.
- **FHIR-47655** — Suggestion to better link and align Section 4 (Mapping) with per-profile mappings.

The core concern is structural: normative mapping logic embedded in an "Example" section may be overlooked by implementers who skip examples, or may create confusion about what is required vs. illustrative.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept — Restructure Example Section to Separate Normative Guidance from Examples
**Resolution:** Persuasive

Accept the request and restructure the Example section of the DICOM SR to FHIR Resource Mapping IG. Extract normative mapping concepts and guidance currently embedded in the Example section into the Mapping section (or a new dedicated "Mapping Guidelines" subsection). Retain only concrete DICOM/FHIR resource instances in the Example section. This restructuring should be coordinated with related tickets FHIR-47673 (alignment between Mapping and Example sections) and FHIR-47655 (linking Section 4 with per-profile mappings) to avoid duplicated or conflicting rework.

### Disposition B: Partial Accept — Add Normative Labels Within Current Structure
**Resolution:** Persuasive with Modification

Acknowledge the concern but rather than a full restructuring, add clear labeling within the existing Example section to distinguish normative mapping guidance from illustrative examples. This could take the form of "Normative Requirement" callout boxes or a "Mapping Rules" preamble at the beginning of each example subsection, while keeping the overall section structure unchanged. This approach reduces the scope of editorial rework while addressing the implementer confusion concern.

### Disposition C: Decline — Current Structure Is Adequate
**Resolution:** Not Persuasive

The current structure, while mixing concepts and examples, is intentional — embedding mapping guidance alongside concrete examples aids reader comprehension by providing context immediately where it is needed. Separating them would require readers to cross-reference between sections. The IG is not yet normative, so the distinction between normative content and examples is less critical at this stage.

## Recommendation

**Disposition A: Accept** is recommended. The reporter raises a legitimate structural concern that affects implementer clarity — normative mapping rules buried in an "Example" section risk being missed and create ambiguity about what constitutes required behavior vs. illustration. Furthermore, this ticket is part of a broader pattern of ballot feedback (FHIR-47673, FHIR-47487, FHIR-47655) all pointing to structural and alignment issues in the same area, suggesting a coordinated restructuring would address multiple concerns simultaneously. The work group should plan this restructuring in conjunction with those related tickets to maximize editorial efficiency.
