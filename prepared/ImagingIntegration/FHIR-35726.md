## Summary

The American Association of Physicists in Medicine (AAPM) submitted a formal letter expressing support for the Radiation Dose Summary for Diagnostic Procedures on FHIR specification. The AAPM endorsed the development of an HL7 FHIR mechanism to distribute radiation dose information for diagnostic and interventional imaging procedures involving ionizing radiation, stating that appropriate implementation could improve patient care by automating and expanding important processes—particularly around high-dose interventional procedures where tissue damage thresholds may be approached. The letter references NCRP Report 168 for additional context on relevant procedures and protocols. The ticket was resolved as "Considered - No action required," and a comment from mckenzie notes that the ticket had been incorrectly marked as a duplicate, and when the erroneous link was removed the status failed to update properly.

To Whom It May Concern:

The American Association of Physicists in Medicine (AAPM) appreciates the opportunity to submit comments on the proposed new mechanism, Radiation Dose Summary for Diagnostic Procedures on FHIR.

The AAPM would like to express its support, in principle, for developing an HL7 FHIR mechanism to distribute appropriate radiation dose information for diagnostic and interventional imaging procedures that use ionizing radiation, to promote its appropriate usage beyond the imaging department. The AAPM believes that the appropriate implementation and use of such a mechanism could improve patient care, helping automate and expand important processes, especially prior to and after the performance of potentially high-dose interventional procedures. Although rare, some of these procedures may result in patient doses that approach or surpass thresholds at which certain tissue damage, due to deterministic effects, can take place. The introduction of the proposed mechanism could help further minimize these events and/or improve patient care if these thresholds are indeed surpassed. More information on the processes involved and procedures to be followed related to these high-dose interventional procedures can be found in Report 168 of the National Council on Radiation Protection and Measurements.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested
#### Proposal
Acknowledge the AAPM's letter of support for the Radiation Dose Summary IG and formally record the endorsement. No specification changes are required since the letter provides supportive feedback rather than requesting modifications.
#### Justification
The AAPM's letter is a statement of support, not a change request. The appropriate response is to acknowledge the feedback and note the professional endorsement. The ticket's existing resolution of "Considered - No action required" already captures this intent. Accepting the comment validates the AAPM's participation in the ballot process.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach
#### Proposal
Close the ticket as "Considered - No action required" and update the ticket status from "Triaged" to a resolved/closed state, correcting the status inconsistency noted by mckenzie. Additionally, consider referencing the AAPM endorsement in the IG's background or introductory material to acknowledge professional society support.
#### Justification
This approach addresses the administrative status inconsistency that mckenzie identified (the ticket shows "Triaged" but has a resolution of "Considered - No action required"). It also goes slightly further by suggesting the AAPM's endorsement be acknowledged in the IG documentation, which could strengthen the IG's credibility and demonstrate community buy-in from a relevant professional organization.

---

### Disposition C: Decline
#### Proposal
Decline the comment as not actionable. The letter provides general support but does not identify specific technical issues, propose changes, or request modifications to the specification.
#### Justification
While the AAPM's support is appreciated, ballot comments are most useful when they identify specific issues or propose concrete changes. A general letter of support does not require workgroup action beyond acknowledgment. The ticket can be closed without further consideration.

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is recommended because it addresses two concerns simultaneously. First, it properly resolves the ticket by correcting the status inconsistency that mckenzie identified—the ticket currently shows "Triaged" status despite having a resolution of "Considered - No action required," which indicates an administrative error when a duplicate link was removed. Second, it acknowledges the substantive value of the AAPM's endorsement. While the letter does not request specification changes, having formal support from a major professional society like the AAPM is meaningful for the IG's credibility. Noting this endorsement in the IG materials would be a low-effort enhancement that demonstrates community validation. The primary action, however, is simply to fix the status and close the ticket properly.
