## Resolution Summary

Resolution: **Persuasive with Modification**. The ticket reports that `Device.property` (revised after R4) reads as if it is for "actual configuration settings" and "properties of the device actually in use", which overlaps and conflicts with `DeviceMetric` (which models settings capabilities) and setting-type Observations (which carry the point-in-time value of a setting). The approved resolution (proposed in detail by Elliot Silver in the ticket's final comment, accepted by the PoCD workgroup on 2022-11-21 with editorial discretion) re-draws the boundary as follows:

- `Device.property` and `DeviceDefinition.property` capture **inherent, essentially fixed** characteristics (e.g., size, material, labeling, regulation status, intended use).
- `DeviceMetric` describes **dynamic** properties — readings and settings of, or from, the device — and is the model for "settings capability".
- `Observation` (unchanged by this ticket) carries the point-in-time value of a `DeviceMetric`.
- The narrative pages for `DeviceMetric` and `Device` (Scope and Usage, Boundaries and Relationships) must be edited to state this contrast explicitly, and the detailed `property` element descriptions in Device and DeviceDefinition must be rewritten to match.

Observation content is explicitly **out of scope** per the reporter's closing note (a separate ticket will refine Observation).

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

Since R5, `Device.property` was expanded with the sentence "The focus is on properties of the device actually in use while DeviceDefinition focuses on properties that are available to be used." That language plus the examples (`actual configuration settings`, `time or timing attributes`) makes `Device.property` read like a container for current settings — which is the documented role of `DeviceMetric` (plus `Observation` for the point-in-time value). Implementers are therefore unable to tell where a runtime setting should live: on `Device.property`, on a `DeviceMetric` tied to the device, or on an Observation referring to that metric. The ticket asks the workgroup to re-draw and publish the boundary, and in particular to clarify that `Device.property` / `DeviceDefinition.property` are for static/inherent characteristics and that `DeviceMetric` + `Observation` are the correct pattern for dynamic settings.

### Proposed Change

Apply the wording in Elliot Silver's proposed-changes comment on the ticket:

**DeviceMetric — Scope and Usage** (`source/devicemetric/devicemetric-introduction.xml`, `<a name="scope"/>` block):

> "The DeviceMetric resource describes **dynamic** properties that characterize a direct or derived, quantitative or qualitative biosignal measurement, setting, or calculation produced by a medical device; i.e., *readings* and *settings* of, or from, the device. The DeviceMetric resource can also be used to describe properties relevant to the metric such as status, last calibration time and type, measurement mode, color, reference link to the parent DeviceComponent to where it belongs, and any capabilities that the metric offers (for example: setting the metric label)."

**DeviceMetric — Boundaries and Relationships** (same file, `<a name="bnr"/>` block): keep the two-bullet list:

- **Device** — The physical, virtual, or software device that this DeviceMetric is for. Note that `Device.property` is used to record static characteristics or properties of the **Device**.
- **Observation** — Used to record the particular value of the DeviceMetric at a point in time.

**Device — Boundaries and Relationships** (`source/device/device-introduction.xml`, `<a name="bnr"></a>` block):

- In the `DeviceDefinition` bullet, keep the wording "Describes a 'kind' of device — not a **specific** instance **of the device**" and "any information that applies to **all instances** of a device".
- In the `DeviceMetric` bullet, add the sentence: "The DeviceMetric models the properties of the Observations generated by and/or about the device, such as whether or not the Observation is a setting. A DeviceMetric describes aspects of the device that may change over time. In contrast, `Device.property` is used to capture inherent, essentially fixed, characteristics of the device, such as a 'large' blood pressure cuff, the bore size of an MRI, the color of a lead. (Such static characteristics may also be recorded in DeviceDefinition.)"

**Device — Detailed Descriptions** (`source/device/structuredefinition-Device.xml` differential):

- `Device.property` — definition, short, and comment rewritten per the resolution.
- `Device.property.type` — definition: "Code that specifies the property, **such as resolution, color, or intended use,** being represented." (Remove the "No codes are specified but the MDC codes are an example" sentence.)
- `Device.property.value[x]` — definition: "The value of the property specified by the associated `property.type` code." Short: "Value of the property". Comment: "The `text` element of the `valueCodeableConcept` choice type is used for properties which would usually be coded (such as the size of an implant, or a security classification) but for which there is not currently an appropriate concept in the vocabulary (e.g. a custom implant size, or a security classification which 'depends on configuration'). Otherwise the `valueString` choice type is used for textual properties, such as intended use, warning text, or assembly instructions."

**DeviceDefinition — Detailed Descriptions** (`source/devicedefinition/structuredefinition-DeviceDefinition.xml` differential):

- `DeviceDefinition.property` — definition: "Static or essentially fixed characteristics or features of this kind of device that are otherwise not captured in more specific attributes, e.g., time or timing attributes, resolution, accuracy, **intended use or instructions for use,** and physical attributes."
- `DeviceDefinition.property` — short: "Inherent, essentially fixed, characteristics of this kind of device, e.g., **regulation status,** time properties, size, **material, labeling,** etc."
- `DeviceDefinition.property` — comment mirrors the `Device.property` comment (dynamic goes on DeviceMetric+Observation; static may also sit on an associated Device instance).
- `DeviceDefinition.property.type` — definition: "Code that specifies the property**, such as resolution, color, or intended use,** being represented."
- `DeviceDefinition.property.type` — terminology binding **Device Property Type** (Example) — keep current binding (already points to `http://hl7.org/fhir/ValueSet/device-property-type`, example strength).
- `DeviceDefinition.property.value[x]` — definition: "The value of the property specified by the associated `property.type` code." Short: "Value of the property". Comment mirrors the `Device.property.value[x]` comment exactly.

No changes to `Observation` in this ticket.

### Design Rationale

Three coupled resources describe overlapping aspects of a device: `Device` (a physical/virtual instance), `DeviceDefinition` (a "kind" of device), and `DeviceMetric` (a measurement/setting capability). The resolution takes the existing three-resource split and tightens the semantics:

- "Kind" vs. "instance" static attributes ⇒ `DeviceDefinition.property` / `Device.property` (same shape, different subject).
- Dynamic attributes (including settings) ⇒ `DeviceMetric` describes the capability, `Observation` records the value at a time.

This is consistent with the existing R5/R6 design and the PoCD minutes captured in the ticket (2022-11-21): "It was agreed that the intention in DeviceMetric was to present current, potentially changing, state. Also the Device properties field should talk about static character of Device.property." It preserves the `property` backbone shape (so no schema-breaking change to instances) and refines only the descriptive content plus the two narrative pages. Alternative: re-typing `Device.property.value[x]` or making static/dynamic an explicit discriminator — not chosen because the reporter specifically argues the fix is a documentation/boundary clarification, not a structural one, and because changing the backbone would be a breaking change for implementers already writing `Device.property`.
