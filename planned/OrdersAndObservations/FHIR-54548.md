## Resolution Summary

Resolution: **Persuasive with Modification**.

Formal resolution description on the ticket:

> We will remove this sentence: "Note that the conclusion and the coded
> diagnoses are part of the atomic data and SHOULD be duplicated in the
> narrative and in the presented form if the latter is present."

The reporter's underlying complaint is that DomainResource.text requires the
narrative to be "clinically safe", which is hard to reconcile with merely
*SHOULD* (RFC 2119 conformance) duplicating the conclusion and coded
diagnoses in the narrative. The reporter offered two acceptable fixes:
either upgrade to **SHALL**, or downgrade to a non-conformance **should**.

The applied PR ([HL7/fhir#4022](https://github.com/HL7/fhir/pull/4022))
implements the **lowercase** option (downgrade `SHOULD` → `should`) rather
than literally removing the sentence. The plan below mirrors PR #4022,
because that is what was actually merged and tagged `Ready_to_Apply`.

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

The "Notes" page for the DiagnosticReport resource
(`source/diagnosticreport/diagnosticreport-notes.xml`) contains the
sentence:

> Note that the conclusion and the coded diagnoses are part of the atomic
> data and **SHOULD** be duplicated in the narrative and in the presented
> form if the latter is present.

`SHOULD` here is normative (RFC 2119) and therefore relaxes the
requirement that `DomainResource.text` be a "clinically safe view" of the
resource. As the reporter observes, it is hard to argue that *omitting*
the conclusion and coded diagnoses from the narrative of a diagnostic
report can ever be clinically safe — yet `SHOULD` permits exactly that
with no enumerated justification. The narrative guidance therefore
contradicts the base DomainResource.text invariant and is, in the
reporter's words, "unsafe".

### Proposed Change

Two surgical edits in the `HL7/fhir` clone, both inside
`source/diagnosticreport/`:

1. **`source/diagnosticreport/diagnosticreport-notes.xml`** — On the line
   beginning `Note that the conclusion and the coded diagnoses…`, change
   the upper-case normative `SHOULD` to the lower-case, descriptive
   `should`. No other words on the line change.

   Before:

   > Note that the conclusion and the coded diagnoses are part of the
   > atomic data and **SHOULD** be duplicated in the narrative and in the
   > presented form if the latter is present.

   After:

   > Note that the conclusion and the coded diagnoses are part of the
   > atomic data and **should** be duplicated in the narrative and in the
   > presented form if the latter is present.

2. **`source/diagnosticreport/diagnosticreport-introduction.xml`** — Add
   a release-note bullet for FHIR-54548 inside the existing
   `<blockquote style="background-color: lightblue">` "Release Notes"
   list, after the FHIR-54163 entry:

   ```xml
   <li><a href="https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54548">FHIR-54548</a></li>
   ```

No StructureDefinition, ValueSet, search parameter, example, or other
resource artefact changes are required. There is no schema, cardinality,
binding, or invariant change.

### Design Rationale

- The reporter explicitly accepted "make `should` lower case" as a valid
  resolution. Lower-casing removes the RFC 2119 normative force of the
  sentence, so it becomes prose advice rather than a conformance rule
  that competes with the `DomainResource.text` "clinically safe"
  requirement. The underlying expectation that narratives be clinically
  safe is preserved by `DomainResource.text` itself and by the existing
  surrounding paragraph in `diagnosticreport-notes.xml` (which already
  states "the narrative must be a clinically safe view of the diagnostic
  report").
- The lowercase variant was preferred over outright deletion (the literal
  resolution text) because it keeps the helpful editorial guidance —
  "duplicate conclusion / coded diagnoses in narrative + presented form"
  — without overstating it as conformance. PR #4022 was approved and
  carries the `Ready_to_Apply` label, indicating WG consensus on this
  reading.
- Adding the FHIR-54548 entry to the DiagnosticReport release-note
  blockquote follows the established pattern in the same file (the
  blockquote already lists FHIR-54554, FHIR-55448, FHIR-54163) and is
  required by the `R6-Release-note` label.

### Step-by-Step Tasks

#### HL7/fhir

1. **Lower-case `SHOULD` in DiagnosticReport narrative guidance**
   - File: `source/diagnosticreport/diagnosticreport-notes.xml`
   - Action: On the line currently reading
     `Note that the conclusion and the coded diagnoses are part of the atomic data and SHOULD be duplicated in the narrative and in the presented form if the latter is present.`
     replace the single token `SHOULD` with `should`. Leave the rest of
     the line and surrounding `<p>` / `<ul>` markup untouched.
   - Details: This corresponds exactly to the `-` / `+` pair in
     PR HL7/fhir#4022 (one-character-class diff: `SHOULD` → `should`).
     The intent is to demote RFC 2119 normative force to descriptive
     prose so that the sentence no longer competes with
     `DomainResource.text`'s "clinically safe" requirement. No other
     `SHOULD`/`SHALL`/`MAY` keywords on the page are affected.

2. **Add FHIR-54548 to the DiagnosticReport release-notes blockquote**
   - File: `source/diagnosticreport/diagnosticreport-introduction.xml`
   - Action: Inside the existing
     `<blockquote style="background-color: lightblue">` "Release Notes"
     `<ul>` (currently lines 23–28), append a new `<li>` after the
     FHIR-54163 entry:
     `<li><a href="https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54548">FHIR-54548</a></li>`
   - Details: Match the exact formatting of the three sibling `<li>`
     entries (single-line `<li><a href="…">FHIR-NNNNN</a></li>`, same
     indentation of 8 spaces). Required by the `R6-Release-note` label.

- **Interoperability considerations:** None. Lowercasing the keyword
  removes a normative obligation that no implementation could have been
  *required* to honor previously (it was a SHOULD, not a SHALL). No
  conformant implementation becomes non-conformant, and no previously
  non-conformant implementation needs to change.
