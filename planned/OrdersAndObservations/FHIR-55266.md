## Resolution Summary

**Resolution:** Not Persuasive with Modification — *"Make this code system and ValueSet experimental"*.

The original request was to consider migrating the `nutritionintake-status-reason` CodeSystem (and its ValueSet of the same name, bound to `NutritionIntake.statusReason` as an example binding) to THO so it could be reused by CDA / V2 / other specifications. The committee did not agree to migrate the artifacts to THO. Instead, both the CodeSystem `http://hl7.org/fhir/CodeSystem/nutritionintake-status-reason` and the ValueSet `http://hl7.org/fhir/ValueSet/nutritionintake-status-reason` are to be flagged as **experimental** (signalling that the codes are not vetted for production use and are subject to change). No element-level binding change to NutritionIntake is required by the resolution itself; the example binding remains.

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

The `nutritionintake-status-reason` CodeSystem currently lives in core FHIR (work group `oo`, standards-status `informative`, `experimental="false"`) and is referenced from the `nutritionintake-status-reason` ValueSet, which is bound (example strength) to `NutritionIntake.statusReason`. The reporter flagged that:

- The codes look like potentially reusable terminology that other HL7 product families (CDA, V2) might want.
- If the codes are intended for real-world use, they belong in HL7 Terminology (THO) so they can evolve independently of FHIR releases.
- Conversely, if they are not vetted for real-world use, they should not be presented in core FHIR as if they were.

In the comment thread, the assignee (Jean Duteau) noted that `NutritionIntake.statusReason` is an *example* binding whose ValueSet composes two systems: the local `nutritionintake-status-reason` CodeSystem plus a single SNOMED CT concept (`419099009 — Dead`). Lloyd McKenzie's comment formalised the disposition: either move the CodeSystem to THO, or mark it experimental.

### Proposed Change

Make the local CodeSystem and its ValueSet experimental in core FHIR. Concretely:

1. **CodeSystem `nutritionintake-status-reason`** (`http://hl7.org/fhir/CodeSystem/nutritionintake-status-reason`)
   - Change `<experimental value="false"/>` → `<experimental value="true"/>`.
   - Leave `status="active"`, `standards-status="informative"`, `wg="oo"`, `content="complete"`, `caseSensitive="true"`, `hierarchyMeaning="is-a"`, the OID identifier, and all defined concepts unchanged.

2. **ValueSet `nutritionintake-status-reason`** (`http://hl7.org/fhir/ValueSet/nutritionintake-status-reason`)
   - Change `<experimental value="false"/>` → `<experimental value="true"/>`.
   - Leave `status="active"`, `standards-status="informative"`, `wg="oo"`, `version="6.0.0"`, the OID identifier, the description, and the existing `compose` block (which includes the single SNOMED CT concept `419099009 — Dead` and the entire local CodeSystem) unchanged.

3. **NutritionIntake.statusReason binding** — no change. The element retains its example-strength binding to `http://hl7.org/fhir/ValueSet/nutritionintake-status-reason`.

The ticket resolution does not adopt the comment-thread suggestion to drop the SNOMED `Dead` concept from the ValueSet; that is left out of scope.

### Design Rationale

Marking the artifacts experimental is the lowest-impact way to honour the disposition while preserving existing references and OIDs:

- The example-strength binding on `NutritionIntake.statusReason` remains valid; implementers can still see and use the codes.
- The `experimental` flag is the FHIR-defined signal for "this artifact is for testing / not vetted for production" and is exactly what HL7 uses elsewhere when terminology is provisional pending THO maturation.
- It avoids the higher-cost alternative of migrating the CodeSystem to THO (which would have required new canonical URLs, deprecation handling in core, and a coordinated UTG release).
- It avoids the more disruptive alternative of removing the codes outright, which would break any implementer already exercising them as examples.

The choice is consistent with how Orders & Observations has handled other "candidate THO" terminology: keep it in core for now, mark it experimental, and revisit migration only if uptake or CDA/V2 reuse becomes concrete.

### Related Tickets

### Step-by-Step Tasks

#### HL7/fhir

1. **Mark the NutritionIntakeStatusReason CodeSystem experimental.**
   - File: `source/nutritionintake/codesystem-nutritionintake-status-reason.xml`
   - Action: Change the existing `<experimental value="false"/>` element (currently on the line immediately after `<status value="active"/>`) to `<experimental value="true"/>`.
   - Details: Do not modify any other element. Leave the two `extension`s for `structuredefinition-wg` (`oo`) and `structuredefinition-standards-status` (`informative`) intact, as well as `id`, `url`, `identifier`/OID (`2.16.840.1.113883.4.642.4.2129`), `name="NutritionIntakeStatusReason"`, `title`, `status="active"`, `description`, `caseSensitive="true"`, `hierarchyMeaning="is-a"`, `content="complete"`, and every `<concept>` entry (`incomplete-data`, `unable-to-provide-care`, `request-clarification`, `allergy`, …).

2. **Mark the NutritionIntakeStatusReason ValueSet experimental.**
   - File: `source/nutritionintake/valueset-nutritionintake-status-reason.xml`
   - Action: Change the existing `<experimental value="false"/>` element (currently on the line immediately after `<status value="active"/>`) to `<experimental value="true"/>`.
   - Details: Leave `id`, `url`, `identifier`/OID (`2.16.840.1.113883.4.642.3.3337`), `version="6.0.0"`, `name="NutritionIntakeStatusReason"`, `title`, `status="active"`, `publisher`, `contact`, `description`, `copyright`, and the entire `<compose>` block unchanged.    - Details: The resolution does not require a binding-strength or target change; this step exists only to guard against accidental drift during the same edit session.
