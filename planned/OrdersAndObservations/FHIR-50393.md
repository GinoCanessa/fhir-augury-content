## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

The reporter observes that the current Vital Signs profile set in FHIR Core
does not make the structural relationships among the profiles explicit. In
particular, readers cannot easily see that:

- A vital-signs **panel** is comprised of other vital-signs measurements
  (e.g., `structuredefinition-profile-vitalspanel.xml` aggregates the
  individual vital-signs profiles).
- **BMI** (`structuredefinition-profile-bmi.xml`) is derived from / depends
  on two other vital-signs profiles (body height and body weight).
- Some profiles carry one value while others carry two (e.g., **body
  weight** is single-valued, **blood pressure**
  `structuredefinition-profile-bp.xml` is two-valued with systolic/
  diastolic components).

The reporter further observes that discussions of these profiles
habitually conflate "any blood-pressure measurement" with "a vital-signs
blood-pressure measurement"; an explicit separation between the general
measurement concept and its vital-signs specialization is missing.

The reporter points at the demo IG
<https://build.fhir.org/ig/frankoemig/vital-signs-demo/> as prior art for
an information-model-style presentation of these relationships.

### Proposed Change

Per the resolution, **no change is proposed to the FHIR core
specification in `HL7/fhir` at this time**. The change proposal is:

1. Develop a Vital Signs Information Model as an **independent artifact**,
   under an Orders & Observations–sponsored project, outside of the FHIR
   core specification. Deliverables, governance, publication location, and
   schedule for this project are not decided in this ticket and are not
   tracked by files in `HL7/fhir`.
2. **Future (deferred) work**: once the Vital Signs Information Model is
   published, open a follow-up ticket to update the Vital Signs Profile
   pages in FHIR core to (a) include a summary of the relevant
   information-model elements and (b) add references (links) to the
   information model. The expected files for that future work are the
   profile-introduction / profile-notes pages under `source/observation/`
   (`observation-vitalsigns-profile-introduction.xml`,
   `observation-vitalsigns-profile-notes.xml`) and potentially the profile
   StructureDefinitions themselves.

### Design Rationale

The O&O work group explicitly chose **not** to embed an information model
in FHIR core, for two reasons evident from the resolution and comments:

- **Scope discipline.** FHIR core is a normative specification of wire
  resources, profiles, and their bindings. A full information model
  explaining the relationships between vital-signs profiles is a
  conceptual / ontological artifact whose natural home is an independent
  HL7 project, not the core specification's narrative pages.
- **Community engagement.** The follow-up comment ("We will work with the
  larger HL7 community to advance the use of information models to inform
  particularly complex concepts such as vital signs.") shows O&O wants
  this work co-produced with the broader HL7 community, which is not
  easily done on the FHIR core publish cadence.

The alternative — hand-authoring an information-model chapter inside
`source/observation/` — was implicitly considered and rejected by the
"Not Persuasive" portion of the disposition. The "with Modification"
portion is the commitment to do the work externally and later reference
it from the Vital Signs Profile.

### Open Questions

- **Home of the Vital Signs Information Model.** The resolution does not
  name the repository or publication target for the O&O-sponsored
  information-model project. This needs to be settled by O&O before
  the deferred follow-up can reference it.
- **Scope of "summary elements".** The resolution commits the Vital
  Signs Profile to ultimately include *"(summary) elements of and
  references to"* the information model. The granularity of those
  summary elements (narrative only? representative structural elements?
  a sidebar diagram?) is unresolved and must be agreed by O&O before
  the follow-up ticket can be actioned.
- **Interaction with the demo IG.** The ticket links to the
  `frankoemig/vital-signs-demo` build.fhir.org IG as prior art. Whether
  any material from that demo is incorporated into the new
  information-model project — or whether the project starts fresh — is
  not decided in this ticket.
- **Briefing scope.** The saved per-repo briefing for `HL7/fhir` was
  originally produced for FHIR-42980 (a Mapping Language prose change).
  Its Facts, Authoring roots, Recommended Change Recipes, and
  Gotchas remain accurate for `HL7/fhir` as a whole and are used
  unchanged here; only the briefing's Ticket-Relevant Paths section is
  specific to FHIR-42980 and has been replaced above with the Vital
  Signs–relevant paths inferred from the clone. No re-run of
  `repo-analysis` is required for this plan, because no files will be
