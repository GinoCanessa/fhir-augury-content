## Resolution Summary

Resolution: **Persuasive** — "Will fix as requested."

The ticket is a technical correction to the `DeviceDefinition.packaging.identifier`
element. Both its short display and definition currently read "packaged
medication", which is incorrect for `DeviceDefinition` (a device-domain resource).
They must be updated to read "packaged device":

- **Short:** `Business identifier of the packaged medication` → `Business identifier of the packaged device`
- **Definition:** `The business identifier of the packaged medication.` → `The business identifier of the packaged device.`

No other changes (cardinality, type, binding, invariants) are requested.

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

`DeviceDefinition.packaging.identifier` describes the business identifier for a
packaged item in the context of a device definition, but its short display and
definition text refer to "packaged medication". This is a copy/paste residue from
medication-domain resources and is misleading to implementers reading the
generated specification. The remainder of the `DeviceDefinition.packaging`
backbone (e.g., `DeviceDefinition.packaging.distributor` short/definition: "An
organization that distributes the packaged **device**") already uses the correct
"device" wording, making `packaging.identifier` an outlier.

### Proposed Change

In `source/devicedefinition/structuredefinition-DeviceDefinition.xml`, on the
element `DeviceDefinition.packaging.identifier`:

- Change `<short value="Business identifier of the packaged medication"/>` to
  `<short value="Business identifier of the packaged device"/>`.
- Change `<definition value="The business identifier of the packaged medication."/>`
  to `<definition value="The business identifier of the packaged device."/>`.

No other element on `DeviceDefinition` references "packaged medication" (verified
by searching `source/devicedefinition/*.xml`). No examples, narrative pages, or
ValueSets need accompanying edits.

### Design Rationale

- Aligns the wording with the resource domain (`DeviceDefinition` describes a
  device kind, not a medication) and with the sibling `packaging.distributor`
  element which already says "packaged device".
- Pure text/definition correction — no schema-affecting change, so it cannot
  break existing instances or downstream artifacts.
- Resolution vote was Persuasive with "Will fix as requested", so the proposed
  text matches the approved wording verbatim.
