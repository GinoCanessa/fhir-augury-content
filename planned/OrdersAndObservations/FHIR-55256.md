1. **Typo fixes** on the DeviceDefinition page:

   - `homogenuous` → `homogeneous`
2. **Prose revisions** to six existing sentences in the page intro (the "Software" subsection of *Scope and Usage* and the *Boundaries and Relationships* section). The resolution gives explicit Current → Revision pairs for each sentence:

| # | Current | Revision |
|---|---------|----------|
| 1 | A software item like a data transformer or converter, or a clinical support algorithm, may be an independently deployed, managed and configured entity that contributes to patient care and needs to be documented just a surely as, say, a physical cardiac monitor. | A software item—such as a data transformer, converter, or clinical decision-support algorithm—may be independently deployed, managed, and configured, directly contributing to patient care and therefore requiring documentation with the same rigor as a physical device, such as a cardiac monitor. |
| 2 | Device instances documenting software items are important to traceability of data and analyzing adverse events, and so have important patient safety roles. | Device instances that document software items are critical for data traceability and adverse-event analysis, and therefore play a vital role in patient safety. |
| 3 | For example one may indicate a specific device as part of a ServiceRequest order detail or a kind of device using DeviceDefinition; one may indicate a specific device to always perform the observation or a kind of device as part of an ObservationDefinition; one may document a specific device or kind of device used to collect a Specimen. | For example, a ServiceRequest may reference a specific device in the order details or a general class of device using a DeviceDefinition; an ObservationDefinition may specify either a particular device that always performs the observation or a category of device; and a Specimen record may document the exact device, or type of device, used for collection. |
| 4 | Note that in some jurisdictions a regulated product might not be considered a device, but may be regulated as a medication or a biologic. | Note that in some jurisdictions, a regulated product may not be classified as a device, but instead be regulated as a medication or a biologic. |

These may also be considered Digital therapeutics (DTx) in some contexts | Device instances that document software items are essential for data traceability and adverse-event analysis, and therefore play a critical role in patient safety; in some contexts, these software items may also be considered digital therapeutics (DTx). |

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

The `DeviceDefinition` specification page (rendered HTML at `devicedefinition.html`) contains:

- A typo `homogenuous` (should be `homogeneous`) and the unhyphenated abbreviation `builtin` (should be `built-in`). These were flagged in the original ballot/QA comment imported from comment 221.
- Several long, run-on, or grammatically loose sentences in the *Scope and Usage → Software* subsection and in the *Boundaries and Relationships* prose. These are difficult to read and inconsistent with the more polished tone of surrounding sections.

There is no functional/behavioural defect — this is purely an editorial correction to bring the page in line with HL7 publication quality bar before the DeviceDefinition normative ballot. The ticket originates from automated spelling/abbreviation checks against the ballot draft.

### Proposed Change

Edit a single file — `source/devicedefinition/devicedefinition-introduction.xml` — to apply the seven textual edits below. All edits are confined to that one authored XHTML page; no schema, StructureDefinition, search-parameter, terminology, or example changes are required.

1. **Line ~65 (`<h3 id="DeviceScopeBoundaryRelationships-Point-of-Caredevice">Point-of-Care Device (PoCD)</h3>` paragraph):** replace `builtin` with `built-in` in the phrase "*… in the monitor, builtin and attached modular measurement capabilities …*".

2. **Line ~71 (Software subsection, sentence 2):** replace the existing sentence with the revised wording from resolution row 1.

3. **Line ~71 (Software subsection, last sentence):** replace `Device instances documenting software items are important to traceability … in some contexts.` with the revised wording from resolution row 6 (preserving the trailing DTx clause).

4. **Line ~90 (Boundaries and Relationships paragraph after the *Where the DeviceDefinition is referenced …* sentence):** replace the `For example one may indicate …` sentence with resolution row 3's revision.

5. **Line ~93 (Boundaries and Relationships paragraph beginning *Note that in some jurisdictions …*):** replace the first sentence with resolution row 4's revision (the trailing clause `These boundaries are not controlled for in the FHIR Resources …` is unchanged and remains in place).

6. **Line ~100 (`<li>Substance:</li>` bullet):** replace the entire bullet text with resolution row 5's revision. This both fixes the `homogenuous` → `homogeneous` typo and applies the broader rewording (`definite` → `defined`, `nutrition product` → `nutritional product`, etc.). The bold `<strong>` / `<b>Substance:</b>` styling pattern used by the surrounding bullets should be preserved (see existing `Medication`, `BiologicallyDerivedProduct`, `NutritionProduct` bullets — they are in plain text, so a plain-text bullet matching the resolution table is acceptable; if the row 5 `<b>Substance:</b>` markup is desired, the other four bullets should remain as-is to avoid expanding scope).

No other files in the repo reference `homogenuous` (verified via repo-wide grep), and `builtin` appears only in this one paragraph within the `devicedefinition` directory, so no follow-on edits are needed.

### Design Rationale

- The change is a literal application of an `Applied`/`Persuasive` resolution that is `Ready_to_Apply`; the OO work group has already approved the exact replacement text. There is no design choice left to make — the wording is dictated by the resolution table.
- The bespoke HL7 publisher (per `repo-analysis` briefing, *Build system*) reads `source/<resource>/<resource>-introduction.xml` directly as authored XHTML, so editing the file in place produces the rendered page change with no additional tooling step.
- Keeping all edits in one file matches the briefing's *Update narrative / prose* recipe and minimises diff surface for reviewers.
