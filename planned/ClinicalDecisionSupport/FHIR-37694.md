## Resolution Description

> Agreed add language describing shared decision making as a possible use case for PDDI triggering at the patient-view hook

## Feature Proposal

This ticket requires adding narrative language to the PDDI CDS Implementation Guide that describes **shared decision making** as a concrete use case for triggering PDDI clinical decision support at the `patient-view` CDS Hook. The original IG contained a TODO placeholder in the patient-view section. The `patient-view` hook fires when a clinician opens a patient's record—outside the CPOE workflow—and the resolution calls for explaining why this is an appropriate place for PDDI CDS, with shared decision making as the primary motivating example.

The implementation should:

1. **Expand the introductory patient-view paragraph** in the IG's main index page (`index.md`) to properly describe shared decision making as a use case, replacing the current rough/incomplete language.
2. **Add use case narrative** to the Patient View section in the CDS Service Specification page (`cds-service-spec.md`) that describes how the `patient-view` hook enables shared decision making for PDDI awareness.
3. **Update the Scope section** in `index.md` to acknowledge the `patient-view` scenario alongside `order-select` and `order-sign`.

## Detailed Implementation Plan

### Step 1: Update `input/pagecontent/index.md` — Patient-View Paragraph

**Location**: Technical Assumptions section, paragraph starting with "A third option that this implementation guide discusses..."

**Action**: Replace the existing rough paragraph with well-structured language such as:

> A third option that this implementation guide discusses is CDS at the time a clinician opens a patient record (`patient-view`). Although this event occurs outside of computerized provider order entry, there are important reasons to provide PDDI CDS at this point in the clinical workflow. The `patient-view` hook has broad adoption among EHR systems, making it a pragmatic trigger for PDDI CDS. One particularly relevant use case is **shared decision making**: when a clinician opens the patient's chart, the CDS service can evaluate the patient's current active medications for potential drug-drug interactions and present relevant information proactively. This creates an ideal opportunity for the clinician and patient to collaboratively review existing PDDIs, discuss risk factors, consider alternative therapies or monitoring strategies, and make informed decisions about the patient's medication regimen. Unlike `order-select` and `order-sign`, which fire during active prescribing, `patient-view` supports a proactive, educational approach to PDDI awareness and risk communication.

### Step 2: Update `input/pagecontent/index.md` — Scope Section

**Location**: The "### Scope" section, the numbered list of scenarios.

**Action**: Add a third scenario to the numbered list:

> 3. CDS at the time a clinician opens a patient record ("patient-view"), supporting use cases such as shared decision making about existing PDDIs

### Step 3: Update `input/pagecontent/cds-service-spec.md` — Patient View Use Case Narrative

**Location**: The `### Patient View` section, immediately after the introductory paragraph and before `#### Summary of Operations`.

**Action**: Insert a new paragraph describing the shared decision making use case, such as:

> A key use case for the `patient-view` hook in PDDI CDS is **shared decision making**. When a clinician opens a patient's chart, the CDS service can proactively evaluate the patient's active medications for known drug-drug interactions without waiting for a new medication order. This supports scenarios where the clinician and patient engage in a collaborative review of the patient's current medication regimen—for example, during a routine office visit or medication reconciliation. The CDS service can present information about existing PDDIs, associated risk factors, and potential mitigation strategies (e.g., lab monitoring, dose adjustments, or alternative therapies). This proactive approach facilitates informed, patient-centered conversations about medication safety and helps both clinician and patient weigh the benefits and risks of continuing current therapies. Because the `patient-view` hook does not involve draft orders, CDS responses at this point focus on the patient's existing medications and clinical context rather than a specific new prescription.

### Step 4: Review and Validate

- Ensure all changes are consistent with each other and with the IG's existing style (Markdown with Jekyll-style formatting directives like `{:.no_toc}`)
- Verify that the new text uses appropriate RFC 2119 language (SHOULD, MAY, etc.) where making normative statements
- Mark new content with the IG's convention for highlighting new material: `{:.new-content}` class and green text/background as noted in the IG's introduction
