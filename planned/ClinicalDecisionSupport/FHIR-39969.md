## Problem Statement

The PDDI IG is designated as a Universal (UV) realm guide, but the Introduction page contained US-specific language that was not helpful to an international audience. Specifically, the "Intended Audiences" section referenced the "National Council for Prescription Drug Programs (NCPDP)," a US-specific organization, when describing e-prescribing medication history. This reference made the guide less universally applicable.

## Resolution Description

The resolution (Persuasive) directs the following text change in the **Intended Audiences** section of the **Introduction** page:

**Before:**
> "If the system can send medication orders present in the **National Council for Prescription Drug Programs (NCPDP)** e-prescribing medication history for outpatient drugs as FHIR resources to the CDS service then, these resources could be included in CDS logic."

**After:**
> "If the system can send medication orders present in the e-prescribing medication history for outpatient drugs as FHIR resources to the CDS service then, these resources could be included in CDS logic."

The change removes the US-specific "National Council for Prescription Drug Programs (NCPDP)" reference, making the sentence universally applicable.

## Feature Proposal

**Type:** Narrative/documentation text edit — removal of jurisdiction-specific reference.

The change is a single text edit that removes the US-centric NCPDP reference from the Introduction page. This aligns the guide's content with its stated UV (Universal) realm designation.
