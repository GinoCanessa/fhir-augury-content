## Problem Statement

The ballot comment questions why the `resource-approvalDate` extension (and many other extensions in the CPG IG) has a cardinality of 0..1. The commenter asks: under what circumstances would the extension be provided (given that it is optional) and yet not have content?

The underlying concern is that **optional extensions with 0..1 cardinality lack guidance** about when they should or should not be populated, making conformance expectations unclear to implementers.

## Resolution Description

> resource-approvalDate would typically not be provided for draft artifacts. We are considering separate profiles for "draft" vs "published" content to make this more clear, but at the very least, we will add additional guidance on when the resource-approvalDate may be known/not known.

**Resolution requires implementation**: Yes (Persuasive).

## Impact Analysis

### Scope of Change

This is a **documentation/guidance-only** change. No structural changes to extension definitions, profiles, or cardinality are required.

The resolution calls for:
1. **Minimum**: Add guidance text explaining when `resource-approvalDate` may or may not be known (e.g., draft artifacts typically lack an approval date).
2. **Aspirational**: Consider separate profiles for "draft" vs "published" content (deferred to future work; subsequently addressed in CRMI IG via FHIR-49132).

### Breaking Changes

**None.** This is purely additive guidance text. No cardinality or structural changes.
