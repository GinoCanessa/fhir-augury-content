## Problem Statement

The CQL timing phrases documentation does not include examples that illustrate the
behavioral difference when a duration offset is used with `before` (or `after`) timing
phrases applied to **date-valued** expressions like `Patient.birthDate` and `Today()`.

Specifically, the expression:

```cql
Patient.birthDate 30 years before Today()
```

is translated using **`same as` semantics** — it is a *point comparison* that checks
whether the patient's birth date is **exactly** 30 years before today. This surprises
users who expect it to behave as a **relative comparison** (i.e., "30 or more years
before today").

To achieve a relative comparison, the `or more` or `or less` modifiers must be used:

```cql
Patient.birthDate 30 years or more before Today()
```

This ticket requests that concrete examples be added to the specification documentation
to illustrate this distinction clearly.

### Origin

The issue was raised by a community member on [Zulip — cql > Newbie Problems with $evaluate-measure](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/179220-cql/topic/Newbie.20Problems.20with.20.24evaluate-measure) who was surprised that
`Patient.birthDate 30 years before Today()` returned no results for patients born in
1970 when run on 2022-12-13 (because the exact date 30 years before was 1992-12-13).

## Feature Proposal

Add a new subsection or extend the existing "Direct comparison with offsets" section
in Appendix H (Time Interval Calculations) of the CQL specification to include
examples that:

1. **Show a date-level duration offset comparison** using `years` and `before Today()`,
   explaining that it uses `same as` (exact match) semantics.
2. **Contrast with relative comparison** using `or more before` / `or less before`
   modifiers to demonstrate how to achieve the expected "at least N years before"
   behavior.
3. **Explain the ELM translation** briefly — `X 30 years before Today()` translates to
   `SameAs(X, Subtract(Today(), 30 'years'))`.

**Proposed content to add** (example — final wording at implementer's discretion):

---

#### Duration offset with years (date-level comparison)

When using timing phrases with a duration offset and date-valued operands, the same
`same as` semantics apply. For example, assuming today is `@2023-06-15`:

```cql
@1993-06-15 30 years before @2023-06-15
```

This returns `true` because June 15, 1993 is **exactly** 30 years before June 15, 2023.

```cql
@1970-01-01 30 years before @2023-06-15
```

This returns `false` because January 1, 1970 is **more than** 30 years before June 15,
2023 — it is not *exactly* 30 years before.

This is a common source of confusion. For example, given the following CQL:

```cql
Patient.birthDate 30 years before Today()
```

This expression evaluates to `true` only for patients whose birth date is **exactly** 30
years before today — in other words, only patients who turn exactly 30 today. The
expression is translated to ELM as:

```
SameAs(ToDate(Patient.birthDate), Subtract(Today(), 30 'years'))
```

To express "patients whose birth date is 30 or more years before today" (i.e., patients
who are 30 years old or older), use the `or more` modifier:

```cql
Patient.birthDate 30 years or more before Today()
```

Similarly, to express "patients whose birth date is 30 years or less before today" (i.e.,
patients who are 30 years old or younger):

```cql
Patient.birthDate 30 years or less before Today()
```
