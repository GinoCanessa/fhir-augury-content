## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

The MCPOL ("managed care policy") concept in the v3 ActCode code system is
referenced from the FHIR `Coverage.type` value set
(`http://hl7.org/fhir/ValueSet/coverage-type`, which composes
`http://terminology.hl7.org/ValueSet/v3-ActCoverageTypeCode`). Its current
definition begins with three sentences that describe a workers' compensation
program (government mandated coverage for workplace injuries, employer
self-funding vs. commercial coverage, employee premium contributions) rather
than a managed care policy:

> "**Definition:** Government mandated program providing coverage, disability
> income, and vocational rehabilitation for injuries sustained in the work
> place or in the course of employment. Employers may either self-fund the
> program, purchase commercial coverage, or pay a premium to a government
> entity that administers the program. Employees may be required to pay
> premiums toward the cost of coverage as well."

Only the subsequent text (starting "Managed care policies specifically
excludeâ€¦" and the *Discussion:* paragraph) actually describes managed care.
The leading paragraph is misleading to implementers reading the binding for
`Coverage.type` and conflates MCPOL with workers'-comp codes (e.g., `WCBPOL`)
that already exist in the same code system.

### Proposed Change

Edit the `definition` value of the `MCPOL` concept in the v3 `ActCode`
CodeSystem source-of-truth file so that the leading workers'-comp paragraph is
removed. The remaining text ("Managed care policies specifically excludeâ€¦"
through the end of the *Discussion:* paragraph) stays intact, and the leading
`**Definition:**` marker should be preserved on the new opening sentence.

**Before** (current `definition` attribute, with sentence boundaries marked):

1. "Government mandated program providing coverage, disability income, and
   vocational rehabilitation for injuries sustained in the work place or in
   the course of employment."
2. "Employers may either self-fund the program, purchase commercial coverage,
   or pay a premium to a government entity that administers the program."
3. "Employees may be required to pay premiums toward the cost of coverage as
   well."
4. *(blank line)* "Managed care policies specifically exclude coverage for
   losses insured under a disability policy, workers' compensation program,
   liability insurance (including automobile insurance); or for medical
   expenses, coverage for on-site medical clinics or for limited dental or
   vision benefits when these are provided under a separate policy."
5. *(blank line)* "*Discussion:* Managed care policies are offered by managed
   care plans that contract with selected providers or health care
   organizations to provide comprehensive health care at a discount to
   covered parties and coordinate the financing and delivery of health care.
   Managed care uses medical protocols and procedures agreed on by the
   medical profession to be cost effective, also known as medical practice
   guidelines. Providers are typically reimbursed for covered services by a
   capitated amount on a per member per month basis that may reflect
   difference in the health status and level of services anticipated to be
   needed by the member."

**After** (proposed `definition` attribute):

> `**Definition:** Managed care policies specifically exclude coverage for
> losses insured under a disability policy, workers' compensation program,
> liability insurance (including automobile insurance); or for medical
> expenses, coverage for on-site medical clinics or for limited dental or
> vision benefits when these are provided under a separate policy.&#xD;&#xA;&#xD;&#xA;*Discussion:* Managed care policies are offered by managed care plans that contract with selected providers or health care organizations to provide comprehensive health care at a discount to covered parties and coordinate the financing and delivery of health care. Managed care uses medical protocols and procedures agreed on by the medical profession to be cost effective, also known as medical practice guidelines. Providers are typically reimbursed for covered services by a capitated amount on a per member per month basis that may reflect difference in the health status and level of services anticipated to be needed by the member.`

Notes on the edit:

- Preserve the literal `&#xD;&#xA;&#xD;&#xA;` paragraph separators between
  the `**Definition:**` paragraph and the `*Discussion:*` paragraph.
- Re-attach `**Definition:**` to the now-leading "Managed care policiesâ€¦"
  sentence so the markdown bolding still scopes the definition paragraph.
- Do **not** modify the concept's `code`, `display`, `status`, or `subsumedBy`
  properties.
- Do **not** touch the other coverage-type values (life insurance, annuity,
  etc.) that the reporter flagged â€” the resolution explicitly defers those to
  a separate code-system review.

### Design Rationale

- The resolution is narrow and editorial: it corrects misleading definition
  text without changing the meaning, status, hierarchy, or identity of the
  MCPOL code. This preserves backward compatibility for all systems that
  already use `MCPOL`.
- Deleting the first three sentences (rather than rewriting them or moving
  them elsewhere) follows the resolution wording verbatim ("Will remove the
  first 3 sentences from the description").
- The remaining paragraphs already describe managed care correctly, so no
  replacement text is required.
- Broader value-set hygiene concerns raised in the original ticket are
  consciously punted to the overall type-code-system review, keeping this
  change minimal and low-risk.
