## Resolution Summary

The resolution (**Persuasive with Modification**) directs the WG to add a new
**extension** definition that decorates `Coverage.type`, rather than modifying
the Coverage resource itself. The exact direction in `metadata.resolution_description`:

> Add an **extension** definition to Coverage 'Type' which would be named
> `policyAdministrationType` `0..*` `CodeableConcept` to capture
> 'HMO, PPO, POS etc.' and bind as **preferred** to a subset of the US
> valueset and codesystem `http://hl7.org/fhir/ValueSet/coverage-type`.
>
> **Short Description / Display:** Method of administration of the policy, as
> typically used in US Healthcare.
>
> **Definition:** A classification of the administration method used to manage
> a health plan, social program, medical plan, accident coverage, workers
> compensation, auto, group health or payment by an individual or organization.
> Examples of common administration types are: HMO, PPO, PPS, FFS etc.

Concretely this requires (a) a new `Extension` StructureDefinition
`policyAdministrationType` whose context is `Coverage.type`, with
cardinality `0..*` and value type `CodeableConcept`; and (b) a new
ValueSet that is a subset of `CoverageTypeAndSelfPayCodes`
(`http://hl7.org/fhir/ValueSet/coverage-type`) limited to US-style
plan-administration codes (HMO, PPO, POS, PPS, FFS, etc.), bound to the
extension's `value[x]` with strength `preferred`.

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

`Coverage.type` (`CodeableConcept`, `0..1`, preferred binding to
`http://hl7.org/fhir/ValueSet/coverage-type`) currently captures the
broad **category** of coverage â€” social program, medical plan, accident,
workers comp, group health, individual payment, etc. (see
`source/coverage/structuredefinition-Coverage.xml` lines 260â€“287).

It does **not** carry the orthogonal US-payer concept of **plan
administration model** â€” Health Maintenance Organization (HMO),
Preferred Provider Organization (PPO), Point of Service (POS), Prospective
Payment System (PPS), Fee-for-Service (FFS), and similar. These values
exist in the broader `CoverageTypeAndSelfPayCodes` value set (which
composes `urn:oid:2.16.840.1.113883.4.642.3.520` with
`http://terminology.hl7.org/ValueSet/v3-ActCoverageTypeCode` and
`http://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/coverage-selfpay`, see
`source/coverage/valueset-coverage-type.xml`), but there is no dedicated
slot for them on `Coverage`, and overloading `Coverage.type` for both
"category of coverage" and "administration model" would be ambiguous and
backward-incompatible.

The reporter (Paul Knapp, FM WG) requested a `planType` element to capture
this concept. After WG review the resolution was modified to deliver the
capability via an **extension** rather than a new core element, keeping
the change additive and non-breaking for R5+.

### Design Rationale

- **Extension, not new core element.** The original ticket asked for a
  `planType` element on Coverage. The WG modified the resolution to
  deliver this via an **extension** instead. This keeps Coverage's
  current cardinality and shape unchanged (no breaking change for R5
  consumers), allows iteration on the value-set scope without
  Coverage-level churn, and matches the resolution wording ("Add an
  extension definition to Coverage 'Type'").
- **Context = `Coverage.type`.** The resolution explicitly anchors the
  extension to Coverage 'Type' â€” the `Coverage.type` element in the
  current StructureDefinition (`source/coverage/structuredefinition-Coverage.xml`,
  line 260). Anchoring to `Coverage.type` rather than `Coverage` makes
  the relationship between coverage *category* and *administration
  model* explicit, and matches the way other clarifying extensions on
  this resource have historically been scoped.
- **`0..*` cardinality.** Mirrors the resolution. Real-world coverages
  may carry multiple administration-model labels (e.g., a POS product
  blending HMO and PPO behaviours).
- **`CodeableConcept` value type.** The resolution requires
  `CodeableConcept`, which keeps the extension consistent with the
  surrounding `Coverage.type` element it decorates, and lets payers
  attach local refinements alongside the standard code.
- **Preferred binding to a *subset* value set.** The resolution says
  "bind as preferred to a subset of â€¦ `coverage-type`". Reusing
  `http://hl7.org/fhir/ValueSet/coverage-type` directly would let
  administration values mix with broad-category values, which is the
  exact problem the extension is intended to solve. A purpose-built
  subset value set (`coverage-policy-administration-type`) preserves
  the resolution intent while staying fully terminology-aligned with
  v3-ActCoverageTypeCode.
- **Authoring location.** Per the FhirCore briefing, all per-resource
  authoring lives under `source/<resource>/`. The two existing
  extensions in the repo (`structuredefinition-extension-operationoutcome-file.xml`
  in `source/operationoutcome/` and the analogous extension in
  `source/implementationguide/`) confirm that resource-scoped extensions
  are co-located with the parent resource's authoring files; this PR
  follows that convention.
