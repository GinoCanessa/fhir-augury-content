## Resolution Summary

**Resolution:** Persuasive ‚Äî *"Correct spelling errors."*

The ticket flags misspellings on the `paymentreconciliation` specification page. The specifics enumerated in the description are:

- `occured,` (should be `occurred,`)
- `occured.` (should be `occurred.`)
- `occurences` (should be `occurrences`)

Despite the title's reference to "abbreviation issues", the reporter (Lloyd McKenzie) clarified in comments that the heading is generic and that the only specifics are the enumerated misspellings ‚Äî there are no abbreviations to expand on this page. The change is therefore strictly a spelling correction in the page narrative.

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

The narrative on the `paymentreconciliation.html` page contains three instances of the misspelled word `occured` (which should be `occurred`) and one instance of `occurences` (which should be `occurrences`). These typos appear in the introduction prose that compares `PaymentReconciliation` to `PaymentNotice` and `ClaimResponse`, and also in the "boundaries / relationships" table cell describing what `PaymentNotice` indicates. Because this narrative is part of the published FHIR Core specification, the typos affect the official spec output and need to be corrected at the source.

### Proposed Change

Edit the authoring file `source/paymentreconciliation/paymentreconciliation-introduction.xml` in `HL7/fhir` to make the following four pure-text substitutions, all of which are case-sensitive and confined to the narrative text (no markup, no element renames):

| Line | Current text (excerpt) | Corrected text (excerpt) |
|------|------------------------|--------------------------|
| 43 | `‚Ä¶tracking of payment occurences is required‚Ä¶` | `‚Ä¶tracking of payment occurrences is required‚Ä¶` |
| 43 | `‚Ä¶or is about to occur. The resource is used commonly when tracking of payment occurences‚Ä¶` (no other change on this line apart from `occurences` ‚Üí `occurrences`) | (same line) |
| 47 | `‚Ä¶does not indicate that payment has occured.` | `‚Ä¶does not indicate that payment has occurred.` |
| 79 | `<td>An indication that a payment has occured, is about to occur, or has been cancelled.</td>` | `<td>An indication that a payment has occurred, is about to occur, or has been cancelled.</td>` |

Concretely, this is two distinct token-level fixes:

- `occured` ‚Üí `occurred` (3 instances: lines 43, 47, 79)
- `occurences` ‚Üí `occurrences` (1 instance: line 43)

Other words on these lines that are sometimes spell-checker noise (e.g., `cancelled` on line 79) are intentionally retained ‚Äî `cancelled` is the spelling used elsewhere in the FHIR specification narrative and is not within the scope of this ticket.

No other paymentreconciliation source files contain these misspellings (verified with a case-sensitive search of `source/paymentreconciliation/*.xml` and `*.html`).

### Design Rationale

- The resolution description is unambiguous ("Correct spelling errors.") and the reporter has explicitly enumerated which words are wrong. There is no design tradeoff ‚Äî these are typo fixes in narrative prose.
- The fix is applied to the hand-authored XML page snippet (`paymentreconciliation-introduction.xml`). Per the per-repo briefing, `source/<resource>/` files are authoring inputs to the bespoke HL7 publisher and HTML/XML edits go in directly; this is the canonical place for narrative changes.
- The reporter has already pushed back on a sub-question about abbreviations (mckenzie comment: *"The heading is generic. The specifics are in the enumerated list. No abbreviations here."*), so the scope of the fix is intentionally limited to the four spelling corrections above. We do not interpret the unrelated "should be 'an event'" remark from the comment thread as in-scope: it is not in the resolution description, no source occurrence of `a event` was found in this resource's authoring files, and it appears to refer to a different page or ticket. If the workgroup intends to bundle it, a separate ticket should drive that change.
