## Resolution Summary

The original request asked to change all Provenance-related conformance statements in the PDex IG section from **SHOULD** to **SHALL**. The resolution was **Persuasive with Modification**: rather than a blanket upgrade to SHALL, a nuanced approach was adopted:

1. **SHALL** is applied specifically to Provenance records received via **Member-authorized Payer-to-Payer exchange**.
2. **SHOULD** is retained for Provenance records from **other sources** (non Payer-to-Payer exchange).
3. **SHOULD** is retained for creating related Provenance records when forwarding FHIR Resources.
4. **SHOULD** is retained for providing Provenance with member-related resources, with clarifying text that it applies "when requested, such as via the `_RevInclude` parameter."
5. A forward-looking statement is added expressing intent to elevate the remaining SHOULD statements to SHALL in future IG versions.

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

The original PDex IG text used **SHOULD** for all three Provenance-related conformance statements in the Provenance section. Submitter Celine Lefebvre argued these should be strengthened to **SHALL** to ensure consistent Provenance tracking across health plans, particularly for Payer-to-Payer exchanges where data provenance is critical for patient safety and data integrity.

### Proposed Change

Revise the Provenance section text from:

> Health Plans **SHOULD** accept and retain Provenance records received with data from other sources such as a Member-authorized Payer-to-Payer exchange.
>
> When a Health Plan forwards information as a FHIR Resource it **SHOULD** create related Provenance record(s) to reflect the original source of the data.
>
> A Provenance resource **SHOULD** be provided with each member-related resource provided by the Health Plan's FHIR API.

To:

> Health Plans **SHALL** accept and retain Provenance records received with data based on Member-authorized Payer-to-Payer exchange.
>
> Health Plans **SHOULD** accept and retain Provenance records received with data from sources other than Member-authorized Payer-to-Payer exchange.
>
> When a Health Plan forwards information as a FHIR Resource it **SHOULD** create related Provenance record(s) to reflect the original source of the data.
>
> A Provenance resource **SHOULD** be provided with each member-related resource provided by the Health Plan's FHIR API when requested, such as via the `_RevInclude` parameter.
>
> ---
>
> It is the intent to re-visit the above **SHOULD** conformance statements to require **SHALL** in future versions of this Implementation Guide.

### Design Rationale

The modification balances implementer readiness with data integrity needs:
- The most critical scenario (Payer-to-Payer exchange) is elevated to **SHALL** because provenance tracking is essential for trusted data exchange between payers.
- Other scenarios remain **SHOULD** to give implementers time to adopt, with a clear signal that future versions will require full compliance.
- The `_RevInclude` clarification improves implementability by specifying *how* Provenance resources should be returned.

### Breaking Changes

- **Conformance level elevation**: Changing from SHOULD to SHALL for Payer-to-Payer exchange Provenance records is a **breaking conformance change**. Implementations that previously omitted Provenance records in Payer-to-Payer exchanges (relying on the SHOULD language) will now be **non-conformant**.
- **Text restructuring**: The single first paragraph is split into two paragraphs (one SHALL for Payer-to-Payer, one SHOULD for other sources), which changes the narrative structure.
- **New qualifying text**: The addition of "when requested, such as via the `_RevInclude` parameter" narrows the scope of the third statement, which may affect implementations that proactively included Provenance resources in all responses.
