## Resolution Summary

Resolution is **Persuasive**. Resolution description: *"Fix punctuation"*.

Concretely, in the ImagingStudy "Boundaries and Relationships" section the
sentence currently reads:

> "The ImagingStudy resource is used to store details of an entire DICOM
> Study and its relationship to other resources, such as Patient.
> ServiceRequest and Encounter."

The reporter requests two punctuation fixes:

1. Replace the period after `Patient` with a comma so the three resources
   form a proper list.
2. Add an Oxford-style comma after `ServiceRequest` (noted as "debatable"
   but accepted with the Persuasive resolution).

A commit referenced in the only ticket comment
([c429239e](https://github.com/HL7/fhir/commit/c429239eac28753c38f5211945b94996e2c86b05))
indicates the change has already been applied on `HL7/fhir` master.

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

Section 10.5.2 of the ImagingStudy resource page contains a typographical
error: a period appears mid-sentence between `Patient` and `ServiceRequest`,
breaking what should be a comma-separated list of related resources
(`Patient`, `ServiceRequest`, `Encounter`). The mistake makes the text grammatically
incorrect and visually confusing on the published spec page.

### Proposed Change

Edit the narrative in `source/imagingstudy/imagingstudy-introduction.xml` so
that the first paragraph of the "Boundaries and Relationships" section reads:

> "The ImagingStudy resource is used to store details of an entire DICOM
> Study and its relationships to other resources, such as Patient,
> ServiceRequest, and Encounter."

Specifically:

- Change `… such as Patient. ServiceRequest and Encounter.` to
  `… such as Patient, ServiceRequest, and Encounter.`
- No element/cardinality/binding/structure changes; this is a prose-only
  technical correction to the resource introduction page.

(Reference commit `c429239eac28753c38f5211945b94996e2c86b05` shows the
identical fix already merged; this plan documents that change for the
record and validates it survives in HEAD.)

### Design Rationale

A "Persuasive" resolution on a Technical Correction ticket simply asks the
editor to apply the requested punctuation fix. The change preserves the
intended meaning (a list of related resources) and aligns the prose with
the standard list construction used elsewhere in the ImagingStudy
introduction (e.g., the bulleted Observation relationships immediately
following). The Oxford comma after `ServiceRequest` is consistent with
common HL7 editorial style and matches the comment in the ticket.
