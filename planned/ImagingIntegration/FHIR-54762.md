## Resolution Summary

**Resolution: Persuasive with Modification.**

The reporter pointed out that the `ImagingStudy` notes/scope text describing what a referenced DICOM SOP Instance can be is grammatically wrong: it currently reads

> "A single- or multi-frame, still or video image captured **by a variety of** imaginging modalities, such as X-ray, MR, and ultrasound;"

A single SOP Instance is captured by exactly one modality, not by "a variety of" modalities. The committee accepted the fix but with a wording modification suggested by Jonathan Whitby: insert the words **"one of"** rather than rewriting the whole sentence as the reporter originally proposed. The agreed-upon text is:

> "A single- or multi-frame, still or video image captured **by one of a variety of** imaging modalities, such as X-ray, MR, and ultrasound;"

This is a pure prose / typo correction. No structural, cardinality, terminology, or behavioural change is implied.

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

In `HL7/fhir`, the `ImagingStudy` resource notes page contains a bulleted enumeration of what a referenced DICOM SOP Instance "could be". The first bullet describes a still or video image and (incorrectly) says it is "captured by a variety of imaging modalities". DICOM (and the surrounding ImagingStudy model) treats `modality` as a single value per image / per series — a single SOP Instance has one capturing modality, not many. The current sentence implies polysemy that does not exist in the underlying DICOM data model and is a literal grammatical error if read against the cardinality of `ImagingStudy.series.modality`.

The reporter (Jeroen Medema, IHE / DICOM perspective) flagged this as a correctness issue against the R6 ballot. The Imaging Integration WG agreed, with Jonathan Whitby refining the wording so the "variety of imaging modalities" phrase (which is meaningful when listing the *kinds* of modalities a referenced SOP Instance might originate from across the population of all instances) is preserved by inserting "one of".

### Proposed Change

Edit the bullet list inside the ImagingStudy Notes page at the point where the kinds of referenced DICOM SOP Instances are enumerated:

- **File:** `source/imagingstudy/imagingstudy-notes.xml` (HL7/fhir, master).
- **Old line (line 9):**
  ```xml
  <li>A single- or multiframe, still or video image captured by a variety of imaging modalities, such as X-ray, MR, and ultrasound;</li>
  ```
- **New line:**
  ```xml
  <li>A single- or multiframe, still or video image captured by one of a variety of imaging modalities, such as X-ray, MR, and ultrasound;</li>
  ```

Only the four characters `one of ` are inserted between `captured by ` and `a variety of`. No surrounding markup, list ordering, anchors, or cross-page links change.

This text is authored HTML/XHTML in the bespoke FHIR publisher source tree (`source/imagingstudy/`) and is included verbatim into the generated `imagingstudy.html` page; no derived/snapshot artifact has to be regenerated by hand.

### Design Rationale

- **Faithful to the DICOM model.** A SOP Instance has exactly one capturing modality; saying it is captured "by a variety of" modalities is wrong at the per-instance level.
- **Minimal, low-risk wording change.** "One of a variety of" preserves the original sentence structure and the existing examples (`X-ray, MR, and ultrasound`), and reads naturally in English. The reporter's alternative ("captured by an image modality, such as …") was rejected because (a) it loses the "variety" framing that was useful for non-imaging-experts and (b) the reporter himself flagged that "image" is not appropriate when the same enumeration is later extended to waveforms.
- **No structural change.** The Imaging Integration WG explicitly preferred a textual fix over a model change because `ImagingStudy.series.modality` (cardinality `1..1` per series, with a series-level constraint that ties it to `ImagingStudy.modality`) already encodes the correct semantics; the prose just needed to match.
- **Consistency with the resolution comment.** Jonathan Whitby's proposal of `<ins><b>one of</b></ins>` is the literal text the WG voted on; Jeroen acknowledged "OK to me." on 2026-03-27. Implementing exactly that wording avoids re-opening the disposition.
