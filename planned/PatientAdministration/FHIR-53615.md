## Resolution Summary

Resolution: **Persuasive**.

The Endpoint.header element's narratives refer to a "channel type", but
Endpoint has no field by that name. The agreed fix is to replace the
ambiguous "channel type" wording with "connection type" (aligning with
the real field `Endpoint.connectionType`) and to tighten the definition
to describe headers sent when connecting to the endpoint rather than a
vague "notification". Per the ticket's Resolution Description, the
Endpoint.header narratives will be updated to read:

- **Definition:** "Additional headers / information to send when
  **connecting to the endpoint**."
- **Short Display:** "Usage depends on the **connection** type"
- **Comments:** "Exactly what these mean depends on the **connection**
  type. The can convey additional information to the recipient and/or
  meet security requirements."

(The apparent typo "The can convey" is preserved verbatim from the
Resolution Description; see Open Questions.)

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

The Endpoint resource's `header` element currently reads:

- short: *"Usage depends on the channel type"*
- definition: *"Additional headers / information to send as part of the
  notification."*
- comment: *"Exactly what these mean depends on the channel type. The
  can convey additional information to the recipient and/or meet
  security requirements."*

The phrase "channel type" does not correspond to any element on
`Endpoint`; it appears to be carried over from Subscription.channel
prose. Implementers following Endpoint are left to guess what "channel
type" refers to. The ticket requester asked either that the prose
define what "channel type" means on Endpoint, or that it be replaced
with the actual field(s) it refers to — most likely
`Endpoint.connectionType`. The resolution takes the second option.

Additionally, "as part of the notification" is overly narrow:
`Endpoint` is a general connection descriptor (REST, messaging,
DICOM WADO-RS, etc.), not strictly a notification channel, so the
definition is reworded to "when connecting to the endpoint".

### Proposed Change

In the Endpoint StructureDefinition differential element for
`Endpoint.header` (id `Endpoint.header`, path `Endpoint.header`),
update three attribute values on the existing element (no cardinality,
type, or binding changes):

- `<short value="Usage depends on the channel type"/>` →
  `<short value="Usage depends on the connection type"/>`
- `<definition value="Additional headers / information to send as part
  of the notification."/>` →
  `<definition value="Additional headers / information to send when
  connecting to the endpoint."/>`
- `<comment value="Exactly what these mean depends on the channel
  type. The can convey additional information to the recipient and/or
  meet security requirements."/>` →
  `<comment value="Exactly what these mean depends on the connection
  type. The can convey additional information to the recipient and/or
  meet security requirements."/>`

No other element on Endpoint, and no introduction/notes page, is
changed by this ticket.

### Design Rationale

- "Connection type" is the exact wording in the Resolution Description
  and aligns with the real element `Endpoint.connectionType` (which is
  what readers are actually being pointed at).
- Keeping the change scoped to prose on a single element avoids any
  conformance impact (no schema changes, no new elements, no binding
  changes) — the easiest disposition to implement for a "Persuasive"
  documentation clarification.
- The alternative of defining "channel type" as a new concept on
  Endpoint was rejected in favor of reusing the existing
  `connectionType` vocabulary, which was already the ticket reporter's
  preferred option.
