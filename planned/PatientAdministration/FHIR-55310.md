## Resolution Summary

**Resolution:** Persuasive.

The resolution description states: *"The mentioned errors are part of the notes: https://build.fhir.org/practitioner.html#notes — second bullet point; both will be fixed accordingly."*

Two concrete textual errors must be corrected in the Notes section (second bullet) of the Practitioner resource page:

1. Misspelling — `idenfitiers` → `identifiers`.
2. Abbreviation/contraction — `doesn't` must be fully spelled out (→ `does not`), per the ticket's requirement that "Abbreviations need to be fully spelled out."

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

The narrative rendered at `practitioner.html#notes` contains a misspelled word (`idenfitiers`) and a contraction (`doesn't`) in the second bullet of the Notes list. The ticket (a Technical Correction imported as comment 274 by Lloyd McKenzie) asserts that the published specification should not use misspellings or contractions; abbreviations/contractions must be fully spelled out for clarity and to match HL7's editorial conventions.

### Proposed Change

Edit the authoring source for the Practitioner Notes narrative, `source/practitioner/practitioner-notes.xml`, so that the second `<li>` reads with the corrected text:

- Replace `Business idenfitiers may be used` with `Business identifiers may be used`.
- Replace `The <code>Practitioner.identifier</code> doesn't convey anything about the status of the license` with `The <code>Practitioner.identifier</code> does not convey anything about the status of the license`.

Exact current text (lines 13–14 of `source/practitioner/practitioner-notes.xml`):

```xml
<a name="identifier"></a>
Business idenfitiers may be used in <code>Practitioner.identifier</code> and/or <code>Practitioner.qualification.identifier</code> based on local requirements.<br/>
The <code>Practitioner.identifier</code> doesn't convey anything about the status of the license (other than period), if this or any additional details are available
```

Target text after the fix:

```xml
<a name="identifier"></a>
Business identifiers may be used in <code>Practitioner.identifier</code> and/or <code>Practitioner.qualification.identifier</code> based on local requirements.<br/>
The <code>Practitioner.identifier</code> does not convey anything about the status of the license (other than period), if this or any additional details are available
```

No structural, semantic, cardinality, type, binding, or anchor changes are proposed — the fix is prose-only. The inline `<a name="identifier"></a>` anchor is preserved.

### Design Rationale

- The resolution explicitly scopes the fix to the two items the reporter cited (`idenfitiers`, `doesn't`); there is no indication in the resolution that other Notes prose should be revised.
- "Fully spelled out" form for `doesn't` is `does not`; alternative phrasings (e.g., rewriting the sentence) would go beyond the stated resolution.
- `identifiers` is the standard FHIR spelling used throughout the rest of the Practitioner page (`Practitioner.identifier`, `Practitioner.qualification.identifier`), making this a pure typo fix with no terminology impact.
- The Patient Administration work group owns this page; edits are made directly to the bespoke HL7-publisher authoring HTML snippet (`practitioner-notes.xml`), consistent with the FhirCore authoring model.
