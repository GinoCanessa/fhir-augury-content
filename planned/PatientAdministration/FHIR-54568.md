## Resolution Summary

**Resolution:** Persuasive with Modification.

Change the `definition` text on the `PractitionerRole.contact` element.

- **From:** *"The contact details of communication devices available relevant to the specific PractitionerRole. This can include addresses, phone numbers, fax numbers, mobile numbers, email addresses and web sites."*
- **To:** *"The contact details of available ways to contact the specific PractitionerRole for specific purposes. This can include addresses, phone numbers, fax numbers, mobile numbers, email addresses,web sites and various addresses."*

The reporter's original concern was that "communication devices" is too narrow — items such as mail, phone, fax, and email are better characterised as contact *methods* / *ways to contact* rather than devices. The approved resolution reframes the element description around "available ways to contact … for specific purposes" rather than changing the element's name, type, cardinality, or binding.

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

The current `PractitionerRole.contact` element definition describes the contents as "communication devices", but the allowed content (ExtendedContactDetail) covers a mix of addresses, phone numbers, fax numbers, mobile numbers, email addresses, and web sites. Several of these (postal addresses, web sites, email) are not "devices" at all. The wording misleads implementers about what belongs in the element and is inconsistent with how the rest of the spec characterises contact information (ways / channels of contacting a party, tied to purpose and period via ExtendedContactDetail).

### Proposed Change

Definition-only (prose) change to a single element on a single resource:

- **Resource:** `PractitionerRole`
- **Element:** `PractitionerRole.contact` (id `PractitionerRole.contact`, path `PractitionerRole.contact`)
- **Field changed:** `<definition value="…"/>`
- **No change** to: `short`, `min`, `max`, `type` (`ExtendedContactDetail`), `requirements`, `alias`, `comment`, `mapping`, `isSummary`, `isModifier`, `binding`, or any invariant.

New `definition` value (verbatim from the resolution, pending typo cleanup — see Open Questions):

> The contact details of available ways to contact the specific PractitionerRole for specific purposes. This can include addresses, phone numbers, fax numbers, mobile numbers, email addresses,web sites and various addresses.

Example of the `<element>` block after the change (differential only — snapshot is regenerated by the publisher):

```xml
<element id="PractitionerRole.contact">
  <path value="PractitionerRole.contact"/>
  <short value="Official contact details relating to this PractitionerRole"/>
  <definition value="The contact details of available ways to contact the specific PractitionerRole for specific purposes. This can include addresses, phone numbers, fax numbers, mobile numbers, email addresses,web sites and various addresses."/>
  <requirements value="Often practitioners have a dedicated line for each location …"/>
  <min value="0"/>
  …
</element>
```

### Design Rationale

- The resolution is "Persuasive **with Modification**": Patient Administration accepted the reporter's concern but adopted a reworded version ("available ways to contact … for specific purposes") rather than the reporter's suggestion ("communication methods"). This aligns the element's definition with the semantics of its type (`ExtendedContactDetail`), which models ways/channels of contact along with purpose and period.
- Restricting the change to `<definition>` only (leaving `short`, type, cardinality, and binding untouched) keeps the fix minimally invasive and avoids any wire-format or conformance impact for existing implementers.
- Parallel elements on sibling resources (Organization.contact, Location.contact, HealthcareService.contact, OrganizationAffiliation.contact, InsuranceProduct.contact) use the same "communication devices" wording (see Related Specifications). The ticket specifically scopes the fix to `PractitionerRole.contact`, so this plan does **not** propagate the wording change; it is flagged as a potential follow-up.

Before committing, confirm with Patient Administration whether the intended wording is literally this string (in which case copy it verbatim) or whether minor editorial cleanup is expected, e.g.:

   > "The contact details of available ways to contact the specific PractitionerRole for specific purposes. This can include addresses, phone numbers, fax numbers, mobile numbers, email addresses, and web sites."

   Default assumption for this plan: copy the resolution text verbatim (including the missing space and the "various addresses" tail) to avoid inadvertently rewriting an approved motion; flag for editorial cleanup in review.
