## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

The Location resource page (`location.html`) embeds a "Location Hierarchy"
example that walks the reader through a fictitious hospital tree. One of the
annotations on `Room 1a` reads:

> `Room 1a (instance) - space in room separatable via a curtain`

`separatable` is not a standard English word; the correct form is `separable`.
Per Patient Administration's editorial standard (Lloyd McKenzie's
spelling/abbreviation pass, comment 265), misspelled or abbreviated words on
specification pages must be corrected and abbreviations expanded.

### Proposed Change

In the cached HL7/fhir clone, exactly one occurrence of `separatable` exists,
in the Location Hierarchy example fragment that is included into `location.html`:

- **File:** `source/location/location-notes.xml`
- **Line 62 (current):**
  `Room 1a (instance) - space in room separatable via a curtain`
- **Line 62 (after change):**
  `Room 1a (instance) - space in room separable via a curtain`

That is the entire substantive change: replace the single token `separatable`
with `separable` in `source/location/location-notes.xml`. No other files in
the clone contain the misspelling (verified via
`git grep -i separatable` — only `source/location/location-notes.xml:62`
matched).

### Design Rationale

- The resolution explicitly localises the defect to the Location Hierarchy
  example on `location.html` and commits to fixing it in place rather than
  rewriting the example. A one-token narrative substitution is the minimum
  change that satisfies the resolution.
- `separable` is the standard English adjective form ("able to be separated")
  and matches the meaning intended by the example (a curtain that allows the
  room's space to be separated). It is also the form Lloyd's editorial pass
  is known to prefer in similar tickets.
- The change is confined to authored example narrative, so it avoids any
  risk of touching generated `<snapshot>` content, terminology, or invariants
  (see Repo Context gotchas below).
