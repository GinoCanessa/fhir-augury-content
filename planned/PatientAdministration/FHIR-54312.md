## Resolution Summary

**Resolution:** Persuasive.

**Resolution description (verbatim):** *"Update the typo (to the wrong resource) as noted."*

The reporter notes that on the `Encounter` resource the `reason` BackboneElement
description reads:

> "The list of medical reasons that are expected to be addressed during the
> **episode of care**."

This wording was copy-pasted from `EpisodeOfCare`. Because the element lives on
`Encounter`, the description must say "during the **encounter**." The agreed
fix is to replace the phrase "episode of care" with "encounter" in the
mistakenly-copied wording on `Encounter.reason` (short and definition).

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

`Encounter.reason` is a BackboneElement that lists the medical reasons being
addressed during an `Encounter`. The current authored short and definition
strings on this element refer to "the episode of care" rather than "the
encounter":

- `<short value="The list of medical reasons that are expected to be addressed during the episode of care"/>`
- `<definition value="The list of medical reasons that are expected to be addressed during the episode of care."/>`

This is contextually wrong (the element is on `Encounter`, not `EpisodeOfCare`)
and is a clear copy-paste error. Implementers reading the published spec for
`Encounter` see incorrect terminology, which is confusing because `Encounter`
and `EpisodeOfCare` are explicitly distinguished elsewhere in the resource
(e.g., `Encounter.episodeOfCare` references `EpisodeOfCare`, and the
"reason" wording on `EpisodeOfCare` is intentional there).

### Proposed Change

In `source/encounter/structuredefinition-Encounter.xml`, on the `Encounter.reason`
element (`<element id="Encounter.reason">`), update the two strings that
mistakenly say "episode of care":

1. **`short`** — replace with:
   `"The list of medical reasons that are expected to be addressed during the encounter"`
2. **`definition`** — replace with:
   `"The list of medical reasons that are expected to be addressed during the encounter."`

No other element metadata changes (cardinality stays `0..*`, type stays
`BackboneElement`, `isSummary` stays `true`).

The `comment` on the same element (line 778) contains additional uses of the
phrase "episode of care," but only the leading sentence
("…that should be addressed during the episode of care.") is in the same
copy-paste pattern as the short/definition; the other occurrences
("a clinical indication that was determined in a previous encounter or episode
of care, or some planned care…") are intentional — they correctly enumerate
both contexts where a prior reason might have originated. See **Open
Questions** below for whether the leading sentence of the comment should also
be retargeted to "the encounter"; the literal resolution wording covers only
the short/definition typo, so the conservative interpretation is to leave the
comment untouched.

### Design Rationale

The change is a pure documentation/typo fix and matches the resolution
("Update the typo (to the wrong resource) as noted"). There is no schema,
cardinality, type, binding, or invariant change — the data model is unchanged.
The replacement word ("encounter") is the only correct subject because the
hosting resource is `Encounter`, and `Encounter.reason` is the
encounter-scoped sibling of `EpisodeOfCare.reason`. Choosing any other phrase
("visit", "interaction", "stay", …) would diverge from the resource's own name
and from the surrounding prose on the same StructureDefinition.

## Implementation Plan

### Prerequisites

None. This is a self-contained text correction in a single authored file.

### Step-by-Step Tasks

#### HL7/fhir

1. **Fix the `short` description on `Encounter.reason`.**
   - File: `source/encounter/structuredefinition-Encounter.xml`
   - Action: On line 776 (inside `<element id="Encounter.reason">`),
     replace
     `<short value="The list of medical reasons that are expected to be addressed during the episode of care"/>`
     with
     `<short value="The list of medical reasons that are expected to be addressed during the encounter"/>`.
   - Details: Only the noun "episode of care" → "encounter" changes. Do
     not touch the surrounding `<extension …structuredefinition-explicit-type-name>`
     or `<path>` elements; preserve XML indentation and attribute ordering
     used elsewhere in the file.

2. **Fix the `definition` description on `Encounter.reason`.**
   - File: `source/encounter/structuredefinition-Encounter.xml`
   - Action: On line 777 (immediately after the `<short>` change above),
     replace
     `<definition value="The list of medical reasons that are expected to be addressed during the episode of care."/>`
     with
     `<definition value="The list of medical reasons that are expected to be addressed during the encounter."/>`.
   - Details: Keep the trailing period (matches the existing definition
     punctuation style in this file). No cardinality, type, binding, or
     invariant edits.

3. **Confirm the comment is intentionally left as-is (or escalate per Open Questions).**
   - File: `source/encounter/structuredefinition-Encounter.xml`
   - Action: Inspect line 778 (`<comment …>`). The phrase "episode of
     care" appears multiple times in this comment; at least one
     occurrence ("a clinical indication that was determined in a previous
     encounter or episode of care, or some planned care…") is intentional
     and must not be changed. The literal resolution covers only the
     `short`/`definition` typo, so do not edit the comment unless the
     work-group clarifies otherwise (see Open Questions).
   - Details: If the work-group later confirms the leading sentence of the
     comment should also be retargeted, update only
     "…that should be addressed during the episode of care." →
     "…that should be addressed during the encounter." in that one
     leading sentence; leave the "previous encounter or episode of care"
     phrase untouched.

4. **Do not touch the `<snapshot>` block.**
   - File: `source/encounter/structuredefinition-Encounter.xml`
   - Action: Verify the edits land only inside `<differential>`. The
     publisher regenerates `<snapshot>` and must produce the corrected

   - Details: Per the FhirCore briefing, hand-editing `<snapshot>` is

### Open Questions

- **Comment text on `Encounter.reason` (line 778).** The resolution's
  literal scope is the typo as flagged ("the description"), which the
  reporter quotes from the `short`/`definition`. The first sentence of
  the `comment` ("The reason communicates what medical problem the
  patient has that should be addressed during the episode of care.")
  shares the same copy-paste pattern. Should it also be retargeted to
  "the encounter"? The conservative read of "Update the typo (to the
  wrong resource) as noted" is **no** — only fix what was noted. Confirm
  with the Patient Administration WG if a broader sweep is desired; if
  so, update only the leading sentence of the comment and leave the
  intentional "previous encounter or episode of care" phrase untouched.
- **Downstream profile descriptions.** Profiles that override
  `Encounter.reason.short`/`definition` (if any) live outside this repo
  and are not in scope for FHIR Core; no action here, but downstream IGs
  may wish to refresh inherited descriptions when they next rebase on
