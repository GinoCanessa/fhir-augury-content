## Resolution Summary

The Pharmacy WG voted *Persuasive with Modification* (Frank/Jeff: 3-0-0, 2025-11-03, confirmed in the 2025-11-04 call) to **restructure the `Dosage` datatype** so that a max-dose limit (and other safety information) can apply across multiple concurrent dosage instructions, rather than being trapped inside a single `Dosage` instance. The agreed resolution:

1. Modify the existing `Dosage` datatype: drop the `sequence` element (confirmed removed per costateixeira's 2025-11-11 comment), keep the existing dosing slots (`condition`, `text`, `additionalInstruction`, `patientInstruction`, `timing`, `asNeeded`, `asNeededFor`, `site`, `route`, `method`, `doseAndRate`, `maxDosePerPeriod`, `maxDosePerAdministration`, `maxDosePerLifetime`), and add a new `Dosage.condition : 0..1 ConditionDetails` and `Dosage.safety : 0..1 DosageSafety`.
2. Introduce a new wrapper datatype **`DosageDetails`** with `renderedInstruction : markdown`, `simple : Dosage`, `step : 0..* { start, end, count, component : 1..* Dosage, safety : 0..1 DosageSafety }`, and a top-level `safety : 0..1 DosageSafety` covering the combined course.
3. Introduce a new datatype **`DosageSafety`** with `doseLimit : 0..*` (each containing `quantity[x] : integer | Quantity | Expression`, `scope : code` bound *required* to `http://hl7.org/fhir/ValueSet/dose-limit-scope|6.0.0-ballot3` (`dosage | period | administration | lifetime`), `period : Duration`, `text : string`) and `ifExceeded : string`.
4. Switch `dosageInstruction` on `MedicationRequest`, `MedicationDispense`, and `MedicationStatement` to a single `0..1 DosageDetails` (was `0..*` `Dosage`), and move `renderedDosageInstruction` into `DosageDetails.renderedInstruction`.
5. Add worked examples (the Symbicort 1-puff-BD-plus-PRN-with-8-puff-daily-cap case from the ticket; the multi-segment weekday/weekend titration case in costateixeira's 2025-11-04 comment), document explicit *Limitations of Use*, and add an STU/balloter note flagging the breaking change and pointing to the examples.

The reporter's motivating use case is the Symbicort 200/6 inhaler: "1 puff twice a day for maintenance + 1 puff PRN, max 8 puffs/day" - two concurrent `Dosage` instances that share a single max-dose ceiling. The new shape lets the daily cap live on `DosageDetails.safety` while the maintenance and PRN regimes remain separate `Dosage` components under `DosageDetails.simple`/`step.component`.

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

In R5 a medication's dosing is expressed as `Medication{Request,Dispense,Statement}.dosageInstruction : 0..* Dosage`, and `maxDosePerPeriod` lives on each `Dosage`. There is no place to attach a *combined* limit when a clinician orders two (or more) concurrent regimens (e.g., scheduled maintenance + PRN rescue) that together must not exceed a daily/weekly cap. Implementers (Bill Lush on `#implementers > Max dose per period for a concurrent dosage instruction`, Zulip thread 530183664) reported being unable to round-trip real prescriptions like the Symbicort SmPC dosing without inventing extensions or duplicating the cap on every concurrent instruction (which is semantically wrong and dangerous if one instance is updated). The same gap blocks expressive titration / step regimes (costateixeira's worked example: Mon-Fri BID, weekend QD, sublingual PRN with weekly *and* monthly caps and a 3-week rollover-reduction rule).

### Proposed Change

Restructure the `Dosage` family of datatypes in FHIR Core (`HL7/fhir`, `source/datatypes/`) and re-point the three medication resources at the new wrapper. The structural target (verbatim from the resolution) is:

**`Dosage` (modified, breaking)**
- *Removed:* `sequence : 0..1 SU integer` (per 2025-11-11 confirmation comment).
- `condition : 0..1 ConditionDetails` *(new)*
- `text : 0..1 SU string` "Free text dosage instructions e.g. SIG"
- `additionalInstruction : 0..* SU CodeableConcept` (example bound to `http://hl7.org/fhir/ValueSet/additional-instruction-codes`)
- `patientInstruction : 0..1 SU string`
- `timing : 0..1 SU Timing`
- `asNeeded : 0..1 SU boolean`
- `asNeededFor : 0..* SU CodeableConcept` (example bound to `http://hl7.org/fhir/ValueSet/medication-as-needed-reason`)
- `site : 0..1 SU CodeableConcept` (example bound to `http://hl7.org/fhir/ValueSet/approach-site-codes`)
- `route : 0..1 SU CodeableConcept` (example bound to `http://hl7.org/fhir/ValueSet/route-codes`)
- `method : 0..1 SU CodeableConcept` (example bound to `http://hl7.org/fhir/ValueSet/administration-method-codes`)
- `doseAndRate : 0..* SU Element`
  - `type : 0..1 SU CodeableConcept` (example bound to `http://terminology.hl7.org/ValueSet/dose-rate-type`)
  - `dose[x] : 0..1 SU Range | SimpleQuantity | Expression`
  - `rate[x] : 0..1 SU Ratio | Range | SimpleQuantity | Expression`
- `maxDosePerPeriod : 0..* SU Ratio`
- `maxDosePerAdministration : 0..1 SU SimpleQuantity`
- `maxDosePerLifetime : 0..1 SU SimpleQuantity`
- `safety : 0..1 DosageSafety` *(new)*

**`DosageDetails` (new)**
- `renderedInstruction : 0..1 SU markdown` "Text summary of the entire dosage"
- `simple : 0..1 SU Dosage` "Dosage details if it is a simple dose"
- `step : 0..* SU Element` "One step in a sequence of steps that comprise the dosage course"
  - `start : 0..1 RelativeTime`
  - `end : 0..1 RelativeTime`
  - `count : 0..1 integer`
  - `component : 1..* SU Dosage`
  - `safety : 0..1 SU DosageSafety` "Safety information about this step of the dose course"
- `safety : 0..1 SU DosageSafety` "Safety information about the combined dose course"

**`DosageSafety` (new)**
- `doseLimit : 0..* SU Element`
  - `quantity[x] : 1..1 SU integer | Quantity | Expression`
  - `scope : 1..1 SU code` *required* binding to `http://hl7.org/fhir/ValueSet/dose-limit-scope|6.0.0-ballot3` (`dosage | period | administration | lifetime`)
  - `period : 0..1 SU Duration`
  - `text : 0..1 SU string`
- `ifExceeded : 0..1 SU string` "What to do if the instructions lead to exceeding the dose limits"

**`DosageCondition` (new, referenced by `Dosage.condition`)** — used to express conditional rules like *"take if tolerated"*. The committed source already includes this datatype together with its supporting `codesystem-dosage-condition-code.xml` / `valueset-dosage-condition-code.xml` / `valueset-dosage-condition-details.xml` (see `source/datatypes/`).

**Resource re-pointing**
- `MedicationRequest.dosageInstruction : 0..* Dosage` → `0..1 DosageDetails`
- `MedicationRequest.renderedDosageInstruction` → removed (folded into `DosageDetails.renderedInstruction`).
- Same change to `MedicationDispense.dosageInstruction` and `MedicationStatement.dosageInstruction`.

**Worked Symbicort example (illustrative target shape)**
```
DosageDetails
  renderedInstruction = "1 puff BD maintenance, 1 puff PRN, max 8 puffs/day"
  simple = Dosage(text="1 puff twice a day", timing=BID, doseAndRate.dose=1 puff)
  step (PRN regime) = component=Dosage(text="1 puff as needed", asNeeded=true, doseAndRate.dose=1 puff)
  safety.doseLimit = { quantity=8 puffs, scope=period, period=1 day,
                       text="combined cap across maintenance and PRN" }
```

### Design Rationale

- A separate **wrapper** (`DosageDetails`) cleanly fixes the original gap: a single `safety` slot now spans every concurrent `Dosage` component instead of being attached to one of them. The Pharmacy WG explicitly preferred this over piling `maxDosePerPeriod` cardinality changes onto `Dosage` itself.
- A separate **`DosageSafety`** datatype keeps the safety vocabulary (`scope` code, `Expression`-typed quantities) out of the dosing path proper, and lets per-step *and* combined-course caps share one structure.
- The `step / component` shape was chosen over re-using `sequence` (and motivates dropping `sequence` from `Dosage`) so that titration courses, weekday/weekend splits, and the costateixeira Mon-Fri/Sat-Sun example all express ordering through structure rather than through magic integer alignment.
- Required binding of `DosageSafety.doseLimit.scope` to the four-code `dose-limit-scope` value set ensures interoperable interpretation of *whether* a limit is per-administration, per-period, per-dosage, or per-lifetime — a known foot-gun in R5.
- `Dosage.condition : ConditionDetails` was added in the same pass because the WG recognized that several concurrent regimens are also conditional (e.g., "take this only if tolerated"); pulling that out of free-text `text` is consistent with the same modelling philosophy.
- Alternatives considered (per Zulip threads 553693224 *committers > nested element in a data type* and 554878545 *Medication > Dosage*): a flat extension on `Dosage` (rejected — extensions across concurrent instances cannot share a target without an out-of-band linking convention), and the Swedish National Medication List one-level-nesting pattern (referenced as inspiration but rejected as not expressive enough for multi-step + combined-safety).

### Breaking Changes

Yes — multiple. The resolution explicitly calls these out and requires a balloter note flagging them.

- `Dosage.sequence : 0..1 integer` is **removed**. Any instance using it is invalid in R6.
- `MedicationRequest.dosageInstruction : 0..* Dosage` → `0..1 DosageDetails`. Cardinality is reduced *and* the type changes — instances that currently carry multiple `Dosage` entries must be reshaped under `DosageDetails.simple` / `DosageDetails.step.component`.
- `MedicationDispense.dosageInstruction` and `MedicationStatement.dosageInstruction` change identically.
- `MedicationRequest.renderedDosageInstruction` is **removed**, replaced by `DosageDetails.renderedInstruction : markdown`. (The previous element was string; widening to markdown is itself an instance-shape change.)
- `DosageSafety.doseLimit.scope` is bound *required* (versioned `|6.0.0-ballot3`) — once introduced, downstream profiles cannot loosen the binding.
- New `Dosage.condition : 0..1 ConditionDetails` and `Dosage.safety : 0..1 DosageSafety` are additive (non-breaking on their own), but the introduction of `DosageDetails` between resources and `Dosage` is breaking for every consumer that walks `Medication{Request,Dispense,Statement}.dosageInstruction.*`.

### Open Questions

- **Search parameters.** The resolution does not specify how `MedicationRequest`-style search parameters that pierce `dosageInstruction` should be re-expressed under `DosageDetails` (e.g., a search by `code`, `route`, or `timing`). FHIR-I will need a clear position before ballot.
- **Profile migration guidance.** The balloter note flags the breaking change but does not specify a migration path for downstream profiles (US Core, IPS, AU Base, etc.). Worth deciding whether a short *Migrating from R5 Dosage* page is required next to `dosage-examples.html`.
- **Versioned binding pin.** `DosageSafety.doseLimit.scope` is bound `|6.0.0-ballot3`. Confirm the WG wants the version pin retained post-ballot or relaxed to unversioned before normative.
- **Limitations boundary.** The costateixeira "Mon-Fri / Sat-Sun + 3-week roll-down" example deliberately exceeds the *Limitations of Use*. Decide whether to leave it as an *aspirational* example showing the redirect to `PlanDefinition` / `RequestOrchestration`, or to drop it entirely from `dosage-examples.html`.
- **Excel artefact stability.** The four `source/datatypes/dosage*.xml` spreadsheets produce huge diff churn whenever Excel reflows them (`9ccfe116a7 FHIR-51540 - close excel`). The WG should confirm the canonical save settings to keep PR review tractable; this is a publisher-tooling concern, not a content one.
- **Cross-ticket coordination.** FHIR-53373 already shipped together with FHIR-51540 in commit `22a7c84819`; FHIR-54523, FHIR-54651, FHIR-54014, FHIR-54848, FHIR-56016 all touched dosage-area files post-merge. None obviously conflict with the resolution above, but the merge author should re-confirm before backporting any further changes onto this branch.
