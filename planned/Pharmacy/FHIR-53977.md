## Resolution Summary

Resolution: **Persuasive**. The reporter identified three minor
grammatical errors on the MedicationStatement page (sections 14.4.1 and
14.4.2 of the resource page) that should be corrected:

1. **14.4.2 (Boundaries and Relationships table):** "MedicationStatement
   is … a report that such a sequence did take place, resulting **is** a
   belief that …" → change `is` to `in`. This also makes the sentence
   match the parallel phrasing already correct in 14.4.1.
2. **14.4.1 (Scope and Usage):** "These should **[be]** recorded as
   Social History Observations" → insert the missing word `be` before
   `recorded`.
3. **14.4.2 (Boundaries and Relationships):** "… record **a**
   medications or substances …" → drop the indefinite article `a` so it
   agrees with the plural noun.

No comments were attached when the resolution was applied; the reporter
text itself is the resolution scope.

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

The MedicationStatement resource page contains three minor grammatical
errors in its narrative prose (introduction / boundaries-and-relationships
sections). They are typographical only and do not affect resource
semantics, but they are visible to every implementer reading the
specification and the reporter has flagged them explicitly with verbatim
quotes.

### Proposed Change

Make three text-only edits to the MedicationStatement narrative prose
that backs sections 14.4.1 and 14.4.2 of the rendered resource page.
The phrases to be corrected (verified by direct grep against the cache
clone of `HL7/fhir` at `source/medicationstatement/medicationstatement-introduction.xml`)
are:

1. `resulting is a belief` → `resulting in a belief`
   (line ~20 of `medicationstatement-introduction.xml`)
2. `These should recorded as Social History Observations` →
   `These should be recorded as Social History Observations`
   (line ~11 of `medicationstatement-introduction.xml`)
3. `record a medications or substances` →
   `record medications or substances`
   (line ~30 of `medicationstatement-introduction.xml`)

No StructureDefinition, ValueSet, search parameter, example, or
operation changes are required.

> **Note on file location:** The above paths were located by raw `grep`
> against the cache clone, **not** confirmed against the per-repo
> briefing's Artifact Map. The plan must be re-verified after
> `repo-analysis` produces a current briefing — in particular to
> confirm whether `medicationstatement-introduction.xml` is the only
> authoring file backing sections 14.4.1 and 14.4.2 (vs. e.g.
> `medicationstatement-notes.xml` or a sibling page-content file).

### Design Rationale

This is a `Technical Correction` with a `Persuasive` resolution — the
fix is mechanical and non-controversial. The corrected wording in fix
(1) explicitly aligns 14.4.2 with the existing correct phrasing in
14.4.1 ("such a sequence (or at least a part of it) did take place,
resulting in a belief") that already exists later in the same file
(line ~58). No alternative wording was discussed in the ticket.
