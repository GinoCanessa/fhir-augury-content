## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

HL7 FHIR specification pages are required to avoid abbreviated or contracted forms so that the text is unambiguous for international readers and consistent with HL7 editorial style. The `Dosage Examples` page (`source/dosage-examples.html`) contains several English contractions — `it's`, `don't`, `There's`, `shouldn't`, `There's` — that the automated QA sweep (Comment 295) flagged. These need to be rewritten in their fully spelled-out forms.

### Proposed Change

Edit `source/dosage-examples.html` in `HL7/fhir` and expand the five contractions identified in the clone at HEAD `db79dcd`. The exact occurrences (typographic apostrophe `’` / U+2019 in every case) are:

| Line | Current text fragment | Replacement |
|------|-----------------------|-------------|
| 1329 | `that does not appear to be computable (it’s skin surface area for the patient)` | `that does not appear to be computable (it is skin surface area for the patient)` |
| 1463 | `Retail tri-phase contraceptives don’t work like this` | `Retail tri-phase contraceptives do not work like this` |
| 1464 | `There's no way to represent 'take the right numbered pill for the day of your cycle' in the DosageDetails structure` | `There is no way to represent 'take the right numbered pill for the day of your cycle' in the DosageDetails structure` |
| 2567 | `implementations that try to use this extension shouldn’t expect the event to actually be understood,` | `implementations that try to use this extension should not expect the event to actually be understood,` |
| 2845 | `There’s no hint as to what the 30% is about` | `There is no hint as to what the 30% is about` |

Notes:

- Line 1464 uses an ASCII `'` in `There's`; all other occurrences use the typographic right single quotation mark `’` (U+2019). The replacement removes the apostrophe entirely — no apostrophe character is left in the expanded form.
- The literal phrase `'take the right numbered pill for the day of your cycle'` on line 1464 contains single quotes used as quotation marks, **not** contractions, and must be left unchanged.
- The ticket description only names `"shouldn't"` as the automated trigger, but the resolution explicitly enumerates `there's`, `shouldn't`, `it's` and ends with `...` indicating the author intends to sweep all comparable contractions on the page. The five entries above are every occurrence detected by an apostrophe scan of this file; fixing all of them satisfies the resolution without leaving a follow-up ticket.
- No other files on the page are affected: there are no generated tables, no example XML/JSON/Turtle, and no extension or value-set references changed. The change is pure prose.

### Design Rationale

The HL7 editorial convention (and the trigger for the automated QA ticket class to which FHIR-55331 belongs) is to spell out contractions. Replacement text is the neutral expansion ("it is", "do not", "There is", "should not") that preserves meaning and sentence rhythm without introducing new technical claims. Because the change is editorial only, there is no alternative design worth considering beyond wording choice; the resolution wording settles that.
