## Resolution Summary

Resolution: **Persuasive**.

Verbatim resolution: *"Add element of 'compositeScoring' 0..1 on MeasureReport.group as a CodeableConcept extensibly bound to https://terminology.hl7.org/7.0.1/ValueSet-composite-measure-scoring.html."*

Concretely this means adding a new sibling element under `MeasureReport.group` (alongside existing `scoring`, `improvementNotation`, etc.) with:

- Path: `MeasureReport.group.compositeScoring`
- Cardinality: `0..1`
- Type: `CodeableConcept`
- Binding: `extensible` to `http://terminology.hl7.org/ValueSet/composite-measure-scoring`

The element mirrors the existing `Measure.group.compositeScoring` (see `source/measure/structuredefinition-Measure.xml`, lines 1250–1272), so that a MeasureReport can carry through the composite-scoring method declared by the reporting Measure.

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

`Measure.group.compositeScoring` has existed for some time (extensibly bound to the `composite-measure-scoring` ValueSet) and captures how component measures are combined for a composite measure. However, `MeasureReport.group` currently only carries `scoring` (`MeasureReport.group.scoring`, lines 556–579 of `source/measurereport/structuredefinition-MeasureReport.xml`) and has no counterpart for composite scoring. Consumers of a MeasureReport for a composite measure therefore cannot tell — from the report alone — which composite scoring method was used, and must resolve and dereference the associated `Measure` to interpret the score. This is inconsistent with how `scoring` and `improvementNotation` are already duplicated into MeasureReport for self-describing interpretation.

### Proposed Change

Add a new element to the MeasureReport differential:

- **Id / Path:** `MeasureReport.group.compositeScoring`
- **Short:** `opportunity | all-or-nothing | linear | weighted` (match the Measure wording).
- **Definition:** "If this is a report of a composite measure, the scoring method used to combine the component measures to determine the composite score. When present, SHOULD match the `compositeScoring` of the corresponding group in the Measure being reported." (Wording parallels the `scoring` element's cross-reference to Measure.)
- **Cardinality:** `0..1`
- **Type:** `CodeableConcept`
- **isSummary:** `true` (parallel to `MeasureReport.group.scoring` at lines 556–579 and to `Measure.group.compositeScoring` at lines 1250–1272).
- **isModifier:** `false`.
- **Binding:**
  - Name: `CompositeMeasureScoring` (via the `elementdefinition-bindingName` extension).
  - Strength: `extensible`.
  - ValueSet: `http://terminology.hl7.org/ValueSet/composite-measure-scoring`.
  - Description: "The composite scoring method of the measure."
- **Placement:** between `MeasureReport.group.scoring` (ends at line 579) and `MeasureReport.group.improvementNotation` (begins at line 580) in `source/measurereport/structuredefinition-MeasureReport.xml`. This mirrors the Measure ordering (scoring → … → compositeScoring) while keeping the new element adjacent to `scoring`, which is the most natural grouping for a consumer.

Illustrative XML snippet (differential-only; the publisher regenerates `<snapshot>`):

```xml
<element id="MeasureReport.group.compositeScoring">
  <path value="MeasureReport.group.compositeScoring"/>
  <short value="opportunity | all-or-nothing | linear | weighted"/>
  <definition value="If this is a report of a composite measure, the scoring method used to combine the component measures to determine the composite score. It is expected to be the same as the compositeScoring element on the referenced Measure."/>
  <min value="0"/>
  <max value="1"/>
  <type>
    <code value="CodeableConcept"/>
  </type>
  <isSummary value="true"/>
  <binding>
    <extension url="http://hl7.org/fhir/StructureDefinition/elementdefinition-bindingName">
      <valueString value="CompositeMeasureScoring"/>
    </extension>
    <strength value="extensible"/>
    <description value="The composite scoring method of the measure."/>
    <valueSet value="http://terminology.hl7.org/ValueSet/composite-measure-scoring"/>
  </binding>
</element>
```

### Design Rationale

- **Symmetry with Measure.** `Measure.group.compositeScoring` already exists with the exact shape specified by the resolution (CodeableConcept, 0..1, extensible binding to `composite-measure-scoring`). Reusing the same element name, type, cardinality, and binding on MeasureReport keeps the two resources aligned and matches the existing pattern for `scoring` and `improvementNotation`, both of which are duplicated on MeasureReport so that a report is self-describing.
- **Extensible binding.** The resolution is explicit: the binding is `extensible`, matching `Measure.group.compositeScoring` and the other scoring-related MeasureReport bindings. A stronger binding would be inconsistent with how composite scoring is bound on Measure.
- **0..1 cardinality.** Composite scoring is only meaningful for composite measures; most MeasureReports will omit it. `0..1` matches both the resolution text and the Measure counterpart.
- **No new ValueSet / CodeSystem needed.** The binding is to `http://terminology.hl7.org/ValueSet/composite-measure-scoring`, which is already maintained in THO/UTG and already referenced from Measure — no additions to HL7/UTG are required.

### Open Questions

- **Exact placement order**: the plan places `compositeScoring` immediately after `scoring` (before `improvementNotation`) on MeasureReport; on Measure, `compositeScoring` sits considerably later in the element list (line 1250 vs. other `group` children). The resolution does not dictate ordering. If the work group wants strict positional parity with Measure, the insertion point should move to just before `MeasureReport.group.component`-equivalent territory — but MeasureReport has no `component` element, so "next to scoring" is the cleanest placement. Confirm with CQI before merging.
- **Definition wording**: the proposed `definition` text is adapted from the Measure wording plus the "expected to be the same as …" phrasing used for `MeasureReport.group.scoring`. CQI may prefer verbatim reuse of the Measure wording without the cross-reference sentence. Either is acceptable against the resolution.
- **Example coverage**: the resolution does not require a new example. Decide whether to add a minimal composite-measure MeasureReport example as part of this change or to defer to a follow-up ticket.
