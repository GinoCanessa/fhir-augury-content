## Resolution Summary

Resolution: **Persuasive** — "Will fix."

The `codesystem` specification page contains two misspellings of "hierarchical" / "hierarchies", written as `poly-heirarchical` and `poly-heirarchies`. The reporter (Lloyd McKenzie) requests that the misspelled / abbreviated words be corrected and fully spelled out. Concretely, the agreed change is to correct the spelling of the two occurrences so that they read `poly-hierarchical` and `poly-hierarchies` respectively. This is a `TEXTONLY` correction — narrative prose only, no model, binding, or conformance change.

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

The published `codesystem.html` page (CodeSystem resource specification, Synonyms and Alternate Codes section) contains a typo: "heirarch" instead of "hierarch". The text currently reads:

> "Another reason for duplicated concepts is to deal with implied poly-heirarchical concepts in systems that do not support formal poly-heirarchies."

These misspellings appear in a normative-page narrative paragraph and reduce the editorial quality of the specification. Because the source is checked in as authored HTML (not generated), the misspellings will continue to appear in every build until corrected.

### Proposed Change

Correct two occurrences of the misspelling in the authored include fragment that is rendered into the `codesystem.html` page:

- `source/codesystem/codesystem-notes.xml`, line 378: replace `poly-heirarchical` with `poly-hierarchical`.
- `source/codesystem/codesystem-notes.xml`, line 379: replace `poly-heirarchies` with `poly-hierarchies`.

After the change, the paragraph will read:

> "Another reason for duplicated concepts is to deal with implied poly-hierarchical concepts in systems that do not support formal poly-hierarchies."

No other content (sentence structure, anchors, surrounding markup) changes. No StructureDefinition, ValueSet, CodeSystem, search parameter, example, binding, invariant, or operation is touched.

### Design Rationale

This is a straightforward editorial fix marked `TEXTONLY` and labeled `Persuasive` with resolution "Will fix". There is no design alternative to consider — the words are simply misspelled English. Limiting the change to the two offending tokens preserves the existing prose and avoids introducing collateral changes that could complicate review or regenerate downstream artifacts. A repo-wide check for the same misspelling pattern (`heirarch`) on other pages is included as a safety task so the same fix is not later requested on other pages.
