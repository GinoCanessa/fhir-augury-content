## Resolution Summary

**Resolution:** Persuasive.

Per the resolution description: *"In alignment with the Sept 2025 Thurs Q1 vote, Condition is planned to go normative in R6"* (see the [2025 Sept Patient Care WGM Agenda and Minutes](https://confluence.hl7.org/spaces/PC/pages/358875423/2025+Sept+Patient+Care+WGM+Agenda+and+Minutes)).

The approved change is to promote the `Condition` resource's FHIR Maturity Level (FMM) to Normative, with `6.0.0` as its first normative version. The one comment on the ticket (jlyle, 2026-03-12) notes that "We need a consistent approach for these Normative tickets, which may involve leaving it to the R6 ballot." — i.e., the mechanics of how/when the flip lands in source should be coordinated with the other R6 normative promotions, but the Patient Care decision itself is affirmative.

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

`Condition` has been one of the most widely-implemented FHIR resources since DSTU2 and is a foundational clinical resource (problems, diagnoses, health concerns). Despite this maturity and cross-realm use, it is still published as Trial Use (FMM 3 / `standards-status = trial-use` in prior releases) rather than Normative. Patient Care voted in the Sept 2025 WGM (Thurs Q1) to advance it to Normative for the R6 release so that implementers have a stable, backward-compatible base going forward.

In the current clone (`HL7/fhir` @ `db79dcd`) the Condition StructureDefinition already carries `standards-status = normative` (line 12) but the surrounding metadata is inconsistent with a fully-normative resource — FMM is still `3`, the `structuredefinition-normative-version` extension is missing, the resource `<status>` is `draft`, and `source/fhir.ini` does not list `Condition` in either the `[normative-package]` or `[first-normative-version]` blocks. This ticket asks that Condition's maturity metadata be reconciled to fully reflect Normative status for R6.

### Proposed Change

Align every maturity-level touchpoint for `Condition` with the pattern used by already-normative clinical resources (`Patient`, `Observation`):

1. **`source/condition/structuredefinition-Condition.xml`**
   - Keep `structuredefinition-standards-status = normative` (already present, line 12).
   - **Add** a `structuredefinition-normative-version` extension with `valueCode = 6.0.0` (immediately after `standards-status`, mirroring `source/patient/structuredefinition-Patient.xml` line 14 and `source/observation/structuredefinition-Observation.xml` line 13).
   - **Change** `structuredefinition-fmm` from `<valueInteger value="3"/>` to `<valueInteger value="5"/>` (line 15 today; Patient/Observation are both `5`).
   - **Change** `<status value="draft"/>` to `<status value="active"/>` (line 33 today; Patient/Observation are both `active`).

2. **`source/fhir.ini`**
   - `[first-normative-version]` (line 949): **add** `Condition=6.0.0` alongside the existing `Patient=4.0.0` / `Observation=4.0.0` entries.
   - `[normative-package]` (line 931): **add** `Condition=patient` (grouping Condition into the `patient` normative package, consistent with the Patient Care / patient-centred nature of the resource; the final package name is a coordination question — see Open Questions).
   - `[fmm]` already has `condition=5` (line 817) — no change needed.
   - `[draft-resources]` (line 137) does not list Condition — no change needed.

3. **`source/condition/bundle-Condition-search-params.xml`**
   - Every SearchParameter already has `structuredefinition-standards-status = normative` (23 entries confirmed). **No change**, but this should be revalidated after promotion because the publisher cross-checks SP status against parent-resource status.

4. **Child ValueSets under `source/condition/`** — not changed by this ticket, but recorded here for visibility (see Open Questions):
   - Already `normative`: `valueset-condition-clinical.xml`, `valueset-condition-ver-status.xml`, `valueset-condition-severity.xml`.
   - Still `informative`: `valueset-condition-category.xml`, `valueset-condition-code.xml`, `valueset-condition-outcome.xml`, `valueset-condition-stage-type.xml`, `valueset-condition-stage.xml`. The resolution scope is the resource's maturity level, not the bound ValueSets, so these stay as-is unless Patient Care issues a follow-up.

5. **`source/condition/condition-introduction.xml`** — update the ballot-note block at the top (currently lists FHIR-54914 / FHIR-54454 as changes since ballot4) to add an entry announcing the Normative promotion under "Non-Compatible Changes" → "Standards Status changed from Trial Use to Normative" with a link back to FHIR-32048.

### Design Rationale

- The pattern used here matches the exact metadata shape emitted for the two previously-normative clinical resources in this repo (Patient and Observation), which is the only precedent for a resource flipping to Normative inside this publisher's bespoke pipeline. Using that same shape avoids publisher surprises and keeps QA clean.
- `first-normative-version = 6.0.0` is the correct value per the resolution text ("planned to go normative in R6") and matches the `<version value="6.0.0"/>` and `<fhirVersion value="6.0.0"/>` already in `structuredefinition-Condition.xml`.
- The jlyle comment raises a sequencing concern about coordinating all R6 Normative flips; that is an implementation-timing concern, not a design choice — the proposal itself is unambiguous.

  - `source/condition/bundle-Condition-search-params.xml` — no edit; revalidate only.
- **Change Scope:** Moderate. The edits themselves are small, but promotion to Normative is a policy event with downstream consequences for implementers (immutability expectations) and for the publisher QA (all child artifacts must be consistent).

### Breaking Changes

Promotion to Normative is, by HL7 policy, a **non-breaking** change on its own — the content of the resource does not change; only its maturity/commitment level rises. However, the *downstream consequence* is breaking-adjacent and must be called out: once Normative, future backward-incompatible changes to Condition (element removals, cardinality tightening, type changes, required-binding additions) are no longer permitted without a follow-up normative-substantive process. Any in-flight Condition tickets proposing such changes must land **before** this ticket is published for R6, or be deferred.

No breaking changes are introduced by the edits in this plan.

## Implementation Plan

### Prerequisites

- Confirm with Patient Care (per the jlyle comment) that the Normative flip is landing in the R6 source tree now, rather than being sequenced with other Normative tickets at ballot cutover.
- Confirm the `[normative-package]` group name for Condition (see Open Questions).
- Ensure no other in-flight Condition tickets are proposing backward-incompatible changes for R6.

### Step-by-Step Tasks

#### HL7/fhir

1. **Add `normative-version` and bump FMM to 5 in the Condition StructureDefinition.**
   - File: `source/condition/structuredefinition-Condition.xml`
   - Action: After the `structuredefinition-standards-status` extension (currently at line 10–12), insert a `structuredefinition-normative-version` extension with `valueCode = 6.0.0`. Then change the `structuredefinition-fmm` extension's `<valueInteger>` from `3` to `5`.
   - Details: Exact shape to match `source/patient/structuredefinition-Patient.xml` lines 10–18. Final ordering: `category` → `standards-status` → `normative-version` → `fmm` → `security-category` → `wg` → `entered-in-error-status`. Do **not** edit any `<snapshot>` block further down in the file — it is regenerated.

2. **Flip resource status from `draft` to `active` in the Condition StructureDefinition.**
   - File: `source/condition/structuredefinition-Condition.xml`
   - Action: Change `<status value="draft"/>` (line 33) to `<status value="active"/>`.
   - Details: Matches Patient (line 36) and Observation (line 34). This is a `StructureDefinition.status` change, not a maturity extension.

3. **Register Condition's first normative version in `fhir.ini`.**
   - File: `source/fhir.ini`
   - Action: Under `[first-normative-version]` (line 949), add `Condition=6.0.0` in alphabetical-ish order alongside `Patient=4.0.0` and `Observation=4.0.0`.
   - Details: Keep the existing entries exactly as they are; just append the new line before the blank line that closes the block.

4. **Register Condition in the normative package list in `fhir.ini`.**
   - File: `source/fhir.ini`
   - Action: Under `[normative-package]` (line 931), add `Condition=<package>` where `<package>` is the Patient Care-chosen grouping (likely `patient`, by analogy with `Patient=patient` on line 946; see Open Questions).
   - Details: Do not touch the existing entries. This section controls which published normative bundle the resource ships inside.

5. **Announce the Normative promotion in the Condition intro page's ballot note.**
   - File: `source/condition/condition-introduction.xml`
   - Action: In the `<blockquote class="ballot-note" id="bn1">` block at the top, add a new `<li>` under "Non-Compatible Changes" reading roughly: `<a href="https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-32048">FHIR-32048</a>: Condition promoted from Trial Use to Normative (first normative version 6.0.0).` Replace the current `<li>None</li>` placeholder under Non-Compatible Changes. Also add the same entry to the "All Changes" list immediately below.
   - Details: Prose-only edit in authored HTML; no publisher schema to worry about. Keep the existing FHIR-54914 and FHIR-54454 entries in their respective sections.

6. **Re-run publisher QA and revalidate search-parameter coherence.**
   - File: `source/condition/bundle-Condition-search-params.xml` (no edit)
   - Action: After tasks 1–5 land, run the publisher locally (`publish.bat` / `publish.sh` per the briefing) and confirm there are no new QA warnings for Condition — in particular that every SP's `standards-status = normative` now lines up with the parent resource's now-fully-normative status.
   - Details: This step is validation, not authoring. Any QA warnings introduced indicate a missed metadata touchpoint.

### Open Questions

- **Sequencing.** Per jlyle's 2026-03-12 comment, Patient Care wants a consistent approach across all R6 Normative promotions. Should this ticket be committed now in isolation, or batched with the other Normative-flip tickets at the R6 ballot cutover? The answer determines whether to land these edits on `master` immediately or on a coordination branch.
- **`[normative-package]` grouping.** `source/fhir.ini` already has `Patient=patient`, `Observation=observation`, plus three infrastructure and three conformance packages. Should Condition join `patient` (grouped with Patient), start a new `condition` / `clinical` package, or land under an existing clinical grouping Patient Care prefers? The task list assumes `patient` pending confirmation.
- **Child ValueSet maturity.** Five Condition-child ValueSets are still `informative` (`-category`, `-code`, `-outcome`, `-stage-type`, `-stage`). The resolution does not address these, so the plan leaves them alone — but Patient Care may want a paired follow-up to align bound ValueSets with the resource's Normative status.
- **Ballot-note wording.** Task 5 proposes a concrete phrasing; Patient Care may have a house style or a standard sentence used for the other R6 Normative promotions that should be reused verbatim.
