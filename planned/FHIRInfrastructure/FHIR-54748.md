## Resolution Summary

Resolution: **Not Persuasive with Modification**.

The reporter requested removing `ExampleScenario.actor` and replacing it with a
required link to `ActorDefinition`. The committee declined that change for two
reasons documented in the resolution:

1. ExampleScenario instances will not always have an `ActorDefinition` defined,
   and requiring authors to create one (even as a contained resource) is "a fair
   amount of overhead".
2. The `title` and `description` of an actor in the context of a particular
   ExampleScenario may be more refined or specific than what is found on the
   `ActorDefinition` itself, so these elements still have value.

The agreed modification is: **add a `requirements` note on the `title` and
`description` elements of `ExampleScenario.actor` that explains this design
decision.** No structural change is made to `ExampleScenario.actor`; the
`actor.definition` element (canonical reference to `ActorDefinition`) remains
optional, as today.

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

`ExampleScenario.actor` carries `key`, `type`, `title`, `description`, and
`definition` (a canonical reference to `ActorDefinition`). The reporter
observed that this overlaps considerably with the standalone `ActorDefinition`
resource and proposed collapsing the in-resource fields in favour of a required
link to `ActorDefinition`. Today nothing in the specification explains *why*
the in-resource `title` and `description` are retained alongside the optional
`actor.definition` link, leaving readers to draw the same conclusion as the
reporter — that the elements are redundant.

### Proposed Change

In the `ExampleScenario` StructureDefinition (FHIR Core), add a
`<requirements>` child to two existing element definitions, populated with
prose that explains why the elements are kept in addition to the optional
`actor.definition` canonical reference to `ActorDefinition`. No element is
added, removed, retyped, or has its cardinality changed.

Concrete edits:

- `ExampleScenario.actor.title` (`source/examplescenario/structuredefinition-ExampleScenario.xml`,
  element id `ExampleScenario.actor.title`, currently lines 524–537) — insert a
  `<requirements value="..."/>` element immediately after `<definition>` and
  before `<min>`, with text along the lines of:

  > ExampleScenario does not require the actor to be backed by an
  > ActorDefinition (creating one — even as a contained resource — is a fair
  > amount of overhead and is not always available). The title given here may
  > also be more specific or refined for the particular scenario than the title
  > on the referenced ActorDefinition.

- `ExampleScenario.actor.description` (same file, element id
  `ExampleScenario.actor.description`, currently lines 538–550) — insert a
  parallel `<requirements value="..."/>` element after `<definition>` and
  before `<min>`, with text along the lines of:

  > ExampleScenario does not require the actor to be backed by an
  > ActorDefinition, so the description must be carryable directly on the
  > ExampleScenario. Even when an ActorDefinition is referenced via
  > `actor.definition`, the description may be refined or made more specific
  > for the role the actor plays in this particular scenario.

The exact wording is the responsibility of the implementer; the substance must
match the resolution's two bullet points (overhead avoidance + scenario-specific
refinement).

XML snippet illustrating the target shape (only the inserted line is new):

```xml
<element id="ExampleScenario.actor.title">
  <extension url="http://hl7.org/fhir/StructureDefinition/elementdefinition-translatable">
    <valueBoolean value="true"/>
  </extension>
  <path value="ExampleScenario.actor.title"/>
  <short value="Label for actor when rendering"/>
  <definition value="The human-readable name for the actor used when rendering the scenario."/>
  <requirements value="ExampleScenario does not require the actor to be backed by an ActorDefinition; creating one (even as a contained resource) is a fair amount of overhead and is not always available. Where actor.definition is supplied, the title here may still be more refined or scenario-specific than the title on the referenced ActorDefinition."/>
  <min value="1"/>
  <max value="1"/>
  ...
</element>
```

### Design Rationale

- **Smallest change that satisfies the disposition.** The committee explicitly
  voted *not* to restructure `ExampleScenario.actor`; they only agreed to
  document the rationale. A `requirements` note is the conventional place in
  StructureDefinition to record *why* an element exists, distinct from `short`
  (label) and `definition` (what it is) and `comment` (usage / interop notes).
- **Consistent with the rest of `ExampleScenario`.** Many existing top-level
  elements in this StructureDefinition already use `<requirements>` for the
  same purpose (e.g., `ExampleScenario.url` line 144, `.identifier` line 167,
  `.experimental` line 283, `.publisher` line 321, `.useContext` line 365).
  Adding `<requirements>` here continues that pattern.
- **Alternatives considered (and why not chosen).** Replacing `actor` with a
  required link to `ActorDefinition` (the reporter's original proposal) was
  rejected by the committee — see the two bullets in the resolution. Recording
  the rationale only in a `<comment>` was avoided because `comment` is meant
  for usage notes, not the design justification for the element's existence.

## Implementation Plan

### Prerequisites

None. The work is self-contained in `HL7/fhir`. No dependent ticket

### Step-by-Step Tasks

#### HL7/fhir

1. **Add `requirements` to `ExampleScenario.actor.title`**
   - File: `source/examplescenario/structuredefinition-ExampleScenario.xml`
   - Action: Inside the `<element id="ExampleScenario.actor.title">` block
     (currently lines 524–537), insert a new `<requirements value="..."/>`
     child immediately after the existing `<definition .../>` element and
     before `<min value="1"/>`.
   - Details: The `value` text must convey both points from the resolution:
     (a) ExampleScenario authors are not required to have an ActorDefinition,
     and creating one (even as a contained resource) is a fair amount of
     overhead, so the title must be carryable in-resource; and (b) when
     `actor.definition` *is* supplied, the title here may still be more refined
     or scenario-specific than the title on the referenced ActorDefinition.
     Mirror the placement, indentation, and self-closing form of existing
     `<requirements>` entries in the same file (e.g., line 144 on
     `ExampleScenario.url`).

2. **Add `requirements` to `ExampleScenario.actor.description`**
   - File: `source/examplescenario/structuredefinition-ExampleScenario.xml`
   - Action: Inside the `<element id="ExampleScenario.actor.description">`
     block (currently lines 538–550), insert a new
     `<requirements value="..."/>` child immediately after `<definition .../>`
     and before `<min value="0"/>`.
   - Details: Parallel wording to task 1, adapted for `description`: the
     description must be carryable directly on the ExampleScenario because an
     ActorDefinition is not required, and even when one is referenced via
     `actor.definition` the description may be refined for the actor's role in
     this particular scenario. Use the same `<requirements value="..."/>`
     self-closing form.

3. **Do not touch the `<snapshot>` block**
   - File: `source/examplescenario/structuredefinition-ExampleScenario.xml`
   - Action: Verify that no edit was made inside any `<snapshot>` element —
     only the `<differential>` `<element>` blocks identified above should
     change. The publisher will regenerate `<snapshot>` content.
   - Details: This is a standing FhirCore rule from the repo briefing;
     hand-edits to snapshot are out of scope and will be overwritten.
