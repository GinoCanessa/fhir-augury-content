## Resolution Summary

The committee accepted the request "Persuasive with Modification". The
existing **FHIRPath Additions** section currently lives only on the
QuestionnaireResponse page (`questionnaireresponse-notes.xml`, anchor
`#fhirpath`), even though the FHIRPath expressions it documents are
defined on the Questionnaire and merely *evaluated* in the context of a
QuestionnaireResponse. The agreed change has four concrete parts (verbatim
from the resolution):

1. **Move the FHIRPath Additions section to Questionnaire**, with the
   exception of the sentence
   *"When working with QuestionnaireResponses, there may be FHIRPath
   [expressions](https://build.fhir.org/metadatatypes.html#Expression)
   defined on the associated Questionnaire that will be evaluated in the
   context of the QuestionnaireResponse."*
2. As part of that move, change the lead-in sentence
   *"The following additional guidance applies to using FHIRPath with
   QuestionnaireResponses"* to
   *"The following additional guidance applies to using
   [FHIRPath](fhirpath.html) with Questionnaires and
   QuestionnaireResponses"*.
3. Place the retained sentence (the "When working with
   QuestionnaireResponses…" paragraph), with its outbound link pointing
   at the new FHIRPath guidance section in the Questionnaire page,
   into the **QuestionnaireResponse intro** as a separate paragraph
   immediately following the existing
   *"QuestionnaireResponse resources can be validated against their
   corresponding Questionnaire to verify that required groups and
   questions are answered…"* paragraph.
4. In that same intro paragraph, turn the word **validated** into a link
   to the validation section of the QuestionnaireResponse notes
   (`questionnaireresponse.html#validation`).

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

The FHIRPath guidance currently lives only on the QuestionnaireResponse
page, but the expressions it describes (the `%resource`, `%context`,
named-variable, `%questionnaire`, and `%qitem` rules) are *authored* on
Questionnaire items and only evaluated in the context of a
QuestionnaireResponse. Authors who consult the **Questionnaire** page
get no signal that this guidance exists or that it constrains how they
write their expressions, so the material is structurally misplaced and
hard to discover from the side that needs it most. The reporter notes
that the FHIRPath additions section "is just as valid on the
Questionnaire page, as on the QR page", and the WG agreed.

### Proposed Change

The change is editorial / documentation-only; no resource definitions,
search parameters, terminology, or code change. Concretely:

1. **Move** the existing `<a name="fhirpath">…<h3>FHIRPath
   Additions</h3>` section (and all of its `<ul>`/`<p>` body) from
   `source/questionnaireresponse/questionnaireresponse-notes.xml` to
   `source/questionnaire/questionnaire-notes.xml`. The destination page
   already has notes such as `#sdc`, `#adaptive`, `#qversusr`, `#math`,
   etc. — append the moved `#fhirpath` block as a peer section in that
   notes page (recommended placement: after `#math` and before
   `#workflow`, since the math section already discusses expressions).

2. **In the moved section**, change the lead-in sentence from:

   > The following additional guidance applies to using FHIRPath with
   > QuestionnaireResponses:

   to:

   > The following additional guidance applies to using
   > [FHIRPath](fhirpath.html) with Questionnaires and
   > QuestionnaireResponses:

   (Render `[FHIRPath]` as
   `<a href="fhirpath.html">FHIRPath</a>`. The page exists at
   `source/fhirpath.html`.)

3. **Drop** the original lead-in sentence
   *"When working with QuestionnaireResponses, there may be FHIRPath
   [expressions](metadatatypes.html#Expression) defined on the associated
   Questionnaire that will be evaluated in the context of the
   QuestionnaireResponse."* from the moved block — it is **not** part of
   the move. It is relocated to the QuestionnaireResponse intro instead
   (step 5 below).

4. **In `questionnaireresponse-notes.xml`**, remove the `<a
   name="fhirpath"> </a>`, `<h3>FHIRPath Additions</h3>`, and all of the
   following `<p>`/`<ul>` content that constituted that section, leaving
   the existing `<a name="profiling"> </a>` section as the last `<h3>`
   before `</div>`. (Optional but recommended: leave a behavior-neutral
   forwarding pointer in `questionnaireresponse-notes.xml` so anyone
   following old `questionnaireresponse.html#fhirpath` links is taken to
   the new location — see Open Questions.)

5. **In `questionnaireresponse-introduction.xml`**, immediately after
   the existing paragraph
   *"<b>QuestionnaireResponse</b> resources can be validated against
   their corresponding <a href="questionnaire.html">Questionnaire</a>
   to verify that required groups and questions are answered and that
   answers fit constraints in terms of cardinality, data type, etc."*,
   add a new paragraph:

   > When working with QuestionnaireResponses, there may be FHIRPath
   > [expressions](metadatatypes.html#Expression) defined on the
   > associated [Questionnaire](questionnaire.html#fhirpath) that will
   > be evaluated in the context of the QuestionnaireResponse.

   The link target on **Questionnaire** must point to the anchor of the
   newly moved FHIRPath Additions section (i.e.,
   `questionnaire.html#fhirpath`).

6. **Also in that intro paragraph**, change the bare word *"validated"*
   to a link to the validation section of the same rendered page:
   `<a href="#validation">validated</a>` (rendered as
   `questionnaireresponse.html#validation`, the anchor that already
   exists on `questionnaireresponse-notes.xml` line 57).

### Design Rationale

- The resolution explicitly chose option (b) from the original ticket
  ("Alternately move this section to Questionnaire, and create a summary
  here on QR referring to the moved content") rather than option (a)
  (duplicate / cross-link). This avoids drift between two copies of the
  same FHIRPath rules.
- Re-titling the moved section to "Questionnaires and
  QuestionnaireResponses" (rather than just QuestionnaireResponses)
  keeps the section accurate now that it lives on the Questionnaire
  page — expressions are authored on Questionnaire and *also* relevant
  on QR.
- The retained one-sentence pointer in the QuestionnaireResponse intro
  preserves discoverability for readers landing on the QR page first,
  which was the reporter's concern.
- Linking the existing word *"validated"* to `#validation` is a small
  navigation fix that the WG bundled in because the new paragraph sits
  next to it; it makes the intro internally consistent with the Notes
  section that defines validation behavior.

### Open Questions

- **Should a forwarding stub be left at the old `#fhirpath` anchor on
  `questionnaireresponse.html`?** The resolution does not address
  external/legacy deep-links. The briefing's Gotchas (the `#api`
  precedent on FML) recommend either updating callers or leaving a
  redirect; for the QR page a minimal stub — a one-line `<p>` such as
  *"FHIRPath additions guidance has moved to <a
  href="questionnaire.html#fhirpath">Questionnaire — FHIRPath
  Additions</a>."* preserved under an `<a name="fhirpath"> </a>` —
  would protect external SDC and training links at near-zero
  maintenance cost. **Recommendation:** include the stub in the same
  PR; flag for the WG if they prefer a hard removal.
- **Exact placement of the moved section in
  `questionnaire-notes.xml`.** The resolution says only *"move this
  section to Questionnaire"*; it does not specify where. The plan
  recommends placing it between `#math` and `#workflow` (expressions
  are already discussed in `#math`), but the WG editor may prefer
  placing it adjacent to `#advanced` (where SDC behavior is summarized)
  or at the very end of the notes page. This is editorial and can be
  resolved during PR review.
