The reported problem itself is unambiguous:

> Links in the section http://hl7.org/fhir/versioning.html#changes redirect to non-existing page http://fhir.org/implementations/usage.

A Persuasive resolution on a Technical Correction means the WG agreed the links are broken and must be fixed. ## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

`source/versioning.html` invites implementers to register their use of FHIR so HL7 can monitor adoption. The two anchors that carry that invitation both point at `http://fhir.org/implementations/usage`, which currently redirects to a non-existent page. The result: the "Strategies for handling Changes Between Versions" section on `http://hl7.org/fhir/versioning.html#changes` tells readers to register usage but gives them a dead link, undermining both implementer onboarding and the credibility of the specification's internal links.

Confirmed in the cached `HL7/fhir` clone at HEAD `db79dcd`:

- `source/versioning.html:231` — `usage <a href="http://fhir.org/implementations/usage">here</a>, which captures contact and other information ...`
- `source/versioning.html:233` — `(Note that implementers can advertise their use of FHIR <a href="http://fhir.org/implementations/usage">here</a>.)`

The same broken URL appears on a sibling page:

- `source/versions.html:164` — `we encourage implementers to <a href="http://fhir.org/implementations/usage">register their ...`
- `source/versions.html:613` — `usage <a href="http://fhir.org/implementations/usage">here</a>, which captures contact and other information ...`

The ticket text only names `versioning.html`, but the Technical Correction clearly applies to every copy of the dead URL in the authoring source; leaving the `versions.html` copies untouched would simply relocate the defect.

### Proposed Change

Replace every occurrence of `http://fhir.org/implementations/usage` in `source/*.html` with the current, working HL7 target for FHIR implementation/usage registration, and adjust the surrounding prose if the surviving destination no longer matches the "register your usage" framing.

Concretely:

1. In `source/versioning.html` lines 231 and 233, replace the `href="http://fhir.org/implementations/usage"` attribute values with the replacement URL approved by FHIR Infrastructure (the WG owning this ticket). If no functionally equivalent registration page exists, the sentence at line 230–234 (the paragraph following `<a name="changes"></a>` / `<h3>Strategies for handling Changes Between Versions</h3>`) must be rewritten to drop the "register your usage `here`" clause and the "implementers can advertise their use of FHIR `here`" note, rather than leaving a live but dangling anchor.
2. Apply the identical substitution at `source/versions.html` lines 164 and 613, to keep the two closely-related specification pages consistent.
3. Use `https://` (not `http://`) for the replacement URL, matching current FHIR spec practice for external HL7 links.

A repo-wide search for `fhir.org/implementations/usage` must return zero hits in `source/` once the change is applied.

### Design Rationale

- The ticket is a *Technical Correction* with resolution *Persuasive*: the correct scope is the narrowest edit that removes the broken reference without changing the surrounding normative guidance.
- The FhirCore briefing makes it clear that specification pages under `source/*.html` are authored content consumed directly by the HL7 publisher (no sushi / IG-publisher pipeline), so an inline HTML edit is both sufficient and idiomatic.
- Repointing rather than deleting is preferred where a replacement URL exists, because the "register your usage" mechanism is referenced from both `versioning.html` and `versions.html` and is part of the WG's monitoring strategy; removing the link entirely loses that signal.
- Applying the same fix to all four occurrences (two pages, four hrefs) is the only durable fix — otherwise the next link-check sweep re-opens the same ticket against `versions.html`.
