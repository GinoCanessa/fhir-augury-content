## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

Philips submitted editorial feedback on the FHIR R5 *Documents* specification page (`source/documents.html`) flagging awkward phrasing, inconsistent statements, and a misordered paragraph in the *Document Presentation* subsection. The original ticket also incidentally noted that the credits page misspelled the submitter's organisation as "Phillips". The page as-published was understandable but contained:

- Hard line breaks in the middle of sentences that hurt readability when rendered.
- A statement that the document bundle "include[s] both human readable and computer processable portions" which over-asserts (resources *may* include narrative; not all do).
- A paragraph defining "attested content" that appeared *after* the rule it logically constrained ("If the document is presented in a different order…"), making the constraint feel disconnected from its rationale.
- Identifier and date bullet lists whose two-sentence pattern ("This is found in *X*. It is …") was wordier than necessary.
- A missing call-out for the role of the subject resource's narrative, even though `Composition.subject` is mandatory and its narrative typically conveys patient identity for the document.
- A "Phillips" misspelling in `source/credits.html`.

R5 was published before this could be acted upon, so the ticket was deferred to R6 (per Lloyd McKenzie's 2023-03-30 comment) and then resolved in the R6 cycle with a curated subset of the second-review edits.

### Proposed Change

Apply the editorial changes captured in PR #2951 to two HTML authoring files. There are no XML/StructureDefinition, terminology, search-parameter, or example changes.

**1. `source/credits.html`** — single typo fix:

- Replace `<li>Bas van den Heuvel (Phillips)</li>` with `<li>Bas van den Heuvel (Philips)</li>`.

**2. `source/documents.html`** — editorial pass:

a. **Reflow soft-wrapped sentences** so the rendered HTML doesn't show stray spaces or hyphenation artefacts. Specifically, collapse the "IHE XDS" link onto one line, the "CapabilityStatement.document … rules defined on this page" sentence onto one line, and the "Parties entering into a trading agreement to do otherwise…" sentence with adjusted line wrapping.

b. **Tighten the Bulk Data exclusion paragraph** from:
> *"FHIR documents are not intended to capture unbounded data sets such as a full EHR. Rather, consider the Bulk Data specification for such use cases."*
to:
> *"FHIR documents are not intended to capture unbounded data sets such as all data stored in an EHR. Rather, consider the Bulk Data specification for use cases requiring access to such data sets."*

c. **Clarify the FHIR-document vs. DocumentReference paragraph** by adding the parenthetical *"(a document bundle containing a Composition resource and potentially other resources)"* immediately after "this document format", and changing "FHIR documents are for documents that are authored…" to "FHIR documents are used for documents that are authored…".

d. **Soften the human-readable assertion** in *Document Structure*:
- Was: "The resources include both human readable and computer processable portions."
- Now: "The resources may include both human readable and computer processable portions."

e. **Editorial micro-edits in *Document Structure*:**
- "The composition resource is the foundation of the clinical document. It:" → "The composition resource is the foundation of the clinical document, it:".
- "The composition set: The Composition resource, and the resources it links to" → "The composition set: the single Composition resource, and the resources it links to".

f. **Re-cast the identifier and date bullets** to a single-sentence form for both *Bundle.identifier* / *Composition.identifier* and *Bundle.timestamp* / *Composition.date* / *Composition.attester.time*. Example before/after:
- Was: "The document identifier (mandatory). This is found in *Bundle.identifier* and is globally unique…"
- Now: "The document identifier (mandatory) found in *Bundle.identifier*. It is globally unique…"
- Apply the same pattern to the Composition identifier, document date, Composition date, and Attestation dates bullets. The Attestation dates bullet pluralises to "These are found in *Composition.attester.time*…".

g. **Wrap the XML/JSON interchange note** (currently a single long `<p>`) onto multiple lines for source readability — content unchanged.

h. **Reorder the *Document Presentation* paragraphs** so that the constraint "If the document is presented in a different order…" appears immediately after the ordered list of presentation segments, *before* the "attested content" definition. Rewrite the attested-content paragraph as:
> *"The presentation of the document is called the 'attested content' of the document. The text of resources outside Composition and the subject resource are not considered attested content (e.g. a Condition resource). Specifically, the `Composition.attester` attests to the presented form of the document."*

i. **Add a new paragraph about the subject resource narrative**, placed after the Composition-narrative paragraph:
> *"The narrative of the subject resource (typically a Patient), referenced in 'Composition.subject' should clearly identify the subject and typically includes fields like name, identifier, etc."*

j. **Reflow the *Document Profiles* paragraph** so the "Documents can identify a profile…" sentence is on a single line.

The full set is the 39-line addition / 33-line deletion across these two files in PR #2951; net effect is +6 lines.

### Design Rationale

- The changes are purely editorial: they preserve every existing normative claim. The "may include" softening (item d) accurately reflects the spec — narrative on contained resources is allowed but not required — without weakening any SHALL.
- The new subject-narrative paragraph (item i) addresses a real gap noted by the Philips reviewers: `Composition.subject` is mandatory and its narrative typically carries patient-identifying information that consumers of a rendered document need, but the prior text only described `Composition`-level and `section.text` narratives.
- The reordering in *Document Presentation* (item h) places the "different order" constraint adjacent to the ordered presentation list it constrains, which is the natural reading order; the "attested content" definition then logically follows.
- "Persuasive with **Modification**" indicates that not every Philips comment was accepted — Rick Geimer's second-review document marks which were dropped or rephrased. The PR codifies only the surviving subset, which is why some original suggestions (e.g., the `cast` transform / `dateOp` items mentioned in other tickets) are not part of this change.
- The credits typo is bundled because it is trivial, was raised in the same submission, and touches no other content.
