## Resolution Summary

The ticket is a **Persuasive** technical correction reporting a missing word in
the ActorDefinition introduction prose. The reporter's existing/proposed
wording captures the entire change:

- **Existing wording:** "The intention of the ActorDefinition is that other
  business process languages will refer the actors and in doing so..."
- **Proposed wording:** "...will refer **to** the actors..."

No comments are recorded on the ticket. The applied fix is purely insertion
of the word "to" between "refer" and "the actors" in the ActorDefinition
introduction page.

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

The ActorDefinition introduction (authored prose, not a generated artifact)
was missing the preposition "to" in the sentence describing how other
business process languages reference actor definitions. The phrase "refer
the actors" is ungrammatical in this context; the intended idiom is "refer
**to** the actors". Ron G. Parker filed this technical correction to
restore the missing word.

### Proposed Change

Insert the word `to` so that the sentence reads:

> The intention of the ActorDefinition is that other business process
> languages will refer **to** the actors and in doing so, bring into scope
> the technical artifacts associated with the actor.

The original wording lived in
`source/actordefinition/actordefinition-introduction.xml` (confirmed via
`git log -S "will refer the actors"` against commit `63a82280f5` "Add
ActorDefinition and Requirements", which introduced the sentence).

> **Important finding from the cache clone (HL7/fhir @ `db79dcd`):** the
> sentence containing "will refer the actors" **no longer exists** in
> `source/actordefinition/actordefinition-introduction.xml`. It was
> superseded by **FHIR-44472 "ActorDefinition intro text"**, which
> rewrote the introduction (commits `31a9a1a458` on 2025-02-20 and
> `6659bb4d26` on 2025-02-26 by Grahame Grieve). The current intro now
> describes ActorDefinition usage with a bulleted list ("ActorDefinitions
> can be referenced in several places: ExampleScenarios, PlanDefinition,
> Requirements, obligation extension, CapabilityStatement…") and contains
> no occurrence of the offending phrase.
>
> Because FHIR-44472 (a separate, broader rewrite) has already deleted
> the sentence the reporter wanted to fix, **FHIR-44470's textual edit is
> already implicitly applied / moot**. The implementation work for this
> ticket reduces to a verification step rather than a new edit.

### Design Rationale

The resolution is a one-word grammatical correction with no design choice
to make. Because FHIR-44472 has already replaced the surrounding paragraph,
no further wording change is required to honour the spirit of FHIR-44470
(the missing-word condition no longer exists). The verification step below
exists to confirm that fact and to ensure the publisher output reflects it.
