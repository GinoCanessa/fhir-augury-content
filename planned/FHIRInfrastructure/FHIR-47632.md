## Resolution Summary

The resolution is **Persuasive**. The "Conformance Language" row in the
"Forward and backward compatibility" rules is to be reworded so that the
exception that permits introducing new `SHALL` statements is broadened
from version-conditional `SHALL`s alone to *also* include `SHALL`s that
hang off a newly-introduced optional element.

Verbatim from the Jira resolution description:

> Will change "new SHALL statements cannot be introduced unless they are
> conditional based on version. E.g. 'Instances in version x and higher
> of FHIR SHALL...'"
>
> to
>
> "new SHALL statements cannot be introduced unless they a) are
> conditional based on version (E.g. 'Instances in version x and higher
> of FHIR SHALL...'), or b) depend on a new optional element introduced
> in that version."

No code, schema, terminology, or example changes are required — only the
prose in `source/versions.html`.

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

The reporter (Bas van den Heuvel) noted that the current text in the
"Forward and backward compatibility" section of the FHIR specification
prohibits introducing any new `SHALL` statement unless it is gated on
version. In practice the WG already permits a second pattern: a new
optional element/feature introduced in a later version may carry its
own `SHALL` clauses that apply only when an implementer chooses to
support that optional feature (the ticket gives the example
"implementers SHOULD implement *amazing new feature*; when they do, the
following *wise thing* SHALL be done"). The current rule under-states
what the WG actually allows, so authors and reviewers reading the rule
literally would incorrectly flag such conditional `SHALL`s as a
backward-compatibility violation.

### Proposed Change

Update the single sentence inside the "Conformance Language" row of the
compatibility-rules table on `source/versions.html` (currently line 540)
to add the second permitted exception. Concrete edit:

- **File:** `source/versions.html`
- **Locate:** the `<tr>` whose first `<td>` contains
  `Conformance Language` (around line 537), specifically the body
  sentence on line 540 that begins
  `SHALL statements cannot be broken …`.
- **Replace** the clause
  `new SHALL statements cannot be introduced unless they are conditional based on version. e.g., "Instances in version x and higher of FHIR SHALL..."`
  with
  `new SHALL statements cannot be introduced unless they a) are conditional based on version (e.g., "Instances in version x and higher of FHIR SHALL..."), or b) depend on a new optional element introduced in that version.`
- **Leave intact** the trailing sentence about
  `Changes of SHOULD to MAY and vice versa as well as introducing new MAY and SHOULD statements are permitted.`

Resulting cell text (target state):

> SHALL statements cannot be broken (though they can be clarified) and
> new SHALL statements cannot be introduced unless they a) are
> conditional based on version (e.g., "Instances in version x and
> higher of FHIR SHALL..."), or b) depend on a new optional element
> introduced in that version. Changes of SHOULD to MAY and vice versa
> as well as introducing new MAY and SHOULD statements are permitted.

No other rows in the table, no surrounding paragraphs, and no other
pages are touched.

### Design Rationale

- The reworded text is a verbatim transcription of the Persuasive
  resolution recorded on the ticket; using the resolution's exact
  wording avoids re-litigating the substance.
- The two-clause `a) … or b) …` form mirrors the structure used
  elsewhere in `versions.html` for layered exceptions and keeps the
  existing version-conditional example intact for readers who already
  rely on it.
- Restricting the new exception to a *new* optional element preserves
  backward-compatibility guarantees: an implementer that does not adopt
  the new optional feature is unaffected, so the rule remains
  observationally backward-compatible.
- An alternative wording that simply said "unless gated on optionality"
  was not adopted because it would also cover *existing* optional
  elements, which would be a genuine breaking change for current
  implementers; the resolution deliberately scopes (b) to elements
  introduced in the same version.
