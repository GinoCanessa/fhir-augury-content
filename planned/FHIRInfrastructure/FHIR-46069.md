## Resolution Summary

**Resolution:** Persuasive.

The reporter flagged a one-character typo in the QuestionnaireResponse resource:
the word `respose` should read `response` in the description of the
`QuestionnaireResponse-source` SearchParameter
("The individual providing the information reflected in the questionnaire
**respose**" → "… questionnaire **response**").

The resolution requires correcting that typo wherever it appears in the
authored QuestionnaireResponse source.

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

The description text of the `source` SearchParameter on QuestionnaireResponse
contained a misspelling: "respose" instead of "response". This is purely
cosmetic — it does not affect search behaviour, code, or bindings — but it
appears in published artifacts (StructureDefinitions, the search-parameters
bundle, and the rendered HTML pages) where it is visible to implementers and
embarrassing for a normative-track resource.

### Proposed Change

Replace the single misspelled word in the SearchParameter description:

- **File:** `source/questionnaireresponse/bundle-QuestionnaireResponse-search-params.xml`
- **SearchParameter:** `QuestionnaireResponse-source`
  (URL `http://hl7.org/fhir/build/SearchParameter/QuestionnaireResponse-source`)
- **Element:** `SearchParameter.description`
- **Before:** `The individual providing the information reflected in the questionnaire respose`
- **After:**  `The individual providing the information reflected in the questionnaire response`

No other elements, cardinalities, types, bindings, or expressions change.
No new artifacts are introduced.

### Design Rationale

A minimal, in-place spelling correction is the only change consistent with a
"Technical Correction" of type "Persuasive". The corrected wording matches
the spelling already used in the equivalent text in earlier release histories
(R3/R4/R4B `tools/history/release*/...` snapshots all read "questionnaire
response"), so this restores consistency across versions rather than
introducing a new phrasing.
