## Resolution Summary

Resolution: **Persuasive** (no separate `resolution_description` was returned by FhirAugury; the approved change is the one described in the ticket body itself — standard handling for "Persuasive" dispositions where the reporter's proposal is accepted as-is).

Update the polling-vs-subscription portion of the FHIR exchange decision tree so that "do you support subscriptions?" is unambiguously read as **"can the data source emit, and the data consumer receive, subscription notifications?"** — i.e. only the notification half of the R5/R4B Subscription framework. Discovery of available subscription topics and creation of `Subscription` resources are **not** required to be in-band; they may be arranged out-of-band (e.g., by configuration, by deployment, or through a separate administrative channel) and the subscription branch of the decision tree is still the preferred option over polling in that case.

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

The FHIR exchange decision tree page (`source/exchanging.html`, §"Subscription capability?", anchor `#subscription`) currently poses the question as a binary "do you support subscriptions?" without qualifying what "support" means. The R5 Subscription framework spans several distinct capabilities:

1. Exposing the list of supported `SubscriptionTopic` resources for discovery.
2. Accepting `Subscription` resources dynamically from data consumers (topic-based subscription, REST create).
3. Emitting `SubscriptionStatus` / bundle notifications to a configured endpoint.
4. Receiving those notifications on the data consumer side.

The ticket's concern is that an implementer reading the current prose may assume they must support **all** of (1)–(4) to take the "subscription" branch of the decision tree, conclude that's out of reach, and fall through to polling unnecessarily. In reality, only (3)+(4) — the notification send/receive leg — materially affects the architectural choice captured by that decision point. Topics and subscription instances can be provisioned out-of-band (pre-configured on the server, negotiated via implementation guide, or managed by an administrator) and the subscription approach is still preferable to polling for the bandwidth, scale, and latency reasons already enumerated in the page's bullets.

Current anchor target (from the clone, `source/exchanging.html` lines 722–743):

```html
<a name="subscription"> </a>
<h4>Subscription capability?</h4>
<p>
  <i>Data consumer</i>-configured exchanges can take one of two forms - subscription or polling.  Subscription offers several benefits:
</p>
<ul>
  <li>It uses less bandwidth and processing power …</li>
  <li>Subscription scales better. …</li>
  <li>Subscription offers the potential of more immediate notification …</li>
</ul>
<p>
  However, subscription requires more sophisticated infrastructure on the part of both the <i>data source</i> and <i>data consumer</i>.  The <i>data source</i> must expose a <a href="subscription.html">subscription</a> end-point and must build in an event-detection mechanism …
</p>
…
<p>
  If subscriptions are appropriate, the next choice is whether the subscription notifications should <a href="#subscription-push">include data</a>.  If subscriptions are not viable, the appropriate solution is polling …
</p>
```

The phrase "must expose a subscription end-point" is exactly the wording the ticket wants softened — an end-point for *receiving subscription requests* is not required, only an end-point/mechanism for *emitting notifications*.

### Proposed Change

Edit the "Subscription capability?" section of `source/exchanging.html` (the decision-tree page anchor `#subscription`, starting at `<a name="subscription"> </a>` on line 722) as follows:

1. **Re-frame the opening question.** Immediately after the `<h4>Subscription capability?</h4>` heading, insert a short clarifying paragraph of roughly this form:

   > For the purpose of this decision, "subscription capability" means only that the *data source* can emit FHIR subscription notifications and the *data consumer* can receive them. It does **not** require that the *data source* expose `SubscriptionTopic` resources for discovery or accept `Subscription` resources created dynamically by the *data consumer* — those parts of the subscription framework may be managed out-of-band (e.g., through configuration, deployment, or an administrative channel) and the subscription approach is still preferable to polling when notification send/receive is available.

   Exact wording to be agreed during implementation; the paragraph should use the same `<i>data source</i>` / `<i>data consumer</i>` styling as the surrounding prose.

2. **Soften the "must expose a subscription end-point" wording (line 733).** Change the sentence in the paragraph beginning *"However, subscription requires more sophisticated infrastructure…"* so that the required infrastructure on the data source is described as:

   - an event-detection mechanism capable of triggering notifications when an event of interest occurs, and
   - a mechanism for delivering those notifications to the data consumer (push via a supported channel, or making them available for pull).

   Explicitly **remove or qualify** the requirement to "expose a subscription end-point" so that a server which has topics/subscriptions pre-configured (and therefore has no REST `Subscription` create endpoint open to consumers) is not excluded from the subscription branch. Keep the existing link to `subscription.html` for readers who do want the full framework.

3. **Touch up the consumer-side paragraph (line 739)** so that the consumer-side requirement is stated as "must be able to receive the agreed-upon subscription notifications (via the agreed channel)" rather than implying the consumer must first create a `Subscription` via REST.

4. **No change** to:
   - The three bullets on subscription benefits (lines 727–731) — they are still accurate.
   - The security paragraph (line 736) — it is about notification-time authorization, which is still in scope.
   - The decision-tree diagram node label ("Subscription capability?") — per the ticket, only the prose beneath the anchor needs clarification. The SVG in `source/fhir-exchanges.svg.html` is referenced by this anchor but not itself edited.
   - Outbound links from `exchanging-polling.html` line 22 and the various `exchanging-subscription.html` links — they continue to point at `exchanging.html#subscription`, which is the anchor being clarified.

No new anchors are introduced and no existing anchors are removed, so every inbound `exchanging.html#subscription` reference continues to resolve.

### Design Rationale

- The ticket is explicitly a **narrative clarification**, not a normative change. The decision tree already recommends subscription over polling "all things being equal"; the edit brings the prose in line with the way real-world FHIR deployments negotiate subscriptions (topics + `Subscription` resources frequently come from an IG or configuration, not a runtime REST handshake).
- Keeping the anchor (`#subscription`) and the heading text ("Subscription capability?") unchanged preserves every inbound deep link (e.g., `exchanging-polling.html` line 22, `exchanging-subscription.html` references, and the `#subscription` click target inside `fhir-exchanges.svg.html`).
- The reporter (Lloyd McKenzie) is also the assignee and the ticket carries the "Persuasive" disposition with no dissenting comments captured in FhirAugury, so there is no alternative proposal to weigh.
- Out of scope (deliberately): editing the Subscription resource page (`source/subscription.html`), the Subscription framework pages, or the `exchanging-subscription.html` walkthrough. The ticket targets only the decision-tree framing.
