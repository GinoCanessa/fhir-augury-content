## Resolution Summary

Resolution: **Persuasive**. The ticket reports a grammatical typo in the
ActorDefinition Resource's "Scope and Usage" prose:

- Existing wording: *"The ActorDefinition Resource an actor that can plays the
  role of a participant in some kind of described process."*
- Proposed wording: *"The ActorDefinition Resource an actor that can **play**
  the role of a participant in some kind of described process."*

The change is a single-word fix ("plays" → "play") in the introduction prose
for ActorDefinition. (As a side note, the original sentence is also missing a
verb between "Resource" and "an actor"; see Open Questions.)

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

The "Scope and Usage" introduction for the ActorDefinition resource contains
the ungrammatical phrase "an actor that can plays the role". The auxiliary
"can" requires the bare infinitive "play", not the inflected "plays". The
sentence is published verbatim on the ActorDefinition page of the FHIR
specification and undermines the readability of a normative resource page.

### Proposed Change

Replace the verb "plays" with "play" in the first paragraph of the
ActorDefinition introduction so that the sentence reads "...an actor that can
play the role of a participant in some kind of described process."

In the current `HL7/fhir` master clone (HEAD `db79dcd`,
`source/actordefinition/actordefinition-introduction.xml`, line 7) the
sentence already reads:

> The ActorDefinition Resource represents an actor that can play the role of
> a participant in some kind of described process.

i.e., the typo has already been corrected (and the missing verb the ticket
overlooked has been filled in as "represents"). The remaining work is to
verify this in `master` and close the ticket; no source edit is required if
the current wording is accepted as fulfilling the resolution.

### Design Rationale

A literal "plays" → "play" substitution is the minimal change that satisfies
the resolution as written. The broader rewording present in `master`
("Resource represents an actor that can play...") is consistent with the
phrasing the reporter proposed (it preserves "can play the role of a
participant in some kind of described process") and additionally fixes the
missing main verb in the original sentence, which also matches normal FHIR
introductory prose style ("The X resource represents…"). No other rewording
is implied by the resolution.
