## Resolution Summary

**Resolution: Persuasive.** The reporter's suggestion is accepted: in
`storage.html` (anchor `#choice`), the phrase "Classic Enterprise Information
Systems (EHRs) tend to behave like this." is to be reworded so that "EHRs"
reads as an example rather than as the expansion of the acronym
"Enterprise Information Systems". Apply the suggested wording from the
ticket — change "Enterprise Information Systems (EHRs)" to
"Enterprise Information Systems (e.g., EHRs)".

The Jira issue carries no comments and no cross-references, so the
applied wording is taken directly from the reporter's suggestion.

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

On the FHIR Core narrative page
[storage.html#choice](https://hl7.org/fhir/6.0.0-ballot1/storage.html#choice),
the body text contains:

> "…highly efficient compared to storing FHIR resources. Classic
> Enterprise Information Systems (EHRs) tend to behave like this."

The parenthetical "(EHRs)" reads grammatically as the acronym expansion
of "Enterprise Information Systems", which it is not. EHRs are one
example of such a system, not a synonym for the term. Readers may
interpret the sentence as defining an incorrect acronym.

### Proposed Change

In `source/storage.html`, in the paragraph that currently spans lines
89–90, replace the parenthetical so that EHRs are clearly presented as
an example:

- **Before** (lines 89–90):
  ```html
  highly efficient compared to storing FHIR resources. Classic Enterprise Information
  Systems (EHRs) tend to behave like this.
  ```
- **After**:
  ```html
  highly efficient compared to storing FHIR resources. Classic Enterprise Information
  Systems (e.g., EHRs) tend to behave like this.
  ```

No other change to the page is required. The `#choice` section anchor,
the surrounding paragraphs (including the parallel "Clinical Data
Repositories tend to behave like this." sentence at lines 97–98), and
all cross-page links remain unchanged.

### Design Rationale

- The wording is taken verbatim from the ticket's accepted suggestion;
  the resolution is "Persuasive" with no additional discussion.
- Inserting "e.g.," is the minimal change that disambiguates the
  parenthetical without restructuring the sentence or affecting the
  page's pedagogical flow.
- The neighbouring sentence ("Clinical Data Repositories tend to behave
  like this.") follows the same rhetorical pattern and does not need
  changes; keeping the edit local preserves symmetry.
- This is prose-only on a hand-authored top-level specification page,
  consistent with the FhirCore briefing's "Update narrative / prose"
  recipe.
