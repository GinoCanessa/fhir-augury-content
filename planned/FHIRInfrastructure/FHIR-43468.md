## Resolution Summary

Resolution: **Persuasive**. The opening sentence of the ActorDefinition "Scope and Usage" section is missing a verb, producing the ungrammatical "The ActorDefinition Resource an actor that can plays the role of a participant in some kind of described process." The reporter proposed inserting "is" so that the sentence reads "The ActorDefinition Resource **is** an actor that can plays the role…". The work group accepted the correction as Persuasive; the implementer should fix the missing verb (and may simultaneously correct the secondary "can plays" → "can play" agreement error in the same clause, since both defects sit in a single sentence and the resolution is to make the sentence grammatical).

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

The ActorDefinition resource page renders a malformed lead sentence in the "Scope and Usage" section. As authored, the sentence has no main verb after the subject ("The ActorDefinition Resource"), so it does not parse as English. This is a published, normative-adjacent piece of narrative on a core resource page and is the first prose readers see when they open `actordefinition.html`. There are no semantic, conformance, or model implications — purely an editorial defect in narrative.

### Proposed Change

Edit the single sentence in `source/actordefinition/actordefinition-introduction.xml` (the `<h2>Scope and Usage</h2>` block, currently line 7 in this clone) so that it is grammatical.

- Current (defective) text quoted in the ticket:
  > "The ActorDefinition Resource an actor that can plays the role of a participant in some kind of described process."
- Reporter's minimal fix:
  > "The ActorDefinition Resource **is** an actor that can plays the role of a participant in some kind of described process."
- Recommended fix (also corrects the subject-verb agreement of "can plays"):
  > "The ActorDefinition Resource **represents** an actor that can **play** the role of a participant in some kind of described process."

No element, cardinality, type, binding, invariant, search parameter, example, or terminology change is required. No other prose blocks on this page need to change.

### Design Rationale

The Persuasive resolution endorses fixing the sentence; it does not constrain the exact wording. Two reasonable surface forms are available:

1. Insert "is" verbatim as the reporter suggested. Smallest possible diff, exactly mirrors the ticket text.
2. Use "represents … can play" — same intent, also resolves the "can plays" agreement glitch that sits inside the same broken sentence.

Option 2 is recommended because the sentence is being rewritten anyway, the second defect ("can plays") is in the immediate clause being touched, and "represents" is the verb conventionally used in FHIR resource scope sentences ("The X resource represents …"). Pick option 1 if the editor wants to keep the diff to a single inserted word and address "can plays" via a follow-up ticket.
