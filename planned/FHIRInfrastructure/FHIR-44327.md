## Resolution Summary

The reporter observed that the Table of Contents (ToC) page numbering in the FHIR Extensions Pack does not match the section numbers shown on individual extension pages. For example, "Extension Registry" is numbered "2" in the ToC but "2.17" on the actual page, and section 2.17 (AppointmentResponse Extensions) appears as "12.15.24" on the extensions page.

The committee determined this difference is **intentional**: the ToC numbering follows the page order within the IG, while the section numbers on individual pages inherit numbering from the FHIR Core specification to maintain the illusion that extension definitions are part of the core spec. Propagating the core-spec section numbers into the ToC would produce random, nonsensical sequences and create unnecessary confusion.

**Resolution action:** Add an explanation of the numbering difference and its rationale to the ToC page.

> *Note:* The resolution is "Not Persuasive with Modification." While the original request to fix the numbering was rejected, the committee committed to adding explanatory text. The status is "Resolved — change required," confirming implementation is expected.

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

The FHIR Extensions Pack ToC page (`toc.html`) currently displays a generic placeholder message: *"This page is a placeholder while we figure out why it's needed."* Users seeing the ToC notice that the sequential numbering in the ToC (1, 2, 3…) does not match the section numbers displayed on individual extension pages (which inherit numbering from the FHIR Core specification). There is no explanation for this discrepancy, causing confusion.

### Proposed Change

Replace the placeholder content on the ToC page with meaningful content that:

1. Acknowledges that the ToC page numbers differ from individual page section numbers.
2. Explains that ToC numbers are based on page order within the IG, while page-level section numbers are inherited from the FHIR Core specification to maintain continuity with the core spec.
3. Briefly notes that aligning the two numbering schemes would produce random, unhelpful sequences in the ToC.

### Design Rationale

- This is a **documentation-only** change with no impact on FHIR definitions, extensions, or tooling.
- The change addresses the user-reported confusion while preserving the intentional design decision.
- The ToC page already exists but contains placeholder content, making it the natural location for this explanation.

## Implementation Plan

### Prerequisites

- Write access to the `HL7/fhir-extensions` repository.
- Familiarity with the FHIR IG Publisher's page generation (XML `<div>` content in `pagecontent/`).

### Step-by-Step Tasks

#### Task 1: Update the ToC page content

**File:** `input/pagecontent/toc.xml`  
**Repo:** `HL7/fhir-extensions`

**Current content** (placeholder):
```xml
<div xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml" ...>
  <a name="holder"> </a>
  <h3>Place holder</h3>
  <p>
    This page is a placeholder while we figure out why it's needed.
  </p>
</div>
```

**Proposed replacement:**
Replace the entire `<div>` content with an explanation of the numbering scheme. The new content should:
- Have a descriptive heading (e.g., "About Section Numbering in this IG")
- Explain that the ToC uses sequential numbering based on page order within the Extensions Pack IG
- Explain that individual extension pages use section numbers inherited from the FHIR Core specification to maintain continuity
- Note that this difference is intentional and by design
- Optionally link to the main [Extension Registry](extension-registry.html) page

Example replacement content:
```xml
<div xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml"
     xmlns:xsi="http://www.w3.org/2001/XMLSchema-instance"
     xsi:schemaLocation="http://hl7.org/fhir ../../input-cache/schemas/R5/fhir-single.xsd">
  <a name="numbering"> </a>
  <h3>About Section Numbering in this IG</h3>
  <p>
    The section numbers shown in this Table of Contents are based on the order
    of pages within the FHIR Extensions Pack IG and are assigned sequentially
    (1, 2, 3, etc.).
  </p>
  <p>
    However, the section numbers displayed on individual extension pages
    (e.g., on the <a href="extension-registry.html">Extension Registry</a> and
    its sub-pages) follow the numbering from the
    <a href="http://hl7.org/fhir">FHIR Core specification</a>. This is
    intentional: the extension definitions maintain the section numbers they
    would have if they were still defined inline within the core specification,
    preserving continuity for implementers familiar with the core spec structure.
  </p>
  <p>
    As a result, the ToC numbers and the page-level section numbers will not
    match. If the core specification section numbers were used in the ToC, they
    would appear out of order and non-sequential, which would be confusing
    rather than helpful.
  </p>
</div>
```

#### Task 2 (Optional): Update the ToC page title in the IG definition

**File:** `input/extensions-ig.xml`  
**Repo:** `HL7/fhir-extensions`  
**Line:** ~59

Consider updating the page title from `"Place Holder ToC"` to something more descriptive such as `"Table of Contents"` or `"Table of Contents — Numbering Note"`, since the page will no longer be a placeholder:

```xml
<!-- Before -->
<title value="Place Holder ToC"/>

<!-- After -->
<title value="Table of Contents"/>
```

### Open Questions

1. **Exact wording:** The committee may want to review and finalize the explanatory text before merge. The proposed text above is a starting point based on the resolution description.
2. **Additional ToC content:** Should the ToC page also contain a rendered version of the actual table of contents (from `toc.xml` at the root), or should it remain purely an explanatory note? Currently the root `toc.xml` generates the full ToC table separately.
3. **Scope of title change:** Should the menu entry and page title both be updated, or just the content?
