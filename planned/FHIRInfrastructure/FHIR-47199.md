## Resolution Summary

Resolution: **Persuasive with Modification**.

Replace the introductory sentence above the "deprecated artifacts" list in
`versions.html`:

- **From:** *"The following artifacts are deprecated in this version of FHIR:"*
- **To:** *"The following artifacts are marked as deprecated in this version of
  FHIR (though the deprecation may have occurred in a previous release):"*

Per the resolution description, the intent of the list is **not** to enumerate
what is *newly* deprecated in the current release, but to show **everything
currently flagged as deprecated**, regardless of the ballot in which the
deprecation occurred. The list contents (currently the single bullet
`OperationOutcome.issue.location`) are not changed by this ticket; only the
prose preamble.

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

The reporter (Ignacio Jauregui) flagged that the paragraph on the FHIR Core
versions page (`source/versions.html`) reads as if it lists the artifacts that
have been newly deprecated in the *current* publication of the spec. In fact,
the only entry — `OperationOutcome.issue.location` — was deprecated in R5, not
R6. As-written, the prose misleads readers into believing R6 introduces new
deprecations, and conversely could be read to imply that previously deprecated
items "fall off" the list once a new release is published.

### Proposed Change

Single-file documentation edit in `HL7/fhir`:

- **File:** `source/versions.html`
- **Line:** 566 (inside the `<p>…</p>` block immediately preceding the
  `<ul>` of deprecated artifacts).
- **Current text:**

  ```html
  <p>
  The following artifacts are deprecated in this version of FHIR:
  </p>
  ```

- **New text:**

  ```html
  <p>
  The following artifacts are marked as deprecated in this version of FHIR (though the deprecation may have occurred in a previous release):
  </p>
  ```

No other changes are required: the surrounding paragraphs (lines 557–564) about
deprecation/withdrawal lifecycle, the `<ul>` list (lines 568–570) with the
`OperationOutcome.issue.location` bullet, the `<hr/>`, and the
Interversion-Compatibility link block (lines 572–575) all remain untouched.

### Design Rationale

The wording change directly captures the resolution committee's intent: the
list is a *standing roster* of currently deprecated artifacts, not a delta from
the previous release. The parenthetical "(though the deprecation may have
occurred in a previous release)" makes that semantics explicit at the point
where the reader encounters the list, which is preferable to relying on the
upstream paragraph (line 559: "Deprecated materials are eligible to be balloted
to be withdrawn two years after their deprecated status is published.") to
convey that some deprecations carry over.

No alternatives are required by the ticket discussion; the resolution text is
verbatim and was approved as a Block-Vote-6 item.
