## Resolution Summary

Resolution: **Persuasive with Modification**.

The committee will **not** remove "Data Expectations" from the formal definition of MustSupport as the reporter requested. Instead, the resolution adds explicit caution language into the **Obligations** section of the FHIR Core specification, stating verbatim:

> "Obligations may dive deeply into the functional capabilities of systems. Most interoperability specifications tread lightly (and SHOULD tread lightly) in this space. However, when a specification is created for use in a narrowly defined space, setting very specific expectations on display, storage, data entry, etc. may be necessary."

In addition, the IG publication tooling will be updated to emit **warnings** when HL7 International IGs use obligations that step into rendering / storage expectations. Those warnings may be revisited based on real-world use.

Per Lloyd McKenzie's 2025-11-06 comment, the core specification change has landed in `HL7/fhir` master at commit `7f1db68f86` ("https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-40306 - added caution on use of obligations"). The publisher-tooling portion is still outstanding.

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

The reporter (Isaac Vetter) argues that interoperability specifications should govern *how data flows between systems*, not *how systems internally store or display that data*. He specifically objects to the "Data Expectations" category in the [Support Handling](https://confluence.hl7.org/pages/viewpage.action?pageId=131040977#SupportHandling-DataExpectation) Confluence page being baked into the formal/discrete definition of MustSupport, on the grounds that storage/display are functional system requirements rather than interoperability requirements.

The committee disagreed with the absolutist framing. Obligations (and, by extension, MustSupport-style assertions) *can* legitimately reach into storage/display when a specification is narrowly scoped (e.g., a single domain or care setting), but that breadth needs to be clearly bounded with guidance and tooling-level guard-rails so general-purpose HL7 International IGs do not casually overreach.

### Proposed Change

**Part A — Specification narrative (HL7/fhir, applied in commit `7f1db68f86`).**

Add a new `<p>` paragraph to `source/obligations.html`, immediately after the existing paragraph that explains that the base specification does not specify application obligations, and immediately before the `<a name="mustSupport"></a>` anchor that introduces MustSupport. Inserted text (verbatim, matching commit `7f1db68f86`):

```html
<p>
Obligations may dive deeply into the functional capabilities of systems.  Most interoperability specifications tread lightly (and <b>SHOULD</b> tread lightly) 
in this space.  However, when a specification is created for use in a narrowly defined space, setting very specific expectations on display, storage, data entry, etc. may be necessary.
</p>
```

This is a 4-line, additive narrative insertion. There are no schema, StructureDefinition, ValueSet, or example changes in `HL7/fhir`.

**Part B — Publisher tooling (out of scope for this cached repo set).**

Update the IG publication tooling so that when an HL7 International IG uses obligations whose codes/semantics target rendering, display, storage, or data-entry behaviour, the publisher emits a **warning** (not an error). The exact list of obligation codes / behaviours to flag is not enumerated in the resolution; the natural source list is the "Data Expectations" sub-categories on the Confluence Support Handling page referenced from the ticket. The warnings are explicitly described as provisional and "may be revisited based on real-world use". This change belongs in `HL7/fhir-ig-publisher` (and possibly the validator IGPublisher harness), neither of which is present in the local cache; see the Open Questions section.

### Design Rationale

- The committee chose **clarification + tooling guardrails** over **definitional restriction** because in narrowly scoped IGs (e.g., point-of-care decision support, prescribing, vaccination credentials) the realistic interoperability contract sometimes *does* legitimately reach into how a receiver displays or persists data — removing the option from the model would cripple those IGs.
- Putting the language in the **Obligations** page (rather than the **MustSupport** page) is consistent with the post-R5 direction where Obligations are the formal mechanism for binding actor-specific behavioural expectations; MustSupport is increasingly treated as a thin convenience flag layered on top of Obligations.
- The **`SHOULD tread lightly`** wording deliberately uses a normative RFC2119 verb, signalling that overreach is permitted but discouraged — and that downstream review (HL7 IG ballot, publisher warning) will hold authors to account.
- Warnings (not errors) in the publisher were chosen because the resolution acknowledges legitimate exceptions; an error would block valid narrowly-scoped IGs.
