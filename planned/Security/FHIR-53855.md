## Resolution Summary

Resolution type: **Persuasive with Modification**.

The resolution, as captured in the ticket's Resolution Description, has two concrete edits to the "Common Use Cases" list in `secpriv-module.html` (section 6.0.2 of the Security and Privacy module):

1. **Reframe the first "use case" as background.** `Security` and `Privacy` are not use cases; they are concepts that the subsequent use cases cover. Replace the opening bullet with introductory prose and drop the bullet, so the lead-in paragraph reads:
   > **Security and Privacy are concepts covered in usecases.** The following common use-cases are elaborated below:
2. **Remove the "Digital and Electronic Signatures" bullet** from the list. Signatures are not elaborated on this page (the current link just exits to `signatures.html`), and signatures are already surfaced in the Security and Privacy module's "materials" list higher up on the same page (`signatures.html` — "Signature Use"), so the bullet is redundant and misleading.

No other bullets in the list are modified.

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

Section 6.0.2 of the Security and Privacy module (`secpriv-module.html`, the "Common Use Cases" list) is internally inconsistent with the content that follows it:

- The first bullet presents "Security" and "Privacy" as use cases, but the corresponding `<h3>Security</h3>` and `<h3>Privacy</h3>` sections on the same page are background/conceptual narrative, not use cases. No `Use case:` lead-in exists in those sections (confirmed in the clone at `source/secpriv-module.html` lines 103–159; the `<em>Use case:</em>` pattern does not begin until `<h3>Authorization and Access Control</h3>` at line ~165).
- The "Digital and Electronic Signatures" bullet links to a different page (`signatures.html`) and has no elaboration in `secpriv-module.html`. The same link is also listed in the module's "materials" table at the top of the page as "Signature Use" (line 77), so the bullet duplicates an entry readers have just seen and promises a use case that does not exist on this page.

Reporter EssienGe flagged this in FHIR-53855 and suggested either adjusting the section or adding a real Digital Signature use case. The applied resolution chose the former.

### Proposed Change

Edit a single file in `HL7/fhir`: `source/secpriv-module.html`. Scope: the list that starts immediately after "The following common use-cases are elaborated below:" (lines 84–100 in the clone at HEAD `db79dcd`).

Concrete HTML edits:

1. **Add the Security/Privacy framing sentence and remove the first bullet.** Before the change:

   ```html
   <p>
   The following common use-cases are elaborated below:
   <ul>
   <li><a href="#security">Security</a> and <a href="#privacy">Privacy</a></li>
   <li><a href="#authorization">Authorization and Access Control</a></li>
   ...
   ```

   After the change:

   ```html
   <p>
   <b><a href="#security">Security</a> and <a href="#privacy">Privacy</a> are concepts covered in usecases.</b>
   The following common use-cases are elaborated below:
   <ul>
   <li><a href="#authorization">Authorization and Access Control</a></li>
   ...
   ```

   The bold framing sentence preserves inbound anchor links to `#security` and `#privacy` (the `<a name="security"></a>` / `<a name="privacy"></a>` targets at lines 103 and 136 of the file remain in place).

2. **Remove the "Digital and Electronic Signatures" bullet.** Delete this line (line 95 in the clone):

   ```html
   <li><a href="signatures.html">Digital and Electronic Signatures</a></li>
   ```

   Leave the rest of the list (`#deId`, `#testData`, `#privacy-of-ids`) untouched.

No section anchors are removed (neither `#security` nor `#privacy` nor `signatures.html` itself). Signatures remain linked from the "materials" table earlier on the page (line 77), so the topic is still reachable from this module page.

### Design Rationale

- **Faithfully tracks the resolution.** The two bullets above are exactly the two edits called out in the Resolution Description (reframe Security/Privacy as background; drop Digital and Electronic Signatures). The reporter's secondary suggestion ("consider adding a proper Digital Signature use case") was explicitly *not* adopted by the resolution; that work is out of scope for this ticket.
- **Preserves existing inbound links.** Readers and other spec pages link to `secpriv-module.html#security` and `#privacy`. Keeping the `<a name="…">` anchors intact avoids breaking cross-references even though the items are no longer phrased as list entries.
- **Avoids a parallel duplicate.** Signatures are already in the "materials" list at the top of the page (`<a href="signatures.html">Signature Use</a>`, line 77). Removing the redundant bullet in the use-cases list tightens the page without hiding the topic.
- **Minimal, narrative-only change.** Consistent with the FhirCore playbook's "Update narrative / prose" recipe — no StructureDefinition, ValueSet, or search-parameter churn.
