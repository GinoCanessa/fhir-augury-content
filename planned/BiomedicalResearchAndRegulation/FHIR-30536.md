# Implementation Plan: FHIR-30536

**Title:** Revisit sex mapping
**Status:** Resolved - change required
**Resolution:** Persuasive with Modification
**Work Group:** Biomedical Research & Regulation
**Specification:** CDISC Mappings (FHIR)
**Resolved:** 2021-05-17

---

## Resolution Summary

The resolution addresses semantic ambiguity between CDISC's DM.SEX variable and FHIR's Patient.gender element. The existing explicit mapping from DM.SEX → Patient.gender was deemed inappropriate because the two concepts conflate distinct facets of sex and gender (physiologic, social, chromosomal, administrative). The work group resolved to:

1. **Remove the explicit DM.SEX → Patient.gender mapping** and replace it with a mapping note (displayed as a "gap" in the mapping table with a grey background) explaining why a standard mapping is not advisable.
2. **Add a regulatory disclaimer** to the contributors/credits section of the IG stating that participation by governmental regulatory agency individuals does not represent policy regarding submission or conversion expectations.

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

The CDISC-to-FHIR Mapping IG previously mapped DM.SEX (SDTM Demographics domain – "Sex") directly to Patient.gender. However:

- **DM.SEX** is defined as "The assemblage of physical properties or qualities by which male is distinguished from female" (NCI) — primarily physiologic/biological sex, though SDTM controlled terminology also covers cultural gender role distinctions.
- **Patient.gender** is explicitly defined as an *administrative* gender concept, not a clinical or biological sex concept.
- FHIR's own guidance states: "Note that gender represents the gender for administrative purposes. Genetic, physiologic, social and other gender aspects are captured as Observations."
- Source EHR systems vary widely in what they actually capture in gender fields — it may be self-identified gender, biological sex, legal sex, or something else entirely.

This semantic mismatch means a direct mapping could lead to incorrect data interpretation in regulatory submissions and clinical research analysis.

### Proposed Change

Two changes to the CDISC Mapping Implementation Guide:

**Change 1 — Replace DM.SEX mapping with a gap note:**

In the DM mapping table, the SEX row should have no FHIR Resource/Attribute mapping. Instead, the FHIR mapping columns should be replaced with a spanning grey-background cell containing the following mapping note text:

> Sex and gender are multi-faceted concepts. Both FHIR and CDISC standards have a large degree of ambiguity in their definitions for their primary data elements describing a subject's sex or gender (DM.SEX and Patient.gender). This ambiguity often exists in original source systems. Depending on the use case, various facets of sex and gender may be utilized or captured within clinical data — physiologic, social, chromosomal, etc. As such, it's not advisable to indicate a mapping where all the facets of sex and gender are ambiguous in both the source and study data standards. Study sponsors and regulators will need to establish policies and look into the quality and nature of the source data as well as the analysis that needs to be performed to determine appropriate mappings.
>
> a) Extensions such as US Core "birth sex" and FHIR core "gender identity" may give more semantically consistent values, but may not be widely populated.
>
> b) Some gender concepts such as physiologic and genetic characteristics may be captured as Observation values rather than as demographics elements on Patient.

**Change 2 — Add regulatory disclaimer to credits:**

Add the following paragraph to the credits/contributors section of the IG:

> "Various individuals from governmental regulatory agencies were involved in mapping discussions and in review of this document prior to publication. Their participation in no way represents policy with respect to expectations around the submission or conversion expectations for real world data or other potential uses of these mappings."

### Design Rationale

- Removing the explicit mapping prevents misuse of a semantically incorrect mapping in regulatory submissions.
- The mapping note educates implementers about the complexity and directs them to more specific FHIR elements (birth sex extension, gender identity extension, Observation-based capture).
- The regulatory disclaimer protects regulatory agencies from having their participation interpreted as endorsement of specific mapping policies.
- The resolution acknowledges that no single "correct" mapping exists — it depends on the specific data source, use case, and regulatory context.

## Impact Analysis

### Affected Repositories

#### HL7/fhir-cdisc-mapping (Ig — not in local cache)

- **Role:** Primary repository for the CDISC-to-FHIR Mapping Implementation Guide. Contains the mapping data files, generated HTML table includes, and IG page content.
- **Affected Files:**
  - `input/cdiscMaps.xml` — The canonical XML mapping data source. The `<element name="Sex">` entry within the DM domain must use a `<gap>` element (instead of `<fhir>` mapping elements) containing the mapping note text. **Current state: Already updated.** The entry currently uses `<gap>` with the resolution text.
  - `input/includes/DM-table.xml` — The generated HTML mapping table for the Demographics domain. The SEX row must show a `colspan="2"` grey-background cell with the mapping note text spanning the FHIR Resource/Attribute and FHIRPath columns, with no FHIR mapping. **Current state: Already updated.** This file is auto-generated from `cdiscMaps.xml` and `Domain_CDISC_FHIR_Mappingsv2.xml` via the `__genSpreadsheet.xlsm` / `__genmaps.bat` tooling.
  - `input/pagecontent/index.xml` — The IG home page with the credits section. Must include the regulatory disclaimer paragraph. **Current state: Already updated.** The disclaimer text is present in the credits section.
  - `input/Domain_CDISC_FHIR_Mappingsv2.xml` — The v2 XML spreadsheet source for mapping data. Should reflect the removal of the Patient.gender mapping for DM.SEX.
  - `input/pagecontent/changes.xml` — The IG change history page. **Current state: Not updated.** Should document the DM.SEX mapping change and regulatory disclaimer addition as part of STU 1 ballot resolution changes.
- **Change Scope:** Minor — The core mapping data changes are textual/content modifications to an existing mapping row (replacing a direct mapping with a gap note) and adding a disclaimer paragraph. No structural, schema, or profile changes are involved.

### Breaking Changes

- **Consumers relying on DM.SEX → Patient.gender mapping:** Any automated tools or ETL pipelines that used this IG's explicit mapping of DM.SEX to Patient.gender will need to be updated. The mapping is now intentionally left unmapped, requiring implementers to make case-by-case decisions.
- **Severity: Low-Medium.** The IG is STU-level (ballot candidate v0.1.0), so there is limited production adoption. However, any existing implementations based on the ballot version would be affected.
