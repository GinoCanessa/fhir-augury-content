# Implementation Plan: FHIR-46450

**Title:** Extend the subjectState.code definitions for on-study / off-study to cover their meaning in retrospective use
**Status:** Resolved - change required
**Resolution:** Persuasive with Modification
**Work Group:** Biomedical Research & Regulation
**Specification:** FHIR Core (FHIR)
**Resolved:** 2025-03-18

---

## Resolution Summary

The work group agreed that the definitions of the `on-study` and `off-study` codes in the `ResearchSubjectState` CodeSystem should be extended to cover their meaning in retrospective study contexts. However, the modification is that these changes should be applied through **THO (HL7 Terminology)** via the UTG repository, rather than through a FHIR Core–based value set. The resolution explicitly states: _"Agreed with the changes applied through THO instead of FHIR-based value set. Will be applied through UP-585."_

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

The current definitions of `on-study` and `off-study` in the `ResearchSubjectState` CodeSystem (`http://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/research-subject-state`) are written exclusively from the perspective of **prospective** clinical studies where subjects actively participate. They do not account for **retrospective** studies, where subjects do not actively participate but are represented by their data contributions.

**Current definitions:**
- **on-study:** `"A person that is enrolled or registered on a study."`
- **off-study:** `"A person that has ended their participation on a study either because their treatment/observation is complete or through not responding, withdrawal, non-compliance and/or adverse event."`

These definitions are inadequate for retrospective study contexts because:
1. Subjects in retrospective studies are not "enrolled" or "registered" in the traditional sense.
2. Subjects do not actively "participate" or "end their participation" — their historical data is selected for inclusion.
3. Without explicit retrospective definitions, implementers may be unclear on how to use these codes for retrospective study workflows.

### Proposed Change

Extend the `<definition>` text of two concepts in the `ResearchSubjectState` CodeSystem to append retrospective study context:

**on-study** — extend the definition to:
> `"A person that is enrolled or registered on a study. In a retrospective study context: the start of the period during which a subject's data is included, observed, or considered as part of the study analysis."`

**off-study** — extend the definition to:
> `"A person that has ended their participation on a study either because their treatment/observation is complete or through not responding, withdrawal, non-compliance and/or adverse event. In a retrospective study context: the end of the period during which a subject's data is included, observed, or considered as part of the study analysis."`

### Design Rationale

- **Additive, not breaking:** The existing prospective definitions are preserved. The retrospective context is appended as additional clarifying text, so systems already using these codes are unaffected.
- **Aligned with FHIR Core narrative:** The `ResearchSubject` resource's Scope and Usage section (in `researchsubject-introduction.xml`) already states: _"The subjectState codes 'on-study' and 'off-study' track the periods during which their data is included, observed, or considered as part of the study analysis."_ This was applied via the related ticket FHIR-46449. The CodeSystem definition updates bring terminology into alignment with this narrative.
- **THO-based approach:** The resolution explicitly directs changes through the UTG/THO process rather than FHIR Core value sets, which is appropriate since the CodeSystem is maintained in THO at `http://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/research-subject-state`.
- **Comment refinement:** Per the ticket comments, the retrospective definitions include "the" for grammatical clarity (e.g., "the start of the period" rather than "start of period").

### Breaking Changes

**None.** This is a purely additive change to concept definitions (text only). No codes are being added, removed, or renamed. No structural changes to the CodeSystem or ValueSet. Existing systems using `on-study` and `off-study` codes will continue to function identically; only the human-readable definition text is extended.
