## Resolution Summary

Resolution: **Persuasive** — "Changes definitions as suggested."

Replace the trivial placeholder definitions of the two concepts in the
`medicinal-product-type` CodeSystem (used to bind `MedicinalProductDefinition.type`)
with the substantive definitions proposed in the ticket:

- **`MedicinalProduct` / "Medicinal Product"** →
  *"Any substance or combination of substances that may be administered to human
  beings (or animals) for treating or preventing disease, or with the intent to
  make a medical diagnosis or to restore, correct or modify physiological
  functions. Its regulatory status is authorized/marketed for commercial use."*
- **`InvestigationalProduct` / "Investigational Medicinal Product"** →
  *"A medicinal product which is being tested or used as a reference, including
  as a placebo, in a clinical trial. Its regulatory status is under evaluation
  in a clinical trial; may or may not have marketing authorization."*

Because the change is substantive content for a resource heading toward
Normative status, an R6-ballot note must also be added to the resource
introduction so balloters are alerted to the change.

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

`CodeSystem/medicinal-product-type` defines the two values bound to
`MedicinalProductDefinition.type` (a major classifying element of the resource).
The current `<definition>` text for both concepts is essentially tautological
("A standard medicinal product." and "An investigational medicinal product.")
and gives implementers no useful guidance on regulatory scope or how the codes
differ. This is especially problematic as MedicinalProductDefinition is a key
resource in the BR&R domain and is targeted for Normative status, which raises
the bar on the precision and quality of element/code definitions.

### Proposed Change

Two file-level changes in `HL7/fhir`, both in
`source/medicinalproductdefinition/`:

1. **CodeSystem `medicinal-product-type` — concept definitions.**
   In `source/medicinalproductdefinition/codesystem-medicinal-product-type.xml`,
   replace each concept's `<definition value="…"/>` exactly as follows:
   - Concept `MedicinalProduct` (display `Medicinal Product`):
     `Any substance or combination of substances that may be administered to
     human beings (or animals) for treating or preventing disease, or with the
     intent to make a medical diagnosis or to restore, correct or modify
     physiological functions. Its regulatory status is authorized/marketed for
     commercial use.`
   - Concept `InvestigationalProduct` (display `Investigational Medicinal
     Product`):
     `A medicinal product which is being tested or used as a reference,
     including as a placebo, in a clinical trial. Its regulatory status is
     under evaluation in a clinical trial; may or may not have marketing
     authorization.`
   No other fields on the CodeSystem (id, url, version, name, title, status,
   bindings, FMM, standards-status, content, etc.) change. The ValueSet
   `valueset-medicinal-product-type.xml` references the CodeSystem and does
   not duplicate concept definitions, so no change is required there.

2. **MedicinalProductDefinition resource introduction — ballot note.**
   In
   `source/medicinalproductdefinition/medicinalproductdefinition-introduction.xml`,
   add a "Note to Balloters" `<blockquote class="ballot-note" id="bn1">`
   immediately after the existing module-page callout `<p>` and before the
   first scope `<p>The MedicinalProductDefinition resource covers …</p>`,
   highlighting that the substantive change since the last R6 ballot is
   the improved definitions of the two values of
   `MedicinalProductDefinition.type`.

Indicative resulting block (ballot note):

```xml
<blockquote class="ballot-note" id="bn1">
  <p><b>Note to Balloters:</b> To ensure this resource is ready for Normative
     status, we are seeking ballot comment on the substantive content. The key
     changes made since the last R6 ballot are:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Updates to the following elements:</li>
    <ul>
      <li>The definitions of the two values of MedicinalProductDefinition.type
          were improved</li>
    </ul>
  </ul>
</blockquote>
```

### Design Rationale

- The resolution is "Persuasive" with `resolution_description = "Changes
  definitions as suggested"`, so the proposed wording in the ticket body is
  adopted verbatim. No alternate phrasings were proposed in comments (the
  ticket has no comments).
- Definitions live on the CodeSystem (the source of truth for code semantics)
  rather than on the resource's StructureDefinition element binding. This is
  the existing pattern for every other `codesystem-medicinal-product-*.xml`
  file in the same folder, so we keep the change inside the CodeSystem.
- The ballot note is added because MedicinalProductDefinition is being
  advanced toward Normative; substantive code definition changes between
  ballots must be flagged to balloters. This matches the standard
  "Note to Balloters" `<blockquote class="ballot-note">` pattern already
  used in the FhirCore codebase for resource-intro ballot comments.

### Step-by-Step Tasks

#### HL7/fhir

1. **Replace `MedicinalProduct` concept definition.**
   - File: `source/medicinalproductdefinition/codesystem-medicinal-product-type.xml`
   - Action: Locate the `<concept>` whose `<code value="MedicinalProduct"/>`
     and replace its `<definition value="A standard medicinal product."/>`
     with `<definition value="Any substance or combination of substances that
     may be administered to human beings (or animals) for treating or
     preventing disease, or with the intent to make a medical diagnosis or to
     restore, correct or modify physiological functions. Its regulatory status
     is authorized/marketed for commercial use."/>`.
   - Details: Keep the surrounding `<code>` and `<display>` lines exactly as
     they are. Preserve the file's existing 2-space indentation and the
     terminating newline behaviour; do not touch the trailing
     no-newline-at-end-of-file marker.

2. **Replace `InvestigationalProduct` concept definition.**
   - File: `source/medicinalproductdefinition/codesystem-medicinal-product-type.xml`
   - Action: Locate the `<concept>` whose `<code value="InvestigationalProduct"/>`
     and replace its `<definition value="An investigational medicinal product."/>`
     with `<definition value="A medicinal product which is being tested or used
     as a reference, including as a placebo, in a clinical trial. Its
     regulatory status is under evaluation in a clinical trial; may or may
     not have marketing authorization."/>`.
   - Details: As above, only the `<definition>` line changes. The XML
     character `&quot;` is not needed because the new strings contain only
     ASCII letters, digits, parentheses, commas, slashes, semicolons, and
     periods — the surrounding `value="..."` quoting is safe.

3. **Add R6 "Note to Balloters" to the resource introduction.**
   - File: `source/medicinalproductdefinition/medicinalproductdefinition-introduction.xml`
   - Action: Immediately after the existing `<p style="border:2px; …">For an
     overview of this resource and others in the Medication Definition
     domain, also see the <a href="medication-definition-module.html">module
     page</a></i></b></p>` line and before the `<p>The
     MedicinalProductDefinition resource covers …</p>` paragraph, insert the
     following block:
     ```xml
     <blockquote class="ballot-note" id="bn1">
       <p><b>Note to Balloters:</b> To ensure this resource is ready for
          Normative status, we are seeking ballot comment on the substantive
          content. The key changes made since the last R6 ballot are:</p>
       <ul>
         <li>Updates to the following elements:</li>
         <ul>
           <li>The definitions of the two values of
               MedicinalProductDefinition.type were improved</li>
         </ul>
       </ul>
     </blockquote>
     ```
   - Details: Use the indentation already used by sibling `*-introduction.xml`
     files in `source/<resource>/`. The `id="bn1"` anchor pattern matches
     other ballot notes in the codebase. Do not modify any other content on
     the page; the rest of the Scope and Usage section, examples, and
     boundaries text remain as-is.
