# Implementation Plan: FHIR-43577

**Title:** Change research-subject-state value set in accordance with BRR consensus
**Status:** Resolved - change required
**Resolution:** Persuasive with Modification
**Work Group:** Biomedical Research & Regulation
**Specification:** FHIR Core (FHIR)
**Resolved:** 2024-01-29

---

## Resolution Summary

The BRR work group reached consensus on a revised set of states for `ResearchSubject.subjectState.code`. The existing `research-subject-state` CodeSystem (13 codes) must be replaced with a streamlined set of 9 codes that better represent the lifecycle of a research subject. The resolution states that the **underlying CodeSystem in UTG must be updated** and a **UTG ticket must be created** to manage the terminology change. The new codes come from the table in the ticket, which provides codes and definitions but not display names—display names must be created as part of the UTG update.

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

The current `research-subject-state` CodeSystem (`http://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/research-subject-state`) contains 13 codes that are overly granular in some areas and missing important states in others. Several codes overlap or are rarely used (e.g., `potential-candidate`, `not-registered`, `pending-on-study`, `withdrawn`), while key screening phases (`in-prescreening`, `in-screening`) and an explicit `in-follow-up` state are absent. The BRR work group reached consensus on a simplified, cleaner 9-code set that better reflects the actual clinical research subject lifecycle.

### Proposed Change

Replace the 13 existing codes in the `research-subject-state` CodeSystem with the following 9 codes:

| # | Code | Proposed Display | Definition |
|---|------|-----------------|------------|
| 1 | `candidate` | Candidate | A subject (ResearchSubject) potentially eligible for participation in a study (ResearchStudy) who has not completed an evaluation to determine whether they are eligible or ineligible for consideration. |
| 2 | `in-prescreening` | In Prescreening | A subject (ResearchSubject) undergoing an initial evaluation to determine if they are eligible or ineligible for consideration for inclusion in a study (ResearchStudy). |
| 3 | `in-screening` | In Screening | A subject (ResearchSubject) undergoing a full evaluation to determine if they are eligible or ineligible for inclusion in a study (ResearchStudy). |
| 4 | `eligible` | Eligible | A subject (ResearchSubject) who has passed a full evaluation against the protocol specified eligibility criteria to determine if they are eligible or ineligible for inclusion in a study (ResearchStudy). |
| 5 | `ineligible` | Ineligible | A subject (ResearchSubject) who has failed an evaluation against the protocol specified eligibility criteria to determine if they are eligible or ineligible for inclusion in a study (ResearchStudy). |
| 6 | `on-study` | On Study | A subject (ResearchSubject) who is enrolled in a study (ResearchStudy). |
| 7 | `on-study-intervention` | On Study Intervention | A subject (ResearchSubject) who is in an intervention phase of a study (ResearchStudy). |
| 8 | `in-follow-up` | In Follow-up | A subject (ResearchSubject) who has completed planned study (ResearchStudy) procedures and tests and is being monitored based on a protocol-prescribed schedule. |
| 9 | `off-study` | Off Study | A subject (ResearchSubject) who is no longer participating in a study (ResearchStudy). |

**Codes removed** (7): `follow-up`, `not-registered`, `on-study-observation`, `pending-on-study`, `potential-candidate`, `screening`, `withdrawn`

**Codes added** (3): `in-prescreening`, `in-screening`, `in-follow-up`

**Codes retained** (6): `candidate`, `eligible`, `ineligible`, `on-study`, `on-study-intervention`, `off-study`

> **Note:** The ticket table lists "on-study intervention" with a space. The existing CodeSystem already uses `on-study-intervention` (hyphenated), and the FHIR Core StructureDefinition `short` value also uses `on-study-intervention`. The hyphenated form should be retained for consistency.

### Design Rationale

- The new code set aligns with the BRR work group's consensus on the actual lifecycle states of a research subject.
- Splitting the old `screening` code into `in-prescreening` and `in-screening` provides finer granularity where it is clinically needed—distinguishing initial eligibility triage from full protocol screening.
- Replacing `follow-up` with `in-follow-up` provides naming consistency with the `in-prescreening` and `in-screening` codes.
- Removing rarely-used codes (`not-registered`, `pending-on-study`, `potential-candidate`, `withdrawn`) simplifies the model without losing critical information (these states can be captured through other mechanisms or are edge cases).
- Removing `on-study-observation` simplifies the on-study model; the intervention phase is retained as the more critical distinction.

### Breaking Changes

**Yes — this is a breaking terminology change.**

- **7 codes removed:** `follow-up`, `not-registered`, `on-study-observation`, `pending-on-study`, `potential-candidate`, `screening`, `withdrawn`. Any systems currently sending or expecting these codes will produce validation errors against the updated CodeSystem.
- **6 codes have updated definitions:** `candidate`, `eligible`, `ineligible`, `on-study`, `on-study-intervention`, `off-study`. The semantic meaning is preserved but definitions are reworded.
- **Mitigation:** The binding strength on `ResearchSubject.subjectState.code` is `example` (not `required` or `extensible`), which means implementers are not constrained to use only these codes. This significantly reduces the practical breaking change impact — existing data using removed codes remains technically valid.

## Implementation Plan

### Prerequisites

1. **Create a UTG ticket** — As stated in the resolution, a UTG ticket must be created to track the CodeSystem changes in the HL7/UTG repository. This ticket should reference FHIR-43577.
2. **Confirm display names** — The ticket provides codes and definitions but not display names. The proposed display names in this plan (e.g., "In Prescreening", "In Screening", "In Follow-up") must be confirmed by the BRR work group or UTG editors before implementation.

### Step-by-Step Tasks

#### Task 1: Update the CodeSystem in HL7/UTG

**File:** `input/sourceOfTruth/fhir/codeSystems/CodeSystem-research-subject-state.xml`

1. Update `<version>` from `1.0.1` to `2.0.0` (major version bump due to removed codes).
2. Update `<date>` and `<meta><lastUpdated>` to the current date.
3. **Remove** the following 7 `<concept>` elements:
   - `follow-up` (line 54–57)
   - `not-registered` (line 64–67)
   - `on-study-observation` (line 83–87)
   - `pending-on-study` (line 88–92)
   - `potential-candidate` (line 93–97)
   - `screening` (line 98–102)
   - `withdrawn` (line 103–107)
4. **Add** 3 new `<concept>` elements:
   ```xml
   <concept>
     <code value="in-prescreening"/>
     <display value="In Prescreening"/>
     <definition value="A subject (ResearchSubject) undergoing an initial evaluation to determine if they are eligible or ineligible for consideration for inclusion in a study (ResearchStudy)."/>
   </concept>
   <concept>
     <code value="in-screening"/>
     <display value="In Screening"/>
     <definition value="A subject (ResearchSubject) undergoing a full evaluation to determine if they are eligible or ineligible for inclusion in a study (ResearchStudy)."/>
   </concept>
   <concept>
     <code value="in-follow-up"/>
     <display value="In Follow-up"/>
     <definition value="A subject (ResearchSubject) who has completed planned study (ResearchStudy) procedures and tests and is being monitored based on a protocol-prescribed schedule."/>
   </concept>
   ```
5. **Update definitions** for retained codes to match the BRR-approved definitions from the ticket table:
   - `candidate`: Update definition to "A subject (ResearchSubject) potentially eligible for participation in a study (ResearchStudy) who has not completed an evaluation to determine whether they are eligible or ineligible for consideration."
   - `eligible`: Update definition to "A subject (ResearchSubject) who has passed a full evaluation against the protocol specified eligibility criteria to determine if they are eligible or ineligible for inclusion in a study (ResearchStudy)."
   - `ineligible`: Update definition to match ticket table text.
   - `on-study`: Update definition to "A subject (ResearchSubject) who is enrolled in a study (ResearchStudy)."
   - `on-study-intervention`: Update definition to "A subject (ResearchSubject) who is in an intervention phase of a study (ResearchStudy)."
   - `off-study`: Update definition to "A subject (ResearchSubject) who is no longer participating in a study (ResearchStudy)."
6. Reorder concepts to match the ticket table ordering: candidate, in-prescreening, in-screening, eligible, ineligible, on-study, on-study-intervention, in-follow-up, off-study.

### Open Questions

1. **Display names:** The ticket explicitly notes that "the table included has codes but not display names." The display names proposed in this plan (e.g., "In Prescreening", "In Screening", "In Follow-up", "On Study", "On Study Intervention", "Off Study") need confirmation from the BRR work group or UTG editors. Some existing display names (e.g., "Candidate", "Eligible", "Ineligible") are straightforward, but new ones need formal approval.
2. **UTG ticket status:** The resolution states a UTG ticket must be created. Has this been done? If so, what is the ticket key? Implementation cannot proceed without it.
3. **Version strategy:** Should the CodeSystem version be bumped to `2.0.0` (since codes are removed, which is a breaking change) or follow a different versioning convention per UTG policies?
4. **Deprecation vs. removal:** Should removed codes be deprecated (marked inactive) rather than deleted outright, to support backward compatibility? UTG may have a policy on this.
5. **Definition for `in-prescreening`:** The ticket table appears to have a copy-paste issue where the definition for `in-prescreening` (row 2) contains the `candidate` definition appended to the end. The intended definition should be confirmed as: "A subject (ResearchSubject) undergoing an initial evaluation to determine if they are eligible or ineligible for consideration for inclusion in a study (ResearchStudy)."
6. **New examples:** Should new examples be created demonstrating the prescreening/screening workflow to illustrate the new codes?
