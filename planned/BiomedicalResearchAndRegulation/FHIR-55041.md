## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

The narrative page `source/medication-definition-module.html` (the Biomedical Research & Regulation work group's "Medication Definition" module landing page in FHIR Core) contains three editorial defects:

1. Two occurrences of the contraction `doesn't`, which violates the FHIR specification's house style of avoiding contractions in normative narrative.
2. One of those occurrences was authored with a non-ASCII / mojibake character in place of the apostrophe (rendered as `doesn’┐Įt`), which displays as a replacement character in browsers.
3. One occurrence of the British spelling `licence`, which is inconsistent with the otherwise-American spelling used elsewhere on the same page (e.g., `licensed`, `license approval` later in the document).

The reporter (Lloyd McKenzie, comment 7 of an automated QA sweep) flagged these as "Spelling/abbreviation issues ŌĆ” Abbreviations need to be fully spelled out." The work group accepted the fix as `Persuasive`.

### Proposed Change

Apply three single-token textual edits to `source/medication-definition-module.html`:

1. **`doesn’┐Įt` ŌåÆ `does not`** in the "Relationship to Prescribing" section paragraph that begins:

   > "The Medication resource, and others in the Medications Module ŌĆ” mainly cover the use of a medication 'by reference' (using a code) ŌĆ”"

   The current sentence reads (note the corrupted apostrophe): "So the Medication resource doesn’┐Įt define medicinal products to any extent, it is more of a selector."

   New text: "So the Medication resource does not define medicinal products to any extent, it is more of a selector."

2. **`doesn't` ŌåÆ `does not`** in the paragraph discussing the medicated-device boundary:

   > "A related example is deciding on the split between a medicated device and a medication, or a device. The workgroup doesn't attempt to define clinical opinion ŌĆ”"

   New text: "The workgroup does not attempt to define clinical opinion ŌĆ”"

3. **`licence` ŌåÆ `license`** in the paragraph contrasting clinical trial / drug licensing / dispense contexts:

   > "The drug info is the drug info and it exists whether it's a clinical trial, a drug licence approval, or a dispense."

   New text: "ŌĆ” whether it's a clinical trial, a drug license approval, or a dispense."

No other element, structure definition, terminology binding, schema, search parameter, or example is touched. There is no programmatic content (XML, JSON, FSH, ANTLR grammar) involved ŌĆö this is a single hand-authored HTML page.

### Design Rationale

- **House style.** The rest of the FHIR Core narrative uses fully-spelled forms ("does not", "is not", "do not") and American spelling ("license", "licensed", "organization"). Expanding the contraction and aligning the spelling is the minimum-surface change that brings these three sentences into conformance.
- **Defect-class fix.** The mojibake `doesn’┐Įt` is the same defect as the contraction issue ŌĆö the character corruption is only visible because the text contains a contraction. Expanding to `does not` removes the apostrophe entirely and eliminates the rendering bug as a side-effect of the spelling fix the work group already approved.
- **Authoring root.** Per the FhirCore briefing, narrative HTML pages under `source/*.html` are authored content edited in place; there is no generated form to regenerate and no FSH/IG-publisher pipeline to trigger.
- **Alternatives not chosen.** Replacing only the corrupted apostrophe (`’┐Į` ŌåÆ `'`) while leaving the contraction in place would fix the rendering glitch but would not satisfy the "abbreviations need to be fully spelled out" half of the resolution. Replacing `licence` with `licensed` (rather than `license`) would change the meaning ("a drug license approval" describes the approval, "a drug licensed approval" is ungrammatical).
