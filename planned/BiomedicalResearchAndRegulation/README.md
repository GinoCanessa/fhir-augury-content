### Change Request

#### Topic: Identifier Cardinality Alignment

Three tickets that extend identifier cardinality from single (0..1) to repeating (0..*) across the Medication Definition module, enabling multiple business identifiers from different naming authorities and registries to coexist. Two tickets (FHIR-55046 and FHIR-55057) directly widen identifier cardinality on core resources and were shipped together in the same ballot-note commit. The third ticket (FHIR-56036) extends the naming-authority terminology to support UMC, enabling proper identification of substances from the Uppsala Monitoring Centre SRS system.

##### Prerequisite Group: FHIR-55046

These tickets form a tightly coordinated change batch addressing identifier cardinality across the Biomedical Research & Regulation module. FHIR-55046 and FHIR-55057 cross-reference each other as related tickets and share the same resolution date (2026-04-07), same reporter (Lloyd McKenzie), and same ballot-note commit (f0c7b541), indicating they were reviewed and applied together as part of a single consistency pass. While not a strict dependency chain, the shared design rationale and simultaneous implementation make them natural prerequisite partners.
