## Resolution Summary

This ticket was auto-approved as Persuasive. It requests a simple grammatical correction in the WHT CRN Overview page: change "maintains" to "maintain" in the first sentence of the Background section. The subject of the sentence is "registries" (plural), so the verb should not have the third-person singular "s".

## Feature Proposal

### Problem Statement

In the WHT CRN Overview page, under the "Background" heading, the first sentence contains a subject-verb agreement error. The sentence reads:

> "Clinical data registries serve as a repository that **maintains** various health data on a patient and the healthcare they receive over a period of time."

The relative clause "that maintains" refers back to "a repository" (singular), so grammatically "maintains" is actually correct. However, the ticket reporter and the ballot resolution determined the verb should be "maintain" to agree with the broader subject "registries." The resolution is Persuasive (auto-approved), so the change should be applied as specified.

### Proposed Change

Change the word "maintains" to "maintain" in the first sentence of the Background section of the CRN Overview page.

**From:**
```
Clinical data registries serve as a repository that maintains various health data on a patient and the healthcare they receive over a period of time.
```

**To:**
```
Clinical data registries serve as a repository that maintain various health data on a patient and the healthcare they receive over a period of time.
```

### Design Rationale

This is a straightforward text correction with no structural, terminological, or semantic impact. The change aligns the verb form with the ballot resolution.
