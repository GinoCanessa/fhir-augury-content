## Summary

The reporter observes that the DICOM SR to FHIR Resource Mapping IG's
**Example** section includes what appears to be guidance on mapping a
`Patient` resource from DICOM SR content, but the IG does not otherwise
address patient identity matching or preservation of patient
demographics. The reporter assumes identity matching is out of scope for
this IG, yet the presence of a Patient mapping example is confusing: it
is unclear whether the example is purely illustrative or is intended to
constitute normative/informative guidance on patient mapping. The
reporter asks the work group to clarify the intent of the Example
section and update the IG accordingly — either by qualifying the example
as non-authoritative and pointing to identity-matching resources
elsewhere, or by adding actual guidance on patient identity handling.

No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were
found for this ticket, so the request stands on its own without a
broader linked discussion.

## Proposed Dispositions

Because there are no GitHub cross-references and no cached briefing for
the DICOM SR SR-to-FHIR Mapping IG, the dispositions below describe the
editorial change at the IG level (Example section / Patient mapping
page) without naming a specific authoring root. The work group should
translate the chosen disposition into the IG's actual source layout when
implementing.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Clarify the Example section of the DICOM SR to FHIR Resource Mapping IG
to state explicitly that:

1. Patient identity matching, identity resolution, and preservation of
   patient demographics across DICOM and FHIR systems are **out of
   scope** for this IG.
2. The `Patient` resource content shown in the Example section is a
   **minimal illustrative example only** — intended to make the
   surrounding SR-to-FHIR mapping example renderable and testable — and
   is **not normative or informative guidance** on how to populate or
   reconcile `Patient` in production.
3. Implementers needing guidance should consult the FHIR Patient
   Identity Matching IG (`HL7/fhir-identity-matching-ig`) and
   jurisdictional patient-matching guidance (e.g., US Core Patient,
   relevant IHE PIX/PDQ profiles).

Concretely, add a short "Scope note on Patient mapping" callout at the
top of the Example section (or on the Patient mapping subsection) with
the above statements, and annotate the example Patient JSON/XML with a
comment reinforcing that it is illustrative.

#### Justification

This directly answers the reporter's question ("is this meant to be
only example, or guidance on patient mapping?") with the least
disruption. The IG's stated focus is SR content mapping, not identity
management, so an explicit scope statement matches the IG's actual
design intent. Pointing to `HL7/fhir-identity-matching-ig` and
established matching profiles keeps implementers from reinventing
identity logic based on a toy example, and aligns with the broader FHIR
pattern of deferring cross-cutting concerns (identity, security,
consent) to dedicated IGs rather than embedding partial solutions in
domain mapping IGs.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than only adding a disclaimer, split the Patient handling into
two explicit pieces in the IG:

1. **Remove or relocate** the inline Patient resource from the main
   SR-to-FHIR mapping example, and reference an external example
   Patient (e.g., a named example bundle) so the mapping example no
   longer implies Patient construction is part of the mapping
   algorithm.
2. **Add a dedicated "Patient context" page** that (a) declares
   identity matching and demographic preservation as out of scope, (b)
   lists the minimum `Patient` elements the mapping logic depends on
   (e.g., `Patient.identifier`, `Patient.name`, `Patient.birthDate`,
   `Patient.gender`) and why, and (c) cross-links to
   `HL7/fhir-identity-matching-ig` and US Core / national Patient
   profiles as the authoritative sources for identity handling.

This keeps the Example section purely about SR content mapping while
giving the IG a principled, bounded statement on how Patient enters and
exits the mapping process.

#### Justification

Disposition A fixes the confusion but leaves the structural issue in
place: a Patient resource still appears inside a mapping example that
is not about Patient mapping. Disposition B removes the ambiguity at
its source by separating "example data needed to render the mapping"
from "guidance on Patient". It also gives implementers a durable page
to cite ("this IG declares identity matching out of scope and points
you here") which is more useful than a one-line disclaimer. The cost
is higher editorial effort and a small restructuring of the Example
section; the benefit is that future tickets in this area
(under-specified Patient handling, contradictions with other IGs) are
less likely to recur.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as "Not Persuasive" on the basis that the IG already
presents its Patient content as part of an example, and examples are —
by FHIR convention — illustrative rather than normative. No text
changes would be made; the reader is expected to interpret content
inside an Example section as non-authoritative.

#### Justification

A defensible argument for declining is that FHIR readers are expected
to understand the convention that examples are not conformance
requirements, and that adding a disclaimer in every place an example
touches an out-of-scope area would bloat specifications. If the work
group has already discussed and confirmed that identity matching is
clearly out of scope, the ticket could be treated as a reader
misunderstanding rather than a spec defect.

This is the weakest of the three because the reporter's confusion is
direct evidence that the convention is **not** self-evident in this
IG's context, and ignoring reporter-surfaced ambiguity tends to
generate repeat tickets.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is recommended. The underlying problem FHIR-47683
surfaces is not just wording — it is that a Patient resource sits
inside a mapping example without a stated relationship to patient
identity handling, and the IG provides no bounded statement of what it
does and does not say about Patient. Disposition A fixes the immediate
confusion but preserves the structural ambiguity, so the next reader
is likely to file a similar ticket. Disposition C ignores clear
reader-surfaced evidence that the current presentation misleads.
Disposition B costs modest editorial effort (one new short page, one
tightened example) and yields a durable, citable scope boundary that
cleanly delegates identity matching to `HL7/fhir-identity-matching-ig`
and jurisdictional Patient profiles — which is where that
responsibility belongs. The work group can fall back to Disposition A
if the editorial cost of a new page is judged too high for this
release cycle.
