## Summary

The reporter (Abderrazek Boufahja) asks that the *Radiation Dose Summary for
Diagnostic Procedures on FHIR* IG insert the registered-trademark sign (®)
beside reserved trademarks such as **FHIR**, **HL7**, **IHE**, and
**SNOMED** wherever they appear on the IG Home page, in line with the
"Trademarks" section of that page. The request is filed as a Technical
Correction under the Imaging Integration work group. The reporter has
already noted in a comment, after consulting the linked Zulip discussion,
that exhaustive marking is not required from a regulatory point of view —
"only few are sufficient." The associated Zulip thread on
`#ig-publishing-requirements > Enforcing (c) symbols` confirms this view:
HL7's licence guidance (`build.fhir.org/license.html#2.20.2.1`) only
asks for the ® symbol on the **first or dominant** use of FHIR® on each
page, and Grahame Grieve and Lloyd McKenzie agree that enforcing the
symbol everywhere would be excessive. The ticket therefore narrows in
practice to: ensure the IG Home page (and especially the dominant first
mentions on each page) uses the ® mark on the trademarks listed in its
own Trademarks section. There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets and
no GitHub PRs/issues linked to FHIR-37519.

### [ig publishing requirements > ✔ Enforcing (c) symbols](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/ig%20publishing%20requirements/topic/%E2%9C%94%20Enforcing%20%28c%29%20symbols)

Started by Grahame Grieve on 2022-05-30, this topic asks whether the IG
publisher should mechanically enforce the (c)/(r) marks. Grahame raised
[fhir-ig-publisher issue #449](https://github.com/HL7/fhir-ig-publisher/issues/449)
as a candidate but immediately said "I think it's not a good idea." The
reporter (Abderrazek Boufahja) initially argued for enforcing ® on
HL7®, FHIR®, and other known trademarks (DICOM, IHE), then — after
re-reading [`build.fhir.org/license.html#2.20.2.1`](https://build.fhir.org/license.html#2.20.2.1)
— concluded that the ® symbol need only appear on the first or
dominant use on each page, not every occurrence. Lloyd McKenzie clarified
that the (c) obligation falls on the copyright holder, not third
parties, and Grahame agreed that enforcing it everywhere would be "too
much." Abderrazek then **marked the topic as resolved** on 2022-05-31,
with the implicit consensus that exhaustive marking is unnecessary and
publisher-level enforcement should not be added. Later messages in the
same topic (June 2022, by Jorge Mansilla and Grahame Grieve) are an
unrelated registry-publishing question and do not change the ®
disposition.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Edit the *Radiation Dose Summary for Diagnostic Procedures on FHIR* IG
Home page so that every reserved trademark listed in its own Trademarks
section — at minimum **HL7®**, **FHIR®**, **IHE®**, **SNOMED CT®**, and
any others enumerated there (e.g., DICOM® if listed) — carries the ®
symbol. Apply the change to the authoring source of the home page (the
IG's `input/pagecontent/index.md` or equivalent narrative file) and
re-publish. Audit the rendered home page after the IG publisher run to
confirm each first mention on the page carries the ®.

#### Justification

This is the literal ask of the ticket and aligns with HL7's own licence
guidance, which expects FHIR® and HL7® to be marked at the first or
dominant occurrence on each page. The Trademarks section already
declares that these marks are reserved; the body of the home page
should be consistent with its own declaration. The change is purely
editorial, scoped to one IG, and carries no normative or schema risk.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Apply ® only on the **first or dominant occurrence** of each
trademarked term on the IG Home page (and, by the same rule, on every
other page of the IG that mentions HL7®/FHIR®/IHE®/SNOMED®), rather
than on every occurrence. Add a short "Use of trademarks in this IG"
sentence to the Trademarks section pointing readers at the
[HL7 licence guidance §2.20.2.1](https://build.fhir.org/license.html#2.20.2.1)
so future editors follow the same rule. Do **not** ask the IG publisher
to enforce this mechanically.

#### Justification

This is the explicit consensus reached on the linked Zulip thread
(`#ig publishing requirements > Enforcing (c) symbols`) — Grahame
Grieve, Lloyd McKenzie, and ultimately the reporter himself agreed that
exhaustive marking is excessive and that publisher-level enforcement
("not a good idea") would over-reach. It also matches HL7's actual
licence wording, which requires the ® symbol "as often as is practical,
at least once on each page … generally in connection with the first or
dominant usage." It satisfies the underlying need (legal/courtesy
acknowledgement of the trademark holders) while keeping the rendered
prose readable, and it gives the IG editor a written rule to apply to
future page additions without waiting for another ticket.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-37519 as **Not Persuasive — Already Addressed**, on the
grounds that (a) the IG already has a Trademarks section that
acknowledges HL7®, FHIR®, IHE®, and SNOMED® as reserved marks, (b)
HL7's own licence guidance does not require every occurrence to be
marked, and (c) the linked Zulip discussion was resolved with the
conclusion that enforcing ® everywhere would be "too much." No further
edit is needed.

#### Justification

Declining is defensible because the ticket conflates two things: a
*declaration* that these marks are reserved (which the IG already
provides) and a *usage rule* (which HL7 already publishes centrally and
which the reporter himself, after re-reading the licence text, agreed
does not require exhaustive marking). The Zulip thread was explicitly
marked resolved by the reporter on 2022-05-31, suggesting the
underlying concern was already settled informally before this Jira
ticket was triaged. Imaging-Integration WG bandwidth is better spent on
substantive ticket backlog than on a ® audit that the licence text
does not require.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B reflects the actual consensus the community already
reached on the Zulip thread that this very ticket points to: mark
trademarks on the **first/dominant** mention per page, not everywhere,
and don't ask the IG publisher to enforce it. It still gives the
reporter what he is genuinely asking for — visible ® acknowledgement on
the IG Home page — without forcing an over-broad edit pass that the
HL7 licence wording does not require, and without committing the
Imaging-Integration WG to police a publisher-level enforcement that
both Grahame Grieve and Lloyd McKenzie warned against. Disposition A is
acceptable in spirit but tends to drift into "® on every occurrence,"
which the same thread already rejected; Disposition C ignores the fact
that the home page narrative does not currently carry the marks at all
and so leaves a small but real editorial inconsistency with the IG's
own Trademarks section. B addresses that inconsistency, codifies the
rule for future pages, and respects the resolved community guidance.
