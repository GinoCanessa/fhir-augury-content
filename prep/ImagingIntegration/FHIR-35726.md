## Summary

FHIR-35726 is a formal comment letter submitted by Paul Nagy on behalf of
the American Association of Physicists in Medicine (AAPM), dated
January 10, 2022, on the proposed *Radiation Dose Summary for Diagnostic
Procedures on FHIR* specification. The letter expresses AAPM's
in-principle support for developing an HL7 FHIR mechanism to distribute
appropriate radiation dose information for diagnostic and interventional
imaging procedures that use ionizing radiation, with the goal of
promoting appropriate use of dose information beyond the imaging
department. AAPM specifically highlights value for high-dose
interventional procedures — where, although rare, doses may approach or
surpass deterministic-effect thresholds — and points to NCRP Report 168
for relevant processes and follow-up procedures. The ticket carries no
specific change request; it is a positive position statement on the
direction of the work. It was already resolved as **Considered — No
action required** on 2024-10-06, and a 2024-10-06 comment from
"mckenzie" notes that the ticket had previously been mistakenly linked
as a duplicate, and the status was not reverted when that link was
removed (explaining why it still shows as Triaged rather than Closed).

## Details

**Description:**

> January 10, 2022
> Health Level Seven International
> 3300 Washtenaw Avenue, Suite 227
> Ann Arbor, MI 48104 USA
> To Whom It May Concern:
>
> The American Association of Physicists in Medicine (AAPM) appreciates
> the opportunity to submit comments on the proposed new mechanism,
> Radiation Dose Summary for Diagnostic Procedures on FHIR.
>
> The AAPM would like to express its support, in principle, for
> developing an HL7 FHIR mechanism to distribute appropriate radiation
> dose information for diagnostic and interventional imaging procedures
> that use ionizing radiation, to promote its appropriate usage beyond
> the imaging department. The AAPM believes that the appropriate
> implementation and use of such a mechanism could improve patient
> care, helping automate and expand important processes, especially
> prior to and after the performance of potentially high-dose
> interventional procedures. Although rare, some of these procedures
> may result in patient doses that approach or surpass thresholds at
> which certain tissue damage, due to deterministic effects, can take
> place. The introduction of the proposed mechanism could help further
> minimize these events and/or improve patient care if these thresholds
> are indeed surpassed. More information on the processes involved and
> procedures to be followed related to these high-dose interventional
> procedures can be found in Report 168 of the National Council on
> Radiation Protection and Measurements.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Accept the AAPM submission as a formal letter of in-principle support
for the *Radiation Dose Summary for Diagnostic Procedures on FHIR*
specification. Concretely: acknowledge receipt in the work-group
minutes; record AAPM's endorsement against the specification's
stakeholder/comment log; and add a non-normative note to the IG's
introduction stating that AAPM has expressed in-principle support and
specifically highlighted the value of this mechanism for high-dose
interventional procedures, with a citation to NCRP Report 168 for
context on follow-up of patients who may approach or exceed
deterministic-effect thresholds.

#### Justification

The submission is a position statement from a recognised professional
society (AAPM) representing medical physicists — a key stakeholder
group for any radiation dose IG. Recording the endorsement and citing
NCRP Report 168 in the IG introduction adds no normative burden but
strengthens the IG's stakeholder/clinical-rationale narrative, which is
material that ballot reviewers commonly look for. It also creates a
durable pointer to the high-dose interventional use case that AAPM
called out as the primary clinical motivator.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Treat the AAPM letter as input — rather than as content to be
reproduced in the IG — and use it to drive a single concrete editorial
change: add (or expand) a "Clinical Motivation / Patient-Safety
Rationale" section in the IG's narrative that explicitly calls out
high-dose interventional procedures and the deterministic-effect
threshold scenario, citing NCRP Report 168. Acknowledge the AAPM
submission in the comment-disposition log without embedding the
endorsement text in the IG itself.

#### Justification

IG narrative is more durable and more useful to implementers than
captured endorsements, which can age poorly (organisations change
positions, contact people move on). Distilling the substance of the
letter — *why* the IG matters clinically, and *which* procedures it most
benefits — into the IG's own voice keeps the rationale aligned with the
spec as it evolves, while still honouring the input. This avoids the
risk of the IG appearing to speak on AAPM's behalf in perpetuity.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (Considered — No Action Required)

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket as **Considered — No action required**: thank the
submitter, log the endorsement in the work-group's comment-disposition
record, and close without changing the specification. The letter does
not request a specific change to any resource, profile, value set, or
narrative section, so there is nothing concrete to action against the
spec.

#### Justification

The submission is explicitly an in-principle endorsement, not a change
request. It identifies no defect, no missing element, no profile
constraint to add, and proposes no specific edit. FHIR ballot
disposition practice is to close such items as "Considered" so that the
input is recorded without forcing a synthetic change. The 2024-10-06
resolution on the ticket records exactly this outcome.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** C

The ticket contains no actionable change request — it is a
professional-society endorsement letter — so closing as **Considered —
No action required** is the correct ballot-disposition outcome and
matches the resolution already recorded on 2024-10-06. This should be
paired with two small housekeeping steps: (1) reset the workflow status
so the ticket no longer shows as *Triaged* (the 2024-10-06 comment
notes that the status was not updated when an incorrect duplicate link
was removed, which is why it still appears open); and (2) ensure the
AAPM endorsement and its NCRP Report 168 reference are captured in the
work group's comment-disposition log so the input is not lost. If the
work group later wants to lift the AAPM rationale into the IG's
narrative, that is better handled as a separate, scoped editorial
ticket rather than by reopening this one.
