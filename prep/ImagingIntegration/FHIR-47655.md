## Summary

The reporter, reviewing the *DICOM SR to FHIR Resource Mapping IG*, asks
the Imaging Integration WG to improve the readability of the IG by
cross-linking the subsections of **Section 4 — "Mapping"** of the IG
narrative with the per-profile **"Mappings"** views (the FHIR mapping
elements emitted on each StructureDefinition page). She also asks that
the WG verify the *content* on those two surfaces actually agrees, since
the current narrative and the per-profile mapping tables are maintained
separately and can drift apart.

This is a presentation/editorial change request against the IG — no new
profiles, elements, or terminology are being requested. There are no
cross-referenced Jira tickets, GitHub items, or Confluence pages
recorded for this issue in Augury.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply two coordinated edits to the IG sources at `HL7/dicom-sr`:

1. **Add anchored, bidirectional links between Section 4 and the
   per-profile Mappings tabs.** In the IG's page-content authoring root
   (typically `input/pagecontent/`), in the page that renders Section 4
   "Mapping" and each of its subsections, add a *"Profile mapping:"*
   link list pointing to the corresponding StructureDefinition page's
   `#mappings` anchor (e.g. `Observation-imaging-measurement.html#mappings`).
   Conversely, on each profile that participates in TID 1500 emission,
   ensure its `StructureDefinition.mapping[*].uri` and `name` resolve to
   the same Section 4 subsection (so the rendered Mappings tab
   hyperlinks back into Section 4).
2. **Normalize the mapping content itself.** For every profile referenced
   in Section 4, audit `StructureDefinition.mapping` (in the FSH or
   JSON sources under `input/fsh/` or `input/resources/`) so the
   element-level `^mapping[dicom-sr].map` strings match the prose in the
   corresponding Section 4 subsection — same source TID/code, same
   target FHIR element, same conditional notes. Where the prose and the
   element mapping disagree, the WG decides which is canonical and the
   other is updated. Add a regression check (a small script or a CI
   note) that flags Section-4 subsection headings that have no profile
   pointing back at them, and vice versa.

#### Justification

The ticket is purely an editorial / authoring request, not a normative
change. FHIR IGs already have a first-class mechanism for per-element
mappings (`StructureDefinition.mapping`), and the IG publisher renders
those on the profile's "Mappings" tab automatically; tying Section 4
sub-headings to that rendered output (and vice versa) is the standard,
low-risk way to keep two surfaces describing the same mapping in sync.
Doing the cross-link plus a one-time alignment pass directly fulfills
both halves of the reporter's ask ("link" *and* "make sure the content
is aligned"). The ballot label `II_SEP_2024_SR_Mapping` indicates this
was raised against the September 2024 ballot of the Mapping IG, so the
fix belongs in the next publication cycle of `hl7.fhir.uv.dicom-sr`.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than maintaining hand-curated cross-links and prose in two
places, **make Section 4 a generated view over the per-profile mappings
and retire the duplicated prose**. Concretely:

- Keep `StructureDefinition.mapping` on every profile as the *single
  source of truth* for the DICOM SR → FHIR mapping (in
  `input/fsh/`).
- Replace the body of Section 4's subsections with an IG-publisher
  *fragment include* (or a small build-time script that emits markdown
  from the StructureDefinitions) that lists, per TID/concept, the
  profiles and elements that consume it, with a deep link to each
  profile's Mappings tab.
- Keep only narrative / context paragraphs hand-authored in Section 4
  (rationale, scope, examples), not the mapping tables themselves.

#### Justification

The root cause the reporter is pointing at — drift between Section 4
prose and the per-profile Mappings tab — is structural: any solution
that leaves the same fact stated in two editable places will drift
again at the next ballot. Generating Section 4 from the
StructureDefinition mappings eliminates the divergence by construction,
reduces the editorial burden on the WG, and keeps the reporter's
"linking" request as a side-effect of generation. The trade-off vs.
Disposition A is up-front tooling cost and reduced freedom to write
prose-only mapping notes; that cost is offset across every future
publication cycle of the IG.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket *Persuasive with Modification — No Change* (or *Not
Persuasive*) on the grounds that (a) the per-profile Mappings tab is
already the FHIR-canonical place to read element-level mappings and is
reachable from each profile page, and (b) Section 4 of the IG is
intended as a human-readable narrative *about* the mapping approach
rather than a normative restatement of the per-element mappings, so
exact alignment is neither required nor desirable. Close with a note
directing implementers to use the per-profile Mappings tab as
authoritative.

#### Justification

There is a defensible reading where the two surfaces are deliberately
different: Section 4 is prose *about* the mapping methodology and
representative examples, while `StructureDefinition.mapping` carries the
machine-readable, element-level facts. Under that reading, the
reporter's complaint is addressed by clarifying which surface is
authoritative rather than by editing every subsection. This is the
lowest-effort disposition and avoids opening a structural rewrite of
Section 4 mid-cycle, but it leaves the reporter's actual usability
complaint (navigation friction and content drift) unaddressed.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The request is small, well-scoped, and the reporter's two asks
(cross-linking + content alignment) have a direct, low-risk
implementation path using mechanisms the IG publisher already
supports. Disposition B is the structurally cleanest answer, but
introduces build-time tooling work that should not block resolving an
editorial ticket; it is a good follow-up to file separately if the WG
wants to pursue it. Disposition C does not actually address the
reported friction and risks the same complaint reappearing in the next
ballot. Recommend the WG accept the ticket and schedule the cross-link
+ alignment pass against `HL7/dicom-sr` in the next publication of
`hl7.fhir.uv.dicom-sr`. If the WG wants concrete file-path proposals,
run `repo-analysis HL7/dicom-sr` and re-prep this ticket so the
Disposition A proposal can name the exact `input/pagecontent/*.md` and
`input/fsh/*.fsh` files to touch.
