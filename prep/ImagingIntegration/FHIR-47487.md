## Summary

The reporter notes that, in the DICOM SR to FHIR Resource Mapping IG, the
example subsections under section 5 use tables whose columns are not
self-explanatory and are not consistent across subsections. In particular,
section 5.5 has a different second and third column from the surrounding
subsections, and section 5.6 omits the third column entirely. The request
is to add clarifying text — most simply, column descriptions or a per-table
legend — so that a reader can tell what each column represents (and why the
shape differs) without having to reverse-engineer the intent from the
content of the cells.

No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were
returned by FhirAugury for this ticket key, so the request stands on its
own with no broader thread of related discussion.

## Details

## Proposed Dispositions

> Note: Because no GitHub cross-references were surfaced, the dispositions
> below cannot name a specific repo or authoring path from a briefing.
> They are scoped to narrative changes in the DICOM SR to FHIR Resource
> Mapping IG's section 5 example subsections — wherever that IG is
> currently authored by the Imaging Integration WG.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update each example subsection under section 5 of the DICOM SR to FHIR
Resource Mapping IG so that every example mapping table is preceded by a
short prose description naming each column (e.g., "Column 1: DICOM SR
content item path; Column 2: corresponding FHIR element; Column 3: notes
on the mapping"). For sections that legitimately deviate from the common
column shape — explicitly section 5.5 (different second/third column) and
section 5.6 (no third column) — call the deviation out and explain *why*
the columns differ for that example. Where the same columns recur across
many subsections, hoist a single "How to read these tables" paragraph to
the top of section 5 and have each subsection only describe the
deltas.

#### Justification

This is the minimum change that satisfies what the reporter is asking
for: the request is purely about narrative clarity, not about changing
the underlying mappings. IG conventions in HL7 (e.g., Patterns of
labeled, legend-bearing example tables in US Core, IPS, and the base
spec's mapping pages) already favor explicit column descriptions, so
adopting them here is consistent with how readers expect FHIR IG
narrative to behave. It also costs nothing in terms of computable
content — only `pages/` / intro narrative is touched — so the risk of
collateral breakage is low.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than only annotating each table, refactor section 5 so that all
example tables share a single canonical column shape, and treat sections
5.5 and 5.6 as the cases that must be brought into line (or, if their
content genuinely cannot fit the canonical shape, split them into a
separate "non-uniform examples" section with its own clearly-described
schema). Add a single "How to read example tables" subsection at the top
of section 5 that defines the canonical columns once. Sections that
cannot follow the canonical shape get an inline note pointing back to
that subsection and listing the specific column overrides.

#### Justification

The reporter's concrete pain point is not just unlabeled columns — it is
that the columns *differ between subsections without explanation*.
Disposition A makes the differences readable; Disposition B removes
most of the differences in the first place, which is a stronger fix and
reduces the long-term maintenance burden of keeping per-section legends
in sync with table contents. The cost is higher: someone has to revisit
each example to confirm its content can be expressed in the canonical
shape, and 5.5 / 5.6 may need real editorial judgement (or even
re-modeling of the example) rather than just a legend. That cost is
worth absorbing once if the IG is still being actively shaped, but is
hard to justify if the examples have already been reviewed and approved
in their current form.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket as "Not Persuasive" / decline on the grounds that the
column meanings are inferable from the column headers in conjunction with
the surrounding section narrative, and that intentional structural
variation between examples (e.g., 5.5 and 5.6) is itself informative —
forcing a uniform shape would obscure the fact that some SR examples do
not have a meaningful third-column counterpart. Leave the IG narrative
as-is.

#### Justification

A decline is defensible only if the WG believes the existing headers and
surrounding prose already give a competent reader enough to interpret
each table, and that the cross-section variation is a feature rather
than a bug. It avoids editorial churn during a publication cycle and
keeps reviewer attention focused on substantive mapping issues. The
weakness is that the request comes from an actual implementer-reader who
found the tables confusing in practice, which is direct evidence against
the "inferable from headers alone" position; declining without at least
revisiting the headers risks the same complaint resurfacing in the next
ballot.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter is asking for a narrative-only clarification in an IG that
is still being shaped by the Imaging Integration WG, and the cost of
adding explicit column descriptions (plus a one-line "why this section
differs" note for 5.5 and 5.6) is very small relative to the recurring
reader-confusion cost of leaving the tables unlabeled. Disposition B is
attractive in principle, but unifying the column shape requires real
editorial judgement about whether 5.5 and 5.6's structural deviations
are intentional, and that judgement is better made as a follow-on
ticket once the columns are at least documented. Declining (Disposition
C) is hard to justify against direct reader feedback that the current
tables are "quite confusing." Accept as requested, and let the WG open
a follow-up if it later decides the per-section variation should be
collapsed.
