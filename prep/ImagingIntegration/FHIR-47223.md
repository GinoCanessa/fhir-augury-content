## Summary

The reporter observes that the "Example" section of the DICOM SR to FHIR
Resource Mapping IG mixes two distinct kinds of content: (1) the conceptual
mapping rules between DICOM SR constructs and FHIR resources, which she
considers normative guidance, and (2) concrete, illustrative DICOM/FHIR
resource instances that demonstrate those rules. She requests that the IG
be restructured so the mapping concepts are promoted out of the Example
section and presented as first-class normative guidelines, with the
specific resource instances retained as examples of those guidelines. No
cross-referenced Jira tickets were surfaced for this issue.

## Details

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Restructure the IG so that the mapping concepts currently embedded in the
"Example" section are extracted into a new top-level (normative) chapter
— for example, "Mapping Guidance" or "Mapping Rules" — that sits alongside
existing narrative pages of the DICOM SR to FHIR Resource Mapping IG. Each
mapping rule (e.g., how a DICOM SR Container/CodedEntry/Numeric/TextValue
maps to its FHIR Observation/DiagnosticReport/Bundle counterparts) is
written as prose guidance with a rule statement, scope, and any
conformance language ("SHALL"/"SHOULD"). The current Example section is
kept, but each example is re-pointed to the rule it illustrates and is
trimmed to the concrete DICOM and FHIR resource instances (no rule text in
the example narrative). Update the IG's table of contents and any
inter-page links accordingly.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter is asking for, and it follows the
standard HL7 IG authoring pattern of separating normative guidance from
illustrative examples (mirroring how FHIR core, US Core, and most other
IGs structure "Guidance" vs "Examples"). Promoting the mapping rules
out of the example narrative makes them discoverable, citable, and
testable, and lets implementers reason about conformance without having
to reverse-engineer rules from instance data. The change is editorial
and structural — no resource model or terminology changes are required.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Leave the existing "Example" section in place, but introduce a new
"Mapping Guidance" page that contains only the conceptual rules (using
short prose plus tabular SR-construct → FHIR-element mappings), and
cross-link from each example back to the relevant guidance entry and
vice versa. The examples themselves are not rewritten; they continue to
carry inline commentary, but a banner at the top of the Example section
clarifies that any conformance-style statements in the examples are
informative and that the normative source is the new Mapping Guidance
page. Optionally, mark conformance-language sentences in the Mapping
Guidance with explicit `{:.normative}` styling to make their status
unambiguous.

#### Justification

This satisfies the underlying need (a clean place to find the mapping
rules) without requiring a full rewrite of the Example section, which
reduces the risk of regressions in worked examples that implementers
may already be referencing. Adding a guidance page is additive and
backward-compatible; existing deep links into examples keep working. The
trade-off versus Disposition A is some duplication (rules appear both in
guidance and, contextually, near examples) and a continued risk of
drift between the two — but for an IG at this maturity level, additive
restructuring is typically lower-cost than a full re-organization.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline as out of scope for the current ballot cycle, on the grounds
that the request is editorial rather than substantive, that no concrete
mapping rules are alleged to be wrong, and that the existing Example
section already conveys the intended guidance to implementers who read
it in context. Suggest the reporter revisit the question once the IG
reaches a higher maturity level (FMM ≥ 3 / Trial Use → Normative
transition), at which point a structural reorganization can be planned
holistically.

#### Justification

This is defensible if the work group judges that the IG is still
actively evolving and that locking in a "normative guidance"
chapter now would be premature — once promoted, those statements
acquire conformance weight and become harder to change. Declining also
avoids spending editorial effort on a restructure that may need to be
redone after subsequent ballot feedback. The cost is leaving the
reported usability problem unaddressed, and signaling to the reporter
that structural feedback isn't actionable at this stage.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter's observation is a real authoring smell: rules and examples
should not be intermixed in an IG that is meant to drive interoperable
DICOM SR → FHIR mappings, because implementers cannot tell which
sentences they are required to honor and which are merely
illustrative. Disposition A directly fixes that, aligns the IG with
standard HL7 authoring conventions, and is purely editorial — there is
no model or terminology risk. Disposition B is a reasonable lower-effort
fallback if the work group is constrained on editorial bandwidth, but it
leaves the duplication/drift problem on the table. Disposition C is hard
to justify given that the change carries no normative risk and the
reporter has already done the diagnostic work of identifying the
section that needs to be split. Recommend accepting and assigning the
restructure to the IG editor, with the existing examples re-pointed to
the new guidance entries rather than rewritten from scratch.
