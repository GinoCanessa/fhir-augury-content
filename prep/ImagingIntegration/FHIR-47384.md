## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the DICOM SR to FHIR Resource Mapping IG so that the diagram of
the **Imaging Measurements Container** on the page where the ticket
was filed is **redrawn to match the diagram in section 4.1**. Treat
section 4.1's diagram as the authoritative version (since it appears
in the dedicated structural section), regenerate or hand-edit the
second diagram to use the same shapes, labels, cardinalities, and
layout conventions, and republish. No normative text or mappings need
to change — this is a visual-only correction.

#### Justification

The ticket is editorial: the description explicitly says "please
align", not "please change the model". An IG that shows two
inconsistent diagrams of the same container is a documentation defect
that confuses implementers and undermines confidence in the rest of
the IG's mappings. Accepting as requested is low-risk, low-cost, and
purely improves clarity. Section 4.1 is the natural source of truth
because it is the structural reference section for the container.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than redrawing one diagram to match the other, **consolidate to
a single canonical diagram** of the Imaging Measurements Container,
authored once (e.g., as a single PlantUML / SVG asset in the IG
source) and **transcluded/included** in both section 4.1 and the page
that originally showed the divergent version. Remove the duplicate
hand-maintained copy entirely. Optionally, while the diagram is being
re-authored, re-check it against the current profile definitions
(Observation, ImagingSelection, ServiceRequest references) and fix any
labels, cardinalities, or relationships that have drifted from the
StructureDefinitions.

#### Justification

The root cause of this ticket is not "one diagram is wrong" but "two
diagrams of the same thing exist and were maintained independently".
Aligning them once (Disposition A) leaves the same drift hazard in
place — the next edit to either page will recreate the inconsistency.
A single-source diagram eliminates the class of bug. The incremental
cost is small (one extra refactor of the IG sources), and the
opportunity to also reconcile the diagram against the actual
StructureDefinitions catches any latent semantic drift that a
visual-only alignment (Disposition A) would miss.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as **Not Persuasive / Editorial — Out of Scope for this
ballot cycle** if the IG is in a frozen state for ballot, on the
grounds that the inconsistency is purely cosmetic and does not affect
the normative mappings or the conformance of any FHIR artifact.
Document the known inconsistency in the IG's errata / known issues
list and defer the diagram alignment to the next publication.

#### Justification

The ticket reports a presentation defect, not a semantic or
conformance defect: the StructureDefinitions, ValueSets, and mapping
tables are unaffected. If the IG is mid-ballot or about to publish, a
diagram refresh can introduce republication churn (broken anchors,
re-rendered figure numbers, image diffs) for no normative benefit.
Recording it as a known issue preserves transparency without blocking
the release. This disposition is only defensible if there is an
imminent publication constraint; otherwise the cost of fixing is
trivially small and Disposition A or B is preferable.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The ticket as written is solvable by Disposition A, and A would close
the ticket cleanly. But the underlying defect is duplicated,
hand-maintained diagrams of the same container — exactly the
condition that produced this ticket in the first place. Spending a
small amount of additional editorial effort to single-source the
Imaging Measurements Container diagram (and, while the file is open,
sanity-check the labels and cardinalities against the actual
profiles) prevents the same ticket from being re-filed against the
next revision. Disposition C is only justifiable if the work group is
under a hard publication freeze; for an unresolved Medium-priority
editorial ticket that has been open since September 2024, that
constraint does not appear to apply. Recommend B, with A as an
acceptable fallback if the IG sources do not lend themselves to a
shared-diagram include mechanism.
