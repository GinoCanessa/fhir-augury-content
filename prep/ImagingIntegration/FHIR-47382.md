## Summary

The reporter notes that in one of the DICOM SR → FHIR mapping diagrams in the
*DICOM SR to FHIR Resource Mapping IG*, only the DICOM **Preliminary Flag** is
shown being mapped to the corresponding FHIR target, while the related
**Completion Flag** and **Verification Flag** (the other two TID 1500-style
status flags that travel with a structured report) are not represented. They
ask the authors to clarify why only the Preliminary Flag was mapped and
whether the Completion Flag and Verification Flag should also be mapped — and
if so, where they would land on the FHIR side (e.g., `DiagnosticReport.status`,
`Composition.status`, or an extension).

The single substantive comment on the ticket, from `jwhitby` (2025-10-29),
captures exactly the open question the IG editors need to resolve before the
ticket can be dispositioned: "Unclear what the destination for these would
be." In other words, the authoring team agrees the omission is real but has
not yet identified a FHIR-side target element for those two additional flags.

No cross-references (Jira, Zulip, GitHub, Confluence) were returned for this

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the SR-status mapping in the *DICOM SR to FHIR Resource Mapping IG*
so that all three TID 1500 status flags are mapped, not just the Preliminary
Flag:

- **Preliminary Flag** → continue to drive a "preliminary" status on the
  generated FHIR report (`DiagnosticReport.status = preliminary` and/or
  `Composition.status = preliminary`).
- **Completion Flag** → drive the "final" / "amended" axis of
  `DiagnosticReport.status` and `Composition.status` (COMPLETE → `final`;
  any post-complete revision → `amended` / `corrected`, mirroring the DICOM
  semantics of a completed-then-revised SR).
- **Verification Flag** → drive whether the report is treated as verified.
  Map VERIFIED to a populated `Composition.attester` (mode `professional` /
  `legal`) and/or `DiagnosticReport.resultsInterpreter`, and use UNVERIFIED
  to keep `Composition.attester` empty and `DiagnosticReport.status` at
  `preliminary` even when the Completion Flag is COMPLETE.

Update both the prose and the diagram(s) under the "SR Document General"
mapping section of the IG so the diagram shows arrows for all three flags
into their FHIR destinations, and add a small mapping table that ties each
flag value to the resulting FHIR status / attester state.

#### Justification

This is the literal answer the submitter is asking for and the only
disposition that fully closes the gap the diagram exposes. The three flags
are not redundant in DICOM SR — Completion and Verification are independent
state machines on top of Preliminary — so a mapping that drops two of them
silently loses information that downstream FHIR consumers need to decide
whether they may rely on the report. FHIR already provides natural targets
for each flag (`DiagnosticReport.status` / `Composition.status` for the
preliminary↔final↔amended axis, and `Composition.attester` /
`DiagnosticReport.resultsInterpreter` for the verification axis), so no new
extensions are required, and the change is purely additive to the IG's
mapping content.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than expanding the diagram with three independent arrows, replace the
single "Preliminary Flag" arrow with one consolidated **"SR status flags"**
mapping element, and document the mapping as a derived rule in prose:

- A new normative table in the "SR Document General" mapping section that
  takes the tuple `(Preliminary Flag, Completion Flag, Verification Flag)`
  as input and produces the resulting `DiagnosticReport.status`,
  `Composition.status`, and presence/absence of `Composition.attester`.
- The diagram is updated to show a single labelled edge ("SR status flags →
  report status / attestation") pointing at both `DiagnosticReport` and
  `Composition`, with the table cited as the authoritative rule.
- Where the DICOM combination has no clean FHIR analogue (e.g.,
  Completion = COMPLETE + Verification = UNVERIFIED), the table prescribes
  the conservative choice (`status = preliminary`, no attester) and a
  `Composition.note` / `DiagnosticReport.conclusion` annotation explaining
  the source state.

#### Justification

The mapping is genuinely a 3-input → 2-output decision, not three
independent assignments — Completion and Verification interact (a "complete
but unverified" SR is not the same FHIR state as "complete and verified"),
and modeling them as three parallel diagram arrows can mislead implementers
into producing inconsistent reports. Driving the mapping from a single
table keeps the diagram readable, makes the interaction explicit, and gives
implementers a single normative rule to test against. It also addresses
jwhitby's open concern — "Unclear what the destination for these would be"
— by forcing the IG to commit to one canonical destination per combination
rather than per flag.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request as currently scoped, on the grounds that the IG's stated
scope for this diagram is limited to the *Preliminary Flag* path because that
is the only status the source DICOM SR profile being mapped is required to
populate; Completion and Verification are out of scope for this particular
mapping table. Add a one-sentence note next to the diagram saying so and
referring readers to the general SR status discussion elsewhere in the IG.

#### Justification

This is defensible only if the editors confirm that (a) the source DICOM SR
template the diagram targets does not in practice carry meaningful
Completion / Verification values, and (b) those flags are already covered
by another mapping in the IG. If both are true, expanding this diagram adds
noise without adding semantic content, and the right fix is an editorial
clarification of scope rather than a new mapping. The risk is that this
reading is not consistent with the comment from `jwhitby` — "Unclear what
the destination for these would be" suggests the editors have not in fact
identified an existing home for these flags, in which case declining would
leave a real gap.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the strongest starting point for the workgroup. It
acknowledges what jwhitby's comment makes clear — that the editors have not
yet committed to a destination for the Completion and Verification flags —
and resolves it by treating the three flags as a single decision with a
documented truth table, rather than as three independent diagram arrows
that risk producing inconsistent FHIR output. It gives the submitter the
substantive mapping they are asking for (Disposition A's coverage), but
avoids the trap of pretending the three flags are orthogonal, which is the
real reason the diagram only shows one arrow today. Disposition C should
only be revisited if the editors can demonstrate that the underlying SR
template does not carry the other two flags in practice; nothing on the
ticket today supports that position.
