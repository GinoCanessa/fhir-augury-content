## Summary

The reporter observes that the DICOM SR to FHIR Resource Mapping IG's
`ImagingMeasurementGroup` profile populates both `Observation.code` and
`Observation.value[x]` while also using `Observation.hasMember` to point at
component observations. Per the FHIR core Observation guidance on grouped
observations (https://build.fhir.org/observation.html#gr-other), the canonical
"grouping" pattern is to set `Observation.code` (to identify the group) and to
leave `Observation.value[x]` empty, with the constituent observations linked
via `hasMember`. The ticket therefore asserts that the IG's modeling of
measurement groups is inconsistent with the FHIR core pattern and asks the
work group either to drop `value[x]` from the group observation or to justify
the deviation. No companion Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were
surfaced in the cross-reference index, so the discussion is contained entirely
within this ticket and its two comments.

## Details

**Description:**

The FHIR specification proposes a modeling pattern for grouped observations
using `.hasMember`, in which only a `.code` is populated, and the `.value` is
empty (https://build.fhir.org/observation.html#gr-other). Hence, filling
`ImagingMeasurementGroup.code` AND `.value` goes against what the spec
suggests.

**Comments:**

- **jwhitby (2024-09-23):** Agrees this goes against the described pattern but
  notes that the core text says this is "not typically done" rather than
  forbidden. Points out that for measurement groups, `code` is mapped to
  Finding Category (typically describing the *type* of observation), while the
  Finding specifies the observation value — i.e., the IG is using `code` and
  `value[x]` to carry distinct semantic content (category vs. finding) rather
  than redundant grouping information.

- **ana_kostadinovska (2025-07-14):** Concedes that the core guidance is
  guidance and not a requirement. Suggests that if there are reasons to
  populate both `code` and `value[x]` on the group observation, the IG should
  add an explicit explanation describing why it deviates from the FHIR
  modeling pattern.

**Resolution (Not Persuasive):** The same FHIR Observation page also describes
a pattern in which both `Observation.code` and `Observation.value[x]` are
present alongside linked observations listed in `Observation.derivedFrom` —
explicitly using BMI (derived from height and weight) as the worked example.
The work-group response is that this "derived" pattern is the better fit for
the imaging measurement-group case, so populating both `code` and `value[x]`
on the group observation is not actually inconsistent with FHIR core.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Change the `ImagingMeasurementGroup` profile in the *DICOM SR to FHIR
Resource Mapping IG* so that the group-level Observation no longer carries a
`value[x]`. Specifically: forbid (or strongly discourage via cardinality 0..0
or a `prohibited` slice) `Observation.value[x]` on `ImagingMeasurementGroup`,
keep `Observation.code` to identify the group (mapped from Finding
Category), and require that any quantitative finding be carried on a member
observation referenced via `Observation.hasMember`. Update the IG narrative
and examples to follow the canonical "grouped observations" pattern at
https://build.fhir.org/observation.html#gr-other.

#### Justification

This aligns the IG strictly with the FHIR core grouping pattern, eliminates
ambiguity for implementers about whether to read the group's `value[x]` or
walk into the members, and removes a frequent source of confusion in
cross-IG validation tooling that flags `code` + `value[x]` + `hasMember` as
suspicious. The reporter and a member of the IG team both agreed in
comments that the current modeling deviates from the documented pattern.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the current `ImagingMeasurementGroup` shape (both `code` and `value[x]`
populated, members linked) but **re-anchor it on `hasMember`'s sibling
pattern, `derivedFrom`**, in IG narrative and examples. Concretely: clarify
in the profile's intro and on the relevant element definitions that the
group Observation is a *derived* observation (BMI-style), where `code`
identifies the derived measurement, `value[x]` carries the derived value,
and the constituent SR measurements are referenced via
`Observation.derivedFrom` (or via `hasMember` when the group is a pure
container with no derived value). Add an explicit note — as
`ana_kostadinovska` suggested — explaining why the IG diverges from the
"empty value[x]" grouping pattern and pointing at the FHIR core "derived"
text that legitimises both elements being populated together. Where the
group genuinely has no derived value, drop `value[x]` to match Disposition
A's pattern.

#### Justification

This is a documentation- and slicing-level fix rather than a structural
change. It honours `jwhitby`'s observation that `code` and `value[x]` carry
*different* semantic content in the imaging case (Finding Category vs.
Finding) and matches the resolver's own rationale that the derived-BMI
example in the FHIR core text is the better analogue. It avoids an IG
breaking change for existing senders while still making the IG defensible
against future challenges of the "code + value[x] + hasMember" combination.
The trade-off vs. A is that implementers must read the IG's deviation note
and understand the `hasMember` vs. `derivedFrom` distinction; the win is
backward compatibility and preservation of clinically meaningful
distinctions already encoded in DICOM SR.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as **Not Persuasive** (the current resolution): the FHIR core
Observation guidance explicitly permits Observations to carry both `code`
and `value[x]` while also linking to other observations — the BMI example
under the "derived observations" subsection is the canonical illustration.
The IG's `ImagingMeasurementGroup` is best understood as a *derived*
observation, not a pure grouping observation, so the modeling is consistent
with FHIR core and no spec change is required.

#### Justification

The reporter's argument rests on the assumption that the group is a "pure"
grouping observation, which is the case the `gr-other` text addresses. The
DICOM SR mapping does not fit that assumption: the group observation carries
a Finding (value) in addition to a Finding Category (code) and references
constituent measurements. The FHIR core text explicitly anticipates this
case via `derivedFrom`. Declining is defensible because (a) FHIR core does
not forbid the combination, (b) the IG semantics genuinely differ from a
container-only grouping observation, and (c) accepting the change as
literally requested would break IG content that relies on conveying the
Finding value at the group level.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition C (the current Not Persuasive resolution) correctly identifies
that the IG's modeling is permissible under FHIR core, but it leaves the
exact issue the reporter raised — the *appearance* of inconsistency —
unaddressed, and both the reporter and an IG team member explicitly asked in
comments for a written explanation of the deviation. Disposition A is the
cleanest from a "follow the canonical pattern" standpoint but discards
information that DICOM SR genuinely conveys at the group level (Finding +
Finding Category), and would be a breaking change for existing IG
implementers. Disposition B keeps the IG semantics intact, formally adopts
the resolver's own rationale (the derived/BMI pattern) inside the IG
narrative, switches to or adds `derivedFrom` where it sharpens the model,
and adds the explanatory note the reporter asked for. That gives the
work-group a path to keep the resolution effectively "Not Persuasive" on the
structural change while still acting on the legitimate documentation gap the
ticket exposed.
