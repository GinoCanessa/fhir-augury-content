## Summary

The reporter flags an ambiguity in the **Boundaries and Relationships**
section that is mirrored across the `ImagingStudy` and `ImagingSelection`
resource pages in FHIR Core. The current text contrasts an `ImagingStudy`
(explicitly typed as a "DICOM Study") with the `ImagingSelection` resource
which "is used to reference a subset of a study". Because the second
sentence uses the bare word "study" — neither qualified as "DICOM study"
nor scoped to an `ImagingStudy` resource instance — it is unclear whether
`ImagingSelection` references a subset of a DICOM Study (the
informational object) or of an `ImagingStudy` resource instance (the FHIR
representation). The reporter asks whether the vagueness is intentional
and requests clarification. The ticket has been **Triaged as Persuasive**
and a concrete text fix has been proposed in the resolution: insert
"DICOM" before "study", and apply the corrected wording to both pages
since the paragraph is duplicated.

No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip discussions, or GitHub items were
returned by the data sources for this ticket; the report below reflects
that absence and works only from the ticket's own description and
resolution.

## Details

**Description:**

> "10.5.2 Boundaries and Relationships
> The ImagingStudy resource is used to store details of an entire DICOM
> Study and its relationship to other resources, such as Patient.
> ServiceRequest and Encounter.
> The ImagingSelection resource is used to reference a subset of a study.
> Instances of this resource are created for specific clinical purposes
> and are unlikely to change once created."
>
> Is it on purpose that the first sentence of the paragraph does not say
> 'DICOM study' or 'ImagingStudy' but says 'study'? It seems vague, and
> I'd expect it to be clear, but it may be on purpose.

**Resolution (proposed text from triage):**

> Proposed text:
> 10.4.2 Boundaries and Relationships
> The ImagingStudy resource is used to store details of an entire DICOM
> Study and its relationships to other resources, such as Patient,
> ServiceRequest, and Encounter.
>
> The ImagingSelection resource is used to reference to a subset of a
> **DICOM** study. Instances of this resource are created for specific
> clinical purposes and are unlikely to change once created.
>
> Apply to both ImagingStudy and ImagingSelection as this text is
> mirrored.

**Comments:** None.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the resolution text exactly as drafted in triage. Concretely, on
both the `ImagingStudy` and `ImagingSelection` resource pages, edit the
"Boundaries and Relationships" narrative paragraph so that:

1. The second sentence reads: "The ImagingSelection resource is used to
   reference to a subset of a **DICOM** study."
2. The first sentence's punctuation is normalized: "…and its
   **relationships** to other resources, such as Patient,
   ServiceRequest, and Encounter." (replace the period after "Patient"
   with a comma, pluralize "relationship", and add the Oxford comma).

Because the paragraph is mirrored on both pages, apply the identical
edit in both places to keep them in sync.

#### Justification

This is a narrative-only clarification with no schema, cardinality,
binding, or invariant impact. The phrase "DICOM study" is already used
elsewhere in the same paragraph to disambiguate the informational object
from the FHIR resource, so inserting it in the second sentence
re-establishes that pattern and removes the reporter's stated ambiguity.
The companion punctuation/grammar fixes in the first sentence are
trivial editorial improvements that the triage already bundled in.
Resolving as Persuasive with the supplied text is consistent with how
Imaging Integration handles documentation-only clarifications and
carries effectively zero implementer impact.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the underlying clarification but rewrite the second sentence to
reference the FHIR resource directly rather than the DICOM informational
object, e.g.:

> "The ImagingSelection resource is used to reference a subset of the
> DICOM Study represented by an `ImagingStudy` resource."

Or, if the working group prefers to stay closer to DICOM language:

> "The ImagingSelection resource is used to reference a subset of a
> DICOM Study (typically the same Study described by an `ImagingStudy`
> resource)."

Apply the same wording to both `ImagingStudy` and `ImagingSelection`
"Boundaries and Relationships" paragraphs.

#### Justification

The reporter's actual question is whether "study" means the DICOM
informational object or an `ImagingStudy` resource instance — and in
practice it is *both* (an `ImagingSelection` references DICOM SOP
Instances that belong to a DICOM Study, and that DICOM Study is
typically also represented by an `ImagingStudy` resource). Disposition A
resolves the ambiguity in one direction only (DICOM-side) and leaves
implementers to infer the FHIR-resource linkage. Naming both sides makes
the relationship between `ImagingSelection`, the DICOM Study, and
`ImagingStudy` explicit, which is precisely what a "Boundaries and
Relationships" section is for. The trade-off is a slightly longer
sentence and a wider-than-strictly-asked edit.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket as *Not Persuasive* on the basis that the surrounding
paragraph already establishes that an `ImagingStudy` represents a DICOM
Study, so a competent reader can infer that "a subset of a study" in the
following sentence refers to the same DICOM Study, and that no spec
change is warranted.

#### Justification

This would be defensible only on a strict "no editorial changes without
demonstrable harm" reading, since the ambiguity is local and does not
affect conformance, validation, or interoperability. However, it is hard
to justify against the fact that the triage has already produced a
concrete, low-risk text fix and labeled the ticket Persuasive, so
declining at this point would require the working group to actively
overturn that triage decision.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is a pure narrative clarification, the triage has already
produced agreed text, and the proposed wording is directly responsive to
the reporter's question. Adopting it as-is is the lowest-risk,
lowest-cost path and preserves the mirrored-text invariant between the
`ImagingStudy` and `ImagingSelection` "Boundaries and Relationships"
sections. Disposition B is more thorough and would arguably produce a
slightly clearer paragraph, but it expands the edit beyond what the
reporter asked for and risks re-litigating wording that the WG has
already converged on; it is a reasonable fallback if a reviewer feels
the FHIR-resource side of the relationship should also be named
explicitly. Disposition C is not recommended because triage has already
agreed the change is warranted and the cost of making it is negligible.
