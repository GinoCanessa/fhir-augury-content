## Summary

The ticket asks the Imaging Integration work group to clarify, in the
DICOM SR to FHIR Resource Mapping IG, how DICOM SR content that lands in
`BodyStructure` should be represented when two distinct elements feed
into that resource. Looking at the mapping diagram, the submitter cannot
tell whether implementers should produce **two** `BodyStructure`
instances — one carrying the mapping for the *Tracking Identifier* and a
second carrying the *Finding Site* value — or **one** `BodyStructure`
instance whose fields are populated from both DICOM sources at once. A
clarification in the IG (diagram and accompanying text) is requested so
that producers and consumers interpret the mapping consistently.

No related Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were surfaced
through cross-references for this ticket.

## Details

**Description:**

> Looking into the diagram, it is not entirely clear how to map the two
> elements that end up in BodyStructure resources. Should implementers
> create two instances, one holding the map to the Tracking Identifier,
> and one instance holding the Finding Site value? Or is it only one
> instance, having to populate the fields from different sources?
> Please clarify.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Clarify, in the *DICOM SR to FHIR Resource Mapping IG* page covering the
`BodyStructure` mapping, that **a single `BodyStructure` instance** is
produced per anatomic site, with its fields populated from multiple
DICOM SR sources. Concretely, document that:

- `BodyStructure.identifier` carries the DICOM **Tracking Identifier**
  (and Tracking UID, where available) for that anatomic site.
- `BodyStructure.includedStructure` (or `location`/`locationQualifier`,
  as the IG already maps) carries the DICOM **Finding Site** code(s).
- The mapping diagram is updated so that both arrows (Tracking
  Identifier → identifier; Finding Site → location/structure) terminate
  on the **same** `BodyStructure` resource box, with an explicit note
  that one `BodyStructure` represents one tracked finding site and that
  multiple findings are represented as multiple `BodyStructure`
  instances (one per Tracking Identifier).

Add a short narrative paragraph next to the diagram and, if the IG
ships examples, include one example `BodyStructure` whose `identifier`
is populated from a Tracking Identifier and whose site coding is
populated from a Finding Site, so the intent is unambiguous to
implementers.

#### Justification

The submitter's confusion is structural: the diagram shows two arrows
into `BodyStructure` without saying whether they bind to one or two
instances, and the resolution recorded on the ticket is **Persuasive**,
which signals that the work group already agreed clarification is
warranted. Treating Tracking Identifier and Finding Site as *facets of
the same finding* (one tracked anatomic site) matches the DICOM
semantics — Tracking Identifier/UID exist precisely to make a finding
site re-identifiable across reports, and Finding Site is what is being
tracked — so collapsing them onto one `BodyStructure` per tracked
finding is the most faithful mapping. A worked example is the lowest-
risk way to lock in that interpretation without changing any
conformance.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Resolve the ambiguity with **explicit conformance language plus a
profile-style table** rather than (or in addition to) a diagram tweak.
On the BodyStructure mapping page:

1. Add a "Cardinality and identity" subsection stating: "Each
   `BodyStructure` instance SHALL correspond to exactly one DICOM
   Tracking Identifier (+ Tracking UID, if present); the Finding Site
   coded value(s) for that tracked finding populate the structure /
   location elements on the **same** instance."
2. Replace the relevant portion of the diagram with a two-column
   *source → target element* table (`Tracking Identifier →
   BodyStructure.identifier`, `Tracking UID → BodyStructure.identifier`,
   `Finding Site → BodyStructure.includedStructure.structure`, etc.),
   so the per-element mapping is unambiguous independent of how the
   diagram is read.
3. Note the boundary cases: when a Tracking Identifier is absent,
   describe the fallback (e.g., generate `identifier` from the
   measurement group context, or omit and rely on `BodyStructure.id`),
   and when multiple Finding Site codes apply to one tracked finding,
   show that they go onto a single `BodyStructure` as repeated
   structure entries.

#### Justification

A diagram clarification (Disposition A) addresses the immediate
question, but the underlying complaint — "I can't tell what the diagram
means" — recurs whenever a reader's eye traces arrows differently. A
normative-style table plus a one-sentence cardinality rule is much
harder to misread, gives implementers something to test against, and
covers the boundary cases (missing Tracking Identifier, multiple
Finding Site codes) that Disposition A leaves implicit. The cost is
slightly more authoring work and an additional review of the
conformance wording, but the IG remains non-breaking — no existing
mapping is changed, only made explicit.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request on the grounds that the existing diagram and
narrative are sufficient: a `BodyStructure` resource is, by definition
in core FHIR, an identified anatomic structure, and DICOM's Tracking
Identifier is by definition the identifier of a tracked finding site,
so an experienced implementer reading both standards would naturally
land on "one `BodyStructure` per tracked finding". No IG change is
made; the ticket is closed as **Not Persuasive**.

#### Justification

The argument for declining is that the question is essentially an
implementation-experience gap rather than a defect in the IG, and that
piling clarifying prose onto every diagram makes the IG harder to read
overall. However, this argument is weak in this specific case: the
ticket has already been adjudicated as **Persuasive** by the work
group, and the submitter is from a major imaging vendor (Philips) who
explicitly could not interpret the diagram — exactly the audience the
IG is written for. Listed for completeness so the work group can
explicitly reject it.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B subsumes Disposition A — it makes the same semantic
commitment ("one `BodyStructure` per tracked finding, populated from
both Tracking Identifier and Finding Site") but encodes it in a form
that is much harder for the next reader to misinterpret: an explicit
cardinality sentence, a per-element source→target table, and worked
treatment of the missing-Tracking-Identifier and multiple-Finding-Site
edge cases. Given that the ticket is already resolved Persuasive, the
work group is committed to *some* clarification; spending the small
additional authoring effort to land a table-plus-rule clarification
(rather than a diagram-only tweak) closes this class of question
permanently for the BodyStructure mapping and provides a template the
group can reuse for other multi-source-to-one-resource mappings in the
same IG. Disposition C is listed only for completeness and is not
recommended.
