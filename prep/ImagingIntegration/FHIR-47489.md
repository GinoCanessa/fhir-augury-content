## Summary

The reporter proposes a small editorial improvement to the **DICOM SR to FHIR
Resource Mapping IG**: in the IG's per-resource subsections, append the word
*"resource"* after the FHIR resource name (e.g., rename a section titled
"Observation" to "Observation resource", "ImagingStudy" to "ImagingStudy
resource", etc.). The IG sits at the boundary between DICOM SR concepts and
FHIR concepts, and several DICOM information objects share or echo names used
by FHIR resources. Without the qualifier, a reader skimming a heading like
"Patient" or "Observation" can reasonably be unsure whether the subsection is
describing a DICOM SR construct or a FHIR resource. Adding the
*"resource"* suffix disambiguates the heading at a glance and signals that the
content below it is FHIR-side material.

The ticket is one of a cluster of editorial review items the reporter filed
against the IG in September 2024 under the `II_SEP_2024_SR_Example` label —
several of which (e.g.,
[FHIR-47487](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-47487),
[FHIR-47510](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-47510),
[FHIR-47223](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-47223),
[FHIR-47655](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-47655)) target the same Examples
section and naming/structure conventions. There are no cross-referenced Jira
tickets, Zulip threads, GitHub PRs, or commits attached to FHIR-47489
directly; it should be treated as a self-contained editorial change against
the IG narrative.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the requested editorial change verbatim. In the DICOM SR to FHIR
Resource Mapping IG, walk every subsection in the Examples section whose
heading is a bare FHIR resource name (e.g., `Patient`, `Observation`,
`ImagingStudy`, `ImagingSelection`, `BodyStructure`, `DiagnosticReport`,
…) and rename the heading to `<ResourceName> resource` (e.g., `Patient
resource`, `Observation resource`). Update the IG table of contents and
any in-page anchors/cross-links to match. No model, profile, or terminology
content changes — text-only edits in the IG narrative pages.

#### Justification

The change is purely editorial and directly addresses a real source of
reader confusion: several FHIR resource names (Patient, Observation,
DiagnosticReport, ImagingStudy) overlap conceptually with DICOM SR
constructs that the IG necessarily also discusses on the same page. The
*"resource"* suffix is a one-word disambiguator that costs nothing in
ambiguity at the heading level. It is also consistent with how other FHIR
IGs that bridge another standard (e.g., V2-to-FHIR, CDA-on-FHIR) often
qualify FHIR-side artifacts in narrative text. Since this is a Medium
priority editorial ticket from a SEP 2024 review batch, the cost of
acceptance is small and the benefit (clarity for first-time readers) is
immediate.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Solve the disambiguation problem more thoroughly by introducing a
consistent heading convention across the entire Examples section instead of
only adding the word *"resource"*. For example, adopt headings of the form
`FHIR <ResourceName> (mapped from <DICOM SR module/IE>)` — e.g., `FHIR
Observation (mapped from SR Measurement)`, `FHIR ImagingStudy (mapped from
DICOM Study IE)`. As part of the same edit, coordinate with the resolution
of [FHIR-47487](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-47487) (column meanings)
and [FHIR-47655](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-47655) (link section 4 to
profile Mappings) so that the new headings double as the link targets used
by the per-profile mappings, and update the TOC, anchors, and any
inbound links accordingly. This is still narrative-only, but it is one
coordinated editorial pass rather than three separate text-tweak tickets.

#### Justification

FHIR-47489 is part of a tight cluster of editorial tickets
(FHIR-47487, FHIR-47223, FHIR-47510, FHIR-47655) that all target the same
Examples section. Fixing each in isolation risks heading churn — the
section may be renamed to "Observation resource" for FHIR-47489, then
renamed again as part of FHIR-47510's "align with the rest of the examples"
proposal, then again when FHIR-47655 introduces stable link anchors for the
profile mappings. A single coordinated editorial pass is cheaper to
publish, easier to review, and avoids breaking inbound links twice. It also
gives the WG an opportunity to make the heading carry the DICOM-side
provenance, which is more informative than just the word *"resource"*.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the rename. Keep the per-resource subsection headings as bare FHIR
resource names (`Patient`, `Observation`, etc.). If reader-disambiguation
between FHIR and DICOM is a concern, address it once at the top of the
Examples section with a short note ("Subsections in this section are named
after FHIR resources unless explicitly prefixed with 'DICOM SR'"), rather
than expanding every heading.

#### Justification

The IG is, by name, a *FHIR* IG — readers arriving at section headings
inside it can reasonably assume the headings refer to FHIR resources unless
otherwise marked. Bare resource-name headings are also the convention used
in the FHIR core spec and in most other FHIR IGs (Patient, Observation,
etc., are headings on their own resource pages). Suffixing every heading
with *"resource"* introduces stylistic inconsistency with the wider FHIR
publication corpus, lengthens TOC entries, and breaks any external deep
links to existing anchors. A single explanatory note at the top of the
section achieves the same disambiguation at lower cost.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The underlying complaint in FHIR-47489 is real — heading-level
disambiguation between FHIR resources and DICOM SR constructs is genuinely
useful in this IG, where the two vocabularies sit side by side. So
Disposition C (decline) understates the problem. But Disposition A (accept
verbatim) under-solves it: it changes the headings once now, and is very
likely to force another heading rename when the workgroup acts on
FHIR-47487, FHIR-47510, and FHIR-47655 (all from the same review batch,
all touching the same Examples section). Doing one coordinated editorial
pass — renaming headings to clearly mark them as FHIR-side, fixing the
column-meaning issue, aligning section 4 with the profile mappings, and
fixing the anchors/inbound links once — is the higher-leverage move. It
also lets the WG choose a heading convention (e.g., `FHIR Observation
(mapped from SR Measurement)`) that carries DICOM-side provenance and
therefore communicates more than the bare word *"resource"* ever could. If
the WG prefers a smaller change, falling back to Disposition A is still a
net improvement over the status quo and is fully compatible with a later
B-style consolidation.
