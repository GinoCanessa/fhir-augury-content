## Summary

The reporter observes that within the DICOM SR to FHIR Resource Mapping IG,
two depictions of the Imaging Measurements Container (IMC) are inconsistent:
the diagram in the page she is referencing shows **Evaluations** and **Derived
Measurements** as children of the IMC, while the textual/structural model in
section **3.1.4.2** treats those nodes as siblings (i.e., not contained inside
the IMC). The request is to reconcile the diagrams so the structural
representation of the IMC is identical wherever it appears in the IG.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat section **3.1.4.2** as the normative definition of the Imaging
Measurements Container's children and update the misaligned diagram(s) on the
overview/landing page of the DICOM SR to FHIR Resource Mapping IG so that
**Evaluations** and **Derived Measurements** are rendered as **siblings** of
the IMC (i.e., peer nodes under the parent SR content tree), not as children
of the IMC. Concretely:

- Locate every diagram in the IG that depicts the IMC sub-tree (overview
  page, any page that re-uses the SVG/PNG, and any inline mermaid/plantUML
  source).
- Remove the edges connecting IMC → Evaluations and IMC → Derived
  Measurements; re-attach those nodes to whatever parent section 3.1.4.2
  shows (typically the same parent that owns IMC).
- Regenerate any image assets and re-publish the IG so all occurrences are
  identical, and add a brief caption clarifying the parent/sibling
  relationship to prevent regression.

#### Justification

Section 3.1.4.2 is the textual specification of the IMC and, by IG
publishing convention, prose in a normative section is authoritative over an
illustrative diagram. Aligning diagrams with the prose preserves DCMR-level
intent (TID 1411 Imaging Measurements typically aggregates Measurement
Groups, while TID 1410 Planar/Volumetric ROI Measurements and other content
items contributing Evaluations / Derived Measurements live elsewhere in the
SR content tree). This is a low-risk, documentation-only change with no
impact on profiles, value sets, or conformance, and it directly resolves the
reporter's confusion.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Before editing diagrams, **verify which representation is correct against the
underlying DICOM SR templates** (DCMR TID 1411 Imaging Measurements and the
templates that contribute Evaluations and Derived Measurements). If the
diagram is in fact the accurate model, update **section 3.1.4.2** instead, so
it matches the diagram and the DCMR source of truth. Either way, add a single
shared figure asset (referenced by every page that needs the IMC picture) so
the two locations cannot diverge again, and add a short note in the IG that
identifies the DCMR template IDs that justify the chosen parent/child
relationship.

#### Justification

The reporter assumes the prose is correct, but the IG editors should confirm
against the DCMR before silently changing the picture — DCMR is the upstream
normative source and a wrong-but-self-consistent IG is worse than a
visibly-inconsistent one. Consolidating to a single diagram asset addresses
the root cause (two hand-maintained copies that drifted) rather than only the
symptom, and explicitly citing the TID(s) makes future review much easier.
The cost is modestly higher than Disposition A (one verification pass plus an
asset refactor) but the durability of the fix is significantly better.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket **Not Persuasive** on the grounds that the inconsistency is
cosmetic, the prose in 3.1.4.2 is normative, and a careful reader can already
determine the correct structure from the text. No diagram changes would be
made.

#### Justification

This is defensible only if WG capacity is constrained and the IG is otherwise
stable, since the cost to readers (and to interoperability between
implementers who follow the diagram vs. those who follow the prose) is real
and recurring. In practice this rationale is weak: documentation alignment is
one of the lowest-risk, highest-clarity-per-effort fixes available, and
declining a clearly-correct bug report against publication artifacts tends to
erode reporter goodwill.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B does what the reporter asks — the diagram(s) and prose end up
aligned — while also forcing a one-time check against the DCMR templates that
the IG is mapping (this is, after all, a *DICOM SR to FHIR* mapping IG, and
the SR content tree is the ground truth). Folding both depictions onto a
single shared diagram asset eliminates the class of bug rather than just this
instance of it, which is worth the small additional effort given how easily
hand-maintained duplicates drift in a published IG. If verification confirms
that section 3.1.4.2 is correct, the visible outcome is identical to
Disposition A; if it reveals that the diagram was right, the WG avoids
silently introducing a new defect by "fixing" the wrong artifact.
