## Summary

This Clinical Genomics ticket is the umbrella request to define the terminology
strategy for the new **Molecular Definition Implementation Guide for Molecular
Data Types** (the "MolDef IG"). With the MolecularDefinition resource being
relocated from FHIR Core into the MolDef IG (alongside the code systems and
profiles that constrain it), the work group needs to decide which elements
should carry terminology bindings, what binding strengths are appropriate, and
which CodeSystems / ValueSets the IG should publish or reference. The reporter
flags that several previously-filed tickets that targeted the *core* spec
(notably the cytoband and binding work captured in
[FHIR-50707](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50707),
[FHIR-50378](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50378), and the broader resource
move tracked by [FHIR-51227](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51227)) may be
closed as duplicates and re-pointed at this ticket so the disposition can be
made once, in IG scope. As of the latest comment (2026-01-30), Aly Khalifa
discussed the topic at the WGM, and the work group plans to vote on a concrete
proposal at a future meeting; nothing has been formally proposed yet on the
ticket itself.

## Related Zulip Discussions

### [genomics > MolecularDefinition cytoband use case(s)](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/genomics/topic/MolecularDefinition%20cytoband%20use%20case%28s%29)

A long, very substantive thread (50+ messages, 2025-05 through 2025-10) on how
to model and bind cytoband locations in MolecularDefinition. Kevin Power
drafted a candidate cytoband CodeSystem from UCSC Genome Browser and NCI
Metathesaurus sources; Bob Freimuth, Bob Dolin, Bret Heale, Jamie Jones, Joel
Schneider, Willie Chang, and Patrick Werner debated representation choices —
single pre-coordinated CodeableConcept vs. structured chr+arm+region+band+
subband fields, the validation difficulty of cross-axis (arm/region/band)
constraints in a single value set, the ISCN nomenclature, and whether to
support ranges via discrete `start/end` cytobands or a list of bands. There
was no firm consensus, but several positions converged: bindings must be at
most extensible/preferred (not required), the IG should publish draft CS/VS
that downstream IGs can refine, and Kevin Power's
`sirkyp/fhir-genomicsterminology` GitHub repo is the working location for
candidate artifacts. Aly Khalifa's later comment (2025-10-14) on
[FHIR-50706](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50706) made changes to the
cytoband interval element off the back of this discussion.

Participants: Kevin Power, Bob Freimuth, Bob Dolin, Bret Heale, Jamie Jones, Joel Schneider, Willie Chang, Patrick Werner, Aly Khalifa, Srikar Chamala.

### [genomics > Terminologies for genomics](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/genomics/topic/Terminologies%20for%20genomics)

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt this ticket as the umbrella for terminology design in the MolDef IG and
take the following concrete actions in the IG:

1. **Treat all terminology bindings on MolecularDefinition elements as
   *extensible* or *preferred* by default**, never *required*, while the
   genomics terminology layer is still maturing.
2. **Define the cytoband binding set as IG-published artifacts** — at minimum
   a `cytoband` CodeSystem (seeded from Kevin Power's UCSC + NCI
   Metathesaurus draft) and a corresponding ValueSet — and bind
   `MolecularDefinition.location.cytobandLocation.cytobandInterval.start` and
   `…end` to that ValueSet with strength `extensible`. Keep the existing
   string fall-back for senders that have only a pre-coordinated form.
3. **Identify the remaining MolecularDefinition CodeableConcept / code
   elements** (chromosome reference system, molecule type, coordinate system,
   strand, etc.) and either bind them to existing external CodeSystems
   (Sequence Ontology, NCBI taxonomy, etc.) or publish IG-level draft
   CodeSystems/ValueSets where no fit exists.
4. **Source the IG's terminology artifacts from the work delivered under
   PSS-2648** and the `sirkyp/fhir-genomicsterminology` repo, migrating them
   into the MolDef IG (or a paired Clinical Genomics terminology IG) once
   stable.
5. **Mark FHIR-50707, FHIR-50378, and similar core-spec terminology tickets
   as duplicates** and roll their accepted dispositions into this work, as
   Aly Khalifa already telegraphed in the 2025-10-28 comment.

#### Justification

This is the path the work group has effectively been on since the
MolecularDefinition resource began moving out of the core specification into
its own IG (FHIR-51227, PSS-2305). FHIR-50707 was explicitly closed as Not
Persuasive on the grounds that "we decided to move the code systems to the IG
together with the profiles" — that is a commitment this ticket is supposed to
honour. Extensible/preferred (rather than required) bindings reflect the
clear consensus in the *MolecularDefinition cytoband use case(s)* Zulip
thread that no candidate ontology yet covers cytobands precisely enough to
validate to a `required` strength, and Kevin Power's 2025-09-10 message
explicitly states "any bindings can't be required but would be extensible or
preferred." Publishing the artifacts at the IG level (with Grahame Grieve's
green light to ship CodeSystem resources from the IG, not THO) avoids both
the THO-membership bottleneck and the long-running cytoband-validation
debate, while still giving implementers something to point at.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Defer **per-element** binding decisions out of this ticket and instead use it
to adopt a *binding policy* for the MolDef IG, with the per-element work
captured as child tickets (one per MolecularDefinition element family that
takes a code/CodeableConcept):

1. **Policy**: any element that today has no clear external code system gets
   no binding (or a `string` datatype where appropriate); any element with a
   plausible but immature code system gets an *example* binding to a
   placeholder ValueSet maintained by the genomics-terminology project; any
   element with a stable, externally-maintained code system gets an
   *extensible* binding.
2. **Vehicle**: bindings live in **profiles published by the MolDef IG**
   rather than on the resource definition itself, so the core MolDef resource
   stays binding-light and downstream IGs can tighten or replace bindings
   without forking the resource.
3. **Cytoband specifically**: follow the FHIR-50378 motion (cytoband as
   `string`) for the unbound element, and add a profile-level extensible
   binding to a draft IG-published CodeSystem only when one is ready.
4. **Process**: spawn one sub-ticket per element family (cytoband,
   chromosome, coordinate system, strand, sequence type, …); this ticket
   becomes the tracker that closes when all children close.

#### Justification

This trades the "one big disposition" of A for a more incremental,
governance-friendly approach. It avoids forcing the work group to vote on
every element binding in a single motion and keeps the door open to
profile-level overrides — the natural home for IG-specific terminology in
FHIR. It also aligns with the *Terminologies for genomics* Zulip thread,
where the group converged on the genomics-terminology project being a
*supply chain* for artifacts rather than a single deliverable, and where
Bret Heale noted (2025-09-16) that "having example VS/CS is one strategy
when an IG is meant to be adopted by more specific downstream IGs." The
trade-off is that nothing changes immediately on the resource itself, which
may frustrate implementers who want bound elements out of the box.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline this ticket as out of scope for a Change Request and instead route
the terminology work entirely through PSS-2648 and the genomics-terminology
project, treating it as project work rather than a spec-change tracker.
Close FHIR-53265 with a pointer to the PSS, the Confluence Genomics
Terminology page, and the `sirkyp/fhir-genomicsterminology` repo, and
require future binding changes to be filed as discrete CRs against the
specific MolecularDefinition elements they touch.

#### Justification

Defensible on the grounds that "design the IG's terminology layer" is a
multi-quarter scope-of-work, not a single change to a single artifact, and a
Jira CR is a poor instrument for tracking it. The umbrella nature of the
ticket also makes it hard to write a normative disposition: there is no
single edit a balloter can vote on. Declining and re-routing forces
discipline (every binding decision becomes its own auditable CR) and
prevents this ticket from becoming a catch-all that hides specific
disagreements (cytoband encoding being the obvious example). The cost is
that the existing duplicate-routing strategy Aly Khalifa described
(2025-10-28) loses its target — closed CRs would have to be re-pointed at
the per-element children instead.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **B**

Disposition B captures what is actually achievable at this stage. The
MolecularDefinition resource has only just landed in IG scope, candidate
code systems are still being assembled in `sirkyp/fhir-genomicsterminology`
under PSS-2648, and the cytoband Zulip thread shows the work group is not
yet aligned on representation, let alone on bindings. Voting on Disposition
A as a single motion would either bake in choices the group has not yet
converged on (cytoband CodeSystem identity, binding strengths per element)
or be diluted to a vague "we will bind things later." Disposition C is too
strong: the ticket is doing useful work as a duplicate-target for older
core-spec tickets and as a visible commitment to terminology in the IG, and
killing it loses that. B preserves the umbrella role (so duplicates still
have somewhere to land), commits to a defensible binding *policy*
(extensible/preferred, profile-level, IG-published artifacts), and pushes
the per-element votes to focused child tickets where the cytoband, sequence
ontology, and chromosome decisions can each get their own well-scoped
discussion. The work group should still expect to take Disposition A's
substance in pieces — B is sequencing, not rejection.
