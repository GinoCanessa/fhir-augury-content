## Summary

The reporter (Aly Khalifa) asks the Clinical Genomics work group to update
the Genomics Reporting Implementation Guide (GRIG) — for the R6 cycle —
so it leverages the new Molecular Definition (MolDef) profiles instead of
expressing molecular details inside patient-specific Observation
components. The MolDef framework already carries molecular-level detail
in non-patient resources/profiles, and the ticket calls out three
specific GRIG profiles that overlap with MolDef equivalents and need to
be aligned: GRIG `Variant` against MolDef `Variation`, plus the
corresponding `Genotype` and `Haplotype` profiles on both IGs.

The ticket is intentionally a placeholder to anchor the WG-level
agreement reached in recent CG calls and at the WGM; concrete
profile-by-profile change proposals are expected to follow in separate
JIRAs. There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets and no related GitHub
items in Augury; all cross-references point at the Zulip thread
`[genomics] > Molecular Definition Profiles On Genomics Reporting IG`,
where the same scope and a possible R4→R6 StructureMap bridging strategy
are being discussed.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Accept the request as a forward-only R6 GRIG initiative: in the new R6
GRIG IG (the separate repo Kevin Power notes the WG has already
requested), realign the `Variant`, `Genotype`, and `Haplotype` profiles
so molecular-level detail is carried by the MolDef
`MolecularDefinition` resource and the MolDef `Variation`, `Genotype`,
and `Haplotype` profiles instead of being inlined into
`Observation.component` slices. Specifically:

- For each affected GRIG profile, replace the component slices that
  capture molecular detail (e.g., `gene-studied`, `dna-chg`,
  `genomic-coord-system`, `genomic-source-class`,
  `representative-coding-hgvs`, allelic state, etc., on the Variant
  profile) with a `hasMember` (or `derivedFrom`) reference to the
  matching MolDef profile, retaining only the patient-/specimen-level
  context (subject, specimen, performer, effective date, status,
  interpretation) on the Observation.
- Add an explicit conformance section to the R6 GRIG describing this
  reuse pattern, with worked examples that mirror Aly Khalifa's
  2026-03-22 deck.
- Leave the R4 GRIG branch untouched (per WG agreement, no MolDef
  backport).

#### Justification

This matches what the ticket literally asks for and what the CG WG has
already agreed in principle in CG calls and at WGM. Pushing molecular
detail into MolDef removes duplication between GRIG and the MolDef IG,
keeps the molecular description non-patient-specific (which is the
MolDef design intent), and aligns GRIG with where the broader R6
ecosystem is heading. The WG has confirmed an R6-only scope and a
separate R6 repo, so there is no R4 disruption risk. The Zulip thread
shows the WG-active members support this direction; the only stated
reservations (Bret Heale's call for a `DiagnosticReport ± MolDef`
comparison) are framed as "nice to have", not blocking.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt MolDef in R6 GRIG **and** ship a `StructureMap` bridge from the
balloted R4 GRIG profiles to the R6 MolDef-based equivalents as a
first-class deliverable of the same ticket. Concretely:

- In the R6 GRIG IG, define the MolDef-based `Variant`, `Genotype`, and
  `Haplotype` profiles exactly as in Disposition A.
- Author and publish, alongside those profiles, a set of
  `StructureMap` resources (using the FHIR mapping language) that map
  R4 GRIG Variant Observation (and Genotype/Haplotype) instances onto
  the corresponding R6 MolDef-based artifacts — exemplars include the
  `sdoh-clinicalcare` and `cda2fhir` StructureMaps, and the
  ahdis `fhir-mapping-tutorial`.
- Optionally, before the vote, publish a short technical brief
  comparing `DiagnosticReport`-with-MolDef vs. the current
  component-based pattern on the metrics Bret Heale called out
  (operation count, payload size, search support) so the
  recommendation is grounded in measured data.

#### Justification

This addresses the underlying need (MolDef alignment) and the two
substantive concerns raised on the Zulip thread that Disposition A
leaves open: (1) how implementers carrying R4 data forward will
interoperate with R6 GRIG, and (2) whether the MolDef-based shape is
actually leaner than the existing components. A computable
`StructureMap` is exactly the bridge Bret Heale proposed, preserves
continuity with the CodeX work, and gives R4 implementers a documented
upgrade path without forcing the WG to backport MolDef to R4. The cost
is additional authoring work (the mapping resources and the
comparison brief), but the artifacts are well-scoped and have direct
precedent in other HL7 IGs.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline this ticket in its current form on the grounds that it is a
direction-of-travel placeholder rather than an actionable change
request, and ask the reporter to refile one ticket per affected GRIG
profile (`Variant`, `Genotype`, `Haplotype`, plus any DiagnosticReport
or panel-level changes) with concrete profile diffs, target MolDef
profile bindings, and slicing/cardinality decisions. Track the
WG-level agreement in the CG Confluence space and meeting minutes
instead of in JIRA.

#### Justification

This mirrors Kevin Power's comment on 2026-03-30 ("we will need more
concrete proposals for future JIRAs … the WG agrees that leveraging
MolDef from GRIG makes sense, but it will be the specific
implementations that might trigger feedback") and Bret Heale's
observation that the request is an "add", not a "must have". A single
multi-profile umbrella ticket is hard to ballot meaningfully — voters
cannot agree or disagree with individual profile decisions, and
disposition history is harder to audit. Declining and refiling produces
better-traceable change requests without losing the WG-level
direction, which is already documented in CG meeting minutes.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The MolDef-on-GRIG direction has WG consensus, is R6-scoped (so there
is no R4 breakage risk), and is unlikely to be relitigated; declining
the umbrella ticket (Disposition C) would only delay capturing that
agreement on the public ballot record and would lose Aly Khalifa's
existing deck as motivation. Disposition A captures the agreement but
leaves the most substantive open question on the Zulip thread —
R4-to-R6 migration — unanswered, which will likely come back as
reviewer comments during the R6 ballot. Disposition B layers a
concrete `StructureMap` deliverable and (optionally) a short
MolDef-vs-component comparison onto the same change, which directly
addresses Bret Heale's bridging concern, supports CodeX implementers
already moving toward MolDef, and gives the R6 GRIG editors a
defensible answer when ballot reviewers ask "what about R4?". The
incremental authoring cost is bounded and has well-known precedents in
other HL7 IGs. The WG can still split the per-profile editorial work
into follow-on JIRAs (Disposition C's good idea) under the umbrella
this ticket establishes.
