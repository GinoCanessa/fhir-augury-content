## Summary

The reporter (Celine Lefebvre) is responding to the existing STU NOTE in the
CDS Hooks "Update stale guidance" section, which solicits implementer
feedback on balancing performance against the patient‑safety risk of stale
guidance. She argues that for clinicians to trust a CDS Service it must not
return stale information, and asks the IG to add stronger language about
ensuring the currency of CDS Service responses — strengthening this section
with additional, more prescriptive specifications.

A near-duplicate ticket, [FHIR-54878](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54878)
("add conformance language"), also from Celine Lefebvre, asks the same
section to upgrade lower-case "recommended"/"should" wording to a
conformance‑level "SHOULD," and is being treated as duplicating this ticket
(per Isaac Vetter's 2026‑03‑05 comment, FHIR‑54878 is the duplicate; this
ticket is the canonical one). A follow‑on ticket
[FHIR-56017](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-56017) was created from a
comment on this issue and proposes deleting the related "CDS Services can
update their previously returned guidance by returning a new set of cards…"
sentence about same‑`id` discovery entries, on the basis that the described
mechanism is overly complicated and likely unimplemented. The proposed
disposition recorded on this ticket renames the section to "Cards and
Transience," establishes transience as the default expectation, gives
concrete discard triggers (chart close, workflow complete), and uppercases
"SHOULD" — and has been pre‑applied via
[HL7/cds-hooks#78](https://github.com/HL7/cds-hooks/pull/78). Both the
reporter (2026‑04‑15) and Isaac Vetter have signaled agreement with that
disposition.

## Details

**Description:**

> See STU NOTE "We are seeking implementer feedback on how best to balance
> the needs of performance for implementations with the critical patient
> safety issues raised by the potential for stale guidance."
>
> Comment: In order for clinicians to trust a CDS service, it must not
> return stale information – that is a huge safety risk. Perhaps the IG
> should add something about ensuring the currency of the CDS Service is a
> critical and ramp up this section with additional specifications.

**Proposed Resolution (recorded on the ticket):**

Rename the section "Update stale guidance" to **"Cards and Transience"** and
replace the current opening sentence:

> In the case that CDS Hooks cards are persisted, clients should take care
> to ensure that stale guidance does not negatively impact patient care.

with:

> CDS Clients SHOULD treat cards as transient to the hook invocation that
> triggered them, discarding cards when the triggering context is no longer
> active (e.g., the patient's chart is closed or the relevant workflow
> completes). If cards are persisted beyond the triggering context, CDS
> Clients SHOULD take care to ensure that stale guidance does not
> negatively impact patient care.

This (a) establishes transient-by-default expectations, (b) supplies concrete
"discard" triggers, and (c) upgrades lowercase "should" to conformance‑level
"SHOULD."

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the resolution already recorded on the ticket and pre‑applied in
[HL7/cds-hooks#78](https://github.com/HL7/cds-hooks/pull/78):

1. Rename the IG section currently titled **"Update stale guidance"** to
   **"Cards and Transience."**
2. Replace the section's opening sentence with:

   > CDS Clients **SHOULD** treat cards as transient to the hook invocation
   > that triggered them, discarding cards when the triggering context is
   > no longer active (e.g., the patient's chart is closed or the relevant
   > workflow completes). If cards are persisted beyond the triggering
   > context, CDS Clients **SHOULD** take care to ensure that stale
   > guidance does not negatively impact patient care.

3. Leave the surrounding paragraphs (including the same‑`id` "update
   previously returned guidance" mechanism) for FHIR‑56017 to address
   separately.

#### Justification

This is the disposition the reporter has already explicitly endorsed
("This disposition looks good to me. Many thanks!" — 2026‑04‑15) and that
Isaac Vetter (CDS WG co‑chair on the thread) has already implemented in PR
#78. It directly satisfies the request: it strengthens the section beyond
the conditional "in the case that cards are persisted" framing, sets a
clear default expectation (transient), provides concrete discard triggers
that align with normal EHR workflow boundaries (chart close, workflow
completion), and upgrades the conformance verb to RFC‑2119 SHOULD —
exactly what the duplicate FHIR‑54878 also asked for. Pre‑application
limits open risk: the workgroup can review the PR diff rather than
re‑negotiate the wording.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt the substance of Disposition A but **stage the patient‑safety
language at SHALL/MAY granularity**, splitting the requirement so that the
patient‑safety guard rail is normative while the discard‑on‑context‑close
heuristic remains a SHOULD. Concretely, replace the opening sentence with
something like:

> CDS Clients **SHALL NOT** allow cards to remain visible to the clinician
> after the clinical context that triggered them has been closed in a way
> that could mislead patient care. CDS Clients **SHOULD** treat cards as
> transient to the triggering hook invocation, discarding them when the
> triggering context is no longer active (e.g., the patient's chart is
> closed or the relevant workflow completes). If cards are persisted
> beyond the triggering context, CDS Clients **SHALL** mark them as
> potentially stale or re‑evaluate them before re‑display.

Pair this with a non‑normative best‑practice note (drawn from Isaac
Vetter's 2026‑02‑24 comment) that designing hooks so the triggering
clinical content re‑fires when modified largely prevents staleness in
practice.

#### Justification

The reporter framed staleness as "a huge safety risk," not merely a
quality issue. Disposition A keeps the entire paragraph at SHOULD, which
arguably under‑weights the safety framing the reporter (and the existing
STU NOTE) explicitly raised. Splitting the requirement preserves
implementer flexibility on *how* to discard or re‑evaluate while making
the *outcome* (no clinically misleading stale cards) testable for
conformance. The trade‑off is real: SHALL language is harder to ballot
than SHOULD, may surface implementer pushback (the very feedback the STU
NOTE was soliciting), and would invalidate the pre‑applied PR #78 — at
least one more cycle of review/re‑PR would be needed. Without the
`HL7/cds-hooks` briefing it is also not possible to assess whether SHALL
language here would conflict with other normative statements elsewhere in
the IG, which is itself an argument for caution.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the request **Persuasive** in spirit but decline a spec edit on the
ground that the existing language ("clients should take care to ensure
that stale guidance does not negatively impact patient care") already
captures the safety expectation, and that stronger normative language
about *when* to discard cards is the responsibility of the host
EHR/workflow context rather than CDS Hooks. Close as Not Persuasive /
Already Specified, optionally adding an editorial clarification only.

#### Justification

CDS Hooks is intentionally narrow: it specifies the request/response
contract between a CDS Service and a CDS Client, not the lifecycle of UI
elements inside the host EHR. Card display, persistence, and dismissal
are arguably out of scope and vary widely across hosts (e.g., the Athena
quote in the comment thread shows cards living "in order groups" and "in
A&P," each with different natural lifetimes). Imposing a SHOULD discard
rule across all hosts could conflict with valid host designs (e.g.,
asynchronous decision‑support hand‑offs) and risks specifying behavior
the WG cannot test. This rationale would, however, contradict the
pre‑applied PR #78 and the reporter's explicit agreement, so it would
need a strong implementer voice on the WG call to carry.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The path forward already has visible consensus: the reporter has
explicitly accepted the wording, a CDS WG co‑chair has pre‑applied it in
[HL7/cds-hooks#78](https://github.com/HL7/cds-hooks/pull/78), and the
companion ticket FHIR‑54878 (the SHOULD‑uppercasing request) is being
resolved by the same edit. Disposition A delivers exactly what the
reporter asked for — stronger, more explicit, conformance‑level guidance
about card currency — without overreaching into host‑side workflow
mechanics that are outside CDS Hooks' core contract. Disposition B is the
strongest fallback if the WG decides the safety framing warrants
normative SHALL language, but it would re‑open a wording negotiation the
reporter has already signed off on, and the absence of an `HL7/cds-hooks`
repo briefing means the WG would be making a SHALL change without a clear
view of cross‑section impact. Disposition C is honest about CDS Hooks'
scope but ignores both the reporter's safety framing and the existing
endorsement of the milder fix, so it is hard to defend in this context.
The only outstanding administrative task is confirming Celine Lefebvre's
in‑person flag is satisfied (her 2026‑04‑15 comment should suffice to
clear it).
