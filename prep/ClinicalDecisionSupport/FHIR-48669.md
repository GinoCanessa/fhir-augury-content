## Summary

The ticket asks the Clinical Decision Support work group to update the
CPGQuestionnaire profile (in the Clinical Guidelines / cqf-recommendations
IG) so that it consumes the `itemImage` and `itemResponseImage` extensions
as they now exist in **Structured Data Capture (SDC)**, rather than the
historical CPG-local copies originally defined on
`cpg-shareablequestionnaire`. Those two extensions were migrated from CPG
into SDC under [FHIR-31513](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-31513)
("Consider extensions for itemImage and itemResponseImage", resolved
**Persuasive with Modification**, Published), with the explicit
expectation that "CPG will evolve to have a dependency on SDC and will
reference the SDC-defined extensions." This ticket is the CDS-side
follow-through on that SDC commitment: replace the CPG profile's
references to its own image extensions with references to the canonical
SDC extensions, so authors and renderers have a single, interoperable
mechanism for image-bearing Questionnaire items and responses.

**Persuasive with Modification / Published** in 2021. The resolution was
to migrate the two image extensions out of CPG's
`cpg-shareablequestionnaire` and into SDC, reference them from the SDC
"advanced rendering" profile, allow them on `QuestionnaireResponse`

dependency on SDC and reference the SDC-defined extensions — which is
exactly what FHIR-48669 is requesting on the CPG side.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the Clinical Guidelines (cqf-recommendations) IG, update the
**CPGQuestionnaire** profile (`cpg-questionnaire`) so that, on
`Questionnaire.item.extension`, it references the SDC-published
extensions:

- `http://hl7.org/fhir/StructureDefinition/questionnaire-itemImage`
  (SDC `itemImage`)
- `http://hl7.org/fhir/StructureDefinition/questionnaire-itemResponseImage`
  (SDC `itemResponseImage`)

instead of the CPG-local equivalents originally defined on
`cpg-shareablequestionnaire`. Concretely:

1. Replace the CPG-local extension slices on `Questionnaire.item.extension`
   in CPGQuestionnaire with slices that pin the canonical URLs to the SDC
   extensions.
2. Add SDC as a dependency in the IG's `package.json` / `sushi-config`
   (whichever is current) at the version that ships the migrated
   extensions, if not already present.
3. Mark the legacy `cpg-shareablequestionnaire`-scoped image extensions
   as **deprecated** and add narrative pointing readers at the SDC
   canonicals; do not delete them in this ballot cycle to preserve
   backward compatibility for existing instances.
4. Update CPGQuestionnaire narrative and any worked examples to use the
   SDC extension URLs, and reiterate the SDC requirement that meaningful
   `item.text` accompany every image (and that renderers SHOULD expose
   that text).
5. Where CPGQuestionnaireResponse exists or is implied, allow the SDC
   `itemResponseImage` extension on `QuestionnaireResponse.item` in
   alignment with the SDC QR profile's "optional" treatment.

#### Justification

This is exactly what FHIR-31513's resolution anticipated ("CPG will
evolve to have a dependency on SDC and will reference the SDC-defined
extensions"). Pointing CPGQuestionnaire at the SDC canonicals removes a
duplicate, divergent definition from the ecosystem, lets implementers
re-use SDC tooling and renderers, and keeps CPG aligned with the WG
that now owns the extension. Deprecating rather than removing the CPG
copies preserves existing instances during the transition.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than only swapping the URLs in CPGQuestionnaire, **derive
CPGQuestionnaire from the SDC "advanced rendering" Questionnaire
profile** (the SDC profile that already references the migrated
`itemImage` / `itemResponseImage` extensions). CPGQuestionnaire would
then constrain *that* profile with CPG-specific obligations (knowledge
artifact metadata, library binding, expression usage, etc.) and inherit
image rendering — and any future SDC rendering improvements — for free.
Concurrently, retire the CPG-local image extensions on the next
non-substantive cycle (mark `Retired`, leave the canonical URLs
resolvable for historical content), and document a one-line migration
note for implementers.

#### Justification

A profile-on-profile relationship is more robust than just rewiring two
extension slices: it expresses the actual semantic relationship ("a CPG
Questionnaire is a profile of an SDC advanced-rendering Questionnaire")
and prevents CPG from silently drifting from SDC's rendering rules
(e.g., the "meaningful `item.text` SHALL accompany the image" obligation
from FHIR-31513's resolution). The trade-off vs. Disposition A is a
larger refactor of CPGQuestionnaire's differential and a hard dependency
on a specific SDC version, which may force coordinated publication
windows; A is the cheaper, more targeted change but leaves CPG carrying
a thinner contract over the SDC mechanics.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change for this cycle. Leave CPGQuestionnaire's image
support as-is on the CPG-local extensions and document that the CPG
extensions are *aliases* of the SDC ones, deferring the cutover until a
broader CPG → SDC alignment ticket is opened.

#### Justification

The only defensible reason to decline is scope/sequencing: CPG may want
to do a single coordinated alignment with SDC (covering not just images
but other rendering and behavior extensions that were similarly
migrated) rather than a one-off swap, and may not want to incur a
breaking-canonical change in mid-cycle. This is weak on its own —
FHIR-31513 was resolved in 2021 and the migration is already published
in SDC, so further delay mostly perpetuates the duplicate-definition
problem the original ticket flagged.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A is the most direct, lowest-risk way to honour the
commitment already made in FHIR-31513 and stop shipping a duplicate
definition of `itemImage` / `itemResponseImage`. It is a small,
self-contained edit to the CPGQuestionnaire profile plus an IG
dependency bump on SDC, with a clean deprecation path for the
CPG-local extensions. Disposition B is architecturally cleaner and is
worth pursuing as a follow-up once CDS confirms the cross-IG release
cadence with SDC, but bundling the profile-on-profile refactor into
this ticket risks delaying the simple fix that implementers are
already expecting. Disposition C should not be selected — the
underlying alignment was agreed three years ago and further inaction
keeps two competing canonical URLs in circulation.
