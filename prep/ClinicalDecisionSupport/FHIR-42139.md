## Summary

The reporter is asking the Clinical Decision Support Work Group to soften
the wording used to describe the purpose of the `appointment-book` CDS
hook in the CDS Hooks Library specification. The current sentence reads:
"This hook enables CDS Services to intervene in the decision of when
future appointments should be scheduled, where they should be scheduled,
what services should be booked, to identify actions that need to occur
prior to scheduled appointments, etc." The reporter objects to the word
"intervene", which they perceive as too aggressive, and proposes
alternatives such as "provide guidance", "guide", or "help". This is a
purely editorial change request scoped to the prose of one section of
the CDS Hooks Library; no behavioural change to the hook contract,
context, or prefetch is implied.

There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub
items recorded in FhirAugury for this ticket.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the `appointment-book` hook overview paragraph in the CDS Hooks
Library specification to replace the word "intervene" with softer,
guidance-oriented phrasing. Concretely, change:

> "This hook enables CDS Services to intervene in the decision of when
> future appointments should be scheduled…"

to:

> "This hook enables CDS Services to provide guidance on when future
> appointments should be scheduled, where they should be scheduled,
> what services should be booked, and to identify actions that need to
> occur prior to scheduled appointments, etc."

The change is editorial only; it touches the description prose for the
`appointment-book` hook and does not modify the hook id, context,
prefetch, fhirAuthorization, or any normative behaviour.

#### Justification

The wording is informational and the reporter's concern is reasonable:
"intervene" implies the CDS Service overrides or interrupts the
clinician, whereas the CDS Hooks design pattern is explicitly advisory
— the EHR consumes Cards/Suggestions and the user remains in control.
"Provide guidance" is consistent with how CDS Hooks is described
elsewhere (Cards, Suggestions, decision support) and aligns with the
non-prescriptive tone used for other hooks in the same library
(e.g., `order-select`, `order-sign`). The change carries no
implementation cost and no risk to interoperability.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than only swapping a single word, take the opportunity to
rewrite the `appointment-book` overview sentence into the same
structural pattern used by the other hooks in the CDS Hooks Library —
a short "When" / "What the service can do" framing — and apply
consistent tone across all hook overviews. For `appointment-book`,
something like:

> "The `appointment-book` hook is invoked when the user is scheduling
> one or more future encounters/visits for the patient. CDS Services
> may use this hook to recommend appropriate timing, location, or
> service selection, to surface preparatory actions (e.g., labs,
> referrals, prior authorization) that should be completed before the
> appointment, or to flag scheduling conflicts."

At the same time, audit the prose of the remaining hooks for similarly
charged language ("intervene", "interrupt", "force", etc.) and
normalize on advisory verbs ("recommend", "suggest", "surface",
"provide guidance").

#### Justification

The complaint is a symptom of a broader inconsistency in the hook
overview prose — different hooks were authored at different times by
different contributors and do not share a uniform voice. Doing a
small, scoped editorial pass produces a more useful result than a
one-word patch and prevents the same complaint from recurring on
another hook. The trade-off versus Disposition A is that it expands
scope (more sections to review, slightly higher review burden for the
work group) but the change set is still purely editorial and
non-normative.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change on the grounds that the existing wording is
acceptable and that "intervene" — while strong — accurately reflects
the role of the CDS Service at the point a clinician is making a
scheduling decision. Mark the ticket "Not Persuasive" with a note
that the work group considered the request and prefers to keep the
current text.

#### Justification

"Intervene" is a defensible word in a clinical-decision-support
context: CDS literature commonly speaks of "decision support
interventions" and the hook does in fact present information at the
moment a decision is being made. Declining avoids re-litigating
editorial preferences and keeps the specification stable. This is the
weakest justification of the three because the cost of accepting the
change is essentially zero and the reporter's concern is reasonable;
however, it remains a defensible position if the work group wants to
hold a stricter line on editorial churn.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The request is small, well-reasoned, and entirely editorial. The
proposed replacement ("provide guidance" / "guide" / "help") aligns
better with the advisory framing CDS Hooks uses everywhere else and
removes a word that can read as overstating the CDS Service's
authority over the clinician's workflow. Disposition B is attractive
in principle but expands scope and is better filed as a follow-up
editorial task once the work group has agreed on a preferred voice.
Disposition C costs the work group little in stability and a small
amount of goodwill; given that the change is non-normative, accepting
it is the cleaner outcome.
