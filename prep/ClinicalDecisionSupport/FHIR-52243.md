## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a brief, one-paragraph description (roughly 1–3 sentences each) at
the top of every appendix from **Appendix D through Appendix M** of the
CQL specification, mirroring the style and length of the existing
descriptive blurbs at the top of the numbered chapters. Each blurb
should state (a) what the appendix contains and (b) when a reader would
turn to it. Leave the existing appendix bodies untouched. The expected
authoring location is the CQL spec source in the `HL7/cql` repository
(repo briefing not yet generated; the editor should confirm the exact
file paths against the current spec build).

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter asked for and what the spec is
already missing. The change is purely editorial — no normative content
moves, no schema or grammar changes — so there is no risk of breaking
implementations and no need for FMM review. It also restores
consistency with the numbered chapters, which the reporter explicitly
called out, and improves the discoverability of appendix material that
today is reachable only by guessing from the appendix titles. As a
"Triaged / Medium" Change Request with no comments and no Zulip pushback,
the workgroup has no signal that the request is contested.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than adding free-form descriptive paragraphs to each appendix,
extend the spec's table of contents (and any "Appendices" landing
section) so that each appendix entry is rendered with a one-line
**summary line** beside or beneath its title — analogous to the
short-description column in resource lists. Where an appendix already
opens with a clear sentence of orientation, reuse that sentence verbatim;
where it does not, add one. This keeps every appendix's body minimally
disturbed and concentrates the editorial change in a single, easy-to-
review TOC fragment.

#### Justification

The reporter's underlying need is navigational ("know what to expect or
where to look for things"), and a TOC-level summary serves that need
without touching ten separate appendix files. It is also easier to keep
in sync over time: a single TOC fragment is less likely to drift from
the appendix bodies than ten separately-maintained intro paragraphs, and
it gives readers the orientation *before* they click into the appendix
rather than after. The trade-off versus Disposition A is that readers
who land on an appendix directly (e.g., from a deep link) won't see the
summary in context — which is why this is offered as an alternative,
not as the default.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request as **out of scope for a normative ballot cycle**:
add a note on the ticket that the CDS work group acknowledges the
inconsistency but will defer the cleanup to the next dedicated editorial
pass on the CQL spec, and close the ticket as "Not Persuasive — Editorial
Backlog" (or the project's nearest equivalent resolution).

#### Justification

The change has no technical impact and no implementer is blocked by it;
the appendices are already fully readable. Declining is defensible
*only* if the work group is currently capacity-constrained and needs to
prioritize normative changes and breaking-change review over cosmetic
TOC improvements. This disposition is the weakest of the three on
merit, because the requested change is small, low-risk, and the reporter
has identified a real consistency gap; it is included here primarily so
the work group has an explicit "no" option to weigh against accepting.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

This is a low-risk, high-clarity editorial improvement that the reporter
described precisely and that costs the work group very little to deliver.
There is no technical, normative, or implementer-facing trade-off to
weigh, no Zulip controversy, and no cross-cutting impact on other
specifications. Disposition B is a reasonable refinement and the editor
should feel free to apply its TOC-summary idea on top of Disposition A
if it falls out cleanly during editing — the two are not mutually
exclusive — but the core deliverable should be exactly what the ticket
asks for: brief descriptions on Appendices D through M of the CQL spec,
matching the style of the existing chapter descriptions. Disposition C
is included for completeness but should only be selected if the work
group genuinely cannot fit the change into the current cycle, in which
case the ticket should be deferred rather than rejected on merit.
