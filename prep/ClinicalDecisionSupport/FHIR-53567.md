## Summary

Lloyd McKenzie is asking the Clinical Decision Support work group to
re-introduce explicit normative language in the CDS Hooks specification
constraining how many times `resolve()` may appear in a single prefetch
expression. Earlier draft text reportedly stated that "Prefetch resources
are only ever relevant if their relationship to other resources are
known. For this reason, `resolve()` SHALL only appear once in a given
expression." That language was removed somewhere along the way, leaving
no guardrail against authors writing prefetch templates that walk two or
more references in a single FHIRPath expression (e.g.
`%A.foo.resolve().bar.resolve()`). The reporter argues this is a safety
issue, not a capability issue: the rule does not prevent deep prefetch
graphs, it only forces each intermediate resource along a chain to be
captured by its own prefetch entry so that the client receiving the
response can actually reconstruct how the returned resources are
connected. He proposes restoring the previous "SHALL only appear once"
sentence to the spec. There are no formally cross-referenced Jira
tickets, but the immediate context comes from the recent CDS Hooks
"prefetch graph traversal features" work that folded a number of Da
Vinci CRD prefetch capabilities (including `resolve()`) into the base
spec.

## Details

**Description:**

> When doing prefetch, it's essential that all of the resources that are
> passed back connect to one another. If there are gaps in connection,
> then you don't know what some of the resources that are passed back
> actually mean. At one point, there was proposed language that stated
> "Prefetch resources are only ever relevant if their relationship to
> other resources are known. For this reason, 'resolve()' SHALL only
> appear once in a given expression." This didn't stop prefetches from
> chaining as deep as they needed to, it merely required that there had
> to be prefetch elements that captured every resource along the path.
> In dropping this wording, that guardrail is now gone.
>
> For example, if you have resources A, B and C that are connected via
> `A.foo -> B.bar -> C`, with the constraint, you'd have to have
> `prefetch1 = %A.foo.resolve()` and
> `prefetch2 = %prefetch1.bar.resolve()`.
>
> Without it, it becomes legal to have
> `prefetch1 = %A.foo.resolve().bar.resolve()`.
>
> That would leave the client with the base node A and prefetch1
> containing a collection of C, but no information about how they're
> connected together.
>
> I believe we need to put the proposed language back in the spec. It
> doesn't remove capability, but ensures safety.

## Related Zulip Discussions

### [cds hooks > prefetch graph traversal features](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/cds%20hooks/topic/prefetch%20graph%20traversal%20features/near/539192162)

Isaac Vetter announced (Sep 2025) that the long-running graph-traversal
branch had been merged into the CDS Hooks CI build, folding most of Da
Vinci CRD's "additional prefetch capabilities" (`today()`, date math,
`ofType()`, `resolve()`, the `|` collection operator) into the base
spec, while *deliberately not adopting* three CRD features: the `%`
prefix on prefetch tokens, a proposed `read()` function, and chained
prefetches (where one prefetch refers to the result of another via a
variable). This is the merge in which the "SHALL only appear once"
sentence ceased to be present in the build text. Lloyd McKenzie pushed
back, arguing that `%` is needed to keep the templates valid FHIRPath /
`x-fhir-query` and that prefetch chaining is "pretty critical" because
without it the sample prefetches become unwieldy. Isaac countered that
requiring `%context` would be a breaking change and that some open-source
FHIRPath libraries already accept the bare-token form. The thread also
includes a side question from Brian Kaney about whether graph traversal
will support `iif()` for `medication[x]` choice handling. No firm
consensus on `resolve()` semantics was reached in the thread; this Jira
ticket is best read as the formal follow-up asking the WG to put the
single-`resolve()` safety constraint back in.

Participants: Isaac Vetter, Lloyd McKenzie, Brian Kaney.

### [Da Vinci CRD > ✔ Use of resolve() in prefetch](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/Da%20Vinci%20CRD/topic/%E2%9C%94%20Use%20of%20resolve%28%29%20in%20prefetch/near/576814515)

A multi-week thread (Feb–Mar 2026) where Marie Casimina and Aditya Dave
worked through the semantics of expressions like
`Device?_id={{context.draftOrders.entry.resource.ofType(DeviceRequest).code.resolve().id}}`
in Da Vinci CRD's standard prefetch IG. Their confusion was exactly the
shape of risk this ticket is trying to head off: when `resolve()` walks
through a reference, it is no longer obvious whether it dereferences
against the hook payload, against the EHR's in-memory data, or against
the EHR's FHIR API, and what the client is supposed to do when the
referenced resource is not present. Lloyd McKenzie clarified that
prefetch is always evaluated by the EHR (never by the payer), that
`resolve()` is in principle executed against the EHR's FHIR API but in
practice can be short-cut from memory, and that the EHR can satisfy a
prefetch by parsing the references itself or by handing the context to a
FHIRPath engine wired up to its repository. The topic was marked
resolved once Aditya understood that resolution happens client-side, but
the thread illustrates how easily multi-`resolve()` expressions become
opaque to consumers — which is the core motivation for FHIR-53567's
proposed guardrail.

Participants: Marie Casimina, Aditya Dave, Lloyd McKenzie.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Re-introduce the previously-proposed sentence into the CDS Hooks
prefetch section as a normative `SHALL`. Suggested wording, taken
verbatim from the ticket and lightly anchored to the existing
"Simpler FHIRPath support for query string syntax" section:

> Prefetch resources are only ever relevant if their relationship to
> other resources is known. For this reason, `resolve()` **SHALL** only
> appear once in any single prefetch expression. Multi-step graph
> traversals **SHALL** be expressed as a chain of separate prefetch
> entries (e.g. `prefetch1 = %A.foo.resolve()` followed by
> `prefetch2 = %prefetch1.bar.resolve()`), so that every intermediate
> resource along the path is itself surfaced as a prefetch result.

Add a non-normative example contrasting the legal chained form with the
now-disallowed `%A.foo.resolve().bar.resolve()` form, and explicitly
note that the constraint preserves graph-traversal capability while
ensuring the client can reconstruct how returned resources connect.

#### Justification

This is the minimal, surgical change that closes the safety gap the
reporter identified. It is consistent with the rest of the prefetch
machinery, which already assumes each prefetch entry yields a
self-contained, identifiable set of resources keyed by name; allowing
`resolve().…​.resolve()` chains silently breaks that assumption because
the intermediate hop is never named or returned. It also aligns with
how Lloyd was already explaining the design in the Da Vinci CRD
"Use of resolve() in prefetch" thread — that prefetch authors are
expected to expose each resource hop. Because the constraint forbids
only a syntactic shape (a second `resolve()` in the same expression)
and not any underlying capability, it is a pure additive guardrail
rather than a functional restriction.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Instead of a hard `SHALL`, add a `SHOULD` plus a CDS-Client-side
processing rule:

1. Mark multi-`resolve()` expressions as **SHOULD NOT** be authored in
   prefetch templates, with a non-normative explanation of the
   information-loss problem.
2. Add a normative requirement on the **CDS Client**: when evaluating a
   prefetch expression that contains more than one `resolve()`, the
   client **SHALL** also surface each intermediate resource (the result
   of every interior `resolve()`) as part of the prefetch response,
   either by returning the intermediate resources alongside the named
   prefetch entry (e.g. as an implicit auxiliary entry) or by populating
   them as a Bundle, so that the receiving service can still reconstruct
   the relationship graph.

This keeps `%A.foo.resolve().bar.resolve()` syntactically legal but
forces the wire-level result to carry the connecting resources.

#### Justification

Some prefetch authors will find the chained-template form awkward, and
a strict `SHALL` precludes future tooling that could optimise the
single-expression form on the wire. Pushing the requirement to the
client makes the specification's intent ("the receiver can always
reconstruct the graph") explicit while leaving authoring style
flexible. The trade-off is real: it is a more invasive change to the
client conformance surface, and it complicates the prefetch response
shape, which today is a simple "map of name → resource(-or-Bundle)".
Implementers may push back because they would have to either define a
naming convention for the auto-surfaced intermediates or extend the
response envelope.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request and leave the spec as-is. Rationale: the relevant
"Simpler FHIRPath support for querystring syntax" section was the
subject of an extensive design pass during the graph-traversal merge
discussed in the cds-hooks Zulip thread, and the WG consciously
narrowed scope to what was already implemented in CRD. A new normative
`SHALL` constraining `resolve()` usage was not part of that scope and
would re-open a recently-closed design question. Authors who want the
chained behaviour can already structure their templates that way; the
WG can address authoring guidance via a non-normative note rather than
spec text.

#### Justification

The single defensible reason to decline is scope discipline around the
recently-merged graph-traversal feature: introducing a new `SHALL` on
top of freshly-balloted text adds churn and risks invalidating
implementations already under construction (notably in Da Vinci CRD,
where the IG's published prefetch templates would need to be audited
for compliance). Declining preserves stability. The cost is that the
information-loss problem the reporter is raising is real and is not
addressed by any other mechanism in the spec, so this disposition
trades safety for stability.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The change Lloyd is asking for is a small, well-scoped normative
sentence that costs the spec nothing in expressive power: every prefetch
graph that is currently expressible with chained `resolve()` calls is
still expressible after the change, just spread across one entry per
hop. The Da Vinci CRD Zulip thread shows real implementers getting
confused about exactly the kind of expression this rule would forbid,
which is empirical evidence that the guardrail is needed. Disposition B
is more flexible but introduces a much larger change to the client
conformance contract and to the prefetch response envelope, which is a
poor trade for a problem that a single sentence already solves.
Disposition C ignores a real safety issue in the name of scope hygiene
and would force the WG to revisit the same question the next time an
implementer writes a confusing `resolve().…​.resolve()` template. The
WG should accept Disposition A, with the non-normative example, and use
the same change to add a brief authoring-guidance bullet pointing
template authors at the chained form.
