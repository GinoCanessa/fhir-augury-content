## Summary

The ticket asks the Da Vinci DTR specification to clarify the bundling rules
for `QuestionnaireResponse` resources that are passed to payers or downstream
service providers. The current text in the "Passing DTR QuestionnaireResponses
to Downstream Processes" section requires the DTR Client to package the
`QuestionnaireResponse` in a `collection` Bundle conforming to the
`questionnaireResponseBundle` profile and to include any resources that are
referenced from `answer.valueReference` (when not already contained). The
reporter notes that the spec only *implies*, but does not explicitly state,
that resources referenced elsewhere in the `QuestionnaireResponse` (for
example via `subject`, `author`, `source`, or other reference-typed elements)
do **not** need to be included in the bundle, and proposes adding an
explicit sentence to that effect. The ticket has already been triaged on the
3/25/2026 BR call as "Persuasive with Modification" and is labelled
`Ready-For-Vote`. No related Jira tickets, Zulip discussions, or GitHub
items were found via cross-references or search.

## Details

**Description:**

> In the "Passing DTR QuestionnaireResponses to Downstream Processes"
> section is the following paragraph:
>
> > In those cases where a QuestionnaireResponse is to be used to convey
> > information either to the payer or to downstream service providers,
> > the DTR Client SHALL place the QuestionnaireResponse in a 'collection'
> > Bundle complying with the questionnaireResponseBundle profile.
> > §spec-156 This Bundle SHALL include no more than one
> > QuestionnaireResponse entry and it SHALL be the initial entry.
> > §spec-157 The bundle SHALL also include any resources that are
> > referenced by the QuestionnaireResponse as answerReference that are
> > not already contained within the QuestionnaireResponse. §spec-158
> > This ensures that all necessary information is delivered without a
> > need for subsequent queries.
>
> It is implied but not explicitly stated that additional referenced
> resources outside of `answer.valueReference` need not be included in the
> bundle. Consider inserting something similar to:
>
> > In those cases where a QuestionnaireResponse is to be used to convey
> > information either to the payer or to downstream service providers,
> > the DTR Client SHALL place the QuestionnaireResponse in a 'collection'
> > Bundle complying with the questionnaireResponseBundle profile.
> > §spec-156 This Bundle SHALL include no more than one
> > QuestionnaireResponse entry and it SHALL be the initial entry.
> > §spec-157 The bundle SHALL also include any resources that are
> > referenced by the QuestionnaireResponse as answerReference that are
> > not already contained within the QuestionnaireResponse. §spec-158
> > This ensures that all necessary information is delivered without a
> > need for subsequent queries. **No other resources referenced in the
> > QuestionnaireResponse need to be included in the bundle.**

**Resolution (per ticket metadata):** Persuasive with Modification.

> Agree but with some modification. Will add the statement:
>
> "Resources referenced elsewhere in the QuestionnaireResponse, such as
> subject or author, do not need to be included in the bundle."

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the "Passing DTR QuestionnaireResponses to Downstream Processes"
section of the US Da Vinci DTR IG by appending the reporter's exact
sentence to the existing paragraph (between §spec-158 and the end of the
paragraph):

> "No other resources referenced in the QuestionnaireResponse need to be
> included in the bundle."

No changes to the `questionnaireResponseBundle` profile, its slicing, or
its cardinalities are required — this is purely a narrative clarification
that the SHALL in §spec-157 is exhaustive with respect to `answerReference`
and that all other reference-typed elements (e.g., `subject`, `author`,
`source`, `questionnaire`) are out of scope for the mandatory inclusion
rule.

#### Justification

The clarification preserves the current normative behaviour exactly while
removing an interoperability ambiguity that implementers have flagged.
Because the existing rule uses SHALL only for `answerReference`, the
proposed sentence is consistent with the literal text and with the
profile's invariants — it adds no new constraints and does not invalidate
any existing implementation. Stating the negative explicitly is a common
FHIR documentation pattern when a SHALL list could otherwise be read as
non-exhaustive, and it directly addresses the question the reporter raised.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt the wording proposed in the ticket's Resolution Description (the
"Persuasive with Modification" wording already endorsed on the 3/25/2026
BR call) instead of the reporter's literal sentence:

> "Resources referenced elsewhere in the QuestionnaireResponse, such as
> subject or author, do not need to be included in the bundle."

This variant differs from Disposition A in two ways: (1) it gives concrete
examples (`subject`, `author`) of the kinds of references it covers, which
helps implementers map the rule to the actual `QuestionnaireResponse`
element names; and (2) it phrases the rule as a permission ("do not need
to") rather than a flat negative, which leaves the door open for senders
to *voluntarily* include such resources — a Bundle conforming to the
`questionnaireResponseBundle` profile is not prohibited from carrying
additional referenced resources today, and the Disposition A wording could
be misread as forbidding it.

#### Justification

This is the wording the work group itself proposed and approved on the BR
call, so it has the strongest signal of consensus. The example-driven
phrasing is more useful to implementers than a bare negative because it
ties the rule to the actual `QuestionnaireResponse` elements they will
encounter. The "do not need to" framing is also more accurate to the
profile's slicing — `questionnaireResponseBundle` constrains
`Bundle.entry` to require the `QuestionnaireResponse` and its
`answerReference` targets, but does not prohibit additional entries, so
the IG narrative should not imply otherwise.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request on the grounds that §spec-157 already uses SHALL only
in connection with `answerReference`, and standard FHIR reading
conventions treat such a SHALL list as exhaustive — anything not required
is therefore optional. Close the ticket as "Not Persuasive — already
addressed by existing normative text" and add a Q&A entry (rather than a
spec change) explaining the reading.

#### Justification

This is defensible only on the narrowest interpretation: the existing text
is technically unambiguous to a careful reader who applies FHIR's
SHALL/SHOULD conventions strictly. A Q&A or implementer-guide entry would
avoid touching the IG narrative at all and would not require a republish
cycle. However, this disposition is in tension with the ticket's already-
recorded "Persuasive with Modification" resolution and the BR-call
approval recorded by jeff_brown, so adopting it would require the work
group to revisit and reverse that decision.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The work group has already triaged this ticket as "Persuasive with
Modification", drafted the exact replacement wording in the Resolution
Description, and recorded BR-call approval on 3/25/2026 with the ticket
labelled `Ready-For-Vote`. Disposition B simply formalises that decision.
It is preferable to Disposition A because the work group's wording names
the concrete elements (`subject`, `author`) implementers actually deal
with and uses permissive ("do not need to") rather than prohibitive
phrasing, which better matches the `questionnaireResponseBundle` profile —
that profile constrains the *required* entries but does not forbid
additional ones, and the IG narrative should not accidentally narrow it.
Disposition C is hard to justify given the recorded BR-call consensus and
would leave the original ambiguity in the published IG. Recommending B
keeps the ballot path on the trajectory the work group has already chosen.
