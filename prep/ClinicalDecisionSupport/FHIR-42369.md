## Summary

Bas van den Heuvel is asking the CDS Hooks Workgroup to clarify (and ideally
simplify) the firing semantics of the `encounter-start` hook in the CDS Hooks
Library spec. He raises two concerns: (1) it is unclear whether the hook fires
for "sub-encounters" within an admission (e.g., a face-to-face meeting with a
clinician during an inpatient stay, or a transfer between departments), and
(2) the spec's distinction between inpatient (fire at admission) and
outpatient/community (fire at patient-check-in) adds confusion and should be
removed in favor of "fire at the start of any Encounter." Workgroup discussion
in the comments surfaces deep workflow ambiguity (check-in vs. arrival vs.
triage vs. roomed; bed-management systems sometimes outside the EHR; multiple
encounters per hospitalization; CQI/QM precedent of using "arrival of
location"). The current resolution is *Considered for Future Use* with a
deferral note explaining that the ticket landed on the base CDS Hooks spec
ballot but actually belongs to the (separately ballotted) hook library spec;
in the interim a "Looking for Feedback" callout was merged into
`docs/hooks/encounter-start.md` via cds-hooks/docs PR #648 to solicit
implementer input. The sister ticket [FHIR-42368](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-42368)
("appoint-book workflow requires improvement") raises a parallel
workflow-clarity issue against the appointment-book hook and is being handled
together procedurally.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Amend the `encounter-start` hook page in `cds-hooks/docs` (`docs/hooks/encounter-start.md`)
to (1) **remove** the inpatient/outpatient distinction in the *Workflow*
section and replace it with a single statement that the hook fires at the
start of *any* `Encounter`, regardless of class; and (2) **explicitly
state** that the hook fires for each new `Encounter` instance, including
"sub-encounters" within an admission (e.g., a department transfer or a new
provider-patient interaction modeled as its own `Encounter`). Retain the
existing `Encounter.status in {planned | arrived | triaged | in-progress}`
constraint and the "multiple 'starts' for the same encounter as each user
becomes engaged" sentence, since both already accommodate Bas's mental
model. Keep the "Looking for Feedback" callout in place until the next
ballot cycle so additional implementer input continues to flow in.

#### Justification

This matches Bas's two literal asks and aligns with how the FHIR `Encounter`
resource is actually used in practice: the resource itself does not encode an
inpatient/outpatient firing rule, so the hook spec layering one on top is
both redundant and inconsistent with FHIR design. The 2023-10-25 WG
discussion (Floyd, Bob, Juliet) effectively concluded that *arrival* /
"check-in-at-interaction" is the right semantic, and that is exactly what
"start of any Encounter, any time `Encounter.status` becomes
planned/arrived/triaged/in-progress" already encodes once the
inpatient/outpatient gloss is removed. The change is a Markdown-only edit on
a single hook page (mirroring the shape of PR #648), so the implementation
burden is minimal.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the firing rule deliberately *configurable per implementation* and
clarify the spec to say so, rather than forcing one definition. Concretely:
rewrite the *Workflow* section of `docs/hooks/encounter-start.md` to (a)
state that the hook SHOULD fire whenever a new `Encounter` instance enters
`planned | arrived | triaged | in-progress`, (b) acknowledge that what
constitutes "the start" varies by setting (admission, ED arrival, room-in,
check-in, telehealth join), and (c) require the CDS Service descriptor to
declare which trigger(s) it expects (via a new optional `triggerHints` field
in the hook documentation, not on the wire). Pair this with explicit
non-normative examples for inpatient admission, ED arrival, ambulatory
check-in, telehealth join, and intra-admission department transfer — each
modeled as a distinct `Encounter`. Defer the appointment-book companion
clarification ([FHIR-42368](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-42368)) into the
same change set so the workflow story is told end-to-end.

#### Justification

The PA co-chair conversation (Coop) and the QM/CQI experience that Floyd
described both demonstrate that real EHRs disagree on what "encounter start"
means, and that some of the relevant signal (bed-management transfers, nurse
vs. PT encounters) is not even in the EHR. A single normative rule will
either over-fire (annoying clinicians) or under-fire (missing CDS
opportunities) somewhere. Making the trigger explicitly configurable with
worked examples preserves Bas's underlying goal — predictable, encounter-any
firing — while honoring the workflow heterogeneity the WG already documented.
Folding in FHIR-42368 also avoids a second pass through the same workflow
debate when the appointment-book hook is touched.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Do not modify the firing semantics in this cycle. Retain the current
inpatient-vs-outpatient text as descriptive (not prescriptive) prose, leave
the "Looking for Feedback" callout merged in PR #648 in place, and re-resolve
the ticket as *Considered for Future Use* pending tangible implementer
responses to the callout. Re-evaluate after the CDS Hooks Library spec's
next ballot window, when the deferral noted in the resolution comment
expires.

#### Justification

The resolution note already records that the ticket was filed against the
base spec but actually targets the library, which is on a different ballot
clock; the substantive change Bas requests is not in scope for the in-flight
base ballot. The 2023-10-25 12-0-0 vote that originally chose CFFU was
explicitly grounded in unanswered workflow questions, and Lynn Laakso's
2024-03-27 reopen did not introduce new evidence — it added the callout to
gather it. Until that callout produces real-world implementer responses,
either Disposition A or B risks codifying a firing rule that the
implementation community will then ask to revise, which would itself be a
breaking change to a hook already at maturity 1.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition A is the cleanest reading of Bas's request and is implementable
as a small Markdown edit, but the workgroup record makes it clear that "any
Encounter, any start" is not a settled concept across inpatient, ED, QM, and
telehealth workflows — the same WG meeting that nominally agreed to defer
also surfaced four competing definitions of "start" (admission, arrival,
triaged, roomed) and a known gap (bed-management systems outside the EHR).
Disposition C is procedurally safe but indefinitely punts a Highest-priority
ticket that has been open since September 2023 and has already produced one
real artifact (PR #648). Disposition B captures Bas's core fix (drop the
binary inpatient/outpatient gloss; fire on any `Encounter`) while explicitly
acknowledging the workflow heterogeneity Floyd, Bob, Juliet, and Coop all
flagged, and it bundles the closely-coupled
[FHIR-42368](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-42368) work so the WG only
litigates encounter workflow once. It also remains a docs-only change in
`cds-hooks/docs/docs/hooks/encounter-start.md` (and `appointment-book.md`),
keeping implementation burden in line with PR #648.
