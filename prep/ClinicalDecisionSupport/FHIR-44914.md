## Summary

FHIR-44914 is a sparsely-described change request from Bryn Rhodes against
the Clinical Guidelines (FHIR) implementation guide (CPG-on-FHIR), asking
the Clinical Decision Support work group to "add support for commonly used
case feature patterns". In the CPG IG, a *case feature* is a logical
characteristic of the subject of care (e.g., "has active diabetes",
"most recent HbA1c > 9", "is currently pregnant") that knowledge artifacts
such as PlanDefinitions, Libraries, and ActivityDefinitions can reference
when expressing eligibility, applicability, dynamic values, or recommendation
triggers. As Bryn noted in a concurrent Zulip discussion, today the IG
already establishes the convention that *"case feature definitions are
structure definitions"* (i.e., profiles on the relevant clinical resource).
This ticket asks the IG to go a step further and ship a curated catalogue
of reusable patterns for the most common shapes those case feature
StructureDefinitions take — the standing equivalent of the "presence of
condition", "active medication", "recent observation above threshold",
"completed procedure within window", etc., so that authors of clinical
guidelines do not have to reinvent the modelling each time.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a new normative section to the CPG-on-FHIR IG titled "Case Feature
Patterns" that ships a small catalogue of reusable case-feature
StructureDefinitions covering the patterns clinical-guideline authors
hit most often. Initial catalogue:

1. **Condition Presence** — profile on `Condition` constraining
   `clinicalStatus` to `active|recurrence|relapse`,
   `verificationStatus` to confirmed-equivalent, and binding `code` via
   a parameterised value-set slot.
2. **Condition History** — profile on `Condition` allowing
   `clinicalStatus` of `inactive|remission|resolved` plus a temporal
   constraint on `onset[x]` / `abatement[x]`.
3. **Active Medication** — profile on `MedicationRequest` (with
   companion `MedicationStatement` profile) constraining `status =
   active`, `intent = order|plan`, and parameterising the medication
   code value-set.
4. **Recent Observation** — profile on `Observation` with `status in
   {final, amended, corrected}`, parameterised `code` value-set, and an
   `effective[x]` "within window" constraint.
5. **Observation Above/Below Threshold** — extends Recent Observation
   with `valueQuantity.value` comparator semantics expressed as an
   invariant.
6. **Procedure Completed Within Window** — profile on `Procedure` with
   `status = completed` and a windowed `performed[x]` constraint.
7. **Immunization History** — profile on `Immunization` with
   `status = completed` and parameterised `vaccineCode`.

Each pattern would ship with: (a) the StructureDefinition itself in
`input/resources` (or `input/profiles`) of `HL7/cqf-recommendations`,
(b) a worked example of a derived case feature definition, (c) a CQL
fragment showing how the pattern is consumed from a `Library`, and
(d) narrative documenting the parameterisation slots authors are
expected to fill.

#### Justification

This is the literal request and it is consistent with the IG's
established design principle, restated by Bryn in the Zulip Substance
thread: *case feature definitions are StructureDefinitions*. The IG
already documents the case-feature concept and uses it in worked
examples; what authors lack is a starting library of the common shapes,
which today they must hand-roll for every guideline. Shipping a small,
opinionated catalogue lowers the authoring barrier, drives
cross-guideline consistency (so two guidelines targeting "active
diabetes" produce structurally comparable case features), and improves
the analysability of CQL libraries that consume them. The proposal is
additive, does not change normative behaviour of existing artifacts,
and is well-scoped for an IG point release.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Instead of shipping concrete StructureDefinition profiles, publish a
**Case Feature Pattern Cookbook** as a narrative chapter in the CPG IG
that catalogues the same seven patterns above as *authoring recipes*
(narrative + CQL templates + StructureDefinition fragments) rather than
as fully-formed, conformance-bearing profiles. Pair it with a small
set of **abstract pattern profiles** (e.g., `case-feature-presence`,
`case-feature-history`, `case-feature-recent-observation`) marked
`abstract = true`, intended only as derivation parents — not as
profiles implementations would conform to directly. Concrete case
features in any given guideline would `derivation = constraint` from
the abstract parent, supplying the value-sets and windows.

#### Justification

The literal "ship a catalogue" approach (Disposition A) risks
producing profiles that are *almost* what every guideline needs but
never quite — different programmes have legitimately different value-set
choices, look-back windows, and status filters. Shipping concrete
profiles invites either (i) a proliferation of variant profiles in
downstream IGs that drift from the canonical ones, or (ii) implementer
pressure on CDS WG to extend the catalogue into a maintenance burden.
The abstract-parent approach captures the *pattern* (which is what the
ticket actually asks for) without committing the IG to any particular
parameterisation, and it gives downstream IGs a clean, shared
derivation point so that "two diabetes case features" remain
*structurally* compatible even when their value-sets differ. This is
also closer to how CQL itself reuses fragments via `define` rather than
via parameterised concrete artifacts.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the ticket as currently written and close as "not
persuasive — out of scope for the IG core". Direct interested parties
to publish case-feature pattern catalogues in companion implementation
guides scoped to specific clinical domains (e.g., the Opioid
Prescribing IG, eCQM measure packs, programme-specific CPG IGs), where
the value-set and window choices can be made authoritatively for the
relevant population.

#### Justification

The IG's job is to define *how* case features work, not to enumerate
*which* ones authors should use. The choice of which conditions count
as "active", which medications count as "current", and what window is
"recent" is inherently programme-specific and clinically contested,
and embedding a curated answer in the base IG creates a false
authoritative-by-default catalogue that downstream IGs will feel
compelled to either adopt or explain away. Existing mechanisms —
StructureDefinition derivation, CQL libraries, and the IG's existing
case-feature documentation — already give authors everything they need
to build their own catalogues; the missing piece is community-built
domain catalogues, not a normative one.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The ticket is genuinely useful — authors do reinvent the same handful
of case-feature shapes for every guideline, and there is real value in
the IG taking a position on what those shapes are. But shipping concrete
profiles (Disposition A) understates how much variation programmes
legitimately need in value-sets and time-windows, and would put CDS WG
on the hook for maintaining a catalogue of named profiles whose only
real differences from each other are slot-fillers. The
abstract-parent + cookbook approach in Disposition B captures the
pattern (which is what Bryn actually asked for) at the level the IG
should own, while leaving the parameterisation decisions to the
guideline authors who have to defend them clinically. Disposition C
overcorrects: declining leaves authors in the same situation that
prompted the ticket. Because the ticket is sparse and was filed by
one of the IG's editors, the work group should treat the
teleconference primarily as a chance to confirm scope (which patterns
are in v1, what naming convention, what derivation rules) rather than
debating whether the work is wanted.
