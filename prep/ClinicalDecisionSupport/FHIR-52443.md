## Summary

The ticket asks the Clinical Decision Support work group to add explicit
guidance to the Clinical Quality Language (CQL) FHIR specification on how
implementations should treat UCUM "arbitrary units" (units defined under
[UCUM §Arbitrary Units](https://ucum.org/ucum#section-Arbitrary-Units),
e.g., `[arb'U]`, `[IU]`, `[USP'U]`). UCUM does not, in general, allow
operations on arbitrary units because their definitions are
context-specific and not interconvertible. However, clinical logic in
CQL frequently has to deal with quantities expressed in arbitrary units,
so the reporter proposes that the spec carve out a narrow, well-defined
exception: comparisons should be supported when (1) the units of the two
operands are exactly the same on a case-sensitive basis, and (2) at
least one of the operands is a literal. Authoring tools should warn
authors that arbitrary units differ by context and that such
comparisons need to be reviewed for appropriateness. The cross-reference
and Zulip stores returned no related items, so this prep is built solely
from the ticket itself.

## Details

**Description:**

UCUM defines arbitrary units:

https://ucum.org/ucum#section-Arbitrary-Units

In general, operations on arbitrary units are not supported. However,
clinical logic may involve these units and so long as the context is
appropriate, should be supported.

Specifically, systems that support UCUM units SHOULD support comparison
of quantities involving arbitrary units so long as 1) The units are
exactly the same (case-sensitively), and 2) One or both operands to the
comparison are literal.

In this case, authoring systems SHOULD provide a warning to authors
that arbitrary units differ based on context and care should be taken
to ensure that the comparison is appropriate.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a normative subsection to the CQL (FHIR) specification — most
naturally under the existing "Quantities" / "Quantity Operations"
discussion of the Author's Guide, mirrored in the Reference for the
`Equal`, `Equivalent`, `Greater`, `Less`, `GreaterOrEqual`, and
`LessOrEqual` operators on `Quantity` — that explicitly addresses UCUM
arbitrary units. The new guidance, taken essentially verbatim from the
ticket, would say:

- Operations on arbitrary UCUM units (units in the UCUM §Arbitrary
  Units section, including `[arb'U]`, `[IU]`, `[USP'U]`, etc.) are in
  general unsupported.
- Implementations that support UCUM SHOULD support **comparison**
  (`=`, `!=`, `~`, `!~`, `<`, `<=`, `>`, `>=`) of two `Quantity` values
  whose units are arbitrary units, **iff**
  1. the unit strings are identical under a case-sensitive comparison,
     **and**
  2. at least one of the two operands is a literal `Quantity`.
- All other operations on arbitrary-unit quantities (arithmetic,
  conversion, aggregation across non-identical arbitrary units) remain
  unsupported and SHOULD produce an error or `null` per existing CQL
  unit-handling rules.
- Authoring tools SHOULD emit a warning whenever a comparison falls
  into this carve-out, noting that arbitrary units are
  context-specific and that the comparison must be validated against
  the data context (typically the originating `Observation` or
  `code`).

#### Justification

The rule is tightly scoped: it only covers comparison, only when units
match exactly, and only when at least one operand is a literal — which
is the realistic authoring pattern (`[Observation: code] O return
O.value > 4 '[IU]'`). Requiring case-sensitive match preserves UCUM's
own case sensitivity rules and avoids accidentally equating distinct
arbitrary units. Requiring a literal operand keeps the engine from
having to reason about whether two run-time arbitrary-unit quantities
"come from the same context" — that responsibility is shifted to the
author, who is reminded by the SHOULD-level warning. This matches how
authors already write CDS logic against `Observation` resources whose
units are `[IU]` or `[arb'U]`, and it avoids the current situation
where conformant engines may be forced to return `null` for plainly
sensible comparisons.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than adding a normative SHOULD to the FHIR-binding of CQL,
locate the guidance in a non-normative "Implementer Guidance" /
"Clinical Authoring Considerations" appendix of the CQL (FHIR)
specification, and frame it as a recommended *engine extension point*
rather than a SHOULD-level conformance rule. Concretely:

- Add an appendix entry "Handling UCUM Arbitrary Units" that describes
  the carve-out (identical case-sensitive unit + at least one literal
  operand) as a **recommended pattern** that implementations MAY
  enable, ideally behind a documented option or tool capability.
- Define an authoring-tool capability statement (informative) that
  implementations can advertise: "supports arbitrary-unit literal
  comparison".
- Keep the actual operator semantics in the CQL reference unchanged
  (operations on arbitrary units remain unsupported by default), and
  keep the authoring-warning recommendation as MAY rather than SHOULD.

#### Justification

UCUM itself is explicit that arbitrary units are not interconvertible
and not, in general, comparable, because their meaning depends on the
calibration context (assay, manufacturer, lot). Promoting comparison
to SHOULD — even a narrow form of it — risks codifying behavior that
some engines deliberately refuse on safety grounds, and may make
otherwise conformant engines non-conformant overnight. Treating the
carve-out as a documented optional capability gives engines and
authoring tools the same building blocks (so libraries can be portable
when both ends opt in) while preserving the safer default of "engine
returns `null` / error for arbitrary-unit operations." This also lets
the CDS WG defer the harder question of whether the carve-out should
also extend to `Equivalent`, terminology-anchored arbitrary units, and
quantities derived from `Observation.valueQuantity` whose `system`
implies UCUM but whose `code` is an arbitrary unit.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change on the basis that the underlying behaviour is
already governed by general CQL `Quantity` semantics plus UCUM's own
rules, and that adding a SHOULD-level carve-out for arbitrary units
introduces conformance risk and authoring ambiguity disproportionate
to the problem. Instead, the workgroup would document this disposition
publicly so that engine authors who wish to be permissive can cite the
WG's reasoning, but no normative spec change would be made.

#### Justification

The current CQL FHIR spec already permits implementations to perform
unit operations only when the engine can establish unit equivalence;
arbitrary units fail that test by definition, and engines that wish to
be permissive can already do so without the spec explicitly blessing
the behaviour (the spec does not forbid it). Adding a SHOULD elevates
optional behaviour to an expectation, which (a) creates a new
conformance gap for engines that intentionally refuse arbitrary-unit
comparisons on patient-safety grounds, and (b) requires every
authoring tool to implement the warning, even when the engine it
targets does not support the carve-out. The reporter's underlying
concern — clinical logic involving `[IU]`-style units — can be
addressed more cleanly at the data layer (constraining `Observation`
to a known `code` so the comparison is no longer "arbitrary-unit vs.
arbitrary-unit") than by changing CQL semantics.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The change is small, the scope is tightly defined, and it codifies a
pattern that authors already write today against `Observation`
resources whose `valueQuantity.code` is an arbitrary UCUM unit such
as `[IU]`. Disposition A is preferable to B because optional
"capabilities" tend to fragment the ecosystem: if half the engines
opt in and half do not, the same CQL library produces silently
different results on different platforms — exactly the situation CQL
is meant to prevent. It is preferable to C because declining leaves
the current grey area in place: engines diverge today on whether
`O.value > 4 '[IU]'` is an error, `null`, or `true`, and authors have
no spec text to rely on. The two safety guards in the proposal — the
case-sensitive same-unit constraint and the literal-operand
constraint — narrow the carve-out enough that engines that are
worried about the patient-safety implications of arbitrary-unit
comparisons retain almost all of their current refusal surface, while
the SHOULD-level authoring warning gives implementers a clear hook to
surface the context dependency to authors. The CDS WG should adopt
Disposition A and, when drafting the spec text, also confirm whether
the rule applies to `Equivalent` (`~`/`!~`) in addition to the
ordering and equality operators called out above.
