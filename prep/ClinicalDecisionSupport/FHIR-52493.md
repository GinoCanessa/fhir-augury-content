## Summary

Chris Moesel reports that the Clinical Quality Language (CQL) specification's
Change Log omits any entry covering the newly-added Appendix M - Messages.
Because Appendix M itself indicates that the messaging facility was introduced
as Trial-Use in CQL 2.0, the Change Log for CQL 2.0 should record this
addition. The request is purely editorial / housekeeping: add a Change Log
entry that documents the introduction of Appendix M - Messages as a
Trial-Use feature in the 2.0 release. No cross-referenced Jira tickets,
Zulip threads, or GitHub items were surfaced for this ticket, so the
context here is limited to the ticket text and the page it points at on the
CQL build site.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the CQL specification's Change Log section for the 2.0 release to
add an entry that records the introduction of Appendix M - Messages as a
new Trial-Use feature. The entry should:

- Identify the change as an addition (new appendix / new normative section
  at Trial-Use maturity).
- Reference Appendix M - Messages by name and link to
  `20-m-messages.html`.
- Note the Trial-Use status to match the maturity statement on the
  appendix itself.
- Be placed alongside the other 2.0 Change Log entries so readers tracking
  what is new in CQL 2.0 see Messages listed.

#### Justification

This is a straightforward documentation-consistency fix. The Change Log
exists precisely to enumerate what changed in each release; an entire new
appendix introduced at Trial-Use is exactly the kind of change that
belongs there. Implementers and reviewers rely on the Change Log to scope
their delta review between releases, and omitting a Trial-Use addition
from the log undermines that contract. The fix is low-risk, has no
normative impact, and aligns the Change Log with the maturity statement
already present in Appendix M itself.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than (or in addition to) a single Change Log line, perform a full
sweep of CQL 2.0 Change Log coverage as part of resolving this ticket:

- Audit Appendix M - Messages and any other appendices/sections that were
  added or substantively changed in 2.0 against the Change Log.
- Add Change Log entries for any other 2.0 additions that turn out to be
  similarly under-documented.
- Establish (or reaffirm) an editorial rule in the CQL publication
  workflow that any new appendix or maturity-level change must include a
  Change Log entry in the same PR.

#### Justification

If the Change Log is missing the Messages appendix, it is plausible that
other 2.0 changes were also missed; treating the report as a symptom rather
than the entire defect avoids a sequence of one-off "Change Log doesn't
include X" tickets. The trade-off versus Disposition A is scope: this
disposition is more work for whoever picks it up, and may surface
further editorial issues that need their own dispositions, but it
produces a cleaner Change Log for readers of CQL 2.0.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket as "Not Persuasive" / decline on the basis that the
Appendix M - Messages page itself already states the Trial-Use status and
that readers can discover the new feature by browsing the table of
contents, so an explicit Change Log entry is unnecessary.

#### Justification

This rationale is weak — the only defensible reason to decline a
documentation-only request is that the documentation already meets the
need. In practice, the Change Log is the canonical place implementers
look for "what's new in this release", and pointing readers at the
appendix itself does not satisfy that workflow. Declining would leave a
known gap for the (small) cost of a Change Log line and is therefore not
recommended.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The request is small, factually correct, and purely editorial. Adding a
Change Log entry for Appendix M - Messages costs essentially nothing,
removes a documented gap, and aligns the Change Log with the maturity
statement that already appears on Appendix M. Disposition B is a
reasonable upgrade if the editor has the bandwidth to sweep the rest of
the 2.0 additions, but it should not block resolving the specific defect
Chris Moesel raised. Disposition C has no real defense given that the
ticket points at a concrete, verifiable omission in the Change Log.
