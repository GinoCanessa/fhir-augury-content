## Summary

The ticket asks the Clinical Decision Support work group to clarify how
Clinical Quality Language (CQL) performs calendar-duration unit
conversions when doing date/time arithmetic, prompted by the cql-tests
case `DateTime(2016,5) - 31535999 seconds = DateTime(2015, 5)` whose
"true" outcome depends on which conversion path is taken. Bryn Rhodes
(reporter) proposes that CQL adopt the FHIRPath quantity-conversion
functions verbatim and explicitly state that calendar conversions are
applied **in granularity order** (seconds → minutes → hours → days →
months → years) rather than skipping granularities, since the two paths
produce different results (`12.16 months ≈ 1.01 years` vs `364.9999 days
/ 365 = 0.9999 years`). The request is closely related to
[FHIR-53250](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53250) (Implementable
Technology Specifications / FHIRPath), which addresses the conflict
between `1 year ~ 365.25 'd'` (UCUM) and `1 year ~ 365 days` (calendar)
in quantity *equivalence*; that ticket has resolved "Persuasive with
Modification" by restricting calendar conversion factors to explicit
`toQuantity` use only. FHIR-53148 must land a CQL-side clarification
that is consistent with that FHIRPath direction. It also references the
implementation-side specification-test issue
[cqframework/cql-tests#72](https://github.com/cqframework/cql-tests/issues/72)
that exposed the ambiguity.

## Details

**Description:**

> The following specification test:
>
> `DateTime(2016,5) - 31535999 seconds = DateTime(2015, 5)`
>
> is currently listed with expected outcome of true, but depending on
> how you apply conversion factors to get there, it might be true or
> it might be false. We should clarify the expectation is that
> conversion of calendar durations moves through granularities, i.e.
> it cannot skip granularities.
>
> In addition, we don't explicitly state calendar conversion factors.
> We should indicate how the conversions are performed. We do say this
> in the FHIRPath spec:
> https://hl7.org/fhirpath/#quantity-conversion-functions
>
> These conversion factors should be added to the CQL specification,
> and that is then how the conversion from seconds to months should be
> performed.
> https://cql.hl7.org/02-authorsguide.html#datetime-arithmetic
>
> And we should be explicit that the conversions are applied in order
> of granularity, so that we get:
>
> ```cql
> 31535999 seconds / 60 = 525599.9833 minutes
>                  / 60 = 8759.9997 hours
>                  / 24 = 364.9999 days
>                  / 30 = 12.16 months
>                  / 12 = 1.01 years
> ```
>
> Which is different than what you would get if you went straight to
> years from days:
>
> `364.9999 days / 365 = 0.9999 years`
>
> This is related to the following specification test issue:
> https://github.com/cqframework/cql-tests/issues/72

**Comments:**

- **bryn.rhodes (2025-11-29):** Re-reads the published Date/Time
  Arithmetic prose ("converting the time-based quantity to the most
  precise value specified in the first argument …") and argues that the
  *precision of the date/time value* should drive the target
  granularity. A `DateTime(2016,5)` (month-precision) result should use
  the `days → months` factor (30), while a `DateTime(2016)`
  (year-precision) result should use `days → years` (365). Proposes
  adopting the FHIRPath quantity conversion functions and adding the
  worked examples above to the CQL Date/Time Arithmetic topic. Notes
  alignment with the proposed disposition for
  [FHIR-53250](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53250).
- **cmoesel (2025-10-14):** Pushes back on the granularity-cascade
  approach. Worries that using `30` as the days→months factor — and
  *requiring* days→years to go through months — implies `364 days < 1
  year` is `false` in CQL, which he sees as worse than the inconsistency
  it would create. Points out FHIRPath itself defines `1 year` as "`12
  months` *or* `365 days`", leaving room to use the `days → years`
  factor (365) directly when the source is days. Acknowledges this
  yields its own oddity (`364 days < 1 year` true while `364 days > 12
  months` true) but prefers it to "364 days > 1 year".

## Related Jira Tickets

### [FHIR-53250: Calendar duration equivalence has conflicts](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53250)

A FHIRPath ticket (Implementable Technology Specifications work group,
Triaged, **Persuasive with Modification**) reporting that the
relationships `1 year ~ 1 'a' ~ 365.25 'd'` (UCUM) and `1 year ~ 365
days` cannot both hold without conflict. The proposed FHIRPath fix is to
remove the sentence in §6.1.2.1 that ties calendar durations into UCUM

explicit `toQuantity` calls only — they cannot be used implicitly in

(years, months) must be explicit, while definite-time conversions remain
transitive. FHIR-53148 is the CQL-side companion: the CQL Author's Guide
must reuse the FHIRPath conversion table and arithmetic semantics that
FHIR-53250 is settling, so both specs say the same thing.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add an explicit *Calendar Duration Conversion Factors* table to the CQL
Author's Guide §2 *Date/Time Arithmetic*, mirroring the FHIRPath
quantity-conversion table (`1 minute = 60 s`, `1 hour = 60 min`, `1 day
= 24 hr`, `1 month = 30 days`, `1 year = 12 months`) and normatively
state that **calendar-duration conversions are applied in order of
granularity and cannot skip a granularity step**. Update the
Date/Time Arithmetic prose so that, when a time-valued quantity is
added to or subtracted from a partial date/time, the quantity is
converted **down to the precision of the partial date/time** by
walking the granularity ladder (seconds → minutes → hours → days →
months → years), and the result is computed at that precision with the
decimal portion truncated. Add the two worked examples Bryn provided as
normative examples:

```cql
DateTime(2016, 5) - 31535999 seconds = DateTime(2015, 5)
// 31535999 s → 525599.9833 min → 8759.9997 hr → 364.9999 d
// → 12.16 mo  (uses days→months = 30, because target precision is month)

DateTime(2016) - 31535999 seconds = DateTime(2016)
// 31535999 s → ... → 364.9999 d → 0.9999 yr
// (uses days→years = 365, because target precision is year)
```

Confirm `cqframework/cql-tests#72`'s expected outcome of `true` for the
month-precision case under this rule.

#### Justification

This is what the reporter is asking for and it is the only reading that
makes the existing cql-tests expected outcome (`true`) defensible. It
also keeps CQL aligned with FHIRPath, which is exactly what FHIR-53250
is converging on (calendar conversion factors are the *only* legal
conversion mechanism for variable-length periods, and they are
explicit). Stating "convert through granularities to the target
precision" gives implementers a deterministic algorithm and removes the
ambiguity that produced the test failure. It also dovetails with the
existing CQL prose that the *precision of the first argument* drives
the result.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt the FHIRPath conversion table verbatim, but change the conversion
*algorithm* to use the **most-direct** factor available for the source
granularity rather than always cascading through every intermediate
granularity. Specifically, when converting a quantity of unit *U* to
target precision *P*, use the single conversion factor `U → P` if one
is defined in the table (e.g., `days → years = 365`); only cascade
through intermediates when no direct factor exists. Republish the
existing test as:

```cql
DateTime(2016, 5) - 31535999 seconds = DateTime(2015, 5)  // false
DateTime(2016)    - 31535999 seconds = DateTime(2016)     // true
```

Add explicit worked examples to §2 Date/Time Arithmetic and revise
`cqframework/cql-tests#72` accordingly. Document that comparisons
involving days, months, and years are intentionally non-transitive
across variable-length periods, mirroring the disposition direction in
FHIR-53250.

#### Justification

This is Chris Moesel's concern in concrete form. Forcing the
days→months→years cascade means `364 days < 1 year` evaluates to
`false`, which is counter-intuitive to clinicians and quality-measure
authors and harder to defend than a localized inconsistency between
`days vs years` and `months vs years`. It also lines up better with
FHIRPath §6.1.2.1's explicit allowance that `1 year` may be defined as
*either* `12 months` or `365 days`. The trade-off vs. Disposition A is
that the previously-published cql-tests expectation flips for the
month-precision case, which is a behavior change for any engine that
already implements the cascade — and it weakens the alignment with
Bryn's reading of "convert to the most precise value specified in the
first argument".

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline as **out of scope for this ticket** on the grounds that the
underlying ambiguity is a FHIRPath quantity-conversion question being
worked in [FHIR-53250](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53250), and
that CQL inherits its calendar-duration semantics from FHIRPath. Close
FHIR-53148 with a pointer to FHIR-53250 and a follow-up tracking item
to mechanically pull the FHIRPath language into the CQL Author's Guide
once FHIR-53250 lands (i.e., editorial sync only, no new CQL semantics).

#### Justification

CQL §2 already says quantity conversions follow FHIRPath
([`02-authorsguide.html#datetime-arithmetic`](https://cql.hl7.org/02-authorsguide.html#datetime-arithmetic)
links the FHIRPath quantity model). Settling the rule in two places
risks the two specs drifting; settling it in FHIRPath and pulling it
through to CQL editorially keeps a single source of truth. The
declination would be defensible *only* if the work group is confident
FHIR-53250's resolution will fully cover the granularity-cascade
question — which today it does not (FHIR-53250 is about *equivalence*,
not arithmetic), so this is the weakest of the three options.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A — Accept As Requested, with one
amendment from Disposition B baked in.

The strongest argument for Disposition A is that it preserves the
already-published cql-tests expectation, gives implementers a single
deterministic algorithm, and is explicitly what the reporter (and CQL
spec editor) is asking for. Chris Moesel's objection is real, but it is
about an *unavoidable* tension between calendar arithmetic and
duration comparison that no disposition can fully eliminate — the
question is only *where* to put the inconsistency. Putting it where
Disposition A puts it (`364 days < 1 year` is `false` because both are
walked through the same granularity ladder) keeps the CQL arithmetic
result stable and matches Bryn's reading of "convert to the most
precise value specified in the first argument." The amendment from
Disposition B worth carrying: explicitly document the `364 days < 1
year = false` consequence in the Date/Time Arithmetic section as a
known, intentional outcome so implementers and measure authors are
not surprised, and add it to `cqframework/cql-tests` as a normative
example. Disposition C is not recommended because FHIR-53250's scope
is equivalence, not arithmetic, so deferring leaves the cql-tests
ambiguity unresolved.
