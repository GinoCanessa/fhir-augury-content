## Summary

The reporter (Angela Flanagan) is requesting a set of small editorial
improvements to the Clinical Quality Language (CQL) Authors Guide. The
requests fall into two categories: (1) add definitions for two key
terms — "construct" (used in §1.2.1 Declarations) and "parameter"
(used in §1.2.1.5 Parameters) — so that readers unfamiliar with
programming-language terminology have a grounded explanation before the
terms are used further in the spec; and (2) make two minor wording
corrections — in §1.2.3 Queries, broaden the description of an alias
from "abbreviation" to "abbreviation or word(s)" (since aliases are
not required to be abbreviations), and in §1.3.2.4.1
DefaultComparisonPrecision repair what appears to be a truncated
sentence that currently begins with "Means" (e.g., change to "This
means" or "It means"). No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip
discussions, or GitHub items were surfaced for this change request.

## Details

**Description:**

Authors Guide 1.2.1 Declarations Consider defining/describing what
constructs are e.g., *Tokens used to build a measure are referred to as
a construct. These constructs dictate how instructions are carried out
and how data is manipulated.*

1.2.1.5 Parameters Consider defining/describing parameters e.g.,
*Parameters are variables specified in a function's declaration or
definition that serve as placeholders for the values provided when the
function is invoked. They enable functions to accept and process various
inputs, enhancing their flexibility and reusability.*

1.2.3 Queries, 4th sentence following the table, since an alias does
not need to be an abbreviation, consider adding *or word(s)* after the
term *abbreviation* (abbreviation or word(s)).

1.3.2.4.1 DefaultComparisonPrecision, following the 2nd box, the
sentence starts with *Means* seems incomplete, consider indicating
*This means* or *it means*.

Thank you.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply all four editorial changes to the CQL Authors Guide as the
reporter wrote them:

1. **§1.2.1 Declarations** — Add a sentence (or short paragraph) near
   the first use of *construct* that defines the term, using language
   along the lines of: "Tokens used to build a measure are referred to
   as a *construct*. These constructs dictate how instructions are
   carried out and how data is manipulated."
2. **§1.2.1.5 Parameters** — Add a definition near the first use of
   *parameter*: "Parameters are variables specified in a function's
   declaration or definition that serve as placeholders for the values
   provided when the function is invoked. They enable functions to
   accept and process various inputs, enhancing their flexibility and
   reusability."
3. **§1.2.3 Queries** — In the 4th sentence following the table,
   change "…an alias (abbreviation)…" (or equivalent phrasing) to
   "…an alias (abbreviation or word(s))…" so the wording matches the
   spec's actual rule that aliases may be any identifier, not just an
   abbreviation.
4. **§1.3.2.4.1 DefaultComparisonPrecision** — In the paragraph
   following the 2nd code/callout box, change the sentence that begins
   "Means …" to "This means …" (preferred) to fix what reads as a
   truncated sentence.

#### Justification

These are narrowly scoped editorial/clarification changes to a
non-normative Authors Guide. The two new definitions sharpen the
on-ramp for authors who are not career programmers (a central audience
for CQL authoring guidance), and the two wording fixes correct
objective inaccuracies: aliases are demonstrably not required to be
abbreviations, and a sentence beginning with a lowercase verb-less
"Means" is plainly a typo. None of the changes alter CQL semantics,
grammar, or conformance rules, so there is no risk to implementers or
to cross-spec consistency.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the intent of all four items, but consolidate (1) and (2) by
**introducing a short Glossary subsection in §1.2 (or cross-linking an
existing glossary)** that defines foundational terms — including
*construct*, *parameter*, *alias*, *expression*, *declaration*,
*context*, and similar terms — in one place, and then link to those
glossary entries from §1.2.1 and §1.2.1.5 rather than inserting
stand-alone definitions inline. Also accept the §1.2.3 and §1.3.2.4.1
wording fixes as-requested (3 and 4 above). If a glossary already
exists elsewhere in the CQL specification family, extend it instead of
creating a new one, and add "See Glossary" links from the two inline
sites the reporter flagged.

#### Justification

Inline definitions inserted only at the first usage tend to drift out
of sync with later uses and make it harder for readers who enter the
Authors Guide mid-document to find a term. A single glossary entry
(with anchors) is more maintainable, supports future additions as more
editorial feedback arrives, and is consistent with how many HL7 IGs
handle pedagogical vocabulary. The trade-off is that this is slightly
more scope than the reporter literally asked for, and it introduces a
small structural change to the Authors Guide; however, the incremental
editorial cost is low and the long-term maintenance benefit is
meaningful. This approach still fully addresses the reporter's
underlying concern (readers bump into undefined terms) while avoiding
the "definition scattered across first-use sites" anti-pattern.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request on the grounds that (a) *construct*, *parameter*,
and *alias* are standard programming-language terms assumed as
background for a CQL author, (b) the Authors Guide is non-normative and
readers needing a general-purpose definition can consult outside
references, and (c) the two "typo-like" items (§1.2.3 *abbreviation*
wording and §1.3.2.4.1 *Means* sentence) can be captured by a future
editorial pass rather than processed as individual ticket
dispositions. Close as *Not Persuasive* / *Works as Intended* with a
comment pointing to existing materials.

#### Justification

A principled reason to decline is that scope-creeping an Authors Guide
with definitions for every general-computing term risks turning it into
a programming primer rather than a CQL authoring guide. For the wording
fixes, one could argue these are small enough to be folded into a bulk
editorial sweep rather than tracked as a standalone change. That said,
this rationale is weak: two of the four items are objectively incorrect
or truncated text (not just stylistic), and the definitions requested
are short and targeted rather than expansive. This disposition is
included for completeness but is not recommended.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A is the right call. All four requested changes are
small, self-contained, non-normative, and either correct an objective
defect (§1.2.3 alias wording, §1.3.2.4.1 truncated sentence) or
materially lower the barrier to entry for new CQL authors
(§1.2.1 construct, §1.2.1.5 parameter). The effort is minimal, there
are no implementer impacts, and the reporter even supplied
ready-to-use definition text that the editors can adopt or refine.
Disposition B (a centralized glossary) is a reasonable direction, but
it expands scope beyond what the ticket asks for and is better raised
as its own change request if the workgroup wants to standardize the
Authors Guide's handling of vocabulary. Disposition C is difficult to
defend given that two of the four items are plainly wrong text in the
published guide. Accept as requested and fold the edits into the next
Authors Guide publication cycle.
