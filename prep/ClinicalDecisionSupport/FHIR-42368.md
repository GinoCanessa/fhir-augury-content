## Summary

Bas van den Heuvel reports that the workflow language for the CDS Hooks
`appointment-book` hook is ambiguous about *when* the hook fires. The
current text leaves implementers unable to tell whether the hook is
triggered once at the start of scheduling, on every change to a draft
appointment, only just before the appointment is saved, or some
combination of these. The reporter asks the work group to revise the
spec to make explicit (1) whether the hook is called once or multiple
times, (2) which moment(s) apply when called once, and (3) which
moments are required versus optional for the EHR to invoke. The
reporter further argues that even if it is called only at save, it
should fire again whenever a previously-suggested change is accepted.

The CDS Hooks WG initially voted (2023-11-01, 17-0-0, Vetter/White) to
mark the ticket "Considered for Future Use" and add an implementer
callout soliciting feedback (questions about which user the hook fires
for, whether it should fire only on confirm/finalize, and how to handle
patient-initiated bookings booked directly into the EHR). That
resolution was reverted post-publication (lynn_laakso, 2024-03-27 —
"re-open post publication") and the ticket was re-resolved as
**Persuasive with Modification** with the modification being to add an
implementer callout asking for feedback on how the `appointment-book`
hook does and should work, rather than redefining the firing semantics
outright. The change was applied in
[cds-hooks/docs PR #649](https://github.com/cds-hooks/docs/pull/649)
(merge commit `f37eb38`, 2024-01-03).

There are no other cross-referenced Jira tickets. Related Zulip
discussion exists in adjacent topics (the Da Vinci CRD stream and the
`cds hooks` stream) but no thread directly references FHIR-42368.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Normatively tighten the "Workflow" section of the `appointment-book`
hook on `cds-hooks/docs` (`site/hooks/appointment-book.md` and the
forward-going snapshot) to specify exactly when the hook MUST and MAY
fire. Concretely:

- The hook **SHALL** fire **once**, **immediately before** the EHR
  finalizes (saves/confirms) the appointment as booked — analogous to
  the published intent for `order-sign` referenced by Isaac Vetter in
  the comments.
- The hook **SHALL** fire **again** whenever a card-suggested change to
  the proposed appointment is accepted by the user, so that the CDS
  service can re-evaluate the now-modified booking.
- The hook **SHALL NOT** fire on every intermediate edit to a draft
  appointment (explicitly close out the "every change" reading the
  reporter listed as ambiguous).
- The hook **SHOULD** fire regardless of which actor (provider,
  scheduler, or patient self-service) is finalizing the booking, with
  a non-normative note acknowledging patient-initiated booking
  workflows.

Replace the current ambiguous "Workflow" prose with this enumerated
list and remove (or fold into a non-normative note) the implementer
callout currently published from PR #649.

#### Justification

This is what the reporter literally asked for: deterministic firing
rules so EHRs and CDS services agree on when `appointment-book` is
invoked. The "fire once at finalize, plus re-fire on accepted changes"
pattern is consistent with the `order-sign` design Isaac Vetter cited
on 2023-10-25 ("I wouldn't do it at each step; seems way too chatty
and awkward"), and is consistent with the questions Bas and Isaac
brainstormed in the 2023-09-13 comment thread. Da Vinci CRD's reliance
on `appointment-book` (visible in the related Zulip threads) means
implementer alignment matters now, not in a future cycle.

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach (the modification already applied — keep the implementer callout, defer normative firing rules)

#### Proposal

Retain the change applied in
[cds-hooks/docs#649](https://github.com/cds-hooks/docs/pull/649): keep
the implementer-feedback callout on the `appointment-book` page asking
when the hook does/should fire, for which users, and how to handle
patient-initiated bookings booked directly into the EHR. Do **not**
add new normative firing rules in this cycle. Pair the callout with a
short non-normative "informally, the hook is intended to fire at
finalize and again on accepted card-suggested changes" sentence so the
spec at least signals the WG's intuition, and open a follow-up ticket
to convert the informative guidance to normative language once the
callout has gathered implementer responses (target: next CDS Hooks
ballot cycle).

#### Justification

This is the disposition that has actually been applied (current
resolution: **Persuasive with Modification**) and is what the WG
chose after reverting the original "Considered for Future Use" vote.
The Zulip "hook maturity" thread shows that real-world implementation
of `appointment-book` is still thin (Isaac: not implemented in
sandbox; Kyle Johnsen: maturity too high → FHIR-42045), which is a
meaningful argument for soliciting implementer experience before
locking in normative SHALLs. It also avoids a breaking change to
existing CDS services and to consumers like Da Vinci CRD without
clear evidence that the proposed semantics match the field. The
follow-up ticket discipline keeps the ambiguity from becoming
permanent.

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline to make any further change beyond the implementer callout
already published. Treat FHIR-42368 as fully resolved by PR #649,
close it, and mark any further firing-semantics work as out of scope
for this ticket. Direct any future re-tightening proposals to a fresh
ticket scoped to a specific normative rule, ideally backed by
responses to the published callout.

#### Justification

The literal request — "revise the language to make it clear" — has
been addressed in the only way the WG has consensus on right now: by
explicitly flagging the ambiguity in-spec and inviting feedback. The
WG's first vote (17-0-0 for "Considered for Future Use") and the
substance of Isaac Vetter's comments show that the WG does not yet
have enough implementer signal to commit to one set of firing rules.
Closing the ticket as Persuasive with Modification (already the
current state) avoids relitigating a question on which the WG has
already taken a deliberate, documented position.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **B** — Alternative Approach (keep the
applied callout, add a single non-normative sentence about the
intended firing model, open a follow-up ticket targeting the next
ballot cycle).

Rationale: Disposition B preserves the WG's already-applied resolution
(so no rework, no re-balloting of PR #649) while addressing the
legitimate criticism in Disposition A's favour — that a callout alone
leaves implementers without even an informative steer. The Zulip
record makes it clear that maturity for `appointment-book` is still
low and that real implementations are sparse, which argues against the
normative tightening in Disposition A this cycle: the WG would be
guessing at semantics rather than codifying observed practice. Pure
Disposition C, on the other hand, leaves the implementer-facing
ambiguity that downstream Da Vinci CRD discussions show is causing
real confusion. Disposition B threads that needle: the spec
acknowledges the WG's intuition (fire at finalize, fire again on
accepted changes), keeps soliciting feedback, and commits the WG to
revisit with a concrete normative proposal once that feedback is in.

Before any change beyond what is already applied, request a
`repo-analysis` run for `cds-hooks/docs` so the next ticket has
verified authoring-root, snapshot, and cross-repo information to act
on.
