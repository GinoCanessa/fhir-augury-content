## Summary

The reporter argues that the CDS Hooks (FHIR) Implementation Guide has design
and layout problems that are inappropriate for a FHIR IG. Two specific
concerns are raised: (1) the Home Page is intended (per the reporter's
understanding of HL7 conventions) to be informative-only, yet the CDS Hooks
home page is full of normative conformance language; and (2) almost all of
the specification's requirements are concentrated on the Home Page rather
than being distributed across a multi-page menu structure that is more
familiar to FHIR implementers. The ticket asks that the IG be restructured
to use a menu/page layout that aligns with HL7's expectations around form.

There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets and no related Zulip threads in
Augury's index. The only cross-reference is a "mentions"-style link from
the ticket's comment thread to a Da Vinci Confluence page
(`104575113` — "Da Vinci IG Developer Guidance Brainstorm"), cited by the
spec lead as an example of group-internal IG structure guidance that is
not an HL7-wide requirement.

## Details

## Proposed Dispositions

Because there is no GitHub cross-reference, none of the dispositions can
name a specific authoring path drawn from a `repo-analysis` briefing. The

applied in that IG's authoring repository per its normal publication
workflow. The dispositions name what would change in the IG, not the
exact file path.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Restructure the CDS Hooks (FHIR) IG so that the Home Page becomes a
purely informative landing page (overview, audience, scope, change
history, navigation) and split the existing normative content out of the
home page into dedicated pages on the IG menu, organised at minimum as:

- **Specification** (or **Conformance**) — the SHALL/SHOULD/MAY
  conformance language currently on the home page (Hook anatomy,
  request/response payloads, Card, Action, Override Reason, Suggestion,
  service discovery, security, FHIR resource access).
- **Hooks** — the catalogue of named hooks and their context.
- **Security** — authorization, JWT/JWKS, CORS guidance.
- **FHIR Resource Access** — the FHIR-server-callback parts of the spec.
- **Examples**, **Glossary**, **Change Log**, **Downloads**, etc., as
  separate menu entries.

Update the IG menu (`menu.xml` / `pages.json`-style configuration in the
IG publisher's authoring layout) so that each new page is reachable from
the top-level navigation, and ensure no normative ("SHALL", "SHOULD",
"MAY") language remains on the Home Page.

#### Justification

This is the layout pattern that the majority of FHIR IGs (including
US Core, Da Vinci IGs, IPS, SDOH-CC) follow, and it is the structure
that the FHIR IG Publisher templates are optimised for. Separating
informative landing material from normative requirements makes it
easier for implementers to (a) onboard via the home page without
wading through SHALLs, and (b) cite specific normative sections by
URL rather than by deep anchor in a single very long page. It also
removes the friction the reporter described for implementers
"accustomed to standard layout of specifications."

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the single-page conformance design (per Isaac's stated rationale
that, at CDS Hooks's current size, a single page improves
discoverability of conformance requirements and readability), but make
two targeted changes that address the reporter's substantive concerns
without restructuring the IG:

1. Add a clear in-page **"Status of this document / Document
   structure"** block near the top that explicitly tells the reader
   the spec is intentionally published as a single normative page, and
   provides a sticky table of contents / anchor menu so the reader can
   jump directly to any section (Hook anatomy, Discovery, Cards,
   Security, FHIR Resource Access, etc.).
2. Refactor the Home Page so that the *first screen* is informative
   only — overview, audience, scope, "what's new" — and the normative
   conformance text begins below that, under a clearly labelled
   "Specification" heading (still on the same page). This preserves
   the single-page architecture while satisfying the convention that
   the very top of the home page is informative.

No new pages are added; menu changes are limited to the in-page table
of contents.

#### Justification

This addresses both of the reporter's concerns (informative-first home
page; predictable navigation for implementers) without paying the cost
of splitting a tightly-cross-referenced normative spec across many
pages. CDS Hooks is small enough that splitting it can actually harm
discoverability — readers lose the "Ctrl-F across the whole spec"
affordance and gain N pages they have to navigate between. It also
matches the precedent Isaac cited
([hl7.org/fhirpath/N1/](https://hl7.org/fhirpath/N1/)), where another
HL7 spec uses a single-page normative layout. The reporter's
March 18 reply ("ok to let go" on the layout point) suggests this
middle path is likely acceptable.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request as out of scope / not actionable. Rationale:
(a) the reporter has not produced evidence of an HL7-wide requirement
that an IG home page be informative-only or that an IG must use a
menu/multi-page structure (Isaac asked twice and no citation was
provided); (b) the reporter has explicitly conceded the layout point
on March 18 ("ok to let go"); and (c) the editorial choice between
single-page and multi-page IG layout is a publication-style decision
delegated to the responsible work group (Clinical Decision Support),
not a conformance issue.

#### Justification

Without an HL7 governance citation, the request reduces to a
preference about IG style. The CDS Hooks WG has consistently chosen
the single-page form, and the reporter has not demonstrated concrete
implementer harm — only a perceived inconsistency with other IGs.
Declining preserves WG editorial authority and avoids a high-cost
restructuring of a stable normative spec. If HL7 later publishes
binding IG-structure guidance, a fresh ticket can be opened against
that guidance.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The exchange between the reporter and the spec lead converged on a
narrow, real concern (the very top of the Home Page mixing
informative framing with normative SHALLs) and a broader stylistic
preference (multi-page vs. single-page) that the reporter himself
walked back on March 18. Disposition A pays a large editorial and
URL-stability cost to address the broader preference that the
reporter no longer pushes. Disposition C dismisses the narrow
concern and leaves the home page as-is, which is harder to defend
when a workgroup colleague raises it in good faith. Disposition B
keeps the WG's intentional single-page architecture (consistent with
the FHIRPath precedent), explicitly documents that choice for
readers, and surfaces an informative-first opening on the home page
— addressing the substantive complaint without rewriting the IG.
The WG should treat this as a small editorial change to the home
page plus an in-page TOC, not as an IG restructuring project.
