## Summary

FHIR-53173 reports an apparent inconsistency in the Clinical Quality
Language (CQL) specification regarding how durations expressed in
calendar units (years, months) are compared with definite-time units
(days and below) when there is no calendar anchor. The Authoring Guide
§1.2.5.4.1 states that "the duration of a year is considered to be 365
days, and the duration of a month is considered to be 30 days" for
comparisons involving time durations where no calendar anchor is
available, which reads as a blanket rule for *all* comparison
operators. However, the normative CQL Reference Appendix B §1.9.5.2
(`=` / equality) says that "conversion between variable length calendar
durations (i.e. years and months) and definite-time durations (i.e.
days or below) results in null," while only §1.9.5.3 (`~` /
equivalence) uses the `1 year ~ 365 days`, `1 month ~ 30 days`
convention. The reporter, Chris Moesel, asks that the Authoring Guide
be clarified to reflect that the 365-day / 30-day rule does *not* apply
to equality, and also asks that the behavior of the ordered comparison
operators (`<`, `<=`, `>`, `>=`) in this same "no anchor, mixed
calendar/definite units" case be specified (e.g., what does
`1 month > 29 days` evaluate to?). No related Jira tickets, Zulip
threads, or GitHub items were surfaced by FhirAugury for this ticket.

## Details

**Description:**

The [Authoring Guide 1.2.5.4.1](https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/cql/02-authorsguide.html#constructing-datetime-values) says:

> For comparisons involving time durations (where no anchor to a calendar is available), the duration of a year is considered to be 365 days, and the duration of a month is considered to be 30 days.

But [Appendix B 1.9.5.2 and 1.9.5.3](https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/cql/09-b-cqlreference.html#equal) indicates that for *equality*, "conversion between variable length calendar durations (i.e. years and months) and definite-time durations (i.e. days or below) results in null" – and for *equivalence* it shows that `1 year ~ 365 days` and `1 month ~ 30 days`.

So... assuming that Appendix B is correct, I think the Authoring Guide language needs to be clarified because it implies that 1 year is 365 days and 1 month is 30 days for *all* comparisons (when it's *not* the case for *equality*).

It's also unclear how `<` and `>` behave in these cases (e.g., is `1 month > 29 days`?).

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply two targeted edits to the CQL specification narrative:

1. **Authoring Guide §1.2.5.4.1 (Constructing DateTime Values).**
   Replace the current blanket statement with language that scopes the
   `1 year = 365 days` / `1 month = 30 days` convention to the *ordered
   comparison* operators (`<`, `<=`, `>`, `>=`) and to the *equivalent*
   (`~`) operator, and explicitly calls out that for the *equal* (`=`)
   operator, a comparison that mixes variable-length calendar
   durations (years, months) with definite-time durations (weeks,
   days, hours, minutes, seconds, milliseconds) when no calendar
   anchor is available returns `null`, matching Appendix B §1.9.5.2.
2. **Appendix B §1.9.5.4–§1.9.5.5 (or wherever `<` / `>` live) —
   ordered comparison of durations.** Add a normative sentence that,
   when comparing an unanchored variable-length calendar duration to
   a definite-time duration, `1 year` is treated as `365 days` and
   `1 month` is treated as `30 days`, so that `1 month > 29 days` is
   `true`, `1 month > 30 days` is `false`, and `1 year < 365 days` is
   `false`. Cross-link this from §1.9.5.3 (equivalent) and the
   updated Authoring Guide section so all three operator families
   tell the same story.

#### Justification

This is the minimum-risk disposition: the reporter is not asking for
a behavior change, only for the Authoring Guide to stop contradicting
the normative Appendix B text for `=`, and for the ordered-comparison
behavior to be made explicit rather than left to inference. Clarifying
the `=` vs `~` split preserves the long-standing CQL stance that `=`
is conservative (null on ambiguity) while `~` is deliberately
lenient (uses conventional durations). Making the `<` / `>` rule
explicit also closes a real portability gap: implementations today
must guess whether to mirror `=` (null) or `~` (365/30) for ordered
comparison, and the reporter's `1 month > 29 days` example shows the
two answers are materially different.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the Authoring Guide rewrite from Disposition A, but *instead of*
pinning `<` / `>` to the 365/30 convention, change Appendix B so that
the ordered-comparison operators follow the same "mixed
calendar/definite durations without an anchor ⇒ `null`" rule as `=`.
Equivalence (`~`) would remain the one operator that keeps the
365/30 shortcut. Concretely:

- In Appendix B, amend the sections defining `<`, `<=`, `>`, `>=` on
  Quantity/duration values so that, when the left and right operands
  mix variable-length calendar units (years, months) with
  definite-time units and no calendar anchor is in scope, the result
  is `null`.
- In the Authoring Guide §1.2.5.4.1 update, replace the blanket
  365/30 statement with a single sentence that says the 365/30
  convention applies *only* to equivalence (`~`) and to explicit
  unit conversions, and that equality and ordered comparison both
  return `null` for this mixed-unit, unanchored case.

#### Justification

This approach is more defensible on semantic grounds: `1 month` and
`30 days` are *not* the same quantity in any calendar that has
February, so answering `1 month > 29 days = true` while
`1 month = 30 days = null` is internally inconsistent. Treating
ordered comparison the same as equality (null on ambiguity) keeps a
single, easy-to-teach rule: "variable-length + definite-time without
an anchor ⇒ null, except under `~`." The cost is that it is a
potential behavior change for any implementation that has been
reading the Authoring Guide's current blanket statement as normative
and returning `true`/`false` for these ordered comparisons; this
would need to be disclosed in the change note.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as "Not Persuasive" (or "Works as Designed") on the grounds
that Appendix B is the normative source and the Authoring Guide is
non-normative tutorial text. The Authoring Guide's statement is not
wrong as long as the reader applies the scoping already documented
in Appendix B §1.9.5.2 / §1.9.5.3, and CQL implementers are expected
to consult the reference appendix for operator-level semantics. No
spec edits are made.

#### Justification

This disposition avoids any risk of a behavior change landing in a
published IG, and it relies on the established CQL convention that
Appendix B is authoritative. If the WG's view is that no current
implementation is actually wrong — and that the Authoring Guide
paragraph is just informative — declining is defensible. The cost is
that the specific ambiguity the reporter flagged ("is
`1 month > 29 days`?") stays unresolved, and future readers of the
Authoring Guide will hit the same contradiction.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Accept-as-requested is the strongest match for what the ticket
actually asks. The reporter explicitly calls out that the Authoring
Guide "needs to be clarified," and the secondary question about
`<` / `>` is a real gap regardless of which way the WG answers it.
Disposition A resolves the documented inconsistency, preserves the
existing operator semantics that implementations have likely settled
on (`=` null, `~` uses 365/30, ordered comparison uses 365/30), and
pays back the clarification debt in the Authoring Guide. Disposition
B is semantically cleaner but is a potential behavior change for
ordered comparison and should not be adopted without evidence that
current implementations agree; the WG can always escalate to B in a
follow-up ticket if a survey of implementers shows divergence.
Disposition C leaves the reporter's concrete example
(`1 month > 29 days`) unanswered in the spec and should be avoided.
