contains one or more phrases that imply missing or incomplete content —
specifically, the literal string "TODO". The reporter (Lloyd McKenzie) asks
that any such "TODO" markers be either removed (when the work has actually

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Locate every occurrence of the literal token "TODO" (case-insensitive) on
the `RequestOrchestration` resource page and its companion authoring
fragments (typically the resource's intro / notes / search-parameter /
operation narrative files), and either:

1. Delete the "TODO" sentence/paragraph outright if the surrounding
   content is now complete and the marker is stale; or
2. Rewrite the "TODO" sentence as a proper implementation note (e.g.,
   "Implementers should be aware that…") when the gap is a real,
   known limitation that needs to be communicated to readers.

After the edits, confirm by searching the rendered page to ensure no
"TODO" string remains visible to readers.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the ticket asks for and matches the standard editorial
hygiene that the spec applies before ballot. "TODO" markers are
internal-author shorthand and have no business surviving into a published
normative or trial-use page; either they reflect work that was finished
without the marker being cleaned up (delete) or they reflect a real gap
that is more useful to readers as a clearly-labelled implementation note
(rewrite). Because the ticket is purely editorial, with no cross-references
or community debate against it, the lowest-friction defensible action is
to do exactly what was requested.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Address the underlying need at the class level rather than per-page:
treat FHIR-55073 as one instance of a broader sweep of "Incomplete
content indicated on: \<page\>" tickets (Comment 39 import), and resolve
this ticket by:

1. Triaging each "TODO" on the `RequestOrchestration` page into one of
   three buckets — *stale (delete)*, *real gap with no resolution this
   cycle (convert to implementation note)*, or *real gap that still
   needs Clinical Decision Support work (split into a follow-on tracker
   with a substantive title)*.
2. For the third bucket, file (or link) a successor ticket per
   remaining real gap so the issue is tracked on its own merits instead
   of being silently buried by an editorial pass.
3. Closing FHIR-55073 only once the page no longer contains a "TODO"
   marker, with the successor tickets carrying any unresolved design
   work.

#### Justification

A blanket "delete or footnote every TODO" pass risks hiding genuine
unresolved design issues behind polished prose, which is the opposite
of what the original "TODO" was trying to flag. This alternative keeps
the editorial cleanup that the ticket asks for, but routes any actual
substantive gaps into properly-titled tickets that the Clinical
Decision Support work group can prioritise. The cost is one extra
triage step per "TODO" and possibly a small number of new tickets;
the benefit is that nothing real is lost in the cleanup.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline FHIR-55073 as not actionable in its current form, on the
grounds that the description does not name *which* "TODO" markers are
problematic or *where* on the page they appear; the reporter has only
quoted the search term. Ask the reporter (or whichever editor opens the
follow-on) to refile with the specific line(s) and proposed wording, or
absorb the work into a routine pre-publication editorial sweep that
does not need a Jira ticket.

#### Justification

"Find and remove all TODOs" is process-level housekeeping rather than a
content change request, and Jira is a poor tracker for it: the ticket
will be repeatedly re-opened any time a new "TODO" appears, and there is
no clear "done" criterion beyond "string not present", which an
automated lint check would catch faster and more reliably than a
human ticket. Declining (with a pointer to a lint or pre-publication
editorial pass) frees workgroup time for substantive design issues.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition A does the right surface-level thing but risks erasing
genuine unresolved design questions that an author originally flagged
with "TODO". Disposition C correctly identifies that a pure
"strip TODOs" task belongs in a pre-publication lint rather than
workgroup review, but unilaterally declining a Highest-priority ticket
filed by Lloyd McKenzie is likely to be reversed without addressing the
underlying page hygiene. Disposition B is the pragmatic middle: do the
cleanup the reporter asked for, but treat each "TODO" as a triage
decision so any real Clinical Decision Support work gets a properly-
named successor ticket instead of being quietly footnoted away. That
preserves the editorial outcome the ticket wants while protecting the
information value the original "TODO" markers were trying to convey.
