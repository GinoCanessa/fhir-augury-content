## Summary

The reporter (Cooper Thompson) observes that the Da Vinci DTR
specification uses the RFC 2119 conformance keyword **MAY** in three
narrative passages where, in his view, no conformance obligation is
actually being expressed. The passages describe optional EHR/payer
behaviors — that an EHR is free to switch among DTR SMART apps, that
an EHR may choose to support multiple DTR SMART apps, and that payers
may operate multiple back-end functions handling different decision
support services. Because none of these statements impose, permit, or
prohibit a testable conformance behavior on a DTR actor, the reporter
suggests that the lowercase word "might" would be more accurate than
the normative "MAY". The ticket is Triaged as Persuasive with the
resolution proposing to replace **MAY** with "might" (or "might
choose") in the three identified sentences. There are no cross-linked
Jira tickets, Zulip discussions, or GitHub items associated with the
issue.

## Details

**Description:**

> We currently have this conformance statement:
>
> *This means that EHRs are free to choose which app they prefer and **MAY** switch apps as they see fit.*
>
> I don't think DTR app selection or switching is a conformance matter. Should this be "might" instead of "MAY"?
>
> Similarly for this statement:
>
> *In some cases, an EHR **MAY** opt to support multiple DTR SMART apps.*
>
> And this:
>
> *Payers **MAY** have multiple back-end functions that handle different types of decision support and/or different types of services.*

**Resolution Description (proposed):**

> Agree with the request. Will make the following changes:
>
> "This means that EHRs are free to choose which app they prefer and **MAY** switch apps as they see fit."
> to
> "This means that EHRs are free to choose which app they prefer and might switch apps as they see fit."
>
> ---
>
> "In some cases, an EHR **MAY** opt to support multiple DTR SMART apps."
> to
> "In some cases, an EHR might choose to support multiple DTR SMART apps."
>
> ---
>
> "Payers **MAY** have multiple back-end functions that handle different types of decision support and/or different types of services."
> to
> "Payers might have multiple back-end functions that handle different types of decision support and/or different types of services."

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the resolution exactly as drafted in the ticket: in the DTR
narrative, replace the three uppercase **MAY** keywords with the
lowercase, non-normative wording proposed by the reporter:

1. *"…and **MAY** switch apps as they see fit."* → *"…and might switch
   apps as they see fit."*
2. *"In some cases, an EHR **MAY** opt to support multiple DTR SMART
   apps."* → *"In some cases, an EHR might choose to support multiple
   DTR SMART apps."*
3. *"Payers **MAY** have multiple back-end functions that handle
   different types of decision support and/or different types of
   services."* → *"Payers might have multiple back-end functions that
   handle different types of decision support and/or different types
   of services."*

These are pure narrative-prose edits to the DTR IG; no
StructureDefinitions, CapabilityStatements, conformance constraints,
or examples are affected.

#### Justification

RFC 2119/8174 reserves the all-caps **MAY** for statements that grant
an actor a conformance-relevant option that an implementer or tester
must be able to evaluate. Each of the three sentences here merely
describes ecosystem reality — that an EHR is free to choose, switch,
or host multiple DTR SMART apps, and that payers may architect their
back-ends however they like — without placing any obligation,
permission, or prohibition on a DTR conformance actor. Using the
lowercase "might" preserves the descriptive intent without falsely
implying a testable conformance choice, which aligns with HL7's
standing guidance on the disciplined use of normative keywords. The
change is editorial only and was already reviewed and approved on the
3/25/2026 BR call (per jeff_brown's comment), and is labelled
Ready-For-Vote.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the underlying point that these are not conformance statements,
but rather than substituting "might" sentence-by-sentence, perform a
broader sweep:

- Re-examine the surrounding paragraphs in the DTR app-selection /
  multi-app / payer-back-end sections for any other RFC 2119 keywords
  (**MAY**, **SHOULD**, **MUST**, **SHALL**) that are similarly used
  in purely descriptive prose, and downgrade those to lowercase too.
- Consider relocating the three sentences (and any peers found in the
  sweep) into a clearly labelled non-normative "Background" or
  "Implementation considerations" subsection so that readers are not
  primed to look for conformance content there.
- Make the same editorial pass in any companion narrative pages that
  mirror this language (e.g., overview, architecture).

#### Justification

If three adjacent narrative statements have all been mis-categorised
as conformance, it is likely the surrounding text has the same
defect. Fixing only the three sentences flagged in the ticket leaves
peer sentences inconsistent and risks a follow-up ticket later. The
trade-off is a larger editorial diff and a slightly larger review
surface, but the change remains purely narrative and low risk.
Disposition A would still be available as a fallback if the broader
sweep is judged out of scope for this ballot cycle.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Reject the change and retain the existing **MAY** wording in all
three sentences, on the grounds that the IG has historically used
**MAY** broadly to flag implementer-visible options, and that an
editorial change carries non-zero risk of unintended interpretation
shifts in downstream readers and implementations.

#### Justification

A "decline" disposition is defensible only if one believes that the
existing **MAY** language is communicating an actual permission to a
conformance actor — for example, that the IG is explicitly authorising
EHRs to switch apps or to host multiple DTR SMART apps as a permitted
conformance behavior, and that removing **MAY** could be read as
withdrawing that permission. Stability of published narrative is also
a (weak) reason to leave well-enough alone close to a ballot.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is narrowly scoped, the proposed wording is already drafted
in the resolution, and the BR call on 3/25/2026 has already reviewed
and approved the change (label `Ready-For-Vote`). The reporter's
underlying point is correct under RFC 2119/8174: none of the three
sentences impose a testable conformance behavior on a DTR actor, so
the all-caps **MAY** is misleading. Disposition B is attractive in
principle but risks turning a rubber-stamp editorial fix into an
open-ended sweep that may not be in scope for the current ballot
window; if reviewers spot peer instances during the vote, those can be
captured as follow-up tickets. Disposition C is hard to defend because
keeping a non-conformance statement dressed up as **MAY** actively
misleads implementers and conformance testers. Accept as requested
and ship the editorial change.
