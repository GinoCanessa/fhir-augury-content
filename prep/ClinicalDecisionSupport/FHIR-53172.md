## Summary

Chris Moesel reports that the CQL specification uses the phrase "not
comparable" when describing the relationship between calendar duration
quantities (e.g., `1 year`, `1 month`) and UCUM definite-duration
quantities (e.g., `1 'a'`, `1 'mo'`) at durations of a week or more, but
never defines what "not comparable" means operationally. The Authors
Guide §1.2.4.2.1 says durations above days are "not comparable" with
UCUM definite-duration units, and the prose example that `1 year` is
"not equal (=) to 1 'a'" while being "equivalent (~) to 1 'a'" strongly
implies that equality returns `false`. However, tests added in
[cqframework/cql-tests PR #78](https://github.com/cqframework/cql-tests/pull/78/files)
expect the result to be `null` (empty), which would align with the
general CQL treatment of incomparable operands but is not what the
current normative text says. The ticket also asks whether the ordered
comparison operators (`<`, `<=`, `>`, `>=`) should return `true`/`false`
when one side is obviously greater (e.g., `1 year < 2 'a'`) or likewise
return `null`.

A closely related FHIRPath ticket,
[FHIR-53250](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53250) ("Calendar duration
equivalence has conflicts"), was dispositioned Persuasive with
Modification by ITS and proposes replacing the ambiguous "not
comparable" language with explicit "results in null" wording, as well as
stating that calendar-duration conversion factors exist only for the
`toQuantity()` function and cannot be used implicitly in equality,
equivalence, or comparison. In his 2025-11-29 comment on FHIR-53172,
Bryn Rhodes proposes the exact same rewording for the analogous
sentences in the CQL Authors Guide and in the Greater / Greater Or Equal
/ Less / Less Or Equal sections of the CQL Reference, keeping CQL
aligned with the FHIRPath disposition. A sibling FHIRPath ticket,
[FHIR-52957](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-52957) ("Inconsistency in
equality/equivalence of calendar/definite durations"), has already been
marked Applied in FHIRPath.

## Details

**Description:**

Several parts of the spec indicate that calendar and definite duration
quantities are "not comparable" for durations above days — but (as far
as I can tell) the spec does not say what "not comparable" means in
terms of implementation. For example, the
[Authors Guide 1.2.4.2.1](https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/cql/02-authorsguide.html#quantities)
just says:

> Durations above days (and weeks) are calendar durations that are not
> comparable with definite quantity UCUM duration units.

There is some hint of what to do further down in the section, by way of
an example:

> For example, 1 year is not equal (=) to 1 'a' (defined in UCUM as
> 365.25 'd'), but it is equivalent (~) to 1 'a'.

This implies that `1 year != 1 'a'` is `true` (it says they are "not
equal"), but tests in a
[recent CQL Tests PR](https://github.com/cqframework/cql-tests/pull/78/files)
indicate that the answer should be `null`. I think that `null` is a
reasonable way to implement "not comparable", but the spec does not make
this clear.

It's also not clear how `<` and `>` should behave. Can we say that
`1 year < 2 'a'` is `true` (since it obviously *is*) or do we still need
to say `null`?

**Comments:**

### bryn.rhodes (2025-11-29)

Consistent with the disposition for this FHIRPath ticket
[FHIR-53250](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53250), we should change
the Authors Guide §1.2.4.2.1 language to:

> Durations above days (and weeks) are calendar durations that are not
> comparable (i.e. result in null) with definite quantity UCUM duration
> units.

and:

> For example, 1 year is not comparable to 1 'a' (i.e. results in null),
> but it is equivalent (~) to 1 'a'.

And similarly for all similar language in the comparison operators in
the CQL Reference:
[Greater](https://cql.hl7.org/09-b-cqlreference.html#greater),
[Greater Or Equal](https://cql.hl7.org/09-b-cqlreference.html#greater-or-equal),
[Less](https://cql.hl7.org/09-b-cqlreference.html#less),
[Less Or Equal](https://cql.hl7.org/09-b-cqlreference.html#less-or-equal).
The current paragraph:

> For time-valued quantities, the UCUM definite-quantity durations above
> days (and weeks) are not comparable to calendar durations. Definite-time
> duration unit conversions shall be performed as specified in ISO-8601,
> while calendar-time duration unit conversions shall be performed
> according to calendar duration semantics. In particular, unit
> conversion between variable length calendar durations (i.e. years and
> months) and definite-time durations (i.e. days or below) results in
> null.

should become:

> For time-valued quantities, the UCUM definite-quantity durations above
> days (and weeks) are not comparable to calendar durations. Definite-time
> duration unit conversions shall be performed as specified in ISO-8601,
> while calendar-time duration unit conversions shall be performed
> according to calendar duration semantics **and must be performed
> explicitly**. Unit conversion between variable length calendar
> durations (i.e. years and months) and definite-time durations (i.e.
> days or below) results in null.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt Bryn Rhodes's 2025-11-29 comment verbatim as the disposition of
FHIR-53172, updating the CQL (FHIR) specification sources as follows:

1. **Authors Guide §1.2.4.2.1 (Quantities)** — change
   "Durations above days (and weeks) are calendar durations that are not
   comparable with definite quantity UCUM duration units." to
   "Durations above days (and weeks) are calendar durations that are
   **not comparable (i.e. result in null)** with definite quantity UCUM
   duration units."
   And change "For example, 1 year is not equal (=) to 1 'a' …" to
   "For example, 1 year is **not comparable** to 1 'a' (i.e. results in
   null), but it is equivalent (~) to 1 'a'."
2. **CQL Reference §Greater, §Greater Or Equal, §Less, §Less Or Equal**
   — in each of the four comparison-operator pages, replace the
   "For time-valued quantities, …" paragraph with the wording Bryn
   proposes: add "**and must be performed explicitly**" to the sentence
   about calendar-time duration unit conversions, and restate that unit
   conversion between variable-length calendar durations (years, months)
   and definite-time durations (days or below) results in `null`.
3. Clarify, as a direct answer to the reporter's second question, that
   the ordered comparison operators (`<`, `<=`, `>`, `>=`) between a
   calendar-duration quantity and a UCUM definite-duration quantity
   above days/weeks return `null` **regardless of the operand values**
   (i.e. `1 year < 2 'a'` is `null`, not `true`), matching the behaviour
   of `=`/`!=` for those operand types.

#### Justification

This is the minimum set of edits that makes the CQL spec say what the
existing conformance test suite (cqframework/cql-tests PR #78) already
verifies, and what has been the de-facto FHIRPath behaviour since
FHIR-28144 in 2020. It aligns CQL (FHIR) with FHIR-52957 (already
*Applied* in FHIRPath) and with the in-flight disposition on FHIR-53250;
all three tickets end up expressing the same rule in the same words,
which is essential for the joint CDS/ITS/CQI reviewers who maintain
these specs in lock-step. Returning `null` for comparisons on
incomparable operands is also the native CQL convention for comparing
heterogeneous quantities and null-propagation generally (see
[Zulip 208868101](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/fhirpath/topic/Date%2FTime%20comparison%20vs%20equality/near/208095292)),
so the change does not introduce any new semantic — it simply makes the
normative prose describe the semantic that already exists.

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Apply the wording fixes from Disposition A **for equality/equivalence**
(`=`, `!=`, `~`, `!~`), but refine the treatment of ordered comparisons
(`<`, `<=`, `>`, `>=`) so that they return a Boolean whenever the
ordering is unambiguous regardless of calendar-vs-definite semantics,
and only return `null` when the ordering genuinely depends on the
calendar-conversion choice. Concretely:

- If the minimum possible value of the larger operand (under any
  defensible conversion, e.g. `365 d ≤ 1 year ≤ 366 d`) still exceeds
  the maximum possible value of the smaller operand, return `true`; if
  the inverse holds, return `false`; otherwise return `null`.
- Example: `1 year < 2 'a'` → `true` because even at the maximum (366 d)
  `1 year` is still less than `2 'a'` (730.5 d). `1 year < 1 'a'` →
  `null` because the two ranges overlap.
- Document this as a new sub-section "Ordered comparison of calendar
  and definite durations" in Authors Guide §1.2.4.2.1, and cross-link
  from the four CQL Reference comparison pages.

#### Justification

This directly answers the reporter's "since it obviously *is*"
intuition: for implementers and authors, it is counter-intuitive that
`1 year < 2 'a'` returns `null`, because no reasonable choice of "how
long is a year" would ever make it ≥ `2 'a'`. Interval-based reasoning
is already used elsewhere in CQL (e.g., uncertain date comparisons),
so the mental model is not foreign. The trade-off is implementation
cost — every CQL engine would need to compute interval bounds for every
calendar/UCUM comparison — and specification complexity. It also
diverges from the FHIRPath disposition on FHIR-53250, which would leave
CQL and FHIRPath saying different things about the same operator, which
the work groups have historically tried to avoid.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-53172 as **Not Persuasive**, leaving the existing "not
comparable" language in the Authors Guide and CQL Reference unchanged.
Rationale: the CQL tests repository is not normative, FHIR-53250 and
FHIR-52957 handle the same concern on the FHIRPath side, and
implementations that follow the prose example (`1 year != 1 'a'` →
`true`) are defensibly conformant. If confusion persists, a non-
normative note could be added pointing readers at the FHIRPath
resolution, but no substantive text change would be made to CQL.

#### Justification

The existing prose does not actually *require* `null`; it only requires
that implementations not pretend the two quantities are interchangeable.
An implementation that returns `false` for `1 year = 1 'a'` and `true`
for `1 year != 1 'a'` is arguably still honouring "not equal." Since
FHIR-52957 / FHIR-53250 are fixing the underlying terminology in
FHIRPath, the CQL spec — which inherits quantity semantics from
FHIRPath by reference in several places — will indirectly benefit
without a dedicated CQL change. This minimises churn in a spec that is
already in the middle of a ballot cycle.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A is the clean alignment play: the CQL reporter, the
FHIRPath reporter (Jing Tang), and the assignee (Bryn Rhodes) have all
already converged on identical wording; the conformance test suite
already behaves that way; and FHIR-52957 has shipped (Applied) in
FHIRPath with this exact rule. Disposition B is intellectually
appealing and does address the reporter's ordered-comparison question
more satisfyingly, but it creates an interval-comparison semantics that
diverges from FHIRPath and adds implementation cost for what is, in
practice, a rare pattern — CDS authors don't typically compare `year`
to `'a'` ordinally. Disposition C leaves the ambiguity in place and
relies on readers to trace their way to the FHIRPath resolution; given
that the CQL Authors Guide is the first document most new users read,
leaving it silent on this is a poor UX. CDS should adopt A, mirroring
the FHIRPath fix, and explicitly document the "ordered comparisons
also return null" answer to the reporter's follow-up question so the
workgroup doesn't have to come back to it.
