## Summary

The reporter notes that the terminology resource profiles published in the
Canonical Resource Management Infrastructure (CRMI) IG do not reflect the
decision made at the May 2025 WGM (joint TI / CDS-CQI Monday Q3 session,
recorded in Confluence page 325454669). That decision — captured in
[FHIR-37959](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-37959) — was that the
Shareable / Publishable / Computable / Executable profiles on CodeSystem,
ValueSet, ConceptMap and NamingSystem would be removed from the R6 core
specification and maintained going forward in CRMI, **but their canonical
URIs would remain unchanged from what they are in the core FHIR
specification** (i.e., `http://hl7.org/fhir/StructureDefinition/...`).
Today the CRMI profiles are still published with the `http://hl7.org/fhir/uv/crmi/StructureDefinition/crmi-...`
("CRMI-prefixed") URIs as their `official` URLs, which contradicts that
decision. The reporter requests that the terminology-related profile
URLs be updated to match the core-spec URLs as agreed.

A closely related ticket, [FHIR-52719](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-52719),
was opened by the same reporter on the same day against the same IG and
asks for the explanatory text under the artifact-capability-profiles
table to be updated to reflect the new arrangement (profiles stay in
CRMI but retain the base specification URLs). Together the two tickets
describe the metadata change (FHIR-52711) and the narrative change
(FHIR-52719) needed to align CRMI with the FHIR-37959 resolution.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the four terminology-resource capability profiles in the CRMI IG
so that their **canonical `url` element** matches the corresponding core
FHIR StructureDefinition URL, and not a CRMI-prefixed URL. Concretely,
for each of CodeSystem, ValueSet, ConceptMap, NamingSystem (and their
Shareable / Publishable / Computable / Executable variants where they
exist), change the profile's `StructureDefinition.url` from
`http://hl7.org/fhir/uv/crmi/StructureDefinition/crmi-<name>` to the
agreed `http://hl7.org/fhir/StructureDefinition/<corresponding-core-name>`
URL that was used when the profiles still lived in core FHIR. Keep the
profile artifacts physically in the CRMI IG (per FHIR-37959's resolved
home), but publish them under the core URLs. Also resolve
[FHIR-52719](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-52719) in tandem so that
the explanatory paragraph under the `profiles.html#artifact-capability-profiles`
table reflects the new arrangement. Defer the resolution of this
ticket until publication-tooling support exists (per Bryn Rhodes'
2025-09-15 comment) so the change can land in the STU2 publication
without breaking the IG build. Once tooling is available, this should
be applied in the next CRMI publication and an upgrade note should be
included describing the URL migration for any IG that currently claims
conformance via the CRMI-prefixed URLs.

#### Justification

This is exactly the resolution that the joint TI and CDS-CQI WGs agreed
to at the May 2025 WGM, Monday Q3 (Confluence 325454669), and that is
captured in [FHIR-37959](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-37959)'s
resolution: the profiles move/remain in CRMI, **but** their URIs stay
unchanged from the core spec. The current state — CRMI publishing them
under `crmi-` URIs — is internally inconsistent with that agreement and
the ticket has already been marked Resolution: Persuasive and Priority:
Highest by the WG. The "Persuasive" decision is already on record; only
the publication-tooling dependency is preventing application. Aligning
the URIs is also what makes the FHIR-37959 deal coherent for
implementers: the value of leaving the URIs alone is that R5
implementers consuming the core URLs and R6+/CRMI implementers
consuming the same URLs all conform to the same artefact identity.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Publish each terminology capability profile under **both** URLs for one
publication cycle: keep the existing
`http://hl7.org/fhir/uv/crmi/StructureDefinition/crmi-shareablevalueset`
profile in place but mark it `status = retired` (or `deprecated`) with
a `replacedBy` extension pointing at a new sibling profile whose `url`
is the core-spec URL agreed in FHIR-37959. The new core-URL profile
becomes the conformance target going forward; the CRMI-URL profile
remains resolvable for one cycle to give downstream IGs (e.g. CQF
Measures, AU Base, US Core terminology bindings, and the work in
[FHIR-44642](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-44642)) a window to
re-point their `meta.profile` claims. Update FHIR-52719's narrative to
explain the dual-publish window. Drop the deprecated CRMI-URL profiles
in the publication after STU2.

#### Justification

The straight URL swap in Disposition A is correct in principle, but
several downstream artefacts already declare conformance to the
CRMI-prefixed URLs (FHIR-44642 explicitly refactored QM IG to do so,
and the Zulip "Artifact owners in IGs" thread shows the CRMI-prefixed
profiles are actively in use in the build pipeline). A dual-publish /
deprecation window costs one extra publication cycle but gives
implementers a non-breaking migration path, which is consistent with
how FHIR usually retires canonical URLs. It also defangs the
publication-tooling blocker mentioned in Bryn Rhodes' comment, because
adding sibling profiles is already supported by the IG Publisher today
— only the eventual removal of the CRMI-URL profiles needs the cleaner
tooling story.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline to change the CRMI profile URLs. Keep the existing
`http://hl7.org/fhir/uv/crmi/StructureDefinition/crmi-*` canonical URLs
as the official identifiers for the terminology capability profiles in
CRMI and instead update the narrative under
`profiles.html#artifact-capability-profiles` (i.e. the FHIR-52719
change) to explain that, although the profiles originated in core FHIR,
their canonical home in CRMI is the `crmi-` URL space. Refer
implementers to that narrative for the rationale.

#### Justification

This option is defensible only if one considers the "URLs unchanged"
half of the FHIR-37959 resolution to have been overtaken by events:
CRMI has been publishing under `crmi-*` URLs long enough that
downstream IGs (FHIR-44642 in QM IG, AU Base, etc.) have built
conformance and tooling around those URLs, and changing them now
imposes migration cost on every consumer for what is, on the wire, an
identifier-only change. Declining preserves the published URLs, avoids
breaking existing `meta.profile` claims, and side-steps the
publication-tooling dependency that has held the ticket up since
September 2025.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B (Alternative Approach — dual-publish
with a deprecation window)

Disposition B preserves the substance of what FHIR-52711 and
FHIR-37959 agreed to (the canonical identifiers for these profiles
should be the core-spec URLs, not CRMI-prefixed ones) while honouring
the operational reality that several IGs and tooling pipelines already
consume the CRMI-prefixed URLs in production. Disposition A is the
"clean" answer and matches the literal text of the WGM resolution, but
applying it as a single hard cut is exactly what Bryn Rhodes flagged
when he held the ticket in September 2025 — it needs publisher support
and a coordinated cutover, and it will silently invalidate
`meta.profile` claims in downstream IGs the moment it ships. The
deprecation/dual-publish path lets the workgroup ship the agreed URI
identity in STU2 (closing FHIR-52711) without forcing a flag day on
implementers. Disposition C should be rejected: it would re-open the
FHIR-37959 decision, which was made jointly with TI and CDS/CQI at the
May 2025 WGM, and the ticket has already been marked Persuasive. The
workgroup should adopt B unless it has an explicit signal from
publishers/implementers (CQF Measures, AU, US Core terminology) that a
hard cut in STU2 is preferable.
