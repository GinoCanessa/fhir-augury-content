## Summary

Bryn Rhodes (CDS WG / CQL editor) is requesting an editorial clarification
to the **Formatting Conventions** appendix of the Clinical Quality Language
(CQL) specification. Two specific issues are called out:

1. The convention for `#` should be clarified to make explicit that **any
   number of digits (plural)** may appear at that location, not just a
   single digit.
2. A **new convention should be added** for angle brackets `< >`, used to
   indicate that some specific (parameterised / placeholder) text is
   included at that location — the example given is `<unit>` to indicate
   that the unit of a quantity is specified.

No related Jira tickets, GitHub items, or substantive Zulip discussions
were found via the FhirAugury indexes. The cross-reference search
returned zero hits, and a content search for the ticket key returned no
hits. The only loosely-related Zulip pointer is a 2019 message from the
reporter that simply links to the same Formatting Conventions appendix
the ticket targets; it does not discuss the conventions themselves.

The change is editorial in nature — it does not alter CQL grammar,
semantics, or any normative behaviour — and is scoped to the informative
formatting-conventions appendix used throughout the CQL specification's
syntax presentation.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply both clarifications, as written, to the CQL specification's
informative **Formatting Conventions** appendix
(https://cql.hl7.org/14-g-formattingconventions.html):

1. Update the `#` entry so the description explicitly reads
   "Any digit(s) may appear at this location" (current single-digit
   wording is the source of confusion). Keep an example such as `#.#`
   already in use to anchor the meaning.
2. Add a new entry for `< >` immediately after the existing notation
   entries:

   > `< >` — Used to indicate that specific text is included at this
   > location. For example, `<unit>` indicates that the unit of a
   > quantity is specified at that position.

Both changes are informative-only; no grammar production, normative
statement, or executable semantic in CQL changes. The change should land
in the next minor/editorial publication of the CQL spec on
`cql.hl7.org`, with the same edit cherry-picked into any in-flight
ballot/CI build branch as appropriate.

#### Justification

The request is editorial and clearly scoped: it sharpens the meaning of
a notation already used throughout the specification (`#`) and
documents a notation (`< >`) that the spec already uses informally
(e.g., the `<unit>` style is recognisable to anyone who has read the
quantity / units sections). Documenting existing practice and removing
ambiguity in the conventions appendix improves readability for every
downstream reader of the CQL spec without any risk of breaking parsers,
implementations, or conformant CQL libraries. Coming from the CQL
specification editor (Bryn Rhodes) further reduces the risk that the
proposed wording mis-describes intended editorial practice.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the substance of both clarifications, but tighten the wording so
the conventions table is internally consistent and unambiguous:

1. For `#`, replace the entry with a wording that is symmetric with
   other "repetition" notations the spec already uses, e.g.:

   > `#` — A digit. May be repeated; `#.#`, `##:##`, etc. denote one or
   > more digits at each `#` position.

   This preserves the original single-character convention (one `#` =
   one digit) but explicitly says repetition is allowed at each
   occurrence, rather than overloading the meaning of a single `#` to
   sometimes mean "one digit" and sometimes "any number of digits".

2. For `< >`, define it as a **metavariable / placeholder** notation
   and explicitly distinguish it from literal angle brackets that may
   appear in CQL string content or in embedded XML/HTML examples:

   > `<name>` — A metavariable / placeholder; `name` describes the kind
   > of text that appears at this location in concrete CQL. Angle
   > brackets are not part of the CQL syntax themselves. Example:
   > `<unit>` indicates that the unit of a quantity (e.g., `'mg'`) is
   > specified at that position.

#### Justification

The literal text proposed in the ticket — "Any digits (plural) may
appear at this location" — is grammatically awkward and arguably
contradicts the existing use of `#` in patterns like `#.#`, where each
`#` is conventionally a single-digit slot and repetition is implied by
writing the symbol multiple times. Disposition B keeps the editorial
intent (allow `#` to mean "digits", clarify `< >` as placeholder
notation) while avoiding a silent change in how readers parse existing
example patterns and pre-empting confusion between "placeholder" angle
brackets and any literal `<`/`>` characters that show up elsewhere in
CQL examples (string content, HTML in narrative, embedded XML). It is
the same scope of edit as Disposition A, with no additional cost, and
yields a more durable conventions appendix.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket "Persuasive — No Change" / "Not Persuasive" on the
grounds that the Formatting Conventions appendix is already informative
and has been understood in practice by CQL implementers and editors for
multiple cycles without these clarifications, and that adding a `< >`
placeholder convention risks colliding with existing uses of angle
brackets in CQL examples (string content, embedded XML, HTML in
narrative) without a corresponding normative benefit.

#### Justification

Declining is defensible only if the WG concludes that (a) no
implementer has actually been confused by the current `#` wording, and
(b) the angle-bracket convention is better left to inline prose at the
point of use (e.g., "where `<unit>` denotes the unit of a quantity")
rather than introduced as a global notation. The bar for declining a
purely editorial request from the specification's own editor is high,
and the current wording does not point to any concrete harm or
implementer confusion that would be caused by accepting it. Including
this disposition primarily to give the WG a complete option set.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the right resolution: it accepts the clear editorial
intent of the ticket — both items genuinely improve the conventions
appendix — but improves the wording so the change does not silently
re-interpret the existing `#`-repetition pattern and so the new `< >`
notation is unambiguously defined as a placeholder (not a literal angle
bracket that might appear in CQL string content or embedded XML). The
edit is no larger or riskier than Disposition A, costs the editor
nothing extra to land alongside the rest of the editorial pass, and
produces a conventions appendix that is more durable for future readers.
Disposition A is an acceptable fallback if the WG prefers to land the
ticket exactly as written; Disposition C should only be selected if the
WG affirmatively concludes the current wording has caused no confusion
in practice.
