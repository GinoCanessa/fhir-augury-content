## Summary

The reporter, Lynn Perrine, is asking for clarification about the intent of a
table that appears in the Clinical Quality Language (FHIR) Implementation
Guide. Specifically, she wants to know whether the table is meant to
enumerate the IG's *current* co-chairs or the co-chairs who were in place at
the time the IG was originally published. The reporter notes that, if the
table is intended to reflect the current co-chair roster, it is inaccurate
and would need to be corrected. No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip
discussions, or GitHub items were located that elaborate on the request, so
the work group will need to make an explicit decision on the table's
intent and then either correct the content or clarify the caption/heading
accordingly.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat the table as the *current* co-chair roster for the Clinical Decision
Support work group on the CQL on FHIR IG, and update its contents to
reflect the present co-chairs. Concretely, edit the IG narrative page
that renders the co-chair table (typically the IG home / index narrative
in the Clinical Quality Language (FHIR) IG source) so that the listed
names, roles, and contact details match the current CDS work group
roster published on confluence.hl7.org. While doing so, add a short
sentence above the table making the intent explicit ("The following are
the current co-chairs of the Clinical Decision Support work group, which
maintains this implementation guide.") so that future readers do not
have to re-derive the intent.

#### Justification

The ticket is essentially a defect report: the reporter is observing
that, under the most natural reading ("current co-chairs"), the data is
wrong. FHIR IGs generally surface "current" governance information
because the IG is a living artifact that is republished and re-balloted
over time; freezing co-chair lists to publication time would diverge
from how every other HL7 IG presents work-group ownership. Correcting
the names and adding an explicit caption is the lowest-risk fix and
directly addresses the reporter's concern.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than maintaining a hand-edited co-chair table inside the IG,
replace the table with a single sentence and a link to the authoritative
source — for example: "This implementation guide is maintained by the
[Clinical Decision Support Work Group](https://confluence.hl7.org/display/CDS),
whose current co-chairs are listed on the work-group page." If the IG
tooling supports it, drive the displayed name(s) from
`ImplementationGuide.contact` (or the equivalent publisher metadata)
rather than free-text narrative, so that there is exactly one place in
the source where co-chair information lives.

#### Justification

Co-chair rosters change on a roughly biennial cadence via HL7 elections,
but IG publications are infrequent and often lag by months. Embedding
a roster directly in narrative guarantees that the IG will drift out of
date again — exactly the situation that triggered this ticket. Pointing
at the work-group page (or driving from `ImplementationGuide.contact`)
removes the dual-source-of-truth problem permanently and is consistent
with how several other HL7 IGs already attribute ownership. The
trade-off vs. Disposition A is one extra click for the reader in
exchange for eliminating the recurring maintenance burden.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as "Not Persuasive" on the grounds that the table is
intentionally a *historical* record of the co-chairs at the time of
original publication and is therefore not inaccurate. Add a short
caption ("Co-chairs at time of original publication") to make that
intent explicit so that future readers do not raise the same question,
but make no change to the listed names.

#### Justification

This disposition is defensible only if the work group genuinely intended
the table to be a historical artifact (e.g., for citation or
attribution purposes). In that case the reporter's premise — that the
list is wrong — is itself wrong, and the right response is a
clarification rather than a data change. Declining is also the lowest
maintenance cost: no recurring obligation to keep the IG narrative in
sync with election results.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B addresses the underlying problem rather than the immediate
symptom. The ticket exists because the IG narrative duplicates
information that is authoritatively maintained elsewhere (the HL7 work
group page and/or `ImplementationGuide.contact`); any "fix the names"
solution (Disposition A) will simply re-create the same defect after the
next CDS election cycle, and a "declare it historical" solution
(Disposition C) is unlikely to match reader expectations on a living,
re-balloted IG. Replacing the table with a link to the authoritative
work-group roster — and, where tooling allows, driving the displayed
contact from `ImplementationGuide.contact` — eliminates the drift, gives
the reporter a correct answer, and follows the same pattern other
HL7 IGs are converging on. If the work group prefers a smaller change
in the short term, falling back to Disposition A (correct the names
*and* add an explicit "current co-chairs" caption) is acceptable, but
the ticket should not be declined: the reporter's observation that the
data is misleading under its most natural reading is correct.
