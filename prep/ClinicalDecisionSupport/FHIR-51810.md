## Summary

The ticket asks the Clinical Guidelines (CPG) IG to add a new `cpg-pertinenceRating` extension (integer, 0–10, cardinality 0..1) that may be applied to any CPG instance resource (i.e., requests/events, but not definitional resources). The rating expresses the *weight* of a pertinence relationship, and may be used either alongside the existing `cpg-pertinence` extension (which carries the *direction* — positive, negative, pathognomonic) or on its own. The ticket also asks for a new `cpg-pertinentTo` extension (Reference, 0..1) that complements the existing `cpg-pertinent` extension by expressing the *target* side of a pertinence relationship, so that the relationship can be modeled from either end (since the data on one side may be outside the author's control). Both `cpg-pertinence` and `cpg-pertinenceRating` should also be allowed on `Reference` so they can be applied directly to the `cpg-pertinent` / `cpg-pertinentTo` reference itself. The reporter notes that negative rating values may be permitted in a future revision.

The Clinical Decision Support work group has resolved the issue **Persuasive with Modification**: the agreed change additionally introduces `instantiatesCaseFeature`, `caseFeatureType`, and `caseFeaturePertinence` extensions on all CPG Request profiles (CPGCommunicationRequest, CPGMedicationRequest, …), and ensures `cpg-pertinenceRating`, `cpg-pertinentTo`, and the related extensions are also usable on the `CPGCaseSummary` and `CPGCasePlanSummary` profiles. No explicit Jira links exist, but the request builds directly on prior CPG work captured in [FHIR-23955](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-23955) (introduced the notion of pertinence on feature definitions), [FHIR-37826](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-37826) (added a parallel `cqfm-pertinence` extension to the US Quality Measures IG re-using the `cpg-caseFeaturePertinence` model), [FHIR-48634](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48634) (added a CPG `rating` extension for recommendations, an analogous mechanism), and [FHIR-24026](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-24026) (the original "Pertinent Information" recommendation work).

## Details

**Description:**

> Add a cpg-pertinenceRating extension, 0..1 on any CPG instance resource (i.e. it cannot be applied to definitional resources). The extension is integer, 0 through 10.
>
> cpg-pertinenceRating can be applied alongside cpg-pertinence or by itself.
>
> If applied alongside cpg-pertinence, cpg-pertinenceRating establishes of the weight of the pertinence relationship.
>
> cpg-pertinence is the direction of relevance, positive, negative, or pathognomonic, for establishing a higher-level clinical finding.
>
> cpg-pertinenceRating is the weight given to facts for inferring a higher-level clinical finding. For example, signs, symptoms, lab values, etc., all have levels of pertinence relative to the finding of a condition or physiological state.
>
> Note that we may extend the value to allow negative values at a later time.
>
> Also add a cpg-pertinentTo extension, 0..1 on any CPG instance resource, that allows the target of a pertinence relationship to be established. The extension is Reference, and can be a reference to any of the CPG resources.
>
> The existing cpg-pertinent extension is used to reference the pertinent information supporting a higher-level clinical finding.
>
> Extension are defined to support establishing the relationship in both directions since there are use cases for describing things both ways, especially given that the data you may want to indicate pertinence of/for is outside your control.
>
> Allow pertinence and pertinenceRating on Reference so that they can be applied to the pertinent or pertinentTo reference.

**Resolution Description (Persuasive with Modification):**

> Add `instantiatesCaseFeature`, `caseFeatureType`, and `caseFeaturePertinence` extensions to all the Request profiles (CPGCommunicationRequest, CPGMedicationRequest, …).
>
> Add a `cpg-pertinenceRating` extension, 0..1 on any CPG instance resource (requests and events) (i.e., it cannot be applied to definitional resources). The extension is integer, 0 through 10.
>
> `cpg-pertinenceRating` can be applied alongside `cpg-pertinence` or by itself; together they express weight + direction of the pertinence relationship.
>
> Add a `cpg-pertinentTo` extension, 0..1 on any CPG instance resource, that allows the target of a pertinence relationship to be established (Reference to any CPG resource). The existing `cpg-pertinent` extension references the pertinent information supporting a higher-level clinical finding.
>
> Allow `pertinence` and `pertinenceRating` on `Reference` so that they can be applied directly to the `pertinent` / `pertinentTo` reference.
>
> Also ensure that these same extensions can be used on the `CPGCaseSummary` and `CPGCasePlanSummary` profiles.

**Comments:**

- **bryn.rhodes (2025-08-20)** — Clarifies that what was wanted on the Pertinence extension is not only directionality (uni- vs bi-directional) but also which entity "owns" the relationship. Notes that (1) any CPG instance resource can carry 0..* pertinence extensions pointing to other CPG instance resources; (2) each pertinence extension is unidirectional ("pertinent with respect to"); (3) because the extension can sit on either side of the relationship, source and target can effectively flip polarity. Gives two patterns: the current Sign/Symptom-with-pertinence-→-Condition pattern, and an alternate (e.g., for CPSV) where a Condition carries N pertinence extensions, each referencing a Sign/Symptom.
- **bryn.rhodes (2025-08-15)** — Provides a more detailed clinical definition of *pertinent*: in medicine, "pertinent" means relevant, clinically meaningful, and directly related to a patient's condition, diagnosis, higher-level finding, etc. — i.e., information that is important for understanding and managing a medical situation.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the Clinical Guidelines (CPG) IG, define three new extension StructureDefinitions and one Reference allowance:

1. `cpg-pertinenceRating` — `Extension`, context = any CPG **instance** resource (requests + events; explicitly *not* definitional resources); cardinality 0..1; `value[x]` = `integer` constrained to `0..10`. Definition: weight of the pertinence relationship; usable alongside `cpg-pertinence` or standalone.
2. `cpg-pertinentTo` — `Extension`, context = any CPG instance resource; cardinality 0..1; `value[x]` = `Reference(Any CPG resource)`. Definition: target side of a pertinence relationship (mirror of the existing `cpg-pertinent`).
3. Update the existing `cpg-pertinence` extension's context list to also include `Reference`, and allow the new `cpg-pertinenceRating` extension's context to include `Reference`, so that both can be attached to the `cpg-pertinent`/`cpg-pertinentTo` reference itself rather than to the resource.
4. Document the bidirectional "ownership" pattern called out in Bryn's 2025-08-20 comment in the IG narrative (likely on the existing pertinence page), with worked examples of the Sign/Symptom-→-Condition and Condition-→-Sign/Symptom directions.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the ticket text asks for and is a natural, additive extension of the established CPG pertinence model defined in [FHIR-23955](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-23955) and the `cpg-caseFeaturePertinence` extension. Splitting *direction* (`cpg-pertinence`) from *weight* (`cpg-pertinenceRating`) keeps each extension single-purpose, which is the pattern the CPG IG already uses elsewhere, and matches how [FHIR-37826](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-37826) re-used the same model in the US Quality Measures IG. Restricting `value[x]` to `integer 0..10` (with a documented future allowance for negatives) gives implementers a stable scale today without locking out the negative-weight use cases the reporter anticipates. Allowing the extensions on `Reference` is consistent with how other CPG annotation-style extensions (e.g., `cpg-rating` from [FHIR-48634](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48634)) are scoped, and is necessary to support the "annotate the link, not the resource" use cases. Because the ticket explicitly excludes definitional resources, no incompatibility with `PlanDefinition`/`ActivityDefinition` is introduced.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach (Persuasive with Modification — matches the WG resolution)

#### Proposal

Adopt the modified resolution already captured on the ticket, namely:

1. Everything in Disposition A, **plus**
2. Add `instantiatesCaseFeature`, `caseFeatureType`, and `caseFeaturePertinence` extensions on every CPG **Request** profile (CPGCommunicationRequest, CPGMedicationRequest, CPGServiceRequest, CPGTaskRequest, CPGImmunizationRequest, etc.), so a request resource can declare the case feature it instantiates, the feature's type, and the pertinence of that feature in one consistent place.
3. Explicitly allow `cpg-pertinenceRating`, `cpg-pertinentTo`, `cpg-pertinence`, and `cpg-pertinent` on the `CPGCaseSummary` and `CPGCasePlanSummary` profiles (in addition to the generic "any CPG instance resource" context), so that summary-level resources can carry the same relationships.
4. Keep the integer 0..10 scale for `cpg-pertinenceRating` for this publication and call out the future negative-value extension as an open issue.

#### Justification

This is the change actually agreed to during workgroup discussion (it is recorded as the ticket's Resolution Description) and is therefore the disposition with the strongest signal of community consensus. It addresses the gap implicitly created by Disposition A: without explicit support on the Request profiles and on `CPGCaseSummary`/`CPGCasePlanSummary`, implementers would either have to add the extensions ad hoc or fall back on `cpg-caseFeaturePertinence` only, which conflates direction and case-feature identity. Pulling the case-feature linkage onto the request profiles (`instantiatesCaseFeature` + `caseFeatureType` + `caseFeaturePertinence`) reuses the existing `cpg-caseFeaturePertinence` semantics introduced in [FHIR-23955](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-23955) and consumed by [FHIR-37826](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-37826), keeping the IG internally consistent. The relationship to `cpg-rating` from [FHIR-48634](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48634) is intentionally kept distinct: `cpg-rating` scores a recommendation as a whole, whereas `cpg-pertinenceRating` weights an individual pertinence link — conflating them would lose information.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the new `cpg-pertinenceRating` and `cpg-pertinentTo` extensions and the broader case-feature changes. Recommend that implementations needing a quantitative weight reuse the existing [`cpg-rating`](https://hl7.org/fhir/uv/cpg/) extension (FHIR-48634) on the resource carrying `cpg-pertinence`, and that implementations needing the reverse-direction "pertinent to" relationship author it from the other side using the existing `cpg-pertinent` extension on the target resource.

#### Justification

The CPG IG already provides (a) `cpg-pertinence` for direction, (b) `cpg-pertinent` for one-sided association, and (c) `cpg-rating` for a numeric score on definitional/request resources. A reviewer could argue that the new `cpg-pertinenceRating` is functionally a `cpg-rating` applied in a pertinence context, and that `cpg-pertinentTo` is just `cpg-pertinent` viewed from the other end. Declining avoids growing the extension surface area and the associated implementation/testing burden, and it sidesteps the modeling question of whether weight semantics on a relationship should be scaled identically to weight semantics on a recommendation. This is the most defensible path *only* if the WG concludes that the existing combination is sufficient in practice.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The ticket has already been resolved **Persuasive with Modification** and is labeled **Ready-For-Vote**, which means the work group has converged on the substance of Disposition B. Disposition A is essentially the same change minus the `instantiatesCaseFeature` / `caseFeatureType` / `caseFeaturePertinence` work on the Request profiles and the explicit allowance on `CPGCaseSummary`/`CPGCasePlanSummary`; without those additions, implementers would shortly run into the very gap the modification was introduced to close. Disposition C is hard to defend: `cpg-rating` and `cpg-pertinenceRating` carry meaningfully different semantics (overall recommendation score vs. weight of a single pertinence link), and asking authors to model "pertinent to" by going to the other resource is brittle when, as Bryn notes, the data on that side may be outside the author's control. Recommending B preserves the work-group's agreement, keeps the CPG model internally consistent with the precedents in [FHIR-23955](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-23955), [FHIR-37826](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-37826), and [FHIR-48634](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48634), and gives implementers a single, coherent way to annotate both individual relationships and case-summary-level structures. The future negative-value question on `cpg-pertinenceRating` should be carried as a separate ticket against the next CPG cycle.
