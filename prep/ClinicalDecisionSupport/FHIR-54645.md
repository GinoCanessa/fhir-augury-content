## Summary

Celine Lefebvre asks the CDS Work Group to strengthen the conformance
language describing `overrideReasons` `Coding.display` in the CDS Hooks
specification. Today the spec says CDS Services **MUST** provide a
human-readable `display` and CDS Clients **MAY** incorporate it into the
UI. The reporter argues that if a CDS Client is going to surface
override reasons at all, the human-readable text is not optional — the
clinician needs to see something legible to pick a reason — so the
client-side conformance should be raised. The ticket proposes two
edits to the same sentence in §"Reasons for rejecting a card":
service-side `MUST` → `SHALL`, and client-side `MAY` → `SHOULD`.

override-reason mechanics is the 2024 thread *"Capturing and

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Edit `cds-hooks/docs` → `docs/specification/current.md`, line 633,
§"Reasons for rejecting a card", replacing the existing sentence:

> When using the coding object to represent a reason, CDS Services
> **MUST** provide a human readable text in the *display* property
> and CDS Clients **MAY** incorporate it into their user interface.

with:

> When using the coding object to represent a reason, CDS Services
> **SHALL** provide a human readable text in the *display* property
> and CDS Clients **SHOULD** incorporate it into their user
> interface.

No example JSON, schema, or other artifact in the cached
`cds-hooks/docs` clone needs to change to support the substitution.

#### Justification

The reporter's substantive point is hard to argue with: if a CDS
Client is going to present override reasons at all, the
human-readable `display` is the only thing the clinician can act on
— a bare `code`+`system` is not a usable selection target.
Promoting the client side from `MAY` to `SHOULD` matches that
clinical reality without forcing every client to render override
reasons. Promoting the service side from `MUST` to `SHALL` keeps the
strength of the obligation but aligns it with HL7's house keyword
when the ticket is filed under the "CDS Hooks (FHIR)" family — a
small consistency win if the WG wants CDS Hooks to read more like
FHIR-family specifications.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Address the reporter's underlying clinical concern (the `display`
must be usable when shown) without changing the keyword vocabulary.
Keep `MUST` and `MAY`, but tighten the `MAY` clause so the
optionality is about *whether* clients render override reasons at
all, not about whether they may show them as opaque codes:

> When using the coding object to represent a reason, CDS Services
> **MUST** provide a human readable text in the *display* property.
> CDS Clients **MAY** choose whether to surface `overrideReasons`
> to end users; CDS Clients that do surface them **SHOULD** present
> the `display` text rather than the raw `code`.

This preserves CDS Hooks' historical RFC 2119 vocabulary
(`MUST`/`SHOULD`/`MAY`, no `SHALL`) and avoids opening a
spec-wide conversion question. It also makes the conditional nature
of the obligation explicit, which is what the reporter is really
getting at.

#### Justification

CDS Hooks `current.md` is written in RFC 2119 style throughout, and
introducing a single `SHALL` would be a stylistic outlier unless the
WG agrees to a sweep of the whole spec — a much larger change than
this ticket scoped. The conditional `SHOULD` ("CDS Clients that do
surface them SHOULD present the `display`") delivers the reporter's
clinical guarantee (no opaque codes shown to physicians) while
leaving room for clients that legitimately do not expose override
reasons (back-office logging, headless integrations, automated
agents). It also keeps the change reviewable as a single-paragraph
edit without re-litigating which conformance vocabulary CDS Hooks
should use.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change as written and close the ticket "Not Persuasive".
Rationale: (1) the existing `MUST` already gives the reporter the
guarantee they actually want — that any `display` a service emits is
human readable; (2) raising the client-side keyword from `MAY` to
`SHOULD` reaches into UI policy that CDS Hooks has historically left
to the EHR; and (3) introducing `SHALL` mixes conformance
vocabularies in a document that uses RFC 2119 elsewhere.

#### Justification

CDS Hooks has consistently scoped itself to the wire contract
between CDS Service and CDS Client and avoided prescribing what the
EHR puts on screen. `overrideReasons` is itself an optional element
on a card; a normative `SHOULD` on the client side would oblige
clients to render something the spec already says they don't have
to honor at all, which is internally awkward. The keyword question
is also non-trivial: switching one occurrence of `MUST` to `SHALL`
sets a precedent the WG would have to either follow or explain
across the rest of the document.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **B (Alternative Approach).**

The reporter's clinical instinct is correct — once override reasons
are shown, they have to be readable — and Disposition B captures
that with a conditional `SHOULD` while respecting two facts the
literal request glosses over: CDS Hooks uses RFC 2119 (not HL7's
`SHALL`) throughout `current.md`, and `overrideReasons` is itself
optional on a card, so an unconditional client-side `SHOULD` overreaches.
Disposition A is defensible and is the smallest possible edit, but it
quietly imports `SHALL` into an otherwise RFC-2119 document and
should not be merged without the WG explicitly choosing to converge
CDS Hooks' keyword vocabulary with FHIR core's. Disposition C
under-serves the reporter; the underlying gap (a client could
technically show raw `code`s) is real and worth closing, just not
with the exact two-word edit proposed. Before merging any of these,
re-run `repo-analysis cds-hooks/docs` so the WG has a current
briefing — including any implementation/sandbox touch points — to
weigh against the change.
