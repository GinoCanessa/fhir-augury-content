## Summary

Bryn Rhodes is requesting that the Clinical Quality Language (CQL)
specification add a best-practice note to its existing discussion of
precision-based timing comparison. The current text describes the
mechanics of how precision works in date/time comparisons but does not
guide authors toward common, safe patterns. The ticket proposes new
prose that (a) warns about the implicit `Date` → `DateTime` conversion
(which fills missing components with null and can yield unexpected
partial-comparison results that callers commonly read as "false"), (b)
recommends `day of` precision when comparing a `Date` to a `DateTime`
or when comparing events against a measurement-period boundary where
date precision is clinically appropriate, (c) recommends `minute of`
or `second of` when sub-day precision is clinically meaningful, and
(d) cross-references the new "default comparison precision" capability
introduced in CQL 2.0 as the language-level mechanism that supports
these practices.

There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub
items recorded in the FhirAugury index for FHIR-53145, so the request
is being considered on its own merits.

## Details

**Description:**

> The current discussion describes how precision-based timing
> comparison works, but there are some best practices that we should
> document as part of the discussion:
>
> Note that comparing Date and DateTime values results in implicit
> conversion of the Date to a DateTime with null components, and this
> can lead to unexpected partial comparison cases. Be explicit about
> precision when comparing Date and DateTime, as comparing with
> mismatched precision can yield null results (which are often
> interpreted as false). In most cases, using 'day of' precision is
> appropriate when comparing a Date and DateTime.
>
> Also recommend using day of when comparing between events within the
> measurement period and date precision is clinically appropriate
> (e.g. comparing medication order dates or procedure dates against
> encounter periods)
>
> Also recommend using minute of or second of when comparing between
> events where time values are clinically appropriate.
>
> In addition, reference the new "default comparison precision"
> capability of 2.0 as a way to facilitate these best practices.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a new "Best Practices" subsection to the precision-based timing
comparison discussion in the CQL (FHIR) specification. The subsection
should incorporate, essentially as written by the reporter:

1. A warning that comparing a `Date` to a `DateTime` triggers an
   implicit promotion of the `Date` to a `DateTime` with null
   time/sub-day components, that comparisons against null components
   propagate null, and that null is commonly (but incorrectly) treated
   as `false` by downstream logic. Authors should make precision
   explicit (e.g., `same day as`, `same or after day of`) rather than
   relying on the default comparison.
2. A recommendation to use `day of` precision when comparing events
   against the measurement period or against other events where date
   precision is clinically sufficient — with the medication-order /
   procedure-date vs. encounter-period example called out.
3. A recommendation to use `minute of` or `second of` when sub-day
   timing is clinically meaningful (e.g., medication administration
   timing within an encounter, sequential events within a procedure).
4. A pointer to the CQL 2.0 "default comparison precision" capability
   as the language-level mechanism that lets a library author set a
   library-wide default so individual comparisons don't have to repeat
   the precision qualifier.

The change is purely additive narrative in the relevant CQL chapter
(no grammar, semantics, or normative-conformance changes), and is
applied to the FHIR-published rendering of the CQL specification owned
by CDS.

#### Justification

This is exactly the gap the reporter — the CQL editor — has
identified, and the proposed text aligns with long-standing community
guidance: the null-propagation behavior of mixed-precision comparisons
is one of the most common authoring pitfalls in eCQM and CDS Hooks
content, and quality measure developers already use `day of` /
`X day of Y` patterns as a matter of course. Documenting the pattern
in the spec, and tying it to the CQL 2.0 default-comparison-precision
feature, gives implementers a single canonical reference rather than
relying on tribal knowledge passed through measure-development
bootcamps. Because the change is non-normative narrative, the risk
profile is low.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the substance of the request, but split the content across two
locations and tighten the cross-references:

- Keep a short, in-place note in the precision-based timing comparison
  section that flags the `Date` → `DateTime` implicit-promotion /
  null-propagation pitfall and links onward.
- Move the longer "use `day of` for measurement-period boundaries,
  use `minute of` / `second of` when sub-day precision is clinically
  meaningful" guidance into a dedicated **Authoring Best Practices**
  appendix (or "Authoring Guidance" section) of the CQL FHIR
  specification, alongside (or expanding) any existing best-practices
  material.
- In both locations, link to the CQL 2.0 default-comparison-precision
  feature description so that a reader who lands on either page can
  follow the trail to the language-level mechanism.

If a best-practices appendix does not yet exist in the CQL FHIR spec,
this disposition would also create that container so future
authoring-guidance tickets have a natural home.

#### Justification

The reporter's text mixes two kinds of guidance: (1) a *semantics
warning* about how the language actually behaves when precisions
mismatch (which belongs next to the precision-comparison rules), and
(2) *content-authoring recommendations* about which precision to pick
for which clinical scenario (which is closer to a style guide than a
language description). Separating them makes each easier to find from
its natural entry point: implementers reading the precision rules see
the trap, and measure authors browsing best practices see the recipes.
It also avoids bloating the precision-comparison section with
clinical-domain examples that may distract a reader trying to
understand the underlying semantics.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the ticket on the basis that the request is satisfied by the
combination of (a) the existing precision-based timing comparison
discussion, which already describes the mechanics, and (b) the new
"default comparison precision" capability in CQL 2.0, which gives
authors a one-line way to opt into the desired behavior library-wide.
Resolve as "Not Persuasive — already addressed" and instead encourage
the underlying guidance to live in measure-development implementation
guides (e.g., the QM-IG, eCQM authoring patterns) where clinical
examples are already curated.

#### Justification

The CQL specification is the language definition; clinical authoring
patterns ("use day-of when comparing medication-order dates against
encounter periods") are arguably out of scope for the language spec
and properly belong in the implementation guides that consume CQL.
Adding scenario-specific best practices to the language spec sets a
precedent that invites further domain-flavored prose and risks
diluting the spec's role as the normative language reference.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter is the CQL editor, the request is narrow and additive
(no normative change, no grammar change, no breaking change), and the
pitfall it documents — null propagation from mixed-precision `Date` /
`DateTime` comparisons being read as `false` — is one of the most
frequently re-discovered CQL gotchas in real measure work. Disposition
C undersells the cost of leaving this undocumented in the language
spec: implementers don't always read the downstream IGs, and the
current text describes *how* precision comparison works but not *what
to do about it*, so the trap keeps catching new authors. Disposition
B is reasonable and the workgroup may well want to do it that way if
an authoring-best-practices section already exists or is planned, but
it adds editorial scope (creating/structuring a new appendix) that
isn't necessary to land the fix the ticket actually asks for. Accept
as requested, with the editor free to choose placement and to call
out the CQL 2.0 default-comparison-precision feature as the
language-level enabler.
