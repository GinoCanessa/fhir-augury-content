## Proposed Dispositions

*Note: This ticket was resolved on 2021-01-20 as "Considered - No action
required". The dispositions below are framed as if the workgroup were
re-opening the ticket today (e.g., to revisit the BPMN ↔ PlanDefinition
methodology page in light of the 2024 Zulip discussion).*

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Re-open and address Ricardo Quintano's original review comments on the
`documentation-methodology.html` page of the CPG-on-FHIR
(formerly cqf-recommendations) IG. Concretely: retrieve the attached
review document from the Jira issue (or the 2020-03-23 Workflow WG
minutes), enumerate each individual comment as a sub-task, and update
the BPMN → `PlanDefinition` mapping table and surrounding prose
accordingly. Where comments call out missing BPMN constructs (e.g.,
gateways, loops, sub-processes), extend the mapping section with worked
examples showing the corresponding `PlanDefinition.action` and
`action.relatedAction` patterns.

#### Justification

The underlying methodology page is still the canonical reference — Bryn
Rhodes pointed back to it as the answer in the 2024 Zulip thread — so
sharpening it has lasting value. Acting on a pre-existing reviewer's
comments is also lower-cost than re-deriving them, because the analysis
work has already been done.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than re-opening this 2020 ticket against a now-superseded IG
(`cqf-recommendations`), file a focused successor ticket against the
current Clinical Guidelines on FHIR specification that consolidates:
(1) the still-applicable items from Ricardo's 2020 review, and
(2) the 2024 Zulip-surfaced gaps — specifically loop/iteration semantics
and whether `PlanDefinition` should optionally carry a BPMN payload
(e.g., `application/bpmn+xml`) the way it carries CQL/ELM today. Treat
the methodology page edits as a deliverable of that new ticket rather
than this one.

#### Justification

The original ticket points at the IG's CI build URL on the
`cqf-recommendations` namespace, which has been refactored into the
`cqf-fhir`/CPG line; mechanically reapplying 5-year-old comments risks
editing stale prose. Folding the still-valid feedback into a current,
properly-scoped ticket keeps the audit trail clean, lets the workgroup
weigh new BPMN-payload-on-PlanDefinition design questions alongside the
old ones, and aligns with the 2024 community discussion which is the
live conversation today.

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** C

The ticket is a 2020 placeholder for an attached review document, was
closed by the workgroup as "Considered - No action required" in
January 2021, and has accumulated no cross-references or follow-on
activity since. Mechanically re-opening it would mean editing a page
that has been restructured under a different IG line, against
five-year-old feedback whose specifics aren't recoverable from the
cached Jira data. The right path forward — if the methodology page
still has gaps, as the 2024 `#workflow` Zulip thread suggests it might

B's successor ticket against the current Clinical Guidelines on FHIR
spec, filed by someone who can articulate the gap concretely. Closing
this one and pointing any new request at a fresh ticket keeps the
backlog honest.
