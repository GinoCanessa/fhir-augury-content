## Summary

FHIR-29519 is a Pharmacy work-group change request against the US Prescription
Drug Monitoring Program (PDMP) FHIR Implementation Guide (tracked under the
"US Medication FHIR Implementation Guide (FHIR)" specification key). The
reporter (Molly Reese) flags that early in the IG narrative there is a
statement that the US Core / "US Meds" `MedicationRequest` and
`MedicationDispense` profiles "aren't widely used in US," and asks that the
text be clarified and revised. The current wording singled out for revision
is the simple line: *"Support the US Meds MedicationRequest and
MedicationDispense Profile."* — which both makes a usage claim and points
implementers at specific upstream profiles, and was felt to be misleading or
out of date.

The ticket was originally dispositioned **Considered for Future Use** on
2023-01-18 (Hatem/McNeil 4-0-0) on the basis that the IG had been withdrawn
and would only be revisited when the project sponsor / WG took it back up.
On 2023-11-28 smrobertson **reverted that resolution and reopened** the
ticket because the project was being revived under PSS-2225 ("PDMP on FHIR"),
so the narrative this ticket targets is back in scope. The ticket is now
Triaged, Highest priority, but unassigned.

A related, more recent Pharmacy/PDMP item is [FHIR-44843 – US Core Variance
Request for MedicationDispense](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-44843). It
was filed by Frank McKinney (PDMP IG team) in Feb 2024 and discussed an
invariant collision between US Core's `MedicationDispense` profile (which
requires `whenHandedOver` when status = `completed`) and the base FHIR
invariant (which requires `whenHandedOver >= whenPrepared`, breaking down
when a Data Absent Reason extension is used). CGP closed FHIR-44843 as
"Resolved – No Change," and Eric Haas suggested CGP log a separate tracker
to soften the base invariant to a warning, while explicitly directing the
DAR/long-term consideration to **HL7 Pharmacy**. That context matters for
FHIR-29519 because any rewrite of the "Support the US Meds
MedicationRequest/MedicationDispense Profile" sentence has to honestly
reflect the *practical* US Core conformance position the PDMP IG ended up
taking — i.e., it cannot merely repeat the original aspirational statement.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Rewrite the IG's introductory sentence
> "Support the US Meds MedicationRequest and MedicationDispense Profile."

to clarify (a) which upstream profiles are actually meant — the **US Core
MedicationRequest** and **US Core MedicationDispense** profiles, since
"US Meds" is not the canonical name for any published HL7 profile family;
(b) the IG's actual conformance posture — i.e., that the PDMP IG profiles
are derived from the *base FHIR* MedicationRequest/MedicationDispense
resources but are designed to meet the information requirements of the
corresponding US Core profiles wherever feasible; and (c) remove the
out-of-date "aren't widely used in US" framing. Suggested replacement
(authored in the PDMP IG narrative pages — typically `input/pagecontent/`
in an HL7 IG repo, e.g. `index.md` / `background.md` / `usage.md`):

> The PDMP profiles for `MedicationRequest` and `MedicationDispense` are
> derived from the base FHIR resources. They are designed so that PDMP
> instances meet the information requirements of the US Core
> MedicationRequest and MedicationDispense profiles where the underlying
> PDMP data permits. A formal US Core variance (FHIR-44843) covers the
> specific cases — notably absence of `whenHandedOver` — where strict
> profile conformance is not achievable.

#### Justification

Honest, specific, implementer-actionable wording is consistent with the
HL7 IG narrative style guide and avoids the discredited "not widely used"
claim. It also accurately documents the relationship to US Core that the
PDMP team ultimately negotiated via FHIR-44843, so implementers
encountering the variance know where it came from and why. Because the
change is purely narrative and confined to the IG's own pagecontent, it
carries no breaking-change risk for instances or profile authors.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than rewriting only the single sentence flagged in the ticket,
expand the change into a small **"Relationship to US Core"** subsection in
the PDMP IG (one or two short paragraphs in the same pagecontent file).
The subsection would: (1) name US Core MedicationRequest /
MedicationDispense as the comparison profiles; (2) state the PDMP IG's
conformance stance ("compatible information requirements, base-FHIR
profile derivation"); (3) cite FHIR-44843 and the resulting variance as
the formal record; and (4) cross-link to the specific PDMP profile pages
(e.g., `StructureDefinition-pdmp-medicationdispense.html`). Also add a
`MustSupport`/conformance summary table mapping PDMP profile elements to
the corresponding US Core elements.

#### Justification

The ticket's underlying concern is that the original sentence was
misleading, but a one-line rewrite (Disposition A) leaves implementers
without context for *why* the PDMP profiles diverge from US Core.
Implementers who encounter the variance via tooling (validators flagging
`whenHandedOver` invariants) need a place in the narrative to land. The
trade-off vs. A is more authoring effort and a slightly larger review
surface; the upside is that the IG materially improves and the same edit
also closes the gap that drove FHIR-44843 in the first place. This stays
within the IG's authoring root (pagecontent + IG-level conformance
material) and does not require any StructureDefinition changes.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-29519 as **Persuasive with Modification – No Action Required**
(or re-apply the prior **Considered for Future Use**) on the grounds that
the offending sentence describes a previous draft of the IG that has
since been overtaken by the PSS-2225 revival work, and the new ballot
package will replace the introductory text wholesale; therefore a
targeted edit against the FHIR-29519 wording is unnecessary.

#### Justification

The original 2023-01-18 disposition already used this reasoning ("IG has
been withdrawn"). With PSS-2225 active, the narrative *is* being rewritten
anyway, and there is a real risk that fixing only this sentence in
isolation creates rework for the team rebuilding the IG. Declining is
defensible if and only if the PDMP IG editors confirm the introductory
section is being fully replaced; otherwise the original out-of-date text
will re-ship. This option leaves no authoring footprint in the IG repo.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The ticket sat dormant for years specifically because the IG was
withdrawn; it was reopened precisely because the IG is being revived under
PSS-2225, and FHIR-44843 (Feb 2024) has since clarified the IG's *actual*
relationship to US Core in concrete, invariant-level terms. A one-line
fix (A) accurately removes the misleading claim but discards a real
authoring opportunity; declining (C) gambles that the rewrite-in-flight
will catch the same issue and risks shipping the legacy wording again.
Disposition B uses the ticket as the natural hook to add the small
"Relationship to US Core" section the IG is going to need anyway — it
addresses the reporter's concern, gives implementers a stable place to
land when they hit the FHIR-44843 variance, and stays cleanly inside the
PDMP IG's pagecontent without touching profiles. If the Pharmacy WG
prefers to scope tightly and let the broader US Core narrative land in a
separate ticket, fall back to Disposition A; do not adopt C unless the
PDMP IG editors explicitly confirm an in-flight wholesale rewrite of the
introductory section.
