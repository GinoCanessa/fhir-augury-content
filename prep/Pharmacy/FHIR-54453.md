## Summary

Eric Haas (Pharmacy) is questioning a change made between R4 and R6 that
expanded `MedicationStatement.effective[x]` from `dateTime | Period` to
`dateTime | Period | Timing`. The reporter argues that the addition of
`Timing` is conceptually misaligned with the intent of `effective[x]`,
which is meant to describe the **duration** (the interval) over which
the patient is/was/will be taking the medication, not the **schedule**
of doses. Schedule information is already conveyed by
`MedicationStatement.dosage.timing`, so adding `Timing` to
`effective[x]` produces redundancy and a likely source of contradictory
data between the two elements.

In addition, the reporter highlights an internal inconsistency in the
element definition: the `effective[x]` comment still tells implementers
that anything more than a simple period "should be conveyed as text",
which suggests that either (a) the comment was left over from when the
type set did not include `Timing` and should now be reworded to point at
`Timing`, or (b) the original intent was to add a `string` type rather
than `Timing`. The proposal is therefore to either revert
`effective[x]` to `dateTime | Period`, replace `Timing` with `string`
(`dateTime | Period | string`), or, if `Timing` is retained, rewrite the
definition and comment to make it crystal clear when `Timing` should be
used and how it relates to `dosage.timing`.

No cross-referenced Jira tickets, GitHub items, or Zulip threads are
indexed against this ticket. The closest relevant community discussion
is the Medication-stream thread "Dealing with contradictory time
indications in MedStatements", which independently confirms that
implementers already struggle to reconcile `effective[x]` with
`dosage.timing` even before `Timing` was added as a third option.

## Details

**Description:**

> R4→R6: *"Add Type Timing"*
>
> `effective[x]` can now be: `dateTime | Period | Timing`
>
> **Issue:**
>
> 1. Timing is a complex data type for recurring schedules (e.g.,
>    "twice daily"). But `effective` is supposed to represent: *"The
>    interval of time during which... the patient is/was/will be taking
>    the medication"*.
>
>    This is the DURATION of taking, not the SCHEDULE of doses. Why
>    would you need Timing here? This describes HOW to take, not WHEN
>    the statement is effective, and `dosage` already contains
>    timing/schedule information.
>
>    Adding Timing to `effective[x]` creates redundancy.
>
> 2. The comment in `effective[x]` states: *"This attribute reflects the
>    period over which the patient consumed the medication and is
>    expected to be populated on the majority of Medication
>    Statements... If something more than this is required, this
>    **should be conveyed as text.**"*
>
>    Was it meant to say *"**should be conveyed as Timing"***, or does
>    this imply that the intent for using the Timing datatype is to use
>    the text field in `Timing.code`, or was the intent to add a string
>    type to the `effective[x]`?
>
> **Propose:**
>
> Reverting `effective[x]` back to: `dateTime | Period` ...or...
>
> `dateTime | Period | string` ...or...
>
> Update the definition and comment to document when and how to use
> Timing to convey what `effective[x]` communicates.

## Related Zulip Discussions

### [Medication > Dealing with contradictory time indications in MedStatements](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/Medication/topic/Dealing%20with%20contradictory%20time%20indications%20in%20MedStatements)

The thread (March 2021, R4-era) directly anticipates this ticket. Morten
Ernebjerg asks how to reconcile `MedicationStatement.effective[x]`
(then `dateTime | Period`) with `dosage.timing`, noting that
`dosage.timing` can itself express durations and absolute dates, so the
two elements can already give contradictory information (e.g.,
`dosage.timing` says "30 days" but `effectivePeriod` is 3 days long).
Christof Gessner replies that `effective[x]` is best read together with
`MedicationStatement.status`, that the typical resolution is to split a
contradictory statement into multiple `MedicationStatement` instances
(one stopped/not-taken with the original `dosage.timing`, another active
with the actual `effective[x]` and revised `dosage.timing`), and that
even at that point the only cleanly machine-processable case is when
`dosage.timing` expresses a *rhythm* (e.g., "every 8 hours") inside the
`effective[x]` window. Ernebjerg agrees and concludes that whenever
`Dosage.timing` involves absolute dates or intervals, the combinatorics
with `effective[x]` are too complex to trust to a machine. No formal
consensus or change was recorded, but the discussion supports the
reporter's concern: even with two time elements (not three), the
overlap between `effective[x]` and `dosage.timing` is already
problematic in practice.

Participants: Morten Ernebjerg, Christof Gessner.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Revert `MedicationStatement.effective[x]` from `dateTime | Period |
Timing` back to `dateTime | Period` in FHIR R6. Concretely, this means
removing the `Timing` choice from the type set on the `effective[x]`
element of the `MedicationStatement` StructureDefinition, regenerating
the snapshot/differential, and updating the element definition and
comment so they no longer hint at conveying schedule-shaped information
on `effective[x]`. The cleanup should also remove (or rewrite) the
sentence "If something more than this is required, this should be
conveyed as text", which is the artifact the reporter flagged as
self-contradictory. Mark the type-set change as a **breaking change** in
the R6 change notes, and add a migration note pointing implementers who
genuinely need a non-contiguous schedule to `dosage.timing`.

#### Justification

`MedicationStatement.effective[x]` is normatively defined as the
interval over which the medication is/was/will be taken — i.e., a
duration, not a schedule. `Timing` is a schedule data type whose
recurrence semantics duplicate (and can directly contradict)
`dosage.timing`, which already exists for exactly that purpose. The
March 2021 Medication-stream thread above shows that even in R4 (with
only `dateTime | Period`) implementers were struggling to reconcile
`effective[x]` with `dosage.timing`; introducing a third, schedule-shaped
choice strictly worsens that ambiguity and gives no implementer benefit
that cannot already be expressed via `dosage.timing`. R6 has not yet
been balloted as Normative for this resource, so reverting the type set
now — while the change is still relatively young — is materially
cheaper than carrying the redundancy forward.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the `Timing` type on `effective[x]` but constrain and document its
use so it can no longer overlap with `dosage.timing`. Specifically:

- Rewrite the element **definition** of
  `MedicationStatement.effective[x]` to read (in essence): *"The
  interval (or set of intervals) during which the patient is/was/will be
  taking the medication. When `Timing` is used, only the
  `Timing.event`, `Timing.repeat.bounds[x]`, and `Timing.code` elements
  are meaningful; `Timing.repeat.frequency`, `period`, and `when` are
  out of scope here and SHALL be expressed via `dosage.timing` instead."*
- Replace the existing comment ("should be conveyed as text") with a
  comment that explicitly states the relationship to `dosage.timing`
  and gives a one-line example of when each is appropriate (e.g.,
  *"Use `effectiveTiming` for a non-contiguous duration such as 'on for
  5 days, off for 2 days, repeated 4 times'; use `dosage.timing` for the
  per-dose schedule within those windows."*).
- Add a FHIRPath invariant on `MedicationStatement` that fires when
  `effectiveTiming.repeat.frequency`, `period`, `periodMax`, or `when`
  are populated, telling implementers to move that content to
  `dosage.timing`.

#### Justification

If the original R5/R6 motivation for adding `Timing` was to support
genuinely non-contiguous treatment windows (chemo cycles, "5-on / 2-off"
regimens, intermittent therapy), then `Timing.event` /
`Timing.repeat.bounds[x]` is the only datatype in FHIR that can express
that without forcing a separate `MedicationStatement` per window.
Disposition B preserves that capability while explicitly removing the
schedule-vs-duration ambiguity the reporter is concerned about, by
narrowing `effective[x]`'s use of `Timing` to its bounding/event facets
and pinning per-dose timing on `dosage.timing` (where it already lives).
This is a non-breaking change relative to the current R6 wire format and
addresses the reporter's second point (the misleading comment) directly.
The trade-off vs. A is that implementers still need to read and respect
the new prose / invariant — they no longer get safety purely from the
type system — but Pharmacy retains the ability to express irregular
treatment intervals in a single resource.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request and retain `MedicationStatement.effective[x]` as
`dateTime | Period | Timing`, on the grounds that the change was
deliberately made to align `MedicationStatement.effective[x]` with the
parallel polymorphic time elements on
`MedicationAdministration.effective[x]` and related "event" resources,
which already accept `Timing`, and that the perceived redundancy with
`dosage.timing` is no worse than the long-standing R4-era overlap that
the community has already learned to live with (see the 2021 Medication
thread above). At most, accept a documentation-only tweak to clarify
the difference between `effective[x]` and `dosage.timing`.

#### Justification

The Pharmacy resources have a consistent pattern of letting the
"effective" / "occurrence" element be polymorphic across
`dateTime`/`Period`/`Timing` so that a single resource can represent
both point-in-time events and irregular treatment windows. Reverting on
`MedicationStatement` alone would break that symmetry, and the
ambiguity the reporter calls out is not new — the 2021 Zulip discussion
shows that implementers were already managing the
`effective[x]` ↔ `dosage.timing` overlap before `Timing` was added.
Backing the change out in R6 risks churn for early adopters of R6
preview builds for a problem that is, at its core, a documentation
issue.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the best balance of preserving the modeling power
that the R5/R6 change was presumably introduced for (single-resource
representation of non-contiguous treatment windows such as chemo cycles
and "5-on/2-off" regimens) while honestly addressing the reporter's two
real complaints: (1) that `Timing`'s schedule-shaped facets duplicate
`dosage.timing`, and (2) that the existing element comment is internally
inconsistent and tells implementers the wrong thing. By narrowing
`effective[x]`'s use of `Timing` to its `event` / `bounds[x]` /
`code` facets via prose plus an invariant, and rewriting the comment to
explicitly contrast `effective[x]` with `dosage.timing`, the workgroup
removes the redundancy the reporter is worried about without paying the
breaking-change cost of Disposition A or ignoring the legitimate
documentation issue called out in Disposition C. Disposition A remains
a defensible fallback if Pharmacy concludes — perhaps after a wider
implementer poll — that no real-world `MedicationStatement` author
needs a non-contiguous bounding interval and that `dosage.timing` plus
multiple `MedicationStatement` instances is sufficient. Disposition C
should be avoided: the reporter's second point (the misleading comment
referencing "text") is a concrete documentation defect that the
workgroup should not leave in the spec regardless of which type-set
direction it ultimately picks.
