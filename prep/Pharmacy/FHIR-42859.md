## Summary

The ticket, raised out of Connectathon 34, asks the US PDMP (FHIR) IG to
explicitly permit *prefetch* of PDMP data so that prescription history can be
retrieved before the actual point-of-care decision (for example, when a
patient is admitted to the ER, or the night before a scheduled encounter) in
order to minimize workflow latency at the time an order is placed. The
submitter notes that any prefetch capability interacts with — and is
constrained by — state-level data-retention rules captured in the related
ticket [FHIR-42853](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-42853) ("Data retention
restrictions"), which observes that most states do not permit ingesting or
persistently storing PDMP responses. The IG editors closed FHIR-42859 as
**Persuasive**, with the resolution that prefetch was incorporated into the
2024-May ballot as named "prefetch" functionality, while the related
retention ticket FHIR-42853 was retracted by the submitter. This review
considers whether the prefetch treatment landed in the May 2024 ballot is
still the right disposition or whether the workgroup should refine it.

## Proposed Dispositions

Note: the ticket has already been resolved as **Persuasive** and folded
into the 2024-May ballot ("prefetch" functionality). The dispositions
below frame what the workgroup could re-affirm, refine, or reverse if
the topic is reopened (for example, in light of ballot comments or the
fact that the related retention ticket FHIR-42853 was retracted without
adding retention guidance).

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Re-affirm the existing May-2024 ballot resolution: keep an explicit
"prefetch" workflow option in the US PDMP (FHIR) IG that allows a
requester to retrieve PDMP data ahead of the actual ordering moment
(e.g., on ER admission, on appointment check-in, or the night before a
scheduled encounter), in addition to the current real-time / synchronous
flow. Document the prefetch pattern as a named workflow variant alongside
the real-time pattern, and call out that an async / deferred response
model is acceptable on the FHIR layer.

#### Justification

This matches the original Connectathon 34 use case (avoiding stat-order
delays in the ER) and the Tech-call observation that FHIR can support an
async model. The submitter and Tech group agreed prefetch is technically
necessary in some clinical settings, and the IG already shipped this as
"prefetch" in the May-2024 ballot, so re-affirming preserves continuity
for implementers who built to that ballot.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the prefetch capability documented in the IG but constrain it
narrowly: define prefetch only as "request bound to a specific patient
encounter event" (admit-to-ER, check-in-at-provider, or a scheduled
appointment occurring within a short window — e.g., ≤24 hours).
Explicitly disallow speculative or batch prefetch detached from a known
upcoming encounter, add a SHALL-level statement that any prefetched
response be discarded when the encounter does not occur within the state's
retention window, and add an audit expectation that ties each prefetch
request to the triggering encounter identifier (e.g., an `Encounter`
or `Appointment` reference carried on the request). FHIR-42853 (now
retracted) flagged that most states do not permit persistent storage of
PDMP responses, so the IG itself should carry the retention guardrails
rather than relying on a separate ticket that no longer exists.

#### Justification

This addresses the 10/4 PDMP-call concern that early/speculative
requests create audit problems ("was the information actually used for
an encounter?") while still enabling the ER and night-before scheduled
use cases the submitter cared about. It also closes the gap left by
FHIR-42853 being retracted: state retention rules were called out as a
known constraint on prefetch, but no separate ticket now carries that
guidance into the IG. Tying prefetch to an encounter event keeps the
workflow auditable and aligns with the call-note guidance that "requests
should be at patient *encounter*, when the patient is first engaged".

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Remove the explicit "prefetch" framing from the IG and document only the
real-time / point-of-care request pattern (request issued at the moment
the clinician needs the PDMP data to make an ordering decision). Note
async FHIR mechanics as a generic transport option without endorsing
prefetch as a named PDMP workflow.

#### Justification

The 10/4 PDMP-call notes captured a clear concern that the IG "should
not imply pre-fetching as an option" because of audit ambiguity, and
most US PDMPs are synchronous today, so the real-world deployment
surface for an explicit prefetch pattern is small. Combined with the
fact that the retention-restrictions companion ticket (FHIR-42853) was
retracted without adding guardrails, there is a defensible argument that
endorsing prefetch in the IG without retention rules introduces
compliance risk that outweighs the workflow benefit. Declining keeps the
IG narrow and lets prefetch be re-introduced later with proper retention
language.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The May-2024 ballot already shipped prefetch (per the existing
**Persuasive** resolution), so reverting it (Disposition C) would churn
implementers and ignore the legitimate ER / scheduled-encounter use cases
the submitter and Tech group endorsed. However, the call notes
explicitly warn that unconstrained prefetch creates audit problems, and
the companion retention ticket FHIR-42853 was retracted without adding
guardrails — leaving the IG endorsing prefetch with no clear retention
or encounter-binding rules. Disposition B keeps the prefetch capability
that was balloted but binds it to a triggering encounter, adds the
retention guidance that FHIR-42853 was originally meant to carry, and
directly answers the 10/4 PDMP-call concern about audit traceability.
That gives the workgroup a clear forward path on both this ticket and
the gap left by the retracted FHIR-42853, without breaking what was
already balloted.
