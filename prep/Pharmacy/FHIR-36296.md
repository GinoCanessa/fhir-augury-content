## Summary

The reporter asks the Pharmacy Work Group to consider adding a dedicated
"pricing guidance" section to the US CARIN Real-time Pharmacy Benefit
Check (RTPBC) FHIR Implementation Guide that mirrors guidance NCPDP
added to its RTPB Standard Version 11 Implementation Guide (published
January 2021). The proposed guidance would (a) make explicit that
RTPB(C) pricing fields are point-in-time *estimates* of patient
out-of-pocket cost, and (b) describe how patient-pay component fields
(copay, co-insurance, deductible, HSA, health-plan-funded assistance,
etc.) relate to the Estimated Patient Financial Responsibility total,
including how plan-benefit accumulators behave at the transaction level
rather than across a sequence of RTPBC requests. The ticket includes
the literal NCPDP narrative and three worked examples (zero-copay,
0% co-insurance, and a 100%-co-insurance-with-deductible-and-HSA
scenario) that the reporter would like reflected in the FHIR IG. No
cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were
found for this ticket, so the request is self-contained.

## Details

**Description:**

NCPDP added a dedicated section to pricing guidance in the RTPB
Standard Version 11 (published in January 2021) Implementation Guide.

-----------------------

**Business Need/Purpose:**

Pricing guidance was added to RTPB Standard Implementation Guide based
on the following two recommendations:

- Add pricing guidance about pricing fields being an estimate.
- Look at patient pay component fields that leverage accumulator fields
  as part of the calculation.
  - Each RTPB transaction submitted will use the current plan benefit
    accumulator fields to calculate the patient pay components, with
    the sum of the components returned in the Estimated Patient
    Financial Responsibility field. This will be at transaction level
    and not a plan benefit level.
  - If the prescriber is submitting 5 RTPB requests, where at a claim
    level the 3rd RX would buy down the deductible, this would not be
    reflected in the patient pay field in the RTPB response.
  - Information about patient pay components and estimated patient
    financial responsibility, including an example with a patient pay
    component for an accumulator, was added to pricing guidance.

-----------------------

**Pricing guidance from RTPB Standard Implementation Guide:**

*Each RTPB transaction (request and response) reflects a moment in time
and is designed to provide an estimate of the patient's out of pocket
costs. A patient's actual out of pocket cost may vary depending on the
timing between the RTPB request(s) and the corresponding NCPDP claim
billing request/response transactions, as well as the order of the
claim billing transactions when multiple prescriptions occur.*

*The total estimated patient's out of pocket cost is reflected in the
ESTIMATED PATIENT FINANCIAL RESPONSIBILITY (F17-YE) field. This field
is a summation of the different patient responsibility components that
may apply based on the benefit structure (e.g.: copay, co-insurance,
deductible, brand drug, non-formulary brand drug, health savings
account, etc.). The following patient responsibility pricing field
guidance associated to NCPDP Telecommunication claim billing responses
should also apply to RTPB responses.*

- *ESTIMATED PATIENT FINANCIAL RESPONSIBILITY (F17-YE) is Required*
  - *Zero is a valid value*
  - *Value cannot be negative*
- *Patient Pay Component Count (C94-KP), Patient Pay Component
  Qualifier (C95-KQ) are Required*
- *Patient Pay Component Amount (C93-KN) is Required*
  - *Zero is a valid value*
- *Negative values may be associated to Specific Patient Pay Component
  Qualifiers (C95-KQ) (e.g., Health Plan Savings Amount).*
- *The sum of the Patient Pay Component Amounts (C95-KQ) must equal
  the Estimated Patient Financial Responsibility (F17-YE)*
- *All Patient Pay Component amounts, applicable to the patient's
  benefit for this claim, that impact the Estimated Patient Financial
  Responsibility (F17-YE) must be returned, even when the impacted
  Patient Pay Component Amount or Patient Pay Amount is $0.*

*Example 1: Plan Benefit Patient Liability = $0 Copay, No HSA, No
Deductible*

- F17-YE ESTIMATED PATIENT FINANCIAL RESPONSIBILITY: $0.00
- C94-KP PATIENT PAY COMPONENT AMOUNT COUNT: 1
- C95-KQ PATIENT PAY COMPONENT QUALIFIER (AMOUNT OF COPAY): 05
- C93-KN PATIENT PAY COMPONENT AMOUNT: $0.00

*Example 2: Plan Benefit Patient Liability = 0% Co-Insurance for
Generics*

- F17-YE ESTIMATED PATIENT FINANCIAL RESPONSIBILITY: $0.00
- C94-KP PATIENT PAY COMPONENT AMOUNT COUNT: 1
- C95-KQ PATIENT PAY COMPONENT QUALIFIER (AMOUNT OF CO-INSURANCE): 07
- C93-KN PATIENT PAY COMPONENT AMOUNT: $0.00

*Example 3: Plan Benefit Patient Liability = 100% co-insurance until
$1,000 plan deductible is met, HSA funds apply*

- F17-YE ESTIMATED PATIENT FINANCIAL RESPONSIBILITY: $0.00
- C94-KP PATIENT PAY COMPONENT AMOUNT COUNT: 2
- C95-KQ PATIENT PAY COMPONENT QUALIFIER (AMOUNT APPLIED TO PERIODIC
  DEDUCTIBLE) 01
- C93-KN PATIENT PAY COMPONENT AMOUNT $110.00
- C95-KQ PATIENT PAY COMPONENT QUALIFIER (HEALTH PLAN-FUNDED
  ASSISTANCE AMOUNT) 09
- C93-KN PATIENT PAY COMPONENT AMOUNT -$110.00

## Proposed Dispositions

> Note: No GitHub repository was cross-referenced from this ticket, so
> no `repo-analysis` briefing applies. The CARIN RTPBC FHIR IG (the
> specification named on the ticket) is published by HL7 but is not
> represented in the cached repo set surfaced by `cross-referenced`.
> The dispositions therefore name the IG and the affected narrative
> pages directly, but defer concrete authoring-root and file-path
> citations to whichever repo the Pharmacy WG identifies for the
> RTPBC IG at disposition time.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a dedicated **"Pricing Guidance"** narrative section to the US
CARIN Real-time Pharmacy Benefit Check (RTPBC) FHIR IG that mirrors,
in FHIR terms, the NCPDP RTPB Standard v11 pricing guidance quoted in
this ticket. Specifically:

1. Add a top-level page (e.g., `pricing-guidance.html`) under the IG's
   narrative table of contents, placed alongside the existing
   request/response and behavior pages, and link it from the IG home
   page.
2. State explicitly that every RTPBC response is a *point-in-time
   estimate* and that actual out-of-pocket cost may vary based on the
   timing of subsequent NCPDP claim billing transactions and the order
   in which multiple prescriptions are adjudicated.
3. Map the NCPDP fields named in the ticket to the RTPBC FHIR
   profiles:
   - F17-YE (Estimated Patient Financial Responsibility) → the
     `PharmacyBenefitResponse` (or current equivalent) element /
     extension that carries the patient's total estimated cost; mark
     it as **required**, allow `0`, disallow negative values.
   - C94-KP / C95-KQ / C93-KN (Patient Pay Component Count /
     Qualifier / Amount) → the per-component slice on the response
     profile, with cardinality `1..*` per component returned and a
     bound qualifier ValueSet aligned with NCPDP's KQ code list.
4. State the invariant that the sum of returned Patient Pay Component
   Amounts MUST equal the Estimated Patient Financial Responsibility,
   expressed as a FHIRPath constraint on the response profile so that
   it is machine-checkable, not just narrative.
5. Carry across the rule that all applicable patient pay components
   must be returned even when their amount is `$0`, and that negative
   amounts are only valid for designated qualifiers (e.g.,
   Health Plan-Funded Assistance). Reflect this in the qualifier
   ValueSet's `negativeAllowed` annotation (or an equivalent
   extension) and in the constraint above.
6. Reproduce the three worked examples from the ticket as FHIR
   `Bundle`/`Example` instances that resolve against the response
   profile, so implementers can run them through validation.

#### Justification

The CARIN RTPBC IG exists specifically to expose NCPDP RTPB semantics
over a FHIR REST surface, so when NCPDP issues normative pricing
guidance for the underlying transaction, the FHIR IG is expected to
stay in lockstep — otherwise an implementer reading only the FHIR IG
will produce responses that NCPDP-aligned trading partners reject as
non-conformant. Promoting the F17-YE summation rule to a FHIRPath
invariant rather than narrative-only text matches how the rest of the
IG (and FHIR IGs in general) handle cross-element math, makes the
constraint testable in the publisher and IG validator, and reduces
ambiguity for implementers porting NCPDP logic.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than authoring a freestanding "Pricing Guidance" section, fold
the new guidance into the existing response-profile narrative and the
profile/ValueSet artifacts themselves:

1. Extend the **definition text** of the existing patient-pay /
   estimated-financial-responsibility elements on the response profile
   to carry the NCPDP narrative (point-in-time estimate, accumulator
   behavior at the transaction level, "5-RTPB-request" worked
   scenario), so the guidance travels with the element when consumed
   by code generators and IG-rendered tables.
2. Bind the patient-pay component qualifier element to a CodeSystem /
   ValueSet (`PatientPayComponentQualifier`) that contains the C95-KQ
   codes, with a `description` per code that includes whether negative
   amounts are valid for that qualifier.
3. Encode the summation rule and the "$0 components must still be
   returned" rule as profile invariants (FHIRPath `constraint`
   elements) instead of new narrative.
4. Add the three worked scenarios as `Example` resources referenced
   from the existing response-profile page, rather than from a new
   page.
5. Add a single short paragraph on the IG home page that points to
   these elements and explains the relationship between RTPBC pricing
   semantics and NCPDP RTPB v11 — i.e., a pointer rather than a full
   restated section.

#### Justification

This keeps the IG's information architecture stable (no new top-level
page, no duplication of NCPDP narrative that may drift as NCPDP
revises RTPB), pushes the substantive rules into the conformance
artifacts where validators and downstream tooling can enforce them,
and treats the NCPDP IG as the source of truth for the prose. The
trade-off vs. Disposition A is reduced discoverability — a reader
looking for a "Pricing" tab in the TOC will not find one — but it
avoids the maintenance burden of keeping a long narrative section in
sync with each NCPDP RTPB version. It also addresses the underlying
need (implementers producing conformant RTPBC responses) more
robustly, because invariants and bound code systems are
machine-checkable in a way that narrative is not.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Do not add the pricing-guidance section to the CARIN RTPBC FHIR IG.
Resolve as **Persuasive with Mod / Not Persuasive — Out of Scope**
with the rationale that pricing semantics are normatively defined by
NCPDP in the RTPB Standard Implementation Guide v11+, and the FHIR IG
should reference NCPDP rather than re-publish NCPDP content. Add a
single sentence to the IG home page that directs implementers to the
NCPDP RTPB Standard IG for pricing guidance, and close this ticket.

#### Justification

The text the reporter wants to add is, almost verbatim, NCPDP-owned
normative content. Re-publishing it in a HL7 IG creates two risks:
(1) the two documents will drift as NCPDP revises the RTPB Standard
(v11 is from 2021; subsequent versions exist), leaving implementers
unsure which version is authoritative, and (2) HL7 may inadvertently
reproduce copyrighted NCPDP material without the appropriate license
arrangement. Declining and pointing to the NCPDP source is defensible
on both maintenance and IP grounds. The ticket itself shows zero
cross-references, no Zulip discussion, and no implementer follow-up
in the ~3 years since it was filed, which suggests low community
demand for the duplication.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the strongest path. It directly addresses the
implementer-facing problem the ticket describes — that the FHIR IG
under-specifies how patient-pay components and the estimated-financial-
responsibility total relate — by promoting those rules to profile
invariants and a bound qualifier ValueSet, which is where conformance
in a FHIR IG belongs. It avoids the IP and drift hazards that make
Disposition C tempting (since it points at NCPDP rather than restating
it) while still giving implementers something concrete and testable to
build against. Disposition A is acceptable but heavier: a freestanding
narrative section adds maintenance load and risks falling out of step
with NCPDP each time RTPB is revised, without producing any artifact
that a validator can enforce. Disposition C is the safest from an IP
standpoint but leaves the IG's actual conformance gap unaddressed and
ignores the reporter's underlying point that the FHIR IG today is
silent on accumulator semantics. The Pharmacy WG should confirm with
NCPDP liaison that referencing rather than restating their text is
acceptable before finalizing B.
