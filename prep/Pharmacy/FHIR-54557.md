## Summary

The ticket asks the Pharmacy work group to clarify how the multiple
`safety` elements that appear at successive levels of the new
`DosageDetails` data type (`DosageDetails.safety`,
`DosageDetails.simple.safety`, `DosageDetails.step.safety`, and
`DosageDetails.step.component.safety`) relate to one another. The
reporter, Chris Moesel, points out that without explicit guidance an
implementer has no way to know whether a higher-level `safety` should be
an aggregate of the lower-level ones, whether `safety` should always be
expressed at the most precise level it applies to, and — given that
`DosageDetails.step` already requires `component` children that each
carry their own `safety` — under what circumstances a `step.safety`
should be populated at all rather than (or in addition to) the
component-level entries. Because these elements are about *patient
safety*, the reporter argues the ambiguity must be resolved in normative
text rather than left to convention.

No formal cross-references were returned by the augury cross-reference
index for this ticket, but it sits inside the larger Dosage / Medication
redesign tranche announced on Zulip in
[#Medication > Dosage](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/Medication/topic/Dosage/near/554682681)
and is closely sibling to several other Pharmacy tickets being worked in
the same window (notably [FHIR-54651](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54651) which
removes the `simple` branch and so eliminates one of the four paths
called out here, and [FHIR-54246](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54246) /
[FHIR-54333](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54333) which surface
real-world confusion about `safety.doseLimit.scope` in published examples).
The ticket has been triaged with resolution **Persuasive** and a draft
resolution that proposes adding clarifying text to **section 14.7.0.2**.

## Details

**Description:**

> In DosageDetails, one or more DosageSafety instances may exist at several paths:
>
> - DosageDetails.safety
> - DosageDetails.simple.safety
> - DosageDetails.step.safety
> - DosageDetails.step.component.safety
>
> Please clarify how these relate to each other (e.g., boundaries of use). Some questions that should be clarified:
>
> - Should higher-level safety elements aggregate lower-level safety elements? If yes, is it merely encouraged or required? If no, is it merely discouraged or forbidden?
> - Should safety elements always be specified at the most precise element they apply to or is it ever acceptable to only specify them at one of the higher levels?
> - DosageDetails.step has a safety element, but it also *requires* component elements, each of which can have their own safety element. Under what circumstances would you populate DosageDetails.step.safety rather than the appropriate DosageDetails.step.component.safety?
>
> Since this is quite literally about safety, I think it is important to be clear about how these safety elements should be populated and used.

**Resolution Description (already recorded on the ticket):**

> Add to 14.7.0.2
>
> The safety elements are used to apply safety considerations to the same level where it is used, i.e.
>
> - Dosage.step.component.safety represents the safety considerations for each individual component
> - Dosage.step.safety represents the safety considerations for all the components in the step
> - Dosage.safety represents the safety considerations for the entire dosage
>
> Higher-level safety elements may aggregate lower-level safety elements, or may present considerations that are not directly an aggregation of those lower-level safety elements (for example, a lifetime limit is not an aggregation of limits per take).
>
> If there is one single step, dosage.safety shall be used, and not the dosage.step.safety.

**Comments:** No public comments are recorded on the ticket.

## Proposed Dispositions

> The ticket already carries a **Persuasive** resolution with concrete
> proposed text for §14.7.0.2 (reproduced under "Details" above). The
> dispositions below are framed as choices for the work group when it
> formally adopts, modifies, or sets aside that resolution.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the recorded resolution text essentially as-is into **section
14.7.0.2** of the Dosage page, with one mechanical edit to keep it
consistent with FHIR-54651: drop any reference (express or implied) to
`DosageDetails.simple.safety`, since the `simple` branch is being
removed. Concretely, add a "Use of safety at multiple levels"
subsection to 14.7.0.2 stating:

- `DosageDetails.step.component.safety` carries safety considerations
  scoped to a single component within a step.
- `DosageDetails.step.safety` carries safety considerations that apply
  to all components in the step taken together.
- `DosageDetails.safety` carries safety considerations that apply to
  the entire dosage.
- Higher-level `safety` instances **may** aggregate lower-level ones,
  but **may also** express constraints that are not aggregations
  (e.g., a lifetime limit that is not the sum of per-administration
  limits). Both uses are permitted; the value of higher-level `safety`
  is not constrained to equal an aggregation of its children.
- When a `DosageDetails` has exactly one `step`, **`dosage.safety`
  SHALL be used and `dosage.step.safety` SHALL NOT be used** (the
  "single-step" rule from the recorded resolution).

No structural / cardinality changes to `DosageDetails` or
`DosageSafety` — narrative-only addition to the page intro.

#### Justification

This is exactly the clarification the reporter requested, and it is
already the work group's recorded position (resolution = Persuasive).
The text answers all three of Moesel's questions: aggregation is
permitted but not required; `safety` is associated with the level it
applies to (so it does not need to be pushed to the most precise
level); and the only hard rule is the single-step collapse, which
removes the otherwise pathological case of a one-step dosage carrying
both `dosage.safety` and `dosage.step.safety`. The single-step
SHALL/SHALL-NOT also gives implementers a deterministic test for the
most common DosageDetails shape. Because the change is narrative-only,
backward compatibility is not at risk.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt the substance of the resolution but **add an invariant** to
`DosageDetails` to make the single-step rule machine-checkable, and
**replace the "may aggregate" sentence with a positive statement plus
an example**. Specifically:

1. Add an invariant on `DosageDetails`:
   `dd-safety-single-step: When step.count() = 1, step.safety.exists()
   = false` (or equivalent expression in the project's standard
   invariant idiom). This expresses the SHALL-NOT in a way validators
   can enforce.
2. Reword the aggregation paragraph as:
   > "A higher-level `safety` instance describes considerations that
   > apply at that level; it MAY duplicate, summarise, or extend
   > lower-level entries. For example, `DosageDetails.safety` MAY
   > carry a lifetime maximum that does not appear at any
   > `step.component.safety`."
3. Drop `DosageDetails.simple.safety` from the prose (per FHIR-54651).
4. Cross-link the new subsection from the `DosageSafety.scope`
   element definition so readers landing on the data type also see the
   level guidance.

#### Justification

The recorded resolution is sound but leaves the "single-step" rule as
a sentence buried in 14.7.0.2 that downstream implementers and
validators will routinely miss. Promoting it to an invariant is
consistent with FHIR's general approach to mutually-exclusive
populations (cf. similar invariants on `Dosage`, `Timing`,
`MedicationRequest`) and turns FHIR-54557 from "documentation fix"
into "documentation fix + a check". Tying the prose to a worked
example also addresses the implementer confusion captured in
[FHIR-54246](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54246) /
[FHIR-54333](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54333) (incorrect scope
in the published examples), and the cross-link from `DosageSafety`
keeps the rule discoverable for readers entering the spec from the
data-type side rather than the page intro.

The cost is small — one invariant and a few extra paragraphs — and the
gain is enforceable safety semantics on a structure that is, as the
reporter notes, "literally about safety."

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket "Not Persuasive" and decline to add the proposed
clarification. Rationale category: **already addressed by existing
mechanism / out of scope for this ballot cycle.** Argue that the
`DosageSafety.scope` element (and its accompanying definition in
§14.7.0.4) already conveys the level at which a safety entry applies,
that the `safety` cardinality at each level is `0..*` so silence is a
permissible answer to "must aggregation occur?", and that further
prose should wait until implementer feedback on the in-flight Dosage
redesign (FHIR-54651, FHIR-51540, FHIR-53983, etc.) settles.

#### Justification

The Dosage redesign is still moving — `simple` is being removed, end
conditions are being defined ([FHIR-53983](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53983)),
and the example set is being corrected. Locking down narrative now
risks contradicting the next round of edits. There is also a defensible
read of the current text that says: each `safety` describes its own
level, period, and the existing `DosageSafety.scope` distinguishes
"administration" / "period" / "lifetime" semantics; nothing further is
required for an implementer who reads both the data-type and the
element they are populating.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition: B**

The work group has already committed to clarifying this (resolution =
Persuasive), so Disposition C — declining — would walk back a position
that survived triage and would leave the demonstrably-confusing
`safety` levels under-specified just as the rest of the Dosage
redesign lands. That leaves A versus B. Disposition A delivers exactly
what the reporter asked for, but the single-step SHALL/SHALL-NOT is
the kind of rule that is silently violated by generated bundles unless
it is enforceable; given that the entire point of these elements is
patient safety, a machine-checkable invariant is worth the small
extra effort. Disposition B also picks up the implementer confusion
documented in
[FHIR-54246](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54246) /
[FHIR-54333](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54333) by anchoring the
new prose to a worked example and cross-linking from `DosageSafety`,
so the clarification is discoverable from both the page intro and the
data-type page. In short: ship the resolution, but ship it as
prose **plus** an invariant, and prune `simple.safety` to stay aligned
with FHIR-54651.
