## Summary

The ticket asks the CARIN Consumer Real-Time Pharmacy Benefit Check (RTPBC)
FHIR specification to add support for a patient's *sex assigned at birth*
on the RTPBC request, alongside the existing patient gender concept. The
request is driven by harmonization with NCPDP RTPB Standard Version 12
(October 2021), which added a new optional "Sex Assigned at Birth" field
on its request transaction in addition to its existing "Patient Gender
Code" field. The business rationale is to reduce treatment delays caused
by "gender mismatch" pharmacy edits when a patient's clinical/biological
sex differs from their gender identity, by giving the requester a way to
optionally communicate sex-at-birth when it differs from gender. No
cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip discussions, or GitHub items were
returned for this issue.

## Details

**Description:**

To ensure harmonization with HL7®, NCPDP added to the RTPB Standard
Version 12 (published in October 2021) an optional field for
communicating a patient's sex assigned at birth on the request
transaction. This data element is in addition to NCPDP's existing
patient gender code field.

------------------------

**Business Need/Purpose**

There is a business need to identify the sex assigned at birth for
medications when someone's gender is different from their sex assigned
at birth to help patients obtain their medication without delays in
treatment for gender mismatch edits that may occur. This new element
will be used as an optional element in the event the patient's sex
assigned at birth differs from their gender.

------------------------

**Information about data elements from NCPDP Data Dictionary:**

Definition: A label given at birth based on medical factors, including
hormones, chromosomes and genitals. Most patients are assigned male or
female at birth based on the appearance of their external genitalia.

Field format: x(1); field length: 1

Valid NCPDP External Code List (ECL) Values:

Sex Assigned at Birth
- 0 = Unknown
- 1 = Male
- 2 = Female
- 4 = Intersex: An individual born with any of several variations in
  sex characteristics, including chromosomes, gonads, sex hormones or
  genitals that do not fit the typical definitions of male or female.

Patient Gender Code
- 0 = Unknown
- 1 = Male
- 2 = Female
- 3 = Non-Binary: An umbrella term for people with gender identities
  that fall somewhere outside of the traditional conceptions of strictly
  either female or male.

**Resolution:**

The proposed addition of Sex Assigned at Birth is deferred due to
restrictions on payers contained in the current administration's
Executive Order 14168 related to gender. It may be reassessed at a
later time. (Resolution: *Considered for Future Use*.)

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add an optional *Sex Assigned at Birth* data element to the RTPBC
**request** in the CARIN Consumer RTPBC IG, alongside the existing
patient gender representation. Concretely, on the request bundle's
patient context (the `Patient` resource referenced from the RTPBC
request), require support for the US Core *Patient Sex Assigned at Birth*
extension
(`http://hl7.org/fhir/us/core/StructureDefinition/us-core-birthsex`)
as an optional (0..1) element with `mustSupport = true` on the RTPBC
request profile of `Patient`. Bind the element to the existing US Core
Birth Sex value set (which aligns conceptually with NCPDP's SAB codes
Unknown/Male/Female/Intersex via the standard mapping documented by
US Core / Gender Harmony). Update the IG narrative to describe when a
sender SHOULD populate SAB (i.e., when it differs from
`Patient.gender`) and add a request example that exercises the
extension.

#### Justification

This option directly satisfies the requester's harmonization goal with
NCPDP RTPB v12 and reuses the already-balloted US Core mechanism rather
than inventing a new RTPBC-local extension. Because RTPBC is a US
realm IG and most consumers/payers are already required to support US
Core Patient, leveraging the US Core birth-sex extension minimizes
implementation cost and avoids divergence between RTPBC and US Core /
USCDI on the same concept. Marking the element optional preserves
backward compatibility with existing RTPBC senders and receivers that
do not collect SAB.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt the broader **HL7 Gender Harmony** model rather than only the US
Core "birth sex" extension: on the RTPBC request `Patient` profile,
optionally permit *Recorded Sex or Gender* (`Patient.extension` for
`recorded-sex-or-gender`) and/or `Patient.gender` together with the US
Core SAB extension, but make the SAB element the only one explicitly
called out as being for the NCPDP harmonization use case (gender
mismatch edits). Provide a usage section that maps each FHIR element
to the corresponding NCPDP RTPB v12 field (Patient Gender Code → 
`Patient.gender`; Sex Assigned at Birth → SAB extension), with an
explicit "send SAB only when it differs from gender" guidance bullet.

#### Justification

Gender Harmony is HL7's strategic direction for representing sex,
gender, and related concepts across FHIR. Hard-wiring only a single
"birth sex" extension on a payer-facing IG risks needing another
breaking change in the next RTPBC ballot cycle once Gender Harmony is
more broadly adopted by US Core. This option positions RTPBC for that
trajectory while still delivering the immediate harmonization with
NCPDP RTPB v12. The trade-off versus Disposition A is more narrative
work in the IG and a slightly larger conformance surface for
implementers, but no additional mandatory elements.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Do not add Sex Assigned at Birth to the RTPBC request at this time.
Retain the current ticket resolution of *Considered for Future Use*,
with an explicit note in the IG's change-history / known-issues that
SAB harmonization with NCPDP RTPB v12 has been deferred and may be
revisited in a future ballot once external policy constraints change.
Rely on `Patient.gender` (already present) for any gender-related
routing in the interim.

#### Justification

This is the disposition the ticket is currently resolved with. The
recorded resolution states the addition is deferred due to
restrictions on payers contained in Executive Order 14168 related to
gender. RTPBC is a payer-facing US realm IG, and adding a normative
SAB element to a request that payers are expected to consume is
materially affected by that constraint. Declining now avoids
publishing conformance requirements that some implementing payers may
be unable or unwilling to honor, while leaving the door open to
revisit when the policy environment changes. The cost is continued
divergence from NCPDP RTPB v12 on this one optional field.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** C

The ticket has already been triaged to *Considered for Future Use*
with a specific, documented external constraint (Executive Order
14168) that targets the very population — US payers — that consumes
RTPBC. Pushing forward with Disposition A or B would put the IG ahead
of what implementers can currently act on, and would force a
conformance change in a near-term ballot cycle if the policy
environment shifts again. Disposition C ratifies the current
resolution, but the workgroup should pair it with two concrete
follow-ups: (1) record the NCPDP RTPB v12 SAB field as a known
harmonization gap in the IG's change log so it is not lost, and
(2) place a tracking ticket on the next RTPBC ballot's backlog to
reassess (favoring Disposition B's Gender Harmony alignment) once
either the external constraint is lifted or US Core's treatment of
SAB / Gender Harmony stabilizes. If the workgroup judges that the
external policy concern does not actually bind RTPBC senders/receivers
(e.g., because the field is optional and no payer is required to
process it), Disposition B becomes the strongest fallback.
