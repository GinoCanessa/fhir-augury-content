## Summary

The ticket, filed by Joan Harper against the **US Specialty Rx (FHIR)** IG owned by the Pharmacy work group, asserts that any **CodeSystem** defined by the IG must have its canonical URL anchored at **`terminology.hl7.org`** (THO) rather than `hl7.org/fhir`, in line with TSMG best practice (see the January 2022 TSMG Internal CodeSystem Identifier Task Force slides and the R5 terminologies guidance at `terminologies.html#system`). The ticket also asks the work group to file a corresponding **UTG ticket** to register the affected CodeSystem(s) in THO. The disposition history shows it was first marked "Considered for Future Use" on 2023-01-30 (vote 6-0-0 by Frank McKinney / John Hatem) pending finalization of the IG-defined-terminology anchoring policy, then **re-opened post STU2 publication** on 2023-05-04 by Lynn Laakso, leaving it in **Triaged / Unresolved** state. No related Jira tickets, GitHub items, or Zulip discussions were surfaced via FhirAugury cross-references; the only outbound link is a Confluence attachment (the TSMG task-force deck, attachment ID `81034628`).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Re-anchor every CodeSystem defined by the **US Specialty Rx (FHIR)** IG so
that its canonical URL lives under `http://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/...`
rather than under the IG's `http://hl7.org/fhir/...` namespace, and file a
companion UTG ticket to migrate / register each affected CodeSystem in the
HL7 Terminology repository. Concretely:

1. Inventory every `CodeSystem` resource currently defined in the IG
   (i.e., every `CodeSystem` whose `url` begins with the IG's
   `hl7.org/fhir/...` base).
2. For each, mint a new `terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/<id>` canonical
   URL, keeping the same `id` where possible.
3. Open a UTG ticket per CodeSystem (or one umbrella UTG ticket
   enumerating all of them) requesting registration in THO under those
   new canonicals; coordinate with TSMG.
4. Once UTG accepts, replace the in-IG CodeSystem definitions with
   *external* references to the THO canonicals, and update every
   `ValueSet.compose.include.system` (and any `Coding.system` examples,
   profile bindings, and narrative) that points at the old URL.
5. In the IG's release notes, document the URL change as a
   substantive-but-non-semantic correction and provide a mapping table
   from old → new canonicals to aid implementers crossing the STU2 →
   next-release boundary.

#### Justification

This is the TSMG-endorsed pattern recorded in the *January 2022 TSMG
Internal CodeSystem Identifier Task Force* deck and codified as best
practice in the R5 terminologies page (`terminologies.html#system`). It
is also consistent with how every other HL7 IG-defined terminology has
been re-homed once the THO anchoring policy was finalized: keeping IG-
local code systems under the IG's own namespace creates URL collisions
across IG editions, makes cross-IG reuse awkward, and prevents central
governance / lookup via the THO terminology server. Filing the UTG
ticket alongside the IG change is the only way to actually make the new
canonicals resolvable.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Apply the THO re-anchoring **only to CodeSystems that have plausible
reuse outside US Specialty Rx**, and leave narrowly IG-internal
CodeSystems anchored at the IG's `hl7.org/fhir/...` namespace with an
explicit note in the IG narrative explaining the choice. Operationally:

1. Triage the IG's CodeSystems into two buckets:
   - **Reusable / cross-IG**: anything that other Pharmacy or non-
     Pharmacy IGs could legitimately bind to (e.g., status codes,
     reason codes, message-type codes).
   - **IG-local**: codes that exist only to satisfy the IG's own
     ValueSets and have no plausible reuse value.
2. File UTG tickets to migrate only the *reusable* bucket to THO under
   `terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/...`, mirroring Disposition A's
   migration steps for those CodeSystems only.
3. For the IG-local bucket, retain the existing `hl7.org/fhir/...`
   canonicals but add a CodeSystem-level extension or narrative note
   citing the TSMG guidance and the rationale for not migrating, so the
   choice is auditable.

#### Justification

This honours the spirit of the TSMG guidance (THO is the authoritative
home for *shared* HL7 terminology) while avoiding a UTG queue and
implementer-impact tax for codes that no one else will ever consume.
Re-homing every code, including ones that only exist to make a single
ValueSet bind in a single IG, can produce a flood of UTG submissions
and a wave of `system` URL changes for implementers that buys little
real-world reuse benefit. The trade-off vs. Disposition A is policy
purity vs. effort: TSMG's published policy is unconditional, so the
work group must be prepared to defend the IG-local exception
explicitly.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change as **out of scope for the existing US Specialty Rx
(FHIR) STU2 publication**, and instead defer any URL re-anchoring until
the next substantive ballot cycle of the IG, when the change can be
managed as part of a coordinated migration. Capture the THO anchoring
intent in the IG's roadmap / "future considerations" section so the
guidance is on record without taking action now.

#### Proposal rationale (category)

*Out of scope / timing* — not "the request lacks merit", but "the
disruption isn't justified mid-stream".

#### Justification

US Specialty Rx (FHIR) has already published STU2 with the existing
CodeSystem canonicals; changing those URLs is by definition a breaking
change for implementers that have hard-coded the old `system` values
in their conformance assets, validators, and persisted Codings.
Performing that migration as a hot-fix yields the worst of both worlds
— breakage **and** a half-finished migration if any CodeSystems are
missed. Lynn Laakso's 2023-05-04 re-open note ties the work to "post
STU2 publication," which is consistent with this view: it acknowledges
the TSMG direction but defers execution. Declining now (with the
roadmap entry) keeps STU2 stable and lets the next ballot cycle do the
re-anchoring cleanly with full migration tooling.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The TSMG CodeSystem Identifier policy, the R5 terminologies guidance,
and TSMG's standing direction to all IGs all point the same way:
IG-defined CodeSystems belong in THO, anchored under
`terminology.hl7.org`. The ticket itself was filed by Joan Harper
against this exact policy, and Lynn Laakso re-opened it after STU2
explicitly to keep it on the work plan rather than letting the
"Considered for Future Use" disposition stick. Disposition B's "only
the reusable ones" carve-out is tempting operationally but invites
ad-hoc judgements about reusability that the work group is unlikely
to want to defend publicly against TSMG; Disposition C preserves
short-term implementer stability but only delays the same migration
work and risks the IG accumulating more non-conforming canonicals in
the meantime. Accepting as requested — re-anchoring all IG-defined
CodeSystems and filing the UTG ticket(s) — aligns the IG with the
finalized policy, gives implementers a single coordinated cutover, and
lets Pharmacy close FHIR-38533 cleanly. The work group should pair the
acceptance with a published mapping table (old → new canonicals) and a
release-note callout to soften the migration for STU2 implementers.
