## Summary

The reporter suggests that the ballot for the (then-named) US Medication FHIR
Implementation Guide — which is the precursor to the published US Prescription
Drug Monitoring Program (PDMP) FHIR IG — should explicitly discuss the
distinction between **workflow integration** (how PDMP queries fit into a
clinician's prescribing/dispensing workflow, e.g., via SMART on FHIR launches,
EHR-embedded portals, or background NCPDP exchanges) and **discrete data
integration** (returning PDMP results as structured FHIR resources that can be
filed back into the EHR's clinical data store and reasoned over). The reporter
acknowledges this distinction may ultimately be out of scope for the IG, but
believes that calling it out would help implementers and reviewers understand
how the IG is meant to be used.

The ticket originated from a 2020 ballot, was once dispositioned "Considered
for Future Use" in January 2023, then reopened in November 2023 when the
PDMP-on-FHIR project (PSS-2225) was reactivated. The IG has since been
published as [STU1 in October
2024](http://hl7.org/fhir/us/pdmp/STU1), which means this ticket can now be
re-evaluated against the actually-published guide.

There are **no Augury-recorded cross-references** from this ticket to other
Jira issues, Zulip threads, GitHub items, or Confluence pages. However,
related Pharmacy/PDMP tickets identified by keyword similarity provide
useful context: [FHIR-46046](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-46046)
(Persuasive with Modification) asked the IG to clarify how it fits into the
existing US PDMP ecosystem (NCPDP exchanges vs. SMART/portal patterns) — the
same workflow-vs-discrete framing this ticket raises.
[FHIR-29459](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-29459) (Triaged) calls out that
NCPDP mappings differ depending on whether the requestor is a prescriber or
pharmacist, which is itself a workflow-context concern.
[FHIR-45871](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-45871) (Resolved – No Change)
asked for additional background framing of a different sort and was declined
on scope grounds — a useful precedent for how the WG has been handling
"please add more context to the IG" requests.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a short subsection to the **Background / Introduction** narrative of
the published US PDMP FHIR IG (`hl7.org/fhir/us/pdmp`) that explicitly
contrasts the two integration styles the IG enables or interoperates
with:

1. **Workflow integration** — the request/response pattern used at the
   point of care (typically a clinician triggers a PDMP query as part of
   the prescribing or dispensing workflow, results are reviewed in
   context, and only an attestation that the check occurred is recorded
   in the EHR). Describe how the IG's `$pdmp` request/response operations
   and SMART-launched contexts support this style.
2. **Discrete data integration** — returning PDMP results as structured
   FHIR `MedicationDispense` resources that the EHR can ingest, persist,
   and reason over alongside other clinical data. Describe how the
   profiles in this IG are designed for that style and reference the
   US Core MedicationDispense variance work that enabled it.

The new subsection should explicitly state which style each profile and
operation in the IG is intended to support, so implementers can choose
the right conformance target for their use case. No normative content
changes; pure narrative addition.

#### Justification

The reporter's concern is well founded: the published IG defines profiles
and operations that can be used in both styles, but does not currently
help an implementer decide which style applies to their deployment. This
is the same gap that FHIR-46046 ("clarify adoption plan given current US
ecosystem for PDMP integration") was found Persuasive with Modification
on, so accepting this ticket is consistent with how the WG has already
disposed of the most directly comparable request. The change is
narrative-only and therefore low risk: it does not alter conformance,
does not require re-balloting normative content, and can be folded into
the next published errata or STU update.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than adding workflow-vs-discrete framing to the **PDMP IG itself**,
add a short non-normative **"Integration Patterns" page** (or extend the
existing Use Cases / Background page) that catalogues the workflow
patterns this IG supports and explicitly cross-references the relevant
existing assets:

- The **NCPDP-to-FHIR mapping** material already in the IG (and discussed
  in the historical `us/meds` predecessor) for back-end exchanges.
- **SMART on FHIR / CDS Hooks** as the canonical way to surface PDMP
  results in EHR workflow.
- The **US Core MedicationDispense variance** that enables discrete-data
  filing of PDMP results.

Explicitly defer the deeper "intricacies" the reporter flagged
(prescriber-vs-pharmacist query differences — see FHIR-29459, attestation
recording, jurisdictional policy variance) to either out-of-scope notes
or to companion guidance, rather than expanding the IG's normative
surface.

#### Justification

This addresses the underlying need (helping implementers understand how
the IG is meant to be used) without committing the WG to maintaining
prose about every workflow nuance — which the reporter themself
acknowledged "may be out of scope for this IG." It also lets the WG
batch related concerns (FHIR-46046 ecosystem framing, FHIR-29459
prescriber/pharmacist query differences) into a single coherent
"Integration Patterns" page rather than treating each as an isolated
narrative tweak. Trade-off vs. Disposition A: marginally more authoring
effort up front, but less repeated re-litigation of "should we mention
X?" requests in future ballot cycles.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve as **Not Persuasive** on the grounds that the reporter themself
flagged the distinction as likely "out of scope for this IG", and that
the published US PDMP FHIR IG (STU1, October 2024) is intentionally
scoped to define the FHIR conformance artifacts (profiles, operations,
value sets) needed for PDMP exchanges, not to provide ecosystem-level
guidance on integration styles. Implementers seeking workflow-style
guidance can be directed to SMART App Launch and CDS Hooks; implementers
seeking discrete-data guidance already have the IG's profiles. No
content change to the published IG.

#### Justification

This is consistent with the WG's disposition of FHIR-45871 (also a
"please add more background context" request, resolved Not Persuasive on
the same IG). The ticket originates from a 2020 ballot of a withdrawn
predecessor IG (US Medication FHIR IG), and the concern was already
implicitly addressed by the IG team's choice to publish profiles
suitable for both styles. Adding narrative the reporter themselves
admitted may be out of scope risks bloating the IG without changing what
implementers actually build.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The reporter's concern is real — implementers reading the published PDMP
IG today do not get an explicit map of "use these artifacts for workflow
integration, use those artifacts for discrete-data integration", and the
WG has already accepted (FHIR-46046, Persuasive with Modification) that
the IG should be clearer about how it fits into the current US PDMP
ecosystem. So a flat decline (Disposition C) is hard to defend
consistently with FHIR-46046's outcome, even though it would mirror
FHIR-45871. At the same time, dropping the discussion straight into the
IG's Background section (Disposition A) risks pulling the IG into
narrative territory that the reporter themselves flagged as potentially
out of scope, and that overlaps with at least two other tickets
(FHIR-46046, FHIR-29459) that would each want a piece of the same
narrative. Disposition B threads that needle: it commits to the
narrative the workgroup has already agreed is needed (per FHIR-46046),
gives the WG a single page on which to land related ecosystem-positioning
tickets coherently, and explicitly defers the deeper intricacies the
reporter said "may be out of scope" to companion guidance — which is
exactly the boundary the reporter drew. The PDMP IG team should also
consider linking FHIR-29480 to FHIR-46046 and FHIR-29459 in Jira so the
three tickets can be dispositioned together against the same
"Integration Patterns" deliverable.
