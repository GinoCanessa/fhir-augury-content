## Summary

The reporter (Scott M. Robertson) is questioning the editorial value of including
mappings to NCPDP **SCRIPT** and NCPDP **PIMX** in the US Medication / PDMP FHIR
Implementation Guide. The reporter's view is that these mappings do not appear to
serve a concrete implementation purpose — they only demonstrate that the FHIR
content can correspond to fields in SCRIPT and PIMX. The question therefore is
whether to keep, scope down, or remove these mapping blocks from the IG.

The ticket was originally dispositioned **Considered for Future Use** on
2023-01-18 (vote 4-0-0, John Hatem / Tim McNeil) on the basis that the IG had
been withdrawn. It was subsequently **reopened on 2023-11-28** by the reporter
in connection with **PSS-2225 PDMP on FHIR**, which is the project sponsor's
move to revive the IG under the Pharmacy work group. The cross-reference index
returned no Jira/Zulip/GitHub links for this ticket, so the ticket needs to be
read in the context of the PSS and the IG itself rather than other tickets.

## Proposed Dispositions

> Note: because the cross-reference index returned no GitHub items for this
> ticket, none of the dispositions below are anchored to a specific repo
> authoring root or briefing. The Pharmacy WG should map any accepted change to
> the active US Medication / PDMP IG source repo at the time of action (the IG
> is being revived under PSS-2225).

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Remove the SCRIPT and PIMX mapping declarations from the US Medication / PDMP
IG. Concretely:

- Drop the `<mapping>` definitions whose `identity` corresponds to SCRIPT and
  PIMX from the IG-level `ImplementationGuide` resource (and from any IG-local
  `StructureDefinition.mapping` declarations that introduce these identities).
- Remove the corresponding `ElementDefinition.mapping` entries (those with
  `identity = "script"` / `identity = "pimx"` or whatever short codes the IG
  currently uses) from every profile and extension in the IG.
- Where the only purpose of a `StructureDefinition` was to carry the SCRIPT/PIMX
  mapping, evaluate whether the profile itself can be retired.
- Update the IG narrative ("Mappings" pages, comparison tables, change-history)
  to reflect the removal and to record that the IG no longer asserts a
  normative relationship to SCRIPT / PIMX.

#### Justification

The reporter is the originator of the SCRIPT/PIMX content in the IG and is
explicitly questioning its purpose. FHIR `mapping` blocks are intended to give
implementers an actionable bridge to other standards; "showcase" mappings that
are not used by tooling, do not drive conformance, and are not consumed by
implementers add maintenance burden (every spec change must re-justify the
mapping) without offsetting benefit. Removing them sharpens the IG's editorial
scope as it is being revived under PSS-2225 and avoids implying a normative
SCRIPT/PIMX alignment that the Pharmacy WG is not committing to maintain.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the SCRIPT and PIMX mappings in the IG, but **scope and contextualize**
them rather than remove them:

- Move the mapping content out of `ElementDefinition.mapping` blocks on every
  profile and into a dedicated **informative "Cross-standard correspondence"
  page** in the IG, marked clearly as non-normative.
- On that page, narrow the scope to the elements that are actually exchanged
  by PDMP workflows (those touched by PSS-2225). For each such element, give
  one row: FHIR path → SCRIPT segment/field → PIMX field → notes / caveats.
- In the `ImplementationGuide` resource, retain only those `<mapping>`
  identities that survive the trim, and add a `comment` on each one that
  describes the intended audience ("for implementers migrating from
  SCRIPT/PIMX-based PDMP submissions").
- Add a maintenance note that the Pharmacy WG owns the table and will refresh
  it per ballot cycle.

#### Justification

The reporter's complaint is "they don't appear to serve a purpose," not "they
are wrong." For a PDMP IG specifically, a curated, narrative-level
correspondence table to SCRIPT and PIMX *does* have a real audience — PDMP
implementers in the U.S. today are typically working from NCPDP-rooted systems
and migrating toward FHIR. Pulling the mappings out of the per-element machine
form (where they bloat every profile and imply a maintained normative
correspondence) and into a single informative page preserves the migration
value for that audience while removing the per-profile maintenance burden the
reporter is implicitly objecting to.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request. Keep the SCRIPT and PIMX `<mapping>` declarations and the
per-element `ElementDefinition.mapping` entries as they currently appear in the
US Medication / PDMP IG. Resolve as **Not Persuasive** with a note that
mappings to incumbent NCPDP standards are part of the IG's stated value
proposition for PDMP implementers and were authored deliberately.

#### Justification

If the Pharmacy WG, on revisiting the IG under PSS-2225, judges that explicit
SCRIPT/PIMX correspondence is a load-bearing feature for PDMP implementers
(rather than incidental documentation), the reporter's premise — "they don't
appear to serve a purpose" — does not hold and the right answer is to keep the
mappings and document the intent more clearly elsewhere. Removing them would
also be a visible scope reduction of a withdrawn-and-revived IG, which can be
politically costly with the NCPDP-aligned community even if technically
defensible.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The ticket is six years old and was reopened specifically because the IG is
being revived under PSS-2225, so the Pharmacy WG has a fresh opportunity to set
editorial scope rather than just answer the literal question. Outright removal
(A) discards real migration value for PDMP implementers — most of whom are
coming from NCPDP-rooted systems — and a flat decline (C) ignores the
reporter's substantive point that per-element SCRIPT/PIMX mapping rows on every
profile are high-maintenance and rarely consumed by tooling. Disposition B
splits the difference in the way that matches how mappings to legacy standards
are typically handled elsewhere in HL7: keep the correspondence as a curated,
informative cross-standard page, scoped to elements actually used in PDMP
workflows, and stop carrying the mappings as per-profile machine metadata. This
also gives PSS-2225 a clean editorial baseline going forward.
