## Summary

The reporter (YolandaLiu) flagged that the *Use Cases and Overview* page of
the US Standardized Medication Profile (SMP) IG
(`https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/smp-ig/usecases.html`) contains text that was
copy-pasted from the PACIO Personal Functioning and Engagement (PFE) IG and
was never adapted for the medication-list scope of SMP. Specifically:

- Section 2.2.1, paragraph 3 references "observational data related to
  mobility, self-care and domestic life", "performance of the same
  activities", and the SNF MDS "mobility and self-care functioning
  observation information" — all PFE concepts unrelated to a medication
  profile.
- Section 2.3, paragraph 3 references "results from the functional status
  assessment instruments" and "medical data" rather than medication data.
- The reporter also notes that medication reconciliation, which is core to
  SMP's scope, is barely mentioned in the body of the page and only appears
  at the end. Recommendation: lead with reconciliation scope at the top.

Resolution applied (per the resolution description on the ticket and the
merged PR `HL7/smp-ig#10`): "Remove the content copied from the PACIO PFE IG
and fixed the typos." The PR is titled "Address FHIR-53560, FHIR-53559,
FHIR-53558, and FHIR-53557" and was merged to `master` on 2026-02-27. There
are no Jira-to-Jira cross-references — the only cross-reference surfaced is
the GitHub PR. The companion narrative tickets (FHIR-53557/53558/53559) were
addressed by the same PR but are not formally cross-linked in Jira.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the resolution already merged in `HL7/smp-ig#10` as the disposition
for FHIR-53560:

1. **`input/pagecontent/usecases.md` §2.2.1 ¶3 (Setting B paragraph) —
   already merged.** Replace the PFE-derived sentence "The SNF shares
   information with Ms. Smith, her daughter, her physician and HCBS
   providers to support care transition and coordination." with the
   medication-scoped sentence "The SNF shares information, **including Ms.
   Smith's medications**, with Ms. Smith, her daughter, her physician and
   HCBS providers to support care **transitions** and coordination." (This
   is the actual change in PR #10 — the earlier "mobility, self-care and
   domestic life" sentence remains in the page; see Disposition B.)
2. **Remove orphan PFE page — already merged.** Delete
   `input/pagecontent/domains.md`, mark `<page key="domains">` as
   `deprecated="true"` in `FHIR-us-smp.xml`, and remove the `domains.md`
   entry from both `pages:` and `menu:` in `sushi-config.yaml`.
3. **Other narrative tightening — already merged.** Tighten the SMP Client
   `CapabilityStatement.description` in
   `input/fsh/smp-capability-client.fsh` and rephrase the "prior
   authorization requests and responses" / RESTful intro / Resource
   Structure paragraphs in `input/pagecontent/specification.md`.
4. **Reconciliation framing.** Defer the reporter's recommendation to lead
   `usecases.md` with medication-reconciliation scope — PR #10 does not
   address this.

#### Justification

The merged PR is a focused, narrative-only editorial fix that removes the
copy-paste-from-PFE residue the reporter flagged, consistent with the
ticket's pre-STU2-ballot label and the WG's existing "Persuasive"
resolution. The change set is small (+7/-35) and confined to authoring
sources under `input/pagecontent/`, `input/fsh/`, `sushi-config.yaml`, and
the IG resource — exactly the conventional editing surface for an HL7 FHIR
IG. The companion deprecation of `domains.md` is correctly mirrored in
both the SUSHI configuration and the IG XML, avoiding broken nav.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach (Accept with Additions)

#### Proposal

Accept PR #10 as the baseline, **and** open a follow-on PR (or amend before
publication) to fully address the reporter's ticket:

1. **Rewrite the PFE-derived sentence in §2.2.1 ¶3 that PR #10 left in
   place:** "Her previous observational data related to mobility, self-
   care and domestic life is shared with the hospital." This sentence is
   the *primary* example the reporter cited and it still ships verbatim in
   `usecases.md` after PR #10. Replace with a medication-relevant
   antecedent, e.g.: "Her current medication list — including prescription,
   over-the-counter, and as-needed medications — is shared with the
   hospital." Adjust the following sentence ("performance of the same
   activities") accordingly so the SNF transfer is described in terms of
   medications, not ICF activity domains.
2. **Rewrite §2.3 ¶3** so it no longer references "results from the
   functional status assessment instruments" or "medical data" — re-cast as
   retrieval of the patient's medication list / MedicationStatement /
   MedicationRequest history at Healthcare Setting A and B via the SMP
   profiles, citing the actual artifact names (`SMPMedicationList`,
   `SMPMedicationStatement`).
3. **Restructure `usecases.md`** to lead with a short "Scope: Medication
   Reconciliation Across Care Transitions" section before the PAC narrative
   walk-through, addressing the reporter's structural recommendation.
4. Capture the §2.3 and reconciliation-framing items as a follow-up Jira
   (or reopen FHIR-53560 with explicit "remaining items" comments) so the
   audit trail is preserved.

#### Justification

The "Persuasive" resolution and PR #10 fix two of the three concrete defects
named in the ticket (the orphan `domains.md` page and the §2.2.1 closing
sentence about "transitions of care"), but they leave the most-cited
PFE language ("mobility, self-care and domestic life", "performance of the
same activities", "functional status assessment instruments", "medical
data") in `usecases.md`. Closing the ticket without addressing those exact
phrases risks a follow-up ballot comment that re-files the same issue.
Disposition B preserves the merged work, costs a single additional editorial
PR, and fully realises the reporter's intent — including the structural
"reconciliation first" recommendation that PR #10 did not touch.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (No Further Action)

#### Proposal

Treat the issue as fully resolved by `HL7/smp-ig#10` and close FHIR-53560
without additional changes. Decline the reporter's structural
"reconciliation-first" recommendation and the residual §2.3 wording change
on the basis that the page now reads coherently after PR #10 and any
remaining PFE-flavoured language is acceptable narrative context for the
PAC use-case scenario.

#### Justification

This disposition is defensible if the WG concludes (a) the §2.2.1 sentences
about activities/observations are illustrative scene-setting rather than
normative IG content, (b) the §2.3 ¶3 wording, while imprecise, does not
mislead implementers because the surrounding profile references make the
medication scope clear, and (c) restructuring the page to lead with
medication-reconciliation scope is editorial polish best deferred to a
post-STU2 cycle. Declining further work avoids re-opening an already-merged
fix during the pre-STU2-ballot window.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **B (Accept with Additions).**

PR #10 is a clean, well-scoped editorial fix and should not be reverted —
but the diff demonstrably leaves the reporter's headline examples ("mobility,
self-care and domestic life", "performance of the same activities", and the
entire §2.3 ¶3 "functional status assessment instruments" / "medical data"
wording) untouched in `input/pagecontent/usecases.md`. Given the ticket is
labelled `pre-STU2-ballot` and the reporter explicitly called out those
phrases as PFE holdovers, closing on Disposition A risks a near-identical
ballot comment in the next cycle. The incremental cost — a single follow-up
editorial PR plus a short "Scope: Medication Reconciliation" lead-in — is
small relative to the credibility cost of shipping a Use Cases page that
still talks about ICF activity domains in a medication IG. Disposition C is
not defensible while those exact phrases remain on the page.
