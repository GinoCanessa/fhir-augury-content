## Summary

This ticket (originally surfaced as "Comment 9" imported by Ron G. Parker
against the US Specialty Rx (FHIR) IG) asks the Pharmacy work group to move
the canonical URL for the IG's ValueSet(s) from the `hl7.org/fhir` namespace
to the HL7 Terminology (THO) namespace at `terminology.hl7.org`. The reporter
notes that only ValueSets supporting a *required* binding to a `code` data
type should remain anchored under `hl7.org/fhir`; everything else should be
registered in THO via a companion UTG ticket. TSMG is named as the contact
for follow-up policy questions. No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip
threads, or GitHub items were found in the Augury index for this key, so
the ticket stands on its own in the available data.

## Details

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Move the canonical URL of every ValueSet defined in the US Specialty Rx
(FHIR) IG from the `http://hl7.org/fhir/us/specialty-rx/ValueSet/...` (or
equivalent IG-rooted) namespace to `http://terminology.hl7.org/ValueSet/...`,
except for ValueSets that are the target of a *required* binding to a `code`
data type, which remain under the IG-rooted namespace. Open a companion UTG
ticket to register each migrated ValueSet in THO so that the canonical
identifier resolves and is governed by Vocabulary. Update all bindings
inside the IG (StructureDefinitions, profiles, extensions, examples,
narrative) to point at the new THO canonical, and add a redirect / deprecation
note in the IG narrative for any external implementers that may have already
consumed the old URLs.

#### Justification

This is the action TSMG and Vocabulary have asked IGs to take, and is
consistent with the policy direction reflected in lynn_laakso's reopen
comment ("re-open post STU2 publication") and the prior committee discussion
about anchoring IG-defined terminology. Anchoring shared terminology in THO
gives the ValueSets a stable, version-managed home outside the IG release
cycle, makes them reusable by other IGs, and aligns US Specialty Rx with the
sibling NCPDP / Pharmacy IGs that have already begun migrating shared
ValueSets to THO. The carve-out for required `code` bindings preserves the
FHIR-spec rule that those enumerations are owned by the artifact that uses
them.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt the THO-anchoring direction in principle, but stage it: (1) inventory
every ValueSet currently defined in US Specialty Rx and classify each one as
"required-binding-to-code" (stays in IG), "shared / reusable" (move to THO),
or "IG-internal but not bound to `code`" (candidate for THO with a tracking
note). (2) For the "shared" set only, file UTG tickets, register in THO, and
re-point bindings in the next IG ballot cycle. (3) Defer the "IG-internal"
group until TSMG/Vocabulary publish the final policy referenced in the
2023-01-30 vote. Document the classification table in the IG so reviewers can
see which ValueSets are intentionally still IG-rooted.

#### Justification

The 2023-01-30 vote (6-0-0) explicitly conditioned this change on a finalized
TSMG policy on IG-defined terminology, and the most recent comment only
re-opened the ticket post-STU2 publication — it did not announce that the
policy is finalized. A staged migration captures the high-confidence wins
(genuinely shared ValueSets) without forcing premature canonical churn on
ValueSets whose home is still under policy debate. It also avoids breaking
implementers who are already consuming STU2 canonicals for the
IG-internal-only ValueSets, by limiting URL changes to the cases where
reusability clearly justifies the cost.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as "Considered for Future Use" (restoring the 2023-01-30 disposition)
on the grounds that the underlying TSMG policy on anchoring IG-defined
terminology is not yet finalized, and that re-anchoring ValueSets in THO is
a non-trivial breaking change to the canonical identifiers consumed by
existing US Specialty Rx STU2 implementers. Revisit when TSMG publishes the
governing policy or when a concrete cross-IG reuse case for a specific
ValueSet is brought forward.

#### Justification

The committee's original close on 2023-01-30 (6-0-0, Frank McKinney / John
Hatem) was explicitly tied to the maturity of the anchoring policy, and
nothing in the ticket history shows that policy has since been finalized;
the only later activity is a procedural re-open. Changing canonical URLs is
a backwards-incompatible change for downstream implementers (resolvers,
caches, conformance profiles that bind to the old URL), and doing it before
the governing policy is settled risks a second migration if the policy lands
differently than expected.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B threads the needle between the TSMG direction the ticket
requests (Disposition A) and the legitimate concern that drove the 2023-01-30
"Considered for Future Use" vote (Disposition C). The reopen comment shows
Pharmacy is no longer comfortable simply parking the ticket post-STU2, but
the absence of a finalized anchoring policy means a wholesale URL flip
(Disposition A) is likely to incur a second migration once the policy lands.
Producing a classified inventory and migrating only the clearly-shared
ValueSets in the next ballot — while filing the corresponding UTG tickets so
THO governance starts immediately — gives Pharmacy demonstrable progress on
the request, keeps the carve-out for required `code` bindings the ticket
explicitly calls out, and bounds the breaking-change surface for current
US Specialty Rx implementers.
