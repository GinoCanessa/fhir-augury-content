## Summary

This ticket asks the US Specialty Rx (FHIR) IG to relocate the canonical
URLs of its ValueSets from the `hl7.org/fhir` namespace to the HL7
Terminology (THO) namespace at `terminology.hl7.org`, on the policy
that IG-defined value sets should be registered in THO and only value
sets that are the target of a `required` binding to a `code`-typed
element should remain anchored under `hl7.org/fhir`. The reporter
indicates that a corresponding UTG ticket should be opened to register
each affected ValueSet in THO, and that TSMG should be consulted for
clarifications. The ticket title's trailing "8" reflects that this is
one of a series of comparable comments imported by Ron G. Parker into
the Pharmacy specifications. There are no Jira links, Zulip threads,
GitHub items, or other Augury-indexed cross-references against this
ticket key. The ticket was previously dispositioned "Considered for
Future Use" by a 6-0-0 vote (Frank McKinney / John Hatem) on
2023-01-30 pending finalization of the IG-defined-terminology anchoring
policy, then re-opened by Lynn Laakso on 2023-05-04 for handling
post-STU2 publication.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

For the US Specialty Rx (FHIR) IG (current Pharmacy WG STU/post-STU2
edition), enumerate every ValueSet defined by the IG and re-anchor its
`ValueSet.url` to a `http://terminology.hl7.org/ValueSet/{id}`
canonical, retaining only those ValueSets that are the target of a
`required` binding to a `code`-typed element under
`http://hl7.org/fhir/us/specialty-rx/ValueSet/{id}`. For each
relocated ValueSet, file a corresponding UTG ticket to register the
ValueSet (and any IG-defined CodeSystems it depends on) in THO so that
its canonical resolves under `terminology.hl7.org`. Update all
`ElementDefinition.binding.valueSet` references in the IG's profiles
and extensions to point at the new THO canonical, and document the
canonical move in the IG's change log. Coordinate the timing of the
THO publication and the IG republication so that bindings do not
dangle.

#### Justification

This is the policy stated by TSMG and applied uniformly across HL7
IGs: ValueSets that are not targets of a `required` binding to a
`code`-typed element are terminology artifacts whose lifecycle is
managed in THO via UTG, not in the IG's own publication. Following
the policy keeps US Specialty Rx aligned with FHIR Core, US Core, and
sibling Pharmacy IGs (where the same comment series has been applied),
makes the ValueSets discoverable through the standard terminology
infrastructure, and avoids the IG owning terminology content that
implementers would otherwise have to look up in two places. The
reporter (Joan Harper) and the WG's reviewer (Lynn Laakso) both
explicitly call for this change, and Lynn re-opened it specifically
to be actioned post-STU2.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Apply the THO anchoring rule selectively rather than wholesale:
re-anchor only those US Specialty Rx ValueSets that are (a) reused
across more than one HL7 specification, (b) bound at `extensible`,
`preferred`, or `example` strength, or (c) likely to be revised
independently of the IG's publication cadence. ValueSets that are
specific to US Specialty Rx, used only internally, and bound `required`
(even to non-`code`-typed elements such as `Coding`/`CodeableConcept`)
remain under `http://hl7.org/fhir/us/specialty-rx/ValueSet/{id}` and
ship with the IG. Document the selection criteria in the IG's
terminology section and file UTG tickets only for the relocated
artifacts.

#### Justification

The policy as stated draws the line at "required binding to a `code`
data type", but in practice the operationally important distinction
for IG implementers is whether a ValueSet has an independent lifecycle
or is tightly coupled to the IG's own profiles. A blanket move
introduces churn (every binding rewritten, every implementer's cached
canonical broken) for ValueSets that nobody else reuses and that
change only when the IG changes. This alternative honours the spirit
of the TSMG policy — terminology that has cross-specification reuse
goes to THO — while limiting the implementer-facing breakage. The
trade-off is that the IG diverges slightly from the strict letter of
the policy, which TSMG would need to bless.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as Persuasive with Mod or Not Persuasive on the
grounds that re-anchoring all non-`code`-bound ValueSets to THO is a
breaking change to every consumer of US Specialty Rx STU2 (cached
canonicals, validator manifests, profile binding URLs) and that the
underlying TSMG anchoring policy referenced in the request was not
finalized at the time the ticket was last reviewed (2023-01-30
"Considered for Future Use" vote, 6-0-0). Recommend that any
re-anchoring be deferred to a future major version of the IG and
handled in bulk alongside the broader Pharmacy IG terminology
realignment, rather than as an isolated change for this IG.

#### Justification

The original WG vote already reached this conclusion ("This change
will be considered when the policy related to anchoring of IG-defined
terminology is finalized"), and nothing in the Augury index shows that
the underlying policy has since been ratified or that the same change
has been applied uniformly across the rest of the Pharmacy IG family.
Acting on this single ticket in isolation risks an inconsistent
treatment across the Pharmacy specifications and forces implementers
to absorb a canonical URL break twice — once now and again when the
policy is finally formalized. Declining (or deferring) until TSMG's
policy is published, then doing the work as a coordinated sweep, is
the lower-risk path.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket has already been re-opened by the WG specifically to be
actioned post-STU2, the request restates settled TSMG terminology
governance policy rather than proposing something novel, and the same
remediation has been applied across other HL7 IGs (FHIR Core, US Core,
sibling Pharmacy work) without lasting harm. Disposition B is
appealing in principle but invites a per-ValueSet judgement call that
the WG would have to defend against TSMG, and Disposition C
reinstates a deferral the WG has explicitly chosen to lift. The
implementer-breakage concern raised in C is real but is best mitigated
inside Disposition A — by coordinating the THO/UTG publications with
the IG republication and by documenting the canonical move in the
change log — rather than by declining the request. The work plan
should be: (1) inventory US Specialty Rx ValueSets and classify each
by binding strength and target type, (2) file UTG tickets for every
ValueSet that does not meet the `required`-on-`code` carve-out, (3)
update the IG's bindings to the THO canonicals once UTG publishes, and
(4) note the change prominently in the next IG release.
