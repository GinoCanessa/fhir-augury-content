## Summary

The ticket reports a defect in **Example 2 of the *Dosage Regime Examples*
page (§14.7.1.3)** of the FHIR Core specification. The narrative for the
example states that the PRN evening 5 mg dose is capped at *2× per week*,
but the corresponding `Dosage.doseAndRate` component encodes the
`safety.doseLimit` with `scope = "administration"` and no `period` — which,
per §14.7.0.4 *DosageSafety*, means "no more than 2 tablets in a single
administration" rather than "no more than 2 tablets per week". The reporter
asks that the example be corrected so the doseLimit on that PRN component
uses `scope = "period"` with a `period` of 1 week, and provides the exact
XML fragment they would expect to see. They additionally note that
**Example 2 currently carries two `dosageInstruction.safety` elements**
when only one is intended, and that the duplicate must be removed.

There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items
for this issue in the FHIR Augury index.

## Details

**Description:**

> Example 2 (14.7.1.3) says "Methylphenidate 10 mg tablets — 10 mg at
> 07:30 and 12:00, 5 mg at 16:00 Mon-Fri; 10 mg at 08:00 Sat-Sun and
> holidays; 5 mg PRN after 17:00 *(max 2×/week)*; do not exceed 150 mg per
> week.".
>
> The last component in the step is meant to express " 5 mg PRN after
> 17:00 (max 2x/week)". However, `component.safety.doseLimit` in the
> example does not contain a period. The
> `component.safety.doseLimit.scope` is set to `"administration"`, which,
> according to 14.7.0.4 *DosageSafety*, defines a dose limit that applies
> for a single administration. This is interpreted as max 2 tablets for a
> single administration and not as max 2 tablets per week.
>
> The suggestion is to set the scope to period and add period to
> `safety.doseLimit` in this example. See below:
>
> ```xml
> <component>
>         <asNeeded value="true"/>
>         <timing>
>           <repeat>
>             <when value="EVE"/>
>           </repeat>
>         </timing>
>         <doseAndRate>
>           <doseQuantity>
>             <value value="5"/>
>             <unit value="mg"/>
>             <system value="http://unitsofmeasure.org"/>
>             <code value="mg"/>
>           </doseQuantity>
>         </doseAndRate>
>         <safety>
>           <doseLimit>
>             <valueQuantity>
>               <value value="2"/>
>               <unit value="tablet"/>
>               <system value="http://unitsofmeasure.org"/>
>               <code value="tbl"/>
>             </valueQuantity>
>             <scope value="period"/>
>             <period>
>               <value value="1"/>
>               <unit value="week"/>
>               <system value="http://unitsofmeasure.org"/>
>               <code value="wk"/>
>             </period>
>           </doseLimit>
>         </safety>
>       </component>
> ```
>
> Example 2 has two `dosageInstruction.safety` elements, this is double
> and has to be only one.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the reporter's two corrections to **Example 2** on the FHIR Core
*Dosage Regime Examples* page (§14.7.1.3):

1. In the PRN evening `<component>` (the one with `<asNeeded value="true"/>`
   and `<when value="EVE"/>`), replace the existing
   `safety.doseLimit` with the fragment shown in the ticket — i.e., set
   `Dosage.doseAndRate.component.safety.doseLimit.scope` to `"period"`
   (UCUM-coded) and add `Dosage.doseAndRate.component.safety.doseLimit.period`
   = `1 wk` (UCUM `wk` from `http://unitsofmeasure.org`). Keep the
   `valueQuantity` of `2 tablet` (UCUM `tbl`).
2. Remove the duplicate `dosageInstruction.safety` element from Example 2
   so that the `dosageInstruction` carries exactly one `safety` element,
   consistent with the structural intent of the example.

No change is proposed to the `Dosage` resource definition itself or to
§14.7.0.4 *DosageSafety*; this is purely an example-content fix.

#### Justification

The reporter has correctly diagnosed a semantic mismatch between the
narrative ("max 2×/week") and the encoded structure (a per-administration
dose limit with no period). Per §14.7.0.4, `doseLimit.scope = period`
**requires** a `period` element to be meaningful, so the
proposed `scope = "period"` + `period = 1 wk` pair is the minimal
correct encoding of the stated intent. The duplicate
`dosageInstruction.safety` is straightforwardly an authoring error in
the example XML and does not reflect any modeling intent. Both are
non-normative example fixes — they correct the documentation without
altering the resource model — so the change is low-risk and brings the
example into alignment with the surrounding guidance.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the reporter's intent (the example is wrong) but encode the cap
differently to better match how the rest of the regimen is specified:
attach the weekly limit at `dosageInstruction.maxDosePerPeriod`
(2 tablet / 1 wk) on the *step* containing this PRN component, rather
than on the PRN `component.safety.doseLimit` itself. In parallel, still
remove the duplicate `dosageInstruction.safety` element. The
`component.safety.doseLimit` on the PRN component would either be
omitted or retained only as a per-administration cap (e.g., 1 tablet
per administration, which matches the narrative's "5 mg PRN" single
dose).

#### Justification

The "do not exceed 150 mg per week" portion of the narrative is already
naturally a `dosageInstruction.maxDosePerPeriod`-style constraint, and
the PRN "max 2×/week" cap is conceptually closer to a regimen-level cap
on a specific component than to a per-administration safety limit.
Pushing the weekly cap up to `maxDosePerPeriod` keeps
`component.safety.doseLimit` reserved for true per-administration safety
(its primary documented use in §14.7.0.4) and demonstrates the broader
modeling pattern to readers. The trade-off is that this changes more
than the reporter asked for and slightly re-shapes what the example is
*demonstrating* — readers expecting a `component.safety.doseLimit` at
period scope (which the spec explicitly supports) would no longer see
that pattern in this example.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline as **Not Persuasive** on the grounds that the example, when read
together with §14.7.0.4 *DosageSafety*, can be interpreted by careful
implementers; close with a note that the narrative phrasing
"(max 2×/week)" is a documentation convenience and that
`component.safety.doseLimit.scope = "administration"` was intentionally
chosen to illustrate the per-administration form. (Including this
disposition for completeness — it is not the recommended outcome.)

#### Justification

A defensible-but-weak argument is that the *Dosage Regime Examples*
page is illustrative only, not normative, and that the surrounding
prose in §14.7.0.4 already explains the difference between
`administration` and `period` scope, so an attentive implementer would
recognise the encoded intent. There is no code or instance validation
broken by leaving the example as-is. The duplicate `safety` element is
harder to defend, however, and even a "decline" disposition would
likely carve out the duplicate-element fix as an editorial cleanup.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The defect is unambiguous: §14.7.0.4 explicitly defines
`scope = administration` as a per-administration cap, so encoding "max
2 tablets per week" with `scope = administration` is incorrect by the
spec's own definitions, and the duplicate `dosageInstruction.safety`
is a clear authoring error. The reporter has supplied a complete,
syntactically-valid replacement fragment that matches the documented
semantics of `doseLimit.scope = "period"` with an associated `period`,
and the change is confined to a non-normative example page. Disposition
B has merit as a modeling discussion but goes beyond what the ticket
asks for and would change *what* Example 2 is intended to teach;
that is better pursued as a separate editorial pass over the dosage
examples if the work group decides the regimen-level pattern should be
showcased. Disposition C is not defensible for the duplicate-element
issue and is weak even on the scope/period question. Accept as
requested, with the understanding that the fix is purely to the example
on §14.7.1.3 and does not touch the `Dosage` resource definition or
§14.7.0.4.
