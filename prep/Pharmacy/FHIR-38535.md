## Summary

This Pharmacy ticket, filed against the US Specialty Rx (FHIR) IG, requests
that any CodeSystem defined by the IG use a canonical URL anchored in
`terminology.hl7.org` (THO) rather than `hl7.org/fhir`. The reporter (on
behalf of TSMG) further requests that a corresponding UTG ticket be opened
to register the affected CodeSystem(s) in THO. The ticket cites the January
2022 TSMG CodeSystem Identifier Task Force guidance and the R5
terminologies guidance as the authoritative best-practice references. There
are no cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items in the
augury index for this issue; the only cross-reference is to a Confluence
attachment that is the TSMG best-practice deck itself. The ticket was
previously voted "Considered for Future Use" (Frank McKinney / John Hatem,
6-0-0, 2023-01-30) on the basis that the policy on anchoring IG-defined
terminology was not yet finalized, then re-opened post STU2 publication by
Lynn Laakso so the issue can be revisited under current TSMG policy.

## Details

The Code System canonical url should be anchored in terminology.hl7.org

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Re-anchor every CodeSystem defined by the US Specialty Rx (FHIR) IG so
that its `CodeSystem.url` (canonical) is under
`http://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/...` rather than
`http://hl7.org/fhir/...` (or any IG-local canonical base). For each
affected CodeSystem:

1. File the corresponding UTG ticket(s) to register the CodeSystem in THO
   (one UTG ticket per CodeSystem), including the chosen THO canonical
   URL, the CodeSystem metadata (name, title, description, content,
   experimental flag, copyright, jurisdiction), and the concept list.
2. Once UTG accepts the registration, change the IG's CodeSystem
   resource(s) so the canonical URL matches the THO-issued URL, update
   any `ValueSet.compose.include.system` references, any
   `Coding.system` examples/fixtures, and any narrative pages that cite
   the old canonical.
3. Add migration guidance to the IG's change log noting the canonical
   URL change and pointing implementers at the THO-published version.

#### Justification

This is exactly what TSMG policy (per the cited January 2022 TSMG
CodeSystem Identifier Task Force deck) and R5 terminologies guidance
prescribe: HL7-defined CodeSystems whose scope is broader than a single
IG should be canonicalized under THO so they are discoverable, versioned,
and governed centrally rather than being trapped under an IG-specific
URL. Re-opening post STU2 reflects that TSMG's anchoring policy is now
considered settled enough to act on. Doing the move and the UTG
registration in lock-step keeps the published canonical and the
authoritative terminology repository consistent for downstream
implementers.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Apply the THO anchoring rule **selectively**, only to CodeSystems that
are reusable outside the US Specialty Rx (FHIR) IG, and keep
truly-local, IG-specific CodeSystems anchored under the IG's canonical
base (with a clear `CodeSystem.jurisdiction` and a note in the IG's
terminology page explaining why each is local). For each CodeSystem,
the editors apply the TSMG decision tree from the referenced deck:

- If the CodeSystem encodes concepts that other IGs or specs are likely
  to reuse → file a UTG ticket and re-anchor under THO (per Disposition
  A).
- If the CodeSystem is genuinely IG-internal (e.g., a small enumeration
  used only by US Specialty Rx profiles) → leave it under the IG
  canonical, document the rationale, and add a `keyword` /
  `useContext` to make the IG-local scope explicit.

#### Justification

Not every CodeSystem belongs in THO; the TSMG guidance is intended to
prevent canonical-URL squatting on `hl7.org/fhir`, not to force every
small IG enumeration through UTG governance. A selective application
respects TSMG's policy intent while avoiding unnecessary UTG load and
the multi-month registration cycle for codes that no other artifact will
ever reference. It also gives the Pharmacy WG a defensible per-CodeSystem
rationale that survives future audits.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline as out of scope for the US Specialty Rx (FHIR) IG itself and
redirect to a single, IG-wide policy ticket under TSMG. Close FHIR-38535
as "Not Persuasive — already addressed by general HL7 policy," noting
that the THO-anchoring requirement is a cross-IG editorial rule that
should be enforced by the TSMG/FMG publication checklist, not by a
per-IG change request. Pharmacy would then adopt the THO-anchored
canonicals during the next normal IG republication cycle, driven by the
publication checklist rather than by this ticket.

#### Justification

The substance of the request is not specific to US Specialty Rx — every
HL7 IG that defines a CodeSystem under `hl7.org/fhir` is in the same
position. Resolving this as a per-IG change risks a patchwork where
some IGs comply and others don't, and it duplicates work that the
TSMG/FMG already own. Declining at the IG level and pushing the change
into the publication-checklist policy is the lower-cost,
higher-consistency path. (This is the rationale that backed the original
2023-01-30 "Considered for Future Use" vote.)

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The TSMG guidance the ticket cites is real and the re-open by Lynn
Laakso indicates the WG no longer wants to wait on policy finalization,
so a flat decline (C) is hard to defend a second time around — the
"policy not finalized" rationale that justified the prior CFFU vote no
longer holds. Conversely, accepting as-requested (A) without a
per-CodeSystem filter risks pushing IG-internal enumerations through
UTG, which is more governance overhead than the TSMG deck actually
asks for and will slow the next US Specialty Rx publication. Disposition
B threads that needle: it commits Pharmacy to applying the THO-anchoring
rule, files the UTG tickets where reuse is plausible, and documents the
reason any remaining CodeSystem is kept IG-local. That gives TSMG a
clear, auditable answer per CodeSystem and lets the WG move forward
without blocking on a generalized cross-IG policy ticket.
