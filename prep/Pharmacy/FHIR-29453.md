## Summary

The ticket is a narrative correction against the introductory text of the
US Prescription Drug Monitoring Program (PDMP) FHIR Implementation Guide
(tracked in Jira as the "US Medication FHIR Implementation Guide"
specification). The reporter points out that PDMPs do **not** track
controlled-substance *prescriptions*; they track controlled-substance
*dispenses*. The IG's introductory sentence —

> "In the United States, every state is deploying a Prescription Drug
> Monitoring Program (PDMP) which track controlled substance
> prescriptions within the state"

— is therefore factually wrong, and should be reworded to say "controlled
substance **dispenses**".

Two closely related Pharmacy/PDMP tickets give context for this request:

- [FHIR-29454](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-29454) — same reporter,
  same IG, makes the broader point that the PDMP IG should be focused on
  communicating *dispenses*, not *prescriptions*, and asks that an
  example query in the IG be reworded from "with a prescription from
  …" to "for medications dispensed from …". Resolved
  *Persuasive with Modification* with the resolution note "rewrite of IG
  addresses references to prescriptions vs dispenses".
- [FHIR-29467](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-29467) — same reporter,
  same sentence, fixes the subject/verb agreement ("track" → "tracks").
  Resolved *Persuasive* and marked *Applied*. Because both tickets
  target the same sentence, any rewording for FHIR-29453 must preserve
  the singular-verb fix from FHIR-29467.

The ticket was originally dispositioned *Considered for Future Use*
(2023-01-18, 4-0-0) when the IG was withdrawn, then re-opened on
2023-11-28 by Scott Robertson under PSS-2225 (the project to relaunch
the PDMP on FHIR IG) and given the current resolution
*Persuasive with Modification*. It now sits in **Triaged** status,
awaiting application of the agreed wording change in the next IG draft.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the wording fix exactly as proposed by the reporter, while
preserving the singular-verb correction already made under
[FHIR-29467](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-29467). In the
introductory section of the US Prescription Drug Monitoring Program
(PDMP) FHIR IG (Pharmacy work group, project PSS-2225), replace:

> "In the United States, every state is deploying a Prescription Drug
> Monitoring Program (PDMP) which tracks controlled substance
> **prescriptions** within the state."

with:

> "In the United States, every state is deploying a Prescription Drug
> Monitoring Program (PDMP) which tracks controlled substance
> **dispenses** within the state."

This is a narrative-only change to the IG's introduction page; no
StructureDefinition, ValueSet, search-parameter, or example artifact is
affected. The current resolution on the ticket already reads *Persuasive
with Modification — "Narrative updated"*, so this disposition simply
completes the agreed action.

#### Justification

PDMPs are statutorily defined data systems that collect
controlled-substance dispensing records reported by pharmacies; they do
not collect prescriptions at the point of prescribing. The existing
sentence is therefore factually wrong, and the proposed wording aligns
the IG's narrative with both reality and the IG's own technical model
(the IG conforms MedicationDispense, not MedicationRequest, as
reinforced by [FHIR-29454](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-29454),
[FHIR-29531](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-29531) and the Argonaut
US Core variance discussion). It is a low-risk editorial fix in the IG
prose, preserves the singular-verb correction from
[FHIR-29467](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-29467), and is consistent
with the broader prescription-vs-dispense cleanup the Pharmacy WG
agreed to under PSS-2225.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt the *intent* of the reporter's wording, but tighten the prose
further so it captures the regulatory nuance and is consistent across
the rest of the IG introduction. Suggested replacement sentence:

> "In the United States, each state operates a Prescription Drug
> Monitoring Program (PDMP) that collects records of controlled
> substance **dispenses** reported by dispensing pharmacies within the
> state."

In addition, sweep the introduction and overview pages of the PDMP IG
for any remaining occurrences of the words "prescription" / "prescribed"
where the actual subject is a dispensing event, and reword them in the
same pass — coordinated with the dispositions of
[FHIR-29454](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-29454),
[FHIR-29510](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-29510),
[FHIR-29531](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-29531) and
[FHIR-29535](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-29535) so the prose is
consistent end-to-end.

#### Justification

The reporter's verbatim wording fixes the headline error but leaves two
weaker phrasings: "every state is deploying" (most states are not
"deploying" — they have operated PDMPs for years), and the implicit
suggestion that the PDMP itself does the tracking, when in fact
pharmacies *report dispenses to* the PDMP. Doing the rewording once,
holistically, avoids re-touching the same paragraph for each related
PDMP-old-issues ticket and reduces the risk of further narrative
inconsistencies. The cost is slightly more editorial review and a
larger ballot diff than Disposition A.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change on the grounds that the IG is currently in a
withdrawn / relaunch posture under PSS-2225 and the introductory text
will be substantially rewritten as part of the next ballot draft, so a
targeted single-sentence patch is unnecessary. Mark the ticket
*Considered for Future Use* and roll the wording in as part of the
PSS-2225 rewrite.

#### Justification

This was, in fact, the original 2023-01-18 disposition (4-0-0, John
Hatem / Tim McNeil) before Scott Robertson re-opened the ticket on
2023-11-28. The argument is that any narrative-only fix landed against
the legacy IG text will be overwritten by the PSS-2225 rewrite and
therefore wastes review cycles.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **A**

The Pharmacy WG has already agreed in principle (resolution *Persuasive
with Modification*, "Narrative updated") that this change is correct
and should be made; the only outstanding action is to apply the agreed
wording. Disposition A is a one-line, low-risk narrative edit that
preserves the singular-verb fix from
[FHIR-29467](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-29467), is internally
consistent with the dispense-centric technical model of the IG, and is
trivially balloted. Disposition B is attractive editorially but
expands the scope of this single ticket and is better handled as a
coordinated pass across the PDMP-old-issues batch (FHIR-29454, -29510,
-29531, -29535) — which can be done on top of A, not instead of it.
Disposition C reverts to the previously-overruled "wait for the
rewrite" posture; given that PSS-2225 is now actively progressing the
IG to ballot (per the Argonaut variance discussion), it is cheaper to
land this fix now than to re-litigate it later. Apply A, and use the
opportunity to flag the broader Disposition-B sweep as a separate
editorial follow-up under PSS-2225.
