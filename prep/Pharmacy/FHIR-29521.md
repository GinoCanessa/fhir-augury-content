## Summary

This Pharmacy work-group ticket against the (since-renamed) US Prescription
Drug Monitoring Program (PDMP) FHIR Implementation Guide — now tracked as the
US Medication FHIR Implementation Guide — challenges a deployment option in
which a PDMP requester would have to query each individual State PDMP system
directly and one at a time. The reporter (Molly Reese) argues that requiring
clinicians/EHRs to fan out queries across many state-level PDMP responders is
operationally burdensome and points to a previously-listed "Option 2"
(intermediary/hub-based query distribution) as the preferable alternative.
The ticket targets the IG's deployment-options narrative where State PDMP
Systems are described as the PDMP Responders exposing the FHIR APIs.

The ticket was originally resolved "Considered for Future Use" on 2023-01-18
when the IG was withdrawn, then re-opened on 2023-11-28 under PSS-2225 ("PDMP
on FHIR") so it could be re-considered as the project resumed. It was finally
closed **Persuasive with Modification** on 2023-11-29 with the resolution
that multi-state PDMP queries are, in practice, handled by intermediaries,
hubs, or state systems themselves, and the per-state-fan-out deployment
option has been removed from the revised IG. No related Jira tickets, Zulip
discussions, or GitHub items were surfaced by the cross-reference index.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the change implied by the ticket: in the US Medication FHIR IG (the
successor to the US PDMP FHIR IG), **remove the deployment option that
positions individual State PDMP Systems as the PDMP Responders queried one
at a time by the requester**. Update the IG's "Deployment Options" /
"Actors" narrative so that the canonical PDMP Responder is an
aggregator/hub/intermediary (or a state system that itself federates to its
peers), and revise the existing wording "*State PDMP Systems which act as
the PDMP Responders using the FHIR APIs*" accordingly. Add a note explaining
that multi-jurisdictional queries are coordinated server-side rather than
fanned out by the requester.

#### Justification

This matches the resolution recorded on the ticket ("Persuasive with
Modification … This deployment option has been removed from the revised
IG") and the operational reality stated in the resolution: PDMP
administrators and vendors today route multi-state queries through
intermediaries, hubs, or state systems. Forcing requesters to perform N
sequential per-state calls would multiply implementer burden, complicate
patient matching/consent handling across jurisdictions, and create
inconsistent latency/error semantics — none of which are desirable in a
clinical workflow where the responder is consulted at the point of
prescribing or dispensing.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than deleting the per-state deployment option outright, **retain it
as a non-normative, optional pattern** but designate the
intermediary/hub-mediated pattern as the **recommended** deployment.
Concretely:

- Define a single PDMP Responder actor whose conformance is independent of
  whether it is implemented by a state system, a multi-state hub, or a
  national intermediary.
- Add an informative deployment-topology section that documents both
  topologies (direct-to-state and via-intermediary), with guidance that
  requesters SHOULD treat the Responder as opaque and SHOULD NOT assume
  geographic scoping.
- Capture cross-jurisdiction concerns (consent, identity matching,
  audit/disclosure) as conformance requirements on the Responder rather
  than on the Requester.

#### Justification

This preserves implementation flexibility for jurisdictions that do not yet
participate in a hub while still steering the ecosystem toward the less
burdensome topology. It also avoids the IG taking a position on the
commercial/regulatory architecture of interstate PDMP exchange (PMIX,
RxCheck, NarxCare-style hubs, etc.), which can vary by state law. The
trade-off versus Disposition A is a longer, more nuanced narrative and the
risk that some implementers continue to build the burdensome pattern; that
risk is mitigated by clearly labelling the recommended topology and by
holding conformance at the Responder rather than the Requester.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change and keep the original wording that names State PDMP
Systems as the PDMP Responders, on the basis that the IG should describe
the regulatory reality (PDMPs are state-operated) rather than a particular
deployment convenience, and that requester-side fan-out is an
implementation concern outside the IG's scope.

#### Justification

Defensible only if the work group concludes that (a) the FHIR IG is purely
an API contract and is silent on deployment topology, and (b) hub/
intermediary behaviour is out of scope for HL7 and belongs to NABP / state
PMP administrators. This rationale is undermined by the fact that the
ticket *was* resolved Persuasive with Modification and the option *has*
been removed from the revised IG — so declining now would re-open a
settled disposition without new evidence.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket has already been resolved Persuasive with Modification, and the
resolution text confirms that PDMP administrators and vendors operate
multi-state queries through intermediaries, hubs, or the state systems
themselves. Disposition A simply ratifies the change that the work group
agreed to make and that the revised US Medication FHIR IG already reflects,
which is the cleanest path: it removes a deployment option that no real
deployment uses, eliminates an avoidable burden on requesters, and aligns
the IG with the actual interstate PDMP exchange architecture. Disposition B
is a reasonable fallback if any state(s) currently expose a direct,
non-federated FHIR endpoint and want it to remain conformant; if the work
group has evidence of such a deployment, B should be preferred over A.
Disposition C should not be adopted absent new use cases that contradict
the resolution discussion.
