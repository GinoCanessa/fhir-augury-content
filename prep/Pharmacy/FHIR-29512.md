## Summary

This ticket requests a small narrative edit to the US Prescription Drug
Monitoring Program (PDMP) FHIR Implementation Guide (tracked under the
"US Medication FHIR Implementation Guide" specification in Jira). The
reporter (Molly Reese) proposes two changes to a single sentence in a
use-case/scenario paragraph: a grammar polish ("has to interact" →
"must interact"; "and access" → "to access") and the addition of an
explicit acknowledgement that leaving the EHR workflow to reach a
separate PDMP web portal is burdensome to physicians. No structural
spec change (no resource, profile, element, or value-set change) is
requested — this is purely an editorial/narrative correction. The
ticket was originally dispositioned in 2023-01-18 as "Considered for
Future Use" because the IG had been withdrawn, and was then reopened
by smrobertson on 2023-11-28 in conjunction with PSS-2225 (PDMP on
FHIR), which restarted work on the IG. There are no cross-referenced
Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items associated with this
ticket in the knowledge base.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the reporter's proposed wording verbatim to the affected
narrative paragraph in the US PDMP FHIR Implementation Guide. Replace:

> "In this case, the Practitioner has to interact with a separate web
> portal not integrated with the EHR and access the PDMP data"

with:

> "In this case, the Practitioner must interact with a separate web
> portal not integrated with the EHR to access the PDMP data. This is
> burdensome to physicians as it takes them out of the EHR workflow."

This is a pure narrative edit — no profiles, extensions, value sets,
operations, or examples are affected. The change should be applied to
the PDMP IG source page that contains the existing sentence (the
ticket does not name the page, but the paragraph is part of the
use-case/motivation narrative referenced in the IG's Confluence
landing page).

#### Justification

The existing wording is grammatically awkward ("has to interact …
and access" reads as a compound action rather than a purpose clause).
The proposed wording fixes the grammar and adds important context
that motivates the IG's existence: the burden of context-switching
out of the EHR to a separate PDMP portal is precisely the problem the
PDMP-on-FHIR project is intended to solve, so calling it out
explicitly in the use-case strengthens the narrative. The change is
zero-risk: it is purely editorial, has no normative impact, does not
affect conformance, and does not alter examples or schemas. The
reporter is the original author of the wording context, and the
ticket is marked Highest priority, suggesting the workgroup considers
the editorial clarity worth fixing once authoring resumes.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the spirit of the change but tighten and neutralize the
language so that it reads as a factual problem statement rather than
an editorial assertion. Suggested wording:

> "In this case, the Practitioner must interact with a separate web
> portal that is not integrated with the EHR in order to access the
> PDMP data, requiring them to leave the EHR workflow."

This keeps the grammar fix and preserves the workflow-burden point,
but drops the value-laden word "burdensome" in favor of the concrete
behavior ("requiring them to leave the EHR workflow"). Optionally,
add a single-sentence forward reference such as "This use case
motivates the PDMP-on-FHIR exchange patterns described in the
following sections." to anchor the paragraph to the rest of the IG.

#### Justification

IG narrative tends to age better when it describes observable
behavior rather than asserting a subjective experience. "Burdensome
to physicians" is defensible but invites debate (which physicians?
how burdensome?) without adding technical content. The behavioral
phrasing carries the same motivational weight while staying neutral,
and the optional forward reference helps a reader understand why the
paragraph is in the IG at all. Trade-off vs. Disposition A: this
deviates slightly from the reporter's exact wording, but it
accomplishes the same goal and is easier to defend in ballot
reconciliation if anyone objects to the editorializing tone.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Not Persuasive — editorial / no normative
impact**, leaving the existing sentence in place. Document that the
existing wording, while imperfect, is understandable and that
narrative polish should not be tracked as Highest-priority change
requests against the IG.

#### Justification

The existing sentence, though awkward, is comprehensible and conveys
the same information as the proposed wording. The IG was withdrawn
once already and only recently reopened under PSS-2225; scarce
workgroup cycles are better spent on substantive (normative) work
than on grammar polish. There is also a mild risk of scope creep: if
"burdensome to physicians" is added, reviewers may reasonably ask for
citations or balancing language about portal-access tradeoffs, which
turns a one-line edit into a discussion. Declining keeps the IG
neutral and avoids the discussion entirely.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

This is a literal one-paragraph editorial fix from the original
reporter, the change is technically zero-risk, and the new wording
both repairs the grammar and reinforces the IG's motivating use case
— exactly the kind of low-cost narrative improvement that should be
made when authoring on the IG resumes under PSS-2225. The neutral
phrasing in Disposition B is reasonable and the workgroup may prefer
it on stylistic grounds, but the difference is small enough that it
isn't worth declining the reporter's suggestion outright. Disposition
C is hard to justify when the ticket is explicitly Highest priority
and the cost to apply is effectively zero. Recommend accepting as
requested, with the workgroup reserving the option to soften
"burdensome to physicians" to behavioral phrasing (per Disposition B)
during final editorial review.
