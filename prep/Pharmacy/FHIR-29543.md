## Summary

The ticket asks the Pharmacy work group to tighten the must-support flags on
the US PDMP `MedicationAdministration` profile. As filed, the profile flags
`MedicationAdministration.dosage` as must-support but leaves all of its
child elements optional and un-flagged. The reporter (Rick Geimer) argues
that at minimum `dosage.text` — the human-readable SIG — should also be
must-support so that implementers consistently surface the dosing
instructions that pharmacists and PDMP consumers rely on. The ticket was
originally deferred ("Considered for Future Use") in January 2023 because
the IG had been withdrawn, but it was reopened in November 2023 when the
PDMP on FHIR project (PSS-2225) picked the IG back up, and it has since
been resolved "Persuasive with Modification" with a broader change than
literally requested. No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or
GitHub items were surfaced for this issue.

## Details

**Description:**

> The MedicationAdministration profile has dosage as must-support, but no
> such flags on any child content (which are all optional). Suggest making
> at least dosage.text (i.e. the SIG) must-support as well.
>
> ### Existing Wording:
>
> dosage SI 0..1

**Resolution (Persuasive with Modification):**

> MedicationAdministration.dosage is now must-support and required (1..1),
> .dosage.text is must-support and optional (0..1) and dosage.dose is
> must-support and required (1..1).

## Proposed Dispositions

> Note: This ticket has already been resolved "Persuasive with
> Modification" by Pharmacy. The dispositions below frame the choices
> the work group effectively faced (and may revisit if the resolution is
> reopened a second time). All proposals target the US PDMP IG profile on
> `MedicationAdministration`; no related FHIR core repo was surfaced by
> the cross-reference query, so authoring root and file paths cannot be
> named from a repo briefing.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the US PDMP `MedicationAdministration` profile, add a must-support
flag to `MedicationAdministration.dosage.text` while leaving the parent
`dosage` element's existing must-support and 0..1 cardinality unchanged
and leaving the other `dosage.*` children unflagged. No cardinality
changes; only the must-support flag on `dosage.text` is added so that
the SIG, when present, must be supported by conformant systems.

#### Justification

This is the minimum, surgical change the reporter asked for: it
addresses the inconsistency of flagging a complex parent element
must-support without flagging any of its children, and it picks the one
child (`text`/SIG) that PDMP stakeholders most directly depend on to
reconcile dosing across prescribers and dispensers. It avoids the
broader scope and the backward-compatibility risk of changing
cardinalities on `dosage` itself, which is more typical of "minimal
must-support clarification" tickets in IGs that already have implementer
adoption.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Tighten the profile more aggressively: make
`MedicationAdministration.dosage` must-support **and** required
(`1..1`), make `dosage.text` must-support and keep it optional
(`0..1`), and make `dosage.dose` must-support and required (`1..1`).
This is exactly what the work group ultimately resolved.

#### Justification

PDMP's value proposition is reconciliation of administered medication
quantities across systems, which only works if the structured dose
(`dosage.dose`) is consistently present and supported, with the SIG
(`dosage.text`) available when it adds clinical clarity. Bumping
`dosage` to `1..1` is reasonable in a use-case-specific IG profile of
`MedicationAdministration` because PDMP records without dosing
information have low utility. The trade-off is breakage for any
implementer who was producing PDMP `MedicationAdministration` instances
without `dosage` or without `dosage.dose`; given that the IG had been
withdrawn and is being relaunched under PSS-2225, this is a low-cost
moment to enforce that constraint before re-balloting.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change and leave the US PDMP `MedicationAdministration`
profile's `dosage` flag as must-support with no must-support flags on
its children. Rationale: must-support semantics already require
implementers to support the full element when it is populated, and
implementers can rely on profile guidance text rather than per-child
flags.

#### Justification

This is the weakest of the three options because FHIR's must-support
semantics on a complex parent are routinely ambiguous in practice —
implementers commonly read "support the parent" as "be able to round-trip
the element" without committing to any specific child. Declining would
also conflict with the explicit reopening under PSS-2225, which signals
that PDMP stakeholders want the children clarified. A defensible decline
rationale exists only if the work group concludes that the broader PDMP
re-launch will redesign dosing capture (for example, by referencing the
US Medication List dosing pattern) and that per-child must-support flags
should wait for that redesign rather than be added now.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B matches the resolution Pharmacy actually adopted
("Persuasive with Modification") and is the right call. The original
request (Disposition A) correctly identifies the ambiguity around an
unflagged child of a must-support complex element, but it under-reaches
for a PDMP-specific profile: a `MedicationAdministration` record without
a structured dose has very limited value in a controlled-substance
monitoring context, so requiring `dosage` (1..1) and `dosage.dose` (1..1)
while keeping `dosage.text` must-support but optional gives consumers a
machine-readable dose every time and a SIG whenever the source system has
one. Because the IG is being reopened under PSS-2225 ahead of a fresh
ballot, the implementer-impact cost of tightening cardinalities now is
minimal compared with locking in the same loose constraints for another
cycle. Disposition C should be set aside unless the work group decides
to redesign PDMP dosing capture wholesale.
