## Summary

Stephen Chu (Pharmacy WG) asks the editors to extend MedicationStatement's
prose — Scope-and-Usage, plus the `Comments` fields on `medication`,
`status`, and `effective[x]` — to make the newly-introduced "no known
medication" use case implementable and unambiguous. The ticket is a
direct follow-on to [FHIR-53425](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53425)
(Jose Costa-Teixeira, Applied / Persuasive with Modification), which the
Pharmacy WG resolved by allowing MedicationStatement to express "no
medications are known / taken" via a coded `medication` (e.g., SNOMED CT
`787481004 |No known medications|`) plus a Note-to-Balloters flagging
that this approach is provisional. FHIR-54315 closes the gap that
FHIR-53425 left open: it does not propose a structural change, only
narrative additions specifying (a) Scope-and-Usage examples, (b) when a
"no known" code applies globally vs. to a specific product/class, (c)
how to interpret `status = active` for such an assertion, and (d) how
`effective[x]` should be read (point-in-time vs. period, and what
"unvalued" means).

The request sits inside a broader, contested R6 conversation. Closely
related tickets include
[FHIR-52286](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-52286) /
[FHIR-52274](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-52274) (Danielle
Tavares-Rixon, Resolved – Persuasive with Modification, AU FHIR) which
asked Pharmacy to adopt the IPS pattern;
[FHIR-54126](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54126) (Brett Marquard,
Triaged) arguing the current "no medications are known / taken" wording
is not publication-ready and conflates "known" vs. "taken";
[FHIR-54566](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54566) (Chris Moesel,
Triaged) asking how a "no known medications" code interacts with
`category`, `dosage`, and other elements; and
[FHIR-53171](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53171) (Patient Care)
asking for parallel clarification in MedicationRequest(IPS) /
MedicationStatement(IPS). FHIR-54315 should be dispositioned together
with these so the prose is internally consistent.

## Details

**Description:**

> **Note to Balloters** has this statement: The
> MedicationStatement.medication element now explicitly supports
> representing statements such as 'no medications are known / taken.'
>
> Given this expanded scope of use, some changes need to be considered
> to improve clarity and facilitate correct usage for this "no known
> medication" use case.
>
> Suggested changes:
>
> **(1) Scope and usage section:** Common usage examples — add "no
> known medication".
>
> **(2) Scope and usage section** — include narrative texts, e.g.: The
> MedicationStatement scope and usage has been expanded to support "no
> known medication". In this use case, (a) MedicationStatement.medication
> – CodeableConcept may include a SNOMED CT code, e.g.
> `787481004 |No known medications|`, `1234391000168107 |No known
> current medication|`.
>
> **(3) MedicationStatement.medication** Comments field — add: If it is
> a global statement about "no known medication", the
> MedicationStatement.medication element should not identify a specific
> product or a specific class of product. In the use case of a negation
> statement about a specific product or a class of product, this
> element should contain a "no known" code for that specific product or
> class of product.
>
> **(4) MedicationStatement.status** Comments field — add: In the
> context of "no known medication" use case, it represents the status
> of the "no known medication" statement at the point when it was
> asserted. The value set for `MedicationStatement.status` contains a
> limited set of `active | entered-in-error | unknown`.
>
> **(5) MedicationStatement.effective[x]** Comments field — add: In
> the context of "no known medication" use case, this attribute
> reflects the dateTime or the period/duration over which no
> information is known about the patient taking any medications. If
> this data element is unvalued / unpopulated, no inference can be made
> about the timing on the "no known medication" assertion.

**Comments:** None recorded on the ticket itself. The substantive
discussion is on Zulip (see below) and on
[FHIR-53425](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53425).

## Related Zulip Discussions

### [Medication > No current medications](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/Medication/topic/No%20current%20medications)

The 60-message Pharmacy/cross-WG thread that produced FHIR-53425 and,
in turn, FHIR-54315. Heath Frankel opened it (2025-10-12) asking how
AU Core should record "no current medications" and whether
`787481004 |No known medications|` belongs on MedicationRequest or
MedicationStatement. Jose Costa Teixeira (IHE / Pharmacy) argued the
output of an EHR summary should always be `MedicationStatement`, and
that US Core's choice of MedicationRequest is an accident worth
revisiting. Peter Jordan (NZ) and Heath debated alignment with IPS
versus alignment with national base/core IGs. Jean Duteau noted that
in Canada "no meds / no allergies" was historically recorded as
`Observation` — Jose initially sympathised but conceded
MedicationStatement is the pragmatic vehicle.

On 2025-11-12 Jose summarised the Pharmacy-WG-Monday discussion of
FHIR-53425 and proposed four options (allergyIntolerance-style coded
negation; DAR on MedicationStatement; dedicated Observation; new
modifier element à la `doNotPerform`). Grahame Grieve summarised the
agreed direction — "change the definition to allow no known medication,
extend the example value set, and add a note that we do not recommend
this approach" — explicitly justifying it as procedurally easier to do
now and revise in ballot. Stephen Chu agreed it was procedurally
expedient but flagged discomfort, and on 2025-11-18/19 expanded that
discomfort into the substantive argument that FHIR-54315 now formalises:
a `MedicationStatement` with `status = active` plus an
`effectiveDateTime` is point-in-time, but a "no known medication"
assertion needs explicit semantics so CDS engines can reason about
currency. Grahame pushed back that the same currency problem applies to
all clinical statements and that the only durable answer is best-practice
guidance to populate `dateAsserted` and/or `effective[x]`. Heath
Frankel reported AU PS's interim resolution: use SNOMED-AU
`1234391000168107 |No known current medicines|` on
`MedicationStatement` with `effectiveDateTime` set and `status = active`.

**Net of the thread:** broad agreement that MedicationStatement is the
vehicle (Observation rejected as impractical given installed base);
agreement that the current ballot prose is insufficient and must be
extended; disagreement on how strongly to flag the approach as
provisional and on whether to recommend a SCT-AU vs. an international
SNOMED concept. No consensus that a structural change (new resource,
modifier element, rename to `.code`) is needed for R6.

Participants: Heath Frankel, Jose Costa Teixeira, Peter Jordan, Jean
Duteau, Rutt Lindström, Grahame Grieve, Stephen Chu, Lloyd McKenzie,
Eric Haas (mentioned), John D'Amore (mentioned), Rob Hausam
(mentioned).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply all five narrative additions as worded in the ticket, in
`HL7/fhir`'s MedicationStatement spec (Scope-and-Usage page and the
element-definition Comments for `medication`, `status`, and
`effective[x]`):

1. Add "no known medication" to the Common Usage Examples list in
   Scope-and-Usage.
2. Add a Scope-and-Usage paragraph explaining the expanded scope and
   pointing to `787481004 |No known medications|` and
   `1234391000168107 |No known current medication|` as illustrative
   SNOMED codings.
3. Extend `MedicationStatement.medication` Comments to distinguish a
   global "no known medication" assertion (must not name a specific
   product/class) from a scoped "no known X" assertion (must carry a
   "no known" code for that specific product/class).
4. Extend `MedicationStatement.status` Comments to clarify that, in
   this use case, `status` reflects the status of the assertion at the
   moment it was made, against the existing `active | entered-in-error
   | unknown` value set.
5. Extend `MedicationStatement.effective[x]` Comments to clarify that
   `effective[x]` describes the dateTime / period over which "no
   known medication" is being asserted, and that an absent
   `effective[x]` does not license any inference about timing.

Cross-reference the new prose to the IPS-aligned wording targeted by
[FHIR-53171](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53171) so core and IPS
profile prose stay aligned.

#### Justification

The Pharmacy WG already accepted the underlying expansion via
[FHIR-53425](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53425) and explicitly
acknowledged on the Zulip thread (Grahame Grieve, 2025-11-13) that the
narrative was overdue. The five edits are surgical, do not change
structure, fit cleanly inside the FhirCore "narrative / prose" change
recipe (edit `source/<resource>/` content directly; no
StructureDefinition snapshot regeneration), and address documented
implementer ambiguity in AU Core, AU PS, IPS, and US Core threads.
Adopting them now also satisfies Grahame's stated criterion that it is
procedurally easier to ship guidance for ballot and revise it than to
omit it.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the *intent* of FHIR-54315 but **rewrite** the proposed text
rather than adopt it verbatim, and **bundle** the disposition with
FHIR-54126, FHIR-54566, FHIR-54453, and the IPS-side FHIR-53171:

- Tighten the value-set / wording split per FHIR-54126: Scope-and-Usage
  and `medication` Comments must distinguish "no known" from "not
  taken"; if only "No known medications" is in the example value set,
  the prose must not promise "no medications are known / taken". Add a
  new example concept for "not taken" or remove that phrase.
- Address FHIR-54566 in `medication` Comments: state explicitly whether
  populating `category`, `dosage`, `reasonReference`, etc. alongside a
  "no known" code is permitted, qualifying, or contradictory; strongly
  recommend leaving those elements absent for a global negation.
- Replace FHIR-54315 item (5) with the best-practice statement Grahame
  Grieve articulated on Zulip: a "no known medication" assertion
  **SHOULD** populate `dateAsserted` and **SHOULD** populate
  `effective[x]` (dateTime preferred); also resolve the FHIR-54453
  question by either removing `Timing` from `effective[x]` or
  documenting that `Timing` is not applicable to a negation assertion.
- Mirror the agreed wording in MedicationRequest(IPS) /
  MedicationStatement(IPS) per FHIR-53171.
- Retain (and sharpen) the Note-to-Balloters that this pattern is
  provisional, naming the alternatives the WG considered (new modifier
  element; dedicated nil-assertion resource) and inviting ballot
  comment.

#### Justification

The Zulip thread shows the requested wording is *necessary but not
sufficient*: Brett Marquard (FHIR-54126) and Chris Moesel (FHIR-54566)
have already raised gaps that FHIR-54315 alone does not close, and
Eric Haas (FHIR-54453) has raised a `effective[x]` problem that
directly intersects FHIR-54315 item (5). Shipping FHIR-54315's text
verbatim without integrating those tickets risks immediate ballot
re-comment. A coordinated wording pass also avoids three or four
overlapping prose edits to the same Comments fields landing in
different commits. Disposition B trades a small amount of WG cycle
time for substantially higher ballot-survival odds and avoids leaving
the Note-to-Balloters as the only safety net.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Withdraw the FHIR-53425 change, remove the Note-to-Balloters
sentence, and decline FHIR-54315 as a downstream consequence — i.e.,
do **not** repurpose `MedicationStatement` for "no known medication"
assertions in R6, and direct implementers to existing mechanisms
(SNOMED-coded `Observation`, AllergyIntolerance-style coded negation
on a future "no known X" resource, or a `dataAbsentReason` extension on
MedicationStatement) until a structural answer is available.

#### Justification

A defensible-but-minority position, articulated on Zulip by Stephen
Chu and partly echoed by Jean Duteau and Jose Costa Teixeira: a
"no known medication" assertion is semantically a finding/observation,
not a statement of medication use, and overloading
`MedicationStatement` for both creates persistent CDS ambiguity around
`status = active` plus a stale `effectiveDateTime`. Declining now
preserves the option to design a clean nil-assertion mechanism for a
later release. The downside — and the reason this is an unlikely
pick — is that AU Core, AU PS, and IPS have already adopted the
MedicationStatement pattern in production guidance, and Grahame
Grieve's procedural argument (it is easier to ballot and revise than
to omit) carries weight with the Pharmacy WG.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The substantive request in FHIR-54315 is correct and the WG has
already committed (via FHIR-53425) to the MedicationStatement-based
pattern, so Disposition C is at odds with the WG's decision and with
deployed AU/IPS guidance. Disposition A would land the ticket but
guarantees a second prose pass during ballot once FHIR-54126,
FHIR-54566, FHIR-54453, and FHIR-53171 are dispositioned, because all
four touch the same Comments fields and the same Scope-and-Usage
paragraph. Disposition B treats FHIR-54315 as the anchor for a single
coordinated wording change covering the whole "no known medication"
cluster, integrates Grahame Grieve's `dateAsserted` / `effective[x]`
best-practice point, resolves the "known vs. taken" wording gap before
ballot, and keeps the Note-to-Balloters in place so the WG can revisit
the structural answer in R7 without committing to it now. The
workgroup should pick up FHIR-54315 in the same agenda item as
FHIR-54126, FHIR-54566, and FHIR-54453.
