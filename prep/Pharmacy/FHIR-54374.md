## Summary

The reporter (Eric Haas) is questioning the inclusion of `MedicationAdministration` as one of the targets of `MedicationDispense.partOf`. The ticket argues the use case is unclear because, in normal clinical workflow, an administration *follows* a dispense rather than the dispense being a component of an administration, and notes the risk of circular references between `MedicationDispense.partOf → MedicationAdministration` and any reverse linkage carried on `MedicationAdministration`. The reporter proposes either (a) removing `MedicationAdministration` from the allowed `partOf` target types, or (b) adding a clarifying rationale (likely as element comments / definition text) explaining when this linkage is appropriate. The single comment so far (Costa Teixeira) sketches a candidate use case: ward-shelf dispensing, where there is no separate formal dispense event but the administration record needs to capture that a dispense step nevertheless happened. The cross-reference index surfaces only the FHIR artifact `MedicationDispense.partOf` itself; no related Jira tickets, GitHub items, or Confluence pages were found by the index.

## Details

**Description:**

MedicationDispense can reference MedicationAdministration it's "part of."

**Unclear use case:** When is a dispense "part of" an administration? Usually, it's the reverse - administration follows dispensing.

This also creates potential circular references:

- MedicationDispense.partOf → MedicationAdministration
- MedicationAdministration (references dispense in some way)

Propose removing MedicationAdministration as a target or adding a rationale for its inclusion in the comments.

**Comments:**

- **costateixeira (2026-04-06):** "i can imagine a dispense from ward, where there is no formal separate dispensation record, but you do want to collect the notion of 'oh and by the way this medication was dispensed from ward shelf'."

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Remove `MedicationAdministration` from the allowed target types of `MedicationDispense.partOf` in FHIR Core. Concretely, in the `MedicationDispense` StructureDefinition, change the `MedicationDispense.partOf` element's `type[].targetProfile` set from its current list (currently includes `Procedure` and `MedicationAdministration`) to `Procedure` only. Update the element's short and definition text to remove any wording that implies an administration can contain a dispense. Add a release note in the changelog under the Pharmacy section flagging this as a non-backwards-compatible target restriction. No new elements or extensions are introduced.

#### Justification

The Event-pattern definition of `partOf` ("a larger event of which this is a component or step") fits poorly with a dispense being a component of an administration; the historical Zulip discussion (implementers > "Medication Dispense.partOf", 2020) explicitly records that `MedicationAdministration` was added to the target list *without* a driving use case, and Melva Peters herself questioned whether the relationship even fits the pattern. The only candidate use case that has surfaced (ward-shelf dispensing — Costa Teixeira's comment) is arguably better modelled by `MedicationAdministration` recording the source of the medication directly (e.g., via `MedicationAdministration` performer/location or an extension), or by issuing a lightweight `MedicationDispense` referenced from the administration via `MedicationAdministration.request` / a future inverse link, rather than by reversing the temporal direction of `partOf`. Removing the target also eliminates the reporter's circular-reference concern.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep `MedicationAdministration` in the `MedicationDispense.partOf` target list, but tighten the element definition and add explicit comments in the `MedicationDispense` StructureDefinition that scope its use. Specifically:

1. Update `MedicationDispense.partOf` `definition` / `comment` text in the `MedicationDispense` StructureDefinition to state that `partOf` referencing `MedicationAdministration` is intended *only* for the case where the administration is the "containing event" and the dispense is an informal sub-step that has no independent existence — the canonical example being ward-shelf or unit-dose dispensing recorded only because an administration occurred.
2. Add a usage note / example to the `MedicationDispense` narrative page (intro/notes) walking through the ward-shelf scenario.
3. Add an invariant or note discouraging bidirectional `partOf`/reverse references between a given `MedicationDispense` and `MedicationAdministration` instance to address the circular-reference concern raised in the ticket.
4. Optionally, surface this in the Pharmacy module page so implementers reading either resource find the guidance.

#### Justification

This preserves backwards compatibility — existing implementations (e.g., the SUSHI/profile examples surfaced by keyword search that already constrain `partOf` to `MedicationAdministration | MedicationDispense | …`) continue to validate. It also captures the one credible use case offered to date (Costa Teixeira's ward-shelf example) without inventing a new mechanism. The Justification for A turns on the *absence* of a use case; B accepts that absence as a documentation gap rather than a modelling error and closes it with definitional text plus a guard against the circular-reference pattern the reporter is worried about. The cost is non-trivial spec wording and vigilance in the wider Pharmacy module to keep the guidance consistent.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change on the grounds that the current target list was a deliberate (if lightly-justified) Pharmacy-WG decision and no breaking change is warranted. Close as "Persuasive with Modifier — No Change", noting that implementers who do not wish to use `MedicationAdministration` as a `partOf` target are free to ignore it, and that the circular-reference concern is a general Reference-graph issue not specific to this element pair.

#### Justification

`partOf` is `0..*` and optional; nothing forces an implementer to populate it with a `MedicationAdministration`. The Event pattern allows broad `partOf` target lists across resources and FHIR has historically tolerated `partOf` targets whose use cases are under-documented (see, e.g., `Observation.partOf` referencing `MedicationAdministration | MedicationDispense | …`, surfaced in the Orders/Observation Zulip thread). Removing a target type is non-backwards-compatible and would force any existing profile / IG that constrains `MedicationDispense.partOf` (the SUSHI thread shows at least one in the wild) to change. Without a concrete report of harm from the existing target — only a request for clarification — the bar for a breaking change is not met.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The Zulip record makes it clear that `MedicationAdministration` was added to `MedicationDispense.partOf` without a recorded use case, which is the legitimate concern behind the ticket — but the lone comment from Costa Teixeira identifies a plausible scenario (ward-shelf dispensing), and the FHIR Core principle of avoiding non-backwards-compatible removals when a documentation fix will do strongly favours B over A. Disposition C ignores the reporter's substantive point that the element's current definition does not justify the target. B addresses both the reporter's literal ask ("add a rationale … in the comments") and the circular-reference concern (via an explicit note/invariant against bidirectional partOf graphs), at the cost of careful wording in the `MedicationDispense` StructureDefinition and the Pharmacy module narrative. If, during WG discussion, no participant can defend a real-world deployment that relies on the `MedicationAdministration` target, the WG should fall back to A and treat this as a cleanup of an under-justified addition.
