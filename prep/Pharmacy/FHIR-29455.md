## Summary

Daniel Zhang is asking for clarification of a statement in the PDMP (US
Prescription Drug Monitoring Program) FHIR Implementation Guide's "PDMP
Responder" section that reads: *"The PDMP Responder is expected to allow
registration of a PDMP Requestor for each individual Provider or a
Provider Organization as required by policies."* The reporter observes
that this wording reads like a policy/onboarding statement about provider
registration and does not appear directly related to the technical
problem the IG is trying to address (the FHIR-based PDMP
request/response exchange). The request is effectively: either explain
what this sentence is trying to tell an implementer, or relocate /
reword it so it fits the IG's scope.

The ticket carries a recorded resolution of *Persuasive with
Modification* with resolution text "Agree. Move this statement about
provider registration out of Security/Responder Requirements and into
the Use Case page as background." It was previously dispositioned
*Considered for Future Use* (2023-01-18, 4-0-0) on the basis that the
IG had been withdrawn, then reopened on 2023-11-28 under the PSS-2225
PDMP on FHIR project reboot, returning it to **Triaged** pending
application of the agreed modification.

No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were
found for this ticket.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the resolution already recorded on the ticket: in the PDMP on FHIR
IG, **remove** the sentence *"The PDMP Responder is expected to allow
registration of a PDMP Requestor for each individual Provider or a
Provider Organization as required by policies."* from the
**Security / Responder Requirements** (PDMP Responder) section, and
**re-home** an equivalent statement on the IG's **Use Case** page as
background/context — framing it as an assumption about provider
onboarding rather than a normative responder requirement. The Security
section should then contain only statements that are actually security
requirements on the Responder actor (authentication, authorization,
audit, etc.). The Use Case page should include a short paragraph along
the lines of: "Before a Provider (or Provider Organization) can submit
PDMP queries, they are assumed to have been registered with the PDMP
Responder per applicable jurisdictional policies; this IG does not
define that registration process."

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter asked for: the current wording mixes a
policy/onboarding precondition with normative security requirements,
which is confusing for implementers and out of scope for the Security
section. The workgroup has already recorded agreement with this move in
the resolution field, and PSS-2225 has revived the IG so the change can
now actually be applied. Treating provider registration as a
background/use-case assumption (rather than a responder obligation) is
consistent with how other FHIR IGs describe prerequisites that are
governed by external policy.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep a responder-facing statement in the IG but **rewrite and relocate
it** rather than reducing it to pure background. Specifically: (1) move
the sentence out of *Security/Responder Requirements*; (2) place a
reworded version in a new "Preconditions" or "Actor Assumptions"
subsection of the Use Case page; and (3) add a short cross-reference
back from the Security section that says something like "Provider /
Provider Organization registration with the PDMP Responder is a
precondition described in *Use Case › Preconditions*; it is out of
scope for this IG to specify." This preserves discoverability for
implementers who will look in the Security section first, while still
removing the false impression that registration is a security/FHIR
conformance requirement.

#### Justification

Implementers of a PDMP Responder will read the Security section
looking for what they must implement. If the statement disappears from
that section entirely (Disposition A), some readers may miss the fact
that they are nevertheless expected to have a registration process in
place out-of-band. A tiny non-normative cross-reference keeps the
signal without reintroducing the scope/requirement confusion, and it is
a lighter editorial load than the bare relocation in A while still
satisfying the reporter's core concern.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Do not modify the IG text. Close the ticket as *Not Persuasive* with a
note that the existing sentence is acceptable as-is because it signals
to implementers that a PDMP Responder must be able to onboard
Requestors, which is operationally relevant even if the registration
protocol itself is out of scope.

#### Justification

Declining is defensible only on the grounds that the sentence, while
awkwardly placed, is not technically wrong and does not produce
incorrect FHIR behavior. Responder implementations really do need some
registration mechanism, and removing the sentence entirely without a
replacement could be read as saying the IG has no opinion on whether
registration must exist at all. This is the weakest option because the
workgroup has already agreed the current placement is wrong and the
reporter's critique (scope mismatch with Security/Responder
Requirements) is valid.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The workgroup has already recorded agreement with the Disposition A
action in the resolution field (*Persuasive with Modification* — "Move
this statement about provider registration out of Security/Responder
Requirements and into the Use Case page as background"), and the IG has
been revived under PSS-2225, which makes the change applicable rather
than deferrable. Disposition A is also the cleanest editorial outcome:
it removes a genuine scope/requirement mismatch that the reporter
correctly identified, without introducing new normative language about
a registration protocol the IG does not define. Disposition B is a
reasonable refinement if the workgroup decides a back-reference from
the Security section is worth the extra editorial surface; that can be
adopted as a friendly amendment during ballot reconciliation without
changing the fundamental direction. Disposition C should be rejected
because it contradicts the recorded resolution and leaves the
scope-mismatch concern unaddressed.
