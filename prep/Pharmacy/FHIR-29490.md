## Summary

The ticket challenges a registration design choice in the US Prescription
Drug Monitoring Program (PDMP) FHIR Implementation Guide (carried under the
"US Medication FHIR Implementation Guide" specification slot). The IG
currently states that the PDMP Responder is expected to allow registration
of a PDMP Requestor for *each individual Provider* or for a Provider
Organization, as required by policies. Josh Mandel observes that if the
intent is for *individual users* (clinicians) to register and obtain
access individually, that scenario is a better match for the SMART App
Launch *user* authorization flow than for the SMART Backend Services
(client credentials, system-level) flow that the IG appears to assume.
In other words, individual-user registration and authorization is a
user-context problem, not a backend/service-account problem, and the IG
should be aligned to the appropriate SMART profile. No cross-referenced
Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were surfaced for this
ticket.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the PDMP IG's security/registration narrative to explicitly
distinguish two registration patterns and align each with the correct
SMART profile:

1. **Organization-to-organization (system) access** — a PDMP Requestor
   organization registers a backend client with the PDMP Responder and
   uses **SMART Backend Services** (asymmetric client credentials,
   `system/*` scopes) for unattended queries.
2. **Individual provider access** — when a clinician personally registers
   to query the PDMP from their EHR or a standalone app, use the
   **SMART App Launch** *user* authorization flow (authorization code +
   PKCE, `user/*` scopes, with the prescriber identity asserted in
   `id_token`/`fhirUser`), rather than registering each provider as a
   distinct backend client.

The IG section that currently says "the PDMP Responder is expected to
allow registration of a PDMP Requestor for each individual Provider or a
Provider Organization" should be rewritten to (a) drop "each individual
Provider" from the backend-services registration requirement, and (b)
add an explicit subsection requiring SMART App Launch with user-context
scopes for individual-clinician access, including guidance on
identifying the prescriber (e.g., NPI/DEA conveyed via `Practitioner`
identifiers and/or `fhirUser`).

#### Justification

This matches the intent of the SMART specifications, which deliberately
separate user-attended (App Launch) and unattended (Backend Services)
flows precisely because they answer different questions about *who* is
making the request. PDMP queries are heavily auditable and policy-bound
on the *prescriber's* identity (state PDMP laws condition lookup on the
querying clinician's role and licensure), which is exactly the
information that the user-authorization flow surfaces and that backend
services intentionally does not. Treating each prescriber as a separate
backend client is also operationally awkward: it forces per-clinician
client-credential issuance, key management, and revocation in a setting
where SMART App Launch already solves the problem with established
patterns. Reporter is Josh Mandel, an author of SMART, which adds weight
to the alignment argument.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the IG's two-tier registration story, but frame it as a *capability
matrix* rather than rewriting it as a SMART-profile choice. Specifically:

- Define the PDMP Responder's registration obligations in terms of the
  *actor* being authorized (Provider Organization vs. Individual
  Provider) and the *transport security* requirements, and then
  reference SMART Backend Services as the **normative** mechanism for
  organizational actors and SMART App Launch (with `user/*` scopes and
  `fhirUser`) as the **normative** mechanism for individual-provider
  actors.
- Retain language that allows a jurisdiction or PDMP operator to require
  *additional* per-provider attestation (e.g., DEA verification) layered
  on top of either flow, since some state PDMP policies require
  out-of-band provider enrollment regardless of OAuth flow.
- Add a non-normative appendix illustrating both flows end-to-end with
  example scopes, token claims, and the resulting audit record.

#### Justification

This addresses the underlying concern (individual-provider registration
should not be modeled as a backend client) without forcing a single
flow onto every deployment. PDMP policy is set state-by-state, and some
jurisdictions may legitimately want a hybrid: a backend-services
integration at the EHR/organization tier *plus* a per-prescriber
attestation step. A capability matrix accommodates that without
sacrificing the conceptual clarity Disposition A provides. It also
limits churn in conformance language — the IG keeps its existing
two-actor framing and just attaches the right SMART profile to each.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark Persuasive but Not Persuasive-Enough at this time and defer.
Rationale: the underlying IG (US PDMP FHIR IG) was previously withdrawn
and is only tentatively being picked back up under PSS-2225; the
ticket carries the `PDMP-old-issues` label and predates the current
project plan. Decline to action this ticket as a stand-alone change and
instead fold the SMART-profile alignment question into the IG-relaunch
work plan owned by the Pharmacy WG and the PDMP project sponsor, to be
re-triaged when the IG re-enters active development.

#### Justification

Spending review cycles on a 2020 ticket against an IG that is not
currently in an active publication cycle risks producing guidance that
will be re-relitigated when the project resumes, especially because the
SMART ecosystem (App Launch v2, scope syntax, `fhirUser`) has evolved
significantly since the ticket was filed. The 2023-01 disposition
("Considered for Future Use") was reverted only because the project
re-opened, not because the issue is now actionable in isolation. A
defer-to-IG-restart disposition keeps the concern visible without
forcing premature normative text.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter has identified a genuine modeling mismatch: SMART Backend
Services is a system-to-system flow and is the wrong tool for
authorizing individual prescriber lookups. PDMP queries are
prescriber-attributable by design (state law conditions access on the
querying clinician's identity and role), which is exactly what SMART App
Launch's user-authorization flow is built to convey. Disposition B is
attractive for its flexibility, but it preserves the ambiguity that
caused the original confusion — implementers reading the current IG
text could still reasonably build a per-provider backend-client
deployment, which is the anti-pattern being flagged. Disposition C is
defensible from a project-management standpoint, but the substantive
guidance fix is small and well-scoped, and getting it captured *now*
(even if the IG remains paused) means the PDMP-relaunch effort starts
from the correct security baseline rather than re-discovering the same
issue. The Pharmacy WG should adopt Disposition A and coordinate with
Security WG and the SMART community to confirm the App-Launch scope
shape used for prescriber-attributable PDMP queries.
