## Summary

Eric Haas (Pharmacy) asks why MedicationAdministration carries two distinct
elements that can both reference a `DeviceDefinition` for the device that is to
administer (or is administering) a medication: `MedicationAdministration.performer.actor`
and `MedicationAdministration.device`. Both currently allow `Reference(DeviceDefinition)`
(directly or via `CodeableReference`), and the spec's own narrative on
`performer` even uses an IV pump as the canonical example — which is exactly
the case `device` is meant to cover. The reporter sees this as a redundant
modeling choice and proposes either (1) removing `MedicationAdministration.device`
outright, or (2) keeping `device` and removing `DeviceDefinition` from
`performer.actor`'s allowed targets so that the two elements have non-overlapping
responsibilities.

Two prior, closed Pharmacy tickets explain how the overlap arose and frame
this discussion: [FHIR-38978](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-38978)
(Published, Persuasive with Modification, 2022) is the source of the original
shape — it asked for `DeviceDefinition` on `performer.actor` and the WG
resolved to make `MedicationAdministration.device` a `CodeableReference(Device)`
and "if we can" also extend `performer.actor` similarly. [FHIR-46961](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-46961)
(Applied, Not Persuasive with Modification, 2025) then added `DeviceDefinition`
to the `device` element so clinicians could indicate the *type* of device
(e.g., "an infusion pump") rather than a specific instance — completing the
overlap that FHIR-54416 now flags.

## Details

**Description:**

Both `performer` and `device` elements can specify a `DeviceDefinition` for
administration.

- `performer`: Can reference `DeviceDefinition` — *"For devices, this is the
  device that is intended to perform the administration."*
- `device`: Can reference `DeviceDefinition` — *"The intended type of device
  that is to be used for the administration."*

The spec even says in the `performer` definition: *"An IV Pump would be an
example of a device that is performing the administration"* — suggesting
`performer`.

But an IV pump for medication administration can go in `performer` because it
"performs" the administration and/or `device` because it's the "device for
administration"? Thus both elements can specify a `DeviceDefinition` for
administration.

Proposal:

1. remove `.device`, **or**
2. remove `DeviceDefinition` as the `.performer` target and use `.device`.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the reporter's option (1): in `MedicationAdministration` (FHIR Core,
Pharmacy), **remove the `device` element** and rely solely on `performer`
(with `performer.function` distinguishing the device's role from a human
performer's role) to carry the device that performed the administration —
whether it is recorded as a specific `Device` instance or as a
`DeviceDefinition` (i.e., a device *type*) via the existing
`CodeableReference`. Update the narrative on `performer.actor` to make
explicit that recording an IV pump or similar piece of administration
equipment belongs here, and that `DeviceDefinition` is the appropriate
target when only the device kind is known. Provide a backward-compatibility
note in the change log, and (because `MedicationRequest.device` exists with
the same shape) coordinate a parallel decision on `MedicationRequest.device`
so the two resources stay aligned.

#### Justification

This option directly removes the overlap and gives the spec one obvious
place to record the administering device. The `performer.actor` path
already supports both `Device` (instance) and `DeviceDefinition` (type) via
`CodeableReference` thanks to FHIR-38978's resolution, so no clinical
information is lost. The `performer.function` slot is the standard FHIR
mechanism for disambiguating roles in a list of performers, which is a
better fit for "this performer is the IV pump" than a sibling element. The
ticket itself quotes the spec's own example ("An IV Pump would be an
example of a device that is performing the administration") as evidence
that the WG already conceptually treats devices as performers.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt the reporter's option (2), with one modification: **keep
`MedicationAdministration.device` and narrow `performer.actor` so it can no
longer reference `Device` or `DeviceDefinition`** — i.e., performers are
restricted to human/organization actors (Practitioner, PractitionerRole,
Patient, RelatedPerson, Organization, CareTeam), and any device
participation in the administration is recorded only on `device`. Apply
the same split to `MedicationRequest.device` vs. `MedicationRequest.performer`
for symmetry. Rewrite the `performer` narrative to drop the IV-pump example
and add it to `device` instead. Provide a clear migration note: instances
that previously placed a device under `performer.actor` should be moved to
`device`.

#### Justification

This alternative also eliminates the overlap but preserves the dedicated
`device` slot, which has clinical value: implementers and consumers can
read "what device was used?" from a single, well-named element rather than
having to walk a polymorphic `performer` list and filter on
`performer.function`. It also matches the original 2022 WG instinct in
FHIR-38978's resolution ("we will not change `performer.actor` from
`Device` to `DeviceDefinition`") — that resolution kept device-as-type out
of `performer.actor` on principle, and FHIR-54416 effectively argues that
adding `DeviceDefinition` back onto `performer.actor` was a mistake.
Trade-off vs. A: this is a more disruptive change for any implementer who
has already populated `performer.actor` with a `Device` reference, and it
slightly weakens the symmetry with other event resources that allow
devices in `performer`. But it leaves the cleanest semantic split.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change as **Not Persuasive**. Keep both
`MedicationAdministration.device` and `MedicationAdministration.performer.actor`
with their current target sets (including `Device` and `DeviceDefinition`).
Add a clarifying paragraph to both element definitions and to the resource
narrative that explicitly addresses the overlap: `performer` records the
device that *actually carried out* the administration (and is the right
home when the device acted as the agent of administration in place of a
human, e.g., an automated IV pump on a closed-loop system), while `device`
records the device that was *used as equipment* during administration but
where a human performer is also recorded on `performer` (e.g., a syringe,
a nebulizer mask, or an IV pump set up and supervised by a nurse). Tighten
the wording so the two are no longer paraphrases of each other.

#### Justification

The two elements are not strictly redundant: `performer` is a participation
slot (with `function`) intended for agents that performed the
administration, whereas `device` is intended for equipment used during the
administration regardless of who performed it. Many real workflows have
both — a nurse (in `performer`) using an infusion pump (in `device`). The
overlap is a documentation problem, not a model problem. Removing either
element after they have shipped in two consecutive cycles (FHIR-38978
applied in 2022, FHIR-46961 applied in 2024–2025) creates a breaking
change for early adopters and risks losing information that today's models
already capture distinctly. A documentation fix is sufficient.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition: B (Alternative Approach).**

Disposition C is the easiest path but does not actually resolve the
ambiguity the reporter raises — both elements already say "device for
administration" in slightly different words, and a third pass at narrative
will not stop implementers from putting the same DeviceDefinition in both
slots. Disposition A is clean and aligns with FHIR's "use participations
with `function` codes" pattern, but it discards a useful, named slot
(`device`) that downstream systems already query directly, and it papers
over the WG's earlier, deliberate instinct (in FHIR-38978's resolution)
that `performer.actor` should not be the home for device *types*.
Disposition B restores that intent: `performer` becomes unambiguously
about human/organization participation, `device` becomes the single,
clearly-named home for the equipment used (whether as a `Device` instance
or as a `DeviceDefinition` type via `CodeableReference`), and the same
treatment can be applied symmetrically to `MedicationRequest.device`
(also a Pharmacy artifact and structurally identical). Yes, it is a
breaking change for any implementer who placed a device under
`performer.actor`, but the migration ("move it to `device`") is mechanical
and the resulting model is materially clearer than either of the other
two options.
