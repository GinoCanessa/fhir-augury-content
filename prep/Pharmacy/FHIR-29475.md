## Summary

Daniel Zhang reports a formatting defect in the US Prescription Drug
Monitoring Program (PDMP) FHIR Implementation Guide. A `SHALL`-level
narrative statement enumerating the information that a PDMP Responder must
collect about each PDMP Requestor renders as a single run-on paragraph
because the intended bullet markers (`**`) were not converted into list
items, and the word "Requestor" is misspelled as "Rqquestor". The
requested change is editorial: re-format the list so that each requestor
attribute (Requestor Identity / name, Requestor Role, Requestor DEA / NPI
number, Requestor Facility information) appears as its own bullet, and
fix the typo so the conformance statement is unambiguous.

The cross-reference index returned no related Jira tickets, Zulip
threads, or GitHub items, so the ticket stands alone. Comment history
shows the ticket was previously resolved as "Considered for Future Use"
on 2023-01-18 (vote 4-0-0, John Hatem / Tim McNeil) on the basis that
the IG had been withdrawn, then re-opened on 2023-11-28 by smrobertson
under the PSS-2225 "PDMP on FHIR" project, indicating the workgroup
intends to revisit PDMP IG tickets when the IG is picked back up.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Persistent / Editorial change to the PDMP FHIR IG narrative that
contains the malformed `SHALL` statement. Reformat the run-on sentence
so the four requestor attributes are rendered as a proper list and fix
the spelling of "Requestor". Suggested replacement wording:

> The PDMP Responder **SHALL** collect the following information about
> each PDMP Requestor, which will be used for both auditing and for
> uniquely identifying the requestor:
>
> - Requestor Identity (name)
> - Requestor Role
> - Requestor DEA and NPI numbers
> - Requestor Facility information

The change is confined to the IG's narrative source for that section
(the "PDMP Responder responsibilities" / requestor-collection paragraph
on the relevant `pages/*.md` or HTML page in the PDMP IG sources) and
does not touch any profile, value set, or capability statement.

#### Justification

The reporter's complaint is purely about readability of a
normative-level statement, and the intent of the original text is
unambiguous: each `**`-prefixed token is a separate list item. Rendering
it as a true bullet list, plus correcting "Rqquestor", removes the
ambiguity without changing the conformance requirement, the cardinality
of collected attributes, or the actor model. Editorial fixes of
malformed narrative are routinely accepted and align with HL7's
publication-quality expectations. Because there are no cross-references
or Zulip threads opposing the change, no community-level disagreement
needs to be reconciled.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Replace the prose enumeration with a structured conformance artifact
rather than a plain bullet list. Specifically:

- Author (or extend) a `Requestor` profile / logical model in the PDMP
  IG that defines the four attributes (Identity, Role, DEA, NPI,
  Facility) as elements with explicit cardinalities and bindings (e.g.,
  Role bound to a value set, DEA / NPI as `Identifier` slices on a
  shared `identifier` element).
- Reduce the narrative `SHALL` to a one-line pointer that says the PDMP
  Responder SHALL collect a `Requestor` instance conforming to that
  profile, with a link to the profile page.
- Apply the editorial bullet-list fix from Disposition A as part of the
  same change so the page is well-formed even before readers click
  through to the profile.

#### Justification

The current `SHALL` mixes a normative requirement with a free-text list
of attributes, which is exactly the situation that produced this ticket
in the first place: any future formatting drift will silently weaken or
obscure a normative requirement. Anchoring the requestor data on a
profile makes the requirement testable, lets DEA and NPI participate in
the IG's identifier-system conventions, and sets up Role for terminology
governance. Trade-off vs. Disposition A: significantly more authoring
effort, may require a Pharmacy WG vote on the profile shape, and
expands scope beyond the editorial fix the reporter actually asked for.
Because the PDMP IG was withdrawn and is only conditionally being
revived under PSS-2225, this expanded scope might not be welcome until
the project plan is re-confirmed.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Persistent — No Change With Comment, on the basis that the PDMP FHIR IG
is currently withdrawn and the ticket is part of the deferred
`PDMP-old-issues` backlog. The comment should record that the editorial
fix is acknowledged as valid but will not be applied to a withdrawn
artifact, and that the ticket should be re-triaged when (and only when)
the PDMP IG is formally re-balloted under PSS-2225 "PDMP on FHIR".

#### Justification

The 2023-01-18 disposition explicitly recorded that PDMP IG tickets are
deferred because the IG was withdrawn; the 2023-11-28 re-opening notes
the IG may be picked back up but does not commit a publication
timeline. Spending Pharmacy WG cycles on editorial changes against a
withdrawn artifact risks producing a corrected version of a document
that is not on a publication path, and it leaves the ticket in an
ambiguous "fixed but unpublished" state. Declining (with a clear
comment) is consistent with how the workgroup has already chosen to
manage this backlog, and re-triaging on revival ensures the fix lands
in a version that will actually publish.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

This is a low-risk, narrow editorial fix to an existing `SHALL`
statement whose intent is unambiguous from the original text. Even
though the PDMP IG is currently withdrawn, the ticket was deliberately
re-opened on 2023-11-28 under PSS-2225, signalling that the workgroup
expects to act on the deferred backlog rather than leave it in
permanent abeyance; landing accumulated editorial fixes now makes the
eventual revival ballot cleaner. Disposition B's profile-based redesign
is attractive in principle but is out of proportion to what the
reporter asked for and would benefit from being raised as its own,
appropriately-scoped change request once the IG's revival plan is
firm. Disposition C correctly captures the project's current status
but would unnecessarily defer a fix that costs almost nothing to apply
and that the workgroup has already implicitly committed to processing
by re-opening the ticket.
