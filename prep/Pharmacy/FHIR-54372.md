## Summary

Eric Haas requests that the Pharmacy work group consider adding new
`MedicationDispense` invariants that constrain the `whenPrepared` and
`whenHandedOver` timestamps based on the resource's `status` code. The
existing constraint `mdd-1` ("whenHandedOver cannot be before
whenPrepared") only enforces the temporal ordering of the two
timestamps relative to each other; it says nothing about whether
either timestamp is appropriate given the dispense lifecycle state.
The reporter highlights two scenarios that today validate cleanly but
appear semantically wrong: a dispense with `status = cancelled` that
nevertheless carries a `whenHandedOver` value, and a dispense with
`status = declined` that has `whenHandedOver` populated even though
nothing was actually handed to the patient. The proposal sketches a
status whitelist for `whenHandedOver` (preparation | in-progress |
completed | entered-in-error | unknown), with `cancelled`, `on-hold`,
`unfulfilled`, and `declined` explicitly struck out as states where
`whenHandedOver` should not be present. No analogous list is sketched
for `whenPrepared`, but the same line of reasoning would apply (e.g.,
`whenPrepared` being meaningless on a `declined` dispense). No
cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were
found for this ticket.

## Details

**Description:**

Currently, there is a Constraint `mdd-1`: *"whenHandedOver cannot be
before whenPrepared."*

But there's no constraint relating these timestamps to `status`:

- `status = cancelled,` but `whenHandedOver` is populated?
- `status = declined` should have `whenPrepared` but not `whenHandedOver`?

Propose adding Status-timestamp invariants like:

`"whenHandedOver only if status = preparation | in-progress | ~~cancelled~~ | ~~on-hold~~ | completed | entered-in-error | ~~unfulfilled~~ | ~~declined~~ | unknown"`

(strikethroughs indicate statuses where `whenHandedOver` should be
disallowed under the proposed invariant)

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add two new FHIRPath invariants on `MedicationDispense` (the same
element scope as the existing `mdd-1`) that gate the two timestamps on
`status`:

- `mdd-2` (severity `error`, expression on `MedicationDispense`):
  `whenHandedOver.exists() implies status in ('preparation' | 'in-progress' | 'completed' | 'entered-in-error' | 'unknown')`
  Human reading: *"whenHandedOver SHALL only be present when status is
  preparation, in-progress, completed, entered-in-error, or unknown."*
- `mdd-3` (severity `error`, expression on `MedicationDispense`):
  `whenPrepared.exists() implies status != 'declined'`
  Human reading: *"whenPrepared SHALL NOT be present when status is
  declined."* (Pharmacy can decide whether to extend this list to
  `cancelled`, `on-hold`, `unfulfilled` after discussion.)

Update the `MedicationDispense` resource intro / Notes section to
mention the new invariants alongside `mdd-1`, and add a ballot/CR note
to the Pharmacy change log. Carry the same constraints into any
Pharmacy IG profiles that derive from `MedicationDispense` only if
they tighten cardinality for these elements; otherwise the base
invariants are inherited automatically.

#### Justification

The reporter's scenarios identify a real semantic gap: today an
implementation can record `status = cancelled` while still asserting a
hand-off time, which contradicts the meaning of `cancelled` ("the
dispense was aborted before the medication was given to the
patient"). FHIR routinely encodes this kind of status/timestamp
coupling as a FHIRPath invariant rather than narrative-only guidance
(e.g., similar invariants exist on `Encounter`, `Task`, and
`MedicationRequest`), so the requested change is consistent with the
methodology already in use for workflow resources. Expressing the
constraint in FHIRPath also gives validators an automatic check, which
is more useful to implementers than prose in the Notes section.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than introducing two new error-severity invariants, address the
underlying concern with a single, narrower `warning`-level invariant
plus expanded narrative guidance:

- Add `mdd-2` (severity `warning`):
  `whenHandedOver.exists() implies status in ('in-progress' | 'completed' | 'entered-in-error' | 'unknown')`
  with human text *"whenHandedOver should typically only be present
  once the medication has actually been handed over."*
- Update the `MedicationDispense.status` and
  `MedicationDispense.whenHandedOver` / `whenPrepared` element
  definitions with explicit prose describing which combinations are
  expected, which are unusual, and how to interpret legacy data.
- Leave `whenPrepared` unconstrained by status, since "prepared then
  cancelled" and "prepared then declined" are real, observed
  workflows.

#### Justification

Promoting these rules to `error`-level invariants risks invalidating
historical and in-flight data that legitimately captures partial
workflows — for example, a pharmacy that cancels a dispense after
handoff was attempted but then reversed, or systems that record
`whenHandedOver` as the time of the (failed) handoff event. A
`warning` flags the same problem to implementers and validators
without breaking conformance for trading partners who already exchange
this data. Restricting the new constraint to `whenHandedOver` (and
leaving `whenPrepared` to narrative) avoids overreach into the
"prepared then cancelled" pattern, which is genuinely valid. The
companion narrative changes ensure the guidance is discoverable
without forcing a normative break.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request and instead document, in the `MedicationDispense`
resource intro and the `status` element definition, that
implementations are responsible for ensuring `whenPrepared` and
`whenHandedOver` are consistent with `status`. Do not add any new
FHIRPath invariants. Categorize the decision as *"already addressable
via implementer guidance / out of scope for base resource
constraints"*.

#### Justification

`MedicationDispense.status` codes are deliberately broad to support
many regional and operational dispense workflows, and the mapping
between status and timestamps is not uniform across jurisdictions.
For example, some pharmacy systems record `whenHandedOver` as the
*scheduled* handoff time before the patient arrives, then later set
`status = cancelled` if the patient never collects the medication —
under Disposition A's invariant, this legitimate pattern would be
non-conformant. Encoding one organization's interpretation of the
status semantics as a normative invariant risks pushing valid data
into `entered-in-error` or forcing IGs to override the base
invariant. Pharmacy IGs (e.g., national medication-dispense IGs) are
the appropriate place to tighten these rules for a specific
ecosystem.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B addresses the real semantic gap the reporter identified
without taking on the regression risk of an `error`-severity change
to a widely-implemented workflow resource. Promoting the rule to a
hard invariant (Disposition A) is appealing on data-quality grounds
but is very likely to break existing implementations and historical
data — particularly around `cancelled`, `on-hold`, and `declined`,
where real-world pharmacy workflows do legitimately record handoff
attempts. Declining outright (Disposition C) leaves the gap that
prompted the ticket and pushes the burden onto every downstream IG.
A `warning`-level invariant scoped to `whenHandedOver`, combined with
clearer element-definition narrative, gives validators something to
flag, signals Pharmacy's preferred semantics, and leaves room for
Pharmacy IGs (or a future ballot cycle) to tighten to `error` once
the community has had a chance to adapt. The work group should still
explicitly decide whether `whenPrepared` deserves its own (narrower)
constraint and whether `cancelled` belongs in the allowed-status list
for `whenHandedOver`.
