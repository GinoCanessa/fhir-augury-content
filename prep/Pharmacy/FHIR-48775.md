## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the requested edit verbatim: remove the entry "Carly Crownover" from the
stakeholder / acknowledgements list of the US Prescription Drug Monitoring
Program (PDMP) FHIR IG. The change is limited to the IG's stakeholder list
content (typically a Markdown/HTML page rendered into the IG's front matter or
"Credits" / "Acknowledgements" page). No StructureDefinitions, ValueSets,
CapabilityStatements, examples, or narrative referring to PDMP semantics are
affected. The change should be applied in the IG's authoring source for the
next published build of the guide and noted in the IG's change log as a
technical correction. Confirm via the attached email (referenced in the
ticket) that the request is from the named individual before publishing.

#### Justification

The request originates from the named individual (Carly Crownover), with
documentary evidence attached to the ticket. Stakeholder lists in HL7 IGs are
attribution / acknowledgement content, and individuals retain the right to
have their names removed on request — both as a matter of professional
courtesy and to align with HL7's general practice of honouring attribution
opt-outs. The change is non-normative, has no implementer impact, carries no
backward-compatibility risk, and is exactly the scope of a "Technical
Correction" disposition. Accepting as requested is the path of least friction
and respects the requester's stated wishes.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the spirit of the request but generalise the fix: rather than only
deleting Carly Crownover's row, take this opportunity to (a) refresh the
entire stakeholder / acknowledgements list against the current Pharmacy WG
and PDMP project roster, (b) document the IG's policy for adding or removing
named contributors (including who to contact and what evidence is required),
and (c) remove Carly Crownover's name as part of that pass. Optionally, link
the stakeholder list to the HL7 confluence-managed project roster so future
changes don't require an IG-source edit and a Jira ticket.

#### Justification

Stakeholder lists in IGs tend to drift out of date between ballot cycles, and
individual removal requests are often a symptom of that drift. Performing a
single broader refresh costs only modestly more than the surgical edit and
prevents an accumulation of one-off "remove me" tickets in the Pharmacy
backlog. Documenting the contributor-management policy at the same time
reduces the support burden on the WG. The trade-off versus Disposition A is
that this approach requires roster work that may not be ready before the next
publication window, which would either delay Carly Crownover's removal or
force splitting the work — so this disposition is only preferable if the WG
has bandwidth and an interested editor.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request and leave the stakeholder list as published. Respond to
the reporter that the IG's published acknowledgement record reflects
historical contribution and is not edited post-publication.

#### Justification

A "no edits to historical attribution" stance is defensible only if the WG
has an established policy that the stakeholder list is an immutable record of
who contributed at the time of publication (analogous to a paper's author
list). In practice, HL7 does not appear to operate such a policy for IG
stakeholder pages, and refusing a direct, documented removal request from the
named individual is poor stewardship and creates an avoidable
attribution/privacy concern. This disposition is included for completeness
but is hard to justify on the facts of this ticket.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Accept as requested. The ticket is a low-risk, non-normative housekeeping
edit, the request is documented (email attachment) and originates from the
named individual, and there is no community discussion (Zulip, linked tickets,
GitHub PRs) suggesting any controversy or wider scope. Disposition B is
attractive in principle but should not block the immediate, courteous removal
the requester asked for; if the WG wants the broader roster refresh, it
should be tracked as a separate ticket so this one can close in the next
publication of the PDMP IG. Disposition C is included for completeness but is
not defensible given the documented opt-out request from the named
individual.
