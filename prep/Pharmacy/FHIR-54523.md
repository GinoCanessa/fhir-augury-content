## Summary

The reporter (Lauri Rondolo) flagged that the Dosage Regime Examples page in
the FHIR R6 ballot 4 specification (`dosage-examples.html`) contains
examples whose textual rendered instruction does not match the structural
representation in JSON/XML. The headline case is **Example 1**: the rendered
instruction is "1 tablet per day for three months" against a medication of
"Perindopril Arginine, 2.5 mg tablet", but the structural `doseAndRate`
shows `doseQuantity: 10 mg` — i.e., four tablets per dose, not one. The
reporter suggests correcting the structural example to either
`doseQuantity: 2.5 mg` or `doseQuantity: 1 tablet` so the text and
structure are consistent.

The ticket is currently `Triaged` with a proposed resolution of
**Persuasive with Modification**: the proposer (Jose Costa Teixeira) plans
to apply the requested Example 1 fix and, while there, also fix a small
cluster of additional cross-format inconsistencies that he and Mattias
Colliander identified in the same page (see Resolution Description in the
Details section). A sibling Jira (`FHIR-56016`) covers a separate
structural problem in another example (timing/doseAndRate placed directly
under `step` instead of `step.component`) and is being progressed in
parallel rather than rolled into this ticket.

There are no explicitly-linked Jira tickets, no PRs, and no formal
related artifacts on this ticket — the cross-references that exist all
trace back to the single Zulip thread where the expanded scope was
discussed and to one commit message that mentions the ticket key.

## Details

**Description:**

The Dosage Regime Examples [https://hl7.org/fhir/6.0.0-ballot4/dosage-examples.html](https://hl7.org/fhir/6.0.0-ballot4/dosage-examples.html)
contain examples where the textual representation does not match with the
structural representation.

Example 1 for instance has a dosage "1 tablet per day for three months"
and a `medication.concept` "Perindopril Arginine, 2.5". The structure for
this example is:

```
dosageInstruction
   renderedInstruction: 1 tablet per day for 3 months
   ...
   doseAndRate
      doseQuantity: 10 mg
```

If the medication refers to 2.5 mg tablets, and the instruction states
1 tablet per day, shouldn't the `doseQuantity` be 2.5 mg? 10 mg is equal
to 4 tablets and not 1 tablet.

Suggestion to change structure (and JSON/XML) to either:

```
MedicationRequest
   medication
       concept
           coding: Perindopril Arginine, 2.5mg tablets
   dosageInstruction
       renderedInstruction: 1 tablet per day for 3 months
       simple
           timing
               repeat
                   boundsDuration: 3 mo
                   frequency : 1
                   period: 1
                   periodUnit: d
           doseAndRate
               doseQuantity: 2.5 mg
```

…or alternatively with `doseQuantity: 1 tablet` (using a unit of "tablet"
instead of mg).

**Resolution Description (proposed — Persuasive with Modification):**

Apply the requested fix to Example 1 (`doseQuantity` 10 mg → 2.5 mg in
JSON and XML). Additionally, correct cross-format mismatches found in
other examples:

- Example 2 JSON missing 5 mg `doseAndRate` on PRN component, safety
  unit "s" should be "tablet";
- Example 2 XML has duplicate 150 mg/week safety block;
- Example 3 JSON and XML have safety period of 2 months, should be 6 to
  match YAML.

**Comments:**

No comments are recorded on the Jira ticket itself. The substantive
discussion has happened on Zulip rather than in Jira (see _Related

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In `HL7/fhir`, edit `source/dosage-examples.html` and change Example 1's
structural representation in **both** the JSON and the XML tab so that
the `doseAndRate.doseQuantity` matches "1 tablet per day" of a 2.5 mg
Perindopril Arginine tablet — i.e., set `doseQuantity` to
`{ "value": 2.5, "unit": "mg", "system": "http://unitsofmeasure.org",
"code": "mg" }` (UCUM) — and leave the rendered text
("1 tablet per day for 3 months"), the timing block (`boundsDuration`
3 mo, `frequency` 1, `period` 1, `periodUnit` d) and the
`medication.concept` ("Perindopril Arginine, 2.5 mg tablet") unchanged.
Make no other edits. Do not touch Example 2 or Example 3, do not touch
`source/datatypes/dosage.xml`, do not touch the underlying
StructureDefinition. Resolution: **Persuasive**.

#### Justification

This is the literal ask in the ticket: Example 1's `doseQuantity` of
10 mg is internally inconsistent with the rendered instruction and with
the named medication strength, and the reporter offers two
mathematically-correct alternatives (2.5 mg, or 1 tablet). Picking the
mg form keeps the example UCUM-only, which is the dominant convention
across the rest of the FHIR core dosage examples and avoids the
controversial question of how to encode "tablet" as a unit. The change
is confined to one HTML file under `source/`, follows the FhirCore
playbook's "update narrative / prose" recipe directly, and carries no
cross-repo or `<snapshot>`-regeneration risk per the briefing.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach (Persuasive with Modification — fix the whole page)

#### Proposal

Adopt the proposed resolution already drafted by Jose Costa Teixeira on
the Medication Zulip thread and recorded in this ticket's Resolution
Description. In a single edit pass over `HL7/fhir`'s
`source/dosage-examples.html`:

1. **Example 1** — change `doseAndRate.doseQuantity` from 10 mg to
   2.5 mg in both the JSON and XML tabs (as in Disposition A).
2. **Example 2 JSON** — add the missing 5 mg `doseAndRate` on the PRN
   component; correct the safety-quantity unit from `"s"` to `"tablet"`.
3. **Example 2 XML** — remove the duplicated 150 mg/week safety block.
4. **Example 3 JSON and XML** — change the safety `period` from 2
   months to 6 months so they agree with the YAML representation on the
   same page.

Keep the underlying datatype definitions (`source/datatypes/dosage.xml`,
`source/datatypes/dosagedetails.xml`, `source/datatypes/dosagesafety.xml`)
untouched — this is an examples-only sweep. Track FHIR-56016 (the
mis-placed `timing/doseAndRate` under `step`) as a separate change so
each ticket carries an auditable, single-issue diff.

#### Justification

The reporter's underlying complaint is "Examples on this page are
internally inconsistent across formats." Fixing only Example 1 leaves
Examples 2 and 3 in the inconsistent state that Mattias Colliander
identified on Zulip and that Jose has already volunteered to fix. Doing
them in one publisher cycle is also lower-cost than three separate
publisher runs against the same file: the FhirCore briefing's "update
narrative / prose" recipe applies identically to each, and the gotcha
about keeping all three (Structure / JSON / XML, plus YAML where
present) representations in sync is best satisfied by editing the page
end-to-end in one go. This is the disposition the proposer has already
recorded ("Persuasive with Modification") and the one Mattias agreed to
on Zulip.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket **Not Persuasive — out of scope for ballot dispositions**
on the grounds that the dosage-examples page is **Informative**
(per the page's own Standards Status banner) and that the structural
representation in Example 1 is itself valid FHIR — it is only the
co-located rendered text that gives it the misleading appearance of
inconsistency. Take no edit to `source/dosage-examples.html`; instead
add (in a follow-up editorial issue) a clarifying sentence at the top of
the page explaining that the rendered text on each example is purely
illustrative and does not imply numerical equivalence with the structure.

#### Justification

This disposition would be defensible only if the WG concluded that the
examples are deliberately decoupled from their narrative captions. The
current page text contradicts that ("Each of the examples below starts
with a textural representation of the dosage. In general, these should
be found in the `renderedInstruction` element…") and the resolution
already drafted by the proposer assumes the opposite. Decline therefore
has the highest community-friction cost (the reporter, the proposer and
a second Zulip participant all want a fix) and no offsetting technical
benefit.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **B**

The ticket is open with a proposed `Persuasive with Modification`
resolution that the WG lead (Jose Costa Teixeira) has already drafted on
Zulip and another implementer (Mattias Colliander) has agreed with. That
resolution corresponds exactly to Disposition B. Disposition A would
technically close the reporter's literal complaint but would knowingly
leave the page in the half-fixed state that Zulip has already
catalogued, almost guaranteeing follow-on tickets against the same file.
Disposition C contradicts the page's own framing and the proposer's
stated direction. Disposition B keeps the change confined to a single
page in a single repo (`HL7/fhir/source/dosage-examples.html`),
consumes one publisher cycle, and respects the FhirCore briefing's
guidance that this category of work is a direct edit to the authored
HTML page with no cross-repo or generated-artifact follow-on.
