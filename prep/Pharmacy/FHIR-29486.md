## Summary

FHIR-29486 reports that the interaction/workflow diagrams presented as
Figure 5A and Figure 5B in the US Prescription Drug Monitoring Program
(PDMP) FHIR Implementation Guide are redundant and confusing. The
reporter, Josh Mandel, argues that the two figures overlap substantially
and together obscure rather than clarify the intended PDMP query
workflow between the PDMP Requester (e.g., an EHR or pharmacy system)
and the PDMP Responder. The ticket asks the Pharmacy Work Group to
consolidate or rework these diagrams so that a single, coherent
depiction of the PDMP on FHIR interactions is presented.

The ticket was originally imported from the legacy ballot spreadsheet
(comment #36 on 2020-10-26). On 2023-01-18 it was dispositioned
"Considered for Future Use" (4-0-0, Hatem / McNeil) because the PDMP IG
had been withdrawn. On 2023-11-28, S. Robertson reverted that
resolution and reopened the ticket under the re-initiated PSS-2225
"PDMP on FHIR" project, so the issue is once again live and needs a
fresh disposition now that work on the IG is resuming.

No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were
returned by the orchestrator for FHIR-29486, so the request stands on
its own. Keyword-similar Pharmacy/PDMP tickets in the same IG family
(e.g., FHIR-29524, FHIR-29534, FHIR-29535, FHIR-29489, FHIR-45405,
FHIR-45908) are noted below for context but are not formal cross-
references.

## Proposed Dispositions

Because there are no GitHub cross-references or repo briefings to
anchor specific file paths, the dispositions below name the target IG
narrative artifact (Figures 5A/5B) and the authoring repository at the
IG level (the Pharmacy WG's PDMP-on-FHIR IG source, re-initiated under
PSS-2225). Exact file paths should be confirmed against the IG
repository once the workgroup re-scopes the PDMP IG project.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Persuant to Persuant's comments: Replace Figures 5A and 5B in the PDMP
on FHIR IG with a single consolidated interaction diagram that depicts
the PDMP query workflow end-to-end, showing: (1) the PDMP Requester
actor (EHR/pharmacy system), (2) the PDMP Responder actor (state PDMP
or aggregator), (3) the SMART Backend Services authorization step, (4)
the `$everything`-style query/response, and (5) the returned
`MedicationDispense`/`Bundle` payload. Remove the now-redundant second
figure and update the surrounding narrative (section headings, figure
numbering, and any cross-references to "Figure 5A"/"Figure 5B"
throughout the IG) to point to the new consolidated figure. The change
lives entirely in the PDMP IG authoring source (under the Pharmacy
WG's PDMP-on-FHIR IG repo that PSS-2225 will re-establish) — narrative
pages plus whatever source-format the figure is authored in (PlantUML /
drawio / SVG).

#### Justification

The reporter's concern (two diagrams covering overlapping content)
remains valid regardless of the IG's publication hiatus, and a single
normative figure is easier to keep in sync with related tickets —
notably FHIR-45405 (actor labelling), FHIR-29524 (adding pharmacies as
an actor), and FHIR-45908 (updating the intermediary flow). Accepting
as requested also avoids re-litigating the 2023-01-18 "Considered for
Future Use" deferral, which was explicitly overturned on 2023-11-28
when the IG was reopened under PSS-2225. Consolidation follows HL7
narrative best practice of "one canonical figure per interaction" and
removes a long-standing clarity complaint on a Highest-priority
ticket.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep two figures but re-scope them so the redundancy disappears:
Figure 5A becomes a **pure actor/role diagram** (PDMP Requester, PDMP
Responder, Pharmacy, EHR, Intermediary, Patient — aligning with
FHIR-45405 and FHIR-29524) with no message-flow arrows, and Figure 5B
becomes a **message-sequence diagram** (auth → query → response,
aligning with FHIR-45908's intermediary flow refresh) with no
actor-definition duplication. Update the narrative to introduce the
two figures as "actors" and "sequence" respectively, and cross-link
them so readers know they are complementary rather than alternative
views.

#### Justification

Many IG authors and reviewers find an actor-context view and a
sequence view genuinely complementary, and splitting responsibilities
between two figures often communicates more clearly than a single
dense figure. This alternative preserves the existing figure
numbering (minimising churn to other IG references, tables of
contents, and ballot comments that cite "Figure 5A"/"Figure 5B"),
addresses the reporter's actual complaint (redundancy) by forcing the
two figures to cover disjoint content, and dovetails with the related
tickets that are expected to land together when the IG is picked back
up (FHIR-29524, FHIR-45405, FHIR-45908). It does, however, require
more authoring effort and a conscious editorial guideline so the two
figures don't drift back into overlap.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-29486 as **Persuasive with Mod — Already Addressed by Later
Work** (or, if the reopened project scope prefers it, **Not Persuasive
— Considered for Future Use**), on the grounds that the PDMP IG figure
rework is being handled collectively by the newer PDMP tickets filed
under the reopened PSS-2225 project (notably FHIR-45405 "Label PDMP
Requestor and PDMP Responder" and FHIR-45908 "Update the intermediary
flow diagram"). Mark FHIR-29486 as superseded by those tickets and
track the figure consolidation there.

#### Justification

FHIR-29486 is a 2020 ballot-spreadsheet import with no captured
description beyond the title, and the Pharmacy WG has since opened
more specific, actionable tickets covering the same figures
(FHIR-45405, FHIR-45908) under the active PSS-2225 scope. Declining
this older, vaguer ticket in favour of the newer, scoped ones avoids
double-tracking the same editorial change and keeps the PDMP IG
backlog clean. The risk is that the "redundancy" complaint itself is
not strictly covered by FHIR-45405 (labelling) or FHIR-45908
(intermediary flow), so closing here without explicit hand-off could
lose the original reporter's point — which is why this is ranked
below A and B.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A (consolidate Figures 5A and 5B into a single canonical
interaction diagram) is the cleanest match for the reporter's actual
complaint and carries the lowest long-term maintenance cost: a single
figure cannot drift into redundancy with itself, and it aligns with
the companion tickets already queued under the reopened PDMP IG
project (FHIR-45405 actor labelling, FHIR-29524 pharmacy-as-actor,
FHIR-45908 intermediary flow). Disposition B preserves numbering but
costs more authoring effort and re-introduces the drift risk that
produced this ticket in the first place; Disposition C risks quietly
dropping Josh Mandel's concern behind more narrowly-scoped newer
tickets. Given the Highest priority flag and the fact that the IG is
being actively re-established under PSS-2225, the workgroup should
make the decisive editorial cut now rather than paper over it.
