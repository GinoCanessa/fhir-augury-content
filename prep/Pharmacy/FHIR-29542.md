## Summary

This ticket asks the Pharmacy Work Group to migrate the US Prescription
Drug Monitoring Program (PDMP) FHIR Implementation Guide off whatever
publishing tooling it had at the time of writing (2020) and onto the
then-new HL7 FHIR publishing template — i.e., the standard FHIR IG
Publisher toolchain (now typically driven by FHIR Shorthand / SUSHI and
the IG Publisher) used by other HL7 FHIR IGs. It is a tooling /
publishing-template request rather than a content change to any specific
profile, resource, or constraint. The cross-reference and Zulip searches
returned no related Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items, so
this prep is based solely on the ticket itself and its comment history.
The most recent comment thread (smrobertson, 2023-11-28) explicitly
re-opens the issue under PSS-2225 "PDMP on FHIR", reverting an earlier
"Considered for Future Use" disposition that had been applied while the
IG was withdrawn; the resolution text on the snapshot already records
that the updated IG "employs FHIR Shorthand, SUSHI and the FHIR IG
Publisher for consistency with other FHIR IGs", marking the request as
Persuasive.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Confirm and ratify the existing resolution: the US PDMP FHIR IG (and,
by extension, any companion content listed under the US Medication FHIR
IG umbrella for which Pharmacy is responsible) is rebuilt on the
standard HL7 FHIR publishing template — i.e., authored in FHIR Shorthand
(`.fsh`), compiled with SUSHI, and published with the HL7 FHIR IG
Publisher, replacing whatever bespoke publishing template was used in
the original PDMP IG. Concretely: adopt the standard `sushi-config.yaml`
+ `input/fsh/` + `input/pagecontent/` layout, the standard `_genonce`
/`_updatePublisher` scripts, and the auto-build CI pipeline used by
other Pharmacy IGs, and remove the legacy template artifacts from the
IG source repository. No normative content change is implied by this
disposition — it is purely a tooling and publishing-template
migration. Close the ticket as Persuasive (matching the snapshot's
recorded resolution) once the migrated build is published.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter requested in 2020 and what the
2023-11-28 reopening under PSS-2225 was intended to enable; the
resolution text already confirms the migrated IG uses FSH / SUSHI /
IG Publisher. Adopting the standard publishing template aligns the
PDMP IG with every other current HL7 FHIR IG (reducing maintenance
burden, enabling shared CI infrastructure, and giving implementers the
same navigation, downloads, and validation experience they get from
other IGs), and it costs nothing in spec semantics because no profile,
extension, or terminology binding is being changed. Ratifying it
formally also clears the stale "Triaged" status on a Highest-priority
ticket that has effectively been completed.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the migration to the standard publishing template (as in A),
but scope the ticket strictly to the PDMP IG source repository and
spin off a follow-up Pharmacy work item to (1) audit the wider US
Medication FHIR IG family for any sibling guides still on legacy
templates, (2) document a Pharmacy-WG-wide "standard IG template"
checklist (FSH layout, `sushi-config.yaml` conventions, IG Publisher
version pinning, auto-build setup), and (3) confirm cross-IG
consistency (shared `ImplementationGuide` resource patterns, common
`pagecontent` structure, shared terminology pages). Close FHIR-29542
on the basis of the PDMP migration alone, and track the broader
template-conformance work in the new ticket.

#### Justification

The literal request is narrow ("the new HL7 FHIR Publishing template"),
but the underlying need is "Pharmacy IGs look and build like every
other HL7 FHIR IG". Splitting the work avoids holding a 2020 ticket
open while a wider audit is performed, gives the Work Group a clean
artifact to drive cross-IG consistency, and keeps the disposition
auditable: FHIR-29542 documents a single concrete change (PDMP IG
migrated), and the new ticket carries the open-ended audit. This is
preferable to A if Pharmacy wants a forcing function for sibling IGs
without rescoping a closed-out request.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request as no longer actionable in its original form: the
ticket was filed in 2020 against publishing tooling that has since
been superseded twice (the "new" template referenced in 2020 is no
longer current), the IG was withdrawn in the interim, and the
resolution snapshot already records that the relaunched IG is built
with FSH / SUSHI / IG Publisher. Close as "Already addressed" /
"Persuasive — applied" with a pointer to the rebuilt IG, rather than
holding the ticket open for a now-historical template choice.

#### Justification

Treating this as Decline-by-completion avoids the workgroup re-opening
a tooling debate that the 2020-vintage wording can no longer usefully
frame: the toolchain landscape has moved on, and any *new* publishing
concern about the PDMP IG should be raised against the current build,
not against a five-year-old request to adopt a template that itself
has been replaced. If implementers have unmet needs against the
current PDMP IG, those are better captured as fresh, specific tickets
(e.g., "PDMP IG should adopt the latest IG Publisher version X.Y" or
"PDMP IG `sushi-config.yaml` is missing dependency Z").

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The snapshot's recorded resolution ("updated IG employs FHIR Shorthand,
SUSHI and the FHIR IG Publisher") already corresponds to Disposition A,
and the 2023-11-28 reopening under PSS-2225 explicitly asked Pharmacy
to revisit and ratify rather than to re-decline. Disposition A simply
makes that ratification explicit and closes a Highest-priority ticket
that has functionally been completed; it incurs no spec risk because
the change is template-only. Disposition B is reasonable if Pharmacy
wants a forcing function for sibling IGs, but that audit can — and
should — be a separate ticket rather than a reason to keep this one
open. Disposition C undersells what was actually done: the requested
migration did happen, so closing the ticket as completed (A) tells a
truer story than closing it as no-longer-actionable (C).
