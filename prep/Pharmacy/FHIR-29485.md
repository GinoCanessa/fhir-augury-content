## Summary

The ticket requests editorial/presentation corrections to figures and images in the US Prescription Drug Monitoring Program (PDMP) on FHIR Implementation Guide (originally filed against the "US Medication FHIR Implementation Guide" in Jira). Specifically, the reporter asks that embedded note text be pulled out of figures and rendered as figure captions, and that all images be regenerated at higher resolution so their text and diagrams are legible. No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip discussions, or GitHub items were returned by FhirAugury for this key, so the only additional context is from the ticket's own comments, which indicate the PDMP IG was withdrawn and the item was previously dispositioned "Considered for Future Use" before being reopened via [PSS-2225 PDMP on FHIR](https://confluence.hl7.org/display/PHAR/US+Prescription+Drug+Monitoring+Program+%28PDMP%29+FHIR+Implementation+Guide) on 2023-11-28.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Accept the editorial correction. When the PDMP on FHIR IG is reactivated (per PSS-2225), the workgroup will (a) move all note text that is currently baked into figure images out of the images and render it as figure captions / following paragraphs in the IG narrative pages, and (b) regenerate every diagram and screenshot at a higher resolution (target: source SVG for diagrams where possible, and at minimum 2× DPI PNG for raster screenshots) so text and arrows remain legible at standard and zoomed rendering. The change is scoped to the PDMP IG's narrative/image assets; no conformance resources (profiles, extensions, value sets, CapabilityStatements) are affected.

#### Justification

The request is a pure presentation/accessibility improvement and is aligned with general IG authoring best practice: caption text belongs in the rendered page (where it is searchable, translatable, and accessible to assistive tech) rather than rasterised into an image. Higher-resolution images also improve legibility on high-DPI displays and when printed. There is no downside to implementers because no normative content is being changed, and the original reporter (Josh Mandel) and the PDMP sponsor both flagged the issue as worth fixing. Classifying the prior "Considered for Future Use" decision as deferral-only (rather than rejection) is consistent with the 2023-11-28 reopen from smrobertson.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the intent but defer execution: mark the ticket as **Persuasive — Hold for IG Revival** with a documented author-guidance note attached to the PDMP IG project backlog. The guidance would say: "On the next substantive revision of this IG, (1) externalise note text from all figures into markdown captions adjacent to the image, and (2) regenerate source artwork from vector sources (SVG/Visio/drawio) at ≥2× resolution; use PNG only where a vector source is unavailable." The ticket would be resolved once that guidance is recorded, without any immediate IG change, since the IG is currently withdrawn and no publication cycle is active.

#### Justification

The ticket has sat open for ~5 years precisely because the PDMP IG was withdrawn; repeatedly voting on "accept" without a publication vehicle produces no implementer-visible change. Turning the request into standing author guidance (rather than a never-executed accept) preserves the substance of Josh Mandel's feedback, retires the Jira item from the Pharmacy triage queue, and guarantees the fix actually happens when the project sponsor picks the IG back up. This matches the spirit of the 2023-01-18 "Considered for Future Use" vote while avoiding the bookkeeping confusion that caused the item to be reverted and reopened.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve as **Not Persuasive — No Current Specification**. The US Medication / PDMP on FHIR IG is withdrawn, there is no active publication the change can be applied to, and the ticket describes purely editorial fixes (caption placement, image resolution) that carry no conformance impact. Close the ticket with a note that, should the IG be republished, the next editor is free to re-apply the editorial improvements as normal authoring hygiene without a formal change request.

#### Justification

Keeping editorial tickets open against withdrawn specifications distorts the Pharmacy workgroup's triage backlog and gives implementers the false impression that a fix is pending. The request is not tied to any conformance rule, resource, or element — it is a suggestion to an author about how to render figures. When (or if) the IG is revived, standard IG authoring practice already requires accessible captions and legible artwork, so the change will happen organically without a tracked ticket. Declining is defensible on scope grounds: there is nothing to change today, and a re-opened IG will pick this up through normal editorial review.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B best matches the actual state of the world: the PDMP IG is withdrawn (per smrobertson's 2023-11-28 comment), so Disposition A would "accept" a change with no publication to apply it to, and Disposition C throws away Josh Mandel's specific, actionable editorial feedback. Converting the ticket into durable author guidance attached to the PDMP project backlog — "captions out of figures, regenerate artwork at ≥2× or from vector source on next revision" — preserves the fix, clears the Pharmacy triage queue, and avoids the cycle of deferral/reopen that already happened once on this ticket. If the workgroup prefers to keep a live ticket against a withdrawn IG for tracking reasons, fall back to A; Disposition C should only be chosen if Pharmacy decides it does not want to carry any PDMP editorial guidance forward.
