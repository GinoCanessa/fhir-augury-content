## Summary

The ticket proposes a small editorial change in the US Prescription Drug
Monitoring Program (PDMP) FHIR Implementation Guide (tracked under the
"US Medication FHIR Implementation Guide" specification banner in Jira).
The reporter recommends rewording a sentence that currently reads
"State PDMP Systems have to support …" to instead read "State PDMP
Systems must support …" for readability and to align with the
conformance verb conventions ("must" / "SHALL") commonly used in HL7
specifications. No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip discussions, or
GitHub items were found, so this prep is grounded solely in the ticket
itself and its comment history.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the requested editorial change in the US PDMP FHIR
Implementation Guide narrative: replace the phrase "State PDMP Systems
have to support" with "State PDMP Systems must support" wherever it
appears (the ticket cites a single instance, but the change should be
applied consistently throughout the IG narrative if there are multiple
occurrences of the colloquial "have to" phrasing). No StructureDefinition,
ValueSet, CodeSystem, or example artifact change is required — this is
a pure wording edit in the IG's authored markdown/HTML pages. Confirm
that "must" here is intended in the conformance sense; if so, also
consider standardizing on "SHALL" to match the HL7 conformance verb
convention used in the rest of the IG.

#### Justification

The existing wording is colloquial and weaker than the conformance verbs
("SHALL", "must") used elsewhere in HL7 specifications. The change
improves readability, removes ambiguity about whether the requirement is
normative, and aligns the sentence with the conformance language style
expected by ballot reviewers. The cost is trivial (one or a few text
edits) and there is no impact on profiles, terminology, or examples.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than swap "have to" for "must" in isolation, perform a small
editorial sweep of the PDMP IG narrative (or at least the conformance
section that contains this sentence) to standardize on the HL7
conformance verbs — `SHALL` / `SHOULD` / `MAY` (or their plain-language
equivalents `must` / `should` / `may`) — wherever colloquial phrasings
("have to", "needs to", "is required to", "is expected to") appear, and
record the chosen convention in the IG's editorial guidance. Update
this sentence as part of that sweep so it reads, e.g., "State PDMP
Systems **SHALL** support …".

#### Justification

The reporter's underlying concern is readability and the strength of
the requirement. A one-off swap fixes the cited sentence but leaves
sibling sentences inconsistent, which is the same kind of issue that
generates further editorial tickets. Doing one editorial pass when the
IG is next opened for substantive work is more efficient than handling
each phrase as a separate ticket, and adopting `SHALL` more closely
matches HL7 conformance conventions than `must` alone. The trade-off is
a slightly larger change set and the need to confirm intent (normative
vs. informative) for each occurrence; both are manageable within a
single editorial pull request.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change as **not persuasive** at this time, on the grounds
that the US PDMP FHIR IG was previously withdrawn and the comment
history shows the ticket was deferred ("Considered for Future Use",
2023-01-18, 4-0-0) precisely because "This IG has been withdrawn so
these items will be deferred until the IG is taken up again by the
project sponsor and/or the workgroup." Mark as `Persuasive with Mod —
Defer` (or re-apply the prior `Considered for Future Use` disposition)
until the IG is actively re-opened under PSS-2225, at which point the
edit can be folded into the broader editorial review.

#### Justification

The change is editorial and has no implementer impact, so there is no
urgency to dispose of it independently of the IG's lifecycle. Spending
workgroup time on a single-word readability change while the IG is
between active publication cycles consumes review bandwidth that would
be better spent on substantive items. Deferring keeps the ticket
visible for the next editorial pass without committing the workgroup
to a publication cycle just to land it. The risk of decline-as-defer is
low because the original wording, while weaker, is not incorrect or
misleading.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

This is a one-line editorial fix with no normative impact, no
downstream artifact churn, and an obvious readability benefit. The
ticket has already been bounced once (deferred in 2023, then reopened
in late 2023 under PSS-2225), so a second deferral risks an indefinite
loop on a trivially actionable item. Disposition A clears the ticket
cleanly and aligns the sentence with HL7 conformance phrasing.
Disposition B is the better long-term answer if the workgroup is
willing to commit to a small editorial sweep at the same time — if
that appetite exists in the room, fold A's change into B and resolve
both this ticket and any sibling editorial tickets together. Disposition
C should only be revisited if the IG's status under PSS-2225 has again
stalled and there is no near-term publication target into which the
edit could land.
