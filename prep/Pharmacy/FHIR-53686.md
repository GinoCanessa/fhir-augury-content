## Summary

The reporter (Craig Newman) observes that the narrative of FHIR R6
`Dosage` section 14.7.0.2 references the `Timing` data type, but none
of the structured elements inside `DosageDetails` actually use `Timing`
— they use the new `RelativeTime` data type instead. As a result, the
only way to express the start/end of a `step` is *relative to an event*
(via `RelativeTime`), with no native way to express a calendar/clock
schedule (e.g., "every Monday at 09:00") at the step boundary level
unless the user falls back to the embedded `Dosage` carried inside
`step.simple` or `step.component`. The reporter asks whether
`step.start` and `step.end` should be choice elements typed as
`Timing | RelativeTime`.

No cross-referenced Jira tickets were returned by the cross-reference
service for FHIR-53686. There are no linked GitHub PRs/issues. There
is one substantively related Zulip thread on `#Medication > R6
DosageCondition vs. dosage-conditions extension` where Kari Heinonen
is exploring R6 `DosageDetails`, including the new `step.start` /
`step.end` elements typed as `RelativeTime`, and the Pharmacy WG
discussed gaps in the R6 design with Grahame Grieve and Lloyd
McKenzie. That discussion provides useful background but does not
itself resolve the reporter's specific Timing-vs-RelativeTime question.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In R6 `Dosage` (`DosageDetails`), change `step.start` and `step.end`
from `RelativeTime` (1..1 of a single type) to choice elements
`step.start[x]` and `step.end[x]` with allowed types `Timing |
RelativeTime`. Update narrative section 14.7.0.2 so that the prose
reference to `Timing` is honoured by an actual structural choice in
the model. Add at least one `Timing`-flavoured example to
illustrate a calendar/clock-bound step boundary (e.g., a step that
runs from a fixed wall-clock date until another fixed wall-clock
date, or that repeats on a `Timing.repeat.dayOfWeek` schedule).
Confirm that downstream constraints/invariants on `DosageDetails`
still hold for both type branches and update the data-type
walkthrough on `dosage.html` accordingly.

#### Justification

This is the smallest change that makes the structure match what the
narrative already implies. It addresses Craig Newman's underlying
observation that there is currently no way to express a non-event
timing at the step boundary except by escaping into the legacy
`Dosage` data type carried by `step.simple` / `step.component`.
Choice between `Timing` and a relative/anchored type is a precedent
already established elsewhere in FHIR (e.g., `Schedule`, several
Definition resources, and `MedicationRequest` itself uses
`Dosage.timing` of type `Timing`), so consistency with the existing
pattern argues for offering `Timing` here too. It is additive, so
no R5-to-R6-draft instances are invalidated.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Do **not** widen `step.start` / `step.end` to a choice. Instead,
either (a) extend `RelativeTime` so that an absolute-calendar anchor
(e.g., `contextReference` to an `Encounter`, `contextDefinition`
canonical, or a `dateTime`/`Period` literal) is a first-class option,
making `RelativeTime` capable of expressing "from 2026-04-01 to
2026-04-30" and "every Monday at 09:00" without needing `Timing`; or
(b) leave `step.start`/`step.end` as `RelativeTime` and direct
implementers to use `step.simple.timing` / `step.component.dosage.timing`
for any calendar/clock-driven schedule, and clarify section 14.7.0.2
to remove the misleading reference to `Timing` at the step boundary.
Either way, add worked examples covering the calendar-anchored case
that motivated the question.

#### Justification

The Zulip thread shows that Grahame Grieve's intent for R6 `Dosage`
is a deliberately consolidated model — "don't extend the design just
because the extension caters for a capability." Introducing a second
top-level timing type at `step.start`/`step.end` doubles the surface
area implementers must support and creates two ways to express the
same intent (especially when a step has only one component whose
embedded `Dosage.timing` already carries a `Timing`). Option (a)
keeps a single timing primitive at the step boundary while removing
the expressivity gap the reporter identified; option (b) accepts the
gap as intentional and patches the spec narrative to stop implying
`Timing` is used where it is not. Both options are less invasive
than introducing a polymorphic element on a brand-new data type that
is still under active R6 review.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Reject the request to make `step.start` / `step.end` a `Timing |
RelativeTime` choice. Instead, treat the ticket as an editorial
issue: correct section 14.7.0.2 so it no longer suggests `Timing` is
in play at the step boundary, and document that calendar/clock
schedules belong on `step.simple.timing` or
`step.component.dosage.timing` (i.e., at the dose level, where R5
already has `Timing`).

#### Justification

The reporter has identified a real *narrative* defect — the prose
mentions `Timing` where `RelativeTime` is actually used — but has
not provided a concrete clinical use case for needing a non-relative
timing at the *step boundary* (as opposed to inside a step's
component). Grahame Grieve's stance in the related Zulip thread is
explicit: real-world requirements with examples should drive any
extension of the R6 `Dosage` model, otherwise the model bloats and
creates the kind of overlap and ambiguity already plaguing
`DosageCondition` vs. the `dosage-conditions` extension. Declining
the structural change while fixing the narrative respects that
position and keeps R6 `Dosage` consolidated.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

R6 `Dosage` is being deliberately re-shaped to consolidate
expressivity (per Grahame's comments in the Zulip thread), and the
reporter's complaint is fundamentally that the *narrative* promises
`Timing` while the *structure* offers only event-relative timing. A
straight Disposition-A choice element fixes the narrative mismatch
but adds redundancy: a step with a single component would then have
two equally-valid places to put a calendar schedule (`step.start` as
`Timing`, *or* the component's `Dosage.timing`). Disposition B
addresses the underlying need without that redundancy: either by
hardening `RelativeTime` so it can carry calendar/absolute anchors
(closing the expressivity gap with a single primitive), or by
accepting that calendar timing belongs at the dose level and
correcting section 14.7.0.2 plus the examples. Either sub-option
under B is a more defensible long-term shape than B's outright
decline (Disposition C), which leaves the structure poorer than the
narrative already implies it should be. The Pharmacy WG should pick
between B(a) and B(b) based on whether implementers actually need
calendar-anchored *step boundaries* independent of any component
dose, ideally by soliciting one or two concrete examples from
prescribing systems before the next R6 draft.
