## Summary

The reporter asks the Pharmacy work group to strengthen a single sentence in
the security-considerations narrative of the US Prescription Drug Monitoring
Program (PDMP) FHIR Implementation Guide (tracked under the "US Medication
FHIR Implementation Guide" specification grouping). The current text reads
"All implementers of FHIR servers and clients should pay attention to FHIR
Security considerations." and the ticket proposes replacing it with "All
implementers of FHIR servers and clients SHALL follow FHIR Security
requirements." The intent is to convert a soft, advisory statement into a
normative conformance requirement so PDMP implementers are obliged — rather
than merely encouraged — to honor core FHIR security guidance.

The ticket has a non-trivial history: it was originally dispositioned
"Considered for Future Use" on 2023-01-18 (4-0-0) on the basis that the IG
had been withdrawn and would be parked until the project sponsor or
workgroup picked it up again. It was then reopened on 2023-11-28 by
S. Robertson under PSS-2225 ("PDMP on FHIR"), which signals the IG work has
resumed. There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets, no Zulip threads, and
no GitHub items linked to this ticket in the augury index, so the
disposition turns purely on the editorial and conformance question.

## Proposed Dispositions

> than at a specific repo path. If the workgroup confirms the active

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the PDMP IG's Security Considerations narrative, replace the sentence
"All implementers of FHIR servers and clients should pay attention to FHIR
Security considerations." with "All implementers of FHIR servers and
clients **SHALL** follow FHIR Security requirements." Render "SHALL" as a
normative RFC 2119 conformance keyword (bold/uppercase consistent with the
IG's other normative statements) and ensure the surrounding paragraph
makes clear what "FHIR Security requirements" refers to — specifically a
link to the core FHIR Security page (`http://hl7.org/fhir/security.html`)
and, where appropriate, to SMART App Launch and the relevant US Realm
security/privacy guidance the PDMP IG already cites.

#### Justification

The proposed wording aligns the IG with FHIR's standard conformance
vocabulary: SHALL is the established normative verb in HL7 IGs and is the
correct construct when an IG intends to make implementer behavior
testable. Given that PDMP is a high-sensitivity domain (controlled-
substance dispensing data subject to state monitoring statutes), elevating
security guidance from advisory to normative is consistent with how
neighboring IGs (e.g., US Core, SMART, Da Vinci) phrase their security
expectations. The reporter's wording also closes an obvious gap: "pay
attention to" is unenforceable and is unusual phrasing for a normative IG.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the spirit of the change but tighten the wording further and make it
specific. Replace the sentence with two sentences in the Security
Considerations section, for example:

> "All implementers of FHIR servers and clients **SHALL** conform to the
> [FHIR Security](http://hl7.org/fhir/security.html) requirements,
> including transport security (TLS), authentication, and authorization.
> Implementations exchanging PDMP data **SHALL** additionally follow the
> [SMART App Launch](http://hl7.org/fhir/smart-app-launch/) framework
> for client authorization where SMART is in scope, and **SHOULD**
> follow applicable US Realm privacy and audit guidance."

This breaks the single broad statement into a normative core (transport
+ AuthN/AuthZ), a domain-specific normative addition (SMART for PDMP
exchange), and an aspirational privacy/audit clause — each of which is
testable on its own.

#### Justification

"SHALL follow FHIR Security requirements" as a single sentence is a
substantive promotion to normative, but "FHIR Security" as a target is
broad — it covers a large surface (TLS, OAuth, audit, narrative privacy
guidance, digital signatures, …) and not all of it is realistically
testable for every implementer. Splitting the requirement makes the
SHALL enforceable and right-sizes the SHOULD. It also avoids the risk
that the bare wording "FHIR Security requirements" gets interpreted
inconsistently by PDMP testers and implementers. This is closer to how
other Pharmacy and US Realm IGs phrase security obligations.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline as **Persuasive with Modification — Defer**: agree in principle
that "should pay attention to" is too weak, but do not change the
wording until the PDMP IG's next ballot cycle is actively scheduled
under PSS-2225. At that point, fold this ticket into a single editorial
pass over the IG's Security Considerations section rather than applying
it in isolation against an IG that is still in a re-engagement phase.

#### Justification

The IG was withdrawn, was previously dispositioned "Considered for
Future Use" for that reason, and was only just reopened under PSS-2225
on 2023-11-28. Applying a normative wording change to a section in
isolation, before the workgroup has re-baselined the IG, risks creating
a normative obligation that does not match the rest of the (yet to be
revised) Security Considerations narrative, and risks being silently
overwritten when the section is rewritten as part of PSS-2225. Holding
the change until the editorial pass keeps the Security section
internally consistent.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The reporter's underlying concern is correct and should be honored:
"should pay attention to" is not normative language and is out of step
with how every other major HL7 IG phrases security obligations.
Disposition C captures a real risk (the IG is mid-re-engagement) but
that risk is best mitigated by *making* the change carefully, not by
deferring it again — the ticket has already been deferred once for
exactly this reason and was reopened precisely because the IG is being
picked back up. Disposition A is acceptable, but "SHALL follow FHIR
Security requirements" is so broad that it is hard to test and risks
being interpreted differently by different implementers in a high-
sensitivity domain (controlled-substance data). Disposition B keeps the
normative promotion the reporter asked for, names the specific
conformance targets (FHIR Security core, SMART App Launch where
applicable), and right-sizes the SHALL/SHOULD split — which is what the
PDMP work under PSS-2225 will need anyway. Recommend the workgroup
adopt Disposition B and apply it as part of the PSS-2225 editorial
pass on the Security Considerations page.
