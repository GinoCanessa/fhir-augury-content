## Summary

The ticket asserts that the directional model shown in Figure 1, Interaction 2
of the (then-current) US Prescription Drug Monitoring Program (PDMP) FHIR
Implementation Guide is incomplete. The reporter notes that pharmacies — in
addition to prescribers — are required in many U.S. states to query PDMPs in
order to assess a patient's controlled-substance utilization before dispensing.
Because of that, the arrow on Interaction 2 should flow in both directions, and
the IG's scope should explicitly include the pharmacy-to-PDMP query path. The
ticket was originally deferred ("Considered for Future Use", 2023-01-18) when
the IG was withdrawn, then reopened on 2023-11-28 once the PDMP-on-FHIR project
was re-chartered (PSS-2225), and was ultimately resolved Persuasive on
2023-11-29 with a note that the new IG clarifies pharmacies as both requesters
and recipients of PDMP records. No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip
threads, or GitHub items were surfaced for this issue.

## Proposed Dispositions

> Note: The ticket has already been resolved Persuasive (2023-11-29) with the
> understanding that the rewritten PDMP IG clarifies pharmacies as both
> requesters and recipients of PDMP data. The dispositions below frame how the
> change should land in the IG narrative and content models so that the
> workgroup can confirm or refine the executed disposition.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update Figure 1 of the US PDMP FHIR Implementation Guide so that the arrow on
Interaction 2 is rendered as bidirectional (pharmacy ⇄ PDMP), and revise the
surrounding narrative to explicitly state that pharmacies — not only
prescribers — query PDMPs prior to dispensing controlled substances where state
law requires. Add the pharmacy-to-PDMP query path to the IG's in-scope actor
list and supporting use-case text, and ensure that any CapabilityStatement or
actor/transaction tables in the IG include "Pharmacy" (or "Dispenser") as a
PDMP requesting actor for the same query operations defined for prescribers.

#### Justification

State PDMP statutes broadly require dispensers to check the PDMP at or before
dispensing controlled substances; modeling Interaction 2 as one-directional
misrepresents real-world workflow and would leave a major class of conforming
implementers outside the IG's scope. Treating pharmacies symmetrically with
prescribers is consistent with how the underlying PDMP query/response
exchanges work technically — the actor's role differs but the FHIR transaction
surface is the same — so adding the pharmacy actor reuses existing artifacts
without inventing new ones. This is also the disposition the workgroup
effectively accepted in 2023-11-29.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than expanding Figure 1's Interaction 2 to a single bidirectional arrow,
introduce a distinct interaction (e.g., Interaction 2a "Prescriber → PDMP" and
Interaction 2b "Pharmacy/Dispenser → PDMP") with its own actor, trigger
(dispense-time check), and conformance expectations. Profile or constrain the
PDMP query/response artifacts per actor where the data needs differ
(e.g., dispense context, pharmacy identifiers, board-of-pharmacy attestations),
and document state-law variability in a dedicated "Pharmacy access to PDMP"
section.

#### Justification

A single bidirectional arrow can hide that the prescriber and pharmacist
workflows differ in trigger, legal basis, and the contextual data that should
accompany the query (e.g., NCPDP pharmacy identifiers, dispense intent,
controlled-substance schedule filters). Splitting the interaction makes
conformance testable per actor, lets the IG carry actor-specific guidance
(including state-law variability), and avoids the trap of "one arrow fits all"
that often produces ambiguous CapabilityStatements. This is more work than
Disposition A but produces a sharper, more implementable IG.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Leave Figure 1 / Interaction 2 unchanged and keep pharmacy access to PDMPs out
of the IG's normative scope, on the basis that the IG is currently scoped to
prescriber-initiated PDMP queries and that pharmacy-side PDMP access is
adequately governed by NCPDP and state-specific exchanges that already exist
outside FHIR.

#### Justification

This would only be defensible if the workgroup deliberately chose a
prescriber-only scope for the rewritten IG and intended pharmacy PDMP access
to be addressed by a separate specification. Given the resolver's note that
the new IG already clarifies pharmacies as both requesters and recipients of
PDMP records, this disposition contradicts the executed resolution and is
included only for completeness.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A matches what the workgroup actually resolved on 2023-11-29 and
matches the regulatory reality that, in most U.S. states, pharmacies are
required to query the PDMP at or before dispensing controlled substances.
Excluding the pharmacy query path (Disposition C) would leave the IG out of
step with state law and with the rewritten PDMP-on-FHIR project (PSS-2225).
Disposition B is technically attractive — actor-split interactions yield
cleaner conformance — but is a larger editorial change and can be layered on
top of Disposition A in a later revision once the bidirectional scope is
established. The pragmatic path is to confirm the bidirectional Figure 1
update and the explicit "Pharmacy" actor in the new IG, then decide separately
whether to formally split the interaction.
