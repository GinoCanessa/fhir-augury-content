## Summary

This ticket, raised against the US Prescription Drug Monitoring Program
(PDMP) FHIR Implementation Guide (filed under the broader "US Medication
FHIR Implementation Guide" specification slot), asks the IG authors to
make it explicit that the *EHR-to-intermediary* leg of a PDMP query
does **not** have to be NCPDP. The reporter (Devann Kirkpatrick)
observes that, while the *intermediary-to-PDMP* leg requires NCPDP,
the EHR-to-intermediary leg is in practice carried over a variety of
standards (FHIR, PMIX/NIEM, NCPDP). Surfacing this flexibility in the
IG narrative would, in the reporter's view, lower the barrier for
additional EHRs to integrate with PDMPs.

The ticket carries no cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or
GitHub items in the Augury index. Its trajectory is captured entirely
in its own comments: it was first dispositioned **Considered for Future
Use** on 2023-01-18 (Hatem / McNeil, 4-0-0) on the grounds that the
PDMP IG had been withdrawn pending project-sponsor revival; that
disposition was reverted on 2023-11-28 by smrobertson when **PSS-2225
PDMP on FHIR** reactivated the project, and the ticket was reopened.
It was subsequently resolved on 2023-11-29 as **Not Persuasive with
Modification** with the rationale that "The Use Case page now
includes info about the participants, standards used, and where FHIR
fits in the IG" — i.e., the IG was updated, but not in the exact
wording the reporter proposed.

## Proposed Dispositions

> Note: this ticket is already resolved in Jira as **Not Persuasive
> with Modification**. The dispositions below are framed as choices the
> Pharmacy WG can take if the ticket is revisited (e.g., during the
> next ballot of the revived PDMP-on-FHIR IG, or in response to a
> reconsideration request from the reporter).

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the PDMP FHIR IG's narrative (currently the **Use Case** page, per
the existing resolution), add an explicit clarifying paragraph in the
form the reporter proposed:

> "While the message from the intermediary to the PDMP requires NCPDP,
> the message from the EHR to the intermediary can be carried over a
> variety of standards, including FHIR, PMIX/NIEM, and NCPDP. The
> choice of EHR-to-intermediary transport is a deployment decision and
> is not constrained by this IG."

Place this paragraph adjacent to the existing description of
participants and standards on the Use Case page, and add a matching
diagram annotation (or callout) on any sequence/architecture diagram
that depicts the EHR ↔ intermediary ↔ PDMP flow, so the flexibility is
visible both in prose and in the picture. Cross-link the paragraph
from the IG's "Scope" / "Boundaries" section so a reader landing on
either page sees the same statement.

#### Justification

The reporter's concern is a *clarity* concern, not a conformance
concern: the current wording is technically compatible with multiple
EHR-to-intermediary transports, but does not say so. Stating it
explicitly costs the IG nothing in terms of conformance, removes a
known source of implementer confusion (raised by an industry
participant), and aligns the narrative with how PDMP integrations are
deployed in practice. Adopting the reporter's exact phrasing also
restores reporter goodwill on a ticket that has been deferred,
withdrawn, reopened, and partially addressed over a multi-year window.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach (Confirm "Modification" already shipped)

#### Proposal

Treat the existing 2023-11-29 resolution as sufficient and close the
ticket as **Not Persuasive with Modification**, but strengthen the
modification before the next publication by:

1. Adding a short, clearly-titled subsection on the Use Case page
   ("EHR-to-intermediary transport options") that enumerates the
   transports observed in the field (FHIR, PMIX/NIEM, NCPDP) and
   states that this IG does not constrain that leg.
2. Adding a one-line note in the IG's Introduction and in any
   conformance-overview section pointing readers at that subsection.
3. Recording the change against this ticket key in the IG's change-log
   so the reporter can see exactly what was done in response.

No new conformance artifacts (profiles, extensions, value sets, capability
statements) are added — the change is purely narrative.

#### Justification

The IG's current author position is that the Use Case page already
covers this ground; the reporter's concern is that it does not cover
it *visibly enough*. A targeted narrative enhancement plus a
change-log entry honours the existing resolution category ("Not
Persuasive with Modification") while measurably closing the
clarity gap that motivated the ticket. It also avoids appearing to
reverse a prior workgroup vote, which would be procedurally awkward
given the ticket's history.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Persuasive but Already Resolved** (or
re-affirm the existing **Not Persuasive with Modification**
disposition without further IG changes), on the grounds that the
2023-11-29 update to the Use Case page already records the
participants, the standards in use, and where FHIR fits — which
collectively communicate the flexibility the reporter is asking for.

#### Justification

This is defensible if the WG considers the current Use Case page text
adequate and does not want to keep iterating on a ticket whose
underlying ask has been substantively addressed. It avoids re-opening
narrative debates on an IG that has only recently been reactivated
under PSS-2225, and it lets the WG focus reviewer cycles on
conformance-bearing content. The risk is that the reporter (and other
implementers) continue to read the IG and *not* see the flexibility
called out, which is the exact gap the ticket was filed to close.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the best fit for where this ticket actually sits.
The Pharmacy WG has already voted to make a modification (the
2023-11-29 resolution) and the IG narrative was changed in response,
so a clean "Accept As Requested" (A) understates what has already
happened, and a flat decline (C) understates the real clarity gap the
reporter identified. Disposition B keeps the existing resolution
category intact, but commits to one concrete, low-cost narrative
enhancement (an "EHR-to-intermediary transport options" subsection
plus a change-log entry citing FHIR-29479) so the reporter — and any
future implementer asking the same question — can point at a specific
paragraph rather than at "info … in the Use Case page." Given the IG
is being revived under PSS-2225, folding this into the next
publication is cheap and protects the IG against the same comment
being re-filed by another implementer.
