## Summary

The ticket asks the Pharmacy work group to copy-edit a normative
("SHALL") statement in the US Prescription Drug Monitoring Program
(PDMP) FHIR Implementation Guide that describes the information a PDMP
Responder must collect about each PDMP Requestor. The reporter (Josh
Mandel) flags two distinct problems with the current text:

1. The phrase "Requestor Identity namely name" is ungrammatical and the
   nested-bullet markup ("** ...") appears to be a wiki-syntax error
   that has leaked into the rendered guide. There is also a typo
   ("Rqquestor").
2. Beyond the prose issue, the requirement itself is conceptually
   unclear: the bulleted list mixes individual-level identifiers
   (name, role, DEA, NPI) with facility-level information, while the
   surrounding registration language elsewhere in the guide implies
   that registration may occur at the *provider organization* level
   rather than the *individual* level. The reporter questions whether
   the IG actually means to mandate per-individual identity capture in
   every case.

No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were
returned by Augury for this ticket. Comment history shows that this
issue was originally dispositioned "Considered for Future Use" on
2023-01-18 (4-0-0, John Hatem / Tim McNeil) when the PDMP IG was
withdrawn, and was then re-opened on 2023-11-28 by smrobertson under
PSS-2225 (PDMP on FHIR project resumption).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat this as a pure editorial fix in the next publication of the US
PDMP FHIR Implementation Guide:

1. Correct the typo "Rqquestor" → "Requestor".
2. Re-render the broken nested-bullet markup. The intended structure
   is a single requirement with a sub-list of data elements; replace
   the inline `**` markers with a properly-formatted bulleted list:

   > The PDMP Responder **SHALL** collect the following information
   > about each PDMP Requestor, for both auditing and unique
   > identification of the requestor:
   >
   > - Requestor identity (name)
   > - Requestor role
   > - Requestor DEA number and NPI number
   > - Requestor facility information

3. Replace "Requestor Identity namely name" with the unambiguous
   phrasing "Requestor identity (name)" so the phrase reads as a
   gloss rather than a malformed list item.

No conformance, cardinality, or data-element semantics change — this
is exclusively prose and markup.

#### Justification

The reporter's first complaint is unambiguously editorial: the source
text has a typo, a markup artifact, and a phrase ("namely name") that
cannot be parsed as English. Fixing those makes the SHALL statement
readable without changing what it requires. As a Highest-priority,
long-open Triaged ticket on a guide that is being resumed under
PSS-2225, addressing the editorial defect first is low-risk and
unblocks downstream review of the substantive question.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Do the editorial fix from Disposition A **and** address the
substantive concern the reporter raised in the second paragraph by
restructuring the requirement so it aligns with the IG's own
registration model:

1. Split the single SHALL into two conformance statements:
   - **Organization-level (always required):** the PDMP Responder
     SHALL collect identifying information for the registered
     requesting organization/facility (organization identifier, name,
     address, and any organization-level credentials such as facility
     DEA where applicable).
   - **Individual-level (conditional):** when a requestor registers as
     an individual practitioner (rather than solely on behalf of a
     registered organization), the PDMP Responder SHALL additionally
     collect the practitioner's name, role, individual DEA number,
     and individual NPI.

2. Add a short narrative cross-reference back to the IG's
   registration section to make the org-vs-individual distinction
   explicit.

3. Audit-related guidance is preserved: whichever identity (org or
   individual) was used to authenticate the request must be the one
   captured for auditing.

#### Justification

The reporter's second observation is the substantive one — the
current bullet list reads as if every PDMP query carries
fully-populated individual identifiers, which contradicts the
registration text elsewhere in the guide that allows registration at
the provider-organization level. A pure copy-edit (Disposition A)
leaves that internal inconsistency in place and will likely generate a
follow-up ticket the moment the IG returns to ballot. Restructuring
the requirement now is a small additional cost while the section is
already being touched, keeps the SHALL conformance language intact,
and aligns with how PDMP queries actually flow in deployed systems
(EHR-as-organization vs. solo-practitioner). It also gives auditors a
defensible, consistent rule about which fields must be present for
which registration mode.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark "Not Persuasive" / decline. Rationale: the underlying US PDMP
FHIR IG was previously withdrawn and is only now being resumed under
PSS-2225; the requirement language quoted in the ticket may be
substantially rewritten as part of that resumption work, so a targeted
edit to the current text is wasted effort. Defer any change until the
project sponsor publishes the next working draft, at which point the
section will be re-reviewed end-to-end.

#### Justification

The 2023-01-18 disposition was effectively this position ("Considered
for Future Use… deferred until the IG is taken up again"), and that
disposition was only reverted because the project formally resumed,
not because the substance of the deferral reasoning was wrong. If the
section is going to be rewritten anyway, locking in a copy-edit now
risks creating churn against the resumption author's preferred
phrasing.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The ticket bundles two real defects: a clear editorial bug ("namely
name", broken `**` markup, "Rqquestor" typo) and a real
internal-consistency bug (the requirement text contradicts the IG's
own registration model on individual vs. organization registration).
Disposition A fixes only the first, leaving a known inconsistency
flagged by an external reviewer (Josh Mandel) sitting in a
Highest-priority normative SHALL — that will resurface at ballot.
Disposition C is defensible procedurally but wastes the work the
reporter already did to identify the substantive issue, and the
resumption under PSS-2225 is exactly the right moment to clean both
problems up together. Disposition B addresses both defects in a
single, scoped edit, preserves the SHALL conformance, and aligns the
requirement with the registration model already described elsewhere
in the guide. The work group should adopt B and assign the section
edits to the PDMP IG resumption editor.
