## Summary

The ticket asks the Pharmacy work group to clarify the IG text so that it
makes explicit that the use of NIEM (National Information Exchange Model)
is **not mandatory** for the affected exchange. The reporter questions why
NCPDP isn't being used directly by EHRs (or X12 by the parties already
using X12) and challenges the requirement to translate to NIEM, asking
whether NIEM is intended only as a canonical modeling tool and whether
the published mappings have been vetted with the standards actually in
use. The ticket originated against the US Prescription Drug Monitoring
Program (PDMP) FHIR Implementation Guide — currently tracked under the
"US Medication FHIR Implementation Guide" specification banner — and is
labelled `PDMP-old-issues`. There are no Jira, Zulip, or GitHub
cross-references on file, so the disposition history below is entirely
based on the ticket itself and its comments.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the (resumed) US PDMP FHIR IG, edit the narrative pages that discuss
the NIEM mapping so they state explicitly that **NIEM is not a required
exchange format** for PDMP data on FHIR — it is provided only as a
canonical/reference modeling artifact to align with PMIX/NIEM-IEPD
consumers. Concretely:

- Add a clear "Conformance / Use of NIEM" note to the IG's main
  narrative (typically `index.md` and any "Background" / "Mappings"
  page) saying that implementers MAY use the NIEM mapping for
  cross-walks but that conformant FHIR exchange requires only the FHIR
  artifacts defined in the IG.
- Where NCPDP and X12 are referenced, state that systems already
  exchanging via NCPDP SCRIPT or X12 are not obligated to translate
  through NIEM to be conformant with this IG.
- Add (or strengthen) a "Mappings" disclaimer noting that the NIEM
  mapping has been provided as informative guidance and identifying who
  has reviewed it (or flagging it as not yet vetted, if that remains
  true at the time of resumption).

#### Justification

This is the smallest change that directly answers the reporter's
concern, and it aligns with general HL7 IG practice: cross-standard
mappings are normally informative unless an explicit conformance
statement makes them required. Marking NIEM as informative also matches
the comment trail — the IG was withdrawn precisely because its scope
and mandatory-content boundaries were unsettled, so clarifying that
NIEM is a modeling aid (not a wire format) removes one of the larger
ambiguities before the IG re-enters ballot.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than only softening the language, restructure the IG so the NIEM
content is moved into a clearly-segregated **informative appendix**
(e.g., "Appendix: NIEM / PMIX Cross-Reference"), and have the
normative pages reference NCPDP SCRIPT / SCRIPT-Standard and X12
exchange semantics directly where those are the actual on-the-wire
standards used by EHRs and PDMPs. As part of this, commission an
explicit mapping-vetting step: have the Pharmacy WG (with NCPDP and an
X12 SME) review and sign off on the NIEM mapping table before
re-balloting the IG, and capture the review outcome in the appendix.

#### Justification

The reporter raised two distinct concerns: (1) NIEM appears mandatory
when it shouldn't be, and (2) the mapping itself may not have been
vetted by the standards communities it touches. Disposition A only
addresses (1). Splitting the IG into a normative core (FHIR + the
standards EHRs and PDMPs actually exchange) plus an informative
NIEM/PMIX appendix addresses both, prevents future readers from
re-raising the same ambiguity, and gives the WG a defensible answer to
"who vetted this?". The trade-off is more editorial work and
potentially a larger diff against the prior PDMP IG draft, but that
cost is acceptable because the IG is being re-opened anyway under
PSS-2225.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as **Not Persuasive — Out of Scope at this time**, on the basis
that the parent IG ("US Medication FHIR Implementation Guide" /
underlying PDMP IG) has been withdrawn and the underlying narrative no
longer exists in a published form to clarify. Defer the substantive
question (NIEM vs. NCPDP vs. X12) to whatever new project scope
statement governs the resumed PDMP work, where it can be addressed at
the design stage rather than as a ballot comment against text that is
not currently published.

#### Justification

This mirrors the WG's previous (now-reverted) action — *Considered for
Future Use* — and reflects the reality that the IG is in flux. If the
narrative the reporter is objecting to is not in any current draft,
"clarify the text" has nothing to bite on. Declining keeps the issue
out of the active backlog without losing the substance, since PSS-2225
is the proper vehicle for revisiting the NIEM question.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The ticket has now been explicitly re-opened by the PSS-2225 PDMP on
FHIR project (per smrobertson's 2023-11-28 comment), so Disposition C —
which essentially repeats the prior "defer" decision — is no longer
defensible: the project sponsor has come back, which is exactly the
condition the previous deferral was waiting on. Between A and B,
Disposition A leaves the second half of the reporter's concern ("has
this mapping been vetted?") unanswered, and the Pharmacy WG will
predictably field the same question again at ballot. Disposition B
costs more editorial effort up front but produces a structurally
clearer IG — normative FHIR + the real exchange standards (NCPDP /
X12), with NIEM moved to a clearly-informative appendix and a recorded
mapping-vetting step — and answers both halves of FHIR-29539 in a way
that is durable across the next ballot cycle.
