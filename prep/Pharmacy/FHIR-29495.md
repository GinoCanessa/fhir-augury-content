## Summary

Josh Mandel observes that the implementation guide (originally filed against
the US Prescription Drug Monitoring Program (PDMP) FHIR Implementation Guide,
now reclassified under "US Medication FHIR Implementation Guide (FHIR)")
contains invalid example search parameters in its documentation, and he
infers from that symptom that the IG has probably not been exercised
end-to-end. He recommends that the Pharmacy workgroup require the IG to be
formally tested — e.g., by building/validating the examples, running the
defined search parameters against a sample server, and confirming profiles
and capability statements are internally consistent — before finalizing its
requirements. He warns that other latent issues are likely present. No
cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were found for
this ticket, so its scope is driven entirely by its own description and
comment history.

## Proposed Dispositions

No repo-analysis briefings apply here because no GitHub cross-references
were surfaced. The dispositions below therefore target the IG's
authoring/build process at the workgroup level rather than a specific
source file.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat "the IG has been exercised end-to-end" as a publication gate for the
revived US Medication / PDMP on FHIR IG under PSS-2225. Concretely, before
re-balloting or publishing:

1. Require every example instance in the IG to validate cleanly against
   its declared profile in the IG Publisher (`qa.html` must show zero
   example errors for the profiles in scope).
2. Require every `SearchParameter` defined or referenced in the IG's
   `CapabilityStatement`(s) to be exercised against a reference server
   (e.g., at a connectathon or via a small automated test harness),
   with the resulting request/response pairs captured as examples in
   the IG.
3. Require the IG Publisher build to be green (no example errors, no
   broken references, no missing bindings) as a precondition for the
   workgroup to finalize requirements.

Record this expectation in the Pharmacy WG's IG proposal / project
artefacts for PSS-2225 and link this ticket as the source of the
requirement.

#### Justification

The reporter's concern is structural: invalid example search parameters
are a leading indicator that the IG has not been round-tripped through an
implementer. FHIR IG methodology (IG Publisher QA, connectathon
participation, and ballot-readiness criteria) already anticipates this,
and the HL7 publication process expects a clean Publisher build. Adopting
this as an explicit gate is consistent with how other HL7 IGs (e.g.,
US Core, SDOH-CC) treat example validation and connectathon testing as
prerequisites for publication, and it directly remediates the root cause
the reporter identified without committing the workgroup to any specific
content change.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than adding a generic "must be tested" requirement, convert this
ticket into a concrete, trackable set of narrower issues:

1. Triage the IG's current examples and `SearchParameter` definitions and
   open one child ticket per concrete defect found (invalid example, bad
   `expression`, unsupported `type`, mismatched `base`, etc.).
2. Close FHIR-29495 as "split" once those child tickets are filed,
   pointing at them from the resolution.
3. Handle the broader "IG should be validated before finalization"
   expectation through the Pharmacy WG's normal ballot-readiness
   checklist / PSS-2225 project plan, not through this ticket.

#### Justification

FHIR-29495 is effectively meta-feedback ("please test this IG") rather
than a specific spec defect. Meta-tickets are hard to dispose of cleanly:
they can neither be "Persuasive" (there's no specific change to make) nor
"Not Persuasive" (the concern is legitimate). Splitting the concern into
(a) concrete defect tickets the authors can fix and close, and
(b) a process commitment tracked in the IG project itself, gives both
halves an owner and a definition of done. This is consistent with how
HL7 workgroups typically handle "review everything" style ballot
comments.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve as "Not Persuasive — Already Addressed" (or "Not Related") on the
grounds that:

- The underlying IG (US Prescription Drug Monitoring Program (PDMP) FHIR
  IG) was withdrawn, and the ticket was only reopened administratively
  (smrobertson, 2023-11-28) in anticipation of the IG being revived
  under PSS-2225.
- The HL7 IG publication process already requires a clean IG Publisher
  build and encourages connectathon testing; any revived PDMP / US
  Medication IG will inherit those requirements automatically.
- The ticket does not identify a specific defect to fix; it expresses a
  general concern from 2020 about an IG version that no longer exists.

#### Justification

Pharmacy can reasonably argue that FHIR-29495 has been overtaken by
events: the specific IG the comment was written against is withdrawn, any
successor IG under PSS-2225 will be re-authored and re-balloted through
the normal HL7 process (which itself mandates Publisher QA), and no
actionable defect is called out. Keeping an open "please test the IG"
ticket on the Pharmacy backlog in perpetuity adds noise without changing
the workgroup's behaviour.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

This ticket is real ballot feedback — Josh Mandel's concern about unvalidated
examples is legitimate and was demonstrably correct at the time — so an
outright decline (C) understates it, and the IG's withdrawal doesn't retire
the underlying quality concern now that PSS-2225 is bringing the work back.
At the same time, "please test the IG" is not something the workgroup can
"accept" as a spec change (A): there is nothing to edit in a resource,
profile, value set, or capability statement. Splitting it (B) converts the
legitimate concern into (i) concrete defect tickets that the revived IG's
authors can actually fix and close against specific examples /
SearchParameters, and (ii) a ballot-readiness commitment attached to
PSS-2225 where it can be enforced. That treatment matches how HL7
workgroups usually dispose of "review everything" ballot comments and
avoids leaving a permanently-open meta-ticket on the Pharmacy backlog.
