## Summary

The reporter (Sandi Mitchell, comment imported by Jean Duteau as comment #37
of the original ballot spreadsheet) proposes that the US Specialty Rx
Implementation Guide raise its conformance level for the FHIR
`Patient/$match` operation on the *Specialty Rx Data Recipient* actor from
**SHOULD** to **SHALL**, on the basis that reliable patient matching is
critical to the success of specialty pharmacy data exchange. The ticket was
previously resolved as "Considered for Future Use" by an 11-0-3 vote
(Frank McKinney / Tim McNeil) on 2021-03-22 with the rationale that other
workflows in the IG can accomplish the use cases without `$match` and that
not all upstream data sources fully support `Patient/$match` today; that
resolution was subsequently reverted on 2021-06-23 by Lynn Laakso to
re-open the question after publication of STU1. The cross-reference and
Zulip indexes contain no other Jira tickets, chat threads, or GitHub items
linked to FHIR-30625, so the discussion record is limited to the comments
on this ticket and the prior workgroup vote.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Raise the conformance level of `Patient/$match` for the *Specialty Rx
Data Recipient* actor from **SHOULD** to **SHALL** in the US Specialty
Rx IG. Concretely:

- Update the Specialty Rx Data Recipient CapabilityStatement so the
  `Patient` resource entry lists `$match` as a SHALL-supported
  operation (`operation.extension[capabilities] = SHALL`, or the IG's
  equivalent `expectation` extension value).
- Update the IG narrative ("Capabilities" / actor-obligation pages)
  that currently reads "SHOULD: Support Patient/$match operation" to
  read "SHALL: Support Patient/$match operation".
- Add (or strengthen) the conformance text describing the minimum
  inputs the Data Recipient must accept on `$match` (at minimum the
  patient demographic parameters the IG already references) and the
  expected behavior on no-match / multi-match.
- Note the change in the IG's "Changes since STU1" / changelog page
  with a forward-pointer to FHIR-30625.

#### Justification

The reporter's stated rationale — patient matching is foundational to
specialty pharmacy use cases and the recipient is the actor that
benefits — is consistent with how comparable IGs treat identity
matching at the *receiving* edge of an exchange (e.g., the
Patient Identity Matching IG and Da Vinci IGs that mandate `$match`
for record-locator-style flows). Raising to SHALL gives implementers
a single, testable interoperability bar and removes the ambiguity that
"SHOULD" creates for procurement and certification. The 2021-03-22
"Considered for Future Use" rationale explicitly anticipated revisiting
the requirement after STU1 implementer experience; STU1 is now
published and the ticket has been re-opened on exactly that basis,
which makes a SHALL upgrade the most direct expression of the
workgroup's stated intent.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the base Data Recipient obligation at **SHOULD** for
`Patient/$match`, but introduce a layered conformance pattern that
makes a stronger requirement testable for implementers who need it:

- Define a new capability flag (or use an existing
  `expectation`/`obligation` extension) that says the Data Recipient
  **SHALL** support `Patient/$match` *whenever it claims support for
  cross-organization patient lookup*.
- Optionally, publish a derived CapabilityStatement (e.g.,
  `CapabilityStatement-specialty-rx-data-recipient-match-shall`) that
  servers can declare conformance to when they meet the stricter bar.
- Tighten the narrative around `$match` to enumerate the minimum input
  parameters and the no-match / multi-match response behavior so that
  a SHOULD-level claim is still meaningfully testable.
- Defer a base-level SHALL to a future major version once usage data
  from STU1 implementers has been gathered.

#### Justification

This addresses the reporter's underlying concern — that patient
matching must be reliable — without invalidating implementations that
the original 2021-03-22 vote was protecting (data sources that do not
yet fully support `Patient/$match`). It preserves a smooth upgrade
path: implementers that can do `$match` today can declare the
stricter capability immediately, while implementers that cannot are
not forced into non-conformance by an STU2 republish. It is also more
consistent with how FHIR generally manages tightening conformance
between published STUs (additive obligation profiles rather than
in-place SHOULD→SHALL changes that may be considered a substantive
change against an existing ballot result).

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Do not raise `Patient/$match` to SHALL. Reaffirm the prior 2021-03-22
disposition ("Considered for Future Use") with an updated note that the
workgroup has re-evaluated post-STU1 and continues to find SHOULD
appropriate. Optionally, add an implementation note pointing recipients
to `$match` as the recommended mechanism when they do support
cross-organization patient lookup.

#### Justification

The substantive reasons cited in the original 11-0-3 vote — that some
IG-supported workflows do not require `$match`, and that not every
upstream data source supports `Patient/$match` fully — are points
about the deployed ecosystem, not about the spec itself, and may not
have changed materially since STU1. There is no Zulip thread, GitHub
issue, or related Jira ticket in the FhirAugury index providing new
post-STU1 implementer evidence that the SHOULD bar has actually
caused interoperability failures. Without that evidence, raising to
SHALL risks creating non-conformant implementations of an otherwise
working IG and re-opening a settled vote on the strength of a single
ballot comment.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The ticket has a real underlying concern — patient matching is in fact
critical to specialty pharmacy data flows, and the reporter is correct
that "SHOULD" provides a weak interoperability guarantee. At the same
time, the prior 11-0-3 workgroup vote rested on concrete ecosystem
constraints (data sources that do not yet support `Patient/$match`),
and the FhirAugury record contains no new post-STU1 evidence — no
Zulip discussion, no GitHub issue, no implementer report — that those
constraints have been resolved. Accepting "as requested" (Disposition
A) overrides a documented WG vote on the strength of one ballot
comment; Declining (Disposition C) leaves implementers with no
additional clarity beyond what STU1 already shipped. Disposition B
splits the difference in the way FHIR conformance machinery is
designed for: keep the base SHOULD so existing conformant
implementations stay conformant, define an obligation/derived
CapabilityStatement that requires SHALL where the recipient does
support cross-org patient lookup, and tighten the `$match` parameter
and response narrative so that even SHOULD-level conformance is
testable. That gives the reporter the stronger requirement they want
in the contexts where it matters, without invalidating the prior vote
or breaking STU1 implementers.
