## Summary

Molly Reese is questioning a passage in the US Medication FHIR Implementation
Guide (the predecessor of what is now scoped under the US Prescription Drug
Monitoring Program (PDMP) FHIR IG) that says the IG "proposes future areas of
guidance on how to create new outpatient prescriptions." Her concern is that
creating new outpatient prescriptions is the established province of NCPDP
SCRIPT e-prescribing, and that the IG's text should reflect that boundary
rather than imply that an HL7 FHIR IG would itself define guidance on
originating new outpatient prescriptions. The ticket is effectively a request
to clarify (or remove) the IG's positioning so it does not appear to overlap
or duplicate NCPDP SCRIPT's well-established e-prescribing scope.

The ticket has had a turbulent lifecycle: it was previously dispositioned
"Considered for Future Use" on 2023-01-18 (John Hatem / Tim McNeil 4-0-0)
because the original IG was withdrawn, and was then reopened on 2023-11-28 by
smrobertson under PSS-2225 ("PDMP on FHIR"), where it now sits at status
Triaged with no assignee. There are no Jira, Zulip, or GitHub cross-references
recorded for this item.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the next published revision of the US PDMP FHIR Implementation Guide
(successor to the withdrawn US Medication FHIR IG, per PSS-2225), edit the
narrative passage that reads "It also proposes future areas of guidance on how
to create new outpatient prescriptions" and replace it with text that
explicitly defers prescription origination to NCPDP SCRIPT. Suggested
replacement: *"Creation and transmission of new outpatient prescriptions are
out of scope for this IG and are addressed by NCPDP SCRIPT e-prescribing.
This IG focuses on PDMP-related queries and reporting and does not duplicate
or replace SCRIPT-based prescription origination workflows."* No
MedicationRequest profile, value set, or operation changes are required —
this is a narrative-only change in the IG's scope/introduction page.

#### Justification

The reporter's observation is factually correct: NCPDP SCRIPT is the
nationally adopted standard for outpatient e-prescribing in the United
States, and HL7 has consistently positioned its FHIR pharmacy work to
complement, not duplicate, SCRIPT. Leaving the original sentence in place
invites confusion with implementers and regulators about whether HL7 intends
to issue competing prescription-origination guidance. A scope clarification
in narrative is low-risk, requires no normative changes, and aligns the IG
with the actual project charter (PDMP), which is about monitoring/querying
controlled-substance prescribing — not originating prescriptions.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than simply deleting the "future areas of guidance" sentence, rewrite
the IG's Scope section to add an explicit "Relationship to NCPDP SCRIPT"
subsection. That subsection would: (1) state that prescription origination,
renewal, change, cancel, and fill-status messaging are owned by NCPDP SCRIPT;
(2) state that this IG focuses on PDMP query/response and PDMP reporting
flows; and (3) call out any FHIR↔SCRIPT mapping work (e.g., via the
HL7-NCPDP collaboration / Da Vinci or PDMP project deliverables) that
implementers should consult when bridging the two. The offending sentence
about "future areas of guidance on how to create new outpatient
prescriptions" would be removed as part of this rewrite.

#### Justification

A bare deletion (Disposition A) addresses the reporter's literal complaint
but leaves implementers with no guidance on *why* the boundary exists or
where to go for prescription origination. Pharmacy-domain implementers
routinely confuse the FHIR / NCPDP boundary, so a positive statement of
scope plus a pointer to SCRIPT is more durable than a silent deletion.
Trade-off: it is a slightly larger edit and may require a brief Pharmacy WG
review of the SCRIPT-relationship language so the IG does not overstate or
understate the relationship between the two SDOs.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change request as no-longer-applicable. Rationale: the original
US Medication FHIR Implementation Guide that contained the offending wording
was withdrawn (per the 2023-01-18 disposition note), so the literal text the
ticket targets no longer ships in any current HL7 publication. Close the
ticket as "Not Persuasive – Already Addressed" or "Not Related to Current
IG," with a note pointing to the active US PDMP FHIR IG (PSS-2225) as the
forum for any equivalent scope-clarification concern.

#### Justification

The defensible basis for declining is that the artifact the ticket targets
is no longer published. The 2023-11-28 reopening note specifically frames
this and other PDMP-old-issues tickets as deferred until the IG is "taken up
again" — but the new PDMP IG is a fresh authoring effort whose scope and
narrative are being written from scratch, not by editing the withdrawn
Medication IG. There is therefore no concrete passage in a current
specification to fix; any analogous wording in the new PDMP IG can be
caught by normal ballot review against that IG's own narrative.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The reporter's underlying point — that an HL7 FHIR IG must not appear to
duplicate NCPDP SCRIPT's prescription-origination scope — is correct, has
durable value, and will recur for the new PDMP IG even though the original
withdrawn Medication IG is no longer published. Disposition C is technically
defensible but throws away a useful scope-boundary statement that the new
PDMP IG will benefit from. Disposition A fixes the literal complaint but
leaves the FHIR↔SCRIPT boundary implicit. Disposition B captures the spirit
of the reporter's concern, gives implementers a positive statement of where
SCRIPT ends and FHIR begins, and lands the change in the appropriate
artifact (the active US PDMP FHIR IG under PSS-2225) rather than in the
withdrawn Medication IG. The Pharmacy WG should assign this ticket against
the new PDMP IG and fold the scope-clarification text into its introduction
during the next ballot cycle.
