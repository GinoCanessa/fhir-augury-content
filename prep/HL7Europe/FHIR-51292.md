## Summary

The ticket, filed against the **EU Imaging Study Report (R5) (FHIR)** IG,
asks that the Imaging Manifest `DocumentReference` be required to carry
**both** a FHIR (ImagingStudy) encoding **and** a DICOM KOS (Key Object
Selection) attachment, so that a consuming client can choose whichever
representation best fits its capabilities. The reporter notes this was
"discussed and agreed" at an IHE connecthaton in Vienna. A tracker
comment indicates the change will be addressed in the newly proposed
IHE **Manifest-access to DICOM Objects (MADO)** profile, and cross-refers
to companion ticket [FHIR-51315](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51315)
("Imaging Manifest Encoding - Prefer ImagingStudy over DICOM KOS"),
which takes the opposite position — that ImagingStudy alone should be
used because requiring both adds burden on source systems and widens
the spec surface. Reading the two tickets together, the underlying
question is *how many* encodings a manifest producer must emit and
which side (producer vs. consumer) carries the burden of format
negotiation.

Companion ticket against the same IG that argues for the **opposite**
design: require only a FHIR ImagingStudy encoding on the manifest and
not DICOM KOS, on the grounds that requiring both adds burden to source

— "creator supports both, consumer selects" vs. "creator and consumer
each support one, with the other optional" — and a 2025-07-01 comment
from the reporter reluctantly endorses "creator produces both, client
picks" as the less-bad compromise. This ticket and FHIR-51292 are the
two sides of the same design decision.

## Proposed Dispositions

Note: No GitHub repo was surfaced for this ticket, so the dispositions
below reference the IG at the conceptual level (the **EU Imaging Study
Report (R5) (FHIR)** manifest `DocumentReference` profile) rather than a
specific authoring path. If a disposition is accepted, the implementer
should locate the manifest `DocumentReference` StructureDefinition in
the HL7 Europe imaging IG source and apply the change there.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the manifest `DocumentReference` profile in the EU Imaging Study
Report IG so that **each manifest instance is required to carry two
`DocumentReference.content` entries**: one whose `attachment.contentType`
is `application/fhir+json` (or `+xml`) referencing an `ImagingStudy`
Bundle/resource, and one whose `attachment.contentType` is
`application/dicom` referencing a DICOM KOS object. Implement this by:
slicing `DocumentReference.content` on
`content.attachment.contentType` (or on `content.format`); setting
minimum cardinality of each slice to 1 and the aggregate `content`
cardinality to `2..*`; binding `content.format` to a value set that
enumerates the two encodings (ImagingStudy-as-FHIR and DICOM-KOS) so
clients can negotiate via format rather than MIME sniffing. Add
narrative documenting the client selection contract and, if possible,
align the slice identifiers and `format` codes with the IHE MADO profile
so that the two specifications can be validated against each other.

#### Justification

This is what the reporter and the IHE connecthaton attendees agreed
to, and it matches "Option 1" documented by the MADO liaison in
FHIR-51315 ("The Content Creator supports both formats and the consumer
selects the preferred one"). In FHIR-51315 the original reporter
(JoshPriebe) explicitly conceded that "server produces both, client
picks" is the less-burdensome overall choice versus "server produces
either, consumer handles both", because it avoids a fragmented
ecosystem where each actor picks a favourite and interoperability
degrades. Slicing `content` rather than introducing a new element keeps
the change expressible in a FHIR profile without a base-spec change and
preserves round-trip with MADO, which already defines bi-directional
mappings between KOS and ImagingStudy.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep `DocumentReference.content` cardinality as `1..*` and **do not**
require both encodings on every instance. Instead, align the EU IG
text with IHE MADO's "Option 2": a Content Creator MAY produce either
a FHIR ImagingStudy encoding or a DICOM KOS encoding, but MUST
advertise which is available (e.g., via a `format` slice value set plus
a capability statement declaration), and an Imaging Document Consumer
MUST be able to accept either. Add an operation or search-parameter
guidance so a consumer can request its preferred encoding (e.g.,
`DocumentReference?format=…` or an `_accept`-style parameter on the
retrieval), with server-side on-the-fly conversion using the MADO
mappings where practical.

#### Justification

This mirrors the concern raised in FHIR-51315 that "server produces
both" duplicates payloads on every manifest and forces producers to
keep two encodings in lockstep, which is expensive for large imaging
archives. It also matches the negotiation pattern already widespread
in FHIR (content negotiation via `_format`, capability statements,
profile declarations). However, FHIR-51315 itself acknowledges the
risk that "each actor will pick their favorite, and we will be left
with a fragmented system", so this disposition needs a hard normative
requirement that **consumers** support both encodings (and/or a
registry/profile declaration that lets a consumer know up front what a
source supports) to avoid fragmentation.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request and keep the IG's current behaviour (ImagingStudy
only, DICOM KOS not required). Add a non-normative note in the IG's
manifest section referencing IHE MADO and explaining that dual-encoding
concerns are being tracked by MADO's Public Comment Open Issue and will
be addressed there rather than in this IG. Close this ticket as
"Persuasive with modifier — defer to MADO".

#### Justification

Both FHIR-51292 and FHIR-51315 end with comments stating that the
encoding decision "will be addressed in the newly proposed
Manifest-access to DICOM Objects IHE profile" and that the MADO Open
Issue "will be closed by the time of Trial Implementation MADO
version". If MADO is the canonical owner of the two encodings and
their mappings, binding a normative "both are required" rule into the
EU IG ahead of MADO's resolution risks contradicting whatever MADO
lands on (which could be either Option 1 or Option 2). Declining now
and pointing to MADO avoids a breaking change later. The cost is that
early implementers of the EU IG have no authoritative guidance on KOS
support in the interim.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A is recommended. It is what was agreed in Vienna, it is
the option the FHIR-51315 reporter explicitly endorsed as the
less-burdensome compromise, and it is the MADO "Option 1" already
documented by the IHE liaison on FHIR-51315. The main counter-argument
— that producing two encodings is expensive for sources — is real but
is outweighed by the cost of a fragmented consumer landscape under
Disposition B, especially given the EU IG's exposure to EHDS-scale
consumers that will not speak DICOM. Implementing this as a slice on
`DocumentReference.content` (rather than a new element or a base-spec
change) keeps the change local to the IG and easy to validate, and
aligning the slice codes with MADO's encodings preserves forward
compatibility with the IHE profile. Disposition C should only be
adopted if the workgroup concludes it cannot afford to ship normative
manifest guidance ahead of MADO's Trial Implementation version.
