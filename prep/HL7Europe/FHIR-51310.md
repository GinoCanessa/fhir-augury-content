## Summary

The reporter argues that, as currently written in the EU Imaging Study Report
(R5) IG, the `Composition.informationRecipient` extension is ambiguous and
asks the HL7 Europe Imaging work group to clarify (or remove) its usage.
Two competing interpretations are called out: (1) a *system* recipient — i.e.
the party that requested or received the report in a given exchange — which
the reporter argues should be carried as transaction-level metadata or in a
logging component rather than on the document itself, because Imaging Report
data exists at rest independently of any specific exchange; or (2) a
*clinical* recipient — i.e. the original requester / authorising provider for
the imaging study — which the reporter argues should be expressed via
`DiagnosticReport.author` (and, if it must also appear on the Composition,
constrained to match the source in `DiagnosticReport`).

The ticket explicitly cross-links the parallel HL7 Europe Extensions ticket
[FHIR-51204](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51204) ("Information Recipient
should include more guidance on Composition specific use-case"), which raised
the same ambiguity against the EU Extensions IG (where the
`information-recipient` extension is actually defined). FHIR-51204 was closed
as **Resolved – No Change / Not Persuasive** by the Extensions group, who
took the position that information recipient is legitimately a document-level
concept (consistent with HL7 CDA and the international FHIR Clinical Document
IG), and that recipient and requester are intentionally distinct elements.
This Imaging-side ticket therefore needs to decide what, if anything, the EU
Imaging Study Report IG should do *given* that the underlying extension is
staying — the practical question is whether to keep using it on the Imaging
Report Composition profile, drop it, or constrain/clarify how it is used in
the imaging context.

## Details

**Description:**

> As written, usage of Composition.InformationRecipient is ambiguous. It could be:
>
> (1) System recipient of the report: This suggest a separate composition should be generated per party that has queried for DiagnosticReports. This assumes a deployment or transaction, Imaging Report data exists at rest regardless of where it fits in a transaction.
>
> (2) Clinical Recipient: this could represent the intended recipient of the DiagnosticReport (Original Requestor / Authorizing Provider for the Imaging).
>
> If this intent is (1), we suggest including this instead in transaction-level metadata (or logging component).
>
> If the intent is (2), we suggest representing this in DiagnosticReport.author and removing the extension from Composition. If it must be included in the Composition as well, this should be constrained to match the source in DiagnosticReport.
>
> Related: [FHIR-51204](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51204) Information Recipient should include more guidance on Composition specific use-case.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the **EU Imaging Study Report (R5)** IG, remove the
`Composition.informationRecipient` extension slice from the
`ImagingReportComposition` profile. Replace any conformance text that refers
to it with guidance pointing implementers to `DiagnosticReport.author`
(and/or `DiagnosticReport.basedOn` → `ServiceRequest.requester` /
`.performer`) for the clinical "who ordered / who is the authorising
provider" question, and to transaction-level metadata
(`Bundle`/`MessageHeader`/`AuditEvent`) for the system-level "who is this
copy being sent to" question. Where the `Composition`-level slot is
retained anywhere in the IG, add an invariant requiring that any
`Composition.informationRecipient` reference, if present, resolves to the
same party as `DiagnosticReport.author` for the contained Imaging Report.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter asked for and matches their two-pronged
analysis: it eliminates the ambiguity by deleting the ambiguous slot from
the imaging profile rather than trying to disambiguate it with prose. It
also respects the at-rest nature of imaging reports — the same
`DiagnosticReport` may legitimately be returned to many queriers, so a
single document-level recipient is at best misleading. The Extensions WG’s
position in [FHIR-51204](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51204) was that
the extension itself stays available, but that does not oblige downstream
IGs to use it; an Imaging-IG-local decision to drop the slice is fully
within the EU Imaging WG’s remit.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach (Recommended)

#### Proposal

Keep the `Composition.informationRecipient` extension slot on the
`ImagingReportComposition` profile, but **constrain and clarify** it in the
EU Imaging Study Report IG so that the ambiguity is resolved in favour of
interpretation (2) — the *intended clinical recipient(s)* of the report at
the time the document was created. Concretely, in the IG:

1. Add narrative guidance on the `ImagingReportComposition` profile page
   explicitly stating that `Composition.informationRecipient` SHALL NOT be
   used to record per-exchange/system recipients (those belong in
   transaction-level metadata such as `Bundle`/`MessageHeader` or
   `AuditEvent`), and SHOULD be used only to record the intended clinical
   recipient(s) at the point of authoring (e.g. referring/ordering
   provider, primary-care provider receiving a copy, the patient).
2. Where present, recommend that one of the recipients be the same party
   referenced from the contained `DiagnosticReport.basedOn` →
   `ServiceRequest.requester`, expressed as a SHOULD-level guidance rather
   than an invariant (to allow standalone or speculative reports).
3. Optionally narrow the allowed reference targets to
   `Practitioner | PractitionerRole | Organization | Patient | RelatedPerson`
   and consider adopting a recipient-type code analogous to the
   international FHIR Clinical Document IG’s use of v3 `ParticipationType`
   (`IRCP`, `PRCP`, `TRC`) — either as a sub-extension or as a separate
   companion extension — so that "primary recipient" vs. "cc / tracker" can
   be distinguished without overloading the slot.

#### Justification

This addresses the underlying ambiguity (which is the actual ask) without
fighting the Extensions WG’s already-recorded resolution on
[FHIR-51204](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51204), which kept the
extension. It is consistent with the HL7 international FHIR Clinical
Documents IG, where `information-recipient-extension` is explicitly defined
as "the intended recipient of the information at the time the document was
created" — a definition the EU Extensions IG itself was noted (in
FHIR-51204 comments) to be slightly less precise about. It also responds to
the comments in FHIR-51204 from `mgraauw` and `JIRAUSER29715` that
recipients legitimately persist with the document at rest (e.g. HDR), while
still excluding the per-query "system recipient" reading that the reporter
correctly identified as untenable for at-rest imaging reports. Constraining
the Composition slot to align with the clinical recipient also prevents the
divergence the reporter is worried about between the Composition and the
underlying `DiagnosticReport`.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve as **Not Persuasive**, mirroring the Extensions WG’s disposition of
[FHIR-51204](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51204). No change to the
`ImagingReportComposition` profile or to the use of the
`Composition.informationRecipient` extension. The rationale would be that
the extension is already defined and resolved at the EU Extensions level,
and that the existing definition (intended recipient of the composition) is
sufficient.

#### Justification

There is a defensible consistency argument: the parent ticket on the same
extension was closed without change, and a second "no change" disposition
keeps the EU Extensions and EU Imaging IGs aligned with the international
FHIR Clinical Documents IG and CDA precedent. However, this leaves the
Imaging-specific ambiguity that the reporter identified — particularly the
"separate Composition per querier" failure mode — unaddressed in the IG
text, and risks the same comment being re-filed in the next ballot round.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the most defensible path forward for the HL7 Europe
Imaging WG. Disposition C effectively re-closes the same complaint without
addressing the Imaging-specific concern (separate Composition per querier),
which is what made FHIR-51310 a distinct ticket from FHIR-51204 in the
first place. Disposition A solves the ambiguity by deletion, but it also
discards a slot that the Extensions WG and the broader CDA / international
Clinical Documents community have deliberately kept, and that participants
in FHIR-51204 (`mgraauw`, `JIRAUSER29715`) gave concrete clinical use cases
for at the document level. Disposition B keeps that slot but pins down its
meaning in the imaging context to the *clinical* interpretation, explicitly
forbids the *system / per-query* interpretation, and aligns with the
existing international Clinical Documents extension definition. That gives
implementers an unambiguous rule, preserves alignment across HL7 Europe IGs
and with international work, and directly answers the reporter’s two
worked-through scenarios.
