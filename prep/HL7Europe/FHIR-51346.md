## Summary

The ticket flags an internal inconsistency in the EU Imaging Study Report
(R5) implementation guide. The "Design Considerations" page lists three
image access mechanisms for which Endpoint profiles "will be defined":
DICOMweb WADO, DIMSE, and "Launch a DICOM viewer". The remainder of the
IG, however, only describes DICOMweb (WADO-RS) and the DICOM viewer
launch (IHE IID) options — DIMSE is never elaborated on, and no Endpoint
profile or guidance for it appears anywhere else in the guide. The
reporter asks the work group to either drop DIMSE from the bullet list
or supply concrete guidance for how DIMSE would be used across the IG.
A subsequent comment on the ticket itself proposes removal, noting that
the EHDS imaging endpoints of concern are only WADO-RS retrieve and the
IHE IID viewer launch. The same comment also points to the closely
related ticket [FHIR-51296](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51296) —
"Clarify the Expectations for IID vs WADO Endpoints" — which asks the
work group to give implementers guidance on when to use WADO-RS vs IID
and whether one should be considered the minimum to support. Together
the two tickets argue for a coherent, narrowed endpoint story in the IG
that drops DIMSE entirely and clarifies the relationship between the two
remaining mechanisms.

## Related Jira Tickets

### [FHIR-51296: Clarify the Expectations for IID vs WADO Endpoints](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51296)

Filed against the same specification (EU Imaging Study Report (R5)) and
work group (HL7 Europe), with the same `manifest` label. The reporter
asks the work group to give implementers clearer guidance on when to use
WADO-RS vs IID endpoints — both are currently optional — and whether one
should be required as a minimum, citing concerns that supporting both
roughly doubles consumer integration cost. The ticket also raises
cross-border-specific issues for IID (viewer localization, NCP traffic
patterns, and end-user usability of foreign viewers) and argues that
WADO-RS should generally be preferred where available. A reply from the
imaging editor explains how the IHE MADO profile carries WADO-RS
endpoint info at series level and IID-style server-side viewer URLs at
study level, notes that WADO-RS is supported both intra- and
cross-community (XC_WADO) while IID has no cross-community variant yet,
and concludes that the choice between WADO and IID must be driven by
the use case. This ticket is the natural companion to FHIR-51346: where
51346 asks to *prune* the endpoint list (remove DIMSE), 51296 asks to
*clarify usage* of the two endpoints that remain.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested — Remove DIMSE from the Design Considerations list

#### Proposal

Edit the EU Imaging Study Report (R5) IG "Design Considerations" page so
that the bullet list under "Endpoint profiles will be defined for at
least the following image access mechanisms" reads:

- DICOMweb WADO
- Launch a DICOM viewer

Specifically, drop the `DIMSE` bullet. Also sweep the page (and any
other narrative text in the IG that paraphrases the same list) to make
sure no surrounding sentence still implies that DIMSE is one of the
supported access mechanisms. No StructureDefinition / Endpoint profile
changes are required, because no DIMSE Endpoint profile was ever
published in the IG; this is a narrative-only change.

#### Justification

This matches the reporter's preferred remediation and the imaging
editor's own follow-up comment on the ticket ("I believe the DIMSE
option should be removed. The end-points of concern for EHDS imaging are
the DICOMweb WADO retrieve and the image viewer launch (IHE IID)"). It
also aligns the Design Considerations text with the rest of the IG,
which already only describes DICOMweb and viewer launch. From an EHDS
deployment standpoint, DIMSE is a connection-oriented, intra-network
DICOM protocol that is not realistic to expose across NCP boundaries,
so listing it as a candidate access mechanism in a cross-border IG is
actively misleading to implementers.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — Keep DIMSE but reframe it as an out-of-scope/local option, and align the wording with FHIR-51296

#### Proposal

Rather than silently dropping DIMSE, rewrite the Design Considerations
section so that:

1. The bullet list is reduced to the two endpoint mechanisms the IG
   actually profiles: DICOMweb WADO-RS and DICOM viewer launch (IHE
   IID).
2. A short note immediately after the list explicitly states that
   classical DIMSE retrieval (e.g., C-MOVE/C-GET) is recognized as a
   valid intra-enterprise mechanism but is intentionally **out of scope**
   for this cross-border IG, and therefore no Endpoint profile is
   defined for it.
3. The same edit takes the opportunity to address
   [FHIR-51296](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51296) by adding one
   short paragraph that states the IG's recommended preference order
   between WADO-RS and IID for cross-border use (preferring WADO-RS
   where available, citing IHE MADO/XC_WADO for the cross-community
   case and IID's current intra-community-only limitation).

#### Justification

DIMSE is still the dominant in-hospital retrieval mechanism for many
European PACS deployments, so simply deleting the word may surprise
readers who expect to see it acknowledged. Calling it out as
deliberately out of scope is more informative than silence and prevents
future "why isn't DIMSE here?" tickets. Bundling the fix with FHIR-51296
also lets the work group close two closely related "endpoint story"
tickets in a single editorial pass, which is cheaper and produces a
more coherent narrative than two independent edits. The trade-off vs.
Disposition A is slightly more wording to agree on (the
"out-of-scope" sentence and the WADO-vs-IID guidance), but the IG ends
up clearer for implementers.

---

### Disposition C: Decline — Keep the current bullet list as aspirational

#### Proposal

Leave the "Design Considerations" bullet list unchanged, on the basis
that the prefatory phrasing — "Endpoint profiles **will be defined for
at least** the following image access mechanisms" — is forward-looking
and intentionally broader than what the current ballot/IG content
covers. Document in the ticket resolution that DIMSE is retained as a
candidate mechanism for a future revision and may be profiled later if
demand emerges.

#### Justification

This is the only defensible "do nothing" path. It would only be
appropriate if the work group genuinely intends to add a DIMSE Endpoint
profile and accompanying narrative in a near-term update of the IG. In
practice this seems unlikely: cross-border DIMSE has well-known transport
and security limitations under EHDS, no implementer feedback in the
ticket asks for DIMSE support, and the IG editor has already signalled
on the ticket that DIMSE should be removed. Declining therefore
preserves a known inconsistency the reporter has explicitly flagged.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition A cleanly resolves the literal complaint and is what both
the reporter and the imaging editor lean toward, but it leaves DIMSE
unspoken-for and does nothing about the closely related and still-open
FHIR-51296. Disposition B does the same removal as A — it also brings
the Design Considerations list down to WADO-RS and viewer launch — but
adds a single explanatory sentence about DIMSE being out of scope for
cross-border use and folds in a brief WADO-vs-IID preference statement
that addresses FHIR-51296 in the same editorial pass. That gives
implementers a more complete and self-consistent endpoint story, costs
very little extra editing effort, and lets the work group close two
related tickets together. Disposition C should only be selected if the
work group commits to publishing a DIMSE Endpoint profile in this IG,
which the current evidence does not support.
