## Proposed Dispositions

> Note: with no GitHub cross-references and no `repo-analysis` briefing in
> scope, the dispositions below name the IG (EU Imaging Study Report (R5))
> and the affected page (Imaging Manifest) but do not pin specific repo
> paths. The HL7 Europe Imaging editors will need to map these to the
> corresponding `pagecontent/` (or equivalent) source for the IG when acting

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the introduction section of the Imaging Manifest page in the EU
Imaging Study Report (R5) IG by:

1. Replacing the existing UML diagram at the top of the page with the
   conceptual overview diagram presented at DevDays (the figure attached to
   this ticket as `26130_image-2025-06-27-12-06-03-192.png`).
2. Re-authoring the surrounding narrative so that the new figure is
   introduced first as a high-level "what the manifest is and how it relates
   to ImagingStudy / ImagingSelection / DocumentReference" overview, with
   the detailed UML moved further down the page (or to a "Detailed model"
   subsection) for readers who want the formal view.
3. Adding alt text and a short caption to both figures so the page meets
   basic accessibility expectations.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter requested and matches the stated problem:
the existing UML is too dense to serve as the reader's first impression of
the manifest, and the DevDays overview has already been validated with a
live audience as a clearer entry point. Keeping the UML on the page (rather
than deleting it) preserves the precise model semantics for implementers
while restoring the introduction's role as orientation. Adding alt text and
captions is consistent with HL7's general accessibility guidance for IG
pages.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than swap one image for another, restructure the Imaging Manifest
page introduction into a layered explanation:

1. **Lead paragraph** — a 2–4 sentence plain-language description of what
   the Imaging Manifest is, who creates it, and who consumes it, with no
   diagram.
2. **"At a glance" figure** — a simplified, IG-authored SVG (not a
   screenshot of a slide) derived from the DevDays overview, so it can be
   re-themed, localized, and kept in sync with future changes. Source the
   SVG from the IG repo so it is diff-able.
3. **"Detailed information model" figure** — keep the existing UML diagram
   but move it under its own subheading later in the page.
4. Cross-link from the introduction to the relevant `ImagingStudy`,
   `ImagingSelection`, and `DocumentReference` profile pages so readers
   can drill in from the overview.

#### Justification

Embedding a PNG of a conference slide solves the immediate accessibility
complaint but creates a maintenance hazard: the figure can't be edited
without a round-trip through slide software, alt text is harder to keep
accurate, and the visual style will drift from the rest of the IG. An
authored SVG plus a layered narrative addresses the same reader-experience
problem more durably and aligns with how other HL7 IGs handle introductory
diagrams. The trade-off vs. Disposition A is editorial effort up front in
exchange for a lower long-term cost.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change as a documentation-only enhancement that does not block
any normative content, and close as "Not Persuasive — Editorial". Capture
the suggestion in the IG's editorial backlog to be considered when the
Imaging Manifest page is next revised for substantive reasons, and close
this ticket without a spec change.

#### Justification

The current page is technically correct: the UML diagram accurately conveys
the model, even if it is dense. There are no implementer reports of being
unable to use the manifest because of the introduction, no Zulip discussion
indicating community demand, and no linked tickets. Spending editorial
cycles to re-do the introduction in isolation could be deferred until the
next planned revision of the page so the work can be batched.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter's complaint is concrete (the UML is not accessible as an
introduction), the proposed remedy already exists (the DevDays overview),
and the change is scoped to a single page. Disposition A delivers the
requested improvement immediately and at low risk, while the suggested
refinement of keeping the UML further down the page preserves the formal
model for implementers. Disposition B is editorially preferable in the long
run but adds work that is hard to justify with no other tickets driving a
broader page revision; it would be a reasonable follow-up. Disposition C
under-serves the reporter, who has identified a real readability problem
and has done most of the work by surfacing a candidate replacement figure.
