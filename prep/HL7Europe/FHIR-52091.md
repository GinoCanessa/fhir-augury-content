## Summary

The reporter requests that two narrative chapters in the EU Hospital Discharge
Report (HDR) FHIR Implementation Guide — section 10.2 "Leveraging ISO IDMP"
and section 10.3 "HL7 FHIR versions" — be removed. Both sections were copied
verbatim from the EU Medication Prescription and Dispense (MPD) IG and, in
the reporter's view, are not contextually relevant to the HDR. The change
is editorial/structural rather than a model change: it removes IG narrative
content without altering any FHIR resources, profiles, value sets, or
examples. The ticket carries the `block-vote-1` label, indicating HL7 Europe
intends to dispose of it as part of a block ballot disposition vote.

No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items are
recorded against this ticket in the FhirAugury index.

## Details

**Description:**

The chapters 10.2 "Leveraging ISO IDMP" and 10.3 "HL7 FHIR versions" are a

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Remove sections 10.2 ("Leveraging ISO IDMP") and 10.3 ("HL7 FHIR versions")
in their entirety from the EU Hospital Discharge Report IG. Concretely:

- Delete (or unpublish) the two narrative pages from the IG's authoring
  source so they no longer render in the table of contents or in chapter 10.
- Renumber any subsequent subsections of chapter 10 so the published TOC
  remains contiguous.
- Audit the rest of the HDR IG for inbound links to the removed sections
  (cross-page references, "see section 10.2" wording, sidebar / index
  entries) and either remove those links or repoint them — most likely to
  the corresponding chapters in the MPD IG, which is where the content
  actually belongs.
- Update any release notes / change log entry for the next HDR build to
  record the removal.

#### Justification

The reporter's diagnosis matches a well-understood HL7 Europe IG pattern:
narrative scaffolding from a sibling IG (MPD) was carried over during the
HDR's bootstrap and never re-scoped. ISO IDMP framing and a generic "HL7
FHIR versions" overview are both unrelated to a discharge-report use case
and risk misleading implementers into thinking IDMP alignment is part of
HDR's conformance posture. Removing duplicated, off-topic narrative is a
zero-risk editorial change — there are no resource, profile, terminology,
or example artifacts to migrate — and tightens the IG's scope ahead of the
block vote.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than delete the sections outright, replace them with short stub
sections (one or two paragraphs each) that explicitly delegate to the
MPD IG:

- 10.2 becomes a brief note that ISO IDMP alignment is handled in the EU
  MPD IG, with a link to the MPD chapter of origin and a one-line statement
  of why HDR does not redefine that material.
- 10.3 becomes a brief note pointing to the MPD IG (or to the HL7 base
  spec) for the canonical "HL7 FHIR versions" discussion, with a single
  sentence stating which FHIR version HDR itself is built against.

If chapter 10 would be left with no remaining subsections after the
removal, fold its remaining content up one level instead of leaving an
orphan heading.

#### Justification

Stubs preserve any incoming external links (other IGs, Confluence pages,
review comments) that may already point at HDR §10.2 / §10.3 anchors, and
they make the relationship between HDR and MPD explicit for first-time
readers, who may otherwise wonder why HDR is silent on IDMP. The cost is
small — two short paragraphs — and the payoff is that implementers are
actively redirected to the right IG instead of being left to guess. This is
particularly valuable while HDR is still pre-publication and external
deep-links to the IG are likely to be rare but not zero.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Keep sections 10.2 and 10.3 as currently published, on the basis that the
content — while inherited from MPD — provides useful background for HDR
implementers who are coming to the IG without prior HL7 Europe context.

#### Justification

A defensible decline argument would be that "Leveraging ISO IDMP" gives
implementers a standards-landscape orientation they would otherwise have to
hunt for, and that "HL7 FHIR versions" is generic enough to be harmless in
any IG. Declining avoids re-numbering chapter 10 and avoids the small risk
of breaking inbound deep-links or printed-PDF page references.

This justification is weak: the ticket explicitly states the content is
copied from MPD and "doesn't seem to make sense in the context of the HDR",
and there is no evidence in the ticket, Zulip, or GitHub that any
implementer has relied on those sections in HDR. The argument is recorded
here for completeness but is not a strong position.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is a clean editorial cleanup raised by a contributor who has
identified a documentation copy-paste artifact. The risk of removing two
off-topic narrative sections from a pre-publication IG is essentially
nil — no FHIR artifacts change, no conformance is altered — and the upside
is a tighter, more on-scope HDR IG ahead of the `block-vote-1` ballot.
Disposition B (stubs with redirects) is a reasonable fallback if the work
group prefers to preserve anchor stability, but absent evidence of external
deep-links into HDR §10.2 / §10.3, the simpler full-removal in Disposition
A is the better default. Disposition C does not have a substantive defense
and should only prevail if a reviewer surfaces a concrete HDR use case for
the inherited material.
