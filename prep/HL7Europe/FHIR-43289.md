## Summary

The reporter (Ulrike Merrick) noticed that the human-readable narrative
generated for an artifact in the HL7 Europe *EU Laboratory Report (FHIR)*
specification contains the phrase "See above" pointing the reader to a
referenced `Observation`, but the referenced `Observation` actually appears
**below** the reference in the rendered output, not above it. The ticket
asks the work group to determine whether this misdirection is an artifact of
the IG publisher's narrative generation (a templating / ordering quirk in the
tooling) or whether the IG itself can be corrected so that the narrative text
either points the reader in the correct direction or uses neutral wording
(e.g., "See below" when appropriate). No related Jira tickets, Zulip
discussions, or GitHub items were found via cross-reference for this
ticket — it appears to stand alone as a usability / narrative-quality
question against the EU Laboratory Report IG.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the affected example(s) in the EU Laboratory Report IG so that the
embedded narrative phrase "See above" is replaced with "See below" (or the
correct directional cue) in every place where the referenced `Observation`
is rendered after the reference. Concretely, this is a content edit to the
narrative `text.div` of the example resource(s) shown in the screenshot
(typically a `DiagnosticReport` or `Observation` panel example whose
`text.div` was hand-authored or partially hand-authored). No structural,
profile, or terminology changes are required.

#### Justification

The reporter's request is a small, low-risk content correction that
directly improves reader comprehension of the published IG. Narrative
text in FHIR resources is normative for human readers (per the FHIR
narrative rules), so accuracy of directional language inside the
`text.div` matters. If the narrative was authored by hand in the example
files, a direct edit is the cleanest fix. Replacing "See above" with
"See below" is also consistent with how the rendered HTML is actually
laid out, which is what the reader experiences.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Treat the issue as an **IG-publisher / rendering-order** problem rather
than a one-off content fix. Two sub-options, applied together:

1. **Re-order the rendered output** so that referenced contained or
   bundled `Observation`s appear *above* the panel/report narrative that
   references them — making "See above" literally true and avoiding
   future drift in any other example.
2. **Rephrase narrative cross-references to be position-neutral** —
   e.g., replace "See above" with "See referenced Observation
   *<id>*" or a hyperlink anchor like `<a href="#obs-glucose">See
   Observation: Glucose</a>`. This makes the narrative robust to any
   rendering order chosen by the IG publisher.

Apply the rephrasing across all examples in the EU Laboratory Report IG
that use directional language ("see above" / "see below") in their
`text.div`, and add a short authoring guideline to the IG's contributor
docs warning against directional words in narrative.

#### Justification

The reporter explicitly raises the question "is that a publisher thing
or can we make sure we point people in the right direction" — i.e., the
underlying concern is broader than one example. A position-neutral
rewrite eliminates the class of bug rather than fixing one instance, and
it also removes a fragile coupling between hand-authored narrative and
the IG publisher's rendering decisions, which can change between
publisher versions. The trade-off vs. Disposition A is more authoring
effort up front (sweep all examples, add a guideline) in exchange for
durability.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Not Persuasive / Out of Scope for the IG**, on
the grounds that the directional wording inside example narratives is a
publisher-level rendering concern and the IG should not chase per-example
text fixes that depend on how the publisher orders contained / bundled
resources. Defer the issue to the IG-publisher project (i.e., file
upstream against the publisher to either order references consistently
or expose a templating hook), and leave the EU Laboratory Report IG
unchanged.

#### Justification

If the narrative in question is **generated** by the IG publisher rather
than hand-authored in the example, then editing the example files will
not fix the rendered output — the publisher will regenerate it on the
next build. In that case the only durable fix lives upstream, and
patching the IG would be cosmetic at best and misleading at worst. This
disposition is only defensible if investigation confirms the text is
generator-produced; otherwise Disposition A or B is preferable.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The reporter's literal request (Disposition A) would silence the immediate
complaint, but the framing of the ticket — "is that a publisher thing or
can we make sure we point people in the right direction" — indicates that
the work group should answer the structural question, not just patch one
example. Position-neutral phrasing (or anchored links) in narrative
`text.div` content is robust to publisher rendering changes, easy for
contributors to follow as a guideline, and avoids re-opening the same
class of ticket the next time the publisher reorders output. Disposition
A is acceptable as a short-term fix while the broader sweep in
Disposition B is scheduled. Disposition C should only be selected if
investigation shows the offending narrative is wholly generator-produced
and unfixable from the IG repo, which is unlikely for a hand-curated
example in a regional Laboratory Report IG.
