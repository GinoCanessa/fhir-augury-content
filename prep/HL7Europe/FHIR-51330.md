## Summary

The reporter (Alexander Zautke) asks where intra-procedural complications
should be documented in the HL7 Europe Imaging Study Report, specifically in
the `ImComposition` profile. In Germany, the DIN 25300-1 norm for imaging
reports has a single, mostly free-text general section for complications, and
the reporter found no clear place in `ImComposition` to map that entire
section. Examples span a very broad range — adverse drug reactions, the
patient falling from the table, claustrophobia leading to repeated MRI
attempts, post-biopsy bleeding, contrast extravasation due to IV
misplacement, and image artifacts caused by an aneurysm clip — which the
reporter uses to argue that an explicit "Complications" section in the
composition would be beneficial. In subsequent comments, an HL7 Europe
contributor (JIRAUSER28670) responded that complications are currently
modeled via the `ImAdverseEvent` profile, with `AdverseEvent.suspectEntity:procedure`
linking to the Procedure and `AdverseEvent.contributingFactor:allergy`
linking to `AllergyIntoleranceEu`, and pointed at the IG's Design
Considerations page for the rationale. The reporter (JIRAUSER29565)
provisionally accepted using the Adverse Event profile to mirror the German
free-text status quo. A later commenter (JIRAUSER29587, January 2026) pushed
back: relying on `suspectEntity:procedure` without a direct reference from
the `Composition` produces `BUNDLE_BUNDLE_ENTRY_REVERSE` QA errors, and
proposed adding an explicit "Complications" section to `ImComposition`,
citing LOINC 87416-4 (used in the DICOM panel) and LOINC 72230-6 (used in
C-CDA) and pointing at the Czech Republic Imaging report
(`cz-composition-imaging`) as a precedent that already includes such a
section. No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items
were surfaced by FhirAugury for this ticket.

## Details

**Description:**

> Resource profile: https://build.fhir.org/ig/hl7-eu/imaging/StructureDefinition-ImComposition.html
>
> Where can Complications that occur during the procedure be documented? In Germany, the DIN 25300-1 norm for the imaging report ist mainly free text and has one general section for complications.
>
> Examples for complications are:
> - an adverse reaction to a medication administered as part of the procedure
> - the patient falls from the examination table
> - the patient is claustrophobic and needed multiple attempts to undergo an MRI
> - post-biopsy bleeding
> - contrast leaking into soft tissue due to IV misplacement
> - artifact caused by an aneurysm clip
>
> These examples are chosen to demonstrate the very wide range of complication-categories. We find it hard to map the complete general german complications-section to a specific element or section in the HL7 Europe Imaging Study Report. Maybe an additional section for documenting Complications would be beneficial also for the HL7 Europe Imaging Study Report.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a new "Complications" `Composition.section` slice to the
`ImComposition` profile in the HL7 Europe Imaging Study Report IG, bound
to a code identifying intra-procedural complications. Use LOINC **87416-4**
("Complications Document") as the section `code` (already used in the
DICOM panel) and constrain `section.entry` to `Reference(ImAdverseEvent)`,
allowing zero-or-many entries. Mark the section as optional
(`min = 0`, `max = *`) and supply a short narrative-only fallback so
implementers without coded adverse-event data can still emit a free-text
complications block — this preserves the German DIN 25300-1 free-text
status quo. Update the IG's Design Considerations page to point at the
new section as the canonical home for intra-procedural complications, and
add an example bundle showing one entry per example category from the
ticket (medication reaction, fall, claustrophobia, post-biopsy bleeding,
contrast extravasation, aneurysm-clip artifact).

#### Justification

This directly answers the reporter's question — "where can complications
be documented" — with a single, discoverable location, and addresses the
QA concern raised by JIRAUSER29587: a dedicated `Composition.section`
that references `ImAdverseEvent` resources gives the bundle a forward
reference from the Composition, eliminating
`BUNDLE_BUNDLE_ENTRY_REVERSE` validation errors that arise from relying
solely on `AdverseEvent.suspectEntity:procedure`. There is precedent in
adjacent IGs: the Czech Republic Imaging report
(`cz-composition-imaging`) already includes a Complications section,
LOINC 87416-4 is the DICOM panel's code for the same concept, and LOINC
72230-6 plays the analogous role in C-CDA — so adding the section is
consistent with both DICOM and CDA structures and would ease
cross-jurisdictional implementation. The breadth of the reporter's
examples (clinical, behavioural, and image-quality) is also better served
by a section than by trying to cram everything onto a single existing
element.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep `ImAdverseEvent` as the single mechanism for representing
complications, but make it discoverable from `ImComposition` without
introducing a new section: add `ImAdverseEvent` as a permitted
`Composition.section.entry` type in **an existing section** of
`ImComposition` (e.g., the procedure / findings section that already
references `Procedure`), and document this binding explicitly. In
parallel, expand the IG's Design Considerations page with (a) a
worked example showing a complication modelled as `ImAdverseEvent` with
both `suspectEntity:procedure` and a contributing factor for the contrast
allergy case, (b) explicit guidance that any complication referenced from
the bundle MUST also be referenced from the Composition (to avoid
`BUNDLE_BUNDLE_ENTRY_REVERSE`), and (c) a note that free-text-only
complications (e.g., the German DIN 25300-1 general block) can be carried
in the section's `text` narrative when no coded adverse event is
available. Optionally, add a `Composition.section.code` extension or
binding hint that flags entries representing complications, without
introducing a separate top-level section.

#### Justification

This avoids fragmenting `ImComposition` with another optional section
while still solving the two real problems the thread surfaces:
discoverability ("where do I put it?") and the QA error
(`BUNDLE_BUNDLE_ENTRY_REVERSE`). Reusing an existing section keeps the
composition shape stable for early implementers (the IG is at
0.1.0-ballot) and avoids divergence from base HL7 imaging report
patterns. It also keeps the modelling pluralistic: complications that are
genuinely adverse events (medication reaction, post-biopsy bleeding,
contrast extravasation) stay in `AdverseEvent`, while operational
complications that are awkward to encode (claustrophobia, patient fall,
aneurysm-clip artifact) can live in the section narrative or be modelled
as `Observation` / `Procedure.note` without forcing every implementer to
populate a coded `AdverseEvent`. The downside, relative to A, is weaker
alignment with DICOM/C-CDA and with the Czech IG, and a continued risk
that implementers will not realise they must reference the
`AdverseEvent` from the Composition.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request to add a "Complications" section to `ImComposition`.
Confirm that complications are already addressable through the existing
`ImAdverseEvent` profile (with `suspectEntity:procedure` and
`contributingFactor:allergy`), as documented in the IG's Design
Considerations page, and close the ticket with a pointer to that
guidance. Suggest that the German free-text "Complications" block be
carried as narrative on the Procedure / Composition rather than as a new
structured section.

#### Justification

`ImAdverseEvent` is already in the IG specifically to represent adverse
events arising from imaging investigations, and the Design Considerations
page documents the intent. Adding a parallel "Complications" section
risks a confusing two-headed model in which the same clinical fact
(e.g., a contrast reaction) could legitimately appear either as an
`AdverseEvent` referenced from a Complications section or only inside
the section's narrative — implementers would have to choose, and
validators would have a harder time enforcing consistency. From a scope
perspective, IG-level structural changes during a 0.1.0-ballot cycle have
ripple effects on examples, validators, and downstream profiles
(including `cz-composition-imaging`), so deferring this request and
reassessing after ballot feedback is defensible.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The strongest signal from the discussion is from the most recent
comment (JIRAUSER29587, 2026-01-05): the current modelling — relying on
`AdverseEvent.suspectEntity:procedure` with no direct reference from
`ImComposition` — generates real validator errors
(`BUNDLE_BUNDLE_ENTRY_REVERSE`) and is therefore not a workable answer
for implementers, regardless of whether `ImAdverseEvent` is sufficient at
the *content* level. Disposition A solves that error structurally rather
than by guidance, lines up with concrete precedent in the Czech Republic
Imaging IG and with the codes already used in DICOM (LOINC 87416-4) and
C-CDA (LOINC 72230-6), and gives German implementers an obvious target
for the DIN 25300-1 free-text complications block (via the section's
narrative) without forcing them to model every operational complication
as a coded `AdverseEvent`. Disposition B is a viable fallback if the
work group wants to minimise structural change in the 0.1.0-ballot
window, but it leaves the QA-error trap in place unless the IG publishes
and tooling enforces the "must reference from Composition" guidance —
which is harder to police than a section slice. Disposition C is hard to
defend given that the most recent commenter has effectively documented a
defect in the current model and provided a concrete fix.
