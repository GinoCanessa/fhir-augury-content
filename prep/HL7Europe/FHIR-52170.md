## Summary

The ticket reports a spelling error in the EU Hospital Discharge Report
(FHIR) IG. The narrative text contains the misspelled word "developped"
in the sentence:

> Providing alignment with existing eHN guidelines, the EHDS logical
> models currently **developped** by the Xt-EHR

The reporter (Alexander Henket) requests that this be corrected to
"developed" (single "p"). The ticket is filed as a Technical Correction
with Medium priority and has no cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip
discussions, or GitHub items associated with it.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the technical correction exactly as requested in the EU Hospital
Discharge Report (FHIR) IG. Locate the narrative content containing the
string "developped" (in the sentence describing alignment with eHN
guidelines and the EHDS logical models developed by Xt-EHR) and replace
the word with "developed". This is a single-character deletion in
narrative copy and does not affect any FHIR conformance artifact
(StructureDefinition, ValueSet, CodeSystem, CapabilityStatement, etc.).

#### Justification

The ticket is filed as a Technical Correction, which is the appropriate
mechanism for non-substantive editorial fixes such as spelling errors.
"Developped" is unambiguously incorrect English; the intended spelling
is "developed". The fix has no semantic impact, no normative impact, no
backward-compatibility implications, and carries effectively zero risk.
Accepting as requested is the standard handling for typo tickets and
keeps the IG narrative professional and trustworthy.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the fix, but rather than treating it as an isolated change, run a
narrative-text spell-check / proofreading pass over the affected page
(and ideally the rest of the EU Hospital Discharge Report IG narrative)
as part of the same edit. Capture any other typos or grammar issues
found and resolve them under this ticket (or a sibling Technical
Correction ticket), so that a single editorial commit cleans up the
page rather than producing repeated single-typo tickets.

#### Justification

Single-typo tickets are individually trivial but collectively expensive
to triage, ballot, and merge. When an external reviewer notices one
spelling error in narrative copy, there are often others nearby. Doing
a one-page proofread under cover of an already-open Technical
Correction ticket reduces churn for the workgroup and produces a more
consistent reader experience. The downside is a slightly larger diff,
which in turn requires a slightly more careful editorial review — but
the change remains non-normative and low-risk.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Reject the ticket on the grounds that the misspelling is in narrative
prose rather than in a normative artifact, and that the meaning is
unambiguous to readers. Close as "Not Persuasive" / "Won't Fix" and
leave the text as-is until the next planned editorial sweep of the IG.

#### Justification

There is essentially no technical defence for declining a one-character
spelling fix in published narrative. The only argument for declining
would be process-driven (avoiding the overhead of a separate ballot
cycle for a trivial change), and even that is weak because Technical
Correction is precisely the mechanism designed to absorb such fixes
without disruption. This option is included for completeness rather
than as a serious recommendation.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

This is a textbook Technical Correction: a single misspelled word in
narrative prose, with no normative or conformance implications and zero
risk. Accept and apply as requested. If the editor making the change
happens to notice other typos in the same paragraph or page while doing
the fix, they should sweep those up as well in the same commit
(effectively folding in Disposition B opportunistically), but a
deliberate workgroup-scope proofreading effort is not warranted by this
ticket alone. Disposition C should not be entertained — declining a
clear typo correction in published narrative would be hard to defend
to the reporter or to readers of the IG.
