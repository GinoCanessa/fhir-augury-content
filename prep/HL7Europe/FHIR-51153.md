## Summary

The reporter is asking the EU Imaging Study Report (R5) work group to clarify
the intent of a profile (apparently the EU ImagingStudy / WADO "manifest"
profile, given the `manifest` label and the EU Imaging Study Report R5
specification) that currently defines **17 required slices for mime types**.
The reporter questions whether a WADO endpoint is genuinely expected to
support all 17 mime types, and asks the work group either to clarify the
intent or to express the rule differently (e.g., as a minimal value set
rather than 17 mandatory slices). The ticket has been triaged with a
resolution of **Persuasive with Modification**: the work group has agreed the
list as-currently-expressed is incorrect and that mime-type requirements
should be re-aligned with DICOM PS3.18 §7 ("Persistent Object Types"), and
that the optional/required mime-type lists should be expressed as value
sets rather than as 17 required slices.

No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip discussions, or GitHub items were
returned by the data sources, so the request stands on its own without
linked community context.

## Proposed Dispositions

> Note: Because no GitHub cross-references were found, the dispositions
> below cannot name a specific authoring root or file path. They name
> the artifact category (the EU ImagingStudy "manifest" profile in the
> EU Imaging Study Report R5 IG) and the kind of change required. The
> implementer assigned this ticket should locate the StructureDefinition
> for the manifest profile (the one carrying the 17 mime-type slices) in
> the EU Imaging Study Report R5 IG repository and apply the changes

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the resolution already recorded on the ticket, in two parts, on the
manifest profile in the EU Imaging Study Report (R5) IG:

1. **Fix the mime-type slice cardinalities** so that they reflect what
   DICOM PS3.18 §7 ("Persistent Object Types") actually mandates, rather
   than promoting all 17 listed mime types to required:
   - WADO-URI: require `application/dicom` and `image/jpeg` (min = 1
     each); make the remaining mime types optional (min = 0).
   - WADO-RS: require `application/dicom`, `application/octet-stream`,
     and `application/dicom+xml` (min = 1 each); make the remaining
     mime types optional (min = 0).
2. **Re-express the rule using ValueSets** instead of 17 in-line required
   slices. Define (at minimum) two ValueSets in the EU Imaging Study
   Report R5 IG:
   - `WADO-URI required mime types` (the two values above) and a
     companion "supported / optional" ValueSet for the remainder.
   - `WADO-RS required mime types` (the three values above) and a
     companion "supported / optional" ValueSet for the remainder.
   Bind the manifest profile's mime-type element to the appropriate
   ValueSet (required strength for the "required" set; preferred or
   extensible for the optional set, depending on conformance intent).
   Replace the 17 required slices with a much smaller slicing
   discriminator that enforces the required entries via the ValueSet
   binding rather than via 17 individual `min = 1` slices.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the work group agreed to in the resolution
("Persuasive with Modification") and it is anchored in a normative DICOM
source (PS3.18 §7), so traceability is clean. Using ValueSets rather than
17 hand-coded required slices is consistent with FHIR design
conventions: terminology belongs in ValueSets, not in profile
slicing. It also reduces the maintenance burden (any future change in
WADO mime-type expectations is a ValueSet edit, not a profile edit) and
makes the conformance statement legible to implementers, who otherwise
have to scroll a long slice list to understand what is actually
mandatory.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Same intent as Disposition A, but instead of *two* ValueSets per WADO
flavor (one "required", one "optional"), publish **one ValueSet per WADO
flavor that enumerates all supported mime types**, and express the
required subset using **invariants** (`constraint`) on the profile that
assert the presence of the mandatory codes (e.g., a FHIRPath invariant
checking that the manifest's mime-type codes contain `application/dicom`
and `image/jpeg` for WADO-URI, etc.). Drop slicing on this element
entirely.

#### Justification

This keeps the profile differential small and avoids fragmenting the
mime-type vocabulary into "required" and "optional" ValueSets that
implementers must keep in sync. Required behaviour stays explicit and
testable (an invariant fires; a validator reports the failure with a
human-readable message), while the supported vocabulary is captured
once. The trade-off vs. Disposition A is that invariants are slightly
less "discoverable" in IG-rendered profile pages than slice-level
cardinality is, so authors reviewing the IG visually may miss the
constraint at first glance. This option is preferable if the work group
expects the supported mime-type list to evolve more often than the
required subset.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change and keep 17 required mime-type slices on the manifest
profile, on the grounds that the existing slicing already documents the
full WADO mime-type surface and that implementers can simply omit the
mime types they do not support at runtime.

#### Justification

This option is included for completeness only. It is hard to defend
because the work group has already accepted on the ticket that the
cardinalities are *wrong* relative to DICOM PS3.18 §7. Keeping 17
required slices would knowingly publish a profile that demands more than
the underlying DICOM standard requires, which would either (a) force EU
implementers to claim conformance to a profile they cannot meet, or
(b) make the profile effectively unimplementable for partial WADO
deployments. It would also leave the original reporter's question
unanswered.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The resolution already recorded on the ticket ("Persuasive with
Modification") effectively commits the work group to Disposition A: fix
the cardinalities so they match DICOM PS3.18 §7, and express the
optional/required mime-type lists as ValueSets rather than 17 required
slices. Disposition A is the most direct execution of that decision,
keeps terminology in terminology artifacts (where validators, IG
publishers, and downstream profilers expect to find it), and is the
least surprising option for implementers reading the rendered profile
page. Disposition B is a reasonable alternative if the work group wants
to minimise differential noise, but it relies on invariants for
mandatory-content enforcement, which is harder to discover from the IG
UI than a ValueSet binding plus a small "required" slice. Disposition C
is not defensible given the resolution already on the ticket.
