## Summary

The reporter (Giorgio Cangioli) asks the EU Imaging Study Report (R5)
specification authors to choose `id` and `name` values for their
StructureDefinitions that make it visually obvious whether a given
profile is an **obligation profile** or a regular **structural profile**.
At present the two kinds of profile are intermixed in the IG's artifact
listings and reviewers cannot quickly tell which is which without opening
each profile. The ticket has been triaged with resolution "Considered -
No action required" and a note from the work group that the request will
be revisited "when obligations are re-added", implying the obligations
layer is currently absent from this build of the IG.

No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were
returned by FhirAugury for this ticket key, so the request stands alone
in the available data.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt an explicit naming convention for the EU Imaging Study Report
(R5) IG that distinguishes obligation profiles from structural profiles
in both `StructureDefinition.id` and `StructureDefinition.name` (and,
for symmetry, the `title`). Concretely:

- Suffix every obligation profile's `id` with `-obligations` (e.g.,
  `ImagingStudyReport-obligations`) and prefix its `name`/`title` with
  `Obligations:` (e.g., `Obligations: ImagingStudyReport`).
- Leave structural profiles' ids and names unchanged so the legacy
  canonical URLs of the structural layer are not disturbed.
- Document the convention in the IG's "Profiles" or "Conformance" page
  so reviewers and downstream implementers can rely on it.
- Apply the convention as part of the work the resolution already
  promises ("when obligations are re-added").

#### Justification

The reporter's underlying complaint is purely about *discoverability*:
when the artifact list mixes the two kinds of profile, a reviewer must
open each one to learn its purpose. A simple, mechanical naming rule
solves that without changing semantics. Using a suffix on `id` keeps
the canonical URLs of the obligation profiles cleanly namespaced
(`…/StructureDefinition/<base>-obligations`), which mirrors common HL7
practice for variant profiles (e.g., `-eu`, `-uv`, `-ips` suffixes seen
across other IGs). Restricting the change to the obligations layer
honours the work group's stated intent to act "when obligations are
re-added", so it does not destabilise the structural layer that is in
the current ballot.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Instead of (or in addition to) renaming, expose the
obligation-vs-structural distinction through **metadata and
presentation** rather than identifiers:

- Tag every obligation profile with a dedicated
  `StructureDefinition.useContext` entry (or a small custom extension
  such as `http://hl7.eu/fhir/StructureDefinition/profile-kind` with
  values `obligation | structural`).
- Add an IG Publisher grouping in `ig.ini` / `sushi-config.yaml` that
  splits the profiles page into two clearly labelled sections
  ("Obligation Profiles" / "Structural Profiles") and renders an
  appropriate badge next to each entry.
- Optionally, prefix only the human-facing `title` (not `id`/`name`)
  with "Obligations:" so canonical URLs and references in
  conformance-bound artifacts are completely unchanged.

#### Justification

This addresses the reviewer's real problem — "I cannot tell at a
glance" — without baking a presentational concern into machine-stable
identifiers. `id` and `name` are referenced from external profiles,
guidance documents, and tooling configuration; renaming them is a
breaking change for any consumer that has already linked to the
obligations layer (or will, once it is re-introduced). A grouping +
metadata approach gives the IG indefinite flexibility to change how the
distinction is rendered without touching identifiers again, and it
scales to other axes (e.g., experimental vs. normative) that the IG may
want to surface later.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as **Considered - No action required** (the existing resolution),
on the basis that the obligations layer is not currently present in
this build of the IG and any naming guidance should be deferred until
obligations are re-introduced, at which point it will be handled as
part of that work rather than as a standalone item.

#### Justification

The work group has already adopted this position: the ticket is
resolved "Considered - No action required" with the note "We will take
this into account when obligations are re-added." Acting now would mean
designing a naming convention against an absent artifact set, with no
way to validate that the chosen convention actually improves
discoverability in the rendered IG. Deferring the decision to the
moment obligations re-enter the IG keeps the design grounded in real
content and avoids a second round of renaming later.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A (with elements of B retained as a
follow-up).

The reporter's concern is concrete, low-risk, and easy to discharge:
introduce a deterministic naming rule for obligation profiles when they
are re-added, and document it. That directly satisfies the request
("help recognition") and matches what the work group has already
committed to in the resolution text. Disposition C is defensible
purely on timing — the obligations layer is not in the current build —
but it leaves the underlying request unanswered and risks the same
ticket being raised again at re-introduction. Disposition B's
metadata/grouping ideas are valuable as *additional* polish (and should
be tracked as a follow-up enhancement to the IG Publisher rendering),
but they do not replace a clear naming convention, which is what
reviewers will see first in the artifact index. Choosing A also gives
the work group a small, self-contained design item to schedule
alongside the obligations re-introduction work, rather than discovering
the gap at that moment.
