## Summary

The reporter asks the HL7 Europe *EU Imaging Study Report (R5)* IG to give
implementers concrete guidance on how access to imaging endpoints
(IID — Image Information Display — and DICOMweb WADO-RS) is controlled when
imaging studies are exchanged across borders and behind National Contact
Points (NCPs). The current IG leaves several questions unanswered:

- Are all queries proxied through the NCP (with the NCP rewriting/resolving
  endpoints to the appropriate record holder), or is direct access from the
  consumer to the source's WADO-RS / IID viewer permitted?
- Do NCPs themselves host IID viewers?
- Where can viewers legitimately be hosted (source, intermediary, local)
  and is there an expectation that an IID viewer URL conveyed cross-border
  is localized to the destination?
- Should the IG leverage existing work — IHE **XC-WADO**, the
  **SMART Imaging Access** profile (jmandel/smart-imaging), and/or
  **SMART Multi Auth** (bvdh/smart-multi-auth) — for the
  authorization/discovery patterns?

The reporter's concrete proposal is to add **end-to-end sequence diagrams**
for the key envisioned workflows (extending the functional process steps on
page 9 of the EC *Guidelines on medical imaging studies and reports* v1.1,
Nov 2024) covering pre-coordination, authentication/authorization, and
endpoint discovery flows. These would be added alongside the
[Transactions](https://build.fhir.org/ig/hl7-eu/imaging/branches/qa-fixes-ballot1/transactions.html)
page currently under development. The intent is to lock in a consistent
shared understanding for ballot review and reduce implementer cost/time.

## Details

**Description:**

It is unclear from the implementation guide how access to the endpoints
(IID, WADO-RS) is controlled. This should be specified so clients and hosts
can achieve consistent access methods to images.

- Do the authors envision that all queries would be proxied through the
  NCP, with the NCP providing endpoints that would direct reference
  resolution to the appropriate record holders? If so, how is this managed?
- Alternatively, is direct access allowed?
- Would the NCPs themselves support IID viewing?
- Who could be hosting the viewer (source, intermediary, local, all of the
  above), and how widely are they deploying access? If an IID viewer is
  communicated cross-border, is there an expectation to localize the viewer
  to the destination?
- Are concepts from
  [XC-WADO](https://drive.google.com/drive/u/0/folders/1VIy0PMgQY4b9TeVzN1CVKo2hRlIo3ilo),
  [SMART Imaging Access](https://github.com/jmandel/smart-imaging) /
  [SMART Multi Auth](https://github.com/bvdh/smart-multi-auth/blob/main/input/pagecontent/solution-dual-smart-launch.md)
  leveraged?

One idea to help implementers would be to create end-to-end sequence
diagrams for the key envisioned workflows. Ideally, this would include
information on the pre-coordination steps anticipated and expected
authentication/authorization and endpoint discovery flows behind NCPs, even
if some details are left to national infrastructure. (Two example mock-up
images were attached: jira attachments 26129 and 26128.)

Expanding on the functional process steps described on Page 9 of the
[Guidelines on medical imaging studies and reports](https://health.ec.europa.eu/publications/ehn-guidelines-medical-imaging-studies-and-reports_en),
Release 1.1, Nov 2024, with diagrams for the key workflows would help the
implementer community during implementation. This would reduce both cost
and time to implement.

Furthermore, creating such documentation now ensures a consistent
understanding of the envisioned flows during design and further balloting
of this specification. This would be very helpful to add with the
[Transactions page](https://build.fhir.org/ig/hl7-eu/imaging/branches/qa-fixes-ballot1/transactions.html)
under development.

**Comments:**

- **JIRAUSER30266 (2025-12-16):** "This is addressed by the IHE XC_WADO
  profile which can be used grouped with the IHE MADO Profile to gain
  access to the retrieve URL via the imaging study manifest (MADO) and be
  formatted (re-written) for Cross-Border retrieval according to the
  XC_WADO Profile."

- **JIRAUSER28578 (2025-12-17):** XC-WADO + MADO handle the manifest
  representation and the conveyance of image links, and gesture at
  transport via MHD/XCA/XDS. What is **still missing** is the
  *authorization* to access the WADO-RS endpoint: when a consumer takes a
  manifest URL and launches it, how is that endpoint authorized and what
  can the consumer expect? Is it assumed to flow through the same auth and
  pipes as the manifest (consistent with the trust model, but pushes heavy
  DICOM payloads through many NCP hops), or does authorization need to be
  separately negotiated?

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a new **"Endpoint Access & Authorization"** narrative section to the
EU Imaging Study Report (R5) IG, co-located with the in-development
[Transactions](https://build.fhir.org/ig/hl7-eu/imaging/branches/qa-fixes-ballot1/transactions.html)
page, that:

1. States the IG's **architectural stance** on imaging endpoint access:
   whether WADO-RS / IID URLs in an `ImagingStudy.endpoint` (and the
   `Endpoint` resources they reference) are intended to be (a) proxied
   through the requesting NCP, (b) rewritten by the NCP and dereferenced
   directly by the consumer, or (c) both, with a profile-level flag or
   `Endpoint.connectionType` convention to distinguish them.
2. Specifies **who may host an IID viewer** (source, intermediary/NCP,
   local) and the localization expectation when a viewer URL crosses a
   border (e.g., MUST/SHOULD support a `lang` query parameter or
   negotiate via `Accept-Language`).
3. Adds **end-to-end sequence diagrams** (PlantUML/Mermaid) for the key
   workflows aligned to the EC *Guidelines on medical imaging studies and
   reports* v1.1 (Nov 2024) §Page 9 process steps, covering at minimum:
   (i) cross-border manifest retrieval, (ii) WADO-RS instance retrieval
   following a manifest, (iii) IID launch. Each diagram MUST show
   pre-coordination steps, the authentication/authorization hop(s), and
   endpoint discovery.
4. Defines the **authorization model** for WADO-RS endpoints reached from
   a manifest (the gap explicitly called out by JIRAUSER28578): either
   "same auth and pipes as the manifest" (NCP proxying, with the trust
   model carrying through) or "separately negotiated" (e.g., a SMART on
   FHIR / SMART Multi Auth dual launch), and reference the IHE **XC-WADO**
   + **MADO** profiles for the URL conveyance/rewriting mechanics
   (per JIRAUSER30266's comment).

#### Justification

The ticket is fundamentally a **specification gap**, not a request to
change a resource or constraint. The two comments converge on the same
diagnosis: existing IHE profiles (XC-WADO, MADO) cover the
manifest-and-URL-rewriting mechanics, but the IG still owes implementers
an explicit statement of the authorization model and viewer-hosting
expectations. Sequence diagrams aligned to the published EC guidelines
are a low-risk, high-value addition that costs no normative change but
materially improves implementability and ballot reviewability — exactly
what the reporter asks for. Anchoring the auth model to existing work
(IHE XC-WADO/MADO; optionally SMART Imaging Access / SMART Multi Auth)
avoids inventing a new mechanism.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than fully *specifying* the access/authorization model in this
ballot cycle, add a **non-normative "Deployment Considerations" appendix**
that:

1. Enumerates the open questions verbatim from the ticket (proxy-vs-direct,
   NCP-as-viewer, viewer hosting/localization, auth on WADO-RS hop) and
   labels them as items to be settled in the next ballot cycle and/or by
   national NCP infrastructure, with explicit hand-offs to the eHDSI /
   MyHealth@EU governance.
2. Provides **one** illustrative sequence diagram (the manifest →
   WADO-RS retrieval flow) drawn against an NCP-proxy assumption, marked
   "informative example."
3. References IHE **XC-WADO** + **MADO** as the recommended starting point
   for URL conveyance and rewriting (capturing the December 2025 comment
   thread), but does **not** mandate them.
4. Adds a `must-support` note on `ImagingStudy.endpoint` and a tightened
   binding on `Endpoint.connectionType` (e.g., requiring
   `dicom-wado-rs` and/or a coded value identifying an IID viewer) so
   that consumers can *recognize* the flavor of endpoint they receive,
   even before the full access/auth model is finalized.

#### Justification

The reporter's underlying need is "shared understanding for design and
ballot." That can be met without the IG committing to a specific
auth/proxy architecture in R5 — a commitment that will be hard to undo
and that depends on national infrastructure decisions outside HL7
Europe's control. An informative appendix and one diagram unblock ballot
review at lower long-term risk, while the `Endpoint.connectionType`
tightening gives implementers something machine-readable today.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket as **Persuasive – Considered for Future Use** (or
equivalent) on the grounds that:

- The mechanics of cross-border WADO/IID URL conveyance are already
  covered by **IHE XC-WADO grouped with MADO** (per the December 2025
  comment from JIRAUSER30266), which the IG can simply reference rather
  than re-document.
- Authentication/authorization between NCPs is governed by the
  **eHDSI / MyHealth@EU** trust framework, which is national-infrastructure
  scope and out of scope for an HL7 FHIR IG.
- Adding speculative sequence diagrams before the auth model is settled
  risks publishing diagrams that contradict the eventual deployment
  reality across Member States.

#### Justification

A FHIR IG is the wrong layer to specify NCP-level transport, trust, and
proxying behavior; those belong to the eHDSI policy and IHE profiles.
The IG can satisfy reviewers by citing XC-WADO/MADO and the eHDSI
framework rather than by re-stating their content. This avoids the IG
overreaching its mandate.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is well-grounded, a workgroup member has already engaged
constructively in the comments, and the reporter has done much of the
work — there are even mock-up sequence diagrams attached. The
December 2025 comment thread also makes the gap precise: XC-WADO + MADO
cover the URL/manifest mechanics, but the *authorization on the WADO-RS
hop* is the missing piece. Closing that gap explicitly in the IG (even
by saying "follow XC-WADO, with auth carried through the same NCP hops
as the manifest") is exactly what an implementation guide is for.

Disposition C under-serves implementers: pointing at IHE and eHDSI
without an integrated narrative is what produced the ticket in the
first place. Disposition B is a reasonable fallback if the workgroup
judges that the authorization model is genuinely unsettled and cannot
be locked down in this ballot cycle — in that case, ship the appendix
+ one diagram + the `Endpoint.connectionType` tightening now, and
schedule the full normative section for the next cycle. But the
*default* should be Disposition A: add the section, the diagrams, and
the explicit auth statement to the Transactions page, and use the
ballot to refine it.
