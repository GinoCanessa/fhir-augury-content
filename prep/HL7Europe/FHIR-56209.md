## Summary

Acting on feedback from the French Ministry of Health, the reporter
asks the EU Laboratory Report (FHIR) IG to extend the value set(s) used
for laboratory result/specimen qualifiers with a list of ~29 additional
SNOMED CT *qualifier value* concepts. The proposed concepts describe
spatial/positional/anatomical qualifiers (e.g., capsular, peripheral,
horizontal, axial, central, sagittal, coronal, transverse, marginal,
intra-articular, internal/external, median) that French laboratories
need in order to express French national reporting practice when
producing EU-conformant lab reports. The ticket is currently *Triaged*
with resolution *Considered for Future Use*, indicating the EU lab
group has acknowledged the request but parked it for a later release.
There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub
artifacts linked to this issue, so the request stands on its own.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add all 29 SNOMED CT qualifier-value concepts listed by France MoH to
the EU Laboratory Report (FHIR) value set that currently constrains
the qualifier slot the reporter has in mind (most plausibly the value
set bound to the body-site / specimen-site qualifier element used for
laboratory observations and specimens — the EU Lab Semantic subgroup
should confirm the exact `ValueSet` resource canonical URL during
disposition). Implement the change by editing the corresponding
`ValueSet` (extensional `compose.include.concept` entries with code +
display) in the EU Laboratory Report IG source, regenerating the
expansion, and noting the addition in the IG's change log for the
next published version. No structural or binding-strength change is
implied.

#### Justification

The codes are all valid SNOMED CT *qualifier value* concepts and
encode legitimate anatomical/positional qualifiers used routinely in
French laboratory reporting. Extending an extensional value set with
additional SCT concepts is a low-risk, additive change that does not
affect existing instances, does not break conformance for current
implementers, and directly supports a national member state requirement
— the core reason the EU Laboratory Report IG exists. The current
resolution status (*Considered for Future Use*) already signals that
the workgroup considers the request reasonable but wants to land it
in a future release; Disposition A simply formalises that.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than enumerating 29 individual SCT codes inside the EU lab
value set, replace (or supplement) the extensional binding with an
*intensional* SNOMED CT expression that selects descendants of the
qualifier hierarchies the French list draws from — e.g.,
`< 49755003 |Morphologically abnormal structure|` is **not** what we
want, but something along the lines of "descendants-or-self of
[topographical / positional qualifier value] sub-hierarchies plus
laterality qualifiers" expressed as an ECL-based
`ValueSet.compose.include.filter`. Pair this with a `ConceptMap` (or
companion preferred-codes list) that France MoH publishes for the
specific concepts it considers in scope, so other member states can
align without the EU IG having to re-cut the value set every time a
country submits an additional code.

#### Justification

The 29-code list is unlikely to be exhaustive — additional positional/
anatomical qualifiers will surface from other member states, and
maintaining an extensional list code-by-code is brittle and creates
a steady stream of low-value tickets. An intensional ECL-based
inclusion is the SNOMED-recommended pattern for "all qualifiers of
type X" use cases, gives stable semantics across SNOMED releases, and
shifts country-specific preferences into national `ConceptMap`s /
preferred-code lists rather than the shared EU value set. The
trade-off versus Disposition A is a one-time analytical effort by the
EU Lab Semantic group to choose the right ECL filter and to confirm
the expansion is a strict superset of the France MoH list (and not
unacceptably broader).

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline to add these concepts to the EU Laboratory Report (FHIR)
value set on the grounds that (a) the EU IG should bind to a stable
international qualifier value set rather than absorb country-specific
preferences, and (b) France MoH can carry the additional codes in a
French national lab profile/IG that derives from the EU IG and either
extends the binding or supplies a national value set, without
requiring a change to the EU artifact.

#### Justification

Adopting national preferred-code lists into a shared EU IG creates
pressure from every member state to do the same and erodes the IG's
role as a harmonisation layer. The FHIR value-set machinery
(profiling, slicing, binding refinement, national `ValueSet`s) is
explicitly designed to let a national IG tighten or extend a parent
binding without modifying the shared artifact. If the EU value set
already permits SNOMED CT codes via a sufficiently broad binding
(e.g., `example` / `preferred`, or an ECL filter that already covers
the qualifier hierarchy), no change is technically required at the
EU level.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The reporter's underlying need — French labs being able to express
positional/anatomical qualifiers in EU-conformant lab reports — is
real and well-founded, so outright declining (C) is not the right
answer; the EU IG exists precisely to absorb member-state requirements.
However, accepting the literal request (A) commits the EU Lab
Semantic group to an extensional list that will need to be re-opened
every time another member state submits a similar list, and there is
no obvious reason to believe France MoH's 29 codes are the closure of
qualifier values needed across Europe. Switching to an intensional
SNOMED ECL-based binding (B), with national preferred-code lists
expressed via `ConceptMap`s, scales better, is consistent with
SNOMED CT's own guidance for qualifier sub-hierarchies, and still
delivers what France MoH asked for in the next published version.
This also matches the spirit of the current resolution
(*Considered for Future Use*): the next-version slot is the right
moment to do the small amount of analytical work needed to pick the
ECL expression.
