## Summary

The reporter argues that the **EU Imaging Study Report (R5)** IG, part of the
XtEHR family, has a severely insufficient use-case description. They contend
that without a clear statement of (a) which use cases the IG is intended to
support, (b) which Actors participate, and (c) the obligations and
transactions exchanged between those actors, the IG cannot be meaningfully
implemented or commented on. They acknowledge that sibling XtEHR IGs do this
slightly better and point to the HDR IG's `obligations.html#overview` page as
an exemplar — but note that even that page does not yet provide a clear
definition of transactions. The ask is therefore that the EU Imaging Study
Report IG be augmented with an actors / obligations / transactions section
comparable to (or better than) the HDR IG's.

This ticket is closely related to [FHIR-51128 — *Revise actors and
transactions*](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51128) on the same
specification, which has already been resolved as **Persuasive with
Modification**: the WG agreed to add additional actors aligned with IHE-MHD
and a forward-looking note about actor-specific obligations, but explicitly
declined to define transactions on the grounds that this IG is about
*content*, not *workflow*. FHIR-51355 effectively asks for the broader
use-case framing that FHIR-51128 only partially delivers.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a new "Use cases, actors, obligations and transactions" narrative section
to the **EU Imaging Study Report (R5)** IG, modelled on (and improving over)
the HDR IG's `obligations.html#overview`. Concretely:

1. Add a top-level *Use Cases* page that enumerates the clinical scenarios
   the IG is intended to support (e.g., cross-border exchange of an imaging
   study report; inclusion of an imaging report in an IPS-style summary;
   patient-mediated access to a prior imaging report).
2. For each use case, list the participating **actors** (re-using the
   IHE-MHD-aligned actor set already agreed for FHIR-51128: content creator,
   content consumer, content provider, content recipient).
3. Describe the **obligations** each actor carries with respect to the
   profiles defined in the IG (must-support behaviour, narrative inclusion,
   provenance / authorship, patient identification, etc.), expressed using
   the FHIR Obligations framework as on the HDR overview page.
4. Describe the **transactions** between actors at a logical level —
   identifying, where possible, the concrete FHIR interaction (e.g.,
   `Bundle` POSTed to `$document`, `DocumentReference` create/read,
   `Composition` retrieval) — and indicate whether the IG normatively
   constrains those interactions or merely informs implementers about how
   the content is exchanged in practice.

#### Justification

This is what the reporter (and, by implication, ballot reviewers) need to be
able to give substantive comments on the IG. HL7 IGs are routinely declined
on ballot when they constrain content without explaining the interoperability
problem they solve, and FHIR-23547, FHIR-46798 and FHIR-52396 show that
"use-case framing is too thin" is a well-precedented basis for substantive
change. Reusing the actor list already adopted for FHIR-51128 keeps the IG
internally consistent.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — Persuasive with Modification

#### Proposal

Treat FHIR-51355 as the *narrative half* of FHIR-51128 and resolve it
**Persuasive with Modification** along the same axis the WG has already
agreed to:

1. Add a *Use Cases* / *Scope* narrative page that describes the in-scope
   clinical scenarios and links them to the actors being introduced under
   FHIR-51128.
2. Describe **actor-level obligations** using the FHIR Obligations framework
   (matching the HDR exemplar the reporter cites), so the IG explains *what
   each actor must do with the content* even though the IG itself is content,
   not workflow.
3. Add the same forward-looking note already adopted for FHIR-51128 stating
   that detailed actor-specific obligations and any transaction-level
   guidance will follow in a future release once XtEHR feedback consolidates.
4. Explicitly **do not** define transactions in this IG. Instead, state in
   the new narrative page that transactions are intentionally out of scope
   because this IG profiles content; cross-reference the XtEHR / IHE-MHD
   transactions that carry the content in deployment.

#### Justification

This is the minimum-friction outcome that (a) directly addresses the
reporter's concern that the IG cannot be meaningfully ballot-commented in its
current form, and (b) stays inside the boundary the WG has already drawn for
this specification on FHIR-51128: *content, not workflow*. It avoids
re-litigating the transactions question that FHIR-51128 already settled, and
it reuses an exemplar (HDR) the reporter themselves accepts as the right
shape — closing the gap they flagged ("HDR is closer but still missing
transactions") by being explicit about *why* transactions are out of scope
rather than silently omitting them.

---

### Disposition C: Decline — Not Persuasive

#### Proposal

Mark FHIR-51355 as **Not Persuasive** on the grounds that (a) FHIR-51128 has
already addressed the actors axis on this specification, (b) the IG is
intentionally a content IG and is not the right vehicle for transaction
definition, and (c) cross-IG narrative for the XtEHR family is being shaped
in sibling IGs (HDR, etc.) rather than duplicated in each member IG. Direct
the reporter to FHIR-51128 and to the XtEHR-level guidance.

#### Justification

The defensible reason for declining would be *duplicate of FHIR-51128 plus
out-of-scope on transactions*. The risk is that this leaves the reporter's
core complaint — that the IG cannot be ballot-commented today — unaddressed,
since FHIR-51128's resolution adds actors but does not add a Use Cases
narrative or describe what each actor is obligated to do at the content
level.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B (Persuasive with Modification).

This ticket and FHIR-51128 are clearly two halves of the same concern, and
the WG has already drawn a defensible boundary on FHIR-51128: actors and
obligations *yes*, transactions *no, this IG is about content*. Resolving
FHIR-51355 on the same boundary — by adding a Use Cases / actor obligations
narrative page modelled on the HDR exemplar the reporter explicitly cites,
and by stating *why* transactions are intentionally out of scope rather than
silently omitting them — directly answers the reporter's "I can't comment on
this IG as it stands" without re-opening the transactions debate. Disposition
A goes further than the WG has previously been willing to go on this
specification (it asks for transaction definitions) and risks being re-cut to
B during the call anyway. Disposition C technically defends the boundary but
leaves the reporter's actionable complaint untouched and is likely to surface
again as a negative ballot comment in September.
