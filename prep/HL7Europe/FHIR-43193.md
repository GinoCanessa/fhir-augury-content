## Summary

Gabriel Leonhartsberger (HL7 Europe) raises a question against the **EU
Laboratory Report (FHIR)** specification about how the narrative
content from the IHE LAB-TF "Group 3" mapping table is to be carried in
the FHIR Composition. The current mapping (A.5 Results → Composition:
Laboratory Report) allows two narrative source elements —
`Result.narrative.report` (A.5.1.1) and `Result.narrative.notes`
(A.5.1.2) — to land in any of three FHIR targets:
`Composition.text`, `Composition.section:lab-no-subsections.text`, or
`Composition.section:lab-subsections.section.text`. The reporter asks
for guidance on (a) how to split the source narratives across these
targets when serialising a report, and (b) how a consumer is supposed
to reconstruct which `*.text` corresponds to "Narrative report" vs
"Comments, interpretation and recommendations" when reading a
Composition. No comments, linked tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub
items have been recorded against the ticket; the only cross-references
the indexer has captured are mentions of the two FHIR elements
themselves (`Composition.text`, `Composition.section`).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the **EU Laboratory Report (FHIR)** mapping table in Group 3
(A.5 → Composition: Laboratory Report) to make the placement of
narrative deterministic instead of a free choice. Concretely:

- Mandate that **`Result.narrative.report` (A.5.1.1)** is carried in
  the section's narrative (`Composition.section:lab-no-subsections.text`
  for the no-subsections variant, or
  `Composition.section:lab-subsections.section.text` for the
  subsections variant), and that the same content is summarised /
  aggregated into `Composition.text` only as the standard FHIR
  Composition narrative roll-up (per the Composition narrative
  generation rules).
- Mandate that **`Result.narrative.notes` (A.5.1.2)** is carried as a
  distinct, machine-identifiable block inside the same section
  narrative, by introducing a fixed structural marker — either a
  required wrapping element (e.g. `<div xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml"
  data-eu-lab-narrative="notes">…</div>`) or a required leading
  heading (e.g. `<h5>Comments, interpretation and recommendations</h5>`)
  — so a consumer can split the section text back into A.5.1.1 vs
  A.5.1.2 deterministically.
- Add narrative-construction guidance and at least one normative
  example to the IG showing both the no-subsections and subsections
  layouts and the expected `data-eu-lab-narrative` markers.

#### Justification

The reporter's question only exists because the current mapping lets
two distinct source items (A.5.1.1 and A.5.1.2) land in the same
target string with no rule for separation. FHIR's general advice is
that section narrative is the human-authoritative view, while
`Composition.text` is a generated overview; aligning the IG with that
pattern keeps it consistent with IPS and other Composition-based IGs.
Using a `data-…` attribute or a required heading is a low-risk,
narrative-only convention (no schema change, no breaking constraint
on existing instruments) but gives consumers an unambiguous way to
recover the IHE LAB-TF semantics — which is exactly what the reporter
asks for.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than encoding A.5.1.1 vs A.5.1.2 inside a free-text narrative,
treat the two source items as semantically distinct payloads:

- Carry **`Result.narrative.report` (A.5.1.1)** in the section
  narrative (`Composition.section.text`) as today, since it is the
  human-readable laboratory report.
- Carry **`Result.narrative.notes` (A.5.1.2)** structurally instead,
  by either:
  - referencing one or more `Observation` resources of category
    `laboratory` with `code` = LOINC `59462-2` ("Note") (or an
    equivalent EU-Lab-defined code) from
    `Composition.section.entry`, **or**
  - using an `Annotation`-bearing element on the diagnostic content
    (e.g. `DiagnosticReport.conclusion` /
    `DiagnosticReport.conclusionCode`, or an `Observation.note` on
    the relevant result), and referencing those from the section.
- Update the Group 3 mapping table accordingly: A.5.1.1 →
  `Composition.section.text`; A.5.1.2 → structural entry, *not*
  narrative.

#### Justification

A.5.1.1 and A.5.1.2 are conceptually different things — a
narrative report versus interpretive comments — and FHIR already
provides first-class places to put interpretive comments
(`DiagnosticReport.conclusion(Code)`, `Observation.note`,
`Observation.interpretation`). Pushing comments into structured
slots eliminates the round-trip ambiguity entirely (the original
question), keeps the IHE LAB-TF semantics machine-readable, and
matches how IPS and US Core treat report-level commentary. The
trade-off is a heavier change to the IG and to existing
implementations that today emit a single narrative blob; that cost
is real, but the resulting model is unambiguous without depending
on narrative formatting conventions.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Answered – No Change**, on the grounds that
the existing IG text plus general FHIR rules already cover the
question:

- `Composition.text` is the FHIR-standard narrative for the whole
  Composition and is generated from the section narratives; it is
  not where IHE A.5.1.1 / A.5.1.2 are "stored".
- The section narrative (`Composition.section.text` /
  `Composition.section.section.text`) is the authoritative place for
  the human-readable result content, and authors are free to combine
  A.5.1.1 and A.5.1.2 into that narrative.
- Reverse-mapping a single narrative blob back into A.5.1.1 vs
  A.5.1.2 is out of scope; consumers that need that split should rely
  on the original CDA/v2 source rather than reverse-parsing FHIR
  narrative.

Add a short clarifying paragraph to the IG's narrative-handling
section to record this answer.

#### Justification

The ticket is filed as a Question, not a defect. If the workgroup is
satisfied that the loose mapping is intentional and that consumers
are not expected to recover IHE semantics from narrative, the
appropriate response is a clarification rather than a normative
change. This is the lowest-cost option and avoids introducing IG-
specific narrative conventions that diverge from generic FHIR
practice — but it leaves the original interoperability concern
(round-tripping narrative content from/to LAB-TF) unresolved.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A directly addresses the reporter's two questions
(authoring and consumption) with the smallest surface change: it
keeps the existing Composition shape, keeps both A.5.1.1 and A.5.1.2
in narrative (which is what implementers are doing today), and adds
a deterministic marker convention plus an example so authors and
consumers both know where each piece belongs. Disposition B is
architecturally cleaner but is a substantively bigger change to the
IG and to existing implementations, and should only be pursued if
the workgroup has appetite for restructuring the Group 3 mapping
this cycle. Disposition C resolves the ticket administratively but
leaves the underlying ambiguity in place, which is what generated
the question in the first place. Recommend A as the starting point
for discussion, with B held in reserve as the longer-term direction
if structural separation of comments is desired.
