## Summary

The reporter asks why `Specimen.subject` carries a cardinality of `0..1`
in the [HL7 Europe Laboratory Report Specimen profile](https://hl7.eu/fhir/laboratory/StructureDefinition-Specimen-eu-lab-definitions.html#Specimen.subject).
The implicit expectation is that any specimen drawn for a laboratory
report is, by construction, attributable to a known patient, so the
profile should arguably tighten the element to `1..1`. The reporter
also offers an alternative interpretation: that the IG intentionally
relies on `DiagnosticReport.subject` (which is `1..1`) as the single
source of subject truth, with the `Specimen` linked only transitively.

This is consistent with the underlying FHIR Core position established in
[FHIR-17424](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-17424) (Persuasive — *Specimen.subject
must be optional*), where O&O explicitly declined to make the element
mandatory because reasonable workflows exist in which the specimen is
handled before the subject is known or recorded. The same answer was
reaffirmed for an analogous IG profile in
[FHIR-38013](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-38013) (At-Home In-Vitro
Test Report, Persuasive). On the European side, related EU Lab Report
tickets [FHIR-43128](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43128) (specimen
should be referenced from `Observation`, Applied) and
[FHIR-43271](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43271) (why specimen is
`0..*` in the Bundle, answered) show the WG has already settled the
"how is the specimen tied to the patient" question by routing through
`DiagnosticReport.subject` and per-`Observation.specimen`, not by
constraining `Specimen.subject` itself.

The ticket carries the `no-ballot` label and is filed as a Question, so
the expected outcome is a documented answer rather than a profile
change.

## Details

**Description:**

> Why is cardinality 0..1 for [Specimen.subject](https://hl7.eu/fhir/laboratory/StructureDefinition-Specimen-eu-lab-definitions.html#Specimen.subject)?
>
> Shouldn't every specimen have a link to the subject, or is the intent
> that the only link is via the diagnosticReport, where subject has
> cardinality of 1..1 — and if specimen exists it is assumed to belong
> to the same subject?

### [FHIR-17424: Specimen.subject must be optional](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-17424)

FHIR Core (Orders & Observations) ticket, Published, **Persuasive**.
Lloyd McKenzie argued — and the WG agreed — that there are reasonable
situations where the handler of a specimen does not know or care about
the subject, so per FHIR methodology `Specimen.subject` must remain
`0..*` (later normalized to `0..1`). This is the canonical justification
for keeping the element optional and applies directly to any IG profile
of `Specimen`.

Reporter: Lloyd McKenzie. Assignee/handler: Orders & Observations.

### [FHIR-38013: Require Specimen.subject](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-38013)

At-Home In-Vitro Test Report (FHIR), Published, **Persuasive**. Craig
Newman raised exactly the same question being raised here — why is
`Specimen.subject` Must Support but only `0..1`, given
`DiagnosticReport.subject` is `1..1`? The WG resolved it without
tightening the cardinality, instead clarifying the intent and the
upstream FHIR Core constraint. This is essentially the precedent answer
for FHIR-43274 on a sibling IG.

Reporter: Craig Newman. Work group: Orders & Observations.

### [FHIR-43271: Why is specimen optional in the bundle?](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43271)

Filed by the same reporter (Ulrike Merrick) one day before FHIR-43274.

Reporter: Ulrike Merrick. Work group: HL7 Europe.

### [FHIR-43128: Missing: specimen needs to be assigned to the observation](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43128)

EU Laboratory Report, **Applied — Not Persuasive with Modification**.
HL7 Germany (Martina Sender / mio42 GmbH) ballot comment, applied via
modification: the specimen↔observation linkage is provided through
`Observation.specimen`, not through any tightening on the Specimen
profile. Demonstrates that the IG's chosen mechanism for tying a
specimen to a patient context is `DiagnosticReport.subject` plus
`Observation.specimen`, which is exactly the alternative interpretation
the reporter raises in FHIR-43274.

Reporter: Kai U. Heitmann (forwarding Martina Sender). Work group: HL7 Europe.

### [FHIR-43253: Cardinality Specimen.type — should be 0..1](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43253)

EU Laboratory Report, **Applied — Persuasive with Modification**.
Adjacent precedent showing the WG is willing to *relax* a Specimen

counterweight: the WG has not tightened a Specimen cardinality on this
IG; recent movement has been the opposite direction.

Reporter: Alexander Zautke (forwarding Franziska van Wüllen / Martina

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Tighten the cardinality of `Specimen.subject` on the EU Laboratory
Report Specimen profile (`Specimen-eu-lab-definitions`) from `0..1` to
`1..1` and require a `Reference(Patient)` (matching the patient
referenced from the parent `DiagnosticReport.subject`). Add an
invariant that asserts the referenced subject equals the Specimen's
parent `DiagnosticReport.subject` when both are present in the same
Bundle. Update the IG narrative to state that within a EU Lab Report
the specimen is always attributable to the laboratory report's patient.

#### Justification

Within the closed scope of an EU Lab Report Bundle, the specimen always
exists in the context of a `DiagnosticReport` whose subject is `1..1`,
so the data is always available; making the link explicit on every
`Specimen` removes ambiguity for downstream consumers and avoids forcing
them to walk back through `DiagnosticReport` (or `Observation.specimen`)
to recover the patient. This matches the spirit of recent EU Lab Report
ballot dispositions that pushed for clearer specimen↔observation
attribution (see FHIR-43128, applied).

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — Answer the question, leave the profile unchanged, improve the IG narrative

#### Proposal

Treat the ticket as a Question and resolve as *Considered – Question
answered* (mirroring the disposition of FHIR-43271 from the same
reporter). Keep `Specimen.subject` at `0..1` to remain consistent with
FHIR Core (FHIR-17424, Persuasive) and with the sibling IG decision in
FHIR-38013 (Persuasive). Add a short clarifying paragraph to the EU Lab
Report Specimen profile page (and/or the IG's "How specimens link to
patients" narrative section) stating:

> "Within an EU Lab Report Bundle the specimen's subject is always
> conveyed by the bundle's `DiagnosticReport.subject` (1..1).
> `Specimen.subject` is intentionally kept optional so that the
> Specimen profile remains aligned with FHIR Core and reusable in
> upstream lab/pre-analytical workflows where the subject is not yet
> bound to the specimen. When `Specimen.subject` is populated, it
> SHALL identify the same Patient as the report's subject."

Optionally add a non-binding `SHOULD` guidance and a SearchParameter
note that consumers can always recover the patient via the report.

#### Justification

This is the precedent answer at every relevant level: FHIR Core
(FHIR-17424), the sibling US IG (FHIR-38013), and the same reporter's
prior EU Lab Report ticket (FHIR-43271). Tightening to `1..1` would
diverge `Specimen` from FHIR Core for no implementer benefit (the
patient is already recoverable from the report) and would create a
profile that cannot accept `Specimen` resources sourced from
pre-analytical / LIS systems that legitimately don't carry a subject
reference at the Specimen level. It also avoids a breaking change for
existing implementers who validate against the current profile. A
narrative + invariant-style "if present, then equals" clarification
delivers the certainty the reporter is asking for without re-litigating
the FHIR Core decision.

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve as *Not Persuasive*. Close the ticket with a one-paragraph
response stating that `Specimen.subject = 0..1` is inherited from FHIR
Core and is settled WG policy (FHIR-17424), and that within a EU Lab
Report the patient is unambiguously available via
`DiagnosticReport.subject (1..1)`. Make no profile or narrative changes.

#### Justification

The question has been answered authoritatively three times already
(FHIR-17424, FHIR-38013, FHIR-43271) and there is no new information in
this ticket that warrants reopening the discussion. The `no-ballot`
label and `Question` type indicate this is not a ballot comment
demanding action.

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **B — Alternative Approach (answer the
question and add IG narrative).**

Disposition A would create an unnecessary divergence from FHIR Core's
`Specimen.subject = 0..1` and contradicts the directly-on-point
Persuasive resolutions in FHIR-17424 and FHIR-38013; it would also
break any existing implementer who legitimately produces a Specimen
without a subject reference (a workflow O&O has explicitly endorsed).
Disposition C correctly identifies the precedent but leaves the
reporter — and future readers of the IG — with the same confusion that
generated this ticket and FHIR-43271. Disposition B does the small
amount of authoring work that closes the gap: it reaffirms the FHIR
Core position, explains *why* the EU Lab Report doesn't need a tighter
cardinality, and (optionally) adds an invariant that pins the semantic
when `Specimen.subject` is in fact populated. The fact that the same
reporter filed FHIR-43271 a day earlier and was satisfied with a
narrative answer is the strongest signal that "answer + clarify" is
the right shape of disposition here.
