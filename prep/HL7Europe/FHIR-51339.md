## Summary

The reporter asks the HL7 Europe Imaging Study Report (R5) IG authors to
clarify the guidance attached to the **Imaging Key Image Document Reference**
profile, which currently states that
`DocumentReference.content.attachment.url` "should be a direct URL to the
image". The submitter wants the IG to be explicit about (a) whether
"direct" means a *fully qualified* URL, and (b) whether record holders are
expected to have these URLs queried *directly by the requesting
organization* in a cross-border scenario, or whether the URL must instead
be reachable by the National Contact Point (NCP) acting as an intermediary
(e.g., "a direct URL accessible to the NCP"). The ticket is explicitly
linked by the reporter to [FHIR-51273](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51273),
which raises the broader question of how access to imaging endpoints
(IID, WADO-RS) is controlled across NCPs and whether traffic is proxied,
direct, or grouped with IHE XC-WADO / MADO profiles. Together the two
tickets ask for an unambiguous architectural statement on cross-border
image retrieval, with FHIR-51339 focused narrowly on the wording of the
manifest URL and FHIR-51273 covering the surrounding endpoint and
authorization story.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the **Imaging Key Image Document Reference** profile guidance in
the EU Imaging Study Report (R5) IG to replace the bare phrase
"`DocumentReference.content.attachment.url` should be a direct URL to
the image" with explicit, normative wording that:

1. Requires `attachment.url` to be an **absolute (fully qualified) URL**
   — i.e., starting with a scheme (`https://`) and host, not a relative
   reference, not a logical reference, and not a `urn:` form — so it can
   be dereferenced without additional context. Add a corresponding
   invariant or, at minimum, a "SHALL be an absolute URL" sentence
   adjacent to the element binding.
2. States who is expected to be able to dereference the URL. Adopt the
   "**accessible to the NCP**" framing the reporter suggests: the URL
   SHALL be resolvable by the requesting NCP (which may then re-write or
   proxy it for the receiving organization), and it SHOULD NOT be
   assumed to be reachable by an arbitrary requesting organization
   directly across borders.
3. Cross-links to whatever resolution is reached on
   [FHIR-51273](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51273) for the
   surrounding endpoint / NCP / WADO-RS access story (e.g., a pointer
   to the Transactions page once it documents the IHE XC-WADO + MADO
   grouping).

#### Justification

The reporter has identified a real ambiguity: "direct URL" is read
differently by implementers depending on whether they assume an open
internet retrieve or an NCP-mediated cross-border retrieve, and the
profile is silent on absolute vs. relative URL form. Both gaps are
fixable as IG-text changes without altering the underlying FHIR
`DocumentReference` resource. Calling out an absolute URL is consistent
with how other FHIR IGs constrain `Attachment.url` when it is meant to
be dereferenced out-of-bundle, and the "accessible to the NCP" framing
aligns with the consensus emerging in FHIR-51273's recent comments that
cross-border retrieval is expected to flow through the NCP using the
IHE XC-WADO / MADO profile grouping.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than tightening only the prose around `attachment.url`, address
the ambiguity structurally by:

1. Keeping `attachment.url` as the human-/machine-resolvable retrieve
   pointer but explicitly **delegating endpoint semantics** to a
   companion `Endpoint` resource (referenced from the
   `ImagingStudy`/manifest), so that the actor expected to perform the
   retrieve, the connection type (e.g., `ihe-xca`, `dicom-wado-rs`),
   and any NCP-mediated re-write rules are conveyed as data, not prose.
2. Adding an **invariant** on the profile that requires
   `attachment.url` to be absolute (`matches('^https?://.*')` or
   equivalent) and a **SHOULD** that it be resolvable by the receiving
   NCP, while leaving direct cross-organization reachability optional.
3. Documenting the expected resolution flow on the IG's Transactions
   page (the change FHIR-51273 is asking for) and pointing the
   profile's element guidance at that page rather than restating the
   architecture inline.

#### Justification

This addresses the root cause — that the IG conflates "what the URL
looks like" with "who can dereference it" — instead of layering more
prose on top of a single element. Using `Endpoint` to carry connection
type and access expectations is the FHIR-idiomatic way to describe
"how do I actually retrieve this?", keeps the profile narrow, and
avoids re-litigating cross-border access wording every time the
architecture in FHIR-51273 evolves. It is, however, a larger change
that touches more of the IG and may slip a ballot cycle.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change on the grounds that the requested clarification is
out of scope for the **Imaging Key Image Document Reference** profile
text, because the substantive question — who can dereference the
manifest URL across NCPs — is being answered by FHIR-51273 and by the
emerging IHE XC-WADO + MADO grouping. Resolve FHIR-51339 as **Not
Persuasive / Duplicate** of FHIR-51273 and let the eventual
Transactions-page text in 51273 carry the architectural definition,
without modifying the per-element guidance on
`DocumentReference.content.attachment.url`.

#### Justification

Defensible only if the workgroup judges that the manifest profile
should not embed cross-border access semantics at all, and that all
such guidance belongs in the Transactions page being added under
FHIR-51273. The risk is leaving the current "direct URL" wording in
place, which the reporter has demonstrated is genuinely ambiguous; on
its own that ambiguity is enough to motivate at least a small text
change, so a pure decline is hard to justify unless paired with
explicit confirmation that 51273's resolution will overwrite or
remove the offending sentence.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is a precise, low-cost ballot comment about a single
sentence of profile guidance, raised by the same reporter who filed
the broader architectural ticket (FHIR-51273) and labelled both with
`manifest`. There is real ambiguity to fix — "direct URL" does not
distinguish absolute vs. relative form, nor open-internet vs.
NCP-mediated reachability — and the reporter has even proposed
acceptable wording ("a direct URL accessible to the NCP"). Disposition
A captures that fix in the smallest possible change, keeps the IG
internally consistent with the XC-WADO/MADO direction emerging in
FHIR-51273's comments, and leaves the larger architectural narrative
to live in the Transactions page where 51273 wants it. Disposition B
is the cleaner long-term design but is too large to land alongside
51339 alone; it should be considered as part of the FHIR-51273
resolution. Disposition C under-serves the reporter and leaves a known
ambiguity in published profile text.
