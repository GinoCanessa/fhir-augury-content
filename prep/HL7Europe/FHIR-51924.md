## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Replace the existing "Figure 1" image asset on the IG home/overview page
of the EU Hospital Discharge Report (FHIR) IG with an updated diagram
supplied by the reporter (or jointly agreed by the HL7 Europe HDR
project team). Concretely:

1. Obtain an updated source diagram (preferably an editable source such
   as `.drawio`, `.svg`, or PowerPoint) from Giorgio Cangioli that
   reflects the current scope and structure of the HDR IG.
2. Export it as the same image format and filename currently referenced
   by "Figure 1" so the markdown/XML reference does not need to change.
3. Commit both the editable source and the exported image to the IG
   repo, replacing the current asset.
4. Verify the figure renders correctly in a local IG build and that the
   surrounding caption / paragraph text still describes the new picture
   accurately; update the caption if the new diagram differs in
   semantics.

#### Justification

The ticket is filed as a Technical Correction by the HDR specification
lead, who is well placed to judge whether the existing figure is
out-of-date. Image refreshes on IG home pages are a routine editorial
maintenance activity and do not affect conformance, validation, or
implementer artifacts. Replacing a stale diagram improves reader
comprehension at zero cost to implementers, which matches the intent of
the Technical Correction issue type.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Before swapping the asset, ask the reporter to clarify the intended
update and bundle this change with a small editorial pass on the HDR
overview page:

1. Request from the reporter (a) the specific shortcomings of the
   current figure and (b) a candidate replacement, ideally with editable
   source.
2. Treat "Figure 1" as part of the overview narrative: review the
   surrounding text, the figure caption, and any later figures (Figure
   2, 3, …) for consistency in style, terminology, and visual
   vocabulary, and refresh them together.
3. Add the editable source under a conventional `input/images-source/`
   (or equivalent) folder so future updates do not require reverse-
   engineering the picture.
4. Land the change as a single editorial PR that closes FHIR-51924 and
   tidies any drift in the surrounding overview text discovered during
   the review.

#### Justification

Replacing a single figure in isolation often leaves the caption,
narrative, and downstream figures stylistically inconsistent — a common
source of follow-up tickets. Coupling the asset refresh with a brief
editorial sweep and capturing editable source costs only marginally
more effort now and reduces the likelihood of a future "update figure
1 again" ticket. It also forces an explicit statement of *what* needs
updating, which the current one-line description does not provide.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the ticket as **Not Persuasive Without Detail / Insufficient
Information**: the request as written ("Use for figure 1 a more updated
picture") does not identify which aspects of the existing figure are
out-of-date or supply a replacement, and there is no community
discussion (Zulip / GitHub) backing a specific change. Close the ticket
with a request for the reporter to re-file with (a) the concrete
deficiencies of the current figure and (b) a proposed replacement
asset.

#### Justification

Workgroups generally do not act on editorial requests that lack a
concrete proposed change, because the work cannot be planned or
verified — any chosen replacement is essentially an editor's guess.
Declining with a clear "please re-file with the new image" pushes the
ball back to the reporter (who is the HDR specification lead and
therefore best positioned to provide the asset) without consuming
work-group cycles on an ambiguous task.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A (with the editable-source step from B
folded in)

The reporter is the HDR specification lead, the issue type is Technical
Correction, and the change is a low-risk editorial asset swap with no
implementer impact. There is no good reason to decline; doing so on
process grounds (Disposition C) would be disproportionate to the size
of the change and would slow down a maintenance task the reporter
clearly considers overdue (the ticket has been open since August 2025
and was last touched in October 2025). The pragmatic path is to accept,
ask Giorgio for the updated picture and — if available — its editable
source, replace the asset on the HDR overview page, and check the
caption/narrative still match. That captures the upside of Disposition
B (editable source, caption check) without blocking on a broader
editorial sweep that is out of scope for this single-figure ticket.
