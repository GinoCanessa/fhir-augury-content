## Summary

The ticket is a comment on the EU Base & Core (R4) `Procedure` profile, raised
during the January 2026 HL7 Europe ballot. The reporter observes that
`Procedure.code` carries a **preferred** binding to the IPS procedure
ValueSet, which they describe as "highly limited" relative to the breadth of
procedures used in European healthcare systems. Because a preferred binding
implicitly steers implementers toward the bound ValueSet, the reporter argues
this is too restrictive for `Procedure.code` and proposes weakening the
binding strength to **example**, which would still expose the IPS ValueSet as
a useful reference without implying that conformant systems should be drawn
from it.

There are no explicitly linked Jira tickets and no Jira-to-Jira
cross-references. The only related discussion is the d-a-ch (German-speaking
affiliate) ballot collection thread on Zulip, where the ticket was posted
alongside a batch of other German EU Base & Core ballot comments but did not
attract focused discussion of its own.

## Details

**Description:**

> In the Procedure profile, Procedure.code uses a preferred binding to the
> IPS ValueSet. However, this ValueSet is highly limited and does not cover
> many procedures commonly used in European healthcare systems. A preferred
> binding may therefore be too restrictive, as it implicitly suggests using
> only codes from this ValueSet, even though many clinically relevant
> procedures are missing. An example binding would offer better flexibility
> while still allowing the IPS ValueSet to serve as a useful reference.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Change the binding on `Procedure.code` in the EU Core `Procedure` profile
(`StructureDefinition-procedure-eu-core`) from **preferred** to **example**,
keeping the bound ValueSet pointer to the IPS procedure ValueSet
(`http://hl7.org/fhir/uv/ips/ValueSet/procedures-uv-ips`). Update the
element's short description and the profile's narrative guidance to make
explicit that the IPS ValueSet is shown as an illustrative reference set
and that implementers are expected to bind nationally appropriate
procedure terminologies (e.g., national SNOMED CT extensions, OPS in DE,
CCAM in FR) at the profile or implementation level.

#### Justification

Per the FHIR core terminology guidance, **preferred** does carry an
expectation that the listed codes "should" be used unless a strong reason
exists not to, whereas **example** explicitly disclaims that intent. The
reporter's concern — that the IPS procedures ValueSet does not cover the
breadth of European national procedure terminologies — is well-known: IPS
intentionally narrows to a small interoperability set, not a domain-complete
catalogue. For a *Base & Core* profile that is meant to be a substrate for
national/affiliate derivations rather than a directly conformant artifact,
**example** is consistent with how EU Core handles other terminology-rich
elements (e.g., `Medication.code` already uses an example binding, per
the parallel discussion in the d-a-ch Zulip thread on FHIR-55505/-55506,
where Joshua Wiedekopf noted that example bindings in EU Core "sind nur
bedingt ernst zu nehmen" and do not block national codes). Aligning
`Procedure.code` with that same pattern reduces the risk of implementers
misreading the preferred binding as a soft mandate.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Leave the binding strength at **preferred** but (a) add an **additional
binding** of purpose `candidate` (or `ui`) pointing to the IPS ValueSet
to keep its role visible, and (b) replace the primary `valueSet` with a
broader EU-level procedure ValueSet — either an existing eHDSI/IPS-EU
procedures set, or a new EU Core ValueSet defined as the union of the
IPS set with SNOMED CT `< 71388002 |Procedure|`. Document in the
`Procedure` profile narrative that affiliates SHOULD refine this binding
nationally.

#### Justification

This addresses the reporter's underlying concern (the IPS set is too
narrow) without giving up the stronger semantic signal that `preferred`
provides. In a Base & Core IG, fully demoting to `example` removes any
uniform expectation across affiliates and weakens cross-border
interoperability — precisely the value proposition of the EU layer. A
broader EU-scoped ValueSet, paired with explicit additional-binding
metadata for the IPS subset, preserves a meaningful default for
implementers who have no national binding to fall back on, while still
covering the European procedure landscape the reporter cites. The
trade-off versus Disposition A is more authoring effort (defining and
maintaining a broader EU procedure ValueSet, with SNOMED CT licensing
implications) and a longer reconciliation cycle, but it produces a more
useful artifact for cross-border use cases (e.g., patient summary
exchange, MyHealth@EU).

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change. Keep `Procedure.code` bound to the IPS procedure
ValueSet at strength `preferred`, on the basis that (a) `preferred` in
FHIR is explicitly non-mandatory, (b) the IPS procedure ValueSet is the
canonical cross-jurisdictional reference set for procedures in
international/EU patient-summary work, and (c) affiliates remain free to
tighten or replace the binding in derived profiles. Optionally, add a
short clarifying paragraph to the profile narrative explaining how
affiliates should override the binding.

#### Justification

`preferred` is, by FHIR definition, advisory: it does not block the use
of any code from any system, and tooling/validators do not flag use of
non-listed codes as errors. The d-a-ch ballot thread itself reached the
same conclusion for similar tickets on `Medication.code`,
`Medication.form`, and `MedicationRequest.intent`, where Joshua
Wiedekopf pointed out that example/weak bindings in EU Core do not
prevent national usage. Demoting to `example` removes a useful default
signal for implementers who have no national procedure terminology
selected and would not, in practice, change validator behaviour for
those who do. The d-a-ch affiliate also chose **not** to attach
FHIR-55520 to its negative vote, treating it as a friendly suggestion
rather than a blocking concern, which supports a "no change required"
outcome.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

A binding strength of `preferred` on `Procedure.code` in a *Base & Core*
profile sends a stronger steering signal than the EU Base & Core IG can
honestly support, given that the bound IPS ValueSet was never designed
to cover the procedure terminologies in routine European use (national
SNOMED CT extensions, OPS, CCAM, NOMESCO, etc.). The reporter's
suggestion to demote to `example` is the smallest, most mechanically
consistent change available: it aligns `Procedure.code` with how EU Core
already treats other terminology-heavy elements such as `Medication.code`
and `Medication.form`, removes the implicit "should use IPS" reading
that the reporter is reacting to, and keeps the IPS pointer visible as
a reference. Disposition B is more ambitious and arguably produces a
better long-term artifact, but it requires content work the workgroup
may not want to fit into the current ballot reconciliation, plus
ongoing terminology maintenance. Disposition C is defensible on a
strict reading of the FHIR binding-strength definitions, but it leaves
the profile narrative misaligned with the reporter's (and likely other
implementers') natural reading of `preferred`. Accepting the change as
requested is the cleanest disposition for ballot reconciliation; if the
workgroup wants to pursue the broader EU-scoped ValueSet from
Disposition B, that can be tracked as a follow-on enhancement.
