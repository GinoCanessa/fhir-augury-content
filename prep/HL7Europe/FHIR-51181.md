## Summary

FHIR-51181 is a multi-point editorial review of the **Design Considerations** page of the *EU Imaging Study Report (R5)* implementation guide raised by Giorgio Cangioli (HL7 Europe). The reporter raises five distinct issues against that page:

1. Internal links to EU guides currently resolve to `build.fhir.org` even when a published version exists; they should be redirected to the canonical site at `https://hl7.eu/fhir`.
2. The page describes the `Composition` resource as defining "the report structure, often just a single section," but the Imaging Report profile in this IG appears to require **eight sections** — the description and the profile must be reconciled.
3. Items currently sitting under an "Open Issues" heading should be **promoted to STU Notes** to comply with HL7 publication guidelines, if they are still relevant.
4. The page does not clarify scope of **actors and their obligations** — e.g., whether actors are restricted to providers, or may be content-focused (author, signer) or content-independent (transport / intermediary).
5. The acronym **MCWG** is used without expansion; it should be defined on first use.

The ticket should be read alongside the broader EU Imaging review tranche by the same and related reporters: [FHIR-51126](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51126) (overall IG structure/template), [FHIR-51283](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51283) (search parameters), [FHIR-51290](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51290) (right-sizing the dataset), [FHIR-51390](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51390) (placement of unstructured reports), and [FHIR-51396](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51396) (Imaging extensions). Several of these are already `Applied`; FHIR-51181 is the still-open companion focused specifically on the Design Considerations narrative.

## Details

**Description:**

- The links to the EU guides currently point to `build.fhir.org`, even for published versions. It is recommended to update these links to point to the official site at **[hl7.eu/fhir](https://hl7.eu/fhir)** instead.
- Regarding the referenced `Composition` resource: *"Defines the report structure, often just a single section."* — Is this also applicable to the **Imaging Report**? It appears that the current version requires **eight sections**, which seems inconsistent with the statement.
- If the questions listed under "Open Issues" are still relevant, they should be **converted into STU Notes**, as per HL7 publication guidelines.
- **Actor requirements and obligations**: Are the actors limited to **providers**? Could an actor be **content-focused** (e.g., author or signer) or **content-independent** (e.g., transport service or intermediary)?
- The acronym **MCWG** is mentioned but not explained. Please consider adding a definition or expanding it on first use for clarity.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply all five corrections to the *Design Considerations* page of the EU Imaging Study Report (R5) IG, in a single editorial PR:

1. **Link hygiene** — Replace every reference to `build.fhir.org/ig/hl7-eu/...` (and any sibling `build.fhir.org/...` link to a published EU IG) with the canonical `https://hl7.eu/fhir/...` URL on the Design Considerations page. Where the linked IG has a published version, use the versioned canonical; otherwise, use the current canonical.
2. **Composition narrative reconciliation** — Reword the "*Defines the report structure, often just a single section*" sentence so it accurately describes the Imaging Report profile. Replace it with text such as: *"The Composition resource defines the report structure. The EU Imaging Report profile organises content into the following sections: <list the eight sections by code/title>."* Then briefly justify the eight-section design (or, if any section is genuinely optional, mark its `min` accordingly and reflect that in the narrative).
3. **Open Issues → STU Notes** — Re-render every still-relevant entry under the "Open Issues" heading using the IG-publisher STU-Note construct (per HL7 publication guidelines). Remove items that are no longer relevant. The "Open Issues" heading itself should be deleted once empty.
4. **Actor scope clarification** — Add a paragraph under the Actor section that explicitly enumerates which actor categories are in scope. Recommend stating that the IG profiles **content-producing** actors (author, signer) and **content-consuming** actors, while explicitly noting that **content-independent** actors (transport, intermediaries) are out of scope; if any of those are in scope, list them with their obligations.
5. **MCWG glossary entry** — Expand "MCWG" on first use (e.g., *"Multimedia Content Working Group (MCWG)"* — confirm the actual expansion with the reporter) and add the term to the IG glossary page.

Open the PR against the EU Imaging Study Report IG source repo (see the coverage-gap note above for the repo-onboarding prerequisite).

#### Justification

Every bullet in the ticket is an editorial defect that has an obvious correct answer: published IGs must point at canonical URLs, narrative must match the profile it describes, "Open Issues" sections violate the HL7 publication checklist (HL7-Pubs requires they be promoted to STU Notes prior to publication), undefined actor scope is a balloter complaint magnet, and undefined acronyms fail the IG style guide. None of the items expand the IG's scope or break interoperability — they tighten what is already there. Splitting the items into separate tickets would multiply PR overhead without benefit; the items all touch the same page and reviewer.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Treat FHIR-51181 as the umbrella for a **page-rewrite** rather than five spot fixes, **and** decompose the structurally larger items into stand-alone follow-on tickets:

- Apply items (1) link hygiene, (3) Open Issues → STU Notes, and (5) MCWG expansion immediately on the Design Considerations page (these are pure editorial and uncontroversial).
- Spin **item (2) — Composition section model** out as a new, dedicated ticket against the Imaging Report profile, because the underlying question ("eight sections vs. one") is a *design* question, not a *narrative* question. Resolution should align with [FHIR-51290](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51290) (core dataset) and [FHIR-51390](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51390) (single unstructured report) so all three converge on one section model.
- Spin **item (4) — Actor obligations** out as a new ticket so it can be reviewed by the WG with input on the FHIR R5 *Obligations* extension, rather than handled as a side-edit on a narrative page.

Close FHIR-51181 once items (1), (3), (5) are merged and the two follow-on tickets are filed.

#### Justification

Items (2) and (4) are not actually editorial. Item (2) implies either *changing the profile* (relaxing `min` on sections) or *changing the IG narrative*; the right choice depends on what the WG concludes about the core dataset, which is the subject of FHIR-51290. Bundling that decision into an editorial PR risks either pre-empting that discussion or producing wording that has to be revised again once FHIR-51290 lands. Item (4), similarly, drives requirements that the IG may want to encode using the FHIR R5 `obligation` mechanism — which is a profile change, not a Design-Considerations rewrite. Splitting separates uncontroversial work (mergeable now) from work that needs WG discussion, and gives each follow-on the visibility it deserves.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the ticket as filed — close it as **Not Persuasive (Editorial)** — on the grounds that:

- The link hygiene point (1) should be enforced by IG-publisher / Sushi link-validation rather than by hand-edits to one page; the right fix is a publication-pipeline rule.
- Items (3) and (5) duplicate the standing **HL7 publication checklist**; the publication facilitator already enforces "no Open Issues sections" and "expand acronyms on first use" as part of the publication request.
- Items (2) and (4) are **questions, not change requests**; without a concrete proposal from the reporter the WG has nothing actionable to apply.

Recommend the reporter raise (2) and (4) as separate, scoped tickets with proposed text, and rely on the publication checklist for (1), (3), (5).

#### Justification

Defensible only on process-purity grounds: the ticket *is* a mixture of editorial nits and unresolved design questions, and the WG can argue that each category belongs in a different workflow (publication pipeline vs. design ticket). This is a defensible disposition for a WG that is volume-constrained and wants to push checklist items back to the facilitator. It does, however, leave a real-world page in a known-broken state until the reporter re-files, which is why it is not the recommendation.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **B**

The ticket genuinely contains two different kinds of work, and that distinction matters for outcomes. Items (1), (3), and (5) are textbook editorial fixes — there is no design judgement to make, the publication checklist already requires them, and they should land in the next routine editorial pass on the Design Considerations page. Bundling them with the section-count question (2) and the actor-obligations question (4) would either (a) delay the editorial fixes behind a longer design discussion, or (b) lock in narrative wording before the related, still-open ticket [FHIR-51290](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51290) has been resolved — a pattern the WG has hit before in this IG family. Splitting per Disposition B preserves the reporter's full intent, gives the design questions the visibility they need (and lets them be triaged against the EU Imaging tranche as a whole), and avoids re-touching the same page twice. Disposition A is the right choice only if the WG is confident that the eight-section design and the actor scope are already settled and just need to be written down — at present, neither appears to be the case.

Procedural prerequisite: before any of the implementation work can be planned at file level, the *EU Imaging Study Report* IG repository should be added to the FhirAugury GitHub source configuration (`IgRepositories` in `src/FhirAugury.Source.GitHub/appsettings.json`) and a `repo-analysis` briefing generated, so future planning passes can name authoring roots and file paths concretely.
