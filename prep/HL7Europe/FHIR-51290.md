## Summary

The reporter recommends that the first published version of the EU Imaging
Study Report IG focus on a minimal "core" imaging dataset and explicitly
exclude four Composition sections currently defined in the guide:
**AdverseEvents**, **RadiationDose**, **Contrast Medications**, and
**Procedure Phases**. The argument is that these sections add scope,
complexity, and implementation cost while providing limited value for the
guide's primary cross-border use case (making completed imaging reports and
images available at the point of care). The reporter proposes that these
data domains be deferred to a later iteration of the IG (or, in the case of
radiation dose, deferred to the dedicated HL7
"Radiation Dose Summary for Diagnostic Procedures on FHIR" IG) once the
implementer community has gained experience with the core report exchange.

No related Jira tickets, Zulip threads, GitHub items, or other cross
references were surfaced by the FHIR Augury indexes for this ticket.

## Details

**Description:**

Currently, the Composition contains dedicated sections for a large set of
data. This includes:

- AdverseEvents
- RadiationDose
- Contrast Medications
- Procedure Phases

We would suggest excluding these from a first version of this guide so that
the interoperability community can focus on the immediate need of making
imaging results available and scaling interoperability across the EU.
Including this in scope increases complexity and cost and distracts from
the immediate goal of making reports available.

As detail:

- Workflows vary significantly for how **AdverseEvents** are tracked, and
  how this is reflected in imaging. If a patient has a minor adverse
  reaction, the imaging may proceed (in which case, the reaction likely is
  not relevant), it may lead to a change of procedure, or it may result in
  the imaging being stopped. Since we expect that only completed procedures
  will be shared across borders, this information is rarely relevant to
  interpreting results. If the goal is to help recipient systems place
  better orders themselves (identify an appropriate protocol for the
  patient, understand why alternatives protocols were not used) this could
  be added at a later date after the implementer community has realized the
  immediate goal of ensuring images are available at the point of care.
- **Radiation Dose** — this information should generally originate from the
  PACS. Most EHRs also have only limited ability to track radiation dose;
  they may support free text fields, DICOM SR templates for CT radiation
  dose, or measurements from the legacy DICOM MPPS standard, but adoption
  is generally low, and the PACS will have more accurate information.
  - This [European Radiology survey (Question 8)](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00330-021-07696-1)
    recommends dose data should be included in a separate dose tracking
    system rather than in the Report.
  - Alternatively, if data must be communicated to the EHR, we recommend
    letting the [Radiation Dose Summary for Diagnostic Procedures on FHIR
    v1.0.0](https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/fhir-radiation-dose-summary-ig/)
    IG further develop the FHIR mapping of this data.
- **Contrast** — while it is important for viewers of imaging to know
  whether contrast was used with a procedure, this is often reflected in
  the performed procedure code (the Procedure resource) and in the
  radiologist's textual narrative. Discretely sharing information regarding
  medications is a large increase in scope for minimal benefit.
- **Procedure Phases** — generally, results are generated for imaging as a
  whole: there are not separate results for imaging that occurred without
  contrast and imaging that occurred with contrast. If documented, this
  information is present in the DICOM data already, and we do not expect
  that requesting clinicians would wish to query only for the imaging of
  the 'non-contrast' portion, without obtaining images from the broader
  study or key images.

## Proposed Dispositions

> Note: The EU Imaging Study Report (R5) IG is maintained by HL7 Europe
> outside the GitHub repositories indexed by FHIR Augury, and no
> repo-analysis briefing is available. The dispositions below therefore
> describe changes at the IG / Composition-profile level rather than at a
> specific authoring file path. If the workgroup wants file-level
> guidance, the appropriate HL7 Europe imaging IG repository should be
> added to the cache and a `repo-analysis` briefing produced before
> implementing.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Remove the four flagged sections from the EU Imaging Study Report
Composition profile for the first published version of the IG:

- Drop the `AdverseEvents` section slice (any slice on
  `Composition.section` whose `code` identifies adverse events; remove the
  slice definition and any associated `Composition.section.entry`
  references to `AdverseEvent` or related resources).
- Drop the `RadiationDose` section slice (and remove the associated
  `Observation`-based radiation dose profile from the IG's "must support"
  set, deferring to the standalone *Radiation Dose Summary for Diagnostic
  Procedures on FHIR* IG via a non-normative cross-reference instead).
- Drop the `Contrast Medications` section slice (rely on the existing
  `Procedure.code` / `Procedure.usedCode` and the radiologist's narrative
  in the report text section to convey contrast usage).
- Drop the `Procedure Phases` section slice (rely on DICOM-side phase
  information and the overall report narrative).

In the IG narrative, add an explicit "Out of scope for this version"
subsection that lists each removed section, the rationale, and where
implementers should look instead (DICOM, the dose-summary IG, the
`Procedure` resource). Mark these as candidates for a future ballot cycle.

#### Justification

This matches the reporter's intent and is consistent with the FHIR
project's general guidance to start IGs with the minimum interoperable
core and grow based on real implementer feedback. The European Radiology
survey cited in the ticket supports keeping radiation dose in dedicated
tracking systems, and an HL7 IG already exists for FHIR-based dose
summaries, so duplicating that mapping here risks divergence. Adverse
events, contrast medications, and procedure phases each pull in additional
resources (`AdverseEvent`, `Medication*`, granular `Procedure` modeling)
whose value for cross-border *report* exchange is not yet demonstrated and
which significantly expand conformance burden on EHR/PACS implementers.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the four sections defined in the Composition profile but lower their
conformance so they are clearly optional and do not block early
implementation:

- Change the cardinality of the `AdverseEvents`, `RadiationDose`, `Contrast
  Medications`, and `Procedure Phases` slices on `Composition.section`
  from `1..1` (or `0..1` MustSupport) to `0..1` **without** MustSupport,
  and remove any invariants that require their presence.
- For `RadiationDose`, replace any IG-local Observation profile with a
  cross-reference to the *Radiation Dose Summary for Diagnostic Procedures
  on FHIR* IG and note that, if dose is conveyed, that IG's profiles
  SHOULD be used.
- For `Contrast`, document that contrast usage MAY be conveyed via
  `Procedure.code` / narrative and that a structured medication entry is
  optional.
- Keep all four section slice *definitions* in the IG so that producers
  who already have the data can populate them in a standards-aligned way,
  and so that future versions of the IG can tighten conformance without
  re-introducing breaking structural change.

#### Justification

This addresses the reporter's core concern — that these sections increase
complexity, cost, and distract from the core "make reports available" goal
— without permanently losing the modeling work that has already been done.
Producers who do have the data (e.g., dose-aware PACS, EHRs with adverse
event workflows) can still emit a conformant report including those
sections, while consumers and minimum-viable producers face no obligation
to handle them. It also preserves a smoother upgrade path: tightening
cardinality / MustSupport in a future ballot is a much smaller change than
re-adding removed slices, especially across MyHealth@EU national
deployments.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request and retain `AdverseEvents`, `RadiationDose`,
`Contrast Medications`, and `Procedure Phases` as currently profiled in
the EU Imaging Study Report Composition. If feedback from implementers
during STU shows the sections are unused or harmful, address them in a
subsequent ballot cycle rather than restructuring the IG mid-stream.

#### Justification

Defensible only if the project team has concrete evidence that these
sections reflect concrete EU-level requirements (regulatory, MyHealth@EU
guidance, or national programs) that would not be satisfied by the
narrative section plus DICOM-side data. In that case, removing them now
would create a gap that a later ballot would have to refill, and removing
them after national projects have started building against the current
draft could be more disruptive than keeping them as defined.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The reporter's diagnosis — that the current scope is broad and risks
slowing first-wave EU adoption — is well argued and is supported by
existing community guidance (the European Radiology survey and the
existence of a dedicated HL7 dose-summary IG). However, fully removing
the four sections (Disposition A) is a structural change that loses
modeling work and forces breaking change later when these data domains
re-enter scope, which they almost certainly will (radiation dose in
particular is a regulated concern in several EU jurisdictions). Lowering
the conformance to `0..1` without MustSupport and pointing
`RadiationDose` at the existing dose-summary IG (Disposition B) gives
first-wave implementers exactly the relief the ticket asks for — they
can ignore these sections entirely and still claim conformance — while
preserving the option to tighten or extend the profile in a later ballot
without re-introducing breaking structural change. This is the path most
consistent with how FHIR IGs typically evolve and with the reporter's own
suggestion that these areas be revisited "after the implementer community
has realized the immediate goal."
