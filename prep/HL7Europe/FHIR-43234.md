## Summary

The reporter (Ulrike Merrick) is raising a question against the HL7 Europe
**EU Laboratory Report (FHIR)** IG's "Notes to Balloters" regarding the use
of FHIR **Obligations** to describe actor expectations. The reporter notes
that in the US, meaningful-use certification of EHRs for the V2-based
Laboratory Results Interface (LRI) IG is enabled by an explicit conformance
specification ([HL7 product #433](https://www.hl7.org/implement/standards/product_brief.cfm?product_id=433)),
and that without such a declaration, certification and functionality testing
cannot be achieved. The reporter observes that while the EU Lab Report
examples assign action verbs to each actor, the **level of expectation
(MAY / SHOULD / SHALL)** is not clear from the current Obligations notation.
The reporter argues that without explicit conformance levels the Obligations
are less useful than intended, and recommends that the European profile
keep those expectation levels **as low as reasonable** so that further
constraints can be layered at the national-profile level.

No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip discussions, or GitHub items were
surfaced for this ticket.

## Details

**Description:**

In the US we have defined something similar to support the meaningful use
certification of EHRs of the V2 based Laboratory Results Interface (LRI) IG
by creating this specification:

https://www.hl7.org/implement/standards/product_brief.cfm?product_id=433

Without such a declaration, certification and system functionality testing
cannot be achieved.

It seems while in the examples you have defined the action verbs for
support by each actor, the level of expectation (MAY, SHOULD, SHALL) is
not clear to me from the current notation (which may be because I haven't
spent much time on researching this in base FHIR) — I believe without the
clear indication of the level of obligation this is not as useful as
expected. I also believe that the European Profile should make these
levels of obligation as low as reasonable, so that further constraints
can happen at the national profile level.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the EU Laboratory Report (FHIR) IG so that every actor-scoped
obligation declared in the "Notes to Balloters" (and the corresponding
profile-level obligation extensions — typically
`http://hl7.org/fhir/StructureDefinition/obligation` applied to
`ElementDefinition` or actor definitions) carries an **explicit
conformance level** using the base-FHIR `obligation.usage` /
`obligation.code` mechanism. Concretely:

1. For each actor × capability pair currently expressed with an action
   verb, pick one of `SHALL:populate`, `SHOULD:populate`,
   `MAY:populate`, `SHALL:handle`, `SHOULD:handle`, `MAY:handle`
   (and the analogous `display` / `no-error` codes where used) and
   surface that code in the rendered obligation tables.
2. Default the European profile to the **lowest reasonable** level
   (e.g., `SHOULD` or `MAY` where there is not a clear pan-European
   interoperability requirement to mandate behaviour), leaving `SHALL`
   for items that the working group has explicit consensus to mandate
   across Europe.
3. Expand the "Notes to Balloters" section to state the policy
   explicitly: the EU Lab Report sets a **floor**, and national IGs
   (DE, FR, BE, …) may derive profiles that raise obligation levels
   (e.g., MAY → SHOULD, SHOULD → SHALL) to meet their jurisdictional
   certification requirements.
4. Add a short example showing the same obligation at EU level
   (`SHOULD`) being tightened at a national level (`SHALL`) so that
   balloters can see the intended layering.

#### Justification

This is what the reporter literally asks for: make the conformance
level visible in the notation and default it low so national profiles
can tighten. It is consistent with how base FHIR's Obligations
framework is designed to be used (the `code` on an obligation is
expected to carry the SHALL/SHOULD/MAY semantics, not just the verb),
and it mirrors the pattern the US LRI IG uses to enable certification
testing. Writing an explicit "floor here, tighten nationally" policy
into the Notes to Balloters also directly answers the reporter's
stated concern that without a clear conformance level the obligations
are "not as useful as expected."

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than re-assigning a SHALL/SHOULD/MAY level to every individual
obligation in the EU Lab Report, **clarify the Notes to Balloters and
the Obligations page** to explain the existing semantics, and adopt
a small, documented convention:

1. Add a paragraph to "Notes to Balloters" (and to the IG's obligations
   documentation page) that explicitly states the default conformance
   level implied by obligations in this IG — e.g., "unless otherwise
   noted, EU-level obligations in this IG are **SHOULD**; obligations
   marked `SHALL:...` are the only ones mandated at the European tier."
2. Keep the current action-verb notation in the examples, but annotate
   the handful of obligations that are mandatory at EU scope with an
   explicit `SHALL` marker (rendered visibly in the obligation table).
3. Add a cross-reference to the base-FHIR Obligations specification so
   readers can see where MAY/SHOULD/SHALL fit into the `obligation.code`
   CodeSystem, rather than duplicating that material.
4. Invite national affiliates to publish derived profiles that raise
   levels as needed, and note in the IG that this layering is the
   intended certification path (analogous to US LRI).

#### Justification

This addresses the underlying concern — unclear conformance
expectations — without requiring the working group to revisit every
obligation in the IG ahead of the current ballot. It leans on the
existing base-FHIR Obligations machinery rather than inventing a
parallel notation, which reduces implementer burden and avoids
divergence from other IGs that use Obligations. It also keeps the
"European floor, national ceiling" principle the reporter endorsed,
but pays for it with a documentation change rather than a pervasive
structural change. The trade-off vs. Disposition A is that individual
obligations are not each tagged with a level — readers have to apply
the documented default — which is less explicit but materially less
work and less disruptive to the current ballot package.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Answered / Not Persuasive**: the conformance
levels for each obligation are already carried by the base-FHIR
Obligations code system (`SHALL:populate`, `SHOULD:handle`, etc.) and
are rendered in the generated obligation tables; the IG does not need
to restate them in the Notes to Balloters, and the "European floor,
national ceiling" model is already the implicit policy for HL7 Europe
IGs. Respond to the reporter pointing to the base-FHIR Obligations
documentation and to examples in the IG where the level is visible.

#### Justification

If the obligations in the EU Lab Report are already expressed using
the base-FHIR Obligations extension with conformance codes, then the
level is not actually missing — it is just not surfaced in the
example prose the reporter was reading. In that case the issue is a
navigation / documentation-discovery problem, not a spec gap, and
closing with a pointer is defensible. Declining also avoids
committing the work group to a per-obligation review under ballot
pressure, and avoids the risk of setting EU-wide `SHALL`s that later
have to be relaxed when national profiles disagree.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the most pragmatic response for an IG that is
already in ballot. The reporter's real pain point is that the
expectation level is not *legible* from the notation they were
looking at, and that the European profile's relationship to national
profiles is not spelled out. Both of those are fixable with a
documentation change to the "Notes to Balloters" and the Obligations
page, plus explicit `SHALL` markers on the handful of obligations
the work group actually wants to mandate at EU scope — without
requiring a per-obligation re-review of the entire IG. This also
keeps the door open to evolve toward Disposition A in a later
version once national affiliates have weighed in on which
obligations they would want raised. Disposition C (decline) is
defensible only if the obligations genuinely already carry explicit
levels in the published artefacts; if any obligation is expressed
with an action verb alone, declining would leave the reporter's
concern unaddressed and should be rejected.
