## Summary

The reporter (Josh Priebe) argues that the HL7 Europe *Imaging Study Report
(R5)* IG should standardize on the FHIR `ImagingStudy` resource as the single
encoding for the imaging manifest, rather than supporting (or requiring) the
DICOM Key Object Selection (KOS) binary encoding alongside it. The motivation
is that several anticipated EHDS-era consumers — exchange gateways, patient
apps, image-thumbnail clients, and clinician summary apps — will not be
DICOM-aware and would benefit from a JSON-native manifest. WADO‑RS would
remain available for full-fidelity image retrieval by DICOM workstations.

The ticket explicitly trades off three options: (a) require both encodings on
the producer (consumer picks), (b) allow either (each producer picks), or (c)
require only one. The reporter prefers (c) standardizing on `ImagingStudy`,
but in his 2025-07-01 follow-up indicates he can accept (a) "server produces
both, client picks" as a closing position because it still lets non-DICOM
consumers function.

The closely related ticket [FHIR-51295](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51295)
("Relationship between Manifest and MCWG KOS", reporter Bas van den Heuvel,
status **Applied / Persuasive**) records the MCWG/IHE-RAD decision to split
the manifest into its own specification (`hl7-eu/imaging-manifest`) and to
align the FHIR `ImagingStudy` manifest with the MCWG KOS specification with
a documented bi-directional, lossless mapping. A comment from the IHE side
(JIRAUSER30266) notes the new IHE **MADO** (Manifest-Access to DICOM Objects)
profile defines both encodings and the mappings between them, with an open
issue exactly mirroring the question this ticket asks (creator vs. consumer
which-format-when), to be closed by the MADO Trial Implementation version.

## Details

**Description:**

> Adding a note for a point not directly addressed in this IG. We're aware
> that there have been discussions about leveraging DICOM KOS for the imaging
> manifest rather than use of ImagingStudy resources.
>
> We believe ImagingStudy resources are the better approach. DICOM KOS
> requires that the parties be able to decode a DICOM binary, rather than a
> simpler object in JSON which is widely understood. We expect that there
> will be use-cases where groups will wish to have a non-DICOM compatible
> consumer, such as:
>
> - An exchange gateway (such as that handling other EHDS exchange)
> - A patient application wishing to share images to patient
> - A clinician app that wants to pull in image thumbnails before loading
>   the complete image
> - A clinician app that wants to know an imaging study happened ("patient
>   has had a CT head last year") before loading those images
>
> Choosing ImagingStudy as the single encoding simplifies implementation for
> those cases, while still maintaining support for DICOM workstations via
> WADO-RS fetches.
>
> Requiring manifest providers to support **both** approaches adds burden to
> source systems, and increases the specification work to keep manifest
> encodings in sync.
>
> Allowing **either** reduces the source burden but keeps the spec burden
> and adds burden to consumer systems who need to support both to get a
> complete set of data.

**Comments:**

- **JIRAUSER30266 (2025-07-21):** "This will be addressed by the newly
  proposed Manifest-access to DICOM Objects IHE profile." (i.e., MADO.)
- **JoshPriebe / JIRAUSER28578 (2025-07-01):** Connects to FHIR-51295. He
  is "OK with the decision that the server produces both formats" as a
  closing criterion. Reiterates that "server produces both" puts duplication
  burden on producers, "server produces either" puts it on consumers, and
  maintaining two formats has spec-layer overhead. On balance he endorses
  *"server produces both, client picks"* as the lesser of the two evils, but
  views it as up for debate.
- **JIRAUSER30266 (2025-12-16):** Restates the IHE **MADO** position: MADO
  defines both encodings and bi-directional mappings; an open issue in the
  Public Comment version captures whether (1) the Content Creator supports
  both formats and the consumer selects, or (2) Content Creator and Imaging
  Document Consumer each support one, with the other as an option. To be
  closed by the Trial Implementation MADO version.
- **JoshPriebe / JIRAUSER28578 (2025-12-18):** "I prefer (1)." Server
  supports both, client picks. Argues that under EHDS most new clients won't
  speak DICOM, and that option (2) will fragment the ecosystem because
  actors will each pick a favorite.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the *EU Imaging Study Report (R5)* / `hl7-eu/imaging-manifest` IG to
**require the FHIR `ImagingStudy`-based manifest as the single normative
encoding** for the imaging manifest. The KOS encoding becomes:

- A **non-normative, informative companion** in the IG (retained as a page
  describing the bi-directional KOS↔`ImagingStudy` mapping defined under
  FHIR-51295), and
- An **optional** representation that producers MAY also publish for
  DICOM-native consumers.

Conformance language for *Manifest Producer* would require publishing the
`ImagingStudy` manifest; conformance for *Manifest Consumer* would require
consuming the `ImagingStudy` manifest. WADO‑RS continues to be the binding
mechanism for retrieving the underlying images and would be cited from the
`ImagingStudy.endpoint` / instance-level references. The IHE MADO open
issue (creator/consumer format-support matrix) would be resolved on the
HL7-EU side by always producing `ImagingStudy`.

#### Justification

- Aligns with the reporter's original ask and with FHIR design principles
  for a JSON-native, resource-oriented exchange.
- Removes the dual-format spec maintenance burden flagged by the reporter
  ("maintain two formats adds overhead on the specification layer").
- Enables the four non-DICOM consumer classes the reporter enumerates
  (EHDS gateway, patient app, thumbnail-first clinician app, "did this
  study happen" lookup), all of which are realistic given the EHDS
  trajectory and the Zulip *Documents vs Resources* thread.
- Gotcha to address: this conflicts with the IHE MADO position (MADO
  currently treats KOS and `ImagingStudy` as peer encodings with
  bi-directional mapping). The IG would need to make clear that the MADO
  KOS encoding is supported via the documented mapping but is not the
  HL7-EU-required wire format.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — "Producer publishes both, consumer chooses"

#### Proposal

Mandate that a conformant *Manifest Producer* in the HL7-EU
`imaging-manifest` IG **MUST publish both** the FHIR `ImagingStudy`
manifest and the DICOM KOS manifest (the two being kept consistent via the
lossless bi-directional mapping defined under FHIR-51295). A *Manifest
Consumer* MUST be able to consume **at least one** of the two encodings
(its choice). Capability statements / actor descriptions would declare
which encodings each system emits or accepts; content negotiation
(`Accept` headers / FHIR `_format` / WADO content types) would be the
selection mechanism.

Concretely:

- Add a normative requirement to the manifest producer's CapabilityStatement
  that both encodings are exposed at well-defined endpoints.
- Add an informative section explaining how a consumer should select one.
- Adopt option (1) from the IHE MADO open issue verbatim, eliminating the
  fragmentation risk Josh raised in his 2025-12-18 comment.

#### Justification

- Matches the reporter's *fallback* preference ("server produces both,
  client picks") expressed in both 2025-07-01 and 2025-12-18 comments —
  i.e., even the original requester accepts this as a viable close.
- Aligns HL7-EU with the IHE MADO direction (both encodings, bi-directional
  mapping), preserving interoperability for DICOM-native ecosystems
  (workstations, IHE-RAD profiles) while still letting EHDS gateways and
  patient apps consume the JSON form.
- Trade-off vs. A: shifts the burden from spec-maintainers (who already
  have to maintain the mapping per FHIR-51295 anyway) onto producer
  implementations. Given that the canonical mapping is defined and
  presumably tooled, the marginal producer burden is the cost of a
  serialiser, not of a parallel data model.
- Trade-off vs. C: the producer-both rule prevents the fragmentation
  scenario ("each actor picks a favorite, ecosystem splits") that the
  reporter and Elliot Silver both raised.

---

### Disposition C: Decline — defer to IHE MADO

#### Proposal

Decline to take a position in HL7-EU's *EU Imaging Study Report (R5)* IG on
which encoding is preferred. Instead, the IG references the IHE **MADO**
profile (and the FHIR-51295 KOS↔`ImagingStudy` mapping) and adopts whatever
creator/consumer support matrix MADO finalises in its Trial Implementation
release. Rationale category: *already addressed by an existing /
in-progress mechanism in the appropriate body (IHE-RAD)*.

#### Justification

- Per JIRAUSER30266 (2025-07-21 and 2025-12-16), the *exact* question this
  ticket asks is an explicit open issue in MADO's Public Comment, scheduled
  to be closed by Trial Implementation. Resolving it independently in
  HL7-EU risks divergence from MADO before MADO settles.
- Per FHIR-51295 (Applied/Persuasive), HL7-EU has already accepted that
  the manifest will live in both formats with a bi-directional mapping;
  re-opening "is one preferred?" inside HL7-EU after that decision is
  arguably out of scope for this IG.
- Accepts the cost flagged by the reporter (continued spec/maintenance
  overhead) on the grounds that the MADO mapping investment is already
  committed.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition: B — "Producer publishes both, consumer chooses".**

This is the position that the reporter himself converges on in his most
recent comments (2025-07-01 and 2025-12-18), it is consistent with the
already-Applied direction of FHIR-51295 (split-off manifest IG with a
documented bi-directional KOS↔`ImagingStudy` mapping), and it actively
resolves the open IHE MADO question rather than waiting for MADO's Trial
Implementation. It is the only disposition that simultaneously (i)
guarantees the EHDS / non-DICOM consumer use cases the reporter cares
about, (ii) preserves interoperability with the DICOM-native IHE-RAD
ecosystem, and (iii) avoids the fragmentation failure mode that both Josh
Priebe and Elliot Silver flag in the *Documents vs Resources* Zulip
thread.

Disposition A is cleaner architecturally but is likely to be rejected by
IHE/MCWG given the MADO direction; Disposition C is procedurally safe but
leaves HL7-EU's IG in a "TBD" state at exactly the moment EHDS adopters
need a definite answer. The workgroup should pick B and align the IG's
producer/consumer conformance language accordingly, then communicate the
decision back into the MADO open issue.
