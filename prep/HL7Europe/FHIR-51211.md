## Summary

The ticket, filed against the HL7 Europe **EU Imaging Study Report (R5)**
IG, questions the intent of a "Group 6" mapping entry that purports to map
from the **Imaging Report** logical model to the **IM ImagingStudy
Manifest**. The reporter (Giorgio Cangioli) expects that, because the IG is
in the *report* space, any mapping from the Imaging Report model should
target the *report* bundle (or its components), not the *manifest* bundle
(which belongs to the separate IM ImagingStudy Manifest IG / profile
family). The ticket therefore asks for clarification — is the mapping
correctly authored, mislabelled, or pointing to the wrong target? A follow-up
comment on the ticket notes that of the three published ConceptMaps
(`EHDSImagingReportBodyMap`, `EHDSImagingReportHeaderMap`, and
`EHDSImagingReportMap`), only one has a Group 6, and that Group 6 is *not*
a map to the IM ImagingStudy Manifest, suggesting the reporter may have
been looking at a stale/different rendering or misreading the target of the
group. No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items
were found for this key, so the ticket stands alone and is currently
blocked pending the reporter's clarification (hence the `blocked` label).

## Proposed Dispositions

Because no GitHub repository briefings are relevant, the dispositions below
are framed around the authoring content of the HL7 Europe Imaging IG
(`hl7-eu/imaging`) ballot branch as cited in the ticket comment — the
`EHDSImagingReportMap`, `EHDSImagingReportBodyMap`, and
`EHDSImagingReportHeaderMap` ConceptMap artifacts — but no concrete repo
paths are asserted here because the associated briefing has not been loaded
under this skill's rules.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat the ticket as a defect: audit the three ImagingReport ConceptMaps
(`EHDSImagingReportMap`, `EHDSImagingReportBodyMap`,
`EHDSImagingReportHeaderMap`) in the EU Imaging Study Report (R5) IG for
any `group` whose `source` is the Imaging Report logical model and whose
`target` is the **IM ImagingStudy Manifest** logical model / profile.
Where such a group exists, either (a) re-target it to the appropriate
*report* logical model / bundle (e.g., the Imaging Report bundle), or (b)
move it out of the ImagingReport-sourced ConceptMaps into a ConceptMap
whose source *is* the ImagingStudy Manifest model, so that the source and
target sides are consistent with the IG's report scope. Also review the
narrative/overview pages that describe "Group 6" to ensure the group
numbering and labels match the ConceptMap content the ballot reader will
see.

#### Justification

A mapping whose source is the Imaging *Report* model but whose target is
the ImagingStudy *Manifest* is conceptually inconsistent with an IG that is
scoped to the report. Even if one row happens to be valid (e.g., mapping an
identifier to the manifest reference), grouping it under the report map is
confusing to implementers and ballot readers, which is exactly the
confusion the reporter flagged. Aligning source/target per ConceptMap is
standard FHIR ConceptMap hygiene and avoids the need to continuously
explain the inconsistency.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the current ConceptMap structure and Group 6 content, but address the
ambiguity **editorially**: (1) add an explicit explanatory note at the top
of each ConceptMap and in the IG narrative describing what each `group`
represents, why Group 6 targets what it targets, and how the report and
manifest concerns relate in the IG; and (2) rename/relabel Group 6 in the
ConceptMap(s) so its `name`/`title` clearly states the target (e.g.,
"Imaging Report → Manifest references for ImagingStudy"). Also update the
reporter-facing ballot material to link directly to the authoritative
ConceptMap rendering so future readers are not pointed at a stale version
(the commenter observed that only one of the three maps has a Group 6, and
its target is *not* the IM ImagingStudy Manifest, suggesting a stale URL or
reader misattribution drove the original question).

#### Justification

If, as the commenter indicates, Group 6 does **not** actually map to the IM
ImagingStudy Manifest, then the real defect is likely documentation /
readability rather than an incorrect mapping. Editorial clarification is
cheaper than re-architecting ConceptMap groupings, carries no model risk,
and directly answers the reporter's confusion. It also avoids creating new
ConceptMaps at ballot, which is a larger change with downstream tooling and
translation impact.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Not An Issue / Persuasive** once the reporter
confirms the specific Group 6 they were looking at. The follow-up comment
already notes that only one of the ImagingReport ConceptMaps has a Group 6
and that its target is not the IM ImagingStudy Manifest, so the premise of
the ticket ("mapping from Imaging report model to IM ImagingStudy
Manifest") appears to be based on a misread. Resolve with a comment
explaining the actual source/target of each group and close without a spec
change.

#### Justification

The `blocked` label and the unanswered 2025-07-09 comment indicate the
reporter has not supplied the pinpoint reference needed to substantiate
the concern. If the concern cannot be reproduced against the current ballot
ConceptMaps, changing the IG would be speculative and risks introducing
regressions in artifacts that other EU projects already reference. Closing
with a clear explanation preserves the current (apparently correct) design
and unblocks the ballot track.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the pragmatic middle path. The commenter's reply
effectively disputes the literal premise of the ticket (nothing in the
ImagingReport maps currently targets the IM ImagingStudy Manifest in the
way the reporter describes), which argues against a structural change
(Disposition A). At the same time, the reporter is a senior HL7 Europe
contributor and the fact that an experienced reader arrived at this
interpretation is itself evidence that the ConceptMaps' group labelling
and/or the surrounding IG narrative is not self-explanatory — so simply
declining (Disposition C) loses that signal. Adding explicit group
titles/notes and tightening the narrative resolves the confusion, protects
the ballot timeline, and leaves the existing (validated) mapping content
in place. If, after the reporter re-confirms against the clarified
material, a genuine source/target mismatch is identified, the workgroup
can reopen as a focused defect under Disposition A without having churned
the ConceptMap structure pre-emptively.
