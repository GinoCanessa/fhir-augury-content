## Summary

The reporter asks that the EU Imaging Study Report (R5) implementation
guide add an explicit cross-reference to the HL7 FHIR Clinical Documents
IG (v1.0.0, `https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/fhir-clinical-document/`).
The Imaging Study Report IG defines the layout and contents of what is,
in effect, a clinical document, but currently does not point readers to
the canonical FHIR guidance on how such documents are structured, signed,
versioned, and managed over their lifecycle. The reporter acknowledges
that fully adopting the clinical-document lifecycle is not yet in scope
for the XtEHR requirements driving this IG, but argues that at minimum
the IG should direct implementers to the Clinical Documents IG for
background and conformance guidance on document dynamics. No Jira links
or cross-references from other sources (Zulip, GitHub) were found by
FHIR Augury for this ticket.

## Details

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the EU Imaging Study Report (R5) IG narrative to add a
prominent informative reference to the FHIR Clinical Documents IG.
Concretely:

- Add a short "Relationship to FHIR Clinical Documents" subsection to
  the IG's home/background page (or to the page that describes the
  report Composition/Bundle), linking to
  `https://hl7.org/fhir/uv/clinical-document/` (published) and noting
  the current build at
  `https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/fhir-clinical-document/`.
- In the page(s) that describe the imaging report `Composition` and
  document `Bundle`, add a "See also" callout pointing at the Clinical
  Documents IG sections on document structure, attestation, and
  lifecycle.
- Leave all profiles, value sets, and conformance rules unchanged —
  this disposition is narrative-only.

#### Justification

The request is narrative, not normative: it asks the IG to point
readers at existing HL7 guidance that already governs FHIR document
dynamics. Accepting as-requested is low-risk, improves implementer
orientation, and aligns the EU Imaging Study Report with the broader
HL7 document ecosystem without enlarging the IG's scope. It also
respects the reporter's own caveat that the XtEHR requirements do not
(yet) mandate full clinical-document conformance.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the intent of the request, but go beyond a narrative pointer by
explicitly aligning the Imaging Study Report `Composition` profile
with the FHIR Clinical Documents IG where feasible:

- Profile the report `Composition` to derive from (or be consistent
  with) the base Clinical Documents `Composition` expectations
  (e.g., `status`, `type`, `subject`, `author`, `attester`, `date`,
  `section.text` requirements).
- Align the document `Bundle` assembly rules (`Bundle.type = document`,
  first entry = `Composition`, signature/attestation handling) with
  the Clinical Documents IG, citing it as the normative source for
  document mechanics.
- Retain the requested narrative cross-reference as in Disposition A,
  but frame it as "this IG conforms to the FHIR Clinical Documents IG
  for document dynamics" rather than "see also".

#### Justification

A pure narrative link is easy to miss and does not actually constrain
implementations. Anchoring the imaging report to Clinical Documents at
the profile level gives implementers a clearer conformance target and
reduces the chance of divergent document-lifecycle handling across EU
IGs. The trade-off is scope: this enlarges the change beyond what the
reporter literally asked for and may surface mismatches with XtEHR
requirements that would need to be negotiated — the reporter
themselves flagged that full conformance is "not within the scope of
the XtEHR requirements (yet)", which argues for doing this carefully
rather than not at all.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as "not persuasive" (or equivalent) on the grounds
that the request is a non-actionable suggestion to add an informative
link, without a concrete conformance problem or implementer blocker
being described. Optionally note that the WG will consider Clinical
Documents alignment as part of a future major revision when XtEHR
requirements explicitly cover document lifecycle.

#### Justification

Defensible only if the WG judges that (a) the IG's current narrative
already implicitly treats the report as a FHIR document and (b)
adding a cross-reference is editorial overhead without clear benefit.
This is a weak rationale in this case — the reporter has identified a
real documentation gap and offered a trivially-actionable fix — so
declining should be a last resort.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A delivers exactly what the reporter asked for, at minimal
cost and risk, and is consistent with the reporter's own scoping caveat
that XtEHR requirements do not yet mandate full clinical-document
conformance. Disposition B is attractive in the longer term but is
materially larger in scope and would likely need XtEHR alignment
before it can be adopted; it is better treated as a follow-up ticket
once the profile impact has been assessed. Disposition C is hard to
justify given the low cost of the requested change and the clear value
to implementers of a pointer to the canonical document-dynamics IG.
The recommendation is therefore to accept as requested and, separately,
open a follow-up to evaluate deeper Clinical Documents alignment when
scope allows.
