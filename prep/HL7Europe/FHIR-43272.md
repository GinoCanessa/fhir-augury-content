## Summary

Ulrike Merrick (the reporter) is asking why the `Bundle.entry:serviceRequest`
slice in the EU Laboratory Report `Bundle-eu-lab` profile has a cardinality of
`0..*`. The implicit question is whether a Laboratory Report can validly be
exchanged without any `ServiceRequest` resource present in the bundle. A
follow-up comment from `rikim` extends the same question to
`DiagnosticReport.basedOn` on the EU Lab `DiagnosticReport-eu-lab` profile,
which is the typed back-reference to `ServiceRequest`. No related Jira tickets
were surfaced by cross-references, but a recent (Feb 2026) `#europe` Zulip
thread on the EU Lab `DiagnosticReport` discusses the underlying design
philosophy and is relevant context for any disposition.

## Details

**Description:**
Why is cardinality for
[Bundle.entry:serviceRequest](https://hl7.eu/fhir/laboratory/StructureDefinition-Bundle-eu-lab-definitions.html#Bundle.entry:serviceRequest)
0..* — can you have a Lab report without a serviceRequest?

## Related Zulip Discussions

### [europe > EU Lab DiagnosticReport - mandatory composition](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/europe/topic/EU%20Lab%20DiagnosticReport%20-%20mandatory%20composition/near/574988557)

This Feb 2026 thread does not directly reference FHIR-43272 but discusses the
same EU Laboratory Report profiles and the design philosophy that drives the
ServiceRequest cardinalities. Kevin Mayfield reports practical issues
implementing `DiagnosticReport-eu-lab` from a non-document-based LIMS world
(no `Composition`, no FHIR Document). Giorgio Cangioli (the IG editor)
explains the EU design choice — a Laboratory Report is always exchanged as a
single FHIR Document with exactly one `DiagnosticReport`, contrasting with
the US/UV approach where a report can map to multiple `DiagnosticReport`
resources, each typically tied to one `ServiceRequest`. Ralf Herzog
summarises the two philosophies. The discussion confirms that the EU IG was
intentionally designed to accommodate workflows where the originating order
may be absent or not represented as a `ServiceRequest` resource (e.g., paper
or out-of-band orders, reflex/sub-contracted orders, screening), which is
exactly what motivates a `0..*` cardinality on the `serviceRequest` slice.

Participants: Kevin Mayfield, Giorgio Cangioli, John Moehrke, Ralf Herzog.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested (Confirm cardinality, add clarifying note)

#### Proposal

Keep `Bundle.entry:serviceRequest` at `0..*` and `DiagnosticReport.basedOn`
at `0..*` in the EU Laboratory Report IG, and add a short narrative note to
both `Bundle-eu-lab` and `DiagnosticReport-eu-lab` (in the
`StructureDefinition` element `short`/`comment` and in the IG narrative
"design choice" page) explicitly stating: a `ServiceRequest` is **not
required** in the Laboratory Report Bundle because the originating order
may be (a) unknown to the lab system at report-time, (b) not represented as
a `ServiceRequest` (e.g., paper or out-of-band order), or (c) represented
only by an identifier carried elsewhere (e.g., `DiagnosticReport.identifier`
/ requisition). When a `ServiceRequest` is included, `DiagnosticReport.basedOn`
SHOULD reference it. Close the ticket as **Persuasive** with the answer
recorded as a note on the IG page.

#### Justification

The `0..*` cardinality is intentional and consistent with the EU design
choice articulated by the IG editor on the `#europe` Zulip thread:
the EU Laboratory Report is centred on a single `DiagnosticReport` exchanged
as a FHIR Document, and orders are deliberately kept optional to support
real-world workflows where no `ServiceRequest` resource exists. The reporter
asked a clarifying *Question* (not a defect), and the IG already supports
the requested behaviour — the gap is documentation, not modelling. Adding a
short clarifying note resolves the confusion without introducing a breaking
profile change.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach (Tighten cardinality and add invariant)

#### Proposal

Change `Bundle.entry:serviceRequest` to `0..1` (or `0..*` with a slicing
discriminator that limits practical multiplicity) and tighten
`DiagnosticReport.basedOn` to `0..1` for `Bundle-eu-lab` /
`DiagnosticReport-eu-lab`. Add an invariant on `DiagnosticReport-eu-lab`
that REQUIRES `basedOn` to resolve to a `ServiceRequest` entry in the same
Bundle whenever any `ServiceRequest` entry exists (`basedOn.exists()
implies Bundle.entry.where(resource is ServiceRequest).exists()`). This
codifies the typical EU pattern of "one report → at most one order" while
still permitting order-less reports.

#### Justification

The Zulip thread shows that in the EU model a `DiagnosticReport` is
deliberately *one* holistic resource per report, which strongly implies a
1:1 (or 1:0) relationship to `ServiceRequest` rather than 1:many. Tightening
to `0..1` would surface modelling errors at validation time and prevent
implementers from accidentally producing reports that bundle multiple
unrelated orders into a single `DiagnosticReport`. Trade-off: this is a
breaking change for any profile/instance currently relying on `0..*`, and
it pre-empts edge cases like reflex testing (raised by Kevin Mayfield in
the same Zulip thread) where one report may legitimately span several
originating orders.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (No change, no narrative update)

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Not Persuasive** / **Question — Answered** with a
one-line response on the ticket pointing the reporter at the IG's existing
"design choice" narrative page
(`https://hl7.eu/fhir/laboratory/design-choice.html`) and noting that the
slice cardinality is correct as published.

#### Justification

The ticket carries the `no-ballot` label and is typed as a `Question`. The
existing IG narrative already explains the document-centric design.
Declining is defensible if the workgroup judges that no IG change — even
documentation — is warranted, and that pointing to existing material is
sufficient. The downside is that the same question has surfaced again in
2026 on Zulip, suggesting the existing narrative is not discoverable from
the `StructureDefinition` pages where readers actually land.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A is the right balance. The reporter's underlying assumption —
that the cardinality is correct and intentional — is true, so a modelling
change (Disposition B) would be disproportionate and would conflict with
known EU workflow requirements (reflex testing, out-of-band orders) raised
in the Zulip discussion. Pure decline (Disposition C) leaves the
documentation gap that produced both this ticket and the recurring Zulip
question, costing editor time on every repeat. A short clarifying note in
both the `Bundle-eu-lab` and `DiagnosticReport-eu-lab` profiles, plus a
back-link from the slice definitions to the IG's "design choice" page,
costs almost nothing, preserves backward compatibility, and directly
addresses both the literal question asked and `rikim`'s follow-up about
`DiagnosticReport.basedOn`.
