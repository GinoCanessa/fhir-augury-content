## Summary

The reporter (HynekKruzik) raises a concern that the Xt-EHR Header
Authorship pattern adopted by the EU Imaging Study Report (R5) IG is the
only mandatory carrier of information about the *producer* of the
imaging report. Because `Authorship` is `1..*` of `EHDSAuthor` and the
`Organization` element on `EHDSAuthor` is optional, the model currently
admits an arbitrary mix of authors and zero-or-more organizations. The
reporter argues that this leaves the *releasing* healthcare-provider
organization under-identified and that it should always equal the
organization responsible for the corresponding `Encounter`. He
recommends (1) adjusting the cardinality of the `Encounter` resource so
it is always present, (2) adding an explicit mapping from
`header.authorship` to `Encounter.organization` (i.e.
`Encounter.serviceProvider`), (3) attaching a consumer Obligation to
display the encounter organization, and (4) adding rendering guidance
for the report.

The first triage comment asks whether the recently-applied related ticket
[FHIR-51393](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51393) ("DiagnosticReport.Performer
is not sufficiently prescriptive", *Persuasive with Modification, Applied*)
already addresses the concern by constraining
`DiagnosticReport.performer[author]` and `Composition.author[organization]`
to reference an `Organization`. JoshPriebe's earlier comment supports
using `DiagnosticReport.performer` (constrained to `Organization`, as IHE
IDR did) as the authoritative source of the *issuing* organization, and
notes that while `Encounter.serviceProvider` is *often* the same
organization, independent reading services, imaging centres, and
inpatient/outpatient practices break that assumption — and that an
outside reader may not even be given the acquisition `Encounter`.

## Details

**Description:**

> Xt-EHR Header Authorship is the only mandatory element containing
> information about producer of the imaging report. Cardinality of this
> element is 1..\* and includes mandatory author of type EHDSAuthor.
> EHDSAuthor includes optional Organization element.
>
> This model actually allows inclusion of one or more authors from zero
> to more organizations and the IG follows this model. This will
> represent a potential problem with identification of a healthcare
> provider organization which is actualy releasing a report. And we
> should resolve this problem according to my opinion.
>
> In fact, the organization which released the report should be equal
> to the organization responsible for the respective encounter. If we
> will agree on this, then we should ensure that encounter information
> will be always present and contains information about the
> organization.
>
> My recommendation is to:
> 1. Adjust cardinality of encounter resource
> 2. Add mapping from header.authorship to the encounter.organization
> 3. Add Obligation for consumer to display encounter organization information
> 4. Provide guidance for displaying of the report

**Comments:**

- **JoshPriebe — 2025-10-23:** The definition of
  `DiagnosticReport.performer` ("The diagnostic service that is
  responsible for issuing the report.") is well suited to identifying
  the issuing organization, and IHE IDR constrained it to reference an
  `Organization` for exactly this purpose. While the `Organization`
  referenced in `Encounter.serviceProvider` for the imaging-acquisition
  encounter will *commonly* be the same organization, independent
  radiology reporting services, imaging centres, and
  inpatient/outpatient practices can produce combinations where the two
  differ. In scenarios where the study is read/reported by an outside
  organization, that outside reader might not be provided with the
  acquisition `Encounter` at all, making `Encounter` a less reliable
  source.

- **Triage (JIRAUSER28670) — 2025-11-04:** Points the reporter at
  [FHIR-51393](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51393), which has
  already been voted and applied, and asks whether that resolution
  covers this ticket. Quick summary: `DiagnosticReport.performer[author]`
  and `Composition.author[organization]` are meant to reference the
  producer of the imaging report, and only allow an `Organization`
  resource type as a reference.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the EU Imaging Study Report (R5) IG, apply all four changes the
reporter proposes:

1. Raise the cardinality of `Encounter` (the `EHDSImagingReportHeader →
   Encounter` mapping) so that an `Encounter` reference is *always*
   present in the `Composition` header (i.e. `1..1` for the
   imaging-acquisition encounter, with a profile constraint that
   `Encounter.serviceProvider` MUST be populated and reference an
   `Organization`).
2. Add an explicit mapping in the EHDS-to-FHIR header logical map from
   `header.authorship` to `Encounter.serviceProvider` (the FHIR
   equivalent of "Encounter.organization").
3. Attach a consumer-side `Obligation` (e.g. an `obligation` extension
   on the `Composition` profile or on the imaging-report bundle profile)
   that requires consumers to *display* the `Encounter.serviceProvider`
   organization alongside the report.
4. Add narrative rendering guidance to the IG describing how the
   encounter organization is to be presented in the rendered report.

#### Justification

The reporter's underlying concern is real: under EHDS the only
mandatory carrier of "who released this report" is `Authorship`, whose
`Organization` slot is optional, so a conformant instance can omit the
releasing organization entirely. Forcing `Encounter` and its
`serviceProvider` to be present, and binding it explicitly to the
authorship mapping, removes that ambiguity at the structural level
without inventing a new element. Adding an Obligation and rendering
guidance is the standard way EU IGs (and Xt-EHR-aligned IGs in general)
project a structural requirement into displayed content. The proposed
change stays inside the `EHDSImagingReportHeader` mapping and the
imaging-report `Composition` profile, so the blast radius is contained
to this IG.

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach (recommended starting point)

#### Proposal

Treat the *issuing organization* concern as already addressed by
[FHIR-51393](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51393) at the FHIR level
(`DiagnosticReport.performer[author]` and `Composition.author[organization]`
both constrained to `Organization`), and resolve FHIR-51733 with a
narrower, two-part change:

1. Update the IG narrative and the `EHDSImagingReportHeader` mapping
   notes to state that the *issuing* organization is conveyed via
   `Composition.author[organization]` / `DiagnosticReport.performer[author]`
   (per the FHIR-51393 resolution), and that
   `Encounter.serviceProvider` conveys the *acquisition-encounter*
   organization, which is **not** required to be the same.
2. Make `Encounter` (and its `serviceProvider`) `0..1` rather than
   mandatory, but add an Obligation that *if* an `Encounter` is
   referenced its `serviceProvider` MUST be populated, and add
   rendering guidance that consumers SHOULD display
   `Composition.author[organization]` as the issuing organization and,
   when present, `Encounter.serviceProvider` as the acquisition
   organization.

#### Justification

JoshPriebe's comment makes the core counter-argument: the issuing
organization and the acquisition-encounter organization are commonly
the same but legitimately differ (independent reading services,
teleradiology, imaging-centre + outside radiologist, etc.), and an
outside reader may not even be given the acquisition `Encounter`.
Forcing `Encounter` to `1..*` (Disposition A's first bullet) would break
those legitimate scenarios and conflate two distinct concepts. FHIR-51393
already supplies the structural answer for "who released the report";
this disposition reuses that resolution and limits FHIR-51733 to
clarifying the *roles* and tightening `Encounter.serviceProvider` only
when an `Encounter` is actually conveyed. It also keeps the EU Imaging
Study Report consistent with the resolution of the analogous lab ticket
[FHIR-52978](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-52978).

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-51733 as **Not Persuasive – Already Addressed**, on the
grounds that the recently-applied FHIR-51393 already constrains
`DiagnosticReport.performer[author]` and
`Composition.author[organization]` to `Organization`, which is the
canonical FHIR mechanism for identifying the producer/issuing
organization of an imaging report. Add a short pointer in the IG's
header-mapping section to FHIR-51393's resolution and take no further
structural action.

#### Justification

If the workgroup accepts the triage comment's framing — that
"identification of the releasing organization" is exactly what
FHIR-51393's applied change accomplishes — then FHIR-51733 is
duplicative at the structural level. The remaining concerns (display
obligations, rendering guidance) can be folded into the existing
header-mapping review (FHIR-51215) without re-opening cardinality
debates. Declining avoids introducing a 1..\* `Encounter` requirement
that would conflict with valid teleradiology/imaging-centre flows
flagged in JoshPriebe's comment.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition: B (Alternative Approach).**

Disposition C under-serves the reporter: FHIR-51393 fixes *what
element* identifies the issuing organization, but it does not address
the reporter's secondary asks (Obligation to display, rendering
guidance, and clarity around the encounter organization). Disposition A
overshoots by forcing `Encounter` to mandatory, which the substantive
comment from JoshPriebe explicitly warns against — outside readers may
have no acquisition `Encounter` available, and conflating
"issuing organization" with `Encounter.serviceProvider` is incorrect
for independent reading services. Disposition B accepts FHIR-51393 as
the structural answer for the *issuing* organization, separately
strengthens `Encounter.serviceProvider` only when an `Encounter` is
actually present, and delivers the requested Obligation and rendering
guidance. It also keeps the EU Imaging Study Report aligned with the
resolution path of the analogous EU Laboratory Report ticket
(FHIR-52978), which is desirable for cross-IG consistency under
HL7 Europe.
