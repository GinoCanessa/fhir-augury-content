### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat this as an editorial Technical Correction and remove the fourth
bullet from the "Support" section of the International Birth And Child
Model (FHIR) IG identified by the reporter. The change would be
narrative-only (no StructureDefinition, ValueSet, or conformance
artifact changes) and would be applied to the relevant Markdown / XML
narrative source file in the IG's authoring root, then republished. The
removed bullet should be quoted verbatim in the resolution comment so
the change is traceable.

#### Justification

The ticket is classified as a Technical Correction with Medium priority
and no comments contesting it, which is consistent with a small
editorial fix. Removing a bullet that the reporter (a Patient Care
contributor) judges unnecessary is low-risk and reversible: it touches
only narrative, does not change any computable conformance, and cannot
break implementations. Accepting respects the reporter's domain
judgement absent any opposing voices in Jira or Zulip.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than delete the bullet outright, **rephrase or relocate** it.
Specifically: (1) ask the reporter for the exact bullet text and the
reason it is "not needed"; (2) if the bullet is redundant with another
bullet, merge the two; (3) if the bullet is misplaced, move it to a
more appropriate "Background" or "Scope" section of the same IG page;
(4) if the bullet is genuinely out-of-scope, then delete it but record
the rationale in a `<!-- removed because ... -->` comment in the source
so future editors do not re-introduce it.

#### Justification

A bare "do not need this bullet" title is ambiguous — the bullet may be
unnecessary in the *Support* section but still useful elsewhere in the
IG, or it may be redundant rather than wrong. A refine-rather-than-delete
approach preserves any latent intent of the original author and makes
the edit auditable, which is helpful for narrative IGs that are
reviewed by multiple workgroups. The cost is one short clarifying
exchange with the reporter before the edit lands.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the ticket as **Not Persuasive — insufficient information**.
The ticket has no description, no comments, no cross-references, and
no Zulip discussion identifying which page or which bullet is at
issue. Close the ticket and invite the reporter to re-file with: (a)
the exact IG page URL, (b) the exact bullet text, and (c) the reason
the bullet is unnecessary.

#### Justification

Patient Care policy for Technical Corrections expects enough context
that a reviewer can act without guesswork. Acting on a one-line title
risks deleting the wrong bullet or a bullet that other stakeholders
rely on. Declining with a request for more detail is preferable to
silently re-interpreting the ask, especially in an IG ("International
Birth And Child Model") where the audience spans multiple jurisdictions
and the editorial bar is correspondingly higher.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The ticket is plausibly a routine editorial fix, so an outright
**Decline** (Disposition C) feels heavy-handed for a Medium-priority
Technical Correction from a known Patient Care reporter. At the same
time, **Accept As Requested** (Disposition A) requires the workgroup to
guess at which bullet to delete because the ticket payload contains no
description text and no cross-references — a guess that is hard to
defend in an international IG. **Disposition B** captures the right
middle path: confirm the bullet with the reporter, then either remove,
merge, or relocate it depending on the actual content. This keeps the
ticket moving, respects the reporter's judgement, and avoids an
unsupervised narrative deletion. If the reporter does not respond to a
clarifying request within the workgroup's normal stale-ticket window,
the disposition can be downgraded to C.
