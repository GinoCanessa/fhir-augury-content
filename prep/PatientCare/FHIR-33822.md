## Summary

Ron G. Parker is asking that the canonical URL for the `observable-entities`
ValueSet shipped with the Ophthalmology Retinal FHIR IG be re-rooted under
`http://terminology.hl7.org` (THO) instead of `http://hl7.org/fhir/...`. The
ticket also asks that a companion UTG ticket be opened to register this
ValueSet in THO so the new canonical URL is properly governed and resolvable.
No cross-referenced Jira tickets, GitHub items, or Confluence pages were
returned by the orchestrator for this issue, but the underlying ValueSet is
the one referenced in the Eye Care Zulip thread (see Related Zulip
Discussions) where IG validators flag codes against
`http://hl7.org/fhir/uv/ophthalmology/ValueSet/observable-entities`.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the Ophthalmology Retinal IG (`HL7/fhir-eyecare-ig`):

1. Change the canonical URL of the `observable-entities` ValueSet from
   `http://hl7.org/fhir/uv/ophthalmology/ValueSet/observable-entities`
   to `http://terminology.hl7.org/ValueSet/observable-entities` (or
   another agreed THO-rooted URL such as
   `http://terminology.hl7.org/ValueSet/v3-eyecare-observable-entities`
   if Patient Care / Vocabulary prefer a namespaced slug).
2. Update every binding inside the IG (Observation profiles, examples,
   narrative, search-parameter docs, etc.) that currently references the
   old `hl7.org/fhir/uv/ophthalmology/ValueSet/observable-entities`
   canonical to the new THO canonical.
3. Open a companion UTG ticket against the `HL7/UTG` repository to
   register the ValueSet (definition, intensional/extensional content,
   metadata, ownership = Patient Care WG) under the new canonical URL,
   so it is published as part of the next THO release.
4. Coordinate the publication order so the THO definition exists at the
   new canonical before the next Eye Care IG publication that uses it.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter requested and is consistent with HL7's
long-standing direction of moving ValueSets that are reusable across
specifications out of individual IG namespaces and into THO. Re-rooting
under `terminology.hl7.org` gives the ValueSet a stable, IG-independent
governance home, allows other IGs (US Eye Care, AU eye care, IPS, etc.)
to bind to the same canonical without taking a dependency on the
Ophthalmology Retinal IG, and makes the ValueSet resolvable through the
standard tx.fhir.org / THO publishing chain that validators already
trust (the Eye Care Zulip thread shows the validator currently hitting
the IG-scoped URL — moving to THO removes that IG-bound resolution
path).

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep an Ophthalmology-Retinal-rooted canonical for the IG-scoped binding
but publish a parallel, normative copy in THO and cross-link them:

1. Author the canonical `observable-entities` definition in `HL7/UTG`
   under a THO URL (e.g.
   `http://terminology.hl7.org/ValueSet/observable-entities`).
2. In `HL7/fhir-eyecare-ig`, retain
   `http://hl7.org/fhir/uv/ophthalmology/ValueSet/observable-entities`
   as a thin wrapper ValueSet whose `compose.include[0].valueSet` points
   to the THO canonical.
3. Mark the IG-rooted ValueSet as `experimental = false`,
   `status = retired` once consumers have migrated, and document the
   THO URL as the preferred binding target in the IG narrative.

#### Justification

This honours the spirit of the ticket (THO becomes the source of truth
and the new governance home) without breaking any existing implementer
that has already hard-coded the
`hl7.org/fhir/uv/ophthalmology/ValueSet/observable-entities` URL in
profiles, server configurations, validator caches, or downstream IGs.
Canonical URLs are identifiers, and silently changing one is a breaking
change for conformance resources that bind to it; keeping the old URL
as a delegating wrapper preserves that identifier while still moving
the substance of the definition into THO. Trade-off vs. A: more moving
parts and two artefacts to maintain, but no external breakage and a
softer migration path.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Leave the ValueSet's canonical URL where it is
(`http://hl7.org/fhir/uv/ophthalmology/ValueSet/observable-entities`)
on the grounds that (a) the ValueSet is currently only used by the
Ophthalmology Retinal IG, (b) the Eye Care Zulip discussion shows the
validator already resolves the existing URL, and (c) re-rooting and
re-registering in THO is a non-trivial UTG/Vocabulary effort that the
ticket alone does not justify with concrete cross-IG reuse cases.

#### Justification

If no other specification has expressed a need to bind to this
ValueSet, moving it to THO is largely a governance preference rather
than an interoperability requirement, and any URL change — even a
"clean" one done at the right semver boundary — is a breaking change
for implementers who already validate or persist against the
IG-rooted canonical. Declining keeps the existing implementer surface
stable and defers the THO migration until there is a documented
multi-IG consumer that needs it.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B (Alternative Approach)

Disposition B captures the reporter's underlying goal — get the
authoritative definition into THO, under terminology governance, with a
`terminology.hl7.org` canonical URL — while protecting any implementer
or downstream IG that already binds to the Ophthalmology-rooted URL by
keeping it as a thin delegating wrapper. Disposition A is
philosophically cleanest and most aligned with where HL7 wants reusable
ValueSets to live, but a hard URL change on a published IG ValueSet is
a breaking change for conformance artefacts that should not be done
silently, especially given that the ValueSet has been live long enough
for the Eye Care community to be validating against it (per the Zulip
thread). Disposition C ignores the legitimate THO governance argument
the reporter raises and leaves the ValueSet stranded in an
IG-namespace where Vocabulary cannot easily curate it. B threads the
needle: it accepts the THO-rooted canonical and the UTG registration
the reporter asks for, while giving Patient Care and Vocabulary a
defensible deprecation path for the existing IG-rooted URL.
