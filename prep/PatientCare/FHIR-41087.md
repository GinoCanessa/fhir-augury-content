## Summary

FHIR-41087 asks the US Physical Activity (FHIR) IG to strengthen its
guidance on the *higher-level grouping observation* that ties together
the two "key questions" of the PA Vital Sign (days per week of
moderate-to-vigorous activity, and minutes per day). The IG currently
states that it "does not require the capture or sharing [of the]
higher-level grouping observation that links the answers to the two key
questions." The submitter argues that the answer to the second question
is not meaningful without knowing how many of "those days" it applies to,
and recommends replacing the phrase **"does not require"** with
**"recommends"**. The submitter also notes that the existing
"additional measures" guidance already addresses the combined
calories/steps grouping, so the two-question grouping is the remaining
gap.

The ticket sits in the broader debate captured by two cross-cutting
related tickets:

- [FHIR-41145](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-41145) — Robert
  Dieterle's request that grouping observations be required wherever
  multiple measurements form a single LOINC panel. Resolved
  *Not Persuasive*; the IG will not mandate a grouping for the
  time-based measures (steps/day, calories/day).
- [FHIR-40807](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-40807) — Matt Wisby
  (Epic) raised the same gap from an SDOH ingestion perspective:
  without a parent assessment grouping the two answers on the same
  date, ingestion has to pair them up by date heuristics. Resolved
  *Not Persuasive*, with the rationale that the PA profiles are
  intentionally aligned with Gravity SDOH at the individual
  Observation level and a grouping observation was deemed
  "not essential."

The original disposition on FHIR-41087 (Considered for Future Use,
6-0-1) was **reverted post-STU publication** by Lynn Laakso so the
work group can revisit whether at least a *recommendation* (not a
requirement) is warranted, in light of implementer feedback.

### [FHIR-41145: Need Grouping Observation](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-41145)

Robert Dieterle asked that, when multiple measurements are part of a

*Not Persuasive*** by Patient Care: the PA IG already expects a

referenced FHIR-41087 directly.

### [FHIR-40807: Physical Activity requiring different handling than SDOH assessments will make ingestion of data more complex](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-40807)

Matt Wisby (Epic) flagged that without a parent assessment grouping
the two PA Vital Sign answers, downstream SDOH ingestion has to pair
the answers up by date, which is fragile. **Resolved *Not
Persuasive*** with a long resolution explaining alignment with the
Gravity SDOH IG at the individual Observation level and concluding
that "for physical activity, having a grouping observation is not
essential and that requiring the use of one would add complexity to
implementers that is unnecessary. We can always revisit this question
in the future if there proves to be a new use-case where standardized
grouping would be helpful." That "future revisit" is exactly what
FHIR-41087 is now asking for, in the softer "recommends" form.

Reporter: Matt Wisby. Approver: Lloyd McKenzie.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Edit the *Physical Activity Measures* narrative page in the
US Physical Activity IG (the page that today reads
"this guide does not require the capture or sharing higher-level
grouping observations that link the answers to the two key
questions") so that:

- The phrase **"does not require"** is replaced with **"recommends"**
  for the higher-level grouping observation that links the two key
  questions (LOINC 89574-8 panel — days per week of
  moderate-to-vigorous activity + minutes per day).
- A short normative note clarifies that the recommendation is
  *non-mandatory*: senders SHOULD send a grouping Observation that
  uses `Observation.hasMember` to point at the two child Observations
  and bears the panel LOINC code; receivers SHALL accept the data
  whether or not the grouping is present.
- The paragraph keeps its current acknowledgement that "additional
  measures" already addresses the steps/calories combination, so the
  two paths (panel-of-two vs. activity-based grouping) are
  consistent.

No profile changes are needed; this is a narrative-only change. The
target file is the `measures.md`/`measures.xml` page under the IG's
authoring root in `HL7/physical-activity`.

#### Justification

This is the lightest-touch change that responds to the submitter and
to the FHIR-40807 implementer feedback without re-litigating the
"Not Persuasive" decisions on FHIR-41145 and FHIR-40807. Those
tickets pushed for *requiring* a grouping Observation and were
declined; FHIR-41087 explicitly asks only for "recommends." A
recommendation aligns the narrative with the pattern Lloyd McKenzie
is already pointing implementers at on Zulip
("you could create a grouping Observation linking to the children
via hasMember"), and removes the friction Epic flagged on FHIR-40807
without imposing a SHALL on the IG's existing implementer base.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Defer the narrative change until the LOINC panel code question
(raised in the work group's prior disposition on this ticket) is
settled, then produce a **single coordinated edit** that:

- Adopts a standard LOINC code for the *full collection of four
  concepts* (days/week, minutes/day, steps/day, calories/day),
  per the previous Patient Care decision to "seek a standard code
  from LOINC to represent the organization of this collection."
- Documents *one* grouping pattern in the IG: a panel Observation
  whose `code` is the new LOINC panel and whose `hasMember` points
  at whichever of the four child Observations are present.
- Re-words the "two key questions" paragraph to *recommend* this
  grouping for any combination of the four — including the
  two-question subset the submitter is concerned about — rather
  than carving out a separate two-question grouping.

In implementation terms, this still touches the IG narrative page,
but it also adds a new value set / code to the IG's terminology
artefacts and updates any profile guidance that references panel
LOINC codes.

#### Justification

The 2023 disposition on this ticket explicitly said a two-element
grouping "does not include the other two" and therefore "will [not]
meet the IG objectives." Disposition A makes the narrative wording
softer but leaves that architectural concern unresolved. Disposition
B addresses both: it gives implementers the recommendation they want
*and* it preserves the work group's preference for a single
four-concept grouping. The cost is that it depends on a LOINC
submission timeline, so the change cannot land in the next minor
update without forerunner work.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Re-affirm the prior "Not Persuasive" line of dispositions on
FHIR-41145 and FHIR-40807 and close FHIR-41087 with no change to
the IG narrative. Rationale category: *already addressed by
existing mechanism* — the IG already permits a grouping Observation
(it does not prohibit it), the "additional measures" guidance
already covers the activity-based grouping, and implementers who
want the panel pattern can use it today via `Observation.hasMember`
without an IG change. Note in the resolution that this remains a
candidate for a future major release once a four-concept LOINC
panel code exists.

#### Justification

Two adjacent tickets (FHIR-41145, FHIR-40807) were resolved
*Not Persuasive* on substantively the same point within weeks of
this ticket, with explicit reasoning that a grouping Observation
"is not essential" for time-based PA measures. Reversing course
purely on the strength of a wording change ("does not require"
→ "recommends") risks looking inconsistent without a corresponding
LOINC panel code to anchor the recommendation. Declining keeps the
IG normative surface stable post-STU.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

This ticket is narrowly scoped — the submitter is not asking the IG
to *require* a grouping Observation, only to soften "does not
require" to "recommends." That is exactly the gap the prior
"Not Persuasive" dispositions left open: FHIR-41145 and FHIR-40807
were declined because they wanted a hard requirement, and the work
group's own resolution on FHIR-40807 explicitly invited revisiting
the question if a use case appeared. Implementer feedback on Zulip
(Lloyd McKenzie pointing real implementers at the
`hasMember`-grouping pattern in the PA IG) is that use case. A
narrative-only change is cheap, backward-compatible (no SHALL added,
no profile constraint changed), and brings the IG text into line
with the de facto guidance the work group is already giving.
Disposition B is the cleaner long-term answer but should not block
the small text fix; it can be tracked as a follow-up tied to the
LOINC submission. Disposition C preserves consistency at the cost
of ignoring the implementer signal that prompted the post-STU
re-open.
