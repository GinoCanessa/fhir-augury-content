## Summary

The reporter (Rick Lisseveld) flagged that footnote 1 in the Overview section of
the US SDOH Clinical Care IG home page links to a Healthy People 2030 URL on
`health.gov` that now returns "Page not found". The cited definition of social
determinants of health (the sentence in the Overview's opening blockquote)
sources its superscript-1 footnote to
`https://health.gov/healthypeople/priority-areas/social-determinants-health`,
which is dead. The reporter has already done the diligence: the Internet Archive
shows that on 2024-10-09 the page itself announced a move to a new domain
(`odphp.health.gov`), and crawls after that date 302-redirect to
`https://odphp.health.gov/healthypeople/priority-areas/social-determinants-health`,
which still hosts the cited sentence. The proposed disposition in the ticket is
simply to update the footnote URL to the new ODPHP location. No related Jira
tickets, Zulip discussions, or GitHub items were surfaced by cross-reference
lookups; the ticket already carries a `Persuasive` resolution and is labelled
`Ready-For-Review`.

## Details

**Description:**

The footnote reference in the Overview section on the IG Home Page pointing to
Healthy People 2030 is resolving to a page not found.

The opening sentence of the Overview section has a superscript 1 at the end of
the sentence that is a [hyperlink to a footnote](https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/fhir-sdoh-clinicalcare/en/index.html#fn:1)
with the text *Healthy People 2030*.

> Social determinants of health (SDOH) are the conditions in the environments
> where people are born, live, learn, work, play, worship, and age that affect
> a wide range of health, functioning, and quality-of-life outcomes and
> risks^1.

Healthy People 2030 is hyperlinked to
`https://health.gov/healthypeople/priority-areas/social-determinants-health`,
which reads:

> Page not found
> The requested page could not be found.

**Potential Disposition:** Replace the URL for Healthy People 2030 with
`https://odphp.health.gov/healthypeople/priority-areas/social-determinants-health`,
which contains the sentence cited.

**Support for disposition:** When searching the link in the Internet Archive
Wayback Machine:

- Crawls after October 9, 2024 result in a 302 redirect to
  `https://odphp.health.gov/healthypeople/priority-areas/social-determinants-health`; and
- The last successful crawl on October 9, 2024 has this text at the top of the
  page:
  > Between October 18–21, this website will move to a new web address (from
  > health.gov to odphp.health.gov). During that time, some functions might not
  > work as expected. We appreciate your patience and understanding as we're
  > working to make this transition as smooth as possible.

**Resolution Description (already on ticket):**

> We will update the Healthy People 2030 link to the following URL:
> `https://odphp.health.gov/healthypeople/priority-areas/social-determinants-health`

**Comments:** No comments are recorded on the ticket.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the footnote target in the Overview section of the US SDOH Clinical Care
IG home page so that footnote 1 — currently pointing at
`https://health.gov/healthypeople/priority-areas/social-determinants-health` —
points instead at
`https://odphp.health.gov/healthypeople/priority-areas/social-determinants-health`.
The change is a single URL substitution in the IG's narrative source for the
home page (the page that renders as `index.html` in the published IG, sourced
from the IG's `input/pagecontent/` markdown for the Overview / index page in
`HL7/fhir-sdoh-clinicalcare`). The visible footnote label ("Healthy People
2030") and the cited sentence remain unchanged. Republish the IG so the new
link is live on `build.fhir.org` and in the next published version.

#### Justification

The reporter has demonstrated, with Wayback Machine evidence, that the original
`health.gov` URL has been permanently moved to `odphp.health.gov` (302
redirects since 2024-10-09) and that the new URL still hosts the exact sentence
the IG is footnoting. This is a no-controversy editorial fix: the citation
intent is preserved, the new URL is the official successor maintained by the
same publisher (ODPHP, which now hosts Healthy People), and the change is
already marked `Persuasive`. Accepting as requested keeps the IG's normative
content untouched and resolves a broken external reference that affects every
reader of the home page. There are no FHIR design implications.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Apply the URL fix as in Disposition A, *and* sweep the IG for any other
`health.gov/healthypeople/...` references (including any references in
StructureDefinitions, ValueSet/CodeSystem descriptions, profile narratives, or
other `pagecontent` files) and update them in the same change. Additionally,
where the citation is intended to be durable, consider routing the link
through a stable redirector or citing Healthy People 2030 by its DOI/program
identifier rather than a deep URL — for example, anchor the citation text
(e.g., "U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, Office of Disease
Prevention and Health Promotion. *Healthy People 2030: Social Determinants of
Health.*") and link to the program landing page so future reorganizations of
the ODPHP site don't reintroduce the same defect.

#### Justification

The same domain migration (`health.gov` → `odphp.health.gov`) almost certainly
affects more than one footnote in an SDOH-focused IG. Catching all instances in
a single editorial pass avoids opening a sequence of near-identical tickets as
readers stumble onto each one, and aligns with normal IG hygiene around
external citations. Citing the program more abstractly (full citation +
landing-page link) trades a small amount of specificity for resilience against
the next site reorganization, which is a known failure mode for U.S. federal
public-health URLs. The trade-off vs. Disposition A is scope: this is a few
hours of editorial work rather than a one-line change, and the resilient
citation style is a small style departure from the current footnote format.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline to make any change in the IG itself, on the rationale that the broken
link is an external-publisher problem and the reader can still find the cited
sentence via a search engine or by navigating from the Healthy People landing
page.

#### Justification

This option is included for completeness only. There is no defensible reason to
decline: the IG publishes a footnote that resolves to "Page not found", which
materially degrades the home page for every reader, the fix is trivial, the
new URL is verified, and the working group has already marked the resolution
`Persuasive`. Declining would leave a known dead reference in normative
narrative content.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is exactly the kind of editorial defect a `Persuasive` resolution
exists for: a verified broken external link with a verified working
replacement, no FHIR semantics involved, and a one-line change in the IG home
page narrative. Disposition A should be applied as written — substitute
`health.gov/healthypeople/priority-areas/social-determinants-health` with
`odphp.health.gov/healthypeople/priority-areas/social-determinants-health` in
the Overview section's footnote — and the ticket closed. Disposition B is
worth doing opportunistically (a `git grep` for `health.gov/healthypeople`
across the IG sources costs nothing and likely finds a couple more
occurrences), but it should not block closing this specific ticket; if the
sweep turns up additional hits they can be folded into the same commit or
tracked as a follow-up. Disposition C is not defensible and is listed only to
make the trade-off explicit for the work group.
