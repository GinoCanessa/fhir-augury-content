## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the "Out of scope" section of the International Birth And Child
Model (FHIR) specification page to append the sentence *"For the time
being this is left out of scope of the current project."* exactly as the
reporter requests. This is a narrative-only, editorial change to the
relevant intro/scope page in the IG; no resources, profiles,
extensions, value sets, or constraints are added or modified.

#### Justification

The change is purely editorial and clarifying. It makes the
project's scoping intent explicit ("deferred, not abandoned"), which
helps implementers and downstream balloters distinguish between things
that are out of scope permanently and things that are out of scope only
for the current iteration. As a Technical Correction with no normative
impact, it is low-risk and consistent with how other HL7 IGs phrase
deferred-scope statements.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than inserting the sentence verbatim, expand the "Out of scope"
section to (a) list the specific topic(s) being deferred, (b) state
*why* they are out of scope for the current project (e.g., insufficient
maturity, dependency on another work item, lack of use cases), and (c)
indicate the intent to revisit in a future release — for example:
*"<topic X> is intentionally not addressed in this release because
<reason>; the work group plans to consider it in a future iteration."*
This treats the reporter's sentence as a prompt to clarify scope
rationale, not just to add boilerplate.

#### Justification

The sentence "For the time being this is left out of scope of the
current project." is grammatically attached to nothing concrete in the
ticket — without naming the deferred topic, readers still won't know
*what* is out of scope or *why*. An expanded statement matches how
other FHIR IGs (e.g., IPS, International Patient Access) document
deferred scope and avoids a cosmetic edit that doesn't actually improve
implementer understanding. The trade-off is more author effort and a
need to confirm the deferred topics with the work group.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the ticket as *Not Persuasive — insufficient information*. The
ticket does not identify which sentence the proposed text should follow,
which topic is being declared out of scope, or why the scoping
clarification is needed. Without that context the editor cannot make a
defensible change, and the IG's existing "Out of scope" wording is not
demonstrably wrong.

#### Justification

Editorial corrections to specification text need enough context for an
editor to apply them unambiguously. As filed, FHIR-41834 supplies a
sentence to add but neither the location within the section nor the
deferred topic it would qualify. Declining (or asking the reporter to
clarify before re-opening) is consistent with normal HL7 ballot-style
handling of under-specified Technical Corrections, and it avoids
inserting boilerplate that may be at odds with the section's actual
intent.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The change is small, editorial, and aligns with the reporter's clear
intent: make the "deferred, not abandoned" posture of the International
Birth And Child Model explicit in its "Out of scope" section. Because
there are no cross-referenced tickets, Zulip debates, or GitHub PRs
challenging the proposal, and because the affected text is narrative
only (no normative artifacts, profiles, or value sets are touched), the
risk of accepting is minimal. Patient Care should accept the addition
as requested, and the editor applying the change can — at their
discretion and without requiring a new ticket — adopt the more
descriptive phrasing from Disposition B if the surrounding paragraph
makes the deferred topic obvious. Disposition C is defensible on
strict-process grounds but is heavier-handed than the trivial nature of
the edit warrants.
