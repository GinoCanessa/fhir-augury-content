## Summary

The reporter notes that within the International Birth And Child Model
(FHIR) implementation guide, bullet points in the narrative content are
punctuated inconsistently — some items end with a full stop and others
do not. The ticket asks the editors to apply a single, consistent
convention to all bullet lists in the IG. No specific page, section, or
artifact is named, so the request is implicitly an IG-wide editorial
sweep. No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip discussions, or GitHub
items were found for this ticket.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt a single house style for bullet-list punctuation across the
International Birth And Child Model (FHIR) IG and apply it to every
bulleted list in the narrative pages. The recommended convention is the
common HL7 IG editorial pattern: end every bullet with a period when the
bullet contains a complete sentence; for short fragment bullets used as
list labels, omit the trailing period — but apply that rule uniformly
throughout the IG. Editors do a one-pass sweep over the IG's narrative
sources (the page content / introduction files for each resource and
profile) to normalise existing bullets, and add a one-line note to the
IG's editorial guidance recording the rule so future contributors stay
aligned.

#### Justification

This is a Technical Correction with the highest priority flag, and the
underlying defect (mixed punctuation in bullet lists) is a real
readability and quality-of-publication issue for a balloted IG. The
change is purely editorial: it touches narrative content, not
conformance, terminology, or structure, so risk to implementers is nil.
Codifying the rule in the IG's editorial notes prevents the same drift
re-appearing as new pages are authored.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than fixing the IG by hand once and hoping it stays clean, add a
lightweight CI/lint check to the IG build that flags bullet lists with
inconsistent terminal punctuation within the same list (e.g., a small
markdown linter rule or a custom script run from the IG's build
workflow). Run the linter, fix the violations it surfaces in a single
PR, and leave the check enabled so future PRs cannot re-introduce the
inconsistency. Document the rule in the IG's contributor guidance.

#### Justification

The reporter's complaint is a symptom of a process gap: there is
nothing today that prevents the inconsistency from recurring as pages
are added or edited. A linter shifts the cost from periodic editorial
sweeps to authors at PR time, which is cheaper over the life of the IG
and keeps the narrative consistently formatted without requiring
another technical-correction ticket every ballot cycle. The trade-off
versus Disposition A is a small one-time tooling investment.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as "Persuasive with Modification — Editorial" and roll
the fix into the IG's normal editorial pass for the next publication,
without committing to a specific style rule beyond "make each list
internally consistent." Optionally, decline as "Not Persuasive — Out
of Scope for a Technical Correction" on the grounds that bullet
punctuation is a stylistic preference rather than a defect in the
specification's content.

#### Justification

The request does not identify a specific page, section, or list, and
there is no evidence (Zulip discussion, linked tickets, implementer
feedback) that the inconsistency is causing confusion or
mis-implementation. Treating it as a Highest-priority Technical
Correction overstates its impact; an ordinary editorial pass will catch
it without the workgroup needing to formally accept a style rule.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

This is a small, low-risk editorial correction in a balloted Patient
Care IG, and the reporter has clearly articulated the defect. Accepting
the request and applying a uniform bullet-punctuation convention across
the IG resolves the ticket directly and is straightforward editorial
work. Disposition B (linting) is attractive but probably out of scope
for a single ticket — it is worth raising as a follow-up process
improvement after the cleanup, not as a precondition. Disposition C
under-weights the fact that the ticket has been triaged at Highest
priority by the workgroup; declining or deferring it would re-open the
same conversation at the next ballot. The recommendation is therefore
to accept, apply the standard "complete-sentence bullets get periods,
fragment bullets do not, applied uniformly" rule, and record it in the
IG's editorial notes.
