## Summary

Lloyd McKenzie has filed a change request against the **US Physical Activity
(PA) FHIR IG** suggesting that the full set of values in `Goal.lifecycleStatus`
is broader than what is meaningful for physical-activity goals. Specifically,
the reporter questions whether codes such as `draft` (i.e., `proposed`/`planned`)
and `rejected` are needed in this context. The proposed remedy is to
constrain `Goal.lifecycleStatus` in the PA IG to a smaller, clinically
useful set. Patient Care reviewed and approved a proposed disposition on
the March 5, 2024 PA call (Laurie/Paul, vote 10-0-0): constrain the
allowed values to **Accepted, Active, On Hold, Completed, Cancelled, and
Entered-in-Error**. The ticket is Triaged with resolution "Persuasive"
and is labelled `Ready-For-Vote`.

The only cross-reference surfaced for this ticket is to the FHIR artifact
`Goal.lifecycle` (i.e., `Goal.lifecycleStatus`); no related Jira tickets,
Zulip threads, or GitHub items were found.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the **US Physical Activity FHIR IG**, define (or update) a profile on
`Goal` (e.g., `PAGoal` / the IG's existing physical-activity Goal
profile) that constrains `Goal.lifecycleStatus` to a **required** binding
against a new IG-defined value set containing exactly: `accepted`,
`active`, `on-hold`, `completed`, `cancelled`, `entered-in-error`. The
codes `proposed`, `planned`, and `rejected` from the base
`http://hl7.org/fhir/ValueSet/goal-status` are excluded. Document in the
profile's narrative why the broader codes are excluded (PA-goal lifecycle
does not require pre-acceptance or rejected states), and add an example
PA Goal instance illustrating the use of `accepted` and `active`.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the Patient Care work group already deliberated and
approved on the Mar 5 PA call (10-0-0), and the ticket is resolved
"Persuasive" / `Ready-For-Vote`. Constraining to a clinically meaningful
subset reduces interoperability ambiguity for PA implementers, and using
a profile-level required binding (rather than touching base FHIR) keeps
the change scoped to the IG that actually has the use-case evidence.
Retaining `accepted` (instead of starting at `active`) preserves the
ability to record a goal that has been agreed with the patient but not
yet started — important for behaviour-change interventions typical in
physical-activity programs.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Apply the same intent at the IG profile level, but use a **`preferred`**
(or `extensible`) binding to the same restricted value set rather than a
`required` binding, and additionally publish **usage guidance** on the
profile page explaining that PA implementers SHOULD use only the six
codes (`accepted`, `active`, `on-hold`, `completed`, `cancelled`,
`entered-in-error`). Optionally, retain `proposed`/`planned` and instead
add an `obligation`/`mustSupport` constraint that the consumer SHALL be
able to render the six "in-scope" codes meaningfully but MAY ignore
others.

#### Justification

A `required` binding is a hard interoperability fence: any pre-existing
PA-adjacent system that already records `proposed` or `planned` PA goals
would become non-conformant. A `preferred` (or `extensible`) binding plus
narrative guidance achieves the same nudge toward the six-code set while
leaving an escape hatch for implementers who have legitimate
pre-commitment workflows (e.g., goals proposed by a coach but awaiting
patient acceptance). This also avoids the situation where a downstream
profile that derives from the PA Goal would inherit a constraint it
cannot loosen.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the constraint and leave `Goal.lifecycleStatus` bound as in base
FHIR (full `goal-status` value set, required binding). Close the ticket
as "Not Persuasive — Out of Scope for IG-level constraint."

#### Justification

`Goal.lifecycleStatus` already has a `required` binding in base FHIR and
the full code set is the result of broad multi-domain Patient Care
review. Removing codes at the IG level fragments the meaning of `Goal`
across implementation guides and makes data exchange between PA systems
and other Patient Care systems (CarePlan, care coordination, social
determinants, etc.) lossy: a `proposed` PA goal received from a partner
system would have no conformant representation. Implementers who do not
need certain codes are already free to simply not use them; codifying
the prohibition adds little and risks future divergence.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The work group has already deliberated, voted 10-0-0, and labelled the
ticket `Ready-For-Vote`; the resolution explicitly enumerates the
six-code set, which matches Disposition A precisely. Disposition B's
softer binding is defensible from a base-FHIR-fidelity standpoint, but
it would not actually deliver the constraint the work group asked for —
implementers would still be free to send `proposed`/`planned`/`rejected`
PA goals, defeating the purpose of the change. Disposition C contradicts
the existing 10-0-0 vote without new evidence. Disposition A should be
implemented in the US Physical Activity IG via a profile-level required
binding to a new IG-defined value set containing exactly the six codes
named in the resolution.
