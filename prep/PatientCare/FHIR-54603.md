## Summary

This ticket requests revisions to **Section 3. SDOH Clinical Care Background** of
the US SDOH Clinical Care (FHIR) IG. Two concrete issues are raised:

1. **Figure 1 (Conceptual Framework):** the figure should adopt the changes
   proposed in [FHIR-54596](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54596), which
   addresses the inconsistent / interchangeable use of the terms "Screening"
   vs. "Assessment" throughout the section. FHIR-54596 proposes a clearer,
   stage-aware workflow distinguishing screening (initial identification of a
   potential social need) from assessment (deeper analysis of risks and
   needs).
2. **Figure 2 (System Interactions):** the reporter argues this figure
   misrepresents real-world CIE/HIE data flows. Specifically, the depicted
   loop showing a "FHIR Application Enabled CBO" sending data directly back
   to a "Provider's EHR" (in response to a patient-initiated request) is
   considered inaccurate because it bypasses the Coordination Platform,
   ignores patient consent and BAA requirements between CBO and provider,
   exposes patient privacy risk, and creates unfunded work for the CBO.
   Practically, CBOs do not push data straight into provider EHRs absent a
   referral, a data-sharing agreement, and a payment arrangement; routing
   through one (or a few) Coordination Platform(s) is the realistic pattern
   and avoids forcing EHRs to maintain N-to-N CRM connections.

The ticket has already been triaged with resolution **Persuasive**: the
Gravity Project intends to (a) update Figure 1 to align with the latest
social-care co-design work (which will be applied via FHIR-54596) and
(b) **remove Figure 2 entirely** for lack of clarity. The ticket is included
in the Patient Care WG **Gravity SDOHCC STU3 — Block Vote 2** batch
(see Zulip thread below).

## Details

**Description:**

1. See [FHIR-54596](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54596) — Inconsistent
   use of terms "Screening" and "Assessment" — for recommended changes to
   Figure 1. Conceptual Framework.
2. Figure 2. System Interactions has several inaccuracies that do not
   reflect interactions and the flow of data in active CIE/HIE
   environments.
   a. There is no overarching loop from "FHIR Application Enabled CBO"
      sending data to "Providers' EHR" from a patient-initiated request for
      services.
      i)  This would bypass the Coordination Platform,
      ii) add the complexity of requiring patient consent to share SDOH
          information back to the provider EHR (when their primary care
          provider's EHR may not even be connected to the Coordination
          Platform),
      iii) violate patient privacy with the CBO in the absence of
           documented consent and a BAA between the CBO and the Provider,
           and
      iv) create an additional unpaid work burden for the CBO to provide
          data to a health system EHR.

In short, a CBO working with a client to address SDOH Needs is not going
to share data directly to a health-care EHR in the absence of a mutually
agreed data-sharing framework addressed in a BAA, an originating referral
from the health provider, and a payment structure to compensate for the
additional work burden. Bypassing the Coordination Platform also creates a
situation where the EHR would have to manage connections to many CBOs
using different CRMs instead of connecting to a single (or small number
of) Coordination Platform(s).

**Resolution (Persuasive):** Update Figure 1 to align with the most
up-to-date version of the social-care co-design work; remove Figure 2
altogether for lack of clarity.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the already-recorded **Persuasive** resolution as-is in Section 3
of the US SDOH Clinical Care IG:

1. Replace **Figure 1. Conceptual Framework for SDOH Clinical Care** with
   the new visual being produced under FHIR-54596, which uses the
   distinct "Screening" vs. "Assessment" stages aligned with the latest
   social-care co-design work. Update the surrounding narrative in
   Section 3 so that "Screening" and "Assessment" are used consistently
   per that figure.
2. **Remove Figure 2. System Interactions** in its entirety, along with
   its accompanying narrative, because the depicted CBO → Provider EHR
   loop misrepresents real-world consent/BAA/payment constraints in
   CIE/HIE environments and there is no agreed alternative diagram ready
   to take its place.

#### Justification

This matches the ticket's request and the Gravity Project's stated
intent (resolution = Persuasive). The Figure 1 fix is delivered via the
sister ticket FHIR-54596 (Persuasive with Modification), so the two
changes are coherent and trace back to a single co-design source of
truth. Removing Figure 2 outright is defensible because the reporter's
critique — that the diagram implies a privacy- and policy-noncompliant
direct CBO→EHR data flow — is substantive and has not been rebutted,
and a misleading normative-looking figure is worse than no figure. It
also mirrors the disposition Aaron Nusstein has lined up for Block Vote 2
on the Patient Care WG Zulip thread, so it can ride the existing block
vote without separate handling.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Apply the Figure 1 update from FHIR-54596, but **keep Figure 2 in the
IG** in a corrected form rather than deleting it. Specifically:

- Redraw Figure 2 so the only data path from a "FHIR Application Enabled
  CBO" back toward a provider context goes **through the Coordination
  Platform**, gated by an explicit Consent step and a referenced
  data-sharing agreement (BAA) actor. Remove the patient-initiated
  CBO→Provider-EHR loop.
- Add a short caption / preamble noting the figure is illustrative of
  one common pattern, not normative, and that real deployments depend
  on local consent, BAA, and payment arrangements.
- Track this as a follow-up implementation task against Section 3 (no
  artifact / profile changes required, narrative IG only).

#### Justification

The reporter's objection is to specific *inaccuracies* in Figure 2, not
to the existence of a System Interactions diagram. A corrected diagram
preserves a useful explanatory tool for implementers — many readers find
the system-interaction view easier than prose for understanding which
actor talks to which — without endorsing a non-compliant flow. Trade-off
vs. Disposition A: more editorial work and another review cycle inside
the Gravity team, and risk of re-litigating the same critique if the
revised diagram is judged still inaccurate. Trade-off vs. Disposition C:
keeps the request meaningfully addressed instead of dismissing it.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark FHIR-54603 as **Not Persuasive** and leave Section 3 (including
Figure 1 and Figure 2) unchanged for this STU3 cycle, deferring any
revision to a future ballot once the social-care co-design work is fully
ratified outside HL7.

#### Justification

A defensible "decline" framing would be (a) the changes are narrative /
illustrative rather than affecting any normative profile, value set, or
operation, and (b) Figure 2's depiction is a reasonable abstraction
whose policy/consent caveats belong in implementation guidance, not in
the diagram itself. However, this rationale is weak: the reporter raises
concrete privacy and policy concerns, and the Gravity Project itself has
already accepted the change (resolution Persuasive). Declining now would
contradict the working group's own triage outcome and surface again at
the next ballot. Included for completeness rather than as a serious
candidate.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket has already been triaged **Persuasive** by the Gravity
Project, the upstream Figure 1 work is being delivered through
FHIR-54596, and the reporter's critique of Figure 2 (consent/BAA/payment
realities, EHR-CBO N-to-N coupling) is substantive and unrebutted in
either the ticket comments or the Zulip thread. Disposition A also
fits cleanly into the Patient Care WG **Gravity SDOHCC STU3 — Block
Vote 2** batch announced on Zulip, minimizing process overhead.
Disposition B is a reasonable fallback if the workgroup believes a
System Interactions diagram is essential for implementer comprehension,
but it incurs additional editorial and review cost and risks
re-introducing the same accuracy concerns; it should only be selected
if a specific corrected diagram is already drafted. Disposition C is
not recommended — it would reverse the standing triage outcome without
new evidence.
