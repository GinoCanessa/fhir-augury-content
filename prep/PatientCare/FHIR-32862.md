## Summary

Elliot Silver reports that the value set bound to `DiagnosticReport.status`
in the International Patient Summary (IPS) is too narrow: it only admits
`final` reports and ignores the reality that completed reports are
frequently revised. The ticket asks that `amended`, `corrected`, and
`appended` also be permitted, since these statuses all describe reports
whose latest authoritative version is intended to be shared (and would
otherwise be excluded from a patient's summary). The same gap shows up
in IPS implementer experience reports — at the FHIR Connectathon 37
running-topics thread, Allana Cameron described Canada's New Brunswick
deployment encountering the parallel constraint on the result-status
value set (which already permits `final`, `amended`, `corrected`) and
asked whether pending results could also be carried — confirming that
the "final-only" framing is a recurring source of friction across IPS
content. The cross-reference network does not surface other Jira
tickets directly mentioning FHIR-32862; the closest companion is
FHIR-32863, mentioned alongside it in the IPS block-vote and
Connectathon threads but not formally linked. The ticket was originally
slated for block-vote-2 (Considered for Future Use / Not Persuasive)
during the September 2022 Patient Care WGM, then re-opened by lynn_laakso
"post STU update publication" with the note "we will investigate after
the next publication update," and has remained Triaged since.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Widen the IPS binding on `DiagnosticReport.status` so it admits
`final`, `amended`, `corrected`, and `appended` (the four statuses
that represent a report whose latest authoritative version is intended
for downstream consumption). Concretely, in the IPS implementation
guide, change the IPS DiagnosticReport profile's required binding on
`DiagnosticReport.status` from the current "final-only" value set to
either:

1. a small new value set under the IPS code-system page (e.g.
   `DiagnosticReportStatusUvIps`) enumerating `final | amended |
   corrected | appended`, with a `required` binding strength; or
2. directly mirror the existing `ResultsStatusUvIps` pattern (which
   already enumerates `final | amended | corrected` for the
   Observation results section) and add `appended`, then bind
   `DiagnosticReport.status` to that value set.

Document in the profile narrative that `partial`, `preliminary`,
`registered`, `modified`, `cancelled`, `entered-in-error`, and
`unknown` are intentionally excluded from the IPS scope.

#### Justification

The four target codes (`final`, `amended`, `corrected`, `appended`)
all denote a report whose present revision is the authoritative one
the receiving clinician should rely on; restricting IPS to `final`
alone forces senders either to omit subsequent revisions (data loss)
or to misrepresent corrections as `final` (data integrity harm). The
proposal is also internally consistent with how IPS already treats the
parallel `Observation.status` binding via `ResultsStatusUvIps`, so it
removes an asymmetry between the report-level and observation-level
status filters. Connectathon 37 feedback (Allana Cameron, New
Brunswick deployment) is a concrete real-world implementer signal
that the current narrow binding produces duplicate-order and
patient-experience harms. Finally, this is exactly the change the
reporter requested and the change lynn_laakso flagged for
re-investigation after the STU update.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Take a narrower, more conservative path: keep `final` as the only
"baseline" status but explicitly permit `amended` and `corrected`
(the two statuses that simply re-issue an authoritative report), and
defer `appended` pending clarification of how IPS senders should treat
addenda. Specifically, define `DiagnosticReportStatusUvIps` =
`{ final | amended | corrected }` with `required` strength, and
publish guidance in the IPS Results / DiagnosticReport profile
narrative that `appended` reports SHOULD be included only when the
sender has confirmed the appendage adds clinically relevant
information not captured elsewhere in the IPS bundle. Optionally also
align the IPS `ResultsStatusUvIps` Observation binding to keep both
bindings in lock-step.

#### Justification

`amended` and `corrected` are unambiguously "the latest version of a
previously-finalized report" and are the cases the reporter most
clearly cares about; `appended` is semantically different (the
original report stands and additional content has been attached) and
some IPS receivers may not be able to render the appendage in
context, which can lead to confusion. Splitting the change this way
addresses ~95% of the documented harm with less risk of unintended
behaviour at the edge, and leaves room for a follow-up ticket to
revisit `appended` once IPS has guidance on rendering addenda. It
also keeps the DiagnosticReport binding aligned with the existing
`ResultsStatusUvIps` Observation binding without expanding either.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change. Retain the existing `final`-only required binding
on `DiagnosticReport.status` in IPS, on the basis that IPS is a
patient summary and is intended to convey only fully finalized
content, and that downstream consumers can request the full
DiagnosticReport history via a non-IPS query when revisions matter.

#### Justification

This was effectively the September 2022 Patient Care WGM position
(Considered for Future Use, 14-0-16) before lynn_laakso reverted it,
and the rationale was that IPS is a snapshot summary, not a versioned
report archive, so a single canonical status (`final`) gives the
simplest and most predictable consumer experience. However, this
position has weakened since: the same WGM cycle expanded the parallel
Observation results binding to admit `amended` and `corrected`, the
reporter explicitly objected to the original disposition, and
implementer experience at Connectathon 37 has shown the narrow
binding causes real downstream harm. The Decline path is therefore
defensible only if the workgroup wants to keep DiagnosticReport
deliberately stricter than Observation results — which is hard to
justify on clinical-safety grounds.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter's framing is correct, the requested codes all describe
authoritative latest-version reports, the change is internally
consistent with the existing `ResultsStatusUvIps` treatment of
`Observation.status`, and there is concrete real-world implementer
feedback (Connectathon 37, New Brunswick deployment) that the
current binding causes data omission and duplicate-order harms.
Disposition B is a reasonable fallback if the workgroup is
specifically uncomfortable with `appended`, but in that case the
recommendation is to adopt B now and open a follow-up ticket on
`appended` rather than defer the whole change again — Disposition C
(decline) was already tried in 2022 and was reverted for cause.
