## Summary

This long-running Patient Care ticket, originated by Emma Jones at the
Clinicians-On-FHIR connectathon in Montreal (May 2016), asks FHIR to
provide a way to capture **patient and/or provider preferences** in the
context of a CarePlan. The motivating examples are non-clinical
preferences such as *"patient does not like peas"*, *"patient prefers
apple juice instead of milk"*, or *"prefers sitting rather than
walking"*. Lloyd McKenzie originally framed the design space as three
options: (1) profile/extend `Observation`, (2) reuse the (now retired)
`Alert` resource, or (3) create a new `Preference` resource.

The ticket has been re-considered repeatedly over nearly a decade. In
2016 PCWG voted to meet the need with a **profile on Observation**
carrying a preference category (nutrition, medication, care), priority
(high/medium/delayed from C-CDA), and explicit **expressor** and
**recorder** participants — but deferred actually building the profile.
That resolution was reverted in 2023, and the ticket was reopened for
R6. In 2024, the ADI (Advance Directives Interoperability) report-out
confirmed broad cross-WG consensus that **person-authored care-experience
and treatment-intervention preferences should be expressed as
`Observation`s** (US Core Treatment Preferences already does this), while
**practitioner-authored portable medical orders use `ServiceRequest`**.
In June 2025 the WG reconsidered and noted that not all preferences fit
the point-in-time `Observation` semantics (e.g., *"never give me mRNA
vaccinations"* is durable like `AllergyIntolerance`/`Condition`), and
Emma indicated a preference for exploring an **extension on CarePlan and
all Request resources**. As of the July 17, 2025 PCWG call, a logical
model has been drafted and largely agreed; how it manifests in CarePlan
is still open and needs further CarePlan-call discussion.

The two cross-referenced FHIR elements illustrate today's partial
coverage: `NutritionOrder.excludeFoodModifier` already accommodates
food/substance preferences within an order, and `Observation.code` is the
slot that would carry the preference *type* (nutrition, medication, care
management, end-of-life) in the profile-on-Observation approach.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Build the long-promised **Preference profile on `Observation`** in FHIR
R6 Patient Care, exactly as voted in 2016 and re-affirmed by the
Sept-2024 ADI consensus:

- New profile (e.g., `observation-preference`) constraining
  `Observation` with:
  - `Observation.category` bound to a new value set distinguishing
    preference *domains* (nutrition, medication, care-management,
    end-of-life, daily-living/positioning, etc.).
  - `Observation.code` carrying the specific preference type, with an
    extensible binding seeded from C-CDA Personal Advance Care Plan
    preference codes.
  - `Observation.value[x]` carrying the preference content (CodeableConcept
    or string), with examples for "prefers apple juice over milk",
    "prefers sitting over walking", etc.
  - **Extension `preferencePriority`** on the profile, bound to the
    C-CDA high/medium/delayed value set, to support ranking competing
    preferences.
  - **Extension `expressor`** (Reference(Patient | RelatedPerson |
    Practitioner | PractitionerRole)) so the person who *voiced* the
    preference can be distinguished from `Observation.performer` and
    from `Observation.subject`.
  - Reuse `Observation.performer` as the **recorder** (i.e., who
    captured/attested the statement) and document this mapping in the
    profile narrative.
  - Provide example instances for nutrition, medication, and
    care-experience preferences.
- Add a **scope-and-usage** bullet to the base `Observation` resource
  page (owned by OO) explicitly listing patient/provider preferences as
  an in-scope use of `Observation`, so implementers find it.
- Reference the new profile from `CarePlan` (and `Request`-pattern
  resources) as the recommended way to convey preferences that should
  influence the plan or order, rather than embedding preferences inside
  CarePlan itself.

#### Justification

This is the path PCWG voted on in 2016 (5-0-0), ratified by the Sept-2024
cross-WG ADI consensus, and already followed in production by **US Core
Treatment Preferences**. `Observation.code` is the natural slot for
preference type (per `eayres`'s 2016-06-30 minutes), and the C-CDA
Personal Advance Care Plan already carries goals, preferences, and
priorities as observations — so this aligns FHIR with an established
real-world pattern. The expressor/recorder/priority gap that
`eayres` identified in 2016 is closed cleanly with three small
extensions, avoiding a brand-new resource. Mckenzie's note about *not*
embedding preferences inside CarePlan (so they can be reused across
plans) is honored.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt a **two-pronged hybrid** that better matches the durability split
PCWG surfaced on the June 12, 2025 call and Emma Jones's stated
preference for an extension-based approach:

1. **Durable preferences** (e.g., *"never give me mRNA vaccinations"*,
   *"no blood transfusions"*, *"do not use restraints"*) are modeled
   alongside other durable patient facts. Either:
   - Define a thin **profile on `Observation`** restricted to
     `effective[x]` semantics for *enduring* statements (status =
     `final`, no `effectiveDateTime` upper bound), **or**
   - Re-use existing durable structures (`Consent` for refusals/permissions,
     `AllergyIntolerance` only when the underlying mechanism is allergic).
2. **Plan/order-scoped preferences** (e.g., *"prefers apple juice over
   milk in this nutrition plan"*, *"prefers sitting over walking during
   PT"*) are conveyed via a **new `preference` extension** on
   `CarePlan` and on the `Request` pattern (so it appears uniformly on
   `ServiceRequest`, `MedicationRequest`, `NutritionOrder`,
   `CommunicationRequest`, etc.). The extension's value would be
   `Reference(Observation)` pointing at the durable-preference
   instance, with an inline `CodeableConcept` summary for lightweight
   cases.

Document `NutritionOrder.excludeFoodModifier` as the canonical mechanism
for food-exclusion preferences expressed inside a nutrition order, so
the new extension does not duplicate it.

#### Justification

The June 12, 2025 PCWG discussion explicitly raised that not every
preference is a point-in-time `Observation` — durable refusals
(*"never mRNA vaccines"*) behave more like `AllergyIntolerance` or
`Condition`. Emma Jones, the original reporter, said on that call she
**prefers exploring an extension on CarePlan and all Request
resources**. A `Request`-pattern extension is also a much cleaner answer
to Sadiq Saleh's 2017 Zulip question (*how do I attach a preference to a
ProcedureRequest / MedicationRequest?*) than the "supporting information
references an Observation" workaround Lloyd acknowledged was
under-specified. This approach concedes that a single profile on
`Observation` does not cover the whole field but stays compatible with
the Sept-2024 ADI consensus by leaving person-authored care-experience
and treatment-intervention preferences on `Observation`.

The trade-off vs. Disposition A is added complexity (two mechanisms
instead of one) and additional cross-WG coordination (FHIR-I owns the
`Request` pattern; OO owns `Observation`); the upside is that the model
matches what implementers actually need to express and avoids forcing
durable refusals into a point-in-time resource.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

**Close as Persuasive with Modification — pointing to existing
mechanisms** rather than adding a new artifact. Document on the
`CarePlan` and `Observation` pages that today's preference use cases are
already coverable with the existing toolkit:

- Food/substance preferences inside a nutrition order →
  `NutritionOrder.excludeFoodModifier` (and the related food-preference
  modifiers).
- Treatment preferences and advance-directive content → **US Core
  Treatment Preferences** profile on `Observation` and the **HL7 ADI
  IG**, both of which already use `Observation` per the Sept-2024
  cross-WG consensus.
- Refusals of specific care → `Consent`.
- Plan-level free-text preferences → `CarePlan.note` /
  `CarePlan.description`.

No core spec change; close FHIR-10028 as **No Change Required**, with a
pointer in the resolution to US Core Treatment Preferences and the ADI
IG.

#### Justification

The ticket has been open for ~10 years across multiple release cycles,
with three prior PCWG votes that did not produce a built profile. In
the meantime, US Core has shipped a Treatment Preferences profile on
`Observation`, the ADI IG has standardized person-authored advance
directives on `Observation`, and `NutritionOrder.excludeFoodModifier`
covers the original "patient does not like peas" example. The June
2025 PCWG call itself listed *"no change needed"* and *"transfer to OO"*
as live options. Adding a base-spec profile now risks duplicating
US Core/ADI work and triggering unnecessary churn for implementers who
have already adopted those IG-level patterns. If a gap remains, it is
better surfaced in a focused IG (e.g., a CarePlan companion guide) than
in FHIR Core.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The ticket has demonstrably resisted both the "single profile on
`Observation`" answer (Disposition A — voted in 2016, never built;
re-confirmed in 2024 but still not realized in core) and the "do
nothing, point to existing mechanisms" answer (Disposition C — every
time PCWG looks at it, *something* still feels missing, most recently
the durable-refusal use case Rob raised on June 12, 2025). Disposition
B captures both of the things the WG keeps converging on: keep
person-authored care-experience and treatment-intervention preferences
on `Observation` (preserving the Sept-2024 ADI consensus and US Core
alignment), **and** give CarePlan/Request authors a first-class,
uniform extension to attach a preference to the artifact that needs to
honor it — which is exactly what Emma Jones (the reporter) said she
wanted on the most recent PCWG call, and what would have answered Sadiq
Saleh's 2017 Zulip question. It is more work than Disposition A and
requires coordination with FHIR-I (Request pattern) and OO
(`Observation` scope-and-usage), but the logical model called out as
"largely agreed" on the July 17, 2025 PCWG call gives the WG a
concrete starting point. If cross-WG coordination stalls, fall back to
Disposition A as the minimum viable outcome for R6.
