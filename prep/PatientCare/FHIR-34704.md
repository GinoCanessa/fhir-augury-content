## Summary

The reporter (Genevieve Luensman) asks the Gravity Project's US SDOH
Clinical Care FHIR IG to explicitly state in its Overview that detailed
information about a person's **work** is **out of scope** for this guide,
and to refer readers to the Occupational Data for Health (ODH) IG for
work-related content. The reporter acknowledges the common SDOH framing
"where you live, work, and play" but argues that, because this IG does
not actually carry detailed occupational data, the Overview should make
that boundary explicit and point implementers to ODH instead.

The ticket is closely related to [FHIR-34701](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-34701),
which raised the same concern against **US Core** and was resolved
*Persuasive with Modification* (Cross-Group Projects, 2022-02-10) by
adding scope-clarifying language and a footnote about future ODH
alignment to the US Core SDOH Guidance page. A prior resolution on
FHIR-34704 itself (*Not Persuasive*, 2022-10-10, Dieterle/Heerman-Langford
8-0-0) argued that the SDOH Clinical Care IG and ODH "do not conflict",
but the reporter reverted that disposition on 2022-12-15, stating the
negative vote was not withdrawn and the issue was not satisfactorily
resolved in the published 2.0.0 STU2 release. The ticket therefore
returns to Patient Care as an open `block-vote-12` item requesting the
same kind of scope clarification FHIR-34701 produced for US Core, but
applied directly to the SDOH Clinical Care IG.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the US SDOH Clinical Care IG **Overview** (home/scope page) to
add an explicit scope statement that closely mirrors the language
adopted for FHIR-34701 in US Core. Specifically:

1. Add a short paragraph to the Overview stating that, while the
   commonly used SDOH framing includes "where you live, **work**, and
   play," **detailed information about a person's work (employment,
   job title, industry/occupation codes, usual work, combat zone
   periods, etc.) is out of scope for this IG**.
2. Add a pointer / link to the **Occupational Data for Health (ODH)**
   IG as the appropriate place to represent such data, together with
   the same forward-looking note used in US Core that future versions
   may incorporate ODH-aligned elements if they succeed on the US Core
   Growth path.
3. No profile, extension, value set, or example changes are required;
   this is purely a narrative clarification on the Overview page.

#### Justification

The change is narrative-only, additive, and non-breaking. It directly
addresses the commenter's unresolved negative ballot from STU2 and
eliminates the ambiguity that led the reporter to revert the 2022-10-10
*Not Persuasive* decision. It is also consistent with the precedent
already set by the Cross-Group Projects disposition on FHIR-34701,
which used effectively the same wording for US Core's SDOH Guidance;
re-using that language preserves alignment across the two IGs that
most implementers read together. Because the Gravity IG genuinely does
not carry detailed work data, an explicit scope disclaimer matches what
the IG actually contains and reduces the risk of implementers
mis-classifying employment/occupational content under SDOH profiles.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than a one-line "out of scope" disclaimer, add a dedicated
**"Relationship to Occupational Data for Health (ODH)"** subsection
(or a cross-IG boundaries section) to the Overview that:

1. Restates the SDOH definition sourced from USCDI (as FHIR-34701 did).
2. Enumerates the categories the IG **does** cover (SDOH Assessments,
   Goals, Interventions, Problems/Health Concerns, and related
   terminology).
3. Enumerates work-related categories that are **explicitly not**
   covered (e.g., occupation, industry, usual work, employment status,
   work schedule, combat zone periods) and directs implementers to the
   ODH IG for each.
4. Adds a compatibility note stating that SDOH Clinical Care and ODH
   are complementary: an implementer needing both social-risk and
   occupational data should profile from both IGs and not attempt to
   repurpose SDOH profiles for occupational content.
5. Includes the same forward-looking statement as Disposition A about
   potential future alignment.

#### Justification

The underlying concern — that readers might conflate SDOH with ODH —
is better addressed by a structured boundary section than by a single
sentence that is easy to overlook. A subsection also gives the
workgroup a natural home to add future guidance as USCDI evolves
(USCDI V3 introduced an Occupation data element) and lets ODH
maintainers link back to a stable anchor. The trade-off versus
Disposition A is slightly more editorial work and the need to agree on
the list of "not covered" categories with ODH authors; the benefit is a
clearer, more durable scope boundary that will also satisfy the
reporter's desire for explicit guidance, not just a disclaimer.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Not Persuasive**, on the basis that the matter
has already been addressed for US Core via
[FHIR-34701](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-34701), and that the SDOH
Clinical Care IG and ODH are acknowledged in the prior 2022-02-03
Cross-Group Project discussion as use-case-specific and
non-conflicting. Optionally add the FHIR-34701 link to a FAQ rather
than changing the Overview.

#### Justification

The primary counter-argument on record (the 2022-10-10 8-0-0
disposition cited by the reporter) is that the two IGs do not conflict
and that US Core's clarification is sufficient because that is where
most SDOH/USCDI readers land first. However, the reporter has already
demonstrated this rationale is not community-accepted — the negative
ballot from STU2 was never withdrawn, the previous resolution was
reverted, and the ticket was re-opened as a `block-vote-12` item.
Declining again without new information would very likely regenerate
the same negative ballot in the next cycle, so the defensibility of
this disposition is weak unless the workgroup is prepared to defend
the prior rationale in person.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A (with language borrowed from B if the
workgroup wants a slightly heavier treatment).

The reporter's request is narrow, narrative-only, and has a direct,
already-agreed precedent in FHIR-34701 that the Gravity IG can simply
mirror on its own Overview page. It costs the workgroup almost nothing
editorially, it clears an unresolved negative ballot that was
explicitly flagged as `block-vote-12`, and it brings the SDOH Clinical
Care IG into narrative alignment with the US Core SDOH Guidance that
most implementers will read alongside it. Disposition C is hard to
defend given the ticket's history (the prior *Not Persuasive* decision
was reverted and the negative vote stands), and Disposition B's extra
structure, while defensible, is larger than what the reporter is
asking for; if the workgroup prefers B, it can be framed as an
enhancement of A rather than a separate path. A clear, short scope
statement plus an ODH pointer is the minimal change that makes the
ballot go away, and it is the recommended starting position for
discussion.
