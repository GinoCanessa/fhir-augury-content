## Summary

The reporter notes that the FHIR community has aligned on a "BodyStructure
pattern": where a resource needs to represent an anatomical site and
supports both coded and referenced representations, it should expose a
single element of type `CodeableReference(BodyStructure)` and remove
other elements that redundantly represent the body structure (notably a
separate `bodySite` element). The ticket asserts that one Patient Care
resource does not follow that pattern and asks the work group either to
correct the resource or to document in the Notes subsection why the
resource deviates.

The ticket itself does not name the target resource in its description,
but the available signals point strongly at **Procedure**:

- The ticket's extracted FHIR keyword set includes `procedure` (the only
  resource-level FHIR-path keyword on the ticket aside from
  `bodystructure` itself).
- The reporter filed this as one of three sibling tickets ([FHIR-54206](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54206),
  [FHIR-54207](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54207),
  [FHIR-54208](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54208)) on the same day
  with identical wording, each targeting a different resource that
  deviates from the pattern. FHIR-54206 (Orders & Observations) targets
  `DeviceAssociation`; FHIR-54207 (Patient Care) targets `Condition`;
  FHIR-54208 (Patient Care) is the remaining sibling and aligns with
  Procedure.
- Independently filed [FHIR-54703](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54703)
  ("Harmonize Procedure.bodyStructure and Observation.bodyStructure")
  documents exactly the deviation described here: `Observation.bodyStructure`
  is `CodeableReference(BodyStructure)` and `Observation.bodySite` was
  deprecated, while `Procedure.bodyStructure` is plain
  `Reference(BodyStructure)` and `Procedure.bodySite` (CodeableConcept,
  0..*) was retained.
- The Zulip thread `#Orders and Observation WG > R6 BodyStructure
  Review` directly raises this same deviation against
  `build.fhir.org/procedure.html` and notes that the Patient Care
  cardinality decision (`bodyStructure` 0..1, `bodySite` 0..*) was
  intentional, anchoring the existing rationale that would have to be
  revisited or written into Notes.

The disposition therefore needs to either (a) refactor `Procedure` to
the agreed `CodeableReference(BodyStructure)` shape, (b) keep the
current shape but record the deviation rationale in Procedure's Notes /
Boundaries section per the new BodyStructure boundary text adopted in
[FHIR-54872](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54872), or (c) decline as
already-tracked work duplicating FHIR-54703.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Refactor Procedure (Patient Care, FHIR Core) to follow the agreed
BodyStructure pattern:

- Change `Procedure.bodyStructure` from `Reference(BodyStructure)` to
  `CodeableReference(BodyStructure)`, bound to the same value set as
  `Observation.bodyStructure`.
- Deprecate `Procedure.bodySite` (consistent with the deprecation of
  `Observation.bodySite`), or, if PC still needs the multi-site
  semantics, raise the cardinality of the new
  `Procedure.bodyStructure` from 0..1 to 0..* (revisiting the
  FHIR-51792 decision) so a single element can carry the data
  previously split across `bodySite` and `bodyStructure`.
- Add a release note flagging the type change as a breaking change for
  R6, and update Procedure's mappings/examples accordingly.
- Coordinate the change with [FHIR-54703](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54703),
  closing FHIR-54208 as duplicated-by FHIR-54703.

#### Justification

This is the option that actually delivers the harmonization the
community has aligned on (per FHIR-54872's adopted boundary text and
the Observation precedent) and resolves the OO ballot expectation
captured in `#Orders and Observation WG > R6 BodyStructure Review`. It
gives implementers a single, predictable shape for body-site
information across Procedure, Observation, ServiceRequest, ImagingStudy,
ImagingSelection, etc., which is the explicit goal of the pattern. The
breaking-change cost is real but is contained to Procedure and is more
defensible at R6 than carrying an outlier into a normative direction.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — Document the Deviation, Don't Change the Shape

#### Proposal

Keep `Procedure.bodyStructure` as `Reference(BodyStructure)` (0..1) and
keep `Procedure.bodySite` as `CodeableConcept` (0..*), and instead add a
"Notes" / "Boundaries and Relationships" subsection on the Procedure
resource page that:

- Cites the new BodyStructure boundary text adopted under
  [FHIR-54872](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54872).
- States that Procedure intentionally deviates from the
  `CodeableReference(BodyStructure)` pattern because PC and OO
  deliberately split the cardinalities (`bodySite` 0..*,
  `bodyStructure` 0..1), per [FHIR-51792](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51792)
  reverting [FHIR-41690](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-41690).
- Gives implementers explicit guidance on when to populate `bodySite`
  vs. `bodyStructure` vs. both.

Mark FHIR-54208 as Persuasive with Modification (text-only change), and
note that the structural harmonization is being tracked separately by
FHIR-54703.

#### Justification

The ticket itself offers this as an acceptable resolution ("Please
correct it or provide explanation … why the resource deviates"). It
honours the prior PC/OO consensus captured in FHIR-51792 and the Zulip
thread, which explicitly chose the current shape for cardinality
reasons. It also avoids a breaking type change in Procedure during R6
ballot when the underlying harmonization question is already being
worked separately under FHIR-54703 (which carries higher priority and
broader scope, including the FHIR-54621 cardinality question). The
trade-off is that Procedure remains an outlier in shape; that is
mitigated by clear on-page guidance.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (Duplicate of FHIR-54703)

#### Proposal

Resolve FHIR-54208 as **Not Persuasive — Duplicate**, on the basis that
[FHIR-54703](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54703) is the authoritative
ticket for harmonizing `Procedure.bodyStructure` (and
`Observation.bodyStructure`), is already at Highest priority in the
Patient Care queue, and explicitly enumerates the four open questions
(deprecate `bodySite`?, clarify boundaries, settle cardinality via
FHIR-54621, change to `CodeableReference`). Link FHIR-54208 to
FHIR-54703 and close.

#### Justification

Closing as duplicate prevents two parallel decisions on the same
element and concentrates the discussion (especially the cardinality and
deprecation questions) in one place. The reporter's underlying concern
is fully captured by FHIR-54703 and the cross-WG ballot expectation is
already on record. Declining as duplicate is defensible because no
substantive information would be lost, and the WG is not foregoing the
change — it is choosing the better-scoped ticket to make it.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B (with FHIR-54703 carrying the structural
change).

The community has already aligned on the destination (Disposition A's
end state), and FHIR-54703 is the right vehicle for getting Procedure
there because it explicitly couples the type change with the
deprecation, boundary, and cardinality questions that Procedure
genuinely needs answered (per FHIR-51792 and FHIR-54621). Doing the
structural change inside FHIR-54208 would either duplicate that work or
make it in isolation from the cardinality decision, which is exactly
how the current outlier shape arose in the first place. Disposition C
(pure duplicate close) is tempting but loses something the reporter
explicitly asked for: an in-spec rationale for the deviation. R6 readers
encountering Procedure today see an unexplained mismatch with
Observation; Disposition B fixes that immediately by writing the
deviation into Notes and citing FHIR-51792, while letting FHIR-54703
drive the eventual harmonization. If the WG decides at ballot that it is
ready to commit to the structural change now, the right move is to
escalate FHIR-54703 (not FHIR-54208) to disposition A.
