## Summary

The reporter (Elliot Silver) observes that none of the existing date elements
on `Condition` cleanly represent the **date a condition was diagnosed**.
`Condition.recordedDate` captures when the assertion was *entered into the
system* and `Condition.onset[x]` captures when the condition is believed to
have *started in the patient* — neither is the same as the date the diagnosis
was made by a clinician. The example given is an adult patient who reports
having been diagnosed as a teenager: recordedDate would be decades later, and
onset may even precede the diagnosis. The reporter notes that the existing
"asserted date" extension also drifts toward "recording" semantics rather
than "diagnosis", and proposes either (a) a new core choice element on
`Condition` modelled like `onset[x]` / `abatement[x]`, or (b) a standard
extension for *date of diagnosis* if the use case is judged to be outside
the 80%.

This request sits squarely on top of a long-running thread of related Patient
Care tickets that have repeatedly tried to disentangle the same four
concepts — onset, assertion, diagnosis, and recording:

- [FHIR-41130](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-41130) — *Problem Date of
  Diagnosis / Date Recorded Missing.* Closed *Persuasive with Modification*.
  USCDI requires "date of diagnosis" support; that ticket addressed the
  CDA→FHIR mapping side (`/author/time` → `Condition.recordedDate`) but
  explicitly left the underlying "where does diagnosis date live" problem
  open and pointed at the `assertedDate` extension.
- [FHIR-33180](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-33180) — clarified the
  boundary between `Condition.recordedDate` and the `assertedDate`
  extension, defining `assertedDate` as "when the assertion was made" and
  `recordedDate` as "when it was entered into the system".
- [FHIR-40864](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-40864) — merged the
  `assertedDate` extension across `AllergyIntolerance` and `Condition` and
  re-defined it as *"when the asserter identified the … condition,
  problem, or diagnosis or other event … that may have risen to a level of
  or remains a concern"* — i.e. the moment the clinician started to follow
  the issue, **not** the moment a formal diagnosis was made.
- [FHIR-17285](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-17285) — clarified the
  interaction of `clinicalStatus` and `assertedDate`.
- [FHIR-50437](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50437) — clarified that
  `Condition.onsetRange` / `abatementRange` are useful for representing
  *ages* (e.g., "started when I was 20"), reinforcing that onset is about
  the patient timeline rather than the clinician timeline.

Taken together, the existing date model on `Condition` distinguishes
*onset* (patient timeline), *assertion* (clinician started following the
problem), and *recording* (system data entry) — but no element directly
represents *diagnosis* (the formal clinical determination). FHIR-52927 asks
the workgroup to either fill that gap with a new core element or with a
standard extension.

## Details

**Description:**

> None of the existing date elements of Condition record the date of
> diagnosis.
>
> Consider an adult patient who reports they were diagnosed as a teenager.
>
> Recorded date would be years after diagnosis. Onset date might be close
> to diagnosis date, but some conditions are present for years before
> diagnosis.
>
> Creation of an asserted date to parallel asserter initially looks
> promising, but again the assertion of the condition is like closer to
> the recording date.
>
> Add an element to record diagnosis. This should probably a choice type
> similar to onset or abatement.
>
> Alternatively if the determination is made that this is not in the 80%
> (which it seems to me it would be, but then I can't figure out how we
> got to this point without needing it), then create a standard extension
> for date of diagnosis.

### [FHIR-33180: Further clarify boundaries between Condition assertedDate (extension) and recordedDate](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-33180)

Established the *current* working definitions: `assertedDate` = when the
human assertion was made; `recordedDate` = when the system received it.
Closed *Applied / Persuasive with Modification*. Important because it
establishes that "assertion" and "diagnosis" are explicitly **not**
synonymous in the current model — supporting the reporter's claim that
`assertedDate` is the wrong slot for diagnosis date.

Participants: Michelle Miller (reporter), Patient Care WG.

### [FHIR-40864: Merge AssertedDate extensions for AllergyIntolerance and Condition](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-40864)

Merged the two extensions (surviving in
`hl7.fhir.uv.extensions` → `condition-assertedDate`) and locked in the
revised definition: *"When the asserter identified the allergy,
intolerance, condition, problem, or diagnosis or other event … that may
have risen to a level of or remains a concern. For example, when the
patient experiences chest pain, the asserted date represents when the
clinician began following the chest pain — not when the patient
experienced the chest pain."* This wording confirms that `assertedDate`
covers "started following the issue", which is upstream of, and distinct
from, "made the diagnosis". Closed *Applied / Persuasive with
Modification*.

Participants: Michelle Miller, Russ Leftwich, Grahame Grieve, Patient Care WG.

### [FHIR-17285: Clarify the intent and definitions/descriptions of clinicalStatus and assertedDate](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-17285)

Older clarifying ticket addressing how `clinicalStatus` evolves over time
relative to `assertedDate`. Resolved *Persuasive*. Useful background for
showing that the workgroup has already accumulated a non-trivial body of
clarifying language in this area.

Participants: Robert Hausam (reporter), Patient Care WG.

### [FHIR-50437: Clarify how Condition.onsetRange and abatementRange are meant to be used](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50437)

Clarified that `onsetRange` / `abatementRange` are used for *ages*, e.g.
"started when I was 20". Resolved *Applied / Persuasive with Modification*.
Relevant to FHIR-52927 because if the workgroup chooses Disposition A (a

Participants: Vadim Peretokin (reporter), Patient Care WG.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a new element `Condition.diagnosed[x]` (cardinality `0..1`) to the
core `Condition` resource, modelled on `onset[x]` and `abatement[x]` with
the same choice-type set: `dateTime | Age | Period | Range | string`. Its
definition would be: *"Estimated or actual date/date-time/age the
condition was clinically diagnosed. This is distinct from `onset[x]` (when
the condition began in the patient), the `assertedDate` extension (when
the asserter began following the concern), and `recordedDate` (when the
record was entered into the system)."* Update the `Condition` narrative to
contrast the four time concepts side-by-side, and update mapping tables
(HL7 v2 DG1-5, CDA, openEHR) to point `diagnosis date` at the new
element. Mark prior US Core guidance ("there is no single element …") as
superseded.

#### Justification

The 2025 Zulip thread plus FHIR-41130 plus the US Core profile note all
demonstrate that implementers are routinely looking for this element and
not finding it; the gap has now been visible for five years across HL7 v2
mapping, USCDI, and US Core. The reporter's comment that *"I can't figure
out how we got to this point without needing it"* is well-founded: the
existing dates each measure something else (FHIR-33180 and FHIR-40864
explicitly draw those boundaries). A `diagnosed[x]` choice with the same
type set as `onset[x]` is consistent with established `Condition`
patterns, allows for fuzzy values such as *"diagnosed as a teenager"*
(directly leveraging the `Range`/`Age` clarification from FHIR-50437),
and removes ambiguity that today is being papered over by per-IG profile
notes. As a purely additive optional element it is non-breaking for
existing implementations.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Do **not** add a core element. Instead, define a new standard extension
`http://hl7.org/fhir/StructureDefinition/condition-diagnosedDate` (in
`hl7.fhir.uv.extensions`, parallel to the existing
`condition-assertedDate`) with `value[x]` = `dateTime | Period | Age |
Range`. Update the `Condition` page to add a comparison table of the four
date concepts (onset / diagnosed / asserted / recorded) and to point at
the new extension for diagnosis date. Encourage US Core / USCDI to bind
"date of diagnosis" to this extension explicitly so implementers stop
overloading `recordedDate` or `assertedDate`.

#### Justification

The reporter themselves acknowledges that an extension is the fall-back
if the workgroup judges this not to be in the 80%. There is real evidence
that demand is jurisdictional and use-case-driven: USCDI cares (FHIR-41130),
HL7 v2 DG1-5 mappers care (Zulip thread), but other realms have not
historically asked for it — Patient Care has accumulated five tickets in
this space without ever adding a core element, which is a signal. An
extension is fully expressive, leaves the core resource stable, fits the
*existing* family of `condition-*Date` extensions (the merged
`assertedDate` extension from FHIR-40864 already establishes the
pattern), and avoids reopening Condition's core schema right after it has
been clarified by FHIR-33180 / FHIR-40864 / FHIR-50437. It is also less
risky for downstream IGs that have just stabilised against the current
date model.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline as *already addressed by existing mechanisms*. Document, on the
`Condition` page and on the `condition-assertedDate` extension page, an
explicit comparison of `onset[x]`, `assertedDate` extension, and
`recordedDate`, with implementation guidance that *"date of diagnosis"*
should be represented by `assertedDate` for "when the diagnosis was
made/asserted", or by `onset[x]` (with a fuzzy `Age`/`Range` value per
FHIR-50437) for "when the patient was first diagnosed", depending on
which semantic the implementer needs. No new core element or new
extension is added.

#### Justification

Patient Care has, through FHIR-33180 and FHIR-40864, deliberately defined
`assertedDate` as the "human assertion" date — and the merged definition
explicitly names *"diagnosis"* as one of the things being identified. A
defensible reading is that `assertedDate` already covers the diagnosis
case once the asserter is the diagnosing clinician. Adding a fourth date
risks further muddying an area the workgroup has spent multiple ballot
cycles trying to clarify, and would force every implementer and every IG
to re-decide which of four near-synonymous date elements they support.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The underlying need is real and well-evidenced — the same gap has been
flagged in 2020, 2023 (FHIR-41130), and 2025 (this ticket and the Zulip
thread), and US Core itself documents the absence — so Disposition C
(*Decline*) is hard to defend. However, Disposition A (*new core
element*) is heavier than the evidence currently supports: the recent
clarification work in FHIR-33180 and FHIR-40864 only just re-stabilised
the date model on `Condition`, and the merged `assertedDate` extension
deliberately broadened its language to cover "diagnosis" without
promoting it to a core element. A standard extension
(`condition-diagnosedDate`) in `hl7.fhir.uv.extensions`, alongside a
clear comparison table on the `Condition` page, gives implementers an
unambiguous, normative target for "date of diagnosis" without disturbing
the core resource, mirrors the pattern Patient Care already chose for
`assertedDate`, and remains forward-compatible with promoting the
concept to a core `diagnosed[x]` element in a future version if uptake
shows it belongs in the 80%. The workgroup should also explicitly invite
US Core / USCDI to point at this extension to retire the current
"there is no single element" guidance.
