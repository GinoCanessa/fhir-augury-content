## Summary

Ben Hamlin (reporter) raises two intertwined concerns about the PACIO
Transitions of Care (TOC) Implementation Guide. First, he asks PACIO to
ensure that a care plan is tied to a *patient outcome* health record so
that **one** longitudinal care plan follows the patient across settings,
rather than multiple parallel care plans authored by different
professionals tied to their own role/setting. As part of that, all
assessments, patient goals, and patient-reported outcomes (PROs) should
be centralized and tracked by the patient (or their advocate). Second,
he argues the IG's "discipline-specific information" model — which today
ties information to seven role types across post-acute care settings —
should be made inclusive and additive of *emerging* navigation roles
such as Board Certified Health & Wellness coaches, life coaches, social
workers, and community health workers acting as agents for the patient
in their health journey.

No related Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were surfaced
through cross-references, so this prep relies solely on the ticket
content.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the PACIO Transitions of Care IG to (a) explicitly assert a
"single longitudinal CarePlan per patient" pattern for the TOC scope —
profiling `CarePlan` (e.g., a `PACIOLongitudinalCarePlan` profile) so
that role-/setting-specific contributions are represented as
`CarePlan.activity` entries, `CarePlan.contributor` references, and/or
sub-plans linked via `CarePlan.partOf`, with the patient/advocate
identifiable as the subject of record for assessments, `Goal`
instances, and patient-reported `Observation`s; and (b) broaden the
discipline-specific information model so the seven existing post-acute
role types are additive rather than exhaustive. Concretely, replace any
closed/required bindings on practitioner-role / care-team-member
value sets with **extensible** bindings, and add codes (or a companion
extension) covering health navigators, Board Certified Health &
Wellness coaches, life coaches, social workers, and community health
workers as legitimate `CareTeam.participant` / `PractitionerRole.code`
values within the TOC IG.

#### Justification

A single, patient-anchored care plan with role-tagged contributions is
consistent with FHIR's existing `CarePlan` / `CareTeam` design (subject
is `Patient`, contributors and team members carry role context) and
with broader patient-centered care movement that PACIO already cites.
Making the role/discipline value sets extensible — rather than closed
to seven post-acute role types — follows standard FHIR terminology
binding guidance and lets the IG accommodate emerging navigation roles
without a new ballot cycle each time a credential category gains
traction.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Treat the request as **two separate work items** and address only the
"emerging roles" part inside the PACIO TOC IG, while routing the
"single longitudinal care plan" concern to a cross-IG conversation
(PACIO Steering / Patient Care WG joint, with Care Coordination
Services and the US Core CarePlan profile authors). For the in-scope
part, add an extensible value set of "TOC participant roles" that
includes the seven existing post-acute role types **plus** health
navigator, Board Certified Health & Wellness coach, life coach, social
worker, and community health worker, and bind it from the TOC IG's
`CareTeam.participant.role` (and any role slots in discipline-specific
profiles). Leave the "one care plan follows the patient" architectural
position as a published narrative principle in the IG's introduction,
with normative requirements deferred until the cross-IG discussion
concludes.

#### Justification

The "one care plan per patient" assertion is bigger than the PACIO TOC
IG — it touches US Core, Care Plan-related IGs, and real-world EHR
behavior where multiple `CarePlan` instances coexist by design (per
condition, per encounter, per program). Forcing it normatively inside
TOC risks conflicting with those existing patterns and creates
implementer pushback that could stall the role-inclusivity change,
which is independently valuable and lower-risk. Splitting lets PACIO
ship the role-extensibility win quickly while the longitudinal-plan
question gets the multi-stakeholder design it actually needs.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the ticket as **out of scope / not actionable as written**, on
the grounds that (i) the "one care plan follows the patient" requirement
is a cross-cutting policy/architectural position that belongs to base
FHIR `CarePlan` / Care Coordination work rather than PACIO TOC, and
(ii) the "emerging roles" ask does not identify which specific PACIO
TOC artifact, profile, value set, or element is too restrictive, so
there is no concrete change to apply. Invite the reporter to refile as
one or more focused tickets that name the specific profile/value set
to change and supply candidate codes (with a code system) for the
proposed additional roles.

#### Justification

Per HL7 ticket triage practice, change requests need a concrete target
artifact and a proposed change to be acted on. As written, the ticket
mixes a broad architectural assertion with a roles concern but does
not point to a specific PACIO TOC profile, value set, or page. Acting
on the architectural half could conflict with `CarePlan` patterns
already in use across other IGs, and acting on the roles half without
a named value set risks the WG choosing the wrong binding to widen.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B captures the legitimate, ballot-ready improvement (open
the post-acute role taxonomy so emerging navigation roles can
participate in TOC `CareTeam` / discipline-specific profiles) while
honestly acknowledging that "one longitudinal care plan per patient"
is a multi-IG design decision that PACIO TOC alone cannot impose
without conflicting with `CarePlan` usage elsewhere. It also gives the
reporter visible progress on the part of the request that is concretely
implementable in this IG, which is more constructive than declining
outright (Disposition C) and less risky than accepting the full
architectural assertion normatively in PACIO TOC (Disposition A).
Workgroup discussion should focus on naming the specific
`CareTeam.participant.role` (or equivalent) binding to widen and on
who PACIO should engage to scope the longitudinal-care-plan
conversation.
