## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt an editorial change to section 1.1.1, paragraph 3 of the
Ophthalmology Retinal (FHIR) IG so the truncated final sentence is made
grammatically complete. Replace the existing fragment

> … to address the data exchange problems through semantically interoperable .

with a concrete, complete formulation supplied by the IG authors —
recommended wording:

> … to address the data exchange problems through semantically interoperable
> exchange of clinical ophthalmic data.

The change is purely narrative (no resource, profile, value-set, or
constraint impact), edits the IG's introduction page only, and should be
applied as a single text edit during the next publication of the
Ophthalmology Retinal IG.

#### Justification

The reporter has identified a clear and uncontested defect — the sentence
literally ends with the adjective phrase "semantically interoperable" and
no noun. Fixing it preserves the author's intent, removes a reader-facing
embarrassment in the published IG, and carries no normative risk because
nothing about FHIR resources, profiles, or conformance is being changed.
Editorial corrections of this kind are routinely accepted by Patient Care
without further debate, and the reporter has already done the diagnostic
work; only the missing noun phrase needs to be supplied by the IG
authors.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than patching the single sentence in isolation, reword paragraph 3
of section 1.1.1 in full. Have the Ophthalmology Retinal IG editors
rewrite the paragraph so that (a) the truncated sentence is replaced with
a complete one and (b) the paragraph as a whole is reviewed for clarity,
including verifying that the surrounding sentences still flow correctly
once the noun phrase is restored. The output is still a narrative-only
change to section 1.1.1 of the IG, but scoped to the paragraph rather
than to a one-word insertion.

#### Justification

Because the original sentence was published with the noun missing, no
external reader can be sure what the authors intended; the "???" in the
proposed wording is the reporter's way of acknowledging that gap. A
paragraph-level rewrite gives the authors room to reconstruct the intent
faithfully (e.g., "semantically interoperable exchange of ophthalmic
clinical data", "semantically interoperable patient summaries", etc.)
and to verify the surrounding sentences still read well after the fix.
Trade-off vs. Disposition A: marginally more editorial effort, but it
avoids shipping a one-word patch that could itself be slightly off-key.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the ticket as **Not Persuasive**, on the basis that the
Ophthalmology Retinal (FHIR) IG is no longer being actively maintained
under Patient Care or its sponsoring work group, and that committing
editorial effort to an unmaintained IG is not justified. Close the
ticket with a note pointing readers to whatever current ophthalmology
artifact (if any) supersedes the affected paragraph.

#### Justification

This disposition is only defensible if the work group confirms that the
Ophthalmology Retinal IG is effectively deprecated or dormant. The
ticket has been in `Triaged` status with no comments and no
cross-references since 2021, which is consistent with an artifact that
no one is actively shepherding. If that read is correct, declining the
fix is honest about the IG's lifecycle; if it is incorrect, this
disposition should not be selected.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

This is a textbook editorial correction: a single sentence in the IG
narrative ends mid-phrase, the reporter has pinpointed exactly where,
and there is no normative or technical content at stake. Disposition A
delivers the fix the reporter asked for with the smallest possible
footprint and the lowest risk of unintended changes elsewhere in the
paragraph. Disposition B is a reasonable upgrade only if the IG editors
feel the surrounding sentences also need a polish pass; if so, the work
group can adopt B without changing the underlying decision to fix the
defect. Disposition C should only be selected if Patient Care formally
confirms the Ophthalmology Retinal IG is no longer maintained — absent
that confirmation, leaving a visibly broken sentence in a published HL7
artifact is the worse outcome.
