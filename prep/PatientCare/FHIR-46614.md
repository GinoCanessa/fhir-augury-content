## Summary

The submitter (Russell Leftwich) asks that the cardinality of
`ClinicalImpression.previous` be widened from `0..1` to `0..*`. The
rationale is that more than one previous assessment may be relevant to
a given `ClinicalImpression` — not just the single most recent one.
Multiple prior assessments may have been performed in the same time
frame, may have been authored by different practitioners, and may all
be relevant to the same problem(s) the new impression addresses.

The closely related ticket
[FHIR-50413](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50413) ("Change previous
to relatesTo", reported by Diana Ovelgoenne) approaches the same
underlying need from a different angle: rather than just changing
cardinality, it proposes restructuring the element along the lines of
`DiagnosticReport.relatesTo`, so that an impression can refer to
multiple earlier assessments with explicit relationship semantics.
FHIR-50413 has been **resolved Persuasive with Modification** by
Patient Care on 2025-11-20: the resolution is to **remove `previous`
and add a new element `comparesTo` with cardinality `0..*` and type
`Reference(RelatedArtifact | ClinicalAssessment)`**. That resolution
therefore already absorbs the cardinality change requested in this
ticket; the open question for FHIR-46614 is whether to align with the
FHIR-50413 disposition or pursue the simpler standalone cardinality
bump.

## Related Jira Tickets

### [FHIR-50413: Change previous to relatesTo](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50413)

Open by Diana Ovelgoenne (2025-04-28), now **Resolved – change
required / Persuasive with Modification** as of 2025-11-20. The ticket
proposed restructuring `ClinicalImpression.previous` along the lines
of `DiagnosticReport.relatesTo` so that comparisons across multiple
timepoints (e.g. oncology, cardiac, orthopedic progression monitoring)
can be expressed. After three Patient Care discussions (2025-08-28,
2025-11-13, 2025-11-20), the agreed disposition is: remove `previous`,
introduce `comparesTo` with cardinality `0..*` and type
`Reference(RelatedArtifact | ClinicalAssessment)`, with a description
explicitly noting that assessments are ongoing and that a clinician
makes new assessments as the patient's condition changes. Because that
resolution already includes the `0..*` cardinality requested here,
FHIR-46614 is effectively superseded by FHIR-50413's resolution.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Change the cardinality of `ClinicalImpression.previous` from `0..1` to
`0..*` in FHIR Core, leaving the element name, type
(`Reference(ClinicalImpression)`), and short/definition otherwise
unchanged. Update the element's definition to clarify that multiple
prior `ClinicalImpression` instances may be referenced when more than
one earlier assessment is relevant (e.g., concurrent assessments by
different authors addressing the same problem set, or a fan-out of
prior assessments rather than a strictly linear chain).

#### Justification

This is the minimal change that addresses the submitter's stated need
and is consistent with how FHIR generally treats "prior" or "related"
references on resources where one-to-many is plausible (e.g., the
`relatesTo` patterns on `DiagnosticReport` and `Observation.derivedFrom`).
The submitter's rationale — that prior assessments can come from
different authors in the same time frame and still all be relevant —
is not satisfied by walking a single linked-list chain via the current
`0..1` cardinality, since that chain implies a single authoritative
predecessor.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach (recommended — align with FHIR-50413)

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-46614 as **Persuasive – No Change Required, superseded by
FHIR-50413**. The change agreed in FHIR-50413 already delivers what
this ticket asks for and more: `previous` is removed and replaced with
a new element `comparesTo` of cardinality `0..*` and type
`Reference(RelatedArtifact | ClinicalAssessment)`, with a description
making the multi-prior-assessment use case explicit. Add a note to
FHIR-46614 pointing at the FHIR-50413 resolution and confirming that
the cardinality concern is fully absorbed.

#### Justification

FHIR-50413 was resolved by Patient Care on 2025-11-20 as Persuasive
with Modification with a concrete spec edit that includes the `0..*`
cardinality requested here. Accepting FHIR-46614 separately as a
cardinality-only change (Disposition A) would either duplicate that
work or — worse — produce a conflicting resolution if it landed first
and locked in the `previous` name and `Reference(ClinicalImpression)`
type that FHIR-50413 has just decided to retire. Closing this ticket
against the FHIR-50413 resolution keeps the spec change coherent and
records the submitter's use case as part of the rationale for
`comparesTo`. The Patient Care discussion notes also explicitly
reflect Russell's concern (multiple prior assessments, "set" rather
than "chain"), so the substance of FHIR-46614 is already accounted
for.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark FHIR-46614 as **Not Persuasive** on the grounds that the existing
`0..1` `previous` element, combined with chain-walking through each
prior `ClinicalImpression.previous`, is sufficient, and that any
additional prior assessments that informed the current impression
should be referenced via existing mechanisms such as
`ClinicalImpression.problem`, `.investigation`, `.protocol`, or a
generic `RelatedArtifact`-style extension rather than by widening
`previous` cardinality.

#### Justification

This would be defensible only if Patient Care wanted to preserve the
narrow "immediate predecessor" semantics of `previous` and push
broader "this impression was informed by these other assessments" use
cases to other elements or to an extension. However, the workgroup's
own discussion on FHIR-50413 (28 Aug, 13 Nov, 20 Nov 2025) explicitly
rejected this interpretation in favour of widening to `0..*`, so a
Decline disposition would contradict the workgroup's recent direction
and is included here only for completeness.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The substantive question raised by FHIR-46614 — "should an impression
be able to point at more than one prior assessment?" — has already been
resolved affirmatively by Patient Care via FHIR-50413, which goes
further than this ticket by also renaming the element to `comparesTo`,
broadening the target type to include `RelatedArtifact`, and tightening
the description. Resolving FHIR-46614 separately as a simple
cardinality bump (Disposition A) risks introducing a conflicting
artifact change in the same release window, and Disposition C would
contradict Patient Care's just-issued resolution on FHIR-50413. Closing
FHIR-46614 as superseded by FHIR-50413, with an explicit cross-link
and acknowledgement that the cardinality concern is satisfied, is the
cleanest outcome and preserves the submitter's contribution as part of
the rationale record.
