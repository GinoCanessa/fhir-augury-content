## Summary

Daniel Vreeman, on behalf of the reviewers of the PACIO Cognitive Status
Implementation Guide, requests that the IG include a published "future
roadmap" describing what the PACIO project plans to address beyond the
current scope. The reporter notes that PACIO is making incremental
progress toward interoperability of post-acute care content, but the
scope of the present IG is unclear. A roadmap section would let
implementers and reviewers understand what is intentionally out of scope
for STU1 (e.g., Instrumental ADLs, communication, sensory impairments)
and what the project intends to tackle next. The ticket was originally
dispositioned "Considered for Future Use" in June 2021 (Wan/Lyle 6-0-0)
with the rationale that emerging run-time filtered value sets in FHIR
might reduce the need for separate cognitive/functional status profiles,
then re-opened by Lynn Laakso in November 2021 for consideration after
STU1 publication. No related Jira tickets, Zulip discussions, or GitHub
items were surfaced through cross-references or content search.

## Details

**Description:**

The PACIO project is making incremental progress towards interoperability
of important content. As noted in our previous comment, the scope of the
present IG is unclear. In addition to the recommendations about defining
scope, it would be useful to include a future roadmap that outlines more
of "what's coming" to help understand what is not covered by the present
guide. For example, the roadmap might include Instrumental ADLs,
communication, sensory impairments, or other future efforts.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a new "Future Roadmap" page (or section in the IG home/scope page)
to the US PACIO Cognitive Status IG. The page should enumerate, at a
minimum: (a) topics intentionally excluded from STU1 (e.g., Instrumental
ADLs, communication assessments, sensory impairments) with brief
rationale; (b) planned PACIO follow-on work and the anticipated sequence
(e.g., functional status, additional PAC domains); and (c) the
relationship to cross-cutting FHIR efforts the project intends to ride
on, in particular run-time filtered value sets (the basis of the
original "Considered for Future Use" disposition). Cross-link the new
page from the existing Scope section so reviewers encounter it
immediately after reading what *is* in scope.

#### Justification

The reporter's underlying complaint is that readers cannot tell what is
deliberately out of scope versus simply unaddressed. A narrative roadmap
is the standard FHIR IG mechanism for communicating that and is
non-normative, so it carries no conformance risk. It also preserves the
substance of the prior 6-0-0 vote, which acknowledged that future PAC
profile work is expected to depend on run-time filtered value sets — that
dependency is exactly the kind of forward-looking context the requested
roadmap is designed to capture. Adding it post-STU1 (per Lynn Laakso's
re-open) is low-cost and improves implementer confidence without
constraining future technical decisions.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Instead of a free-standing roadmap, expand the existing **Scope**
section of the IG to (i) explicitly list out-of-scope domains
(Instrumental ADLs, communication, sensory impairments, etc.) with a
one-line "why deferred" note, and (ii) point to the PACIO project's
external project page / confluence space as the canonical, living source
of roadmap information. Optionally add a short "Project Direction"
paragraph that references the run-time filtered value sets initiative as
the anticipated technical foundation for future profiles, without
committing the IG itself to a delivery schedule.

#### Justification

Roadmaps embedded in published IGs age poorly: once an IG is balloted
and published, the roadmap is frozen until the next publication, while
the actual project plan continues to evolve. Pushing the living roadmap
to the project's external site (where PACIO already coordinates work)
keeps it current and avoids creating a maintenance obligation tied to
the IG release cycle. Tightening the in-IG Scope section addresses the
reporter's core concern — "the scope of the present IG is unclear" —
without conflating normative scope with aspirational planning. This also
honors the original committee concern that future PAC work may be
restructured around run-time filtered value sets, which would make any
detailed in-IG roadmap obsolete.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline to add a roadmap to the IG, on the grounds that (a) the original
2021-06-28 disposition ("Considered for Future Use", 6-0-0) already
recorded the committee's view that future PAC profile direction is
contingent on the run-time filtered value sets work, and committing to a
roadmap now would pre-judge that architecture; and (b) roadmap content
is properly the responsibility of the PACIO project and HL7 Patient Care
governance pages, not of a normative IG.

#### Justification

The committee already weighed this request once and concluded the
technical path forward was too uncertain to commit to publicly. That
uncertainty has not materially changed: run-time filtered value sets
remain in development, and any concrete roadmap published in the IG
would either repeat that uncertainty (adding little) or commit the
project to a direction it may need to revise. Declining keeps the IG
focused on what it normatively specifies and avoids creating implementer
expectations that the workgroup cannot reliably meet across publication
cycles.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B best reconciles the reporter's legitimate concern (unclear
scope) with the substantive reason the committee originally deferred the
request (uncertainty about the run-time filtered value sets foundation,
per the 2021-06-28 vote). Tightening the IG's Scope section to call out
explicitly-deferred domains is cheap, durable across publications, and
directly answers "what is *not* covered by the present guide?" Pointing
to an external PACIO project page for the living roadmap avoids baking a
volatile plan into a normative artifact, which was the committee's
real objection in 2021. Disposition A is reasonable but creates a
maintenance burden the IG team cannot easily meet between releases;
Disposition C ignores Lynn Laakso's deliberate re-open post-STU1, which
signals the workgroup wants *some* response, not silence.
