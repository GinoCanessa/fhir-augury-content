## Summary

Corey Spears (Patient Care, against the **US SDOH Clinical Care (SDOHCC)** IG)
asks the workgroup to broaden the resource types allowed as the `subject` (and
potentially other participants) on SDOHCC profiles. Today, several SDOHCC
profiles — for example `SDOHCC-Condition` and `SDOHCC-ServiceRequest` —
constrain `subject` to `US Core Patient`, which prevents SDOH data from being
recorded against non-individual subjects such as a **family**, **community**,
**household**, or other group-level entity that the social-services domain
routinely needs to describe. The reporter explicitly calls out coordination
with the **Human and Social Services (HSS) WG** and the **Social Services
Domain Analysis Model (DAM)** project, and notes that the review should not be
limited to the few profiles he flagged but should sweep all SDOHCC profiles
that carry a `subject` or participant reference. The only structured
cross-reference attached to the ticket is the FHIR artifact `Condition.subject`,
which corroborates that the immediate evidence sits on `SDOHCC-Condition`'s
`subject` slice. There are no linked Jira tickets, no Zulip threads, and no
GitHub PRs/issues currently associated with this ticket.

## Details

**Description:**

> Several SDOH CC Profiles restrict the resource types allowed for subject and possibly other participants.
>
> An example of this is the Condition.subject and ServiceRequest are restricted to US Core Patient.
>
> To support subjects/participants that may represent more than an individual, such as may be needed to represent family, community, or others, please review the constraints for the resource reference types across the profiles. This should involve working with the HSS WG and consider the work being done in the Social Services Domain Analysis Model (DAM) project.
>
> I have identified a few of the profiles that need to be considered, but this should be done across all profiles that include subjects/participants.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Conduct a sweep of every profile in **US SDOH Clinical Care** that carries a
`subject` or participant reference (notably `SDOHCC-Condition`,
`SDOHCC-ServiceRequest`, `SDOHCC-Procedure`, `SDOHCC-Goal`,
`SDOHCC-ObservationScreeningResponse`, `SDOHCC-ObservationAssessment`,
`SDOHCC-Task...`, and any `*-Reference` carrier extensions) and broaden the
allowed `targetProfile` set on the relevant ElementDefinitions. Concretely, on
each profile's `subject` (and analogous participant) element, replace the
current `Reference(US Core Patient)` constraint with `Reference(US Core
Patient | Group)` at minimum, and consider adding `RelatedPerson` and/or
`Patient` (base) where DAM-driven use cases support it. Coordinate with the
HSS WG and align target profiles with the Social Services DAM so that
"family", "community", and "household" are representable via `Group` (with
appropriate `Group.type`/`Group.code` guidance) rather than inventing new
SDOHCC-specific subject types. Update the IG narrative pages for each profile
to explain the broadened cardinality and provide a worked example for a
non-individual subject.

#### Justification

This is the literal ask, and it is well-grounded: the base FHIR resources
(`Condition.subject`, `ServiceRequest.subject`, `Observation.subject`, etc.)
already permit `Group` and other non-Patient targets, so the SDOHCC restriction
to `US Core Patient` is a profile-level narrowing rather than a base-spec
limitation. Loosening it back toward base FHIR is a non-breaking *expansion*
for senders (existing instances remain valid) and aligns SDOHCC with the
realities of SDOH data, where household- and community-level interventions are
common. Coordinating with HSS/DAM is what the reporter explicitly requests and
avoids SDOHCC inventing a parallel modeling approach.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Instead of broadening `subject` on every SDOHCC profile, keep `subject`
constrained to `US Core Patient` (preserving today's strong US Core
alignment) and introduce an **explicit "non-individual subject" extension**
(e.g., `sdohcc-non-individual-subject` of type `Reference(Group |
RelatedPerson)`) that profiles can carry alongside the patient subject.
Document a clear rule: when the SDOH event truly applies to a family,
community, or household, populate the extension and use a representative or
proxy `Patient` (or omit `subject` where cardinality permits and rely on the
extension). Pair this with IG guidance and examples developed jointly with
HSS/DAM.

#### Justification

This preserves backward compatibility with US Core consumers that today rely
on `subject` always resolving to a `US Core Patient` (some validators, FHIR
analytics pipelines, and downstream USCDI-aligned systems make that
assumption). It also keeps the "SDOH about an individual" path —
overwhelmingly the dominant case — unchanged, while giving a well-scoped,
opt-in mechanism for the minority non-individual case. Trade-off vs.
Disposition A: it adds modeling overhead (a new extension and accompanying
guidance), and consumers must learn to look in two places for the subject;
but it avoids re-profiling many elements at once and reduces the risk of
unintended divergence from US Core's `Reference(US Core Patient)` pattern.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change as currently scoped. Position SDOHCC as an
**individual-focused** SDOH IG and direct family-/community-level use cases
to a separate IG or to the forthcoming Social Services / HSS work products,
so SDOHCC's `subject` constraint to `US Core Patient` remains intact.

#### Justification

A defensible argument is that SDOHCC's value comes from its tight US Core
alignment and that broadening `subject` invites scope creep into population-
and group-level modeling that overlaps with HSS, public-health, and emerging
"community-as-subject" work. Implementers have built around the current
constraint, and changing target profiles across many SDOHCC profiles risks
churn for downstream IGs (e.g., MCC, Gravity-derived content, and any
profiles deriving from SDOHCC) without a corresponding ballot-cycle commitment
to community-subject semantics, value sets, and examples. If the
non-individual use cases are genuinely first-class, they deserve a dedicated
IG (or HSS-owned profiles) rather than a sweep through SDOHCC.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter's framing — "review the constraints across all profiles that
include subjects/participants" and "work with HSS WG and the Social Services
DAM" — is exactly what SDOHCC needs to stay credible as the SDOH IG of
record. The current `US Core Patient`-only constraint is a profile-level
narrowing of a base FHIR element that already permits `Group` and other
targets, so Disposition A is a *relaxation*, not a breaking change: existing
Patient-subject instances stay valid. Disposition B (extension-based opt-in)
preserves US Core alignment but adds permanent modeling debt — two places to
look for "who is this about" — that we will regret as community- and
household-level SDOH interventions become more common. Disposition C punts
the problem and would force implementers to choose between SDOHCC and a
parallel "community SDOH" IG, fragmenting the ecosystem. The right answer is
to do the sweep with HSS, broaden `subject`/participant target profiles to
include `Group` (and `RelatedPerson` where the DAM justifies it), and
document the non-individual pattern in the IG narrative — accepting the
ballot-cycle cost as the price of getting the model right.
