## Summary

Elliot Silver (on behalf of an anaesthesia/cardiac-cath/general-surgery use
case) is asking Patient Care for guidance, and possibly new mechanisms, for
representing a **procedure log** in FHIR — i.e., the time-ordered journal
of fine-grained steps that occur during a larger procedure (e.g.,
anaesthesia, central venous catheterisation, cardiac catheterisation, total
knee arthroplasty). The reporter is unsure whether each recorded step
should itself be a `Procedure` or be an `Observation` of the activity, and
notes that the steps interleave fine-grained body-site references,
multiple devices and sub-device locations (lumens, ports, clips, needle
tip), and physiologic device readings (BP, HR). The ticket explicitly
asks for "addition of guidance on how to record this log using existing
resources, modification of existing resources, or addition of new
resources."

Across the discussion thread the working group has converged on two use
cases:

- **Use Case 1 (less complex steps, e.g., epidural, line placement, basic
  imaging):** combine existing resources — `Observation`, `Procedure`,
  `MedicationAdministration`, `Device` — and group/order them with `List`
  (using `List.orderedBy`), or with `Procedure.partOf` for nested
  sub-procedures. Kevin O'Donnell additionally proposed a lightweight
  timestamped `Procedure.log` element of `CodeableReference` for tiny
  steps that don't merit their own resource.
- **Use Case 2 (complex surgical procedures, e.g., total knee
  replacement):** model as a clinical document using `Composition`, with
  individual `Composition.section`s for pre-op templating, prophylactic
  antibiotics, anaesthesia, patient prep, each operative step, and
  closure. A separate **Anesthesia FHIR IG** is being authored on the HCD
  side to handle persistence/exchange for the anaesthesia case.

The May 2025 OO/PC joint WGM and the September 2025 OO/PC joint WGM (per
Stephen Chu and confirmed by Grant Forrest and Martin Hurrell) recommended
**closing this ticket** by adding guidance text rather than by changing
the spec structurally — specifically, by updating the **Clinical Summary
Module** page and the **Procedure** resource (likely Scope and Usage) to
describe the two recommended approaches above. The June 26, 2026 PC FHIR
call lists the next step as confirming agreement with Elliot and Grant
before bringing it back to PC for vote.

The cross-references surfaced by Augury are all *mentions* (the Confluence
modelling page and the FHIR elements `List.orderedBy`,
`Composition.section`, `Procedure.partOf`, and the proposed
`Procedure.log`); there are no linked Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or
GitHub items.

## Details

**Description:**

> A use case was brought forward for the need to create an anaethesia
> procedure log. (The need is almost identical for cardiac
> catheterization, and it is believed that a general surgery note would
> be similar.)
>
> It isn't clear if each recorded step within the larger procedure is
> itself a procedure, or if it would be recorded as an Observation of
> the activity of the step. (On the surface it would seem preferrable
> that the record of all steps be captured in the same resource type,
> but that should be discussed.)
>
> Attached is a list of steps involved in a procedure, each of which
> should be journaled. Some of the notable aspects of the steps are:
>
> - Internal and external body sites are often precisely identified,
>   often in relation to other body sites or reference points of a
>   device ("insert guidewire through needle [into blood vessel] to a
>   depth of 20 cm")
> - Multiple devices are often referenced in the same step ("advance
>   needle under US guidance", "advance dilator over guidewire", etc.)
> - Current modelling suggests each lumen is modelled as a device, all
>   within a parent catheter device. However "needle tip," "brown port,"
>   "blue/white clip", etc. aren't currently viewed as sub-devices, so
>   it isn't clear how to reference these locations on the devices.
> - Procedure steps may be interlaced in the journal with device
>   readings such as BP or heart rate.
>
> If we use Observation to record these steps, I presume the *subject*
> would be the patient, but it isn't clear what the singular *focus*
> element would be, or if the *device* element would be used (the
> device element is the device making the observation, not being
> manipulated).
>
> We request the addition of guidance on how to record this log using
> existing resources, modification of existing resources, or addition
> of new resources to address this general use case.
>
> *(An attached image illustrates the step list.)*

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the consensus reached at the May 2025 and September 2025 OO/PC
joint WGM sessions and recorded by Stephen Chu: **add narrative guidance,
no resource-structural change.** Specifically:

1. In the **Clinical Summary Module** page (`clinicalsummary-module.html`
   in the FHIR Core spec), add a "Recording procedure logs / journals"
   subsection that describes the two agreed patterns:
   - **Use Case 1 — synoptic step list (less complex procedures):** a
     time-ordered set of `Observation`, `Procedure` (often via
     `Procedure.partOf` for sub-steps), `MedicationAdministration`, and
     `Device` instances, optionally grouped/ordered with a `List`
     resource using `List.orderedBy`. Note that timestamps are required
     on every entry and that physiologic device readings are first-class
     entries in the log.
   - **Use Case 2 — clinical document (complex surgical procedures):** a
     `Composition` with `Composition.section` instances for pre-op
     templating, prophylactic antibiotics, anaesthesia, patient prep,
     individual operative steps (e.g., femoral cut, tibial cut), and
     closure.
2. In the **Procedure** resource (`procedure.html`) Scope-and-Usage
   section, add the same two-pattern guidance, plus an explicit note
   that the resource is intended to capture *that* a procedure
   happened — for fine-grained intra-operative step capture, pair with
   `Procedure.partOf`, link to `Observation`/`MedicationAdministration`,
   and consider a `Composition`-based document for complex cases.
3. Note that the **HCD Anesthesia FHIR IG** is the place where the
   detailed, exchange-focused step models for anaesthesia will live, and
   reference it from the guidance.
4. Cross-reference Andrea Pitkus's specimen/laboratory caveat: this
   guidance is scoped to anaesthetic and surgical procedure logs and
   does **not** prescribe patterns for specimen collection,
   histopathology, public-health reporting, or imaging acquisition
   workflows — those remain out of scope and pending separate analysis.
5. Resolve the ticket as **Persuasive** with the above guidance text.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the ticket asks for ("addition of guidance on how
to record this log using existing resources") and what the workgroup has
already converged on across multiple PC, OO, and joint OO/PC calls and
two WGMs. It is the lowest-risk option: no breaking change to the
Procedure resource, no new resource, no new mandatory elements. It
gives implementers an authoritative reference point for the two
genuinely different problem shapes (synoptic vs document/narrative) and
preserves room for the in-flight HCD Anesthesia IG to do the
domain-specific heavy lifting. The two PC FHIR-call follow-ups (Jun 26
2025, Aug 1 2025) and Sep 17 2025 WGM Q4 already line this up as the
"back to PC for vote" path; Disposition A simply executes that vote.

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt the guidance in Disposition A **and additionally add a new
optional `Procedure.log` element** as proposed by Elliot Silver on
2024-10-18: a timestamped, repeating element whose entries are
`CodeableReference` so each entry can be free text, a coded concept, or
a reference to another resource (sub-`Procedure`,
`MedicationAdministration`, `Observation`, etc.). Concretely:

- `Procedure.log` 0..* BackboneElement
  - `Procedure.log.timestamp` 1..1 dateTime *(or instant)*
  - `Procedure.log.entry` 1..1 CodeableReference (Any)
  - `Procedure.log.note` 0..* Annotation

This change would land in the FHIR Core source for the Procedure
resource (authoring root in `HL7/fhir`); the Clinical Summary Module
and Procedure Scope-and-Usage updates from Disposition A would also be
included. Mark the new element **trial use** so it can mature alongside
the HCD Anesthesia IG.

#### Justification

Elliot's `Procedure.log` proposal gathered supportive engagement
(Martin's "small sub-procedural items work okay as Observations" was a
qualified agreement; nobody objected on the thread). It cleanly handles
the trio Elliot identified at WGM-on-imaging:

- basic diagnostic imaging needs only a tiny log → text/code entries;
- image-guided procedures need a few references → reference entries;
- interventional procedures need the full surgical pattern → still
  available via Composition + sub-Procedures, with the in-resource log
  giving a low-friction landing place.

The trade-off vs Disposition A is non-trivial: it adds a
representation choice (log entries vs sub-Procedures vs sectioned
Composition) and could fragment implementations if the guidance isn't
firm about when to use which. Stephen Chu's "synoptic vs detailed"
framing helps: `Procedure.log` is the **synoptic** lane, sub-Procedures
+ Composition is the **detailed** lane. Mitigation: explicit "when to
use" guidance and trial-use status. This is a more powerful answer to
the literal request than narrative guidance alone, but it raises the
implementation cost and the open-question burden (subject/focus/device
semantics that Elliot called out in the description still need to be
nailed down for a normative shape).

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve as **Not Persuasive — Already Addressed** on the basis that the
existing combination of `Procedure` (with `Procedure.partOf`),
`Observation`, `MedicationAdministration`, `Device`, `List` (with
`List.orderedBy`), and `Composition` (with `Composition.section`)
already provides every capability needed to represent a procedure log,
and that domain-specific patterns belong in implementation guides — in
particular the in-flight **HCD Anesthesia FHIR IG** — rather than in
FHIR Core. Recommend the requester pursue the modelling there. Make no
changes to FHIR Core.

#### Justification

Defensible only on a strict reading of FHIR Core's scope: core
resources should be general-purpose, and *recipes* for combining them
in a domain (anaesthesia, cardiac cath, complex orthopaedics) are the
canonical IG concern. The HCD Anesthesia IG already exists for the
hardest of the three named use cases, and Stephen Chu has explicitly
noted the patterns won't generalise to specimen/lab/imaging workflows
anyway — arguing against any one-size-fits-all guidance in Core.

This is **not recommended**: the ticket has been triaged and discussed
across nine months, the workgroup has reached actual consensus on
adding guidance, and "use existing resources, but figure it out
yourself" is exactly the answer the requester said wasn't enough when
they filed it. Declining now would re-open a closed discussion.

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket has been worked through to a real PC/OO consensus that does
not require changing the Procedure resource: update the **Clinical
Summary Module** narrative and the **Procedure** Scope-and-Usage to
document the two agreed patterns (synoptic = grouped
Observation/Procedure/MedicationAdministration/Device, optionally via
`List`/`Procedure.partOf`; detailed = `Composition` with sectioned
operative content), and reference the HCD Anesthesia IG for the
detailed exchange model. The September 2025 OO/PC joint Q4 confirmed
both Grant Forrest and Martin Hurrell agree with this recap, and PC
already plans to take it to a vote — Disposition A executes the vote
the workgroup queued up.

Disposition B (add `Procedure.log`) is a credible "do more" option and
should be re-raised if the HCD Anesthesia IG and downstream
implementers find the synoptic pattern in Disposition A inadequate in
practice. It is intentionally held back here because (a) the
subject/focus/device semantics that Elliot flagged in the original
description are still genuinely open and would need work to land
cleanly, and (b) the workgroup has already agreed that guidance, not
structure, is the right next step. Disposition C should be rejected
because it returns the requester an answer they have already explicitly
said is insufficient.
