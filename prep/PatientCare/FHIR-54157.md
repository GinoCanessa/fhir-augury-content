## Summary

Eric Haas reports an editorial inconsistency in the Condition resource's
Terminology Bindings table (§ 9.2.4.1). The narrative descriptions for
`Condition.clinicalStatus` and `Condition.category` — which are sourced
from each ValueSet's `description` element — both characterise the binding
as **"Preferred"**, while the actual binding strengths declared on the
`Condition` StructureDefinition are **Required** for `clinicalStatus`
and **Example** for `category`. The reporter proposes editing the two
ValueSet `description` strings so the prose matches the StructureDefinition
binding strengths. No formal Jira links or Zulip cross-references are
recorded for this ticket; however, prior tickets
[FHIR-10272](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-10272) and
[FHIR-10292](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-10292) — both resolved
*Persuasive* — established that `Condition.clinicalStatus` is bound
**Required**, which is the source of today's mismatch with the still
"Preferred"-worded ValueSet description.

## Details

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Edit the `description` element of each affected ValueSet so the prose
matches the binding strength declared on the `Condition`
StructureDefinition:

- `ValueSet/condition-clinical` — change the `description` opener from
  "Preferred value set for Condition Clinical Status" to "**Required**
  value set for Condition Clinical Status."
- `ValueSet/condition-category` — change the `description` opener from
  "Preferred value set for Condition Categories" to "**Example** value
  set for Condition Categories."

No change is made to the `Condition` StructureDefinition itself; only
the two ValueSet narrative descriptions are touched. After regeneration,
§ 9.2.4.1 Terminology Bindings on the Condition page will read
consistently between the strength column (sourced from
StructureDefinition) and the description column (sourced from
ValueSet.description).

#### Justification

This is a pure editorial alignment of two pieces of metadata that are
already supposed to agree. The binding strengths themselves were
deliberately set: *Required* for `clinicalStatus` was the resolution of
[FHIR-10272](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-10272) ("Persuasive — Change
to required"), and *Example* for `category` is consistent with how
Condition categorisation is typically extended in IGs. Updating the
ValueSet `description` is the lowest-risk way to remove reader confusion
and is the change the reporter explicitly proposed. There is no
behavioural impact on instances or validators; only the rendered narrative
on `condition.html` changes.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Fix the underlying defect, not just the symptom: stop sourcing the
"strength" prose from a hand-written `ValueSet.description` and instead
have the publisher render the binding-strength label for the
StructureDefinition-side binding directly into § 9.2.4.1, drawing strictly
from `ElementDefinition.binding.strength`. As part of the same change:

1. Audit `ValueSet.description` elements across all core ValueSets and
   strip leading "Preferred / Required / Example / Extensible …"
   qualifiers — those words describe how a *resource* uses the
   ValueSet, not the ValueSet itself, and belong in the
   StructureDefinition binding, not in the ValueSet narrative.
2. As a one-time correction for this ballot, also apply the FHIR-54157
   text fix to `condition-clinical` and `condition-category` so the page
   is correct in the meantime.

#### Justification

The mismatch reported in FHIR-54157 is not unique to Condition — any
ValueSet whose `description` was authored before its referencing
StructureDefinition's binding strength was changed will drift in exactly
this way. A targeted edit to two ValueSets (Disposition A) leaves the
same trap in place for every other `code`/`CodeableConcept`-typed
element bound to a HL7-authored ValueSet. Treating
`ValueSet.description` as the *ValueSet's* description (its scope,
contents, intent) and treating *binding strength* purely as a property
of the binding is also better aligned with the conceptual model in the
Terminology module. Trade-off vs. A: more publisher/IG-author work and
a wider review surface, with no narrative change for implementers
beyond Condition in this ballot cycle.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline as **out of scope / not actionable as filed**: the reporter is
asking us to overwrite editorial text in two ValueSets to match a
*resource-side* binding strength, but `ValueSet.description` is
authored on the ValueSet (which can be referenced from many resources
at potentially different binding strengths), and rewriting it to assert
"Required" or "Example" pre-commits the ValueSet to that single
strength. Close the ticket and instead track only the rendering /
publisher concern (i.e., do not show the ValueSet's own description as
the "binding strength" prose on the resource page).

#### Justification

ValueSets are intentionally reusable across resources and IGs. The
`condition-clinical` ValueSet, for example, can be re-bound in a profile
at *Extensible* or *Preferred* even though core binds it *Required*; if
its `description` literally says "Required value set …" that statement
is false in every other binding context. Declining in favour of a
publisher-rendering fix avoids embedding context-specific binding
strength language inside reusable terminology artifacts.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The mismatch the reporter identified is real, public-facing, and
unambiguous: the strength column on `condition.html` § 9.2.4.1 says
*Required* / *Example* while the adjacent description says "Preferred"
in both rows. Disposition A removes that contradiction immediately,
costs essentially nothing (two narrative edits in two ValueSets), and
preserves the *Persuasive* resolution of
[FHIR-10272](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-10272) which deliberately
set `clinicalStatus` to *Required*. Disposition B is the philosophically
cleaner fix and is worth tracking as a follow-up Patient Care ↔ FHIR
Infrastructure tooling item, but it is too broad to land as the
resolution of a single editorial ticket and would delay the
user-visible correction. Disposition C over-reads the request: the
reporter is not proposing to bake binding strength into ValueSets in
general; they are pointing at two specific descriptions on two
PC-owned ValueSets that are wrong today. Recommend Patient Care accept
A for this ballot cycle and, if the workgroup agrees with the broader
concern, file a companion Infrastructure ticket aligned with B.
