## Summary

The reporter (Lisa Nicolaou) requests a narrative revision to the final
paragraph of **Section 2.2** of the **US SDOH Clinical Care (FHIR)** IG so
that the text explicitly recognizes that the social risk / SDOH care
processes occur **across sectors of care** and that documenting an
individual's journey across those sectors is part of the IG's intent. The
proposed alternate language adds emphasis on (a) co-designing care
processes across sectors, (b) the explicit relationship between health
and social care professionals (e.g., in managed-care contexts), (c) the
role of Health Information Technology in seamless electronic exchange,
and (d) standardization/harmonization of SDOH concepts **and care
processes**. The ticket has been resolved **Persuasive** and is bundled
into the Patient Care WG **Block Vote 2** for the Gravity SDOHCC STU3
ballot reconciliation (see related Zulip thread). It travels alongside
companion ticket [FHIR-53798](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53798)
("Language to Recognize that the data must move across sectors of care",
resolved *Persuasive with Modification*), which addresses the same
across-sectors theme; FHIR-53799 is the narrower Section 2.2 wording
change.

## Details

**Description:**

> 1. In the final paragraph of section 2.2 there should be recognition
>    again that we are also attempting to document the individual journey
>    through care provided across sectors to better understand risk and
>    how healthcare can intervene most appropriately.
>    1. Alternate language proposed:
>       1. Based on the growing collection of social risk data in health
>          care systems, an immediate opportunity exists to support data
>          collection, data aggregation, data sharing, quality
>          measurement, benchmarking, and risk adjustment. **Because
>          these care processes occur across sectors of care, the care
>          processes must be co-designed to best serve the needs of each
>          sector. The data movement creates the opportunity to recognize
>          an explicit relationship between health and social care
>          professionals for service provision** **(for example, as part
>          of managed care relationships).** Many assessment tools are
>          currently in use, but the concepts addressed vary, and some
>          tools merit further validation and testing across a variety of
>          settings and clinical workflows. **Health** **Information
>          Technology** can play a critical, untapped role in enabling
>          the **seamless** electronic exchange and use of this data.
>          However, standardization and harmonization of SDOH concepts
>          **and care processes** regardless of the social risk
>          assessment instrument used, requires a consensus-based
>          approach to maximize adoption buy-in, consistency of
>          implementation, and usefulness of data collected.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the reporter's proposed alternate paragraph **verbatim** as the
final paragraph of **Section 2.2** of the *US SDOH Clinical Care (FHIR)*
IG, replacing the existing closing paragraph. Concretely, the IG
narrative authoring source for Section 2.2 should be updated so the
paragraph reads (with the reporter's bolded emphasis preserved or
de-emphasized at editorial discretion):

> "Based on the growing collection of social risk data in health care
> systems, an immediate opportunity exists to support data collection,
> data aggregation, data sharing, quality measurement, benchmarking, and
> risk adjustment. Because these care processes occur across sectors of
> care, the care processes must be co-designed to best serve the needs
> of each sector. The data movement creates the opportunity to recognize
> an explicit relationship between health and social care professionals
> for service provision (for example, as part of managed care
> relationships). Many assessment tools are currently in use, but the
> concepts addressed vary, and some tools merit further validation and
> testing across a variety of settings and clinical workflows. Health
> Information Technology can play a critical, untapped role in enabling
> the seamless electronic exchange and use of this data. However,
> standardization and harmonization of SDOH concepts and care processes,
> regardless of the social risk assessment instrument used, requires a
> consensus-based approach to maximize adoption buy-in, consistency of
> implementation, and usefulness of data collected."

This is a documentation-only edit (no FSH/StructureDefinition/value-set
change); ship it in the next STU3 publication build alongside the other
block-vote items.

#### Justification

The change is purely narrative, low-risk, and aligned with the
already-recorded resolution of **Persuasive**. It restores symmetry with
the IG's stated scope of documenting an individual's journey across
sectors of care, which is also the subject of the companion FHIR-53798.
Accepting verbatim respects the submitter's original wording and avoids
re-litigation in the block vote. There are no implementer-facing
artifacts impacted, so backward compatibility and conformance are not
at risk.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach (Accept with light editorial polish)

#### Proposal

Accept the substance of the proposed paragraph but apply minor
editorial cleanup before publication:

1. Drop the bold emphasis once incorporated (the bolding was used by
   the submitter to highlight diffs; it should not appear in the
   published narrative).
2. Insert the missing comma after "*regardless of the social risk
   assessment instrument used*" for grammatical correctness.
3. Lower-case "Health Information Technology" (or expand to "health
   information technology (IT)") to match the IG's existing prose
   style.
4. Cross-link the new sentence about cross-sector co-design to the
   companion text being added under FHIR-53798 so the two paragraphs
   read as a coherent pair rather than restating the same idea.

The semantic content remains exactly what the submitter requested.

#### Justification

The submitter's intent is fully preserved while keeping the IG's prose
style internally consistent and avoiding cosmetic artifacts (stray
bolding) that would otherwise reach the published HTML. Coordinating
with FHIR-53798 prevents two adjacent ballot fixes from creating
overlapping or redundant prose. This is a routine editorial pass that
the WG can delegate to the Gravity editorial team without re-opening
substance.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Reject the proposed paragraph rewrite and leave Section 2.2 unchanged,
on the grounds that (a) the existing paragraph already covers
standardization/harmonization, and (b) the cross-sector framing is
being introduced by the companion ticket FHIR-53798, which makes
FHIR-53799 redundant.

#### Justification

This would be defensible only if the WG concluded that FHIR-53798's
edits sufficiently cover the cross-sector point and that further
amplification in the same section adds noise without new information.
However, this position is hard to sustain given the resolution already
recorded as *Persuasive* and the explicit submitter language calling
out a missing concept.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The ticket has already been resolved *Persuasive* and is in **Block
Vote 2**, so the WG is not being asked to relitigate substance — they
are being asked to authorize publication. Adopting the submitter's
words verbatim (Disposition A) would introduce stray bold formatting
and a small grammatical issue into the published IG, and would risk
producing two adjacent paragraphs (with FHIR-53798) that say
overlapping things in slightly different voices. Disposition B keeps
the submitter's intent intact, lets the Gravity editors land a clean
paragraph in Section 2.2, and explicitly coordinates with FHIR-53798 so
the cross-sector messaging reads as one coherent narrative thread.
Disposition C should be declined because the resolution of record is
*Persuasive* and there is no indication on the ticket or in the
block-vote Zulip post that the WG wants to walk that back.
