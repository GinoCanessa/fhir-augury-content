## Summary

The submitter (TomPage) observes that the US SDOH Clinical Care IG
introduces helpful new language distinguishing **Screening** (first-stage
identification of a potential social need) from **Assessment**
(second-stage gathering and analysis of risk and need data), but applies
those terms inconsistently across the IG narrative — and in some places
omits them as distinct activities altogether. The most prominent example
cited is **Figure 1: Conceptual Framework for SDOH Clinical Care**,
which is also said to misrepresent how the workflow is actually performed
in the field by depicting a circular process that implies a routine
re-screening immediately after a completed intervention. The reporter
proposes a revised Figure 1 expressed as a linear flow: Screening →
Clinical Observation → Diagnosis → Referral for SDOH Assessment → SDOH
Assessment → Patient-Centered Goals / Identification of potential
support / interventions → Referral for SDOH Support → Planned
interventions / SDOH Planning → SDOH Service Provided / Completed
interventions. The ticket has been resolved as **Persuasive with
Modification**: the Gravity Project authors have agreed Figure 1 needs
improvement and are producing a new visual that expresses a workflow
that can be variable and non-linear, rather than adopting the proposed
strictly-linear flow verbatim. There are no Jira cross-references; the
ticket is bundled into the Patient Care WG **Block Vote 2** for the
Gravity SDOHCC STU3 ballot.

## Details

**Description:**

> The new language differentiating between screening activities vs.
> assessment activities is a welcome change to distinguish between the
> first stage of identifying the existence of a potential social need
> and the second stage of gathering and analyzing data to determine
> specific risks and needs, but throughout the document these terms are
> not used consistently or are omitted as distinct activities.
>
> The first example of this is in the accompanying **Figure 1:
> Conceptual Framework for SDOH Clinical Care.** This figure also does
> not reflect the process as it is commonly performed in the real world.
>
> We propose a revised Figure 1 that shows the following steps as a
> linear process with the following steps: Screening → Clinical
> Observation → Diagnosis → Referral for SDOH Assessment → SDOH
> Assessment → Patient Centered Goals / Identification of potential
> support / interventions → Referral for SDOH Support → Planned
> interventions / SDOH Planning → SDOH Service Provided / Completed
> interventions.
>
> As stated above, the diagram should present a linear flow, not a
> circular process that makes it appear that an additional screening
> will take place as a standard follow up after the Completed
> Intervention.

**Resolution Description:**

> We are updating the image in Figure 1 to better express the social
> care referral workflow.

## Proposed Dispositions

> Note: the ticket already carries a resolution of **Persuasive with
> Modification** and an authoring comment confirming that a replacement
> Figure 1 is being produced. The dispositions below frame the choices
> the workgroup can ratify (or override) at the block vote.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the submitter's exact proposal: replace **Figure 1: Conceptual
Framework for SDOH Clinical Care** in the US SDOH Clinical Care
(SDOHCC) IG with a strictly **linear** diagram showing the steps
Screening → Clinical Observation → Diagnosis → Referral for SDOH
Assessment → SDOH Assessment → Patient-Centered Goals / Identification
of potential support / interventions → Referral for SDOH Support →
Planned interventions / SDOH Planning → SDOH Service Provided /
Completed interventions. In addition, sweep the IG narrative (Sections
1.x and the SDOH Clinical Care Background sections, including the
companion ticket FHIR-54608's §1.1 alignment) so that "Screening" is
used consistently for the first-stage detection activity and
"Assessment" is reserved for the second-stage detailed evaluation
activity, and that neither term is silently omitted where the activity
is being described. The diagram swap is a narrative-page / image-asset
change in the SDOHCC IG sources; no profile, value set, extension or
example artifact would change.

#### Justification

The submitter's framing makes the same first-stage / second-stage
distinction the IG itself introduces, so adopting their wording verbatim
maximizes alignment between the figure, the narrative, and the
submitter's mental model of the workflow. A linear depiction is also
the easiest to read and the easiest to align with downstream artifacts
(SDOH Assessment, Goal, ServiceRequest, Procedure) that are themselves
modelled as discrete steps. The replacement is purely editorial — image
asset plus narrative wording — so the implementer impact is minimal and
the consistency benefit is immediate.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach (matches the recorded resolution)

#### Proposal

Replace Figure 1 with a **redrawn** diagram authored by the Gravity
Project that captures the same Screening → … → Service Provided
progression but explicitly depicts the workflow as **variable and
potentially non-linear** (e.g., allowing re-screening after a change in
condition, branching from Clinical Observation back to Screening, or
parallel Assessment / Referral activity), rather than as a strict
left-to-right pipeline. Pair the new figure with a narrative sweep that
applies "Screening" and "Assessment" consistently per the IG's stated
definitions, and explicitly document in the figure caption why some
edges loop or branch (so that the previous "circular = automatic
re-screening after every intervention" misreading does not recur).
Coordinate the wording portion with the closely related ticket
FHIR-54608 (Align "Screening and Assessment" with §1.1) so that the
narrative changes are consistent. This is the change Gravity has
indicated they are already producing in the 2026-02-25 comment and is
the substance of the **Persuasive with Modification** resolution.

#### Justification

Real-world social-care workflows are not strictly linear — patients are
re-screened on change of condition, assessment can run in parallel with
referral, and interventions frequently surface new needs that re-enter
the cycle. A strict linear diagram (Disposition A) would mis-state that
reality and could be cited by implementers as an authoritative claim
that re-screening is out of scope. Redrawing the figure to express
variability while still annotating the canonical primary path captures
the submitter's core complaints (terminology consistency, the
misleading-loop reading of the current figure) without locking the IG
into an over-simplified workflow. This also matches the response
already recorded by the Gravity authors and the ticket's existing
resolution.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Not Persuasive**: keep the current Figure 1 and
the current narrative usage of "Screening" and "Assessment", on the
grounds that (a) the figure is a *conceptual* framework rather than a
prescriptive workflow, (b) the submitter's proposed strictly-linear
sequence would itself misrepresent real practice, and (c) any
inconsistencies in the surrounding narrative can be handled as
editorial fixes via separately-filed, more specific tickets (e.g.,
FHIR-54608 already targets §1.1).

#### Justification

A pure decline is defensible on the narrow grounds that the submitter's
proposed *replacement* (a strictly linear flow) is arguably less
accurate than what the IG already publishes, and that "the terms are
inconsistently used" is broad enough that it is hard to act on without
specific section-level tickets. However, this disposition does not
address the documented misreading of the current figure as implying
automatic re-screening, and it conflicts with the Gravity authors'
already-recorded agreement that Figure 1 has opportunities for
improvement, so it should only be considered if the workgroup believes
the figure does not in fact need to change.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B aligns with the resolution already recorded on the ticket
(*Persuasive with Modification*), with the Gravity Project's 2026-02-25
comment that a new visual is in flight, and with the apparent intent of
the block-vote bundle on the Patient Care WG Zulip thread. It captures
the two real complaints in the ticket — terminology drift between
"Screening" and "Assessment", and the misleading circular reading of
the current Figure 1 — without committing the IG to a strictly linear
workflow that would itself misstate real-world social-care practice
(re-screening on change of condition, parallel assessment / referral,
intervention-driven new needs). It also dovetails naturally with the
related ticket FHIR-54608 on §1.1 wording alignment, allowing both the
figure swap and the narrative sweep to ship as a single coherent
editorial pass on the SDOHCC IG. Disposition A is acceptable as a
fallback if the workgroup prefers to honor the submitter's wording
exactly, and Disposition C should be reserved for the case where the
workgroup concludes Figure 1 does not need to change at all — which the
Gravity authors have already conceded it does.
