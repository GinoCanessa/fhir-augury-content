## Summary

The ticket asks the PACIO project (under Patient Care) to merge the
PACIO Functional Status FHIR IG and the PACIO Cognitive Status FHIR IG
into a single implementation guide. The reporter argues that the two
IGs share more than 80% of their narrative, that their use cases are
not meaningfully distinct, and that profiles for both are largely drawn
from the same CMS patient assessment instruments (e.g., MDS, OASIS).
The proposal is to create a common "functioning data" core with a small
number of domain-specific profiles organized as subsections, on the
view that this will be a more robust foundation as PACIO contemplates
additional content domains. The ticket has no cross-referenced Jira
tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items in the augury index.

## Details

**Description:**

After reviewing both PACIO IGs (Functional Status and Cognitive
Status), it is unclear why two separate IGs are needed. There is
substantial overlap between the two implementation guides, and no
discernable differences in the driving use cases. To the reader, it
feels as though they are > 80% of the narrative is the same. Although a
few parts (e.g. extensions) differ, the detail of the profiles and
element bindings are basically the same. Splitting them into two IGs
and having different profile names for subsections of content drawn
from the same overall instrument (e.g. the CMS-required patient
assessment instruments like MDS, OASIS, etc) imposes additional burden
on implementers (and receivers). The rationale for splitting the IGs
was not described in the IG. We propose merging the two IGs into a
common core for functioning data, with the small number of
domain-specific profiles that are needed being organized into
subsections. Looking ahead to the other kinds of content being
contemplated by the PACIO project, we believe this will be a more
robust foundation.

Is there value in having two IGs? Please stongly consider merging the
two IGs.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Merge the PACIO Functional Status FHIR IG and the PACIO Cognitive
Status FHIR IG into a single "PACIO Functioning" (or similarly named)
IG. Concretely:

- Create one canonical IG (new package id and canonical URL) that owns
  a shared core: a base `Observation` profile (and any common
  `QuestionnaireResponse`, `Composition`, and category/section
  profiles) representing functioning assessments.
- Move the Functional Status–specific and Cognitive Status–specific
  profiles, extensions, and value sets into clearly labeled subsections
  (or sub-IGs / pages) under that common core, retaining only the
  differences (e.g., distinct extensions, distinct LOINC code/value-set
  bindings) as derived profiles of the shared base.
- Deprecate the two existing IGs in their next published version,
  pointing implementers to the merged IG and providing a profile
  cross-walk so existing instances can be re-mapped.
- Coordinate canonical URL changes and versioning with the PACIO
  project and HL7 publishing so legacy references remain resolvable.

#### Justification

The reporter's analysis (>80% shared narrative, profiles and element
bindings substantially identical, both drawing from the same CMS
assessment instruments) matches the FHIR design principle of avoiding
gratuitous profile/IG fragmentation. A single core with derived
profiles is a more conventional FHIR pattern than two near-duplicate
IGs and reduces implementer burden (one package to import, one
conformance surface to validate against, one place to track changes).
Organizing domain-specific differences as derived profiles within one
IG is also more consistent with how PACIO is reportedly planning to
expand into additional functioning-data domains, and makes future
additions cheaper than spinning up another peer IG each time.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the two IGs as separate published artifacts but extract the
shared content into a new **PACIO Functioning Common** base IG that
both Functional Status and Cognitive Status depend on. Concretely:

- Stand up a new lightweight base IG containing the shared
  `Observation` (and related) profiles, common extensions, common value
  sets, and the shared narrative on assessment instruments.
- Refactor the Functional Status and Cognitive Status IGs so each
  contains only its domain-specific derived profiles, extensions, and
  bindings, plus narrative about its use cases — depending on the
  common base for everything else.
- Use this layering to host the run-time code-system/value-set
  selection mechanism that PACIO and Gravity are investigating (per
  Lynn Laakso's 2021-11-04 comment) at the common-base level, so both
  domains pick it up uniformly.

#### Justification

This addresses the reporter's core concern — duplicated content,
duplicated maintenance, and implementer burden — without forcing a
disruptive merge of two already-published IGs and the associated
canonical-URL churn. It preserves the existing audience-facing IG
boundaries (which some PACIO stakeholders and downstream programs may
have already adopted by name) while ensuring that profile, extension,
and value-set definitions live in exactly one place. It also gives the
in-flight Gravity collaboration on run-time value-set selection a
natural home: the common base. The trade-off vs. Disposition A is more
moving parts (three IGs instead of one) but less disruption to
implementers who have already aligned to the published STU1 IGs.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the merge request. Retain the PACIO Functional Status IG and
PACIO Cognitive Status IG as separate IGs, on the basis that (a) the
domains are governed by different CMS instruments, clinical workflows,
and stakeholder communities; (b) the in-flight PACIO/Gravity work on
run-time code-system/value-set selection is expected to diverge the
LOINC bindings between the two domains over time, justifying separate
profile stacks; and (c) STU1 of both IGs has already been published and
adopted, making a merge a breaking change for existing implementers.

#### Justification

The 2021-06-28 PACIO vote (6-0-0, "Considered for Future Use", per
Lynn Laakso's comment) explicitly contemplated this and concluded the
domains were likely to diverge once focused LOINC value sets were
adopted. If that divergence materializes, a single merged IG would end
up needing to switch profile bindings on context anyway — exactly the
problem the Gravity-collaboration mechanism is being designed to
solve. Until that mechanism exists, declining preserves clean,
domain-specific conformance surfaces and avoids breaking already
published STU1 artifacts and their canonical URLs.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B threads the two competing pressures honestly. The
reporter's critique is substantively correct: today the two IGs share
the great majority of their content, and shipping that content twice
imposes real cost on implementers and on PACIO maintenance. At the
same time, the 2021 PACIO vote and Lynn Laakso's comment flag a
plausible future in which Functional Status and Cognitive Status need
to bind to different focused LOINC value sets, and that divergence is
easier to model with two domain-facing IGs sitting on top of a shared
base than by retrofitting context-sensitive bindings onto a single
merged IG. Extracting a common base IG eliminates the duplication
called out in the ticket, gives the PACIO/Gravity run-time
value-set-selection work a single place to land, and avoids the
canonical-URL and implementer-disruption cost of a hard merge of two
already-published STU1 IGs. Disposition A remains the cleanest answer
if PACIO concludes the domains will not in fact diverge; Disposition C
is the weakest because it accepts the ongoing duplication without
addressing the reporter's concern.
