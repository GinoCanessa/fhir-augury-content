## Summary

Russell Leftwich is reporting that the `ClinicalImpression.changePattern`
element refers to "status of a subject's condition" in its short
description and definition, but the term "status" used in that prose is
not itself defined. He is concerned the wording could be conflated with
the `Condition` resource (and in particular with `Condition.clinicalStatus`
or `Condition.verificationStatus`), since `changePattern` is intended to
describe the *trend* (worsening / improving / no change) of a subject's
condition rather than any of the formal status concepts already carried
on `Condition`. The element was added to `ClinicalImpression` in R5 as
the resolution of [FHIR-36197](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-36197)
— the Patient Care change request "Representing improving | deteriorating
patient responses in R4/R5 Condition resource" — where the WG explicitly
chose the phrasing "Change in the status/pattern of a subject's condition
since previously assessed, such as worsening, improving, or no change.
It is a subjective assessment of the direction of the change." This
ticket is in effect a documentation-quality follow-up on that earlier
resolution.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Reword `ClinicalImpression.changePattern`'s short description and
definition so the term "status" no longer appears, eliminating any
collision with `Condition.clinicalStatus` /
`Condition.verificationStatus`. Concretely, replace the current text
("Change in the status/pattern of a subject's condition since
previously assessed, such as worsening, improving, or no change. It
is a subjective assessment of the direction of the change.") with
phrasing that is purely about *trend*, e.g.:

- **Short:** "Trend (e.g. worsening, improving, no change) of the
  subject's condition since the last assessment."
- **Definition:** "A subjective assessment of the direction of change
  of the subject's condition since it was previously assessed, such as
  worsening, improving, or no change. This element describes the trend
  of the condition over time and is independent of
  `Condition.clinicalStatus` and `Condition.verificationStatus`, which
  describe the clinical lifecycle and verification of the Condition
  itself."

No element name change, no cardinality change, no value-set change —
this is a definition-text-only update to `ClinicalImpression` in FHIR
Core, plus a corresponding tweak to the element's W5 / mappings
narrative if it repeats the wording.

#### Justification

The reporter's concern is real: `Condition` already uses the word
"status" for two formally bound elements, so reusing "status" in a
sibling resource's definition without explicit scoping is genuinely
ambiguous. The fix is low-risk because it touches only descriptive
text, not the element name, cardinality, or binding established by
[FHIR-36197](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-36197). It also matches
how the implementer community is already explaining the element on
Zulip (as a "trend"), so the rewording aligns spec text with actual
usage and leaves the R5 element semantics unchanged.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Leave the element definition essentially as-is, but add an explicit
*comment* (`element.comment`) on `ClinicalImpression.changePattern`
that defines what is meant by "status" here and disambiguates it from
`Condition.clinicalStatus` and `Condition.verificationStatus`. For
example:

> "In this element, 'status/pattern' refers to the subjective trend of
> the condition (e.g. worsening, improving, no change). It is not
> related to `Condition.clinicalStatus` (active | recurrence |
> remission | resolved …) or `Condition.verificationStatus`
> (provisional | confirmed …), which describe the lifecycle and
> verification state of the Condition resource itself."

Optionally, also cross-reference this comment from the relevant
`Condition` element comments so an implementer reading either resource
finds the disambiguation.

#### Justification

Editing the short/definition risks breaking downstream tooling, IG
narrative, and translations that have already absorbed the published
R5 wording. Adding a comment is non-breaking, preserves the existing
text the WG agreed to in FHIR-36197, and still directly answers the
reporter's complaint by defining the word "status" in context. The
trade-off versus Disposition A is that the ambiguous phrase remains in
the short/definition — implementers who only read those two fields
will still see "status" without scope until they scroll to the
comment.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as "Not Persuasive": the existing R5 wording
("Change in the status/pattern of a subject's condition…") was
deliberately chosen by Patient Care during the resolution of
[FHIR-36197](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-36197), the element is
named `changePattern` (not `status`), the value-set examples (Worsening
/ Improving / No status change) make the trend-orientation clear, and
no implementer confusion has been reported on Zulip — the only related
threads either re-state the trend semantics or are about IG plumbing
of the value set. The conflation risk is therefore considered
hypothetical and not worth a normative-text edit.

#### Justification

This is a minimal, recently-published R5 element with an
already-agreed wording and an example binding that telegraphs intent.
A spec-text change for a hypothetical conflation that has not surfaced
in the implementer community could be deferred until concrete misuse
is observed.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter is the Patient Care co-chair flagging a wording issue in
his own WG's element, and the fix is purely editorial: rename the
ambiguous prose, keep the element, cardinality, value set, and
behaviour intact. Disposition A directly removes the source of
confusion (re-use of the loaded word "status") rather than papering
over it with a comment that many implementers will not read, and it
costs nothing in interoperability because no on-the-wire artifact
changes. Disposition B is a reasonable fallback if the WG wants to
preserve the published short/definition verbatim, and Disposition C is
not advisable given that the concern comes from inside the WG and the
Zulip evidence shows implementers do reach for `changePattern`
specifically to express trend — making clear, unambiguous element text
worth the small editorial cost.
