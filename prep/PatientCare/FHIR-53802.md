## Summary

The submitter requests that **Section 3.3 (Scope of Interactions)** of the US
SDOH Clinical Care IG be updated so that the narrative and the accompanying
data-flow diagram cover the case where **no Coordination Platform (CP) is
involved** in the patient ↔ provider ↔ CBO interaction. As written, the
section's first line acknowledges that intermediation by a CP is only
"possible," but the diagram and surrounding text only depict the
CP-mediated topology. The reporter proposes either (a) splitting the existing
diagram into two — one with a CP, one without — or (b) re-drawing the single
diagram so that the CP and its data flows are explicitly optional, paired with
revised narrative that consistently treats the CP as conditional ("when
present"). A subsequent comment offers the alternative of keeping the proposed
text revisions but **removing the diagram altogether**, and the published
resolution ("Persuasive with Modification") follows that alternative: update
the Section 3.3 text to reflect optional CP intermediation, and **remove the
diagram** because it was not adding value. This ticket has no linked or
cross-referenced Jira tickets; it is one of a tranche of Gravity SDOHCC STU3
ballot items being grouped into "Block Vote 2" for Patient Care WG.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the published resolution as written: in the US SDOH Clinical Care IG's
**Section 3.3 Scope of Interactions**, replace the existing introductory
narrative with the reporter's proposed language (which reframes the
Coordination Platform as conditional — "There may be scenarios where there will
be intermediation by a Coordination Platform (CP)…CPs **(when present)** will
act as both clients and servers…") and **remove the existing data-flow
diagram** entirely (per the JIRAUSER24103 alternative comment that the
resolution adopted). The section then describes the patient / provider / CBO
interaction model in prose only, with the CP called out as an optional
intermediary rather than depicted as a fixed node in a diagram.

#### Justification

This is the disposition the WG and the ticket reporter have already converged
on: the resolution is recorded as "Persuasive with Modification" and the
modification (remove the diagram, keep the text update) is explicitly captured
in the comments. The narrative change cleanly addresses the reporter's core
complaint — that the wording said "possible intermediation by a CP" while the
diagram implied a CP was always present — without requiring the IG editors to
maintain two parallel diagrams or invent a new optional-flow notation. Removing
the diagram also avoids the "confusion of what the diagram is representing"
flagged by the commenter, which was a real risk given that a single
patient/provider/CBO/CP figure cannot easily convey both the
with-CP and without-CP topologies.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt the reporter's text revision in Section 3.3, but **retain a diagram**
(rather than deleting it) and update the figure to make the CP visually
optional — for example, render the CP node and any arrows that terminate at it
in a dashed/outlined style with a legend entry "Coordination Platform (when
present)," or split the figure into two clearly-labelled sub-figures (3.3-a
"Without CP," 3.3-b "With CP intermediation"). The narrative and figure
caption explicitly cross-reference each other so the conditional flows are
unambiguous.

#### Justification

Diagrams are a common entry point for implementers skimming an IG, and Section
3.3 is the canonical "what does this IG cover" section; deleting the figure
removes a high-value at-a-glance summary even though it fixes the original
ambiguity. A dashed/optional rendering — or a split into two figures, which is
exactly what the reporter's first preference was — keeps the visual aid while
honouring the "CP is optional" requirement. The cost is editorial: someone
has to redraw the figure and keep it consistent with future text changes,
which is the very burden the WG appears to have wanted to avoid. This option
should be on the table only if reviewers feel that text-only is a regression
in usability for the IG.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the change request "Not Persuasive" and leave Section 3.3 (text and
diagram) as published, on the grounds that the existing wording ("with possible
intermediation by a Coordination Platform") already signals that the CP is
optional, and the diagram is illustrative rather than normative.

#### Justification

This option is included for completeness; it is not defensible on the merits.
The reporter's observation is concrete (the diagram does not represent the
no-CP scenario and the narrative does not consistently treat the CP as
conditional), and a workgroup commenter independently confirmed the diagram is
confusing. Declining would leave a known clarity defect in a Scope-of-
Interactions section, which is exactly the kind of section new implementers
read first. The only scenario in which this is reasonable is if the WG decides
the entire Section 3.3 narrative is being rewritten under another ticket and
this one becomes redundant — but no such linked ticket was found in the
cross-reference index.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket already carries a resolution of "Persuasive with Modification" that
captures the JIRAUSER24103 alternative (update the text, drop the diagram),
and there is no Zulip pushback against that resolution — the only Zulip
mention is the Block Vote 2 announcement which lists FHIR-53802 with this
exact disposition. Accepting As Requested aligns the WG vote with the
already-recorded resolution, lets this ticket move through Block Vote 2 with
the rest of the Gravity SDOHCC STU3 tranche, and avoids opening a new
editorial task (maintaining a redrawn or split diagram) that the publishing
team has implicitly de-scoped. Disposition B is a reasonable fallback if a
reviewer argues the diagram has independent pedagogical value worth the
maintenance cost; Disposition C should be rejected because the underlying
clarity defect is real and acknowledged on the ticket.
