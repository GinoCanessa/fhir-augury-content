## Summary

The reporter (Mary Kay McDaniel) notes that in the US PACIO Personal
Functioning and Engagement IG, section **1.10 Intellectual Property
Considerations**, the boilerplate paragraph attributing source material
to HL7 Terminology (THO) — "This material derives from the HL7
Terminology (THO). THO is copyright ©1989+ Health Level Seven
International and is made available under the CC0 designation. For more
licensing information see: https://terminology.hl7.org/license" —
appears **three times in a row, identically**. The reporter flags this
as likely a typo/duplication bug rather than intentional content, and
in the comment notes the same duplication appears across **several of
the SEP Ballot IGs**, suggesting a shared template or generation
defect. There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or
GitHub items linked to this issue.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat this as a **Persuasive — Technical Correction** in the US PACIO
Personal Functioning and Engagement IG. Edit the rendered "1.10
Intellectual Property Considerations" section so the THO attribution
paragraph appears **exactly once**. Because the reporter notes the same
duplication shows up in "several of the SEP Ballot IGs", the fix should
be applied uniformly to every affected SEP IG (e.g., the other PACIO
Personal Functioning and Engagement-family IGs balloted in the same
cycle). Investigate whether the duplication originates in IG-publisher
behaviour for repeated dependencies on `hl7.terminology` (e.g., the IG
declares THO via more than one path — direct dependency plus a
transitive dependency from another package — causing the IP paragraph
to be emitted once per occurrence). If so, deduplicate the dependency
declarations in `sushi-config.yaml` / `ig.ini` so the IP page renders a
single THO attribution; otherwise patch the rendered content directly.

#### Justification

The duplication is plainly unintentional: the three paragraphs are
character-identical and serve no editorial purpose. The IP page is
required and consumed by implementers, regulators, and IP/legal review,
so accuracy and tidiness here is high-value at low risk. Fixing the
underlying dependency-emission cause (vs. only patching one IG) is
consistent with the reporter's observation that this is a class of
defect across the SEP IGs and avoids re-occurrence in the next ballot
publish.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than fixing only this IG, **escalate to FHIR Infrastructure / IG
Publisher maintainers** to investigate and fix the duplicate-emission
behaviour in the IG-publisher / template that renders the
"Intellectual Property Considerations" section, and confirm the
expected one-paragraph-per-distinct-dependency contract. Patient Care
records the disposition as Persuasive but defers the remediation to a
cross-cutting tooling fix, then verifies the affected SEP IGs render
correctly on the next publish. As an interim, ask the SEP IG editors
to manually trim duplicates in any IGs being re-balloted before the
tooling fix lands.

#### Justification

If the root cause is in shared tooling (template or publisher), fixing
it once benefits every IG that depends on THO — not just US PACIO
Personal Functioning and Engagement — and prevents the same defect
re-appearing in future ballots. This is consistent with the reporter's
note that the issue spans multiple SEP IGs. The trade-off vs.
Disposition A is timing: a tooling fix takes longer to land than a
local content edit, but it is the only durable solution if the cause is
publisher-side.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark as **Not Persuasive** on the grounds that the duplication is
purely cosmetic, the meaning of the IP statement is unchanged, and the
section still satisfies the IP-disclosure requirement. No change to the
IG content or tooling.

#### Justification

The duplicated paragraphs do not introduce factual inaccuracy or alter
the legal effect of the IP statement; the THO attribution and CC0
licence reference remain correct. Workgroup time may be better spent on
substantive corrections. This rationale is weak — the workgroup has
historically treated cosmetic publication defects in normative IP
sections as worth correcting, especially when called out by a reviewer
— and is included only for completeness.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A (with the Disposition B follow-up if the
root cause is in the IG publisher).

This is a clear, low-risk **Technical Correction** in a normative,
reader-facing section of a balloted IG. Accepting and fixing the IG is
the right immediate action, and because the reporter explicitly notes
the same defect appears across several SEP Ballot IGs, the editors
should also briefly inspect whether the duplication is caused by the
way THO is declared as a dependency (or by the IP-page template) and
deduplicate at the source if so. Declining (Disposition C) is hard to
defend for an obvious copy-paste/render bug raised by a named reviewer
in a balloted IG.
