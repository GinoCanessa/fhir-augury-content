## Summary

Eric Haas reports a possible semantic conflict in the **AdverseEvent**
resource between the `actuality` discriminator and the
`preventiveAction` / `mitigatingAction` backbone elements. Specifically,
`preventiveAction` reads as something done *before* an adverse event
occurs (so it would only make sense when `actuality = "potential"`,
i.e., a near-miss), while `mitigatingAction` reads as something done in
response to harm (so it would only make sense when `actuality =
"actual"`). The ticket asks the work group to either (1) add invariants
that bind these elements to the appropriate `actuality` value, or
(2) leave the elements unconstrained but clarify in the element comments
/ definitions what each means under the opposite `actuality` value.

The ticket sits in a small cluster of recent AdverseEvent tickets that
revisit the same underlying axis. [FHIR-32990](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-32990)
("Clarify AdverseEvent.actuality", *Persuasive*, Published) already
established the editorial direction of clarifying — rather than
constraining — the meaning of `actuality` (explicitly calling out
"near miss" semantics). [FHIR-34317](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-34317)
("Remove Actuality from AdverseEvent", *Not Persuasive*) shows the work
group has previously declined to remove or further restrict `actuality`,
preferring to keep it as an open discriminator. [FHIR-55559](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-55559)
(also from Eric Haas, currently *Submitted*) is part of the same
review pass and targets `effect[x]` vs `detected` naming on the same
resource.

## Details

**Description:**

> **Semantic Conflicts: `preventiveAction` vs. `actuality = actual`**
>
> **Missing Constraints?** Should there be a constraint when
> `preventiveAction` is only valid when `actuality = "potential"`?
> Similarly, should there be a constraint when `mitigatingAction` is
> only valid when `actuality = "actual"`?
>
> Recommendations:
>
> 1. Add these constraints …or…
> 2. Clarify in comments or description what `preventiveAction` means
>    when `actuality = "actual"` and `mitigatingAction` when
>    `actuality = "potential"`.

**Comments:**

- **bheale (2026-02-06):** "`actuality = "potential"` implies a
  near-miss, so mitigating action is still valid." — pushes back on
  half of the proposed invariant: a near-miss can still warrant a
  mitigating response.
- **JIRAUSER17683 / BRR review (2026-02-10):** "BRR has reviewed and
  recommends forgoing the constraint and suggests Option 2 to clarify
  in the comments. At the resource level, you do not want to put a
  constraint and would rather leave that to the IGs to dictate based on
  the specific circumstance or use case." — explicit recommendation
  from the Biomedical Research & Regulation WG to use Option 2 and
  defer any tightening to downstream IGs.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested (add invariants)

#### Proposal

Add two FHIRPath invariants to **AdverseEvent** in FHIR Core:

- `aev-X`: `preventiveAction.exists() implies actuality = 'potential'`
  — severity `error`, on `AdverseEvent`.
- `aev-Y`: `mitigatingAction.exists() implies actuality = 'actual'` —
  severity `error`, on `AdverseEvent`.

Author the constraints in the AdverseEvent StructureDefinition and add
matching prose to the element `comment`s explaining the intended
pairing.

#### Justification

Strict invariants would make the ambiguity unambiguous and would prevent
implementers from populating both action types regardless of context.
However, this is the *less-supported* option in the discussion: it
contradicts BRR's review recommendation, runs against PC's prior
disposition on [FHIR-34317](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-34317)
(which kept `actuality` deliberately broad), and it is directly
challenged by bheale's comment that a near-miss (`actuality =
"potential"`) can still warrant a mitigating action — which would make
`aev-Y` outright wrong as written. To salvage Option A, `aev-Y` would
have to be dropped, leaving an asymmetric pair of constraints that is
itself confusing.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — clarify definitions/comments only (Option 2)

#### Proposal

Make the change purely editorial in the AdverseEvent
StructureDefinition:

- Update `AdverseEvent.preventiveAction.definition` and `.comment` to
  state that `preventiveAction` typically applies when `actuality =
  "potential"` (near-miss), but MAY also be recorded against an
  `actual` event when an action was taken in response to learning of
  the event to prevent a similar future occurrence.
- Update `AdverseEvent.mitigatingAction.definition` and `.comment` to
  state that `mitigatingAction` typically applies when `actuality =
  "actual"` (harm occurred and was mitigated), but MAY also apply when
  `actuality = "potential"` because near-miss responses still
  constitute mitigation, per bheale's note.
- Add a brief paragraph to the AdverseEvent resource narrative
  ("Boundaries and Relationships" or "Notes" section) that summarises
  the relationship between `actuality` and the
  `preventiveAction` / `mitigatingAction` / `contributingFactor` /
  `supportingInfo` elements, and explicitly states that any tighter
  pairing is left to derived profiles / IGs.
- No FHIRPath invariants. No cardinality changes.

#### Justification

This is the option BRR explicitly recommended ("forgoing the constraint
… leave that to the IGs to dictate based on the specific circumstance
or use case") and it directly accommodates bheale's counter-example
about near-miss mitigation. It is also consistent with the precedent
set by [FHIR-32990](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-32990), which
solved an analogous ambiguity on `actuality` itself by clarifying
definitions rather than constraining structure, and with the PC stance
in [FHIR-34317](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-34317) of keeping
`actuality` deliberately broad. It is non-breaking for existing
implementers (no new invariant failures, no element shape change) and
remains compatible with the structural changes already applied in
[FHIR-46599](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-46599) (CodeableReference
form). Implementation cost is a focused doc edit on a single resource;
risk to derived IGs is essentially zero.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (Not Persuasive)

#### Proposal

Resolve as **Not Persuasive**, on the grounds that the perceived
conflict is editorial rather than structural and that the existing
element names (`preventiveAction`, `mitigatingAction`) are sufficiently
self-explanatory in the context of `actuality`. No spec change.

#### Justification

The argument for declining is that ratifying *every* request to
clarify natural-language semantics expands the resource definition
without changing behaviour. However, this position is weaker here than
it usually is: there are at least two reviewers (the reporter and BRR)
who actively want the clarification, the cost of a definition tweak is
trivial, and refusing to clarify when the WG actually agrees with
Option 2 risks the same ticket recurring.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition: B (Persuasive with Modification — clarify
definitions and comments; no invariants).**

Disposition B is the only option that satisfies every signal already on
the ticket: BRR's explicit Option-2 recommendation, bheale's correction
that near-miss mitigation is legitimate (which falsifies Disposition
A's `aev-Y`), the PC precedent from
[FHIR-32990](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-32990) of solving
`actuality` ambiguities through prose, and the prior PC decision in
[FHIR-34317](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-34317) to keep
`actuality` broad. It is also the lowest-risk path with respect to the
recently-applied [FHIR-46599](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-46599)
restructure and leaves room for downstream IGs (e.g., a
pharmacovigilance or patient-safety IG) to impose tighter pairing as
profile-level constraints when their use case warrants it. The work
group should reject Disposition A on the strength of bheale's
counter-example alone, and should prefer B over C because the
clarification has explicit reviewer support and the editorial cost is
small.
