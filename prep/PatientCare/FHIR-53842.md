## Summary

The ticket asks the Gravity Project / Patient Care WG to make and document
a deliberate modeling decision for "Protective Factors" within the US
SDOH Clinical Care (SDOHCC) IG: should protective factors be represented
as `Condition`, `Observation`, or some unique construct? The reporter
notes that strengths and barriers — the closest analogues in current
practice — are most often captured today within the workflow of other
observations, so the choice is not obvious. The recommendation in the
original ticket is to perform an explicit evaluation of the options and
document the chosen approach in the IG. The ticket is currently in
"Triaged" state with a proposed resolution of **Persuasive**, and the
resolution narrative records that the decision is to use the
**`Condition`** resource and to add explanatory language to the
SDOHCC Condition profiles' "Protective Factors" section explaining why
`Condition` best aligns with the use case. No cross-referenced Jira
tickets surface in Augury for this issue; the only cross-reference is
the Patient Care WG block-vote announcement on Zulip in which this
ticket is bundled with other Gravity SDOHCC STU3 ballot dispositions.

## Details

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the resolution already drafted on the ticket: model Protective
Factors using the `Condition` resource within the US SDOH Clinical Care
IG, and add explanatory narrative to the **Protective Factors** section
of the SDOHCC Condition profile(s) (i.e., the `SDOHCC-Condition`-family
profiles already used for SDOH problems/health concerns) describing why
`Condition` best fits the use case. Concretely:

- Document the modeling decision in the IG's Protective Factors
  narrative page and in the relevant Condition profile intro(s).
- Reuse the existing SDOHCC Condition profile(s) (no new structural
  changes), scoping protective-factor `Condition` instances by
  `category` / `code` value sets per the Gravity content already in the
  IG.
- Cross-link the Protective Factors page to the broader SDOH
  Strengths/Barriers guidance so implementers see the contrast with
  observation-based capture.

#### Justification

This is the disposition the Gravity Project has already worked through
and is bringing to Patient Care WG as **Persuasive** in block vote 2.
Using `Condition` keeps protective factors aligned with how SDOHCC
already represents problems/health concerns, preserves a single
patient-summary surface for clinically actionable factors (problem
list / `Condition` category), and supports the reasonRefs from
`Goal`, `ServiceRequest`, and `Procedure` that Gravity workflows rely
on. Adding explicit narrative — rather than a new resource or extension
— addresses the reporter's core ask ("evaluation and documentation of
the decision") with minimal IG churn.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Model Protective Factors as **`Observation`** (or allow either
`Condition` or `Observation` with profile-level guidance on when each
applies), aligning with the reporter's note that strengths and barriers
are most often captured today inside other observation workflows
(screening responses, assessments). Specifically:

- Define an `SDOHCC-ObservationProtectiveFactor` profile parallel to the
  existing SDOHCC Observation profiles (e.g., for screening responses
  and assessments), with category `social-history` / `survey` as
  appropriate and a value set bound to the Gravity Protective Factors
  codes.
- Update the IG's Protective Factors narrative to explain when an
  implementer should use the new `Observation` profile vs. the existing
  `Condition` profile (e.g., point-in-time assessment finding vs.
  ongoing clinically-relevant factor on the problem list).

#### Justification

Many protective factors surface as discrete answers to assessment
instruments (PRAPARE, AHC-HRSN, etc.) and are naturally captured as
`Observation` alongside the screening/assessment results that produced
them. An `Observation`-first model avoids inflating the problem list
with items clinicians may not consider conditions, and it mirrors how
Gravity already handles screening responses. The trade-off is a
heavier IG (a new profile + value-set wiring) and the burden on
implementers to disambiguate `Condition` vs. `Observation` for the
same concept — which is why this option was not the WG's first choice.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the ticket on the grounds that it asks a methodological
question rather than identifying a defect, and that no IG change is
strictly required: implementers can already represent protective
factors with `Condition` (or `Observation`) using the existing SDOHCC
profiles, and the `Persuasive` resolution can be recorded without
publishing additional normative guidance.

#### Justification

The ticket is not pointing at broken content; it is requesting an
architectural decision and its documentation. If the WG considers the
existing profile set sufficient and is concerned about scope creep
late in the STU3 cycle, declining (or marking "Considered — No Action
Required") is defensible. The downside is that implementers continue
to face the ambiguity the reporter raised, and the question is likely
to recur in subsequent ballot cycles.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The Gravity Project has already evaluated the options and is bringing
this to Patient Care WG as part of **Block Vote 2** with a
**Persuasive** disposition that selects `Condition` and adds
explanatory IG narrative. That outcome directly satisfies the
reporter's ask ("evaluation and documentation of the decision"),
keeps protective factors consistent with how SDOHCC already models
problems/health concerns, and avoids introducing a new
`Observation` profile and value-set wiring late in the STU3 cycle.
Disposition B remains a credible fallback if the WG hears strong
implementer feedback that protective factors are predominantly
captured inside assessment workflows; Disposition C should only be
chosen if the WG concludes the IG narrative change is out of scope
for this ballot cycle.
