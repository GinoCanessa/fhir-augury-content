## Summary

FHIR-33835 asks that the canonical URL for the **`visual-field-observations`
CodeSystem** (delivered as part of the *Ophthalmology Retinal (FHIR)*
specification) be re-rooted from `http://hl7.org/fhir/...` to
`http://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/...`, and that a companion **UTG
ticket** be filed to register the CodeSystem in THO. The change brings the
artifact in line with HL7's terminology-management policy, under which
HL7-authored terminology content lives in THO (managed via the Unified
Terminology Governance process) rather than alongside resource definitions
in the core FHIR canonical namespace.

The ticket was filed during a September 2021 sweep of the Ophthalmology
Retinal IG (and was originally numbered as "Comment 17" in the ballot
import). It is one of a coordinated series of identically-shaped tickets
the same reporter raised across multiple specifications. Sibling tickets
returned by keyword similarity include
[FHIR-33826](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-33826),
[FHIR-33828](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-33828),
[FHIR-33829](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-33829),
[FHIR-33832](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-33832),
[FHIR-33833](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-33833), and
[FHIR-33834](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-33834) (other

[FHIR-33758](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-33758) /

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

1. Re-root the canonical URL of the `visual-field-observations`
   CodeSystem from `http://hl7.org/fhir/CodeSystem/visual-field-observations`
   (or whichever `hl7.org/fhir/...` URL is currently published) to
   `http://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/visual-field-observations`
   in the *Ophthalmology Retinal (FHIR)* IG source.
2. File a corresponding UTG ticket against `HL7/UTG` requesting that the
   CodeSystem (concepts, properties, designations, and `caseSensitive` /
   `content` flags) be migrated into THO under the new canonical, with
   Patient Care named as the steward.
3. Add the THO canonical to the IG's `dependsOn` / terminology imports
   so the IG continues to render unchanged after migration, and update
   any internal references in profiles / examples that point at the
   old URL.
4. Treat the prior `hl7.org/fhir/...` URL as deprecated; if the IG is
   already published, keep a redirect or a `retired`/`replaced-by`
   note for implementers who have bound to the old canonical.

#### Justification

This is the standard HL7 terminology-governance pattern: HL7-authored
CodeSystems and ValueSets are owned and versioned in THO via UTG, with
canonicals under `terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/...`. Keeping
terminology in the per-IG namespace creates parallel governance,
duplicates change-control, and makes cross-IG reuse harder — exactly the
problems UTG was created to solve. The request matches a wider
2021 cleanup the same reporter raised across many specs (sibling tickets
listed above), and accepting it keeps Ophthalmology Retinal aligned
with how every other HL7 IG manages its terminology.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — Defer to a Coordinated Batch

#### Proposal

Acknowledge that the canonical URL **should** be in THO, but rather
than handling FHIR-33835 in isolation, batch it with the other
Ophthalmology Retinal sibling tickets
([FHIR-33826](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-33826),
[FHIR-33828](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-33828),
[FHIR-33829](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-33829),
[FHIR-33832](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-33832),
[FHIR-33833](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-33833),
[FHIR-33834](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-33834), …). File a
single umbrella UTG ticket that registers all in-IG CodeSystems for
*Ophthalmology Retinal* in THO at once, then update the IG in one PR
that re-roots every canonical and refreshes every internal reference.
Mark the individual tickets as resolved by the umbrella ticket.

#### Justification

The substantive change is identical to Disposition A; this only changes
*how* the work is sequenced. Doing it as one coordinated migration
avoids an unstable IG state where some CodeSystems live in THO and
others still live under `hl7.org/fhir`, reduces the per-ticket
overhead of seven near-identical UTG submissions, and ensures the
IG's internal cross-references all flip in the same publication.
Implementers see one breaking-canonical event instead of several. The
trade-off is calendar time: nothing moves until the batch is ready.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the canonical-URL move on the grounds that the *Ophthalmology
Retinal (FHIR)* specification is dormant — there has been no Jira
activity on this ticket since 2021-11-09, no Zulip discussion of the
requested change, no PR on file, and the spec is not represented in
the active repo set. Recommend that the ticket be closed as
*Persuasive – with mod* / *Not Persuasive* depending on WG preference,
with a note that if the IG is revived for a future ballot the THO
re-rooting will be done as part of that work, not as a standalone
task now.

#### Justification

A canonical-URL change to a CodeSystem that has *no* implementer-facing
churn outside its parent IG yields no user-visible benefit while the
IG is inactive, and re-rooting a published canonical is itself a
breaking change for any implementer who did adopt the old URL.
Spending UTG cycle time and Patient Care meeting time to migrate
terminology for a spec nobody is actively iterating is hard to justify
ahead of higher-priority work. If/when the IG is revived, accepting
the request at that point (Disposition A or B) costs the same effort
and lands the change with the rest of the spec refresh.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition: B (Coordinated Batch).**

The underlying ask is correct and aligned with HL7 policy, so
declining outright (C) is hard to defend on principle even if the
spec is currently quiet — and a four-year-old "Triaged" status is a
poor reason to close a still-valid governance request. But handling
FHIR-33835 in isolation (A) means filing one of seven near-identical
UTG tickets against the same IG and producing an interim state in
which `visual-field-observations` lives in THO while its sibling
CodeSystems still live under `hl7.org/fhir/...`. That partial state
is exactly the inconsistency the original ticket is trying to remove.

Disposition B captures the policy benefit of A, eliminates the
sibling-ticket fragmentation, and gives the work group a clean
coordination point: one umbrella UTG submission, one IG PR, one
publication event. It also forces the prerequisite work (a
`repo-analysis` of the Ophthalmology Retinal IG and `HL7/UTG`) to be
done once for the whole batch instead of seven times, which
addresses the repo-context gap noted above.
