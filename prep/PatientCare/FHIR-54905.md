## Summary

The reporter (Celine Lefebvre) asks the US SDOH Clinical Care (SDOH-CC) IG
to promote FHIR Subscriptions as the preferred change-notification
mechanism, relegating Polling to a fallback used only when Subscriptions
are unavailable or infeasible. The ticket recommends three concrete
edits to the IG narrative: (1) expand and strengthen the Subscriptions
section, (2) reorder the Polling section to appear *after*
Subscriptions, and (3) tighten the Dependencies section to
"strongly recommend" Subscriptions. The motivation given is that polling
imposes computational, energy, and connectivity costs that are avoidable
when a subscription channel is available. The work group has already
flagged the resolution as **Persuasive**, agreeing that Subscriptions
should be foregrounded and that updated guidance is in order. No
linked or cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items
were surfaced for this issue.

## Details

**Description:**

> Polling is generally only used when Subscription is not available or
> feasible, as it causes computational, energy, and connectivity delays.
> The IG should make it clear that Subscription is strongly recommend and
> Polling is only as a backup. Suggest beefing up the subscriptions
> section, and moving polling section to after subscriptions section, and
> also in the dependencies section change the language to strongly
> recommend subscriptions.

**Resolution Description (already entered on the ticket):**

> We agree that we should promote subscription capabilities in the IG
> better. We will update the Subscriptions section to include more
> updated information, as well as note that subscriptions are preferred
> over polling methods.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the three narrative edits the reporter asks for, scoped to the
US SDOH Clinical Care IG:

1. **Subscriptions section** — expand it with up-to-date guidance:
   describe the R5-style topic-based Subscription model and (if the IG
   targets R4) point to the Subscriptions R5 Backport IG; show at least
   one worked example of a Subscription against an SDOH-CC profile
   (e.g., `SDOHCC-Observation-ScreeningResponse`,
   `SDOHCC-ServiceRequest`, `SDOHCC-Goal`, `SDOHCC-Task` or the
   referral Task chain); list the SubscriptionTopics that are most
   relevant for SDOH workflows (new/updated screening responses,
   referral status changes, goal achievement updates).
2. **Polling section** — move it so it appears *after* the Subscriptions
   section in the page sequence (and update the navigation/menu and
   any cross-links accordingly), and reframe its lead paragraph as a
   fallback for environments where push notification is unavailable.
3. **Dependencies section** — change the language from neutral mention
   of subscription capability to **"strongly recommend"** that
   conformant systems support FHIR Subscriptions for change
   notification, with polling listed as an acceptable fallback.

This matches the ticket's resolution note ("subscriptions are preferred
over polling methods") and the **Persuasive** disposition the work
group has already recorded.

#### Justification

The change is purely narrative/guidance — no profile, value-set, or
operation behavior is being altered — so the implementer impact is
limited to documentation. The R4/R5 FHIR specs both treat
Subscriptions as the preferred push mechanism, and US Core,
Subscriptions Backport, and several Da Vinci IGs already follow this
convention; aligning SDOH-CC with that pattern improves cross-IG
consistency. Promoting Subscriptions also addresses the legitimate
operational concern raised in the ticket (battery/bandwidth/latency
cost of polling) without forbidding polling, which preserves
backward-compatible behavior for implementers that cannot yet host a
subscription channel.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Make the same content investments as Disposition A, but stop short of
saying "strongly recommend" in the Dependencies section. Instead:

- Restructure the page so Subscriptions is presented *first* and
  Polling second (the reordering the reporter asked for).
- In the Dependencies section, mark the **Subscriptions Backport IG**
  (or R5 Subscriptions, depending on the IG's FHIR version) as a
  **referenced dependency** with a "SHOULD support" conformance
  statement scoped to servers that publish change-notifiable SDOH
  resources, and call out polling as an explicitly permitted
  alternative for clients/servers that do not implement subscriptions.
- Add an SDOH-CC-specific subsection inside Subscriptions that
  enumerates the recommended SubscriptionTopics (or the criteria for
  filter-based subscriptions if the IG stays on R4-style).

#### Justification

"Strongly recommend" is informally clear but is not a normative
conformance verb in the IG-template sense, so an "expected to
implement" / "SHOULD" framing on a named dependency is more actionable
for testers and implementers than a prose intensifier. It also keeps
the door open for SDOH workflows where subscriptions are genuinely
impractical (e.g., constrained CBO endpoints behind firewalls,
batch-oriented HIE intermediaries) without contradicting the IG's own
conformance statements. This still satisfies the reporter's intent —
promoting subscriptions and demoting polling — while making the
guidance enforceable.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the textual restructuring and leave the Subscriptions, Polling,
and Dependencies sections in their current form on the grounds that
the request is editorial, the existing pages already mention both
options, and no implementer pain or interoperability defect has been
reported. If desired, address the concern with a one-paragraph
"Choosing between Subscriptions and Polling" note rather than a
section reordering and a stronger conformance verb.

#### Justification

This would be defensible only if the work group judged that the
current narrative is already balanced and that elevating one mechanism
over the other risks pushing implementers off polling-based deployments
that work well today (e.g., low-volume, batch-oriented community
referral exchanges). It would, however, contradict the resolution
already entered on the ticket ("we agree that we should promote
subscription capabilities in the IG better"), so this disposition is
included for completeness rather than as a serious candidate.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A (with the conformance-language
refinement from B folded in).

The ticket has already been marked **Persuasive** and the resolution
text on the issue commits the work group to exactly the change the
reporter asked for, so Disposition C would reverse a position the work
group has effectively taken. Disposition A delivers everything the
reporter asked for and is low-risk because it is narrative-only.
Disposition B's substantive contribution is the more rigorous
conformance phrasing in the Dependencies section ("SHOULD support" on
the named Subscriptions dependency rather than the prose
"strongly recommend"); that refinement is worth adopting on top of
A so the guidance is testable. The combined change keeps polling as a
sanctioned fallback, aligns SDOH-CC with the broader HL7 trend toward
push-based change notification, and addresses the reporter's
operational concerns without forcing a profile or operation change.
