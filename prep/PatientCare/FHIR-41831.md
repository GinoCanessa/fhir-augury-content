## Summary

The reporter (Ana Kostadinovska) flags an editorial / consistency
problem in the International Birth And Child Model (FHIR) IG. In the
narrative description of "Step 5", the final sentence drops to a
significantly lower level of abstraction than the surrounding steps by
calling out a specific FHIR resource element to use. The reporter asks
that Step 5 be rewritten so that all steps in the sequence stay at the
same conceptual level — i.e., describe *what* happens in Step 5 without
prescribing *which* element of *which* resource implements it. A
follow-up comment from the reporter notes that this should have been
filed as a Change Request rather than a Technical Correction, but the
Jira form did not let her change the type after submission.

There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, Confluence
pages, or GitHub items associated with FHIR-41831 in the FhirAugury
index, so the entire context for the request comes from the ticket
itself.

## Proposed Dispositions

Because the cross-reference index surfaces no GitHub repository for the
International Birth And Child Model IG, the dispositions below name the
spec by title and describe the change at the narrative-page level. If
the work group identifies the canonical IG repository at triage, the
chosen disposition's "where" should be tightened to the specific
authoring file (typically a narrative `.md`/`.html` page under the IG's
`input/pagecontent/` or equivalent authoring root).

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Edit the narrative page in the International Birth And Child Model
(FHIR) IG that contains the workflow step list, and rewrite the last
sentence of **Step 5** so that it no longer names a specific FHIR
resource or element. The revised sentence should describe Step 5 at the
same conceptual level used for Steps 1–4 (what business event happens,
who is responsible, what information is captured) and leave the
resource/element binding to the IG's resource-mapping section, profile
definitions, or implementation guidance pages. No StructureDefinitions,
profiles, value sets, or examples need to change — this is a
prose-only edit on the narrative authoring page.

#### Justification

The reporter is correct that mixing abstraction levels inside a
sequenced step list reads as inconsistency and creates the false
impression that Steps 1–4 do not also have a concrete resource/element
binding. HL7 IG narrative conventions favour describing the workflow
in business terms and keeping the FHIR mapping in a dedicated
section/table so readers can reason about the workflow independently
of the resource-binding choice. The change is purely editorial, scoped
to one paragraph, and has no balloted-content impact, so it can ride
along with the next minor publication of the IBCM IG. Filing under
*Technical Correction* (as the reporter accidentally did) is in fact
the correct lane for a prose-only, non-substantive edit, despite the
reporter's follow-up note asking to reclassify it.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than only deleting the resource/element reference from Step 5,
**normalise the entire step list** so that every step is presented in
two registers: a one-sentence business description (the abstract
level the reporter wants), followed by an explicit, separately-styled
"FHIR mapping" callout that names the relevant resource and element(s).
Apply this to Steps 1–5 (and any additional steps in the same list).
This can be done either inline (e.g., a small table or definition list
under each step) or by adding a single "Resource mapping for the
workflow" table immediately after the step list and removing all
inline resource references from the step prose.

#### Justification

The reporter's underlying complaint is *inconsistency*, not the
existence of resource detail per se. Removing the detail from Step 5
(Disposition A) fixes the symmetry but loses information that is
genuinely useful to implementers, who otherwise have to hunt for the
resource binding elsewhere. Promoting the resource binding to a
first-class, structurally-uniform element of every step preserves the
information, satisfies the consistency request, and matches the
pattern used by mature HL7 IGs (e.g., narrative step lists paired with
mapping tables). The cost is a slightly larger editorial change and
one round of co-editor review, but it is still narrative-only and
ballot-neutral.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change as a no-op editorial preference. Close as
*Persuasive with Mod* or *Not Persuasive* with the explanation that
the apparent abstraction-level mismatch in Step 5 is intentional:
Steps 1–4 describe events that have multiple possible resource
realisations, while Step 5 has a single, canonical element binding
that is worth surfacing inline because it removes ambiguity for
implementers. No change to the IG.

#### Justification

A decline is defensible only if the IBCM authors confirm that the
inline element reference in Step 5 is load-bearing — i.e., omitting it
would cause implementers to pick the wrong element or to overlook a
required binding. In that case, the consistency cost is outweighed by
the implementation-clarity benefit, and the better fix is for the
authors to leave Step 5 alone and instead add a short note explaining
*why* this step is described at a finer grain than the others.
Without that confirmation, decline is hard to justify because the
ticket is editorial, low-risk, and easy to action.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition A satisfies the literal request but throws away useful
implementer-facing information, which is the wrong direction for an
IG whose readers are precisely the people who need the
resource/element bindings. Disposition C presumes a load-bearing
reason for the inconsistency that is not evidenced anywhere in the
ticket or the surrounding discussion. Disposition B addresses the
reporter's actual concern (consistency across the step list) while
*also* improving the page for implementers by giving every step the
same business-prose-plus-mapping structure. The change stays
narrative-only, has no ballot impact, and converts a one-off editorial
fix into a small, durable improvement to the IG's documentation
pattern. The work group should ask the IBCM authors to confirm there
is no hidden semantic reason Step 5 was singled out before adopting B.
