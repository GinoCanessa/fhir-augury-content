## Summary

The ticket asserts that the canonical URL for the `observation-visual-field`
ValueSet, defined in the Ophthalmology Retinal FHIR IG, is incorrectly rooted
under `hl7.org/fhir` and should instead be rooted under
`terminology.hl7.org` (THO). The reporter further notes that a companion UTG
ticket should be opened so the ValueSet is properly registered in THO. No
cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were surfaced
by FHIR Augury for this ticket, so the analysis is grounded solely in the
ticket text itself.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Re-root the `observation-visual-field` ValueSet's canonical URL under THO.
Specifically:

1. In the Ophthalmology Retinal FHIR IG source, change the ValueSet's
   `url` element from the current `http://hl7.org/fhir/...` form to
   `http://terminology.hl7.org/ValueSet/observation-visual-field` (or the
   equivalent THO path agreed with the Vocabulary work group).
2. Open a companion UTG ticket so the ValueSet (and any backing CodeSystem
   that is similarly mis-rooted) is formally registered in THO and
   published under that canonical.
3. Once the THO publication is in place, retire / redirect the old
   `hl7.org/fhir/...` canonical so existing references resolve.

#### Justification

This matches HL7 policy that terminology artifacts (CodeSystems,
ValueSets, NamingSystems, ConceptMaps) live under `terminology.hl7.org`
rather than `hl7.org/fhir`, and routes governance through UTG. The
reporter's request is exactly that pattern, and the change is local to
one ValueSet definition plus a UTG submission.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the existing `hl7.org/fhir/...` canonical for backward compatibility
but additionally register the ValueSet in THO with a THO canonical, and
declare the THO canonical as the authoritative one going forward (e.g.,
via a `replaces`/`derivedFrom` relationship and a deprecation notice on
the old canonical). The IG would continue to reference the artifact by
its THO canonical, while the legacy URL remains resolvable to avoid
breaking existing instances and downstream profiles.

#### Justification

The ValueSet has been published under the current canonical for some
time; implementers may already bind to it. A hard re-root risks breaking
those bindings. A dual-publish-with-redirect approach honours the policy
intent (THO is the system of record) without invalidating in-flight
implementations, and matches how other mis-rooted terminology artifacts
have been migrated in past UTG cycles.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request and leave the canonical URL as-is, on the basis that
the Ophthalmology Retinal IG is a low-maturity / experimental
specification and any URL churn should wait until the IG itself is
re-balloted, at which point all canonicals can be normalised together.

#### Justification

If the IG is not actively being consumed at scale, the cost of changing
canonicals (UTG submission, IG republish, redirect maintenance) may
outweigh the benefit of policy alignment in the short term. Bundling
the change with a future ballot cycle reduces churn for implementers
and avoids a half-migrated state.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket reports a straightforward policy violation: a terminology
artifact is published under the wrong canonical root. HL7's stated
direction is that ValueSets and CodeSystems live under
`terminology.hl7.org` and are governed via UTG, and the reporter has
already flagged the need for the companion UTG ticket. Disposition B's
dual-publish hedge is reasonable only if there is concrete evidence of
existing implementer bindings to the legacy URL — none was surfaced in
the available cross-references or Zulip history. Disposition C defers a
known issue with no offsetting benefit. Patient Care should accept the
request, coordinate with Vocabulary on the UTG submission, and update
the Ophthalmology Retinal IG to bind to the THO canonical in the next
publication.
