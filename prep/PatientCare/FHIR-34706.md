## Summary

The reporter asks that the US SDOH Clinical Care (SDOHCC) IG **reuse the
already-published `odh-EmploymentStatus` profile from the Occupational
Data for Health (ODH) IG** rather than defining its own employment-status
representation. The intent is to avoid two parallel HL7 representations
of the same concept (employment status) — one in the SDOHCC IG and one
in ODH — which would burden implementers and risk semantic drift between
guides. The ticket is specifically scoped to the SDOHCC IG (Patient Care
work group), as opposed to its sibling ticket
[FHIR-34701](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-34701), which addressed
the same SDOH-vs-ODH boundary problem in **US Core** (Cross-Group
Projects). FHIR-34701 was resolved Persuasive with Modification — adding
guidance clarifying that the SDOH construct in US Core does not cover
detailed work information and footnoting that ODH content may be pulled
into US Core later via the Growth path. FHIR-34706 was originally
dispositioned **Not Persuasive** by Patient Care (vote Bob Dieterle /
Laura Heerman‑Langford 8‑0‑0, 2022‑10‑10) on the rationale that the
SDOHCC and ODH representations are use-case-specific and do not
conflict. The reporter rejected that resolution: the negative vote was
not withdrawn and SDOHCC 2.0.0 STU2 published without satisfying the
request, so on 2022‑12‑14/15 the reporter reverted the disposition,
flagged the issue as distinct from FHIR-34701, and asked Patient Care
to discuss. The ticket therefore lands back in the work group as an
unresolved negative ballot at Highest priority on a published STU.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the **US SDOH Clinical Care IG (Patient Care)** to depend on the
published Occupational Data for Health (ODH) IG and reuse
`http://hl7.org/fhir/us/odh/StructureDefinition/odh-EmploymentStatus`
as the canonical representation of employment status within SDOHCC.
Concretely:

- Add ODH as a `dependsOn` IG dependency in the SDOHCC `ImplementationGuide`
  resource (pin to the version that publishes
  `odh-EmploymentStatus`).
- Where SDOHCC currently surfaces "employment status" — whether as a
  bare `Observation` pattern, a category code, a value set entry, or a
  derived profile — replace that representation with a normative
  reference to `odh-EmploymentStatus` (e.g., narrative + a profile
  binding / "use this profile when recording employment status").
- If SDOHCC has its own employment-status profile, deprecate it in
  favor of `odh-EmploymentStatus` and add a migration note in the IG
  changelog for STU2 → next.
- Add a guidance page (or extend the existing Social History / SDOH
  guidance) explaining the ODH↔SDOHCC boundary: SDOHCC consumers
  capturing employment status in an SDOH workflow SHOULD instantiate
  `odh-EmploymentStatus`; richer occupational data (industry,
  occupation, usual work, employment classification, etc.) remains in
  ODH.

#### Justification

This is what the reporter asked for and what the negative ballot vote
is gated on; it directly resolves the unwithdrawn negative on STU2.
HL7's general design principle is "one normative representation per
concept" — having SDOHCC and ODH publish independent employment-status
representations forces every implementer who touches both domains to
write translation logic and creates real risk of value-set / code drift
between guides. ODH's `EmploymentStatus` is already published, has been
used in production, and is owned by the work group with the deepest
domain expertise on occupational data. Reuse via an IG dependency is
the standard FHIR mechanism for exactly this case and is consistent
with the forward-looking footnote already accepted in FHIR-34701 ("US
Core may add more detailed work information, such as ODH"). The cost
is a new IG dependency for SDOHCC and a versioning coordination
burden, both of which are normal IG hygiene rather than novel risk.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Do not change SDOHCC's own structures, but **publish a
harmonization/guidance page** (and matching narrative in the relevant
SDOHCC profile pages) that:

1. Explicitly maps SDOHCC's employment-status representation to
   `odh-EmploymentStatus` element-by-element, including code-system /
   LOINC alignment.
2. States that an instance conforming to SDOHCC's employment
   representation SHOULD also conform to `odh-EmploymentStatus` when
   the data is being shared with an ODH-aware consumer, and provides
   an example resource that validates against both.
3. Commits SDOHCC + ODH to joint maintenance of the value set(s) used
   for employment status (single source of truth in THO or ODH, with
   SDOHCC binding to the same value set), so that the two
   representations cannot semantically diverge even if they remain
   structurally distinct.
4. Schedules SDOHCC's own employment-status profile for deprecation in
   the next major SDOHCC release, contingent on ODH being on the US
   Core / SDOHCC growth path by then.

This is "Persuasive with Modification" rather than full adoption: the
SDOHCC IG remains structurally self-contained for STU2's installed
base, but the spec stops asserting that the two representations are
unrelated and removes the implementer ambiguity.

#### Justification

This addresses the substantive complaint — that SDOHCC implicitly
re-invents employment status in parallel with ODH — without forcing a
breaking IG-dependency change into a published STU. It also respects
the original 2022‑10‑10 vote's underlying point that the two IGs were
written for different use cases and have different installed bases:
existing SDOHCC implementers do not have to take an ODH dependency
overnight. By binding both IGs to the same value set and providing a
normative cross-walk, the work group eliminates the practical harm
(divergence, double-mapping) while leaving the structural cleanup for
a deliberate, scheduled deprecation. This is also consistent with the
disposition Cross-Group Projects took for FHIR-34701, where the fix
was clarifying guidance rather than restructuring profiles.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Re-affirm the prior **Not Persuasive** disposition: SDOHCC's
employment-status representation and ODH's `odh-EmploymentStatus` serve
different use cases (SDOH screening / social-history capture vs. full
occupational-data exchange). SDOHCC will continue to publish its own
representation and will not take a normative dependency on the ODH IG.
Add (at most) a non-normative "see also" pointer from the SDOHCC
employment-related content to the ODH IG so implementers are aware
that ODH exists for richer occupational data.

#### Justification

This is the work group's already-recorded position (8‑0‑0 vote,
2022‑10‑10) and matches the resolution that Cross-Group Projects took
for the parallel US Core ticket FHIR-34701, which deliberately kept
SDOH and ODH separate and only added clarifying prose. Taking a
normative dependency on ODH from a *published* SDOHCC STU2 is a
non-trivial change: it imposes an ODH IG dependency on every SDOHCC
implementer, requires version-pinning maintenance, and risks breaking
existing SDOHCC instances that already conform to SDOHCC's own profile.
If the work group has not heard from SDOHCC implementers asking for
this consolidation (the ticket itself is from the ODH community, not
the SDOHCC implementer community), declining is defensible.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

A straight Decline (C) is hard to defend a second time on the same
ticket: the negative ballot was never withdrawn, the SDOHCC STU2
publish has already shipped without satisfying it, and the reporter
has explicitly rejected the SDOH-vs-ODH "different use cases"
rationale. Continuing to reassert C without new substance invites the
negative vote to escalate. Accept-as-Requested (A) is the cleanest
long-term answer and is what the reporter actually wants, but
introducing a hard ODH IG dependency and deprecating an existing
SDOHCC profile mid-STU is a meaningful breaking change for current
implementers and, given that no SDOHCC GitHub repository surfaced in
the cross-references, the work group should not commit to it without
checking the implementation impact in the live IG repo first.

Disposition B threads that needle: it gives the reporter a normative,
auditable harmonization (shared value set, published cross-walk,
scheduled deprecation) that removes the *substantive* divergence
between SDOHCC and ODH, while deferring the structural IG-dependency
change to a planned major release. It is also symmetric with how
Cross-Group Projects resolved the sibling US Core ticket FHIR-34701
(Persuasive with Modification via guidance, with a forward path to
fuller ODH adoption), which makes it easier to coordinate jointly with
CGP and ODH stewards. Patient Care should adopt B and explicitly
commit to A in the next major SDOHCC release, contingent on ODH being
on the agreed growth path.
