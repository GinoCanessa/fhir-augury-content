## Summary

The ticket, raised by John Stamm against the US SDOH Clinical Care (SDOHCC)
IG, observes that "enrollment status" is a need shared with other use cases
and notes that the base FHIR resources `EnrollmentRequest` and
`EnrollmentResponse` are categorised as Financial resources. The reporter
recommends that the SDOHCC IG either align with those existing mechanisms
or explicitly describe how its enrollment-status need differs from the
similar need addressed by other guides (suggesting, for example, that
elements such as a recertification date may be unique to the SDOHCC use
case). No related Jira tickets were surfaced via cross-references; the
only cross-references found point to two Zulip block-vote announcement
threads (mirrored on Patient Care WG and Public Health) that listed this
ticket among items proposed to be dispositioned **Not Persuasive with
Modification**.

## Details

**Description:**

Enrollment status seems to be a shared need with other use cases.
EnrollmentRequest and EnrollmentResponse are currently listed as Financial
resources.

Recommend that the guide either aligns with or describes the differences
between this need and the similar need in other guides. For example, maybe
recertification date would be something to include unique to this use case.

**Resolution Description (from snapshot):**

EnrollmentRequest and EnrollmentResponse are immature resources in the FHIR
Base specification (FMM Level 0), so we do not feel that these would be
useful for our IG. We are going to review the Coverage resource based on
feedback from FM WG. Due to a request for more clarity on our decisions,
please review the comment from Corey Smith in the Comments section for
more detail.

**Comments:**

- **JIRAUSER24103 (Aaron Nusstein, 2026-01-29):** Confirmed that the
  Gravity team reviewed `EnrollmentRequest` / `EnrollmentResponse` and
  considered them insufficient for the proposed workflows because they
  are stubs at FMM Level 0. After consultation with the Financial
  Management WG, the team was advised to also consider `Coverage` and
  intends to investigate that resource.
- **JIRAUSER16901 (Corey Smith, 2026-04-03 — Patient Care block-vote
  clarification):** Provided three clarifications:
  1. **Use case.** Gravity's enrollment status enables providers and
     CBOs to share information about a person's or household's
     enrollment in social-care programs, in order to (a) improve care
     coordination, (b) reduce administrative burden of repeatedly
     investigating program enrollment, and (c) prevent duplicated
     services across overlapping programs.
  2. **Modelling choice.** Gravity chose `Observation` for enrollment
     status because the resource is widely adopted and lowers the
     implementation barrier.
  3. **Why not EnrollmentRequest/EnrollmentResponse.** Both are FMM
     Level 0; both are interpreted as scoped to health-insurance
     subscriber/dependent enrollment; and neither allows the program
     into which an individual is enrolled to be expressed as a simple
     `CodeableConcept` (e.g., SNAP). Gravity therefore intends to
     proceed with `Observation` and is open to revisiting if
     `EnrollmentRequest`/`EnrollmentResponse` mature.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the SDOHCC Observation Program Enrollment Status profile page (and
any companion guidance page on Coverage exploration) to add an explicit
**"Relationship to other enrollment-related resources"** section that
itemises:

- `EnrollmentRequest` / `EnrollmentResponse` — financial-domain,
  insurance-subscriber/dependent scope, currently FMM Level 0; not
  selected for SDOHCC.
- `Coverage` — under active investigation per FM WG advice; describe its
  fit (or lack thereof) once the analysis is complete.
- The chosen `Observation`-based representation — explain why it was
  preferred (program identified by `CodeableConcept` such as SNAP,
  widespread implementer familiarity, lower adoption barrier).

The same section should call out elements that are unique to the SDOHCC
need and not naturally expressed by the financial resources, with
**recertification date** named explicitly per the reporter's example, and
should commit to revisiting alignment as `EnrollmentRequest`/
`EnrollmentResponse` mature.

#### Justification

This is what the reporter literally asked for: "either align with or
describe the differences." The Gravity team's existing comments already
contain the substance of the rationale (FMM Level 0, insurance-subscriber
scope, inability to express programs as `CodeableConcept`); promoting
that rationale from a Jira comment into the IG narrative is
low-risk, low-cost, and pre-empts the same question being raised by
future reviewers and implementers. It is also consistent with FHIR's
general guidance to discuss boundaries with adjacent resources on profile
intro pages.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Mark this ticket **Persuasive with Modification** but split the work:

1. In the SDOHCC IG, add only a short cross-reference paragraph on the
   Program Enrollment Status profile page noting that `EnrollmentRequest`
   /`EnrollmentResponse` exist in the Financial domain, are FMM 0, and
   are not used by SDOHCC; defer the deeper "Coverage vs. Observation"
   analysis to ticket
   [FHIR-54912](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54912) ("Add technical
   requirements and guidance from Enrollment Status to the SDOHCC
   Observation Program Enrollment Status Profile page"), which is
   already in the same block vote and is the natural home for technical
   guidance on this profile.
2. File (or surface, if it exists) a base-spec / FM-WG cross-IG ticket to
   capture the broader observation that "program enrollment status" is a
   shared need, so that resource maturation work on
   `EnrollmentRequest`/`EnrollmentResponse` (and any harmonisation with
   `Coverage`) is tracked in the right venue rather than only inside an
   STU3 IG.

#### Justification

This avoids duplicating guidance that another in-flight ticket
(FHIR-54912) is already chartered to write, while still giving the
reporter a visible response inside SDOHCC. It also recognises that the
underlying request — "shared need across guides" — is genuinely a
cross-IG / base-spec concern and is unlikely to be solved by SDOHCC
narrative alone; routing the harmonisation question to FM WG / FHIR-I
keeps it on a track where it can actually move, particularly given that
`EnrollmentRequest`/`EnrollmentResponse` would need to mature beyond FMM
0 before SDOHCC could realistically reconsider.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (current resolution: Not Persuasive with Modification)

#### Proposal

Confirm the existing resolution of **Not Persuasive with Modification**:
the SDOHCC IG retains its `Observation`-based Program Enrollment Status
profile, does not adopt `EnrollmentRequest`/`EnrollmentResponse`, and
does not undertake additional alignment narrative beyond what is already
on the profile page. The "Modification" component is the clarifying
comment posted by Gravity (Corey Smith, 2026-04-03) explaining the
choice; that comment, plus any minor tightening Gravity already plans
under FHIR-54912, is treated as sufficient response.

#### Justification

The Gravity team has already done the analysis the reporter asked for:
they have evaluated `EnrollmentRequest`/`EnrollmentResponse`, consulted
FM WG, are independently investigating `Coverage`, and have documented
their reasoning in two ticket comments. The resources the reporter
points at are FMM Level 0 stubs scoped to insurance subscriber/dependent
enrollment and cannot, today, express enrollment in a non-insurance
program as a `CodeableConcept`; using them would actually *reduce*
implementer accessibility relative to `Observation`. Declining preserves
the IG's modelling choice without committing to additional editorial
work, and the Jira record itself preserves the rationale for future
readers.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

While the existing resolution (C) is technically defensible — Gravity
*has* done the work — the reporter's actual ask is editorial: make the
reasoning visible inside the IG so that the next reviewer or
implementer does not have to find a Jira comment from April 2026 to
understand why SDOHCC chose `Observation` over the financial enrollment
resources. Doing that surgically on the Program Enrollment Status profile
page (Disposition A) costs little, directly addresses the comment that
prompted the block-vote clarification request in the first place, and
leaves the door open for `Coverage` or a matured
`EnrollmentRequest`/`EnrollmentResponse` to be reconsidered later.
Disposition B is a reasonable second choice if the workgroup judges that
FHIR-54912 already carries this narrative; in that case, A's content
should be folded into FHIR-54912's resolution rather than dropped
entirely.
