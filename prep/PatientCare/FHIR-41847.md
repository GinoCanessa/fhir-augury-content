## Summary

The reporter, Ana Kostadinovska, asks Patient Care to correct and clarify a
statement in the International Birth And Child Model (FHIR) IG. Specifically,
in the first bullet point of the page in question, the sentence reads "The
woman is the subject of this data, the focal subject could be a different
entity like the pregnancy." The reporter finds the term "focal subject"
unclear and notes that pregnancy is more naturally described as an *episode*
than as an *entity*. They request that the sentence be rewritten with more
precise terminology and additional explanation. No cross-referenced Jira
tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were surfaced for this ticket, so the
discussion context is limited to the ticket itself.

## Proposed Dispositions

The dispositions below are editorial in nature because the ticket is a
narrative-clarification request against IG prose. Because no GitHub
cross-references were surfaced, the specific authoring root and file path
cannot be drawn from a repo-analysis briefing; the workgroup should map the
chosen disposition to the IBCM IG source repo before applying.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Rewrite the offending bullet in the IBCM IG narrative to (a) drop the
ambiguous term "focal subject", (b) correctly characterise pregnancy as an
*episode* rather than an *entity*, and (c) explain why the woman is recorded
as the `subject` even when the clinical focus is the pregnancy. Suggested
replacement text (to be polished by the editor):

> "The `subject` of these resources is the woman, because the data is
> recorded about her. The clinical focus of the information, however, may be
> a related concept such as the pregnancy itself — which is modelled as an
> `EpisodeOfCare` (or equivalent) rather than as a separate patient. Where
> the resource supports it (for example, `Observation.focus`), the pregnancy
> episode can be referenced explicitly so that consumers can distinguish
> 'data about the woman' from 'data whose clinical focus is the pregnancy'."

#### Justification

The reporter's complaint is concrete and well-founded: "focal subject" is not
a defined term anywhere in core FHIR, and calling a pregnancy an "entity"
conflates a temporal episode with a participating thing. The proposed
rewrite uses terminology that is already established elsewhere in FHIR
(`subject`, `focus`, `EpisodeOfCare`), so it improves clarity without
introducing new concepts. Editorial fixes of this kind are routine and
low-risk for an IG that is still maturing.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than only rewriting the single bullet, take the opportunity to add a
short **"Subject vs. focus" sub-section** to the IBCM IG that explains, once
and in one place, the modelling pattern the IG uses for woman-vs-pregnancy
data. Replace the bullet with a one-sentence pointer to the new sub-section
("See *Subject vs. focus* for how the woman, the pregnancy, and the child
are related on these resources."). The new sub-section should:

1. State that the `subject` element always identifies the human patient
   (woman, neonate, or child) the record is about.
2. State that pregnancy is represented as an `EpisodeOfCare` (or a
   profile/extension where the IG defines one) and is referenced from
   resources via `Observation.focus`, `Encounter.episodeOfCare`, or the
   IG-defined extension(s).
3. Provide a small worked example showing an Observation about the woman
   whose focus is the pregnancy episode.

#### Justification

The same confusion that prompted FHIR-41847 is likely to recur on other
pages of the IG that talk about "the woman" and "the pregnancy" together.
Centralising the explanation prevents the IG from accumulating subtly
inconsistent narrative in multiple bullets, and gives implementers a single
canonical statement to cite. The cost is modestly higher than a one-bullet
edit, but the maintenance benefit over the life of the IG is meaningful.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change on the grounds that it is purely editorial and that the
intended meaning is recoverable from context: "focal subject" can be read as
informal English meaning "the thing the data is really about", and the
woman/pregnancy distinction is conventional in maternity informatics. Mark
the ticket as "Not Persuasive – Editorial Only" and close.

#### Justification

The IG is still under development and editorial polish is normally batched
into a pre-publication pass; one ambiguous bullet does not change conformance
or interoperability. Declining preserves WG cycles for substantive issues.
The reporter can be invited to submit a pull request against the IG source
during the next editorial window.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition A correctly fixes the immediate problem, but the underlying
"woman vs. pregnancy" modelling question is foundational to a maternity IG
and is almost certainly being asked (or quietly mis-answered) by readers in
other places too. Disposition B costs only a little more than A — a short
sub-section plus a pointer — and produces a single, citable explanation that
the rest of the IG can reuse, eliminating the entire class of confusion that
FHIR-41847 represents. Disposition C is hard to defend: the reporter has
flagged that the existing wording introduces an undefined term ("focal
subject") and an incorrect ontological claim ("pregnancy ... entity"), and
both are cheap to fix now while the IG is pre-ballot. The workgroup should
therefore adopt B, with A as an acceptable fallback if editorial bandwidth
is tight.
