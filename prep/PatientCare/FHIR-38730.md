## Summary

Robert Dieterle raised this ticket against the US SDOH Clinical Care
implementation guide to drive a conversation about how race and ethnicity
observations should be structured. Although the guide currently recommends
collecting race and ethnicity together, in practice they are frequently
recorded separately, which the existing single-Observation-with-components
approach does not handle gracefully. The reporter notes three concrete
concerns: (1) separate collection should be representable as separate
Observations; (2) the SDOH approach should remain consistent with US Core,
which models race and ethnicity as **separate extensions** on the Patient
profile rather than as one combined structure; and (3) the use of
`dataAbsentReason` at the Observation level applies to *all* components,
which means race cannot be exchanged independently of ethnicity (or vice
versa) when one is absent. The ticket explicitly defers the broader design
question â€” whether SDOH-CC should adopt a Personal Characteristics-style
parent grouping Observation to bundle related demographic observations
that are collected at the same time â€” to a follow-up ticket. No
cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were found
for this issue in the available indexes.

## Details

**Description:**

While there is a specific recommendation regarding the collection of race
and ethnicity at the same time, they are frequently collected separately
and should be represented as separate observations. In addition, we have
tried to follow the current US Core approach to race and ethnicity which
uses separate extension to the Patient profile. Finally, the use of
`dataAbsentReason` can only apply to all components and would prevent the
exchange of race or ethnicity without both being present.

Should have further conversations regarding Personal Characteristics or a
parent grouping for multiple observations as part of one grouping (need to
consider timing of data collection). Will require a new ticket to create a
grouping observation.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Restructure the SDOH-CC race and ethnicity guidance so that race and
ethnicity are modeled as **two separate Observation profiles** (e.g.,
`SDOHCC-ObservationRace` and `SDOHCC-ObservationEthnicity`) rather than
as a single Observation carrying both as components. Each profile would:

- Use a distinct `Observation.code` (race vs. ethnicity, drawn from the
  same value sets US Core uses for the corresponding Patient
  extensions).
- Carry the coded value(s) in `Observation.value[x]` (or repeating
  `component` for OMB category + detailed + text, mirroring the US Core
  race / ethnicity extension internal structure).
- Allow `dataAbsentReason` to be set independently per Observation, so
  one demographic can be exchanged with a known value while the other
  is asserted absent/unknown.
- Update the SDOH-CC IG narrative to remove the "collect together"
  preference, or at least demote it from a structural assumption to a
  workflow note, and explicitly state alignment with the US Core
  Patient race/ethnicity extension model.

The "grouping Observation" question (a parent Observation that bundles
related personal-characteristic Observations collected at the same
encounter) is split out into a follow-up ticket as the reporter
requests, rather than being resolved here.

#### Justification

This proposal directly addresses all three concerns the reporter raised.
Splitting into two Observations matches real-world collection patterns
(race and ethnicity are routinely captured in separate UI controls,
separate forms, or asynchronously) and lets `dataAbsentReason` operate
at the right granularity â€” per demographic â€” so that a known race can be
sent alongside an unknown/declined ethnicity without forcing both to be
absent. It also keeps SDOH-CC consistent with US Core, where race and
ethnicity are already two distinct extensions on `Patient`; mirroring
that separation in the Observation model reduces cognitive load for
implementers who are already mapping between the Patient extensions and
SDOH-CC Observations. Deferring the parent-grouping question to a new
ticket honors the reporter's explicit guidance and keeps the scope of
this change reviewable.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep a single `SDOHCC-ObservationRaceAndEthnicity` profile but loosen
its structure so it can represent either or both demographics:

- Make both the race `component` and the ethnicity `component` optional
  (cardinality `0..*`).
- Allow `dataAbsentReason` at the **component** level (each component's
  `Observation.component.dataAbsentReason`) rather than relying on the
  Observation-level `dataAbsentReason`, so race-absent / ethnicity-known
  (and vice versa) can be expressed within one resource.
- Add IG guidance that when only one demographic is collected, the
  other component MAY simply be omitted (since it is `0..*`), or MAY be
  included with `component.dataAbsentReason` if the absence itself is
  meaningful.
- Optionally constrain the profile so that at least one component
  (race or ethnicity) is present (`component.where(...).count() >= 1`).

#### Justification

This preserves the current SDOH-CC structural pattern and the message
that race and ethnicity are conceptually paired demographics, which is
how clinical/SDOH workflows often *intend* them to be collected even
when execution differs. Using component-level `dataAbsentReason` is a
standard FHIR pattern and resolves the asymmetric-absence problem
without doubling the number of Observation profiles or breaking
existing implementations that already produce the combined Observation.
The trade-off versus Disposition A is divergence from the US Core
*Patient extension* model: US Core uses two separate extensions, so
keeping a single combined Observation forces implementers to map
1-extension-to-1-component on the way in and 1-component-to-1-extension
on the way out, which is more bookkeeping than two parallel
Observations.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the structural change and instead add **non-normative IG
guidance** clarifying that:

- The combined race-and-ethnicity Observation is the SDOH-CC preferred
  pattern when both values are collected in a single workflow event.
- Implementers who collect the demographics asynchronously SHOULD
  continue to use the US Core Patient race and ethnicity extensions
  directly for those data, reserving the SDOH-CC Observation for cases
  where both are present and an Observation-shaped record is required
  (e.g., screening encounter capture).
- Asymmetric absence is out of scope for this Observation; if a value
  is unknown, the existing US Core Patient extension already supports
  data-absent-reason on a per-extension basis.

#### Justification

A defensible "decline" rests on the fact that FHIR already provides a
mechanism for race and ethnicity that handles asymmetric absence
cleanly: the US Core Patient race and ethnicity extensions, each with
their own `ombCategory`, `detailed`, `text`, and (via standard FHIR
extension semantics) absence handling. Adding either a second
Observation profile (Disposition A) or a more permissive component
model (Disposition B) creates two ways to say the same thing about a
patient and obliges receivers to reconcile them. Restricting the
Observation form to "joint collection" use cases preserves a single
authoritative source for steady-state demographics (the Patient
extensions) while still letting SDOH-CC capture the encounter-level
event when both are gathered together.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A is the cleanest answer to what the reporter actually
described: real-world separate collection, alignment with US Core's
already-separate extension model, and per-demographic
`dataAbsentReason`. Disposition B (component-level `dataAbsentReason`)
solves the absence problem in isolation but leaves the
US-Core-vs-SDOH-CC structural mismatch in place and forces implementers
to keep mapping a combined Observation onto two separate Patient
extensions. Disposition C is intellectually defensible but in practice
is unlikely to satisfy the SDOH-CC community, because the IG already
defines race and ethnicity Observations as part of its core model, and
walking that back to "use the Patient extensions instead" would be a
larger consistency break than splitting one profile into two. Choosing
A also preserves the reporter's stated next step â€” a follow-up ticket
to design a parent grouping Observation for related personal
characteristics â€” without preempting that design.
