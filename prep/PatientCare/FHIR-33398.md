## Summary

The ticket asks how a clinician (or system) should record the *administration*
of a biological product — for example, a blood transfusion — in FHIR Core,
and notes that the `Procedure` resource appears to be a plausible candidate.
The reporter is essentially asking for guidance/normative direction on which
resource(s) to use for BDP administration, which today is implicit at best.

Two related Patient Care tickets give this request a much wider scope than
the headline suggests:

- [FHIR-51207](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51207) ("Resources for
  tracking BDP are possibly incomplete") tabulates the `Medication`/`Device`
  request–dispense–administration–statement–definition pattern against
  `BiologicallyDerivedProduct` and shows that BDP only has a `Dispense`
  resource — there is no analogue to `MedicationRequest`,
  `MedicationAdministration`, or `MedicationStatement`. It explicitly raises
  the same "how do I record 'I had a blood transfusion 20 years ago'"
  question.
- [FHIR-13047](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-13047) ("Add
  DosageInstructions to Procedure") proposes patching `Procedure` with a
  `dosageInstruction` element so that blood-product administration can be
  represented inside a `Procedure` instance.

This ticket has had a long disposition history. Patient Care voted
"Considered for Future Use" on 2023-02-09 (6-0-0, Lyle/Townley O'Neill),
deferring resolution until after R5. After R5 publication it was reopened
for R6 (2023-03-30), and on 2025-03-14 was marked **CONSIDERED (pending
deep dive on use case)**. The most recent comment (2025-09-17) flags an
Orders & Observations linkage via FHIR-51207.

## Related Jira Tickets

### [FHIR-51207: Resources for tracking BDP are possibly incomplete](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51207)

Filed by Elliot Silver (Triaged, Unresolved). Documents the gap between the
Medication / Device families (which both have request, dispense,
administration/usage, statement and definition resources) and the BDP
family, which currently only has `BiologicallyDerivedProduct` and
`BiologicallyDerivedProductDispense`. It asks Patient Care / OO to decide
whether BDP administration, request, and statement should be modelled by
adding new resources, by extending existing ones (e.g., letting
`MedicationStatement.medication` reference a BDP), or by continuing to use
`Procedure` / `ServiceRequest`. This is the broader, structural counterpart
to FHIR-33398's narrower "where does a transfusion go?" question.

Users involved: Elliot Silver (reporter), Michelle Moseman Miller, Lloyd
McKenzie, Jean Duteau, Yanick Gaudet, Stephen Chu (via the linked Zulip
thread).

### [FHIR-13047: Add DosageInstructions to Procedure](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-13047)

Filed by Jamie Hignite (Triaged, Unresolved). Proposes adding a `0..*`
`dosageInstruction` element to `Procedure` so that blood-product
administration can be modelled within a single `Procedure` (with multiple
dosage entries tied to a `ProcedureRequest`). This represents the
"stay-with-Procedure but enrich it" school of thought for FHIR-33398.

Users involved: Jamie Hignite (reporter).

## Related Zulip Discussions

### [#implementers > Patient assertion of blood transfusion](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/implementers/topic/Patient%20assertion%20of%20blood%20transfusion)

Started by Elliot Silver in June 2025 and explicitly cited from FHIR-51207.
Elliot asks where, after the fact, a blood transfusion should be recorded —
`Procedure`, `MedicationStatement` (awkward because blood is a BDP, not a
`Medication`), `Observation`, or `Condition` (for "history of …"). Yanick
Gaudet wonders whether the same event could be a `Procedure` when performed
and an `Observation` (SNOMED 161664006, history-of blood transfusion) when
the patient mentions it later. Lloyd McKenzie pings Michelle (Moseman)
Miller for a Patient Care view. Jean Duteau reports that the ICSR AE
Reporting IG profiled blood transfusions on `Procedure`
([icsr-transfusion](https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/fhir-icsr-ae-reporting/StructureDefinition-icsr-transfusion.html))
and would record post-hoc transfusions there too, but concedes they did not
explicitly consider patient-reported history. Stephen Chu (late July 2025)
splits the question by exchange need: `Procedure` is fine for *summary*
recording (code, outcome, complication), but for full administration
detail — pack/donation IDs, blood group, expiry, start time, rate/volume,
total administered, plus per-interval vitals and reactions — he advocates
`MedicationAdministration` for (1) product and (2) administration data and
`Observation` for (3) the time-lapse vitals. No consensus was reached; the
thread closes with Patient Care leadership tagged but not yet responding.

Participants: Elliot Silver, Yanick Gaudet, Lloyd McKenzie, Jean Duteau,
Stephen Chu (with Michelle Moseman Miller pinged).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt `Procedure` as the canonical FHIR Core mechanism for recording the
administration of biologically derived products (blood transfusions, organ
transplants, cell therapies, etc.), and codify that decision in the
specification:

- Add explicit narrative to the `Procedure` resource boundaries/scope
  page stating that BDP administration is in scope and is the preferred
  mechanism, with `Procedure.code` carrying the SNOMED procedure code
  (e.g., `33389009 | Transfusion of whole blood`), `Procedure.subject`
  the patient, `Procedure.performed[x]` the time, and
  `Procedure.used[x]` referencing the `BiologicallyDerivedProduct`(s)
  consumed.
- Add cross-links from `BiologicallyDerivedProduct` and
  `BiologicallyDerivedProductDispense` resource pages to `Procedure` for
  the administration step.
- Add a worked example (e.g., `procedure-example-transfusion`)
  demonstrating the pattern, including the link to the dispense and to
  the BDP itself.
- Note the patient-reported variant: a historical assertion ("I had a
  transfusion 20 years ago") is also a `Procedure` with
  `Procedure.status = completed`, `Procedure.reportedBoolean = true`
  (or `reported[x]` reference) and possibly `Procedure.recorder` /
  `Procedure.asserter`-style elements as already exist.

#### Justification

This is the lowest-disruption answer and aligns with current implementer
practice: the ICSR AE Reporting IG already profiles `Procedure` for blood
transfusion, and Stephen Chu confirms `Procedure` is appropriate for
summary recording. `Procedure.used[x]` was specifically added to let
products consumed by a procedure (devices, BDPs) be referenced, so the
plumbing already exists — the gap is purely documentation/guidance, which
is the lightest possible R6 lift and resolves the originally-asked
question. It is also consistent with how implant procedures are recorded
against `Device`, which the FHIR-51207 narrative explicitly draws as the
precedent.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Treat FHIR-33398 as a thin slice of the structural problem in
[FHIR-51207](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51207) and resolve it by
introducing BDP-family analogues to `MedicationAdministration` and
`MedicationStatement`:

- Add a new `BiologicallyDerivedProductAdministration` resource (and at
  least a `MedicationStatement`-equivalent for patient-reported history,
  e.g., `BiologicallyDerivedProductUsage` or by allowing
  `MedicationStatement.medication` to be a `CodeableReference` to a
  `BiologicallyDerivedProduct`).
- Define cardinalities and elements parallel to
  `MedicationAdministration` (dosage, rate, performer, route, body site,
  reason, device, supportingInformation, note) so that the
  product/donation/expiry/rate/volume detail Stephen Chu enumerates on
  Zulip can be exchanged structurally rather than crammed into
  `Procedure.note`.
- Update `Procedure` guidance to point at the new resource for detailed
  administration data, while still allowing `Procedure` for summary
  recording of the clinical event (so backwards compatibility for the
  many `Procedure`-based profiles, including the ICSR AE one, is
  preserved).
- Coordinate with Orders & Observations (per Michelle Miller's
  2025-09-17 comment) since FHIR-51207 sits primarily in the
  Medication / BDP space owned jointly with OO.

#### Justification

The Zulip thread shows that `Procedure` is "good enough for summary, not
good enough for full administration detail" — exactly the asymmetry that
`MedicationAdministration` already solves on the medication side. Adding
the missing BDP resources would (a) give `BiologicallyDerivedProductDispense`
a peer it currently lacks, (b) match the
Request/Dispense/Administer/Statement pattern users have already learned,
and (c) give the patient-reported "I had a transfusion" use case a clean
home that does not abuse `MedicationStatement.medication`. The downside is
scope: this is a multi-resource addition that very likely cannot land
without OO co-sponsorship and substantive ballot effort, which is exactly
why Patient Care deferred it to R6 in the first place. If the WG is
willing to invest that effort in R6, this disposition genuinely resolves
both FHIR-33398 and FHIR-51207 together.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve as **Persuasive with Mod / Not Persuasive — already addressed**:
no spec change. Capture in the resolution comment that BDP administration
is, by existing design, recorded with `Procedure` (using
`Procedure.code` + `Procedure.used[x]` referencing the
`BiologicallyDerivedProduct`), and that patient-reported history is
recorded the same way with `Procedure.reported[x] = true`. Direct future
implementer questions to the existing `Procedure` documentation and to
`BiologicallyDerivedProductDispense` for the supply-chain side.

#### Justification

The original question is fundamentally a guidance question, not a
modelling defect. The mechanisms exist, an established IG (ICSR AE
Reporting) already uses them, and Patient Care has deferred this
repeatedly since 2021 precisely because a structural answer is
disproportionate to the one-paragraph original ask. Declining here would
not block FHIR-51207 (which remains the proper place to do the
structural review with OO) and would not invalidate FHIR-13047 (which can
be considered on its own merits). The risk of declining is that the
"deep dive on use case" promised by the 2025-03-14 CONSIDERED note never
happens, and implementers continue to ask the same question on Zulip.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A (accept and document `Procedure` as the canonical
mechanism, with worked examples and cross-links) is the right next move
for R6. The Zulip thread shows real implementer confusion that is
already being resolved in IGs by reaching for `Procedure`, so the
spec should follow that path-of-use rather than leave it implicit. It
also keeps FHIR-33398 from continuing to bounce across ballot cycles
without action — a CONSIDERED ticket from 2021 with five comments and
no resolution is a poor signal to the community. Disposition B is
strictly better in modelling terms and is exactly the right answer for
[FHIR-51207](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51207), which is where
that work belongs (with OO co-sponsorship); pursuing it under
FHIR-33398 conflates two tickets and risks neither getting done in R6.
Disposition C should be avoided because doing nothing for a fourth
ballot cycle leaves the documentation gap unaddressed and doesn't honour
the 2025-03-14 commitment to a deep dive. The recommendation is
therefore: in R6, accept FHIR-33398 by clarifying `Procedure` guidance
and adding a transfusion example, and explicitly defer the
new-resource(s) question to FHIR-51207 with OO.
