## Summary

The ticket asks the IPS project (Patient Care work group) to clarify and
ultimately resolve the ambiguity between the **IPS as a FHIR document** and
the **IPS as a library of reusable profiles ("building blocks")**. The IPS
was originally designed as a Composition-anchored document, but in practice
much of the implementer interest is in the underlying minimal,
non-exhaustive profiles (Patient, AllergyIntolerance, Condition, Medication
statements, etc.) that could be reused outside the document context — for
example by the International Patient Access (IPA) work, by national IGs, or
by other cross-jurisdictional exchange scenarios. The reporter proposes (as
a future-consideration item) that a future version of the IPS FHIR IG might
publish *two layers* of profiles: (1) those used inside the IPS document,
and (2) less-constrained "library" profiles representing the minimal
building blocks suitable for reuse beyond the document scope. The same
two-layer approach is suggested for the CEN/future ISO IPS dataset. The
ticket has no cross-referenced Jira tickets and no GitHub references; the
only external link is the originating Zulip thread on `#IPS > IPS:
Document Vs Library`. It was originally resolved "Considered for Future
Use" on 2020-02-04 (12-0-1, Hausam/Zautke) with a request to identify
concrete examples justifying the less-constrained profiles, and was
re-opened post-STU update publication in November 2022 by Lynn Laakso so
that the discussion can be revisited.

could be reused across domains and jurisdictions. He notes the same

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Commit, in a future major version of the IPS FHIR IG (HL7/fhir-ips), to
publishing **two layers of profiles** for each IPS-relevant resource:

1. A **library/base layer** — minimally constrained "IPS building block"
   profiles (e.g., `IPS-Patient-Library`, `IPS-AllergyIntolerance-Library`,
   `IPS-Condition-Library`, `IPS-MedicationStatement-Library`,
   `IPS-Immunization-Library`, etc.) capturing only the minimum data set
   agreed for international interoperability, with no document-context
   constraints.
2. A **document layer** — the existing IPS profiles, now formally derived
   from the library-layer profiles, adding the constraints required for
   inclusion in an IPS Composition/Bundle (slicing of `Composition.section`,
   document-narrative requirements, mandatory `subject = Patient`, etc.).

Update the IPS Composition and section profiles so that their `entry`
references resolve to the document-layer profiles, while other IGs (IPA,
national IGs, point-to-point APIs) can target the library-layer profiles
directly. Provide a small set of motivating examples — at minimum the IPA
overlap, a national-IG reuse case, and an EHR query/API reuse case —
satisfying the 2020-02-04 vote condition.

#### Justification

This directly addresses the ambiguity Giorgio raised on Zulip and the
re-open rationale from Lynn (2022-11-22). It is also the structurally
honest answer: implementers are already using IPS profiles outside of
documents, and forcing document-only constraints onto reusable building
blocks creates friction (and silent non-conformance) for IPA and similar
projects. A two-layer profile structure with explicit derivation makes
the relationship traceable in the IG, which is consistent with how other
HL7 frameworks layer base profiles and use-case profiles (e.g., US Core
vs. downstream IGs).

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Do **not** fork the IPS profile set. Instead, keep the existing IPS
profiles as the single normative artifact and address reuse through:

- Adding explicit guidance in the IPS IG describing which constraints are
  *document-context* vs. *intrinsic to the building block*, so consumers
  can knowingly reuse the profiles outside a document.
- Coordinating with the IPA project to either (a) reference IPS profiles
  directly where compatible, or (b) define IPA-specific profiles that
  *derive from* IPS profiles and relax document-only constraints via the
  normal profile-derivation mechanism.
- Documenting in the IPS narrative the supported "library mode" usage and
  any caveats (e.g., narrative may be optional outside document context).

This avoids changing the IPS resource/profile inventory while still giving
implementers and other IGs a clear, sanctioned path to reuse the IPS
building blocks.

#### Justification

Splitting every IPS profile into a base/library variant and a document
variant doubles the artifact count, complicates conformance tooling and
implementer messaging, and risks two slightly divergent definitions of
"the IPS Patient." Most of the document-only constraints in IPS are
already few and isolated (largely around Composition and narrative); the
delta does not obviously justify a full two-layer model. Profile
derivation in downstream IGs (IPA, national) is the FHIR-idiomatic way to
relax constraints for a different context. This option keeps IPS stable
through the current STU cycle, defers to IPA / national IGs where the
real reuse pressure exists, and still resolves the documented ambiguity
through guidance.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Not Persuasive — Considered, No Action**. The IPS
IG remains scoped to producing an interoperable patient-summary
*document*; reuse of IPS profiles outside the document context is the
responsibility of the consuming IG (e.g., IPA), which can profile or
reference IPS artifacts as needed under existing FHIR mechanisms. No
change to the IPS IG is required.

#### Justification

Defensible on scope grounds: the IPS IG's stated purpose is the
international patient summary document, and the request — first filed in
2019 as "for future consideration," resolved as *Considered for Future
Use* in 2020, and re-opened in 2022 without new evidence in the ticket —
has not yet been accompanied by the concrete set of examples the WG
asked for in the 2020-02-04 vote (12-0-1). Without that evidence base,
restructuring the profile set is premature, and the existing FHIR profile
derivation mechanism already gives downstream IGs everything they need to
reuse IPS profiles in non-document contexts.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The Zulip thread and the ticket history show genuine implementer pain
around reuse of IPS profiles outside the document context — declining
outright (C) ignores that signal and the explicit re-open by Lynn in
2022. But Disposition A's full two-layer model is a large structural
commitment for an STU IG, doubles the conformance surface, and is hard
to justify until the WG has the concrete reuse examples that the
2020-02-04 vote asked for. Disposition B threads that needle: it
formally acknowledges the document/library distinction in IG guidance,
clears the path for IPA and national IGs to derive from IPS profiles,
and keeps the IPS artifact inventory stable. If, after that guidance and
IPA coordination land, the field still demands separately published
library profiles, the WG can promote to Disposition A in a later major
version with the evidence in hand.
