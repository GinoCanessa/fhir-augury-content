## Summary

The reporter (Pepijn Wortelboer, co-authored with Erik Moll of Philips) requests
that the US Physical Activity FHIR Implementation Guide include a recommendation
on supporting **detailed/high-frequency physiological observations** — in
particular heart rate sampled at much finer granularity than once per 5
minutes (e.g., averaged values over 5–15 second windows), and similarly fine
step-count data captured every few seconds. The reporter argues that simple
goals such as "x minutes of vigorous activity per day or week" cannot be
reliably evaluated without this level of detail, and that combined HR + step
data at sub-minute resolution is necessary for the IG's stated therapeutic
use cases. They further point out an internal contradiction in the IG text:
it asserts that detailed information is not needed, while simultaneously
claiming "effective therapy" as a goal, which in their view does require
this level of detail.

The ticket has no Jira, Zulip, or GitHub cross-references in Augury. It was
originally resolved (Considered for Future Use, vote 6-0-1, 2023-07-13) but
that resolution was reverted on 2023-08-25 ("re-open post STU publication"),
and it is currently in **Triaged** status with the **Block-vote-2** label,
awaiting workgroup re-disposition.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a new (informative) section to the US Physical Activity IG narrative —
e.g., under "Observations" / "Implementation Guidance" — titled something
like "Supporting Detailed/High-Frequency Physiological Observations". This
section would:

1. Explicitly acknowledge that some use cases (exercise prescription,
   HR-recovery monitoring, vigorous-activity time-in-zone calculation)
   require sub-minute-resolution heart rate and step data.
2. Recommend that systems exchanging such data use either:
   - `Observation` resources with `Observation.valueSampledData` (for
     waveform-style HR streams), citing typical periods of 5–15 s, or
   - Multiple `Observation.component` entries / linked
     `Observation.hasMember` summaries when a Bundle of discrete fine-grain
     samples is preferred.
3. Reconcile the contradiction the reporter calls out: rewrite the existing
   IG text that says "no need to include detailed information" so it instead
   distinguishes the **mandatory summary observations** the IG profiles from
   **optional supporting fine-grain observations** that implementers MAY
   exchange when therapy requires them.
4. Reference the Vital Signs profiles (in particular the heart rate vital
   sign profile) and SHALL/SHOULD cardinalities they impose on
   `effectivePeriod` and sampling.

No new profiles, value sets, or extensions are required — this is purely
narrative guidance plus, at most, examples.

#### Justification

The ticket describes a real internal inconsistency in the published IG
text, not just a feature request. Even the original disposition acknowledged
that "Nothing in this IG prohibits the sharing of high-volume raw data
between systems that wish to share it" — making that statement *normative*
in the IG (as guidance) costs nothing and resolves the contradiction the
reporter identified. Re-opening "post STU publication" suggests the WG now
sees value in revisiting this, and adding non-normative guidance is the
lowest-risk way to do so without expanding the IG's required conformance
surface.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than adding new IG content for fine-grain HR/step data, **clarify
the existing text only** to remove the contradiction, and **defer
high-frequency physiological observation guidance to a separate companion
artifact** — either:

- a future "Continuous Physiological Monitoring" IG (e.g., owned jointly
  with Devices/MoH WG), or
- a profile in the IHE PCD / Personal Health Device space that the US
  Physical Activity IG would simply cite.

Concrete change to this IG: rewrite the offending paragraph to say
something like "This IG profiles summary-level activity observations.
Exchange of high-frequency raw physiological samples (e.g., HR at 5–15 s
resolution, sub-minute step counts) is out of scope for this IG; see the
base FHIR Vital Signs profiles and `Observation.valueSampledData` for the
underlying mechanism." Add a forward pointer to any future companion IG
once it exists.

#### Justification

Addresses the documented contradiction (the reporter's primary, concrete
complaint) without committing the WG to authoring or maintaining
high-frequency monitoring guidance inside an IG whose scope is explicitly
summary-level. Avoids the "data volume and analyst-expertise" concerns
raised in the original 2023-07-13 disposition, which still apply: detailed
guidance in this IG could imply implementers SHOULD support fine-grain
exchange even when neither side is equipped to handle it. Keeps the IG
small and focused while removing the legitimate editorial defect.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Reaffirm the original 2023-07-13 disposition ("Considered for Future Use",
6-0-1) and close as **Persuasive with Modification** only insofar as the
contradictory paragraph is reworded for clarity; **decline** to add a
recommendation on supporting detailed heart-rate / step observations.
Rationale category: insufficient demonstrated cross-organization use case
+ already addressed by existing FHIR mechanisms (Vital Signs profile +
`Observation.valueSampledData`).

#### Justification

The original WG vote (6-0-1) found no strong cross-clinician use case for
exchanging this granularity of data, expressed concrete concerns about
data volume hitting clinical systems 2–3 orders of magnitude beyond
current summary sharing, and noted that most clinicians cannot analyze raw
streams. None of those facts have changed; the re-open was procedural
("post STU publication") rather than driven by new evidence. The base
FHIR Vital Signs profile and `Observation.valueSampledData` already
provide the mechanism for any two systems that *do* want to exchange
fine-grain data, so the IG would be adding guidance on top of an already
well-defined capability without a documented exchange partner asking for
it.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The reporter's strongest, most defensible point is the **internal
contradiction** in the IG text — "no need to include detailed information"
sitting next to "effective therapy" as a goal. That is an editorial
defect the WG should fix regardless of how it feels about high-frequency
data exchange. At the same time, the original 2023-07-13 disposition's
concerns are still valid: the US Physical Activity IG is scoped to
summary-level activity reporting, the data-volume and clinician-analysis
concerns have not been refuted by new use cases, and FHIR already provides
`Observation.valueSampledData` and the Vital Signs profile for any
implementer who needs to exchange waveform-style HR data. Disposition B
splits these cleanly: rewrite the offending paragraph to make scope
explicit (resolving the reporter's concrete complaint) and forward-point
to base-FHIR mechanisms / a possible future companion IG, without
committing this IG to authoring fine-grain monitoring guidance it cannot
currently justify with documented cross-organization use cases. If a
future ticket arrives backed by named exchange partners and a concrete
clinical workflow, the WG can revisit and escalate to Disposition A.
