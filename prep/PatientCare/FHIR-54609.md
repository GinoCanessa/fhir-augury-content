## Summary

The reporter (TomPage) observes that Section 9.2.1 of the US SDOH Clinical
Care IG — which describes the *polling* mode of task/service-request
exchange — does not say anything about how the system maintaining the data
should validate that the patient has consented to share information back
to the polling "client". The ticket asks the IG to add an explicit
privacy-protection requirement: before returning Task/ServiceRequest data
in response to a poll, the server should perform a consent validation
check, and (in the suggested language) the search should be filtered to
only include patients who have provided affirmative consent to share with
the requesting organization.

The ticket has been resolved as **Persuasive with Modification**: the
work group is willing to acknowledge consent in §9.2.1, but wants to
loosen the language from a hard requirement ("the system performs a
consent validation check…") to a permissive one ("when possible, the
system may perform a consent validation check…"), and to scope the
search-filter clause specifically to the case where consent is *not*
bidirectional. The committee comments make clear that the SDOHCC IG is
explicitly **not** prescribing consent practices — the SDOHCC Consent
profile is a starting point to test the viability of exchanging consent
information, not a complete treatment — and that a broader, cross-IG
statement on consent is being considered separately by the Gravity
Project.

## Details

**Description:**

> This section is missing any description of the requirement for the
> system maintaining the data to validate patient consent to share
> information back to the 'client' requesting the update. This privacy
> protection must be incorporated into the standard.
>
> **Suggested language:**
>
> In this mode, the system needing information (the 'client')
> occasionally queries the system maintaining the relevant Tasks and/or
> ServiceRequests. **The system performs a consent validation check to
> verify the patient has consented to share their 'new' data back to the
> 'client' requesting the update.** Clients may need to perform queries
> against multiple clients if relevant Tasks and ServiceRequests will
> not be maintained on the same server. To poll, the client will perform
> a [search](http://hl7.org/fhir/R4/search.html) for Tasks that are
> filtered to those either owned or requested by the searching
> organization. The search would also filter to only include those
> Tasks that had been created or changed since the server last looked
> **for patients who have provided affirmative consent to share their
> data with searching organization**.

**Resolution (from snapshot):**

> We would like to add the following text into the Section 9.2.1, with a
> couple of tweaks to loosen the language a bit:
>
> "In this mode, the system needing information (the 'client')
> occasionally queries the system maintaining the relevant Tasks and/or
> ServiceRequests. **When possible, the system may perform a consent
> validation check to verify the patient has consented to share their
> 'new' data back to the 'client' requesting the update.** Clients may
> need to perform queries against multiple clients if relevant Tasks
> and ServiceRequests will not be maintained on the same server. To
> poll, the client will perform a

> Tasks that had been created or changed since the server last looked.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the reporter's original suggested language verbatim into Section
9.2.1 of the US SDOH Clinical Care IG (`US SDOH Clinical Care (FHIR)`).
Concretely, this would:

1. Insert a normative ("performs") sentence immediately after the
   first sentence of §9.2.1 stating: *"The system performs a consent
   validation check to verify the patient has consented to share their
   'new' data back to the 'client' requesting the update."*
2. Extend the existing search-filter sentence so that it also filters
   results to only patients who have provided *affirmative* consent to
   share with the searching organization.

Operationally this would mean that conformant servers MUST perform a
consent check on every poll response and MUST exclude patients who have
not affirmatively consented from the result set (likely backed by an
SDOHCC Consent instance, since that is the only consent representation
the IG defines today).

#### Justification

This is the strongest privacy posture and the one the reporter is
explicitly asking for. The polling exchange returns
Task/ServiceRequest data about a patient to a requesting organization
without any per-request user-mediated authorization step, so an
affirmative-consent gate is a defensible privacy default and aligns
with the "SHALL not disclose without consent" principle that other US
realm IGs lean on. It also keeps the consent semantics explicit at the
point in the IG where the disclosure actually happens, rather than
deferring to a future cross-IG statement that does not yet exist.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach (Workgroup Resolution)

#### Proposal

Adopt the modified language already captured in the ticket's resolution
field. Concretely, in §9.2.1:

1. Replace the reporter's normative sentence with the permissive
   variant: *"When possible, the system **may** perform a consent
   validation check to verify the patient has consented to share their
   'new' data back to the 'client' requesting the update."*
2. Leave the existing search-filter sentence unchanged, and append a
   new sentence scoped to the non-bidirectional case: *"In cases where
   the consent is not bidirectional, the search would filter to only
   include those Tasks for which the patient has consented to share
   back with the referral source."*
3. Coordinate with the Gravity Project's planned global consent
   statement (per the 2026-03-18 comment) so the §9.2.1 wording can
   later defer to that higher-level guidance once it lands.

#### Justification

This is the disposition the work group has effectively already endorsed
("Persuasive with Modification") and is the path of least disruption.
It acknowledges the reporter's privacy concern without making the IG
more prescriptive about consent than its scope warrants — a stance the
work group reiterated in the 2026-03-13 comment ("we are not
prescribing any consent practices or guidelines in this IG"). The
permissive "may" avoids forcing implementers to wire up a full consent
infrastructure in deployments where consent is already handled out of
band (e.g., a closed referral network with a signed BAA), while still
calling out the bidirectional-consent edge case where filtering is
genuinely necessary. It also keeps room for the Gravity Project's
planned cross-IG consent statement to take precedence later without
having to relax normative SHALL/SHOULD language in this revision.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline to add consent validation language to §9.2.1, on the basis
that consent handling is out of scope for the SDOHCC IG. Close the
ticket as "Not Persuasive" with a pointer to (a) the SDOHCC Consent
profile as a starting point for implementations that wish to exchange
consent, and (b) the Gravity Project's pending global consent statement
as the appropriate venue for normative guidance. Make no textual
change to §9.2.1.

#### Justification

The work group has stated on the ticket that the SDOHCC IG is
deliberately not prescribing consent practices, and that the SDOHCC
Consent profile is a starting point rather than a complete treatment.
Inserting consent normative-or-permissive language into the polling
section risks implying a level of conformance the IG cannot back up
with profiles or operations, and would create inconsistency with
sections 9.1, 9.3, and elsewhere that say nothing about consent.
Deferring to the planned global statement keeps the consent guidance
in one place and avoids text the work group will likely have to revise
again as soon as that statement lands.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The ticket has already been resolved as *Persuasive with Modification*
and is on the `block-vote-3` ballot list with `Ready-For-Vote`, so the
work group has signalled it wants to act on the comment but not in the
maximalist form the reporter proposed. Disposition B captures both
halves of that signal: it gives the reporter visible acknowledgement
in §9.2.1 (the consent check is named, and the bidirectional-consent
edge case is addressed in the search filter), while honouring the work
group's repeated position that SDOHCC is not the place to write
normative consent rules. Disposition A would over-commit the IG to
behaviour it cannot fully specify today (there is no operation, no
SearchParameter, and no profile constraint that would make the SHALL
testable), and Disposition C would silently overrule a comment the
group has already agreed is persuasive. The Gravity Project's planned
global consent statement (per the 2026-03-18 comment) is the right
long-term home for this guidance; Disposition B leaves §9.2.1 in a
state that can cleanly defer to that statement once published.
