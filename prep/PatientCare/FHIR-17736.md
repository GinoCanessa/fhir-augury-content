## Summary

The ticket asks Patient Care to add explicit, normative-style guidance to
the FHIR Core specification explaining how the cluster of resources used
to represent a clinical "Concern" — Condition, Observation, Linkage,
List, EpisodeOfCare, and (later raised) ClinicalImpression and CarePlan
— fit together and are intended to be used in combination, including
how that aggregate model maps to the C-CDA notion of a Health Concern.
The request originates from real C-CDA → FHIR mapping experience where
implementers (see the cross-referenced
[cda to fhir > CCDA Health Concerns to FHIR](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/cda%20to%20fhir/topic/CCDA%20Health%20Concerns%20to%20FHIR/near/153991325)
thread) found no single place in FHIR that explains the relationship
between a Condition record, the problem List that gathers concerns, the
Linkage resource that aggregates multiple representations of the same
underlying problem, and the EpisodeOfCare that ties longitudinal
encounters/observations to a concern. The same gap was cited as a
blocker for raising the Condition resource's FMM level (see
[implementers > Resource Maturity](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/implementers/topic/Resource%20Maturity/near/154025488)),
which makes this not just a documentation nicety but a maturity-model
prerequisite. The bot post in
[committers > tracker-item](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/committers/topic/tracker-item/near/153991323)
is simply the original GF#17736 announcement. Discussion on the ticket
between 2021 and 2023 produced a draft narrative by Jay Lyle (covering
Observation, ClinicalImpression, Condition, List, CarePlan, with
EpisodeOfCare suggested by Lloyd) that PC has reviewed on multiple WG
calls but never landed in the spec, and a Confluence working page on
the Concern/Condition boundary already exists at
https://confluence.hl7.org/pages/viewpage.action?pageId=46892408.

## Related Zulip Discussions

### [cda to fhir > CCDA Health Concerns to FHIR](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/cda%20to%20fhir/topic/CCDA%20Health%20Concerns%20to%20FHIR/near/153991325)

Implementer Michael Allange asked how to map the C-CDA Health Concern
section (where each entry refers to one or more related conditions,
problems, or medications) into a FHIR Condition with all of its
related entries. Lloyd McKenzie answered that, in FHIR, "Concern" is
expressed by including an entry on the practitioner's/patient's problem
**List**, with **Linkage** tying together multiple representations of
the same underlying problem, supporting information attached via
appropriate relationships, and **EpisodeOfCare** used to bind together
everything related to a specific condition over time. Michelle (Moseman)
Miller added that "Concern" is overloaded in the US: while Linkage is
the technically-correct aggregator, the
[US Core Condition profile](http://hl7.org/fhir/us/core/StructureDefinition-us-core-condition.html)
overloads `Condition` itself to represent patient-reported concerns that
may or may not appear on the problem list, and that any spec guidance
will need to thoughtfully differentiate Condition vs Linkage. Allange
confirmed that US Core implementers had been confused and were seeing
examples that put information into the Condition narrative instead of
using Linkage. The thread is the direct trigger for filing
[FHIR-17736](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-17736) — Lloyd posted
"I've added GF#17736" in the same thread. Lisa Nelson revisited the
thread in March 2020 asking whether US Core Condition.category had
since closed any of the C-CDA → FHIR mapping gaps for Problem Concern;
no resolution was recorded.

Participants: Michael Allange, Lloyd McKenzie, Michelle (Moseman)
Miller, Lisa Nelson (with @Matt Lord and @Jean Duteau pinged).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Land Jay Lyle's drafted narrative as a new dedicated Patient Care
module page in FHIR Core titled "Representing Concerns" (or similar),
sitting alongside the existing
[Clinical Summary module](http://hl7.org/fhir/R4/clinicalsummary-module.html)
under the clinical module set. The page would:

1. Define "Concern" and the problem-oriented record (intro paragraph
   plus the Weed citation flagged in the August 2023 draft).
2. Walk through the six resources that participate, in this order, with
   one short paragraph each: **Observation**, **ClinicalImpression**,
   **Condition**, **List**, **CarePlan**, and **EpisodeOfCare** (per
   Lloyd's 2021-06-26 ask) — making explicit (a) the
   procedural-not-semantic distinction between Observation and
   Condition, (b) the "tracked" heuristic vs. the "of concern"
   heuristic for promoting an Observation to a Condition, and (c) the
   fact that not everything on a problem list is a Condition (e.g., a
   CABG operation), motivating List.
3. Add an explicit "Aggregating multiple representations of the same
   problem" section that names **Linkage** as the canonical aggregator
   and contrasts it with the US Core practice of overloading
   `Condition` for patient-reported concerns.
4. Add a "C-CDA Mapping" subsection that maps the C-CDA Health
   Concern Act and Problem Concern entry templates onto the FHIR
   pattern (Condition + List + Linkage + optional EpisodeOfCare),
   resolving the question that originated this ticket.
5. Add cross-links from the Notes section of `Condition`, `List`,
   `Linkage`, `EpisodeOfCare`, `ClinicalImpression`, and `CarePlan`
   into the new page (the Notes-section-empty bug Michelle flagged for
   `Condition` in R4 should be confirmed fixed in R5/R6 build before
   relying on it).

Resolve the embedded editorial flags before publishing: confirm or
remove the Weed citation, decide on the
`[that providers can address]` qualifier, and **explicitly answer**
the open question of whether `ClinicalImpression.investigation` and
`ClinicalImpression.prognosis` should be folded back into Condition —
either by closing it in the prose ("we are keeping ClinicalImpression
because…") or by spinning it out as its own tracker.

#### Justification

This is what the ticket literally asks for ("a particular place in the
specification that talks about how they all fit together"), what the
[CCDA Health Concerns to FHIR Zulip thread](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/cda%20to%20fhir/topic/CCDA%20Health%20Concerns%20to%20FHIR/near/153991325)
asked Patient Care to produce ("we should add guidance to the
specification" — Michelle Miller, 2018-08-28), and what the
[Resource Maturity Zulip thread](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/implementers/topic/Resource%20Maturity/near/154025488)
identifies as a pre-condition for advancing Condition's FMM. A draft
already exists (Jay Lyle, refined on the 2023-08-17 PC call) and a
Confluence working page already exists at
https://confluence.hl7.org/pages/viewpage.action?pageId=46892408, so
the lift is editorial polish + page placement, not new design work. A
dedicated page (per Lloyd's 2021-06-26 suggestion) is preferable to
appending the narrative to one resource's Notes section, because the
content describes a multi-resource pattern — burying it on `Condition`
would mis-signal that Condition is the centerpiece, which is exactly
the misconception the ticket is trying to correct.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than producing one consolidated module page, ship the guidance
as **three coordinated, smaller deliverables** anchored on existing
spec real estate:

1. A focused C-CDA → FHIR Concern mapping note added to the
   `Condition` resource's Notes section (and to the equivalent section
   on `List` and `Linkage`), explicitly stating the canonical pattern:
   *Concern = entry on a problem-list `List`, with `Linkage` to tie
   together the Condition + supporting Observations/Procedures/etc.,
   and optionally an `EpisodeOfCare` for longitudinal grouping.*
2. A short "Concern vs. Condition" note appended to the
   [US Core Condition profile](http://hl7.org/fhir/us/core/StructureDefinition-us-core-condition.html)
   guidance (raised separately with CGP/US Realm) acknowledging that
   US Core overloads Condition and showing the FHIR Core canonical
   pattern alongside.
3. Defer the broader problem-oriented-record / Observation /
   ClinicalImpression / CarePlan narrative (Jay's full draft) to a
   future companion piece, opened as a new tracker, since that
   material is genuinely about clinical-summary architecture rather
   than the narrow C-CDA Concern question that triggered this ticket.

#### Justification

The ticket as filed in 2018 is narrow ("how do these pieces fit
together and how does that map to CCDA Concern"), but Jay's 2023 draft
has grown to cover the whole problem-oriented record including
ClinicalImpression and CarePlan, which is why it has been bouncing on
PC calls without landing — the scope keeps re-opening (witness the
"Has anyone implemented [ClinicalImpression]? Should we just move
'investigation' and 'prognosis' to Condition?" flag in the
2023-08-17 draft). Splitting the deliverable lets PC close the
original ticket on the part that has consensus (the
Condition/List/Linkage/EpisodeOfCare pattern that Lloyd articulated
on day one and Michelle endorsed) without being held hostage to the
unresolved ClinicalImpression question. It also addresses the US-Core
overloading concern Michelle raised, which a single FHIR Core page
cannot fix unilaterally because US Core is governed by a different IG.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-17736 as **Persuasive with Mod / Won't Fix — addressed by
existing material** on the grounds that (a) the
[Clinical Summary module](http://hl7.org/fhir/R4/clinicalsummary-module.html)
already provides cross-resource guidance for the problem-oriented view,
(b) `Linkage`, `List`, and `EpisodeOfCare` each describe their own
aggregation role in their resource pages, and (c) the Concern/Condition
boundary discussion lives on the Confluence page at
https://confluence.hl7.org/pages/viewpage.action?pageId=46892408,
which can be linked from the relevant resource Notes sections without
producing a new normative page. Action would be limited to adding
"see also" links into Condition, List, and Linkage Notes sections.

#### Justification

The ticket has been open since August 2018, has been on the PC agenda
repeatedly between 2021 and 2023, and has not produced consensus text
in over five years despite a complete draft having been circulated.
That pattern suggests the WG either lacks the bandwidth or lacks
agreement on scope, and continuing to carry the ticket consumes agenda
time without progress. The substantive guidance is recoverable from
existing pages and the Confluence note. Declining is, however, the
weakest option because it leaves the FMM-blocker for `Condition`
(documented in the
[Resource Maturity thread](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/implementers/topic/Resource%20Maturity/near/154025488))
in place and leaves implementers like Michael Allange in the same
position they were in when the ticket was filed.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the practical path to actually closing this ticket
after seven-plus years on the backlog. The original 2018 question is
narrow and answerable today: Lloyd's first reply on the
[CCDA Health Concerns to FHIR thread](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/cda%20to%20fhir/topic/CCDA%20Health%20Concerns%20to%20FHIR/near/153991325)
already states the canonical pattern (List + Linkage + EpisodeOfCare
around Condition), and Michelle's follow-up identifies the one real
nuance (US Core overloads `Condition` for patient-reported concerns).
Shipping that as targeted Notes-section guidance on `Condition`,
`List`, and `Linkage` plus a coordinated note for US Core resolves
the C-CDA mapping question that motivated FHIR-17736 and removes the
specific item Michelle named as blocking Condition's FMM bump in the
[Resource Maturity thread](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/implementers/topic/Resource%20Maturity/near/154025488).
The broader problem-oriented-record narrative in Jay's draft is
genuinely valuable but its scope creep — particularly the open
ClinicalImpression `investigation`/`prognosis` question — is exactly
what has prevented Disposition A from landing in three successive
years. Splitting that work out to a fresh tracker lets PC make
incremental, defensible progress. If the WG decides scope cohesion is
more important than closing this ticket, Disposition A is the
fallback; Disposition C should be avoided because it leaves the FMM
blocker un-addressed.
