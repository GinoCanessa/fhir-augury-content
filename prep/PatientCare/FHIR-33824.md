## Summary

The ticket asks Patient Care, in the context of the Ophthalmology Retinal
FHIR IG, to change the canonical URL of the IG's `qualifiers` ValueSet so
that it is rooted at `terminology.hl7.org` (THO) rather than under
`hl7.org/fhir`. The reporter also asks that a corresponding UTG ticket be
opened so the ValueSet is registered in THO once the canonical URL is
moved.

The request is administrative / governance in nature: it does not change
the ValueSet's content or expansion, only its identity (canonical URL)
and the registry of record. It reflects HL7's general policy of hosting
broadly-reusable terminology under THO (managed through UTG) rather than
inside an individual FHIR IG's namespace, so that the ValueSet can be
referenced consistently by other specifications without coupling to the
Ophthalmology Retinal IG's publication cadence. It originated as a
review comment ("Comment 6") imported by Ron Parker against the IG.

There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub
items associated with FHIR-33824 in the augury index, so the ticket is
considered in isolation.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the Ophthalmology Retinal FHIR IG, change the canonical URL of the
`qualifiers` ValueSet from its current `http://hl7.org/fhir/...` form to
a `http://terminology.hl7.org/ValueSet/<id>` form, and open a companion
UTG ticket against the THO repository to register the ValueSet (and any
supporting CodeSystem(s) it references that are also IG-local) under
THO. Once UTG accepts the registration, the IG's binding(s) that
currently point at the old canonical are updated to the new THO
canonical, and the IG narrative / changelog records the URL move. The
old canonical is left as a redirect / historical alias if HL7 publishing
infrastructure supports it, so existing implementer references do not
silently break.

#### Justification

This is the path the reporter explicitly asked for and aligns with HL7's
established practice of hosting cross-domain / reusable terminology
under THO via UTG rather than inside an individual FHIR IG namespace.
ValueSets used to qualify clinical observations are exactly the kind of
shared vocabulary other specs (e.g., other Patient Care IGs, CDA, V2
mappings) are most likely to want to reuse, and a THO canonical makes
that reuse stable across IG publication cycles. Doing the UTG
registration in the same change keeps the canonical URL and the
registry of record in sync, which is the configuration HL7 governance
expects.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the ValueSet defined inside the Ophthalmology Retinal IG (under
`hl7.org/fhir/...`) for the current ballot/publication cycle, but flag
it in the IG as a candidate for THO migration and open a UTG ticket
that proposes the migration as a separate, scheduled work item. The
disposition would explicitly defer the canonical-URL change until UTG
has accepted the ValueSet (and any underlying CodeSystem it depends on)
and a coordinated republication of the IG can repoint the bindings in a
single, advertised breaking change.

#### Justification

Changing a ValueSet's canonical URL is a breaking change for any
implementer or downstream artifact that has bound to the existing URL,
even if the content is identical. Sequencing the move through UTG first
(rather than flipping the canonical in the IG and chasing the UTG
registration afterwards) avoids a window in which the IG advertises a
THO canonical that THO has not yet published. It also gives Patient
Care a chance to confirm with the Ophthalmology Retinal authors that
the ValueSet is genuinely reusable across IGs (the trigger for THO
hosting) rather than IG-specific, which is the usual gating question
for UTG acceptance.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change and keep the `qualifiers` ValueSet's canonical URL
under `hl7.org/fhir/...`, on the basis that the ValueSet is scoped to
the Ophthalmology Retinal IG and is not (currently) reused by other
specifications, so the additional UTG governance overhead is not
justified. The ticket would be closed as "Persuasive with Mod / Not
Persuasive" with the rationale that IG-local terminology is allowed to
live under the IG's own canonical namespace.

#### Justification

HL7 policy does not require every ValueSet to be hosted under THO;
genuinely IG-local vocabularies are routinely published under the
owning IG's canonical. If there is no concrete cross-IG reuse demand
for the qualifiers ValueSet, moving it to THO and opening a UTG ticket
adds governance overhead (UTG review cycles, separate publication
cadence, additional cross-repo coordination) without a corresponding
implementer benefit, and creates a breaking change for anything already
bound to the existing canonical.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the most defensible balance of the reporter's intent
and HL7's terminology-governance reality. The reporter is correct in
principle — qualifier-style ValueSets are exactly the kind of artifact
HL7 prefers to host under THO via UTG — so an outright Decline (C) is
hard to justify for a Triaged ticket that has been open since 2021.
But Accept-as-Requested (A) compresses two coupled changes (canonical
URL flip in the IG, and ValueSet registration in THO/UTG) into a
single disposition, and the canonical URL change is a breaking change
for any implementer already bound to the existing URL. Sequencing the
UTG registration first and then repointing the IG's bindings in a
coordinated republication, as in B, gets the ticket to the same
end-state the reporter asked for while avoiding a window in which the
IG advertises a THO canonical that THO does not yet publish, and gives
Patient Care a clean opportunity to confirm cross-IG reuse before
committing to THO hosting.
