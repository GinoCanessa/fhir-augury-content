## Summary

The reporter (LisaNicolaou) requests a language update to **Section 12.1
"Goal"** of the *Referral for Further Assessment* (RFFA) use case in the
**US SDOH Clinical Care** IG. The proposed edits clarify that an initial
screening may surface social needs that the clinical care team lacks the
"time, resources, or specialized skills" to assess in depth, and that an
RFFA can establish a community-based organization (CBO) or other partner
organization as a member of the treatment team — potentially enabling
payment for additional service pathways such as care coordination and
home-based services. The reporter also flags a concern that, if the
clinical team lacks knowledge of available community interventions, it
is unrealistic for that team to drive goal-setting and intervention
identification on its own.

In a follow-up comment, the reporter (acting on Gravity team feedback)
narrows the ask: rather than codifying specific exchange partners and
direction in Section 12.1, the section should be **revised to be
agnostic about exchange partners and the direction of the RFFA
exchange**. The ticket's current resolution ("Persuasive with
Modification") accepts the spirit of the request but rewrites the
proposed text to soften prescriptive language ("This action **is one
way to** establish…", "**in some cases, may** enable payment…", "**may
lack** the capacity…") and reorders the bulleted goals so that
"Establish the treatment relationship of the community care team"
becomes one of several intents rather than the lead intent.

No cross-referenced Jira tickets, GitHub items, or Zulip threads were
surfaced for this ticket.

## Details

**Description:**

> 1. **Section 12.1 Goal:**
>    1. Proposed language change:
>       1. In many clinical situations, an initial screening may indicate the presence of one or more health-related social needs, but the clinical care team may not have **the time, resources, or specialized skills to perform a deeper, more comprehensive assessment.** The primary goal of this use case is to enable a healthcare provider or other entity (e.g. behavioral health or other non-healthcare entity) to refer an individual to a community-based organization (CBO) or another partner organization that is equipped to conduct a more thorough assessment. **This action establishes the community care team as a member of the treatment team and can enable payment for additional service pathways (for example, care coordination, home-based services, or other specific contracted services).**
>       2. This exchange is intended to:
>          1. **Establish the treatment relationship of the community care team.**
>          2. Enable a deeper understanding of an individual's HRSN to create a more holistic picture of their needs and goals.
>          3. Connect individuals with organizations and people skilled in performing comprehensive social needs assessments **which may initiate social care coordination and intervention pathways in community.**
>          4. Ensure the results of the assessment are shared back with the referring provider to inform the individual's overall care plan.
>       3. Complement, not replace, existing workflows for referring a patient for specific services. A referral for further assessment can be initiated independently of, or in conjunction with, a referral for a specific service (e.g., provision of a meal).
>       4. For example, a patient might screen positive for food insecurity. During the follow-up conversation, the healthcare provider suspects other needs may be present but lacks the capacity **or specialized skills** to investigate further. The provider can then send a "Referral for Further Assessment" to a trusted CBO to gain a more complete understanding of the individual's situation and identify appropriate services.
>
> I would note that if this is true then the ability for the healthcare based care team to dive into goal setting and intervention identification when they know nothing of the interventions available in community is highly unlikely.

**Comments:**

  partners in a Referral for Further Assessment exchange, and the

  section how that should be done. Our main takeaway here is that we
  should revise this entire Goal section to reflect that."

**Resolution Description (already recorded as "Persuasive with Modification"):**

> We are going to revise this section to be more agnostic about the exchange partners in the RFFA workflow.
>
> - In many clinical situations, an initial screening may indicate the presence of one or more health-related social needs, but the clinical care team may not have the time, resources, or specialized skills to perform a deeper, more comprehensive assessment. The primary goal of this use case is to enable a healthcare provider or other entity (e.g. behavioral health or other non-healthcare entity) to refer an individual to a community-based organization (CBO) or another partner organization that is equipped to conduct a more thorough assessment. **This action is one way to establish the community care team as a member of the treatment team and, in some cases, may enable payment for additional service pathways (for example, care coordination, home-based services, or other specific contracted services).**
> - This exchange is intended to:
>   1. Enable a deeper understanding of an individual's HRSN to create a more holistic picture of their needs and goals.
>   2. Connect individuals with organizations and people skilled in performing comprehensive social needs assessments **which may initiate social care coordination and intervention pathways in community.**
>   3. Ensure the results of the assessment are shared back with the referring provider to inform the individual's overall care plan.
>   4. Complement, not replace, existing workflows for referring a patient for specific services. A referral for further assessment can be initiated independently of, or in conjunction with, a referral for a specific service (e.g., provision of a meal).
>   5. **Establish the treatment relationship of the community care team.**
> - For example, a patient might screen positive for food insecurity. During the follow-up conversation, the healthcare provider suspects other needs may be present but **may lack** the capacity **or specialized skills** to investigate further. The provider can then send a "Referral for Further Assessment" to a trusted CBO to gain a more complete understanding of the individual's situation and identify appropriate services.

## Proposed Dispositions

This ticket is narrative-only — it touches the "Goal" prose of Section
12.1 of the *US SDOH Clinical Care* IG (RFFA use case). There are no
StructureDefinitions, value sets, profiles, examples, or operations
implicated. No GitHub cross-references means no per-repo briefing
constrains the proposals; any of the dispositions below would be
implemented by an authoring change to the IG's narrative page for
Section 12.1 (RFFA Goal) in the HL7 *fhir-us-sdoh-clinicalcare* IG
source.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the reporter's originally-proposed Section 12.1 wording verbatim
in the RFFA Goal narrative. Specifically:

- Insert "the time, resources, or specialized skills to perform a
  deeper, more comprehensive assessment" into the opening paragraph.
- Add a new sentence: "This action establishes the community care team
  as a member of the treatment team and can enable payment for
  additional service pathways (for example, care coordination,
  home-based services, or other specific contracted services)."
- Add "Establish the treatment relationship of the community care team"
  as the **first** bullet under "This exchange is intended to:".
- Add "which may initiate social care coordination and intervention
  pathways in community" to the bullet about connecting individuals
  with skilled assessors.
- Add "or specialized skills" to the food-insecurity example.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter (and a Patient Care / Gravity
stakeholder) asked for, and the additions are clarifying — they
articulate *why* an RFFA matters (treatment-team membership, payment
pathways, intervention initiation) rather than introducing new
conformance requirements. Because the change is narrative-only in an
STU IG, the implementer impact is essentially zero. It also directly
addresses the reporter's noted concern that an RFFA needs to be framed
as more than a one-off question, since the clinical team may not be
positioned to set goals or identify interventions in the community.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach (Recommended)

#### Proposal

Apply the **revised wording captured in the ticket's Resolution
Description** ("Persuasive with Modification") rather than the original
proposal. Concretely, in the RFFA Section 12.1 Goal page of the *US
SDOH Clinical Care* IG, replace the current Goal narrative with the
agnostic version:

- Soften the treatment-team / payment sentence to:
  *"This action **is one way** to establish the community care team as
  a member of the treatment team and, **in some cases, may** enable
  payment for additional service pathways…"*
- Reorder the "This exchange is intended to:" list so that "Establish
  the treatment relationship of the community care team" appears as
  one of several goals (last in the list per the resolution), not the
  lead item.
- Pull "Complement, not replace, existing workflows…" into the same
  goal list rather than leaving it as a separate top-level bullet.
- Soften the example to "may lack the capacity or specialized skills to
  investigate further".
- Keep the wording deliberately **agnostic about the exchange
  partners** (sender vs. receiver, which type of organization) and the
  **direction** of the RFFA exchange, per the reporter's 2026-02-13
  comment.

#### Justification

This is the disposition the reporter herself converged on after
reviewing the original proposal with the Gravity team, and it is what
the resolution text already records. The softened "is one way / may
enable" framing avoids implying that an RFFA *must* establish a formal
treatment relationship or *must* unlock payment — neither is universally
true across jurisdictions, payer models, or CBO arrangements, and
overstating it could be read as a normative claim in an IG. Keeping the
wording agnostic about exchange partners and direction also future-
proofs the section against the variety of real-world workflows
(provider→CBO, CBO→provider, behavioral-health hubs, payer-coordinated
referrals) without forcing the IG to prefer one. Because Section 12.1 is
narrative, this can land as a single editorial PR with no schema or
example impact.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Leave Section 12.1 as currently written and close the ticket as "Not
Persuasive". Optionally, capture the additional points the reporter
raised (treatment-team membership, payment pathways, partner-agnostic
language) in implementation guidance or an FAQ rather than the Goal
section.

#### Justification

A defensible-but-weak position would be that Section 12.1 is already a
high-level "Goal" statement and that adding language about treatment-
team membership and payment risks reading as normative claims about
billing and care-team composition that are out of scope for an
exchange-focused IG. However, this is unlikely to be a strong
disposition here because (a) the reporter is the workgroup-aligned
Gravity participant, (b) the changes are narrative-only and low risk,
and (c) the workgroup has already moved the ticket to "Persuasive with
Modification" with a concrete rewrite, indicating consensus that *some*
update is warranted.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B (Alternative Approach)

Disposition B is the cleanest fit because it codifies the resolution
the workgroup has effectively already reached — both the reporter's
2026-02-13 comment and the ticket's recorded Resolution Description
land on partner-agnostic, softened language rather than the original
prescriptive wording. Disposition A is acceptable but slightly
overcommits the IG to specific claims about treatment-team membership
and payment that may not hold across all RFFA implementations.
Disposition C ignores the workgroup's existing alignment and the
reporter's own follow-up. Because the change is narrative-only in an
STU IG (no StructureDefinitions, value sets, or examples affected),
the cost of adopting B is low and it can ship as a single editorial
update to Section 12.1 of *US SDOH Clinical Care*.
