## Summary

The ticket asks Patient Care to add a `status` element (and an accompanying
`statusReason`) to `CareTeam.participant` so that the participation state of
an individual member on a care team can be represented explicitly, in
addition to the existing `CareTeam.status` which applies to the team as a
whole. The reporter (Emma Jones) proposes a relatively rich value set
covering workflow-like states (proposed, requested, received, accepted,
rejected, ready, cancelled, active, on hold, not started / aborted,
completed, entered-in-error). Over the ticket's multi-year life the Patient
Care WG has narrowed the proposal toward a smaller set appropriate to
team membership (e.g., proposed | pending | active | suspended | inactive |
permanently-inactive | entered-in-error), bound extensibly, with `isModifier
= true` because of `entered-in-error`. The discussion has repeatedly
surfaced overlap between participant-level status and the existing
`CareTeam.participant.coveragePeriod` / `period`, and has evolved into a
broader restructuring idea — promoting `participant` to a `CareTeam.role`
backbone with an inner `member` backbone, at which point status could live
at either the role or the member level. Cross-references are all to FHIR
artifacts (no cross-referenced Jira or Zulip items surfaced), and the
related PA ticket [FHIR-34379](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-34379) (Add
employment category in PractitionerRole, Resolved – Not Persuasive) was
called out during the Jan 2024 WGM as thematically related but has already
been declined by PA.

## Details

**Description:**

Participants on a care team can have varying participation status and status reason on a care team. Example includes:

1. Proposed - participation is not yet ready to be acted upon.
2. Requested - participation is ready to be acted upon and action is sought.
3. Received - potential performer has claimed ownership of the participation and is evaluating whether to perform it.
4. Accepted - potential performer has agreed to execute the participation but has not yet started work.
5. Rejected - potential performer who claimed ownership of the participation has decided not to execute it prior to performing any action.
6. Ready - participation is ready to be performed, but no action has yet been taken. Used in place of requested/received/accepted/rejected when request assignment and acceptance is a given.
7. Cancelled - participation was not completed.
8. Active - participation has been started but is not yet complete.
9. On Hold - participation has been started but work has been paused.
10. Not Started or Aborted - participation was attempted but could not be completed due to some error. Clinical reasons for fails could be no order, no coverage, attempts to contact failed.
11. Completed - participation has been completed.
12. entered-in-error - participation should never have existed and is retained only because of the possibility it may have been used.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add `CareTeam.participant.status` to the FHIR Core CareTeam resource as
follows (aligned with Emma's 2020-09-01 spreadsheet proposal, refined by
subsequent PC calls):

- Path: `CareTeam.participant.status`
- Cardinality: `0..1`
- Type: `CodeableConcept`
- Binding: `CareTeamParticipantStatus`, strength `extensible`
- Initial codes (new value set): `proposed | pending | active | suspended |
  inactive | permanently-inactive | entered-in-error`
- `isModifier = true`, `isModifierReason = "contains entered-in-error which
  means the participant record should not be treated as valid"`
- `isSummary = true`
- Add a new `CareTeam.participant.statusReason` (`CodeableConcept`, `0..1`,
  preferred/example binding to a PC-curated reason value set) to carry the
  clinical / administrative rationale behind the status change.
- Add an invariant on `CareTeam.participant` preventing `status =
  permanently-inactive` or `proposed` from being combined with a
  `coveragePeriod` whose `end` is in the future (per the 2022-03-10 and
  2022-09-01 discussions about status/coveragePeriod conflicts).
- Update the element definition of `CareTeam.participant.coveragePeriod`
  to explicitly disambiguate "participant is active" vs. "participant is
  covering for another participant", per Stephen's 2022-03-24 TO-DO.

#### Justification

This matches the original request and the refined May 2024 WGM consensus
that a participant-level status is required for care coordination,
quality reporting, and home-based care. Using `CodeableConcept` with an
extensible binding is consistent with other CareTeam-style status
elements (e.g., `EpisodeOfCare.status`-adjacent patterns where richer
clinical codes are expected) and lets implementers extend where national
realms need finer granularity. Flagging the element `isModifier` follows
FHIR's standard modeling rule for any status element that includes
`entered-in-error`. Adding `statusReason` preserves the "reason" half of
the original "status and status reason" request from Emma's opening
description and is consistent with how other Patient-Care resources
(e.g., Condition, AllergyIntolerance) already pair a status with a
reason. The invariant directly addresses the overlap with
`coveragePeriod` that has been the main technical blocker in the
ticket's history.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Do not add a participant-level `status` to the core CareTeam resource.
Instead:

1. Restructure `CareTeam.participant` into `CareTeam.role` (0..*) with an
   inner `CareTeam.role.member` (0..*) backbone, as sketched in the Sept
   2022 WGM Tues Q1 notes. Place a **role-level** status on
   `CareTeam.role.status` (covering the role's lifecycle —
   `proposed | active | suspended | inactive | permanently-inactive |
   entered-in-error`, bound extensibly, `isModifier = true`) and leave
   individual membership state to be inferred from
   `CareTeam.role.member.coverage[x]` (Period | Timing) plus a small
   additional `CareTeam.role.member.onBehalfOf`/`substitute` signal. Add
   `CareTeam.role.reason` to carry the status reason.
2. Until the restructure lands, publish a standard extension
   `http://hl7.org/fhir/StructureDefinition/careteam-participant-status`
   (value set `CareTeamParticipantStatus` with the same codes as
   Disposition A) so implementers blocked today can use an HL7-stewarded
   extension instead of vendor-private ones, and promote it into core
   only after the `role` restructure is agreed in R6.
3. Explicitly note in CareTeam's narrative that
   "accept / reject / ready / completed" workflow states belong on a
   `Task` that references the CareTeam, not on `CareTeam.participant`
   itself (per Lloyd's 2024-01-31 and 2019-12-13 comments and the
   2020-01-09 call conclusion that CareTeam is not in the workflow
   pattern).

#### Justification

Lloyd's pushback that "things with statuses often want to be
independent resources" and that accept/reject overlap Task is a
substantive modeling concern that Disposition A does not address. Most
currently-observed real-world `CareTeam` usage is a snapshot of current
state (Grahame's 2023-09 analysis of existing extensions), which argues
for keeping the per-member modeling lean and handling richer workflow
(pending / received / accepted / rejected) via Task. The role/member
restructure also resolves Emma's 2022-09-01 point that role and member
have distinct life cycles (e.g., a Rehab-PT role fulfilled by two
different therapists with different coverage periods). Shipping the
extension first is substantially less disruptive than a backbone
rename/restructure in-place and is consistent with HL7's general
preference to prove a concept via a stewarded extension before promoting
into core — especially given the resource is FMM 2 / 3-era and the
restructure would be a breaking change that should be aligned with R6
rather than a minor-version release.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close this ticket as **Not Persuasive**. Advise that:

- `CareTeam.status` already describes the team-as-a-whole lifecycle.
- Per-participant "is this person on the team right now" is already
  expressible via `CareTeam.participant.period` /
  `CareTeam.participant.coveragePeriod` and via adding/removing the
  participant entry (which is what most in-market profiles do, per
  Grahame's extension survey).
- Workflow-style states (`proposed | requested | received | accepted |
  rejected | ready | cancelled | on-hold | completed`) are already
  expressible with a `Task` whose `focus` references the CareTeam, and
  `CarePlan` already covers the "care actions" space. Duplicating these
  states on `CareTeam.participant.status` creates two sources of truth
  for the same workflow.
- If a realm needs a non-workflow participant status (e.g.,
  `suspended` vs. `inactive` where period alone is insufficient), the
  existing extension mechanism is sufficient and several IGs already
  ship such extensions.

#### Justification

`CareTeam` has been repeatedly described in this ticket's own history as
not part of the workflow pattern, and the strongest modeling objections
from the ticket comments (Lloyd 2019-12 and 2024-01, Rob 2019-12) have
not been answered. A Not Persuasive resolution mirrors what PA did for
the adjacent [FHIR-34379](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-34379)
employment-category request and avoids adding a modifier element whose
semantics overlap with existing elements (`period`,
`coveragePeriod`, membership add/remove) and resources (Task, CarePlan).
Declining also lets PC concentrate its R6 effort on the `role` /
`member` restructure, which is the underlying model issue, without
first entrenching a participant-level `status` that the restructure
would then have to re-home.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The ticket has been open for roughly five years and has cycled through
three distinct framings (simple `active/inactive`, a workflow-flavored
code list, and a structural restructure into `role`/`member`). The May
2024 WGM LHS discussion restored momentum behind "yes, we need
per-participant status", which makes Disposition C unlikely to stick —
the use cases (seasonal caregivers, PCP churn on insurance change,
rehab planned-but-not-started, suspended-vs-inactive distinction) are
real and not cleanly expressible with `period` alone. At the same time,
Disposition A bakes a modifier status directly into
`CareTeam.participant` while unresolved modeling objections (overlap
with Task, role vs. member separation, snapshot vs. history semantics)
are still on the table; if PC accepts A now and then adopts the Sept
2022 role/member restructure for R6, the new status element will have
to be re-homed almost immediately, which is exactly the churn the
`isModifier` flag makes most painful for implementers. Disposition B
threads the needle: it gives implementers who need a status today an
HL7-stewarded extension (and a canonical value set) rather than forcing
them to invent their own, it keeps workflow semantics in Task where
Lloyd's and Grahame's comments push them, and it aligns the eventual
core change with the `role`/`member` restructure the WG has been
circling since 2022. Patient Care should pair this ticket with the
restructure discussion in R6 planning and confirm with Patient
Administration — given the FHIR-34379 precedent — that the extension's
scope stays on CareTeam membership and does not encroach on
PractitionerRole employment semantics.
