## Summary

The reporter identifies an unclear statement in the International Birth And
Child Model (FHIR) implementation guide. The first bullet under "Step 1"
begins with the phrase "In this chapter…", but no chapter in the guide is
named "Step 1" — bullets in a step list and chapters are different
structural elements, so the cross-reference is dangling and confuses
readers. The ticket asks the authors to clarify the wording so that the
bullet either points to an actually-named chapter or rephrases the
self-reference appropriately. No related Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or
GitHub items were surfaced via cross-reference or search, so the request
must be evaluated on its own.

## Proposed Dispositions

Because the ticket is a narrative-only technical correction against the
International Birth And Child Model IG and no GitHub repository is
cross-referenced in Augury, the dispositions below describe edits to the
IG's narrative source rather than naming a specific repo path. If Patient
Care confirms the IG's source repository during triage, the chosen
disposition can be re-targeted to the concrete authoring path at that
time.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Edit the offending bullet on the "Step 1" page of the International Birth
And Child Model (FHIR) IG so that the phrase "In this chapter…" is
replaced with a phrasing that matches the actual structural element. The
two mechanically simplest fixes are:

1. Replace "In this chapter…" with "In this step…" (matching the "Step 1"
   heading the bullet sits under), or
2. Replace "In this chapter…" with an explicit link/title of the chapter
   that the bullet is genuinely referring to (e.g., "In *<Chapter Name>*…"
   with a hyperlink to that chapter's anchor).

The change is narrative-only: no resource, profile, value set, or
constraint is affected.

#### Justification

The reporter is correct that "Step 1" is a step heading, not a chapter,
so the current wording is internally inconsistent and misleads readers
trying to follow the cross-reference. A wording fix is the lowest-risk
way to satisfy the request, has zero implementer impact, and aligns the
narrative with how the IG is actually structured. The ticket type
(Technical Correction) and Highest priority both indicate the workgroup
already views this as a documentation defect rather than a design
question.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than only patching the single bullet, perform a small editorial
pass on the "Step 1" page (and any sibling Step pages with the same
template) to:

- Standardize the self-reference vocabulary across all Step pages —
  always say "step", never "chapter", when the referent is a step.
- Where a bullet really does refer to another section of the IG, replace
  the bare "In this chapter…" phrasing with a hyperlink to that section
  using the IG's standard cross-reference style.
- Add a short note in the IG's authoring/contributor guidance (if one
  exists) that "chapter" is reserved for top-level table-of-contents
  entries, to prevent recurrence.

#### Justification

If "In this chapter…" appears once on the Step 1 page, it almost
certainly appears in the same template-driven location on the other Step
pages, since FHIR IG step narratives are typically copy-pasted from a
common skeleton. Fixing all instances together costs only marginally more
than fixing one and avoids a tail of follow-up tickets reporting the
same defect on Step 2, Step 3, etc. The trade-off versus Disposition A
is a slightly larger diff and a slightly longer review, in exchange for
permanent removal of the inconsistency.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as "Not Persuasive" / "Not Related" on the grounds that
"chapter" is being used informally to mean "section of narrative" and is
not a defined term in the IG, so no normative or structural correction
is required.

#### Justification

This is the defensible decline path only if the workgroup decides the
phrasing is acceptable colloquial English and that readers can infer the
intent from context. It avoids spending editorial cycles on a wording
nit and keeps the IG text stable. However, the cost of the fix is
trivial and the reporter explicitly flagged the wording as unclear, so
the bar for declining is high.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The reported defect is real and the fix is cheap, so Disposition C is
hard to justify against a Highest-priority Technical Correction filed by
an external reviewer. Between A and B, the IG's Step pages are almost
certainly templated, which means the same "In this chapter…" wording
likely recurs on every Step page; fixing only the single bullet (A)
invites a stream of duplicate tickets against the sibling pages. A small
editorial pass that normalizes "step" vs. "chapter" across all Step
narratives, plus a one-line contributor-guidance note, resolves the
reporter's concern and removes the underlying recurrence risk in a single
ballot cycle.
