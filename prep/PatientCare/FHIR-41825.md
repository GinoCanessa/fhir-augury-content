## Summary

The reporter flags an ambiguous sentence in the International Birth And Child
Model (FHIR) IG. The ticket points at the second sentence of the second bullet,
which reads: *"Note that the recipient could be the initiator who has placed
a service request."* The reporter asks whether the phrase "the initiator" is a
back-reference to an initiator named in the first sentence of the bullet, or
whether the wording is in error and should instead read "an initiator" (the
generic, indefinite form). The request is therefore an editorial / technical
correction to remove ambiguity in the narrative text — no schema, resource, or
constraint change is implied.

No related Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were surfaced by
cross-reference or search, so the request stands on its own as a wording
clarification.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat the ticket as a technical correction to the International Birth And Child
Model (FHIR) IG. Reword the second sentence of the bullet so that "the
initiator" is unambiguously a back-reference to an initiator role already
introduced in the first sentence of the same bullet. Concretely, edit the note
to read something like: *"Note that this recipient could also be the initiator
of the service request — i.e., the same actor that placed the original
ServiceRequest may be receiving the result."* This preserves the intent
(highlighting the round-trip case where the requesting party is also the
receiving party) while removing the article-vs-antecedent ambiguity that the
reporter identified.

#### Justification

The ticket type is "Technical Correction" and the change is purely editorial,
with no impact on the model, profiles, value sets, or examples. Clarifying the
antecedent is consistent with HL7 IG narrative guidance that each note should
be self-explanatory in context. Because the IG is targeting implementers who
may not be native English speakers (the reporter's question itself reflects
that), removing definite/indefinite article ambiguity has a real downstream
benefit and effectively zero cost.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than tweaking only the second sentence in isolation, restructure the
two-sentence bullet so the relationship between *recipient* and *initiator* is
spelled out by naming the actors explicitly the first time they are mentioned,
and then referring back to them by the same name. For example, introduce
"requesting practitioner" / "receiving practitioner" (or whichever role names
the IG already uses) in sentence one, and in sentence two say: *"Note that the
receiving practitioner may also be the requesting practitioner — i.e., the
party that initiated the ServiceRequest can also be the party that receives
the response."* If similar ambiguous "the X" / "an X" patterns appear elsewhere
in the same page, sweep them in the same edit.

#### Justification

The reporter's question is symptomatic of a broader pattern (definite article
used without a clear antecedent within a bullet). Fixing only the one sentence
leaves the same trap for the next reader on a different page. A small
narrative-consistency pass on the page costs roughly the same effort as a
single-line fix once the page is open for editing, and it produces a more
robust result. Trade-off: it widens the scope of the change slightly, which is
a minor risk for an IG already published in the targeted version.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as "Not Persuasive" / decline on the basis that the existing
wording is intelligible in context and that the reporter's reading ("the
initiator" = the initiator referenced in the first sentence of the bullet) is
the intended one. Optionally add a clarifying comment on the ticket confirming
the intended antecedent before closing, so the public Jira record itself
removes the ambiguity for future readers.

#### Justification

The change is cosmetic and the IG is already published. Reopening the IG
narrative for a single-word edit may not be justified if no implementer has
reported being misled in practice (only one reporter raised it, with no Zulip
or community follow-up in two-plus years). Declining is defensible only if the
workgroup is confident the back-reference reading is in fact the one intended
by the original authors.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

This is a "Highest" priority Technical Correction filed against an IG, with a
trivial editorial fix and no implementation, conformance, or backward-
compatibility implications. The reporter's question is itself evidence that the
current wording is ambiguous to at least one careful reader, so the assumption
underpinning Disposition C ("everyone reads it the intended way") is already
falsified by the existence of the ticket. Disposition A delivers the
clarification with minimal scope and minimal risk; if the work group editing
the page notices the same pattern elsewhere on the same page, they can
opportunistically adopt Disposition B for those occurrences without
broadening the formal scope of this ticket.
