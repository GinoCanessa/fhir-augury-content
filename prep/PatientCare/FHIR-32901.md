## Summary

The submitter (an implementer) finds the current FamilyMemberHistory (FMH)
guidance — which directs implementers to use the `List` resource (with
`List.emptyReason = "nilknown"`) to express patient-level "no known family
history" assertions — cumbersome. Their system has no real `List` endpoint,
so they would prefer to express patient-level "no significant FH", "unknown
FH", and "unable to obtain FH" states using a single `FamilyMemberHistory`
resource per patient. Their proposed pattern uses `FMH.patient`,
`FMH.status` (`completed` or `health-unknown`), `FMH.dataAbsentReason`
(`nil`, `subject-unknown`, `unable-to-obtain`), and a generic
`FMH.relationship` such as `self` or `FAMMEMB`, with no other content.
The ticket asks Patient Care to update the spec guidance to allow this
single-resource pattern as an alternative to the List-based mechanism.

There are no explicitly-linked or cross-referenced Jira tickets. The
ticket does, however, mention three FHIR artifacts —
`FamilyMemberHistory.dataAbsentReason`, `FamilyMemberHistory.condition.code`,
and `List.emptyReason` — all of which are central to the disposition.

## Details

**Description:**

> For our use of FamilyMemberHistory, we find the guidance around leveraging
> the List resource to be cumbersome for our app developers. We do not have
> a true List endpoint in our system and have come up with a solution to
> leverage only FamilyMemberHistory for all of our workflows, even when
> there is no known family history for a patient or one of their relatives:
>
> Since relationship is 1..1, we are considering using a generic
> relationship like 'self' or 'family-member' (or something else completely
> since we don't love these) to support a patient level status:
>
> - **No significant Fam Hx for a given patient:**
>   - `FMH.status = completed`
>   - `FMH.dataAbsentReason = nil`
>   - `FMH.patient = populated`
>   - `FMH.relationship = self or fammeb or something else`
>   - No other details
> - **Unknown Fam Hx for a given patient:**
>   - `FMH.status = health-unknown`
>   - `FMH.dataAbsentReason = subject-unknown`
>   - `FMH.patient = populated`
>   - `FMH.relationship = self or fammeb or something else`
>   - No other details
> - **Unable to Obtain Fam Hx for a given patient:**
>   - `FMH.status = health-unknown`
>   - `FMH.dataAbsentReason = unable-to-obtain`
>   - `FMH.patient = populated`
>   - `FMH.relationship = self or fammeb or something else`
>   - No other details
>
> It would allow developers to just call FMH rather than have to know when
> to call List vs. FMH depending on the WRITE workflow they are dealing with.
>
> Proposal to update the guidance to allow for flexibility to use only
> FamilyMemberHistory for these workflows and not need to rely on the List
> resource.

**Comments:**

### michelle.m.miller — 2023-01-16 (Jan 2023 WGM, Monday Q2)

Patient Care first walked through the existing §9.4.3.4 "No Known Patient
History, No Known Problems, and Negated" section and proposed a refactor
into four named cases:

- **Not Reviewed, Not Asked** — "no FamilyMemberHistory resource will be
  necessary".
- **Reviewed, None Identified for Family** — `FMH.status = completed` and
  `FMH.condition` empty.
- **Reviewed, No Information Available for a Family Member** —
  `FMH.dataAbsentReason` indicates why that individual relative's history
  is unavailable (e.g., subject unknown).
- **Reviewed, No Known Problems or Negated Condition for a Family Member** —
  `FMH.condition.code` captures "No Known Problems" or negated conditions
  (e.g., "No history of malignant tumor of breast") for a single relative.

The List/`List.emptyReason = "nilknown"` mechanism was kept as the way to
assert there are no conditions or significant events for any family
members. **TO DO:** update the definition of `FMH.dataAbsentReason`.

### michelle.m.miller — 2023-01-16 (Jan 2023 WGM, Monday Q3)

The group iterated on the §9.4.3.4 rewrite and made these working
decisions:

- **Re-define `FMH.dataAbsentReason`** to mean "why the family member's
  history does not include any conditions or procedures."
- **Value-set changes**, replacing the current FMH-specific codes with
  codes from `http://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/data-absent-reason`:
  - `subject-unknown` → `asked-unknown`
  - `withheld` → `asked-declined`
  - `unable-to-obtain` → `temp-unknown`
  - `deferred` → `temp-unknown`
  - Add `unknown`, `not-applicable`, `not-asked`, `as-text`.
- Replace the "Processing information about the Family Member History"
  section: state that FMH is most often used independently of List, but
  List remains a way to bundle FMH/Condition/Observation items for
  family-history relevant assertions about the patient.

**TO DO:** confirm whether `not-applicable` belongs in the value set and
whether `dataAbsentReason` needs to move to (or also exist on) the
condition/procedure backbone elements.

### michelle.m.miller — 2023-02-02 (Patient Care FHIR Conf Call)

The committee converged on the structural question:

- **Decision — Preferred Option 1:** keep a single `dataAbsentReason` that
  applies to both `condition` and `procedure` (rationale: the absent
  reason is rarely different between the two).
- Option 2 (separate `conditionDataAbsentReason` + `procedureDataAbsentReason`)
  and Option 3 (both plus a root-level `dataAbsentReason`) were considered
  and rejected. The earlier idea of moving `dataAbsentReason` into the
  backbone elements (Rob H, Stephen) was struck through.
- Re-confirmed the `FMH.dataAbsentReason` re-definition and the value-set
  swap to the standard `data-absent-reason` code system.
- **TO DO:** confirm `not-applicable` in the value set; add a comment on
  `FamilyMemberHistory.relationship` to mitigate the risk of misusing the
  generic `FAMMEMB` code (the binding includes it to convey ambiguity
  about a *single* relative, not to represent multiple relatives).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update `FamilyMemberHistory` §9.4.3.4 ("No Known Patient History, No
Known Problems, and Negated Items") in FHIR Core to **explicitly bless a
single-resource, patient-level pattern** as an equal alternative to the
existing `List` / `List.emptyReason = "nilknown"` mechanism. Concretely:

- Rewrite the "Not Reviewed / No Known Family History for the patient"
  case to allow a single `FamilyMemberHistory` instance with
  `FMH.patient` populated, `FMH.relationship` bound to a generic code
  (e.g., `FAMMEMB` from `v3-RoleCode`), `FMH.status` of `completed` or
  `health-unknown`, `FMH.dataAbsentReason` from the standard
  `http://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/data-absent-reason` value set,
  and no `condition` / `procedure` content.
- Apply the in-flight Patient Care decisions from Jan/Feb 2023:
  re-define `FMH.dataAbsentReason` as "why the family member's history
  does not include any conditions or procedures" (single root-level
  element — Preferred Option 1); migrate the value-set bindings as listed
  in the comments (`subject-unknown` → `asked-unknown`, `withheld` →
  `asked-declined`, `unable-to-obtain`/`deferred` → `temp-unknown`; add
  `unknown`, `not-applicable` (pending confirmation), `not-asked`,
  `as-text`).
- Add a usage note on `FMH.relationship` warning that the `FAMMEMB`
  generic code expresses ambiguity about a *specific* relative, not a
  patient-level summary; permit (but do not require) a patient-level
  summary expressed via this single-resource pattern.
- Retain the `List` / `List.emptyReason = "nilknown"` mechanism as a
  parallel option for systems that already use it.

#### Justification

This matches the implementer's stated need (no `List` endpoint in their
stack) and is largely consistent with the direction Patient Care has
already taken in two WGM quarters and the Feb 2023 conference call.
Carrying through "Preferred Option 1" (single `dataAbsentReason`) and
the value-set realignment also closes long-standing TO DOs in the same
section, avoiding a partial fix. Allowing both patterns side-by-side is
backward-compatible: existing implementations using `List` continue to
validate, while implementers with no `List` deployment gain a sanctioned
single-resource pattern. The added relationship caveat addresses the
WGM-noted risk of misusing `FAMMEMB`.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Do **not** introduce a new patient-level pattern using a sentinel
`FMH.relationship`. Instead, scope the change to two narrower,
non-overlapping edits:

1. **Inside FamilyMemberHistory:** apply the Jan/Feb 2023 Patient Care
   decisions exactly as discussed — re-define `FMH.dataAbsentReason`,
   migrate to `data-absent-reason` value-set codes, keep a single
   root-level `dataAbsentReason` (Preferred Option 1), and add the
   `FMH.relationship` usage caveat. This addresses the
   "individual-relative-unknown" cases without changing the meaning of
   `relationship`.
2. **For the patient-level "no known FH" case:** keep `List` /
   `List.emptyReason = "nilknown"` as the *only* normative mechanism,
   but expand the §9.4.3.4 narrative with a worked JSON example and a
   companion paragraph in the *Background and Context* / boundaries
   text noting that systems without a List endpoint may construct the
   List inline in a Bundle (e.g., transaction or document) without
   exposing a separate List interaction surface.

#### Justification

The submitter's pattern overloads `FMH.relationship` (1..1, bound to a
real relative-relationship value set) with a sentinel meaning, which
risks data-quality regressions: downstream consumers that treat
`relationship` as "this resource is about *this* relative" will silently
mis-classify a patient-level summary as a relative-level one. `FAMMEMB`
exists to express ambiguity about a single relative, not to flag a
patient-level summary — exactly the misuse the WGM TO DO already flagged.
Bundles let an implementer satisfy the existing List mechanism without
running a List endpoint, so the underlying ergonomic pain is solvable
without a semantic overload. This option still delivers the bulk of the
Patient Care work in flight, just without the new patient-level pattern.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request to allow a single-`FamilyMemberHistory`,
patient-level "no known FH" pattern. Status reason: **already addressed
by an existing mechanism** (`List` with `List.emptyReason = "nilknown"`),
and the proposed mechanism would re-purpose `FMH.relationship` in a way
inconsistent with its definition and binding. The Patient Care
re-definition of `FMH.dataAbsentReason` and the value-set migration
should be tracked under the Jan/Feb 2023 follow-up TO DOs (and any
sibling tickets), not under this one.

#### Justification

The reporter's underlying complaint is operational ("we don't have a
List endpoint") rather than a gap in the FHIR information model: `List`
+ `List.emptyReason = "nilknown"` already conveys exactly the assertion
they want to make at the patient level. Re-using a 1..1 element bound
to a relative-relationship code system to mean "this is a patient-level
summary" introduces semantic ambiguity and a real risk of mis-aggregated
family histories in consuming systems. Since the FMH-internal
improvements (per Patient Care's Jan/Feb 2023 calls) are independently
beneficial and orthogonal to this ticket's literal ask, declining here
does not block them.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B preserves the substantive Patient Care work already in
motion (the `FMH.dataAbsentReason` redefinition, the data-absent-reason
value-set realignment, and the `FMH.relationship` usage caveat) while
avoiding the semantic overload that Disposition A would cement into the
spec. The reporter's pain is real but operational — they need a way to
assert "no known family history" without standing up a List endpoint —
and that need is fully solvable by constructing the List inline in a
Bundle, which the spec already supports. Accepting the sentinel
`FMH.relationship` pattern (Disposition A) directly contradicts the WGM
caution that `FAMMEMB` "is NOT used to convey multiple family members"
and would create a downstream interoperability hazard. Disposition C is
defensible on the literal ask, but it leaves the in-flight FMH cleanup
without a clear home; folding that work into the response under
Disposition B keeps the ticket productive for Patient Care and useful
for the reporter.
