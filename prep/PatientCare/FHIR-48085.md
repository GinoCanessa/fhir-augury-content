## Summary

This is a TSMG-style governance ticket filed against the **PACIO Personal
Functioning and Engagement (PFE) IG** (Patient Care WG). It asks the IG
authors to review the TSC-approved [Terminology Guidance for IG
Authors](https://confluence.hl7.org/display/TSMG/Terminology+Expectations+for+IG+Developers)
and, for every CodeSystem the IG defines locally, decide one of:

1. Move the CodeSystem to **terminology.hl7.org (THO)** so it is
   centrally curated and reusable across HL7 specifications, **or**
2. If the CodeSystem is genuinely tightly bound to the IG and should
   not live in THO, **request a formal exemption** from TSMG (via the
   TSMG listserv, a TSMG meeting, or the relevant Zulip thread).

There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets, no Zulip threads, and no
GitHub items linked to FHIR-48085 in the indexed data. The ticket is a
standing TSMG action item on the PFE IG, not a substantive content
change to the IG itself. Disposition is essentially an audit-and-decide
exercise: enumerate the IG's locally-defined CodeSystems, classify each,
and either migrate or seek exemption.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Conduct a full audit of the **PACIO Personal Functioning and Engagement
(PFE) IG** for every locally-defined `CodeSystem` resource (i.e.,
CodeSystems whose canonical URL is rooted under the IG's canonical base
rather than `http://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/...`). For each one:

1. Evaluate against the TSC-approved [Terminology Guidance for IG
   Authors](https://confluence.hl7.org/display/TSMG/Terminology+Expectations+for+IG+Developers).
2. If it represents a generally-reusable concept domain → submit it to
   **THO**, replace the IG-local definition with a reference to the THO
   canonical, and update all dependent ValueSets / bindings in the IG
   to point at the THO URL.
3. If it is genuinely IG-bound → file an **exemption request** with
   TSMG (listserv, TSMG meeting, or the TSMG Zulip thread) documenting
   the rationale, and record the granted exemption in the IG (e.g., in
   the IG's terminology section or as a comment on this ticket).

Close the ticket once every locally-defined CodeSystem in PFE has a
recorded outcome (migrated or exempted).

#### Justification

This is exactly what the TSC-approved policy requires. PACIO PFE is a
US-realm IG produced under the Patient Care WG, and the PACIO suite
already shares many functional-status concepts with other domains
(post-acute care, rehabilitation, FHIR US Core); centralising those in
THO maximises reuse and reduces the risk of divergent CodeSystem URLs
for the same concept. Performing the audit now (rather than declining)
keeps PFE compliant with TSMG governance and avoids a publication-time
block from TSMG on the next IG ballot/STU update.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Treat the ticket as a **two-phase tracking item** rather than a single
audit-and-close:

- **Phase 1 (now):** Patient Care / PACIO leadership produces a single
  spreadsheet/table of every locally-defined CodeSystem in PFE with
  columns: canonical URL, concept-domain summary, PACIO-only? (Y/N),
  proposed disposition (Migrate-to-THO / Request-Exemption /
  Already-in-THO), and owner. Attach the table to FHIR-48085 and
  request TSMG review of the *classifications* before any migration
  work begins.
- **Phase 2 (driven by TSMG feedback):** Open one child ticket per
  CodeSystem flagged for migration (so each migration is independently
  trackable, has its own substantive change record, and can be balloted
  cleanly) and one consolidated exemption request to TSMG covering all
  IG-bound CodeSystems with their rationales.

Then close FHIR-48085 once the child tickets / exemption request are
filed.

#### Justification

The original ticket conflates *deciding* with *doing*. Splitting them
makes the work auditable, lets TSMG sanity-check the classification
before the IG team invests in migrations that may be re-scoped, and
produces clean per-CodeSystem change history that ballot reconciliation
can reference. It also reduces the risk that a single large audit
ticket sits in "Triaged" indefinitely because one or two CodeSystems
are contentious. PACIO has multiple IGs with overlapping vocabularies;
a documented classification table is also reusable for sibling TSMG
tickets that almost certainly exist (or will be filed) against PACIO
Cognitive Status, ADI, etc.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (Not Persuasive / Out of Scope for Patient Care)

#### Proposal

Decline the ticket on the grounds that FHIR-48085 is a **process
obligation owned by the IG-publishing project (PACIO)**, not a content
change to a Patient-Care-stewarded artifact, and therefore should not
sit on the Patient Care WG ballot/triage queue. Recommend either:

- Reassigning the ticket to the PACIO project workstream and closing
  the Patient Care instance as "Not Persuasive — wrong owner", or
- Closing as "Already addressed by existing process" if PACIO has
  already documented its CodeSystem disposition with TSMG outside Jira.

#### Justification

Strictly speaking, "audit your CodeSystems against TSMG policy" is a
recurring publishing-process step rather than a substantive change
request that requires a Patient Care WG vote. If PACIO has its own
governance forum (which it does), the work belongs there and FHIR-48085
risks becoming a perpetual open ticket on Patient Care's queue with no
clear definition of done at the WG level. Declining keeps Patient
Care's queue focused on substantive PFE content issues.

The risk is real but bounded: TSMG will simply re-file or escalate if
the IG ships with non-compliant CodeSystems, so declining without
handing the work off cleanly is not safe — hence the recommendation to
reassign rather than simply close.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the most defensible because it respects what the TSC
policy actually requires (a *decision* per CodeSystem, recorded and
visible to TSMG) without conflating the decision with the migration
work itself. Disposition A is correct in spirit but tends to stall in
practice — a single ticket trying to track N migrations plus an
exemption negotiation is unwieldy and produces poor change history.
Disposition C is tempting on workload grounds but leaves the IG exposed
to a TSMG block at publication time and pushes the problem onto a
forum (PACIO project) that the workgroup cannot directly task.

Concretely, the workgroup should ask PACIO PFE leadership to produce
the classification table within one cycle, post it as an attachment /
comment on FHIR-48085, and notify TSMG via the documented Zulip /
listserv channel. Child tickets and the exemption request can then be
opened with concrete, per-CodeSystem scope.
