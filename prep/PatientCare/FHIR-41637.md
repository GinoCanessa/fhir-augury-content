## Summary

FHIR-41637 asks Patient Care to clarify the *semantics* of two BackboneElements
on `ConditionDefinition`: `.observation` and `.precondition`. Specifically, the
ticket wants the spec to explain whether each entry expresses a *necessary*
criterion, a *sufficient* criterion, both, a strength (analogous to
`precondition.type`), or a different relationship altogether — and whether
groupings are required so that, e.g., `ObservationDefinition #1` is necessary
while `#3` and `#4` together are sufficient. The ticket also raises whether
sensitivity / specificity should be captured.

This ticket is a direct follow-on from
[FHIR-39334](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-39334) ("ConditionDefinition
should use canonical(ObservationDefinition)"), which Patient Care marked
**Persuasive / Applied**. FHIR-39334's resolution explicitly defers
`ConditionDefinition.precondition` to **this** ticket: *"(ConditionDefinition.precondition
will be addressed via https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-41637)"*. So FHIR-41637
is the second half of the ConditionDefinition rework — the typing change for
`.observation` (BackboneElement → `canonical(ObservationDefinition)`) was
applied in PR HL7/fhir#2958, but the semantic question of how multiple
observations / preconditions combine, and how `.precondition` should be
modeled, was deliberately split out to be answered here.

## Details

**Description:**

> Address semantics of ConditionDefinition.observation and .precondition.
> Explain whether they are necessary, sufficient, express strength (like
> precondition.type), or some combination of different relationships, or may
> require grouping (e.g,. ObsDef 1 is nec; 2 is sufficient; 3 & 4 are
> sufficient; specificity and sensitivity may also be useful).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add explicit semantic typing to both `ConditionDefinition.observation`
and `ConditionDefinition.precondition` so that each entry can be marked
as necessary, sufficient, or contributory, and so that grouped /
combinatorial criteria can be expressed:

1. In `source/conditiondefinition/` (StructureDefinition differential):
   - Add `ConditionDefinition.observation.criterionType` (1..1 `code`,
     bound to a new required ValueSet such as
     `condition-criterion-type` with codes `necessary | sufficient |
     supportive | exclusion`). Mirror `precondition.type`'s pattern so
     authors already know the shape.
   - Add `ConditionDefinition.observation.group` (0..1 `string` or
     `positiveInt`) so multiple `.observation` entries with the same
     `group` value form a *sufficient set* (any-of within group, all
     groups conjoined). Repeat the same pair
     (`.criterionType`, `.group`) on `.precondition`.
   - Optionally add `.observation.sensitivity` and
     `.observation.specificity` as 0..1 `decimal` (0..1 range,
     percentage) where evidence supports them.
2. In `source/conditiondefinition/conditiondefinition-notes.html` (or
   the resource intro page): add a normative paragraph spelling out the
   combination rules — *"Within a `group`, criteria are OR'd; across
   groups, criteria are AND'd; `necessary` criteria must hold for the
   ConditionDefinition to apply; `sufficient` criteria, when met,
   establish it; `supportive` criteria are informative only."*

#### Justification

This is exactly what the ticket asks for: an explicit, machine-readable
representation of the semantics that authors of `ConditionDefinition`
instances need. It mirrors the existing `precondition.type` pattern
(consistency with current FHIR design), aligns `.observation` and
`.precondition` so authors learn one model, and puts the
`group`-based combinatorial logic in the resource itself rather than in
prose. Because the FHIR-39334 retype to
`canonical(ObservationDefinition)` is already applied (PR HL7/fhir#2958),
the new sub-elements attach cleanly to the already-reshaped
BackboneElement-replacement structure — the differential touched is
contained to `source/conditiondefinition/`. Per the FhirCore briefing,
this is a standard "Update resource StructureDefinition (differential
only) + Update narrative / prose" recipe, identical to the recipe used
for #2958, and the publisher will regenerate `<snapshot>` automatically.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Resolve the question with **prose only** plus an extension, rather than
adding new core elements:

1. In the resource notes page for ConditionDefinition, add a normative
   *Combination Semantics* section that explicitly states the default
   reading: "All `.observation` entries are interpreted as
   `supportive / necessary` taken together; `.precondition.type` continues
   to express strength on a per-precondition basis. Implementers needing
   richer semantics (sufficient sets, sensitivity / specificity) SHALL use
   the standard extension defined below."
2. Define one extension in `HL7/fhir-extensions` —
   e.g., `condition-criterion-semantics` — carrying
   `criterionType` (code) and `group` (string) sub-extensions, applicable
   to both `ConditionDefinition.observation` and
   `ConditionDefinition.precondition`. Reference it from the
   ConditionDefinition spec page.
3. Optionally publish a second extension
   `condition-observation-evidence` for sensitivity / specificity.

This keeps the core resource shape unchanged but gives implementers a
standardized vocabulary to express the semantics the ticket asks about.

#### Justification

`ConditionDefinition` is a low-volume, definitional resource and the
combinatorial-semantics question may not be settled in clinical
practice. Pushing the typed elements out to standard extensions:

- avoids breaking implementers who have already started using the
  R5-applied `.observation: canonical(ObservationDefinition)` shape from
  FHIR-39334;
- keeps required-binding ValueSets out of the core spec until the
  vocabulary stabilises;
- per the briefing's cross-repo touch-point note, contains the change
  to `HL7/fhir` (notes pages) and `HL7/fhir-extensions` rather than
  pulling in `HL7/UTG` for a brand-new required ValueSet on a core
  element.

The cost is implementer ergonomics — these become non-default
extensions — but reversing course later (extension → core element) is
much easier than the reverse.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request as currently scoped. Close FHIR-41637 with a
*"Not Persuasive — already addressed"* resolution stating that
`ConditionDefinition.precondition.type` already provides per-precondition
strength, and that combination semantics across multiple `.observation`
and `.precondition` entries are the responsibility of the consuming
profile / clinical guideline rather than the core resource. Recommend
that implementers needing richer semantics define them in their own IG
profiles.

#### Justification

`ConditionDefinition` has minimal real-world adoption to date, and the
core team has historically been cautious about adding code-bound
elements to under-used definitional resources without clear evidence of
divergent implementer practice. The Zulip context for the parent
discussion contains only three messages and no implementer demand for
the specific combinatorial semantics requested. Declining preserves the
already-applied FHIR-39334 shape, avoids the cost of a new required
ValueSet (and a `HL7/UTG` touch-point), and keeps the spec change
surface minimal.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition: A.**

Patient Care has already accepted the structural premise — that
`ConditionDefinition`'s definitional observations should be
canonical-typed (FHIR-39334, *Persuasive / Applied*) — and FHIR-39334's
own resolution text explicitly defers the `.precondition` question and
the combination-semantics question to *this* ticket. Closing FHIR-41637
without a substantive answer (Disposition C) would leave a known gap
that Patient Care itself has acknowledged. Disposition B (extensions
only) is a defensible fallback if the work group is uncomfortable
adding a required ValueSet, but it punts the very ergonomics question
the ticket raises into implementer-side guesswork and creates a
two-tier model where `precondition.type` is core-typed but
`observation`'s parallel concept is extension-typed — exactly the
inconsistency that FHIR-39334 was raised to fix in the first place.
Disposition A keeps `.observation` and `.precondition` symmetrical,
gives authors one mental model, and — per the FhirCore briefing — is a
single-repo, differential-only change that the bespoke publisher can
absorb without cross-repo coordination. The work group should review
the proposed code set (`necessary | sufficient | supportive |
exclusion`) and the `group` mechanic; once those are agreed, the edit
itself is small and self-contained.
