## Summary

Bryn Rhodes asks the work group to retire twelve "architecture" extensions
currently defined in the US Public Health Profiles Library (USPHPL) and
replace them with equivalents already published in the HL7 FHIR Extensions
Pack (or in the CQL/CQF extension family). Eight of the listed extensions
(Async indicator, Data encrypted, Data encryption algorithm, Expected
response time, Message processing category, Named event type, Receiver
JWKS Url, Trust service endpoint) would simply be moved to the extensions
pack as-is. Four are to be deprecated in favour of named pre-existing
replacements: *Alternative expression* → `cqf-alternativeExpression`,
*Expansion parameters* → `cqf-expansionParameters`, *Usage warning* →
`valueset-warning`, and *Related data* → `cqf-relatedRequirement` (the
extension defined by [FHIR-44779](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-44779),
*Support relatedRequirements*, which has been Applied in the Using CQL
With FHIR IG and HL7/fhir-extensions PR #162).

This is the omnibus form of a clean-up Bryn filed as a wave of tickets in
2025. Three of the four "replace" line items have already been disposed
individually as **Persuasive / Published** against USPHPL under the
`2025_USPHPL_ARCHITECTURE_PROFILES` label
([FHIR-52763](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-52763) for
cqf-alternativeExpression,
[FHIR-52764](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-52764) for
cqf-expansionParameters,
[FHIR-52765](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-52765) for
valueset-warning), and an earlier expansion-parameters ticket
([FHIR-49700](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-49700)) was Resolved
*Persuasive with Modification* and pointed at FHIR-52764 for the actual
work. The parallel MedMorph-scoped ticket
([FHIR-49780](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-49780)) was Resolved
**Not Persuasive** because MedMorph is being retired at the end of STU.
What therefore remains genuinely open on this ticket is the eight
"move to the extensions pack" extensions — none of which appear to have
been individually disposed yet — plus a routing question (the ticket is
on the Patient Care queue but every sibling and the spec itself sit
with Public Health).

## Proposed Dispositions

Note on routing: the ticket is currently on the **Patient Care** WG
queue, but the specification is *US Public Health Profiles Library*
and every sibling ticket (FHIR-49780, -52763, -52764, -52765, -49700,
-38348) is owned by **Public Health**. Whichever disposition is
chosen, the WG should also reassign the ticket to Public Health (or
formally pass it across) to keep this work consolidated under
`2025_USPHPL_ARCHITECTURE_PROFILES`.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat FHIR-49781 as the umbrella ticket, transfer it to Public Health
WG, and dispose it as **Persuasive** with the following resolution:

1. Mark the four "replace" line items as **already addressed**, citing
   the published siblings:
   - *Alternative expression* → addressed by FHIR-52763
     (`cqf-alternativeExpression`).
   - *Expansion parameters* → addressed by FHIR-52764
     (`cqf-expansionParameters`).
   - *Related data extension* → addressed by FHIR-44779
     (`cqf-relatedRequirement`).
   - *Usage warning* → addressed by FHIR-52765 (`valueset-warning`).
2. For each of the eight remaining "move to extension pack"
   extensions, file a child ticket under
   `2025_USPHPL_ARCHITECTURE_PROFILES` (mirroring the
   FHIR-52763/4/5 pattern) so each extension's move is tracked,
   reviewed, and balloted individually:
   - `us-ph-async-indicator` → `uv-extensions/async-indicator` (or
     equivalent name) in HL7/fhir-extensions.
   - `us-ph-data-encrypted`
   - `us-ph-data-encryption-algorithm`
   - `us-ph-expected-responsetime`
   - `us-ph-messageprocessingcategory`
   - `us-ph-named-event-type`
   - `us-ph-receiver-jwks-url`
   - `us-ph-trust-service-endpoint`
3. Once all child tickets are Applied, deprecate the USPHPL local
   copies (mark `status = deprecated`, leave a `replaced-by`
   reference) for one publication cycle before removing them, to give
   MedMorph and other consumers an upgrade path.

#### Justification

This is exactly the pattern the WG has already endorsed. Three
"replace" siblings (FHIR-52763/4/5) and the precursor FHIR-49700 are
all Persuasive/Published, and FHIR-44779 has already shipped the
relatedRequirement replacement. Re-using shared extensions is the
stated direction of the FHIR Extensions Pack effort: it removes
duplicate normative artifacts, lets implementers reuse one canonical
URL across PH/CQL/CDS contexts, and shrinks the USPHPL maintenance
surface. Splitting the eight remaining extensions into individual
child tickets matches the granularity of FHIR-52763/4/5 and keeps the
ballot trail clean — each extension's move can be reviewed by
HL7/fhir-extensions on its own merits, since some (e.g. the JWKS URL
and trust-service-endpoint extensions) carry security semantics that
deserve scrutiny in a pack-wide context.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-49781 as **Persuasive with Modification** and split it
explicitly into two tracks:

- **"Replace" track (close immediately as already addressed):** the
  four substitutions are already disposed by FHIR-44779, FHIR-52763,
  FHIR-52764, and FHIR-52765. Resolution text simply cross-references
  those tickets.
- **"Move-to-pack" track (defer):** the eight pure relocations are
  not moved to `HL7/fhir-extensions` immediately. Instead, USPHPL
  marks them `experimental = false, status = active` (no change in
  USPHPL R1) and submits a single batched proposal to the extensions
  pack maintainers asking whether the pack wants to adopt them as
  generic (non-PH-specific) extensions. Only those the pack accepts
  are then migrated; the rest stay in USPHPL with a comment
  explaining that the extensions pack declined to generalise them.

#### Justification

Several of the eight "move" extensions are not obviously generic. The
`us-ph-receiver-jwks-url`, `us-ph-trust-service-endpoint`,
`us-ph-data-encrypted`, and `us-ph-data-encryption-algorithm`
extensions encode an opinionated trust/encryption model that grew up
inside the MedMorph reporting architecture; lifting them verbatim into
the cross-domain extensions pack risks blessing one IG's choices as
the FHIR-wide answer. Likewise `us-ph-messageprocessingcategory` and
`us-ph-named-event-type` are tightly coupled to the MessageHeader-based
PH reporting flow. Disposition B keeps the easy "replace" wins
(which the WG has already taken) while letting the extensions pack
maintainers decide whether each "move" candidate actually belongs in
a cross-IG pack — and it leaves USPHPL functional in the meantime.
The trade-off vs. Disposition A is slower migration and the
possibility that some extensions never leave USPHPL.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve FHIR-49781 as **Not Persuasive (Already Addressed)**. The
substantive work is being done by the per-extension siblings under
the `2025_USPHPL_ARCHITECTURE_PROFILES` label
(FHIR-52763 / -52764 / -52765, plus FHIR-44779 for relatedRequirement);
the parallel MedMorph copy (FHIR-49780) was already declined because
MedMorph is being retired. The omnibus list adds no actionable
content beyond what the per-extension tickets capture. Direct any
remaining "move-to-pack" appetite into individual tickets filed by
the requester so each extension can be triaged on its own merits.

#### Justification

The umbrella ticket is now strictly less informative than the per-
extension tickets it spawned, and trying to dispose all twelve items
in one resolution risks losing the per-extension nuance that
HL7/fhir-extensions reviewers will want. Closing the umbrella avoids
a long-running "partly Applied" state where four of twelve items are
done and the rest are open. Implementers tracking the work should
follow `2025_USPHPL_ARCHITECTURE_PROFILES`, not this ticket.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The WG has already, in practice, committed to this clean-up: every
sibling ticket that has been disposed has been disposed Persuasive,
including the three explicit "replace with cqf-*/valueset-warning"
items. Closing the umbrella as Persuasive (Disposition A) records
that decision in one place, makes the eight remaining "move-to-pack"
items explicit by spawning child tickets that mirror the proven
FHIR-52763/4/5 pattern, and gives implementers a single anchor ticket
to follow. Disposition B's caution about whether all eight extensions
*belong* in the pack is real, but it is best resolved per-extension by
HL7/fhir-extensions reviewers on the child tickets, not by deferring
the umbrella. Disposition C is technically defensible but loses the
useful planning signal — the eight "move" items have no per-extension
ticket yet, and dropping the umbrella without spawning them risks
quiet stagnation. Recommend Accept, transfer to Public Health WG to
match the sibling tickets, and assign the child-ticket creation back
to the reporter.
