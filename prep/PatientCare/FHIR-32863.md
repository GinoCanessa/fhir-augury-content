## Summary

The ticket asks the IPS (International Patient Summary) work group to widen
the value-set binding on `Observation.status` used by the IPS Results
section. As authored in 2021, the IPS results binding restricted observation
status to `final` only, which excludes the legitimate "post-signoff"
statuses (`amended`, `corrected`, `appended`). The reporter (Elliot Silver)
argues that observations may legitimately move beyond `final` after
correction or amendment, and that excluding those values causes valid
finalised results to be inadmissible in an IPS document. A directly related
sibling ticket, [FHIR-32862](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-32862), makes
the identical request for `DiagnosticReport.status` and should be considered
together with this one. Subsequent IPS publications partially addressed
this — the current `ResultsStatusUvIps` value set now includes `final`,
`amended`, and `corrected` (per the connectathon discussion captured in
Zulip) — leaving `appended` (and the broader "should pending/preliminary
statuses also be displayable?" question raised at Connectathon 37) as the
outstanding gap that re-justifies keeping this ticket open.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the IPS (International Patient Summary, FHIR) Results-section
status binding so it admits the full set of post-signoff
`ObservationStatus` codes. Concretely, broaden the `ResultsStatusUvIps`
value set so that, in addition to `final` (and the already-added `amended`
and `corrected`), it includes `appended`. Symmetrically update
`DiagnosticReport.status` per FHIR-32862 by adjusting the matching
`ResultsStatusUvIps` (or sibling) value set used for the IPS DiagnosticReport
binding to add `appended`. The binding strength stays as currently defined
(required), and the change is value-set-only — no profile cardinality or
slicing changes are required.

#### Justification

`appended` is defined in core FHIR `ObservationStatus` precisely as a
post-signoff state ("subsequent observations have been added"), so it is
exactly the kind of valid finalised-then-updated state the IPS Results
section is meant to summarise. Excluding it forces implementers either to
suppress legitimate results from the IPS or to misrepresent their status,
both of which are worse than the small value-set widening. The consistency
argument is also strong: `amended` and `corrected` were already added in
the previous STU update (per the Connectathon 37 thread), so admitting
`appended` simply completes a partial fix and matches what the original
ticket asked for in 2021.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Solve the underlying implementer problem more broadly than the literal
ticket text. Two pieces:

1. Add `appended` to `ResultsStatusUvIps` (as in Disposition A) — this is
   the no-controversy core change.
2. In addition, address the Connectathon 37 use case (New Brunswick PHR)
   by either (a) widening the IPS Results value set to also permit
   non-final-but-clinically-relevant statuses such as `preliminary`, or
   (b) introducing a separate "Pending / In-Progress Results" subsection
   in the IPS structure so non-finalised observations have a defined,
   distinct place to live rather than being conflated with finalised
   results. Apply the symmetric change to FHIR-32862 for
   `DiagnosticReport.status`.

#### Justification

The 2024 connectathon discussion shows the binding gap is wider than the
2021 ticket framing — implementers are losing not just `appended`
observations but also `preliminary`/`registered` ones. Disposition A
unblocks the literal request but predictably leaves the next ticket from
the same implementers waiting; B addresses the systemic issue. The
sub-section variant (2b) preserves the clinical semantics that "an IPS
Results entry is a finalised result" while still letting an IPS document
carry pending data, which is closer to the existing IPS information model
than simply opening up the value set. Trade-off vs. A: more design work,
needs a focused IPS / Patient Care discussion, and (for variant 2b) is a
structural change to the IPS guide rather than a value-set tweak — but it
is the response most likely to actually satisfy the field reports.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as Considered-for-Future-Use / Not Persuasive on the residual
`appended` ask, on the grounds that the substantive concern raised in
2021 has already been addressed by the addition of `amended` and
`corrected` to `ResultsStatusUvIps`, and that any further widening
(including `appended` and `preliminary`) should be deferred until the
broader "non-final results in IPS" discussion (Connectathon 37 / New
Brunswick) produces a clear design proposal — at which point a fresh,
scoped ticket should drive that work.

#### Justification

This is the disposition the work group originally voted (14-0-16, block
vote 2, Sept 2022), and the comment trail shows it was reverted only as a
parking action ("we will investigate after the next publication update")
rather than because new evidence overturned the substance. Adding
`appended` in isolation arguably nibbles at a problem whose real shape —
how IPS handles non-finalised results — still needs design work. Declining
keeps the IPS Results section semantically tight (finalised results only)
and avoids a value-set change that may be superseded by a structural one.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The literal request (add `appended`) is uncontroversial and should land
regardless. But the Connectathon 37 conversation has materially changed
the picture since the 2022 vote: implementers in production deployments
(New Brunswick PHR) are reporting that the binding is still too narrow in
practice, beyond just `appended`. Going with A in isolation will
predictably generate another "binding too narrow" ticket from the same
constituency within a publication cycle. Going with C ignores fresh
evidence and reverses the November 2022 decision to keep the ticket open.
B threads the needle: it ships the trivial fix immediately and uses this
ticket to commission the small, scoped piece of design work (value-set
widening vs. dedicated sub-section) that the Patient Care / IPS group has
already heard implementer demand for. FHIR-32862 should be dispositioned
identically.
