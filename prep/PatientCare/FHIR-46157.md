## Summary

The ticket asks the Patient Care work group, on behalf of the US SDOH
Clinical Care (Gravity) IG, to relax the binding strength of
`Observation.code` on the **SDOHCC Observation Screening Response**
profile. The reporter notes that for emerging SDOH domains the Gravity
project has not yet curated LOINC codes, and that implementers who need
to author **grouper** Observations (which combine multiple screening
responses) often have no LOINC code to use for the grouper itself. As a
result, those implementers cannot stay conformant with the IG. The
proposed change is to switch the binding from its current strength to
**extensible**, allowing implementers to use locally-defined or
non-LOINC codes when no suitable LOINC is available, while still
preferring LOINC concepts when one exists.

No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

On the **SDOHCC Observation Screening Response** profile in the US SDOH
Clinical Care IG, change the binding strength of `Observation.code` from
its current strength to **extensible**, keeping the existing Gravity-
curated LOINC ValueSet as the bound ValueSet. Update the profile's
narrative to explain that LOINC codes from the bound ValueSet MUST be
used when one exists for the screening concept, and that implementers
MAY supply an alternative `Coding` (including a local code) only when no
appropriate LOINC concept is available — most commonly for
implementer-defined grouper Observations or for SDOH domains not yet
covered by Gravity. Add or update an `obligation`/usage note clarifying
that `Observation.code.text` SHOULD always be populated when a non-LOINC
code is used, so consumers retain a human-readable label.

#### Justification

This matches the request literally and removes the conformance barrier
the reporter describes: implementers building groupers or covering not-
yet-modelled SDOH domains can stay IG-conformant without waiting for
Gravity to publish a LOINC code. Extensible is the conventional FHIR
strength used precisely for this case ("use the value set unless no
appropriate code exists"), and it preserves the IG's preference for
LOINC while allowing controlled deviation. It is also consistent with
how other US Realm IGs (e.g., US Core) handle Observation profiles where
LOINC coverage is incomplete.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep `Observation.code` bound as **required** to the existing LOINC
ValueSet on the *base* SDOHCC Observation Screening Response profile,
but (1) extend the bound ValueSet to include a small set of Gravity-
managed grouper codes (or formally include LOINC's screening-grouper
codes where they exist), and (2) publish a **derived profile** —
e.g., *SDOHCC Observation Screening Response Grouper* — that rebinds
`Observation.code` to **extensible** against a broader ValueSet that
permits implementer-defined codes for the grouper case only. Individual
screening *answer* Observations would continue to use the strict
binding; only the grouper layer would be relaxed.

#### Justification

This preserves the strong terminology guarantee that downstream
analytics, quality measurement, and exchange partners rely on for
individual screening responses, while still solving the concrete pain
point the reporter raised (groupers and not-yet-modelled domains).
Splitting the use case into two profiles makes the looser binding an
explicit, documented choice rather than a global relaxation, which keeps
the conformance signal high for the common case (a single screening
answer) and reduces the risk of implementers reaching for local codes
when a perfectly good LOINC already exists.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request and keep the current required binding. Direct
implementers who need codes for new SDOH domains or for grouper
Observations to (a) submit the missing concept to Gravity / LOINC for
inclusion in the bound ValueSet, or (b) use a different resource or
profile (for example, a generic `Observation` outside the SDOHCC
Screening Response profile) when they cannot meet the binding.

#### Justification

A required binding is the IG's primary mechanism for guaranteeing
semantic interoperability of screening data across SDOH implementers
and downstream consumers (CMS, payers, analytics). Relaxing it broadly
creates a long-term risk that implementers will introduce divergent
local codes for concepts that *do* have, or will soon have, LOINC
representation, fragmenting the data. The Gravity submission path
already exists to add missing concepts, and the grouper use case can be
served by a non-conformant profile rather than by weakening the IG's
core terminology contract.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter has identified a real, recurring conformance gap: Gravity's
LOINC coverage will always lag behind implementation needs, and the
grouper use case in particular is unlikely to be fully addressed by
LOINC in the near term. An extensible binding is the standard FHIR tool
for exactly this situation — it preserves Gravity's strong preference
for LOINC (implementers MUST use a LOINC code when one fits) while
removing the hard barrier that currently forces implementers off-IG.
Disposition B is attractive in principle but materially increases IG
maintenance cost (a second profile, additional ValueSet curation) for a
benefit Disposition A largely already delivers via the "use the bound
code if one exists" semantics of extensible. Disposition C does not
solve the problem the reporter raised and would push real-world SDOH
implementations outside the IG, weakening the IG's reach. The work
group may wish to ask Gravity to confirm whether an extensible binding
is acceptable from the program's perspective before final adoption.
