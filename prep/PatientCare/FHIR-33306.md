## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a dedicated **Use Cases** page to the Ophthalmology Retinal (FHIR)
IG and expand the existing introductory/overview narrative with
explanatory text. Concretely:

- Author a new `usecases.md` (or equivalent narrative page) under the
  IG's `input/pagecontent/` (or comparable authoring) directory and
  register it in the IG's `ImplementationGuide` resource definition /
  `sushi-config.yaml` so it appears in the rendered table of contents.
- For each major retinal-imaging / ophthalmology workflow the IG
  intends to support (e.g., DICOM-sourced retinal image reporting,
  diabetic retinopathy screening referrals, optometrist↔ophthalmologist
  referral exchange, post-procedure follow-up), describe: actors,
  trigger, preconditions, FHIR resources exchanged, and outcome.
- Expand the IG home / overview page with explanatory text covering
  scope, in-scope vs. out-of-scope conditions, relationship to the
  broader Imaging IGs and to other Patient Care specifications, and a
  glossary of ophthalmology-specific terms used in the profiles.
- Cross-link each profile page to the use case(s) it participates in.

#### Justification

The reporter is asking for content that virtually every HL7 IG ships
(use cases + explanatory narrative are part of the standard IG
template). Adding them strengthens implementer adoption and matches the
HL7 IG style guide expectation that profiles be motivated by named
workflows. Because the request is purely additive narrative, it carries
no breaking-change risk and does not require ballot-affecting changes
to any conformance artifact.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than treating this as a single bulk-content change, **convert
FHIR-33306 into a tracking/parent ticket** and split the work into
narrowly-scoped child tickets that the work group can prioritize and
ballot-track individually:

- One child ticket per major use case to be authored (each child cites
  the specific workflow and the profiles it touches).
- A separate child ticket for the IG overview / scope rewrite.
- A separate child ticket for a glossary / terminology page.

Resolve FHIR-33306 itself as "Persuasive with Mod — split into children"
and leave it open only as the umbrella reference.

#### Justification

The ticket as filed is too unspecific to act on directly: there is no
"existing wording", no "proposed wording", no enumeration of which use
cases are missing, and no named author for the new content. Splitting
preserves the reporter's intent while giving the work group items it can
actually estimate, assign, and ballot. It also avoids a single sprawling
PR against the IG that would be hard to review. This is a common
disposition pattern for "the IG needs more narrative" tickets across
HL7.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve as **Not Persuasive — insufficient detail to action**. The
ticket states only "Include Use Cases and explanitory text within the
IG" with both Existing Wording and Proposed Wording marked "NA" and is
flagged as an imported comment fragment ("Comment 4 - imported by: Chris
Shawn"). Close with a request that the original requester (or any
interested implementer) re-file with concrete proposed use cases and
draft explanatory text.

#### Justification

HL7's change-request process expects, at minimum, a description of the
problem and either proposed wording or a concrete proposal. This ticket
supplies neither, has been open since 2021-08-31 with no comments, no
assignee, and no engagement, and appears to be a stub captured from
another conversation rather than a deliberately authored change
request. Declining (with an invitation to re-file with specifics) keeps
the backlog clean and avoids the work group committing to open-ended
narrative work without a defined scope or owner.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The reporter's underlying point — that the Ophthalmology Retinal IG
would benefit from use cases and explanatory narrative — is reasonable
and aligns with HL7 IG conventions, so a flat decline (C) would discard
useful signal. At the same time, accepting as requested (A) commits the
work group to author an unbounded amount of narrative content with no
named owner, no enumerated workflows, and no proposed wording, which is
precisely the situation that produces stalled tickets like this one
(open four years, zero comments). Splitting the request into a small
set of concrete, scoped child tickets (B) preserves the intent, makes
the work assignable and ballot-trackable, and forces the conversation
the ticket actually needs: *which* use cases, written by *whom*, against
*which* IG version. The work group should additionally confirm the
current maintenance status and source repository of the Ophthalmology
Retinal IG before scheduling the children, and may wish to ask the
reporter (or another ophthalmology SME) to seed the use-case list.
