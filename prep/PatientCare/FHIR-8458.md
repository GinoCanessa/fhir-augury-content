## Summary

The ticket asks how a blood transfusion (e.g., SNOMED 116859006 "Transfusion of
blood product (procedure)") should be modeled in FHIR. The submitter notes
that although SNOMED classifies it as a procedure, the `Procedure` resource
has no element to capture quantity (number of units / mL) of the product
infused, which makes the use case look closer to `MedicationAdministration`.
Patient Care has revisited this multiple times since 2015 and consistently
landed on the position that neither `Procedure` nor `MedicationAdministration`
is a clean fit, and that a dedicated administration-style resource is needed
for the administration of blood and other biologically derived products
(plasma, platelets, stem cells, tissue, organs, and possibly synthetic
substitutes; with related discussion about radiation/light therapy as an
adjacent gap). The most recent comment links the ticket to Orders &
Observations work captured in [FHIR-51207](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51207),
which catalogues the broader gap in BDP request/administration/statement
resources alongside the existing `BiologicallyDerivedProduct` and
`BiologicallyDerivedProductDispense`. Implementer discussion on Zulip
("Which resource to use to capture blood transfusion given …", "Procedure
resulting from ServiceRequest – missing quantity", "Radiation therapy", and
"Blood, Tissue, Organ: Donation") confirms the same pattern: implementers
today fall back to `Procedure` (sometimes with extensions) or
`MedicationAdministration`, and at least one IG (HL7 ICSR AE Reporting)
profiles `Procedure` for transfusion. A 2025-06-23 O&O HCP discussion noted
that for partially-infused products the volume can presently be modelled as
`Procedure.outcome`, but flagged that the broader administration story for
BDPs (especially cell-therapy products that are not drugs) remains unsettled
and that the cleanest options are either to extend `MedicationAdministration`
to allow a `CodeableReference` to a BDP or to introduce a new
"BDP-Administration" / `BloodProductAdministration` resource — the option
Patient Care has historically preferred.

## Details

**Description:**

An example of a use case is the snomed code:

- *Name:* Transfusion of blood product (procedure)
- *Concept ID:* 116859006

which lists it as a procedure, but the FHIR Procedure resource does not have
a way to indicate quantity (# units or # mL), so that seems to make it closer
to a MedicationAdministration… Thoughts?

Thanks!

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Accept the long-standing Patient Care direction recorded in this ticket:
introduce a new resource — currently scoped on the ticket as
`BloodProductAdministration` — to capture the administration of biologically
derived products (whole blood, packed red blood cells, plasma, platelets,
stem cells, tissues, and other BDPs). The new resource would carry, at
minimum:

- a reference (and `CodeableReference`) to `BiologicallyDerivedProduct` or
  to a `Substance` / coded transfusion product when an in-system BDP
  instance is unavailable,
- the administered quantity (`SimpleQuantity`, supporting units such as
  units, mL, products), separately from any "ordered" quantity,
- start/stop timing and rate,
- patient and encounter context, performer, location, lot/identifier
  pass-through (ISBT 128), reactions / adverse events, and a status that
  follows the standard event-resource status pattern,
- explicit cross-references on `Procedure` and `MedicationAdministration`
  introductions pointing implementers to the new resource for transfusion
  use cases.

Pair the resource with a Patient Care–owned PSS and explicitly invite
co-sponsorship from Pharmacy and Orders & Observations so it can also be
the home for stem-cell-product administration (per the 2025-06-23 O&O HCP
discussion captured in the comment by JIRAUSER19407) and align with
[FHIR-51207](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51207).

#### Justification

This is what the comment history has been pointing at since 2015. The
2015-08-06 Patient Care vote (9-0-0), the 2020-03-28 reversion of the
"Considered for Future Use" resolution, the 2021-05-06 "next step is to log
a FHIR Resource proposal for a new `BloodProductAdministration` resource"
note, and the 2025-06-23 O&O HCP discussion all converge on the same
conclusion: neither `Procedure` (no quantity) nor `MedicationAdministration`
(BDPs are not drugs and are not currently referenceable from
`MedicationAdministration`) is a clean fit. The Zulip "Blood, Tissue,
Organ: Donation" thread has the same conclusion from the implementer
side and is what produced `BiologicallyDerivedProduct` in the first place;
[FHIR-51207](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51207) shows that O&O sees
the analogous gap for the request/dispense/statement axis. A dedicated
administration resource keeps the BDP family parallel to the Medication
and Device families and avoids overloading either.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than introducing a new resource, extend `MedicationAdministration`
(and, by symmetry, `MedicationRequest` / `MedicationStatement`) to permit
a `CodeableReference` to `BiologicallyDerivedProduct` as an alternative to
`Medication`, and add a precise mapping pattern that documents transfusion
modelling as "`MedicationAdministration` referencing
`BiologicallyDerivedProduct` (or an ISBT 128 / SNOMED-coded product) with
quantity expressed via the existing `dosage.dose[x]` / `rate[x]` slots".
Until that change is balloted, formally document the existing alternative
the 2025-06-23 O&O HCP comment describes — modelling administration through
`Procedure` and capturing volume on `Procedure.outcome` — as a published,
non-normative interim guidance in the BDP/Procedure introductions, with
worked examples for whole-blood transfusion, platelet/PRBC transfusion,
and partial stem-cell infusion.

#### Justification

Lloyd McKenzie's 2021-05-06 caution ("a transfusion-specific resource will
be a hard sell at FMG, and we also need a solution that works for light
therapy, radiation therapy, etc.") is real: the same modelling gap surfaces
in the Radiation therapy thread, in the Procedure-missing-quantity thread,
and in the Brachytherapy / external-beam discussions. A new resource scoped
narrowly to blood products risks being too narrow to absorb those adjacent
problems while still requiring the spec to invent yet another resource for
radiation. Extending `MedicationAdministration`'s reference target via
`CodeableReference` reuses an already-balloted dose model, gets the change
in front of implementers faster, and lines up with how at least one IG
(HL7 ICSR AE Reporting) and several Zulip respondents already use the
medication-style dose pattern. The Procedure.outcome interim guidance is
already present in the example binding for `Procedure.outcome` and is the
de-facto pattern several implementers report using today; codifying it
gives implementers a defensible answer in the meantime without committing
the spec to a new resource it may later regret.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-8458 as **Persuasive with Mod** in the form of "no normative
change to `Procedure`; modelling guidance only". Specifically, decline to
add a quantity element to `Procedure` for this use case, decline to create
a new `BloodProductAdministration` resource at this time, and instead
direct implementers to:

1. use `Procedure` to record the act of transfusion,
2. reference the product via a `BiologicallyDerivedProduct` instance (or
   an ISBT 128 / SNOMED `CodeableConcept` if no instance exists), and
3. capture the administered volume / units via a `Procedure.outcome`
   `Observation` (per the 2025-06-23 O&O HCP comment) — which is already
   consistent with the example binding.

Document this in the Patient Care section of the BDP and Procedure
introductions and close the ticket.

#### Justification

The ticket has been open since 2015, has been resolved-and-reverted at
least once, and a new resource has yet to attract a PSS author or a
volunteer to drive it through FMG; meanwhile, multiple implementers
(ICSR AE Reporting IG, mCODE, the implementers cited in the Zulip
threads) have already shipped using `Procedure` (with or without
extensions) and report it as workable. The 2025-06-23 O&O HCP comment
explicitly states that the partial-volume case can be handled today via
`Procedure.outcome`. If the workgroup does not have capacity to drive a
new resource (the 2016-03-17 comment shows this has been the limiting
factor for a decade), publishing clear interim modelling guidance is more
useful to implementers than leaving the ticket open indefinitely. This
also avoids a hard FMG conversation (Lloyd McKenzie's 2021-05-06 warning)
and lets [FHIR-51207](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51207) be the
forcing function for any future BDP family expansion if O&O has more
appetite to drive it than Patient Care has had.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A — accept the new-resource direction, but
explicitly co-sponsor with Orders & Observations and Pharmacy and align
the scope with [FHIR-51207](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51207).

This is the position the workgroup has converged on in writing four times
(2015 vote, 2017 deferral, 2020 reversion, 2021 PSS plan), and the
2025-06-23 O&O HCP discussion plus the 2025-09-17 cross-link to FHIR-51207
shows that O&O is now actively raising the same gap on the request /
dispense / statement axis. Continuing to defer through Disposition C
preserves a known modelling debt that implementers have been working
around with extensions and ad-hoc `Procedure.outcome` patterns for ten
years; Disposition B (extending `MedicationAdministration`) is technically
attractive but the JIRAUSER19407 2025-06-23 comment is explicit that BDPs
"are not currently referenceable via Medication Administration aside from
SNOMED bindings to Medication" and that "BDPs such as cell-therapy
products for transplant are often not directly related to drug products,
meaning it may not be suitable to be modeled in medication contexts" — i.e.,
the workgroup most familiar with the BDP family has already considered
the medication route and rejected it. The right starting move for
discussion is therefore to commit to Disposition A and use FHIR-51207 to
scope the wider BDP request/administration/statement family rather than
litigating a transfusion-only resource a second time. The Disposition B
"Procedure.outcome interim guidance" is still valuable and should be
published immediately as a stop-gap while the new resource(s) are being
balloted.
