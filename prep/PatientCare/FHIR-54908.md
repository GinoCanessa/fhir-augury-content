## Summary

This ballot comment on the US SDOH Clinical Care (FHIR) IG asserts that a
referral should not be sent automatically simply because a community-based
organization (CBO) or other referral target has availability. The reporter
argues that the referral source (the provider/originating system) must be
given an explicit opportunity to confirm the referral before it is
transmitted, especially when the user has not specifically chosen a target
to query. While automation may reduce administrative burden, the reporter
maintains that the provider should review the CBO(s) presented and then
actively initiate the referral, rather than have the system fire it off as
a side effect of a successful availability check or match.

No related Jira tickets, Zulip discussions, or GitHub items were surfaced
through the cross-reference index or through Zulip / multi-source content
search for `FHIR-54908`, so the request is treated on its own. The
workgroup has already commented that they agree the referral should not be
auto-forwarded and have asked the reporter to point at the specific
guidance text that suggests otherwise.

## Details

**Description:**

> The referral source needs to have an opportunity to confirm that they
> want to send a referral. There are more than a few reasons that they
> might not want to automatically send it just because there is
> availability. Automating it might reduce admin burden, but if they're
> not specifically selecting a referral target to query then it shouldn't
> be automatic. The provider should send referral after reviewing CBO
> presented.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add explicit narrative to the US SDOH Clinical Care IG workflow pages
that describes referral submission as a provider-initiated, confirm-then-send
step. Concretely, in the IG's referral / CBO interaction workflow
section(s), insert a normative-style sentence such as:

> "Discovery of an available referral target (e.g., a CBO with capacity)
> SHALL NOT be interpreted by an implementing system as an instruction to
> transmit the referral. The referral source SHALL provide the
> originating user (typically the ordering provider) with an explicit
> opportunity to review the candidate referral target(s) and confirm
> submission before any ServiceRequest / Task representing the referral
> is sent to the receiver."

Where the IG presents an end-to-end sequence diagram or step list for
"find target → send referral", split the "send referral" step into two
substeps ("review candidate(s)" and "confirm and submit") so the
distinction is visible in the diagram, not just buried in prose. Where
relevant, also add a short note that this requirement applies regardless
of whether the user explicitly selected a target or the system performed
a broader availability query.

#### Justification

This is a narrative/conformance clarification rather than a model change,
so it is low-risk and matches the workgroup's stated position in the
March 2026 comment ("we agree that the referral source should not be
automatically be forwarded"). Making the requirement explicit in the IG
text closes the gap the reporter perceived and prevents an implementer
from reading silence as permission to auto-send. It is also consistent
with general FHIR workflow practice, where state transitions on Task
(e.g., `requested` → `received`) and the act of POSTing a ServiceRequest
to an external endpoint are user-driven, not implied by a successful
search.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than adding a single global "do not auto-send" sentence, address
the concern structurally by documenting the expected Task / ServiceRequest
state lifecycle for the SDOH referral pattern:

1. Add (or strengthen) guidance that the act of *discovering* a CBO is
   represented separately from the act of *referring* — e.g., the
   discovery step does not create or transmit a `ServiceRequest`/`Task`
   intended for the receiver; only an explicit user action does.
2. Recommend that referral-source systems represent the
   pre-confirmation state as a draft (`ServiceRequest.status = draft`
   and/or `Task.status = draft|requested` held locally) and that the
   transition to `active`/transmission to the receiver requires a
   user-confirmed event.
3. Add a SHOULD-level note that systems offering "auto-route" behaviour
   make it opt-in per user/organization and surface a confirmation UI as
   the default.

This puts the requirement into the same vocabulary the IG already uses
(Task / ServiceRequest states) instead of relying on a free-text
prohibition.

#### Justification

A statuses-and-states framing is more testable and more useful to
implementers than a narrative "don't do this" sentence. It also
accommodates legitimate workflows where a downstream system *does* want
to streamline referral submission for a known, pre-authorised target
(e.g., a closed network with standing agreements) by making the
opt-in/consent surface explicit rather than forbidden. This avoids
inadvertently outlawing reasonable automation patterns the IG may want
to support in future versions while still satisfying the reporter's
underlying concern.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the issue as "Not Persuasive" / "No change required" on the basis
that the IG does not, in any current page, instruct or imply that
referrals should be transmitted automatically upon discovery of an
available CBO. Per the workgroup's March 2026 comment, the existing
guidance already assumes a provider-driven submission step, and the
reporter has not (yet) cited specific narrative that contradicts that
position. Close the ticket with a short response asking the reporter to
re-open with a specific page/section reference if such text is
identified in a future review.

#### Justification

If no IG text actually says "auto-forward", then there is nothing to
change; adding a "do not auto-forward" sentence in response to silence
risks creating a precedent of writing prohibitions for every conceivable
implementer misreading. The workgroup has already done the diligence and
cannot find the offending narrative, which suggests the comment may be
based on an inferred behaviour rather than a documented one. Declining
keeps the IG focused and avoids over-specifying user-experience
requirements that are typically out of scope for a FHIR IG.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The workgroup has already publicly agreed with the substance of the
reporter's concern, so a "Not Persuasive" close (Disposition C) is
likely to land badly even though the workgroup cannot find the
contradicting text — the absence of a clear "do not auto-forward"
statement is itself the gap the reporter is pointing at, and ballot
comments are precisely the venue for closing that kind of clarity gap.
Disposition B is intellectually attractive and would produce a more
durable artifact, but it expands the scope from "answer this comment"
to "design the SDOH referral state model", which is a heavier lift than
a single ballot cycle typically supports and risks slipping past the
publication window. Disposition A delivers exactly what the reporter
asked for, matches the workgroup's stated position verbatim, is cheap to
implement, and leaves room to fold the more structural Task/ServiceRequest
guidance from Disposition B into a follow-up ticket for the next
ballot. It also gives the workgroup a concrete artifact to reference
when responding to the reporter's outstanding question about *where* the
guidance lives.
