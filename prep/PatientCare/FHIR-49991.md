## Summary

The reporter, on behalf of the BR&R Work Group's Pharmaceutical Quality /
Chemistry, Manufacturing & Controls (PQ/CMC) project, requests that the
Patient Care Work Group broaden the Scope and Usage section (§9.3.1) of the
**Procedure** resource so that it explicitly accommodates drug-manufacturing
use cases. Specifically, the project plans to use Procedure to record steps
performed on a *medication* (drug product) or *substance* (drug substance)
during the manufacturing process. The proposed change is purely textual:
add "medication, substance" to the list of subjects on which a procedure may
be performed, and add "manufacturing process" to the list of example
procedure types. No element-level, cardinality, or binding changes are
requested.

No related Jira tickets surfaced in the cross-reference index; the only
cross-reference is to a BR&R Confluence page that hosts general PQ/CMC
project context.

## Details

**Description:**

> For the BR&R WG sponsored PQ/CMC project, we plan to leverage the Procedure
> resource to support the drug product (medication) and drug substance
> (substance) manufacturing process. To support this use case, we request
> that the scope of this resource be broadened. Please see suggested edits to
> the resource scope and usage text below (edits in blue font).
>
> This resource is used to record the details of current and historical
> procedures performed on, with, or for a patient, practitioner, device,
> organization, **medication, substance**, or location. Examples include
> surgical procedures, diagnostic procedures, endoscopic procedures,
> biopsies, counseling, physiotherapy, personal support services, adult day
> care services, non-emergency transportation, home modification, exercise,
> verification of enrollment qualifications for a social program,
> **manufacturing process**, etc. Procedures may be performed by a
> healthcare professional, a service provider, a friend or relative or in
> some cases by the patient themselves.

(Bold text above corresponds to the additions the reporter highlighted in
blue in the original Jira description.)

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the reporter's suggested narrative edits verbatim to the Procedure
resource Scope and Usage section (§9.3.1) in HL7/fhir:

1. Append "medication, substance" to the existing list of subjects in the
   sentence beginning "This resource is used to record the details of
   current and historical procedures performed on, with, or for a patient,
   practitioner, device, organization, … or location."
2. Append "manufacturing process" to the example list ("…verification of
   enrollment qualifications for a social program, manufacturing process,
   etc.").

This is a documentation-only change to the Procedure introduction page; no
element definitions, value-set bindings, search parameters, or invariants
are altered. `Procedure.subject` already permits a `Reference(Any)`-style
broad target set in trial-use, so the supporting structure is in place
without schema changes.

#### Justification

The change is narrative, additive, and non-breaking. PQ/CMC is an active
HL7 cross-WG project with documented FDA submission relevance, and the
Procedure resource is in fact the closest fit for stepwise manufacturing
activity that has actors, timing, outcomes, and reason codes. Explicitly
naming "medication" and "substance" as valid subjects, and "manufacturing
process" as a valid example, removes ambiguity for implementers building
PQ/CMC profiles and signals Patient Care WG concurrence with the cross-WG
reuse. There is no implementer cost, and the existing text already lists
non-clinical examples (transportation, social program enrollment), so a
manufacturing example does not change the resource's character.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the *intent* but tighten the wording so the Procedure scope text
remains anchored in healthcare/regulated-product activity rather than
generic industrial process. Concretely:

1. Add "medicinal product or substance" (or "medicinal product, substance")
   instead of the bare "medication, substance" — using the
   pharmaceutical-domain phrasing that aligns with the
   `MedicinalProductDefinition` / `SubstanceDefinition` family already used
   by the BR&R resources.
2. Replace the bare "manufacturing process" example with a more specific
   phrase such as "pharmaceutical manufacturing or compounding step" so the
   example remains within the regulated-healthcare-product domain.
3. Add a one-sentence cross-reference in the Scope and Usage section
   pointing implementers to BR&R's PQ/CMC IG for profiling guidance, so
   that core spec readers understand this is an explicitly profiled use
   case rather than an invitation to model arbitrary industrial workflows
   on Procedure.

#### Justification

The literal edit risks scope creep: "manufacturing process" without a
qualifier could be read as endorsing Procedure for any industrial
manufacturing (food, electronics, etc.), which is well outside FHIR's
healthcare scope. Using "medicinal product or substance" and
"pharmaceutical manufacturing or compounding step" preserves the
implementer-facing benefit for PQ/CMC while keeping Procedure visibly
healthcare-scoped, and it harmonizes with terminology used by the
BR&R-owned resources (`MedicinalProductDefinition`,
`SubstanceDefinition`, `ManufacturedItemDefinition`). A pointer to the
PQ/CMC IG also makes it clear where the constrained, machine-checkable
profile guidance lives, reducing the risk of implementers stretching
core Procedure beyond what BR&R intends.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Do not adopt the requested narrative change to the Procedure Scope and
Usage section. Instead, respond that the BR&R PQ/CMC project should model
manufacturing activity using the BR&R-owned regulated-product resources —
notably `ManufacturedItemDefinition`, `MedicinalProductDefinition`, and
the related substance/ingredient resources — and capture stepwise
manufacturing semantics either via a dedicated profile of an existing
non-Patient-Care resource or via a project-scoped extension, rather than
by broadening a Patient Care-owned clinical resource's scope statement.

#### Justification

Procedure historically describes activities performed on or for a person
(or, by extension, the device/organization/location supporting a person's
care). Even though the resource technically allows a wide subject, the
Patient Care WG's stewardship and the surrounding clinical examples
(surgery, biopsy, counseling, physiotherapy) frame implementer
expectations. Explicitly endorsing "manufacturing process" in the Patient
Care-owned narrative invites mis-reuse and confuses the resource's
clinical center of gravity. Because BR&R already owns a regulated-product
resource family designed for manufacturing-domain modeling, declining the
narrative change and redirecting to those resources keeps each WG's
stewardship boundaries clean and avoids long-term drift in Procedure's
scope.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The underlying request is reasonable — PQ/CMC is a real, sponsored cross-WG
project, Procedure is a defensible fit for stepwise manufacturing activity,
and the change is narrative-only with no schema impact, so an outright
decline (C) would unnecessarily friction a sister WG's deliverable.
However, the literal edits in Disposition A are unacceptably broad: "manufacturing
process" without a healthcare qualifier opens the door to using Procedure
for any industrial workflow, and "medication, substance" doesn't match the
phrasing the BR&R resources actually use. Disposition B captures the
implementer-facing benefit Smita is asking for (explicit permission to use
Procedure for drug-product / drug-substance manufacturing steps) while
keeping the Scope and Usage text demonstrably healthcare-scoped and
pointing readers to the PQ/CMC IG for the constrained guidance. Patient
Care should accept the change in principle and negotiate the final wording
with BR&R before applying it to the Procedure introduction page.
