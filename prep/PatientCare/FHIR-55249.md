## Summary

David Hill observes that several workflows currently scoped to the US SDOH
Clinical Care IG — specifically Capacity Status, Enrollment Status,
Self-Referral, Referral for Further Assessment, and the broader Referral
Workflow — appear to have value beyond SDOH/CBO use cases. He suggests the
Patient Care WG consider promoting these workflows for reuse across
healthcare more generally (e.g., post-acute care), potentially via a
separate Referral IG. The request is exploratory and asks the WG to
investigate cross-domain applicability rather than propose specific
profile or resource changes. Related ticket
[FHIR-54021](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54021) (Interoperability
Gap Between SDOH Clinical Care IG and FAST NDH HealthcareService
Profiles) gives concrete shape to the harmonization theme by calling out
the SDOHCC-HealthcareServiceForReferralManagement profile and asking it
be coordinated with — or derived from — the FAST National Directory
HealthcareService profile. Both tickets resolved as
"Considered for Future Use" with the Gravity Project committing to
ongoing harmonization work with PA, FAST NDH, FM (Coverage), and the
O&O Clinical Orders Workflow IG beyond the STU3 timeframe.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the Gravity Project's stated disposition: **Persuasive with
Modification — Considered for Future Use**. Acknowledge in the US SDOH
Clinical Care IG that the Capacity Status, Enrollment Status,
Self-Referral, Referral for Further Assessment, and Referral Workflow
patterns are intentionally generalizable, and add a forward-looking
note that Gravity is collaborating with PA + FAST NDH on capacity
status, with FM on enrollment status / Coverage alignment, and with
O&O's Clinical Orders Workflow IG on referral workflows. Defer the
actual extraction of these workflows into a separate "Referral IG" (or
into a more generic Patient Care / Workflow IG) to a post-STU3 work
item, tracked by FHIR-55249 as the parent and FHIR-54021 (NDH
HealthcareService harmonization) as the first concrete deliverable.

#### Justification

This matches exactly what the reporter asked for ("look into this
further... perhaps in a separate Referral IG") without committing the
WG to a disruptive in-cycle restructuring. The resolution text already
captures concrete cross-WG collaborations (PA, FM, O&O, FAST), and the
companion ticket FHIR-54021 — also resolved Considered for Future Use
— provides the first scoped piece of work (HealthcareService
harmonization with NDH). Treating FHIR-55249 as a tracking ticket for
the broader generalization theme keeps the future-use commitment
visible and traceable.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than carrying FHIR-55249 forward as an open-ended "future use"
tracking ticket, **decompose it now into specific, scoped follow-up
tickets** — one per workflow that has a real cross-domain consumer:

1. Capacity Status — coordinate with PA and FAST NDH (link to
   FHIR-54021).
2. Enrollment Status — coordinate with FM, evaluate Coverage as the
   underlying resource.
3. Self-Referral / Referral for Further Assessment / Referral Workflow
   — coordinate with O&O Clinical Orders Workflow IG.

Resolve FHIR-55249 itself as **Persuasive with Modification — split
into the above tickets** rather than leaving it as a single broad
"Considered for Future Use" item. Each new ticket would be filed
against the appropriate sponsoring WG's IG/specification with explicit
cross-references back to FHIR-55249 and to FHIR-54021 where relevant.

#### Justification

A single "Considered for Future Use" disposition on a wide-scope
ticket tends to lose traction once the ballot cycle closes. Splitting
the request into per-workflow follow-ups (a) gives each workflow a
concrete owning WG and accountable engagement, (b) makes it easier to
prioritize and resource the work post-STU3, and (c) aligns the
harmonization commitments listed in the resolution text with actual
JIRA tracking. It still preserves the reporter's intent — generalize
these workflows — but trades one deferred ticket for several
actionable ones. Trade-off: more WG bookkeeping up front, and risk of
filing tickets against IGs/WGs that aren't yet ready to accept the
work.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve FHIR-55249 as **Not Persuasive — out of scope for STU3**, on
the grounds that the request is exploratory ("I need to look into this
further"), proposes no concrete change to any artifact in the US SDOH
Clinical Care IG, and the cross-WG harmonization work it implies is
already happening informally (Gravity ↔ PA / FAST NDH / FM / O&O) and
is captured by more concrete tickets such as
[FHIR-54021](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54021).

#### Justification

A defensible decline rests on three points: (1) no change to a
specification artifact is being requested, so there is nothing to
"adopt" in STU3; (2) the underlying need — generalization beyond SDOH
— is already addressable via existing cross-WG collaboration channels
and via concretely-scoped tickets like FHIR-54021; and (3) "Considered
for Future Use" risks becoming a permanent parking lot. The downside
is that declining loses the explicit, IG-visible signal that the
Gravity team is committed to this generalization work.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The Gravity Project has already proposed FHIR-55249 as **Persuasive
with Modification** in the 2026-02-18 block vote announcements to both
the Patient Care WG and Public Health stream, and the resolution text
already enumerates concrete partner WGs (PA, FM, O&O) and concrete
companion work (FAST NDH harmonization, Coverage alignment, Clinical
Orders Workflow IG alignment). Disposition A simply ratifies that
position: it preserves the reporter's intent, keeps the
generalization theme visible in the IG and in JIRA, and lets
FHIR-54021 act as the first concrete deliverable. Disposition B is
attractive operationally and would be a strong follow-on if the WG
wants tighter tracking, but it adds churn for a STU3 cycle that is
about to close. Disposition C understates the cross-WG momentum that
is already underway and is hard to defend given the supporting
evidence the Gravity team has already linked (COW Referrals
Harmonization page, FAST NDH meeting minutes).
