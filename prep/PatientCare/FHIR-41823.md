## Summary

The reporter notes that the International Birth And Child Model (FHIR) IG
currently places the sentence *"This project is intended to solely address
issues on the informational aspect of data handling in the normal process
flow of day to day pregnancy and birth healthcare"* in the **Out of scope**
section of the IG's scope description. They observe that this sentence
actually describes what the project *does* cover (the informational aspect
of normal pregnancy and birth data handling) rather than what it excludes,
and therefore recommend relocating it into the **In scope** section. This
is a narrow, editorial / technical-correction request against the IG's
scope narrative — no resource or profile changes are implied. No
cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were found
for this ticket.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the editorial change exactly as the reporter describes: in the
International Birth And Child Model (FHIR) IG's scope narrative, **remove**
the sentence *"This project is intended to solely address issues on the
informational aspect of data handling in the normal process flow of day to
day pregnancy and birth healthcare"* from the **Out of scope** subsection
and **add it verbatim to the In scope subsection** (preferably as the
opening or summary sentence of "In scope", since it characterises the
project's overall focus). No model, profile, terminology, or example
changes are required. Because no GitHub cross-references resolved for this
ticket, the precise authoring file (the IG's scope page in the IBCM IG
publication source) must be confirmed by the WG editor before the change
is committed; the change itself is a one-paragraph move with no downstream
artifact regeneration impact.

#### Justification

The sentence is grammatically and semantically a positive description of
what the project covers ("intended to solely address issues on the
informational aspect of data handling … of day to day pregnancy and birth
healthcare"), not an exclusion. Leaving it under "Out of scope" creates a
direct contradiction with the surrounding narrative and is likely to
confuse implementers reading the IG's scope to decide whether their use
case is supported. The fix is a pure editorial / technical correction with
no normative or compatibility risk, which aligns with the ticket's
declared type (*Technical Correction*) and is exactly the kind of change
this issue type exists to handle.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than moving the sentence verbatim, **rewrite the scope section** so
that "In scope" and "Out of scope" each contain a short, parallel-structured
list of bullets, and fold the sentence's intent into the In-scope bullets.
Concretely:

- In **In scope**, add a bullet such as: *"Informational aspects of data
  handling in the normal process flow of day-to-day pregnancy and birth
  healthcare."*
- In **Out of scope**, replace the misplaced sentence with explicit
  exclusions that genuinely belong there (e.g., *"Workflow orchestration,
  decision support logic, billing/financial data, and abnormal/critical
  obstetric pathways are out of scope for this release."*) — pulling the
  exclusions from elsewhere in the IG's introduction or from prior WG
  minutes if available.

#### Justification

The reporter's proposed move corrects the immediate error but leaves the
underlying weakness intact: the scope section appears to be free prose
that mixes inclusions and exclusions, which is what allowed the sentence
to be miscategorised in the first place. Restructuring into parallel
bullet lists improves readability for implementers and makes future scope
edits less error-prone. The trade-off vs. Disposition A is editorial
effort and the risk of introducing scope language that has not yet been
agreed by the WG; that risk is real but bounded because the change is
still confined to narrative.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change on the grounds that it is a low-impact wording
preference whose interpretation depends on broader IG scope decisions
that the WG has not yet revisited, and resolve the ticket as **Not
Persuasive** (or **Persuasive with Modification → defer to next IG cycle**
if the WG prefers to keep the issue open without acting on it now).

#### Justification

A literal "move this sentence" change presumes the sentence is correctly
phrased and that the WG agrees the project's scope *is* purely
informational and confined to normal-flow pregnancy/birth data. If the WG
intends to broaden scope (e.g., to include abnormal pathways, decision
support, or care-plan workflow) in a future cycle, accepting the move now
would entrench a scope statement that may need to be revised again
shortly. Declining (or deferring) avoids churn in the IG's narrative
until scope is reaffirmed.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is explicitly tagged *Technical Correction*, and the request is
exactly that — the sentence as written describes what is *in* scope, so
its placement under *Out of scope* is a clear authoring error rather than
a substantive scope question. Disposition A fixes the contradiction with
no normative impact, no implementer-visible artifact change, and no risk
to downstream profiles or examples; it is also the cheapest action and
the most faithful to what the reporter asked for. Disposition B has merit
as a follow-up clean-up of the scope section's structure, but bundling it
into this ticket would expand the change beyond a technical correction
and require WG agreement on new scope language, so it is better filed as
a separate ticket if the WG wants to pursue it. Disposition C would leave
a self-contradictory scope statement in the published IG, which is hard
to defend for a *Highest*-priority technical correction with no opposing
viewpoint on record.
