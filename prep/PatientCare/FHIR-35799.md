## Summary

Anne Belford (reporter) raises an interoperability/guidance gap in the
International Patient Summary (IPS) IG: EMRs can reliably partition past
health events into `Procedure` versus `Condition` resources only when the
source record carries a code that classifies it as one or the other. When
the EMR stores a free-text past health event without any classifying code,
the system has no deterministic way to decide whether to emit it as a
`Condition` (Past Illness) or a `Procedure` (History of Procedures), even
though the IPS sections expect those two distinct resource types. The
reporter asks IPS to publish explicit guidance for this case and proposes
that non-codified past health events default to `Condition`.

The ticket has no linked or cross-referenced sibling Jira issues that
substantively expand its scope. It was bundled into the IPS "block-vote-2"
batch at the September 2022 Patient Care WGM (see the Zulip thread below),
voted Considered for Future Use 14-0-16, and then explicitly reopened by
Lynn Laakso post-STU-update for re-investigation in a future publication
cycle. It currently sits in **Triaged** with no assignee.

## Details

**Description:**

> EMRs are able to distinguish between procedure codes and condition codes
> where codification exists. The issue is where there is no coding, as
> EMRs; in this case, EMRs cannot determine whether the text refers to a
> Past Illness (Conditions) and History of Procedures (Procedures) as they
> are stored together within the same category in the EMR.
>
> Guidance is required. Our recommendation is that non-codified past health
> events be recorded as Conditions.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the reporter's recommendation in the IPS IG: when an EMR holds a
past health event that has no code that classifies it as either a
procedure or a problem/diagnosis, the IPS producer SHOULD represent it as
a `Condition` resource (with `category` = `problem-list-item` or
`encounter-diagnosis` per existing IPS profiling and `clinicalStatus` /
`verificationStatus` populated as best the source allows, e.g.
`unknown` / `unconfirmed`), placed under the **Past History of Illness**
section. Add a normative paragraph to the IPS IG narrative for both the
Past History of Illness and History of Procedures sections stating this
default and explaining the rationale (loss-less round-tripping of a
free-text legacy entry without inventing a procedure event). Reflect the
guidance in the section composition guidance and in the IPS authoring
notes for `Condition (IPS)` and `Procedure (IPS)`.

#### Justification

The IPS sections for past illness and history of procedures are designed
to be filled by EMRs whose source data is frequently free text. Today the
IG silently leaves ingestion behavior implementation-defined, so two
conformant IPS producers can reasonably emit the same source string into
different sections — directly undermining cross-border consumability,
which is the IPS's central goal. A `Condition` default is the safer of
the two choices: a procedure asserts that an act *occurred*, with
implicit attribution and clinical meaning, whereas a `Condition` with
`verificationStatus` = `unconfirmed` (or similar) is honest about
provenance and easy for a consuming clinician to reclassify. The reporter
also notes that this matches how many EMRs already store "Past Medical
History" — as a generic problem-flavored bucket. Accepting this guidance
is also low-risk because it is purely additive narrative; it does not
change cardinalities, value sets, or invariants on either profile.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than mandating `Condition` for all non-codified past entries,
introduce explicit guidance that the producer SHOULD preserve the entry
in the **Past History of Illness** narrative section as a `Condition`
**unless the source field is semantically labelled "procedure"** in the
EMR (e.g., the EMR's own UI category is "Past Procedures"), in which
case it SHOULD be emitted as a `Procedure` with `status` =
`completed` and `code.text` carrying the original string. Pair this with
a small set of normative SHALL/SHOULD statements:

- IPS producer SHALL emit the original free text in `Condition.code.text`
  (or `Procedure.code.text`) so no information is lost.
- IPS producer SHALL set `verificationStatus` = `unconfirmed` (or
  equivalent) on `Condition` instances created this way.
- IPS producer SHOULD include a `meta.tag` or extension flagging the
  resource as derived from non-codified source text, so consumers can
  treat it accordingly.

Document this in the section composition guidance for both Past History
of Illness and History of Procedures and add a worked example in the IG.

#### Justification

Disposition A's blanket "default to Condition" rule loses information in
the (common) case where the EMR *does* know the entry is a procedure but
just doesn't have a SNOMED/CPT/ICD code for it. EMRs almost universally
separate "Past Medical History" from "Past Surgical History" at the UI /
field level; using that field-level provenance is a stronger signal than
the absence of a code, and it lets producers preserve the procedure-vs-
illness distinction wherever the source actually has it. The
`verificationStatus` / provenance-tag pieces address the deeper issue
the reporter raised — that consumers currently can't tell which entries
are coded-and-confirmed versus inferred-from-free-text — which is more
valuable than picking a single default resource type. Trade-off vs. A:
slightly more implementation work for IPS producers, but materially
better semantics for IPS consumers.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as Not Persuasive and document in the IG that the choice between
`Condition` and `Procedure` for non-codified past health entries is an
EMR/source-system mapping decision outside the scope of the IPS
specification. Point implementers to general FHIR `Condition` and
`Procedure` modeling guidance and to local national IPS profiles for any
jurisdiction-specific rules.

#### Justification

A defensible reading of IPS scope is that the IG defines *how* a Past
History of Illness or History of Procedures entry is structured once the
producer has decided what it is, not *how* the producer should classify
ambiguous source data. Encoding a global default risks codifying a
clinically wrong interpretation in jurisdictions whose EMRs already use a
different convention, and it pushes a content-mapping rule into a content
profile. The Sept 2022 block vote already landed on Considered for Future
Use (14-0-16) for closely related procedural reasons, suggesting limited
WG appetite for taking this on as a normative spec change.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B preserves the spirit of the reporter's request — IPS
producers need a deterministic rule when the source code is missing —
while avoiding Disposition A's information loss in the common case where
the EMR's UI/field already tells you whether the entry is a procedure
or a problem. The `verificationStatus = unconfirmed` plus
provenance-tag pieces directly address what is actually breaking
interoperability today: not the resource-type choice in isolation, but
the consumer's inability to distinguish coded/confirmed entries from
free-text-derived ones. Disposition C is hard to justify given that the
ticket has been formally reopened post-STU and Lynn Laakso explicitly
flagged it as a topic to investigate after the next publication update;
declining now would re-tread the 2022 block-vote outcome that was
deliberately reverted. The workgroup should treat B as the starting
point and refine the exact wording of the field-provenance heuristic and
the `meta.tag`/extension mechanism with the IPS authors.
