## Summary

The reporter asks the Patient Care work group to clarify what is meant by
the phrase **"the normal process flow"** as used in the scope statement of
the International Birth And Child Model (FHIR) specification. The
sentence in question reads: *"This project is intended to solely address
issues on the informational aspect of data handling in the normal process
flow of day to day pregnancy and birth healthcare."* The reporter finds
the term ambiguous and recommends the project provide a clearer
definition or rewording so readers understand the intended scope of the
model. No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items
were found for this ticket, so the request stands on its own as an
editorial clarification of scope language.

## Details

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Edit the scope/introduction text of the International Birth And Child
Model (FHIR) so that "the normal process flow" is replaced (or
immediately followed) by an explicit definition. Concretely, reword the
sentence to something like:

> "This project is intended to solely address the informational
> (data-exchange) aspects of routine, expected day-to-day care during
> pregnancy and birth — i.e., the standard antenatal, intrapartum, and
> immediate postnatal/newborn care pathway as practiced in
> uncomplicated pregnancies — and does **not** address exception
> handling, escalation, or specialised obstetric or neonatal care
> pathways."

Where the IG also has a glossary or "Scope" page, add a one-line
definition of *normal process flow* that matches the rewording. If the
ticket's `Related Pages` field is later populated, target the page(s)
named there; otherwise target the IG's `index` / `scope` narrative page.

#### Justification

The reporter raises a real ambiguity: "normal process flow" is not a
defined term in FHIR, in the IBCM, or in mainstream perinatal-care
literature, so different readers will read it in different ways
(workflow vs. clinical pathway vs. uncomplicated pregnancy). Replacing
it with concrete, observable scope language ("routine antenatal,
intrapartum and immediate postnatal care") is consistent with FHIR's
preference for unambiguous, implementer-facing scope statements and
costs nothing in terms of conformance — it is purely editorial, which
matches the ticket's `Technical Correction` type.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than rewording in place, **add a glossary / definitions section**
to the IBCM (or extend an existing one) that defines "normal process
flow" as a term-of-art for this IG, and link the sentence in the scope
statement to that definition. The scope sentence itself is left intact;
the reader clicks through to the glossary entry. The glossary entry
would describe the in-scope care pathway (routine antenatal,
intrapartum, immediate postnatal/newborn) and explicitly call out
what is *out* of scope (high-risk obstetrics, NICU pathways, fertility
treatment, etc.).

#### Justification

The phrase appears (per the reporter) in a scope/introduction sentence,
which is often referenced verbatim in project charters, ballot intros,
and external slides. A glossary-based clarification preserves any
existing citations of the sentence while still removing ambiguity for
new readers, and it gives the work group a single place to refine the
definition over time without re-editing scope prose every cycle. The
trade-off vs. Disposition A is an extra click for the reader and
slightly more IG scaffolding, but it is friendlier to downstream reuse
of the scope statement.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Not Persuasive / No Change** on the grounds that
"the normal process flow of day to day pregnancy and birth healthcare"
is, taken in context, sufficiently self-explanatory: the qualifiers
*"day to day"* and *"pregnancy and birth healthcare"* already signal
that the IG is scoped to routine, non-exception perinatal care.

#### Justification

The sentence is in a scope/introduction paragraph, not in a normative
conformance statement, so the looseness does not affect implementation.
No downstream tickets, Zulip discussions, or GitHub issues have been
raised against this wording, which suggests the ambiguity is not
actively confusing implementers. Editorial churn on scope prose risks
invalidating prior project-approval citations of the sentence with no
implementer-visible benefit.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is a `Technical Correction` of the highest priority and is
explicitly editorial. The reporter is correct that "normal process
flow" is not a defined term, and the cost of fixing it in place is
trivial — a single sentence rewrite plus, ideally, a one-line glossary
entry. Disposition A delivers the clarification the reporter asked for
in the location they flagged, without introducing extra IG
infrastructure (Disposition B) and without dismissing a fair editorial
concern that no one has yet pushed back on (Disposition C). If the
work group wants the durability benefits of B, the cleanest path is to
*do A and also* add the glossary entry — which is fully compatible
with this recommendation.
