## Summary

This is an International Patient Summary (IPS) ballot comment (originally
"IPS #54") submitted by Martin Yadrick. The submitter affirms the IPS
ballot but asks that two related concerns be addressed: (1) call out
patient-safety considerations relating to dysphagia and enteral feeding,
and (2) add **Nutrition** as an optional section of the IPS composition.
The ticket was originally dispositioned in 2020-02-04 as
"Considered for Future Use" (Hausam/Zautke 12-0-1) and then re-opened
post-STU update publication in 2022 to be re-evaluated, with a note to
share the comment with the ISO 27269 (IPS Dataset) team. No related Jira
tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were surfaced by the augury
cross-reference index, so the ticket appears to stand on its own in the
current corpus.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a new **optional** `Nutrition` section to the IPS Composition
(`Composition-uv-ips` / `IPS Composition` profile in `HL7/fhir-ips`).
Concretely:

- Define a new `Composition.section` slice `section:sectionNutrition` in
  the IPS Composition profile, with LOINC code (e.g., `61144-2`
  "Diet and nutrition Narrative") and `section.entry` references to
  `NutritionOrder`, `NutritionIntake` (or `Observation` for nutrition
  status), and food-type `AllergyIntolerance`, plus narrative.
- Mark the new slice as **optional** (cardinality `0..1`) so the change
  is non-breaking for current IPS implementers.
- In the existing IPS Composition narrative guidance and the relevant
  section pages, add explicit patient-safety guidance that (a) dysphagia
  and aspiration-risk findings should be communicated in the
  Functional Status / Problems / Nutrition sections and (b) active
  enteral-feeding regimens belong in the Nutrition section (and/or
  Medical Devices when a feeding tube is in situ), so they are visible
  at point of unscheduled care.
- Liaise the change with the ISO 27269 (IPS Dataset) team as Lynn's
  comment requests, so the FHIR IPS and the ISO dataset stay aligned.

#### Justification

The request is well-scoped: an optional section is additive and does not
break existing IPS implementations or summaries. Dysphagia and enteral
feeding are well-known patient-safety hazards in transitions of care
(aspiration, drug–nutrient interactions, accidental oral feeding of NPO
patients), and there is no current dedicated home in IPS for that
information — making it discoverable at the section level is consistent
with how IPS already singles out other safety-critical content
(Allergies, Medications). Adding the section as `0..1` follows the IPS
pattern for non-mandatory sections (e.g., Functional Status, Social
History, Plan of Care) and Lynn's 2022 reversal explicitly preserves the
intent to revisit. The ISO 27269 liaison is already part of the
disposition note.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Do **not** add a new top-level Nutrition section in this cycle. Instead:

- Add **non-normative patient-safety guidance** to the existing IPS
  Composition narrative covering dysphagia, aspiration risk, and active
  enteral feeding, pointing implementers at the existing sections that
  should carry the information today: Allergies and Intolerances
  (food/texture intolerances), Medical Devices (feeding tubes),
  Functional Status (swallowing impairment), and the Plan of Care.
- Define a **NutritionSection extension** (or a documented use of
  `Composition.section` as an open extension point) that implementers
  who need a Nutrition section can use immediately, without changing the
  base IPS Composition cardinalities.
- File a follow-up tracker (or attach to the next IPS ballot cycle) to
  promote the extension to a formal optional section once there is
  implementation experience and once ISO 27269 alignment is confirmed.

#### Justification

This addresses the underlying safety concern immediately (the dysphagia
/ enteral-feeding guidance) without committing the IPS to a new section
slice before ISO 27269 alignment is secured. It also lowers the
ballot-impact and connectathon-burden of the change in the current
cycle, while still giving implementers a sanctioned place to put
nutrition data via an extension. The trade-off vs. Disposition A is
discoverability: a guidance paragraph and an extension are easier to
miss than a first-class section.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve as **Persuasive with Modification — covered by existing
sections**, declining the addition of a Nutrition section. Justify that
dysphagia, aspiration risk, and enteral-feeding information are already
representable in IPS via:

- `AllergyIntolerance` (food / texture) in the Allergies section,
- `DeviceUseStatement` / `Device` (feeding tubes) in the Medical Devices
  section,
- Swallowing-impairment observations in the Functional Status section,
- `MedicationStatement` for enteral formulas administered as
  medications, and
- the Plan of Care section for active dietary orders.

Add a one-line clarification to the IPS guidance pointing implementers
at those sections, and close the ticket.

#### Justification

A defensible case can be made that the IPS is intentionally minimal and
that every concern in the comment can already be expressed in the
current STU. Adding sections expands the surface area of the summary,
increases divergence risk with ISO 27269, and may pressure other
jurisdictions (e.g., national IPS profiles) to follow suit. Declining
keeps the IPS lean and focused while still giving the submitter an
actionable answer.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the strongest starting point for the workgroup. The
submitter's safety concern (dysphagia / enteral feeding) is real and
should be addressed in writing now, but committing the base IPS
Composition to a new optional Nutrition section before the ISO 27269
team has weighed in — which Lynn explicitly flagged as a follow-up in
the 2022 re-open — risks creating divergence between the FHIR IPS and
the ISO IPS Dataset that the comment was originally trying to avoid.
Shipping the patient-safety guidance plus a NutritionSection extension
in this cycle, and tracking promotion to a formal section in the next
ballot once ISO alignment is confirmed, gives implementers something
usable today, keeps the door open for Disposition A in the next cycle,
and avoids the "decline and forget" failure mode of Disposition C. If
the ISO 27269 liaison comes back quickly with concurrence, the workgroup
can upgrade to Disposition A in-cycle.
