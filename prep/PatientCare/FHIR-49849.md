## Summary

The reporter flags a recurring typo in the titles of several ValueSet
artifacts in the US Public Health Profiles Library: the word "ValueSet" is
spelled "VaueSet" (missing the letter "l"). The typo appears in at least six
distinct ValueSet titles, suggesting it was either copy-pasted from a
template or auto-generated by tooling that has the misspelling baked in.
This is a documentation / metadata defect rather than a structural change to
the ValueSets' content — the underlying codes, expansion, and binding
behaviour are not in question. No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip
threads, or GitHub items were surfaced for this ticket.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Persuasive — fix each affected ValueSet title in the US Public Health
Profiles Library so that "VaueSet" reads "ValueSet". Specifically, update
the `ValueSet.title` element (and `ValueSet.name` if it carries the same
typo) on at least the following artifacts:

- US Public Health VaueSet - Supplemental Example → "US Public Health
  ValueSet - Supplemental Example"
- US Public Health VaueSet - Triggering Chlamydia Example → "… ValueSet -
  Triggering Chlamydia Example"
- US Public Health VaueSet - Message Types → "… ValueSet - Message Types"
- US Public Health VaueSet - PlanDefinition Action → "… ValueSet -
  PlanDefinition Action"
- US Public Health VaueSet - Pregnancy Status → "… ValueSet - Pregnancy
  Status"
- US Public Health VaueSet - TriggerDefinition NamedEvent → "… ValueSet -
  TriggerDefinition NamedEvent"

In addition, sweep the IG sources for any other occurrences of the literal
string "VaueSet" (titles, narrative, intro/notes pages, generated tables,
xrefs) and correct them in the same change. Canonical URLs and ValueSet
identifiers/ids should NOT be changed — only human-readable title (and
`name` if affected) — to avoid breaking existing references and
implementations.

#### Justification

The defect is a clear, low-risk spelling error in display metadata. Title
changes do not affect binding semantics, code system content, or canonical
identifiers, so there is no implementer impact beyond improved readability.
The correction makes the IG consistent with the rest of HL7 nomenclature
("ValueSet" is the standard FHIR resource name) and removes a small but
persistent embarrassment in published artifacts. Since the typo appears in
at least six artifacts, a targeted sweep is the right grain of fix.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Persuasive with mod — accept the fix, but expand the scope and harden the
process. In addition to the title corrections in Disposition A:

1. Audit `ValueSet.name`, `ValueSet.title`, `description`, narrative
   `text.div`, and any IG narrative pages / intros that quote the titles,
   and correct every occurrence of "VaueSet" (and any near-miss variants
   such as "Vauseset"/"Valueet").
2. Verify whether the typo originated in a generation template, a
   spreadsheet, or a tooling artifact (e.g., a FSH alias, a publisher
   resource template, or a copy-paste from another IG); fix it at the
   source so future regenerations don't re-introduce it.
3. Add a simple lint check (e.g., a CI grep / IG publisher QA rule) for the
   literal "VaueSet" so the regression cannot recur unnoticed.

#### Justification

The reported list ("including the following") strongly implies the typo may
exist in additional artifacts, narrative, and tooling beyond the six named.
Fixing only the listed titles risks leaving the typo elsewhere and
re-introducing it on the next IG build if the upstream template still
contains it. A small lint rule is cheap insurance against the same defect
re-appearing in future US Public Health releases. The trade-off versus
Disposition A is a slightly larger PR and a one-time CI tweak — modest cost
for a durable fix.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Not Persuasive — decline the change on the grounds that the request is out
of scope for Patient Care and should be redirected to the work group that
owns the US Public Health Profiles Library (Public Health WG), or close as
a duplicate if the typo has already been corrected in a newer build of the
IG.

#### Justification

This is the weakest disposition. The defect is real, the fix is trivial,
and the artifacts are clearly named in the ticket. Declining only makes
sense if (a) Patient Care is genuinely not the owning work group for these
artifacts and the ticket needs reassignment to Public Health WG, or (b) the
typo has already been silently fixed in the published IG and this ticket is
stale. Neither has been confirmed from the available data, but the ticket
is currently filed under Patient Care, which raises a routing question
worth at least flagging during triage.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The defect is a straightforward typo and should obviously be fixed
(Disposition A captures that), but the reporter's wording — "including the
following" — and the fact that the same misspelling appears across six
distinct artifacts indicate a templating or copy-paste origin rather than
six independent slips. Disposition B addresses both the surface defect and
the likely root cause, and the additional cost (a wider grep and a tiny CI
rule) is trivial compared to the risk of the typo reappearing on the next
publication. Patient Care should also confirm during triage that the US
Public Health Profiles Library is the right home for this fix versus
forwarding to Public Health WG; if ownership is wrong, reassign rather than
close.
