## Summary

The reporter (Kevin O'Donnell, working in an IHE-IDR context) observes that
`Condition`'s current definitions for `asserter` and `recorder` are
asymmetric: the `recorder` text explicitly carries a "responsibility for
content" clause, while the `asserter` text does not, which implicitly
suggests the recorder is the clinically responsible party. Using the
canonical example of a radiologist (asserter) and a transcriptionist
(recorder), the reporter argues this is misleading. They propose either
dropping the responsibility language entirely or, preferably, making the
two definitions symmetric so that the **asserter** carries clinical
accuracy responsibility and the **recorder** carries only the
responsibility for accurately capturing the statement in the record.

Concretely, the reporter proposes:

- **Condition.asserter** — Definition unchanged ("Individual or device that
  is making the condition statement"). New comment:
  *"The asserter is responsible for the clinical accuracy of the condition
  statement."*
- **Condition.recorder** — Definition reworded to *"Individual or device
  that captured the condition statement in the medical record"*. Comment
  reworded so the recorder is responsible *for accurately recording*
  information (not for content), and the line about `recordedDate` is
  dropped.

This ticket is closely related to and partially overlapped by
[FHIR-48804](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48804) — *"Confusion persists
around Condition & Allergy asserter and recorder"* (Jay Lyle / Patient
Care). FHIR-48804 was **Applied** with resolution *"Not Persuasive with
Modification"* on the 2025-11-06 PC call: the WG **only** updated
`Condition.recorder`'s definition to *"Individual who recorded the record
and takes responsibility **for accurately recording** its content"*,
explicitly leaving `Condition.asserter` and the `Condition.recorder`
comment alone. That partial fix landed via
[HL7/fhir PR #3961, commit 3421106](https://github.com/HL7/fhir/pull/3961/commits/3421106).
FHIR-49653 is still **Triaged** and effectively asks for the *additional*
asserter-side and comment-side clarifications that FHIR-48804's resolution
did not adopt. The ticket's J# committee-notes listing on
`Condition.recorder` in `structuredefinition-Condition.xml` already
includes `J#48804` but **does not yet include `J#49653`**.

A standalone Zulip thread on `chat.fhir.org` (`#implementers >
Condition.asserter vs Condition.recorder`, April 2024) shows external
implementers asking the same question (Patrick Dürke from a Danish
healthcare context: nurse registering a diagnosis on behalf of a doctor),
which corroborates that real-world confusion is the driving motivation.

## Details

**Description:**

> The current text makes a point of explicitly highlighting the
> responsibility of the recorder for the content but glosses over any
> responsibility of the asserter for the content (sort of implying that
> the primary responsibility is with the recorder). I'm imagining the
> case of, say, a radiologist as asserter and transcriptionist as
> recorder.
>
> We could drop the responsibility text (since we don't often do that)
> or modify to distinguish the responsibilities and be symmetric.
>
> **Proposal:**
>
> *Condition.asserter*
> Definition: Individual or device that is making the condition
> statement.
> Comments: **The asserter is responsible for the clinical accuracy of
> the condition statement.**
>
> *Condition.recorder*
> Definition: Individual **or device that captured the condition
> statement in the medical record** (~~who recorded the record and takes
> responsibility for its content~~).
> Comments: The recorder **is** responsible for accurately recording
> information in the record. The recorder is the most recent author. The
> recorder might or might not be the asserter. (~~By contrast, the
> recordedDate is when the condition was first recorded.~~)

**Comments:**

- **JIRAUSER28753 (2025-01-24, reporter follow-up):** Notes there are two
  patterns at play. In `Condition`, the **clinician is typically the
  asserter**, while in `AllergyIntolerance` the **patient may be the
  asserter** and the clinician/staff is the recorder. The clinician is
  typically clinically responsible in both — implying any wording change
  must work across both resources.
- **michelle.m.miller (2025-03-06):** Cross-links the ticket to
  [FHIR-48804](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48804)
  ("Confusion persists around Condition & Allergy asserter and
  recorder").

## Related Zulip Discussions

### [#implementers > Condition.asserter vs Condition.recorder](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/implementers/topic/Condition.asserter%20vs%20Condition.recorder/near/434978744)

Patrick Dürke (Danish healthcare implementer, April 2024) opens the
thread asking how to model "a nurse registering a diagnosis on behalf of
a doctor" — i.e., should the nurse be the asserter or the recorder, and
where does the doctor go? René Spronk gives the short summary: "recorder
= person writing/typing the information; an asserter (an author of a
statement) is responsible for content." John Moehrke confirms with the
canonical pair of patterns: patient is typically the asserter for
patient-reported items (a patient asserting "I have foot pain") with
nurse/doctor as recorder, **versus** the clinician being both asserter
and recorder when they themselves diagnose — sometimes delegating only
the recording to an assistant. Patrick Dürke confirmed this resolved his
question. John Silva closed with an open question about US-jurisdiction
"legal responsibility" sitting with the asserter, which was not
explicitly answered. The thread reached informal consensus that matches
the proposal in FHIR-49653 (asserter = clinically responsible for the
statement; recorder = the typist), but it is a one-off implementer Q&A
on `chat.fhir.org`, **not** a Patient Care WG decision.

*Participants:* Patrick Dürke, René Spronk, John Moehrke, John Silva.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the reporter's proposal verbatim to
`source/condition/structuredefinition-Condition.xml` in `HL7/fhir`,
within the existing `<differential>`:

- On `Condition.asserter` (currently at line ~590):
  - Keep `<definition value="Individual or device that is making the
    condition statement."/>` unchanged.
  - Add a `<comment value="The asserter is responsible for the clinical
    accuracy of the condition statement."/>` element (Condition.asserter
    currently has no `<comment>`).
  - Append `J#49653` to the existing `committee-notes`
    `<valueString>` (currently `GF#11089, GF#22270, J#39602, J#41403`).
- On `Condition.recorder` (currently at lines 562–588):
  - Replace `<definition value="Individual who recorded the record and
    takes responsibility for accurately recording its content."/>` with
    `<definition value="Individual or device that captured the condition
    statement in the medical record."/>`.
  - Replace the existing `<comment value="Because the recorder takes
    responsibility for accurately recording information in the record,
    the recorder is the most recent author. The recorder might or might
    not be the asserter. By contrast, the recordedDate is when the
    condition was first recorded."/>` with `<comment value="The recorder
    is responsible for accurately recording information in the record.
    The recorder is the most recent author. The recorder might or might
    not be the asserter."/>` (drops the `recordedDate` sentence per the
    proposal).
  - Append `J#49653` to the existing `committee-notes` (currently
    `GF#14007, J#34394, J#39602, J#41403, J#48804`).
- Do **not** touch `<snapshot>`; the publisher regenerates it. Do not
  edit a spreadsheet copy.

#### Justification

The proposal is symmetric, matches the informal consensus on
`#implementers > Condition.asserter vs Condition.recorder`, and is
mechanically a low-risk, narrative-only change (no cardinality, type,
or invariant changes). It also adds the missing `<comment>` on
`Condition.asserter`, which is the gap the reporter is actually
complaining about. The bespoke-publisher gotchas in the FhirCore
briefing are all satisfied: `<differential>` only, append (don't
overwrite) committee-notes, no snapshot touch, no spreadsheet edit.

#### Risks / Caveats

- The ticket's recorder-definition rewording **partially conflicts** with
  the wording the WG just adopted on FHIR-48804 (which kept "takes
  responsibility for accurately recording its content" rather than the
  reporter's "captured the condition statement in the medical record").
  Accepting verbatim means re-opening that wording two weeks after it
  applied — the WG should consciously choose to do so.
- Dropping the `recordedDate` contrast sentence removes a useful
  disambiguator that has been in the comment for several cycles.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach (recommended)

#### Proposal

Make the **asserter** edit the reporter asks for, but **keep the WG's
already-applied `Condition.recorder` definition** from FHIR-48804 and
adjust the recorder *comment* only minimally. Concretely, in
`source/condition/structuredefinition-Condition.xml`:

- On `Condition.asserter`: add the new `<comment value="The asserter is
  responsible for the clinical accuracy of the condition statement.
  The asserter is not necessarily the recorder; for example, a patient
  or family member may assert an allergy or symptom that a clinician
  records."/>` (the second sentence accommodates the
  AllergyIntolerance pattern raised by the reporter in the
  2025-01-24 follow-up comment and confirmed by John Moehrke on Zulip).
  Append `J#49653` to `committee-notes`.
- On `Condition.recorder`:
  - **Leave `<definition>` as `Individual who recorded the record and
    takes responsibility for accurately recording its content.`** (the
    FHIR-48804 outcome).
  - Trim the `<comment>` to: `The recorder is responsible for
    accurately recording information in the record (not for the
    clinical accuracy of the assertion itself). The recorder is the
    most recent author and might or might not be the asserter. By
    contrast, the recordedDate is when the condition was first
    recorded.` This explicitly cleaves "responsibility for accurate
    recording" from "responsibility for clinical accuracy", which is
    the asymmetry the ticket is complaining about, **without**
    re-litigating FHIR-48804.
  - Append `J#49653` to `committee-notes`.
- Apply the same asserter-comment pattern to
  `AllergyIntolerance.asserter` and `AllergyIntolerance.recorder`
  comments in `source/allergyintolerance/structuredefinition-AllergyIntolerance.xml`,
  to address the cross-resource scope flagged in FHIR-48804 and in the
  reporter's 2025-01-24 comment about the two different patterns.

#### Justification

This delivers the substantive request (make the responsibility split
between asserter and recorder explicit and symmetric) **without**
overwriting wording the WG just settled on the 2025-11-06 call. It also
tackles the related-resource scope (Condition + AllergyIntolerance) that
the WG kept noting in the FHIR-48804 thread — accepting the spirit of
the IHE-IDR concern while staying inside the prose-only,
`<differential>`-only edit envelope the briefing endorses. Keeping the
`recordedDate` sentence preserves a useful contrast that has answered
recurring implementer questions. There are no cross-repo or Provenance
ripple effects (FHIR-49221) because we do not change what a "recorder"
*is*, only spell out what they are and aren't responsible for.

### Disposition C: Decline (close as duplicate of FHIR-48804)

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-49653 with resolution **Persuasive but Already Addressed** /
**Duplicate of FHIR-48804**. Specifically: cite that the underlying
"recorder takes responsibility for content" wording was already adjusted
on the 2025-11-06 PC call (FHIR-48804 → "…**for accurately recording**
its content", PR #3961 commit 3421106), and that the WG explicitly
considered and *rejected* further loosening of `Condition.asserter`'s
definition on the same call. Add `J#49653` to the existing
`committee-notes` on `Condition.recorder` and `Condition.asserter` in
`source/condition/structuredefinition-Condition.xml` purely as a paper
trail.

#### Justification

Defensible on procedural grounds: FHIR-48804 was filed earlier
(2024-11-08), covered the same ground plus AllergyIntolerance, went
through Patient Care discussion, and reached a decision on
2025-11-06. Re-opening the asserter side ten days later via FHIR-49653
risks the WG having to rehash the same ground. This is also the cheapest
disposition in terms of churn for downstream IGs (US Core via
FHIR-49221, IHE-IDR).

#### Risks / Caveats

- The applied resolution to FHIR-48804 only touched
  `Condition.recorder.definition` — it did **not** add the asserter
  comment that FHIR-49653 specifically asks for, and it did **not**
  address the asymmetry from the asserter side. So "already addressed"
  is technically only partially true.
- Closing without action will leave the implementer-visible asymmetry
  that drives the recurring `#implementers` Zulip question (and IHE-IDR
  ballot comment) unresolved.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **B**

Disposition B is the right answer because the WG's own 2025-11-06
discussion on FHIR-48804 already converged on a key principle —
"recorder is responsible for *accurately recording*, not for *clinical
content*" — but stopped short of writing the **counterpart** sentence on
`Condition.asserter`. FHIR-49653 is asking for exactly that missing
counterpart. Disposition A would also deliver it, but at the cost of
re-opening FHIR-48804's freshly-applied wording (which the WG had clear
reasons to land at), and Disposition C leaves the implementer-visible
asymmetry — the very pain point that produced both this Jira ticket and
the recurring `#implementers` thread (Patrick Dürke, John Moehrke) —
unresolved. B threads the needle: it preserves the FHIR-48804 outcome,
adds the asserter clarification the reporter actually needs, addresses
the AllergyIntolerance pattern flagged by the reporter and consistent
with John Moehrke's Zulip explanation, and stays inside the
narrative-only, `<differential>`-only, single-repo envelope that the
HL7/fhir briefing flags as low-risk. The WG should also append `J#49653`
to the relevant `committee-notes` extensions either way to maintain the
audit trail.
