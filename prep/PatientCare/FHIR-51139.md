## Summary

The submitter asks why the SDOHCC Coordination Platform CapabilityStatement
sets the conformance expectation for the `Device._lastUpdated` search
parameter to **SHALL** on the Client side but only **SHOULD** on the Server
side, while the corresponding `search-type` interaction is SHOULD on both
sides. The reporter notes the asymmetry appears inconsistent — a client
cannot reliably rely on search-by-`_lastUpdated` against Device when
servers are only required to SHOULD-support that interaction/parameter.
The ticket also references the prior ticket
[FHIR-50071](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50071), which raised a
broader issue that `_lastUpdated` is marked SHALL for Organization and
Device in the same CapabilityStatement while `meta.lastUpdated` itself is
not required via cardinality or Must Support. FHIR-50071 was resolved
Persuasive with an agreement to relax the Device (Client) and Organization
(Server) `_lastUpdated` expectations from SHALL to SHOULD; FHIR-51139 is
the Device-side companion and has been resolved Persuasive, with the
agreed change being to lower the Client-side `Device._lastUpdated`
conformance from SHALL to SHOULD so it aligns with the Server side.

## Details

**Description:**
Why is the conformance expectation for Device search parameter
`_lastUpdated` SHALL for
[Client](https://hl7.org/fhir/us/sdoh-clinicalcare/CapabilityStatement-SDOHCC-CoordinationPlatform.html#Device1-4),
but SHOULD for
[Server](https://hl7.org/fhir/us/sdoh-clinicalcare/CapabilityStatement-SDOHCC-CoordinationPlatform.html#Device2-4)?

This ticket may also be influenced by the discussion in a previously
submitted HL7 JIRA [FHIR-50071](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50071).

**Resolution (from snapshot):** Persuasive. "We will update the
conformance of `_lastUpdated` in the Client Interaction of the Device
Resource in the SDOHCC CP CapabilityStatement from **SHALL** to
**SHOULD**." Resolved 2026-03-27.

**Comments:**

- **JIRAUSER16901 (2026-03-20):** "The mismatch between client and server
  seems incorrect. We need to explore the use case further to decide
  whether both should be 'SHOULD' or something else."

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Lower the conformance expectation of the `Device._lastUpdated` search
parameter on the **Client** interaction in
`CapabilityStatement-SDOHCC-CoordinationPlatform` from **SHALL** to
**SHOULD**, so it matches the Server-side expectation (SHOULD) and the
`search-type` interaction expectation (SHOULD on both sides). No change
to Server-side expectations, to other resources, or to
`meta.lastUpdated` cardinality / Must Support is made under this
disposition. This matches exactly the ticket's Resolution Description.

#### Justification

The current asymmetry is indefensible on its own terms: a client is
required (SHALL) to support searching Device by `_lastUpdated`, but the
server it talks to is only required to SHOULD-support the `search-type`
interaction on Device and only SHOULD-support the `_lastUpdated`
parameter. A client's SHALL therefore cannot be relied upon in practice,
which is the pattern FHIR-50071 already flagged across the
CapabilityStatement. Relaxing Client to SHOULD aligns the two sides,
matches the interaction-level expectation, and is the minimum change
needed to restore internal consistency. The Patient Care / Gravity
comment (JIRAUSER16901, 2026-03-20) explicitly recognized the mismatch
as incorrect, and the already-recorded Persuasive resolution agrees.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than relax Client to SHOULD, raise the Server side and the
underlying data contract to make `_lastUpdated` genuinely reliable:

1. Keep `Device._lastUpdated` at **SHALL** on the Client interaction.
2. Promote `Device._lastUpdated` on the **Server** interaction from
   SHOULD to **SHALL**.
3. Promote the `search-type` interaction on Device to **SHALL** on the
   Server (and, optionally, on the Client).
4. Add a Must Support flag to `Device.meta.lastUpdated` (or add an
   explicit narrative statement in the CapabilityStatement) so the
   parameter has a supported field to operate on — addressing the root
   concern raised in FHIR-50071 that `_lastUpdated` is SHALL while
   `meta.lastUpdated` is not required.

#### Justification

`_lastUpdated` is primarily useful for delta / polling workflows, which
are an operational fit for a Coordination Platform that must stay in
sync with peer systems across sectors of care. If the Gravity Project
sees enough value in `_lastUpdated` to mandate it on the Client, the
symmetric server-side requirement — plus a supported `meta.lastUpdated`
— arguably better serves implementer reliability than downgrading the
Client. The main trade-off is implementation burden: some existing
server implementations may not persist or expose `meta.lastUpdated`
consistently, and raising Server to SHALL is a breaking-tightening of
conformance that could invalidate published claims of conformance.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as Not Persuasive and leave the CapabilityStatement as-is: Client
SHALL, Server SHOULD for `Device._lastUpdated`. Document the rationale
that "SHALL for Client" is intentional guidance to implementers building
coordination-platform clients, even though the companion Server
expectation is SHOULD.

#### Justification

A defensible decline would rest on (a) not wanting to re-open a settled
STU3 CapabilityStatement for a cosmetic conformance-level change, and
(b) the observation that a Client SHALL is not harmful in practice — it
simply obligates client implementers to exercise `_lastUpdated` when the
server supports it. However, this is weak in light of FHIR-50071's
already-accepted resolution and the workgroup's own on-record comment
that the mismatch is incorrect; declining here would contradict the
companion ticket's resolution and leave the CapabilityStatement
internally inconsistent.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A is the least disruptive, directly addresses the reported
inconsistency, and aligns with the outcome the workgroup has already
agreed to on FHIR-50071 (lower Device-Client and Organization-Server
`_lastUpdated` from SHALL to SHOULD). It keeps the Gravity SDOHCC STU3
block-vote slate intact and matches the Persuasive resolution already
recorded on this ticket. Disposition B is architecturally attractive
but materially tightens conformance on servers and drags in a
`meta.lastUpdated` Must Support discussion that belongs in a separate,
broader change; it would likely block a clean STU3 resolution.
Disposition C is hard to defend given the workgroup's own acknowledgment
that the asymmetry is incorrect and the already-resolved predecessor
ticket. Patient Care should accept A as part of Gravity SDOHCC STU3
Block Vote 2.
