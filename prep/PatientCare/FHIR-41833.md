## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the punctuation fix exactly as the reporter describes: locate the
sentence in the *International Birth and Child Model (IBCM) R1* narrative
that begins with the relevant clause containing the word "collected" and
insert a comma immediately after that word in the first sentence of the
page in question. The change is editorial only — no resource definitions,
profiles, value sets, examples, or constraints are altered. Because the
ticket does not name the page, the editor will need to identify the
referenced page from the IBCM IG narrative pages (likely a top-level
introductory page such as `index.md` or a methodology/data-collection
page) and confirm with the reporter before publishing if there is any
ambiguity.

#### Justification

The request is a "Technical Correction" of type punctuation, which is the
lowest-risk change class an HL7 IG can take: it has no normative impact, no
backward-compatibility concerns, and no implementer impact. Accepting as
requested honours the reporter's careful proofreading and improves IG
readability, which is consistent with HL7 editorial practice for narrative
typos. The change touches only the affected page's narrative source and
will be picked up automatically by the IG publisher.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the spirit of the request but, as part of the same editorial pass,
re-read the entire first paragraph (or the full opening section) of the
identified page and apply any other obvious copy-edit fixes (missing
articles, comma splices, agreement errors, etc.) at the same time. Where
the sentence would still read awkwardly after just inserting one comma,
restructure it lightly rather than only inserting the punctuation. The
reporter's specific change ("add `,` after 'collected'") is treated as the
trigger, not the literal limit, of the edit.

#### Justification

Editorial tickets are cheap to action individually but expensive in
aggregate (each one consumes workgroup-meeting time and a publication
cycle). Bundling adjacent copy-edits behind a single ticket is consistent
with how many HL7 work groups handle narrative cleanup and reduces the
chance that the same paragraph generates a follow-up ticket in a later
ballot cycle. The risk is small: the change is still narrative-only and
remains within the same paragraph the reporter already flagged.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket as "Not Persuasive — Insufficient Information" on the
grounds that the description does not identify the page, section, or
sentence being corrected. The first sentence of *which* page on the IBCM
IG contains the word "collected" is not unambiguous from the description
alone, and the IG has multiple narrative pages. Decline without prejudice
and invite the reporter to resubmit with the page URL and the full
sentence quoted.

#### Justification

Acting on an editorial change without confirming the exact target risks
either changing the wrong sentence or introducing a comma in a place
where it is not actually warranted (e.g., before a restrictive clause).
HL7 ballot procedure normally requires the comment text to be specific
enough to be acted on; "first sentence" is a relative reference that does
not identify a unique location in the IG. Declining for clarification is
defensible until the reporter or workgroup pins the location down.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

For a single-character punctuation fix flagged as a "Technical Correction"
by an engaged community reporter, the cost of accepting is essentially
zero and the cost of declining or alternative-routing is disproportionate
workgroup overhead. The IBCM R1 IG is small enough that an editor can
identify the sentence containing "collected" in its narrative pages
without ambiguity in a few minutes; if any genuine ambiguity is found
during that triage, the editor can reach out to Ana Kostadinovska directly
for the page URL rather than burning another review cycle. Disposition B
is reasonable as an editorial *practice* but should not gate this
ticket's resolution. Disposition C overweights process for a change with
no normative or implementation impact.
