## Summary

Paul Knapp asks the Ophthalmology Retinal (FHIR) IG to stop relying on
HL7 Confluence pages for any material that is required to understand or
implement the guide. Because Confluence pages have no long-term
preservation guarantee, any content that an implementer needs in order
to read or build against the IG must live inside the published IG
itself. References that point outward to durable resources — the FHIR
base specification, government regulations, and similar — remain
acceptable, but in-line "see the HL7 Confluence page for more details
and context here" pointers should be replaced with the actual content
folded into the appropriate IG narrative section. No specific Confluence
URL or destination IG section is named in the ticket; the fix is a
sweep across the IG narrative pages.

No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were
returned by the FhirAugury indexes for this ticket, so the request
stands on its own.

## Proposed Dispositions

Because no GitHub cross-references were surfaced, the dispositions
below are limited to the editorial scope described in the ticket
itself: a sweep of the Ophthalmology Retinal IG narrative pages to
replace "see the Confluence page" pointers with the underlying
content. Any disposition that resolves with a code/spec change will,
when scheduled, need the Patient Care editor to identify the IG's
GitHub authoring repo and the specific narrative pages that carry
the offending links; that mapping is not available from the data
gathered here.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Audit every narrative page in the Ophthalmology Retinal (FHIR) IG for
phrases of the form "see the HL7 confluence page for more details" (and
equivalent variants) that point to HL7 Confluence. For each hit:

1. Open the linked Confluence page.
2. Decide whether the linked content is **required** to understand or
   implement the IG (definitions, required workflows, conformance
   expectations, must-support semantics, code-system rationale, etc.)
   or **supplementary** (background, meeting notes, history).
3. For required content, copy the substantive text into the appropriate
   narrative page in the IG source (typically the same page that today
   carries the "see Confluence" pointer), edit it for IG voice, and
   remove the outbound Confluence link.
4. For supplementary content, either drop the link entirely or
   reword it as informative ("Background discussion is available on the
   HL7 Confluence space"), making clear that the Confluence material is
   not required reading.
5. Leave outbound references to durable sources — the FHIR base spec,
   ISO/IEC standards, CMS/HHS regulations, ICD/SNOMED/LOINC browsers,
   etc. — unchanged.

The publish-side deliverable is an updated IG build in which a search
for "confluence" across the rendered narrative returns zero
content-bearing hits.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter asks for, and it aligns with HL7's
own publishing principle that a balloted IG must be self-contained: a
reader who has only the published IG bundle must still be able to
implement it correctly. Confluence pages are not part of the published
artifact set, are not under change control, and have repeatedly been
moved or retired (the HL7 Confluence migrations of 2018, 2022, and the
ongoing Atlassian Cloud transition all broke historical deep links).
Folding the material in is also good for accessibility and
translatability — the IG renderer indexes IG narrative for search,
Confluence content is not indexed by `hl7.org/fhir`. The change is
purely editorial and carries no FHIR conformance impact, so the cost is
bounded by the number of offending links.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than inlining all required Confluence content, adopt a tiered
policy and codify it in the IG's authoring/contribution guide:

1. **Tier 1 — Required content.** Inline into the IG narrative, as in
   Disposition A.
2. **Tier 2 — Reference content under HL7 stewardship that has a stable
   home.** Replace the Confluence link with a link to the HL7 work
   group's Confluence *space landing page* (which is more durable than
   any individual page) **plus** a one- or two-paragraph synopsis in the
   IG narrative that captures the operative point. This keeps the IG
   self-sufficient for implementers while preserving an audit trail back
   to the work group's working notes.
3. **Tier 3 — Background / meeting-notes content.** Drop the link
   entirely; the IG should not advertise unstable working artifacts.

Add a short "External References" subsection to the IG's
`index.html`/home page explaining the policy, so future contributors
understand why bare Confluence pointers should not be re-introduced.

#### Justification

The reporter's underlying concern is **durability**, not the existence
of Confluence as a tool. Some Confluence pages — especially work group
process pages, governance pages, and standing meeting agendas — are
genuinely useful as a *trail* for future maintainers even when they are
not strictly required for implementation. A blanket "no Confluence
links" rule risks throwing away that traceability, and a future
contributor who doesn't remember this ticket may simply paste the
Confluence link back in. A written policy in the IG repo (Disposition B)
operationalizes the principle from Disposition A while preventing
regression. The synopsis-plus-link pattern also matches what other HL7
IGs (notably US Core's "Background and Future Work" pages) already do.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve as **Persuasive with Mod / Not Persuasive — Editorial Practice
Already in Effect**. State that the IG editorial policy is already to
inline required content and that any remaining "see the Confluence
page" pointers will be addressed during the normal QA pass before the
next ballot publication, without a dedicated ticket.

#### Justification

The ticket is from 2021 and has been **Triaged** without further
discussion since 2022. It carries no Connectathon dependency, no
implementer escalation, and no cross-references to any other Jira
ticket, PR, or Zulip thread — i.e., no one has been blocked by a
concrete broken link in four years. The Patient Care / Ophthalmology
Retinal IG cadence already includes a pre-publication QA review, and a
single grep of the rendered IG for `confluence` is a low-cost task that
naturally folds into that review. Spending committee time on a
formal disposition for an editorial-cleanup change request that is best
handled in the QA pass arguably deflects attention from substantive
ballot issues.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

This is a multi-year-old housekeeping ticket whose practical fix is
editorial, but the *underlying* problem — implementers reading "see the
Confluence page" and finding either nothing or stale content — is real
and recurring, and Disposition C trusts an unwritten convention that
has demonstrably failed (the offending wording is still in the IG, four
years later). Disposition A solves the immediate problem but does not
prevent regression. Disposition B captures the principle Paul Knapp
articulated, inlines the required content the same way A does, and
preserves a documented, repeatable rule for future contributors so the
ticket does not need to be re-filed against the next IG version. The
work group should pair the disposition with an action item to identify
the IG's GitHub authoring repo (so a concrete follow-up PR can be
opened) — that mapping was not available from the data sources used to
prepare this report.
