### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Sweep every conformance artifact published by the **Ophthalmology
Retinal (FHIR)** IG — StructureDefinitions (profiles and extensions),
ValueSets, CodeSystems, ConceptMaps, CapabilityStatements, OperationDefinitions,
SearchParameters, and any example/instance pages — and ensure each one
has a populated `description` element (and, for narrative pages, a
human-readable purpose paragraph). Where the artifact already has a
title but no description, author a one- to three-sentence description
that states (1) what the artifact represents, (2) the clinical or
operational context in which it is used in retinal ophthalmology, and
(3) any constraint or behavior the implementer must be aware of. Track
the sweep as a single epic / parent ticket with one child task per
artifact so that progress is visible to the work group.

#### Justification

This is what the ticket literally asks for, and it aligns with general
HL7 publication expectations: every conformance artifact in a balloted
or published IG should carry a description sufficient for an
implementer to understand it without having to read the underlying
profile diff. Missing descriptions are a recurring QA finding from IG
publisher quality checks and from balloters, and adding them is a
non-breaking, narrative-only change. Because the ticket is filed
against an IG (not the FHIR core spec), the change is fully scoped to
the IG's own repository and does not affect downstream implementers'
existing instances.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than treating FHIR-33307 as a single bulk-edit task, convert it
into a tracking ticket that establishes a **description policy** for
the Ophthalmology Retinal IG, then opens one focused child ticket per
artifact category (profiles, extensions, value sets, code systems,
operations, search parameters). The policy would specify: (a) minimum
description length and required content (purpose, scope, key
constraints, conformance expectations); (b) a style guide aligned with
the rest of the Patient Care work group's IGs; and (c) an IG-publisher
QA rule (for example, a `description.empty()` invariant check or a
`jira-spec-internals.json` rule) that fails the build when a new
artifact is committed without a description. This prevents the same
gap from recurring after the sweep.

#### Justification

A one-time bulk fill closes the current gap but does nothing to prevent
the next artifact from being added without a description. Encoding the
expectation as a publication-time check shifts the cost left and turns
"add descriptions" from recurring tech debt into a normal authoring
requirement. It also gives reviewers a concrete, falsifiable definition
of "has a description" rather than relying on subjective judgement
during ballot reconciliation. The trade-off versus Disposition A is
slightly more up-front policy work and the need to coordinate the QA
rule with the IG-publisher / sushi toolchain, but the long-term
maintenance cost is materially lower.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Not Persuasive — insufficient specificity**. The
description does not name any specific artifact, does not provide
proposed wording, and does not cite an existing wording problem; the
"Existing Wording" and "Proposed Wording" sections are both `NA`. Ask
the reporter to refile one ticket per artifact (or per logical group of
artifacts) with concrete proposed text so the work group can review
and apply changes in a reviewable way.

#### Justification

The HL7 ticket workflow expects each Change Request to be actionable
on its own, with enough detail that a reviewer can apply or reject a
specific change. A blanket "many artifacts are missing descriptions"
request cannot be reviewed at the artifact level, cannot be balloted
cleanly (no proposed wording to vote on), and risks producing
descriptions that are written by the resolver rather than by a domain
expert in retinal ophthalmology. Declining-with-guidance keeps the
ticket queue auditable and pushes the work back to the form that the
process is designed to handle.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition A is the most direct response to what the ticket literally
asks for, but it leaves no durable artifact behind: the next round of
profiles added to the Ophthalmology Retinal IG will reintroduce the
same gap, and a future ticket identical to FHIR-33307 will be filed
again. Disposition C is defensible on process grounds but is unlikely
to produce a better outcome — the underlying observation (artifacts
lack descriptions) is correct and is worth fixing whether or not the
ticket meets the usual specificity bar. Disposition B captures the
benefit of A (the IG actually gets descriptions) while also putting a
guardrail in place so the work group does not have to relitigate this
in the next ballot cycle. Patient Care should adopt B, with the bulk
fill from A executed as the first wave of work under the new policy,
and individual artifact descriptions reviewed by a retinal subject
matter expert before publication.
