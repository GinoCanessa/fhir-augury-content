## Summary

Craig Newman observes that the `UseOfDevice` profile defined in the PACIO
Functional Status IG appears to be essentially identical to a counterpart
profile in the PACIO Cognitive Status IG, differing only in narrative text.
He raises the same concern for two extensions that travel with that
profile — the *assistance* extension and the *device* extension — which
likewise appear duplicated between the two IGs. The ticket asks whether
one of the IGs (or a shared base) could define these artifacts once and
have the other IG reuse them, rather than each IG carrying near-duplicate
copies. No cross-referenced Jira tickets were found in the Augury index,
so the request stands on its own from a Jira-link perspective.

## Details

**Description:**

The UseOfDevice profiles between the cognitive and functional status IGs
seem identical except for some narrative text. Can one IG define it and
have it used by both? Similarly, the assistance and device extension look
the same other than narrative text and may be able to be reused.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Refactor the duplicated artifacts so they live in a single PACIO IG and
are referenced by the others. Concretely:

- Promote the `UseOfDevice` profile (built on `DeviceUseStatement` in the
  PACIO context), the *assistance* extension, and the *device* extension
  out of the PACIO Functional Status IG and into a shared/base PACIO
  artifact set. The most natural homes are either (a) a new lightweight
  "PACIO Common" / "PACIO Base" IG that both Functional Status and
  Cognitive Status depend on, or (b) the older/more foundational of the
  two IGs (typically Functional Status, since it published STU1 first),
  with Cognitive Status declaring an `IG dependency` on it.
- Update Cognitive Status to import the canonical URLs of those
  artifacts and delete its near-duplicate copies, preserving only the
  IG-specific narrative as profile/IG-level documentation rather than as
  a separate StructureDefinition.
- Coordinate canonical URL stability and versioning so the migration
  does not invalidate existing instances pointing at the
  Cognitive-Status-flavoured canonicals.

#### Justification

This is the FHIR-conformant way to express "same artifact, different
narrative" — narrative is not part of the differential, so two
StructureDefinitions that differ only in `text` are by definition the
same profile and should be one artifact. Reuse via IG dependency is a
first-class FHIR mechanism, eliminates drift between the two PACIO IGs,
and makes downstream implementer behaviour deterministic (one canonical,
one set of slicing/binding rules). It also directly addresses the
scenario Craig describes in the ticket without changing any
clinical semantics.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the artifacts physically defined in each IG but eliminate semantic
divergence by:

- Defining each artifact (the `UseOfDevice` profile and the two
  extensions) once in a shared PACIO "common" location and having each
  IG either (i) re-publish a thin profile that derives from the shared
  one with no constraint changes, or (ii) bind to different value sets
  per IG where the *only* legitimate per-IG variation lies — which is
  exactly the divergence flagged in the 2021-11-04 comment ("the PACIO
  team will consider addressing the issue of defining different value
  sets for the two IGs").
- Lock the structural differential and slicing to the shared base and
  push all per-IG specialization into bindings/narratives, so future
  edits to the shape of `UseOfDevice` happen in one place.

#### Justification

This preserves the existing canonical URLs in each IG (no implementer
churn, no broken references in already-published instances) while still
removing the duplication that the ticket complains about. It also
explicitly accommodates the only known per-IG difference — value-set
binding — that the PACIO team itself flagged as the reason they paused
on a straight reuse path. Trade-off vs. Disposition A: there are still
two profile resources to maintain, but their differential is mechanical
and small, so the maintenance cost is much closer to "one artifact" than
to today's "two near-duplicates".

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket as "Considered – No Change" / "Persuasive with Mod – Out
of Scope" on the grounds that (a) restructuring profile ownership across
two already-published PACIO IGs is a publishing/governance decision for
the PACIO project rather than a Patient Care WG ballot item, and (b) the
2021-08-09 vote already landed on "Considered for Future Use" with the
caveat that per-IG value-set differences are real and need to be sorted
out before any reuse refactor.

#### Justification

The ticket is genuinely a packaging/governance concern about two
external (PACIO-published) IGs rather than a defect in a profile, an
extension, or the FHIR core. The PACIO team has explicitly said they
need a clear direction on the value-set divergence before they can
collapse the artifacts, and Patient Care does not own the PACIO
publishing pipeline. Declining (or explicitly deferring back to PACIO)
avoids Patient Care taking on a refactor it cannot execute.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The ticket is correct on its face — two profiles whose only differential
is narrative are the same profile — and ignoring that (Disposition C)
leaves a real interoperability hazard in place: implementers cannot tell
whether the two `UseOfDevice` profiles are intentionally distinct or
accidentally divergent, and any future structural edit to one is almost
guaranteed to drift from the other. Disposition A is the cleanest
long-term answer, but it forces a canonical-URL migration that the PACIO
team has already signalled they are not ready to commit to, precisely
because of the legitimate per-IG value-set differences noted in the
2021-11-04 comment. Disposition B threads that needle: it consolidates
the *structure* once in a shared PACIO base, keeps each IG's published
canonicals stable, and isolates the only real per-IG variation
(bindings) to the place where it actually belongs. That gives Patient
Care a defensible disposition to send back to the PACIO project without
either pretending the duplication is fine or forcing a breaking
republish.
