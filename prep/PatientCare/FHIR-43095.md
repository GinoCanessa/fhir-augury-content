## Summary

The reporter notes an inconsistency in the footer of the *International Birth
And Child Model (FHIR)* implementation guide: the footer text reads
"HL7 International, CHOICE Workgroup", but the accompanying hyperlink points
to the Patient Care Workgroup page rather than to the CHOICE Workgroup. The
ticket is a Question/defect against the IG's publication metadata; the
underlying question is which of the two — the displayed text or the link
target — is the authoritative owning workgroup, and to align the footer
accordingly. No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub
items were found for this ticket, so the request is taken at face value.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat the displayed text as authoritative and fix the **link target** in
the IG footer so that "HL7 International, CHOICE Workgroup" points to
the CHOICE Workgroup page
(`https://confluence.hl7.org/display/CHOICE` or the equivalent current
URL), not to the Patient Care Workgroup page. Concretely, update the
workgroup link in the IG's publication configuration (e.g., the
`workgroup` setting in `sushi-config.yaml` / `ig.ini` and any custom
footer template under the IG's authoring root) so the rendered footer's
href matches the rendered text.

#### Justification

This is the literal request of the ticket and is appropriate **if** the
IG is in fact maintained by the CHOICE Workgroup. It removes the visible
inconsistency with a one-line metadata change and does not affect the
substantive content of the IG. It also aligns the published artifact
with the IG Publisher's standard workgroup-link mechanism, which is the
designated way to attribute an IG.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Treat the link target as authoritative and fix the **displayed text** in
the footer to read "HL7 International, Patient Care Workgroup" instead
of "CHOICE Workgroup". In the IG's publication configuration, set the
`workgroup` value (and any hand-authored footer string) to the Patient
Care Workgroup so that both the rendered text and the href consistently
identify Patient Care as the owning workgroup. This is reinforced by
the Jira ticket's own metadata, which records the Work Group as
**Patient Care**.

#### Justification

The ticket itself is filed against Patient Care, suggesting that
Patient Care — not CHOICE — is the workgroup that actually owns the
*International Birth And Child Model*. If that is correct, then the
displayed "CHOICE Workgroup" string is the error and the link is right;
fixing the text is then the substantive correction rather than a
cosmetic re-aim of the link. This option avoids silently re-attributing
an IG to a workgroup that may not have agreed to take stewardship.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Not Persuasive / Already Addressed** if Patient
Care confirms that the IG has since been re-published with corrected
footer attribution (the ticket dates from late 2023 and has had no
activity since). No spec change is made, but a comment is added to the
ticket pointing reviewers at the current rendered footer for
verification.

#### Justification

This is defensible only in the narrow case where the inconsistency has
already been resolved in a later build of the IG. Declining without
verification would leave a documented mismatch between the displayed
workgroup name and the linked workgroup page, which is a real (if
small) defect in publication metadata. Decline should not be used to
sidestep deciding which workgroup actually owns the IG.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The ticket is filed against Patient Care, and Patient Care is the
workgroup being asked to disposition it — both signals strongly suggest
that Patient Care is the actual steward of the *International Birth And
Child Model* and that the footer's "CHOICE Workgroup" text is the
typo, not the link. Disposition B aligns the footer with the workgroup
that is, in practice, triaging issues against the IG, and avoids
quietly handing stewardship to CHOICE just because the text happens to
say so. Patient Care should confirm ownership at review; if the
workgroup determines that CHOICE is in fact the owner, the disposition
can be flipped to A with the same mechanical change applied to the
text instead of the link.
