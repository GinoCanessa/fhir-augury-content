## Summary

The reporter, Molly Reese, applauds the SDOH Clinical Care IG's
acknowledgement that "The Gravity Project recognizes the need to
appropriately manage privacy and consent related to a patient's social
risk issues." She argues, however, that the IG's privacy and consent
narrative should not be scoped *only* to the floor of obtaining consent
to share data with non-Covered Entities (non-CEs). She asks the IG to
also remind implementers — and, through them, patients — that
information shared with Community-Based Organizations (CBOs) may not
remain private or secure once it leaves the HIPAA-regulated environment,
and that patients should be encouraged to discuss CBOs' own privacy and
security safeguards directly with those organizations. The intent is
not to discourage SDOH data sharing but to ensure patients give truly
informed consent and to protect the patient–clinician trust
relationship from downstream harms. No cross-referenced Jira tickets
were returned by the augury index for this issue.

## Details

**Description:**

> "The Gravity Project recognizes the need to appropriately manage
> privacy and consent related to a patient's social risk issues."
>
> This is great and a helpful flag for implementers to think about
> these issues. We suggest that these conversations not be limited to
> consent to share data with a non-CE — though that is a critically
> necessary floor — and that implementers ensure patients understand
> that information shared with CBOs may not remain private and/or

> understand who may have access to their potentially sensitive
> information — failure to do so may result in harm to the patient or
> their family down the line and could jeopardize the trust the patient
> has with their clinician.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Expand the **Privacy and Consent** narrative section of the US SDOH
Clinical Care IG (the same section that currently contains the quoted
"The Gravity Project recognizes the need to appropriately manage
privacy and consent…" text) to add explicit implementer guidance that:

1. Consent to share SDOH data with a non-Covered Entity is a *floor*,
   not the entire scope of the privacy/consent conversation.
2. Implementers SHOULD make patients aware that data shared with CBOs
   leaves the HIPAA-regulated environment and may be subject to
   different (and potentially weaker) privacy and security protections.
3. Implementers SHOULD encourage workflows in which patients are
   prompted, before referral or data exchange, to ask the receiving CBO
   about its own privacy and security safeguards (retention,
   re-disclosure, breach handling, family-member access, etc.).
4. Cite the Gravity Project Data Principles and any newly-available
   ONC/HL7/CMS guidance on consent in non-HIPAA settings.

This is purely a narrative/guidance change — no `Consent` resource
profile, value set, or extension changes are implied. The change would
land in the IG's privacy/consent page (the same page already
referenced by the original requester).

#### Justification

The underlying concern was already accepted by the workgroup on the
2022-04-13 SDOH FHIR IG call (per `rcdieterle`'s comment). The 2022-05
"Considered for Future Use" resolution was contingent on receiving more
external guidance, and Lynn Laakso's 2022-11 re-open note explicitly
flags that the IG should revisit this post-STU2. Patient Care has a
long-standing pattern in informational IGs of giving narrative
implementation guidance on privacy issues that fall outside the
normative `Consent`/`Provenance` machinery, and SDOH data — by
definition shared with non-HIPAA actors — is exactly the case where
that narrative is most needed. Accepting the request keeps the IG
honest about the limits of consent-to-share and protects the
patient–clinician trust relationship the reporter highlights.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Instead of (or in addition to) free-form narrative, add a **structured
checklist / "Implementer Considerations" callout box** to the IG's
privacy and consent page that enumerates the specific questions a
patient should be supported in asking a CBO before SDOH data is
exchanged — for example:

- Does the CBO have a written privacy policy?
- How long does the CBO retain the data?
- With whom may the CBO re-disclose the data (funders, partners,
  government agencies)?
- Does the CBO have a breach-notification process?
- Can the patient revoke sharing and have data deleted?

Pair this with a short note that these considerations are
informational and that the IG does not constrain the CBO side of the
exchange (no profile changes). Where appropriate, reference the
Gravity Project Data Principles and any cross-WG materials from
Community Based Care and Privacy (CBCP) or Security WG rather than
duplicating their content.

#### Justification

A bulleted checklist is more actionable for implementers than additional
prose and is easier to lift into patient-facing materials, training,
and EHR consent UIs. It also avoids the IG appearing to make
prescriptive statements about what non-HIPAA CBOs *must* do — which
the IG has no authority to require — while still satisfying the
reporter's substantive ask. Cross-referencing CBCP/Security/Gravity
Data Principles keeps SDOH Clinical Care from drifting into territory
owned by other WGs and reduces the risk of contradicting future
ONC/CMS guidance.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Persuasive with Modification → Not Persuasive**,
on the grounds that (a) the existing privacy and consent section
already references the Gravity Project Data Principles, which cover
the broader patient-education concern at the program-policy level, and
(b) detailed guidance on consent in non-HIPAA environments is properly
the domain of ONC, CMS, and the HL7 Security and CBCP work groups —
not the SDOH Clinical Care IG. Recommend the reporter raise the
broader concern through the Gravity Project policy channel and/or via
a Security WG ticket, and keep the IG's normative footprint narrow.

#### Justification

This was the workgroup's original 2022-05-23 instinct (Bob Dieterle /
Jay Lyle 7-0-1): defer until ONC/HL7/CMS produce authoritative
guidance on managing consent in non-HIPAA covered environments. If
that guidance still does not exist at the time of disposition,
declining avoids the IG making claims it cannot back up and avoids
duplicating Gravity Project Data Principles content inside the IG.
The risk of decline is that the patient-protection concern the
reporter raised remains unaddressed in the artifact most implementers
actually read.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B captures the substantive patient-protection ask of
FHIR-35641 — making sure implementers (and through them, patients)
understand that data shared with CBOs leaves the HIPAA perimeter —
while staying inside what an SDOH *clinical care* IG can credibly
say. The 2022-04-13 SDOH FHIR IG call already accepted the concern in
principle, and Lynn Laakso's 2022-11 re-open explicitly invites the WG
to add language now that STU2 is out the door. Pure narrative
(Disposition A) is acceptable but tends to be skimmed; declining
(Disposition C) re-litigates a concern the WG has already conceded and
leaves a known patient-trust gap unaddressed in the implementer-facing
artifact. A short, citable "Implementer Considerations" callout —
cross-linked to the Gravity Data Principles and to Security/CBCP
guidance where it exists — is the lowest-risk way to deliver the
reporter's intent without overreaching the IG's scope.
