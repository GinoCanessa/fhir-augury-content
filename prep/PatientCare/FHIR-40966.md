## Summary

The reporter asks Patient Care to **add Device** to the CapabilityStatement(s) of the **US Physical Activity (PA) FHIR IG**. Although filed as "Highest" priority and tagged for block voting (`Block-vote-2`), the ticket body itself is a one-line request with no narrative. Context comes from its history: on 2023-05-30 the project call approved a "Considered for Future Use" disposition (Jay Lyle / Rob Eastwood, vote 6-0-1) on the basis that "this IG does not require support for referencing device data from Observation. It was decided that knowledge of device information was a more advanced set of functionality than was necessary to meet the 'minimum viable product' for the IG." On 2023-08-25 lynn_laakso reverted that resolution and re-opened the ticket post-STU publication, signalling that the workgroup wants to reconsider Device support now that the baseline IG has shipped. The work group must therefore decide how (or whether) the PA IG's CapabilityStatement should declare conformance expectations for the `Device` resource so that Observation.device references to wearables, accelerometers and other physical-activity sensors are interoperable across PA actors.

No cross-references (Jira, Zulip, GitHub, Confluence) were returned by FhirAugury for this ticket, so the analysis below is grounded in the ticket itself, the comment history, and the keyword-related Patient Care / IG conformance tickets surfaced by the keyword index.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add `Device` to the US Physical Activity IG's CapabilityStatement(s) — both the **PA Producer (server)** and **PA Consumer (client)** capability statements published by the IG — declaring at minimum:

- `resource[type=Device]` with `supportedProfile` pointing at a new (or re-used) PA Device profile constrained to physical-activity sensors (wearables, accelerometers, heart-rate monitors, etc.).
- Interactions: `read` and `search-type` as `SHALL` on the server side; `create`/`update` left out unless an observed PA workflow requires write.
- Search parameters: at minimum `_id`, `patient`, and `type`.
- A short narrative paragraph in the PA CapabilityStatement intro explaining how `Observation.device` is used by PA Observations (e.g., step-count, energy-expended Observations) to point at the recording device, and that the `Device` resource is the reference target.

The companion change is to make `Observation.device` `SHALL Support` (or at least `SHOULD Support`) on the existing PA Observation profile(s) so the new CapabilityStatement entry is exercisable end-to-end.

#### Justification

This is what the reporter literally asked for, and the workgroup explicitly **reverted** the prior "Considered for Future Use" resolution post-STU publication, signalling that the original "minimum-viable-product" rationale no longer applies. PA Observations are overwhelmingly device-generated in practice (wearables, smart watches, fitness equipment), so a CapabilityStatement that omits `Device` understates real-world PA exchange. The keyword-related conformance tickets (FHIR-51138, FHIR-51139) flag the trap of inconsistent expectations across resource / interaction / search-parameter levels, so the proposal above sets all three explicitly to avoid that class of follow-up ballot comment.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Add `Device` to the PA CapabilityStatement(s) at **`SHOULD Support`** rather than `SHALL Support`, and declare `Observation.device` as **`SHOULD Support`** on the PA Observation profile, with guidance that:

- Implementers MAY satisfy the device-attribution requirement by populating the lightweight `Observation.device` reference together with `Device.identifier` and `Device.type` only (no full Device read interaction required), or
- Alternatively populate the (already-supported) `Observation.deviceName` extension when a full `Device` resource is impractical.

In other words, declare the capability so it is discoverable and testable, but do not force every PA actor to stand up a full Device endpoint in this STU update.

#### Justification

Several real PA implementers (mobile apps, gateway services) emit step-count Observations without backing them with a queryable Device endpoint. Forcing `SHALL Support` on `Device` (Disposition A) risks failing those implementers' conformance tests for what is, in practice, optional metadata. `SHOULD Support` keeps the IG honest about the role Device plays while preserving interoperability for lighter-weight producers — a pattern US Core has used repeatedly for resources whose presence is real but not universal. It also defers some of the conformance-symmetry questions raised by FHIR-51138 / FHIR-51139 until PA has implementer feedback to lean on.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (re-affirm "Considered for Future Use")

#### Proposal

Re-apply the original 2023-07-13 disposition: do not add `Device` to the PA CapabilityStatement(s) in this STU update, and explicitly defer to a future PA release. Add a single sentence to the IG's "Future Considerations" section noting that Device support is on the roadmap, so the gap is documented rather than silent.

#### Justification

The IG was published as STU on the explicit basis that Device support is "a more advanced set of functionality than was necessary to meet the minimum viable product." Re-opening the question without new implementer feedback or new use-case material in the ticket itself (the ticket body is one sentence; no Zulip threads, no linked tickets, no community comments) means the workgroup would be making the change on the strength of the re-open alone. Declining now and routing this to the PA roadmap preserves stability for current STU implementers and keeps the change paired with the broader Device-conformance discussions already in flight (e.g., FHIR-51138 / FHIR-51139).

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition: A — Accept As Requested**

The deciding factor is the August 2023 re-open: the workgroup actively walked back the "future use" resolution **after** STU publication, which is the strongest signal in the record that PA now considers Device support in-scope for the next STU update. Disposition C asks the workgroup to re-litigate the very decision lynn_laakso explicitly reverted, and Disposition B understates a capability that, in practice, every PA producer already exercises (PA Observations are essentially always device-generated). Disposition A also sets the conformance levels at all three layers (resource / interaction / search parameter) at once, which is the cleanest way to avoid the class of follow-up ballot comments illustrated by FHIR-51138 and FHIR-51139. If the workgroup is uncomfortable mandating `SHALL Support` on Device for every PA actor, the natural fallback is to adopt Disposition A's structure but downgrade the resource-level expectation to `SHOULD Support` — i.e., merge A's specificity with B's softer expectation — rather than declining outright.
