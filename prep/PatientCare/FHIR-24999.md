## Summary

The ticket requests that **Nutrition** be added as an *optional* section
of the International Patient Summary (IPS) Composition profile. The
reporter (Becky Gradl) argues that, while general dietary information
may not be critical for unplanned care, specific nutrition status can
be a patient safety issue — examples include patients on a dysphagia
diet, those with a fluid restriction, and those requiring enteral
feeding instructions. The change would extend the IPS Composition
section list (alongside existing optional sections such as Functional
Status, Plan of Care, Social History, etc.) with a Nutrition section
that profiles relevant FHIR resources to convey diet orders and
nutrition-related observations.

The ticket was previously dispositioned in the 2020-02-04 IPS ballot as
*Considered for Future Use* (Hausam/Zautke 12-0-1) with the suggestion
to "share the comment with the ISO IPS team for further discussion".
That resolution was reverted on 2022-11-22 by Lynn Laakso to re-open
the request post the STU update publication, which is why it now sits
back in **Triaged** status awaiting Patient Care disposition. No other
Jira tickets, Zulip discussions, or GitHub items were surfaced as
cross-references in the FHIR Augury index for this ticket.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add **Nutrition** as a new *optional* section to the IPS Composition
profile (`Composition-uv-ips`) in the IPS Implementation Guide
(authored in `HL7/fhir-ips`). Concretely:

- Define a new `Composition.section` slice named `sectionNutrition`
  with `section.code` bound to a new IPS-specific value-set entry
  (e.g., LOINC `61144-2` "Diet and nutrition Narrative" or a more
  specific concept agreed with the IPS terminology team).
- Allow `section.entry` to reference a profiled set of nutrition
  artifacts: `NutritionOrder` (active diet orders, including dysphagia
  diets and fluid restrictions), `NutritionIntake` (R5+; or
  `Observation` with a nutrition LOINC for R4 IPS), and any narrative
  needed to convey enteral feeding instructions.
- Mark the slice with `min = 0` (optional) and `max = 1`, mirroring
  the existing optional-section pattern (Functional Status, Plan of
  Care, Social History, etc.).
- Author the IPS section guidance page describing the intent, scope
  (safety-relevant nutrition information for unplanned care), and
  examples.

#### Justification

The reporter's safety argument is concrete and well-bounded: dysphagia
diets, fluid restrictions, and enteral feeding instructions are the
kind of information that, if missed during unplanned care, can cause
direct harm. The IPS Composition pattern of layering *optional*
sections on a small *required* core is purpose-built for exactly this
case — it lets jurisdictions and use-cases that need Nutrition
populate it without imposing a burden on summary producers that do
not. The 2022-11-22 re-open by the IPS editor signals that the
original "Considered for Future Use" disposition is no longer the
intended end-state, and the post-STU-update window is the natural
place to add an optional section without breaking conformance for
existing producers.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Instead of standing up a new top-level Nutrition section, address the
underlying patient-safety need by **extending the existing Plan of
Care and/or Allergies & Intolerances sections** with explicit IPS
guidance to include safety-critical nutrition information there:

- In the IPS IG, add narrative guidance to the **Plan of Care**
  section stating that active `NutritionOrder` resources (dysphagia
  diet, fluid restriction, enteral feeding) SHOULD be included as
  entries when present.
- In the **Allergies & Intolerances** section, reinforce that food
  intolerances/allergies remain in scope.
- Optionally introduce a small extension on the Composition
  (`composition-section-nutrition-summary`) carrying a coded "no
  active diet restrictions" / "diet restrictions present" flag to
  give consumers a fast safety check without requiring a full
  section.

#### Justification

This keeps the IPS Composition surface area unchanged — important for
implementers who have just completed STU conformance work — while
still surfacing the safety-critical information the reporter calls
out. It also avoids the terminology-binding work that a new
section.code slice would require, and it sidesteps overlap with the
broader (non-safety) "general healthful diet" content that the
reporter herself acknowledges is *not* needed for unplanned care.
Trade-off vs. Disposition A: implementers must know to look for
nutrition data inside Plan of Care, which is less discoverable than a
dedicated section header.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as **Persuasive with Modification → Not Persuasive** for *this*
release, with the rationale that nutrition-as-a-section is out of
scope for the current IPS update cycle and that safety-critical diet
information is already conveyable today via the **Plan of Care**
section (`NutritionOrder` entries) and the **Allergies and
Intolerances** section (food intolerances). Recommend the requester
file a follow-up with the ISO IPS team for harmonised inclusion in a
future major IPS release.

#### Justification

Defensible on two grounds: (1) the original 2020-02-04 IPS ballot
already disposed of this comment 12-0-1 with the explicit instruction
to route it through ISO IPS, and there is no evidence in the ticket
that ISO IPS has come back asking HL7 to add the section; (2)
`NutritionOrder` is already a permitted entry in `Composition.section`
under existing IPS guidance, so the safety information *can* be
conveyed today — the ticket is really asking for *prominence*, not
*capability*. Adding a new optional section without a coordinated
international value-set for `section.code` risks divergent
implementations that the IPS project has historically tried to avoid.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The 2022-11-22 re-open by the IPS editor is the strongest signal in
the record: it explicitly reverses the earlier "Considered for Future
Use" outcome and places the ticket back in front of Patient Care for
real disposition. The patient-safety justification is concrete,
narrowly scoped (dysphagia diets, fluid restrictions, enteral
feeding), and aligns with the IPS Composition's existing
optional-section pattern, so Disposition A can be delivered without
disturbing the required core or breaking existing producers.
Disposition B is a reasonable fallback if Patient Care decides the
new section.code binding is too costly to harmonise with ISO IPS in
this cycle, but it pays a discoverability cost that undermines the
safety argument — exactly the cost the reporter is trying to avoid.
Disposition C is the weakest path given that the editor has already
chosen to re-open the ticket; declining now would simply reset the
loop. The workgroup should adopt A and engage the IPS terminology
team early on the `section.code` binding so the new optional section
ships with an internationally-acceptable code from day one.
