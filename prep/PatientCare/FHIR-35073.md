## Summary

The reporter (Daniel Vreeman, the LOINC steward) commends the SDOH Clinical
Care IG STU2 update for adopting the US Core terminology pattern of providing
domain-specific ValueSet subsets for `Condition`, `ServiceRequest`, and
`Procedure`. He requests that a comparable ValueSet-based approach be
defined for the IG's **screening/assessment** content (currently delivered
via a supplemental "guide spreadsheet" rather than as a normative
ValueSet artifact). He acknowledges that additional implementer guidance
is needed to traverse from a ValueSet of assessment instruments
(LOINC panel codes) → child item codes → allowed answer values
(LOINC answer lists), but notes the LOINC distribution and the
`fhir.loinc.org` terminology server already expose the structural links
needed to support this. The ticket carries the `block-vote-11` label and
priority Highest, and was reopened post-STU2 publication after a prior
"Considered for Future Use" disposition was reverted.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add normative ValueSet artifacts to the US SDOH Clinical Care IG that
enumerate the screening/assessment content currently published only in
the supplemental guide spreadsheet. Concretely:

1. Define a top-level **`SDOHCC ValueSet Screening Assessment
   Instruments`** ValueSet that includes-by-codeSystem-and-filter the
   LOINC panel codes corresponding to the instruments referenced by the
   IG (e.g., AHC-HRSN, PRAPARE, Hunger Vital Sign, etc.), expressed
   either as an enumerated `compose.include.concept` list or as a
   filter on `LP*` panel ancestry.
2. For each in-scope instrument, define an **item-level ValueSet** that
   binds to `Observation.code` / `Questionnaire.item.code` for the
   instrument's child LOINC items, sourced from the LOINC panel
   hierarchy (i.e., codes whose parent is the instrument's panel
   `LP`/`LL` code).
3. For each instrument item that has a LOINC answer list, expose the
   corresponding answer-list ValueSet (or use LOINC's existing `LL...`
   answer-list ValueSets directly) as the binding for
   `Observation.value[x] (CodeableConcept)` /
   `Questionnaire.item.answerValueSet`.
4. Bind the existing SDOH screening/assessment profiles
   (`SDOHCC-ObservationScreeningResponse`, the related Questionnaire
   profiles) to these new ValueSets at an appropriate strength
   (`extensible` for the instrument-level binding so additional vetted
   instruments can be used; `preferred` or `extensible` for the item
   and answer bindings to permit local LOINC-aligned variants).
5. Add an implementer-guidance page that walks the
   instrument → item → answer traversal, including example
   `$expand`/`$lookup`/`$validate-code` calls against `fhir.loinc.org`.
6. Retire (or mark "informative supplement") the spreadsheet so the
   ValueSets become the single source of truth.

#### Justification

This is exactly the pattern the IG already adopted in STU2 for
`Condition`, `ServiceRequest`, and `Procedure`, so extending it to
screening/assessment content keeps the IG internally consistent and
aligns with the broader US Core direction. ValueSets are the FHIR-native
mechanism for terminology binding; spreadsheets are not machine-validatable
by FHIR validators or terminology servers, so implementers cannot today
get authoritative `$validate-code` or `$expand` answers about the
instruments they are expected to support. The reporter is the LOINC
steward, which directly addresses the prior "we'll revisit when
structural links are in place" concern: the LOINC panel/answer-list
hierarchy and `fhir.loinc.org` already expose those links. Adopting
ValueSets also gives downstream implementers (US Core, USCDI v3, payer
SDOH programs) a stable, referenceable identifier for the instrument
set rather than embedding spreadsheet conventions.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Instead of authoring a curated, IG-maintained set of instrument and
item ValueSets, publish a thin **"intensional ValueSet" layer** plus a
companion **Questionnaire/Library catalog** in the IG:

1. Define a small number of **intensional** SDOH ValueSets that simply
   reference LOINC panel ancestry (e.g.,
   `compose.include.filter` on `parent` / `descendent-of` for the
   appropriate `LP`/`LG` SDOH grouping codes) so the IG never has to
   re-publish item-level enumerations as LOINC evolves.
2. For each in-scope instrument, publish a canonical
   **`Questionnaire`** resource (or reference an externally-maintained
   one) whose `item.code` and `item.answerValueSet` already encode the
   instrument's structure; bind `Observation.code` extensibly to the
   instrument-level ValueSet from (1) and rely on the Questionnaire to
   carry item-level structure (using the SDC
   `questionnaire-itemControl` / `questionnaire` extensions on the
   Observation to tie a response back to its instrument).
3. Provide implementer guidance that the **authoritative traversal is
   `Questionnaire` → `QuestionnaireResponse` → derived `Observation`**,
   and that ValueSets are deliberately intensional/loose so that LOINC
   updates flow through automatically.
4. Keep the existing spreadsheet as a human-readable index, but
   regenerate it from the published Questionnaires/ValueSets so it
   cannot drift.

#### Justification

This addresses the same underlying need — machine-actionable
terminology for SDOH screening/assessment — without committing the IG
to maintain (and republish) deep enumerations of LOINC content that the
LOINC release cycle is better positioned to maintain. It also better
matches how SDOH instruments are actually authored and exchanged in
practice (Questionnaire/QuestionnaireResponse plus derived
Observations, per SDC). The trade-off vs. Disposition A is that
implementers do not get a single flat ValueSet they can `$expand` to
"all SDOH screening item codes"; instead they expand per-instrument or
walk the panel hierarchy. That is arguably more faithful to how the
content is actually used and avoids the IG silently going out of date
when LOINC adds or deprecates panel members.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark this request as **Considered for Future Use / Not Persuasive at
this time** and direct implementers to (a) the existing supplemental
spreadsheet for the curated instrument list and (b) the LOINC panel
hierarchy and `fhir.loinc.org` for programmatic traversal. Decline to
publish IG-maintained ValueSets for screening/assessment content in
this revision.

#### Justification

Defensible primarily on scope and maintenance grounds: the LOINC
distribution already encodes the instrument → item → answer-list
relationships, `fhir.loinc.org` exposes them via standard FHIR
terminology operations, and any IG-maintained enumeration risks drift
from the upstream LOINC release. Authoring and ballot-quality-checking
a comprehensive set of instrument-level and item-level ValueSets is
non-trivial work for the WG. This was in fact the WG's previous
position (vote 7-1-0, June 2022) before being reverted. The downside
is real, however: implementers and validators cannot today consume the
spreadsheet, and deferring again invites the same ballot comment in
the next cycle from the same (authoritative) reporter.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter is the LOINC steward and is explicitly stating that the
LOINC structural links the WG previously cited as a blocker are now in
place and reachable via `fhir.loinc.org`. The IG already adopted
exactly this pattern in STU2 for `Condition`, `ServiceRequest`, and
`Procedure`, so extending it to screening/assessment is a consistency
fix, not a new design. Continuing to ship the instrument list only as a
supplemental spreadsheet leaves SDOH screening data outside the
machine-validatable terminology surface that the rest of the IG (and
US Core) now relies on, and Disposition C has already been tried and
reopened. Disposition B is attractive but is better treated as an
*implementation* of A (use intensional LOINC-anchored ValueSets where
possible, enumerate where LOINC ancestry is insufficient) rather than
as a substitute — the WG should accept the request and let editorial
work decide, per ValueSet, between intensional filters and explicit
enumerations.
