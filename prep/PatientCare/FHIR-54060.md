## Summary

The reporter (Brian D. Handspicker), backed by community-based organization
(CBO) representatives, argues that the newly proposed *capacity status*
values in the 2026-Jan ballot of the US SDOH Clinical Care IG do not align
with how socialcare CBOs actually triage incoming referrals. Capacity to
deliver a socialcare service is rarely a binary "available / unavailable":
CBOs run intake screening, eligibility checks, waitlist management, and
funder-driven workflow constraints, so a simple capacity-status answer is
often misleading. The ticket asks the Gravity / Patient Care work group
either to (1) remove all capacity status values from the SDOH E3 STU
ballot, or (2) add a value that explicitly represents "requires (a
referral for) further screening and assessment", so a CBO can answer a
capacity check truthfully when more work is needed before capacity can be
determined.

The Gravity Team has accepted the request as **Persuasive with
Modification**: they agree to add a new concept meaning "requires further
assessment" into the *SDOHCC Capacity Status* value set (via the SDOHCC
Temporary Code System), and to move both the
`SDOHCC-ExtensionHealthcareServiceCapacityStatus` extension and the
`SDOHCC-HealthcareServiceForReferralManagement` profile to **draft**
status to acknowledge low maturity. Open disagreement remains on the
exact display term: Gravity has proposed `additional assessment required`
(modelled on SNOMED US patterns such as `428981000124101 | Additional
medication therapy required (finding)`); the reporter rejects that
wording, insisting the concept must convey `requires a referral for
further assessment`, because the responding CBO has not yet performed any
socialcare-compliant assessment and needs a *referral* in order to bill
for the assessment work.

No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were
returned by the augury index for this ticket key.

## Details

**Description:**

The capacity to deliver a specific socialcare service can vary
significantly due to numerous factors. The capacity to accept a
socialcare referral depends on the individualized service that is being
delivered and cannot be tracked or documented. Socialcare organizations
would be unable to implement this specification because of technical,
structural, and administrative barriers. As a result, socialcare
community-based organizations (CBOs) commonly screen participants to
make informed decisions regarding which services or programs are the
most suitable, taking into account availability, client demographics,
income, and other eligibility criteria. Very few services can be simply
classified as "available" or "unavailable." Additionally, many
organizations maintain waiting lists with specific criteria to
prioritize moving clients off their lists as funds or slots become
available. A lack of a referral could mean that the client is not placed
on the waiting list, further extending the time they will wait for
specific social support. Socialcare professionals specialize in
evaluating client needs and comparing them to a myriad of complex
programs with multiple layers of eligibility to find the best fit for
each client's situation and individual preferences.

**Resolution Description (Persuasive with Modification):**

The Gravity Team agrees to create a `CodeableConcept` representing
"requires further assessment" in the *SDOHCC Capacity Status* value set,
and to move the
[`SDOHCC-ExtensionHealthcareServiceCapacityStatus`](https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/fhir-sdoh-clinicalcare/en/StructureDefinition-SDOHCC-ExtensionHealthcareServiceCapacityStatus.html)
extension and the
[`SDOHCC-HealthcareServiceForReferralManagement`](https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/fhir-sdoh-clinicalcare/en/StructureDefinition-SDOHCC-HealthcareServiceForReferralManagement.html)
profile to *draft* status.

**Comments:**

- **JIRAUSER29740 (2026-01-15)** — Among CBOs they work with, two paths
  would resolve the issue: (a) remove all capacity status values from
  the 2026-Jan SDOH E3 STU, or (b) add a value such as "needs further
  screening and assessment" to the capacity status values.

- **JIRAUSER24235 (2026-01-16)** — Argues it is impossible to "simply"
  determine CBO capacity from a screen and referral; capacity requires
  (1) full assessment of need, (2) eligibility determination, and
  (3) availability/waitlist evaluation. Notes that screens and
  assessments being treated as the same workflow under FHIR is
  inconsistent with how upstream drivers are managed in the CBO
  ecosystem. Recommends removing all capacity status values from the
  2026-Jan SDOH E3 STU ballot AND adding a value like "needs further
  screening and assessment" or "referral for further assessment".

- **JIRAUSER24103 / Gravity Team (2026-02-25)** — Acknowledges the
  complexity of socialcare workflows but notes some entities do enable
  CBOs to perform capacity management. Agrees to add a `CodeableConcept`
  representing "requires further assessment" to the *SDOHCC Capacity
  Status* value set; suggests `CapacityNotes` (free-text element on the
  extension) for additional nuance such as waitlist duration; commits to
  adding narrative and example workflows.

- **JIRAUSER16901 (2026-04-01)** — Confirms a new concept will be added
  to the SDOH CC Temporary Code System for "requires further assessment"
  (Rob McLure suggested "will assess"); raises an FHIR-I question about
  using *maturity* as a lever to signal the experimental nature of
  capacity status.

- **JIRAUSER24103 (2026-04-03)** — Proposes the term `additional
  assessment required` for the new concept.

- **JIRAUSER24103 (2026-04-03)** — Defends the wording: it aligns with
  the SNOMED US Extension precedent (e.g., `428981000124101 | Additional
  medication therapy required (finding)`); clarifies the term is used
  by the entity *responding to* the capacity status check, not by the
  referral source.

- **bdhandspicker (2026-04-09)** — Strenuously objects to "additional
  […] required" wording: (1) "additional assessment required" implies a
  socialcare-compliant assessment has already been performed, which is
  unlikely on first receipt of a healthcare → socialcare referral;
  (2) what is actually needed is a *referral for further assessment* so
  the CBO can bill for the comprehensive assessment. Argues the
  semantics required are specifically "requires **a referral** for
  further assessment".

- **JIRAUSER24103 (2026-04-10)** — Replies: (1) sees no demonstrable
  difference between "additional" and "further"; (2) wants to align with
  an existing SNOMED-published pattern; (3) considers "requires a
  referral for further assessment" too narrow — prefers a broader
  concept covering multiple capacity-response use cases; (4) notes the
  binding strength is *extensible*, so implementers can add other
  concepts as needed.

- **bdhandspicker (2026-04-10)** — Maintains that "additional assessment
  required" is not what was agreed in the reconciliation meeting and is
  not an acceptable resolution to his negative ballot comment;
  emphasises that "further assessment required" is not a *finding* but
  a follow-on request.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt exactly the wording the reporter has insisted on. In the *SDOHCC
Temporary Code System* add a new concept whose display is
**"Requires a referral for further assessment"** (definition:
"The responding healthcare/social-care service cannot determine capacity
to accept the referral until a comprehensive assessment of the subject
has been performed; a referral for that further assessment is
required."). Add this concept to the *SDOHCC Capacity Status* value set,
and keep that value set bound to
`SDOHCC-ExtensionHealthcareServiceCapacityStatus.value[x]` as
`extensible`. Per the existing resolution, also move both
`SDOHCC-ExtensionHealthcareServiceCapacityStatus` and
`SDOHCC-HealthcareServiceForReferralManagement` to
`draft` (lower their FMM / standards-status), and add narrative
illustrating CBO intake, screening-vs-assessment, eligibility and
waitlist workflows so the experimental nature of capacity status is
clear.

#### Justification

This is the wording the reporter has explicitly asked for in his
4-09 and 4-10 comments and matches what JIRAUSER24235 and JIRAUSER29740
(the CBO voices) describe as the real-world workflow: a CBO that
receives a capacity-status check from a healthcare referral source
typically has not yet performed a socialcare-compliant assessment and
needs to *generate a referral* in order to bill for that assessment.
Saying "requires a referral for further assessment" preserves the
follow-on-request semantics the reporter is defending and avoids the
implication (which "additional assessment required" carries) that an
initial assessment has already been done. The `extensible` binding
remains, so other implementers can still add broader codes if they
need them. Lowering the maturity to `draft` directly addresses
JIRAUSER16901's open FHIR-I question about using maturity as a signal
for the experimental nature of this capability.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Use the broader concept Gravity has been advocating for, but disambiguate
it with display + definition wording that captures both readings. In the
*SDOHCC Temporary Code System*, add a single concept with:

- **code:** `further-assessment-required` (or a stable opaque code such
  as `cap-fa-req`)
- **display:** **"Further assessment required"**
- **definition:** "The responding service cannot yet determine capacity
  to accept this referral; the prospective subject must first undergo
  further assessment (which may itself require a referral) before a
  capacity decision can be made."

Add it to the *SDOHCC Capacity Status* value set with the existing
`extensible` binding. Keep `CapacityNotes` (free-text element on
`SDOHCC-ExtensionHealthcareServiceCapacityStatus`) as the documented
mechanism for stating *what kind* of further assessment is needed,
whether a referral is required, and any waitlist/eligibility nuance.
Per the existing resolution, also move
`SDOHCC-ExtensionHealthcareServiceCapacityStatus` and
`SDOHCC-HealthcareServiceForReferralManagement` to `draft`, and add the
narrative + example workflows Gravity has already committed to.

#### Justification

This bridges the two camps. Gravity's concern that "requires a referral
for further assessment" is too narrow is reasonable — there are CBO
workflows where the next step is an internal assessment, not a formal
external referral, and a single capacity-status code should not
prejudge that. The reporter's concern that "additional assessment
required" implies an assessment has already been done is also
linguistically valid; "further assessment required" sidesteps that
implication while still using the SNOMED-style "[…] required" phrasing
that Gravity is comfortable mapping onto. Pushing the
"referral-vs-internal-assessment" detail into `CapacityNotes` matches
how that free-text field was originally introduced in this ticket's
discussion. Trade-off: it does not literally satisfy the reporter's
wording demand, so the negative ballot may not be withdrawn — but the
*semantics* he is defending are preserved.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (in the strong form: remove capacity status from this STU)

#### Proposal

Honour the reporter's first-listed remediation: **remove all *capacity
status* values from the 2026-Jan SDOH E3 STU**, retire (or mark as
`draft`/withdrawn) the
`SDOHCC-ExtensionHealthcareServiceCapacityStatus` extension and the
*SDOHCC Capacity Status* value set for this ballot cycle, and either
defer `SDOHCC-HealthcareServiceForReferralManagement` to a future
release or strip its capacity-status binding. Document the deferral in
the IG with a pointer back to FHIR-54060 and an explicit invitation for
CBO/Gravity collaboration on a proper capacity model in the next
ballot.

#### Justification

Both negative-ballot CBO comments (JIRAUSER29740 and JIRAUSER24235)
explicitly listed "remove all capacity status values from the 2026-Jan
SDOH E3 STU" as their *first* preferred resolution; only as a fallback
did they ask for a "needs further screening and assessment" code. The
substantive critique is that the model itself — a single status code on
a `HealthcareService` — does not represent how CBO capacity is actually
determined (eligibility tiers, funder rules, waitlists, screen-vs-
assessment distinction). A new code papered onto a fundamentally
mis-shaped model risks codifying a workflow CBOs cannot implement, and
later breaking changes are likely once a proper model is developed.
Withdrawing for this STU is conservative and avoids locking in a debate
that the reconciliation meeting itself did not settle.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **B**

The reconciliation history makes A and C politically expensive in
opposite directions. C reopens an item the work group has already
*agreed* to resolve as Persuasive with Modification and would surprise
implementers who have built to the 2026-Jan ballot; the reporter
himself listed it only as one of two options. A literally adopts the
reporter's wording, but Gravity has consistently said it is too narrow
to cover the broader set of capacity-response use cases — accepting A
risks Gravity reopening the ticket from the other side after the
reconciliation. Disposition B preserves the *substance* of what the
reporter is defending (no implication that an assessment has already
been done; a clear "more assessment is needed before we can answer
capacity" semantic), aligns with Gravity's preference for a broader
concept and the SNOMED-style "[…] required" phrasing, and uses the
already-agreed `CapacityNotes` element to carry the
"referral-vs-internal-assessment" nuance the reporter wants to make
explicit. Combined with the already-agreed move of
`SDOHCC-ExtensionHealthcareServiceCapacityStatus` and
`SDOHCC-HealthcareServiceForReferralManagement` to `draft`, this gives
implementers room to learn from real CBO deployments before the model
hardens, which is exactly the maturity-as-a-lever signal
JIRAUSER16901 asked FHIR-I about.
