## Summary

The reporter (Corey Spears) observes an inconsistency between the
`ConditionDefinition` and `Condition` resources: `ConditionDefinition.bodySite`
is constrained to `0..1`, while an instance `Condition.bodySite` can carry
multiple body sites. The reporter sees no design rationale for limiting a
*definition* of a condition to a single body site when an *instance* of that
condition can have many, and asks the workgroup to align the two cardinalities.

The discussion in comments has broadened beyond the literal request. The
related ticket [FHIR-55388](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-55388) ("Change
bodyStructrure to CodeableReference") proposes converting `bodyStructure` to a
`CodeableReference(BodyStructure)` and deprecating `bodySite` more generally,
which directly affects what shape any cardinality change to ConditionDefinition
should take. At the Patient Care call on 2026-01-22 and the joint PC/OCC
session on 2026-01-26-30 (Wed Q4), three patterns were enumerated:

- **Option 1** — single `CodeableReference 0..1` (cannot carry multiple
  CodeableConcepts).
- **Option 2** — `CodeableReference 0..*` (creates two ways to express
  multiple body structures: repeating the element vs. using
  `BodyStructure.includedStructure`).
- **Option 3** — two elements with an invariant preventing both being
  populated: `CodeableConcept 0..*` + `Reference 0..1` (the action item from
  the OO minutes was to "capture ticket for changes to OO resources in
  alignment with option 3").

Workgroup opinion is split: Rob favours `0..1`; Emma and Stephen favour `0..*`;
Stephen would constrain the `0..*` form with an invariant that the Reference
form remain `0..1`. Amit cautioned that consistency alone is a weak driver
when implementers are affected, but noted that ConditionDefinition is
maturity 0 with minimal deployment, lowering the cost of change here.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Change `ConditionDefinition.bodySite` cardinality from `0..1` to `0..*`,
keeping the existing `CodeableConcept` data type unchanged. This is a
narrow, surgical edit to the ConditionDefinition resource definition that
brings it into alignment with `Condition.bodySite` (which is already `0..*`)
and matches the literal ask in the ticket. No other resources are touched
and no invariants are added.

#### Justification

This is the minimum change that resolves the asymmetry the reporter
identified. ConditionDefinition is a maturity-0 resource with minimal
deployed implementations (per Amit on the 2026-01-22 PC call), so
cardinality widening carries little implementer cost. The change is
backward compatible: existing instances with at most one bodySite remain
valid. Rob's argument that Condition and ConditionDefinition are "pairs"
where consistency is warranted directly supports this disposition. The
broader CodeableReference / deprecation debate from FHIR-55388 is left
untouched — this disposition does not foreclose any of the options being
weighed there.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Defer FHIR-50481 and resolve it as part of FHIR-55388. Replace
`ConditionDefinition.bodySite` (`CodeableConcept 0..1`) with
`bodyStructure 0..* CodeableReference(BodyStructure)` and mark the
existing `bodySite` element as deprecated, mirroring whatever final shape
PC adopts for `Condition.bodySite` under FHIR-55388. The ConditionDefinition
edit becomes a downstream consequence of the Condition decision rather
than a standalone change. If the workgroup ultimately picks Option 3
from the WGM minutes (separate `CodeableConcept 0..*` + `Reference 0..1`
with an invariant), apply that same pattern to ConditionDefinition.

#### Justification

The 2026-01-22 PC minutes describe `bodyStructure 0..1
CodeableReference(BodyStructure)` as the "ideal pattern", and Michelle's
comment on FHIR-55388 explicitly flags ConditionDefinition for parallel
treatment. Resolving FHIR-50481 in isolation risks landing a cardinality
change on `bodySite` that PC then has to revisit (and possibly revert or
restructure) when FHIR-55388 lands a data-type / deprecation change on
the same element. A single coordinated edit is cheaper for the spec
editors and clearer for implementers. Stephen's invariant pattern (limit
the Reference form to 0..1 even when the CodeableConcept form is 0..*)
neatly handles jlyle's concern that arbitrary multi-site cases should
fall back to `BodyStructure.includedStructure` rather than repeating the
element.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Reject the cardinality alignment. Keep `ConditionDefinition.bodySite` at
`0..1` and direct authors who need to define a condition with multiple
body sites to either (a) define multiple narrower ConditionDefinitions,
or (b) reference a `BodyStructure` resource with multiple
`includedStructure` entries — the same pattern jlyle defends for Condition
itself. Close FHIR-50481 as "Not Persuasive" with a pointer to FHIR-55388
for any further bodySite/bodyStructure design work.

#### Justification

jlyle's comment lays out the strongest case for this position: the
single-code design is deliberately simple, and adding cardinality just to
handle edge cases sets a precedent for further model stratification
(e.g., laterality next). A *definition* of a condition arguably *should*
be narrower than an *instance* — definitions are templates, and a
template that pins one site is more useful for downstream constraint and
validation than one that admits any number. Rob's stated preference for
`0..1` and his discomfort with invariants align with this reading. Amit's
caution that consistency-for-consistency's-sake is a weak justification
also supports declining absent concrete use cases for multi-site
ConditionDefinitions.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The substantive PC/OCC discussion has clearly moved past the narrow
cardinality question. FHIR-55388 is in flight and explicitly tags
ConditionDefinition for parallel review; the WGM action item on Option 3
points at a coordinated solution across OO/PC resources. Landing
Disposition A now would touch the exact same element that FHIR-55388 is
about to restructure, almost guaranteeing a second edit (and likely a
second round of implementer notification) in the same ballot cycle.
Disposition C is defensible on simplicity grounds but ignores both Rob's
"pairs" argument and the practical reality that ConditionDefinition's
maturity-0 status makes alignment cheap. Disposition B keeps FHIR-50481
open as the tracking ticket for the ConditionDefinition piece of the
broader bodyStructure work, ensures the cardinality, data-type, and
naming questions are resolved together, and lets PC adopt whichever of
Options 1–3 it ultimately settles on without re-litigating
ConditionDefinition separately.
