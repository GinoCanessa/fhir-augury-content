## Summary

The ticket asks the IPS (International Patient Summary) Implementation Guide
to broaden the target types of the `participant-uv-ips` extension's
`valueReference` from `Reference(PractitionerRole | Organization)` to
`Reference(Practitioner | PractitionerRole | Organization)`. The reporter,
Ken Sinn, argues that when no `PractitionerRole`-specific information is
available (e.g. `PractitionerRole.code`, `Practitioner.organization`),
forcing senders to wrap a bare `Practitioner` in an otherwise empty
`PractitionerRole` "shell" adds bulk without information value.

Important context: the closely related ticket
[FHIR-23887](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-23887) — *Revise the participant
element* (Patient Care, IPS) — was resolved Persuasive with Modification on
2019-11-12 and **removed the `participant-uv-ips` extension from IPS
altogether**, in favour of `Patient.contact`, `Patient.generalPractitioner`,
and `CareTeam`. The change was pre-applied in
`HL7/fhir-ips@689e0e1dd089b291422a89b7a40142e6d235185d`. FHIR-25012 was first
resolved *Considered for Future Use* on 2020-02-04 by Hausam/Zautke (12-0-1)
on the basis that "this comment doesn't apply anymore based on decision on
item #23887". That resolution was reverted on 2022-11-22 by Lynn Laakso to
re-open the issue "post STU update publication", which is why it is back in
the Triaged queue today. The underlying artifact the ticket targets, however,
no longer exists in the published IPS specification.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Re-introduce the `participant-uv-ips` extension target list (or reopen the
relevant slot on whatever element supersedes it) and broaden
`Extension.valueReference` from `Reference(PractitionerRole | Organization)`
to `Reference(Practitioner | PractitionerRole | Organization)` in the IPS IG.
Concretely this means editing the StructureDefinition for
`participant-uv-ips` (historically at
`http://hl7.org/fhir/uv/ips/StructureDefinition/participant-uv-ips`) under
`HL7/fhir-ips`, regenerating snapshots, updating any narrative/intro
referencing the participant extension, and adjusting examples that previously
nested a `Practitioner` inside a `PractitionerRole`.

#### Justification

The reporter's underlying point is editorially sound: where no
`PractitionerRole`-specific data exists, requiring senders to construct a
hollow `PractitionerRole` wrapper around a `Practitioner` is wasteful and
inconsistent with how most FHIR base resources allow `Reference(Practitioner
| PractitionerRole | Organization)` triplets (e.g. `Encounter.participant`,
`CarePlan.author`, `ServiceRequest.requester`). Aligning with that
established pattern is low-risk for implementers and consistent with FHIR
design practice.

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep IPS aligned with the FHIR-23887 outcome and **do not reinstate the
participant extension**. Instead, publish IG-level guidance (and worked
examples) clarifying that "non-patient participants" in an IPS are conveyed
via `Patient.contact` (relationship-based contacts), `Patient.generalPractitioner`
(`Reference(Organization | Practitioner | PractitionerRole)` — already
supports bare `Practitioner`), or `CareTeam` (with
`CareTeam.participant.member` already supporting `Practitioner`). If the WG
agrees that *reference-target permissiveness* is what the reporter actually
needs, verify those three pathways already accept bare `Practitioner` (they
do at the FHIR R4/R5 base level) and document the mapping in the IPS
narrative rather than reviving the deprecated extension.

#### Justification

FHIR-23887 explicitly removed the `participant-uv-ips` extension and replaced
it with mechanisms that *already* allow `Reference(Practitioner)`. Solving
FHIR-25012 by reinstating an extension that has since been superseded would
unwind a 12-0-1 ballot decision and re-introduce parallel, redundant ways to
say the same thing. Documentation guidance avoids the breaking change while
delivering the reporter's actual ergonomic ask (no need to wrap a bare
`Practitioner` in a `PractitionerRole` shell).

### Disposition C: Decline — *Not Persuasive (already addressed)*

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-25012 as *Not Persuasive — already addressed by FHIR-23887*. The
`participant-uv-ips` extension that this ticket targets was removed from IPS
as the resolution to FHIR-23887, and the use case (referencing a bare
`Practitioner`) is fully supported today via `Patient.generalPractitioner`
and `CareTeam.participant.member`. No spec change is required. This is
effectively a re-affirmation of the original 2020-02-04 *Considered for
Future Use* vote, updated to reflect that the change has now actually
shipped.

#### Justification

The artifact the ticket asks to modify no longer exists in the published IPS
IG. Reopening it post-STU (as Lynn Laakso did in 2022) restored it to the
queue procedurally, but the substantive request is moot: the reporter wanted
to avoid an empty `PractitionerRole` shell, and IPS already provides
shell-free pathways to reference a `Practitioner`. The 2020 vote (12-0-1)
already reached this conclusion; reaffirming it is the cleanest outcome and
keeps IPS aligned with FHIR-23887.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** C

The original Patient Care vote (12-0-1) was correct and remains correct: the
`participant-uv-ips` extension was removed in the FHIR-23887 resolution and
the reporter's ergonomic concern — having to wrap a `Practitioner` in an
empty `PractitionerRole` — is fully resolved by the replacement mechanisms
(`Patient.generalPractitioner` and `CareTeam.participant.member`, both of
which already accept `Reference(Practitioner)` at the FHIR base). Disposition
A would unwind a settled, balloted decision to modify an extension that no
longer exists in IPS, and Disposition B does useful documentation work but
overstates the gap — the existing IPS guidance is already consistent with
those pathways. The cleanest, least-disruptive outcome is to close
FHIR-25012 as *Not Persuasive — already addressed by FHIR-23887*, optionally
adding a one-line cross-reference in the IPS *Participant* narrative pointing
readers to `Patient.generalPractitioner` / `CareTeam` so future readers
landing on the deprecated 2019SEP extension URL understand where the
functionality went.
