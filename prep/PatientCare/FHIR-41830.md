## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Rewrite the **Step 5** subsection of the *Methodology* page in the International Birth And Child Model IG so that it explicitly states:

1. **What Step 5 produces** as a distinct artefact (e.g., a FHIR-resource-mapping view, a profile selection, a binding view) — using a single one-sentence definition at the top of the section.
2. **How the Step 5 artefact differs from the Step 3 logical model**, with a concrete example drawn from the IG (e.g., a Step 3 logical element shown alongside its Step 5 mapping to a FHIR resource and any constrained extensions).
3. **Why** the additional step is required by the methodology — typically because Step 3 captures *clinical* relationships in implementation-neutral form while Step 5 is the FHIR-paradigm-specific projection (resource selection, profiling, terminology binding decisions).

If the editorial review confirms the reporter's reading — that Step 5 *is* in fact a redundant restatement of Step 3 — collapse Step 5 into Step 3 (or into a Step 3.x sub-step) and renumber the subsequent steps.

#### Justification

The reporter is a domain expert raising a legitimate readability concern at "Highest" priority during an IG review window. The ask is editorial, not normative: it does not change the IG's conformance surface or any artifact, only the methodology narrative. Tightening the Step 3 vs. Step 5 distinction (or merging them) directly addresses the reporter's concern, lowers the cognitive load for future readers, and is consistent with how analogous "Step N unclear" tickets (e.g., FHIR-39898, FHIR-22037) are typically resolved against other IGs. Because there is no spec/repo briefing available for this IG, the work group should also use this ticket to confirm the IG's authoring repo so the change can actually be applied.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep both Step 3 and Step 5 in the methodology, but **restructure the Methodology page** rather than rewrite Step 5 in isolation:

- Add a short "Relationship between the steps" preface that visualises the methodology as a pipeline: clinical concept → logical model (Step 3) → … → FHIR projection (Step 5) → conformance artefacts.
- Annotate each step with **Input / Output / Distinct purpose** bullets so any overlap (or genuine redundancy) becomes structurally obvious to the reader and to future maintainers.
- For Step 5 specifically, add a worked example (one resource end-to-end) that the reader can follow from Step 3's logical element through Step 5's resource mapping.

If, after this restructuring, Step 5 still has no Input/Output that is distinct from Step 3, fall back to Disposition A (merge or remove).

#### Justification

The reporter's complaint is symptomatic of a broader problem common to methodology pages: individual steps are described in isolation, so readers can't see whether each step has a distinct contribution. A page-level restructuring fixes the underlying readability issue once and prevents the same complaint from recurring against Steps 4, 6, etc. Trade-off vs. Disposition A: this is more editorial work for the IG authors and slightly delays the targeted fix the reporter asked for, but it produces a more durable result and gives the work group a defensible structure for future ballot review.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket as **Not Persuasive** with the rationale that Step 5 has a distinct, intended purpose in the methodology — namely, the FHIR-paradigm-specific projection of the Step 3 logical model into resources, profiles, and terminology bindings — and that this is already documented elsewhere in the IG (e.g., the introduction or a referenced design rationale). Add a short clarifying sentence at the head of Step 5 pointing to that existing rationale, but make no substantive change.

#### Justification

If the IG authors believe Step 5 is in fact distinct and adequately described — and that the reporter's confusion is an isolated reading rather than a systemic clarity problem — a Decline keeps the methodology stable and avoids editorial churn during the ballot cycle. This disposition is only defensible if (a) the existing Step 5 text genuinely differentiates itself from Step 3 on re-reading, and (b) the work group confirms no other reviewers raised the same concern. Given the ticket is "Highest" priority and the description is specific (not a vague "I don't get it"), this disposition carries reputational risk: a Declined Highest-priority Technical Correction from a domain-expert reviewer typically generates follow-up tickets.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter's question is concrete and actionable: *what is the added value of Step 5 over Step 3*. The cheapest, most direct way to answer it is exactly what the reporter requested — make Step 5 more concrete, explicitly contrast it with Step 3, and (if the contrast genuinely cannot be drawn) merge it. Disposition B is attractive but is a larger editorial undertaking that the work group may not have capacity for in this ballot cycle; it can be pursued as a follow-up if multiple Step-N tickets accumulate. Disposition C is hard to defend against a "Highest" priority Technical Correction with no comments arguing the other side and no evidence in the cross-reference index of prior consensus that Step 5 is fine as-is. Before committing to A, the work group should also use this ticket as a prompt to (i) confirm where the IG's methodology page is actually authored (no repo briefing is currently available for this IG in the Augury cache) and (ii) check whether FHIR-41968 and any sibling tickets from the same review window should be addressed in the same editorial pass.
