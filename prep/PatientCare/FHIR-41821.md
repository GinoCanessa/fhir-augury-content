## Summary

The reporter notes an internal inconsistency in the International Birth and
Child Model (IBCM) IG: the first paragraph of the "In scope" section reads as
though the child *after* birth is out of scope, yet other parts of the
specification (e.g., section 5.1.1) clearly cover the post-birth child. The
reporter requests that the IG clarify, throughout the specification, whether
only the fetus is in scope or whether the child after birth is also in scope —
and, if the latter, until what age. They also ask that the IG make explicit
which terms (fetus, newborn, child, etc.) are used for each life-stage
referenced in the spec. No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or
GitHub items were surfaced for this ticket, so the request stands on its own.
The reporter subsequently commented that this should be classified as a
*Change Request* rather than a *Technical Correction*, but they could not
amend the type after submission.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Revise the IBCM IG narrative to (1) state explicitly, in the "In scope"
section, that the IG covers both the fetus *and* the child after birth, with
a defined upper age boundary (e.g., the end of the neonatal period — 28 days
post-term — or, if the workgroup intends broader coverage, through early
childhood); and (2) add a short *Terminology / Definitions* subsection (or
extend the existing glossary) that fixes the meaning of the life-stage terms
used throughout the IG: *fetus*, *newborn / neonate*, *infant*, *child*. Then
sweep the rest of the IG (including section 5.1.1 and any other sections that
reference the post-birth subject) to align wording with these definitions.

#### Justification

The reporter has identified a genuine internal contradiction between the
"In scope" statement and downstream content such as section 5.1.1. Resolving
the contradiction by tightening the scope statement and pinning down
terminology is the lowest-risk, highest-clarity fix: it does not change any
profile, extension, or value-set semantics, and it directly answers the two
questions the reporter raised ("is the post-birth child in scope?" and "what
terms are used?"). Establishing canonical life-stage definitions is
consistent with how other HL7 IGs (e.g., Patient Care's other narrative IGs)
disambiguate overlapping demographic terms.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than expanding the "In scope" statement to include the post-birth
child, *remove the post-birth content from the IG* (or move it into a
clearly-marked "future work / informative" appendix) so the IG's scope can
remain narrowly focused on the antenatal/fetal period. Update section 5.1.1
and any other sections that currently reference the post-birth child to
either be reworded as fetal-only or be relocated to the appendix. Add a
short paragraph that points implementers to other Patient Care artifacts
(e.g., the base Patient resource and existing newborn/infant profiles) for
post-birth modeling.

#### Justification

If the workgroup's original intent was indeed for the IG to be fetus-only,
then the post-birth references in section 5.1.1 are the bug, not the scope
statement. Narrowing the IG keeps it tightly scoped, avoids overlap with
existing newborn/infant work elsewhere in Patient Care, and prevents the
IG from having to define and maintain age-band terminology it may not need.
The trade-off vs. Disposition A is that any implementer already relying on
the post-birth content would need to migrate, so this option is only
defensible if such reliance is known to be minimal.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change on the basis that the IG's scope is already adequately
expressed and that the apparent contradiction is a matter of reading
emphasis, not substance — the "In scope" paragraph describes the IG's
*primary* focus (the fetus / pregnancy), while sections such as 5.1.1
necessarily touch the child immediately after birth as part of the
continuum of perinatal care. Note this resolution in a short clarifying
sentence in the next publication if desired, but make no structural
changes.

#### Justification

This option is only defensible if the workgroup believes the existing text
is unambiguous to the target audience and that adding explicit age-range
language risks over-constraining the IG (e.g., locking it to a specific
upper bound that not all jurisdictions share). It avoids re-opening
terminology debates and keeps the IG stable. The reporter's concern is
real, however, so declining outright should be paired with at least a
narrative clarification.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter's observation is concrete and verifiable: the "In scope"
narrative and section 5.1.1 do not currently agree, and there is no
glossary entry that pins down which life-stage terms are in use. Disposition
A resolves both halves of the request with a narrative-only change — no
profile, extension, or terminology binding is affected — which makes it
low-risk for implementers and easy to ballot. Disposition B (narrowing the
IG to fetus-only) is a much larger semantic change and should only be
chosen if the workgroup affirmatively decides the post-birth content was
included in error; absent that decision, A is safer. Disposition C
(decline) does not address the contradiction and would likely re-surface
as a duplicate ticket. Recommend the workgroup adopt A and, as part of
that work, decide and record the upper age boundary (neonatal period vs.
early childhood) so the terminology subsection has a definite anchor.
