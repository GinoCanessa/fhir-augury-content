## Summary

The ticket proposes removing the `note` element from the `ServiceRequest`
profile in the US Physical Activity (PA) FHIR IG. The reporter (Lloyd
McKenzie) argues that carrying narrative notes on both `ServiceRequest`
and `Task` is redundant and unhelpful: `Task` is the more granular
artifact in the PA workflow and is already written to by both providers
and care managers, making it the natural home for free-text notes about
the work being performed. The ticket has a resolution of **Persuasive**
and a label of **Ready-For-Vote**, indicating the Patient Care project
group has already endorsed the direction and the change is queued for
formal disposition. There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip
threads, or GitHub items currently on file for this ticket.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Remove `ServiceRequest.note` from the US Physical Activity IG's
`ServiceRequest` profile by constraining its cardinality to `0..0` (or
by dropping it from the differential and adding an explicit "must not be
populated" constraint). Update the profile's narrative and any IG
guidance pages that describe how implementers should record narrative
information about a physical-activity service order, redirecting them to
`Task.note` on the corresponding `Task` resource. Update any conformance
examples that currently populate `ServiceRequest.note` so that the
equivalent text appears on `Task.note` instead. No changes to the base
FHIR `ServiceRequest` resource are implied — this is a profile-level
restriction within the USPA IG only.

#### Justification

The Patient Care project group has already reviewed and approved the
change ("Persuasive", `Ready-For-Vote`), so accepting as requested
matches the working consensus. Functionally, the IG models the
provider/care-manager interaction primarily through `Task`, and
`Task.note` is the element those actors are already expected to write
to; allowing `ServiceRequest.note` in parallel creates two valid
locations for the same narrative content, which fragments
implementations and makes downstream consumers guess where to read.
Constraining the element at the profile level (rather than the base
resource) preserves the general FHIR pattern while enforcing the
USPA-specific workflow.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Instead of forbidding `ServiceRequest.note` outright, retain the element
but mark it `0..0` only on the *active* USPA `ServiceRequest` profile
that is fulfilled by a `Task`, and add explicit IG guidance describing
the division of responsibility: `ServiceRequest.note` may be used for
"order-time" narrative supplied by the requester at the moment the
service is requested (rationale, requester caveats), while `Task.note`
is the canonical location for ongoing operational notes from providers
and care managers as the work progresses. If the project group prefers
the cleaner one-location model, the same guidance page can simply state
that the IG places all narrative on `Task.note` and that
`ServiceRequest.note` SHOULD NOT be used, without an absolute `0..0`
constraint — preserving forward compatibility for senders that already
populate it.

#### Justification

A `MustSupport = false` / `SHOULD NOT use` guidance approach is less
disruptive for implementers who may already be populating
`ServiceRequest.note` based on the base resource. It also keeps the door
open for legitimate "order-time" rationale that does not naturally
belong to the fulfilment `Task`. The trade-off versus Disposition A is
that two valid carriers continue to exist in the wire format, which
re-introduces some of the ambiguity the ticket is trying to eliminate
and makes validators less able to detect misuse.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Leave `ServiceRequest.note` in place in the USPA `ServiceRequest`
profile and close this ticket as **Not Persuasive** on the grounds that
having narrative on both resources is consistent with the base FHIR
specification and that distinguishing "order-level notes" from
"task-level notes" can be clinically useful. Document the convention in
IG narrative rather than removing the element.

#### Justification

`ServiceRequest.note` and `Task.note` exist independently in base FHIR
because they describe distinct artifacts (the order vs. the work).
Removing the element at the profile level prevents senders from
recording context that legitimately belongs to the order itself (for
example, the rationale supplied by the requester before any `Task` has
been instantiated). However, this disposition is hard to defend given
that the Patient Care project group has already endorsed removal as
"Persuasive".

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The Patient Care project group has already reviewed and approved the
change on 2024-02-06 and the ticket is labelled `Ready-For-Vote` with a
resolution of `Persuasive`, so the workgroup direction is clear.
Disposition A implements that direction cleanly with a profile-level
`0..0` constraint, which gives implementers an unambiguous,
machine-checkable rule and eliminates the duplicate narrative carrier
that the ticket flags. Disposition B's softer "SHOULD NOT" guidance is
worth keeping in reserve if implementer feedback during the vote
indicates real-world need for order-time narrative, but in the absence
of any such feedback (no Zulip threads, no linked tickets, no GitHub
issues) the cleaner constraint is preferable. Disposition C contradicts
the prior project-group decision and should only be revisited if new
implementer evidence emerges.
