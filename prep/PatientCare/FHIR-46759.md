## Summary

The reporter (Mary Kay McDaniel) is asking that the **US PACIO Personal Functioning and Engagement (PFE) IG** add the standard "previous | bottom | next" navigation links that the HL7 IG publisher renders at the top and bottom of every page when the IG configures `show-nav: true` (commonly expressed as `shownav: 'true'` in the SUSHI `sushi-config.yaml` parameters block). This is purely an IG-publisher / configuration change — no FHIR resource, profile, or terminology is affected — and the requester reports it materially improves page-by-page navigation through the IG.

The same reporter has filed an identical request against several other IGs as a small "navigation usability" campaign:
- [FHIR-46760](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-46760) — US Da Vinci HRex (resolved **Persuasive**).
- [FHIR-46761](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-46761) — US Da Vinci PDex (**Resolved – change required**, Persuasive).
- [FHIR-50178](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50178) — FHIR Core (Waiting for Input).
- [FHIR-54455](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54455) — FHIR Core (Submitted).

[FHIR-43440](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43440) (Genomics Reporting, **Persuasive**) is the most useful precedent because it documents the actual mechanism: SUSHI passes the `shownav` parameter through to the IG publisher, which then renders the prev/bottom/next tabs in the page header and footer (the Subscriptions R5 Backport IG is the worked example). Two earlier Financial Mgmt tickets — [FHIR-24839](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-24839) and [FHIR-25674](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-25674) — and [FHIR-45540](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-45540) (MDI) show that workgroups have consistently treated this category of "make the IG easier to navigate" request as Persuasive (with or without modification).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Enable the prev/bottom/next navigation strip in the **US PACIO Personal Functioning and Engagement** IG by adding the `shownav` parameter to the IG's SUSHI configuration. Concretely, in the IG repository's `sushi-config.yaml`, add (or set) under the `parameters:` block:

```yaml
parameters:
  shownav: 'true'
```

(Equivalently, if the IG uses the JSON-style IG parameter list rather than SUSHI parameters, add a `code = "show-nav"`, `value = "true"` parameter to the `ImplementationGuide.definition.parameter` array.) After republication, the IG publisher will render `<prev | bottom | next>` links at the top and bottom of every generated page. No FHIR resources, profiles, value sets, or narrative content need to change.

#### Justification

This is the exact mechanism documented in the persuasive precedent [FHIR-43440](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43440) and is what was applied for the same reporter's sister tickets [FHIR-46760](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-46760) (HRex) and [FHIR-46761](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-46761) (PDex), both of which were resolved **Persuasive**. The change is a one-line configuration edit, has no normative impact, costs essentially nothing to implement, and visibly improves navigation for implementers who read the IG sequentially. Workgroups across HL7 (Financial Mgmt, Clinical Genomics, Public Health, Clinical Interoperability Council) have consistently treated this category of request as Persuasive (FHIR-24839, FHIR-25674, FHIR-45540, FHIR-43440, FHIR-46760, FHIR-46761), so accepting it keeps PFE consistent with the rest of the published IG ecosystem.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the underlying intent (improve PFE IG navigation) but go beyond just enabling `shownav`. In the same `sushi-config.yaml` change, also:

1. Set `shownav: 'true'` (as in Disposition A).
2. Audit and enrich the IG's `menu.xml` (or `sushi-config.yaml` `menu:` block) so the top navigation bar surfaces the IG's primary landing pages (Background, Use Cases, Profiles index, Terminology index, Examples) rather than only the auto-generated TOC — addressing the broader pattern called out in [FHIR-25674](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-25674) and [FHIR-24839](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-24839).
3. Optionally add a "Table of Contents" entry to the top nav bar per [FHIR-45540](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-45540).

#### Justification

The reporter's literal request (Disposition A) treats one symptom (no prev/next chrome). The closely-related Persuasive-with-Modification tickets show that workgroups have repeatedly recognized that "easier navigation" usually needs both page-to-page chrome **and** a curated top-nav. Doing the broader pass once now avoids a second round of similar tickets against PFE later. The trade-off is a slightly larger change (touches `menu.xml` / menu config in addition to one parameter) and requires the PFE editors to decide which top-nav entries matter most — modest editorial work, still no normative impact.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket **Not Persuasive** on the basis that the IG already exposes navigation via the publisher-generated table of contents and the in-page section headers, and that adding the prev/bottom/next strip is a publisher-level cosmetic preference that should be addressed (if at all) by a global IG-publisher default rather than per-IG ticket.

#### Justification

Defensible only on a "scope / venue" argument: the request is not specific to PFE content and arguably belongs against the IG publisher tooling so every HL7 IG inherits the same default. This rationale is weak, however — the IG publisher already supports the parameter; nothing about PFE prevents adopting it; and refusing on venue grounds when sister IGs (HRex, PDex, Genomics) accepted the same request would be inconsistent.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A is the right call. The change is a single-line edit to `sushi-config.yaml` (`shownav: 'true'`) with zero impact on PFE's normative content, it has direct Persuasive precedent from the same reporter on two sister IGs ([FHIR-46760](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-46760), [FHIR-46761](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-46761)) and from [FHIR-43440](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43440) which documents exactly this mechanism, and declining would put PFE out of step with the broader HL7 IG ecosystem on a clear usability win. Disposition B is attractive but expands the scope beyond what was requested and is better tracked as a separate "navigation polish" item if the PFE editors want it; the workgroup can layer that on later without blocking this ticket. Disposition C is not defensible given the precedent.
