## Summary

The reporter requests that the PACIO Functional Status IG (and, by
implication, the companion Cognitive Status IG) substantially expand its
narrative to clarify the IG's scope. Specifically, the IG is asked to:
(1) state which anchoring framework or standard terminology grounds its
domain definition — with a strong recommendation to adopt the WHO's
International Classification of Functioning, Disability and Health
(ICF), aligning with future ICD-11 and current USCDI proposals; (2)
reconsider the term "status," which implies a single axis of assessment,
in favour of a broader umbrella such as "functioning" with ICF-aligned
subdomains for performance, capability, goals, and physiological /
personal / environmental factors; and (3) make explicit which resource
types are in scope (today's Observation focus is acknowledged, but the
inclusion or exclusion of Condition, Goal, Medication, Intervention,
etc., is unclear).

The ticket is tightly coupled to the related Patient Care request
[FHIR-30014](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-30014) ("Merge the
Functional Status IG and Cognitive Status IG"), filed by the same
reporter on the same day, which argues the two PACIO IGs are >80%
duplicative and should be merged. FHIR-30015 explicitly proposes its
naming/scope changes "in conjunction with our recommendation to merge
the IGs," so the two should be considered together. The most recent
disposition history shows PACIO had previously closed both as
"Considered for Future Use" (2021-08-23 vote 11-0-0); Lynn Laakso
reverted that resolution and re-opened the ticket on 2021-11-04 for
reconsideration post-STU1 publication, noting that PACIO had since
adopted the ICF conceptual framework and intends to broaden the IG to
additional functioning observations over time. The ticket has been
sitting in Triaged / Unresolved since then.

## Details

**Description:**

The narrative does not truly describe the scope of this IG. Please help
readers and implementers by addressing the central issue: how does this
IG define "cognitive status" and what kinds of information is it
intended to cover.

Let's unpack this issue a little further.

What anchoring framework or standard terminology is used as the basis
for defining the domain? Is it just about physical functioning? Should
it include assessments related to household tasks, ability to perform
duties at work? What about the range of neuropsychological functional
abilities/limitations? We recommend that both PACIO IGs anchor their
labels and concept domains to the most well-established international
framework for describing functioning and disability: the WHO's
International Classification of Functioning, Disability and Health
(ICF). The ICF framework will also be part of future ICD-11 which
increases the validity of the framework and facilitates a stucture that
can be used globally. Such a framework will be invaluable when trying to
represent content from complex domains.

Further, "status" implies a singular axis of assessment. From both the
ICF and the CMS-required assessment instruments, it is clear that
several dimensions of functioning can be described (current performance,
capability, goals, etc) and are related to both physiological, personal,
and environmental factors. "Status" does not adequately capture this
breadth. Rather, we propose (in conjunction with our recommendation to
merge the IGs) that a simpler umbrella term of "functioning" be applied
and that subdomain areas be identified as needed using the ICF's
conceptual framework. This is consistent with the current proposals in
the USCDI.

Last, the scope of both IGs should be clarified with respect to the
kinds of data (e.g. Resource types) that have been modeled. Rightly so,
there has been heavy focus on Observations. Is it intended to only cover
content contained in the CMS-required assessment instruments? Further,
it is not clear whether representation of Conditions (diagnoses), Goals,
Medications, Interventions, etc are in scope (now or in the future).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the US PACIO Functional Status IG narrative (and align the
Cognitive Status IG narrative in parallel) to:

1. Add a new "Scope" / "Conceptual framework" section to the IG home
   page that explicitly anchors the IG's domain definition to the
   WHO International Classification of Functioning, Disability and
   Health (ICF), names ICF as the source of the IG's subdomain
   structure, and notes alignment with future ICD-11 and current USCDI
   proposals.
2. Replace or supplement the framing word "status" throughout the
   narrative with the umbrella term "functioning," and identify
   subdomains (current performance, capability, goals, physiological /
   personal / environmental factors) using ICF terminology. Profile and
   resource technical names are *not* renamed in this disposition (to
   avoid breaking existing implementations); the terminology change is
   confined to narrative pages, section titles, and prose definitions.
3. Add a "Resources in scope" subsection enumerating which FHIR resource
   types the IG currently profiles (Observation-centric today) and
   stating, with explicit "in scope now" vs. "anticipated for future
   versions" lists, where Condition, Goal, MedicationStatement /
   MedicationRequest, Procedure / ServiceRequest (interventions), and
   related resources stand.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter asked for and what Patient Care/PACIO
already partially committed to in the 2021-11-04 comment (PACIO has
"come up with a definition" and "is now using the conceptual framework
of the ICF"). Anchoring the narrative to ICF is consistent with WHO's
established framework, with the planned ICF integration into ICD-11,
and with USCDI direction, all of which are explicitly cited in the
ticket. Limiting the change to narrative, section headings, and an
explicit in-scope resource list avoids any breaking impact on profile
URLs, canonicals, or conformance artifacts, so no implementer migration
is needed. The "status" → "functioning" wording shift is acknowledged
in the comment history as deferred to be handled with FHIR-30014, so
this disposition explicitly does not rename profiles — it only updates
the narrative framing — keeping the change tractable for a near-term
PACIO publication.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Resolve FHIR-30015 jointly with [FHIR-30014](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-30014)
by treating both as a single planned next-major-version restructuring of
the PACIO IGs:

- In the current STU release line, apply only the *narrative* updates
  from Disposition A (ICF anchoring, explicit "Resources in scope"
  list), and add a forward-pointing note that a future major version
  will (a) merge the Functional Status and Cognitive Status IGs into a
  single "PACIO Functioning" IG, (b) reorganise existing profiles as
  ICF-aligned subdomains, and (c) reconsider the use of "status" in
  IG-, profile-, and section-level naming.
- Open / link a tracking artefact (PACIO project board item or follow-up
  Jira) to coordinate the joint redesign with the Gravity Project's
  runtime value-set selection work that PACIO previously cited on
  FHIR-30014, since that mechanism is the technical enabler for
  collapsing parallel functional/cognitive profiles into shared ones.

#### Justification

The reporter explicitly framed FHIR-30015's recommendations "in
conjunction with our recommendation to merge the IGs." The 2021-11-04
PACIO comment likewise tied the "status" naming question to FHIR-30014.
Resolving the two tickets jointly avoids a half-measure where narrative
adopts ICF but profile/IG names still imply a single-axis "status"
view, and it lets PACIO sequence the breaking restructuring (profile
renames, IG merge, value-set rebinding) into a planned major release
rather than mid-STU. It also preserves implementer stability in the
near term while honouring the substantive intent of the ticket.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-30015 as "Considered for Future Use" (or "Not Persuasive")
on the grounds that PACIO has already responded to the substantive
concern: the IG narrative has been augmented with a definition of
functional status and an explicit statement that the ICF conceptual
framework is being adopted. Any further structural changes (umbrella
"functioning" framing, profile naming, resource-scope expansion) are
better handled through PACIO's normal roadmap and through FHIR-30014
rather than as a Patient Care ballot disposition on this ticket.

#### Justification

Defensible only on the narrow basis that PACIO publicly committed in
the 2021-11-04 comment to having added a functional-status definition
and to having adopted ICF, and that the deeper "status" / resource-scope
questions are explicitly being deferred to FHIR-30014. Declining keeps
the Patient Care ballot queue focused and avoids re-litigating a
question the IG's stewards have already taken a position on. The
weakness of this disposition is that the ticket was *re-opened*
specifically because the prior "Considered for Future Use" outcome was
judged inadequate, so closing it again on similar grounds is unlikely
to satisfy the reporter or the FMG reviewers who tracked the revert.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The reporter's own framing — and Patient Care's own 2021-11-04 comment
— treats FHIR-30015 and FHIR-30014 as one programme of work, not two
independent tickets. Disposition A delivers the narrative clarification
the reporter asked for but leaves the inconsistency that the IG
narrative would adopt ICF and "functioning" while the IG title,
profile names, and section headings continue to assert a single-axis
"status" model — exactly the gap the reporter flagged. Disposition C
re-states the position that was already explicitly reverted in 2021 and
is unlikely to land. Disposition B keeps the implementer-safe narrative
update from A in the current STU line, formally couples the deeper
restructuring to FHIR-30014, and ties the work to the Gravity / runtime
value-set effort PACIO is already pursuing — giving the workgroup a
clear two-step path (narrative now, structural redesign in the next
major version) rather than forcing a binary accept/decline on the full
scope today.
