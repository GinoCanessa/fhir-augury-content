## Summary

The ticket asserts that the canonical URL for the `observation-visual-field`
ValueSet — currently rooted under `http://hl7.org/fhir/...` — is in the wrong
namespace. The reporter (Ron G. Parker, in an imported "Comment 8") states
that the canonical URL should instead be rooted under
`http://terminology.hl7.org` (THO), since the ValueSet is a terminology
artifact and HL7 policy is to register such artifacts in THO rather than the
FHIR core / IG namespace. The reporter also notes that a UTG ticket should
be raised in parallel to register the ValueSet in THO.

No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were found
for this ticket in the cached data, so the request stands on its own.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Re-root the canonical URL of the `observation-visual-field` ValueSet from
the FHIR Ophthalmology Retinal IG namespace
(`http://hl7.org/fhir/.../ValueSet/observation-visual-field`) to a THO
namespace
(`http://terminology.hl7.org/ValueSet/observation-visual-field`), and
file a companion UTG proposal to register the ValueSet (and any
underlying CodeSystem(s) it composes that are not already in THO) under
terminology.hl7.org. Update every binding in the Ophthalmology Retinal
IG that references the old canonical to point at the new THO canonical,
and add a `ValueSet.identifier` (or equivalent) preserving the prior
canonical so existing implementers can resolve historical references.

#### Justification

This aligns the artifact with current HL7 terminology governance
practice: ValueSets that are not specific to a single specification —
and that are in principle reusable across IGs — are expected to live in
THO under UTG stewardship rather than in the publishing IG's namespace.
Re-rooting now, while the IG is still maturing and adoption is limited,
minimises the implementer impact of a later forced migration. Filing
the UTG ticket in parallel (as the reporter explicitly requests)
ensures THO becomes the authoritative home before the new canonical is
published.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Leave the existing `http://hl7.org/fhir/...` canonical URL in place for
backward compatibility, but **mirror** the ValueSet into THO under a
THO canonical and mark the IG-rooted ValueSet as deprecated (status
`retired` once the THO copy publishes, with `ValueSet.replacedBy` /
narrative pointing at the THO canonical). Update internal IG bindings
to use the THO canonical going forward; keep the old URL resolvable so
existing instances and profiles in the wild do not break.

#### Justification

Canonical URLs are identity. Silently relocating a published canonical
breaks any consumer that has already bound to it (validators, profiles,
test data, downstream IGs). A deprecation-and-mirror strategy delivers
the same governance outcome (THO becomes authoritative, UTG manages
the artifact) without invalidating in-flight implementations. It also
gives Patient Care / the Ophthalmology Retinal authors a clean window
to coordinate with UTG on naming, OID assignment, and CodeSystem
registration before flipping bindings.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request as written and keep the canonical URL rooted under
`http://hl7.org/fhir/...` within the Ophthalmology Retinal IG, on the
basis that the ValueSet is specific to this IG's scope (ophthalmic
visual field observations), is not currently consumed by other
specifications, and therefore does not meet the "shared terminology"
threshold that motivates THO/UTG hosting. If and when another
specification needs to reuse it, revisit at that time.

#### Justification

THO/UTG hosting carries process overhead (UTG submission, terminology
governance review, cross-publication coordination) and a permanent
change of stewardship away from the originating workgroup. For a
ValueSet that is single-IG, single-domain, and low-volume, that
overhead may not be justified, and IG-local hosting keeps editorial
control with the Patient Care workgroup that owns the clinical
content.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The reporter's underlying point is correct: terminology artifacts
belong in THO under UTG, and the `observation-visual-field` ValueSet
is a terminology artifact. However, canonical URLs are identifiers,
not labels — quietly re-rooting a published canonical (Disposition A)
risks breaking any validator, profile, or instance that has already
bound to the existing URL, and there is no evidence in the ticket or
cached discussions that the migration impact has been scoped.
Disposition B reaches the same governance end-state (THO becomes
authoritative, UTG assumes stewardship, the companion UTG ticket the
reporter asks for still gets filed) while preserving backward
compatibility through a deprecation-and-mirror window. Disposition C
is the weakest option: the reporter's THO-vs-IG-namespace argument is
a standing HL7 policy preference, not a per-ticket judgement call, so
declining outright would just defer the same request. Recommend B,
with the caveat that the workgroup confirm the actual implementer
footprint of the existing canonical before scheduling the retirement.
