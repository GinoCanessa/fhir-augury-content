## Summary

The submitter reports that, in the Ophthalmology Retinal FHIR Implementation
Guide, a profile on `Observation` binds `Observation.code.coding` (the repeating
`Coding` child) to the *Ophthalmology VA Observable Entities* ValueSet rather
than binding `Observation.code` (the parent `CodeableConcept`). Because the
binding is placed on the individual `coding` element, every `Coding` in the
`CodeableConcept` must come from that ValueSet, which effectively disallows
implementers from carrying translations or alternate codings (for example, a
local code paired with the required ophthalmology concept). The requested fix
is to move the ValueSet binding up one level from `Observation.code.coding` to
`Observation.code`, so that only one `Coding` must be drawn from the ValueSet
and additional translations are permitted.

No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were surfaced
for this ticket; the automated cross-reference query returned only FHIR element
mentions for `Observation.code` and `Observation.code.coding`.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the Ophthalmology Retinal IG profile on `Observation` (the profile that
currently pins visual-acuity coding), move the `required` / specified binding
to *Ophthalmology VA Observable Entities* from
`Observation.code.coding` up to `Observation.code`. Concretely, in the
profile's `StructureDefinition`:

- Remove the `ElementDefinition.binding` element from the
  `Observation.code.coding` differential entry (and drop the `coding` slicing
  if it was only introduced to carry this binding).
- Add / retain the `ElementDefinition.binding` element on the
  `Observation.code` differential entry, pointing to
  `http://hl7.org/fhir/uv/eyecare/ValueSet/observation-visual-acuity` (or the
  equivalent canonical used in the IG), with the same binding strength
  (`required` unless the workgroup decides to relax it — see Disposition B).

This matches the FHIR convention for `CodeableConcept`: the binding governs
the concept as a whole, and at least one `Coding` in `code.coding` must be
drawn from the bound ValueSet, while additional `Coding` entries are allowed
as translations or alternate representations.

#### Justification

The FHIR core specification is explicit that bindings on a `CodeableConcept`
are satisfied when *any* of the `Coding`s within it is from the bound
ValueSet, which is the mechanism the submitter wants restored. Binding the
repeating `coding` element forces *every* `Coding` to be in the ValueSet,
which is almost never the intent for an observation code and directly blocks
the common interoperability pattern of carrying a local or legacy code
alongside the required standard code. Moving the binding to
`Observation.code` aligns the profile with both the FHIR methodology guidance
on `CodeableConcept` bindings and how other HL7 IGs (US Core, IPS, etc.) bind
`Observation.code`.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Move the binding to `Observation.code` as in Disposition A, **and** at the
same time reconsider its strength and scope:

- Bind `Observation.code` to the ophthalmology visual-acuity ValueSet with
  strength `extensible` (or `preferred`) rather than `required`, so that
  implementers with codes not yet in the ValueSet are not blocked, while the
  intended concepts are still clearly expressed.
- If the profile is meant specifically for visual-acuity observations, split
  it into a dedicated "Visual Acuity Observation" profile that binds
  `Observation.code` to the visual-acuity ValueSet, and leave other
  ophthalmology observations to a more general profile with a broader or
  `example` binding.
- Optionally add an invariant or guidance note stating that at least one
  `Coding` in `Observation.code.coding` SHOULD be from the ophthalmology
  ValueSet, to preserve the original intent without blocking translations.

#### Justification

The submitter's underlying concern is that implementers cannot carry
translations; simply moving the binding (Disposition A) solves that, but it
leaves the binding as `required`, which still means exactly one `Coding` must
be from the ValueSet. For an IG that is likely to be used alongside local
institutional or legacy terminologies, `extensible` is the more pragmatic
strength and is consistent with how US Core and many other IGs bind
`Observation.code`. Splitting profiles by concept area is the usual FHIR
pattern when different observations need different bindings, and avoids the
"one big profile" antipattern. The trade-off versus Disposition A is more
structural change to the IG and potentially more artifacts to maintain, but
it resolves both the literal defect and the likely next-round concern about
binding strength.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as *Not Persuasive* on the basis that the current binding on
`Observation.code.coding` is intentional: the Ophthalmology Retinal IG
requires every `Coding` in the visual-acuity observation to come from the
ophthalmology ValueSet, and translations/alternate codings are expected to be
conveyed by other means (e.g., `Observation.code.text`, a parallel profiled
extension, or separate `Observation` instances keyed to local codes).

#### Justification

This disposition is defensible only if the workgroup's deliberate intent is
to forbid non-ophthalmology codings in the primary `code` element — for
example, to guarantee unambiguous semantic equivalence across systems that
consume the IG. It would need to be backed by an explicit statement in the
IG narrative, because it is contrary to the FHIR convention for
`CodeableConcept` bindings. No such rationale is captured on the ticket or
in its cross-references, so the primary risk of this disposition is that it
leaves implementers without a supported way to carry translations, which is
precisely the interoperability problem the reporter raised.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Moving the binding from `Observation.code.coding` to `Observation.code` is the
straightforward, low-risk fix that matches both the reporter's request and
long-standing FHIR methodology: bindings on `CodeableConcept` are meant to sit
on the parent element precisely so that translations and alternate codings
remain legal. Disposition B is attractive and should be raised as a
follow-on discussion (especially whether `required` is the right strength for
an ophthalmology-specific ValueSet), but bundling a strength change into this
ticket risks expanding scope beyond what the reporter asked for and what the
workgroup has discussed. Disposition C is not supported by any evidence in
the ticket or its cross-references and would leave a known interoperability
gap in the IG. The workgroup should apply Disposition A now and, if desired,
open a separate ticket to reconsider binding strength and profile slicing
along the lines of Disposition B.
