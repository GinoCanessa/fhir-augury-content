## Summary

The reporter has identified two small editorial errors in the narrative
example that appears near the end of section 8.2.1 of the
*International Birth And Child Model (FHIR)* specification. The current
text reads:

> "MS MS Discharged – 1 day after EL/LSCS MS was discharged next day and
> asked to be followed up at medical clinic ..."

Two corrections are requested in the same sentence: the duplicated token
"MS MS" at the start should be reduced to a single "MS", and the
prepositional phrase "at medical clinic" should be corrected to "at a
medical clinic" (insertion of the missing article). The proposed
replacement reads:

> "MS Discharged – 1 day after EL/LSCS MS was discharged next day and
> asked to be followed up at a medical clinic ..."

This is a pure technical correction to the narrative text of an example;
it does not change any FHIR resource definition, profile, value set,
example instance, or normative content. No cross-referenced Jira
tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were found, so the request
stands on its own.

## Details

**Description:**

Near end of section 8.2.1 :

"MS MS Discharged – 1 day after EL/LSCS MS was discharged next day and
asked to be followed up at medical clinic ..."

should be:

"MS Discharged – 1 day after EL/LSCS MS was discharged next day and
asked to be followed up at **a** medical clinic ..."

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply both editorial corrections to the narrative example in
section 8.2.1 of the *International Birth And Child Model (FHIR)*
specification exactly as the reporter described:

1. Replace the duplicated token "MS MS Discharged" with "MS
   Discharged".
2. Insert the missing indefinite article so that "asked to be followed
   up at medical clinic" becomes "asked to be followed up at a medical
   clinic".

The change is confined to the source page that renders section 8.2.1
(typically a markdown / HTML narrative file under the IG's `input/pages`
or equivalent authoring root in the upstream repository — the exact
path will need to be confirmed in the IG repo since no `repo-analysis`
briefing is available for this IG). No structural FHIR content (resource
profiles, examples, value sets, capability statements) is touched.

#### Justification

The ticket is filed as a `Technical Correction` and is unambiguously
editorial: a duplicated token and a missing article in a single English
sentence. There is no semantic ambiguity, no impact on conformance, and
no implementer-facing breakage. Accepting both corrections as written is
the minimum-effort, minimum-risk action that fully resolves the
reporter's concern and improves readability of the example. There is
also no community discussion (no Zulip threads, no related tickets)
that would suggest any disagreement with the wording.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the typo fix ("MS MS" → "MS") verbatim, but, while the section
is open for editing, do a short editorial pass over the surrounding
narrative in section 8.2.1 to:

- Spell out the abbreviations on first use (e.g., "MS = mother/subject",
  "EL/LSCS = elective lower-segment caesarean section") or link them to
  a glossary, so non-clinical readers and international implementers
  can follow the example without prior context.
- Confirm consistent use of articles ("a" / "the") and tense throughout
  the discharge narrative, addressing the "at medical clinic" issue as
  one instance of a broader cleanup rather than a single insertion.

The two changes the reporter explicitly asked for are still applied;
the alternative simply broadens the scope of the same edit pass.

#### Justification

Section 8.2.1 is a narrative example whose value depends on being
readable by an international audience that may not share the reporter's
clinical shorthand. If the section is being touched anyway for an
editorial fix, expanding the scope slightly is cheap and reduces the
likelihood of further trickle-in technical-correction tickets against
the same paragraph. The trade-off versus Disposition A is a small
amount of additional editor time and a slightly larger diff to review;
there is no risk to conformance because, like A, this only edits prose.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change on the basis that the ticket targets a narrative
example in an IG that may already be frozen at a published version, and
that the reporter can adequately understand the intended meaning
despite the typo. Close as "Not Persuasive" without modifying the IG
text.

#### Justification

This is the weakest of the three options and is included for
completeness. The only defensible reasons to decline would be that the
specific published version of the *International Birth And Child Model
(FHIR)* IG is locked (e.g., a normative or trial-use publication that
the work group has decided not to re-publish for editorial-only fixes)
or that maintenance capacity on this IG is currently zero. Even then,
the standard practice in FHIR is to hold the fix for the next IG
republish rather than to refuse it outright, so a true decline is hard
to justify on the merits.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

This ticket is a textbook `Technical Correction`: two unambiguous
editorial errors in a single sentence, no conformance impact, no
disagreement in the community, and no cross-referenced discussion that
would suggest a more nuanced reading. Disposition A applies exactly the
fix the reporter described, keeps the diff minimal and easy to review,
and resolves the ticket cleanly. Disposition B is reasonable if a
Patient Care editor is already planning a pass over section 8.2.1, but
should not block the simple fix; if no broader pass is planned, B
becomes scope creep relative to the cost of the change. Disposition C
is not recommended — declining a clearly correct editorial fix would
leave a duplicated token and a missing article in published narrative
text without a defensible reason.
