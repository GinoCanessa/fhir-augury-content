## Summary

The ticket asks Patient Care to defer making the `Condition` resource Normative.
The reporter argues that as eCQM authoring transitions from QDM to FHIR, the
clinical-quality-measurement community will accumulate additional implementation
experience with `Condition` that may surface needed changes. Because changes to
Normative content are substantially harder to make than changes to Trial Use
content, the request is to keep `Condition` non-Normative for now so that any
QDM-to-FHIR migration findings can still be incorporated. No related Jira
tickets were surfaced via cross-references, and no related Zulip threads or
GitHub items were found in Augury for this ticket; the only direct context is
the two ticket comments (see below).

## Details

**Description:**

> As eCQMs transition from QDM to FHIR, clinical quality measurement will gain
> additional experience with the Condition resource which may identify changes.
> Change is more difficult when it in Normative, so do not make Condition
> normative yet.

**Comments:**

- **michelle.m.miller (2022-02-11):** Tags Paul Denning and asks him to
  elaborate on his reasoning, noting that Patient Care's goal *is* to make
  `Condition` Normative. Points out that individual elements can be marked
  Trial Use within an otherwise Normative resource, and asks whether the
  concern applies to specific elements.
- **mitrep9g / Paul Denning (2022-06-13):** Notes that QI-Core 4.1.1
  `Condition` (based on US Core 3.1.1, with the Sept-2022 ballot moving QI-Core
  to US Core 5.0.0) still relies on two R4 extensions —
  `condition-dueto` and `condition-occurredfollowing` — and asks whether these
  should be promoted to first-class elements on base `Condition` in R5 instead
  of remaining extensions. References the R5 Roadmap Zulip announcement that
  R5 will not add Normative resources, so there is "some more time to decide,"
  and commits to raising the topic in CQI WG to gather measure-developer input.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Hold `Condition` at Trial Use for the FHIR R5 (and prospectively R6) cycle —
do **not** advance the resource, in whole or in part, to Normative maturity.
Concretely: leave `Condition`'s resource-level `Maturity Level` at its current
FMM (Trial Use), do not add the Normative standards-status flag to the
StructureDefinition (`http://hl7.org/fhir/StructureDefinition/structuredefinition-standards-status`
= `normative`) or the resource header, and do not add `Condition` to the list
of resources targeted for Normative balloting in the relevant Patient Care WG
roadmap. Re-evaluate after at least one full eCQM publication cycle on FHIR
(post-QDM transition) has produced field experience with the resource.

#### Justification

This is the safest path with respect to the QDM→FHIR transition risk the
reporter raises: any change-requests that emerge from eCQM implementers can
still be processed under Trial Use rules without invoking the substantive-change
constraints that apply to Normative content. It is also already
de-facto aligned with the R5 direction the second commenter cites (R5 not
adding Normative resources), so accepting this for R5 carries minimal cost.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Advance `Condition` to Normative at the **resource** level, but keep the
elements most likely to churn from QDM/eCQM experience marked Trial Use via
element-level `standards-status = trial-use` extensions. At a minimum, mark
the following as Trial Use within an otherwise-Normative `Condition`:

- `Condition.severity`
- `Condition.stage` (and its components)
- `Condition.evidence`
- Any element added in R5 to absorb the R4 extensions Paul identified —
  i.e., a first-class `Condition.dueTo` and `Condition.occurredFollowing`
  (promoted from `extension-condition-dueto` and
  `extension-condition-occurredfollowing`) — should ship as Trial Use even
  if the surrounding resource is Normative.

This directly answers Michelle Miller's point that element-level Trial Use is
available, while simultaneously addressing Paul's concrete extension-promotion
question.

#### Justification

This preserves the WG's stated goal of bringing `Condition` to Normative
(stability for implementers, signalling of maturity) without locking down the
specific elements where QDM-to-FHIR implementation experience is most likely
to identify needed changes. It is consistent with the precedent of mixed-
maturity Normative resources (e.g., `Patient`, `Observation`) and explicitly
addresses the QI-Core extension-promotion question, which is the only concrete
change-pressure surfaced in the comment thread.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request and proceed with the WG's existing plan to advance
`Condition` to Normative on its current schedule, on the basis that (a) R5
will not in fact be promoting additional resources to Normative
(per the R5 Roadmap announcement Paul himself cites), so for the R5 ballot
the request is moot; and (b) the request does not identify any specific
element that is unstable — element-level Trial Use markers (Disposition B)
already exist as a finer-grained tool than blocking the whole resource.
Resolution category: *Already Addressed by Existing Mechanism*.

#### Justification

The ticket's core risk — being unable to absorb QDM/eCQM-driven changes
post-Normative — is mitigated by two pre-existing mechanisms: the R5 cycle's
own decision not to add Normative resources, and the per-element Trial Use
extension. Without a concrete list of at-risk elements, a blanket "wait"
verdict is hard to defend against the implementer-stability benefits Patient
Care is targeting.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the most defensible because it directly reconciles the two
voices already in the ticket: it honours Patient Care's stated goal of moving
`Condition` to Normative (Michelle Miller's position) while explicitly
protecting the elements where QDM-to-FHIR experience is most likely to drive
change (Paul Denning's concern), including the QI-Core extension-promotion
question he raised about `condition-dueto` / `condition-occurredfollowing`.
Disposition A is overly broad — it blocks the resource on speculative future
findings — and Disposition C dismisses a real ecosystem signal (active
QI-Core extension use) that the WG should at least respond to in the
Normative review. The recommendation can be tightened once the CQI WG
discussion Paul committed to brings back a concrete list of elements at
risk; until then, B sets up the right structural answer.
