## Summary

The reporter (Elliot Silver) flags an internal-consistency concern across the
IPS Observation profiles: certain `Observation` elements are prohibited
(cardinality `0..0`) in some IPS Observation profiles but left unconstrained in
sibling profiles that cover similar subject matter. Three concrete examples are
given:

1. `Observation.bodySite` is prohibited in the **pregnancy-related** Observation
   profiles, but **not** in the **alcohol use** or **tobacco use** Observation
   profiles.
2. `Observation.method` is prohibited in the **pregnancy EDD (estimated date of
   delivery)** profile, but **not** in any of the other IPS Observation
   profiles.
3. The reporter further asks whether `Observation.referenceRange` (or specific
   sub-elements thereof) makes any sense for **alcohol use** or **tobacco use**
   observations and, if not, whether it should be prohibited there for
   symmetry.

The ticket is therefore not a feature request but a **harmonization /
consistency** ask: either justify the asymmetric `0..0` constraints by
documenting the clinical rationale, or remove / re-balance them so the IPS
Observation profile family applies the same reasoning across the board.

The ticket has had a non-trivial lifecycle. In the 2022 Sept Patient Care WGM
it was placed on a Patient Care + IPS block vote (block-vote-2) and dispositioned
**Considered for Future Use** with a 14-0-16 vote (John D'Amore / Gay Dolin)
on 2022-09-20. Lynn Laakso then **reverted** that resolution post the STU
update publication ("re-open post STU update publication", 2022-11-22), and the
ticket has sat at status **Triaged** with no resolution since. There are no
linked Jira tickets and no GitHub cross-references; the only Zulip
context is the IPS block-vote announcement thread itself.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat the asymmetry as a defect in the IPS Observation profile family and
**harmonize the prohibitions**, choosing one consistent rationale and applying
it across all IPS Observation profiles:

1. **`Observation.bodySite`** — Decide, per IPS authoring policy, whether body
   site is meaningful for the *category* "social-history / behavioral
   observation" (alcohol use, tobacco use) and for "pregnancy-related"
   observations as a class. The most defensible harmonization is to **prohibit
   `Observation.bodySite` (`0..0`) on the alcohol-use and tobacco-use
   Observation profiles** as well, on the same reasoning that already applies
   to the pregnancy-related profiles (these are subject-level behavioral or
   physiological-state observations with no anatomical site).
2. **`Observation.method`** — Either (a) **remove** the `0..0` constraint from
   the pregnancy EDD profile (allowing implementers to record the method used
   to estimate the date of delivery — e.g., LMP-based vs. ultrasound-based),
   or (b) **add** matching `0..0` constraints to the other IPS Observation
   profiles. The clinically stronger option is **(a)**, because EDD method is
   a known clinically meaningful distinction.
3. **`Observation.referenceRange`** — **Prohibit `Observation.referenceRange`
   (`0..0`) on the alcohol-use and tobacco-use profiles**, since reference
   ranges are not clinically applicable to behavioral / categorical
   observations of this kind.

Concretely, this requires editing the StructureDefinition / FSH for the
relevant IPS Observation profiles in `HL7/fhir-ips` and either (i) adding
`element[bodySite].max = 0` and `element[referenceRange].max = 0` to the
alcohol-use and tobacco-use profiles, or (ii) relaxing `element[method].max`
on the pregnancy-EDD profile, depending on which sub-decisions the WG takes.

#### Justification

This treats the issue as the consistency defect the reporter described and
yields a profile family where each prohibition is justified by a stated
clinical rationale. It also closes the door on validator surprises in which an
implementer learns that `bodySite` is rejected on one profile but silently
accepted on a sibling profile that arguably has even less anatomical
relevance. The reporter's framing in the ticket and his remark on the Zulip
thread that he was content with prior dispositions both suggest the WG could
land any consistent answer here without controversy, provided it is documented.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than mass-editing prohibitions, **document the rationale for each
existing constraint in the profile narrative and leave cardinalities
unchanged**, then add a non-normative "Profile design notes" sub-page (or a
short paragraph in each profile's intro) that explains:

- why `bodySite` is prohibited in pregnancy-related profiles (no meaningful
  anatomical site for the observation as scoped),
- why `method` is prohibited specifically in the pregnancy EDD profile (per
  whatever IPS-WG decision originally produced that constraint), and
- the deliberate decision **not** to prohibit those elements on the
  alcohol-use and tobacco-use profiles (e.g., to preserve compatibility with
  jurisdictions that already populate them, or because the IPS team did not
  judge them harmful).

This treats the asymmetry as deliberate-but-undocumented rather than
accidental, and addresses the reporter's underlying concern (an implementer
cannot tell why the rules differ) without changing the wire format.

#### Justification

IPS is widely deployed and any new `0..0` constraint is a **breaking change**
for any implementer who is currently sending `bodySite` or `referenceRange` on
an alcohol-use or tobacco-use Observation: instances that previously validated
will start failing. Conversely, *relaxing* the pregnancy-EDD `method`
prohibition is forward-compatible but still produces a perceptible change in
the published profile set. A documentation-only fix avoids both risks during
the current STU window and still resolves the reporter's "why are these
inconsistent?" question. The downside is that the asymmetry remains and the
WG is effectively asserting, in print, that the two profile families have
different element-applicability rules — which may still feel unsatisfying.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve as **Not Persuasive** (or a documentation-only follow-up): the
reporter has identified an inconsistency but has not articulated a concrete
implementer harm caused by the asymmetric prohibitions. The IPS Observation
profiles cover clinically distinct domains (pregnancy state vs. social
history), and the existing `0..0` constraints reflect domain-specific
modeling choices made by their original authors. Re-litigating those choices
across all IPS Observation profiles is out of scope for an internal
consistency complaint, and the WG should preserve published behavior.

#### Justification

The ticket is an aesthetic / consistency observation rather than a defect
report, and was already dispositioned **Considered for Future Use 14-0-16**
at the Sept 2022 Patient Care + IPS block vote — i.e., the WG's own prior
position was that no immediate change is warranted. The only reason it is
still open is Lynn Laakso's procedural re-open after the STU update
publication ("we will investigate after next publication update"); that
investigation can simply conclude that no implementer harm has surfaced in
the intervening years and re-affirm the original disposition. Any
additional prohibition is a breaking change for existing IPS implementers
(see Disposition B), and any relaxation of `method` on the pregnancy-EDD
profile re-opens design discussion that the original profile author closed
deliberately.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B (Alternative Approach — document the
rationale, leave cardinalities unchanged for this STU cycle, and queue the
harmonization in Disposition A as a candidate for the next major IPS
revision).

This is the only path that simultaneously (i) honors Lynn Laakso's
reasoning for re-opening the ticket — the WG agreed to revisit it after the
STU update, not necessarily to *change* anything — (ii) avoids a breaking
change to deployed IPS Observation instances that today validate against
profiles where `bodySite` / `referenceRange` are unconstrained, and (iii)
gives the reporter (and future implementers) an explicit, written answer
to the "why are these different?" question, which is the actual defect.
Disposition A is the clinically right answer in the long run but should be
proposed as a coordinated profile-family pass during the next IPS major
revision (with explicit IPS-WG agreement on the treatment of `method` on
the pregnancy-EDD profile, since that is the one sub-decision where the
clinically defensible direction is *relaxation*, not addition, of a
prohibition). Disposition C ignores Lynn Laakso's deliberate re-open and
leaves the documented asymmetry in place with no rationale, which is what
the reporter complained about in the first place.
