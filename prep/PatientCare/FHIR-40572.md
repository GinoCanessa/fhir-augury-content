## Summary

Cooper Thompson asks the SDOH Clinical Care work group to reconsider whether
"Recorded Sex or Gender" (RSG) belongs in the Personal Characteristics
profiles family in US SDOH Clinical Care. The reporter notes that the Gender
Harmony project (tracked under HL7 ticket OTHER-2463) has reshaped how RSG is
modeled and communicated, and that under that new approach a single,
undifferentiated "RSG" personal characteristic no longer makes sense. The
ticket recommends that, instead of carrying RSG as a generic personal
characteristic, the SDOH IG should identify the specific kind(s) of
sex/gender information that are actually relevant for SDOH use cases and
profile those concretely. The ticket was previously dispositioned
"Considered for Future Use" (Saul Kravitz / Emma Jones, 7-0-0, 2023-06-22)
and then re-opened post STU 2.1 publication for consideration in STU 3. No
sibling Jira tickets, GitHub PRs/issues, or focused Zulip threads were found
in the cross-reference index for this ticket.

## Details

**Description:**
The Gender Harmony group has (or will be soon) made significant changes to
how we communicate Recorded Sex or Gender (RSG). See
https://jira.hl7.org/browse/OTHER-2463, which links to a draft google doc
that summarizes the current approach.

The new approach for RSG being adopted means that including RSG as a
characteristic itself doesn't really make sense. Rather, the specific type
of sex/gender information that is relevant for SDOH should be defined and
named rather than using the vague "RSG" term.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Remove the generic "Recorded Sex or Gender" Personal Characteristic
profile from US SDOH Clinical Care in STU 3. In its place, define one or
more SDOH-specific Personal Characteristic Observation profiles that
correspond to the discrete concepts produced by the Gender Harmony work
(e.g., Sex for Clinical Use, Sex Assigned at Birth, Gender Identity,
Recorded Sex/Gender as it appears on a specific document) — choosing
only those concepts that have a stated SDOH use case. Each new profile
should bind to the LOINC / Gender Harmony codes adopted by HL7 and
follow the same Observation pattern (Observation.code, .value, with the
existing `Observation.focus` / `.subject` flexibility for caregiver /
provider already used by the SDOH Personal Characteristics profiles).
Update the IG narrative, profile index, and any examples that currently
reference the generic RSG profile, and add a guidance section that
explains why RSG is decomposed and how implementers should choose among
the new profiles for SDOH screening, assessment, and goal/intervention
flows.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter requests and aligns SDOH-CC with the
Gender Harmony output that Patient Care, Vocabulary, and the Gender
Harmony Project have endorsed in OTHER-2463. The Gravity context
captured in the Zulip thread shows that the current Personal
Characteristics family explicitly scopes "recorded sex and gender" as a
single concept, which is precisely the over-broad framing Gender Harmony
moved away from. Naming the concrete sex/gender concept(s) that matter
for SDOH avoids ambiguity for implementers, prevents duplicate or
conflicting modeling between SDOH-CC and other IGs that adopt Gender
Harmony, and is cheaper to do in STU 3 than after another normative
cycle. The change is additive-then-deprecating from an implementer
perspective: existing systems can continue to populate the generic
profile during a transition window if the IG marks it deprecated rather
than deleted at first publication.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Retain a single Personal Characteristic profile in SDOH-CC for sex /
gender information, but **rebase** it onto the Gender Harmony logical
model rather than the legacy "RSG" framing. Concretely: rename the
profile to align with the Gender Harmony vocabulary (e.g., "Personal
Characteristic — Sex/Gender for SDOH"), narrow the value-set binding to
the Gender Harmony-aligned codes, and require a `component` or qualifier
that identifies *which* Gender Harmony concept (Sex for Clinical Use,
Gender Identity, Recorded Sex/Gender, etc.) the Observation is
asserting. Provide implementation guidance and examples for each
qualifier so consumers can filter on the qualifier instead of on
distinct profiles.

#### Justification

This addresses the reporter's underlying concern — the "vague RSG term"
— while keeping a single Personal Characteristic slot in the SDOH-CC
profile family. That avoids profile proliferation in an IG that already
maintains a sizeable Personal Characteristics set, keeps the Gravity
data-collection workflow uniform (one Observation shape per personal
characteristic), and reduces the migration burden on implementers who
have already built against the existing RSG profile. The trade-off vs.
Disposition A is that consumers must inspect a qualifier to know which
sex/gender concept is being communicated, which is a slightly weaker
contract than profile-level typing and is harder to validate purely
with `meta.profile`.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark FHIR-40572 as "Not Persuasive — covered elsewhere" and leave the
existing Recorded Sex or Gender Personal Characteristic profile in
SDOH-CC unchanged. Direct implementers who need finer-grained sex/gender
modeling to the Gender Harmony IG / OTHER-2463 deliverables and to the
host system's Patient resource (e.g., `Patient.gender`,
US Core sex/gender extensions), rather than re-modeling those concepts
inside SDOH-CC.

#### Justification

The Personal Characteristic profiles in SDOH-CC are scoped to capture
*equity-relevant attributes of a person in the context of SDOH
screening*, not to be the system of record for sex/gender semantics.
Keeping a single, intentionally coarse RSG slot avoids SDOH-CC having to
track Gender Harmony's evolving model and avoids duplicating profiles
that other IGs (Gender Harmony, US Core) own. Declining is defensible
if Patient Care concludes the SDOH use cases that motivated the
original RSG profile do not actually need the finer Gender Harmony
distinctions to operate. The risk is that this leaves SDOH-CC out of
step with HL7's accepted direction for sex/gender modeling, which the
reporter has already flagged.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter's premise is correct: HL7 has, through Gender Harmony /
OTHER-2463, moved away from a single "Recorded Sex or Gender" concept
toward several distinguishable, individually meaningful elements, and
the Gravity Zulip context confirms that SDOH-CC's current Personal
Characteristics family treats RSG as one undifferentiated slot.
Disposition C ignores that direction and would leave a normative-track
IG out of alignment with HL7's adopted sex/gender modeling.
Disposition B preserves a single profile but pushes the discrimination
burden onto a qualifier, which is harder to validate and harder for
implementers to discover than profile-typed Observations. Disposition A,
done in STU 3 while the IG is still pre-normative, gives SDOH-CC the
chance to (a) explicitly choose which Gender Harmony concepts have a
real SDOH use case, (b) profile only those, and (c) ship migration
guidance for the legacy RSG profile. That is the cleanest path to
long-term alignment and the lowest risk of carrying ambiguous semantics
into normative status. The work group should confirm with the Gender
Harmony Project leads which Gender Harmony concepts SDOH-CC will
actually consume before drafting the new profiles.
