## Summary

The reporter (Rob McClure) flags that the US Physical Activity (PA) FHIR
Implementation Guide is using "temporary codes" inline in its artifacts and
that Patient Care needs to coordinate with HL7 Terminology Infrastructure
(TI) to get those codes into appropriate, externally-curated code systems
— with the suggestion that many of them are likely to map to SNOMED CT.
The ticket is essentially a process/governance request to retire the
provisional codes used during STU development by submitting them upstream
(to SNOMED International or another suitable steward) so that the IG can
ultimately bind to permanent, well-governed codes rather than IG-local
placeholders. No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub
items were surfaced via `fhir-augury-cli` for this ticket, so the request
stands on its own without explicit linkage to companion tickets.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Patient Care formally takes on, post-STU, the work of inventorying every
"temporary" / IG-local code currently used in the US Physical Activity
(FHIR) IG, classifying each one by its target steward (predominantly
SNOMED CT, with LOINC, SCT, or HL7-maintained code systems considered
where appropriate), and working with HL7 Terminology Infrastructure (TI)
and the HL7 Terminology Authority (HTA) to submit the codes upstream.
The deliverables are: (1) a tracked inventory of provisional codes with
proposed permanent stewards; (2) submission packages to TI/SNOMED for
each batch; and (3) a follow-up IG ballot cycle that re-binds the
relevant ValueSets / CodeSystems to the now-permanent codes and removes
the temporary CodeSystem(s) from the IG. The work is sequenced as
discussed in the comments — kicked off after STU publication and once
the underlying PA resources reach FMM3, so that implementer experience
informs the term requests actually sent to TI.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter and the comment thread are asking for,
and it aligns with HL7's standing terminology governance principles:
IG-local "temporary" codes are tolerated during STU development but are
expected to be retired in favor of properly stewarded codes from
recognized terminologies (SNOMED CT first, per the reporter's reading of
the content). Lynn's note that the work should wait until the PA
resources are at FMM3 is preserved here — implementers should exercise
the codes first so that what we submit to TI/SNOMED reflects real-world
need rather than speculative modeling. Doing this work in coordination
with TI (rather than unilaterally) is the path that the HTA and TI
expect for any IG that wants its terminology to remain stable across
future SNOMED releases.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than treating this as a single, monolithic "submit all temporary
codes" task, split it into two stages with explicit gating. Stage 1
(immediately, in coordination with TI) is a *registration-only* step:
move the IG's temporary codes out of an IG-local CodeSystem and into a
neutral HL7-curated "provisional terminology" CodeSystem (or use the
HL7 `TemporaryCodes`-style mechanism), so that the codes have stable,
HL7-governed URIs even before SNOMED accepts them. Stage 2 (gated on
FMM3 + implementer feedback) is the actual SNOMED CT / external steward
submission and re-binding. ValueSets in the IG would, in the interim,
include the HL7-provisional codes plus any already-existing SNOMED codes
that are clearly correct, leaving only the genuinely novel concepts in
the submission queue.

#### Justification

The literal request — "submit all temporary codes to a code system, work
with TI" — risks stalling the IG: SNOMED submission cycles are slow,
and waiting for full SCT acceptance before retiring IG-local codes
leaves implementers exposed to URI churn. Two-stage handling protects
implementers (codes get governed, citable URIs immediately) while still
honoring the reporter's underlying concern that IG-local "temporary"
codes shouldn't persist. It also matches the comment thread's caveat
that we want implementer experience to shape the SNOMED requests — by
deferring only Stage 2 to FMM3, we get governance benefits sooner
without prematurely committing to specific SCT concepts.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Not Persuasive / Already Addressed**, on the basis
that (a) provisional codes in an STU IG are an accepted HL7 mechanism,
(b) the IG already declares those codes inside an explicit IG-local
CodeSystem so consumers can see they are provisional, and (c) the
existing HL7 process (HTA + TI engagement at the time of normative
ballot) already prescribes that submissions to SNOMED CT happen as the
artifacts mature — meaning no separate Patient Care work item is
required.

#### Justification

This disposition is defensible only if Patient Care concludes that the
existing HTA/TI normative-progression process is sufficient guarantee
that the temporary codes will be addressed at the appropriate time, and
that an open, work-group-owned ticket adds process overhead without
changing the outcome. However, the comment history pushes back against
this view: the original "Considered for Future Use" resolution was
explicitly *reverted* by the work group, with a directive to re-open
post-STU. Declining now would re-litigate a decision the work group has
already made, so this disposition carries a higher bar to justify.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The work group has already, on the record, reversed its earlier "defer
indefinitely" stance and asked that this be picked up post-STU once the
PA resources reach FMM3. That is exactly what Disposition A formalizes,
and it directly satisfies the reporter's concern. Disposition B is
attractive operationally and the work group should consider folding its
Stage 1 idea (interim HL7-governed URIs) into the implementation plan,
but it should not replace Disposition A as the formal disposition,
because the substantive commitment the ticket is asking for — engaging
TI to get codes into proper code systems — is unchanged. Disposition C
is hard to defend given that the work group has already explicitly
declined to leave this as "Considered for Future Use." The clean path
is to accept, schedule the work for the post-STU / FMM3 window already
agreed in the comments, and use the implementation plan to decide
whether to layer in Disposition B's two-stage refinement.
