## Summary

The reporter observes that the profiles in the Ophthalmology Retinal
(FHIR) IG (the "OCT" IG) apply inconsistent constraints to `.subject`
across resource types. The base Observation profile constrains
`.subject` to `1..1` and to a `Reference(Patient)`. The base Condition
profile also requires `.subject` (`1..1`) but appears to allow
`Reference(Patient | Group)`. The OCT DiagnosticReport profile does not
add any constraint at all, leaving it `0..1` and the target type the
unrestricted base set (`Patient | Group | Device | Location`). The
reporter asks the workgroup to either (a) explain why the per-profile
scope differs and document it, or (b) align the constraints across the
profiles. No cross-referenced Jira, Zulip, or GitHub items were
discovered for this ticket, so the request stands on its own.

## Details

**Description:**

> The various profiles have slightly different constraints on the
> `.subject` element. For example, the base observation profile
> requires `.subject` (1..1) and constrains it to a Patient reference.
> In contrast, the base condition profile also requires it (1..1) but
> seemingly allows references to either Patient or Group. The OCT
> DiagnosticReport profile doesn't add any constraints, leaving it
> optional (0..1) and referencing Patient, Group, Device or Location.
>
> If there are functional reasons why constraints are different, please
> clarify the scope of the various profiles. Otherwise, syncing them
> up would make sense.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Harmonize `.subject` across the Ophthalmology Retinal IG profiles by
adopting a single, intentional pattern for clinical-imaging-of-a-patient
data. Specifically:

- **Observation (OCT) profile:** keep `.subject` `1..1`, `Reference(Patient)`
  — this is the strictest of the three and matches the IG's clinical
  intent (an OCT measurement is taken on an individual patient).
- **Condition (OCT) profile:** tighten `.subject` from
  `Reference(Patient | Group)` to `1..1 Reference(Patient)`. Group-level
  conditions are not in the IG's stated use cases.
- **DiagnosticReport (OCT) profile:** add the missing constraints —
  raise cardinality to `1..1` and constrain the target to
  `Reference(Patient)`, removing `Group | Device | Location` which are
  not meaningful for an ophthalmology retinal report.

Document the rule in the IG's profiling-conventions section so future
profiles inherit the same `.subject` shape.

#### Justification

The reporter's observation is correct: the three profiles diverge with
no documented rationale. The IG's scope is patient-centric ophthalmic
imaging, so the strictest pattern (`1..1 Reference(Patient)`) is the
one that matches the use cases. Aligning all three profiles removes
implementer ambiguity, makes validation behavior predictable, and is
consistent with how other clinical-imaging IGs constrain `.subject`. It
also closes the most surprising gap — the DiagnosticReport profile
that adds no constraint at all, which currently lets implementers send
a report with no subject or with a `Device`/`Location` subject.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than collapse to the strictest shape, **align cardinality but
preserve the base resource's reference targets**, and document the
chosen shape per profile:

- All three profiles: `.subject` `1..1` (mandatory).
- Observation, Condition, and DiagnosticReport profiles: `Reference(Patient | Group)`
  consistently, mirroring the base Condition allowance and avoiding a
  narrower-than-base constraint on Observation.
- Add an IG-level "Profiling Conventions" page that states
  `.subject` must be present in every clinical resource and explains
  why `Patient | Group` is permitted (cohort/population imaging
  studies, screening Groups).

#### Justification

Tightening the Observation profile from `Reference(Patient | Group)` —
the base Observation allowance — to `Reference(Patient)` (as it is
today) is a stricter constraint than the other profiles. If Patient
Care wants the IG to support population/cohort retinal screening in the
future, locking Observation to Patient-only forecloses that without
re-balloting. Disposition B keeps the door open while still fixing the
real interoperability problem (the optional, unconstrained
DiagnosticReport `.subject`) and documents the rule explicitly. It
also avoids a breaking change to the Observation profile for any
existing implementers.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change as out of scope / not actionable. Mark the ticket
**Persuasive with Mod / Not Persuasive** with the rationale that the
profiles were authored independently to fit the use cases of each
resource and that the reporter has not identified concrete
interoperability failures, only structural inconsistency. Recommend
the reporter return with a specific exchange scenario that breaks
because of the divergence.

#### Justification

The Ophthalmology Retinal IG is a low-traffic specification and the
ticket has been Triaged at Highest priority but unassigned and
uncommented since 2021. It is plausible that the workgroup considered
the profiles fit-for-purpose at authoring time and that the divergence
reflects deliberate choices (Condition allowing `Group` for
population-screening conditions; DiagnosticReport intentionally left
loose so report-level aggregation across subjects could be expressed).
Without a reported implementation failure, declining preserves
existing behavior and avoids breaking changes.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is filed at **Highest** priority by an experienced FHIR
reviewer, and the inconsistency is real and surprising — particularly
the DiagnosticReport profile that adds no constraint at all and
therefore allows a retinal report with `subject = Device` or
`subject = Location`, which is almost certainly not the IG's intent.
Harmonizing on `1..1 Reference(Patient)` matches the IG's clinical
scope (OCT scans of an individual patient), removes implementer
ambiguity, and is the change the reporter explicitly asked for. The
risk of foreclosing a future cohort/population use case (the concern
that motivates Disposition B) is hypothetical for this IG and can be
revisited via a separate change if/when such use cases are formally
chartered. Disposition C is hard to defend given the unconstrained
DiagnosticReport target list, which is a genuine quality issue rather
than a stylistic one.
