## Summary

The reporter observes that, in the HL7 Ophthalmology Retinal (Eye Care) IG,
the `ObservationVisualAcuity` profile binds `Observation.method.coding` to
the `observation-visual-acuity-methods` ValueSet, but that ValueSet
enumerates **visual-acuity charts/devices** (e.g., Snellen, ETDRS, LogMAR
charts) rather than the **methods** by which visual acuity is measured.
`Observation.method` is defined as "The mechanism used to perform the
observation," so binding it to a list of test instruments is a semantic
mismatch: a chart is the instrument/device used, not the method itself.
The reporter asks that the method binding be replaced with a ValueSet that
actually enumerates visual-acuity measurement methods, and implicitly that
the device-oriented codes be moved to a more appropriate element (such as
`Observation.device` or an instrument-specific extension).

No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub issues/PRs were

## Proposed Dispositions

The dispositions below reflect the ticket's stated problem — a mismatch
between `Observation.method` semantics and the contents of
`observation-visual-acuity-methods`. Because no repo briefing is loaded,
file paths are described at the conceptual (IG / ValueSet / profile)
level rather than by authoring-root path; concrete paths should be
confirmed against a fresh briefing before implementation.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the Ophthalmology Retinal (Eye Care) IG, rebind
`ObservationVisualAcuity.method.coding` to a **new or existing
method-oriented ValueSet** that enumerates visual-acuity *measurement
methods* (e.g., distance visual acuity, near visual acuity, pinhole,
habitual, best-corrected, uncorrected, preferential-looking,
Teller-cards method, etc.) drawn from SNOMED CT where possible. The
existing `observation-visual-acuity-methods` ValueSet, which actually
enumerates chart/device codes (Snellen, ETDRS, LogMAR charts, …),
should either (a) be renamed to
`observation-visual-acuity-charts` (or similar) and bound to
`Observation.device` via slicing/reference, or (b) be deprecated in
favor of a chart-specific extension. Update the profile's
`StructureDefinition`, the ValueSet resource, and the narrative
examples in the IG to match.

#### Justification

This is the most direct reading of the ticket and aligns the profile
with the base-resource semantics of `Observation.method` ("The
mechanism used to perform the observation"). Keeping "method" and
"device" conceptually distinct is consistent with how `Observation` is
modeled elsewhere (e.g., laboratory observations bind method to test
techniques, not analyzer models). It also makes the profile
implementable by EHRs that already distinguish the acuity *method*
(distance vs. near vs. pinhole) from the *chart* used, which is common
in ophthalmology documentation.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the current `observation-visual-acuity-methods` ValueSet content
in place but (1) **rename** it to
`observation-visual-acuity-instruments` (or `-charts`), (2) **move the
binding** from `Observation.method.coding` to `Observation.device`
(via a profiled reference to a `Device` resource constrained by a
companion `DeviceVisualAcuityChart` profile, whose `Device.type` is
bound to the renamed ValueSet), and (3) add a *new, smaller*
method-oriented binding on `Observation.method` restricted to a
preferred set of SNOMED CT visual-acuity test methods. This preserves
the codes the IG has already curated while correcting the element they
are attached to.

#### Justification

The chart codes already assembled in the existing ValueSet are useful
and were clearly intentional; discarding them (as a literal read of
Disposition A could imply) loses prior work. Rebinding them to
`Observation.device` is more faithful to the FHIR information model
and avoids forcing implementers to choose between "method" and
"device" semantics. The trade-off is a larger authoring change — a new
`Device` profile and an additional method ValueSet — and an
IG-internal migration note for early adopters, but this is lower-risk
than leaving the binding misaligned and is closer to how other clinical
IGs separate instruments from techniques.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline on the basis that `Observation.method` binding strength in the
IG is *example* / *preferred* rather than *required*, and that the
existing ValueSet — while mis-named — is documented and in use. Retain
the current binding and clarify the ValueSet's narrative to say that
it captures the *chart or test instrument* commonly recorded as the
"method" of visual-acuity assessment in ophthalmic practice.

#### Justification

This option is only defensible if (a) implementers have not actually
been confused by the mismatch, and (b) the binding strength is loose
enough that a narrative clarification suffices. It avoids any breaking
change to downstream profiles or to examples already circulating. It
is, however, a weak position: the ticket correctly identifies a
semantic error, and narrative-only fixes tend to accumulate technical
debt in IGs. Decline should be reserved for the case where the
workgroup concludes that the IG is effectively frozen or that the fix
is better deferred to a larger ophthalmology-observation redesign.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B preserves the curated chart codes the Eye Care IG
authors have already assembled (they are clinically useful and
implementers likely reference them), while correcting the structural
mistake the reporter is calling out: charts are instruments, not
methods. Moving them to `Observation.device` (via a small
`Device`/`DeviceVisualAcuityChart` profile) and introducing a proper
method-oriented ValueSet on `Observation.method` produces a profile
that is both semantically correct and backwards-usable for
implementers who were already sending those codes — they just move to
a different element rather than disappearing. Disposition A is cleaner
conceptually but throws away the existing curation effort; Disposition
C is not defensible long-term because the reporter's critique is
fundamentally correct. Before implementation, the workgroup should
request a `repo-analysis HL7/fhir-eyecare-ig` run so the concrete
authoring paths (profile StructureDefinition, ValueSet source, and any
generated pages to avoid editing) can be cited in the change ticket.
