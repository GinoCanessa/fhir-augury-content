## Summary

Eric Haas requests that `Condition.clinicalStatus` be changed back to a
minimum cardinality of 0 (it was made mandatory — `min = 1` — in the R6
Condition resource). His argument is that `clinicalStatus` is only
meaningful when the Condition is being used as a problem-list item; for
the other values in the `condition-category` binding
(`encounter-diagnosis`, `diagnostic-report-impression`) the status is
typically not available, not populated, or simply "unknown". As
evidence, he cites that US Core's Condition Encounter Diagnosis Profile
removed the status elements entirely for the encounter-diagnosis
use-case (referencing the Confluence page at
`https://confluence.hl7.org/.../345085086/...`), and notes that the R6
Condition resource's own comments acknowledge "the clinicalStatus may
be unknown" for non-problem-list conditions (e.g., conditions derived
from claims). His recommendation is to (a) restore `min = 0` on
`Condition.clinicalStatus` and (b) update the guidance to state that
the element SHOULD be populated when `Condition.category` is
`problem-list-item`.

The ticket is part of a small cluster of related Patient Care tickets
(all sharing the `ConditionStatuses` label) that question the design of
the Condition status elements:
[FHIR-54841](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54841) (the required
`condition-clinical` ValueSet is inadequate for many real-world
conditions), [FHIR-53241](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53241) (the
ValueSet description says "preferred" but the binding is "required"),
and [FHIR-54914](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54914) (clarify the
`verificationStatus` comments — already resolved with modification).
Together they suggest the workgroup should consider the
`Condition.clinicalStatus` cardinality, value set, binding strength,
and surrounding guidance as a coherent package rather than in
isolation.

## Related Jira Tickets

### [FHIR-54841: the required ValueSet for Condition.clinicalStatus is inadequate to represent clinical status](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54841)

Russell Leftwich argues that the required
`condition-clinical` value set cannot adequately express the clinical
status of many real-world conditions — chronic conditions like asthma
or sickle cell that are intermittently asymptomatic, and self-limited
conditions like strep throat or a sprained ankle. He gives concrete
examples showing how the existing values (`active`, `inactive`,
`remission`, `resolved`) can produce misleading population-health
inferences. This is directly adjacent to FHIR-54868: if the value set
is inadequate, mandating `clinicalStatus` (i.e., `min = 1`) forces
implementers to choose a value that misrepresents reality, which
strengthens the case for relaxing cardinality and/or the binding.
*Users involved:* Russell Leftwich (reporter, assignee).

### [FHIR-53241: Condition clinical status valueset description is inconsistent with its use](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53241)

Jose Costa-Teixeira points out that the `condition-clinical`
ValueSet's own definition calls it the *Preferred* value set for
Condition Clinical Status, while the actual binding on
`Condition.clinicalStatus` is *Required*. This inconsistency is
relevant because PCWG may want to consider binding strength alongside
cardinality — a "preferred" binding plus `min = 0` would together
give implementers room to omit the element or use an alternate code
when the supplied values do not fit. *Users involved:* Jose
Costa-Teixeira (reporter).

### [FHIR-54914: Clarify Condition.verificationStatus comments](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54914)

Kevin O'Donnell asked PCWG to clarify the `verificationStatus`
comments because the binding is Required but the comments imply
additional codes may be used. The ticket has been resolved (Persuasive
with Modification) with new wording explicitly noting that, *if
populated*, one code from the binding must be present but additional
codes may be added. The disposition implicitly accepts that
`verificationStatus` may be absent — a precedent that supports
similarly relaxing `clinicalStatus`. *Users involved:* Kevin O'Donnell
(reporter).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Restore `Condition.clinicalStatus` cardinality to `0..1` in R6
(reverting the recent change to `1..1`), and update the element's
Comments / guidance to state that `clinicalStatus` SHOULD be populated
when `Condition.category` is `problem-list-item`. Add an invariant on
Condition (e.g., `con-clinicalStatus-when-problem-list`) that
expresses the SHOULD as a warning-level constraint — for example:
`category.where(coding.code = 'problem-list-item').exists() implies
clinicalStatus.exists()`. Leave the existing `con-3`/`con-4`-style
constraints (entered-in-error and clinicalStatus-only-when-active)
intact, and update the Notes section that already acknowledges
clinicalStatus "may be unknown" for non-problem-list conditions to
cross-reference the new guidance.

#### Justification

This is precisely what the reporter requested and matches the
direction Rob Hausam proposed in the IPS Zulip thread back in 2021,
so there is multi-year cross-WG support. It mirrors the change that
was already made to AllergyIntolerance (`clinicalStatus` is `0..1`
there). It honours the resource's own Comments text, which today says
clinicalStatus "may be unknown" for non-problem-list conditions —
language that is incoherent with `min = 1`. And it removes a
real-world implementer burden: US Core's Encounter Diagnosis Profile
already had to suppress the status elements, IPS authors are forced
to invent semantically wrong values for "no known problems", and
claims-derived conditions cannot honestly express their lack of
status. The accompanying problem-list-item invariant preserves the
clinical safety intent (problem-list entries really do need a
status) without forcing it on use cases where it is meaningless.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep `Condition.clinicalStatus` at `1..1`, but make the change holistic
across the related Condition-status tickets: (1) add `unknown` (and
optionally `not-applicable`) to the `condition-clinical` value set so
the element can always be populated honestly; (2) relax the binding
strength from Required to Extensible (addressing FHIR-53241's
"preferred vs required" inconsistency); (3) clarify the Comments to
explain when `unknown` is appropriate (claims-derived,
encounter-diagnosis, IPS no-known-problems); and (4) coordinate with
FHIR-54841 so the value set additions also cover the chronic-but-
asymptomatic and self-limited cases.

#### Justification

This preserves the safety property that every Condition carries an
explicit, machine-checkable clinical status — a status of `unknown`
is more useful for query and analytics than an absent element,
because consumers do not have to distinguish "absent because not
applicable" from "absent because not asserted". It also folds in
adjacent ConditionStatuses tickets ([FHIR-54841](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54841),
[FHIR-53241](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53241)) so the workgroup
solves the underlying problem once rather than relaxing cardinality
now and revisiting binding/value set later. The trade-off is that it
is a bigger change (value set + binding + comments) and requires US
Core and IPS to re-profile to start emitting `unknown`, which is more
disruption than simply restoring `0..1`.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the cardinality reversion. Keep `Condition.clinicalStatus` at
`1..1` and direct profiling guides (US Core, IPS) to populate
`clinicalStatus` with `unknown` (or `active` for the no-known-problems
sentinel) in the use cases that today omit it. Document this expected
pattern in the resource Notes.

#### Justification

The R6 change to `min = 1` was deliberate and grounded in the desire
to make Condition records self-describing for clinical safety:
problem-list integration, decision support, and longitudinal record
analysis all benefit when every Condition has an explicit status
rather than a silent gap. Reverting to `0..1` is a normative-track
breaking change to a heavily used element — it forces every R6
implementer who has already updated to drop a constraint, and it
re-opens the door to the silent-omission ambiguity the change was
intended to close. The existing values (especially `active` and the
prospective `unknown` value, if added under Disposition B) are
adequate to populate the element in all the cited cases without
relaxing cardinality.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A is the most defensible immediate response: it is exactly
what the reporter asked for, it aligns with the precedent already set
on AllergyIntolerance, it is consistent with the resource's own
Comments text, and it has documented multi-year community support
from the IPS Zulip thread (where Rob Hausam — who chairs Vocabulary
and is heavily involved in PCWG — explicitly recommended the same
change). The proposed problem-list-item invariant preserves the
clinical-safety intent of `min = 1` for the use case where it
actually matters, while removing the burden from
encounter-diagnosis, IPS, and claims-derived Conditions.
Disposition B is the better long-term answer and the workgroup
should take it up — but it is properly the scope of FHIR-54841 and
FHIR-53241 and should not block this ticket. Disposition C is hard
to defend given that US Core has already had to work around the
mandatory status, the Condition Comments themselves contradict
`min = 1`, and the workgroup recently accepted (in
[FHIR-54914](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54914)) the analogous
"if populated" framing for `verificationStatus`.
