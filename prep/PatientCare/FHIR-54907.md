## Summary

The reporter is asking a series of clarifying questions about the
referral request/response flow described in the US SDOH Clinical Care
IG. Specifically, they want to confirm whether the request/response
pattern is fully documented elsewhere, and — given that the IG's
"Out of Scope" section excludes bulk status checks and directory-style
queries — what behavior implementers should expect when only a single
result can be returned. The questions fall into two groups: (1) a
clarification on whether the referral source is targeting a specific
referral target ("a specific CBO") or making a general "find any CBO
with capacity" query; and (2) if it is general, what prioritization
governs the single returned result, whether repeat queries are
deterministic, and whether returning only one result is adequate when
language, distance, or other client-specific criteria might prefer a
different option. The author has already responded that the IG is
strictly a one-at-a-time, targeted-referral model (the upstream
selection of the target is out of scope) and pointed to the existing
Capacity Status and Healthcare Service for Referral Management Profile
pages as the canonical request/response documentation. No
cross-referenced tickets or related Zulip discussions were found.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat the ticket as a documentation/clarification request against the
US SDOH Clinical Care IG and update the relevant narrative pages
(notably the *Request/Response* description and the *Capacity Status*
and *Healthcare Service for Referral Management Profile* pages) so
that the ballot reader does not have to ask these questions. Concrete
edits:

- In the page that describes the request/response interaction, add an
  explicit sentence stating that the referral source always queries a
  *specific*, pre-selected referral target (CBO), and that selecting
  the target is upstream and out of scope.
- Add a back-reference from the "Out of Scope" bullets (no bulk status
  checks, no directory-style queries) to the new clarifying paragraph,
  so the implication "therefore one result at a time" is explicit
  rather than inferred.
- On the *Capacity Status* page, document the response cardinality
  (single Healthcare Service / capacity assertion per request),
  determinism expectations (the same target queried twice should
  return the same data subject to capacity changes between calls), and
  state plainly that prioritization across multiple CBOs is not
  performed by this IG.
- Add a "Future Considerations" paragraph that records the
  reporter's downstream concerns (language match, proximity,
  multi-result search) as known limitations to revisit in a future
  release, without committing to them in this version.

#### Justification

The ticket is labeled `ballot` and is currently `Triaged` with no
resolution; the comment from the IG author already supplies the
substantive answers. Folding those answers into the narrative is the
lowest-risk way to close the comment, satisfies the ballot
reconciliation requirement (the reporter must be able to see their
question addressed in the spec, not only in the Jira thread), and
respects existing FHIR IG practice of keeping the conformance
boundary explicit in prose rather than only in conformance
artifacts. Because no profiles, value sets, or operations are being
changed, there is no risk to existing implementer commitments.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Address the underlying need — implementers and reviewers being
unsure how to use the request/response flow when only one result is
ever returned — by adding non-normative *guidance* rather than only
prose clarifications. Specifically:

- Add a worked example (Bundle / scenario walkthrough) on the
  *Healthcare Service for Referral Management Profile* page showing
  the upstream selection step (out of scope, but illustrated as a
  caller-side activity), the targeted capacity query, and a typical
  single-result response.
- Add an `additional` SearchParameter or a documented `_count=1` /
  `_sort` convention only if review confirms the underlying
  interaction actually uses a `search` interaction; otherwise document
  that the interaction is a direct `read`/`Task`-driven query against
  a known target and that FHIR search semantics (sort, count,
  pagination) intentionally do not apply.
- Open a follow-up tracker (new Jira) for the multi-result / directory
  use case so the workgroup has a place to collect future
  requirements without reopening this ticket.

#### Justification

This addresses the same comment but goes one step further than
Disposition A by giving implementers a concrete pattern to copy,
which historically reduces the number of follow-up clarification
tickets in subsequent ballots. It also separates "fix this ballot
comment" from "design the next-generation directory/capacity search,"
which keeps this ticket's scope tight. The trade-off vs. A is more
authoring effort and slightly more review surface for the workgroup;
the upside is a measurably better implementer experience and a clean
parking spot for the reporter's deeper concerns.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (Not Persuasive / Already Answered)

#### Proposal

Resolve the ticket as *Not Persuasive — Answered in Comments* (or the
equivalent disposition the workgroup uses for clarification-only
tickets). Record the substantive answer already given by the IG
author in the resolution text — the referral request flow is
one-at-a-time against a pre-selected referral target, target
selection is upstream and out of scope, and the *Capacity Status* and
*Healthcare Service for Referral Management Profile* pages already
describe the request/response — and close without changing the
specification.

#### Justification

The ticket asks four questions; all four were answered authoritatively
in the single comment from the IG author, and the cited IG pages
already exist. Strictly speaking, no normative or even narrative
change is *required* for the ballot to be internally consistent.
Declining is defensible on scope grounds: the deeper concerns the
reporter raises (language, proximity, multi-result) are explicitly
acknowledged as out of scope for this version of the IG, and
expanding to address them would constitute a substantive design
change well beyond a clarification ticket.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter's questions are real reader-comprehension issues, not
just a private misunderstanding: they read the "Out of Scope" section,
correctly inferred a behavioral consequence ("only one result"), and
could not find that consequence stated anywhere. That is the textbook
signature of a documentation gap, and the ballot reconciliation
process expects gaps to be closed in the spec rather than only in
Jira. Disposition C is technically defensible but tends to attract
repeat tickets in the next ballot cycle from different reviewers
hitting the same gap. Disposition B is attractive but couples this
ticket to a larger authoring effort (a worked example) that the
workgroup may not have bandwidth to land in this cycle; it is a good
*follow-up* to A rather than a substitute for it. Accepting as
requested — narrowly, as a documentation clarification on the
existing *Request/Response*, *Capacity Status*, and *Healthcare
Service for Referral Management Profile* pages — closes the comment,
records the existing limits as deliberate, and leaves room to pursue
the multi-result / directory work as a separate, properly scoped
future change.
