## Summary

Lloyd McKenzie reports that projects are increasingly using the
`Communication` resource as if it were equivalent to `MessageHeader`,
which blurs the line between a *record of a communication* (Communication)
and the FHIR *messaging paradigm* (MessageHeader + Bundle of type
`message`). He cites two contrasting Da Vinci examples: PDeX uses
`CommunicationRequest`/`Communication` to ask for and record the sharing
of provider-level documentation (which he considers acceptable), while
Da Vinci Alerts uses `Communication` inside a Bundle to convey what is
effectively the equivalent of v2 ADT notifications (admit/discharge,
result available, prescription cancelled), which he considers messaging
in disguise. The ticket asks Patient Care (owners of Communication),
INM (owners of MessageHeader/Messaging), FHIR-I, and MnM to agree on,
and write into the spec, explicit "Boundaries and Relationships"
language for `Communication`, `MessageHeader`, and the Messaging page so
that designers and implementers can tell the two paradigms apart.

A directly related ticket — [FHIR-28235](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-28235)
"Consider rephrasing the text and explanation" (Patient Care, Applied,
Persuasive with Modification) — has already adjusted some of the
introductory wording on `Communication`, and the most recent comment by
Michelle Miller (2023-08-24) flags that work as a starting point and
asks the WG to propose any further boundary language between
`Communication` and `MessageHeader`. The ticket was last touched in
2024 but remains in `Triaged`/`Unresolved`.

## Details

**Description:**

Projects are starting to use Communication as equivalent to MessageHeader
- which is a problem.  We need to clarify the scope of Communication and
MessageHeader so that everyone knows when each should be used.

As examples: Da Vinci PDex is using CommunicationRequest to ask for
documentation to be shared and then Communication to document that the
information was shared (and doing the sharing as part of that). I think
this is ok because it's happening at the provider level (providers
asking for the data, providers prepping and providing the data.)

Da Vinci Alerts, on the other hand, is using Communication within a
Bundle to essentially do the equivalent of v2 ADT messages sending
notifications that patient has been admitted, patient has been
discharged, lab result has been received, prescription has been
cancelled, etc.  That seems to be stepping squarely into the space
expected to be covered by messaging.

However, there is no guidance in MessageHeader, Communication or
messaging section that explains what the boundaries are.  We desparately
need to provide such language - and get consensus on what the boundaries
should be.

## Related Jira Tickets

### [FHIR-28235: Consider rephrasing the text and explanation](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-28235)

Patient Care change request asking for the introductory wording of the
`Communication` resource to be revised — in particular, dropping the
implication that examples must be in the past tense and rephrasing
"This resource is a record of a communication even if it is planned or
has failed." in favour of language that frames `Communication` as a
container/conveyance of communication between parties whose timing
(planned/failed/succeeded) is contextual. The ticket has been resolved
"Persuasive with Modification" and is in `Applied` status; Michelle
Miller's 2023 comment flags it as the most recent text update relevant
to the FHIR-23061 boundary work and suggests reviewing it before
drafting any additional boundary language.

Reporter: Jens Villadsen; Assignee: Unassigned; Work Group: Patient
Care.

## Related Zulip Discussions

### [#implementers > Messaging vs. Communication](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/implementers/topic/Messaging%20vs.%20Communication/near/172543286)

Lloyd McKenzie opened the thread (2019-08-05) on the same day he filed
FHIR-23061 to gather cross-WG input on the boundary between Messaging
and `Communication`. Grahame Grieve initially didn't see the overlap;
Lloyd illustrated it by pointing out that both `Communication` and
`MessageHeader` can identify a sender, recipient, and reason and
reference a payload — bundle either one and POST it to a `process-this`
operation and they look indistinguishable. Paul Knapp argued the topic
belongs to INM (since notification/messaging is in their scope), and
the group agreed that Patient Care (Communication owner), INM
(MessageHeader owner), FHIR-I (paradigms), and MnM (methodology) all
need to be at the table. After several scheduling exchanges, the
participants agreed to take a quarter at the Atlanta WGM (Tue Q3,
joint PC/FHIR-I, with INM invited) to work on it.

On substance, John Moehrke and Thomas Tveit Rosenlund both said they
had implicitly assumed `Communication` was for one-off, human-driven
items (SMS, email) while messaging was for routine back-end push, and
were surprised by the overlap. Thomas argued the original Communication
text ("a record of a communication that has occurred — does not
represent the actual flow of communication") should be reasserted at
the very top of the resource, and that introducing a
"second MessageHeader flavour" would just add complexity. Eric Haas was
skeptical that prose alone would help ("if it looks and acts like a
duck…") and proposed instead a master table mapping transactions to
recommended paradigms, possibly as a new "How to in FHIR" page; he
worried a quarter spent on wording would yield little. Lloyd disagreed
with putting the guidance only in an architecture page — implementers
won't find it — and pushed for explicit "Boundaries and Relationships"
text in `Communication`, `MessageHeader`, and the Messaging page, plus
clear "what to do instead" guidance to discourage abuse without killing
legitimate uses. No final wording was agreed; the thread converged on
the need for spec text and a WGM quarter to draft it.

Participants: Lloyd McKenzie, Grahame Grieve, Paul Knapp, Michelle
(Moseman) Miller, Eric Haas, John Moehrke, Vassil Peytchev, Thomas
Tveit Rosenlund.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add explicit "Boundaries and Relationships" prose (and complete the
work that FHIR-28235 started on `Communication`'s opening paragraph) in
three places:

1. **`Communication` resource page** — extend the existing
   "Boundaries and Relationships" / "Wrapping" sections so the very
   first paragraph reasserts that `Communication` is the *record* that
   some information was conveyed between a sender and a recipient,
   independent of the channel used (FHIR, v2, CCDA, SMS, email,
   phone, paper). Add a short subsection "Communication vs
   MessageHeader" stating that `Communication` SHOULD NOT be used as
   the envelope of an event-driven, multi-resource push between
   systems (that is the role of a `message` Bundle headed by
   `MessageHeader`); when the *act* of conveyance itself needs to be
   recorded clinically, `Communication` is the right resource even if
   the underlying transport happened to be FHIR messaging.
2. **`MessageHeader` resource page** — mirror language under
   "Boundaries and Relationships": `MessageHeader` is the routing /
   event header of a `message` Bundle and is intended for asynchronous,
   event-driven, multi-resource pushes (the v2 ADT-class use cases);
   it is not a clinical record of the fact that information was shared.
3. **Messaging page** (`messaging.html`) — add a paragraph
   distinguishing the messaging paradigm from the use of
   `CommunicationRequest`/`Communication` for ad-hoc sharing of a
   payload between two named parties at the provider/business level
   (PDeX-style), and stating that recurring, system-to-system event
   notifications (Alerts-style) belong in messaging.

Adopt Lloyd's 2020 draft Google doc as the starting text and
incorporate any wording changes already applied via FHIR-28235.

#### Justification

This is what the ticket explicitly asks for, what the joint PC/FHIR-I
WGM quarter agreed to produce, and what most Zulip participants
endorsed: prose in the spec, in the places implementers actually
look. The change is editorial (no resource-model change), backward-
compatible, and leverages work already drafted (the 2020 Google doc)
and partially landed (FHIR-28235). It directly addresses the two
real-world examples in the description (PDeX vs Alerts) by giving
designers a defensible answer to "should I use `Communication` for
this?".

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

In addition to (a slimmer version of) the boundary text in Disposition
A, add a new architecture-section page — e.g.
`exchange-paradigm-selection.html` — owned jointly by FHIR-I/MnM, that
contains a decision table mapping common interoperability scenarios
(record that a clinician was notified, push an ADT-style event, share
a one-off document with a named recipient, request that data be sent,
asynchronous bulk delivery, …) to the recommended paradigm
(REST + `Communication`, REST + `Task`, messaging + `MessageHeader`,
subscriptions, etc.). Cross-link the table from the
"Boundaries and Relationships" sections of `Communication`,
`CommunicationRequest`, `MessageHeader`, `Task`, `ServiceRequest`, and
the Messaging and RESTful API pages, so implementers always have one
canonical answer.

This addresses the broader "CommunicationRequest vs Task" boundary
that the WGM minutes also flagged and that GF#13389 covers.

#### Justification

Eric Haas's critique on Zulip — that prose tucked into individual
resource pages will not stop implementers from treating
`Communication` as a bundle header if it structurally fits — is
plausible, and a single decision table is easier to cite in design
reviews and IGs than three separate paragraphs. It also lets us solve
the closely-coupled `CommunicationRequest` vs `Task` boundary in the
same pass, which the Patient Care WGM minutes explicitly called out
as the second half of the same problem. Trade-off vs Disposition A:
slightly more work and requires buy-in from FHIR-I/MnM as page owners,
plus the risk Lloyd flagged that implementers may never read an
architecture page — which is mitigated by mandating
"Boundaries and Relationships" links from every relevant resource into
the table.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as "Not Persuasive — Already Addressed". Argue that the wording
changes applied via FHIR-28235 (resolved Persuasive with Modification,
currently in `Applied`) plus the existing
`Communication` "Wrapping" section
(http://build.fhir.org/communication.html#wrapping) and the
`MessageHeader` "Boundaries" text are now sufficient, and that
projects choosing between `Communication`-in-a-Bundle and a `message`
Bundle should be guided by their own implementation guides rather than
by additional core-spec prose.

#### Justification

Defensible only on the grounds that the ticket has sat in `Triaged`
since 2019, partial wording fixes have landed via FHIR-28235, and no
one has produced final boundary text in the intervening five years.
Closing it would acknowledge that the WG cannot reach consensus and
that the issue is better handled in implementation guides (e.g. the
Da Vinci IGs that triggered the report). This is the weakest option:
the underlying problem Lloyd reported — implementers reaching for
`Communication` as a stand-in for `MessageHeader` — was not resolved
by FHIR-28235 (which only retouched the introductory paragraph), and
the joint PC/INM/FHIR-I community on Zulip explicitly asked for spec
wording. Declining now would also leave the
`CommunicationRequest`-vs-`Task` boundary (raised in the same WGM
minutes and in GF#13389) without a home.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket has an unusually strong consensus trail behind it: a
multi-WG Zulip thread that explicitly asked for spec wording, a
PC/FHIR-I WGM quarter that converged on the same conclusion, an
existing draft text from the reporter (the 2020 Google doc), and a
recent (2023) WG comment that asks the group to finish the job on top
of FHIR-28235. The change is editorial and low-risk, fits cleanly into
the existing "Boundaries and Relationships" sections of `Communication`
and `MessageHeader`, and requires no resource-model changes. The
strongest argument against pure-prose (Eric Haas's "looks like a duck"
objection) is best handled by *adding* the decision-table page from
Disposition B as a follow-up, not by replacing the per-resource
boundary text — implementers really do read the resource pages they
are working with, and that is the point of intervention. Patient Care
should adopt Lloyd's 2020 draft (reconciled with FHIR-28235), publish
the PC-owned text in `Communication` and the joint PC/INM-owned text
in `MessageHeader`/Messaging, and then open a follow-up tracker for
the `exchange-paradigm-selection` page if the WG wants to pursue the
Disposition B angle.
