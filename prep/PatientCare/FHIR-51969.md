## Summary

Heath Frankel (reporter) observes that the IPS PregnancyHx Observation entry
profiles — `ObservationPregnancyEddUvIps`, `ObservationPregnancyOutcomeUvIps`,
and `ObservationPregnancyStatusUvIps` — flag their respective `value[x]`
slices as Must Support and attach obligations of `SHALL:populate-if-known`,
`SHALL:handle`, and `SHOULD:display`. The IPS socialHistory Observation entry
profiles `ObservationAlcoholUseUvIps` (slice `valueQuantity`) and
`ObservationTobaccoUseUvIps` (slice `valueCodeableConcept`) do not follow that
pattern: their `value[x]` slices are not flagged Must Support and have no
obligations attached. The reporter sees no design reason for the
inconsistency and recommends adding MS plus the same three obligations to the
relevant value slices in the two socialHistory profiles, and references his
own pull request [HL7/fhir-ips#85](https://github.com/HL7/fhir-ips/pull/85)
which implements the proposed change. The IPS weekly call on 2025-08-27
discussed the request (see comment from `jdamore`) and noted that the
asymmetry was deliberate because ISO 27269 imposes substantially fewer
requirements on social-history observations than on pregnancy observations;
the ticket was resolved as **Considered for Future Use** (after IPS 2.0
publication) and the PR was closed without merge on 2025-09-03.

There are no other Jira tickets cross-referenced to this issue.

## Proposed Dispositions

> The dispositions below are written against the ticket text, the linked PR,
> and the IPS-call discussion. They will need to be re-grounded against the

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the `HL7/fhir-ips` IG, update the StructureDefinitions for
`ObservationAlcoholUseUvIps` and `ObservationTobaccoUseUvIps` so that the
`value[x]` slice on each profile (`valueQuantity` for alcohol use,
`valueCodeableConcept` for tobacco use) is flagged `mustSupport = true` and
carries the same three obligations already used on the PregnancyHx entry
profiles' value slices: `SHALL:populate-if-known`, `SHALL:handle`, and
`SHOULD:display`. This is the change implemented by PR
[HL7/fhir-ips#85](https://github.com/HL7/fhir-ips/pull/85), which can be
re-opened (or re-submitted) and merged into the post-2.0 development
stream. Update the relevant profile narrative / intro pages so the new MS
flags and obligations are documented for implementers, and add a brief
note to the IPS change log.

#### Justification

The proposal restores intra-IG consistency: every value slice on an IPS
Observation entry profile would carry the same MS + obligation pattern,
which is easier for implementers to reason about and easier for IG-level
tooling (Inferno, validators) to verify. The reporter is the author of the
already-prepared PR, so the editorial cost is low. Aligning MS/obligations
across all entry profiles is also consistent with the general FHIR
direction of using obligations to express implementer responsibilities
explicitly rather than leaving them implicit.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Defer the change until **after IPS 2.0 publication** (matching the
existing "Considered for Future Use" resolution), and in the interim
treat it as input to a broader review of MS + obligation policy across
all IPS Observation entry profiles. Concretely: keep the ticket open
against the post-2.0 milestone, ask the reporter to leave PR
[HL7/fhir-ips#85](https://github.com/HL7/fhir-ips/pull/85) on the branch
as a reference implementation, and add an action on the IPS workgroup to
produce a short policy note covering (a) which value slices on which
entry profiles get MS, (b) which obligations apply, and (c) how those
choices map to ISO 27269 requirements. Apply the alcohol/tobacco changes
together with any other consistency fixes that policy review surfaces, in
a single coordinated PR for the next IPS release.

#### Justification

The IPS-call discussion (jdamore, 2025-08-27) makes clear the asymmetry
is **not** an oversight — it reflects a deliberate decision driven by
ISO 27269 requirements being weaker for social history than for
pregnancy. Accepting the request piecemeal risks creating a different
inconsistency: MS/obligations diverging from the ISO baseline that is
the IG's normative source. A short policy review keeps the door open to
the change the reporter wants while avoiding ad-hoc, profile-by-profile
decisions, and avoids destabilising IPS 2.0 immediately before
publication. It also gives the workgroup a venue to revisit other entry
profiles (procedure, allergy, etc.) under the same lens.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Persuasive with Modification — No Change** (or
equivalent): document the reporter's observation in the IPS profile
intro narratives for `ObservationAlcoholUseUvIps` and
`ObservationTobaccoUseUvIps`, explicitly stating that the difference in
MS/obligation treatment between socialHistory and pregnancyHx entry
profiles is intentional and rooted in the differing ISO 27269
requirements, but make no change to the StructureDefinitions themselves.
Do not merge PR [HL7/fhir-ips#85](https://github.com/HL7/fhir-ips/pull/85).

#### Justification

The asymmetry the ticket flags is the result of a conscious design
choice tied to the IG's normative source (ISO 27269), as captured by
jdamore on the 2025-08-27 IPS call. Adding MS and obligations the
underlying standard does not require would over-constrain implementers
of the IPS social-history section without a corresponding requirement
from ISO. Documenting the rationale in the profile narratives addresses
the reporter's underlying concern (lack of visible reason for the
asymmetry) without changing the conformance surface of IPS 2.0.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B (Alternative Approach — defer to post-2.0
and pair with a short MS/obligations policy review).

The reporter's observation is a legitimate consistency concern and the
PR is already prepared, so an outright decline (C) leaves a recurring
question on the table and discards usable work. At the same time, the
IPS workgroup has explicitly stated on the 2025-08-27 call that the
current treatment is deliberate and tied to ISO 27269 — so adopting the
change as-is right before IPS 2.0 publication (A) risks introducing
MS/obligation choices that the IG's normative source does not support,
and sets a precedent of profile-level adjustments without an explicit
policy. Disposition B preserves the reporter's contribution, matches the
existing "Considered for Future Use" resolution, and gives Patient Care
/ IPS a small, scoped piece of policy work to anchor the alcohol/tobacco
change (and any analogous future asks) in a defensible rule rather than
case-by-case judgment. Once `repo-analysis` for `HL7/fhir-ips` is
available, the implementation steps in B should be re-grounded in the
briefing's authoring roots and change recipes.
