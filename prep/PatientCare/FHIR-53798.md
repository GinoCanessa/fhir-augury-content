## Summary

This ticket asks the SDOH Clinical Care (Gravity) IG authors to strengthen
the narrative in Section 2.2 ("Challenges of SDOH Data Interoperability")
so that it explicitly recognises that SDOH data must move across sectors
of care — i.e., that intervention activity routinely originates outside
the direct healthcare provider landscape. The reporter proposed adding a
new bullet stating that "80% or more of the interventions that are
intended to resolve identified needs are based on actions from outside
of the direct healthcare provider landscape," with a follow-up comment
softening the claim from a numeric threshold to "a large majority."

The ticket has been resolved **Persuasive with Modification** and is
slated for HL7 Patient Care block vote (label `block-vote-2`, see the
related Zulip post). The agreed modification adds a new bullet to the
Section 2.2 list — "A large majority of the interventions that are
intended to resolve identified needs are based on actions from outside
of the direct healthcare provider landscape. There are technological
barriers that limit the ability to exchange data." — and rewords the
section introduction to add "exchange" alongside "documentation" and
"aggregation," and to add "social care systems" alongside "EHRs … and
related systems." No structural / artifact / profile change is
involved; the modification is narrative-only.

There are no Jira ticket cross-references and no GitHub PR/issue links
on this ticket, so its full context is the ticket itself plus the
block-vote announcement on Zulip.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the resolution exactly as recorded on the ticket: in the US SDOH
Clinical Care IG, Section 2.2 ("Challenges of SDOH Data
Interoperability"), (a) add a new bullet — "A large majority of the
interventions that are intended to resolve identified needs are based
on actions from outside of the direct healthcare provider landscape.
There are technological barriers that limit the ability to exchange
data." — and (b) rewrite the section's introductory sentence to
"Challenges related to the systematic documentation, **exchange,** and
aggregation of SDOH data in EHRs, **social care systems,** and related
systems include:". This is a narrative-only edit to Section 2.2 of the
SDOHCC IG; no profile, value-set, extension, or example changes are
implied.

#### Justification

This is the wording that the WG and submitter converged on through the
ticket comments (the reporter's "80% or more" was explicitly softened to
"a large majority" by JIRAUSER24103 and supported by JIRAUSER30951),
and it was already filed as Persuasive with Modification. It frames a
real, repeatedly-cited interoperability concern (SDOH workflows
crossing healthcare and social-care domains), it adds the missing
verb "exchange" to the section's framing — which aligns Section 2.2
with the IG's actual purpose (data exchange, not just internal
documentation) — and it adds "social care systems" as a first-class
peer to EHRs, which is consistent with how Gravity defines the
ecosystem elsewhere in the IG. Because the change is purely
introductory narrative, there is no implementer impact and no risk to
existing conformance.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt the spirit of the resolution but tighten the second sentence to
avoid an unsupported empirical claim. Concretely: keep the
introduction edit unchanged ("documentation, **exchange,** and
aggregation … in EHRs, **social care systems,** and related systems"),
but replace the new bullet with: "Many of the interventions intended
to resolve identified needs originate outside the direct healthcare
provider landscape — for example, with community-based organizations,
social-service agencies, and government programs — and current
technology stacks impose barriers to exchanging the necessary data
across these sectors." Optionally, attach a citation or link to the
Gravity Project's reference materials supporting the prevalence
claim.

#### Justification

The original "80% or more" figure was withdrawn in comments precisely
because it could not be defensibly sourced; "a large majority" is the
softened replacement, but it is still an unattributed quantitative
claim in a normative-style narrative section. Substituting an
illustrative, qualitative formulation (with concrete examples of the
non-healthcare actors and an explicit naming of the technology
barrier) preserves the ticket's actual intent — making the
cross-sector reality visible — while removing a claim a reviewer or
auditor could object to. The introduction edit is uncontroversial and
should be kept either way.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket without changing Section 2.2. Treat the cross-sector
framing as already implicit in the IG's existing description of SDOH
domains, referrals (`SDOHCC ServiceRequest`) and task handoff
(`SDOHCC Task`), and decline to add narrative bullets that are not
tied to a normative requirement.

#### Justification

The change is non-normative narrative; it does not add or modify any
profile, extension, value set, or example, and reviewers could argue
that a ballot disposition should not be spent on prose tweaks that do
not change conformance. There is also a defensibility argument: a
narrative bullet asserting "a large majority of interventions … are
based on actions from outside of the direct healthcare provider
landscape" is an empirical claim with no citation in the IG, and
declining avoids enshrining an unsourced figure. This option is
weakest because it overrides a Persuasive-with-Modification resolution
that the WG and submitter already agreed to, and it loses the genuinely
useful "exchange" / "social care systems" introduction edit.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is already resolved Persuasive with Modification, the
reporter and a second commenter have explicitly endorsed the softened
wording, and the change is bounded to one section's narrative — there
is no profile, value set, or implementer impact to weigh against it.
The introduction edit ("exchange" + "social care systems") is a real
substantive improvement: it brings Section 2.2's framing in line with
what the SDOHCC IG actually specifies (cross-organisation data
exchange between EHRs and social-care systems), and several other
tickets in the same block vote (notably FHIR-53799 "Language revision
to recognize sectors of care" and FHIR-54603 "Revisions to Section 3.
SDOH Clinical Care Background") suggest the WG is intentionally
sweeping the IG's narrative for cross-sector framing. Disposition B is
a reasonable fallback if a reviewer at the block-vote meeting objects
to the unsourced "large majority" phrasing — in that case the WG can
adopt the qualitative wording above without re-opening the resolution
— but the default should be to apply the agreed text as written and
move the ticket through the block vote.
