## Summary

Rob McClure reports that the LOINC codes assigned to the document
sections shown in the PACIO Transitions of Care (ToC) IG's "Transitions
of Care Document Structure Bundle" image do not match the assumed
clinical meaning of those sections. Two specific examples are called
out: an "Instructions" section bound to LOINC `59271-7`, and a
"Medication equipment" section bound to LOINC `57829-4`. The reporter
believes other section codes in that diagram may be similarly
mismatched, and asks that all section LOINCs be reviewed and corrected,
noting that **new LOINC codes may have to be requested from Regenstrief**
where no suitable existing code can be found. The image is currently the
only place in the IG that enumerates the intended LOINCs for these
sections, so any fix must address both the diagram and any normative
binding artifacts that flow from it. No cross-referenced Jira tickets,
Zulip threads, or GitHub items were surfaced for this ticket.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat this as a substantive content fix against the PACIO Transitions
of Care IG. Concretely:

1. Enumerate every section shown in the "Transitions of Care Document
   Structure Bundle" diagram and treat that list as the authoritative
   working set of section codes for the IG.
2. For each section, validate the currently-assigned LOINC against the
   official LOINC concept (long common name, component, scale,
   property) and document whether it matches the section's intended
   clinical meaning.
3. Replace mismatched codes with the correct existing LOINC where one
   exists. The two examples called out — "Instructions" using `59271-7`
   and "Medication equipment" using `57829-4` — must be re-evaluated as
   part of this pass.
4. Where no suitable LOINC concept exists, file new-term requests with
   Regenstrief and track the request IDs in the ticket so the IG can
   adopt the new codes once published.
5. Lift the section/code list out of the diagram into a normative
   artifact that downstream profiles bind to (e.g., a ValueSet of
   permitted section codes plus, where appropriate, a fixed/required
   binding on the corresponding `Composition.section.code` slices).
   Regenerate the diagram from that source so the diagram and the
   normative binding cannot drift again.

#### Justification

The reporter is asserting a factual mismatch between section names in
the IG diagram and the LOINC codes bound to them, and flags that the
diagram is the *only* place the IG enumerates these codes. That is a
correctness problem (wrong code on a section) compounded by a
maintainability problem (a picture as the source of truth). FHIR design
guidance is consistent on both points: terminology bindings should live
in ValueSets/CodeSystems and be referenced from profiles, not encoded in
images, and section codes in CDA/FHIR documents are expected to align
with the LOINC concept's clinical meaning so that downstream consumers
(e.g., USCDI/USCDI+ aligned receivers, C-CDA-on-FHIR mappings) can rely
on them. Going through Regenstrief for genuinely missing concepts is
the standard escape hatch when no existing LOINC fits.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the underlying concern but scope the immediate fix narrowly and
defer the structural change:

1. In this ballot cycle, correct **only** the two sections explicitly
   identified by the reporter ("Instructions" → re-bind to a LOINC whose
   meaning is "discharge/transfer instructions to patient"; "Medication
   equipment" → re-bind to a LOINC whose meaning matches durable
   medical equipment / medication-administration devices), and update
   the diagram to match.
2. Open a follow-on tracker (linked to FHIR-50316) for a full audit of
   the remaining sections in the diagram, plus the diagram-to-ValueSet
   refactor described in Disposition A.
3. Add an editorial note in the IG stating that the diagram is
   illustrative and that section codes are normatively defined in the
   Composition profile / referenced ValueSet, to set reader expectations
   while the audit is in flight.

#### Justification

The reporter explicitly says "I have not reviewed them all", so the
evidence base for changing every section is currently anecdotal. A
two-step approach lets the WG ship the unambiguous corrections quickly
without holding the ballot on a wider review that may itself require
new LOINC term requests with Regenstrief turnaround time. Splitting the
work also separates the *terminology* fix from the *IG structure* fix
(diagram vs. normative binding), which lowers reviewer load and reduces
the chance of regressions in the document Bundle example. Trade-off vs.
A: the IG temporarily ships with known-suspect codes in sections that
were not individually re-verified, which is mitigated by the editorial
note and the follow-on tracker.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the ticket as filed on the grounds that it does not
specifically identify which codes are wrong nor propose replacements,
asking the reporter to refile per-section tickets with the offending
section, the current LOINC, the proposed LOINC (or a Regenstrief term
request), and the rationale.

#### Justification

The Patient Care WG can defensibly push back on a "review everything,
some are wrong" request because actioning it requires the WG itself to
do the per-code analysis the reporter says has not yet been done. In
practice, however, this is the weakest option: the reporter has
already identified two concrete mismatches with codes named, and the
diagram-as-source-of-truth concern is a structural defect in the IG
regardless of which specific codes turn out to be wrong. Declining
risks shipping an IG where the WG has been told a normative diagram is
incorrect and has chosen not to investigate.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B threads the needle between A and C. It commits the WG to
fixing the two concrete mismatches the reporter has actually evidenced
("Instructions"/`59271-7` and "Medication equipment"/`57829-4`) within
this ballot cycle, while opening a tracked follow-up for the full
section-by-section audit and for moving the section-code catalogue out
of the diagram and into a normatively-bound ValueSet referenced by the
Composition profile. Disposition A is the right *eventual* end state
but bundles a potentially multi-section LOINC audit (some of which may
require new Regenstrief terms) into a single ticket and risks blocking
the ballot on items that are not yet substantiated. Disposition C
ignores the two named, concrete defects the reporter has already
documented. B fixes what is known to be wrong now, sets up the IG for
a clean structural fix later, and leaves an explicit editorial signal
to readers in the interim.
