## Summary

The reporter objects to language in **Section 3.2 "Scope of this IG"** of the US SDOH Clinical Care IG, specifically the final bullet that describes the IG as "defining a RESTful **closed loop** referral process to manage **interventions**." The reporter argues this language is misaligned with the Gravity Project's co-designed care and data model, because roughly 80% of social-care interventions occur *outside* the scope of healthcare practitioners and documentation systems, and many are delivered in community settings independently of any healthcare screening/referral. The ticket asserts that (a) the IG cannot legitimately claim to define a process for another sector of care, and (b) the current phrasing creates implementer expectations that will make FHIR data flows unimplementable. The reporter proposes reframing the RESTful process as *establishing a treatment relationship between healthcare teams and social care/social service providers*, with interventions largely occurring in community settings.

The ticket was resolved as **Not Persuasive with Modification**: the workgroup declined to adopt the reporter's specific language but committed to revising Section 3.2 to better reflect the IG's scope and to align it with the Social Care Co-Design work. The ticket is bundled into the Gravity Project's Patient Care WG **Block Vote 2** (see Zulip discussion below). There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets and no GitHub cross-references.

      1. The language employed here creates expectations that do not align with the co-designed care and data model and will cause FHIR enabled data flows to be unimplementable.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Replace the final bullet of Section 3.2 "Scope of this IG" in the US SDOH Clinical Care IG so that, instead of framing the IG as "defining a RESTful closed loop referral process to manage interventions," it states that the IG "defines a RESTful process that establishes a treatment relationship between healthcare teams and social care / social service providers, recognising that the resulting interventions are largely provided in community settings and outside the direct scope of healthcare documentation systems." Remove or soften the phrase "closed loop … to manage interventions" wherever it appears in the scope narrative, and add a sentence acknowledging that approximately 80% of social-care interventions occur outside healthcare practitioner workflows.

#### Justification

This literally applies the reporter's proposed text. It is consistent with the Gravity Project's co-designed care and data model and with the framing in related tickets (FHIR-53798, FHIR-53799) that have already been accepted to recognise sectors of care. It eliminates implementer expectations that the IG could audit or guarantee completion of community-based interventions, which the ticket argues is the root cause of unimplementable data flows.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach (Rewrite Section 3.2 for Alignment Without Adopting the Reporter's Exact Wording) — matches actual resolution

#### Proposal

Do not merge the reporter's literal wording. Instead, revise Section 3.2 in its entirety to (1) drop or re-scope the "closed loop referral process to manage interventions" phrasing so that it no longer implies the IG governs intervention execution in non-healthcare sectors; (2) explicitly delimit the IG's scope to the data exchanges that occur *between* healthcare teams and social care / social service providers (referrals, task assignment, status updates, outcome reporting); (3) add a brief acknowledgement that a large portion of actual interventions are delivered in community settings outside the documentation systems this IG profiles; and (4) cross-reference the Social Care Co-Design materials and any companion ticket outputs (FHIR-53798, FHIR-53799, FHIR-54603) so the narrative is consistent across the Section 3 rewrite. Mark the ticket **Not Persuasive with Modification**.

#### Justification

This is the disposition the WG has actually recorded on the ticket. It addresses the underlying concern (misalignment with the co-design model, unrealistic implementer expectations) without inheriting the drafting style of the reporter's suggested sentence, which mixes normative assertions ("establishes a treatment relationship") with scoping language in a way that could create its own interpretation problems. It also lets Section 3.2 be edited holistically together with the adjacent "sectors of care" tickets (FHIR-53798/53799) rather than patched bullet-by-bullet, which the Zulip block-vote bundle implies is the WG's intent.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket **Not Persuasive** and leave Section 3.2 unchanged. Rationale: the existing "closed loop referral process to manage interventions" language describes the *FHIR data exchange* the IG profiles (Task/ServiceRequest/Communication flows between healthcare and social-care actors), not the underlying real-world intervention, and readers of an HL7 implementation guide are expected to interpret scope statements in terms of the data artefacts the IG constrains.

#### Justification

Defensible only on the narrow ground that "closed loop" is established FHIR terminology for referral-status round-tripping and does not, on its own, claim jurisdiction over non-healthcare intervention execution. However, this position is weakened by the fact that the reporter is citing the Gravity Project's own co-design model, and two sibling tickets in the same block vote (FHIR-53798, FHIR-53799) have already been accepted for exactly this class of "recognise sectors of care" language revision — declining outright here would leave Section 3.2 inconsistent with its neighbouring sections.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B matches the resolution the WG has already recorded on the ticket ("Not Persuasive with Modification — we are going to revise Section 3.2 to better reflect scope and align with Social Care Co-Design") and is the only option that is coherent with the parallel dispositions on FHIR-53798, FHIR-53799, and FHIR-54603. Disposition A is too prescriptive — adopting the reporter's sentence verbatim would lock in phrasing ("establishes a treatment relationship") that has not been reviewed against the co-design materials and could conflict with the companion tickets' accepted language. Disposition C is untenable given that the WG has already accepted the general "sectors of care" framing in neighbouring tickets; declining FHIR-53801 while accepting FHIR-53798/53799 would produce an internally inconsistent Section 3. Reviewers should focus discussion on *which* editor owns the Section 3.2 rewrite and how it will be coordinated with the other accepted scope/language tickets in Block Vote 2.
