## Summary

Emma Jones agrees with the SDOH Clinical Care IG's premise that race and
ethnicity (R/E) data are more consistent with an `Observation` than with
purely demographic data, and therefore that profiles on `Observation` are an
appropriate carrier. However, she objects to *limiting* R/E representation to
`Observation` only. She argues that R/E can be subjective and that R/E as
demographic data has different contexts and uses than R/E captured as a
clinical observation (where source and method are observation-level metadata
about the R/E datum). She gives a concrete example: a provider may observe
and record source/method for a patient's race using `Observation`, while the
patient's own affirmation of race — outside any observation event — is more
naturally a demographic statement. The ticket therefore asks the SDOH
Clinical Care IG to permit *both* `Observation`-based R/E and the existing
demographic R/E extensions (e.g., the US Core race/ethnicity extensions on
`Patient`), rather than mandating the `Observation` form exclusively.

No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were
returned by the augury index for FHIR-34877, so the summary is grounded
solely in the ticket itself and its on-ticket comments.

**Description:**

> I agree with this statement "Since the characteristics of race and
> ethnicity data are more consistent with an observation than demographic
> data, we have elected to model the specification as profiles on the base
> Observation resource."
>
> However, I don't think R/E information should be limited to the
> Observation resource only. Here are my points:
>
> 1. R/E data can be subjective (unless the Observation Resource is used to
>    capture a person's genetic data that confirms their R/E information).
>
> 2. R/E as demographic data can have different contexts and different uses
>    than R/E observation data. For observation data, the action of
>    capturing the source and method is metadata about the R/E data. This
>    is different than the actual R/E data as demographic data. For
>    example, a provider may observe and capture the source and method for
>    a patient's race and the patient may affirm their race outside of an
>    observation — a demographic statement. In this situation, the
>    Observation Resource can be used for the information the provider
>    captured and demographic can be used for the patient affirmation.

## Proposed Dispositions

Note: no GitHub cross-references or repo briefings exist for this ticket,
so the dispositions below cannot name specific authoring file paths. They
do, however, identify the affected specification (US SDOH Clinical Care
IG) and the conceptual artifacts that would be touched (the personal
characteristic Observation profiles and the conformance rules that govern
exclusivity vs. coexistence with the US Core demographic extensions).

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the US SDOH Clinical Care IG so that race and ethnicity (and, by
extension, the other "personal characteristics" the IG profiles — sexual
orientation, gender identity, personal pronouns, preferred name) MAY be
exchanged either as instances of the IG's `Observation`-based personal
characteristic profiles **or** via the existing US Core race/ethnicity
extensions on `Patient` (and equivalent extensions where they exist for the
other characteristics). Concretely:

1. Soften the IG's narrative that currently frames the `Observation`-based
   profiles as the only carrier; explicitly state that demographic-style
   extensions on `Patient` remain valid and are the preferred carrier when
   the data is a self-affirmed demographic statement rather than a
   provider-captured observation event with source and method.
2. Add guidance describing when each form is appropriate:
   - `Observation` profiles when source and method are themselves data of
     interest (e.g., provider-captured, with provenance about *how* the R/E
     was determined, including support for multiple observations over time).
   - `Patient` extensions when the data is a stable, self-affirmed
     demographic statement without observation-level metadata.
3. Add interoperability conformance text describing how systems that hold
   both forms should expose them (e.g., that the `Patient` extensions
   reflect the currently-asserted demographic value, while the `Observation`
   instances form the auditable history of how that value was determined).

#### Justification

This matches the requester's argument and aligns with how the broader US
realm already exchanges R/E today — every conformant US Core implementation
populates `Patient.extension:race` and `Patient.extension:ethnicity`. A
soft-mandate that R/E *must* be exchanged as observations would put the IG
in direct conflict with US Core and create needless implementer burden.
Permitting both is also consistent with the IG's own intent: the
`Observation` form adds expressive power (source, method, history) without
needing to invalidate the demographic form for the simple "what does the
patient currently affirm?" question. This disposition also tracks
lynn_laakso's 2022-11-21 note that "any approach should support multiple
observations of the relevant data element."

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep `Observation`-based personal characteristic profiles as the IG's
single normative carrier for R/E (and the other personal characteristics),
**but** add a derived/synchronization rule and a "current value" pattern
rather than declaring the US Core demographic extensions to be valid
alternative carriers within this IG:

1. The `Observation` profiles remain the source of truth for R/E captured
   under SDOH Clinical Care.
2. Add an informative section describing the expected synchronization
   between the SDOH `Observation` profile(s) and the US Core
   `Patient.extension:race`/`extension:ethnicity` values: the
   `Patient`-level extension reflects the *currently-asserted* value;
   `Observation` history captures *how* it was determined, including
   self-affirmation as a valid `Observation.method`/source.
3. Define a self-affirmation pattern within the `Observation` profile (a
   value-set or example for `Observation.method` / `Observation.performer =
   Patient`) so the requester's "patient affirmation outside of an
   observation event" use case is still expressible *as* an Observation,
   without forcing implementers to abandon `Patient` extensions for
   day-to-day demographic exchange.

#### Justification

This addresses the underlying need — making subjective and self-affirmed
R/E first-class — while preserving the IG's design coherence ("one
approach" per lynn_laakso's 2022-11-21 reversion comment). It also avoids
the divergence risk of having two normatively-equal carriers in the same
IG, which can lead to ambiguity about which to trust when both are
populated. Trade-off vs. Disposition A: implementers who already exchange
R/E only via US Core extensions get less explicit IG-level permission to
keep doing so, but they are not blocked, because the SDOH IG's scope is
clinical-care SDOH workflows rather than baseline demographics.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (Persistent / Considered for Future Use)

#### Proposal

Decline the change for STU2 of the US SDOH Clinical Care IG and route the
underlying question to the joint US Core / Cross-Group Projects (Gender
Harmony, Patient Administration) decision on whether personal
characteristics should be modeled as `Observation`s, as `Patient`
extensions, or both — which is exactly the open question lynn_laakso flags
in the 2022-11-21 comment ("Ultimately US Core will decide if R/E should
be observations or extensions"). Mark the ticket as *Persistent* / pending
that cross-IG decision, with no spec change in this IG cycle.

#### Justification

The 2022-02-28 disposition already chose *Considered for Future Use*, and
the 2022-11-21 reversion was driven by the realization that the answer
depends on an upstream decision in US Core / R5. Acting unilaterally in
the SDOH Clinical Care IG risks creating a third pattern that US Core then
has to retrofit. Declining here is also defensible because the requester's
core use case — patient self-affirmation — is already representable using
the existing US Core `Patient` race/ethnicity extensions, so no implementer
is currently *blocked*; they are inconvenienced by IG narrative, not by a
missing capability. Note: this is a "decline for now" rationale, not a
"decline forever" — the ticket should be re-considered as part of the next
SDOH Clinical Care IG ballot after US Core/Cross-Group has spoken.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket has been on the books since January 2022, was approved on the
PC Gravity SDOH IG call on 2022-02-16, was then bounced to *Considered for
Future Use*, and was explicitly re-opened post-STU2 publication. The
requester's argument is straightforward and operationally correct: real
implementations already exchange R/E through `Patient` extensions, and the
IG's `Observation`-based profiles add value precisely *because* they carry
provenance the extensions cannot. Telling implementers they may not
exchange R/E demographically once they adopt the SDOH Clinical Care IG is
both impractical (because US Core requires the extensions) and unnecessary
(because the two carriers answer different questions). Disposition B is
reasonable design-purist work but kicks the user-facing problem down the
road; Disposition C is procedurally defensible but leaves the IG's stance
ambiguous in the meantime. Disposition A — explicitly permit both, with
clear guidance on when each is appropriate — gives implementers a clean
answer now and is consistent with both the original 2022-02 PC Gravity
approval and the 2022-11 "support multiple observations of the relevant
data element" guidance.
