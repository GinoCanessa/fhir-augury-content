## Summary

The ticket raises two related concerns about the `ClinicalImpression`
resource. First, it argues that `ClinicalImpression.finding.basis` —
defined as a single `string` (`0..1`) describing "which investigations
support finding or diagnosis" — is structurally inadequate when the
basis for a finding (e.g., a "worsening" or "improving" pattern) needs
to be backed by concrete clinical evidence such as one or more
`Observation`, lab/pathology, or imaging results. Second, it asks the
work group to clarify whether `ClinicalImpression.supportingInfo`
(`0..*`, `Reference(Any)`) is intended to carry that supporting
evidence, and to provide definitional/scope guidance distinguishing the
two elements.

This concern is closely tied to two newer Patient Care tickets that the
work group has already started to disposition. [FHIR-50409](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50409)
("Clean up of descriptions and narrative") asks the same fundamental
question — *are `basis` and `supportingInfo` both needed, and if so how
do they differ?* — and was resolved "Persuasive with Modification" in
Jan 2026 with a draft resolution to (a) clarify
`supportingInfo`'s definition as "Information supporting the clinical
assessment (e.g. investigation results)" and (b) **change
`finding.basis` from `string` to `Reference(Any)`**, suggesting
`Observation | Condition | AllergyIntolerance | MedicationRequest |
MedicationStatement | DiagnosticReport | DocumentReference |
AdverseEvent | FamilyMemberHistory | RiskAssessment | ImagingStudy |
Immunization | QuestionnaireResponse` as the example targets. That is
substantively the structural change FHIR-37796 has been asking for
since 2022. [FHIR-50411](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50411) ("Copy
the structure of supportingInfo from DiagnosticReport") was the
companion request to add a `supportingInfo.type` slot and was resolved
"Not Persuasive" at the Sept 2025 WGM, on the rationale that
`supportingInfo` is intentionally broad and no concrete use case for a
type code was offered. [FHIR-37797](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-37797),
filed by the same reporter on the same day as FHIR-37796, is the
sibling request to capture clinical-intervention context for a
`ClinicalImpression.finding`, and remains Triaged/Unresolved.

## Details

**Description:**

> ClinicalImpression.finding.basis  cardinality = 0..1   datatype = string
> Definition: Which investigations support finding or diagnosis.
> *Issue*:
> To support assertion of clinical impression finding (e.g. that a
> patient condition change pattern = worsening | improving), the
> "basis" may need to include more solid clinical evidences, e.g. one
> or multiple instances of Observation details, and | or diagnostic
> (e.g. lab/path or imaging) findings. The structural design:
> cardinality = 0..1 and datatype = string is inadequate to support
> such requirements.
>
> ClinicalImpression.supportingInfo cardinality = 0..*  datatype = Reference(Any)
> Definition: Information supporting the clinical impression, which can
> contain investigation results.
> *Issue*:
> It is unclear whether the ClinicalImpression.supportingInfo is
> intended to include supporting the ClinicalImpression.finding.basis
> (when cardinality = 0..1 and datatype = string are inadequate).
> Definitional and scope of use clarifications | guidance are required.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the structural change requested in FHIR-37796 by retyping
`ClinicalImpression.finding.basis` from `string` (`0..1`) to
`Reference(Any)` (cardinality may stay `0..1` or be widened to `0..*`
as the work group prefers; the FHIR-50409 draft uses `Reference(Any)`
without explicitly addressing cardinality). Update the element
definition to read along the lines of: *"What evidence supports the
finding or diagnosis (e.g. Observation | Condition | AllergyIntolerance
| MedicationRequest | MedicationStatement | DiagnosticReport |
DocumentReference | AdverseEvent | FamilyMemberHistory | RiskAssessment
| ImagingStudy | Immunization | QuestionnaireResponse)."* In parallel,
clarify `ClinicalImpression.supportingInfo`'s definition to:
*"Information supporting the clinical assessment (e.g. investigation
results not already cited as `finding.basis`)."* Mark FHIR-37796 as
**duplicate of FHIR-50409** since FHIR-50409's "Persuasive with
Modification" resolution already implements this change.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter asked for and it is consistent with
where Patient Care has already landed in FHIR-50409 (resolved
2026-01-08). Moving `basis` from `string` to `Reference` removes the
free-text-only constraint the reporter flagged as inadequate for
evidence such as Observations, lab/pathology and imaging findings, and
aligns the element with the typed-reference patterns used throughout
the rest of the resource (`finding.item` is already a
`CodeableReference`). Clarifying `supportingInfo` in the same pass
addresses the second half of the ticket (the definitional ambiguity
between `basis` and `supportingInfo`) and matches the shared
understanding documented in the FHIR-50409 call notes that
`supportingInfo` is the broader "open bucket" while `basis` is the
specific evidence underpinning a finding. Reference targets remain
`Any` per the FHIR-50409 resolution, so no new dependencies are
introduced.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Change `ClinicalImpression.finding.basis` to a **`CodeableReference`**
(`0..*`) targeted to a clinically meaningful list (Observation,
Condition, DiagnosticReport, ImagingStudy, AllergyIntolerance,
MedicationStatement/Request, FamilyMemberHistory, RiskAssessment,
QuestionnaireResponse, DocumentReference, AdverseEvent, Immunization),
mirroring the pattern already used for `finding.item`. Keep `Reference.display`
or the `CodeableConcept` half of the `CodeableReference` available for
the simple narrative-string case the current `string` datatype
supports, so that legacy implementers do not lose the ability to
provide a short prose basis. Cardinality `0..*` lets a single finding
cite multiple pieces of evidence, which is the concrete scenario the
reporter raised (one finding backed by several Observations and/or
diagnostic reports). Re-state `supportingInfo`'s definition as in
Disposition A.

#### Justification

A `CodeableReference` keeps backward compatibility with the
"free text basis" case (via `concept.text` or
`reference.display`) while adding the typed-reference capability the
reporter needs, and it matches the existing pattern at
`ClinicalImpression.finding.item`, giving the resource an internally
consistent shape (item is what was found, basis is what supports it,
both are `CodeableReference`). Widening to `0..*` is a faithful
response to the reporter's "one or multiple instances" wording, which
neither the current `string 0..1` nor the FHIR-50409 draft (which does
not call out cardinality) clearly enables. The trade-off vs.
Disposition A is slightly more model surface, an additional
breaking-change beat (string → CodeableReference rather than string →
Reference), and the need to align with FHIR-50409's already-issued
resolution, which Patient Care would have to revisit.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-37796 as **Already Addressed / Duplicate** of
[FHIR-50409](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50409). No further
spec change is initiated by this ticket. The structural change to
`finding.basis` and the definitional clarification of
`supportingInfo` will land via FHIR-50409's "Persuasive with
Modification" resolution; FHIR-50411 already disposes of the related
ask for an additional `supportingInfo.type` ("Not Persuasive"). Notify
the reporter that the merge of `investigation` into `supportingInfo`
([FHIR-18860](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-18860)) plus FHIR-50409
collectively cover both halves of the original concern.

#### Justification

FHIR-37796 has been Triaged since 2022 and is now functionally
overtaken by events: the R6 ballot has already removed `investigation`
and merged it into `supportingInfo`, and Patient Care has, in
FHIR-50409, agreed to retype `finding.basis` to `Reference(Any)` with
an explicit list of clinically relevant targets and to tighten
`supportingInfo`'s definition. Carrying FHIR-37796 separately risks a
second, divergent change set against the same two elements.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

FHIR-37796 has been sitting open for over three years and the reporter's
original concern — that `finding.basis` is structurally too weak and
that the `basis` vs. `supportingInfo` distinction is unclear — has
already been validated by Patient Care via FHIR-50409's "Persuasive
with Modification" resolution. Disposition A formally accepts the
request, ties it to the change Patient Care has already agreed to, and
gives the reporter clean closure. Disposition C reaches the same
spec outcome but does so by closing the older ticket as a duplicate
without explicitly affirming the original ask, which is less
satisfying for a long-standing community submission. Disposition B
(CodeableReference, `0..*`) is technically attractive and is more
faithful to the reporter's "one or multiple instances" wording, but
adopting it now would require re-opening FHIR-50409 and re-litigating
a resolution Patient Care has already reached; if the work group
believes cardinality and CodeableReference are worth revisiting, that
case is better made as a deliberate amendment to FHIR-50409 rather
than as a fork through FHIR-37796.
