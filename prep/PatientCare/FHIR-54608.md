## Summary

The reporter requests that the US SDOH Clinical Care IG consistently
distinguish between **"Screening"** and **"Assessment"** as defined in
Section 1.1, where Screening identifies a patient's general SDOH risk at
an initial encounter (e.g. "Food Insecurity") and Assessment identifies
specific, intervention-actionable needs (e.g. inability to purchase food
vs. need for medically tailored meals). Section 7 currently subsumes all
SDOH-related data collection under "Assessment" alone, which obscures the
distinction between an initial Screening that triggers a Referral for
Further Assessment (Section 12) and the subsequent Assessment encounter
itself. The proposed remediation is to revise references in Section 7
(and any other sections that conflate the two) so that they read
"Screening and Assessment" — or use the appropriate specific term —
matching the distinction drawn in Section 1.1.

The Patient Care WG has triaged this ticket as **Persuasive with
Modification**. The agreed scope of change targets Section 1.1 (the
bulleted definitions), the Glossary, and the in-progress updated
Conceptual Model; downstream edits to Sections 7 and 12 are deferred
until the foundational definitions and conceptual model are settled.
The ticket carries the `block-vote-2` label and was bundled into the
Gravity SDOHCC STU3 Block Vote 2 announced on Zulip
([Patient Care WG > Gravity SDOHCC STU3 - Block Vote 2](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/Patient%20Care%20WG/topic/Gravity%20SDOHCC%20STU3%20-%20Block%20Vote%202/near/584477949)).
The cross-references search returned no other Jira tickets and no GitHub
items, but FHIR-54596 ("Inconsistent use of terms 'Screening' and
'Assessment'") is bundled in the same block vote and is clearly the
sibling ticket addressing the same terminology issue from a different
angle.

## Details

**Description:**

> Revise all references to "Assessment" to "Screening and Assessment"
>
> Section 1.1 makes an essential distinction between "Screening" which
> identifies a patient's general SDOH risk (e.g. "Food Insecurity" at
> an initial encounter, and "Assessment" which identifies specific
> needs to be addressed through a planned intervention (e.g. the

> care process and the different data collection instruments used at
> each stage of the process.

> Assessment will be done in the following areas:
> - Section 1.1 (bulleted section)

>
> Changes to Section 7 and 12 may arise after this work is completed.

## Proposed Dispositions

The work group has already resolved this ticket as **Persuasive with
Modification**; the dispositions below are framed as the choices the
block vote is effectively ratifying or could revisit.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the literal request: do a sweeping textual revision of the US
SDOH Clinical Care IG so that every occurrence of "Assessment" that
refers to the broader SDOH data-collection workflow is rewritten as
"Screening and Assessment." Concretely:

- Update Section 7 headings and prose so that "Assessment" → "Screening
  and Assessment" wherever the text covers both initial screening and
  subsequent assessment.
- Cross-check Section 12 ("Referral for Further Assessment") and the
  surrounding workflow narrative for consistency.
- Leave Section 1.1's existing definitions untouched (the reporter
  treats them as the source of truth) and leave instances where
  "Assessment" specifically means the post-referral encounter as-is.

#### Justification

This is the lowest-friction interpretation of the reporter's request
and immediately removes the conflation in Section 7 that prompted the
ticket. It also keeps each edit auditable as a one-for-one terminology
substitution. It is consistent with how Patient Care has historically
handled "terminology hygiene" tickets in narrative IG sections — bulk
find-and-replace with a single editorial pass.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach (the WG's actual modification)

#### Proposal

Treat the terminology issue as a **conceptual** problem first and a
textual one second. Specifically:

1. Update **Section 1.1** (the bulleted definitions) so that the
   distinctions between "Screening", "Assessment", and "Screening and
   Assessment" (as a combined process step) are stated unambiguously
   and in alignment with the updated conceptual model currently in
   flight.
2. Mirror those definitions in the **Glossary** so there is exactly one
   normative source of terminology for the IG.
3. Incorporate the same vocabulary into the **Updated Conceptual
   Model** work so diagrams and narrative agree.
4. **Defer** the Section 7 / Section 12 narrative rewrite until items
   1–3 land. Once the conceptual model stabilises, do a follow-up pass
   that uses "Screening", "Assessment", or "Screening and Assessment"
   precisely as warranted in each paragraph — not via blanket
   substitution.
5. Coordinate this work with the sibling ticket
   [FHIR-54596](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54596) so both
   resolve against the same terminology baseline.

#### Justification

This is the disposition the WG and the comment thread already endorsed
("Section 1.1 is not in alignment with our further approach and will
need to be updated. Observations of Section 7 will be included in the
global review around the glossary and our conceptual model."). It
avoids the failure mode of Disposition A, where a mechanical
"Assessment" → "Screening and Assessment" rewrite would, in places
where "Assessment" really does mean the post-referral encounter
(notably Section 12), introduce *new* ambiguity by re-conflating the
two stages. Anchoring on Section 1.1 + Glossary + Conceptual Model
also gives FHIR-54596 and FHIR-54608 a single shared resolution
substrate, which is cheaper to maintain than two parallel narrative
edits. Trade-off: implementers reading STU3 in its current form
continue to see the ambiguous Section 7 wording until the follow-up
narrative pass lands.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change on the grounds that Section 1.1 already defines the
two terms, and readers are expected to interpret Section 7 in light of
those definitions. Mark as Not Persuasive — "already addressed by
existing normative definitions in Section 1.1" — and close.

#### Justification

Defensible only on a strict reading that the IG's normative
definitions live in Section 1.1 and that downstream prose is
illustrative. Realistically this is weak: the reporter is a domain
expert who read the IG end-to-end and was confused, the WG's own
comment confirms Section 1.1 itself "is not in alignment with our
further approach," and a sibling ticket (FHIR-54596) raises the same
inconsistency. Declining would leave a known terminology defect in
place and would be inconsistent with the WG's broader conceptual
model refresh.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **B (Alternative Approach — Persuasive
with Modification).**

Disposition B is what the WG has effectively already chosen, and it is
the right choice on the merits. The reporter's underlying complaint —
that Section 7 erases the Screening / Assessment distinction — is real,
but a blanket textual substitution as in Disposition A would create a
mirror-image problem in Section 12, where "Assessment" is used in its
narrow, post-referral sense. By fixing the **definitions** (Section 1.1
+ Glossary) and the **conceptual model** first, the WG gets a stable
vocabulary that the eventual Section 7 and Section 12 narrative pass
can apply precisely. It also lets FHIR-54596 and FHIR-54608 be
resolved against the same source of truth, avoiding two divergent
editorial passes. The main cost — that the Section 7 wording remains
ambiguous in the interim — is acceptable given that the conceptual
model work is already underway and that this ticket is being resolved
as part of an STU3 block vote rather than a final normative
publication. Disposition C should be rejected; the WG itself
acknowledges Section 1.1 needs updating, so "no change required" is
not defensible.
