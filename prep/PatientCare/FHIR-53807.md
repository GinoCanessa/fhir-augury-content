## Summary

The submitter (LisaNicolaou) raises three concerns about Section 12.4
(Workflows) of the US SDOH Clinical Care IG. First, the third paragraph
of the section drifts into contracted-services / care-coordination
guidance — specifically a sentence describing how a CBO may need to seek
authorization from the original referring entity for reimbursement, and
how the referring provider may be made aware of the broader social care
plan. The submitter believes that this strays into specific workflow /
payment guidance that is out of scope for this IG. Second, more broadly,
the paragraph appears to wander into "specific workflow guidance" the IG
should not be prescribing. Third, the accompanying diagram repeatedly
ties the *Intermediary* role to a *Coordination Platform*, when
Coordination Platform is only one possible realization of that role.

The third concern (Intermediary vs. Coordination Platform terminology)
is being handled separately as
[FHIR-53806](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53806) — "Consistent use
of term Intermediary" — which has already been resolved Persuasive and
will update the RFFA page to acknowledge that the intermediary role can
be accomplished by multiple actors. This ticket therefore effectively
narrows to concerns (1) and (2): rewording the payment / authorization
sentence so that it neither over-specifies a workflow nor implies the
IG endorses any particular reimbursement pathway. The Gravity team's
posted resolution rewrites the third paragraph in (renumbered) Section
13.4 to: *"Executing this plan may require authorization from the
responsible party to ensure reimbursement for subsequent services."*

## Details

**Description:**

1. **Section 12.4 Workflows**:
   1. In the third paragraph the guidance on workflows seems to trail
      off into contracted services / care coordination guidance —
      *"Before executing this plan, the CBO may need to seek
      authorization from the original referring entity or another
      responsible party to ensure reimbursement for subsequent services.
      In this scenario, the referring provider may be made aware of not
      only the assessment results but also the broader social care
      plan."*
   2. Unclear that this should be involved in this guide as it seems
      well out of scope and treading into more specific workflow
      guidance?
   3. The diagram continues to always align a Coordination Platform
      with an intermediary when it is only one option. We should
      consistently and simply state that this is an Intermediary role
      without aligning to a specific system or pathway.

**Resolution (Persuasive with Modification, 2026-04-08):**

> For 1 and 2, we will update the text in the third paragraph in
> Section 13.4 to the following:
>
> **Executing this plan may require** authorization from the
> responsible party to ensure reimbursement for subsequent services.
>
> For 3, we agree with this notion, we will address this change as part
> of our work in [FHIR-53806](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53806).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Strip the contested narrative from Section 12.4 (renumbered 13.4 in
the in-flight draft) of the US SDOH Clinical Care IG entirely. Remove
the third-paragraph sentences:

> "Before executing this plan, the CBO may need to seek authorization
> from the original referring entity or another responsible party to
> ensure reimbursement for subsequent services. In this scenario, the
> referring provider may be made aware of not only the assessment
> results but also the broader social care plan."

Replace with a one-line scope disclaimer matching JIRAUSER30894's
2026-02-10 proposal: *"Electronic pathways to manage authorization and
reimbursement remain outside the scope of this IG."* Also remove or
re-label the "Coordination Platform" callouts in the RFFA workflow
diagram so the diagram refers only to the *Intermediary* role
(deferring the actual diagram fix to FHIR-53806).

#### Justification

This is the cleanest interpretation of the submitter's complaint: the
sentence in question prescribes a specific authorization workflow and
a specific data-sharing behavior (sharing the broader social care plan
with the referring provider) that the IG never normatively defines.
Removing it eliminates the scope creep entirely, avoids creating
implementer expectations that the IG does not back with conformance
artifacts, and is consistent with the IG's overall posture of
describing actors and exchanges without prescribing reimbursement
mechanics.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach (Persuasive with Modification — current resolution)

#### Proposal

Keep the paragraph but rewrite it to a generalized, non-prescriptive
single sentence, exactly as the Gravity team has already drafted in
the resolution:

> **Executing this plan may require** authorization from the
> responsible party to ensure reimbursement for subsequent services.

Concretely:

- In the US SDOH Clinical Care IG narrative source for Section 13.4
  (RFFA Workflows), replace the third paragraph with the sentence
  above.
- Drop the second sentence ("In this scenario, the referring provider
  may be made aware…") entirely, since it specifies a data-sharing
  behavior the IG does not constrain.
- Defer the diagram / "Coordination Platform vs. Intermediary" fix to
  FHIR-53806, as the resolution already states.

#### Justification

This addresses both the scope-creep complaint *and* the Gravity team's
reasonable point (comment 2026-02-13) that some implementers do use
the IG for authorization/payment-adjacent use cases. A single
generalized sentence acknowledges that authorization may be a real
gating step without prescribing *who* authorizes, *when* in the
workflow it happens, or *what* information is shared with the
referring provider. It is also consistent with JIRAUSER16901's 4/1/26
direction to "generalize to not just before provision of service, and
just by referral target." Splitting the diagram fix to FHIR-53806
keeps each ticket focused on a single artifact change.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change. Retain the existing third-paragraph language in
Section 12.4 as-is, on the grounds that it is informative narrative
(non-normative) and reflects real-world referral-to-CBO behavior that
implementers find useful when wiring the IG into existing
authorization/reimbursement flows.

#### Justification

A defensible-but-weak argument: the paragraph is purely narrative, the
IG does not bind any conformance to it, and the Gravity team's
2026-02-13 comment confirms there are implementers using the IG to
support authorization and reporting use cases who may benefit from the
contextual guidance. Declining preserves continuity for those
implementers and avoids editorial churn during ballot.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The ticket is already labelled `Ready-For-Vote` / `block-vote-3` with
resolution **Persuasive with Modification**, which corresponds
directly to Disposition B; the Patient Care / Gravity team has both
the editorial wording and the split with FHIR-53806 already negotiated
on-ticket. Disposition B captures the submitter's substantive concern
(the IG should not be prescribing a specific authorization /
information-sharing workflow) while preserving a one-sentence
acknowledgement that authorization is a real concern — which the
Gravity team has reasonably argued reflects implementer reality.
Disposition A (full removal) over-corrects given that a working
compromise has already been drafted and accepted by the proposer's
working group; Disposition C ignores a clearly-articulated scope-creep
concern that the submitter, the Gravity team, and the WG have all
agreed merits change. The workgroup should ratify the existing
Persuasive-with-Modification text and confirm that the diagram fix
travels with FHIR-53806.
