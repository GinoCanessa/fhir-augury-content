## Summary

The ticket asks the Gravity SDOH Clinical Care IG editors to expand the
introductory language of **Section 12.0 Referral for Further Assessment
(RFFA)** so the page is framed as more than a single point-to-point data
flow. The reporter notes that prior workgroup discussions established
that the RFFA pathway should be presented as an extensible mechanism
that can support varying downstream services (notably care coordination
and contracted services), aligned with the Gravity Co-Design Care and
Data Model. The ticket proposes specific replacement text for the
overview that explicitly calls out alignment with the co-design model
and frames the initial referral as creating a treatment-relationship
data link across cross-sector care environments.

The ticket has been triaged with a resolution of **Persuasive**, and
the resolver-supplied text differs from the proposed text only by
softening one verb ("creates" → "can create"). It is bundled into the
Gravity SDOHCC STU3 Block Vote 2 for Patient Care. There are no
linked Jira tickets and no GitHub cross-references; the only
cross-reference is the Zulip block-vote announcement thread.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the **overview paragraph of the US SDOH Clinical Care IG page
"Section 12.0 Referral for Further Assessment (RFFA)"** to read
exactly as proposed in the ticket:

> "This page describes the use case, actors, systems, and information
> flows for referring an individual for a more comprehensive assessment
> of their health-related social needs (HRSN). **This initial referral
> aligns with the Gravity Co-Design Care and Data Model (link) and
> creates a data link establishing a treatment relationship with care
> partners in cross sector care environments and is intended to be an
> extensible pathway for combining service requests for care
> coordination and contracted services in future iterations.**"

This is a **narrative-only** change in the IG's RFFA page source;
no profiles, value sets, examples, search parameters, or capability
statements are affected. The "(link)" placeholder must be resolved to
a working URL for the Gravity Co-Design Care and Data Model before
publication.

#### Justification

The wording was developed in WG sessions and the reporter is the
substantive author. Accepting verbatim minimizes editorial drift
between what the WG agreed to and what is published. The change is
purely descriptive scope-setting on the RFFA page and carries no
conformance impact, so the risk of accepting as-is is low.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach (Persuasive with Modification — adopt the resolved wording)

#### Proposal

Adopt the **resolved text already recorded on the ticket** rather than
the originally proposed text. The only substantive difference is
"creates a data link" → "**can create** a data link":

> "This page describes the use case, actors, systems, and information
> flows for referring an individual for a more comprehensive assessment
> of their health-related social needs (HRSN). **This initial referral
> aligns with the Gravity Co-Design Care and Data Model (link) and
> _can create_ a data link establishing a treatment relationship with
> care partners in cross sector care environments and is intended to
> be an extensible pathway for combining service requests for care
> coordination and contracted services in future iterations.**"

In addition: replace the "(link)" placeholder with the canonical
Gravity Co-Design Care and Data Model URL, and fold the new sentence
into the existing RFFA overview paragraph rather than appending it as
a standalone sentence, so the page reads coherently.

#### Justification

The Gravity team comment on 2026-02-13 explicitly flagged that they
were "generally aligned with the spirit of this addition, but [...]
would like to do some work to revise this statement." The resolved
text on the ticket reflects that revision — softening the assertion
that the referral *creates* a treatment-relationship data link to one
that it *can create* such a link. This is more defensible because
RFFA is a request for assessment, not a guaranteed treatment
relationship: not every referral will be accepted or acted on, and
the resulting "data link" is conditional on downstream behaviour by
the receiving party. Honouring the resolved wording also keeps the
ticket's adopted text consistent with what other STU3 ballot items
in Block Vote 2 (e.g., FHIR-53798, FHIR-53799, FHIR-53802,
FHIR-54603) are doing — broadening framing toward cross-sector,
co-designed flows.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the proposed wording change and leave the existing RFFA
overview unchanged for STU3, on the grounds that:

1. The new text introduces a forward-looking promise ("intended to be
   an extensible pathway for combining service requests for care
   coordination and contracted services in future iterations") that
   is not yet backed by published profiles, examples, or operations
   in this IG version, and
2. The reference to the Gravity Co-Design Care and Data Model is a
   currently-unresolved external link.

If the WG wants the broader framing in writing, defer it to a later
publication once the supporting artifacts and the Co-Design model URL
are stable.

#### Justification

A defensible Decline rests on avoiding aspirational normative-adjacent
text in an STU. IG narrative shapes implementer expectations even
when it is not formally conformant; promising an "extensible pathway
for combining service requests for care coordination and contracted
services in future iterations" without those artifacts in this
publication risks setting expectations the IG does not yet meet.
However, this rationale is weak in practice: the change is to
overview narrative, not conformance text, and the WG has already
discussed and agreed to broaden the framing.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B (Persuasive with Modification — adopt
the resolved wording).

The ticket is already resolved as **Persuasive**, the Gravity team
explicitly asked for revision before adoption, and the resolved text
on the ticket captures that revision (the "creates" → "can create"
softening). That softening is substantively better: RFFA is a
*request* for further assessment, so claiming it "creates" a
treatment-relationship data link overstates the mechanism, whereas
"can create" correctly reflects that the link is contingent on the
receiver's action. Disposition B also gives the WG an opportunity to
fix two practical issues that Disposition A leaves outstanding: the
"(link)" placeholder for the Co-Design Care and Data Model and the
need to integrate the new sentence into the existing overview
paragraph rather than tacking it on. Disposition C overweights
hypothetical implementer confusion against narrative scope-setting
that the WG has already endorsed.
