## Summary

The ticket asks Patient Care to clarify and broaden how `ConditionDefinition`
relates to other clinical concepts — primarily medications, but also
procedures, vaccines, devices, and similar definitional resources. Today
`ConditionDefinition` carries a `medication` element, but the resource gives
no guidance on whether that medication represents a *cause of* the condition
or a *treatment for* it, and the element is constrained to `Medication`
rather than the broader `MedicationKnowledge` class of definitional content.
The reporter (Corey Spears) recommends three coordinated changes: (1) convert
`ConditionDefinition.medication` to a `CodeableReference` that can point to
either `Medication` or `MedicationKnowledge`; (2) make the cause-vs-treatment
relationship explicit, either by adding a relationship indicator or by
splitting the element into two clearly-labelled relationships; and (3)
generalise the pattern so that other definitional artifacts (procedures /
`ActivityDefinition`, vaccine codes, `DeviceDefinition`, etc.) can be
associated with a `ConditionDefinition` in the same way.

The Augury cross-reference index returned no explicit Jira, Zulip, or GitHub
cross-references for this key. The most relevant prior community discussion
is the long-running 2020 Zulip thread `#implementers > ConditionDefinition`,
where Jose Costa Teixeira, Lloyd McKenzie, Rik Smithies, and Grahame Grieve
debated the scope of the resource, its overlap with `ClinicalUseIssue`, and
whether "Definition" was even the right suffix — context that bears
directly on how broadly Patient Care should now extend it.

## Details

**Description:**

> ConditionDefinition has a medication element (Consider making this a
> codeable reference which could reference Medication or perhaps
> MedicationKnowledge). It is unclear what relationship this medication
> has to the Condition. Is it a potential the cause of the treatment for
> the condition? Consider enabling both by adding some sort of
> relationship indicator (or having 2 different elements that clearly
> note the relationship). If Medications are identified that could either
> be a cause or the treatment for the condition, why isn't a procedure
> (or activityDefinition), vaccine code, device definition, or any other
> types of definitions (all for which a CodeableReference should be
> considered)?

## Related Zulip Discussions

### [#implementers > ConditionDefinition](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/implementers/topic/ConditionDefinition)

A 2020 thread on the original purpose and scope of `ConditionDefinition`.
Jose Costa Teixeira opened by asking how `ConditionDefinition` differs
from / overlaps with `ClinicalUseIssue` (now `ClinicalUseDefinition`),
particularly when expressing things like "this medication has indication
X". Lloyd McKenzie characterised `ClinicalUseIssue` as the definition of
a `DetectedIssue` (contraindication-style) and called `ConditionDefinition`
"a grab-bag of stuff related to a condition". Rik Smithies pushed back
that `ConditionDefinition` doesn't actually *define* a condition — it's
more supporting/observational information about one — and questioned
whether "Definition" or even "Condition" is the right name. Lloyd and
Grahame Grieve agreed `ConditionKnowledge` would arguably be a better
label, while Grahame defended `ConditionDefinition` as the right name
because it links to the `Condition` resource. There is rough consensus
that the resource is patient-context-free (parallel to
`ObservationDefinition`) and that the model conflates several concerns.
There is **no** consensus on naming, on scope boundaries vs.
`ClinicalUseDefinition`, or on what kinds of links the resource should
carry — exactly the open questions FHIR-50482 is now raising.

Participants: Jose Costa Teixeira, Grahame Grieve, Lloyd McKenzie, Rik
Smithies (a stray off-topic HAPI question from "Raed MO" appears in the
same thread and is not relevant).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply all three requested changes to `ConditionDefinition` in FHIR Core:

1. Change the type of `ConditionDefinition.medication` from a plain
   `Reference(Medication)` to `CodeableReference(Medication |
   MedicationKnowledge)`.
2. Replace the single `medication` element with a repeating backbone
   element — e.g. `ConditionDefinition.relatedMedication` with
   children `relationship` (CodeableConcept, bound to a new value set
   containing at minimum `cause-of`, `treatment-for`, `risk-factor`,
   `contraindicated-for`) and `item` (`CodeableReference(Medication |
   MedicationKnowledge)`). Mark the original `medication` element as
   deprecated.
3. Add sibling backbone elements for the analogous definitional
   artifacts the reporter calls out: `relatedProcedure`
   (`CodeableReference(ActivityDefinition | PlanDefinition)`),
   `relatedImmunization` (`CodeableReference(Immunization |
   ImmunizationRecommendation | ActivityDefinition)`), and
   `relatedDevice` (`CodeableReference(Device | DeviceDefinition)`),
   each with the same `relationship` indicator.

#### Justification

`CodeableReference` is the standard FHIR pattern for "code or pointer
to a definitional resource", and other R5/R6 resources (e.g.
`MedicationRequest.reason`, `Condition.evidence`) already follow it.
Adding an explicit `relationship` element resolves Corey's core
complaint that today's `medication` element is semantically
ambiguous — a downstream consumer cannot tell a causal agent from a
therapeutic one. Generalising to procedures, vaccines, and devices
keeps `ConditionDefinition` internally consistent rather than
arbitrarily privileging medications, and aligns it with the
`ClinicalUseDefinition` work that the 2020 Zulip thread already
identified as overlapping. Because `ConditionDefinition` is still
draft / low-maturity in FHIR Core, the cost of restructuring its
clinical-association elements is comparatively low.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than expanding `ConditionDefinition` with parallel
`relatedMedication` / `relatedProcedure` / `relatedDevice` elements,
introduce a single generic backbone:

```
ConditionDefinition.relatedArtifact (0..*)
  .relationship  : CodeableConcept   // cause-of | treatment-for |
                                     // risk-factor | contraindicated-for | …
  .item          : CodeableReference(Any definitional/clinical resource)
```

Keep the existing `medication` element for backwards compatibility but
mark it `Deprecated`, with guidance to use `relatedArtifact` instead.
At the same time, file a companion ticket against FHIR Infrastructure
/ BR&R asking whether `ClinicalUseDefinition` should be the canonical
home for cause/treatment/contraindication relationships, and whether
`ConditionDefinition` should *reference* `ClinicalUseDefinition`
rather than re-implement the same modelling.

#### Justification

A single typed-relationship list is more extensible than three or four
purpose-named ones — the reporter's own list ("procedure or
activityDefinition, vaccine code, device definition, or any other
types of definitions") is essentially open-ended, and Disposition A
will need another change request the next time someone wants
`relatedSubstance` or `relatedExposure`. The generic shape also keeps
`ConditionDefinition` from drifting further into territory the 2020
Zulip thread already flagged as overlapping with
`ClinicalUseDefinition`; raising the cross-WG question now avoids a
later breaking re-alignment. The cost is a slightly less
self-documenting model and a single open `CodeableReference` target
that implementers must validate against the value set rather than
against element types.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change for the upcoming ballot cycle. Close as "Not
Persuasive — Existing Mechanism" with the response that
(a) cause-vs-treatment style relationships between conditions and
medications already have a home in `ClinicalUseDefinition`
(`indication`, `contraindication`, `interaction`, etc.), and
(b) `ConditionDefinition` is intentionally a thin definitional anchor
that is not the right place to enumerate every clinical artifact that
might relate to a condition. Direct the reporter to model
medication/procedure/device relationships via `ClinicalUseDefinition`
and (where applicable) `PlanDefinition`.

#### Justification

The 2020 implementer discussion already surfaced the concern that
`ConditionDefinition` is a "grab-bag" and that
`ClinicalUseDefinition` is the resource designed for the
cause/treatment/contraindication semantics Corey wants to express.
Adding more typed relationships to `ConditionDefinition` risks
duplicating that model and entrenching the overlap rather than
resolving it. Declining is defensible because the underlying use
cases are already implementable today using existing resources, and
the ticket does not present new requirements that those resources
cannot meet.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B captures the substantive intent of the ticket — making
the cause-vs-treatment relationship explicit and not arbitrarily
privileging medications — while avoiding the change-request treadmill
that Disposition A creates by enumerating each artifact type
separately. It also forces the cross-WG conversation that the 2020
Zulip thread foreshadowed (`ConditionDefinition` vs.
`ClinicalUseDefinition` scope), which is genuinely unresolved and
will only get harder to fix later. Disposition C is too dismissive
given that the reporter has correctly identified a real semantic
ambiguity in `ConditionDefinition.medication`; even if
`ClinicalUseDefinition` is ultimately the better home, the current
element's lack of a relationship indicator is still a defect worth
fixing. Patient Care should be prepared to coordinate with BR&R on
the boundary question before the change lands in a normative ballot.
