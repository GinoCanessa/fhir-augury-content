## Summary

Retinal (FHIR)* Implementation Guide — the "Eyes on FHIR® background and
introduction" heading — currently contains text that reads like a note to

to draft suitable background-and-introduction prose. There are no
cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip discussions, or GitHub items
associated with this ticket in Augury, so this is a standalone editorial

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Accept the ticket as a "Persuasive — with mod" editorial change against
the *Ophthalmology Retinal (FHIR)* IG. The Patient Care / Ophthalmology
authoring team replaces the current Section 1.1 text under the
"Eyes on FHIR® background and introduction" heading with reader-facing
narrative covering: (1) the clinical/business background that motivated
"Eyes on FHIR®" (eye-care interoperability needs, common ophthalmology
exchange use cases such as retinal imaging, exam findings, and referral),
(2) the scope and intended audience of the IG (clinicians, EHR vendors,
device/imaging vendors, payers), and (3) a brief orientation to how the
rest of the IG is organized. Any implementer-directed material currently
in Section 1.1 is moved into a clearly-labeled "Notes for implementers"
sidebar (or removed if redundant with later sections).

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter asked for: the existing copy in
Section 1.1 reads as authoring scaffolding rather than published
narrative, and an IG's introduction is precisely where readers expect a
plain-language background and orientation. Replacing scaffolding with
real introductory prose costs nothing in terms of conformance (no
resources, profiles, or constraints change) and is consistent with how
other HL7 IGs structure their Section 1 ("Background", "Scope",
"Audience"). The change is purely editorial and carries no backward-
compatibility risk.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Restructure rather than just rewrite Section 1.1. Split it into named
subsections — `1.1 Background`, `1.2 Scope`, `1.3 Intended Audience`,
and (optionally) `1.4 How to read this guide` — and populate each with
short, targeted prose. Move any genuinely implementer-only content (TODO
notes, build instructions, authoring conventions) out of the published
narrative entirely and into a non-published authoring `README.md` in the
IG source repo. Coordinate the wording with the Ophthalmology project
leadership so the background reflects the current state of the
"Eyes on FHIR®" initiative.

#### Justification

A single "background and introduction" heading is a known smell in
early-draft IGs; splitting it produces a navigable Table of Contents and
makes the IG friendlier for first-time readers (clinicians and product
managers in particular). It also forces the authoring team to decide
explicitly what is implementer-only versus reader-facing, which is the
underlying problem the reporter surfaced. The cost is slightly more
editorial work than Disposition A, but the resulting structure aligns
with conventions readers see in mature HL7 IGs (e.g., US Core, IPS) and
reduces the chance the same complaint recurs in later ballots.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline as "Not Persuasive" on the basis that the ticket does not
include proposed wording ("Proposed Wording: NA"), and that Section 1.1
content is the authoring team's editorial responsibility rather than a
balloted normative concern. Close with a note that the Ophthalmology
Retinal IG editors will address introductory copy as part of the next
publication pass, and that a fresh ticket with concrete proposed wording
is welcome if a specific change is desired.

#### Justification

A pure "please write this section" request with no proposed text is
hard to action through the change-request process — the work group has
no proposal to vote on, only an observation. If the IG editors have
already scheduled an editorial pass on the introduction, this ticket
duplicates that work. Declining with a clear pointer to the editorial
pipeline avoids leaving a long-open ticket on an editorial item where
the disposition is essentially "we agree, we'll write it when we
write it."

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter's observation is straightforwardly correct — the current
Section 1.1 reads as authoring scaffolding rather than an introduction
the reader can use, and the cost of fixing it is negligible (no
resources, profiles, value sets, or constraints are affected). The work
group should accept the ticket as a persuasive editorial change and ask
the Ophthalmology Retinal IG editors to draft proper background and
introduction prose for Section 1.1. Disposition B (the restructure) is
attractive and may well be where the editors land, but it goes beyond
what was asked and can be tracked as follow-on editorial work rather
than blocking this ticket. Declining (Disposition C) on the technicality
that no proposed wording was supplied would leave a real defect in the
published IG and is hard to defend given the issue is plainly visible
in the current Section 1.1.
