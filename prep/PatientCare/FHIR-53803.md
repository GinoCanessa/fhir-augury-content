## Summary

The ticket, filed by LisaNicolaou against the **US SDOH Clinical Care
(FHIR)** IG, asks the Patient Care work group to revise the language and
diagrams in **Section 8 — Referral Workflow** so that the workflow does
not implicitly assume the screening healthcare provider performs goal
setting and intervention identification, and so that "Coordination
Platform" is not presented as the only intermediary actor between
referral source and target. The reporter notes that, in practice,
community-based organizations (CBOs) and other partners often perform
much of this work, and that the diagram in 8.0 (and parallel content in
8.2 and 8.3) shows a "Coordination Platform" without flagging it as
optional and without naming a generic intermediary. They suggest
reverting/keeping the actor label as **Intermediary**, which they argue
aligns better with the existing direct and direct-light referral
descriptions, and ask that any worked examples cite more than just a
coordination platform.

The committee has already engaged on the substance: a Patient Care
respondent (JIRAUSER24103) accepted that the middle actor in indirect
referral scenarios needs a better definition and committed to propose
language through this ticket, and JIRAUSER30907 seconded the request.
The ticket has been resolved **Persuasive** with a published resolution
to **generalize roles in Section 8 to Referral Source / Intermediary /
Referral Target**, list example actors for each role (Referral Source =
Healthcare Provider, CBO, etc.; Intermediary = Coordination Platform,
HIE, etc.; Referral Target = CBO, etc.), and use the **Section 8.3.5
Indirect Referral – Light** diagram as the reference visual for the
update. It is currently labelled `Ready-For-Vote` and assigned to
`block-vote-3`. No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip discussions, or
GitHub items were surfaced for this ticket.

## Details

**Description:**

1. **Section 8.0 Referral Workflow:**
   1. The "1 General Referral Workflow" description continues to imply
      that the care provider operating in the healthcare space where
      screening is performed is also where goal setting and
      interventions are identified. The reporter argues this is "wildly
      out of touch with the realities in practical application" and
      that the IG should provide pathways for community care teams to
      do this work in partnership with healthcare, where these
      activities are already part of their normal processes.
   2. The diagram identifies *Patient, Provider, Coordination Platform,
      CBO(s)* but presents the Coordination Platform as if it were a
      stand-in for CBOs, without marking it as optional and without
      naming a generic intermediary. If the intent is for the
      Coordination Platform to act as an intermediary (as an HIE
      could), the reporter suggests it would be better to keep the
      wording as **Intermediary**.
      1. Using "Intermediary" as the actor would align with the
         descriptions used to define the direct and direct-light
         referral processes.
   3. The same language change should be reconciled across **Sections
      8.2 and 8.3**, and any worked examples should list more than just
      a coordination platform.

**Resolution (already published on the ticket):** "We will be more
general about the **roles** that we are asserting in the workflows in
Section 8 (Referral Source, Intermediary, Referral Target). We will
provide examples of the **actors** that can fill those roles (e.g.,
Referral Source = Healthcare Provider, CBO, etc.; Intermediary =
Coordination Platform, HIE, etc.; Referral Target = CBO, etc.). We
will use the diagram in
[Section 8.3.5](https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/fhir-sdoh-clinicalcare/en/referral_workflow.html#indirect-referral-light)
as a reference point for this work."

**Comments:**

## Proposed Dispositions

> Note: the ticket is already resolved **Persuasive** with a published
> resolution. The dispositions below are framed as the choices the work
> group can ratify in vote, including the option to confirm the
> existing resolution. Because no GitHub cross-references were found,
> none of the proposals can name an authoring path drawn from a repo
> briefing; they reference Section 8 of the **US SDOH Clinical Care**
> IG narrative as the change locus.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the change literally as the reporter described it and as the
work group has already committed to in the published resolution.
Specifically, in the **US SDOH Clinical Care (FHIR)** IG narrative for
**Section 8 — Referral Workflow** (Sections 8.0, 8.2, and 8.3, and the
diagram currently used in 8.0):

1. Re-state the workflow in terms of three **roles** — *Referral
   Source*, *Intermediary*, and *Referral Target* — instead of naming a
   specific kind of organisation in the prose and diagram.
2. Replace the present "Coordination Platform" actor in the workflow
   diagram(s) and accompanying narrative with the generic
   **Intermediary** actor, mirroring the terminology already used to
   describe direct and direct-light referrals.
3. After each role is introduced, add a non-normative *Examples of
   actors that can fill this role* line:
   - Referral Source: Healthcare Provider, CBO, etc.
   - Intermediary: Coordination Platform, HIE, etc.
   - Referral Target: CBO, etc.
4. Reuse the existing **Section 8.3.5 Indirect Referral – Light**
   diagram as the canonical visual; redraw or relabel the Section 8.0,
   8.2, and 8.3 diagrams to be consistent with it.
5. Update the General Referral Workflow narrative in 8.0 so that goal
   setting and intervention identification are not implicitly placed
   only with the screening healthcare provider; describe these as
   activities that any party in the appropriate role may perform,
   depending on the partnership.
6. Walk through any worked examples in 8.2/8.3 and replace
   "Coordination Platform" with at least one additional intermediary
   example (e.g., HIE) so the IG does not read as endorsing a single
   product category.

#### Justification

This is the disposition that matches the published **Persuasive**
resolution verbatim and the consensus expressed in the comment thread
(committee accepted the need; reporter and at least one other
participant explicitly agreed). Switching from naming organisation
types to naming **roles + example actors** is consistent with the
pattern already established for direct and direct-light referrals and
is a well-worn FHIR/IG editorial practice (define role, then list
example implementers). It is also a narrative-only change — no
StructureDefinition, profile, or value-set churn — so the implementer
impact is limited to documentation and diagrams. Anchoring the visuals
to the existing 8.3.5 diagram avoids inventing a new figure and keeps
the change cost contained.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt the role-based language change in Section 8 (as in Disposition A)
**but** also formalise the actor list in a small companion artefact
rather than only inline prose. Concretely:

1. Make the same Section 8 narrative and diagram updates as in
   Disposition A.
2. Add a short, normatively-numbered subsection (e.g., **Section 8.1
   Roles in the Referral Workflow**) that defines each of *Referral
   Source*, *Intermediary*, and *Referral Target* with a one-paragraph
   definition and an explicit, bulleted **Examples of conforming
   actors** list.
3. Optionally publish a companion **CodeSystem / ValueSet** (or reuse
   an existing one if one already covers these participant types) for
   the three roles, so that downstream profiles and example bundles
   referencing `PractitionerRole`, `CareTeam.participant.role`, or
   `Task.requester` / `Task.owner` can bind to a stable code rather
   than free text.
4. Cross-link Sections 8.2, 8.3, and 8.3.5 back to the new 8.1 so the
   role definitions live in one place.

#### Justification

This addresses the same underlying problem (the IG is conflating an
optional product category with a generic role) but pays a small extra
editorial cost up front in exchange for a more durable solution: a
single canonical place to define the roles, plus optional codes that
let implementers express which role a participant is filling without
re-inventing local terminology. Compared to Disposition A, it reduces
the risk that future tickets re-open the same ambiguity in other IG
sections, and it gives downstream profiles (e.g., SDOH `Task` flows or
`ServiceRequest` referrals) a hook for the role concept. The trade-off
is scope creep beyond the literal request and additional review
overhead for any new code system / value set.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket **Not Persuasive** (overriding the current Persuasive
resolution) and leave Section 8 as published, on the grounds that the
existing text is sufficient if read alongside the direct and
direct-light referral sections, and that the "Coordination Platform"
example is illustrative rather than prescriptive.

#### Justification

This option is included for completeness only. It is **not defensible
on the record**: the work group has already accepted in comments that
the middle actor needs a better definition, the reporter's concern was
seconded, and the published resolution already commits to the change.
Declining would also leave a real readability problem in place — the
8.0 diagram does present "Coordination Platform" as if it were the
canonical intermediary, which contradicts the Intermediary terminology
used elsewhere in Section 8 — and would invite a near-duplicate ticket
in the next ballot cycle.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The work group has already converged on the substance via the
**Persuasive** resolution and the `Ready-For-Vote` / `block-vote-3`
labelling, and the change is narrowly scoped to Section 8 narrative and
diagrams in the US SDOH Clinical Care IG. Disposition A locks that
agreement in with concrete, action-ready editorial steps (role
re-statement, generic Intermediary actor, example-actor lists, reuse
of the 8.3.5 diagram). Disposition B is a reasonable longer-term play
and could be queued as a follow-up ticket if Patient Care wants a
formal Roles subsection or a coded vocabulary, but bundling it here
risks scope creep that could delay an otherwise clean block vote.
Disposition C is not defensible given the existing committee
acceptance and reporter agreement.
