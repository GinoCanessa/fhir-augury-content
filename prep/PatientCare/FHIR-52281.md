## Summary

The reporter notes that the IPS Procedure profile binds SNOMED CT
`787480003 |No known procedures (situation)|` (via the
`procedures-uv-ips` value set, *preferred* binding) as the way to assert
that a patient has no known procedures, but the IG provides **no
guidance on how to populate the surrounding Procedure elements** when
the resource is being used for that "absent" assertion. Specifically,
implementers are left to guess at the appropriate value of
`Procedure.status` (in-progress? completed? unknown? not-done?) and at
the meaning of `Procedure.performed[x]` for an instance that, by
construction, represents the absence of a procedure (is it the date of
assertion? the date the patient was asked? must it be present at all?).
The ticket asks Patient Care / IPS to add explicit guidance and a
worked example.

The request was triggered by the AU Patient Summary work — see
[FHIR-52276](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-52276) ("No known
procedures - consider adoption of IPS approach"), which is the
HL7 Australia FHIR sibling that referred the underlying clarification
question to the IPS project rather than answering it inside AU Base /
AU Core / AU PS. Two parallel "no-known-X" clarifications for the
other recommended IPS sections were filed at the same time:
[FHIR-52277](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-52277) for immunizations
and [FHIR-52278](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-52278) for
medications. A later, more narrowly-scoped follow-up,
[FHIR-53568](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53568) (Gay Dolin), asks
the same question — "what `status` and `date` values do we expect?" —
specifically for *No Known Medications* in MedicationStatement (IPS)
and is still Unresolved. The IPS project call on 2025-09-03 reviewed
this ticket and elected to **defer the work until after IPS 2.0
publishes**; the resolution is therefore "Considered for Future Use",
not a normative answer.

## Details

**Description:**

> In each of the Procedure (IPS) profile the concept of `787480003 |No
> known procedures|` is bound via [Procedures - IPS][vs] (preferred) as
> a means to represent no known procedures.
>
> What is missing is guidance on the expected population of the
> following elements when populating an instance of Procedure to
> represent no known procedures:
>
> - `Procedure.status`
> - `Procedure.performed[x]` — the semantics of what this means, is it
>   date of assertion?
>
> Please provide clarification and an example.

[vs]: https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/fhir-ips/ValueSet-procedures-uv-ips.html

**Resolution Description:**

> Discussed on 9/3 IPS call. Will revisit after 2.0 publication.

**Comments:**

- **JIRAUSER25409 (2025-09-03):** Notes that FHIR R4 defines Procedure
  very broadly ("surgical procedures, diagnostic procedures,
  endoscopic procedures, biopsies, counseling, physiotherapy, personal
  support services, adult day care services, non-emergency
  transportation, home modification, exercise"). IPS itself does not
  narrow that definition for the Past Procedures section, which makes
  it semantically slippery to assert "none of those". The SNOMED
  concept `787480003 |No known procedures (situation)|` is correspondingly
  broad — covering admin services, blood-bank procedures, regimes and
  therapies, community-health procedures, etc. — and arguably needs to
  be re-scoped to mean "I have not had one of the procedures included
  in the IPS `procedures-uv-ips` value set", not literally "I have had
  no procedures of any kind".

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a normative *"Representing no known procedures"* sub-section to the
**Procedure (IPS) profile** narrative in the IPS IG (and to the *Past
Procedures* section page), specifying the canonical instance shape:

- `Procedure.code` = `787480003 |No known procedures (situation)|`
  (already supported by the preferred binding to `procedures-uv-ips`).
- `Procedure.status` = **`completed`**, with a sentence in the
  guidance that the asserted "fact" — that the patient has no known
  procedures — *is* itself the completed assertion, and that
  `in-progress` / `not-done` / `unknown` are inappropriate for this
  pattern.
- `Procedure.performed[x]` = `performedDateTime` set to **the date of
  assertion** (the date the absence was recorded / known to the
  authoring system), with a clarifying note that this is *not* a
  procedure date and that consumers must not interpret it as one. The
  guidance should be explicit that `performedPeriod`,
  `performedAge`, `performedRange` and `performedString` are *not* used
  for this pattern.
- Add a worked **Example** instance (e.g.,
  `Procedure-no-known-procedures-example`) referenced from the
  Procedure (IPS) profile page and surfaced in the IG examples list.
- Mirror the resolution adopted for `FHIR-52278` ("Persuasive with
  Modification") so the medications / procedures / immunizations
  sections present a consistent "no known X" pattern.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the AU PS team asked for in FHIR-52276 when they
referred the question to IPS, and it is the same answer shape that
IPS has already accepted in principle for medications (FHIR-52278,
*Persuasive with Modification*). Making `status = completed` is
consistent with the SNOMED modelling of `787480003` as a *situation
with explicit context* (Procedure context = *Action status unknown*) —
the assertion of the situation has been completed even though no
procedure has been performed. Anchoring `performedDateTime` to the
date of assertion is the only interpretation compatible with the
absence semantics; using a procedure date would be incoherent. Adding
a worked example removes the largest source of implementer guesswork.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Instead of overloading the Procedure resource with an "absent" instance
shape, recommend that IPS continue to use the **`absent-unknown-uv-ips`
code system** (i.e., `no-known-procedures`) inside the *section
emptyReason / dataAbsentReason* path on the IPS Composition's *Past
Procedures* section, and **explicitly deprecate or de-recommend** the
practice of constructing a Procedure resource that means "no
procedure". In parallel, file a SNOMED CT change request to rescope
`787480003 |No known procedures|` to "no known procedures from the
IPS `procedures-uv-ips` value set" (as JIRAUSER25409 argued in the
sole comment on FHIR-52281).

Concrete IPS changes:

- Update *Past Procedures* section guidance to use the
  Composition-level absent/unknown pattern (consistent with how
  Allergies and Problems handle "no known X" today via the
  `absent-unknown-uv-ips` code system).
- Add a "do not use" note on the Procedure (IPS) profile page
  explaining why standalone `Procedure.code = 787480003` is
  problematic (no good `status` value, no meaningful
  `performed[x]`, semantic breadth).
- Add a tracker reference for the SNOMED CT rescoping request.

#### Justification

Building a "Procedure that is not a procedure" creates downstream
problems for every consumer that treats `Procedure` as evidence of an
event (clinical-decision-support rules, analytics, payer logic). The
historical IPS Absent-Unknown thread shows the community already
considered this problem and chose the section-level absent-unknown
pattern; the SNOMED-CT-based "no known X" approach was adopted later
for parity with allergies/medications, but the modelling
inconsistencies Rob Hausam catalogued (Action-status-unknown vs
Finding-context-Unknown vs Known-absent) are still unresolved.
Disposition B sidesteps the `status` / `performed[x]` question
entirely instead of legislating a workaround.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (Hold for IPS 2.0+)

#### Proposal

Maintain the current resolution — **"Considered for Future Use",
revisit after IPS 2.0 publishes** — and explicitly close FHIR-52281
without normative changes, deferring the work into a single
post-2.0 IPS work-stream that bundles:

- FHIR-52281 (procedures),
- FHIR-52277 (immunizations),
- FHIR-53568 (medications status/date),
- the obligations / multi-cardinality work tracked in
  FHIR-53114 / FHIR-55396 / FHIR-55398 / FHIR-53720,
- a SNOMED CT engagement on the modelling inconsistency for
  `787480003`-family concepts.

Document the deferral on the Procedure (IPS) profile page so
implementers know the gap is acknowledged.

#### Justification

The IPS Project call already chose this disposition on 2025-09-03 for
sound reasons: the question is intertwined with the broader
Obligations / Must Support / multi-cardinality discussion that the
group is actively working through, and answering the procedures case
in isolation risks producing a guidance pattern that will need to be
re-stated as soon as the obligations work lands. Deferral is also
consistent with the AU PS resolution on FHIR-52276, which explicitly
removed `787480003` from AU PS Release 1 pending the same
clarification.

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **A**, with an explicit cross-reference
to the medications resolution (FHIR-52278, FHIR-53568) so the
procedures / immunizations / medications stories land as one
consistent "no known X" pattern across IPS profiles.

The strongest argument against staying with C is that AU PS has
already shipped Release 1 *without* this guidance and has openly told
the community it is waiting on IPS to answer; FHIR-52278 has been
accepted for modification on the medications side, so leaving
procedures unanswered creates a deliberately inconsistent IPS surface.
Disposition B is intellectually appealing — it would be the
*architecturally* cleanest answer — but the ship sailed when IPS
adopted the SNOMED `787480003`-family bindings, and the work to
reverse that (plus a SNOMED CT change request) is far larger than
adding a half-page of guidance and one example. Disposition A is the
minimum change that (a) directly answers what the reporter asked
for, (b) matches the precedent set on the medications sibling, and
(c) gives implementers a deterministic shape for the resource today
without prejudging the eventual outcome of the broader Obligations /
absent-unknown work. The workgroup can still re-open the deeper
modelling question as part of IPS 2.x if the Obligations work
suggests a better pattern.
