## Summary

The reporter raises an interoperability concern that the **US SDOH Clinical
Care IG** defines `SDOHCC-HealthcareServiceForReferralManagement` (constraining
`HealthcareService.category` and `HealthcareService.type` for social-care
referral workflows) without establishing any formal relationship to the
**HealthcareService** profile published by the **FAST National Directory (NDH)
IG**. Because the SDOHCC IG narrative anticipates that future implementations
may rely on standards-based directories such as NDH, the absence of explicit
coordination risks divergent constraints, value sets, and semantics for service
classification, and increases burden on Coordinating Platforms / CBOs that need
to participate in both ecosystems. The submitter asks Patient Care / Gravity to
clarify one of three paths: (a) declare SDOHCC-HealthcareServiceForReferralManagement
as a derivation of (or compatible with) NDH HealthcareService, (b) normatively
reference NDH HealthcareService for directory discovery and layer SDOH-specific
constraints on top, or (c) collaborate with the FAST NDH IG to extend NDH
HealthcareService and its value sets so NDH itself can serve as the
authoritative directory for both healthcare and social-care services. There are
no cross-referenced Jira tickets that materially extend the request — the only
Jira cross-references are to the FHIR core elements `HealthcareService.category`
and `HealthcareService.type`.

## Details

**Description:**

> Issue Summary:
> The SDOH Clinical Care IG defines the SDOHCC-HealthcareServiceForReferralManagement
> profile to support Coordinating Platform discovery of CBO services and referral
> management. This profile constrains HealthcareService.category and
> HealthcareService.type with values appropriate for social care workflows
> (e.g., SDOH, Assessment).
>
> However, the IG does not define a formal relationship between this profile and
> the HealthcareService profile in the FAST National Directory (NDH) IG, despite
> the SDOH Clinical Care IG indicating that future implementations may leverage
> standards-based directories such as NDH.
>
> Impact:
> The absence of explicit coordination between SDOHCC and NDH HealthcareService
> profiles introduces interoperability risks, including:
>
> 1. Uncertainty as to whether SDOHCC-HealthcareServiceForReferralManagement
>    instances are expected to be compatible with NDH-based directories.
> 2. Potential divergence in constraints, value sets, and semantics for service
>    classification and program-level representation.
> 3. Increased implementation burden for Coordinating Platforms and CBOs that
>    wish to support both SDOH referral workflows and NDH directory publication.
> 4. Reduced consistency in service discovery behavior when NDH is used as the
>    underlying directory for SDOH workflows.
>
> Interoperability Concern:
> Given the stated intent to leverage the FAST National Directory, it remains
> unclear whether:
>
> 1. SDOHCC-HealthcareServiceForReferralManagement is intended to be a
>    specialization of NDH HealthcareService,
> 2. NDH HealthcareService is intended to be adapted to directly support SDOH
>    referral management requirements, or
> 3. The two profiles are expected to coexist independently with
>    implementer-defined mappings.
>
> The lack of guidance in this area may lead to fragmented or non-interoperable
> implementations.
>
> Recommendation:
> To support interoperable, standards-based service discovery and referral
> workflows, the SDOH Clinical Care IG should explicitly coordinate with the
> FAST National Directory IG by documenting one or more of the following
> approaches:
>
> 1. Declaring SDOHCC-HealthcareServiceForReferralManagement as derived from or
>    compatible with the NDH HealthcareService profile;
> 2. Normatively referencing NDH HealthcareService for directory-based
>    discovery, while layering SDOH-specific constraints as needed; or
> 3. Collaborating with the FAST NDH IG to adapt NDH HealthcareService profiles
>    and value sets to incorporate SDOH referral management requirements,
>    allowing NDH to serve as the authoritative directory representation for
>    both healthcare and social care services.

**Resolution (already proposed by Gravity, 2026-02-27):**

> The Gravity Project is actively coordinating with FAST and the NDH IG. We are
> going to wait for the NDH IG to go through formal publication, as it is
> currently undergoing revision, and will work to harmonize our HealthcareService
> profiles after that occurs. Selected approach: "Collaborating with the FAST
> NDH IG to adapt NDH HealthcareService profiles and value sets to incorporate
> SDOH referral management requirements, allowing NDH to serve as the
> authoritative directory representation for both healthcare and social care
> services."

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add normative coordination text to the SDOH Clinical Care IG's
HealthcareService guidance page and to the
`SDOHCC-HealthcareServiceForReferralManagement` profile narrative explicitly
stating the relationship to the FAST NDH `HealthcareService` profile. Concretely:

- Update the profile's intro to declare that SDOHCC-HealthcareServiceForReferralManagement
  instances **SHOULD also conform to** the NDH HealthcareService profile when
  published in an NDH-aligned directory, and that constraints on
  `HealthcareService.category` (SDOH categories) and `HealthcareService.type`
  (Assessment, etc.) are layered **on top of** NDH's expectations rather than
  replacing them.
- Document the resolution path between SDOHCC's value sets for `category` /
  `type` and the corresponding NDH bindings (mapping table or value-set
  union/extension), so a single instance can satisfy both profiles.
- Add an interoperability section pointing implementers at the NDH IG for
  directory mechanics (Endpoint, Location, OrganizationAffiliation, restrictions)
  while keeping SDOHCC focused on referral-management semantics.

#### Justification

This is the literal ask. The SDOHCC IG already signals that NDH-style
directories are the expected long-term substrate; making that explicit removes
ambiguity for Coordinating Platforms and CBOs and prevents value-set drift on
`HealthcareService.category` / `HealthcareService.type`. Layering SDOHCC
constraints on top of an NDH baseline is consistent with the prevailing US
Realm pattern (US Core ↔ domain-specific IGs) and aligns with the third
recommendation in the original ticket — the option Gravity itself selected in
the resolution narrative.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach (Recommended — matches Gravity's posted resolution)

#### Proposal

**Defer the harmonization work** to a subsequent SDOHCC publication cycle and
record the dependency on NDH explicitly. Specifically:

- Mark the issue as **Considered for Future Use** for STU3 (already the
  proposed disposition in the block vote).
- Add a short non-normative note to the
  `SDOHCC-HealthcareServiceForReferralManagement` profile page acknowledging
  that NDH HealthcareService is the intended long-term directory representation
  and that formal harmonization is pending NDH's next publication.
- Track a follow-up tracker ticket against the SDOHCC IG to revisit
  derivation / value-set alignment once the FAST NDH IG completes its current
  revision cycle, at which point Gravity and FAST will jointly decide between
  derivation, normative reference, or NDH extension.

#### Justification

NDH is itself undergoing revision; pinning SDOHCC to a moving target risks
publishing constraints that conflict with the next NDH ballot and forcing a
breaking change in SDOHCC STU4. A short note acknowledging the gap preserves
implementer expectations without committing to a specific harmonization
mechanism prematurely. This mirrors the resolution Gravity has already
recorded ("waiting for NDH formal publication, then harmonize") and is the
disposition listed in the Patient Care block-vote thread, so it should
proceed cleanly through the block vote.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request on the grounds that NDH compatibility is **out of scope**
for the current SDOHCC STU3 publication: the SDOHCC IG defines social-care
referral semantics, the NDH IG defines directory mechanics, and implementers
that need both can compose them today using standard FHIR profile
intersection. Close the ticket as **Not Persuasive — Out of Scope** without
adding NDH-coordination text to the SDOHCC IG.

#### Justification

A defensible (but weak) reading is that nothing in SDOHCC STU3 prevents an
implementer from publishing a `HealthcareService` instance that conforms to
both `SDOHCC-HealthcareServiceForReferralManagement` and the NDH profile;
profile composition is a built-in FHIR mechanism. Declining keeps the SDOHCC
publication free of cross-IG dependencies on an unstable NDH revision.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **B**

Disposition B matches the resolution Gravity has already posted on the ticket
("Considered for Future Use" pending NDH formal publication) and is the
disposition listed in the **Gravity SDOHCC STU3 - Block Vote 2** Zulip thread
that Patient Care is being asked to approve, so it has the smoothest path
through the block vote. It also avoids the real risk in Disposition A:
publishing concrete derivation / value-set bindings against an NDH profile
that is itself mid-revision would lock SDOHCC STU3 to a soon-to-change
target and could force a breaking change in the next SDOHCC publication.
Disposition C is too dismissive — the reporter has identified a genuine
interoperability gap that *will* need to be closed; declining outright sends
the wrong signal to implementers building toward NDH-backed directories.
B is the honest middle path: acknowledge the gap, defer the binding decision
to a coordinated SDOHCC ↔ FAST NDH effort, and revisit once NDH stabilises.
