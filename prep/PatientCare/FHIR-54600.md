## Summary

The reporter (TomPage) requests that two additional items be appended to the
"Challenges of SDOH Data Interoperability" list in the US SDOH Clinical Care
FHIR implementation guide. The first proposed addition highlights that
different systems screen for different SDOH domains, creating consistency
problems for data collection, exchange, and interoperability. The second
addition observes that initial data-collection instruments vary in structure
and often conflate screening and assessment questions, which makes consistent
coding and exchange of the resulting findings and observations difficult. The
ticket has already been triaged with a "Not Persuasive" resolution: the work
group concluded the proposed challenges are already covered by the existing
items 1 (data standardization issues) and 2 (terminology inconsistency).
There are no linked or cross-referenced Jira tickets, no related Zulip
discussions, and no associated GitHub items in the indexed sources.

## Details

**Description:**

Add the following challenges:

4. Different systems screen for different domains which creates challenges
   for consistency in data collection, data exchange and interoperability.

5. Inconsistent structure and the types of questions asked on initial data
   collection instruments often conflates screening and assessment questions
   which can create challenges for consistently coding and exchanging findings
   and observations.

**Resolution:**

The proposed updates to the list of challenges already fall under
challenges 1 (data standardization issues) and 2 (terminology inconsistency),
so we will not add these to the IG.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the "Challenges of SDOH Data Interoperability" narrative section of
the US SDOH Clinical Care IG to append two new numbered items, worded
substantively as the reporter wrote them:

- **4. Domain-coverage variability** — "Different systems screen for
  different SDOH domains, creating challenges for consistency in data
  collection, data exchange, and interoperability."
- **5. Instrument structure variability** — "Initial data-collection
  instruments vary in structure and often conflate screening and assessment
  questions, which can create challenges for consistently coding and
  exchanging findings and observations."

The change is editorial/narrative only — it touches the prose of the
challenges page in the IG and does not alter any conformance resources,
profiles, value sets, or examples.

#### Justification

The two proposed items name distinct, implementer-visible problems that
existing items 1 (data standardization) and 2 (terminology inconsistency)
gloss over. Item 4 is about *which questions are even asked* (domain
coverage) — a scope problem that is upstream of standardization. Item 5 is
about the *shape of the instruments themselves* (screening vs. assessment
conflation) — a modeling problem that affects how Observations and their
derived findings should be coded and linked. Calling these out explicitly
helps readers and implementers understand why a single canonical set of
SDOH Observations is hard to achieve, and prepares them for the IG's
guidance on screening response patterns. Because the change is narrative,
the cost is low and there is no compatibility risk.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than appending two new bullets verbatim, **revise items 1 and 2** in
place to make their scope explicit, and add a single new item that
specifically addresses screening-vs-assessment conflation:

- Reword item 1 (data standardization issues) to call out domain-coverage
  variability between screening programs as a concrete example of the
  standardization gap.
- Reword item 2 (terminology inconsistency) to note that inconsistent
  instrument structure compounds the terminology problem.
- Add a single new item (e.g., #4) focused narrowly on the
  screening-vs-assessment distinction, with a forward-link to the IG's
  guidance on Observation profiles for screening responses vs. derived
  assessments.

#### Justification

This approach acknowledges the work group's stated position that the new
items overlap with existing items 1 and 2, while still capturing what the
reporter actually contributes that *isn't* already there: the
screening-vs-assessment conflation point is a concrete, IG-specific concern
that the existing items don't name. Folding the domain-coverage point into
item 1 avoids duplication, and adding a focused new item avoids growing the
list with overlapping prose. Trade-off vs. A: more editorial work and a
risk of disturbing wording that may have been ballot-reconciled
previously; vs. C: still gives the reporter a visible response in the IG.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (Not Persuasive)

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Not Persuasive** — the disposition already applied
by the work group. Communicate to the reporter that the two proposed
challenges are subsumed by existing item 1 (data standardization issues)
and item 2 (terminology inconsistency), and no IG change will be made.

#### Justification

The challenges section is intentionally a high-level narrative list, not an
enumeration of every concrete failure mode. Domain-coverage variability is
a downstream symptom of the standardization gap that item 1 already names;
screening-vs-assessment conflation is a specific instance of the
terminology and modeling problems that item 2 names. Adding overlapping
items risks signalling to implementers that these are *separate* problems
requiring *separate* mitigations, when in practice the IG's existing
guidance on profiles and value sets addresses both. Declining preserves
the section's brevity and avoids re-balloting narrative for items already
covered.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The work group's "Not Persuasive" rationale is defensible — the new items
*do* overlap with items 1 and 2 — but Disposition C leaves real,
implementer-relevant content on the table. In particular, the
screening-vs-assessment conflation point is genuinely distinct from
generic "terminology inconsistency": it is about *instrument design* and
*Observation modeling*, not about codes. Disposition B captures that
distinct contribution with a single targeted addition while honoring the
work group's concern about list duplication, and it gives the reporter a
visible, substantive response. Disposition A risks the bloat the work
group flagged; Disposition C risks discouraging future narrative
contributions that don't slot cleanly under an existing heading.
