## Summary

FHIR-37754 is a Patient Care follow-up to [FHIR-36197](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-36197)
("Representing improving | deteriorating patient responses in R4/R5 Condition resource"),
which was resolved *Persuasive with Modification* by adding `ClinicalImpression.changePattern`
to record whether a subject's condition is worsening, improving, or unchanged. While drafting
that resolution, Patient Care noticed that the existing definitional comment on
`ClinicalImpression.effective[x]` — *"This SHOULD be accurate to at least the minute, though
some assessments only have a known date"* — is awkward: it is written as if it applies to all
assessments, when in fact many `ClinicalImpression` instances are only known to date precision
(e.g., a paper review captured later). The reporter (Michelle Miller) asks two coupled
questions:

1. Should the wording on `ClinicalImpression.effective` be softened/clarified so it does not
   imply minute precision is expected for *all* impressions?
2. For the new `changePattern` element, do we need a *separate* element to convey the time
   reference against which the change is being judged (i.e., the "since when?" that the
   workgroup repeatedly asked about in the parent ticket — see FHIR-36197 comments and the
   Patient Care WG Zulip thread "Pre-existing condition status trend (worsened)")?

The reporter sketches three candidate mechanisms for the "last assessed" anchor:

- a `Reference(ClinicalImpression)` to the prior impression,
- a `Reference(Encounter)` to the visit at which the previous assessment occurred, or
- a primitive `dateTime` (`lastAssessed`).

The ticket has been Triaged at Medium priority since 2022-07-07 and has had no substantive
movement (last touch 2024-09-12) — it is squarely a "decide and dispose" item for Patient Care.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Make two coordinated edits to the `ClinicalImpression` resource in FHIR Core (`HL7/fhir`,
`source/clinicalimpression/clinicalimpression.xml` plus the corresponding fragments and the
narrative intro):

1. **Loosen the comment on `ClinicalImpression.effective[x]`** so it does not imply minute
   precision is expected for every impression. Replace the current
   *"This SHOULD be accurate to at least the minute, though some assessments only have a
   known date."* with something closer to *"When the assessment is tied to a specific
   clinical event (e.g., a bedside review or encounter), the value SHOULD be accurate to at
   least the minute. Many assessments are only known to date precision, in which case `date`
   is acceptable."* No type change to `effective[x]` (it remains `dateTime | Period`).
2. **Add `ClinicalImpression.previous` as `Reference(ClinicalImpression)`** with cardinality
   `0..1`, sequenced near `changePattern`, with a short description such as *"Previous
   assessment that this impression's `changePattern` is being judged against."* Definition:
   *"When `changePattern` is populated, this element identifies the prior `ClinicalImpression`
   that establishes the baseline for the change. If absent, the change is relative to the
   most recent prior impression for the same subject and problem(s), or is implicit."*
   Note: `ClinicalImpression` already has a `previous` element in some historical revisions —
   if it is still present in R5/R6 the proposal collapses to a definition refresh that ties
   `previous` explicitly to `changePattern`.

This realises the reporter's first option ("Reference to last ClinicalImpression"), which is
the cleanest fit because `ClinicalImpression` is itself the resource that carries the prior
assessment.

#### Justification

This addresses both halves of the ticket directly, keeps the data model aligned with how
`ClinicalImpression` already chains (every assessment is itself a clinical impression with
its own `effective` time), and avoids inventing a parallel `lastAssessed` primitive whose
semantics would duplicate `previous.effective`. The Zulip thread shows that Patient Care
already concluded the assessment chain belongs on `ClinicalImpression`, and the Jul 7 2022
WG call notes on FHIR-36197 record Larry/Stephen's preference that the "since when" be the
*last event/visit* rather than an arbitrary boolean or future trend. The wording fix on
`effective[x]` is a low-risk doc change that resolves a long-standing inaccuracy flagged on
the resource.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt the reporter's third option instead: keep the `effective[x]` wording change from
Disposition A, but add a small primitive element **`ClinicalImpression.changePattern.lastAssessed`**
(or, if extension-modelling is preferred, a `changePattern` `CodeableConcept` plus a sibling
`lastAssessedDateTime` of type `dateTime`, `0..1`) instead of leaning on
`Reference(ClinicalImpression)`. Optionally extend it to `dateTime | Period | Reference(Encounter)`
via a `[x]` choice so implementers can either provide an explicit timestamp or point at the
encounter where the prior assessment occurred. Document explicitly that, when absent, the
"since when" is left to the consumer to infer.

#### Justification

Lloyd McKenzie's Zulip caveat — *"ClinicalImpression isn't widely supported right now"* — and
the Patient Care WG's repeated complaint that a prior `ClinicalImpression` may simply not
exist in the record (the encounter or bedside review never produced one) argues against
*requiring* that `previous` be the only anchor. A primitive `lastAssessed` is cheaper for
implementers, can be populated even when no separate prior impression exists (e.g., NLP
pipelines extracting "worsening since last visit" from a narrative — Tom Oniki's use case in
FHIR-36197 comment 35252), and avoids creating a cycle of `ClinicalImpression` references in
records that only ever carry one impression per encounter. Trade-off vs. A: less referential
integrity, but lower implementation burden and broader applicability across the use cases the
WG actually surfaced.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark FHIR-37754 *Not Persuasive*. Take only the trivial wording fix on
`ClinicalImpression.effective[x]` (move the "minute precision" sentence into a usage note and
qualify it with "where applicable"), and decline to add any new "last assessed" element. Tell
implementers that:

- the existing `ClinicalImpression.previous` (where present in the resource) already chains
  prior impressions, and
- where a non-`ClinicalImpression` anchor is required, an implementer-defined extension on
  `changePattern` is the right vehicle until concrete cross-implementation use cases
  demonstrate the need for a core element.

#### Justification

`ClinicalImpression` has low real-world deployment (Lloyd's note) and the WG's own discussion
in FHIR-36197 surfaced *three* candidate mechanisms without converging on one — that is a
classic signal to wait for implementer feedback rather than bake a guess into the core
resource. R5 is published; adding a new element now is a Trial Use change that costs the
broader community more than it saves the (small) `ClinicalImpression`-using community. The
underlying "since when" semantics can be carried by `previous.effective` today for
implementers who chain impressions, and by an extension for those who do not, with no
breaking change.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Patient Care has been circling this question since March 2022 and the WG transcripts in
FHIR-36197 show repeated convergence on two ideas: (1) the "since when" anchor needs to
exist, and (2) the chain of assessments naturally lives on `ClinicalImpression`. Disposition
A captures both with the minimum new modelling — a `previous` reference (the model `ClinicalImpression`
already has the right shape for) plus a defensible wording fix on `effective[x]` — without
inventing a primitive that overlaps `previous.effective`. Disposition B is a reasonable
fallback if the WG decides Lloyd's "ClinicalImpression isn't widely supported" objection is
fatal to the reference-based approach, and Disposition C should only win if Patient Care
genuinely wants to push the question back to implementers — but given that Patient Care
*is* the implementer community for `ClinicalImpression`, deferring further is unlikely to
produce new evidence. Take A, ship it as Trial Use, and revisit on the next ballot cycle if
implementer feedback contradicts the choice.
