## Summary

Ben Hamlin proposes that the PACIO Transitions of Care Implementation
Guide be positioned to ensure care plans are anchored to a longitudinal,
patient-centered "patient outcome health record" (an expansion of the
PHR concept) rather than to discrete, role- or setting-specific care
plans authored by individual professionals. The intent is that a single
care plan, together with the assessments, patient goals, and
patient-reported outcomes that surround it, follows the patient across
care settings and is tracked by the patient or their advocate, thereby
supporting patient engagement, activation, and self-management. The
reporter frames this as consistent with a broader vision of a digital
health system that transcends the EHR and aggregates data from multiple
sources via APIs, and asserts that PACIO is well placed to drive that
transformation. No specific resource, profile, extension, or page is
called out — the ticket is essentially a strategic / scope direction
for the IG.

There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets, no related Zulip
discussions, and no related GitHub items recorded for this ticket in
the FHIR Augury index.

## Details

**Description:**

PACIO is best suited to also ensure that the care plan is tied to a
patient outcome health record (expansion of PHR) so that ONE care plans
follows the patient versus several care plans generated by professionals
that create several care plans tied to their role/setting. This also
means having all assessments, patient goals, patient-reported outcomes
centralized and tracked by the patient/advocate--impacting patient
engagement AND activation AND self-management. A longitudinal record is
needed. This is not far reaching given the goal is a digital health
system that transcends settings and captures data from multiple sources
using APIs. This extends the reliance beyond the EHR to a vision of a
digital health system with persons at the center of the longitudinal
health journey. PACIO can transcend the divide and be the transformation.

## Proposed Dispositions

> Note: with no cross-referenced artifacts, no Zulip thread, and no
> GitHub items in the index, the dispositions below are grounded in
> the ticket text and the named specification (PACIO Transitions of
> Care IG). Repo-level paths are not asserted because this skill has
> no `repo-analysis` briefing for any PACIO-ToC repo to draw from.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the PACIO Transitions of Care IG narrative — specifically the
scope, use-case / actors, and care-planning sections of the IG home /
introduction pages — to explicitly state that the IG targets a single,
longitudinal, patient-anchored care plan that follows the patient
across settings, and that aggregates assessments, patient goals, and
patient-reported outcomes under the patient/advocate's stewardship.
Reinforce this by:

- Adding (or strengthening) a "Patient as primary subject and
  steward" actor / principle in the IG's actors & roles narrative.
- Asserting in the conformance narrative that conforming systems
  SHOULD be capable of contributing to, and reading from, a single
  longitudinal `CarePlan` instance per patient transition episode
  rather than producing setting-scoped duplicates.
- Cross-referencing PHR / patient-facing API patterns (e.g., SMART
  patient-launch, US Core Patient access) so implementers see the
  expected ecosystem.

No new resources, profiles, extensions, or value sets are introduced;
this is a scope and guidance change inside the PACIO ToC IG narrative.

#### Justification

The request is fundamentally a positioning / scope statement for the
IG, not a structural change to FHIR resources. Accepting it as a
narrative-level clarification keeps the IG aligned with widely-shared
FHIR principles — `CarePlan` is already designed to support a
longitudinal, multi-author plan per patient, and `Goal`,
`Observation` (including PROs via the patient-reported observation
patterns), and assessment instruments are already in scope for PACIO.
Making the patient-centered, longitudinal stance explicit costs little
and reduces the risk that early implementers replicate the
fragmented, role-scoped care-plan pattern the reporter is warning
against.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than amending PACIO ToC's scope to own a "patient outcome
health record," treat the request as **input to a cross-WG patient-
centered care-coordination companion document**, and respond in the
PACIO ToC IG by:

1. Adding a short "Relationship to PHR / patient-facing access"
   section that points at existing artifacts (US Core, International
   Patient Access (IPA), International Patient Summary (IPS),
   SMART patient launch, and the Patient Care WG's CarePlan / Goal
   guidance) instead of redefining a new "patient outcome health
   record" inside PACIO.
2. Optionally profiling `CarePlan.subject` / `CarePlan.author` usage
   guidance to recommend a single per-patient longitudinal plan with
   multi-author contributions, rather than per-setting plan
   instances.
3. Referring the broader "longitudinal patient-stewarded record"
   vision to Patient Care WG (and, where relevant, FHIR-I and PA) as
   a coordination ask rather than a PACIO-only deliverable.

#### Justification

The reporter's vision — one longitudinal, patient-stewarded record —
spans well beyond Transitions of Care. PHR semantics, patient
stewardship, and cross-setting aggregation are the proper concern of
patient-facing IGs (IPA / IPS / US Core patient access) plus the
broader Patient Care CarePlan guidance. PACIO ToC should align with
those rather than redefine them, which avoids divergence and keeps
PACIO's scope focused on the transitions use case it was chartered
for. This still honors the substance of the request — a single
patient-centered care plan and centralized PROs / goals / assessments
— without expanding PACIO's normative footprint.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline as **out of scope** for PACIO Transitions of Care, on the
grounds that the request is a strategic / vision statement for the
broader US digital-health ecosystem (a patient-stewarded longitudinal
"patient outcome health record") rather than a concrete change to a
PACIO artifact, and that the underlying technical capability — one
longitudinal `CarePlan` per patient, with associated `Goal`s,
assessments, and patient-reported `Observation`s — is already
expressible with existing FHIR resources and existing PACIO profiles.
Close as "Persuasive with mod" / "Not Persuasive" with a pointer to
Patient Care WG's CarePlan guidance and to patient-access IGs
(US Core / IPA / IPS) for the PHR aspects.

#### Justification

The ticket does not identify a specific defect, missing element,
profile gap, or constraint to change. It is a directional ask. PACIO's
normative artifacts already permit the longitudinal, patient-anchored
pattern the reporter advocates; nothing in the IG forces per-role or
per-setting plan duplication. Adopting the request as written would
require PACIO to take on PHR scope that belongs to other groups, with
attendant risk of duplication, divergence, and scope creep.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The reporter is raising a real and important concern — fragmented,
role-scoped care plans are a known anti-pattern, and PACIO is well
placed to push back on it — but the right response is not to expand
PACIO ToC into a PHR specification (Disposition A overreaches into
narrative territory that other groups own) and not to dismiss the
input outright (Disposition C leaves a real gap in IG guidance).
Disposition B threads the needle: PACIO ToC adds a focused
"Relationship to PHR / patient-facing access" section and tightens
its `CarePlan` usage guidance to recommend a single longitudinal,
multi-author plan per patient, while explicitly deferring the broader
patient-stewarded longitudinal record vision to Patient Care WG and
to patient-access IGs (US Core, IPA, IPS). That keeps PACIO's
deliverables tractable, avoids stepping on adjacent IGs, and still
moves the ecosystem in the direction the reporter is asking for.
