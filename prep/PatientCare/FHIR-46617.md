## Summary

Russell Leftwich reports that the `ClinicalImpression.problem` element has
three related deficiencies in FHIR Core. First, because the element is
`0..*`, both its short display and its definition should be expressed in
the plural ("problem(s)") rather than singular. Second, the short display
is not consistent with the longer definition. Third — and most
substantively — the definition does not anchor the listed problems to
the *context* of the assessment that makes them relevant; that context
is the same concept as the proposed assessment category requested in
companion ticket [FHIR-46597](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-46597).
The reporter also observes that other elements on the same resource
(e.g., `ClinicalImpression.prognosis`) can be specific to individual
problems within `problem` rather than to the resource as a whole, which
the current data shape does not express. Taken together with FHIR-46597
(which asks for a `category` element on `ClinicalImpression`), the two
tickets argue that `ClinicalImpression` does not currently make clear
*what kind of assessment* a given instance represents nor *what scope*
its findings are tied to.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply three coordinated edits to `ClinicalImpression.problem` in FHIR
Core:

1. Update the **short display** so it reads in the plural and matches
   the definition — for example, "Relevant problem(s) in scope of this
   assessment" — making the `0..*` cardinality obvious from the label.
2. Rewrite the **definition** to explicitly tie the listed problems to
   the *context* of the assessment instance, e.g.: "The problem(s)
   considered in scope of this clinical impression. Which problems are
   in scope is determined by the type/category of assessment (see
   `ClinicalImpression.category`) — e.g., a comprehensive evaluation
   may list all active problems, while a focused or emergent
   assessment lists only the problem(s) the assessment was performed
   for." (This wording is contingent on FHIR-46597 being accepted; if
   it is not, replace the parenthetical with prose that describes
   "context" without naming a non-existent element.)
3. Add explanatory **comments / usage notes** on the resource (or on
   `problem` and on the related `finding` / `prognosis` elements)
   stating that other resource elements may be scoped to one or more
   entries in `problem` rather than to the resource as a whole, and
   that implementers needing to associate a specific finding,
   prognosis, or follow-up action to a specific problem should do so
   via referencing or via `finding.itemReference` rather than
   relying on positional correspondence.

#### Justification

The first two changes are pure documentation/clarification —
short-display/definition consistency is a recognized quality bar in
FHIR Core, and aligning the wording with `0..*` cardinality has no
implementation impact. The third change addresses a real expressivity
gap that the reporter has correctly identified: today, a reader of a
`ClinicalImpression` cannot tell whether `prognosis` applies to one
specific problem or to the patient overall. Tying these clarifications
to FHIR-46597's proposed `category` keeps the two related tickets
internally consistent and avoids the work being undone the next time
either ticket is revisited.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt the documentation/short-display fixes from Disposition A
(items 1 and 2, with the `category` reference made conditional on
FHIR-46597), but address the "scope of finding/prognosis" concern
structurally rather than by usage notes alone. Specifically:

- Keep `ClinicalImpression.problem` as-is in cardinality and target
  type, but add an optional **back-reference** on `finding` (e.g.,
  `ClinicalImpression.finding.relatedProblem : Reference(Condition)`
  or a `0..*` element identifying which entries of `problem` a finding
  pertains to) so a finding can be explicitly associated with one or
  more in-scope problems.
- Apply the same treatment to `prognosis` (e.g., split or extend so a
  prognosis can be associated with a specific problem).
- Hold the broader "context of the assessment" wording until
  FHIR-46597 is dispositioned, and only then make the
  `problem`-definition wording reference `category`.

#### Justification

The reporter's structural observation — that `prognosis` and findings
may be problem-specific — is real and is poorly served by prose alone.
Implementers today either lose the per-problem association or invent
extensions to capture it; standardizing the back-reference closes that
gap and prevents divergent extensions. Sequencing the wording change
behind FHIR-46597 also avoids forward-referencing an element that may
or may not be added. The trade-off vs. Disposition A is a larger and
potentially breaking change to the resource shape (new optional
elements, possible re-slicing of `finding`), which Patient Care should
weigh against the actual implementer demand for this association.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the structural/contextual aspect of the request and accept
only the trivial editorial parts (short-display plural wording and any
typographical inconsistency between short display and definition).
Specifically: do **not** rewrite the definition to introduce the
"context of the assessment" concept, do **not** add new elements to
associate findings/prognosis with specific problems, and **do not**
make the wording change contingent on FHIR-46597. Rationale provided
back to the submitter: the per-problem scoping concern is already
addressable by referencing the relevant `Condition` resource(s)
directly from a separate `Observation`, `RiskAssessment`, or
follow-up `CarePlan`/`ServiceRequest`; the "context" the reporter is
asking for is supplied by the surrounding `Encounter`,
`encounter.serviceType`, and the choice of resource itself.

#### Justification

`ClinicalImpression` is in the Patient Care domain at maturity level 0
and is intentionally lean. Adding scope-tying mechanics here
duplicates capabilities that already exist on adjacent resources and
risks pushing `ClinicalImpression` toward becoming a mini–care-plan.
Declining the structural change avoids a normative-track change to a
low-maturity resource and avoids forward-coupling 46617 to 46597,
which is itself unresolved. The editorial items are individually so
small that they can be folded into a routine documentation pass
without a separate disposition.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A is the cleanest resolution because it directly answers
all three points the reporter raised, without committing the work
group to the larger structural change implied by Disposition B before
FHIR-46597 has been dispositioned. The editorial fixes (short display,
plural wording, definition/short-display alignment) are unambiguous
quality wins. The "context" wording can be written today in a way that
is conditional on FHIR-46597's outcome — if 46597 is accepted, the
wording naturally references `ClinicalImpression.category`; if it is
declined, the same wording can stand on its own by referring to the
assessment's purpose and the associated `Encounter`. Disposition B
should be revisited as a separate ticket if implementers report real
problems associating findings/prognosis with specific problems;
without that signal (and there is no Zulip or GitHub traffic on this
ticket), pre-emptively expanding the resource shape is over-reach.
Disposition C unnecessarily leaves the contextual gap that the
reporter — a Patient Care subject-matter expert — has explicitly
flagged, and would likely resurface as a follow-up ticket.
