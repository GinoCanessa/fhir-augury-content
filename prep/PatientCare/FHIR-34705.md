## Summary

The reporter (Genevieve Luensman, NIOSH) is asking the HL7 US SDOH
Clinical Care IG to explicitly call out — in its "Out of Scope" section
— that providing standards for a person's *work information* is **not**
in scope, and to direct readers instead to the HL7 Occupational Data
for Health (ODH) implementation guide (`http://hl7.org/fhir/us/odh/`).
The proposed insertion is short and is intended as a boundary statement
between the SDOH-CC IG and the ODH IG so that implementers do not
attempt to encode occupational/work data through SDOH constructs.

This ticket is the SDOH-CC counterpart to the now-published
[FHIR-34701](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-34701) ("Clarify that SDOH
data elements does not include work information (see ODH)"), which
asked the same essential clarification of **US Core** and was resolved
*Persuasive with Modification* in February 2022 by adding scoping
language and an ODH footnote to the US Core SDOH Guidance page. The
reporter has explicitly stated that FHIR-34705 is **distinct** from
FHIR-34701 — it targets the SDOH-CC IG itself rather than US Core — and
wants it discussed on its own merits. A prior disposition of *Not
Persuasive* (Bob Dieterle / Laura Heerman-Langford, 8-0-0, 2022-10-10)
was reverted by the reporter on 2022-12-15, citing that the matter was
not satisfactorily resolved in the now-published 2.0.0 STU2 release and
that the original negative ballot vote was never withdrawn. The ticket
carries `block-vote-12` and Highest priority and remains in **Triaged**
status awaiting Patient Care WG action.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the SDOH Clinical Care IG home page (or wherever the IG's "Scope" /
"Out of Scope" content lives — typically the IG's `index` / home
narrative), add a new bullet to the **Out of Scope** list reading
substantively:

> Providing standards for a person's work information. See the
> [Occupational Data for Health (ODH) implementation
> Guide](http://hl7.org/fhir/us/odh/).

Concretely this is a narrative-only addition to the SDOH-CC IG home
page in the IG's authoring source (the IG's introduction/scope
markdown/HTML page) — no profiles, value sets, extensions, examples, or
conformance artifacts change. Republish the IG with this clarification
in the next ballot/STU update so the reporter's negative ballot
position can be withdrawn.

#### Justification

The change is minimal, low-risk, and reflects the substantive consensus
reached on the parallel US Core ticket
([FHIR-34701](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-34701), Persuasive with
Modification): SDOH and ODH cover distinct use cases and should not be
conflated. The reporter is the work-group lead for the ODH IG and is
the appropriate authority on the boundary; she has stated that "the
representations in the SDOH IG and ODH work are use case specific and
do not conflict with each other," which is exactly what the proposed
text records. Adding it to the IG's own "Out of Scope" section (rather
than only to US Core, as FHIR-34701 did) is necessary because
implementers reading the SDOH-CC IG directly will not necessarily see
the US Core guidance. Accepting the request also closes a long-standing
unresolved negative ballot (`block-vote-12`) that has been outstanding
since STU2.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the intent but adopt a more comprehensive scope-boundary
treatment instead of a single bullet. Specifically:

1. Add the requested ODH bullet to the **Out of Scope** list, reusing
   the language from FHIR-34701's accepted resolution for consistency
   with US Core (e.g., note that future versions *may* reference ODH
   constructs if/when they are adopted via the relevant growth path).
2. Add a short **"Relationship to other IGs"** subsection on the IG
   home page that explicitly enumerates the adjacent IGs the SDOH-CC
   community most often confuses with SDOH-CC — at minimum ODH, US
   Core's SDOH Guidance, and (where applicable) HRSN/Gravity-derived
   profiles — with a one-line "what belongs there, not here" for each.
3. Cross-link from the ODH IG's "Related IGs" section back to SDOH-CC
   (coordinated with the ODH editors, including Amanda Deering and
   Lori Fourquet, whom the reporter asked to be CCed).

All of this remains narrative-only in the SDOH-CC IG authoring source
(IG home page); no conformance artifacts change.

#### Justification

The pattern of "person works → file under SDOH" is not unique to ODH;
implementers regularly confuse SDOH-CC with US Core's SDOH guidance and
with Gravity Project profiles as well. A single bullet addresses the
literal request but leaves the underlying confusion latent. Spelling
out the IG-to-IG boundary once — and reciprocating the link from ODH —
gives implementers a more durable answer and aligns the SDOH-CC
narrative with the language already accepted in US Core
(FHIR-34701), avoiding subtle wording divergence between the two IGs.
Trade-off vs. Disposition A: slightly more editorial work and one
cross-WG coordination touchpoint with the ODH editors, but no change
in scope or risk profile.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket Not Persuasive on the grounds that the matter has
already been substantively addressed by the resolution of
[FHIR-34701](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-34701) and that the
SDOH-CC IG's existing scope language, taken together with the published
US Core SDOH Guidance, is sufficient to communicate that detailed
work/occupational data belongs in ODH rather than SDOH-CC. Recommend
the reporter close the negative ballot on that basis.

#### Justification

This is the disposition that was previously taken (8-0-0, 2022-10-10,
Bob Dieterle / Laura Heerman-Langford) and subsequently reverted by the
reporter. It is defensible only if the WG is satisfied that the US Core
SDOH Guidance text already does the work of distinguishing SDOH from
ODH for SDOH-CC implementers as well — an assumption the reporter has
explicitly contested, noting that STU2 published without addressing
the concern and the negative ballot was never withdrawn. Choosing this
disposition again would re-open the same disagreement and would leave
`block-vote-12` unresolved; it should only be selected if the WG can
articulate a *new* reason that did not apply in 2022.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The change is a one-bullet narrative addition to the SDOH-CC IG's "Out
of Scope" section. It is exactly what the reporter has asked for, it
costs effectively nothing to make, it is consistent with the
already-published resolution on the companion US Core ticket
(FHIR-34701, *Persuasive with Modification*), and it cleanly clears a
Highest-priority `block-vote-12` negative ballot that has been
outstanding since STU2 and that was previously closed in a way the
reporter formally rejected. Disposition B is a reasonable extension if
the WG has appetite for a slightly broader cleanup of the IG's scope
narrative and is willing to coordinate with the ODH editors, but it
should not be used as a reason to delay accepting the core ask. The
WG should explicitly avoid Disposition C: re-applying the 2022 *Not
Persuasive* outcome was already shown to be contested and would simply
re-perpetuate the open ballot.
