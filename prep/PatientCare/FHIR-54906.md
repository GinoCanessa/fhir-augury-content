## Summary

This ballot comment against the **US SDOH Clinical Care (FHIR)** IG asks the
Patient Care work group to strengthen a conformance statement: the IG currently
says implementers **SHOULD** follow the **Gravity Project Data Principles**
when the final version of those principles is published. The reporter
(Celine Lefebvre) requests that this be raised to **SHALL**, making adherence
to the Gravity Project Data Principles a hard conformance requirement of the
IG rather than a recommendation. A single comment on the ticket notes that
the work group discussed the item on 2026-01-30 and is debating the
conformance language for the entire section (17.2) in which this requirement
sits, with a follow-up discussion planned. No cross-referenced Jira tickets,
Zulip threads, or GitHub items were surfaced for this ticket by FhirAugury.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the **US SDOH Clinical Care (FHIR)** IG, locate the narrative
sentence in **section 17.2** that currently reads (paraphrased from the
ticket): "Implementations SHOULD follow the Gravity Project Data
Principles when the final version is published." Change `SHOULD` to
`SHALL`, so that conformance to the Gravity Project Data Principles
becomes a hard requirement of the IG, contingent on the publication of
the final version of those principles. The change is a one-word
narrative edit to the IG page that renders section 17.2 (in the IG's
authoring repository, almost certainly `HL7/fhir-sdoh-clinicalcare`,
under its `input/pagecontent/` directory — to be confirmed against a
fresh `repo-analysis` briefing). No StructureDefinition, ValueSet,
CodeSystem, or example artifact changes are required. Because the
requirement is gated on "when the final version is published," the IG
should also keep (or add, if missing) a clarifying sentence noting that
the SHALL is conditional on the existence of a published final version
of the Gravity Project Data Principles.

#### Justification

The ticket's literal request is well-scoped and surgical: a single
conformance verb in narrative content. The Gravity Project Data
Principles are the foundational design framework for the SDOH Clinical
Care IG itself, and treating them as a recommendation rather than a
requirement weakens the IG's claim to be a faithful FHIR realization of
that framework. The comment from the 2026-01-30 work group call
indicates the WG is already weighing the conformance language for all
requirements in section 17.2, so accepting this comment aligns with the
direction of that ongoing review. Conditioning the SHALL on the final
publication of the principles preserves implementer-friendliness: no
implementer is held to a SHALL against a moving target.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep `SHOULD` as the conformance verb in section 17.2 today, but commit
in the IG to re-evaluating each requirement in section 17.2 against the
Gravity Project Data Principles **once the final version of those
principles is published**, with the explicit intent of moving the
strongest-supported requirements to `SHALL` at the next ballot cycle.
Concretely: (1) leave the current sentence as `SHOULD`; (2) add a brief
"Future direction" or "Conformance roadmap" callout in section 17.2
naming the dependency on the Gravity Project Data Principles' final
publication and the WG's intent to raise specific requirements to
`SHALL` then; (3) track the affected requirements in a single Jira
ticket so the next ballot can take them up as a coherent block, rather
than one-off `SHOULD`→`SHALL` flips.

#### Justification

The ticket itself, and the 2026-01-30 WG comment, both flag a real
problem: the IG is asserting a SHALL against a document
(`Gravity Project Data Principles`, final version) that does not yet
exist at the time of ballot. Even with a "when published" qualifier,
shipping a SHALL whose target is not yet normative creates an
unenforceable conformance claim and risks implementer confusion ("am I
non-conformant today, or only after some future date?"). A
roadmap-style alternative addresses the underlying intent — make these
requirements stronger over time — without committing to a SHALL that
can't be tested today, and gives the WG room to evaluate **all** of
section 17.2 together rather than piecemeal.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Persuasive — Resolve as **Persuasive but not Persuasive Enough** (or
**Not Persuasive**, depending on WG preference). Rationale category:
"requirement targets a not-yet-published external document; current
SHOULD is the appropriate strength until that dependency is normative."
Keep section 17.2 unchanged in this ballot cycle.

#### Justification

A `SHALL` whose binding ("when the final version is published") points
at an external, not-yet-final document is effectively unverifiable
today and risks creating a conformance gap that no implementer can
close. FHIR conformance language guidance generally reserves `SHALL`
for requirements that are testable at the time the IG is published.
The current `SHOULD` accurately conveys the WG's intent — strong
encouragement, not an enforceable rule — and avoids cascading impact
on downstream profiles, conformance test tooling, and certification
programs that consume the IG. Declining now does not preclude
strengthening the language in a future ballot once the Gravity Project
Data Principles are themselves final.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The ticket reporter and the 2026-01-30 WG discussion both point in the
same direction — the conformance language in section 17.2 deserves to
be tightened — but the literal `SHOULD`→`SHALL` change in Disposition A
binds the IG to an external document that is not yet final, which is
exactly the kind of forward-reference SHALL that creates
"unverifiable-at-publish" conformance and tends to draw negative
ballot comments of its own. Disposition C correctly identifies that
risk but does nothing with the WG's clear interest in strengthening
section 17.2. Disposition B threads the needle: it acknowledges the
reporter's intent, aligns with the WG's stated review of section 17.2
as a whole, and defers the actual `SHALL` flip until the Gravity
Project Data Principles are published and the WG can audit each
requirement against the final principles in a single coordinated pass.
The work group should treat this ticket as the trigger for that
roadmap entry rather than as a standalone one-word edit.
