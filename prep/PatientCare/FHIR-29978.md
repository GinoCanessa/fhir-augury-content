## Summary

The submitter (Daniel Vreeman) asks the PACIO Cognitive Status IG editors to substantially expand the IG narrative so readers and implementers can understand the IG's true scope. Three concrete clarifications are requested: (1) anchor the IG's domain definition to the WHO International Classification of Functioning, Disability and Health (ICF) — also forming the basis of ICD‑11 — so that "cognitive status" is defined against an internationally recognized framework; (2) reconsider the term "status," which implies a single axis of assessment, in favor of an umbrella concept of "functioning" with ICF‑aligned subdomains (e.g., mental functioning), aligning with USCDI direction; and (3) explicitly state which kinds of FHIR resources are in scope (Observations are clearly central, but the position of Conditions, Goals, Medications, Interventions, etc. is unclear) and whether the IG is bounded to CMS-required assessment instruments (MDS, OASIS, etc.). The ticket is tightly coupled to [FHIR-29977](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-29977) (proposing that the PACIO Functional Status and Cognitive Status IGs be merged); the submitter explicitly frames the "functioning" umbrella recommendation as a companion to that merge proposal. The cross-reference index returned no related Jira/Zulip/GitHub items for FHIR-29978, but the linkage to FHIR-29977 is established directly in the comment thread (and PACIO has committed to handle the title/scope question together with that merge proposal in a future cycle).

## Details

**Description:**

The narrative does not truly describe the scope of this IG. Please help readers and implementers by addressing the central issue: how does this IG define "cognitive status" and what kinds of information is it intended to cover.

Let's unpack this issue a little further.

What anchoring framework or standard terminology is used as the basis for defining the domain? Should it include assessments related to intellectual disabilities, learning disorders, or substance-induced impairments? What about the range of neuropsychological functional abilities/limitations? We recommend that both PACIO IGs anchor their labels and concept domains to the most well-established international framework for describing functioning and disability: the WHO's International Classification of Functioning, Disability and Health (ICF). The ICF framework will also be part of future ICD-11 which increases the validity of the framework and facilitates a structure that can be used globally. Such a framework will be invaluable when trying to represent content from complex domains.

Further, "status" implies a singular axis of assessment. From both the ICF and the CMS-required assessment instruments, it is clear that several dimensions of functioning can be described (current performance, capability, goals, etc) and are related to both physiological, personal, and environmental factors. "Status" does not adequately capture this breadth. Rather, we propose (in conjunction with our recommendation to merge the IGs) that a simpler umbrella term of "functioning" be applied and that subdomain areas be identified as needed using the ICF's conceptual framework. For example, mental functioning. This is consistent with the current proposals in the USCDI.

Last, the scope of both IGs should be clarified with respect to the kinds of data (e.g. Resource types) that have been modeled. Rightly so, there has been heavy focus on Observations. Is it intended to only cover content contained in the CMS-required assessment instruments? Further, it is not clear whether representation of Conditions (diagnoses), Goals, Medications, Interventions, etc are in scope (now or in the future).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Expand the IG's narrative pages (Home / Background / Scope) of the *US PACIO Cognitive Status* IG (and, in coordination with FHIR‑29977, the *US PACIO Functional Status* IG) so that they explicitly:

1. **Define the domain** by anchoring it to the WHO International Classification of Functioning, Disability and Health (ICF), naming ICF as the conceptual framework, and noting alignment with the forthcoming ICD‑11 functioning chapter.
2. **Re-frame the umbrella concept** from "cognitive status" to "mental functioning" as an ICF subdomain of an overall *functioning* family, and add narrative explaining that "status" was intentionally retained for STU1 backwards compatibility but is understood as one facet of a broader functioning model. (If FHIR‑29977 is also accepted, replace the IG title accordingly.)
3. **State scope inclusions/exclusions explicitly**: list the in‑scope CMS assessment instruments (MDS, OASIS, IRF‑PAI, etc.) and call out which FHIR resource types are currently modeled (Observation today; Condition, Goal, MedicationStatement, Procedure, etc., as out‑of‑scope-for-now-but-planned), with a forward‑looking section flagging memory, orientation, attention, and mood functions as planned future content.
4. **Add a glossary entry / definition box** for "cognitive status" so implementers and reviewers can answer "is *X* in scope?" directly from the IG.

This disposition does not change conformance — it is purely narrative and definitional — and aligns with the partial work PACIO already reported in the 2021-06-28 vote comment.

#### Justification

The reporter's underlying problem is implementer confusion (see the *implementers > Cognitive and Functional Status* Zulip thread, where an implementer could not tell from the IG which resource was correct), not a technical defect in profiles. Narrative expansion is the lowest-risk, highest-leverage fix and is directly addressable by the WG. Anchoring to ICF is consistent with WHO/ICD‑11 direction and with USCDI's evolving functioning content, so it lowers the risk of future re-work. PACIO's own previous disposition (vote 6‑0‑0) already moved in this direction, so accepting now codifies and finishes that work.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the spirit of the request but **scope the work tightly to a "Scope and Definitions" section** (rather than a sweeping narrative rewrite) and **defer the title/umbrella-term change** to be jointly resolved with [FHIR-29977](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-29977). Concretely:

1. Add a single new IG page — *Scope and Definitions* — that (a) provides a one‑paragraph definition of "cognitive status" as used in this IG, (b) cites ICF as the informative reference framework without requiring conformance to ICF, (c) enumerates in‑scope assessment instruments and resource types, and explicitly lists Conditions/Goals/Medications/Interventions as **out of scope for the current release**.
2. Leave the IG title and the term "status" unchanged for STU1.x, and create a tracker note that the umbrella renaming ("functioning" vs. "status") will be revisited only if FHIR‑29977 (the merge) is accepted, since renaming one IG without the other will increase, not decrease, implementer confusion.
3. Add a "Future Considerations" callout listing the ICF subdomains (memory, orientation, attention, mood, etc.) PACIO intends to incorporate.

#### Justification

The full rename / umbrella-term reframe is only coherent if FHIR‑29977 is also accepted (PACIO's own 2021-11-04 comment makes this explicit). Acting on the title/term portion of FHIR‑29978 in isolation risks producing a Cognitive Status IG that is partially renamed while the Functional Status IG retains the old framing, worsening the very inconsistency the reporter complained about. A bounded "Scope and Definitions" page captures the vast majority of the implementer benefit (clear domain definition, clear in/out-of-scope statement) at a fraction of the editorial cost and keeps optionality open until the merge question is resolved. ICF is cited informatively rather than normatively to avoid binding implementers to a framework they may not yet adopt.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark FHIR‑29978 as *Not Persuasive* on the grounds that (a) PACIO's STU1 publication already added a working definition of "cognitive status" and ICF-aligned framing in response to other ballot comments (as recorded in Lynn Laakso's 2021-11-04 comment), and (b) the remaining items the ticket asks for — renaming "status" to "functioning" and merging the two IGs' scope statements — are properly the subject of [FHIR-29977](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-29977) and should be tracked there rather than duplicated here.

#### Justification

The most defensible decline rationale is *already addressed / duplicate of a more focused ticket*. The STU1 publication did pick up the ICF framing and a definition (per PACIO's own disposition notes), so the highest-priority concrete request in FHIR‑29978 has been at least partially delivered. The remaining concerns — title, umbrella term, scope of resource types — overlap heavily with FHIR‑29977 and risk being adjudicated twice. Closing FHIR‑29978 keeps the ticket queue clean and forces the substantive scope/naming discussion to happen in one place.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B threads the needle. The reporter's strongest, most actionable point — that an implementer reading the IG cannot tell what is in scope — is real (the *implementers > Cognitive and Functional Status* Zulip thread is direct evidence), and PACIO can fix it with a bounded narrative addition (a Scope and Definitions page plus a Future Considerations callout) that does not require touching profiles or breaking STU1 implementations. Disposition A is attractive but couples this ticket too tightly to the umbrella-term rename, which is structurally dependent on the FHIR‑29977 merge decision and on the still-maturing Gravity/PACIO mechanism for run-time value-set selection that PACIO cited in its 29977 disposition. Acting on the rename now, in isolation, would introduce exactly the cross-IG inconsistency the reporter is complaining about. Disposition C understates the work still left: although the STU1 narrative did absorb an ICF-flavored definition, the IG still does not enumerate which resource types and assessment instruments are in scope, which is the implementer pain point. Recommend that the WG accept B for FHIR‑29978 and explicitly link it to FHIR‑29977 so the title/umbrella decision is taken once, jointly, in the next cycle.
