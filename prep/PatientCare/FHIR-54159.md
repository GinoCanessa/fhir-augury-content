## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested — Populate the Section

#### Proposal

Patient Care identifies the resource or topic page that owns section
9.5.5 in the current build of FHIR Core, and authors substantive prose
for the **"Open Issues"** heading describing the genuinely outstanding
design questions for that page (e.g., known modelling debates, deferred
items, or items the work group is actively tracking for the next
ballot). The change is made in `source/<resource-or-page>/…` (or the
top-level `source/<page>.html` if it is a specification-level page) in
`HL7/fhir`, replacing the empty heading with one or more paragraphs /
bullets. No StructureDefinition, ValueSet, or normative constraint is
touched; this is a pure narrative edit.

#### Justification

"Open Issues" sections only serve readers when they actually enumerate
issues. Filling the section preserves the page structure that downstream
readers and translators may already rely on (anchors, table-of-contents
numbering) while delivering on the heading's promise. Because the
section is non-normative (per Corey Spears' triage note), populating it
carries no compatibility risk and aligns with how other Patient Care
resource intro pages handle the same heading.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — Remove the Empty Section

#### Proposal

Delete the `<h2>`/`<h3>` heading and any surrounding empty container for
section 9.5.5 in the relevant `source/` page within `HL7/fhir`, and
remove any in-page anchor (e.g., `<a name="…">`) that the heading
exposes so no inbound link is left dangling. Re-run the publisher
locally to confirm section numbering downstream of 9.5 still resolves
and that no other page links to the removed anchor (a repo-wide
`grep` for the section's anchor name should be run before merging, as
the FHIR Core briefing flags for similar narrative deletions).

#### Justification

If the work group has no outstanding issues to record for this page,
keeping an empty heading is worse than removing it: it implies content
that does not exist and leaves the impression of unfinished
documentation. Removal is the cheapest, lowest-risk option and matches
the second half of Eric's own proposal ("propose fill in section or
remove"). The trade-off versus Disposition A is that any reader
expecting a stable anchor at section 9.5.5 will no longer find it; this
is mitigated by the anchor sweep before merge.

---

### Disposition C: Decline — Not Persuasive

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Not Persuasive** on the grounds that an empty
"Open Issues" section is a known idiom in the FHIR Core authoring
templates and is intentionally left in place so future ballot cycles
can drop content in without restructuring the page.

#### Justification

This is the weakest of the three options. It relies on the empty
section being intentional template scaffolding, which the ticket's
triage note ("a non-normative section… request to remove or populate")
does not actually support. Declining without action also leaves the
reader-visible defect in the published spec, which the reporter has
already flagged as confusing.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B (Remove the Empty Section)

Disposition B is the cleanest and most defensible response given the
information actually on the ticket. The reporter explicitly offered
"fill in or remove" as equivalent acceptable outcomes, and the triage
comment confirms the section is non-normative — meaning removal carries
no semantic risk. Disposition A is preferable *only if* Patient Care
already has a list of open issues it wants to publish for that page;
absent that list, asking the work group to invent content to justify
keeping a heading inverts the usual editorial direction. Disposition C
is hard to justify because the ticket's own triage note treats the
empty section as a defect rather than a deliberate template artefact.
The work group can override toward A if, in discussion, members surface
genuine open issues that belong on the page; otherwise, B should be the
default action.
