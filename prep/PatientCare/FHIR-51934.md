## Summary

The submitter requests that the IPS Social History profiles for Alcohol Use
and Tobacco Use fix `Observation.category` to the `social-history` code from
the standard FHIR `observation-category` value set
(`http://hl7.org/fhir/R4/valueset-observation-category.html`). Today these
profiles do not constrain `Observation.category` to that code, even though
the underlying observations are conceptually social-history observations.

No other Jira tickets are cross-referenced from the index, but the request
is closely related to a long-running IPS Zulip discussion ("Observation
Results" in the IPS stream, Nov 2023 → Feb 2024) about validator errors of
the form *"Element matches more than one slice — observation-tobacco-use,
observation-results"* in the IPS Bundle. Implementers (Peter Jordan,
Richard Langford, Gabriel Leonhartsberger, Kari Heinonen) all hit the same
ambiguous-slice problem; Rob Hausam acknowledged the underlying cause is
that the Alcohol/Tobacco profiles do not fix `category` (and that the more
general Results profile is too loosely constrained), and that this needed
to be cleaned up as part of IPS 2.0. The ticket was reviewed on the
8/27/2025 IPS call and explicitly deferred to *after* the 2.0 publication.

## Related Zulip Discussions

### [IPS > Observation Results](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/IPS/topic/Observation%20Results/near/402396910)

Peter Jordan opened the thread (Nov 2023) noting that the IPS Alcohol and
Tobacco Use Observation profiles do not mandate `Observation.category`
(`social-history`) or `Observation.code`, which causes implementers to hit
*"Element matches more than one slice — observation-tobacco-use,
observation-results"* validator errors against the IPS Bundle profile
(reproduced by Grahame Grieve). Rob Hausam confirmed the issue is real and
intentional to address as part of the IPS 2.0 ballot cleanup: the Results
profile is the parent of the more specific lab/pathology/radiology
profiles, but because it (and the social-history profiles) do not
sufficiently fix `category`, slicing matches too broadly. Implementers
(Richard Langford, Gabriel Leonhartsberger, Kari Heinonen) reported the
same problem and worked around it by re-pointing tobacco observations at
the Results profile or by deviating from the published IPS profile until
the spec is fixed. Rob also raised the possibility of an IPS technical
correction in a separate topic. Consensus in the thread: the profiles
should be tightened (including fixing `category`), but it must wait for an
IPS update.

*Participants:* Peter Jordan, Grahame Grieve, Rob Hausam, Richard
Langford, Kari Heinonen, Gabriel Leonhartsberger.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update `Observation-alcoholuse-uv-ips` and `Observation-tobaccouse-uv-ips`
in the IPS IG to add a fixed pattern on `Observation.category` of
`http://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/observation-category#social-history`
(value `social-history`). Concretely, in each profile add a slice on
`Observation.category` (slice by `coding`, discriminator `pattern` on
`coding`) with `min = 1` and `patternCodeableConcept` fixing the
`social-history` coding from the standard `observation-category`
CodeSystem. Keep this aligned with the FHIR core `social-history` Category
example pattern. Schedule the change for the IPS release that follows
2.0 publication, consistent with the workgroup's deferral.

#### Justification

Fixing `Observation.category` to `social-history` is the standard FHIR
pattern for social-history observations (it is exactly what the FHIR core
"Social History" Observation example/profile does), and it is the most
direct fix for the long-standing implementer pain documented on the IPS
Zulip "Observation Results" thread, where the absence of a category
constraint contributes to "Element matches more than one slice" errors
between the Tobacco/Alcohol profiles and the more general Results
profile. It also makes the profiles self-describing: a recipient seeing
the bare resource can recognise it as a social-history observation
without consulting the IPS Bundle slicing context. The change is low
risk because IPS instances that already follow the FHIR convention will
continue to validate; only instances that omit `category` (which were
arguably non-conformant in spirit) will need to be updated.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than fixing `Observation.category` only on the Alcohol and Tobacco
profiles, take the broader cleanup that Rob Hausam outlined on Zulip:
(1) make `Observation.category` `min = 1` with a fixed/pattern
`social-history` coding on **both** `Observation-alcoholuse-uv-ips` and
`Observation-tobaccouse-uv-ips`, and *additionally* tighten the slicing
discriminator on the IPS Bundle/Composition Results section so that
social-history observations cannot also match the `observation-results`
slice. This could be done by changing the bundle/composition slicing
discriminator to include `category` (in addition to `code`/`profile`) so
that the validator can unambiguously distinguish social-history entries
from laboratory/pathology/radiology results.

#### Justification

This addresses the underlying defect that implementers actually report
(ambiguous slicing in the IPS Bundle), not just the surface request. It
keeps the spirit of the requester's ask (fixed `social-history` category)
while also fixing the consequent validator error highlighted by Peter
Jordan, Richard Langford, Gabriel Leonhartsberger and Kari Heinonen, and
acknowledged by Rob Hausam as in-scope for the IPS 2.0 cleanup. The
trade-off vs. Disposition A is scope: it touches the Bundle/Composition
slicing as well as the two profiles, requires more careful regression
testing of existing IPS examples, and is a slightly larger compatibility
event for implementers — but it delivers a coherent fix instead of
leaving the slicing problem partially addressed.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Do not make the change. Treat the existing profiles as sufficient on the
basis that `Observation.category` for these profiles is already
implicitly conveyed by the `Observation.code` (LOINC tobacco/alcohol use
codes) and by the IPS Bundle context (the social-history Composition
section), and that implementers can supply `social-history` themselves
without it being a fixed value.

#### Justification

This is the weakest option but is included for completeness. It is
defensible only on a strict "minimise change in IPS 2.0" reading: the
profiles as written have shipped for several IPS releases, and any change
that flips a previously-valid optional element to a fixed value is a
breaking change for instances that supplied a different (or no)
`category`. However, the Zulip evidence shows that this position leaves
implementers with real, recurring validator errors in the IPS Bundle and
forces non-conformant workarounds, which is why even the workgroup
accepted that the issue should be revisited rather than closed —
declining outright would contradict that position.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is recommended because the IPS Zulip "Observation Results"
thread makes clear that the missing `social-history` category is only
half of the real problem — the other half is the slicing in the IPS
Bundle/Composition that makes Tobacco/Alcohol observations match the
generic Results slice. Rob Hausam explicitly framed the post-2.0 cleanup
as covering both, and implementers (Jordan, Langford, Leonhartsberger,
Heinonen) have been working around the slicing defect for over a year.
Doing only Disposition A would satisfy the literal text of FHIR-51934
but would still leave the validator errors that motivated the Zulip
discussion in place, so a small amount of additional scope on the Bundle
slicing produces a much better outcome for implementers. The workgroup
has already deferred this work to after IPS 2.0 publication, which is
the right vehicle for the slightly broader change Disposition B
requires.
