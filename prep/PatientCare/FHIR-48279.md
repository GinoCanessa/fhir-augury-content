## Summary

Steve Eichner, writing against the US PACIO Personal Functioning and
Engagement (PFE) FHIR IG, reports that the patient-facing language
throughout the guide is framed predominantly around limitations and
constraints — what a patient *cannot* do — and asks that the IG also
recognize successes, achievements, and positive aspects of a patient's
functioning and engagement. The request is editorial/narrative in
nature rather than a structural change to resources, profiles, or
value sets: it targets the wording and framing used in the IG's
descriptive text and, potentially, in code and value-set displays used
to capture observations. No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip
threads, or GitHub items were surfaced by the tooling, so the
workgroup will need to establish scope (narrative-only vs. content
model) during disposition.

## Details

**Description:**

> The patient-focused language of the zig is generally negative,

## Proposed Dispositions

Because no GitHub repo is linked in the tooling, the dispositions
below name the PACIO PFE IG by specification identifier rather than by
repo path. If the workgroup chooses to act, the actual edits would
land in the PACIO PFE IG source repository; a follow-up
`repo-analysis` run against that repo will be needed before committing
to specific file paths.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Perform an editorial pass across the PACIO Personal Functioning and
Engagement IG narrative to rebalance language so that both
*limitations/constraints* and *achievements/strengths* are represented.
Concretely:

1. Update the IG's Home, Background, and "Functioning and Engagement"
   conceptual pages to explicitly frame scope as capturing both
   challenges and successes (e.g., "what a person can do as well as
   what is difficult").
2. Audit the narrative descriptions of each profile (PFE Observation
   profiles for Function, Engagement, Mobility, etc.) and rewrite
   any text that describes them exclusively in deficit terms so they
   describe capability and achievement as first-class use cases.
3. Where code-system or value-set displays / definitions carry
   deficit-only phrasing (e.g., "difficulty with…"), add paired or
   neutral definitions so that positive/neutral observations are
   representable without semantic distortion.
4. Add at least one worked example Observation showing a
   positive/achievement-oriented finding.

#### Justification

The request is consistent with strengths-based / function-oriented
frameworks such as the WHO ICF, which explicitly models both
*activity* and *activity limitation*, *participation* and
*participation restriction*. A PACIO IG that only narrates the
limitation side is inconsistent with that model and with the broader
"whole-person" goals PACIO states. Because the change is primarily
narrative plus a modest terminology audit, it is low-risk from a
conformance standpoint — existing profiles and servers remain valid —
and it materially improves the IG for implementers who need to record
rehabilitation progress, functional gains, and patient-reported
achievements.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than a broad editorial sweep, scope the change to two focused
deliverables:

1. Add a new IG narrative section titled "Representing Strengths and
   Achievements" that documents the recommended pattern for recording
   positive findings using the *existing* PFE Observation profiles
   (e.g., `Observation.interpretation` = `POS` / improvement-oriented
   SNOMED concepts, `Observation.value[x]` on capability-oriented
   scales, and guidance on when to use `dataAbsentReason` vs. an
   explicit positive finding).
2. Leave profile definitions, value sets, and existing narrative
   unchanged except for cross-links pointing to the new section.

#### Justification

This narrows the blast radius: no existing text is rewritten, no
value sets are touched, and implementers who have already built
against the published IG see no conformance drift. It also treats
the ticket as what it most likely *is* in practice — a gap in
guidance, not a defect in the content model — and gives the
workgroup a concrete, boundable unit of work that can ship in a
point release. Trade-off vs. Disposition A: the deficit-framed
wording in existing profile descriptions and examples remains in
place, which Steve Eichner's report arguably targets directly.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline as *out of scope for the content model*, on the grounds that
the PACIO PFE IG's Observation profiles are already neutral with
respect to polarity (they carry a measured value and an
interpretation) and therefore can represent both limitations and
achievements today. Close with a note directing implementers to use
`Observation.interpretation` and positively-valenced codes from the
bound value sets to record achievements, and defer any narrative
rebalancing to the next IG publication cycle.

#### Justification

If the ticket is read strictly as "the resources don't support
positive findings", the decline is defensible: FHIR `Observation` is
polarity-neutral and PACIO's bindings do not preclude positive codes.
However, this reading ignores the reporter's stated concern about
*language/framing*, which is a real implementer-experience issue even
when the content model is technically sufficient. Declining is the
weakest option here and should only be chosen if the workgroup
concludes the IG is frozen for the current ballot cycle and no
editorial change is permissible.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the recommended starting point. The ticket is
"Highest" priority and comes from a named, credible reporter, which
argues against Disposition C; but Disposition A commits the workgroup
to an open-ended editorial sweep across an entire IG — work that is
easy to start and hard to bound, and that risks re-opening settled
wording late in a publication cycle. A focused "Representing
Strengths and Achievements" section (B) directly answers the
reporter's concern, can be drafted and reviewed in a single cycle,
and is explicitly extensible: if the workgroup later decides a full
rewrite is warranted, the new section becomes the style guide that
drives it. Implementers get concrete guidance now, without the IG
churning under them. If the workgroup is willing to fund the fuller
editorial pass, escalate to Disposition A using the new section as
the pattern.
