## Summary

The reporter proposes a single-word terminology change to the AI
Transparency on FHIR IG (`hl7.fhir.uv.aitransparency`, 2026-Jan ballot).
The Scope statement currently reads: *"…thus enabling transparency for
**users** of the data to determine the relevance, validity, applicability,
and suitability of the data."* The reporter argues that "users" is
ambiguous about the role being played and suggests replacing it with
**"consumers"** (i.e., parties who consume / read the data, as opposed
to the broader, role-agnostic "users" who may also produce or curate
it). The change is purely editorial / definitional and does not touch
any FSH artifact, profile, value set, or example. The ticket is filed
as a Technical Correction at Highest priority and was triaged as part
of the Jan-2026 ballot intake (it appears in the EHR WG triage table
posted by John Moehrke on the AI Transparency on FHIR Zulip stream
under the "Documentation & Clarity" bucket — 31 issues, of which this
is one). No cross-referenced Jira tickets were surfaced, and no Zulip
discussion has occurred beyond the triage listing.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Replace the single occurrence of "users" with "consumers" in
`input/pagecontent/index.md`, line 10, inside the `### Scope` section
of the AI Transparency on FHIR IG. The corrected sentence reads:

> "The purpose of this project is to enable observability for the use
> of AI algorithms in the production or manipulation of health data,
> thus enabling transparency for **consumers** of the data to determine
> the relevance, validity, applicability, and suitability of the data."

No other file is changed. No `sushi-config.yaml`, FSH, value-set, or
example update is required. No anchor changes (the heading `### Scope`
is untouched). Mark the ticket *Persuasive* / *Resolution: Persuasive*
and resolve via a one-line PR against `HL7/aitransparency-ig`.

#### Justification

The ticket is filed as a Technical Correction and the request is
strictly editorial. "Consumer of data" is the precise role being
described in the surrounding sentence — a party who reads the data and
must judge its relevance, validity, applicability, and suitability.
"User of data" is broader (it can include producers, curators,
algorithm operators, governance roles), which conflicts with the rest
of the IG narrative where "user" sometimes refers to clinicians or
operators of AI systems rather than downstream readers. The repo
briefing confirms the change is mechanically safe (one file, no
anchors broken, no generated artefacts affected, no cross-repo
dependency touched), and the EHR WG triage already classified this as
"Documentation & Clarity" — the bucket in which 31 of the IG's Jan-26
ballot items live and which is being processed as straightforward
editorial corrections.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than substituting a single word, replace "users of the data"
with a more explicitly disambiguated phrase that matches terminology
used elsewhere in the IG and in HL7 generally — for example
**"downstream consumers and interpreters of the data"** or **"data
recipients (clinicians, decision-support systems, and other downstream
consumers)"**. Apply the change in the same location
(`input/pagecontent/index.md`, line 10, `### Scope`). At the same
time, audit the rest of `index.md`, `general_guidance.md`,
`use_cases.md`, and `explainer.md` for other occurrences of "user" /
"users" that suffer from the same ambiguity (operator-vs-reader) and
flag/fix them in the same PR for narrative consistency.

#### Justification

The reporter's underlying concern is role ambiguity, not the specific
word "consumers". A one-word swap fixes the cited sentence but leaves
the rest of the IG with the same ambiguity wherever "user" recurs —
and "consumer" alone may itself be over-loaded (some readers will
hear "patient/consumer" in the consumer-mediated-exchange sense). A
slightly fuller phrase explicitly names the role (downstream reader
of AI-influenced data) and removes the ambiguity at the source. The
repo briefing shows narrative edits in the IG are low-cost and
self-contained, so the marginal effort of a coordinated narrative
sweep is small relative to the editorial benefit. Trade-off vs A:
slightly more reviewer time on the PR, and a small risk of scope
creep beyond what FHIR-54158 literally asks for; mitigate by limiting
the sweep to the four narrative pages and listing each touched
sentence in the PR description.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket *Not Persuasive*. Leave `input/pagecontent/index.md`
unchanged. Rationale: "users" in the Scope sentence is already a
common, well-understood English term for "people who make use of the
data", the surrounding context (relevance, validity, applicability,
suitability) makes the role clear, and the IG does not formally
define either "user" or "consumer" elsewhere — so swapping the word
adds no precision and risks importing other connotations of "consumer"
(e.g., patient-as-consumer in CMS / consumer-mediated-exchange usage)
that the IG does not intend.

#### Justification

Defensible if the WG concludes the ambiguity is hypothetical rather
than observed in implementer feedback. There is no Zulip discussion,
no implementer report, and no cross-referenced ticket asserting that
the current wording has caused an actual misunderstanding. Declining
keeps the ballot text stable, avoids risk of introducing a different
ambiguity, and is consistent with the WG's bar for editorial-only
corrections to ballot text under time pressure.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **A**

The change is the textbook case for *Persuasive*: a one-word, one-line
narrative edit in a single file, with no anchor / link / FSH /
value-set side effects per the repo briefing, addressing a real
ambiguity in a Scope statement that the IG explicitly relies on to
frame its purpose. Disposition B has merit and the WG may want to
keep its broader audit in mind for a future ticket, but bundling a
multi-page terminology sweep into a Technical Correction risks
expanding the Jan-2026 ballot footprint beyond what was filed and
makes resolution harder to track. Disposition C is hard to defend in
the absence of any countervailing argument on Zulip or in the ticket
comments — the reporter's distinction (reader-of-data vs
operator-of-AI) is a real one and "consumers" tightens the sentence
without changing meaning. Recommend the WG accept A and, if there is
appetite, file a separate follow-up ticket for the broader narrative
audit described in B.
