## Summary

[FHIR-56340](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-56340) asks the AI Transparency
IG (work group: Electronic Health Record) to treat "PDF interpreted by AI into
FHIR" as a proper use-case rather than as guidance content sitting inside the
General Guidance page, and to ensure the supporting examples are compliant
with the IG's own profiles. The reporter (John Moehrke) requests three
coordinated edits: (1) relocate the PDF-importing material out of generic
guidance and into the IG's use-case content, (2) revise the surrounding wording
so it reads as a use-case description (scenario, actors, flow) rather than as
normative guidance, and (3) correct the accompanying example instances so they
validate against the IG's Provenance profile and Tagging conventions. The
ticket has already been triaged with a resolution of "Persuasive with
Modification", so the disposition under review is essentially a confirmation
or refinement of the planned move-and-fix work, not a question of whether to
act. There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub
items in the indexed corpus to broaden the picture.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Implement the resolution exactly as written in the AI Transparency IG
(`HL7/aitransparency-ig`):

1. In `input/pagecontent/general_guidance.md`, remove the `### PDF
   interpreted by AI into FHIR` block (and any subordinate `####`
   subsections specific to PDF ingestion). Leave behind a one-sentence
   pointer that links to the new use-case location.
2. In `input/pagecontent/use_cases.md`, add a `## PDF interpreted by AI
   into FHIR` use-case with the standard use-case framing — actors
   (source EHR / document repository, AI ingestion service, target FHIR
   server), preconditions, narrative flow, and expected `Provenance` /
   Tagging output — reusing the prose from the old guidance section but
   rewritten in use-case voice.
3. Update the example instances cited from that use-case
   (`input/fsh/EX_Doc_ingest.fsh` and any related `EX_Provenance.fsh` /
   `EX_Tagging.fsh` snippets) so each one validates against
   `SD_Provenance.fsh` and uses the canonical Tagging codes (e.g.,
   `AIAST`, `DICTAST`) per the IG's Tagging guidance.
4. Audit and repoint any `general_guidance.html#pdf-…` anchors in
   `index.md`, `explainer.md`, the menu in `sushi-config.yaml`, and any
   other narrative pages.
5. If IG-publisher emits any new anchor or example-validation warnings,
   fix them at the source; only append to `input/ignoreWarnings.txt` if
   the warning is intentional and harmless.

#### Justification

This is the literal action the resolution committed to ("will move the
pdf importing to a use-case, proper wording, and correct examples to be
compliant"). It cleanly separates *guidance* (general rules that apply
across the IG) from *use-cases* (concrete scenarios that motivate the
profiles), which is the structural intent of the IG's existing
`general_guidance.md` / `use_cases.md` split. Making the examples
profile-compliant is a precondition for trust-marking the IG as ballot-
ready (`version: 1.0.0-ballot`, FMM 2, trial-use): non-compliant
examples in a ballot draft are routinely cited as blocking comments.
The change is contained to one repo (`HL7/aitransparency-ig`), one
authoring root (`input/`), and a small file set called out in the
briefing's *Ticket-Relevant Paths* — low blast radius.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the PDF content where it is in `general_guidance.md` but split it
into two cross-linked halves: a *short, normative-flavoured "PDF
ingest" subsection in General Guidance* (one or two paragraphs covering
the rules: what `Provenance` activity codes to use, what Tagging codes
apply, what `Resource.meta.security` labels are required), plus a
*new "PDF interpreted by AI into FHIR" entry in `use_cases.md`* that
illustrates those rules end-to-end with the corrected examples. The
example fixes (Disposition A item 3) are still made, and anchor
audits (item 4) are still performed, but the General Guidance page
retains a hook for readers who arrive looking for the rule rather than
the scenario.

#### Justification

The ticket frames this as a binary "move it" decision, but the source
material plausibly serves two different reader needs: (a) "what do I
have to do to be conformant when I ingest a PDF?" — guidance — and
(b) "show me an end-to-end example of doing that" — use-case. Splitting
preserves discoverability for readers entering from the rules side
while still satisfying the underlying ask (use-case framing for the
worked example, profile-compliant examples). The trade-off vs.
Disposition A is mild duplication of intent across two pages and a
slightly larger anchor-audit (now two anchors instead of one), set
against a cleaner mental model for implementers. This option also
avoids the in-page anchor breakage that Disposition A's outright
removal causes — the existing `#pdf-…` anchor in `general_guidance.md`
can survive, just with thinner content behind it.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline to act on this ticket as a separate change. Treat the existing
`### PDF interpreted by AI into FHIR` section in `general_guidance.md`
as adequate for STU1 ballot, and limit work to the narrow example-
correctness fix (i.e., make `EX_Doc_ingest.fsh` and related instances
validate against `SD_Provenance.fsh`), without restructuring narrative
or moving content between pages. Mark the wording / use-case-shape
concern as out-of-scope for this ballot cycle and revisit post-ballot
based on reviewer comments.

#### Justification

The structural concern (guidance-vs-use-case framing) is a presentation
preference, not a conformance defect. The IG is already at
`1.0.0-ballot` / STU1, and reviewer churn during a ballot window has a
real cost (anchor breakage across `index.md`, the menu, and external
links into the published IG; potential new IG-publisher warnings; risk
of further restructuring requests in the same area). The
example-compliance defect is the only piece with concrete downstream
impact (implementers copying broken examples), and it can be fixed
without touching page structure. Declining defers the larger edit
until ballot feedback has actually arrived, at which point the move
can be done once with the benefit of reviewer signal rather than
twice. Note: this contradicts the recorded resolution
("Persuasive with Modification"), so it is offered only as a defensible
counter-position for the workgroup to weigh, not as the expected path.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is already resolved *Persuasive with Modification*, the
reporter is the work-group chair-style author John Moehrke, and the
resolution language is unambiguous about what should happen ("move…
proper wording… correct examples to be compliant"). Disposition A
executes that decision directly in the one obviously affected repo
(`HL7/aitransparency-ig`) using the recipes the briefing already
endorses (*Edit narrative*), with the example-compliance fix carried
along. Disposition B is a sensible nuance but adds duplication and a
second anchor surface for marginal benefit; if a reviewer raises that
specific concern during ballot, the General-Guidance hook can be
re-introduced cheaply on top of A. Disposition C is hard to defend
given the recorded resolution and the fact that the example-
compliance issue alone justifies opening the same file for edit
anyway. The work is contained, well-scoped per the briefing's
*Ticket-Relevant Paths*, and the only real execution risk is the
in-page anchor breakage called out in *Gotchas* — which the
disposition explicitly addresses.
