## Summary

The reporter requests that the AI Transparency (FHIR) IG add a worked Prior
Authorization (PA) use case. The motivating concern is the rapidly expanding
use of AI in PA workflows — particularly that AI should not be used to deny
care or coverage without a human reviewer in the loop, an issue that is
receiving significant industry and state-legislative attention. The ticket
asks the IG to flesh out PA as a featured use case so that the IG speaks to
this high-profile area. No related Jira tickets were surfaced via
cross-references; the only cross-reference is a single Zulip triage post
([AI Transparency on FHIR > IG - Jan ballot](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/AI%20Transparency%20on%20FHIR/topic/IG%20-%20Jan%20ballot/near/570161461))
that bins this ticket under "Use Cases & Examples" alongside several other
use-case requests for the January ballot (e.g., FHIR-54644, FHIR-54642,
FHIR-54731, FHIR-54706). The ticket has already been triaged with a
proposed disposition of **Not Persuasive**, with the disposition rationale
being that the IG is scoped to *tracking actual AI use* rather than defining
appropriate vs. inappropriate uses or required workflows.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a new "Prior Authorization" subsection to the Use Cases page of the AI
Transparency (FHIR) IG (`uv/aitransparency`). The subsection would describe
a worked PA scenario in which a payer applies an AI/ML model as part of an
authorization decision and uses the IG's mechanisms (provenance tagging,
AI-use indicators on the decision artifact) to record that AI participated
in the determination, including which model/version and the human reviewer
(when present). The narrative would frame PA as an exemplar high-volume,
high-scrutiny use case and cross-link to relevant payer IGs (e.g., Da Vinci
PAS / CRD / DTR) without making any normative statements about whether AI
*should* drive denials — only documenting how its use is tracked when it
occurs.

#### Justification

The follow-up commenter is correct that PA is, in fact, an actual AI use
case in widespread production today, and the IG's scope ("tracking actual
use") arguably supports including it. A worked example anchored in a
high-visibility industry workflow strengthens implementer guidance and
demonstrates how the IG's transparency mechanisms apply outside clinical
decision support. The reporter's normative concerns (no AI-only denials,
human-in-the-loop) can be acknowledged contextually without the IG itself
issuing rules — the use case can simply *show* how human reviewer
involvement is captured in Provenance.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Do not add PA as a standalone use case in this IG, but add a brief
**non-normative cross-reference** in the Use Cases / Background page noting
that AI is increasingly used in payer workflows (including Prior
Authorization) and that this IG's transparency mechanisms apply to such
workflows in the same way they apply to clinical use cases. Point readers
to the Da Vinci PA family of IGs for the workflow specifics, and reiterate
that this IG is mechanism-only (it tracks AI use, it does not regulate it).
Optionally file a companion ticket against the relevant Da Vinci PA IG to
incorporate AI Transparency profiles into PA exchanges.

#### Justification

This approach honors the work group's stated scope discipline (the IG is
deliberately not a policy document about appropriate AI use) while still
acknowledging the reporter's substantive point that PA is a real and
prominent AI use case. It avoids the risk of the IG being read as endorsing
or regulating AI in PA — which is the implicit concern behind the
"Not Persuasive" resolution — but stops short of dismissing the topic
entirely. It also keeps PA workflow specifics in the IGs that own them,
where they can be maintained correctly.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (current proposed disposition: Not Persuasive)

#### Proposal

Maintain the current disposition: **Not Persuasive**. Do not add a Prior
Authorization use case to the AI Transparency (FHIR) IG. Reaffirm in the
disposition rationale that the IG's scope is limited to *tracking actual
AI use* via Provenance and tagging mechanisms, and is intentionally silent
on which AI uses are appropriate, what workflow rules apply (e.g., human
reviewer requirements), or which clinical/administrative domains warrant
worked examples. Direct the reporter to the Da Vinci PA family of IGs for
PA-specific work and to relevant policy bodies for normative AI-in-PA
guidance.

#### Justification

The literal request — "flesh out a PA use case" with a focus on "AI should
not be used for denials of care/coverage, there should always a human
reviewer" — is explicitly normative. Accepting it as written would push the
IG outside its declared scope into appropriate-use policy, which the work
group has consistently refused to do. PA workflow detail also belongs in
the Da Vinci PA IGs, which would be the better home for any AI-Transparency
integration. Declining preserves scope discipline and avoids setting a
precedent for adding workflow-specific normative content to a
mechanisms-only IG.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The reporter's underlying observation — that PA is a real, high-volume,
high-scrutiny AI use case — has merit and is corroborated by the follow-up
comment. Disposition C (the current Not Persuasive) is defensible on scope
grounds but reads as dismissive of a legitimate point and risks repeated
re-filings on the same theme. Disposition A goes too far: writing a full
worked PA use case duplicates Da Vinci PA territory and pulls the IG into
workflow detail it has avoided elsewhere. Disposition B threads the needle
— it acknowledges PA explicitly as an in-scope *example* of AI use that the
IG's mechanisms cover, redirects the reader to the Da Vinci PA IGs for the
workflow specifics, and keeps the AI Transparency IG firmly mechanism-only.
It also gives the work group a constructive disposition message to send
back to the reporter rather than a flat refusal.
