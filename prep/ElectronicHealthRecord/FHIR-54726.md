## Summary

Brian Postlethwaite is asking the AI Transparency (FHIR) IG editors to revisit
the wording of a sentence in the IG's Scope section. The current text defines
"AI algorithm" broadly as any computer-based logic that touches health data
"in a way that might **change the understanding** of the data downstream."
The reporter questions whether "change the understanding" is really the
intended framing, and proposes the alternative phrasing "in a way that might
**impact the confidence in** the data downstream." There are no other Jira
tickets cross-referenced to this issue; the only external reference is a
Zulip triage post by John Moehrke that buckets this ticket under the IG's
"Documentation & Clarity" category for the January 2026 ballot.

## Details

**Description:**

In the scope the sentance:

> *"In this project, AI algorithm is defined broadly to include any
> computer-based logic that touches health data in a way that might change
> the understanding of the data downstream"*

Is this really meant to be "**change the understanding**"?

Should it be something more along the lines of "in a way that might
**impact the confidence in** the data downstream".

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the Scope section of the AI Transparency IG so the defining sentence
reads:

> "In this project, AI algorithm is defined broadly to include any
> computer-based logic that touches health data in a way that might
> **impact the confidence in** the data downstream."

This is a pure narrative edit to the IG's Scope page (no StructureDefinition,
ValueSet, or example changes). No other places in the IG would need to
change unless the same phrase appears verbatim elsewhere (e.g., an
abstract, an introduction, or the executive summary), in which case those
should be updated for consistency.

#### Justification

The reporter's wording is more precise about what AI Transparency is
trying to achieve. The IG's broader story is about provenance, trust, and
the consumer's ability to judge the reliability of AI-influenced data;
"impact the confidence in" maps directly onto that downstream-trust
framing, whereas "change the understanding" is ambiguous (it could be
read as merely reformatting or restating the data). Adopting the
suggested phrasing aligns the scope sentence with the IG's stated
purpose and with how the WG already talks about provenance metadata.
This is a low-risk editorial change — the kind of "Documentation &
Clarity" item the triage post explicitly flags FHIR-54726 as.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than swapping one verb phrase for another, broaden the definition
to capture both intents and any other downstream effects an AI step might
have. Replace the sentence with something like:

> "In this project, AI algorithm is defined broadly to include any
> computer-based logic that touches health data in a way that may affect
> how the data is interpreted, weighted, or trusted downstream — for
> example by transforming, summarising, classifying, scoring, or
> annotating it."

Optionally add a short non-normative bullet list of representative
examples (transformation, classification, scoring/risk, summarisation,
NLP extraction) so implementers see what is and is not in scope without
having to reason from a single abstract verb.

#### Justification

Both the original wording ("change the understanding") and the proposed
wording ("impact the confidence in") describe a *single* downstream
effect. In practice, an AI step can affect downstream data in several
ways: it can alter meaning, alter the level of confidence, or simply
re-present the data in a form that is easier or harder to act on. A
single verb will inevitably under- or over-reach. Anchoring the
definition in the *kinds of effects* (interpretation, weighting, trust)
and giving illustrative examples removes the wording-quibble class of
ballot comments entirely and is more useful to implementers deciding
whether their pipeline falls inside the IG's scope.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (Persuasive)

#### Proposal

Reject the proposed wording change, on the basis that "change the
understanding of the data" is the intended framing for the AI
Transparency IG. Respond to the reporter that the IG's scope is
deliberately broader than confidence/trust: it covers any logic that
alters how downstream consumers comprehend the data, including
transformations and summaries that do not change confidence per se.
Leave the sentence as-is.

#### Justification

If the WG's authoritative position is that the IG must cover any
AI-driven *interpretive* change — not only changes that affect
trustworthiness — then "change the understanding" is the broader and
more correct phrase. "Impact the confidence in" arguably *narrows*
the scope to provenance/quality concerns and could exclude in-scope
cases such as automatic re-coding or summarisation that do not lower
(or raise) confidence but do shift how the data is understood. A
persuasive-with-mod-style response that simply explains the original
intent costs nothing implementation-wise and preserves authorial
intent.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The change is editorial, the reporter's phrasing is closer to the IG's
own provenance-and-trust narrative, and the Zulip triage already places
this ticket in the "Documentation & Clarity" bucket — exactly the class
of items the WG is expected to absorb during ballot reconciliation.
Disposition B is attractive but expands the ticket beyond what was
asked and risks pulling in re-scoping debate that this ticket was not
designed to host; if the WG agrees the definition is genuinely
under-specified, B is better tracked as a separate scope-rewrite
ticket. Disposition C is hard to defend given the IG's own framing
around trust and transparency. Accept the wording change as proposed,
sweep the IG narrative for other instances of "change the understanding
of the data downstream", and apply the same edit consistently.
