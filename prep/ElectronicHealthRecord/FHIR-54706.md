## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat this ticket as a documentation/editorial change against the AI
Transparency IG's `use-cases` narrative page. Specifically:

1. Rewrite the Use Case 2, Use Case 3, and Use Case 4 sections of the
   IG's `use-cases` page so each one consistently includes:
   actor(s), trigger/scenario, the AI artifact(s) involved, the
   transparency information that needs to surface, the FHIR
   resource(s)/profiles that carry it, and the expected
   outcome/disposition.
2. Run a copy-edit pass over Use Cases 1–4 (typos, grammar, capitalisation,
   acronym expansion on first use, internal links).
3. Fold in the concrete narrative suggestions captured in the sibling
   "Use Cases & Examples" tickets (notably FHIR-54642, FHIR-54578,
   FHIR-54577, FHIR-54278) so the rewrite is internally consistent
   across the batch rather than being done ticket-by-ticket.
4. Record this in the change log as "Persuasive with Modification" — the
   modification being that the actual edit text comes from the sibling
   tickets that did supply concrete proposals.

#### Justification

The reporter has identified a real and acknowledged problem (the WG's
own resolution agrees the use cases need improvement). The IG's
`use-cases` page is the single source of truth readers hit when trying
to understand why the IG exists, so under-specified narratives are a
disproportionately visible defect. Doing the rewrite as part of a
coordinated batch with the other Use-Cases tickets (per the Jan-ballot
triage Zulip thread) avoids contradictory edits, matches the WG's
already-recorded disposition, and keeps the change purely editorial
(no profile, value-set, or extension impact, no normative risk).

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than expanding the existing four use cases in place, restructure
the `use-cases` page around a small set of **scenario templates** plus a
table of concrete instantiations:

- Define 1–2 reusable scenario templates (e.g., "AI-assisted clinical
  decision shown to a clinician at the point of care" and "AI-derived
  data persisted into the chart"), each with a fixed set of slots
  (actors, AI artifact, transparency obligation, FHIR carrier
  resources, downstream provenance).
- Re-express Use Cases 1–4 (and any sibling-ticket additions) as filled
  instances of those templates, in a comparison table, so readers can
  see at a glance where the scenarios differ.
- Move the long prose into per-scenario subsections only where needed.

#### Justification

Several of the sibling Use-Cases tickets (per the Jan-ballot triage)
are also asking for more detail in the same area; if each is handled
independently the page risks growing into four loosely-aligned essays
that drift apart. A template-driven structure makes the difference
between scenarios explicit, makes future use-case additions cheap, and
nudges authors to fill the same slots every time — which directly
addresses the reporter's "context and additional details" concern by
making the missing context structurally visible. The trade-off is more
restructuring work than the WG's stated minimum, and it changes more of
the page than the reporter literally asked for, which is why this is
the alternative rather than the recommended path.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-54706 as a duplicate of the more specific Use-Cases tickets
(e.g., FHIR-54642, FHIR-54578, FHIR-54577, FHIR-54278) on the basis
that those tickets already carry concrete improvement proposals and
will drive the actual edits, and that a standalone "expand the use
cases" ticket without specific text becomes unactionable once the
sibling tickets are resolved.

#### Justification

The WG's own resolution note acknowledges that this ticket "did not
provide an improvement recommendation, where other tickets did" and
that the other tickets' recommendations will be followed. From a
ticket-hygiene standpoint, that makes FHIR-54706 a meta-ticket whose
substance is fully covered by the others. Declining (as duplicate /
already addressed) avoids double-counting the work and avoids leaving
an open loop where this ticket has to be re-resolved after the sibling
tickets land.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket has already been triaged by the WG as **Persuasive with
Modification** with the explicit note that the sibling Use-Cases
tickets supply the concrete edit text. Disposition A simply codifies
that: do the editorial pass on Use Cases 2–4 (plus typos), and source
the actual wording from the sibling tickets. It honours the reporter's
ask, matches the WG's recorded resolution, and keeps the change
editorial — no profile, value-set, or normative impact. Disposition B
is more ambitious and arguably better long-term, but it changes the
shape of the page beyond what the reporter requested and is better
raised as its own structural ticket once the editorial pass lands.
Disposition C is tempting on hygiene grounds but discards the
reporter's framing of the problem (the page as a whole is sparse, not
just individual scenarios) and would lose the cross-cutting
typo/copy-edit pass that none of the sibling tickets explicitly own.
