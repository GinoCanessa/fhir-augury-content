## Summary

The submitter (Ignacio Jauregui) reviewed the AI Transparency on FHIR
January 2026 ballot and questioned the role of two elements in the IG's
AI observability logical model:

- **Data quality** — described as evaluating completeness, accuracy,
  consistency, and reliability of input data.
- **Data qualification** — described as addressing validation,
  certification, and regulatory compliance status of data sources.

The reporter raises three connected concerns: (1) it is unclear what
function these elements perform in the IG model; (2) if the IG intends
to capture not only the input data passed to AI but also its quality
and qualification, the encoding (data type, value sets, cardinality)
is not specified; and (3) "data quality" in particular may sit outside
the scope of an AI transparency IG and therefore may not belong in
the model at all.

No other Jira tickets are explicitly linked. The only cross-reference
is the work-group's January-ballot triage thread on Zulip
([AI Transparency on FHIR > IG - Jan ballot](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/AI%20Transparency%20on%20FHIR/topic/IG%20-%20Jan%20ballot/near/570161461)),
in which John Moehrke groups FHIR-54235 under
**Documentation & Clarity** (one of 31 issues in that bucket among 90
total ballot issues).

## Details

**Description:**

- **Data quality**: evaluates the completeness, accuracy, consistency,
  and reliability of input data
- **Data qualification**: addresses the validation, certification, and
  regulatory compliance status of data sources

It is not clear what role those elements play in the IG model. Is the
intention to capture, not only the input data that is passed to AI but
also its quality and qualification? In that case how are they encoded?
Data quality seem to beyond the scope of this IG.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested (Persuasive — Clarify model intent and encoding)

#### Proposal

Treat this as a Documentation & Clarity ticket and resolve it with two
coordinated edits inside `HL7/aitransparency-ig`:

1. **Refine the logical model in `input/fsh/LM_AI.fsh`** so that the
   two elements have:
   - Explicit, IG-scoped element definitions stating what each captures
     (Data quality = an *assertion about* the suitability of the input
     data passed to the AI; Data qualification = an *assertion about*
     the validation / certification / regulatory compliance status of
     the source from which the input data was obtained).
   - A concrete data type for each — `CodeableConcept` for both, with
     `text` allowed, so implementers can supply free-text claims when
     no controlled vocabulary applies.
   - Optional cardinality (`0..*`) on both, since neither is universal
     across AI use cases.
   - A note in the element definition that bindings are deliberately
     left example-only / unbound for STU1, with a placeholder
     ValueSet stub if the work group prefers an explicit example
     binding.

2. **Add a clarifying paragraph in
   `input/pagecontent/general_guidance.md`** under the existing
   "Defining the AI Model" or "Context of AI Usage" heading (whichever
   the work group prefers) that:
   - States that the IG captures *metadata about* input data, not the
     input data itself.
   - Distinguishes Data quality (about the data) from Data
     qualification (about the source / provenance) and gives one
     short example of each.
   - Calls out explicitly that detailed data-quality scoring is out of
     scope for this IG and that implementers wishing to convey
     numerical scores should profile / extend on top of the logical
     model rather than inline them here.

#### Justification

The ticket is filed against an STU1 ballot and the only Zulip context
puts it in a "Documentation & Clarity" bucket, signalling the
work-group view that the elements should stay but their *meaning* and
*usage* need to be made unambiguous. Defining both as
`CodeableConcept 0..*` is consistent with how the rest of the AI
observability factors are typed in `LM_AI.fsh` and avoids forcing a
controlled vocabulary the IG does not yet own. The narrative
clarification directly answers the reporter's three questions ("what
role", "what encoding", "is data quality in scope") in one place
without restructuring the model. Per the briefing, narrative edits to
`general_guidance.md` are a low-risk recipe but require an anchor
audit if any heading is renamed — this disposition adds prose under
existing headings rather than renaming, so anchor breakage is
avoided.

---

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification (Drop "Data quality", keep "Data qualification")

#### Proposal

Accept the reporter's scope concern and **remove the "Data quality"
element** from `input/fsh/LM_AI.fsh`, while **retaining and tightening
"Data qualification"**:

1. In `input/fsh/LM_AI.fsh`, delete the `Data quality` element from
   the relevant logical model class (likely under
   `LM_AI` → `Context` / `Process`, depending on where it currently
   sits). Renumber / re-order remaining siblings as needed.
2. Re-define `Data qualification` with type `CodeableConcept 0..*`,
   short = "Validation / certification / regulatory status of the
   data source", and definition text scoped explicitly to the
   *source* (e.g., FDA-cleared dataset, IRB-approved registry,
   accredited reference lab) — not to the data values themselves.
3. Update `input/pagecontent/general_guidance.md` to (a) note the
   removal in the Changes section, (b) explain that data-quality
   assessment is intentionally deferred to other specifications
   (e.g., observation-level data quality patterns or downstream
   profiling), and (c) document the narrowed semantics of Data
   qualification.
4. Add an entry to `input/pagecontent/changes.md` recording the
   ballot-cycle removal so STU1 implementers see it.

#### Justification

The reporter's strongest argument is that "data quality" is a
multi-faceted, well-studied problem (completeness, accuracy,
consistency, reliability) that an AI Transparency IG cannot meaningfully
constrain at the logical-model level without either being trivial or
duplicating other work. Data qualification, by contrast, is naturally
within scope: knowing whether the *source* of input data is validated
or regulated is itself an AI transparency claim. Trimming the model
sharpens the IG and pre-empts implementer confusion about which scoring
schemes are expected. Per the briefing, this is a pure-FSH plus
narrative edit; no `sushi-config.yaml`, IG-publisher template, or
cross-repo dependency is impacted. The cost is a minor backwards
incompatibility for any pre-ballot consumer that already populated a
"Data quality" instance, but this is STU1-ballot, so the change window
is open.

---

### Disposition C: Not Persuasive (Decline)

#### Proposal

Decline the request and close as **Not Persuasive**, with the
explanation that:

- The element definitions in `LM_AI.fsh` already convey purpose; the
  short text reproduced in the ticket *is* the current short
  definition.
- Encoding is implicit from the FSH type declaration; a
  `CodeableConcept` (or whatever the current type is) supports both
  coded and text content, which is sufficient for STU1.
- The IG does not need to police data-quality scoring; the elements
  are placeholders to *carry* such claims when implementers choose to
  populate them.

No FSH or narrative changes are made.

#### Justification

Defensible only if the work group judges that the existing definitions
are clear on inspection of the rendered model and that adding more
prose risks over-specifying STU1. The position would also rest on
treating the model as a data-bearing scaffold whose semantic
contracts are deliberately thin at this maturity level (FMM 2,
trial-use). However, this disposition does not address the reporter's
specific questions and leaves the same ambiguity for the next
implementer to re-discover, which is what the work group's own
"Documentation & Clarity" triage bucket is intended to surface and
fix.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **A**

Disposition A is the smallest change that actually answers the
reporter's three questions (role, encoding, scope) and aligns with the
work group's own triage bucket (Documentation & Clarity, per the
January ballot Zulip thread). It keeps the model surface stable for
implementers who have already started prototyping against the STU1
ballot and confines the change to two well-understood files
(`input/fsh/LM_AI.fsh` and `input/pagecontent/general_guidance.md`)
with no cross-repo or anchor-renaming risk. Disposition B is a
plausible second choice if the work group decides on review that
"Data quality" really is outside IG scope; in that case, B should be
adopted in full rather than partially, including the changelog
entry, to give STU1 implementers a clean signal. Disposition C is not
recommended — declining without clarification leaves the reported
ambiguity in the ballot text and is likely to attract repeat ballot
comments at the next cycle.
