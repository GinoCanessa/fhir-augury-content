## Summary

The AI Transparency on FHIR IG's General Guidance currently documents
three distinct mechanisms for conveying a Model Card alongside an AI
System: `Device.note` (R4, but usable in R5/R6 as well), `Device.property`
(R5/R6 only), and a separate `DocumentReference` linked to the `Device`
via `Provenance`. Hans Buitendijk (Cerner/Oracle Health) raises that the
guidance does not make it sufficiently clear *when* implementers should
choose one mechanism over another as they move from R4 to R5/R6, nor
why a free-text note vs. an inline attachment vs. a fully separate
DocumentReference would be appropriate in different situations. The
ticket suggests the IG should ideally pick **one** mechanism that works
across all FHIR versions — the reporter's preference is `DocumentReference`
— or, failing that, provide explicit selection guidance per version. No
cross-referenced Jira tickets were returned by the FHIR Augury index;
the only external mention is in the `AI Transparency on FHIR` Zulip
ballot triage roll-up, which classifies this issue under "Profile &
Model Structure" and flags it as one of the items that could
plausibly span multiple categories.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the IG's General Guidance to **standardize on
`DocumentReference` (linked to the `Device` via `Provenance`) as the
single, normative mechanism for conveying a Model Card across R4, R5,
and R6**. Specifically:

- In the General Guidance "Model Card" section, replace the current
  three-option presentation with a single recommended pattern: a
  `DocumentReference` whose `content.attachment` carries (or links to)
  the Model Card document, related to the AI `Device` through a
  `Provenance` resource that ties Device + DocumentReference to the
  asserted resource(s).
- Demote `Device.note` (R4/R5/R6) and `Device.property` (R5/R6) to a
  short "Alternatives observed in the wild / not recommended" note,
  explaining that they remain technically valid carriers but should
  not be produced by conformant implementations of this IG.
- Update any examples currently using `Device.note` or
  `Device.property` for Model Card content to use the
  DocumentReference + Provenance pattern.
- Add a single "Versioning" callout that says the same pattern works
  identically in R4, R5, and R6 — explicitly addressing the reporter's
  cross-version concern.

#### Justification

The reporter, the IG lead (John Moehrke, in his comment), and the
Zulip triage all converge on the same direction: a single mechanism is
clearer for implementers and easier to validate. `DocumentReference` is
the only one of the three options that is fully expressive in **every**
targeted FHIR version, that supports rich metadata about the document
itself (author, date, format, language, attachment hash), and that
already integrates cleanly with `Provenance` — which the IG is already
using for AI System attribution. Free-text `Device.note` cannot carry
structured Model Card metadata, and `Device.property` does not exist in
R4, so neither can be the single answer.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

**Keep all three mechanisms, but add explicit selection guidance
keyed to FHIR version and use case**, rather than collapsing to one.
Specifically:

- Add an "Choosing how to convey the Model Card" subsection to the
  General Guidance with a small decision matrix:
  - **R4 only**, no document store available, brief textual card →
    `Device.note`.
  - **R5/R6**, structured key/value Model Card facets needed →
    `Device.property` (with a named/coded property for the Model Card,
    where the binding is documented by this IG).
  - **Any version**, full Model Card document (PDF/HTML/JSON) needs
    to be retrievable as an artifact, signed, versioned, or shared
    independently of the Device → `DocumentReference` linked via
    `Provenance`.
- For each option, list the trade-offs (queryability, document
  fidelity, version support, signing/integrity, ability to revise the
  card without revising the Device).
- Mark `DocumentReference` as the **preferred** option when no
  constraint forces one of the other two, and require the IG's
  conformance examples to use it.

#### Justification

This preserves implementer choice for environments that already have
production data using `Device.note` or `Device.property` (notably R4
deployments where DocumentReference plumbing may not be in place), and
avoids breaking-change pressure on existing implementations between
ballots. It still resolves the reporter's underlying complaint — the
ambiguity about *when* to use which — by making the selection rule
explicit. It also addresses Moehrke's specific request for "an R5/R6
section that discusses the future," because the matrix naturally
becomes the place where the future direction (toward
`DocumentReference`) is documented.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request to consolidate or rewrite. Keep the existing
General Guidance text describing all three mechanisms, on the basis
that selection is implementer judgment and the IG should not constrain
it further. Close as **Persuasive with Mod – Considered for Future
Use** (or equivalent), noting that a deeper Model Card conformance
profile is anticipated in a future version of the IG.

#### Justification

Defensible only if the work group concludes that (a) the three
mechanisms genuinely serve different production scenarios, (b)
narrowing now would invalidate existing R4 deployments that already
rely on `Device.note`, and (c) the IG is still maturing and a
prescriptive choice is premature. However, this is the weakest option:
the ticket's core complaint is *lack of guidance*, and "we deliberately
leave it open" does not actually answer it. The reporter would
reasonably re-file at the next ballot.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B (selection guidance + DocumentReference as preferred,
all three retained) best reflects the actual state of the discussion:
the reporter has stated a preference for DocumentReference, the IG
lead has agreed that consolidation on DocumentReference is "a good
plan" *and* asked specifically for an R5/R6 forward-looking section,
and the IG cannot ignore that `Device.property` doesn't exist in R4
while existing implementers may already be using `Device.note`.
Disposition B captures both ideas: it gives implementers an
unambiguous selection rule today (closing the reporter's gap), it
endorses `DocumentReference` as the preferred path (closing
Moehrke's), and it leaves a clean migration story for a future ballot
to promote `DocumentReference` to "only" if the work group later
chooses to. Disposition A is the right *eventual* destination but is
likely too aggressive for a single ballot cycle without first staging
a deprecation. Disposition C is not defensible against the ticket as
written.
