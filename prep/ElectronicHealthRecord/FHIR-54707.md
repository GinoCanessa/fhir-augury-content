## Summary

The reporter asks the AI Transparency (FHIR) IG to stop using the word
"Tag" (especially capitalized as "Tag" / "Tagging") to describe the
metadata placed on AI-generated or AI-influenced content, because that
term carries a distinct technical meaning in FHIR that conflicts with
how the IG is using it. In FHIR, `Meta.tag` is a specific element and
"security label" (Coding carried via `Meta.security` and related
structures, as elaborated in HL7 IGs such as Data Segmentation for
Privacy / DS4P) is an established, well-understood concept. The
reporter recommends the IG either drop the word "Tag" entirely in favor
of the already-known FHIR concept of "label", or, if "Tag" is retained,
explicitly define it within the IG so readers are not misled into
assuming it means `Meta.tag` or a DS4P-style security label.

There are no linked Jira tickets and no GitHub cross-references. The
only cross-reference surfaced is a Zulip triage post for the January
ballot of the AI Transparency on FHIR stream, which categorises this
ticket under "Provenance & Tagging" alongside nine other related items

FHIR-54242, FHIR-54141, FHIR-53973), indicating the editors are already
clustering tag/provenance feedback for coordinated disposition.

## Details

**Description:**

> Using Tag and Tagging especially capitalized implies a special meaning
> which contrasts with the FHIR's use of 'label'. While there is
> distinctions between the two terms as discussed in other HL7 IGs (like
> the DS4P) it seems that for the purposes of this IG it's best to avoid
> using this term and stick to 'label' which is readily a known concept
> in FHIR.

**Comments:**

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Carry out an editorial sweep of the AI Transparency (FHIR) IG narrative
pages and replace every occurrence of "Tag" / "Tagging" (including
capitalized headings, section titles, and body prose) with "label" /
"labeling" wherever the text is describing the metadata applied to
AI-generated or AI-influenced content. Also rename any IG-defined
concept or diagram caption that uses "Tag" so the IG consistently uses
"label" as its term of art. Do not add any new IG-specific defined
term for "Tag". No StructureDefinitions, ValueSets, CodeSystems, or
operation definitions are renamed by this disposition — this is a prose
change to the published guide pages only.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter asked for and is consistent with the
FHIR community's existing vocabulary: "label" is widely understood via
`Meta.security`, DS4P, and other HL7 IGs, whereas "Tag" in a FHIR
context typically refers to `Meta.tag`, which is not what the AI
Transparency IG is describing. John Moehrke's comment on the ticket
shows the editor is open to the change; he only avoided "label" out of
concern that DS4P imposes a formal provenance-of-the-label requirement.
That concern can be addressed at the IG level by stating explicitly
that the IG's use of "label" is the informal FHIR sense (a Coding
applied to a resource for categorisation) and is not an assertion of
DS4P-style security-label semantics unless the IG says so. Aligning
terminology across the IG also keeps this ticket consistent with the
nine sibling "Provenance & Tagging" tickets that are likely to be
dispositioned together.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the term "Tag" in the IG, but add a dedicated "Terminology" (or
"Notational Conventions") subsection at the top of the IG that defines
"Tag" precisely for the scope of this guide — including how the IG's
use of "Tag" relates to, and differs from, both `Meta.tag` and DS4P's
formal security-label model. Every first use of "Tag" in a major
section links back to that definition. Optionally, also introduce the
lowercase word "label" in prose where the IG is clearly referring to
the FHIR general concept (e.g., a Coding carried on a resource) and
reserve capital-T "Tag" for the IG-specific, well-defined concept.

#### Justification

This preserves the editor's deliberate choice (John Moehrke noted that
he used "Tag" precisely because "label" in the DS4P sense carries
obligations — "a label requires that it explain where the tag value
came from" — that the IG does not want to impose). Defining the term
in the IG is the standard HL7 mechanism for resolving a naming
collision with FHIR core vocabulary without having to rename anything,
and it avoids churn in any downstream references, diagrams, or
implementer materials that already use "Tag". The trade-off versus
Disposition A is that readers still need to learn IG-local terminology;
the mitigation is that the definition is one link away from every
first use.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as Not Persuasive. Rationale: the IG already uses
"Tag" in a scoped, self-explanatory way, and readers of an AI
Transparency IG are expected to read the guide's narrative rather than
import meaning from `Meta.tag` or DS4P. No terminology change is made.

#### Justification

Declining is the weakest option here. The only defensible ground is
editorial stability — avoiding a sweep that touches many pages late in
a ballot cycle. That is not a strong argument against a terminology
fix that the IG editor has already publicly agreed is worth
considering, and it does not address the reporter's core concern that
a capitalized "Tag" in a FHIR IG will be read by FHIR implementers as
a reference to `Meta.tag`. Declining would also leave the
"Provenance & Tagging" cluster of sibling tickets without a clean
terminological foundation to disposition against.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Accepting the request and sweeping "Tag" → "label" throughout the IG
narrative is the cleanest answer for readers who come to this guide
from elsewhere in FHIR, and the IG editor (John Moehrke) has already
signalled in his comment that he is receptive. The only real concern
he raised — that "label" in the DS4P sense carries a provenance
obligation — is resolvable with one sentence in the IG's scope or
terminology section clarifying that the AI Transparency IG uses "label"
in the general FHIR sense of a Coding applied for categorisation, and
that any DS4P-style obligations, where they apply, come from DS4P
rather than from this IG's use of the word. Doing this now also sets a
consistent vocabulary for the nine sibling "Provenance & Tagging"
tickets the editors have clustered for the January ballot, which is
more efficient than dispositioning each one against an unresolved
terminology baseline. Disposition B is a reasonable fallback if the
workgroup decides the editorial churn is too large this late in the
cycle, but it leaves FHIR readers to learn IG-local vocabulary that
conflicts with the FHIR default, which is precisely the harm the
ticket is trying to prevent.
