## Summary

The reporter requests that the EHR-S Functional Model — Record Lifecycle
Events (FHIR) function **CP.1.6#02** (the conformance criterion related to
immunization / vaccination recordkeeping) be expanded to require capture of
additional immunization data elements. Specifically, the comment asks for
inclusion of: vaccine record type (a flag distinguishing currently
administered vaccines from historically administered ones), body route, body
site, and the Vaccine Information Statement (VIS) information given to the
patient. No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip discussions, GitHub items,
or Confluence pages were located via the FHIR Augury cross-reference and
search indexes for this ticket, so the request is being evaluated on its own
merits and against the underlying FHIR `Immunization` resource model.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the EHR-S FM Record Lifecycle Events (FHIR) profile of function
**CP.1.6 (Manage Immunization Administration)**, criterion **#02**, to
explicitly require the system to capture and persist the following data for
each immunization event:

1. **Vaccine record type** — an indicator distinguishing whether the
   immunization was administered at the encounter ("current"/primary source)
   or was recorded from a historical/external source. In the FHIR mapping
   this aligns with `Immunization.primarySource` (boolean) plus
   `Immunization.reportOrigin` (CodeableConcept) when `primarySource =
   false`.
2. **Body route** — the route of administration, mapped to
   `Immunization.route` (e.g., `IM`, `SC`, `PO`).
3. **Body site** — the anatomic site of administration, mapped to
   `Immunization.site` (e.g., left deltoid).
4. **Vaccine Information Statement (VIS) information** — the VIS
   document(s) presented to the patient, including publication date and
   presentation date, mapped to `Immunization.education` with
   `documentType`, `publicationDate`, and `presentationDate`.

The conformance language for CP.1.6#02 should be updated to "The system
**SHALL** capture vaccine record type (current vs. historical), route of
administration, anatomic site, and Vaccine Information Statement
information for each immunization event," and the FHIR mapping table
should reference the four `Immunization` elements above.

#### Justification

Each requested item already exists as a first-class element on the FHIR
`Immunization` resource (`primarySource` / `reportOrigin`, `route`, `site`,
`education`), so requiring them in CP.1.6#02 aligns the EHR-S FM RLE
profile with the FHIR data model and with widely-used immunization
profiles (US Core Immunization, IIS-reporting profiles, CDC IIS HL7 v2
guidance). Distinguishing current vs. historical immunizations is a
long-standing public-health requirement (immunization registry
reconciliation), and route/site/VIS capture is mandated by the U.S. CDC
NCIRD reporting requirements and by 21st Century Cures certification
criteria for immunization. Strengthening the criterion brings the EHR-S
FM RLE in line with the actual conformance burden implementers already
carry.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than promoting all four elements to **SHALL** within CP.1.6#02,
restructure the criterion as follows:

- **SHALL** capture route of administration (`Immunization.route`) and
  anatomic site (`Immunization.site`).
- **SHALL** capture a primary-source/historical flag
  (`Immunization.primarySource`, with `Immunization.reportOrigin` when
  applicable).
- **SHOULD** capture VIS information (`Immunization.education`
  including `publicationDate` and `presentationDate`), with a note that
  jurisdictions or realms requiring VIS distribution (e.g., U.S. NCVIA)
  effectively elevate this to SHALL via realm-specific profiles.

Add a non-normative note pointing readers to US Core Immunization and to
the IHE / CDC IIS guidance for the realm-specific terminology bindings
(NDC/CVX for vaccine, SNOMED CT body-site, NCIT VIS document codes).

#### Justification

The four requested data elements are not equally universal. Route, site,
and the current-vs-historical distinction are required for any clinically
defensible immunization record worldwide. VIS, however, is a U.S.-specific
artifact arising from the National Childhood Vaccine Injury Act; outside
the U.S. an equivalent "vaccine information leaflet" practice exists but
is not uniformly mandated. Splitting the criterion preserves the EHR-S FM
RLE's realm-neutral posture while still strengthening the core
immunization data set, and it lets U.S. realm guides (or HL7 Public
Health Work Group guidance) carry the SHALL for VIS where it actually
applies.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request and leave CP.1.6#02 unchanged. Document in the
disposition that the underlying FHIR `Immunization` resource already
exposes the requested elements (`primarySource`, `reportOrigin`, `route`,
`site`, `education`) and that downstream implementation guides — most
notably US Core Immunization and CDC IIS reporting guidance — already
specify the conformance expectations for capturing them. The EHR-S FM RLE
profile's role is to describe lifecycle events, not to re-specify the
content model; adding element-level SHALLs duplicates work and risks
drift from the source IGs.

#### Justification

The FHIR `Immunization` resource already defines the elements the reporter
wants captured, and any EHR-S FM-conformant system that exchanges
immunizations through US Core, the IIS Reporting IG, or HL7 v2 VXU is
already required to capture them. Encoding those requirements a second
time in CP.1.6#02 creates two sources of truth that must be kept in sync
across ballot cycles — a known pain point for the EHR-S FM Functional
Profile family. Declining keeps CP.1.6 focused on lifecycle behavior and
defers content-model conformance to the appropriate IGs.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the most defensible balance of the three. The reporter
has identified a real gap — CP.1.6#02 today is silent on data elements
that are clinically and operationally mandatory for an immunization
record — and Disposition C's "the IGs already cover it" argument
understates the EHR-S FM's role as the system-level conformance baseline
that those IGs build on. Disposition A, on the other hand, over-reaches
by elevating VIS to a universal SHALL even though VIS is a U.S.-specific
regulatory artifact and the EHR-S FM RLE is a realm-neutral
specification. Disposition B captures route, site, and primary-source
status as SHALLs (the universally clinically necessary elements), keeps
VIS as a SHOULD with explicit realm-profile guidance, and avoids
re-specifying terminology bindings already owned by US Core / IIS guides.
This gives the workgroup a concrete, FHIR-element-accurate change to
ballot while leaving room for U.S.-realm tightening through profile
inheritance.
