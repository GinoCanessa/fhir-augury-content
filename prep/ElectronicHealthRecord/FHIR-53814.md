## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the "AI-assisted patient appointment" scenario from the linked
blog post as a new (or replacement) worked use-case in the AI
Transparency (FHIR) IG. Concretely: add a new narrative use-case page
under the IG's "Use Cases" section that walks through an ambient /
AI-assisted appointment-scheduling workflow, including the actors
(patient, scheduler, AI assistant, EHR), the data flow, and the
points at which AI Transparency artifacts (Provenance with the
AI/`autogenerated` agent role, AuditEvent entries, and any IG-defined
extensions on `Appointment` / `AppointmentResponse` /
`Communication`) are produced and consumed. Cross-link the new
use-case from the IG home page and from the Provenance / AuditEvent
profile pages so readers can see the resources in their full
end-to-end context. Cite the blog post in the use-case's
"References" section as the source narrative.

#### Justification

The IG's January 2026 ballot triage explicitly carved out a "Use Cases
& Examples" category with 12 open issues, and FHIR-53814 sits in that
bucket — adopting a concrete, real-world scenario directly addresses a
gap the work group has already acknowledged. Appointment scheduling is
an early, high-volume target for ambient AI in production EHRs, so a
worked example here gives implementers a recognizable hook that
generalizes to other ambient-AI workflows (documentation, order entry,
messaging). The reporter is John Moehrke — the IG's primary editor and
a long-standing security/privacy authority — which makes the source
material a strong fit for the IG's voice and scope.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than lifting the blog post wholesale, extract the *requirements*
the AI-assisted-appointment scenario exposes and use them to **augment
the IG's existing use-cases** instead of adding a new top-level one.
Specifically: (1) add a short "ambient AI in scheduling" sub-section to
whichever existing use-case page most closely matches (or to a new
"Patterns" appendix), focused on what AI Transparency metadata MUST /
SHOULD be present on `Appointment`, `AppointmentResponse`, and any
backing `Communication` / `DocumentReference` resources; (2) add one or
two example instances (JSON/XML) under the IG's `examples/` folder
demonstrating Provenance + AuditEvent for an AI-suggested appointment
slot; and (3) leave the blog post as a non-normative "Further reading"
link rather than the canonical narrative. Coordinate with the other
"Use Cases & Examples" tickets (FHIR-53770, FHIR-53937, FHIR-54731,
FHIR-54577/54578, FHIR-54642–54644, FHIR-54706) so the IG ends up with
a coherent set of examples rather than a patchwork.

#### Justification

The ticket as written is a pointer to external content rather than a
specified change, so accepting it verbatim risks the IG either copying
blog prose (with licensing/attribution issues) or rendering an
under-specified narrative that doesn't tie back to the IG's normative
profiles. Pulling the *requirements* out and expressing them as
example instances + targeted narrative keeps the IG's normative
content in charge and produces artifacts implementers can actually
conform to. Folding the work into the broader "Use Cases & Examples"
sweep also avoids the IG growing a parallel, blog-shaped use-case
that drifts from the rest of the document.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (Persuasive with Mod)

#### Proposal

Mark FHIR-53814 as **Persuasive with Modification: editor's
discretion** — accept the *intent* (the IG should have at least one
ambient-AI worked example) but decline to commit to the specific blog
post's framing. Note in the resolution that the editors will consider
the linked scenario alongside other Use Cases & Examples tickets when
revising the use-case section for the next ballot, and that a
substantive new ticket with concrete proposed text would be welcome.
Close the ticket once the next ballot's use-case revisions land.

#### Justification

The ticket provides essentially no specification: it is a single
sentence plus an external URL, with no explicit mapping to an IG
section, no proposed text, no resource-level guidance, and no comments
or follow-up to clarify. Per HL7 ballot-resolution practice, comments
that amount to "consider this external article" are typically resolved
as Persuasive with Modification or Not Persuasive with Mod rather
than as concrete change requests, because the editorial team must
still do the design work of translating the source into normative
content. Declining as a discrete change preserves editor flexibility
and avoids committing to a use-case framing the work group hasn't
actually reviewed.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The ticket is unmistakably pointing the editors at a real,
ballot-relevant gap (ambient-AI appointment scheduling is exactly the
kind of scenario the AI Transparency IG exists to govern), so an
outright decline (C) would leave a known weakness unaddressed and
ignore the reporter's standing as the IG's lead editor. At the same
time, accepting the request literally (A) would mean importing a
blog-shaped narrative whose details have not been reviewed by the work
group and which is not aligned with the IG's existing use-case
structure. Disposition B threads the needle: it commits the work group
to producing the worked example the reporter is asking for, but does so
through the IG's normal mechanisms — example instances + targeted
narrative bound to the IG's normative profiles — and explicitly
coordinates with the other 11 "Use Cases & Examples" tickets in the
same triage bucket so the IG ends up with one coherent set of
examples instead of twelve disjoint ones. The work group should ask
the reporter to either supply concrete proposed text/example
instances or to nominate a co-author to draft them before the next
ballot.
