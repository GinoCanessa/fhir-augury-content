## Summary

(FHIR) Implementation Guide be accompanied by a visual diagram that
communicates the flow / actors of that use case. The ticket provides a
single example image (attachment 26530, "AIToF Use Cases") to illustrate

resources, or conformance; it affects only the narrative pages that
enumerate use cases in the IG. There are no cross-referenced Jira

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a diagram to every use case currently enumerated on the AI
Transparency IG's Use Cases page(s). Each diagram should depict, at a
minimum, the principal actors (e.g., AI developer/producer, AI
deployer, clinician, patient, regulator as applicable), the FHIR
resources / artifacts they exchange (e.g., the Transparency Summary
artifact and associated Provenance/DocumentReference references), and
the sequence or context of the interaction. Diagrams must be authored
in a format that is editable with open-source or widely-available
tooling (per john_moehrke's comment) — recommended options are
PlantUML (text-based, renders to SVG) or Mermaid sequence / flow
diagrams; PowerPoint `.pptx` is also acceptable. Commit both the
editable source and the rendered PNG/SVG alongside the narrative page,
and embed the rendered image in the `.md`/XML page that introduces
each use case.

#### Justification

The request is purely editorial and improves implementer comprehension
without touching any profiles, extensions, value sets, or conformance
rules — a low-risk, high-value clarity improvement. The example image
attached to the ticket (swim-lane style actors-and-artifacts diagram)
is a proven pattern also used by many other HL7 IGs (e.g., Da Vinci
family, IPS). Enforcing an open-source editable-source requirement
matches john_moehrke's constraint and aligns with HL7 IG maintenance
practice, where IGs that accept diagrams only as flattened images
typically accumulate stale or un-fixable visuals over time.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than committing the IG to one diagram per use case universally,
adopt a tiered approach: (1) require a diagram *only for complex /
multi-actor use cases* where narrative alone is ambiguous, and
(2) for simple use cases, satisfy the ticket with a concise bulleted
"Actors / Inputs / Outputs / Trigger" block rendered from a small
YAML/JSON side-car file. The tiered policy is added to the IG's
editorial conventions page and applied to the use-cases section; the
diagrams that *are* produced follow the same editable-source rules
as in Disposition A (PlantUML / Mermaid / PPTX). Bundle this work
with the other "Use Cases & Examples" ballot tickets
(FHIR-54731, FHIR-54706, FHIR-54644, FHIR-54643, FHIR-54642,
FHIR-54583, FHIR-54578, FHIR-54577, FHIR-54278, FHIR-53814,
FHIR-53770) so the whole section is revised once.

#### Justification

Accept-as-requested ties ballot closure to producing N diagrams (one
per use case), which is high effort for little marginal clarity on
trivially simple use cases. The tiered approach captures the reporter's
real intent — make complex use cases visually comprehensible — while
avoiding make-work on use cases where a short structured block is
clearer than a diagram. Coupling with the sibling "Use Cases &
Examples" tickets lets the workgroup re-author that section once
rather than in piecemeal responses to each individual ballot comment.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline FHIR-53937 on the grounds that it is a stylistic preference
rather than a defect: the use-case narratives already convey the
required information textually, diagrams would not change any
conformance behaviour, and producing and maintaining editable diagrams
adds non-trivial editorial burden for each future revision of the IG.
The reporter's example image is illustrative only and the ticket does
not identify any specific use case that is unclear without a diagram.

#### Justification

Declining is defensible when a ballot comment expresses a personal
preference for visual aids without identifying an actual gap in the
spec. However, this defense is weak for a still-maturing IG where
diagrams *are* a known aid to implementer adoption, so this option
exists primarily to give the workgroup a clear "do nothing" choice
to weigh against A and B.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The reporter's underlying need — that readers of the AI Transparency
IG can grasp each use case without parsing dense prose — is
legitimate and clearly in-scope for an IG that is headed toward
publication. But Disposition A locks the workgroup into producing a
diagram for every use case regardless of complexity, which over-fits a
stylistic preference into a maintenance obligation. Disposition B
honours the intent, respects john_moehrke's editable-source
constraint, and pairs cleanly with the other eleven "Use Cases &
Examples" ballot tickets that the January-ballot Zulip triage has
already clustered together — making this a single coordinated edit of
the section rather than twelve disjoint ones. Disposition C is not
recommended: declining a reasonable documentation-quality comment on
a still-maturing IG sends the wrong signal to implementer reviewers
and leaves the sibling tickets without a coherent editorial policy to
hook into.
