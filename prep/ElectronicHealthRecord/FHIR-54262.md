## Summary

The AI Transparency (FHIR) IG currently uses two distinct phrases —
"An AI" and "The AI System" — when describing what is represented by
the FHIR `Device` resource in the IG. Reporter Hans Buitendijk points
out that the difference between the two phrases is not defined and
introduces ambiguity for implementers. Because `Device` represents the
software providing AI capabilities, the more natural reading is that
`Device` represents "The AI System". The reporter suggests aligning
the wording by replacing "An AI" with "An AI System" wherever it
appears so that the IG consistently uses a single term for the thing
modeled by `Device`.

The ticket has been triaged as **Persuasive** by the work group, with
the resolution noting "agree, remove unintended confusion." No
explicitly linked Jira tickets were returned, and the only related
discussion surfaced is the Electronic Health Record / AI Transparency
WG triage post for the January ballot, which categorises this issue
under "Documentation & Clarity" alongside ~30 other comparable
wording-cleanup items (FHIR-54260, FHIR-54274, FHIR-54275, FHIR-54276,
etc.). There is no separate Zulip thread debating the wording change
itself, and no GitHub PR or commit currently links to this ticket in
the augury index.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply a global wording change in the AI Transparency (FHIR) IG
narrative so that every occurrence of the bare phrase "An AI" (when
used to refer to the thing represented by the `Device` resource) is
replaced with "An AI System". Concretely:

- Update the IG's introductory / conformance text where it currently
  reads "An AI is defined using the Device resource" to "An AI System
  is defined using the Device resource."
- Sweep the rest of the IG narrative (background, profile intros,
  examples narrative, glossary) to use "AI System" consistently when
  referring to the software entity modeled by `Device`. Keep the bare
  word "AI" only in adjectival uses (e.g., "AI capabilities", "AI
  model", "AI Transparency") where it is not the noun naming the
  modelled entity.
- Optionally add a one-line note in the glossary / definitions
  section stating that within this IG, "AI System" is the term used
  for the software entity modelled by `Device`, to lock the
  convention in for future authors.

No StructureDefinitions, profiles, ValueSets, or examples need to
change — this is a narrative-only edit.

#### Justification

This is what the reporter asked for and matches the resolution the WG
already recorded ("Persuasive — agree, remove unintended confusion").
Using a single, consistent term for the modelled entity removes a
pure documentation defect with zero downstream impact on
implementers, profiles, conformance, or terminology bindings. It is
also consistent with how the IG's title ("AI Transparency") and the
Device resource (which models a software/system rather than an
abstract concept "AI") naturally read.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than mechanically substituting "An AI" → "An AI System",
introduce a short **terminology section** at the front of the IG that
explicitly distinguishes the concepts and then audit usage against
those definitions. For example:

- **AI** (or "AI capability"): the abstract capability or technique
  (machine learning, predictive model, generative model, etc.).
- **AI System**: the deployed software that provides one or more AI
  capabilities; this is what is represented by the `Device` resource
  in this IG.
- **AI Model**: the specific trained artefact (parameters / weights)
  used by the AI System; documented via the Model Card profile.

Then update the IG narrative so the three terms are used
consistently, replacing "An AI" with "An AI System" only where the
sentence is talking about the deployed software entity, and keeping
or substituting "AI" / "AI Model" elsewhere when those are actually
what is meant.

#### Justification

The ticket title flags ambiguity in defining **both** "AI" *and*
"Model Card" — the underlying problem is that the IG conflates three
related-but-different concepts (capability, deployed system, trained
model) under one overloaded label. A pure search-and-replace
(Disposition A) fixes the specific sentence cited but does not
prevent the same ambiguity from re-emerging in adjacent text or in
new sections added before publication. A short terminology block plus
a definitions-driven audit is a slightly larger edit but produces a
more durable fix and helps reviewers of other "Documentation &
Clarity" tickets in this ballot batch (FHIR-54260, FHIR-54274,
FHIR-54275, FHIR-54276, etc.) by giving them a shared vocabulary to
align against.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket as **Not Persuasive** on the grounds that the IG's
intent is already explained in surrounding context — the reader can
reasonably infer that "An AI" and "The AI System" refer to the same
modelled entity, and the rest of the IG (especially the Device
profile definition) makes the binding to `Device` unambiguous. Leave
the existing wording as-is.

#### Justification

A defensible "decline" rationale would be that the change is purely
cosmetic, does not affect conformance, and risks being one of many
small editorial nits that delay the ballot reconciliation cycle
without materially improving implementer outcomes. If the WG is
trying to keep the diff against the balloted text minimal, declining
small wording-only changes is a legitimate stance.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The work group has already signalled (Resolution: Persuasive,
"agree, remove unintended confusion") that they accept the underlying
point, so Disposition C is inconsistent with the WG's own triage and
is not recommended. Disposition A is the literal answer to the
reporter's suggestion and is the path of least effort, but it leaves
two latent problems: (1) the ticket title also calls out *Model Card*
as ambiguous, which a pure "An AI" → "An AI System" substitution does
not address, and (2) the IG batch contains roughly thirty other
Documentation & Clarity tickets in the same ballot pass that almost
certainly suffer from the same unstated overloading of "AI" / "AI
System" / "AI Model" / "Model Card". Disposition B costs only a short
terminology block plus a focused narrative audit, but it gives the
editors a single source of truth to apply across all of those
tickets, makes the eventual reconciliation defensible to ballot
voters, and still satisfies the reporter's request as a special case.
If the WG decides B is too large for this ticket alone, fall back to
A here and open a follow-up ticket explicitly for the AI / AI System /
AI Model / Model Card glossary.
