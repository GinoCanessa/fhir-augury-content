## Summary

FHIR-54735 ("Define Model Card better") is a January-ballot Change Request
against the AI Transparency on FHIR IG (work group: Electronic Health
Record). The reporter, Virginia Lorenzi, asks the IG to add a clearer
definition of a "Model Card" — described in the ticket as a standard
representation of the description and metadata about a specific AI model
— and points to the Hugging Face model card and the CHAI applied model
card as industry exemplars. The ticket carries the `Block-Vote-2` label
and was bundled into the IG's January ballot triage.

The ticket has already been **resolved as "Persuasive with Modification"**.
Per the resolution wording, the page was restructured and the chosen
formulation reads: *"Chief among these is the nature of the AI itself.
The user would like to understand what algorithm / model was used, who
developed it, how it was trained, any certifications it has, and so
on… To do this, the guide outlines the use of the Provenance resource,
which can then be linked to Device and DocumentReference to point to a
Model-Card."* The implementing change shipped as part of
[HL7/aitransparency-ig#22](https://github.com/HL7/aitransparency-ig/pull/22).
There are no Jira-to-Jira cross-references for this ticket; the only
incoming link is the `[AI Transparency on FHIR] IG - Jan ballot` Zulip
triage table, which categorises FHIR-54735 under "Documentation &
Clarity" alongside ~30 other January-ballot comments. The disposition
proposals below are therefore framed as **confirming, refining, or
revisiting that already-merged restructure** rather than as net-new
spec authoring.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Confirm the merged restructure in
[HL7/aitransparency-ig#22](https://github.com/HL7/aitransparency-ig/pull/22)
and explicitly call out the Model Card definition in the IG narrative.
Concretely, in `input/pagecontent/general_guidance.md`, under the
existing `### Defining the AI Model` subsection of the General Guidance
page, add a short normative-style paragraph that:

1. Defines a Model Card as *"a standard, human- and machine-readable
   representation of the description and metadata about a specific AI
   model — including what algorithm/model was used, who developed it,
   how it was trained, intended use, known limitations, and any
   certifications."*
2. Cites the **Hugging Face Model Card** and the **CHAI Applied Model
   Card** as the two industry exemplars named in the ticket (with
   external links).
3. States the linkage already chosen in the resolution: a Model Card
   is referenced from `DocumentReference` (or `Device.url` /
   `Device.note`, as applicable), and `Provenance.entity` /
   `Provenance.agent.who` ties the AI usage event to the Device that
   in turn points at the Model Card via DocumentReference.
4. Adds at least one example (extending or referencing
   `input/fsh/EX_Provenance.fsh` and a new `EX_DocRef_ModelCard.fsh`
   instance under `input/fsh/`) showing the
   Provenance → Device → DocumentReference → Model Card chain end
   to end.

No `sushi-config.yaml` page changes are required (the content lives
inside an existing page); audit `general_guidance.html#…` anchors
elsewhere only if a heading text changes.

#### Justification

This is the smallest change that satisfies the ticket as written: it
gives implementers a single, citable definition of "Model Card" and
shows the FHIR mechanism the IG has already adopted to point at one.
It is consistent with the merged PR #22 wording (which already names
Provenance → Device → DocumentReference) and with FHIR's general
preference for narrative + worked example over net-new structural
constraints. Because the change is confined to
`input/pagecontent/general_guidance.md` plus optional FSH examples,
it does not touch any cross-repo dependency (HL7/fhir, HL7/UTG, or
the DS4P dependency) and avoids the
"section numbering is render-time" gotcha (no heading reshuffle, so
no anchor breakage).

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — Promote Model Card to its own page and a logical model

#### Proposal

Treat "Model Card" as a first-class IG concept rather than a paragraph
inside General Guidance. Specifically:

1. In `input/pagecontent/`, add a new `model_card.md` page that
   carries the definition, the Hugging Face / CHAI references, the
   Provenance → Device → DocumentReference linkage diagram, and
   conformance expectations for what a Model Card DocumentReference
   should carry (e.g., `type`, `category`, `content.attachment`
   metadata).
2. Register the new page under `pages:` (and optionally under
   `menu: → Guidance`) in `sushi-config.yaml`.
3. In `input/fsh/`, add a `LM_ModelCard.fsh` logical model that
   enumerates the Model Card fields the IG considers in scope
   (e.g., `modelName`, `developer`, `version`, `intendedUse`,
   `trainingDataSummary`, `evaluationMetrics`, `knownLimitations`,
   `certifications`), and a companion `SD_DocRef_ModelCard.fsh`
   profile of `DocumentReference` whose `content.attachment` carries
   a Model Card payload conforming to that logical model.
4. Update `input/pagecontent/general_guidance.md` to *link to* the
   new page from `### Defining the AI Model` instead of carrying the
   full definition inline; audit anchor references in `index.md`,
   `use_cases.md`, and `explainer.md`.

#### Justification

The ticket's two named exemplars (Hugging Face and CHAI) both treat the
Model Card as a structured artifact, not just a sentence of prose.
Promoting it to its own page plus a logical model gives implementers
something to *conform to*, which is what the ticket's authoring style
("a standard representation") is really asking for. This is more in
keeping with how the IG already handles AI observability (an
`LM_AI.fsh` logical model under `input/fsh/`). Trade-off: the change
is materially larger than Disposition A, requires `sushi-config.yaml`
edits (per the briefing's "`pages:` is author-managed" gotcha),
and may break in-page anchors used by sibling pages — both call for
careful audit before merge. It is also non-trivially broader than what
PR #22 already shipped, so it would re-open a resolution that the
work group has already marked Persuasive-with-Modification.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (Already Resolved — No Further Action)

#### Proposal

Decline to take any further action beyond what was merged in
`HL7/aitransparency-ig#22`. The ticket is already marked
`Triaged` with resolution `Persuasive with Modification`, and the
agreed wording — *"… To do this, the guide outlines the use of the
Provenance resource, which can then be linked to Device and
DocumentReference to point to a Model-Card."* — is in the General
Guidance page. Confirm the resolution at the workgroup, close the
ticket, and route any future Model-Card scoping work (logical model,
profile, dedicated page) through a new ticket so it can be triaged
on its own merits.

#### Justification

The category here is **already addressed by existing mechanism**:
PR #22 implemented the resolution the original requester was offered
and accepted. The Block-Vote-2 label and the Zulip triage thread both
indicate the workgroup intended to bundle FHIR-54735 with the rest of
the January-ballot Documentation & Clarity comments and move on.
Re-opening it now to add a logical model (Disposition B) without a
fresh ticket risks scope creep on the Block-Vote and conflates
narrative refinement with structural conformance. Declining further
work *on this ticket* preserves the workgroup's prior consensus while
keeping the door open for a follow-up ticket if implementer feedback
shows the merged wording is insufficient.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **A**

Disposition A is the right balance for FHIR-54735 as it stands. The
ticket asks for a *better definition*, not for a new conformance
artifact, and the workgroup has already converged (via PR #22) on
the Provenance → Device → DocumentReference linkage. A short, citable
paragraph plus a worked example closes the original ask without
re-litigating the resolution: it adds the two industry exemplars
the reporter named (Hugging Face, CHAI), it is confined to a single
narrative file, and — importantly per the briefing — it avoids the
heading/anchor-rename gotchas that any larger restructure would
trigger across `index.md`, `use_cases.md`, `explainer.md`, and the
menu. Disposition B (own page + logical model + DocumentReference
profile) is the right *long-term* direction if implementer feedback
shows the inline definition is insufficient, but it is a materially
larger change than what FHIR-54735 was scoped to and should be raised
as a fresh ticket so it can be triaged on its own merits rather than
folded into a Block-Vote-2 close-out. Disposition C understates the
ticket: the original request was specifically for the *definition* to
be improved, and adding the explicit Hugging Face / CHAI references
plus a worked example is a low-cost, high-value refinement the work
group can adopt without disturbing the merged resolution.
