## Summary

FHIR-54211 is a STU1 ballot comment against the AI Transparency on FHIR
Implementation Guide (`hl7.fhir.uv.aitransparency`, owned by Electronic
Health Record). The reporter (Ignacio Jauregui) observes that the
"Observability Factors" table in General Guidance, under the **Process**
factor, mentions "multiple actors, including the human" and the actor's
**role**, but the IG never explains *how* an implementer should
represent that human-in-the-loop interaction (e.g., a clinician
accepting, rejecting, or modifying an AI inference). The ticket asks
the WG to (a) describe how to encode the human-in-the-loop element of
the AI usage in the IG model, and (b) provide a generic workflow plus
an example instance.

The ticket has already been dispositioned `Persuasive with
Modification` and resolved on 2026-04-13. The committed resolution
text states: *"We rewrote the page and now talk about how they can be
authors in Provenance,"* implemented via
[HL7/aitransparency-ig#22](https://github.com/HL7/aitransparency-ig/pull/22).
That is, instead of inventing a new model element for "human in the
loop", the WG chose to push human actors into the existing Provenance
mechanism (Provenance.agent / Provenance.agent.role / who), and to
rewrite the General Guidance narrative accordingly. There are no
linked Jira tickets and no other Jira cross-references; the only
external context is the AI-Transparency-on-FHIR Zulip triage thread
for the January ballot, where this ticket appears in the
"Data Sources & Context" bucket alongside FHIR-54210, FHIR-54233,
FHIR-54239, FHIR-54433, and FHIR-53967.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Confirm and ratify the resolution already implemented by
[HL7/aitransparency-ig#22](https://github.com/HL7/aitransparency-ig/pull/22):
the rewritten `input/pagecontent/general_guidance.md` explicitly
describes how a human reviewer of an AI inference is captured as
`Provenance.agent.who` (the human Practitioner / PractitionerRole)
with `Provenance.agent.role` carrying the nature of the interaction
(author / verifier / informant / etc.), and the AI engine remains a
separate `Provenance.agent` entry with its own role. Augment that
narrative with:

- An explicit "human-in-the-loop" sub-paragraph under the **Process**
  factor in `general_guidance.md` that names the three observed
  interaction outcomes called out by the reporter — *accept*, *reject*,
  *modify* — and maps each to a concrete Provenance shape (e.g.,
  `accept` → human as additional `agent` with role `verifier`;
  `reject` → human as `agent` on a superseding Provenance with
  `activity` set to indicate refutation; `modify` → human as `author`
  on the resulting derived resource, with `Provenance.entity` pointing
  back at the AI-generated source).
- A generic workflow diagram in `input/images/` referenced from the
  same section.
- A worked example instance in `input/fsh/` (e.g.,
  `EX_Provenance_HumanInLoop.fsh`) that materialises the *modify*
  variant end-to-end.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter asked for: a model, a workflow, and
an example. Provenance is the FHIR-base mechanism for representing
authorship, derivation, and multi-agent activity, so reusing it costs
nothing in terms of new conformance surface and is consistent with the
IG's existing decision to make Provenance the spine of AI
attribution (already reflected in `SD_Provenance.fsh`,
`EX_Provenance.fsh`, and the just-merged PR #22). The Zulip triage
classified this comment as "Data Sources & Context", aligning with
adding worked examples and tightening narrative rather than inventing
new structures. Because all changes land in the IG's narrative and
example layers (`input/pagecontent/`, `input/fsh/`), they avoid the
"section-numbering is render-time" and "in-page anchors break easily"
gotchas as long as a single new `### Human in the Loop` heading is
added rather than renaming existing ones.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep PR #22's Provenance-as-authorship narrative, but represent the
*moment of human review* using **AuditEvent** (or a profile of it)
rather than always reaching for Provenance. Add a new
`SD_HumanReviewAudit` profile in `input/fsh/` constraining
`AuditEvent` to record AI-output review events: `agent` = the human
reviewer, `entity` = the AI-generated resource under review,
`outcome` / a small extension to capture *accept / reject / modify*,
and a corresponding `EX_HumanReviewAudit.fsh` example. Update
`general_guidance.md` to draw the line: Provenance records *what was
authored / derived*; AuditEvent records *that a human looked at it
and what they did*. Leave `SD_Provenance.fsh` untouched.

#### Justification

The reporter's three verbs — accept, reject, modify — are
behavioural, transient events more than they are claims of
authorship. AuditEvent is the FHIR resource specifically designed for
"who did what, when, to which resource, with what outcome", which
matches *reject* (where no derived resource exists) better than
Provenance does. Provenance arguably overloads the "reject" case,
since there is no new artifact whose provenance is being asserted.
This split also keeps Provenance instances small and aligned with
their canonical "thing was created/derived" semantics, and it dovetails
with the IG's existing dependency on `hl7.fhir.uv.security-label-ds4p`
(an audit-adjacent profile pack). Trade-off: it adds a second
conformance artifact and a second narrative branch, increasing
implementer surface; and it diverges from the path PR #22 already
locked in, which would force a re-vote on the resolution.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline as **Already Addressed**. The general-guidance page has been
rewritten by PR #22 to describe how human actors are represented as
`Provenance.agent`; no additional new model, workflow, or example
needs to be added in this ballot cycle. Close the ticket without
further IG changes and direct any unresolved nuance (e.g., a worked
example) to a follow-up ticket against the post-ballot release.

#### Justification

This is defensible only on a narrow reading: the literal asks
("describe how to encode" and "Provenance covers it") are arguably
satisfied by PR #22, and the IG is in active ballot triage with
limited bandwidth for new narrative, examples, and diagrams. Declining
keeps the ballot disposition simple and consistent with the
"Persuasive with Modification" tag the WG already applied. It does,
however, leave the reporter's two *secondary* requests — the generic
workflow and the example instance — unsatisfied, and "human in the
loop" is a high-visibility AI-governance topic where reviewers are
likely to expect a fully worked example in STU1.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

PR #22 is already merged and the resolution text on the Jira ticket
explicitly cites it, so the WG's centre of gravity is clearly behind
the Provenance-authorship modelling. Disposition A simply finishes
the job the WG started: keep the Provenance framing, but layer on the
two pieces the reporter explicitly asked for and which the merged PR
does *not* yet include — a named **Human in the Loop** sub-section
that maps *accept / reject / modify* to concrete Provenance shapes,
plus a worked `EX_Provenance_HumanInLoop.fsh` example. All edits
remain inside `input/pagecontent/general_guidance.md` and
`input/fsh/`, fully within the IG's authoring root, with no
cross-repo touch points and no need to renumber sections. Disposition
B has architectural merit (AuditEvent really is a better fit for
*reject*) but it would re-open a resolution the WG already approved
and would expand STU1's conformance surface mid-ballot, which is a
poor trade. Disposition C under-delivers on the *workflow* and
*example instance* asks that the reporter raised by name, which is
likely to attract a follow-up ballot comment and erode confidence in
the IG's AI-governance story.
