## Summary

FHIR-54740 is a January-2026 ballot comment against the AI Transparency
on FHIR IG ("General Guidance" page). The reporter (Brian Postlethwaite)
observed that the page opens with a summary table that is supposed to
preview the sections that follow, but the table's labels do not line up
with the actual sub-headings — specifically, the third sub-heading is
titled "Context" while the corresponding row in the table is "Data
Sources". The reporter also questions the placement of "Operations",
suggesting it might belong under "Process" rather than "Data Sources".

The ticket is currently **Triaged** with a resolution of **Persuasive
with Modification**: per the resolution note, the workgroup decided to
remove the offending table altogether and replace it with a list of
factors that does not mention "Operations" (since the IG, in its current
version, does not cover them). The change has already been implemented
and merged via [HL7/aitransparency-ig#22](https://github.com/HL7/aitransparency-ig/pull/22).

There are no other Jira tickets directly linked to FHIR-54740. It is
one of ~90 January-ballot tickets being triaged together by the AI
Transparency on FHIR workgroup (see the linked Zulip thread), where it
is grouped under "Documentation & Clarity".

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Accept the ticket as a request to make the General Guidance summary
table consistent with the sub-headings that follow it. Concretely, in
`input/pagecontent/general_guidance.md`, edit the existing summary
table so that (a) row 3's label matches the actual third `### …`
heading (rename the row or the heading so the label reads the same in
both places — most naturally renaming the table row from "Data Sources"
to "Context"), and (b) the row that currently reads "Operations" is
either moved under "Process Utilizing AI" or, if Operations is not
covered by the IG in this version, removed from the table entirely.
Re-validate any in-page anchors (`general_guidance.html#…`) referenced
from `index.md`, `use_cases.md`, and `explainer.md` after the rename.

#### Justification

This is the literal ask in the ticket and it is purely editorial — no
profile, value-set, or extension semantics change. The reporter has
identified two specific defects (label mismatch on sub-heading 3 and a
suspect categorization of "Operations") and the cheapest fix that
closes both is to align the table with the headings. Keeping the
summary table also preserves the page's "preview" affordance for
readers who skim. Disposition A is consistent with the way ballot
comments in the **Documentation & Clarity** bucket of the
AI-Transparency Jan-ballot Zulip thread are typically resolved: tighten
the wording, do not redesign the page.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Apply the modification that has already been merged via
[HL7/aitransparency-ig#22](https://github.com/HL7/aitransparency-ig/pull/22):
in `input/pagecontent/general_guidance.md`, **remove the summary table
entirely** and replace it with a bulleted list of "AI observability
factors" that mirrors the actual `### …` headings (Defining the AI
Model, Context of AI Usage, Process Utilizing AI, Tagging, etc.). Do
**not** mention "Operations" in the new list, on the grounds that the
IG (version `1.0.0-ballot`, `STU1 Ballot`) does not cover Operations
yet. Audit and update any cross-page anchors that pointed into the old
table or its rows. Mark the Jira ticket **Persuasive with
Modification** with this PR cited as the resolution.

#### Justification

This is the in-flight resolution and is already implemented on `main`.
It addresses both halves of the reporter's concern simultaneously — the
mismatch (no table, no possible mismatch) and the "Operations
miscategorized" worry (Operations is dropped, deferred to a future
version). It is also less brittle than Disposition A: a hand-aligned
table tends to drift again the next time someone reorders or renames a
sub-heading, whereas a list that is conceptually the same shape as the
heading set is easier to keep in sync. The briefing's "in-page anchors
break easily" gotcha applies either way and must be handled in this
disposition too.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the ticket as **Not Persuasive — already addressed**, on the
grounds that the underlying narrative defect has been resolved by
HL7/aitransparency-ig#22 (which restructured the General Guidance page
and eliminated the mismatched table). Close the ticket without a
further spec change.

#### Justification

Strict reading of "decline" is hard to defend here because the reporter
identified a real editorial inconsistency, but a Decline disposition
could be framed as "the request is moot — the page no longer has the
table the comment is about". The risk in declining is that the workgroup
loses the audit trail of *why* the table was removed (the resolution
note already provides that, however, so the cost is small). This option
is worth recording but is dominated by Disposition B, which captures
the same outcome while honoring the reporter's contribution.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The ballot resolution as written in Jira already maps to Disposition B,
the implementing PR (HL7/aitransparency-ig#22) is merged, and the
cross-cutting "Documentation & Clarity" reconciliation thread on Zulip
treats this as a one-line editorial fix rather than a debate. Going
back to Disposition A (keep the table, just realign it) would re-open
a class of bug — table-vs-heading drift — that the workgroup has
already chosen to remove. Declining (Disposition C) would technically
clear the ticket but discards the contributor's signal and the
already-applied modification. Disposition B should be confirmed by EHR
on the block vote (the ticket carries the `Block-Vote-2` label), with
a note instructing the editor to verify that any
`general_guidance.html#…` anchors referenced from `index.md`,
`use_cases.md`, or `explainer.md` still resolve after the table's
removal — the briefing's "in-page anchors break easily" warning is the
only follow-up risk worth calling out on the floor.
