## Summary

The reporter asks the AI Transparency on FHIR IG (January ballot) to add two
new use cases to the IG narrative:

1. **AI-assisted post-structuring of medical records** — a document is
   initially captured as free text and later structured by an AI process and
   represented using FHIR.
2. **Post-Market Surveillance (PMS) of AI** — using FHIR to document the
   real-world use of an AI system with enough provenance that the
   manufacturer can satisfy regulatory PMS obligations (e.g. EU AI Act,
   FDA Total Product Life Cycle guidance).

In the comment thread, a contributor (JIRAUSER26199) supplies fully drafted
narrative for the PMS use case — naming it "Use Case X: Supporting
Post-Market Surveillance (PMS) of High-Risk AI Systems / AI-Enabled
Devices" — and notes it overlaps with, and could enhance, the IG's existing
use case 3. The proposed narrative leans on the IG's existing model: a
`Device` resource representing the AI/model version, with traceability via
`Provenance.agent` references to that Device, and secondary references via
`Observation.device` and `Composition.author`. John Moehrke (IG author /
EHR WG lead) replied in the ticket that he agrees with both use cases in
principle, believes (1) is already technically supported but not listed as
a use case, and asks for clearer wording on (2). No other Jira tickets are
cross-referenced; the only related discussion thread is the EHR WG's
Jan-ballot triage roll-up on Zulip.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add **two** new use cases to the AI Transparency on FHIR IG narrative
(Use Cases page in the IG, the same page that currently hosts use cases
1–3):

1. **"AI-assisted post-structuring of clinical documents"** — describe the
   workflow where a `Composition` / `DocumentReference` is initially
   captured as free narrative and is subsequently structured by an AI
   process. The narrative should name the FHIR mechanisms the IG already
   relies on: a `Device` representing the AI model/version, a
   `Provenance` whose `agent` references the Device and whose `target`
   references the structured Composition (and any newly created
   `Observation`/section content), and `Composition.author` (or
   `Observation.device`) references to the same Device.
2. **"Use Case X: Supporting Post-Market Surveillance (PMS) of High-Risk
   AI Systems / AI-Enabled Devices"** — adopt JIRAUSER26199's drafted
   text from the 2026-02-14 comment, lightly edited for IG voice.
   Anchor the use case on the EU AI Act and FDA TPLC framing, list the
   PMS activities (real-world performance monitoring, drift detection,
   misperformance/adverse-event identification, sub-group performance,
   ongoing compliance), and explicitly enumerate the traceability paths
   the IG already supports: `Provenance.agent → Device`,
   `Observation.device → Device`, and `Composition.author → Device`.

No new profiles, extensions, or constraints would be added — these are
purely narrative additions. Optionally, cross-link the new PMS use case
to existing case 3 (which already covers a related "rectify AI
mistakes" angle) so readers see the relationship.

#### Justification

The reporter has supplied fully drafted text for the PMS use case in the
2026-02-14 comment, addressing the only specific gap John Moehrke flagged
("not clear enough for me"). For use case (1), Moehrke himself states it
is "indeed already included technically although not listed as a
use-case", so the only outstanding work is narrative. The
cross-reference engine confirms the proposed PMS narrative reuses
elements that the IG already centres on (`Provenance.agent`,
`Observation.device`, `Composition.author`), so accepting introduces no
new modelling work and no breaking change. Adding the use cases also
serves the IG's regulatory framing for the May/September ballot cycles,
where EU AI Act and FDA PMS expectations are increasingly important to
implementers.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept use case (1) as-requested (post-structuring narrative), but treat
use case (2) as a **scope expansion** rather than a simple narrative add.
Specifically:

- Add the post-structuring use case as in Disposition A.
- For PMS, **rewrite and broaden existing Use Case 3** ("AI mistake /
  rectification") to explicitly enumerate the PMS lifecycle (drift,
  sub-group performance, adverse events) instead of authoring a
  standalone "Use Case X". Add a short non-normative "Regulatory
  context" callout on the Use Cases page that names the EU AI Act and
  FDA TPLC, but does not bind the IG to specific regulatory citations.
- Add a one-paragraph "Traceability pattern for PMS" note in the IG's
  modelling section (not in Use Cases) that lists the
  `Provenance.agent → Device`, `Observation.device → Device`, and
  `Composition.author → Device` paths — so the reusable pattern lives
  with the model, not buried in a use case.

#### Justification

JIRAUSER26199 explicitly says PMS "is relevant to but may not [be]
limited to current case 3" — i.e. there is real overlap. Folding PMS
into case 3 (rather than adding "Use Case X") avoids two problems:
(a) two adjacent use cases that read very similarly, which tends to
attract follow-up ballot comments asking why they aren't merged;
(b) committing the IG to specific regulatory citations (EU AI Act, FDA
TPLC) inside a numbered use case, which can age badly if those
frameworks shift. Putting the traceability pattern in the modelling
section also makes it discoverable to implementers who are not reading
the Use Cases page first — which is the primary audience for
post-market surveillance work.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline as **not actionable in current form** for the January ballot
disposition, with guidance: the post-structuring use case (1) should be
deferred on the grounds that the underlying mechanism is already
documented in the IG (per Moehrke's 2026-01-26 comment) and a non-
substantive narrative reorganisation does not warrant a use-case add at
this stage; the PMS use case (2) should be redirected to a future ballot
cycle where the IG can do a coordinated regulatory-context pass
(EU AI Act, FDA TPLC, IMDRF SaMD) rather than a one-off mention. Close
as "Persuasive with Mod – Future" with a note recommending the reporter
re-file (2) once the WG has a regulatory-framing strategy.

#### Justification

A defensible decline rests on two points: (1) Moehrke has already
asserted that case (1) is technically covered by the existing IG, so
adding it as a use case would be editorial rather than substantive;
(2) committing the AI Transparency IG to PMS framing without a
considered regulatory strategy risks the IG being read as making
regulatory claims it cannot back. However, given that the reporter has
already supplied drafted text for (2) and the Highest priority of the
ticket, this disposition is the weakest of the three.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The reporter's request is legitimate and the supplied text is high
quality, so outright decline (C) is not warranted given the Highest
priority and the IG author's own agreement with the use cases. However,
straight acceptance (A) creates two narrative problems: a new "Use Case
X" that the reporter himself acknowledges overlaps with existing case 3,
and an in-use-case commitment to specific regulatory frameworks (EU AI
Act, FDA TPLC) that the WG should think about coherently across the IG
rather than land via a single ticket. Disposition B captures everything
the reporter is asking for — the post-structuring narrative is added
verbatim; the PMS substance is incorporated into case 3 with the
reporter's own wording where possible; the regulatory framing appears
once, as a deliberate callout; and the reusable
`Provenance.agent`/`Observation.device`/`Composition.author` traceability
pattern moves to the modelling section where downstream implementers
will actually find it. This is also the disposition most likely to
satisfy the WG's Jan-ballot triage classification of this ticket as a
"Use Cases & Examples" item rather than a profile/model change.
