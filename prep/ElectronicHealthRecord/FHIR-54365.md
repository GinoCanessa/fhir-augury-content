## Summary

FHIR-54365 is a documentation-structure change request against the AI
Transparency on FHIR IG (`hl7.fhir.uv.aitransparency`, STU1 Ballot,
v1.0.0-ballot). The reporter (Martin Rosner) finds the rendered General
Guidance page hard to follow and proposes that the rendered "section
2.3" (Tagging) and "section 2.4" (Provenance) be demoted so they sit as
sub-sections under what he reads as the intended parent — "section 2.2
AI Usage Implementation Methods" — i.e., reframe Tagging and Provenance
as the *mechanisms* by which the AI-usage information described in 2.2
is conveyed, rather than as peer top-level sections.

The cross-reference graph pulls in no other Jira tickets directly; the
only siblings are the bulk Jan-ballot triage thread on Zulip (where
this ticket was grouped under "Documentation & Clarity" with ~30 other
narrative-clarity items) and the GitHub PR that implemented the agreed
change (`HL7/aitransparency-ig#22`, "We restructured the page to
improve it"). The ticket is currently marked Triaged with resolution
**Persuasive with Modification**, so the workgroup has already accepted
the spirit of the request and folded it into the broader page
restructure rather than a literal one-for-one demotion.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In `HL7/aitransparency-ig`'s `input/pagecontent/general_guidance.md`,
adopt a literal demotion: keep `### AI Usage Implementation Methods`
(introducing it if absent, immediately after `### AI Observability
Factors`) as a single parent `###` heading, and demote the existing
`### Tagging` and the existing `### …` heading covering Provenance to
`#### Tagging` and `#### Provenance` directly underneath it. Push their
existing `####` children down one level (`####` → `#####`) so the
hierarchy stays consistent. Audit and update every intra-IG link of the
form `general_guidance.html#tagging` and `general_guidance.html#provenance`
(scan `index.md`, `use_cases.md`, `explainer.md`, the `menu:` block in
`sushi-config.yaml`, any FSH `^extension[…].valueUrl` definitions, and
all in-page `(#tagging)` / `(#provenance)` anchors) to point at the new
headings; if anchor stability matters, add explicit HTML anchors (`<a
name="tagging"/>`) immediately above the demoted headings so existing
external citations keep working.

#### Justification

This is the smallest change that satisfies the reporter literally:
"just need to demote those two sections." It preserves all existing
prose and examples — the only edits are heading levels, the optional
insertion of one new parent heading, and link/anchor housekeeping.
Reframing Tagging and Provenance as *implementation methods* under "AI
Usage Implementation Methods" matches the IG's own framing earlier in
the page, where Tagging is described as "1st level observability" and
Provenance as the carrier of "2nd level observability" — i.e., they
are the two mechanisms by which AI usage is conveyed. Keeping it
purely structural also keeps the change low-risk for the STU1 Ballot
window and avoids inviting scope creep into prose rewrites.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt the broader restructure already shipped in
`HL7/aitransparency-ig#22` and record FHIR-54365 as
**Persuasive with Modification**. Rather than a mechanical
demotion of two `###` headings, the General Guidance page is
reorganised end-to-end so that the two-level-observability framing
(Tagging vs. AI Observability Factors) drives the heading hierarchy:
keep "AI Observability Factors" as the section that introduces Model /
Context / Process, and present Tagging and Provenance as the
*delivery mechanisms* for those factors — Tagging for 1st-level
observability and Provenance threaded through the Model / Context /
Process subsections for 2nd-level observability. Confirm in the
disposition write-up that the merged structure (the one observed in
the current `main` clone: `### AI Observability Factors`, `###
Tagging`, `### Defining the AI Model`, `### Context of AI Usage`, `###
Process Utilizing AI`, `### PDF interpreted by AI into FHIR`) fully
addresses the reporter's complaint, and link to PR #22 as the
implementation.

#### Justification

This is what the workgroup has, in fact, already done — the resolution
text says "We restructured the page to improve it" and points at PR
#22. The merged shape is more ambitious than the literal request:
instead of just nesting Tagging and Provenance under a new parent, it
reorganises the page around the IG's own two-level-observability
model, which is the conceptual scaffolding the ticket was reaching for
when it asked for "AI Usage Implementation Methods" as the umbrella.
Recording the disposition as Persuasive with Modification (a) credits
the reporter for surfacing the underlying clarity problem, (b) avoids
re-litigating heading levels at the workgroup table when the page has
already changed, and (c) keeps the audit trail honest: the ticket
asked for X, the WG agreed with the intent and shipped Y, both are
documented.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Reject the demotion request as a no-op restyle and close FHIR-54365 as
**Not Persuasive**, on the basis that the General Guidance page
already opens with an explicit two-level-observability framing
(Tagging = 1st level, AI Observability Factors / Provenance = 2nd
level) before the `### Tagging` heading appears, so the rendered
section numbering is not actually misleading. No source change to
`input/pagecontent/general_guidance.md`.

#### Justification

A defensible decline rationale would be: the reporter's premise —
that "section 2.3 Tagging" and "section 2.4 Provenance" are misplaced
peers — depends on render-time numbering rather than the source
narrative, and the source narrative does explain the relationship
between them in its first paragraphs. Heading shuffles also cost the
IG anchor stability against external citations that may already
reference `general_guidance.html#tagging`, which the briefing flags as
a known gotcha for this kind of edit.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The workgroup has already chosen this path: the ticket is marked
*Persuasive with Modification* and the resolution explicitly cites
PR #22 ("We restructured the page to improve it") in
`HL7/aitransparency-ig`. The repo briefing confirms that the live
`main` (clone HEAD `b8d931d`) carries that restructured General
Guidance page — the `###` headings are now organised around the
observability-factors framing rather than a flat list of mechanisms,
which is exactly the conceptual fix the reporter was reaching for. A
literal Accept-As-Requested (Disposition A) would either undo or
duplicate the merged work and force a second round of anchor
housekeeping, while Decline (Disposition C) is hard to defend given
the reporter's complaint clearly resonated enough for the WG to
restructure the page. Disposition B captures the actual outcome,
gives the reporter credit, and leaves a clean audit trail tying the
ticket to the merged PR.
