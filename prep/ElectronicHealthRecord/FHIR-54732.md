## Summary

The ticket asks the AI Transparency (FHIR) IG authors to reword a use-case
sentence so that the value proposition for capturing AI-generation metadata
is framed in terms of *informing* training decisions rather than as a flat
rule against training on AI-generated content. Specifically, the reporter
proposes replacing "When AI trains on clinical content, it should avoid
training on data that was generated by AI." with "It is useful to know if
clinical content was generated by AI when considering training a model. If
the information was AI generated, it should not be used in training a
model." The substantive policy (do not train on AI-generated data) is
preserved, but the rewording makes explicit the prerequisite — that
consumers of the data need to *know* the content is AI-generated — which is
the actual use case the IG enables. No cross-referenced Jira tickets were
returned for this issue; the only inbound reference is the AI Transparency
triage roll-up thread on Zulip.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the reporter's wording verbatim in the AI Transparency (FHIR) IG
use-case narrative: replace the sentence "When AI trains on clinical
content, it should avoid training on data that was generated by AI." with
"It is useful to know if clinical content was generated by AI when
considering training a model. If the information was AI generated, it
should not be used in training a model." This is a narrative-only change
in the IG's use-case page — no StructureDefinitions, ValueSets, examples,
or conformance artifacts are affected. The ticket is already marked
**Resolution: Persuasive**, consistent with accepting the proposed
rewording.

#### Justification

The ticket is a pure documentation/clarity change in the "Use Cases"
section of the IG, with no downstream profile or terminology impact. The
proposed wording explicitly links the IG's technical purpose (letting
consumers *know* content is AI-generated) to the downstream policy (do
not train on AI-generated content), which is a clearer articulation of
why implementers should care about the IG's tagging/provenance
machinery. The triage roll-up on Zulip classifies this with the 31 other
Documentation & Clarity issues and does not flag any objection. Accepting
verbatim is the lowest-risk path and matches the existing Persuasive
resolution.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the intent but refine the wording to keep the policy statement
normatively softer and the IG's scope statement crisper — e.g., "Knowing
whether clinical content was generated by AI is useful when deciding what
data to use for model training; AI-generated content generally should not
be used to train other models." This keeps the two ideas the reporter
wants (knowability first, training-exclusion second) but (a) replaces the
bare imperative "should not be used" with a non-normative "generally
should not be used" so the IG does not appear to levy a training-policy
requirement it cannot enforce, and (b) applies the same edit to any
parallel occurrences of the original sentence elsewhere in the IG (e.g.,
overview, executive summary, or any example vignette) so the narrative
stays consistent.

#### Justification

The IG's job is to make AI-generation *discoverable* via FHIR; downstream
training policy is properly the remit of AI governance frameworks and
organizational policy, not an interoperability IG. Softening the
prescriptive clause avoids the IG being read as setting a training-policy
rule. Sweeping for other occurrences protects against the exact
inconsistency the reporter flagged re-appearing elsewhere. Trade-off vs.
Disposition A: slightly more editorial work and a minor deviation from
the reporter's literal text, in exchange for tighter scoping of the IG's
voice.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the rewording on the grounds that the original sentence adequately
conveys the intent within the use-case section and that the IG's current
narrative has already been balloted and reviewed in its existing form.
Close the ticket as Not Persuasive and leave the text unchanged.

#### Justification

Defensible only if the workgroup concludes the original sentence is
already unambiguous to the IG's target audience and that further editing
risks churn across translations/downstream references. However, this is
a weak rationale given the reporter is an EHR-WG participant citing
actual reader confusion, the change is small, and the IG is still in
ballot (Jan 2026). Declining would also contradict the ticket's current
Persuasive resolution.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Accept as requested. The ticket is already marked Persuasive, the change
is confined to IG narrative in the Use Cases section, there is no
downstream profile or terminology impact, and the reporter's wording
cleanly separates the IG's in-scope contribution (knowability of
AI-generated content) from the downstream consequence (don't train on
it). Disposition B's softening is attractive but is best raised as a
separate editorial pass on the IG's normative voice rather than held
against this specific ticket; doing it here risks re-opening a resolved
Persuasive item for a stylistic refinement. Disposition C is not
supportable given the reporter's concrete rewording and the ticket's
existing resolution.
