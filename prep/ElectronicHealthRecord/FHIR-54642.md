## Summary

The reporter raises a concern about AI systems retaining the data they
ingest for interpretation, and asks whether the disclosure surfaced by
the AI Transparency on FHIR IG should also cover **how long (if at all)
an AI keeps ingested data in its model**. The ticket does not propose a
specific element, profile, or extension — it is a scoping question
about whether model-training-data retention belongs in the IG's
disclosure surface. The current resolution (`Not Persuasive`) reflects
the WG view that this IG is about *use* of AI and tracking of
data created/updated by AI, not about how training models are
maintained.

No other Jira tickets are explicitly linked to or cross-referenced
from FHIR-54642. The only cross-reference is a triage roll-up post in
the **AI Transparency on FHIR > IG - Jan ballot** Zulip topic that
files this ticket under the *Use Cases & Examples* category alongside
peers FHIR-54731, FHIR-54706, FHIR-54644, FHIR-54643, FHIR-54583,

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Extend the AI Transparency on FHIR IG's disclosure surface to include
**model data-retention** information about the AI service used. In
practice this would mean adding (a) narrative guidance to the IG
describing what implementers SHOULD disclose about ingested-data
retention by the AI/model (e.g., whether ingested PHI is retained in
the model, in logs, in a prompt cache, or for retraining; for how long;
under what controls), and (b) a structured carrier — for example, a
new optional extension on the AI/algorithm `Device`/`DeviceDefinition`
or on the `Provenance` produced by an AI act — capturing
`dataRetentionPolicy` (CodeableConcept), `retentionPeriod` (Duration),
and `usedForTraining` (boolean). The IG would also add example
disclosures and a use case showing a consumer/EHR reading the
retention disclosure before sending data to the AI.

#### Justification

Patient/consumer transparency around how AI handles their data is
exactly the kind of trust signal the IG aims to surface, and the
reporter's concern aligns with broader regulatory direction (e.g., HHS
guidance, EU AI Act transparency obligations, state-level health-AI
laws). If the IG asserts that it makes AI use "transparent" but is
silent on whether the AI keeps the data, it leaves a gap a clinician
or patient would reasonably expect it to close. Modeling retention as
an *optional* extension keeps the change additive and non-breaking.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Do not add structured retention elements to this IG. Instead, add a
short **"Out of Scope / Related Concerns"** subsection (or expand the
existing scope statement) in the IG narrative that:

1. Explicitly acknowledges model-training data retention as an
   important and frequently-raised concern;
2. States that this IG covers *use of AI and tracking of data
   created/updated by AI* and does not standardize how AI training
   corpora are managed; and
3. Cross-references where retention/governance is appropriately
   handled — e.g., HL7 Privacy & Security WG materials, FHIR
   `Consent` for purpose-of-use restrictions, organizational BAAs and
   model cards, and any forthcoming HL7 work on AI governance.

Optionally, add a non-normative pointer to existing FHIR mechanisms
(`Consent`, `Contract`, `Provenance.policy`) that an implementer
could already use today if they wish to expose retention terms.

#### Justification

This addresses the reporter's underlying worry — *that the disclosure
feels incomplete* — without expanding the IG's normative footprint
into AI governance, which is a much larger problem space owned (or
co-owned) by other WGs and external regulators. It is cheap, ballot-
cycle-friendly, preserves the IG's tight scope (which the WG has
explicitly defended in the resolution text), and gives readers a
defensible answer when they ask "what about retention?". It also
avoids creating an extension that implementers would be forced to
populate without consensus on a value set or on what "retention" even
means for stateless inference vs. fine-tuning vs. RAG caches.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Confirm the existing **Not Persuasive** resolution as-is. Close
FHIR-54642 with the rationale already on the ticket: this IG governs
the *use* of AI and the *tracking of data created/updated* by AI; it
does not govern AI training, model lifecycle, or how a model retains
ingested data. Direct future requests on training-data retention to
HL7 Privacy & Security and to relevant regulatory frameworks rather
than to this IG.

#### Justification

The ticket itself is exploratory ("Not sure where this would go
but…") and does not propose a concrete artifact change. The IG's
scope is intentionally narrow — provenance/disclosure of AI-touched
data — and stretching it to cover model training/retention would
import a large, unsettled, partly-regulatory problem space that the
WG is not chartered to solve in this ballot cycle. Existing FHIR
mechanisms (`Consent`, `Contract`, `Provenance.policy`) and external
artifacts (model cards, BAAs) already provide places to express
retention terms when needed, so declining does not leave implementers
without options. The resolution text is also already aligned with the
WG's stated scope, so reaffirming it is internally consistent.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The reporter's concern is legitimate and predictable from anyone reading
the IG ("if you're telling me AI was used, am I also being told what it
keeps?"), so a flat decline (Disposition C) — though defensible and
consistent with the current resolution — risks the same comment
reappearing every ballot cycle and erodes the perception that the IG
takes transparency seriously. Disposition A overreaches: training-data
retention is genuinely a different problem from AI-use disclosure,
spans multiple WGs and regulators, and lacks the value-set and
modeling consensus needed to ship a normative element this cycle.
Disposition B threads the needle: it keeps the IG's scope crisp
(matching the existing resolution rationale), gives the reporter a
substantive answer in the spec rather than just on the ticket, and
points implementers at existing FHIR mechanisms (`Consent`,
`Contract`, `Provenance.policy`) plus the appropriate WGs for the
governance side. The WG can adopt B with a small narrative edit and
revisit structured retention disclosure in a future version once the
broader HL7 AI-governance picture matures.
