## Summary

Reporter Celine Lefebvre asks the AI Transparency on FHIR IG authors to
rephrase guidance that frames the requirement as "distinguishing between
AI-used and not-AI-used" content. The reporter argues that the practical
need is simply to clearly identify *where AI was used*, and that calling
out the negative case ("where it wasn't used") is unnecessary and makes the
text feel roundabout. The ticket also notes that the word "distinguishes"
appears to be a typo wherever it occurs in this context. The same phrasing
reportedly recurs in more than one place in the IG, so the requested change
is a small, narrative-level wording fix repeated wherever the
"distinguish/distinguishes" language is used. No related Jira tickets were
returned by cross-reference, and the only related discussion is a triage
roll-up Zulip thread that bucketed this ticket under "Documentation &
Clarity" along with ~30 other January-ballot comments.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the reporter's editorial direction throughout the AI Transparency on
FHIR IG: wherever the IG currently says (or implies) that the goal is to
"distinguish between" AI-generated/AI-used content and non-AI content,
rephrase to a positive-only formulation such as "clearly identify where AI
was used" / "record when AI was used." Concretely:

1. Audit every occurrence of "distinguish", "distinguishes", and
   "distinguishing" in the IG narrative (introduction, principles, usage
   pages, and any profile-level guidance prose).
2. Replace each occurrence with wording that frames the requirement as
   *recording / identifying use of AI*, matching John Moehrke's framing in
   the comment ("The IG is focused on recording when AI was used.").
3. Fix the spelling/usage error the reporter flagged as a typo wherever
   it appears.
4. Leave the underlying conformance requirements (which elements,
   extensions, or profiles carry the AI provenance information) unchanged
   — this is a narrative-only change.

#### Justification

The ticket is a narrative-clarity request, not a model change. The
maintainer's own comment confirms that the IG's intent matches the
reporter's framing ("recording when AI was used"), so the current
"distinguish between" wording understates the actual design intent and
invites confusion (it suggests implementers must somehow assert the
*negative*, that AI was *not* used, which is neither testable nor
required). Rephrasing in line with the maintainer's stated intent improves
clarity at zero cost to interoperability and at zero risk of breaking
existing implementations. As an editorial-only change it is also
appropriate to apply during ballot reconciliation rather than escalating
to a substantive change.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rephrase as in Disposition A, but explicitly preserve a brief note (one or
two sentences in the IG's introduction or "How to use this IG" section)
acknowledging that the *absence* of an AI-use marker on content is
significant — i.e., that consumers of records SHOULD treat unmarked content
as "not known to be AI-generated" rather than as a positive assertion of
human authorship. Keep the primary requirement framed positively
("identify where AI was used"), and replace the offending "distinguishes"
wording, but retain a short paragraph clarifying how implementers and
consumers should reason about *unmarked* content.

#### Justification

The reporter's critique is well-taken (the positive framing is clearer),
but completely dropping any treatment of the "no marker" case can leave
implementers uncertain about how to interpret records that lack AI
provenance markers — particularly in mixed-provenance environments where
both legacy human-authored and new AI-assisted content coexist. A short
"absence of marker means the source is not asserted to involve AI" note
addresses Celine's clarity concern while preventing a different class of
confusion among downstream consumers. The trade-off vs. Disposition A is a
small amount of additional narrative for a meaningful gain in implementer
guidance.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark FHIR-54641 as "Not Persuasive" on the basis that the ticket is
purely stylistic, that the maintainer has stated the intent matches
the existing language, and that no implementer-affecting change is
required. Optionally fix only the standalone typo on "distinguishes" if
one exists, but leave the surrounding "distinguish between" framing in
place.

#### Justification

A defensible decline rests on the argument that the existing "distinguish
between" language, while perhaps slightly verbose, is not technically
inaccurate; that the reporter herself describes the concern as possibly
"splitting hairs"; and that re-litigating wording during ballot
reconciliation can churn the document without improving conformance.
However, this disposition is hard to defend strongly: the IG maintainer's
own comment effectively endorses the reporter's framing ("recording when
AI was used"), which undercuts the rationale for keeping the existing
wording. Decline is therefore included for completeness but is the
weakest of the three options.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter raises a small but legitimate clarity issue, the IG
maintainer's own comment ("The IG is focused on recording when AI was
used.") effectively agrees with the reporter's positive framing, and the
change is narrative-only with no impact on profiles, extensions, or
conformance. The Zulip triage explicitly bucketed this ticket as
"Documentation & Clarity," signaling that the work group is already
prepared to handle it as an editorial fix. Disposition B is a reasonable
hedge if the work group is worried about implementers misreading unmarked
content, but it can be applied later as a follow-up clarification ticket
without blocking this fix. Disposition C is hard to justify given the
maintainer's comment. Recommend accepting as requested and rolling the
rewording (and the typo fix) into the ballot-reconciliation editorial
pass for the AI Transparency on FHIR IG.
