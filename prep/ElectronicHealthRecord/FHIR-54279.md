## Summary

The submitter is asking the AI Transparency on FHIR IG to add explicit
reader guidance that AI attribution for a clinical resource (such as an
`Observation`) is asserted **via a separate `Provenance` resource**, not
embedded inside the clinical resource itself. Because `Provenance.target`
is a one-way reference (Provenance → Observation), consumers cannot tell
from the clinical resource alone that AI was involved; they must
discover the Provenance via search (e.g., `Provenance?target=...`) or
receive it co-bundled with the clinical resource. The ticket therefore
asks the IG to (a) state the "Provenance is authoritative, not embedded"
principle plainly, and (b) describe how a consumer is expected to find
that Provenance, calling out co-bundling as one viable solution to the
one-way-linkage problem.

The single cross-reference for this ticket is the Jan 2026 ballot triage
post in the **AI Transparency on FHIR** Zulip stream, where John
Moehrke groups this issue with nine other "Provenance & Tagging" ballot
items (FHIR-54707, FHIR-54281, FHIR-54280, FHIR-54279, FHIR-54255,
FHIR-54245, FHIR-54244, FHIR-54242, FHIR-54141, FHIR-53973). FHIR-54280
and FHIR-54281 in particular are sibling tickets from the same reporter
on adjacent Provenance/tagging concerns and should likely be dispositioned
together.

## Details

**Description:**

> Communicate to the reader of this IG that the Provenance Resource is
> the authoritative evidence that this resource (e.g. observation) was
> influenced by AI, and this assertion is not embedded in the clinical
> resource.

> Provenance via search or co-bundling.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a short, explicit "How AI involvement is asserted and discovered"
subsection to the AI Transparency on FHIR IG narrative (most naturally
inside the existing Provenance / "Recording AI Involvement" page in the
IG, not on individual profile pages). The subsection should state:

1. **Authoritative assertion.** Evidence that a clinical resource (e.g.,
   `Observation`, `DiagnosticReport`, `MedicationRequest`) was produced
   or influenced by AI is conveyed by a separate `Provenance` resource
   conforming to the IG's AI-Provenance profile, **not** by extensions,
   tags, or elements embedded in the clinical resource itself.
2. **Direction of linkage.** The link is one-way:
   `Provenance.target → Observation`. The clinical resource has no
   pointer back to its Provenance, so the absence of an in-line AI flag
   does **not** imply absence of AI involvement.
3. **How a consumer discovers it.** List the supported discovery
   mechanisms with brief examples:
   - Reverse search:
     `GET [base]/Provenance?target=Observation/{id}`
   - `_revinclude` on the clinical query:
     `GET [base]/Observation/{id}?_revinclude=Provenance:target`
   - **Co-bundling**: when a clinical resource is exchanged in a
     `Bundle` (document, message, transaction, or search response), the
     producer SHOULD include the relevant `Provenance` resource(s) in
     the same Bundle so the consumer does not have to perform a
     follow-up query.
4. **Implementer obligation.** A SHOULD-level statement that producers
   exchanging single resources outside a server context (e.g., bulk
   files, payload-only API responses) co-bundle Provenance with the
   target resource.

#### Justification

This is the smallest narrative-only change that fully answers the
ticket: it states the "Provenance is authoritative, not embedded"
principle the reporter asks for and addresses Moehrke's follow-up
("some few sentences could be added describing the various ways one
discovers Provenance"). It is consistent with the FHIR core Provenance
guidance and with how other IGs (e.g., US Core, Da Vinci) document
Provenance discovery, so the IG is not inventing a new pattern. It is
narrative-only, so it has no impact on conformance resources, examples,
or downstream tooling, and it can land in the Jan-ballot reconciliation
without triggering re-balloting of profiles. Grouping the disposition
with the sibling Provenance & Tagging tickets (FHIR-54280, FHIR-54281)
keeps the IG's discovery story coherent.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt the narrative addition from Disposition A **and** strengthen the
discoverability story with a normative conformance hook so the principle
is not purely advisory:

- Add a SHALL-level conformance rule on the IG's "AI-influenced
  exchange" capability/actor: when an AI-influenced clinical resource
  is delivered inside a `Bundle` (document, message, transaction, or
  search-set), the producer **SHALL** include the corresponding
  AI-Provenance resource(s) in the same Bundle.
- Add (or update) a worked example Bundle that contains an
  `Observation` plus its AI `Provenance`, referenced from the new
  narrative subsection.
- Optionally add an `_revinclude=Provenance:target` example to the IG's
  search guidance page.

#### Justification

Disposition A relies on the reader to act on a SHOULD; in practice,
producers frequently strip Provenance from outbound bundles, which
re-creates exactly the discoverability gap the reporter is highlighting.
A conformance hook plus a concrete example Bundle gives validators and
implementers something to enforce against, not just a paragraph to
read. The trade-off is scope: a SHALL on Bundle composition is a
substantive change that may need re-ballot review and could conflict
with sibling tickets (FHIR-54280 / FHIR-54281) that may propose their
own discovery mechanisms — those should be reconciled together. Moehrke's
"trust the reader to know FHIR basics" concern is addressed because the
extra material is normative + example, not a tutorial.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request as **out of scope / already addressed by base
FHIR**. Discovery of Provenance via reverse reference, `_revinclude`,
and co-bundling is described in the core FHIR Provenance resource page
and in general Bundle/search guidance; the AI Transparency IG does not
need to re-document FHIR mechanics. Close as "Persuasive with
Modification — no change" or "Not Persuasive" with a pointer to the
core spec sections.

#### Justification

This mirrors the reviewer comment from John Moehrke that the IG's
audience can be assumed to understand FHIR basics. Repeating core-spec
mechanics in every IG bloats the narrative and creates a maintenance
burden if the core guidance evolves. If the only deliverable is "a few
sentences," it is debatable whether the IG is the right venue versus a
link to the existing core Provenance documentation.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is fundamentally a documentation gap, not a model gap, and
Disposition A closes it with the lowest possible cost and risk: a short
narrative subsection on the existing Provenance page that names the
authoritative-assertion principle, the one-way-link consequence, and
the three concrete discovery mechanisms (reverse search, `_revinclude`,
co-bundling). It directly accommodates the reviewer's
"few-sentences-on-discovery" compromise from the 2026-02-16 comment, so
it should land easily in reconciliation. Disposition B is the right
follow-on if the workgroup decides during reconciliation that
co-bundling needs to be enforceable rather than advisory — but that
decision is better made jointly with sibling tickets FHIR-54280 and
FHIR-54281 in the same Provenance & Tagging bucket, and should not block
this ticket. Disposition C under-serves the ticket: even if the
mechanics are "FHIR basics," the *policy* — that AI attribution lives in
Provenance and is not embedded in clinical resources — is unique to this
IG and must be stated here.
