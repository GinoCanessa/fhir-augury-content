## Summary

The reporter notes that the AI Transparency on FHIR IG defines an "AI
algorithm" extremely broadly — "any computer-based logic that touches health
data in a way that might change the understanding of the data downstream" —
which would encompass rule-based systems and traditional imaging algorithms,
even though the IG's data model and use cases appear tailored to machine
learning and large language models (LLMs). The ticket asks the workgroup
either to **narrow the definition** so it actually matches the ML/LLM-shaped
artifacts in the IG, **or** to **broaden the model** so it genuinely
accommodates rule-based systems — in which case it should be harmonized with
the artifacts produced by the Clinical Decision Support (CDS) work group to
avoid overlap or contradiction. There are no explicitly-linked or
cross-referenced Jira tickets to fold in; the only cross-reference is to the
AI Transparency IG ballot triage post on Zulip, which classifies this ticket
under "Documentation & Clarity" alongside ~30 other comments on the same
ballot.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Narrow the IG's definition of "AI algorithm" so that it explicitly matches
the artifacts the IG actually defines (model card / model metadata /
training-data provenance / inference-time transparency profiles oriented
to ML and LLMs). Concretely, replace the current broad sentence
("…any computer-based logic that touches health data in a way that might
change the understanding of the data downstream.") with a definition
restricted to systems whose behavior is **learned from data** (statistical,
machine-learned, or generative models, including LLMs), and explicitly
exclude deterministic rule-based decision logic and conventional
deterministic imaging algorithms from scope. Add a short non-normative
"Out of scope" subsection in the IG's introduction/scope page that lists
rule-based CDS, hand-coded image processing, and similar deterministic
logic, and points readers at the CDS work group's existing artifacts
(CPG-on-FHIR, PlanDefinition, etc.) for those use cases.

#### Justification

This aligns the *definition* with what the IG actually delivers. The
reporter's concern is that the current text writes a check the artifacts
don't cash: rule-based systems and traditional imaging algorithms have
none of the bias, training-data provenance, or generative-output concerns
the IG's transparency profiles are designed to expose. Narrowing the
definition removes that mismatch, avoids implementers shoehorning
deterministic logic into model-card-shaped profiles, and respects existing
CDS-on-FHIR territory (PlanDefinition / ActivityDefinition /
clinical-reasoning) without duplicating it. It also gives a clean answer
to the reporter and resolves the ticket as documentation/clarity, which
matches the bucket triage placed it in.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the broad working definition of "AI algorithm" *but* split the IG's
model into a small **common transparency core** (provenance, intended use,
contraindications, responsible party, version) that is honestly applicable
to any "computer-based logic that touches health data" — including
rule-based and imaging algorithms — and a set of **ML/LLM-specific
extensions/profiles** (training-data characterization, evaluation metrics,
bias assessment, generative-output disclosure) that only apply to learned
systems. Add a section in the IG that explicitly maps the rule-based
slice to the CDS work group's artifacts (CPG-on-FHIR, PlanDefinition,
Library, ArtifactAssessment) and harmonizes terminology with them, and
liaise with CDS before publishing to confirm the boundary. Effectively the
IG keeps its broad scope statement and answers Ignacio's "expand the
model" branch.

#### Justification

This is the reporter's second suggested branch ("expand the model… with
harmonization with CDS"). It avoids prematurely shrinking the IG's
applicability — transparency about non-learned algorithms that
nevertheless influence clinical interpretation (e.g., a deterministic
imaging post-processing step) is a real implementer need — while
acknowledging that the *bias / training-data* parts of the model are
genuinely ML-specific. Splitting "core transparency" from
"ML-only transparency" is a well-established IG pattern and avoids forcing
implementers of rule-based systems to populate ML-shaped slots with N/A.
The down-side is real coordination cost with CDS WG and a larger
publication scope; that is the principal trade-off vs. Disposition A.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Leave the current definition as written and resolve the ticket as
"Persuasive with Modification — no normative change." Add only a brief
clarifying paragraph to the IG scope page explaining that the broad
definition is *intentional* (transparency obligations apply to any
algorithmic logic that affects clinical interpretation, regardless of
implementation technique), and that the IG's specific profiles are
*examples* of how to satisfy those obligations for the most common modern
case (ML/LLM), not an exhaustive set.

#### Justification

Defensible only if the workgroup believes the definition–artifact
mismatch the reporter identifies is a feature, not a bug — i.e., the IG
is deliberately staking out a broad transparency principle and inviting
future profile work for non-ML logic. The cost is that implementers of
rule-based or deterministic imaging systems will be told they're "in
scope" but find no profiles that fit, which creates exactly the kind of
ambiguity the reporter is flagging. For that reason this disposition is
the weakest of the three but is included as the procedural option to
reject the change.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A is the cleanest answer to the ticket as written and to the
"Documentation & Clarity" bucket the triage post placed it in. The IG's
delivered artifacts are visibly ML/LLM-shaped, and the reporter's core
complaint is a definition–artifact mismatch — narrowing the definition
fixes that mismatch surgically, in the introduction/scope text, without
expanding the IG's surface area or forcing a coordination dependency on
CDS WG before publication. Disposition B is the right *long-term*
answer if the workgroup wants the IG to genuinely cover non-learned
algorithms, but it is materially larger work (new profile split, formal
liaison with CDS, possible re-ballot of restructured content) and should
be raised as a separate scoped change rather than absorbed into a
clarification ticket. Disposition C is not recommended: it preserves the
ambiguity Ignacio is flagging and shifts the cost onto every downstream
implementer of a rule-based or imaging system.
