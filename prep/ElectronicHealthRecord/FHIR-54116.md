## Summary

The submitter is reviewing the AI Transparency on FHIR Implementation
Guide (Jan 2026 ballot) and finds the introductory sentence describing
the IG's aim insufficiently clear. Specifically, in the sentence
*"This FHIR Implementation Guide (IG) provides guidance for representing
the usage of AI in influencing FHIR resources"*, the word "influencing"
is awkward — the reporter suggests it likely should be "influenced"
(i.e., AI-influenced FHIR resources), and notes that, beyond fixing the
verb form, the IG would benefit from a more comprehensive description of
its aim. The ticket is filed against the Electronic Health Record work
group and the AI Transparency (FHIR) specification, is typed as a
Technical Correction with Highest priority, and is currently Triaged
with no assignee. No other Jira tickets are linked or cross-referenced;
the only cross-reference is a Zulip ballot-triage roll-up post by the
WG co-chair that lists FHIR-54116 alongside ~30 other "Documentation &
Clarity" items for the January ballot.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the AI Transparency on FHIR IG introduction (the opening narrative
on `index.html` of the `2026Jan` ballot publication), replace the
sentence:

> "This FHIR Implementation Guide (IG) provides guidance for
> representing the usage of AI in influencing FHIR resources"

with a corrected and expanded paragraph along the lines of:

> "This FHIR Implementation Guide (IG) provides guidance for
> representing how AI has influenced FHIR resources — that is, how to
> record, expose, and reason about the contribution of AI software
> (models, agents, decision-support tools) to the data captured in a
> FHIR resource. It defines profiles, extensions, and provenance
> patterns that let producers signal AI involvement in the creation,
> derivation, or transformation of clinical data, and lets consumers
> evaluate that involvement when interpreting or acting on the data."

The minimum change is the verb fix (`influencing` → `influenced`); the
expanded text addresses the second half of the reporter's request for
a "more comprehensive description of the aim of the IG."

#### Justification

This is a Technical Correction at Highest priority filed during ballot
reconciliation, and the reporter's grammatical reading is correct —
"AI influencing FHIR resources" is awkward and most readers will parse
the IG's scope as "FHIR resources that have been influenced by AI."
Fixing the verb form removes a stumbling block on the very first
sentence a reader encounters, and expanding the paragraph to name what
the IG actually does (record, expose, reason about AI involvement) is
consistent with how other UV IGs frame their scope statements. The
WG-level Zulip triage already places FHIR-54116 in the "Documentation
& Clarity" bucket, which signals consensus that the change is
in-scope and editorial rather than normative.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Make only the minimal grammatical correction now (`influencing` →
`influenced`) and defer the broader "comprehensive description of the
aim of the IG" to a separate, larger introduction rewrite tracked as
its own ticket against the same IG page. The single-word fix would
land in this ticket; a follow-up ticket (or an existing one in the
"Documentation & Clarity" bucket — e.g., one of the bolded items in
the WG triage roll-up such as FHIR-54732, FHIR-54735, FHIR-54740, or
FHIR-54801) would carry the prose rewrite and be reviewed by the IG
authors as a coordinated narrative pass.

#### Justification

The two halves of the reporter's request have very different review
costs: the verb fix is unambiguous and uncontroversial, while a
"more comprehensive description of the aim" is a prose change that
benefits from being authored and reviewed alongside other
introduction-section tickets so the IG doesn't end up with a Frankenstein
introduction edited one sentence at a time. Splitting keeps this ticket
small enough to clear quickly during reconciliation and lets the IG
editors batch the larger narrative change with the other 30
documentation tickets in the same triage bucket.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline FHIR-54116 as **Not Persuasive — editorial only**, leaving the
current sentence ("…the usage of AI in influencing FHIR resources")
unchanged. The original phrasing is grammatically defensible if read as
"the usage of AI [, an act of] influencing FHIR resources," and the
overall scope of the IG is conveyed by the rest of the introduction
and the IG's table of contents.

#### Justification

This is the weakest option. It can only be defended on the grounds
that (a) the sentence is technically grammatical and (b) editorial
nits at the very front of an IG can be deferred indefinitely. Both
arguments are thin: the sentence reads awkwardly to native and
non-native readers alike, and the cost of fixing one sentence on
`index.html` is trivial. Decline should only be used here if the
WG decides to bundle the rewrite into a larger restructuring that
makes a piecemeal change wasteful — but in that case Disposition B
(split / defer) is a cleaner record than Decline.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is exactly the kind of small, high-value editorial fix that
ballot reconciliation exists to absorb: the reporter has identified a
real readability problem on the first sentence of the IG, has proposed
a concrete one-word fix, and has flagged a reasonable follow-on
improvement. Accepting both halves in a single edit (verb fix plus a
short, more descriptive paragraph) is cheap, lands in a single PR
against the IG's introduction page, and removes a stumbling block that
every reader hits before getting to anything substantive. Splitting
into two tickets (Disposition B) is also defensible, but the WG's own
Zulip triage already groups FHIR-54116 with 30 other "Documentation &
Clarity" items, so adding another ticket adds tracking overhead
without buying meaningfully better review. Decline (C) is not
recommended — the original wording is awkward enough that leaving it
in a published IG is a poor outcome for a Highest-priority technical
correction.
