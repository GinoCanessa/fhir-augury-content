## Summary

The reporter, writing on behalf of the AMA, requests that the AI Transparency
on FHIR IG "flesh out" guidance on AI training that uses clinical data.
Specifically, the ticket suggests the IG should require labeling of training
content along the five dimensions called out in the AMA's AI principles:
(1) data provenance, (2) data size and completeness, (3) data timeframes,
(4) data diversity, and (5) data labeling accuracy. The reporter notes
that surfacing these attributes can also create healthy pressure against
training on generative-AI-produced ("synthetic") data. The AMA AI principles
PDF is offered as reference material. The ticket has been triaged as
"Persuasive with Modification": the workgroup's recorded resolution is that
the IG does not cover AI *training* — its scope is AI *use* that results in
creating or updating FHIR resources — and that the AMA's framing should be
used to make that scope boundary clearer in the IG. No other Jira tickets
are cross-referenced; the only related discussion is the January-ballot
triage thread on Zulip where this ticket appears in the "Use Cases &
Examples" bucket alongside other use-case-focused items (e.g., FHIR-54731,
FHIR-54706, FHIR-54643, FHIR-54642).

## Details

**Description:**

AMA data principles says the following about transparency in training content. Should always label:

(1) Data provenance

(2) Data size and completeness

(3) Data timeframes

(4) Data diversity

(5) Data labeling accuracy

Labeling things like this can create pressure to avoid using gen-AI data for training.

Feel free to reference our materials
https://www.ama-assn.org/system/files/ama-ai-principles.pdf

**Resolution Description:**

This IG does not have in scope the AI training. It is on AI use that results in creation or updating of resources. Use the provided AMA explanation in clarity of scope.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Extend the AI Transparency on FHIR IG so that AI **training** datasets used
to build models that later produce or update FHIR resources can be described
along the five AMA-aligned dimensions: data provenance, data size /
completeness, data timeframes, data diversity, and data labeling accuracy.
Concretely, this would mean either (a) defining an `AITrainingDataset`
profile (e.g., on `Evidence`, `EvidenceVariable`, `DocumentReference`, or a
new logical model) carrying these five attributes, or (b) adding an
extension bundle (`ext-ai-training-provenance`, `ext-ai-training-size`,
`ext-ai-training-timeframe`, `ext-ai-training-diversity`,
`ext-ai-training-label-accuracy`) that can be hung off whichever resource
the IG already uses to describe an AI model/algorithm. Cite the AMA AI
principles PDF in the IG's narrative as the source of the five dimensions.

#### Justification

This is the most literal reading of the reporter's request and explicitly
adopts a respected external policy framework (AMA) into the IG. It would
let implementers communicate exactly the kinds of training-data attributes
that downstream clinicians and regulators are increasingly being asked to
evaluate, and it materializes the reporter's secondary goal — that
labeling provenance/size/timeframes/diversity creates pressure against
opaque or synthetic-only training corpora.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach (Persuasive with Modification — matches the recorded resolution)

#### Proposal

Do **not** add training-data profiles or extensions to the AI Transparency
on FHIR IG. Instead, update the IG's *Scope* (and, where relevant, the
"Background" / "Out of Scope" narrative pages) to state explicitly that:

1. The IG covers AI **use** — i.e., resources created or updated as a
   result of AI inference / decision support — and the provenance and
   transparency of that *use*.
2. AI **training** transparency (data provenance, data size and
   completeness, data timeframes, data diversity, data labeling accuracy
   — the five AMA dimensions) is **out of scope** for this IG, with a
   short explanation of why (different audience, different lifecycle,
   addressed by other policy frameworks).
3. Reference the AMA AI principles document as the source of the
   five-dimension training-transparency framing, so a reader who *is*
   looking for training-side guidance is pointed at it directly.

This matches the workgroup's recorded resolution ("Use the provided AMA
explanation in clarity of scope.") and would be implemented in the IG's
narrative content (e.g., the `index.html` / `scope.html` / `background.html`
pages) rather than in the StructureDefinition layer.

#### Justification

The current IG is deliberately about AI *use* in the context of resource
creation/update — the provenance trail of an AI-mediated clinical event,
not the training corpus behind the model. Pulling in five training-data
dimensions would significantly expand scope and would overlap with
governance/regulatory frameworks (FDA SaMD, EU AI Act, ONC HTI-1) that
operate at the dataset-and-model level rather than at the per-resource
level FHIR is designed for. Acknowledging the AMA framing in the scope
narrative gives the reporter what they actually need — visibility for
the five dimensions and a recognized reference — without committing the
EHR WG to maintain a parallel training-metadata model. This is the
disposition the workgroup has already recorded as "Persuasive with
Modification".

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request outright as out of scope. Close the ticket as
"Not Persuasive" with a one-sentence explanation that the IG addresses
AI use, not AI training, and make no narrative or structural changes.

#### Justification

A pure decline is defensible because the IG's name and stated charter are
about transparency of AI *use* in clinical workflows, not the upstream
training pipeline; nothing in the IG today depends on training-data
metadata, and adding it would push the IG into territory better handled
by model-governance standards. However, this option ignores the reporter's
legitimate point that the AMA framework exists and is widely cited — and
the EHR WG has already concluded the request is partly persuasive — so
a clean decline understates the real value of acknowledging the AMA
material in the IG narrative.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B mirrors the workgroup's recorded resolution
("Persuasive with Modification — This IG does not have in scope the AI
training. It is on AI use that results in creation or updating of
resources. Use the provided AMA explanation in clarity of scope.") and is
the cleanest answer to the underlying problem the reporter surfaced: the
IG's scope boundary around training-vs-use is not currently obvious to a
casual reader, and that ambiguity is what invited the request in the
first place. Accepting the request literally (Disposition A) would commit
the EHR WG to defining and maintaining a training-data metadata model
that overlaps materially with model-governance frameworks outside HL7's
charter, and would likely surface additional ballot comments at the next
cycle. A pure decline (Disposition C) leaves the underlying scope
ambiguity in place and gives no credit to the AMA framework the reporter
cited. Disposition B closes the issue, satisfies the AMA's transparency
intent by name-checking the five dimensions in the narrative, and keeps
the IG's structural surface focused on AI use — which is what the EHR WG
already agreed is in scope.
