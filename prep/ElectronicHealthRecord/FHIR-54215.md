## Summary

The submitter (IgnacioJauregui) argues that the "How was it recorded"
sub‑category under the **Process** AI Observability Factor in the AI
Transparency on FHIR IG is too vague to stand on its own. The single
example given for the subcategory — using MCP to redirect a model to a
controlled terminology corpus — is, in the submitter's view,
mis-classified: it speaks more to the **Context** factor (what data /
sources the model was given) than to **Process**, and it is more
naturally framed as an **accuracy** improvement than a **bias-reduction**
strategy. The reporter therefore asks the work group to either remove the
"How was it recorded" sub‑category outright or to redefine it so that the
intent (and what implementers are expected to capture / disclose) is
unambiguous.

No other Jira tickets are explicitly linked to FHIR-54215. It does appear
in the AI Transparency IG ballot triage roll-up on Zulip (under
"Documentation & Clarity"), so it has been treated as a clarity /
wording fix rather than a structural model change. The Jira record marks
the ticket as **Persuasive with Modification**, with the resolution
noting that PR
[HL7/aitransparency-ig#22](https://github.com/HL7/aitransparency-ig/pull/22)
*partially* addressed the concern by rewording the bullet to "Guardrails
for bias reduction, inappropriate responses, undesired actions, …", and
that the work group intends to refine that further to "Guardrails to
prevent bias, …". The disposition options below are framed as confirming,
sharpening, or revisiting that already-merged refinement.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Confirm and finalise the wording change already made in
[HL7/aitransparency-ig#22](https://github.com/HL7/aitransparency-ig/pull/22)
and apply the work group's planned follow‑up refinement in
`input/pagecontent/general_guidance.md` under `### Process Utilizing AI`:

- Replace the existing `**How was it recorded**` bullet block with a
  redefined sub‑bullet whose label and example make the *intent* of the
  Process factor explicit. Concretely:
  - Rename the `**How was it recorded**` lead-in to `**Guardrails
    applied during recording**` (or keep the original label and tighten
    the body — work group's call).
  - Replace the "Bias reduction strategies" bullet with the refined
    text: *"Guardrails to prevent bias, inappropriate responses, and
    undesired actions (e.g., constraining the model's vocabulary or
    actions via a controlled corpus / Model Context Protocol; tying the
    captured guardrail invocation back to `Provenance`)."*
  - Keep the `tie back to Provenance` line as a sub‑bullet so the
    Provenance linkage the original author intended is preserved.

This delivers what the reporter asked for ("better define this
sub‑category") without dropping the concept entirely.

#### Justification

The reporter's complaint is fundamentally about *clarity*, not about
removing capability. Tightening the wording — as PR #22 has already
started and as the work group has committed to extending — directly
answers the comment: the sub‑category is now framed as **guardrails**
applied at recording time (a Process concern), rather than as the
ambiguous "How was it recorded" label. The MCP / controlled‑terminology
example is preserved but reframed as *one mechanism* by which a
guardrail can be enforced, which addresses the reporter's
"this looks like Context, not Process" objection without disturbing the
LM_AI logical model. Per the briefing, this is a single‑file
in‑page narrative edit (`input/pagecontent/general_guidance.md`) with no
cross‑repo impact and no FSH change. Anchor risk is low because the new
heading text is still a sub‑bullet under `### Process Utilizing AI`, not
a renamed `###` heading.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Promote the underlying concept from a free‑text bullet into a named
element in the AI Observability logical model, then collapse the
ambiguous "How was it recorded" wrapper:

- In `input/fsh/LM_AI.fsh`, add (or reuse) a `Process.guardrails`
  element on the existing `LM_AI` logical model with a short definition
  ("Guardrails or constraints applied during the AI's recording of an
  observation/recommendation to prevent bias, inappropriate output, or
  undesired action; cite the mechanism, e.g., controlled vocabulary, MCP
  redirection, output filtering").
- In `input/pagecontent/general_guidance.md`, under `### Process
  Utilizing AI`, **delete** the "How was it recorded" sub‑bullet block
  and replace it with a one‑paragraph reference to the new
  `Process.guardrails` model element, plus the existing Provenance
  linkage.
- Audit `input/pagecontent/index.md`, `use_cases.md`, and
  `explainer.md` for any links that pointed at
  `general_guidance.html#how-was-it-recorded` and update or add
  redirects (or an entry in `input/ignoreWarnings.txt`).

#### Justification

This treats the reporter's comment as evidence of a deeper modelling
gap: the sub‑category was always trying to be a named *observability
attribute* but was expressed only as narrative, which is exactly why a
reader cannot tell whether it belongs under Process or Context. Pulling
"guardrails applied at record time" up into LM_AI gives implementers a
machine‑readable hook for the same concept, makes it unambiguous that
the attribute lives under Process, and lets the narrative shrink to a
short pointer. Trade‑offs vs. Disposition A: this is a *bigger* change
(touches FSH, requires a SUSHI rebuild, and risks anchor breakage per
the briefing's gotchas), and it goes beyond what PR #22 already shipped,
so it would either invalidate part of #22 or be a follow‑on PR. The
upside is durable clarity; the downside is scope creep for what was
filed as a clarity ticket.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the literal "remove the sub‑category" reading of the request
and treat FHIR-54215 as **already addressed** by the merged PR
[HL7/aitransparency-ig#22](https://github.com/HL7/aitransparency-ig/pull/22)
(the existing "Guardrails for bias reduction, inappropriate responses,
undesired actions, …" wording). No further source change in
`input/pagecontent/general_guidance.md` or `input/fsh/LM_AI.fsh`. The
ticket would be closed as Persuasive with Modification on the strength
of the wording already in main, citing PR #22.

#### Justification

This is defensible if the work group judges that PR #22's wording
already eliminates the ambiguity the reporter flagged: the bullet is no
longer titled "How was it recorded" with a single MCP example; it is
now framed as guardrails covering bias, inappropriate responses, and
undesired actions, with MCP/controlled‑corpus offered as an example
mechanism. Removing the sub‑category outright (the literal request)
would lose useful guidance to implementers about *when* to capture
guardrail metadata, and would leave Provenance‑tagged AI outputs
without a narrative hook. The risk of declining is that the Jira
resolution itself acknowledges further refinement is still desired ("We
will further refine to say 'Guardrails to prevent bias, …'"), so closing
without applying that refinement contradicts the work group's own
stated plan.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter's concern is real and specific (label is vague, example is
mis‑placed), the work group has *already* started fixing it in
[HL7/aitransparency-ig#22](https://github.com/HL7/aitransparency-ig/pull/22),
and the Jira resolution explicitly commits to one further wording pass.
Disposition A simply lands that committed pass — a single‑file
narrative edit in `input/pagecontent/general_guidance.md`, with no
FSH/SUSHI churn, no anchor breakage, and no cross‑repo impact (per the
HL7/aitransparency-ig briefing). Disposition B is technically the
cleanest long‑term answer, but it expands a Block‑Vote‑2 clarity
ticket into a logical‑model change, which is disproportionate for the
scope the reporter raised and would re‑open work already block‑voted.
Disposition C under‑delivers on the work group's own stated plan ("We
will further refine"). Picking A keeps the IG aligned with what the
group already promised in resolution, and it leaves the door open to
do B as a separate, properly scoped modelling ticket if reviewers want
to pursue it post‑ballot.
