## Summary

The ticket is filed against the **AI Transparency on FHIR** IG (January
2026 ballot) and concerns the IG's published versioning statement that
the IG is an R4 IG, that none of the features it uses changed in R4B, and
that R4 (`hl7.fhir.uv.aitransparency.r4`) and R4B
(`hl7.fhir.uv.aitransparency.r4b`) packages are both available. The
reporter (IgnacioJauregui) suggests that the IG should additionally
state the **intention — and ideally timelines — for producing R5 and R6
versions of the IG**, so implementers planning forward-looking
deployments can understand whether/when the IG will be available against
newer FHIR releases.

The only cross-reference Augury surfaces for this ticket is a single

listed under the **Exchange & Implementation** category alongside

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add an explicit **"FHIR Version Roadmap" subsection** to the IG's
versioning / introduction page (the same page that today carries the
"This is an R4 IG…" statement quoted in the description). The new text
would:

1. Reaffirm the current state: the IG is published as R4 and is usable
   as-is on R4B; both `hl7.fhir.uv.aitransparency.r4` and
   `hl7.fhir.uv.aitransparency.r4b` packages are produced.
2. Declare the **intent** to publish R5 and R6 variants of the IG once
   the underlying profile content has been reviewed against the R5/R6
   versions of the resources it constrains (notably Provenance,
   AuditEvent, DocumentReference, and any extensions used).
3. Include a **target timeline**, e.g., "An R5 package
   (`hl7.fhir.uv.aitransparency.r5`) is targeted for the next
   STU/normative ballot cycle following this January 2026 publication;
   an R6 variant will follow once R6 is published and the IG's
   dependencies are stable in R6."
4. State the criteria that gate each version (no breaking changes to the
   resources/extensions the IG depends on, R5/R6 publication of the base
   spec, and successful test of the IG's profiles under
   `sushi`/IG-publisher against the R5/R6 core packages).

#### Justification

The reporter's request is reasonable and low-risk: it asks only for
narrative clarity, not for a normative change to any profile,
extension, or value set. Implementers planning multi-year deployments
benefit from knowing the WG's intent — particularly because R4B is
already the current "in-use" target for many CDS/AI workflows that pair
this IG with US Core (R4-based) and because R5 and R6 are increasingly
being adopted by EHR vendors. Stating intent and a coarse timeline is
consistent with how other HL7 IGs (e.g., several FHIR Accelerator IGs)
publish a "FHIR Version Compatibility" or "Roadmap" page.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt a **lighter-weight intent statement** rather than committing to
specific timelines. Replace the existing single-paragraph version
statement with a two-paragraph block:

- Paragraph 1 (unchanged in substance): "This is an R4 IG; usable
  unchanged on R4B; both R4 and R4B packages are published."
- Paragraph 2 (new): "The work group intends to produce R5 and R6
  variants of this IG (`hl7.fhir.uv.aitransparency.r5` and
  `hl7.fhir.uv.aitransparency.r6`) once the IG's profiled resources and
  extensions are validated against those FHIR versions. **No specific
  publication date is committed at this time**; updates will be
  announced on the *AI Transparency on FHIR* Zulip stream and in the
  HL7 EHR WG meeting minutes."

Optionally, add a single-row table to the IG's home page summarising
"FHIR R4 — Published; R4B — Published (same package content); R5 —
Planned; R6 — Planned" so the status is discoverable without reading
the prose.

#### Justification

This addresses the underlying need — telling implementers that R5/R6
support is on the WG's radar — without committing the WG to a calendar
that it may not be able to honour. STU IGs frequently slip publication
dates because of dependencies on the base spec, accelerators, or
upstream tooling; publishing a date that later slips can be more
damaging to implementer trust than publishing a clear "planned, no
date" statement. This option also avoids the editorial overhead of
keeping the timeline section accurate across future republications.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request as **out of scope for the current ballot**, on the
grounds that:

1. The IG already states its current FHIR-version posture (R4, with R4B
   compatibility), which is the substantive information implementers
   need today.
2. R5/R6 plans depend on resources and tooling outside the IG's control
   (the R5/R6 publication state of Provenance/AuditEvent/etc., and the
   IG-publisher's support for cross-version IGs), and committing to
   them in the IG narrative either over-commits the WG or produces
   stale text.
3. The HL7 EHR WG already publishes its forward plan in its work group
   pages; duplicating that information in the IG narrative risks
   divergence.

Recommend the reporter raise the question through the EHR WG project
plan / HL7 confluence space instead.

#### Justification

Defensible if (a) the WG genuinely has no concrete R5/R6 intent, or (b)
WG project planning is published elsewhere and the IG narrative is
intentionally kept free of forward-looking statements. However,
"decline as out of scope" is a hard line for what is essentially a
documentation-only request, and reviewers typically push back on
declining a clarification unless there is a stronger reason than
"published elsewhere."

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **B**

Disposition B (lighter-weight intent statement, no committed date) is
the best balance. It honours the reporter's underlying concern —
implementers want to know the IG's forward FHIR-version posture — while
protecting the WG from publishing a calendar it cannot confidently
meet. STU IGs that depend on the base spec, on multiple resources, and
on cross-version IG tooling almost always discover slippage between
ballot cycles, and Disposition A's explicit timeline ("targeted for the
next STU/normative ballot") risks becoming stale text the WG must then
revisit each republication. Disposition C is too heavy-handed for what
is fundamentally a documentation clarification. Disposition B can be
implemented as a small narrative edit in the IG's introduction /
versioning page (typically `input/pagecontent/*.md` in the IG repo) and
needs no profile, extension, or value-set changes — making it a
low-cost, high-clarity win for January-ballot disposition.
