"Plain Language Summary" page or section and asks the work group to add

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a new "Plain Language Summary" page to the AI Transparency IG
front matter for the next published version (post-January-2026
ballot). Concretely: create a new `input/pagecontent/plain-language-summary.md`
authored in jargon-free language (~300–600 words) that covers (a)
what the IG is about, (b) why AI transparency matters in healthcare,
(c) what artefacts the IG defines at a high level (the
`AIModelInfo` / `AIUseContext`-style profiles, provenance/tagging
patterns, etc.), and (d) what it does **not** do. Register the new
page in `sushi-config.yaml` under `pages:` and add a top-level menu
entry in `menu.xml` (or the equivalent IG Publisher menu file) so the
page appears in the IG's navigation. Mark it explicitly as
"informative, non-normative."

#### Justification

A plain-language summary is consistent with the direction many recent
HL7 and US-Core / US-PH IGs have taken to broaden the audience of FHIR
specifications beyond implementers, and the AI Transparency IG —
explicitly a *transparency* IG aimed at clinicians, patients, and
regulators in addition to implementers — is a natural fit. The ask is
purely additive (a new informative narrative page), so it does not
change any profile, value set, example, or normative behaviour and
therefore poses no implementation risk to systems already building
against the ballot content. Treating the request as a substantive
"new content" item respects the reporter's framing that something is
*missing* from a ballot artefact whose stated purpose is communication.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than introduce a separate "Plain Language Summary" page,
expand and re-frame the IG's existing `index.md` (Home) and/or
`background.md` pages with a clearly delimited "Plain Language
Summary" section at the top — a short opening box with a heading
such as `## Plain Language Summary (non-normative)` followed by a
few accessible paragraphs. Optionally add a brief "Audience" callout
that names the intended readers. Defer this change to the next
ballot/publication after January 2026 (i.e., do **not** treat it as
in-scope for the January ballot disposition itself), aligning with
John Moehrke's comment that plain-language summary content is not
part of the current ballot material.

#### Justification

This addresses the underlying need (an accessible orientation for
non-implementer readers) without committing to a new top-level page,
which historically attracts maintenance burden (translation, sync
with normative content, version drift) and an additional menu slot.
Embedding the summary in the IG's home/background pages also
guarantees that every reader sees it on first visit, whereas a
separate page can be missed. Scheduling the work for the next cycle
respects the "not part of ballot material" position while still
honouring the reporter's request — the work group commits to adding
the content, just not as a ballot deliverable for January 2026.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket "Persuasive with Mod" → close as **Not Persuasive**
(or "Considered – No action this ballot") on the grounds stated by
John Moehrke: a plain-language summary is editorial framing rather
than balloted content, the IG already provides a Home page, a
Background page, and per-artefact descriptions that together convey
the IG's purpose, and adding new narrative pages is out of scope for
the January 2026 ballot disposition cycle. Suggest the reporter
re-file as a future-cycle enhancement if she wishes to see a dedicated
PLS page in a subsequent publication.

#### Justification

The argument that plain-language summaries are not ballot material has
some basis: HL7 ballot reconciliation typically focuses on normative
profiles, value sets, operations, conformance constraints, and
substantive narrative defects — not on whether to add new informative
front-matter pages, which can be added between ballots without
re-balloting. The request as filed ("Missing Plain Language Summary -
please add") is also extremely thin: it does not specify audience,
length, content scope, or where in the IG the summary should live,
giving the work group little to act on. Declining at this triage
preserves work-group bandwidth for ballot-blocking issues among the
89 other AI Transparency ballot comments while leaving the door open
for a properly-scoped enhancement request.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the most defensible compromise. It accepts the
reporter's substantive point — the AI Transparency IG genuinely does
benefit from an accessible, jargon-free orientation aimed at the
clinician/policy/patient audience the IG is *named for* — while
acknowledging John Moehrke's correct procedural observation that
inserting brand-new informative pages is not the work of ballot
reconciliation. Embedding a "Plain Language Summary" section into the
existing `index.md` / `background.md` for the next publication after
January 2026 is low-risk (no normative content changes, no menu
restructuring, no impact on implementers building today), is
discoverable (every reader hits the Home page), avoids the maintenance
overhead of a dedicated page, and gives the reporter a concrete,
visible win without forcing the work group to relitigate ballot scope.
Disposition A is the right *long-term* shape but inappropriate as a
ballot disposition; Disposition C respects ballot process but leaves a
real usability gap unresolved and risks the same comment recurring
verbatim in the next ballot.
