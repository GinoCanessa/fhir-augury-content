## Summary

The reporter asks the workgroup to evaluate whether the AI Transparency
(FHIR) IG needs a **modifier extension** that flags a resource as
AI-generated, in addition to (or alongside) the existing
`meta.security` tag mechanism. The argument is that for some
AI-generated payloads — the example given is a critical CDSS output —
knowing that the data was produced by an AI materially changes how the
resource should be interpreted, and a non-modifier signal (a security
tag) is not strong enough to *force* downstream systems to take that
fact into account. The proposed extension would not replace the
security tag; it would be an additional, optional mechanism reserved
for cases where the AI-provenance fact is interpretation-changing.

This ticket sits in the **Provenance & Tagging** cluster of the AI
Transparency IG January ballot triage by John Moehrke (Zulip thread
*AI Transparency on FHIR > IG - Jan ballot*), alongside related
tagging tickets such as
[FHIR-54244](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54244) (capture AI
inference certainty as an extension),
[FHIR-54242](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54242) (correct
`meta.security.code` in a diagram), and
[FHIR-54255](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54255) (UML diagram for
inline tagging). Other related tickets reinforce that the IG's current
mechanism is a `meta.security` / `meta.tag` style marker rather than
an extension —
[FHIR-54725](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54725) explicitly asks
whether the AI Data profile's marker should be `meta.security` vs
`meta.tag` and what "AI used vs AI created" means, and
[FHIR-54580](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54580) is a bug ticket
about an example missing the AI tag entirely. Historical precedent
from FHIR Core ([FHIR-38977](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-38977),
*Resolved - No Change*) shows that the workgroup has previously
declined a "make this a modifier extension" request on similar
grounds, which is directly relevant context here.

## Details

**Description:**

> For some AI generated data the interpretation of the resource might
> change its meaning by knowing that it was generated by an AI (e.g.
> critical CDSS). In these cases, having a modifier extension will
> make sure that the receiver knows that the data comes from an AI.
> This could be a new extension that exists alongside the current
> security tag mechanism for cases in which this is not necessary.

**Comments:**

- **JIRAUSER26199** *(2026-01-27)* — Raises the patient-safety angle:
  AI-generated content can be unverified or incorrect (e.g., AI-based
  CADx producing a wrong diagnosis, AI-translated reports with errors
  on critical content). Argues the AI-generated nature can have the
  *is-modifier* character (i.e., it changes the validity/meaning of
  the content) and that the WG should at least not overlook the
  safety risk. Tempers the position by warning against overkill that
  would put too much burden on AI systems, and explicitly says they
  are flagging the issue for WG discussion rather than insisting on
  the change.
- **john_moehrke** *(2026-01-26)* — Pushes back: "the use-case is
  unclear to me." States he understands the assertions but cannot
  identify a realistic use case where the AI-generated fact is not
  already conveyed by the *normal elements of a resource* (the
  existing security-tag mechanism, Provenance, etc.).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Define a **new modifier extension** in the AI Transparency IG —
something like
`http://hl7.org/fhir/uv/aitransparency/StructureDefinition/ai-generated-modifier`
— that, when present on a resource, asserts that the resource (or a
specified portion of it) was AI-generated *in a way that changes the
interpretation of the resource*, and that downstream systems MUST NOT
process the resource without understanding that fact. The extension
would carry at minimum a coded indication of the AI-generation nature
(initially a single bound code such as `ai-generated-interpretation-altering`,
extensible later) and would coexist with the existing
`meta.security` AI tag: the security tag remains the default
mechanism for routine AI-generated data, while this modifier
extension is reserved for high-risk cases (e.g., critical CDSS output,
AI-translated clinical narrative) where silent processing is unsafe.
Add narrative in the IG that gives explicit guidance on *when* an
author should choose the modifier extension over the security tag,
plus at least one example resource demonstrating its use.

#### Justification

This directly addresses the reporter's and JIRAUSER26199's stated
patient-safety concern: a `meta.security` tag is informational and
does not invoke the FHIR conformance rule that an unrecognised
modifier extension forces a system to refuse to process the
resource. For genuinely interpretation-changing AI provenance (the
critical-CDSS / AI-translation cases), that "must understand"
semantics is exactly what FHIR's modifier-extension mechanism is
designed for. Keeping the existing security-tag mechanism in place
preserves backward compatibility and avoids forcing every
AI-generated resource into a heavyweight signalling path, addressing
JIRAUSER26199's explicit "do not want it to be overkill" caveat.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Do **not** introduce a new modifier extension. Instead, address the
underlying patient-safety concern with two narrower changes inside
the existing tagging mechanism:

1. Strengthen the IG narrative around the existing AI `meta.security`
   tag to spell out the *receiver's responsibility* when the tag is
   present on clinically-significant resources (CDSS output,
   translated narrative, image findings), and add explicit examples
   covering the critical-CDSS and AI-translation use cases the
   reporter and JIRAUSER26199 describe.
2. Where stronger semantics are genuinely needed, recommend
   conveying the AI-generation fact via **`Provenance`** linked to
   the resource — `Provenance.activity` / `Provenance.agent.type` is
   already the FHIR-native place to assert "this was produced by an
   AI agent" with full auditable detail (who/what model, when, with
   what inputs), which a `meta.security` tag cannot carry.

Defer (do not close) the modifier-extension question pending a
concrete implementer use case where neither the security tag nor
Provenance is sufficient.

#### Justification

This is consistent with john_moehrke's comment that the use-case for
a modifier extension is not yet clear and that the *normal elements
of a resource* should be tried first. It also aligns with the wider
FHIR pattern that AI provenance is a Provenance/AuditEvent concern,
not a payload-level modifier — keeping AI-transparency signalling
out of the resource body avoids profile-explosion across every
resource type that an AI might produce. The historical precedent in
[FHIR-38977](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-38977) — where a
similarly-shaped "promote this to a modifier extension" request was
closed *No Change* — supports caution about adding modifier
extensions without a demonstrably-unmet need. This option does not
reject the safety concern; it routes it through mechanisms FHIR
already has.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as *Persuasive with Modifier* → no spec change,
documenting that the AI Transparency IG's `meta.security` tagging
mechanism (and, where audit-grade detail is needed,
`Provenance.agent`) is the agreed transport for the AI-generated
fact, and that no modifier extension will be defined for AI
provenance in this ballot cycle. Explicitly note that the WG
considered the patient-safety argument and concluded that a
*payload-level* modifier signal is the wrong layer at which to
mitigate AI-output safety risks (those belong in clinical workflow,
display, and review policy, not in resource conformance).

#### Justification

Defensible on three grounds. **First**, no concrete use case has
been put forward where the existing security tag plus Provenance
fail — Moehrke's comment is on the record asking for one and none
has been offered. **Second**, modifier extensions impose a hard
processing burden on every receiver, including those who legitimately
do not need to react to the AI fact (analytics warehouses, transport
brokers, FHIR storage layers); that cost should not be paid by every
deployment to mitigate a risk that is genuinely deployment-specific.
**Third**, the IG is mid-ballot and has unresolved ambiguity about
its current mechanism (see
[FHIR-54725](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54725) — is it
`meta.security` or `meta.tag`? "used by AI" or "created by AI"?);
introducing an additional, stronger signal before the basic
mechanism is stable would compound that confusion rather than
resolve it.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The patient-safety concern raised by the reporter and reinforced by
JIRAUSER26199 is real and should not be brushed aside, which makes
a flat *Decline* (Disposition C) too dismissive of the substantive
issue on the record. At the same time, john_moehrke's
"use-case-is-unclear" objection is also on the record and is
materially the same objection that led the WG to close
[FHIR-38977](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-38977) as *No
Change*; minting a brand-new modifier extension across an entire IG
without a demonstrated concrete failure of the existing mechanism
(Disposition A) sets a precedent the WG has historically resisted,
and it would land on top of an IG whose basic tagging mechanism is
itself still being clarified ([FHIR-54725](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54725)).
Disposition B threads that needle: it strengthens the narrative
guidance around the existing `meta.security` AI tag, explicitly
points implementers at `Provenance` for the audit-grade case, and
keeps the modifier-extension question open pending a concrete
implementer report. If, between ballot cycles, an implementer
returns with a use case where neither the security tag nor
Provenance is sufficient, the WG can reopen this ticket and move to
Disposition A on solid ground.
