## Summary

The reporter argues that the AI Transparency on FHIR IG's examples are
incomplete: they implicitly require readers to either (a) search for
`Provenance` by `target`, or (b) rely on co-bundling the clinical
resource and its `Provenance` in the same `Bundle`, in order to
discover that a resource was influenced by AI. Because the IG narrative
does not state this discovery model clearly, and because many examples
show only one side of the pattern (clinical resources without an
embedded "AI-generated" indicator, or `Provenance` instances shown in
isolation), reviewers can reasonably — but incorrectly — conclude that
the IG is missing an explicit AI marker on the resource itself. The
request is to make the discovery model and complete pattern visible in
the examples.

This ticket is one of a closely related set raised by the same reporter
against the AI Transparency IG. [FHIR-54279](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54279)
asks the IG to add explicit guidance that "AI attribution is asserted
via `Provenance`, not embedded in clinical resources," and to call out
that consumers must discover `Provenance` via search-by-target or
co-bundling. [FHIR-54280](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54280)
asserts that `meta.security` labels are discovery hints rather than
authoritative evidence of AI authorship, and that searching `Provenance`
is therefore critical. Together, FHIR-54279 (narrative), FHIR-54280
(label semantics), and FHIR-54281 (examples) form a single thematic
request: make the `Provenance`-as-authoritative-source model
unambiguous in narrative, label semantics, **and** examples.
[FHIR-54707](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54707) ("Refrain from
using Tag or define it") is in the same Provenance & Tagging triage
bucket but addresses terminology rather than discovery, and is only
loosely related.

The single comment from the WG co-chair (John Moehrke) acknowledges
that `Provenance` discovery is general FHIR-core behavior and
indicates willingness to add a better explanation, while cautioning
against turning the IG into a tutorial on `Provenance` discovery.

## Details

**Description:**

The examples require readers to infer that they must either: search
for Provenance by target, or rely on co-bundling of resources and
Provenance in the same Bundle. This discovery model is not stated
clearly in the narrative guidance, and many examples show only parts
of the overall pattern (e.g., clinical resources without visible AI
indicators, or Provenance shown separately). As a result, reviewers
may reasonably expect to see an explicit "AI-generated" indicator
within the resource and conclude—incorrectly—that such marking is
missing.

**Comments:**

- **john_moehrke (2026-01-26):** "the discovery of Provenance is not
  something specific to this IG, so we tried to leverage FHIR core.
  Will look to provide better explanation, although we should not
  become an IG that explains all about Provenance discovery."

## Proposed Dispositions

Note: the cross-reference index returned no GitHub repository links
for this ticket, so there is no `repo-analysis` briefing to anchor
specific authoring-root or file-path callouts. Dispositions below name
the affected IG (AI Transparency on FHIR) and the conceptual
authoring areas (narrative guidance pages and example resources) but
cannot cite specific repo paths until a briefing is available for the
hosting repo. The WG co-chair's comment indicates the IG source is
managed in the standard HL7 IG-publisher layout for the AI
Transparency IG.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the AI Transparency on FHIR IG, make the `Provenance`-discovery
pattern visible end-to-end in the examples:

1. For each canonical clinical example currently shown without its
   companion `Provenance`, add (a) the corresponding `Provenance`
   instance with `Provenance.target` pointing at the clinical
   resource and (b) a short narrative caption identifying which
   element(s) (`Provenance.agent.who`, `Provenance.agent.type`, the
   AI-related `Provenance.agent.role` / activity coding) carry the
   AI-influence assertion.
2. Add at least one `searchset` `Bundle` example showing the result
   of `GET [base]/Provenance?target={ClinicalResource}/{id}` so the
   "search-by-target" discovery path is concrete.
3. Add at least one `document` or `collection` `Bundle` example that
   co-bundles a clinical resource with its `Provenance`, demonstrating
   the co-bundling discovery path.
4. Cross-link these examples from the IG's narrative discovery
   section so a reader who lands on a clinical example can navigate
   to the matching `Provenance` and vice versa.

#### Justification

This directly addresses the reporter's concern: reviewers seeing only
half the pattern infer that AI marking is missing from the IG.
Showing both halves — and both discovery paths — removes that
inference without inventing new conformance. It is consistent with
FHIR core's `Provenance` design (one-way linkage from `Provenance` to
target) and with the WG co-chair's commitment to "provide better
explanation." It also reinforces the parallel narrative changes
requested in FHIR-54279 and the label-semantics clarification in
FHIR-54280, producing a coherent set of fixes for the January ballot.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than expanding every clinical example, add **one prominent
"How to discover AI influence" exemplar page** that shows a single
clinical resource together with: (a) its `Provenance`, (b) a sample
`GET Provenance?target=...` searchset `Bundle`, and (c) a co-bundled
`Bundle`. Then, in each existing clinical example, add a short
sidebar (or an "Examples → see also: AI-influence discovery
exemplar" cross-link) that points readers to that single canonical
page. Keep the existing examples otherwise unchanged.

#### Justification

This honors the WG co-chair's caution that the IG should not become
"an IG that explains all about Provenance discovery." A single
exemplar page is materially cheaper to author and maintain than
duplicating `Provenance` companions across every clinical example,
and it concentrates the discovery teaching in one authoritative
spot — which is also easier to keep aligned with FHIR core. The
trade-off vs. Disposition A is that a reviewer who lands on a single
clinical example still has to follow a link to see the full pattern;
that risk is mitigated by making the cross-link prominent and by
pairing this with the FHIR-54279 narrative changes.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket as "Not Persuasive — Already Addressed by Core" with
the rationale that `Provenance` discovery semantics are defined
normatively in FHIR core (search by `Provenance.target`, plus generic
`Bundle` semantics), and that the AI Transparency IG already
references those mechanisms. Optionally, add a one-paragraph
narrative pointer in the IG's "Discovery" section to FHIR core's
`Provenance` discussion, with no new examples.

#### Justification

This aligns with the co-chair's stated reluctance to "explain all
about Provenance discovery" inside the IG, and with the FHIR design
principle that IGs should reuse core mechanisms rather than duplicate
their documentation. If the WG concludes that the existing references
to FHIR core, plus the existing examples, are sufficient for
implementers, then expanding the examples adds maintenance cost
without adding normative content. The cost is reviewer experience:
the very pattern that prompted this ticket — a reviewer concluding AI
marking is missing — is unlikely to be solved by a pure narrative
pointer, so this disposition accepts that risk.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the best balance of the reporter's concern and the
co-chair's scope concern. The reporter's underlying issue is
discoverability of the discovery pattern, not the absence of
`Provenance` semantics; a single, prominent exemplar page that shows
search-by-target and co-bundling end-to-end resolves that concern at
roughly one-tenth the authoring and maintenance cost of expanding
every clinical example (Disposition A). It also avoids the failure
mode of Disposition C, where a reviewer lands on a clinical example,
sees no embedded AI indicator, and again concludes the IG is
incomplete. Pairing this exemplar with the narrative change requested
in FHIR-54279 and the label-semantics clarification in FHIR-54280
gives the WG a coherent, low-burden response to the entire
"Provenance authoritativeness" cluster of January-ballot issues. If
the WG ultimately wants stronger reviewer assurance, Disposition B
can be expanded toward A incrementally (e.g., add `Provenance`
companions to the two or three most-visited clinical examples)
without re-architecting the IG.
