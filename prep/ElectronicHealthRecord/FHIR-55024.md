## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In section 2.1 of the AI Transparency on FHIR IG, add a short
sub-section (e.g., "Algorithm determinism") that defines and contrasts
the three terms:

- **Deterministic** — given the same inputs and model state, the
  algorithm always produces the same output (e.g., classical
  rules-based systems, fixed-weight inference with greedy decoding and
  no stochastic sampling).
- **Non-deterministic** — outputs may vary across runs for identical
  inputs because the algorithm incorporates randomness, sampling, or
  state that is not fully captured by the inputs (e.g., LLMs with
  temperature > 0, Monte Carlo methods, ensembles with random
  seeding).
- **Hybrid** — combines deterministic and non-deterministic components
  (e.g., a deterministic rules layer wrapping a stochastic ML model,
  or a stochastic generation step followed by deterministic
  post-processing / constraint checking).

For each, give one or two healthcare-flavoured examples and note the
transparency implication (i.e., that consumers of AI output need to
understand which class an algorithm falls into in order to interpret
reproducibility, auditability, and validation requirements
appropriately). Cross-link to relevant external sources (e.g., NIST AI
RMF glossary, ISO/IEC 22989 AI concepts and terminology) rather than
attempting to be authoritative on AI taxonomy in an HL7 IG.

#### Justification

The request is squarely within scope for an IG whose stated purpose is
transparency about AI behaviour: a reader cannot meaningfully evaluate
the rest of the IG (provenance, validation, monitoring) without a
shared vocabulary for how the underlying algorithm behaves. The change
is narrative-only, low risk, and consistent with how other foundational
concept sections in HL7 IGs are written. Pointing at external standards
(NIST, ISO/IEC) keeps HL7 out of the business of defining AI taxonomy
de novo while still giving implementers a concrete anchor. The Zulip
triage explicitly placed this in "Documentation & Clarity", which is
the lightest-touch category and aligns with this disposition.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than defining the three terms in section 2.1's narrative, add a
**glossary entry** (in the IG's existing glossary / terminology
appendix, creating one if absent) for "algorithm determinism" with the
deterministic / non-deterministic / hybrid distinction, and replace the
section 2.1 prose with a single sentence pointing readers to that
glossary entry and to one external reference (e.g., NIST AI RMF). At
the same time, audit other sections of the IG that use the words
"deterministic" or "non-deterministic" and link them to the same
glossary entry so the terminology is defined exactly once.

#### Justification

The terms reappear elsewhere in any AI-focused IG (validation,
monitoring, provenance discussions all hinge on whether outputs are
reproducible). Defining them in-line in section 2.1 risks definitional
drift if the same words are explained differently later, and bloats a
foundational-concepts section that is supposed to read quickly. A
single glossary entry plus link-throughs is the canonical HL7 pattern
for shared vocabulary and avoids the ticket being addressed in section
2.1 only to leave the same gap elsewhere. Trade-off vs. Disposition A:
slightly more editorial work up front, and it touches more files than
just section 2.1; the payoff is consistency across the IG.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket as **Persuasive with Modification — Out of Scope** (or
the equivalent ballot disposition for "not for this IG"), with a
rationale that defining general AI taxonomy is the province of NIST AI
RMF, ISO/IEC 22989, and the broader AI-standards literature, and that
section 2.1 should simply assume the reader brings that vocabulary or
follow a single external link in the IG's "References" section.
Optionally add a one-line "see [References]" pointer in 2.1.

#### Justification

HL7 IGs generally avoid restating definitions that are well-covered in
adjacent standards, both to keep IGs short and to avoid HL7 becoming
the de-facto authority on terms it does not maintain. If the IG already
cites NIST AI RMF or ISO/IEC 22989 in its References section, the most
defensible answer to a "please define X" comment is "X is defined in
[cited reference]". The risk of declining is real, however: a ballot
comment from an EHR-WG reviewer asking for clarity is exactly the
audience the IG needs to land with, and "go read NIST" is a
non-trivial ask compared to two short paragraphs.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The underlying problem the ticket reports — that section 2.1 doesn't
make the deterministic / non-deterministic / hybrid distinction
explicit — is real and worth fixing; outright declining (Disposition C)
sends the wrong signal to ballot reviewers on a foundational
"transparency" IG, where shared vocabulary is the whole point.
Disposition A solves the immediate complaint but bakes the definitions
into one section, where they will inevitably be re-used (and possibly
re-defined) elsewhere in the IG. Disposition B is the same edit done in
the right place: a single glossary entry, linked from section 2.1 and
from any other section that uses the terms, plus a pointer to NIST
AI RMF / ISO/IEC 22989 for readers who want the full taxonomy. It is a
slightly larger editorial change but pays dividends for every other
"please define X" ballot comment the IG receives. The workgroup should
override to Disposition A if the IG does not yet have a glossary
appendix and the editorial team prefers not to add one for this ballot
cycle.
