## Summary

The reporter (Gay Dolin) asks the AI Transparency on FHIR IG editors to
sharpen the language in the IG's §1.2 Scope section so that readers
understand the guide is about **disclosure of known AI involvement**, and
is explicitly **not** about detecting or inferring AI involvement. She
proposes concrete suggested text stating that the guide does not define
how AI use is determined, and that determination of AI involvement is
assumed to be made by the producing/modifying system, with the IG only
providing a way to express that system-made assertion declaratively. No
other Jira tickets are formally linked; the only cross-reference is a
ballot-triage roll-up post in the `AI Transparency on FHIR > IG - Jan
ballot` Zulip topic, where John Moehrke placed FHIR-54885 in the
"Documentation & Clarity" bucket alongside ~30 other editorial tickets
against the same IG.

## Details

**Description:**

> Please emphasize more clearly in the 1.2 Scope section the this IG
> addresses disclosure of known AI use, **not detection or inference of
> AI involvement**, reducing ambiguity for implementers and reviewers.
>
> Here is some suggested text to add:
>
> This guide **does not define how AI use is determined**. Determination
> of AI involvement is assumed to be made by the system or application
> producing or modifying the FHIR resource. Assertions of AI involvement
> expressed using this guide are declarative statements made by the
> producing system.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the reporter's suggested text essentially verbatim in §1.2 Scope
of the AI Transparency on FHIR IG (current ballot build at
`https://hl7.org/fhir/uv/aitransparency/2026Jan/index.html`). Concretely,
add a short paragraph at (or near) the end of the §1.2 Scope narrative
that reads:

> This guide addresses **disclosure of known AI involvement**, not the
> detection or inference of AI involvement. This guide **does not
> define how AI use is determined**. Determination of AI involvement is
> assumed to be made by the system or application producing or
> modifying the FHIR resource. Assertions of AI involvement expressed
> using this guide are declarative statements made by the producing
> system.

Also add an explicit "Out of Scope" bullet (or extend the existing
Out-of-Scope list if one is present) stating "Detection or inference of
AI involvement in clinical data." No profile, extension, value set, or
element changes are required; the change is purely narrative in the
IG's scope page.

#### Justification

The request is editorial and consistent with how FHIR IGs routinely use
the Scope section to bound implementer expectations. The proposed
wording faithfully reflects the IG's actual mechanics — the IG supplies
a vocabulary and profiles for *asserting* AI involvement; it does not
and cannot mandate how a producing system arrives at that assertion.
Making this explicit reduces the risk that implementers, reviewers, and
regulators interpret the IG as endorsing a detection capability, which
is an important guardrail given the policy sensitivity around AI in
healthcare. The reporter has supplied ready-to-paste text, the ticket
is grouped with other approved "Documentation & Clarity" items in the
Jan-ballot triage, and no implementer-visible artifact changes.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the intent but generalize and re-site the wording. Rather than
appending the suggested text to §1.2 Scope verbatim, split the
clarification into two places: (1) a short one-sentence statement at
the top of §1.2 Scope ("This guide addresses disclosure of known AI
involvement, not its detection or inference."), and (2) a dedicated
"Scope Boundaries" or "Out of Scope" subsection (e.g., §1.2.x) that
contains the fuller explanation — that determination of AI involvement
is assumed to be made by the producing/modifying system, that
assertions in this guide are declarative, and that detection/inference
techniques, audit of AI assertions, and conformance of producing
systems are out of scope. Cross-link this subsection from the
Background, Conformance, and (where relevant) the AIAssertion / AI
Provenance profile pages.

#### Justification

The same guardrail is delivered, but the "not detection" boundary is
surfaced in the two places readers most often arrive at — the top of
Scope, and the profile pages where they are actually designing
instances — rather than being buried at the end of a narrative
paragraph. A dedicated Out-of-Scope subsection is also easier to
reference from future ballot comments and from related IGs (e.g.,
Provenance-based tagging guides) that need to draw a clean line at the
same boundary. The trade-off vs. Disposition A is slightly more editor
effort and a small risk of wording drift between the two locations,
which can be mitigated by keeping the longer statement canonical and
the top-of-Scope sentence a short pointer.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket as "Not Persuasive" on the grounds that the existing
§1.2 Scope text, read in context with the Background and Conformance
sections, already communicates that the IG is a disclosure mechanism
for producing systems and does not define detection logic, so adding
the suggested text is redundant and risks making the Scope section
longer without adding technical content.

#### Justification

Defensible only if the editors judge that §1.2 already states the
boundary clearly, or that the clarification belongs in a non-normative
companion document rather than the IG itself. Scope sections tend to
accrete text over a ballot cycle, and each addition has a maintenance
cost. However, this justification is weak here: the reporter is an
experienced IG author and explicitly reports finding the current
wording ambiguous, which is direct implementer-side evidence that the
existing text is not doing the job.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is a cheap, low-risk editorial clarification with
ready-to-paste text supplied by the reporter, no artifact impact, no
breaking change, and a concrete policy benefit — making explicit that
the IG is a disclosure vocabulary and not a detection/inference
framework. The Jan-ballot triage has already placed it in the
"Documentation & Clarity" bucket alongside a cohort of similarly
scoped items, which is the right lane for a straight accept. If the
editors later decide the stronger "Out of Scope" framing of Disposition
B reads better on the page, that refactor can be done in-place when
applying this change without needing a separate ticket; the important
commitment at triage time is to adopt the reporter's substantive point
in §1.2 Scope.
