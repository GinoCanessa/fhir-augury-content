## Summary

The reporter (John Carter) flags that the "Appendectomy" example in the
**AI Transparency on FHIR** IG (`hl7.fhir.uv.aitransparency`) does not
visibly convey any AI involvement. Looking at either the rendered page
or the raw JSON, the resource appears to be a plain `Procedure` with
nothing tying it to an AI system. The supporting FSH (`EX_Provenance.fsh`,
instance `proc`) confirms this: the Procedure carries no `meta.security`
AIAST tag, no profile claim, and no in-resource pointer back to the
authoring AI. The AI relationship is expressed only in the *separate*
Provenance example ("Provenance of AI Authored Procedure.followup.text"),
which targets `Procedure/proc` and names a `Device/TheAI` author plus a
human verifier. From a reader who lands on the Procedure example, that
linkage is invisible — the example illustrates the IG's Tagging /
Provenance pattern only when the Provenance instance is read alongside it.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat the ticket as accurate and surface the AI involvement directly on
the Appendectomy Procedure example. Concretely, in
`HL7/aitransparency-ig` at authoring root `input/fsh/EX_Provenance.fsh`,
modify `Instance: proc` (the "Appendectomy Procedure") to:

1. Add the AIAST security label, mirroring the existing `satO2` example:
   `* meta.security = http://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/v3-ObservationValue|4.0.0#AIAST "Artificial Intelligence asserted"`
   so the JSON itself signals AI authorship.
2. Update the `Description` to explicitly cross-reference the partner
   Provenance instance (`Provenance of AI Authored Procedure.followup.text`),
   so the IG-publisher rendered example page tells readers where to look
   for the AI-authoring detail (`Procedure.followUp.text` was AI-authored
   and human-verified).
3. In `input/pagecontent/general_guidance.md` (Tagging and Provenance
   subsections), add a one-paragraph callout that names the
   Appendectomy / Provenance pair as the canonical worked example of
   the Tagging + Provenance combination. Re-check intra-page anchors
   per the briefing's gotcha.

No `sushi-config.yaml` changes are needed (no new pages or dependency
bumps); no `fsh-generated/` or `output/` edits.

#### Justification

The reporter's complaint is empirically correct against the current
source: `EX_Provenance.fsh` lines 70–85 show a Procedure with no
`meta.security`, no profile claim, and no extension pointing at the
authoring AI. Meanwhile the IG's own Tagging guidance prescribes
`meta.security = AIAST` (and the sibling `satO2` Observation example
already demonstrates exactly that pattern at line 113). Bringing the
Appendectomy Procedure into line with the IG's documented Tagging
pattern is the smallest, most consistent fix and makes the example
self-explanatory in both the rendered HTML and the raw JSON. It also
preserves the Provenance-driven storytelling the example was originally
authored for, rather than discarding it.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep `Procedure/proc` deliberately "clean" — i.e., do not modify the
Procedure JSON — and instead address the discoverability problem by
restructuring how the example is presented. In
`input/pagecontent/general_guidance.md` (and/or a small new callout
block in `use_cases.md` or `explainer.md`), introduce a worked example
section titled e.g. "Worked example: AI-authored follow-up on an
Appendectomy" that renders the *pair* (Procedure + Provenance) together
and explains explicitly that, by design under this IG, AI involvement
in a previously-recorded resource is conveyed *only* via Provenance
referring back to the unchanged target — not by tagging the target
itself. Optionally add a `seealso` / "Linked example" line on each
example's intro (FSH `Description`) so a reader landing on either
resource is immediately pointed at the other.

#### Justification

The Appendectomy example was very likely chosen *because* the AI did
not author the Procedure as a whole — only the `followUp.text`. Adding
`meta.security = AIAST` to the entire Procedure would arguably
overstate the AI contribution (the procedure itself was performed and
recorded by humans; the AI only proposed follow-up text that a human
verifier accepted). Disposition B preserves that nuance, aligns with
the FHIR principle that Provenance is the canonical mechanism for
post-hoc attribution of changes, and acknowledges the briefing's
gotcha that the IG-publisher version in use does not currently allow
the IG's own `AIdata` profile to be claimed alongside other resource
types. The fix is purely narrative / authoring — no FSH change to the
Procedure, no terminology binding to defend, no risk of misleading a
downstream consumer who would otherwise treat the entire Procedure as
AI-asserted.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Not Persuasive** with the rationale: the
Appendectomy Procedure is *intentionally* a plain Procedure, included
only as the target referenced by the AI-authored Provenance example;
the IG's Tagging and Provenance guidance already documents that the AI
linkage lives on the Provenance, not on the target resource. Direct
the reporter to the existing Provenance example
("Provenance of AI Authored Procedure.followup.text") and to the
Tagging / Provenance sections of `general_guidance.md`.

#### Justification

This disposition is defensible because (a) the Procedure example is a
*supporting* artifact for `EX_Provenance` rather than a standalone AI
example, (b) the IG already publishes a separately-tagged Observation
example (`satO2`) that demonstrates the AIAST pattern on a resource
that *was* AI-asserted in whole, and (c) the briefing notes that the
`AIdata` profile cannot currently be applied to multiple resource
types in this IG-publisher level, which constrains what can be done on
the Procedure side without introducing publisher warnings. If the
reporter's confusion is purely a navigation issue, that is a documented
WG concern but not a spec defect.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The ticket reflects a real reader-experience problem — landing on the
Appendectomy Procedure gives no hint that AI is involved — but the
underlying design is deliberate and consistent with how FHIR
Provenance attaches AI authorship to a *change* on a previously-recorded
resource, not to the resource as a whole. Disposition A's fix
(stamping `meta.security = AIAST` on the entire Procedure) overstates
the AI contribution and would teach implementers a pattern at odds
with the granularity the IG is otherwise modelling (AI authored
`Procedure.followUp.text`, not the Procedure). Disposition C ignores a
genuine and easy-to-fix discoverability gap. Disposition B keeps the
example semantically honest, requires only narrative edits in
`input/pagecontent/general_guidance.md` (and optionally a one-line
`Description` cross-link in `EX_Provenance.fsh`), and avoids the
`AIdata`/IG-publisher gotcha called out in the briefing. The workgroup
may still prefer A if it wants the IG's headline examples to be
"obvious in the JSON"; in that case, the Procedure should at minimum
be re-described so it's clear the AIAST tag is shorthand for a partial
contribution recorded in detail by the linked Provenance.
