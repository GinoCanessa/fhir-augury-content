## Summary

The reporter (IgnacioJauregui) asks the **AI Transparency on FHIR** IG to
distinguish between two distinct scenarios that today are conflated under the
single notion of an "AI-inferred FHIR resource":

1. **AI-inferred *information* wrapped by a deterministic system into FHIR** —
   for example, a Generative AI / LLM produces a free-text patient summary,
   and a traditional rule-based pipeline then constructs the FHIR resource
   (e.g., a `Composition` or `DocumentReference`) around that text.
2. **AI-generated *FHIR resource* around deterministically produced data** —
   the underlying data is captured/derived by conventional means but the
   structuring of it into FHIR (element selection, coding, references,
   normalization, etc.) is performed by an AI.

The reporter notes that the current IG language ("a FHIR resource has been
inferred, in whole or part, by an AI") only really fits scenario #2, and that
the model offers no way to encode which of the two situations applies. They
are asking the WG to (a) describe both scenarios in the narrative and (b)
provide a mechanism to differentiate them on the wire.

No other Jira tickets were returned by the cross-reference query, so this
ticket stands on its own in the Augury index. It does, however, sit inside
the larger January-ballot reconciliation set for the AI Transparency IG (see
the Zulip triage thread below), where it is grouped under "Documentation &
Clarity" alongside ~30 other ballot comments.

## Proposed Dispositions

> Note: because no GitHub cross-references exist, the dispositions below are
> framed against the AI Transparency on FHIR IG narrative and profile pages
> (`https://hl7.org/fhir/uv/aitransparency/`). Specific authoring paths
> cannot be cited from a `repo-analysis` briefing — none are available for
> this IG in the cache.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the AI Transparency on FHIR IG to (1) explicitly describe **both**
scenarios in the narrative — "AI generated the *content* that was then
wrapped in a FHIR resource by a deterministic process" vs. "AI generated the
*FHIR resource structure* itself around deterministically-produced content"
— and (2) introduce a representation that lets implementers distinguish
them on the wire.

Concretely:

- **Narrative change**: rewrite the offending sentence on the IG's overview
  page (and any of the use-case / scenario pages that repeat it) to call
  out the two patterns side-by-side, with at least one example per pattern
  (e.g., LLM-authored discharge summary text → `DocumentReference` vs.
  rule-extracted lab values → AI-assembled `DiagnosticReport`).
- **Model change**: Use the IG's existing `Provenance`-based pattern but
  separate the two facets via either:
  - distinct `Provenance.entity.role` values (e.g., `source` for the
    inferred content, `derivation` for the inferred structure) combined
    with `Provenance.activity` codes for "content-inference" vs.
    "structure-inference"; or
  - two related `Provenance` instances — one targeting the narrative/text
    payload, one targeting the resource as a whole — each with its own
    `agent.type` and `activity` coding.
- Add a value set / extend an existing one in the IG with codes for
  `ai-inferred-content`, `ai-inferred-structure`, and `ai-inferred-both` so
  receivers can branch on the distinction without parsing prose.

#### Justification

The reporter has identified a real ambiguity: the current sentence only
fits one of two genuinely distinct provenance situations, and downstream
consumers cannot today decide whether the *clinical content* or the *FHIR
plumbing* (or both) was machine-generated. That distinction matters for
review, liability, and re-use policy. Provenance is already the IG's
designated mechanism for AI attribution, so layering the distinction onto
`Provenance.activity`/`entity.role` extends the existing pattern rather
than inventing a new one and is consistent with general FHIR practice for
expressing "what was done, by whom, to which artifact" through Provenance.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Address the issue **as a narrative-only clarification** for the current
ballot cycle, and defer any model change to a future version. Specifically:

- Reword the IG's framing sentence to: *"a FHIR resource has been
  inferred, in whole or part, by an AI — where 'inferred' may refer to
  the clinical content carried by the resource, the FHIR structure that
  carries it, or both."*
- Add a short subsection ("What 'AI-inferred' means in this IG") with one
  worked example for each of the three cases (content-only, structure-
  only, both).
- For the "encoding the distinction" part, document the **existing**
  Provenance pattern: recommend that authors use
  `Provenance.entity.what` to point at the AI-inferred content (text
  Attachment, contained Resource, etc.) vs. the resource as a whole, and
  use `Provenance.activity` from the IG's existing activity value set to
  distinguish the act. No new value-set bindings or extensions are
  introduced in this ballot; a tracker is filed to revisit a dedicated
  code/extension in the next normative cycle.

#### Justification

The triage classification on the Zulip thread places this ticket in
"Documentation & Clarity," which signals that the working group already
sees it as primarily a narrative issue rather than a structural one. The
distinction the reporter raises *can* be expressed today by careful use of
`Provenance.entity` + `Provenance.activity` — there is nothing in FHIR
Provenance that prevents pointing the activity at the text payload vs.
the wrapping resource. Shipping a narrative clarification now closes the
ballot comment without taking on the cost and risk (interop churn, value
set governance, profile changes) of inventing new codes mid-ballot. A
follow-on tracker preserves the option of formalizing the distinction
later if implementers actually request it.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket "Not Persuasive" / "Considered – No Change," on the
grounds that the IG already accommodates the distinction through generic
FHIR Provenance: implementers can already aim a `Provenance` (or
`Provenance.entity`) at the inferred narrative/contained content
separately from one aimed at the resource as a whole, and can choose
`activity` codings to describe what the AI did. No spec change is
required.

#### Justification

The two scenarios the reporter describes are not new artifact types; they
are different provenance assertions about the same artifact, and FHIR
already has the machinery for that. Adding new IG-specific codes or
extensions to encode "inferred-content vs. inferred-structure" risks
divergence from how Provenance is used elsewhere in FHIR and creates
parsing burden for receivers who already understand `Provenance.entity`
roles. The narrative wording is acceptable as written if read alongside
the Provenance examples in the IG.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the right balance for the January-2026 ballot cycle. The
reporter has surfaced a genuine ambiguity in the IG narrative — Disposition
C understates that — but a structural change introduced this late
(Disposition A) would expand the ballot's scope, require new value-set
governance, and almost certainly invalidate examples and tests for an IG
that is already absorbing 90 reconciliation items. The Zulip triage
already sorts this comment into "Documentation & Clarity," which is
consistent with handling it as a narrative clarification plus an explicit
recipe for using existing Provenance machinery to separate
content-inference from structure-inference, and filing a follow-on tracker
to revisit a dedicated code or extension in a future cycle if
implementers ask for it. That gives the reporter a substantive answer
without taking on mid-ballot model risk.
