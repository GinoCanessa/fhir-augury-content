## Summary

The reporter (Chirag Bhatt) asks the eLTSS work group to give clearer guidance
on how to represent complex service quantity / interval scenarios on the
eLTSS `ServiceRequest` profile. The current eLTSS "Additional Mapping
Details" guidance directs implementers to use `quantityQuantity` for simple
counts (e.g., "5 trips"), `quantityRatio` for rate-style intervals (e.g.,
"8 hours a day"), and prohibits using both at the same time. The reporter
argues that real-world LTSS schedules — e.g., "2 hours twice a day, one AM
and one PM", "4 hours each afternoon", or "Mon and Thu of each week" —
cannot be cleanly captured by `quantityRatio` alone, and that combining
`quantityRatio` with `occurrenceTiming` for a single concept becomes
confusing. The reporter's suggestion is to standardize on
`occurrenceTiming` (using `timeOfDay`, `dayOfWeek`, etc.) for all such
schedule-bearing quantities and avoid `quantityRatio` for multi-occurrence
patterns.

There are no FhirAugury cross-references for this ticket (Jira, Zulip,
Confluence, or GitHub). However, the comment trail explicitly mentions two
sibling tickets filed by the same reporter on the same eLTSS
`ServiceRequest` profile:

- [FHIR-23054](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-23054) — *Service
  Start/End Date — Use of occurrenceTiming or occurrencePeriod* (Triaged,

## Details

**Description:**

> In reference to Additional Mapping Details:
>
> 2) `quantityQuantity` can be used to represent simple quantities such as
> "1 installation" or "5 trips". `quantityRatio` can be used to represent
> quantities with intervals such as "8 hours a day" or "7 units per week".
> Either `quantityQuantity` or `quantityRatio` can be used, but not both
> for the same `ServiceRequest`.
>
> 4) numerator and denominator are used to represent a quantity with an
> interval. For example, to represent 8 hours a day, numerator → value
> would be "8" and numerator → unit would be "hour", while denominator →
> value would be "1" and denominator → unit would be "day".
>
> Comment: This provides very good guidance on how to populate quantity
> and units. However, there are instances in LTSS home services that are a
> little more complex, e.g., "2 hours twice a day, one AM and one PM" or
> "4 hours each afternoon/PM" or "Mon and Thu of each week", etc. These
> will need to be handled differently. Maybe these special situations will
> require using `occurrenceTiming → timeOfDay` or `dayOfWeek` etc. But
> note that it will get complex if `quantityRatio` and `occurrenceTiming`
> elements are used in combination to represent a single concept. So it
> will be better to use `occurrenceTiming` by itself for all situations
> without using `quantityRatio`, since numerator and denominator are
> probably not meant for representing multiple occurrences.

**Comments:**

- **bheale (2024-04-02):** `quantityRatio` has the capacity for coded
  units, and since 2020 the use can be for things such as frequency per
  day. Points to the current eLTSS `ServiceRequest` profile
  ([build](https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/eLTSS/StructureDefinition-ServiceRequest-eltss.html),
  [2024Jan](https://hl7.org/fhir/us/eltss/2024Jan/StructureDefinition-ServiceRequest-eltss.html)).
- **lynn_laakso (2024-05-27):** Reverted the previous resolution
  (Considered for Future Use, 2020-07-21, vote 12-0-1 Amber
  Patel/Kathleen Connor). Argues that `occurrenceTiming` is the right
  element to express frequency, duration and period; `quantityRatio` is
  not really a ratio in the "per day" sense and historically used free
  text for units. Suggests consulting O&O. Notes the disposition depends
  on what happens with [FHIR-23054](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-23054)
  and [FHIR-23055](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-23055) (cardinality
  on occurrence).
- **lynn_laakso (2024-05-27):** Re-open post STU2 publication.
- **bheale (2024-12-05):** This is nuanced. Coding numerator/denominator
  units is more precise. Occurrence indicates the number of repetitions
  of a service; the two are complementary, e.g., "every day for ten days
  (occurrenceTiming), give 3 shots per day (quantityRatio)". Cites the
  eLTSS 2024Jan implementation page on
  [Quantities, UCUM and the Timing data type](https://hl7.org/fhir/us/eltss/2024Jan/implementation.html#quantities-ucum-and-the-timing-data-type),
  whose worked example is `ServicePlan.quantity = 5 unit=[USD]/day` with
  `occurrenceTiming.boundsPeriod` May 31 – June 23 2023,
  `dayOfWeek = Mon, Wed`, `timeOfDay = 3 pm` — i.e., `quantityRatio`
  expresses the rate (dollars/day) while `occurrenceTiming` expresses
  *when* the rate applies.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the eLTSS `ServiceRequest` profile and its accompanying
implementation guidance (in `HL7/eLTSS`, the IG source for the eLTSS
`ServiceRequest-eltss` StructureDefinition and the `implementation.md`
section *"Quantities, UCUM and the Timing data type"*) to recommend
`ServiceRequest.occurrenceTiming` as the **single** carrier for all
multi-occurrence / scheduled service patterns, and explicitly discourage
using `ServiceRequest.quantity[x] = Ratio` to express
"per day" / "per week" frequency. Concretely:

- Tighten the profile's binding/guidance on `ServiceRequest.quantity[x]`
  to indicate that `Quantity` is the expected choice for simple totals
  (e.g., "5 trips", "1 installation"), and that `Ratio` should be
  reserved for true ratio quantities with coded UCUM units (e.g.,
  `[USD]/d`) — not for expressing schedule frequency.
- Strengthen the worked examples in the *Quantities, UCUM and the Timing
  data type* page to show frequency / day-of-week / time-of-day cases
  modelled solely with `occurrenceTiming.repeat` (`frequency`, `period`,
  `periodUnit`, `timeOfDay`, `dayOfWeek`).
- Add an explicit "do not use `quantityRatio` to express scheduling
  frequency; use `occurrenceTiming` instead" note.

#### Justification

The reporter's concern is real: when both `quantityRatio` and
`occurrenceTiming` carry frequency-shaped information, two profiles can
encode the same schedule in incompatible ways, harming interoperability.
`Timing` is FHIR's canonical schedule data type and is normative across
the spec for exactly this purpose, with first-class support for
`frequency`, `period`, `dayOfWeek`, and `timeOfDay`. Standardizing on
one element removes the ambiguity the reporter calls out and aligns
eLTSS with broader FHIR practice (e.g., MedicationRequest dosage
timing).

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep both elements available but **define a single, unambiguous
modelling rule** in eLTSS guidance, rather than removing `quantityRatio`
from the schedule-bearing role:

- In `HL7/eLTSS` `implementation.md` *Quantities, UCUM and the Timing
  data type*, codify the bheale 2024-12-05 division:
  - `ServiceRequest.occurrenceTiming` carries **when** the service
    happens (frequency, period, day-of-week, time-of-day,
    boundsPeriod).
  - `ServiceRequest.quantity[x]` carries **how much** of the service per
    occurrence: `Quantity` for absolute counts; `Ratio` only when the
    "amount per time" is intrinsic to the *unit* (e.g., `[USD]/d`,
    funded-hours/week budgets) and not a substitute for scheduling
    frequency.
- Add invariants / `mustSupport` notes (or human-readable guidance with
  a clear example) showing the canonical pattern from the existing
  worked example (`ServicePlan.quantity = 5 [USD]/d` +
  `occurrenceTiming.boundsPeriod / dayOfWeek / timeOfDay`).
- Provide concrete mapped examples for the reporter's three cases:
  - "2 hours twice a day, one AM and one PM" → `occurrenceTiming.repeat`
    with `frequency=2`, `period=1`, `periodUnit=d`,
    `timeOfDay=[09:00,15:00]`, `duration=2`, `durationUnit=h`.
  - "4 hours each afternoon" → `occurrenceTiming.repeat` with
    `when=[AFT]` (or `timeOfDay`), `duration=4`, `durationUnit=h`,
    `frequency=1`, `period=1`, `periodUnit=d`.
  - "Mon and Thu of each week" → `occurrenceTiming.repeat` with
    `dayOfWeek=[mon,thu]`, `frequency=1`, `period=1`, `periodUnit=wk`.

#### Justification

This addresses the reporter's actual pain point — implementer confusion
between two overlapping representations — while preserving the more
expressive option Brett Heale relies on. `quantityRatio` with coded UCUM
units genuinely is the right element when the *quantity itself* is rate-
shaped (a budget of dollars per day, a maximum hours-per-week
allocation), and removing that capability (Disposition A) loses
fidelity. The risk Disposition A solves is overlap; Disposition B solves
overlap by writing down a clear rule rather than by amputating an
element. It is also less disruptive to the published STU2 profile and to
existing implementers who already follow the eLTSS 2024Jan worked
example.

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve as *Persuasive with Mod* / *Not Persuasive* — close as
**Considered – No Change** on the grounds that the existing eLTSS
`ServiceRequest-eltss` profile and the *Quantities, UCUM and the Timing
data type* implementation page already provide a working pattern (the
2024Jan `ServicePlan.quantity = 5 [USD]/d` + `occurrenceTiming` example)
and that the choice between `Quantity` and `Ratio` for
`ServiceRequest.quantity[x]` is governed by the base FHIR spec, which is
out of scope for an eLTSS-only change.

#### Justification

The previous work-group disposition (2020-07-21, vote 12-0-1, Amber
Patel / Kathleen Connor) reached a similar conclusion ("Considered for
Future Use"). Since 2020 the eLTSS IG has shipped guidance and a worked
example covering the very pattern the reporter asks for, and FHIR-23055
(the sibling start/end-date question) has been *Resolved – No Change*.
Re-opening was procedurally driven (post-STU2 publication) rather than
prompted by new use-cases or implementer evidence. With no Zulip
discussion, no GitHub PR, and no other tickets cross-referencing it, the
case for a normative profile/guidance change has not been re-made.

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the right balance. The reporter's concern is genuine
and is not fully answered by the existing implementation guidance —
implementers reading just the *Additional Mapping Details* "either
quantityQuantity or quantityRatio, but not both" rule will not
naturally arrive at the bheale division of *quantity = how much*,
*occurrenceTiming = when*. Disposition A overcorrects by removing a
representation (`quantityRatio` with coded UCUM units) that is
demonstrably useful for budgeted-rate scenarios in LTSS funding and
that the IG's own worked example depends on. Disposition C is hard to
defend on the merits given that the previous "Considered for Future
Use" resolution was deliberately reverted in 2024-05 to allow
re-decision in light of the new IG content. Disposition B can be
delivered as a guidance / examples update on `HL7/eLTSS`
(`implementation.md` *Quantities, UCUM and the Timing data type* plus,
optionally, profile-level `mustSupport` / human-readable notes on
`ServiceRequest.quantity[x]` and `ServiceRequest.occurrence[x]`),
without touching base FHIR. It also unblocks a parallel decision on
[FHIR-23054](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-23054) by clarifying
which `occurrence[x]` choice eLTSS expects in which scenario, and
keeps the work-group's posture consistent with
[FHIR-23055](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-23055)'s "answer
already exists" resolution (the answer just needs to be made more
discoverable). bheale's suggestion to consult **Orders & Observations**
should be honoured before merge, since `ServiceRequest.occurrence[x]`
and `ServiceRequest.quantity[x]` are owned there.
