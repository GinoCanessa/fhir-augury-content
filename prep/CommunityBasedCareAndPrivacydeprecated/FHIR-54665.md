## Summary

John Moehrke reports that the narrative **Dependencies** section of the
Scalable Consent Management (FHIR) IG explicitly calls out only two of the
guide's four declared dependencies (Subscription and IHE-PCF), omitting
**US Core** and **IHE-BALP**. The IG Publisher's auto-generated dependency
fragment correctly enumerates all four, so the inconsistency is purely in
the hand-authored prose. The reporter asks that all four explicit
dependencies receive equal treatment in the narrative section.

The ticket has already been triaged with a resolution of **Persuasive** and
a resolution note of "We will ensure that all dependent guides are
explicitly listed and linked." There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets,
Zulip threads, or GitHub items associated with this issue in the indexed
data.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the hand-authored **Dependencies** narrative page in the Scalable
Consent Management (FHIR) IG so that all four declared dependencies receive
equivalent treatment:

1. Add narrative subsections (or list entries) for **HL7 US Core** and
   **IHE Basic Audit Log Patterns (IHE-BALP)** matching the depth and
   structure of the existing **Subscription** and **IHE-PCF** entries.
2. For each dependency, include: a short prose description of why the
   guide depends on it, a hyperlink to the canonical IG, and the version
   pin that matches `sushi-config.yaml` / `ImplementationGuide.dependsOn`.
3. Keep the IG Publisher auto-generated dependency fragment in place; the
   prose section should complement (not duplicate) it.
4. Verify that the page table-of-contents and any cross-page references
   to "the dependencies" still resolve correctly after the edit.

#### Justification

The reporter and the resolver have already converged: the auto-generated
fragment proves the underlying `dependsOn` declarations are correct, so
this is purely an editorial mismatch between hand-written prose and
machine-generated content. Treating the four dependencies asymmetrically
in narrative is misleading to implementers — readers may incorrectly
infer that US Core and IHE-BALP are optional or transitively pulled in,
when in fact they are first-class dependencies. The change is low risk,
non-normative, and aligns with the IG's stated dependency posture.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than maintaining a parallel hand-authored list that can drift again,
**replace the bespoke Dependencies prose with a thin wrapper around the IG
Publisher's auto-generated dependency table**, plus a single short
paragraph per dependency explaining *why* the guide depends on it. The
"what / version / link" content is sourced exclusively from the generated
fragment (`{% include dependency-table.xhtml %}` or equivalent), and the
authored prose is reduced to rationale only.

#### Justification

This addresses the root cause (drift between authored narrative and
machine-generated content) instead of the symptom (this particular
omission). It's consistent with how several other HL7 IGs structure their
dependency pages and prevents the same ticket from being filed again the
next time a `dependsOn` entry is added. Trade-off: it requires slightly
more editing now (restructuring the page rather than adding two entries)
and removes the editorial flexibility to write dependency-specific prose
in a uniform style.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request on the grounds that the IG Publisher's auto-generated
dependency fragment already enumerates all four dependencies correctly,
and the hand-authored Dependencies section is intended only to highlight
those dependencies the editors consider most relevant for narrative
discussion (Subscription and IHE-PCF), not to be exhaustive.

#### Justification

This is defensible only if the work group explicitly intends the prose
section to be selective rather than exhaustive — which is not the
position the reporter or the existing resolution note take. Declining
would leave the asymmetry in place and risk implementer confusion about
the status of US Core and IHE-BALP as dependencies. Listed for
completeness; not a strong option given the resolver has already
endorsed the fix.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is a small, well-scoped editorial fix that the resolver has
already marked Persuasive with a resolution note that maps directly onto
Disposition A ("ensure that all dependent guides are explicitly listed
and linked"). Implementers reading the IG should not be left to wonder
why two of four dependencies are discussed in prose while the other two
appear only in a generated table. Disposition B is a defensible
longer-term refactor and worth flagging to the editors as follow-up work,
but it is broader than what FHIR-54665 asks for and shouldn't block this
fix. Disposition C contradicts the existing resolution and the reporter's
clearly correct observation that the page is internally inconsistent with
its own generated content.
