## Summary

The reporter (Craig Newman) is asking the Community-Based Care and Privacy
work group to clarify, within the Scalable Consent Management specification,
what the `ConsentAuditEvent` artifact is meant to audit. Specifically, the
ambiguity is around the terms "disclosure" and "information": it is not clear
whether the audit event records (a) that the *content of the Consent record
itself* was shared/disclosed, or (b) that *some piece of information governed
by the Consent* (e.g., lab results that the patient has consented to share)
was shared/disclosed. The distinction materially changes how implementers
interpret the event's outcome. The ticket has been triaged as Persuasive,
and the work group's resolution direction (captured on the ticket) is to
recast the audit event as something a system creates whenever a Consent
record is *consulted* to make an information-sharing decision, to introduce
a new actor in the business cases, and to substantially rewrite the audit
event section (noting that there will be no operations, so the rewrite
will focus on how patients can discover when their Consent resources were
consulted). No related Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were
surfaced via cross-references.

## Details

**Description:**

> Clarification on the meaning of "disclosure" and "information" would be
> helpful. Is this an audit of whether or not the content/information
> contained in the Consent record was shared/disclosed or whether any
> information covered by the Consent (consent to share lab results, etc)
> was shared/disclosed? This impacts the implementer's understanding of
> what the outcome is.

**Resolution Description (from ticket snapshot):**

> The intent was that a FASTAuditEvent would be created by a system
> whenever a Consent record was consulted and a decision made on whether
> information should be shared or not. We are going to include a new
> actor in our business cases around this. And we are going to
> extensively revise the section on the audit event as there will be no
> operations so we will be discussing how patients can find out when
> consent resources were consulted for making an information sharing
> decision.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the Scalable Consent Management specification's `ConsentAuditEvent`
(a.k.a. `FASTAuditEvent`) section to add an explicit, normative-style
clarification of the terms "disclosure" and "information" as used by
this artifact. Concretely:

- Add a definition paragraph stating that, in this profile,
  "disclosure" refers to the *act of consulting the Consent resource
  and making an information-sharing decision*, and "information" refers
  to the data governed by the Consent (e.g., lab results), not to the
  Consent record's own contents.
- Add an `outcome` semantics table that maps the audit event's outcome
  values to the consent-decision result (permitted / denied /
  indeterminate) for the governed information.
- Add at least one worked example showing a `ConsentAuditEvent`
  instance produced when a system consults a Consent record and
  releases (or withholds) a lab result, with annotations pointing to
  which fields carry the "what was disclosed" vs. "which Consent was
  consulted" semantics.

#### Justification

The reporter's question is a definitional ambiguity, not a structural
defect — the cleanest fix is to disambiguate the prose in the place
implementers will read it (the audit-event section). The ticket's own
resolution direction confirms this is the intent: the audit event
records that a Consent resource was *consulted* to make an
information-sharing decision. Spelling that out, with an outcome
mapping and an example, removes the ambiguity for implementers without
restructuring any artifacts.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt the broader rewrite already described in the ticket's resolution
text rather than a localized clarification. Specifically:

- Rename or reframe `ConsentAuditEvent` so the artifact name itself
  signals "Consent-consulted audit" (e.g., keep the type
  `FASTAuditEvent` but make the section title and narrative pivot on
  "Auditing Consent consultations").
- Introduce the new actor described in the resolution (the system
  component that consults the Consent and records the audit) into the
  Scalable Consent Management business cases / actor model, and wire
  the audit event to that actor.
- Drop or defer any operation-shaped narrative (the resolution notes
  "there will be no operations") and replace it with a patient-facing
  section on *how patients discover when their Consent resources were
  consulted*, including the queries / filters they would use against
  the audit event store.
- Roll the Disposition A clarifications (definitions, outcome mapping,
  example) into this rewrite.

#### Justification

Per the ticket's resolution, the work group already plans to
"extensively revise" this section and to add a new actor. If that
larger rewrite is going to happen anyway, doing the clarification as
part of it (rather than as a standalone patch that will be overwritten)
avoids churn and produces a more coherent narrative — the
patient-discovery story and the actor model are the things that
actually make "disclosure" and "information" unambiguous in context,
because the actor and the patient-facing query make it obvious that the
audit is about *consultations of the Consent*, not about disclosures of
the Consent record itself. The trade-off vs. Disposition A is scope and
schedule: this is a larger edit and will land later.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket without spec changes, on the grounds that the existing
`AuditEvent` semantics already cover the scenario: the
`AuditEvent.entity` slots distinguish the Consent resource (the
*reference* / policy entity) from the data that was acted upon (the
*target* entity), and `AuditEvent.action` / `AuditEvent.outcome` carry
the result. Direct readers to the base `AuditEvent` guidance and mark
the question as "answered, no spec change required."

#### Justification

Defensible only if the work group judges that the ambiguity is purely a
reader-side issue and that the existing base `AuditEvent` documentation
already disambiguates Consent-as-policy vs. data-as-target. This keeps
the spec stable and avoids re-opening the audit-event section.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The ticket is marked Persuasive and the resolution text on the ticket
already commits the work group to an extensive rewrite of the audit
event section, the addition of a new actor, and a pivot to a
patient-discovery narrative. Disposition A would land a clarification
that the planned rewrite will then immediately overwrite, and
Disposition C is hard to defend given that an experienced reviewer
(Craig Newman) read the section and could not tell which of two very
different things the artifact was auditing — that's prima facie
evidence that base `AuditEvent` guidance is not enough in this context.
Disposition B folds the requested clarification into the work the group
already intends to do, producing a single coherent revision: a renamed
/ reframed audit section, a new actor in the business cases, an
outcome-semantics mapping, and a patient-facing discovery story. The
trade-off is that it will land later than a standalone clarification,
which the work group should weigh against the cost of re-editing the
same section twice.
