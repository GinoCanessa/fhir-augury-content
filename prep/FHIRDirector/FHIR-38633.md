## Summary

The ticket asks FHIR Core to add an optional element (or a core/standard
extension) capable of carrying a *patient's awareness* of a particular
clinical fact — for example, whether the patient has been informed of a
diagnosis or test result. The submitter points to the existing v3
TargetAwareness code system
(`2.16.840.1.113883.5.137`) and its bound value set
(`ValueSet-v3-TargetAwareness`), and notes that CDA already supports the
concept inline on results (via `<subject>/<awarenessCode>` plus a
`<relatedSubject>` describing whose awareness is being asserted). The
reporter believes the same capability should be available across all
Clinical Summary and Diagnostics resources (e.g., Condition,
Observation, DiagnosticReport).

The ticket has bounced through several work groups without resolution.
Patient Care/OO (Jan 2023) leaned toward a complex extension carrying
`patientAwareness (boolean)`, `additionalActor`, `relationshipType`,
`status`, and `time`. Security (John Moehrke, Sep 2024) argued an
extension is the wrong shape because the concept is needed on *any*
medical data and likely needs context (who, when, what was discussed),
suggesting an Encounter/EpisodeOfCare-shaped record might already cover
it. Patient Empowerment (Floyd Eisenberg, Sep 2024) framed the broader
need as a `subjectVerification`/`patientVerification` parallel to
existing `verificationStatus` mechanisms. FHIR-I reviewed at the May
2025 WGM and agreed the concept belongs in FHIR but identified open
design questions (whose awareness — patient, caregiver, family group,
etc.; whether timing/longitudinal tracking is required; whether
graduated levels of understanding need to be modelled) and reassigned
to Patient Care for clinical design with input from Patient
Empowerment. The Jan 2026 WGM Thurs Q4 FMG discussion routed the
ticket to **FHIR Director** for arbitration, which is where it sits
today.

The only cross-reference is the originating Zulip thread
([#terminology > Target Awareness (patient awareness)](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/terminology/topic/Target%20Awareness%20%28patient%20awareness%29));
no related Jira tickets, GitHub items, or Confluence pages are linked.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Define a single complex standard extension in the HL7 Extensions Pack
named `target-awareness` (canonical
`http://hl7.org/fhir/StructureDefinition/target-awareness`) usable on
any DomainResource (Context = `DomainResource`), with the structure
proposed by OO on 2023-01-10:

- `awareness` (root, complex extension)
  - `awarenessCode` — `code`, 1..1, bound (extensible) to a refreshed
    value set derived from
    `http://terminology.hl7.org/ValueSet/v3-TargetAwareness` (or a new
    UTG-managed value set if the v3 set is judged stale).
  - `subject` — `Reference(Patient | RelatedPerson | Practitioner |
    Group)`, 0..1, identifying *whose* awareness is asserted (defaults
    to the resource's subject when omitted).
  - `asserter` — `Reference(Practitioner | PractitionerRole |
    Patient | RelatedPerson)`, 0..1, who recorded the assertion.
  - `asserted` — `dateTime`, 0..1, when awareness was assessed.
  - `note` — `Annotation`, 0..*, free-text qualification.

Publish via the next Extensions Pack release as STU (no R6 ballot
dependency). Update the Clinical Summary and Diagnostics module pages
to mention the extension as a recommended hook for recording
patient/subject awareness on Condition, Observation, DiagnosticReport,
and AllergyIntolerance.

#### Justification

Mirrors the CDA capability the reporter cites and stays close to the
OO-proposed shape. Using the Extensions Pack avoids gating on R6 (FMG
and the reporter both flagged the deadline-vs-utility tension) and
keeps the change reversible while real-world usage matures. Adding
`subject`, `asserter`, and `asserted` directly addresses FHIR-I's open
questions (whose awareness, when assessed) without escalating to a
full new resource. Lloyd McKenzie's Zulip note confirms FHIR-I owns
the extension domain when scope is "any DomainResource".

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Decline to add a new extension and instead document a normative
guidance pattern in the Communication and Security/Privacy modules
that combines two existing mechanisms:

1. **Recording that information *was* shared with the patient** — use
   `Communication` (or `CommunicationRequest` / `Task` for planned
   sharing), with `Communication.about` pointing at the relevant
   clinical resource(s), `Communication.recipient` carrying the
   patient/RelatedPerson, and `Communication.topic` or a small bound
   `reason` slice naming the awareness event.
2. **Recording that information *should not* be shared** — use the
   existing `Resource.meta.security` confidentiality/handling
   vocabulary (e.g., the `_ActPrivacyPolicy` codes that include
   "redact"/"do not disclose" semantics) as Lloyd McKenzie noted on
   Zulip.

Publish a short "Patient Awareness" pattern page under the Clinical
Summary module with worked examples for Condition, Observation, and
DiagnosticReport, and add cross-references from those resource pages.
No StructureDefinition changes are needed.

#### Justification

Addresses John Moehrke's concern that patient awareness is not really
a property of the clinical fact but rather a record of an
interaction (who told whom, when, in what context), which is what
Communication is designed for. Avoids proliferating a new optional
extension that may or may not be populated consistently — and avoids
the FHIR-I worry that an "applies-to-any-DomainResource" extension
becomes a dumping ground. Reuses already-deployed terminology
(`_ActPrivacyPolicy`) for the negative case ("do not tell"), which has
production tooling support today. Trade-off vs. A: implementers cannot
make a single point assertion alongside the result (they must walk to
a separate Communication), but the data model is honest about the
fact that awareness is an event with an actor and a time.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (with redirect)

#### Proposal

Decline the request as filed and close as **Persuasive with Mod / Not
Persuasive** with the disposition: "Out of scope for FHIR Core. The
need is real but the modelling questions (audience scope, temporal
tracking, levels of understanding, interaction context) make this
unsuitable for a core extension applied to all clinical resources."
Recommend the requester (and NIH/MCC IG community) instead:

- File the awareness pattern as a profile-level extension in the
  Multiple Chronic Care IG (or a dedicated patient-empowerment IG),
  where it can mature against real implementations, and
- Bring a refined, scoped proposal back through Patient Empowerment
  for a possible R7+ resource (e.g., `PatientEmpowermentRecord` or
  the `subjectVerification` element pattern Floyd Eisenberg
  suggested) once usage data exists.

Document the redirect in the resolution comment so future submitters
can find the prior art.

#### Justification

The thread shows three years of unresolved design debate across four
work groups (Terminology → OO → Security → Patient Care/Patient
Empowerment → FHIR-I → FHIR Director) precisely because the concept
is broader than "a flag on Observation". Shipping a thin extension
now risks freezing a design that the community has explicitly said
needs more thought (FHIR-I's May 2025 comment, Moehrke's Sep 2024
comment). The reporter has already said an STU extension is
acceptable; an IG-scoped extension delivers the same implementer
value without burning a core extension URL on a still-evolving
concept. Existing mechanisms (Communication, `meta.security`,
verification-status patterns) cover the immediate clinical-safety
case ("do not tell patient") today.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition: A**

The ticket has been open since 2022 and the only thing keeping it
from closing has been the lack of a venue and a champion. The
substantive design questions FHIR-I raised in May 2025 (whose
awareness, when, what level) are all answerable inside the
extension shape OO proposed in 2023-01 with three small additions
(`subject`, `asserter`, `asserted`). Disposition A delivers that as a
standard extension via the Extensions Pack, which is exactly the
release vehicle the reporter and Grahame's earlier comment pointed
to, and it does not block on R6. Disposition B is conceptually
cleaner but asks every implementer to author a separate
Communication resource just to assert "patient knows about this
finding," which is a step backward in ergonomics from the inline CDA
pattern the reporter is migrating from — and Communication is not
universally implemented on the diagnostics side. Disposition C is
the safe parking-lot answer but effectively repeats the cycle that
got the ticket to FHIR Director in the first place. Recommend FHIR
Director route the ticket back to FHIR-I with a directive to publish
under the Extensions Pack on the schedule of A, with the OO + FHIR-I
comments captured in the change-note to forestall the next round of
re-litigation.
