## Summary

Virginia Lorenzi requests a wording change in the Personal Health Record
(PHR) FHIR Implementation Guide to clarify that the IG defines a *FHIR
specification for communicating PHR content* — not a specification for
building a PHR system itself. The current text frames the "Standard
Patient Health Record" as if it were itself a record system that assumes
FHIR-format contents; the requested replacement reframes the document as
an Implementation Guide that provides a standard mechanism for
communicating any of the records contained in a particular PHR. No
cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were
surfaced for this ticket.

## Details

**Description:**

> Change "At the core of the Standard Patient Health Record is the
> assumption that it will contain records in FHIR format, which provides
> a data model describing health history that has a pedigree of 30 years
> of industry experience and use in major EHRs." to "The intention of
> the PHR FHIR Implementation Guide is to provide the standard mechanism
> for the communication of any of the records contained in a particular
> PHR"
>
> Rationale - it is important that the spec not be confused with a spec
> for a PHR - it is meant to be used with PHRs or by PHRs.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Replace the sentence in the PHR FHIR IG narrative that currently reads:

> "At the core of the Standard Patient Health Record is the assumption
> that it will contain records in FHIR format, which provides a data
> model describing health history that has a pedigree of 30 years of
> industry experience and use in major EHRs."

with the wording supplied by the reporter:

> "The intention of the PHR FHIR Implementation Guide is to provide the
> standard mechanism for the communication of any of the records
> contained in a particular PHR."

The change is purely narrative (no resource, profile, extension, value
set, or constraint impact). Apply the edit in the introductory page of
the Personal Health Record (FHIR) IG where the original sentence
appears, and verify no other places in the IG repeat the same framing
(updating those for consistency if they do).

#### Justification

The reporter's wording directly addresses the ambiguity she identifies:
the original sentence personifies "the Standard Patient Health Record"
as if the IG itself were the PHR, while the replacement explicitly
frames the artifact as an Implementation Guide whose purpose is
communication of PHR content. This is consistent with how other HL7
FHIR IGs introduce themselves (an IG describes interoperability
mechanisms; it is not the system being interoperated). Because no
normative content changes, the risk of the edit is minimal and it can
land as an editorial fix.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the *intent* of the request but expand the edit into a small
scope-clarification paragraph rather than a single-sentence swap.
Concretely, in the same introductory section:

1. Open with a one-sentence "Scope of this IG" statement modeled on the
   reporter's wording: "The PHR FHIR Implementation Guide defines the
   standard FHIR mechanism for communicating the records contained in a
   Personal Health Record (PHR); it does not specify how a PHR system
   is built or operated."
2. Follow with one or two sentences that retain the useful framing from
   the original text — that PHRs in scope are expected to hold records
   represented in FHIR, and that FHIR provides a mature data model for
   health information — but attribute those statements to FHIR / the
   ecosystem rather than to "the Standard Patient Health Record."
3. Cross-reference (or add) a short "Out of Scope" bullet list
   explicitly calling out PHR system architecture, storage, UI, and
   conformance of PHR products.

#### Justification

The reporter's replacement sentence fixes the misleading framing but
also drops content (the FHIR data-model maturity argument) that is
plausibly useful context for new readers. A slightly larger edit
preserves that context while making the IG-vs-system distinction
unambiguous, and an explicit "Out of Scope" call-out reduces the chance
the same confusion resurfaces from a *different* sentence later. This
is still an editorial change, but it addresses the root concern (the
spec being mistaken for a PHR specification) more durably than a
single-sentence swap.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change as a no-op editorial preference: leave the existing
sentence in place on the grounds that the surrounding IG material
already makes clear that this is an Implementation Guide rather than a
PHR product specification, and reword only if a concrete misreading is
demonstrated.

#### Justification

This is the weakest of the three options and is included for
completeness. It is defensible only if the workgroup believes the
existing text, read in context, is not actually misleading — e.g.,
because the IG title, publisher metadata, and adjacent paragraphs
already establish that the artifact is an IG. Declining avoids any
churn in the introductory narrative but does nothing to address a
concern raised by an experienced PHR-domain reporter, who is unlikely
to flag this as "Highest" priority unless the framing has actually
caused confusion.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The reporter's underlying concern — that readers confuse the IG with a
PHR system specification — is real and worth fixing, so doing nothing
(Disposition C) is hard to justify, especially given the reporter
filed this at Highest priority. A literal one-sentence swap
(Disposition A) is acceptable and low-risk, but it discards the useful
framing that PHRs in scope hold FHIR-format records and that FHIR is a
mature data model. Disposition B keeps that context, fixes the
misleading personification of "the Standard Patient Health Record," and
adds an explicit out-of-scope statement that prevents the same
confusion from recurring elsewhere in the IG. Because the change is
still purely editorial, the additional cost over Disposition A is
small while the clarity gain is meaningfully larger.
