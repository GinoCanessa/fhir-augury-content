## Summary

The reporter raises a structural critique of the Personal Health Record (PHR)
FHIR IG, asserting that the IG's overall organization makes it hard for
implementers to navigate. Three concrete observations are made:
(1) the "Longitudinal Records" page is positioned at the very end of the IG
even though it appears to be foundational; (2) functional model references
are scattered rather than grouped; and (3) substantial content that is not
strictly about the sPHR data exchange mechanism — Apple HealthKit material,
and discussions of Algorithms, Physiological Domains, and Annotations — is
mixed in with the core normative material. The reporter suggests that the IG
might be split into multiple IGs, with the HealthKit-oriented content and
the algorithm/physiology/annotation guidance pulled out into separate,
implementation-oriented artifacts so that the core sPHR exchange mechanism
stands on its own. No related Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items
were surfaced via cross-references for this ticket.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat this as a structural/editorial work item against the PHR IG and
execute the reorganization the reporter describes:

1. Promote **Longitudinal Records** to a top-level navigational position
   (early in the table of contents, alongside the other foundational
   conformance pages) rather than leaving it at page 23.
2. Group all **functional model** references onto (or under) a single
   parent page so that implementers can find the conformance/functional
   material in one place.
3. Carve the IG into multiple deliverables:
   - A core **sPHR data exchange** IG containing only the resources,
     profiles, operations, and conformance material needed for PHR data
     exchange.
   - A separate **Apple HealthKit mapping** artifact (which, per the
     resolution note, has already been broken out and sent to LOINC).
   - A separate informative **PHR implementation guidance** artifact
     containing the Algorithms, Physiological Domains, and Annotations
     content.
4. Update the IG's introduction and navigation/menu to reflect the new
   structure and to cross-link the spun-out artifacts.

#### Justification

This matches what the reporter actually asked for and aligns with the WG's
own resolution note ("Persuasive… we substantially agree"), which records
that the HealthKit content has already been moved out and PGHD content has
already been merged in. FHIR IG navigability is repeatedly cited as an
implementer pain point, and HL7's IG publishing tooling has no problem
hosting a small family of related IGs that link to each other. Promoting
Longitudinal Records and consolidating functional-model pages is purely
editorial and carries no normative risk.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the PHR IG as a **single deliverable** but restructure it internally
so it reads like a layered guide rather than splitting it into multiple
IGs:

- Reorder the table of contents into clearly-named sections
  (e.g., *Foundations* → *Conformance & Functional Model* →
  *Exchange Mechanism* → *Profiles & Resources* → *Implementation
  Guidance (informative)* → *Appendices*).
- Move Longitudinal Records into *Foundations* and group all functional
  model pages under *Conformance & Functional Model*.
- Mark the Algorithms, Physiological Domains, Annotations, and any
  remaining HealthKit-flavored content explicitly as **informative**
  appendices within the IG, rather than spinning them out into separate
  IGs.
- Add a navigation landing page that explains the layering and tells
  different implementer personas (PHR app vendor, EHR integrator,
  device/HealthKit integrator) which sections they need to read.

#### Justification

A single IG with a strong information architecture preserves a single
canonical URL and version stream, avoids the overhead of standing up,
balloting, and maintaining multiple IGs in parallel, and avoids cross-IG
dependency management. Many of the reporter's concerns (page ordering,
grouping, separation of normative vs. informative material) can be solved
without an IG split. This is also closer to what has actually happened in
practice: the WG's resolution note describes incremental restructuring and
content moves rather than a full split. An informative-vs-normative
labeling pass plus a TOC reorder addresses ~80% of the complaint at a
fraction of the cost of multi-IG separation.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the ticket on the grounds that it is too broad to be actionable as
a single change request: it bundles editorial reordering, page grouping,
and a strategic decision to split the IG into multiple deliverables. Ask
the reporter to refile as a set of narrowly-scoped tickets — for example,
one ticket per page move and a separate work-group-level proposal for any
IG split.

#### Justification

"Reorganize the IG" tickets are difficult to disposition because they
have no objective acceptance criteria and tend to drift across ballot
cycles. Splitting an IG also has downstream consequences (new IG ids,
new package identifiers, dependency declarations, and re-ballot
obligations) that are out of scope for a single Change Request and that
warrant a WG-level decision rather than a ticket-level one.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A (with the scoping discipline of B as
guardrails)

The ticket is already marked **Persuasive with Modification**, and the
WG's own resolution note confirms that the HealthKit content has been
moved out and PGHD has been merged in — i.e., the substantive direction
of Disposition A is already underway. The remaining work (promote
Longitudinal Records, group functional-model pages, finish carving out
informative implementation guidance, finalize navigation) is editorial
and squarely within Patient Empowerment's authority over the PHR IG.
Declining (Disposition C) would contradict the resolution that has
already been recorded. Disposition B's value is as a *constraint* on A:
when applying A, prefer reordering and informative-section labeling over
spinning up entirely new IGs unless there is a concrete reason
(governance, separate ballot cadence, distinct audience) that justifies
the additional artifact. A short editorial pass at the end of ballot
reconciliation — as awatson1978 already proposed in the 2025-08-21
comment — is the right vehicle to land the remaining changes.
