## Summary

Craig Newman reports that the Personal Health Record (PHR) FHIR Implementation
Guide contains significant overlap among several "persona" pages — specifically
calling out the Patient Personas page (11), the William Sim page (12), and the
Personas Index page (19). The reporter asks the Patient Empowerment work group
to ensure each piece of persona content appears exactly once across the IG
rather than being duplicated across multiple narrative pages. The ticket is a
pure documentation/IG-structure change request: it does not propose any
conformance, profile, extension, terminology, or example change. No related
Jira tickets, Zulip discussions, GitHub PRs/issues, or other cross-references
were surfaced for this ticket in the indexed data, so the request stands on its
own.

## Proposed Dispositions

> Note: because no `repo-analysis` briefing is available for the PHR FHIR IG

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat this as a Persuasive editorial change against the PHR FHIR IG narrative.
Concretely:

1. Designate one canonical home for each piece of persona content. Recommended
   structure:
   - `Personas Index` (page 19): becomes the single landing/overview page
     listing each persona by name with a one-line summary and a link to the
     persona's own detail page. It contains *no* persona biographical detail
     itself.
   - `Patient Personas` (page 11): merge any unique content from this page
     into either the Personas Index (if it is meta/overview material) or
     into the corresponding per-persona detail page (e.g., William Sim) if
     it is biographical. Then either redirect/remove the page, or replace
     its body with a short pointer to the Personas Index.
   - `William Sim` (page 12) and any other per-persona pages: keep as the
     single canonical home for that persona's narrative, demographics,
     story, and any example references. Remove any duplication of this
     content from the Personas Index or Patient Personas pages.
2. Audit the IG menu (`input/data/menu.json` or equivalent in the PHR IG
   sushi/IG-publisher project) to ensure each page appears in exactly one
   logical location and that the navigation reflects the new
   index → per-persona structure.
3. Add cross-page links from the Personas Index entries to each per-persona
   page so navigation is preserved without duplicating prose.

#### Justification

This matches what the ticket literally asks for ("content is only presented
once"). It preserves all existing personas and narrative, reduces
maintenance burden (any future edit to a persona's biography happens in
exactly one file), and prevents drift between duplicated copies. The pattern
of "index page + per-item detail pages" is the standard IG narrative pattern
used elsewhere in HL7 IGs. The change is editorial and does not affect any
StructureDefinition, ValueSet, CapabilityStatement, or example, so risk to
implementers is minimal.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than restructuring three pages into an index + per-persona layout,
**collapse the persona material into a single page** and remove the other
two. Concretely:

- Keep `Patient Personas` (page 11) as the single home for *all* persona
  narrative, with one section per persona (William Sim, etc.).
- Remove the `William Sim` standalone page (page 12) and the
  `Personas Index` page (page 19) from the IG, updating the menu and any
  inbound links from other IG pages and from examples.
- Add anchor links (`#william-sim`, etc.) so any external references can
  still deep-link to a specific persona section.

#### Justification

Personal Health Record IGs typically use personas only as illustrative
narrative; they are not normative artifacts and rarely justify a page each.
Collapsing to one page removes the duplication problem by eliminating the
opportunity for it, simplifies the table of contents, and keeps the
narrative compact for readers. Trade-off vs. Disposition A: it sacrifices
the "index + detail" structure (which scales well if the PHR IG later adds
many personas) in favour of immediate simplicity. Precedent for
consolidating or removing persona content exists in other HL7 IGs (see
related FHIR-31142 in US SDOH, where a Personas page was dropped entirely
as Persuasive).

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve as **Not Persuasive — Editorial / No Change Required**, on the
grounds that the persona pages serve different audiences (an Index for
quick scanning, a per-persona page for detail, and a Patient Personas
overview for context) and the perceived "overlap" is intentional summary
duplication that aids navigation in a long IG.

#### Justification

This disposition is defensible only if the work group has previously made a
deliberate editorial choice to maintain summary content in multiple places
(for navigation/SEO/printability reasons) and is willing to accept the
maintenance cost. It would be the appropriate response if the ticket has
mischaracterized intentional summarization as duplication. However, the
reporter is a credible domain reviewer (Craig Newman) and the request is
trivial to honour, so this disposition would need a strong editorial
rationale to be sustainable on appeal.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A (Accept As Requested with an Index + per-persona-detail
restructure) is recommended. It directly addresses the reporter's concern,
follows the dominant IG narrative pattern (index page + detail pages),
preserves all existing persona content, and leaves room to add more
personas in future without re-introducing duplication. Disposition B
(collapse to one page) is a reasonable simpler alternative and should be
chosen if the work group does not anticipate adding more personas; it
mirrors the "drop the persona page" precedent from FHIR-31142 in US SDOH.
Disposition C (Decline) is hard to defend given the request is editorial,
low-risk, and originates from an external reviewer — declining without a
clear editorial rationale would invite an appeal. Whichever of A or B is
chosen, the work group should also verify the IG menu and any inbound
links from example narratives are updated in the same pull request so the
change lands atomically.
