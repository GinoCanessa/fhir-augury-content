## Summary

This ticket asks the Patient Empowerment work group to re-anchor the
canonical URL of the `PatientCorrectionOutputTypes` CodeSystem in the
Patient Corrections (FHIR) IG so that it lives under
`terminology.hl7.org` (THO) rather than `hl7.org/fhir/uv/patient-corrections`.
The current canonical
(`http://hl7.org/fhir/uv/patient-corrections/CodeSystem/PatientCorrectionOutputTypes`)
follows the old IG-local pattern, but TSMG best practice — captured in
the January 2022 TSMG CodeSystem Identifier Task Force deck and the R5
terminologies guidance — is that HL7-defined CodeSystems should be
registered in THO and use THO-anchored canonicals. The reporter notes
that a corresponding UTG ticket should be opened to register the
CodeSystem in THO so that the IG can then point at it. The ticket was
originally dispositioned "Considered for Future Use" in October 2022,
but Lynn Laakso reverted that resolution on 2025-07-10 with a note to
"re-open post STU1 publication", indicating the work group now intends
to act on it after the STU1 ballot/publication of Patient Corrections.
There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets, no Zulip discussions, and
no related GitHub items in the indexed sources for this ticket — only
the Confluence link to the TSMG slide deck is detected.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Accept the request and migrate the `PatientCorrectionOutputTypes`
CodeSystem to be anchored in THO. Concretely:

1. Open a companion UTG ticket against the `HL7/UTG` repository to
   register `PatientCorrectionOutputTypes` in
   `terminology.hl7.org` with canonical
   `http://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/PatientCorrectionOutputTypes`,
   carrying over `name`, `title`, `status`, `experimental`,
   `caseSensitive`, `content`, `concept[]`, designations, and
   properties unchanged.
2. Once UTG publishes the CodeSystem, change the
   `CodeSystem.url` of `PatientCorrectionOutputTypes` in the
   Patient Corrections IG sources from
   `http://hl7.org/fhir/uv/patient-corrections/CodeSystem/PatientCorrectionOutputTypes`
   to the THO canonical above.
3. Update every ValueSet `compose.include.system` reference within
   the Patient Corrections IG that points at the old IG-local
   canonical to point at the THO canonical, and update narrative /
   documentation pages that quote the old URL.
4. Coordinate the change with TSMG and ensure the Patient
   Corrections IG depends on the THO release that publishes the
   relocated CodeSystem.

This is a post-STU1 change, consistent with Lynn Laakso's
2025-07-10 note to re-open after STU1 publication.

#### Justification

This is exactly what TSMG best practice (January 2022 CodeSystem
Identifier Task Force deck) and the R5 `terminologies.html#system`
guidance prescribe: HL7-defined CodeSystems with cross-IG / cross-
realm potential live in THO, not under an individual IG's
canonical namespace. Honouring that policy keeps the CodeSystem
discoverable through the standard HL7 terminology infrastructure,
allows other IGs and implementers to reuse it without taking a
dependency on the Patient Corrections IG, and aligns Patient
Corrections with the pattern applied across the rest of the HL7
universal IGs. Doing it post-STU1 (rather than during STU1) avoids
disrupting the in-flight ballot while still resolving the long-
standing TSMG conformance issue before STU2.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the migration in principle, but avoid breaking implementers
of STU1 by introducing a **deprecation alias** rather than a hard
canonical swap:

1. Register the CodeSystem in THO at
   `http://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/PatientCorrectionOutputTypes`
   (same UTG step as Disposition A) and treat that as the
   normative canonical going forward.
2. In the Patient Corrections IG, retain the old
   `http://hl7.org/fhir/uv/patient-corrections/CodeSystem/PatientCorrectionOutputTypes`
   canonical as a documented alias / "previous canonical" entry
   (e.g., via `CodeSystem.identifier` carrying the old URL, plus a
   narrative note in the IG's terminology page) for at least one
   STU cycle.
3. Update IG-internal ValueSet bindings to the new THO canonical,
   but publish a guidance note that servers may continue to accept
   the legacy URL for inbound resources for a defined transition
   period.
4. Plan to remove the legacy alias in the STU release after the
   transitional one.

#### Justification

The CodeSystem has been published since the IG's pre-STU1
snapshots, which means some early implementers / connectathon
participants may already be persisting `Coding.system` values that
use the old IG-local canonical. A bare canonical swap (Disposition
A) silently invalidates those resources against the new ValueSets.
A documented alias period preserves the TSMG conformance goal of
this ticket while giving implementers a clear, time-bounded
migration path. The cost is modest — extra narrative and an
`identifier` element — and the trade-off favours implementer
goodwill, particularly for an IG whose stakeholders include patient-
facing tooling vendors where forced URL churn is expensive.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the relocation and keep
`http://hl7.org/fhir/uv/patient-corrections/CodeSystem/PatientCorrectionOutputTypes`
as the canonical for the
foreseeable future, on the grounds that the CodeSystem is currently
used only inside the Patient Corrections IG and that the cost /
risk of a THO migration outweighs the conformance benefit. Document
in the IG that the CodeSystem is IG-local by design, and close
this ticket as "Not Persuasive — IG-local CodeSystem".

#### Justification

A defensible-but-weak rationale: the CodeSystem describes outputs
specific to the Patient Corrections workflow, has no observed
cross-IG reuse today, and a relocation introduces a UTG dependency,
a publication coordination step with TSMG, and (without the
Disposition B alias) a breaking change for early implementers. If
the work group judges that the operational cost is greater than the
TSMG-policy benefit — particularly given the 2022 work group vote
that originally deferred this — decline is internally consistent
with that earlier position.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the right balance. The substantive question — "should
this CodeSystem live in THO?" — has effectively been answered by TSMG
policy and by the R5 terminologies guidance, and the work group's
2025-07-10 reversal of the prior "Considered for Future Use"
resolution signals an intent to comply, not to defer again. So
Disposition C is hard to defend on the merits and would simply
re-litigate a settled policy question. Disposition A is the
"clean" answer, but because `PatientCorrectionOutputTypes` has
already been published under the IG-local canonical and STU1
implementers are likely to be coding against it, a hard swap is
needlessly disruptive. Disposition B captures the TSMG-conformance
win that this ticket was opened to achieve, while giving
implementers an explicit, bounded migration runway via a documented
legacy-canonical alias. It also lines up cleanly with the
"post-STU1" timing Lynn Laakso called out: the THO registration
and IG canonical change land in the next STU, the alias rides
along for that STU, and it is removed in the STU after.
