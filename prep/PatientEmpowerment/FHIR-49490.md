## Summary

FHIR-49490 reports that the Personal Health Record (PHR) IG, although
published as a Universal Realm specification, is heavily US-centric — most
visibly on the Getting Started page, where the un-annotated "relevant law"
list reads as a US-only artifact. The reporter argues the IG must either
be re-scoped to US Realm or take in substantially more non-US input so it
genuinely behaves as a UV specification.

The ticket is tightly coupled to [FHIR-49224](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-49224)
("If the IG is Universal, I would expect also other set of Laws"), filed
by Ricardo Quintano against the same Section 1.3, which calls out the
same gap in narrower terms. Both tickets were dispositioned as
**Persuasive** by Patient Empowerment, with a commitment to reach out to
the Netherlands and Japan for local PHR-related laws and additional
examples. The work was carried out in
[HL7/personal-health-record-format-ig#72](https://github.com/HL7/personal-health-record-format-ig/pull/72)
("FHIR-49224 - Table info on international laws"), which adds an
international laws table to `input/pagecontent/index.md` and was merged
to `master` on 2025-09-29.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Formally close FHIR-49490 as **Persuasive — Resolved**, ratifying the
change already merged in
[`HL7/personal-health-record-format-ig` PR #72](https://github.com/HL7/personal-health-record-format-ig/pull/72).
That PR replaced the un-annotated US-centric "relevant law" list in
`input/pagecontent/index.md` (Section 1.3, Getting Started) with a table
of international laws, addressing the headline complaint that the
Getting Started page reads as US-only. Verify that the merged content:
(1) annotates each entry with its jurisdiction, (2) includes the
non-US jurisdictions Patient Empowerment committed to (Netherlands and
Japan, plus any others returned), and (3) makes clear in surrounding
narrative that the IG remains UV and the listed laws are illustrative,
not exhaustive. Mirror the same disposition on FHIR-49224.

#### Justification

The reporter's stated remedy was "either move to US Realm or take in
more non-US input." Patient Empowerment chose the second option, did
the outreach (Netherlands, Japan), and shipped the change as a merged
PR explicitly tagged for both FHIR-49490 and FHIR-49224. The motion
(Hasashi / Rachel, 4-0-0) is already recorded. Re-classifying or
re-scoping the IG as US Realm at this point would invalidate already-
shipped UV content and would be disproportionate to a narrative-page
defect. Accepting as requested also keeps the disposition consistent
with FHIR-49224, which is the technical-correction sibling of this
change request.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the merged PR #72 content as a partial answer, but expand the
remediation beyond a single table on the Getting Started page:

1. Extract the international-law content from
   `input/pagecontent/index.md` into a dedicated narrative page (e.g.
   `jurisdictional-considerations.md`) so the Getting Started page can
   stay short and UV-neutral.
2. Add an explicit "Realm and Jurisdictional Scope" callout near the
   top of the IG that states the IG is UV, that the law table is
   illustrative, and that implementers must confirm their own
   jurisdiction's requirements.
3. Open a small follow-up ticket to track ongoing community
   contributions of jurisdiction entries (Canada, EU/GDPR, AU, UK,
   etc.) so the table doesn't quietly drift back to a US-leaning state.

#### Justification

The reporter's framing ("US-centric … as well as may other aspects")
suggests the Getting Started "relevant law" list is the most visible
example of a broader pattern. A single PR fixes the headline example
but does not structurally protect the IG against re-introducing
US-centric assumptions elsewhere. Splitting jurisdictional content out
of Getting Started and adding an explicit realm/jurisdiction callout
gives reviewers and implementers a stable, signposted home for non-US
material and a clear contract about scope. The follow-up ticket
operationalises the WG's outreach commitment so it doesn't end with
just Netherlands and Japan.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-49490 as **Not Persuasive** on the grounds that the issue
is duplicative of FHIR-49224 and conflates an editorial complaint
("Getting Started reads as US-only") with a structural request
("re-scope the IG"). Decline the implicit "re-scope to US Realm"
option, leaving FHIR-49224 as the single tracked Technical Correction
for the international-law gap.

#### Justification

FHIR-49224 already covers the concrete defect (Section 1.3 listing
only US laws) and has its own resolution path. FHIR-49490 adds a
broader assertion ("as well as may other aspects") without supplying
specific examples beyond the same "relevant law" list, which makes the
ticket hard to action independently. Declining as duplicative would
keep the workgroup's tracking clean without losing any actual change,
since the merged PR is already attributed to FHIR-49224.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket has already been dispositioned **Persuasive** by Patient
Empowerment, the outreach to Netherlands and Japan was performed, and
the resulting PR
[`personal-health-record-format-ig#72`](https://github.com/HL7/personal-health-record-format-ig/pull/72)
is merged to `master` (2025-09-29) with the explicit body
"Addresses: FHIR-49224, FHIR-49490". The existing motion (Hasashi /
Rachel, 4-0-0) ratifies that path. Disposition A simply records the
state on the ground and aligns the resolution of FHIR-49490 with its
sibling FHIR-49224. Disposition B is a sensible *follow-up* (and the
workgroup should consider opening a separate ticket for the structural
"realm scope callout" and a tracking ticket for additional
jurisdictions), but it should not block closing this ticket.
Disposition C is hard to defend now that a merged PR explicitly cites
this ticket key. Before any further material expansion of the
international-laws content, run the `repo-analysis` skill for
`HL7/personal-health-record-format-ig` so future dispositions can be
grounded in a current repo briefing.
