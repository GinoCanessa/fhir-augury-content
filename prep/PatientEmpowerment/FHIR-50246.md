## Summary

The reporter flagged a typo in the History section of the Personal Health
Record (PHR) FHIR specification: the word "physicials" should be
"physicians" in the sentence "Medical records have been around since as
long as there have been practicing physicials". Alongside the typo, the
reporter raises a broader, secondary question about long-term
preservation of electronic health records over a patient's (potentially
100+ year) lifespan, and whether the spec should call out the need for a
standardized migration pathway across present and future storage media
so that a concise chronological patient history remains available for
differential-diagnosis use. No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip
threads, or GitHub items were found for this ticket.

## Details

**Description:**

> Typo in PHR History section. "Medical records have been around since as
> long as there have been practicing physicials" Should this be

> span? (disks, diskettes, tape, CD, devices, etc vs written/typed

> of developing a differential diagnosis suggesting the need for a
> standardized migration pathway across present and future devices?

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat this as a pure editorial Technical Correction against the PHR
(Personal Health Record) FHIR specification's History narrative page.
Replace the misspelling "physicials" with "physicians" in the sentence
"Medical records have been around since as long as there have been
practicing physicials." No structural, conformance, or schema change is
implied. The companion "associated question" about long-term record
preservation is acknowledged but explicitly out of scope for this
correction; if the work group wants to act on it, it should be split
into a separate, non-Technical-Correction ticket.

#### Justification

The change is a single-character spelling fix in narrative text on a
PHR page. It carries zero conformance impact, no breaking-change risk,
and matches the ticket's `Technical Correction` type. Splitting off the
"records over a 100-year lifespan / media migration" question keeps
this ticket scoped to something that can be applied immediately while
the bigger, genuinely substantive question gets the discussion it
deserves rather than being silently bundled into a typo fix.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Apply the typo fix as in Disposition A, **and** add a short editorial
note (one or two sentences) to the same History section
acknowledging long-term record preservation and media migration as an
emerging concern for PHR systems — without normative requirements.
Concretely: after fixing "physicials" → "physicians", append a sentence
along the lines of "Because a patient's record may need to remain
accessible for a lifetime that can exceed a century, PHR implementers
should plan for migration of records across changing storage media and
formats." No conformance rules, no new elements, no profile changes —
purely narrative.

#### Justification

This responds to both halves of the reporter's submission in one
editorial pass and avoids a future, separate ticket re-raising the
preservation point. Because the addition is non-normative narrative on
an existing History page, it does not change conformance and is still
appropriate for a Technical Correction-style edit. The trade-off vs.
Disposition A is that it slightly expands scope beyond a strict typo
fix, and the work group should confirm the wording is on-message for
PHR rather than letting it drift toward records-management policy.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the ticket on the basis that the substantive concern is the
long-term-preservation question — which is a policy/architecture topic
outside the PHR specification's scope — and that the typo, while real,
should be folded into a periodic editorial sweep rather than tracked as
a `Highest`-priority Technical Correction.

#### Justification

This is the weakest of the three options and is included for
completeness. The typo is unambiguous and trivially fixable, so
declining the ticket outright is hard to defend; at most, the work
group could decline the *preservation* sub-question while still
applying the spelling fix. Use this disposition only if the work group
wants to formally signal that the long-term-preservation question
belongs elsewhere (e.g., to a different work group or to architectural
guidance) and is willing to clean up the typo via a separate editorial
pass.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is a single-word spelling correction in PHR narrative text.
Disposition A applies that fix immediately with zero conformance impact
and explicitly carves the "records over a 100-year lifespan" question
into its own ticket, where it can get a real discussion rather than
riding along with a typo fix. Disposition B is reasonable if the work
group wants to acknowledge the preservation point in the same edit, but
it expands scope and risks the narrative addition becoming a
mini-debate that delays the trivial fix. Disposition C is not
defensible for the typo itself. Recommend the work group accept A and,
if the preservation question has merit, ask the reporter (or the chair)
to file a follow-up ticket against PHR for substantive consideration.
