## Summary

FHIR-48376 is a small, two-part technical correction to the Patient resource
overview page (`overview-patient.html`) in the FHIR Core specification.
Tone Southerland reports that (1) the link to the CARIN "My Health
Applications" gallery in section 2.1.18.3 is broken (404) and should
point to `https://www.myhealthapplication.com/#apps`, and (2) the
section title in 2.1.18.8 currently reads "Patient Implementators" and
should be corrected to "Patient Implementers". No cross-referenced
Jira, Zulip, or GitHub items were surfaced for this ticket; it is a
self-contained editorial fix.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply both editorial fixes to `HL7/fhir` `source/overview-patient.html` exactly
as the ticket requests:

1. **Line 56 (CARIN link).** Replace
   `<a href="https://myhealthapplication.com/health-apps/gallery">My Health Applications</a>`
   with
   `<a href="https://www.myhealthapplication.com/#apps">My Health Applications</a>`,
   matching the URL the ticket supplies.
2. **Line 119 (section heading).** Change
   `<h3 id="patient-implementators">Patient Implementators</h3>` to
   `<h3 id="patient-implementers">Patient Implementers</h3>`, correcting both
   the visible heading text and the anchor `id`. Before merging, run a
   repo-wide search for `patient-implementators` (with and without the
   `overview-patient.html#` prefix) across `HL7/fhir/source/` and update any
   inbound links to use `#patient-implementers`.

No other content on the page is touched. No StructureDefinition,
ValueSet, or generated artifact is affected; this is a pure narrative
edit handled directly by the HL7 publisher.

#### Justification

This is a bounded, low-risk technical correction with no semantic impact on
the Patient resource. The reporter has supplied the corrected URL and the
correct spelling explicitly, and both can be verified in the live clone of
`HL7/fhir`. The repo briefing confirms `source/overview-patient.html` is
authored content edited in place, so there is no generation pipeline to
coordinate. Updating the anchor `id` alongside the visible heading keeps
the two consistent and avoids leaving a misspelled fragment identifier in
the published spec; the briefing's standard anchor-rename guidance
(repo-wide grep for the old fragment) covers the only realistic risk.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the typo fix as in Disposition A, but treat the CARIN link more
defensively:

1. **Section heading (2.1.18.8).** Same as Disposition A — fix the
   heading text and the anchor `id`, with the inbound-link sweep.
2. **CARIN link (2.1.18.3).** Rather than replacing the URL with the
   exact value supplied in the ticket
   (`https://www.myhealthapplication.com/#apps`), update the link to
   the current canonical CARIN Alliance landing page for consumer apps
   (verified at the time of the edit) and add an HL7 confluence
   redirect or a short editorial sentence such as "maintained by the
   CARIN Alliance" so that future link rot is easier to repair without
   another ticket. If the supplied URL is verified live at edit time,
   use it; otherwise coordinate with CARIN Alliance liaison (via
   Patient Empowerment) to confirm the durable URL before merging.

#### Justification

The CARIN gallery has already moved at least once (the 404 in this
ticket). Hard-coding another exact URL without a brief verification
step risks the same ticket re-opening in 12–18 months. Naming CARIN
Alliance as the maintainer in the prose makes the link's provenance
explicit and gives a future editor a clear path to the new URL even if
the gallery moves again. The trade-off vs. Disposition A is a small
amount of additional editorial scope and a brief liaison check; the
heading fix is identical because it is purely internal and carries no
external dependency.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the ticket as out of scope for a substantive resolution and
close it as "not persuasive — to be handled editorially". Rationale:
both items are pure editorial defects (one broken external link, one
misspelled section heading) on a non-normative overview page, and the
project handles such fixes through routine editorial sweeps rather
than per-defect tracker tickets.

#### Justification

A defensible "decline" position would be that overview pages are
informative, the spec's normative content is unaffected, and the
overhead of tracking two trivial fixes through a Jira disposition
exceeds the cost of folding them into the next editorial pass.
However, this position is weak in practice: the ticket *is* the
mechanism by which the editorial sweep gets prioritised, and a 404 on
a CARIN-branded link in the Patient overview is a visible quality
issue for a workgroup whose audience explicitly includes
patient-facing app developers.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

This is a textbook accept-as-requested ticket: the reporter has
identified two concrete defects on a single non-normative page,
supplied the exact corrections, and the changes can be made and
verified against the `HL7/fhir` clone in a single small edit to
`source/overview-patient.html`. Disposition B's defensive framing of
the CARIN link is sensible and could be folded into the commit message
or a brief editor's note, but it is not a reason to delay the fix.
Disposition C is not credible for a 404 on a Patient Empowerment-owned
overview page. Recommend Patient Empowerment accept, assign, and merge
the two-line edit, sweeping inbound `#patient-implementators` links
before publish.
