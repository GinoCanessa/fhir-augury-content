## Summary

May Terry (Patient Empowerment) requests clearer guidance, and a worked
example, for representing a patient-stated goal together with a trial period
on the ADI Portable Medical Order (PMO) **Medically-Assisted Nutrition
ServiceRequest** profile in the US PACIO Advance Directive Interoperability
(ADI) IG. Two real-world PMO patterns motivate the request:

1. Some PMOs (e.g., DC MOST) carry the trial period and a free-text "stated
   goal" inline on the Medically-Assisted Nutrition field.
2. Some PMOs treat **Goal of Care** as a separate concept that applies to
   the form as a whole.

The Medically-Assisted Nutrition profile is built on `ServiceRequest`,
which has no direct `Goal` reference; the ticket notes that
`ServiceRequest.orderDetail` and a recurrence/timing element can in
principle carry these aspects, but asks the IG to spell out the recommended
representation and provide an example.

The ticket has **no Jira issue links and no recorded cross-references** in
the FhirAugury index (Jira, Zulip, GitHub). However, the FhirAugury
keyword-similarity index surfaces a closely-related cluster of ADI / PMO
tickets that frame the answer:

- **[FHIR-46912](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-46912)** (Applied) already
  established the *mechanism* for goals on PMO orders: an ADI Personal Goal
  is referenced from `ServiceRequest.supportingInfo` on the ADI PMO Initial
  Treatment ServiceRequest. FHIR-49876 is essentially asking that the same
  pattern be documented (and exemplified) for the Medically-Assisted
  Nutrition profile, and that the form-wide "Goal of Care" case also be
  addressed.
- **[FHIR-52757](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-52757)** (Submitted) asks
  for a Trial Period representation for interventions and points at
  `ServiceRequest.occurrence[x]` (Timing) as the natural slot.
- **[FHIR-52546](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-52546)** (Submitted)
  proposes a broader redesign of the PMO ServiceRequest hierarchy, which
  could affect *where* the goal/trial-period guidance ultimately lives.
- **[FHIR-46913](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-46913)** (Applied)
  introduced the temporary LOINC codes for Medically-Assisted
  Nutrition/Hydration order details, including a "trial" answer code.
- **[FHIR-53823](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53823)** (Applied,
  US Core) clarified PMO format wording but is structurally unrelated.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add explicit guidance, and a worked example, to the **ADI PMO
Medically-Assisted Nutrition ServiceRequest** profile in the
`HL7/fhir-pacio-adi` IG that:

1. Represents the **patient-stated goal** by mirroring the FHIR-46912
   pattern: reference an **ADI Personal Goal** from
   `ServiceRequest.supportingInfo`, and use `Goal.description.text` (or
   `Goal.note`) for the free-text statement when no coded goal is
   available. Add a constraint or guidance note saying the supportingInfo
   reference SHOULD be present when the source PMO carries a stated goal.
2. Represents the **trial period** on the order itself by populating
   **`ServiceRequest.occurrence[x]` as `Period`** (start/end) when the PMO
   prescribes a bounded trial, or as `Timing` when a recurrence is
   intended. Add an example showing a Medically-Assisted Nutrition order
   with a trial period and a stated-goal reference.
3. For the **form-level "Goal of Care"** case, point to the existing PMO
   `Composition` and recommend a `Composition.section` for "Goal of Care"
   referencing one or more `Goal` resources, rather than attaching a
   form-wide goal to each individual ServiceRequest.

Deliver the change as: (a) narrative additions on the
Medically-Assisted Nutrition profile page, (b) at least one new IG
example bundle, and (c) a "PMO Guidance" page entry covering the
form-level Goal of Care section pattern.

#### Justification

This option does exactly what the ticket asks for — guidance plus an
example — without changing any cardinalities or introducing new
extensions. It reuses the precedent set by **FHIR-46912** (Personal Goal
via `supportingInfo`), which gives the IG a single, consistent mechanism
for "goal attached to a PMO ServiceRequest" and avoids re-litigating the
"why not Goal reference?" question on a per-profile basis. Using
`occurrence[x]` for the trial period is the standard FHIR pattern and
aligns with **FHIR-52757**'s observation that Timing already covers this
need. Treating the form-wide Goal of Care via `Composition.section`
respects the ticket's distinction between *intervention-level* and
*form-level* goals.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Instead of profile-by-profile narrative additions, hoist the guidance to
the **abstract PMO ServiceRequest** layer (or to whatever common layer
results from the FHIR-52546 redesign) so that all derived PMO profiles —
Medically-Assisted Nutrition, Hydration, Initial Treatment, etc. —
inherit the same patterns:

1. Define a single **PMO Guidance** page section describing the canonical
   representation of (a) a per-order patient-stated goal via
   `supportingInfo` → ADI Personal Goal, and (b) a per-order trial period
   via `ServiceRequest.occurrence[x]`.
2. Add the guidance once on the abstract PMO ServiceRequest profile and
   cross-link from each derived profile, rather than repeating it.
3. Defer concrete examples on the Medically-Assisted Nutrition profile
   until **FHIR-52546** is dispositioned, then express the example
   against the resulting structure (current abstract+Composition design,
   or the proposed sliced-category design).
4. Optionally introduce a small `pmo-trial-period` extension *only* if
   downstream review shows that `occurrence[x]` cannot carry a
   distinct "trial" semantic (i.e., when the trial period must be
   distinguishable from a regular scheduled occurrence). Default
   recommendation is **no extension**; reuse `occurrence[x]`.

#### Justification

The ticket's two use cases (intervention-level goal/trial, form-level
Goal of Care) are not specific to Medically-Assisted Nutrition — they
apply to any PMO order. Solving them once at the common layer is
consistent with how FHIR-46912 handled the Initial Treatment ServiceRequest
and avoids the IG accumulating near-duplicate guidance on every PMO
profile. Sequencing the Medically-Assisted Nutrition example after
FHIR-52546 prevents authoring an example against a profile shape that may
be restructured. The trade-off vs. Disposition A is responsiveness: the
ticket's reporter is asking for guidance now, and Disposition B asks them
to wait on a related ticket; the workgroup should weigh implementer
demand for an example against the cost of producing one twice.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline as **Already addressed by existing mechanisms** with a not-
persuasive justification. Resolve the ticket by pointing the reporter to:

- `ServiceRequest.supportingInfo` → ADI Personal Goal, as established by
  the resolution of **FHIR-46912**, for the patient-stated goal.
- `ServiceRequest.occurrence[x]` (Period or Timing) for the trial period.
- The PMO `Composition` and its sections for any form-wide Goal of Care.

No spec change; no new example.

#### Justification

The mechanisms the ticket needs already exist in the resource and in the
IG; FHIR-46912 has set the precedent and the FHIR core ServiceRequest
already supports the timing semantics. From a strict "is the spec
broken?" lens, nothing is missing. This option is defensible only if the
workgroup judges that no implementer is actually blocked — i.e., the
need is purely educational and can be answered by ticket comment rather
than IG content. The downside is that the IG will continue to lack a
worked Medically-Assisted Nutrition example, which is the concrete
deliverable the reporter is asking for; declining therefore risks the
same question being re-raised in a future ballot cycle.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition: A (Accept As Requested).**

The ticket's request is narrow and well-scoped: clearer guidance and an
example for a profile that the IG already publishes. The mechanism
question is essentially settled — FHIR-46912 has already chosen
`supportingInfo` → ADI Personal Goal as the way a PMO ServiceRequest
references a goal, and `ServiceRequest.occurrence[x]` is the obvious slot
for a trial period. Disposition A simply propagates that decision to the
Medically-Assisted Nutrition profile and adds the missing example, which
is exactly what the reporter asked for and what implementers will look
for when building DC MOST-style PMOs. Disposition B is appealing from a
"don't repeat yourself" standpoint and would be the right answer *if*
FHIR-52546 were close to resolution, but as long as that ticket is still
in Submitted state the workgroup risks blocking a small, useful change
behind a much larger architectural debate. Disposition C
under-serves the reporter and leaves a real authoring gap in the IG. If
the workgroup wants the best of both worlds, it can adopt A now and
re-home the guidance during the FHIR-52546 disposition if the PMO
ServiceRequest hierarchy is restructured. While accepting, also coordinate
with **FHIR-52757** so the trial-period guidance is expressed identically
in both tickets' resolutions.
