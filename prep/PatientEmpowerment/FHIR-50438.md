## Summary

The reporter (Michael van der Zel) is asking that the Personal Health Record
(FHIR) IG (`hl7.org/fhir/uv/phr/`) update its outbound links so that they
point to the new PHR-S Functional Model R2 ballot publication at
`https://hl7.org/ehrs/uv/phrsfmr2/2025May/`, rather than to older PHR-S FM
references that are still embedded in the May 2025 ballot of the PHR IG.
Two specific pages in the `2025May` snapshot of the PHR IG are called out as
containing the "old" links: `functionality.html` and `recordlifecycle.html`.
The reporter notes that section 5.1 of the IG already correctly mentions the
"FHIRized" PHR FM, so the underlying narrative is fine — only the
cross-references to the PHR-S FM publication need to be refreshed. There are
no related Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items linked to this
request in the cached community data.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the PHR IG narrative pages so that every reference to the PHR-S
Functional Model points to the new ballot publication
`https://hl7.org/ehrs/uv/phrsfmr2/2025May/` (and, where appropriate, to the
canonical, version-less URL `https://hl7.org/ehrs/uv/phrsfmr2/`).
Concretely, in the PHR IG source:

- Edit `input/pagecontent/functionality.html` (the source for
  `functionality.html`) to replace each "old" PHR-S FM link with the
  PHR-S FM R2 2025May URL.
- Edit `input/pagecontent/recordlifecycle.html` (the source for
  `recordlifecycle.html`) the same way.
- Sweep any other `input/pagecontent/*.{html,md}` pages for stray links
  to earlier PHR-S FM publications (e.g., `wiki.hl7.org` PHR-S FM pages,
  prior `hl7.org/ehrs/...` snapshots) and bring them in line.
- Re-publish the IG and confirm the next ballot/CI build of
  `hl7.org/fhir/uv/phr/` no longer contains the old URLs.

#### Justification

This is exactly the maintenance the reporter requested and the change is
purely editorial — it does not affect any FHIR conformance artifact,
StructureDefinition, ValueSet, or capability. The reporter has already done
the diff: section 5.1 correctly mentions the FHIRized PHR FM, and only
`functionality.html` and `recordlifecycle.html` carry stale cross-references.
Pointing readers at the in-ballot PHR-S FM R2 publication is consistent with
HL7's normal practice of cross-IG referencing the most recent published or
balloted version, and it removes a real source of confusion for implementers
who would otherwise land on an older PHR-S FM page.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Update the links, but instead of pointing at the version-pinned ballot
snapshot `https://hl7.org/ehrs/uv/phrsfmr2/2025May/`, target the
**version-less canonical** URL `https://hl7.org/ehrs/uv/phrsfmr2/` for
narrative cross-references, and pin to the dated snapshot only where the
text is specifically discussing the 2025 May ballot. As part of the same
edit, add a short footnote/admonition on `functionality.html` and
`recordlifecycle.html` explaining the relationship between the FHIRized PHR
IG and the PHR-S FM R2 publication, so readers understand which document is
authoritative for which content. Optionally, lift any duplicated
PHR-S-FM-derived prose into a single included fragment
(`input/includes/phrs-fm-link.xml` or similar) so future republications of
the PHR-S FM only require one edit.

#### Justification

Version-less URLs let the PHR IG continue to track the "current" PHR-S FM
publication automatically as PHR-S FM R2 progresses from ballot to STU/normative
without requiring another link-maintenance ticket each cycle. Centralizing the
cross-reference in a single included fragment further reduces the chance that
stale links creep back in on the next IG revision — which is exactly the
problem this ticket is fixing. The trade-off versus Disposition A is that
version-less URLs can change underneath the IG (e.g., when the canonical
publication moves), so the dated snapshot is still the safer choice when the
narrative is specifically describing what the 2025 May ballot says.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Do not change the IG. Treat the existing links as historically accurate for
the 2025 May ballot snapshot of the PHR IG, and leave any URL refresh to
the next regular republication cycle of the IG.

#### Justification

A defensible argument for declining is that `2025May/...` is itself a
frozen ballot snapshot — by definition it should reflect what was true at
the moment of that ballot. Republished IG snapshots should not be edited
in place after the fact, and the PHR IG's `current`/next-build will pick up
the corrected links naturally on the next publication. If the work group's
position is that the request is really "fix this in the next build, not in
the 2025May snapshot," then no spec change is needed today.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

This is fundamentally a low-risk editorial fix that the reporter is right
to flag — readers landing on `functionality.html` or `recordlifecycle.html`
today are being pointed at a stale PHR-S FM URL when the PHR-S FM R2
ballot is the live document the IG is supposed to align with. Disposition C
is too purist about snapshot immutability for a change of this kind (HL7
routinely corrects broken or stale outbound links in narrative pages without
treating it as a substantive ballot change). Between A and B, B is the
better answer because the underlying problem is recurring: every time the
PHR-S FM republishes, the PHR IG will need another link-maintenance ticket
unless the cross-references are version-less and centralized. Recommending
B accepts the reporter's request in full while also giving the work group
a small, durable fix that prevents the same ticket from reappearing on
the next ballot cycle.
