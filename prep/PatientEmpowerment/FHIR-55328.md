## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In `HL7/fhir`, edit `source/overview-patient.html` to fix both items
called out by the reporter:

1. **Line 100 — Argonaut paragraph (misspelling).** Change
   `refering to Jason and the Argonauts` to
   `referring to Jason and the Argonauts`.
2. **Line 25 — intro paragraph (contraction).** Replace
   `…have had to come together and agree on how they’re going to send
   and share data…` with
   `…have had to come together and agree on how they are going to send
   and share data…`, preserving the surrounding curly-apostrophe typographic style on the rest of the page.

Commit on a topic branch in `HL7/fhir`, cite FHIR-55328 in the commit
message, and resolve the ticket as **Persuasive** with the change
applied.

#### Justification

This is a Patient-Empowerment-owned, Highest-priority **Technical
Correction** with a precise, two-token fix list provided by the
reporter. Both strings exist verbatim at the lines named above, and the
HL7/fhir briefing confirms `source/overview-patient.html` is an
authored HTML page in the canonical authoring root, so the edit lands
exactly where the publisher consumes it without any generated-artefact
risk. There is no cross-repo touch point and no downstream IG impact.
"Referring" is unambiguously the correct spelling, and expanding
"they're" → "they are" is consistent with the reporter's stated rule
that abbreviations on this page be fully spelled out.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the misspelling fix at line 100 (`refering` → `referring`)
exactly as requested, but **decline the contraction expansion at line
25** as a separate sub-decision. Instead, do a one-pass copy edit of
the entire page, applied consistently:

- Fix `refering` → `referring`.
- Leave `they're` (and other natural-language contractions on the page)
  in place as deliberate informal style, since `overview-patient.html`
  is an audience-facing introductory page aimed at non-implementers and
  the FHIR spec does not, as a rule, ban contractions in narrative
  prose.
- Optionally tighten any other genuine misspellings or jargon noticed
  during the pass (none required by this ticket).

Resolve the ticket as **Persuasive with Modification**, recording that
"they're" is being kept intentionally as conversational tone for a
patient-facing overview.

#### Justification

The reporter's framing ("Abbreviations need to be fully spelled out")
treats a contraction as an abbreviation, but most editorial style
guides — and the existing tone of `overview-patient.html`, which is
explicitly written for a lay audience — distinguish the two.
Mechanically expanding every contraction risks making the page read
more stiffly without improving clarity, and there is no other Patient
Empowerment guidance (no linked tickets, no Zulip discussion) that
established a project-wide rule banning contractions on overview
pages. Fixing the unambiguous misspelling is uncontroversial; treating
the contraction as a style choice keeps the audience-friendly voice
intact.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve as **Not Persuasive**, on the grounds that the requested
changes are at the threshold of a routine editorial pass that does not
require workgroup tracker handling, and the contraction part of the
request conflates "abbreviation" with "contraction."

#### Justification

The work group could reasonably argue that single-token typo fixes on
introductory narrative pages should be handled via direct PRs to
`HL7/fhir` rather than per-token Jira tickets, and that the
"they're → they are" portion is a stylistic preference rather than a
defect. Declining frees the Patient Empowerment agenda from a Highest
-priority Technical Correction whose underlying defect (a single
misspelling) is below the substantive-change bar that workgroup ballot
review is designed for.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

FHIR-55328 is a small, well-scoped Technical Correction with both
flagged tokens confirmed in `source/overview-patient.html` at the lines
named in the briefing-derived likely-touched-paths analysis. The
reporter is explicit about both items and about the rule being applied,
the page is owned by the work group taking the disposition, and the
fix is a single-file narrative edit in the canonical FhirCore authoring
root with no cross-repo or generated-artefact risk. Disposition B has
real editorial merit (contractions are not technically abbreviations,
and a patient-facing overview benefits from a conversational tone), and
the work group may well prefer it on stylistic grounds — but it
requires the group to substitute its editorial judgement for the
reporter's, which is a discussion better had in the meeting than
pre-empted here. Disposition C undersells a Highest-priority
Technical Correction filed by the spec's lead editor. Accepting as
requested fixes the defect, honours the reporter's stated rule, and
costs almost nothing to implement.
