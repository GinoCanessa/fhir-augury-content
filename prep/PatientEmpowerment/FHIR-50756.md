## Summary

Virginia Lorenzi flags a section of the Personal Health Record (PHR) FHIR
IG that describes "record lifecycle operations" as confusing and
under-specified. She raises three concrete clarification asks: (1) the
acronym **SPHR-FM** is undefined and needs to be spelled out (she
suspects the author means the HL7 Personal Health Record System
Functional Model and notes the acronym itself may be wrong); (2) a
companion diagram labels the first commit to the EHR as the "Fifth"
step, which appears to be a numbering/labelling error and should likely
be "First"; and (3) the section's relationship to the PHR FHIR IG
itself is unclear — it is not obvious whether the section is normative
guidance about how the `.phr` / `.sphr` file is built and processed by
each side of an exchange, informative background, or something else.
She also asks the authors to justify why the section is needed at all.

No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were
returned by FhirAugury for this ticket, so the request stands on its
own as PHR ballot feedback.

## Proposed Dispositions

> Note: FhirAugury surfaced no GitHub cross-references for this ticket
> and therefore no repo briefings were loaded. The dispositions below
> reference the Personal Health Record (FHIR) IG generically; a final
> disposition action would land in the IG's authoring sources for the
> "record lifecycle operations" page once the page's source path is
> confirmed by the WG.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat the ticket as a documentation defect against the "record
lifecycle operations" section of the Personal Health Record (FHIR) IG
and apply three concrete edits:

1. **Define SPHR-FM on first use.** Replace the bare acronym with the
   spelled-out term and a citation, e.g. "the HL7 Personal Health
   Record System Functional Model (PHR-S FM)." If the intended acronym
   is in fact `PHR-S FM` (as the published HL7 product is named),
   correct `SPHR-FM` to `PHR-S FM` everywhere in the section and add a
   one-line definition (and a link to the PHR-S FM product page) the
   first time it appears.
2. **Renumber the lifecycle diagram.** Start the step numbering at
   "First" for the initial commit to the EHR, and renumber the rest of
   the steps consecutively so the narrative and the diagram agree. If
   the "Fifth" label was deliberate (e.g., the diagram is meant to
   start mid-flow), add a preceding visual or prose anchor that makes
   steps 1–4 explicit; otherwise drop the offset.
3. **Frame the section's purpose.** Add a short lead-in paragraph that
   states (a) whether the section is normative or informative, (b) who
   the audience is (PHR producer, PHR consumer, EHR, or all three),
   and (c) how the section relates to the `.phr` / `.sphr` file format
   — specifically whether it describes how each side **builds** or
   **processes** the file. If the section is intended to be normative
   guidance for either side, restate the requirements using SHALL /
   SHOULD language so the obligations are unambiguous.

#### Justification

All three asks are clarifications, not design changes: defining
acronyms on first use, fixing a step-numbering error, and stating the
purpose/audience of a section are baseline IG documentation hygiene
that the PHR IG should meet. Accepting as requested also directly
addresses the reporter's "explain why this section is needed" question
without committing the WG to any technical change. The reporter is a
long-standing reviewer and the priority is **Highest**, which suggests
a low-risk, high-value editorial fix is the right response.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the editorial fixes from Disposition A (acronym, numbering,
purpose statement) **and additionally restructure the "record
lifecycle operations" section** so that the lifecycle is anchored on
the FHIR resources/operations that actually carry the lifecycle (e.g.,
`AuditEvent`, `Provenance`, document `Bundle` / `Composition` for
`.phr` / `.sphr` payloads), with a small table mapping each lifecycle
step to the responsible actor (PHR, EHR) and the FHIR artifact that
realises it. Keep the diagram, but redraw it so each numbered step
points to a row in the new table rather than to a free-standing
narrative.

#### Justification

The reporter's deeper concern is that the section's relationship to
the PHR FHIR IG is unclear. A pure clarification (Disposition A) fixes
the surface defects but leaves the section as prose detached from the
IG's normative artifacts. Anchoring lifecycle steps on FHIR resources
and operations makes the section actionable for implementers and ties
the PHR-S FM concepts back to PHR FHIR IG content, which is the natural
home for them. Trade-off vs. A: more authoring effort and potentially
new ballot exposure for any wording that crosses into normative
territory; mitigated by clearly marking which subsections are
informative.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request on the grounds that the section is informative
background sourced from the HL7 PHR-S FM and is intentionally written
at a higher level than the FHIR resources in the IG; close the ticket
as **Not Persuasive** with a note pointing the reader to the PHR-S FM
product page for definitions and to the IG's existing capability
statements / profiles for the FHIR-specific behaviour.

#### Justification

This is defensible only if the WG has already decided that the section
is informative cross-reference material and not normative IG content,
in which case the "why is this section needed" question has a standing
answer (orientation for readers coming from the PHR-S FM world).
However, even under this reading, the SPHR-FM acronym definition and
the "Fifth/First" numbering issue are real defects, so a pure decline
would leave documented errors in the published IG. This option should
only be chosen if the WG also commits to a separate editorial pass on
those two specific points.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is a Highest-priority Patient Empowerment clarification
request from a careful reviewer, with three small, independently
actionable asks (define an acronym, fix a step number, state the
section's purpose). None of the three requires a design change, and
all three improve the PHR IG's readability and ballot defensibility.
Disposition B is attractive but expands the scope into authoring work
that is not what the ticket asks for and risks dragging the fix into
the next ballot cycle; it can be raised as a follow-up ticket if the
WG agrees the section needs deeper restructuring. Disposition C is
hard to justify because the SPHR-FM acronym and the "Fifth" labelling
are objective defects regardless of whether the section stays
informative. Accept as requested, land the edits in the next
publication, and open a separate ticket if the WG wants to pursue the
Disposition B restructuring.
