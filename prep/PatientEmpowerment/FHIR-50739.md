## Summary

Virginia Lorenzi asks the Patient Empowerment work group to remove the
"Record Life Cycle" section from the Personal Health Record (FHIR)
specification. She argues that the section overlaps with the PHR System
Functional Model (PHR-S FM), provides no FHIR-specific interoperability
guidance, and would require ongoing low-value maintenance to stay aligned
with each version of the functional model. She further notes that the EHR
work group now maintains a FHIR Implementation Guide intended to deliver
PHR interoperability, so the better path is to delete the page and, on
the Functional Model page, point readers to that forthcoming FHIR IG.

No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, GitHub items, or
comments were returned by FhirAugury for this ticket, so the request
stands on its own narrative.

## Details

**Description:**

> This section seems to be in overlap with the functional model and does
> not offer any guidance on how to provide interoperability for these PHR
> features through FHIR.  As a result, it is confusing having it here and
> un-useful maintenance would be required to keep it aligned with each
> version of the PHR system functional model.   In addition, the EHR WG
> now has a FHIR IG for this purpose.  Recommend removing this page and
> on the Functional Model page, reference the up and coming FHIR IG that
> is designed to provide PHR interoperability for this purpose.

## Proposed Dispositions

(No GitHub repositories surfaced for this ticket, so the dispositions
below operate against the Personal Health Record (FHIR) specification
narrative pages held by the Patient Empowerment work group rather than
against a specific repo / authoring root from a briefing.)

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Remove the "Record Life Cycle" page from the Personal Health Record
(FHIR) specification entirely. On the PHR "Functional Model" page,
replace any inbound links to the deleted page with a single short
paragraph that (a) names the PHR-S Functional Model as the authoritative
source for the record-life-cycle conformance criteria, and (b) directs
implementers seeking FHIR-level interoperability for these capabilities
to the EHR work group's forthcoming FHIR IG (cited by name once the
exact title and canonical URL are confirmed with the EHR WG). Update
the PHR specification's table of contents / navigation to drop the
removed page, and add a redirect (or at minimum a "this page has been
retired — see Functional Model" stub) so existing deep links do not
404.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter — the long-standing Patient
Empowerment co-chair — is asking for, and the rationale is internally
consistent: a narrative page that paraphrases another HL7 product
without adding FHIR-specific guidance violates the standing
specification-hygiene principle that each normative page should pull
its own weight. Pointing readers at the EHR WG's PHR-oriented FHIR IG
keeps the interoperability story intact while shifting maintenance to
the group that owns it, which is also consistent with the Patient
Empowerment group's general posture of borrowing rather than
re-stating EHR / PHR-S FM material.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the "Record Life Cycle" page in the Personal Health Record (FHIR)
specification, but rewrite it as a short signpost page rather than a
parallel restatement of the PHR-S FM. The rewritten page would: (1) one
paragraph summarising why record life cycle matters in a PHR context,
(2) an explicit pointer to the PHR-S Functional Model section that owns
the requirements, (3) a pointer to the EHR WG's forthcoming FHIR IG for
interoperability, and (4) a small table mapping the life-cycle phases
to the FHIR resources / elements (e.g., `Provenance`, `AuditEvent`,
`Composition.status`, resource `meta.lastUpdated`) that PHR
implementers will most often touch. Mark the page non-normative.

#### Justification

Keeping a thin signpost preserves discoverability for readers who land
on "Record Life Cycle" via search or external bookmarks, and gives the
PHR spec a place to surface the small but real FHIR-specific guidance
(Provenance / AuditEvent / status fields) that the current page is
missing. This trades a small amount of ongoing maintenance — keeping
two pointers and one table fresh — against the loss of all in-spec
context that Disposition A entails. It is more conservative than full
removal and is reversible if the EHR WG IG slips or changes scope.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request and leave the "Record Life Cycle" page in the
Personal Health Record (FHIR) specification unchanged for this ballot
cycle. Document the rationale on the ticket — that the page is a
deliberate narrative anchor for PHR readers and that removal should
wait until the cited EHR WG FHIR IG has actually published, has a stable
canonical URL, and has been reviewed by Patient Empowerment for
coverage of the PHR record-life-cycle scenarios.

#### Justification

The reporter's argument depends on a future artefact ("the up and
coming FHIR IG") that is not yet published; deleting a normative page
in favour of a not-yet-balloted IG risks leaving PHR readers with no
guidance at all in the interim. Specification removals are also harder
to reverse than additions, and external implementers may already be
relying on the current page. Declining for now — with a clear
re-evaluation trigger tied to the EHR WG IG's publication — is a
defensible "do no harm" position.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B threads the needle between the reporter's legitimate
concern (a redundant, maintenance-heavy narrative page that adds no
FHIR-specific value) and the risk Disposition A creates (sending PHR
readers off-spec to an IG that has not yet published, with no
in-specification fallback). Rewriting the page as a short, explicitly
non-normative signpost — pointing at the PHR-S FM for requirements, at
the EHR WG's PHR FHIR IG for interoperability, and at the handful of
FHIR resources that actually surface in record-life-cycle scenarios —
removes the duplication the reporter is objecting to while preserving
discoverability and giving the PHR spec a single, low-cost place to
keep the story coherent across ballot cycles. If the work group is
confident the EHR WG IG will publish before the next normative cycle
and is willing to accept a redirect-only approach, Disposition A is a
reasonable fallback; Disposition C should only be chosen if the group
believes the cited IG is too speculative to plan around.
