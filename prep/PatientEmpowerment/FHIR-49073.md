## Summary

This ticket is a "Technical Correction" against the Personal Health Record
(PHR) FHIR specification, reporting a collection of editorial typos and
small grammatical issues spread across several sections of the narrative
pages. None of the items are normative changes — they are all spelling
errors, a missing word, a subject/verb agreement issue, an incomplete
sentence, an incorrect/non-incrementing section number on the Annotations
page, and a "lead" → "led" tense correction. The reporter (Craig Newman)
itemizes each occurrence with the surrounding sentence so the editor can
locate them quickly. The single comment from the work group (awatson1978,
2025-01-30) marks the ticket "Persuasive. Will fix.", and the ticket has
since been resolved as **Persuasive** with a resolution note of "Typo
fixed." There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or
GitHub items.

## Details

**Description:**

- **Section 1.4 says:** "As such, a modern Patient Health Record needs to
  essentially be able to receive captured data from throughout the
  hospital. Emergency room, operating room, intensive care unit,
  laboratory, pharmacy, nursery, **psychaitry**. All of it is
  **relavent**." → *psychiatry* and *relevant* have typos.
- **Section 2 says:** "**espcially** considering that" → *especially* has
  a typo.
- **Section 2.3.1 says:** "The **process flows below is** based" →
  Plural/singular issue. Should be "process flow … is" or "process flows
  … are".
- **Section 2.3.1 says:** "and optionally sign **then** with a FHIR
  Signature." → *then* should be *them*.
- **Section 5 says:** "ought to be **inlcluded** in a" → *included* has a
  typo.
- **Annotations Page:** The section number is incorrect and doesn't
  consistently increment.
- **Section 7.3 says:** "A patient may (should) hesitate to pass on a
  patient summary containing information." → Sentence appears
  incomplete; all summaries will contain information, so the qualifier
  is missing.
- **Section 9 says:** "the **assachusetts** General Hospital Utility
  Multi-Programming System" → leading "M" is missing (should be
  *Massachusetts*).
- **Section 9 says:** "eventually **lead** to the" → should be *led*.

**Resolution Description:** "Typo fixed."

**Comments:**

- **awatson1978 (2025-01-30):** "Persuasive. Will fix."

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the editorial corrections enumerated by the reporter to the PHR
specification narrative pages exactly as listed:

1. Section 1.4: change "psychaitry" → "psychiatry" and "relavent" →
   "relevant".
2. Section 2: change "espcially" → "especially".
3. Section 2.3.1: rewrite "The process flows below is based …" as either
   "The process flow below is based …" or "The process flows below are
   based …" (recommend the plural form to keep the existing diagram
   reference).
4. Section 2.3.1: change "and optionally sign **then** with a FHIR
   Signature" → "and optionally sign **them** with a FHIR Signature".
5. Section 5: change "inlcluded" → "included".
6. Annotations page: renumber the section headings so they increment
   correctly and match the surrounding TOC.
7. Section 7.3: rewrite "A patient may (should) hesitate to pass on a
   patient summary containing information." to make the qualifier
   explicit — e.g., "A patient may (should) hesitate to pass on a
   patient summary containing **sensitive** information."
8. Section 9: insert the missing leading "M" so the phrase reads
   "**M**assachusetts General Hospital Utility Multi-Programming System".
9. Section 9: change "eventually lead to the" → "eventually led to the".

#### Justification

Every item is purely editorial — no normative content, no resource,
profile, value-set, or operation is affected, and no implementer
behaviour changes. The work group has already commented "Persuasive.
Will fix." and the ticket has been resolved as Persuasive, so this
disposition simply codifies what the work group has already agreed to
do. Fixing typos in publicly-visible specification narrative is also
low-risk and high-value: it preserves credibility with implementers and
reviewers, and the changes are independently verifiable on a quick diff.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept all of the unambiguous corrections as in Disposition A, but
handle the two ambiguous items by checking back with the reporter (or
the original author of those sections) before publishing:

- **Section 2.3.1 plural/singular fix.** Choose between "process flow …
  is" and "process flows … are" based on whether the underlying diagram
  shows one flow or several; do not pick blindly.
- **Section 7.3 incomplete sentence.** Confirm with the original author
  what qualifier was intended (e.g., "sensitive", "identifying",
  "behavioral-health") before inserting a word, since the choice
  changes the policy nuance of the sentence.

For the Annotations page section-number issue, also verify whether the
numbering is generated by the IG publisher tooling versus hand-authored;
if generated, file a follow-up tooling ticket rather than patching
numbers by hand.

#### Justification

Most of the items in this ticket are pure spelling fixes where there is
exactly one correct answer, but two of them require an editorial
judgement that the original author is best positioned to make. Picking
arbitrarily risks subtly changing the meaning of normative-adjacent
guidance (especially in Section 7.3, which discusses patient
willingness to share summaries containing certain information). Routing
the section-number issue through the publishing toolchain — if that's
where the bug actually lives — also prevents the same drift from
recurring on the next regen.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket without making changes, on the basis that the affected
content is non-normative narrative and the typos do not impact
implementer conformance.

#### Justification

This is a defensible position only if the work group has decided to
freeze the PHR narrative pages (e.g., the spec is being retired,
withdrawn, or moved to a successor IG and edits are not worth the
publication cycle). It is **not** the right disposition here, because
the work group has already committed in-comment to fixing the typos,
the corrections are cheap, and leaving visible spelling and grammar
errors in a published HL7 specification undermines the reader's
confidence in the rest of the content.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is a textbook editorial correction against narrative pages of
the Personal Health Record (FHIR) IG. The work group has already marked
it Persuasive in-comment and the resolution is recorded as "Typo
fixed.", so Disposition A simply ratifies the path the work group is
already on. Disposition B's caution about the two ambiguous items
(Section 2.3.1 plural/singular and Section 7.3 incomplete sentence) is
worth folding into A as a small drafting note for the editor, but it
does not warrant deferring the rest of the corrections — the unambiguous
items should land as part of the same fix-up commit. Disposition C is
not defensible given the work group's existing commitment and the
near-zero risk of the change.
