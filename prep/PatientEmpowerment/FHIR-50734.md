## Summary

Virginia Lorenzi requests that the Personal Health Record (PHR FHIR IG)
replace its current narrative paragraph describing what a PHR "should be"
(medical-grade, able to incorporate any record from any hospital
department, etc.) with a concise statement of the specification's
purpose: *"The purpose of this specification is to provide standard
mechanisms for a PHR to interoperate, facilitating sharing of
information obtained by the PHR from healthcare encounters, personal
documentation and measurement, and other sources."* The ticket also
asks that the section heading "Design Philosophy" be dropped because
the section is not actually a statement of design philosophy. The
existing prose describes aspirational properties of a PHR product
rather than the scope or purpose of the IG, which makes it hard for
readers to understand what the specification itself is for. No related
Jira tickets were surfaced by cross-references; the only cross-reference
is the GitHub PR (HL7/personal-health-record-format-ig#76) where the
agreed wording was applied.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In `HL7/personal-health-record-format-ig`, edit the intro/purpose page
(the one currently bearing the "Design Philosophy" heading and the
"medical grade…" paragraph) to:

1. Replace the existing paragraph with the requested wording verbatim:
   *"The purpose of this specification is to provide standard mechanisms
   for a PHR to interoperate, facilitating sharing of information
   obtained by the PHR from healthcare encounters, personal documentation
   and measurement, and other sources."*
2. Remove the "Design Philosophy" heading. If the surrounding section
   still contains genuinely philosophical material, retitle it to
   something descriptive (e.g., "Scope" or "Purpose"); otherwise drop the
   header outright and let the new purpose paragraph stand as the
   section's opening.
3. Stage the edit on the IG's authoring branch and publish via the
   normal IG-Publisher build (no FSH or profile changes required).

This is the change actually applied in
[HL7/personal-health-record-format-ig#76](https://github.com/HL7/personal-health-record-format-ig/pull/76),
already approved 4-0-0 (Motion: Rachel, Second: Hisashi).

#### Justification

The existing paragraph describes properties of an idealized PHR product
rather than the purpose of *this specification*, which is what a reader
of an IG's introductory page expects. The proposed wording is concise,
states the IG's interoperability goal, names the categories of data
sources it covers (healthcare encounters, personal documentation and
measurement, other sources), and aligns with the typical "Purpose / Scope"
opener used across HL7 FHIR IGs. Dropping the "Design Philosophy" heading
avoids mislabelling the section. The change is editorial, has no impact
on conformance, profiles, value sets, or examples, and was accepted
unanimously by the Patient Empowerment WG, which is strong evidence the
disposition reflects community consensus.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Apply the spirit of the change but split the rewrite into two clearly
labelled paragraphs on the same page:

1. A **Purpose** paragraph using essentially the requested wording:
   "The purpose of this specification is to provide standard mechanisms
   for a PHR to interoperate, facilitating sharing of information
   obtained by the PHR from healthcare encounters, personal
   documentation and measurement, and other sources."
2. A short **Scope** paragraph that preserves the *intent* of the
   removed prose — i.e., that a PHR is expected to be able to ingest
   data from across the spectrum of clinical encounters (ED, OR, ICU,
   lab, pharmacy, etc.) and from patient-generated sources — but framed
   as scope statements about the IG, not aspirational claims about a
   PHR product.

Replace the "Design Philosophy" heading with two subheadings ("Purpose"
and "Scope") instead of removing it without replacement.

#### Justification

The original paragraph, while poorly framed, does carry useful
information: it signals to readers that a PHR is expected to handle
data from *any* clinical setting and from patient-generated sources.
Reducing the page to only the new single-sentence purpose loses that
signal. Splitting into Purpose + Scope keeps the editorial improvement
the reporter asks for while retaining the breadth-of-data message in a
form appropriate for an IG (a scope statement, not a product spec).
This is also closer to the convention used by other HL7 FHIR IGs that
distinguish Purpose, Scope, and Background on their introductory pages.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change. Leave the existing "Design Philosophy" section and
its current paragraph in place.

#### Justification

There is no defensible reason to decline this ticket. The current
wording is editorially weak (it describes a PHR product, not the IG),
the heading is misleading, the proposed replacement is concise and
appropriate, the work group voted 4-0-0 to accept, and the change has
already been applied via PR #76. Recording this disposition only for
completeness; it should not be selected.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The change is purely editorial, the proposed wording cleanly states the
IG's purpose, and the "Design Philosophy" heading is genuinely
mislabelled — there is no design-philosophy content under it. The
Patient Empowerment WG already voted 4-0-0 in favour and merged
[PR #76](https://github.com/HL7/personal-health-record-format-ig/pull/76),
so Disposition A simply ratifies the action already taken. Disposition B
is a reasonable refinement worth raising in discussion if the WG feels
the breadth-of-data message from the original paragraph was worth
preserving as an explicit Scope statement, but it should be treated as
a follow-up improvement rather than a reason to revisit FHIR-50734
itself. Disposition C has no merit.
