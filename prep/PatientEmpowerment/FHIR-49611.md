## Summary

The reporter (Gay Dolin) asks the Patient Empowerment work group to add a
"Plain Language Summary about this Guide" section to the home page of the
Personal Health Record (PHR) FHIR IG. The intent is to make the IG more
accessible to non-technical readers — patients, caregivers, journalists,
policy folks — by giving them a concise, jargon-free explanation of what
the guide is for and what it does. The ticket points to the MCC IG
(`https://hl7.org/fhir/us/mcc/`) as a working example and recommends
omitting the "About HL7" boilerplate for new summaries. It also points
authors at the candidate summaries staged in
`HL7/plain-language` (and the original draft repo
`jmandel/chat.fhir.org-channels`) as a starting point.

There are **no cross-referenced Jira tickets** — the only cross-reference
returned by FhirAugury is to the GitHub pull request
`HL7/plain-language#15`, which the assignee/commenter awatson1978 opened
to contribute the PHR-specific summary text into the central
plain-language repo. The ticket has been resolved **Persuasive** with the
disposition acknowledging that the PHR home page is being iterated and a
PR has been filed against the central plain-language summaries repo.
This review confirms the resolution and proposes how to close the loop on
the PHR IG side.

## Details

**Description:**

HL7 encourages Implementation Guides (IGs) to voluntarily include a
"Plain Language Summary about this Guide" on their home page. This
addition can enhance accessibility and understanding for non-technical
audiences, including patients and the general public.

For an example, see the "Plain Language Summary about HL7 and this Guide"
section on the MCC IG homepage:
[https://hl7.org/fhir/us/mcc/#plain-language-summary-about-hl7-and-this-guide](https://hl7.org/fhir/us/mcc/#plain-language-summary-about-hl7-and-this-guide).
Note that FHIR leaders recommend excluding the "About HL7" portion for
new summaries.

To assist with this effort, IG authors can reference existing summaries
available in this GitHub repository (you may edit as you see fit):
[https://github.com/jmandel/chat.fhir.org-channels/tree/ae9bd11d491e3a62a0f2cd40a2ebff53dcf7afd8/summaries](https://github.com/jmandel/chat.fhir.org-channels/tree/ae9bd11d491e3a62a0f2cd40a2ebff53dcf7afd8/summaries).

This recommendation aligns with a growing trend among peer-reviewed
journals to include Plain Language Summaries (PLS). These summaries
simplify complex concepts, improving engagement, transparency, and
understanding for diverse audiences. In healthcare, PLS have become
essential for fostering public involvement; SAGE, Taylor & Francis, and
PLOS provide templates or require PLS as part of submission.

By adopting this approach, HL7 can promote inclusivity, better serve its
stakeholders, and align with best practices in other domains.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Accept the request as Persuasive (already so resolved) and close the
loop with two concrete actions:

1. **Land `HL7/plain-language#15`** so that the PHR IG package id has a
   summary file (`summaries/<phr-package-id>.md`) starting with
   `# General` and containing awatson1978's 2025-06-12 second-revision
   text (which correctly drops the unsupported "strong security
   protections" claim and incorporates patient-generated data). Patient
   Empowerment leadership (Gay Dolin and the PHR IG editors) reviews
   and approves the wording before merge, per the
   IG-author-review pattern established in the patient-empowerment
   Zulip thread.
2. **Verify rendering on the PHR IG home page.** Once the PR is merged
   and the next PHR IG build runs against IG Publisher ≥ the
   2025-05-29 release, confirm that the injected
   `showPlainLanguage(<phr-package-id>, <version>)` block on the PHR
   landing page resolves to the merged summary (i.e., the placeholder
   "Plain Language Summary goes here" is replaced). File any rendering
   issues against `HL7/plain-language` rather than the PHR IG.

No changes are needed inside the PHR IG source repo itself — the
publisher injects the block.

#### Justification

This is the path the work was already on at the time of resolution and
matches the central PLS mechanism FMG/TSC approved in September 2024
(per the patient-empowerment Zulip thread). It preserves the design
principle Grahame articulated in that thread — keep the editable
human-language content *outside* the IG so it can iterate without
technical corrections — while still meeting Gay Dolin's requirement
that the summary be visible on the IG landing page (not on a separate
product page). The 2025-06-12 revision already addresses the
review-quality concerns Debi Willis / Virginia Lorenzi raised about
AI-introduced wording errors. The publisher gotcha Mark Scrimshire hit
on 2025-05-29 is fixed in the released publisher, so the operational
risk to PHR is low.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the intent but, in addition to landing PR #15 in
`HL7/plain-language`, also publish a short author-controlled
plain-language paragraph **directly in the PHR IG `index.md`** (above
the table of contents, below the title) as a redundant local fallback,
plus a "Last reviewed" date and a link to the central summary. Wire the
WG into a recurring review cadence (e.g., each ballot cycle) to
re-approve both the in-IG paragraph and the central summary.

#### Justification

The central mechanism is correct in principle but, per the Zulip
discussion, has shipped with operational quirks: the placeholder still
renders when no summary is registered, the inline script has tripped
the publisher in the past, and the content lives in a different repo
from the IG (so a CI build of an older publisher, or a snapshot/print
view, may not render it). For the PHR IG specifically — whose primary
audience explicitly *includes* patients and the public — having the
summary survive even when the central injection fails is a
defense-in-depth win. The trade-off is mild duplication and a small
maintenance burden on the WG, which a "review at every ballot" cadence
makes manageable.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline to make any change in the PHR IG itself, on the grounds that
the request is already addressed by an HL7-wide mechanism that operates
*outside* the PHR IG: the IG Publisher injects the plain-language
summary block on every published IG home page automatically, and the
authoritative summary content lives in `HL7/plain-language`. Treat
FHIR-49611 as a documentation/process pointer rather than a PHR IG
change request, and close it with a comment directing the PHR editors
to land HL7/plain-language#15 in that repo instead.

#### Justification

Strictly read, the ticket asks for a change to "the home page", but the
mechanism HL7 actually adopted (and that FMG/TSC ratified in September
2024) does that injection centrally. Making no PHR-side change is
defensible because there is literally nothing to change in the PHR IG
source — the publisher does the injection — so there is no PHR ballot
artifact for the WG to vote on. The ticket's outcome is a PR in a
different repo, which is best tracked there.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The request has already been resolved Persuasive, and PR #15 is the
concrete deliverable the central PLS workflow expects. Disposition A
matches the mechanism FMG/TSC approved, keeps PHR aligned with how
every other IG (e.g., US Core 7.0, Da Vinci PDex) is being onboarded,
and lets the PHR editors keep the wording editable without technical
corrections. Disposition B's defense-in-depth has real merit given the
publisher gotchas surfaced in the IG-creation Zulip thread, but adding
duplicated copy inside `index.md` re-introduces exactly the
"summary trapped in a technical correction cycle" coupling Grahame
designed the central mechanism to avoid — the WG should instead push
fixes to the placeholder/script behaviour upstream in
`HL7/plain-language`. Disposition C under-delivers: the WG owes its
public-facing audience an explicit sign-off on the wording (Debi /
Virginia's lessons from the Patient Corrections review apply in full),
which means PHR leadership *must* actively approve the PR rather than
treat the central mechanism as fire-and-forget.

The PHR work group's action items reduce to: (1) review and approve the
2025-06-12 wording on `HL7/plain-language#15`, (2) merge, (3) confirm
the rendered output on the next PHR IG build, (4) optionally file a
follow-up issue against `HL7/plain-language` for the
empty-summary-placeholder behaviour Mark Scrimshire reported.
