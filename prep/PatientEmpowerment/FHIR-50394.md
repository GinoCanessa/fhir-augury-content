## Summary

Jacob Andersen reports that the Personal Health Record (PHR) FHIR IG —
balloted as a "universal realm" (UV) specification — is in practice
strongly US-centric in two specific places. Section 1.3 "Relevant Law"
enumerates US regulations without naming non-US frameworks such as the
EU's European Health Data Space (EHDS) Regulation, and Section 3
"Conformance Recommendations" anchors conformance to US-Core without
acknowledging equivalent base/core IGs from other affiliates (notably
the in-progress HL7 Europe Base Profiles, which are being built to meet
EHDS requirements). The reporter is asking the Patient Empowerment WG
to broaden the IG so that the "UV" label is genuinely justified, by
(a) listing additional jurisdictional regulations (starting with EHDS)
in the "Relevant Law" section, and (b) coordinating with other affiliates
so that non-US base/core IGs (e.g., HL7-EU base) are referenced alongside
US-Core in the conformance recommendations.

The only system cross-reference surfaced is the Confluence page for the
HL7 Europe Base Profiles (id `265491037`), which is mentioned directly
in the ticket description as the canonical pointer to the EU base work.
No related Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were returned.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the Personal Health Record (UV) IG in two places:

1. **Section 1.3 "Relevant Law"** — Restructure the section so that it
   is not implicitly a US-only list. Group entries by jurisdiction
   ("United States", "European Union", "Other"), and add at minimum an
   entry for the **European Health Data Space (EHDS) Regulation**, with
   a one- or two-sentence description and a citation to the official EU
   text (with the Wikipedia entry as a secondary reference). Add a
   prefatory sentence stating that the list is illustrative, not
   exhaustive, and that implementers in other jurisdictions should
   apply their own applicable regulations.
2. **Section 3 "Conformance Recommendations"** — Re-frame the
   "use US-Core" recommendation as "use the regional/national base or
   core IG appropriate to the deployment realm". Provide an explicit,
   non-exhaustive table of known regional base/core IGs (US-Core for
   US deployments; **HL7 Europe Base Profiles** —
   <https://build.fhir.org/ig/hl7-eu/base/>, described at
   <https://confluence.hl7.org/spaces/HEU/pages/265491037/Base+Profiles>
   — for EU deployments) and invite affiliates to submit additions.
   Mark the HL7 Europe entry as "in development" until that IG reaches
   a stable release, and add an editorial note inviting other affiliates
   (e.g., HL7 Australia, Canada Health Infoway-aligned profiles, etc.)
   to be listed.

#### Justification

The reporter's observation is factually correct: an IG balloted under
the universal realm should not bake US-only law and US-only profile
choices into its normative narrative sections. The change is text-only
in the two named sections — no resource, profile, or extension changes
are required — so the implementation cost is low and the change is
non-breaking. Explicitly naming EHDS and HL7 Europe Base Profiles is
also consistent with the ongoing cross-affiliate coordination pattern
already used by other UV IGs (e.g., IPS, IPA), where regional core
profiles are recognised alongside US-Core. This disposition directly
addresses both bullet points in the ticket without committing the WG
to an open-ended scope expansion.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than enumerating jurisdictions and regional base IGs inline in
the PHR IG, **introduce a single new sub-section** ("Realm-specific
considerations") that:

- States the IG's universal-realm intent and acknowledges that its
  examples and prior text leaned on US sources;
- Defines an extension point — a short, structured list maintained by
  the WG — where affiliates can register "approved realm overlays"
  (a regional base IG plus a pointer to that affiliate's relevant-law
  summary);
- Seeds that list with the two known entries (US-Core + US Relevant Law
  block; HL7 Europe Base Profiles + EHDS) but keeps the body of
  Section 1.3 and Section 3 jurisdiction-neutral.

In parallel, file a follow-up Patient Empowerment work item to formally
solicit affiliate submissions before the next ballot cycle.

#### Justification

This avoids two failure modes of Disposition A: (i) the IG having to
re-ballot every time a new jurisdiction is added or a regulation
changes, and (ii) the appearance that any law or IG _not_ enumerated is
out of scope. By making the list a maintained registry rather than
prose, the WG keeps the IG's normative narrative stable while still
honouring the reporter's underlying concern that the UV IG be genuinely
multi-realm. It also positions Patient Empowerment to coordinate
proactively with HL7 Europe and other affiliates rather than reacting
ticket-by-ticket. Trade-off vs. A: more upfront editorial work and a
new ongoing maintenance burden, in exchange for a more durable and
genuinely UV-shaped solution.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request as out of scope for this ballot cycle, on the basis
that (a) the EU base profiles cited are explicitly a preliminary build,
not a published HL7 IG, and the PHR IG should not normatively reference
unpublished work; and (b) the "Relevant Law" section is informative —
not normative — and has always been illustrative rather than exhaustive.
Suggest the reporter resubmit once HL7 Europe Base Profiles reach a
published release, at which point a targeted update can be made.

#### Justification

Referencing in-development affiliate work in a published UV IG creates
a maintenance dependency: if the HL7 Europe build URL or scope shifts,
the PHR IG would need erratum updates. Similarly, opening "Relevant
Law" to non-US jurisdictions invites scope creep that the WG has no
mechanism to maintain (every jurisdiction's regulations evolve on its
own cadence). Declining now, with a clear path to re-raise the issue
once the cited EU artifacts are published, is defensible and avoids
publishing references the WG cannot keep current.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter's core point — that a UV-realm IG should not be implicitly
US-only in its narrative sections — is correct, and the requested
changes are localised, text-only, and non-breaking. Disposition A
addresses the issue directly in the two sections the reporter named,
which is the minimum credible response for an "Highest"-priority ballot
comment from an affiliate reviewer. Disposition B is attractive in the
long term but is a larger editorial undertaking and introduces a new
maintained registry that the WG would have to commit to operating; it
would be a reasonable follow-up issue but is heavier than this single
ticket needs. Disposition C is the weakest option: declining a UV-realm
internationalisation comment from an HL7 Europe-aligned reviewer on the
grounds that the cited EU IG is "preliminary" risks reinforcing exactly
the US-centric perception the reporter is raising — and the change can
be hedged by labelling the HL7 Europe Base Profiles entry as
"in development" without pretending the work doesn't exist.
