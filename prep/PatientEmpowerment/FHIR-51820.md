## Summary

The reporter notes that a sentence in the PACIO Advance Directive
Interoperability (ADI) IG narrative describing how scanned PDF documents are
to be conveyed lacks a normative conformance verb. The current text
declaratively says scanned PDFs "are represented as a base64 encoded
attachment with `DocumentReference.content.attachment.contentType` set to
`application/pdf`." The reporter requests that the wording be tightened to
"**SHALL be** represented as a base64 encoded attachment with
`DocumentReference.content.attachment.contentType` set to `application/pdf`."
to make the requirement unambiguously normative. The ticket has already been
resolved as **Persuasive** with the proposed wording, and was approved by the
ADI community as part of Block Vote 4 (May Terry / Yolanda Liu, 9-0-0). No
related Jira tickets, Zulip discussions, or GitHub items were surfaced by
cross-reference or search — the change is purely an IG narrative wording fix.

## Details

**Description:**

> Scanned PDF documents are represented as a base64 encoded attachment with

> should be

**Resolution Description:**

> We agree with the reporter's call for a stronger wording in the narrative
> when the guidance calls for fixed values, such as the attachment MIME type
> for pdf.

> Scanned PDF documents ***SHALL be*** represented as a base64 encoded
> attachment with `DocumentReference.content.attachment.contentType` set to
> "application/pdf".

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the PACIO ADI IG narrative sentence describing scanned PDF conveyance
to read: "Scanned PDF documents **SHALL be** represented as a base64 encoded
attachment with `DocumentReference.content.attachment.contentType` set to
`application/pdf`." Apply this in the page(s) of the PACIO ADI IG that
discuss scanned PDF attachments on `DocumentReference`. No StructureDefinition,
profile, or example changes are required — this is a pure narrative wording
fix introducing the RFC 2119 conformance verb **SHALL**.

#### Justification

The reporter's request is the minimum change needed to remove ambiguity:
without a conformance verb, implementers may treat the sentence as
descriptive ("this is what people typically do") rather than normative
("this is what you must do"). Because the IG already constrains how scanned
PDFs are exchanged on `DocumentReference.content.attachment.contentType`,
making the requirement explicit with **SHALL** aligns the narrative with the
underlying intent and with HL7's standard use of RFC 2119 keywords. The ADI
community has already approved this wording in Block Vote 4 (9-0-0), and the
ticket carries a Persuasive resolution along these lines.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt the **SHALL** wording as in Disposition A, but pair it with a small
profile/conformance check rather than relying on the narrative alone. For
example, document the requirement on the appropriate ADI
`DocumentReference` profile (or an attached invariant) so that scanned-PDF
content is constrained by the profile's `contentType` fixed/required value,
and have the narrative reference that profile constraint. The narrative
sentence would then read: "Scanned PDF documents **SHALL be** represented
as a base64 encoded attachment with
`DocumentReference.content.attachment.contentType` fixed to
`application/pdf` (see `[ProfileName]`)."

#### Justification

Narrative-only **SHALL** statements are easy to miss in machine validation
and in tooling-driven conformance testing. Anchoring the requirement to a
profile element constraint makes the rule discoverable from the
StructureDefinition itself and validatable by FHIR validators, which is the
preferred HL7 pattern when a fixed value is intended. The trade-off is
scope: this requires touching the ADI profile artifacts (not just the
narrative), and may need additional ADI WG review beyond the wording fix
already block-voted. It is more invasive than the reporter requested.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Leave the narrative unchanged on the grounds that the surrounding ADI IG
text and the existing `DocumentReference` profile constraints already
communicate the expectation, and that adding **SHALL** here without
auditing every analogous narrative sentence in the IG risks inconsistency.

#### Justification

Selective insertion of **SHALL** in one paragraph while comparable
sentences elsewhere in the IG remain declarative could be more confusing
than the current state — implementers may read the contrast as deliberate
and infer that the un-strengthened sentences are non-normative on purpose.
A blanket conformance-language sweep would arguably be the right scope.
This rationale is weak, however, because the ticket has already been
resolved Persuasive and approved by Block Vote 4, indicating the WG does
not view the wording fix as harmful in isolation.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

This is a minimal, low-risk narrative fix that removes a real ambiguity in
the PACIO ADI IG: a sentence that describes a fixed-value requirement
without a conformance verb. The ADI community has already approved the
exact wording in Block Vote 4 (9-0-0), and the resolution recorded on the
ticket aligns with Disposition A. Disposition B's profile-level constraint
is a stronger long-term solution but is out of scope for what was reported
and would require additional artifact changes; it can be pursued
separately if the WG wants to harden conformance more broadly.
Disposition C does not have community support and would leave a known
ambiguity in the published narrative.
