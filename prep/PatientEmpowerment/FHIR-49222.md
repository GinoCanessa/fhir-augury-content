## Summary

The reporter flagged an apparent inconsistency in the Personal Health
Record (PHR) FHIR Implementation Guide: the IG's stated purpose
describes helping the reader implement a "**Patient** Health Record",
while the IG itself is named "**Personal** Health Record". The reporter
asks which term is correct and whether there is a meaningful difference
between the two. There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip
threads, Confluence pages, or GitHub items associated with this issue,
so the request stands on its own as an editorial / consistency question
about the IG's own framing language. The single comment from
@awatson1978 confirms that the working group has gone back and forth on
the terminology and is harmonising on "Personal Health Record", with a
TODO to audit the IG for any remaining "Patient Health Record"
references. The ticket has been resolved as **Persuasive** with a
resolution description of "Typo fixed."

## Details

**Description:**

> In "*The purpose of this implementation guide is to help the reader

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat the occurrence of "Patient Health Record" in the IG's purpose
statement as a typo and replace it with "Personal Health Record" so
that the IG's stated purpose matches its title. In the same change,
audit the rest of the IG narrative pages, glossary, profile
descriptions, and any extension / capability-statement narratives for
remaining occurrences of "Patient Health Record" and convert them to
"Personal Health Record" (matching the @awatson1978 TODO captured on
the ticket). No conformance artifacts (resources, profiles, value
sets, extensions) are added or removed; the change is purely editorial
in the IG's authoring narrative.

#### Justification

The IG's title and the work group's stated direction (per
@awatson1978) are both "Personal Health Record"; the body text saying
"Patient Health Record" is internally inconsistent and likely to
confuse first-time readers about the IG's scope. The ticket is filed
as a Technical Correction, which is the standard category for this
kind of editorial fix. The current resolution of **Persuasive** with
"Typo fixed" is consistent with this disposition, suggesting the
working group has effectively already taken this path; this
disposition simply ratifies that and commits the working group to the
follow-up audit so the fix is not partial.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — Define and Disambiguate Both Terms

#### Proposal

Rather than treating "Patient Health Record" as a pure typo, add a
short paragraph (or glossary entry) to the IG explaining the
relationship between the two terms — e.g., that *Personal Health
Record* (PHR) is the patient-controlled record this IG targets, while
*Patient Health Record* is sometimes used as a near-synonym in the
broader literature — and then standardise on "Personal Health Record"
throughout the IG body, with one explicit cross-reference back to the
glossary entry. Optionally, add the alternate phrasing as an `alias`
on relevant CapabilityStatement / IG-level metadata so that text
searches for either term land on this IG.

#### Justification

@awatson1978's comment notes that the two terms are "closely related"
and that the working group has gone back and forth, which suggests
the ambiguity is real in the wider community, not just in this IG.
Disposition A silently erases that ambiguity; Disposition B
acknowledges it once, in one place, and then follows the same final
text-replacement Disposition A proposes. This costs slightly more
authoring effort but reduces the chance of a future reviewer (or
another implementer in the same situation as the reporter) re-opening
the same question. It also gives the work group a single canonical
home for any future PHR-vs-EHR / PHR-vs-PCC distinctions.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (Not Persuasive)

#### Proposal

Decline the ticket on the grounds that "Patient Health Record" and
"Personal Health Record" are interchangeable in common usage and the
sentence in question is unambiguous in context — the IG title makes
the canonical name clear. Close as Not Persuasive with no spec change.

#### Justification

This would be defensible only if the working group considers the
terms truly synonymous and the reader's confusion negligible. It
preserves a long-standing piece of narrative without churn.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is a small, low-risk, editorial Technical Correction and
the work group has already signalled (both in the comment and in the
recorded **Persuasive / "Typo fixed"** resolution) that the canonical
name is *Personal Health Record*. Disposition A is the cheapest path
that fully satisfies the reporter and keeps the IG's purpose
statement consistent with its title. Disposition B is a reasonable
upgrade if the work group wants to forestall the same question being
re-asked, but it can also be filed as a follow-up ticket (e.g., "Add
PHR vs. Patient Health Record glossary entry") rather than blocking
this fix. Disposition C is not recommended: the reporter's confusion
is direct evidence that the inconsistent wording does cost the
reader, and there is no upside to leaving it in place.
