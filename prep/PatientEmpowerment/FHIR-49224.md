## Summary

The reporter observes that the Personal Health Record (PHR) FHIR Implementation
Guide presents itself as a "Universal" guide, yet Section 1.3 of the IG enumerates
only a narrow set of laws (effectively a single jurisdiction's legal framework
around PHR / patient data rights). The reporter argues that a Universal IG
should also surface other jurisdictions' relevant laws, so implementers in
non-US locales see their own legal context reflected. The Patient Empowerment
work group already triaged the ticket as **Persuasive** and committed to
broadening the legal references; a follow-up comment notes a TODO to reach out
to the Netherlands and Japan for their PHR-related laws, and a pull request
([HL7/personal-health-record-format-ig#72](https://github.com/HL7/personal-health-record-format-ig/pull/72))
was opened with the international laws update. No related Jira tickets, no
Zulip discussions, and no other GitHub items were surfaced by cross-references.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the resolution already implemented by the work group. In
`HL7/personal-health-record-format-ig`, expand the Section 1.3 narrative page
under `input/pagecontent/` to enumerate PHR-relevant legal frameworks from a
representative cross-section of jurisdictions in addition to the current set:
at minimum the EU (GDPR, including Articles 15 / 20 on right of access and
data portability), the United States (HIPAA Privacy Rule and the ONC
Information Blocking rule), the Netherlands (national PHR / "PGO" framework
referencing the MedMij standard and Wgbo / AVG implementation), and Japan
(Act on the Protection of Personal Information / APPI as it applies to
medical records and PHR services). Frame each entry as a cited reference,
not as paraphrased statutory language, with a hyperlink to the authoritative
source. Land this as the existing PR
[HL7/personal-health-record-format-ig#72](https://github.com/HL7/personal-health-record-format-ig/pull/72).

#### Justification

The IG self-identifies as Universal, and the ticket simply asks the narrative
to live up to that scope. The work group has already voted **Persuasive**
4-0-0 (Motion: Hasashi, Second: Rachel) and the PR exists, so accepting as
requested merely confirms work that is already in flight. The change is
narrative-only, has no impact on conformance resources, profiles, or
implementer behavior, and addresses an obvious credibility gap for non-US
readers of a "Universal" IG. Citing rather than quoting statutes also
sidesteps the copyright gotcha noted above.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Instead of (or in addition to) enumerating individual jurisdictions inline in
Section 1.3, restructure that section to (i) state the *categories* of legal
obligations a PHR system typically must satisfy (e.g., right of access, right
of correction, data portability, breach notification, lawful basis for
processing, retention limits) and (ii) provide a short, curated table of
**example** jurisdictional implementations of each category, with hyperlinks
out to the authoritative source — rather than treating any one country's
laws as canonical. Keep the per-jurisdiction list intentionally short and
move the longer enumeration to a companion sub-page (e.g.,
`input/pagecontent/laws-by-jurisdiction.md`) so Section 1.3 stays
introductory.

#### Justification

A Universal IG cannot realistically maintain an exhaustive, current legal
catalogue for every realm — laws change frequently and HL7 is not in a
position to keep that synchronised. Re-framing Section 1.3 around
*categories* of obligation (which are stable) and treating jurisdictions as
*examples* is more durable, scales to additional realms over time without
re-balloting, and avoids implying that the listed countries are
authoritative. Compared with Disposition A this trades a slightly larger
narrative refactor for a much lower long-term maintenance burden, and it
also better matches the FHIR community's general preference for
realm-neutral universal guides with realm-specific adaptations layered on
top.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as Not Persuasive on the grounds that legal/regulatory
context is out of scope for an HL7 IG, and direct readers to consult counsel
in their own realm. Leave Section 1.3 as a brief acknowledgement that PHR
deployments are subject to local law, without attempting to enumerate any
jurisdiction.

#### Justification

HL7 IGs specify interoperability and conformance, not legal compliance.
Maintaining accurate cross-jurisdictional legal references is a substantial
ongoing burden, exposes HL7 to the risk of presenting outdated or incorrect
legal guidance, and risks the appearance of legal advice. A minimal
"consult your local counsel" stub is defensible for a Universal IG and
keeps the spec focused on the technical surface.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A (with a path toward B in a future revision)

The work group has already voted Persuasive 4-0-0 and a PR is open, so the
practical choice is to land Disposition A and close the ticket. The reporter's
concern is legitimate — a Universal IG that lists laws from only one realm
is a real credibility issue — and accepting as requested is the lowest-risk
way to discharge the ticket on the current cycle. Disposition C
under-serves the reporter and ignores a vote that has already happened.
Disposition B is the more sustainable end state, but it is a larger
narrative refactor than what PR #72 contemplates and should be raised as a
follow-up ticket once the immediate addition lands. Before merging, the
work group should also (i) confirm the PR cites rather than quotes statutes
to sidestep the copyright gotcha, and (ii) trigger
`repo-analysis HL7/personal-health-record-format-ig refresh` so future
ticket-prep runs have a cached briefing for this repository.
