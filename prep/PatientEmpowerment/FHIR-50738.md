## Summary

The reporter, writing on behalf of the U.S. Federal Electronic Health Record
Modernization (FEHRM) program office, registers a **negative ballot vote** on
the *Personal Health Record (FHIR)* (SPHR) specification because the SPHR
data model carries a **"Gender Identity"** element on the Patient resource
that was contributed by the HL7 Gender Harmony project. FEHRM states that
including this element is incompatible with current federal agency policy
under U.S. Executive Order 14168, *"Defending Women from Gender Ideology
Extremism and Restoring Biological Truth to the Federal Government"*, which
directs federal agencies to remove materials that promote or institutionalize
gender-identity concepts. The ticket itself does not propose specific
spec-level wording, but it implicitly asks the Patient Empowerment work group
to remove (or otherwise disable) the SPHR Patient profile's Gender Identity
representation so that federal agencies can vote affirmatively and adopt
SPHR.

There are **no cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items
linked to FHIR-50738** in the Augury index. However, several thematically
related items provide context the work group should weigh:

- [FHIR-50177](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50177) — *EO Change Proposal*
  for **US Core 8.0.0** (Cross-Group Projects work group). This is the most
  directly comparable proposal: it lays out a 12-point reconciliation plan
  for US Core that includes deprecating the Gender Identity Extension,
  removing it from the US Core Patient profile, and replacing "gender"
  language with "sex" to align with EO 14168. SPHR materially derives from /
  layers on US Core, so whatever Patient Empowerment decides for SPHR should
  be consistent with how Cross-Group Projects resolves the US Core proposal.
- [FHIR-50421](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50421) — *Remove "gender
  identity" references…* in the **NHSN dQM Reporting IG** (Public Health).
  Already resolved **Persuasive**: that IG agreed to strip gender-identity
  references in narrative, profiles, and examples to comply with the EO. It
  establishes an early precedent that other IG work groups have been willing
  to remove gender-identity content rather than block on the federal vote.
- A cluster of older Gender Harmony adoption tickets
  ([FHIR-43835](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43835),
  [FHIR-43867](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43867),
  [FHIR-46892](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-46892),
  [FHIR-46893](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-46893),
  [FHIR-47779](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-47779),
  [FHIR-44123](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-44123),
  [FHIR-35368](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-35368)) — these are the
  *original* requests across multiple specs to ADOPT the Gender Harmony
  Gender Identity (GI) element, including in patient-facing artifacts. They
  represent the standing community position FHIR-50738 is asking SPHR to
  reverse, and they include the Federal-Rule / Cures-mandated counter-view
  (FHIR-35368) that argued some gender-identity content is required by law
  and should not be removed.

A reasonable reading is that FHIR-50738 is the SPHR-specific instance of the
same federal-vote pressure already documented for US Core (FHIR-50177) and
NHSN dQM (FHIR-50421), and the work group's response should be coordinated
with those tickets and with the Gender Harmony Project owners.

### [FHIR-50177: EO Change Proposal](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50177)

US Core 8.0.0 reconciliation proposal (Cross-Group Projects) that lays out
12 specific spec edits to comply with EO 14168, including: making
`Patient.gender` optional, removing the "Name to Use" mapping, dropping the
Sexual Orientation profile from USCDI requirements, removing the BirthSex /
Pronouns / Sex-For-Clinical-Use extensions from the US Core Patient profile,
**deprecating the Gender Identity Extension and removing it from the US Core
Patient profile**, redefining the US Core Sex extension against an HHS
"Federal Administrative Sex" value set, and replacing "gender" with "sex"
throughout examples and guidance. Because SPHR derives from / aligns with US
Core, whatever pattern US Core lands on for Gender Identity directly informs
what SPHR should do.

*Users involved:* Authored on the US Core / Cross-Group Projects side; full
participant list not exposed by the Augury query.

### [FHIR-50421: Remove "gender identity" references to comply with initial guidance regarding President Trump's Executive Order](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50421)

NHSN dQM Reporting IG ticket (Public Health work group), reported by Rick
Geimer, **resolved Persuasive on 2025-06-12**. Calls for removing
gender-identity content from narrative, profiles, and examples in the dQM
IG. Establishes a precedent that an HL7 IG work group, when faced with the
same EO pressure, agreed to remove the references rather than dispute the
federal position. SPHR (Patient Empowerment) faces the same trade-off but
on a more central artifact (the SPHR Patient profile itself).

*Users involved:* Reporter Rick Geimer; assignee unassigned at last
snapshot.

### [FHIR-44123: Use updated content included in FHIR R6 regarding Sex and Gender](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-44123)

Asks SPHR / related specs to align with the FHIR R6 Patient resource Sex &
Gender content from the Gender Harmony Project. This is effectively the
inverse of FHIR-50738: it argues that SPHR *should* align with Gender
Harmony, including Gender Identity. The work group will need to reconcile
these two requests rather than treat FHIR-50738 in isolation.

### [FHIR-43835: Adoption of Gender Harmony Gender Identity (GI) data element](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43835)

The foundational request to adopt the Gender Harmony Gender Identity element
across patient-related artifacts. This ticket is the upstream rationale for
why the GI element is in SPHR in the first place. Backing the element out
without a coordinated decision here would create inconsistency with the
Gender Harmony adoption commitment.

### [FHIR-43867: Adoption of Gender Harmony Recorded Sex or Gender (RSG) data element](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43867)

Companion to FHIR-43835 covering RSG adoption. Relevant because any decision

### [FHIR-47779: IPS should include Gender Harmony defined elements, specifically Gender Identity](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-47779)

Parallel request that the International Patient Summary include Gender

realm-relevant) on Gender Identity has interoperability consequences.

### [FHIR-46892: Gender vs Gender Identity for Patient](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-46892)

Discussion-style ticket about the conceptual distinction between
`Patient.gender` (administrative) and Gender Identity. Relevant to the SPHR
work group's framing if it elects to repurpose `Patient.gender` (per
FHIR-50177's pattern) instead of carrying a separate Gender Identity
element.

### [FHIR-46893: Gender identity for practitioner](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-46893)

Companion ticket extending Gender Identity to Practitioner. Mentioned only
because removing GI from Patient in SPHR while leaving it on other resources

### [FHIR-35368: Suggest don't remove values required by Federal Rule/Cures/2015 Edition](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-35368)

Argues the opposite of FHIR-50738: that some gender-identity content is

The thread opens with Abbie Watson asking whether US Core 8 is "dead on

and HL7 (as an SDO) is not itself a federal agency subject to the order.

There is **no consensus** in the thread. Positions range from "remove to
comply" (Moehrke), to "the EO doesn't bind HL7 directly, wait it out"
(Grieve), to "implementations will keep it regardless" (Thompson, McKenzie).
The Patient Empowerment work group should expect the same range of opinion
when disposing of FHIR-50738.

## Proposed Dispositions

These dispositions are constrained by the fact that the ticket has no
linked GitHub PR/repo evidence in Augury and no Zulip cross-references; the
SPHR repo is also not present in the local `cache/github/repos/`
briefings index. Where a disposition implies an edit to SPHR sources, it
names the artifact at the *FHIR* level (Patient profile / Gender Identity
extension binding) rather than a specific source path; the work group
should confirm the exact authoring path against the SPHR build before
acting.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat FHIR-50738 as **Persuasive** and remove the Gender Harmony "Gender
Identity" representation from the SPHR Patient profile in the next SPHR
ballot:

- Drop the SPHR Patient profile's reference to the Gender Harmony Gender
  Identity extension (and any associated `mustSupport` / `differential`
  entries that surface a Gender Identity element).
- Remove SPHR examples and narrative guidance that demonstrate or describe
  Gender Identity.
- Add an informational note on the Patient profile page explaining that
  Gender Identity has been removed from SPHR's Patient profile and pointing
  implementers to the standard Gender Harmony extension if they need it
  outside the SPHR conformance surface.
- Mirror the US Core direction (per FHIR-50177) where applicable so that
  SPHR and US Core remain consistent.

#### Justification

This is the disposition FEHRM is explicitly asking for and matches the
precedent already set by NHSN dQM (FHIR-50421, resolved Persuasive). It
unblocks the federal negative vote, allowing FEHRM-aligned agencies (VA,
DoD, IHS, USCG via FEHRM) to adopt SPHR. It also keeps SPHR aligned with
the direction US Core appears to be heading in FHIR-50177. The Gender
Harmony extension itself is *not* deleted from FHIR — implementers who need
it can still use it as a standard extension; SPHR simply stops requiring /
illustrating it on its profile.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Treat FHIR-50738 as **Considered for Future Use** and respond with a
narrower, profile-layered fix rather than a removal:

- Keep the Gender Harmony Gender Identity extension referenced from the
  SPHR Patient profile, but mark it **`mustSupport = false`** and downgrade
  any `min = 1` cardinality to `0..1`. Make clear in profile guidance that
  exposing or populating Gender Identity is **optional** for an SPHR-
  conforming system.
- Add a "federal realm" implementation note on the Patient profile page
  documenting that federal agencies operating under EO 14168 may legally
  decline to populate or surface Gender Identity, and that doing so still
  yields a conforming SPHR Patient.
- Coordinate with the Gender Harmony project owners and the US Core team
  (FHIR-50177) on shared wording so SPHR, US Core, and Gender Harmony tell
  the same story.

#### Justification

This addresses FEHRM's actual blocker — that adopting SPHR would force
federal agencies to surface a Gender Identity element they are policy-bound
to remove — without dropping the element entirely and breaking with the
existing community decisions to adopt Gender Harmony (FHIR-43835,
FHIR-44123, FHIR-47779). It also avoids creating a divergence between SPHR
and IPS / international expectations and avoids contradicting the Cures /
2015-Edition position raised in FHIR-35368 (some gender-identity content
may be regulatorily required for non-FEHRM realms). Trade-off vs. A: SPHR
keeps the schema surface available to the larger community; FEHRM gets a
written, unambiguous "you don't have to populate this" guarantee that
their compliance teams can point to.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

**Not Persuasive.** Decline the request to remove Gender Identity from the
SPHR Patient profile. Rationale: the Gender Identity element is part of an
HL7-wide Gender Harmony adoption decision (FHIR-43835, FHIR-43867,
FHIR-44123, FHIR-47779), HL7 is a standards development organization not a
federal agency subject to EO 14168, and at least one prior ticket
(FHIR-35368) argues that removing gender-identity content would conflict
with extant U.S. federal regulation (Cures / 2015-Edition CEHRT). Direct
FEHRM to populate the element with no value (or to suppress it at the
implementation layer) rather than asking SPHR to remove it from the
standard.

#### Justification

EO 14168 binds executive-branch agencies, not voluntary consensus standards
bodies. Removing the element from SPHR would cause the standard to lurch in
response to a single agency's vote, contradict the broader Gender Harmony
adoption commitment, diverge from international peers (IPS — see
FHIR-47779), and possibly conflict with other federal regulation
(FHIR-35368). Per the Patient-Empowerment Zulip thread above, both Grahame
Grieve (the EO is vague and likely to be litigated; HL7 isn't bound by it)
and Cooper Thompson (implementations will keep the support regardless)
articulated this position. The community precedent of FHIR-50421 (where
NHSN dQM accepted the removal) is distinguishable: dQM is a narrower
reporting IG, not a foundational personal-health-record data model.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition: B (Alternative Approach — make Gender Identity
optional with explicit federal-realm guidance).**

Disposition A satisfies FEHRM but throws away years of cross-work-group
work on Gender Harmony adoption and creates a real divergence with IPS and
with the Gender Harmony project, which other realms and the international
community continue to depend on. Disposition C respects the SDO's
independence but leaves FEHRM (and the agencies it represents — VA, DoD,
IHS, USCG) holding a negative vote, which materially reduces SPHR's reach
in exactly the population SPHR is most useful for. Disposition B threads
the needle: it preserves the Gender Harmony schema commitment and the
standard's reach into international realms, while giving FEHRM a written,
profile-level guarantee that conforming SPHR systems can omit Gender
Identity. It also positions SPHR consistently with the direction US Core is
heading in FHIR-50177 (deprecate / make optional rather than delete) and
follows Patient Empowerment's traditional pattern of leaving choice with
the patient and the implementer rather than legislating it from the
standard. The work group should coordinate the exact wording with
Cross-Group Projects (FHIR-50177) and the Gender Harmony project owners
before committing the change.
