## Summary

**Personal Health Record (FHIR)** specification. The reporter notes that
*picture 1.2* in the IG is presented without supporting prose and asks the
authors to add explanatory text so readers can interpret the figure and

and recommends dropping that header. Both tickets have the same proposed

[PR HL7/personal-health-record-format-ig#76](https://github.com/HL7/personal-health-record-format-ig/pull/76)

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add explanatory narrative immediately adjacent to *picture 1.2* in the
Personal Health Record (FHIR) IG (the page currently containing
"picture 1.2" in Section 1.2 of `HL7/personal-health-record-format-ig`),
as already merged in
[PR #76](https://github.com/HL7/personal-health-record-format-ig/pull/76).
Concretely:

1. Insert prose before/after the figure that explains what the figure
   depicts (PHR-as-medical-grade record receiving data from healthcare
   encounters, personal documentation/measurement, and other sources)
   and ties each visual element back to a sentence in the surrounding
   text.
2. Adopt the FHIR-50734 replacement purpose sentence — *"The purpose of
   this specification is to provide standard mechanisms for a PHR to
   interoperate, facilitating sharing of information obtained by the
   PHR from healthcare encounters, personal documentation and
   measurement, and other sources."* — as the lead-in framing for the
   figure.
3. Add a figure caption that names the figure (e.g., "Figure 1.2 —
   Sources of information flowing into a Personal Health Record") so it
   can be referenced from the surrounding text and from other pages.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter asked for, what the workgroup already
voted Persuasive (Motion: Rachel, Second: Hisashi, 4-0-0), and what is
already implemented in PR #76. It is a documentation-only change with no
profile, terminology, or conformance impact; the risk of regression is
limited to IG build / cross-reference hygiene. Pairing the figure prose
with the FHIR-50734 wording keeps the two tickets in sync and avoids a
second round of edits to the same page. It also follows the broader HL7
authoring norm that every figure in normative-style narrative should
have introductory prose and a numbered caption so screen readers and
indexes can attach to it.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the spirit of FHIR-49223 but expand the scope of the edit beyond
a single explanatory paragraph. Specifically:

1. Replace *picture 1.2* with a captioned, figure-numbered diagram
   (e.g., `Figure 1.2`) and include both alt-text (for accessibility)
   and a short prose explanation that maps each box/arrow in the
   diagram to a concept in the spec.
2. Restructure Section 1.2 so that the new purpose statement
   (FHIR-50734) is the lead paragraph, the figure follows, and a brief
   "How to read this figure" subsection sits beneath it. Drop the
   "Design Philosophy" header per FHIR-50734.
3. Add a forward link from Section 1.2 to the relevant downstream
   sections (capabilities, data sources, exchange patterns) so the
   figure functions as an entry point to the rest of the IG.

#### Justification

The literal request — "provide some text to explain it" — is easy to
satisfy with one paragraph, but the underlying problem is that the
figure currently floats without context, caption, or accessibility
metadata, and lives in a section whose header (`Design Philosophy`)
does not match its content. Doing the edit once, holistically, costs
little more than the single-paragraph fix, prevents a third ballot
ticket on the same page, and makes the figure usable by readers using
assistive technology. The trade-off vs. Disposition A is slightly more
editorial work and a marginally larger diff, but the diff is still
narrative-only and carries no normative or interoperability impact.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-49223 as **Not Persuasive** on the grounds that the figure
is informative-only, the surrounding section already conveys the
concepts in prose, and adding figure-explanation text is editorial
polish rather than a defect in the specification. Keep the figure
in place unchanged and address any clarity concerns through the
companion ticket FHIR-50734's purpose-statement edit alone.

#### Justification

A defensible "decline" rests on (a) the change being editorial, not
conformance-affecting, and (b) FHIR-50734 already rewriting the
surrounding narrative such that the figure may become self-evident
afterwards. If the workgroup wants to limit the number of narrative
churn cycles in this section before the next ballot, declining 49223
and accepting only 50734 is the minimal-change path. The cost is that
the figure will continue to lack a caption and explanatory prose,
which is a known accessibility and readability gap and the exact issue
the reporter raised.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket has already been voted Persuasive by the Patient Empowerment
work group (4-0-0) and the change is implemented in
[PR #76](https://github.com/HL7/personal-health-record-format-ig/pull/76),
which also satisfies the companion ticket FHIR-50734. The change is
narrative-only, carries no conformance or interoperability risk, and
directly resolves the reporter's concern that the figure is unexplained.
Disposition B is attractive as a quality improvement and the work group
should *consider* adopting its caption/accessibility points as
follow-on editorial polish, but it should not block closing FHIR-49223
on the disposition already taken. Disposition C is hard to defend
given the existing Persuasive vote and the very low cost of the edit.
Confirm the merged PR matches the ticket text, mark FHIR-49223 (and
FHIR-50734) Resolved, and — if the work group wants the captioned-
figure / "How to read this figure" treatment from Disposition B —
file a small follow-up editorial ticket rather than reopening this one.
