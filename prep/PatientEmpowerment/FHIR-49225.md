## Summary

Ricardo Quintano reports that Section 1.5 of the **Personal Health Record
(FHIR)** IG is mis-titled or mis-scoped: it is presented as "use cases"
but actually describes *concepts*. He asks the Patient Empowerment work
group to populate the section with a set of common use cases that
explain the aim of the IG to a new reader, citing the IHE Scheduling
Volume 1 use-case section as a model for the level of detail expected.

Abbie Watson (`awatson1978`) marked the ticket **Persuasive** on
2025‑01‑30 with a note that the work group could expand the use cases
and a TODO to discuss with the work group whether to move them to their
own page. On 2025‑03‑13 she posted a substantial draft of expanded
use-case content covering twelve scenarios (Longitudinal Health Records,
Longitudinal Studies, Snowbirds, Symptom Tracking, Long COVID, Multiple
Chronic Conditions, Differential Diagnoses, Alternative Care, Bring
Your Own Device, Foster Care System, Migrants/Immigrants, Climate
Refugees), each with a representative patient narrative. A later
comment on 2025‑08‑28 mentions reviewing how the IG is handling Patient
Request for Corrections, suggesting that work-group review of the draft
material is still in progress.

No Jira tickets, Zulip threads, GitHub issues/PRs, or commits are
cross-referenced to FHIR-49225 in the Augury index, so the ticket
appears to be a stand-alone editorial / structural request against the
PHR IG.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt Abbie Watson's 2025‑03‑13 draft as the new content of **Section
1.5** of the Personal Health Record (FHIR) IG. Concretely:

- Rename Section 1.5 from its current "concepts" framing to **"Use
  Cases"** (or split: keep a brief "Concepts" section and add a sibling
  "Use Cases" section/page).
- Populate the section with the twelve narrative use cases drafted in
  the ticket (Longitudinal Health Records, Longitudinal Studies,
  Snowbirds, Symptom Tracking, Long COVID, Multiple Chronic Conditions,
  Differential Diagnoses, Alternative Care, Bring Your Own Device,
  Foster Care System, Migrants/Immigrants, Climate Refugees), each
  rendered as a labelled subsection with: (a) a one-line definition of
  the scenario class, and (b) the representative patient narrative.
- Add a short introductory paragraph on the page explaining that the
  use cases are illustrative (not normative) and meant to communicate
  the breadth of patient-controlled health-record scenarios the IG
  intends to support.
- Cite the IHE Scheduling Volume 1 §1.5.5.4.2 example referenced by the
  reporter in a "Further reading" footnote so future editors understand
  the format target.

#### Justification

The reporter explicitly asked for "a set of common use cases" modelled
on an IHE volume-1 example, and the work-group lead has already drafted
the content and disposed the ticket **Persuasive**. Accepting as
requested closes the smallest possible gap: it answers the literal
complaint (Section 1.5 is mislabelled), it uses content the work group
has already authored, and it follows an established HL7/IHE convention
of front-loading IGs with patient-narrative use cases. Twelve scenarios
is generous but not excessive for a PHR IG whose value proposition is
its breadth across consumer, research, foster-care, immigration, and
climate-displacement contexts; trimming should be left to editorial
review rather than a disposition decision.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Restructure rather than just rewrite. Specifically:

- Keep the existing "Concepts" content in (a renamed) Section 1.5, so
  that conceptual framing stays adjacent to the IG introduction.
- Move the use-case narratives to a **dedicated top-level "Use Cases"
  page** (e.g., `usecases.html`) added to the IG menu next to the
  existing introduction, rather than nesting them under Section 1.5.
- On that new page, organise the twelve narratives into 3–4 thematic
  groups (Continuity Across Time, Continuity Across Place,
  Patient-Generated & Device Data, Vulnerable Populations) so a reader
  can scan to a relevant scenario quickly.
- Include a short table mapping each use case to the PHR IG capability
  it primarily exercises (e.g., "Bring Your Own Device" → device data
  ingest; "Foster Care System" → role-based access / consent), so the
  use cases are wired back to normative content rather than reading as
  prose only.
- Link from a one-paragraph stub in Section 1.5 ("Use Cases — see
  *Use Cases* page") so readers landing on the introduction still find
  the bridge.

#### Justification

This matches the explicit TODO in awatson1978's 2025‑01‑30 comment
("discuss with workgroup whether to separate out to its own page") and
preserves the existing conceptual framing instead of overwriting it.
Twelve narratives at one or two paragraphs each will roughly double the
length of the current introduction; nesting that volume inside §1.5
risks burying the rest of the IG's narrative chapters. A standalone
page also makes it cheaper to add or revise individual use cases later
(each use case is editorially independent), and the capability-mapping
table addresses a known weakness of free-text use cases — that
reviewers cannot tell which normative profiles or operations they are
meant to exemplify. Trade-off vs. Disposition A: more editorial work up
front (grouping, mapping, menu changes) in exchange for a more
maintainable and navigable result.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline to expand Section 1.5 in this way. Instead, simply **rename**
Section 1.5 to "Concepts" so the heading matches the content, and close
the ticket without adding the twelve narrative use cases.

#### Justification

This option is included for completeness but is hard to defend: the
reporter's underlying need is for new readers to understand the aim of
the IG, and the work group has already agreed (via the **Persuasive**
disposition) that expanded use cases are warranted. Declining would
also strand awatson1978's substantial 2025‑03‑13 draft. The only
scenario in which this is the right call is if the work group decides
the narratives belong in a separate companion document (e.g., a
Confluence rationale page) rather than in the normative IG itself —
in which case the ticket should be declined with a pointer to where
the use-case material will live, not silently closed.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **B (Alternative Approach)**

The ticket has already been internally accepted as Persuasive, so the
substantive choice is between *replacing* §1.5 with the new use cases
(A) and *adding a dedicated Use Cases page while keeping the existing
concepts content* (B). Disposition B is recommended because (1) the
work-group lead's own 2025‑01‑30 comment explicitly raises separation
to its own page as the open design question, (2) the draft content is
large enough (twelve patient narratives) that nesting it inside the
introduction will dominate the chapter and obscure the rest of the IG
structure, (3) a separate page leaves room to grow the use-case set
over future ballot cycles without re-litigating the IG introduction
each time, and (4) adding a capability-mapping table converts the
narratives from purely motivational prose into navigation aids that
point readers at the normative profiles, operations, and exchange
patterns the IG defines — which is the actual value the reporter is
asking for ("helps the reader in understanding the aim of the IG").
The work group should still feel free to merge §1.5's existing concept
content into the new page's preamble if, on review, the conceptual
framing reads better immediately above the use cases.
