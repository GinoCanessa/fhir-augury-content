## Summary

TSMG, via reporter Joan Harper (comment 4 imported by Ron G. Parker), is asking
the Patient Empowerment WG to re-anchor the canonical URL of the
`PatientCorrectionTaskTypes` CodeSystem in the Patient Corrections IG away from
the IG namespace (`http://hl7.org/fhir/uv/patient-corrections/CodeSystem/PatientCorrectionTaskTypes`)
and into terminology.hl7.org (THO), per the TSMG "CodeSystem Identifier"
guidance and the FHIR R5 best-practice text in
`terminologies.html#system`. The request includes a companion ask to register
the CodeSystem in THO via a UTG ticket. The ticket was originally resolved
"Considered for Future Use" (vote 7-0-0, 2022-10-17), and was re-opened by
Lynn Laakso on 2025-07-10 "post STU1 publication" so the change can be
reconsidered for the next publication of Patient Corrections.

This is one of a large family of identical tickets Joan Harper filed against
many IGs to enforce the same TSMG CodeSystem-canonical-URL policy
(see Related Jira below); FHIR-37161 is the Patient Corrections-specific
instance.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Move the canonical URL of `PatientCorrectionTaskTypes` from the IG namespace
to THO in the next Patient Corrections publication:

- **Old:** `http://hl7.org/fhir/uv/patient-corrections/CodeSystem/PatientCorrectionTaskTypes`
- **New:** `http://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/patient-correction-task-types`
  (exact slug subject to UTG/THO naming review)

Concretely:

1. File a UTG ticket against `HL7/UTG` to register the new CodeSystem in THO,
   carrying over `id`, `status`, `experimental`, `caseSensitive`, `content`,
   the existing concept set, and adding TSMG-required metadata
   (`jurisdiction`, `publisher`, `copyright`, `hierarchyMeaning` if relevant).
2. Once UTG publishes, in the Patient Corrections IG source
   (FSH `CodeSystem: PatientCorrectionTaskTypes` or the corresponding
   resource JSON):
   - Change `* ^url` (or the JSON `url`) to the THO canonical.
   - Replace any in-IG concept enumeration with a `* ^content = #not-present`
     declaration (or remove the local CodeSystem entirely) so the IG depends on
     THO rather than redefining the codes.
   - Add the appropriate THO version to the IG's `dependencies` in
     `sushi-config.yaml` / `ig.ini`.
3. Update every ValueSet that includes this CodeSystem (including
   `Task.code` bindings on Patient Corrections Task profiles) to point at the
   new THO canonical.
4. Document the URL change in the IG's "Change Notes" / "Migration" page so
   implementers of STU1 know how to remap.

#### Justification

This is exactly the TSMG-published policy (Jan 2022 CodeSystem Identifier
Task Force deck, attachment 81034628) and is restated as best practice in the
R5 specification (`terminologies.html#system`). Adopting it brings Patient
Corrections into alignment with the rest of the family of tickets Joan Harper
filed (FHIR-37149/37150/37158/37159/37160/37192 and the FHIR-38xxx wave) and
removes a long-standing anomaly: a "shared" terminology asset living inside an
IG namespace that other specifications cannot cleanly reuse. Re-opening
post-STU1 (per Lynn Laakso, 2025-07-10) is the right window — STU1 published
under the IG-namespaced URL is now fixed; the next normative/STU2 cycle is the
correct time to re-anchor.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — Re-host in THO but keep an IG-namespace alias for backward compatibility

#### Proposal

Do everything in Disposition A, **plus**:

1. Retain a stub CodeSystem at the original URL
   `http://hl7.org/fhir/uv/patient-corrections/CodeSystem/PatientCorrectionTaskTypes`
   with `status = retired`, `content = not-present`, and a narrative pointing
   readers to the new THO canonical. Do not remove the URL outright.
2. In the IG NarrativeLink / Change Notes page, publish an explicit URL-mapping
   table and recommend that implementers follow the THO canonical going
   forward, but make clear that the legacy URL will still resolve (to the
   retired stub) for at least one publication cycle.
3. In the Task profile binding, bind to the THO ValueSet but keep an
   `extensible` (rather than `required`) binding for one cycle if that is not
   already the case, so STU1 instances using the old codes (or extensions
   carrying them) still validate.

#### Justification

The community impact of changing a published canonical URL is real: every
STU1 implementer's validators, terminology servers, and stored Task instances
reference the IG-namespaced URL. A clean cut (Disposition A) is technically
correct but strands STU1 deployments. Disposition B trades a small amount of
ongoing maintenance (the retired stub) for a much smoother migration path,
which is consistent with how FHIR has historically handled canonical-URL
relocations (the original URL is not reused for something else; it is
retired, never re-pointed). It also gives the WG a defensible answer to TSMG
("we adopted the policy") without imposing a hard break on early adopters.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark FHIR-37161 as "Not Persuasive" / "Considered for Future Use" again, on
the basis that:

1. The Patient Corrections IG defines `PatientCorrectionTaskTypes` for its own
   internal use — it is a Task-code enumeration tightly bound to this IG's
   workflow profiles, with no current evidence of cross-IG reuse demand.
2. The ask is policy-driven rather than driven by a concrete implementer pain
   point, and re-anchoring would invalidate published STU1 references with no
   offsetting interoperability gain.
3. The WG can revisit if and when (a) another IG actually wants to import
   these codes, or (b) TSMG reissues the policy as a normative requirement
   rather than guidance.

#### Justification

This is essentially the disposition the WG reached in 2022 (vote 7-0-0,
"Considered for Future Use… once further code changes are no longer
considered"). The conditions that prompted that vote — STU work in progress,
no demonstrated cross-IG reuse — still arguably apply. Declining keeps STU1
implementers stable and avoids spending IG-cycle effort on a URL change with
no functional improvement. The defensible category is "out-of-scope policy
alignment without concrete implementer demand."

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **B (Re-host in THO with a retired-stub alias)**.

Disposition C is the path of least effort but is hard to defend a second time:
the original 2022 vote was conditional ("once further code changes are no
longer considered"), and Lynn Laakso has explicitly re-opened the ticket to
revisit it post-STU1. The TSMG policy is now several years old, has been
re-stated in the R5 specification, and has been applied uniformly to dozens of
peer artifacts via Joan Harper's parallel tickets — Patient Corrections will
become an outlier if it declines.

Disposition A is the correct end-state but, taken as a single step, breaks
canonical references in published STU1 artifacts and any deployed Task
instances. Disposition B reaches the same end-state while honoring FHIR's
long-standing convention that published canonical URLs should not be reused
or silently relocated; the retired stub is cheap to maintain and gives
implementers a one-cycle deprecation window.

The WG should pair this with the corresponding UTG registration ticket as
the dependency (the THO CodeSystem must exist before the IG can rebind), and
should look at how the FHIR-38xxx-series sibling tickets are being dispositioned
in other WGs to keep wording and migration guidance consistent.
