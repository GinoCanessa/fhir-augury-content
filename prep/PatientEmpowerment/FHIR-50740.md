## Summary

The reporter (Chris Melo) observes that the Personal Health Record (PHR)
FHIR IG is published in the **Universal (UV) realm** but its substantive
content is overwhelmingly US-specific: the only legal/regulatory citations
are to US law, the supporting references are US-relevant, and the data
model is built on US-realm artifacts (US Core, C-CDA on FHIR, and
Argonaut). The ticket asks the work group to either (a) broaden the IG so
that it genuinely represents international needs — adding non-US legal
frameworks and non-US base profiles — or (b) re-scope the IG as a US
realm IG that is honest about its actual scope.

This complaint is part of a recurring pattern across HL7 IGs. The closely
related ticket
[FHIR-50394: UV-realm profile very US-specific](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50394)
(also Patient Empowerment, also against the PHR FHIR IG, also Highest /
Triaged, opened ~10 days earlier by Jacob Andersen) makes essentially the
same complaint with concrete suggestions: add the EU Health Data Space
(EHDS) Regulation under §1.3 "Relevant Law", and in §3 "Conformance
Recommendations" allow international base/core IGs (e.g., the
HL7-Europe EU base profiles at `https://build.fhir.org/ig/hl7-eu/base/`)
alongside US Core. FHIR-50740 should be considered jointly with
FHIR-50394 because they target the same IG sections and effectively
propose the same fix.

The pattern also has prior art in other work groups:
[FHIR-45883: Consider universal realm](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-45883)
(CQI, US Quality Measures) was resolved **Persuasive** and the IG was
moved to UV (new repo `HL7/fhir-cqm`), and
[FHIR-27422](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-27422) (CQI, US FHIR
Guidance — Quality Reporting) was resolved **Persuasive with
Modification** with the same "start international, then constrain for
US" guidance. Both precedents support taking this kind of feedback
seriously rather than declining it.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat FHIR-50740 as a duplicate-in-spirit of [FHIR-50394](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50394)
and accept both jointly: keep the PHR IG in the **Universal (UV)
realm** but make the IG live up to that scope. Concretely, in the next
ballot cycle:

1. **§1.3 "Relevant Law"** — broaden to a non-exhaustive,
   jurisdictionally-balanced list. Add the EU Health Data Space (EHDS)
   Regulation as a starting point (per FHIR-50394) and invite
   submissions for at least one Asia-Pacific and one Latin American
   regulatory framework so the IG is not left with US + EU only. Frame
   the section as "examples of regulatory drivers across realms" rather
   than as a US legal recital.
2. **§3 "Conformance Recommendations"** — replace the de-facto
   US-Core-only conformance assumption with realm-aware conformance:
   profiles MAY conform to US Core (US realm), HL7-Europe Base
   (`https://build.fhir.org/ig/hl7-eu/base/`, EU realm), or other
   recognized base IGs. Where the PHR IG itself constrains resources,
   factor those constraints so they layer cleanly over multiple base
   IGs rather than presupposing US Core invariants.
3. **Model derivation** — explicitly call out where the model is drawn
   from US Core / C-CDA on FHIR / Argonaut, and identify equivalents in
   non-US base IGs. Where the dependency on a US-only artifact is hard
   to avoid (e.g., a profile pinned to a US Core invariant), tag that
   spot in the IG narrative as US-specific guidance within an otherwise
   UV IG.
4. **Project scope** — open the work to international PE work-group
   participation (HL7 affiliates, HL7-Europe) before the next ballot
   so the broadened content is authored with non-US input rather than
   retrofitted by the existing US contributor base.

#### Justification

This honors the reporter's stated preference (the request opens with
"There should be more representation for the need from the
international community rather than being so US focused"). It is also
consistent with how the IG is already published — UV — so it avoids the
disruption of a realm change. FHIR-50394 has already given the work
group concrete, low-cost first edits (add EHDS, point at HL7-EU base),
which means Disposition A can show measurable progress in one ballot
cycle even if full international coverage takes longer. Precedent in
other work groups (FHIR-45883 / CQI, FHIR-27422 / CQI) shows HL7 has
accepted this category of feedback as **Persuasive** before.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — Re-realm the IG to US

#### Proposal

Take the second branch the reporter explicitly offered ("Otherwise make
this a US Realm FHIR IG.") and re-publish the Personal Health Record
FHIR IG as a **US-realm IG** at the next ballot. Concretely:

1. Change the realm in the IG's `ImplementationGuide` resource and
   `sushi-config.yaml` (or equivalent) from `uv` to `us`.
2. Move the canonical from `http://hl7.org/fhir/uv/phr` (or whichever
   UV path the IG currently occupies) to `http://hl7.org/fhir/us/phr`.
   Treat this as a breaking change — schedule it in a NIB for a full
   ballot cycle, not as ballot reconciliation, exactly as CQI did in
   FHIR-45883 (where the FMG explicitly required deferring the realm
   change to the next ballot because of the canonical URL impact).
3. Keep the existing US-Core / C-CDA-on-FHIR / Argonaut dependencies
   and the existing US-only "Relevant Law" section as-is, but reframe
   the IG narrative ("This IG profiles personal health record exchange
   in the United States.") so the US scope is no longer a hidden
   assumption.
4. Optionally publish a small UV-realm "PHR core" companion later, if
   demand emerges, that the US IG can derive from.

#### Justification

If the reality of the contributor base, the dependencies, and the
regulatory framing is US-centric, the most honest fix is to change the
label rather than dilute the content. This is mechanically simpler than
Disposition A — there is no need to recruit international contributors
or audit every profile against a non-US base IG before the next ballot
— and it lines up with how the IG is actually used today. The
trade-off is that this **is a breaking change** (canonical and IG code
change), per the FHIR-45883 / CQI experience, and it removes any
optionality for international adoption later without re-doing the
realm change in the opposite direction. It also leaves the reporter's
underlying concern — that international PHR needs are unmet — formally
unaddressed at HL7 level until someone files a UV-realm successor.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (Not Persuasive)

#### Proposal

Mark FHIR-50740 as **Not Persuasive** (or **Considered for Future
Use**) on the basis that: (i) the IG is published as UV but does not
*forbid* international use — it simply has US-centric examples and a
US-centric law section, which the work group considers acceptable for
the current STU; (ii) international affiliates are free to publish
realm-specific PHR IGs that derive from this one; and (iii) re-scoping
or re-realming requires resources the work group does not currently
have. Recommend the reporter (and reporter of FHIR-50394) bring forward
a concrete PR with the EU/EHDS additions and the HL7-EU base reference
if they want the change applied sooner.

#### Justification

A defensible-but-weak position. It is consistent with the IG being
"Triaged" rather than already-targeted for change, and it puts the
burden of broadening on the people asking for the change rather than
on a US-resourced work group. However, it is hard to square with HL7's
treatment of identical complaints in other work groups — both
FHIR-45883 (Persuasive) and FHIR-27422 (Persuasive with Modification,
filed by the same reporter as FHIR-50740) were accepted, and both were
ranked Highest priority, the same as this ticket. Declining therefore
risks looking inconsistent with HL7's own recent practice on realm
scoping.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **A — Accept As Requested**, jointly with
[FHIR-50394](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50394).

The two tickets are the same complaint at different levels of detail —
FHIR-50740 frames the principle ("be UV or be US, not both"),
FHIR-50394 hands the work group a starting edit list (EHDS in §1.3,
HL7-EU base in §3). Accepting them together gives a clear, low-risk
work plan that can land most of the change inside the next ballot
without forcing a canonical-URL break. Disposition B (re-realm to US)
is the honest alternative, but it is a breaking change that, by CQI's
own experience in FHIR-45883, costs a full ballot cycle and a repo
move; that cost is only worth paying if the work group has actively
decided it will not pursue international participation. Disposition C
(decline) is hard to defend given that the same reporter's earlier
identical complaint (FHIR-27422) was accepted by another work group as
Persuasive, and CQI's universal-realm change (FHIR-45883) was likewise
accepted — the workgroup should be ready to explain why PHR is
different if it goes that way.
