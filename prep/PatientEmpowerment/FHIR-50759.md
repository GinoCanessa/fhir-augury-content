## Summary

Virginia Lorenzi has filed two related concerns about the "record
lifecycle" narrative in the Personal Health Record (PHR) FHIR
Implementation Guide. First, the IG points readers at the
[Patient Data Receipt IG](https://open-health-manager.github.io/patient-data-receipt-ig/)
and refers to it as a "standard". That guide is vendor-owned (MITRE /
Open Health Manager) and is not an HL7-balloted standard, so the
reporter questions whether HL7 has the authority — or the editorial
judgement — to point at one vendor's IG and brand it a standard. She
recommends either removing the specific reference or generalising the
language to describe the *capability* needed (atomic update receipts)
without naming a particular IG.

Second, the same section asserts that "the EHR system acts as the
central 'master' repository, serving as the authoritative source of
truth for the patient's health data. Updates and changes originate from
the EHR." From a PHR perspective this framing is wrong: a typical
patient interacts with multiple provider EHRs, none of which is
"central" across the patient's full record. The PHR is the only thing
that aggregates across organisations, so the IG should re-frame the EHR
as authoritative *only* within its own organisational scope and
position the PHR (not the EHR) as the patient-centric aggregator.

No cross-referenced Jira tickets, GitHub items, or active Zulip threads
were surfaced for this ticket. A 2022 Zulip thread in
`patient empowerment > SPHR Track Proposal` mentions the Patient Data
Receipt work in passing (Abbie Watson noted the connectathon proposal
might add Patient Data Receipt components), but it predates this
ticket and is not a substantive discussion of the language under
review.

> Recommend providing language that recognizes that a single provider
> EHR is not central, the PHR is.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Edit the record-lifecycle narrative in the Personal Health Record
(FHIR) IG to:

1. **Remove the direct reference** to
   `https://open-health-manager.github.io/patient-data-receipt-ig/` and
   the word "standard" applied to it. Replace the sentence
   *"We envision these atomic updates as eventually including the
   Patient Data Receipt standard"* with a vendor-neutral capability
   statement, e.g.: *"We envision these atomic updates as eventually
   carrying a structured acknowledgement / receipt that records what
   the patient (or PHR) accepted, rejected, or amended. The exact
   receipt format is out of scope for this IG."*
2. **Re-word the EHR-as-master paragraph** so that the EHR is described
   as authoritative only within its own organisational scope, and the
   PHR is positioned as the patient's cross-organisation aggregator.
   Suggested replacement: *"Within a single provider organisation, the
   EHR is typically the authoritative source of truth for the
   encounters and clinical data captured there. A patient who receives
   care from multiple organisations will have data spread across
   multiple EHRs; in that case the PHR — not any one EHR — is the
   patient-centric aggregate record. Updates may therefore originate
   from any contributing EHR or from the patient via the PHR."*

#### Justification

Both edits are narrative-only and address concrete factual / editorial
problems flagged by the reporter. HL7 has a longstanding editorial
norm against branding a non-balloted, vendor-published IG as a
"standard", and against pointing at one vendor's IG from a normative
or informative HL7 publication without an explicit relationship
agreement. The EHR-as-master framing also contradicts the basic
premise of a PHR IG: if the EHR were authoritative across the
patient's whole record there would be no role for a PHR. Accepting
the change as requested keeps the IG editorially defensible and
aligns the lifecycle narrative with the IG's own scope.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Make the same two narrative corrections as Disposition A, **and**
additionally:

- Replace the removed Patient Data Receipt link with a *non-normative
  "Related Work" callout* listing the Patient Data Receipt IG (and any
  other comparable community efforts) as **examples** of receipt
  patterns the PHR community is watching, explicitly labelled as
  "non-HL7, vendor-published, informative only".
- Add a short subsection to the record-lifecycle page titled
  "Authority and aggregation" that states the EHR/PHR relationship
  rule once, and have other places in the IG link to it instead of
  re-stating it.

#### Justification

Disposition A removes a problematic citation, but doing so loses real
information: the Patient Data Receipt IG is the only known concrete
example of the receipt pattern the PHR IG is gesturing at. Burying
it in a clearly-labelled informative "Related Work" section preserves
the pointer for implementers while removing the misleading
"standard" branding and the implication of HL7 endorsement. The new
"Authority and aggregation" subsection also fixes the underlying
issue — that the EHR-master framing is repeated implicitly in
several places — rather than only patching the one paragraph the
reporter quoted.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Leave the record-lifecycle narrative as-is. Rationale category:
*editorial preference, not a defect.* Argument would be that the
existing language is forward-looking ("we envision … eventually") and
that readers understand "standard" colloquially.

#### Justification

This disposition is included for completeness but is hard to defend.
The reporter's two points are factually correct: the Patient Data
Receipt IG is not an HL7 standard, and a single EHR is not the
authoritative source of truth across a multi-provider patient's
record. Declining would leave the IG vulnerable to ballot comments
and to challenge from other vendors who could reasonably ask why
their IGs were not similarly cited.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B addresses both of the reporter's concerns while
preserving the genuinely useful pointer to prior art. The editorial
risk the reporter identifies is real — calling a vendor IG a
"standard" inside an HL7 publication is the kind of phrasing that
draws ballot comments — but simply deleting the link loses
implementation context that the PHR community will want. Putting the
reference into a clearly-labelled informative "Related Work" callout
removes the endorsement implication while keeping the link
discoverable. Folding the EHR/PHR authority correction into a small
named subsection (rather than only patching the one paragraph) also
prevents the same misframing from re-appearing elsewhere in the IG.
If Patient Empowerment prefers a smaller, lower-risk change, falling
back to Disposition A is fully acceptable; both A and B are clearly
preferable to C.
