## Summary

The reporter questions why the **Personal Health Record (PHR) FHIR
Implementation Guide** contains an "Operating Systems" page. They do not
understand the rationale for including operating-system-specific
implementation guidance in a FHIR IG, nor do they see how the content of
that page is connected to FHIR implementation. The ticket effectively
asks the work group to either justify and rework the page so its
relevance to FHIR is clear, or remove it from the IG. No related Jira
tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were surfaced by the
cross-reference index for this ticket, so the disposition discussion
rests on the ticket itself plus the comment thread between the reporter
and the IG editor.

## Details

> I don't understand why this page is in the IG. Why is important to

>

## Proposed Dispositions

The dispositions below are necessarily framed at the IG-content level
rather than at a specific repo path, because no repo briefing is
available for the Personal Health Record IG (see Repo Context). Once
the PHR IG repo is registered, each disposition would translate into a
concrete edit to the page's source markdown (or its inclusion in the
IG's `ImplementationGuide` resource / `sushi-config.yaml` page list).

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Remove the "Operating Systems" page from the Personal Health Record
FHIR IG. Concretely:

- Delete the page's source file from the IG's `input/pagecontent/`
  authoring root (the standard HL7 IG layout used by IG Publisher and
  SUSHI).
- Remove the page entry from the IG's page list — either from the
  `pages` block in `sushi-config.yaml` or from the `definition.page`
  tree of the `ImplementationGuide` resource — so it no longer appears
  in the table of contents.
- Remove or redirect any internal links from other pages that point at
  the Operating Systems page; if some narrowly-scoped tip (e.g. "where
  to find an exported file on common operating systems") is judged
  worth keeping, fold a short paragraph into the existing
  patient-facing "getting started" / export-handling page instead of
  keeping a standalone OS page.

#### Justification

This matches both the reporter's concern and the IG editor's most
recent comment ("We don't need to dive into OS in the guide; very
difficult to maintain in future"). FHIR IGs are intended to constrain
and document FHIR artefacts and their use; OS-level instructions are
not FHIR conformance content and create an ongoing maintenance burden
that is disproportionate to their value (OS UIs change frequently,
links rot, and there is no standards body process keeping the page
honest). The editor has already characterised the page as a "hodge-podge
and scrap yard of notes," which is a strong signal that the cost of
bringing it up to IG quality outweighs the benefit. Removing it
sharpens the IG's scope without losing FHIR-specific guidance.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the page in the IG but rescope and rewrite it as a clearly
labelled, non-normative "For patients and patient advocates: getting
started with your exported records" page. Specifically:

- Rename the page from "Operating Systems" to something audience-led
  (e.g. `getting-started-with-your-export.md`).
- Add an explicit non-normative banner at the top stating the page is
  informational guidance for non-developer readers and is not part of
  the IG's conformance requirements.
- Trim OS-specific content to only what is necessary to help a
  non-technical user locate, open, and inspect a FHIR export
  (Bundle/NDJSON/JSON file) on their own machine, and link out to
  vendor documentation rather than duplicating it.
- Cross-link the page from the IG home and from any
  export/download-related pages so the audience that needs it can find
  it.

#### Justification

This addresses the underlying tension surfaced in the editor's January
comment — that the IG has two distinct audiences (professional
implementers and patients/advocates) and that some hand-holding content
is genuinely useful for the latter. Renaming and rescoping the page
makes its purpose obvious (which is exactly what the reporter says is
missing) and constrains the maintenance surface to the small slice of
OS guidance that is actually load-bearing for using a FHIR export.
Non-normative framing is consistent with how other HL7 IGs handle
patient-facing or implementer-onboarding narrative content, and it
avoids implying that OS choice is a FHIR conformance concern. The
trade-off vs. Disposition A is ongoing maintenance, but bounded:
linking out to vendor docs instead of restating them keeps the page
small.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (Keep As-Is)

#### Proposal

Decline the request and leave the Operating Systems page unchanged.
Rationale category: *no consensus to change yet — defer pending the
work group's audience decision.*

#### Justification

The editor's January comment explicitly committed to a work-group
discussion and vote on the IG's intended audience before deciding the
fate of this page. Acting on FHIR-49262 in isolation — either by
deleting the page (Disposition A) or by rewriting it (Disposition B) —
prejudges that broader audience decision. Declining now would keep the
page in place until the audience question is resolved, after which a
follow-up ticket can implement the agreed direction. This is the
weakest of the three options because it leaves a page the editor
already calls a "hodge-podge" in front of readers, and because the
editor's later (August) comment has effectively answered the audience
question in favour of *not* covering OS material.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter's complaint and the IG editor's most recent comment
converge on the same conclusion: OS-level instructions are not FHIR
conformance content, the existing page is admittedly a scrap yard of
notes, and the maintenance cost of keeping it accurate is high.
Disposition B is attractive in principle — the audience tension it
addresses is real — but it imports a non-trivial editorial commitment
(a sustained, well-curated patient-facing onboarding section) that the
work group has not yet signalled it has the bandwidth to maintain.
Disposition C defers a decision the editor has effectively already
made. Removing the page now (A) sharpens the IG's scope, aligns with
the editor's August direction, and leaves the door open to introducing
a properly scoped, non-normative patient-onboarding page later as a
deliberate piece of work rather than as a salvage of the current
content. The work group should still hold its audience-scope vote, but
it can do so without this page in the IG.
