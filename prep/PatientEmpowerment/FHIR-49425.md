## Summary

This Change Request, submitted by LizTuri on behalf of Matt Elrod, asks the
Patient Empowerment work group to update the Personal Health Record (FHIR) IG
to reflect the consolidation of two PACIO implementation guides. The IG
currently references **"PACIO - Cognitive Status"** and **"PACIO - Functional
Status"** as related/companion guides; both are being archived by the PACIO
project and superseded by the unified **"PACIO - Personal Functioning and
Engagement (PFE)"** IG (`https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/fhir-pacio-pfe/`). The
request is to replace the two outdated references with a single reference to
the new PFE IG so PHR implementers are not pointed at deprecated guidance.

The resolution recorded on the ticket (Persuasive with Modification, dated
2025-03-13) indicates the editor accepted the substitution, fixed a typo,
clarified the listing as non-exhaustive ("These implementation guides may
provide information on how to obtain information from EHR and payor systems
and bring them into a PHR. Note that it is not an inclusive list. Implementers
are encouraged to research adoption on each guide."), and additionally updated
a separate reference to "CH AllergyIntolerance". No cross-referenced Jira
tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were surfaced for this change.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the Personal Health Record (FHIR) IG, in the section that lists related /
companion implementation guides, remove the two entries:

- "PACIO - Cognitive Status"
- "PACIO - Functional Status"

and replace them with a single entry:

- "PACIO - Personal Functioning and Engagement"
  (`https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/fhir-pacio-pfe/`)

This is exactly what the submitter requested and matches what the ticket's
recorded resolution already implements (Persuasive with Modification, with the
addition of a clarifying lead-in sentence noting the list is non-exhaustive
and a typo fix; the unrelated "CH AllergyIntolerance" rename appears to have
been folded in opportunistically and would be tracked separately if needed).

#### Justification

The change is purely an editorial / link-hygiene update to narrative content,
with no impact on conformance resources, profiles, value sets, capability
statements, or examples. PACIO has stated the Cognitive Status and Functional
Status IGs are being archived in favour of PFE, so leaving the old references
in place would point PHR implementers at deprecated guidance and risk them
building against IGs that will not receive further updates. Adding the
non-exhaustive disclaimer also matches standard FHIR IG practice for
"see-also" lists and protects the PHR IG from being read as endorsing a
closed set of companion guides. The submitter is the PACIO contact for the
superseding IG, so the source of the request is authoritative.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the substitution as in Disposition A, but additionally:

1. Retain a brief historical note next to the new PFE entry, e.g.,
   *"(supersedes the archived PACIO Cognitive Status and PACIO Functional
   Status IGs)"*, so readers arriving from older publications, slide decks,
   or downstream IGs that still cite the prior PACIO guides have a clear
   bridge to the replacement.
2. Where the IG already deep-links to specific pages in the old Cognitive /
   Functional Status IGs (if any such links exist outside the companion-IG
   list — search the rendered IG and the source narrative for "PACIO" before
   publishing), update or remove those deep links in the same change so the
   IG is internally consistent.
3. Pin the PFE reference to the current published (or build) version
   explicitly rather than only the canonical landing page, so the citation
   remains stable as PFE iterates.

#### Justification

A bare substitution (Disposition A) is correct but loses the discoverability
trail for implementers and tooling that already reference the old PACIO IGs
by name. A short supersedes note is a small narrative cost that materially
improves continuity, and sweeping for any other PACIO mentions in the same
edit avoids a follow-up ticket. Pinning the link reduces the risk of the
referenced URL drifting out from under the PHR IG between PFE rebuilds.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Do not change the IG narrative; leave the existing references to "PACIO -
Cognitive Status" and "PACIO - Functional Status" in place.

#### Justification

This is included only for completeness; it is not defensible here. The
submitter (a PACIO representative) has stated the referenced IGs are being
archived, the proposed replacement IG exists at a stable HL7 canonical, the
change is purely editorial (no normative or conformance impact), and the
ticket has already been resolved Persuasive with Modification. Declining
would knowingly leave the PHR IG pointing at deprecated guidance.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The substitution itself is uncontroversial and effectively already accepted
(the ticket carries a recorded resolution of Persuasive with Modification
that performs the swap and adds a non-exhaustive-list disclaimer). The small
extension proposed in Disposition B — keeping a one-line "supersedes …" bridge
next to the new PFE entry, sweeping the rest of the narrative for any
remaining deep links to the archived PACIO IGs, and pinning the PFE citation
— costs the editor very little and prevents both broken-deep-link and
"where did Cognitive/Functional Status go?" follow-ups from PHR implementers
who learned the IG under its previous name. If the work group prefers to
keep the change strictly minimal, Disposition A is a fine fallback and aligns
exactly with what the ticket already records as resolved.
