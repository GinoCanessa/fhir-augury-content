## Summary

The reporter flagged a one-word typo in narrative text in the Personal Health
Record (PHR) FHIR specification. A sentence currently reads "**If** would be
good to have a consistent workflow around IPS handling whether the document is
held directly by the patient or not." The opening word "If" should be "It"
(i.e., "**It** would be good to have a consistent workflow…"). The change is
purely editorial — no normative content, no schema, no conformance impact.
There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items for
this ticket, so context is limited to the ticket itself.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Replace the leading word "If" with "It" in the narrative sentence in the PHR
(FHIR) specification page that currently reads:

> "If would be good to have a consistent workflow around IPS handling whether
> the document is held directly by the patient or not."

so that it becomes:

> "It would be good to have a consistent workflow around IPS handling whether
> the document is held directly by the patient or not."

This is a one-character source edit in the PHR IG narrative (the page
discussing IPS / patient-held document workflow). No StructureDefinition,
ValueSet, examples, or generated artifacts are touched.

#### Justification

The reporter is correct: "If would be good to have…" is grammatically
incorrect and reads as a sentence fragment, while "It would be good to have…"
is the intended idiomatic phrasing. The change is editorial, has zero
normative or conformance impact, is backward-compatible by definition, and is
already accepted by the work group (resolution = *Persuasive*; the assigned
editor commented "Will fix"). Accepting as requested is consistent with how
HL7 routinely handles narrative typos via the *Technical Correction* type.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the typo fix, and at the same time tighten the surrounding sentence so
the narrative reads more clearly — for example:

> "A consistent workflow around IPS handling — whether the document is held
> directly by the patient or not — would be helpful."

This still resolves the reporter's grammatical concern but improves the
sentence rather than only swapping one letter.

#### Justification

The reporter's underlying issue is that the sentence does not parse cleanly.
Re-flowing the sentence addresses that root cause and avoids leaving an
awkward "It would be good to…" construction in normative-adjacent narrative.
The trade-off is that any rewording beyond the literal typo expands the scope
of a *Technical Correction* into editorial style, which the work group may
prefer to handle separately to keep the change set minimal and reviewable.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as *Not Persuasive* on the grounds that the sentence is
informative narrative whose meaning is recoverable in context, and the cost
of opening a publication-affecting change for a single character is not
warranted.

#### Justification

This rationale is weak in this case: the IG can be republished cheaply, the
fix is unambiguous, and HL7 explicitly supports the *Technical Correction*
issue type for exactly this kind of editorial fix. Declining would also
contradict the assigned editor's existing "Will fix" commitment. Disposition C
is included only for completeness; it is not defensible here.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

This is a single-character editorial fix in PHR (FHIR) narrative, already
acknowledged by the editor with "Will fix" and pre-marked *Persuasive*. There
is no community discussion, no normative impact, and no implementer burden.
Disposition A delivers exactly what the reporter asked for at minimum risk;
Disposition B is reasonable but expands the scope beyond a *Technical
Correction* and should be handled as a separate editorial pass if the work
group wants broader sentence cleanup. Disposition C is not justifiable.
