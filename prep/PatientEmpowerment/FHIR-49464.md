## Summary

Rebecca Baker reports that the diagram in Section 1.2 ("What is a Personal Health Record") of the Personal Health Record (PHR) FHIR IG mis-represents the International Patient Summary (IPS) — the figure shows IPS as containing only "Immunizations", whereas the IPS Composition actually encompasses Conditions, Medications, Imaging, Laboratory results, and other sections (see Figure 2 of the IPS IG). The reporter further suggests that the PHR specification should align with both the FHIR IPS IG (`hl7.org/fhir/uv/ips`) and the FHIR International Patient Access (IPA) IG (`hl7.org/fhir/uv/ipa`), and recommends engaging HL7 International (specifically HL7 Europe) to bring an international perspective to the PHR work — citing global travel as a use case where alignment across these closely-related specifications would benefit patients worldwide.

The Augury cross-reference index returned no related Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items for `FHIR-49464`, so the request currently stands on its own.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Replace or update the diagram in Section 1.2 ("What is a Personal Health Record") of the Personal Health Record (FHIR) IG so that the IPS box accurately reflects the full IPS Composition rather than only "Immunizations". Concretely:

1. Re-author the figure (and its source — typically a PlantUML/SVG/PNG asset under the IG's `input/images/` or equivalent) to list the IPS sections per `http://hl7.org/fhir/uv/ips/Composition-IPS.html` Figure 2 — including Allergies & Intolerances, Medication Summary, Problems (Conditions), Immunizations, Results (Laboratory), and the optional sections (Vital Signs, Past History of Procedures, Medical Devices, Diagnostic Imaging, etc.), or reference the IPS Composition by name with a representative subset.
2. Add a narrative paragraph immediately under the figure that:
   - Notes that the PHR aligns conceptually with the IPS IG (`http://hl7.org/fhir/uv/ips/`) for the cross-border / travel summary use case, and with the IPA IG (`http://hl7.org/fhir/uv/ipa/`) for patient-mediated API access.
   - References both IGs by canonical URL.
3. Add the PHR project as a standing item on the Patient Empowerment ↔ HL7 Europe / Patient Care liaison agenda so the international perspective requested by the reporter is captured during the next ballot cycle, and record the engagement in the IG's introduction or governance page.

#### Justification

The reporter's factual claim is correct: the IPS Composition is broad (per the IPS IG and the EU eHN Patient Summary guideline), and depicting it as "Immunizations only" is misleading to readers comparing PHR scope against existing international patient-summary standards. Fixing the diagram is a low-risk, narrative/figure-only change that does not alter any conformance artefact, profile, value set, or operation in the PHR IG; it improves clarity for the same audience the section is written for. Aligning narrative references to IPS and IPA is consistent with HL7's general direction of cross-referencing related international IGs rather than re-defining them, and explicit liaison with HL7 Europe is the established mechanism for picking up the international perspective the reporter asks for.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the figure correction (item 1 of Disposition A) but **do not** commit the PHR IG to formal alignment with IPS / IPA in this ticket. Instead:

1. Update the Section 1.2 diagram so the IPS box correctly reflects the IPS Composition.
2. Replace the requested "should align with IPS and IPA" narrative with a softer "Related specifications" subsection that links to IPS, IPA, and (where relevant) HL7 Europe Base/Core, without asserting structural alignment or conformance dependency.
3. Open a separate, larger-scope ticket (or a Patient Empowerment work item) titled something like "Define PHR ↔ IPS / IPA alignment strategy" that the WG can scope, prioritise, and — if pursued — co-ordinate with HL7 Europe and Patient Care via the normal liaison process. That separate ticket would be the right place to decide whether PHR profiles should derive from / reference IPS profiles, whether the PHR data-access pattern should be IPA-conformant, etc.

#### Justification

The figure error and the alignment-with-IPS/IPA recommendation are two very different change classes. The figure is editorial; the alignment recommendation is potentially structural (it can pull profile, value-set, and conformance decisions into scope and obligates cross-WG/cross-affiliate coordination). Bundling them risks either (a) over-promising alignment in narrative without backing it up in the actual artefacts, or (b) blocking the easy fix on a much harder design discussion. Splitting also gives HL7 Europe and the Patient Care WG a clean entry point to engage on the substantive question rather than reacting to a one-line narrative change. This keeps the PHR ballot cycle predictable while still honouring both halves of the reporter's request.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the ticket as written, on the basis that the Section 1.2 figure is intentionally illustrative (not normative or exhaustive), and that PHR alignment with IPS / IPA is an architectural decision out of scope for an introductory figure. Close as "Not Persuasive — illustrative figure, see IPS IG for authoritative IPS Composition; PHR / IPS / IPA relationship to be addressed separately if and when the WG prioritises it."

#### Justification

A defensible — but in our view weak — basis for declining is that introductory diagrams in IGs are commonly stylised and that any reader who needs the authoritative IPS scope is expected to follow the link to the IPS IG. Declining also avoids drawing the WG into a multi-affiliate alignment conversation it has not yet scoped. The downside is that the reporter has identified a genuinely misleading visual (depicting IPS as "Immunizations only" is not stylisation, it is incorrect), so this disposition leaves a known defect in the published narrative and signals to the international community that the WG is not interested in alignment.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B captures everything that is clearly correct in the report — the figure is wrong and should be fixed in this ticket — while keeping the much larger "PHR should align with IPS and IPA, with HL7 Europe involvement" question on a track where it can be properly scoped, resourced, and co-ordinated with the IPS, IPA, and HL7 Europe communities. Disposition A is tempting because it answers the reporter in full, but committing the IG narrative to "alignment" without an accompanying technical work item risks creating an expectation the PHR profiles do not yet meet. Disposition C ignores a real defect and is hard to defend to an international reviewer. Splitting the ticket — fix the figure now, open a follow-up for alignment strategy — is the cleanest way to give the reporter a substantive response in the current ballot cycle while protecting the WG's ability to make the alignment decision deliberately.
