## Summary

Elliot Silver reports that the narrative discussion of **required bindings** in
the FHIR Core terminologies page only addresses `CodeableConcept` and `code`,
and does not directly cover `Coding` (it appears only as a child of
`CodeableConcept`). He asks that the rules for a required binding on a `Coding`
element be stated explicitly. He also flags two related ambiguities in the same
section:

1. The sentence *"If a required binding is applied to an element with maximum
   cardinality > 1, the binding applies to all the elements"* is unclear —
   readers cannot tell whether it is scoped to `CodeableConcept` only or to
   `code`/`Coding` as well, and whether it applies to the repeated `Coding`
   elements *within* a `CodeableConcept`. He suggests rewording to
   *"If a required binding is applied to an element of type CodeableConcept
   with maximum cardinality greater than 1, the binding applies to each
   repetition of the element."* and notes that similar text in other binding
   strengths should be reviewed and updated.
2. The phrase *"One of the Coding values SHALL be from the specified value
   set"* does not say whether `code` and `system` must both be supplied (or
   whether `code` alone suffices), nor when `version` must also be present.
   The same ambiguity exists for the other binding strengths.

A follow-up comment adds that the section should also state explicitly that a
required binding **cannot be satisfied with `CodeableConcept.text` alone**
(text-only, no coding).

The TI tracker call on 2026-03-23 agreed the section needs improvement and
proposed using the **extensible binding section** (which breaks the rules out
per datatype with specifics) as the template for rewriting the **required
binding** section. The agreed resolution is **Persuasive** with the plan to
post the proposed rewrite to Zulip and vote on a future TI call.

No Jira cross-references were returned for this ticket.

## Details

**Description:**

The discussion of required bindings discusses CodeableConcept and code, but
omits Coding (except as part of CodeableConcept). The rules for required
binding on Coding should be included.

===

The following statement is unclear as to whether it is scoped to only
CodeableConcepts or to code/Coding as well, and whether this applies to the
repeated Coding elements within a CodeableConcept:

> If a required binding is applied to an element with maximum cardinality > 1,
> the binding applies to all the elements.

(Also, I dislike use of "> 1" in the midst of narrative.)

Suggest changing to *"If a required binding is applied to an element of type
CodeableConcept with maximum cardinality greater than 1, the binding applies
to each repetition of the element."*

Similar text occurs in other bindings, review and update if necessary.

===

> One of the Coding values SHALL be from the specified value set

It is unclear whether `code` and `system` must both be specified in order for
the value to meet this requirement; is supplying only `code` sufficient? Are
there circumstances when a `version` must also be specified?

This is also unclear for other binding strengths.

**Resolution Description (from snapshot):**

> Replace / enhance the content in the required binding section with something
> similar to what is in the extensible binding section where each datatype is
> a separate entry and there are specifics provided. TI tracker call attendees
> agree that the information in this section could be improved.

## Related Zulip Discussions

### [committers > Required binding with CodeableConcept](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/committers/topic/Required%20binding%20with%20CodeableConcept)

Initiated 2025-09-03 (about a week after the ticket was filed) and continuing
into 2025-09-04, this thread is the public-discussion counterpart to
FHIR-51935. John Moehrke opens by questioning why a `CodeableConcept` is the
target of a required binding at all when `Coding` would suffice, and notes
that `CodeableConcept.text` without any `coding` appears to undermine the
intent of a required binding. Elliot Silver explains the design rationale —
`CodeableConcept` allows alternate code systems that express the same idea or
the same idea at different granularities, while `Extensible` is for *different*
ideas — and confirms that with a required binding only **one** member of the
`coding` collection needs to come from the bound value set, and that
text-only (no `coding`) is not allowed. Lloyd McKenzie and John Moehrke then
debate whether adding new codes to a required binding constitutes a breaking
change and what HL7's compatibility promise actually is, with Lloyd noting that
HL7 promises old instances remain valid rather than no breaking changes per se,
and that "Other"/"Unknown" should be included in required value sets when the
space might not be fully covered. Gino Canessa and Elliot Silver branch into a
side discussion of "fixed bindings" and immutable value sets (referencing
`FHIR-52328`). The conversation does not reach formal consensus on rewording
but converges on the substantive points the ticket asks the spec to make
explicit: required binding semantics for `Coding` vs. `CodeableConcept`,
behaviour on repeating elements, the role of `text`, and the
`code`/`system`/`version` requirements.

Participants: Elliot Silver, John Moehrke, Lloyd McKenzie, Gino Canessa.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Restructure the **Required binding** subsection of the FHIR Core
`terminologies` narrative page so that, like the Extensible binding
subsection, it has an explicit per-datatype entry and resolves the three
ambiguities raised in the ticket:

- **Per-datatype rules.** Add explicit, separately-titled paragraphs for
  required bindings applied to:
  - `code` (primitive): the element value SHALL be one of the codes in the
    bound value set.
  - `Coding`: `Coding.code` SHALL be present and SHALL be one of the codes
    in the bound value set; `Coding.system` SHALL be present and SHALL be
    the system from which that code is drawn (and, where the bound value set
    is version-specific, `Coding.version` SHALL match accordingly).
  - `CodeableConcept`: at least one entry in `CodeableConcept.coding` SHALL
    satisfy the `Coding` rule above; `CodeableConcept.text` alone does NOT
    satisfy a required binding (state this explicitly, per esilver's
    2025-08-26 comment); additional `coding` entries beyond the satisfying
    one are unconstrained by the binding.
- **Repeating-element clarification.** Replace *"If a required binding is
  applied to an element with maximum cardinality > 1, the binding applies
  to all the elements"* with *"If a required binding is applied to a
  repeating element (maximum cardinality greater than 1), the binding
  applies to each repetition of the element."* and state separately that
  *within* a single `CodeableConcept`, only one repetition of `coding` need
  satisfy the binding. Avoid the "> 1" mathematical shorthand inside
  narrative, per the reporter's note.
- **system/version requirement.** Replace *"One of the Coding values SHALL
  be from the specified value set"* with explicit text that names
  `Coding.system` and `Coding.code` as both required, and describes when
  `Coding.version` is required (i.e., only when the bound value set is
  itself version-pinned).
- **Sweep other binding strengths.** Apply the same per-datatype, repeating-
  element, and `system`/`version` clarifications to the **Extensible**,
  **Preferred**, and **Example** subsections wherever the same ambiguous
  language appears. (TEXTONLY change; no schema or StructureDefinition impact.)

#### Justification

The change is squarely in the Terminology Infrastructure WG's remit, the
ticket has been resolved **Persuasive** by TI, and the 2026-03-23 tracker
call has already endorsed using the Extensible section as the template. The
substantive answers to all three ambiguities are well-attested in long-
standing community practice (see the 2016 *translations > coded values*
note from Grahame Grieve, the 2021 *Data-absent-reason* thread, and the
2025-09-03 *Required binding with CodeableConcept* thread), so the change is
a genuine clarification rather than a behavioural shift. Labelling the
ticket TEXTONLY accurately reflects scope: no element definitions, value
sets, validators, or generated artefacts need to change — only narrative
in the terminologies page. Doing the sweep across all binding strengths in
the same edit prevents the same ambiguity from being re-litigated against
extensible/preferred bindings later.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt the substantive clarifications from Disposition A, **but** package
them differently to limit blast radius and preserve normative wording:

- Keep the existing high-level paragraph in the Required binding subsection
  largely intact, and add a new sub-table titled *"Required binding by
  datatype"* with three rows (`code`, `Coding`, `CodeableConcept`) giving
  the per-datatype rule, plus a footnoted column for the
  repeating-element behaviour. The `CodeableConcept` row explicitly states
  that `text`-only does not satisfy the binding, and the `Coding` row
  explicitly states the `system`/`code`/`version` requirement.
- Defer the analogous rewrite of the Extensible / Preferred / Example
  subsections to follow-up tickets, on the basis that the Extensible
  section is already datatype-specific and the Preferred / Example
  sections have weaker normative force.
- Replace the *"> 1"* phrasing in narrative wherever it appears.

#### Justification

A tabular form is easier for implementers to skim than three new prose
paragraphs and is less likely to perturb the surrounding normative text or
disturb cross-references from other pages and IGs that link into the
Required binding subsection by anchor. It also de-risks the change for
ballot reconciliation, since the existing wording is preserved and the new
material is clearly additive. The trade-off versus Disposition A is that
similar ambiguities elsewhere on the page are not addressed in this pass —
acceptable given the ticket explicitly only *suggests* (not requires) the
sweep, and TI can take that up via separate tickets if needed.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Not Persuasive – No Change Required**, on the basis
that:

- The behaviour the reporter is asking the spec to state explicitly
  (required binding applies to `code`, `Coding`, and at least one
  `CodeableConcept.coding`; `text` alone does not satisfy; `code` and
  `system` must both be present; the binding applies per repetition of a
  repeating element) is already implied by the existing narrative read in
  conjunction with the datatype definitions for `Coding` and
  `CodeableConcept`, and is enforced by the published validators.
- The TEXTONLY label means there is no implementer-visible behaviour change
  at stake.

#### Justification

(Defensible only on stylistic / editorial-budget grounds.) If the WG were
constrained on editorial bandwidth and judged the existing wording
"defensible if read carefully", they could leave it. The 2024-02-29 and
2025-09-03 Zulip threads, however, demonstrate the wording recurringly
confuses experienced committers, which substantially weakens this position.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is already resolved Persuasive and the TI tracker call has
explicitly endorsed the Extensible-section template for the rewrite, so
Disposition A is closest to the WG's stated direction. Disposition B's
table-based variant is appealing for clarity but would leave the analogous
Extensible / Preferred / Example wording un-swept and the reporter has
specifically called out that *"similar text occurs in other bindings,
review and update if necessary"* — doing the sweep in one TEXTONLY pass is
cheaper than three follow-up tickets and avoids a future editor having to
re-derive consensus on the same questions. Disposition C is hard to
justify in light of repeated committer confusion (2016, 2021, 2024,
2025-09) about exactly the points the ticket raises. The recommended
single change is to rewrite the Required binding subsection to be
datatype-structured with the three explicit clarifications above, then to
make the same `system`/`code`, repeating-element, and `text`-only
statements consistently in the Extensible, Preferred, and Example
subsections.
