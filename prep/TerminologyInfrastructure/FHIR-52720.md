## Summary

The reporter (Carmela A. Couderc, on behalf of Terminology Infrastructure)
asks the Canonical Resource Management Infrastructure (CRMI) IG authors to
review and justify the FHIR Maturity Model (FMM) levels assigned to the
terminology-related profiles in CRMI. The concern is that these CRMI profile
FMM levels appear high relative to the FMM levels of the base resources they
profile in R4B and R5 — for example, `ShareableCodeSystem` is FMM 0 in R5 but
FMM 4 in CRMI, and `PublishableCodeSystem` jumped from 0 to 3. The ticket
asks the IG authors to discuss the levels with TI and adjust them as needed,
attaching a table of the affected profiles. CRMI authors (Bryn Rhodes)
responded that base-spec FMM was not tracked for profiles, and that the
Shareable/Publishable profiles have lived in CRMI since STU1 (and in the
base spec since DSTU2/STU3 for ShareableValueSet/ShareableCodeSystem), so the
levels reflect real connectathon and production use. After review by Marc
Duteau and discussion at the 2026-01-12 TI tracker call (and on the joint
CQI/CDS WGM agenda), the ticket was resolved **Not Persuasive** with the
ballot-proposed levels (ShareableValueSet 4, ShareableCodeSystem 4,
ShareableConceptMap 1, ShareableNamingSystem 1, ShareableTerminologyCapabilities 2,
PublishableCodeSystem 3, PublishableConceptMap 1, PublishableNamingSystem 1,
PublishableTerminologyCapabilities 2, PublishableValueSet 3,
ComputableValueSet 3, ExpandedValueSet 3) accepted as-is.

## Details

**Description:**
Maturity levels for the terminology profiles are not aligned with R5 or R4B
levels. For example - ShareableCodeSystem is FMM level 0 in R5, but 4 in this
IG. PublishableCodeSystem jumped from 0 to 3. See attached table and adjust
FMM levels after discussion with TI.

**Comments:**

- **bryn.rhodes (2025-10-22):** The FMM levels are 0 in R4/R4B/R5 because
  FMM was not tracked or published for profiles in the base specification.
  ShareableValueSet has been in the base spec since DSTU2 and
  ShareableCodeSystem since STU3. The Shareable and Publishable profiles for
  the other terminology resources have been in CRMI since STU1, so the
  balloted maturity levels reflect actual connectathon and production use.
- **bryn.rhodes (2025-10-22):** The CRMI authors believe the profile levels
  are set appropriately and are seeking review/approval from TI to continue
  as proposed.
- **carmela_couderc (2026-01-12):** Discussed at the 2026-01-12 TI tracker
  call; this ticket and TI-owned artifacts in general were added to the
  joint CQI/CDS agenda during the January WGM.
- **carmela_couderc (2026-02-23):** Notes this should not be included in a
  block vote because it carries an in-person request; suggests adding it to
  a main TI workgroup call agenda.
- **marc.duteau (2026-03-09):** Reviewed FMM levels against the FHIR
  Maturity Model Confluence page (noting that artifacts merely moved,
  renamed, or refactored do not have maturity reset unless FMG agrees the
  change is significant). FMM 1 is the floor; the two FMM 2 profiles meet
  the testing requirement but lack evidence of broad review; the four FMM 3
  profiles look defensible but are not quite at FMM 4; ShareableCodeSystem
  and ShareableValueSet are clearly at least FMM 4. Overall the proposed
  levels make sense, especially since the Shareable CodeSystem/ValueSet
  profiles should not change in non-backward-compatible ways without
  implementer consultation.

**Resolution Description:** TI agrees the maturity levels as proposed in the
ballot are appropriate (final table, see Summary above). Disposition: Not
Persuasive.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Lower the FMM levels of the CRMI terminology profiles so they no longer
exceed the FMM levels of the corresponding base resources (and/or of those
resources in R4B/R5). Concretely, drop `ShareableCodeSystem`,
`ShareableValueSet`, `PublishableCodeSystem`, `PublishableValueSet`,
`ComputableValueSet`, and `ExpandedValueSet` to FMM 0–1 (matching the
"profiles in the base spec are FMM 0" baseline cited in the ticket), and
re-evaluate `ShareableTerminologyCapabilities` and
`PublishableTerminologyCapabilities` (currently 2) downward as well. The
edits would be made in the CRMI IG repo (`HL7/crmi-ig`) on each profile's
`StructureDefinition` (the `fhir-version`/`fmm` extension on the SD) and on
the corresponding artifact intro/notes file that renders the maturity
banner.

#### Justification

This is the literal request: align CRMI profile FMM with the FMM of the
underlying base resources/profiles in R4B and R5. It removes the
inconsistency that prompted the ticket (e.g., a profile at FMM 4 of a base
resource published at FMM 0) and avoids signalling more stability than the
rest of the FHIR ecosystem grants the same artifacts.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the CRMI profile FMM values at the levels proposed in the ballot but
publish an explicit FMM-justification note on each affected
`StructureDefinition` (and in the IG's maturity narrative page). The note
would cite (a) that base-spec profiles historically did not have FMM tracked
or published, (b) the DSTU2/STU3 lineage of `ShareableValueSet` and
`ShareableCodeSystem`, and (c) the connectathon and production usage record
that the CRMI authors invoked in their comments. Optionally, drop only the
two FMM 2 profiles (`ShareableTerminologyCapabilities`,
`PublishableTerminologyCapabilities`) to FMM 1 because Marc Duteau noted
they meet testing but lack evidence of broad review, and leave the
ShareableCodeSystem/ShareableValueSet at FMM 4 and the FMM 3 cluster as-is.

#### Justification

This addresses the underlying concern — that the levels look unjustified —
without overriding the IG authors' direct knowledge of how widely these
profiles are used. It also matches Marc Duteau's review almost verbatim
(FMM 1 floor; FMM 2 group lacks broad-review evidence; FMM 3 group
defensible; Shareable CodeSystem/ValueSet clearly ≥ FMM 4) and respects the
FMG rule that maturity is not reset for moved/renamed/refactored artifacts.
It is also less disruptive to downstream IGs that already depend on these
profiles being marked stable.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (Not Persuasive)

#### Proposal

Resolve the ticket "Not Persuasive" and retain the ballot-proposed FMM
table exactly: ShareableValueSet 4, ShareableCodeSystem 4,
ShareableConceptMap 1, ShareableNamingSystem 1,
ShareableTerminologyCapabilities 2, PublishableCodeSystem 3,
PublishableConceptMap 1, PublishableNamingSystem 1,
PublishableTerminologyCapabilities 2, PublishableValueSet 3,
ComputableValueSet 3, ExpandedValueSet 3. No changes to the CRMI IG
artifacts. Record the rationale (base-spec FMM not tracked for profiles,
DSTU2/STU3 lineage, CRMI usage since STU1, connectathon/production
evidence) in the resolution notes so that the question is documented for
future reviewers.

#### Justification

The premise of the ticket — that the CRMI levels are "high without
justification" — is rebutted by the IG authors and confirmed by an
independent review. Base-spec FMM 0 is an artefact of not tracking FMM on
profiles in the base specification, not a statement that the artefacts are
immature. The Shareable/Publishable profiles have shipped in CRMI since
STU1 and have demonstrable connectathon/production use, which is exactly
what FMM 3–4 require. Lowering FMM would mislead implementers and would
violate the FMG rule that maturity is not reset without a significant
reason.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** C

The ticket's substantive concern is answered by the IG authors' explanation
that the base-spec comparison is an apples-to-oranges artefact (profiles in
R4B/R5 simply did not carry FMM) and by Marc Duteau's independent review,
which concluded that the proposed levels make sense and that
ShareableCodeSystem / ShareableValueSet are clearly at least FMM 4. The TI
tracker call and the CRMI authors converged on the same outcome, which is
why the ticket already carries a "Not Persuasive" resolution with the
ballot table accepted. Disposition B is a reasonable fallback if TI wants
extra defensiveness in the published IG (an FMM-justification note costs
little and pre-empts the same comment in future ballots), but it should
not be coupled with FMM reductions beyond the two FMM 2 profiles, and even
those reductions are weakly motivated. Disposition A overcorrects: it
treats the absence of base-spec profile FMM as evidence of immaturity,
which it isn't, and would understate the real-world maturity of the
Shareable artefacts.
