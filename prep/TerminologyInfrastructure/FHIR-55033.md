## Summary

FHIR-55033 is a consolidation ticket opened by Reuben Daniels asking the
Terminology Infrastructure work group to address the remaining duplicate
terminology artifacts (CodeSystems and ValueSets) that exist in more than
one of three places: the FHIR core specification, the FHIR Extensions
Pack, and HL7 Terminology (THO). The ticket itself contains only a link
to a shared Google Sheet that tracks each duplicate, the proposed
disposition for each, and the current status. It explicitly supersedes
the two long-standing precursor tickets [FHIR-47498](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-47498)
("Duplicate Terminology Resources Against THO" — 14 CodeSystems and 14
ValueSets in both Extensions Pack and THO) and
[FHIR-47270](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-47270) ("Duplication of
code systems and value sets with THO (using different URLs)" — same
artifacts under both `http://hl7.org/fhir/...` and
`http://terminology.hl7.org/...` URLs). Per the comment thread, those
two tickets are intended to be closed in favour of FHIR-55033, which
holds the latest analysis and per-artifact recommendations driven by the
FMG terminology-expectations policy that Lloyd McKenzie summarised at
the September 2025 WGM ("things bound as code should live in the
extension pack; things not bound as code should live in THO").

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the per-artifact dispositions captured in the Reuben/Jess Google
Sheet (`docs.google.com/.../1nClb8ESBO0pOtVkzOuBEHAsZPh030rZteYmH7GUylfg`)
as the authoritative resolution for FHIR-55033, and execute them under
the rule Lloyd articulated at the Sep 2025 WGM:

- **Code-bound artifacts** (CodeSystem/ValueSet referenced by a binding
  on an extension or core element) live in the FHIR Extensions Pack
  (or FHIR core, where appropriate) under `http://hl7.org/fhir/...`.
- **Non-code-bound artifacts** (used only as examples, narrative, or
  background terminology) live in THO under
  `http://terminology.hl7.org/...`.
- For each duplicate pair on the sheet: (a) keep the canonical instance
  on the side dictated by the rule, (b) retire the duplicate by
  marking its `status = retired` and adding a `replacedBy` /
  `successor` extension pointing at the surviving canonical, and
  (c) ensure both publications carry an explicit, machine-readable
  pointer to the survivor so that existing implementers can follow the
  redirect.

Track completion in the spreadsheet and close the precursor tickets
FHIR-47498 and FHIR-47270 as Persuasive (already done in April 2026)
with the resolution text pointing at FHIR-55033.

#### Justification

This is what the consolidating reporter and the WG leadership have
already converged on: the September 2025 motion (Grahame/Lloyd, 19-0-1)
delegated the per-artifact resolution to a subgroup, the subgroup
(Reuben + Jessica) produced the spreadsheet, and FMG is finalising the
Terminology Expectations policy that codifies the same rule. Accepting
"as requested" simply ratifies that work and authorises Reuben/Jess to
keep executing the per-artifact retirements during the R6 QA window
(per Jess's Nov 2025 comment on FHIR-47498). Anything weaker risks
re-opening a debate that has already been held publicly and resolved
unanimously.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the per-artifact dispositions in the spreadsheet, but do **not**
treat FHIR-55033 as a single monolithic ticket. Instead, split it into
one child Jira per surviving duplicate pair (or per logical batch of
related artifacts — e.g., all `implant*` artifacts together), each with
an explicit before/after canonical URL, the chosen survivor, the
chosen retirement mechanism, and a target publication (Extensions Pack
release X.Y or THO release N). FHIR-55033 then becomes a tracker that
stays open until every child is resolved. Additionally, require each
child ticket to specify the redirect / `replacedBy` mechanism on the
retired artifact and to confirm that the surviving artifact's `version`
and `status` are explicitly updated, rather than implicitly carried by
publication.

#### Justification

The current shape of FHIR-55033 — a one-line description that defers to
an external Google Sheet — makes it impossible for downstream
implementers, ballot reviewers, or the eventual FHIR R6 QA reviewers to
see what actually changed and why. The Google Sheet is not a HL7
artifact; it can be edited or revoked outside Jira's audit trail and
can't be referenced from a balloted spec. Splitting into child tickets
preserves the per-artifact decisions in HL7's permanent record, lets TI
disposition each one individually if needed (e.g., when an external
implementer disagrees with one specific retirement), and prevents
FHIR-55033 from being closed before all duplicates are actually retired.
It also makes ballot-period negative votes (which Jess explicitly
anticipates on FHIR-47498) easier to scope: a NV against one specific
duplicate doesn't block the rest.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline FHIR-55033 as duplicative of, and procedurally inferior to, the
two existing tickets it tries to supersede (FHIR-47498 and FHIR-47270),
both of which already carry Persuasive resolutions. Re-open FHIR-47498
as the canonical container for the duplicate inventory, attach the
Reuben/Jess spreadsheet there as a versioned attachment, and close
FHIR-55033 as a duplicate of FHIR-47498.

#### Justification

FHIR-55033 contains no analysis of its own — its description is a
single external link, with no committed disposition for any specific
artifact. Both precursor tickets already have substantive comment
trails, formal resolutions, and a clear paper trail back to the WGM
motion. Adding a third ticket whose only content is "see the
spreadsheet" arguably fragments the audit trail rather than
consolidating it, and does so without bringing any new technical
content to the table. If the WG genuinely needs one consolidated
container, the existing FHIR-47498 — which already references the same
spreadsheet and has the higher-priority record — is the better home.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The work itself (Lloyd's rule, the Reuben/Jess spreadsheet, retiring
duplicates with explicit redirects) is correct and should proceed —
that is the substance of Disposition A and the WG already supports it.
But FHIR-55033 in its current form is too thin to function as a Jira
record: a future reader will see only "see Google Sheet" and a
spreadsheet that may or may not still be accessible. Disposition B
keeps the technical decisions intact while moving them into Jira where
they belong, gives ballot reviewers something concrete to react to per
artifact (which Jess flagged is likely during R6 QA), and protects
against the situation where one specific retirement turns out to be
controversial and needs to be reversed without destabilising the rest.
Disposition C is too aggressive — there is value in having a single
post-policy container that points forward rather than re-opening older
tickets — but the WG should insist that the container actually carry
the per-artifact decisions in HL7's own audit trail rather than in a
shared Google Sheet.
