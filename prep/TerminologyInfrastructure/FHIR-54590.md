## Summary

The reporter asks Terminology Infrastructure to add explicit normative
guidance to the R6 ballot text on **how Additional Bindings are validated**
and **when slicing should be used in their place**. Three concrete sources
of confusion are called out: (1) interaction with an **extensible** main
binding, (2) behavior on **repeating** elements, and (3) the meaning of
`any=true` for satisfying additional bindings across repeats. The ticket
also asks the spec to clarify which Additional Binding **purposes** are
intended to be directly enforceable in instance validation versus which are
informational/application-obligation, how binding **strength** on an
additional binding interacts with the strength of the base binding, and
when an IG author should reach for **slicing** instead.

This ticket is one of a closely-coupled trio raised by the same reporter
on the same day:

- [FHIR-54589](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54589) — *Clarify whether
  Additional Bindings must be subsets of the main binding* — **Resolved
  (Persuasive with Modification)** at the Jan 2026 WGM. Its resolution
  text is explicit that its **first added paragraph also satisfies
  FHIR-54590**, namely:
  > "The conceptual spaces of all bindings on an element must be
  > overlapping. For example, it would not be appropriate to have a
  > binding for concepts representing color and a binding for concepts
  > representing shape on the same element unless handled through
  > slices."
  
  plus the follow-on note that for `code`/`Coding` data types the value
  must satisfy **all** bindings, and disjoint conformance expectations
  produce an unsatisfiable element. The 54589 resolution also commits to
  (a) examples on the FHIR examples page demonstrating additional
  bindings, and (b) prose in the IG Guidance IG (Lloyd McKenzie to draft).
- [FHIR-54128](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54128) — *Additional
  Bindings Documentation is under-specified* — currently **Waiting for
  Input**; Gay Dolin notes that the same WGM resolution package is
  expected to satisfy this ticket as well.

In comments on this ticket (March 2026), both Carmela Couderc (TI co-chair)
and the reporter herself explicitly question whether FHIR-54590 should be
**withdrawn** in favor of FHIR-54589's resolution, deferring to Eric Haas
and Bryn Rhodes for confirmation. The reporter additionally notes that US
Core has decided to **stop using Additional Bindings** because of this
modeling/validation ambiguity — a piece of community signal worth recording
even if the ticket itself is closed administratively.

## Details

**Description:**

> ### Comment
>
> The current R6 ballot text does not clearly describe how additional
> bindings are expected to be validated, particularly when:
>
> - the base binding is **extensible**,
> - the element is **repeating**, and
> - `any=true` is used to allow satisfaction of additional bindings across
>   repeats.
>
> It is also unclear when additional bindings are sufficient versus when
> **slicing** should be used to express distinct, enforceable binding
> requirements.
>
> ### Why this matters
>
> In practice, IG authors struggle to determine:
>
> - which additional bindings are directly testable in resource instances,
> - which represent application obligations or documentation only, and
> - when slicing is required to achieve predictable validation behavior.
>
> This ambiguity leads to inconsistent IG designs and unpredictable
> validator outcomes.
>
> ### Requested change
>
> Please add explicit guidance describing:
>
> - which binding purposes are intended to be directly enforceable vs
>   informational,
> - how binding strength on additional bindings interacts with base
>   binding strength, and
> - when additional bindings should be used versus slicing for enforceable
>   alternative or contextual bindings.

**Comments:**

- **carmela_couderc (2026-03-10):** Notes that the resolution recorded on
  FHIR-54589 is intended to also satisfy this ticket and asks Gay Dolin
  and Bryn Rhodes whether they agree the ticket can be withdrawn.
- **gdolin (2026-03-13):** Replies that she is not convinced FHIR-54589's
  resolution actually addresses the *validation interplay* between the
  main binding and additional bindings. She also flags a broader
  modeling concern — that several "additional binding" purposes are not
  really bindings but "uses" — and reports that **US Core intends not to
  use Additional Bindings going forward**. She defers the
  withdraw/keep-open decision to Eric Haas and Bryn Rhodes.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Withdraw / "Already addressed by FHIR-54589 resolution"

#### Proposal

Mark FHIR-54590 **Not Persuasive — Already Addressed**, pointing to the
FHIR-54589 resolution committed at the Jan 2026 WGM:

1. The new paragraph added at `terminologies.html#binding` (above the 2nd
   table) — *"The conceptual spaces of all bindings on an element must be
   overlapping … unless handled through slices."* — is the explicit
   "additional binding vs. slicing" guidance FHIR-54590 asks for.
2. The follow-on paragraph — *"if the element data type is a code or
   coding, the value must satisfy all bindings. If the conformance
   expectations of the bindings are disjoint, it will not be possible to
   satisfy the bindings …"* — is the explicit validation-interplay
   statement FHIR-54590 asks for.
3. The 54589 resolution further commits to (a) worked examples on the
   FHIR examples page and (b) authoring guidance in the IG Guidance IG
   (LM to draft). Those artifacts cover the "which purposes are
   enforceable vs. informational" and "how strength interacts" questions
   FHIR-54590 raises.

The ticket would be closed with a comment cross-linking the 54589
resolution and the Jan 2026 WGM minutes; no separate text change is made.

#### Justification

This disposition matches what is **already on the table**: Carmela
Couderc explicitly asked the reporter to withdraw on these grounds, and
Gay Dolin expressed willingness to defer to Eric Haas/Bryn Rhodes. The
54589 resolution text was deliberately drafted to "also satisfy
FHIR-54590" — that is in the resolution description verbatim. Closing
this ticket avoids parallel resolutions diverging in TI's text and keeps
the ballot reconciliation tidy. Risk: the reporter has flagged in
comments that she is **not** convinced 54589's text resolves the
*validation* interplay; if TI accepts that signal, Disposition B is
preferable.

---

### Disposition B: Persuasive with Modification — additive guidance scoped to validation + slicing decision

#### Proposal

Keep FHIR-54590 open as its own change and resolve **Persuasive with
Modification** by adding a *new* short subsection (not just a paragraph)
to `terminologies.html#binding`, immediately after the FHIR-54589 text,
with three sub-bullets the ticket explicitly asks for:

1. **Enforceability by purpose.** State, per `additional.purpose` value
   (`maximum`, `minimum`, `required`, `extensible`, `current`, `preferred`,
   `ui`, `starter`, `component`, `candidate`), whether instance
   validators are expected to (a) emit `error`, (b) emit `warning`, or
   (c) treat the binding as informational only. Cross-link to the
   binding-purpose value set.
2. **Strength interaction.** State explicitly that an additional
   binding's strength is interpreted **independently** of the main
   binding's strength (the main binding always applies at its own
   strength, per the existing sentence) and give the matrix for the
   four most-asked combinations
   (`required` main × `required`/`extensible`/`preferred` additional;
   `extensible` main × same).
3. **`any=true` on repeating elements.** State that on a repeating
   element, `any=true` means "at least one repeat satisfies the
   additional binding" and the default is "every repeat must satisfy".
4. **When to slice instead.** Add a short decision rule: use slicing
   (rather than an additional binding) whenever the IG needs to enforce
   *different* required/extensible bindings on *distinguishable*
   instances of a repeating element, or whenever the conceptual spaces
   of the bindings would otherwise be disjoint.

In parallel, contribute a worked example to the FHIR examples page that
shows (i) an extensible main binding with a `required` additional
binding, (ii) the same scenario expressed with slicing, and (iii) the
expected validator output for both.

#### Justification

This is the disposition that **directly answers the three concrete
gaps** the ticket calls out (enforceability-by-purpose, strength
interaction, repeating + `any=true`) — none of which are covered by the
FHIR-54589 wording, which addresses subset/overlap relationships rather
than validation behavior. It also aligns with the IG Guidance IG work
LM committed to in 54589 by giving that prose normative anchors in core.
The trade-off vs. Disposition A is more text-churn in
`terminologies.html` and a need for TI + FHIR-I sign-off on the
enforceability-by-purpose table; the upside is that IG authors and
validator implementers (the audience the reporter cites) get a single
authoritative reference instead of an Implementation Guide.

---

### Disposition C: Defer to FHIR-I / IG Guidance IG, keep this ticket as a tracking record

#### Proposal

Resolve FHIR-54590 **Considered — Deferred**, with a note that:

- The *terminology-side* clarification (overlap of conceptual spaces,
  satisfaction across multiple bindings on `code`/`Coding`) is delivered
  by FHIR-54589 in `terminologies.html#binding`.
- The *validation-behavior* clarification (per-purpose enforceability,
  `any=true` semantics on repeats, slicing-vs-additional-binding
  decision) is **out of scope** for the Terminology Infrastructure work
  group's spec section and is forwarded to FHIR Infrastructure (the
  validator-behavior owner) and to the IG Guidance IG (the authoring
  guidance owner) for Lloyd McKenzie's planned content. A follow-up
  ticket is opened against FHIR-I capturing the validator-behavior
  questions explicitly.

#### Justification

Defensible if TI takes the position that the questions in FHIR-54590 are
fundamentally about *validator and IG-authoring behavior* rather than
about terminology semantics, and so do not belong in
`terminologies.html`. It also respects the reporter's signal that she
distrusts conflating the modeling answer (54589) with the validation
answer (54590), while still ensuring the work doesn't fall on the floor.
Risk: ballot reviewers see "Deferred" as kicking the can; mitigated by
opening the FHIR-I follow-up ticket as part of the disposition.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition: B — Persuasive with Modification (additive
guidance scoped to validation + slicing decision).**

Disposition A is administratively the cleanest path and has the chairs'
implicit support, but the reporter's 2026-03-13 comment is on point: the
text added under FHIR-54589 answers *"may an additional binding be a
non-subset?"* and *"what happens when bindings are disjoint on a
`code`-typed element?"*, but it does **not** answer *"is a `preferred`
additional binding enforced by validators?"*, *"what does `any=true`
mean on a repeating element?"*, or *"when do I slice instead?"* — and
those are exactly the three questions this ticket raises. Closing the
ticket as already-addressed re-creates the original gap in a slightly
different place (`additional.purpose` semantics) and is precisely the
"conflicting guidance" pattern the reporter cites US Core as having
exited the Additional Bindings feature over.

Disposition B keeps the work small and bounded — one new subsection in
`terminologies.html#binding` plus a worked example — and gives the IG
Guidance IG content LM is drafting under 54589 a normative anchor to
point at. Disposition C is the right *fallback* if TI concludes (with
FHIR-I) that the per-purpose enforceability table belongs in the
validator's documentation rather than in core; in that case A is
preferable to C, because deferral without a concrete owner tends to
strand these tickets.

Recommend bringing B to the next TI call with a short straw-man of the
"Enforceability by purpose" table for review, and explicitly inviting
Eric Haas, Bryn Rhodes, and Lloyd McKenzie (the people Gay deferred to
in comments) to confirm before final disposition.
