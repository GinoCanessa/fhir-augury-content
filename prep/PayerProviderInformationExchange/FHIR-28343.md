## Summary

The ticket asks the Payer/Provider Information Exchange work group to harmonize
the section structure of two `Composition`-based profiles in the US Dental Data
Exchange IG: the **Dental Referral Note** and the **Dental Consult Note**. Both
profiles include similar-looking sections — `plan_of_treatment`, `assessment`,
`procedures`, and payer information — but they are organized differently
between the two profiles. The reporter (Craig Newman) is asking whether the
shared sections (assuming content equivalence) can be organized into the same
hierarchy and presented in the same order across both Composition profiles, so
that consumers reading either document encounter the shared concepts in a
consistent place.

No related Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were returned by the
cross-reference index for this ticket, so the request stands on its own. The
ticket has already been triaged with a resolution of **Persuasive** (resolved
2022-09-08); the work group's recorded resolution noted that both profiles
derive from their respective CCDAonFHIR profiles, that dependent profiles must
preserve the top-down snapshot ordering of their base `StructureDefinition`,
and that section ordering would be aligned across the Composition profiles to
the extent the FHIR IG publisher permits.

## Details

>
> Sections will be found in the same order across Composition profiles to the
> extent allowed by the FHIR IG publisher.

## Proposed Dispositions

> Note: the work group has already recorded a resolution of **Persuasive** on
> this ticket (2022-09-08). The dispositions below are framed as the choices
> that were available at the time the ticket was being prepared for review,
> and the recommendation aligns with the recorded resolution.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Harmonize the section structure of the Dental Referral Note and Dental Consult
Note `Composition` profiles in the US Dental Data Exchange IG so that the
shared sections — `plan_of_treatment`, `assessment`, `procedures`, and payer
information — appear under the same `Composition.section` slice names, with
the same parent/child nesting, and in the same top-down order in both
profiles. Where the two profiles inherit from different CCDAonFHIR base
Composition profiles, align the differentials (slice names, slice ordering,
cardinalities, and bound code systems for `Composition.section.code`) so that
the shared sections are described identically in both profiles' differentials,
and re-publish the IG so the snapshots reflect the harmonized structure to the
extent the IG publisher allows.

#### Justification

This directly satisfies the reporter's request and produces a more consistent
implementer experience: a system that knows where to find `assessment` or
`plan_of_treatment` in a Dental Referral Note will find it in the same logical
location in a Dental Consult Note. It also reduces the risk of authoring
errors when content is reused between the two document types. The main
constraint to acknowledge in the work is that, per FHIR's profiling rules,
dependent profiles preserve the top-down ordering of snapshot elements from
their base `StructureDefinition`; harmonization therefore has to be performed
in a way that respects each profile's CCDAonFHIR base and that works within
what the IG publisher emits — exactly the caveat captured in the resolution.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than aligning the two Composition profiles' section trees in place,
factor the shared section definitions out into a small set of reusable
`Composition.section` slice patterns (or shared sub-profiles / extensions for
section metadata) and reference those from both the Dental Referral Note and
the Dental Consult Note profiles. Document a normative section-ordering table
in the IG narrative that both profiles cite, and rely on that table — rather
than the inherited snapshot order — as the source of truth for "where does
section X go".

#### Justification

This addresses the underlying need (consistent placement of shared sections)
without requiring the two profiles' snapshots to look identical, which is hard
to guarantee when they inherit from different CCDAonFHIR base profiles and
are emitted by the IG publisher. A shared section-pattern artefact plus a
narrative ordering table is more robust to future divergence in the upstream
CCDAonFHIR profiles and gives implementers a single, citable definition of
each shared section. The trade-off is added IG complexity and an extra layer
of indirection for readers who expect to see the structure on the Composition
profile page itself.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the harmonization request on the grounds that the two Composition
profiles inherit from distinct CCDAonFHIR base profiles whose snapshot
ordering is fixed by the base `StructureDefinition`, and that any apparent
inconsistency between the two profiles is a downstream consequence of those
different bases rather than a defect in the US Dental Data Exchange IG.
Implementers should be directed to the per-section `Composition.section.code`
binding (LOINC) to identify shared sections, regardless of their position in
the document.

#### Justification

This is defensible if the work group concludes that forcing identical section
ordering across two profiles with different CCDAonFHIR bases would either
require non-conformant profiling or would push complexity into the IG
publisher beyond what it can express. It also leans on the standard FHIR
guidance that consumers of `Composition` documents should locate sections by
`section.code` rather than positional order. The cost is leaving implementers
with the inconsistency the reporter flagged, and accepting that "look at the
codes, not the order" places more burden on consuming systems.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Accepting the request as written is the right starting point: the reporter's
concern is concrete, implementer-facing, and shared by anyone who has to
consume both Dental Referral Notes and Dental Consult Notes. Harmonizing the
shared sections (`plan_of_treatment`, `assessment`, `procedures`, payer
information) in slice names, nesting, and ordering is achievable within FHIR's
profiling rules as long as the work explicitly respects each profile's
CCDAonFHIR base ordering and works within what the IG publisher can emit —
which is exactly the caveat the work group's recorded resolution captures.
Disposition B is a reasonable refinement to consider during implementation
(and could be folded into A if the work group wants reusable section
patterns), but on its own it adds indirection without first delivering the
visible consistency the ticket asks for. Disposition C should be reserved for
the case where the publisher genuinely cannot express the alignment; the
recorded resolution suggests the work group did not reach that conclusion.
