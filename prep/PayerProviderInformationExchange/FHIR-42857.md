## Summary

The reporter (Russell Ott, on behalf of Kyle Pelkey, DMD) asks the
Payer/Provider Information Exchange work group to update Scenario 2 in
the Background section (item 3.4) of the **US Dental Data Exchange
(FHIR)** implementation guide so that the worked clinical example is
consistent with current ADA Clinical Practice Guidelines (CPGs) and the
ISO/ANSI/ADA Standard 3950 designation system for teeth. Specifically,
the prescribed antibiotic in the scenario is no longer accurate, the
order of clinical activities is implausible (e.g., a penicillin allergy
would be known well before the next appointment is scheduled), and the
tooth/oral-cavity nomenclature needs to follow the standard
arch–side–tooth ordering. The reporter also asks that "second molar"
be used in place of "back molar" when referring to tooth #18, to avoid
confusion with tooth #17. A revised wording and example table are
provided in the Word attachment `Change to Background item 3.4 in
IG-1.docx`. The ticket previously received a **Persuasive** disposition
on 2024-01-29 by a 21-0-0 vote (Russell Ott / Greg Zeller), but was
reverted on 2024-03-19 after the work group confirmed the agreed change
had never been applied to the IG; PIE WG was asked to re-open and
re-discuss. The most recent comment (2025-09-23) again notes that the
status needs to be revisited with the reporter. No cross-referenced
Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were found via
fhir-augury, so this report is grounded entirely in the ticket itself
and its comment history.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Re-apply the originally-agreed edits (the work-group's 2024-01-29
Persuasive vote, 21-0-0) to Background §3.4 / Scenario 2 of the **US
Dental Data Exchange (FHIR)** IG, exactly as set out in the attachment
`Change to Background item 3.4 in IG-1.docx`. Concretely, in both the
narrative paragraph and the accompanying example table:

1. Replace the currently-named antibiotic with the regimen that
   reflects the current ADA Clinical Practice Guidelines for dental
   antibiotic prophylaxis (per Kyle Pelkey's revised attachment).
2. Re-order the clinical activities in the scenario so that the
   patient's penicillin allergy is identified **before** the next
   appointment is scheduled, rather than after.
3. Rewrite tooth references using ISO/ANSI/ADA Standard 3950
   (arch → side → tooth) ordering throughout the scenario and table.
4. Replace "back molar" with "second molar" when referring to tooth
   #18, to disambiguate from tooth #17.

The change is narrative only, classified Non-substantive / Enhancement,
and does not alter any FHIR resource, profile, extension, or
conformance constraint. Apply the edit, mark the ticket Resolved
(Persuasive — re-applied), and verify the rendered Background page
reflects the attachment.

#### Justification

The PIE WG already deliberated and voted 21-0-0 on this exact change
on 2024-01-29; the only reason the ticket was reverted is that the
agreed edit was never published to the IG, not that the work group
changed its mind. The substance is uncontested, the requesting subject
matter expert (Kyle Pelkey, DMD) is a dental clinician, and the
provided attachment is a clean redline ready to apply. The edits
improve clinical accuracy, align with ADA CPGs and ISO/ANSI/ADA
Standard 3950 (which an implementer-facing dental IG should follow for
tooth nomenclature), and carry zero conformance risk because they
touch only the worked Background example. Re-applying the prior
disposition is therefore the path that respects the earlier ballot
while finally closing the loop.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Apply the change in two passes rather than one:

- **Pass 1 (this ticket):** Update **only** the antibiotic name in
  Background §3.4 / Scenario 2 (both narrative and table) to match
  current ADA CPGs. This is the minimum edit needed to remove
  clinically misleading content. Resolve this ticket Persuasive with
  Modification.
- **Pass 2 (new follow-up ticket, opened at the same time):** Track
  the remaining structural edits — re-ordering of activities so the
  penicillin-allergy discovery precedes appointment scheduling, and
  rewriting all tooth references in ISO/ANSI/ADA Standard 3950
  (arch–side–tooth) order including the "back molar" → "second molar"
  clarification — for separate review and disposition at a future PIE
  WG call.

Both edits remain narrative-only against the **US Dental Data Exchange
(FHIR)** IG's Background section.

#### Justification

The 2024-01-29 vote bundled clinical-content correction (antibiotic),
workflow-realism re-ordering (activity sequence), and a documentation
convention change (ISO 3950 nomenclature) into a single Persuasive
disposition. Two years later, only one of those — the antibiotic —
is unambiguously a *correctness* fix; the other two are improvements
to plausibility and to convention. Splitting the ticket lets the
clinically-incorrect text be removed immediately without re-litigating
the broader rewrite, and it gives the PIE WG a clean opportunity to
decide whether ISO 3950 ordering should be applied just to this
example or systematically across the whole IG (which would be a
larger consistency commitment). The trade-off vs. Disposition A is an
extra ticket and a longer total path to the full rewrite, in exchange
for tighter scoping and an explicit decision on IG-wide nomenclature
policy.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve as **Not Persuasive**, on the basis that Background §3.4 /
Scenario 2 is a non-normative narrative example that does not affect
any FHIR resource, profile, extension, terminology binding, or
conformance constraint, and that no implementer harm has been
demonstrated in the two-plus years the ticket has been open. Direct
the reporter to raise a fresh ticket if there is a specific normative
or conformance concern that follows from the example, and close this
one without action.

#### Justification

The IG's Background section is illustrative, not normative; an
out-of-date antibiotic name in a worked example does not cause any
implementation to behave incorrectly, fail validation, or
mis-represent a profile. The ticket has been Triaged, Persuasive
(reverted), and re-Triaged across multiple cycles without ever being
applied, suggesting the change is not a high-leverage use of the work
group's narrowed bandwidth. Declining keeps the IG's normative content
exactly where it is and avoids precedent-setting work to chase
clinical-guideline drift in non-normative narrative — drift that, by
its nature, will recur as ADA CPGs evolve.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The work group has already debated and unanimously approved this
change (21-0-0 on 2024-01-29); the only failure was administrative —
the agreed edits were never published. The change is clinically
correct, narrative-only, classified Non-substantive / Enhancement,
carries no conformance risk, and is supplied as a ready-to-apply
attachment by the dental clinician (Kyle Pelkey, DMD) who originated
the request. Re-running the same dispositional analysis (Disposition
B) just to split the work is process for its own sake, and declining
(Disposition C) would leave a clinically-inaccurate antibiotic
recommendation in a worked dental example, which is poor optics for an
ADA-aligned dental data-exchange IG even if it is non-normative. The
PIE WG should re-confirm the Persuasive disposition, hand the ticket
to whoever owns publication of the US Dental Data Exchange (FHIR) IG,
and verify the rendered Background page reflects the attachment
before closing.
