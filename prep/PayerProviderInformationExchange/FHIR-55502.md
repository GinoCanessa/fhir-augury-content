## Summary

The ticket requests a purely editorial change to the US CARIN Digital
Insurance Card (DIC) IG: rename the page currently titled
*"Comparison with the International Patient Summary IG"* to
*"Using the Digital Insurance Card with the International Patient
Summary IG"*. Reporter Eric Haas notes that the existing heading does
not accurately reflect what the page actually covers — the page
describes how the DIC is used together with the IPS rather than
performing a feature-by-feature comparison of the two IGs. The ticket
has already been triaged with resolution **Persuasive** and is queued
for block vote (`Block-Vote-2`, `Ready-For-Vote`). No cross-referenced
Jira tickets, Zulip discussions, or GitHub items were surfaced.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Rename the affected page in the US CARIN Digital Insurance Card (DIC)
IG from *"Comparison with the International Patient Summary IG"* to
*"Using the Digital Insurance Card with the International Patient
Summary IG"*. Concretely:

- Update the page's `<h1>`/heading element in the IG source (the page
  intro / narrative file backing the "Comparison with the
  International Patient Summary IG" page).
- Update the page's title in the IG menu / table of contents so the
  navigation entry matches the new heading.
- Update any in-IG cross-links, anchors, or "see also" pointers that
  reference the old title (search the IG source for
  "Comparison with the International Patient Summary" and adjust
  prose accordingly).
- Leave the page's URL/anchor stable if possible to avoid breaking
  external deep links; if the slug is derived from the title and must
  change, add a redirect or leave the old anchor as a stable target.

No FHIR conformance artifacts (resources, profiles, value sets,
extensions) are affected — this is a narrative/IG-presentation change
only.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter asked for, the change is purely
editorial, and the workgroup has already marked the resolution
**Persuasive**. The new title (*"Using the … with …"*) accurately
describes a "how to use together" page, whereas the current
*"Comparison with …"* implies a feature/coverage comparison that the
page does not actually perform. Aligning the heading with the page
content reduces reader confusion and matches common HL7 IG conventions
for cross-IG usage pages. The ticket is already on `Block-Vote-2` /
`Ready-For-Vote`, indicating no substantive opposition.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the intent of the change but adopt a slightly broader edit:

1. Rename the page to *"Using the Digital Insurance Card with the
   International Patient Summary IG"* (as requested).
2. At the same time, add a one- or two-sentence lead paragraph
   immediately under the new heading that explicitly states the
   page's purpose (e.g., *"This page describes how implementers can
   use the US CARIN Digital Insurance Card together with the
   International Patient Summary (IPS) IG, including which DIC
   artifacts are relevant when an IPS is being assembled or
   exchanged."*).
3. If any subsection within the page still uses the word
   *"comparison"* in a way that conflicts with the new framing, gently
   rephrase those subsection headings/intros to match
   *"using together"* language.

#### Justification

The reporter's underlying complaint is that the heading misrepresents
the content. Renaming the heading fixes the title-vs-content mismatch,
but a reader landing on the page from external search or a deep link
may still expect a "comparison" based on prior references in the wild.
A short lead paragraph and consistent in-page wording reinforce the
new framing and reduce future confusion at very low editorial cost.
This is still a narrative-only change, still backwards-compatible at
the conformance level, and stays within the scope of an editorial
ticket.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Leave the heading as *"Comparison with the International Patient
Summary IG"*, on the basis that the change is cosmetic, the existing
heading has been published in prior ballot/preview material, and
renaming risks invalidating external references (slides, blog posts,
deep links) that point at the current page title.

#### Justification

This is the standard "decline cosmetic change" rationale: the page
content does not change, the conformance behavior of the IG does not
change, and any reader who reaches the page can read the first
paragraph and understand its intent. Renaming a published page costs
implementer goodwill (broken links, stale references) for a benefit
that could equally be achieved by tightening the page's lead
paragraph. The decline rationale here would be **"editorial
preference, insufficient justification to break existing references."**

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The workgroup has already triaged this as **Persuasive** and queued it
for `Block-Vote-2`, the change is editorial, the new title genuinely
describes what the page does, and no cross-referenced Jira tickets,
Zulip threads, or GitHub items were found that would suggest hidden
controversy or a broader rework. Disposition B is reasonable but adds
scope (lead paragraph, subsection rewording) that the reporter did not
request and that the workgroup did not pre-discuss; if reviewers feel
the page also needs a clarifying lead paragraph, that can be raised
during the edit but should not block this ticket. Disposition C
under-weighs the actual confusion caused by a misleading heading on a
cross-IG usage page; in HL7 IG practice, pages titled *"Comparison
with X"* are expected to be feature-comparison pages, and this one
isn't, so leaving the title unchanged perpetuates a navigation bug.
Take Disposition A and merge with the block vote.
