## Summary

The ticket reports that as a downstream consequence of making
DeviceDefinition a canonical resource (tracked in
[FHIR-39389](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-39389)), the publisher
was forced to reduce the cardinality of `ObservationDefinition.device`
from `0..*` to `0..1`. The reduction is mandatory because
`ObservationDefinition.device` is a choice element (`device[x]` between
`deviceReference` and `deviceCanonical`), and FHIR's choice-element
rules prohibit cardinalities greater than 1. The reporter asks the
workgroup to choose between (1) accepting the reduced `0..1`
cardinality, or (2) restructuring the element so the choice is split
into separate, non-choice elements that can remain `0..*`.

The original ticket also covered `NutritionOrder.enteralFormula.deliveryDevice`,
which suffers from the identical pattern. That portion was split off into
[FHIR-53386](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53386) on 2025-11-07 (now
"Applied"), so FHIR-53307 is now scoped strictly to ObservationDefinition.
The element itself was added between R4B and R5 as part of generic
"follow the request pattern" work in
[FHIR-36664](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-36664), without explicit
discussion of cardinality at the time.

## Details

**Description:**

With DeviceDefinition becoming a canonical resource
([FHIR-39389](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-39389)) the cardinality
of ObservationDefinition.device was forced to change from `0..*` to
`0..1` by the publisher.

> ERROR @ ObservationDefinition.device[x]: Elements with a choice of
> types cannot have a cardinality > 1

This is a known characteristic of the choice element
(<https://build.fhir.org/formats.html#choice>).

On ObservationDefinition this is caused by the choice on `device[x]`
between `deviceReference` and `deviceCanonical`.

**Options for resolution:**

1. Accept the revised cardinality.
2. Separate out the choice element into component elements.

**Comments:**

- **esilver (2025-11-03):** Notes the element was added between R4B and
  R5 via [FHIR-36664](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-36664) as part of
  generic "make this follow the pattern" work, with no recorded
  discussion of cardinality. Personally leans toward `0..1` of a single
  choice element.
- **jduteau (2025-11-03):** Confirms the symmetric NutritionOrder
  element was discussed for addition in that earlier ticket; if `0..1`
  is acceptable, that's fine.
- **JIRAUSER19407 (2025-11-04, OO on FHIR Nov 4 minutes):** Committee
  leaned toward accepting reduced cardinality on NutritionOrder.
  "Considerable debate" on ObservationDefinition and whether it should
  reference a Device instance at all. Possible "fix" mentioned: change
  to `0..* canonical(DeviceDefinition)` only (no choice). Ralf Herzog
  was asked to bring back instance-level use cases.
- **esilver (2025-11-07, x2):** Records that NutritionOrder scope was
  split into [FHIR-53386](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53386). On
  the substance, proposes a backbone-element alternative:
  `deviceUsed` Backbone `0..*` containing `device[x] 1..1`
  (deviceReference / deviceCanonical / deviceCodeableConcept), with an
  optional `deviceUsed.role CodeableConcept 0..1` to distinguish
  alternative devices (same role) from devices used together
  (different roles). Notes the role enhancement existed under the old
  `0..*` design too.
- **ralf.herzog (2025-11-18):** Provides the requested instance-level
  scenarios. Lab instruments where a specific rarely-run expensive test
  is installed on only one of N otherwise-identical devices; or 5 of
  10 instruments have a particular test set / testkit / calibration /
  QC state. Argues that the actual test eligibility depends on per-device
  installation, testkit loading, calibration, and QC state — properties
  not modelled at DeviceDefinition granularity. Concludes that
  restricting `ObservationDefinition.device` to `DeviceDefinition` alone
  is insufficient.
- **rikim (2025-11-24):** Counter-pattern from LIVD — model the device,
  testkit, calibrations, etc. as separate `DeviceDefinition` instances
  and a composite `DeviceDefinition` that aggregates them; that
  composite is what `ObservationDefinition.device` would point to. Notes
  FHIR doesn't currently model dynamic device states at the
  DeviceDefinition level and questions whether it should.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested (Option 1 — keep `0..1`)

#### Proposal

Resolve `ObservationDefinition.device` as `0..1 device[x]` with the
existing choice between `Reference(Device)` and `canonical(DeviceDefinition)`,
exactly as the publisher was forced to express it after FHIR-39389.
Concretely, in the FHIR Core spec change `ObservationDefinition.device`
to:

- `device[x]` `0..1`
  - `deviceReference` Reference(Device)
  - `deviceCanonical` canonical(DeviceDefinition)

Add a short note to the element definition explaining that, when an
ObservationDefinition is conceptually associated with multiple device
models, implementers should either (a) author one ObservationDefinition
per device, or (b) reference a composite `DeviceDefinition` that
aggregates the participating devices via `DeviceDefinition.hasPart` /
`DeviceDefinition.link.relatedDevice` (per the LIVD pattern raised by
rikim).

#### Justification

- Mirrors the disposition the workgroup has already applied to the
  parallel ticket [FHIR-53386](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53386)
  for `NutritionOrder.enteralFormula.deliveryDevice`. Treating the
  two symmetric problems differently would be hard to defend.
- Honors FHIR's invariant that choice elements (`x[x]`) are `0..1`,
  preserving consistency with every other `device[x]` / `subject[x]` /
  `medication[x]` element across the spec.
- The composite-`DeviceDefinition` pattern (rikim's LIVD example)
  already gives implementers a documented way to express
  "multiple devices" without expanding cardinality, and supports
  reuse across observation definitions.
- Lowest implementation impact: the publisher already enforces it, so
  no further restructuring of the resource is required.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — `deviceUsed` backbone with `0..*`

#### Proposal

Restructure `ObservationDefinition.device` into a backbone element so
that multiple devices can be associated with a single
ObservationDefinition while retaining the choice between Reference,
canonical, and (newly) CodeableConcept. Following esilver's 2025-11-07
sketch, replace the current element with:

- `deviceUsed` BackboneElement `0..*`
  - `device[x]` `1..1`
    - `deviceReference` Reference(Device)
    - `deviceCanonical` canonical(DeviceDefinition)
    - `deviceCodeableConcept` CodeableConcept (preferred binding to a
      Device Type code system)
  - `role` CodeableConcept `0..1` — devices sharing a role are
    alternatives; devices with different roles are used together.

This is a breaking, structural change to ObservationDefinition. It
should be accompanied by migration notes, and the WG should consider
whether to apply the same shape to
`NutritionOrder.enteralFormula.deliveryDevice` (re-opening
[FHIR-53386](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53386)) so the two
elements remain symmetric.

#### Justification

- Directly addresses Ralf Herzog's instance-level scenarios (one of N
  otherwise-identical lab instruments has the testkit / calibration
  needed) without forcing the implementer to either (a) duplicate the
  ObservationDefinition or (b) author and maintain a synthetic
  composite DeviceDefinition for what is fundamentally a per-instance
  property.
- The `0..* canonical(DeviceDefinition)` "fix" floated at the OO Nov 4
  meeting solves the cardinality problem but discards
  `Reference(Device)`, which the WG has not yet agreed to remove and
  which Ralf Herzog's use cases explicitly need.
- Adding `deviceCodeableConcept` to the choice gives a third widely-
  requested option (point at an unspecified instance of a device type)
  that the current resource cannot express at all.
- The `role` discriminator is an enhancement, not a regression — the
  prior `0..*` choice element could not express it either, so Option B
  strictly improves expressiveness over both today's `0..1` and the
  pre-FHIR-39389 `0..*`.
- Trade-off vs. A: this is more invasive, breaks existing
  ObservationDefinition instances that populate `device[x]`, and adds a
  new BackboneElement to author tooling. Implementers will pay a real
  cost; the WG must judge whether the documented use cases warrant it.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (with redirect)

#### Proposal

Decline the request to restore `0..*` cardinality on
`ObservationDefinition.device`. Confirm `0..1` as the resolved state and
mark FHIR-53307 as "Persuasive with Modification" / "Applied" in
parallel with [FHIR-53386](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53386).
Redirect the use cases raised by Ralf Herzog (per-instance test
installation / testkit / calibration / QC state) to a separate ticket
proposing per-device state modelling either on `Device` itself or via
a dedicated capability resource — those concerns are about
*operational state of an instance*, not about which device a
*definition* nominally targets.

#### Justification

- The committee on Nov 4 already leaned toward accepting reduced
  cardinality on the NutritionOrder sibling, and FHIR-53386 has been
  applied; declining FHIR-53307 keeps the two resources symmetric.
- Ralf Herzog's scenarios are arguably out of scope for an
  ObservationDefinition, which is meant to describe "the measurement
  model of device or actual device used to produce observations of
  this type" (per the OO Nov 4 minutes), not to enumerate every lab
  instrument in a particular configuration. Conflating definition with
  per-instance operational state risks pulling complex stateful
  modelling into a definitional resource.
- The composite-`DeviceDefinition` pattern (rikim) plus authoring one
  ObservationDefinition per genuinely distinct device model already
  covers the design intent without a structural change.
- Avoids a breaking change to a resource that has just been published
  in R5 and is being adopted by IGs.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B — adopt the `deviceUsed` backbone with
`0..*` cardinality.

The committee record favors symmetry and the path of least disruption
(Disposition A), and that case is real: FHIR-53386 has already been
applied for the structurally identical NutritionOrder element, and
the choice-element invariant is one of FHIR's bedrock rules. However,
the substance of Ralf Herzog's Nov 18 comment is the strongest piece
of design evidence in the thread, and it lands directly on the
question the OO meeting explicitly asked him to come back with: are
there real scenarios where an ObservationDefinition needs to
reference a specific Device instance? His lab-instrument examples
(per-instrument testkit / calibration / QC state) are credible,
common, and not naturally expressible through `DeviceDefinition`
alone, even with rikim's composite pattern. Discarding
`Reference(Device)` (the OO-floated `0..* canonical(DeviceDefinition)`
fix) would foreclose those use cases, and accepting `0..1` keeps them
expressible only by ObservationDefinition duplication.

Disposition B keeps every modelling option already present
(Reference, canonical), adds the missing `CodeableConcept` choice,
restores `0..*` legitimately by moving the choice inside a backbone,
and introduces `role` to clarify alternatives vs. co-use — a
long-standing ambiguity that the prior `0..*` choice element also
failed to address. The cost is a one-time structural change to an R5
resource; the workgroup should weigh that against the recurring cost
to implementers of either authoring N parallel ObservationDefinitions
or maintaining synthetic composite DeviceDefinitions for what is
fundamentally an instance-level concern. If B is adopted,
[FHIR-53386](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53386) should be
re-opened and aligned so NutritionOrder and ObservationDefinition
continue to share a shape.
