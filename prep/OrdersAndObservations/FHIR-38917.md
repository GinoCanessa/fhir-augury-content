## Summary

The submitter, Elliot Silver, asks Orders & Observations to add an
example and/or normative guidance describing how a clinical
`Observation` produced by a device should be associated with the
`Observation`s that record the device's settings in effect at the time
of the clinical measurement. He notes that the community currently
holds a range of opinions: some implementers tie the clinical
Observation to its setting Observations via `Observation.hasMember`
(directly or through an aggregating "settings set" Observation), while
others record settings Observations independently (on change or on a
schedule) and reconstruct the in-effect settings retrospectively from
the most recent prior settings Observations. He further notes
confusion between "device settings" (modeled as Observations linked
through `DeviceMetric` to `Device`) and `Device.property` /
`DeviceDefinition.property`, and asks for an example that ties these
together — ideally one that starts from a clinical Observation and
walks out to the relevant settings Observations.

The ticket has had an eventful disposition history. It was first moved
to "Considered for Future Use" on 2023-02-13 with a motion linking it
to [FHIR-40288](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-40288) ("Add a device
module with landing page") so the guidance could live on that new
module page. FHIR-40288 has since been Applied (Persuasive, resolved
2025-03-18), so a Device module landing page now exists. A subsequent
motion on 2025-10-27 again deferred FHIR-38917 to "Considered for
Future Use" (5-0-0), but on 2026-02-02 the workgroup voted 11-0-1
(David Barwin / Riki Merrick) to **reopen** the ticket — putting it
back on the table for active disposition.

## Details

**Description:**

There seems to be a range of opinions on how to associate clinical
observations generated by devices with the settings in effect on the
device at the time the clinical observation was made. Associating the
device settings and clinical observations is important to provide
context for the clinical observations, and to provide historical
evidence.

Some have suggested that the clinical observation should use
`hasMember` relationships to the settings observations (either directly
or indirectly through a "settings set" observation). This has the
advantage of unambiguously tying the clinical observation to the
settings in effect.

Others have suggested simply recording the settings observations
either when they change, or at defined times (daily at noon, at the
start of a procedure, etc.) and recording the clinical observations
without regard for the settings; the settings in effect for any
clinical observation can be determined by finding the most recent
setting observations (for each of the device metrics) prior to the
clinical observation. This approach doesn't laden the clinical
observations with concern over the settings observations; but makes
it more difficult to detect a missing/deleted setting.

There is also some confusion about the difference between device
settings (recorded as Observations linked to DeviceMetrics linked to
Devices) and device properties, and in general how to record device
settings.

An example or guidance that ties these all together, perhaps starting
with a clinical Observation linking to settings Observations, would
help provide a common approach to these challenges.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a worked example and accompanying narrative to the Device module
(the page introduced by FHIR-40288) that demonstrates the **explicit-
linkage** pattern: a clinical `Observation` whose `hasMember` (or
`derivedFrom`) references one or more settings `Observation`s, each
of which links via `Observation.device` to a `DeviceMetric`, which in
turn links via `DeviceMetric.parent` to the parent `Device`. The
example would also show an aggregating "settings-set" Observation
variant for situations with many settings. The narrative would (a)
state when to use each pattern, (b) draw the line between "device
settings recorded as Observations" and `Device.property` /
`DeviceDefinition.property` (out-of-band, design-time vs. operational
data), and (c) include a JSON example bundle. No resource definitions
or invariants change — this is module-page guidance plus example
artifacts.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the submitter asked for, what the 2023-02-13 and
2025-10-27 motions both anticipated, and what the 2025-07-10 review
comment was looking for on the updated module page. FHIR-40288
already created the host surface (the Device module landing page is
Applied), so the change is additive and low-risk: it does not touch
schemas, terminology bindings, or invariants. Providing a single,
canonical worked example is consistent with how other FHIR modules
(e.g., Workflow, Medications) disambiguate competing valid patterns
without forcing one mechanically. The 2026-02-02 reopen vote (11-0-1)
strongly indicates the workgroup believes the module page does not
yet contain this guidance, so the gap the submitter identified is
still real.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Address the underlying need with a **two-part deliverable** rather
than a single example: (1) on the Device module page, document **both**
patterns the submitter described — explicit linkage via
`Observation.hasMember` / a settings-set Observation, **and** the
implicit "look-up most recent settings Observation per metric"
pattern — with an explicit decision matrix (e.g., "use the explicit
pattern when missing-settings detection matters or when the data
crosses a system boundary; use the implicit pattern for high-rate
intra-system streams where the settings change rarely"); and (2)
publish a small, standalone **ExampleScenario** (folding in
[FHIR-38712](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-38712)) that walks both
patterns end-to-end. Optionally also clarify on the
`Observation.device` element page that `DeviceMetric` is the expected
target for granular settings observations.

#### Justification

The Zulip discussion explicitly bracketed FHIR-38917 with FHIR-38712
("Create ExampleScenarios for the Device resources"), and the
submitter's description openly notes that **both** patterns are in
use in the field with legitimate trade-offs. Picking only one
(Disposition A) risks declaring valid implementations non-conformant.
A documented decision matrix preserves implementer choice while
removing the ambiguity that motivated the ticket; an ExampleScenario
makes the patterns testable and renderable, which is more durable
than prose. This approach is moderately more work than A but
materially better than A at retiring the ambiguity that has kept the
ticket open across two ballot cycles.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket "Not Persuasive — Already Addressed" on the basis
that FHIR-40288 (Applied 2025-03-18) introduced the Device module
landing page, which is the surface FHIR-38917's prior dispositions
identified as the home for this guidance. Direct readers to that
page and close.

#### Justification

The 2023-02-13 and 2025-10-27 motions both treated FHIR-38917 as
satisfied-by-FHIR-40288 in principle. If the workgroup judges that
the module page now contains sufficient guidance — or that adding a
canonical example would over-prescribe one of two equally valid
patterns — declining is defensible. This option also avoids the
asymmetric risk of an ExampleScenario or canonical example being
read as a normative requirement after the fact.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The 2026-02-02 reopen (11-0-1) is dispositive against Disposition C —
the workgroup has just affirmed, by a wide margin, that the current
Device module page does **not** sufficiently address the submitter's
question, so "already addressed" is no longer credible. Between A
and B, the submitter's own description is explicit that two patterns
are in legitimate use, each with real trade-offs (missing-settings
detection vs. payload-weight on clinical Observations); endorsing
only one (Disposition A) would silently invalidate one of two
existing implementer practices and is likely to generate follow-on
tickets. Disposition B costs only the incremental authoring of a
decision matrix and an ExampleScenario over Disposition A, while
also tidily folding in the related FHIR-38712 request from the same
guidance bucket of the 2023-01 WGM tracker review. It is the
disposition most likely to actually retire FHIR-38917 (and
FHIR-39244) in the R6 cycle and remove the `Pending-SME-Input` label.
