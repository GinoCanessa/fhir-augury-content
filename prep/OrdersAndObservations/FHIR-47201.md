## Summary

FHIR-47201 is a short, comment-type ticket filed by Giorgio Cangioli against
the HL7 Europe **Laboratory Report (FHIR)** specification. It is not a defect
or change request: it is a *STU-note style endorsement* of an existing design
approach. Specifically, the reporter recommends that the IG **continue using a
FHIR "document" Bundle that contains both a Composition and a DiagnosticReport**
to represent a laboratory report, on the grounds that this maintains
consistency with the established European FHIR document pattern already in use
across other EU IGs (e.g. IPS-style documents, prior EU lab work).

No other Jira tickets were returned by the cross-reference index for this key,
and no GitHub PR/issue/commit was linked. The ticket therefore stands on its
own as a feedback comment to be considered alongside the broader, ongoing
"results grouping" debate in O&O and the EU community (see the Zulip threads
below) when the workgroup decides whether to retain, change, or refine the
Composition + DiagnosticReport design in the Laboratory Report IG.

## Proposed Dispositions

Because the ticket is itself a *Comment-type* STU note (an endorsement,
not a change request), the dispositions below are framed as the workgroup's
options for how to **act on the feedback** in the IG.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat FHIR-47201 as a confirmation that the EU Laboratory Report design
(FHIR Document Bundle = Composition + DiagnosticReport + Observations) is
correct as drafted, and explicitly **lock that pattern into the next STU
update** of the Laboratory Report (FHIR) IG. Concretely, in the Lab Report
IG narrative:

- Retain `Bundle` (type = `document`) as the top-level container.
- Retain `Composition` as the first entry, with structured `section` blocks
  carrying narrative + entries.
- Retain `DiagnosticReport` as a referenced entry that aggregates the lab
  Observations, so consumers that traverse from either Composition (clinical
  document view) or DiagnosticReport (lab-system view) get a consistent
  picture.
- Add an explicit "Design rationale" subsection to the IG home page citing
  this ticket and the EU/IPS interop consistency argument.

#### Justification

This is the cheapest, lowest-risk disposition and matches what the EU IG
authors (including the reporter) say is already deployed. The
[europe > EU Laboratory Report and IPS](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/europe/topic/EU%20Laboratory%20Report%20and%20IPS)
thread shows that downstream consumers (national labs, IPS aligners) expect
*both* Composition and DiagnosticReport to be present, and removing either
would break already-deployed implementations. Endorsing the current design
also closes the ticket without re-litigating a design choice the EU
community has already converged on.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the underlying *intent* of the comment (preserve EU/IPS consistency)
but harmonise the design with the parallel O&O work on grouping. Rather than
silently locking the current design, the IG would:

- Keep Composition + DiagnosticReport in the document Bundle, **but**
  publish a normative-direction profile that explicitly defines *which*
  resource is the grouper for which scenario:
  - Composition.section is the grouper for **clinical organisation** of
    the report (e.g., Chemistry, Microbiology sections).
  - DiagnosticReport.result groups the **issued lab result set** as a
    whole.
  - A parent `Observation` with `hasMember` (the pattern Cangioli
    confirms in the
    [MyHealth EU LRR](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/europe/topic/MyHealth%20EU%20Laboratory%20Result%20Report)
    thread) is the grouper for **panels** below the report level.
- Add cross-references / mapping tables to the AU Base Pathology and IPS
  profiles so that implementers can move between the patterns without
  ambiguity.

#### Justification

This addresses the same EU-consistency goal Disposition A does, but it
also resolves the open question raised by the Australian and US members in
the [DiagnosticReport vs. Composition](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/Orders%20and%20Observation%20WG/topic/DiagnosticReport%20vs.%20Composition)
thread — namely, that "use both" is only safe if the IG nails down *what
each one is for*. A bare endorsement (Disposition A) leaves the
DiagnosticReport-vs-Composition redundancy unresolved, which the Zulip
record shows has been a recurring source of confusion for at least two
years. Disposition B keeps the EU design while removing the ambiguity.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline to take any spec action on FHIR-47201 on the grounds that it is a
**comment, not a change request**: there is no defect, no proposed wording,
no missing artifact. Record the comment as community feedback in the
ticket's closing note, mark it `Persuasive – No Change`, and let the
substantive design discussion continue under the existing
`results-grouping` tickets in the Lab Report (FHIR) backlog.

#### Justification

The ticket adds a single sentence ("keep the current design") and supplies
no normative text or behavioural test. Closing it as `Persuasive – No
Change` is the standard HL7 disposition for unsolicited endorsements, and
it avoids prematurely freezing the design ahead of the broader O&O / EU
results-grouping work that is still active on Zulip. If the workgroup
wants to formalise the EU design, it should do so on a dedicated change
request (where wording, examples and conformance can be reviewed
properly), not on a free-text comment ticket.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The reporter is right that the EU design is well-established and should
not be casually dropped, so a flat `Decline` (Disposition C) under-uses
the signal even if it is procedurally clean. But Disposition A — simply
"keep what we have" — is also too small: the Zulip record from
2022 through early 2025 (`DiagnosticReport vs. Composition`,
`EU Laboratory Report and IPS`, `MyHealth EU Laboratory Result Report`)
shows that the *real* friction is not whether to use both Composition and
DiagnosticReport, but **what each one is responsible for grouping** —
clinical sectioning, issued-report grouping, or panel grouping. Disposition
B preserves the EU pattern Cangioli is endorsing *and* resolves the
ambiguity that has driven implementer questions, with the additional
benefit of giving IPS / AU Base profile authors a clear mapping target.
The workgroup should adopt B and use FHIR-47201 as the trigger to commit
the resulting clarification to the next STU update.
