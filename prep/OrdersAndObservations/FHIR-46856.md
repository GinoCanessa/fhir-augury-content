## Summary

The reporter requests that the "Navigating the profiles" section of the
Laboratory Report (FHIR) IG be updated so the diagram of inter-profile
relationships visually communicates which links are **optional** versus
**required**. As drawn today, the diagram implies that a Composition
profile MUST always be used to wrap a Laboratory Report, whereas the
IG's design intent is to permit — but not mandate — the Composition
wrapper. Specifically, the edge between **Composition: Laboratory
Report** and **DiagnosticReport: Laboratory Report** should be marked
optional in both directions. Other relationships that represent
choice (e.g., animal vs. person subject, or elements that may not
always be present) are noted as less critical but would also benefit
from optionality cues, provided the diagram does not become unreadable.
The cross-reference index returned no related Jira tickets, Zulip
threads, or GitHub items for this issue.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the "Navigating the profiles" page of the Laboratory Report
(FHIR) IG so the profile-relationship diagram explicitly distinguishes
optional from required links. Concretely:

1. Re-render the diagram (PlantUML / draw.io / SVG, whichever is the
   IG's source format) with two distinct edge styles — for example
   **solid arrows** for required links and **dashed arrows** for
   optional links — and add a legend on the same page that explains the
   convention.
2. Mark the edge between **Composition: Laboratory Report** and
   **DiagnosticReport: Laboratory Report** as optional in both
   directions, since the design allows but does not require the
   Composition wrapper.
3. Apply the same optional styling to other relationships that
   represent design-time choices (e.g., subject of type Patient vs.
   non-human/animal subject, observations that may or may not be
   present), but only where doing so does not materially clutter the
   diagram.
4. Add a short paragraph above or below the diagram restating in prose
   that the Composition profile is offered as an option for clients
   that need a document-style packaging, and that the DiagnosticReport
   profile is sufficient on its own.

#### Justification

The change is purely editorial / narrative: it does not alter any
StructureDefinition, cardinality, binding, or invariant. It directly
addresses a documented mismatch between the diagram and the IG's stated
design intent (Composition is permitted, not mandatory). Visual
distinction of optional vs. required relationships is a well-established
documentation pattern (used, for example, in the FHIR base spec resource
relationship diagrams and in IGs such as US Core), so implementers will
have a familiar mental model. Because no normative artifact changes, the
risk of breaking existing implementations is effectively zero, and the
fix lifts a real source of confusion for new readers of the IG.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than re-styling the existing single diagram, replace the current
"Navigating the profiles" diagram with **two complementary views**:

1. A **minimum-conformance view** showing only the required profiles
   and the required edges between them (essentially: the
   DiagnosticReport: Laboratory Report profile and its mandatory
   targets).
2. An **extended/optional view** layering in the optional profiles
   (Composition: Laboratory Report, optional Observations, alternate
   subject types, etc.) with a clear "optional" callout.

Pair the two diagrams with a short table that lists each profile-to-
profile reference and labels it `Required` / `Optional` / `Choice`,
plus a one-line rationale for each. Keep the existing single combined
diagram only if reviewers feel removing it would lose useful overview.

#### Justification

This addresses the underlying need — making the Composition wrapper's
optionality unmistakable — and goes further by separating the "must
implement" surface from the "may implement" surface, which is typically
the first question a new implementer asks. A table of edges complements
the diagram for accessibility (screen-reader friendly) and gives
reviewers an unambiguous, citable reference when arguing about specific
relationships in future tickets. The trade-off vs. Disposition A is
extra authoring/maintenance effort and a slightly larger page; the
benefit is that the IG's conformance surface becomes self-evident
without relying solely on diagram conventions.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change on the grounds that the formal optionality of every
profile-to-profile reference is already captured authoritatively in the
StructureDefinitions (cardinality, `mustSupport`, and reference target
profiles), and that the "Navigating the profiles" diagram is intended
as an at-a-glance topology map rather than a normative cardinality
chart. Add at most a one-sentence note above the existing diagram
clarifying that "edges in this diagram show possible relationships;
consult the individual profiles for cardinality and conformance."

#### Justification

Diagrams that try to encode cardinality/optionality in addition to
topology can become harder to read, not easier — particularly when the
reader must learn a per-IG legend. The conformance source of truth is
the StructureDefinition, and reviewers/implementers are expected to
consult it. A minimal narrative disclaimer is cheaper to maintain and
avoids re-rendering the diagram every time the profile graph changes.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter is not asking for a normative change; they are flagging a
genuine discoverability bug in the IG narrative where the diagram
contradicts the IG's own design intent ("Composition is allowed, not
required"). Disposition A fixes exactly that, with minimal effort,
no normative impact, and a familiar documentation idiom (solid =
required, dashed = optional, plus a legend). Disposition B is a more
ambitious rework that may be worth doing eventually, but it is larger
in scope than what the ticket asks for and can be tracked separately
if the workgroup wants the additional table/dual-view treatment.
Disposition C is the weakest option here — pointing readers back to
StructureDefinitions does not address the fact that the *current*
diagram actively misleads them about the Composition wrapper. The
workgroup should accept the change, scope it to the "Navigating the
profiles" page only, and require that the optional/required convention
be documented in an adjacent legend so the diagram remains
self-explanatory.
