## Summary

Kevin O'Donnell requests a way to record the degree of confidence/certainty
that a clinical condition is **absent**, motivated by radiology reports
(and other diagnostic processes) that frequently make assertions of
absence with explicit confidence — sometimes as the primary purpose of
the imaging study (rule-out exams). The request specifically calls for
support of both qualitative scales (low / moderate / high confidence)
and quantitative values (e.g. 80% likely), citing the
[Diagnostic Certainty Scale (RAD-ASSIST)](https://rad.bwh.harvard.edu/diagnostic-certainty-scale/)
as an exemplar, and notes that AI models and other clinical
applications increasingly emit certainty metrics that need a place to
land in FHIR.

The ticket is the "absent" companion to
[FHIR-54931](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54931) (Patient Care),
which asks the same question for the **present** case and would be
addressed on `Condition`. Because the reporter notes that absence of a
condition is conventionally encoded on `Observation` (rather than
`Condition`, except for the refuted/resolved/inactive/in-remission/
entered-in-error cases), this ticket lands in O&O and the change would
attach to `Observation` (or an extension thereon) rather than to
`Condition`. Patient Care's preliminary discussion on FHIR-54931
(2026-03-12) leaned toward an **extension** with a numeric value or
quintile code, and explicitly suggested that the diagnostic-report-side
representation may belong with O&O — i.e. it foreshadows this ticket.

## Details

**Description:**

> Radiology reports (and presumably other diagnostic processes) often
> make assertions of the absence of a condition that include a degree
> of certainty/confidence. Some imaging is specifically done with the
> primary goal of ruling out some condition or other.
>
> There needs to be a way to record those degrees of certainty.
> Consider allowing qualitative (low confidence, moderate, high, etc)
> and quantitative (80% likely) values. E.g.
> [Diagnostic Certainty Scale - RAD-ASSIST](https://rad.bwh.harvard.edu/diagnostic-certainty-scale/)
>
> AI Models and other Clinical applications may also output certainty
> metrics.
>
> This JIRA relates to
> [FHIR-54931](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54931) Support degree
> of confidence/certainty that a Condition is present. Presence of a
> condition is encoded as a Condition while absence of a condition is
> encoded as an Observation (unless it's refuted, resolved, inactive,
> in remission, or entered-in-error).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add first-class support on **`Observation`** for representing the
confidence/certainty of an absent finding, mirroring the pattern PC is
exploring on `Condition` for present findings (FHIR-54931). Concretely:

1. Define a standard FHIR extension
   `http://hl7.org/fhir/StructureDefinition/observation-certainty`
   with `value[x]` of either `CodeableConcept` (bound preferred to a
   new value set `observation-certainty-qualitative`, seeded with the
   five-point RAD-ASSIST scale: *highly likely absent*, *likely
   absent*, *indeterminate*, *unlikely absent*, *highly unlikely
   absent*, plus suitable inverses for "present" use) **or**
   `Quantity` constrained to UCUM `%` for probability values
   (0–100), **or** an ordinal `integer` for differential ranking.
2. Permit the extension at `Observation` and at
   `Observation.component` so that multi-finding reports (one
   `Observation` per ruled-out condition, or one `Observation` with
   a component per finding) can both carry per-finding certainty.
3. Co-define the same extension shape so it is also usable on
   `Condition` (one StructureDefinition, two contexts), giving
   FHIR-54931 a coordinated answer if PC concurs.
4. Add a short narrative section in the `Observation` resource page
   ("Recording the certainty of an absent finding") that points
   implementers at the extension and shows an example using the
   absent-finding pattern (`Observation.code` = the absent finding,
   `Observation.value` boolean/CodeableConcept = "absent", plus the
   new certainty extension).

#### Justification

This matches the explicit ask in the ticket (qualitative *and*
quantitative scales), preserves O&O's established convention of
recording absent findings on `Observation`, and avoids overloading
`Observation.interpretation` (which is bound to a value set whose
semantics are about clinical interpretation of measured values, not
epistemic confidence). Defining the extension once and re-using it on
`Condition` directly addresses Patient Care's 2026-03-12 conclusion
that "if adopted, it would be an extension" and that the
diagnostic-report side likely belongs with O&O — keeping the two
tickets aligned avoids divergent extensions on either side.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Do **not** mint an O&O-specific certainty mechanism. Instead, ask
implementers to use existing patterns:

- **`Observation.interpretation`** with codes drawn from (or added
  to) `v3-ObservationInterpretation` to flag confidence bands, *or*
- **`Observation.method`** to record that the finding is the output
  of a probabilistic model, *or*
- a `component` whose `code` is "probability of finding" and whose
  `valueQuantity` is `%`.

In parallel, defer to the cross-resource work on representing AI/ML
model output (which has its own emerging patterns for model
provenance and confidence) rather than creating a parallel certainty
construct now.

#### Justification

`Observation` is deliberately flexible; many implementers have
already shoehorned certainty into `interpretation`, `method`, or a
probability component. A new extension risks fragmenting the
ecosystem just as ML-output guidance is stabilising. This option
keeps the spec lean, lets niche use cases be handled by IGs (e.g.
imaging IGs, AI IGs) where the value sets can be tuned to the
domain, and avoids committing the base spec to a certainty model
before consensus on the qualitative scale exists.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline FHIR-54933 on the basis that **representing certainty of an
absent finding is a domain-specific concern best handled in
implementation guides** (radiology, pathology, AI/ML output) rather
than in FHIR Core. Close as "not persuasive — out of base scope" and
direct interested parties to the imaging IGs and the AI/ML model-
output work for a tailored solution. PC could decline FHIR-54931 on
the same grounds for symmetry.

#### Justification

The 2026-03-12 PC discussion noted that this capability is "not
common functionality in existing EHRs," which raises the bar for
adding it to base FHIR. The few clinical contexts that do need it
(radiology rule-outs, AI inference output) are exactly the kinds of
specialised workflows IGs exist to serve, and an IG can constrain
both the qualitative scale and the quantitative units far more
prescriptively than base FHIR ever should. Adding a base extension
with a permissive `value[x]` invites inconsistent population.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket's underlying need is real and explicitly cross-cuts
radiology workflows and the rapidly growing class of AI-derived
findings, where confidence is a first-class signal — not metadata
that can comfortably be pushed entirely to IGs without fragmentation.
Disposition A is also the option that keeps O&O and PC in lockstep:
defining a single extension StructureDefinition usable on both
`Observation` and `Condition` directly satisfies PC's
2026-03-12 conclusion (an extension, with a numeric value or
quintile code) and gives the imaging community a stable hook today.
Disposition B's existing-mechanism approach has empirically failed
to coalesce — implementers picking between `interpretation`,
`method`, and ad-hoc components is the *current* state and is what
prompted this ticket. Disposition C is defensible only if PC also
declines FHIR-54931; pursuing it unilaterally on the absent side
while PC entertains the present side would create exactly the
asymmetry the reporter is trying to avoid. Recommend O&O accept,
coordinate the StructureDefinition with Patient Care ahead of the
April 23 PC discussion of FHIR-54931, and stage the value set work
against the RAD-ASSIST scale as the seed.
