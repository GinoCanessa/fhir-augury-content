## Summary

Brian Swinkels is asking the Orders & Observations work group to revisit
the *Cancelling and Modifying Orders* (a.k.a. *Cancelling and Modifying
Requests*) page in the Clinical Order Workflow (COW) IG. A prior first-ballot
ticket, [FHIR-50310](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50310), flagged that the
page contained "a great deal" of duplicative text; that ticket was resolved
*Persuasive with Modification* with only a small edit (removal of two
sentences before §6.5.2). The reporter believes that change did not address
the underlying feedback that the page is *confusing* — not merely redundant —
and points to a mockup branch (`BMS-Ballot-Updates` of `HL7/fhir-cow-ig`) that
restructures the narrative on cancellation and modification flows. The ask is
therefore an editorial / structural rewrite of the page rather than a
normative change to resources or constraints.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the structural rewrite shown on the
[`BMS-Ballot-Updates` mockup of `cancelling-and-modifying-requests.html`](https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/fhir-cow-ig/branches/BMS-Ballot-Updates/en/cancelling-and-modifying-requests.html)
into the COW IG's authoring source for that page (the `input/pagecontent/`
file backing `cancelling-and-modifying-requests.html` in `HL7/fhir-cow-ig`),
including the reorganised section headings and the worked Placer / Filler
flow descriptions, and resolve the ticket *Persuasive*. Carry forward the
cancellation-Task framing (cancellation expressed as a Task that the Filler
may accept or reject, with a successor request `replaces`-ing the original
where appropriate) raised on the Zulip thread, since the mockup is consistent
with that framing.

#### Justification

The reporter has already done the editorial work and shown the result on a
public branch, which lowers the cost of acceptance to a review-and-merge
exercise. FHIR-50310 was resolved with a two-sentence deletion that
demonstrably did not satisfy the underlying ballot comment, so a more
substantive rewrite is consistent with HL7's obligation to honour the
*intent* of persuasive ballot feedback rather than its narrowest literal
reading. Because this is editorial / narrative content (no resource,
profile, or constraint changes), the change is low-risk for implementers
and does not affect conformance.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the mockup as a starting point but treat it as an *agenda* rather
than a finished edit: open a focused review on the
*placer-decision-to-cancel-part-of-a-request* section first (the area
explicitly contested on Zulip), reach agreement on the cancellation-Task
vs. direct-ServiceRequest-cancellation guidance and on how partial
fulfilment is described (referencing `task.output` /
`task.businessStatus` / extensions as options without mandating any),
then merge the rewrite section by section. Track each section as a
sub-disposition / follow-up ticket so the workgroup can ballot-trace
which clarifications landed where.

#### Justification

The Zulip thread shows the rewrite touches genuinely contested design
ground (when, if ever, may a Placer cancel an in-flight ServiceRequest;
how much partial-execution detail belongs in the base IG). Bulk-accepting
the mockup risks codifying one contributor's interpretation on those
points. Splitting acceptance keeps the editorial-clarity wins (which
nobody has objected to) decoupled from the still-open semantic
questions, and gives reviewers a smaller diff to assess for each
substantive choice. It also provides a clean audit trail back to
FHIR-50310's "great deal of duplicative text" feedback, sub-comment
by sub-comment.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket *Not Persuasive* on the grounds that FHIR-50310 has
already been dispositioned and applied, and that a further rewrite of
the same page should be raised as new, scoped tickets identifying the
specific passages judged confusing rather than as a wholesale "improve
clarity" request backed by a mockup branch.

#### Justification

The ticket as written does not enumerate which passages are unclear or
why; it points at a mockup and asks reviewers to infer the deltas. That
makes ballot disposition hard to record cleanly and risks reopening
already-applied resolutions (the FHIR-50310 deletion before §6.5.2)
without explicit consent. A decline-with-guidance response — "please
refile as one or more sectioned tickets identifying the specific
clarity concerns" — preserves the workgroup's ability to evaluate each
change on its merits.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B captures what the reporter is actually asking for (better
clarity on a page where prior ballot feedback was under-addressed) while
respecting the Zulip-surfaced reality that parts of the rewrite encode
non-trivial design choices about cancellation semantics and partial-
fulfilment reporting. Disposition A would close the loop fastest, but
risks merging Brian's interpretation of the cancellation-Task pathway and
of partial-execution disclosure before Ralf's concerns (and any others not
yet voiced) have been resolved. Disposition C is technically defensible
but unsatisfying: the original ballot comment really wasn't fully
addressed, and bouncing the ticket back for re-filing wastes the editorial
work already done on the mockup. A sectioned acceptance — editorial wins
landed quickly, contested semantics resolved deliberately — gives the
workgroup the best of both: visible progress on the clarity complaint
and a clean disposition trail for the harder questions.
