## Summary

Ulrike Merrick proposes expanding the "Boundaries and Relationships" section of
the SpecimenDefinition resource page so that it more completely describes how
SpecimenDefinition is used by other FHIR resources. Specifically, she asks for
two new bullets: one stating that ObservationDefinition references
SpecimenDefinition to indicate which kind(s) of specimen are needed for a
given type of observation, and one stating that SpecimenDefinition can also be
referenced for planning purposes by ActivityDefinition, PlanDefinition, and
RequestOrchestration. The cross-reference index returned no related Jira
tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items for this issue, so it stands on its
own; however, the OO comment thread on 2026-03-20 noted that the existing
SpecimenDefinition page still references the Catalog resource, which no longer
exists in FHIR R6, and the in-call draft disposition therefore extends the
scope to also remove the Catalog bullet and to add explicit hyperlinks to the
SubstanceDefinition entry. The resolution recorded on the ticket is
"Persuasive with Modification" reflecting that broader scope, and the ticket
carries the `SpecimenBlockVote` label for ballot reconciliation.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the SpecimenDefinition resource's "Boundaries and Relationships"
narrative, append exactly the two bullets supplied by the reporter:

1. *The SpecimenDefinition resource is referenced by the
   [ObservationDefinition](observationdefinition.html#ObservationDefinition)
   to indicate what kind(s) of specimen are needed for this type of
   observation.*
2. *The SpecimenDefinition resource can also be referenced for planning
   purposes by the
   [ActivityDefinition](activitydefinition.html#ActivityDefinition),
   [PlanDefinition](plandefinition.html#PlanDefinition), and
   [RequestOrchestration](requestorchestration.html#RequestOrchestration).*

Make no other changes. Leave the existing Catalog and SubstanceDefinition
bullets untouched.

#### Justification

This is the literal request and is consistent with how other definitional
resources document their inbound references in their Boundaries and
Relationships sections. The bullets are factually correct: ObservationDefinition
already includes a reference to SpecimenDefinition for required specimen
types, and the planning resources (ActivityDefinition, PlanDefinition,
RequestOrchestration) all permit references to definitional artifacts.
Accepting as-requested is the lowest-risk option and avoids scope creep
that could delay the SpecimenBlockVote reconciliation.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach (Accept With Modification)

#### Proposal

Implement the reporter's two new bullets, **and** also:

1. **Remove** the existing bullet that reads roughly: *"The SpecimenDefinition
   resource is referenced by the Catalog resource, which represents a whole
   catalog, to enable the creation and maintenance of kinds of specimens
   within a catalog of orderable diagnostic services."* — Catalog is no
   longer a FHIR resource in R6, so the bullet is stale and misleading.
2. **Update** the existing SubstanceDefinition bullet so that the resource
   name is rendered as a hyperlink to `substancedefinition.html` (matching
   the linking style of the new bullets).

The end-state of the Boundaries and Relationships list therefore covers
ObservationDefinition (new), the planning resources (new), and an updated,
properly linked SubstanceDefinition entry, with the obsolete Catalog
reference removed.

#### Justification

This mirrors the OO-call draft disposition recorded by JIRAUSER19407 on
2026-03-24 and matches the ticket's recorded resolution of "Persuasive
with Modification". The Catalog cleanup is both correct (Catalog was
removed from FHIR core) and necessary — leaving a dangling reference in
the SpecimenDefinition page would create a documentation defect at
publication. Linking SubstanceDefinition is a small consistency win that
costs nothing and benefits readers. Bundling these adjacent narrative
fixes into one PR also avoids re-opening the same paragraph in a later
ticket. Trade-off versus Disposition A: marginally larger scope and a
second reviewer pass on the Catalog removal, but no schema or invariant
impact.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as Not Persuasive on the grounds that the relationships
between SpecimenDefinition and the listed resources are already discoverable
via the auto-generated "Resources that use this resource" cross-reference
table at the bottom of every resource page, so additional manually-curated
prose duplicates information that the publisher already produces.

#### Justification

This is defensible only if the work group is unwilling to accept any
narrative changes during ballot reconciliation and is comfortable with
implementers relying on the auto-generated cross-reference tables alone.
However, the existing Boundaries and Relationships section already
contains hand-written bullets for SubstanceDefinition and (incorrectly)
Catalog, so the page sets a precedent for narrative coverage of these
relationships; declining now would leave that section both incomplete
and partly stale.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the right call. It honours the reporter's request in full,
incorporates the cleanup that the OO call already identified as necessary
(Catalog no longer exists, so the dangling bullet must go), and harmonises
the link style across the bulleted list. It also matches the ticket's
existing resolution of "Persuasive with Modification" and the draft
disposition posted by the work group lead on 2026-03-24, so adopting B
keeps the ticket aligned with the SpecimenBlockVote reconciliation
narrative without re-litigating decisions already taken on the call. The
change is narrative-only, has no impact on StructureDefinitions,
invariants, or examples, and can ship as a single small PR against the
SpecimenDefinition page in `HL7/fhir`.
