## Summary

The reporter requests that the cardinality of the `DiagnosticReport`
reference within the UV Laboratory Report IG's `Composition-uv-lab`
profile be changed from `1..1` to `1..*`. The use case is anatomic
pathology / complex specimens where the workflow historically (in
HL7 v2.5.1) carries two distinct reports — a **gross examination**
section and a **pathologist's report** — for the same specimen, each
with its own ORC/OBR/OBX trio. Under the current UV Lab profile, only
one DiagnosticReport may be referenced from the Composition, which
forces these two clinically-related reports into separate Document
Bundles, breaking the clinical context that has historically traveled
together in a single message. The reporter explicitly considered
relaxing the cardinality at the `Bundle-uv-lab` level instead, but
rejects that option because the gross and pathologist report belong in
the same document for clinical interpretation.

No related Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were surfaced
through cross-references for this ticket.

## Details

**Description:**

> How should complex specimens with separate reports be handled?
> Typically in V2.5.1 we put a gross section and pathologist report in
> 2 separate OBRs and ORCs, in the current UV lab it looks like we'd
> need 2 separate documents.
> E.g. the HL7 V2.5.1 structure would be:
>
> ```
> MSH
> PID
> PV1
> ORC Gross
> OBR Gross
> OBXs
> ORC Pathologist report
> OBR Pathologist report
> OBXs
> ```
>
> Proposed change: Make DiagnosticReport `1..*` in
> [Composition](https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/uv-lab-rep-ig/StructureDefinition-Composition-uv-lab.html)
>
> So that both DiagnosticReports can be in the same Document Bundle.
>
> Alternatively this cardinality could be changed at the
> [bundle](https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/uv-lab-rep-ig/StructureDefinition-Bundle-uv-lab.html)
> level, however I don't think that would work as well because the
> Gross and the pathologist report SHOULD be in the same message for
> clinical context.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the UV Laboratory Report IG, change the cardinality of the
`DiagnosticReport` entry within `Composition-uv-lab` from `1..1` to
`1..*`. Concretely, in `StructureDefinition-Composition-uv-lab`, locate
the `Composition.section` slice that constrains `entry` to a
`DiagnosticReport` (the laboratory report section) and update its
`min`/`max` from `1`/`1` to `1`/`*`. Add narrative guidance to the
profile and to the IG's "Composing a Lab Report" / pathology guidance
page explaining that multiple `DiagnosticReport` entries in this
section SHOULD represent clinically-related reports for the same
specimen / encounter (e.g., the gross examination and the pathologist's
interpretive report) that belong together in a single Document Bundle,
and that systems receiving multiple entries SHALL treat them as a
single clinical package. Examples in the IG should be extended with a
gross + pathologist anatomic-pathology example mirroring the v2.5.1
ORC/OBR/OBX layering described in the ticket.

#### Justification

The change is minimally invasive (one cardinality relaxation, plus
guidance and an example) and is non-breaking for existing
implementations: any conformant single-report Composition remains
valid under `1..*`. It directly addresses a real interoperability gap
between v2.5.1 anatomic-pathology messaging and the current UV Lab
Report IG, which the reporter flags as forcing a one-document-per-report
workflow that loses clinical context. Carrying gross + pathologist
together in a single document is consistent with how these reports are
authored, signed, and interpreted in practice, and with the FHIR design
principle that a Composition represents a single attested clinical
document — here, the complete laboratory report for the specimen.
Relaxing at the Composition level (rather than the Bundle level)
preserves the semantic that all entries within a section share the
section's coded type and clinical intent.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than relaxing `Composition.section.entry` cardinality on the
existing single laboratory-report section, model anatomic-pathology
multi-report use cases via **section composition**: keep the primary
laboratory `DiagnosticReport` section at `1..1`, and either (a) add an
optional sub-section (`Composition.section.section`) under the lab
report section dedicated to a "Gross Examination" `DiagnosticReport`,
or (b) introduce a separate optional sibling section coded for "Gross
Examination" with `entry` to a `DiagnosticReport`, with `min=0 max=1`.
Provide a coded section type (LOINC, e.g., a gross-description
document/section code) and update the IG's section-type value set
accordingly. Document and example as in Disposition A.

#### Justification

This preserves a clear, typed structural distinction between the
pathologist's interpretive report and the gross examination, which
some downstream consumers (CDR ingestion, viewers, decision support)
may rely on to render or route the two reports differently. Coded
sections are a standard FHIR Composition pattern for exactly this kind
of "related but distinct" clinical content and avoid the ambiguity of
"why are there two unordered DiagnosticReports in this section?" that
Disposition A introduces. The trade-off is a larger profile change
(new section slice, value-set extension, new examples) and additional
authoring burden, and it is a slightly larger lift for senders who
already produce a single combined report. It is, however, more
explicit about clinical intent than a bare cardinality bump.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the cardinality change. Recommend that complex anatomic-
pathology workflows continue to model gross and pathologist reports as
**two separate Document Bundles**, optionally linked via
`Composition.relatesTo` (e.g., `appends` / `transforms` /
`replaces`-style relationships) or via a shared
`DiagnosticReport.basedOn` / `Specimen` reference. Close as "Persuasive
with mod — use existing linkage mechanisms" or "Not Persuasive" if the
workgroup judges the existing single-report-per-document model
sufficient.

#### Justification

The "one Composition = one attested clinical document" pattern is a
deliberate FHIR design choice, and Composition.relatesTo plus shared
Specimen/ServiceRequest references already provide a standards-based
mechanism for tying related reports together without changing profile
cardinality. Declining avoids opening the door to ambiguous semantics
("which DiagnosticReport in the section is *the* report?") and avoids
the maintenance burden of rewriting examples and downstream profile
expectations. It also keeps the UV Lab Report IG aligned with the
broader IPS/IPA pattern of single-report documents.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter has described a concrete, well-established v2.5.1
workflow (gross + pathologist OBR/OBX layering for anatomic pathology
on the same specimen) that the current UV Lab Report IG cannot
represent in a single Document Bundle, and they have already
considered and explained why the Bundle-level relaxation alternative
is clinically wrong. Disposition A is non-breaking, minimally invasive,
and preserves the "one document, one clinical event" semantics that
matter for anatomic pathology — the gross and pathologist report are
two views of the same specimen examination and clinicians expect them
together. Disposition B is defensible and arguably cleaner, but the
extra structural machinery (new coded sub-section / sibling section,
value-set work) is heavier than the problem warrants and will slow
adoption; if the workgroup prefers explicit typing, B can be folded in
later as an optional refinement on top of A. Disposition C is hard to
defend given the reporter's explicit clinical argument that gross and
pathologist reports must travel together for interpretation, and
relatesTo across separate Bundles introduces fragility (lost links,
out-of-order delivery) that a single Document Bundle avoids.
