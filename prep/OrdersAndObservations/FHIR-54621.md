## Summary

Eric Haas asks Orders & Observations to revisit the R6 cardinality of
`Procedure.bodyStructure`, which was tightened to `0..1` (down from R4
`Procedure.bodySite` `0..*`), and to restore it to `0..*`. The submitter
characterizes the tightening as a significant capability reduction that
breaks bilateral procedures (e.g., both knees, both eyes), multi-site
biopsies, and procedures spanning multiple anatomical regions, and notes
that the resource's own Requirements text still alludes to multiple sites
— an internal inconsistency with the `max=1` constraint. The ticket also
recommends removing the `procedure-targetBodyStructure` extension's
ServiceRequest context, on the grounds that the extension becomes
redundant once `Procedure.bodyStructure` is `0..*`.

This request reopens a long-running cross-WG debate captured in
[FHIR-41690](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-41690) (Patient Care,
Resolved – No Change / Not Persuasive),
[FHIR-48110](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48110) (Orders &
Observations, Applied / Not Persuasive with Modification), and
[FHIR-51792](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51792) (Patient Care,
Applied / Persuasive). The prior O&O disposition on FHIR-48110 kept
`ServiceRequest.bodyStructure` at `0..1` and instead expanded the
`BodyStructure` resource to convey multiple morphologies in a single
instance; FHIR-51792 then realigned `Procedure.bodyStructure` back to
`0..1` to match. Companion ticket
[FHIR-54614](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54614) (Patient Care,
Triaged), filed by the same submitter on the same day, attacks the
related `prc-1` constraint ("bodyStructure SHALL only be present if
Procedure.bodySite is not present") and proposes collapsing `bodySite`
and `bodyStructure` into a single `CodeableReference`. The two tickets
should be considered together because the cardinality and the
mutual-exclusivity constraint are entangled.

## Details

**Description:**

> *`bodyStructure` Cardinality Changed from 0..* to 0..1 (Breaking Change)*
>
> **R4:** `bodySite` was `0..*` (multiple body sites allowed)
>
> **R6:** `bodyStructure` is `0..1` (only single body structure)
>
> **Issues:**
>
> 1) This is a significant capability reduction; even the
>    `bodyStructure` requirements text alludes to multiple sites.
>
>    | Requirements | Knowing where the procedure is performed is important for tracking if multiple sites are possible. |
>    |---|---|
>
>    which illustrates an internal inconsistency with the `max=1`
>    constraint.
>
>    **Lost use cases:**
>    - Bilateral procedures (both knees, both eyes)
>    - Multi-site biopsies
>    - Procedures spanning multiple anatomical regions
>
> 2) The extension
>    [procedure-targetBodyStructure](https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/fhir-extensions/StructureDefinition-procedure-targetBodyStructure.html)
>    is redundant.
>
> **Recommendation:**
> 1. Change `bodyStructure` to `0..*`
> 2. Remove ServiceRequest context from the
>    [procedure-targetBodyStructure](https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/fhir-extensions/StructureDefinition-procedure-targetBodyStructure.html)
>    extension

**Comments:**

- **JIRAUSER19407 (2026-03-17):** "Note that a single BodyStructure
  instance can have multiple included structures." (i.e., the existing
  R6 design intentionally relies on `BodyStructure.includedStructure`
  to convey multi-site situations from a single reference — this is the
  design rationale for keeping the cardinality at `0..1` and is the
  central point the disposition must engage with.)

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the FHIR Core R6 `Procedure` resource, change the cardinality of
`Procedure.bodyStructure` from `0..1` to `0..*`. Update the element's
short/definition/comments text to describe multi-site usage (bilateral,
multi-site biopsy, multi-region) and remove or rewrite the `bodyStructure`
short/comments wording that currently implies a single target. Re-evaluate
the `prc-1` invariant ("bodyStructure SHALL only be present if
`Procedure.bodySite` is not present") in coordination with Patient Care
on FHIR-54614 — at minimum it must be rewritten so it does not implicitly
constrain to one site (e.g., drop it, or restate as guidance that the
same anatomical site SHOULD NOT be described both as a `bodySite` code
and as a `bodyStructure` reference). In the FHIR Extensions Pack,
remove `ServiceRequest` from the contexts of
`procedure-targetBodyStructure` (note the submitter actually means
"remove Procedure-only contexts that are now redundant"; align wording
with the actual change made). Coordinate with Patient Care because
`Procedure` is owned by PC, not O&O — FHIR-54621 should be re-routed or
co-dispositioned with PC, mirroring the FHIR-51792 / FHIR-41690 pattern.

#### Justification

Restores the R4 `bodySite` `0..*` modelling capacity and removes a
genuine breaking-change regression. The submitter's enumerated use
cases (bilateral procedures, multi-site biopsies, procedures spanning
multiple regions) are real, common clinical scenarios, and the existing
Requirements text on the element already documents the multi-site
expectation — closing that internal inconsistency is itself a
maintenance win. It also removes a pattern that the O&O R6 review
thread already flagged Procedure as out of step on (Brian Postlethwaite:
Procedure "still has `bodySite` and `bodyStructure`. Other places seem
to have moved to a `CodeableReference`"). The counter-argument captured
in the comment on this ticket — that `BodyStructure.includedStructure`
already lets one instance carry multiple structures — is true, but it
forces every implementation that wants per-site identifiers, per-site
images, or per-site morphology metadata to either (a) coin synthetic
parent BodyStructures or (b) lose the per-site identity, which is
exactly the radiotherapy concern Martin von Siebenthal raised on the PC
Zulip thread.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — Consolidate via CodeableReference

#### Proposal

Instead of widening `Procedure.bodyStructure` from `0..1` to `0..*` as a
standalone change, replace `Procedure.bodySite` (`CodeableConcept 0..*`)
and `Procedure.bodyStructure` (`Reference(BodyStructure) 0..1`) with a
single repeating element such as `Procedure.site :
CodeableReference(BodyStructure) 0..*`. Drop the `prc-1` invariant
(handled by FHIR-54614). Mark the legacy elements with a renaming
modifier-style tombstone for one cycle if needed. Retire (or
constraint-deprecate to "use the core element") the
`procedure-targetBodyStructure` extension and the `bodySite` extension's
`Procedure` context, since the consolidated repeating
`CodeableReference` covers their use cases. Coordinate with Patient
Care (resource owner) and Orders & Observations (BodyStructure owner),
since this is a joint disposition with FHIR-54614.

#### Justification

This is the direction Brian Postlethwaite already flagged on the O&O R6
review Zulip thread ("other places seem to have moved to a
`CodeableReference`") and the direction Eric Haas himself proposes in
the companion FHIR-54614. It addresses the *root* defect — the awkward
split between a coded list (`0..*`) and a structured reference (`0..1`)
gated by a mutual-exclusivity invariant — instead of just relaxing one
side of the split. It also makes the redundant
`procedure-targetBodyStructure` extension genuinely retirable, which
Disposition A only partially achieves. Trade-off: this is a larger
breaking change (removes/renames two elements rather than widening
one), it must be co-dispositioned with FHIR-54614 and probably
FHIR-51792, and it introduces new search-parameter / migration work for
implementers tracking the element rename across R5 → R6. Given that R6
is still in ballot and the BodyStructure design itself is being
reviewed, the appetite for that scope is plausible — but it should be
an explicit cross-WG decision, not a quiet edit.

---

### Disposition C: Decline — Not Persuasive

#### Proposal

Reject the request. Retain `Procedure.bodyStructure` at `0..1` and
retain the `prc-1` invariant. Document — directly in the
`Procedure.bodyStructure` definition / comments — that multi-site
scenarios (bilateral, multi-site, multi-region) are intended to be
expressed via `BodyStructure.includedStructure` on a single referenced
`BodyStructure`, with `Procedure.bodySite` `0..*` available for
purely-coded multi-site cases that don't need a structured target. Do
not change the `procedure-targetBodyStructure` extension contexts.

#### Justification

This is the *current* disposition the workgroups already converged on,
expressed in FHIR-48110 (O&O Applied / Not Persuasive with Modification)
and re-confirmed in FHIR-51792 (PC Applied / Persuasive). The design
rationale — "a single BodyStructure instance can have multiple included
structures" — is exactly the comment posted on FHIR-54621 itself, and
it is the design `BodyStructure` was reshaped to support specifically
to avoid this `0..*` change. Reverting now, mid-R6-ballot, would
re-open a long-running cross-WG dispute that was closed less than 12
months ago and would also force a revisit of the corresponding
ServiceRequest decision on FHIR-48110 (since misalignment between
ServiceRequest and Procedure cardinalities is itself a defect). The
honest weakness of this disposition is that it does *not* explain the
ticket's strongest point — that the resource's own Requirements text
already asserts multi-site expectations — so even a "decline" must
update that narrative so the spec stops contradicting itself.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B (with C as the fallback if cross-WG
appetite is absent)

The B path is the cleanest answer to what the submitter is *actually*
identifying — not just one cardinality, but a design split between
`Procedure.bodySite` (`0..*` coded) and `Procedure.bodyStructure`
(`0..1` reference) gated by `prc-1`, plus a redundant extension. Eric
Haas filed FHIR-54621 and FHIR-54614 together on the same day, and the
O&O R6 review Zulip thread already noted Procedure as the outlier that
hasn't moved to `CodeableReference` like other resources. Treating
these as one consolidated disposition resolves both tickets, retires
the redundant extension cleanly, and aligns Procedure with the
cross-resource direction. The major risk is scope and cross-WG
coordination cost (Procedure is PC-owned; BodyStructure is O&O-owned;
ServiceRequest's prior FHIR-48110 disposition would need to be
revisited for symmetry), so if PC and O&O are unwilling to take that on
during the R6 ballot window, fall back to C and at minimum fix the
internal inconsistency in the `bodyStructure` Requirements/comments
narrative. Disposition A (the literal request) is the worst of the
three options: it inherits all the cross-WG coordination cost of B but
leaves `prc-1`, the `bodySite` / `bodyStructure` split, and the
redundant extension only partially addressed.
