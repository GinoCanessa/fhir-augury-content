## Summary

This ticket requests the creation of a new extension named
`supplementalInformation` (the ticket title contains a typo —
"supplementalInormation" — confirmed by the reporter in a comment) in
the FHIR Extensions Pack. The extension's intended **context of use**
is `Observation`, and its **value[x]** would be constrained to
`Reference(Observation)`. The motivation comes from the laboratory
reporting domain: a lab observation (or a group of lab observations)
sometimes carries supplemental material — raw data, electrophoresis
curves, images, sample-data observations, etc. — and the reporter
wanted a standard hook to attach these supplemental Observations to a
parent result. The need was raised in the OO Lab call on 2026-01-09 and
the HL7 Europe Lab Report FHIR IG meeting on 2026-01-12. By the
2026-03-09 HL7 Europe Lab Report meeting, the participants had
concluded that the use cases could be satisfied by the existing
`Observation.derivedFrom` element, and the reporter has indicated in
comments that she is considering retracting the ticket. There are no
cross-referenced Jira tickets and no related GitHub items; the only
external chat reference is a Zulip thread in `fmg > Extensions Pack`
where the reporter asked whether the ticket would make the v5.3.0
Extensions Pack ballot, and FMG/Lloyd moved the ticket from FHIR-I to
Orders & Observations.

## Proposed Dispositions

Note: there is no GitHub-cached repo briefing involved because the
ticket has no GitHub cross-references. Where a disposition implies a
spec change, the relevant publication surface is the **FHIR Extensions
Pack** (`HL7/fhir-extensions`, `Ig` / `FhirExtensionsPack` category),
which is the specification recorded on the ticket itself.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Define a new extension in the FHIR Extensions Pack with:

- **URL / code:** `supplementalInformation` (correctly spelled, per
  Ralf Herzog's correction and the reporter's confirmation).
- **Context of Use:** `Observation`.
- **Type / value[x]:** constrained to `Reference(Observation)`.
- **Cardinality:** `0..*` (each parent observation can carry multiple
  supplemental observations such as raw data, sample data,
  electrophoresis curves, or images).
- **Short / definition:** "A reference to one or more additional
  Observations that provide supplemental information (raw data,
  sample data, attached images, derived sub-results, etc.) for the
  containing Observation or group of Observations."
- Add usage notes that compare and contrast with
  `Observation.derivedFrom` and `Observation.hasMember`, and provide a
  worked lab-report example.
- Author the StructureDefinition under the Extensions Pack authoring
  area in `HL7/fhir-extensions` and target the next Extensions Pack
  release (post-v5.3.0, since FHIR-54209 was already declared
  out-of-scope for v5.3.0 ballot per the Zulip thread).

#### Justification

This is the literal request and was the consensus direction of the
2026-01-09 OO Lab call and the 2026-01-12 HL7 EU Lab Report meeting
that triggered the ticket. Adding an extension is a low-risk change
that does not touch any base resource; it gives lab implementers a
named, discoverable hook for "supplemental" sub-observations. It also
preserves the option for implementers who prefer a clearly-typed
extension with a focused definition over a general-purpose element.

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Decline to add a new extension and instead update the **lab-reporting
guidance** (in the relevant HL7 EU Lab Report IG and/or in the
Extensions Pack registry notes / Observation IG-level guidance) to
document the agreed pattern: model supplemental material — raw data,
images such as electrophoresis curves, and sample-data observations —
as separate `Observation` instances and link them to the parent
result with `Observation.derivedFrom` (one-to-many). If `derivedFrom`
semantics are felt to be too narrow, document `Observation.hasMember`
as the alternative for "group of observations" cases. Capture the
2026-03-09 HL7 EU Lab Report meeting conclusion in the IG so that
future implementers don't re-raise the same question.

#### Justification

The reporter herself, after meeting with HL7 EU Lab on 2026-03-09,
concluded that `Observation.derivedFrom` covers both use cases (image
attachments and sample-data observations) and proposed retracting the
ticket. Reusing an existing core element is preferred over adding a
parallel extension that overlaps semantically with `derivedFrom` /
`hasMember`; FHIR design generally avoids creating new ways to express
something already expressible. Documenting the pattern in lab-specific
guidance addresses the underlying need (interoperable, predictable
linking of supplemental observations) without polluting the
Extensions Pack with an extension that would compete with a base
element.

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline FHIR-54209 as **already addressed by an existing mechanism**
(`Observation.derivedFrom`, with `Observation.hasMember` available for
grouping). Close the ticket as "Not Persuasive" / "Already Addressed",
referencing the 2026-03-09 HL7 EU Lab Report meeting conclusion and
the reporter's 2026-02-17 comment indicating she is considering
retraction. Do not add new specification content; do not add new IG
guidance.

#### Justification

The Extensions Pack policy discussed in the linked Zulip thread (and
elsewhere) discourages adding extensions that duplicate existing
semantics, because once published an extension's definition is
effectively frozen and any subsequent retraction or rework creates
churn for implementers. The reporter and the HL7 EU Lab Report group
have already concluded that `derivedFrom` is sufficient. Adding the
extension anyway would create two equally-valid ways to model the
same relationship, increasing implementer confusion and increasing
the long-term maintenance surface of the Extensions Pack for no net
expressive gain.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The strongest signal in the record is from the reporter herself: after
the 2026-03-09 HL7 EU Lab Report meeting she stated that the use
cases "all can be represented with a derivedFrom relationship" and
that she was considering retracting the ticket. That makes Disposition
A (build the extension) hard to justify on technical grounds.
Disposition C (pure decline) is also defensible, but it leaves the
underlying problem — that lab implementers were not sure how to model
supplemental observations and felt they needed a new mechanism —
undocumented. Disposition B captures the consensus answer
(`derivedFrom` for derived/image observations, `hasMember` for
groups), records it where future implementers will find it (the
HL7 EU Lab Report IG and/or Extensions Pack registry notes), and
honours the Extensions Pack hygiene principle of not minting a new
extension when a core element already does the job. OO can adopt B
and ask the reporter to formally withdraw FHIR-54209 once the
guidance update is queued.
