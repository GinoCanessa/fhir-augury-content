## Summary

Craig Newman is asking the Clinical Order Workflow (COW) IG to clarify what
role intermediaries play between a Placer and a Fulfiller. Today the IG only
describes intermediaries as "communication" actors; the ticket asks whether an
intermediary can take a more active part in the exchange — for example, ever
update the Task, host the Task itself, or maintain a copy that the next-hop
actor updates instead of the original (especially when direct communication
between actors is not possible). The ticket asks that the IG state explicitly
what is expected of intermediary interactions with the Task.

The discussion in comments has converged on a "Persuasive with Modification"
resolution drafted by Vassil Peytchev and the COW team: introduce a short
prose paragraph in the Core Concepts page acknowledging that intermediaries
exist for business/architectural/technological reasons but are not a distinct
role (because they may take on different responsibilities); remove the current
section 3.5; and rework the Sharing Content page to (a) distinguish FHIR
brokers from intermediate record holders (pointing at Grahame's white paper),
(b) briefly tie this to multi-step workflows such as the reference-lab and
pharmacy examples, and (c) make explicit that the chain of placement is not
the same as the chain of output. Vassil also drafted a 3×3 "Active
Intermediaries" matrix (Task at Placer / Task at Fulfiller perspectives)
describing when an intermediary must rewrite references and when it must
extract/duplicate the Task and synchronize state.

The ticket relates closely to [FHIR-50414](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50414)
("Describe references across servers"), which was filed against the same COW
example set asking how Task/request resources reference each other across
server boundaries. FHIR-50414 has been triaged as a Duplicate of this thread
of work; Vassil agreed to handle the duplicate disposition. JIRAUSER24237
(Rob Hausam-style draft author) has produced a working branch
(`BMS-Ballot-Updates`) on `HL7/fhir-cow-ig` containing a first cut of the
intermediaries content on the `coordinating-across-servers` page, but flagged
that the active/passive distinction in the proposed table needs clarification
and that the draft is currently too narrative-heavy.

## Details

**Description:**

> When intermediaries are involved, is it expected that they would play a
> more active role in the exchange rather than just playing the communication
> role described? For example, who the intermediary ever have reason to
> update the Task resource? Would they go so far as to house the Task itself
> or create a copy of the task that the N+1 actor would update rather than
> the original Task, particularly where direct communication between actors
> is not possible? Please clarify what, if any, expectations there are with
> respect to intermediary interactions with the Task.

**Resolution Description (drafted, "Persuasive with Modification"):**

- At the "Core Concepts" page, immediately after the actors list, add the
  following text:

  > There may be entities throughout a specific workflow that are present
  > between the requestor and the performer for various business,
  > architectural, technological, or other reasons. Such *intermediaries*
  > are not represented by a distinct role, as they may take upon different
  > types of responsibilities.

- Remove section 3.5 from the Core Concepts page.
- Update the Sharing Content page to contain documentation about
  intermediaries:
  1. Delineate FHIR brokers from intermediate record holders from an
     architectural standpoint, describe their requirements (cite Grahame's
     white paper).
  2. Briefly describe how this relates to multi-step workflows using the
     reference-lab and pharmacy examples.
  3. Make clear that the chain of placement is not the same as the chain of
     output.

- Working input drafted below the line in Jira recognises two kinds of
  intermediaries:
  - **Passive intermediary** — has no impact on the description in this IG;
    note that some passive intermediaries are data processors if they store
    or handle the data per local jurisdictional rules.
  - **Active intermediary** — plays the role of Placer to the Filler and
    Filler to the Placer; the intermediary may host the Task but is not
    required to.

- Vassil's table for **Active Intermediaries** (paraphrased):

  | | From Fulfiller view: Task at Placer | From Fulfiller view: Task at Fulfiller |
  |-|-|-|
  | **From Placer view: Task at Placer** | Intermediary rewrites references so each side works as if communicating directly. | Intermediary extracts the Task from Placer, creates a duplicate at Fulfiller, and reflects any Task changes at Fulfiller back to Placer; also rewrites additional references. |
  | **From Placer view: Task at Fulfiller** | The Task is at the Intermediary; this is the one case where rewriting references may not be necessary. | Intermediary rewrites references so each side works as if communicating directly. |

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the resolution already drafted in the ticket (and largely implemented
in the `BMS-Ballot-Updates` branch on `HL7/fhir-cow-ig`) verbatim:

1. In `input/pagecontent/core-concepts.md` (Core Concepts page), insert
   immediately after the actors list the paragraph:
   *"There may be entities throughout a specific workflow that are present
   between the requestor and the performer for various business,
   architectural, technological, or other reasons. Such intermediaries are
   not represented by a distinct role, as they may take upon different types
   of responsibilities."*
2. In the same Core Concepts page, **delete section 3.5** (the existing
   intermediaries-as-communication-actor description).
3. In the Sharing Content page (`input/pagecontent/sharing-content.md` /
   `coordinating-across-servers.md`), add a new "Sharing Content when
   Intermediaries are Present" section that:
   - Distinguishes FHIR brokers from intermediate record holders, citing
     Grahame Grieve's white paper on FHIR brokers/intermediaries.
   - Names the two intermediary modes from the working text (passive vs.
     active), with the explicit caveat that passive intermediaries do not
     change anything described in this IG (and may be data processors under
     local jurisdiction).
   - Includes Vassil's 3×3 Active Intermediaries table describing reference
     rewriting and Task duplication/synchronisation across the
     Placer-view × Fulfiller-view matrix.
   - Uses the existing reference-lab and pharmacy-dispense examples to make
     the placement-chain-vs.-output-chain distinction concrete.
4. Close FHIR-50414 as a duplicate, citing the new Sharing Content section as
   the resolution.

#### Justification

This is the disposition the workgroup has already converged on through
multiple O&O calls and in-person sessions (Vassil drafted it, Ralf marked it
"Done in person", JIRAUSER24237 has a working branch). It directly answers
Craig Newman's question — yes, intermediaries can play more than a
communication role; here is precisely when they need to host/duplicate the
Task and rewrite references — and it does so without inventing a new actor in
the COW workflow, which preserves backward compatibility for existing
Placer/Filler implementations. Pointing to Grahame's broker white paper
avoids re-litigating cross-IG architecture in COW. Closing FHIR-50414 as a
duplicate is supported by both reporters' explicit recognition that the
tickets overlap.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the Core Concepts paragraph and the removal of section 3.5 as in
Disposition A, but **replace the active/passive intermediary table** with a
simpler model framed as JIRAUSER24237 suggested in their 2025-11-14 comment:
distinguish only "**proxying intermediaries**" (which forward requests and
responses in real time, hold no state, and require no IG-level treatment)
from "**stateful intermediaries**" (which maintain their own resources,
potentially including a Shared Coordination Task). For stateful
intermediaries, describe the *required behaviours* directly — reference
rewriting and Task synchronisation — without the 3×3 perspective matrix.
Move the matrix into a non-normative appendix or example for implementers
who want it. Also: instead of a deep cross-reference between FHIR-50293 and
FHIR-50414, treat FHIR-50414 as in-scope and explicitly add a "Cross-Server
References" subsection that addresses Task.focus / Request identity across
server boundaries (the original FHIR-50414 ask), so that the duplicate
disposition is substantive rather than purely procedural.

#### Justification

JIRAUSER24237 — the person actually drafting the page — flagged that the
active/passive split is confusing and that "passive" arguably describes
something below the Application layer (and so doesn't belong in a FHIR IG).
Renaming to proxying/stateful captures the distinction implementers care
about (do I need to manage Task identity?) and avoids the doctrinal
"passive" category that has no IG implications. Folding FHIR-50414's
cross-server reference question into the same section produces one coherent
chapter on coordinating across servers rather than two thinly connected
ones, and gives implementers a single place to find guidance instead of
chasing duplicate-closure trails. The trade-off vs. Disposition A is more
authoring work and a slightly different conceptual model than what Vassil
proposed; the upside is a clearer narrative and a stronger answer to
FHIR-50414.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline to add normative or substantive content about intermediaries to the
COW IG. Instead, add a single sentence to the Core Concepts page noting that
intermediaries may exist between Placer and Filler for business or technical
reasons but that their behaviour is out of scope for this IG, and refer
implementers to Grahame Grieve's FHIR brokers/intermediaries white paper and
to FHIR-I for general cross-server reference guidance. Close FHIR-50414 as
"Not Persuasive — out of scope".

#### Justification

The COW IG describes a clinical order workflow, not a transport or
intermediary architecture. Defining how an intermediary must rewrite
references or duplicate Tasks effectively standardises broker behaviour
inside a clinical workflow IG, where it will inevitably conflict with other
IGs that touch the same intermediaries (e.g., subscriptions, FHIR
Messaging). A single pointer to the existing white paper is a defensible
minimum that satisfies the "please clarify what, if any, expectations there
are" form of Craig Newman's question — the answer being "none from COW
specifically".

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The workgroup has already done the substantive thinking captured in
Disposition A and JIRAUSER24237 has it drafted on a branch — so the marginal
cost of Disposition B over A is small (rename the categories, restructure
the table as an example, fold in FHIR-50414's cross-server reference
question). The marginal benefit is significant: the only person who has
actually tried to write the page text said the Active/Passive framing was
confusing, and the current draft is acknowledged to be "too narrative
heavy". Adopting B gets the COW IG a single coherent "Coordinating Across
Servers / Intermediaries" chapter that answers both FHIR-50293 (what role
can an intermediary play with the Task?) and FHIR-50414 (how do
cross-server references work?) in one place, which is what implementers
have been asking for in both tickets. Disposition A would also be defensible
and matches the in-progress draft; Disposition C should be rejected because
the COW use cases (reference lab, pharmacy dispense) genuinely require
cross-server Task coordination and "see the white paper" leaves
implementers without a concrete answer.
