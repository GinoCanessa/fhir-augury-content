## Summary

The reporter notes that the BodyStructure Scope and Usage section ends with
two sentences describing how BodyStructure can stand outside the context of
another resource and "be the target of a Procedure or Observation resource."
The ticket asks the Orders & Observations work group to add corresponding
content in **§10.7.2 Boundaries and Relationships** of the BodyStructure
page that describes that relationship (BodyStructure ↔ Procedure /
Observation) explicitly, so the boundary text reflects what the Scope and
Usage already promises.

For context, the Scope and Usage wording the reporter quotes was introduced
by the Persuasive resolution to
[FHIR-38646](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-38646) ("Body Structure scope
and usage shall be updated"), which expanded the resource description to
cover multi-location and standalone use. Other recent BodyStructure-area
tickets such as
[FHIR-48789](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48789) (precoordination
wording in BodyStructure) and
[FHIR-55548](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-55548) (which extension to
use to point Procedure at a body structure) show ongoing churn around how
BodyStructure relates to Procedure and Observation. This ticket is a
narrow, editorial follow-on: align the Boundaries and Relationships
narrative with the Scope and Usage narrative and the existing extension
pathways (e.g., `procedure.bodySite`, `Observation.bodySite` /
`Observation.bodyStructure`).

## Details

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a new paragraph to **BodyStructure §10.7.2 Boundaries and Relationships**
that describes how BodyStructure relates to Procedure and Observation,
mirroring the closing sentences of Scope and Usage. Suggested wording:

> BodyStructure represents an anatomic location or structure on a patient
> independently of any clinical activity. Other resources that need to
> identify a body site reference a BodyStructure rather than embed an
> anatomic location of their own. In particular:
>
> - **Procedure** uses `Procedure.bodySite` (and the `bodySite` extension
>   in earlier versions) to point at the BodyStructure(s) the procedure
>   was performed on.
> - **Observation** uses `Observation.bodyStructure` (with
>   `Observation.bodySite` retained for backward compatibility) to point
>   at the BodyStructure(s) the observation pertains to.
>
> Use BodyStructure when the same anatomic location is referenced by more
> than one resource, when laterality/qualifiers cannot be expressed via a
> precoordinated code, or when the structure must be tracked over time
> independently of any single Procedure or Observation.

#### Justification

The reporter is asking for a documentation alignment, not a model change.
Scope and Usage already promises that BodyStructure "can be the target of
a Procedure or Observation resource" (text introduced via FHIR-38646), so
calling that out in Boundaries and Relationships is consistent with the
rest of the page and with how O&O has historically documented
cross-resource boundaries. Naming `Procedure.bodySite`,
`Observation.bodyStructure`, and `Observation.bodySite` keeps the new
narrative aligned with the model that just shipped — including the
removal of `procedure-targetBodyStructure` resolved in FHIR-55548. The
edit is narrative-only and low risk.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Apply Persuasive with Modification: instead of adding only a "BodyStructure
↔ Procedure / Observation" paragraph, refactor §10.7.2 into a short
bulleted boundaries table that covers all the resources O&O has identified
as legitimate consumers of BodyStructure (at minimum: Procedure,
Observation, Specimen, ImagingStudy/ImagingSelection,
Condition.bodySite-style references), with one row per relationship
describing the canonical pointer (element or extension) and when to use
BodyStructure vs. an inline `bodySite` CodeableConcept. The Procedure /
Observation rows specifically address what the reporter asked for.

#### Justification

Several recent tickets (FHIR-38646, FHIR-48789, FHIR-55548) and the
keyword-related set show that BodyStructure's boundary story is being
relitigated piecemeal. A small structural cleanup — done once now — gives
implementers a single place to look, and prevents the next reporter from
filing the same shape of ticket for Specimen, ImagingStudy, etc. The
trade-off is a slightly larger editorial change than the reporter asked
for and a longer review path; the change is still narrative-only and
non-breaking.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark Not Persuasive: close the ticket on the basis that the Scope and
Usage section already states the relationship ("can be the target of a
Procedure or Observation resource"), and that the resource references
themselves (`Procedure.bodySite`, `Observation.bodyStructure`,
`Observation.bodySite`) are documented on the respective resource pages
and in the data dictionary. No additional Boundaries and Relationships
text is required.

#### Justification

This position is internally consistent — the relationship *is* mentioned,
just not in §10.7.2 — but it is weak. The Scope and Usage sentence is a
single trailing clause, the bidirectional pointers live on the *other*
resource pages, and the BodyStructure page's Boundaries section is the
canonical place readers go to understand inter-resource relationships.
Declining mostly defends the status quo against an editorial improvement
the reporter has already half-drafted.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter is asking for a small, well-scoped documentation fix that
aligns Boundaries and Relationships with the Scope and Usage text that
O&O itself approved via FHIR-38646. It is editorial, non-breaking, and
easy to ballot. Disposition B is attractive in principle but expands
scope into resources the reporter did not raise and risks dragging
unsettled questions (e.g., the Procedure-bodySite vs.
`procedure-targetBodyStructure` history from FHIR-55548) into a ticket
that does not need them; if the work group wants the broader cleanup,
spinning a follow-on ticket after Accepting this one keeps each change
reviewable on its own. Disposition C is hard to defend given the Scope
and Usage wording already promises the relationship and the ask is
purely to mirror it where readers actually look.
