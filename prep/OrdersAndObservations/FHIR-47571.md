## Summary

Sarah Gaunt is asking the Orders & Observations work group for clearer
guidance, in the Laboratory Report (FHIR) IG, on the relationship between
the `DiagnosticReport` that a `Composition` references from inside its
sections and the `DiagnosticReport` that the IG already requires as a
top-level entry in the report `Bundle`. Specifically: when a Composition
in the Bundle points to a DiagnosticReport, must that be the *same*
DiagnosticReport instance as the one mandated at the Bundle level, or
may it be a different one? The ticket reads as a documentation /
clarification request rather than a structural change to the profiles
themselves. No other Jira tickets are linked or cross-referenced in the
Augury index for this issue (the historically related FHIR-29258, which
sought to clarify the Composition vs. DiagnosticReport boundary in core
FHIR, is mentioned in community discussion but is not indexed locally).

## Details

**Description:**

> If the Bundle contains a Composition, and the Composition references a
> DiagnosticReport, does the DiagnosticReport have to be the same DR as
> the required DR in the Bundle?

> Maybe it's just me not understanding, but also, maybe some clear

## Related Zulip Discussions

### [europe > EU Lab DiagnosticReport - mandatory composition](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/europe/topic/EU%20Lab%20DiagnosticReport%20-%20mandatory%20composition)

Kevin Mayfield reported that when he based an implementation on the HL7
Europe Laboratory Report IG (0.1.1), the IG forced a mandatory
`Composition` (and therefore a document-style Bundle), which did not
match his REST/messaging use cases (no EPR/LIMS producing clinical
documents). John Moehrke responded with the strict view that "if you
can't be compliant, don't claim to be compliant." Giorgio Cangioli
explained the EU IG's design choice — the EU Lab Report intentionally
requires the Composition as a compromise across stakeholder needs — and
contrasted it with the in-progress UV Lab Report
(`hl7.fhir.uv.lab-report`), where the FHIR-document representation is
*one of three* options and the Composition is therefore not mandatory.
Kevin resolved his issue by switching his base from the EU Laboratory
Report profile to the EU Base `diagnosticReport-eu-core` profile. Ralf
Herzog and Kevin then explored reflex and sub-contracted-order scenarios
that further stress the "single Composition references the canonical
DiagnosticReport" assumption. The thread is directly relevant: it
illustrates that practitioners are uncertain about, and sometimes
working around, the same Composition↔DiagnosticReport relationship that
FHIR-47571 asks the IG to clarify.

Participants: Kevin Mayfield, John Moehrke, Giorgio Cangioli, Ralf Herzog.

### [europe > Laboratory Report - Composition sections](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/europe/topic/Laboratory%20Report%20-%20Composition%20sections)

Gabriel Leonhartsberger asked whether a Laboratory Report Composition
may carry sections that contain only narrative text (other than the
designated annotation section, LOINC 48767-8). Giorgio Cangioli replied
that the design intent was that every Composition must have at least one
result-bearing section, and explicitly re-stated the rule that
*"beside the Composition you need to have a DiagnosticReport that has
at least one result."* Kari Heinonen pushed back that section slicing
is open and a section with a distinct code containing only text would
technically pass schema validation. The thread is relevant because it
re-asserts, in the IG authors' own words, that the Bundle's
DiagnosticReport is the canonical results carrier — implying (but never
spelling out in the IG) that the DiagnosticReport referenced from the
Composition's results section is meant to be that same instance.

Participants: Gabriel Leonhartsberger, Giorgio Cangioli, Kari Heinonen.

### [implementers > DiagnosticReport - Composition/Bundle boundaries](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/implementers/topic/DiagnosticReport%20-%20Composition%2FBundle%20boundaries)

A 2020 thread that frames the same question at the core-spec level.
Robin Bosman asked how to reconcile DiagnosticReport with Composition in
a non-REST architecture. Lloyd McKenzie answered that both are
"organizers" with overlap: Composition organizes anything for human
presentation, while DiagnosticReport is focused on diagnostic findings;
a Bundle can legitimately contain both, with the Composition referencing
the DiagnosticReport as a section entry. Hans Buitendijk pointed at
**FHIR-29258** as the existing tracker item to clarify the distinction
in core FHIR and noted that DiagnosticReport is "effectively the
diagnostic-context organizer." This thread provides the long-standing
community consensus that the Composition's reference to a
DiagnosticReport is intended to point at the *single* canonical
DiagnosticReport in the Bundle — exactly the clarification FHIR-47571
is asking the IG to make explicit.

Participants: Robin Bosman, Lloyd McKenzie, Hans Buitendijk, Forest,
Jose Costa Teixeira (mentioned).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the Laboratory Report (FHIR) IG, add an explicit normative-tone
guidance paragraph (and an accompanying invariant where feasible) to
the Bundle profile and to the Composition profile stating that, when a
Composition is present in a Laboratory Report Bundle, every
`Composition.section.entry` reference of type `Reference(DiagnosticReport)`
**SHALL resolve to the same DiagnosticReport instance** that satisfies
the Bundle's mandatory DiagnosticReport entry (i.e., the
`Bundle.entry[diagnosticReport]` slice). Add a worked example to the
"Bundle structure" / "Composition structure" page and a note on the
DiagnosticReport profile's intro page cross-referencing this rule.
Where technically expressible, encode the rule as a FHIRPath invariant
on the Composition profile such as
`section.entry.where(reference.resolve() is DiagnosticReport).reference.resolve() = %resource.contained.where(...) | %bundle.entry.where(resource.is(DiagnosticReport)).resource`
(the exact expression depends on the Bundle slicing scheme already in
the IG).

#### Justification

This matches the IG authors' stated intent as captured on Zulip
("beside the Composition you need to have a DiagnosticReport that has
at least one result"), aligns with the long-standing core-FHIR
guidance that DiagnosticReport is the canonical diagnostic organizer
(Lloyd McKenzie, FHIR-29258), and removes a real ambiguity that
implementers (Kevin Mayfield, Sarah Gaunt) are demonstrably hitting.
Encoding it as both prose *and* an invariant prevents the rule from
being silently lost in derived IGs.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Add the clarifying guidance, but frame it as a **SHOULD**, not a
**SHALL**, and accompany it with a short "Composition vs.
DiagnosticReport in this IG" explainer page modelled on
`design-choice.html` in the EU Lab Report IG. The explainer would say:
(a) the Bundle's mandatory DiagnosticReport is the canonical, machine-
processable result; (b) the Composition is the human-facing organizer;
(c) the Composition's results section *should* reference that same
DiagnosticReport, but the IG permits referencing additional
DiagnosticReports from other sections (e.g., prior reports,
sub-contracted reports, reflex-test reports) as long as the canonical
one is among them. No invariant is added; instead, a "must-support"
flag is set on `Composition.section.entry` where it targets a
DiagnosticReport, and the Bundle profile gains a comment that the
canonical DiagnosticReport is the one that satisfies the mandatory
slice.

#### Justification

The Zulip discussion on `EU Lab DiagnosticReport - mandatory
composition` shows real implementations (genomics, oncology, reflex
and sub-contracted orders) where multiple DiagnosticReports
legitimately appear in one Bundle; a hard SHALL forcing the
Composition to reference *only* the mandatory one would block these
cases or push them out of the IG entirely. A SHOULD plus an explicit
explainer page resolves the original confusion (Sarah's question) and
preserves room for the reflex/sub-contracted use cases Ralf Herzog and
Kevin Mayfield raised, at the cost of weaker conformance.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as **Not Persuasive — already addressed by existing material**:
note that FHIR-29258 (in core) and the existing Bundle/Composition
profile descriptions in the Laboratory Report IG already imply that
the Composition's DiagnosticReport reference is the canonical one in
the Bundle, and that further normative text risks duplicating, and
therefore drifting from, the core-FHIR DiagnosticReport vs.
Composition guidance.

#### Justification

If the work group considers the answer self-evident from the existing
profile (the Bundle slice for DiagnosticReport is already 1..1; a
second DiagnosticReport in the Bundle would itself be unusual under
the current slicing), then there is nothing to change. Declining keeps
the IG aligned with core-FHIR's general organizer guidance and avoids
proliferating IG-specific rules that overlap with core. The cost is
that the question keeps recurring, as evidenced by the 2020
implementers thread and the 2025/2026 Europe Zulip threads on the same
topic.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is, on its face, a request for *clearer guidance* — and the
Augury record shows the underlying confusion is not Sarah's alone: the
2020 implementers thread, the 2025 Composition-sections thread, and
the 2026 EU Lab thread all circle back to the same question of how
many DiagnosticReports a Laboratory Report Bundle can have and which
one the Composition is supposed to point at. The IG's own authors
(Giorgio Cangioli) have repeatedly said on Zulip that the Composition
and the canonical DiagnosticReport are designed to be coupled, but
that intent is nowhere normatively captured in the IG. Disposition A
makes that coupling explicit in both prose and an invariant, which is
the smallest change that actually prevents the question from being
asked again. Disposition B is a defensible fallback if the work group
hears use cases for *additional* DiagnosticReports in the Bundle and
wants to leave room for them, but the SHOULD framing should not be
chosen by default — it just relocates the ambiguity. Disposition C
should be rejected: the recurring Zulip traffic is direct evidence
that the existing material is *not* clear enough.
