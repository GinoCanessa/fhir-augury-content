## Summary

The reporter (Robert Hausam) proposes that, **conditional on the adoption of
[FHIR-55019](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-55019)** (which would add `List`
as a choice for `DiagnosticReport.result`), the `Observation.organizer` element
should be removed from the `Observation` resource. The argument is that once
`List` is available as the panel/grouper representation under
`DiagnosticReport.result`, `Observation.organizer` becomes redundant and is
likely to cause confusion as a competing grouping mechanism. Because
`Observation.organizer` was only added in the previous ballot cycle and was
flagged as Trial Use prior to this first R6 normative ballot, the reporter
asserts that removing it would not constitute a breaking change to the
normative resource.

This ticket is part of a broader O&O conversation about how to represent
laboratory and other diagnostic panels (the `organizer-grouping` label),
including [FHIR-55019](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-55019) (the `List`
proposal it depends on), [FHIR-55020](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-55020)
(extending `List` status/metadata for grouping use),
[FHIR-54887](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54887) (a bundle of Observation
changes coming out of the same discussion),
[FHIR-54458](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54458) (renaming `organizer` to
`grouper`), and several others (FHIR-54741, FHIR-54808, FHIR-54562, FHIR-54836,
FHIR-54569, FHIR-54525, FHIR-54897). Disposition of FHIR-55023 should not be
considered in isolation from those tickets.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Conditionally remove `Observation.organizer` from the `Observation` resource
in FHIR R6, with the removal taking effect **only if FHIR-55019 is accepted**
(i.e., `List` is added to the choice type of `DiagnosticReport.result`).
Concretely:

- Delete the `Observation.organizer` element definition (and its associated
  invariants, examples, and narrative) from the `Observation` resource in HL7
  FHIR core.
- Update the `Observation` and `DiagnosticReport` panel/grouping guidance to
  direct implementers to use `List` (referenced from
  `DiagnosticReport.result`) as the panel grouper.
- Coordinate with FHIR-54887 (Observation-side changes), FHIR-54836 (grouping
  narrative), FHIR-55020 (List status/metadata extensions), FHIR-54569 and
  FHIR-54525 (re-target to the `List` mechanism), and FHIR-54458 (which is
  superseded — `organizer` is not renamed but removed).

#### Justification

`Observation.organizer` was added in the previous ballot cycle as Trial Use
and has not yet been published as normative; per the reporter's argument and
HL7 FMM/normative-stability policy, removing a Trial-Use element prior to it
becoming normative is permissible without classifying the change as a
breaking change to the normative resource. Carrying two independent grouping
mechanisms (`Observation.organizer` *and* `List` under
`DiagnosticReport.result`) creates exactly the kind of "two ways to do the
same thing" ambiguity that has driven the long-running grouping confusion
documented across the `organizer-grouping` ticket cluster (FHIR-54897,
FHIR-54741, FHIR-54808, FHIR-54562). Picking one mechanism — and the
`DiagnosticReport.result`-anchored `List` is the more report-centric and
extensible of the two — is consistent with FHIR's preference for a single,
clear modeling pattern per use case.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep `Observation.organizer` in the resource for R6 but **demote / constrain
its use** rather than remove it. Specifically:

- Leave `Observation.organizer` in place at its current cardinality.
- Reduce its FMM/maturity (e.g., return it to Trial Use within the
  otherwise-normative resource, or mark it as a candidate for retirement in
  R7) and add a normative `usage note` / `mustSupport`-style guidance
  steering implementers toward `DiagnosticReport.result = List` for
  panel-grouping use cases.
- Optionally accept FHIR-54458 in spirit by clarifying the element's narrow
  intended use (intra-Observation hierarchy, not panel grouping) without
  necessarily renaming it.
- Plan the actual removal for a future release once R6 implementer
  experience confirms `List`-based grouping is sufficient.

#### Justification

Removal-on-condition (Disposition A) leaves the WG with two possible R6
shapes for `Observation` depending on the disposition of a *different*
ticket (FHIR-55019), which complicates ballot reconciliation and creates a
risk that one ticket is accepted and the other rejected, leaving the spec
internally inconsistent. Several existing tickets (FHIR-54569, FHIR-54525)
explicitly *use* `Observation.organizer`, which suggests that some
implementers value it. A staged deprecation lets the WG: (a) accept
FHIR-55019's `List` mechanism without immediately destabilising any
implementer who has already adopted `Observation.organizer`, (b) preserve
the option to remove the element cleanly in R7 once usage data justifies
it, and (c) keep the normative resource shape stable through ballot
reconciliation.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline FHIR-55023 and retain `Observation.organizer` in `Observation` as
defined. Disposition of FHIR-55019 (`List` under `DiagnosticReport.result`)
should be made on its own merits. If both mechanisms end up in the spec,
publish clear narrative guidance (per FHIR-54836) on when to use each.

#### Justification

`Observation.organizer` was deliberately added in the last cycle to address
a gap that the WG had spent multiple cycles debating; removing it in the
*same* cycle that the resource is moving normative — and contingent on the
acceptance of a separate ticket — risks looking like the WG cannot settle
on a stable design. The two mechanisms serve subtly different roles: a
`DiagnosticReport.result`-level `List` is a report-context grouping (it
only makes sense inside a `DiagnosticReport`), while `Observation.organizer`
is an Observation-intrinsic grouping that is meaningful even outside a
`DiagnosticReport` (e.g., standalone vital-signs panels — see FHIR-54525).
Removing the element forces every standalone-Observation use case to either
fabricate a `DiagnosticReport` wrapper or fall back to extensions, neither
of which is clean.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The reporter's underlying concern — that having both `Observation.organizer`
*and* a `List`-based grouper creates confusion — is legitimate and well
supported by the volume of tickets in the `organizer-grouping` cluster.
However, Disposition A's "remove now" path is fragile: it is explicitly
conditional on FHIR-55019, it conflicts with FHIR-54458 (rename) and with
the use cases captured in FHIR-54569 and FHIR-54525, and it makes a
ballot-window reversal of a deliberate prior decision. Disposition C
ignores the reporter's valid concern about modeling duplication.

Disposition B threads the needle: accept the reporter's direction of
travel (`List` becomes the recommended grouper, `Observation.organizer`
loses prominence) without making R6 normativity contingent on the
disposition of another ticket, and without immediately stranding
implementers who have built on `Observation.organizer`. It also gives the
WG a clean path to do the actual removal in R7 once `List`-based grouping
has real implementation evidence behind it. The WG should pair this
disposition with explicit narrative updates (FHIR-54836) so that the
"prefer `List`" guidance is unambiguous.
