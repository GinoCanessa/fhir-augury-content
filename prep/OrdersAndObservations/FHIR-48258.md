## Summary

LabCorp (via reporter Kathy Walsh) observes that the laboratory-report
interaction model is awkward when a single order requests multiple panels:
because `DiagnosticReport.code` has cardinality `1..1`, implementers feel
forced to emit a separate `DiagnosticReport` for every orderable panel
code, even when those panels were ordered together. The ticket originally
proposed extending `DiagnosticReport.basedOn` so a `DiagnosticReport` can
reference another `DiagnosticReport` (mirroring `ServiceRequest`'s
self-reference), giving implementers a way to express a "parent" panel
report that groups child panel reports.

Subsequent discussion has reframed the problem as a broader
results-grouping question. The committee suggestion captured in the
2025-03-07 comment is to leave `ServiceRequest.code` at `0..1` and
`DiagnosticReport.code` at `1..1`, and instead document the existing
grouping mechanisms — primarily that multiple `ServiceRequest`s sharing a
common `ServiceRequest.requisition` constitute one logical order, and
that a single `DiagnosticReport` may carry a higher-level grouping code
that does not need to match the child `ServiceRequest.code`s. The final
shape of the answer is explicitly tied to the resolution of the closely
related ticket [FHIR-48255](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48255)
("Allow Diagnostic Reports to be nested"), which proposes a high-level
`DiagnosticReport` containing nested `DiagnosticReport`s for each
ordered panel — i.e., the same use case approached from the report
nesting side rather than the cardinality side.

The ticket has been resolved as **Persuasive with Modification**, with
the recorded resolution proposing two `Bundle` profiles — one for each
grouping pattern (Pattern #1: `Bundle.entry.DiagnosticReport 1..1`;
Pattern #2: `Bundle.entry.DiagnosticReport 1..*`) — leaving the base
resource cardinalities unchanged but giving the Laboratory Report IG a
profile-level mechanism to express both single-report and multi-report
result bundles.

## Details

**Description:**

DiagnosticReport and ServiceRequest code property have a cardinality of
1. This constraint suggests that a resource be created for each
orderable panel code. Like ServiceRequest already does, we propose
extending `DiagnosticReport.basedOn` to accept references to
`DiagnosticReport`.

**Resolution Description:**

Propose to create 2 bundle profiles - one for each pattern; for Pattern
#1 `bundle.entry.DiagnosticReport 1..1` and for Pattern #2
`bundle.entry.DiagnosticReport 1..*`.

### [FHIR-48255: Allow Diagnostic Reports to be nested.](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48255)

A companion change request from the same reporter (Kathy Walsh /
LabCorp) asking that a Laboratory Report be allowed to use a
high-level `DiagnosticReport` that contains multiple per-panel
`DiagnosticReport`s, instead of the current pattern of sending a
non-document `Bundle` of multiple top-level `DiagnosticReport`s. The
reporter explicitly does *not* want `Observation`s to be nested for
this purpose, arguing that grouping headers belong on
`DiagnosticReport`, not on `Observation`. The 2025-03-07 comment on
FHIR-48258 makes the cardinality decision dependent on the outcome of
this ticket; the 2024-12-13 comment here notes that removing the
existing ability to nest `Observation`s is "neither possible nor
desirable" since `Observation` is normative and has supported linked /
nested observations since DSTU 1 (2014). Status: Triaged, Unresolved.

Users involved: Kathy Walsh (reporter), Rob Hausam (commenter).

## Related Zulip Discussions

### [Orders and Observation WG > Grouping options for Observation/DiagnosticReport](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/Orders%20and%20Observation%20WG/topic/Grouping%20options%20for%20Observation%2FDiagnosticReport/near/511035774)

A long-running thread (originally opened in May 2023 as "Usage of panel
of Observations" and renamed by Rob Hausam) revisited in April 2025
expressly to convene a broader US/EU/AU meeting on results-grouping for
laboratory data. David McKillop (AU) lays out the core problem: there is
no shared answer across FHIR Core, US Core, IPS, and AU Base/Core for
how to represent a panel (e.g., FBC) in `Observation` /
`DiagnosticReport`. Two patterns are described:

- **Option 1** — `DiagnosticReport.code = FBC`; `DiagnosticReport.result`
  references one `Observation` per individual component (Hb, RCC, WBC,
  …). This aligns with US Core's Laboratory Result Observation profile.
- **Option 2** — `DiagnosticReport.code = FBC`; `DiagnosticReport.result`
  references a single grouping `Observation` with `Observation.hasMember`
  pointing at component `Observation`s. This aligns with IPS and AU
  Base/Core (which goes further by treating individual tests the same
  way as panels).

Rob Hausam's April 2025 post pins both [FHIR-48255](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48255)
and [FHIR-48258](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48258) as the Jira
items the meeting is intended to resolve, and explicitly invites Angus
Millar, David McKillop, Grahame Grieve, and Giorgio Cangioli. No
consensus is recorded in the thread itself; the thread documents that
the WG is still working toward one and that the answer will need to be
internationally aligned (FHIR Core vs. US Core vs. IPS vs. AU
Base/Core).

Participants: Rob Hausam, David McKillop, Angus Millar, JD Nolen, Jay
Lyle, Hans Buitendijk, Marti Velezis, Grahame Grieve (mentioned),

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Modify the `DiagnosticReport` resource so that `DiagnosticReport.basedOn`
accepts `Reference(DiagnosticReport)` in addition to its current targets
(`CarePlan`, `ImmunizationRecommendation`, `MedicationRequest`,
`NutritionOrder`, `ServiceRequest`). Leave `DiagnosticReport.code`
cardinality at `1..1` and `ServiceRequest.code` at `0..1`. Document the
new pattern in the Laboratory Report IG: a "parent" panel
`DiagnosticReport` (whose `.code` carries the higher-level / requisition
code) is referenced by each per-panel child `DiagnosticReport` via
`DiagnosticReport.basedOn`, mirroring the way `ServiceRequest.basedOn`
already chains orders to parent orders.

#### Justification

This is a minimally-invasive structural change that delivers exactly
what the ticket asks for: a way to express that several `DiagnosticReport`
instances were produced as part of a single, higher-level reporting
event, without forcing implementers into either Composition / FHIR
documents or into bundling-only conventions. The change preserves
backward compatibility (existing senders that do not populate
`DiagnosticReport.basedOn` with a `DiagnosticReport` reference are
unaffected) and is symmetric with the established `ServiceRequest`
pattern — implementers already understand "follow `.basedOn` upward to
find the parent", so reusing the idiom on `DiagnosticReport` minimises
new cognitive load.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach (Bundle profiles in the Laboratory Report IG, no base-resource change) — matches the recorded resolution

#### Proposal

Do not change `DiagnosticReport.basedOn` or the cardinality of either
`DiagnosticReport.code` or `ServiceRequest.code` in the base
specification. Instead, in the Laboratory Report IG, define **two
`Bundle` profiles**:

- **Pattern #1** — single-report bundle:
  `Bundle.entry.where(resource is DiagnosticReport)` constrained to
  `1..1`. Used when one logical order is reported by exactly one
  `DiagnosticReport`.
- **Pattern #2** — multi-report bundle:
  `Bundle.entry.where(resource is DiagnosticReport)` constrained to
  `1..*`. Used when one logical order produced multiple per-panel
  `DiagnosticReport`s; the constituent `ServiceRequest`s are tied
  together by a shared `ServiceRequest.requisition`, and each
  `DiagnosticReport.basedOn` points at its corresponding
  `ServiceRequest`.

Add narrative in the Laboratory Report IG explaining (a) when to pick
which pattern, (b) that `DiagnosticReport.code` and
`ServiceRequest.code` need not match, and (c) that grouping across
panels is expressed via shared `ServiceRequest.requisition` rather than
via a parent `DiagnosticReport`. Defer "nested `DiagnosticReport`"
representation to the resolution of
[FHIR-48255](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48255).

#### Justification

This is the disposition actually recorded on the ticket ("Persuasive
with Modification") and aligns with the 2025-03-07 WG comment. It
addresses the underlying need — implementers want a defensible way to
report multi-panel orders — without changing a base resource that is
widely deployed and that other IGs (US Core, IPS, AU Base/Core)
constrain in subtly different ways. Keeping the change in IG-level
`Bundle` profiles avoids a normative ripple through every consumer of
`DiagnosticReport`, and it also avoids prejudging the
nested-`DiagnosticReport` design that FHIR-48255 is still negotiating
internationally on the linked Zulip thread. The trade-off vs.
Disposition A is that there is no first-class "parent report" pointer;
consumers have to assemble the group by walking
`ServiceRequest.requisition` (or by relying on the `Bundle` boundary
itself), which is slightly more work but does not require a base-spec
change.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request to change `DiagnosticReport.basedOn` or the
cardinality of `DiagnosticReport.code` / `ServiceRequest.code`.
Rationale: the underlying use case is **already addressable** with
existing FHIR mechanisms — `ServiceRequest.requisition` for grouping
orders, `DiagnosticReport.result` + `Observation.hasMember` for
grouping observations within a report, `Composition` / FHIR documents
for true report-of-reports semantics, and `Bundle` for transport-level
grouping. Close the ticket with guidance pointing implementers at those
mechanisms.

#### Justification

A straight decline is defensible on the grounds that adding another
target to `DiagnosticReport.basedOn` (Disposition A) introduces a
*second* way to express grouping that overlaps with
`ServiceRequest.requisition` and with `Composition`, increasing the
risk that two senders express the same logical fact in two different,
non-interoperable ways. However, this disposition ignores the
documented international demand surfaced on the Zulip thread (US, EU,
AU all asking for clearer guidance) and the fact that the WG has
already accepted the substantive concern (resolution recorded as
*Persuasive with Modification*). It is included here for completeness
but is the weakest of the three options.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the recommended path because it matches the
resolution the WG has actually recorded on the ticket (*Persuasive
with Modification*, two `Bundle` profiles in the Laboratory Report
IG), it avoids destabilising `DiagnosticReport` — a resource that
US Core, IPS, and AU Base/Core all already profile in subtly
incompatible ways — and it cleanly defers the deeper "nested report"
design to its proper home in
[FHIR-48255](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48255), where the
international group convened via the Zulip thread is still working
toward consensus. Disposition A delivers exactly what the original
reporter asked for, but solving the problem at the base-resource level
ahead of FHIR-48255's resolution risks locking in a grouping pattern
that the cross-jurisdiction discussion may yet reject. Disposition C
disregards the recorded WG decision and the clear international demand
for better guidance, and so should not be adopted.
