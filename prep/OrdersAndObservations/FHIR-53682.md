## Summary

The reporter asks the COW (Clinical Order Workflow) IG editors to restructure
and tighten the Core Concepts page so it can scale as more patterns are added
during the R6 normative ballot cycle. Specifically, the page should (1) define
the term "intermediaries" at a conceptual level (no mechanisms or technical
details), (2) define "resolving references" at the same level, (3) add a
paragraph in or below the intermediaries section that enumerates the aspects
intermediaries impact (e.g., sharing additional information), with hyperlinks
out to the detailed pattern pages where each aspect is mechanically explained,
and (4) move the mechanism-level material into those detailed pattern pages or
sub-pages. The reporter also wants the workgroup to discuss whether the page
title "Concepts" should be renamed to something less ambiguous (e.g., "Ideas").

The "intermediaries / resolving references" thread is tightly bound to
[FHIR-50280](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50280) ("Describe how references
should be handled"), which the workgroup explicitly carved out of this
ticket's resolution: the resolution motion accepts items 1, 3, and 4 from this
ticket but defers the actual reference-handling description to FHIR-50280
(which has its own resolution targeting the new `coordinating-across-servers`
page). A first author comment also clarifies that the phrase "A few decisions"
in the description was a typo — no decisions had been taken at the time the
ticket was filed.

### [FHIR-50280: Describe how references should be handled](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50280)

Cooper Thompson's ticket asking the COW IG to describe, for RESTful
create/update interactions on `Task`, how references inside the `Task` should
be handled — enumerating four options (contained resources, references created
on the fulfiller before `Task.create`, references back to the placer's server,
and logical / identifier-only references). The resolution agrees that
references-to-the-placer is the default expectation and that logical
references are appropriate for static resources (Practitioners, Locations);
contained resources and fulfiller-pre-creation are explicitly discouraged. The
write-up will live on the proposed `coordinating-across-servers` page (or
wherever FHIR-50293 lands). FHIR-53682's resolution explicitly defers item #2
("define resolving references") to this ticket so the two are not duplicated.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Implement the four-step plan from the description verbatim on the COW IG
Core Concepts page:

1. Add a new "Intermediaries" subsection on the Core Concepts page that
   defines the term at a conceptual level only, with two narrow definitions
   to anchor the rest of the IG:
   - **Workflow Intermediary** — an actor that participates in the
     orchestration of a Task between Placer and Fulfiller (e.g., routes,
     queues, transforms, or supervises Tasks).
   - **Communication Intermediary** — an actor that sits on the
     transport/exchange path (e.g., a relay, broker, or facade server) but
     does not alter workflow semantics.
2. Add a "Resolving References" subsection on the Core Concepts page with a
   conceptual definition only (no mechanism), and cross-link it to
   FHIR-50280's destination text on `coordinating-across-servers`.
3. Below the Intermediaries definition, add a paragraph of the form
   "Intermediaries impact the following aspects: sharing additional
   information, …" with each aspect rendered as a hyperlink to the relevant
   section of the detailed pattern page that mechanically explains it.
4. Move (or add) all mechanism-level descriptions of how intermediaries
   affect each aspect into the detailed pattern pages / sub-pages, leaving
   the Concepts page free of technical detail.

Defer the page-rename question (Concepts → Ideas or other) to a follow-up
discussion item; the resolution did not commit to a rename.

#### Justification

This mirrors what the resolution already accepted: items 1, 3, and 4 of the
description, plus deferral of item 2's mechanics to FHIR-50280. The structural
split (high-level Concepts page + detailed pattern pages) is consistent with
how other HL7 IGs (e.g., the SDOH-CC, US-Core narrative pages) keep
conceptual material discoverable while mechanics live near the resources /
profiles that implement them. Splitting "Workflow Intermediary" from
"Communication Intermediary" up front prevents the two roles from being
conflated as more patterns are added during the R6 ballot cycle, which is
exactly the scaling problem the reporter raised.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt the same content additions as Disposition A, but invert the page
hierarchy: instead of a single "Core Concepts" page that links out to many
pattern pages, promote "Actors" (including the two intermediary subtypes) to
its own top-level IG page and turn "Core Concepts" into a thin landing page
that orients the reader and links to: Actors, Resolving References (defined
on the `coordinating-across-servers` page from FHIR-50280), and the pattern
catalog. Defer any rename of "Concepts" until the landing-page restructure is
in place — the rename question typically resolves itself once the page is
purely navigational.

#### Justification

The resolution already says intermediaries should be added "in the Actors
Section", which implies Actors is the natural home for the new definitions —
not the Concepts page. Treating Concepts as a landing page (rather than the
container for definitions) keeps the IG navigable as new patterns land
without forcing repeated edits to one large narrative page, and it sidesteps
the duplication risk between this ticket and FHIR-50280 by letting both
tickets write into their natural pages (Actors and `coordinating-across-
servers` respectively) while Concepts only links to them. This is a slightly
larger edit than Disposition A but is structurally cleaner for the R6
ballot.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the ticket on the grounds that the Core Concepts page restructuring
is editorial / housekeeping and is already substantially covered by
FHIR-50280's resolution (which adds the `coordinating-across-servers` page
with the actual reference-resolution mechanics). Close FHIR-53682 with a
note pointing readers to FHIR-50280 and to whatever Actors-section edits the
editors choose to make as routine maintenance, without creating a separate
ballot disposition for it.

#### Justification

The four bullets in the description are a mix of editorial work (move
content, add hyperlinks, add definitions) and a question (rename
"Concepts"?). None of them changes the IG's conformance surface, and
FHIR-50280 already commits the editors to writing the reference-resolution
text. Declining avoids two ballot artifacts re-stating overlapping intent.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The workgroup has already voted "Persuasive with Modification" with a motion
that closely matches Disposition A: add Workflow Intermediary and
Communication Intermediary in the Actors section, defer the
reference-resolution mechanics to FHIR-50280, and push the per-pattern
mechanics into the detailed pattern pages. Disposition A is essentially that
motion turned into concrete editorial steps, plus an explicit deferral of the
"rename Concepts" question that the resolution did not decide. Disposition B
is attractive structurally but is a larger restructure that risks slipping
the R6 ballot window; if there is editorial bandwidth, B's landing-page
treatment can be applied as a follow-up after A lands. Disposition C
under-delivers — the reporter's concern is precisely that without a
high-level Concepts framing, future pattern additions will accrete onto
unrelated pages, and FHIR-50280 alone does not solve that.
