## Summary

FHIR-39244 asks Orders & Observations to publish an example and/or
narrative guidance for how to associate device-derived clinical
Observations with the device settings that were in effect when the
observation was made. The reporter notes that implementers are taking
divergent approaches: some link the clinical Observation to settings
Observations through `Observation.hasMember` (directly or via an
intermediate "settings set" Observation), while others record settings
Observations independently (on change or at fixed times) and rely on
"most recent value" reasoning to reconstruct the settings context. The
ticket also notes confusion between device *settings* (typically
Observations linked through DeviceMetric to Device) and device
*properties*, and asks for an example that ties Observation,
DeviceMetric, Device, and possibly DeviceAssociation together.

This ticket is essentially a duplicate of [FHIR-38917](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-38917)
(same title and description, also reported into OO and still
Triaged/Unresolved). It is also tightly linked to
[FHIR-40288](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-40288) — "Add a device
module with landing page" — which was applied (Persuasive) and is the
agreed home for narrative guidance about how Device, DeviceMetric, and
Observation relate. The OO committee has repeatedly proposed that
FHIR-39244 be addressed by content placed on that Device module page,
together with the cluster of "Observation.component vs. .hasMember vs.
.subject vs. .focus" tickets and ongoing work on DeviceMetric and
DeviceAssociation. As of 2026-01-30, OO voted (11-0-1) on a disposition
that reopened the ticket after a previous "Considered for Future Use"
resolution was reverted.

## Details

**Description:**

There seems to be a range of opinions on how to associate clinical
observations generated by devices with the settings in effect on the
device at the time the clinical observation was made. Associating the
device settings and clinical observations is important to provide
context for the clinical observations, and to provide historical
evidence.

Some have suggested that the clinical observation should use hasMember
relationships to the settings observations (either directly or
indirectly through a "settings set" observation). This has the
advantage of unambiguously tying the clinical observation to the
settings in effect.

Others have suggested simply recording the settings observations
either when they change, or at defined times (daily at noon, at the
start of a procedure, etc.) and recording the clinical observations
without regard for the settings; the settings in effect for any
clinical observation can be determined by finding the most recent
setting observations (for each of the device metrics) prior to the
clinical observation. This approach doesn't laden the clinical
observations with concern over the settings observations; but makes it
more difficult to detect a missing/deleted setting.

There is also some confusion about the difference between device
settings (recorded as Observations linked to DeviceMetrics linked to
Devices) and device properties, and in general how to record device
settings.

An example or guidance that ties these all together, perhaps starting
with a clinical Observation linking to settings Observations, would
help provide a common approach to these challenges.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a worked **Observation example** to the FHIR Core (R6) Observation
resource that demonstrates a device-derived clinical Observation
linked to its in-effect device settings via
`Observation.hasMember`, with the settings expressed as separate
Observations bound to `Observation.subject = Patient` and
`Observation.focus = Device | DeviceMetric`. Use the existing draft
[`observation-example-devicemetricfocus.xml`](https://build.fhir.org/observation-example-devicemetricfocus.xml.html)
as the starting point, finalise it (the comments confirm it is "not
yet agreed"), and add a short narrative paragraph in the Observation
*Notes* / "Boundaries and Relationships" section that explains the
recommended pattern, the role of DeviceMetric, and when (and when
not) to use `Observation.component` to carry settings inline.
Cross-link from the Device module landing page (FHIR-40288) so the
guidance is discoverable from the device side as well.

#### Justification

This is the literal ask of the ticket — an example plus guidance —
and it builds on a candidate example that has already been drafted in
the v6.0.0-ballot2 build. The `hasMember`-based pattern is the one
the reporter highlighted as having "the advantage of unambiguously
tying the clinical observation to the settings in effect," and it
aligns with the existing FHIR convention of expressing structured
Observation groupings via `hasMember` (e.g., panels). Because
FHIR-40288 is already Applied/Persuasive, the Device module page
exists as an integration target, so the change does not require a
new vehicle.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Resolve FHIR-39244 (and FHIR-38917) by **adding only narrative
guidance to the Device module landing page** delivered under
FHIR-40288, rather than committing to a single Observation linking
pattern. The page would:

1. Distinguish device **settings** (mutable operational state,
   captured as Observations whose `focus` is the Device or
   DeviceMetric) from device **properties** (intrinsic, captured on
   `Device.property` / `DeviceDefinition.property`).
2. Document **two valid patterns** for associating settings with
   clinical Observations: (a) explicit grouping via
   `Observation.hasMember` from the clinical Observation to settings
   Observations (or to a "settings set" Observation that itself uses
   `hasMember`); and (b) temporal correlation, where settings
   Observations are recorded on change / on schedule and the
   "settings in effect" are reconstructed by querying the most
   recent settings prior to the clinical Observation.
3. Note the trade-offs (auditability vs. payload size; ability to
   detect deleted settings; behaviour across `Observation.component`
   vs. `.hasMember` vs. `.subject` vs. `.focus`).
4. Provide one or two short XML/JSON snippets rather than a
   committee-blessed canonical example.

Mark FHIR-39244 and FHIR-38917 as duplicates of each other; resolve
both with the same disposition.

#### Justification

The committee's own pattern, visible in the comment trail and in the
2023-01 WGM agenda Zulip thread, has consistently been to defer this
ticket to the Device module page rather than to a single example.
There is genuine community disagreement (captured in the description
itself) about which linking pattern is correct, and committing to
one pattern in a normative example risks codifying a contested
choice. Narrative guidance that names both patterns and their
trade-offs gives implementers actionable advice without precluding
either approach, and avoids re-litigating the
component/hasMember/subject/focus question that the OO discussion
hbuitendijk flagged on 2025-07-21 has not yet converged. It also
naturally absorbs the duplicate FHIR-38917 with no extra work.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (Considered for Future Use)

#### Proposal

Re-apply the disposition that the committee has twice landed on and
twice reverted: **Considered for Future Use**, with the explicit
caveat (from the 2025-10 vote) that the ticket will be inserted as
ballot feedback in the first R6 round of ballot reconciliation. Do
not attempt to add an example or narrative for the current
publication cycle. Mark FHIR-38917 the same way.

#### Justification

The ticket has been open since 2022-10, has had two prior "Consider
for Future Use" resolutions reverted (2023-02 and 2025-10), and the
2026-01-30 OO vote (11-0-1) reopened it again specifically because
the underlying Device/DeviceMetric/Observation relationship work has
not converged. The hbuitendijk comment of 2025-07-21 explicitly
states that the candidate example is "not yet agreed" and depends on
the resolution of related component/hasMember/subject/focus tickets
and the DeviceMetric and DeviceAssociation work. Forcing an example
into R6 ahead of that convergence risks either codifying a contested
pattern or shipping guidance that contradicts the eventual Device
module direction. Declining for this cycle preserves option value.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the best fit because it actually closes the ticket
(rather than rolling it forward yet again, as C would) while
respecting the unresolved design question that has prevented the
committee from agreeing on a single canonical example (the risk in
A). The vehicle already exists — FHIR-40288 is Applied/Persuasive,
so the Device module landing page is committed for R6 — and the
ticket cluster (FHIR-38917 duplicate, the
component/hasMember/subject/focus questions, and the DeviceMetric
and DeviceAssociation work) is exactly the cluster the committee has
repeatedly said this ticket should be addressed with. Narrative
guidance that documents *both* the explicit `hasMember` linkage
pattern and the temporal-correlation pattern, together with the
settings-vs-properties distinction, gives implementers the common
approach the reporter asked for without prematurely settling the
linkage debate. If, during the R6 cycle, the OO/Devices joint
discussion converges on a single recommended pattern (the
`devicemetricfocus` draft example becomes agreed), the example from
Disposition A can be added on top of the narrative under the same
ticket without rework.
