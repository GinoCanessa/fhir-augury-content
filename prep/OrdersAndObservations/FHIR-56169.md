## Summary

The reporter asks the Orders & Observations work group to revisit the
guidance currently published in section **10.1.5.6 "Refining the
interpretation of an Observation using additional codes or
Observations"** of the Observation resource page, in light of the
broader workgroup discussion on the **organizer / grouping** pattern
(tracked via the `organizer-grouping` label). The specific concern is
item **#3** in that section, which today says implementers may, as an
alternative to using `Observation` grouping (`Observation.hasMember` /
`Observation` with `category = "survey"` / panel-style organizers), use
**extensions to reference other observations** needed for understanding
or interpreting an observation. That alternative needs to be reconciled
with whatever direction the grouping discussion converges on, so that
the Observation page does not continue to recommend an extension-based
work-around alongside (or instead of) the canonical grouping mechanism.
No related Jira tickets or Zulip discussions were surfaced by
cross-reference search; the only cross-reference is to the FHIR
artifact `Observation.code`, which is the element discussed in items #1
and #2 of the same section.

> Need to review the following notes in relation to the Grouping

>
> **10.1.5.6 Refining the interpretation of an Observation using
> additional codes or Observations**
>
> The following list provides guidance on using codes or other
> observations to provide additional context that may alter how an
> observation is interpreted:
>
> 1. If possible, use the most specific code you can.
>
>    e.g. `Observation.code.coding = { system: http://loinc.org, code:
>    6689-4, display: "Glucose [Mass/volume] in Blood --2 hours post
>    meal" }`
>
> 2. Alternatively, use additional codings in `Observation.code` as
>    described
>    [above](https://build.fhir.org/observation.html#mult-codes).
>
>    e.g. `Observation.code` with two codings — coding-1: 59408-5
>    "Oxygen saturation in Arterial blood by Pulse oximetry", coding-2:
>    20564-1 "Oxygen saturation in Blood".
>
> 3. As described
>    [above](https://build.fhir.org/observation.html#obsgrouping),
>    observations are typically grouped together to provide additional
>    information needed for correctly understanding and interpreting
>    the observation. **As an alternative to grouping observations,
>    extensions may be used to provide references to other observations
>    needed for understanding and interpreting an observation.**

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Rewrite section **10.1.5.6** of the Observation resource page so that
item #3 is brought into line with the work group's organizer/grouping
direction (as flagged by the `organizer-grouping` label). Concretely:

- Keep items #1 (use the most specific code) and #2 (use multiple
  codings on `Observation.code`) substantially as-is.
- Replace item #3's current wording — which presents
  **extension-based references to other Observations** as an
  *alternative* to grouping — with guidance that names the canonical
  grouping mechanism first (`Observation.hasMember` / `Observation`
  panels / `Observation.derivedFrom` where appropriate) and only
  permits an extension-based reference as a narrowly-scoped fall-back,
  if at all.
- Cross-link the revised paragraph to the organizer/grouping section
  (`#obsgrouping`) and to whatever new normative or trial-use narrative
  the grouping discussion produces, so a reader of 10.1.5.6 lands on
  the same recommendation as a reader of the grouping section.

This is a narrative-only change to `observation.html` (the source page
for the resource intro / notes); no `StructureDefinition`, element
definition, invariant, or value-set is altered.

#### Justification

The reporter explicitly asks for a review against the grouping
discussion, and the current item #3 sits awkwardly with the resource's
mainline grouping pattern: it tells implementers they *may* avoid
`Observation.hasMember` by reaching for an extension, which can splinter
the ecosystem into "panel" implementations and "extension-pointer"
implementations for the same conceptual need. Tightening the narrative
to lead with the canonical grouping mechanism is consistent with FHIR's
"prefer core elements, use extensions only when core is insufficient"
design principle, and it keeps the two anchors that already exist on
the page (`#mult-codes`, `#obsgrouping`) as the canonical references.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than rewriting item #3 in isolation, **defer the edit** until
the parent organizer/grouping work (the `organizer-grouping` tranche)
has produced its target design, and then perform a single
**coordinated rewrite** that touches:

- Section 10.1.5.6 item #3 (this ticket).
- The grouping section the page already links to (`#obsgrouping`).
- Any companion narrative on `Observation.hasMember` /
  `Observation.derivedFrom` / panel guidance.

In the meantime, replace item #3 with a short placeholder note —
"Guidance on grouping Observations is being revised; see
[`#obsgrouping`](https://build.fhir.org/observation.html#obsgrouping)" —
so the section is internally consistent without committing to a
specific extension-vs-grouping recommendation that may be reversed by
the broader work.

#### Justification

The ticket itself frames the work as a *review in relation to the
Grouping discussion*, not a stand-alone fix. Editing item #3 ahead of
the rest of the grouping narrative risks a second churn pass when the
grouping discussion lands, and risks the page contradicting itself
(item #3 says "X", `#obsgrouping` says "Y"). A coordinated rewrite
also lets reviewers see the full picture in one ballot cycle, which is
how O&O normally handles cross-cutting Observation narrative changes.
The trade-off vs. Disposition A is calendar latency — the placeholder
sits in the spec until the grouping work converges — but the editorial
quality of the final result is higher.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request as **out of scope for an isolated change** and
close it as either *Persuasive with Mod / Not Persuasive — Already
Tracked* against whatever umbrella ticket eventually represents the
organizer/grouping rewrite. Leave 10.1.5.6 item #3 unchanged on the
grounds that the extension-based alternative is a documented existing
pattern, and any rewrite should be driven from the grouping ticket
itself, not from a narrative-only ticket against a single sub-section.

#### Justification

The current text is not factually wrong — extensions *can* be used to
reference other Observations — and there is no implementer report of
harm cited on the ticket (no comments, no Zulip discussion, no linked
GitHub issue or PR). If the work group is going to redo the grouping
narrative anyway, a ticket whose entire content is "review this in
light of the other discussion" duplicates that other discussion's
ticket and adds tracking overhead without adding new requirements.
Declining keeps the backlog focused on tickets that propose a concrete
change.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The ticket is a *prompt for coordinated editorial work*, not a
self-contained change request: the description literally says "Need to
review the following notes in relation to the Grouping discussion".
Disposition A is defensible but risks a second edit pass once the
grouping discussion converges, and Disposition C throws away the
useful signal the reporter is providing (that 10.1.5.6 item #3 is one
of the places the grouping rewrite must reach). Disposition B keeps
the ticket open as the explicit hook for that coordinated rewrite,
inserts a low-risk placeholder so the page does not actively recommend
a pattern that may be deprecated, and lines the work up to be balloted
together with the rest of the organizer/grouping changes in a single
coherent pass — which is the outcome an O&O reviewer would most likely
want.
