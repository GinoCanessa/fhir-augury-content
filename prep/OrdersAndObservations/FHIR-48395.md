## Summary

The ticket asks Orders & Observations (jointly with Patient Care) to define
a first-class FHIR mechanism for requesting that a *device* change its own
settings, rather than asking a *person* to operate the device. Today, an
implementer can model "ask a clinician to change ventilator settings" with
`Task` or `ServiceRequest`, but there is no defined pattern for conveying
the request to the device itself, nor for representing the structured
desired settings, nor for tracking the lifecycle of those settings
(requested → pending → as-applied). The ticket scopes three deliverables:
(1) a structured representation of requested settings, (2) a transport
mechanism to convey the request to the device — likely a profile or
extension of `Task`, and/or a new `Device` operation — and (3) state
tracking that distinguishes requested, pending, and applied values. The
request originated from Stephen Chu and is being incubated under
"OO Incubator (FHIR)" with Joint-OO-PC labelling. No cross-referenced Jira
tickets were surfaced by FhirAugury for this key.

## Related Zulip Discussions

### [implementers > Modelling device settings](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/implementers/topic/Modelling%20device%20settings/near/457395946)

Simone Fricke (a FHIR newcomer) asked how to model device settings for ICU
equipment such as mechanical ventilators and intra-aortic balloon pumps,
noting that `DeviceMetric` describes the metric (type/unit/range) but does
not carry the value, and asking whether `Observation` with various
combinations of `subject`, `device`, and `focus` was the right shape.
Elliot Silver (Devices WG) confirmed the current guidance: an `Observation`
with `subject=Patient` and `device=Device/DeviceMetric` is the correct
representation of a settings value when captured in the context of patient
care; `subject=Device` is reserved for calibration / device-only contexts;
`focus` is currently reserved for the case where one device reports on
another device. The thread covers *recording* current settings, not
*requesting* changes, so it is adjacent rather than a direct duplicate —
but it confirms that the "read" side has a documented pattern and that
this ticket is about adding the missing "write/request" side.

Participants: Simone Fricke, John Moehrke, Elliot Silver.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat this as a Devices/OO incubator work item and produce, in the OO
Incubator (FHIR) project, three coordinated artifacts:

1. A structured *desired settings* payload — model it as a new resource
   (working name `DeviceSettingsRequest`) or, if the WG prefers
   reuse, as a profile of `Task` whose `Task.input` carries
   `DeviceMetric.code`-keyed `(value[x], unit, target/range)` tuples.
2. A transport pattern. Either (a) profile `Task` so
   `Task.focus = Device`, `Task.code = "change-settings"`, and
   `Task.input/output` carry the structured payload from (1); or
   (b) add a new operation `$apply-settings` on `Device`/`DeviceMetric`
   that accepts the same payload and returns a `Task` representing the
   in-flight request.
3. A lifecycle model that distinguishes `requested`, `pending`,
   `applied`, `rejected`, and `superseded` settings — most cleanly
   layered on top of `Task.status` / `Task.businessStatus` plus a new
   `as-applied` `Observation` per setting (subject = Device or Patient
   per the existing Devices guidance) so that the audit trail of what
   the device actually did is queryable.

Land this in the OO Incubator IG first; promote to core only after at
least two implementers confirm the shape.

#### Justification

The ticket asks for exactly these three pieces, the workgroup has voted
11-1-0 in favour of action (per the 2026-02-02 comment), and the
incubator track is the FHIR-canonical place to prove out new
device-control patterns before disturbing core. Reusing `Task` for
transport keeps this aligned with how every other "make this happen"
workflow already works in FHIR (orders, fulfilment, supply requests),
and pairing it with an as-applied `Observation` is consistent with the
"recording" guidance that Elliot Silver gave on the
[Modelling device settings](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/implementers/topic/Modelling%20device%20settings/near/457395946)
Zulip thread. The Joint-OO-PC label means PC sign-off is needed for the
clinical-safety implications of having a device act on a request without
a human in the loop; the incubator gives that conversation room to
happen.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Do not invent a new resource or a new operation. Instead, publish a
*profile of `Task`* plus an *extension on `Device`* that together solve
the problem:

- `Task` profile `device-settings-change-request` with
  `Task.focus = Device | DeviceMetric`,
  `Task.code = http://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/task-code | change-settings`,
  and a required `Task.input` slice per setting that carries a
  `Coding` (the `DeviceMetric.type`/code), the desired value,
  and an optional target range.
- `Task.output` slice carries the as-applied value once the device
  acknowledges, plus a reference to the `Observation` that captured it.
- An extension on `Device` (`pending-settings-task`) lets a query
  against a `Device` discover any in-flight settings-change `Task`s
  without requiring a separate operation.

No new operation, no new resource, no core spec change — purely an OO
Incubator IG deliverable.

#### Justification

`Task` was designed precisely as the "make this happen" envelope, and
its existing `status`/`businessStatus`/`input`/`output` machinery already
covers requested/pending/applied without inventing parallel state. A
profile-only solution can ship inside the OO Incubator IG without core
spec churn, can be piloted by implementers immediately, and avoids the
substantial governance cost of a new resource at this stage of R6. It
also keeps Devices WG free to graduate to a dedicated resource later if
implementer feedback shows the `Task`-profile shape is too constraining
— deferring the resource decision is cheaper than retracting one. The
trade-off vs. Disposition A is loss of a custom operation surface, but
operations can always be layered on later without breaking the profile.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as "Considered for Future Use" / out of current scope, on the
basis that (a) FHIR already lets implementers represent the workflow
with `Task`/`ServiceRequest` directed at a human operator plus
`Observation` to record the resulting settings, (b) actively *commanding*
a medical device from a FHIR request crosses into device-safety and
regulatory territory that FHIR has historically left to device
protocols (IEEE 11073, vendor APIs), and (c) no implementer cohort has
publicly committed to building against the proposed mechanism.

#### Justification

There is real risk in standardising a "change my ventilator settings"
HTTP-shaped request without a clear safety, authentication, and
acknowledgement story; getting any of those wrong in a normative spec
is far worse than leaving the gap. The existing Zulip discussion on
[Modelling device settings](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/implementers/topic/Modelling%20device%20settings/near/457395946)
is purely about the *read* side, which suggests the implementer demand
for the write side is not yet broad. Declining (or deferring) keeps R6
focused and lets Devices WG re-open the request once a concrete
implementer use-case and safety model are on the table.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The 11-1-0 vote and the reversal of the earlier "Considered for Future
Use" resolution show the workgroup wants to act, which rules out
Disposition C. Between A and B, B delivers everything the ticket
requires — structured requested settings, a transport mechanism, and
explicit requested/pending/applied tracking — using only artifacts the
OO Incubator IG can ship without core-spec churn and without
prematurely committing to a new resource. It reuses the well-understood
`Task` lifecycle, stays consistent with Elliot Silver's existing
"settings as `Observation`" guidance from the Zulip thread, and leaves
the door open to graduate to a dedicated resource (Disposition A) once
implementer feedback justifies it. Recommend the workgroup adopt B for
the OO Incubator and treat A as a 6-12 month review checkpoint.
