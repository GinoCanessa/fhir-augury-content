## Summary

The reporter (Ulrike Merrick) flags that the existing narrative on the Specimen
resource page is misleading where it states that the resource "does not address
the recursive nature of containers or the tracking of the location of a
container within its parent container (for instance: a tube in a tray in a rack
in a freezer)." Because the container element has cardinality `0..*` and
because the `Device` resource provides `Device.parent` (and `Device.location`)
to express physical containment, the recursive-nesting use case is in fact
already covered. The ticket asks the workgroup to revise that text and, while
doing so, to clarify when to use `Specimen.collection.device` versus
`Specimen.container`. The ticket has been triaged as **Persuasive with
Modification** with a draft resolution that proposes replacing the sentence
above with: "The recursive nature of container should be expressed via
`Specimen.device.parent`."

No related Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were surfaced by the
cross-reference index — the only cross-references are FHIR artifact mentions
(`Specimen.collection.device`, `Specimen.container`, `Specimen.device.parent`).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the existing draft resolution verbatim. In the Specimen resource
narrative (the "Notes"/"Boundaries and Relationships"-style paragraph that
discusses container handling), replace the sentence:

> "It does not address the recursive nature of containers or the tracking of
> the location of a container within its parent container (for instance: a tube
> in a tray in a rack in a freezer)."

with:

> "The recursive nature of container should be expressed via
> `Specimen.device.parent`."

No structural change to the Specimen resource; this is a narrative-only edit.

#### Justification

The replacement sentence directly corrects the inaccuracy raised by the
reporter: recursive container nesting is in fact representable, via the chain
`Specimen.device → Device.parent → Device.parent → …`, which mirrors how the
broader FHIR model expresses physical containment of devices. The change is
non-breaking, requires no schema or constraint modification, and matches the
ballot-vote guidance signalled by the `SpecimenBlockVote` label that this
issue should be resolved as a coordinated narrative cleanup.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the spirit of the ticket but extend the narrative beyond the single
sentence in the draft resolution so that it answers both questions the
reporter raised. Replace the offending sentence with a short paragraph (kept
to one or two sentences each) that:

1. States that recursive container nesting is expressed by linking the
   container Device via `Specimen.device.parent` (i.e. the chain of
   `Device.parent` references), and that physical location may additionally be
   carried on `Device.location` where needed.
2. Adds explicit guidance on when to use `Specimen.collection.device` versus
   `Specimen.container` — for example: `Specimen.collection.device` describes
   the device used during the *act of collection* (e.g. a phlebotomy needle or
   swab), whereas `Specimen.container` (and the linked `Device` it references)
   describes the *holding container(s)* the specimen currently occupies and
   their nesting/location.

The structural model is unchanged; only the narrative text is expanded.

#### Justification

The reporter explicitly asked the workgroup to "provide better text here to
explain how nesting containers are to be handled (and maybe also explain when
to use Specimen.collection.device and when Specimen.container)". Disposition A
addresses only the first half of that ask. Adding the collection-vs-container
guidance heads off the very confusion that motivated the ticket and reduces
the likelihood of follow-up tickets on the same paragraph, at minimal
editorial cost and with no normative impact. It also stays well within
"Persuasive with Modification" because no element semantics change.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change and leave the existing Specimen narrative as-is, on the
grounds that the disputed sentence is a generalised caveat about the
limitations of in-resource modelling rather than a claim that recursive
nesting cannot be represented at all, and that the ticket therefore does not
identify a defect in the specification.

#### Justification

This is the weakest of the three options and is included for completeness.
Declining could be defended only by reading the original sentence very
narrowly. In practice the reporter — and almost certainly other implementers —
read it as asserting that recursive nesting is unsupported, which is
materially misleading given that `Device.parent` exists precisely for this
purpose. Declining would also be inconsistent with the ticket's existing
triage as **Persuasive with Modification**.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B fully discharges what the reporter asked for: it both corrects
the misleading sentence (the substance of Disposition A) and clarifies the
`Specimen.collection.device` vs. `Specimen.container` distinction, which the
reporter raised in the same breath. Because the change remains narrative-only
and introduces no structural or constraint changes, it stays consistent with
the existing **Persuasive with Modification** triage and the ballot-block
intent (`SpecimenBlockVote`). Disposition A is acceptable but partial;
Disposition C is not defensible given the existing resolution and the absence
of any community pushback on the cross-reference / Zulip surface.
