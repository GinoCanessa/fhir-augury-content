## Summary

Elliot Silver asks the Orders & Observations work group to reconcile and
broaden the participant (subject / receiver / deliverFor / deliverTo) target
types between the `SupplyRequest` and `SupplyDelivery` resources, and to
align their naming. Today `SupplyRequest.deliverFor` allows only `Patient`,
`SupplyRequest.deliverTo` allows `Organization | Location | Patient |
RelatedPerson`, while `SupplyDelivery.subject` allows `Patient | Group |
Organization` and `SupplyDelivery.receiver` allows `Practitioner |
PractitionerRole | Organization | Group`. The ticket requests (1) a
justification for why the analogous lists differ, (2) consideration of a
uniformly broader target list (Practitioner, PractitionerRole, Organization,
Group, RelatedPerson, CareTeam, HealthcareService, Location, Patient) on
both resources or documented rationale for why particular types are
excluded, (3) renaming `SupplyRequest.deliverFor` to `subject` for
alignment with `SupplyDelivery.subject` and choosing a consistent pair of
names for the "destination" concept (e.g., `deliverTo` and `deliveredTo`),
and (4) corresponding updates to search parameters and compartment
definitions for any added or removed target types. The cross-reference
check returned no related Jira tickets or Zulip threads — only element
mentions for `SupplyRequest.deliverFor` and `SupplyRequest.deliverTo`
within the ticket's own body.

## Details

**Description:**

SupplyRequest only supports a deliverFor Patient, and deliverTo
Organization, Location, Patient, RelatedPerson. SupplyDelivery supports
subject of Patient, Group, or Organization, and receiver of Practitioner,
PractitionerRole, Organization, or Group.

1. It isn't clear why the deliverFor/subject lists differ, and the
   deliverTo/receiver lists differ. I would expect that if an item can be
   delivered to a Group, it can be requested to be delivered to that Group,
   and so on. It may be that I'm not aligning the elements correctly
   between the two resources, but if there are elements in the one that
   align with the other, then I expect the subject lists to be the same.
2. It isn't clear why the deliverFor/subject lists and deliverTo/receiver
   lists are so narrow. Why can't a pad of paper be requested for a
   Practitioner? Consider widening the various lists of actors in the
   resources to include Practitioner, PractitionerRole, Organization,
   Group, RelatedPerson, CareTeam, HealthcareService, Location and Patient
   consistently. Alternatively, provide rationale in the Scope and Usage
   for why it doesn't make sense to have those resources listed.
3. Renaming SupplyRequest.deliverFor to subject to align with
   SupplyDelivery and common practice. Rename SupplyDeliver.destination or
   SupplyRequest.deliverTo to the same term (or similar, e.g. deliverTo
   and deliveredTo).
4. Ensure search parameters are updated if necessary to reflect the above
   changes.
5. Ensure compartment membership is updated based on any added or removed
   target types.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the OO Incubator IG (per the 2025-10-24 work group decision), profile
or version the resources as follows:

1. **Align target-type lists.** Replace the current asymmetric lists with
   a single shared superset on both resources:
   - `SupplyRequest.deliverFor` (to be renamed — see step 3) →
     `Patient | Group | Practitioner | PractitionerRole | RelatedPerson |
     CareTeam | Organization | HealthcareService | Location`.
   - `SupplyDelivery.subject` → same superset.
   - `SupplyRequest.deliverTo` (to be renamed) → `Location | Organization
     | Patient | RelatedPerson | Practitioner | PractitionerRole | Group
     | CareTeam | HealthcareService`.
   - `SupplyDelivery.destination` → same superset (currently
     `Location` 0..1 in R5; widen in the incubator treatment).
2. **Widen receiver consistently.** `SupplyDelivery.receiver` should
   accept the same actor set as any renamed `SupplyRequest` analogue, so
   the resource pair is symmetric end-to-end.
3. **Rename for alignment.** Rename `SupplyRequest.deliverFor` → `subject`
   to match `SupplyDelivery.subject`. Choose a consistent pair for the
   "where" concept — e.g., `SupplyRequest.deliverTo` and
   `SupplyDelivery.deliveredTo` (deprecating `SupplyDelivery.destination`),
   or align both on `destination`. The incubator IG should pick one
   convention and apply it to both.
4. **Search parameters.** Add `SearchParameter`s for any newly-allowed
   target types (e.g., `supplyrequest-practitioner`,
   `supplyrequest-careteam`, `supplydelivery-location`), and rename the
   existing `supplyrequest-deliverfor` / `-deliverto` parameters to match
   the new element names with `old-name` set for continuity.
5. **Compartments.** Update `CompartmentDefinition/Patient`,
   `/Practitioner`, `/RelatedPerson`, `/Device` (if relevant), and add
   entries for any newly-allowed types. For types removed from a target
   list (none in this proposal), remove the corresponding compartment
   entries.

#### Justification

The asymmetry between `SupplyRequest` and `SupplyDelivery` target-type
lists has no stated clinical or workflow rationale in the resource
narratives, and the reporter's "pad of paper for a Practitioner" example
shows real use cases that the current lists exclude. Aligning target
types, renaming `deliverFor` → `subject`, and standardising the
"destination" term follow the same alignment principles already applied
across the Request/Event pattern (e.g., `ServiceRequest.subject` /
`Procedure.subject`, `MedicationRequest.subject` /
`MedicationDispense.subject`). Doing this work in the OO Incubator IG
matches the work group's stated direction on 2025-10-24 and avoids the
backward-compatibility cost of renaming core FHIR elements outright.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Address only parts (1), (2), (4), and (5) of the ticket — widen the
target-type lists on both resources to the symmetric superset and update
search parameters / compartments — but **do not rename** the elements.
Keep `SupplyRequest.deliverFor`, `SupplyRequest.deliverTo`,
`SupplyDelivery.subject`, `SupplyDelivery.receiver`, and
`SupplyDelivery.destination` as their current names, and add a Scope and
Usage note to both resources documenting the Request↔Event mapping
(`deliverFor` ↔ `subject`, `deliverTo` ↔ `destination`) for readers.
Optionally, publish an Implementation Guide note in the OO Incubator IG
flagging the naming inconsistency as an item for the next major version.

#### Justification

Renaming core elements has a high cost: every implementation, profile
(including US Core, IPA, IPS, and jurisdictional supply IGs), test
script, and conformance resource that uses `SupplyRequest.deliverFor` or
`SupplyDelivery.destination` would need to update. The underlying
interoperability problem the reporter raised — that real-world requesters
can't cite a Practitioner or CareTeam as the party being supplied — is
solved entirely by widening the target lists. The naming concern, while
valid, is ergonomic rather than interoperability-breaking, and can be
documented in the narrative until a future non-substantive resource pass.
This preserves forward compatibility of existing instances and search
queries while still meeting the reporter's stated clinical motivation.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the ticket on the grounds that (a) the specific asymmetries in
target types were deliberate — `SupplyRequest` is patient-centred because
the dominant use cases in the existing implementer base are
patient-directed supply requests, and `SupplyDelivery` already carries a
broader set to cover logistics to non-patient actors (warehouses, wards,
practitioners) — and (b) renaming `deliverFor`/`deliverTo` would be a
breaking change to a Normative-candidate resource family with no
identified implementer demand beyond one reporter. Close the ticket with
a Scope and Usage addition explaining the rationale, as the ticket itself
proposes as an acceptable alternative in item (2).

#### Justification

The ticket explicitly offers "provide rationale in the Scope and Usage
for why it doesn't make sense to have those resources listed" as an
acceptable outcome. Without additional implementer evidence of use cases
for Practitioner/CareTeam/HealthcareService as `deliverFor`/`subject`,
and without cross-referenced Zulip discussion or linked tickets
corroborating demand (none were found in the Augury cross-reference or
Zulip search), a narrative-only response is the lowest-risk path. It
preserves instance validity, does not churn search parameters or
compartment definitions, and respects the pattern of requiring
demonstrated interoperability need before widening target lists.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the strongest match for the work group's current
position. The 2025-10-24 comment already commits to relocating the
change to the OO Incubator IG, and the incubator is the right place to
widen target-type lists without the backward-compatibility cost of
renaming core elements. The reporter's core concern — that practical
supply workflows need Practitioner, PractitionerRole, CareTeam, and
HealthcareService as legitimate "deliverFor" and "deliverTo" actors — is
fully resolved by widening the lists, and the documentation note covers
the naming alignment question in the near term. Disposition A goes
further by renaming elements, which is defensible in an incubator IG but
would still force downstream profile updates (US Core supply, IHE SPCS,
jurisdictional supply IGs); it should be held in reserve for a future
substantive pass once the widened target lists have seen implementer
feedback. Disposition C understates the interoperability gap the reporter
identified and should be rejected. The work group should ask Jose
Costa-Teixeira (per hbuitendijk's 2026-01-05 ping) to draft the symmetric
target-type superset and the Scope and Usage alignment note as the
concrete starting artifact.
