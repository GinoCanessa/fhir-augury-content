## Summary

FHIR-54835 is a tracker / housekeeping ticket whose entire body is a single
link to [FHIR-50600](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50600) ("Revisit
boundary description to composition"). FHIR-50600 was Deferred with
resolution "Considered for Future Use" in October 2025 because it is
entangled with the in-flight Grouping discussion that would not complete
before the November 1, 2025 ballot cut-off. FHIR-54835 exists to make sure
the deferred work is actually picked back up by Orders & Observations and
not silently lost.

The substantive change being tracked is a re-write of the wording in the
DiagnosticReport "Boundaries and Relationships" section that today says
Composition is more appropriate for narrative, low-workflow reports
(e.g., histology, mortuary). The reporter and recent commenters
(jdlnolen, marti_velezis) want the guidance flipped: **all** diagnostic
reports should use DiagnosticReport, with Composition layered on
top (via `DiagnosticReport.composition`) only when section-based
structure or whole-report authentication is needed. marti_velezis's
2025-10-16 comment captures the proposed replacement wording.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Re-open / un-defer FHIR-50600 against the next FHIR Core cycle and apply
marti_velezis's 2025-10-16 proposed rewrite to the "Boundaries and
Relationships" paragraph on the DiagnosticReport resource page. Concretely,
replace the current paragraph that reads:

> "If you have a highly structured report, then use DiagnosticReport — it
> has data and workflow support. Details about the request for a diagnostic
> investigation are captured in the various 'request' resources (e.g., the
> ServiceRequest) and allow the report to connect to clinical workflows.
> For more narrative driven reports with less work flow (histology/mortuary,
> etc.), the Composition resource would be more appropriate."

with text along the lines of:

> "Details about the request for a diagnostic investigation are captured in
> the various 'request' resources (e.g., the ServiceRequest) and allow the
> report to connect to clinical workflows. **All diagnostic reports should
> use the DiagnosticReport resource — it has data and workflow support.**
> The Composition resource **should be used in addition to DiagnosticReport
> (via `DiagnosticReport.composition`) when the content needs to be more
> structured with section headers, or when the entire report needs to be
> authenticated.**"

The change is text-only on the DiagnosticReport intro page; no element
definitions, cardinalities, invariants, or value sets are touched.
Mark FHIR-54835 as the tracker that drove the work and resolve
FHIR-50600 as "Persuasive" once the text lands.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the deferred ticket asks for and matches the
explicit consensus of the two commenters (jdlnolen, marti_velezis):
DiagnosticReport is the entry point for diagnostic work, and the
existing `DiagnosticReport.composition` element already provides the
structuring/authentication hook when implementers need it. The current
guidance is the genuine source of confusion the ticket is trying to
fix — implementers reading "use Composition for narrative reports"
have routed histology and mortuary content away from DiagnosticReport,
which is contrary to the resource's stated purpose. Because the change
is purely narrative, the implementer impact is low and the FMM /
normative status of DiagnosticReport is not affected.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the spirit of the rewrite, but split it into two co-ordinated edits
rather than a single paragraph swap, **and resolve the
`organizer-grouping` dependency first** before un-deferring FHIR-50600:

1. On the DiagnosticReport "Boundaries and Relationships" page, replace
   the paragraph as in Disposition A but add an explicit forward link to
   the Grouping / Organizer guidance once it lands, so the boundary text
   doesn't go stale a second time.
2. On the Composition resource page, add a reciprocal note clarifying
   that for diagnostic content Composition is expected to be referenced
   *from* `DiagnosticReport.composition` rather than used standalone.
3. Track both edits under FHIR-54835 and only close FHIR-50600 once both
   pages are aligned and the Grouping discussion has produced its
   guidance.

#### Justification

FHIR-50600's resolution explicitly cites the Grouping discussion as the
reason for the defer. Re-introducing the rewrite without first
landing the Grouping outcome risks producing a third round of
inconsistent boundary text. Updating both DiagnosticReport and
Composition in lock-step also closes the loop for implementers reading
the Composition page first — they currently get no signal that
`DiagnosticReport.composition` is the preferred wiring. The
implementer impact is still low (text-only on two intro pages), but
the change is more durable and less likely to be re-opened.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-54835 as "Not Persuasive — Already Tracked" and leave
FHIR-50600 in its current Deferred / "Considered for Future Use"
state. Do not change the DiagnosticReport "Boundaries and
Relationships" text in this cycle.

#### Justification

FHIR-50600 was deliberately deferred by Orders & Observations because
it is coupled to the unresolved Grouping / Organizer work, and the
existing deferred status already keeps it visible on the future-cycle
backlog. FHIR-54835 contributes no new technical content — it is
purely a "please don't forget" pointer — so its work is arguably
already done by virtue of FHIR-50600 still being in the queue. This
disposition is only defensible if O&O concludes that the Grouping
discussion is still the gating dependency and that no narrative-only
fix can be safely landed ahead of it.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The substantive guidance change in FHIR-50600 is clearly the right
direction — both commenters agree, and the current "use Composition
for narrative reports" wording actively misroutes implementers — so
some form of acceptance (A or B) is the right answer rather than a
decline. However, FHIR-50600 was not deferred by accident: O&O
explicitly tied it to the Grouping / Organizer discussion. Disposition
B keeps the technical content of A intact but sequences it behind the
Grouping outcome and adds the reciprocal note on Composition so the
two resource pages stop disagreeing with each other. That sequencing is
cheap (text-only edits, no schema impact) and removes the most likely
reason this ticket would land in front of the workgroup a third time.
If the workgroup judges the Grouping work is far enough along to no
longer be a real blocker, fall back to Disposition A and apply the
rewrite immediately.
