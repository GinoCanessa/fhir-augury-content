## Summary

The reporter, Hans Buitendijk, asks the EU Laboratory Report (FHIR) IG
authors to clarify the introduction text and the accompanying conceptual
diagram so that readers immediately understand that the IG supports two
exchange shapes: (a) a full FHIR Document (Composition-anchored Bundle
with all sections), **or** (b) a DiagnosticReport conveyed with just the
relevant Observations. As written, the introduction and diagram lean
toward the document-centric view and do not make the DiagnosticReport-only
path obvious to implementers, which has caused confusion about whether
the IG mandates FHIR Documents.

No cross-referenced Jira tickets were returned by the
`cross-referenced` query, so this is a stand-alone editorial/scope
clarification request rather than part of a multi-ticket cluster.

## Details

### [europe > Laboratory Report - FHIR/v2 Workflow verse FHIR Document](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/europe/topic/Laboratory%20Report%20-%20FHIR%2Fv2%20Workflow%20verse%20FHIR%20Document/near/535424321)

Kevin Mayfield opened the thread (Aug 2025) describing a UK/EU
implementation context that mixes HL7 v2 / IHE LTW workflow with FHIR
RESTful and FHIR Document patterns, and asked how `DiagnosticReport`
relates to `Composition` in the EU Laboratory Report IG. Rob Hausam
pointed at §5.1 "Design Choice" of the EU Lab IG, which explains that
the IG deliberately compromises between the FHIR-Document approach and
the DiagnosticReport approach by leveraging the R5 design where
`DiagnosticReport` carries a reference to `Composition`. Jose Costa
Teixeira agreed the report is the end-point of a workflow that may or
may not produce a document, and Rob acknowledged the compromise
"satisfies both everyone — and no one." The discussion confirms exactly
the confusion this ticket is trying to fix in the IG narrative: readers
infer that a `Composition` is mandatory when in fact the IG intends to
support both shapes.

Participants: Kevin Mayfield, Jose Costa Teixeira, Rob Hausam.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Edit the EU Laboratory Report (FHIR) IG **Introduction** page and the
accompanying conceptual diagram to explicitly call out two supported
exchange shapes:

1. **Full FHIR Document** — a Bundle of `type = document` anchored on a
   `Composition` (with the laboratory-report sections), referencing all
   relevant `Observation`, `Specimen`, `Practitioner`, `Patient`, etc.
2. **DiagnosticReport-only** — a `DiagnosticReport` instance plus the
   relevant `Observation` (and supporting) resources, exchanged via FHIR
   RESTful or messaging, *without* a wrapping `Composition` /
   document Bundle.

Concretely:

- Add a short "Exchange paradigms" subsection at the top of the
  Introduction that names the two shapes and links to §5.1 "Design
  Choice".
- Update the architecture diagram so the `DiagnosticReport`-only path
  is drawn as a peer (not a child) of the `Composition`/Document path,
  with a label such as "or: DiagnosticReport with referenced
  Observations".
- Add a one-line note that the R5 `DiagnosticReport.composition`
  reference is the bridge between the two shapes — present when a
  document is produced, absent when only the report is exchanged.

No structural changes to profiles, value sets, or examples are required;
this is editorial / diagram work.

#### Justification

The ticket is an editorial clarification, not a model change, and the
Zulip "europe" thread confirms that real implementers (Kevin Mayfield's
NW England programme) already read the IG as document-centric and
needed §5.1 pointed out to them. Rob Hausam's summary of the design
choice — that the IG intentionally accommodates both shapes via the R5
`DiagnosticReport → Composition` reference — is exactly what is missing
from the Introduction. Accepting the request closes the gap between
intent and presentation, costs no schema churn, is fully backward
compatible, and reduces the risk that downstream IGs (e.g. genomics,
national profiles) will misread the EU Laboratory Report IG as
mandating FHIR Documents.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than only adjusting the Introduction and diagram, **add a
dedicated "Conformance & Exchange Options" page** to the IG that:

- Enumerates the supported exchange shapes (Document, DiagnosticReport
  + Observations, and — optionally — a PDF/MHD attachment shape that
  the Zulip thread flagged as common in practice).
- For each shape, lists the must-support resources, the cardinality
  expectations on `DiagnosticReport.composition`, and which
  conformance resources (CapabilityStatements, profiles) apply.
- Provides one minimal example per shape.
- Cross-links from the Introduction, the architecture diagram, and
  §5.1 "Design Choice".

#### Justification

The reporter's underlying need is that implementers cannot tell which
shape the IG expects. A dedicated conformance page addresses that need
more durably than diagram tweaks alone, because it gives implementers a
single normative-feeling location to point at in procurement and
gateway-configuration discussions. It also surfaces the third pattern
(PDF in MHD) that the Zulip thread shows is widespread in EU/UK
practice but currently absent from the IG narrative. The trade-off
versus Disposition A is more authoring effort and the need for the WG
to agree on whether PDF/MHD is in scope; if it is not, the page can
still cover the two FHIR-native shapes and the introduction edits from
Disposition A become a subset of this work.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Not Persuasive** on the grounds that §5.1 "Design
Choice" already documents the rationale for supporting both shapes, and
the IG's profiles do not require a `Composition` to be present when a
`DiagnosticReport` is exchanged on its own. Direct readers to §5.1 and
mark the ticket as a documentation-discoverability issue to be handled
in normal IG editing rather than as a tracked change request.

#### Justification

The IG technically already supports both shapes — the R5
`DiagnosticReport.composition` reference is optional, and §5.1
explicitly describes the compromise. A reviewer could reasonably argue
that no specification text needs to change and that the fix is for
implementers to read §5.1. This disposition keeps the IG ballot/change
log cleaner.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket asks for a low-cost editorial fix to a real, observed source
of implementer confusion, and the Zulip europe-stream thread is direct
evidence that even engaged community members (Kevin Mayfield, Jose
Costa Teixeira) had to be pointed at §5.1 to discover the intended
dual-shape model. Disposition C is defensible only on the narrowest
"the spec is already correct" reading and ignores that the
*introduction and diagram are the first things readers see* — exactly
the surfaces the reporter named. Disposition B is attractive long-term
but is materially larger work, drags in the in-scope/out-of-scope
question for PDF/MHD, and risks slipping past the current ballot
window. Accepting as requested (A) lands the clarification quickly and
leaves Disposition B available as a follow-up if the WG later wants a
formal conformance-options page; the edits proposed in A are a strict
subset of B and would not be wasted.
