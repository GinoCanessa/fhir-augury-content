## Summary

FHIR-54971 is an Orders & Observations change request, raised by Marti Velezis
at highest priority, that asks the workgroup to clean up the status surface
and terminology of the `DeviceAssociation` resource ahead of its progression.
Three related concerns are bundled in this single ticket:

1. The status-style elements on `DeviceAssociation` — `status`,
   `associationStatus`, and the (currently missing) `availabilityStatus` —
   need to be made clearly distinct from each other. The reporter notes
   that `availabilityStatus` was not included, even though it may overlap
   with `associationStatus`, and asks the WG to reconcile this.
2. The `relationship` element (and presumably the
   `deviceassociation-relationship` value set behind it) needs a
   completeness review.
3. The CodeSystems and ValueSets bound by `DeviceAssociation` should be
   migrated to THO (terminology.hl7.org) unless they are merely examples.

The request sits in a tight cluster of related DeviceAssociation tickets.
[FHIR-54463](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54463) (Triaged) is essentially
the same concern from Elliot Silver, asking that the definitions and bindings
of `relationship`, `status`, `statusReason`, and `associationStatus` be made
clear, distinct, and comprehensive — and explicitly asks how `relationship`
relates to `status` / `period` (e.g., does `relationship` flip from
"implanted" to "explanted" when the device is removed?).
[FHIR-55254](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-55254) (Triaged, Persuasive
with Modification, raised by Lloyd McKenzie via FMG comment imports)
identifies the specific value sets that are THO migration candidates:
`deviceassociation-relationship`,
`deviceassociation-status-reason`, and
`deviceassociation-association-status`. Together with the already-Applied
[FHIR-46962](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-46962) (status narrative
referencing names that no longer exist; `availabilityStatus` missing) and
[FHIR-48713](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48713) (Expand
DeviceAssociation-status) plus the R6 Ballot Notes
[FHIR-53628](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53628), this ticket is the
"umbrella" that should drive a coordinated disposition rather than a
piecemeal fix.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the three asks as a coordinated bundle on the `DeviceAssociation`
resource:

1. **Status elements (re-introduce `availabilityStatus` and disambiguate):**
   - Keep `DeviceAssociation.status` as the resource-level workflow status
     (active | entered-in-error | …).
   - Keep `DeviceAssociation.associationStatus` for the *association*
     between the Device and the subject (e.g., implanted, attached,
     scheduled, removed).
   - Re-introduce `DeviceAssociation.availabilityStatus` for the
     *availability* of the device-as-equipment in the context of the
     association (e.g., available, in-use, unavailable, abandoned), with a
     value set that subsumes the `abandoned` code added under
     [FHIR-48713](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48713).
   - Tighten each element's definition, comment, and the status-narrative
     section so the three are explicitly orthogonal. Cross-reference
     [FHIR-46962](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-46962) so the
     status-narrative element list is corrected at the same time.
2. **Relationship completeness review:** Audit
   `deviceassociation-relationship` against the use-cases enumerated in
   [FHIR-54463](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54463) (especially the
   implant/explant lifecycle question) and add codes for any gap
   (operator role, custodian, transport carrier, etc.). Document in the
   element's comment whether `relationship` mutates over time or whether
   a new `DeviceAssociation` instance is expected.
3. **Move CodeSystems / ValueSets to THO:** Migrate
   `deviceassociation-relationship`,
   `deviceassociation-status-reason`, and
   `deviceassociation-association-status` (i.e., the three identified by
   [FHIR-55254](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-55254)) plus the
   `availabilityStatus` value set introduced under (1) to THO. Leave any
   strictly example-only systems in the FHIR Core spec.

Resolve FHIR-54971, FHIR-54463, and FHIR-55254 together with this single
coordinated change set, and link FHIR-46962 / FHIR-48713 as superseded /
subsumed where appropriate.

#### Justification

The three asks are tightly coupled: the value-set migration in (3) is
cleanest if the value sets are first stabilised by (1) and (2); and
re-introducing `availabilityStatus` resolves the very confusion
[FHIR-46962](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-46962) flagged about
narrative referring to elements that didn't exist. Doing this as one
WG decision is also consistent with the R6 ballot-notes commitment in
[FHIR-53628](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53628), which already
signals balloters that DeviceAssociation status semantics are being
reviewed in this cycle. Migrating the named value sets to THO matches
HL7 governance: these vocabularies are plausibly reusable in CDA / V2
device-binding contexts (Lloyd's import-comment 219), and THO is the
right home once they are stable.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — Split & Sequence

#### Proposal

Decline FHIR-54971 *as a single change* and instead use it as a tracking
ticket whose resolution is "see linked tickets". Split the work
explicitly:

- Resolve the **status-element disambiguation** under
  [FHIR-54463](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54463) (which already
  carries the more detailed analytical statement of the problem).
  Include the explicit decision on whether `availabilityStatus` is
  re-introduced as a distinct element, modelled as a slice / extension
  of `associationStatus`, or dropped in favour of `statusReason`-style
  qualification.
- Resolve the **value-set / THO migration** under
  [FHIR-55254](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-55254), but **only after**
  FHIR-54463 lands, so that the migrated artefacts do not need a
  follow-up rev in THO immediately after publication.
- Resolve the **relationship completeness** review as a small focused
  child ticket spun off this one (or fold it into FHIR-54463), with a
  concrete checklist of use-cases (implant / explant, attach / detach,
  ownership, custody, operator) explicitly enumerated.

Mark FHIR-54971 as Persuasive with Modification, recording the split.

#### Justification

FHIR-54971's text is intentionally short and bundles three quite
different kinds of change: a model change, a vocabulary completeness
review, and a terminology-governance migration. Each has a different
review path (FMG / TSMG vs. WG vs. internal), and bundling them into a
single resolution risks one sub-decision blocking the others. Sequencing
also reduces churn for THO: migrating value sets that are still being
edited would force at least one immediate re-publication. Both of the
linked tickets that already host the substantive analysis
([FHIR-54463](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54463),
[FHIR-55254](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-55254)) are still Triaged
/ Unresolved, so this ticket can act as the umbrella without losing any
of the asks. The cost is one extra round of WG bookkeeping versus
Disposition A.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline FHIR-54971 as a duplicate / umbrella restatement of work already
captured by other open tickets. Specifically:

- The status-element disambiguation and relationship-completeness asks
  are already covered, in greater detail, by
  [FHIR-54463](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54463).
- The THO-migration ask is already covered, with the specific value sets
  named, by [FHIR-55254](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-55254) (which
  is already Persuasive with Modification).
- The `availabilityStatus` gap is already on the record via
  [FHIR-46962](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-46962).

Close FHIR-54971 as duplicate, linking to the three tickets above as the
canonical record.

#### Justification

Closing duplicate ballot-style change requests is a routine WG hygiene
action and avoids a situation where the same model question gets two
different WG decisions in two different tickets. The substantive work
will still happen — just under the better-scoped tickets that already
hold the analysis and (in FHIR-55254's case) a partial WG decision.

The trade-off is that the reporter (Marti Velezis) framed FHIR-54971 as
a coordination request — an explicit "do these three together" — and
that framing is lost if it is closed as duplicate. If the WG values
that coordination signal, Disposition A or B is preferable.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A (Accept As Requested, as a coordinated bundle).

The three asks in FHIR-54971 are not really independent: deciding whether
`availabilityStatus` exists drives both which value sets need to be defined
(and therefore migrated to THO under FHIR-55254) and how the
`relationship`-completeness review must answer the implant/explant lifecycle
question raised in FHIR-54463. Sequencing them (Disposition B) introduces
real bookkeeping cost without removing the underlying coupling, and
declining as duplicate (Disposition C) discards the reporter's explicit
coordination signal at exactly the moment R6 ballot disposition makes that
signal most useful (per FHIR-53628). The "Highest" priority and the fact
that the reporter is Marti Velezis — the same person who carried the R6
ballot-notes ticket through to Applied — suggests the WG is already
expecting a coordinated answer here. Accepting as requested, while
explicitly resolving FHIR-54463 and FHIR-55254 in the same disposition
discussion, is the cleanest path to a single, defensible R6 story for
DeviceAssociation status semantics and terminology governance.
