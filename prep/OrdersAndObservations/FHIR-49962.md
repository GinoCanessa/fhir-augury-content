## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Persist exactly what the reporter has already pushed to
`HL7/fhir-order-catalog` `master`:

1. In `input/ig-order-catalog.xml`, keep the added `<extension>` and
   `<parameter>` entries on the `ImplementationGuide` resource and the
   corrected `<contact>` and `<publisher>` blocks (commit
   [`aa75464`](https://github.com/HL7/fhir-order-catalog/commit/aa7546450cffb725e9560bda9e4170c06130e720),
   +95 / −2 lines, single file).
2. Keep the newly-added repo-root [`oids.ini`](https://github.com/HL7/fhir-order-catalog/commit/d5e561a43697535afbac0f03d55011231594f823)
   (103 lines) alongside `ig.ini`, so the IG-publisher has a stable OID
   manifest to draw from.
3. Re-run `_genonce.{sh,bat}` (after `_updatePublisher.{sh,bat}` if needed)
   and visually confirm the rendered IG home page shows the corrected
   publisher and contact, and that the IG QA report no longer flags missing
   `extension` / `parameter` declarations.
4. Resolve FHIR-49962 as **Persuasive** (or **Persuasive with Modification**
   only if QA reveals follow-on edits) once the build is clean on the CI
   branch.

#### Justification

This is a Technical Correction in the strictest sense: the reporter is also
the committer, the change is scoped entirely to IG-publisher control-file
metadata (`input/ig-order-catalog.xml` + `oids.ini`), and it does not touch
any normative profile, terminology binding, or narrative. The briefing's
"authoring roots" and "no `sushi-config.yaml` at root" facts confirm that
`input/ig-order-catalog.xml` is the correct surface for this kind of edit,
and the change is internally consistent with the rest of the IG (no FSH
duplication, no generated-area edits). The commits are already on `master`
upstream; ratifying them simply matches OO process to the as-shipped state.

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the substance of the change but split the disposition along
artifact lines before resolving:

1. Accept commit
   [`aa75464`](https://github.com/HL7/fhir-order-catalog/commit/aa7546450cffb725e9560bda9e4170c06130e720)
   under FHIR-49962 — its scope (`input/ig-order-catalog.xml` extensions /
   parameters / contact / publisher) matches the ticket title exactly.
2. Move the `oids.ini` addition (commit
   [`d5e561a`](https://github.com/HL7/fhir-order-catalog/commit/d5e561a43697535afbac0f03d55011231594f823))
   to a separate, freshly-filed Technical Correction (e.g., "Add `oids.ini`
   for stable OID assignment in fhir-order-catalog IG"), with its own
   description spelling out which OIDs are being claimed and the rationale
   for managing them in-repo rather than via the publisher's auto-assignment.
3. Cross-link the new ticket to FHIR-49962 in Jira so the audit trail shows
   both pieces of work.

#### Justification

`oids.ini` is conceptually distinct from "fix `ImplementationGuide` resource
extensions/parameters and the contact/publisher metadata" — it is a new
publisher-input file that affects OID assignment policy for every artifact in
the IG, not a metadata correction on an existing artifact. The briefing notes
that the IG is XML-driven and that `fsh-generated/` etc. are generated areas;
introducing a new persistent repo-root `*.ini` deserves its own paper trail
both for QA reviewers (so they know where to look) and for downstream
implementers who read OO Jira to understand IG-level changes. This disposition
preserves the reporter's work but improves traceability at the cost of one
extra Jira issue.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the ticket as **Not Persuasive (Procedural)** on the grounds that
FHIR-49962 was filed without any description, comments, or proposal text and
that all substantive change had already been pushed to `master` by the
reporter the same day. Ask the reporter to re-file with (a) a written
statement of which `ImplementationGuide` extensions / parameters were missing
and why each was added, (b) what the prior `contact` / `publisher` values
were and what the corrected values are, and (c) a separate ticket for
`oids.ini`. In the meantime, keep the commits in place (they are not being
reverted) but mark the Jira issue **Not Persuasive** so the OO ballot record
is not polluted with empty-bodied technical corrections.

#### Justification

OO disposition rigor depends on the Jira record being self-contained: a
future reviewer reading FHIR-49962 today cannot tell, from Jira alone, what
was changed or why. Declining as a procedural matter — without rejecting the
underlying technical work — sets a healthy precedent that even reporter-author
"Technical Correction" tickets need to spell out the change in the issue body
so the workgroup can vote on something more than a commit title.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The change is unambiguously IG-housekeeping, the reporter is the de facto
custodian of the Order Catalog IG, and the edits land exactly where the
briefing says metadata edits belong (`input/ig-order-catalog.xml`, plus a
new repo-root `oids.ini` outside the publisher-generated areas). The
commits' scope (one file in `aa75464`, one new file in `d5e561a`) is small
and safely reversible. Disposition B's split-the-ticket suggestion has
real audit-trail value but adds Jira churn for what is, in practice, a
single-author maintenance push; the workgroup can adopt B's hygiene rule
going forward without holding up this specific fix. Disposition C's
procedural objection is fair on principle, but unilaterally declining a
reporter-author Technical Correction whose code is already on `master`
would invite avoidable rework with no spec-level benefit. Accept as
requested, ratify the as-shipped state, and — if the workgroup agrees with
B's hygiene point — capture it as a process note for future Order Catalog
IG technical corrections rather than as a precondition for resolving
FHIR-49962.
