## Summary

FHIR-50677 asks the Cancer Pathology Data Sharing (FHIR) IG to provide
explicit guidance and worked examples for representing specimen
derivation hierarchies typical of anatomic pathology — original
specimen → block → sections → slides — and to clarify the IG's expected
use of `Specimen.parent` (and, where applicable, `Specimen.derivedFrom`)
so each pathology Observation (e.g., microscopic exam, ER/PR/HER2) can
be traced back to the specific derived specimen from which it
originated. The original ticket text was modest, but the May 15th and
follow-up discussions (captured in the comments) expanded the scope to
cover comprehensive parent-child chains across multiple labs (in-house
processing, send-outs, reference labs). The ticket was closed during
STU2 as "Considered for Future Use" with the IG taking a flattened MVP
approach, then **re-opened post STU2 publication** on 2025-10-03 by
lynn_laakso so it can be addressed for the next ballot cycle. The
related ticket [FHIR-51787](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51787)
(Resolved – Persuasive with Modification) added the IG's "items
considered for future use" section and is the placeholder under which
this issue was previously parked.

## Details

**Description:**

In pathology, specimens go through multiple derived states e.g.,
original specimen → block → slices → slides. Each derived specimen is
used for specific observations (e.g., microscopic exam, ER/PR/HER2).
The IG should clarify how this hierarchy is modelled using the Specimen
resource, including appropriate use of `parent` and `derivedFrom`. The
HL7 specimen working group may have some examples.

Proposed Resolution: Include guidance and examples showing the
derivation tree of specimens and how each is linked to the observations
that originate from them.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a dedicated "Specimen Derivation Hierarchies" guidance section to
the Cancer Pathology Data Sharing IG that:

1. Normatively describes the IG's use of `Specimen.parent` (chain back
   to the originally collected specimen) and clarifies when (if ever)
   `Specimen.derivedFrom` should be used in this IG vs. relying on
   `Specimen.parent` only — pick one canonical pattern and forbid the
   other to avoid implementer drift.
2. States the obligations already piloted in the MVP as conformance
   rules: (a) every `Observation.specimen` SHALL reference the
   immediate specimen the observation was performed on; (b) every
   exchanged child `Specimen` SHALL carry `Specimen.parent` back to a
   top-of-chain specimen carrying the collection site, collection
   date/time, and specimen type; (c) source site, collection
   date/time, and specimen type SHALL appear on the top-of-chain
   specimen and need not be repeated on derived specimens.
3. Adds at least two worked examples covering the patterns called out
   in the discussion: (i) a breast core biopsy → block → slide chain
   with ER/PR/HER2 IHC observations bound to the slide-level
   specimens, and (ii) a multi-lab-area bone marrow biopsy producing
   touch imprints, blocks, flow aliquots, and a cytogenetics aliquot,
   with each downstream Observation/DiagnosticReport bound to the
   correct derived specimen. Borrow narrative from NAACCR (per the
   2025-05-14 comment) and re-use / extend the existing
   `Specimen-specimen-breast` example and the IHE SDC
   `Specimen_Lung.fsh` pattern.
4. Adds a third example (or note) covering the **send-out / reference
   lab** case so the chain crosses organizations.
5. Removes the FHIR-50677 placeholder from the IG's "Considered for
   Future Use" section once the guidance lands.

#### Justification

This is what the ticket originally asks for, what the May 15th
discussion ultimately deferred only because of timeline pressure, and
what the post-STU2 re-open explicitly invites. It also aligns with
apitkus's CLIA / CAP-eCP argument: pathology reports cannot be
faithfully or compliantly represented when the link from each
synoptic Observation back to the actual derived specimen is left
implicit. The MVP rules are already well-formed (they were the
2025-05-20 consensus); promoting them from a deferred intent to
normative IG guidance plus examples is a low-risk, high-value win for
implementers and is consistent with how other anatomic-pathology FHIR
work (and the IHE SDC sample data) already model the hierarchy.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt the same MVP rules normatively (as in A.2 above) but **stage the
deliverable**: in this ballot cycle land (i) the conformance language
on `Specimen.parent` and `Observation.specimen`, (ii) the two
in-IG-scope examples (breast IHC chain and bone marrow multi-area
chain), and (iii) a dedicated narrative page describing the derivation
model. Defer the cross-organization send-out / reference-lab example
and any new `Obligation`-based machine-readable expression of these
rules to the next iteration, tracking them as separate child tickets
under FHIR-50677. Choose `Specimen.parent` as the **only** chaining
mechanism in this IG and explicitly mark `Specimen.derivedFrom` as
out-of-scope (do not use) to avoid two parallel hierarchies.

#### Justification

The May 15th and July 14th discussions show that the workgroup's
hesitation was scope/complexity, not direction — deferral was
explicitly because cross-lab chains are hard. Splitting "in-house
hierarchy + conformance + two examples" (low-risk, mostly text and
example artifacts) from "cross-organization chain + obligations"
(higher-risk, needs more cross-WG conversation, possibly Specimen
profile constraints and `Provenance`) lets the IG ship the bulk of
the implementer-facing benefit now without re-litigating the harder
multi-org modelling under ballot pressure. Pinning the IG to
`Specimen.parent` only addresses Riki's note that the prior MVP text
in the resolution shouldn't be ambiguous about which mechanism is
canonical, and reduces the risk of two implementers picking different
links and breaking interoperability.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline FHIR-50677 as a stand-alone change to the Cancer Pathology
Data Sharing IG and instead keep it parked under the "Considered for
Future Use" section established by
[FHIR-51787](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51787), on the grounds
that (a) base FHIR `Specimen.parent` already provides the modelling
mechanism, (b) the IG already includes specimen example artifacts
(e.g., `Specimen-specimen-breast`), and (c) the comprehensive
multi-lab derivation work needs broader cross-WG (specimen, OO, PHWG)
input than this ticket can drive on its own.

#### Justification

This is the position taken at the 2025-08-14 vote (13-0-1) and is
defensible on the grounds that the IG already references existing
mechanisms and that nothing in base FHIR prevents an implementer from
chaining specimens today. It also avoids re-opening a contested area
under post-publication time pressure.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B captures the genuine signal from the conversation: the
workgroup did not disagree with the substance of FHIR-50677, only with
trying to ship the entire multi-organization story in one cycle. The
post-STU2 re-open by lynn_laakso, the CAP/CLIA arguments from apitkus,
and the existence of usable model examples (the IHE SDC Lung FSH and
the IG's own breast specimen) together make declining (C) hard to
defend — implementers consuming the IG today have no normative
guidance on how to bind synoptic Observations to derived specimens,
which is the central failure mode the ticket describes. Pure
acceptance (A), including the cross-organization example, risks
re-creating the same scope pressure that caused the May 15th deferral
and may not survive ballot. Staging the work — normative parent/
observation rules + two in-house examples now, send-out and
Obligation-based expressions in a tracked follow-up — gives the
workgroup a concrete, shippable deliverable while preserving room for
the harder cross-org modelling to mature with input from the HL7
Specimen WG (as the original ticket suggested) and the broader
anatomic pathology community.
