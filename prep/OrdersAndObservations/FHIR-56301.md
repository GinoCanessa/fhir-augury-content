## Summary

Lloyd McKenzie argues that the disposition applied for [FHIR-53384](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53384) — which broadened `Specimen.subject` to allow `RelatedPerson` (and added clarifying narrative that the related person can stand in for animals or other non-patient sources) — is not workable from a Patient-compartment / search-parameter perspective. His position is that every resource that lives in a particular patient's record MUST hold a direct reference to that `Patient`, because the `patient` search parameter and Patient compartment are defined directly (and chaining through `RelatedPerson` is not permitted in search parameter definitions). He therefore asks O&O to instead follow the `Observation.subject` / `Observation.focus` pattern: keep `Specimen.subject` pointing at the record's `Patient`, and add a separate `Specimen.focus` element (or equivalent) for the entity the specimen actually came from when that is something other than the record's patient (e.g., a dog suspected of rabies, or a parent in trio testing). The ticket is effectively a follow-on / re-open request against [FHIR-53384](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53384), which has already been marked Applied with a "Persuasive with Modification" resolution and merged via [HL7/fhir#4027](https://github.com/HL7/fhir/pull/4027); FHIR-56301 captures Lloyd's stated concern from that ticket's comment thread so it can be tracked and resolved on its own merits before the R6 normative ballot.

## Details

**Description:**

Every resource associated with a patient's record MUST have a direct reference to the Patient resource to support the 'patient' search parameter which is necessary to allow the resource to be in the Patient compartment (which in turn is necessary for access control and other reasons). The patient search parameter cannot be defined via chaining through related person because chaining isn't allowed in search parameter definitions. Please follow the same approach as used by Observation and have a separate 'focus' element if a Specimen needs to be tied to someone/something other than the patient whose record the specimen is tied to.

## Related Zulip Discussions

### [patient administration WG > Specimen source RelatedPerson | Patient](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/patient%20administration%20WG/topic/Specimen%20source%20RelatedPerson%20%7C%20Patient)

The originating discussion (started 2025-12-10, with later updates through early 2026) where HL7 EU / EHDS and O&O modeled how to represent specimens whose source is not the record's patient — e.g., a dog suspected of rabies, a tick carrying a pathogen, or parents in genomic trio testing. Two camps emerged. One camp (Richard Townley-O'Neill, Patrick Werner, Lloyd McKenzie, Alexander Henket, Rik Smithies) argued that `RelatedPerson` is a better fit than coercing animals/other sources into `Patient` (the record's subject), because `Patient` is by definition the focus of the health record and the dog/tick/parent is *not* receiving care in this record. The other camp (Brian Postlethwaite, John Moehrke) preferred either keeping `Specimen.subject` as the record's `Patient` (with the animal recorded elsewhere — `Reference.display`, an extension, or no `subject` at all), or noted that `Specimen.subject` is optional anyway. Several participants observed that `RelatedPerson`'s name and current `Scope & Usage` lean toward humans/service animals and would need narrative clarification. The thread converged enough for Patrick Werner to file [FHIR-53384](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53384) in O&O to permit `RelatedPerson` on `Specimen.subject`; the open question (the Patient-compartment / `patient` search-parameter implication that Lloyd raised here and now in FHIR-56301) was not resolved in the thread.

Lloyd McKenzie, Brian Postlethwaite, Patrick Werner, Richard Townley-O'Neill, Rik Smithies, Alexander Henket, John Moehrke, Riki Merrick.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Reverse the `Specimen.subject` widening from [FHIR-53384](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53384) and instead adopt the `Observation` pattern on `Specimen`:

1. In `HL7/fhir`, edit the `Specimen` StructureDefinition's `<differential>` under `source/specimen/` to **remove `RelatedPerson` from the allowed target types of `Specimen.subject`**, restoring the pre-FHIR-53384 set (`Patient | Group | Device | Substance | Location | BiologicallyDerivedProduct`). `Specimen.subject` continues to point at the `Patient` whose record the specimen lives in (or `Group`/`Device`/`Substance`/`Location`/`BiologicallyDerivedProduct` for non-patient specimens), preserving the `patient` SearchParameter and Patient-compartment contract.
2. Add a new optional `Specimen.focus 0..* Reference(Patient | Group | Device | Substance | Location | RelatedPerson | BiologicallyDerivedProduct)` (or `CodeableReference` of the same target set, per the FHIR-53384 wording) to carry "the entity the specimen actually came from" when that differs from `subject`. This mirrors `Observation.subject` / `Observation.focus`.
3. Update `Specimen.subject` Comments to drop the "RelatedPerson can be used for specimens from other entities" sentence added under FHIR-53384 motion #2, and instead point readers at `Specimen.focus` for that scenario.
4. Confirm/keep the existing `patient` SearchParameter for `Specimen` defined directly on `Specimen.subject` so Specimen remains in the Patient compartment.

#### Justification

This is exactly Lloyd's stated request and is the only option that keeps `Specimen` cleanly inside the Patient compartment without forcing implementers to chain a SearchParameter through `RelatedPerson` (which the spec does not allow). It reuses an established, normative pattern (`Observation.subject` / `Observation.focus`) that O&O already maintains, so implementer guidance, profiling patterns, and downstream IGs (e.g., Genomics Reporting trio testing, veterinary use cases the original Zulip thread described) have a precedent to follow. It also resolves the practical concern multiple PA participants (Brian Postlethwaite, John Moehrke) raised in the Zulip thread that `Specimen.subject` should remain the record's patient. The cost is real but bounded: it is a breaking change relative to the just-merged [HL7/fhir#4027](https://github.com/HL7/fhir/pull/4027), and it adds one new element to a near-normative resource on the eve of the R6 normative ballot — both of which the recommendation below weighs.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the `RelatedPerson` target on `Specimen.subject` that was added under FHIR-53384, **and** additionally add a new `Specimen.focus` (as in Disposition A) for richer cases, but **also** define the `patient` SearchParameter / Patient-compartment membership for `Specimen` such that the contract Lloyd invokes is preserved:

1. Retain the FHIR-53384 change to `Specimen.subject` (allowing `RelatedPerson`) under `source/specimen/`.
2. Add `Specimen.focus 0..* Reference(...)` (same target set as Disposition A, minus `Patient`/include as appropriate) for non-subject sources of the specimen.
3. Define a top-level `Specimen.subject` element invariant (or narrative requirement) that when the specimen lives in a particular patient's record, **either** `Specimen.subject` is a `Patient` reference **or** an additional element (e.g., a new `Specimen.recordSubject Reference(Patient)` or reuse of an existing pattern) carries the record's `Patient`, so the `patient` SearchParameter can be defined directly. The `RelatedPerson` use of `Specimen.subject` is then explicitly limited to cases where the specimen is *not* in a specific patient's record.
4. Update `source/compartmentdefinition-patient.xml` (or equivalent) so Specimen's contribution to the Patient compartment uses whichever element ends up holding the direct `Patient` reference.

#### Justification

This option respects the work O&O already did on FHIR-53384 (motions 5-1-1 and 4-0-0) and the use cases the Zulip thread surfaced, while still giving Lloyd's compartment / search-parameter concern a place to land. It avoids a straight reversal three days after merge, which would be uncomfortable politically and create churn against [HL7/fhir#4027](https://github.com/HL7/fhir/pull/4027). The trade-off is real: it introduces two ways to express "subject" (a polymorphic `Specimen.subject` that may be `RelatedPerson`, plus a separate Patient-bearing element or invariant), which is conceptually heavier than Disposition A and risks implementer confusion. It also requires non-trivial work on the Patient compartment definition just before normative ballot.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline FHIR-56301 as **out of scope for the R6 normative ballot cycle** and close as "Not Persuasive" or "Persuasive but defer." Rationale: FHIR-53384 was voted, applied, and merged ([HL7/fhir#4027](https://github.com/HL7/fhir/pull/4027)) before FHIR-56301 was filed; the Patient-compartment concern, while valid, can be addressed at the implementation-guide / profiling layer (e.g., US Core, IPS, Genomics Reporting can require `Specimen.subject` to be a `Patient` reference in their profiles) without a base-spec breaking change. Document the limitation: when `Specimen.subject` is a `RelatedPerson`, the resource is not in any single `Patient` compartment via the `patient` SearchParameter, and implementers requiring compartment membership must constrain `Specimen.subject` to `Patient` in their profile.

#### Justification

This option preserves the O&O motion outcome, avoids reopening a freshly merged PR on a near-normative resource, and recognises that implementers already routinely tighten cardinalities and target types in profiles. It is defensible because (a) `Specimen.subject` is optional today, so any system that doesn't populate it has the same compartment-membership problem regardless of FHIR-53384, and (b) the search-parameter / compartment behaviour Lloyd describes is, in practice, resolved by profiling rather than by base-resource gymnastics. The trade-off is that base FHIR `Specimen` would ship with a documented hole in the Patient-compartment contract, which is precisely the design principle Lloyd flags as MUST-level — and several PA / O&O participants in the Zulip thread share that concern.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The Patient-compartment / `patient`-SearchParameter contract Lloyd cites is a long-standing, near-invariant rule across the patient-centric resources, and `Observation` already shows that the `subject` (record holder) vs. `focus` (entity the data is actually about) split is a clean, well-understood way to satisfy both halves of the requirement. Both Zulip camps (those who wanted `RelatedPerson` and those who wanted `subject` to stay on `Patient`) get what they care about, implementers don't have to invent profile-level workarounds, and the Patient compartment definition for `Specimen` stays trivially correct. Disposition B is workable but introduces parallel mechanisms on the eve of normative; Disposition C concedes a known structural problem in a resource that is heading to normative ballot. The cost — reverting one allowed target on `Specimen.subject` from the just-merged [HL7/fhir#4027](https://github.com/HL7/fhir/pull/4027) and adding `Specimen.focus` in `source/specimen/` — is small and is exactly the kind of pre-ballot correction the R6-Normative-Ballot label is meant to surface. O&O should coordinate with PA, who hosted the original Zulip discussion, before applying the change.
