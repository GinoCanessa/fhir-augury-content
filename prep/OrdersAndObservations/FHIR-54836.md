## Summary

Ticket FHIR-54836 is a deliberately-thin "follow-up text" change request raised
by Ulrike Merrick against the FHIR Core R6 ballot. It asks Orders & Observations
(OO) to update the grouping-related narrative wherever it appears in the
specification once the workgroup has decided how panels (and similar
groupings of Observations) will be represented. The ticket itself proposes no
specific edit; it is a placeholder so the editorial work is not lost when the
underlying design discussion concludes.

The "grouping discussion" being deferred to is the broader OO debate captured
in a cluster of sibling tickets all labeled `organizer-grouping`:

- [FHIR-54562](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54562) (Hans Buitendijk) frames the
  decision and argues against the newly-proposed `Observation.organizer` element,
  preferring either a `dataAbsentReason = grouper` pattern, a new grouper resource,
  or extending `DiagnosticReport.result` to allow `Group`/`List`.
- [FHIR-54808](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54808) (Ralf Herzog) enumerates the
  candidate options and points to the
  [Grouping Option Analysis Confluence page](https://confluence.hl7.org/spaces/OO/pages/403871266/Grouping+Option+Analysis)
  and the UV Lab Report / Observation Grouping calls.
- [FHIR-54887](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54887) (Ulrike Merrick) is the
  companion change-list ticket that names the *specific* artifacts (Observation
  notes sections `obsgrouping` and `gr-result`, the example value-set binding for
  `Observation.code` when `organizer = true`) that will need editing — the
  surface area FHIR-54836 will operate on.
- [FHIR-54897](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54897) (Kathy Walsh, Highest
  priority) registers a strong preference for representing a panel as a
  `DiagnosticReport` (with bundled DiagnosticReports for multi-requisition
  cases), and only as a fallback either a new `Panel` grouper resource or a
  grouper Observation flagged via `dataAbsentReason`.
- [FHIR-50534](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50534) (Deferred) collects
  feedback on the existing balloter notes for `Observation.organizer`.
- [FHIR-53553](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53553) (Applied) is the R6 ballot
  notes ticket for Observation, where some of this grouping narrative currently
  lives.

In short: FHIR-54836 is the editorial hook that follows whichever decision the
sibling tickets settle on. It cannot be dispositioned in isolation — its
disposition is a function of the disposition reached on FHIR-54887/54562/54808/54897.

## Proposed Dispositions

Because FHIR-54836 is by design a follow-on editorial ticket, all three
dispositions below are framed in terms of how it should be sequenced relative
to the in-flight grouping decision (FHIR-54887 / 54562 / 54808 / 54897), not in
terms of *what* the new text should say — that wording is downstream of the
design choice.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Hold FHIR-54836 in `Triaged` until the OO grouping decision lands (i.e., until
FHIR-54562 / FHIR-54808 / FHIR-54887 / FHIR-54897 are dispositioned and a
single representation pattern is chosen). Then, as a single editorial pass,
rewrite the grouping narrative in the FHIR Core R6 spec to match the chosen
pattern. Concrete in-scope text:

- The Observation resource notes section "Grouping Observations"
  (`observation.html#obsgrouping`).
- The Observation `gr-result` / "Result Reporting via DiagnosticReport"
  narrative (`observation.html#gr-result`).
- The corresponding Diagnostics Module page sections that describe panel /
  battery grouping.
- The balloter notes / "Notes to Implementers" callouts attached to
  `Observation.organizer` (or the replacement mechanism, if `organizer` is
  removed).

The rewrite must (a) state the chosen panel-representation pattern
unambiguously, (b) show one canonical example (flat panel) and one nested
example (e.g., micro isolate + sensitivities), and (c) cross-link to
`DiagnosticReport.result` and to the Diagnostics Module.

Bundle the actual file edits with FHIR-54887 (which already enumerates the
specific artifacts) so the two tickets are applied as one PR.

#### Justification

This is what the ticket literally asks for and is the lowest-risk option. The
ticket is a known-deliberate placeholder; performing the editorial pass after —
not before — the design decision avoids re-writing the same paragraphs twice
and keeps the spec text consistent with whatever model the workgroup adopts.
Bundling with FHIR-54887 reflects the strong overlap (same reporter, same
`organizer-grouping` label, overlapping target sections) and avoids a
fragmented PR history.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Convert FHIR-54836 from a standalone ticket into an **acceptance criterion** on
the parent design ticket(s). Specifically, mark FHIR-54836 as a duplicate of
(or merged into) FHIR-54887, and amend FHIR-54887's resolution requirements to
explicitly state that the disposition is not "Applied" until the grouping
narrative on `observation.html#obsgrouping`, `observation.html#gr-result`, and
the Diagnostics Module page has been rewritten consistent with the chosen
panel-representation pattern.

Rationale for the merge: FHIR-54836 contains no detail beyond "rewrite the
text" and FHIR-54887 already enumerates the specific paragraphs, value-set
re-binding, and balloter-note clarification that need editing. A single ticket
is easier to track, reduces the chance the editorial pass is forgotten, and
removes the risk of the two tickets being dispositioned inconsistently.

#### Justification

The ticket has zero unique technical content — it is purely a reminder. Keeping
two tickets that target the same paragraphs creates ambiguity about which one
"owns" the edit and risks one being closed administratively while the other
languishes. Folding it into FHIR-54887 preserves all the information (the
reminder is now explicit on the richer ticket) and gives the editor a single
ticket to disposition.

The trade-off vs. Disposition A: A loses the loose coupling benefit (separate
ticket = separate audit trail showing the editorial pass was performed), so if
the workgroup values the audit trail (e.g., for ballot-comment dispositions
that must each be tracked individually), A is better; if the workgroup
prioritizes minimizing redundant tickets, B is better.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-54836 as **Not Persuasive** / *placeholder, no actionable change
described*, on the grounds that it requests no specific edit and is fully
covered by the editorial work that will naturally accompany whichever
disposition is reached on FHIR-54887, FHIR-54562, FHIR-54808, and FHIR-54897.
Add a Jira comment recording that any text rewrite required by the grouping
decision will be tracked under those tickets, then resolve.

#### Justification

A defensible position because the ticket adds no new requirement: rewriting
narrative text to match a normative change is part of the routine editorial
hygiene of dispositioning the underlying design tickets. Closing it removes a
zero-content ticket from the workgroup's queue and prevents it from being
double-counted against the ballot disposition statistics.

The risk: editorial follow-through is genuinely easy to forget, especially when
the chosen pattern (per FHIR-54897's preference) is "leave the resource alone
and use a `DiagnosticReport`-only model" — in which case there is no normative
change to force the editor to revisit the prose, and stale text describing
`Observation.organizer` could survive into the published R6. Disposition C
relies on the editor remembering on their own.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B (merge into FHIR-54887, with FHIR-54836 closed
as a duplicate / merged).

FHIR-54836 carries no information that FHIR-54887 does not already carry more
specifically. FHIR-54887 names the exact files and sections (the `obsgrouping`
and `gr-result` notes, the example value-set binding for `Observation.code`,
the balloter notes on `Observation.organizer`); FHIR-54836 just says "rewrite
the text." Keeping them separate creates two tickets pointing at the same
paragraphs with no clear handoff between them and invites the inconsistent-
disposition risk that Disposition A is structured to avoid only by
*also* bundling them at PR time — at which point the audit value of the
separate ticket is largely illusory.

Disposition C is tempting (the ticket really is content-free) but is the
weakest option in the realistic scenario where the workgroup chooses
FHIR-54897's preferred path (no `Observation.organizer` element, panel ≅
DiagnosticReport): in that world there is *no* normative change to drag the
editor back to the Observation prose, and the existing
`Observation.organizer`-shaped narrative will quietly become wrong. Disposition
B keeps an explicit, unmissable acceptance criterion attached to the design
ticket that *will* be touched. Disposition A is the safe second choice if the
workgroup prefers, for ballot-tracking reasons, to keep one ticket per ballot
comment.
