## Summary

FHIR-54965 asks the Orders & Observations work group to refresh the
Diagnostic Module page in FHIR Core, specifically the Roadmap section
and any "missing use cases" content. The reporter (Marti Velezis) flags
the page as out of date relative to where the diagnostic resources have
moved during the R6 ballot cycle. A follow-up comment from a workgroup
member adds two more concrete drivers: (1) the FMM levels rendered on
the roadmap likely changed during R6 and may also need to be presented
differently, and (2) any diagnostic resources that have since been moved
into the Incubator IG must no longer be described on this page as if
they were part of the core diagnostic module. There are no cross-linked
Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items associated with this
ticket in the Augury cache, so the request stands on its own as a
documentation/roadmap maintenance item with no surfaced dependencies.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat this as a documentation / module-page maintenance task against
the Diagnostic Module landing page in FHIR Core. Concretely:

1. Refresh the **Roadmap** section to reflect the post-R6-ballot state:
   updated FMM levels for each diagnostic resource (Observation,
   DiagnosticReport, ImagingStudy, Specimen, BodyStructure,
   MolecularSequence/GenomicStudy, etc.), removal of any roadmap items
   already delivered in R6, and addition of newly identified roadmap
   items the workgroup is committing to for the next cycle.
2. Move or remove any descriptions of resources that have been
   migrated to the **Diagnostics Incubator IG** so the core module
   page does not double-describe them; replace with a short pointer
   ("These resources are now developed in the Diagnostics Incubator
   IG — see <link>").
3. Fill in the "missing use cases" — enumerate the diagnostic-domain
   use cases the module is intended to cover (e.g., laboratory result
   reporting, imaging order/result, point-of-care testing, genomics,
   pathology, anatomical findings) and tie each to the resources that
   support it.
4. Optionally re-render FMM presentation (e.g., a table grouped by
   maturity rather than inline badges) per the comment from
   JIRAUSER24005.

#### Justification

The ticket is explicitly a roadmap + use-case refresh, not a normative
behaviour change, and the comment confirms the intent is to bring the
page into line with R6 ballot state and the Incubator IG split.
Accepting as requested is the lowest-risk option: it has no impact on
schemas, profiles, validators, or implementers, and it keeps the
module landing page truthful for ballot reviewers. FHIR module pages
are explicitly intended to summarise current state and direction, so
allowing them to drift is a known source of confusion in ballot
comments.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the intent, but split the work into two tracked deliverables
rather than one ticket:

1. **B1 — Mechanical R6 sync (this ticket):** Update FMM levels,
   remove resources now in the Incubator IG, and fix any stale links
   on `diagnostics.html`. Treat as an editorial change with a fast
   turnaround so the page is correct for the R6 ballot reconciliation
   window.
2. **B2 — Roadmap & missing use cases (spin-off ticket):** Open a new
   ticket whose scope is to author the forward-looking roadmap and
   the missing-use-case catalogue. This is a substantive work-group
   exercise (it implies committing to future direction) and benefits
   from explicit O&O agenda time and possibly a Zulip thread for
   wider input before the page is rewritten.

Additionally, consider introducing a small reusable include
(`diagnostics-roadmap.xml` partial) so the roadmap content can be
maintained independently of the rest of the module narrative.

#### Justification

The ticket as written conflates two very different efforts: a purely
editorial sync to R6 reality (low controversy, can be done by a single
editor), and a forward-looking roadmap / use-case definition (requires
work-group consensus and probably community input). Bundling them
risks the editorial fix being held hostage by the roadmap discussion,
or the roadmap being silently rewritten by whoever picks up the
ticket. Splitting also gives the reviewer a clean audit trail: the
mechanical fix can ship immediately, while the roadmap text gets the
review it deserves.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as **Not Persuasive / Out of Scope for a tracker change request**
on the grounds that module roadmap pages are maintained continuously
by the work group as part of normal editorial workflow, not via the
Jira tracker. Redirect the reporter to raise the specific stale items
(individual FMM mismatches, individual resources that should be
removed, individual missing use cases) as discrete tickets or as a
PR against `diagnostics.html`.

#### Justification

This disposition is defensible only narrowly: the request is too
unspecific to action ("update Roadmap and missing use cases") and a
tracker ticket without a concrete diff is hard to resolve and verify.
However, given that the reporter is the work group co-chair and the
comment on the ticket is from another work-group member already
scoping the work, declining would be unhelpful — the work clearly
needs to happen and the ticket is the work group's chosen vehicle for
tracking it.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The work clearly needs to happen, so Disposition C (decline) is not
appropriate — but Disposition A (accept as a single ticket) risks
collapsing two distinct deliverables into one and making it hard for
the workgroup to ballot the result. Disposition B keeps the editorial
R6 sync moving on this ticket (where it can be closed quickly with a
diff against `diagnostics.html`) while ensuring the roadmap and
missing-use-case work — which is genuinely a workgroup design exercise
— gets its own ticket, its own discussion, and its own review.
Recommend O&O accept under B, assign the editorial sync to a named
editor with a short deadline, and open the spin-off ticket on the
roadmap/use-cases at the same meeting so it does not get lost.
