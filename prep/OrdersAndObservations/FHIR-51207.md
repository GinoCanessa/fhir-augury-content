## Summary

The reporter (Elliot Silver) observes that FHIR's resource coverage for
Biologically Derived Products (BDPs) — such as blood, blood components,
tissue, organs, and cell-therapy products — is asymmetric compared with
the Medication and Device families. Today, BDP requests are typically
modeled with `ServiceRequest` and "application" of a BDP (e.g., a blood
transfusion or organ transplant) is recorded with `Procedure`. There is
a `BiologicallyDerivedProductDispense` resource paralleling
`MedicationDispense`/`DeviceDispense`, but there is no BDP analogue for
`MedicationRequest`/`DeviceRequest`, no analogue for
`MedicationAdministration`, and no analogue for
`MedicationStatement`/`DeviceUsage` to capture historical, after-the-fact
patient assertions ("I had a blood transfusion 20 years ago").

The ticket asks O&O to (1) decide whether the existing pattern
(ServiceRequest → BiologicallyDerivedProductDispense → Procedure) is
sufficient, (2) determine whether new request, administration, and/or
statement resources are needed for BDPs, and (3) if existing resources
are reused, decide whether to broaden them — for example, allow
`MedicationStatement.medication` (and possibly `MedicationRequest` and
`MedicationAdministration`) to reference `BiologicallyDerivedProduct`,
or add a dosage-style structure to `Procedure`.

The cross-referenced FHIR artifacts (`MedicationStatement.medication`
and `MedicationAdministration.dosage`) reflect the two concrete
extension points the description and Zulip thread keep returning to:
allowing BDP references on the medication-family resources, and
expressing "how much was given" outside the medication context. Several
related Patient Care tickets cover the same ground from PC's side:
[FHIR-8458](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-8458) (whether transfusion
is closer to medication administration or procedure, with prior PC/Pharmacy
agreement that it should be a separate resource and even a draft
"BloodProductAdministration" proposal),
[FHIR-33398](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-33398) (how to store
administration of biological products, currently CONSIDERED for R6),
[FHIR-13047](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-13047) (add
DosageInstructions to Procedure to allow BDP-style dosing), and
[FHIR-12993](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-12993) (a generic
Administration / NonMedicationAdministration resource). The September
2025 WGM resolved to "explore additional resources to help fill in
these gaps" within the OO-Incubator project, and Patient Care's 2026-03-12
note recommends a joint PC × Pharmacy × O&O session to resolve the
remaining questions (including the "reported" / historical-assertion
question and guidance for transfusion, radiation, and chemotherapy).

## Details

**Description:**

It is unclear how to record certain activity involving biologically
derived products.

At the moment, "application" of a BDP, say a blood transfusion, or organ
transplant, is recorded with a Procedure. This is analogous to how
implantation of an artificial hip (Device) is recorded. Although
appropriate for some situations, it may or may not be appropriate for
other use cases.

For example, how would a patient assertion of "I had a blood transfusion
20 years ago" be recorded? Is the preferred way to create a Procedure?
Is this an Observation? Perhaps (in this case) we would prefer that it
be a MedicationStatement. (Note that in most cases, a BDP resource
representing the actual product wouldn't be available, only a code
representing the type of BDP.)

Comparing with similar resources Medication and Device, we see:

| Medication | Device | BDP |
|---|---|---|
| MedicationRequest | DeviceRequest | ? |
| MedicationDispense | DeviceDispense | BiologicallyDerivedProductDispense |
| MedicationAdministration | (none) | ? |
| MedicationStatement | DeviceUsage | ? |
| Medication | Device | BiologicallyDerivedProduct |

Right now BDP requests are tracked through ServiceRequest. Is there need
for a separate resource? Is this a special kind of MedicationRequest? Or
is ServiceRequest sufficient?

Is Procedure sufficient for a historical BDP-use statement, or is there
a need for a BDPStatement?

And finally, there doesn't appear to be a distinction between a
Procedure and "administration" of a BDP; this may align with how BDPs
are used or may be an oversight.

Please review the existing resources for these various BDP uses, and
determine if changes are needed to the preferred existing resources or
if new resources are needed. For example, if MedicationStatement is the
preferred way of recording "I had a blood transfusion", then should
`MedicationStatement.medication` allow reference to BDP?

This was discussed on
[#implementers > Patient assertion of blood transfusion](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/channel/179166-implementers/topic/Patient.20assertion.20of.20blood.20transfusion/with/524957076).

## Related Zulip Discussions

### [implementers > Patient assertion of blood transfusion](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/implementers/topic/Patient%20assertion%20of%20blood%20transfusion/near/524957076)

Elliot Silver opened the thread (2025-06-19) asking which resource
should be used to record a blood transfusion after the fact —
specifically whether `MedicationStatement` is appropriate when the
"medication" is a blood product. Yanick Gaudet floated the idea of
recording a current transfusion as a `Procedure` and a patient-reported
historical one as an `Observation` (using SNOMED 161664006). Jean
Duteau countered that he would record it as a `Procedure` even when
reported after the fact, citing the
[ICSR AE Reporting IG's `icsr-transfusion` profile](https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/fhir-icsr-ae-reporting/StructureDefinition-icsr-transfusion.html)
on `Procedure`, but acknowledged the IG team did not consider
post-reporting of transfusions. Stephen Chu (2025-08-01) gave the most
detailed view: `Procedure` is fine for summary information and
historical assertions (Procedure.code = SNOMED 33389009 / 71493000,
performedDateTime, .outcome, .complication), but is *not* suited to
exchanging full administration details (blood pack ID, blood group, ABO/
Rh compatibility, expiry, container, transfusion duration, etc.); for
that you need either `MedicationAdministration`-style structure or a
purpose-built resource. Lloyd McKenzie pinged Michelle Miller into the
thread but did not stake out a position there.

No consensus was reached. The thread converges on "Procedure works for
historical / patient-reported assertions" but explicitly leaves open
the question of where rich administration data should live, which is
exactly the gap FHIR-51207 asks O&O to close.

Participants: Elliot Silver, Yanick Gaudet, Jean Duteau, Lloyd McKenzie,
Michelle (Moseman) Miller, Stephen Chu.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat this ticket as the umbrella driver and split the work into the
three concrete spec changes the description asks O&O to evaluate, all
landed in the FHIR Core spec under O&O ownership of BDP and in
coordination with Pharmacy (Medication family) and Patient Care
(Procedure):

1. **Request side:** Add a new `BiologicallyDerivedProductRequest`
   resource (parallel to `MedicationRequest` / `DeviceRequest`) and
   deprecate the use of `ServiceRequest` for BDP requests in narrative
   guidance.
2. **Administration side:** Add a new
   `BiologicallyDerivedProductAdministration` resource (parallel to
   `MedicationAdministration`) carrying transfusion/infusion details
   (volume infused, container/blood-pack identifiers, ABO/Rh
   compatibility checks, two-person verification, periodic vitals via
   contained or referenced `Observation`s, reactions). This addresses
   Stephen Chu's "Procedure can't carry the full details" gap from the
   Zulip thread.
3. **Statement side:** Add a new `BiologicallyDerivedProductStatement`
   resource (parallel to `MedicationStatement` / `DeviceUsage`) whose
   `product[x]` allows either a reference to a
   `BiologicallyDerivedProduct` *or* a CodeableConcept-only assertion
   (since historical patient assertions almost never have a product
   instance). This addresses the originating "I had a blood transfusion
   20 years ago" use case.

Pursue this development inside the **OO-Incubator** project, consistent
with the Sept 2025 WGM decision and the `Incubator` label already on
this ticket. Schedule the joint Pharmacy × PC × O&O session
recommended by PC on 2026-03-12 to confirm boundaries with
`MedicationAdministration`, `MedicationStatement`, and `Procedure`
before draft resources go to FMG.

#### Justification

This is the only option that fully discharges what the ticket asks for
("determine if changes are needed to the preferred existing resources
or if new resources are needed"). It also realises the long-standing
PC direction recorded on FHIR-8458 (2015 and 2021 PC/Pharmacy/O&O
consensus that BDP administration "doesn't fit in the scope of existing
resources") and the consensus reflected in the 2026-01-21 comment that
the September 2025 WGM agreed to *explore additional resources* via the
OO-Incubator. It mirrors the Medication and Device resource families,
which is the symmetry the table in the description is highlighting and
the symmetry implementers will reach for first. Keeping BDP-specific
data on BDP-specific resources avoids overloading
`MedicationAdministration` with concepts (donor, ABO/Rh, blood-pack
container ID, infusion-reaction monitoring) that have no meaning for
medications and would otherwise have to live in extensions on every
implementation.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Avoid creating three new resources. Instead, fill the gaps by extending
the medication and procedure resources already in scope:

1. **Allow BDP on the medication family.** Change
   `MedicationRequest.medication`, `MedicationAdministration.medication`,
   and `MedicationStatement.medication` from `CodeableReference(Medication)`
   to `CodeableReference(Medication | BiologicallyDerivedProduct)`. This
   directly satisfies the ticket's worked example
   ("should `MedicationStatement.medication` allow reference to BDP?")
   and the cross-referenced artifacts
   `MedicationStatement.medication` and
   `MedicationAdministration.dosage`. Update the resource boundaries
   narrative on `MedicationAdministration` to permit transfusion
   modelling.
2. **Strengthen Procedure for transfusion-style administration.** Adopt
   [FHIR-13047](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-13047)'s proposal to
   add a `dosage` (or `dose 0..*`) backbone to `Procedure` capable of
   carrying volume, units, rate, and route, so transfusions and similar
   administrations recorded as `Procedure` can carry "how much" without
   a parallel `MedicationAdministration`.
3. **Defer a dedicated request resource.** Continue to use
   `ServiceRequest` for BDP requests (with stronger narrative guidance
   and possibly an O&O-published profile), since
   `BiologicallyDerivedProductDispense.basedOn` already references
   `ServiceRequest` and there is little implementer pull for a separate
   request resource in the Zulip discussion.

#### Justification

This option addresses the same use cases at substantially lower cost
to the spec and to implementers: no new resource boundaries to police,
no migration of existing transfusion-as-Procedure data, and no
duplication of FSM/state-machine work. It directly closes the loop on
both cross-referenced artifacts (`MedicationStatement.medication`,
`MedicationAdministration.dosage`) without inventing parallel
administration semantics. It also accommodates the ticket's reminder
that historical assertions usually only have a *code* — a
`CodeableReference` cleanly handles a code-only BDP without requiring
the existence of a `BiologicallyDerivedProduct` instance.

The trade-off versus Disposition A is expressiveness: the
JIRAUSER19407 (2025-06-26) comment and Stephen Chu's Zulip post both
note that some BDP-administration data (blood-pack identity, ABO/Rh
checks, two-nurse verification) does not map cleanly onto medication
constructs, and Lloyd McKenzie's standing concern (FHIR-8458) that any
solution must also work for radiation/light/chemo argues against
overloading the medication family. Where Disposition B falls short on
those, profiles or extensions on `Procedure` /
`MedicationAdministration` can fill the gap until R7 — which is
preferable to publishing three new resources whose boundaries are not
yet tested by implementers.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark this ticket "Persuasive with Mod / Not Persuasive" on the basis
that the existing pattern — `ServiceRequest` →
`BiologicallyDerivedProductDispense` → `Procedure`, with
`Observation`/`Procedure.outcome` for volumes and reactions, and
`Procedure` (current or historical, including patient-reported) for
the administration record — is already sufficient to cover the
described use cases, and that the gaps the ticket identifies are
better addressed by IG-level profiles (e.g., the ICSR AE Reporting
`icsr-transfusion` profile cited in the Zulip thread) than by changes
to FHIR Core. Close FHIR-51207 with guidance that BDP requests use
`ServiceRequest`, BDP administrations use `Procedure` (with detailed
data carried via contained/referenced `Observation`s and
`Procedure.outcome`), and historical patient assertions also use
`Procedure` with `recorder`/`reportedBoolean` semantics.

#### Justification

Defensible on three grounds: (1) the request side is already
implementable today (multiple production IGs, including ICSR AE
Reporting, model transfusion as `Procedure`); (2) Jean Duteau's
position in the Zulip thread — "I would think you record it as a
Procedure even if it is being reported after the fact" — is a workable
default for the originating use case; and (3) creating three new
resources for a domain that, per the JIRAUSER19407 (2025-06-26) comment,
spans an extremely heterogeneous range (blood, tissue, cells, future
xeno-derived products) carries a real risk of resources whose
boundaries shift under us before there is implementation evidence.

The weakness of this option is that it ignores Stephen Chu's
detailed list of administration data points that `Procedure` cannot
currently carry, ignores the long-running PC and Pharmacy direction
recorded on [FHIR-8458](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-8458) (which
twice resolved that BDP administration warrants a separate resource),
and ignores the Sept 2025 WGM decision to "explore additional
resources" via the OO-Incubator. Choosing this disposition therefore
requires an explicit reversal of those prior positions.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **A**

The September 2025 WGM has effectively already chosen this path
("explore additional resources to help fill in these gaps... within
the OO-Incubator project"), the `Incubator` and `AdditionalResources`
labels on this ticket reinforce it, and PC's 2026-03-12 note proposing
a joint Pharmacy × PC × O&O session is consistent with incubating new
resources rather than overloading existing ones. Disposition B is the
right *short-term* mitigation and should not be discarded — in
particular, opening up
`MedicationStatement.medication`/`MedicationAdministration.medication`
to BDP would address the literal example in the description for R6
even while incubation continues — but it does not solve the structural
problem that BDP administration carries data
(donor, blood-pack identity, ABO/Rh verification, infusion-reaction
monitoring) that medication semantics are not designed for, and
Lloyd McKenzie's standing objection on
[FHIR-8458](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-8458) about radiation /
light / chemo therapy implies the same generic-administration question
will return regardless. Disposition C requires reversing two prior
PC/Pharmacy/O&O positions and the Sept 2025 WGM decision, with no new
implementer evidence to justify the reversal.

The workgroup should commit to incubating
`BiologicallyDerivedProductRequest`,
`BiologicallyDerivedProductAdministration`, and
`BiologicallyDerivedProductStatement` under the OO-Incubator (Disposition
A), and consider folding the Disposition B `medication` codeable-reference
broadening into the same R6 ballot cycle as a transitional measure for
implementers who cannot wait for the new resources to mature.
