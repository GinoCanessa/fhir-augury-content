## Summary

The reporter requests that the At-Home In-Vitro Test Report (AHIVTR) IG
be expanded beyond its current public-health-only scope to also cover a
Personal Health Record (PHR) and/or provider-based Electronic Health
Record (EHR) reporting use case for at-home test results. Unlike the
existing public-health flow, this additional use case is described as a
within-device transaction — the testing app handing the result off to
another app on the patient's smart device (the patient's PHR app, or a
provider's EHR-tethered patient app) for ingestion — rather than an
over-the-network exchange to a public-health authority. There are no
cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items recorded
in FHIR Augury, so the scoping context comes entirely from the ticket
itself and its comment history. The most recent comment (Jan 12 2026
O&O HCP call) notes the data flow has structural similarities to the
CGM (Continuous Glucose Monitoring) IG but differs because at-home
testing produces sparse rather than streaming data; it also notes that
no stakeholders are currently lined up to work out the implementation
details.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Expand the scope of the At-Home In-Vitro Test Report IG to include a
PHR/EHR use case alongside the existing public-health one. Concretely:

1. Add a new use-case narrative page to the AHIVTR IG describing the
   on-device, app-to-app handoff from the testing app to a PHR or
   EHR-tethered patient app.
2. Add a second exchange pattern (alongside the existing PHA-bound
   transaction) that conveys the same `Observation` /
   `DiagnosticReport` (with associated `Patient`, `Device`, `Specimen`,
   and result-value bindings) into the patient's PHR or provider's EHR.
   Keep the resource shapes consistent with the existing AHIVTR
   profiles so a single produced bundle satisfies both flows where
   possible.
3. Add a CapabilityStatement (or a new capability section) for the
   "PHR/EHR receiver" actor, including conformance for ingest of the
   AHIVTR profiles via an on-device handoff (e.g., document-style
   bundle posted via FHIR REST `create` or platform intent / share
   sheet — recorded as a non-network transport binding).
4. If the workgroup confirms the eCR linkage flagged by apitkus, add a
   downstream-reporting note pointing to the relevant
   public-health-reporting IGs and clarifying that EHR ingestion does
   not relieve the provider of statutory eCR obligations.

#### Justification

This is what the ticket explicitly asks for, and the workgroup already
reverted the original "Considered for Future Use" disposition with the
expectation that the topic would be re-opened post-STU1. The structural
similarity to the CGM IG noted on the Jan 2026 HCP call gives the WG a
working analogue for the data flow, and the AHIVTR profiles already
exist, so the work is largely pattern-and-actor expansion rather than
new resource design. The interim guidance lynn_laakso added in 2023
(advising patient-portal workarounds) becomes obsolete once the IG
formally supports the flow. Apitkus's eCR concern is addressable by
explicit cross-references rather than by changing this IG's payload
shape.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than expanding AHIVTR itself, publish the PHR/EHR ingest pattern
as a separate, narrowly-scoped companion IG (e.g., "Consumer-Performed
Test Result Ingest"), and have AHIVTR simply reference it. Concretely:

1. Keep AHIVTR focused on its current public-health receiver actor.
2. Stand up a companion IG that defines the on-device app-to-app
   handoff actor pair (Testing App → PHR/EHR Patient App), reusing the
   AHIVTR profiles by reference.
3. Broaden the companion IG's scope from the start to cover other
   consumer-performed tests beyond COVID (HIV, influenza, drug screen,
   urinalysis, CBC items), as apitkus explicitly suggested may warrant
   separate work.
4. Add a short "Related work" page to AHIVTR pointing to the companion
   IG so that current AHIVTR readers find the new flow.

#### Justification

The Jan 2026 comment notes that "there are not stakeholders ready to
work out the details" of the in-IG expansion, and the on-device,
app-to-app exchange is materially different in transport, security,
consent, and conformance from the existing over-the-network public-
health flow. A separate IG lets the AHIVTR project ship what it has
without taking on a transport binding (non-network, intent/share-sheet)
and a new receiver actor that may not be straightforward to ballot.
It also better accommodates apitkus's broader scope (other consumer-
performed tests), which is awkward to fit inside an IG titled "At-Home
In-Vitro Test Report". Trade-off vs. A: more publication overhead and
two IGs for implementers to track, but cleaner separation of concerns
and lower risk of stalling AHIVTR maintenance on a use case that
currently lacks stakeholders.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the requested scope expansion. Specifically: do not extend
AHIVTR to cover PHR/EHR ingest, and instead retain the existing
"considered for future use" guidance with the lynn_laakso note that
implementers wanting to communicate at-home results to a provider
should use other channels (patient portal, manual entry) until a
properly-scoped successor effort is launched. Mark the ticket
"Persuasive with Mod" if the WG wants to keep the "future use" page
already added to the IG, or "Not Persuasive" if the WG wants to drop
the PHR/EHR aspiration entirely.

#### Justification

Two stated facts make declining defensible: (1) the assignee notes
explicitly on the Jan 2026 HCP call that no stakeholders are currently
ready to work out the details, and (2) the original 2023 disposition
("out of scope for this project") was made on a 13-0-1 vote and was
only reverted procedurally to keep the door open post-STU1 — not
because the WG had taken a position on doing the work. AHIVTR was
designed and balloted around a public-health receiver; bolting an
on-device, app-to-app PHR/EHR flow onto it without committed
stakeholders risks producing an under-specified actor and a transport
binding the WG cannot maintain. The patient-portal interim guidance
already in the IG continues to give implementers a workable answer.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The ticket is genuinely valuable — apitkus's 2023 comment and the
Jan 2026 HCP discussion both indicate continuing community interest —
but the assignee's own Jan 2026 note that "there are not stakeholders
ready to work out the details" is hard to reconcile with Disposition A
inside AHIVTR's normal release cadence. Disposition C is too
dismissive given that the WG already chose to revert the 2023 closure
specifically to revisit this. Disposition B threads the needle: it
keeps AHIVTR shippable on its current scope, properly recognizes that
on-device app-to-app exchange is a different transport / actor problem
than over-the-network public-health reporting, and creates a natural
home for the broader consumer-performed-testing scope that apitkus
flagged. It also gives the WG a clean way to invite the PHR vendors,
EHR patient-app teams, and CGM-IG authors (whose flow Daniel Rutz
already cited as a partial analogue) into a separate project where
they actually need to engage, rather than absorbing them into AHIVTR
maintenance.
