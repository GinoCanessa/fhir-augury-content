## Summary

The reporter asks the Orders & Observations work group to clarify how
`Observation.focus` and `Observation.bodyStructure` interact, and to
tighten both the `bodyStructure` definition and the `focus` Comment
bullets so that implementers can model a consistent set of "observation
about something on/about/inside the patient" cases. Four scenarios are
offered as a sanity check: (1) measured head circumference of a fetus
carried by a mother, (2) measured diameter of a tumor in a patient's
liver, (3) measured diameter of a stent placed in a patient's
descending coronary artery, and (4) measured diameter of the descending
coronary artery itself where the stent is placed. The reporter shows
how each scenario "should" map onto `subject`, `focus`, `code`, and
`bodyStructure` and then proposes a small wording change to
`bodyStructure` ("…on the body of the subject (or the focus if
present)…") plus a critique of two of the existing `focus` Comment
bullets — the "tumor as focus" bullet (which collides with
`bodyStructure.morphology`) and the device-metrics bullet (which is
hard to parse and lacks an example). The cross-reference store only
links this ticket to the two FHIR elements it discusses
(`Observation.focus`, `Observation.bodyStructure`); there are no linked
Jira tickets, GitHub items, or Zulip threads carrying the FHIR-54890
key.

## Details

**Description:**

Consider observations that record:

1. the measured head circumference of a Fetus B of mother Patient A.
2. the measured diameter of a tumor in the liver of Patient A
3. the measured diameter of a stent in the descending coronary artery of Patient A.
4. the measured diameter of the descending coronary artery of Patient A where the stent is placed.

It seems logical that

1. `.subject = Patient A`, `.focus = Fetus B`, `.code = Circumference`, `.bodyStructure = Head`
2. `.subject = Patient A`, `.code = Diameter`, `.bodyStructure.morphology = tumor` / `.include = liver`
3. `.subject = Patient A`, `.focus = Device(Stent)`, `.code = Diameter`
4. `.subject = Patient A`, `.code = Diameter`, `.bodyStructure = DCA`

Might need to clarify the `.bodyStructure` Definition:

> "Indicates the body structure on the ~~subject's~~ body of the subject **(or the focus if present)** where the observation was made (i.e. the target site)."

since the bodyStructure is the head of the fetus not the head of the mother.

And the `.focus` Comment bullets seem misleading:

- A parent is trained to change their child's tracheostomy tube. The child is the subject, and the parent is the focus.
- A fetal heart rate recorded in a mother's record. The mother is the subject, and the fetus is the focus.
- Features of a body structure, such as a tumor. The patient is the subject, and the specific body structure is the focus.
- Metrics captured from a medical device. The patient-attached device is the subject, the specific device metric is the focus, and the observing device itself is referenced in `.device`.

The first two sound right. The third bullet does not sound right —
`BodyStructure` already handles morphological abnormalities like
tumors. The fourth bullet is confusing: there seem to be two devices
(described variously as a medical device, a patient-attached device, a
specific device, and an observing device) and a patient, but the
patient does not seem to be captured and it isn't clear what is going
on between the devices. An example would help.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the wording changes proposed in the ticket against
`Observation.bodyStructure` and `Observation.focus`:

1. **`Observation.bodyStructure` definition** — change to
   "Indicates the body structure on the body of the subject (or the
   focus, if present) where the observation was made (i.e. the target
   site)." This makes it explicit that when `focus` is populated, the
   `bodyStructure` is anchored on the focus's body, not the subject's.
2. **`Observation.focus` Comments** — keep the parent-trains-tracheostomy
   and fetal-heart-rate bullets unchanged, **remove** the
   "features of a body structure, such as a tumor" bullet (since
   `BodyStructure` and `BodyStructure.morphology` are the intended
   modeling path for that case), and **rewrite** the device-metrics
   bullet with a worked example, e.g. "An infusion pump's flow-rate
   metric: the patient-attached infusion pump is `Observation.subject`,
   the specific flow-rate `DeviceMetric`/`DeviceComponent` is
   `Observation.focus`, the bedside monitor that observed the metric is
   `Observation.device`, and the human patient is **not** captured on
   this Observation — record patient context via a separate Observation
   that references the patient as `subject`."
3. **(Implied)** Add the four ticket scenarios as worked examples in the
   Observation page's Notes/Boundaries narrative so that the
   `subject` / `focus` / `bodyStructure` partition is illustrated end-to-end.

#### Justification

The reporter's analysis is internally consistent and aligned with
existing community usage on Zulip: the genomics, oncology, and wound
threads above all use `focus → BodyStructure` for "an observation about
a specific anatomical thing", and the maternal/fetal pattern is already
the canonical motivating example for `focus`. The proposed
`bodyStructure` definition tweak is small, backward-compatible, and
removes a real ambiguity about whose body the `bodyStructure` belongs to
when `focus` is set. Removing the "tumor as focus" bullet brings the
narrative into line with the explicit `BodyStructure.morphology`
modeling path and avoids documenting two parallel mechanisms for the
same case. Replacing the device-metrics bullet with a worked example
addresses the reporter's "an example would help" ask without altering
any element definitions or cardinalities.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the **definition clarification** for `Observation.bodyStructure`
and the **rewrite-with-example** for the device-metrics `focus` bullet,
but **retain (and clarify) the "features of a body structure" bullet**
on `Observation.focus` rather than removing it. Specifically:

- Keep the bullet but reword it to "Features measured *about* a specific
  `BodyStructure` instance the implementer has chosen to manage as a
  resource — e.g. a tracked tumor, lesion, or wound — where individual
  measurements (length, depth, stage) are recorded as separate
  Observations whose `focus` points at the shared `BodyStructure`. When
  the abnormality is captured inline on the Observation rather than as
  a separately-managed resource, use `Observation.bodyStructure` with
  `BodyStructure.morphology` instead."
- Add a non-normative note on the Observation page that both modeling
  paths are valid and call out when each should be preferred (longitudinal
  tracking of the structure → resource + `focus`; one-off measurement →
  inline `bodyStructure`).

#### Justification

The Zulip threads above show that "tumor as `focus`" is not a
documentation accident — it is in active use by oncology and wound IGs,
and removing the bullet outright (Disposition A) would silently
invalidate guidance these implementers are already following. The
underlying tension the reporter identifies is real, but the resolution
is to disambiguate the two valid paths rather than to eliminate one of
them. Keeping the bullet (with a sharper definition and a sibling
"otherwise use `bodyStructure`/`morphology`" pointer) preserves
backward compatibility, avoids breaking already-deployed profiles, and
still satisfies the reporter's core ask of making the
`focus`/`bodyStructure` interaction unambiguous.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as **Persuasive with Modifier — no normative change**. Keep all
four `focus` Comment bullets and the current `bodyStructure` definition
as-is, and instead publish the reporter's four scenarios (fetus head
circumference, liver tumor diameter, stent diameter, DCA diameter) as
an **Observation Notes / Examples** section on the Observation page,
explicitly mapping each to `subject` / `focus` / `code` /
`bodyStructure`. Treat the existing wording as already correct once
the worked examples exist.

#### Justification

The element definitions for `Observation.subject`, `Observation.focus`,
and `Observation.bodyStructure` have been stable across multiple
ballots, are widely implemented, and were re-affirmed in OO WG threads
going back to 2018 (Eric Haas's block-vote summary above). Changing
either the `bodyStructure` definition or the `focus` Comment bullets —
even cosmetically — risks invalidating downstream profiles that quote
the wording (e.g., US Core, oncology / mCODE, wound-care IGs). All four
of the reporter's scenarios already map cleanly onto the current
elements; the reported pain is "I had to think about it", which is
better solved with examples than with a normative wording change.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition A is the most faithful to the reporter's literal request,
but it discards a documentation bullet that the broader implementer
community is actively using (oncology, wound care, genomics). Disposition
C avoids that risk but leaves the genuinely-ambiguous `bodyStructure`
definition unchanged — and the reporter's "whose body is this on?"
critique is correct: when `focus` is populated, the current wording
silently asserts the structure is on the *subject's* body, which is
wrong for the fetal scenario. Disposition B threads the needle: it
adopts the small, low-risk `bodyStructure` definition tweak, adds the
worked device-metrics example the reporter asked for, and disambiguates
(rather than removes) the "tumor as `focus`" bullet so existing
implementers keep a supported modeling path. It is the change with the
best ratio of clarity gained to compatibility risk taken on, and it
gives the work group concrete language to discuss without committing
to deprecating an already-deployed pattern.
