Hynek Kružík opened the thread with a FSH `RuleSet` that authors an
Obligation extension (with `code`, `actor`, `documentation`
sub-extensions) for use on laboratory-report-style profiles, and was
hitting `ext-1` constraint failures and "does not match any known

same suppression noise in IPS 2.0 (676 suppressed messages); Kari
Heinonen identified the cause as the Publisher-injected

slicing. Grahame Grieve and Dusica Bojicic noted the impact is
limited to QA-report noise with no downstream implementer effect, and
the original author (Hynek) ultimately found that an empty
`documentation` parameter in his RuleSet was the trigger and split the
RuleSet to avoid it. The thread is **not** about FHIR-47210 directly,
but it is the live community conversation about the practical
mechanics, gotchas, and tooling friction of authoring Obligations on
laboratory-report-shaped profiles — relevant background for anyone
implementing the action this ticket asks for.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the resolution already captured on the ticket: in the
Laboratory Report (FHIR) IG, perform a profile-by-profile sweep and
**add or revise an Obligations block** on every defined profile,
covering at minimum the laboratory `DiagnosticReport` profile(s), the
laboratory `Observation` profile(s), and the supporting `Specimen`,
`ServiceRequest`, and `Patient`/`Practitioner` profiles the IG
constrains. For each profile:

- Identify the actors the IG already defines (e.g., laboratory report
  producer, laboratory report consumer) and bind Obligations to those
  actor canonicals.
- Use the standard Obligation codes (`SHALL:populate`,
  `SHALL:able-to-populate`, `SHOULD:populate`,
  `SHALL:able-to-process`, `SHOULD:able-to-process`,
  `SHALL:display`, etc.) consistently across profiles for the same
  semantic intent.
- For elements currently flagged only with `mustSupport`, decide
  whether the must-support is producer-side, consumer-side, or both,
  and replace/augment with the corresponding Obligation(s).
- Add `documentation` sub-extensions on Obligations whose intent is
  not self-evident (e.g., what "able-to-populate" means for a
  reflexive test result, a corrected report, or an addendum).

Track the sweep with an issue per profile in the IG's GitHub repo so
reviewers can confirm coverage.

#### Justification

The reporter, the resolution text, and the specification metadata
(work group, IG, "Persuasive" resolution) all align: the IG's
Obligations layer is incomplete, and uneven Obligations between
profiles in the same IG produce inconsistent implementer
expectations. Doing the sweep also brings the Laboratory Report IG
into line with how IPS, IPA, and AU IGs are using Obligations today
(per the Zulip thread above), which will make cross-IG comparison and
tooling support easier. Aligning Obligations with declared actors is
the design pattern the Obligations spec is built around, and it makes
producer/consumer expectations explicit instead of leaving them to
implementer interpretation of `mustSupport`.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Instead of a blanket "Obligations on every profile" sweep, take a
**risk-/use-case-driven** approach: define the IG's actors first
(laboratory report producer, laboratory report consumer, and any
intermediary), then add Obligations only where the actor's required
behaviour is **not already unambiguously expressed** by cardinality,
`mustSupport`, terminology bindings, or invariants. For elements
where `mustSupport` is sufficient on a single actor, leave the
profile as-is and document the producer/consumer split in the IG's
"Conformance" / "Actor expectations" narrative page rather than on
each profile. This keeps the profile snapshots smaller, avoids
multiplying the Publisher's "does not match any known slice" noise
flagged in the Zulip discussion above, and concentrates Obligations
on the elements where producer/consumer behaviour actually diverges
(e.g., status handling, corrected/amended reports, reference ranges,
reflexive results, attachments).

#### Justification

The Obligations extension is still maturing and, as the IG-creation
Zulip thread shows, today carries non-trivial tooling cost (snapshot
slicing warnings, suppression-list churn, inheritance quirks across
derived profiles). A targeted application — driven by the actors the
IG actually defines — delivers most of the implementer value of
Disposition A while keeping the IG's QA reports clean and the
profiles easier to read. It also respects backward compatibility:
existing implementers who built against `mustSupport` are not asked
to reinterpret every element under a new conformance lens. The
trade-off vs. Disposition A is reduced uniformity; the win is lower
maintenance and lower QA noise.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline as out-of-scope-for-now: do not modify the Obligations layer
of the Laboratory Report IG in this cycle. Document in the IG that
producer/consumer expectations continue to be conveyed via
`mustSupport` plus narrative Conformance text, and revisit
Obligations after the underlying Obligations extension and its
tooling stabilise (the extension is still in ballot, and the
slicing/inheritance issues raised on Zulip are unresolved at the
tooling level).

#### Justification

Declining is defensible if the work group judges that (a) the
Obligations extension is not yet stable enough for IG-wide adoption,
(b) the existing `mustSupport` + narrative pattern already conveys
the IG's intent to its known implementer base, and (c) the editorial
cost of a profile-by-profile sweep — plus the QA-noise burden the
Zulip thread documents — outweighs the implementer benefit at this
maturity level. This option preserves the option of doing the sweep
later, once Obligations tooling matures.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket has already been resolved as **Persuasive** by the work
group, with an explicit action to "Specify obligations for all the
defined profiles." That establishes work-group consensus that the
Obligations layer should be completed across the IG. Disposition A
honours that resolution directly. Disposition B is an attractive
hedge, but its main motivation — Publisher noise from Obligation
slicing — is, per Grahame Grieve, John D'Amore, and Dusica Bojicic in
the Zulip thread, **not** an implementer-facing problem and is
already being absorbed by suppression files in IPS, IPA, and AU IGs
that have made the same commitment. Disposition C contradicts the
already-recorded resolution and would leave the IG with the
inconsistent Obligations coverage that prompted the ticket. The
practical refinement to take from Disposition B and from the Zulip
discussion is to do the sweep in Disposition A **actor-first**: the
IG should publish (or confirm) its actor canonicals, and every
Obligation added should bind to one of them, with a `documentation`
sub-extension where intent is not obvious — that gives Disposition
A's coverage with Disposition B's clarity.
