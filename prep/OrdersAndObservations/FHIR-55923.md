## Summary

[FHIR-55923](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-55923) asks Orders & Observations
to provide a way for `ServiceRequest` to carry administration / dosing
instructions for the requested service (e.g., titration, rate, quantity, timing
and other procedural parameters that are not otherwise represented on the core
resource). The request originates from a joint OO/PC discussion at the
September 2025 WGM (Wed Q4, see Confluence page 358886935), which recommended
that the construct be aligned with `MedicationDispense.dosageInstructions` and
use the same Dosage Details structure published in R6
(`https://build.fhir.org/dosage.html#DosageDetails`). Because the Extensions
Pack is published against the current R5 Normative substrate, the proposed
resolution introduces a draft simple extension
(`http://hl7.org/fhir/StructureDefinition/servicerequest-dosing-instructions`)
typed as the R5 `Dosage` data type rather than the R6 `DosageDetails`, with a
disposition note recommending a re-evaluation (and a possible `startVersion`
bump) once R6 is published.

The same WGM session anticipated a parallel need on the event side
(`Procedure`); the long-standing companion ticket
[FHIR-13047](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-13047) (Patient Care, "Add
DosageInstructions to Procedure") tracks that side of the work and is being
re-discussed in PC and at the upcoming Rotterdam WGM. There is also open
discussion in the comment thread about whether the extension should be
generalized so that both `ServiceRequest` and `Procedure` can be context
resources.

## Details

**Description:**

> Discussion between OO and PC at Sept 2025 WGM identified the need for a
> ServiceRequest to include dosing instructions as a part of the order. It is
> anticipated that Procedure might see the same. See Wed Q4:
> https://confluence.hl7.org/spaces/OO/pages/358886935/2025-09-15-19_WGM.
>
> The recommendation was for an extension be created on ServiceRequest aligning
> with MedicationDispense.dosageInstructions using the same Dosage Details
> structure: https://build.fhir.org/dosage.html#DosageDetails.
>
> In the future this might be promoted to a backbone element on ServiceRequest
> or replaced with a more specific net-new resource. In the meantime
> considering this as an extension would be appropriate.
>
> From an extension standpoint this should likely be 0..* given the potential
> for dosing instructions to change over the administration period.

**Resolution (Persuasive with Modification):**

Introduce a draft simple extension to add dosing instructions to ServiceRequest
with the following profile:

- **Title:** Service Request Administration / Dosing Instructions
- **Name:** `ServiceRequestDosingInstructions`
- **URL:** `http://hl7.org/fhir/StructureDefinition/servicerequest-dosing-instructions`
- **Cardinality:** 0..1 (modified down from the requested 0..*)
- **Value cardinality:** 1..1
- **Value data type:** `Dosage` (R5-compatible — `https://hl7.org/fhir/dosage.html#Dosage`)
- **Description:** "Provides structured administration parameters or dosing
  instructions associated with a service request when the requested service
  involves titration, rate, quantity, timing, or related procedural parameters
  not otherwise represented in the core resource."
- **Short text:** "Administration instructions for the request"
- **Disposition note:** in R5 the published data type is `Dosage`, and
  therefore that is what the extension references; the R6-era `DosageDetails`
  cannot be used until R6 is published. It may be worth holding this until
  extensions can target R6 content and adding an R6-specific `startVersion`.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Publish the extension exactly as requested in the original ticket: a 0..*
simple extension on `ServiceRequest` typed as the R6 `DosageDetails` structure
defined at `https://build.fhir.org/dosage.html#DosageDetails`, mirroring
`MedicationDispense.dosageInstructions` as suggested by the WGM. The extension
URL would be `http://hl7.org/fhir/StructureDefinition/servicerequest-dosing-instructions`,
short text "Administration instructions for the request", and it would be
publishable in the FHIR Extensions Pack as a draft / trial-use artifact.

#### Justification

This most directly satisfies the WGM-recorded recommendation and the ticket
reporter's intent. A 0..* cardinality matches the reporter's reasoning that
dosing instructions can change over an administration period and would align
behavior with `MedicationDispense.dosageInstructions`, which is also repeating.
Using the R6 `DosageDetails` structure future-proofs the extension against the
forthcoming R6 publication and avoids the modelling drift between
`Dosage` (R5) and `DosageDetails` (R6) that Michelle Miller and David Barwin
flagged in the comments.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach (Persuasive with Modification — current resolution)

#### Proposal

Publish the extension as captured in the resolution: a draft simple extension
`ServiceRequestDosingInstructions` at
`http://hl7.org/fhir/StructureDefinition/servicerequest-dosing-instructions`
with:

- **Cardinality:** `0..1` (modified down from the requested `0..*`)
- **Value:** `1..1 Dosage` — the published R5 data type, not the R6
  `DosageDetails`
- **Description / short text:** as drafted in the resolution

Explicitly record the disposition note in the extension narrative so
implementers understand the R5/R6 trade-off, and create a follow-on ticket to
re-evaluate the value type and add an R6-specific `startVersion` once R6 is
published (and once the Extensions Pack tooling supports targeting R6
artifacts). Coordinate with Patient Care on
[FHIR-13047](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-13047) at Rotterdam to decide
whether a single generalized extension (with `ServiceRequest` and `Procedure`
both as context resources) should replace this purpose-built one before
publication.

#### Justification

This is the disposition currently captured on the ticket and represents the
pragmatic compromise:

- The Extensions Pack is published against the current Normative substrate
  (R5), so the value type **must** be the R5 `Dosage`. Publishing against R6
  `DosageDetails` today (Disposition A) is not technically feasible — that is
  the `ExternallyBlocked` label on this ticket. Disposition A's R6-typing is
  therefore not actually deliverable in the current pack and would have to wait
  for R6 anyway.
- The cardinality reduction to `0..1` keeps the first published draft narrow
  and easy to revise; a 0..1 extension can later be relaxed to 0..* without
  breaking implementers. (Some workgroup members will want to discuss this
  trade-off explicitly given the reporter's stated rationale for 0..*.)
- Capturing the R6 follow-up in the disposition note (rather than holding the
  ticket entirely until R6 publishes) gives implementers a usable artifact for
  request-side dosing today while leaving a clear migration path.
- Coordinating with FHIR-13047 ahead of publication respects Costa Teixeira's
  concern about adding "DosageInstructions" semantics to an event resource and
  avoids shipping an OO-only artifact that PC then has to re-litigate.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request and close as Not Persuasive with Modification: do not
publish a `servicerequest-dosing-instructions` extension at this time.
Instead, direct implementers either to (a) wait for R6, where
`ServiceRequest` (and the dosing model generally) can be revisited as a
first-class change, or (b) carry administration parameters today via
`ServiceRequest.orderDetail`, supporting `MedicationRequest` /
`MedicationAdministration` resources alongside the `ServiceRequest`, or a
domain-specific profile that constrains `ServiceRequest` and references the
appropriate medication / administration resources.

#### Justification

A defensible declination rests on three points: (1) FHIR already provides
`MedicationRequest` for ordering medications and `MedicationAdministration` for
recording administration, both of which carry richer dosing semantics than
`Dosage` on a generic `ServiceRequest`; (2) the comment thread shows this is a
genuinely unsettled modelling question (request side vs. event side, single
extension vs. resource-specific extensions, R5 `Dosage` vs. R6
`DosageDetails`), and shipping an extension now risks crystallising a model
that the joint OO/PC group may want to revise at Rotterdam; and (3) the ticket
is `ExternallyBlocked` on R6 publication for the truly desired data type
(`DosageDetails`), so any artifact published now is provisional by definition
and may need to be deprecated immediately on R6.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **B (the current "Persuasive with Modification" resolution)**.

This is the right balance for OO. Disposition A is not actually deliverable
against the current Extensions Pack substrate — the `ExternallyBlocked` label
and David Barwin's own comment confirm that R6 `DosageDetails` is unavailable
to extensions published today, so accepting "as requested" would either delay
the ticket indefinitely or quietly downgrade to the R5 `Dosage` type anyway
(which is exactly what Disposition B does explicitly). Disposition C
under-serves a real and concrete implementer need that the joint OO/PC WGM
already validated: there are request-side dosing scenarios (titration, cannula
flow rates, cellular-therapy ordering, and the anesthetic / "do not use"
scenarios esilver raised) that do not fit cleanly into `MedicationRequest` and
that implementers are otherwise carrying as opaque text today.

The two open items the workgroup should explicitly debate before final
publication are (i) cardinality — keep at `0..1` per the resolution, or relax
to `0..*` per the reporter's rationale that instructions evolve over the
administration period (the reporter's case is reasonable; suggest accepting
`0..*`); and (ii) whether to coordinate with Patient Care on FHIR-13047 to
publish a single generalized extension with both `ServiceRequest` and
`Procedure` as context resources, deferring publication until after the
Rotterdam WGM. Both adjustments fit cleanly within Disposition B and should be
captured as discussion points when the disposition is voted on.
