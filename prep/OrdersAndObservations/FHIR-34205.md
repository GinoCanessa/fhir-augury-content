## Summary

Hans Buitendijk (Orders & Observations WG, LAB call) is asking FHIR Core to
extend `ServiceRequest.status` and `ServiceRequest.reason.concept` to support a
"re-interpretation" workflow: once a `ServiceRequest` has reached
`status = completed` and the ordering provider later decides that the
associated `Observation` results need to be re-interpreted (because of a
clinical-profile concern, a demographic correction, or a change in an
ask-at-order-entry answer), the existing `ServiceRequest` would be moved back
into an in-flight state until re-interpretation finishes and then returned to
`completed`. Specifically the proposal adds two new `ServiceRequest.status`
codes (`under-revision`, `under-review`), three new
`ServiceRequest.reason.concept` codes (`clinical-reinterpretation`,
`demographic-reinterpretation`,
`ask-at-order-entry-response-reinterpretation`), and a comment on
`ServiceRequest.reason` that the observations to be re-interpreted should be
referenced via `ServiceRequest.reason.reference`. The ticket notes that when
an AOE change drives the re-interpretation, the related `Observation.status`
would correspondingly move to `corrected`/`amended`.

The ticket originated from the 2021 O&O "Reflex/Follow-Up/Add-On Orders"
thread on the FHIR chat (with siblings FHIR-34204, FHIR-34206, FHIR-34207). It
was voted Persuasive in 2023-12, reverted and reopened 2024-05 (4–0–1,
Jose/Yanick), and has since attracted substantive pushback from apitkus
citing CLIA 42 CFR 493.1291(k)/(i) requirements (preserving the original
report, issuing corrected reports, referring labs "must not revise results"),
and from Riki Merrick / Ulrike Merrick pointing to the IHE LCC
(LAB-6/LAB-7 "fulfillment order") pattern, which models re-interpretation as
a **new** order with a relationship to the original rather than reopening the
completed one. The ticket is currently Triaged / Unresolved pending a
revised proposal aligned with that IHE approach and a terminology review
requested of Jose Costa-Teixeira.

## Details

**Description:**

To enable follow-up steps on a ServiceRequest with a status of complete (e.g.,
the results were communicated as final but the ordering provider wishes the
observation to be re-interpreted in light of, e.g., the patient's clinical
profile and/or demographic data updates) additional status codes are needed
that can be valued after a `ServiceRequest.status` was set to "complete".

Additionally it is then necessary to indicate the reason why the related
observation(s) need to be re-interpreted.

Once the re-interpretation is completed, the `ServiceRequest.status` is set
back to "complete".

Proposal is to add:

- `ServiceRequest.status` codes for:
  - `under-revision` — a change to AOEs or demographics yielded the re-interpretation
  - `under-review` — the results do not appear to fit the patient's clinical profile
- `ServiceRequest.reason.concept` codes for:
  - `clinical-reinterpretation` — Re-interpretation (clinically questioned) + text (valid only with `under-review`)
  - `demographic-reinterpretation` — Re-Interpretation (demographic change) (valid only with `under-revision`)
  - `ask-at-order-entry-response-reinterpretation` — Re-interpretation (AOE change) (valid only with `under-revision`)
    - Note: for AOEs the `Observation.status` would have changed to `corrected`/`amended`.
- `ServiceRequest.reason` comments addition:
  - When a re-interpretation is requested, the observation(s) that are to be re-interpreted should be included as a reference in `ServiceRequest.reason.reference`.

**Comments:**

- **ehaas (2021-11-01):** Recommends adding/refreshing the `ServiceRequest`
  state-machine diagram if these codes are added — otherwise the new
  transitions will confuse readers.
- **apitkus (2023-02-09):** Extensive pushback citing CLIA 42 CFR
  493.1291(k) and (i): when errors are detected the lab must (k1) notify the
  orderer, (k2) issue corrected reports, and (k3) **maintain duplicates of
  the original and corrected report**; referring labs "must not revise
  results" per (i). Concludes completed orders should **not** be "re-opened
  and re-completed" — amended reports are the correct mechanism. Also flags
  real-world side effects (billing double-charges, CDS/notification alerts
  re-firing on re-completion, e.g., a biopsy-follow-up letter sent a second
  time).
- **jdlnolen (2024-05-09):** Motion to reopen carried 4–0–1
  (Jose/Yanick). Reverted the 2023-12-08 "Persuasive" resolution (Riki
  Merrick / Rob Hausam 7-0-1, Impact: Compatible substantive; Category:
  Enhancement) that had accepted the proposal as written.
- **jdlnolen (2025-06-06):** "Needs another look from a terminology
  perspective" — tasks Jose Costa-Teixeira with updates.
- **hbuitendijk (2025-07-11):** Action on Riki to "propose an approach
  analogous to the IHE approach involving a new order for the
  re-interpretation and how the completed order and/or observation is
  updated/amended/other to reflect the re-interpretation."
- **apitkus (2025-07-11):** Reiterates (citing Ulrike Merrick on the call)
  that IHE LCC uses a separate/new order for re-interpretation; updating the
  existing resource is not global best practice. Also quotes from the call
  chat that regulation-defined "error correction" requires both original and
  revised values to remain available, not in-place revision.
- **rikim (2025-07-21):** Provides IHE LCC
  (https://www.ihe.net/uploadedFiles/Documents/PaLM/IHE_PaLM_Suppl_LCC.pdf)
  context. Quotes section X.4: LAB-7 is a new transaction carrying "orders
  for additional work on one or more results" (confirmation, interpretation,
  assertion of error, annotation) linked to the source result via an REL
  segment with `SVTGT` relationship and new REL-17/REL-18 fields identifying
  source/target types. Response can be an addendum/amendment to the original
  result or a report attached to the LAB-7 order. Concludes this most often
  supports correcting-with-comment or amending a prior report; re-issuing a
  new report is typically QC-only — which argues against adding new
  `ServiceRequest.status` codes and would also require state-machine
  updates.
- **rikim (2025-10-03):** Pointer back to the July 2025 comment with the
  IHE LCC diagram.
- **apitkus (2025-10-24):** Asks whether this is intended to cover error
  correction (with its regulatory duplicate-retention requirements).
- **apitkus (2025-10-24):** Confirms the intent is the communication /
  amended-report piece, not in-flight changes to the original `ServiceRequest`
  or `Observation`; regulatory "error correction" processes must be honored
  regardless of HL7 technical feasibility.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add the exact codes listed in the ticket to the `ServiceRequest.status`
required value set (`under-revision`, `under-review`) and to the
`ServiceRequest.reason.concept` value set
(`clinical-reinterpretation`, `demographic-reinterpretation`,
`ask-at-order-entry-response-reinterpretation`), apply the stated
conditional pairing (reason-code ↔ status-code), update the
`ServiceRequest.reason` element description to document that the
observation(s) being re-interpreted must be referenced via
`ServiceRequest.reason.reference`, and — per ehaas's 2021 comment —
update the `ServiceRequest` state-machine diagram to show transitions
from `completed` into `under-review`/`under-revision` and back. Update
the `Observation.status` guidance to note that AOE-driven
re-interpretation typically accompanies an `Observation.status` of
`corrected`/`amended`.

#### Justification

Matches what the original O&O LAB call agreed to (and what the WG voted
Persuasive in December 2023 at 7-0-1) and uses mechanisms already
present in the resource. It is a compatible, substantive change:
existing implementers that never re-open a completed `ServiceRequest`
would be unaffected, while those that want to express an in-flight
re-interpretation now have spec-defined codes instead of bespoke
extensions. Adding a state-machine diagram update addresses ehaas's
readability concern. However, this disposition must reconcile the CLIA
duplicate-retention and "referring lab must not revise results"
requirements that apitkus raises (42 CFR 493.1291(k),(i)) — the
expectation here is that implementers satisfy those via FHIR versioning
/ `Provenance` / `Observation.status = corrected` rather than through
`ServiceRequest` semantics, and that expectation should be stated in
the resource/element descriptions.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach (IHE LCC-style new fulfillment order)

#### Proposal

Decline the in-place status additions and instead document a
**fulfillment-order pattern** on `ServiceRequest`, aligned with the
IHE LCC LAB-7 transaction that Riki Merrick cited. Concretely:

- Leave `ServiceRequest.status` value set unchanged; a completed request
  stays `completed`.
- Model a re-interpretation as a **new** `ServiceRequest` that points
  back to the original via `ServiceRequest.basedOn` (or a new
  `relationship`-style code if WG prefers, analogous to the IHE
  `SVTGT`), with `ServiceRequest.intent = reflex-order` or a new
  `reinterpretation` intent if needed.
- Add a bound value set for `ServiceRequest.reason.concept` covering
  the three reasons originally proposed
  (`clinical-reinterpretation`, `demographic-reinterpretation`,
  `ask-at-order-entry-response-reinterpretation`) — so the clinical
  intent is still expressible — and let
  `ServiceRequest.reason.reference` carry the `Observation`(s) under
  re-interpretation.
- On the result side, keep using `Observation.status = corrected` /
  `amended` (with `Observation.previous`/history) to record the
  revised value alongside the original.
- Update the `ServiceRequest` narrative (and, if helpful, the state
  diagram) to describe this pattern instead of new statuses, and
  cross-reference `Provenance` for audit.

#### Justification

Resolves the substantive objections without losing the clinical signal
the ticket wants to convey. The IHE LCC/LAB-7 pattern (Riki Merrick,
2025-07-21) is the accepted global practice for
reflex/follow-up/re-interpretation in lab; apitkus (2025-07-11,
2025-10-24) and Ulrike Merrick have said explicitly that re-opening a
completed order is not best practice and that CLIA 42 CFR
493.1291(k)(3) requires the **original** report to be preserved
alongside the corrected one. A new linked `ServiceRequest` keeps the
original order's audit trail intact, avoids the downstream side effects
apitkus flagged (billing re-drops, re-firing notifications on
`completed` transitions), and lets jurisdictions that forbid
in-place revision (referring-lab constraint, 42 CFR 493.1291(i))
remain compliant. It also directly tracks the July 2025 action item
Hans Buitendijk assigned to Riki Merrick ("propose an approach
analogous to the IHE approach"). Trade-off vs. Disposition A: slightly
more implementer work to introduce the fulfillment-order link, but the
mechanism is already present in `ServiceRequest.basedOn` /
`Observation.basedOn`, so no breaking change is introduced.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request as written (no new `ServiceRequest.status` codes,
no new `ServiceRequest.reason.concept` codes) and close the ticket with
a "Not Persuasive — already addressed by existing mechanisms"
resolution. Point implementers to the existing toolkit: new
`ServiceRequest` with `basedOn` / `replaces` for a follow-up order,
`Observation.status = corrected`/`amended` with `Observation.previous`
for the corrected result, and `Provenance` for audit — with a pointer
to IHE LCC for the clinical pattern.

#### Justification

The regulatory commentary (apitkus, 2023-02-09 and 2025-10-24) is
clear that `ServiceRequest.status` must not be used to reopen a
completed order: CLIA 42 CFR 493.1291(k)(3) mandates that the **original**
completed report be preserved, and 493.1291(i) prohibits referring labs
from revising results at all. Adding `under-review`/`under-revision`
codes to the required status value set risks pushing conformant
implementers toward a workflow that is non-compliant in regulated
jurisdictions. The clinical need — expressing *why* a re-interpretation
is happening — is already satisfiable today through
`ServiceRequest.basedOn` + `ServiceRequest.reason` on a new order and
`Observation.status = corrected`/`amended` on the revised result. The
2023 Persuasive vote was reverted in 2024 precisely because these
concerns surfaced late, and no revised proposal that resolves them has
been submitted.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the only option that (a) preserves the clinical
intent Hans Buitendijk and the LAB call wanted to capture —
distinguishing clinical vs. demographic vs. AOE-driven
re-interpretation — while (b) honoring the CLIA and referring-lab
constraints apitkus, Ulrike Merrick, and Riki Merrick have documented,
and (c) aligning with the prevailing IHE LCC LAB-7 pattern that
Orders & Observations has repeatedly invoked in this thread. It is
also what Hans Buitendijk's own 2025-07-11 action item to Riki
effectively points at: "propose an approach analogous to the IHE
approach involving a new order for the re-interpretation". Disposition
A keeps the ticket's literal wording but carries forward the exact
concerns that caused the WG to reopen it in 2024, and would still
require a state-machine diagram revision and a terminology review;
Disposition C is defensible but discards the useful signal the new
reason codes provide. The workgroup should drive the revised proposal
to the fulfillment-order pattern, keep the three reason codes (scoped
to `ServiceRequest.reason.concept`), and leave `ServiceRequest.status`
unchanged.
