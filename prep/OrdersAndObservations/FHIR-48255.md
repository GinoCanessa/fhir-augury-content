## Summary

The submitter (Kathy Walsh, on behalf of LabCorp) asks the Laboratory
Report (FHIR) IG to permit a "high-level" `DiagnosticReport` that
contains nested `DiagnosticReport` resources — one per ordered panel —
rather than relying on a transmission-style `Bundle` of multiple
parallel reports. The submitter explicitly does **not** want this
grouping to be carried by nested `Observation` resources, because
"Observation is referenced by the DiagnosticReport to provide the
atomic results for a particular investigation," and treating an
Observation as a panel header would force empty `Observation.value`
elements.

The closely-linked sibling ticket
[FHIR-48258](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48258)
("DiagnosticReport and ServiceRequest code property have a cardinality
of 1") proposes the structural enabler for nesting: extend
`DiagnosticReport.basedOn` to accept references to `DiagnosticReport`,
analogous to how `ServiceRequest.basedOn` already supports
`ServiceRequest`. FHIR-48258's proposed resolution
("Persuasive with Modification") explicitly defers the in-report
grouping mechanism to FHIR-48255, and proposes creating two Lab Report
Bundle profiles — one with `bundle.entry.DiagnosticReport 1..1` and
one with `bundle.entry.DiagnosticReport 1..*` — to cover both grouping
patterns. The 2025-03-07 comment on FHIR-48255 itself notes that a
larger international group discussion was scheduled for 2025-03-18 to
move this forward, and the related Zulip thread shows the discussion
has continued (and remains unresolved) into early 2026 with the
"Grouping Option Analysis" Confluence page being driven through OO Lab
calls in the lead-up to R6 ballot reconciliation.

## Details

**Description:**

> A Laboratory report should allow a high-level DiagnosticReport
> containing multiple DiagnosticReports for each ordered panel. We are
> currently sending a bundle containing multiple Diagnostic Reports
> but not a FHIR 'document' Bundle. We do not think Observations
> should be nested since "The Observation resource is referenced by
> the DiagnosticReport to provide the atomic results for a particular
> investigation." We do Not think an Observations should include
> panel headers (and thus have empty Observation.value attributes,
> etc.).

**Comments:**

- **rhausam (2024-12-13) — 2024-12-13 Lab Report ballot rec:** It
  seems neither possible nor desirable to remove the ability to
  "nest" Observation resources, as Observation is normative and
  has supported nesting since DSTU 1 (Sept 2014). Kathy Walsh
  submitted the issue for LabCorp but is not the original author;
  she will invite her colleague to the Jan 10 call to plan how to
  address it in detail. The issue is related to
  [FHIR-48258](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48258), which
  discusses the possibility of nesting `DiagnosticReport`
  resources.
- **rhausam (2025-03-07):** Larger group discussion on this
  (including Europe, Australia and US) is planned for March 18
  3:00 PM EDT.

## Related Jira Tickets

### [FHIR-48258: DiagnosticReport and ServiceRequest code property have a cardinality of 1](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48258)

Sibling ticket from the same reporter on the same Lab Report
specification. It proposes extending `DiagnosticReport.basedOn` to
accept references to `DiagnosticReport`, mirroring the existing
`ServiceRequest.basedOn -> ServiceRequest` pattern, so that a panel
report can be assembled by linking subordinate `DiagnosticReport`
resources without forcing each panel to be its own top-level report.
The current proposed resolution is "Persuasive with Modification":
keep `DiagnosticReport.code` at `1..1`, keep `ServiceRequest.code` at
`0..1`, group orders via shared `ServiceRequest.requisition`, and
create two Lab Report `Bundle` profiles to cover both grouping
patterns. The note explicitly states that the in-report grouping
mechanism is deferred to FHIR-48255, making the two tickets
mutually-dependent.

## Related Zulip Discussions

### [Orders and Observation WG > Grouping options for Observation/DiagnosticReport](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/Orders%20and%20Observation%20WG/topic/Grouping%20options%20for%20Observation%2FDiagnosticReport/near/511035774)

The single, very long-running OO thread (originally titled "Usage of
panel of Observations") collects the international debate that
FHIR-48255 and FHIR-48258 are intended to settle. The April 2025 post
that was indexed is Rob Hausam's announcement of a global meeting on
2025-04-15 to drive consensus, with explicit links to both
FHIR-48255 and FHIR-48258 and direct mentions of @Angus Millar,
@David McKillop, @Grahame Grieve, and @Giorgio Cangioli.

The full thread (86 messages, 2023-05 → 2026-02) lays out the
substantive options being weighed:

- **Option 1** — `DiagnosticReport.code = panel`, with
  `DiagnosticReport.result` pointing directly at each component
  `Observation` (e.g., Hb, RCC, WBC for a CBC/FBC). Aligns with US
  Core's Laboratory Result Observation Profile.
- **Option 2** — `DiagnosticReport.code = panel`, with
  `DiagnosticReport.result` pointing at a single grouping
  `Observation` whose `Observation.hasMember` references the
  components. Aligns with IPS Observation Results: laboratory and
  with AU Base / AU Core Pathology profiles, which go further by
  treating individual tests the same as panels.
- **Italian Lab Report IG variant** — fixes
  `DiagnosticReport.code = LOINC 11502-2` and uses an "Observation
  battery" profile with `value` and `component` constrained out
  (`0..0`) for panels.
- **`Observation.component` vs `Observation.hasMember`** — Bryn
  Rhodes, Elliot Silver and others articulate the rule of thumb that
  `component` is appropriate when the parts cannot meaningfully
  stand alone (systolic vs diastolic blood pressure), and
  `hasMember` otherwise. The R4 vital-signs profile is cited as
  normative for BP and is staying in core.
- **Open governance question** — John Moehrke asks whether FHIR core
  needs to pick one model at all, given that both are already
  supported, or whether OO should provide guidance only. No
  consensus on that question is reached in the thread.
- **Recent activity (Jan–Feb 2026)** — Ralf Herzog announces a
  dedicated UV Lab call on 2026-01-16 to converge on a position
  before R6 ballot reconciliation, and Marti Velezis points
  reviewers at the Confluence page
  `Grouping Option Analysis` (`/spaces/OO/pages/403871266/`) updated
  during the WGM. The thread is still active and has not yet
  declared a winning option.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the Laboratory Report (FHIR) IG to formally permit a
"container" `DiagnosticReport` whose `DiagnosticReport.result` is
empty (or omitted) and which instead aggregates per-panel
`DiagnosticReport` resources via a new IG-level convention. Concretely:

1. In the Lab Report IG, define a "Lab Report — Grouping
   DiagnosticReport" profile and a "Lab Report — Panel
   DiagnosticReport" profile.
2. Adopt the FHIR-48258 proposed mechanism: extend
   `DiagnosticReport.basedOn` (in the IG profile) to accept
   `Reference(DiagnosticReport)` so that each panel
   `DiagnosticReport` cites the grouping report as its parent. (At
   the IG layer this is achievable today via an extension or via
   profiling `basedOn` if/when the core resource permits it.)
3. Publish the second of the two Bundle profiles proposed in
   FHIR-48258's resolution (`bundle.entry.DiagnosticReport 1..*`) as
   the canonical container for this pattern, with the grouping
   `DiagnosticReport` as `Composition`-style root entry.
4. Keep `Observation.hasMember` / `Observation.component` strictly
   for atomic-result grouping inside a panel, per the submitter's
   request that panel headers not be modelled as value-less
   Observations.

#### Justification

This directly answers what LabCorp submitted: a way to express a
laboratory report as a hierarchy of DiagnosticReports without
forcing them into a value-less Observation chain or a flat
transmission Bundle. The structural enabler (`DiagnosticReport.basedOn
-> DiagnosticReport`) is already proposed and tentatively accepted in
the linked sibling FHIR-48258, so no orphan change is required.
Defining the grouping at the IG profile layer (rather than mutating
the normative resource in core) avoids breaking existing
implementations and is consistent with the long-standing OO position
documented in the 2024-12-13 ballot rec comment on this ticket: the
ability to "nest" `Observation` is normative and cannot be removed,
so the right place to express the new pattern is alongside it, not in
its place.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Do **not** introduce a new top-level grouping `DiagnosticReport`.
Instead, publish in the Lab Report IG a clear "Grouping Option
Analysis" decision (the artifact already in flight on Confluence
`/spaces/OO/pages/403871266/`) that:

1. Recognises three permissible patterns (Option 1 — flat,
   Option 2 — `Observation.hasMember` grouper, Italian/IPS-style
   battery profile) and constrains the IG to **Option 2** as the
   canonical pattern, with `Observation.hasMember` carrying the
   panel structure and `DiagnosticReport.result` pointing at the
   grouping `Observation`s.
2. Defines a single Lab Report `Bundle` profile (the
   `bundle.entry.DiagnosticReport 1..1` variant from FHIR-48258's
   resolution) so that exactly one `DiagnosticReport` ever appears
   per Bundle.
3. Accommodates the submitter's panel-header concern by either
   (a) profiling the grouping `Observation` to require
   `Observation.code` and constrain `Observation.value[x]` to `0..0`
   (the Italian "Observation battery" pattern), or (b) using
   `Observation.dataAbsentReason` on the grouper.

#### Justification

The 86-message Zulip thread, the AU Base / IPS / Italian Lab Report
profiles, and Bryn Rhodes' / Elliot Silver's analysis all converge
on `Observation.hasMember` (and, where genuinely atomic,
`Observation.component`) as the *existing* mechanism for in-report
grouping. Adding a *second* mechanism — nested DiagnosticReports — at
the same time as the `hasMember` pattern is being formally documented
risks fragmenting implementations and doubling reviewer/validator
effort. The submitter's specific concern (that header Observations
must be value-less) is real but is already solved by the
"Observation battery" profile pattern, which is in production use in
the Italian Lab Report IG. This disposition keeps the wire format
compatible with US Core, IPS and AU Core without forcing any of them
to adopt a new container.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request to introduce a grouping/container
`DiagnosticReport`. Mark the ticket "Not Persuasive" with the
rationale that:

1. The pattern the submitter wants is already expressible today via
   `Observation.hasMember`, optionally combined with the
   "Observation battery" profile pattern — neither requires any
   change to the Lab Report IG or to the base `DiagnosticReport`
   resource.
2. The closely-linked structural enabler (FHIR-48258's
   `DiagnosticReport.basedOn -> DiagnosticReport`) can stand on its
   own to express *workflow* relationships between reports without
   implying an in-report grouping semantics; that is the more
   conservative reading.
3. Two competing grouping mechanisms (nested DRs *and* `hasMember`)
   in the same IG would force every consumer (US Core, IPS, AU
   Core, vendors) to handle both, with no clear precedence rule.

#### Justification

The Zulip thread and the comments on this ticket itself show the
workgroup has not reached consensus that a new mechanism is needed —
only that the existing options need to be documented better. John
Moehrke explicitly questions whether FHIR core needs to pick one
model at all. The 2024-12-13 ballot-rec comment from rhausam states
that nesting Observations is normative and cannot be removed; that
position is in tension with the submitter's premise that
Observations are the wrong place for this. Declining preserves the
status quo, keeps the IG aligned with US Core, IPS and AU Core
guidance, and lets FHIR-48258 proceed independently as a workflow
fix.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The Zulip thread (still active in Feb 2026, explicitly tied to the
"Grouping Option Analysis" Confluence page being driven for R6 ballot
reconciliation) makes clear that the workgroup is converging on the
`Observation.hasMember` / "Observation battery" pattern, not on a new
nested-`DiagnosticReport` container. Disposition B honours the
submitter's underlying use case (a single coherent lab report covering
multiple ordered panels) and the explicit submitter requirement
(panel headers should not appear as value-less Observations) while
staying inside a single, IG-published mechanism that is already in
production use internationally. It also leaves FHIR-48258's
`Bundle`-profile resolution intact and makes the "1..1
DiagnosticReport per Bundle" variant the canonical wire format,
which keeps validation tractable. Disposition A is a defensible
fallback if the workgroup believes the panel-as-Observation modelling
will not be acceptable to LabCorp and other large US reference labs;
Disposition C should only be chosen if the workgroup concludes the
existing text already gives implementers enough guidance, which the
86-message thread strongly suggests it does not.
