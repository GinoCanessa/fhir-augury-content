## Summary

The submitter (Alex Goel) requests that the **Cancer Pathology Data Sharing
(FHIR) IG** add explicit guidance on how `Specimen` is referenced from
`DiagnosticReport.specimen` and `Observation.specimen`, and on how
specimen-related information should be partitioned between the `Specimen`
resource and the `Observation` resources that describe it. The motivating
example is the IG's `observation-procedure-breast-excision` instance, in
which a pathologist captures specimen-related details inside an Observation
generated from a CAP electronic Cancer Protocol (eCP) section — leaving it
unclear when implementers should populate the `Specimen` resource itself
versus encoding the same facts as Observations linked to it.

The ticket is closely entangled with two other O&O Cancer Pathology
issues. [FHIR-50677](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50677)
("Representation of Specimen Derivation Hierarchies") covers parent/child
specimen modeling (e.g., gross specimen → blocks → slides → stains) — work
that the WG concluded must precede a complete answer to FHIR-50682, since
"what belongs in Specimen vs. Observation" depends on whether derived
specimens are modeled at all. [FHIR-50676](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50676)
("Basic Report Elements Required by CLIA") was the home of the parallel
CLIA / CAP accreditation discussion that surfaced in this ticket's comment
thread; it has since been resolved as Not Persuasive after CAP confirmed
that the IG must *support* CLIA elements rather than *enforce* them.

The most recent activity (2025-10-03) re-opened the ticket post-STU2
publication after a previously recorded "Considered for Future Use" vote
(10-2-3, motion by Alex Goel / Patrick Joy) was reverted. The ticket is
back in Triaged / Unresolved and is expected to be aligned with the
deferred specimen-hierarchy work in a future ballot cycle.

## Details

**Description:**

Create guidance on whether to link both the `DiagnosticReport.specimen` and
`Observation.specimen` to a `Specimen` resource.

Provide guidance on what information should go in the specimen resource
versus the Observation when referring to the specimen. E.g., when to use
this example —
<https://hl7.org/fhir/us/cancer-reporting/2025May/Observation-observation-procedure-breast-excision.html>
— which should be created when the Pathologist fills out a section in the
eCP which includes specimen details.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a new narrative section to the Cancer Pathology Data Sharing (FHIR)
IG — e.g., a "Specimen vs. Observation Guidance" page in the IG narrative
— that explicitly tells implementers:

1. **`DiagnosticReport.specimen`** lists every `Specimen` involved in the
   report (parent and any modeled derivatives that are individually
   referenced).
2. **`Observation.specimen`** is required on each component Observation
   that derives a result from a particular sample, and its target must
   resolve to a `Specimen` in `DiagnosticReport.specimen`.
3. **Intrinsic specimen facts** (collection site/body site, collection
   method, container, fixative, identifier/accession, parent/derivation
   chain, time-in-fixation, cold-ischemia time) belong in the **US
   Pathology Specimen** profile and **must not** appear as free text on
   `DiagnosticReport.specimen` or be duplicated as Observations.
4. **Pathology-procedure findings** (e.g., the
   `observation-procedure-breast-excision` example) belong as
   `Observation` instances answering specific eCP questions; they
   reference the relevant `Specimen` via `Observation.specimen` rather
   than re-stating intrinsic specimen facts.
5. Provide one fully-worked example showing a single accessioned parent
   specimen + multiple derived specimens (block → slide → biomarker
   stain) bound back through `Specimen.parent`, with `Observation`
   instances pointing at the appropriate level of the chain.

Add inline cross-references between this page and the existing US
Pathology Diagnostic Report, US Pathology Service Request, and US
Pathology Specimen profile pages.

#### Justification

This is exactly the deliverable the submitter and several commenters
asked for, and Alex Goel explicitly stated (2025-07-21) that "some
narrative describing this would be helpful for implementers." The
guidance reuses existing FHIR mechanisms (`DiagnosticReport.specimen`,
`Observation.specimen`, `Specimen.parent`,
`ServiceRequest.specimen` ↔ `Specimen.request`) without changing
conformance, so it does not introduce a breaking change. It also
crystallizes the May 15 WG conclusions ("intrinsic specimen facts belong
in Specimen, not free-text in DiagnosticReport") into something
implementers can ship against.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Defer the deep guidance to a future iteration aligned with FHIR-50677
("Representation of Specimen Derivation Hierarchies"), but in this cycle
land a **minimal "flat-specimen" guidance block** plus a **navigation
fix**:

1. Add a short paragraph to the IG narrative (e.g., on the US Pathology
   Diagnostic Report and US Pathology Specimen profile pages) stating
   the minimum-viable pattern from the May 15 discussion:
   - one `Specimen` per `DiagnosticReport` bundle,
   - every Observation populates `Observation.specimen` pointing at it,
   - no free-text shortcuts in `DiagnosticReport.specimen`,
   - intrinsic specimen facts live in `Specimen`, not in `Observation`
     `valueString`s.
2. Annotate the `observation-procedure-breast-excision` example
   description to clarify it answers an eCP procedure question and is
   *not* a substitute for populating the `Specimen` resource.
3. Defer the multi-derived-specimen guidance, parent/child examples, and
   multi-lab workflow guidance to the same iteration that resolves
   FHIR-50677, and record that linkage on both tickets.

#### Justification

This honors the WG's August consensus (Goel / Joy 10-2-3 motion to
"consider for future use and track in the open issues list") while still
giving implementers something concrete in the current cycle, addressing
Alex Goel's "narrative would be helpful" comment without making promises
the WG cannot back up before FHIR-50677 lands. It avoids the trap that
JIRAUSER28539 flagged on 2025-07-22 — producing "incomplete, misleading
guidance" by writing the full derived-specimen story before the
hierarchy model exists. It is also cheaper to ballot than Disposition A.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve as **Not Persuasive / No Change**, on the grounds that the IG
already provides the necessary linking mechanisms and the request as
written is a duplicate of work tracked under FHIR-50677. Specifically:

- `Specimen` is must-support on the US Pathology Diagnostic Report.
- `ServiceRequest.specimen` ↔ `Specimen.request` provides bidirectional
  linkage.
- `Observation.specimen → Specimen` is the standard FHIR pattern and
  needs no IG-specific restatement.
- The `observation-procedure-breast-excision` example documents an eCP
  procedure response and was never intended to substitute for the US
  Pathology Specimen profile.
- The deeper "what goes where" question is conditional on the
  derived-specimen hierarchy model being decided in FHIR-50677 and
  cannot be answered independently.

#### Justification

This mirrors the WG's 2025-07-18 "Reject Request / No Change" proposed
disposition, which has been re-stated in nearly identical form three
times in the comment history (2025-06-29, 2025-07-18, and 2025-10-03).
The mechanisms the submitter is asking for guidance on already exist;
the open question is *editorial* (where to write the guidance) rather
than *conformance*. Declining keeps the queue clean and pushes the real
substantive work to FHIR-50677, where the derived-specimen modeling
question is properly scoped.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The comment thread shows the WG repeatedly oscillating between
"reject — guidance already exists" (Disposition C) and "defer to align
with FHIR-50677" (Disposition B-flavored), with the most recent
2025-10-03 re-open explicitly calling out that the deeper work will be
prioritized for a future iteration alongside FHIR-50677. Disposition A
is attractive on the merits but, as JIRAUSER28539 warned on 2025-07-22,
writing the full Specimen-vs-Observation story before the hierarchy
model is settled risks producing misleading IG narrative that the WG
will have to retract once FHIR-50677 lands. Disposition C is technically
defensible but ignores Alex Goel's explicit and uncontested point that
"some narrative describing this would be helpful for implementers" — and
the third re-open of this ticket suggests "no change" is not actually
sticking with the WG.

Disposition B threads that needle: ship a small, low-risk narrative
block that captures the May 15 minimum-viable pattern and annotates the
breast-excision example, formally link this ticket to FHIR-50677, and
defer the deep multi-derived-specimen / multi-lab guidance to that
ticket's resolution. It also makes the CLIA scope discussion explicit
out-of-scope-here (already settled in FHIR-50676) so that thread does
not re-derail the next pass.
