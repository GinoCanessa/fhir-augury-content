## Summary

The reporter identifies two minor grammatical issues in the *HL7 FHIR® Implementation Guide: Quality Measures, Edition 1 — US Realm* (Quality Measure IG / QM IG). First, in **Summary section 1.1**, the first sentence contains a stray comma immediately after the parenthetical expansion "The Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resource (FHIR) Quality Measure Implementation Guide (QM IG)," which the reporter recommends removing for readability. Second, in **section 1.3.3 "Data Model Standards Landscape"**, within the STU Note, the sentence ends abruptly after the word "implementers"; the reporter suggests adding a preposition (their example: "implementers on") to complete the phrasing. There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items associated with this ticket — it is an isolated editorial cleanup request.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply both editorial corrections to the *Quality Measure IG (US Realm), Edition 1* narrative:

1. **Section 1.1 (Summary):** In the first sentence, delete the trailing comma immediately following "(QM IG)" so the sentence reads naturally without the unnecessary pause after the parenthetical acronym expansion.
2. **Section 1.3.3 (Data Model Standards Landscape), STU Note:** Insert the missing preposition after "implementers" — most likely "on" as the reporter suggests, but the editor should verify against the surrounding sentence structure (alternatives such as "for" or "with" may fit better depending on what follows). If the sentence is truncated rather than missing a single preposition, complete it to convey the intended meaning.

Both edits are confined to the IG's narrative pages and require no FHIR resource, profile, value set, or conformance changes. Republish the IG (or include in the next errata/STU update) once corrected.

#### Justification

These are pure editorial defects in published narrative — neither affects normative content, conformance, or implementation behaviour. Accepting and applying them improves the readability and professionalism of a published HL7 specification at near-zero cost and risk. The reporter has identified the exact sections and the exact text to change, so the engineering effort is trivial and easily verifiable on review.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the editorial intent but **batch the fix with other pending narrative corrections** for the Quality Measure IG rather than producing a one-off republish. Open a tracking ticket (or label this one) as part of an "editorial sweep" for the IG, then resolve all narrative-only tickets together in the next scheduled errata release. As part of that sweep, also have a technical writer or co-chair re-read sections 1.1 and 1.3.3 in full to catch any nearby issues the reporter may not have flagged (e.g., the abrupt-sentence problem in 1.3.3 may indicate a larger truncation worth reviewing in context).

#### Justification

Republishing an IG carries some publication overhead (build, validation, posting, change-log entry) that is disproportionate to a two-character fix. Bundling editorial-only changes reduces churn for implementers tracking the published artifact and gives the work group a chance to apply consistent copy-editing standards across the IG. The trade-off versus Disposition A is a slightly longer time to publication of these specific fixes, which is acceptable given the low severity (no implementer is blocked or misled by either issue).

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket as "Not Persuasive" / "Not Related" on the grounds that pure typographical and grammatical issues without functional impact do not warrant individual ticket processing in the FHIR Jira workflow, and direct the reporter to submit such corrections via a pull request against the IG source repository (or via the publication editor) instead.

#### Justification

This rationale is defensible only if the work group has an established policy of routing pure editorial fixes outside Jira. Absent such a policy, declining a clearly-correct, low-cost editorial fix is hard to justify: the reporter has done the work of locating the defects, and refusing the change leaves known errors in a published HL7 specification. This option is included for completeness but is the weakest of the three.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Accept and apply both edits as requested. The reporter has pinpointed specific text in two specific sections of a published IG; the changes are unambiguous, carry no normative risk, and improve the quality of an HL7-branded artifact. Disposition B's "batch with other editorial fixes" approach is reasonable as an *implementation tactic* if the CQI work group already has a backlog of similar tickets queued for the next errata, but that is an editorial-process decision rather than a reason to defer agreement on the change itself — the work group should still resolve this ticket as Persuasive / Accept and let the editor schedule the actual republish. Disposition C should only be selected if the work group has explicitly decided that grammar-only tickets must be filed elsewhere, which would also warrant clearer guidance to reporters going forward.
