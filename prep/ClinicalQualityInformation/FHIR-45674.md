## Summary

The reporter identifies two typographical errors in the *HL7 FHIR®
Implementation Guide: Quality Measures, Edition 1 – US Realm* (the "US
FHIR Guidance – Quality Reporting (FHIR)" specification owned by
Clinical Quality Information):

1. On the home page, section **1.8 IP Statements**, the word "Furthe" is
   missing a trailing **"r"** (should be "Further").
2. In **§5.3 Subject-level Linear Combination Scoring** of the
   Composites page, the last sentence contains "indicates" misspelled
   without its **"d"** (i.e., "inicates" should be "indicates").

This is a pure narrative-text technical correction; no resource,
profile, value set, or constraint is affected. There are no
cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items linked to
this issue in the Augury index, so there is no broader discussion or
implementation context to fold into the request.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Persuasive with mod — apply both narrative corrections to the *HL7
FHIR® Implementation Guide: Quality Measures, Edition 1 – US Realm*:

1. **Home page §1.8 IP Statements** — change "Furthe" → "Further" in the
   sentence flagged by the reporter.
2. **Composites §5.3 Subject-level Linear Combination Scoring** — change
   "inicates" → "indicates" in the last sentence of the section.

Both edits are confined to narrative markdown/HTML in the IG's
authoring source (Quality Measures IG, owned by Clinical Quality
Information). No resource, profile, extension, value set, code system,
example, or constraint is touched. The fix should ship in the next
errata / technical-correction publication of the IG.

#### Justification

These are unambiguous spelling errors in published normative-track
narrative text. HL7's Technical Correction process exists precisely for
this class of change: zero semantic impact, zero implementer burden,
purely improves the readability and professionalism of the
specification. There is no design trade-off, no backward-compatibility
concern, and no ambiguity about the intended words ("Further",
"indicates") given the surrounding sentences cited by the reporter.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the two specific fixes as in Disposition A, and additionally
perform a one-pass spell-check sweep over the entire IG narrative
(home page, all chapters, and the Composites section in particular)
before publishing the technical correction. Track any additional
typos found in this ticket as sub-items (or a follow-up tracker
ticket) so they are corrected in the same publication cycle rather
than dribbling in as separate tickets over time.

#### Justification

Reporters typically surface only the typos they happened to notice.
When two unrelated misspellings are reported in the same submission,
it is a strong signal that the IG has not been spell-checked in a
while and almost certainly contains additional minor errors. Bundling
a sweep with the requested fixes amortises the publication overhead
(build, QA, ballot bookkeeping) and reduces future ticket volume of
this type. The cost is small — narrative-only edits do not require
re-balloting — and the reputational benefit of a clean IG is
disproportionate.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Not Persuasive — close without action on the basis that errata of this
size do not warrant a Jira workflow / publication cycle, and direct
the reporter to submit a pull request against the IG source
repository instead.

#### Justification

This disposition is technically defensible only as a process argument:
the workgroup could assert that single-character typo fixes are below
the threshold that justifies tracking, balloting, and a republish, and
that the community PR channel is the appropriate venue. It is **not**
recommended, because (a) the reporter has used the official feedback
channel in good faith, (b) Technical Correction is exactly the issue
type filed, and (c) declining a clearly-correct fix damages the
feedback loop the workgroup depends on for future substantive
comments.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A (with the optional sweep from B if
publication scheduling allows).

These are two unambiguous spelling errors in published narrative,
filed as a Technical Correction by the reporter. There is no design
question, no community disagreement (no Zulip or Jira discussion
exists), and no implementer impact. Accept and apply both edits in the
next errata publication of the *Quality Measures IG, Edition 1 – US
Realm*. If the workgroup has bandwidth before the next publication
window, also run a quick spell-check pass over the IG narrative
(Disposition B) to absorb any remaining typos in the same cycle and
reduce future ticket churn. Declining (Disposition C) would be a
process-only argument that is not warranted for a correctly-filed
Technical Correction.
