## Summary

Bryn Rhodes is asking the Quality Measures (FHIR) IG (the `HL7/fhir-cqm`
publication) to add a fully worked **risk-adjusted measure example**
based on the CDC **Catheter-Associated Urinary Tract Infection (CAUTI)
Standardized Infection Ratio** measure. The source content for the
example is the CAUTI CQL maintained in
`cqframework/dqm-content-qicore-2026` (`input/cql/CAUTI.cql`) plus the
existing CAUTI walkthrough in the `CQL-Formatting-and-Usage-Wiki`
("Cooking With CQL" #93). The 2/25/26 CQI subgroup discussion (captured
in the comments) further scoped the request: in addition to dropping in
the example, the IG should grow a **new narrative section dedicated to
risk-adjusted measures** — placed after the scoring-type sections —
that explains how to use ratio and continuous-variable measure scoring
together with a risk-adjustment model (e.g., linear regression). The
ticket has already been resolved as **Persuasive**, with the
implementation landed in `HL7/fhir-cqm` commit
`4c969a636ad696f3d86d18f2fa2d3a1fbc4cc36e`. There are no other Jira
tickets cross-referenced from this issue.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Accept the request as stated and as already implemented in
`HL7/fhir-cqm` commit `4c969a6`: add the CAUTI Standardized Infection
Ratio example to the Quality Measures (FHIR) IG as a fully worked
risk-adjusted measure example. The example would include the
`Measure` resource, supporting `Library` resource(s), the CQL drawn
from `cqframework/dqm-content-qicore-2026/input/cql/CAUTI.cql`, and the
matching value sets / supporting artifacts so a reader can trace the
end-to-end SIR calculation. In addition (per the 2/25/26 subgroup
note that the resolver folded into the change), add a new narrative
section to the QM IG, placed **after the scoring-type sections**,
titled along the lines of "Risk-Adjusted Measures." That section would
explain how `ratio` and `continuous-variable` scoring are combined with
a risk-adjustment model (e.g., linear regression), with most of the
prose drawn from `Cooking With CQL #93 / CAUTI.md`, and would link to
the new CAUTI worked example as the canonical illustration.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter asked for, what the 2/25/26 CQI
subgroup scoped, and what was implemented and merged. The resolution is
already marked **Persuasive**, and the commit is referenced in the
ticket. Risk-adjusted scoring (especially SIR-style measures) has long
been a gap in the IG's worked examples — every other major scoring type
has at least one walkthrough. Reusing the CDC CAUTI CQL gives the
example real provenance, and reusing the existing Cooking-With-CQL
narrative avoids re-inventing prose the community has already vetted.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the **narrative section** in the QM IG (the "Risk-Adjusted
Measures" page after the scoring-type sections, derived from
Cooking With CQL #93), but **link to** the CAUTI Measure/Library
artifacts where they are authoritatively maintained — i.e., in
`cqframework/dqm-content-qicore-2026` and/or a dedicated examples
package — rather than copying them into the IG itself. The IG would
host a short worked snippet (the population criteria expressions and
the risk-adjustment model summary) plus deep links to the canonical
artifacts. This keeps the IG narrative-rich without duplicating CQL,
ValueSets, and Measure JSON that will need to be re-synced every time
the upstream CAUTI content changes.

#### Justification

Worked examples baked into the IG tend to drift from their upstream
sources, and CDC-derived measures (CAUTI/SIR) get yearly revisions.
Hosting the prose in the IG and pointing at the upstream content
preserves a single source of truth for the executable artifacts and
reduces ongoing publication burden on the QM IG. The trade-off vs. A
is reduced standalone-readability of the IG (readers have to follow
links to see the full CQL), and the example becomes a moving target
unless the upstream link is pinned to a tag.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline to add a CAUTI-specific worked example to the QM IG. Limit
the change to a brief informative paragraph in the existing scoring
sections noting that ratio and continuous-variable measures may be
combined with an external risk-adjustment model, and direct readers to
the `cqframework` content (`dqm-content-qicore-2026` and Cooking With
CQL #93) for a worked illustration.

#### Justification

The QM IG is meant to specify the conformance surface for quality
measure artifacts; risk adjustment itself is an analytic technique that
sits outside the FHIR conformance boundary. A specific CDC measure
example may be perceived as endorsement of one risk-adjustment
methodology over others, and pulling 30+ pages of CAUTI walkthrough
into the IG inflates publication and review burden. Declining keeps
the IG focused on conformance.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The work is already done and merged (`HL7/fhir-cqm@4c969a6`) under a
**Persuasive** resolution, with explicit CQI subgroup support for both
the worked example *and* the new narrative section, and the reporter is
the WG's CQL/measures editor — there is no community signal pushing
back. Disposition B's "link, don't copy" instinct is reasonable in
principle, but in practice the QM IG already inlines worked Measure /
Library / CQL examples for its other scoring types, so breaking that
convention only for risk-adjusted measures would be inconsistent and
would make the new section the *least* useful page in the IG for
readers learning risk adjustment for the first time. Disposition C
under-serves implementers: SIR-style measures are a real and growing
class of quality measures, and the absence of a worked example has
been a recurring complaint. The workgroup should ratify the merged
change and, if desired, separately track the upstream-sync concern
raised in B as a follow-up maintenance task rather than as a reason to
hold the example out of the IG.
