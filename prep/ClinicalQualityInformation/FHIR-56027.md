## Summary

The reporter observes that, under the QI-Core v6.0.0+ design (which sources
Present-on-Admission (POA) information from Claims rather than from a
QI-Core extension on Condition), the set of encounter diagnoses captured
in Claims can be a strict subset of those captured in the EHR. Claims
generally carry only those diagnoses needed for billing, whereas the EHR
may record additional encounter-level diagnoses that never reach the
claim. The ticket asks for workgroup guidance on how data should be
submitted for quality measurement: should a submitter send only the
claim-sourced diagnoses (preserving the Claims-as-authoritative-POA
position), or should it send every EHR-recorded encounter diagnosis and,
for EHR-only diagnoses that have no POA value in Claims, assign a
surrogate POA value such as "W" (clinically undetermined) or another
sentinel? The reporter is not proposing a spec change per se — they are
asking CQI to state the expected submission pattern so measure
developers and data submitters behave consistently.

The issue has no explicit Jira links and no cross-referenced Zulip/GitHub
items in the augury index, but it sits directly on top of an existing
QI-Core design discussion surfaced in the Zulip thread
*"Diagnosis Present on Admission not used?"* (May–Sep 2025), where Bryn
Rhodes confirmed that the QI-Core POA extension was intentionally
retired in v6.0.0 in favour of Claim-based POA, and where Cooper
Thompson (Epic), Lloyd McKenzie, and Brian Postlethwaite debated
whether POA is purely a billing artefact or also a clinical one.
That thread references the two related Patient Administration tickets
[FHIR-50824](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50824) (encounter-diagnosis
"use" value set gaps) and
[FHIR-50827](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50827) (Encounter.diagnosis.use
binding VS not helpful), both of which affect whether EHR-only encounter
diagnoses can even be meaningfully tagged with a non-billing use code.

The only comment on the ticket, from Anne Smith (2026-04-10), records
that no best practice currently exists; the short-term plan is to add
general guidance to QI-Core and author questions for implementers,
aiming to pilot at the September Connectathon and to pull in NHSN (POA
is one of their priority measures) for cross-program alignment.

## Details

**Description:**

> As we agree that the Claims data is an appropriate source for retrieving the POA indicator information.  Since Claims data only captures billing codes, EHRs may contain more Diagnosis codes related to the encounter. How to solve the  discrepancies for data submissions?  Should only billing codes are submitted or submitted all EncDx available in EHR and assigning missing POA with W or other value when EncDx is missing from the billing codes? An appropriate guidance would be helpful.

**Comments:**

- **anne_smith (2026-04-10):** No real best practice or way to do this
  yet. Short term: add general guidance and potentially questions we
  could ask implementers; we need to know if there is a lot of
  variation in the industry, and to give guidance to measure developers
  on how they should approach this (e.g., does the measure require
  confirmation from the clinical source?). Consider asking a hospital
  association whether they have quantitative data on this, covering
  both inpatient and potentially outpatient. Target: have questions
  ready for the September Connectathon. POA is in the NHSN priority
  measures, so they should be involved — Anne to reach out to the NHSN
  contact to see if they have thought about this.

## Related Jira Tickets

### [FHIR-50824: Encounter diagnosis use value set is missing a lot of things](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50824)

Patient-Administration change request (Triaged, Highest, Unresolved)
opened by Lloyd McKenzie that flags gaps in the FHIR Core
`Encounter.diagnosis.use` value set. This is relevant to FHIR-56027
because a coherent submission pattern for "EHR-only" encounter
diagnoses largely depends on having a diagnosis-use value that
distinguishes billing-driven vs. clinically-observed-but-not-billed
diagnoses; without it, receivers cannot tell whether an EHR-only
diagnosis should carry a surrogate POA at all.

*Users:* Lloyd McKenzie (reporter), unassigned.

### [FHIR-50827: Encounter.diagnosis.use binding VS is not helpful for differentiating several Condition roles](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50827)

Patient-Administration change request (Triaged, Highest, Persuasive with
Modification) reported by Thomas Debertshäuser. It overlaps FHIR-50824
and directly affects FHIR-56027: if `Encounter.diagnosis.use` is
refined to separate admitting / working / discharge / billing roles,
then CQI can define the submission rule in terms of those codes rather
than having to invent a surrogate POA value for EHR-only diagnoses.

*Users:* Thomas Debertshäuser (reporter), unassigned.

## Related Zulip Discussions

### [implementers > Diagnosis Present on Admission not used?](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/implementers/topic/Diagnosis%20Present%20on%20Admission%20not%20used%3F/near/517558899)

Lloyd McKenzie opened the thread (May 2025) after noticing that QI-Core
still defined a `diagnosisPresentOnAdmission` extension but no profile
referenced it. Bryn Rhodes confirmed that the extension was
intentionally dropped in QI-Core v6.0.0 because POA, principal
diagnosis, and principal procedure are now derived from Claim data,
pointing at the CQL Formatting & Usage Wiki authoring patterns page as
the normative guidance; the extension is gone entirely in QI-Core
v7.0.0. Cooper Thompson (Epic) described how Epic tracks POA on the
Condition (encounter-diagnosis) and then copies it onto the Account
that flows to Claim, noted that PoA is often added post-hoc by coders
during chart review, and initially framed PoA as predominantly a
billing concern — later (Sep 2025) he issued a correction that PoA has
clinical uses too and that Epic is considering whether a new value-set
code or extension/flag is needed, to be handled via FHIR-50827 and
FHIR-50824. Lloyd argued that the clinical use case ("was this rash
present on admission?") may already be served by
`Condition.onsetDateTime`; Brian Postlethwaite suggested
`Encounter.diagnosis.use` is the right slot. No firm consensus was
reached; the thread explicitly notes the value set needs work and that
a CQI-specific Zulip stream would be created.

*Participants:* Lloyd McKenzie, Bryn Rhodes, Cooper Thompson, Brian
Postlethwaite.

### [implementers > eCQM Data Retrieve Present on Admission Indicator](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/implementers/topic/eCQM%20Data%20Retrieve%20Present%20on%20Admission%20Indicator/near/340381154)

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt FHIR-56027 as a **QI-Core guidance change** (non-normative
narrative; no profile/extension changes). Add a new subsection to the
QI-Core Authoring Patterns / "Conditions Present on Admission and
Principal Diagnoses" guidance (currently at
`cqframework/CQL-Formatting-and-Usage-Wiki` and mirrored in the QI-Core
IG authoring narrative) that explicitly states:

1. For quality measurement, the **authoritative set of encounter
   diagnoses with POA** is the set sourced from `Claim` / `ExplanationOfBenefit`
   per the v6.0.0 pattern — i.e., only the billing-coded diagnoses.
2. EHR-only encounter diagnoses (Condition resources referencing the
   Encounter that do not appear in the Claim) **SHOULD still be made
   available** to measure implementers as separate QICore-Condition
   instances, but **SHALL NOT** be assigned a synthetic POA value;
   their POA is simply absent.
3. When a measure needs to reason about "present on admission" for an
   EHR-only diagnosis, implementers should use `Condition.onsetDateTime`
   compared against `Encounter.period.start` rather than inventing a
   surrogate POA code.
4. Ship the guidance as FAQ-style narrative in US QI Core, and publish
   the corresponding implementer questions (per Anne's 4/10 comment)
   for the September Connectathon, coordinated with NHSN.

#### Justification

This is the interpretation most consistent with the v6.0.0 decision
already captured by Bryn Rhodes in the Authoring Patterns wiki: POA is
a Claim-sourced concept, full stop. Assigning a manufactured POA (e.g.,
"W") to EHR-only diagnoses would silently inflate the POA=W bucket with
data that was never clinically adjudicated for POA and would make
measure denominators/numerators diverge between sites depending on
coding workflow timing (Cooper Thompson noted coders often add POA
post-discharge). Using `Condition.onsetDateTime` for the clinical
"was-it-there-on-admission?" question is exactly the path Lloyd
McKenzie and Cooper Thompson converged on in the May 2025 Zulip
thread, and it does not require any spec change or any coordination
with the two open PA tickets (FHIR-50824/50827). The guidance also
aligns with Anne Smith's short-term plan on the ticket.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Instead of ruling EHR-only diagnoses out of the POA-bearing set,
define a **submission convention** in US QI Core that carries all
EHR-recorded encounter diagnoses **and** an explicit
"POA-not-determined" signal for the ones missing from Claims. Concretely:

- Keep the v6.0.0 position that the POA *code* comes from Claims.
- Add a QI-Core narrative rule that, when a submitter is materialising
  the joined EHR+Claim view for a measure, every encounter-diagnosis
  Condition must be present, and EHR-only ones carry either
  `Encounter.diagnosis.use` = "billing-not-applicable" (pending the
  FHIR-50827 value-set refresh) **or**, transitionally, POA = "W"
  (Clinically undetermined) with a data-absent-reason marker so
  downstream consumers do not confuse it with a clinician-asserted "W".
- Make the convention conditional on FHIR-50824 / FHIR-50827 landing,
  so the "use" axis is the long-term home and the surrogate POA is an
  interim bridge only.

#### Justification

Disposition A assumes measure developers are happy losing visibility
into EHR-only diagnoses; several Zulip participants (notably Cooper
Thompson in his Sep 2025 correction, and Floyd Eisenberg in 2023) have
pushed back that POA / diagnosis-present has clinical, not just
billing, utility. Disposition B preserves that signal and gives NHSN
and other measure developers a single joined resource set to query
against, at the cost of inventing a convention that is explicit about
the data-source provenance. Its main trade-off is tight coupling to
the Patient-Administration tickets: if FHIR-50827's
"Persuasive with Modification" resolution does not actually ship a
useful "billing-not-applicable" concept, QI-Core is left carrying a
transitional POA="W" convention indefinitely — a real risk given the
value-set debate is still unresolved. It also contradicts Cooper
Thompson's observation that POA is frequently coder-assigned *after*
the encounter, which means any real-time EHR feed will have
legitimately-absent POA that a surrogate would mask.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-56027 with a **"Not Persuasive — implementer question,
already addressed by existing guidance"** disposition. Point the
reporter at:

- The QI-Core v6.0.0 Authoring Patterns page (CQL Formatting & Usage
  Wiki) that already states Claims is the POA source.
- The Patient-Administration tickets FHIR-50824 and FHIR-50827, which
  are the correct venue for evolving `Encounter.diagnosis.use` so that
  EHR-only diagnoses have a proper non-POA home.
- Refer the cross-program question (how NHSN-style measures should
  handle the discrepancy) to the measure developers and to the
  NHSN/CQI liaison Anne Smith is establishing.

No narrative is added to US QI Core beyond what already exists.

#### Justification

This ticket is, strictly read, a request for implementer guidance — it
does not propose a concrete spec change — and the CQI co-chair's
comment on the ticket effectively says "there is no industry best
practice yet; we need to collect information first." Issuing guidance
before the September Connectathon survey results and before FHIR-50827
lands would risk QI-Core publishing advice that has to be reversed.
Declining (with clear pointers) is defensible on the grounds that
(a) normative guidance already exists and points to Claims, and
(b) a precipitate ruling would pre-empt the PA work on
`Encounter.diagnosis.use`. The cost is leaving submitters without an
authoritative CQI position for another ballot cycle, which is exactly
what the reporter is trying to avoid.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A is the cleanest match to what QI-Core v6.0.0 already
decided, it is the one position that can be published as narrative
guidance *today* without waiting on FHIR-50824 or FHIR-50827, and it
directly incorporates Anne Smith's short-term plan (guidance +
Connectathon questions + NHSN engagement) without pre-committing
QI-Core to a synthetic POA value that would be hard to retract.
Disposition B is attractive in principle but is effectively blocked on
the PA value-set work and risks normalising a surrogate POA="W" that
Cooper Thompson's workflow evidence suggests would be misleading.
Disposition C under-serves the reporter and the measure-developer
community — there is a real, recurring question here (the 2023 Floyd
Eisenberg thread shows it has been open for three years), and CQI is
the right workgroup to answer it. The recommended path is: ship the
Disposition A narrative in the next QI-Core update, flag the
EHR-only-diagnosis behaviour as a Connectathon track item, and keep
FHIR-56027 open as the tracking issue until the Connectathon data and
PA ticket outcomes tell us whether the Disposition B surrogate
convention is still needed.
