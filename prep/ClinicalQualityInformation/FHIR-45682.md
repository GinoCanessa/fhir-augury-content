## Summary

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat FHIR-45682 as an actionable change request and revise the `short`
strings on `Measure.relatedArtifact:predecessor` and
`Measure.relatedArtifact:citation` in the CQFM Publishable Measure profile
(section 17.53.1.1 of the US FHIR Guidance - Quality Reporting IG) to be
more descriptive — for example, making explicit that `:predecessor` points
to the prior version or derivation source of this measure, and that
`:citation` points to the bibliographic / normative citation(s) supporting
the measure logic.

#### Justification

The literal request is reasonable on its own merits: slice-level `short`
descriptions are the first thing implementers see in published
StructureDefinitions and IG narrative, and vague text there is a genuine
documentation defect. Accepting the request keeps CQFM aligned with the
broader FHIR convention that each slice carry a purpose-specific `short`.

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-45682 as a duplicate of [FHIR-45681](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-45681)
("Persuasive with Modification" is not appropriate; "Not Persuasive –
duplicate" or the workgroup's local "duplicate" resolution is). Redirect
all substantive discussion and any spec changes to FHIR-45681, linking the
two tickets so the audit trail is preserved, and apply the actual
`short`-description rewording there.

#### Justification

The reporter himself asked within three minutes of filing that this ticket
be closed as an unintentional duplicate, and the ticket's title carries the
`CLONE -` prefix. FHIR-45681 holds the exact same text and scope and is
already the natural home for the work. Closing 45682 as a duplicate avoids
two parallel dispositions on identical wording, prevents divergent
resolutions, and honors the reporter's stated intent. This is consistent
with standard HL7 WG practice for accidental clones.

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve FHIR-45682 as "Not Persuasive" without change on the narrow
grounds that it is an accidental duplicate with no independent content,
and take no action on the underlying `short`-description concern under
this ticket key.

#### Justification

This option is defensible only as a housekeeping move: the ticket adds no
information beyond FHIR-45681, and the reporter has asked for it to be
closed. It is weaker than Disposition B because "Not Persuasive" without
the explicit "duplicate" framing can read as rejecting the substantive
request, which is misleading given that the same request is still live on
FHIR-45681.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the right answer. The reporter expressly asked for this
ticket to be closed as an unintentional duplicate, the title is already
flagged `CLONE -`, and the identical-wording twin FHIR-45681 exists and
is still open for the real work. Accepting the change here (Disposition
A) would either duplicate effort with FHIR-45681 or risk the two tickets
being resolved inconsistently. A bare "decline" (Disposition C) would
obscure the fact that the underlying `short`-description concern is
legitimate and still under active consideration on FHIR-45681. Closing
as a duplicate with an explicit link to FHIR-45681 preserves the audit
trail, honors the reporter's request, and leaves the substantive CQFM
Publishable Measure `relatedArtifact` slice wording discussion exactly
where it belongs.
