## Summary

FHIR-42405 is a global comment on the US Da Vinci Value-Based Performance
Reporting (VBPR) IG raised against all of the IG's reports. The reporter
asks for two transparency capabilities to help providers confirm the
accuracy of payer-produced performance reports:

1. **Patient-level drill-down** — given a `MeasureReport`, a provider
   should be able to see which patients (members) were included in the
   measure population(s).
2. **Methodology link** — the report should expose a link the provider
   can click to reach the payer's documented methodology describing how
   the measure was calculated.

The ticket was originally triaged in 2023 as "Considered for Future Use"
(out of scope for STU1, with a 13-2-0 vote), then reopened by Lynn
Laakso on 2024-06-17 for post-STU1 work. A 2025-10-16 comment from
Yan Heras notes that the related ticket
[FHIR-52950](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-52950) (Persuasive,
Published) has already changed the Value-Based Performance
`MeasureReport.subject` from `Organization` to the ATR Group profile,
which "would potentially allow provider to drill down to see the list
of members/patients that are included for the report." That change
partially — but not fully — addresses request (1), and does not address
request (2) at all.

There are no formal cross-references recorded for this ticket; the link
to FHIR-52950 was discovered via the most recent comment.

## Related Jira Tickets

### [FHIR-52950: Use ATR Group as subject](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-52950)

Cited in the most recent comment on FHIR-42405. This ticket (Highest /
Persuasive / Published, resolved 2025-09-26) changes the Value-Based
Performance `MeasureReport.subject` from `Organization` to the ATR Group
profile (removing the `orgSubject` extension) and adds US Core
Practitioner and PractitionerRole as allowed `MeasureReport.reporter`
choices alongside US Core Organization. Because the report subject is
now an ATR Group whose members are the attributed patients, this
provides a structural path for a consumer to enumerate the patients
covered by a report — addressing part (1) of FHIR-42405's request.
It does **not** address part (2) (methodology link).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat FHIR-42405 as two independent STU2 enhancements to the Value-Based
Performance MeasureReport profile in the US Da Vinci VBPR IG:

1. **Patient drill-down:** Document the conformance expectation that the
   payer-produced `MeasureReport` SHALL be capable of conveying the
   patients included in each population. Concretely, in addition to the
   ATR Group subject already adopted via FHIR-52950, require population
   stratifier-level `MeasureReport.group.population.subjectResults` (a
   `Reference(List)`) that resolves to a `List` of patient
   references, OR populate `MeasureReport.evaluatedResource` with the
   patient/member references that drove each population. Add a narrative
   page describing how a provider tool is expected to render the
   patient list.
2. **Methodology link:** Add a constraint or guidance that the `Measure`
   referenced by `MeasureReport.measure` SHALL include a
   `Measure.relatedArtifact` of `type = documentation` whose `url`
   points to the payer's published methodology for that measure, and
   that consuming applications SHOULD surface this link to providers
   when displaying the report.

#### Justification

Both pieces fulfil the stated provider need (verify accuracy, audit the
calculation) using existing FHIR machinery rather than new extensions:
`MeasureReport.group.population.subjectResults` and
`MeasureReport.evaluatedResource` are the standard FHIR mechanisms for
exposing the subjects that contributed to a measure score, and
`Measure.relatedArtifact` is the standard mechanism for attaching
documentation. The 2024-06-17 reopening explicitly anticipated that
this would be addressed in STU2, and FHIR-52950's adoption of ATR Group
as the subject already lays the structural groundwork for the
drill-down by giving every report a navigable membership.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Split FHIR-42405 in two and partially close-as-resolved:

- **Part (1) — drill-down:** Mark as **Persuasive with Modification**
  and resolve by reference to FHIR-52950. Add a single short paragraph
  to the Value-Based Performance MeasureReport profile page explaining
  that, because the subject is now an ATR Group, a consuming
  application can enumerate the included patients via
  `Group.member.entity` on the report's subject. Do not introduce any
  new conformance requirements on `subjectResults` or
  `evaluatedResource`.
- **Part (2) — methodology link:** Spin the methodology-link request
  out into a new ticket and address it via a lightweight extension on
  `MeasureReport` (e.g., `vbpr-methodology-url` of type `url`) rather
  than constraining `Measure.relatedArtifact`. Mark this ticket as
  Persuasive with Modification once that follow-on is filed.

#### Justification

This avoids re-litigating the report subject (already settled by
FHIR-52950) and avoids adding new conformance load on the `Measure`
resource, which is owned by CQI and shared across many measure-based
IGs — a constraint added in VBPR alone would not be enforceable on
upstream `Measure` content from CMS or NCQA. A small VBPR-local
extension for the methodology link keeps the cost of compliance on the
payer producing the report, where it belongs, and is easier to evolve
in future STU revisions than a base-resource constraint.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark FHIR-42405 as **Not Persuasive** on the grounds that (a) the
patient drill-down request has already been addressed structurally by
FHIR-52950's adoption of ATR Group as `MeasureReport.subject`, so no
further normative change is required, and (b) the methodology link is
out of scope for the IG's data exchange contract — how a payer
publishes its methodology and how a provider UI surfaces that link is
an implementation/UX concern, not a FHIR conformance concern.

#### Justification

The IG already permits payers to convey the included population via
the ATR Group subject (post-FHIR-52950) and via the existing
`MeasureReport.evaluatedResource` element; no implementer has reported
that the existing surface area is insufficient. Forcing a methodology
URL into the exchange risks codifying an obligation that payers can
satisfy outside the IG (portal links, contract documents, etc.) and
would be hard to enforce uniformly. The original 2023 triage outcome
("outside the scope of STU1") was the right call; the reopening should
not be read as an obligation to act if no concrete implementer demand
has materialized in the intervening two years.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The drill-down half of the original request is already substantially
addressed by FHIR-52950 (which is Published / Persuasive), so a
verbatim "Accept As Requested" risks redundant or conflicting
constraints on `MeasureReport`. At the same time, fully declining the
ticket ignores the second, distinct ask — a methodology link — which
is a real provider transparency need that has no current home in the
profile. Disposition B captures both realities: it formally closes the
drill-down concern by pointing at the already-shipped FHIR-52950 work
and adds a short explanatory paragraph so future readers understand the
linkage, while spinning the methodology-link ask into its own scoped
follow-up using a VBPR-local extension. This keeps the change surface
small for STU2, avoids touching the cross-IG `Measure` resource, and
gives the workgroup a clean two-sentence record of why FHIR-42405 is
considered handled. The workgroup should confirm with the original
reporter (Erica Martin) that the ATR Group subject path satisfies the
drill-down expectation before final closure.
