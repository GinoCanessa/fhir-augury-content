## Summary

The reporter, Kimberly Figueroa, is raising a terminology / spelling question
against the *US FHIR Guidance — Quality Reporting (FHIR)* implementation guide.
Specifically, in **Figure 1-2** of that guide (the "Consumers" diagram), one of
the consumer roles is labeled "Payers". The reporter asks whether this should
instead be spelled "Payors", and notes that the FHIR **Coverage** resource uses
the element name `payor` — implying the IG should be internally and
cross-resource consistent with that spelling. No cross-referenced Jira
tickets, Zulip discussions, or GitHub items were surfaced for this issue, so the
review is based solely on the ticket text and the well-known FHIR conventions
around "payor" vs. "payer".

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the *US FHIR Guidance — Quality Reporting (FHIR)* IG so that **Figure
1-2** (the "Consumers" diagram) and any associated narrative use the spelling
**"Payor" / "Payors"** instead of "Payer" / "Payers". This is a
narrative/diagram-only change (re-render the figure source and update any
inline references to the role label). Apply the same edit consistently
anywhere else in the same IG where the consumer role is named, so the IG does
not contain both spellings. The change is purely editorial — no resources,
profiles, value sets, or conformance artifacts are altered.

#### Justification

The FHIR Coverage resource uses the element name `payor` (R4 / R4B), and that
spelling has historically been carried into adjacent FHIR text where the
"organization that pays for care" role is named. Aligning the IG figure with
the spelling that already appears in the underlying resource element name
removes a small but real source of confusion for readers who jump between the
IG narrative and the Coverage resource. Because this is a non-normative
diagram/text edit, there is no implementer impact and no breaking-change risk.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the existing **"Payer / Payers"** spelling in Figure 1-2 and the IG
narrative, and instead add a one-line editorial footnote (or glossary entry)
clarifying that the role labeled "Payer" in this guide corresponds to the
party represented by `Coverage.payor` in the FHIR Coverage resource. Optionally
file an upstream tracker note observing that "payor" in the Coverage element
name is itself a long-standing FHIR spelling carry-over and that mainstream US
healthcare narrative (CMS, ONC, payer organizations themselves) overwhelmingly
uses "payer" in plain English text.

#### Justification

In modern US healthcare communications, "**payer**" is the dominant English
spelling — it is the form used by CMS, ONC, AHIP, and the major commercial
plans in their own publications. The "payor" spelling persists primarily in
legacy contract / legal text and in a handful of FHIR element names that are
locked in for backward compatibility. Narrative text aimed at a broad CQI
audience arguably reads more naturally as "payer," and a footnote bridging the
narrative term to the `Coverage.payor` element preserves cross-reference
clarity without re-rendering the figure or introducing a spelling that some
readers will perceive as archaic. Trade-off vs. Disposition A: less internal
spelling consistency with `Coverage.payor`, but better alignment with how the
target US audience actually writes the word today.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change as **editorial-only with no functional impact**, and close
as *Persuasive with Mod* / *Not Persuasive — Editorial* per the work group's
preference. No change to the figure or narrative; the answer to the reporter's
literal question is "yes, this role corresponds to `Coverage.payor`, and the
spelling difference is purely stylistic."

#### Justification

Both "payer" and "payor" are accepted English spellings for the same role, and
the FHIR ecosystem already contains both forms (Coverage.payor element vs.
"payer" in many narrative contexts and in resources such as
ExplanationOfBenefit text). Re-rendering Figure 1-2 and chasing every other
narrative occurrence in the IG to enforce one spelling is low-value editorial
churn that does not affect interoperability, validation, or implementer
behavior. Declining keeps the work group's editorial budget focused on
substantive quality-reporting issues.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter's underlying observation is correct and easy to act on: the IG
figure says "Payers," the closely-related FHIR resource element is spelled
`Coverage.payor`, and a reader who notices the mismatch has to stop and ask
whether the two refer to the same role — which is exactly what happened here.
Disposition A removes that friction at trivial cost (a figure re-render plus
find-and-replace in the surrounding narrative of a single IG), with zero
implementer or conformance impact. Disposition B is defensible on
plain-English grounds, but it leaves the spelling mismatch in place and asks
every future reader to consult a footnote. Disposition C effectively tells the
reporter "you're right, but we won't fix it," which is a poor outcome for an
unambiguous editorial nit raised against a non-normative figure. CQI should
take Disposition A and apply the spelling change consistently across the
*Quality Reporting* IG.
