## Summary

The reporter (Aaron Nusstein) asks the Clinical Quality Information work
group to revise the short descriptions on the `predecessor` and `citation`
slices of `Measure.relatedArtifact` in the CQFM Publishable Measure
profile (Quality Measures IG, section 17.53.1.1). The current short
descriptions are described as somewhat intuitive but not clear or
descriptive of what implementers are expected to populate, and the
reporter suggests reworking them to be more helpful to the reader. The
ticket targets only the human-readable short text on the existing slices;
it does not propose changes to cardinality, types, bindings, or the
slicing structure itself. No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip
threads, or GitHub items were found, so the request stands on its own.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Revise the `short` text on the two slices of `Measure.relatedArtifact`
in the CQFM Publishable Measure profile so they describe what the slice
is for in implementer terms, rather than restating the slice name.
Concretely, replace the existing short text on:

- `Measure.relatedArtifact:predecessor.short` — set to something along
  the lines of *"Prior version of this measure that this measure
  supersedes"* (i.e., a `RelatedArtifact` of type `predecessor` pointing
  at the earlier canonical of the same measure).
- `Measure.relatedArtifact:citation.short` — set to something along the
  lines of *"Bibliographic citation for evidence supporting this
  measure"* (i.e., a `RelatedArtifact` of type `citation` referencing
  source literature, guideline, or evidence).

This is a documentation-only change to the CQFM Publishable Measure
StructureDefinition; element types, cardinalities, and the
`RelatedArtifact.type` slicing discriminator are unchanged. The narrative
in section 17.53.1.1 should be re-rendered to pick up the new short
descriptions.

#### Justification

The reporter explicitly identifies the short descriptions as the source
of confusion, and short text is the field that drives most rendered
tables and tool tips that implementers see first. Tightening the wording
without touching structure is a low-risk, backward-compatible change that
matches how other CQFM slices (e.g., `:component`, `:supplementalData`)
are documented elsewhere in the IG, and it aligns with FHIR's general
guidance that `short` should give a one-line, human-meaningful summary
distinct from `definition`.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Leave the `short` text alone (or make only a minimal tweak) and instead
strengthen the per-slice `definition`, `comment`, and the surrounding
narrative paragraph in section 17.53.1.1. Add a short usage example for
each slice — a `predecessor` reference pointing at a prior canonical of
the same measure, and a `citation` reference pointing at the underlying
guideline or evidence — so the reader sees a concrete shape rather than
a re-worded one-liner.

#### Justification

The underlying complaint is reader comprehension, not a defect in the
short text per se. Many implementers consume the IG narrative and
examples rather than reading slice metadata in isolation, so investing
in `definition` + comment + worked example often does more to remove
ambiguity than rewording `short`. This also avoids a per-slice
documentation churn cycle every time a reviewer feels the short text
could be phrased better, and it keeps `short` close to the base
`RelatedArtifact` short for searchability.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change as Not Persuasive. The current short descriptions on
`Measure.relatedArtifact:predecessor` and
`Measure.relatedArtifact:citation` re-use the well-understood
`RelatedArtifact` slice semantics already defined in the base
specification, which the reporter themselves describes as "somewhat
intuitive". No misuse, interoperability problem, or implementer error
has been documented, and no alternative wording has been proposed in the
ticket. Recommend the reporter file a follow-up with concrete suggested
text if they want to pursue the change.

#### Justification

CQI typically prioritises behavioural and conformance changes over
wording adjustments to short descriptions, especially when the existing
text is acknowledged to be intuitive. Without a proposed replacement
string or a documented example of an implementer being misled, the
disposition risks bike-shedding a doc field across an entire publishable
measure profile.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The change is small, well-scoped, and addresses a real readability gap
that the reporter has identified in a prominent profile (CQFM Publishable
Measure). It is documentation-only, backward-compatible, and does not
touch slice structure, types, or the `RelatedArtifact.type` discriminator,
so the implementer impact is limited to refreshed narrative. Disposition
B's deeper investment in `definition`/comment/examples is worthwhile but
is additive — it doesn't preclude tightening `short`, and ideally CQI
should do both. Disposition C undersells a real complaint from a named
reporter at essentially zero risk. The work group should ask the reporter
(or the disposition author) to propose the exact replacement strings as
part of accepting, so the ballot record contains the final wording.
