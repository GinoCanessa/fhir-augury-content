## Summary

FHIR-48663 raises that the AU eRequesting Implementation Guide (Release 1)
does not currently define what *Must Support* means when an AU eRequesting
profile references an AU Core profile. Because eRequesting introduces its
own actors (Placer, Filler, Server, Patient Access) that differ from the
AU Core actors, the AU Core obligations attached to *Must Support* do not
flow through automatically, leaving implementer obligations on referenced
elements ambiguous. The reporter (Stephanie Ong) proposes that the IG add
narrative text defining the default meaning of *Must Support* on
referenced AU Core profiles **per eRequesting actor**, as a Release 1
fix. The background note records that this issue was raised in the AU
eRequesting co-chairs/facilitators meeting on 10 October 2025 in
response to [FHIR-47008](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-47008)
(Persuasive / Applied — making `ServiceRequest.encounter` mandatory),
where the cross-actor consequences of *Must Support* surfaced. The
ticket also flags a roadmap intent to move from narrative obligations to
explicit Obligation-only profiles in a future release.

**Description:**

> *Issue*:
>
> eRequesting does not inherit AU Core Obligations as the actors are different.
>
> In eRequesting, the meaning of *Must Support* for referenced AU Core
> profiles has not yet been defined. Without a clear definition, the
> obligations for systems to meaningfully support these elements are
> ambiguous, affecting implementation.
>
> *Proposal*:
>
> It is proposed to define in narrative, the default meaning of *Must
> Support* for referenced AU Core profiles, for each eRequesting actor.

> *Background*:
>
> This issue arose from discussions in the AU eRequesting

> raise this issue with the proposed solution of narrative definitions
> for Release 1 to clarify *Must Support* obligations. It was also
> discussed that the roadmap for eRequesting IG may include progressing
> the narrative Obligations to explicit Obligation-only profiles.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a normative narrative section to the AU eRequesting IG (Release 1)
titled something like *"Must Support for referenced AU Core profiles"*
that, for each eRequesting actor (Placer, Filler, Server, Patient
Access), states the default meaning of *Must Support* on any element
that AU eRequesting profiles inherit by reference from an AU Core
profile. Concretely:

- Placer: SHALL populate the referenced AU Core element when known
  (`SHALL:populate-if-known`).
- Filler: SHALL handle the referenced element without error and process
  it where workflow-relevant (`SHALL:handle`).
- Server: SHALL handle on receipt and SHALL be able to populate on
  return (`SHALL:handle`, `SHALL:able-to-populate`).
- Patient Access: SHALL not error on the referenced element
  (`SHALL:no-error`).

These mirror the obligation pattern already accepted in FHIR-46939
(`ServiceRequest.priority`) and would be placed in a new
"Conformance / Must Support" page (or section of the existing
conformance page) of the eRequesting IG, with a cross-reference from
each profile that references AU Core. No StructureDefinitions change in
Release 1; the change is narrative only.

#### Justification

This is the lowest-risk fix for Release 1. It (a) directly answers the
ambiguity FHIR-48663 raises, (b) reuses the per-actor obligation pattern
the workgroup has already adopted in FHIR-46939, and (c) avoids touching
StructureDefinitions on the eve of Release 1, which keeps the change
surface small and leaves the door open to the roadmap item of explicit
Obligation-only profiles in a later release. The Zulip discussion of
ServiceRequest.priority (Frankel, Bond, Wilson) shows the TDG already
reasons in per-actor terms, so codifying that as the default is a
faithful continuation of agreed direction.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Skip the narrative default and instead introduce **explicit
Obligation-only profiles** layered over the referenced AU Core profiles
(one per eRequesting actor, e.g., `au-erequesting-placer-encounter`,
`au-erequesting-filler-encounter`), each carrying actor-specific
`obligation` extensions on the inherited Must Support elements. The AU
eRequesting profiles would reference these actor-scoped obligation
profiles instead of AU Core directly.

#### Justification

This is the roadmap target the ticket itself anticipates ("progressing
the narrative Obligations to explicit Obligation only profiles"). Doing
it now is more rigorous: tooling (validators, IG publisher actor pages,
Forge/Trifolia) can mechanically surface obligations per actor, and
implementers don't have to read narrative to determine conformance.
The trade-off is significantly more authoring and review work, possible
churn in the AU Core ↔ AU eRequesting relationship, and a much larger
test surface immediately before Release 1 — risks the TDG explicitly
chose to defer in the 10 October 2025 meeting.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline as out of scope for Release 1: rely on the existing AU Core
*Must Support* definitions on each referenced element and on the
forthcoming roadmap of explicit Obligation profiles to address actor
specificity in a future release.

#### Justification

Defensible only on the grounds that AU Core itself already defines
*Must Support* and that introducing actor-scoped narrative could
contradict or shadow AU Core if not carefully maintained. However, the
ticket's premise — that AU Core's actors are different from
eRequesting's actors and therefore the obligations do not flow — is
correct, and leaving this undefined in Release 1 leaves implementers
with no authoritative guidance. Decline would not address the
implementability concern raised by Stephanie Ong and acknowledged by
the TDG.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A is the right call for Release 1. The TDG has already
agreed (per the 10 October 2025 co-chairs/facilitators discussion
recorded in the ticket background) that a narrative clarification is
the appropriate Release 1 response, and the Zulip thread on
ServiceRequest.priority shows the per-actor obligation pattern is
already how the workgroup reasons about *Must Support* in eRequesting.
FHIR-46939 has set the precedent with the exact obligation matrix
proposed here, so generalising it as the default for *Must Support* on
referenced AU Core elements is consistent and low-risk. Disposition B
is the right end-state and should be tracked as a roadmap item for the
next release, but pulling it into Release 1 carries authoring,
validation, and review risk that the TDG has already chosen to avoid.
Disposition C leaves implementers without authoritative guidance and
fails to address the underlying concern.
