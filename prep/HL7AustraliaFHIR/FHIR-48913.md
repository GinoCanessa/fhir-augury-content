## Summary

The reporter (Marika Carr) asks the HL7 Australia FHIR Work Group to make two
related changes to AU Base so that implementers can use **unpadded** PBS and
MBS item numbers without being forced to refer to the Services Australia
"modified" code systems:

1. **PBS** — AU Base's `CodeSystem-pbs-item-external` currently carries an
   `identifier` that ties it to `urn:oid:1.2.36.1.2001.1005.22`, the
   Services-Australia–modified PBS code system whose codes are zero-padded
   to a fixed length. Implementers consuming raw data from the PBS source
   cannot conform without modifying their data. The request is to **detach
   that OID** from AU Base's PBS CodeSystem and to clarify that either
   padded or unpadded codes may be used.
2. **MBS** — AU Base publishes no MBS CodeSystem/ValueSet at all. The only
   widely referenced ValueSet (used by ADHA's `dh-explanationofbenefit-medicare-mbs-1`
   profile) points at the equivalent padded OID `urn:oid:1.2.36.1.2001.1005.21`.
   The request is for HL7 AU to **publish an empty MBS CodeSystem and
   ValueSet** that does *not* reference the padded system, with the same
   "padded or unpadded both acceptable" clarification.

Two related tickets bound the scope:

- **[FHIR-49933](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-49933)** — *Remove OID from
  PBS Item Codes CodeSystem*: the FHIRWG **already accepted (Persuasive,
  Applied)** the PBS half of this request at the 2025-01-29 meeting,
  removing the OID from `CodeSystem.identifier` on `pbs-item-external`. Per
  the comment thread on FHIR-48913, that leaves only the **MBS half** of
  this ticket open for disposition.
- **[FHIR-47173](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-47173)** — *Metadata-only
  CodeSystems should be retired and published in THO*: a broader policy
  ticket that was **Deferred** ("Considered for Future Use") to a 2025
  HL7 AU project. Whatever AU Base does for MBS here will need to be
  consistent with that future policy direction (THO publication of
  metadata-only external CodeSystems, with possible use of `NamingSystem`
  instead).

## Details

**Description:**

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

For the still-open MBS half of FHIR-48913 (the PBS half is already
**Applied** via FHIR-49933):

1. **Publish a new metadata-only `CodeSystem` in AU Base** for unmodified
   MBS item numbers, e.g. `CodeSystem-mbs-item.{json,xml}` under AU
   Base's CodeSystem authoring root, with:
   - `url` = `http://hl7.org.au/fhir/CodeSystem/mbs-item` (mirroring the
     existing `pbs-item-external` naming convention but renamed to drop
     `-external` since the artefact will be PBS-aligned post-FHIR-49933;
     the equivalent PBS artefact may also be renamed for symmetry as a
     follow-up).
   - `content` = `not-present` (metadata only — same pattern as
     `pbs-item-external`).
   - **No `CodeSystem.identifier` element pointing at
     `urn:oid:1.2.36.1.2001.1005.21`** — that OID identifies the
     Services-Australia-modified system, which is a different
     CodeSystem.
   - `description` text that explicitly states **either padded or
     unpadded MBS item numbers may be used** under this CodeSystem and
     that this CodeSystem is *not* the same as the
     Services-Australia-modified variant identified by
     `urn:oid:1.2.36.1.2001.1005.21`.
2. **Publish a matching `ValueSet`** (e.g. `ValueSet-mbs-item`) that
   `compose.include`s the new CodeSystem `url`, mirroring the structure
   of the existing PBS ValueSet.
3. **Update the AU Base §9.2 external CodeSystems table** to list the
   new MBS CodeSystem alongside PBS, and add the same padded/unpadded
   clarification narrative for both PBS and MBS in the relevant
   prose page.
4. Optionally, register a note in the IG that points implementers who
   need the *modified* representation toward the existing ADHA OIDs and
   `https://healthterminologies.gov.au/fhir/ValueSet/australian-dhs-modifications-pbs-mbs-dva-item-1`.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter asked for and what Brett Esler endorsed
in Zulip on 2024-11-07; it mirrors the structure already chosen for PBS
(`CodeSystem-pbs-item-external` plus its ValueSet), so it carries no
modelling novelty. The PBS half of the ticket has already been resolved
along the same lines (FHIR-49933, **Persuasive / Applied**) — accepting
the symmetrical MBS half preserves consistency. Andy Bond confirmed in
Zulip that implementers are not padding in practice; Michael Lawley
confirmed that the padded OIDs are technically a different code system
and should not be conflated with raw MBS. Publishing an empty,
unambiguous AU Base CodeSystem unblocks Marika Carr's use case (and
others' similar use cases) without committing HL7 AU to maintain
copyright-restricted code content.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — `NamingSystem` registration only, defer publishable artefacts

#### Proposal

Instead of publishing a new MBS `CodeSystem` and `ValueSet` in AU Base,
register the unmodified MBS code system identity through a
**`NamingSystem`** entry in AU Base (with `kind = codesystem`,
`uniqueId` of type `oid`/`uri` for both the canonical URL and any
applicable OIDs, and `usage` text that captures the padded/unpadded
guidance). Simultaneously, for the PBS side already covered by
FHIR-49933, expose the same identity model so PBS and MBS are described
the same way. Defer the publishable empty `CodeSystem` and `ValueSet`
until the 2025 HL7 AU policy project (FHIR-47173, FHIR-47166,
FHIR-47149, FHIR-47148, FHIR-47174) has decided whether such metadata-only
records belong in AU Base or in THO.

This is the path Brett Esler hinted at in the FHIR-47173 thread:
"this might be better achieved by a recognised NamingSystem methodology".

#### Justification

The reporter's underlying need is *identity* — a stable URI that
implementers can put in `Coding.system` to mean "unmodified MBS item
number" — not a publishable code list (which AU Base cannot ship anyway
due to copyright). `NamingSystem` is the FHIR-native mechanism for that
identity registration and avoids the awkwardness of publishing a
`CodeSystem` whose `content` is `not-present`. It also aligns AU Base
with the direction of FHIR-47173 (retire metadata-only CodeSystems in
favour of THO records or NamingSystems), reducing the chance that the
artefacts published now have to be retired again in a year.

Trade-offs vs. Disposition A: implementer ergonomics suffer slightly —
existing tooling (validators, IG publishers, terminology browsers) is
better tuned to `CodeSystem`/`ValueSet` than to `NamingSystem`, and
profiles that want to bind to "MBS items" still need a `ValueSet`. This
disposition therefore likely needs the placeholder ValueSet from
Disposition A even if the CodeSystem is dropped.

---

### Disposition C: Decline — defer all action to the 2025 AU FHIR policy project

#### Proposal

Decline FHIR-48913 in its remaining (MBS) scope on the grounds that:

- The PBS half is already addressed by FHIR-49933 (no further action
  here).
- The MBS half overlaps materially with the deferred policy ticket
  FHIR-47173, which has been routed to a dedicated 2025 HL7 AU project
  covering FHIR-47166, -47149, -47148, -47174. Acting now risks
  publishing artefacts that will need to be retired or moved to THO once
  that project lands.

Mark FHIR-48913 as **Persuasive with Modification** for the PBS portion
(already done via FHIR-49933) and **Considered for Future Use** for the
MBS portion, with a forward-link to the 2025 policy project.

#### Justification

There is precedent: FHIR-47173, raised against the same artefact family
by a different reporter, was resolved this way. Spinning up a one-off
MBS CodeSystem now will lock AU Base into a publication pattern that the
broader policy work may explicitly disallow. Implementers asking for an
identity *today* can use the org-private placeholder pattern that
Michael Lawley recommended in Zulip
(`https://<your-domain>/CodeSystem/mbs`) until the policy project's
output is published — this is what at least one participant (Marika
Carr's organisation) was already prepared to do.

The downsides are real: Marika Carr and others continue to lack an
authoritative AU canonical URI for unmodified MBS items, and the
inconsistency that arose between PBS (acted upon) and MBS (deferred) is
itself a small interoperability problem.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition: A (Accept As Requested).**

The PBS half of this ticket has already been accepted and applied via
FHIR-49933 with the same shape proposed here, so the work group has
effectively pre-committed to the pattern. Accepting the MBS half closes
an asymmetry (PBS now has a clean CodeSystem; MBS has nothing in AU
Base) that would otherwise persist for a release cycle while the
deferred policy work in FHIR-47173 plays out. The Zulip evidence is
clear that (a) implementers are not padding, (b) the padded OIDs are a
distinct code system that should not be conflated, and (c) AU FHIRWG —
through Brett Esler — already signalled willingness to publish a
placeholder MBS CodeSystem with no padding requirement. The artefact is
metadata-only with no copyright exposure, so the cost to HL7 AU is
small.

The Justification on Disposition A should explicitly note that if the
2025 policy project (FHIR-47173 et al.) later decides metadata-only
CodeSystems should move to THO, the new MBS CodeSystem will move with
the existing PBS one — i.e., this disposition does not pre-judge that
policy outcome, it simply applies the *current* AU Base pattern
consistently. Disposition B (NamingSystem) is the second-best option
and could be folded into Disposition A as a complementary registration
if the work group prefers a belt-and-braces approach. Disposition C
should be rejected because it leaves a known-asymmetric AU Base in
place for an unbounded period.
