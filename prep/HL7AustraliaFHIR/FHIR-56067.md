## Summary

The reporter asks the HL7 Australia FHIR work group to re-base the **AU
Patient Summary Composition** profile so that it derives from the
cross-paradigm **FHIR Clinical Document Composition Profile** (the
`http://hl7.org/fhir/StructureDefinition/clinicaldocument` profile that
serves as the canonical FHIR representation of clinical documents and
that is intended as the common starting point for FHIR-based clinical
document specifications, including organisations transitioning from CDA).
The motivation has three strands: (1) increase structural consistency
with other national patient-summary guides that align with the
**International Patient Summary (IPS) Implementation Guide**; (2)
benefit from the implementation experience accumulated in HL7 CDA
clinical-document deployments; and (3) position AU PS for the expected
future direction in which IPS itself derives its Composition profile
from the FHIR Clinical Document profile, so that AU PS does not later
have to be re-parented under churn. No Zulip discussion, GitHub PR/issue
or other Jira ticket is currently cross-referenced to FHIR-56067 in the
Augury index.

## Details

**Description:**

> Please consider deriving the **AU Patient Summary Composition** from
> the **FHIR Clinical Document Composition Profile**.
>
> Using this profile as the base would increase structural consistency
> across national patient summary implementations aligned with the
> **International Patient Summary (IPS) Implementation Guide** while
> leveraging decades of implementation experience from the widely
> deployed **HL7 Clinical Document Architecture (CDA)**. The FHIR
> Clinical Document specification provides a canonical representation of
> clinical documents in FHIR and is intended as a common starting point
> for FHIR-based clinical document specifications and for organizations
> transitioning from CDA-based document exchange.
>
> Additionally, many IPS stakeholders intend for future IPS versions to
> derive from the FHIR Clinical Document specification. Aligning AU PS
> with this approach now would position the guide for smoother future
> IPS alignment and improved interoperability across national
> implementations.

## Proposed Dispositions

> the spec level rather than at a specific authoring root + file path.

> should be raised against the AU PS IG repository in the
> `hl7au` GitHub organisation, against the `Composition` StructureDefinition

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Re-parent the **AU Patient Summary Composition** profile so that its
`StructureDefinition.baseDefinition` points to
`http://hl7.org/fhir/StructureDefinition/clinicaldocument` (the FHIR
Clinical Document Composition profile) instead of `Composition` (or
instead of the IPS Composition profile, whichever is the current base in
the AU PS build). Concretely, in the AU PS IG sources:

1. Change `baseDefinition` on the AU PS Composition StructureDefinition
   to the `clinicaldocument` profile canonical.
2. Re-snapshot the profile and reconcile the differential against the
   constraints already inherited from `clinicaldocument` (e.g., on
   `Composition.type`, `subject`, `author`, `attester`, `section`,
   `text`, and the document-Bundle invariants), removing any constraints
   that the parent already imposes more tightly.
3. If AU PS currently derives from the IPS Composition profile, layer
   IPS-derived constraints back in as additional invariants/slices on the
   AU PS Composition (or via an intermediate profile) so no
   IPS-required content is lost during the re-parenting.
4. Update the AU PS document-`Bundle` profile (if any) so that its
   `entry[0].resource` reference still resolves to the new AU PS
   Composition, and verify the document-bundle invariants flow through.
5. Update narrative, conformance prose, and the IG’s “Derived from”
   pages, and add a release-note entry calling out the breaking change
   in profile lineage.

#### Justification

This is the change the ticket literally asks for and is consistent with
HL7's positioning of the FHIR Clinical Document profile as the canonical
parent for FHIR-based clinical-document specifications. Aligning the
parent now — rather than after IPS has re-parented — minimises future
churn and makes AU PS structurally comparable to other national
patient-summary guides that follow the same pattern. It also lets AU PS
inherit the document-Bundle invariants centrally rather than restating
them, which reduces the surface area for AU-specific drift.

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the current `baseDefinition` of the AU PS Composition profile
unchanged for this ballot cycle, but adopt one of the following
intermediate alignments:

- **B1 (preferred alternative):** Keep deriving from the IPS Composition
  profile (preserving the existing IPS-AU alignment story), and add a
  conformance statement plus an automated profile-comparison artifact
  that demonstrates AU PS Composition is *compatible with* the FHIR
  Clinical Document Composition profile — i.e., every constraint the
  Clinical Document profile imposes is satisfied by AU PS, even though
  AU PS does not formally inherit from it. Track the formal re-parenting
  as a follow-up tied to the next IPS major version.
- **B2:** Introduce an **intermediate AU profile** (e.g.,
  `AUClinicalDocumentComposition`) that derives from
  `clinicaldocument`, and have `AUPatientSummaryComposition` derive from
  that. This keeps the ticket's intent (Clinical Document lineage) while
  isolating the re-parenting impact in a single, reusable AU base
  profile that other AU document IGs (discharge summary, referral, etc.)
  can also adopt.

In either variant, document the rationale in the IG and raise an IPS
ticket to coordinate timing with the IPS work group.

#### Justification

Re-parenting a Composition profile is a meaningful breaking change for
existing AU PS implementers: it shifts the inheritance chain, can
invalidate cached snapshots, and risks subtle differences in
slicing/discriminator semantics between the IPS Composition profile and
the Clinical Document profile. B1 captures the substantive interop
benefit (demonstrable Clinical Document conformance) without the
breaking change, and lets AU and IPS re-parent in lockstep rather than
AU getting ahead of IPS. B2 captures the lineage benefit the ticket asks
for but contains the blast radius and creates a reusable AU clinical
document base for other AU document IGs, which is consistent with how
several jurisdictions are factoring their CDA-to-FHIR transitions.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request to change `baseDefinition` on the AU PS Composition
profile. Resolution category: **Persuasive with Modifier — Not
Persuasive in this cycle.** Rationale: the FHIR Clinical Document
Composition profile and the IPS Composition profile both ultimately
constrain the same `Composition` resource, and AU PS already meets the
substantive document-Bundle requirements; the marginal interop gain
does not justify a breaking change to the profile lineage at this stage,
particularly while IPS itself has not yet re-parented onto the FHIR
Clinical Document profile. Recommend revisiting once IPS publishes an
IPS version that derives from `clinicaldocument`.

#### Justification

`baseDefinition` is part of a profile's identity for tooling: changing
it changes snapshot generation, slicing inheritance, and validator
behaviour for every implementer that has built AU PS instances or
downstream profiles. Until IPS itself moves, doing so unilaterally in
AU PS risks creating a transient three-way mismatch (AU PS →
ClinicalDocument vs. IPS → Composition vs. other national PS guides →
IPS), which is the opposite of the consistency the ticket is trying to
achieve. Declining now and tracking the change against the next IPS
major release keeps AU PS aligned with the broader patient-summary
community.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B (specifically **B2** — introduce an
intermediate `AUClinicalDocumentComposition` profile that derives from
`clinicaldocument`, and have the AU PS Composition derive from that).

The ticket's underlying goals are sound: structural consistency with
other clinical-document FHIR IGs, leverage of CDA implementation
experience, and forward-alignment with where IPS is heading. Disposition
A delivers all of that, but at the cost of a breaking change to the AU
PS profile lineage *ahead of* the IPS work group making the equivalent
move — which risks AU PS being temporarily out of step with IPS, the
very alignment the ticket cites. Disposition C protects implementers
but leaves the AU document-IG ecosystem without a shared FHIR
clinical-document base, which is a missed opportunity given several
other AU document IGs (discharge summary, referral, etc.) face the same
question. **B2** captures the lineage benefit the reporter is asking
for, contains the breaking change behind a reusable AU clinical-document
base profile, and makes the eventual hard re-parent (when IPS moves)
mechanical rather than disruptive. The work group should also consider
filing a coordination ticket against the IPS specification so the AU
re-parenting can be sequenced with IPS's own re-parenting plan.
