## Summary

The Australian Digital Health Agency's Clinical Informatics team is consolidating Provider Connect Australia (PCA) and HealthConnect (HC) ValueSets/CodeSystems into NCTS-managed artifacts that can serve both programs. To do that, the AU Base CodeSystem `service-provision-conditions` needs to express a "mixed billing" concept currently carried only in the NHSD CodeSystem `http://fhir.nhsd.com.au/CodeSystem/rc-feeAndBillingOption` as `MIX` ("Bulk billing available with conditions, fees may apply"). The reporter offered two paths: (1) add `MIX` to the AU Base CS, or (2) create a new NCTS CS that subsumes the AU Base CS and add `MIX` there.

Discussion in subsequent comments broadened the scope well beyond a single code addition. A series of meetings with ADHA, Healthdirect (NHSD) and PCA stakeholders surfaced several related issues with the existing CodeSystem: `BBO` ("Bulk Billing Only") display and definition are misaligned and arguably should be deprecated in favour of a cleaner `BB` ("Bulk Billing"); `FBB` ("Fees and Bulk Billing") overlaps with the requested `MIX` and may be redundant once definitions are tightened; `FAP` ("Fees Apply") has a definition that says nothing about fees; `NFE` ("No Fee") wording can be sharpened; and `COP` has an unnecessary capitalisation of "Private Insurance". A separate "Discussion item" — when an AU Base CS should migrate to NCTS — was raised but resolved (per a 2025-12-12 comment) to leave the CS in AU Base until it stabilises.

By 2026-02-03 the reporter recorded that, following a final stakeholder meeting, the originally-requested change and all the surrounding fixes have been split into five follow-on tickets and that **this ticket can be closed once they are accepted**: FHIR-55575 (add `BB`, deprecate `BBO`), FHIR-55576 (add `MIX`, deprecate `FBB`), FHIR-55577 (refine `FAP` definition), FHIR-55578 (refine `NFE` definition), and FHIR-55579 (de-capitalise the `COP` definition).

## Details

**Description:**

> The Clinical Informatics (CI) team at the Agency are looking at converting a number of Provider Connect Australia (PCA) and HealthConnect (HC) ValueSets (VS) and CodeSystems (CS) from being separate CS's and VS's for PCA and HC to NCTS VS's and CS's that can be used across both PCA and HC.
>
> This VS needs to accommodate the codes:
>
> ```json
> "include" : [
>   {
>     "system" : "http://terminology.hl7.org.au/CodeSystem/service-provision-conditions",
>     "concept" : [
>       { "code" : "BBO", "display" : "Bulk Billing is offered under Medicare." },
>       { "code" : "FAP", "display" : "All or some of the services provided are offered at the Healthcare Service." },
>       { "code" : "NFE", "display" : "All or some of the services provided are offered free of charge." }
>     ]
>   },
>   {
>     "system" : "https://healthterminologies.gov.au/fhir/CodeSystem/service-provision-1",
>     "concept" : [
>       { "code" : "MIX", "display" : "Mixed Billing" }
>     ]
>   }
> ]
> ```
>
> Hence the code "MIX" needs to be included along side the codes in the HL7 AU Base [CodeSystem: Service Provision Conditions Australian Concepts](http://terminology.hl7.org.au/CodeSystem/service-provision-conditions). This can be achieved by:
>
> - **Option 1:** add the code "MIX" to the HL7 AU Base CS; OR
> - **Option 2:** create a new NCTS CS that subsumes HL7 AU Base CodeSystem: Service Provision Conditions Australian Concepts and add the code "MIX" to the new NCTS CS.
>
> Discussion item: When should an AU Base CS be moved to an NCTS CS?

## Proposed Dispositions

> Note: because there are no related GitHub repositories surfaced for this ticket, the dispositions below name the **AU Base CodeSystem artifact** (`http://terminology.hl7.org.au/CodeSystem/service-provision-conditions` in the AU Base IG v5.x source) as the change target rather than a specific repo path. The Australia work group should confirm the authoring location of that CodeSystem (typically the HL7 AU base IG source) when scheduling implementation.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the reporter's recommended path: close FHIR-53415 by progressing the five follow-on tickets it spawned, treating this parent ticket as a **discussion / triage** record. Concretely, the working group should:

1. Review and disposition FHIR-55575, FHIR-55576, FHIR-55577, FHIR-55578 and FHIR-55579 against the AU Base CodeSystem `service-provision-conditions`.
2. On acceptance, in the AU Base IG source for that CodeSystem:
   - Add concept `BB` (display "Bulk Billing", definition "No fee for eligible services, with a Medicare card presented.") and mark `BBO` deprecated (`status = deprecated` / `inactive = true`, with a `deprecationDate` and a "use BB instead" annotation).
   - Add concept `MIX` (display "Mixed Billing", definition "Bulk billing available with conditions, fees may apply.") and mark `FBB` deprecated with a "use MIX instead" annotation.
   - Replace the `FAP` definition with "Fees apply for services provided."
   - Replace the `NFE` definition with "A free service where no payment or Medicare card is required."
   - Decapitalise "Private Insurance" → "private insurance" in the `COP` definition.
3. Bump the CodeSystem `version` and add a release-notes entry. Leave the CodeSystem in AU Base (per 2025-12-12 decision) — do **not** migrate to NCTS as part of this change.
4. Close FHIR-53415 once the five children are resolved, citing them as the resolution.

#### Justification

This is exactly the path the reporter and the cross-organisational meeting (ADHA, Healthdirect, ADHA Clinical Informatics, AU FHIR WG) converged on. It keeps each editorial change reviewable in isolation (deprecations, additions, definition changes, capitalisation) which is the standard FHIR-terminology approach to CodeSystem maintenance. Adding `MIX` and `BB` while deprecating `FBB` and `BBO` (rather than removing them) preserves backward compatibility for existing PCA / HealthConnect data. Keeping the artifact in AU Base honours the team's stated rule of waiting for stability before NCTS transition, and avoids creating a parallel NCTS CodeSystem that would have to be re-aligned every time AU Base changes.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Consolidate the five follow-on tickets into a single coordinated CodeSystem revision shipped as one PR against AU Base, and use this ticket (FHIR-53415) as the umbrella. Specifically:

- Treat FHIR-53415 as the tracked change; close FHIR-55575–FHIR-55579 as duplicates that point to it.
- Produce one revision of `CodeSystem/service-provision-conditions` that simultaneously: adds `BB` and `MIX`; deprecates `BBO` and `FBB` with `replacedBy` extensions / properties pointing at `BB` and `MIX` respectively; rewrites the `FAP`, `NFE` and `COP` definitions; and (optionally) reserves but does not yet add the politically-sensitive `QBB` concept.
- Publish a single AU Base release-notes entry summarising the bulk-billing terminology rework so implementers and Healthdirect / PCA see one coherent migration story.
- Defer the `QBB` concept to a separate ticket once DHDA wording is agreed (per the 2025-12-12 caveat that `QBB` "has political sensitivities and may take time").

#### Justification

The five child tickets describe one logically cohesive change ("rework the AU Base service-provision-conditions billing concepts to align with NHSD/PCA/HC and DHDA endorsements"). Splitting them risks shipping a partial state (e.g., `BB` lands but `BBO` deprecation is delayed, leaving two coexisting bulk-billing-only concepts), and makes the implementer migration narrative harder to follow. A single coordinated revision is cheaper to ballot and review, and keeps the deprecation/replacement pairings together where reviewers can verify them in one diff. The cost is that this ticket would have to be reopened from "Triaged" and progressed to a real Resolution, contradicting the reporter's stated intent to close it — but the workgroup is the right body to make that call.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline this ticket as **superseded / no further action**: close FHIR-53415 with resolution "Won't Do" or "Superseded" because the actual work has already been split into and re-scoped under FHIR-55575–FHIR-55579, all of which are independent Submitted Change Requests. The discussion item embedded in the description ("when should an AU Base CS be moved to an NCTS CS?") has been answered in-thread (stay in AU Base for now) and does not require any spec change.

#### Justification

Strictly speaking, FHIR-53415 no longer asks the workgroup to make a change to anything: every concrete change it raised has been re-filed as its own ticket, and the CodeSystem-migration discussion item was resolved in comments without producing an action item. Closing it without a separate disposition is defensible as a housekeeping decision and matches the reporter's own 2026-02-03 comment ("it is not anticipated that there will be any further work coming from this Jira ticket and it can be closed"). The risk is purely cosmetic: a future reader may have to follow the comment trail to discover the five successor tickets; this can be mitigated by adding "is replaced by" Jira links to each of the five children before closing.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter has already done the work of negotiating with ADHA, DHDA, Healthdirect and PCA, secured DHDA endorsement for the `BB` addition, and split the resulting changes into appropriately-scoped tickets that the AU FHIR work group can review individually. Disposition A simply ratifies that path and gives the children an explicit go-ahead. Disposition B is tempting because the changes are conceptually one rework and shipping them together has migration-narrative benefits, but it requires reopening FHIR-53415 against the reporter's stated wishes and re-scoping five already-Submitted children — administrative churn for a benefit that can also be obtained in A by simply bundling the children into one AU Base release. Disposition C is technically correct but loses the small-but-real benefit of formally accepting Disposition A's package as the agreed plan. Before closing FHIR-53415, the work group should add explicit Jira "is replaced by" links from FHIR-53415 to FHIR-55575/55576/55577/55578/55579 so the provenance survives, and should record its position on the (separately) proposed `QBB` concept so that future ticket has a clear starting point.
