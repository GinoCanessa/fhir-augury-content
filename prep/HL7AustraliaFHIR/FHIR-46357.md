## Summary

The ticket asks the HL7 Australia FHIR work group to add explicit support
and guidance for a `ServiceRequest.focus` extension on the AU Diagnostic
Request profile (`StructureDefinition-au-diagnosticrequest`), as a pre-
adoption of the R5 `ServiceRequest.focus` element. The motivating use
case is diagnostic imaging requests where the focus of the request is
not the patient subject but a related artefact — specifically, imaging
requested to determine tumour margins on a surgical pathology specimen
(common in breast-conserving surgery for tumour removal). The original
report came in via GitHub issue
[hl7au/au-fhir-base#770](https://github.com/hl7au/au-fhir-base#770) from
Jaymee Murdoch (on behalf of the Agency) and was also raised as a
ballot issue for AU Base 4.1.0
([FHIRIG-227](https://jira.hl7australia.com/browse/FHIRIG-227)). The
HL7 Australia FHIR work group has triaged the ticket and the Agency
has agreed to transfer it to the AU eRequesting Technical Design
Group (TDG) backlog. The cross-reference index surfaces only a
"mentions" link to the FHIR element `ServiceRequest.focus`; no
related Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were found in
FHIR Augury.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Within the AU eRequesting (FHIR) IG (target of the agreed TDG
transfer), define and publish a pre-adoption R5 backport extension
`ServiceRequest.focus` (cardinality `0..*`, `Reference(Any)` per R5)
named e.g. `http://hl7.org.au/fhir/StructureDefinition/servicerequest-focus`.
Apply this extension to the AU eRequesting `ServiceRequest`-derived
profile(s) (e.g., the AU Diagnostic Request equivalent) and add
narrative guidance with a worked example for the imaging-of-pathology-
specimen use case (a `ServiceRequest` whose `subject` is the patient
but whose `focus` references the `Specimen` representing the excised
tumour tissue, used to evaluate surgical margins). Mark the extension
as a clearly-flagged pre-adoption of the R5 element and align the
canonical/extension definition with the equivalent HL7 International
backport extension if one exists, so it can be retired cleanly when
AU eRequesting moves to R5.

#### Justification

`ServiceRequest.focus` is an existing R5 element specifically intended
to capture "what the service request is about, when it is not about
the subject of record" — exactly the imaging-of-specimen pattern
described in the ticket. Pre-adoption via an extension is the
established FHIR mechanism for surfacing a future-version element on
an R4-based IG, and the 2025-05-19 comment records Agency agreement to
move this work to the eRequesting TDG, where the AU eRequesting IG is
authored — making this the natural home for a domain-specific
extension and worked example. Defining it as a standalone, clearly
named pre-adoption extension (rather than embedding it in a broader
profile change) keeps the migration to R5 mechanical.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Do not mint a new AU-specific extension. Instead, in the AU eRequesting
IG, recommend reuse of the existing pattern available today on
`ServiceRequest` in R4 — i.e., document a usage convention that pairs
`ServiceRequest.subject` (the patient) with a reference to the
`Specimen` via `ServiceRequest.specimen` for specimen-as-focus
scenarios, and via `ServiceRequest.reasonReference` /
`supportingInfo` for non-specimen focus references. Provide a worked
example for the breast-conserving-surgery margin assessment use case
using these existing elements, and defer formal `ServiceRequest.focus`
adoption to the AU eRequesting R5 migration.

#### Justification

R4 `ServiceRequest` already has a `specimen` element (`0..* Reference(Specimen)`)
that covers the cited motivating use case — imaging of an excised
tumour specimen to assess margins — without any extension or
pre-adoption machinery. Avoiding a bespoke pre-adoption extension
reduces the migration burden when AU eRequesting moves to R5 (no
extension to deprecate, no instances to rewrite) and keeps the AU
profile consistent with the rest of the FHIR R4 ecosystem. The
trade-off is reduced semantic precision when the focus is genuinely
not a `Specimen` (e.g., a `BodyStructure` or `Device`); for those
cases, guidance can direct implementers to `supportingInfo` with a
narrative caveat.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket "Persuasive with Modification → Consider for Future
Use" (consistent with the 2024-07-16 evote already recorded) and close
on the basis that (a) the request never advanced to a concrete
proposal in 18+ months, (b) HL7 Australia FHIR has formally agreed to
transfer the substantive work to the AU eRequesting TDG, and (c) the
specific use case — imaging on a pathology specimen — can already be
modelled in R4 via `ServiceRequest.specimen` plus narrative guidance,
so no AU Base or AU eRequesting normative change is strictly required.
Record the FHIRWG/Agency decision and direct any follow-up to the
eRequesting TDG backlog rather than carrying the ticket open in this
work group.

#### Justification

The ticket history shows the work group has already moved this twice
("Consider for Future Use" then "transfer to eRequesting TDG") without
a concrete change emerging, and the 2025-04-28 co-chair comment
explicitly invites either withdrawal or transfer. R4
`ServiceRequest.specimen` already provides a workable target for the
single concrete clinical scenario described, so leaving the issue
open against HL7 Australia FHIR risks duplicating work the
eRequesting TDG will do at the right level of fidelity when AU
eRequesting is on a proper R5 (or later) base.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The work group has already accepted (via the 2025-05-19 comment) that
the substantive work belongs in the AU eRequesting TDG, so the
disposition that best reflects what has actually been agreed is to
move forward with a concrete pre-adoption extension in the
AU eRequesting IG rather than declining outright (Disposition C) or
papering over the gap with `specimen`/`supportingInfo` (Disposition B).
`ServiceRequest.focus` exists in R5 precisely for this scenario, the
clinical use case (imaging of an excised tumour specimen for margin
assessment) is well-evidenced, and a clean pre-adoption extension is
both the standard FHIR mechanism and the lowest-risk path to the
eventual R5 cutover. Disposition B remains a reasonable fallback
if the eRequesting TDG decides the existing `specimen` element fully
covers the in-scope eRequesting use cases, but that determination
should be made inside the TDG with the extension proposal on the
table, not by closing the ticket here.
