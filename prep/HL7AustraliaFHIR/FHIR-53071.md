## Summary

The reporter requests that AU Base be extended so that an Australian
healthcare *organisation* (e.g., a pharmacy or PBS-prescribing practice)
can record its participation in the Closing the Gap PBS Co-payment
Programme (CTG / CTGPBS). Today the AU Base extension
`closing-the-gap-registration`
(`http://hl7.org.au/fhir/StructureDefinition/closing-the-gap-registration`)
has a context limited to `Patient`, capturing whether an Aboriginal or
Torres Strait Islander individual is registered for the programme.
There is currently no defined way to flag that a service provider
(Organization) is itself eligible/registered to dispense or prescribe
under CTG.

The ticket presents two options for resolution: **Option 1** — define a
new, organisation-scoped extension for CTG participation; or
**Option 2** — broaden the context of the existing
`closing-the-gap-registration` extension to also allow `Organization`.

There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets recorded against this issue
in FHIR Augury. The originating discussion is in the
`australia > AU Base` Zulip topic (Sep 2025), where Heath Frankel
explicitly advised the reporter to raise a Jira ticket to either expand
the context of the existing extension or create a new one — i.e., the
two options now in this ticket are direct outputs of that conversation.
The same item ("Organisation Closing the Gap Registration") was later
included by the Australia work group in an April 2026 Zulip poll on
candidate scope for AU Base R7, indicating active community interest.

> would land in the AU Base IG repository owned by HL7 Australia FHIR

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested — Option 2 (broaden the existing extension's context)

#### Proposal

Modify the existing AU Base extension
`http://hl7.org.au/fhir/StructureDefinition/closing-the-gap-registration`
to add `Organization` to its `Extension.context`. Specifically:

- Add a `context` entry of type `element` with expression
  `Organization`, in addition to the existing `Patient` context.
- Update the extension's short/definition text and usage guidance to
  state that the extension may be applied either to a Patient (existing
  meaning: the individual is registered for CTG) or to an Organization
  (new meaning: the service provider is registered/eligible under CTG
  for dispensing/prescribing).
- Review the extension's value/data-type definition to confirm that the
  same value semantics make sense in both contexts; if a single boolean
  flag is sufficient for "registered: yes/no", retain it. If the
  organisational meaning needs richer detail (e.g., effective period,
  registration identifier, programme variant), add sub-extensions
  rather than overload the existing payload.
- Add an example showing use on `Organization`, mirroring the existing
  Patient example.
- Bump the AU Base IG version per its usual versioning policy and note
  the context change in the IG release notes.

#### Justification

This is the literal request and the option Heath Frankel flagged on
Zulip as available. Re-using one extension keeps the CTG concept in a
single, discoverable place, which matches the FHIR design preference
for cohesive, well-scoped extensions over a proliferation of
near-duplicate ones. Implementers already familiar with the Patient
usage incur minimal new learning, and existing Patient instances
remain valid because adding a new context entry is additive at the
StructureDefinition level. The Australia community has already
acknowledged this work as a candidate for AU Base R7 (Apr 2026 poll),
making R7 a natural release vehicle.

The chief design question this disposition must resolve is whether the
*meaning* of the extension is genuinely the same on both contexts.
"This patient is registered for CTG" and "this organisation is
eligible to dispense under CTG" are conceptually adjacent but not
identical (subject vs. provider eligibility). If reviewers conclude
the semantics diverge enough to confuse implementers, Disposition B
becomes preferable.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — Option 1 (new Organization-scoped extension)

#### Proposal

Leave `closing-the-gap-registration` unchanged (Patient-only) and
define a new AU Base extension, e.g.
`http://hl7.org.au/fhir/StructureDefinition/organization-closing-the-gap-registration`
(exact slug to be agreed by the work group), with:

- `Extension.context` of type `element` with expression `Organization`.
- A definition that explicitly describes "the organisation is
  registered/eligible to provide care under the Closing the Gap PBS
  Co-payment Programme (CTGPBS)".
- Payload aligned to the organisational use case: at minimum a
  boolean/registration-status code; optionally a period of
  registration and a programme/scheme identifier as sub-extensions, so
  that future variants (e.g., dispensing vs. prescribing eligibility)
  can be modelled without further breaking change.
- A cross-reference in both extensions' descriptions noting the
  Patient-side counterpart, so implementers can find the
  organisational extension when starting from the Patient one and
  vice-versa.
- Examples on `Organization` and updated AU Base "Potentially useful
  extensions" guidance.

#### Justification

This keeps the two semantics — "patient registered" vs. "provider
registered" — cleanly separated, which reduces the risk of
implementations conflating subject- and provider-level eligibility
when filtering or reporting. It also avoids any backwards-compatibility
ambiguity for existing Patient-only consumers: validators, profile
authors, and conformance tools that today assume the extension's
context is `Patient` continue to behave identically. The cost is one
more extension URL to publish and document, and a small risk of
implementer confusion about which to use; that risk is mitigated by
explicit cross-references between the two definitions.

Compared to Disposition A, B is the safer choice if the organisational
payload is likely to grow (effective period, programme code,
dispensing/prescribing distinction), because the new extension can be
shaped to that need without re-shaping the long-standing Patient
extension.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change request on the grounds that organisational
eligibility for the Closing the Gap PBS Co-payment Programme can be
represented using existing FHIR mechanisms without changes to AU Base.
For example: an `Organization.identifier` (or
`HealthcareService.eligibility` on a `HealthcareService` referencing
the Organization) carrying a CTG-specific identifier or coded
eligibility, or an `Endpoint` / `Coverage` arrangement where
applicable. The reporter would be advised to model the use case with
those resources and to return with a more concrete gap analysis if
they prove insufficient.

#### Justification

A decline is defensible only if the work group concludes that
organisational CTG registration is adequately handled by existing
FHIR/AU Base mechanisms (notably `HealthcareService.eligibility`,
which exists precisely to express programme/funding eligibility of
provided services). Going this route avoids both a new extension and a
context change to a long-standing one. However, it places the
representational burden on each implementer to choose and align an
encoding, which is the kind of fragmentation AU Base extensions
typically exist to prevent. The Zulip thread shows the work group
member who responded (Heath Frankel) did not propose this path —
he assumed an extension change was the appropriate response — which
weakens the case for declining.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B (new Organization-scoped extension).

Both A and B address the requested capability; the choice is whether
"registered for CTG" should be a single concept with two contexts (A)
or two distinct, context-specific concepts (B). The use case Bharti
confirmed on Zulip — "to record eligibility of service providing the
care" — is, in standards terms, *provider eligibility for a programme*,
which is a different notion from a patient's enrolment in that
programme. Provider eligibility tends to grow attributes over time
(effective period, scheme variant, dispensing vs. prescribing
authority), and modelling it in its own extension gives AU Base room
to evolve that payload without disturbing the Patient extension that
implementers already rely on. It also keeps the existing extension's
context narrow, which protects current consumers and validators from
unexpected occurrences on `Organization`.

Disposition C is not recommended: the work group has already implicitly
endorsed an extension-based path on Zulip and has placed
"Organisation Closing the Gap Registration" on the AU Base R7 candidate
list, so declining would contradict that direction without new
evidence. Disposition A is a reasonable fallback if the work group
prefers the operational simplicity of a single extension and is
comfortable that the Patient and Organization meanings will not
diverge.
