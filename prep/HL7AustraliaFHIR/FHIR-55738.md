## Summary

The ticket asks AU Core to remove the profile-level invariant `au-core-org-01`
on the `AU Core Organization` profile, which currently asserts:
"A National Organisation Identifier (type=NOI) shall be an HPI-O or PAI-O"
(constrained via FHIRPath to systems `http://ns.electronichealth.net.au/id/hi/hpio/1.0`
or `http://ns.electronichealth.net.au/id/pcehr/paio/1.0`).

The reporter argues the constraint is too narrow because the underlying
`NOI` code in AU Base's `IdentifierType AU` (hl7VS-identifierType - AU Extended)
is defined more broadly as "an identifier unique to an organisation assigned
by an identifier scheme managed at a national level", and Services Australia
is in the process of issuing two additional national organisation identifier
types — **HSP-O** (Healthcare Service Provider Organisation) and **HAE**
(Healthcare Affiliated Entity) — both of which would legitimately carry
`type = NOI` but cannot satisfy the current AU Core invariant. New companion
tickets [FHIR-54923](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54923) (Create HSP-O
profile of Identifier) and [FHIR-54928](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54928)
(Create HAE profile of Identifier) are tracking the new identifier profiles
in AU Base, and FHIR-55738 is the AU Core companion that prevents the new
identifier types from being immediately invalid against the published AU
Core Organization profile.

Per the most recent comments on the ticket, the work group has already
**decided to remove the constraint**, and a pull request implementing the
change ([hl7au/au-fhir-core#411](https://github.com/hl7au/au-fhir-core/pull/411))
is open. This review prep therefore primarily exists to confirm the agreed
disposition and weigh whether anything narrower (e.g., relaxing rather than
removing the invariant) should be reconsidered.

## Details

**Description:**

> The definition of NOI in [IdentifierType AU - AU Base Implementation Guide v6.0.1-ci-build](https://build.fhir.org/ig/hl7au/au-fhir-base/CodeSystem-au-v2-0203.html) allows it to include other national identifiers, such as [HSP-O](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54923) and [HAE](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54928).
>
> The constraint on AU Core Organization [au-core-org-01](https://build.fhir.org/ig/hl7au/au-fhir-core/StructureDefinition-au-core-organization.html#constraints-2) limits the use of NOI to HPI-O and PAI-O.
>
> That is too strict. Remove the constraint.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In `hl7au/au-fhir-core`, remove invariant `au-core-org-01` from the
`AU Core Organization` profile. Concretely (matching open PR #411):

- Edit `input/resources/au-core-organization.xml`, `Organization.identifier`
  element: delete the `<constraint key="au-core-org-01" …>` block and the
  sibling `<condition value="au-core-org-01"/>`. Leave the `slicing`
  declaration and the `hpio` / `paio` slices intact.
- Edit `input/pagecontent/changes.md`: add a "Changes in This Version"
  entry under "AU Core Organization" recording the removal and linking
  to FHIR-55738.
- Merge `hl7au/au-fhir-core#411` once review by the requested reviewers
  (brettesler-ext, dt-r, dbojicic, JayMurdoch) is complete; re-run IG
  publisher and update the QA baseline to drop the expected
  `au-core-org-01` failure entries.

No coordinated change in AU Base is required for FHIR-55738 itself; the
NOI code definition in `hl7VS-identifierType - AU Extended` already
allows the broader semantics. The new HSP-O / HAE identifier profiles
land separately under FHIR-54923 / FHIR-54928 in AU Base.

#### Justification

This is the option the workgroup already explicitly chose on 2026-03-18
("Decided to remove the constraint."), and is what PR #411 implements.
It restores AU Core to the looser, code-system-driven semantics of NOI
defined in AU Base, which is the more authoritative artifact for what
the code means. It future-proofs AU Core against the imminent HSP-O and
HAE identifier types (and any further national identifier schemes
Services Australia introduces under the amended Healthcare Identifiers
Act) without requiring AU Core re-publication every time a new national
identifier is minted. The relaxation is profile-conformance-monotone:
any instance that was valid before remains valid, so it cannot break
existing AU Core implementers. The Zulip thread shows the workgroup
considered narrower options (Disposition B below) and converged on
removal as the cleanest answer.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Instead of deleting `au-core-org-01` outright, **relax** it to the union
of currently-known national organisation identifier systems. Modify the
constraint expression in `input/resources/au-core-organization.xml` so
that NOI implies the identifier `system` is one of: HPI-O
(`http://ns.electronichealth.net.au/id/hi/hpio/1.0`), PAI-O
(`http://ns.electronichealth.net.au/id/pcehr/paio/1.0`), HSP-O (system
URI to be defined under FHIR-54923) and HAE (system URI to be defined
under FHIR-54928). Optionally weaken severity from `error` to `warning`
so that future, not-yet-known national identifier systems do not produce
hard validation failures while still surfacing unfamiliar combinations
to implementers.

This requires AU Core to take a hard dependency on the system URIs
allocated for HSP-O and HAE under FHIR-54923 / FHIR-54928, so the AU
Base companion tickets must land first.

#### Justification

This preserves the original intent of `au-core-org-01` — to flag
instances where a NOI-typed identifier carries a system that is *not* a
recognised national identifier scheme — while adding the two
identifiers that Services Australia is about to issue. Andy Bond's
question on the Zulip thread ("Do we just keep congregating any
govt-assigned org identifier under NOI?") and Danielle's caution about
who actually uses `type` both point to the constraint having genuine
diagnostic value when the value set is a known closed list. The trade-
off versus Disposition A is significant: AU Core must be re-published
whenever Services Australia issues a new national identifier scheme,
and AU Core gains a sequencing dependency on AU Base FHIR-54923 and
FHIR-54928 landing first. Lowering severity to `warning` mitigates the
re-publication treadmill but introduces a profile-versus-implementer
ambiguity about what "national identifier" really means.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Keep `au-core-org-01` as-is. AU Core retains the strict constraint that
a NOI-typed identifier must be HPI-O or PAI-O. Implementers wishing to
record HSP-O or HAE identifiers on Organization either (a) use a
different `Identifier.type` code rather than NOI, (b) carry the
identifier on a non-AU-Core profile, or (c) wait for a future major
version of AU Core that explicitly accommodates the new identifier
schemes.

#### Justification

There is a defensible "no change" reading: the original
`au-core-org-01` was written to give implementers a hard signal about
which identifier systems are expected on AU Core Organization, and
removing it weakens the profile's diagnostic value. However, this
reading conflicts with both the AU Base definition of NOI (which is
explicitly broader than HPI-O/PAI-O) and with the workgroup's recorded
decision on 2026-03-18 to remove the constraint. The stronger
counter-argument is that NOI carries no system-restriction semantics in
AU Base, and AU Core re-asserting one creates a contradiction between
the two specs that implementers will eventually have to reconcile —
likely by ignoring AU Core. Decline is therefore weak.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A — Accept As Requested.

The workgroup has already made and recorded this decision, the
implementation PR is open and small (2 files, ±8 lines), and the change
is profile-conformance-monotone (no existing AU Core Organization
instance becomes invalid). The conflict between AU Core's narrow
`au-core-org-01` and AU Base's broader NOI definition is a real
spec-vs-spec inconsistency that the imminent HSP-O and HAE rollouts
turn from a theoretical concern into an operational one; relaxing AU
Core to defer to AU Base's code semantics is the cleanest fix.
Disposition B is a reasonable fallback if the workgroup wants to
preserve a diagnostic signal, but it locks AU Core into a publication
cadence driven by Services Australia and creates a hard sequencing
dependency on AU Base FHIR-54923 / FHIR-54928, neither of which is
attractive. Disposition C contradicts the recorded 2026-03-18 decision
and AU Base's own definition of NOI and should not be revisited absent
new information. Reviewers (brettesler-ext, dt-r, dbojicic, JayMurdoch)
should be asked to confirm PR #411's narrative entry in `changes.md`
adequately signals the relaxation to downstream implementers, and the
IG QA baseline should be refreshed to drop expected `au-core-org-01`
failures.
