## Summary

The reporter asks the HL7 Australia FHIR work group to draft normative
guidance, inside the AU Base FHIR Implementation Guide, on when and how
"domain specific" profiles are permitted to live in AU Base rather than
in a separate domain IG. AU Base has historically housed agnostic,
locally-flavoured profiles of base FHIR resources (the "non-domain"
profiles), but in practice domain-specific profiles also land there when
no other HL7 AU FHIR IG exists to host them. The ticket requests policy
and management text describing what modelling is appropriate in AU Base
for both flavours — agnostic profiles that only define local concepts,
and domain-specific profiles that add use-case-driven constraints (e.g.,
narrower reference targets, tighter terminology bindings/strengths,
defined use case descriptions). No related Jira tickets, Zulip threads,
or GitHub items are linked through the cross-reference index, so the
scope must be inferred from the ticket text alone.

## Details

**Description:**

Draft guidance material on domain specific profiles appearing in AU Base.

Suitable policy and management content is needed to be added to AU Base
to describe what is appropriate modelling in AU Base.

Domain specific profiles may appear in AU Base if there are no other
suitable HL7 AU FHIR IG publication to host them. Unlike other profiles
in AU Base they will have more specific use cases defined and consequent
modelling constraints to support the use case.

This is to cover defining appropriate modelling and descriptions for
profiles that are intended to be domain specific in AU Base FHIR IG.

This may include appropriate use case descriptions, reference target
types, terminology bindings/strength etc.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a new "Profiling Guidance" (or "Modelling Policy") page to the AU
Base FHIR IG that explicitly distinguishes two classes of profiles
hosted in AU Base and states the modelling rules for each:

1. **Agnostic / Local-Concept Profiles** — profiles whose sole purpose is
   to express Australian-locality concerns on a base FHIR resource
   (e.g., national identifier systems, AU-specific extensions, local
   terminology bindings) without committing to a particular clinical or
   business use case. Rules: minimal `mustSupport`, no narrowing of
   resource-level reference targets beyond what AU-locality requires,
   `preferred` or `extensible` bindings rather than `required` unless
   the AU terminology is genuinely closed, neutral (use-case-free)
   `purpose`/`description` text.
2. **Domain-Specific Profiles in AU Base** — permitted only when no
   other HL7 AU FHIR IG is a suitable host. Rules: each such profile
   MUST carry an explicit `purpose` describing the in-scope use case(s);
   tightened `Reference()` target types, slicing, terminology binding
   strengths, cardinalities, and `mustSupport` flags MUST each be
   justified by that documented use case; the profile MUST be flagged
   in the IG TOC (e.g., a "domain-specific" badge or a separate sub-
   section) so implementers can see at a glance that it is not a
   general-purpose AU profile; and the IG MUST record an exit criterion
   ("when an appropriate domain IG exists, this profile will be
   migrated").

Land this as new narrative pages under the AU Base IG authoring tree
(the page-content area used by the Australian Digital Health Agency /
HL7 AU's AU Base IG repo on GitHub), plus a short `purpose`-text
template that future profile authors can copy. No
StructureDefinition changes are required for this ticket itself; it
delivers policy text only.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter and JIRAUSER25123's comment ask for,
and it covers both classes the comment thread explicitly calls out
(domain *and* non-domain). Putting the policy in AU Base itself — rather
than in a meta-document — keeps it discoverable by the implementers and
profile authors who actually need it, and gives the work group a stable
reference to point to when triaging future profile-scope debates. The
two-class split mirrors how AU Base is already used in practice and
gives reviewers an objective checklist (purpose statement? justified
narrowings? migration criterion?) instead of relying on case-by-case
judgement.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than embedding policy inside AU Base, publish the
domain-vs-agnostic profiling guidance as a **separate HL7 AU "FHIR
Profiling Conventions"** companion document (a small standalone IG or a
Confluence-published policy page) that AU Base — and every other HL7 AU
IG — links to from its own "Profiling Conventions" section. AU Base
would then carry only a short pointer plus an AU-Base-specific addendum
(e.g., the migration-out criterion for domain profiles that should
eventually move to a dedicated IG). The companion document would define:
the two profile classes, mandatory metadata for each
(`purpose`, use-case statement, exit criterion), defaulting rules for
binding strength and reference targets, and a decision tree for
authors choosing between AU Base, a dedicated AU IG, or pure base FHIR.

#### Justification

The underlying need is HL7 AU-wide, not AU-Base-specific: the same
"where does this profile belong, and what may it constrain?" question
arises for every AU IG (AU Core, AU eRequesting, AU Pathology, etc.),
and any answer baked solely into AU Base will drift from sibling IGs
over time. A shared companion document is reusable, versionable
independently of AU Base's release cadence, and avoids inflating AU
Base with policy text that is conceptually upstream of it. Trade-off
versus Disposition A: somewhat lower discoverability for AU Base
implementers (one extra hop) and a new artefact to maintain, but
significantly better long-term consistency across the HL7 AU FHIR
portfolio.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline on the grounds that profile-scoping decisions are inherently
case-by-case and are already adequately handled through normal AU FHIR
work-group review of incoming profiles. Do not author new policy text;
instead, rely on existing FHIR conformance machinery (`purpose`,
`useContext`, `mustSupport`, binding strength) and on the work group's
review process to flag inappropriate domain content in AU Base.

#### Justification

Defensible only if the work group judges that the cost of writing,
maintaining, and enforcing formal profiling policy outweighs the
benefit, and that ad-hoc review has been catching the cases that
matter. The reporter's framing — and the comment that explicitly calls
out a gap for non-domain agnostic profiles too — suggests this is
*not* the case in practice, so this disposition is included for
completeness rather than as a serious recommendation.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A directly answers what the reporter asked for, addresses
the gap that JIRAUSER25123's comment widens to include agnostic /
local-concept profiles, and lands the guidance in the artefact (AU
Base) where the affected profile authors and reviewers will actually
encounter it. Disposition B is intellectually cleaner — profiling
policy *is* an HL7-AU-wide concern — but starting a new companion
artefact materially raises the cost and timeline, and AU Base would
still need an addendum, so it doesn't reduce the work in AU Base by
much. The pragmatic path is to ship the policy text in AU Base now
(Disposition A), and revisit promotion to a shared HL7 AU profiling-
conventions document later if and when a second AU IG needs the same
text. Disposition C is not recommended: the ticket exists precisely
because case-by-case review has not produced consistent outcomes.
