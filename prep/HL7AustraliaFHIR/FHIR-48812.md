## Summary

This ticket asks the HL7 Australia FHIR work group to revisit the textual
**definition of the AU eRequesting Server actor** in the AU eRequesting
(FHIR) implementation guide. The reporter (Heath Frankel) notes that the
current actor definition is no longer representative after the changes
applied for [FHIR-47141](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-47141)
("Provide eRequesting Responder Actor and Capability Statement"). That
related ticket was resolved *Persuasive with Modification* and, instead
of introducing a separate Responder actor, downgraded `ServiceRequest`
`create` and `update` in the AU eRequesting Server CapabilityStatement
from **SHALL** to **SHOULD** (while keeping `read` and `search-type` as
**SHALL**). Consequently, a Placer system that exposes its own service
requests via read/search can now legitimately conform to the AU
eRequesting Server CapabilityStatement without ever accepting created
requests, which the existing actor description does not capture.

The reporter offers two candidate replacement definitions. The first,
suggested for alignment with existing actor definitions in the IG, is
deliberately minimal: *"The AU eRequesting Server is a system that makes
diagnostic service requests accessible and can accept diagnostic service
requests."* He notes this feels vague and unhelpful. He prefers a more
explicit alternative that names the counterparties: *"The AU eRequesting
Server is a system that makes diagnostic service requests accessible to
filler and patient record systems and can accept diagnostic service
requests from placer systems."*

## Details

>   requests accessible to filler and patient record systems and can
>   accept diagnostic service requests from placer systems.

## Linked Jira Tickets

### [FHIR-47141: Provide eRequesting Responder Actor and Capability Statement](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-47141)

This is the directly motivating ticket. It originally proposed a new
*eRequesting Responder* actor so that placer/intermediary systems could
expose their own service requests via read/search without implementing
the full create interaction. The work group instead resolved it
*Persuasive with Modification* (Applied 2024-12-09) by **changing
ServiceRequest `create` and `update` in the AU eRequesting Server
CapabilityStatement from SHALL to SHOULD**, while leaving `read` and
`search-type` as SHALL. That resolution broadens the set of systems that
can claim conformance to the AU eRequesting Server actor (now including
placer/responder-style systems that only expose existing requests),
which is exactly why the actor definition in FHIR-48812 needs revisiting.

Reporter: Heath Frankel; Assignee: John Carter.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the **AU eRequesting Server actor definition** in the AU
eRequesting (FHIR) implementation guide to the reporter's preferred,
more explicit wording:

> The AU eRequesting Server is a system that makes diagnostic service
> requests accessible to filler and patient record systems and can
> accept diagnostic service requests from placer systems.

The change is text-only, applied to the actor definition page/section
that backs the AU eRequesting Server actor referenced from the AU
eRequesting Server CapabilityStatement. No CapabilityStatement
interactions are altered (those were already addressed by FHIR-47141:
`read`/`search-type` SHALL; `create`/`update` SHOULD). The actor
description in any narrative pages, the actor diagram caption, and the
CapabilityStatement `description` (if it restates the actor definition)
should be aligned with the new wording.

#### Justification

The reporter's preferred wording explicitly names the counterparty
actors (filler, patient record system, placer) and therefore makes the
post-FHIR-47141 reality discoverable from the actor definition alone:
both a traditional eRequesting "intake" server and a placer/responder
system that simply exposes its own service requests via read/search can
legitimately claim conformance. This matches the SHALL/SHOULD split
introduced by FHIR-47141 (read/search SHALL; create/update SHOULD) and
is consistent with FHIR's preference for actor descriptions that name
the transactional partners they interact with. It is also the wording
the reporter — who raised both FHIR-47141 and this ticket — has
explicitly endorsed.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt the **shorter "aligned with existing actor definitions"
wording** suggested in the ticket, with one small clarification, and
defer the richer counterparty wording to a separate explanatory
paragraph elsewhere on the actor page.

Actor definition (one sentence, pattern-matched to the rest of the IG):

> The AU eRequesting Server is a system that makes diagnostic service
> requests accessible and can accept diagnostic service requests.

Then add a short *Conformance scenarios* paragraph (or bullet list)
beneath the definition explaining the two implementation modes the
post-FHIR-47141 CapabilityStatement permits:

- Systems that accept new diagnostic service requests (supporting
  `create`/`update` per the SHOULD interactions), and
- Systems (typically placer or intermediary systems) that expose
  existing diagnostic service requests via `read` and `search-type`
  without supporting create/update.

#### Justification

This keeps the *actor definition* itself stylistically uniform with
other actor definitions in the AU eRequesting IG (which is the original
reason the shorter wording was suggested) while still capturing the
nuance the reporter is rightly worried about. Putting the
implementation modes in a clearly-marked conformance paragraph is more
discoverable to implementers than embedding "filler and patient record
systems / placer systems" inside a single long sentence, and it avoids
a slight risk in Disposition A's wording — that readers infer "accept
diagnostic service requests *from placer systems*" implies a
*requirement* to support create/update, contradicting the SHOULD
downgrade from FHIR-47141. Trade-off vs. A: requires editing two
adjacent blocks instead of a single sentence, and the actor definition
read in isolation remains slightly abstract.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as *Not Persuasive*: leave the existing AU eRequesting Server
actor definition unchanged and rely on the CapabilityStatement (and its
SHALL/SHOULD interaction matrix as set by FHIR-47141) as the
authoritative description of what an AU eRequesting Server may do.

#### Justification

The substantive conformance change a placer/responder-style system
needs to claim AU eRequesting Server conformance has already been made
under FHIR-47141 — `create`/`update` were dropped from SHALL to SHOULD.
A reader inspecting the CapabilityStatement therefore already gets the
correct picture of what is required and what is optional. Declining
would be defensible if the work group judges that the actor definition
text is supplementary narrative and that re-wording it adds editorial
churn (and translation/derivative work) without clarifying any
normative requirement. The cost is real: the reporter — who is the same
person who raised the underlying FHIR-47141 — has explicitly said the
current definition is "no longer representative", so declining leaves a
documented mismatch between the actor narrative and the
CapabilityStatement.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the strongest balance of the considerations on the
ticket. The reporter himself noted that the shorter wording was
suggested for *alignment with existing actor definitions*, and that
alignment is a real, recurring editorial value in the AU eRequesting
IG; Disposition A's longer sentence breaks that pattern. At the same
time, Disposition C ignores the reporter's substantive point that the
current definition no longer matches the post-FHIR-47141
CapabilityStatement, and leaves the most important nuance (that a
placer/responder system can legitimately claim AU eRequesting Server
conformance via read/search alone) buried in the conformance matrix.
Disposition B keeps the actor definition uniform with the rest of the
IG while making the two valid implementation modes — accept new
requests, or expose existing ones — explicit and discoverable
immediately beside the definition. It also avoids the subtle "from
placer systems" framing in Disposition A that could be re-read as a
hard requirement to accept created requests, which would contradict
FHIR-47141. The work group can of course choose the reporter's
preferred wording (Disposition A) if they value the explicit
counterparty naming over stylistic alignment.
