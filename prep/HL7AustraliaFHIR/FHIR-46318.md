## Summary

The ticket proposes adding a new AU Base extension to capture a patient's Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander community, tribe, or "mob" affiliation — a concept not currently represented in AU Base or the underlying international Patient resource. It was filed by Brett Esler in Jira on behalf of a Zulip discussion held during the November 2023 Melbourne Connectathon (stream **#australia**, topic *AU Core Track - 2023-11 Melbourne Connectathon*) and is mirrored in GitHub issue [hl7au/au-fhir-base#813](https://github.com/hl7au/au-fhir-base/issues/813). Grahame Grieve proposed a strawman extension `http://hl7.org.au/fhir/StructureDefinition/community-affiliation` with a `valueString` (e.g., "Karjaganujaru"), arguing the content is not enumerable and hence not codeable — particularly once immigrant/diaspora affiliations are considered. Participants (Brett Esler, Gavin Morris, Marvin Malcolm, Danielle Tavares, Michael Lawley) discussed whether the data should be captured as `string` versus `CodeableConcept.text` to leave room for future terminology binding; Gavin Morris confirmed that products like Communicare do not record the concept structurally, and that the closest related concept ("skin group") is semantically distinct. No cross-referenced Jira tickets were returned by the Augury cross-reference service, and no separate indexed Zulip items were found — the Zulip transcript is captured inline in the ticket description.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Define and publish a new AU Base extension at `http://hl7.org.au/fhir/StructureDefinition/community-affiliation` applicable to `Patient` (and, by the usual AU Base pattern, to `RelatedPerson` if desired). The extension is a simple leaf extension with a single `value[x]` of type `string`, 0..1 on the root `Patient`, context `Patient`. Short description: "Free-text description of a patient's Aboriginal / Torres Strait Islander community, tribe, or 'mob' affiliation (or, more generally, the patient's self-described cultural community)." Mark as informative/trial-use for the first release. Add the extension to AU Base's published extension index and add an example fragment on the AU Base `Patient` profile page demonstrating its use with a `valueString` (per Grahame's strawman, e.g., "Karjaganujaru"). Do not bind to a terminology; do not constrain via invariant.

#### Justification

This is a minimal, non-breaking addition that directly satisfies the request and matches Grahame's strawman. Using `valueString` aligns with the clinical reality surfaced in the Zulip thread: the information is patient-reported, local knowledge that is not enumerated by any naming system and is not expected to ever be fully codeable (especially for immigrants / non-indigenous cultural groups). An extension on `Patient` is consistent with the standard FHIR pattern for jurisdiction-specific demographic concepts, and hosting it under `hl7.org.au` keeps it squarely in the AU jurisdictional namespace rather than polluting AU Core. No existing FHIR element or Patient extension directly represents cultural/community affiliation (it is distinct from `Patient.communication`, from ethnicity, and from "skin group").

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Publish the same extension (`http://hl7.org.au/fhir/StructureDefinition/community-affiliation`, context `Patient`, cardinality 0..\*), but type it as `CodeableConcept` rather than `string`, with **no** `valueSet` binding (or an `example`-strength binding to an empty/placeholder value set). Document that implementers MUST populate `CodeableConcept.text` and MAY populate `CodeableConcept.coding` if a suitable terminology is available locally. In parallel, open a companion ticket tracking eventual terminology work (e.g., a tentative AU community-affiliation code system for well-known groupings such as "Koori", "Murri", "Noongar", while keeping text as the primary carrier). Changing cardinality to 0..\* allows recording multiple affiliations (a concern raised implicitly by the "immigrants" comment — someone may identify with more than one community).

#### Justification

This approach preserves a forward path to terminology support — echoing Michael Lawley's and Danielle Tavares's concern that modelling the data as raw `string` forecloses future decision-support, linkage, and analytics use cases. `CodeableConcept.text` carries the same free-text payload as `string` with no loss of expressivity, so it meets today's requirement while leaving the door open. It is also consistent with AU Base's broader pattern of preferring `CodeableConcept` for demographic/cultural qualifiers. The trade-off versus Disposition A is marginally higher implementer burden (one more level of nesting) and a perceived invitation to code data that is often uncodeable; Grahame's counter-argument — that immigrant/diaspora affiliations are fundamentally not codeable — is real but does not prevent using `CodeableConcept.text` alone in those cases.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline to add a dedicated `community-affiliation` extension to AU Base at this time, on the grounds that (a) the use cases presented are anecdotal and connectathon-driven rather than backed by real-world implementer demand, (b) the data is already captured today as free-text notes or in `Patient.extension` slots that implementers can define locally if needed, and (c) introducing a jurisdictional extension creates an ongoing maintenance and documentation obligation that is not justified by the evidence of use. Recommend that interested implementers define the extension in their own IG (or a small supplementary AU IG) and request promotion to AU Base once there is demonstrated uptake.

#### Justification

The Zulip discussion explicitly surfaced that at least one major Australian product (Communicare) does not currently record this concept structurally, and that where it is recorded it lives in free-text narrative. The thread did not produce consensus, did not identify any naming-system sources, and did not surface implementer commitments to populate the element. AU Base extensions — once published — become part of the jurisdictional contract and are costly to deprecate; publishing one on the basis of a single strawman and a handful of "looks OK to me" comments risks either premature specification or orphaned content. Declining does not foreclose the concept; it defers it until a concrete implementer need drives the design.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Accepting the request as originally drafted is the lowest-risk, highest-signal move the work group can make. The data is unambiguously real (multiple connectathon participants, including a product vendor, acknowledged the concept), the design is trivially small (one extension, one `valueString`, `Patient` context), and the non-codeability of the content is not a temporary state of the world — it is, as Grahame noted, an intrinsic property of how patients self-describe community affiliation, especially once non-indigenous/immigrant cultural groups are folded in. Disposition B's future-proofing argument is reasonable on its face, but `CodeableConcept.text` buys very little over `string` in practice and adds structural complexity that implementers routinely get wrong; if a codeable sub-portion of this space ever materialises, it can be introduced later as a sibling extension without breaking deployed `string` data. Disposition C over-indexes on the absence of implementer uptake pre-publication — this is exactly the kind of low-cost jurisdictional extension that AU Base exists to seed. The work group should proceed to publish the extension and invite feedback from AU Core implementers in the next cycle.
