## Summary

Virginia Lorenzi reports that the Scalable Consent Management (FHIR) IG
is internally inconsistent about whether it concerns *consent to access*
or *consent to share* data. The IG's high-level definition frames it as
"consent to access data," but the body of the specification reads as if
it is governing permission to share data with external parties. The
reporter notes that, in New York, both designs are in use — national
networks tend to use "consent to share" while local HIEs use "consent to
access" — so the ambiguity has practical implementation consequences.
She asks the workgroup to clarify which (or whether both) is intended.

No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, GitHub items, or
Confluence pages were surfaced for this ticket; the search and
cross-reference indexes returned zero hits in every direction. The
ticket already carries a "Persuasive" resolution on Jira indicating the
workgroup has accepted the request and proposed a wording change (see
Resolution under Details).

## Details

**Description:**

> The definition says this spec is about consent to access data.
> However, from reading the rest of the spec, it feels like it means
> consent to share data. Access sounds like accessing from external
> provider, share would be permission to share externally. Or perhaps
> you mean both. In NY there are varying designs of this employed. The
> national networks use consent to share while the local HIEs use
> consent to access. Please clarify what you are referring to.

**Resolution (already recorded on the ticket, 2026-02-20 — Persuasive):**

> We believe that sharing is a sub-type of access. We will change the
> phrase "pertaining to granting access to data associated with a
> patient" to be "pertaining to granting access to data, *potentially
> including sharing,* associated with a patient".

**Comments:**

- **david.pyke (2026-02-18):** Possibly add "or sharing" to the Consent
  definition.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the wording already recorded in the Jira "Persuasive" resolution
verbatim in the Scalable Consent Management (FHIR) IG: change the
definitional phrase "*pertaining to granting access to data associated
with a patient*" to "*pertaining to granting access to data, **potentially
including sharing,** associated with a patient*". Apply this in the IG's
scope/definition narrative (and any duplicated copies in the spec home
page or executive summary). Treat "share" as a sub-type of "access" for
the purposes of this IG and add a one-sentence parenthetical making that
relationship explicit, so implementers in both the national-network
("consent to share") and local-HIE ("consent to access") camps can map
their model onto the IG.

#### Justification

This is the disposition the workgroup has effectively already chosen on
the ticket (Resolution = Persuasive, with the exact replacement
wording). It is the smallest change that resolves the ambiguity Virginia
Lorenzi raised, it preserves the IG's existing "access"-centric
vocabulary throughout the body of the spec (avoiding a costly find-and-
replace), and David Pyke's comment ("possibly add 'or sharing' to
Consent definition") supports the same direction. Framing share as a
sub-type of access is consistent with how FHIR `Consent` itself models
permissions broadly over data flows rather than partitioning
"access" and "share" as disjoint operations.

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than expanding the single definition with an inline
parenthetical, add a short "Scope: access vs. sharing" subsection near
the top of the IG (and link to it from the home-page definition). The
subsection would: (1) state explicitly that the IG covers *both*
internal access decisions and onward sharing to external parties, (2)
define the two terms as the workgroup uses them, (3) cite the NY
national-network vs. local-HIE example from the ticket as motivation,
and (4) point out which IG profiles/use-cases primarily exercise each
mode. Leave the existing "access" wording in the body intact but add a
small glossary entry for "share" that defers to the new subsection.

#### Justification

Inline edits to a single definition (Disposition A) clarify that
sentence but don't address the deeper concern Virginia raised — that
*the rest of the spec* reads as if it is about sharing. A dedicated
scope subsection makes the dual-purpose framing discoverable to a
reader who never re-reads the opening definition, and gives implementers
a stable anchor to cite when explaining their deployment model. The
trade-off is a larger editorial change and the need for the workgroup
to commit to a clear glossary entry, but it pays back across every
future Conceptual ticket against this IG. Backward compatibility is
identical to A (no normative artifact changes).

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change as out-of-scope clarification: keep the existing
"consent to access" framing and treat the ticket as resolved by
implementer education (e.g., a FAQ entry on the IG home page or a Zulip
pinned message) rather than a spec edit.

#### Justification

This would be defensible only if the workgroup believes the existing
text is unambiguous to a careful reader and that the NY-specific
distinction between "access" and "share" is a deployment concern rather
than a specification concern. It is the weakest of the three options
because the ticket itself documents a real, named implementer who read
the spec and was unsure which model it intended; declining preserves
that ambiguity for the next reader. Cite only if the workgroup wants to
avoid touching definitional text during a stabilization window.

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A is recommended because it matches the resolution the
workgroup has already recorded on the ticket (Persuasive, with the exact
replacement wording), it is the lowest-risk edit that directly answers
Virginia Lorenzi's question, and David Pyke's comment endorses the same
direction. Disposition B is the better long-term answer if the workgroup
sees this ticket as one of a pattern of "what does this IG actually
cover" questions — in that case, treat A as the immediate fix and open
a follow-up to do B in the next publication cycle. Disposition C should
only be selected if the workgroup explicitly decides the spec text is
already clear, which the existence of this ticket argues against.
