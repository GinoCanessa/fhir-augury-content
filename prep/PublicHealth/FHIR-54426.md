## Summary

The submitter (a NAACCR community member, filed on their behalf by AshleyKnealing)
argues that the US Central Cancer Registry Reporting (CCRR) Content IG should
strengthen the conformance of the Patient address in the CCRR Patient profile.
Specifically, they propose that `Patient.address.line` (street),
`Patient.address.city`, and `Patient.address.postalCode` (zip) be made
**required (`Should`/SHALL be supported, with `dataAbsentReason` permitted when
truly unavailable)** rather than optional. The reporter notes that EHRs almost
always carry address data for billing, that the current cardinality is weaker
than the conformance the IG already places on telephone and email (which they
view as inconsistent), and that complete address is critical for central cancer
registries to perform record linkage on incoming reports.

This request is a direct extension of the previously-published change
[FHIR-35131](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-35131) (resolved *Persuasive*,
*Block-Vote-2*), which already tightened `Patient.name.family` to 1..1,
`Patient.name.given` to 1..* and `Patient.address.state` to 1..1, all paired
with `dataAbsentReason`, "as requested by the central cancer registries."
FHIR-54426 effectively asks the workgroup to finish that pattern for the
remaining address sub-elements (line, city, postalCode). It is also part of a
small cluster of CCRR triaged tickets in the same window asking for tighter
required-data semantics for cancer reporting — see also
[FHIR-54408](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54408) (patient identifiers) and
[FHIR-54607](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54607) (vital status as a
required initial-report element).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Tighten the cardinality of the address sub-elements on the **CCRR Patient
profile** (the same profile already modified by FHIR-35131) as follows, and in
each case add `dataAbsentReason` so EHRs that genuinely lack the value can
still produce conformant instances:

- `Patient.address.line`: 1..* required, with `dataAbsentReason` permitted.
- `Patient.address.city`: 1..1 required, with `dataAbsentReason` permitted.
- `Patient.address.postalCode`: 1..1 required, with `dataAbsentReason`
  permitted.
- Mark each as `mustSupport = true` consistent with the rest of the CCRR
  Patient profile.
- Leave `Patient.address.state` unchanged (already 1..1 + `dataAbsentReason`
  per FHIR-35131) and `Patient.address.country` unchanged unless the work
  group wants to discuss US-only assumptions.

Update the corresponding profile narrative / "Patient" intro page in the IG to
explain why the central registries require complete address and how the
`dataAbsentReason` escape hatch should be used.

#### Justification

This is a direct, scoped extension of the precedent already accepted as
*Persuasive* in [FHIR-35131](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-35131). The work
group has already conceded the design pattern (tighten cardinality + permit
`dataAbsentReason`) for `Patient.name.family`, `Patient.name.given` and
`Patient.address.state` "as requested by the central cancer registries."
Adopting the same pattern for `address.line`, `city` and `postalCode` is
internally consistent, addresses the reporter's explicit complaint that the
current profile is harder on telephone/email than on street address, and
materially helps record-linkage at the receiving registry — the use case the
IG exists to support. The `dataAbsentReason` mechanism preserves the safety
valve for EHRs that genuinely cannot supply the value, so the change does not
create a hard reportability blocker.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the published cardinalities at 0..1 / 0..* but add a **slice on
`Patient.address`** in the CCRR Patient profile that requires a `home` /
`postal` address with all of `line`, `city`, `state`, `postalCode` populated
(or with `dataAbsentReason`). Pair this with an explicit invariant on the
profile (e.g., `ccrr-patient-address-1`) that states "If
`Patient.address.use = home` (or no use) then `line`, `city`, `state`, and
`postalCode` SHALL be present or carry `dataAbsentReason`," and bump
`mustSupport` on those sub-elements. Leave the bare element cardinalities
alone so a Patient resource carrying only a work or temporary address is still
valid.

#### Justification

This addresses the underlying registry-linkage need without raising the
cardinality floor on the bare element paths, which keeps the profile
compatible with sources that legitimately exchange other address kinds (work,
temp, billing) and avoids the slightly awkward situation of *every* address
slice — even a temporary address — being forced to carry city/zip. It is
arguably a better fit for FHIR's "address may have several uses" model than a
flat 1..1 on every sub-element. Trade-off vs. Disposition A: it is more
expressive but harder to validate and harder to communicate to implementers
than the simple "tighten cardinality + dataAbsentReason" pattern the IG has
already established for name and state — which is a real cost in a Public
Health IG aimed at EHR vendors.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request and keep the current CCRR Patient address cardinalities
unchanged. Rationale category: **already addressable by existing mechanisms**
— central registries that need stricter conformance can publish a
derived/jurisdictional profile of CCRR Patient that tightens
`address.line`/`city`/`postalCode` themselves, without requiring the base US
CCRR IG to do so. Recommend updating the IG's narrative to point to that
profiling pattern.

#### Justification

A decline is defensible if the work group concludes that (a) the comment
provides only one stakeholder voice (a single NAACCR member, no Zulip
discussion, no co-signers in the comment thread), (b) tightening cardinality
in a *base* US national IG forecloses on jurisdictions where complete address
genuinely cannot be obtained for some reportable cohorts (unhoused patients,
international patients receiving care in the US, etc.), and (c) the existing
FHIR profiling story already lets registries publish a stricter derivative.
The weakest part of this position is that the same arguments could have been
raised against FHIR-35131 and were not — the work group already accepted the
"tighten + `dataAbsentReason`" pattern there — so declining here is hard to
justify on consistency grounds.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A — Accept As Requested.

The IG has already accepted the exact same change pattern in
[FHIR-35131](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-35131) for `name.family`,
`name.given` and `address.state` "as requested by the central cancer
registries." Refusing to extend the same pattern to `address.line`, `city` and
`postalCode` would leave the CCRR Patient profile internally inconsistent —
exactly the inconsistency the reporter calls out (telephone/email today have
stronger conformance than street address). Disposition A is also the lowest-
cost change for the work group: it reuses an existing, already-balloted
mechanism (`mustSupport` + tighter cardinality + `dataAbsentReason`), it
matches FHIR-54607's "make registry-required data actually required at
submission" theme, and the `dataAbsentReason` escape hatch preserves
conformance for EHRs that legitimately lack the value. Disposition B is more
elegant in pure FHIR terms but adds slicing/invariant complexity that this IG
has so far avoided on the Patient profile, and Disposition C is hard to
defend against the FHIR-35131 precedent. The work group should adopt
Disposition A and, if it wants to address the multi-address-use concern, do so
narratively in the profile guidance rather than by holding the cardinality
change.
