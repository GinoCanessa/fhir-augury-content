## Summary

FHIR-28533 is an enhancement request asking the Public Health work group to
keep the **Occupational Data for Health (ODH) FHIR IG** aligned with the
**HL7 Military Service History IG** by reusing a common base/abstract profile
for an "Employment History Episode" rather than each IG inventing its own
parallel structure. The reporter, Ioana Singureanu, points to
`StructureDefinition-employmentHistoryEpisode` in the Military Service IG as
the profile intended for reuse, and proposes that ODH derive (or otherwise
align) its episode-of-employment profile from that abstract base so both IGs
stay in sync over time.

The single committer comment (lfourquet, 2021-04-08) acknowledged the request
and indicated it would be addressed in the next *full* release of ODH (not the
contemporaneous dot release). Since then, the abstract base profile has in
fact been established and consumed on the Military Service side: a related
ticket against the Military Service IG, [FHIR-33434](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-33434)
("Conflicting basis for Military Service Episode"), was resolved **Persuasive**
in April 2022 with the disposition "Will update the profile parent to reflect
ODH employment episode," and downstream tickets such as
[FHIR-33439](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-33439) and
[FHIR-33435](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-33435) explicitly describe an
abstract Employment History Episode profile sitting above both the Military
Service Episode and ODH Past or Present Job profiles. From the ODH side,
however, this ticket itself remains in **Triaged** status with no recorded
resolution, so the workgroup needs to decide whether to formally close it as
already addressed, scope a remaining ODH-side alignment task, or decline.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the abstract Employment History Episode pattern from the Military
Service IG on the **ODH** side: change the `odh-PastOrPresentJob` profile
(in `HL7/fhir-occupational-data`) so its `baseDefinition` is the Military
Service IG's `StructureDefinition/employmentHistoryEpisode` rather than
`Observation` directly, and add the canonical reference plus a dependency on
the Military Service IG in ODH's `ImplementationGuide` resource. Update
ODH's narrative pages (Background / Profile pages for PastOrPresentJob) to
state that the profile derives from the cross-IG abstract base, and add a
regression note in the ODH IG's release notes.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter asked for and is consistent with the
existing pattern already adopted by the Military Service IG (see
[FHIR-33434](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-33434),
[FHIR-33435](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-33435)). Aligning derivations
gives implementers a single, stable parent for both military and civilian
employment-episode observations and matches the long-standing FHIR design
principle of factoring shared structure into a parent profile rather than
maintaining parallel sibling profiles. The Military Service IG already
advertises ODH Past or Present Job as a derived profile, so this disposition
makes the published documentation true on the ground.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — Close as "Already Addressed" with a Documentation Hook

#### Proposal

Resolve FHIR-28533 as **Already Addressed** (no normative change to ODH).
Rationale: the abstract Employment History Episode profile has been
established and is in use on the Military Service IG side
([FHIR-33434](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-33434) Persuasive,
[FHIR-33435](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-33435) / [FHIR-33439](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-33439)
Persuasive). Instead of changing ODH's profile derivation chain, add a
non-normative "Relationship to Military Service IG" section to the ODH IG
home / background page that explicitly documents the cross-IG mapping
(ODH `PastOrPresentJob` ↔ Military Service `EmploymentHistoryEpisode`) and
links the canonical URL. No change to ODH's `baseDefinition`.

#### Justification

ODH is a published US Realm IG with downstream implementers; changing the
`baseDefinition` of `PastOrPresentJob` is a structural change that ripples
through validation, profile snapshots, and any tools that walk the parent
chain — non-trivial backward-compat risk for what is fundamentally an
alignment goal. The reporter's underlying concern — "ensure ODH and the
Military Service IG stay in sync" — is satisfiable with explicit
documentation cross-links plus continued joint review of changes; the
Military Service IG already names ODH as a consumer, so the relationship is
asymmetric but coherent.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve as **Not Persuasive** / decline. Treat the two IGs as independently
governed: ODH's `PastOrPresentJob` continues to derive from `Observation`,
the Military Service IG's profiles continue to do whatever their work group
sees fit, and any "alignment" is left to implementation guidance authored
by each IG independently.

#### Justification

A cross-IG abstract base profile creates a hard publication-order
dependency: ODH would have to consume an artifact owned by another work
group (Cross-Group Projects / Military Service), pin a version, and
re-publish whenever the parent moves. For an IG as widely deployed as ODH
that coupling is a real cost. The community has already lived with the
divergence for several years without observable harm, and the original
reporter's only stated motivation is "stay in sync," which can be addressed
through governance rather than profile inheritance.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **B — Close as "Already Addressed" with a documentation hook.**

The substantive technical request (create a reusable abstract Employment
History Episode profile) has been satisfied: it lives in the Military
Service IG, is published, and is referenced by tickets resolved Persuasive
in 2022. Re-parenting ODH's `PastOrPresentJob` (Disposition A) would
deliver formal symmetry but at the cost of introducing a normative
dependency from a widely-implemented US Realm IG onto a sibling IG owned
by a different work group, with non-trivial impact on snapshots, validation,
and version coordination — and the original ticket explicitly frames the
goal as "staying in sync," not as profile inheritance per se. Disposition C
ignores that the workgroup did agree the two IGs should be coordinated and
leaves the ticket unresolved after five years. Disposition B closes the
ticket honestly, captures the cross-IG relationship in ODH's own narrative
where implementers will actually find it, and leaves the door open for a
future, separately-scoped re-parenting ticket if implementer feedback shows
the documentation-only approach is insufficient.
