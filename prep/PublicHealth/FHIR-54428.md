## Summary

The reporter — submitting on behalf of a NAACCR community member — raises a
concern about the use of `Data Absent Reason` (DAR) on the SSN identifier
slice of the patient profile in the **US Central Cancer Registry Reporting
Content IG (CCRR)**. Their position is that fewer practitioners now
collect SSN, that whether the SSN exists is essentially binary against the
EHR (it is either present or it is not), and that requiring providers to
indicate *why* SSN is absent — when SSN itself is not a required element —
imposes an unnecessary documentation burden. The ticket is a Question
rather than a change request, asking the workgroup to reconsider whether
DAR on SSN is appropriate for CCRR.

The PHWG has already triaged the issue and proposes the disposition
**"Considered – Question answered / Not Persuasive"** on the grounds that
DAR on identifiers is inherited from the parent **US Public Health
Library (USPHL) Patient profile** and cannot be removed without
re-parenting CCRR Patient (e.g., to US Core Patient), which would forfeit
extensions and constraints CCRR depends on. The workgroup also argues that
DAR remains optional ("should" semantics, not required) and that
rules-driven population by sender systems should reduce, not increase,
implementer burden.

There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub
items associated with this ticket in the knowledge base, so the analysis
below is based solely on the ticket content, its single PHWG comment,
and the resolution narrative.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Remove the `dataAbsentReason` extension from the SSN identifier slice on
the CCRR Patient profile. Concretely, this would mean adding a
profile-level constraint on `Patient.identifier:SSN.extension` that
prohibits (or zeroes-out) the
`http://hl7.org/fhir/StructureDefinition/data-absent-reason` extension
for that specific slice, while leaving DAR available on other identifier
slices (MR, etc.). Because the extension is inherited from the USPHL
Patient profile, this would require either (a) overriding the slice with
a tighter constraint in the CCRR profile, or (b) re-parenting CCRR
Patient off USPHL Patient and onto US Core Patient (or base FHIR
Patient), then re-adding only the USPHL-derived constraints CCRR
actually needs. Update the CCRR Block 1 narrative pages to document the
SSN-specific behavior.

#### Justification

This honors the reporter's underlying concern that asking providers to
explain *why* a SSN is missing — for a non-required element that is
increasingly not collected at all — is a poor cost/benefit trade in a
cancer-registry reporting flow. Removing DAR for SSN keeps the IG aligned
with the practical reality the reporter describes (SSN is either in the
EHR or it isn't; "why not" is not meaningful information for the
registry).

#### Trade-offs

Option (a) — adding a slice-level constraint that overrides the parent —
is the lighter touch but creates a profile-inheritance inconsistency with
the rest of USPHL, which other USPHL-derived IGs do not share. Option
(b) — re-parenting — is structurally cleaner but is a substantial change
that loses USPHL's accumulated extensions/constraints and would need to
be backfilled. Both options break the "USPHL Patient is the consistent
parent for public health reporting" story the wider Public Health WG has
been building.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the inherited DAR slot intact (no profile structural change), but
**add explicit guidance in the CCRR Patient narrative** that for the SSN
identifier slice specifically:

1. SSN is optional and there is **no expectation** that senders provide
   `dataAbsentReason` when SSN is simply not collected.
2. `dataAbsentReason` should be populated only when the sending system
   has *meaningful* knowledge of why SSN is absent (e.g.,
   `asked-declined`, `masked`), not as a default.
3. Validators / IG publication tooling should not flag the absence of
   DAR on SSN as a warning.

Optionally, supply a small implementation note pointing to the
recommended `DataAbsentReason` codes that *do* make sense for SSN in a
cancer-registry context, and explicitly state which codes are *not*
expected (e.g., `unknown` is not informative for SSN).

#### Justification

This addresses the reporter's actual operational worry — that providers
will be asked to fill in DAR every time SSN is missing — without
disturbing the USPHL inheritance chain or the structural guarantees that
CCRR relies on for other identifier slices. It also matches the
workgroup's own observation that DAR is `should`, not `shall`: making
that explicit in narrative form, scoped to SSN, removes the perceived
ambiguity that drove the ticket. It is low-cost, ballot-friendly, and
preserves backward compatibility with USPHL-conformant implementations.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (Not Persuasive)

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Not Persuasive / Considered – Question Answered**,
matching the resolution already drafted in JIRAUSER30293's comment. No
profile changes; no narrative changes. Reply text would summarize the
five points already on the ticket: DAR is inherited from USPHL,
re-parenting would lose required CCRR constraints, DAR is optional
("should"), sender systems are expected to populate DAR via rules rather
than manual provider action, and DAR availability *reduces* validation
friction rather than adding it.

#### Justification

Defensible because (1) DAR is structurally optional, so no implementer is
*forced* to populate it; (2) the inheritance from USPHL is deliberate
and carries cross-IG benefits the workgroup has already accepted; (3) the
reporter's concern about "burden on providers" assumes DAR is captured at
the point of care, but in CCRR's batch-reporting context DAR is populated
by the sending system, not by clinicians; and (4) any change here would
ripple through every USPHL-derived IG, which is out of scope for a CCRR
ballot ticket.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **B (Alternative Approach)**

Disposition C is the workgroup's current direction and is technically
correct — nothing in the spec *requires* a provider to fill in DAR for
SSN. But the reporter's concern is plainly a *user-experience* concern,
not a conformance one: implementers (and the NAACCR community member
this was filed on behalf of) are reading the presence of `DataAbsentReason`
on the SSN slice as an implicit expectation, and "it's optional — go read
the conformance language" is a thin response that tends to come back as a
reconsideration request after ballot. Disposition A overcorrects:
re-parenting or selectively suppressing an inherited extension just to
satisfy one slice introduces structural debt across the USPHL-derived
profile family that will outlast this ticket. Disposition B threads the
needle — it leaves USPHL inheritance intact (preserving the workgroup's
five-point rationale verbatim) while adding the specific narrative
guidance the reporter is implicitly asking for: a clear statement that
*absent SSN does not require a DAR explanation in CCRR*, plus practical
guidance on which DAR codes are meaningful when DAR is supplied. That
turns the current "Not Persuasive" close into a "Considered – Persuasive
with Modification" outcome that addresses the underlying concern at near
zero structural cost. The workgroup can lift the existing comment text
nearly verbatim into the resolution, then add one paragraph of
SSN-specific narrative to the CCRR Patient page.
