## Summary

FHIR-15326 is a 2018 ballot-era change request against the (now-deprecated)
Electronic Case Reporting (eCR) FHIR IG. The submitter (Steve Eichner) flags
the IG's existing narrative — "Health Data Networks (HDNs) like the
eHealth Exchange, Carequality, CommonWell, and DirectTrust also may play
important roles" — and asks whether it should be updated to reflect the
"proposed framework recently announced," which in early 2018 referred to
the draft Trusted Exchange Framework and Common Agreement (TEFCA)
published by ONC. No specific replacement wording is proposed; the comment
is essentially a request that the editors consider mentioning TEFCA
alongside (or in place of) the listed HDNs. The ticket was triaged and
resolved as "Considered for Future Use" on 2018-08-08, i.e. parked for a
later revision rather than acted on in that ballot. There are no
cross-referenced Jira tickets and no GitHub items; the only
cross-reference is an automated Zulip tracker-item bot post echoing the
ticket title.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the eCR IG narrative paragraph that lists Health Data Networks so
that it mentions TEFCA explicitly. Concretely: in the section that
currently reads "Health Data Networks (HDNs) like the eHealth Exchange,
Carequality, CommonWell, and DirectTrust also may play important roles,"
add a sentence noting that the ONC Trusted Exchange Framework and Common
Agreement (TEFCA) — and the Qualified Health Information Networks (QHINs)
designated under it — provides an additional nationwide exchange pathway
that may carry eICR / RR content between providers and public health
authorities. Do **not** prescribe TEFCA as required transport; keep the
language descriptive and non-normative, consistent with the surrounding
HDN paragraph.

#### Justification

The original 2018 wording is now factually out of date: TEFCA has moved
from "proposed framework" to an operational program with designated
QHINs, and public health use cases (including eCR-style reporting) are
explicitly within scope of TEFCA's exchange purposes. Mentioning it
alongside the existing HDNs keeps the narrative current without changing
any conformance requirements or conformance resources, and aligns the
eCR guide with how implementers actually describe US nationwide exchange
in 2024+. This is a low-risk editorial change confined to narrative
prose.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than enumerating named exchange networks at all, generalize the
paragraph to describe the *category* of nationwide / regional exchange
infrastructures that may carry eCR content, and move the specific
network names (eHealth Exchange, Carequality, CommonWell, DirectTrust,
TEFCA/QHINs) into a non-normative footnote or appendix labeled
"Examples of US exchange infrastructures (informative, point-in-time)."
The body text would say something like: "eCR content may also flow
across nationwide and regional health information exchange
infrastructures; the specific networks and frameworks available evolve
over time — see Appendix X for current examples."

#### Justification

The underlying problem the ticket exposes is that the IG narrative
hard-codes a snapshot of the US exchange landscape, which then ages
poorly (TEFCA in 2018 vs. TEFCA in 2024 is the obvious case, but the
HDN list itself has shifted — e.g., CommonWell/Carequality alignment).
Pushing the named-network list into a clearly informative,
point-in-time appendix preserves the useful "here's what exists today"
information without making the normative-feeling narrative go stale on
every revision. This also avoids implying HL7 endorsement of any
particular network or framework.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline as **No Action — Obsolete Ticket**. The ticket targets the
*Electronic Case Reporting [deprecated] (FHIR)* IG, which is itself
flagged deprecated in the metadata; the request is a 2018 narrative
nit, was already resolved at that time as "Considered for Future Use,"
and the relevant work has migrated to the current eCR FHIR IG family
maintained by the Public Health WG. Note in the disposition that any
TEFCA-aware narrative update belongs in the *current* eCR IG, not in
the deprecated artifact this ticket is filed against, and recommend
that the WG open (or confirm there already is) an equivalent ticket
against the current IG if the narrative is still missing TEFCA.

#### Justification

Spending ballot bandwidth editing narrative in a deprecated IG provides
no value to implementers, who should not be reading or building against
the deprecated guide. The ticket has also already been dispositioned
once (2018-08-08, "Considered for Future Use") with no subsequent
activity for ~7 years, suggesting the WG has implicitly agreed it is
not worth reopening in its original location. Declining here keeps the
deprecated artifact frozen and routes the substantive concern (TEFCA
visibility in eCR narrative) to the active IG where it can actually
reach implementers.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** C

This ticket is a 2018 narrative comment against an IG whose
specification metadata is explicitly marked *deprecated*, and it was
already parked once as "Considered for Future Use" with no activity
since. Reworking the wording in the deprecated guide — whether via
Disposition A (add TEFCA) or Disposition B (generalize and move to an
appendix) — would not reach current eCR implementers, who are working
against the live eCR FHIR IG maintained by the Public Health WG. The
substantive idea is sound and worth carrying forward, so the WG should
decline this ticket as obsolete *and* confirm (or file) an equivalent
narrative-refresh ticket against the current eCR IG, where Disposition
B's "describe the category, list specific networks in an informative
appendix" pattern would be the right shape of fix. That approach
addresses TEFCA today without locking the narrative to whichever
exchange frameworks happen to exist at the next ballot.
