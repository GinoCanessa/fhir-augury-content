## Proposed Dispositions

Because no repo briefing is available, the dispositions below are framed
in terms of the BSeR IG editorial workflow (a structured review against
the TSMG checklist) rather than naming specific authoring-root file paths.
If/when the BSeR IG repo is added to Augury and analysed, the
Justifications should be revisited so that they reference the concrete
authoring root and terminology folder for the IG.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat this ticket as a substantive checklist sweep: the BSeR editor(s)
formally walk the BSeR IG against the TSMG "Terminology Expectations for
IG Developers" page and resolve each expectation in turn. Concretely:

1. Inventory every `CodeSystem`, `ValueSet`, and `ConceptMap` defined or
   referenced by the BSeR IG (and every `Coding`/`CodeableConcept`-typed
   element in BSeR profiles) and confirm that:
   - HL7-authored terminology is published on (or migrated to)
     Terminology.HL7.Org (THO), not maintained inline in the BSeR IG.
   - Canonical URLs follow the THO/HL7 conventions and are stable.
   - `CodeSystem.content`, `caseSensitive`, `hierarchyMeaning`, and
     `copyright` are populated correctly for each authored CodeSystem.
   - Each `ValueSet` either reuses an existing standard value set or
     justifies a new one, with intensional definitions where possible.
2. Audit every binding on BSeR profiles to confirm an explicit binding
   strength (`required`/`extensible`/`preferred`/`example`) consistent
   with the BSeR exchange semantics, and add `bindingName` plus a clear
   `description` where missing.
3. For any externally-owned terminologies referenced (LOINC, SNOMED CT,
   ICD-10-CM, RxNorm, CVX, NUCC, etc.), confirm copyright statements,
   versioning policy, and any required IP notes are present.
4. Record the outcome of the sweep as comments on FHIR-42191 (and as
   follow-on tickets where individual gaps are found) and flip the
   ticket to `Persuasive`/`Resolved` once complete. Tag the ticket
   `BSeR_THO` items as fixed in the next BSeR publication.

#### Justification

The reporter asked a yes/no compliance question, but the only useful
yes/no answer is one backed by an actual review against the TSMG page.
A structured, item-by-item walk of the checklist is the cheapest way to
produce that answer, and it directly serves the `BSeR_THO` label, which
already commits BSeR to aligning its terminology with THO conventions.
This disposition also creates a durable artifact (the comment trail and
any follow-on tickets) that future BSeR ballots can point at when the
same question is asked again.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Convert this ticket into a tracking/parent ticket and split the work into
discrete, file-scoped child tickets, one per category in the TSMG
checklist (e.g., "BSeR – migrate authored CodeSystems to THO", "BSeR –
add binding strengths and bindingName to all CodeableConcept elements",
"BSeR – add copyright/IP notices for SNOMED/LOINC/ICD usage", "BSeR –
collapse inline value sets into THO-published value sets"). Each child
ticket names the specific BSeR IG inputs it touches, an owner, and a
target ballot cycle. FHIR-42191 itself is then resolved as
`Considered for Future Use` or `Persuasive with Modification`, with
the resolution text linking to the child tickets.

#### Justification

The original ticket bundles many independent compliance questions into
one. Decomposing them gives the BSeR work group a backlog they can
schedule, lets reviewers verify each category independently, and avoids
the failure mode where a single ticket lingers in `Triaged` because no
one wants to claim the entire TSMG checklist at once. It also surfaces
which BSeR_THO items are genuinely contentious (likely the LOINC/SNOMED
licensing notes and any inline value sets that need to move to THO)
rather than hiding them behind a single yes/no answer. The trade-off
versus Disposition A is process overhead: more tickets to triage, but
materially better tracking of actual conformance work.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve as `Not Persuasive` on the basis that FHIR-42191 is a generic
"have you done your homework?" comment rather than an actionable defect:
no specific BSeR artifact, terminology, or binding is identified as
non-conformant. Direct the reporter to file targeted change requests
against the specific BSeR pages, profiles, value sets, or code systems
where they believe the TSMG expectations are not met, and close this
ticket without a content change.

#### Justification

Triaging an open-ended compliance question consumes work-group cycles
that are better spent on substantive defects, and any "we reviewed it"
answer that is not itself backed by a per-artifact audit is impossible
to verify. The TSMG page is publicly available to the BSeR editors at
all times, and BSeR has the `BSeR_THO` label precisely to track concrete
THO-alignment work; this ticket adds no information beyond what that
label already conveys. Declining keeps the ticket queue focused while
preserving the reporter's ability to file specific, actionable issues.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The ticket is meaningful — TSMG's expectations really do need to be met
by every HL7 IG, and BSeR has explicitly committed to that work via the
`BSeR_THO` label — but as written it is too coarse to act on as a single
unit (Disposition A) and too substantive to discard (Disposition C).
Splitting it into per-category child tickets gives the Public Health
work group a credible compliance plan, lets editors close out individual
items as they finish them, and produces an audit trail that future
ballots and the TSMG itself can verify against. The recommendation also
matches how FHIR work groups typically handle "review against checklist"
ballot comments on other large IGs (US Core, IPS, etc.), where the
checklist is decomposed into trackable work items rather than answered
in a single comment.
