## Summary

The ticket asks the US Central Cancer Registry Reporting (CCRR) Content
IG to leverage content from the mCODE Implementation Guide, noting that
mCODE had advanced to STU3 and that the CDC cancer team wished to reuse
mCODE-defined content within CCRR rather than redefine cancer
data-element semantics independently. The request is scoped to the CCRR
IG and is a directional ask (adopt mCODE profiles where they apply)
rather than a list of specific element-level changes. There are no
cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, Confluence pages, or
GitHub items recorded for this ticket; the only context beyond the
description is the recorded resolution, which states that CCRR v2.0 was
in fact updated to use mCODE 4.0 profiles where applicable, superseding
the original STU3-era request.

## Details

## Proposed Dispositions

The ticket has already been resolved on Jira as **Persuasive with
Modification**, with CCRR v2.0 reusing mCODE 4.0 profiles. The
dispositions below frame the workgroup discussion in terms of
ratifying, broadening, or narrowing that resolution.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Ratify the recorded resolution: confirm that the CCRR IG depends on and
reuses mCODE profiles where applicable. Concretely, in the next CCRR
publication cycle, declare an explicit IG dependency on mCODE (current
published version), and where CCRR profiles cover concepts already
modeled by mCODE (e.g., `CancerPatient`, `PrimaryCancerCondition`,
`SecondaryCancerCondition`, `CancerStageGroup`,
`TNMClinicalStageGroup`, `CancerDiseaseStatus`, cancer-related
procedure / medication profiles, tumor observations), derive CCRR
profiles from the corresponding mCODE profiles rather than re-deriving
from the base FHIR resource. Document each reuse point and any CCRR-
specific differential constraints in the CCRR IG narrative.

#### Justification

This matches the reporter's intent and the recorded "Persuasive with
Modification" resolution: a single oncology semantic model (mCODE) is
maintained by the Cancer Interoperability community, and CCRR can
inherit those constraints rather than maintain a parallel set. Reuse
reduces implementation burden for systems that already speak mCODE
(EHRs, oncology registries) and shrinks CCRR's authoring/maintenance
surface. The "Modification" piece is captured by pinning to the mCODE
version actually consumed (mCODE 4.0 in v2.0) and by documenting the
delta in the CCRR IG, rather than blanket adoption of every mCODE
profile.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Treat mCODE as a *reference* model rather than a hard dependency: where
CCRR needs the same concept mCODE addresses, CCRR profiles cite the
mCODE profile as a comparison/derivation source in narrative and align
element bindings, but CCRR continues to derive its profiles directly
from the base FHIR resource. CCRR would publish a structured mapping
(e.g., a ConceptMap-style or narrative crosswalk page) between each
CCRR profile and the equivalent mCODE profile, and would coordinate
with the mCODE work group on terminology bindings and slicing
patterns, but would not pin a hard `dependsOn` against a specific
mCODE version.

#### Justification

CCRR is a registry-reporting IG with a fixed cadence driven by NAACCR
and CDC release schedules; mCODE evolves on its own cadence with
substantive normative changes between STU versions (STU3 → 4.0 →
later). A hard dependency forces CCRR to either (a) republish whenever
mCODE republishes or (b) ship against an older mCODE that is no longer
the community-current version. A reference-only alignment keeps CCRR's
publication cycle decoupled while still preserving semantic
interoperability with mCODE-aware systems through documented mapping.
It also avoids constraint conflicts where mCODE's must-support /
slicing decisions are appropriate for clinical-care exchange but not
for registry reporting (which has different completeness rules).

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request as stated and instead note that CCRR will continue
to model cancer-registry-reporting concepts directly against base FHIR
resources, without a formal mCODE dependency or alignment mapping.
Mark the ticket as superseded by the v2.0 work and close without
introducing further mCODE-specific structure.

#### Justification

This is the most defensible position only if the workgroup judges that
mCODE's clinical-care framing is materially mismatched with registry
reporting (e.g., differing cardinalities, must-support semantics,
terminology bindings) and that a CCRR-native model is clearer for
implementers whose primary obligation is NAACCR/state registry
submission rather than oncology care exchange. Given that the recorded
resolution explicitly *did* adopt mCODE 4.0 in v2.0, this disposition
should only be selected if the workgroup wishes to revisit and reverse
that decision for v3.0+.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket has already been resolved as Persuasive with Modification
and the resolution narrative confirms that CCRR v2.0 uses mCODE 4.0
profiles where applicable, so the workgroup's discussion is really
about ratifying that direction for ongoing maintenance. Disposition A
formalizes what was actually done and gives implementers a single,
clear story: CCRR reuses mCODE oncology semantics, with CCRR-specific
deltas documented. Disposition B's release-cadence concern is real but
manageable through an explicit version pin (the same mechanism CCRR
already used by anchoring on mCODE 4.0) and by coordinating
publication windows with the Cancer Interoperability work group;
documenting the mapping without a hard dependency would re-fragment
the oncology profile space the reporter explicitly wanted to avoid.
Disposition C contradicts the recorded resolution and would require
unwinding v2.0 work without a stated technical driver. The workgroup
should ratify A and, in doing so, decide the cadence/version-pin
policy that addresses Disposition B's legitimate concern about mCODE
release timing.
