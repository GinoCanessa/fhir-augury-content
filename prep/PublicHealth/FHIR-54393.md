## Summary

The ticket, submitted on behalf of a NAACCR community member against the US
Central Cancer Registry Reporting (CCRR) Content IG, requests that the IG
require a date/time on laboratory test result observations used in the
cancer-registry use case. The reporter notes that "specimen collection date"
is not the same as "procedure date" — particularly important when procedure
date is mapped to NAACCR data item #1200 (RX Date Surgery). They observe that
the US Core Laboratory Result Observation Profile already supports
`Observation.effective[x]` (and a `period` with start/end on associated
Specimens) and ask the IG authors to make the date-time expectation for lab
values explicit in the use-case narrative. No related Jira tickets, Zulip
threads, or GitHub items were surfaced via cross-reference or search, so the
request stands alone in the available context.

## Details

**Resolution (Not Persuasive — recorded on the snapshot):**
The work group has decided to leave the specimen collection date for the
laboratory observation, the procedure date, and the medication date elements
as-is for now, with willingness to revisit if reporting issues are observed.
Cited reasons:

- Specimen-collected data often originates from the lab system, so the IG is
  dependent on what labs supply; adding burden on EHRs to document why it is
  unavailable is undesirable.
- EHRs typically send specimen-collection info at requisition time and the
  lab returns it alongside the result, but this hand-off is a known fragile
  point in lab workflows.
- The specimen-collected data element is not mapped to any NAACCR item.
- Restricting these dates as proposed could prevent otherwise-useful reports
  from reaching registries when dates are missing.
- EHRs are expected to send procedure and medication dates whenever
  available; some legitimate scenarios (older or external data) may lack
  them.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the CCRR IG so that, for the Laboratory Test Result use-case
observation profile, `Observation.effective[x]` is constrained to
`effectiveDateTime | effectivePeriod`, with cardinality `1..1` and
must-support, and the use-case narrative is amended to spell out:

1. The lab observation MUST carry a date/time (or period) for the result
   itself, distinct from `Specimen.collection.collected[x]` and from any
   `Procedure.performed[x]` (which feeds NAACCR #1200 RX Date Surgery).
2. When a `Specimen` is referenced from the observation, its
   `collection.collected[x]` SHALL also be populated, mirroring the US Core
   Laboratory Result Observation Profile's expectations.
3. Mapping guidance is added to clarify which date populates which NAACCR
   item, so RX Date Surgery is never silently sourced from a lab/specimen
   date.

#### Justification

This directly addresses the reporter's concern that ambiguous date semantics
risk a specimen or lab date being mapped into NAACCR #1200 (RX Date Surgery),
which is clinically misleading. US Core already requires must-support on
`Observation.effective[x]` for lab results, so tightening cardinality from
`0..1` to `1..1` in this IG is consistent with US Core practice and with the
analytical needs of central cancer registries, where date precision is
foundational to incidence, treatment, and outcome reporting.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep cardinality on `Observation.effective[x]` at the US Core baseline
(`0..1`, must-support) but add an IG-level invariant and narrative guidance:

- Invariant on the Laboratory Test Result profile:
  `Observation.effective.exists() or Observation.issued.exists()` (or a
  similarly worded constraint), so that at least one authoritative timestamp
  accompanies every lab observation submitted to a registry.
- Use-case narrative additions:
  - A glossary distinguishing "specimen collection date", "result date",
    "procedure date", and "RX Date Surgery (NAACCR #1200)".
  - A mapping table showing exactly which FHIR element feeds each NAACCR
    date item, with explicit "do NOT source #1200 from
    `Specimen.collection.collected[x]` or `Observation.effective[x]`".
- Optional: a CapabilityStatement / ImplementationGuide-level requirement
  that submitters populate `Observation.effective[x]` whenever known.

#### Justification

This honours the underlying intent (no ambiguity about which date is which
when feeding NAACCR) while preserving submitter flexibility for the legacy
and external-data scenarios the work-group flagged in their resolution
rationale (older records, lab/EHR hand-off gaps). An invariant is a softer
mechanism than 1..1 cardinality, lets implementers fall back to `issued` when
`effective[x]` is genuinely unknown, and avoids hard-failing reports the
registry might still find useful.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Persist with the current resolution of **Not Persuasive**: leave specimen
collection date, procedure date, and medication date elements as currently
profiled in the IG, and revisit only if real-world reporting reveals concrete
problems traceable to the looser cardinality.

#### Justification

The work group's posted rationale — that specimen-collected info often
originates from lab systems outside EHR control, that the lab/EHR hand-off
for that field is a known fragile workflow, that the data element is not
mapped to any NAACCR item, and that tightening cardinality risks blocking
otherwise-valid registry reports — is a defensible answer to the reporter's
concern. Date semantics can be clarified through narrative and IG
educational material without changing profile constraints, and the work
group has explicitly committed to revisiting if implementation experience
shows the looser approach is causing harm.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The reporter's central worry is semantic conflation — that a specimen or
laboratory date could end up populating NAACCR #1200 (RX Date Surgery). That
is a real interoperability hazard and is best addressed by the IG making the
distinctions explicit, not by leaving them implicit (as Disposition C does)
and not by hard-constraining the cardinality (Disposition A) in ways that
collide with the work group's stated concerns about lab/EHR workflow gaps
and external/legacy data. Disposition B captures the reporter's intent —
every lab observation should carry an authoritative timestamp and the
mapping to NAACCR date items should be unambiguous — while staying
compatible with US Core baseline cardinality and with the legitimate
fallback scenarios the work group cited. It also gives the work group a
clear, low-risk path to "do more than nothing" without rewriting the
existing must-support pattern.
