## Summary

The submitter, on behalf of a NAACCR community member, asks the US
Central Cancer Registry Reporting (CCRR) Content IG to strengthen the
constraints on patient name in its CCRR Patient profile. Their reasoning
is that the cardinality already implies the element is required, and
that an EHR in an ambulatory practice always has a patient name on
file, so the IG should disallow data-absent and require the value. The
specific ask is to set `Patient.name` to cardinality `1..1`,
`mustSupport = true`, and to forbid `dataAbsentReason`.

The ticket has been Triaged with a resolution of *Considered - No action
required*. A workgroup reviewer (JIRAUSER30293) recommended a "not
persuasive" disposition citing broader use cases (non-CoC hospitals),
international name conventions, alignment with the parent profile for
interoperability, situations where the name is not yet known when the
report is generated, and the fact that `mustSupport` already guarantees
the element flows through when present. No cross-referenced Jira
tickets, Zulip threads, GitHub items, or Confluence pages were returned
by the FhirAugury cross-reference and search APIs for this key.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the CCRR Patient profile (the profile that the IG layers on top of
its parent — most likely US Core Patient), tighten `Patient.name` to:

- `min = 1`, `max = 1` (cardinality `1..1`).
- `mustSupport = true` (already true; reaffirm).
- Add a constraint forbidding `Patient.name.extension` of type
  `data-absent-reason`, and document that
  `Patient.name.family.extension(data-absent-reason)` and
  `Patient.name.given.extension(data-absent-reason)` are likewise
  disallowed in CCRR submissions.
- Update narrative to state that a patient name is required at the
  time of CCRR report generation, and that submissions lacking a name
  are non-conformant.

#### Justification

The submitter is correct that, in the central cancer registry
reporting workflow, a name is operationally available: registries
match cases on identity, and reports without a name create
de-duplication problems downstream. Setting `1..1` and disallowing
`dataAbsentReason` makes the IG's expectations explicit instead of
relying on `mustSupport` semantics, which only oblige producers to be
*able* to send the element. This is consistent with how other
public-health IGs constrain identity on the reporting side
(e.g., eCR profiles tightening Patient identifiers). It also aligns
the IG with the submitter's NAACCR-side expectation that name be
treated as a hard requirement.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Leave `Patient.name` cardinality and `dataAbsentReason` as inherited
from the parent profile, but add a CCRR-specific **invariant** on the
Patient profile that requires *at least one* `Patient.name` entry with
either a `family` or a `given` value populated when the report is
submitted (rather than at the moment the patient record is created).
Express this as a FHIRPath invariant such as:

```
Patient.name.where(family.exists() or given.exists()).exists()
```

with severity `error` and a human-readable message explaining the CCRR
submission rule. Optionally, add a `mustSupport` flag on `family` and
`given` while keeping their cardinality permissive, and document in
narrative that internationally-formatted names (single-token names,
mononyms, etc.) satisfy the invariant via `text` plus either component.

#### Justification

This addresses the submitter's underlying concern — that CCRR reports
should not flow through with a missing name — while preserving
interoperability with the parent Patient profile and with international
naming conventions raised by the workgroup reviewer. An invariant is
the right mechanism when the constraint is *report-time* rather than
*record-time*: it leaves the upstream EHR profile alone and only binds
producers at the moment they package a CCRR submission. It also keeps
the IG free of a hard `1..1` that would block conformant
implementations whose source records use `dataAbsentReason` for known,
permitted reasons (e.g., John/Jane Doe trauma cases later updated).

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Keep the resolution that has already been recorded on the ticket:
*Considered - No action required.* The CCRR Patient profile inherits
its `Patient.name` constraints from its parent profile; `mustSupport`
on the element ensures that any name present in the source record is
included in the CCRR report. No differential change is made.

#### Justification

The workgroup's recorded reasoning is defensible and well-grounded:
(1) the IG's implementer set is broader than ambulatory practices and
includes non-CoC hospitals whose data flows differ; (2) requiring
both family and given names breaks for legitimate international name
patterns; (3) divergence from the parent profile creates
interoperability friction with US Core / general Patient consumers
without a corresponding gain (since `mustSupport` already covers the
"include it when you have it" case); (4) names can be unknown at
report-generation time (e.g., trauma admissions, neonates) and the
reporting deadline can fall before identity resolution; and
(5) cancer registries already have post-hoc identity matching and
update workflows, so a hard `1..1` would surface as conformance
failures rather than as data-quality wins. There is no cross-reference
or community discussion in FhirAugury (Jira, Zulip, GitHub,
Confluence) suggesting broader support for the original request.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** C

The resolution already on the ticket is the right call, and the Public
Health workgroup should affirm it. The submitter's concern is real but
narrow to one operational segment (ambulatory EHR → registry
reporting), while the IG's conformance surface has to absorb hospitals,
non-US naming patterns, and report-time vs. record-time identity
gaps. Tightening to `1..1` plus forbidding `dataAbsentReason`
(Disposition A) trades broad interoperability for a guarantee that
`mustSupport` already substantially provides. Disposition B (a
report-time invariant) is the strongest *constructive* alternative and
worth holding in reserve if the workgroup later sees evidence of
nameless CCRR submissions causing downstream harm; it is not justified
on the current evidence base, which contains zero supporting Zulip or
cross-ticket discussion. Recommend closing as *Considered - No action
required* with the existing reviewer rationale captured in the
disposition note, and inviting the submitter to bring concrete
counts of nameless submissions if they wish to reopen the question.
