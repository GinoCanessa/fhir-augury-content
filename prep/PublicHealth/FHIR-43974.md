## Summary

The submitter (NIOSH, on behalf of the ODH community) asks the **US Birth
and Fetal Death Reporting (BFDR) IG** to add the mother's and father's
**current job** alongside the already-included "usual work" (longest-held
type of work) on the birth / fetal death record. The argument is that a
parent's current occupation may differ from their longest-held one and
can itself be epidemiologically significant for vital-statistics
analysis. The reporter explicitly references the ODH (Occupational Data
for Health) modeling pattern, which distinguishes "Usual Work"
(longest-held) from "Past or Present Job" (current/most recent).

This ticket is part of a clearly-coordinated NIOSH submission cluster.
The companion tickets give important framing:

- [FHIR-43973](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43973) — same request
  against BFDR; previously closed as **Duplicate / Considered for Future
  Use**. FHIR-43974 is the live carrier of this issue.
- [FHIR-43975](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43975) — companion ask
  to ensure parents' current job also flows to **jurisdiction reports**
  (i.e., the inter-jurisdictional message bundles), not just the local
  composition.
- [FHIR-43978](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43978) — same concept
  proposed at the **VRCL (Vital Records Common Library)** layer: add a
  "current job" Observation profile distinct from the existing
  Usual-Work observation, mirroring ODH's *Past or Present Job* vs.
  *Usual* split.
- [FHIR-43976](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43976) /
  [FHIR-43977](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43977) — companion ask
  to refresh the **industry and occupation vocabulary** (NIOSH ↔ ONC)
  in BFDR and VRCL respectively.
- [FHIR-43971](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43971) — same submitter
  asking for ODH-based work information in **RESP-NET** (different IG,
  same modeling pattern).

The earlier disposition (2024-02-15) was *Considered for Future Use*
based on emailed approval from Amanda Deering (CDC), citing CDC's note
that "NCHS will consider adding the observations requested based on
feedback from the jurisdictions. Occupation and industry is not
collected within the facility and patient worksheets that complete the
birth certificate and the fetal death report. Usual Work is included in
BFDR and VRCL since it is part of the inter-jurisdictional elements as
states may choose to collect and share among each other." The ticket
was **re-opened post STU2 publication** by Lynn Laakso (2024-10-15) and
is now back on the Public Health workgroup's queue for re-disposition.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add an observation for the **current (most recent) job** of the mother
and father in BFDR, modeled directly on ODH's *Past or Present Job*
profile, and slot it next to the existing usual-work observation in the
appropriate Mother / Father observation lists in the BFDR Composition.
Concretely:

- In the BFDR IG (target repo: `HL7/fhir-bfdr`, not yet cached locally),
  add two new profiled `Observation` artifacts — `Observation-current-job-mother`
  and `Observation-current-job-father` — that constrain the ODH *Past or
  Present Job* shape (or directly `derive-from` it) and bind to the same
  industry/occupation value sets used by the existing usual-work
  observations.
- Reference these new profiles from the BFDR Composition / mother &
  father bundle entries that today carry the usual-work observation.
- Coordinate with companion ticket
  [FHIR-43978](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43978) so that the base
  *current job* Observation profile actually lives in **VRCL** and BFDR
  derives from it; and with [FHIR-43975](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43975)
  so the new observation also propagates into the jurisdiction-report
  message bundle.
- Defer the value-set choice to the resolution of
  [FHIR-43976](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43976) (BFDR I&O
  vocabulary refresh) so BFDR is not pinned to a code system NIOSH/ONC
  is in the middle of replacing.

#### Justification

ODH already encodes exactly this distinction: "Usual Work" (longest-held)
and "Past or Present Job" (current/most recent) are separate profiles
precisely because epidemiologists and surveillance programs treat them
as separate signals. Modeling parents' work the same way preserves
semantic compatibility with ODH-conformant patient records and avoids
re-inventing terminology. The submitter is NIOSH, who maintain ODH and
who have first-hand evidence that mother/father current work is
analytically valuable for birth- and fetal-death surveillance.

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — Optional Extension Bound to ODH, Not a Required Profile Change

#### Proposal

Rather than introducing new mandatory observation profiles in BFDR's
Composition slices, add an **optional extension** (or simply
0..1 `Observation` reference at the bundle level with `MustSupport=false`)
that allows jurisdictions who *do* collect parents' current occupation
to send it using the ODH *Past or Present Job* profile, while
jurisdictions that don't (which CDC notes is the current reality —
"Occupation and industry is not collected within the facility and
patient worksheets that complete the birth certificate and the fetal
death report") incur no new burden. Mirror the same optional shape in
VRCL ([FHIR-43978](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43978)) and in
jurisdiction reports ([FHIR-43975](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43975)).

#### Justification

The 2024-02 CDC feedback (preserved in the comment thread) is the
binding constraint here: the data isn't currently collected on the
facility/patient worksheets that feed the birth and fetal-death
records. Making the new content **mandatory or `MustSupport=true`** in
BFDR would create conformance pressure on jurisdictions for data they
do not collect and have not committed to collecting. An optional /
extension-only path lets NIOSH and the volunteer jurisdictions exercise
the modeling immediately, generates real-world examples for the NVSS
CoP discussion CDC referenced, and avoids breaking BFDR conformance for
implementers who shipped against STU2. If/when the NVSS CoP signals
broad jurisdictional adoption in a future STU, the same shape can be
promoted to a first-class profile slice.

### Disposition C: Decline — Defer to a Future STU After NVSS CoP Input

#### Proposal

Re-close the ticket as **Considered for Future Use** (i.e., restore the
2024-02 disposition) with an explicit note that re-disposition is
deferred until the NVSS Community of Practice has had the chance to
weigh in, per CDC's commitment in the 2024-02 comment, and until
companion ticket [FHIR-43978](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43978)
has resolved how/whether VRCL hosts a current-job observation. Do not
modify the BFDR IG in the current cycle.

#### Justification

CDC (NCHS) has stated on the record that occupation/industry is not
captured on the worksheets that feed BFDR today, and has explicitly
committed to bringing the question to the NVSS CoP for jurisdiction
input. Adding a profiled observation to BFDR before that input arrives
risks specifying a shape the jurisdictions then ask to change. The
ticket's *Considered for Future Use* status was always meant to
auto-resurface for future ballots; the post-STU2 re-open appears to be
procedural ("re-open post STU2 publication") rather than a change in
the underlying readiness signal from CDC.

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition: B (optional ODH-bound extension / non-MustSupport observation, coordinated with VRCL).**

Disposition C is the most conservative reading of the CDC comment, but
the data the submitter is asking to carry is genuinely useful (NIOSH is
the authoritative voice on this) and there is no good reason to make
the IG actively *unable* to carry it while the NVSS CoP discussion runs
in parallel. Disposition A is the right end-state but is premature: it
imposes conformance weight on jurisdictions that CDC has already said
do not collect this data on their birth-certificate worksheets, which
is exactly the friction the 2024-02 disposition was trying to avoid.

Disposition B threads the needle: it lets NIOSH, ODH-aligned EHRs, and
willing jurisdictions exercise the modeling immediately by referencing
the ODH *Past or Present Job* profile, gives the NVSS CoP concrete
examples to react to, and keeps the door open to promoting the
extension to a first-class slice in the next STU once jurisdictional
appetite is clearer. It also pairs cleanly with the companion tickets:
the underlying Observation profile is defined once in VRCL
(FHIR-43978), referenced optionally by BFDR (this ticket), and carried
optionally into jurisdiction reports (FHIR-43975), with vocabulary
binding deliberately left flexible until the NIOSH/ONC refresh
(FHIR-43976 / FHIR-43977) lands.
