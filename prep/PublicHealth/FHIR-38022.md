## Summary

Craig Newman is asking whether HL7 has a standard mechanism for tracking
where profiles defined in one IG (particularly a "Library" IG such as the
US Public Health Profiles Library — USPHPL) are reused by other IGs,
either as-is or further constrained. He suspects the USPHPL IG itself is
not the right place to maintain this information and wonders whether it
could live on an HL7 Confluence page. The motivation is two-fold:
implementers would benefit from concrete examples of how USPHPL profiles
are being used downstream, and the Public Health Work Group would gain
visibility into where common needs (and therefore candidates for new
shared profiles) are emerging across the Public Health IG portfolio.

There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub
items associated with FHIR-38022 in the indexed sources, so the request
stands on its own. The most directly related context comes from the
ticket's own comments: at the 2022-12-01 Public Health WG meeting the
issue was originally dispositioned "Considered for Future Use"
(23-0-4, Sarah Gaunt / Nagesh Bashyam), with the WG agreeing the
USPHPL IG was not the right home for tracking and noting that Grahame
Grieve was building a dashboard page tracking profile usage across
registered HL7 IGs that could meet the need. Lynn Laakso re-opened
the ticket on 2023-08-17 post-STU publication so the Work Group can
revisit how to leverage (or contribute to) that dashboard for USPHPL
profiles specifically.

## Details

**Description:**

Is there a standard way to track where IGs use profiles defined in
other FHIR IGs (or more likely FHIR Library IGs)? If not, how
impractical would it be to maintain a score of which IGs are using
USPHPL profiles (either as is or further constrained)? I suspect the
USPHPL IG is not the place to do this, but could it be done on an HL7
Confluence page? I think having examples of how these profiles are
used would help implementers consistently use them and probably help
the PH Work Group track where there is a common need for new profiles.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat this as a non-normative tooling/process request rather than a
specification change against the USPHPL IG itself. The Public Health
Work Group commits to surfacing profile-usage information for USPHPL
profiles via two coordinated artifacts:

1. **Leverage the existing FHIR registry-wide profile dashboard** that
   Grahame Grieve has been building (referenced in the 2022-09-20 and
   2023-08-17 comments) by requesting (a) a stable URL for the
   USPHPL-derived view and (b) a filter/facet that scopes the dashboard
   to IGs sponsored by the Public Health Work Group.
2. **Add a non-normative "Profile Usage" page to the USPHPL IG** (or a
   sibling Confluence page if the IG cannot be revised mid-cycle) that
   links out to that filtered dashboard, briefly describes its
   methodology, and points implementers to representative downstream
   IGs/profiles that derive from each USPHPL profile.

No structural changes to USPHPL profiles, value sets, or extensions are
implied. The deliverable is a documentation page plus an external
dashboard link maintained outside the IG build.

#### Justification

The request explicitly anticipates that the USPHPL IG is not the right
place to *store* the data and proposes a Confluence page as one option.
The 2022-12-01 PH WG vote (23-0-4) already endorsed this framing and
identified Grahame's dashboard as the natural source. Re-opening the
ticket post-STU lets the WG move from "considered" to a concrete linkage
without the cost or risk of a normative change. Pointing implementers at
real downstream uses directly addresses the reporter's stated benefit
("help implementers consistently use them") and gives the WG visibility
into where new shared profiles are warranted.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Address the underlying need by **declaring USPHPL's downstream-usage
visibility as a registry / package-server responsibility**, not a
USPHPL deliverable, while still closing the loop for implementers:

- The USPHPL IG adds a single short paragraph in its introduction (or
  "How to Use This IG" page) explaining that profile-derivation usage
  across HL7-registered IGs is discoverable through the FHIR IG
  registry / package server's profile-usage views, with a link.
- The PH Work Group separately files a feedback / enhancement request
  against the FHIR registry tooling (or Grahame's dashboard project)
  asking for a "sponsoring work group" facet so that PH-sponsored IGs
  can be filtered as a group.
- No PH-WG-maintained Confluence page or curated profile-usage list is
  created; the PH WG relies on the registry as the single source of
  truth and avoids a manual-maintenance burden.

#### Justification

The 2023-08-17 comment explicitly flags that "trying to track manually
would be difficult and time consuming." A curated Confluence page
(Disposition A's option 2) risks becoming stale the moment a new
downstream IG is published. Pushing the persistence of usage data to
registry tooling is consistent with the ecosystem-wide direction
already underway and avoids duplicating Grahame's work. The trade-off
is reduced PH-specific narrative — implementers get raw data instead
of curated examples — but it is far more sustainable and keeps USPHPL
focused on its normative scope.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket "Persuasive with Mod / Not Persuasive" with the
rationale that profile-usage tracking is **out of scope for the USPHPL
IG**: the IG defines profiles and their constraints; how those
profiles are subsequently consumed by other IGs is properly the
concern of the FHIR IG registry, the package server, and individual
downstream IGs. No change to the USPHPL IG content or build is
required, and the PH Work Group takes no on-going maintenance
commitment. Implementers are directed to the FHIR registry for
discovery.

#### Justification

The reporter himself states "the USPHPL IG is not the place to do
this," and the 2022-12-01 WG vote agreed. Declining is defensible
because (a) there is no normative gap in USPHPL that this ticket
identifies, (b) any tracking artifact would live outside the IG, and
(c) accepting as a USPHPL ticket conflates IG content with ecosystem
tooling, setting a precedent for similar requests against every
Library IG. If the WG wants to invest in usage analytics it can do so
as a separate work item, not as a USPHPL change.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A is the best fit because it directly honors the
reporter's stated benefits — implementer guidance and WG visibility
into emerging shared-profile needs — while respecting the WG's prior
view (and the reporter's own acknowledgement) that the USPHPL IG is
not the place to *store* the data. It builds on infrastructure that
already exists (Grahame's dashboard) rather than committing the WG to
a manually curated list, and it keeps the change to USPHPL itself
minimal and non-normative (a documentation page or external link).
Disposition B is attractive for its low maintenance cost but leaves
implementers with raw registry output and no PH-curated narrative,
which is exactly the gap the reporter is trying to close. Disposition
C is technically defensible but ignores that the ticket was
deliberately re-opened post-STU to do *something* concrete; pure
decline would close the ticket without addressing the implementer-
facing need that prompted the re-open.
