## Summary

The reporter (acting on behalf of a NAACCR community member) asks whether
a **Laboratory Test Date** can be added to the lab-results section of the
**US Central Cancer Registry Reporting (CCRR) Content IG** and made
**required when laboratory results are present**. The motivating example
is PSA: a PSA value can change materially during treatment, so registries
need to know whether a reported PSA result reflects the diagnostic baseline
or a follow-up. The ticket targets the Laboratory Test Results section of
the CCRR IG, which derives lab observations from the US Core Laboratory
Result Observation Profile.

A workgroup comment notes that the date the reporter is asking for already
exists on the US Core Laboratory Result Observation Profile (`Observation.effective[x]`,
Must Support, cardinality `0..1`) and recommends **Not Persuasive** on the
grounds that (a) most labs do carry the date, (b) tightening cardinality
to `1..1` would cause otherwise-valid reports to be rejected when the EHR
lacks the value, and (c) the CCRR IG should stay aligned with its US Core
parent profile rather than diverging on a single element. A second comment
records that the issue was filed on behalf of a NAACCR community member.

There are no Jira, Zulip, GitHub, or Confluence cross-references for this
ticket in Augury, so the analysis below is grounded in the ticket text and
the cited US Core profile alone.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the CCRR IG's CCRR Laboratory Result profile (the IG's slice of the
Laboratory Test Results section, derived from
`USCoreLaboratoryResultObservationProfile`), tighten `Observation.effective[x]`
from `0..1` Must Support to **`1..1` Must Support**, with a short
description noting that registries require a date to interpret quantitative
results that change over time (e.g., PSA at diagnosis vs. during
treatment). Add an `obeys`-style invariant or narrative guidance making
clear that any of `effectiveDateTime`, `effectivePeriod`, `effectiveTiming`,
or `effectiveInstant` satisfies the requirement.

#### Justification

This matches the literal request and gives registries a hard guarantee
that every reported lab result carries a temporal anchor. The existing
US Core element is the right hook — no new extension is needed — and
constraining cardinality from `0..1` to `1..1` is the standard FHIR
mechanism for "required when lab results exist". It is consistent with
how other Public-Health–oriented IGs (e.g., eCR) tighten US Core
cardinalities when the public-health use case demands it, and the change
would eliminate ambiguity for the PSA-style scenarios the reporter cites.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep `Observation.effective[x]` at `0..1` Must Support on the CCRR
Laboratory Result profile, but add a **per-resource conformance
requirement** expressed as either:

- a FHIRPath invariant on the profile (e.g.,
  `effective.exists() or dataAbsentReason.exists()`), and/or
- a narrative "Conformance Expectations" entry in the Laboratory Test
  Results section requiring senders to populate
  `Observation.effective[x]` when known and to use a `dataAbsentReason`
  (with a code such as `unknown`, `asked-unknown`, or `temp-unknown`)
  when it is genuinely unavailable.

This preserves alignment with US Core's cardinality while still surfacing
a registry-grade signal about missing dates so receivers can triage them
rather than silently accepting undated results.

#### Justification

This addresses the reporter's underlying need — knowing whether a PSA
value reflects diagnosis or treatment — without forcing report rejection
for the small fraction of records where the EHR truly lacks the date.
It also keeps the profile aligned with `USCoreLaboratoryResultObservationProfile`,
which the workgroup comment explicitly identifies as a value worth
preserving. Using `dataAbsentReason` is the FHIR-idiomatic way to
distinguish "we don't know" from "we forgot to send it" and gives
registries a structured way to measure data quality without breaking
interoperability.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (Not Persuasive)

#### Proposal

Resolve the ticket as **Not Persuasive** and make no change to the CCRR
Laboratory Result profile. Record in the resolution that
`Observation.effective[x]` is already present and Must Support on the US
Core parent profile (`USCoreLaboratoryResultObservationProfile`,
cardinality `0..1`), so EHR-recorded dates will already flow through to
registries on the existing element. Optionally add a short paragraph in
the Laboratory Test Results section reminding implementers that the date
is Must Support and should be populated whenever known.

#### Justification

This mirrors the workgroup's own recommendation in the ticket comments
and rests on three points the comment makes explicitly: (1) tightening
cardinality to `1..1` would cause otherwise-valid reports to be rejected
when the EHR lacks the date, harming registry data capture overall;
(2) most labs do carry the date, so the marginal benefit of `1..1` is
small; and (3) divergence from US Core for a single element creates a
maintenance burden and an implementer-education tax that outweighs the
benefit. Must Support already requires senders to populate the element
when they have it, which is the substantive obligation the reporter
appears to be after.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** C (Not Persuasive), with a strong nudge to
adopt the narrative-guidance half of Disposition B.

The workgroup's own comment on the ticket already lands on Not
Persuasive, and that judgement is well-supported: `Observation.effective[x]`
is already Must Support on the US Core parent, so EHR systems that record
the date are already obligated to send it; tightening to `1..1` would
mainly hurt registries by causing reports to be rejected for the long
tail of records that genuinely lack the value. Diverging from US Core for
a single element also sets an awkward precedent for future CCRR profiles
and complicates downstream tooling that assumes US Core conformance.

That said, the reporter's underlying concern — being unable to interpret
a PSA value without knowing whether it reflects diagnosis or treatment —
is real. Before closing, the workgroup should consider folding the
narrative half of Disposition B into the resolution: a short
"Conformance Expectations" paragraph in the Laboratory Test Results
section reminding implementers that `effective[x]` is Must Support, that
it should be populated whenever the date is known in the EHR, and that
`dataAbsentReason` is the right mechanism when it is not. That keeps the
ticket Not Persuasive on the literal ask while giving the NAACCR community
member a concrete, registry-friendly answer.
