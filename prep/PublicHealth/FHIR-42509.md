## Summary

The ticket raises a gap in the US Bidirectional Services eReferrals (BSeR)
implementation guide: the specification does not describe what should happen
when the recipient of a referral needs additional information beyond what was
supplied in the original referral package. The reporter observes this scenario
will not be uncommon and suggests BSeR either adopt an HRex-like messaging
pattern for requesting additional information or point implementers at HRex as
the appropriate RESTful solution. No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip
threads, or GitHub artifacts were surfaced by the Augury index for this ticket,
so the disposition analysis below is grounded solely in the ticket text and the
named specification (BSeR) and its cited peer (Da Vinci HRex).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a normative section to the BSeR IG (e.g., a new "Requesting Additional
Information" page under the workflow/transactions portion of the guide) that
defines a BSeR-native mechanism for the referral recipient to ask the
initiating provider for more information. Concretely:

- Define a BSeR `Task` profile (e.g., `BSeR-Task-RequestForInfo`) whose
  `Task.code` identifies "Request for additional referral information",
  `Task.focus` points at the original referral `ServiceRequest`, and
  `Task.for` points at the subject patient.
- Define the expected exchange pattern (recipient creates the Task against the
  initiator's Task endpoint; initiator updates `Task.status` and supplies the
  requested payload via `Task.output` or linked `DocumentReference` /
  `QuestionnaireResponse`).
- Add conformance requirements on both the initiator and recipient actors to
  support this transaction, and add must-support flags on the relevant Task
  slices.
- Add narrative pages describing the trigger, the happy path, and error /
  refusal handling, plus one or more `Example` instances.

#### Justification

The ticket names this as a common operational scenario that is currently
undefined. Making it explicit inside BSeR gives implementers a single,
deterministic path to follow rather than leaving the question to bilateral
agreements. Using `Task` keeps the approach consistent with how BSeR already
orchestrates the referral lifecycle and avoids introducing a new resource
vocabulary for the same workgroup. Defining it in-guide also lets BSeR attach
the specific data-element expectations (what additional info is being
requested, how it is returned) that a generic cross-IG pointer cannot.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Do not re-specify a request-for-information mechanism inside BSeR. Instead, add
a short "Requesting Additional Information" section to the BSeR IG that:

- States that any party to the referral MAY use the Da Vinci HRex
  `Task Data Request` profile and associated transactions to request more
  information about the subject of the referral.
- Specifies how the HRex Task relates back to the BSeR `ServiceRequest`
  (`Task.focus` = the BSeR referral `ServiceRequest`; subject alignment; actor
  identity conventions).
- Calls out any BSeR-specific constraints (e.g., which BSeR actors are expected
  to support the HRex endpoints, which BSeR data categories are in-scope for
  such a request).

No new BSeR profile is introduced; BSeR becomes a consumer of HRex rather than
a parallel definer of the same pattern.

#### Justification

The reporter explicitly floats this option ("perhaps point to HRex as the
appropriate RESTful solution"). HRex already defines a general-purpose
`Task`-based data-request pattern with community tooling and reference
implementations, so reusing it avoids divergent, near-identical mechanisms in
adjacent IGs. This is cheaper to maintain, reduces implementer burden for
systems that already implement Da Vinci guides, and keeps BSeR focused on the
referral workflow rather than on general data-request plumbing. The trade-off
is a dependency on HRex's publication cadence and scope, and a (likely small)
set of BSeR actors who are not Da Vinci-aligned and would need to add HRex
support.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change for the current BSeR cycle on the grounds that the
scenario, while real, is already handled by out-of-band clinical workflows and
general FHIR mechanisms (`Communication`, `CommunicationRequest`, direct
queries against the initiator's FHIR endpoint), and does not require a BSeR-
specific specification. Close the ticket with guidance that implementers may
use these general mechanisms and revisit if implementer feedback identifies
interoperability failures in the field.

#### Justification

BSeR is a focused bidirectional referral IG. The ticket itself acknowledges
that "the specification doesn't talk about" the scenario, not that
implementations are failing because of it. General FHIR has multiple adequate
resources for ad-hoc information requests, and over-specifying a second
mechanism risks conflict with whatever trading partners already do. Declining
preserves scope and avoids adding conformance obligations for a transaction
that has not yet been shown to be a field-level interoperability problem.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B best matches both the reporter's own suggestion and the broader
HL7 direction of reusing Da Vinci HRex for `Task`-based data requests rather
than minting IG-specific equivalents. It closes the documented gap in BSeR
(implementers will have a named, conformant path to follow when the recipient
needs more information) without duplicating a pattern that HRex already
defines, tests, and maintains. Disposition A would work but creates ongoing
maintenance overhead and risks drifting from HRex as that guide evolves;
Disposition C leaves the original gap unresolved despite the reporter
flagging it as "not super uncommon", which is exactly the kind of ambiguity
IG-level specifications are meant to remove. The workgroup should confirm
that the BSeR actors are willing to take an HRex dependency; if that
dependency is unacceptable for some participant class, fall back to
Disposition A scoped to just those actors.
