## Summary

FHIR-29867 is a Public Health change request (filed against the
Situation Awareness for Novel Epidemic Response — SANER — FHIR IG)
asking HL7 to develop a Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) inventory
data standard at the granularity of **manufacturer, model number, and,
where available, NIOSH approval number** (for respirators). The
reporter (NIOSH‑NPPTL) cites direct experience from a 2020 C4MI-led
alpha PPE Supply Management App deployment in which 78 hospitals
reported near-daily inventories for 15 weeks, but only ~74% of model
numbers and only ~one‑third of manufacturer values could be verified —
limiting the ability to detect shortages, trends, counterfeit
respirators, and substandard equipment. The proposal is motivated by
the observed need for a consistent nomenclature to support automated
PPE surveillance during future public health emergencies. No
cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were
surfaced for this ticket, so the request stands on its own.

## Details

**Description:**

It is apparent with each public health crisis experienced that there is
a need to establish an information architecture system that can be used
to support nationwide PPE monitoring and analysis within the healthcare
industry. NIOSH-NPPTL identified and initiated a contract with the
Center for Medical Interoperability (C4MI). C4MI is a 501(c)(3)
cooperative research and development lab funded by health systems to
simplify and advance data sharing among medical technologies and
systems using a centralized, vendor-neutral approach. Under a research
and development contract, C4MI developed an alpha PPE Supply Management
App Version 1 that was deployed in 2020. This initial application
focused on exporting data from hospital systems that were willing to
provide limited hospital characteristics and PPE inventory data. The
alpha deployment included a limited set of PPE types (N95 filtering
facepiece respirators (FFRs), surgical masks, and face shields).
Hospitals were asked to report the current quantities of PPE currently
in their possession in "eaches" for each PPE type and broken down by
individual units by manufacturer and model number.

For 15 weeks 78 hospitals reported near-daily PPE inventories. The
data, however, could not be quickly processed for its original
intention of identifying potential shortages and trends in PPE use
across the country. Specifically, after data cleaning and validation,
74.2% of the PPE model numbers provided could be verified. Within the
three PPE categories, 60% of the N95 respirator models reported could
be verified; 40% of face shields, and 84% of surgical masks.
Additionally, only about one-third of the manufacturers provided were
correct; the remaining number were those of private labels or marketers
of the PPE. In order to utilize future automated PPE surveillance
systems, it is clear that a PPE data standard that outlines consistent
nomenclature is critical. NIOSH proposes that such a standard would
require reporting of PPE model numbers and manufacturers and, if
available, NIOSH approval numbers. This level of granularity is
necessary as a means to quickly identify counterfeit and substandard
respiratory protection. Capturing PPE inventory at the level of
manufacturer and model number provides a means to immediately notify
healthcare entities and others of equipment that could put workers at
risk.

**Existing Wording:** None

**Proposed Wording:** Develop a PPE inventory data standard at the
granularity of the manufacturer, model number, and, if available, the
NIOSH approval number for respirators.

**Resolution Description:** *Not within scope of this or Helios work.*

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Extend the SANER (Situation Awareness for Novel Epidemic Response) IG
to include a normative PPE inventory data standard with reporting at
manufacturer / model-number / NIOSH-approval-number granularity.
Concretely:

- Define (or adopt) a canonical PPE product identification model built
  on `Device` / `DeviceDefinition` with bindings to FDA GUDID UDI-DI
  where applicable, plus a NIOSH-approval-number identifier slice for
  respirators.
- Author a profile family: `PPEProductDevice`,
  `PPEProductDeviceDefinition`, and a `PPEInventory` MeasureReport /
  Observation-based profile (matching SANER's existing
  MeasureReport-driven situational awareness pattern) with required
  elements for manufacturer, model number, PPE category (N95 FFR,
  surgical mask, face shield, …) and quantity-on-hand expressed in
  "eaches".
- Bind PPE categories to a ValueSet seeded from the NIOSH-recognized
  respirator categories and surgical mask / face shield FDA product
  codes.
- Provide example bundles and a supply-side MeasureReport population
  guide so hospital EHR / supply-chain exports can satisfy the
  reporting contract without free-text manufacturer strings.

#### Justification

The reporter's operational data (74.2% model-number verifiability, only
~one-third manufacturer verifiability) is exactly the type of
evidence-of-need the workgroup typically requires for an IG-level
structural change, and the need was explicitly re-raised post‑STU1
publication (lynn_laakso 2021-09-07). SANER is the correct host IG
because PPE-inventory reporting is a situational-awareness capability
exercised during public-health emergencies, and MeasureReport-based
aggregation is the IG's established reporting mechanism. Using
`Device` / `DeviceDefinition` with UDI-DI and a NIOSH-approval-number
slice aligns with existing FHIR device-identification patterns and
avoids inventing a parallel identifier stack.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Instead of standing up a PPE data standard inside SANER, publish a
**small, focused companion IG or supplemental guide** (e.g., "PPE
Inventory Reporting for Public Health") that:

- Reuses base FHIR `Device` / `DeviceDefinition` without new profiles,
  relying on UDI-DI (FDA GUDID) as the authoritative manufacturer +
  model identifier and adding a single extension or identifier slice
  for NIOSH approval number on respirator devices.
- Defines a minimal `Observation`- or `MeasureReport`-based inventory
  reporting pattern that points at those Device instances, rather than
  embedding manufacturer/model as strings.
- Stays silent on workflow / submission endpoints, so the content can
  be adopted independently by SANER, Helios, or a future public-health
  emergency IG without coupling to any one of them.

#### Justification

The actual gap surfaced by NIOSH's 2020 deployment is identifier
quality, not reporting structure — 60% of N95 model numbers and
~one‑third of manufacturers failed verification because they were
captured as free text rather than reconciled against GUDID/NIOSH
registries. Addressing that with UDI-DI + a NIOSH-approval extension
on `Device` reuses mechanisms FHIR already supports, keeps the SANER
core IG scope-stable, and lets the Helios FHIR Accelerator or a future
public-health-emergency IG pick up the content where it fits best.
This avoids committing SANER STU-level ballot cycles to material the
current resolution ("Not within scope of this or Helios work")
explicitly flagged as a scope mismatch.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (Not Persuasive)

#### Proposal

Decline the request as *Not Persuasive* on the grounds that (a) a PPE
manufacturer/model/NIOSH-approval-number data standard is out of scope
for the SANER IG, which is a situational-awareness reporting IG
structured around MeasureReport aggregates rather than a
device-identification data standard, and (b) the underlying
identification need is already addressable using existing FHIR
mechanisms (`Device` / `DeviceDefinition` with UDI-DI per FDA GUDID and
an identifier slice for NIOSH approval numbers), so no new normative
content is required from Public Health WG. Record that the work should
be re-scoped to a sponsor with a device/supply-chain remit (e.g.,
Orders & Observations, PHER FHIR Accelerator / Helios, or an FDA-led
UDI companion guide) if there is continued sponsor interest.

#### Justification

This mirrors the disposition already applied to the ticket on
2024-01-11 ("Not within scope of this or Helios work"). The
request describes a product-identification / supply-chain data
standard, not an epidemic situational-awareness measure, and the
community discussion captured in the comments shows a 2021-04-22
vote to defer ("Considered for Future Use") followed by two later
revisits without a concrete author or design emerging, which
indicates sustained lack of sponsor capacity in Public Health WG.
FHIR already supports the data pattern at the element level
(`Device.udiCarrier`, `Device.identifier`, `DeviceDefinition`), so
declining does not leave implementers without a path — it just
declines to standardize one inside SANER.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** C

The ticket has effectively already been dispositioned this way on
2024-01-11, and the substantive reasoning still holds: SANER's
mandate is situational-awareness reporting via MeasureReport, not a
device-identification data standard; the specific problem the
reporter describes (unverifiable manufacturer/model strings) is a
data-quality problem best solved by binding to FDA GUDID UDI-DI plus
a NIOSH-approval-number identifier on `Device`, which FHIR already
provides; and the ticket's own history (deferred 2021-04-22, reopened
2021-09-07, no assignee ever picked up, resolved Not Persuasive
2024-01-11) shows Public Health WG has repeatedly declined to staff
this work. Disposition A would commit SANER to a scope it wasn't
designed for, and Disposition B — while technically more satisfying —
still requires a sponsor that has not materialized in three years.
Declining while pointing implementers at existing `Device` / UDI
mechanisms is the honest answer and keeps the door open for a
better-scoped sponsor (e.g., an FDA/GUDID-aligned companion IG or a
Helios-led follow-on) if one emerges.
