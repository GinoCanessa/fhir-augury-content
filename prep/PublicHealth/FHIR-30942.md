## Summary

The ticket asks the Public Health work group (against the Electronic Case
Reporting / eCR FHIR IG) to make Oregon's REALD (Race, Ethnicity, Language,
and Disability) data elements available in FHIR. The reporter's strong
preference is that REALD content be picked up in US Core — modeled
analogously to the existing US Core Race and Ethnicity extensions — rather
than expressed as a Public-Health-specific extension, on the grounds that
REALD information is essential to identifying and addressing disparities in
care and therefore should not be siloed inside one PH IG. The change request
originated from a 2021 spreadsheet import of comment #19 by Jean Duteau and
has been carried in the eCR backlog since then.

No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were
returned by the orchestrator for FHIR-30942, so this summary is grounded
entirely in the ticket itself and its comment history.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Pursue REALD inclusion *in US Core* (not in eCR or another PH IG) by
defining a US-Core-aligned extension family for the Oregon REALD data
elements, modelled directly on the existing
`us-core-race` and `us-core-ethnicity` extensions on `Patient`:

- A `us-core-reald-race` extension on `Patient` (granular OMB+REALD
  race coding, "Other"/"Unknown" handling, narrative text), bound to a
  REALD-aligned race value set.
- A `us-core-reald-ethnicity` extension on `Patient` (granular REALD
  ethnicity codings + text), bound to a REALD-aligned ethnicity value
  set that is a superset of the OMB ethnicity bindings already used by
  US Core.
- A REALD language profile/extension expressed against
  `Patient.communication` (preferred + spoken/written, proficiency).
- Disability-status capture as `Observation` profile(s) (one per REALD
  disability domain) referenced from `Patient`, *or*, if US Core wants
  to keep parity with race/ethnicity, a thin `Patient` extension that
  points at those Observations.

Because this ticket is filed against the *Electronic Case Reporting*
spec, accepting it "as requested" means the eCR work group formally
forwards/co-sponsors the request to US Core (Cross-Group Project, FHIR-I
+ CGP + PH joint review) rather than implementing the change in the eCR
IG itself.

#### Justification

This is the disposition that the original Public Health vote
(21-0-0, "Referred and tracked" for consideration by US Core) and the
reporter's wording both point to. Putting REALD on `Patient` via
US-Core-style extensions keeps demographic equity data adjacent to the
existing OMB race/ethnicity scaffolding implementers already consume, so
downstream eCR (and other PH) profiles can derive from US Core Patient
without introducing a PH-only fork of demographics. Disability-status
modelling as `Observation` is consistent with how US Core already
handles status-bearing clinical concepts and avoids overloading the
Patient extension surface.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach (Not Persuasive — defer to USPHPL/USCDI track)

#### Proposal

Do **not** create a new REALD extension family in this ticket. Instead,
record the substantive answer already given by the work group in 2025
as the ticket's formal disposition:

- REALD **disability-status** content is already on the path to ballot
  in the US Public Health Profiles Library
  (https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/fhir-us-ph-library/), which is being
  aligned with USCDI+.
- Adoption into **US Core** is gated on inclusion in **USCDI** (and
  USPHPL adoption is gated on USCDI+); the US Realm Steering Committee
  / USCDI process — not the eCR ticket queue — is the correct lever for
  the race/ethnicity/language pieces.
- Direct the reporter to file USCDI / USCDI+ comments asking for the
  REALD race, ethnicity, and language elements, and point them at
  USPHPL for the disability-status work that is already in flight.

Optionally, the eCR IG can add a non-normative pointer in its
Demographics/Patient guidance noting that REALD-aligned demographic
capture is being progressed through USPHPL + USCDI rather than via an
eCR-specific extension.

#### Justification

This matches the work group's current understanding (per minigrrl's
2025-07-02 comment) and avoids creating a third REALD modelling pattern
that would compete with the USPHPL disability work already at ballot
and with whatever US Core ultimately publishes if/when REALD lands in
USCDI. It also respects the standing US Realm policy that US Core
demographic content is driven by USCDI; a unilateral US Core extension
authored from the eCR backlog would be unlikely to be accepted by FHIR-I
without that USCDI signal. The ticket then closes with an actionable
next step for the reporter rather than indefinite "Triaged" status.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-30942 as **Not Persuasive — out of scope for the eCR IG**.
Rationale: the eCR specification consumes US Core Patient and is not
the venue for inventing new cross-cutting US-realm demographic
extensions; the request is for a US Core / USCDI change, not an eCR
change. Note in the resolution that REALD disability-status content is
already addressed in USPHPL.

#### Justification

Declining is defensible because (a) the request explicitly asks for the
work to land in US Core "and not as a unique extension for PH", which
puts it outside the change scope of the eCR IG that owns this ticket,
and (b) a parallel REALD venue (USPHPL disability + USCDI/USCDI+) is
already active. Holding the ticket open against eCR creates the
expectation that eCR will deliver the change, when in fact eCR has no
authority to publish a US Core extension. The current "Not Persuasive"
resolution recorded on the ticket is consistent with this disposition.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the most accurate reflection of where the work
*actually* lives in 2025 and gives the reporter a concrete next step
(file against USCDI / USCDI+, watch USPHPL for the disability-status
piece) without leaving the ticket dangling in Triaged. Disposition A
captures the right *technical* shape of the change but mis-routes it:
eCR cannot land US Core extensions, and unilaterally drafting them from
this ticket would duplicate work already underway in USPHPL and pre-empt
the USCDI process that US Core depends on. Disposition C is essentially
the same routing answer as B but without the constructive hand-off, so
it leaves the reporter with no path forward and risks the same comment
being re-filed against future eCR ballots. Recommending B keeps the
"Not Persuasive" outcome the work group has already converged on, while
explicitly recording the USPHPL + USCDI/USCDI+ next steps as the
disposition text.
