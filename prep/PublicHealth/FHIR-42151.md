## Summary

[FHIR-42151](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-42151) is a question raised
by Rick Geimer against the US Bidirectional Services eReferrals (BSeR)
implementation guide (Sep 2023 ballot). The ticket asks whether the BSeR
authors considered using `Measure` and CQL as the mechanism for
gathering the data needed for the "additional referral use cases" flow,
instead of the currently-documented approach of issuing a batch `Bundle`
containing multiple `search`/`read` requests. The reporter points
specifically at the
[adaptation_for_additional_referral_use_cases.html](https://hl7.org/fhir/us/bser/2023Sep/adaptation_for_additional_referral_use_cases.html)
page, which describes the batch-query approach. No alternative design
is proposed in the ticket itself; it is framed as a "dumb question" /
sanity check on the design decision. There are no cross-referenced Jira
tickets, no Zulip threads, and no GitHub items linked to this ticket
in the Augury index, so the question appears to stand on its own.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat the ticket as a design question and answer it on the ticket:
confirm in the BSeR work group whether `Measure` + CQL was evaluated as
the data-collection mechanism for the "additional referral use cases"
flow. If it was evaluated and rejected, capture the rationale (e.g.,
implementer base, tooling availability, payload shape, server support
for `$evaluate-measure`) directly in the
`adaptation_for_additional_referral_use_cases.html` page so future
readers do not have to re-litigate the choice. Close the ticket as
"Persuasive with Mod" / "Not Persuasive" with that rationale recorded.

#### Justification

The ticket is filed as a Question, not a change request, and the
reporter is asking for justification rather than proposing a concrete
edit. Recording the design rationale in the IG narrative is the lowest
risk path: it does not change any conformance behaviour, it preserves
the current batch-Bundle approach that BSeR has already balloted, and
it directly addresses what the reporter actually asked for. It is also
consistent with how Public Health IGs typically handle design-question
tickets where the chosen mechanism has working implementations.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt a hybrid: keep the existing batch-`Bundle`-of-queries flow as the
normative mechanism in the
`adaptation_for_additional_referral_use_cases.html` page, but add a
non-normative section (or appendix) describing how the same data set
could be expressed as a `Measure` with CQL data requirements and
retrieved via `$evaluate-measure` / `$collect-data`. Reference the
Clinical Reasoning module and the relevant CQF IG patterns. This gives
implementers who already use CQL a path that aligns with quality
measurement tooling, without forcing implementers without CQL engines
to adopt a new dependency.

#### Justification

This addresses the underlying concern in the ticket — that CQL/Measure
may be a more idiomatic way to express "here is the bundle of clinical
data I need for a referral" — while protecting the implementers who
have already built against the balloted batch-query approach. It avoids
a breaking change to the IG and keeps the Sep 2023 conformance
expectations intact, but acknowledges the alternative on the record.
The trade-off vs. Disposition A is documentation cost and the risk that
the non-normative alternative diverges from the normative one over
time; that risk is manageable as long as the data set defined by the
Measure is kept aligned with the queries in the batch Bundle.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as "Not Persuasive" on the basis that BSeR has
deliberately chosen a batch-`Bundle`-of-queries approach for the
additional referral use cases, the design has been balloted, and there
is no concrete change request, use case, or implementer demand attached
to the ticket that justifies revisiting the mechanism. Note in the
resolution that implementers remain free to layer CQL/Measure on top of
the batch Bundle in their own deployments without an IG change.

#### Justification

The ticket is a single-line question from August 2023 with no follow-up
comments, no Zulip discussion, no linked tickets, and no implementer
behind it asking for the change. The cost of restructuring the
adaptation page around `Measure`/CQL — new dependencies (Clinical
Reasoning, CQL engine, `$evaluate-measure`/`$collect-data` support),
new conformance expectations, and re-balloting — is not justified by a
single design-curiosity question. Declining is defensible because the
existing mechanism is interoperable on plain FHIR REST and does not
require a CQL runtime in the responding system.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is explicitly framed as a question ("did you consider…"), it
carries no proposed edit, no implementer push, no Zulip discussion, and
no linked work. The most useful outcome for the work group and for
future readers of the IG is to capture the answer — whatever the BSeR
authors actually decided about Measure/CQL — directly on the ticket and
in the adaptation page, then close it. Disposition B is a reasonable
fallback if the work group finds during discussion that there is real
implementer interest in a CQL-based alternative; if so, the
non-normative appendix can be added without disturbing the normative
batch-query flow. Disposition C achieves the same operational outcome
as A (no spec change) but throws away the opportunity to record the
design rationale, so it is the weakest choice. Recommending A keeps the
record clean, respects the Sep 2023 ballot, and gives the reporter a
concrete answer.
