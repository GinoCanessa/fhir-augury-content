## Summary

The submitter is asking the Public Health work group to publish (or
otherwise make available) a side-by-side comparison/map between the
SANER `Measure` profile and the `Measure` profile used by US SAFR. The
concern is twofold: (1) the name "US SAFR" appears to imply a focus on
*continuous variable* measures even though the IG actually carries
other measure types as well, and (2) the SANER `Measure` profile
includes a number of extensions and slices whose mapping (or
intentional omission) in US SAFR is not currently documented. The
reporter wants assurance that nothing was inadvertently dropped during
the alignment work with Da Vinci DEQM, and a clear statement of what
was deliberately left out of scope as US SAFR narrowed its situational
awareness reporting focus (e.g., bed capacity, HRD).

The cross-reference index for FHIR-50364 returned no related Jira
tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items, so the ticket stands on its
own as a documentation/traceability request rather than a known cluster
of related work.

## Details

**Description:**

When comparing the SANER Measure profile with the Measure profile used
in US SAFR the US SAFR name seems to imply that only continuous
variable measures are used, even though we have identified others as
well. This may relate to observing that the SANER profile includes
numerous extensions and slices that are unclear as to how they are
mapped to SAFR. Is there a comparison/map to help validate we did not
miss anything and clearly understand the changes that were necessary
due to alignment with DEQM and those that are deemed out of scope for
situational awareness reporting beyond bed capacity and HRD?

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Author and publish a SANER ↔ US SAFR `Measure`-profile comparison as
part of the US SAFR IG. Concretely:

- Add a new narrative page under US SAFR (e.g., `saner-comparison.md`)
  that lists each element, slice, and extension on the SANER `Measure`
  profile and, for each, records one of: *carried forward to US SAFR
  (with link to the corresponding element/extension/slice in
  US SAFR)*, *replaced by a DEQM-derived equivalent (with link)*, or
  *intentionally out of scope (with rationale)*.
- Where the US SAFR profile materially differs from SANER because of
  DEQM alignment (cardinalities, must-support flags, scoring code
  bindings), call those differences out explicitly with a one-line
  reason.
- Update the US SAFR `Measure` profile narrative to disclaim the
  "continuous variable" implication of the IG name and enumerate the
  measure scoring types actually supported (proportion, ratio,
  cohort, continuous-variable, etc.) along with examples.
- Cross-link the new page from the SANER profile description and from
  the US SAFR home page so reviewers can find it without prior
  knowledge.

#### Justification

This is exactly the deliverable the work group already committed to
in the 2025-08-12 task ("Review the extensions and slices in the SANER
profiles and describe how they map to USSAFR equivalents if
applicable"). The reporter's concern is fundamentally a traceability
gap, and traceability gaps are best closed with explicit,
publication-quality documentation rather than profile changes. It
also addresses the secondary "name implies continuous variable only"
concern without renaming the IG, which would be disruptive at this
stage.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Instead of a free-standing comparison page, add the mapping as
**`Measure.mapping` entries** (or, where structural, as
`StructureDefinition.mapping`) on the US SAFR `Measure` profile
itself, with `mapping.identity = "saner"` and `mapping.uri` pointing
at the SANER `Measure` profile canonical. Each US SAFR element that
corresponds to a SANER element/slice/extension would carry a
`mapping.map` string describing the relationship; SANER elements that
were intentionally dropped would be enumerated in the profile
description under a "SANER elements not carried forward" subsection
with rationale. The IG name / continuous-variable concern would be
addressed separately by a one-paragraph clarification in the IG home
page.

#### Justification

Embedding the mapping inside the `StructureDefinition` makes it
machine-readable, survives IG re-publishing, and is the FHIR-native
way to express cross-profile correspondence (the same mechanism
already used for v2/CDA mappings on core resources). It is more
durable than a hand-maintained narrative page that can drift from the
profile. Trade-off vs. Disposition A: it is more effort up front,
harder for non-implementers to read at a glance, and does not lend
itself as well to listing *omitted* extensions (which is part of the
reporter's ask) — those still need a narrative section. It is also
slightly awkward given that SANER is currently expired, so the mapping
target is not a live, normative artifact.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Restore the prior 2025-09-16 resolution of **Considered for Future
Use**: do not produce a SANER ↔ US SAFR mapping in this cycle, and
revisit only if/when the SANER IG is itself revived — ideally with
SANER positioned as a parent IG of US SAFR, at which point the
correspondence becomes structural rather than documentary.

#### Justification

The prior workgroup vote (12-0-2) on this exact rationale is recent
and well-supported: SANER is currently expired and is not compatible
with the Da Vinci DEQM alignment that US SAFR has adopted. Producing
and maintaining a detailed map to an expired IG carries an ongoing
maintenance cost (the SANER artifact may change or be retired
entirely) for relatively narrow benefit. The "name implies continuous
variable only" concern is a separate, smaller issue that can be
addressed with a one-line clarification without spinning up a full
mapping exercise.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The work group has already accepted, in the 2025-08-12 action item,
that the mapping work needs to happen; the 2026-03-31 re-open after
STU1 publication confirms the work group is no longer comfortable
leaving this as "Considered for Future Use." Disposition A delivers
the artifact the reporter is actually asking for — a clear,
human-readable comparison plus an honest statement about which
measure scoring types US SAFR really supports — at the lowest risk to
the published profile. Disposition B's machine-readable mappings are
attractive in principle but mapping into an expired SANER artifact
overstates the durability of the link, and the omitted-elements piece
still needs narrative anyway. Disposition C is hard to justify a
second time given the explicit re-open and the WGM discussion already
held; if the work group does choose C, the IG-name / measure-types
clarification should still be picked up as a small standalone edit.
