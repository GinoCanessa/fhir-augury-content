## Summary

This ticket — comment 7 in a 9-comment series imported by Ron G. Parker
on behalf of TSMG (Terminology Services Management Group) — asks the
Public Health work group to move the canonical defining URL of the
MIHR (Maternal and Infant Health Research, "mmm" IG) ValueSet
`PregnancyProcedureDelivery` from the IG namespace
(`http://www.hl7.org/fhir/us/mihr/ValueSet/PregnancyProcedureDelivery`)
to the terminology namespace at terminology.hl7.org (THO). TSMG's
guidance, restated in the description, is that only ValueSets used as
required bindings on a primitive `code` data type should remain
anchored under `hl7.org/fhir`; everything else — including
externally-reusable ValueSets like `PregnancyProcedureDelivery` —
should live in THO and be registered there via a UTG ticket. The
ticket is part of the **Block-Vote-1 / Terminology** batch (FHIR-37136
through FHIR-37144) that applies the same THO-anchoring request to nine
distinct MIHR ValueSets; FHIR-37142 specifically covers
`PregnancyProcedureDelivery`. The original 2022-06-02 disposition
(*Considered for Future Use*, vote 21-0-2 by Ruby Nash / Sarah Gaunt)
deferred the work pending IG maturity and finalization of the draft
THO process; lynn_laakso reverted that disposition and re-opened the
ticket post-STU publication on 2023-03-29 so it can now be acted on.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Persuant to the TSMG guidance restated in the description, relocate
the `PregnancyProcedureDelivery` ValueSet's defining/canonical URL
from
`http://www.hl7.org/fhir/us/mihr/ValueSet/PregnancyProcedureDelivery`
to a THO-anchored URL of the form
`http://terminology.hl7.org/ValueSet/PregnancyProcedureDelivery` (or
the THO-conventional form the TSMG/UTG steward designates). Concretely:

1. File a UTG ticket against `HL7/UTG` to register the ValueSet (and
   any code-system pieces it depends on that are not already in THO)
   under terminology.hl7.org, including both the SNOMED and CPT
   variants surfaced in the connectathon Zulip thread.
2. Once UTG accepts and publishes, update the MIHR IG sources
   (`HL7/fhir-mmm-ig`, expected location
   `input/resources/valueset/PregnancyProcedureDelivery-mmm-*.xml` per
   the Zulip pointer) to reference the new THO canonical and remove
   the locally-defined ValueSet from the IG, replacing it with a
   dependency on the THO version.
3. Coordinate the same change across the eight sibling tickets
   (FHIR-37136 / -37137 / -37138 / -37139 / -37140 / -37141 / -37143 /
   -37144) so the entire Block-Vote-1 batch lands as one consistent
   IG release.

Mark FHIR-37142 *Persuasive*; track the UTG ticket via an issue link
and close FHIR-37142 only when the published MIHR IG points at the
THO-anchored canonical.

#### Justification

This disposition does exactly what TSMG asks and is consistent with
the established HL7 pattern: ValueSets that are not the target of a
required binding to the FHIR `code` primitive belong in THO so they
can be reused outside MIHR (e.g., by other Public Health IGs and
research IGs working on maternal health). It also restores the
original intent that was deferred only because, in 2022, the THO
intake process was still being finalized — a constraint
lynn_laakso's 2023-03-29 re-open comment explicitly says no longer
applies. Treating the nine sibling tickets as a block matches how the
batch was filed (Block-Vote-1) and how it was originally voted.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the *intent* of TSMG's guidance but stage the work
differently: instead of opening one UTG ticket per MIHR ValueSet,
file a **single umbrella UTG ticket** that lists all nine ValueSets
from the FHIR-37136..FHIR-37144 block (including
`PregnancyProcedureDelivery` and its SNOMED + CPT variants); resolve
all nine FHIR tickets *Persuasive with Modification* pointing to that
umbrella; and gate the IG-side edits in `HL7/fhir-mmm-ig` behind a
single MIHR release that swaps every affected canonical at once.

Where a ValueSet is a thin wrapper over a value set already published
elsewhere (e.g., a VSAC OID such as `2.16.840.1.113883.3.464.1003...`
seen in the September-2022 Zulip post), prefer **deprecating the
MIHR-defined ValueSet entirely** and binding directly to the upstream
canonical, instead of re-publishing it under THO.

#### Justification

The nine tickets share a description, reporter, label, and history;
processing them as a single UTG umbrella avoids nine parallel UTG
backlogs and keeps the MIHR IG's terminology section stable through
exactly one release cycle instead of nine. Eliminating MIHR
ValueSets that are pure wrappers of external code lists also reduces
the long-term stewardship burden — every additional THO-hosted
ValueSet is a future maintenance commitment for TSMG. The trade-off
versus Disposition A is sequencing risk: one umbrella ticket means
nothing ships until UTG approves the whole batch, which can stall
individual ValueSets behind the slowest one (e.g., the CPT variant,
which carries licence-handling complexity).

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark FHIR-37142 *Not Persuasive*. Keep
`http://www.hl7.org/fhir/us/mihr/ValueSet/PregnancyProcedureDelivery`
as the defining URL inside the MIHR IG, on the basis that (a) the
ValueSet is currently used *only* by MIHR profiles, (b) MIHR has
already published as STU under that canonical, and (c) the TSMG
"defining URL must be in THO" rule is guidance, not a balloted
normative requirement, and changing the canonical of a published STU
ValueSet is itself a breaking change for any implementer that bound
to it during the connectathon cycle (see the September-2022 Zulip
thread).

#### Justification

There is a real cost to relocating canonicals after STU publication:
existing implementers must re-bind, prior connectathon test data
(with mmm-SNOMED and mmm-CPT XML payloads called out in Zulip)
becomes inconsistent with the spec, and the IG needs at minimum a
"moved to" stub to remain navigable. If the ValueSet is genuinely
single-use inside MIHR, the cost of moving it to THO outweighs the
reuse benefit, and the THO guidance can be honoured prospectively for
new ValueSets in future MIHR ballots without retrofitting this one.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B (Alternative Approach — umbrella UTG
ticket, batched MIHR release, deprecate wrappers).

Disposition C underestimates the strategic cost of letting MIHR
remain out of step with TSMG: every other Public Health and research
IG that wants to reuse `PregnancyProcedureDelivery` (and the
connectathon evidence shows there is interest) is currently forced to
either copy/redefine the ValueSet or take a dependency on a non-THO
canonical, which is exactly the antipattern TSMG is trying to retire.
Disposition A is correct in spirit but treats the nine sibling
tickets as nine independent units of work; in practice they were
filed and voted as a block, lynn_laakso re-opened them as a block,
and they touch the same IG release artifact — managing them as a
block (Disposition B) is operationally cheaper for both Public
Health and UTG, and lets the workgroup retire any MIHR ValueSet that
is a pure wrapper of an external value set (for example the
VSAC-OID-only entries in the September-2022 Zulip post) instead of
re-publishing it. The remaining sequencing risk — one slow ValueSet
holding up the rest — is mitigated by allowing UTG to publish the
umbrella incrementally as long as the MIHR IG release waits for the
final entry.
