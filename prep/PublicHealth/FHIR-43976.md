## Summary

conversation with ONC about updating the **industry and occupation (I&O)
vocabulary**. Because the US Birth and Fetal Death Reporting (BFDR) FHIR

track that vocabulary refresh and update BFDR's bindings, examples, and
narrative once NIOSH/ONC publish the new value set(s). The ticket also

correspondence. No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the updated NIOSH/ONC industry and occupation vocabulary in BFDR
once it is published. Concretely, in the next BFDR release after the
NIOSH/ONC update is finalized:

1. Update the BFDR value set(s) and ConceptMap(s) that bind industry and
   occupation concepts (the BFDR mother-occupation, mother-industry,
   father-occupation, and father-industry bindings) so they point at
   the refreshed NIOSH/ONC code system / value set version.
2. Refresh any narrative pages and worked examples (mother / father
   demographics, occupation/industry exemplars) that show the prior
   codes, so the published examples validate against the new bindings.
3. Coordinate with US Core so the BFDR profile binding, US Core's
   occupation/industry guidance, and any shared value sets stay
   aligned, avoiding divergent terminology versions.
4. Add Amanda Deering and Lori Fourquet to the notification /
   stakeholder list for the change.

#### Justification

The reporter is the NIOSH terminology owner; aligning BFDR with the
authoritative federal I&O vocabulary is consistent with FHIR's general
preference for using maintained external terminologies rather than
forking. The 2024-10-15 comment thread already establishes work-group
intent ("re-open post STU2 publication") and the 2024-02-15 ballot
vote (25-0-2) showed strong consensus for the underlying change once
the vocabulary update lands. This disposition simply executes that
plan.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the intent of the change but **soften the binding strength and
sequence the work** instead of doing a hard cutover:

1. In the next BFDR publication, leave the existing required/extensible
   bindings in place but add a **must-support** note and an editorial
   warning that the I&O vocabulary is undergoing a NIOSH/ONC refresh,
   and reference the in-flight value set.
2. Introduce the new NIOSH/ONC value set as an **example or
   preferred-strength** binding alongside the current one, with a
   ConceptMap from old → new codes.
3. Promote the new binding to required/extensible only once NIOSH/ONC
   publishes a stable, normative artifact and US Core has aligned.

#### Justification

NIOSH/ONC have not yet published the updated vocabulary; tying a
required binding in BFDR to an unfinished external artifact creates
schedule risk for the IG and for implementers who must validate
production data. A staged binding plus a ConceptMap preserves backward
compatibility, lets implementers transition on their own timeline, and
keeps BFDR shippable independently of ONC's release cadence. It also
mirrors how US Core handles in-flight terminology changes (preferred →
extensible → required).

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline to act on this ticket in BFDR until NIOSH/ONC publish the
updated industry and occupation vocabulary. Close as **Not Persuasive
With Modifier** ("Considered for Future Use") with the modifier text
explaining that the work group will revisit once the upstream
vocabulary is published, and that the change should originate as a new
ticket against the affected BFDR bindings at that time.

#### Justification

The change request is contingent on an external artifact that does not
yet exist. FHIR ballot dispositions normally require a concrete
proposal the work group can implement; here, the specific codes,
hierarchies, and any needed mappings are unknown. Carrying the ticket
in "Triaged / Highest" indefinitely against an unscheduled external
deliverable increases backlog noise without producing a shippable
change. Declining now (with a clear stakeholder list — Amanda Deering
and Lori Fourquet — recorded on the ticket) and re-opening when the
NIOSH/ONC artifact is available keeps the queue actionable.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The 2024-02-15 ballot vote (25-0-2) and the 2024-10-15 comment to
re-open post STU2 make it clear the work group does intend to adopt
the refreshed NIOSH/ONC vocabulary, so an outright decline (C) misreads
the consensus. However, accepting as requested (A) commits BFDR to
republish on ONC's schedule and to a value set that has not yet been
finalized — a real risk for an IG that has just shipped STU2 and that
shares I&O semantics with US Core. Disposition B captures the work
group's intent, gives implementers a documented transition path
through a ConceptMap, keeps BFDR's release independent of upstream
slippage, and lets the binding tighten to required/extensible once
NIOSH/ONC and US Core are aligned. Add Amanda Deering and Lori
Fourquet to the stakeholder list as the reporter requested, regardless
of which disposition the work group selects.
