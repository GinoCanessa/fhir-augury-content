## Summary

The submitter requests that the **Background** section of the *Maternal
and Infant Health Research (MIHR) FHIR IG* be expanded to acknowledge
the influence that the **work / occupation of the mother and father**
can have on maternal and infant health outcomes. The ticket is
narrative-only — it asks that the IG's contextual background
discussion call out occupation/work as a meaningful determinant —
rather than asking for any new resource, profile, extension, or value
set at this time. The ticket carries `NegativeVote` and `Block-Vote-2`
labels because it was filed as a negative ballot comment that the
submitter has been unable to withdraw, and it was re-opened post-STU
publication with the disposition reverted from "Considered for Future
Use" so it can be revisited. No related Jira tickets, Zulip threads,
or GitHub items were found in the augury index.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a new paragraph (and, if appropriate, a short bulleted list of
example exposures) to the **Background** page of the MIHR IG that
explicitly calls out the **occupation / work** of both parents as a
factor that can materially affect maternal and infant health. The
addition should:

- Acknowledge that **maternal occupation** during preconception,
  pregnancy, and lactation can affect outcomes via physical
  exposures (chemicals, radiation, biologic agents), ergonomic
  stressors (lifting, prolonged standing, shift work), and psychosocial
  stressors.
- Acknowledge that **paternal occupation** prior to and around
  conception can also influence outcomes (e.g., reproductive toxin
  exposures affecting fertility and fetal development).
- Frame work/occupation as one element of the broader social and
  environmental determinants of maternal-infant health that motivate
  the research scope of the IG.
- Note that capturing structured work-related observations is
  out of scope for this STU but is a relevant area for future
  iterations.

The change is narrative-only — no profiles, extensions, value sets,
or examples are added. Cite an authoritative source (e.g., NIOSH,
ACOG, or peer-reviewed literature) so reviewers can validate the
claim.

#### Justification

The ask is modest, narrative, and aligned with the IG's research
framing. The 2022-07-14 disposition's stated concern — that
implementers might be confused if the Background mentioned
work-related needs without corresponding artifacts — can be defused
by making the Background paragraph explicit that work is being
mentioned as a **determinant motivating future research**, not as
something the current STU's resources directly capture. Adding the
text both honors the negative-ballot commenter's substantive concern
and unblocks her inability to withdraw the negative vote in Jira
(comment 2023-12-20). It is also consistent with broader Public
Health / SDOH guidance that occupational exposures are a
recognized determinant of perinatal outcomes.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than singling out parental occupation, expand the Background
section with a **broader determinants-of-health framing** that names
several non-clinical factors known to influence maternal and infant
outcomes — *occupation/work, environmental exposures, housing,
nutrition, access to care, social support* — and lists work as one
example. Include a forward-looking statement that future versions of
the IG may add structured representations (profiles/extensions or
references to existing SDOH/Occupational Data for Health work) for
some of these factors as use cases mature. Coordinate the wording
with the IG's existing SDOH narrative if any, and cross-reference
existing HL7 work (e.g., **Occupational Data for Health (ODH)**)
without normatively binding to it.

#### Justification

The underlying need — that the IG's Background acknowledges
non-clinical determinants of maternal-infant outcomes — is broader
than work alone. A determinants-of-health paragraph that includes
work scales better, future-proofs the Background against further
single-factor ballot comments, and lets the IG point implementers
at existing HL7 work (ODH) for occupation specifically. It still
satisfies the original commenter (work is named) while reducing
the risk that the Background reads as elevating one determinant
above others. The trade-off vs. Disposition A is a slightly larger
narrative edit and the need for editorial coordination with any
existing SDOH text.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request on the basis that the IG's current scope is
research-oriented and does not yet model work / occupation
artifacts; adding background text about work without supporting
profiles or value sets risks setting implementer expectations that
the IG cannot meet at STU. Re-affirm the original 2022-07-14
disposition ("Considered for Future Use") and defer the narrative
expansion to a future ballot cycle that also introduces the
relevant artifacts (e.g., references to ODH).

#### Justification

This mirrors the original work-group reasoning that prevailed at
the prior vote (21-0-2). It is defensible on scope-management
grounds: an IG's Background should describe what the IG actually
addresses, and adding determinants the IG does not represent could
mislead implementers. However, this option leaves the negative
ballot commenter unable to withdraw her vote (per her 2023-12-20
comment) and re-litigates a position the work group already chose
to revisit by re-opening the ticket post-STU.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B captures everything Disposition A does — the
Background will explicitly name parental work/occupation as a factor
in maternal and infant health — while addressing the original
work-group concern that prompted the 2022 "Considered for Future
Use" decision: that singling out work in the Background, without
supporting artifacts, could confuse implementers. Framing work as
one of several determinants, and explicitly cross-referencing
existing HL7 work (notably **Occupational Data for Health**) as
the future home for structured representation, gives implementers
the right context, satisfies the negative-ballot commenter
(unblocking her inability to withdraw the vote), and positions the
IG for a coherent SDOH/determinants story in a future ballot cycle
without committing the current STU to new normative content.
Disposition A is a reasonable fallback if the work group prefers a
minimal edit; Disposition C should be avoided because the ticket
was deliberately re-opened post-STU to revisit the prior decline,
and re-declining without engaging the substance is unlikely to
resolve the negative vote.
