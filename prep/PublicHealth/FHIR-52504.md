## Summary

This ticket is a wording-change comment (logged as "Comment 4") against the
US Helios Query and Response (FHIR) IG. The reporter notes that the IG's
historical framing — which contrasts FHIR-based query/response with
previous "push"-style standards such as HL7 v2 and CDA — omits the
widely deployed HL7 v2-based **immunization queries**, which are a
recognized counter-example of HL7 v2 being used in a query/response (not
purely "push") pattern in U.S. public health. The reporter asks the IG
to acknowledge HL7 v2 immunization queries for full context, while still
making the case that for **new** query/response work HL7 FHIR is the
better capability and that HL7 v2-based queries will not be displaced
quickly. The ticket has been dispositioned **Persuasive with
Modification**, with a resolution note that text will be added as
suggested. No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub
items were surfaced for this ticket.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the IG narrative passage that currently reads:

> "Whereas previous interoperability standards such as HL7 v2 and CDA
> provided public health with the ability to receive data 'pushed' by
> data submitters …"

so that it explicitly acknowledges that HL7 v2 has long been used for
**query/response** in U.S. public health, with **immunization queries
(IIS query/response, e.g., HL7 v2 VXQ/VXR and the QBP^Q11/RSP^K11
profiles defined by CDC/AIRA)** as the canonical counter-example.
Suggested rewrite (workgroup to refine final wording):

> "Whereas previous interoperability standards such as HL7 v2 and CDA
> primarily supported public health receiving data 'pushed' by data
> submitters, HL7 v2 has also been used for query/response in selected
> domains — most notably immunization queries between providers and
> Immunization Information Systems (IIS). HL7 FHIR provides improved
> query/response capabilities for new work; existing HL7 v2 query
> exchanges are expected to remain in production for the foreseeable
> future."

This matches the resolution comment ("will add text as suggested") and
keeps the ticket in scope as a wording change (label
`Q&R-WordingChanges/Additions`).

#### Justification

The reporter's point is factually correct and is already reflected in
deployed U.S. public-health infrastructure (IIS query/response is one
of the most widely used HL7 v2 query patterns in the country). Adding
the qualification removes a misleading absolute ("previous standards
… push") without weakening the IG's argument that FHIR is the right
foundation for new query/response work. It also preempts a likely
class of negative ballot comments from IIS / immunization
implementers who would otherwise read the current wording as
dismissive of their existing investment. The change is narrative only
— no conformance, profile, or computable artifact is affected — so the
implementation cost is negligible.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the substance of the request but limit the IG change to a
**single qualifying clause** rather than a full rewrite of the
historical-context passage. For example, change "previous
interoperability standards such as HL7 v2 and CDA provided public
health with the ability to receive data 'pushed' by data submitters"
to "… provided public health with the ability to receive data
'pushed' by data submitters, and in selected domains (notably
immunization information systems) to perform basic query/response."
Defer any deeper background discussion of HL7 v2 query patterns
(VXQ/VXR, QBP/RSP, IIS profile) to a footnote or to the
"Background / Related Standards" section if one exists.

#### Justification

Disposition A risks expanding an introductory framing paragraph into
a mini history of HL7 v2 query patterns, which is out of scope for
this IG. A minimally-invasive qualifier still resolves the reporter's
substantive concern (the wording no longer reads as if HL7 v2 was
push-only) while keeping the introductory section short and on-topic.
This also reduces editorial review burden for the block vote and
makes the change easier to ballot as a non-substantive wording fix.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket "Not Persuasive" on the grounds that the contested
sentence is a high-level historical generalization in an
introductory section, not a normative claim about HL7 v2's
capabilities, and the IG already states elsewhere that HL7 FHIR is
the recommended foundation for new query/response work. Leave the
existing wording unchanged.

#### Justification

The disputed sentence is descriptive of the *predominant* historical
pattern, and intro paragraphs in IGs frequently use such
generalizations for readability. Calling out HL7 v2 immunization
queries specifically risks pulling other "but HL7 v2 also did X"
counter-examples into the same paragraph (lab orders/results queries,
ADT queries, etc.), bloating the introduction without changing the
IG's actual recommendations. This disposition would only be
defensible if the workgroup believes the wording fix sets a precedent
of having to enumerate every HL7 v2 query pattern in the IG
introduction.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A (with B as an acceptable lighter-weight
fallback).

The Jira resolution already records the workgroup's intent
("Persuasive with Modification — will add text as suggested"), and
the ticket carries the `BlockVote6HeliosQR` label, indicating it is
queued for block-vote ratification. The reporter's factual point is
correct and well-known in the U.S. public-health community —
immunization queries are one of the most visible HL7 v2 query/response
deployments in production. The cost of the change is one edited
paragraph in the IG's historical-context narrative, with no impact on
profiles, conformance, or computable artifacts. Accepting the
modification removes an avoidable irritant for IIS / immunization
implementers reviewing the IG and aligns the framing with the IG's
own thesis (FHIR is better *for new work*) rather than overstating
what HL7 v2 was previously used for. Disposition B is a reasonable
fallback if the workgroup wants the smallest possible textual delta,
but A is preferred because it more clearly carries the reporter's
intent and reduces the chance of another wording ballot comment in
the same paragraph.
