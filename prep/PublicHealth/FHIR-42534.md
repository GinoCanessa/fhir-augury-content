## Summary

FHIR-42534 is an open question raised against the US Bidirectional Services
eReferrals (BSeR) IG asking whether it is realistic for community-based
organizations (CBOs) — the typical destinations for social-services referrals
in BSeR — to host secure FHIR Subscriptions over the data they hold, and how
that subscription model is supposed to behave when a referral intermediary
(referral hub / aggregator / network) sits between the referral source and
the CBO. The reporter specifically calls out the case where the intermediary
is a *pure pass-through*, which raises questions about who owns the
subscription endpoint, where the topic and notification live, and how
authorization and trust are established end-to-end. The ticket is filed as a
"Question" rather than a change request, and there are no cross-referenced
Jira tickets, GitHub items, or Zulip threads in the Augury index that
elaborate on it, so the workgroup will need to take a position on it without
prior public discussion to anchor on.

## Details

**Description:**

Is it realistic for community-based organizations (CBOs) to offer secure
subscriptions to data in their systems? How would that work when there is an
intermediary? When the intermediary is a pure pass through?

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat the ticket as a request for normative guidance and add a new
"Subscriptions and CBOs" section to the BSeR IG narrative (under the existing
"Exchange patterns" / workflow pages of the `HL7/bser` IG) that:

1. Affirms that CBOs **MAY** expose a FHIR Subscription endpoint on the
   `Task` (referral) resource and on key referral payload resources
   (e.g., `ServiceRequest`, `Procedure`, feedback `Observation` profiles)
   so that referral sources can be notified of status changes and feedback
   without polling.
2. Recommends the R4 Subscriptions R5 Backport (`SubscriptionTopic` +
   `Subscription` with `R4/Backport` profiles) as the conformance target,
   which aligns BSeR with the broader US Realm direction (US Core, Da Vinci,
   Bulk Notifications) rather than legacy R4 criteria-based subscriptions.
3. Defines two intermediary patterns:
   - **Pass-through intermediary:** the CBO hosts the Subscription
     endpoint; the intermediary forwards subscription create / delete and
     notification traffic without altering payloads. Authorization tokens
     are scoped to the CBO; the intermediary does not re-sign notifications.
   - **Active intermediary:** the intermediary hosts the
     `SubscriptionTopic` and notification endpoint on behalf of the CBO and
     fans out updates from CBO systems via internal mechanisms. The
     intermediary becomes the Subscription "server" of record.
4. Lists the small number of `SubscriptionTopic` resources BSeR would need
   to publish (referral status changed, referral feedback available,
   referral cancelled) and pins the `Subscription.channel.type` options
   the IG will support (rest-hook initially, with message and email noted
   as optional).

#### Justification

This directly answers the ticket's question with a concrete, testable
conformance story. It reuses the R5 Backport Subscriptions framework that
Public Health and other US Realm IGs are already standardising on, so BSeR
implementers are not asked to invent a new mechanism. Distinguishing the
"pure pass-through" intermediary case (which the reporter explicitly calls
out) from an active intermediary clarifies who issues tokens, who is the
Subscription server of record, and where notifications originate — the
exact ambiguity that prompted the ticket. The change is additive narrative
plus a small set of `SubscriptionTopic` instances; it does not alter
existing BSeR profiles and so is low-risk for current implementers.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than mandating that *CBOs* host Subscriptions, recommend an
**intermediary-mediated subscription pattern** as the default for BSeR and
treat CBO-hosted subscriptions as an optional advanced capability.
Specifically:

- Publish `SubscriptionTopic` definitions in the BSeR IG (referral status
  changed, feedback available, cancellation), but specify that the
  *referral intermediary / hub* is the expected Subscription server.
- Require the intermediary to translate CBO-side updates (whether they
  arrive via FHIR `Task`/`Bundle` POSTs, HL7 v2, CSV, or a portal action)
  into FHIR notifications that conform to the BSeR topic.
- Add a "Capability Tiers" matrix that documents three CBO conformance
  levels — (i) portal-only CBO (no FHIR), (ii) FHIR-receiving CBO,
  (iii) FHIR-publishing CBO with subscriptions — and aligns each tier
  with realistic data flows.
- For the "pure pass-through" case, explicitly state it is **not**
  recommended for subscription traffic, because pass-through forwarding
  cannot solve the trust / authorization split between CBO and source.

#### Justification

Field experience in social-services referral networks (211s, Unite Us,
findhelp / Aunt Bertha, regional CIE / closed-loop referral platforms)
shows that the majority of CBOs do not operate FHIR servers at all, let
alone secure Subscription endpoints with token issuance, replay /
retention, and 24×7 availability. Putting the Subscription server at the
intermediary keeps the burden where the FHIR capability actually exists,
matches Da Vinci / SDOH-CC patterns where a network platform mediates
between EHRs and CBOs, and avoids publishing conformance language that
implementers will simply ignore. It also gives a defensible answer to the
"pure pass-through" question — pass-through is fine for unidirectional
referral delivery but is not an appropriate place to terminate
subscriptions, because the intermediary cannot then rotate or scope
tokens, deduplicate notifications, or guarantee delivery on the CBO's
behalf.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket with the disposition **Persuasive with Modification —
Not Persuasive for spec change**: acknowledge the question, but do not
add subscription guidance to the BSeR IG at this time. Instead, note in
the ticket resolution that:

- BSeR's current normative content is the request / response / feedback
  flow over `Task` and supporting profiles; subscriptions are out of
  scope of the current STU.
- General Subscription guidance is owned by the FHIR-I Subscriptions
  workstream (R5 Backport IG) and any cross-IG patterns belong there or
  in SDOH-CC, not duplicated in BSeR.
- The question can be reopened as a concrete change request once a CBO
  / intermediary implementer brings forward a real-world subscription
  use case to the Public Health WG.

#### Justification

The ticket is filed as a *Question*, not a change request. There are no
linked tickets, no Zulip discussion, no GitHub PRs, and no in-person
requesters — i.e., no implementer is currently blocked. Adding
speculative subscription narrative to BSeR risks (a) diverging from the
authoritative R5 Backport Subscription guidance once it stabilises, and
(b) imposing conformance expectations on CBOs that very few can meet.
Declining keeps the BSeR IG focused on the referral exchange it was
chartered to standardise, and defers a multi-IG / multi-WG concern to
the venues that already own it.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The reporter is asking a *practical feasibility* question, not requesting
a specific spec change, and the honest answer in the current US realm is
"most CBOs cannot realistically run a secure FHIR Subscription endpoint;
the intermediary almost always has to." Disposition B encodes that
reality: it gives BSeR a clear, tiered conformance answer, publishes the
small set of `SubscriptionTopic`s the workflow actually needs, and
explicitly addresses the "pure pass-through" sub-question by ruling it
out for subscription termination — which is exactly the ambiguity Sarah
raised. Disposition A is attractive but over-promises CBO capability and
will likely produce paper conformance; Disposition C closes a legitimate
implementer question without giving the community any guidance. B
threads the needle: it commits the WG to a defensible subscription
pattern without burdening CBOs, and it leaves room to add a
CBO-as-Subscription-server profile later if a real implementer brings
that use case.
