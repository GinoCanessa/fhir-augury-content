## Summary

The ticket asks the MedMorph Research Data Exchange Content IG to relax its
position that "data captured outside the Data Source and communicated directly
to data marts" is entirely out of scope. The reporter notes that, in real-world
healthcare organizations, the path from automated extract to the researcher is
frequently a hybrid one: dedicated personnel review, augment, and reconcile
extracted data before it is transmitted, because the IG-driven extract cannot
realistically close every gap. The concrete suggestion is to add an **optional,
manual data staging and review step** to the "Figure – Research ETL process
steps" diagram in section 3.2, placed between the automated extract and the
"End" of the flow, so the diagram acknowledges that automatically-extracted
data may legitimately be staged, reviewed, and adjusted before final
transmission to the researcher. There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets,
Zulip threads, or GitHub items associated with this ticket in the indexed data.

## Details

**Description:**

While it's tidy to indicate that "Data captured outside the Data Source and
communicated directly to data marts," is out of scope of the Content IG, for
some use cases Healthcare Organizations will desire a hybrid automated/manual
process for the ultimate data submission to the Researcher. This is because
there will continue to be gaps and issues with the data that cannot be
addressed by the Content IG. Additionally, there are often dedicated personnel
within the Healthcare Organization who perform such tasks and are responsible
for the ultimate integrity of the outbound data. One suggestion would be to add
an optional, manual data staging and review step to the "Figure – Research ETL
process steps" diagram in 3.2, prior to the "End" of the flow. This would allow
automatically extracted data to be staged, reviewed, and augmented or adjusted
where necessary prior to final transmission to the researcher.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the "Research ETL process steps" content in section 3.2 of the US
MedMorph Research Data Exchange Content IG so that the workflow explicitly
recognizes a hybrid automated/manual path:

1. In the figure, insert an **optional "Stage & Review"** step between the
   automated extraction step(s) and the "End" terminator. Render it as an
   optional/conditional node (e.g., dashed border or labeled "Optional")
   so it is clear the step is not mandated by the IG.
2. Add a short narrative paragraph immediately under the figure that:
   - Acknowledges that some Healthcare Organizations will route extracted
     data through an internal staging area where designated personnel may
     review, augment, or correct the data prior to transmission.
   - States that this manual step is **out of conformance scope** for the
     Content IG (i.e., the IG does not constrain *how* the staging is
     performed) but is recognized as a valid implementation pattern.
   - Notes that the data ultimately transmitted to the Researcher must
     still conform to the IG's profiles and bundle expectations,
     regardless of whether it passed through a manual review step.
3. Keep the original "directly to data marts" language but reframe it as
   one of two recognized patterns rather than the only in-scope pattern.

#### Justification

The change is editorial / narrative — it does not alter any FHIR profile,
extension, search parameter, or operation. It addresses a real gap reported
from implementation experience: in practice, healthcare organizations rarely
transmit raw automated extracts straight to a researcher without some form
of curation, and the current IG language can read as discouraging that
pattern. Acknowledging the optional staging step keeps the IG's conformance
surface unchanged while making the documented workflow consistent with how
implementers will actually deploy it. This pattern (recognizing
out-of-conformance operational steps in narrative) is common across HL7 IGs
when the standard wants to describe a realistic end-to-end flow without
constraining local processes.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than modifying the figure, add a new **non-normative narrative
subsection** (e.g., 3.2.x "Operational Considerations: Manual Staging and
Review") that:

- Describes the hybrid automated/manual pattern in detail.
- Explicitly calls out that any manual augmentation/correction must
  preserve provenance and that the conformance bundle delivered to the
  Researcher remains governed by the IG's profiles.
- Recommends (but does not require) capturing the manual-review activity
  using `Provenance` resources referencing the affected resources, so
  downstream Researchers can distinguish purely-automated data from
  data that was manually adjusted.
- Cross-links to the existing "out of scope" statement so the reader
  sees both the conformance boundary and the recognized operational
  pattern in one place.

The figure in 3.2 is left unchanged to avoid re-balloting the diagram.

#### Justification

This satisfies the reporter's underlying need — formal recognition of the
hybrid workflow — without modifying the canonical process diagram, which
is often more disruptive to update (image regeneration, downstream
references, translated artifacts). A narrative subsection is also a
better venue for the nuance the reporter raises: that integrity of the
outbound data is owned by HCO personnel, that gaps in automated extract
will persist, and that there is a recommended (but optional) provenance
pattern. The Provenance recommendation is value-added: it gives
Researchers a way to reason about data lineage without requiring it,
keeping the change non-breaking. The trade-off versus Disposition A is
that readers who only look at the figure may still infer the workflow
is purely automated; mitigated by an explicit "see §3.2.x" callout
beneath the figure.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change on the basis that the existing "out of scope"
language is correct and intentional: the Content IG governs the
*conformance contract* for data exchanged with the Researcher, not the
operational processes inside a Healthcare Organization. Implementers are
already free to perform manual staging, review, and augmentation prior
to transmission; the IG does not need to depict every optional internal
step to permit it. Mark the ticket as "Not Persuasive" with a comment
pointing the reporter at the existing scope statement and noting that
internal HCO workflows are intentionally unconstrained.

#### Justification

Adding optional, non-normative steps to a normative process diagram can
create confusion about what is and is not part of the IG's conformance
surface, especially for implementers who read figures more closely than
narrative. There is also a slippery-slope concern: any number of
optional operational steps (de-identification review, legal review,
IRB sign-off, queue management) could be argued for inclusion on the
same basis. Keeping the IG silent on internal HCO workflow preserves a
clean conformance boundary and avoids implying that the listed manual
step is somehow privileged among the many internal steps an HCO may
choose to perform.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B best balances the reporter's concern with the IG's need to
keep its conformance surface uncluttered. The reporter's actual problem
is that the IG's current language reads as discouraging a hybrid
automated/manual workflow; a focused, non-normative subsection directly
addresses that without re-touching the canonical figure or expanding the
normative scope of the IG. The added Provenance recommendation is a
small but meaningful upgrade: it gives Researchers a way to reason about
data lineage when manual augmentation occurs, which is the underlying
risk the reporter is actually flagging ("integrity of the outbound
data"). Disposition A is a reasonable second choice if the workgroup
prefers visual recognition in the diagram, but it incurs more editorial
churn and risks readers interpreting the optional node as part of the
conformance surface. Disposition C is defensible on scope grounds but
leaves the reporter's documented friction unaddressed and arguably
under-serves implementers who are already shipping the hybrid pattern in
production.
