## Summary

This ticket is a forward-looking "breadcrumb" filed by Saul Kravitz at the
end of the VRDR STU3 development cycle. During 2024, members of the NVSS
Community of Practice (CoP) leveraged VRDR resources to integrate funeral
home systems with Electronic Death Registration Systems (EDRSs), primarily
to let funeral homes supply decedent demographic information. The companion
Medicolegal Death Investigation (MDI) IG is also developing support for
Vital Records Office–Medical Examiner/Coroner (VRO–ME/C) interactions that
have a Funeral Home–VRO aspect. The ticket asks the Public Health WG to
work with funeral home and EDRS implementers in the STU4 cycle to expand
the set of funeral home interactions formally supported by VRDR — examples
named are: requesting permission to cremate, requesting permission to
move/disinter the remains, and submitting/updating decedent demographic
information. No specific resources, profiles, or operations are proposed
in the ticket; it exists explicitly so the topic is reconsidered in STU4.

There are no Jira cross-references to this ticket in the augury index, so
no other tickets directly extend or constrain its scope.

## Details

**Description:**

During 2024, several members of the NVSS COP have leveraged VRDR resources
to integrate funeral home systems and EDRSs in support of funeral homes
providing decedent demographic information. The MDI IG is development
support for VRO-ME/C interactions that also have a Funeral Home - VRO
aspect. VRDR should work with Funeral Home system developers and EDRS
developers to expand the set of funeral home interactions supported by
VRDR. Examples of these interactions could include:

- request permission to cremate
- request permission to move/disinter the remains
- submission/update of decedent demographic information
- etc

This ticket is a breadcrumb created at the end of the STU3 development
cycle to ensure that these issues are reconsidered in the future.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Accept the ticket as a tracked STU4 work item. Concretely, the Public
Health WG (VRDR project) commits to scoping, during the STU4 cycle, an
explicit set of Funeral Home ↔ EDRS / Funeral Home ↔ VRO interactions in
the VRDR IG. The minimum scoped set should cover the three examples named
in the ticket:

1. **Request permission to cremate** — modeled as a Task / ServiceRequest
   exchange between a Funeral Home actor and the EDRS / VRO, with a
   defined response indicating authorization status.
2. **Request permission to move / disinter the remains** — modeled
   similarly to (1), distinguished by a coded reason / activity.
3. **Submission / update of decedent demographic information** — a write
   path from a Funeral Home system into the EDRS, reusing the existing
   VRDR Decedent profile and demographic components, with provenance
   identifying the funeral home as the source.

The work item should be entered into the VRDR STU4 backlog with named
follow-on tickets per interaction, coordinated with the NVSS CoP
funeral-home-to-EDRS subgroup and with the MDI IG team for VRO-side
alignment.

#### Justification

This is what the ticket literally asks for: a breadcrumb to ensure the
topic is reconsidered in STU4. The reverted "Considered for Future Use"
resolution and the comment trail explicitly direct the WG to reopen this
post-STU3 publication. NVSS CoP work is already happening in the field
(Axiell and others actively participating in a funeral home → EDRS
subgroup, per Death-on-FHIR Zulip activity), so accepting the request
formalizes a real implementer demand rather than inventing new scope.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than expanding VRDR's IG with a broad new family of funeral-home
interactions in a single STU4 ticket, decompose this breadcrumb into two
narrower commitments:

1. **In VRDR STU4:** add only the demographic-submission/update path
   (Funeral Home → EDRS), because it is a write against the existing
   Decedent / demographic profiles and does not require new
   choreography. Tighten existing profiles and add a Provenance pattern
   identifying the funeral home as author.
2. **Move the cremation-authorization and disinterment-authorization
   interactions out of VRDR** and into either the MDI IG (where VRO
   choreography already lives) or a separate "Funeral Home Operations"
   companion IG, since these are operational permissions rather than
   vital-records reporting and risk widening VRDR's scope from
   *reporting deaths* to *operating funeral services*.

Close FHIR-46571 by replacing it with three child tickets reflecting this
split — one in VRDR for demographics, two in MDI / a new IG for the
authorization flows.

#### Justification

VRDR's primary mission is standardized death reporting to NVSS, not
governance of funeral home operations. Cremation and disinterment
authorizations are workflow / permissioning interactions that more
naturally align with MDI's existing VRO ↔ ME/C choreography or a
purpose-built funeral-operations IG. Splitting the work keeps VRDR's
profile set focused, avoids accidental coupling between reporting
profiles and operational authorizations, and lets the MDI WG continue to
own VRO-facing choreography it has already invested in. The demographic
update path, by contrast, is a natural extension of the existing VRDR
Decedent surface and belongs in VRDR.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-46571 as **Not Persuasive – out of scope for VRDR**, on the
basis that (a) funeral-home ↔ EDRS demographic exchange is already
implementable today using existing VRDR Decedent profiles plus standard
FHIR write/update interactions, and (b) cremation / disinterment
authorizations are operational workflows outside VRDR's vital-records
reporting scope. Implementers wanting to formalize those flows should
raise concrete tickets against MDI or propose a dedicated funeral-home
IG, rather than expanding VRDR.

#### Justification

The ticket itself is a placeholder — it proposes no specific change to
any VRDR resource, profile, value set, or operation. Block-voting a
breadcrumb that just says "reconsider this later" is weak grounds for
keeping a Change Request open across cycles; if implementers have
specific gaps, they should file specific tickets with concrete
deficiencies. Declining now does not foreclose the work: the NVSS CoP
subgroup activity surfaced in Zulip can continue, and any concrete
deficiencies they uncover can be filed as targeted tickets in the
appropriate IG.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

This ticket is, by design, a tracked commitment rather than a concrete
spec change, and the comment history makes that intent unambiguous: the
WG explicitly reverted a "Considered for Future Use" close and asked
that it be reconsidered in STU4. There is real, ongoing implementer
activity in the NVSS CoP funeral-home-to-EDRS subgroup, so the
underlying need is not speculative. Disposition C undersells that
demand and discards a useful planning anchor; Disposition B has merit
on scope grounds, but making that split now — without concrete child
tickets, implementer input, or alignment with the MDI WG — risks
prematurely partitioning work that the funeral-home subgroup may want
treated holistically. Accepting as requested keeps the breadcrumb live
for STU4, where the WG can decide the VRDR-vs-MDI split with the
benefit of concrete proposals from the subgroup, and lets follow-on
tickets carry the actual normative changes.
