## Summary

Reporter Craig Newman flags an apparent editorial mix-up in the VRDR IG's
section 3.1.2, where two return bundles — the demographics bundle and the
coded causes of death bundle — are described. He observes that the lead
sentences of the two introductory paragraphs appear to have been swapped:
the first paragraph opens with "cause of death" but then describes race
and ethnicity content (matching the figure beneath it), while the second
paragraph opens with "race and ethnicity" but then describes cause-of-death
content. The request is for the Public Health WG to review the section
and, if the inversion is confirmed, correct the prose so each paragraph's
opening sentence matches the bundle it describes (and the figure that
follows). No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items
were found that would expand the scope of this report.

## Details

**Description:**

In section 3.1.2, the demographics and coded causes of death bundles are
described. It feels like the first sentences of both paragraphs are
reversed. The first paragraph starts out with cause of death but then
talks about race and ethnicity (as does the image below it). The second
paragraph starts out with race and ethnicity but then talks about cause
of death. Please review, and if appropriate update, this section.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat the report as a confirmed editorial defect and swap the first
sentences of the two paragraphs in VRDR section 3.1.2 so that:

- The paragraph immediately above the demographics-bundle figure opens
  with a sentence that introduces the **demographics return bundle**
  (race, ethnicity, and any other demographic content actually shown in
  that figure), and the rest of the paragraph continues to describe
  demographics.
- The next paragraph opens with a sentence that introduces the **coded
  causes of death return bundle** and continues to describe cause-of-death
  content, matching its associated figure.

The change is narrative-only: edit the two opening sentences (and any
adjacent connective text such as "this bundle …" / "the bundle below …")
in the IG page that renders section 3.1.2 — typically a markdown file
under the VRDR IG's `input/pagecontent/` directory. No StructureDefinition,
profile, value set, or example needs to change. Re-publish the IG so the
TOC, anchor IDs, and figure captions remain stable.

#### Justification

The reporter's reading is consistent with normal IG conventions: each
narrative paragraph that introduces a bundle should match both the bundle
it describes and the figure shown directly beneath it. Inverted opening
sentences are a classic copy-edit artefact (paragraphs were rearranged
without re-touching the leads) and they actively mislead a first-time
reader who scans paragraph leads before diving into the figure. Because
the fix is purely editorial — no normative content, no element
definitions, no constraints — there is no backward-compatibility cost
and the change can land in the next VRDR errata / publication cycle
with minimal review burden.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than only swapping the two lead sentences, restructure section
3.1.2 so that each return bundle gets its own clearly-titled
sub-subsection (e.g., `3.1.2.1 Demographics return bundle` and
`3.1.2.2 Coded causes of death return bundle`), with the figure for each
bundle anchored inside its own sub-subsection. Each sub-subsection's
opening paragraph then unambiguously describes only its own bundle.

Concretely, edit the same `input/pagecontent/` page that renders 3.1.2 to:

1. Promote the existing two paragraphs to two `###`-level sub-subsections
   under 3.1.2.
2. Move the corresponding figure (and its caption) inside each
   sub-subsection so prose-and-figure stay co-located.
3. Re-write the two lead sentences to be self-contained ("This bundle
   contains …") rather than relying on paragraph order.
4. Leave a short shared introductory paragraph at 3.1.2 itself that
   simply names the two return bundles and links to the sub-subsections.

#### Justification

The same defect (paragraph-vs-figure mismatch) is hard to introduce when
each bundle owns a titled section with its own figure: future editors
cannot accidentally swap "lead sentences of adjacent paragraphs" because
there are no adjacent paragraphs to swap. It also gives implementers
direct anchor links (`#demographics-return-bundle`,
`#coded-causes-of-death-return-bundle`) that they can cite in tickets and
chat — useful given that VRDR is consumed by jurisdictions and EDRS
vendors who tend to reference IG sections by URL. The trade-off is a
slightly larger edit than Disposition A and a re-numbering of any
downstream references to 3.1.2 sub-content, which is why this is offered
as an alternative rather than the default recommendation.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as **Not Persuasive / Working as Intended** after WG review, on the
basis that the section was deliberately written to (a) introduce both
return bundles in a single paragraph each, with the lead sentence naming
the *contrast* (e.g., "Unlike the cause-of-death bundle described next,
this bundle …") rather than the topic, and (b) the figures are positioned
to illustrate the *paragraph that follows*, not the paragraph that
precedes. If the WG confirms this reading on inspection, leave the
narrative as-is and instead add a one-sentence editor's note clarifying
the figure-to-paragraph relationship.

#### Justification

Defensible only if the WG, on re-reading the published section together,
agrees that the apparent inversion is intentional and that the contrast-
first phrasing is preferable to a topic-first lead. The reporter is an
experienced VRDR contributor (Craig Newman), and his read is the natural
read for a first-time consumer, so declining outright should require an
explicit rationale that the current phrasing is intentionally
contrast-first; otherwise the request stands as a real defect and
declining will look like the WG dismissing a clear editorial bug.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

This is a low-risk, narrative-only correction reported by a credible
contributor against a published IG section, with a self-evident defect
description (paragraph leads inverted relative to the figures and the
following sentences). Disposition A directly fixes the reader-facing
problem with minimal editorial churn and no normative impact, and it
can be folded into the next VRDR errata publication. Disposition B is a
genuinely better long-term structure and the WG should consider it as a
follow-up if section 3.1.2 grows further, but proposing it now risks
expanding the scope of a one-paragraph fix and triggering a re-numbering
discussion that this ticket does not require. Disposition C should only
be selected if the WG, on re-reading the published section, explicitly
concludes that the contrast-first phrasing was intentional — absent that
confirmation, declining would be hard to defend to the reporter.
