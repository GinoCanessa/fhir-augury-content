## Summary

The reporter (Mitra Rocca) asks whether the US Central Cancer Registry
Reporting Content IG (CCRR) project intends to leverage **USCDI Version
2**, noting that the IG's stated objective is to capitalize on the
maturation of ONC-aligned standards (FHIR, CCDS, USCDI v1, standardized
patient APIs). Filed in January 2022 against the IG that, at the time,
referenced USCDI v1, the ticket is effectively a request to upgrade the
IG's USCDI alignment from v1 to v2. The ticket has since been re-opened
post STU1 publication (May 2024) and most recently updated (March 2026)
to record that the CCRR work group is now incorporating **USCDI v3** in
CCRR IG 2.0, making the original v2-specific request moot. No
cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were
surfaced for FHIR-35037.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the CCRR IG narrative and conformance content to explicitly
align with **USCDI Version 2**: replace USCDI v1 references in the IG
introduction / scope text with USCDI v2, audit each profile (Patient,
Condition, Observation/Lab, Medication*, Procedure, AllergyIntolerance,
Encounter, Immunization, etc.) against the USCDI v2 data class /
element list, and add must-support flags or value-set bindings for any
elements newly required by USCDI v2 (e.g., the v2 additions around
clinical tests, encounter information, and provenance). Document the
USCDI v2 alignment in a dedicated page of the IG.

#### Justification

The ticket's literal ask is a v1 → v2 upgrade. Accepting As Requested
keeps the IG aligned with the certification baseline that the
introduction promises to "leverage". It also preserves a clean audit
trail back to the originally filed concern.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Skip USCDI v2 and align CCRR IG 2.0 directly to **USCDI v3** (and
record this on FHIR-35037 as the resolution). Concretely: update the
IG's introductory text to cite USCDI v3 instead of v1, re-derive
must-support elements from the USCDI v3 data class set, and — where
practical — anchor the CCRR profiles on the corresponding US Core
profiles whose current published versions are USCDI v3-aligned, rather
than re-implementing v2-era constraints that will be superseded
immediately.

#### Justification

USCDI v2 was published in July 2021 and has since been superseded by v3
(2022) and later versions in the ONC's annual cycle. Investing in a v2
alignment for an IG entering its 2.0 cycle would ship already-stale on
day one, force a second migration almost immediately, and produce two
sets of conformance churn for implementers. Aligning to v3 honors the
*intent* of the ticket (track the current USCDI baseline) while
matching the work group's stated direction in the 2026-03-06 comment
("we are incorporating USCDI V3 in CCRR IG 2.0").

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve as **Not Persuasive** (the current resolution on the ticket):
the request to adopt USCDI v2 is no longer actionable because CCRR IG
2.0 is being aligned to USCDI v3, which fully supersedes v2 for the
data classes CCRR cares about. Close the ticket with a pointer to the
USCDI v3 alignment work in CCRR IG 2.0; no v2-specific spec change is
made.

#### Justification

The ask was filed in 2022 against the v1 baseline; in the intervening
years ONC has published USCDI v3 and beyond, and the CCRR work group
has already chosen v3 as the next alignment target. Adopting v2 now
would either (a) duplicate the v3 work or (b) ship an IG that is
out-of-date on publication. There is no implementer use case that USCDI
v2 satisfies but USCDI v3 does not, and there is no breaking-change
risk in skipping v2. This matches the disposition recorded by the
work group on 2026-03-06.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** C

The ticket has effectively been overtaken by events: the USCDI baseline
has advanced two revisions since FHIR-35037 was filed, and the CCRR
work group has already committed to incorporating USCDI v3 in CCRR IG
2.0 (per the 2026-03-06 comment and the recorded *Not Persuasive*
resolution). Disposition A would lock the IG to a now-superseded USCDI
revision and impose a redundant migration burden on implementers.
Disposition B captures the right *intent* but the actual USCDI v3
alignment work is best tracked under its own dedicated ticket(s) tied
to the CCRR 2.0 release, not retro-fitted onto a 2022 v2 request.
Closing FHIR-35037 as Not Persuasive — with an explicit pointer in the
resolution comment to wherever the USCDI v3 alignment work is being
tracked for CCRR IG 2.0 — keeps the ticket queue honest and lets the
v3 work proceed on its own terms.
