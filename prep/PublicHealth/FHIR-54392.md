## Summary

The submitter (a NAACCR community member, entered on their behalf) questions
why the "Date Report Generated" data element in the US Central Cancer
Registry Reporting (CCRR) IG is neither required nor Must Support, given
that vendor software always stamps a generation date and that NAACCR data
item 2110 (the corresponding registry element) is required by both NPCR and
SEER. Leaving the element optional and not-MS in the IG conflicts with
those downstream conformance expectations.

The submitter further recommends a broader treatment of date/time
information in the bundle: capture (1) the date/time for each contributing
resource (so trigger events that fall between collection cycles can be
attributed to the moment they actually occurred, not the moment the bundle
was rolled up), and (2) a clear date/time at which the bundle was
considered "complete." They tie the definition of a complete bundle to the
USCDI+Cancer Registry use case minimum data set (the 26 strongly-recommended
elements) and ask that the IG be made unambiguous about which dates are
required where. They note the IG appears to already require an "asserted
date" on every trigger event but say that intent is not stated clearly.

The closely related ticket [FHIR-54430](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54430)
("Date report generated"), filed by the same reporter on the same day,
narrows in on the Bundle-level `bundle.timestamp` and asks for clarification
of what triggers bundle assembly (encounter open vs. encounter close). Its
proposed resolution — make `bundle.timestamp` 1..1 and Must Support, and
update the use-case description to clarify that triggers are based on
encounter close — is the change FHIR-54392's resolution adopts for the
"date report generated" piece. The broader recommendation in FHIR-54392
(date/time on every contributing resource, formal definition of a
"complete" bundle tied to USCDI+Cancer) is deferred to a future ticket per
the proposed resolution.

## Details

**Description:**

> Why is Date Report Generated not required and not supported? The
> date/stamp is part of vendor software so not requiring it is puzzling.
> In addition, Date Report Generated is mapped to NAACCR data item 2110,
> which is required by NPCR and SEER -allowing Date Report Generated it
> to be "Not Required"/"Not must support" goes against those two
> requirements.
>
> Recommendation: 1) Capture the date/time for each contributing
> 'resource' and 2) the date/time when the FHIR bundle was considered
> assembled ("complete"). If the trigger event happened between T0 and
> T15, or T15 and T30, it needs to be noted for each trigger event. Just
> because the report (FHIR "bundle") is generated at T15 or T30 does not
> meant that the trigger event occurred at that time. The definition of
> a 'complete' FHIR bundle should tie back to the minimum data set (the
> 26 data items that were defined as strongly recommended for a cancer
> case/tumor as part of USCDI + Cancer Registry use case) such that, at
> the minimum, the date/time fields listed in the USCI+Cancer data
> elements list need to be incorporated in the FHIR bundle, that is, the
> CCRR IG needs to allow those data elements to be included and
> supported. Finally, it seems that it indeed does that by requiring the
> "asserted date" for every trigger event, but it is not stated clearly.

**Proposed Resolution (Persuasive with Modification):**

> We agree with the recommended change to tighten the constraint to 1..1
> and MS on the reporting bundle date (see also FHIR-54430).
>
> For the recommended change to dates in all other resources, a separate
> ticket will be created for potential future consideration.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the full scope the reporter described, in two coordinated edits to
the CCRR IG:

1. **Bundle-level date.** On the Central Cancer Registry Reporting Bundle
   profile, change the constraint on `Bundle.timestamp` (the "Date Report
   Generated" element) from optional/not-MS to **1..1 and Must Support**.
   Update narrative on the use-case description page to state plainly that
   bundle assembly is triggered by encounter **close**, and that
   `Bundle.timestamp` records the moment of assembly, not the moment the
   underlying clinical event occurred.

2. **Per-resource date/time.** Walk every contributing-resource profile in
   the CCRR IG (Conditions, Observations, Procedures, etc. that the IG
   uses as trigger events) and require + Must Support an "asserted date"
   element on each, so the actual moment of each trigger event is
   preserved independently of when the bundle was rolled up. Tie the
   definition of a "complete" CCRR bundle, in the IG narrative, to the
   USCDI+Cancer Registry minimum data set (the 26 strongly-recommended
   cancer case/tumor elements), and explicitly call out which date/time
   elements from that list are required in the bundle.

#### Justification

Both halves of the request map directly to documented downstream
requirements: NAACCR data item 2110 (the registry-level analogue of
`Date Report Generated`) is required by both NPCR and SEER, so leaving the
FHIR analogue optional creates a known conformance gap. The per-resource
asserted-date requirement is what lets registries reconstruct *when* a
trigger event actually occurred when bundles are produced on a cadence
(T15 / T30) — without it, downstream timing analytics collapse to the
bundle-generation timestamp. Tying "complete bundle" to the USCDI+Cancer
minimum data set gives implementers a single, unambiguous bar to clear and
aligns the IG with the use-case it was scoped against.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach (Adopt the Resolution as Filed)

#### Proposal

Adopt only the bundle-level change now, exactly as proposed in the
resolutions of FHIR-54392 and the related FHIR-54430:

- Tighten `Bundle.timestamp` on the Central Cancer Registry Reporting
  Bundle to **1..1 and Must Support**.
- Update the use-case description page to clarify that the trigger for
  bundle assembly is encounter **close**.
- Do **not** tighten the per-resource "asserted date" elements in this
  pass; defer the broader date/time treatment (per-resource dates,
  USCDI+Cancer alignment, formal "complete bundle" definition) to a
  newly-filed future-consideration ticket, as the resolution promises.

This is the change set the WG appears to have already converged on.

#### Justification

The bundle-timestamp tightening is low-risk and uncontested: the data is
machine-generated by the producing system, the NAACCR / NPCR / SEER
requirement is documented, and FHIR-54430 already proposes the same fix.
Splitting the broader per-resource and "complete bundle" work into its own
ticket lets the WG (a) actually enumerate which contributing-resource
profiles need date constraints raised, (b) test impact on existing CCRR
implementers before tightening cardinality across many profiles, and (c)
coordinate with the USCDI+Cancer minimum-data-set work without holding the
bundle-date fix hostage. FHIR-54430's own resolution explicitly walked
back tightening the encounter start date "due to concerns about making the
profile overly restrictive" — the same concern applies, in spades, to a
sweeping per-resource change. A staged path mitigates that risk while
still closing the immediate NPCR/SEER gap.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (Not in This Cycle)

#### Proposal

Decline FHIR-54392 in its filed form on the grounds that the bundle-date
piece is fully addressed by [FHIR-54430](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54430)
(same reporter, same date, same recommended fix), and the remaining
"date/time on every contributing resource + formal complete-bundle
definition" piece is too broad to land without explicit per-profile impact
analysis and USCDI+Cancer alignment. Mark FHIR-54392 as a duplicate of
FHIR-54430 for the bundle-date change and split the broader recommendation
out as a stand-alone enhancement ticket against the next CCRR IG cycle.

#### Justification

The two tickets cover the same surface and arrived together; resolving
both as "Persuasive with Modification" with the same fix risks duplicate
PRs and confusing change-history. Treating FHIR-54392 as a duplicate
keeps the audit trail clean. The broader recommendation — touching every
contributing-resource profile and re-anchoring "complete bundle" to the
USCDI+Cancer 26-element list — is enhancement-scoped, not a bug, and
deserves its own discovery rather than being pulled into a "tighten one
cardinality" change request.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B matches the resolution language already on both tickets
("Persuasive with Modification" with the bundle-timestamp tightening and a
deferred future-consideration ticket for the broader changes) and is the
cleanest path to actually shipping a fix. Disposition A is the right
*destination* but bundles too much risk into one change: tightening
cardinality and Must Support on date elements across every contributing
resource in the CCRR IG would meaningfully restrict existing and in-flight
implementations, and FHIR-54430's own resolution already shows the WG
backing away from a similar tightening on encounter start date. Disposition
C is administratively tidier but throws away the reporter's broader
recommendation rather than tracking it; the resolution text on FHIR-54392
already commits to filing a follow-up ticket, so B captures both the
immediate fix and the deferred work without losing either. The WG should
confirm the CCRR IG repository, run `repo-analysis` against it, and then
plan the `Bundle.timestamp` constraint change plus the use-case page edit
as a single PR, with the deferred per-resource work tracked as a separate
ticket.
