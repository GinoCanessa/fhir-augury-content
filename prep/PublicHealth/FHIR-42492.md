## Summary

The submitter (Lloyd McKenzie, originally as Comment 43 imported into Jira)
asks the BSeR authors to clarify how response payloads delivered back to the
referral source are scoped to the originating referral. Two specific
sub-questions are raised:

1. When a recipient returns reports/observations/etc., does the requester get
   *every* report from the encounter, or only those that are (directly or
   indirectly) `basedOn` the initiating `ServiceRequest`?
2. Are pre-referral data (results, observations, history that pre-date the
   referral) considered in scope of the response, or only data generated as a
   result of executing the referred service?

The cross-reference index returns **no** linked Jira, Zulip, GitHub or
Confluence items for FHIR-42492. However, it is part of a coordinated batch
review of the BSeR IG that Lloyd filed in early September 2023; several
sibling tickets touch the same response/payload-scoping concern and provide
useful context for disposition (see *Related Jira Tickets* below). In
particular, FHIR-42476 challenges the single-payload assumption,
FHIR-42483 calls for a defined retrieve operation, FHIR-42490 proposes a
filter-on-source RESTful retrieval model, FHIR-42484 challenges the use of
Composition for response packaging, and FHIR-42515 (already
*Resolved – change required*) loosens the `basedOn` constraint that this
question depends on.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a normative "Response Scope" section to the BSeR IG that answers both
sub-questions explicitly:

- **In-scope response data MUST chain to the initiating
  `ServiceRequest`** via `.basedOn` (directly or transitively through `Task`,
  `CarePlan`, or another permitted initiator after FHIR-42515 lands). Any
  resource returned in the response Bundle MUST either:
  - have a `basedOn` (or equivalent workflow back-pointer such as
    `Procedure.basedOn`, `Observation.basedOn`,
    `DiagnosticReport.basedOn`, `MedicationRequest.basedOn`,
    `CarePlan.basedOn`) that resolves to the initiating request; **or**
  - be a `Provenance`/supporting resource referenced from a resource that
    does.
- **Pre-referral data is out of scope of the response** by default. If
  pre-referral context is needed, it must be carried in the *referral
  (request) payload* (the existing "data requirements" / pushed Bundle),
  not in the response.

Materially, this would add: an invariant on the response Bundle profile
(every entry's "primary clinical resource" SHALL chain to
`%resource.where(...)` — the initiating `ServiceRequest`), a paragraph in
the Response Workflow page, and one or two illustrative examples showing
both an in-scope and an out-of-scope resource.

#### Justification

This matches the IG's already-stated workflow assumption (push of a
referral, then bounded response) and uses the FHIR-native `basedOn` chain
that the recipient's EHR is most likely to be able to populate. It is also
the narrowest reading of "response to a referral", which is desirable for
information-sharing/PHI minimization in a Public Health context. It is
internally consistent with FHIR-42515's resolution (which explicitly
expects `basedOn` to be the linkage mechanism, just to a wider set of
initiator types) and with FHIR-42476's request that the *requester* control
how much detail to retrieve — once the in-scope set is defined, depth
filtering becomes a separate, clean conversation.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Address FHIR-42492 by adopting the source-side filter model proposed in
FHIR-42490, instead of (or in addition to) a `basedOn`-only definition:

- Define a **`$bser-retrieve` operation** (per FHIR-42483) on the referral
  source. Inputs: the initiating `ServiceRequest` reference (or its
  identifier) and an optional recipient-supplied query Bundle / FHIR Query
  expression. Output: a batch-response `Bundle` constrained to the data
  the source deems relevant to that referral.
- The "relevance" rule the source enforces is layered:
  1. The candidate resource is `basedOn` (directly or transitively) the
     initiating `ServiceRequest`, **or**
  2. The candidate resource is in a recipient-requested category (e.g.,
     "labs from the last 12 months", "active medications") **and** is
     permitted by the IG's per-service-code data-requirements profile for
     that referral type.
- This approach answers "do you get pre-referral data?" with "only when the
  service code's data-requirements profile says you may, and the recipient
  asked for it."

#### Justification

Disposition A answers FHIR-42492 in isolation but leaves several
adjacent BSeR review tickets (FHIR-42476, -42483, -42484, -42490)
without an architectural home. Disposition B threads them together: the
source becomes the policy decision point (so the recipient can ask freely,
which is RESTful and avoids the "trusted recipient" problem flagged in
FHIR-42490), the question of "all reports vs. only `basedOn` reports" is
answered by the operation's contract, and pre-referral data can be
included *when, and only when,* the per-service-code data-requirements
profile says so. The trade-off is scope: this is closer to a redesign of
the response workflow than a clarification, and it depends on resolving
FHIR-42483 and FHIR-42490 first. It is, however, a one-time architectural
move that pays down several tickets at once.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket *Persuasive with Modification* and close as **already
addressed by FHIR R5 workflow conventions plus FHIR-42515**: state that the
in-scope response data is whatever resolves through the standard
`*.basedOn` workflow chain to the initiating request (now that FHIR-42515
has broadened the allowed initiator types), and that pre-referral data is
out of scope unless explicitly required by the receiving service's data
requirements. No new IG text beyond a one-line cross-reference to the FHIR
core workflow module would be added.

#### Justification

The two questions in the ticket arguably already have FHIR-core answers:
`*.basedOn` is the workflow back-pointer, and there is no FHIR convention
under which pre-existing data is implicitly "in scope of" a workflow
request. A conservative reading is that BSeR does not need its own rule —
just a pointer to the workflow module. This is defensible if the WG
believes additional IG text would over-specify and risk diverging from
core. The downside is that real implementers have already asked the
question, which is itself evidence that the implicit answer is not
discoverable; declining will likely reproduce the question in subsequent
ballot cycles.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition: B (Alternative Approach)** — with **A** held in
reserve as the fallback.

FHIR-42492 is one of a tightly-coupled cluster of Lloyd McKenzie tickets
(FHIR-42476, -42483, -42484, -42490, -42515) that together describe a
single architectural problem in the BSeR response workflow: *who decides
what counts as "the response", and how is it transported?* Disposing of
FHIR-42492 in isolation under Disposition A produces a correct but narrow
answer that will need to be revisited as soon as the WG works the related
tickets, because the `basedOn`-only rule does not resolve the
recipient-as-untrusted-querier problem (FHIR-42490) or the "interim and
role-dependent results" problem (FHIR-42476). Disposition B answers
FHIR-42492 *and* gives the WG a coherent place to land the others: the
operation contract becomes the canonical statement of in-scope response
data, with `basedOn`-chain membership as the default rule and per-service
data-requirements profiles handling pre-referral and recipient-tailored
cases. If the WG judges B too large for this ballot cycle, fall back to A
(it is forward-compatible with B), and avoid C — declining will not stop
implementers from re-asking the question.
