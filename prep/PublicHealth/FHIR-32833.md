## Summary

Craig Newman (Public Health) is asking the US Birth Defect Reporting (BDR)
FHIR IG to consider harmonizing its representation of **gravida** (number
of pregnancies) and **para/parity** (number of deliveries) with already
established international profiles, specifically:

- The Australian NCDHC child-health profiles
  `ncdhc-observation-gravida` and `ncdhc-observation-parity`
  (au-fhir-childhealth IG).
- The Dutch ZIB 2017 STU3 obstetric profiles for gravida and parity
  (Nictiz package 145782 / 145764).
- The CHOICE International Birth and Child Model on Simplifier.

The ticket is a "consider" request rather than a concrete change
proposal — it asks the BDR work group to evaluate alignment with these
prior-art profiles when designing how gravida and parity are captured
in BDR.

No related Jira tickets are linked or cross-referenced. There are no
GitHub cross-references in the FHIR Augury index. Recent (2025) Zulip
discussion in the IPS stream independently confirms that gravida/parity
representation remains a live cross-IG harmonization concern, including
LOINC codes 11996-6 (Gravidity) and 11977-6 (Parity) and the broader
TPAL pattern (Term, Preterm, Abortus, Living).

## Related Zulip Discussions

No Zulip messages reference `FHIR-32833` directly. The two threads below
do not mention the ticket key but are highly topical to the gravida/parity
modeling question and provide useful context for the work group.

### [IPS > Representing a full history of pregnancies](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/IPS/topic/Representing%20a%20full%20history%20of%20pregnancies)

Started by Morten Ernebjerg (2022) and re-opened by Grahame Grieve and
Carl Severson in June 2025, this thread debates how (and whether) the
IPS pregnancy profiles can represent a complete pregnancy history rather
than a single point-in-time summary. Carl Severson argues that
**gravidity and parity are basic clinical measures that the IPS does
not capture today** and proposes LOINC 11996-6 (Pregnancies) and 11977-6
(Parity) plus optional TPAL (Term/Preterm/Abortus/Living) as the
minimal interoperable representation. Grahame Grieve agrees that explicit
support is needed "for interoperable outcomes" and that the existing
Observation Pregnancy - Outcome profile is not appropriate for a list
of pregnancies. The thread surfaces real ambiguity (multiples, fetal
death, jurisdictional definitions of parity) but converges on the view
that gravida + parity (with optional TPAL granularity) is the smallest
useful structured representation.

Participants: Morten Ernebjerg, Josh Mandel, Giorgio Cangioli, Rob Hausam,
Grahame Grieve, Carl Severson.

### [implementers > FHIR Resource for Pregnancy Episode](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/implementers/topic/FHIR%20Resource%20for%20Pregnancy%20Episode)

A 2016 thread where Binu DGIT describes an EHR with a pregnancy episode
identifier (ANC-NO) capturing gravida, parity, gestational age and
risk factors across antenatal, delivery and postnatal encounters.
Grahame Grieve and Brian Postlethwaite recommend modeling the episode
as `EpisodeOfCare` (or linked Encounters via `Encounter.partOf`) and
the discrete maternal data as a combination of `Condition` and
`Observation`, noting that "dealing properly with pregnancy care is on
the future roadmap." This historical context confirms that gravida and
parity have always been modeled as Observations in core FHIR, not as
dedicated elements.

Participants: Binu DGIT, Grahame Grieve, Brian Postlethwaite, Lloyd
McKenzie.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add (or align existing) Observation profiles in the US Birth Defect
Reporting IG named `Observation-gravida-bdr` and `Observation-parity-bdr`
that mirror the structure of the Australian `ncdhc-observation-gravida`
/ `ncdhc-observation-parity` profiles and the Nictiz ZIB 2017 gravida
and parity profiles. Specifically:

- `Observation.code` fixed to LOINC **11996-6 "[#] Pregnancies"** for
  gravida and LOINC **11977-6 "[#] Parity"** for parity (these are
  the codes already used by the AU and NL profiles and are the codes
  Carl Severson cites in the IPS thread).
- `Observation.value[x]` constrained to `valueQuantity` with UCUM unit
  `{#}` (count), cardinality 1..1.
- `Observation.subject` constrained to the BDR mother Patient profile.
- `Observation.effective[x]` left as `dateTime` (point-in-time count).
- A short narrative section in the BDR IG cross-walking these two
  profiles to the AU NCDHC, NL ZIB and CHOICE equivalents to make the
  harmonization explicit.

#### Justification

Adopting the LOINC codes and shape already used by NCDHC, ZIB and
CHOICE means BDR data can be consumed by international tooling that
recognizes those codes, and BDR consumers can read AU/NL pregnancy
profiles without re-mapping. The IPS Zulip thread (June 2025) confirms
the wider community is converging on these same LOINC codes as the
minimal representation. The reporter (Craig Newman) explicitly named
these three sources, so this disposition delivers exactly what the
ticket asks for.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than authoring BDR-specific profiles, **reuse an existing
upstream profile by reference**. The most defensible options are:

1. Reference the IPS pregnancy profiles once IPS adopts gravida/parity
   (the active 2025 IPS thread suggests this is on the table), and
   add a BDR-local extension only if BDR needs jurisdictional fields
   (e.g., TPAL granularity).
2. If IPS adoption is not imminent, define BDR profiles as **derived
   from** the AU NCDHC profiles (or copy their element constraints
   verbatim) and bind to the same LOINC value set, while leaving room
   for an optional `tpal` extension component for `term`, `preterm`,
   `abortus`, `living` counts to handle the multi-gestation edge cases
   surfaced in the IPS thread.

#### Justification

Authoring a fresh BDR-only profile risks divergence from the very
international profiles the ticket asks to harmonize with. Anchoring on
an upstream (IPS) or directly-derived (NCDHC) profile makes
harmonization structural rather than narrative, and the optional TPAL
component addresses the multi-pregnancy ambiguity Carl Severson
documents (G1P1(L2) twins, vanishing twin, etc.) without imposing it
on every BDR submitter. Trade-off vs. Disposition A: more upstream
coordination overhead, but lower long-term maintenance cost and lower
risk of profile drift.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline as **not actionable in the current BDR scope**. Rationale: BDR
is a birth-defect reporting IG focused on the *infant* defect record;
maternal pregnancy-history elements such as gravida and parity are
already representable with plain core FHIR `Observation` + the LOINC
codes 11996-6 and 11977-6, without any BDR-specific profile. Defer
harmonization to the IPS or to a dedicated maternal-health IG.

#### Justification

The ticket is a "consider" request from 2021 with no comments, no
linked tickets and no implementer pressure recorded in the FHIR Augury
index. BDR's primary mission is reporting birth defects, not
standardizing gravida/parity. The IPS work group is actively debating
this exact representation in 2025; duplicating it inside BDR risks
producing a third incompatible profile that then needs to be reconciled
later. Implementers who need gravida/parity in a BDR submission can
already use the LOINC-coded `Observation` shape, which is what the AU
and NL profiles also do at their core.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B captures the reporter's intent (genuine harmonization
with NCDHC, ZIB and CHOICE) without committing BDR to maintain a
parallel profile that will inevitably drift from the upstream sources.
Pinning to the AU NCDHC structure plus an optional TPAL component
covers both the simple "G1P1" use case and the multi-gestation edge
cases that the 2025 IPS Zulip discussion shows are real and
unresolved. Disposition A is a reasonable fallback if the work group
prefers a self-contained BDR profile and is willing to track upstream
changes manually. Disposition C understates the value of explicit
harmonization — saying "use Observation with the right LOINC code" is
exactly the kind of unwritten convention that the AU, NL and CHOICE
profiles exist to make explicit, and BDR submitters benefit from the
same explicit guidance.
