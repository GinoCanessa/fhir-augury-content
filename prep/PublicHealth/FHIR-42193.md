## Summary

FHIR-42193 ("Consider anchoring canonical in THO 2") is a brief change request
filed against the US Bidirectional Services eReferrals (BSeR) FHIR IG by Ron
G. Parker (Public Health work group) during ballot reconciliation. It asks the
BSeR authors to consider "anchoring canonical in THO" — i.e., publishing the
IG's terminology-style canonical artifacts (value sets, code systems, naming
systems, and any other reusable terminology resources) under the HL7
Terminology (THO, `terminology.hl7.org`) canonical base rather than under the
BSeR IG's own canonical base. The body is a single-line ballot import (Comment
5, marked imported by Ron G. Parker) and the title is suffixed with "2",
strongly suggesting it is a duplicate or follow-up of an earlier "anchoring
canonical in THO" comment on the same ballot. The ticket is labelled
`BSeR_THO` and was filed at `Highest` priority but has remained `Triaged` /
`Unassigned` since 2023.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Migrate BSeR's reusable terminology artifacts to THO as their canonical home.
Concretely:

- Identify every `CodeSystem`, `ValueSet`, `NamingSystem`, and `ConceptMap`
  defined in the BSeR IG that is (a) potentially reusable across IGs, and
  (b) not strictly tied to BSeR-internal modelling.
- For each such artifact, change its canonical from
  `http://hl7.org/fhir/us/bser/...` to `http://terminology.hl7.org/...`,
  contribute it to the THO repository
  (`HL7/UTG` / `terminology.hl7.org`), and remove the local definition from
  the BSeR IG. BSeR profiles, extensions, and value-set bindings then
  reference the THO canonicals.
- Keep BSeR-specific artifacts (those with no plausible reuse outside BSeR)
  anchored under the BSeR canonical.
- Coordinate with the Vocabulary work group (THO stewards) and time the move
  to a BSeR republication so that downstream binding URLs are updated in one
  pass.

#### Justification

Anchoring shared terminology in THO is the HL7-recommended pattern: it
gives terminologies an IG-independent identity, lets them be versioned and
governed by the Vocabulary WG, and avoids the BSeR-IG canonical "owning"
content that other IGs (e.g., other Public Health referrals work) would also
benefit from. The ticket was filed by Ron G. Parker, who has been a
consistent advocate for THO-anchoring across IG ballots, and the
`BSeR_THO` label indicates the BSeR authors had already grouped this work
for batch resolution.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Do a scoped, reuse-driven migration rather than a blanket move:

- Define a short reuse test: an artifact moves to THO only if it is (i)
  already used by another HL7 IG, (ii) a clear candidate for reuse by an
  in-flight IG, or (iii) a registry/identifier system whose semantics are
  not BSeR-specific.
- Move only the artifacts that pass that test; everything else stays under
  the BSeR canonical with a documented rationale.
- For each migrated artifact, leave a short "moved to THO" note in the BSeR
  IG narrative pointing to the THO canonical, so implementers tracking the
  old URL find the new one.
- Use this ticket to capture the reuse review and spawn one
  follow-up Jira per artifact actually being moved, so each move is
  individually tracked through Vocabulary WG.

#### Justification

A blanket "anchor everything in THO" move is operationally expensive and
risks moving artifacts that genuinely have no consumer outside BSeR, which
adds governance overhead without reuse benefit and can break implementer
URLs that were stable for years. A reuse-driven move keeps THO focused on
genuinely shared terminology while still resolving the substance of Ron
Parker's comment for the artifacts where it actually matters. It also
gives the Vocabulary WG a reviewable, per-artifact change set rather than a
single bulk migration.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request. Keep BSeR's terminology artifacts anchored under the
BSeR IG canonical (`http://hl7.org/fhir/us/bser/...`). Document in the
disposition that the BSeR-defined value sets and code systems are
sufficiently BSeR-scoped that THO anchoring would add governance overhead
without producing reuse, and that any future cross-IG reuse can be handled
on demand by migrating the specific artifact at that time.

#### Justification

Declining is defensible if the BSeR authors can show that the IG's
terminology is genuinely BSeR-internal — most BSeR value sets describe
referral types, service categories, and form structures that are not
obviously consumed by other IGs. THO migration carries real cost
(coordination with the Vocabulary WG, republication of THO, updating every
binding URL in BSeR, implementer churn on canonical URLs) and is not free
to reverse. If no concrete cross-IG consumer is identified, holding the
canonical URLs stable in BSeR is the lower-risk option. The original
ballot comment is also a single line with no specific list of artifacts,
which makes "Decline, persistent disagreement noted" a procedurally
acceptable response.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the right starting point for the work group. The substance
of Ron Parker's comment — that genuinely reusable terminology should live in
THO, not in an IG canonical — is a long-standing HL7 architectural
preference and is almost certainly correct for at least *some* of BSeR's
artifacts (referral reason codes, service-type identifiers, naming systems
for referral platforms are typical candidates). At the same time, a blanket
Disposition A move is heavier than the one-line ballot comment justifies
and would force the Vocabulary WG to absorb artifacts that may have no
consumer outside BSeR. Disposition B captures the intent of the comment,
gives the BSeR authors a defensible, per-artifact criterion to apply, and
produces an auditable trail (one follow-up Jira per artifact actually
moved) that the work group can track to closure. Disposition C should only
be selected if the BSeR authors can demonstrate that *none* of the IG's
terminology artifacts are reuse candidates, which is unlikely for a
referral IG.
