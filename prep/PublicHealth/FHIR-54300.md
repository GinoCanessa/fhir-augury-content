## Proposed Dispositions

> Note: because the ticket body is a single sentence with no specifics and

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Ask the reporter (Sarah Gaunt) to clarify the specific guidance gap, then
update the Electronic Case Reporting IG narrative to:

1. Add an explicit "Vital Signs" sub-section under the eCR profiling /
   Observation guidance that states whether eCR senders SHALL/SHOULD/MAY
   conform to the base FHIR `http://hl7.org/fhir/StructureDefinition/vitalsigns`
   profile (or the corresponding US Core Vital Signs profiles) when
   communicating vital-sign Observations as part of an eICR or RR bundle.
2. Spell out which of the standard vital-signs panel members (BP systolic /
   diastolic, body height, body weight, body temperature, heart rate,
   respiratory rate, oxygen saturation, BMI, head circumference) are
   in-scope for eCR use cases, and how to handle absent or unknown values
   (`dataAbsentReason` vs. omission).
3. Cross-link the new section from the existing Observation / clinical-data
   guidance pages so that implementers landing on either page find the
   vital-signs rules.
4. If the work group concludes that eCR should *constrain* the base profile
   (e.g., to require a specific LOINC panel code or units), capture those
   constraints in a new `Observation-eCR-vitalsigns` profile rather than only
   in narrative.

#### Justification

The reporter is asking for "updating guidance," which most naturally maps to a
narrative change rather than a structural one. Aligning eCR explicitly with
the base `vitalsigns` profile (and, where applicable, US Core Vital Signs)
keeps eCR consistent with the broader FHIR ecosystem and matches the pattern
the work group has used elsewhere when layering profiling rules over base
Observation guidance. Capturing any genuinely eCR-specific constraints in a
dedicated profile (rather than prose only) keeps validators able to enforce
them.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than authoring new eCR-specific vital-signs guidance, update eCR to
**defer to US Core Vital Signs** (and through it the base FHIR `vitalsigns`
profile) by reference. Concretely:

1. In the eCR IG's Observation guidance page, add a short subsection that
   says vital-signs Observations in eICR/RR SHALL conform to the
   corresponding US Core Vital Signs profile when one exists (e.g.,
   us-core-blood-pressure, us-core-body-height, us-core-pulse-oximetry, …)
   and otherwise SHOULD conform to the base FHIR `vitalsigns` profile.
2. Do not author an eCR-specific vital-signs profile; instead, slice the
   existing eICR / RR composition / bundle profiles to point at the US Core
   profiles for the vital-signs `Observation` references.
3. Add a worked example to the IG showing a US Core blood-pressure
   Observation included via a Section.entry in an eICR.

#### Justification

eCR in the US realm is already tightly coupled to US Core, and most
implementers building eCR senders/receivers also build to US Core. Layering
yet another vital-signs profile on top of US Core Vital Signs risks
divergence and increases the maintenance burden on the work group every time
US Core re-profiles a vital sign. Deferring keeps eCR thin and lets US Core
own the vital-signs rules, at the cost of slightly less self-contained
guidance for non-US-Core implementers — which is acceptable because eCR is a
US-realm IG.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the ticket as **not actionable as filed** and ask the reporter to
re-file with: (a) the specific eCR IG page or section whose vital-signs
guidance is inadequate, (b) a description of the implementer problem this
guidance change would solve, and (c) a proposed wording change or constraint.
If the reporter does not respond within the work group's standard
not-enough-info timeout, close as "Not Persuasive — Insufficient Information."

#### Justification

The ticket as written is a single-sentence prompt with no description body,
no comments, no linked tickets, no Zulip discussion, and no referenced
artefacts. Acting on it would require the work group to invent both the
problem and the solution, which is not a sound basis for an IG change. FHIR
process generally treats Change Requests without an articulated problem
statement as not actionable, and triaging this back to the reporter is the
lowest-cost way to surface the real issue (if any) without spending work-group
cycles on speculation.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** C

The ticket contains no information beyond its title — no description body, no
comments, no cross-references, no related Zulip threads, and no GitHub items
in the Augury index. Both Disposition A and Disposition B require the work
group to guess what "guidance on Vital Signs" the reporter has in mind, and
either could end up solving a problem the reporter wasn't actually raising.
The cheapest and most respectful next step is to ask Sarah Gaunt to expand
the ticket with a concrete problem statement and proposed change, and only
then decide between an eCR-authored vital-signs profile (Disposition A) or a
defer-to-US-Core approach (Disposition B). If, during the work-group call, the
reporter is present and can articulate the gap in real time, the disposition
can be upgraded on the spot to A or B without waiting for a re-file.
