## Summary

Craig Newman has submitted a wording/structure change request against the
**US Helios Query and Response (FHIR)** Implementation Guide's *Introduction*
section. The ticket asks the editors to (1) introduce TEFCA earlier in the
document — possibly by moving the existing *Relationship to TEFCA* content
up into the *Introduction* — (2) expand all acronyms on first use (TEFCA in
the *Policy Consideration* section is cited as the offending example), and
(3) consolidate concepts that are currently defined or discussed in multiple
places (Minimum Necessary; the role of QHINs/HIEs; the manual nature of
existing exchanges and data-use agreements) into a single up-front definition
that later sections can reference.

The ticket has been **Triaged** and a resolution of *Persuasive with
Modification* has already been applied: the editors agreed to make the
requested changes but flagged a need to confirm exactly *how* to surface
TEFCA content earlier than its current page 6 location. There are **no
cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items** linked to
this ticket in FHIR Augury, so all context comes from the ticket itself.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Restructure the IG's `index.md` (Introduction) page so that:

1. A new *TEFCA Context* subsection is added at the top of the Introduction,
   constructed by moving the existing *Relationship to TEFCA* prose up from
   its current location (≈page 6) into the Introduction. Leave a one-line
   pointer where the section used to live so readers following an old anchor
   still land in the right place.
2. Every acronym used in the Introduction and *Policy Consideration*
   sections is expanded on first use — at minimum **TEFCA** (Trusted
   Exchange Framework and Common Agreement), **QHIN** (Qualified Health
   Information Network), **HIE** (Health Information Exchange), and any
   others surfaced by an editorial pass. Add the expansions to the IG's
   acronym/glossary table if one exists; create one if it does not.
3. The recurring concepts called out by the reporter — *Minimum Necessary*,
   the role of *QHINs/HIEs*, and the *manual nature of existing exchanges /
   data use agreements* — are each defined once in a new *Key Concepts*
   subsection of the Introduction. Downstream sections that currently
   re-define those concepts are trimmed to a sentence-level reference that
   links back to the canonical definition.

#### Justification

The ticket has already been resolved *Persuasive with Modification*, so the
working group has effectively pre-committed to making the requested changes.
The proposal is purely editorial — no resources, profiles, value sets,
operations, or conformance statements change — so the implementer impact is
zero while the reader-comprehension benefit is real. Defining acronyms on
first use and consolidating duplicated concepts are standard HL7 IG style
expectations, and pulling TEFCA context forward matches the way most US
Realm IGs (e.g., US Core, USCDI-aligned guides) frame the regulatory
backdrop before diving into transactions.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Address the underlying clarity problem without physically relocating the
*Relationship to TEFCA* section:

1. Add a short **"At a Glance"** paragraph (3–5 sentences) at the top of the
   Introduction that names TEFCA, QHINs, and HIEs in plain language and
   links forward to the existing *Relationship to TEFCA* and *Policy
   Consideration* sections rather than moving them.
2. Introduce an **acronym/glossary table** at the end of the Introduction
   and require all body sections to expand acronyms on first use *within
   that section* (rather than only the first use document-wide), so readers
   landing on a deep page from a search engine still see expansions.
3. For the duplicated-concept concern, add a small **"See also"** callout
   box at each duplicated definition pointing to a single canonical
   definition, rather than deleting the in-context definitions outright.
   This preserves the readability of each section when read in isolation.

#### Justification

Moving the *Relationship to TEFCA* section wholesale (Disposition A) breaks
existing inbound anchors and any external citations that point at its
current location, and it forces TEFCA-specific content on readers who only
need the transactional parts of the IG. The alternative keeps the existing
section structure stable, addresses the editor's "confirm if/how to add
TEFCA content earlier" caveat in the recorded resolution, and improves
discoverability via glossary + cross-references without forcing readers to
read regulatory framing before they can get to the FHIR mechanics. It also
keeps each downstream section self-contained, which is friendlier to
implementers who land on a single page from search.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request as out-of-scope for the current ballot cycle: the
content of the IG is unchanged by these edits, the Introduction is already
considered informative (not normative), and the reporter has not identified
a concrete reader who was misled. Close as *Not Persuasive* with a note
that editorial polish can be deferred to a future publication.

#### Justification

This is the weakest of the three options and is included primarily as the
"do nothing" baseline. It is defensible only on the narrow grounds that
purely editorial tickets compete with substantive ballot reconciliation
work for editor time. Given that the working group has already marked the
ticket *Persuasive with Modification*, declining now would contradict that
recorded position and would need a fresh vote.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is already resolved *Persuasive with Modification*, the changes
are purely narrative, and there is no implementer impact to weigh against.
Disposition A directly executes what the reporter asked for and what the
work group has effectively already agreed to. The one open question the
editors flagged — *how* to surface TEFCA earlier than page 6 — is best
answered by the concrete relocation in Disposition A, with the small
forward-pointer breadcrumb at the old location to mitigate the only real
downside (broken anchors) that Disposition B was designed to avoid. If, in
implementation, the editors find that moving the *Relationship to TEFCA*
section wholesale is too disruptive to the surrounding flow, they can fall
back to the "At a Glance" paragraph from Disposition B without re-balloting.
