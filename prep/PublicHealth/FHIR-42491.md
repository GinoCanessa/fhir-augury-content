## Summary

The ticket asks the BSeR work group to clarify whether the bundle defined for a
referral represents the **complete set of data the receiver is permitted to
have** or merely **the initial push payload** that may be supplemented later by
the receiver querying the sender for additional data. Lloyd McKenzie raises the
distinction because in a push-only exchange model the difference is largely
academic, but in a pull (or push-then-pull) model it is operationally and
governance-critical: it determines what data the receiver may legitimately
request, what the sender must hold available for query, and what minimum
necessary / consent boundaries apply. In a follow-up comment, the reporter
identifies the bundle in question as the content described on the BSeR build
page for "Referral Service Request Resources"
(`https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/bser/referral_service_request_resources.html`)
and characterizes the "initial push plus query-for-more" pattern as "much more
fragile." No cross-referenced Jira tickets were found.

## Proposed Dispositions

> Note: BSeR's implementation guide source repository was not surfaced as a
> GitHub cross-reference for this ticket, and no `repo-analysis` briefing has
> been loaded. Concrete file paths therefore cannot be cited from a briefing.
> The dispositions below name the BSeR IG (per the ticket's specification
> field) and the bundle/profile content currently published at the build page
> identified by the reporter, and remain at the conformance/narrative level
> that the IG itself owns.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat the question as a request for an explicit normative statement and add
narrative + conformance language to the BSeR IG that declares the referral
bundle (the content described on
`referral_service_request_resources.html` — i.e., the BSeR Referral
ServiceRequest Bundle and its profiled members, anchored from MessageHeader)
to be the **complete authorized payload** for a referral exchange. Specifically:

- Add a "Scope of Exchanged Data" subsection to the referral resources page
  stating that the profiled bundle constitutes the full set of data the
  sender is permitted to disclose and the receiver is permitted to retain for
  the referral.
- State that BSeR does **not** define or endorse a follow-on query-back from
  receiver to sender for additional referral content, and that any such
  query is out of scope of conformance.
- Add a corresponding conformance bullet in the BSeR Conformance/Capability
  page (and reflect it in the `MessageHeader` / `Bundle` profile narratives)
  so that implementers, security reviewers, and governance bodies have a
  single citable statement.

#### Justification

The reporter's core concern is governance and predictability: a "push plus
query-for-more" model is, in his words, "much more fragile" because the
permitted-data envelope is undefined, which complicates consent, minimum
necessary, audit, and receiver-side retention rules. Public-health referral
exchanges are particularly sensitive to disclosure scope. Declaring the
bundle to be the full authorized envelope is the most defensible default for
a messaging-style IG, aligns with how other HL7 message-based exchanges
(e.g., Da Vinci PDex push, ADT-style messages) treat their payloads, and
gives implementers a single, testable conformance statement. It also matches
the closed-bundle assumption already implicit in the existing BSeR profiles.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Acknowledge both exchange models explicitly in the BSeR IG and document the
boundary between them rather than collapsing them. Concretely:

- Add an "Exchange Models" subsection (e.g., on the BSeR home page or the
  Referral Service Request Resources page) describing two supported patterns:
  (1) **Push-only / closed bundle** — the referral Bundle is the complete
  authorized payload, no follow-on query; and (2) **Push + scoped pull** —
  the Bundle is the initial payload, and a defined set of follow-on queries
  (e.g., `Patient/{id}/$everything` constrained to the referral context, or
  enumerated `_include` queries on the referenced ServiceRequest) is
  permitted.
- For pattern (2), enumerate exactly which resources/searches are allowed,
  what authorization scopes / consent assertions are required, and how the
  receiver MUST stop honoring the additional access once the referral is
  closed.
- Mark pattern (1) as the default for BSeR conformance and pattern (2) as a
  capability that requires explicit declaration in the receiver's
  `CapabilityStatement` and trading-partner agreement.

#### Justification

Some real-world referral workflows genuinely do need follow-on data (e.g., a
specialist later requesting historical labs or imaging reports that weren't in
the original packet). Forbidding this outright (Disposition A) may push
implementers to use out-of-band or non-conformant mechanisms, which is worse
for governance than naming the pattern and constraining it. By making the
"closed bundle" the default and the "scoped pull" an opt-in capability with
strict enumeration, the IG keeps the simple-and-safe path simple while giving
the complex path a defined shape — addressing Lloyd McKenzie's "fragility"
concern by removing the ambiguity rather than removing the capability.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **answered** without a normative IG change. Record the
work group's position in the resolution comment: "The BSeR referral Bundle
defined on `referral_service_request_resources.html` is the complete payload
exchanged for a referral; BSeR does not define a follow-on query-back. No
specification text change is required." Optionally add a short FAQ entry
pointing at this disposition, but make no profile, conformance, or narrative
changes.

#### Justification

The ticket is filed as a **Question**, not a Change Request, and the
reporter's own follow-up reframes it as a request for clarification of intent
rather than a defect. If the work group's standing intent has always been
"the bundle is the whole payload" and no implementer has reported confusion
in the field, then a Jira-recorded answer may be a sufficient and
proportionate response, especially given that BSeR is still in build / not
in a publication window where rewording is cheap. Declining preserves
work-group capacity for tickets with concrete defects or interoperability
gaps and avoids opening a scope debate (push-only vs. pull-supplemented) that
the reporter himself frames as "should be discussed" rather than "must be
changed."

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is a Question, but it is filed at **Highest** priority by the
specification's most prolific reviewer and the underlying ambiguity has
direct downstream consequences for consent, minimum-necessary, audit, and
receiver-side retention — areas where Public Health work-group artifacts are
held to a high bar. Disposition C (close as answered with no IG change)
leaves that ambiguity exactly where the reporter found it, in the heads of
the work group rather than on the IG page an implementer reads. Disposition
B is attractive but materially expands BSeR's scope: defining a conformant
"scoped pull" pattern is non-trivial (enumerated searches, scope tokens,
revocation on referral closure) and arguably belongs in a separate ticket
once a real implementer has the use case. Disposition A — adding an
explicit, narrow "the bundle is the complete authorized payload, no
follow-on query is in scope" statement to the referral resources page and
the conformance section — directly answers the reporter's question, costs
the IG only narrative + a conformance bullet, and matches the
closed-bundle assumption already implicit in the existing profiles. If a
later implementer surfaces a genuine need for follow-on queries, that can
be opened as a separate, properly-scoped change request building on the
clear baseline that Disposition A establishes.
