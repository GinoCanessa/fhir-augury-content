## Summary

In the BSeR (US Bidirectional Services eReferrals) IG, the "Referral-Notify
and Pull" scenario includes a "Notify of Referral" step. An open question
in the IG asks whether the notification payload should carry both the
Task and the originating ServiceRequest, or only the Task. The reporter
(Kyle E. Pelkey) recommends sending **only the Task**, on the grounds
that the originating ServiceRequest may never be acted on by the
recipient specialist — for example, a general dentist may send a Task
to "evaluate and treat infection of tooth #X" together with a
ServiceRequest for a root canal, but the receiving specialist may
determine a different procedure (e.g., root-end surgery) is appropriate
and therefore never fulfill the original ServiceRequest. There are no
cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items in the
Augury index for this ticket.

## Details

**Description:**

> Responding to questions at top of the page.
>
> In the Notify of Referral step in the "Referral-Notify and Pull"
> scenario, the question asked was if both the Task and ServiceRequest
> should be sent, or just the Task.
>
> We feel it would be best to simply send the Task only.
>
> Reason: It is possible that the ServiceRequest may not be answered by
> the specialist referred to. Example in the Dental environment:
> General Dentist sends a patient with a Task to evaluate and treat the
> patient for infection of tooth #X. Dentist also sends ServiceRequest
> for a root canal. However, the recipient dental specialist determines
> that a different procedure (e.g. root end surgery) is more
> appropriate. So the ServiceRequest for a root canal will never be
> responded to as it will not be completed.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Resolve the BSeR IG's "send Task only vs. Task + ServiceRequest"
open question on the **Notify of Referral** step of the
*Referral-Notify and Pull* scenario by stating that the notification
payload SHALL carry **only the Task** (the BSeR Referral Task
profile), and SHALL NOT include the originating ServiceRequest.
Update the scenario narrative, the message-content / Bundle
description for the Notify step, and any conformance language
("Bundle SHALL contain a Task; SHALL NOT contain a ServiceRequest")
so that the question called out at the top of the page is answered
in line. Where the receiving system needs the underlying
ServiceRequest, document the Pull step as the canonical mechanism to
retrieve it via `Task.focus`.

#### Justification

This matches the reporter's stated workflow rationale: the recipient
specialist owns the clinical decision and may not act on the
originating ServiceRequest, so shipping it inside the notification
risks treating an unfulfilled request as authoritative. It also keeps
the Notify step lightweight and aligned with the *Notify-and-Pull*
pattern (the notification advertises that work is available; the pull
fetches detail), which is consistent with how BSeR uses Task to
coordinate cross-organization referrals.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Send the Task as the primary payload of the Notify step and include
the originating ServiceRequest **by reference only** via
`Task.focus`, rather than embedding the ServiceRequest resource in
the Bundle. Optionally, add a non-normative note that senders MAY
include a contained or summary representation of the ServiceRequest
when out-of-band retrieval is not feasible, but receivers SHALL
treat the Task as the source of truth for what action is being
requested. The Pull step remains the conformant way to retrieve the
full ServiceRequest.

#### Justification

This preserves the reporter's core concern — receivers must not
assume the original ServiceRequest will be honored verbatim — while
still giving the recipient enough information at notify-time to
triage (e.g., to decide whether to accept the referral at all)
without an extra round trip. It avoids a hard SHALL-NOT that could
later need to be relaxed for jurisdictions or use cases where the
notify channel must be self-contained. Trade-off vs. A: slightly
more complexity in the Bundle profile and conformance language, but
better alignment with how Task/ServiceRequest are normally linked in
FHIR workflow.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline to constrain the Notify-of-Referral payload to "Task only"
in normative IG text. Instead, leave the question open / mark it as
implementation guidance, on the basis that the choice between Task-
only and Task+ServiceRequest is a deployment decision driven by the
specific service category (e.g., diabetes prevention, hypertension,
dental) rather than something the BSeR IG should fix for all
participants.

#### Justification

The ticket is a single reporter's recommendation grounded in one
dental example, with no Zulip or workgroup discussion captured in
the index and no cross-referenced tickets to indicate broader
consensus. A normative restriction to Task-only could be a breaking
change for existing BSeR implementers who already include the
ServiceRequest, and the underlying need (recipient may diverge from
the original request) is already addressed by Task lifecycle states
and `Task.focus`, so a hard constraint may not be necessary.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B directly answers the open question called out in the
IG (so reviewers and implementers are no longer blocked) while
respecting the reporter's clinical rationale that the originating
ServiceRequest must not be treated as a binding instruction to the
specialist. Linking the ServiceRequest only via `Task.focus` keeps
the notification small, aligns with standard FHIR Task/Workflow
patterns, and leaves room for the Pull step to do what it was
designed to do. Disposition A is also defensible and should be the
fallback if the workgroup prefers a strict SHALL-NOT; Disposition C
is weakest because it leaves the IG question unanswered, which is
what triggered this ticket in the first place.
