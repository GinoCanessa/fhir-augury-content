## Summary

The ticket asks the Public Health work group to consider updating the
US Healthcare Associated Infections (HAI) FHIR Implementation Guide
from FHIR R3 (STU3) to FHIR R4. The request is framed as a high-level
"consider" change rather than a specific technical change to a single
artifact: it concerns the version of the FHIR base specification that
the entire HAI IG builds against. No cross-referenced Jira tickets,
Zulip threads, or GitHub items were surfaced for this issue, so the
ticket stands on its own and the workgroup will need to weigh the
republication effort against the value of moving HAI off STU3.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Schedule a republication of the US Healthcare Associated Infections
(HAI) Implementation Guide against FHIR R4. Concretely, this means:

- Re-baseline the IG's `package.json` / IG-publisher configuration on
  `hl7.fhir.r4.core` (replacing the existing STU3 dependency).
- Walk every conformance artifact in the IG (StructureDefinition
  profiles, ValueSets, CodeSystems, examples, search-parameter
  definitions, capability statements) and update R3-only constructs
  (e.g., `DSTU2`/`STU3`-style references, deprecated datatypes,
  cardinality and binding-strength differences between R3 and R4) to
  their R4 equivalents.
- Reconfirm bindings against R4 terminology (especially any HL7-managed
  ValueSets whose canonical URLs or codes shifted between versions).
- Update narrative, version literals, and conformance pages to declare
  R4 as the published target.
- Run the IG publisher against R4 and resolve any QA errors before
  reballoting the guide as an R4 STU.

#### Justification

The HAI IG is currently anchored to FHIR R3 (STU3), which is no longer
the actively-maintained base release. R4 is the long-stable, normative
base used by every other recently-balloted Public Health IG (eCR, US
Public Health Profiles Library, etc.), so an R4 republication brings
HAI back into the supported toolchain and into alignment with other
public-health implementers. Implementers who have already moved their
production stacks off STU3 can otherwise no longer validate against the
current HAI IG with current tooling, which directly justifies the
upgrade the reporter is asking for.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than republish the existing R3 IG as-is on R4, pair the version
upgrade with a scope refresh:

- Stand up a new R4 (or R4B/R5-aware) HAI IG project, cleanly forked
  from the R3 sources.
- Take the upgrade as an opportunity to re-evaluate the artifact set:
  retire profiles whose use cases have moved to other Public Health
  IGs (e.g., reporting flows now covered by eCR or by NHSN-aligned
  measure IGs), and add any HAI-specific elements that R4 enables
  natively (e.g., richer `Encounter`, `Condition`, and `Observation`
  modeling for infection surveillance).
- Publish the result as a new STU on R4, with the existing R3 IG
  marked as superseded rather than transformed in place.

#### Justification

A mechanical R3→R4 conversion preserves design choices that may no
longer reflect current Public Health practice or the broader
US-Core / Public Health Profiles Library landscape. Treating the
upgrade as a re-scoping exercise avoids carrying forward STU3-era
modeling debt, prevents collisions with newer Public Health IGs that
already cover overlapping ground, and gives the workgroup a clean
versioning story (R3 STU → superseded; R4 STU → current) instead of
a same-name IG that silently changes base version.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request and leave the HAI IG on FHIR R3, on the grounds
that there is no demonstrated implementer demand or active maintenance
sponsor for an R4 republication. The ticket is a 2019 "consider" with
no comments, no cross-referenced discussion, no linked PRs, and no
declared assignee, indicating it has not attracted active engagement
in the work group.

#### Justification

Republishing an IG to a new FHIR version is non-trivial: it requires
sponsor commitment, editor capacity, an IG-publisher refresh,
connectathon validation, and at minimum an STU ballot. Doing so
without identified implementers or a maintenance owner risks producing
an "R4-shaped" artifact that nobody validates against. If HAI
surveillance use cases are in fact being met by other current Public
Health IGs (eCR, NHSN-aligned measure IGs, US Public Health Profiles
Library), the workgroup is better served by formally retiring HAI
than by spending cycles porting it.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Accepting the request as written is the most defensible starting
point. The reporter (David DeRoode) is a recognized Public Health
contributor, and the underlying problem — HAI being stranded on STU3
while the rest of the Public Health IG portfolio sits on R4 — is real
and only worsens with time. Disposition B's re-scoping is attractive
but is a strictly larger commitment than the ticket asks for, and the
workgroup can elect to fold scope changes into the R4 republication
work plan once it is approved. Disposition C is hard to justify
without first confirming with NHSN/CDC stakeholders that HAI is
genuinely deprecated in favor of other IGs; absent that signal, the
default should be to keep the IG alive and current. The workgroup
should accept the change request, identify a maintenance sponsor and
editor, and slate an R4 republication on the next available
Public Health publishing cycle.
