## Summary

FHIR-15173 is a 2018-era comment against the (now deprecated) Electronic
Case Reporting (eCR) FHIR specification. The reporter (Catherine Staes)
flags two undefined terms in §1 "Reporting, investigation and management"
of the narrative: the phrase "population cases" and a downstream
reference to a "universal FHIR population case resource". The ticket
asks the authors to define what these terms mean, since neither is
introduced or anchored to a concrete artifact in the surrounding text.
The ticket is editorial / clarification in nature — it does not propose
a new resource or element, only that the spec text either define the
terms or remove/replace the language. The ticket has been triaged with
resolution "Considered for Future Use" and the underlying specification
is now double-deprecated, which materially constrains the realistic
disposition options. There are no other Jira cross-references and no
GitHub cross-references; the only Zulip touch is an automated
`tracker-item` bot post mirroring the original GForge entry.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat as an editorial clarification against the eCR narrative §1
"Reporting, investigation and management". Either (a) add a definition
of "population case" inline at first use and explicitly tie the
"universal FHIR population case resource" phrase to a concrete artifact
(or remove that phrase if no such resource was ever planned), or (b)
strike the two undefined sentences entirely and replace them with
language that uses already-defined eCR concepts.

#### Justification

The reporter's request is a legitimate readability concern: the spec
introduces two terms with no definition and no link to a defining
artifact, which is exactly the kind of issue HL7 narrative review is
meant to catch. Resolving it improves the historical record. The
mechanical change is small and limited to the eCR narrative — no
resource, profile, or terminology change is required.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Acknowledge the editorial issue but redirect any concrete follow-up to
the active successor IGs in the Public Health space (the current eCR /
eICR FHIR IG line), rather than amending the deprecated specification.
Capture the term "population case" as a question to be answered (or
explicitly dropped) in the successor IG's narrative, and close
FHIR-15173 against the deprecated spec with a pointer to where the
clarification lives now.

#### Justification

The specification this ticket is filed against is marked
"[deprecated] [deprecated]". Spending editorial cycles on a deprecated
document yields little implementer benefit; readers are already being
steered away from it. Carrying the conceptual question forward to the
live IG is where the change actually helps users, and it keeps the
historical artifact frozen.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Persisted disposition: confirm "Considered for Future Use" / no change
to the deprecated eCR specification, with a brief note on the ticket
explaining that the underlying document is no longer maintained and
that "population case" / "universal FHIR population case resource" were
exploratory phrasing that did not survive into the active successor
work.

#### Justification

The specification is double-deprecated, the ticket has been in
"Triaged" / "Considered for Future Use" since 2018, no implementer has
re-raised the concern in the seven years since, and there are no
related Jira tickets, GitHub issues/PRs, or substantive Zulip
discussion. Re-opening editorial work against a deprecated artifact is
hard to justify against the workgroup's other backlog, and the
practical risk of leaving the language as-is is essentially zero
because readers are already redirected to the successor IG.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** C

The specification under review is double-deprecated, the ticket has sat
at "Considered for Future Use" since 2018, and there is no community
signal — no linked tickets, no GitHub activity, no human Zulip
discussion — that the underlying clarification still matters to anyone
implementing today. Editing a deprecated narrative to define
exploratory terms that did not survive into the active eCR / eICR IG
line spends workgroup time without producing implementer value.
Disposition B is a defensible compromise if the workgroup wants to
preserve the substantive question, but in practice the successor IGs
have already moved past this phrasing, so closing the ticket as "no
change, deprecated spec" (Disposition C) is the cleanest resolution and
matches the existing triage outcome.
