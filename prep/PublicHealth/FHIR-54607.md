## Summary

The ticket, submitted on behalf of Jake Richards of the Tennessee Department
of Health Cancer Registry Program, asks that **Vital Status** be added to the
list of "initial data elements" that the US Central Cancer Registry Reporting
Content IG (CCRR) requires to be present at 15-day / 30-day post-encounter
queries when sending the **initial report (T0)** to a Central Cancer Registry.
The submitter argues that Vital Status is a NAACCR-required data element and
is needed for passive cancer surveillance, and proposes appending "Vital
Status" to the existing bullet list (Patient Name, Address State, Primary
Laterality, Primary Cancer Condition, Date of Diagnosis, Diagnostic
Confirmation, Behavior, Primary Site, Histology, Clinical Status, Date of
Birth, Facility Identifier/Name).

The CCRR authoring team's response (captured in comments) clarifies that the
T0 list is intentionally a **minimum trigger set** for early-incidence
reporting, not the full payload — Vital Status, when present in the EHR,
will still flow in the initial report and in all subsequent report types per
the IG profiles, it just is not required to fire T0. Because the encounter
is recently closed, vital status is implicitly "alive" at that point. The
submitter, after seeing the proposed clarifying language, agreed that it
addressed his concern and did not require a meeting. The ticket has been
resolved **Persuasive with Modification**: the IG will reword the Use Case
page to make the "minimum-to-trigger vs. full-content" distinction explicit
rather than adding Vital Status to the trigger list.

There is one cross-reference of interest, a FHIR element mention of
`Condition.code` arising from the description's discussion of how the
Primary Cancer Condition is identified (encounter-diagnosis category +
reportability-listed code, active clinicalStatus, confirmed
verificationStatus). No related Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub
items were surfaced.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add **Vital Status** as an additional bullet to the T0 minimum-data-element
list on the CCRR IG Use Case page, alongside Patient Name, Address State,
Primary Laterality, Primary Cancer Condition, Date of Diagnosis, Diagnostic
Confirmation, Behavior, Primary Site, Histology, Clinical Status, Date of
Birth, and Facility Identifier/Name. In effect, this would make the
presence of an Observation conforming to the IG's Vital Status profile
(or equivalent representation of patient deceased/alive status) a
gating condition for firing the initial (T0) report at the 15-day /
30-day query points. The Use Case page narrative would be updated to
include Vital Status in the bulleted list, and any companion guidance
(e.g., reportability triggering text) would be aligned to match.

#### Justification

Vital Status is a required NAACCR field and a core element for passive
cancer surveillance, so requiring it in T0 maximises the downstream
utility of the initial report for state cancer registries. Adopting
the request literally honours the commenter's stated need and keeps
the T0 minimum aligned with NAACCR completeness expectations rather
than relying on later report types to fill in the gap.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach (clarify scope of the T0 minimum list rather than expand it)

#### Proposal

Leave the T0 minimum-data-element list unchanged with respect to Vital
Status, but reword the surrounding narrative on the CCRR IG Use Case
page to make explicit that the list is a **minimum trigger set** for
**sending** the initial report — not the full payload — and that the
full content of every report type (including T0) is governed by the
IG profiles, which do carry Vital Status when it is present in the
EHR. Concretely, replace the current sentence

> "…will be queried … for the presence of the following content:"

with the resolution-described text

> "…will be queried … for the presence of the following list of
> minimum data elements. This list defines the minimum elements that
> must be complete in the EHR to trigger **sending** the **initial**
> report only. The content of all report types will include all data
> elements, not only those in this list, as specified by the IG
> profiles."

This is the disposition the IG team has already coordinated with the
commenter and is the change captured by the ticket's
"Persuasive with Modification" resolution.

#### Justification

The T0 list was deliberately scoped as a **minimum** by a
multi-stakeholder group (CDC, NCI, NAACCR, ONC/ASTP, registries) to
keep early-incidence reporting fireable from real-world EHR content
at 15 days post-encounter, when many secondary fields — including
Vital Status — are commonly absent. Because T0 is anchored on a
recently closed encounter, the patient is implicitly alive, so
gating T0 on Vital Status would in practice add an EHR-completeness
hurdle without changing what registries learn from the report.
Crucially, when Vital Status **is** present in the EHR, the
profile-driven report content already includes it, so the
underlying need (Vital Status reaching the registry) is met without
making it a trigger. The clarifying wording also dissolves the
misreading that motivated the ticket — the commenter himself
confirmed in the 2026-02-23 comment that the rewrite addresses his
concern. This avoids a real interoperability cost (failed or delayed
T0 reports from EHRs that do not consistently populate Vital Status)
while still landing a substantive IG change.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Reject the request, leaving the T0 minimum-data-element list and the
surrounding Use Case page narrative unchanged. Close the ticket as
"Not Persuasive" with a short note pointing at the existing
profile-driven report content (which already carries Vital Status when
known) and at the multi-stakeholder rationale for keeping the T0
trigger set minimal.

#### Justification

Defensible on the grounds that the underlying need is already met by
the profile-defined report content (Vital Status flows when present)
and that the T0 list was a deliberate, multi-stakeholder design
artefact intended to be minimal. However, this disposition leaves the
documented confusion in place — the commenter and presumably others
read the Use Case bulleted list as the **complete** initial-report
payload, which is exactly the misreading the IG team has already
acknowledged. Declining without a clarifying edit therefore preserves
a real risk of repeat tickets and inconsistent implementer behaviour.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the right answer and is in fact what the resolution
field already records. It addresses the substantive ambiguity that
motivated the ticket — that readers were treating the T0 bullet list
as the full report payload — without taking on the operational cost
of gating T0 on Vital Status, which the multi-stakeholder design
group explicitly rejected for early-incidence reporting. It also has
the rare advantage of being pre-validated with the original
commenter, who confirmed on 2026-02-23 that the proposed wording
captures his intent and that no further meeting is needed.
Disposition A would re-open a settled multi-stakeholder design
decision and risk delaying or suppressing T0 reports from EHRs
without consistent Vital Status capture; Disposition C leaves the
documentation defect that produced this ticket in place. The
workgroup should ratify the Use Case page rewrite quoted above and
close the ticket as Persuasive with Modification.
