## Summary

FHIR-54303 is a Public Health change request against the Electronic Case
Reporting (eCR) FHIR IG asking the work group to review, and if necessary
update, the IG's guidance on what the conformance verb **SHOULD** means.
The ticket is conditional ("if needed") — the reporter is flagging that
the current treatment of SHOULD in eCR may not be sufficiently explicit
or aligned with implementer expectations, and is asking the work group

## Proposed Dispositions

Note: because the augury index surfaced no GitHub cross-references for
this ticket, no `repo-analysis` briefing was loaded. The dispositions
below therefore reference the eCR (FHIR) IG at a logical level (the
"Conformance" / "Guidance" narrative pages of the IG) rather than a
specific authoring path. If the work group accepts a change-bearing
disposition, the implementer should consult the eCR IG repo's
`repo-analysis` briefing before editing.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a short, dedicated subsection to the eCR (FHIR) IG's conformance /
guidance narrative that explicitly defines how the IG uses the
conformance verb **SHOULD**. The subsection should:

1. Reaffirm the RFC 2119 / FHIR conformance definition of SHOULD ("there
   may exist valid reasons in particular circumstances to ignore a
   particular item, but the full implications must be understood and
   carefully weighed before choosing a different course").
2. State explicitly how SHOULD is applied in the eCR IG — i.e., that
   SHOULD-level expectations are strong recommendations that conformant
   senders/receivers are expected to meet by default, but that
   non-conformance at SHOULD level does not by itself make an instance
   non-conformant to the IG.
3. Distinguish SHOULD (narrative-level recommendation) from
   `MustSupport` (profile-level capability obligation) and from SHALL
   (hard conformance), since these are routinely conflated by
   implementers.
4. Provide one or two eCR-specific worked examples of SHOULD-level
   guidance currently in the IG (e.g., recommended elements that are
   not `MustSupport`, recommended terminology bindings at extensible
   strength) so readers can see the rule in context.

#### Justification

The eCR IG is implemented by a wide range of EHR, public health agency
(PHA), and intermediary vendors, many of whom approach the IG with
different conformance traditions. An explicit, IG-local definition of
SHOULD reduces interpretation drift between senders and receivers and
gives conformance testers a clear reference when triaging
non-conformance reports. The change is purely narrative and
non-breaking; it does not alter any profile, value set, or
StructureDefinition, so it is low risk and high value for implementer
clarity. It is also consistent with the practice in other mature HL7
IGs (e.g., US Core) of calling out conformance-verb semantics
explicitly rather than relying on the FHIR core specification by
reference.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than authoring a new subsection from scratch, add a concise
"Conformance verbs in this IG" callout (one paragraph plus a small
table) to the existing IG home / conformance page that:

- Lists SHALL, SHOULD, MAY, and `MustSupport` side-by-side with a
  one-line operational definition for each as used by eCR.
- Links out to the FHIR core conformance page
  (`http://hl7.org/fhir/conformance-rules.html`) and to the IG's
  `MustSupport` definition, rather than restating those definitions in
  full.
- Notes the IG's expectation for testing/validation behavior at each
  level (e.g., validators flag SHALL violations as errors, SHOULD
  violations as warnings).

#### Justification

This alternative addresses the same underlying need — implementer
ambiguity about SHOULD — but does so with a smaller editorial footprint
and avoids duplicating normative text from the FHIR core spec, which is
a known maintenance hazard (drift if the core definition is later
refined). A compact, link-rich table is easier for implementers to scan
during conformance triage than prose, and folding all four verbs into
one place prevents the "SHOULD got an explanation but MAY didn't"
asymmetry that often emerges when only one verb is clarified. It also
sidesteps the risk that an eCR-local restatement of SHOULD inadvertently
narrows or widens the FHIR core meaning.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request as **not persuasive / no change required**, on the
grounds that the meaning of SHOULD is already defined normatively by
the FHIR core specification (which the IG inherits) and by RFC 2119,
and that the eCR IG already conveys conformance expectations primarily
through profiles, `MustSupport` flags, and invariants rather than
through the narrative verb SHOULD. Recommend that the reporter return
with a specific example of guidance in the IG that is ambiguous or
misinterpreted in practice, so a targeted clarification (rather than a
generic one) can be considered.

#### Justification

The ticket itself is hedged ("if needed") and does not cite a concrete
passage of the IG that has caused implementer confusion, a Zulip
thread where the meaning was disputed, or an interoperability incident
attributable to the current treatment of SHOULD. Adding generic
narrative about a conformance verb without a documented gap risks
producing boilerplate that implementers skip over, and may even
introduce subtle inconsistencies with the FHIR core conformance rules.
A request-for-example response keeps the door open without committing
work-group editorial cycles to a speculative change.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the best balance of implementer value and editorial
cost. The reporter's "if needed" framing signals real uncertainty
about SHOULD in the eCR context, and Public Health IGs in particular
are read by a heterogeneous implementer audience (EHR vendors, PHAs,
intermediaries, regional HIEs) who do not all share the same
conformance vocabulary — so doing nothing (Disposition C) is likely to
defer rather than resolve the underlying confusion. At the same time,
authoring a dedicated SHOULD subsection in isolation (Disposition A)
risks an asymmetry where SHOULD is explained but the more frequently
misunderstood relationship between SHOULD and `MustSupport` is not.
Adding a single, compact "Conformance verbs in this IG" callout that
covers SHALL/SHOULD/MAY/`MustSupport` together, links to the FHIR core
conformance rules, and states how validators are expected to react at
each level gives implementers a one-stop reference, avoids restating
normative text, and is non-breaking. If the work group later
identifies specific eCR passages that are misread, those can be tuned
in place without revisiting the verb definitions.
