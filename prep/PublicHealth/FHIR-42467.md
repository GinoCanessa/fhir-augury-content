## Summary

The reporter (Lloyd McKenzie) asserts that the BSeR IG's "Notify and Pull"
exchange option is significantly underspecified and asks the Public Health
work group to flesh it out. Specifically, the IG needs to make clear: (a)
where the `Task` resource lives — on the Referral source vs. the Referral
performer — and how that choice depends on recipient capability; (b) how
intermediaries (e.g., HIE/HIN brokers) participate in the flow; (c) the
concrete query process the recipient uses to retrieve the referral payload,
both at the moment the `Task` is evaluated and any time afterward; (d) how
the IG's "transactions" map to underlying RESTful interactions, including
cases where REST does not cleanly mirror the abstract transaction model;
and (e) the lifecycle / status transitions on the `Task` throughout a
notify-and-pull exchange. The cross-reference query returned no related
Jira tickets, no related Zulip threads, and no related GitHub items, so
this ticket appears to stand alone in the Augury index.

## Details

**Description:**

The Notify and Pull option is underspecified.  There's a whole lot that
needs to be fleshed out here:

- the Task can live on the Referral source or the Referral performer
  (depending on the capabilities of the recipient)
- How intermediaries fit
- What the query process looks like to retrieve information (whether when
  evaluating the Task or later)
- How RESTful actions correlate to the 'transactions' (and the fact that
  RESTful interactions won't always neatly fit into the transaction model)
- the basics of what happens to status in the Task.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a new normative subsection to the BSeR IG's exchange-options chapter
titled "Notify and Pull" (or expand the existing one) that covers, at a
minimum:

1. **Task placement.** Define two valid placements of the BSeR `Task`:
   (i) on the Referral source's server, when the recipient lacks a
   FHIR-conformant inbox; (ii) on the Referral performer's server, when
   the recipient can host. State explicitly which `CapabilityStatement`
   interactions / `BSeR-*-Capabilities` profile elements the recipient
   must declare to drive the choice, and define a deterministic
   selection rule (e.g., performer-hosted preferred when recipient
   declares `Task` `create`/`update`).
2. **Intermediary roles.** Describe how an intermediary (HIE/HIN, broker,
   queue) appears in the flow: as `Task.owner` proxy, as a re-publisher
   of the `Task`, or as a transparent transport. Specify the
   `Task.requester` / `Task.owner` / `Task.for` semantics in each case
   and how `Provenance` is carried end-to-end.
3. **Pull query.** Specify the exact query the recipient performs to
   retrieve the referral payload: starting `Task` reference, the
   `Task.input`/`Task.output` slices that point at the bundle/document,
   and the FHIR REST interactions used (`GET Bundle/{id}`,
   `GET Task/{id}/$everything`, or a named operation). Cover both
   "pull at evaluation time" and "pull later" scenarios and what the
   server must keep available.
4. **Transaction-to-REST mapping.** For each abstract BSeR transaction in
   this option, add a normative mapping table to the underlying RESTful
   interactions, and explicitly call out cases where the mapping is
   not 1:1 (e.g., when polling, conditional reads, or paged
   `_history` are required).
5. **Task status lifecycle.** Add a state diagram and accompanying text
   for `Task.status` across the notify-and-pull exchange — at minimum
   covering `requested` → `received` → `accepted`/`rejected` →
   `in-progress` → `completed`/`failed`/`cancelled` — and state which
   actor is responsible for each transition.

#### Justification

The reporter's five bullets are concrete gaps, not stylistic preferences:
implementers cannot build interoperable BSeR Notify-and-Pull exchanges
today without making private decisions on every one of them, which is
exactly the kind of variation IGs exist to remove. The proposed sections
align with how comparable FHIR IGs (Da Vinci PDex, CDex, US Core
subscriptions) handle equivalent ambiguities, and they reuse mechanisms
already present in core FHIR (`Task`, `CapabilityStatement`,
`Provenance`) without inventing new machinery.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than expanding the BSeR IG inline with a full notify-and-pull
specification, factor the exchange option out into a thin **companion
guide** (or an appendix profile pack) that:

- Reuses the **Subscriptions R5 backport** for the "notify" half
  (recipient subscribes; source publishes a `SubscriptionStatus`
  notification carrying the `Task` reference), avoiding bespoke
  notification framing.
- Reuses **Da Vinci CDex / HRex** patterns for the "pull" half (named
  operation or `Task`-driven fetch with documented `Bundle` shapes).
- Defines BSeR-specific bindings (Task profiles, value sets, identifier
  systems) but defers the transport pattern to those existing IGs by
  reference.
- Leaves only a short conformance section in the main BSeR IG that says
  "if you implement notify-and-pull, you SHALL conform to companion
  guide vX.Y."

#### Justification

The reporter's gaps are real, but cataloguing them inside BSeR risks the
work group re-solving problems other HL7 IGs have already solved (and
keep iterating on). Aligning with Subscriptions-backport + CDex/HRex
keeps BSeR implementers on the same rails as other US-Realm exchange
work, reduces long-term maintenance for the Public Health WG, and avoids
locking BSeR to a transport pattern that may diverge from the rest of
the ecosystem. The trade-off vs. Disposition A is dependency
management: BSeR implementers now have to track two specifications
instead of one, and the WG must coordinate releases.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket "Persuasive with Mod / Not Persuasive" with a rationale
that the Notify-and-Pull option, as currently described, is intentionally
left as a permitted-but-not-fully-profiled exchange pattern, and direct
implementers needing more guidance to use the fully-specified
Push/Notify-with-Payload options instead. Optionally remove or
de-emphasize the Notify-and-Pull option in the next BSeR ballot rather
than expanding it.

#### Justification

If the Public Health WG has limited capacity and there is no documented
implementer demand actively blocked on Notify-and-Pull, fully specifying
it is a substantial body of work (Task placement rules, intermediary
semantics, query mechanics, transaction mapping, status lifecycle) for
an option that may see little real-world uptake. Declining-and-pruning
is defensible because the IG already supports interoperable referral
exchange via the better-specified options, and underspecified options
that survive in a published IG cause more interoperability harm than
removing them.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter's five bullets are not a wishlist — they are the minimum
set of decisions any implementer must make to ship Notify-and-Pull, and
shipping an IG with that gap effectively guarantees non-interoperable
deployments under the IG's own banner. Disposition B is attractive in
principle but requires firm cross-WG dependencies (Subscriptions
backport, CDex/HRex versions) that the Public Health WG cannot unilaterally
guarantee on a BSeR ballot timeline; it is a strong candidate for a
follow-up ticket once Disposition A's normative content is stable.
Disposition C is only defensible if the WG is genuinely willing to
remove the option, and the existence of this ticket — filed by a senior
modeler — suggests the option is being relied on in the field. Accept
the ticket, scope the work into the five bullets above, and assign an
owner for the next BSeR connectathon cycle.
