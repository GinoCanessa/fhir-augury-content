## Summary

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a non-normative *"Relationship to other specifications"* (or
*"Future Considerations"*) section to the **US Helios Bulk Data for
Immunizations (FHIR) IG** that:

1. Names the **FHIR Access Control IG**
   (<https://awatson1978.github.io/fhir-access-control-ig/>) as a
   related, currently-informational specification of interest.
2. States that future versions of the Helios Bulk Data IG **may**
   harmonize with that IG to express **FHIR Consent–based access
   control** over bulk immunization data exports.
3. Flags the dependency on FAST registration of the FHIR Access Control
   IG so readers understand the maturity gap.

No normative conformance language, no new profiles, and no changes to
the bulk-export operation are introduced.

#### Justification

The reporter explicitly framed the suggestion as *"may be worth
harmonizing… in the future"* and labeled the underlying IG as merely
informational. A forward-pointing, non-normative reference is the
lightest action that honours the request, gives implementers awareness
of the related work, and avoids committing the IG to a dependency on a
specification that is not yet FAST-registered. It also creates a
discoverable hook for the next ballot cycle once the FHIR Access
Control IG matures.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Treat consent as **out of scope for the Bulk Data white paper itself**,
but open a **companion tracking issue** (or a brief Public Health WG
discussion item) that:

- Asks the FHIR Access Control IG authors to file a concrete
  harmonization proposal once their IG is FAST-registered, scoped to
  bulk immunization data sharing.
- Documents the question raised by craig.newman ("how would the two
  mesh?") so a future review has a clear starting point.
- Optionally records the relationship in the **Helios FHIR Accelerator
  Confluence space** (or equivalent) rather than the IG itself, keeping
  the IG free of speculative cross-references.

The Bulk Data IG remains unchanged in this ballot cycle.

#### Justification

The Helios Bulk Data IG is positioned as a white paper and, per
craig.newman, deliberately does not address consent. Embedding even a
non-normative reference inside it risks implementer confusion about
whether consent enforcement is expected today. Deferring the linkage
to a tracking artifact keeps the IG focused, gives the FHIR Access
Control IG time to reach FAST registration, and respects the
maturity asymmetry between the two specifications. It also gives the
reporter a concrete next step (file a follow-up once the upstream IG
is ready) without forcing premature design choices.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the suggestion for this ballot cycle, with the rationale
**"Insufficient actionable content / dependency on an external
informational IG that is not yet FAST-registered."** Resolve the
ticket as *Persuasive with Mod – Not Persuasive* (or equivalent), and
in the resolution comment:

- Acknowledge the FHIR Access Control IG link.
- Note that the Helios Bulk Data white paper does not currently model
  consent (per craig.newman's comment), so there is no concrete
  harmonization surface to act on.
- Invite the reporter to refile a specific change request once the
  FHIR Access Control IG is FAST-registered and identifies concrete
  harmonization points.

#### Justification

The ticket is filed as a **Comment** rather than a change request, the
referenced IG is **informational and pre-FAST**, the description
contains no concrete change to the spec, and the lone comment thread
is unresolved and points out a scope mismatch ("we don't talk much
about consent in that document"). With no cross-references in Jira,
Zulip, or GitHub, and no consent-related sections in the current Bulk
Data IG to attach a change to, declining is defensible: there is
literally nothing concrete to alter. Declining now does not preclude
future work — it just removes a stale, unactionable item from the WG
queue.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **B**

The reporter's intent is clearly forward-looking ("may be worth… in
the future") and the upstream FHIR Access Control IG is not yet
FAST-registered, so embedding even a non-normative pointer inside the
Bulk Data IG (Disposition A) is premature and risks implementer
confusion — especially given craig.newman's observation that the
white paper does not currently address consent. Outright declining
(Disposition C) is also reasonable but loses the institutional memory
that the harmonization opportunity exists. Disposition B threads the
needle: it keeps the IG unchanged this cycle, formally records the
question raised in the comment thread, and creates a clean re-entry
point for the FHIR Access Control IG authors to propose a concrete
harmonization once their IG matures. It also gives the WG a polite,
defensible reply to the reporter that is neither a hard "no" nor an
unfunded promise.
