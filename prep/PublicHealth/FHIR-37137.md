## Summary

The ticket asks the Maternal and Infant Health Research (MIHR) IG authors to
re-anchor the canonical URL of the `pregnancy` ValueSet (currently
`http://www.hl7.org/fhir/us/mihr/ValueSet/pregnancy`) under
`terminology.hl7.org` (THO) instead of `hl7.org/fhir`, in line with the
TSMG-stated policy that only ValueSets that are the target of a required
binding to a `code` data type should remain anchored at `hl7.org/fhir`. Per
the ticket, anchoring in THO would be accompanied by a UTG ticket to
register the ValueSet in THO. The ticket was originally dispositioned
"Considered for Future Use" (2022-06-02, vote 21-0-2) pending IG maturity
and finalization of the draft THO process; that resolution was reverted on
2023-03-29 to be re-opened post-STU publication, and the ticket is now
Triaged on the Public Health Block-Vote-1 ballot list. No cross-referenced
Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were surfaced by the
augury index for this ticket.

## Details

**Description:**

The Value Set canonical url should be anchored in terminology.hl7.org

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Re-anchor the canonical URL of the MIHR `pregnancy` ValueSet from
`http://www.hl7.org/fhir/us/mihr/ValueSet/pregnancy` to a THO-hosted
canonical (e.g.
`http://terminology.hl7.org/ValueSet/pregnancy-status` or a
similarly-scoped THO identifier agreed with TSMG/UTG). Concretely:

1. File a UTG (Unified Terminology Governance) ticket against the
   THO source to register the ValueSet (and any non-FHIR-core CodeSystem
   it depends on) under the `terminology.hl7.org` canonical space.
2. Once the THO canonical is assigned, update the MIHR IG
   `ValueSet-pregnancy.json` (or equivalent FSH/JSON authoring file)
   so its `url` is the new THO canonical and remove the IG-local
   ValueSet definition (or leave a stub that simply references THO,
   per current TSMG guidance).
3. Update every binding in MIHR profiles/extensions that references
   the old `hl7.org/fhir/us/mihr/ValueSet/pregnancy` canonical to
   point to the new THO canonical.
4. Add a migration/IG-history note documenting the canonical change
   for downstream implementers who may have pinned the prior URL.

This applies the rule that only ValueSets bound `required` to a `code`
data type stay anchored at `hl7.org/fhir`; the `pregnancy` ValueSet
does not meet that criterion and therefore belongs in THO.

#### Justification

This is exactly the corrective action TSMG asked for, and Lynn Laakso
explicitly re-opened the ticket post-STU so the change could be made
once the THO process matured. Centralizing reusable ValueSets in THO
avoids duplication across IGs (the `pregnancy` concept is reused well
beyond MIHR), keeps governance with the terminology stewards rather
than the IG, and aligns MIHR with the broader HL7 pattern that has
been applied to many cross-IG concepts since 2022. Doing it now,
rather than at first publication, has lower implementer cost because
the IG is still pre-Normative.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the ValueSet definition physically in the MIHR IG for this STU
cycle, but assign it the THO canonical URL via a coordinated
"pre-registration" with TSMG/UTG: the IG retains the authoring source
of the ValueSet, while the canonical (`url` element) is the
THO-namespaced one and the THO publication tooling pulls the
definition from the IG via reference. Specifically:

1. Coordinate with TSMG to reserve `http://terminology.hl7.org/...`
   for the `pregnancy` ValueSet without requiring the full UTG
   migration of the source artifact in this cycle.
2. In the MIHR IG, change only the `ValueSet.url` to the THO
   canonical and add a `ValueSet.identifier` retaining the original
   `hl7.org/fhir/us/mihr/...` URI for backward traceability.
3. Schedule the full UTG-managed migration of the source artifact for
   the next THO release window, once UTG capacity allows.

#### Justification

This alternative honors the spirit of the request (canonical anchored
in THO, satisfying TSMG policy and downstream binders) without
forcing the MIHR team to wait on a UTG submission queue or the THO
publication cycle to ship its STU update. It is materially less
disruptive because authoring stays in the IG repo where MIHR editors
already have permissions and CI, and it leaves a single, clean
breaking change for implementers (the canonical URL) rather than
splitting it across two cycles. The trade-off is governance
ambiguity: TSMG generally prefers source-of-truth and canonical to
live together, so this is acceptable only as a transitional pattern.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Persist not to re-anchor the canonical for this STU cycle and instead
re-resolve the ticket as **Considered for Future Use**, with a
commitment to revisit at the next substantive MIHR ballot. Document
the decision and link it to the original 2022-06-02 disposition so
the rationale chain is preserved.

#### Justification

The original 2022-06-02 vote (21-0-2) explicitly tied the change to
"the maturity of the IG and when the draft THO process has been
finalized." If the MIHR IG is still pre-Normative and there is no
active UTG submission queued, performing a canonical change *now*
forces a breaking URL change on STU implementers without yet
delivering the long-term THO governance benefit. Declining is
defensible if (a) UTG/TSMG cannot guarantee a THO canonical inside
this cycle, or (b) the ValueSet is not actually reused outside MIHR
and so the cross-IG centralization argument is weak. Note: declining
should *not* be chosen merely on cost grounds — TSMG policy stands
either way.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket has already been deferred once on the explicit grounds
that the THO process was immature; that condition no longer holds in
2024–2025, and the same Public Health WG owner (Lynn Laakso)
re-opened the ticket specifically to action it post-STU. TSMG's rule
that non-`code`-bound ValueSets belong in THO is now the established
norm across HL7 IGs, and the MIHR `pregnancy` ValueSet is a
high-reuse concept that other public-health IGs are likely to want to
bind against — exactly the case THO exists to serve. The breaking
canonical-URL change is a real cost to early implementers, but it is
strictly cheaper to absorb now than after Normative publication, and
Disposition B's split-governance arrangement creates more long-term
ambiguity than it saves in short-term effort. The workgroup should
adopt Disposition A and pair this ticket with a concrete UTG
submission for the THO registration before closing it.
