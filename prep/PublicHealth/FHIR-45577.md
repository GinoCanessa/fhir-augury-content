## Summary

FHIR-45577 ("FDA Use cases") is a high-priority, very thinly-described change
request against the US Vital Records Death Reporting (VRDR) IG. The reporter
(Mitra Rocca, FDA) asks the VRDR work group to "include FDA use cases in
future releases", without enumerating concrete profile, element, or binding
changes. The ticket was originally dispositioned in May 2024 as
"Considered for Future Use" by a 23-0-0 vote (Saul Kravitz / Diana Wright)
with the note that VRDR looked forward to engaging with FDA but had no
specific requirements to drive STU3 development. In October 2024 Lynn Laakso
reverted that resolution and re-opened the ticket post-STU3 publication, so
it is once again Triaged and on the Block-Vote-1 list.

The companion VRDR ticket [FHIR-45527](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-45527)
("Include Use Cases for FDA RWD - Death Data") is the more concrete sibling
of this request — it asks specifically that VRDR death data be usable by FDA
as real-world data to support active surveillance and registry use cases.
The MDI work group received the parallel pair
[FHIR-45578](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-45578) and
[FHIR-45524](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-45524), both of which were
closed as Duplicate of the VRDR tickets with the note that MDI will harmonize
with future VRDR work where appropriate. More recently,
[FHIR-52246](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-52246) ("Public Health Use
Cases") opened the same conversation against the US Public Health Profiles
Library and was likewise deferred ("We will consider the FDA public health
use case in the future"). Taken together, the cluster represents a standing
FDA ask for VRDR/MDI/USPHPL to formally accommodate FDA real-world-data,
active-surveillance, and registry use cases, with each IG so far deferring
for lack of concrete requirements.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat FHIR-45577 (VRDR) and its sibling [FHIR-45527](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-45527)
as a paired in-scope work item for the next VRDR release. Concretely:

- Add a new "FDA real-world data, active surveillance, and registry"
  section to the VRDR IG's Use Cases narrative page, naming FDA as a
  consumer alongside the existing CDC/NCHS use cases.
- Add an FDA representative (Mitra Rocca and/or Marti Velezis) to the
  VRDR project as a named SME and stand up a short-lived FDA-VRDR
  requirements track to enumerate concrete data needs (e.g.,
  cause-of-death coding granularity, decedent demographics required for
  RWD signal detection, registry-linkage identifiers).
- Coordinate with MDI so that the duplicates [FHIR-45578](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-45578)
  and [FHIR-45524](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-45524) get matching
  narrative on the MDI side, and with USPHPL so that the parallel
  [FHIR-52246](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-52246) is dispositioned
  consistently.

#### Justification

The ticket has been re-opened post-STU3 by the VRDR co-chair specifically
to keep the FDA conversation alive, has the highest priority, and is on
the Block-Vote-1 list. The original 23-0-0 deferral was explicitly
predicated on "no specific requirements" — accepting the ticket is the
mechanism by which those requirements get gathered. FDA RWD and active
surveillance are also a clearly-articulated public-health priority
(USCDI+) and align with the cross-IG ask now appearing in USPHPL
(FHIR-52246).

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Convert this ticket into a tracked **engagement / requirements-gathering
commitment** rather than a spec change. Specifically:

- Re-resolve FHIR-45577 and [FHIR-45527](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-45527)
  as **"Persuasive with Modifier — Engagement Action"** (rather than
  Considered for Future Use), with the modifier being a written commitment
  from the VRDR work group to:
  1. Schedule a joint FDA-VRDR working session within the current cycle.
  2. Stand up a shared FDA-VRDR requirements document (Confluence page or
     equivalent), referenced from the VRDR project page.
  3. Open follow-on, narrowly-scoped child tickets for each concrete
     requirement that emerges (e.g., "Add code X to Activity at Death
     value set" — the MDI duplicate FHIR-45578 already hints at this
     pattern).
- Coordinate the same modifier on the MDI duplicates and on USPHPL
  [FHIR-52246](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-52246) so that all
  three IGs carry consistent language.

#### Justification

Disposition A risks committing the VRDR IG to spec changes whose shape
nobody can yet describe — the ticket body is a single sentence and the
co-chairs have already noted the absence of concrete requirements.
Disposition B respects that reality but still moves the ball forward: it
replaces an indefinite "Considered for Future Use" with a concrete
engagement deliverable, which is what the re-open by Lynn Laakso actually
seems to be asking for. It also gives the work group a clean way to
generate properly-scoped follow-on tickets without polluting the current
ballot with under-specified change requests.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Re-resolve as **"Not Persuasive"** (or restore the prior "Considered for
Future Use" disposition) on the basis that no actionable change is
described and that the ticket duplicates work already captured by
[FHIR-45527](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-45527) (which itself names
the concrete RWD/surveillance/registry use case). Direct the reporter
to file specific, atomic change requests against VRDR (e.g., a value-set
addition to Activity at Death, an extension for registry linkage) once
FDA's needs are concrete, and close FHIR-45577 as a duplicate-ish
catch-all that adds no information beyond FHIR-45527.

#### Justification

The literal text of the ticket — "Include FDA use cases in future
releases" — is not a change request that can be executed. It does not
name a profile, element, value set, binding, or extension. The companion
ticket FHIR-45527 already covers the concrete RWD/surveillance/registry
ask, and the original VRDR vote (23-0-0) made the work group's position
clear: willing to engage, no requirements to act on. Carrying an
under-specified ticket on Block-Vote-1 indefinitely risks the appearance
of a commitment the work group has not actually made, and FDA's real
needs are better captured as discrete tickets once articulated.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition: B (Alternative Approach — engagement
commitment).**

Disposition C is technically defensible but politically tone-deaf: the
ticket was deliberately re-opened by the VRDR co-chair after STU3
publication to keep the FDA conversation visible, and closing it again
without a deliverable will simply produce a third re-open. Disposition A
goes too far in the other direction — committing the IG to spec changes
no one can describe is exactly what produced the original 23-0-0 deferral.
Disposition B threads the needle: it converts the ticket into a concrete,
trackable engagement action (joint session, shared requirements doc,
follow-on atomic tickets), which is the actual missing piece. It also
scales naturally to the MDI duplicates (FHIR-45578, FHIR-45524) and to
the USPHPL parallel (FHIR-52246), letting the Public Health work group
respond once across all three IGs instead of triaging the same paragraph
four times. The work group should pair the disposition with a named
owner and a target date for the first FDA-VRDR working session, so the
"engagement" modifier is verifiable rather than aspirational.
