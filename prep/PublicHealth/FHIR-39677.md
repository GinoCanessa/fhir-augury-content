## Summary

The submitter requests that the US MedMorph Research Data Exchange Content
IG define a proactive notification mechanism for changes to Knowledge
Artifacts (e.g., the planDefinition / library bundles that drive MedMorph
reporting). The argument is that Healthcare Organizations (HCOs) cannot,
in practice, react to Knowledge Artifact changes on the fly: changes to a
plan definition typically require coordinated development work in either
the EHR or the Healthcare Data Exchange App (HDEA, i.e., the MedMorph
Backend Service App / trigger-and-action engine) before the new artifact
can be safely executed in production. The ticket asks the IG to specify
that artifact authors / distributing authorities push out advance change
notifications with sufficient lead time to fit normal HCO and vendor
release cycles, instead of expecting downstream systems to consume new
or revised artifacts dynamically.

No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were
found for this ticket via FhirAugury, so the summary is grounded only in
the ticket text itself and the MedMorph IG's published architecture for
Knowledge Artifact distribution.

## Details

**Description:**

> When changes occur in the Knowledge Artifacts, at least for the
> foreseeable future new development may be required on the part of the
> Healthcare Organization to support them via the EHR or the HDEA.
> Therefore, a proactive mechanism, with appropriate lead time to
> accommodate development cycles, should be implemented to reduce
> friction and errors. It is unlikely that HDEAs will be capable of
> reacting to changes in the Knowledge Artifacts dynamically.

## Proposed Dispositions

The MedMorph Research Data Exchange Content IG distributes Knowledge
Artifacts (PlanDefinitions, Libraries, ValueSets, etc.) from a Knowledge
Artifact Repository (KAR) to Backend Service Apps (HDEAs) deployed at
HCOs. The dispositions below all assume any change lands in the
MedMorph RDE Content IG repository owned by Public Health (the IG
itself is not present in FhirAugury's GitHub-cross-reference set, so
exact paths cannot be sourced from a `repo-analysis` briefing — the
proposals therefore name the IG's published guidance pages by name
rather than by file path).

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add normative guidance in the MedMorph RDE Content IG's "Knowledge
Artifact Distribution" / "Knowledge Artifact Lifecycle" section
requiring KAR operators to publish change notifications ahead of
making a new or revised PlanDefinition (and its dependent Libraries,
ValueSets, and CodeSystems) "live." Concretely:

- Define a minimum advance-notice window (e.g., 90 days for non-urgent
  changes, with a documented fast-path for security/safety fixes).
- Require the KAR to expose a Subscription-able notification feed for
  artifact lifecycle events (e.g., `R5 Subscription` topic such as
  `http://hl7.org/SmartApp/medmorph/topic/knowledge-artifact-change`,
  carrying the artifact's `canonical` URL, prior and new `version`,
  `status` transition, and effective date).
- Require artifact authors to populate `PlanDefinition.version`,
  `.date`, `.status` (`draft` → `active` → `retired`), and
  `.relatedArtifact` (with `type = predecessor`) so HDEAs and HCOs can
  diff versions deterministically.
- Mandate that the KAR keep prior `active` versions retrievable for the
  full notice window, so HCOs may continue to execute the prior
  artifact while integrating the new one.

#### Justification

This addresses the submitter's request directly: HCOs and HDEAs gain a
predictable, machine-readable change feed they can wire into their
release planning, and the IG retains a clear contract between the KAR
and the HDEA. It reuses existing FHIR mechanisms (Subscription,
canonical URL + version, `RelatedArtifact.predecessor`) rather than
inventing new infrastructure, and it is consistent with how other
HL7 IGs (e.g., CDS Hooks, Da Vinci CRD/DTR) treat artifact versioning
as an explicit lifecycle.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than baking lead-time and notification requirements into the
normative IG, add a non-normative "Knowledge Artifact Change Management"
implementation guidance page that:

- Recommends (rather than requires) a Subscription/notification feed,
  and points to the R5 Subscription Backport IG for R4 KAR
  implementations.
- Profiles `PlanDefinition` (and `Library`) only enough to require the
  lifecycle metadata needed to support out-of-band change management:
  `version` (mandatory, semver-style), `status`, `date`,
  `effectivePeriod`, and `relatedArtifact[predecessor]` populated
  whenever a successor is published.
- Defers the actual lead-time policy (90 days vs. 30 days vs. emergency
  fast-path) to the KAR operator's published governance document,
  referenced from the IG by URL rather than encoded as a SHALL.

#### Justification

The IG cannot realistically dictate operational SLAs on third-party KAR
operators (CDC, state PH agencies, research consortia), and a hard
"SHALL give 90 days notice" is likely to be ignored or formally
non-conformant in practice. Constraining the artifact metadata that
makes change management *possible*, while leaving the *policy* to the
KAR operator, gives HCOs the deterministic versioning they need
without overreaching. It also keeps the IG scope within "data exchange
content" rather than expanding into governance.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as **Not Persuasive** on the grounds that the requested mechanism
is operational/governance, not specification content: nothing prevents a
KAR operator today from publishing a Subscription topic for artifact
changes or from maintaining a release calendar, and the existing FHIR
artifact lifecycle (`status`, `version`, `date`,
`RelatedArtifact.predecessor`) already supports the technical side of
change tracking. Direct the submitter to the KAR operator's governance
process and to the (forthcoming) MedMorph reference implementation
release notes.

#### Justification

A defensible decline rests on (a) scope — the IG describes content and
exchange patterns, not operator SLAs — and (b) duplication — the FHIR
artifact lifecycle already gives implementers the metadata needed to
diff and adopt new artifacts. Imposing notification requirements via
the IG risks creating SHALLs that real-world KARs can't honor, leading
to broad non-conformance and weakening the IG's credibility.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The submitter's underlying concern is real and well-founded — HDEAs
genuinely cannot pick up arbitrary new PlanDefinition logic without HCO
and EHR-side work — but the right response is to make the *technical
substrate* for change management normative (artifact versioning
metadata + a recommended Subscription topic shape), not to legislate
operational lead times that the IG cannot enforce on a KAR operator.
Disposition B keeps the IG within its scope, gives implementers a
deterministic versioning contract they can build automation against,
and leaves room for individual KAR operators (CDC, state PH agencies)
to publish their own governance/lead-time policies that the IG
references by URL. Disposition A is the right *direction* but is
likely to be down-graded to SHOULDs in ballot anyway; Disposition C
underweights the legitimate implementer pain the ticket describes.
