## Summary

The submitter (Bas van den Heuvel) is asking how the BSeR (US Bidirectional
Services eReferrals) IG supports information exchange between the moment a
referral is *accepted* and the moment it is *completed*. As written, the IG
focuses on initiating the referral and communicating final results, but it
does not describe a mechanism for the Referral Initiator to observe the
intermediate progress of work performed by the Referral Recipient. The
submitter proposes that the Referral Recipient generate `Task` resources
representing the discrete steps that are completed, in progress, or planned,
so the Initiator can retrieve those Tasks (and any related resources) to
follow progress over time. There were no cross-referenced Jira tickets,
Zulip threads, or GitHub items returned for this ticket via FHIR Augury, so
the request stands on its own and has not yet been deliberated through other
channels visible to this review.

## Details

**Description:**

> What options are available for information exchange between the referral
> being accepted and completed?
>
> The current specification only communicates the results, not the
> progress/status.
>
> I suggest to add this by allowing the Referral Recipient to generate Tasks
> that indicate the steps that were completed, are in progress and are
> planned to be taken. Retrieval of those Tasks and related resources will
> allow Referral Initiators to see progress.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the BSeR IG to define an explicit progress-reporting pattern in
which the Referral Recipient SHALL (or SHOULD) generate one or more `Task`
resources tied to the original referral, where:

- The original referral is represented by a `ServiceRequest` (or the
  existing BSeR referral `Task`, if BSeR already wraps the referral as a
  Task).
- Each progress `Task` references the originating referral via
  `Task.basedOn` (or `Task.partOf` if the referral itself is a Task), with
  `Task.focus` pointing at the relevant clinical resource where applicable.
- `Task.status` is constrained to a defined progression (e.g.,
  `requested → accepted → in-progress → completed | cancelled | failed`),
  with `Task.businessStatus` carrying BSeR-specific milestones (e.g.,
  "Intake scheduled", "Assessment complete", "Service delivered").
- `Task.lastModified` and `Task.executionPeriod` are required so the
  Initiator can interpret when each step changed.
- The Initiator retrieves progress by querying `Task?based-on=<referral>`
  (or `Task?part-of=<referral>`) against the Recipient's endpoint, and may
  optionally use FHIR Subscriptions (R4 `Subscription` or R5 `topic-based`
  Subscription on `Task`) for push notification.

The IG would add: a new "Progress Reporting" page to the narrative; a
profile such as `BSeRReferralProgressTask`; example instances; a
CapabilityStatement update for the Recipient to advertise `Task` `read`
and `search-type` with the relevant search parameters; and (optionally)
a SubscriptionTopic for `Task` updates scoped to BSeR referrals.

#### Justification

This is the path the submitter explicitly asked for, and it is consistent
with how FHIR more broadly models multi-step workflow: `Task` is the
designated resource for tracking the lifecycle of work, including
sub-tasks via `partOf` and links to triggering requests via `basedOn`.
Other workflow-centric IGs (e.g., Da Vinci PDex/PAS, the Workflow module's
own examples) already use `Task` plus `Task.businessStatus` for exactly
this kind of staged progress reporting, so adopters and tooling will
recognize the pattern. Doing it inside BSeR (rather than asking
implementers to invent their own pattern per deployment) materially
improves interoperability between Initiator EHRs and Recipient public
health / community service systems, which is the IG's whole reason for
existence.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than adding a new BSeR-specific progress profile, treat the
underlying need as "the Initiator wants to observe state changes on the
referral itself" and address it by:

1. Tightening the existing referral `Task` (the BSeR referral request
   `Task`, not a new sibling) so that `Task.status` and
   `Task.businessStatus` are normatively used by the Recipient throughout
   the engagement, with a value-set of BSeR business statuses
   (e.g., "received", "scheduled", "in service", "service complete",
   "ineligible", "lost to follow-up").
2. Recommending FHIR R4/R5 `Subscription` (topic-based where supported)
   on the referral `Task` so the Initiator is notified of state changes
   instead of polling.
3. Documenting that Recipients MAY attach detail resources
   (`Encounter`, `Procedure`, `CarePlan`, etc.) with `.basedOn` pointing
   at the referral, and that Initiators MAY follow those references for
   richer detail without BSeR mandating sub-Tasks.

No new "ProgressTask" profile is introduced.

#### Justification

This minimizes the surface area added to BSeR and avoids the modeling
debate of "is each step a Task?" — which can become non-trivial when
Recipients have very different internal workflows (clinic vs.
community-based organization vs. social services) and would otherwise be
forced to fabricate uniform sub-Tasks. Reusing the *existing* referral
Task plus `businessStatus` and Subscriptions is also less disruptive for
implementers who have already built BSeR endpoints, and it keeps the IG
out of the business of standardizing the sub-steps of every recipient's
operations. It does, however, give the Initiator strictly less structured
visibility than Disposition A, since milestones are conveyed as coded
strings rather than first-class resources.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline as out of scope for the current BSeR cycle: BSeR's chartered
purpose is to standardize the *initiation* and *result reporting* of
bidirectional referrals; intra-engagement progress reporting is workflow
content that is already covered by the FHIR Workflow module
(`Task` with `partOf`/`basedOn`, `businessStatus`, Subscriptions). Note
in the resolution that implementers may apply the Workflow module's
existing patterns today, and reopen the topic in a future BSeR ballot
cycle once concrete implementer demand and a recipient-side use-case
analysis exist.

#### Justification

Defensible if (a) the requester is the only party currently asking for
this, (b) the BSeR work group has not yet collected real-world
recipient-side workflow examples to base a profile on, and (c) the work
group is concerned about scope creep that could delay the next BSeR
ballot. The mechanism the submitter wants (`Task` graphs scoped to a
referral) is already legal FHIR; the cost of declining is "no
standardized BSeR way to do it", which is unfortunate but not blocking.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B addresses the submitter's actual pain point — the Initiator
cannot tell what is happening between accept and complete — without
forcing BSeR to standardize the sub-steps of every recipient's internal
workflow. Reusing the existing referral `Task` with a constrained
`businessStatus` value set, plus a recommendation (not requirement) for
Subscriptions, is the smallest viable change that delivers visibility,
keeps the IG implementable for the heterogeneous mix of BSeR Recipients
(clinical, public health, community-based), and aligns with the broader
FHIR Workflow module patterns. Disposition A is the more "complete"
answer and should remain on the table if the work group finds during
discussion that implementers do want first-class sub-Tasks; Disposition
C should only win if the work group concludes there is not yet enough
implementer signal to act in this cycle, in which case the resolution
should still point implementers at the Workflow module patterns so the
ticket leaves them with a usable answer.
