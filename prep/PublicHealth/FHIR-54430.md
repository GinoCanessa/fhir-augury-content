## Summary

A NAACCR community reviewer of the **US Central Cancer Registry Reporting
Content IG** (CCRR IG) flagged that the "Date Report Generated" concept on
the Central Cancer Registry Reporting Bundle is currently neither required
nor Must Support, and asked the work group to clarify what that date
actually represents. Specifically, the commenter is unclear whether the
report bundle is assembled when an encounter occurs or when the provider
*closes* the encounter, and notes that knowing when a report was generated
is useful for downstream consumers that auto-process many encounters per
patient/cancer and need to compare reports over time.

This ticket sits inside a small cluster of CCRR IG ballot comments from
the same reporter on the same theme:

- [FHIR-54392](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54392) (Use case data
  element "Date Report Generated") is the parent of this ticket — it asks
  why "Date Report Generated" is not required/MS, points out that NAACCR
  data item 2110 is required by NPCR and SEER, and recommends capturing
  both a per-resource date/time and a bundle-assembly date/time. The
  CCRR work group's resolution on FHIR-54392 explicitly defers the
  bundle-level fix to **this** ticket (FHIR-54430), and splits the
  per-resource date question off into a separate future ticket.
- [FHIR-54409](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54409) (Triggers and
  Reporting for encounter-based triggers) is from the same reporter and
  pushes back on report cadence (every-30-day cap, lab-test volume),
  reinforcing the need for clearer trigger semantics — exactly the
  use-case-page text this ticket proposes to update.

The work group has already proposed a resolution: tighten
`Bundle.timestamp` on the Central Cancer Registry Reporting Bundle to
`1..1` and Must Support, and update the use-case description page to
state that triggers are based on encounter **close** (not encounter open).
After consideration, the team explicitly rejected tightening the
Encounter start-date constraint to `1..1` because of concerns about
making the profile overly restrictive.

## Details

**Description:**

> This is not required and not "must support". It is not fully clear to
> me what actually triggers the report bundle to be assembled: is it
> when an encounter occurs or when the provider "closes" the encounter?
> If closing the encounter is the actual date at which the report is
> generated, it may be informative to have it. Additionally, for
> auto-processing of many encounters per patient/cancer may include
> comparing reports over time, and this date may assist in that process.

**Resolution Description (work group draft):**

> We propose to make the following changes:
> - Change the constraints on the Central Cancer Registry Reporting
>   Bundle date (`bundle.timestamp`) MS and 1..1
> - Update the use case description page to make it clear that the
>   triggers are based on encounter **close**
>
> After further consideration, we propose **not** to tighten the
> constraint to 1..1 on the encounter start date due to concerns about
> making the profile overly restrictive.

## Related Zulip Discussions

### [implementers > Profile constraints on Bundle.timestamp and Meta.lastUpdated](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/implementers/topic/Profile%20constraints%20on%20Bundle.timestamp%20and%20Meta.lastUpdated/near/584061284)

The CCRR IG team (Wendy Blumenthal) brought the FHIR-54430 / FHIR-54392
ballot comments to the `implementers` stream to gauge implementer
feasibility. Wendy framed two questions: (a) tightening
`Bundle.timestamp` on the Central Cancer Registry Reporting Bundle from
`0..1` to `1..1` MS, and (b) tightening `Meta.lastUpdated` to `1..1`
across every profile in the IG. The thread reached a clear consensus on
both:

- **(a) `Bundle.timestamp` → 1..1 MS:** Grahame Grieve called this
  "easy"; Eric Haas noted that bundles are created on demand and are
  therefore amenable to capturing a timestamp; no implementer pushed
  back. This directly supports the FHIR-54430 resolution proposal.
- **(b) `Meta.lastUpdated` → 1..1 everywhere:** Strong pushback. Lloyd
  McKenzie pointed out that `Meta.lastUpdated` reflects "when did this
  data change on the server you happen to be looking at?" and is not a
  meaningful event date. Eric Haas and Gino Canessa cited US Core's
  prior work showing that façade servers often do not populate it
  reliably. Cooper Thompson and Lloyd ultimately reframed the request as
  really being about *clinically effective dates* on each contributing
  resource (e.g., `Procedure.performedDateTime`,
  `MedicationAdministration.effectiveDateTime`), not about
  `Meta.lastUpdated`. Wendy concluded the team would likely find request
  (b) **not persuasive** and reconsider per-resource clinical dates
  separately.

The Zulip outcome explains the asymmetric resolution on this ticket:
the bundle-level change (FHIR-54430) is well-supported, while the
per-resource-date path (the other half of FHIR-54392) was deferred
because the right element is *not* `Meta.lastUpdated`.

Participants: Wendy Blumenthal, Grahame Grieve, Eric Haas, Lloyd
McKenzie, Gino Canessa, Cooper Thompson.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the **US Central Cancer Registry Reporting Content IG**, on the
**Central Cancer Registry Reporting Bundle** profile:

1. Tighten `Bundle.timestamp` to **`1..1`** and mark it **Must
   Support**. In FSH this is a `* timestamp 1..1 MS` rule (or the
   equivalent `Constraint`/`Profile` edit) on the bundle profile under
   `input/fsh/`.
2. Update the use-case description page (the
   `usecases.html#use-case-triggers-and-reporting` section, authored
   under `input/pagecontent/usecases.md` or equivalent) to state
   explicitly that the report bundle is generated/assembled on
   **encounter close**, and that `Bundle.timestamp` MUST carry that
   instant.
3. Do **not** alter the cardinality of the Encounter start-date
   constraint (per the work group's stated decision in the resolution
   description).

#### Justification

This is exactly the change vetted on Zulip: Grahame Grieve and Eric Haas
both endorsed `Bundle.timestamp 1..1 MS` as low-burden because the
bundle is constructed on demand at report time, so the sender already
has the value. It satisfies the NAACCR / NPCR / SEER requirement (item
2110) raised in the parent ticket FHIR-54392 without dragging in the
contentious `Meta.lastUpdated` change request from the same Zulip
thread. Pinning the trigger semantics to encounter close also addresses
FHIR-54409's concern about report cadence by making the trigger boundary
unambiguous. The deliberate decision to leave Encounter start-date as-is
preserves implementer flexibility, which was the work group's stated
concern.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt Disposition A's `Bundle.timestamp 1..1 MS` change and use-case
clarification, **and additionally** add an invariant (FHIRPath
constraint) on the Central Cancer Registry Reporting Bundle requiring
that `Bundle.timestamp` be **greater than or equal to** the
`period.end` of the focal Encounter referenced from the bundle's
Composition. Author this as a `Bundle`-scoped invariant in the same
profile FSH file, with severity `error` and a human-readable message
such as "The bundle timestamp must be on or after the focal encounter's
end (close) time."

#### Justification

Disposition A says, in narrative, "the bundle is generated on encounter
close." Disposition B turns that narrative into a machine-checkable
invariant, which gives validators something to enforce and protects
downstream CCR auto-processing (the use case the reporter explicitly
called out — comparing many reports over time per patient/cancer). The
trade-off vs. A is implementation cost: senders that compute
`Bundle.timestamp` at any wall-clock moment will pass the invariant
trivially, but senders that pre-stage bundles before encounter close
will need to reorder their pipeline. Given that A *already* requires
encounter close as the trigger, this is a clarification of behavior the
sender must already exhibit, not a new burden. The Zulip thread did not
discuss invariants, so this is incremental risk the work group should
weigh; if implementer feedback is uncertain, this can be added in a
later cycle.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-54430 as **Not Persuasive**: leave `Bundle.timestamp` at
`0..1` non-MS on the Central Cancer Registry Reporting Bundle, and do
not change the use-case page wording around encounter triggers.

#### Justification

A "decline" path is hard to defend here and is included for
completeness only. The base FHIR `Bundle.timestamp` element is already
defined and is universally supported by FHIR servers/clients, so
tightening costs the ecosystem essentially nothing (Grahame Grieve's
"easy" verdict). The reporter's NAACCR / NPCR / SEER mapping to data
item 2110 is a real downstream requirement, and refusing it forces
every implementer to convey the same information out-of-band. The only
coherent rationale for declining would be that the underlying trigger
semantics ("when is a report generated?") are still under active
discussion — but that ambiguity is precisely what the use-case page
update in Disposition A resolves.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **A**

Disposition A is exactly the resolution the work group has already
drafted on the ticket, and it is the one fully supported by the
`implementers` Zulip thread (Grahame Grieve, Eric Haas, no implementer
objections to part (a)). It satisfies the NAACCR / NPCR / SEER
requirement raised in FHIR-54392, clarifies the encounter-close trigger
semantics that FHIR-54409 also flagged, and respects the work group's
explicit decision to *not* over-constrain Encounter start dates.
Disposition B is a defensible enhancement — and worth flagging as a
follow-up — but should not block this ticket: the Zulip discussion did
not vet a new invariant and adding one without implementer feedback
risks slowing the resolution of an otherwise consensus item.
Disposition C is not defensible given the explicit downstream
regulatory mapping (NAACCR 2110) and the negligible implementer cost.
