## Summary

Sarah Gaunt has filed this ticket against the Electronic Case Reporting (eICR)
FHIR IG (currently in ballot) to revisit how antimicrobial susceptibility results
are modelled. As authored, eICR represents susceptibility using a dedicated
**Susceptibility Observation** profile that is linked from the **Organism
Detected Observation** via `Observation.hasMember` — the long-standing OO
"panel" pattern. The reporter is asking the workgroup to consider an
alternative in which the separate Susceptibility Observation profile is
**removed** and the susceptibility content is instead expressed as
**`component` constraints on the eICR Organism Detected Observation** (with a
possible rename of that profile to something more meaningful).

The trigger is the recent Orders & Observation WG Zulip discussion in
`#Orders and Observation WG > Blood Type`, where Lloyd McKenzie argued that
each Observation must be meaningful on its own, so the drug being tested and
its susceptibility result belong in a single Observation (drug in
`Observation.code` when possible, otherwise in `Observation.component`). Rob
Hausam and Andrea Pitkus defended the existing OO/lab convention of one
Observation per antibiotic linked via `hasMember` from the organism-identified
Observation. The ticket asks the Public Health WG to decide whether eICR
should pivot before publication. There are no other cross-referenced Jira
tickets and no related GitHub items.

## Related Zulip Discussions

### [Orders and Observation WG > Blood Type](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/Orders%20and%20Observation%20WG/topic/Blood%20Type)

A long-running thread (originally opened by Grahame Grieve in 2018, revived
in 2025 around IPS blood-group bindings, and re-revived in January 2026 by
the susceptibility sub-discussion that triggered this ticket). On the
susceptibility question specifically: **Rob Hausam** and **Andrea Pitkus**
maintain the OO position that microbiology susceptibility results should be
modelled as one Observation per antibiotic — the drug is identified in
`Observation.code` (e.g., LOINC `18861-5` *Amoxicillin [Susceptibility]*),
the result in `Observation.value` and/or `Observation.interpretation`
(S/I/R, MIC), and the panel is held together via `hasMember` from the
organism-identification Observation (a shortcut for the derived isolate
specimen). Andrea additionally argues that this matches how LIS systems
actually order/report AST panels (gram-positive panel, gram-negative panel,
etc.) and that pre-coordinating ABO+Rh or drug+result causes reporting
problems when one half is missing. **Lloyd McKenzie** counters that any
Observation must be meaningful in isolation and that "susceptible" without
the drug — or "susceptible to amoxicillin" without the result — is not safe;
he ultimately narrows his ask to: *when the drug is **not** in
`Observation.code`, it must go in `Observation.component`, not in a
separate Observation*. **Cooper Thompson** sides with Lloyd in principle and
points to Epic's published micro structure. Rob proposes that any
post-coordination of a "tested antimicrobial drug" qualifier could be done
via an extension or new element rather than `component`; Lloyd pushes back
that `component` is exactly the right place for qualifiers (cuff size, body
position, etc.) and is what generic Observation viewers will render.
**Sarah Gaunt** (the reporter) closes the loop by noting eICR currently uses
the `hasMember` pattern and filing this Jira so the eCR ballot can pivot to
`component` if OO/PH agree. **Shelley Chang** later adds blood-bank-specific
input on ABO/Rh historical vs. current observations (relevant to the
broader thread, peripheral to the susceptibility question). No consensus
was reached.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Withdraw the standalone **eICR Susceptibility Observation** profile and
re-express susceptibility on the **eICR Organism Detected Observation**
profile (renamed to a clearer name such as *eICR Organism Identification and
Susceptibility Observation*). Specifically:

- Remove the Susceptibility Observation profile (and the corresponding
  example/instance and search-parameter usages) from the eICR IG.
- Add a slice on `Observation.component` of the renamed Organism Detected
  Observation profile, one slice per panel member, where each slice
  constrains:
  - `component.code` to the susceptibility test concept (LOINC
    "[antibiotic] [Susceptibility]" code, e.g. `18861-5`), bound to a
    must-support value set of common AST LOINCs.
  - `component.value[x]` to `Quantity` (MIC) or `CodeableConcept`
    (qualitative result) with a binding to the S/I/R/SDD value set.
  - `component.interpretation` available for S/I/R when a quantitative MIC
    is reported.
- Replace the existing `hasMember` linkage from Organism Detected →
  Susceptibility Observation with the inline component representation;
  document the migration for implementers currently aligned with the ballot
  draft.

#### Justification

This is what the ticket literally requests and what Lloyd McKenzie advocates
for: the resulting Observation is self-describing — a recipient who receives
just the organism Observation also receives every drug/result pair that
qualifies it, so each piece of data is meaningful in context without
requiring traversal of `hasMember` to reassemble the panel. Component-based
qualifiers are also the pattern Observation already uses for analogous
"qualifier" data (cuff size, body position) and are renderable by generic
Observation viewers without IG-specific code. For a Public Health payload,
self-contained-on-receipt is a real virtue, because eICR messages can be
filtered, transformed, or partially routed before they reach the consuming
PHA.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

**Keep the separate Susceptibility Observation profile but tighten it, and
do not collapse into `component`.** Concretely:

- Retain the eICR Susceptibility Observation as a distinct profile, one
  instance per antibiotic, with `Observation.code` bound to AST LOINCs
  (e.g. `18861-5`), `Observation.value[x]` typed to `Quantity` (MIC) or
  `CodeableConcept` (qualitative), and `Observation.interpretation` bound
  to S/I/R/SDD.
- Make the **link from Organism Detected → Susceptibility Observation
  explicit and required** by mandating `Observation.hasMember` (or
  `Observation.triggeredBy` of type `reflex`, as Rob Hausam mentioned the
  EU group has been exploring) with a typed reference to the
  Susceptibility Observation profile and `mustSupport = true` on both ends.
- Optionally rename the Organism Detected Observation profile to make its
  panel role clear (e.g. *eICR Organism Identification Observation*) and
  keep the susceptibility profile under a more descriptive name (e.g.
  *eICR Antimicrobial Susceptibility Result*).
- Document the safety expectation that consumers must follow the
  `hasMember`/`triggeredBy` link to recover the isolate/organism context.

#### Justification

This matches the OO Diagnostics-module convention that Rob Hausam describes
and the laboratory operational reality that Andrea Pitkus describes:
LIS/LIMS systems already model AST as a reflex panel order with one result
field per antibiotic, mapped to LOINC. The drug *is* in `Observation.code`
in real lab data, so Lloyd's "must be in `component`" condition does not
actually trigger in the typical eICR payload. Disposition A would diverge
eICR from US Core / IPS / ELR-aligned lab modelling and create extra
mapping work for every LIS-fed eCR sender. The remaining concern Lloyd
raises — that an isolated Susceptibility Observation lacks organism context
— is real but is solved by *requiring* the link rather than by inlining
into components, and avoids over-loading a single Observation with what is
operationally a multi-result panel.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change as out of scope for the current eICR ballot cycle. Leave
the Susceptibility Observation profile and the `hasMember` linkage from
Organism Detected as published in the ballot draft, and refer the broader
modelling debate to OO. Track any future change under a new ticket once
OO has reached a position on the underlying
`hasMember`-vs-`component`-vs-`triggeredBy` question for microbiology.

#### Justification

The Zulip thread is not converged: OO members and a board-certified lab
informatician argue for the existing `hasMember` pattern; Lloyd and Cooper
argue for `component` (in a narrower set of cases); Rob has floated a third
option (`triggeredBy` for reflex relationships) that has not been evaluated
for eICR. Re-modelling a profile that is currently in ballot, on a topic
that the owning workgroup (OO) has not settled, risks publishing a
structure that diverges from what OO ultimately recommends and that would
need to change again next cycle. Holding for an OO decision protects
implementers from churn and avoids eICR diverging from the broader FHIR
microbiology pattern.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The literal request in Disposition A is well-motivated by Lloyd's safety
argument, but it would put eICR — a Public Health IG that consumes lab
output — at odds with how laboratories actually order, perform, and report
AST today (LOINC-coded one-result-per-antibiotic, organised into reflex
panels), and with the existing OO Diagnostics-module guidance. Disposition
C is defensible but leaves a known weakness in the ballot (an under-specified
link between organism and susceptibility) untouched. Disposition B captures
the real benefit Lloyd is asking for — every Susceptibility Observation
must be safely interpretable, with its organism context recoverable — by
making the `hasMember` (or `triggeredBy`) linkage *required and typed*,
while preserving alignment with OO, US Core, IPS and existing LIS workflows.
A profile rename for clarity (per the reporter's secondary suggestion) is
worth doing under either A or B and should be carried in this disposition as
well. If OO subsequently lands on a `component`-based recommendation for
post-coordinated AST, eICR can revisit in a later cycle without having
shipped a divergent model in the interim.
