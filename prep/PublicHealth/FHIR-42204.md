## Summary

The reporter (Ron G. Parker, originally raised as Comment 16 against the BSeR
ballot) asks the BSeR authors to provide the rationale for requiring
`Task.requester` (min = 1) on the **BSeR Referral Task** profile when that
Task is already required to reference a **BSeR Referral Service Request**
instance, and the BSeR Referral Service Request profile itself already
constrains `ServiceRequest.requester` to min = 1. The submitter views the
duplicated `requester` requirement as adding implementation burden without an
obvious clinical or workflow justification, and is asking either for a stated
rationale (so implementers understand why both elements must be populated and
whether they can ever legitimately differ) or for the constraint to be
relaxed.

No Jira cross-references, no Zulip discussions, and no GitHub items were
returned by the data sources for this ticket.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat this as a **request for documentation** and add explicit rationale text
to the BSeR Referral Task profile page (and the IG's general "Task vs.
ServiceRequest" guidance, if any) explaining why `Task.requester` is
constrained to min = 1 even though the referenced BSeR Referral Service
Request profile already mandates `ServiceRequest.requester`. The added
narrative should:

1. State that `Task.requester` is the actor *requesting the action represented
   by the Task* (i.e., the party asking that the referral be acted on / routed
   / fulfilled), while `ServiceRequest.requester` is the *clinical author* of
   the underlying order.
2. Acknowledge that in many BSeR exchanges these two parties will be the same
   organization or practitioner, but call out scenarios where they can
   legitimately differ (e.g., a coordinating hub or intermediary forwarding a
   clinician-authored referral, a delegated administrative requester acting
   on behalf of the clinical author).
3. Clarify whether implementers MAY echo the ServiceRequest's requester into
   the Task when the two parties are identical, and confirm that doing so is
   conformant.

No structural change to the Task profile is required under this disposition;
the change is purely narrative on the BSeR Referral Task profile page.

#### Justification

The reporter is explicitly asking for a *rationale* — the literal request is
informational, not a constraint change. FHIR Task and ServiceRequest were
deliberately designed with separate `requester` elements to capture the
distinction between the *order author* and the *requester of the workflow
step*; preserving the existing cardinality but explaining it is consistent
with how the base FHIR specification distinguishes Task from the request it
fulfills. Adding the rationale closes out a "Question"-type ticket cleanly
without breaking any existing conformant implementations and matches the
intent of the `BSeR_GUIDANCE` label, which signals that the resolution is
expected to land as guidance text.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Relax `BSeR Referral Task.requester` from `min = 1` to `min = 0` (keeping
must-support) and add a profile-level invariant or narrative rule of the
form: *"If `Task.requester` is absent, the requester is taken to be the
`requester` of the referenced BSeR Referral Service Request; if present, it
SHALL identify the party requesting that the Task be acted on, which MAY
differ from the underlying ServiceRequest.requester."* Also update the
profile guidance to state that populating `Task.requester` is only required
when it differs from the linked ServiceRequest's requester.

#### Justification

This directly addresses the reporter's "additional burden" concern by
removing the duplicative population requirement in the common case where the
two requesters are the same party, while preserving the ability to express a
different Task-level requester when the workflow legitimately separates
order authorship from referral routing. The trade-off versus Disposition A
is conformance impact: existing BSeR senders that already populate
`Task.requester` remain conformant, but receivers that previously relied on
`Task.requester` always being present would need to fall back to the linked
ServiceRequest. Because BSeR is an IG layered on top of US Core / FHIR R4,
relaxing a min from 1 → 0 is a backward-compatible change for senders but a
forward-compatible change for receivers, so the profile version should be
bumped and the change called out in the IG's change log.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request as **Not Persuasive** on the basis that the existing
constraint is intentional: BSeR profiles `Task.requester` to min = 1 because
Task and ServiceRequest model two different actors in the referral workflow
(the workflow requester vs. the clinical order author), and the IG requires
both to be explicit so receivers do not have to dereference and traverse the
linked ServiceRequest to determine who is asking that the referral be acted
on. Close the ticket with a short Resolution Description citing FHIR's
Task/Request separation of concerns and pointing to the existing must-support
guidance.

#### Justification

If the BSeR authors' design intent is that `Task.requester` always be
independently asserted (so that consumers of the Task can route, audit, and
respond without parsing the embedded ServiceRequest), then the duplication
is a deliberate and well-grounded conformance requirement, and changing it
would weaken the IG's interoperability story. Declining is defensible when
the cost of the requirement (one additional reference per Task) is small
relative to the receiver-side benefit of having the workflow requester
materialised on the Task itself. The risk is that without an accompanying
guidance note explaining this rationale, the same question is likely to
recur in future ballot cycles.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

This ticket is filed as a `Question` with the `BSeR_GUIDANCE` label, the
reporter's literal ask is for the *rationale*, and there is no Zulip or
GitHub discussion suggesting that the BSeR community is pushing for a
structural relaxation. Disposition A satisfies the stated request, costs
the workgroup nothing in terms of conformance impact, and pre-empts the
recurrence captured by Disposition C's risk. Disposition B is a credible
follow-up if, once the rationale is written down, the workgroup concludes
that the duplication really is gratuitous in the common case — but that
decision should be made deliberately on its own ticket rather than folded
into a "Question" disposition. A brief paragraph on the BSeR Referral Task
profile page, plus a one-line note on the BSeR Referral Service Request
profile cross-referencing it, is the lowest-risk way to close this out.
