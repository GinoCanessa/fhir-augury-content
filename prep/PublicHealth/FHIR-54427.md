## Summary

A NAACCR community member, submitting through reporter AshleyKnealing, asks
why the US Central Cancer Registry Reporting (CCRR) IG's patient industry
code and patient occupation code data items appear to require a
`dataAbsentReason` when those elements are themselves optional
(cardinality not required) and `mustSupport = false`. The submitter views
the apparent obligation to populate `dataAbsentReason` for non-required,
non-must-support elements as inconsistent and is requesting clarification
or removal of that obligation. No related Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or
GitHub items were found in the cross-reference index for this ticket; the
question has already been triaged with a resolution of
"Considered - Question answered".

## Details

**Description:**

> Data items are not required and Must support = No, so it is not clear
> to me why a Data Absent Reason is required.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat the request as a substantive change request against the CCRR IG
and remove (or weaken) the `dataAbsentReason` obligation on the
patient industry code and patient occupation code elements when they are
themselves neither required (`min = 0`) nor `mustSupport = true`. In
practical terms this would mean either (a) loosening the binding /
invariant in CCRR's local profile of the ODH `OccupationalDataForHealth`
/ `Occupation` / `Industry` elements so that `dataAbsentReason` is no
longer required, or (b) annotating the CCRR profile to clarify that the
`dataAbsentReason` obligation is inherited from ODH and should not be
enforced for non-must-support elements in CCRR.

#### Justification

If the elements are genuinely optional and not must-support in CCRR,
then forcing implementers to populate `dataAbsentReason` whenever the
value is absent imposes an asymmetric burden — implementers must do
work for an element the IG itself does not require them to support.
Removing or scoping down the obligation aligns the constraint with the
element's own usage rules and reduces implementer confusion, which
matches the submitter's reading of the spec.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the underlying ODH profile constraints intact (since CCRR reuses
ODH "without change", per the resolution comment) and instead address
the confusion in the CCRR IG's narrative: add a brief clarifying note
on the CCRR profile pages that use the ODH industry / occupation
elements, explaining that the `dataAbsentReason` obligation is inherited
from the ODH profile and applies whenever the element is *included but
empty*, not whenever it is simply omitted. Where appropriate, also raise
a corresponding ticket against the ODH IG (Occupational Data for Health,
HL7.FHIR.US.ODH) so the underlying constraint is reviewed in its
authoring IG, as the CCRR work group already directed the commenter to
do.

#### Justification

The resolution comment is explicit that the elements come from ODH and
are used in CCRR "without change". Modifying the constraint locally in
CCRR would fork the ODH profile semantics and create a divergence
between the two IGs — exactly the situation profile reuse is meant to
avoid. A documentation clarification in CCRR plus a referral to the
authoring IG resolves the implementer confusion without breaking the
"reuse unchanged" contract, and respects the work-group ownership
boundary between Public Health (CCRR) and the ODH editors.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Confirm and retain the existing resolution: "Considered - Question
answered." No change is made to the CCRR IG. The submitter is referred
(as already done in the resolution comment) to the ODH co-editors —
Lori Reed-Fourquet, Rob Hausam, and Mark Kramer — to pursue the
question against the IG that owns the constraint.

#### Justification

The ticket is a Question, not a change request, and the constraint in
question is not authored in CCRR — it is inherited verbatim from the
ODH IG. The CCRR work group has no standing to relax a constraint that
belongs to another IG, and doing so unilaterally would create a silent
divergence from ODH. The right venue for the substantive question is
the ODH IG, and the resolution comment already routed the submitter
there.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** C

The ticket is a Question whose subject matter sits in another IG (ODH),
and the work group has already executed the only action it can
defensibly take on its own: explain the provenance of the constraint
and direct the submitter to the authoring IG's editors. Disposition A
would silently fork an ODH profile inside CCRR, which the resolution
comment explicitly disclaims. Disposition B is a reasonable
quality-of-life improvement (add a clarifying narrative in CCRR and
file a follow-up ticket against ODH) and the work group may want to
adopt it as a follow-on action, but the disposition of *this* ticket
should remain "Considered - Question answered" and be confirmed as
such.
