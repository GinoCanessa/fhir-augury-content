## Summary

The ticket asks the Public Health work group (specifically the Maternal and
Infant Health Research / MIHR FHIR IG) to re-anchor the canonical URL of the
`hypertensive-disorders-pregnancy-SNOMED` ValueSet under
`terminology.hl7.org` (THO) instead of `hl7.org/fhir`, and to open a
companion UTG ticket so the ValueSet is registered in THO. Per TSMG
guidance cited in the ticket, only ValueSets that participate in a *required*
binding to a `code` data type should remain anchored under `hl7.org/fhir`;
all other ValueSets — including informative/example bindings such as the one
in MIHR — should live in THO. This is a "Block-Vote-1" terminology hygiene
change rather than a content change to the ValueSet itself, and it was
originally dispositioned "Considered for Future Use" in 2022 pending IG
maturity and finalization of the draft THO process, then re-opened post STU
publication in March 2023 by lynn_laakso.

No other Jira tickets are cross-referenced with FHIR-37139 in the Augury
index, so the request stands on its own; however, it is a member of the
"4" series of analogous "Defining URL should be anchored in THO" tickets
that TSMG has filed against multiple HL7 IGs, so disposition decisions
should be consistent with how peer IGs have handled the same request.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Migrate the ValueSet currently published at
`http://www.hl7.org/fhir/us/mihr/ValueSet/hypertensive-disorders-pregnancy-SNOMED`
to THO. Concretely:

1. File a UTG ticket (per TSMG instruction in the ticket body) to register
   the ValueSet in THO under a canonical such as
   `http://terminology.hl7.org/ValueSet/hypertensive-disorders-pregnancy-SNOMED`
   (final canonical to be agreed with TSMG/UTG).
2. In the next MIHR build, replace the in-IG ValueSet definition with a
   dependency on the THO-published ValueSet and update any
   `ElementDefinition.binding.valueSet` references inside MIHR profiles,
   measures, and CQL libraries to point at the new THO canonical.
3. Retain a redirect / deprecation note on the old `hl7.org/fhir/us/mihr`
   URL so existing implementer references resolve, and add a release-note
   entry calling out the canonical move.
4. Repeat the same treatment for the other MIHR ValueSets that are not
   required-bound to a `code` data type (the ticket title "...4" suggests
   peer tickets exist for each of them); MIHR's `ValueSet-*.xml` files
   such as `hypertensive-disorders-pregnancy-ICD` and
   `PregnancyProcedureDelivery-CPT` (visible in the Connectathon thread)
   are likely covered by the same TSMG policy.

#### Justification

This is the disposition TSMG explicitly asked for, and it aligns MIHR with
HL7 terminology policy: example/non-`code`-required ValueSets belong in
THO so they are versioned, browsable, and reusable across IGs rather than
being scoped to a single product family's URL. The 2023 re-open comment
("re-open post STU publication") signals that the original blockers — IG
immaturity and the unfinished THO publishing pipeline — no longer apply,
and other Public Health IGs have moved comparable ValueSets to THO since.

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the policy intent but stage the move: leave the canonical URL of
`hypertensive-disorders-pregnancy-SNOMED` unchanged in the next MIHR
release and instead (a) publish a parallel copy in THO with a new
canonical, (b) add a `ConceptMap`/cross-walk note in MIHR pointing to the
THO copy as the preferred canonical going forward, and (c) flip MIHR's
internal bindings to the THO canonical only when MIHR is re-balloted.
This avoids breaking any implementer references created during the STU1
period while still satisfying TSMG's request to land the artifact in THO.

#### Justification

The ValueSet is already in active use by implementers (the September-2022
Connectathon thread shows it being exercised against live FHIR servers
for the PIHCohort measure). A hard canonical change post-STU could break
saved Bundles, validators that pin to the old URL, and downstream
measures that resolved the ValueSet by canonical. Staging the migration
respects TSMG policy without forcing an immediate breaking change on the
implementer community, and matches how several other STU IGs have
sequenced their THO migrations.

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change for the MIHR STU1 errata cycle on the grounds that
re-anchoring a published ValueSet's canonical URL is a breaking change to
implementers and that the IG's terminology layer should be addressed
holistically in the next normative or STU2 ballot rather than via
piecemeal per-ValueSet tickets. Recommend that TSMG/UTG instead absorb
the existing canonicals into THO via aliasing if their goal is THO
discoverability without disturbing implementers.

#### Justification

A canonical URL is, by FHIR convention, an immutable identifier; changing
it after publication invalidates resolved references in saved instances,
profiles, and quality measures. Block-vote terminology hygiene tickets
filed in bulk by TSMG against many IGs do not always weigh per-IG
implementer impact, and MIHR is a small-footprint research IG where the
remediation cost (UTG ticket, build changes, re-publication, implementer
notification) outweighs the marginal discoverability benefit of THO
hosting for a ValueSet that is already linked from the published IG.

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The TSMG policy is sound and the 2023 re-open is justified, so a flat
decline (C) is hard to defend at the work-group level — it would put MIHR
out of step with peer IGs and with the very policy MIHR is expected to
follow. However, a straight Accept-As-Requested (A) underestimates the
cost of swapping a canonical on an artifact that is already exercised by
implementers in Connectathon and reference-implementation environments.
Disposition B threads that needle: file the UTG ticket and stand the
ValueSet up in THO immediately (satisfying TSMG and unblocking other IGs
that want to reuse it), but defer the in-IG canonical flip to the next
MIHR ballot so existing references continue to resolve through the STU1
window. This is the path most consistent with FHIR canonical-URL
stability conventions while still moving MIHR toward the target
terminology architecture.
