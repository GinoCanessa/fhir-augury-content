## Summary

This ticket, submitted by Craig Newman on behalf of Amanda Payne (JMC),
consolidates implementer responses to a set of ballot questions posed by the
Electronic Case Reporting (eCR) FHIR IG. The respondent provides direction on
four open design questions: (1) whether eCR should align Occupation and
Industry with the US Core Occupation profile (and the GenV3 MMG move toward
ONETSOC/NAICS) instead of the Occupational Data for Health (ODH) IG;
(2) whether Aborted Medications should be disallowed; (3) whether Pregnancy
Intent should be carried in eCR documents; and (4) which Vital Signs are most
pertinent to public health case reporting. No related Jira tickets, Zulip
threads, or GitHub items were surfaced through cross-reference, search, or
linked-issue lookups, so the reviewer should treat this ticket as a
standalone disposition input for the eCR ballot reconciliation.

## Details

**Description:**

Submitted on behalf of Amanda Payne (JMC)

- **Occupation & Industry:** US Core 6.1.0 contains the
  [US Core Occupation Profile](https://hl7.org/fhir/us/core/STU6.1/StructureDefinition-us-core-observation-occupation.html)
  that implements the US Core Data for Interoperability (USCDI) Occupation
  and Occupation Industry requirements. Currently eCR uses the
  [Occupational Data for Health (ODH)](http://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/us-odh/).
  Going forward should eCR:
  - Occupation and Industry: change to using only the US Core profiles. The
    draft GenV3 MMG is also proposing to move to the ONETSOC and NAICS codes
    for industry and occupation, so it would be nice to have consistency
    between terminologies used in eCR and case notifications.
- **Aborted Medications:** yes, these should be disallowed. They are not used
  by RCKMS and I don't really see a public health need for them.
- **Pregnancy Intent:** Could maybe be used to flag Hep B, Hep C, HIV cases
  for potential perinatal follow-up, but in reality, it would probably just
  create more noise. I don't think it should be included.
- **Vital Signs — What vital signs are most pertinent?**
  - Most pertinent: oxygen saturation, head circumference (for some birth
    defects), body temperature, blood pressure, heart rate, respiratory rate.

## Proposed Dispositions

Because this ticket bundles four independent ballot questions, the
dispositions below treat the bundle holistically — the workgroup will
likely need to split it into per-topic resolutions, but a single
recommended posture is given so the discussion has a starting point.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt all four respondent positions in the next eCR FHIR IG publication:

1. **Occupation & Industry.** Replace the ODH-based occupation/industry
   pattern in the eCR composition with profiles that derive from (or
   directly reuse) `us-core-observation-occupation`. Bind industry and
   occupation codes to ONETSOC (occupation) and NAICS (industry) value
   sets to match the GenV3 MMG draft, and remove any normative
   references to ODH profiles from the eCR IG (retain a non-normative
   migration note).
2. **Aborted Medications.** In the eCR Medication-related profiles,
   constrain `MedicationRequest.status` (and any equivalent slice on
   `MedicationStatement.status`) to exclude `entered-in-error` /
   `stopped` / "aborted" semantics — i.e., disallow medications that
   were never actually administered or were withdrawn before
   administration — by tightening the value-set binding to a required
   subset that omits those codes.
3. **Pregnancy Intent.** Remove the Pregnancy Intent observation slice
   from the eCR composition's pregnancy section (or mark it
   `0..0`/`MustSupport=false`), and document that it is intentionally
   out of scope for case reporting.
4. **Vital Signs.** Constrain the eCR Vital Signs section to the
   six measures the respondent named (oxygen saturation, head
   circumference, body temperature, blood pressure, heart rate,
   respiratory rate) — implemented either as `MustSupport` slices on
   the US Core Vital Signs profiles, or as a tightened value-set
   binding on the slice discriminator.

#### Justification

The respondent represents a real implementing program (JMC), and the
positions align eCR with already-published HL7 US Realm work
(US Core 6.1.0) and a parallel public-health initiative (GenV3 MMG).
Aligning Occupation/Industry with US Core reduces implementer burden,
because most eCR senders already have to support US Core for other
obligations. Disallowing aborted medications and pregnancy intent
removes data elements that the operational consumer (RCKMS) does not
use, which trims sender effort and shrinks payload. Constraining Vital
Signs to the six clinically-relevant measures is consistent with how
US Core Vital Signs is already factored.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the spirit of the responses but soften the mechanism so eCR does
not lose existing implementer investment or block jurisdictions whose
needs differ from the respondent's:

1. **Occupation & Industry.** Add `us-core-observation-occupation` as a
   second supported pattern alongside the current ODH-based profiles
   for the next ballot cycle, with `MustSupport` on the US Core
   pattern and `MustSupport=false` on the ODH pattern. Publish ONETSOC
   and NAICS as the preferred (but `extensible`, not `required`)
   bindings. Schedule full ODH removal for the cycle after that, once
   the US Core path has burn-in data.
2. **Aborted Medications.** Rather than removing the codes from the
   value set, keep them allowed but add an invariant / guidance note
   that senders SHOULD NOT include medications in a "not-taken" /
   "aborted" status in eCR documents unless explicitly relevant to
   the reportable condition.
3. **Pregnancy Intent.** Keep the observation in the IG as an optional
   (`0..*`, `MustSupport=false`) slice so jurisdictions that do want
   to use it for perinatal Hep B / Hep C / HIV follow-up are not
   blocked, with guidance that it is not expected in the general case.
4. **Vital Signs.** Mark the six named measures `MustSupport=true` but
   leave the Vital Signs section open (`0..*`) so additional
   condition-specific vitals can still flow when clinically relevant.

#### Justification

eCR is a multi-jurisdiction, multi-condition framework; absolute
exclusions made on a single respondent's input risk breaking other
implementers' workflows or pre-empting future condition-specific
needs. The "MustSupport + soft binding + planned removal" pattern is
the standard FHIR mechanism for steering implementers toward a target
without producing immediate breaking changes, and it preserves
backwards compatibility for senders mid-build. It also keeps optional
data flowing for jurisdictions whose triggering rules disagree with
RCKMS.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline this ticket as a normative change driver, on the grounds that
it is a single implementer's response to ballot questions rather than
a defect or a consensus position. Record the four answers in the
ballot disposition log, route them to the respective topic-specific
tracker tickets (one each for Occupation/Industry, Aborted Medications,
Pregnancy Intent, Vital Signs), and resolve this ticket as
"Considered — for Future Use" without changing the IG until the
companion topic tickets accumulate enough convergent input to act.

#### Proposal — concretely

Resolve `FHIR-54526` as `Considered for Future Use` with a public
comment that the four responses have been logged into the eCR ballot
disposition spreadsheet and folded into the per-topic reconciliation
tickets.

#### Justification

A single ballot response, however well-informed, is not by itself a
sufficient basis for normative changes that affect every eCR sender —
especially for topics that span jurisdictional triggering rules
(Pregnancy Intent), terminology realignment (ODH → US Core /
ONETSOC / NAICS), and value-set tightening (medication status).
Bundling four unrelated technical decisions into one disposition also
makes the audit trail harder to follow at the next ballot. Declining
this ticket as the change driver, while preserving the input via the
topic-specific tickets, is the cleanest way to keep the ballot
reconciliation traceable.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B best matches how the respondent's input actually arrived:
strong, useful, but single-source. Outright acceptance (A) hard-codes
one implementer's view into normative constraints — most damagingly for
Pregnancy Intent (where another jurisdiction may legitimately want
perinatal follow-up signaling) and for ODH removal (where existing
senders have invested in ODH and need a deprecation path, not a cliff).
Outright decline (C) wastes substantive ballot input and forces the
workgroup to re-litigate the same four topics on follow-up tickets.
Disposition B captures the respondent's direction as `MustSupport` and
deprecation signals while keeping the IG open enough that other
public-health jurisdictions can still do their job, and it sets up a
clean path to the stronger constraints in Disposition A at the
following ballot cycle. The workgroup should also strongly consider
splitting this ticket into four child tickets so each topic can be
tracked and resolved on its own merits.
