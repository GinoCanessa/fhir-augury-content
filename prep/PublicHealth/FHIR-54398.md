## Summary

The submitter, on behalf of a NAACCR community member, asks the US Central
Cancer Registry Reporting Content IG (US CCRR) authors to extend the IG's
example coverage beyond the existing breast cancer use case. Specifically,
they request worked-out workflow examples for additional incident cancer
cases — prostate, lung (including bronchial washings), cervix, Hodgkin
lymphoma, and acute leukemia — so that implementers can confirm the
necessary FHIR resources are present and that terminology bindings are
exercised across a representative range of disease sites and specimen
types. The IG's editors triaged the request and resolved it as
"Considered for Future Use," noting that the work is substantial and is
planned for a subsequent version. No cross-referenced Jira tickets,
Zulip threads, GitHub items, or Confluence pages were returned by the
augury index for this ticket.

## Proposed Dispositions

> **Note:** the ticket has already been triaged with the resolution
> "Considered for Future Use." The dispositions below are framed as
> options the work group could revisit if priorities or capacity change,
> consistent with the existing triage outcome.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a complete worked example bundle to the US Central Cancer Registry
Reporting Content IG for each of the five requested incident cancer
cases — prostate, lung (with a bronchial washings Specimen variant),
cervix, Hodgkin lymphoma, and acute leukemia. Each example would be a
self-contained `Bundle` of the patient-journey resources the IG already
profiles (Patient/Group, Condition for the incident diagnosis,
Specimen(s) including site/collection method, Procedure(s) for the
work-up, DiagnosticReport(s) for pathology and lab results, and
Observation(s) for staging, grade, biomarkers, and any
disease-specific elements), wired to the IG's profiles and value-set
bindings so that the example actually exercises each binding.
Publication should include narrative pages walking through each
scenario and the example bundles linked from the existing breast
cancer use-case page.

#### Justification

Worked examples are the most direct way to demonstrate that the IG's
profile set and terminology bindings are sufficient for the range of
incident cancers the registry is expected to receive, and they give
implementers concrete templates they can adapt. The submitter's list
deliberately covers a solid-tumor variety (prostate, lung, cervix), a
hematologic lymphoma (Hodgkin), and an acute leukemia, which together
stress different parts of the model — particularly Specimen typing
(e.g., bronchial washings) and disease-specific Observations
(staging vs. lineage/blast counts). Acceptance also acknowledges the
NAACCR community origin of the request, where the IG's adopters live.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than committing to fully authored example bundles for all five
cancer types in one cycle, accept the underlying need by (1) publishing
a documented "patient-journey example template" derived from the
existing breast cancer scenario that explicitly enumerates which
profiles, must-support elements, and bound value sets need to be
instantiated per cancer type, (2) prioritizing one or two of the
requested cancers (e.g., prostate and lung-with-bronchial-washings,
which together cover solid-tumor variation and a non-trivial Specimen
case) for fully built-out example bundles in the next ballot cycle,
and (3) opening tracker items for the remaining cancers (cervix,
Hodgkin lymphoma, acute leukemia) so they can be picked up
incrementally without blocking publication. Optionally invite NAACCR
community contributors to supply candidate clinical content that the
editors then convert into compliant example bundles.

#### Justification

This addresses the editors' stated capacity concern (recorded in the
resolution description) while still delivering tangible coverage
beyond breast cancer in the near term. Spreading the work over
releases avoids a large one-shot example backlog and lets each new
example be reviewed against actual implementer feedback. Sharing the
authoring load with NAACCR community members — who are already the
source of this request — increases the odds that the examples reflect
real registry workflows rather than synthetic ones.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (ratify the existing "Considered for Future
Use" outcome)

#### Proposal

Confirm the current resolution: do not add the additional example
bundles in the current publication of the US Central Cancer Registry
Reporting Content IG, and record the request on the work group's
backlog of post-ballot enhancements. Communicate to the submitter that
the breast cancer use case remains the canonical worked example for
this version, and that additional cancer-type scenarios are planned
for a future version.

#### Justification

The triage outcome already reflects an explicit capacity constraint
("a significant amount of work that we will not be able implement in
the current version"). Declining for the current cycle keeps the IG's
publication scope realistic, avoids slipping the schedule for an
example set that would be incomplete or under-reviewed, and preserves
the request as a tracked future enhancement rather than dropping it.
The IG's profile and binding correctness can also be validated through
other means (validator runs, conformance test bundles) while the more
elaborate narrative examples are queued for a later release.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The submitter's underlying concern — that the IG only ships one fully
worked patient-journey example, and that this leaves the resource
coverage and terminology bindings under-exercised across cancer
types — is legitimate and worth acting on, especially given that the
request comes from the NAACCR implementer community the IG is built
for. At the same time, the editors' capacity argument behind
"Considered for Future Use" is reasonable and should not be dismissed.
Disposition B threads the needle: it commits to a structured template
plus a small number of additional example bundles that genuinely
stress different parts of the model (solid tumors, hematologic
malignancies, non-trivial Specimen handling), and it leaves the
remaining cancer types as tracked, scoped work rather than dropping
them entirely. That gives the work group a concrete path forward to
discuss without forcing an all-or-nothing choice between Disposition A
(accept the full scope now) and Disposition C (defer indefinitely).
