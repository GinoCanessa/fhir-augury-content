## Summary

This ticket asks the **US Public Health Profiles Library (USPHPL)** to
accommodate **work-related conditions** — specifically calling out the use
of `payer-type` (i.e., the Coverage payer / source-of-payment typing
mechanism) as a potential signal for identifying when an encounter,
condition, or claim is work-related (e.g., workers' compensation). The
ticket was originally raised by Genny Luensman as part of
[FHIR-38593](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-38593) ("Needs to Accommodate
Work Information, Work-Related Conditions") and was split out so the
work-related-condition aspect could be addressed independently.
[FHIR-38593](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-38593) was resolved
*Persuasive with Modification* — sentence 1 was addressed by adding
narrative guidance pointing to and recommending the **Occupational Data
for Health (ODH) IG**, sentence 3 was addressed by determining the
`Organization` profile suffices for the surveillance-agent use case, and
sentence 2 (work-related conditions / payer-type) was deferred into this
ticket. After being closed *Considered for Future Use* on 2022-09-21
(vote 18-0-0), the ticket was reverted/re-opened on 2023-08-17 by Lynn
Laakso "post STU publication" so that USPHPL can give it further
analysis in a subsequent cycle.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the **US Public Health Profiles Library (USPHPL)**, add explicit
support for identifying work-related conditions by leveraging
**source-of-payment / payer-type** signals on `Coverage`. Concretely:

- Add narrative guidance to the USPHPL "Coverage" / "Encounter" /
  "Condition" sections describing how to identify work-relatedness via
  `Coverage.type` (e.g., NUBC source-of-payment codes such as `WC`
  Workers' Compensation, or HL7 v3 `ActCoverageTypeCode` analogues) and
  via `Coverage.payor` typing.
- Recommend (and bind, where appropriate) a value set for
  `Coverage.type` that includes the workers' compensation /
  work-related concepts, consistent with US Core Coverage and the
  US Public Health Profiles Library's existing payer modeling.
- Add a usage example showing a `Condition` linked through `Encounter →
  Account → Coverage` (or `Claim.insurance.coverage`) where
  `Coverage.type` indicates work-relatedness, plus a cross-reference to
  the **Occupational Data for Health (ODH) IG** for richer occupational
  context (industry/occupation, employment status, work classification).

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter asked for and is consistent with how
US Core and other US realm IGs already use `Coverage.type` to convey
workers' compensation. It also dovetails with the parent ticket
[FHIR-38593](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-38593)'s already-accepted
guidance to point readers at the ODH IG for occupational data, giving
USPHPL implementers a single coherent story: payer-type for "is this
work-related?", ODH for "what is the work?". No new resources or
elements are required — the change is profile-level constraints,
narrative, and example content within USPHPL.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than treating `payer-type` as the primary signal of
work-relatedness in USPHPL, defer the modeling to the **Occupational
Data for Health (ODH) IG** and add only a thin pointer from USPHPL.
Specifically:

- In USPHPL, add narrative that says work-relatedness of a condition
  SHOULD be conveyed through ODH-aligned profiles
  (`UsageContext`/extensions on `Condition` such as the work-relatedness
  observation pattern already used in occupational health, or an
  ODH-defined extension on `Condition`).
- Treat `Coverage.type = WC` as a *secondary* indicator that MAY be
  used when ODH structures are unavailable, but not the canonical
  source.
- Coordinate with the ODH IG owners to ensure a recommended
  Condition-level work-relatedness extension or profile exists; if it
  does not, raise a companion ticket against the ODH IG to add one.

#### Justification

`Coverage.type = WC` only flags that *the bill is being paid by a
workers' comp carrier*, not that the underlying clinical condition is
in fact work-related. Many work-related conditions are billed to
ordinary medical coverage (especially in screening, surveillance, or
delayed-onset scenarios), and conversely some workers'-comp claims
cover non-clinical services. Anchoring the semantics in ODH avoids
encoding a payer-billing artifact as a clinical truth and keeps
USPHPL's core scope tight. The trade-off versus Disposition A is added
implementer burden (two IGs instead of one) and a dependency on ODH
publishing a suitable Condition-level construct.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as **Not Persuasive** (or **Considered for Future Use**, restoring
the original 2022-09-21 disposition) on the basis that work-relatedness
of conditions is already addressed by the **Occupational Data for
Health (ODH) IG**, which the parent ticket
[FHIR-38593](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-38593) already directs
USPHPL implementers to via narrative guidance. No new USPHPL profile
changes are needed.

#### Justification

The accepted resolution of the parent ticket already added narrative
pointing readers to ODH for occupational context, which is the
HL7-endorsed home for work-related information. Re-encoding the same
concept inside USPHPL via `payer-type` risks duplicating (and
contradicting) ODH semantics, and conflates billing source with
clinical causation. If the workgroup judges the existing ODH pointer
sufficient, declining keeps USPHPL focused and avoids profile churn.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B reflects the substantive concern in the ticket — USPHPL
*should* let consumers identify work-related conditions — without
adopting `payer-type` as the canonical semantic, which would conflate
billing arrangements with clinical work-relatedness. It also honors the
direction the workgroup already set in the parent ticket
[FHIR-38593](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-38593): point at ODH for
occupational data. Disposition A is acceptable as a fallback if ODH
cannot, in the relevant timeframe, supply a Condition-level
work-relatedness construct that USPHPL can reference; in that case
USPHPL should cite `Coverage.type = WC` *and* clearly mark it as a
billing-derived hint. Disposition C under-serves implementers who
currently have no concrete USPHPL guidance for work-related conditions
beyond a generic "see ODH" pointer.
