## Summary

The submitter (Alex Goel) observes that the US Central Cancer Registry
Reporting (Cancer Reporting / MedMorph Cancer Content) IG's `PlanDefinition`,
together with the `cancer-core-reportability-codes` ValueSet, appears to drive
case-finding/triggering exclusively from `Condition` resources. In real
implementations, however, important cancer-relevant data — for example
pathology diagnoses — frequently arrive as `Observation` or
`DiagnosticReport` resources that may not be linked back to a `Condition`.
The ticket asks the workgroup to clarify whether the IG itself should
broaden the trigger / data-of-interest definitions to include
`Observation` and `DiagnosticReport`, or whether that responsibility should
be delegated to downstream IGs / implementers via CQL or FHIRPath
expressions inside the `PlanDefinition`. No cross-referenced Jira tickets,
Zulip threads, or GitHub items were returned by the tooling for this
ticket.

## Details

**Description:**

The PlanDefinition and [ValueSet Cancer Core Reportable Codes](https://hl7.org/fhir/us/central-cancer-registry-reporting/2022Jan/ValueSet-cancer-core-reportability-codes.html)
seem like they'll be focused on retrieving Conditions. Observations and
DiagnosticReports may not link to Condition, and these resources may
contain data relevant for the cancer reporting use case (e.g. pathology
diagnoses). Is this relevant or addressable in the MedMorph Cancer Content
IG, or should this be left up to other implementation guides or specific
implementations to define in the PlanDefinition what content is required
using CQL or FHIRPath?

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the Cancer Reporting IG's `PlanDefinition` so that triggering and
data-of-interest definitions include `Observation` and `DiagnosticReport`
in addition to `Condition`. Concretely:

- Add `PlanDefinition.action.trigger` entries of type
  `named-event` / `data-changed` for `Observation` (e.g., pathology
  results, tumor markers, staging observations) and `DiagnosticReport`
  (e.g., surgical pathology reports, molecular diagnostics).
- Bind the new triggers to a companion ValueSet (or extend
  `cancer-core-reportability-codes` and split into condition-code and
  observation/report-code intent) so that reportability codes for
  pathology and lab findings are first-class.
- In `PlanDefinition.action.input` / `output`, add `DataRequirement`
  entries for `Observation` and `DiagnosticReport` profiled appropriately
  (e.g., pathology Observation/DiagnosticReport profiles), so the
  reporting bundle assembly recognises them as primary case-finding
  artifacts rather than secondary context only reachable through a
  `Condition` link.

#### Justification

This directly resolves the gap the reporter identifies: in the cancer
domain, the canonical signal of reportability is frequently a pathology
report or staging observation, not a coded `Condition`. Restricting
triggering to `Condition` causes under-reporting in EHRs that do not
auto-promote pathology findings into problem lists. The MedMorph
backbone already supports multi-resource triggering, so the change is
mechanical at the IG profile / ValueSet level rather than requiring a
spec extension. It also aligns with how Central Cancer Registries
actually receive data today (path reports + abstracted observations).
The lynn_laakso 2024-05-31 comment explicitly re-opens the ticket *post
STU1 publication* and reverts the earlier "Considered for Future Use"
resolution, signalling that the workgroup is now willing to take this on
in the next normative cycle.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the IG's normative `PlanDefinition` and `cancer-core-reportability-codes`
ValueSet `Condition`-centric, but explicitly document and demonstrate the
extension pattern: provide a non-normative companion `PlanDefinition`
example (or appendix page) that shows how a registry / implementer can
add `Observation`-based and `DiagnosticReport`-based triggers and
`DataRequirement`s using CQL / FHIRPath. Add narrative guidance in the
"Triggering" / "Reportability" sections clarifying:

1. The base IG defines the *minimum* trigger set, anchored on
   `Condition`.
2. Implementers and downstream IGs (state-specific cancer registry IGs,
   pathology reporting IGs) are expected to extend the `PlanDefinition`
   with additional `Observation`/`DiagnosticReport` triggers via CQL or
   FHIRPath, and SHOULD do so when their EHR sources do not promote
   pathology to the problem list.
3. Provide one worked example (e.g., a pathology-driven trigger using a
   CAP eCC Observation or a US Core DiagnosticReport for Pathology).

#### Justification

This addresses the underlying need without forcing every cancer
registry profile / state IG to absorb a broader (and harder to validate)
core trigger set. It also avoids destabilising `cancer-core-reportability-codes`,
which is already in use by STU1 implementers. Trade-off vs. Disposition
A: it keeps the base IG smaller and more interoperable, but it shifts
the burden to downstream implementers and risks inconsistent coverage
across registries — which is precisely the interoperability problem the
reporter is flagging. It is, however, a defensible STU2 step that
preserves backward compatibility for STU1 conformant systems.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline to extend the IG's `PlanDefinition` triggers/data-requirements
beyond `Condition`. Document the position that the cancer reporting
trigger model is intentionally `Condition`-anchored, and that
`Observation`/`DiagnosticReport` data should be carried as supporting
content in the reporting bundle (already addressable via the existing
MedMorph data-extraction PlanDefinition pattern), not as triggers in
their own right.

#### Justification

Defensible only on the narrow grounds that triggering on every relevant
pathology Observation/DiagnosticReport could produce excessive duplicate
reports for the same case, and that registries already de-duplicate at
ingestion. Declining is hard to justify here: the reporter's scenario
(pathology diagnosis with no linked `Condition`) is realistic and well
documented in cancer surveillance literature, and the workgroup itself
has already reverted the earlier "Considered for Future Use" resolution
in 2024-05-31, indicating that "decline" no longer reflects workgroup
intent. This option should be carried only to make the trade-off
explicit during the vote.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the recommended starting point. The reporter raises a
legitimate gap, and Disposition C is effectively foreclosed by the
2024-05-31 re-open comment. However, jumping straight to Disposition A
(adding `Observation` and `DiagnosticReport` triggers and an expanded
ValueSet to the normative IG) is risky: it would change the core
trigger surface of an STU1-published IG, force re-binding of
`cancer-core-reportability-codes`, and require coordination with
state-level cancer registry IGs and pathology IGs that are still
stabilising. Disposition B captures the reporter's intent — making
pathology-driven case finding *a first-class, supported pattern* — while
keeping the normative trigger set stable for STU2 and giving the
workgroup a concrete extension example to point implementers at. The
workgroup should explicitly schedule Disposition A's broader trigger
expansion as a tracked item for the STU3 / normative cycle, so that
this is sequenced rather than dropped.
