## Summary

The reporter (CDC/NIOSH submitter Genevieve Luensman, with Amanda Deering and
Lori Fourquet on copy) is asking the Vital Records Common Profiles Library
(VRCL) to add an observation that captures a person's **current job**, in
addition to the existing **Usual Work** observation. The argument is that
"Usual Work" — modeled after the ODH
[`UsualWork`](https://hl7.org/fhir/us/odh/StructureDefinition-odh-UsualWork.html)
profile and intended for the longest-held type of work — is appropriate for
mortality (death records), but for natality the parents' **current** job(s)
are the relevant data point and can differ from their usual/longest-held job.
The submitter points at ODH's
[`PastOrPresentJob`](https://hl7.org/fhir/us/odh/StructureDefinition-odh-PastOrPresentJob.html)
profile as the model that already captures current job(s).

Context from related tickets reframes this request:

- [FHIR-43974](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43974) — the companion
  request filed against **US Birth and Fetal Death Reporting (BFDR)** asking
  for the parents' current job per ODH. Same reporter, same ask, but
  correctly scoped at BFDR.
- [FHIR-43975](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43975) — sibling request to
  carry parents' current job into BFDR jurisdiction reports.
- [FHIR-46273](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-46273) — **Published,
  Persuasive**: a later decision moved the Usual Work profile out of VRCL
  back to VRDR, on the explicit grounds that mortality records "Usual Work"
  while natality records "Present Work", so the two should not be shared via
  VRCL. Present Work was to be created in BFDR.

Read together, FHIR-43978 is the VRCL-scoped instance of a need that the
workgroup has since resolved by **moving the work-history profiles out of
VRCL**: Usual Work → VRDR, Present Work → BFDR. The "current job"
observation requested here therefore belongs in BFDR (covered by
FHIR-43974), not in VRCL.

The ticket was originally resolved 2024-02-22 as *Considered for Future
Use* (vote 22-0-2; Cindy Bush / NCHS noted that occupation/industry are not
collected on facility/patient worksheets and that NVSS CoP input was needed
for a future STU). Lynn Laakso re-opened it post-STU2 publication
(2024-10-11) so it would be available for the next ballot cycle.

## Details

**Description:**

Usual work is used for 'regular' death records and refers to the person's
longest-held type of work, which can be different from their current job.
The mother's and father's work information should include their current
job(s), which would be a different observation. See
[ODH Past or Present Job profile](https://hl7.org/fhir/us/odh/StructureDefinition-odh-PastOrPresentJob.html)
(vs the
[ODH Usual profile](https://hl7.org/fhir/us/odh/StructureDefinition-odh-UsualWork.html)).

Please include Amanda Deering and Lori Fourquet on all communications.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a new observation profile to **VRCL** that models a person's *current
job(s)*, derived from
[`odh-PastOrPresentJob`](https://hl7.org/fhir/us/odh/StructureDefinition-odh-PastOrPresentJob.html)
(use the "present" semantics: `Observation.effective[x]` open-ended /
ongoing, with current employer + occupation + industry). Apply it to the
mother and father parent records used by BFDR and any other VRCL consumer
that needs a parent's *current* work, alongside the existing VRCL
Usual-Work-derived observation. Bind occupation/industry to the same code
systems already used by the VRCL Usual-Work observation, with a note to
re-evaluate once FHIR-43977 lands the NIOSH/ONC vocabulary update.

#### Justification

This is the most literal reading of the submitter's request and would let
both mortality (Usual Work) and natality (current job) be served from a
single common profiles library, as Cindy Bush's 2024 comment described —
"inter-jurisdictional elements" that states may choose to share. It also
keeps parity with ODH's two-profile model (UsualWork + PastOrPresentJob)
that the submitter is explicitly aligning to.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — Resolve in BFDR, not VRCL (Persuasive with Modification)

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-43978 in VRCL as **Persuasive with Modification**, on the
ground that the actual home for a "parents' current job" observation is
**US Birth and Fetal Death Reporting (BFDR)**, not VRCL. Direct the
substantive design work to FHIR-43974 (and its jurisdiction-report
counterpart FHIR-43975), where a BFDR-specific *Present Work* profile —
modeled on `odh-PastOrPresentJob` — is added and referenced from the
Mother and Father parent observations / patient extensions used by BFDR.
Leave VRCL with only what is genuinely shared across mortality and
natality.

#### Justification

[FHIR-46273](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-46273) (Published,
Persuasive) has already established the workgroup's position: Usual Work
is mortality-only and is being moved to VRDR; Present Work is natality and
is to be created in BFDR; VRCL should not host either because the two use
cases legitimately diverge. Adding a "current job" observation to VRCL
under Disposition A would re-introduce exactly the cross-use-case coupling
that FHIR-46273 was filed to remove. Pointing this ticket at FHIR-43974
keeps it in scope for the next BFDR ballot cycle (which is why Lynn
Laakso re-opened it in October 2024) without contradicting an already
published disposition. It also sidesteps the open vocabulary question
in FHIR-43977 by letting BFDR pick the binding once.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (Not Persuasive — already addressed by FHIR-46273)

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-43978 as **Not Persuasive** on the basis that the request was
substantively addressed by [FHIR-46273](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-46273):
the Usual / Present Work profiles have been removed from VRCL by design,
so VRCL is no longer the right artifact in which to add a "current job"
observation. The natality use case is already represented by the open
BFDR-scoped tickets FHIR-43974 / FHIR-43975, which the submitter has
filed.

#### Justification

Adding another work-history observation to VRCL after FHIR-46273
explicitly removed work-history from VRCL would be net-new complication,
and the original submitter's underlying need (parents' current job in
birth records) is already tracked against the correct IG. The 22-0-2
"Considered for Future Use" outcome from 2024-02-22 reflected the same
view from NCHS — current job is not on the facility/patient worksheets
that produce the birth certificate, so there is no jurisdictional source
data to populate it today. Declining here keeps the design coherent with
FHIR-46273 without losing the request, since FHIR-43974/43975 remain
open.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The workgroup has already decided, in the **Published / Persuasive**
disposition of [FHIR-46273](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-46273), that
work-history profiles do not live in VRCL: Usual Work belongs in VRDR and
Present Work belongs in BFDR. Disposition A would walk that decision back
and re-couple mortality and natality through VRCL, which is exactly what
FHIR-46273 was filed to stop. Disposition C is defensible but loses the
signal — the submitter's underlying ask (parents' *current* job in birth
records) is real and live, just mis-scoped to VRCL. Disposition B
preserves the FHIR-46273 architecture, honors Lynn Laakso's October 2024
re-open by keeping the request in flight for the next cycle, and routes
the substantive work to FHIR-43974 (and the jurisdiction-report companion
FHIR-43975) where BFDR can add a Present-Work observation modeled on
`odh-PastOrPresentJob` and pick the occupation/industry binding once
FHIR-43977's vocabulary update is settled. That is the disposition most
consistent with both the submitter's intent and the workgroup's standing
architectural choice.
