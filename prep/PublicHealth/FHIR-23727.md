## Summary

Reporter Craig Newman is reviewing the US Healthcare Associated Infections
(HAI-LTCF) FHIR Implementation Guide and is confused by the contents of the
"Other" tab, which is labeled with a heading suggesting it lists "other
resources". The label implies the entries are FHIR resources (or at least
profiles), but on inspection the items are heterogeneous — some appear to be
mappings, others look like examples, and others still seem to be neither. The
ticket asks the Public Health work group to clarify what these "other
resources" actually are, either by relabeling/regrouping the entries on that
tab or by adding explanatory narrative so readers understand what each link
represents and why it lives under "Other".

No cross-referenced Jira tickets, GitHub items, or substantive Zulip
discussions were surfaced for this ticket.

## Details

## Proposed Dispositions

Because this is a documentation/IG-navigation clarification request against
a specific IG (HAI-LTCF) and no GitHub cross-references were surfaced, the
dispositions below are framed against that IG's authoring sources
(typically `input/` and `input/pagecontent/` in an HL7 IG repository such
as `HL7/HAI-LTCF`). If/when this ticket is acted on, a `repo-analysis`
briefing for `HL7/HAI-LTCF` should be generated to confirm the exact
authoring paths.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the HAI-LTCF IG so the "Other" tab clearly explains what each
listed item is. Concretely:

1. In the IG's TOC/menu configuration (typically `input/menu.xml` or the
   `sushi-config.yaml` `menu` block, depending on how this IG is built),
   either rename the "Other" tab to a more accurate label (e.g.,
   "Supporting Artifacts" or "Mappings, Examples & Other Artifacts") or
   split it into per-artifact-kind tabs ("Mappings", "Examples",
   "Supporting Artifacts").
2. Add an introductory page (e.g.,
   `input/pagecontent/other-artifacts.md`) that lists each entry under
   the tab and explains, per entry, whether it is an example, a
   mapping, a supporting CodeSystem/ValueSet, a Questionnaire used as
   reference content, etc.
3. Where the IG publisher is auto-generating the "Other" tab from
   non-conformance resources, override the title/description metadata
   on those resources (e.g., `^title`, `^description` on the FSH
   instance / resource) so that each link in the tab is
   self-describing.

#### Justification

The complaint is concrete and verifiable: the label "other resources"
incorrectly implies StructureDefinitions/profiles, when the entries are a
heterogeneous mix. Renaming the tab and providing explanatory narrative
is consistent with the IG Publisher's intent that each artifact has a
human-readable title and description, and with the general principle
that an IG should be navigable by its target audience (public-health
implementers) without insider knowledge of how IG Publisher buckets
artifacts. This is a low-risk, documentation-only change with no
normative impact on the HAI-LTCF profiles, ValueSets, or examples.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than reorganizing the "Other" tab in HAI-LTCF in isolation,
address the underlying problem upstream by:

1. Requesting (via FHIR-I / IG Publisher) that the publisher's default
   "Other" bucket be renamed to something more accurate (e.g., "Other
   Artifacts") and/or auto-emit per-entry type badges (Example, Mapping,
   Supporting Resource).
2. In the meantime, add only a short introductory paragraph to the
   HAI-LTCF "Other" tab page (`input/pagecontent/`) explaining that the
   entries are a mix of examples, mappings, and supporting resources,
   without renaming the tab or restructuring the menu.

#### Justification

The "Other" tab's confusing labeling is not unique to HAI-LTCF — every
HL7 IG built with the IG Publisher inherits the same default bucket.
Fixing it once in the publisher benefits all IGs, while a HAI-LTCF-only
fix risks divergence from sibling IGs and may be re-broken whenever
the IG is republished against a newer publisher version. The minimal
local clarification keeps reader confusion bounded without committing
the work group to maintaining custom menu structure.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket "Not Persuasive" on the basis that the "Other" tab is a
standard, publisher-generated navigation element common to all HL7 FHIR
IGs, and that its contents are already individually labeled by their
underlying resource metadata. Reviewers seeking specific artifact types
should use the dedicated tabs (Profiles, Extensions, ValueSets,
CodeSystems, Examples) for those types.

#### Justification

The "Other" tab is a known IG Publisher convention; readers familiar
with HL7 IGs understand it as a catch-all. No normative content is
incorrect. The fix would be cosmetic and would create maintenance drift
relative to other Public Health IGs (Occupational Data, Vital Records,
etc.) that follow the same pattern.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter is an experienced FHIR implementer (Craig Newman) and was
still confused by the tab's contents — that is strong evidence that the
default labeling is failing the IG's actual audience, not just a
newcomer. The change is purely editorial: a tab rename plus an
introductory page in `input/pagecontent/` (and possibly a few `^title` /
`^description` overrides on the underlying instances) carries no
normative risk and no breaking-change concern. Disposition B's "fix it
in the IG Publisher" instinct is correct in the long run, but it should
not block a small, local clarification in HAI-LTCF that costs the work
group very little to ship and immediately improves the reading
experience. Disposition C understates the cost of leaving a misleading
label in place in a public-health IG that is read by implementers
outside the HL7 inner circle.
