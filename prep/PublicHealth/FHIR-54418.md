## Summary

The submitter, writing on behalf of a NAACCR (North American Association
of Central Cancer Registries) community member, raises a concern with the
**Cancer (Primary) Histology/Behavior** data element in the US Central
Cancer Registry Reporting Content IG. They agree that the element is
appropriately required and cannot be absent, but object to the fact that
the requirement can be satisfied with **uncoded free text**. Their
position is that uncoded text imposes additional burden on Central
Cancer Registries (CCRs) — particularly when the text is not clinically
useful — and that the IG should require **discrete, coded** information
for the cancer diagnosis (primary site, histology, behavior, and
diagnosis date). They draw a contrast with the Cancer (Primary) Body
Site element, which is required but allowed to carry a `dataAbsentReason`,
and argue that EHRs should be expected to capture this information in a
structured form at least once by the provider rather than relying on
default-filled or free-text values. Without this tightening, the
submitter does not expect the IG to deliver meaningful improvement over
prior efforts at extracting useful data from EHRs.

The ticket has been triaged and resolved as **Considered – No action
required**, with the working group acknowledging the concern but
declining to tighten the binding now, citing the risk that stricter
requirements would prevent reports from reaching registries at all
during the early adoption window.

No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were
returned for this ticket.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Tighten the **Cancer (Primary) Histology/Behavior** data element in the
US Central Cancer Registry Reporting Content IG so that the requirement
can only be satisfied by a **coded** value drawn from the IG-bound
terminology (e.g., ICD-O-3 morphology codes for histology, with the
behavior digit) rather than free text. Concretely:

- On the underlying profile, change the `Condition` (or `Observation`)
  element used to carry histology/behavior so that:
  - `code.coding` is `1..*` and `mustSupport = true`,
  - `code.text` alone is no longer sufficient to satisfy the element,
  - the binding strength is `required` (or `extensible` with a
    documented narrow value set) against the existing histology/behavior
    value set,
  - `dataAbsentReason` is **not** permitted on this element (mirroring
    the submitter's framing that this element "cannot be absent"), in
    contrast to Cancer (Primary) Body Site which retains its current
    allowance.
- Add an invariant (e.g., `ccrr-hist-1`) along the lines of
  `code.coding.exists() and code.coding.all(system.exists() and code.exists())`
  with severity `error`, and update narrative guidance and examples to
  show only coded submissions.
- Apply the same expectation to companion elements identified by the
  submitter — primary site, behavior, and diagnosis date — wherever
  free text currently satisfies the constraint.

#### Justification

This directly addresses the concern raised: registries spend significant
effort interpreting non-discrete narrative, and a `must-support` coded
binding is the standard FHIR mechanism for ensuring downstream systems
receive computable data. It is also consistent with how other public
health reporting IGs (e.g., eCR, Cancer Pathology Data Sharing) handle
diagnosis-bearing elements, where coded values are required and free
text is supplemental. Aligning histology/behavior with that pattern
reduces ambiguity for implementers and gives CCRs the discrete data
they need to actually realise the IG's stated value.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the element's cardinality and the ability to send text, but
**raise the floor** on data quality through layered guidance rather than
a hard breaking change:

- Retain `code.coding 0..*` and `code.text 0..1` on the profile, but
  mark `code.coding` as `mustSupport = true` and add normative
  guidance: "Senders SHALL populate `code.coding` with a value from
  [histology/behavior value set] whenever the information is recorded
  in the EHR in coded form. `code.text` alone is permitted only when
  no coded equivalent exists in the source system."
- Introduce a `warning`-severity invariant
  (`code.coding.exists() or extension('uncoded-rationale').exists()`)
  that flags text-only submissions and require senders to document a
  reason via a small `uncoded-rationale` extension (codes such as
  `no-coded-source`, `legacy-record`, `provider-narrative-only`).
- Add a new conformance/quality reporting expectation in the IG
  narrative: registries MAY reject or downgrade submissions whose
  histology/behavior is text-only without an `uncoded-rationale`,
  signalling intent to converge on the Disposition A behaviour in a
  future version.
- File a companion forward-looking ticket so the next ballot cycle can
  upgrade the warning to an error once EHR adoption telemetry supports
  it.

#### Justification

This honours the working group's resolution rationale — that tightening
the binding today risks registries receiving no report at all from
EHRs not yet aligned on the terminology — while still giving CCRs a
mechanism to distinguish "EHR genuinely had no code" from "EHR was
lazy". The `uncoded-rationale` extension creates a measurable signal
that lets the working group, CDC, and NAACCR observe real-world
behaviour and justify a future hard requirement with data, which is
explicitly what the existing resolution proposes to do informally.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Maintain the current resolution: **Considered – No action required.**
Keep the existing must-support / `code.text`-acceptable definition of
Cancer (Primary) Histology/Behavior in the US Central Cancer Registry
Reporting Content IG, and document the working group's rationale (risk
of suppressing reports during early adoption, planned re-evaluation in
a future version, complementary CDC-side NLP investments) in the IG's
known issues / future considerations section so the concern is on the
record.

#### Justification

The IG is in an early adoption phase where the larger public health
risk is **non-reporting** rather than under-coded reporting: a registry
can apply NLP, manual abstraction, or follow-up to a free-text
histology, but it cannot recover information that was never sent.
Tightening the binding now would also be a breaking change against any
implementer already conforming to the published profile, which is hard
to justify when the IG itself commits to revisiting the requirement
once EHR terminology alignment matures. The submitter's underlying
concern is real but is being managed through the planned future
enhancement and the CDC NLP track noted in the resolution.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition C reflects the working group's already-recorded resolution
and is defensible, but it leaves the submitter's concern essentially
unanswered and offers CCRs no new lever between "everything is great"
and "we get unusable text". Disposition A is the right long-term shape
but is a breaking change against an IG that is still trying to land
broad EHR adoption, which is exactly the risk the resolution
identifies. Disposition B is the strongest middle ground: it preserves
the current "do not block reports" stance the working group has
committed to, but raises the floor by making coded data must-support,
introducing a warning-level invariant, and capturing *why* a text-only
submission happened via an `uncoded-rationale` extension. That gives
CDC and NAACCR the empirical basis they say they want for a future
tightening, and it directly answers the submitter's concern that
default-filled or non-useful text should not be silently acceptable.
The working group can keep the existing "Considered – No action
required" outcome on this specific ballot comment while opening a
follow-on ticket to land the Disposition B mechanics in the next IG
cycle.
