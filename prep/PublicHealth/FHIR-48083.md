## Summary

The reporter proposes a small wording change to the **Data Aggregator** actor
definition in the NHSN dQM Reporting IG. The current sentence reads:

> "When FHIR enabled, a Data Aggregator acts as a US Core Server when playing
> the role of a Data Source after aggregation."

The reporter suggests rephrasing it as:

> "When a FHIR-enabled Data Aggregator acts as a US Core Server, it plays the
> role of a Data Source after aggregation."

The change is purely editorial — it does not alter the meaning, conformance
expectations, or the relationship between the Data Aggregator, US Core Server
conformance, and the Data Source role. The intent is to make the sentence read
more naturally by promoting "FHIR-enabled" to a modifier on "Data Aggregator"
and restructuring the clause order.

The ticket has been resolved as **Not Persuasive**, with the resolution note
that "This content has moved to the US SAFR IG" — implying that the affected
text is no longer maintained in the NHSN dQM Reporting IG and any equivalent
edit would need to be raised against the US SAFR IG instead.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the reporter's exact wording change to the Data Aggregator actor
definition. Replace:

> "When FHIR enabled, a Data Aggregator acts as a US Core Server when playing
> the role of a Data Source after aggregation."

with:

> "When a FHIR-enabled Data Aggregator acts as a US Core Server, it plays the
> role of a Data Source after aggregation."

Because the affected content has moved to the US SAFR IG (per the existing
resolution note), the edit would be made there, in whichever page now defines
the Data Aggregator actor, rather than in the NHSN dQM Reporting IG.

#### Justification

The change is purely editorial and improves readability by avoiding the
awkward "When FHIR enabled, … when playing the role …" double-`when`
construction. It introduces no normative change: the same conditions
(FHIR-enabled, acting as a US Core Server) still imply the same role (Data
Source after aggregation). Editorial improvements in actor descriptions are
low-risk and reduce reader confusion, which is particularly valuable in
public health reporting IGs where actor distinctions drive conformance
expectations.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt a more explicit two-sentence rewrite that separates the conformance
claim from the role assignment, e.g.:

> "**Data Aggregator:** A FHIR-enabled Data Aggregator SHALL conform to the
> US Core Server capability statement. When acting in this capacity after
> aggregation, it plays the role of a Data Source."

Apply this in the US SAFR IG (the current home of the content) rather than
the NHSN dQM Reporting IG.

#### Justification

The reporter's suggested wording, while clearer than the current text, still
leaves the conformance assertion ("acts as a US Core Server") implicit and
conditional. Splitting the sentence makes the conformance requirement
explicit (using SHALL) and cleanly separates *what the actor must conform to*
from *what role it plays*. This is more useful to implementers reading actor
definitions to determine which CapabilityStatements apply. Trade-off vs.
Disposition A: it is a larger edit and introduces a normative SHALL where
the current text only describes behavior, so it should only be adopted if
the workgroup confirms the conformance intent.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Maintain the existing "Not Persuasive" resolution. The content has moved out
of the NHSN dQM Reporting IG to the US SAFR IG, so there is nothing to
change in the originally-targeted specification. If the reporter still wants
the wording improved, they should raise a fresh ticket against the US SAFR
IG with the same proposed text.

#### Justification

This ticket is filed against the NHSN dQM Reporting IG, but the affected
sentence is no longer part of that IG. Acting on it here would have no
effect on any published artifact in the NHSN dQM IG, and editing the US
SAFR IG on the basis of a ticket that names a different specification
bypasses that workgroup's normal triage process. The change is also purely
editorial with no semantic impact, so there is no urgency that would
justify a cross-IG fix-up. Declining and pointing the reporter to the
correct IG is the cleanest disposition and matches the existing resolution.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** C

The ticket has already been resolved as Not Persuasive precisely because
the content has migrated to the US SAFR IG. The proposed change, while a
genuine readability improvement, is editorial-only and does not alter any
conformance expectation, so there is no implementer harm in leaving the
NHSN dQM Reporting IG untouched and routing the edit through the US SAFR
IG's own triage. Re-opening this ticket to make a downstream edit in a
different IG would set a confusing precedent for ticket scope. If the
workgroup wants the wording fixed, the right next step is a new ticket
against US SAFR carrying either Disposition A's minimal rewrite (low-risk,
preserves intent) or Disposition B's explicit SHALL form (only if the
conformance intent is confirmed).
