## Summary

The submitter, Rick Geimer, asks the NHSN dQM Reporting IG authoring team to
review and update (or comment out) the "Birth Sex" field present in the four
published Patient example instances (`patient-example-ach-ach-pass1`,
`patient-example-ach-ach-pass2`, `patient-example-ach-ach-pass3`, and
`patient-example-sameresultdiffday`). The motivation cited in the ticket
title is alignment with a recent U.S. Executive Order touching on Birth Sex
and Gender Identity. The change is scoped strictly to example instances
(not to the underlying Patient profile or terminology bindings) and is a
documentation/example-content adjustment rather than a structural change.
The ticket has already been triaged by Public Health with a "Persuasive"
resolution and a stated intent to "remove the requested fields." There are
no cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, GitHub PRs/issues, or
Confluence pages associated with this ticket in the augury index.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the NHSN dQM Reporting IG (HL7/nhsn-dqm), edit the four named Patient
example instances —

- `patient-example-ach-ach-pass1` (LOS/MEN)
- `patient-example-ach-ach-pass2` (Hypoglycemia)
- `patient-example-ach-ach-pass3` (CDI/HOB)
- `patient-example-sameresultdiffday` (Hypoglycemia)

— and remove the "Birth Sex" entry. Concretely, delete the
`Patient.extension` entry whose `url` is
`http://hl7.org/fhir/us-core/StructureDefinition/us-core-birthsex` (the
US Core Birth Sex extension) from each example. Leave the underlying
Patient profile, its differential, must-support flags, and any
constraints unchanged — the change is example-only. Rebuild the IG and
verify that the four example pages no longer render a "Birth Sex" row.
This matches the resolution already recorded on the ticket
("Will remove the requested fields") and the Persuasive disposition.

#### Justification

The request is narrow, factual, and has already been triaged Persuasive
by the Public Health work group with a stated intent to remove the
fields. Examples in an IG are illustrative only — removing a populated
extension from an example does not alter conformance, value-set
bindings, must-support, or implementer obligations, so there is no
downstream breaking impact for existing implementers. Aligning published
sample data with current U.S. federal guidance on Birth Sex / Gender
Identity reduces the risk that implementers misread the examples as a
recommendation, while preserving the IG's structural ability to carry
the data when a jurisdictional context still requires it.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than silently removing the Birth Sex extension from the four
examples, replace each occurrence with a brief inline note in the
example narrative (and/or an IG-level note on the Patient page)
explaining that NHSN dQM does not require Birth Sex and that the
examples intentionally omit it, while keeping the underlying profile's
ability to carry US Core Birth Sex unchanged for implementers in
jurisdictions that still capture it. Operationally: remove the
`us-core-birthsex` extension from the four example resources (same as
Disposition A), and add a short paragraph either on each example page
or once on the Patient profile intro page noting the rationale and
linking to the US Core Birth Sex extension for implementers who need it.

#### Justification

This approach achieves the same example-content outcome the ticket asks
for, but adds a one-time documentation note so that future readers (and
future workgroup members revisiting these examples) understand *why* the
field is absent. It avoids the risk that someone later "fixes" the
examples by re-adding Birth Sex without context, and it makes the IG
explicit about how it relates to US Core's optional extension. The
trade-off vs. Disposition A is a small additional documentation edit
and the need to wordsmith a politically sensitive note.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket without changing the examples, on the basis that the
NHSN dQM IG examples are illustrative of the *data shape*, not of any
particular jurisdiction's data-collection policy, and that the US Core
Birth Sex extension is itself an optional element drawn from US Core.
Implementers are expected to populate or omit the extension based on
their own legal and policy environment.

#### Justification

This would defend the position that example content should not track
short-cycle policy shifts (executive orders are subject to change with
each administration), and that the IG's neutrality is best preserved by
leaving examples as-is. However, this position is hard to sustain when
the workgroup has already triaged the ticket as Persuasive with a stated
intent to remove the fields, and when the cost of complying is trivial
(four example edits).

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The work group has already triaged this ticket as Persuasive and
recorded a resolution to "remove the requested fields," and the change
itself is an example-only, non-conformance edit that costs almost
nothing to implement and ship. Disposition B is reasonable and worth
considering as a low-cost addition (a short note on the Patient page
explaining why the examples don't carry Birth Sex), but it should not
block Disposition A — the cleanest path is to land the four example
edits per the existing resolution, and optionally fold in B's
explanatory note in the same PR. Disposition C contradicts the
work group's existing triage and offers no implementer benefit
proportional to the friction of declining a Persuasive request.
