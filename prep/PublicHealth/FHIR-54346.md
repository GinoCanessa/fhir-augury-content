## Summary

Craig Newman is requesting clarifications to the "Relevant Time Period for
Retrieving Data" section of the Electronic Case Reporting (eCR) FHIR
Implementation Guide. The current text gives senders a window for selecting
encounter, procedure, and patient-state data to include in an eCR, but the
reporter argues several of its terms and assumptions are ambiguous and could
lead to inconsistent implementations. Specifically, the ticket asks the
Public Health work group to (1) clarify whether every eCR must be tied to a
"relevant encounter" or whether reports can be independent of an encounter
(e.g., lab-triggered reports, multi-occurrence orders, or procedures
ordered in one encounter but performed in another), (2) add explanatory
narrative around how to choose the relevant encounter in those cases,
(3) define what "year" means in the time-period rules (calendar year vs.
365 days from the trigger date), and (4) clarify what "pregnancy" means
in this context (current pregnancy, intent to become pregnant, or any
pregnancy-related state during the look-back window — including how to
handle multiple states across the year). No Jira, Zulip, or GitHub
cross-references were surfaced for this ticket, so the request stands on
its own and has not yet attracted public discussion in the indexed
sources.

## Details

**Description:**

> The "Relevant Time Period for Retrieving Data" section is useful, but
> may benefit from from additional clarifications:
>
> - Will every eCR have a relevant encounter or can there be a report
>   independent of an encounter? Where the report was triggered by a lab
>   results, should it be the ordering encounter that is relevant? What
>   if the order was for multiple occurrences of the test and it's only
>   the latest one (maybe some time since the original order) that is
>   triggering? If a procedure was ordered at one encounter but performed
>   on another encounter, which should be sent?
> - Adding some explanatory narrative would be helpful
> - Please define meaning of "year". Is that the calendar year or 365
>   days from the day of the report being triggered?
> - What is meant by "pregnancy"? Is that the patient was pregnant?
>   Intended to become pregnant? What if over the course of the year she
>   had multiple states?

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Revise the "Relevant Time Period for Retrieving Data" page in the eCR
FHIR IG (Public Health work group) to add explicit narrative and
normative-language fixes addressing all four reporter questions:

1. **Encounter relevance.** Add a sub-section "Reports without a
   relevant encounter" stating that an eICR MAY be produced without an
   `Encounter` reference (e.g., reportable lab result returned outside
   any in-person visit). When an order-driven trigger fires, the
   relevant `Encounter` is the encounter at which the *triggering*
   order/observation occurred (the most recent occurrence whose
   result satisfies the trigger), not the original ordering encounter
   when they differ. For procedures, send the encounter in which the
   procedure was *performed*; if only an order exists, send the
   ordering encounter and flag the procedure as `not-done`/`preparation`
   per the Procedure status profile.
2. **Explanatory narrative.** Add worked examples (lab-triggered with
   no visit, standing-order lab, procedure ordered vs. performed in
   different encounters) immediately after the rules table.
3. **Definition of "year".** Define "year" as **365 days ending at the
   trigger event timestamp** (a rolling look-back window), not a
   calendar year. State this once at the top of the section and use a
   consistent phrase ("365-day look-back window") throughout.
4. **Definition of "pregnancy".** Define "pregnancy" as *any*
   `Observation`/`Condition` indicating the patient was pregnant
   (active pregnancy status) at any point during the 365-day window;
   include all distinct pregnancy episodes that fall within the
   window. Exclude "intent to become pregnant". Cite the value set
   used for pregnancy status.

These edits are content-only changes to the IG narrative pages — no
StructureDefinition, profile, or value-set changes are required.

#### Justification

The reporter is the eCR specification lead (Craig Newman) and the label
`eCR_DISCUSS_WITH_SME` indicates the work group already plans to confirm
the answers with subject-matter experts before publication. All four
questions are real interoperability risks: without a fixed definition of
"year" and "pregnancy", senders will produce eICR bundles with
materially different content, and consumers (PHAs) will not be able to
rely on the look-back semantics. Adding the encounter-vs-no-encounter
guidance also aligns with how lab-triggered reporting is already known
to operate in production. These are clarifications to existing intent,
not new requirements, so the change is low-risk and substantive.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Split the ticket into two tracks and resolve each differently:

- **Track 1 — Editorial (accept now):** Apply only the unambiguous
  editorial fixes — define "year" as a 365-day rolling window ending
  at the trigger event, and clarify that an eICR MAY exist without a
  relevant `Encounter` (lab-triggered case). Land these in the next
  IG publication.
- **Track 2 — SME review (defer):** Hold the encounter-selection rules
  for ordered-vs-performed procedures, multi-occurrence orders, and
  the precise definition of "pregnancy" for SME review (per the
  `eCR_DISCUSS_WITH_SME` label). Capture the SME outcomes as a
  follow-up ticket and, if the answers warrant value-set or profile
  changes, address them there rather than in narrative alone.

#### Justification

Disposition A bundles low-risk editorial clarifications with
semantically loaded decisions (which encounter "counts" when an order
and performance straddle visits; what counts as "pregnancy"). Those
loaded decisions can affect downstream public-health analytics and may
require value-set selection or new conformance statements, which
warrants explicit SME sign-off rather than being resolved by IG editors.
Splitting the ticket lets the Public Health WG ship the obvious
clarifications quickly while preserving a paper trail for the harder
questions, and avoids re-opening this section repeatedly if the SME
discussion changes the encounter-selection guidance.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Not Persuasive** on the grounds that the existing
"Relevant Time Period for Retrieving Data" section is intentionally
guidance, that implementers are expected to apply clinical judgment
when selecting the relevant encounter, and that "year" and "pregnancy"
have generally understood meanings in clinical reporting that do not
require IG-level definition.

#### Justification

This is the weakest of the three dispositions. It is defensible only if
the work group concludes that prescribing precise look-back semantics
would over-constrain implementers who already make these choices based
on local data availability. However, declining leaves the spec exposed
to exactly the inconsistency the reporter — who is the eCR SME — is
warning about, and the ticket is already labelled for SME discussion,
which signals that the WG itself does not view it as out of scope.
Recorded for completeness only.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B best matches the ticket's actual posture. The reporter is
the eCR lead and has already tagged the ticket `eCR_DISCUSS_WITH_SME`,
which is the work group's own signal that the harder questions
(encounter selection across order/performance boundaries, definition of
"pregnancy") need SME input before being written into normative
guidance. At the same time, two of the four asks — defining "year" as a
rolling 365-day window and explicitly allowing eICRs without a relevant
encounter — are pure editorial clarifications that the work group can
land immediately with high confidence and zero conformance impact.
Splitting the ticket lets the WG deliver immediate value to
implementers, keeps the SME-bound questions visible and tracked, and
avoids the risk of re-opening this section in a later cycle if SME
review changes the encounter-selection rules. Disposition A is a close
second and would be the right choice if the SME review can be completed
inside the current ballot cycle; Disposition C is not recommended given
the reporter is the SME and the WG has already flagged the ticket for
substantive discussion.
