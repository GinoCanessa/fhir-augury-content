## Summary

Saul Kravitz (Public Health WG) is asking the Vital Records Common
Profiles Library (VRCL) to **eliminate the `partialDateTime` extension
from `Patient.birthDate`** in a future version of the IG. He argues
that the extension adds substantial complexity and is not aligned with
the vital-records use case, which actually *requires* a complete
birth date together with a `birthTime`. The ticket notes that the
VRCL `usage.html` page already declares the `partialDateTime` extension
deprecated and explains the valid uses of `birthDate`/`birthTime`, so
the request here is to follow through on that deprecation by removing
the extension from `Patient.birthDate` outright.

The cross-reference index returned no formally linked Jira tickets,
Zulip threads, or GitHub items for FHIR-50422. The cross-references
that do exist are FHIR artifact mentions of `Patient.birthDate` /
`Patient.birthdate`. However, several keyword-related Vital
Records / Patient Administration tickets provide important context:
[FHIR-44596](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-44596) (Persuasive)
clarified usage of `PartialDate` / `PartialDateTime` in VRCL;
[FHIR-41647](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-41647) (Persuasive,
MDI) is the FHIR-I challenge that drove MDI to drop its own partial
date-time extension and rely on the VRDR/VRCL one; and
[FHIR-43302](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43302) (Applied) extended
the standard `patient-birthTime` extension to `Person`, `RelatedPerson`,
and `Practitioner`, which is the canonical vehicle for representing
birth time without resorting to `partialDateTime`. Together these
indicate a clear direction in the broader community: prefer the core
`patient-birthTime` extension (plus a fully populated `birthDate`)
over `partialDateTime` for vital-records use.

### [FHIR-44596: Clarify Further use of PartialDate and PartialDateTime](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-44596)

A prior VRCL ticket (Public Health, **Resolved Persuasive**, 2024-01)
that retained the `partialDateTime` mechanism but added narrative
guidance about how it should be used — specifically that when both
`value` and `_value` (extension) are provided and inconsistent,
implementers should treat the extension as authoritative and keep the
two in sync. This is the most direct predecessor to FHIR-50422: it
chose to *clarify* rather than *eliminate* the extension. FHIR-50422
asks the WG to take the next step and remove it from
`Patient.birthDate` altogether.

Reporter: Saul Kravitz · Assignee: Unassigned

### [FHIR-41647: Please drop or come chat w/ FHIR-I about partial date-time](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-41647)

A FHIR-I-driven challenge (MDI IG, **Resolved Persuasive**, 2023-08)
that questioned the very existence of a `partialDateTime` extension
because the underlying `date`/`dateTime` data type already permits
`YYYY`, `YYYY-MM`, and `YYYY-MM-DD`. The resolution removed MDI's own
extension, redirected MDI to the VRDR `PartialDateTime` definition,
and added guidance that a `value` must be sent alongside the extension
whenever possible (with a Data Absent Reason for unknown components).
Lloyd McKenzie's comments lay out the FHIR-I conditions for the
extension to progress past FMM 2 (consolidate on a single extension,
require `value` when year is known, never use the extension when the
date is fully known, and drop the all-mandatory-component requirement).
Highly relevant: it shows external pressure from the FHIR
infrastructure WG to simplify or eliminate this mechanism.

Reporter: Lloyd McKenzie · Commenters: JIRAUSER17453, mckenzie

### [FHIR-43302: Allow Person Context for patient-birthTime extension](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43302)

Patient Administration ticket (FHIR Extensions Pack, **Applied**,
2024-12) that broadened the `patient-birthTime` extension's context
to include `Patient`, `RelatedPerson`, `Person`, and `Practitioner`.
Confirms that the core `patient-birthTime` extension is the
community-endorsed vehicle for carrying a precise time of birth and
is actively being maintained. Important because Disposition A's
proposal — drop `partialDateTime` and rely on `Patient.birthDate` +
`patient-birthTime` — depends on this extension being available and
fit-for-purpose.

Reporter: SheridanCook · Commenters: egelkraut, brian.postlethwaite,
linesele, mckenzie

### [FHIR-3731: Request to separate Birth Date from Birth Time](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-3731)

Historical core-spec ticket (M&M, **Persuasive with Modification**,
resolved 2018-07) that changed `Patient.birthDate` from `dateTime` to
`date` and pushed birth time into a standard extension
(`patient-birthTime`). This is the foundational decision that
FHIR-50422 builds on: VRCL has the same shape (use `birthDate` for
the date plus `patient-birthTime` for the time) and FHIR-50422 is
asking VRCL to stop carrying an additional partial-date-time
mechanism on top of it.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the next published version of the Vital Records Common Profiles
Library, **remove the `partialDateTime` extension slice from the
`Patient.birthDate` element** in any VRCL Patient profile that
currently declares it (e.g., `Patient - Child`, `Patient - Mother`,
`Patient - Decedent` and equivalents). Concretely:

- Drop the `Patient.birthDate.extension:partialDateTime` differential
  slice in the affected StructureDefinitions.
- Update VRCL examples and snapshots so that `Patient.birthDate` is
  populated with a complete `YYYY-MM-DD` value.
- For instances where a precise birth time is required, continue to
  use the standard core `patient-birthTime` extension on
  `Patient.birthDate` (now broadened by FHIR-43302).
- Update `usage.html` to state that `partialDateTime` is no longer
  permitted on `Patient.birthDate` in VRCL, with migration guidance for
  implementers that previously used it.
- Add a Vital Records-specific invariant or mustSupport flag on
  `Patient.birthDate` requiring a complete date (no missing components)
  to make the use-case constraint explicit.

#### Justification

This matches the reporter's stated requirement — vital-records use
cases require a fully known birth date plus an explicit birth time —
and follows the path the IG already telegraphed by deprecating the
extension in `usage.html`. The `partialDateTime` slot on
`Patient.birthDate` cannot legitimately be used in a vital-records
record (a partially-known DOB is non-conformant for the use case),
so leaving it in place only invites incorrect instances and increases
implementer burden. Removing it also aligns with the FHIR-I direction
expressed in [FHIR-41647](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-41647)
("don't use the extension when the date is fully known") and avoids
the `value`/`_value` synchronization complexity highlighted in
[FHIR-44596](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-44596). The core
`patient-birthTime` extension, recently broadened in
[FHIR-43302](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43302), is sufficient to
carry the time component.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than deleting the slice, **retain the `partialDateTime`
extension on `Patient.birthDate` in the StructureDefinition but
constrain it out** for the vital-records use case:

- Set the `partialDateTime` slice cardinality on `Patient.birthDate` to
  `0..0` in VRCL Patient profiles where a complete birth date is
  required (and matching `Patient.birthDate` itself to `1..1`).
- Where a profile genuinely needs to express a partial DOB (e.g.,
  fetal-status / unknown-newborn cases), keep the slice but flag it
  `mustSupport=false` and require a `data-absent-reason` extension
  for any unknown components.
- Add an explicit narrative section in VRCL documenting the migration
  from `partialDateTime` to `birthDate` + `patient-birthTime`, so
  prior implementers can transition without conformance breakage.
- Mark the `partialDateTime` extension StructureDefinition itself as
  `status=deprecated` (rather than `retired` / removed), keeping URLs
  resolvable for downstream IGs that depend on it.

#### Justification

This honours the reporter's intent — partial dates are not legal in
the vital-records use case — without performing a hard removal that
could break dependent IGs (notably MDI, which
[FHIR-41647](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-41647) explicitly redirected
to the VRDR/VRCL `PartialDateTime` definition). Constraining the
slice to `0..0` in profiles is enforced by validators just as
effectively as deletion, while leaving the underlying StructureDefinition
in the IG preserves canonical URLs and gives downstream IGs a deprecation
window. It also lets VRCL keep a single carve-out (e.g., unknown-DOB
cases) if the WG later finds one, without re-introducing a previously
removed extension. Trade-off vs. Disposition A: more cleanup work and
slightly more complex StructureDefinitions, but lower risk of breaking
external consumers that resolve VRCL URLs.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request and **leave the `partialDateTime` extension on
`Patient.birthDate` as-is** in VRCL, on the grounds that the extension
is already declared deprecated in `usage.html` and that its presence
in the StructureDefinition is needed for backward compatibility with
existing vital-records exchanges and downstream IGs (e.g., MDI). Capture
the existing deprecation guidance more prominently and revisit removal
in a future major-version revision once known consumers have migrated.

#### Justification

The extension has been on the books long enough to be in production
use for vital-records exchange. Removing it from
`Patient.birthDate` is technically a breaking change for any
implementer that emits the extension (instances would fail validation
against the new VRCL profiles), and FHIR-44596 explicitly chose to
*clarify* rather than *eliminate* the extension only ~15 months ago.
Combined with the lack of any Zulip or GitHub conversation surfacing
implementer support for removal, declining (or deferring) is defensible
on stability grounds. The downside is well-documented in
[FHIR-41647](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-41647): leaving the
extension in place perpetuates complexity that FHIR-I has already
flagged as problematic.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the strongest fit because it delivers exactly what the
ticket asks for *in the vital-records use case* — no `partialDateTime`
on `Patient.birthDate` — while preserving the StructureDefinition
URL so that dependent IGs (notably MDI per
[FHIR-41647](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-41647)) are not broken
overnight. Constraining the slice to `0..0` in the VRCL Patient
profiles is enforced by FHIR validators identically to Disposition A's
deletion, but it leaves a deprecation/migration runway and keeps a
single, well-documented escape hatch should a legitimate
unknown-component case (e.g., abandoned-newborn child records) emerge
during ballot. Disposition A is a viable second choice if the WG is
confident no downstream IG still needs the extension URL; Disposition C
is the weakest option because it perpetuates a known source of
complexity that FHIR-I has already pushed back on, with no concrete
implementer feedback in the record arguing for keeping the slice
populated. The WG should also coordinate with the reporters of
FHIR-44596 and FHIR-41647 to confirm the deprecation messaging is
consistent across VRCL, VRDR, and MDI.
