## Summary

Hans Buitendijk asks the US SAFR (US Situational Awareness Framework for
Reporting) authors to clarify how the situational-awareness capabilities
provided by the SANER `MeasureReport` profile are carried forward in the
US SAFR `MeasureReport` profile. Specifically, he is requesting a
side-by-side comparison or mapping between the two profiles that would
let reviewers validate that the scope of SANER situational-awareness
reporting (e.g., resource utilisation counts, measure-group structure,
subject/reporter conventions) is fully addressed by US SAFR. The ticket
was reviewed at WGM (2025-09) and originally voted "Considered for
Future Use" (12-0-2) on the basis that SANER had expired and was not
compatible with the Da Vinci DEQM `MeasureReport` shape that US SAFR
adopts. After STU1 of US SAFR published, the ticket was reopened
(2026-03-31) so the comparison question can be revisited in the
post-STU1 cycle.

No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were
returned by the FHIR Augury index for this ticket, so the review is
based solely on the ticket content and comments.

## Details

**Description:**

When comparing SANER MeasureReport profile and the MeasureReport profile
used in US SAFR it is not clear how the SANER capabilities are available
in US SAFR for situational awareness reporting. Is there a
compare/mapping available that would help validate that to ensure scope
is comparable and addressed?

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Produce and publish a SANER ↔ US SAFR `MeasureReport` mapping document
as part of the US SAFR IG (e.g., a new "Comparison with SANER" page
under the IG's narrative pages). The mapping would walk each SANER
`MeasureReport` capability — top-level fields (`status`, `type`,
`measure`, `subject`, `reporter`, `period`, `date`), `group`/
`stratifier`/`population` structure, `MeasureReport.group.code` use for
resource-utilisation measures (e.g., bed counts), and the SANER
extensions used for situational awareness (e.g.,
`measureReport-supplementalData`, location/reporter conventions) — and
record for each row how the same capability is expressed (or
deliberately omitted) in the US SAFR profile, with the rationale tied
to DEQM alignment. Where US SAFR does not carry forward a SANER
capability, the mapping must explicitly say so and point to the DEQM
mechanism that replaces it.

#### Justification

The reporter's question is fundamentally a documentation and
traceability gap, not a profile bug, and the WG already conceded at WGM
that SANER compatibility "will be revisited" if SANER moves forward.
Reopening the ticket post-STU1 implies the WG is now in a position to
publish the comparison without blocking STU1. A formal mapping page is
the lowest-cost way to (a) answer Hans's review question definitively,
(b) give SANER implementers a migration path into US SAFR, and (c)
serve as the evidence base for any future "SANER as parent IG"
discussion. The work is additive (narrative only), keeps US SAFR's DEQM
alignment intact, and is reusable by ballot reviewers.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than ship a full SANER-vs-US SAFR mapping inside the US SAFR IG,
ask Hans (per JIRAUSER25103's 2025-08-12 comment) to enumerate the
specific SANER capabilities he believes are missing or unclear, then
address them targetedly: for each capability he names, either (i) point
to the existing US SAFR profile element / extension that carries it,
(ii) add a short clarification to the relevant US SAFR profile's
narrative, or (iii) open a follow-up change request scoped to that
specific capability. The mapping is built up incrementally from concrete
gaps rather than as a comprehensive document.

#### Justification

US SAFR was deliberately built on the DEQM `MeasureReport` shape, not
the SANER shape, and SANER is currently expired. Producing a full
side-by-side mapping risks (a) implying parity with SANER that US SAFR
does not actually claim, (b) baking expired-IG semantics into a
published US SAFR page, and (c) consuming significant editorial effort
on capabilities no implementer has actually asked for. The author's
existing question on the ticket ("please list the specific
capabilities…") shows the WG already prefers a targeted response. This
disposition codifies that preference: keep US SAFR DEQM-aligned, and
treat SANER comparability as a series of concrete, demand-driven
clarifications.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as "Not Persuasive" (or re-apply the prior "Considered
for Future Use" disposition) on the grounds that US SAFR has
intentionally adopted the Da Vinci DEQM `MeasureReport` shape rather
than the SANER shape, SANER itself is expired, and the situational-
awareness capabilities US SAFR commits to (reporting codes and counts)
are already documented in the US SAFR profile narrative. Any future
SANER alignment work would be revisited only if SANER returns as an
active (and ideally parent) IG.

#### Justification

This was the WG's actual September 2025 disposition (12-0-2) and the
rationale recorded by Lynn — "SANER is currently expired and not
compatible with the Da Vinci DEQM IG" — has not changed. The reopen
comment is procedural ("re-open post STU1 publication") and does not
introduce new technical evidence or a concrete missing capability.
Declining preserves US SAFR's DEQM alignment, avoids creating
authoritative comparison content against an expired IG, and is
consistent with the question still standing on the ticket from the US
SAFR author asking Hans to enumerate concrete gaps before any work is
undertaken.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B best matches the actual state of the ticket. The US SAFR
author has already asked Hans on the ticket (2025-08-12) for the
specific SANER capabilities he believes are missing or unclear, and
that question is unanswered; no concrete capability gap, Zulip
discussion, or GitHub follow-up has surfaced for this ticket. Spending
editorial effort on a comprehensive SANER-vs-US SAFR mapping
(Disposition A) before that list exists risks publishing
authoritative-looking comparison content against an expired IG and
implying a parity claim US SAFR does not make. Outright declining
(Disposition C) is defensible but ignores the fact that the WG itself
chose to reopen the ticket after STU1, signalling willingness to revisit
the question. Disposition B threads the needle: it respects the reopen,
keeps US SAFR firmly on the DEQM `MeasureReport` shape, and converts a
broad mapping request into a tractable, evidence-driven series of
clarifications — any of which can be promoted to a follow-up change
request if it turns out to require a profile change rather than just
narrative.
