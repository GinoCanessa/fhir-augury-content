## Summary

The reporter (Virginia Lorenzi) observes that the Electronic Case Reporting
(eCR) FHIR IG already enumerates a fairly comprehensive list of stakeholders
— including standards development organizations — but does not include
**patients**. She asks that patients be explicitly added to the stakeholder
list. The ticket has been triaged as **Persuasive**, with the noted
resolution being to add Patients to the stakeholder list and to mirror the
same change in the companion CDA IG. There are no cross-referenced Jira
tickets, no related Zulip discussions surfaced by the cross-reference index,
and no linked GitHub items.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add **Patients** as an explicit entry in the stakeholder list of the
Electronic Case Reporting (FHIR) IG. Concretely, edit the IG's narrative
"Stakeholders" page (typically an authoring-tier markdown/xml/html page in
the IG sources, alongside the existing entries for clinicians, public
health agencies, EHR vendors, and SDOs) to add a Patients bullet with a
short paragraph describing patient interest in case reporting (e.g.,
awareness/transparency that a report concerning them is being submitted,
and the role of patient advocates in governance bodies that influence eCR).
Mirror the same addition in the companion CDA eCR IG so the two
specifications remain aligned, as called out in the resolution description.
No conformance resources (profiles, value sets, extensions, capability
statements) need to change — this is narrative-only.

#### Justification

The change is narrative, low-risk, and reversible, and it aligns with the
resolution already recorded on the ticket ("Persuasive — will add Patients
to stakeholder list (will also do this in the CDA IG)"). Patient inclusion
matches HL7's broader direction of treating patients as first-class
stakeholders (e.g., Patient Empowerment WG, patient representatives on the
FHIR Management Group), and the comment thread cites concrete precedent
(governance bodies such as Utah's Digital Health Commission) for treating
patients/patient advocates as stakeholders even in domains where they are
not the primary data source. Keeping the FHIR and CDA eCR IGs synchronized
on this list avoids divergence between the two parallel specifications that
implementers and policy reviewers compare side-by-side.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the underlying intent but broaden the change. Restructure the
stakeholder section into two sub-groupings — **Stakeholders directly
involved in case reporting workflows** (clinicians, EHRs, PHAs, intermediaries,
SDOs) and **Stakeholders with an interest in case reporting** (patients,
patient advocacy organizations, the broader public, researchers using
de-identified surveillance outputs). Place patients (and, as appropriate,
patient advocacy organizations) in the second grouping with a short
paragraph that distinguishes their interest (transparency, governance
participation, secondary use of aggregate data) from operational roles.
Apply the same restructuring in the CDA IG.

#### Justification

This addresses minigrrl's threshold question ("are patients really
stakeholders?") by acknowledging that patients are not operational
participants in the case-report exchange itself, while still recognising
their legitimate interest. A flat list risks reader confusion about why
patients appear alongside roles like "EHR vendor" that have direct
implementation obligations; a two-tier structure preserves accuracy without
excluding patients. The cost is slightly more authoring effort (a small
section reorganisation in both IGs) versus a one-line addition in
Disposition A.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change. Leave the stakeholder list as-is on the basis that
the eCR IG enumerates stakeholders who have a *direct operational role* in
generating, transmitting, receiving, or governing the standard for case
reports (clinicians, EHR vendors, public health agencies, intermediaries,
SDOs). Patients, while important, do not author, transmit, receive, or
govern the technical specification, and their interest in being informed
about case reporting is a policy/consent matter handled outside the IG's
stakeholder enumeration.

#### Justification

This is the position implied by minigrrl's initial comment ("Q: are
patients really stakeholders?"). Adding patients without further
qualification could imply implementation obligations toward patients
(notification, consent UX, patient-facing access to submitted reports)
that the IG does not currently specify and that public health reporting
laws often explicitly do not require. Declining keeps the stakeholder
list tightly scoped to parties with conformance-relevant roles.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket has already been triaged as Persuasive, the reporter and a
second commenter (minigrrl) have converged on patient inclusion with
concrete precedent, and the change is narrative-only with no impact on
profiles, value sets, or conformance resources — so the implementation
risk is minimal and the cross-IG alignment with the CDA eCR IG is
straightforward to maintain. Disposition B is reasonable and could be
folded into A as an editorial improvement if the Public Health WG wants
to address the "operational vs. interested" distinction at the same time,
but it should not block the simple addition. Disposition C is hard to
defend given the recorded Persuasive resolution and the precedent cited
in the discussion, and runs against HL7's broader posture of recognising
patients as stakeholders across the FHIR ecosystem.
