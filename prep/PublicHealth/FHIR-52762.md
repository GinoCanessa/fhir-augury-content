## Summary

FHIR-52762 asks the US Public Health Profiles Library (USPHPL) to layer
its `us-ph-messagedefinition` profile on top of the new CRMI
MessageDefinition profiles requested in
[FHIR-52761](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-52761) (CDS / CRMI), rather
than continuing to author MessageDefinition profile machinery
independently within USPHPL. FHIR-52761 has already been resolved
**Persuasive** by CDS, with agreement to create
`ShareableMessageDefinition` and `PublishableMessageDefinition` in CRMI;
those CRMI profiles are not yet published, however, so USPHPL has no
upstream artifact to derive from today. The Public Health work group
initially dispositioned this ticket as *Considered for Future Use*
(Sarah Gaunt / Ravi Kafle, 17-0-4, 2025-09-25), and Lynn Laakso
subsequently reverted that resolution and flagged the ticket to be
re-opened post-STU2 publication so the USPHPL "architecture" profiles
can be re-aligned with CRMI in a future release.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Re-base `us-ph-messagedefinition` (USPHPL) on the forthcoming CRMI
`ShareableMessageDefinition` and `PublishableMessageDefinition`
profiles created under FHIR-52761. Concretely, in the next USPHPL
release after CRMI publishes those profiles:

- Set `StructureDefinition.baseDefinition` of `us-ph-messagedefinition`
  to the CRMI `PublishableMessageDefinition` canonical (deriving
  transitively from `ShareableMessageDefinition`).
- Drop USPHPL-local "architecture" constraints on `MessageDefinition`
  that are now expressed by the CRMI parents (e.g., `url`, `version`,
  `name`, `title`, `status`, `experimental`, `publisher`, `contact`,
  `description`, plus the publishable additions such as approval/last
  review dates, effective period, topic, author/editor/reviewer/endorser,
  related-artifact, etc.) and retain only USPHPL-specific narrowings.
- Update USPHPL IG dependencies to declare a dependency on CRMI at the
  appropriate version, and refresh the must-support / cardinality
  matrix so it overlays the CRMI baseline rather than duplicating it.
- Add a release note in USPHPL documenting the realignment for
  implementers.

#### Justification

This is exactly the alignment Bryn requested and matches the work
group's stated long-term goal, captured in Lynn Laakso's comment, of
moving the USPHPL "architecture" profiles into CRMI. CDS has already
agreed via FHIR-52761 to publish the upstream profiles (Persuasive,
Block-Vote-2 / Ready-For-Vote), so the design pattern has cross-WG
endorsement. Deriving from CRMI eliminates duplicated authoring
machinery, gives implementers a consistent canonical-resource
management story across IGs, and lets future CRMI improvements
(approval workflow, effective period, related artifacts, etc.) flow
into USPHPL automatically.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Persuasive with mod / "Considered for Future Use" with an explicit
schedule: keep the current `us-ph-messagedefinition` definition as-is
for STU2 publication, but commit the change for the **first USPHPL
release after the CRMI MessageDefinition profiles are published**. To
make this concrete:

- Add a roadmap / "Future Direction" note in the USPHPL
  `us-ph-messagedefinition` page stating that the profile will be
  re-based on `CRMIPublishableMessageDefinition` once CRMI publishes
  it, with a link to FHIR-52761.
- Track the rebasing under the existing
  `2025_USPHPL_ARCHITECTURE_PROFILES` label so it is picked up in the
  next architecture-profiles work cycle.
- Re-open this ticket (as Lynn has already flagged) post-STU2
  publication and convert it into the implementation ticket at that
  time.

#### Justification

This honors the prior Public Health vote (17-0-4) that the change is
desirable but not yet executable, and addresses Lynn's concern that the
CRMI profiles do not yet exist. STU2 publication is in progress, so
re-basing a normative-track profile on an unpublished upstream creates
real risk: USPHPL would either need to ship a forward reference to a
nonexistent canonical, or hold its own publication for CRMI. Documenting
the planned realignment keeps implementers informed without coupling
the STU2 cut date to CRMI's publication schedule, while still
guaranteeing the alignment Bryn is asking for.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve as *Not Persuasive* and keep `us-ph-messagedefinition` derived
directly from the base FHIR `MessageDefinition`, with USPHPL continuing
to author its own "architecture" constraints (publisher, status,
description, etc.) locally.

#### Justification

A pure decline is hard to defend here: the work group has already
agreed on direction (vote was *Considered for Future Use*, not
*Rejected*), the CRMI parent profiles have been agreed by CDS, and
Lynn explicitly stated that the long-term goal is to move USPHPL
architecture profiles into CRMI. The only credible reason to decline
outright would be an architectural concern that USPHPL must remain
independent of CRMI — which neither the ticket history nor the linked
FHIR-52761 supports. Listed for completeness so the workgroup can
record an explicit rejection if they wish to step back from the
prior direction.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The work group has consistently signaled that the CRMI alignment is
the right end state but that the upstream CRMI MessageDefinition
profiles do not yet exist; that is precisely why the original vote was
*Considered for Future Use* and why Lynn reverted the resolution rather
than letting it close. Disposition B captures both halves of that
position: it keeps STU2 on schedule by deferring the actual re-basing,
while putting an on-the-record commitment (roadmap note plus the
existing `2025_USPHPL_ARCHITECTURE_PROFILES` label and a re-open
trigger tied to CRMI publication of FHIR-52761) so the change is not
forgotten. Disposition A is the right outcome eventually, but adopting
it now would force USPHPL to depend on an unpublished CRMI artifact
during ballot/publication. Disposition C is included only for
completeness — it contradicts the work group's own stated roadmap.
