## Summary

The reporter (David DeRoode) requests that the Helios Query and Response
Work Group guidance document add citations to exemplar Public Health
Agencies (PHAs) and Health Information Exchanges (HIEs) for two specific
claims made in the "Security, Authentication, Authorization, Access"
section (p. 13): (1) that "a data use agreement, signed by a PHA, HIE,
and its members, allows the PHA to retrieve data without requiring
individual user authentication"; and (2) that "in some states, patients
can initiate data sharing by authenticating themselves with a state
portal." The ticket is intended to ground these statements in real-world
deployments that workgroup readers can reference. A commenter (forwhite)
noted that this comment substantially overlaps with
[FHIR-52208](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-52208), which asked for
citations to specific inter-state PHA data-sharing agreements in a
nearby paragraph of the same guidance document; FHIR-52208 was resolved
"Persuasive with Modification" with revised wording and exemplar
citations (Washington's OHP HIE, Maryland CRISP, New York's tri-state
DUA, IZ Gateway). The current ticket is currently dispositioned as
"Considered - No action required" on the basis that the broader
citation work was performed under FHIR-52208.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the "Security, Authentication, Authorization, Access" section
(p. 13) of the Helios Query & Response Work Group Guidance Document
([Confluence page 288398512](https://confluence.hl7.org/spaces/PH/pages/288398512/Helios+Query+Response+Work+Group+Guidance+Document))
to add concrete exemplar citations for both contested statements:

1. For "a data use agreement, signed by a PHA, HIE, and its members,
   allows the PHA to retrieve data without requiring individual user
   authentication," cite at least one operational PHA↔HIE DUA — e.g.,
   the Washington State Department of Health agreement with OneHealthPort
   HIE, or the Maryland Department of Health agreement with CRISP — with
   a footnote/URL pointing to the published agreement or program page.
2. For "in some states, patients can initiate data sharing by
   authenticating themselves with a state portal," cite at least one
   live state portal (e.g., MyIR Mobile / state immunization portals,
   or a state HIE patient-access portal) and link to its authentication
   landing page.

The edits are wording-only (label `Q&R-WordingChanges/Additions`); they
do not change any FHIR conformance artifact.

#### Justification

The reporter's underlying complaint — that two factual claims about
real-world PHA/HIE practice are made without evidence — is identical in
form to FHIR-52208, which the workgroup already accepted as Persuasive
with Modification and resolved by adding exemplar citations (OHP HIE,
CRISP, NY tri-state DUA, IZ Gateway). Treating FHIR-52210 the same way
keeps the section internally consistent: every assertion about
real-world PHA/HIE practice in this part of the guidance document would
then be backed by a named exemplar. The same exemplars used for
FHIR-52208 (CRISP, OneHealthPort) directly support the DUA-based
no-individual-authentication claim, so the incremental editorial cost is
low.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Resolve as **Considered - No action required (consolidated under
FHIR-52208)**, but explicitly extend the FHIR-52208 fix so the new
paragraph that cites Washington/OHP HIE, Maryland/CRISP, NY tri-state
DUA, and IZ Gateway is positioned (or cross-referenced) so that it also
visibly supports the two p.13 claims that FHIR-52210 calls out. In
practice, this means either (a) moving / duplicating the citation
sentence into the "Security, Authentication, Authorization, Access"
subsection, or (b) inserting a short forward-pointer in that subsection
("see exemplars listed in §X") and explicitly noting which of those
exemplars operate under DUAs that waive individual user authentication
and which expose patient-authenticated portals.

#### Justification

This honours the intent of FHIR-52210 (don't leave the two p.13
statements unsourced) without re-opening a separate edit cycle on a
ticket the workgroup has already classified as a comment / duplicate. It
also avoids the risk of the two tickets producing two independently
worded citation lists that drift apart in future revisions. The cost is
a single sentence/cross-reference in the document, and it preserves the
single-source-of-truth structure FHIR-52208 introduced.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (current resolution: "Considered - No action required")

#### Proposal

Maintain the existing resolution: "Considered - No action required" on
the grounds that FHIR-52210 is a relative duplicate of FHIR-52208, and
that the FHIR-52208 resolution (which added exemplar citations such as
Washington's OHP HIE, Maryland CRISP, NY tri-state DUA, and the IZ
Gateway) substantively addresses the broader concern about exemplar
citations in this section of the guidance document. Close the ticket
with a comment pointing the reporter to the revised paragraph in the
4-2-2026 ballot reconciliation draft.

#### Justification

The ticket type is "Comment" (not "Change Request"), and the reporter
did not propose specific exemplars or specific text. The reporter
themselves acknowledged on FHIR-52208 that the same author/topic
applies. The companion ticket FHIR-52208 already drove a concrete text
change in the same document with named exemplars. Adding a separate
edit purely to satisfy FHIR-52210 risks duplicating citations and
creating editorial drift across a small section. Declining is
defensible because the documented underlying need — give readers
real-world examples — is now met.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the cleanest path: it accepts the substantive complaint
(the two p.13 statements should not stand uncited) without re-opening a
ticket the workgroup has already chosen to consolidate under FHIR-52208,
and it prevents the two tickets from producing competing citation lists
in future revisions. Disposition C (the current state) is technically
defensible because FHIR-52208 added exemplars to the same section, but
those exemplars sit in a different paragraph from the two specific
sentences FHIR-52210 calls out, so a reader scanning p.13 may still
encounter the unsupported DUA / patient-portal claims first. Disposition
A is correct in spirit but creates an unnecessary parallel edit when a
short cross-reference (or relocated sentence) in the same section
suffices. Recommend the workgroup either (a) leave the resolution as
"Considered - No action required" but commit to the small forward-
reference edit described in Disposition B during final ballot
reconciliation, or (b) re-classify as Persuasive with Modification and
make that small edit explicitly.
