### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat this as an editorial / guidance-correctness ticket against the
Electronic Case Reporting (FHIR) IG. The work group performs a focused
review of every page and profile narrative that states or implies a
"lookback" / "how far back to retrieve data" rule — typically including the
data-collection guidance for the eICR Composition and its sections
(encounters, conditions, observations, results, immunizations, medications,
problems), and any FHIR Operation / query guidance that supplies a `_since`,
`date=ge…`, or comparable filter. For each occurrence, confirm against the
intended public-health policy that the stated window (e.g., "since the start
of the encounter", "since the most recent reportable event", "all relevant
historical data", "last N days/months/years") is correct, internally
consistent, and consistent with the underlying CDA-based eICR guidance where
applicable. Apply text fixes where the guidance is wrong, ambiguous, or
contradictory, and add a short rationale where the rule is intentional.

#### Justification

The reporter has framed the ticket as a correctness check on guidance
text rather than a structural change. A targeted editorial sweep is
the lowest-risk way to fulfil the literal ask: it requires no profile
or value-set changes, does not affect conformance, and lets the work
group publish a corrected narrative in the next IG build. It also
matches how Public Health typically handles "is the guidance still
right?" reports against the eCR IG.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as written and split the work into a small set of
specific child tickets, one per location where lookback guidance
appears (for example: eICR Composition encounters section, conditions
section, results section, immunizations section, medications section,
plus any `$everything` / bulk-export guidance). Each child ticket
records the current guidance text, the proposed corrected text, the
rationale, and an owner. Optionally introduce a single "Data Lookback
Guidance" page (or anchored section) in the IG that all profile
narratives link to, so future corrections happen in one place.

#### Justification

"Ensure that guidance for how far back to get data is correct" is
under-specified as a standalone ticket: it does not name a page,
profile, or current text, which makes it hard to track resolution and
hard for an external reviewer to verify the fix. Decomposing into
location-specific tickets gives the work group a clear audit trail and
avoids the risk that one missed page leaves contradictory guidance in
place. Centralising the guidance on a single page additionally reduces
future drift, at the cost of one structural edit to the IG.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the ticket as not actionable in its current form and request
that the reporter resubmit with: (a) the specific page(s) and/or
profile narrative(s) where the current guidance is believed to be
wrong, (b) the text as it stands today, and (c) the proposed corrected
text or, at minimum, the policy the guidance should reflect.

#### Justification

The ticket as written contains only a one-sentence directive and
provides no diff, no citation, and no policy reference. Without a
concrete pointer the work group would either have to re-derive the
reporter's concern from first principles or perform an open-ended
audit of the entire IG. Either path risks "fixing" guidance that is
already correct or missing the location the reporter actually had in
mind. Asking for a more specific submission is a defensible way to
keep the backlog grounded and is consistent with how other Public
Health "guidance is wrong" tickets have been triaged.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The literal ask in Disposition A is reasonable, but a single
"sweep-and-fix" task with no named page or current text is hard to
scope, hard to review, and hard to mark as resolved. Disposition C is
defensible but pushes the entire burden back to the reporter and risks
the underlying correctness concern being lost. Disposition B keeps the
reporter's intent intact, breaks the work into auditable units the
work group can actually disposition, and — by introducing a single
canonical lookback-guidance page — reduces the chance that this same
ticket has to be filed again in a future cycle. It is the option most
likely to leave the eCR (FHIR) IG measurably better while still
respecting that the reporter has flagged a real, concrete concern.
