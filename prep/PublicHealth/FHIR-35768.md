## Summary

Virginia Lorenzi (the reporter) recommends that the **US Central Cancer
Registry Reporting (CCRR) Content IG** explicitly cross-reference any
existing V2 or CDA cancer-reporting specifications and that the IG
authors collaborate with — and relate the IG's content to — the
**HL7 CodeX / mCode** community's work. The ticket is a generic
"please coordinate with adjacent cancer specifications" request rather
than a request to add or change a specific profile, element, or
binding. It was originally dispositioned **Considered for Future Use**
on 2022-03-10 (vote 30-0-0, Bashyam / Michaels) on the grounds that the
existing collaboration with CodeX was sufficient for STU1 and that a
deeper review would delay publication; that resolution was **reverted
on 2024-05-31 by Lynn Laakso** to be re-opened post-STU1 publication,
and the ticket is now back in Triaged status with the `Block-Vote-1`
label, awaiting a fresh disposition for the next version of the IG.
There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub
items linked to this issue in the indexed FHIR Augury data.

## Proposed Dispositions

> Note: Because the ticket text and the surviving comments are
> generic ("collaborate", "reference"), all three dispositions are
> framed at the IG-narrative / cross-reference level rather than at the
> individual profile / element level. There is no Repo Context to
> anchor file paths against; any concrete change would land in the
> CCRR IG repository (the IG's content/narrative pages, e.g. an
> updated `index`, `background`, or new `relationship-to-other-igs`
> page, plus dependency declarations in the IG's `sushi-config.yaml`
> / `package.json`). If repo-analysis briefings for the CCRR IG repo
> become available, this section should be tightened to name those
> files explicitly.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

For the next version of the CCRR Content IG, add an explicit
**"Relationship to Other Specifications"** narrative page that
catalogues:

1. The HL7 V2 message profiles used today for cancer reporting to
   central registries (NAACCR / CDC NPCR Volume V messaging,
   ORU^R01-based pathology reporting), with version pointers.
2. The HL7 CDA "Reporting to Public Health: Cancer Registry Reporting"
   IG (the production CDA IG that the current FHIR IG is already based
   on), with a profile-by-profile mapping table (CDA template ↔ FHIR
   profile).
3. The HL7 FHIR **mCODE** IG and the **CodeX** Use Case work products,
   listing each CCRR profile that is derived from or aligns with an
   mCODE profile (Cancer Patient, Cancer Condition, Cancer Stage Group,
   Cancer-Related Procedure, etc.) and noting the CCRR-specific
   constraints layered on top.

In addition, declare mCODE as a formal IG dependency in the IG's
package manifest (if not already), so the inheritance is machine-
checkable rather than narrative-only, and add a short "How this IG
relates to mCODE" subsection on each affected profile page.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter asked for and what the working group
itself committed to in the 2022-02-09 and 2022-02-16 comments
("a work item for the next version of the IG", "for future versions
there will be additional complete review of mCode"). The 2024-05-31
re-open was explicitly to revisit this commitment now that STU1 has
shipped, so the timing fits. A relationship-to-other-specs narrative
plus a formal mCODE dependency declaration is consistent with how
other Public Health IGs (e.g., eCR) document their relationship to
companion FHIR/CDA specifications, and it gives implementers a single
authoritative place to understand the V2 / CDA / FHIR landscape for
central cancer registry reporting.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than producing a single in-IG narrative page, treat this as a
**joint harmonization deliverable** with the CodeX community:

1. Stand up (or reuse) a shared CCRR ↔ mCODE alignment artifact
   maintained outside the CCRR IG itself — e.g., a CodeX Use Case
   page, a Confluence harmonization matrix, or a small companion FHIR
   IG / ConceptMap package — and link to it from the CCRR IG's home
   page.
2. In the CCRR IG, limit the change to (a) declaring an mCODE
   dependency in `sushi-config.yaml`, (b) replacing CCRR-defined
   profiles with `imposeProfile` / direct-derivation references to
   mCODE profiles where the constraints are equivalent, and (c)
   adding a one-line pointer on each profile page to the external
   harmonization artifact.
3. For the V2 / CDA side, add only a short "Prior Art" subsection
   with normative references to the existing NAACCR V2 messaging and
   the CDA Cancer Registry Reporting IG, rather than reproducing
   their content.

#### Justification

The 2022 disposition rationale ("existing collaboration with CodeX is
deemed sufficient") suggests the working group's underlying concern
is duplication and divergence, not lack of awareness. Pushing the
mapping table out to a jointly-owned CodeX artifact reduces the risk
that CCRR and mCODE drift in their narrative descriptions of the same
profiles, and it gives the cancer-data community a single home for
cross-IG alignment. It also keeps the CCRR IG itself focused on the
registry-reporting use case rather than turning it into a survey of
adjacent specs. The trade-off vs. Disposition A is that implementers
must follow an outbound link to get the full picture, and the cross-
references become co-owned (and therefore harder to keep in sync on a
single ballot cycle).

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as **Persuasive with Mod / Already Addressed**: keep the
existing collaboration posture documented in the prior comments and
add only a single short paragraph on the IG's home page noting that
the IG was developed in collaboration with the CodeX community,
reuses mCODE profiles where applicable, and supersedes the CDA Cancer
Registry Reporting IG and NAACCR V2 messaging for FHIR-based reporting
— without producing a profile-by-profile mapping table or new
dependency declarations.

#### Justification

The original 30-0-0 vote in 2022 reflected a genuine workgroup
consensus that the underlying request was already satisfied by the
existing CodeX/mCODE reuse. The ticket as written is non-specific
("might want to collaborate", "if one exists, seem to recall they
do") and does not identify a concrete defect — no missing element, no
incorrect binding, no broken cross-reference. Re-opening it post-STU1
without tightening the ask risks scope creep into a multi-IG mapping
exercise that the workgroup has previously judged to be high effort
relative to the implementer benefit. A short narrative acknowledgement
discharges the comment without committing to an open-ended cross-IG
maintenance burden.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter's request, the workgroup's own 2022 commitment ("work
item for the next version of the IG", "additional complete review of
mCode"), and Lynn Laakso's 2024-05-31 deliberate re-open all point in
the same direction: now that STU1 is published, the workgroup said it
would do this, and this ticket is the placeholder for that work. The
`Block-Vote-1` label and `Highest` priority make a "Decline" outcome
hard to defend without revisiting that prior commitment on the
record. Disposition A is the most faithful execution of what was
promised; Disposition B is a reasonable refinement if the Cancer
Registry team and the CodeX community agree to co-own a shared
alignment artifact, and the workgroup may want to combine them
(produce the in-IG narrative for STU2 *and* contribute the mapping
matrix back to a CodeX-hosted artifact). Either way, the change
should land in the CCRR IG's narrative pages and `sushi-config.yaml`
in the CCRR IG repository; no profile semantics need change to
satisfy the ticket.
