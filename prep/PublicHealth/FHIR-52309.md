## Summary

Rick Geimer requests that the US Helios Query and Response (Q&R) informative
guidance—currently planned/published as a PDF white paper—be delivered instead
as a standard FHIR Implementation Guide (IG) using the online HL7 IG
publication format. The reporter argues the content is largely narrative, so
the conversion effort should be small, while the IG format would make the
guidance significantly more discoverable and useful to implementers. The only
caveat noted is that, because the guide contains no profiles or other
computable artifacts, the IG publication may surface warnings/errors about
missing computable content, which (per the reporter) FMG typically waives for
narrative-only IGs.

The ticket has already been triaged with a resolution of **Persuasive**. The
recorded resolution text states: "Agree this informative guidance document
will be published in FHIR IG online format." The Helios accelerator confirmed
the same direction in a parallel FMG Zulip thread (see Related Zulip
Discussions), where FMG (Bryn Rhodes) clarified that an informative IG still
requires a FHIR IG proposal even though it has no computable artifacts. There
are no cross-referenced Jira tickets, GitHub items, or comments on the ticket
itself.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the request as-is: publish the US Helios Query and Response guidance as
a narrative-only HL7 FHIR Implementation Guide using the standard online IG
publication toolchain, replacing the PDF white-paper deliverable. Concretely:

- Submit (or confirm) a FHIR IG proposal for the Helios Q&R informative IG, as
  required by FMG (per Bryn Rhodes' Zulip confirmation), even though the IG
  contains no profiles, terminology, or examples.
- Publish from the existing in-progress build at
  `HL7/us-helios-QR` (`https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/us-helios-QR/index.html`)
  as the canonical IG.
- Categorize the IG as **informative**, and in the IG's narrative explicitly
  state that it carries no computable artifacts and that any IG-publisher
  warnings/errors about missing profiles/examples are expected for a
  narrative-only informative IG.
- Either deprecate the bulk-data PDF white paper in favor of a parallel
  informative IG, or scope this ticket strictly to the Q&R deliverable and
  leave the bulk PDF (`product_id=669`) untouched. Recommendation: scope this
  ticket to Q&R only and track bulk separately.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter asked for and what the recorded resolution
("Persuasive – will be published in FHIR IG online format") commits to. The
parallel FMG Zulip thread confirms that informative IGs are an accepted
publication pattern (precedent: "Common CQL Assets for FHIR – US") and that
the only procedural requirement beyond what Helios is already doing is a FHIR
IG proposal. Switching from PDF to an online IG materially improves
discoverability (versioned URL, cross-IG linking, build dashboards, FHIR
registry indexing) at low marginal effort, since the narrative content has
already been drafted. The known downside—IG-publisher complaints about
missing computable artifacts—is, per FMG practice, routinely waived for
narrative-only informative IGs.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Publish in the FHIR IG format as requested, but **require the Q&R IG to carry
at least a minimal set of computable anchors** so it conforms more cleanly to
"normal" IG expectations and avoids relying on FMG goodwill to ignore
publisher warnings. Concretely, before publishing:

- Add at least one CapabilityStatement summarizing the query/response
  conformance pattern(s) the guide describes (it is a Q&R guide; the patterns
  are inherently capability-shaped).
- Add a small ValueSet/CodeSystem stub (or reuse existing) for any controlled
  vocabulary the guide references in narrative.
- Add one or two example Bundles illustrating a query and a response.
- Keep the IG flagged informative, but with these anchors so the IG publisher
  output is clean.

If the Helios authors have no appetite for adding computable artifacts, fall
back to Disposition A.

#### Justification

Pure narrative-only IGs are permitted, but they are a long-tail case in the
HL7 IG ecosystem and depend on FMG explicitly excusing publisher warnings
each time. Adding even minimal computable anchors (especially a
CapabilityStatement, which is conceptually free for a Q&R guide) gives the IG
a cleaner publication footprint, makes it indexable by tools that look for
capabilities, and makes it more useful to implementers searching the FHIR
registry. The trade-off is a modest additional authoring cost up front, but
this avoids re-litigating "should this even be an IG?" with FMG on every
republish, and it keeps the door open to evolving the guide into a
profile-bearing IG later without a publication-format change.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Keep the Helios Q&R guidance as a PDF/white-paper deliverable (matching how
the Helios bulk-data paper was already published) and decline the conversion
to a FHIR IG. Rationale category: **publication format already in place; no
new normative or computable content to justify the IG-track overhead**
(IG proposal, IG-publisher CI, FHIR registry registration, ongoing IG
maintenance).

#### Justification

The content is purely narrative, with no profiles, terminology, examples, or
operations. Standard FHIR IGs are designed primarily to carry computable
conformance artifacts; a narrative-only IG forces FMG to repeatedly waive
publisher warnings and uses IG infrastructure for what is effectively a
white paper. Helios already has a published-PDF precedent for the bulk paper
(`product_id=669`), so keeping the Q&R guide on the same track preserves
parallelism within the Helios accelerator's outputs and avoids splitting
maintenance across two formats. Implementers who need it can still find it
via the standard HL7 product page.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket has already been resolved Persuasive with a commitment to publish
in FHIR IG online format, the Helios authors have an in-progress IG build at
`HL7/us-helios-QR`, and FMG has explicitly confirmed in Zulip that
narrative-only informative IGs are acceptable provided an IG proposal is
filed. Disposition A simply formalizes that path. Disposition B's idea of
adding a CapabilityStatement and example bundles is attractive and worth
raising as a follow-up enhancement, but it should not block the primary
publication-format decision and is strictly out of scope for what FHIR-52309
asks for. Disposition C contradicts both the reporter's request and the
already-recorded resolution and would only be defensible if Helios were
unwilling to file the IG proposal—which is not the case here. The Public
Health WG should ratify Disposition A and, optionally, capture B's computable
anchors as a separate enhancement against the Helios Q&R IG.
