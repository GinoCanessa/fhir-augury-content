## Summary

The submitter (Saul Kravitz) reports an internal inconsistency in the US
Birth and Fetal Death Reporting (BFDR) IG: of the three Composition
profiles defined in the NCHS→VRO section, two declare `Composition.subject`
as optional, but the **Coded Cause of Fetal Death Composition** keeps it
required (1..1). Because NCHS→VRO Compositions are produced by NCHS for
return to the VRO and may not always carry a meaningful subject reference,
the requirement to populate `subject` forces senders to either invent or
reuse a placeholder reference (the ticket notes the current workaround is
to populate `reference.display` on a dummy `Reference`). The ticket asks
that the cardinality of `subject` on the Coded Cause of Fetal Death
Composition be relaxed so that all three NCHS→VRO Compositions are
consistent. The ticket has been triaged with resolution **Persuasive**:
the BFDR work group has agreed to update the cardinality.

There were no cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub
items returned by the augury index for FHIR-49904, so the analysis below
is grounded solely in the ticket itself plus the cited BFDR artifacts
page.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Relax the cardinality of `Composition.subject` on the Coded Cause of
Fetal Death Composition profile (the BFDR NCHS→VRO Composition that
currently has `subject 1..1`) from **1..1 to 0..1**, matching the
cardinality already in place on the other two NCHS→VRO Compositions.
No other slicing, binding, or invariants on the profile are altered.
Update the profile's narrative to note that `subject` is optional in
the NCHS→VRO direction (consistent with the existing siblings), and
regenerate the IG snapshots and example instances so any examples that
currently include a placeholder `subject` continue to validate.

#### Justification

The change is exactly what the reporter (and the work group, per the
"Persuasive" resolution) asked for. It removes an internal
inconsistency between three sibling Composition profiles that play the
same role in the same exchange direction, eliminates the need for the
documented workaround of supplying a dummy `Reference.display`, and is
backward-compatible for senders that already populate `subject` (a
relaxed cardinality never invalidates an instance that previously
satisfied the stricter cardinality). FHIR profiling guidance favors
relaxing rather than retaining requirements that implementers
demonstrably cannot meet without dummy data, and the Public Health WG's
own resolution endorses this approach.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Instead of relaxing all three NCHS→VRO Composition profiles' `subject`
to `0..1` uniformly, **tighten the other two to 1..1** and require the
Coded Cause of Fetal Death Composition to keep `subject 1..1`, with
guidance in the IG that NCHS implementations populate `subject` with a
contained or logical `Reference` (e.g., a Patient resource representing
the decedent fetus or mother as appropriate to the composition, or a
canonical placeholder defined by the IG). This treats the inconsistency
as a defect in the *other* two profiles rather than in the Coded Cause
of Fetal Death profile.

#### Justification

`Composition.subject` is semantically important — it tells a consumer
*who/what the composition is about* — and leaving it optional on
clinical compositions weakens downstream traceability, indexing, and
record linkage. If NCHS already has enough information to produce a
Coded Cause of Fetal Death Composition, it almost always has enough to
identify the subject (the fetus/decedent record on the VRO side); the
"dummy reference" workaround the reporter mentions is a symptom of
under-specified guidance, not of an over-strict cardinality. This
disposition trades short-term implementer convenience for long-term
data-quality and conformance benefits.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change and keep `Composition.subject` at 1..1 on the Coded
Cause of Fetal Death Composition. Document the existing workaround
(populate `Reference.display` with an identifying string when no
resolvable subject reference is available) as the supported pattern in
the IG's NCHS→VRO guidance, rather than altering profile cardinalities
mid-cycle.

#### Justification

`Composition.subject` is `1..1` in the base FHIR Composition resource;
relaxing it in a profile is unusual and may surprise implementers who
rely on the base-resource contract. If the workgroup judges that the
inconsistency is cosmetic and the workaround already in production is
acceptable, declining preserves the strongest possible conformance
constraint and avoids any regeneration / re-publication cost during a
ballot cycle. This is the most conservative option but is hard to
defend against the WG's own "Persuasive" resolution.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The work group has already resolved this ticket as **Persuasive** with
the explicit comment "Agree with the change. Will update cardinality,"
which directly endorses Disposition A. The change is internally
consistent (it brings the third Composition into alignment with the two
siblings), backward-compatible (relaxing 1..1 → 0..1 never invalidates
existing conforming instances), and removes a documented "dummy
reference" workaround that has no semantic value. Disposition B is
defensible on data-quality grounds but would require new guidance,
likely new examples, and pushes work back onto NCHS implementers
without an articulated use case driving it; Disposition C contradicts
the WG's own resolution and ossifies the inconsistency. Workgroup
review should confirm Disposition A and proceed with the cardinality
update on the Coded Cause of Fetal Death Composition.
