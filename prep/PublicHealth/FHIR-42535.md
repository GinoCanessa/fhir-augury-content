## Summary

This ticket asks the BSeR (US Bidirectional Services eReferrals) authors
to clarify exactly which FHIR resources are exchanged at each step of
the referral choreography, with a specific worked example for the
"Notify of Referral" step in the "Referral – Notify and Pull"
interaction. The reporter, Sarah Gaunt, raises two related questions:
(1) at the Notify step, should the sender transmit only the `Task` that
represents the notification, or should it also transmit the
`ServiceRequest` that the `Task` is acting on; and (2) when a resource
is sent, should all of its referenced resources (e.g.,
`Patient`, `Practitioner`, `Organization`, supporting `Observation`s,
`Condition`s, etc.) travel with it as a Bundle, or only the root
resource? The reporter explicitly notes that the same ambiguity should
be reviewed and resolved across all use case steps in the IG, not only
the Notify step. The request is documentation/IG-text in nature
(triaged Question) rather than a structural change to a profile or
operation. No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip discussions, or
GitHub items were surfaced via the augury index for this key.

## Proposed Dispositions

> "Referral – Notify and Pull" and the per-step contents tables)

> chosen disposition, a follow-up should resolve the BSeR IG
> repository against the `repo-analysis` cache and append the file

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat this as a documentation defect and update the BSeR IG to spell
out, for every step of every use-case interaction (including but not
limited to "Notify of Referral" in "Referral – Notify and Pull"),
exactly which FHIR resources are transmitted and what their reference
closure looks like. Concretely, for the Notify of Referral step,
state that the sender transmits a Bundle containing the `Task` (with
`Task.focus` referencing the `ServiceRequest`) **and** the referenced
`ServiceRequest`, plus the closure of resources the `ServiceRequest`
itself references that are required to act on the referral
(`Patient`, `Practitioner`/`PractitionerRole`,
`Organization`, and any supporting clinical resources mandated by the
relevant BSeR profiles). Add a per-step "Contents" subsection to each
use-case page in the IG using a consistent table (Resource | Profile
| Cardinality | Notes) so the same ambiguity cannot recur in another
step. Mirror the same table on the receiver side ("what the receiver
must be prepared to consume").

#### Justification

The label `BSeR_NOTIFY_SEND_CONTENTS` and the reporter's explicit
"Check resources sent in all steps" instruction indicate this is a
known IG-wide gap, not a one-off question. FHIR best practice for
workflow patterns (see the Workflow module's guidance on `Task` and
`Task.focus`) is to transmit `Task` together with the resource it
references when the receiver needs to act on it; sending `Task` alone
forces the receiver into a separate fetch round-trip that BSeR's
"Notify and Pull" pattern explicitly tries to avoid for the notify
leg. Documenting the closure also satisfies implementers' need for
deterministic message contents and aligns BSeR with peer Public
Health IGs that publish per-step contents tables.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Instead of enumerating contents step-by-step in narrative tables,
formalize the answer by defining (or referencing) **MessageDefinition
/ Bundle profile artifacts** for each BSeR interaction step. For
"Notify of Referral", publish a Bundle profile that constrains the
entries to a `Task` (BSeR Notify profile) plus its referenced
`ServiceRequest` (BSeR Referral profile) and the minimum required
supporting resources, with explicit cardinalities. Use
`Bundle.entry.resource` slicing to nail down what MUST, SHOULD, and
MAY appear, and reference these Bundle profiles from the use-case
narrative rather than describing contents in prose. For "Pull"
steps, define the corresponding `searchset` / response Bundle shape
analogously. Apply the pattern uniformly across all use-case steps.

#### Justification

Narrative tables (Disposition A) are easy to drift out of sync with
the conformance artifacts and cannot be validated mechanically.
Bundle/MessageDefinition profiles make the contents
machine-checkable, give implementers a concrete validation target,
and keep the IG narrative honest because the prose simply cites the
profile. The trade-off is heavier authoring effort and a steeper
learning curve for IG readers; however, BSeR is a workflow-heavy IG
where deterministic payloads matter more than narrative brevity, and
the wider FHIR community is moving toward profiled exchange Bundles
for exactly this reason.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as "Not a defect – existing guidance applies": point the
reporter at FHIR's general Workflow module guidance on `Task` and
`Task.focus`, note that BSeR already profiles the relevant `Task`
and `ServiceRequest`, and decline to add per-step contents tables
or new Bundle profiles. Resolve as `Persuasive with Modification`
only to the extent of adding a single sentence at the top of the
"Referral – Notify and Pull" page saying "Senders SHALL include the
`Task` and any resources it references via `Task.focus` and
`Task.for`."

#### Justification

The question can be answered from existing FHIR core specifications:
`Task.focus` defines the resource the task is acting on, and
standard reference-resolution rules already say a receiver may need
the referenced resource to process the `Task`. Adding per-step
tables or Bundle profiles to every interaction expands the IG
surface area substantially for what is, in this framing, a reading
question rather than a missing constraint. Declining keeps the IG
lean and avoids locking BSeR into a contents enumeration that may
need to change as profiles evolve.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A is the right starting point because the reporter's own
framing ("Check resources sent in all steps") and the dedicated
`BSeR_NOTIFY_SEND_CONTENTS` label make clear the workgroup has
already acknowledged this as an IG-wide narrative gap, not a
core-FHIR reading question. Disposition C underestimates how much
real-world implementer confusion comes from BSeR's choreography
prose not naming the resources on the wire at each step;
implementers consistently report that "look it up in the Workflow
module" is not a workable substitute for an IG that explicitly lists
its message contents. Disposition B is technically the strongest
long-term answer and should be kept on the table as a follow-on
work item, but committing to Bundle profiles for every step is a
significantly larger lift than the ticket asks for and risks
delaying the simple narrative fix the reporter actually requested.
The pragmatic path is to land Disposition A in the next BSeR
publication cycle (per-step "Contents" tables driven from the
existing Task/ServiceRequest profiles, applied uniformly across all
use-case steps) and open a separate, scoped ticket to evaluate
Disposition B's Bundle/MessageDefinition profiling for a future
version.
