## Summary

The submitter (David Pyke) reports that the SANER IG's Transactions section
contains requirements that are intrinsic to an actor rather than to a specific
transaction. In particular, the wording "The MeasureDefinitionSource SHALL
demonstrate the FHIR read operation on the Measure, Library, ValueSet,
CodeSystem and ConceptMap resources." is framed as a transaction-level SHALL,
but it is really a capability the `MeasureDefinitionSource` actor must
provide regardless of any one transaction. The request is to relocate (and
rephrase) such actor-scoped obligations out of the Transactions section and
into the corresponding actor definition / requirements sections, leaving the
Transactions section to describe only what each transaction itself does. The
ticket has been marked **Persuasive**, with a noted resolution direction of
"adjust language to not be an actor requirement but a transaction
requirement" — i.e., reframe the existing wording so it correctly belongs in
the section it sits in. No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or
GitHub items were surfaced for this ticket.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat this as an editorial restructure of the SANER IG. In the
Transactions section, remove conformance statements that constrain an
actor's intrinsic capabilities (rather than the transaction's behavior),
and move them — rephrased in actor terms — into the corresponding actor
definition / requirements section.

Concretely, for the example called out in the ticket:

- Remove from the relevant Transactions section: "The
  MeasureDefinitionSource SHALL demonstrate the FHIR read operation on
  the Measure, Library, ValueSet, CodeSystem and ConceptMap resources."
- Add to the `MeasureDefinitionSource` actor's requirements / capability
  description: "A MeasureDefinitionSource SHALL support the FHIR `read`
  interaction on `Measure`, `Library`, `ValueSet`, `CodeSystem`, and
  `ConceptMap` resources." Reflect this in the actor's CapabilityStatement
  (interactions on those resources marked as required).
- Audit the remaining Transactions section narrative for any other
  "actor SHALL …" statements that describe steady-state actor capability
  rather than per-transaction behavior, and migrate those the same way.
- Leave behind in the Transactions section only conformance statements
  that describe what the transaction itself does (request payload,
  response payload, error handling, sequencing), referencing the actor
  requirements where appropriate.

#### Justification

This matches the IHE-style actor/transaction model that SANER follows:
actor definitions express the capabilities an implementation must
provide, and transactions express the message exchanges between actors.
Mixing the two in the Transactions section makes the conformance surface
ambiguous (does a system that never participates in this transaction
still need `read` on those resources?) and makes the actor's
CapabilityStatement incomplete. The ticket reporter is the original
author of the comment, the WG previously voted 18-0-0 on a related
disposition, and the recorded resolution direction is to "adjust
language" — all consistent with treating this as a corrective editorial
move rather than a substantive change of obligation.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than physically moving text, add an explicit normative cross-link
pattern: keep the SHALL statements where they currently sit in the
Transactions section, but introduce a one-time editorial convention at
the top of the Transactions section stating that any "actor SHALL …"
clause in a transaction is also a binding requirement on that actor's
CapabilityStatement, and mirror each such clause as a non-normative
"see also" pointer in the actor section. Update the actor
CapabilityStatement(s) to include the implied interactions explicitly,
so that conformance tooling sees the requirement even if the prose
remains in Transactions.

#### Justification

This preserves the existing section structure (lower diff churn,
lower risk of broken inbound links and ballot regressions) while
addressing the underlying complaint that the actor's obligations are
discoverable only by reading transactions. The CapabilityStatement
update means downstream validators and IG publisher checks see the
correct actor capability set even if the narrative is left in place.
Trade-off versus A: the Transactions section continues to carry
actor-scoped SHALLs, which keeps the structural smell the reporter
flagged; reviewers who care about clean separation of concerns may
view this as a half-measure.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as "Not Persuasive — editorial preference." Leave the existing
wording in the Transactions section unchanged, on the basis that the
SHALL is unambiguous to implementers (the
`MeasureDefinitionSource` actor is named in the statement, so its
obligation is clear regardless of which section the sentence sits in)
and that further restructuring risks breaking inbound references and
existing implementer mental models post-STU1 publication.

#### Justification

A Decline disposition would be defensible only if the WG concludes
that (a) the current wording, while imperfect, is not actually causing
implementer confusion, and (b) the editorial cost of restructuring
across the IG outweighs the clarity benefit. However, the ticket has
already been marked **Persuasive**, the prior "Considered for Future
Use" disposition was explicitly **reverted**, and the reporter is
naming a structural defect rather than a stylistic one — so this
disposition exists primarily to make the trade-off visible, not as a
strong recommendation.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is already flagged Persuasive on the Jira record and the WG
previously walked back a "Considered for Future Use" disposition,
signalling that the group wants this addressed rather than deferred
again. Disposition A directly fixes the structural complaint by putting
actor capabilities in the actor section and leaving the Transactions
section to describe transactions, which is the model SANER inherits
from the IHE-style actor/transaction framing. Disposition B leaves the
actor obligations embedded in transaction prose, which preserves the
exact issue the reporter raised; Disposition C contradicts the existing
Persuasive marking. The work should be scoped as a focused editorial
pass over the Transactions section to identify all "actor SHALL …"
sentences, plus a corresponding update to each affected actor's
requirements text and CapabilityStatement so the conformance surface
moves with the prose.
