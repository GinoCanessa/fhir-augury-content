## Summary

The submitter (Mitra Rocca) reports that the Electronic Case Reporting
(eCR) FHIR IG does not clearly tell public health systems and downstream
consumers how to discover and retrieve `Provenance` resources associated
with reported clinical content, and how that discovery should be
coordinated with metadata indicators that flag special handling or
derivation (e.g., automation, decision support, or AI-assisted
generation). The ticket asks the IG to add explicit Public
Health-oriented guidance that (a) recommends `Provenance` as the
representation of how case-report data was generated, curated, or
automated; (b) calls out FHIR `_revinclude=Provenance:target` as the
recommended, US Core-aligned mechanism for retrieving reported data and
its provenance together; (c) clarifies that metadata indicators such as
security/process labels — including those aligned with the AI
Transparency IG — are *discovery signals* that provenance exists, not
substitutes for `Provenance` itself; and (d) adds at least one worked
public-health example (e.g., retrieving `Observation`s from an eICR
`Bundle` together with their `Provenance`, such as
`GET [base]/Observation?patient={id}&_revinclude=Provenance:target`).
The submitter suggests the new content can live in General Guidance
§3.2.11 Search Syntax, in the Security page as a new §8.2 "Provenance
Discovery and Retrieval", or both. No cross-referenced Jira tickets,
Zulip threads, or GitHub items were surfaced for this ticket.

## Details

**Description:**

The IG does not clearly describe how public health systems and
downstream consumers are expected to discover and retrieve `Provenance`
associated with reported clinical resources, nor how provenance
discovery should be coordinated with metadata indicators that signal
special handling or derivation (e.g., automation, decision support, or
AI-assisted generation).

Please add explicit Public Health-oriented guidance that:

- recommends `Provenance` be used for representing how case-report data
  was generated, curated, or automated;
- explicitly mentions that FHIR search using
  `_revinclude=Provenance:target` is a recommended and supported
  mechanism for public health systems to retrieve reported data and the
  associated provenance together, consistent with US Core capabilities.
  This explicit mention can be included on the General Guidance in
  §3.2.11 Search Syntax, or in the Security page under a new section
  §8.2 "Provenance Discovery and Retrieval".

Metadata indicators (e.g., security or process labels, including those
aligned with the [AI Transparency
IG](https://hl7.org/fhir/uv/aitransparency/2026Jan/index.html)) should
be treated as discovery signals that provenance exists, not as a
substitute for `Provenance` itself.

Please add at least one example demonstrating this pattern explicitly
in a public-health context (e.g., retrieving `Observation`s from an
eICR `Bundle` along with their `Provenance`). For example:

```
GET [base]/Observation?patient={id}&_revinclude=Provenance:target
```

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add explicit Public Health-oriented guidance to the eCR FHIR IG that
covers Provenance discovery and retrieval, in two coordinated places:

1. **General Guidance → §3.2.11 Search Syntax.** Add a short
   subsection ("Retrieving Provenance with reported data") stating
   that `Provenance` is the canonical representation of how case-report
   content was generated, curated, or automated, and that
   `_revinclude=Provenance:target` is the recommended, US
   Core-consistent mechanism for retrieving reported clinical
   resources together with their `Provenance` in a single request.
   Include the worked example exactly as supplied by the submitter:

   ```
   GET [base]/Observation?patient={id}&_revinclude=Provenance:target
   ```

   plus a parallel example against an eICR `Bundle` (e.g., resolving
   `Observation`s inside an eICR `Bundle` and their `Provenance`).
2. **Security page → new §8.2 "Provenance Discovery and Retrieval".**
   Add a section that (a) restates the `_revinclude` retrieval
   pattern, (b) enumerates the metadata indicators consumers may see
   (security labels, process labels, and labels aligned with the AI
   Transparency IG at
   `https://hl7.org/fhir/uv/aitransparency/2026Jan/index.html`), and
   (c) makes it explicit that those indicators are *discovery
   signals* that `Provenance` exists for the tagged resource — they
   are **not** a substitute for retrieving the `Provenance`
   resource(s).

Cross-link the two sections so a reader landing on either reaches the
other.

#### Justification

This is precisely what the submitter asked for and aligns with
established FHIR R4/R5 patterns: `_revinclude=Provenance:target` is the
documented US Core mechanism for "give me the resource and its
provenance in one call", so adopting it in eCR keeps public-health
implementers on a well-trodden path. Treating security/process labels
as discovery signals (rather than as a substitute for `Provenance`)
matches the FHIR core position that labels convey handling semantics,
not the audit trail. Naming the AI Transparency IG explicitly future-
proofs eCR for AI-assisted generation of case-report content without
requiring eCR to itself define those labels.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Address the same underlying need, but consolidate the new content into
a **single new normative-style section in General Guidance** —
"Provenance for Reported Data" — instead of splitting between General
Guidance §3.2.11 and a new Security §8.2. Specifically:

1. Add **General Guidance → "Provenance for Reported Data"** with three
   subsections: (a) when `Provenance` SHALL/SHOULD be supplied by
   senders for case-report content; (b) the `_revinclude=Provenance:
   target` retrieval pattern with the submitter's example; (c) the
   relationship between security/process/AI-Transparency labels and
   `Provenance` (labels = discovery signals, `Provenance` = the audit
   record).
2. From §3.2.11 Search Syntax and from the Security page, add **only
   short pointer paragraphs** linking to the consolidated section.
3. Add the eICR-`Bundle`-flavoured worked example to the eCR Examples
   page (rather than embedding it inside General Guidance), so the
   pattern shows up alongside other end-to-end retrieval examples
   readers already consult.
4. If feasible without scope creep, add a `CapabilityStatement`
   expectation that public-health Receivers SHOULD support
   `_revinclude=Provenance:target` for the resource types that
   participate in case reporting (e.g., `Observation`, `Condition`,
   `MedicationRequest`, `Patient`, `Encounter`).

#### Justification

Splitting the same idea across General Guidance and Security risks
drift: future editors update one location and not the other, and
implementers must read both to assemble the rule. A single
"Provenance for Reported Data" section keeps the conformance language
authoritative in one place while still reachable from §3.2.11 and the
Security page via pointers. Putting the worked example on the Examples
page matches how readers actually navigate the IG. The optional
`CapabilityStatement` assertion converts the guidance from prose-only
into something testable by Inferno-style validators, which is more
durable than narrative text alone. The trade-off vs. Disposition A is
that this is a slightly larger edit (touches Examples and possibly the
CapabilityStatement) and may need a second pass with the workgroup to
ratify the SHOULD-level conformance.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request as **already addressed by existing FHIR/US Core
mechanisms** and out of scope for net-new eCR IG sections. Specifically,
respond that:

- `_revinclude=Provenance:target` is a base FHIR search capability,
  documented in US Core's Provenance guidance, and eCR Receivers that
  conform to US Core inherit that capability without restating it.
- Security and process labels are defined in FHIR core and in the AI
  Transparency IG; redefining their relationship to `Provenance` in eCR
  risks divergence from those normative sources.
- The eCR IG is large; adding new General Guidance and Security
  sections for a pattern that is already discoverable elsewhere
  increases maintenance burden without changing implementer behaviour.

If accepted, close as "Persuasive with Modifier" with a pointer to the
US Core Provenance guidance and the AI Transparency IG.

#### Justification

Defensible only if the workgroup believes the existing US Core +
core-FHIR coverage is enough for public-health implementers to
discover the pattern unaided. The risk is real-world: implementers
report (per this very ticket) that they cannot find the guidance in
the eCR IG, which suggests the inherited material is not actually
discoverable in context. Decline is also weak because the submitter's
ask about *AI Transparency labels as discovery signals, not
substitutes* is a genuinely new clarification specific to eCR's
public-health workflows — that is not stated anywhere upstream today.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B delivers exactly what the submitter is asking for —
explicit, eCR-specific guidance on Provenance discovery, the
`_revinclude=Provenance:target` retrieval pattern, the worked
public-health example, and the "labels are discovery signals, not
substitutes" clarification — while avoiding the maintenance hazard of
duplicating the same rule across General Guidance §3.2.11 and Security
§8.2. Consolidating into a single "Provenance for Reported Data"
section with pointers from §3.2.11 and the Security page keeps the
conformance language authoritative in one place, and putting the
worked example on the Examples page matches how implementers actually
navigate the IG. The optional `CapabilityStatement` SHOULD on
`_revinclude=Provenance:target` is the part most worth debating in
the workgroup: it is what turns this from advisory prose into
something Inferno can test, but it also means signing eCR Receivers
up to a concrete capability — which is exactly the kind of decision
the workgroup should make rather than the editor. Disposition A is a
reasonable fallback if the workgroup prefers the submitter's exact
two-location placement; Disposition C is not recommended because the
submitter's report itself is evidence that the pattern is not
discoverable in eCR today.
