## Summary

Malcolm Clarke reports that Section 3.3.1 of the Personal Healthcare
Devices (PHD) FHIR IG mis-states the primary motivation for using a
float-based numeric encoding. The current text reads "The primary reason
for this encoding is to indicate the precision of the measurement,"
which inverts the actual rationale: the principal reason is to support
a wide *range* of values, with precision indication being a secondary
(but still important) benefit. The ticket asks the Devices Work Group
to correct the wording so it reflects both motivations and assigns them
the right relative weight. No related Jira tickets, GitHub items, or
Zulip threads were surfaced by the cross-reference index — only the
Devices WG minutes from 2025-11-12 (where the issue was discussed and
deferred) are linked.

## Details

**Description:**

> Section 3.3.1. "The primary reason for this encoding is to indicate
> the precision of the measurement" - actually the reason for using a
> float representation is to allow for a wide range of values. It can
> also be used to indicate precision.

**Comments:**

- **erikmoll (2026-01-28):** Proposes a concrete rewording — "Reasons
  for this encoding are that it supports a wide range of values and

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt Erik Moll's proposed rewording for Section 3.3.1 of the Personal
Healthcare Devices (FHIR) IG. Replace:

> The primary reason for this encoding is to indicate the precision of
> the measurement.

with:

> Reasons for this encoding are that it supports a wide range of values
> and that it indicates the precision of the measurement.

Apply the change in the next revision of the PHD IG (post STU2, as
agreed by the Devices WG on 2025-11-12). This is a documentation-only
edit to the IG narrative for §3.3.1; no resource, profile, extension,
or value-set changes are implied.

#### Justification

The reporter is the original author of much of the IEEE 11073 device
specialisation work that the PHD IG mirrors, and the correction
matches the well-established rationale for MDER float / IEEE 11073
float encoding: dynamic range first, precision indication second.
Erik Moll's rewording preserves the precision point that downstream
implementers rely on while restoring the primary motivation. The
Devices WG has already discussed this and informally agreed it should
land in the next revision, so accepting as requested is consistent
with the recorded WG position. As a Technical Correction with no
normative artifact change, the risk to implementers is negligible.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the substance of the correction but expand the rewrite slightly
to make the two motivations explicit and to tie the precision aspect
to the IEEE 11073 MDER float semantics that PHD inherits. Suggested
text for §3.3.1:

> A float representation is used here for two reasons. First, it
> supports the wide dynamic range of values that personal health
> device measurements can produce (from sub-microvolt signals to
> multi-thousand-unit readings) without overflow or scaling tricks.
> Second, when used correctly (e.g., preserving the source mantissa
> and exponent rather than rounding through an intermediate decimal),
> it conveys the precision the device actually reported.

Bundle this edit with a one-line editorial pass over any neighbouring
sentences in §3.3.1 that still imply precision is the sole or primary
driver, so the section is internally consistent.

#### Justification

The literal rewording in Disposition A fixes the headline sentence
but leaves the surrounding paragraph open to the same misreading,
because the original section was structured around precision as the
*reason*. A slightly longer rewrite costs the WG nothing (still
narrative-only, no schema impact) and removes a class of follow-up
tickets from implementers who quote §3.3.1 out of context. The
trade-off vs. A is a small amount of additional editorial review;
the upside is that implementers who only skim §3.3.1 take away the
correct two-part rationale.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as "Not Persuasive" / "No Change," on the grounds
that the existing sentence is not technically incorrect — precision
*is* a reason for the float encoding — and that re-ordering the
stated motivations does not change the on-the-wire representation or
any conformance requirement.

#### Justification

PHD STU2 has shipped with the current wording, no implementer has
filed a conformance issue caused by it, and the change is purely
editorial. Reopening narrative text post-publication carries a small
but non-zero cost (publication mechanics, regression review of any
translations or derivative profiles) for a benefit that is essentially
stylistic.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The reporter's underlying point is correct and the Devices WG has
already agreed in principle (per the 2025-11-12 minutes) that the
wording should change in the next PHD revision, so Disposition C is
hard to defend on the merits — it would also leave a known incorrect
statement standing in a published IG. Between A and B, Erik Moll's
one-sentence fix (A) is safe and low-effort, but it patches a single
sentence inside a paragraph that was originally written around the
wrong premise. Disposition B accepts Erik's substance, reuses his
phrasing as the core, and additionally normalises the surrounding
prose so the section reads coherently. Because this is a
documentation-only Technical Correction queued for the post-STU2
revision, the marginal editorial cost of B over A is negligible and
the result is more durable. The WG should treat A as the acceptable
fallback if reviewers prefer the minimal change.
