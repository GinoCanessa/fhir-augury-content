## Summary

FHIR-53349 is a Technical Correction filed against the Personal Healthcare
Devices (PHD) FHIR IG, Section 2.8. The reporter (Malcolm Clarke) observes
that the IG handles "relative time" inconsistently: in Section 2.8 a
relative time is coded as a `Quantity`, while elsewhere in the IG the same
underlying concept (an IEEE 11073-10206 / MDS Time Information attribute
expressing a time value) is mapped onto a UTC `dateTime`/`instant`.
Implementers must therefore choose between two different FHIR
representations of the same conceptual value depending on which section
they read, which complicates implementation, validation, and
interoperability between PHD-conformant systems.

The ticket was originally resolved as "Considered for Future Use" and then
re-opened by the work group after STU2 publication; the work group has
deferred any normative change to the next revision of the PHD IG (per the
2025-11-12 Devices Main Call). No Zulip threads or GitHub PRs/issues
linking to this ticket were surfaced in the indexed corpus, so the
disposition discussion is largely confined to the Devices work group call
record.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply a technical correction to PHD IG Section 2.8 so that "relative time"
is represented the same way as in the other sections of the IG (i.e., as
a UTC-based `dateTime`/`instant` mapped through the IG's existing time
profiles such as `PhdCoincidentTimeStampObservation` and the
absolute-time companion observations), rather than as a `Quantity`.
Concretely:

- Rewrite Section 2.8 (PHD `Time` attribute mapping) so the canonical
  representation of a Mds-Time-Info reading is the UTC-anchored
  `dateTime` already used elsewhere in the IG.
- Remove or demote the `Quantity` form to a non-normative example, or
  delete it outright if it is not referenced by any profile / example /
  validation rule.
- Touch the IG narrative under the PHD authoring root (the section 2.x
  pages in `HL7/uv-pocd`) and re-render examples that relied on the
  `Quantity` form.

#### Justification

The reporter's complaint is about internal inconsistency, not about
missing capability. Aligning Section 2.8 with the rest of the IG removes
the ambiguity in one direction (the direction the rest of the IG already
takes) and is the smallest change that satisfies the literal text of the
ticket. It also reduces validator surprises for implementers who follow
the rest of the IG's conventions and then hit a different shape in 2.8.
Because the work group has already deferred the change to the next PHD
revision, this fits cleanly into a Technical Correction in that revision
without disturbing STU2.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Treat the inconsistency as semantic, not just editorial: keep both
representations but disambiguate them by use case. Specifically:

- Use `dateTime`/`instant` (UTC) wherever the device reports an
  **absolute** wall-clock-anchored time (the existing pattern used
  outside Section 2.8).
- Use `Quantity` (with UCUM `s`/`ms` and an explicit reference epoch
  documented in narrative — e.g., "seconds since device power-on" or
  "offset from a coincident absolute timestamp") wherever the device
  legitimately produces a **relative** time that has no UTC anchor (the
  IEEE 11073-10206 "Relative-Time" / "HiRes-Relative-Time" attribute
  family).
- Update Section 2.8 to spell out which IEEE 11073 source attributes map
  to which FHIR shape, and update any other section that conflated the
  two so that absolute-time mappings stay on `dateTime` and relative-time
  mappings stay on `Quantity`. Add a profile-level invariant or slice
  guidance on `Observation.effective[x]` /
  `PhdCoincidentTimeStampObservation` that codifies the split.

#### Justification

In the underlying IEEE 11073-10206 model, "relative time" and "absolute
time" are genuinely different attributes: a relative time has no UTC
anchor and is meaningful only against a coincident absolute timestamp or
device boot reference. Forcing it onto a UTC `dateTime` (Disposition A)
loses information and risks implementers fabricating a UTC value, which
is precisely the kind of failure mode the IG should prevent. A `Quantity`
with UCUM seconds is the FHIR-idiomatic way to carry an offset, so the
right fix may be to *embrace* the dual representation but document the
boundary clearly. This is more work than Disposition A but is more
faithful to the source standard.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as "Not Persuasive — already addressed." Keep Section
2.8 as-is on the basis that the `Quantity` mapping in Section 2.8 and the
`dateTime` mapping elsewhere are intentionally describing different IEEE
11073 attributes (Relative-Time vs. Absolute-Time), not the same
attribute mapped two ways, and that the existing narrative — together
with `PhdCoincidentTimeStampObservation` — is sufficient to disambiguate
them.

#### Justification

If review of Section 2.8 confirms that it is mapping the
**Relative-Time** attribute (which has no UTC semantics) and the other
sections are mapping **Absolute-Time** / wall-clock attributes, then
there is no real inconsistency — only two different source attributes
mapped to two different FHIR data types, as is standard practice
elsewhere in FHIR. In that case the ticket is best closed without
normative change, perhaps with an editorial sentence in 2.8 cross-linking
to the absolute-time section so that future readers do not raise the
same concern.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The reporter's framing ("relative time is coded as a quantity whereas
previously mapped to UTC") strongly suggests they see the two
representations as describing the same concept, which is exactly the
implementer confusion the IG should resolve — so Disposition C
("nothing to fix") is unsafe even if technically defensible. Disposition
A removes the inconsistency cheaply but does so by erasing the legitimate
distinction between IEEE 11073 Relative-Time and Absolute-Time
attributes, which would force implementers to invent UTC values for
relative readings and would silently lose information at the FHIR
boundary. Disposition B keeps both representations, anchors each one to a
distinct IEEE 11073 source attribute, and uses Section 2.8 itself as the
place to document the split — which directly addresses the reporter's
"not consistent with other section" concern without sacrificing
fidelity to the underlying device standard. Because the work group has
already deferred this to the next PHD IG revision (per the 2025-11-12
call), there is room in scope for the slightly larger editorial-plus-
profile work that B implies.
