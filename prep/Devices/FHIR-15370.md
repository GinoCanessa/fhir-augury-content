## Summary

The ticket reports a single-word typographical omission in the
"Traceability and Provenance" section of the Point of Care Devices (PoCD)
FHIR specification. The existing wording reads "Using the DIM allows
tracking down an observation the precise instrument, module, and channel
that originated it," which is missing the preposition "to" between
"observation" and "the." The reporter proposes inserting "to" so the
sentence reads "...allows tracking down an observation **to** the precise
instrument, module, and channel that originated it." The change is
purely editorial / non-substantive (the labels include `R1NFAN`,
indicating R1 Non-Substantive). The ticket was originally resolved as
"Considered for Future Use" in 2018, but that resolution was reverted in
April 2026 and the item has been re-triaged for the upcoming September
ballot. A reviewer has additionally noted that the relevant section has
been substantially rewritten since 2018, which may mean the original
sentence no longer exists in the current specification text.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the editorial fix to the PoCD "Traceability and Provenance"
narrative: insert the word "to" so the sentence reads "Using the DIM
allows tracking down an observation **to** the precise instrument,
module, and channel that originated it." First confirm the sentence still
exists in the current PoCD source (per erikmoll's comment, the section
may have been rewritten); if so, edit the corresponding narrative file
in the Devices PoCD specification source. Mark the change as
non-substantive / R1NFAN consistent with the existing label and the
2018 vote (Reinhold/Faughn 9-0-0).

#### Justification

The change is unambiguously a typo fix that improves readability without
altering meaning, normative content, conformance, or any computable
artifact. It carries no implementer impact and was already approved
non-substantive in 2018. Resolving it now keeps the September ballot
content clean and discharges a long-standing low-cost item, consistent
with the Devices WG agreement on 2026-04-08.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Treat the ticket as **Not Persuasive — No Change Required (Already
Addressed)**: verify against the current PoCD narrative whether the
original 2018 sentence still exists. If the "Traceability and Provenance"
section has been rewritten such that the offending sentence is gone (as
erikmoll observed on 2026-03-25), close the ticket with a short
disposition note quoting the current wording and citing the rewrite
commit/PR rather than editing.

#### Justification

The ticket is eight years old and the underlying text has demonstrably
moved on. Spending review cycles re-applying a fix that may no longer be
applicable risks introducing a phantom edit or, worse, regressing the
newer wording. Verifying the current text once and closing the ticket as
"already addressed" is faster than carrying it through ballot machinery
and is faithful to the reporter's intent (the typo is, by hypothesis, no
longer present).

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change as out of scope for substantive ballot review:
restore (or leave) a "Considered for Future Use" / "Persuasive with Mod"
posture and bundle this with any other R1NFAN editorial items into a
batch editorial pass outside the September ballot cycle.

#### Justification

This is the weakest option: the request is plainly correct and trivial
to apply, and the work group has already explicitly resolved (on
2026-04-08) to address it for the September ballot. Declining would
contradict that decision and leave a known typo in the published
specification with no operational benefit. Included only because the
skill requires three options.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B (with a fallback to A)

The work group should first verify, against the current PoCD source,
whether the 2018 sentence still exists. Erik Moll's 2026-03-25 comment
asserts the section was "fully rewritten since 2018," which makes
**Disposition B (No Change Required — Already Addressed)** the most
honest outcome: close the ticket with a one-line disposition quoting the
current wording. If, on inspection, the sentence (or a near-equivalent
missing-"to" construction) is still present, fall back to **Disposition
A (Accept As Requested)** and apply the one-word editorial fix as
non-substantive, consistent with the original 2018 vote and the
2026-04-08 Devices call decision. Either way, the ticket should be
closed before the September ballot; declining (Disposition C) would
contradict the work group's standing direction and is not defensible.
