## Summary

Malcolm Clarke (reporter) flags that Section 3.3 of the Personal Healthcare
Devices (PHD) FHIR IG states that the GHS (Generic Health Sensor) specification
supports an optional **Observation Schedule descriptor**, and that its contents
can be mapped to `DeviceMetric.measurementPeriod`. The ticket raises two
distinct clarity problems with that statement:

1. It is not clear *how* the Observation Schedule descriptor content is
   actually meant to be used once mapped to `DeviceMetric.measurementPeriod`
   (i.e., what a consumer of the resulting `DeviceMetric` should do with the
   mapped value, and how it relates to the observation timing it is intended
   to describe).
2. When a PHD device exposes multiple sensors, it is unclear in the GHS
   side which "sensor" the Observation Schedule descriptor applies to, so
   the mapping target on the FHIR side (which `DeviceMetric`, tied to which
   sensor) is ambiguous.

The request is scoped as a Technical Correction against the PHD IG narrative,
not a change to base FHIR resources. The original disposition was
*"Considered for Future Use"*; after Dev WG review on 2025-11-12 the resolution
was reverted and the ticket was **deferred to the next revision of the PHD
IG** (post-STU2) — the ticket is currently `Triaged` and Unresolved.

There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip discussions, or GitHub
items — the only cross-reference is to the 2025-11-12 Devices Main Call
minutes page on Confluence, captured in the resolution comment.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat this as a narrative clarification in Section 3.3 of the PHD IG (the
next revision, per the deferral). Rewrite the paragraph on the Observation
Schedule descriptor to:

1. State explicitly what the descriptor is (a GHS-side optional field
   describing the schedule on which observations are produced) and that
   its value maps to `DeviceMetric.measurementPeriod` (a `Timing`) on the
   `DeviceMetric` resource that represents the *metric* the schedule
   governs — not the `Device` as a whole.
2. Spell out the intended consumer behavior (e.g., "a receiver may use
   `DeviceMetric.measurementPeriod` to know the expected cadence of
   associated Observations and to detect missed samples").
3. Resolve the multi-sensor ambiguity by stating that when a GHS device
   exposes multiple sensors / metrics, the Observation Schedule descriptor
   is mapped to the `DeviceMetric.measurementPeriod` of the **specific
   `DeviceMetric` corresponding to the sensor the descriptor is attached
   to in the GHS model**, and give a short example (e.g., a multi-parameter
   monitor with separate SpO₂ and pulse-rate sensors ⇒ one `DeviceMetric`
   per sensor, each with its own `measurementPeriod`).
4. If GHS itself does not unambiguously scope the descriptor to a sensor
   in some profile, state that explicitly and mark the mapping as
   "only applicable when the descriptor is scoped to a single sensor in
   the source GHS data" so implementers don't silently guess.

#### Justification

The reporter is describing a documentation ambiguity, not a model defect,
so a narrative fix is the minimum change that actually resolves the
concern. The Dev WG has already decided to defer this to the next PHD IG
revision, which is consistent with treating it as editorial / clarifying.
Resolving the multi-sensor ambiguity via an explicit
per-`DeviceMetric` mapping rule aligns with how `DeviceMetric` is used
elsewhere in the IG (one `DeviceMetric` per measured quantity/sensor) and
avoids introducing any new extension or element.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than only rewording Section 3.3, use this ticket as the hook to
introduce a small structured **Observation Schedule mapping table** in
the PHD IG that enumerates, for each GHS Observation Schedule descriptor
field, the exact target on the FHIR side. Concretely:

- Add a mapping table near Section 3.3 with columns: *GHS descriptor
  field* → *FHIR target (resource.element)* → *Applies to (Device vs.
  DeviceMetric-per-sensor)* → *Notes*.
- For the schedule-period field, target
  `DeviceMetric.measurementPeriod` and mark *Applies to* as
  "per-sensor `DeviceMetric`".
- For any schedule sub-field that does not have a clean FHIR target
  (e.g., schedule offset, phase, or activation conditions if present in
  GHS), either (a) map it to a listed extension on the same
  `DeviceMetric`, or (b) explicitly mark it "not mapped; preserve in the
  source GHS payload" so implementers know not to invent a target.
- Add a worked example of a multi-sensor device to the IG examples so
  the per-sensor resolution is concrete.

#### Justification

If the WG anticipates more GHS ↔ PHD mapping questions of this shape
(and the "multiple sensors" question in the ticket strongly suggests
they will), a structured mapping table is cheaper to maintain than
scattered narrative and makes future clarification tickets a matter of
filling in a row rather than negotiating prose. It also pre-empts the
most common failure mode in Disposition A: a narrative that is still
silent about the schedule sub-fields the reporter did not happen to
name. The cost is higher editorial effort now and a commitment to
maintain the table in subsequent revisions — but it is still entirely
an IG-narrative change, with no impact on base FHIR artifacts.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as *Not Persuasive / Already Addressed*: keep Section 3.3 as-is,
on the grounds that `DeviceMetric.measurementPeriod` is already the
documented and correct target for the Observation Schedule descriptor,
and that the multi-sensor case is implicitly handled by the general PHD
rule that each sensor is represented by its own `DeviceMetric` (so the
mapping naturally targets the `DeviceMetric` for the sensor the
descriptor is attached to).

#### Justification

The ticket does not allege an incorrect mapping — only that the
narrative is hard to interpret. If the WG considers the existing
`DeviceMetric` modeling guidance elsewhere in the IG sufficient to
answer the multi-sensor question, then the ticket reduces to a request
for nicer prose that may not justify churn on a section that is already
published in STU2. Declining would be defensible if (and only if) the
WG can point to the specific paragraph that already states the
per-sensor `DeviceMetric` rule.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter explicitly calls out two concrete ambiguities ("not sure
how this can be used" and "unclear … to which sensor"). Disposition C
leaves both unresolved and relies on the reader to stitch together the
per-sensor `DeviceMetric` rule from other sections — which is exactly
the inference the reporter could not make, so declining is hard to
defend. Disposition B is attractive but expands scope (a new mapping
table and a commitment to maintain it) beyond what the ticket asks for,
and the WG has already chosen to defer this to the next PHD IG revision
rather than to launch a broader mapping effort. Disposition A fits the
deferral decision cleanly: it is a targeted, editorial change to
Section 3.3 that (1) clarifies the intended consumer behavior of the
`DeviceMetric.measurementPeriod` mapping and (2) pins the multi-sensor
case to the per-sensor `DeviceMetric`, which is the smallest change
that actually answers the reporter's questions. The WG can always
upgrade to Disposition B later if similar clarification tickets
accumulate.
