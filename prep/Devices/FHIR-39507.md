## Summary

Javier Espina raised that the Point of Care Devices (POCD) FHIR IG does
not give implementers clear guidance on how device-data submitters
should handle Patient and Location resources. The RESTful Transfer page
covers generic FHIR transfer mechanics, but it does not say when a
device or gateway should create and include a Patient/Location resource
in the device-data bundle versus rely on a pointer (reference) to a
resource that already exists on the destination FHIR server, nor when
the latter is even possible. The reporter also calls out the need to
align with whatever Patient/Location profiles a particular hospital
mandates (e.g., US Core, IPA), and notes a potential overlap with O&O
WG workflow discussions ("Order Logical Model Application to FHIR").

The ticket is currently **Triaged** with a proposed resolution of
**Persuasive**: erikmoll has drafted a "Patient and Location Resource
Handling" section to add to the Profiles Overview, describing three
reference patterns (reference existing, conditional create in the
bundle, include in the bundle), minimum identity requirements for
Patient (MRN / facility identifier) and Location (bed label /
identifier), and a Conformance to Destination Profiles paragraph
pointing implementers at US Core / IPA. A preview of the change is
available on the FHIR-39507 build branch of `HL7/uv-pocd`.

No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were
returned by the orchestrator for this key.

## Details

**Description:**

> The RESTFUL transfer page describes some FHIR-generic options for
> transferring device-sourced data. However I could not find clear
> recommendation in the IG on the approach to follow regarding the
> Patient and Location resources. For instance, creating and including
> it in the device-data bundle vs. having a pointer to the resource on
> the destination FHIR server (when is the latter possible?). Another
> example is the alignment with additional resource profiles that a
> specific hospital may have. Such further guidance would probably
> require identifying some uses cases / deployment archetypes.
>
> This may be connected to ongoing discussions on workflow aspects at
> the O&O WG as well (#Order Logical Model Application to FHIR).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt erikmoll's drafted "Patient and Location Resource Handling"
section verbatim into the POCD IG Profiles Overview page
(`profiles.html`), as previewed on the `FHIR-39507` branch of
`HL7/uv-pocd`. The section adds:

1. **Reference Patterns** — three named patterns: *Reference existing
   resources* (search-by-identifier against the destination server,
   the enterprise default), *Conditional create in the bundle*
   (transaction entry with `ifNoneExist` keyed off MRN / unique
   Location name), and *Include in the bundle* (device is the source
   of truth — rare for Patient, plausible for Location in mobile /
   temporary setups).
2. **Minimum Identity Requirements** — Patient must carry at least an
   MRN or facility-specific identifier from ADT / device association;
   Location must carry at least a bed label or location identifier
   that can be matched on the destination server, with a pointer to
   `Device.location` on the MDS Device profile.
3. **Conformance to Destination Profiles** — explicit statement that
   the POCD IG does not profile Patient or Location for clinical use
   (existing Patient/Practitioner profiles are mapping-only) and that
   implementers must conform to whatever Patient/Location profiles
   their deployment requires (US Core, IPA, etc.); when the
   device/gateway creates resources via conditional create it should
   populate elements required by the destination's profiles to the
   extent possible.
4. **Future Work** — defers deployment archetypes and workflow
   integration (including device-observation ↔ clinical-order
   relationships) to a future edition.

Apply javier_espina's 2026-03-19 comment by cross-checking the Future
Work paragraph against the current POCD project scope statement and
trimming/aligning the wording before merge. Also fix the broken link
in the proposed text — the "RESTful Transfer" reference currently
points at a `vscode-resource://` URL and must be rewritten to the
relative `transfer.html#conditional-create` anchor inside the IG.

#### Justification

The drafted text directly answers each of the reporter's questions
(when to point vs. include, how to align with hospital profiles), is
consistent with how other reference IGs treat third-party-managed
resources, and is informative — not prescriptive — which matches both
esilver's 2022-11-28 framing ("approaches, pros and cons") and
mcros's WGM-Sept25 proposal to "add informative description of
different approaches." The reporter has already signalled the
proposed resolution is sufficient for the 1.x edition, so the WG can
land it now and use the deferred Future Work bullet to schedule the
deployment-archetype / workflow analysis later. The link/scope
fix-ups are editorial and do not change the substance.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the substance of erikmoll's draft, but restructure it to
explicitly incorporate mcros's WGM-Sept25 ask: add a short
"Related Specifications" subsection beneath Reference Patterns that
summarises and links to the **IHE PCD Real-Time Location Services
(RTLS) Profile** and **IHE MEM-LS (Medical Equipment Management,
Location Services)** as worked examples of how Location resources are
handled in adjacent device ecosystems, plus a one-line acknowledgement
of the in-progress O&O "Order Logical Model" work for the
device-observation ↔ order linkage. Move the "Future Work" bullet's
workflow-integration sentence into a `{% include note.html %}`-style
informative note that is timestamped to the 1.x edition, so the
forward reference is visible inline rather than buried at the end.
Optionally add a small "deployment archetype" decision table
(columns: *Patient known on server?*, *Location known on server?*,
*Device authoritative?* → recommended pattern) so implementers can
self-select a pattern at a glance.

#### Justification

This preserves Disposition A's content (and therefore the reporter's
sign-off) while addressing mcros's explicit WGM action item to point
implementers at the existing IHE work rather than re-invent it, and
honours esilver's "situations when they might be selected" framing by
making the choice criteria machine-checkable in a small table. The
trade-off is added editorial scope and a slight delay if the IHE
references need a careful read; against that, it removes a likely
future ballot comment ("why doesn't this acknowledge RTLS / MEM-LS?")
and reduces the chance the section is reopened in the 2.x cycle. It
also keeps the IG scope-respectful — nothing here profiles Patient or
Location, nothing here mandates a workflow.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket "Not Persuasive" / "Not Persuasive with Modification"
on the basis that handling of non-device resources (Patient, Location,
Encounter, Practitioner, Organization) is a cross-cutting deployment
concern already addressed by the FHIR core spec (transaction bundles,
conditional create / `ifNoneExist`, search-by-identifier) and by
deployment-specific IGs (US Core, IPA, IHE PCD RTLS, IHE MEM-LS), and
duplicating that guidance inside the POCD IG risks divergence from
those normative sources. Recommend the reporter raise an O&O / FHIR-I
ticket for any cross-IG deployment guidance that is genuinely missing
upstream.

#### Justification

A defensible decline rationale exists: the POCD IG's job is to profile
device-side resources (MDS Device, the device-sourced Observation
shapes, alerts, etc.), not to teach FHIR transaction patterns that are
already documented in the base specification and in destination
profiles. Adding this guidance creates a maintenance burden every time
the upstream patterns change, and risks contradicting US Core / IPA /
IHE conventions if those evolve. That said, this rationale is weak
against the actual evidence on the ticket: the reporter, the WG
sign-off at WGM-Sept25, and the drafted preview all point the other
way, so a decline would need a strong reason the WG has not yet
articulated.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the strongest reading of the ticket because it
captures the concrete deliverable everyone has already converged on
(erikmoll's drafted section, which the reporter has accepted) **and**
discharges mcros's WGM-Sept25 action item to acknowledge the IHE RTLS
and MEM-LS work and esilver's 2022-11-28 framing about presenting
options with situations and trade-offs. Disposition A is acceptable
and could ship as-is, but it leaves two known asks unaddressed (IHE
references; pros/cons-by-situation framing) and would foreseeably
attract ballot comments asking for exactly those additions.
Disposition C is not defensible against the record on the ticket: the
reporter has signed off, the proposed resolution is "Persuasive," and
a build preview already exists. The marginal editorial cost of
folding in the IHE references and a short decision table is small
relative to the risk of revisiting this section in a future cycle.
Before merge, also apply the editorial fixes: tighten the Future Work
paragraph against the current POCD project scope (per
javier_espina's comment) and replace the broken `vscode-resource://`
link in the Conditional Create bullet with a relative
`transfer.html#conditional-create` anchor.
