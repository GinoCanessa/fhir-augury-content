## Summary

Malcolm Clarke is requesting a clarification in Section 3.3 of the
Personal Healthcare Devices (PHD) FHIR Implementation Guide regarding
how `Observation.effectivePeriod` is populated when an Observation is
derived from a PHD measurement that carries a "period" (a measurement
window). He notes that the `effectivePeriod.end` value must coincide
with the measurement timestamp, because the timestamp represents the
end of the measurement period in PHD source data. He further asks
whether the IG should state that the timestamp is always present in
this scenario (i.e., whether `effectivePeriod.end` is effectively
mandatory whenever a period-derived Observation is produced).

The ticket was originally resolved as "Considered for Future Use"
(deferred to the next PHD IG revision per the Devices WG call on
2025-11-12) and then re-opened by Lynn Laakso post-STU2 publication so
the WG can re-dispose it for the next ballot cycle. There are no
linked or cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub
items.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In Section 3.3 of the PHD IG (Observation), add a clarifying
paragraph beneath the description of `Observation.effectivePeriod`
that states:

1. When an Observation is derived from a PHD measurement that carries
   a measurement period, `Observation.effectivePeriod.end` SHALL
   coincide with the measurement timestamp emitted by the device,
   because the PHD "period" structure uses the timestamp as the end of
   the period.
2. Because PHD measurements always carry a timestamp, in this
   scenario `Observation.effectivePeriod.end` is effectively always
   populated; implementers SHOULD treat its absence as a data
   integrity error from the source device.

No changes to cardinality, profile constraints, or invariants — this
is a narrative-only clarification in the IG prose for §3.3.

#### Justification

The request is a small, well-scoped technical correction to IG
narrative. The mapping rule (timestamp → `effectivePeriod.end`) is
already implicit in how PHD data is shaped, but it is not stated
explicitly, which leaves implementers free to map the timestamp to
`effectivePeriod.start` or to `effectiveDateTime` instead, producing
inconsistent Observations across PHD producers. Calling out the
mapping in §3.3 brings the IG in line with the IEEE 11073 source
semantics (where the timestamp marks the end of a measurement window)
and aligns with FHIR guidance that `Period.end` represents the time at
which the period stops being valid. No conformance machinery is
disturbed.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Make the clarification narrative-only (as in A) but stop short of any
"always present" language. Specifically:

1. State the mapping: when `Observation.effectivePeriod` is used and
   the source PHD measurement carries a period, `effectivePeriod.end`
   coincides with the measurement timestamp.
2. Add a parallel note that when the source carries only a single
   timestamp (no period), implementers SHOULD use
   `Observation.effectiveDateTime` rather than `effectivePeriod`.
3. Do **not** add a SHALL-style "timestamp is always present"
   statement in §3.3; instead, point implementers at the existing
   PHD-to-FHIR timestamp mapping section (whichever section currently
   covers PHD timestamp handling).

#### Justification

Disposition A folds two distinct questions together: (1) "does
`effectivePeriod.end` equal the timestamp?" and (2) "is the timestamp
always present?". The second question is really about PHD device
behavior and the choice between `effectiveDateTime` and
`effectivePeriod`, not about §3.3 specifically. Splitting the two
keeps §3.3 focused on the mapping rule, avoids accidentally locking
in a "timestamp always present" assumption that some PHD device
classes may not actually meet, and steers implementers toward
`effectiveDateTime` for the timestamp-only case, which is the FHIR-
idiomatic choice. This lowers the risk of a downstream technical
correction in the next ballot cycle.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as "Not Persuasive — Already Addressed by Existing
Mechanism". Rationale: the semantics of `Period.end` are already
defined by core FHIR (the time at which the period ends), and the
PHD-to-FHIR mapping section of the IG already describes how a PHD
period maps to `Observation.effectivePeriod`. No additional narrative
is required in §3.3.

#### Justification

The request is a narrative clarification rather than a substantive
defect. If the existing PHD mapping text in the IG already states
that the PHD `period` structure uses the timestamp as the period end,
then duplicating that statement in §3.3 risks divergence between two
sections of the IG over time. Declining keeps the IG terser and
relies on the cross-reference between §3.3 and the mapping section.
This is the weakest position because the reporter (an active PHD
domain expert) is on record that §3.3 as written is not clear enough,
which is itself evidence that the existing text is insufficient.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B addresses the underlying ambiguity Malcolm raised
without overcommitting the IG to a "timestamp is always present"
guarantee that the WG would have to re-validate against every PHD
device class. It captures both halves of the question — the mapping
rule (timestamp → `effectivePeriod.end`) and the "what if there is no
period?" case (use `effectiveDateTime`) — which together are what an
implementer actually needs to act on. Disposition A is also
defensible and may be preferred if the WG can confirm on-call that
PHD measurement timestamps are in fact mandatory in every supported
device profile; if so, A and B converge. Disposition C should be
rejected: the ticket was already deferred once and re-opened post-
STU2 specifically because the reporter is still not satisfied that
§3.3 is clear, and "no narrative change" leaves that open issue
unresolved heading into the next PHD revision.
