## Summary

This ticket asks the Devices work group to review the seven CodeSystems defined locally in the Personal Healthcare Devices (PHD) FHIR IG against the TSC-approved [Terminology Expectations for IG Developers](https://confluence.hl7.org/display/TSMG/Terminology+Expectations+for+IG+Developers) policy and either (a) move each CodeSystem into `terminology.hl7.org` (THO) or (b) request a documented exemption from TSMG for any CodeSystem the work group believes is too tightly bound to the IG to belong in THO. Cross-reference traversal surfaces a single related artifact — the TSMG Confluence policy page itself (Confluence ID 161063724) — and no related Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were found through the cross-reference index. The ticket has been re-opened post STU2 (lynn_laakso, 2026-03-31) after a previous "Considered for Future Use" disposition was reverted, so it is once again live work for the work group.

## Proposed Dispositions

The dispositions below are written without per-repo briefings because the cross-reference index surfaces no GitHub repositories for this ticket. The actionable artifacts implied by the dispositions live in two well-known HL7 repositories — `HL7/phd` (the PHD IG source, where the seven CodeSystem resources currently reside) and `HL7/UTG` (the source of truth for `terminology.hl7.org`). The work group should confirm those repo paths against the corresponding `repo-analysis` briefings before raising PRs.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the per-CodeSystem plan already drafted in mfaughn's 2025-07-23 comment, and execute it in two parallel work streams ahead of the next PHD IG ballot:

1. **Move to THO** (UTG submission) for the CodeSystems that are useful beyond the PHD IG and need to grow without an IG republish:
   - `CodeSystem-ASN1ToHL7` (IEEE 11073-10206 BITs measurement bit values).
   - `CodeSystem-ContinuaDeviceIdentifiers` (device identifier kinds — EUI / Bluetooth / MAC).
   For each, raise a UTG proposal that mints a `http://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/...` URL, migrates the concept set, and replaces the IG's local CodeSystem resource with a redirect note in the IG narrative.
2. **Request a TSMG exemption** (minuted) for the CodeSystems that are inherently IG-bound and should remain in `HL7/phd`:
   - `CodeSystem-ContinuaPHDInterfaceIDs`, `CodeSystem-ContinuaHFS` (Continua/PCHA-specific).
   - `CodeSystem-PhdObservationCategories` (used as the conformance tag for IG-conformant Observations).
   - `CodeSystem-SimpleAlertOperationalStatesCS` (only retained for the R4 line; will be retired in any R5/R6 update).
3. **Remove from the IG** the redundant `CodeSystem-PhdReferenceRangeMeaning` and re-author affected profiles/examples to use `SampledData.lowerLimit` / `SampledData.upperLimit` directly.
4. Update every internal IG reference (profile bindings, narrative links, examples) to the new THO URLs for items in (1) and remove dead references for items in (3) before the next ballot snapshot.

#### Justification

This is the disposition the work group itself has converged on across two reverted resolutions and a re-open: it explicitly answers the TSMG policy question for every CodeSystem, separates "spec-shaped" terminology from "ecosystem-shaped" terminology, and removes a CodeSystem (`PhdReferenceRangeMeaning`) whose semantics are already provided by the FHIR core type. It also matches Item 4 of *Terminology Expectations for IG Developers*, which is the TSC-approved policy this ticket points to. The only outstanding question is timing relative to FMM (jsnell vs. mfaughn discussion), and the *Move-to-THO + minuted exemption* split addresses both interpretations: even if Item 4 is read narrowly (exemption only required at FMM 3), submitting the exemption petition now (as mfaughn suggests is acceptable) gives FMG the documentation it needs for publication without forcing a re-vote later.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Defer the per-CodeSystem actions and instead adopt a **two-step, FMM-gated plan** matching the original "Considered for Future Use" disposition that was reverted on 2026-03-31:

1. *Now (post-STU2):* document the work group's intended terminology home for each of the seven CodeSystems (the table from mfaughn's 2025-07-23 comment) inside the PHD IG itself — for example, in a new "Terminology placement" section of the IG's `terminology.html` narrative — and link the TSMG policy. No CodeSystem URLs change yet.
2. *At FMM 3 promotion:* execute the actual UTG migrations, IG removals, and TSMG exemption petitions, scheduling the URL changes alongside the FMM 3 ballot so consumers see one breaking terminology change instead of two.

#### Justification

This honours the original mfaughn / Erik Moll 8-0-0 vote and the work group's reading of *Terminology Expectations for IG Developers* Item 4 (exemption strictly required at FMM 3). It avoids two breaking URL changes for early adopters of the STU2 line — moving CodeSystems to THO will alter the canonical URL of every concept and break instances and any external profiles binding directly to the current `https://hl7.org/fhir/uv/phd/CodeSystem/...` URLs. By batching the move with FMM 3, downstream implementers absorb a single breakage on a clearly-signalled maturity boundary. Trade-off vs. Disposition A: this leaves the TSMG policy question formally open in the meantime, which is exactly what jsnell's comment flags as risky for FMG sign-off. The plan therefore depends on TSMG / FMG agreeing that the documented intent is a sufficient interim deliverable.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request as **Not Persuasive — already addressed by stated intent and policy timing**, on the grounds that *Terminology Expectations for IG Developers* (per the work group's reading of Item 4) does not strictly require a THO migration or a recorded exemption until the IG reaches FMM 3, and that the PHD IG has, in tracker comments, already articulated the destination of each CodeSystem. Close FHIR-48081 with a pointer to the per-CodeSystem table in mfaughn's 2025-07-23 comment.

#### Justification

This is defensible only if the work group is willing to argue (a) that the TSMG policy text genuinely permits a wait-until-FMM-3 deferral with no current-cycle action, and (b) that recording the intended placement on the ticket itself is sufficient documentation. In its favour: it is the minimum-disruption option for STU2 implementers, requires no IG changes, and matches the spirit of the now-reverted "Considered for Future Use" vote. Against it: the ticket has already been re-opened twice — once by mfaughn against an FMG-driven concern from jsnell, and once by lynn_laakso post-STU2 publication — which signals that the work group itself, FMG, and TSMG do not consider "we'll deal with it later" a stable answer. Declining without at least raising the exemption petition (jsnell's concern) leaves the IG vulnerable to a publication request blocker.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The work group has already done the substantive analysis — the per-CodeSystem destination table in mfaughn's 2025-07-23 comment is exactly the artifact TSMG's *Terminology Expectations for IG Developers* policy is asking for. The ticket has been re-opened twice (mfaughn reverting the *Persuasive with Modification* vote in July, then lynn_laakso reverting the *Considered for Future Use* vote post-STU2 publication), and the recurring pattern shows that "defer to FMM 3" is not surviving contact with FMG and TSMG review. Disposition B preserves the deferral but leaves jsnell's concern about FMG / publication blockers unanswered; Disposition C is harder to defend now that the ticket has been formally re-opened post-STU2. Disposition A is also the lowest-risk path for downstream implementers in the long run: doing the THO migration *and* the TSMG exemption petition in the same ballot cycle creates a single, well-documented breaking change at a known boundary, rather than letting the ambiguity drift into the FMM-3 ballot. The work group should split the work into the three streams above (UTG submission, exemption petition, IG cleanup of `PhdReferenceRangeMeaning`) and confirm the destination repos against the `HL7/phd` and `HL7/UTG` repo-analysis briefings before opening PRs.
