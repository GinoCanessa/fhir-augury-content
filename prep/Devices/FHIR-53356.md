## Summary

The ticket reports that Section 2.9.7.2 of the Personal Healthcare Devices
(PHD) FHIR Implementation Guide does not address the **status of clocks and
timestamps** on personal health devices, even though the reporter
(MalcolmClarke) considers this an important topic. Personal health devices
frequently have unsynchronized, drifting, or unset internal clocks, which
directly affects how observation `effectiveDateTime`/`effectivePeriod` and
related timestamps should be interpreted by receivers. The ticket asks the
PHD IG to add explicit guidance on how implementers should represent and/or
qualify the status of a device's clock when reporting timestamped
observations.

No related Jira tickets, GitHub items, or Zulip threads were found in the
cross-reference index. The only cross-reference is to the Devices WG
Confluence minutes for 2025-11-12, which document the WG's decision to
defer this work to the next revision of the PHD IG.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a new normative narrative subsection under PHD IG Section 2.9.7.2 titled
"Status of Clocks and Timestamps" that:

1. Identifies the clock-status modes a personal health device may report
   (e.g., *unset*, *relative-to-power-on*, *manually-set*, *externally-
   synchronized*, *synchronized-with-known-source-and-accuracy*).
2. Specifies how each mode SHALL be communicated alongside the timestamp on
   measurement Observations — i.e., by populating an existing PHD-defined
   `Device.property` slot for clock status, plus a `Observation.extension`
   (or pre-existing PHD time-quality extension) on each Observation that
   carries `effectiveDateTime` / `effectivePeriod`.
3. States receiver behavior: when clock status is *unset* or *relative*,
   receivers MUST NOT treat `effectiveDateTime` as wall-clock time and
   SHOULD instead rely on the upload time / Observation.issued, optionally
   adjusting using the offset reported by the gateway.
4. Provides at least one worked example for each clock-status mode.

#### Justification

This is the most direct response to the reporter's concern and matches the
ticket type ("Technical Correction"): the IG currently leaves a known
behavior of personal health devices undocumented, which causes ambiguous
data on the wire. The Continua / IEEE 11073 device model already exposes
clock-status concepts, so the IG would be aligning the FHIR mapping to an
existing source-of-truth rather than inventing new semantics. The Devices
WG already agreed in principle that the topic is in-scope (the
2025-11-12 minutes deferred it rather than declined it), so accepting it
now and scheduling the change for the next PHD revision is consistent
with the WG's stated direction.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than adding new narrative + a new clock-status mechanism, **reuse
existing infrastructure**: in Section 2.9.7.2, add a short normative
paragraph that (a) requires PHD-conformant Observations to populate
`Observation.issued` whenever the device clock cannot be trusted, (b)
points to the existing PHD `Device.property` time-related codes from IEEE
11073 (e.g., MDC time-sync attributes) as the canonical place to convey
clock state, and (c) adds a single MUST-SUPPORT cross-reference from
Observations back to the producing `Device` so receivers can look up the
device's reported clock-sync attribute. No new extensions or value sets.

#### Justification

This keeps the change small and avoids creating a new
PHD-IG-specific extension that would compete with the IEEE 11073-aligned
`Device.property` codes the IG already imports. It also avoids the
backward-compatibility cost of telling existing implementers to populate
a new element. The trade-off is that receivers must do a `Device` lookup
to interpret a timestamp, which is more friction than an inline
extension on the Observation; for batch-reporting PHGs this is usually
acceptable because the Device is bundled in the same submission anyway.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket "Persuasive with Mod / Not Persuasive" and decline to add
new clock-status guidance to PHD Section 2.9.7.2 in this revision, on the
basis that (i) the underlying IEEE 11073 device specifications already
define clock-sync attributes that flow through `Device.property`, and
(ii) base FHIR already provides `Observation.issued` distinct from
`Observation.effectiveDateTime`, which together are sufficient for a
careful implementer.

#### Justification

The reporter's concern is real but is arguably already covered by
existing mechanisms — the IG would be re-stating, not adding,
functionality. Spending IG real-estate on guidance that mirrors the
underlying 11073 documents risks drift between the two specs over time.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter's concern is concrete, the ticket is filed as a Technical
Correction (not an enhancement), and the Devices WG has already agreed
on the 2025-11-12 call to address it in the next PHD revision rather
than to decline it. Disposition C is hard to defend in light of that WG
decision. Disposition B is attractive for its small surface area, but it
relies on receivers being aware that they must dereference the Device to
interpret a timestamp — a behavior that today's PHG/consumer
implementations frequently get wrong precisely because the IG is silent.
Disposition A pays the up-front cost of writing the guidance and a
worked example, then leaves implementers with unambiguous instructions
for both senders and receivers; this is the right level of investment
for a specification whose entire purpose is interoperability of
timestamped device measurements.
