## Summary

Malcolm Clarke proposes a wording change to Section 3.4 of the Personal
Healthcare Devices (PHD) FHIR IG. The current text frames support for
proprietary (non-IEEE 11073‑10206 ACOM) medical devices in a tentative,
qualified way — saying it "may be possible" to map their measurements to
the PHD profiles, and closing with an aside that the IG authors have
done this for some Serial Port Protocol (SPP) and Bluetooth SIG PHD
devices. The reporter considers this framing "too negative" and the
final sentence (about the authors' own past work) "not relevant," and
suggests replacing the paragraph with a single, affirmative sentence:
"Proprietary devices are supported by this IG by mapping the
observations, clock, and power to the objects of IEEE 11073-10206."
This is a narrative-only technical correction; no resource, profile,
extension, or value-set change is implied. No related Jira tickets,
Zulip threads, or GitHub items were surfaced via cross-references; the
sole cross-reference is to the Devices WG main-call minutes for
2025-11-12, where the WG decided to defer the change to the next
revision of the PHD IG (post‑STU2).

## Details

**Description:**

> Section 3.4 - "In addition to PHDs implementing GHS or another
> Bluetooth PHD profile, there are numerous proprietary medical devices
> on the market. It may be possible to map the measurements coming from
> these devices to the PHD profiles from this IG, but that mapping
> would require that the resulting FHIR resources be created and
> populated as if they had come from an IEEE 11073-10206 ACOM compliant
> device. The writers of this guide have performed this task for a
> number of proprietary Serial Port Protocol (SPP) and Bluetooth SIG
> PHD devices." This is too negative. The last sentence is not
> relevant.
>
> Could replace as "Proprietary devices are supported by this IG by
> mapping the observations, clock, and power to the objects of IEEE
> 11073-10206."

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Replace the existing third paragraph of Section 3.4 of the PHD IG
narrative with the reporter's exact suggested sentence:

> "Proprietary devices are supported by this IG by mapping the
> observations, clock, and power to the objects of IEEE 11073-10206."

Concretely, in the next PHD IG revision, edit the Section 3.4 source
(narrative page in the IG's `input/pagecontent/` area) so that the
paragraph beginning "In addition to PHDs implementing GHS or another
Bluetooth PHD profile…" through "…proprietary Serial Port Protocol
(SPP) and Bluetooth SIG PHD devices." is removed and replaced
verbatim by the reporter's sentence. No profile, extension, or
terminology artifact changes.

#### Justification

The reporter is the principal subject-matter expert for the PHD IG and
the suggested replacement is an unambiguous improvement on tone: it
reframes proprietary-device support from a qualified "it may be
possible" to a definite statement, while preserving the essential
technical claim (that the mapping target is IEEE 11073-10206 ACOM
objects). Dropping the closing sentence about the authors' own SPP /
Bluetooth SIG work is consistent with normative IG style, which avoids
first-person provenance statements about the editors. Because this is
purely narrative, it carries no implementation cost and no risk of
breaking existing PHD IG conformance.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt the reporter's tone change but keep the additional context that
the original paragraph was trying to convey. Replace Section 3.4's
third paragraph with something like:

> "Proprietary medical devices — including Bluetooth SIG PHD profile
> devices and Serial Port Protocol (SPP) devices — are supported by
> this IG by mapping their measurements, clock, and power
> characteristics to the corresponding objects defined in IEEE
> 11073-10206. Implementers populating FHIR resources from such
> devices SHOULD treat the mapped data as if it had originated from an
> IEEE 11073-10206 ACOM-compliant source."

This keeps the affirmative framing the reporter asked for, but
preserves two pieces of information the current paragraph carries that
the proposed one-liner drops: (a) examples of which classes of
proprietary device the IG has in mind, and (b) the conformance
expectation that the resulting FHIR resources should look the same as
those from a native ACOM device.

#### Justification

The current paragraph's examples (Bluetooth SIG PHD profiles, SPP) and
its conformance hint ("populated as if they had come from an IEEE
11073-10206 ACOM compliant device") are useful guidance for
implementers, even if the wording is overly hedged. The reporter's
specific objection is to the negative tone and to the editorial aside
about who has done the mapping work — neither of which requires
discarding the substantive content. This option addresses the
reporter's actual concern (tone) while keeping the IG more
self-explanatory for newcomers.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as Persuasive – Not Persuasive (or equivalent), on the
grounds that the existing wording is technically accurate and that no
reader has reported a comprehension or conformance problem with it.
Keep Section 3.4 as published in STU2.

#### Justification

The existing text is not factually wrong: mapping proprietary device
data to PHD profiles genuinely is a non-trivial exercise, and the
hedge ("may be possible") fairly reflects that the mapping quality is
the implementer's responsibility. The closing sentence about the IG
authors' prior work, while editorial, gives readers signal that the
approach has been exercised in practice. Without a use case showing
that the current wording has misled an implementer, a tone-only
narrative edit may not justify the cost of opening the next PHD IG
revision for this single change.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The reporter's underlying concern — that the current paragraph reads
as discouraging support for proprietary devices — is legitimate and
worth fixing in the next PHD IG revision (the WG has already
committed to revisiting at that time, per the 2025-11-12 minutes), so
Disposition C is too conservative. However, Disposition A's one-line
replacement is shorter than the original at the cost of dropping
implementer-useful detail: which classes of proprietary devices are in
scope (Bluetooth SIG PHD, SPP), and the conformance expectation that
the resulting FHIR resources should be indistinguishable from those of
a native ACOM device. Disposition B preserves both of those while
adopting the affirmative framing the reporter asked for, and avoids
the editorial first-person aside the reporter specifically called out.
The Devices WG should confirm the exact wording with Malcolm Clarke
before merging, since he is both the reporter and the principal SME.
