## Summary

Malcolm Clarke is requesting a technical correction to Section 3.4 of the
Personal Healthcare Devices (PHD) FHIR Implementation Guide. The current
wording reads: *"In general, if a PHD provides timestamps with its
measurements, it needs to provide a means for the PHG to get its sense of
current time. If it does, one can likely use this implementation guide with
that device."* The reporter argues this passage is confusing, conflicts with
earlier statements in the same section, and asserts a requirement
(*"needs to provide"*) that is not actually mandated by the IG. The proposed
remedies are either to remove the passage outright or to soften the verb
phrase from *"needs to provide"* to *"should provide"*. No related Jira
tickets were surfaced via cross-reference; the only cross-reference is to
the Devices Work Group meeting-minutes Confluence page where the disposition
was discussed (the resolution was reverted from "Considered for Future Use"
and the ticket was reopened post-STU2 publication, deferred to the next
revision of the PHD IG).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the PHD FHIR IG narrative, edit Section 3.4 to soften the conformance
language. Change the sentence *"In general, if a PHD provides timestamps
with its measurements, it needs to provide a means for the PHG to get its
sense of current time."* to *"In general, if a PHD provides timestamps
with its measurements, it should provide a means for the PHG to obtain
its sense of current time."* Retain the follow-on sentence *"If it does,
one can likely use this implementation guide with that device."* unchanged,
since once the verb is downgraded from a hard requirement to a
recommendation the conditional reading no longer conflicts with the
earlier discussion in Section 3.4 about how the PHG synchronises time.

#### Justification

This is the lighter-weight half of the reporter's two suggestions and is
the most defensible technical correction: it removes the implied
normative obligation (*"needs to"* reads as a SHALL even though the
surrounding text is descriptive prose), aligns the verb with the ballot's
established conformance-verb conventions (SHOULD = "should"), and resolves
the conflict the reporter flagged with earlier statements in the same
section without dropping useful guidance for implementers evaluating
whether their device is a fit for the IG. Because the change is narrative
only — no StructureDefinition, ValueSet, or extension is touched — it is
a pure technical correction appropriate for the next revision of the PHD
IG (per Lynn Laakso's deferral note).

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Remove the two sentences entirely from Section 3.4 and, if the underlying
guidance is still considered useful, relocate it to a new bullet under the
existing "PHG time synchronisation" / clock-handling discussion earlier in
the same section, rephrased so it reads clearly as an *applicability
note* rather than a conformance statement: e.g., *"Devices that emit
timestamped measurements but provide no mechanism for the PHG to align
its clock with the device's clock are out of scope for this guide."*
This eliminates the contradiction the reporter identified by collapsing
the duplicated guidance into a single authoritative paragraph.

#### Justification

The reporter offered "Remove" as the first option, and the comment thread
shows that the Devices WG already had reservations about retaining the
text as-is (the original "Considered for Future Use" resolution was
reverted, then deferred). Simply softening the verb (Disposition A)
leaves two paragraphs in Section 3.4 saying overlapping things about
PHG/PHD time alignment, which is the root cause of the "confusing /
conflicts" complaint. Consolidating into one paragraph is a slightly
larger edit but produces a cleaner narrative and is still a non-breaking
technical correction. The trade-off versus Disposition A is the editor
time required to find the right insertion point and to make sure the
applicability statement does not unintentionally exclude devices the WG
wishes to keep in scope.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket without changing Section 3.4. Treat the existing
sentence as informative narrative whose intent ("a device with no way to
share time with the PHG is unlikely to be usable with this IG") is
already understood by implementers, and rely on the IG's overall
conformance framework rather than the verb in this sentence to determine
what is normative.

#### Justification

The change has no effect on conformance artefacts (profiles, extensions,
value sets) and the surrounding text in Section 3.4 already explains the
PHG/PHD time-handling expectations. If the WG judges that no implementer
has actually been confused or misled by the wording in practice, the
editorial churn — including the need to re-publish the IG — may not
justify the benefit. This disposition is weakest given that the reporter
is a long-standing PHD subject-matter expert flagging the wording as
confusing, and given that the WG has already explicitly reopened the
ticket post-STU2 for action in the next revision.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A is the smallest, lowest-risk change that directly addresses
the reporter's concern: it converts an accidentally-normative *"needs
to"* into a properly-scoped *"should"*, which is exactly the convention
the rest of the PHD IG follows for non-mandatory guidance. The Devices WG
has already reopened this ticket for the next revision of the PHD IG and
flagged it as a Technical Correction, so the editorial cost is already
budgeted. Disposition B (consolidation) is a defensible follow-up if the
editor finds, while applying A, that the two paragraphs in Section 3.4
still read as duplicative; that judgement is best made in-editor rather
than committed to in advance. Disposition C should be rejected because
the reporter — who is a recognised PHD authority — has explicitly
identified the wording as confusing and self-contradictory, which is
exactly the kind of defect a Technical Correction ticket exists to fix.
