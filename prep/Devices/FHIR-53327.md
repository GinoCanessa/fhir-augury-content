## Summary

The reporter asks the Personal Healthcare Devices (PHD) FHIR IG to soften
the normative language in Section 3.4. The current text states that the
IG **requires** PHDs that generate timestamped measurements to also be
able to report their sense of current time. The reporter believes a hard
requirement creates an adoption barrier for devices the WG actually
wants implementers to use, and proposes rephrasing the sentence as a
**recommendation** — e.g., "This IG recommends that PHDs that generate
measurements that have timestamps should be able to report their sense
of current time to allow creating accurate timestamps." No related Jira
tickets were surfaced via cross-references; the only cross-referenced
artifact is the Devices Main Call meeting minutes from 2025-11-12 where
the WG agreed to defer the change to the next PHD IG revision.

## Details

**Description:**

Section 3.4 - "This IG requires that PHDs that generate measurements
that have timestamps also be able to report their sense of current
time." This should not be a requirement. We want this to be used. It
could read "This IG recommends that PHDs that generate measurements
that have timestamps should be able to report their sense of current
time to allow creating accurate timestamps."

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the Personal Healthcare Devices (FHIR) IG, edit Section 3.4 of the
narrative to replace the current sentence:

> "This IG requires that PHDs that generate measurements that have
> timestamps also be able to report their sense of current time."

with the reporter's suggested wording:

> "This IG recommends that PHDs that generate measurements that have
> timestamps should be able to report their sense of current time to
> allow creating accurate timestamps."

This is a pure narrative change (lower-case "recommends/should") — no
SHALL→SHOULD changes in any structure definition, profile differential,
or invariant are required, since the reporter did not identify a
matching machine-readable conformance rule and only the prose is at
issue. Apply the change in the next PHD IG revision (consistent with
the WG's 2025-11-12 deferral note).

#### Justification

The reporter is the long-standing PHD subject-matter expert and the
narrative as currently written is more prescriptive than the rest of
the IG's profiling actually enforces — there is no profile-level
constraint that mandates a current-time observation be sent alongside
timestamped measurements, so the prose overstates the IG's true
conformance posture. Softening "requires" to "recommends" aligns the
narrative with what the artifacts actually require, removes a
needlessly high adoption bar for device implementers, and matches the
HL7 convention of reserving "requires/SHALL"-level language for
constraints that are testable and present in the conformance
resources. The Devices WG already agreed on 2025-11-12 to take this
change in the next PHD IG revision, so Disposition A simply executes
that decision.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Instead of a single-sentence wording change, restructure Section 3.4
into two paragraphs:

1. A **recommendation** paragraph using the reporter's wording
   ("recommends … should be able to report their sense of current
   time…"), explicitly explaining *why* current-time reporting matters
   (clock-drift detection, post-hoc timestamp correction by the
   gateway/receiver).
2. A **conditional requirement** paragraph stating that *when* a PHD
   does report current time, it SHALL do so using the existing
   current-time Observation profile defined elsewhere in the IG, so
   that conformant consumers can reliably parse it.

This keeps the door open for receivers to depend on a stable shape when
current time *is* present, while no longer forcing every transmitter to
emit it.

#### Justification

The reporter's concern is the entry barrier for devices, not the
mechanism itself. Splitting the narrative preserves interoperability
guarantees (a receiver still knows what a current-time report looks
like) while removing the universal "requires" that the reporter
objects to. This pattern — "MAY/SHOULD send, but SHALL conform when
sent" — is widely used in other HL7 IGs and avoids a regression in
parser strictness for receivers that already rely on the current
shape.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Keep Section 3.4 as written. Decline the change on the grounds that
accurate timestamps are central to the clinical and analytical value
of PHD measurements, and that a normative requirement for a device to
expose its sense of current time is the only reliable way for
downstream systems to detect and correct clock drift. Mark the ticket
"Not Persuasive — Considered for Future Use" if the WG wishes to
revisit later.

#### Justification

Personal health device data is frequently consumed long after capture
(uploaded in batches, synced opportunistically), and consumers have no
other means to validate that a device's reported timestamps are
trustworthy. Demoting the requirement to a recommendation risks a
class of devices that emit timestamped observations with no
self-reported clock context, which silently degrades data quality
across the ecosystem. If the WG values data integrity over breadth of
adoption, holding the line on the existing "requires" wording is the
defensible choice.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The Devices WG has already discussed this on the 2025-11-12 Main Call
and explicitly chose to defer it to the next PHD IG revision rather
than reject it — that is effectively an in-principle Accept that just
needs to be executed in the next publication cycle. The reporter is
the PHD domain expert, the change is a narrow narrative edit, and
there is no matching profile-level conformance constraint that would
need to be relaxed in lockstep, so the implementation cost is
near-zero. Disposition B is a reasonable refinement if reviewers want
to preserve receiver-side guarantees around the *shape* of any
current-time report that is sent, and the WG could fold its second
paragraph into Disposition A during ballot prep without changing the
spirit of the request. Disposition C should only be revisited if a WG
member can point to a concrete profile-level constraint that depends
on the current "requires" wording, which the cross-reference set does
not surface.
