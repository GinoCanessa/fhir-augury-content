## Summary

The reporter (Malcolm Clarke) flags an ambiguity in **Section 3.2** of the
Personal Healthcare Devices (PHD) FHIR IG. The narrative states: *"In the
IEEE 11073-10206 Abstract Information Content Model (ACOM), IEEE
11073-10101 nomenclature codes are used to indicate what the items
are."* The word **"items"** is undefined — it is unclear whether it
refers to ACOM attributes, ACOM objects, or something else, and the
sentence as written wrongly implies that 10101 nomenclature is always
used to identify them. In practice, non-10206 transports (GHS, BLE GATT,
proprietary protocols) do not carry 10101 codes for these "items," yet
the same conceptual things are still being conveyed. The reporter's
proposed fix is to clarify that the 10101 nomenclature codes identify
**"coded values" — specifically the *type* and *unit* of a measurement** —
rather than vaguely "items."

The ticket has no cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or
GitHub items. It does reference one Confluence page: the 2025-11-12
Devices Main Call minutes, where the WG decided to **defer** the change
to the next revision of the PHD IG (it had previously been resolved as
*Considered for Future Use*, then re-opened post-STU2 publication).

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In **Section 3.2** of the PHD FHIR IG, replace the sentence
*"In the IEEE 11073-10206 Abstract Information Content Model (ACOM),
IEEE 11073-10101 nomenclature codes are used to indicate what the items
are."* with wording that:

1. Drops the undefined term **"items"**.
2. States that 10101 (MDC) nomenclature codes are used **as coded values
   — specifically to identify the *type* and *unit* of a measurement (and
   related metric metadata)** — within ACOM-aligned exchanges.
3. Explicitly notes that **non-10206 transports (e.g., GHS, BLE GATT
   profiles, proprietary protocols) may convey the same conceptual
   measurements without using 10101 codes**, and points the reader to
   how the IG maps such payloads to FHIR.

A concrete redraft (suggested):

> *"Within the IEEE 11073-10206 Abstract Content Information Model
> (ACOM), IEEE 11073-10101 (MDC) nomenclature codes are used as coded
> values to identify the type and unit of each measurement and related
> metric metadata. Other transports — such as the Generic Health Sensor
> (GHS), Bluetooth LE GATT health profiles, and proprietary protocols —
> may carry equivalent measurements without 10101 codes; this IG defines
> how those payloads are mapped into the same FHIR representations."*

This is a narrative-only change — no StructureDefinitions, ValueSets, or
extensions are touched.

#### Justification

The reporter is correct: **"items"** has no defined meaning in either
ACOM or this IG, and the current sentence over-claims by implying that
10101 codes are universally used to identify them. The proposed wording
preserves the original intent while:

- Aligning with how 10101 nomenclature is actually used (as type/unit
  codes attached to metric observations);
- Acknowledging the multi-transport reality the IG has always supported;
- Being a pure editorial clarification, so it is low-risk and
  appropriate for a *Technical Correction* ticket.

The Devices WG already agreed in the 2025-11-12 call to make this
change in the next revision; this disposition simply executes that
agreement.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Make a **smaller, more conservative editorial fix**: replace the bare
word *"items"* with a defined ACOM term — most likely
**"metric observations"** or **"observed values"** — and add a
parenthetical pointing the reader to the IG's terminology section, but
**do not** expand the sentence to discuss GHS / BLE / proprietary
transports inline.

Example redraft:

> *"Within the IEEE 11073-10206 Abstract Content Information Model
> (ACOM), IEEE 11073-10101 nomenclature codes identify the type and unit
> of each metric observation (see §X for the full ACOM-to-FHIR
> mapping)."*

The transport-coverage caveat (GHS, BLE, proprietary) would instead be
addressed once, in a dedicated subsection of Section 3 that the
narrative cross-links to — rather than re-asserted in every paragraph
that mentions 10101.

#### Justification

The reporter raised two concerns: (1) "items" is undefined, and (2) the
sentence over-implies universal 10101 use. Disposition A folds the
fix for both into one sentence, which can make the paragraph long and
duplicative if other paragraphs already discuss transport coverage.
Disposition B fixes the immediate ambiguity (the reporter's primary
complaint) with a minimal edit and consolidates the multi-transport
caveat in one place, keeping Section 3.2 lean. Trade-off: it relies on
the rest of Section 3 actually carrying the multi-transport message
clearly, which the implementer should verify.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Persuasive — Already Accepted / No Change Needed**
on the grounds that the surrounding paragraph and the IG's broader
narrative already make clear, in context, that "items" refers to ACOM
metric objects/attributes and that 10101 use is scoped to ACOM-aligned
exchanges. No edit is required.

#### Justification

This is the weakest of the three options and is included for
completeness. It is defensible only if a careful reading of Section 3.2
in its full surrounding context shows that "items" is unambiguous in
practice and that no implementer has been confused. Given that the
reporter — a long-time PHD subject-matter expert — *did* find the
sentence ambiguous, and that the WG has already voted to defer the
*change* (not to decline it), this rationale is hard to sustain.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is a narrow, narrative-only Technical Correction filed by a
domain expert, and the Devices WG has **already agreed** (per the
2025-11-12 minutes) to make the change in the next revision of the PHD
IG — they only deferred the *timing*, not the *substance*. Disposition A
faithfully implements the reporter's specific suggestion ("coded values
… type and unit") and additionally surfaces the transport-coverage point
that the reporter implicitly raised, both of which improve reader
comprehension at zero risk to conformance. Disposition B is a reasonable
fallback if the Section 3 narrative is being restructured anyway and a
single transport-coverage subsection exists or is being added; the WG
can pick between A and B based on the surrounding paragraph's current
shape. Disposition C should be rejected — declining contradicts the WG's
own prior decision to act on this ticket.
