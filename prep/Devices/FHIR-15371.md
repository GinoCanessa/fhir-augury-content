## Summary

Filed against the 2018-Jan PoCD ballot (item #38), this Change Request
reports that the Devices-on-FHIR `measurement-status` terminology
artifacts are effectively empty / unresolvable. The reporter inspected
the draft `ValueSet` at canonical URL
`http://devices.fhir.org/ValueSet/measurement-status` (v0.1, status
Draft since 2017-11-29, experimental, intended to carry the
ISO/IEEE 11073-10201 MeasurementStatus codes) and observed that no
values are rendered in the "Value set" section, and that neither the
ValueSet URL nor the referenced `CodeSystem`
(`http://devices.fhir.org/CodeSystem/measurement-status`) resolves.
The summary captures it bluntly: "Terminology resources incomplete."

There are no other Jira tickets cross-referenced from FHIR-15371. The
ticket was originally resolved Not Persuasive in 2018, then re-opened
and reverted on 2026-04-16 by the Devices WG (per the
[2026-04-08 Devices Main Call minutes](https://confluence.hl7.org/spaces/HCD/pages/453903324/2026-04-08+Devices+Main+Call+Agenda+and+Minutes))
to be addressed in preparation for the September 2026 ballot. A
contemporary comment from Erik Moll (2026-03-25) asserts that
publisher rendering of value sets "now works properly" and proposes
closing without further changes — i.e., the underlying terminology may
already be intact and only the legacy-site rendering was broken.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat the ticket as a "Persuasive — omissions will be corrected"
correction (matching the 2018 vote that mcros restored on 2026-04-16).
Concretely, in the Point-of-Care-Devices IG that publishes the
`measurement-status` artifacts (the canonical now lives under
`http://hl7.org/fhir/uv/pocd/{ValueSet,CodeSystem}/measurement-status`):

1. Populate `CodeSystem/measurement-status` with the full enumeration
   of ISO/IEEE 11073-10201 MeasurementStatus codes (the 16-bit bit
   positions: invalid, questionable, not-available, calibration-ongoing,
   test-data, demo-data, validated-data, msmt-ongoing,
   msmt-state-in-band, etc., per 11073-10201 §6.5.1.46) with code,
   display, definition, and `caseSensitive=true`. Set
   `content=complete`, `status=active`, `experimental=false`, and bump
   `version` for ballot.
2. Update `ValueSet/measurement-status` to include all codes from that
   CodeSystem (`include.system = …/CodeSystem/measurement-status` with
   no `concept` filter), set `status=active`, `experimental=false`,
   bump `version`.
3. Verify both canonicals resolve in the published IG and in
   `tx.fhir.org` so that downstream profiles (e.g., PHD's
   `PhdBaseObservation` whose
   `…element.fixed.ofType(uri)` binds this canonical) no longer raise
   the `does not resolve` ERROR Brian Reinhold reported in 2021.
4. Add a brief note to the IG narrative pointing out that the
   `devices.fhir.org` canonicals from the 2018 draft are superseded by
   the `hl7.org/fhir/uv/pocd` canonicals.

#### Justification

The original Devices WG vote (Reinhold/Faughn 9-0-0, Impact:
Compatible/substantive, Category: Correction) explicitly accepted that
the omissions would be corrected; the 2026-04-16 reversion brings the
ticket back to that posture. The change is purely additive
(populating an empty value set with the codes it was always declared
to bind) and therefore strictly Compatible. Brian Reinhold's 2021
publisher output and Jacob Andersen's 2023 MDC discussion both confirm
the terminology gap is still being felt by implementers. The ballot
window (September 2026) is the natural opportunity to land it.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than authoring a PoCD-local CodeSystem of MeasurementStatus
codes, retire `…/CodeSystem/measurement-status` and rebind
`ValueSet/measurement-status` directly to the existing UTG-published
ISO/IEEE 11073 MDC code system
(`urn:iso:std:iso:11073:10101`) using a `concept`-list `include`
that enumerates the relevant MDC partition codes for measurement
status (e.g., the `MDC_ATTR_MSMT_STAT_*` family). Mark the local
CodeSystem `status=retired` with a `replacedBy` extension pointing at
the MDC system, and update any profile bindings (PHD
`PhdBaseObservation`, `DeviceMetric`-side bindings if any) to point
at the rebuilt ValueSet. Coordinate with the Vocabulary/UTG WG to
ensure the MDC codes used are present in the published MDC
distribution.

#### Justification

The PoCD-local CodeSystem duplicates concepts that 11073 already
defines in the MDC nomenclature; minting parallel codes is the
"second source of truth" anti-pattern Devices has been moving away
from (see the ongoing MDC-code work in the
[terminology > Missing MDC code](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/terminology/topic/Missing%20MDC%20code/near/344209698)
thread). Rebinding to MDC eliminates the canonical-resolution problem
at the source, removes a maintenance burden from the Devices WG, and
keeps PHD/PoCD aligned with the rest of the 11073-derived FHIR
artifacts. The trade-off vs. Disposition A is added coordination
with UTG and a one-time migration cost for any consumers already
pinned to the local canonical.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as **Not Persuasive with Modifier**, citing Erik Moll's
2026-03-25 comment: publisher rendering of value sets now works
properly, the original 2018-Jan ballot product is long superseded by
the current PoCD IG, and the specific draft artifact at
`http://devices.fhir.org/ValueSet/measurement-status` v0.1 is no
longer a maintained canonical. Close without further spec change and
record that any remaining "URL does not resolve" errors should be
re-filed against the current PoCD IG canonical with a fresh ticket.

#### Justification

The reported artifact is an eight-year-old draft (v0.1, status Draft
since 2017-11-29) on a host (`devices.fhir.org`) that is not the
current PoCD canonical base. If the underlying terminology has since
been republished correctly under
`http://hl7.org/fhir/uv/pocd/...`, then the literal complaint is
moot. Spending ballot bandwidth on an artifact no one currently
binds to is hard to justify when newer, scope-specific tickets can
target the live canonical instead.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The Devices WG already voted to accept this as a correction (the 2018
Reinhold/Faughn 9-0-0 vote), and that disposition was deliberately
restored on 2026-04-16 with the explicit goal of landing it for the
September 2026 ballot. Disposition C contradicts that decision and
relies on Erik Moll's "rendering now works" observation, which only
confirms that the publisher tooling is healthy — it does **not**
confirm that the `measurement-status` CodeSystem is actually
populated; Brian Reinhold's 2021 publisher output is direct evidence
that the canonical was still unresolvable years after the original
ticket. Disposition B is architecturally cleaner but introduces UTG
coordination and a migration step that almost certainly will not fit
inside the September 2026 ballot window, and it can be pursued as a
follow-up tracker if Devices/Vocabulary later agree to consolidate on
MDC. Disposition A is the smallest, lowest-risk, ballot-ready
correction that delivers exactly what the ticket asks for and what
the WG has twice voted to accept.
