## Summary

The PoCD IG's SDC → FHIR mapping guidance covers `DeviceMetric.calibration`
only at the **Channel** level, but in ISO/IEEE 11073‑10207 SDC the
`CalibrationInfo` structure (which carries calibration **state** and
calibration **type** value sets) lives on `AbstractDeviceComponentState`
and may therefore appear at the **MDS**, **VMD**, or **Channel** level.
The reporter — driven by an anesthesia‑device self‑test use case in which
`ComponentCalibrationState` transitions from `Req` to `Cal` after a
successful boot‑time self‑test — needs guidance on how to represent
MDS‑/VMD‑level `CalibrationInfo` (state, type, results, and any required
human interaction such as error codes or interaction IDs) in the PoCD
FHIR profiles. The Zulip thread `#devices > Precise Mapping of SDC
CalibrationInfo to FHIR` is the source discussion that produced this
ticket; in it Todd Cooper suggested an extension on `Device` for MDS/VMD
levels, while the reporter pushed back that a single `Observation` (or a
plain extension) cannot capture the multi‑observation nature of
human‑mediated calibration. Two later comments on the ticket from
erikmoll narrow the resolution path to either (a) **a new extension on
Device** carrying calibration state/type at MDS/VMD, or (b) **adding
explanatory text to the SDC mapping page** stating that `DeviceMetric`
(with `DeviceMetric.parent` referencing the VMD/MDS Device) plus
`Observation` for results can be used; erikmoll has already drafted
candidate text targeting **PoCD 1.1.0**.

There are no linked or sibling Jira tickets surfaced in cross‑references
— this issue stands alone.

## Details

**Description:**

Right now there is no mapping for the SDC **CalibrationInfo** structure
to FHIR resources according to the [HL7 SDC to FHIR Mapping
Guidelines](https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/uv-pocd/mappingsdc.html). The
guidelines specify value set mappings for both **calibration state** and
**calibration type**, which suggest that these elements should be
represented as properties of a `DeviceMetric` resource.

In SDC, `CalibrationInfo` may be associated with the MDS, VMD, or
Channel state level. However, the current [DeviceMetric
profiles](https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/uv-pocd/profiles.html) are only
defined at the Channel level.

Guidance on how to map `CalibrationInfo` to FHIR is appreciated. The use
case is to display the self‑test of an anesthesia device, including
results, errors, and required interactions. See Zulip:
[#devices > Precise Mapping of SDC CalibrationInfo to
FHIR](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/channel/179199-devices/topic/Precise.20Mapping.20of.20SDC.20CalibrationInfo.20to.20FHIR).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt erikmoll's proposed narrative addition to `mappingsdc.md`
verbatim: add a new H4 section **"CalibrationInfo at MDS and VMD Level"**
to `input/pagecontent/mappingsdc.md` in HL7/uv-pocd, immediately after
the existing Channel‑level `DeviceMetric.calibration` mapping tables.
The section explains that `CalibrationInfo` is part of
`AbstractDeviceComponentState` and may appear at MDS/VMD/Channel; it
acknowledges that current PoCD profiles only address the Channel level;
and it lists the two implementation options (a Device extension at
MDS/VMD, or `DeviceMetric` with `DeviceMetric.parent` plus
`Observation` for results) without yet selecting one. Ship in **PoCD
1.1.0**. Defer the normative profile/extension change to a follow‑up
ticket.

#### Justification

This is the lowest‑risk path that closes the immediate documentation gap
the reporter identified. The Devices Zulip thread did **not** reach
consensus on whether the right mechanism is a Device extension or a
DeviceMetric+Observation pattern (Todd Cooper favored the extension;
Ilja Stechmann argued the extension cannot handle multi‑Observation
human‑interaction cases). Codifying both options in the SDC mapping
page, with a clearly worded "requires further analysis" note, lets
implementers proceed with informed discretion in 1.1.0 while the
workgroup continues the design conversation. It also keeps PoCD aligned
with the OO decision to move component containment to `Device.parent`,
because the text does not endorse a `DeviceMetric`‑only hierarchy.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Define a normative **`DeviceCalibrationInfo` extension on the `Device`
resource** in the PoCD IG, applicable to the MDS and VMD `Device`
profiles, with sub‑extensions for `calibrationState` (bound to the
existing PoCD calibration state value set) and `calibrationType` (bound
to the existing calibration type value set). For human‑mediated
calibration result detail (error codes, interaction IDs, measured
values), require `Observation` resources whose `Observation.device`
references the MDS/VMD `Device` (or, where appropriate, a DeviceMetric
representing a discrete calibration result code). Update
`input/pagecontent/mappingsdc.md` with a normative subsection that
selects this approach and shows worked examples for the anesthesia
self‑test case. Target **PoCD 1.1.0**.

#### Justification

This is essentially Todd Cooper's recommendation from the Zulip thread,
hardened into a normative artifact. It respects the OO decision that
device hierarchy lives on `Device.parent` (not `DeviceMetric.parent`),
which keeps the IG aligned with R5/R6 directionality. It directly
answers the reporter's anesthesia‑device use case (the MDS‑level
`Req → Cal` transition can be carried by the new
`Device.calibrationState` extension), while honoring Ilja Stechmann's
objection by *not* trying to cram human‑interaction detail into the
extension itself — that detail is carried by `Observation`s, which are
designed for it. Trade‑off vs. Disposition A: more upfront design work
and a normative profile change, which means longer ballot/review
horizon; but it ships a real solution rather than an "options" note.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request as currently scoped, on the grounds that the
existing `DeviceMetric.calibration` mappings at the Channel level
combined with `Observation` (for calibration measurements) and
`Device.parent` (for hierarchy) are already sufficient to convey
calibration state and outcomes, and that the reporter's use case is
better served by guidance issued outside the IG (e.g., a
domain‑analysis note or example bundle) rather than by altering the
mapping page or adding a new extension.

#### Justification

A defensible "decline" rests on the observation that FHIR already
provides the building blocks: `DeviceMetric.calibration` carries state
and type at the channel where measurements happen; `Observation` carries
result values, error codes, and interaction identifiers; and
`Device.parent` carries hierarchy. From this perspective, MDS/VMD‑level
"calibration state" is an aggregate of the underlying Channel‑level
calibration states and need not be modeled separately. **However, this
is the weakest of the three options**: the reporter's anesthesia
self‑test scenario describes a *device‑level* calibration gate
(`ComponentCalibrationState = Req` after boot, blocking use until `Cal`),
which has no natural home in any Channel‑level metric, and the workgroup
champion (Todd Cooper) has already endorsed action on the ticket.
Declining would likely just push the same problem into a future ticket.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A (with a follow‑up ticket queued for B).

The Zulip discussion explicitly ended with Todd Cooper's "Just get the
Jira ticket created recognizing the general issue and tie it to Device
and DeviceMetric. We can work through the requirements and solution
during subsequent discussion." The workgroup signaled it is **not yet
ready** to choose between an extension on `Device` and a
DeviceMetric+Observation pattern — Ilja Stechmann's objection that a
single extension cannot capture multi‑Observation human‑interaction
detail remains unresolved on the thread. Disposition A delivers the
exact artifact the workgroup champion already drafted (the
`CalibrationInfo at MDS and VMD Level` text), closes the documentation
gap that prompted the ticket in PoCD 1.1.0, and preserves both design
options. The right next step after acceptance is to open a successor
ticket for the normative profile work described in Disposition B so the
design conversation Todd called for has a concrete home, ideally with
Stefan Schlichting / David Gregorczyk pulled in to settle the underlying
"why is `CalibrationInfo` on `AbstractDeviceComponentState`?" question.
Disposition C is not recommended — it ignores both the workgroup
champion's direction and the genuine modeling gap at MDS/VMD level.
