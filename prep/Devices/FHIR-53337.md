## Summary

The ticket challenges blanket assumptions made in **Section 3.1** of the
Personal Healthcare Devices (PHD) FHIR IG about the nature of Personal
Health Devices (PHDs), Personal Health Gateways (PHGs), and the network
they use. The IG currently characterises PHDs as "consumer devices that
are low cost", PHGs as "consumer devices or applications running on
consumer devices", and the data-transport assumption that traffic flows
"over the public network" with patients potentially "paying for data
rates". The reporter (Malcolm Clarke) argues these are not safe
assumptions: hospitals routinely **provision clinical-grade PHDs and
dedicated PHGs** to patients, and in those programs the cellular/data
connectivity is **paid for and managed by the provider as a service**,
not by the patient. The request is therefore to soften or qualify the
language so the IG accommodates the hospital-provisioned, clinical-grade
deployment model alongside the consumer-grade one.

The Devices WG initially closed this as **Considered for Future Use**;
that resolution was subsequently reverted by Lynn Laakso and the ticket
was **deferred to the next revision of the PHD IG** per the
[2025-11-12 Devices Main Call](https://confluence.hl7.org/spaces/HCD/pages/404097645/2025-11-12+Devices+Main+Call+Agenda+and+Minutes),
with a note to **re-open post STU2 publication**. There are no
cross-referenced Jira tickets or GitHub items, so this report is built
from the ticket itself and the Devices WG meeting reference.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Edit the **PHD IG, Section 3.1 ("Personal Health Devices" / Background)**
narrative to remove the categorical assertions and replace them with
**qualified, deployment-model-neutral** wording. Concretely:

- Replace "PHDs are consumer devices that are: Low cost …" with
  language that explicitly admits **two deployment models**: (1)
  consumer-purchased, low-cost devices, and (2) **hospital- or
  payer-provisioned clinical-grade devices** loaned/issued to the
  patient. State that the IG applies to both.
- Replace "PHGs are consumer devices or applications running on consumer
  devices" with wording that also covers **dedicated, vendor- or
  provider-supplied PHGs** (including hardware appliances issued to the
  patient).
- Replace the data-transport assumption "Data will be transferred over
  the public network … Patients may be paying for data rates" with
  language noting that **connectivity may be patient-funded (e.g.,
  consumer broadband / personal cellular plan) or
  provider-funded/managed-as-a-service** (e.g., embedded SIM,
  hospital-paid cellular, or private network), and that the IG does not
  presume either.

This is a pure narrative/Technical Correction in the PHD IG; no
StructureDefinitions, profiles, value sets, or examples need to change.

#### Justification

The reporter is correct that the existing wording is **factually too
narrow**: hospital-issued, clinical-grade PHDs and dedicated PHGs are a
well-established deployment pattern in remote patient monitoring (RPM)
programs, and provider-paid cellular / managed-service connectivity is
commonplace in those programs. Leaving the current language in place
risks implementers concluding that the IG is **not intended for** the
clinical-grade / hospital-provisioned model, which would be both
inaccurate and harmful to adoption in the very settings (RPM, hospital-
at-home, chronic-disease monitoring) where the IG arguably has the most
clinical value. Because the change is narrative-only and additive (it
broadens scope rather than constrains it), it does not break any
conformance and is a clean fit for **Technical Correction** as already
classified. The Devices WG has already agreed the issue is real by
reverting the "Considered for Future Use" resolution and deferring the
change to the next PHD IG revision.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than rewriting Section 3.1 to enumerate both deployment models
inline, restructure the section into **two named sub-sections** (or a
short comparison table):

1. **Consumer-grade deployment** — the current text, retained roughly
   as-is, explicitly scoped to the consumer model (low-cost device,
   patient-owned, public-network/patient-paid connectivity).
2. **Clinical-grade / provider-provisioned deployment** — new text that
   describes hospital-issued PHDs, dedicated PHGs, and managed-service
   connectivity, and notes the differences in device lifecycle,
   ownership, calibration/maintenance responsibility, and connectivity
   funding.

Add a brief lead-in paragraph stating that the IG applies to both
deployment models and that downstream sections (security, identity,
provenance) should be read with whichever model is in use.

#### Justification

This addresses the same underlying gap as Disposition A but does so in a
way that is **more useful to implementers**, who in practice have to
make a number of correlated decisions (device identity, ownership,
maintenance, connectivity funding, data ownership) based on which model
they are in. A two-model framing makes those correlations explicit and
gives the WG a natural place to add follow-on guidance later (e.g., a
provider-managed-connectivity security note) without further narrative
upheaval. The trade-off vs. A is more editorial work and a slightly
larger diff for what is still ostensibly a Technical Correction; some WG
members may prefer the lighter-touch wording fix in A and defer the
structural rewrite to a substantive change request.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as **Persuasive with Modification — already deferred**, on the
basis that the WG has already taken an action consistent with the
ticket: the "Considered for Future Use" resolution was reverted and the
change has been **deferred to the next revision of the PHD IG**
post-STU2. Treat this ticket as the tracking item for that future
revision and decline any change to the published STU2 narrative.

#### Justification

The ticket arrived after STU2 was on the publication path, and the WG's
own minutes (2025-11-12 Devices Main Call) already record the decision
to defer rather than fix in-flight. Reopening the narrative of Section
3.1 immediately risks destabilising the published STU2 and creating
inconsistency with already-balloted text. The reporter's concern is
acknowledged and captured; deferring keeps the published IG stable while
giving the WG room to do a more considered rewrite (along the lines of
Disposition B) in the next revision cycle. The cost is that the
published STU2 continues to carry inaccurate scoping language for the
duration of the cycle.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The change requested is genuinely a **factual correction** to narrative
text, not a design change: Section 3.1 currently asserts assumptions
about PHDs, PHGs, and connectivity funding that are demonstrably false
for a large and important class of real deployments (hospital-issued
clinical-grade RPM kits with provider-paid connectivity). Disposition A
fixes that with the smallest possible footprint — narrative-only edits
to one section, no profile or conformance impact — which is exactly the
shape of change that **Technical Correction** is designed to carry.
Disposition B is attractive editorially and is the better long-term
answer, but the structural rewrite is large enough that it is better
filed as a separate substantive change request rather than smuggled in
under a Technical Correction label. Disposition C (defer entirely) is
defensible procedurally but leaves provably inaccurate scoping language
in the published IG for an entire cycle, which is hard to justify when
the fix is a few sentences. Recommend the WG take Disposition A in the
next PHD IG revision (as the WG has already agreed to do), and open a
follow-up to consider the Disposition B restructure as a substantive
change.
