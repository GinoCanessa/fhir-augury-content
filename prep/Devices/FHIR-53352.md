## Summary

The reporter (Malcolm Clarke) is requesting a clarifying note in
Section 2.9 of the Personal Healthcare Devices (PHD) FHIR Implementation
Guide. Their observation is that, in general, a generic Personal Health
Gateway (PHG) will not have a human-readable `text` value to populate on
a `CodeableConcept`, because the PHG simply forwards the numeric codes
that arrive from the upstream Personal Health Device (PHD) — the gateway
typically has no display dictionary of its own. The ticket asks the IG
to explicitly call this out in Section 2.9 (and possibly elsewhere) so
that implementers do not interpret the presence of `CodeableConcept.text`
as a requirement. No cross-referenced Jira tickets were surfaced; the
only cross-reference is to the 2025-11-12 Devices WG meeting minutes,
where the WG decided to defer the change to the next revision of the
PHD IG (the ticket has since been re-opened post STU2 publication).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update Section 2.9 of the Personal Healthcare Devices FHIR IG to add an
explicit note that, in a generic PHG deployment, the `text` element on
`CodeableConcept`-typed elements (notably `Observation.code`,
`Observation.value[x]` when CodeableConcept, `Observation.component.code`,
and any device-property `CodeableConcept`s) will typically be absent
because the gateway only relays the coded values supplied by the PHD and
does not maintain a local display dictionary. The note should state that
implementers MUST NOT treat `CodeableConcept.text` as required when a
resource originates from a generic PHG, and that consumers should be
prepared to render a display from the code system (e.g., MDC / IEEE 11073)
rather than relying on `text`. Mirror the wording in any other section
that currently implies `text` will be populated.

#### Justification

This is a documentation-only / narrative change to the PHD IG and does
not alter cardinality, terminology bindings, profiles, or conformance.
It aligns the IG with the underlying FHIR rule that `CodeableConcept.text`
is optional, and it reflects the actual behaviour of conformant gateways
that do not embed display strings. Calling this out reduces the risk
that downstream profiles or validators will (incorrectly) require `text`
on PHD-sourced observations. The Devices WG has already reviewed the
ticket and accepted the underlying observation; the only blocker was
publication timing, and the ticket is now re-opened post STU2.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than adding only a Section 2.9 narrative note, treat the issue as
a cross-cutting clarification: (1) add a short subsection to the IG's
"Conformance" or "Implementer Guidance" page titled something like
"Display text and the PHG passthrough model" that documents the absence
of `CodeableConcept.text` in PHG-relayed resources once, with examples;
(2) link to that subsection from Section 2.9 and from each other section
that currently shows a populated `text`; and (3) where examples in the
IG show `CodeableConcept.text` on PHD-sourced observations, regenerate
or annotate them so they reflect the realistic
"code-only, no display text" shape, so readers see the expected pattern
in the example payloads as well as in the prose.

#### Justification

The ticket reporter explicitly hints at this ("Could mention here as
well"), suggesting the issue is not unique to Section 2.9. A single
canonical explanation plus targeted back-links is easier to keep
consistent over future revisions than scattered notes, and updating the
examples removes a second, subtler source of confusion (implementers who
copy IG examples and assume `text` is expected). Trade-off vs. A: more
authoring work and a slight risk of churn in regenerated examples, but
materially better implementer experience.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as "no change" on the grounds that `CodeableConcept.text`
is already optional in core FHIR (cardinality `0..1`) and the PHD IG
does not impose any constraint that would make it appear mandatory.
Implementers consulting the FHIR core spec will already know that `text`
may be absent, and the IG's existing examples are illustrative rather
than normative.

#### Justification

Defensible only on a strict reading: nothing in the IG today actually
*requires* `CodeableConcept.text`, so the "problem" is one of perception
rather than conformance. Adding narrative text carries a small but
non-zero risk of contradicting future revisions or drifting out of sync
with regenerated examples. However, this rationale is weak because the
reporter — a long-standing PHD subject-matter expert — has flagged that
the current text *reads* as if `text` will be populated, which is itself
evidence that an IG clarification is warranted.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is recommended. The reporter's "Could mention here as
well" explicitly anticipates that more than one section of the IG needs
the clarification, and the Devices WG has already endorsed addressing
the issue in the next revision (per the 2025-11-12 minutes). Centralising
the explanation in one implementer-guidance subsection — and back-linking
from Section 2.9 and any other affected section — gives a single
source of truth that is cheaper to maintain than scattered notes
(Disposition A), and simultaneously fixes the example payloads that
would otherwise continue to imply `text` is populated. Disposition C is
not recommended: although strictly defensible against the FHIR core
cardinality, it ignores the stated implementer-experience problem that
prompted the ticket, and the WG has already signalled willingness to
make the change.
