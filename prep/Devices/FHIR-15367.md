## Summary

This is a 2018-vintage ballot comment against the Point of Care Devices
(PoCD, FHIR) implementation guide, originally filed by Eric Haas as
"PoCD #34". The reporter observes that the IG's normative text states
that `Device.type` and `DeviceComponent.type` "shall be present and have
a coding from the 11073 MDC device nomenclature", that `DeviceMetric.type`
"shall have a coding from the 11073 MDC metrics nomenclature", and that
`DeviceMetric.unit` is similarly constrained — but that no actual ValueSets
are bound (or referenced) to back those normative assertions. The request
is to add the missing ValueSet references / bindings so the conformance
language is enforceable by validators and consistent with the underlying
ISO/IEEE 11073-10101 nomenclature (MDS, VMD, Channel codes per the
11073-10201 DIM).

The ticket has had a long disposition history: in 2018 it was marked
*Deferred* on the grounds that ValueSets were defined but the supporting
CodeSystem was still under construction; in 2019 it was adjusted to *Not
Persuasive* while remaining Deferred. In March 2026 a Devices WG member
(erikmoll) noted that the suggested bindings are now in place and that no
further action is needed, and on 2026-04-08 the Devices WG (per the
Confluence agenda referenced in the cross-references) formally reverted
the prior *Not Persuasive* resolution and re-opened the item to be
addressed in preparation for the upcoming September ballot. The only
non-Jira cross-reference is an automated `fhir_bot` Zulip tracker
announcement from 2018 — there is no community discussion thread on file.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the PoCD IG, add explicit ValueSet bindings (and the corresponding
ValueSet resources, if not already published) on the affected elements,
matching the normative SHALL language already present in the code-system
section:

- `Device.type` → bind to a ValueSet `PoCDDeviceType` (extensional or
  filter-based) drawn from the IEEE 11073-10101 MDC device nomenclature
  (MDS / VMD / Channel partitions per 11073-10201 DIM). Binding strength
  **required** for the MDC coding, with the element typed as
  `CodeableConcept` so additional codings from other systems remain
  permitted.
- `DeviceComponent.type` → bind to the same `PoCDDeviceType` ValueSet
  (DeviceComponent is the R3-era spelling; if the IG has migrated to
  R4+/R5, apply the equivalent binding to the surviving element, e.g.
  `DeviceDefinition.type` / `Device.type`).
- `DeviceMetric.type` → bind to a ValueSet `PoCDMetricType` drawn from
  the IEEE 11073-10101 MDC metrics nomenclature. Binding strength
  **required** on the MDC coding, with `CodeableConcept` permitting
  additional codings.
- `DeviceMetric.unit` → bind to a ValueSet of 11073 MDC units of
  measure (`PoCDMetricUnit`), again **required** on the MDC coding,
  `CodeableConcept` allowing additional unit codings (UCUM, etc.).

Each ValueSet must reference a published CodeSystem (the 11073 MDC
CodeSystem the IG already builds on). For the September 2026 ballot,
verify each ValueSet is expandable (or that intensional definitions are
correctly authored against the published CodeSystem) so validators can
operate against it.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the original commenter and the long line of
deferral notes have asked for: align the formal bindings with the
normative SHALL prose so conformance is testable by tooling instead of
relying on prose alone. The 2018 Deferred / Not Persuasive cycle was
explicitly grounded in the then-incomplete state of the underlying
CodeSystem (jrhoads, 2018-11-05); erikmoll's 2026-03-26 comment that
"the suggested bindings are in place" indicates the blocker has cleared,
and the Devices WG's 2026-04-08 decision to revert the prior *Not
Persuasive* resolution and address the item for the September ballot
shows current WG intent to land the bindings rather than continue to
defer. Using `CodeableConcept` with a required binding on the MDC
coding preserves the IG's design freedom for additional codings while
making MDC presence machine-checkable.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Confirm and consolidate rather than add new artifacts. Treat the ticket
as a reconciliation task:

1. Inventory the current PoCD IG and confirm that ValueSets / bindings
   for `Device.type`, `DeviceComponent.type` (or its R4+ successor),
   `DeviceMetric.type`, and `DeviceMetric.unit` already exist (this is
   what erikmoll asserted on 2026-03-26).
2. Where bindings exist but are weaker than the normative SHALL prose
   (e.g., `extensible` or `preferred`), strengthen them to **required**
   on the MDC coding within the `CodeableConcept`, or — if a single
   strength on the binding is too blunt — add an invariant /
   `mustSupport` rule that asserts "at least one coding shall come from
   the 11073 MDC nomenclature" on the affected element.
3. Where ValueSets exist but the normative section's prose drifts from
   the binding (different filter, different code system version, etc.),
   align the prose to the binding rather than authoring new ValueSets.
4. Resolve the ticket with an explanatory note pointing reviewers at
   the existing ValueSets / bindings and the alignment edits.

#### Justification

If erikmoll's assessment is correct, much of what Disposition A would
"add" already exists, and authoring duplicate ValueSets risks creating
two bindings for the same element with subtly different intensional
definitions. A reconciliation pass also lets the WG choose between two
mechanisms for the SHALL conformance — binding strength vs. an
invariant — which matters because a single `required` binding on a
`CodeableConcept` only forces *the binding's* coding to be from the
ValueSet; it does not on its own express the "at least one MDC coding
required, others permitted" pattern. Picking the right mechanism in
one place is cleaner than retrofitting after the September ballot.
This approach also addresses the "incomplete without this" concern in
the reverted resolution while minimising the diff against the current
IG and keeping CodeSystem stewardship undisturbed.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve as **Persuasive with Modification → Already Addressed** (no
substantive ballot change), citing erikmoll's 2026-03-26 comment that
the suggested bindings are now in place. Close the ticket with a
pointer to the current ValueSet / binding artifacts in the PoCD IG, and
do not open the September 2026 ballot scope on this item.

#### Justification

The original 2018 reporter's concern was that the SHALL prose lacked
backing ValueSets, and the IG's primary maintainer states that those
bindings now exist. If a confirmation pass shows the bindings are
present and correctly strength-constrained, there is nothing further to
ballot, and re-opening the item risks substantive churn (binding
strength changes are noticed by every validator and downstream profile)
just before a September ballot. Closing the item with evidence is the
defensible outcome if and only if the WG's reconciliation walk
(Disposition B step 1) actually confirms the bindings exist *and* that
their strength matches the normative prose.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The 2026-04-08 WG decision to revert the prior *Not Persuasive* and
schedule the item for the September ballot is hard to reconcile with
simply closing the ticket as already-addressed (Disposition C) on the
strength of a single comment — the WG has signalled it wants something
to land. At the same time, Disposition A's "add ValueSets and required
bindings" instruction is risky if those artifacts already exist in
some form; duplicate or conflicting bindings on `Device.type` and
`DeviceMetric.type` would be worse than the status quo. Disposition B
splits the difference: it starts with the inventory step the WG
genuinely needs, makes the binding-strength-vs-invariant choice
explicitly (which matters for a `CodeableConcept` element where the
SHALL-have-an-MDC-coding pattern is what conformance really wants),
and lets the WG decide on a per-element basis whether the right ballot
delta is "tighten the binding", "add an invariant", or "no change,
update prose". If step 1 of Disposition B reveals the bindings are
genuinely missing rather than merely incomplete, the work converges
with Disposition A and nothing is lost; if it reveals they are
already in place and correctly strength-constrained, the WG can fall
through to Disposition C with documented evidence rather than relying
on a single in-line comment.
