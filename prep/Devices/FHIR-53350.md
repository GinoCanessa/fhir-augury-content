## Summary

The ticket asks the Devices work group to clarify how IEEE 11073-20601's
`source-handle-reference` concept maps into IEEE 11073-10206 and, by
extension, into the Personal Healthcare Devices (PHD) FHIR IG. In
20601, `source-handle-reference` lets facets of a metric (for example,
the meal-time annotation that accompanies a glucose reading) be linked
back to the metric they qualify. The reporter (Malcolm Clarke)
observes that 10206 — and therefore the FHIR mapping derived from it —
does not make this linkage transparent, and asks for clarification and
possibly normative changes to 10206 and to the device specialisations
so the relationship can be carried through to FHIR. No other Jira
tickets are cross-referenced, so the request stands on its own. The
most recent activity is administrative: the previous "Considered for
Future Use" resolution was reverted and the ticket re-opened post-STU2
publication, with the work deferred to the next revision of the PHD IG
per the 2025-11-12 Devices Main Call.

## Details

**Description:**

IEEE 11073-20601 contains the concept of "source-handle-reference"
which allows facets of a metric to be linked to the metric. This has
been used in devices such as the glucose meter to allow information
such as meal time to be linked to the glucose measurement. It is not
clear how this concept is handled in 10206, and thus in FHIR. This
needs clarification, and perhaps changes to 10206 and device
specialisations to allow transparent mapping.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a normative section to the next revision of the Personal
Healthcare Devices FHIR IG that defines an explicit, round-trippable
mapping for the 11073-20601 `source-handle-reference` relationship.
Concretely:

1. In the PHD IG mapping pages for the affected device specialisations
   (glucose meter first, then any specialisation that uses facet
   linkage in 20601 — e.g., pulse oximeter SpO2/PR pairings, blood
   pressure with motion/cuff-fit facets), document that a 20601
   `source-handle-reference` between a primary metric and a facet
   metric SHALL be represented as `Observation.derivedFrom` from the
   primary `Observation` to the facet `Observation` (with the
   specialisation profile pinning the reference target).
2. Where the facet is genuinely a sub-component of the same
   measurement event (rather than an independently-observed value),
   represent it as `Observation.component` on the primary
   `Observation` and explicitly call out the choice criterion in the
   IG.
3. Coordinate with the IEEE 11073-10206 editors to add an equivalent
   facet-linkage construct to 10206, so that the FHIR mapping is not
   silently introducing a relationship that 10206 itself does not
   carry.

#### Justification

The reporter is correct that 20601 carries this linkage explicitly and
10206 does not, which leaves the PHD IG without an unambiguous landing
spot. `Observation.derivedFrom` is the existing FHIR mechanism for
expressing "this observation is qualified/derived from another
observation", which is exactly the semantics of
`source-handle-reference`. Pinning the choice in the IG (rather than
leaving it to implementer discretion) is consistent with how the
Devices work group has handled other 20601→FHIR ambiguities, and
matches the direction the OO thread cited above was already pointing
toward in 2018. The administrative trail on the ticket — re-opened
post-STU2 and explicitly deferred to the next PHD IG revision —
indicates the work group already views this as in-scope follow-on
work.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than introducing a normative IG mapping immediately, define a
PHD-specific extension (e.g.,
`http://hl7.org/fhir/uv/phd/StructureDefinition/source-handle-reference`)
of type `Reference(Observation)` that carries the 20601 linkage
verbatim, and apply it on the PHD `Observation` profile(s) for the
affected specialisations. In parallel, raise a change request against
IEEE 11073-10206 to add the linkage at the underlying standard layer.
Once 10206 carries it natively, retire the extension in a future PHD
IG revision in favour of `derivedFrom`/`component`.

#### Justification

This option preserves the 20601 round-trip immediately without
committing the IG to a specific FHIR mechanism (`derivedFrom` vs
`component` vs `hasMember`) before 10206 has resolved its own gap.
Extensions are the FHIR-sanctioned escape hatch for exactly this
situation — a concept that exists in the source standard but has no
clean home in the target — and they avoid prematurely binding the IG
to a mapping choice that may need to change once 10206 is updated. It
also acknowledges the community uncertainty visible in the historical
OO thread: more than one core element has been proposed to carry this
relationship over the years.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Persuasive with Modification — no spec change
required**, on the grounds that the existing
`Observation.derivedFrom` (and, for sub-measurements,
`Observation.component`) already provide a sufficient FHIR mechanism
for expressing facet linkage, and that the issue is one of IG
guidance rather than a defect in the FHIR spec or in PHD itself.
Replace the requested change with a non-normative implementation note
on the PHD glucose-meter mapping page pointing implementers at
`derivedFrom` for facet linkage.

#### Justification

FHIR already provides the building blocks; the gap is documentary,
not structural. If implementers can already produce conformant PHD
`Observation` resources that carry the meal-time facet via
`derivedFrom`, then the cost of a normative IG change (and a
coordinated 10206 change) is hard to justify against the benefit of a
short implementation note. Declining keeps the PHD IG STU2 surface
stable.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The work group has already taken an explicit position on this ticket
— the previous "Considered for Future Use" resolution was reverted
and the work was deferred to the next revision of the PHD IG, which
means the WG has already agreed the issue is real and in scope.
Disposition C contradicts that decision. Disposition B is defensible
but introduces an extension that the WG would then have to commit to
retiring, which is a worse outcome for implementers than picking the
right core mechanism the first time. Disposition A picks the
existing FHIR mechanism (`Observation.derivedFrom`, with
`Observation.component` for true sub-measurements) that the
historical OO discussion was already converging on, applies it
consistently across the affected device specialisations, and pairs
the IG change with the upstream 10206 change the reporter actually
asked for. That gives implementers a single, transparent mapping for
20601 facet linkage and avoids leaving a 20601→10206→FHIR translation
gap open for another cycle.
