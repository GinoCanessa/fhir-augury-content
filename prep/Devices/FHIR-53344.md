## Summary

Malcolm Clarke is reporting an internal inconsistency in Section 3.3
"Relative time" of the Personal Healthcare Devices (PHD) FHIR IG. The
section currently says (or implies) that relative time should be mapped
to UTC. The reporter argues this is wrong on two counts: (1) the whole
purpose of "relative time" is that it is independent of any external
clock so it can be used to compute accurate intervals between events
without being subject to clock drift, time-zone changes, or
re-synchronisation jitter, and (2) the IG itself, later in the same
section, treats relative time as just a quantity of small units (e.g.
microseconds), so the earlier "map to UTC" instruction contradicts the
later definition. The proposed correction is to drop the UTC mapping
and consistently define relative time as a unitless count (e.g. number
of microseconds) since the device's epoch / boot.

This ticket is one of a small cluster of related Devices WG tickets
that all push back on how time is described in the PHD IG:
[FHIR-53349](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53349) raises the same
"relative-time-is-coded-as-a-Quantity, not as a UTC-mapped value"
inconsistency from the perspective of Section 2.8;
[FHIR-53353](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53353) points out that
because relative time is *not* synchronised, the
"timeSynchronizationMethod" property does not apply to it (Section
2.9.7); [FHIR-53348](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53348) notes that
a PHG may not even have a UTC offset to map to; and
[FHIR-48183](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48183) is the older,
already-published correction that established "relative time" as a
microsecond-resolution count and clarified the referenced 11073
specification. Together they point to a single underlying remedy:
re-state Section 3.3 (and the Section 2.x cross-references) so that
relative time is consistently a unit-bearing count of small intervals,
never a UTC-mapped instant.

The ticket has been Triaged and was deferred at the 2025-11-12 Devices
Main Call to the next revision of the PHD IG (re-opened post-STU2
publication).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the next revision of the PHD IG, rewrite Section 3.3 ("Relative
time") so that:

1. Any wording that says or implies that relative time is "mapped to
   UTC" is removed.
2. Relative time is defined as a unit-bearing integer count of small
   intervals (e.g. microseconds) since an unspecified, device-local
   epoch (e.g. boot, sensor power-on). The two existing resolutions
   ("relative time" = 125 µs, "high resolution relative time" = 1 µs)
   established by [FHIR-48183](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48183)
   are kept and explicitly cited as the source of truth.
3. The text explicitly states that relative time is **not**
   wall-clock-bearing and **must not** be combined with a UTC offset or
   time-zone in transport; it is only meaningful for computing
   intervals between events from the same source.
4. A short cross-reference is added at the end of Section 3.3 to the
   later subsection that already defines the unit/quantity form, so a
   future reader cannot read the earlier paragraph in isolation and
   reach the wrong conclusion.

#### Justification

The reporter's substantive point is correct on its own terms: if the
purpose of "relative time" is to give implementers a stable interval
clock that survives time-zone changes and clock re-syncs, then mapping
it to UTC at any point in the pipeline destroys that property. The IG
already defines relative time as a microsecond count later in the same
section (and was previously corrected in this direction by the
already-published [FHIR-48183](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48183)),
so this disposition simply removes a stale, contradictory paragraph
rather than introducing new semantics. It also leaves Section 3.3
internally consistent with [FHIR-53349](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53349)
(the Section 2.8 Quantity treatment) and
[FHIR-53353](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53353) (relative time is
not synchronised), which are the natural sibling fixes.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than only fixing Section 3.3 in isolation, treat FHIR-53344 as
the entry point for a coordinated, single-PR correction across the
whole "time" subsystem of the PHD IG. Specifically:

1. Define a single normative subsection (e.g. a new "3.x Time concepts
   in the PHD IG") that distinguishes three concepts and then
   *references* that subsection from Sections 2.1.1.3, 2.8, 2.9.7, 3.3
   and any other site that mentions time. The three concepts:
   - **Absolute time** — wall-clock instant, may carry a UTC offset
     when the PHG can supply one (covers FHIR-53348's "may only have
     UTC, local or no time" reality).
   - **Relative time** — unit-bearing integer count (125 µs / 1 µs
     resolutions per FHIR-48183), device-local epoch, never mapped to
     UTC, never carrying a `timeSynchronizationMethod`.
   - **Synchronised time** — relationship between the two, plus the
     `timeSynchronizationMethod` property, applied **only** to absolute
     time.
2. Replace the offending Section 3.3 paragraph with a short pointer
   into that new normative subsection.
3. Resolve [FHIR-53349](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53349),
   [FHIR-53353](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53353) and
   [FHIR-53348](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53348) against the same
   change set so the IG's time vocabulary becomes consistent in one
   editing pass instead of four.

#### Justification

FHIR-53344 is one of a tightly-related family of tickets, all from the
same reporter, all flagging that the IG conflates "relative time" with
"absolute / UTC-mapped time" in different places. Fixing only Section
3.3 will leave Sections 2.1.1.3, 2.8 and 2.9.7 still inconsistent, and
implementers reading the IG end-to-end will continue to be confused.
The Devices WG already deferred this set "to the next revision of PHD
IG" (per the 2025-11-12 minutes), which is the natural moment to do the
larger, coordinated edit rather than a series of point fixes. Trade-off
vs Disposition A is scope: Disposition B is more editorial work up
front, but it removes the risk of a "we fixed Section 3.3 but Section
2.8 still says the opposite" outcome.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as "Not Persuasive" or "Considered for Future Use" on the grounds
that (a) Section 3.3 does later define relative time as a microsecond
count, so a careful reader can already reconcile the two paragraphs,
and (b) the IG was previously corrected in this area by the published
[FHIR-48183](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48183), which the
workgroup may consider sufficient.

#### Justification

The Devices WG initially resolved this ticket as "Considered for Future
Use" in 2025, which is a defensible position when no implementer has
reported being misled in practice and the resolution to FHIR-48183 is
already in normative wording downstream of the offending paragraph.
Declining keeps editorial churn out of a stable, recently-balloted IG.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B (coordinated correction) is the right call. The reporter
has filed at least four tickets against the PHD IG's time model
(FHIR-53344, FHIR-53349, FHIR-53353, FHIR-53348), and they all describe
facets of the same underlying inconsistency: the IG sometimes treats
"relative time" as a UTC-mapped instant and sometimes as a unit-bearing
count, and it attaches synchronisation properties to a concept that is
by construction unsynchronised. Disposition A would technically
satisfy this single ticket but leaves the IG self-contradictory in
adjacent sections, which guarantees more tickets of the same shape next
ballot. Disposition C is hard to defend now that the WG itself has
already reverted the "Considered for Future Use" resolution and
deferred the work to the next revision — i.e. the WG has implicitly
agreed there is something to fix. A single, coordinated PR against the
PHD IG sources, resolving FHIR-53344 jointly with FHIR-53349 /
FHIR-53353 / FHIR-53348 and citing the already-published wording from
FHIR-48183 as the source of truth, is the lowest-risk way to make the
IG internally consistent in one pass.
