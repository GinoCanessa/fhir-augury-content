## Summary

The reporter asks the Point of Care Devices (PoCD) IG to clarify how LOINC
and SNOMED CT codes should be used alongside the ISO/IEEE 11073-10101 MDC
(Medical Device Communication) nomenclature for device observations. They
observe that LOINC and SNOMED concepts exist for the majority of commonly
reported device observations and, when known, should be carried in device
data. To operationalize this, they recommend defining a slice on
`Observation.code.coding` with a binding to the MDC Metric Nomenclature
so that the structural role of MDC (primary) versus LOINC/SNOMED
(supplementary) is explicit.

No Jira cross-references, related Zulip threads, or GitHub cross-references
were surfaced for this ticket by the augury index, so the analysis below is
grounded in the ticket content, its resolution notes, and the comments on
the ticket itself.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Modify the PoCD IG's Device Observation profile (the profile that
constrains `Observation` for PoCD-sourced device data) to add a
`code.coding` slice structure with at minimum these slices:

- `code.coding:mdc` — discriminator `system`, fixed/required binding to
  the MDC Metric Nomenclature (`urn:iso:std:iso:11073:10101`),
  cardinality `1..1`.
- `code.coding:loinc` — discriminator `system`, binding to LOINC
  (`http://loinc.org`), cardinality `0..1`, with guidance that when a
  LOINC concept is known for the observation it SHOULD be populated.
- `code.coding:sct` — discriminator `system`, binding to SNOMED CT
  (`http://snomed.info/sct`), cardinality `0..1`, with parallel guidance
  to LOINC.

Narrative in the profile would state that MDC is the authoritative
structural code (preserving the 11073-10201/10207 → FHIR mapping) and
that LOINC/SNOMED are supplementary codings provided when known.

#### Justification

This is what the reporter literally requested and it makes the
co-existence of MDC / LOINC / SNOMED machine-checkable rather than
narrative-only. Slicing on `Observation.code.coding` by `system` is the
standard FHIR pattern for "must have X, may also have Y", is already
used across other HL7 IGs (e.g., US Core vital signs, IPS Observation
profiles), and is a natural fit for implementers consuming device data
who already expect MDC and would benefit from a contractual signal that
LOINC/SNOMED may appear on the same `CodeableConcept`.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Do not change the Device Observation profile's structural constraints.
Instead, update the PoCD IG **Overview** page — specifically the "Value
of the MDC Nomenclature Model" section — with a new, explicitly
normative paragraph that:

1. States MDC is the required primary coding, per the existing profile.
2. States that when a LOINC or SNOMED CT concept is known for a given
   device observation, it SHOULD be included as an additional
   `Observation.code.coding` entry alongside the MDC coding.
3. Gives a short worked example showing a `code.coding` array with MDC
   first and LOINC (or SNOMED CT) second.
4. Cross-links to the Device Observation profile so readers land on the
   structural rules.

Optionally, add the same paragraph (or a link to it) as a `comment` on
`Observation.code` in the profile's StructureDefinition so it shows up
in the generated profile page without changing any constraint.

#### Justification

This matches what erikmoll proposed in comments and preserves the
explicit design intent that the Device Observation profile is a
structural mapping artifact for IEEE 11073-10201/10207, not a general
clinical observation profile. Adding `coding` slices with LOINC/SNOMED
bindings risks being read as "LOINC/SNOMED are part of the device
interoperability contract", which they are not — they are opportunistic
enrichment. Guidance-only carries the same operational message to
implementers (populate LOINC/SNOMED when known) without inflating the
profile surface, and it avoids the tooling churn that comes with
re-slicing a widely-referenced profile.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change on the grounds that it is **already addressed** by
existing IG content and profile constraints, mirroring the ticket's
current resolution of "Considered - No action required":

- The Device Observation and Device Metric profiles already require MDC
  codes, which covers the primary-coding half of the request.
- The Overview page's "Value of the MDC Nomenclature Model" section
  already addresses the relationship between MDC and LOINC/SNOMED.

Record the rationale on the ticket and close.

#### Justification

The IG author (erikmoll) has reviewed the current content and confirmed
that the substantive point — MDC is required, LOINC/SNOMED are valuable
when known — is already present in the specification. Adding either a
slice (Disposition A) or additional narrative (Disposition B) without
new implementer feedback risks re-stating the same requirement twice,
and slicing carries a small but real backwards-compatibility cost for
consumers of the profile. Absent a concrete implementer report that the
current guidance is being missed or misapplied, the minimal-change
answer is to leave it.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition C is defensible and in fact aligns with how the ticket was
ultimately resolved, but the reporter's concern is that the current
guidance is easy to miss — the Overview page mentions MDC/LOINC/SNOMED
in passing, but nothing on the profile page itself or in a prominent
"How to code a device observation" paragraph makes the additive-coding
expectation explicit. Disposition B costs very little (a short,
targeted narrative update plus an optional `comment` on
`Observation.code`) and directly addresses that discoverability gap.
Disposition A delivers more enforcement but, per erikmoll's explicit
design note, the Device Observation profile is intentionally a
structural mapping artifact for IEEE 11073-10201/10207; adding
LOINC/SNOMED slices pulls it toward being a clinical-coding profile and
risks muddying that boundary. B keeps the profile's role intact while
giving implementers the clear, locatable guidance the ticket is asking
for.
