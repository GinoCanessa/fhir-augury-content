## Summary

The reporter (Malcolm Clarke) asks the Personal Healthcare Devices (PHD)
FHIR IG to give Section 3.4 greater emphasis on the IG's support for
**proprietary devices** — devices that do not natively speak IEEE 11073
but can be mapped through a gateway using nomenclature codes such as
**MDC 10206** and **10101** to form compliant PHD objects. The reporter
notes that proprietary devices are the majority of the market, and that
the value proposition of "consistent messages" is best sold by
highlighting the **gateway's intermediate role** in converging
proprietary device data, Bluetooth profiles, and GHS into the PHD
architecture. The ticket specifically requests an updated/expanded
**diagram** in Section 3.4 showing proprietary devices feeding the
gateway, illustrating the convergence pattern the reporter has used to
map ~10 proprietary devices to the PHD architecture.

The ticket was previously resolved as "Considered for Future Use" and
then reverted; per the 2025-11-12 Devices WG call it has been **deferred
to the next revision of the PHD IG** (post-STU2). It is currently
re-opened (Triaged, Unresolved) so the WG can decide on its disposition
for the next PHD IG cycle. No related Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or
GitHub items were surfaced through cross-reference search; the only
linked artifact is the Devices WG meeting agenda where the deferral was
recorded.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the next revision of the Personal Healthcare Devices (PHD) FHIR IG,
revise **Section 3.4** of the narrative to give first-class treatment to
proprietary-device support. Concretely:

1. Add an introductory paragraph to Section 3.4 stating that the IG
   explicitly supports proprietary (non-11073-native) devices via a
   gateway, and that this represents the majority of real-world device
   deployments.
2. Replace or augment the existing architecture diagram in Section 3.4
   with a new convergence diagram showing several proprietary device
   sources (e.g., proprietary BLE devices, Bluetooth SIG GATT
   profiles, GHS) feeding into the gateway, which maps each onto IEEE
   11073-10206 / 10101 compliant PHD objects emitted as FHIR resources
   downstream.
3. Add a short worked example (or pointer to one) showing how a
   proprietary measurement is mapped through the gateway using
   nomenclature codes 10206 and 10101.
4. Cross-link the new material from the existing gateway/architecture
   sections so that implementers landing in Section 3.4 can navigate to
   the mapping guidance.

The reporter has offered to supply a diagram and example mappings
(~10 proprietary devices, plus BT profiles and GHS); the disposition
should invite that contribution as a starting artifact.

#### Justification

The change is narrative/diagrammatic only — it does not alter
conformance, profiles, terminology bindings, or any normative content
— and it directly addresses a long-standing implementer concern that
the PHD IG is perceived as IEEE 11073-only. Highlighting the gateway as
the convergence point is consistent with the IG's existing architecture
(11073-10206/10101 are already the canonical object model) and aligns
with how vendors actually deploy PHD today. The Devices WG has already
acknowledged the request as valid and deferred only on timing
(post-STU2), so the disposition is essentially a scheduling commitment
plus a concrete content plan.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than expanding Section 3.4 in place, create a **new dedicated
sub-page or chapter** in the next PHD IG revision titled something like
"Supporting Proprietary Devices via the Gateway", and:

1. Keep Section 3.4 focused on the existing 11073-centric architecture
   summary, but add a prominent forward-link ("See: Supporting
   Proprietary Devices") at the top of the section.
2. In the new sub-page, place the convergence diagram, the rationale
   for the gateway pattern, the mapping recipe (proprietary → 10206 /
   10101 → PHD objects → FHIR), and one or two worked examples
   (proprietary BLE, Bluetooth SIG profile, GHS).
3. Add a corresponding entry to the IG TOC and to the
   "Implementer Guidance" landing area so the material is discoverable
   without already knowing to look in Section 3.4.

#### Justification

Section 3.4 today is a high-level architecture summary; appending
proprietary-device guidance, a new diagram, and worked examples risks
overloading it and burying both the original content and the new
material. A dedicated page gives the topic the visibility the reporter
is asking for ("greater emphasis"), keeps the architecture overview
clean, and creates a stable URL to which Zulip threads, vendor docs,
and future tickets can link. It also allows the new content to grow
(more examples, BT profile mapping tables, GHS guidance) over future
revisions without further reshaping Section 3.4.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request as **out of scope for editorial change** on the
grounds that the PHD IG is a normative-leaning IG focused on conformant
11073-derived content, and that gateway-side mapping of proprietary
devices is an implementation concern rather than something the IG
should normatively or editorially endorse. Direct the reporter to
publish the proprietary-device convergence material as an external
companion guide or vendor white paper, and close the ticket as "Not
Persuasive — Implementation Guidance Out of Scope."

#### Justification

This option is defensible only on the narrow ground that the PHD IG
already implicitly supports any input that can be mapped to 11073
objects, and that documenting specific gateway mapping patterns risks
the IG appearing to bless particular proprietary mappings it cannot
actually validate. It would avoid the editorial work and the diagram
maintenance burden, and would keep the IG narrative tightly scoped to
11073-conformant artifacts.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The change is editorial, low-risk, and addresses a substantive concern
from a long-time PHD contributor: that the IG's real-world reach is
under-represented because Section 3.4 reads as 11073-only. The Devices
WG has already deferred (not declined) the ticket and has explicitly
re-opened it for the next PHD IG revision, so the WG's prior posture
already favours acceptance. A new convergence diagram and a paragraph
of framing in Section 3.4 cost little, increase implementer confidence
that the gateway pattern is supported, and can be sourced from
material the reporter has offered to provide. Disposition B is a
reasonable refinement if Section 3.4 turns out to be too crowded once
the diagram is drafted, and can be adopted during editing without
re-balloting the disposition; Disposition C should be rejected because
the IG already depends on the gateway pattern in practice and silence
on it is itself a documentation gap.
