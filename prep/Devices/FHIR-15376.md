## Summary

The reporter asserts that in the January 2018 PoCD (Point-of-Care Devices)
IG, the three introductory pages — *Purpose*, *Devices on FHIR Initiative*,
and *Audience* — overlap in content and scope and should be consolidated
into a single introductory section rather than maintained as three separate
pages. The ticket was originally dispositioned in 2018 as *Considered for
Future Use* (Reinhold / Faughn 9-0-0, non-substantive, "Will rearrange
text as suggested"). That prior resolution was reverted in April 2026 by
`mcros` so the change can be addressed in preparation for the upcoming
September ballot, as agreed by the Devices WG on the
[2026-04-08 Devices Main Call](https://confluence.hl7.org/spaces/HCD/pages/453903324/2026-04-08+Devices+Main+Call+Agenda+and+Minutes).
A comment from `erikmoll` (2026-03-25) notes that in the *current* version
of the IG these three sections already appear together on the home /
introduction page, so the remaining work is primarily a cleanup / prose
rearrangement rather than a structural rewrite. No explicitly-linked Jira
tickets or additional cross-referenced Jira items were found.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Consolidate the *Purpose*, *Devices on FHIR Initiative*, and *Audience*
introductory pages of the Point-of-Care Devices IG into a single
introductory page (e.g., `index` / home) with three sub-sections
(`#purpose`, `#devices-on-fhir-initiative`, `#audience`). Remove the
three standalone page files from the IG's `input/pagecontent/` (or
equivalent) and any references to them from the IG's table of contents
/ `ImplementationGuide` resource so only the consolidated page is
published. Normalise the prose while merging to remove duplicated
statements of scope.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the ticket requests, the Devices WG already agreed
on 2026-04-08 to address it for the September ballot, and the 2018 vote
characterised it as non-substantive ("Will rearrange text as
suggested"). `erikmoll`'s observation that the three sections already
appear together on the home page in the current IG means much of the
work is already done; completing the request is a small editorial
follow-up (dropping the orphaned standalone pages and tidying the
merged prose). The change has no normative impact on conformance,
resources, or profiles.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep a single consolidated *Introduction* page as in Disposition A, but
retain the three headings as anchored sub-sections (`#purpose`,
`#devices-on-fhir-initiative`, `#audience`) **and** leave lightweight
redirect stubs (or anchor-only pages) for the original three page
slugs so that any existing external links into
`.../Purpose.html`, `.../DevicesonFHIRInitiative.html`, and
`.../Audience.html` (including the URLs in the ticket description) do
not 404. Additionally, add a short "About this IG" lead paragraph that
orients new readers before the three sub-sections.

#### Justification

The reporter's underlying goal — a single cohesive intro rather than
three fragmented pages — is preserved, while mitigating link-rot for
older references (the ticket itself cites two 2018Jan URLs that
external material may still link to). This keeps the change
non-substantive but adds minor forward-compatibility value at very
small cost. Appropriate where the WG wants the consolidation without
silently breaking deep links from older publications or slide decks.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as *Not Persuasive / Already Addressed*, on the
grounds that — per `erikmoll`'s 2026-03-25 comment — the current IG
already presents *Purpose*, *Devices on FHIR Initiative*, and
*Audience* as sections of the home/introduction page. The original
2018 observation referred to the *2018Jan* snapshot, which is a frozen
historical publication that will not be retroactively restructured.

#### Justification

If the three sections are already co-located on the home page in the
working IG, there is arguably nothing left to do for a live publication,
and HL7 does not rewrite frozen ballot snapshots. Declining avoids
spending ballot-prep cycles on work that may already be complete and
keeps the editorial surface stable.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A most faithfully honours both the original request and
the Devices WG's 2026-04-08 decision to re-open the ticket for
September ballot prep. The 2018 vote already classified the change as
non-substantive, so there is no normative risk. `erikmoll`'s note
suggests the bulk of the merge is already done in the current IG, so
accepting means a short, scoped editorial pass: remove any lingering
standalone page files for *Purpose* / *Devices on FHIR Initiative* /
*Audience*, confirm they are only referenced as anchors on the
introduction page, and tighten the merged prose. Disposition B is a
reasonable refinement if the WG is worried about external deep links
to the 2018Jan page slugs, and can be folded into A as a minor
implementation detail during execution. Disposition C is not
recommended: declining contradicts the WG's explicit April 2026
decision to address the ticket, and a quick confirmation of the IG
source is lower-cost than re-litigating the disposition.
