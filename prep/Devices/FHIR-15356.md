## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a short "Background — Devices on FHIR (DoF) Initiative" section to
the introductory page of the Point of Care Devices IG. The section
should:

1. State that the IG is produced under the HL7 Devices on FHIR
   Initiative.
2. Briefly describe DoF's mandate (enabling interoperable
   representation of medical-device data using FHIR, building on
   IEEE 11073 work) in 1–2 paragraphs.
3. Explain how that mandate shapes the IG's **purpose** (carry PoCD
   semantics into FHIR), its **audience** (device implementers,
   clinical-data integrators, EHR/PHR vendors), and its **scope**
   (which device data, profiles on `Device`, `DeviceMetric`,
   `Observation`, etc., are in/out of scope).
4. Cross-link to existing PoCD IG home-page content rather than
   duplicating it.

The change is non-substantive (narrative only), so it does not
introduce new resources, profiles, or constraints — it edits the
existing introductory section identified by the comment as "Devices on
FHIR Initiative".

#### Justification

The Devices WG has already agreed (2026-04-08 main call, recorded in
the Confluence minutes) that this comment will be addressed for the
September 2026 ballot, and explicitly reverted the prior Not Persuasive
disposition. The change is editorial / non-substantive and aligns with
the "Accepted – will add the requested content" decision noted in the
comment history. erikmoll's earlier note ("purpose and intended readers
are covered on home page of the IG") is relevant context: the new
background paragraphs should link to, rather than duplicate, the
existing home-page coverage, keeping the addition focused on the DoF
initiative itself.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than adding a new section, **fold the DoF background into the
existing IG home page** (where, per erikmoll, purpose and audience are
already covered). Concretely:

1. Add 1–2 introductory sentences on the home page identifying the IG
   as a DoF Initiative deliverable and linking out to a short
   standalone "About Devices on FHIR" page (one paragraph plus a link
   to the DoF Confluence space / HL7 Devices WG page).
2. Replace the "Devices on FHIR Initiative" placeholder section with a
   pointer to that consolidated page so the same text isn't maintained
   in two places.

#### Justification

The IG home page is the natural single source of truth for purpose and
audience; duplicating that material in a separate background section
risks drift. A short pointer plus a one-paragraph "About DoF" page
satisfies the reporter's request (the background is documented and
discoverable) while minimising future maintenance burden and keeping
the IG narrative coherent. This honours both Terrie Reed's original
request and erikmoll's observation.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Not Persuasive — already addressed**, on the
grounds that the IG home page already covers the IG's purpose and
intended audience (per erikmoll's 2026-03-26 comment) and that the
"Devices on FHIR Initiative" placeholder text is sufficient context for
implementers to find the DoF Initiative through the HL7 Devices WG
pages.

#### Justification

This was the WG's original 2018 disposition (Not Persuasive, 9-0-0)
and was re-affirmed in 2019 ("Adjusted to Not Persuasive, but left as
Deferred"). The change is purely narrative and does not affect
conformance or interoperability; reviewers wanting DoF background can
follow existing links to the Devices WG. Declining keeps editorial
scope tight ahead of the September 2026 ballot.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The Devices WG has already, on the 2026-04-08 main call, explicitly
reverted the prior Not Persuasive resolution and committed to address
this comment for the September 2026 ballot. That call is the most
recent and most authoritative signal from the workgroup, so re-declining
the ticket (Disposition C) would directly contradict a recent WG
decision. Disposition B is reasonable and worth discussing — keeping
purpose/audience on the home page and adding a focused "About DoF"
pointer would avoid duplication — but it is a stylistic refinement of
A rather than a different outcome. Disposition A most directly executes
the WG's stated commitment with the smallest editorial footprint
(a non-substantive narrative addition to the introductory section the
reporter called out), and the editor can incorporate B's "link, don't
duplicate" guidance while drafting it.
