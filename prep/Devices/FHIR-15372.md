## Summary

The reporter (Swapna Abhyankar) asks the Devices work group to clarify, in
the Point of Care Devices (PoCD) FHIR IG itself, how the PoCD IG relates
to the Personal Health Devices (PHD) IG. Specifically, she requests a
high-level explanation of how the two IGs are related, how they differ,
and why they use almost entirely different profiles, plus
references/links between the two IGs at points of overlap or key
divergence so a reader can quickly compare them. The ticket is a
non-functional, ballot-prep clarification (label `R1NFAN`) originally
filed against the 2018-Jan PoCD #39 ballot. After being closed in 2018
("Considered for Future Use"), it was reverted by `mcros` in April 2026
to be addressed in time for the upcoming September ballot, while
`erikmoll` notes (March 2026) that the relationship is already explained
on the PoCD IG home page and "no further action needed."

No related Jira tickets were surfaced by the cross-reference index — the
only cross-references are the Devices WG meeting minutes (Confluence)
where the ticket was put back on the agenda, and an automated FHIR-Bot
tracker-item announcement on Zulip. There are no related GitHub items.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add an explicit "Relationship to the Personal Health Devices (PHD) IG"
section to the PoCD IG home/overview page that:

1. States the scope of each IG (PoCD: acute/clinical point-of-care
   device data; PHD: continua-aligned personal/consumer health device
   data).
2. Lists which FHIR resources each IG profiles (e.g., `Device`,
   `DeviceMetric`, `Observation`, `MedicationAdministration`) and calls
   out the one shared/equivalent profile noted by the reporter.
3. For each significant overlap or divergence point, adds an inline
   link from the PoCD profile/page to the corresponding PHD
   profile/page (and ideally a reciprocal link from PHD back to
   PoCD).
4. Mirrors the section in the PHD IG home page so readers approaching
   from either side land on the same comparison.

Mark the ticket non-substantive/clarification (`R1NFAN`) and resolve
once the home-page section and cross-links are in place for the
September ballot.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter asked for and what the Devices WG
re-affirmed on 2026-04-08 when reverting the 2018 "Considered for
Future Use" resolution. Ballot reviewers were explicitly told to
review both IGs together for harmonization, so the IG itself is the
right place to set that context — without it, every reviewer must
re-derive the comparison. `erikmoll`'s 2026-03-30 comment indicates
home-page text already exists, so the work is largely about confirming
coverage of all the points above (especially the cross-links at points
of overlap) rather than starting from scratch.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Instead of (or in addition to) extending the PoCD home page, publish a
single jointly-owned "PoCD ↔ PHD Harmonization" page hosted by the
Devices WG (e.g., on the HL7 Confluence Devices space, linked from
both IG home pages) that provides:

- A side-by-side resource/profile comparison table (PoCD profile ↔ PHD
  profile ↔ FHIR base resource ↔ rationale for divergence).
- A short narrative on scope boundaries and why the two IGs
  intentionally use different profiles.
- Versioned per ballot cycle so it does not drift as either IG
  evolves.

Both IG home pages would carry a short paragraph and a link to this
page rather than each maintaining their own prose.

#### Justification

A standalone harmonization document removes the duplication risk of
maintaining the same comparison in two IG home pages that ship on
different cadences, and it gives implementers a single canonical
artifact to cite. The downside — added discoverability burden, since
readers must follow a link off-IG — is mitigated by linking
prominently from both home pages. This is preferable when the WG
expects the comparison to grow (more profiles, more divergence
points) or to be reused outside the IG narratives (e.g., in
implementer guidance or connectathon track materials).

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as "Already Addressed" / "Persuasive with Mod" with the
rationale that `erikmoll`'s 2026-03-30 update — "The relation with the
PHD IG is now explained on the PoCD IG home page. No further action
needed." — already satisfies the request, and that no spec change is
warranted before the September ballot. If a reviewer disagrees during
ballot, file a fresh ticket pointing at specific gaps in the existing
home-page text.

#### Justification

The author of the relevant home-page text reports the work is done.
The original 2018 resolution was also "Considered for Future Use"
(non-substantive clarification) by 9-0-0, suggesting the WG has not
historically viewed this as blocking. Re-opening risks consuming
ballot-prep effort on prose that already exists, and leaves the door
open for fresh, more concrete tickets if the existing text is
insufficient.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The Devices WG explicitly reverted the prior resolution on 2026-04-08
*after* `erikmoll`'s "no further action needed" comment, which is a
strong signal that the WG considers the existing home-page text
inadequate (or at least unverified) for the September ballot.
Disposition A keeps the work inside the IG where reviewers will
actually look, satisfies the reporter's specific ask for inline
cross-links at points of overlap, and is scoped as a non-substantive
clarification — so the cost is low and the ballot risk of leaving it
unaddressed is real. Disposition B is a reasonable enhancement but
should not block the ballot; it can be layered on later if the
comparison grows. Disposition C contradicts the WG's documented
2026-04-08 decision and should only be revisited if, on review, the
current home-page text already covers the four bullets in
Disposition A's proposal.
