## Summary

The reporter (Malcolm Clarke) flags a clarity defect in **Section 3.2** of
the Personal Healthcare Devices (PHD) FHIR IG. The cited sentence
introduces IEEE 11073-10206 ACOM PHD specializations and uses the terms
**"objects"**, **"attributes"**, and **"observation types"** in close
succession in a way that reads as if they are interchangeable, even
though in the underlying 11073-10206 model they are distinct concepts
(MDC objects carry attributes; observation *types* are a separate
classification of what a PHD reports). The request is editorial /
technical-correction in nature: rewrite the passage so that the
relationship between objects, their attributes, and the observation
types a PHD emits is unambiguous to a FHIR-only reader who is not
already steeped in 11073 terminology.

There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub
items for this ticket. The only cross-reference surfaced by Augury is
to the Devices WG meeting-minutes Confluence page
([2025-11-12 Devices Main Call](https://confluence.hl7.org/spaces/HCD/pages/404097645/2025-11-12+Devices+Main+Call+Agenda+and+Minutes)),
where the WG reviewed and **deferred** the change to the next revision
of the PHD IG (i.e., the resolution was reverted from "Considered for
Future Use" and the ticket was reopened post-STU2 publication, per the
2026-03-31 comments by lynn_laakso).

## Proposed Dispositions

> Note: Because the ticket has no GitHub cross-references, no per-repo
> briefing exists in `cache/github/repos/`. The dispositions below

> specific authoring root. The Devices WG should confirm the exact

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Rewrite the offending paragraph in **Section 3.2 of the Personal
Healthcare Devices (FHIR) IG** so that "object", "attribute", and
"observation type" are each introduced once and then used
consistently. Concretely:

1. Open with a one-sentence definition of an **MDC *object*** (an
   instance of an 11073-10206 information-model class) and an **MDC
   *attribute*** (a typed property of such an object), explicitly
   noting that attributes have data types analogous to FHIR resource
   elements.
2. Introduce **observation type** as a separate concept — the *kind of
   measurement* a PHD reports (e.g., body temperature, tidal volume) —
   and clarify that a single PHD specialization may emit one or many
   observation types using the same shared set of objects/attributes.
3. Re-state the existing examples (thermometer = one observation type;
   home ventilator = several; sleep apnea breathing therapy PHD =
   80+ observation types) using the now-disambiguated vocabulary.
4. Add a short glossary entry (or link to the existing IG glossary, if
   one exists) for *object*, *attribute*, and *observation type*.

No normative content changes; conformance, profiles, and value sets
remain untouched.

#### Justification

This is a **Technical Correction** — exactly the change class for
narrative clarity defects. The cited sentence is misleading to FHIR
implementers who do not have 11073-10206 background; without the fix,
they may conflate "objects" with "observation types" and produce
incorrect Observation/Device/DeviceMetric mappings. The change has no
breaking impact on profiles or instances and is consistent with how
adjacent IGs (e.g., the Point-of-Care Devices IG) introduce 11073
concepts before using them. The Devices WG already endorsed acting on
this ticket — they merely deferred the work to the next PHD IG
revision per the 2025-11-12 minutes — so the substantive question is
*when*, not *whether*.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Address the confusion structurally rather than by paragraph rewrite:

1. Add a new short subsection (e.g., **3.1.x "Information-model
   primer: objects, attributes, and observation types"**) that
   defines the three terms once, with a small diagram or table mapping
   each 11073 concept to its FHIR analog
   (object → DeviceComponent / Device, attribute → DeviceMetric /
   Observation.component, observation type → Observation.code).
2. Reduce the Section 3.2 paragraph to a *reference* to that primer
   plus the concrete PHD-specialization examples.
3. Apply the same vocabulary discipline across the IG by linking other
   appearances of these terms back to the primer.

#### Justification

The reporter's complaint is symptomatic of a wider problem: the IG
repeatedly assumes 11073 fluency. A localized rewrite (Disposition A)
fixes one paragraph but leaves the same trap elsewhere. A primer
subsection is a slightly larger edit but pays compounding clarity
dividends and gives the WG a single canonical place to evolve the
terminology in future ballots. Trade-offs vs. A: more authoring effort
now and a (very small) risk of cross-references elsewhere needing to
be touched; offset by lower long-term maintenance and better onboarding
for non-Devices implementers.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Persuasive with Modification — Already Addressed**
(or simply *Not Persuasive*) on the grounds that the terms "object",
"attribute", and "observation type" are defined in the upstream
**IEEE 11073-10206 ACOM** standard and that the PHD IG explicitly
profiles that standard; readers are expected to consult ACOM for
definitions. Add at most a single sentence linking the reader to the
ACOM specification.

#### Justification

This disposition is defensible only on the basis that PHD IG readers
are, by audience, device-domain implementers who will already know
11073 terminology, and that duplicating ACOM definitions in a FHIR IG
risks drift. It also avoids any rewrite work in the next IG revision
window. However, it ignores the WG's own 2025-11-12 decision to keep
the ticket alive for the next revision, and it does not address the
specific charge that the *single sentence* uses the terms
*synonymously* — a defect that exists regardless of the reader's prior
knowledge.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **A** (with a strong nudge toward B if
authoring bandwidth permits).

The Devices WG has already signalled that this is a real defect worth
fixing — the ticket was explicitly reverted from "Considered for Future
Use" and reopened post-STU2 with a deferral to the next PHD IG
revision. Disposition C contradicts that decision and should only be
revisited if the WG changes its mind. Disposition A is the lowest-risk,
lowest-effort way to honour that decision: a narrative-only edit to one
paragraph in Section 3.2, with no normative impact and no profile
churn. Disposition B is strictly better for long-term IG quality but is
a bigger ask for a single STU-correction ticket; the pragmatic path is
to land A in the next PHD IG revision and open a follow-up ticket to
introduce the primer subsection if the WG agrees the underlying
clarity gap is broader. Either way, the rewrite should preserve the
existing thermometer / home-ventilator / sleep-apnea examples, since
they are concrete, well-loved, and exactly the kind of anchor that
helps the new vocabulary stick.
