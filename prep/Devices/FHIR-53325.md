## Summary

The reporter (Malcolm Clarke) requests that the Personal Healthcare Devices
(PHD) FHIR Implementation Guide explicitly mention the IEEE 11073-20601
specification and document where mappings *differ* from IEEE 11073-10206 —
specifically in areas such as **power reporting** and **clock/time**
attributes — while noting that **metric (measurement) observations are the
same** between the two. The current PHD IG positions -20601 as an
"outdated predecessor" of -10206; commenters point out that -20601 is
still being actively revised, remains the methodological basis for many
PHG implementations, and is in fact still the foundation for parts of
this IG. The Devices WG triaged this on 2025-11-12 and decided to defer
to the next revision of the PHD IG (post-STU2 publication) rather than
declining outright; lynn_laakso reverted an earlier "Considered for
Future Use" resolution and re-opened the ticket on 2026-03-31 for
disposition in the next cycle.

There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets, no Zulip threads that cite
this ticket key directly, and no related GitHub artifacts. The only
cross-reference is the Devices WG meeting minutes page on Confluence
where the deferral was recorded.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a new narrative subsection to the PHD IG's "Background / Source
Specifications" page that:

1. Explicitly cites IEEE 11073-20601 (Optimized Exchange Protocol)
   alongside IEEE 11073-10206 (Application Hosting Device Profile),
   describing -20601 as the still-active basis on which many deployed
   Personal Health Gateways (PHGs) are built rather than as an
   "outdated predecessor".
2. Adds a "Mapping differences" table (or bullet list) that names the
   areas where the FHIR mapping diverges between -20601 and -10206 —
   specifically **power-source / battery reporting attributes** and
   **clock / time-synchronization attributes** — and states that
   **metric (measurement) observation mappings are unchanged**.
3. Replaces any current wording in the IG narrative that calls -20601
   "outdated" with neutral language (e.g., "predecessor protocol, still
   in active use"), and adds a normative reference entry for IEEE
   11073-20601.

#### Justification

The reporter and at least one commenter (JIRAUSER29419) document that
-20601 is being actively revised and remains the basis for parts of the
IG itself; the Zulip thread *ContinuaDeviceIdentifiers codesystem* shows
the WG continues to cite -20601:2019 normatively. Documenting only the
*differences* (rather than restating equivalent metric mappings) keeps
the narrative compact and matches the reporter's own scoping ("Metric
observations are the same"). It is also consistent with how the IG
already documents source-spec mappings for -10201 DIM and -10207 SDC
(per the *PoCD and PHD IG's* Zulip thread).

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than expanding the IG narrative, add a single dedicated page
(e.g., `phd-20601-relationship.html`) that:

1. Scopes the relationship between -20601 and -10206 in one place,
   including the "outdated predecessor" wording correction.
2. For each PHD profile / mapping page that has a meaningful divergence
   (power, clock), adds a short "20601 mapping note" callout block that
   links back to the dedicated page rather than duplicating the table.
3. Defers any change to actual profiles, extensions, or value sets —
   this is a documentation/narrative-only change.

#### Justification

This keeps the change surgical and isolated from profile artifacts,
which reduces ballot risk and makes the change easy to reverse if
-20601's revision lands with material differences before the next
publication. It also gives implementers a single canonical page to
cite, which is more maintainable than scattering -20601 caveats across
many profile intros. Trade-off versus Disposition A: implementers
reading just the source-spec background section would miss the
mapping-difference table unless they follow the link, so discoverability
is slightly weaker.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as **Not Persuasive** with the rationale that the IG already
mentions -20601 as the predecessor to -10206 (per erikmoll's 2025-11-12
comment) and that detailed -20601-vs-10206 mapping notes are out of
scope for the PHD IG, which is normatively built on -10206.

#### Justification

This is the position erikmoll initially proposed ("No further action
needed"). It is defensible on scope grounds: the IG's normative source
is -10206, and exhaustive divergence documentation could be left to the
IEEE specifications themselves. However, this position was effectively
overruled when lynn_laakso reverted the resolution on 2026-03-31 and
re-opened the ticket for the next PHD IG cycle, so the WG would need a
strong new argument to revisit it.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The Devices WG has already, in effect, chosen not to decline this
ticket — the previous "Considered for Future Use" resolution was
reverted and the ticket explicitly deferred to the next revision of the
PHD IG. That sequence indicates the WG accepts that some change is
warranted; the open question is scope, not whether. Disposition A
matches both the reporter's literal request and the substantive concern
raised in the comments (that calling -20601 "outdated" misrepresents
its ongoing role in PHGs), while remaining a documentation-only change
with no impact on profiles, value sets, or conformance. Disposition B
is a reasonable structural alternative if the WG prefers a single
dedicated page, and the recommendation can easily be downgraded to B
during the call if the editors prefer a single-source-of-truth page
over inline narrative edits. Either way, the change should be tagged
for the post-STU2 PHD IG revision per lynn_laakso's re-open note.
