## Summary

The ticket reports a defect in Section 2.11 of the Personal Healthcare
Devices (PHD) FHIR IG. The narrative currently states that "The required
`Patient.identifier` entry contains an `identifier.type`", but the
reporter notes that some deployments do not send patient-identifiable
information at all — instead mapping the device ID to the patient on the
back end. On that basis, the reporter asks for "required" to be removed
from the narrative so that the prose matches real deployment practice
and the profile constraints.

The ticket is closely tied to [FHIR-48224](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48224)
("PhdPatient is overly constrained."), which was resolved Persuasive
and changed the formal `PhdPatient` slicing so that a patient identifier
using v2-0203 (with system and value) is required *only when a Patient
resource is actually created by the PHG*, while other identifiers no
longer require system/value. As commenter `erikmoll` points out, the
profile's formal view no longer shows `Patient.identifier` as mandatory
after FHIR-48224, so the Section 2.11 prose is now inconsistent with
the profile — it still speaks of a required `Patient.identifier` entry
even though (a) a Patient resource may never be uploaded by the PHG,
and (b) when one is uploaded the identifier is only required on the
v2-0203 slice. The fix is therefore a narrative/text alignment in the
IG rather than a profile change.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In Section 2.11 of the Personal Healthcare Devices (PHD) IG, remove the
word "required" from the sentence "The required `Patient.identifier`
entry contains an `identifier.type`." Reword the surrounding prose so
that it states that *if* a Patient resource is created by the PHG and
*if* a `Patient.identifier` entry is included, *then* the v2-0203 slice
must carry `identifier.type.coding.system`,
`identifier.type.coding.code`, `identifier.system` and
`identifier.value`. Cross-reference the existing sentences in the same
section that already say "The PHD IG does not require uploaders to send
any patient information" and "There is a special case where the PHG
will not upload a Patient resource" so that the narrative is internally
consistent. This is a pure narrative/IG-page change, not a profile
change.

#### Justification

The `PhdPatient` profile was formally relaxed by
[FHIR-48224](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48224) (Persuasive,
Published): the formal view no longer marks `Patient.identifier` as
present/required. erikmoll's comment documents this precisely.
Leaving Section 2.11 saying "required" contradicts the normative
profile and the adjacent narrative, and it misleads implementers into
thinking they must populate patient identifiers even in deployments
that map device → patient on the back end. Aligning the prose with the
profile is a low-risk technical correction that faithfully reflects
the reporter's request. The Devices WG has already flagged this as a
next-IG-revision item (deferred by lynn_laakso on 2026-03-31 per the
2025-11-12 Devices Main Call), so accepting it slots naturally into
that work.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than just deleting "required", rewrite Section 2.11 to mirror
the two-path structure already implied by `PhdPatient`:

1. Path A — PHG does **not** upload a Patient resource. Call out
   explicitly that no `Patient.identifier` constraints apply from
   `PhdPatient`; the logical id must be supplied to the PHG by other
   means (already stated elsewhere in the section).
2. Path B — PHG **does** upload a Patient resource. State the exact
   formal requirement from the post-FHIR-48224 profile: at least one
   `Patient.identifier` slice using v2-0203 with
   `identifier.type.coding.system`, `identifier.type.coding.code`,
   `identifier.system`, and `identifier.value` populated; other
   identifier slices have no such constraint.

Additionally, add a short "Per [FHIR-48224](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48224)"
provenance note so future readers can trace the narrative to the
profile change. Audit neighbouring sections (as FHIR-48224 itself asks:
"Review other IG artifacts for similar issues and correct.") and fix
any parallel wording there in the same pass.

#### Justification

The underlying problem isn't just one word — the section currently
asserts a blanket "required" that is not aligned with either the
formal profile *or* the other paragraphs of the same section. A
minimal edit (Disposition A) removes the immediate contradiction but
leaves the section ambiguous about *when* identifiers are required.
Rewriting to the two-path structure matches how the profile actually
behaves post-FHIR-48224, closes the door on similar misreadings, and
discharges the "Review other IG artifacts" instruction that
FHIR-48224's resolution explicitly asked for. It costs slightly more
editorial work for materially better text.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as Not Persuasive on the grounds that Section 2.11
already contains the surrounding caveats ("The PHD IG does not require
uploaders to send any patient information", "There is a special case
where the PHG will not upload a Patient resource", etc.) and that a
careful reader can therefore reconcile the "required" wording with
those caveats without further text changes.

#### Justification

Defensible only on the grounds that the formal profile is normative
and the narrative is informative, so a profile-vs-prose mismatch is
not strictly a conformance defect. In practice this rationale is
weak: the prose is directly contradictory ("required" vs. "does not
require"), the reporter has identified a real deployment pattern
(back-end device→patient mapping) that the text actively misrepresents,
and a Devices WG member (erikmoll) has already agreed the issue
should be accepted. Declining would leave an internal inconsistency
in a published IG section that was explicitly called out by the
resolution of FHIR-48224 ("Text needs to be updated to reflect what
the profile requires.").

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the right call. The ticket as literally written
(Disposition A) is correct but too narrow — it only removes the word
"required", which leaves the section silent about *when* identifiers
are in fact mandated by the post-FHIR-48224 profile. FHIR-48224's own
resolution instruction ("Review other IG artifacts for similar issues
and correct.") points at exactly the kind of two-path rewrite
Disposition B proposes, and erikmoll's comment already enumerates the
specific conditional constraints the rewritten text should capture.
Given the change has already been deferred to the next PHD IG revision
per the 2025-11-12 Devices call, picking up the slightly larger edit
now (while the profile/narrative mismatch is fresh) is cheaper than
revisiting Section 2.11 again later. Disposition C is not defensible:
the contradiction is explicit and a WG member has already agreed it
should be fixed.
