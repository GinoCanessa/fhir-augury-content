## Summary

The reporter observes that the **Differential view** rendered for the
Observation-derived profiles in the Point of Care Devices (PoCD) IG
(e.g., `NumericObservation`) shows `effective[x]`, `effectivePeriod`,
`effectiveDateTime`, and `effectiveInstant` as **siblings** rather than
showing the three concrete typed slices nested under the polymorphic
`effective[x]` choice. Because `effectiveDateTime`, `effectivePeriod`,
and `effectiveInstant` are type-specific renderings of the same choice
element, treating them as independent elements in the differential is
misleading and inconsistent with how choice elements should be
displayed.

The issue was originally filed in 2021 and previously resolved as
*Considered for Future Use* (Martin Rosner / Stefan Karl 6-0-0,
2021-12-08), with the working theory that this was an IG-publisher
rendering bug rather than a defect in the IG source. In March 2026
Erik Moll re-checked the current Continuous Integration build
(`build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/uv-pocd/...`) against the 2021Sep snapshot and
reported that the current publisher renders the differential
correctly, asking whether that is sufficient. On 2026-04-08 the
Devices WG (recorded on the Confluence cross-reference,
`HCD/pages/453903324/2026-04-08+Devices+Main+Call+Agenda+and+Minutes`)
agreed to address this in preparation for the September ballot, and
the previous *Considered for Future Use* resolution was reverted
(comments by `mcros`, 2026-04-16). The ticket is now back in
**Triaged** state and labelled `Block-Vote-1` for that ballot cycle.

The cross-reference machinery surfaced only the Confluence WG-minutes
page; no related Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub PRs/issues
were returned by `cross-referenced` or by a Zulip search on the ticket
key.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Confirm the rendering defect against the **current** PoCD CI build
(`build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/uv-pocd/`) for **every** Observation-derived
profile in the IG (at minimum `NumericObservation`, but the original
report says "the same applies for all Observation profiles"). For each
profile whose differential still shows `effective[x]`,
`effectivePeriod`, `effectiveDateTime`, and `effectiveInstant` as
peers rather than nested under the `effective[x]` choice, treat the
issue as **resolved by tooling** for any profile that now renders
correctly under the latest IG-publisher / SUSHI versions, and **lock
the IG to that publisher version (or newer)** in the IG's
`publication-request` / `package-list.json` / `sushi-config.yaml` so
the September ballot build cannot regress. Where a profile still
renders incorrectly, file a tooling bug against
`HL7/fhir-ig-publisher` (or `FHIR/sushi`, depending on which layer
produces the differential view), referencing this ticket and supplying
a minimal reproducer profile.

Mark FHIR-33668 *Persuasive – With Modification* with a substantive
text along the lines of: *"Verified against IG-publisher vX.Y.Z that
the differential view now nests the typed `effective[x]` slices
correctly for all PoCD Observation profiles. Pinned the IG build to
that publisher version for the September ballot. No source changes to
the StructureDefinitions are required."*

#### Justification

This is exactly what the ticket asks for: make the differential render
correctly for every Observation profile in PoCD. Erik Moll's 2026-03-19
comment already provides initial evidence that the current publisher
renders correctly; the WG only needs to (a) verify across **all**
affected profiles, not just `NumericObservation`, and (b) make the
fix durable for ballot by pinning the publisher version. The original
2021 disposition (*Considered for Future Use*) explicitly contemplated
this path: "if it is not resolved by tooling changes it will be further
investigated for any possible cause in the IG source material" — i.e.,
tooling-side resolution was always the preferred route. Accepting as
requested also matches the `Block-Vote-1` label, which implies a
straightforward, low-risk technical correction the WG is willing to
batch into a single ballot vote.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Resolve the issue at the **IG source** layer rather than relying on
the publisher version. Audit the affected Observation-derived
profiles' source (FSH under `input/fsh/` or JSON under `input/resources/`
in `HL7/uv-pocd`) and ensure that constraints on the `effective[x]`
choice are expressed against the **choice element itself**
(`Observation.effective[x]`) using SUSHI / StructureDefinition
mechanics that the publisher renders nested — for example, by
constraining `effective[x]` types or by adding `* effective[x] only
dateTime or Period or instant` rather than separately constraining
`effectiveDateTime`, `effectivePeriod`, and `effectiveInstant` as
distinct paths. If any current profile differential carries entries
keyed by the typed paths (`effectiveDateTime`, etc.) instead of by
`effective[x]`, restructure those constraints so the differential is
expressed purely in terms of `effective[x]` plus type-restriction
rules.

Couple this with Disposition A (publisher-version pin) as defence in
depth, but record the source-side cleanup as the *primary* fix and the
publisher pin as a guardrail.

#### Justification

The 2021 WG note ("if it is not resolved by tooling changes it will be
further investigated for any possible cause in the IG source
material") explicitly leaves the door open to a source-side cause.
Even if the current publisher hides the symptom, profiles that author
constraints against the typed paths instead of the choice itself are
fragile: any future publisher refactor of choice-element rendering
could re-expose the same misleading view, and the same constraints
will look wrong to humans inspecting the FSH / JSON directly. Fixing
the source makes the IG self-describing and immune to future tooling
churn, at the cost of more authoring work and a small risk of
re-touching the snapshot.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve as **Not Persuasive** (or, equivalently, *Already Addressed*),
on the grounds that the symptom described in 2021 is no longer
reproducible against the current IG-publisher build, the original
disposition (*Considered for Future Use*, 2021-12-08, 6-0-0) already
characterised this as a tooling rendering issue rather than an IG
defect, and no IG source change is warranted. Close the ticket without
modifying any StructureDefinition, with a comment pointing to Erik
Moll's 2026-03-19 verification and to the upstream IG-publisher /
SUSHI projects as the correct venue for any residual rendering
concerns.

#### Justification

Erik Moll's 2026-03-19 comment establishes that the differential
renders correctly today, and the original WG vote already concluded
the IG itself is not at fault. Re-opening the ticket and producing a
ballot block-vote entry for what is in effect a no-op risks reviewer
confusion ("what changed in PoCD?") and burns ballot bandwidth that
could be spent on substantive corrections. If the publisher
regresses, a fresh tracker against `HL7/fhir-ig-publisher` would be
the more direct remedy.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The Devices WG has already, on 2026-04-08, agreed to address this
ticket before the September ballot and reverted the 2021 *Considered
for Future Use* resolution; declining now (Disposition C) would
contradict that decision and waste the WG's recent triage effort.
Disposition B is technically attractive but requires a source audit of
every Observation-derived profile in `HL7/uv-pocd` and risks moving
snapshots in a ballot window — disproportionate work for a defect that
the current publisher already renders correctly. Disposition A
captures the WG's intent precisely: verify the rendering across **all**
Observation profiles (not just `NumericObservation`), pin the IG build
to a publisher version that renders the choice element correctly, and
record a *Persuasive – With Modification* response that explains why
no StructureDefinition source change was needed. That gives the
balloters a clear, auditable answer, satisfies the `Block-Vote-1`
label, and leaves Disposition B available as a follow-up tracker if a
future publisher regression ever re-exposes the symptom.
