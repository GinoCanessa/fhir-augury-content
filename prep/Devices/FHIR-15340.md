## Summary

This ticket, originally filed by Todd Cooper against the January 2018
Point of Care Devices (PoCD) FHIR ballot (comment #7), asks the Devices
work group to expand the Use Cases section of the PoCD Implementation
Guide so that it explicitly motivates and demonstrates the *full* set
of profiled resources — `Device`, `DeviceComponent`, and `DeviceMetric`
— and not just the common "Patient → Observation → value" reporting
flow that most enterprise systems implement today. The reporter's core
concern is that omitting use cases for the device-component and
device-metric content under-sells what "Devices on FHIR" enables and
risks implementers continuing to ignore the structured device-side
content that the IG profiles. Cross-references on this item are limited
to the Devices WG's 2026-04-08 main-call minutes
(Confluence page 453903324), where the WG agreed to address this in
preparation for the upcoming September ballot, plus an automated
fhir_bot tracker-item announcement in the `#committers` Zulip stream;
there are no linked sibling Jira tickets and no related GitHub items.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the WG's already-agreed plan (2026-04-08 Devices main call) and
expand the **Use Cases / Overview** section of the Point of Care
Devices (FHIR) IG to add explicit, narrative use cases that exercise
the full set of profiled resources — not just `Patient` +
`Observation`, but also `Device`, `DeviceComponent`, and
`DeviceMetric`. Concretely:

- Add at least three new use case narratives in the IG Overview, each
  highlighting *why* the corresponding profile exists (one focused on
  the device hierarchy via `DeviceComponent`, one on per-metric
  metadata via `DeviceMetric`, and one on a multi-metric streaming /
  alarming scenario that uses all three together).
- For each new use case, link the narrative to the matching profile
  page(s) in the IG so the rationale is one click away from the
  conformance content.
- Frame the section as illustrative ("not exhaustive") per the
  2026-03-30 comment, and call out explicitly what each use case adds
  *over and above* the FHIR Core Device/Observation pattern.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter (Todd Cooper) asked for, and it is
already the documented direction of the current Devices WG (mcros,
2026-04-16). The change is editorial / narrative — it does not alter
profile cardinalities, value sets, or invariants — so the
"Non-substantive" impact assessment recorded in 2018 still applies and
the risk of regression for existing implementers is minimal. The IG
itself is the right home for this content because the gap is precisely
that implementers reading the IG cannot tell why
`DeviceComponent`/`DeviceMetric` are profiled at all.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than expanding the IG's own Overview, address the gap by
authoring **stand-alone example bundles** that exercise `Device` +
`DeviceComponent` + `DeviceMetric` end-to-end (e.g., a multi-parameter
patient-monitor snapshot, an infusion-pump telemetry sample) and link
those examples from a much shorter "see examples" pointer in the IG
Overview. The use case narrative is then carried by realistic
instances rather than prose.

#### Justification

Implementers tend to learn from worked examples faster than from
narrative use cases, and example resources are testable / loadable
(unlike prose). This also keeps the IG's Overview lean and avoids the
maintenance burden of keeping a long use-case section in sync with
profile changes over future ballot cycles. Trade-off vs. Disposition
A: examples alone do not explain *why* a given profile exists, so this
approach risks under-serving readers who want the rationale before the
JSON — which was explicitly the reporter's complaint.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Persuasive with Modification → already addressed
elsewhere**, on the grounds that the IG's per-profile pages and the
IHE PCD / IEEE 11073 source material that the profiles derive from
already document the rationale for `DeviceComponent` and
`DeviceMetric`, and that the IG Overview is intentionally a short
landing page rather than a use-case catalogue.

#### Justification

This is defensible only on a strict-scope reading: ballot comments
that ask for additional explanatory prose can sometimes be deferred to
companion documents (white papers, IHE technical frameworks) rather
than the conformance-bearing IG. The argument is that the IG should
state *what* conformance requires, not *why* implementers should care
— marketing the profiles is a different deliverable.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The Devices WG has already decided on this direction in its 2026-04-08
main call, and the WG explicitly reverted the 2018 "Considered for
Future Use" resolution because, in the WG's own words, "this
important concept needs emphasis and explanation." Disposition A is a
non-substantive, narrative-only change that lands in the IG's
Overview, costs nothing in terms of breaking changes or tooling, and
directly answers the reporter's concern that implementers keep
reducing PoCD to "Patient + Observation" and ignoring the
device-side profiles that justify the IG's existence. Disposition B is
a worthwhile *addition* but should not replace narrative use cases;
the workgroup can adopt it as a follow-on, alongside A, by also
authoring example bundles. Disposition C contradicts the WG's recent
vote and the reporter's documented intent, and would leave the
already-acknowledged "important concept needs emphasis and
explanation" gap unaddressed in the September ballot.
