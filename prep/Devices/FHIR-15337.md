## Summary

The reporter (Beth Pumo, January 2018 PoCD ballot comment #4) asks why the
Point-of-Care Device (PoCD) IG uses the generic `SampleArray` data type while
the Personal Health Device (PHD) IG uses the more specific
`Real-Time-Sample-Array` (RTSA) construct, and requests an explanation for
the variation if it is intentional. The substantive answer was already given
in 2018 by jrhoads: PHD deliberately constrains its profile to a single
real-time waveform pattern, whereas PoCD intentionally allows additional
`SampleArray` subtypes (e.g., trend data) and therefore cannot be narrowed
to RTSA without losing in-scope use cases. erikmoll concurred in
March 2026 ("No good reason to change."). The ticket was originally resolved
as "Considered for Future Use" on 2018-03-28 (Reinhold/Faughn 9-0-0) and
marked a duplicate of FHIR-15336, but that resolution was reverted on
2026-04-16 by mcros so the question can be revisited and formally answered
in preparation for the September 2026 ballot, per the Devices WG
2026-04-08 main call. FHIR-15336 (the cited duplicate) is not present in
the Augury index, so no companion ticket context could be retrieved here.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat the ticket as a documentation request and add an explanatory note to
the PoCD IG (Point of Care Devices, FHIR) — in the narrative section that
introduces device observations / waveforms — stating that PoCD uses the
generic `SampleArray` data type (rather than a profiled
`Real-Time-Sample-Array`) **on purpose**, because PoCD must accommodate
multiple `SampleArray` subtypes such as trend arrays, episodic captures,
and non-real-time recordings, in addition to real-time waveforms. Cross-
reference the PHD IG's `Real-Time-Sample-Array` profile and explain that
PHD intentionally narrows scope to a single real-time waveform pattern.
Mark the change-request impact as **Non-substantive / Considered for
Future Use → Persuasive with Modification** for the September 2026 ballot.

#### Justification

This addresses exactly what the original reporter asked for: an
explanation of the variation. The substantive answer already exists in
the comment thread (jrhoads, 2018-02-19) and was endorsed again by
erikmoll in March 2026. Capturing it in the published narrative prevents
the question from being re-raised by future ballot reviewers, keeps PoCD
and PHD intentionally distinct, and is consistent with HL7's general
practice of documenting deliberate inter-IG divergences in the affected
spec rather than forcing alignment. The Devices WG already agreed on the
2026-04-08 call to revisit the ticket for the September ballot, which
makes a narrative update the lowest-risk way to "address" it.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Instead of a narrative-only change, publish a **comparison table** in the
PoCD IG (and optionally a mirror in the PHD IG) that enumerates each
`SampleArray` subtype PoCD permits (real-time waveform, periodic trend,
episodic capture, etc.), shows which are in scope for PHD vs. PoCD, and
links each row to the underlying IEEE 11073 / Observation.value[x]
guidance. Optionally introduce a lightweight `SampleArray`-typed
*example* Observation per subtype so implementers can see the shape of
each variant. Resolution category: **Persuasive with Modification**.

#### Justification

A table plus examples is more durable than free-text narrative: it gives
implementers an unambiguous, side-by-side view of the PoCD/PHD choice and
makes it harder for the same question to recur with each ballot cycle.
Trade-off vs. Disposition A: more authoring effort and a marginally
larger surface area to maintain, but the artifact also serves several
adjacent Devices tickets that periodically ask about waveform vs. trend
modeling. No code/schema change is implied — the binding to
`Observation.value[x] = SampleArray` stays unchanged — so backward
compatibility is preserved.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket **Not Persuasive** (or restore the prior **Considered for
Future Use** disposition from 2018-03-28, Reinhold/Faughn 9-0-0) on the
grounds that (a) the answer already exists in the public Jira comment
thread, (b) the PoCD/PHD divergence is intentional and previously
ratified, and (c) no specification text actually needs to change.

#### Justification

The substantive question was answered in 2018 and reaffirmed in 2026
("No good reason to change."). The ticket is, strictly speaking, a
question rather than a change request, and HL7 has historically closed
such items as *Considered for Future Use* / *Not Persuasive* when the
existing design is correct. Declining avoids spending September-ballot
cycles on a non-substantive narrative tweak.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The Devices WG has already explicitly decided on the 2026-04-08 main call
to *re-open* this ticket and address it for the September 2026 ballot,
which rules out Disposition C — leaving the prior "Considered for Future
Use" in place would directly contradict that decision and the
mcros 2026-04-16 revert. Between A and B, Disposition A is the right
starting point: the underlying answer is already well-formed in the
comment thread and only needs to be moved into the published PoCD
narrative as a short, clearly-labelled "Why `SampleArray` and not
`Real-Time-Sample-Array`?" note that cites the PHD profile. That fully
satisfies the reporter's original ask, costs almost nothing to author,
and is straightforwardly **Non-substantive**. Disposition B (table +
examples) is a reasonable upgrade if the WG wants to invest more authoring
effort, but it should be treated as an enhancement layered *on top of*
Disposition A rather than a replacement for it.
