## Summary

The reporter (Malcolm Clarke) flags an incorrect assertion in Section 3.2 of
the Personal Healthcare Devices (PHD) FHIR IG. The current narrative says that
the IEEE 11073-10206 ACOM objects are "analogous" to FHIR resources and that
ACOM attributes are "analogous" to FHIR resource elements. The reporter
asserts this is historically wrong: Devices on FHIR was modeled on IEEE
11073-**10201** (the Domain Information Model / DIM), not 10206 (ACOM), and
the relationship to 10201 is a direct equivalency rather than an analogy.
The ask is therefore a technical correction to the IG's narrative wording
(and conceptual framing) so it accurately reflects the standards lineage on
which the Device / DeviceMetric / Observation mappings were built. No related
Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were surfaced; the only
cross-reference is the Devices WG meeting page (2025-11-12) where the WG
agreed to defer the change to the next revision of the PHD IG.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply a narrative technical correction to Section 3.2 of the Personal
Healthcare Devices (PHD) FHIR IG. Replace the sentence asserting an analogy
between IEEE 11073-10206 (ACOM) objects/attributes and FHIR resources/elements
with text stating that Devices on FHIR was modeled on IEEE 11073-**10201**
(the Domain Information Model / DIM), and that the relationship between DIM
objects/attributes and FHIR `Device` / `DeviceMetric` / `Observation` (and
their elements) is a **direct equivalency** rather than an analogy. Where the
IG still needs to discuss 10206/ACOM, do so as a separate, clearly-scoped
note distinguishing ACOM from the 10201 DIM that informed the FHIR model.
Apply on the next revision of the PHD IG, consistent with the WG's
2025-11-12 decision to defer post-STU2 publication.

#### Justification

The reporter is the long-standing IEEE 11073 PHD subject-matter expert and
states the historical record directly: the FHIR PHD profiles were derived
from the 10201 DIM, not from 10206 ACOM. Leaving the current text in place
risks misleading implementers about which IEEE standard the FHIR mappings
trace to, and undermines the IG's authority on the very provenance it
documents. The fix is a localized narrative edit with no normative,
conformance, or schema impact, and the Devices WG has already agreed in
principle (per the 2025-11-12 meeting) to incorporate it in the next PHD IG
revision.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than replacing the ACOM-analogy sentence outright, rewrite Section
3.2 to present **both** lineages explicitly: (a) a primary statement that
the FHIR PHD resources (`Device`, `DeviceMetric`, `Observation`, related
profiles) are a direct equivalent of the IEEE 11073-10201 DIM
objects/attributes on which they were modeled, and (b) a secondary
statement that they are also broadly *analogous* to the more recent
11073-10206 ACOM objects/attributes, with a brief note on how ACOM and the
DIM relate. Frame the two paragraphs so that 10201 is unambiguously the
source model and 10206 is contextual cross-reference for readers coming
from the ACOM world.

#### Justification

A growing share of the IEEE 11073 community is working from ACOM (10206)
rather than the legacy DIM (10201), so completely removing the ACOM-side
framing may make the IG harder to read for newer adopters who only know
ACOM. Keeping both, with 10201 clearly labelled as the actual basis of the
FHIR model, corrects the technical inaccuracy the reporter flagged while
preserving a navigational on-ramp for ACOM-aware readers. The trade-off
versus Disposition A is a slightly longer narrative section and the need to
characterise the DIM↔ACOM relationship correctly — something the WG should
ask Malcolm Clarke (or another 11073 SME) to draft rather than improvise.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change on the basis that the existing sentence uses the word
"analogous" loosely and is intended as a high-level framing aid rather than
a normative statement of provenance, and that the FHIR Device /
DeviceMetric / Observation mapping tables elsewhere in the IG already make
the actual element-level relationships explicit.

#### Justification

If the WG took the view that Section 3.2 is an introductory narrative whose
purpose is to give non-11073 readers a rough mental model — and that the
formal mappings appear in dedicated mapping sections — then the
"analogous to ACOM" sentence is arguably tolerable as a soft framing
device, even if not historically precise. Declining would avoid spending
revision capacity on a non-normative wording change. However, the reporter
is the SME, and the WG has already accepted the change in principle, so
this rationale is weak in context.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter is the recognised IEEE 11073 PHD expert, the existing wording
is factually wrong about which IEEE standard Devices on FHIR was modelled
on, and the Devices WG has already agreed (2025-11-12) to defer the fix to
the next PHD IG revision rather than reject it. Disposition A is the
cleanest implementation of that decision: a targeted narrative correction
in Section 3.2 with no normative impact and no risk to existing
implementations. Disposition B is a reasonable refinement if the WG wants
to keep an ACOM-side framing for readers who know only 10206, but the
substantive correction it carries is the same as A and can be folded in
during drafting. Disposition C is not defensible given the SME's input and
the WG's prior agreement.
