## Summary

The reporter (Malcolm Clarke) flags that section **2.9.10 "Regulation"** of
the **Personal Healthcare Devices (PHD) FHIR IG** treats regulatory marking
as if FDA were the only relevant regime. He argues this is factually wrong:
**CE marking** (EU) is at least as important globally, and additional
national regimes such as those in **Japan** and **China** also apply to
personal-health devices. The ticket asks the Devices Work Group to broaden
the IG's regulation guidance so it does not give the impression that
declaring "FDA" is sufficient. The most recent WG action (Lynn Laakso,
2026-03-31) reverted an earlier "Considered for Future Use" resolution and
**deferred the change to the next revision of the PHD IG**, with the
ticket re-opened post STU2 publication so the substantive fix can be
designed in the next cycle. No related Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or
GitHub items were surfaced via cross-reference; the only linked artifact
is the Devices WG meeting notes that recorded the deferral.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Rewrite **§2.9.10 "Regulation"** of the Personal Healthcare Devices FHIR
IG narrative so it no longer reads as if FDA were the universal or
default regulator. Concretely:

- Replace the current FDA-centric prose with jurisdiction-neutral
  guidance that lists at minimum **FDA (US)**, **CE marking under
  EU MDR (Regulation (EU) 2017/745)**, **PMDA / PMD Act (Japan)**, and
  **NMPA (China)** as examples of regulatory regimes a PHD may carry.
- State explicitly that a PHD may be marked under multiple regimes
  simultaneously and that implementers MUST NOT assume a single
  regulator.
- Where the IG points to a coding mechanism for regulatory status (e.g.
  `Device.identifier`, `Device.regulatoryAuthorization` in R5+, or
  the IG-defined extension currently used in PHD), note that the value
  set / code system used SHALL be capable of representing each of the
  named regimes, and add (or recommend) corresponding code-system
  entries for CE / PMDA / NMPA alongside the existing FDA entry.
- Add a short "no exhaustive list" caveat acknowledging other national
  regulators (e.g., UK MHRA, Health Canada, TGA, ANVISA) without
  enumerating every regime.

Schedule the change for the **next revision of the PHD IG** (consistent
with Lynn Laakso's 2026-03-31 deferral note).

#### Justification

The ticket's factual claim is correct: CE marking is mandatory for
medical devices placed on the EU market and is at least as widespread as
FDA clearance globally; Japan (PMD Act / PMDA) and China (NMPA) are
likewise major regimes. An IG that names only FDA misleads non-US
implementers and risks encoding US-centric assumptions into downstream
profiles and value sets. The Devices WG has already accepted the
substantive direction by reverting "Considered for Future Use" and
deferring to the next IG revision rather than declining outright, which
is a strong signal that "Accept" is the correct disposition.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than enumerate specific regulators inline in §2.9.10, **refactor
the section to be regulator-agnostic** and delegate the actual list of
regimes to a **bound value set** (e.g., a new
`PHDRegulatoryJurisdiction` value set, or alignment with an existing
externally-maintained terminology such as ISO 3166 country codes paired
with a "regulator" code system).

- Section 2.9.10 narrative would describe the *concept* of regulatory
  marking, point at the element/extension used to carry it, and link to
  the bound value set.
- The value set would seed FDA, CE/EU MDR, PMDA, NMPA, and a small set
  of others, with an extensible binding so jurisdictions can be added
  without re-balloting narrative.
- Provide one worked example per regime (FDA UDI, CE marking under EU
  MDR, etc.) in an appendix rather than the main narrative.

#### Justification

This addresses the reporter's underlying concern (FDA isn't the only
regulator) without re-opening the narrative every time a new regulator
becomes relevant or a name changes (e.g., MDR replacing MDD; CFDA
becoming NMPA). It also gives implementers a single machine-readable
hook (the value set) instead of relying on prose, which is more
consistent with how other FHIR IGs handle jurisdiction (e.g.,
`jurisdiction` on conformance resources binds to a value set rather
than enumerating countries inline). The trade-off is that creating /
maintaining the value set adds editorial work and a terminology review
step that Disposition A avoids.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Not Persuasive** on the grounds that §2.9.10 is
narrative guidance only, that the *machinery* the IG uses to express
regulatory status (the relevant element/extension and its binding) is
already jurisdiction-neutral, and that FDA was named as an *example*
rather than as the sole permissible value. Add at most a one-sentence
clarifier that "FDA is one example" if any change is made at all.

#### Justification

If the underlying data model already permits arbitrary regulators (which
is the case for FHIR's `Device.regulatoryAuthorization` in R5 and for
extension-based approaches in earlier versions), the ticket reduces to a
narrative wording preference. Declining keeps the IG stable, avoids a
re-ballot of §2.9.10, and lets jurisdiction-specific guidance live in
realm-specific IGs (e.g., a future EU PHD profile) rather than in the
international PHD IG itself.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The Devices WG has already implicitly accepted the ticket's premise:
the previous "Considered for Future Use" resolution was reverted on
2026-03-31 and the change was deferred to the next PHD IG revision
rather than declined, which forecloses Disposition C. Between A and B,
Disposition A is the right starting point because the immediate harm
the reporter identifies is *narrative misinformation* — readers come
away thinking FDA is the universal regulator — and the cheapest, most
direct fix for that is to rewrite the prose to name the major regimes
(FDA, CE/EU MDR, PMDA, NMPA) and explicitly disclaim exhaustiveness.
Disposition B's value-set approach is a strict improvement on the
*machinery* and is worth doing, but it is a larger editorial and
terminology project; it should be tracked as a follow-on enhancement
rather than blocking the narrative correction the reporter actually
asked for. The recommended path is therefore: ship the §2.9.10 rewrite
(A) in the next PHD IG revision, and open a follow-up ticket to bind a
`PHDRegulatoryJurisdiction` value set (B) for a subsequent cycle.
