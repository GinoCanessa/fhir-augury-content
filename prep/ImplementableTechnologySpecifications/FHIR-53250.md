## Summary

The ticket reports an internal contradiction in the FHIRPath specification's
treatment of calendar-duration equivalence. Specifically, the equivalences
`1 year ~ 1 'a' ~ 365.25 'd'` (via UCUM) and `1 year ~ 365 days ~ 365 'd'`
(via the FHIRPath calendar-duration conversion table) cannot all be true
simultaneously. The conflict stems from §6.1.2.1 Quantity Equivalence, which
currently says that implementations supporting units shall do so "as
specified by [UCUM] as well as the calendar durations as defined in the
toQuantity function" — i.e., the spec implicitly admits the calendar-duration
conversion factors into equivalence/equality/comparison, even though those
factors are inherently approximate (years, months are variable-length).

The reporter (Jing Tang) proposes removing that sentence and clarifying that
the calendar-duration conversion factors are only usable via an *explicit*
`toQuantity(unit)` call, and never implicitly during equality, equivalence,
comparison, or arithmetic. Bryn Rhodes broadened the resolution to also fix a
loose end from [FHIR-28438](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-28438): §6.7
Date/Time Arithmetic still uses the "above seconds" boundary for
definite-duration errors, but the rest of the spec was already realigned to
"above days (or weeks)". The disposition therefore touches §§ 4.1.8.1,
5.5.7.1, 6.1.1.1, 6.1.2.1, 6.2, 6.6, and 6.7, with new worked examples in
§6.7.

This ticket sits in a tightly-coupled cluster:
[FHIR-53249](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53249) (quantity equivalence is
ambiguous — also raised by Jing Tang),
[FHIR-53148](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53148) and
[FHIR-53172](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53172) (CQL-side calendar
duration clarifications, called out by Bryn as needing aligned resolutions),
[FHIR-28438](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-28438) (the original
"above-days vs. above-seconds" realignment), and
[FHIR-56190](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-56190), Brian Postlethwaite's
omnibus quantity/calendar-duration rewrite (preview branch
`BP-2026-03-quantity-preview`). The current resolution comment from
BrianPos points readers to 56190 as the actual implementation vehicle:
"refer to disposition in FHIR-56190 instead".

## Details

**Description:**

> See discussion here #fhirpath > Transitivity and determinism of equivalence for durations
>
> The following relationships can't all be true, or we end up with a conflict:
>
> 1 year ~ 1 'a' ~ 365.25 'd' (the final conversion is defined in ucum)
> 1 year ~ 365 days ~ 365 'd'
>
> Bryn suggested in his reply that 1 year ~ 365 days is not true.
>
> But there is this sentence:
> "Implementations that do support units shall do so as specified by [UCUM] as well as the calendar durations as defined in the toQuantity function."
> under 6.1.2.1 quantity equivalence (https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/FHIRPath/#quantity-equivalence).
>
> Proposal: remove that sentence. As well as adding in the toQuantity documentation that the calendar duration conversion factors are only to be used by toQuantity function to explicitly convert to a different duration. They cannot be used implicitly in determining equivalence or equality or in comparison.

**Resolution Description (Persuasive with Modification):**

*BrianPos: refer to disposition in [FHIR-56190](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-56190) instead.*

The standalone disposition (now superseded by FHIR-56190) was:

1. In §6.6 Math, §6.1.1.1 Quantity Equality, §6.1.2.1 Quantity Equivalence, and §6.2 Comparison, change
   *"Implementations that do support units shall do so as specified by [UCUM] as well as the calendar durations as defined in the toQuantity function."*
   to *"Implementations that do support units shall do so as specified by [UCUM]. Note that calendar unit conversions for months and years must be performed explicitly, as defined in the [toQuantity(unit)] function."*
2. In §6.7 Date/Time Arithmetic, replace the "above seconds" boundary with "above days" / "for years or months", so definite-quantity durations for years or months in a date/time arithmetic calculation signal an error. Add an info note that for all units below years/months, calendar durations are equal/equivalent to their UCUM definite-time counterpart, modulo leap seconds (which implementations typically ignore).
3. In §5.5.7.1 toQuantity(unit), change the "unanchored calculations" sentence to: *"Note that because calendar duration conversion factors are approximate, they must be invoked explicitly using the `toQuantity(unit)` function. Attempts to perform comparisons or arithmetic (other than Date/Time arithmetic) requiring calendar duration conversions result in empty ( {} )."*
4. Add worked examples to §6.7 covering `=`, `~`, `+` between calendar units and UCUM units (e.g., `1 year = 1 'a'` → empty; `1 year ~ 1 'a'` → true; `1 year = 12 months` → empty; `1 year ~ 12 months` → false; `1 year + 12 months` → empty; `1 year + 12 'mo'` → empty; `1 year.toQuantity('month') + 12 months` → 24 months; `@2025-11-29 + 1 'a'` → error; `@2025-11-29 + 1 year` → @2026-11-29; etc.).
5. Adjust §4.1.8.1 Time-valued quantities accordingly.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the standalone resolution recorded on FHIR-53250 to the FHIRPath spec
(without folding it into FHIR-56190):

1. In §6.6 Math, §6.1.1.1 Quantity Equality, §6.1.2.1 Quantity Equivalence,
   and §6.2 Comparison, replace the "as well as the calendar durations as
   defined in the toQuantity function" clause with the explicit-only
   wording: *"Implementations that do support units shall do so as specified
   by [UCUM]. Note that calendar unit conversions for months and years must
   be performed explicitly, as defined in the [toQuantity(unit)] function."*
2. In §6.7 Date/Time Arithmetic, move the definite-duration error boundary
   from "above seconds" to "above days" / "for years or months", and add the
   info note about leap seconds.
3. In §5.5.7.1 toQuantity(unit), require explicit invocation of the calendar
   conversion factors and define that disallowed implicit comparisons /
   arithmetic return empty (`{}`).
4. Add the eight worked examples to §6.7 (`=`, `~`, `+` over calendar
   vs. UCUM units, including `@2025-11-29 + 1 'a'` → error and
   `@2025-11-29 + 1 year` → @2026-11-29).
5. Update §4.1.8.1 Time-valued quantities to remove the now-stale
   "comparable" sentence.
6. Adopt Bryn's 2026-02-23 follow-up clarification distinguishing
   variable-length conversions (years, months — explicit-only) from
   equivalent definite-time conversions (e.g., `1 day.toQuantity('minutes')`
   — valid).

#### Justification

The disposition has already been refined through ~5 months of fhirpath
discussion with the originator, the WG chair (Bryn), an active
implementer (Yury) and Brian; it is internally consistent (`{}` for
equality with unsupported conversions, `false` for equivalence, `error` for
date/time arithmetic) and removes a real conflict in the spec that
implementations must currently invent their own answer for. The text-level
edits are surgical and confined to a known set of sections, and they align
the FHIRPath spec with the long-standing CQL outcome from FHIR-28438.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — Defer to FHIR-56190 (current Persuasive-with-Modification stance)

#### Proposal

Resolve FHIR-53250 as **Persuasive with Modification** and record the
disposition as: *"Refer to disposition in FHIR-56190 — Extensive
clarifications of handling quantities, and specifically unit conversions and
calendar/time-valued quantities."* Apply no edits directly from this ticket;
all FHIRPath spec changes covered here are made via the FHIR-56190 omnibus
disposition, whose preview branch
(`build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/FHIRPath/branches/BP-2026-03-quantity-preview`)
already implements them, alongside related cleanups for FHIR-49525 and
FHIR-53249 to ensure cross-section consistency. Mark FHIR-53250 closed when
56190 is applied. (This is the position currently recorded in the ticket's
Resolution Description and reinforced in the
"Quanity equivalence unit granularity" Zulip thread.)

#### Justification

The five spec sections touched by this ticket overlap heavily with the
sections rewritten in FHIR-56190 (which also covers FHIR-49525 and
FHIR-53249). Applying 53250 independently risks merge conflicts, drift, and
duplicated review effort against the same paragraphs. The 56190 preview
branch already implements the agreed direction and lets the WG review all
quantity changes as a coherent block. This is also the disposition Brian has
publicly signalled in #fhirpath, and matches the pattern used for the other
two clustered tickets.

---

### Disposition C: Decline — Persist current spec language and rely on implementation guidance

#### Proposal

Decline the proposed wording changes. Treat the apparent transitivity
conflict (`1 year ~ 1 'a' ~ 365.25 'd'` vs `1 year ~ 365 days ~ 365 'd'`) as
already implicitly resolved by §4.1.8.1 Time-valued quantities (which
already states that 1 year is *not comparable* to 1 'a' but *is equivalent*
to 1 'a'), and address any remaining ambiguity through non-normative
implementer guidance and additional test cases in `fhir-test-cases` rather
than normative spec edits.

#### Justification

The §6.1.2.1 sentence the reporter wants removed is an instruction to
implementations about *which units they must support*, not an assertion that
calendar conversion factors are valid for `~`. A defensible reading already
makes the spec consistent if §4.1.8.1 is treated as authoritative. Avoiding
normative edits sidesteps the risk that the new "empty for `=`, false for
`~`, error for date arithmetic" matrix interacts badly with existing
implementations and deployed tests (e.g., the
`fhir-test-cases/r4/fhirpath/tests-fhir-r4.xml` row Yury cited would change
expected outcomes).

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B (Defer to FHIR-56190).

The technical content of Disposition A is correct and is what the WG
actually wants in the spec — but the same content is already in flight via
FHIR-56190, with a working preview branch and an agreed scope that pulls in
the immediately-adjacent tickets (FHIR-49525, FHIR-53249) and addresses the
cross-section text consistency issues that piecemeal edits to 53250 alone
would miss. Brian's explicit removal of 53250 from the fhirpath block vote
("mostly good, just want to re-check things in person"), the
"Quanity equivalence unit granularity" announcement that 56190 supersedes
this work, and the existing `BP-2026-03-quantity-preview` branch all point
the same way: keep the substantive resolution recorded on 53250 for
traceability, but make the spec change exactly once, through FHIR-56190.
Disposition C should be rejected — the conflict is real, implementers have
already requested clarification on Zulip, and existing implementations
already disagree on the answer. If the WG decides 56190 is too large to
land in the current cycle, fall back to Disposition A as a self-contained
plan B; it is detailed enough to apply directly.
