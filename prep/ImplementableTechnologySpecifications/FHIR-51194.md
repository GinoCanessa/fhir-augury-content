## Summary

Grahame Grieve reports that XAdES-X-L digital signatures, as profiled by
the FHIR Digital Signatures page, are unreliable in practice because the
digest over the `xades:SignedProperties` fragment depends on how the
chosen XML canonicalisation library treats inter-element whitespace.
Different libraries make different decisions about whether the
whitespace inside `SignedProperties` is significant when canonicalising
the dereferenced fragment, producing different digests and therefore
signatures that do not validate across implementations. He proposes
that the spec add a strong recommendation that `SignedProperties` MUST
contain no whitespace other than the single spaces required between
attributes, noting that the IG publisher and the validator already
produce and verify signatures on this basis. While the page is being
touched, he also asks that the Digital Signatures page be properly
registered (on the documentation index and in JIRA), and that
signature/canonicalization content currently scattered across the
Datatypes, JSON, and XML pages be consolidated onto the Digital
Signatures page (with back-references from the originating pages) and
reconciled.

The ticket has no formally linked Jira issues and only one
cross-reference: a Security and Privacy Zulip topic ("Signature") in
which the broader signature programme — including a possible pivot
away from the standalone `Signature` datatype toward Provenance — is
being worked through. Several related signature trackers were named in
that thread (FHIR-47197, FHIR-46788, FHIR-50935, FHIR-41522, FHIR-51115,
FHIR-50934, FHIR-47269, FHIR-48937, FHIR-51143) but none are formal
links of FHIR-51194; they are context for how the workgroup is
sequencing signature work.

## Details

**Description:**

On the Digital Signatures page, the spec says that the Signature SHOULD
conform to XAdES-X-L (https://www.w3.org/TR/XAdES/) for support of
Long-Term signatures, which adds the timestamp of signing, inclusion of
the signing certificate, and statement of revocation.

The description embeds a worked XAdES signature block (`<Signature>`
with `<SignedInfo>`, two `<Reference>` entries — one over `#` with the
FHIR canonicalisation transform, one over `#SignedProperties` — plus
`<KeyInfo>` and an `<Object>` carrying `<xades:QualifyingProperties>`
with `<xades:SignedProperties>` containing
`SignedSignatureProperties` (SigningTime, CommitmentTypeIndication →
ProofOfAuthorship) and `SignedDataObjectProperties` (DataObjectFormat
for `application/fhir+xml`, UTF-8)). The Reference over
`#SignedProperties` is the focal point: its digest is established by
dereferencing the `SignedProperties` element and canonicalising that
fragment.

While adding XML DigSig support to the browser, Grahame found that
canonicalisation libraries differ in whether they treat the whitespace
inside `SignedProperties` as significant when generating the digest,
which makes signatures unreliable and unverifiable across stacks. He
therefore proposes the SHOULD bullet on the Digital Signatures page be
extended along the lines of:

> Due to variations in whitespace handling when generating digests,
> applications SHOULD ensure that there is no whitespace in the XAdES
> `SignedProperties` block other than a single space character where
> required for attributes.

He notes that the HL7 IG publisher and validator both produce and
verify signatures on that basis today, so the proposed normative
recommendation reflects what implementers must already do to
interoperate with the reference tooling.

In the same ticket Grahame also asks that, while the page is being
touched, the workgroup:

- register the Digital Signatures page properly (e.g., on the
  documentation index and in JIRA);
- move all digital-signatures and canonicalization content currently
  spread across the Datatypes, JSON, and XML pages onto the Digital
  Signatures page, refer back to it from those pages, and reconcile the
  content while consolidating.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the Digital Signatures page on the FHIR Core spec to extend the
existing XAdES-X-L SHOULD bullet with normative wording substantially
equivalent to:

> Due to variations in whitespace handling when XML canonicalisation is
> applied to the dereferenced `xades:SignedProperties` fragment,
> applications producing or verifying XAdES-X-L signatures over FHIR
> resources SHALL serialise `xades:SignedProperties` (and its
> descendants) without insignificant whitespace between elements.
> Whitespace MAY only appear where required by XML syntax (e.g., the
> single space separating attributes on a start tag). Applications
> generating digests for the `Reference` whose `Type` is
> `http://uri.etsi.org/01903#SignedProperties` SHALL canonicalise the
> dereferenced fragment after this whitespace constraint has been
> applied.

Land the change in the FHIR Core authoring source (the digital
signatures narrative page), keeping the existing XAdES-X-L SHOULD as
the surrounding context. Do **not** alter the Signature datatype or
Provenance in this ticket; the change is purely to the narrative
guidance and does not touch StructureDefinitions.

In addition, action the two side-requests Grahame raised on the same
ticket:

1. Register the Digital Signatures page in the canonical documentation
   index (so it surfaces alongside the other narrative pages) and in
   JIRA (so it appears as a selectable specification page on tickets).
2. Consolidate signature/canonicalization narrative currently split
   across the Datatypes, JSON, and XML pages onto the Digital
   Signatures page. The Datatypes/JSON/XML pages should retain only a
   short pointer to the consolidated page. While consolidating,
   reconcile any contradictions between the page versions in favour of
   the worked XAdES-X-L profile on the Digital Signatures page.

#### Justification

The whitespace problem is real, well-known in the XML DigSig
community, and not specific to FHIR; it is a property of the
interaction between XML canonicalisation modes (Inclusive C14N vs.
Exclusive C14N) and the way `SignedProperties` is dereferenced before
digest generation. Two different verifiers can both behave correctly
per their canonicalisation implementation and still disagree on the
digest of the same `SignedProperties` element if it contains
inter-element whitespace. Without a normative constraint the SHOULD
profile is not interoperable in practice.

The HL7 IG publisher and the FHIR validator already produce and
consume signatures on this basis, so the proposed text codifies
existing reference-implementation behaviour rather than introducing a
new requirement. That makes adoption cost low for the largest
production toolchains and aligns the spec with the implementations
that most ballot voters will exercise.

The two housekeeping requests (registering the page; consolidating
signature content) reduce duplication and make the ticket itself
discoverable: today, "Digital Signatures" is not a first-class
specification page in JIRA, which makes it hard to file or route
signature tickets correctly — exactly the friction Grahame is
experiencing.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt the technical guidance in Disposition A but tighten and narrow
the mechanism, so that the burden lands on the canonicalisation step
rather than on the serialisation:

1. Mandate **Exclusive XML Canonicalisation 1.0**
   (`http://www.w3.org/2001/10/xml-exc-c14n#`) for the `Reference`
   whose `Type` is `http://uri.etsi.org/01903#SignedProperties`, and
   require that the `Transforms` element on that `Reference` make the
   chosen canonicalisation explicit. Today the worked example in the
   description omits the canonicalisation transform on the
   SignedProperties reference, which is itself part of the
   interoperability problem.
2. **Recommend** (not require) the no-whitespace serialisation as a
   defensive practice for producers, on the basis that some libraries
   still mis-handle Exclusive C14N around prefixed element content.
3. Apply the documentation-housekeeping items from Disposition A
   unchanged: register the page in JIRA + the documentation index, and
   consolidate the canonicalization material from Datatypes / JSON /
   XML.

#### Justification

The root cause is canonicalisation behaviour, not serialisation; making
the canonicalisation deterministic and explicit fixes the digest
mismatch even for producers that do emit pretty-printed XML. Pinning
the algorithm in the `Transforms` of the `SignedProperties` reference
also makes the contract self-describing: a verifier no longer has to
guess. This is closer to how XAdES is profiled by other communities
(e.g., ETSI baseline profiles), reducing FHIR-specific divergence.

The trade-off vs. Disposition A is that requiring whitespace-stripping
at serialisation is robust against *all* canonicalisation bugs,
whereas pinning Exclusive C14N only fixes the well-behaved-library
path. The IG publisher and validator currently emit
no-whitespace `SignedProperties`, which means producers built against
those tools satisfy Disposition A "for free"; producers built against
generic XML signature stacks would more naturally satisfy
Disposition B. Disposition B is therefore a better long-term spec
shape but a worse fit with what the existing reference tooling
actually does today.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the requested change and instead defer to the in-flight
Security & Privacy programme of work that is reconsidering whether
FHIR should keep the standalone `Signature` datatype at all (see the
Zulip thread; FHIR-47197 in particular is exploring placing
signatures directly on resources). Mark FHIR-51194 as
"Persuasive — Considered for Future Use" with a note that any
whitespace guidance will be folded into the larger signatures rewrite
once the datatype/Provenance question is resolved.

Reject the documentation housekeeping items as out of scope for a
single change request: split them into separate tickets owned by the
work group that maintains each affected page (Datatypes, JSON, XML).

#### Justification

The Signature mechanism in FHIR is in flux. Investing normative text
in a specific corner of the XAdES-X-L profile risks locking the spec
into wording that may need to be re-derived when the broader
direction settles. The Zulip thread shows the WG actively debating
fundamentals (keeping vs. dissolving the `Signature` datatype, the
roles of JAdES/XAdES/CAdES) without consensus, and one participant —
in the context of a related signature ticket — explicitly said "we
should not do what is asked for". Adding interoperability text now and
again after the rewrite increases churn for implementers.

The trade-off is that the immediate, concrete interoperability
problem Grahame describes (digests differing across libraries) is real
and unaffected by the larger redesign — XAdES-X-L will still have a
SignedProperties element with the same canonicalisation hazard
whether it lives in the Signature datatype or in Provenance. So
declining leaves a known unverifiability hazard in the spec until the
broader work lands.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The whitespace hazard is independent of every other open question in
the signature programme: regardless of whether `Signature` is kept,
folded into Provenance, or re-pivoted onto the resource itself, an
XAdES-X-L profile that dereferences `SignedProperties` and digests the
canonicalised form will exhibit exactly this canonicalisation-mode
divergence. The proposed text codifies behaviour the HL7 reference
implementations (IG publisher, validator) already implement, so
accepting it makes the spec describe reality rather than ask
implementers to discover the constraint by failure. Disposition B is
technically cleaner but requires every existing implementation to
change its `Transforms` element and risks introducing a new
interoperability gap during the transition; it is a better candidate
for a follow-up ticket once Disposition A is in place. Disposition C
under-serves implementers who are trying to ship FHIR signatures
today.

The two housekeeping items (registering the Digital Signatures page in
JIRA + the documentation index, and consolidating signature /
canonicalization content from Datatypes, JSON, and XML) should be
accepted with FHIR-51194 because they directly address the
discoverability problem the ticket itself exposes — that "Digital
Signatures" is not currently a first-class page in the workflow — but
the workgroup may choose to track each as a sub-ticket so the
narrative reorganisation can be reviewed page-by-page.
