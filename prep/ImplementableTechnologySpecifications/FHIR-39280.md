## Summary

The ticket proposes a governance/ownership change: the FHIR OIDs page
(`hl7.org/fhir/oids.html`) — which lists all OIDs assigned to content
defined in the FHIR specification, including the FHIR root OID
`2.16.840.1.113883.4.642` and per-resource sub-roots — should be
attributed to the FHIR Management Group (FMG) rather than the
Implementable Technology Specifications (ITS) work group. No content
change to the OID assignments themselves is requested; this is purely a
work-group ownership reassignment for the page. No cross-referenced
Jira tickets, Zulip discussions, or GitHub items were found for this
ticket.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Reassign the FHIR OIDs page (`source/oids.html` in `HL7/fhir`) from the
ITS work group to FMG, exactly as requested. Concretely:

1. In `HL7/fhir`, edit `source/oids.html` line 13 to replace
   `[%wg its%]` with `[%wg fmg%]` and `[%wgt its%]` with
   `[%wgt fmg%]`, so the rendered "Responsible Owner" attribution
   reads "FMG Work Group" and links to the FMG work-group page.
2. In the HL7 JIRA spec-page-to-work-group configuration, change the
   work-group binding for `oids.html` (and its rendered URL
   `hl7.org/fhir/oids.html`) from ITS to FMG so that future tickets
   filed against the page are routed to FMG by default.
3. Re-route this very ticket (FHIR-39280) to FMG once the change is
   accepted, since it is itself an example of an OIDs-page ticket
   landing on the wrong work group's queue.
4. Leave `source/oids.ini` and the OID assignments untouched. The
   FHIR Product Director remains the gatekeeper for OID-content
   changes per the file's own header comment.

#### Justification

The OIDs page is a registry of identifiers for the specification as a
whole rather than an "implementable technology" topic (parsing,
serialisation, REST, etc.) that defines ITS's normal remit. FMG is
already the body responsible for cross-cutting governance of the FHIR
Core specification — versioning, the standards-process page, the OID
root assignment itself — so attaching the OIDs page to FMG aligns
ownership with the page's actual subject matter. The mechanical change
in `source/oids.html` is a two-token edit on a single line, with no
content regeneration and no downstream IG impact, so the cost of
accepting is essentially zero. The dispositioning should explicitly
include the JIRA mapping flip; otherwise the misrouting that produced
this ticket will recur.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the page-ownership change to FMG, but split governance
explicitly between **page authorship** (FMG) and **OID assignment**
(FHIR Product Director, as already documented in
`source/oids.ini`). To make that split visible to readers and PR
reviewers:

1. Apply the `[%wg fmg%]` / `[%wgt fmg%]` edit from Disposition A.
2. Add a short editorial note immediately under the OIDs page heading
   stating that page maintenance is the responsibility of the FHIR
   Management Group, while assignment of new OIDs is the
   responsibility of the FHIR Product Director, and link to
   `versions.html#std-process` for the relevant governance.
3. Mirror that split in `source/oids.ini`'s header comment so the
   on-page text and the in-source comment agree.
4. Update the JIRA spec-page-to-work-group mapping for `oids.html` to
   FMG, and add a JIRA component or label that flags
   OID-assignment-only tickets so they can still be triaged to the
   Product Director without a second hop through FMG.

#### Justification

The literal request only changes who "owns" the page, but in practice
the OIDs page mixes two governance tracks: editorial maintenance of
the page (which FMG is well-suited to own) and assignment of new OIDs
under the FHIR root (which `source/oids.ini` already reserves to the
FHIR Product Director). Disposition A leaves that ambiguity in place
and may simply move it from "ITS vs FMG" to "FMG vs Product
Director." Calling the split out on-page and in the JIRA routing
configuration prevents future tickets from bouncing again, at the
cost of a short documentation paragraph and a JIRA configuration
tweak. There is no breaking change, and the briefing-flagged risk of
mismatched `[%wg ...%]` and `[%wgt ...%]` macros is handled the same
way as in Disposition A.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Not Persuasive — out of ITS scope to
self-deprecate**. ITS does not unilaterally hand a page to another
work group; an ownership transfer should be initiated by FMG itself
(or by the Methodology / FMG-coordinated process) rather than being
decided in an ITS triage queue. Recommend the requester re-file the
request against FMG, who can accept the page on their own queue and
then coordinate the publisher-macro change in `HL7/fhir` and the JIRA
spec-page mapping update.

#### Justification

The ticket is filed against the ITS work group, but its remedy is for
*another* work group (FMG) to take ownership. ITS can only "give
away" a page if the receiving work group agrees to take it, and that
agreement is not in evidence on the ticket: there is no FMG
co-signing comment, no Zulip thread, and no linked governance
decision. Declining without prejudice — and pointing the reporter at
FMG — is procedurally cleaner than ITS asserting a transfer that FMG
has not formally accepted. The OIDs page continues to render
correctly under its current attribution in the meantime, so there is
no implementer harm from declining.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The change is mechanically trivial (two tokens on one line in
`source/oids.html`), introduces no breaking change, and aligns the
page's stated ownership with the work group whose remit actually
covers it. The reporter is the FHIR architect, which is strong
evidence that FMG would in fact accept the page even though no formal
acceptance is attached to the ticket. Disposition B's editorial split
is appealing but solves a problem (Product-Director vs FMG ambiguity)
that is largely already handled by the existing `source/oids.ini`
header comment, so it's better filed as a follow-up if FMG asks for
it once they own the page. Disposition C is procedurally tidy but
penalises a low-cost cleanup with extra ticket churn. The workgroup
should accept, make the two-token HTML edit, and — critically — flip
the JIRA spec-page-to-work-group mapping in the same change so the
next OIDs ticket lands on FMG's queue rather than ITS's.
