## Summary

The reporter (JingTang, Google) raises two related defects in the FHIRPath
*Quantity equivalence* section (§6.1.2.1):

1. The section never actually defines **how** equivalence between two
   `Quantity` values is determined. It only discusses unit handling. The
   ticket proposes the spec say plainly: *the two quantity values should be
   converted to a common unit (where possible under UCUM), and equivalence is
   then determined by applying the existing decimal-equivalence rules to the
   numeric parts.*
2. The current text says *"the comparison will be made using the most
   granular unit of either input"*, but the immediately preceding
   decimal-equivalence section says *"comparison is done on values rounded
   to the precision of the least precise operand."* Combining the two yields
   the paradox in the worked example `4 'g' ~ 4040 'mg'`: converting to the
   most granular unit produces `4000 'mg'` and `4040 'mg'`, but rounding to
   the least precise operand effectively undoes the conversion. The reporter
   asks the WG to either drop the "most granular unit" wording or make
   explicit that decimal-equivalence rounding does **not** apply after unit
   conversion.

The ticket is part of a tightly linked cluster of FHIRPath quantity tickets
that are now all expected to be resolved together via Brian Postlethwaite's
roll-up ticket [FHIR-56190](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-56190)
("Extensive clarifications of handling quantities…"), with a preview branch
at `BP-2026-03-quantity-preview`. Bryn Rhodes' separately-tracked proposal
in [FHIR-49525](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-49525) ("Quantity math and
unit conversion") to convert operands to UCUM **base units** before
comparison is the proposed mechanism, and
[FHIR-53250](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53250) ("Calendar duration
equivalence has conflicts") is the sibling ticket dealing with the
calendar-duration side of the same problem space. The current
resolution description on this ticket already redirects to FHIR-56190 and
records that the January WGM agreed to revise the wording to use "most
granular unit, where granularity is determined by conversion to base units
and comparison of the resulting magnitudes."

## Details

**Description:**

1. In the [quantity equivalence](https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/FHIRPath/#quantity-equivalence)
   section, it does not actually mention how the equivalence should be
   determined. It only talks about the handling of different units. **It
   should say: the two quantity values should be converted to the same
   unit if possible under UCUM, and then the equivalence is determined by
   the equivalence of the numeric parts using the definition of decimal
   equivalence.**
2. In the same section, it states: *"The comparison will be made using the
   most granular unit of either input."* But in the decimal equivalence
   section just above, it states: *"comparison is done on values rounded
   to the precision of the least precise operand"*. This creates an odd
   situation: you would convert the two quantities to the most granular
   unit, but then you are comparing the two values using the less precise
   one, essentially undoing the conversion. For example, the test case
   `4 'g' ~ 4040 'mg'`: convert to `4000 'mg'` and `4040 'mg'`. But then
   determining the equivalence of decimals... we decide they are
   equivalent, since `4000` only has 1 digit of significance — but then
   why convert in the first place? The intention of the spec is unclear.
   **Propose: either remove the part about using the most granular unit,
   or make it clear that we should not be following the same rules as
   decimal equivalence.**

Also see: <https://github.com/FHIR/fhir-test-cases/pull/243>

**Resolution Description (snapshot):**
> *BrianPos: refer to disposition in FHIR-56190 instead*
>
> TODO: Based on discussion at the January WGM, revise this approach to be
> consistent with choosing the most granular unit, where "granularity of a
> unit" is determined by conversion to base units and comparison of the
> resulting magnitudes.
>
> In the quantity equivalence discussion, we will change the sentence
> "The comparison will be made using the most granular unit of either
> input." to:
>
> "For quantities expressed in UCUM units, implementations that support
> UCUM unit conversion SHALL perform the comparison by converting the
> arguments to common UCUM [base units](https://ucum.org/ucum#section-Base-Units)
> and then determining equivalence of the decimal values using
> decimal-equivalence semantics. Implementers can refer to the
> [NLM Base Unit Conversion](https://ucum.nlm.nih.gov/ucum-service.html#toBaseUnits)
> service for more information."

**Comments:**

- **brian.postlethwaite (2026-02-18):** Suggests equivalence should pick
  the *least* precise unit (kg in the example) and equality should pick
  the *most* precise unit (g), then apply the corresponding decimal
  semantics. Notes this still doesn't help when units aren't on the same

- **JingTang (2025-12-03):** Agrees the two algorithms appear to converge
  on the same end result.
- **plynch (2025-12-02):** Points out FHIRPath only knows *precision*
  (digits after the decimal point with trailing zeros ignored), not
  significant digits. Asks how many digits of precision should be retained
  when converting `4 'mg'` (0 digits) to `0.004 'g'` (3 digits). Proposes
  expressing the rule in terms of picking the *more* (or *less*) granular
  unit, not significant digits.
- **JingTang (2025-12-02):** Tries to reconcile Bryn's "preserve
  significant digits" with Paul's "convert to most granular unit"; thinks
  they agree on outputs but differ on description. Argues the WG should
  pick whichever is easiest to specify clearly.
- **plynch (2025-12-02):** Demonstrates that converting to base units
  alone still produces inconsistencies: `4 'g' ~ 4040 'mg'` becomes true,
  but `4 'kg' !~ 4040 'g'` becomes false. Proposes the procedure:
  (1) convert both to base units to determine which is more granular,
  (2) convert the other operand to that more granular unit, (3) compare
  using the decimal procedure. Notes this yields `4 'g' !~ 4040 'mg'`,
  and that picking the *least* granular unit instead would give the
  arguably more intuitive `~ true`.
- **bryn.rhodes (2025-12-02):** Confirms his proposal in FHIR-49525 covers
  this ticket: implementations supporting UCUM SHALL convert both operands
  to UCUM base units and then compare. Acknowledges JingTang's request and
  agrees implementations should be told explicitly to "preserve
  significant digits as part of unit conversion."
- **JingTang (2025-12-02) ×2:** Asks that the requirement to preserve
  significant digits be normative, not implementation-defined; accepts a
  "more granular unit" formulation provided "granularity" is defined,
  noting it implies `1/week` is more granular than `1/day` (a non-obvious
  outcome).
- **plynch (2025-12-01):** Notes the NLM `toBaseUnits` API documents a
  conversion service but does not prescribe a comparison algorithm.
- **bryn.rhodes (2025-12-01):** Wants to align with the UCUM service's
  base-unit approach; expects implementations will "preserve significant
  digits" as part of conversion.
- **plynch (2025-12-01):** Walks through the inconsistencies of a pure
  base-unit approach (same `4 'g' ~ 4040 'mg'` vs `4 'kg' !~ 4040 'g'`
  paradox) and lays out his three-step "more granular unit" proposal.
- **bryn.rhodes (2025-11-30):** First proposes following UCUM "base
  units" (per FHIR-49525) plus adding "Equivalence is then performed on
  the decimal values of the quantity using decimal-equivalent semantics."
- **JingTang (2025-11-24):** Asks how *granularity* itself is to be
  defined (`per-week` vs `per-day`, `km/h` vs `m/s`).
- **plynch (2025-11-24):** Argues `4000` and `4040` have the same
  precision under FHIRPath's definition (digits after the decimal point),
  so there is no actual problem with using the most granular unit — the
  reporter's worry rests on importing scientific-notation semantics.
- **JingTang (2025-11-24):** Refines her concern: it's about whether
  precision of `4000 mg` (converted from `4 g`) is 1 or 4 digits. Either
  reading is reasonable but the spec must say which. Also flags that
  unit conversion can yield non-terminating decimals (e.g. converting
  per-week to per-day → `1/7`).
- **JIRAUSER29321 / Yury Sedinkin (2025-11-22) ×2:** Walks through worked
  examples of decimal equivalence, points out that always using the left
  operand's units yields a non-symmetric `~`, and suggests adopting the

  same bug and intends to fix it.
- **brian.postlethwaite (2025-10-29):** Provides the symmetric example
  `5 'g' ~ 5001 'mg'` / `5000 'mg' ~ 5001 'mg'` / `5 'g' ~ 5.001 'g'` to
  expose the edge case from both directions; asks what UCUM itself says.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In FHIRPath §6.1.2.1 *Quantity equivalence*, replace the current
sentence:

> The comparison will be made using the most granular unit of either
> input.

with text that does both of the things the reporter asks for:

1. Define how equivalence is computed: *"For two `Quantity` values whose
   units are convertible under UCUM, the operands SHALL first be
   converted to a common unit. Equivalence is then determined by applying
   the decimal-equivalence rules (§6.1.2) to the numeric parts."*
2. Pin down which common unit and break the tie with decimal precision:
   *"The common unit is the more granular of the two input units, where
   the granularity of a unit is determined by converting it to its UCUM
   base unit and comparing the resulting magnitudes (the unit with the
   smaller magnitude per base-unit count is more granular). After
   conversion, the resulting decimal values are compared using
   decimal-equivalence semantics; trailing zeros introduced by the
   conversion are treated as significant for the purpose of that
   comparison (i.e. the converted operand retains the precision implied
   by the conversion factor)."*

Also add a worked example showing `4 'g' ~ 4040 'mg'` → `false` (because
`4 g` becomes `4000 mg` with 4 significant digits after conversion, and
`4000 ≠ 4040`), and a counter-example showing `4.04 'g' ~ 4040 'mg'`
→ `true`. Add a forward reference from §6.1.2 *Decimal equivalence* so
the reader knows the post-conversion comparison rule overrides the
"least precise operand" wording.

#### Justification

This is the most literal reading of what the reporter asked for and what
Paul Lynch's three-step procedure spells out in the comments. It
preserves the spec's existing "most granular unit" wording (so it is the
smallest editorial change that still produces a deterministic result),
fixes the symmetry bug Yury Sedinkin found in `fhirpath.js`, and
addresses Jing Tang's repeatedly-stated requirement that *granularity*
itself be defined in the spec rather than left to implementations. It
also matches Brian Postlethwaite's eventual position that equivalence
goes to the less precise unit and equality to the more precise — by
collapsing both into "most granular unit + decimal-equivalence on the
converted values" the spec stops contradicting itself.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — Defer to FHIR-56190 (UCUM base units)

#### Proposal

Mark FHIR-53249 as **Persuasive** but record the disposition as *"see
FHIR-56190"* (which is already what the resolution description does).
The substantive change ships in FHIR-56190 and lands in the FHIRPath
spec via the `BP-2026-03-quantity-preview` branch. Concretely, the
sentence in §6.1.2.1 becomes:

> For quantities expressed in UCUM units, implementations that support
> UCUM unit conversion SHALL perform the comparison by converting the
> arguments to common UCUM
> [base units](https://ucum.org/ucum#section-Base-Units) and then
> determining equivalence of the decimal values using
> decimal-equivalence semantics. Implementers can refer to the
> [NLM Base Unit Conversion](https://ucum.nlm.nih.gov/ucum-service.html#toBaseUnits)
> service for more information.

…together with a normative requirement that "implementations preserve
significant digits as part of unit conversion" (so that, after
conversion to base units, the decimal-equivalence rules behave the way
implementers would expect). The calendar-duration side of the cluster
is handled in FHIR-53250 in the same edit pass.

#### Justification

This is the path the WG has actually been on since the January 2026
WGM. It has three concrete advantages over Disposition A: (1) it
guarantees a single, consistent definition of "what unit do we compare
in" across equivalence, equality, comparison and arithmetic, by
anchoring everything to UCUM base units; (2) it lets implementers reuse
an existing, externally-documented service
(`ucum-service.html#toBaseUnits`) instead of inventing a bespoke
"granularity" definition (which Jing Tang flagged is unintuitive in
edge cases like `1/week` vs `1/day`); (3) it produces a single
co-ordinated edit on the `BP-2026-03-quantity-preview` branch covering
FHIR-49525, -53249, -53250 and -56190, which the WG can review and
ballot as one unit. The cost is that the worked example
`4 'g' ~ 4040 'mg'` will yield a counter-intuitive `true` unless the
"preserve significant digits" requirement is normative — so the
disposition must keep that requirement explicit, as Jing Tang has been
asking.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-53249 as *Not Persuasive* on the grounds that the spec
already, when read together with §6.1.2 *Decimal equivalence*, gives a
working definition: convert to the most granular unit, then apply
decimal-equivalence (which rounds to the precision of the least precise
operand). Add only a non-normative editorial note pointing readers to
the decimal-equivalence rules.

#### Justification

This would be defensible if one accepts Paul Lynch's 2025-11-24
argument that under FHIRPath's own (positional) definition of
*precision*, `4000` and `4040` have the same precision and there is no
real ambiguity — only an apparent one imported from
significant-digits / scientific-notation reasoning. On that reading the
existing wording is already self-consistent and the fix is just an
editorial cross-reference, not a normative change.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **B**

The substantive technical work and WG consensus have already moved to
the umbrella ticket FHIR-56190 and are staged on the
`BP-2026-03-quantity-preview` branch. The current resolution on
FHIR-53249 already says "refer to disposition in FHIR-56190 instead",
the January WGM agreed on the base-units approach, and Brian
Postlethwaite explicitly asked on Zulip (2026-04-02) that FHIR-49525,
FHIR-53249 and FHIR-53250 be marked as redundant against FHIR-56190.
Disposition B records that reality, keeps the four tickets editorially
coherent, and lets the WG ballot a single set of changes rather than
three overlapping ones — which is what implementers like Yury Sedinkin
(fhirpath.js) need to align against.

The one risk to flag in committee is the gap Jing Tang has called out
twice in comments: under a pure "convert to UCUM base units +
decimal-equivalence" rule, `4 'g' ~ 4040 'mg'` evaluates to `true`,
which most readers find counter-intuitive. The recommendation
therefore is **B with the explicit normative requirement that
implementations preserve the precision implied by the conversion
factor** (so the converted `4 'g'` retains its 1-digit precision as
`4000 mg`-with-1-significant-digit, not `4000 mg`-with-4-significant-
digits). If the committee is unwilling to make that requirement
normative, fall back to Disposition A — which gets to the same place
via the "most granular unit" framing the section already uses, and
matches what Paul Lynch and Yury Sedinkin have implemented or are about
to implement. Disposition C is not recommended: the contradiction the
reporter identified between §6.1.2 and §6.1.2.1 is real, and ignoring
it leaves at least one widely-used implementation (fhirpath.js) and
the test-cases repo (PR #243) in disagreement with each other.
