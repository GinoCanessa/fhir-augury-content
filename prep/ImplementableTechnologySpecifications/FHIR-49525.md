## Summary

The reporter (Bas van den Heuvel) observes that the FHIRPath specification is
silent — or at best ambiguous — about which UCUM unit the result of a
Quantity-valued arithmetic expression should carry. He gives the canonical
example `1 'kg' + 100 'g'`, which an engine might legitimately return as
`1.100 'kg'` or as `1100 'g'`. He asks the spec to either pin down the
behaviour or explicitly state that implementations are free to choose, in
which case it should also tell consumers to use `toQuantity(unit)` whenever
they need a result in a specific unit.

Discussion in #fhirpath established that §6.6 Math already says *"The unit
of the result will be the most granular unit of either input"*, but the
language is descriptive rather than normative, the term "most granular" is
not defined, and the rule does not explain how to pick the result unit for
operations whose arguments are not commensurable (e.g. multiplication that
produces a derived unit). Empirical evidence from FHIRPath-Lab shows
implementations diverge: most engines return the unit of the left argument,
while Firely converts to the UCUM base unit.

Two related tickets surfaced in the same conversation —
[FHIR-53249](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53249) (Quantity equivalence
ambiguity) and [FHIR-53250](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53250)
(Calendar duration equivalence conflicts) — and Brian Postlethwaite
subsequently rolled all three into the umbrella ticket
[FHIR-56190](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-56190) ("Extensive
clarifications of handling quantities…") which carries a preview branch
(`BP-2026-03-quantity-preview`) of the proposed prose. The current
disposition on FHIR-49525 is *Not Persuasive with Modification*, deferring
the substantive change to FHIR-56190 and adding a clarifying note in §6.6
Math that "most granular" SHALL be determined by comparing each input's
conversion factor to its UCUM base unit and selecting the largest factor,
plus a pointer that callers wanting a specific unit should use
`toQuantity(unit)` (e.g. `(1 'cm' + 20 'mm').toQuantity('m')`).

## Details

**Description:**

> The specification is silent on the unit that is the result of a calculation.
> The result of `1 'kg' + 100 'g'` might be `1.100 'kg'` or `1100 'g'`. I
> think the implementation should be free to choose the preferred approach.
>
> The specification should make this more clear and indicate that when a
> specific unit (`'kg'` instead of `'g'`) is needed, `toQuantity` should be
> used.

**Resolution (Not Persuasive with Modification):**

Per the resolution text, the substantive disposition has been migrated to
[FHIR-56190](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-56190). In §6.6 Math the
introductory sentence will be extended to cross-link to the Date/Time
Arithmetic section, and the existing N1 sentence ("The unit of the result
will be the most granular unit of either input.") will be retained but
augmented with a normative note:

> *Note:* The most granular unit SHALL be determined by comparing the
> conversion factor of the unit to the UCUM
> [base unit](https://ucum.org/ucum#section-Base-Units), and selecting the
> largest factor. This approach can also be used to make a determination
> for more complex units such as velocities. If a specific unit is desired
> in the result, use [`toQuantity(unit)`](#fn-toquantity) to specify the
> unit. e.g. `(1 'cm' + 20 'mm').toQuantity('m')`.

**Comments:**

  The spec should be explicit about the resulting unit; he proposes that
  the implementation may freely choose, and that the spec should

  specific unit.
- *bryn.rhodes (2025-08-13):* Doesn't see what more is being asked — the

- *bvdh (2025-08-14):* Reiterates that the result unit is unclear and that
  implementers may forget to inspect the resulting `Quantity.unit`; asks

- *bvdh (2025-08-27):* Notes that §6.6 Math already says *"The unit of the

- *bryn.rhodes (2025-11-30):* Counter-proposal: in §Comparison and §6.6

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Amend the FHIRPath specification (HL7/FHIRPath, §6.6 Math, plus the
adjacent Quantity-related sections) to state explicitly that the unit of
the result of a Quantity-valued arithmetic operation is
**implementation-defined** — implementations MAY return the result in
either operand's unit (or any commensurable unit) — and add a normative
sentence telling callers that, when a specific unit is required, they
SHALL convert the result with `toQuantity(unit)` (e.g.
`(1 'kg' + 100 'g').toQuantity('kg')`). Add the example
`1 'kg' + 100 'g'` to the Math section showing both `1.100 'kg'` and
`1100 'g'` as valid results.

#### Justification

This is the literal request in the ticket and is honest about the current
state of practice (the FHIRPath-Lab survey in the
[#fhirpath > FHIR-49525 Quantity Math](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/fhirpath/topic/FHIR-49525%20Quantity%20Math/near/575933717)
thread shows engines already diverge — most return the left operand's
unit, Firely returns the UCUM base unit). Documenting the divergence and
giving callers a deterministic escape hatch (`toQuantity`) preserves
backward compatibility with every existing engine. The cost is that two
conformant engines can produce literally-different `Quantity.value`
representations for the same expression, which complicates conformance
testing and surprised authors.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach (recommended)

#### Proposal

Resolve FHIR-49525 as **Not Persuasive with Modification**, deferring the
substantive change to [FHIR-56190](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-56190)
and applying only the targeted clarification already drafted in the
ticket's resolution: in §6.6 Math, retain the existing sentence
*"The unit of the result will be the most granular unit of either
input."* and add the normative note:

> *Note:* The most granular unit SHALL be determined by comparing the
> conversion factor of the unit to the UCUM
> [base unit](https://ucum.org/ucum#section-Base-Units), and selecting
> the largest factor. This approach can also be used to make a
> determination for more complex units such as velocities. If a specific
> unit is desired in the result, use [`toQuantity(unit)`](#fn-toquantity)
> to specify the unit. e.g. `(1 'cm' + 20 'mm').toQuantity('m')`.

Also add the cross-link from the §6.6 introduction to the Date/Time
Arithmetic section, as already drafted. Mark the ticket *Not Persuasive
with Modification* and reference FHIR-56190 in the resolution text so the
reviewer can see the broader rewrite.

#### Justification

This is the disposition currently captured on the ticket and the one
endorsed in the
[Quanity equivalence unit granularity](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/fhirpath/topic/Quanity%20equivalence%20unit%20granularity/near/583183656)
thread. It (a) gives the original reporter what he actually needed —
deterministic guidance on how to pin a result to a specific unit via
`toQuantity` — (b) defines "most granular" rigorously in terms of UCUM
base-unit conversion factors so engines can converge, and (c) avoids
re-litigating the much wider Quantity rewrite (Quantity equality,
equivalence, comparison, calendar-vs-UCUM duration handling, division
unit derivation, precision rules) that is being handled coherently in
FHIR-56190 / the `BP-2026-03-quantity-preview` branch. Splitting the
clarification across multiple tickets would risk inconsistent prose;
deferring keeps the spec internally consistent.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve as **Not Persuasive**, on the grounds that §6.6 Math already
states *"The unit of the result will be the most granular unit of either
input"*, which the original reporter himself acknowledged
(2025-09-23 comment) does answer his concern. No spec change is required;
implementers can already determine the result unit from the existing text
and use the existing `toQuantity` function when they want a specific
unit.

#### Justification

This is a defensible reading of Bryn Rhodes' 2025-08-13 comment ("We are
[explicit]; we state that implementations shall use UCUM. We cannot be
more explicit than that without restating the UCUM specification."), and
the reporter's eventual acceptance of the existing N1 wording. However,
the discussion in the
[Quanity equivalence unit granularity](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/fhirpath/topic/Quanity%20equivalence%20unit%20granularity/near/583824030)
thread shows that the existing wording is in fact ambiguous in practice
(implementers diverge; "most granular" is not defined; precision is
unaddressed), so declining outright would leave a known interoperability
gap on the table.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B reflects the path the work group has effectively already
taken on the ticket: the resolution text is drafted, FHIR-56190 exists
with a preview branch, and the
[Quanity equivalence unit granularity](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/fhirpath/topic/Quanity%20equivalence%20unit%20granularity/near/583183656)
thread reached rough consensus that the umbrella rewrite is the right
home for the broader Quantity / unit-conversion / equivalence /
comparison clarifications. Disposition A is too permissive — codifying
implementation-defined output units without the FHIR-56190 normative
backbone (commensurable-unit rules, base-unit conversion, precision
guidance) would lock in today's divergence. Disposition C ignores the
practical evidence of implementer divergence and the reporter's own
follow-up requests for explicit `toQuantity` guidance. The block-vote
slate already shows FHIR-49525 was pulled in anticipation of this
deferral, so the work group should formally accept Disposition B,
confirm the resolution text quoted above, and process FHIR-56190 as the
substantive vote.
