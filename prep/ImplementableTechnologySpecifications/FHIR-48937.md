## Summary

Rik Smithies reports an editorial defect on the FHIR XML Canonicalization
page (`http://hl7.org/fhir/canonicalization/xml#narrative`). In the table
that enumerates canonicalization methods, the description of the
`#narrative` method is truncated and ends without a closing parenthesis:
"The method only retains the Resource.id and Narrative (Resource.text".
The opening `(` before `Resource.text` is never closed, leaving the
sentence visibly broken in the rendered specification. The ticket is
purely an editorial / typographical correction; no normative behaviour
is in question.

The cross-references for this ticket are limited to two Zulip threads in
the **Security and Privacy** stream where it was bundled, alongside many
other tickets, into the broader signing-and-narrative / canonicalization
roll-ups. No related Jira tickets and no GitHub items are linked, so the
fix is scoped to the XML canonicalization page in the FHIR core
specification.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply an editorial / technical-correction fix to the XML canonicalization
page (`canonicalization/xml#narrative`, in the FHIR Core specification)
so that the `#narrative` row of the canonicalization-method table reads
as a complete, well-punctuated sentence. The minimal fix is to close the
parenthesis around `Resource.text`, e.g.:

> The method only retains the `Resource.id` and Narrative
> (`Resource.text`).

While touching the cell, also confirm that the trailing text (which
appears truncated mid-sentence in the source) is restored to its
intended wording — at minimum, the sentence must end cleanly with a
matching `)` and a terminating period. No normative or schema content
changes; this is a Technical Correction in the editorial sense.

#### Justification

The reporter has identified a concrete, verifiable defect: an unclosed
`(` in a table cell on a published spec page. The fix is small, has no
downstream impact on conformance, schemas, examples, or tooling, and
does not require WG consensus on semantics. Letting an obviously broken
sentence remain in the canonicalization table — a page actively
referenced by the Security and Privacy community in ongoing signature
discussions (see both Zulip threads above) — undermines reader trust in
a section that needs to be unambiguous. ITS routinely accepts and lands
this class of editorial correction in the next technical-correction
window.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the typo fix from Disposition A, **and** take the opportunity to
make the `#narrative` row of the canonicalization-method table fully
self-consistent with the surrounding signature / canonicalization
guidance: explicitly call out that the method retains only
`Resource.id` and the `Narrative` data type held in `Resource.text`
(including `status` and `div`), and cross-link to the
`#data` method row and to the signature page so that readers landing
from `Provenance.signature`/`Bundle.signature` discussions see the
relationship between `#narrative`, `#data`, and the per-resource
exclusions being debated in tickets such as FHIR-50934
(Composition.section.text exclusion). Treat the missing parenthesis as
the trigger for a small editorial sweep rather than a one-character edit.

#### Justification

The two Zulip threads above show that readers of this exact table are
actively trying to reason about which bytes are in scope when they
sign, and they are bouncing between the `#narrative` row, the `#data`
row, and the signature page. A bare one-character fix is correct but
leaves the row's wording terse and easy to misread. A slightly broader
editorial pass — still scoped as a Technical Correction, still
non-normative — would land the typo fix and remove a class of follow-on
clarification tickets in the same area. The cost is a marginally larger
diff and a slightly longer review.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Not Persuasive / No Change** on the grounds that
the missing parenthesis is a trivial rendering artefact that does not
affect the normative meaning of the `#narrative` canonicalization
method, and that any reader can infer the intended wording from
context.

#### Justification

This disposition is included for completeness only. The defect is
narrowly editorial and has no conformance impact, so a strict reading
could argue it is below the threshold that warrants a published
technical correction. In practice, however, ITS treats visible defects
on canonicalization pages as worth fixing because that page is cited
from signature tooling and audited carefully; declining a
straightforward editorial fix here would be inconsistent with prior
ITS dispositions on similar typo tickets.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter has flagged a concrete, verifiable defect on a page that
is being read closely by the signature community, and the fix is a
single closing parenthesis (plus, if the source is genuinely truncated,
restoration of the trailing words). There is no normative debate, no
backwards-compatibility concern, and no need for cross-WG coordination.
Disposition B is attractive but expands the scope beyond what the
reporter asked for and risks dragging the ticket into the larger,
unresolved signing/canonicalization conversations visible in the Zulip
threads — better handled in their own tickets (e.g., FHIR-50934,
FHIR-46788, FHIR-51133). Disposition C would leave a visibly broken
sentence on a high-visibility specification page for no benefit. ITS
should accept and land the editorial fix in the next technical
correction batch.
