## Summary

The reporter asserts that the canonical (defining) URL for the
`military-service-solor-extension` CodeSystem in the US Military Service
(FHIR) specification is rooted under `hl7.org/fhir`, when by HL7 policy
HL7-managed terminology artifacts should be defined under
`terminology.hl7.org` (THO). The ticket asks that the CodeSystem's
canonical URL be re-rooted to THO, and that a companion UTG (Unified
Terminology Governance) ticket be opened to register the CodeSystem in
THO so it lives in the appropriate terminology home.

No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were
returned by FhirAugury for this issue, so the request is reviewed on its
own.

## Details

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Re-root the canonical URL of the `military-service-solor-extension`
CodeSystem from a URL under `http://hl7.org/fhir/...` to a URL under
`http://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/...` in the US Military Service
(FHIR) specification. As part of the change:

1. Update the CodeSystem resource's `url` element (and any `valueSet`
   default-binding URI it carries) to the new THO-rooted canonical.
2. File a companion UTG ticket to register the CodeSystem in
   `terminology.hl7.org`, so the resolved canonical resolves to the
   THO-published artifact.
3. Update every internal reference (ValueSet `compose.include.system`,
   profiles, examples, narrative) within the US Military Service IG to
   use the new canonical.
4. Document the URL change in the IG's change log so implementers know
   the prior canonical is retired.

#### Justification

HL7's terminology-hosting policy is that HL7-stewarded code systems
should live under `terminology.hl7.org` rather than under the FHIR
specification's own URL space. Hosting them under `hl7.org/fhir` ties
their lifecycle and versioning to the FHIR core spec, which is the
wrong governance boundary for terminology — UTG is the canonical home.
Re-rooting the URL also unblocks normal terminology governance (THO
publishing, UTG change control). The reporter explicitly raised this
as the policy violation to fix.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the existing `hl7.org/fhir`-rooted canonical URL in place for the
already-published version of the US Military Service (FHIR) IG, but:

1. Register a THO-rooted canonical (the one Disposition A would adopt)
   via UTG and publish it as the authoritative CodeSystem in THO.
2. In the next substantive version of the US Military Service IG,
   switch the IG to consume the THO-rooted CodeSystem and mark the
   `hl7.org/fhir`-rooted URL as a retired/deprecated alias that
   resolves (via redirect or a thin retired CodeSystem) to the THO
   artifact.
3. Document the migration in both the IG and THO release notes.

#### Justification

Changing a published canonical URL is a breaking change for any
implementer who has bound profiles, value sets, or instances to the
existing `hl7.org/fhir` URL. This alternative honors the underlying
policy intent (THO is the system of record) without silently breaking
those references: the THO copy becomes authoritative, and the legacy
URL is preserved as a deprecated alias rather than removed. It also
avoids needing to re-publish the existing IG just for a URL move.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (as currently triaged)

#### Proposal

Leave the canonical URL of `military-service-solor-extension` as
published, on the basis that the URL-hosting policy the ticket cites
("CodeSystems must be rooted at `terminology.hl7.org`") is, as the
ticket's own resolution notes, expressly stated for FHIR R5 but not
for the FHIR R4 line under which this artifact was published. Treat
the request as non-persuasive for the existing publication. Track the
THO migration as future work tied to the next major version of the
US Military Service specification rather than as a corrective change
to the published artifact.

#### Justification

This matches the disposition already recorded on the ticket
("Considered for Future Use" / non-persuasive). The argument is that
re-canonicalizing a published artifact is itself disruptive, and the
authoritative cross-WG rule the reporter is invoking was not in force
at publication time. Declining now defers the change to a moment when
it can be done as part of a planned, versioned republication rather
than as a retrofit.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The underlying complaint is correct as a matter of HL7 governance —
HL7-stewarded code systems belong in THO, and `military-service-solor-extension`
is exactly that kind of artifact. Disposition C (the currently-recorded
"non-persuasive / future use" outcome) addresses the procedural
question but leaves the policy violation in place and gives implementers
no migration path. Disposition A is the cleanest answer in principle
but silently breaks any implementer who has already bound to the
`hl7.org/fhir`-rooted canonical, which is exactly the kind of
gratuitous breakage HL7 normally avoids on published content.
Disposition B threads the needle: it satisfies the policy by making the
THO-rooted CodeSystem authoritative (and creates the requested UTG
registration ticket), while preserving the legacy URL as a deprecated
alias so existing instances and profiles keep resolving. That gives
Cross-Group Projects a defensible, implementer-safe migration story
rather than either ignoring the issue or breaking deployed content.
