## Summary

The reporter (Keith Salzman, on behalf of comment author Chris Shawn) asks
the US Military Service (FHIR) IG to take an explicit position on clinical
conditions that represent health risks tied to a patient's military career
exposures — for example Post Traumatic Stress (PTSD), asbestos exposure,
burn pit exposure, and depleted uranium exposure. The implication is that
the IG should either (a) include the required codes for risk status and
the profiles/extensions that would capture these conditions so the data
can be surfaced for clinician review or patient-reported entry, or (b)
explicitly call this need out in a "deferred" section together with
documentation of how such information is expected to be captured. The
ticket was originally resolved 2022-07-28 as **Considered for Future
Use** (vote 11-0-1, Sean Muir / Eric Haas), then re-opened by lynn_laakso
on 2023-05-30 for re-disposition post-STU1 publication. No
cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were
returned by FhirAugury, so the request stands on its own ticket history.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Expand the US Military Service (FHIR) IG to formally cover clinical
conditions arising from military career exposures. Concretely:

- Define (or adopt) a value set of "military exposure-related risk
  conditions" covering at minimum PTSD, asbestos exposure, burn pit
  exposure, and depleted uranium exposure, and bind it to a Condition
  profile (e.g., `MilitaryExposureRelatedCondition`) constraining
  `Condition.code` to that value set and tying `Condition.subject` to
  the IG's Patient/Veteran profile.
- Define (or reuse) an Observation profile for "exposure" events
  (Observation.code from a "military exposure" value set, with `subject`
  = patient, `effective[x]` capturing the exposure period, and
  `value[x]` / components for intensity / duration where appropriate).
- Add an extension (or reuse `Condition.evidence` /
  `Observation.derivedFrom`) that links a risk-status Condition back to
  the underlying exposure Observation(s), so a clinician's "review of
  risks" view can be assembled from the data.
- Update the IG's narrative (the page that today only says "The
  clinician may be working with Veteran in the community and must
  assess the health risks associate with military service.") to point
  at the new profiles, value sets, and the data-capture pattern (who
  records exposure, who records derived risk Conditions, and how
  patient-entered data is reconciled with provider-entered data).

#### Justification

The ticket's substantive ask — that the IG take ownership of how
exposure-driven health risks are captured and surfaced — is consistent
with the IG's stated scope of supporting clinicians assessing health
risks from military service. A profile-plus-value-set approach uses
existing FHIR mechanisms (Condition, Observation, value sets,
extensions) rather than inventing new ones, and aligns with how other
US-realm IGs (e.g., US Core, USCDI-aligned IGs) carry similar "risk /
exposure" content. It also closes the documentation gap that the
re-opening comment implicitly flags: STU1 documents *time spent* in
service but not the *risks attributable to* that service.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt a **scoping** change rather than a content change: keep the IG
focused on its current STU1 spine (documenting service time and
context) and add an explicit **"Deferred / Out-of-Scope for STU1"**
page that:

- Names exposure-driven risk conditions (PTSD, asbestos, burn pit,
  depleted uranium, etc.) as a known, important use case the IG
  acknowledges.
- States that the IG does not currently define profiles, value sets, or
  extensions for those conditions, and points implementers at existing
  Condition/Observation guidance plus any relevant external code
  systems (e.g., SNOMED CT, ICD-10-CM, LOINC) for capture today.
- Commits to revisiting this content in a future ballot cycle, with a
  brief note on what would have to be settled (terminology, who
  authors the exposure record, patient-entered vs. provider-entered
  data, linkage between exposure and downstream risk Condition).
- Updates the existing narrative paragraph so it no longer reads as if
  the IG itself supports the risk-assessment workflow when in fact the
  data model for risks is out of scope.

#### Justification

This addresses the *acknowledgement* half of the original ask without
expanding STU2 scope into terminology and clinical-domain work the
work group may not be staffed to land on a ballot timeline. It also
honours the original 2022-07-28 vote (11-0-1, "Considered for Future
Use") while fixing the documentation defect that prompted the
re-open: the IG currently mentions risk assessment in narrative but
provides no machinery for it, which is misleading to implementers. A
deferred-scope page is cheap, low-risk, and forward-compatible with
Disposition A in a later cycle.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as **Persuasive with Mod / No change beyond keeping current
scope**, on the basis that exposure-driven clinical conditions
(PTSD, asbestos exposure, burn pit exposure, depleted uranium
exposure) are general clinical-condition content already adequately
representable with base FHIR Condition / Observation and existing
US-realm clinical IGs, and therefore do not need new artifacts in the
US Military Service IG.

#### Justification

The four named conditions are not unique to military service in their
FHIR representation — PTSD is a Condition coded from standard problem
lists, and the three exposures are Observation-shaped events that any
exposure-history use case can already model. Pulling that content into
the US Military Service IG risks duplicating (and diverging from)
work better done in a clinical IG, and would expand the IG's
maintenance surface (terminology curation, conformance to other US
IGs) without a clear delta over base FHIR. The original 2022-07-28
vote already concluded the same thing.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The 2023-05-30 re-open comment is explicit that the trigger for
revisiting was post-STU1 publication review, not new use-case
evidence, and the work group's earlier 11-0-1 vote concluded that
exposing risk status was a *future* concern. Disposition B respects
that conclusion while fixing the real defect the ticket surfaces:
the IG's own narrative implies it supports risk assessment from
military service, but STU1 provides no profiles, value sets, or
extensions to actually do so. A "Deferred / Out-of-Scope for STU1"
page (plus a small narrative tweak) closes that documentation gap
cheaply, signals intent to implementers, and leaves the door open to
Disposition A in a subsequent ballot once terminology and authoring
responsibilities are settled. Disposition A is the right *eventual*
answer but is too large to land as a single re-opened ticket without
a scoped sub-project; Disposition C ignores the documentation
mismatch the re-opener was reacting to.
