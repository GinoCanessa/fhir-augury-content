## Summary

The ticket asks the US Vital Signs with Qualifying Extensions (FHIR) IG
to clarify how an implementer should differentiate, on an averaged blood
pressure Observation, whether the contributing readings were taken at
the **same anatomical site** (e.g., the same brachial cuff position over
multiple readings on one arm) versus **different sites** (e.g., readings
mixed across the left and right arm, or across other body locations).
The current guidance for the average BP profile does not give a clear,
machine-readable way to communicate that distinction, which matters
clinically because mixing sites can change the interpretation of the
average. Although the orchestrator surfaces no direct cross-referenced
Jira tickets for FHIR-28459, several closely related tickets address
the broader "what does 'average' mean and how is it specified" problem
in this IG family — notably
[FHIR-28268](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-28268) (expectations for
extensions on averaged BP, the most closely scoped sibling),
[FHIR-43402](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43402) and
[FHIR-43459](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43459) (definition and
contribution rules for the average in US Core), and
[FHIR-45414](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-45414) (algorithm /
protocol clarification in CardX Hypertension Management). Together they
show that the workgroup has, since 2020, repeatedly converged on
clarifying *how* an average is constructed but has not yet adopted a
specific representation for the *site* dimension that this ticket
calls out.

### [FHIR-43459: Which blood pressure measurements should contribute to the average?](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43459)

Vitals.

Reporter BenEaton.

### [FHIR-45414: Unclear how to "specify" the "specified algorithm or protocol" used for average BP](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-45414)

Filed in 2024 by Daniel Rutz against CardX Hypertension Management.
Resolved Persuasive with Modification: the IG dropped the requirement to
identify a "specified algorithm or protocol" and adopted "the

explicitly choosing *less* prescriptive structure around how an average

site/method context onto Observation-level attributes rather than onto
the algorithm string.

Reporter Daniel Rutz; commenter JIRAUSER19604.

## Proposed Dispositions

Because there are no GitHub cross-references, the dispositions below
name the affected IG and profile (US Vital Signs with Qualifying
Extensions, specifically the average BP profile / its qualifying
extensions) rather than concrete repo paths. If the workgroup adopts
Disposition A or B, a follow-on `repo-analysis` of the IG's source
repository will be required before the change can be drafted.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add explicit guidance and a normative representation in the US Vital
Signs with Qualifying Extensions IG so that an averaged BP Observation
unambiguously declares whether all contributing readings were taken at
the same anatomical site or across different sites. Concretely:

1. On the average-BP profile, require `Observation.bodySite` (or the
   IG's existing body-site qualifying extension) to be populated when
   all contributing readings share a single site, and bind it to the
   existing body-site value set.
2. Define a new qualifying extension
   `same-site-vs-multi-site-readings` (boolean or coded:
   `single-site` | `multi-site` | `unknown`) that MUST be present on
   the averaged BP when the average is derived from more than one
   reading. When `multi-site`, `Observation.bodySite` SHALL be absent
   (or carry a "mixed" code) and each contributing reading referenced
   via `derivedFrom` / `hasMember` SHALL itself carry its own
   `bodySite`.
3. Add invariants and worked examples (single-arm home cuff, mixed-arm
   clinical capture) to the IG narrative.

#### Justification

This is the most direct response to the request. It makes the
single-site vs multi-site dimension first-class on the averaged BP
Observation rather than implicit, which is consistent with how the IG
already treats other "qualifying" context (method, device, time
window — see FHIR-28268). Carrying both a profile-level constraint and
a small explicit extension is also defensible against the precedent
set by [FHIR-43459](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43459), which
declined to constrain *which* readings contribute: by exposing the
site context, downstream consumers (population health, hypertension
management apps including the CardX IG affected by
[FHIR-45414](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-45414)) can apply their
own contribution rules without losing information.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Do not introduce a new qualifying extension. Instead, document a
recommended pattern in the IG narrative that uses already-available
FHIR mechanics:

- The averaged BP Observation MUST link to its contributing readings via
  `Observation.derivedFrom` (or `hasMember` for grouping).
- Each contributing reading carries its own `Observation.bodySite`.
- The averaged Observation's `bodySite` is populated only when every
  contributing reading shares the same site; otherwise it is omitted.
- A profile invariant enforces that when `derivedFrom` references more
  than one reading and the readings have differing `bodySite` values,
  the averaged Observation's `bodySite` SHALL be empty.
- Add narrative examples and a "how to interpret missing bodySite on an
  average" paragraph.

#### Justification

This reuses base-FHIR semantics (`derivedFrom`, `bodySite`, profile
invariants) without minting new extensions, which the recent
[FHIR-45414](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-45414) resolution favoured
(less prescriptive scaffolding around averaged BPs in CardX). It also
reduces the implementer burden — particularly relevant given the
existing slicing/publisher fragility around the average-BP profile noted
in the *IG creation > New slicing error* Zulip thread above. The
trade-off vs. Disposition A is that "site differs" is encoded
*implicitly* (by the absence of `bodySite` plus the presence of
multiple `derivedFrom` references with differing `bodySite`s), which is
slightly harder to query directly and provides no place to express
`unknown` — both points the workgroup should weigh.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark FHIR-28459 as Resolved – No Change with the rationale that the
question is already addressable using existing FHIR mechanics and does
not warrant new IG-specific structure. Direct implementers to populate
`Observation.bodySite` (or omit it) per the standard semantics on the
averaged BP, and to use `derivedFrom` / `hasMember` to expose
contributing readings whose own `bodySite`s answer the question.

#### Justification

Defensible on two grounds. First, the close sibling
[FHIR-43459](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43459) was resolved Not
Persuasive on the closely related "which measurements should contribute
to the average?" question, which suggests the workgroup is reluctant to
add prescriptive constraints around how the average is composed.
Second, the recent CardX resolution
([FHIR-45414](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-45414)) explicitly moved
*away* from prescribing structure around how an average BP is
specified, in favour of a plain "statistical mean" definition.
Declining keeps the IG aligned with that direction. The cost is that
the gap Cooper Thompson and Rob Hausam are still discussing on Zulip
in 2025 remains unaddressed in the IG narrative.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B addresses the substance of FHIR-28459 — implementers can
unambiguously distinguish single-site from multi-site averaged BPs —
without minting a new qualifying extension that the IG would then need
to maintain, version, and reconcile against the parallel work in US
Core and CardX. It is consistent with the directional signal from
[FHIR-43459](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43459) (don't over-
constrain how the average is composed) and
[FHIR-45414](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-45414) (prefer the
plain-language "mean" definition over algorithm/protocol scaffolding),
while still providing a profile invariant that gives the workgroup
something concrete to ballot. Disposition A is a reasonable fallback if
the workgroup decides that the implicit "absent bodySite means mixed
sites" rule is too subtle for downstream consumers — in that case the
explicit `single-site / multi-site / unknown` code from A delivers
better queryability at the cost of a new extension. Disposition C
should only be chosen if the workgroup is prepared to leave the issue
open for a third cycle, given that it has resurfaced on Zulip as
recently as August 2025.
