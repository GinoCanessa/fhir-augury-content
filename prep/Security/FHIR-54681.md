## Summary

The reporter (John Moehrke) observes that the Scalable Consent Management
(FAST Consent) IG explicitly leaves enforcement out of scope, and asks the
authors to point readers to the IHE Privacy Consent on FHIR (IHE-PCF)
profile, which *does* cover enforcement and does so in a way that
leverages OAuth. The reporter further notes that, although IHE-PCF does
not explicitly call out the HL7 SMART App Launch IG, IHE-PCF was designed
to be compatible with SMART App Launch — so IHE-PCF is a natural
"enforcement companion" for an IG that, like this one, restricts itself
to consent representation and exchange.

Two other Security tickets against the same IG sit on the same axis and
the workgroup has already chained them to this one in their resolutions:

- [FHIR-54662](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54662) — "authentication
  scope" (also Moehrke). The proposed resolution there explicitly defers
  the OAuth / SMART App Launch wording to FHIR-54681, treating
  authentication itself as an out-of-scope-but-required precondition.
- [FHIR-56088](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-56088) — "Relationship
  with IHE PCF and CDA Privacy Consent Directives not clear" (Hans
  Buitendijk). Its resolution explicitly cites FHIR-54681 and commits to
  using the same change to clarify how FAST Consent profiles relate to
  IHE-PCF profiles (the FAST profiles being a minimal/US-Core-flavoured
  variant that an instance can simultaneously validate against IHE-PCF).

Taken together, the requested change is narrow — add a paragraph (or
short section) in the IG that (a) names IHE-PCF as the recommended
enforcement companion, (b) states that IHE-PCF leverages OAuth, and (c)
notes the (implicit) compatibility with the HL7 SMART App Launch IG.

Asks whether FAST Consent replaces or depends on IHE-PCF / CDA Privacy
Consent Directives, and whether the IHE-PCF artifact examples are also

Directives are out of scope. This ticket is the strongest case for
treating FHIR-54681's edit as a structural "Relationship to IHE-PCF"
section rather than a one-line note.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a short, targeted note (2–4 sentences) in the IG's existing
"enforcement out of scope" paragraph (or in the section that already
references IHE-PCF) that says, in substance:

> Enforcement of consent is out of scope for this IG. Implementers who
> need an enforcement model are referred to **IHE Privacy Consent on
> FHIR (IHE-PCF)**, which defines an enforcement model that leverages
> OAuth (see
> [IHE-PCF Volume 1 — Actors and Transactions](https://profiles.ihe.net/ITI/PCF/volume-1.html#actors-and-transactions)).
> While IHE-PCF does not explicitly cite the HL7 SMART App Launch IG,
> IHE-PCF is designed to be compatible with SMART App Launch and can be
> deployed alongside it.

No new conformance statements, no profile changes. Drop one cross-link
to the IHE-PCF Volume 1 anchor named in the ticket.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter asked for, exactly what the
workgroup's own resolution description committed to ("we will also call
out the OAuth point in this issue"), and matches the resolution status
that has already been applied (Persuasive with Modification, resolved
2026-02-27). It is a pure narrative addition: zero risk to conformance,
no impact on existing Consent profiles or operations, and no impact on
Ballot scope. It also discharges the OAuth/SMART text that
[FHIR-54662](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54662) explicitly defers
to this ticket, so accepting it closes a small dependency cluster in
one edit.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — Promote to a "Relationship to IHE-PCF" Section

#### Proposal

Rather than a 2–4 sentence inline note, introduce a short dedicated
section (e.g., "Relationship to IHE Privacy Consent on FHIR" under the
IG's background / scope material) that bundles three points the
workgroup has already committed to in adjacent tickets:

1. **Enforcement & OAuth (this ticket, FHIR-54681).** Enforcement is
   out of scope; IHE-PCF is the recommended companion and leverages
   OAuth; SMART App Launch compatibility is implicit.
2. **Profile alignment (FHIR-56088).** State explicitly that the FAST
   Consent profiles are a minimal, "US-Core-ized" flavour of the
   IHE-PCF Consent profiles and that a single Consent instance can be
   designed to validate against both. State that CDA Privacy Consent
   Directives are out of scope.
3. **Actors and operations vs IHE transactions** (also called out in
   the workgroup's resolution to this ticket). Map FAST Consent's
   actors and operations to the IHE-PCF actor/transaction model so
   readers coming from IHE can find the equivalent.

The new section would also be the natural anchor target for the OAuth
note that [FHIR-54662](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54662) defers
here.

#### Justification

The workgroup's resolution to FHIR-54681 already enumerates *four*
items it intends to make more explicit (actors, operations vs
transactions, "US-Core-ized" profile flavour, OAuth note). At that
volume, an inline note inside an unrelated paragraph is awkward; a
sub-section is the cleaner home and makes the cross-IG relationship
discoverable in the IG's TOC. It also gives FHIR-56088 a single canonical
landing place rather than two near-duplicate paragraphs in different
sections. Trade-off vs. Disposition A: slightly more authoring work and
one more page to balloters, but no conformance impact and a meaningful
reduction in future "where does IHE-PCF fit?" tickets.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline on the basis that the IG already references IHE-PCF in several
places and that adding an enforcement/OAuth pointer risks implying a
normative dependency on IHE-PCF (and, by extension, on SMART App Launch)
that the workgroup has not actually committed to. Close as
"Not Persuasive — already addressed by existing IHE-PCF references."

#### Justification

Defensible only on a strict scope reading: this IG has consciously
limited itself to consent representation and exchange, and any text
that recommends a specific enforcement companion edges toward
prescribing implementer architecture. If the workgroup wants to keep
enforcement a hard "out of scope," a non-normative pointer can still be
read as guidance.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **B (Alternative — promote to a
"Relationship to IHE-PCF" section).**

The workgroup has *already* resolved this ticket as "Persuasive with
Modification," so Disposition C is essentially off the table — the
review question now is the *form* of the edit, not whether to make it.
Disposition A is the minimum-viable version of that edit and is fine in
isolation, but the workgroup's own resolution description for
FHIR-54681 enumerates four distinct clarifications (actors,
operations-vs-transactions, US-Core-ized profile flavour, OAuth /
SMART note), and FHIR-56088's resolution explicitly piggy-backs on the
same edit to clarify the FAST↔IHE-PCF profile relationship and to
exclude CDA Privacy Consent Directives. That is the surface area of a
small section, not a footnote. Landing all of it in one named
"Relationship to IHE Privacy Consent on FHIR" sub-section gives
implementers a single discoverable anchor, lets FHIR-54662 cite a
stable target for its deferred OAuth wording, and avoids
sprinkling near-duplicate IHE-PCF references through the rest of the
guide. The recommendation is therefore Disposition B with the explicit
note to wire the new section as the resolution target for FHIR-54662
and FHIR-56088 as well.
