## Summary

Hans Buitendijk reports that the Scalable Consent Management (FAST Consent
Management / "FastConsent") FHIR IG does not make clear how it relates to
two prior bodies of work: the IHE Privacy Consent on FHIR (PCF) profile
suite and the older CDA Privacy Consent Directives. The reporter wants
the IG to state explicitly whether FastConsent is intended to **replace**
those artifacts, **depend on** them (e.g., embedding an existing
PCF/CDA consent as the substantive consent document with computable
rules layered on top), or merely **co-exist** with them. He suggests
showing the relationship concretely via an example that references an
underlying consent through `FastConsent.source[x]` (by `DocumentReference`)
or via `.provision`, and asks whether the existing IHE PCF artifact
samples are intended to be valid FastConsent instances as-is.

The closely related ticket [FHIR-54681](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54681)
(also Buitendijk / Moehrke, same IG, resolved *Persuasive with
Modification*) tackles a neighbouring question — that the IG does not
cover enforcement and should call out that IHE-PCF does cover
enforcement via OAuth (and is compatible with SMART App Launch). The
proposed resolution to FHIR-56088 leans on FHIR-54681's planned edits:
"As a result of FHIR-54681, we will be adding more clarity on the
relationship between the IHE-PCF guide and this guide. Our profiles are
the minimal needed elements to enable the federation of consent
management systems. They also are inline with IHE-PCF guide in that one
could implement a Consent instance that validated against our profile
and against the IHE-PCF profiles." The same resolution explicitly
states that **CDA Privacy Consent Directives have not been considered**
in the creation of this IG. Buitendijk's follow-up comment accepts the
direction but pushes for the IG to make it clear that FAST Consent
Management effectively *replaces* IHE PCF, and to bring the IHE PCF
artifact set across as a starter set, observing that IHE PCF on its
own does not yield consistent computable interpretation across data
holders.

## Details

**Description:**
It is not clear whether FastConsent is meant to replace both IHE PCF or
CDA Privacy Consent Directives, or depends on them for the specific
consent (including any computable rules to the extent available).
Suggest to clarify the relationship and include an example of how they
are referenced through `FastConsent.source[x]` by way of
`DocumentReference`, or perhaps in `.provision`. Or are the consent
examples here
([https://profiles.ihe.net/ITI/PCF/1.1.0/artifacts.html](https://profiles.ihe.net/ITI/PCF/1.1.0/artifacts.html))
meant to be samples of FastConsent instances as-is? Any clarification
on relationship and use would be helpful.

**Resolution (Persuasive):**
"As a result of FHIR-54681, we will be adding more clarity on the
relationship between the IHE-PCF guide and this guide. Our profiles are
the minimal needed elements to enable the federation of consent
management systems. They also are inline with IHE-PCF guide in that one
could implement a Consent instance that validated against our profile
and against the IHE-PCF profiles. We have not considered CDA Privacy
Consent Directives in the creation of this guide."

**Comments:**

- **hbuitendijk (2026-03-25):** Generally OK with the proposed
  disposition, but asks the IG to make explicit that FAST Consent
  Management effectively *replaces* IHE PCF. Suggests pulling the
  *Artifacts Summary — Privacy Consent on FHIR (PCF) v1.1.0* into FAST
  Consent Management as a starter set. Notes that IHE PCF on its own
  does not make clear how consents are interpreted consistently across
  implementations when the data scope is not all-or-nothing — i.e., it
  does not get implementers to computable consents with consistent
  interpretation by all data holders for the patient.
- **hbuitendijk (2026-03-23):** Posts the corrected URL for IHE PCF:
  [Privacy Consent on FHIR (PCF) Home v1.1.0](https://profiles.ihe.net/ITI/PCF/1.1.0/).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the *Persuasive* resolution as written and execute it concretely
in the IG narrative and examples:

1. Add a new (or expand an existing) "Relationship to other IGs / prior
   work" page in the FAST Consent Management IG that:
   - States explicitly how FAST Consent Management relates to IHE PCF
     (positioning, scope overlap, what is added — federation /
     computable rules — and what is intentionally not duplicated —
     enforcement transport, which lives in IHE PCF + OAuth/SMART, per
     FHIR-54681).
   - States explicitly that CDA Privacy Consent Directives are out of
     scope and have not been considered, and gives implementers
     guidance on how an existing CDA consent can be referenced (as a
     `DocumentReference` from `FastConsent.source[x]`) without being
     re-modelled.
2. Add at least one worked example instance demonstrating the
   relationship: a `FastConsent` instance whose `source[x]` points (via
   `DocumentReference`) at an IHE PCF consent document, with
   `.provision` carrying the computable layer that FAST adds. If
   feasible, add a parallel example whose `source[x]` references a CDA
   Privacy Consent Directive document.
3. Add a short note clarifying whether the IHE PCF
   `artifacts.html` sample consents are intended to validate as
   `FastConsent` as-is (per the resolution text: "one could implement
   a Consent instance that validated against our profile and against
   the IHE-PCF profiles" — say so explicitly, and ideally include one
   such dual-validating example in the IG's example set).

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter asked for and what the WG already
committed to in the resolution. It also dovetails cleanly with
FHIR-54681's planned IHE-PCF cross-references, so the two tickets can
land as a single editorial pass without rework. Concrete worked
examples (rather than only narrative) directly answer Buitendijk's
"are these meant to be FastConsent instances as-is?" question and
remove a real source of implementer confusion. Calling out CDA
explicitly — even just to scope it out — closes a known gap rather
than leaving readers to infer it.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Do everything in Disposition A **plus** adopt Buitendijk's stronger
framing from his 2026-03-25 comment: position FAST Consent Management
as the FHIR successor to IHE PCF for federated, computable consent,
and import the IHE PCF v1.1.0 artifact set as a *starter set* of
FastConsent examples (curated and re-published as IG examples, with
provenance back to IHE PCF). Add a brief gap analysis section
explaining what computable behaviour FAST adds on top of IHE PCF (so
that consents are interpreted consistently across data holders, not
just all-or-nothing).

#### Justification

Buitendijk's comment is direct community feedback that the *Persuasive*
disposition, while accepted, doesn't go far enough — implementers will
still be left guessing whether to start from PCF or from FAST. A
curated starter set anchored in PCF's existing artifacts gives
implementers a tested baseline, and an explicit "what FAST adds"
section frames the IG's value proposition (federation + computable
interpretation) instead of leaving it implicit. The cost is higher:
it requires coordination with IHE on republication / attribution of
their artifacts, and the WG would need to commit to maintaining the
starter set against future PCF revisions. CDA remains explicitly out
of scope under this option as well.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request to add IHE-PCF / CDA relationship narrative and
examples to the FAST Consent Management IG, on the grounds that
positioning vs. IHE PCF is already being addressed under FHIR-54681
and that CDA Privacy Consent Directives are out of scope for a FHIR
IG. Refer the reporter to FHIR-54681's edits and close as
*Not Persuasive* / duplicate-of-FHIR-54681.

#### Justification

Defensible only on a narrow reading: FHIR-54681 already commits the IG
to more explicit IHE-PCF cross-references, and the FAST IG has no
mandate to document a CDA artifact. However, this disposition leaves
the reporter's specific asks unanswered (the worked
`FastConsent.source[x]` → `DocumentReference` → IHE PCF example, and
the explicit statement on whether PCF samples validate as FastConsent
as-is), and the WG has already voted *Persuasive* on the ticket — so
declining now would require reversing that vote. Not recommended.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **A**

The WG has already voted *Persuasive* and committed in the resolution
to add the requested clarifications, leveraging the editorial work
planned under FHIR-54681. Disposition A simply makes that commitment
concrete: a "Relationship to other IGs" page, an explicit out-of-scope
statement for CDA, and at least one worked
`FastConsent` ↔ IHE PCF example (with `source[x]` → `DocumentReference`
demonstrating Buitendijk's exact use case). This closes the ticket on
the terms the WG has already accepted and aligns naturally with the
edits already promised under FHIR-54681, so both tickets can be
delivered in one editorial pass.

Disposition B (PCF as a starter set, with gap analysis) is attractive
and reflects the reporter's follow-up comment, but it expands scope
beyond what the WG has voted on and creates an ongoing maintenance
relationship with IHE PCF artifacts. It is worth raising in the WG
discussion as an *additional* commitment on top of A, but should not
block landing A. Disposition C contradicts the existing *Persuasive*
vote and leaves the reporter's concrete requests unresolved.
