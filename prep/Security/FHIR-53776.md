## Summary

The Scalable Consent Management (FHIR) IG, in its conformance section, defines
operation/conformance statement OP7, which mandates that conformant servers
support a fixed set of `Consent` search parameters: `patient`, `controller`,
`manager`, `date`, `status`, and `scope`. The reporter (Abderrazek Boufahja)
asks whether the `controller` and `manager` search parameters could be
downgraded from mandatory to *optional* or *recommended*, noting that they
are not default `Consent` search parameters in FHIR R4 and that some use
cases â€” for example, consent management entirely within a single organization
â€” would not need them. No related Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items
were surfaced via cross-references for this ticket; the request stands on its
own as a conformance-loosening proposal against the IG's CapabilityStatement.

## Details

**Description:**

> https://hl7.org/fhir/us/consent-management/2026Jan/conformance.html#conformance-statements-in-this-guide
>
> OP7 mandates to support all search parameters: patient, controller, manager, date, status, and scope.
>
> Can controller and manager search parameters be optional or recommended instead of mandatory? Those are not default search parameters in R4; and for some use cases, they may not be needed, like a management of the Consent in the same organization.

**Comments:**

None recorded on the ticket.

**Resolution (already applied on the ticket â€” `Not Persuasive with Modification`):**

> The `controller` and `manager` search parameters are fundamental to the
> federated consent management model that is the core purpose of this IG.
> Searching by `manager` enables subscriber systems to find consents for which
> they must maintain subscriptions; searching by `controller` enables systems
> to locate consents relevant to their data governance role. Making these
> optional would undermine the interoperability goals of the IG in federated
> deployments. However, we will add implementation guidance acknowledging
> that in single-organization deployments the practical utility of these
> parameters may be limited, and explaining clearly why conformant
> implementations must support them to participate in multi-system consent
> propagation networks.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the Scalable Consent Management IG's CapabilityStatement (the resource
backing the "Conformance Statements in this Guide" page, conformance
statement OP7), demote `Consent.controller` and `Consent.manager`
search-parameter conformance from `SHALL` to `SHOULD` (recommended), leaving
`patient`, `date`, `status`, and `scope` as `SHALL`. Update the OP7 prose on
`conformance.html` to match, and add a note that servers participating in
federated/multi-organization consent propagation are still expected to
support both parameters.

#### Justification

This directly addresses the reporter's concern: `controller` and `manager`
are not standard R4 `Consent` search parameters, and a single-organization
deployment may legitimately have no use for them. Aligning the IG with R4
defaults lowers the barrier to claiming conformance for narrowly-scoped
deployments and matches FHIR's general preference for using `SHOULD` when a
capability is genuinely optional in some realistic deployment topologies.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach (selected as resolution)

#### Proposal

Keep `Consent.controller` and `Consent.manager` as `SHALL` in OP7, but
modify the IG to explicitly explain *why*: add implementation guidance to
`conformance.html` (and/or a narrative section on the Consent profile page)
that (a) describes the federated consent-management model the IG targets,
(b) states that `manager` is required so subscriber systems can locate the
consents they must subscribe to, (c) states that `controller` is required so
systems can find consents relevant to their data-governance role, and
(d) acknowledges that in single-organization deployments the practical
utility of these parameters may be limited but that supporting them is the
price of admission to multi-system consent-propagation networks.

#### Justification

The IG's purpose is *scalable* (i.e., cross-organization, federated) consent
management; allowing servers to opt out of `controller`/`manager` search
fragments interoperability for the very deployments the IG exists to serve.
Keeping the `SHALL` preserves a single, predictable conformance contract
across all conformant servers and avoids the "optional in name, required in
practice" trap. Adding the rationale text answers the reporter's underlying
concern (the requirement felt unjustified) without weakening conformance.
This is the disposition already recorded on the ticket
(`Not Persuasive with Modification`).

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Reject the request outright with no IG changes: leave OP7 mandating all six
search parameters and add no new explanatory text. Close as
`Not Persuasive`.

#### Justification

Defensible only on the grounds that the rationale for federated search by
`controller` and `manager` is already inferable from the IG's stated scope.
However, given that an implementer was confused enough to file a ticket
about this, declining without modification leaves the same documentation
gap unaddressed and invites repeat tickets â€” which is why this option is
weaker than B.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the right balance. Disposition A would weaken the IG's
core value proposition: the whole point of *Scalable* Consent Management is
the federated, cross-organization model in which subscriber and controller
systems must be able to discover the consents that bind them, and that
discovery is exactly what the `manager` and `controller` search parameters
enable. Demoting them to `SHOULD` would let conformant servers drop
capabilities that other conformant servers depend on, fragmenting
interoperability. Disposition C ignores the legitimate signal in the
ticket â€” the requirement read as unmotivated to a careful implementer â€” and
leaves the documentation gap in place. Disposition B keeps the conformance
bar where it needs to be while adding the explanatory text that would have
prevented the ticket in the first place, which is also the resolution
already applied on FHIR-53776.
