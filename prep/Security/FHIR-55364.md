## Summary

FHIR-55364 (Security WG, Scalable Consent Management IG) observes that the IG's
implementation notes describe a clear behavioural model around the Consent
`manager` extension — the originating system records itself as the manager,
downstream systems preserve that identifier on ingest, and ingesting systems
subscribe to updates from the manager — but none of this is currently expressed
as normative conformance language. The reporter (Joseph M. Lamy) raises the
concern that the propagation model cannot be relied upon if participants are
not actually required to play their assigned roles.

The ticket has already been resolved as **Persuasive**, with a resolution that
proposes adding three normative statements: (1) the creating system **SHALL**
record its system identifier in the `manager` extension; (2) ingesting systems
**SHALL** preserve the `manager` value unchanged; and (3) ingesting systems
**SHOULD** subscribe to updates from the system identified in `manager`. The
resolution explicitly defers subscription authorisation/revocation behaviour to
the companion ticket [FHIR-54101](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54101)
("Authorization to Subscribe") and notes that requirement (1) — recording the
manager identifier on creation — was already committed under
[FHIR-54678](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54678) ("Consent Network
Relationship needs clarity"). FHIR-55364 therefore narrows to (2) preservation
on ingest and (3) the SHOULD-subscribe statement, layered on top of the
mechanism FHIR-54678 introduced and the authorisation framework FHIR-54101 is
adding.

## Details

**Description:**

> There is no normative usage defined for the manager extension. The
> implementation notes seem to establish some clear expectations: the system
> that creates a Consent adds itself as the manager, systems that ingest
> Consents preserve the manager and subscribe to updates at the manager. Will
> the propagation model work if systems are not required to play their parts?

**Resolution Description (already recorded as Persuasive):**

> We will add normative conformance statements for manager extension usage that
> complement the subscription authorization requirements established in
> FHIR-54101. Specifically: (1) the system that creates a Consent SHALL record
> its system identifier in the manager extension (already committed in
> FHIR-54678); (2) systems that ingest a Consent from another system SHALL
> preserve the manager extension value unchanged; (3) ingesting systems SHOULD
> subscribe to updates from the system identified in the manager extension.
> The subscription authorization and revocation behavior is handled by
> FHIR-54101.

**Comments:** None on this ticket.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the resolution exactly as already drafted on the ticket. In the Scalable
Consent Management IG, on the page that currently describes the `manager`
extension and the Consent propagation/network model, replace the implementation
notes with three normative conformance statements:

1. **SHALL — origination.** *"When a Consent resource is created on a system,
   that system SHALL record its own system identifier in the `manager`
   extension on the Consent."* (Cross-link to FHIR-54678, which introduces the
   originating-system rule.)
2. **SHALL — preservation on ingest.** *"When a system ingests a Consent
   resource that was created on another system, it SHALL preserve the value of
   the `manager` extension unchanged."*
3. **SHOULD — subscription on ingest.** *"A system that ingests a Consent
   resource from another system SHOULD subscribe to updates to that Consent
   from the system identified in the `manager` extension, subject to the
   authorisation and revocation rules in [the section delivered by
   FHIR-54101]."*

Mark the page hosting these statements as Normative (per the same pattern
applied in FHIR-54678 — pages with conformance statements must not be
Informative). Add a forward link to FHIR-54101's Privacy & Security section so
implementers see authorisation/revocation in context.

#### Justification

This proposal is the resolution the WG has already marked Persuasive, and it
is the minimum change needed to make the propagation model the IG already
describes actually enforceable. It composes cleanly with two adjacent
already-Persuasive tickets: FHIR-54678 supplies obligation (1) and FHIR-54101
supplies the authorisation/revocation framework that the SHOULD-subscribe
clause depends on. Splitting (2) "preserve unchanged" off as a SHALL is
consistent with how FHIR generally treats provenance-bearing extensions —
downstream resources MUST NOT silently rewrite originator metadata — and
keeping the subscribe behaviour as SHOULD (rather than SHALL) leaves room for
implementations that legitimately cannot subscribe (e.g., one-way data
recipients, archival stores) without losing conformance.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt obligations (1) and (2) as SHALLs as in Disposition A, but recast (3)
the subscribe behaviour as a CapabilityStatement-driven obligation instead of
a free-standing SHOULD on every ingesting system:

- Define a Consent-Recipient capability tag (or actor) in the IG's actor /
  CapabilityStatement section.
- For systems claiming that capability, the SHOULD-subscribe is upgraded to a
  conditional SHALL: *"A Consent Recipient that supports propagation SHALL
  subscribe to updates from the system identified in the `manager` extension,
  per the authorisation rules in [FHIR-54101's section]."*
- Systems that do not claim the propagation capability are explicitly carved
  out and only owe obligations (1) and (2).

#### Justification

This alternative addresses the concern in the original ticket more directly:
"will the propagation model work if systems are not required to play their
parts?" A bare SHOULD is testable but not enforceable in conformance reviews,
and the reporter's worry is precisely that informative/SHOULD-only language is
why the model is currently brittle. Tying the subscribe behaviour to a declared
capability (and making it SHALL within that capability) gives the IG a real
conformance hook without forcing it on systems that are legitimate one-way
recipients. The trade-off is more IG surface area (a new actor /
CapabilityStatement section), and it pushes the change beyond the wording-only
scope the WG already agreed to under "Persuasive".

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Withdraw the resolution and leave the implementation notes describing the
`manager` propagation model as informative narrative only, on the basis that
the substantive normative content the reporter is asking for is already
captured by the two companion tickets (FHIR-54678 supplies the
originator-records-itself rule; FHIR-54101 supplies the authorisation,
exchange, and revocation framework that governs subscriptions between
systems). Add only a non-normative forward-link from the manager-extension
section to those two sections.

#### Justification

A defensible "decline" rationale is that FHIR-55364 is largely already
addressed once FHIR-54678 and FHIR-54101 land. With FHIR-54678's SHALL on
recording the manager identifier and FHIR-54101's SHALLs on
authorisation/revocation, the only genuinely new content from FHIR-55364 is
"preserve unchanged on ingest" and "SHOULD subscribe" — the first arguably
falls out of generic FHIR resource-handling conventions, and the second is the
weakest of the three statements. Avoiding a third overlapping conformance
statement reduces the risk of subtle inconsistencies between the three
sections. The cost is leaving the reporter's original concern only partially
addressed: the propagation model still rests on implication rather than an
explicit "preserve `manager`" obligation.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The WG has already marked the ticket Persuasive, and Disposition A is the
direct execution of that resolution. It is also the smallest change consistent
with the reporter's concern: it converts the existing implementation notes
into testable conformance language, composes deterministically with FHIR-54678
and FHIR-54101 (so the three resolutions can land in any order without
contradicting each other), and keeps the SHOULD-subscribe clause permissive
enough that implementations with legitimate one-way ingestion patterns are not
inadvertently rendered non-conformant. Disposition B's CapabilityStatement /
actor framing is attractive on conformance-rigor grounds and is worth holding
in reserve if the WG decides during review that a bare SHOULD is too weak —
but it expands scope beyond what was previously agreed and would be better
introduced as a follow-up ticket. Disposition C should only be considered if
the WG has new information that "preserve `manager` unchanged" is fully
implied by other normative text already in the IG; absent that, leaving the
preservation rule implicit is exactly the gap FHIR-55364 was raised to close.
