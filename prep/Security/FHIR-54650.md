## Summary

John Moehrke reports that a section of the Scalable Consent Management
(FHIR) IG titled "Who has consulted my Consent record to make a decision
on sharing my health information?" conflates two distinct concepts: an
**Authorization Decision** (consulting a Consent record to decide whether
sharing is permitted) and a **Disclosure** (the act of actually sharing
health information). The text frames the use case as an authorization
decision but then uses the word "disclosure," which in IHE-BALP refers to
a different, specific permitted action with its own AuditEvent profile.
The reporter asks the IG to use the correct BALP profile — the Consent
Authorized Decision Audit Message
([BALP §3.57.6](https://profiles.ihe.net/ITI/BALP/content.html#3576-consent-authorized-decision-audit-message))
rather than (or alongside, with clear delineation from) the Privacy
Disclosure Audit Message
([BALP §3.57.7](https://profiles.ihe.net/ITI/BALP/content.html#3577-privacy-disclosure-audit-message))
— and to make clear that these are two related but different audit
events. As a secondary issue, the reporter calls out the prose
"Nestled in between..." as poor style and questions why this third case
appears between cases 1 and 2 in the section ordering.

The Jira resolution already recorded on the ticket is **Persuasive**, with
a resolution description that commits to: (1) rewriting the section to
properly distinguish the *authorization decision* audit event from the
*disclosure* audit event; (2) referencing the correct BALP profiles for
each (Consent Authorized Decision Audit Message and Privacy Disclosure
Audit Message respectively); and (3) rewriting the "nestled in between"
language for professional clarity. No related Jira tickets, Zulip
discussions, or GitHub items were surfaced by cross-reference or search.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the section "Who has consulted my Consent record to make a
decision on sharing my health information?" in the Scalable Consent
Management (FHIR) IG to:

1. Explicitly distinguish two audit event types — *authorization
   decision* (consulting a Consent to determine whether access is
   permitted) and *disclosure* (the actual release of information) — in
   the section's introductory prose.
2. Reference the IHE-BALP **Consent Authorized Decision Audit Message**
   profile
   (`https://profiles.ihe.net/ITI/BALP/content.html#3576-consent-authorized-decision-audit-message`)
   as the correct profile for "consulted my Consent record to make a
   decision," and reference the **Privacy Disclosure Audit Message**
   profile
   (`https://profiles.ihe.net/ITI/BALP/content.html#3577-privacy-disclosure-audit-message`)
   only when describing the corresponding disclosure event.
3. Rewrite the "Nestled in between..." sentence in formal IG style.
4. Reorder the case so that case 3 no longer appears between cases 1 and
   2 (or renumber the cases) to give the section a logical sequence.

#### Justification

This is the action already committed in the recorded resolution
(*Persuasive*). The reporter is the original author of IHE-BALP and is
pointing to a real conformance hazard: implementers reading the current
text could reach for the Privacy Disclosure profile when emitting audit
events for authorization decisions, producing audit data that does not
match what actually happened. Aligning the IG with BALP's existing,
well-defined profiles preserves cross-IG consistency, avoids inventing
parallel terminology, and is consistent with FHIR's general practice of
referencing established audit profiles rather than re-defining them. The
narrative and ordering fixes are pure editorial improvements with no
downstream impact.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt the editorial corrections from Disposition A (clarify the
authorization-decision vs. disclosure distinction in prose, fix the
"nestled in between" sentence, and resequence the cases), but instead of
embedding direct links to two specific BALP sections inline, add a short
**"Audit Events for Consent Decisions and Disclosures"** subsection (or
sidebar) in the IG that:

- Defines the two concepts once, in IG-native language.
- Provides a small table mapping each scenario (decision vs. disclosure)
  to the corresponding BALP profile with stable links.
- Has the case-3 paragraph reference that subsection rather than
  carrying its own BALP citations.

#### Justification

This addresses the same underlying conformance hazard as Disposition A
but reduces the maintenance burden if BALP republishes its anchors or
renumbers sections, because there is one place in the IG to update
rather than several inline citations. It also makes the
decision/disclosure distinction discoverable from the IG's own
table-of-contents instead of being buried in a single FAQ-style section,
which benefits readers approaching the IG from other entry points (e.g.,
implementers writing audit emitters who never read the section the
ticket is about). The trade-off is slightly more scope than the reporter
literally asked for — a new subsection rather than a single edit — and
the BALP citations end up one click away from the case-3 narrative
instead of inline.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the substantive content change on the grounds that the
authorization-decision vs. disclosure distinction is a property of
IHE-BALP and is already documented there, and that the IG's narrative
section is intentionally informal. Limit the change to the editorial
fixes only ("nestled in between" wording and case ordering), and leave
the BALP profile selection to the implementer-facing conformance
sections of the IG.

#### Justification

A defensible argument for declining the BALP-profile portion is that the
section called out is a *patient-facing FAQ* ("Who has consulted my
Consent record…?") rather than a conformance statement, and that
deferring the profile choice to BALP avoids duplicating BALP's own
definitions in a place where they could drift. However, this rationale
is weak: the reporter is explicitly asking for the IG not to misuse
BALP's defined terms, and "Disclosure" is already a load-bearing word
inside BALP, so leaving it ambiguous in the IG's narrative does not
actually keep the IG out of BALP's territory — it just risks
contradicting it.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The recorded resolution is already *Persuasive* and the proposed change
matches Disposition A exactly. The reporter is the author of IHE-BALP
and is identifying a concrete terminology mismatch with a downstream
conformance impact (implementers picking the wrong AuditEvent profile),
not a stylistic preference. The fix is small, additive, and entirely
within the Scalable Consent Management IG's narrative — there is no
breaking change to profiles, value sets, or examples, and no
cross-repository coordination is required (no GitHub cross-references
were found). Disposition B's centralized subsection is attractive long
term but is more scope than the ticket asks for and can be opened as a
follow-up if the same confusion recurs elsewhere in the IG. Disposition
C is not defensible given the resolution already on file and the
reporter's standing on this exact subject. Accept as requested,
including the editorial fixes for the "nestled in between" sentence and
the case ordering.
