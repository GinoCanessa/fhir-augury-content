## Summary

John Moehrke is challenging the inconsistent way the Scalable Consent
Management IG handles AuditEvent profiling across three cases. In
**case 1** the IG implies AuditEvent is the answer but does not pin it
down with the IHE-BALP profiling needed to make it interoperable. In
**case 2** the IG simply references IHE-BALP. In **case 3** the IG
defines a custom Record Disclosure *operation* **and** layers its own
AuditEvent profile on top of the BALP profile. The reporter argues this
asymmetry is unjustified: if the operation already constrains the
exchange, an additional profile in case 3 buys little, while cases 1
and 2 — which arguably need the constraints more — are left without
one. The recommendation in the ticket is that case 3 should not be
profiled.

The ticket is closely tied to a cluster of other Security / consent
tickets that all push back on the Record Disclosure operation itself:
[FHIR-54692](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54692) (AuditEvent
search/read should not require Create),
[FHIR-54648](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54648) (recording a
disclosure should be a plain REST create, not an operation),
[FHIR-53779](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53779) (rationale for the
operation vs. native FHIR REST POST),
[FHIR-55374](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-55374) (which system is
the disclosing system that must record the AuditEvent), and
[FHIR-53755](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53755) (clarifying what
"disclosure" and "information" mean in ConsentAuditEvent). The
documented resolution on this ticket — *Persuasive* — links explicitly
to those companion tickets and states that case 3's custom AuditEvent
profile will be removed when the Record Disclosure operation is
withdrawn, and that the remaining AuditEvent text will be reworked to
use IHE-BALP consistently as part of FHIR-53755.

Asks for clarification of "disclosure" and "information" — whether the

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the reporter's recommendation literally: in the Scalable Consent
Management IG, **do not define a custom AuditEvent profile for case 3**
(the Record Disclosure operation). Leave the operation in place but
state that the AuditEvent it produces conforms to the relevant IHE-BALP
profile only — the same profile already referenced for case 2 — and
remove the IG-local AuditEvent profile/StructureDefinition, the
profile-narrative on the IG's `technical.html#record-disclosure-operation`
page, and any conformance language that depends on the local profile.
Cases 1 and 2 are left untouched in this disposition.

#### Justification

This is the smallest change that removes the inconsistency the ticket
flags. It preserves the operation (which other implementers may already
be coding against) while eliminating the duplicate profiling that the
reporter calls out as not pulling its weight. It is also the change the
reporter explicitly asks for ("I would recommend you don't profile
case 3"). The cost is that the ticket's deeper concern — that the
*operation itself* is the awkward construct — is left unaddressed,
which contradicts the direction of FHIR-54648/FHIR-54692/FHIR-53779
all marked Persuasive.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach (RECOMMENDED — already committed on the ticket)

#### Proposal

Resolve case 3 by **removing the Record Disclosure operation entirely**
along with its custom AuditEvent profile, in coordination with
FHIR-54692, FHIR-54648, FHIR-55374, and FHIR-53779. Then, under the
umbrella of FHIR-53755, **rewrite the IG's AuditEvent section so that
all three cases are described uniformly in terms of IHE-BALP**: the
disclosing system is required to expose AuditEvents via an
ITI-81/Audit-Consumer-style search that conforms to the appropriate
BALP BasicAudit profile(s); the IG does not specify how AuditEvents
are *created* or stored. Clarify (per FHIR-53755) whether each
AuditEvent records sharing of the *Consent* record itself or of
*information governed by* the Consent, and (per FHIR-55374) which
participant in a multi-system consent propagation is the "disclosing
system" that must be discoverable via the search. This is the
disposition recorded as "Persuasive" on the ticket today.

#### Justification

This addresses the underlying complaint — inconsistent profiling — by
converging all three cases on a single, externally-maintained profile
family (IHE-BALP) rather than picking which case gets a local profile.
It is also internally consistent with the cluster of related Security
tickets, all of which are heading toward "use REST + BALP, drop the
custom operation". Aligning with IHE-BALP avoids re-litigating the
profile semantics inside HL7 and reduces implementer burden, since
implementers already supporting BALP get conformance "for free".
Trade-offs vs. Disposition A: it is a larger change and may be
breaking for any implementer who has begun coding against the
Record Disclosure operation, but the operation is at *Triaged* status
in a pre-publication IG and the broader cluster of Persuasive
dispositions makes the breaking change unavoidable. The IG-repo gap
flagged under Repo Context means the editor will need to confirm the
exact source files (likely under the IG's `input/pagecontent/` and
`input/resources/` or `input/profiles/` directories) at edit time
rather than from the Augury cache.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket *Not Persuasive* on the grounds that case 3 *does*
benefit from a tighter US-Core-style profile because it is the only
case where the IG itself owns the create-side semantics (via the
operation), and a stricter profile is needed to constrain what the
operation must populate. Keep the case 3 profile, and instead address
the inconsistency by **adding** comparable IG-local profiles (or
explicit BALP profile *bindings*) to cases 1 and 2 so that all three
cases are profiled, just from different starting points.

#### Justification

A defensible argument for declining is that profiling an operation's
output is qualitatively different from profiling search results: the
operation is the IG's own surface, so the IG can and arguably should
constrain it precisely. This disposition keeps the operation and
reaches consistency by *adding* profiling rather than removing it.
However, it directly contradicts the cluster of Security/CBCP tickets
(FHIR-54648, FHIR-54692, FHIR-53779, FHIR-55374) all dispositioned
Persuasive against the operation, and it would expand — not reduce —
the IG's bespoke FHIR-AuditEvent surface. It should only be selected
if the workgroup decides to revisit those companion dispositions.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

This is also the disposition currently captured on the ticket (Resolution
= *Persuasive*, with the resolution narrative pointing at FHIR-54692,
FHIR-54648, FHIR-55374, FHIR-53779, and FHIR-53755). It is the only
option that makes the IG internally consistent *and* consistent with the
direction the rest of the Security/CBCP ticket cluster has already
agreed to. Disposition A leaves the IG with a custom operation whose
underpinning rationale has been rejected elsewhere, which would
re-open the same debate at the next ballot cycle. Disposition C
doubles down on local profiling at exactly the moment the rest of the
cluster is moving away from it. The workgroup should confirm B and
make sure the rewrite under FHIR-53755 explicitly answers the case-3
question raised here so the same comment does not recur after the
operation is removed.
