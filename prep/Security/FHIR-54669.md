## Summary

John Moehrke reports that the Scalable Consent Management IG carries
two sections that overlap in stated purpose: the Overview's "Content
and Organization" subsection and the standalone "Reading this IG"
page. He further observes that the "Reading this IG" page does not
actually describe how to read this IG — its content is boilerplate
links into FHIR core, which a reader can be assumed to already know.
The request is therefore to remove or consolidate the duplication so
that each navigation target carries distinct, IG-specific value.

This ticket is closely tied to [FHIR-54686](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54686)
("where is Technical Background?"), which complains that the link
labelled *Technical Background* lands the reader on a page titled
*Reading this IG* whose content is FHIR-core boilerplate. The two
tickets are two facets of the same problem and the resolution of
FHIR-54669 explicitly defers to FHIR-54686: that ticket will rename
the page and add substantive sections (Subscription Backport,
IHE-PCF, IHE-BALP), after which the Overview's "Content and
Organization" section will be reviewed to remove or consolidate
content that duplicates the updated page.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat FHIR-54669 as already covered by the committed work on
[FHIR-54686](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54686) and mark it
**Persuasive** (matching the recorded resolution). Concretely, after
FHIR-54686 lands (rename the link, expand the page beyond FHIR core to
cover Subscription Backport, IHE-PCF, and IHE-BALP), edit the
Overview's "Content and Organization" subsection in the Scalable
Consent Management IG to remove any prose that now duplicates the
updated "Reading this IG" page, leaving only IG-specific
chapter/section orientation in the Overview.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the resolution text on the ticket already
proposes and is the minimum-impact path: the reader-facing complaint
(two pages, same purpose; one of them not actually about reading the
IG) is fixed once FHIR-54686 gives "Reading this IG" real,
IG-relevant content and FHIR-54669's editorial pass strips the
overlap from the Overview. It honours the reporter's stated trust in
the reader (we can assume they know FHIR core) and it keeps both
navigation targets, each with a distinct job: Overview = a map of
this IG; Reading this IG = the curated set of dependent
specifications.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than keeping both pages and editorially deduplicating, **merge
"Content and Organization" into the renamed "Reading this IG" page**
and delete the standalone Overview subsection. The merged page would
open with the IG's chapter/section map (formerly "Content and
Organization") and follow with the dependent-spec orientation
(Subscription Backport, IHE-PCF, IHE-BALP) added under FHIR-54686.
The Overview page would then link directly to that consolidated page
under a single heading such as "How to read this IG."

#### Justification

The reporter's underlying complaint is that two navigation targets
cover similar ground; deduplicating prose between two pages preserves
the original confusion (which page do I open?). A single,
authoritative "Reading this IG" page is harder to misread and
cheaper to maintain across ballot cycles. Trade-off vs. Disposition
A: this requires re-pointing any inbound links from elsewhere in the
IG to the merged target, and it removes a top-level Overview
subsection that some readers may have bookmarked. Given the
Infrastructure/Editorial label and that the IG is still in active
balloting (2026Jan snapshot), the cost of the relinking is low.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket as **Not Persuasive / duplicate of FHIR-54686** and
take no separate editorial action: rely entirely on the FHIR-54686
work to address the user-visible problem (link label and page
content), and leave the Overview's "Content and Organization"
subsection untouched on the basis that an Overview map and a
"Reading this IG" page serve different purposes (intra-IG navigation
vs. dependent-spec orientation).

#### Justification

This is defensible only if the workgroup judges that, once FHIR-54686
lands, the two sections will no longer feel duplicative because they
are answering different questions ("what's in this IG?" vs. "what
should I have read first?"). It avoids spending editorial cycles on a
page that is about to change anyway. The risk is that the reporter's
original observation — that the two sections feel like the same
purpose/content — survives the FHIR-54686 changes, and the ticket
gets reopened against the next ballot.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket's own resolution already records workgroup consensus
(*Persuasive*) that the duplication will be cleaned up as a follow-on
to FHIR-54686. Disposition A is the lowest-risk path that delivers
exactly that: it preserves the existing two-page structure (which
some readers and external links depend on), defers the substantive
content fix to the place where it has already been committed
(FHIR-54686), and limits this ticket to a focused editorial pass
across the Overview's "Content and Organization" subsection once
"Reading this IG" has been rewritten. Disposition B is a defensible
stronger move if the workgroup wants a single canonical page, but it
is out of scope for what the reporter asked for and would expand the
editorial blast radius. Disposition C under-delivers: it bets that
FHIR-54686 alone will resolve the perceived duplication, with no
explicit pass to confirm.
