## Summary

John Moehrke is asking the Security work group's *Scalable Consent
Management* IG to drop its custom `artifact_overview` page on the grounds
that it adds no value over the publisher-generated `artifacts` page and
introduces unnecessary confusion for IG readers who already understand
the standard artifacts layout. The ticket has been triaged with a
resolution of **Not Persuasive**, on the rationale that the IG authors
prefer the `artifact_overview` page because it lets them provide more
information and group/sort artifacts in a way the standard page does
not. John pushed back in a follow-up comment, characterizing the
rejection as essentially "I like my page" and re-stating his concern
about consistency for the broader IG-reader audience. There are no
cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items, so the
debate as captured in Augury is wholly contained in this single ticket.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Remove the custom `artifact_overview.md` (and any toc entry pointing to
it) from the Scalable Consent Management IG sources, and rely on the
publisher-generated `artifacts.html` page as the sole artifact index.
Update any narrative pages, the IG `ImplementationGuide` `definition.page`
tree, and the menu/`navbar` entries that link to `artifact_overview` so
that they instead link to `artifacts.html`. Verify with an IG Publisher
build that no broken links remain.

#### Justification

Consistency across HL7 IGs is itself a usability feature: readers who
move between IGs benefit when the artifact index lives at the same
predictable location with the same layout. The standard artifacts page
is automatically kept in sync with the IG's resources by the publisher,
which removes a class of editorial drift (artifacts added to the IG but
forgotten in `artifact_overview`). John's concern is that custom
overview pages impose a per-IG learning curve for no reader-visible
benefit, and the editorial-label scoping of this ticket is consistent
with that framing.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the `artifact_overview` page but reshape it so it adds something
the standard `artifacts.html` cannot: e.g., grouping artifacts by
*consent scenario* (decision support, disclosure, withdrawal, …),
calling out which Profiles/Operations/CapabilityStatements are
normative-track vs. example, and linking each grouping into the
narrative chapters that explain it. Then either (a) leave
`artifacts.html` in place as the auto-generated complete index and
demote `artifact_overview` to a supplementary "Reading Guide", or (b)
rename it (e.g., `reading-guide.html` / `artifacts-by-scenario.html`)
so its purpose is unambiguous to readers arriving from another IG.

#### Justification

This preserves the IG authors' stated value — additional context,
ordering, and grouping — while addressing the reporter's core
complaint: a page named "artifact overview" that competes with the
publisher's own artifact index *is* confusing, but a clearly-scoped
*reading guide* that complements it is not. Renaming and re-scoping is
a cheap editorial change that defuses the ambiguity without losing
content the authors believe is useful, and it sidesteps any debate
about which page is "the real one".

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Not Persuasive** (the current state): the IG
retains its `artifact_overview` page unchanged. Optionally add a short
intro sentence on that page that explains how it relates to
`artifacts.html` (e.g., "This page provides a curated, grouped view of
this guide's artifacts; for the complete auto-generated index, see
[Artifacts](artifacts.html).").

#### Justification

The IG authors have explicitly stated that the page provides
information and sorting they cannot get from the standard artifacts
page, and the FHIR Tooling/IG Publisher framework allows IGs latitude
in supplementary navigation. The ticket is purely editorial, no
correctness or interoperability concern is raised, and no Zulip
discussion or related ticket has been surfaced that broadens the
complaint beyond the single reporter. A small clarifying sentence (if
desired) addresses the "confusion" framing without removing content the
authors find valuable.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The ticket has a real kernel — two pages whose names suggest they cover
the same ground *do* create reader friction, especially for readers
moving between HL7 IGs — but Disposition A throws away content the IG
authors say they need, and Disposition C (the current resolution) leaves
the naming/role conflict unresolved and predictably re-litigable. A
focused rename + repurpose of `artifact_overview` into something like a
"Reading Guide" or "Artifacts by Consent Scenario" preserves the
authors' editorial value, removes the apparent overlap with
`artifacts.html`, and is small enough to land as an editorial change in
the next publication of the Scalable Consent Management IG. The
workgroup can override toward A or C, but B is the disposition most
likely to satisfy both the reporter's consistency concern and the
authors' content-curation goal.
