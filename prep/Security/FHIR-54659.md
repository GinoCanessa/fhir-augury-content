## Summary

The reporter asks the Scalable Consent Management (FHIR) IG to promote
"Ceremony" — a term already used in the IG's narrative — to a formal,
glossary-style definition. The narrative explains the concept of a
consent ceremony in passing, but it is never defined as a discrete term
that other parts of the IG (and downstream readers) can reference. The
request is purely editorial: introduce an explicit definition for
"Consent Ceremony" so the term can be cited consistently throughout the
guide. No cross-referenced Jira tickets were returned for this issue,
so the scope is bounded to this single editorial improvement; the
ticket has already been triaged as **Persuasive** with a noted
intention to add "Consent Ceremony" to the IG's Definitions section.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add "Consent Ceremony" as a formal entry in the **Definitions** /
glossary section of the Scalable Consent Management (FHIR) IG. Source
the wording directly from the existing narrative passage that
introduces the term, lightly edited to stand alone as a definition
(e.g., something like: *"Consent Ceremony — the act and surrounding
process by which a grantor expresses, refuses, or modifies consent,
typically witnessed and captured as a Consent resource instance."*).
Cross-link the new definition from the first occurrence(s) of "consent
ceremony" in the narrative so readers can navigate to it.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter — also the long-time Security WG
co-chair who uses the term on Zulip — asked for, and the resolution
already records the WG's intent to do this. It is a low-risk editorial
change with no normative impact, no schema change, and no breaking
implications. Promoting widely-used informal terminology to formal
definitions is consistent with FHIR IG editorial practice and reduces
the kind of drift visible in the Zulip threads above, where
implementers stretch "ceremony" to mean somewhat different things.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than (or in addition to) adding a local IG-only definition,
register "Consent Ceremony" as a defined term that can be reused
across consent-adjacent specifications: define it once in the
Scalable Consent Management IG's Definitions section, and also propose
the same definition (or a pointer to it) be added to the base FHIR
Consent resource's narrative glossary so that other Security WG
artefacts (Consent base resource, Privacy Consent Directive IGs, US
Interstate Interoperability work) can cite a single canonical
definition. Use a `<dfn>`/anchor in the IG so the term can be linked
from other HL7 publications.

#### Justification

The Zulip evidence shows that "ceremony" is used across multiple WGs
(Security and Privacy, implementers, SMART, patient empowerment) with
slightly different shading. Defining the term only inside one IG
solves the immediate readability problem but leaves the cross-spec
ambiguity in place. A shared, citable definition addresses the root
cause and is still inexpensive. Trade-off vs. Disposition A: requires
coordination with the base FHIR Consent maintainers and a slightly
longer publication path, but gives much more leverage.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline on the grounds that the term is already explained in the
narrative and adding a redundant glossary entry adds maintenance
burden without changing normative behaviour. Mark the ticket "Not
Persuasive — Already Addressed."

#### Justification

A formal-looking rationale is that IG glossary entries can themselves
diverge from the narrative they were extracted from, creating two
sources of truth that drift over time. If the narrative explanation is
clear, requiring readers to also consult a glossary entry adds little.
However, this rationale is weak in this specific case because (a) the
narrative explanation is brief and not styled as a definition, and
(b) the term is referenced from multiple places that would benefit
from a stable anchor.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is small, editorial, and already has a Persuasive
resolution from the WG with the proposed wording strategy spelled out.
Disposition A delivers exactly the requested improvement with minimal
cost and no normative risk, and it is consistent with how other FHIR
IGs handle frequently-used domain terms. Disposition B is attractive
in principle and worth pursuing as a follow-up (a separate base-spec
ticket against Consent), but bundling it into this ticket would expand
scope, drag in another WG, and delay a fix that the reporter and the
implementer community on Zulip clearly want now. Disposition C is hard
to defend given the existing Zulip evidence that "ceremony" is being
used inconsistently across the community.
