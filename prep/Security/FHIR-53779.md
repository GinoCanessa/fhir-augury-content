## Summary

FHIR-53779 questions the design of the Scalable Consent Management IG's
`Record Disclosure` operation defined in the
[2026Jan technical page](https://hl7.org/fhir/us/consent-management/2026Jan/technical.html#record-disclosure-operation).
The reporter (Abderrazek Boufahja) asks why the IG introduces a new
custom FHIR `$operation` to record an audit event rather than relying
on the native FHIR REST interaction — a plain `POST` to the
`AuditEvent` endpoint. The implication is that creating a bespoke
operation adds implementation burden and divergence from base FHIR
patterns when the same outcome could be achieved through the standard
RESTful create.

This ticket is part of a closely-related cluster of tickets (all
against the *Scalable Consent Management* spec, resolved together in
the 2026 Jan/Feb cycle) that converged on the same conclusion:
[FHIR-54648](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54648) ("Recording a
disclosure should not require an operation"),
[FHIR-54692](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54692) ("AuditEvent
Search/Read would not require Create") and
[FHIR-55374](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-55374) ("Which system(s)
do disclosures need to be recorded against?") all attack the
operation-vs-REST design from different angles.
[FHIR-54682](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54682) goes further and
argues the disclosure-recording requirement itself is out of scope
because the access-control decision is out of scope.
[FHIR-54649](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54649) and
[FHIR-54650](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54650) raise consequent
problems with the bespoke AuditEvent profile that backs the operation
(mismatched profiling vs. IHE-BALP, conflation of "authorization
decision" and "disclosure" audit messages). The IG editors have
already recorded a Persuasive resolution on FHIR-53779: the
`Record Disclosure` operation will be removed and the IG will use
RESTful create/search of `AuditEvent` (with IHE-BALP profiles) for
both recording and retrieval.

## Related Jira Tickets

### [FHIR-54648: Recording a disclosure should not require an operation](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54648)

Asks the same core question as FHIR-53779 — why a `$operation` is used
to record disclosure rather than simple REST creation of an
`AuditEvent`. Resolved Persuasive on 2026-01-26 with the same
disposition: remove the `RecordDisclosure` operation in favor of
RESTful creation/retrieval, and update the section to indicate how
audit events are expected to be recorded and retrieved.

Reporter: John Moehrke. Work Group: Community-Based Care and Privacy
[deprecated]. Spec: Scalable Consent Management.

### [FHIR-54692: AuditEvent Search/Read would not require Create](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54692)

Argues that, like US-Core for Observation, the IG only needs to
constrain how `AuditEvent` is **searched and read** (e.g., via
IHE-BALP / ITI-81), not how it is created — many systems hold audit
data in non-FHIR stores. Resolved Persuasive on 2026-01-26: remove the
`Record Disclosure` operation and rewrite the section to discuss FAST
Audit Events being searchable, with the existing profile defining the
data to be communicated.

Reporter: John Moehrke.

### [FHIR-54682: If access control decision is not in-scope, then neither can disclosure audit](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54682)

Targets §OP12 (and by extension §OP8), which makes the Record
Disclosure Operation a SHALL whenever a system accesses a Consent
instance. Argues this can't be a requirement because the access
control decision and enforcement are out of scope for the IG.
Resolved Persuasive on 2026-02-20: split the Consent Decision System
into an out-of-scope decision-making/recording portion and an
in-scope query portion.

Reporter: John Moehrke. Commenters: david.pyke, john_moehrke.

### [FHIR-54649: AuditEvent profiling](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54649)

Calls out an inconsistency: cases 1 and 2 reference IHE-BALP without
profiling, but case 3 defines an operation **and** a custom IG-level
profile of `AuditEvent`. Recommends not profiling case 3. Resolved
Persuasive on 2026-04-16, explicitly noting this inconsistency is
resolved as a *direct consequence* of removing the Record Disclosure
operation tracked in FHIR-54692, FHIR-54648, FHIR-55374, and
**FHIR-53779**. The custom case-3 profile is removed with the
operation; remaining cases use IHE-BALP profiles.

Reporter: John Moehrke. Work Group: Security.

### [FHIR-55374: Which system(s) do disclosures need to be recorded against?](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-55374)

Questions where, in a multi-system Consent propagation topology, the
disclosing system is required to record disclosures. Resolved
Persuasive with Modification on 2026-01-26: remove the Record
Disclosure operation and rely on RESTful actions for create/search of
audit events; rewrite the section accordingly.

Reporter: Joseph M. Lamy. Work Group: Security.

### [FHIR-54650: Disclosure is not the same as Authorized Decision](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54650)

Distinguishes an *authorization decision* (consulting Consent to
decide whether sharing is allowed) from a *disclosure* (the actual
sharing). Recommends using the matching IHE-BALP profiles
(Consent Authorized Decision Audit Message vs. Privacy Disclosure
Audit Message). Resolved Persuasive on 2026-04-16 — both event types
will be properly distinguished and mapped to their BALP profiles.

Reporter: John Moehrke. Work Group: Security.

### [FHIR-53757: Meaning of Success](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53757)

Closely related downstream concern: clarifies what `AuditEvent.outcome`
means for consent-related events. Resolved Persuasive: success
indicates the audited operation completed without error, not whether
data was shared or withheld; permit/deny outcomes belong in
`outcomeDesc` or BALP-defined elements.

Reporter: Craig Newman. Work Group: Security.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Remove the `OperationDefinition-record-disclosure` from the *Scalable
Consent Management* IG entirely. Replace the
"Record Disclosure Operation" section in `technical.html` with a
description that mandates RESTful creation of `AuditEvent` resources
(`POST [base]/AuditEvent`) by the disclosing system and search/read
(`GET [base]/AuditEvent?...`) for retrieval. Anchor the data
expectations on the existing IHE-BALP profiles — specifically the
**Privacy Disclosure Audit Message** for actual disclosures and the
**Consent Authorized Decision Audit Message** for consent-decision
events (per FHIR-54650) — and remove the bespoke IG-level case-3
AuditEvent profile flagged in FHIR-54649. Update §OP8 / §OP12
conformance language so the SHALLs reference RESTful create/search
behaviour rather than invoking the now-deleted `$record-disclosure`
operation, and align with the in-scope/out-of-scope split agreed in
FHIR-54682 and the multi-system clarification in FHIR-55374.

#### Justification

This matches the resolution already recorded on FHIR-53779
("Persuasive — We will remove the Record Disclosure operation and
just use RESTful means to record and retrieve") and is *consistent*
with the persuasive resolutions agreed on every sibling ticket
(FHIR-54648, FHIR-54692, FHIR-55374, FHIR-54649, FHIR-54650,
FHIR-53757). It also aligns with FHIR's general design principle that
custom `$operation`s are reserved for behaviour that cannot be
expressed as a RESTful interaction; recording an `AuditEvent` is an
ordinary `create`, so the operation provides no incremental value
over `POST [base]/AuditEvent` while imposing additional implementation
and conformance burden. Reusing IHE-BALP profiles (which the IG
already references for cases 1 and 2) eliminates the inconsistency
called out in FHIR-54649 and brings the IG into line with the FAST
audit ecosystem.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep `AuditEvent` recording and retrieval RESTful (as in Disposition
A), but **retain a thin, optional `$record-disclosure` operation** as
a convenience wrapper for clients that need server-side enforcement of
a few invariants — e.g., automatically populating `recorded`,
`source.observer`, and the BALP-required `agent`/`entity` slices for
the Privacy Disclosure profile, or echoing the canonical
`AuditEvent.id` back to the caller. Mark the operation as **SHOULD
support / MAY use**, not SHALL, and define it as semantically
equivalent to a profile-validated REST `create`. Implementations that
prefer pure REST remain conformant; servers may offer the operation
to reduce client-side profile-construction errors.

#### Justification

This addresses the underlying complaint (custom operation duplicating
REST) without losing the value the IG editors presumably saw in
having a single, well-typed entry point: server-driven population of
mandatory audit slices is a known pain point with BALP profiles, and
several FHIR IGs (including some HRex / SMART-launch artifacts) keep
optional convenience operations for exactly this reason. However, it
trades against the consensus already reached across the ticket
cluster — FHIR-54648 and FHIR-54692 explicitly argued the operation
should not exist at all, and FHIR-54649's clean-up depends on it
going away. Keeping even an optional operation re-introduces the
case-3 profile/operation pairing that FHIR-54649's resolution removes.
This is therefore a compromise position rather than the path of least
disruption.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline to change the IG: keep the `Record Disclosure` operation as
defined, on the rationale that the operation provides a single
contractual entry-point for cross-organisational disclosure recording
and that the IG's bespoke AuditEvent profile encodes constraints the
base resource does not enforce.

#### Justification

This position is hard to defend in light of the recorded discussion.
FHIR's design guidance favours REST over custom operations when the
behaviour is expressible as a CRUD interaction; `AuditEvent` is
expressly a "record this happened" resource and a `POST` covers its
intended use. The same six sibling tickets (FHIR-54648, FHIR-54692,
FHIR-54682, FHIR-54649, FHIR-54650, FHIR-55374) all reach the
opposite conclusion, and FHIR-54682 further argues that requiring
disclosure recording at all conflicts with the IG's stated
out-of-scope boundary on access-control enforcement. Declining
FHIR-53779 in isolation would also leave FHIR-54649's persuasive
resolution incoherent, since that resolution is *predicated* on the
operation being removed. Decline is included for completeness but
should not be selected.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The persuasive resolution is already on the ticket and is the only
disposition that is internally consistent with the rest of the
Scalable Consent Management ballot cluster. Every closely-related
ticket (FHIR-54648, FHIR-54692, FHIR-55374, FHIR-54682, FHIR-54650,
FHIR-54649) has been resolved persuasive in a way that *requires* the
Record Disclosure operation and its case-3 AuditEvent profile to go
away; selecting Disposition B or C would force the work group to
unwind those resolutions. Disposition A also has the cleanest impact:
it deletes the `OperationDefinition-record-disclosure` artifact,
rewrites a single technical section to describe RESTful create/search
of `AuditEvent` against IHE-BALP's Privacy Disclosure Audit Message
profile, and updates §OP8/§OP12 conformance language — all changes
the editors have already committed to in writing. Recommend the work
group ratify Disposition A and confirm the editors should land it
together with the changes from FHIR-54649 and FHIR-54650 to keep the
AuditEvent story coherent.
