## Summary

The reporter notes a typographical error in the FHIR Data Segmentation for
Privacy (DS4P) Implementation Guide. A sentence referencing the ONC
"Certified HIT Product List" abbreviates the program name as **"CHLP"**,
but the correct ONC abbreviation is **"CHPL"** (matching the linked URL
`chpl.healthit.gov`). The fix is a single-character transposition in the
narrative prose; the hyperlink target itself is already correct. No
cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip discussions, or GitHub items are
associated with this ticket in the indexed sources.

## Details

**Description:**

> The sentence: "According to the ONC [Certified HIT Product List **(CHLP**)](https://chpl.healthit.gov/#/search), 64 EHR Products are certified to meet segmentation at the Header level,"
>
> Should be: "According to the ONC [Certified HIT Product List **(CHPL)**](https://chpl.healthit.gov/#/search), 64 EHR Products are certified to meet segmentation at the Header level,"

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the typo correction in the FHIR Data Segmentation for Privacy IG
narrative: change the parenthetical abbreviation "(CHLP)" to "(CHPL)" in
the sentence beginning "According to the ONC Certified HIT Product
List…" The hyperlink target (`https://chpl.healthit.gov/#/search`) is
already correct and requires no change. This is a single-character,
narrative-only edit to the page that introduces the EHR certification
statistic for header-level segmentation.

#### Justification

The reporter is unambiguously correct: ONC's program is the **Certified
Health IT Product List (CHPL)**, as evidenced by the very URL the
sentence links to. "CHLP" is not an ONC acronym and risks confusing
implementers searching for the certification list. The change is a
non-substantive editorial correction with zero normative impact, no
schema or terminology effect, and no backward-compatibility concern,
which aligns precisely with the "Technical Correction" issue type
already assigned to the ticket.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the spelling fix (CHLP → CHPL) **and**, while the page is being
touched, expand the abbreviation on first use to read "ONC Certified
Health IT Product List (CHPL)" and verify that the surrounding
certification statistic ("64 EHR Products … certified to meet
segmentation at the Header level") is still current, updating or dating
it (e.g., "as of <year>") if it has drifted. If the statistic is stale
or unverifiable, replace it with a link to the live CHPL search rather
than a hard-coded count.

#### Justification

The same editorial pass that fixes the typo can prevent a second class
of defect — the "H" in CHPL stands for **Health IT**, not just "HIT", and
the underlying count of certified products almost certainly no longer
matches a 2022-era figure. Bundling these tweaks costs no extra ballot
cycles and reduces the chance the page is reopened later for the same
paragraph. The trade-off is a slightly larger diff than what was
literally requested, which the work group may prefer to keep narrowly
scoped.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Not Persuasive / No Change** on the grounds that
the DS4P IG (in the FHIR specification namespace) is not actively being
re-published and the typo is in surrounding narrative that does not
affect conformance, schemas, terminology, examples, or the working
hyperlink to chpl.healthit.gov.

#### Justification

This is the weakest of the three options and is offered only for
completeness. Declining a clearly correct editorial fix — especially one
filed under "Technical Correction" with a one-character delta — is hard
to defend publicly, and the reporter took the time to file an actionable
ticket with the exact before/after text. The only scenario in which
declining is reasonable is if the work group has formally frozen the
artifact and routes all narrative fixes to a successor IG; absent that,
declining costs goodwill for no editorial benefit.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

This is a textbook Technical Correction: the reporter supplied the
incorrect text, the corrected text, and evidence (the linked URL) that
the correction is right. There is no design debate to have, no
implementer impact to weigh, and no Zulip or GitHub discussion
suggesting a broader concern. Accept the change as requested. Disposition B
is reasonable if the work group is already planning a republish and
wants to bundle minor cleanups, but it should not block the simple fix;
Disposition C should be rejected unless the artifact is formally
deprecated.
