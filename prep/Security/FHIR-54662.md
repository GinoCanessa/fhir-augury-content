## Summary

John Moehrke is asking the Scalable Consent Management (FHIR) IG authors to
strengthen how the guide treats authentication. He acknowledges that
authentication is intentionally out of scope, but argues the guide should
explicitly call out that authentication is a *required precondition* for any
of the operations the IG enables — not merely an unrelated topic.

He also raises a second, broader question: in a typical FHIR / OAuth
deployment, "authentication" identifies both a user **and** the application
acting on their behalf (e.g., an oncology research app, an EHR, a workers'
compensation app). He asks whether the IG's notion of "agent" is intended to
cover apps as well as users, or whether agents are constrained to users only —
because Consent records may need to authorize an app independently of the
user behind it.

A closely related companion ticket from the same reporter, [FHIR-54681](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54681)
("IHE-PCF"), asks the IG to point at IHE-PCF as the OAuth-based enforcement
profile, noting that IHE-PCF is explicitly designed to be compatible with
HL7 SMART App Launch. The Security WG has already drafted a "Persuasive"
resolution for FHIR-54662 that adds a scope/definitions note about
authentication being a required precondition, and explicitly defers the
OAuth / SMART App Launch wording to FHIR-54681.

## Details

**Description:**

> Authentication is indeed out-of-scope. but it is possibly better to indicate that it is a required predicate for everything enabled by the IG.
>
> Also, in an OAuth environment, which is common with FHIR, Authentication is not just about the user, but also the app (tools) they are using. It is potentially possible that a Consent is authorizing an App regardless of the User (the ABC Oncology Research App), or the St.Mary's EHR, or Workers Compensation App at CMS.
>
> Or you constraining agents to be only users?

**Resolution (already drafted by WG, "Persuasive"):**

> We will add a note in the scope/definitions section indicating that while authentication is out of scope for this IG, it is a required precondition for implementing the operations defined here — systems cannot exchange consents securely without an authentication layer. Note: the OAuth-specific considerations (including SMART App Launch compatibility) are being addressed as part of FHIR-54681.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the WG's already-drafted "Persuasive" resolution verbatim:

1. In the **Scope / Definitions** section of the Scalable Consent
   Management (FHIR) IG, add a clearly-flagged note that authentication
   is out of scope for this IG **but is a required precondition** for any
   of the operations the IG defines — i.e., conformant systems cannot
   exchange consents securely without an authentication layer in place.
2. Cross-link that note to FHIR-54681's resolution, where the
   OAuth / SMART App Launch / IHE-PCF wording is being added, so that a
   reader landing on the authentication note immediately sees the
   concrete OAuth-based enforcement story.
3. Do **not** redefine "agent" in this ticket; defer the user-vs-app
   question to a follow-up if the WG decides it needs IG-level wording
   beyond what FHIR-54681's IHE-PCF reference already implies.

#### Justification

This is the minimum change that directly answers the reporter's first
concern (authentication is a required predicate, not an unrelated topic)
without expanding scope mid-cycle. It is consistent with how other FHIR
IGs that lean on SMART App Launch handle the "auth is upstream" framing,
and it keeps the OAuth-and-app-identity material concentrated in
FHIR-54681 where it has its own resolution path. The reporter is the same
person on both tickets, so a coordinated split between the two is
unlikely to surprise him.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the scope/definitions note from Disposition A, **and additionally**
add a short subsection (or a glossary entry) that explicitly answers the
"are agents only users?" question. Concretely:

- Define "agent" in the IG glossary as covering **both** end users and
  the applications acting on their behalf (e.g., EHRs, research apps,
  payer apps), aligned with how SMART App Launch and OAuth treat
  client identity.
- Add a non-normative example showing a Consent that authorizes an
  application identity independent of the end user (e.g., "the ABC
  Oncology Research App may access X data on behalf of any patient who
  has granted this consent"), mirroring the scenarios the reporter
  named.
- Reference IHE-PCF (via FHIR-54681) for the enforcement mechanics.

#### Justification

Disposition A leaves the reporter's second, more substantive question
("Or you constraining agents to be only users?") unanswered, and that
question has direct downstream impact on Consent profiling: if apps are
first-class agents, profiles and example bundles need to demonstrate
app-as-actor patterns. Pinning the answer down now in the IG glossary is
cheaper than discovering during connectathon that implementers diverge
on whether `Consent.provision.actor` may legitimately be a non-person
agent. The cost is modest (a glossary entry plus one example) and the
clarification reinforces, rather than conflicts with, the FHIR-54681
work.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket without changing the IG. Rationale: authentication
already carries the standard "out of scope" disclaimer, FHIR-54681 is
adding the IHE-PCF / OAuth / SMART App Launch reference that covers the
enforcement story, and the user-vs-app question is more a property of
the chosen enforcement profile (IHE-PCF, SMART App Launch) than of this
IG itself.

#### Justification

The reporter's concrete asks are already addressed elsewhere:
authentication is consistently flagged as out of scope across HL7 IGs,
and OAuth/SMART semantics for "who is the client" live in those
specifications, not in a consent-management IG. Adding more text risks
duplicating — and eventually drifting from — normative wording owned by
SMART App Launch and IHE-PCF.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The WG's drafted "Persuasive" resolution (Disposition A) is correct as
far as it goes, but it only answers half of what FHIR-54662 actually
asks. The reporter's second paragraph — about app identity vs. user
identity in an OAuth world, and the explicit question "Or you
constraining agents to be only users?" — is a substantive modeling
question that affects how implementers will read `Consent` provisions
and write profiles. Punting that entirely to FHIR-54681 (which is about
pointing at IHE-PCF, not about the IG's own definition of "agent") will
likely round-trip back as a new ticket from the same reporter.

Disposition B keeps everything in Disposition A, defers the OAuth
mechanics to FHIR-54681 as planned, and adds a small, scoped glossary
clarification plus one example to make the IG's stance on "agent =
user and/or app" explicit. That gives the WG a clean answer to both
halves of the ticket in a single editorial pass and reduces the chance
of an interoperability surprise at the next connectathon.

Disposition C is not recommended — the reporter raised a real gap, and
the existing "authentication is out of scope" sentence is exactly the
wording he is pushing back on.
