## Summary

The reporter (John Moehrke) flags conformance statement **§OP10** of the
Scalable Consent Management IG as oddly worded. §OP10 currently reads:
"Systems **MAY** return an OperationOutcome with a success to be
consistent." Two concerns are raised:

1. Why is the requirement only **MAY** (and what does "to be consistent"
   refer to)?
2. Why does the statement only address the **success** case, with no
   parallel guidance for failure / partial-success responses?

The ticket sits inside the broader cleanup of §OP10 / §OP11 conformance
language. Related ticket
[FHIR-53775](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53775) (Abderrazek
Boufahja, also Persuasive) raised that "systems" in §OP10 / §OP11 was
ambiguous, and that work group resolved to introduce explicit
**Consent Client** and **Consent Server** actor terms. The resolution
recorded on this ticket explicitly hooks into FHIR-53775: the §OP10
wording will be cleaned up by removing "to be consistent" and replacing
it with concrete language describing **when** a system may return a
success OperationOutcome — namely when it wishes to convey additional
structured information alongside a successful **File Consent** or
**Revoke Consent** operation — and the wording will be aligned with the
Consent Client / Consent Server actor terms from FHIR-53775.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the resolution as drafted. Specifically, in
`technical.html` §OP10 (Conformance Statement for the **File Consent**
and **Revoke Consent** operations of the Scalable Consent Management
IG):

1. **Remove** the trailing phrase "to be consistent."
2. **Rewrite** the §OP10 statement so that it reads, in substance:
   > "A **Consent Server** **MAY** return an `OperationOutcome` resource
   > in the response body of a successful **File Consent** or
   > **Revoke Consent** operation when it needs to convey additional
   > structured information (e.g., warnings, informational issues,
   > version notes) alongside the success status."
3. **Replace** the bare term "Systems" with the **Consent Server** actor
   defined in FHIR-53775 (and add a parallel **Consent Client** behavior
   note if applicable — i.e., that a Consent Client **SHOULD** be
   prepared to process an OperationOutcome on a 2xx response).
4. Keep the conformance verb at **MAY** (the operation succeeds with or
   without the OperationOutcome; carrying one is purely informational).
5. Leave failure-case OperationOutcome guidance to the existing
   FHIR-core HTTP/operation rules unless the workgroup decides to add a
   companion §OP10b for non-success paths (see Disposition B).

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter asked for and what the workgroup has
already agreed to in the Persuasive resolution. The change:

- Keeps the cardinality at **MAY**, which matches base FHIR rules:
  servers are never required to return an OperationOutcome on success
  (see FHIR R5 §RESTful API and the operations framework), and forcing
  it would be a breaking change for existing implementers.
- Replaces the unexplained "to be consistent" with a concrete,
  testable condition (carrying additional structured information),
  removing the ambiguity Moehrke flagged.
- Aligns with FHIR-53775 by naming the **Consent Server** actor, which
  is the resolution the workgroup has already committed to.

The change is editorial in spirit — it tightens an existing MAY
statement — so it is low-risk and unlikely to invalidate any
implementer's current behavior.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the wording cleanup from Disposition A **and** broaden §OP10 to
explicitly cover the failure / partial-success path that the reporter's
second question implies. Concretely:

1. Apply all changes from Disposition A.
2. Add a companion conformance statement (e.g., **§OP10b**) along the
   lines of:
   > "On a failed or partially-failed **File Consent** or
   > **Revoke Consent** operation, a **Consent Server** **SHALL** return
   > an `OperationOutcome` describing the error(s), and a
   > **Consent Client** **SHALL** be able to process it."
3. Cross-link both §OP10 and §OP10b from the operation definitions for
   File Consent and Revoke Consent so the success/failure pairing is
   visible in one place.

#### Justification

The reporter's question — "why is it only about success?" — points at a
real asymmetry. The IG documents the success-with-OperationOutcome
behavior (MAY) but is silent on the failure case, leaving Consent
Clients uncertain whether they can rely on a structured error body.
Adding a SHALL on the error path:

- Is consistent with general FHIR practice (operations that fail are
  expected to return an OperationOutcome with `severity = error`).
- Improves interoperability for clients that want to surface error
  reasons (e.g., consent conflicts, missing references) to end users.
- Has a slightly higher implementation burden than Disposition A (it
  raises a SHALL on Consent Servers), but most conformant servers
  already do this.

Trade-off vs. A: B is more invasive and adds a new conformance bar that
existing implementers may not have explicitly tested for, so it carries
some breaking-change risk for the current ballot cycle.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Reject the change and leave §OP10 as-is, on the grounds that:

1. The base FHIR specification already covers when an
   OperationOutcome may accompany a 2xx response, and §OP10 is just a
   reminder.
2. The "to be consistent" phrasing, while inelegant, does not change
   conformance behavior (still MAY).
3. Editorial-only changes between published versions of an IG should be
   batched and absorbed into a future ballot rather than reopened
   ad-hoc.

#### Justification

A defensible "no-change" rationale would lean on the fact that §OP10
imposes no new normative obligation and that the underlying FHIR rules
for OperationOutcome on success are already complete. Declining keeps
the IG text stable and avoids touching conformance numbering during the
current cycle.

This disposition is weak in this case because the workgroup has
already marked the ticket **Persuasive** and has committed to the
FHIR-53775 actor-terminology rewrite that touches the same paragraph
anyway — declining now would mean the §OP10 paragraph still gets
edited (for actor terms) but with the unhelpful "to be consistent"
phrase intentionally left in. That is a worse outcome than just
fixing both at once.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A matches the workgroup's existing Persuasive resolution
verbatim and is the lowest-risk path: it removes a genuinely confusing
phrase, replaces it with a concrete reason a server might attach an
OperationOutcome to a successful response, and folds in the
**Consent Server** actor terminology that FHIR-53775 has already
committed the IG to using. Keeping the conformance verb at **MAY**
preserves backward compatibility with implementers who do not return an
OperationOutcome on success today.

Disposition B is attractive because it answers the reporter's second,
implicit question (the missing failure-path guidance), and the
workgroup may want to consider it as a follow-on ticket. However,
raising a new SHALL on Consent Servers in the same ballot cycle is a
larger behavioral change than this ticket scopes; better to land A now
and open a separate ticket for the failure-path SHALL if desired.

Disposition C is not defensible given that the §OP10 paragraph is
already being edited for FHIR-53775 — declining the wording fix would
leave the IG demonstrably worse than fixing it.
