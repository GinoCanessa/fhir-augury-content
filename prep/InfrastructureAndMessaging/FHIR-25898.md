## Summary

FHIR-25898 asks the Da Vinci Member Attribution (ATR) IG authors to design
in, from the start, a notification mechanism so that providers learn when
their attribution lists change — particularly when new members are
attributed to them. The ticket targets the IG section currently titled
"Notification of changes in Member Attribution Lists" and argues that this
capability is too important to defer.

The ticket has been resolved as **Duplicate**. Per the Resolution
Description it duplicates [FHIR-25915](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-25915)
("Consider notification on file changes"), which in turn was treated as a
duplicate of [FHIR-40128](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-40128)
("Subscription functionality"). FHIR-40128 was disposed
**Persuasive with Modification** and is now **Published**: the IG was updated
to describe how a Consumer can use Subscriptions to be notified of changes
to an Attribution List, with a SHOULD-strength requirement on Producers to
advertise Subscription support in their CapabilityStatement. So the
substantive ask of FHIR-25898 has already been addressed in the published
spec via the Subscriptions backport, and this ticket is being brought to
the Block-Vote-2026-April list purely to confirm the duplicate disposition.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Re-open FHIR-25898 against the current US Da Vinci ATR IG and add a
first-class, normative-strength notification mechanism for attribution
changes — i.e., promote the existing Subscription guidance from a SHOULD
on the Producer's CapabilityStatement to a SHALL, and add explicit
requirements that Producers publish a SubscriptionTopic for "new member
attributed" and "membership change" events. Update the IG section
"Notification of changes in Member Attribution Lists" to specify the
required SubscriptionTopic canonical(s), the required event payload
shape, and Consumer-side handling expectations.

#### Justification

The reporter's stated need — that providers learn when *new* members have
been attributed to them — is operationally important and currently only
optionally supported. The published resolution of FHIR-40128 introduced
the Subscriptions plumbing but kept it at SHOULD strength, which means a
conformant Producer can still ship without notifications, leaving the
original FHIR-25898 use case unmet in practice. Strengthening the
conformance and pinning a SubscriptionTopic would close that gap and is
consistent with how other Da Vinci IGs (e.g., PDex notifications) treat
notification capabilities as core rather than optional.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep FHIR-25898 closed as **Duplicate** of FHIR-40128, but spawn a
**new, narrowly-scoped follow-up ticket** (against US Da Vinci ATR, owned
by Financial Management) to (1) define and publish a canonical
SubscriptionTopic for attribution-list changes, and (2) add an
implementation guidance subsection covering the "new member attributed"
event specifically. Leave the Producer SHOULD as-is for this cycle.
Cross-link the new ticket back to FHIR-25898 and FHIR-40128 so the
provenance is clear.

#### Justification

The substantive request of FHIR-25898 was already addressed by FHIR-40128
and is in a Published version of the IG, so re-opening FHIR-25898 itself
risks confusing the change-history. At the same time, the published
guidance is fairly thin — it describes *that* Subscriptions can be used
but does not nail down *which* SubscriptionTopic or *what* event
filters Consumers should expect, which is the part the reporter
originally cared about. A purpose-built follow-up ticket against the
correct work group (FM, which now owns the Subscription work per
FHIR-25915 / FHIR-40128) is a cleaner vehicle than re-opening a
2020-vintage ATR ticket in InM. Note that the home work group for the
follow-up should be FM, not InM, mirroring Isaac Vetter's 2022 comment on
FHIR-25915.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Confirm the existing **Duplicate** resolution and close FHIR-25898 with
no further action as part of the Block-Vote-2026-April batch. The
Resolution Description already cites FHIR-40128 (Published) as the ticket
where the change was made, and the comment chain (nageshbashyam 2024-02-05,
ekivemark 2026-03-27 PDex review) shows the disposition has been
deliberately reviewed twice. No new substantive request is on the table.

#### Justification

Block votes exist precisely to dispose of tickets like this — older
tickets whose substance has already been handled elsewhere and which need
only a procedural confirmation. The original wording the ticket points at
("Notification of changes in Member Attribution Lists") has been
superseded by the published Subscriptions section delivered under
FHIR-40128. Reopening would burden the work group without delivering new
spec content, and the appropriate forum for any *additional* tightening
(e.g., promoting SHOULD to SHALL) is a fresh ticket rather than this
duplicate.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** C

This ticket has been worked, duplicated through FHIR-25915 to FHIR-40128,
implemented via the Subscriptions backport, and Published. It was already
re-reviewed in the PDex community call on 2026-03-27 and tagged for the
April 2026 block vote. The cleanest action is to confirm the existing
**Duplicate** resolution and close it. If the work group still feels the
published Subscription guidance is too weak (Disposition A) or
under-specified (Disposition B), that conversation belongs on a new
ticket against the current ATR IG owned by Financial Management — not on
a 2020 InM ticket whose substance has already moved on. Recommend C, with
the explicit option to file a follow-up if the workgroup wants to
strengthen the conformance level or pin a SubscriptionTopic.
