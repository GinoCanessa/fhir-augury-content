## Summary

The ticket asks Infrastructure & Messaging to "align the message-header 5ws"
— that is, to reconcile the `MessageHeader` resource with the FHIR FiveWs
(who/what/when/where/why) pattern so that the mappings are either fully
satisfied or have curated, non-"Unknown" exception reasons recorded in
`source/messageheader/messageheader-fivews-mapping-exceptions.xml`. The
request was filed by Tony Julian after he observed `INFORMATION` build
warnings indicating that `MessageHeader` contains differences from the
FiveWs pattern with reason "Unknown". Tony has posted a proposed mapping
on the INM Confluence page
([Message Header five ws](https://confluence.hl7.org/display/INM/Message+Header+five+ws)),
and Lloyd McKenzie's most recent comment (2025-12-01) indicates he has
reviewed and annotated those proposed values and is passing the page
back to Tony for next steps.

There are no other Jira tickets cross-referenced to FHIR-45275, so the
scope is bounded: update the `MessageHeader` ↔ FiveWs mappings (and/or
curate the exception reasons in the messageheader exceptions XML) so the
build no longer reports "Unknown" reasons and the Detailed Analysis tab
on the FiveWs analysis page tells a coherent story.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the alignment proposed by the reporter on the
[Message Header five ws](https://confluence.hl7.org/display/INM/Message+Header+five+ws)
Confluence page, incorporating Lloyd McKenzie's review comments. For
each row of the FiveWs pattern (`who`, `what`, `when[init|done]`,
`where[init|done]`, `why`, plus the source/author/witness slots), do
one of:

1. Add a `<mapping>` entry on the corresponding `MessageHeader` element
   in `source/messageheader/messageheader.xml` with
   `identity="w5"` and the agreed FiveWs path
   (e.g. `MessageHeader.author → w5: who.author`,
   `MessageHeader.responsible → w5: who.witness`,
   `MessageHeader.sender → w5: who.source`,
   `MessageHeader.destination.receiver → w5: who.cause.subject`,
   `MessageHeader.reason → w5: why`,
   `MessageHeader.focus → w5: what`,
   etc., as agreed on the Confluence page); and
2. For any FiveWs slot that genuinely has no MessageHeader counterpart,
   replace the "Unknown" entry in
   `source/messageheader/messageheader-fivews-mapping-exceptions.xml`
   with a curated reason that explains why the slot is not used by
   `MessageHeader` (e.g. "MessageHeader is a transport envelope and
   does not record clinical timing").

After the edits, run a full Core build and confirm both that the
"Resource contains differences from the fivews pattern that have an
'Unknown' reason" INFORMATION goes away and that the Detailed Analysis
tab of `fivews-analysis.html` shows the curated reasons.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the ticket asks for, what the reporter has
prepared, and what Lloyd has already reviewed; the Confluence page is
the working artefact and the workgroup just needs to ratify it. The
FiveWs pattern explicitly contemplates two valid outcomes per element
— a real mapping or a curated exception — and `MessageHeader` is one
of the few core resources still emitting "Unknown" reasons, which is
visible noise on the published `fivews-analysis.html`. Aligning brings
`MessageHeader` into parity with peer infrastructure resources (e.g.
`Bundle`, `OperationOutcome`) that already have either mappings or
curated exceptions, and it closes the loop on the committers thread
that triggered Lloyd's revision of the "Mapping to Patterns" guidance.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than landing the mappings element-by-element on
`MessageHeader`, treat this as a two-step change:

1. **Step 1 (this ticket): exceptions-only.** Replace every "Unknown"
   reason in `messageheader-fivews-mapping-exceptions.xml` with a
   curated, human-readable explanation (drawn from the Confluence
   page), but do **not** add new `<mapping identity="w5">` entries on
   `MessageHeader` itself. This silences the build INFORMATION and
   makes the Detailed Analysis tab readable today.
2. **Step 2 (spin off a follow-up ticket):** for the small number of
   `MessageHeader` elements where the Confluence proposal asserts a
   real FiveWs correspondence, raise a separate Change Request that
   adds the `w5` mappings to the resource definition, lets that
   proposal go through normal element-level review, and (if accepted)
   re-syncs the exceptions file.

#### Justification

Adding `<mapping identity="w5">` rows is technically a normative-ish
change to the resource definition: implementers who consume the
StructureDefinition mappings will see new entries, and any
forward-derivation (R5 → R6) will need to carry them. By contrast,
curating the exception reasons is a documentation-only edit to a
build-time exceptions file with no downstream conformance impact. The
committers thread also surfaced a real concern — reason edits are
"rather flaky" and concurrent PRs can overwrite them — so getting the
exceptions cleaned up on its own merit (and exercising the workflow)
de-risks the larger mapping change. Splitting the work also lets INM
publish the exception cleanup quickly while the mapping debate (which
is the part that needs cross-WG eyes, especially Patient Care for the
"why/who" semantics) plays out at its own pace.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-45275 as **Persuasive with Modification → not now / out of
scope for FHIR Core**, on the grounds that `MessageHeader` is a
transport envelope rather than a clinical/administrative event record,
and forcing it into the FiveWs (who/what/when/where/why) pattern
distorts both the resource and the pattern. Instead, keep the existing
exceptions file with a single curated reason at the resource level
("MessageHeader is a transport envelope; FiveWs is not applicable")
and leave the per-element entries in their current state.

#### Justification

The FiveWs pattern was designed for resources that record
clinical/administrative events (encounters, observations, procedures,
requests). `MessageHeader` is a messaging-infrastructure construct
that wraps the actual events conveyed in `MessageHeader.focus`; its
own elements describe the message, not the event. Forcing FiveWs
mappings risks misleading implementers — for example, mapping
`MessageHeader.sender` to `w5: who.source` conflates the system that
sent the *message* with the agent that performed the *event*, which
are routinely different (e.g. an interface engine vs. the ordering
clinician). A blanket "not applicable" exception is honest, removes
the build noise, and avoids encoding a mapping that downstream tooling
might over-interpret.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition A is the most direct response to what the ticket asks for,
and if the workgroup is comfortable with the mappings as already
reviewed by Lloyd on the Confluence page, it is fine to take it. The
reason to prefer Disposition B is operational risk management: (1) the
committers thread documented that edits to the exceptions XML can be
silently overwritten by concurrent PRs, so the cleanup deserves its
own focused PR rather than being bundled with normative mapping
additions; (2) adding `w5` mappings to a published resource is a
StructureDefinition change that some implementers and downstream IGs
do consume, and it benefits from being raised as a discrete ticket
that can attract review from Patient Care and the FiveWs custodians
rather than landing inside an INM cleanup. Disposition C is hard to
defend given that the reporter and Lloyd have already done the
mapping work and several `MessageHeader` elements (notably
`author`, `responsible`, `sender`, `reason`, `focus`) have plausible
FiveWs correspondences; declining outright would also leave the
"Unknown" build INFORMATION in place, which is the concrete annoyance
that started this thread. Recommend B: land the curated exceptions
now under FHIR-45275, and spin off a follow-up Change Request for the
new `w5` mappings.
