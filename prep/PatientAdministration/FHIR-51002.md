## Summary

The reporter argues that the Identity Matching IG's IDIAL1.5 level should not
be described as capable of establishing a "unique human identity." IDIAL1.5
relies on basic demographic evidence (first name, last name, date of birth,
home address, plus sources such as MRNs and insurance cards), which the
reporter contends is insufficient to uniquely distinguish individuals because
these attributes are commonly shared (twins, similar names, reused addresses)
and the cited sources lack real-time authoritative validation. Without
stronger resolution logic such as authoritative database matching or
biometric/cryptographic binding, the reporter says the level allows the same
person to create multiple, unverifiable digital identities with overlapping
attributes — directly contradicting the goal of uniqueness. The reporter
recommends removing language that frames IDIAL1.5 as the lowest assurance
level needed to establish unique human identity.

The work group (Julie Maas) responded that IDIAL1.5/1.6 are deliberately low
and labelled as only useful for true business-to-business matching (where the
data requester is a HIPAA Covered Entity), with IDIAL1.8 or higher required
for consumer access, Digital Identifier assignment, or any case where data is
returned to a consumer. Editorial updates were made during the ballot cycle
to qualify the IDIAL1.5 description, and the related technical-correction
ticket [FHIR-51016](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51016) (Persuasive,
applied 2025-05-31) removed an erroneous reference to IDIAL1.5 from §4.2
Digital Identity, which is likely the section that motivated this concern.
The reporter ultimately agreed with the work group's position on 2025-06-18
and the ticket was retracted on 2025-07-23.

## Details

**Description:**

The current IDIAL1.5 guidance claims to support identity verification that
can establish a unique human identity that exists in the real world; however,
this level does not provide a reliable and unique method for identifying
individuals in a way that would guarantee uniqueness. In its current form,
the allowed evidence inputs and fallback mechanisms permit individuals to
create **multiple, unverifiable digital identities** with similar or
overlapping attributes — which directly contradicts the goal of ensuring
uniqueness.

IDIAL1.5 allows identity resolution using evidence to verify first name,
last name, date of birth, and home street address; however, this combination
of demographic attributes is not sufficient to uniquely distinguish one
individual from another. These data points — even when correct — are
commonly shared (e.g., among twins, people with similar names, or across
reused addresses), and the sources cited (MRNs, insurance cards) often lack
real-time authoritative validation.

Without stronger resolution logic (e.g., authoritative database match,
biometric or cryptographic binding), multiple records with similar
demographic data can pass this threshold — enabling the same individual to
create duplicate or synthetic identities using variations on legitimate
information.

**Recommendation:** Remove verbiage establishing IDIAL1.5 as the lowest
level identity assurance policy needed to establish unique human identity.

**Resolution Description:** Retracting as per Peter Eivaz's email request on
07/23/2025.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Edit the Identity Matching IG (HL7 FHIR Identity Matching IG, work group
Patient Administration) to delete every passage that frames IDIAL1.5 as a
level capable of establishing a "unique human identity." Concretely:

- In the IDIAL value-set/concept description for IDIAL1.5 (currently echoing
  the NIST SP 800-63-2 LoA-2 wording about "unique identity that exists in
  the real world"), strip the "unique human identity" phrasing and replace
  it with language that scopes IDIAL1.5 strictly to "minimum bar for
  business-to-business matches where the requester is a HIPAA Covered
  Entity" and explicitly states it is not sufficient on its own to assert
  individual uniqueness.
- Audit the Match Requirements (§3.2), Guidance on Identity Assurance, and
  Digital Identity (§4.2) pages and remove or qualify any remaining
  references that imply IDIAL1.5 alone yields a uniquely identified
  individual.

#### Justification

This directly satisfies the reporter's stated concern that the wording
overstates what IDIAL1.5 can guarantee. It is consistent with the work
group's own clarification that IDIAL1.5 is deliberately low and only
appropriate for B2B/Covered-Entity matches, and with Julie Maas's Zulip
explanation that consumer-facing matches require IDIAL1.8+. Tightening the
language reduces the risk that implementers misread IDIAL1.5 as a
sufficient assurance level for downstream identity decisions.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Treat the issue as already addressed by the technical correction in
[FHIR-51016](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51016) (which removed the
problematic IDIAL1.5 reference from Digital Identity §4.2) and resolve
FHIR-51002 as **Persuasive with Modification**. Add a small editorial pass
to the IDIAL1.5 concept description that explicitly states the level is the
*minimum threshold for B2B / HIPAA Covered-Entity matches* and is *not, on
its own, an assertion of unique human identity*, but stop short of removing
the underlying NIST-derived definition. Cross-link IDIAL1.5 to §3.2 Match
Requirements and to the Guidance tab so readers always see the contextual
qualifications.

#### Justification

The reporter's most concrete grievance — the §4.2 wording — has already
been fixed under FHIR-51016, and the reporter agreed on 2025-06-18 that
labelling IDIAL1.5 as a B2B-only bucket addresses the practical concern.
The IDIAL1.5 definition derives from NIST SP 800-63-2 LoA-2 and is
referenced by external profiles and partners; deleting the historical
phrasing wholesale (Disposition A) risks breaking traceability. A
clarifying qualifier plus stronger cross-linking preserves NIST alignment
while preventing the misreading the reporter flagged, and it matches the
work group's stated editorial intent during the ballot cycle.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve FHIR-51002 as **Retracted** with no further IG change, on the basis
that (a) the reporter formally withdrew the request on 2025-07-23 after
agreeing with the work group's explanation on 2025-06-18, and (b) the only
concrete mis-statement identified during discussion (the §4.2 reference)
was already corrected under the Persuasive technical-correction ticket
[FHIR-51016](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51016). Record a short
disposition note pointing future readers to FHIR-51016 and to the Match
Requirements §3.2 / Guidance pages that scope IDIAL1.5 to B2B use.

#### Justification

The Jira record already shows Resolution = Retracted with an explicit
retraction email from the reporter, and Peter Eivaz's June 18 comment
expressly says he is "ok to close." Reopening editorial work after the
reporter has withdrawn would expand scope without a remaining advocate. The
Patient Administration work group has documented its intent that IDIAL1.5
is a deliberately low B2B threshold, and the misleading §4.2 sentence was
already fixed. Declining preserves the NIST SP 800-63-2 LoA-2 lineage and
avoids re-litigating ballot-cycle consensus that IDIAL1.5/1.6 are useful
labels for low-assurance B2B matches.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** C

The ticket has already been retracted by the reporter, and the substantive
defect he was reacting to was corrected under FHIR-51016 during the same
review cycle. Peter Eivaz explicitly agreed on 2025-06-18 that the B2B
framing answers his concern, and Julie Maas has documented the
ballot-cycle rationale for keeping the IDIAL1.5/1.6 labels. Re-opening
editorial work (Disposition A) would re-litigate that consensus without an
active proponent and risks fracturing alignment with NIST SP 800-63-2
LoA-2, while Disposition B's clarifying pass — though defensible — is
already largely covered by §3.2 Match Requirements, the Guidance tab, and
the FHIR-51016 fix. The cleanest action is to formally close as Retracted
with a disposition note that points future readers to FHIR-51016 and the
B2B scoping language in §3.2; if the work group later observes that the
IDIAL1.5 concept description still reads too strongly in isolation, that
can be picked up as a fresh editorial ticket rather than carried on a
retracted one.
