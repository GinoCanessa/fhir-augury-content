## Summary

Mohammad Jafari (Security WG perspective) is asking the US National
Directory (NDH) IG to deprecate the `restrictFhirPath` extension defined
on the `ndh-Restriction` profile of `Consent`. He argues the use case —
inline, partial security labeling driven by a FHIRPath expression — is
already addressed in a more general way by the **Data Segmentation for
Privacy (DS4P)** security-label IG, and that NDH is not the appropriate
place to define security-labeling guidance. His proposed direction is
to remove (deprecate) `restrictFhirPath` from NDH and instead reference
the DS4P extensions (e.g.,
`extension-sec-label-related-artifact`) for partial inline labeling.

PA discussed the ticket at the WGM on 29 Jan 2024. Brian Postlethwaite
noted that DS4P, on a quick review, does not capture the level of
identity / detail about the restriction that NDH requires, but that PA
would refer the question back to Security to confirm. John Moehrke
reinforced the broader Security position that data should not point at
policy — instead, policy artifacts (Consent, Permission, etc.) should
point at the data — which is the architectural rationale for preferring
DS4P-style labels over an NDH-local FHIRPath restriction extension.

The ticket has no explicit Jira links, no cross-references in the
Augury index, and no directly-related Zulip threads, so the conversation
captured on the ticket itself is the entire decision record at this point.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Mark `Consent.extension:restrictFhirPath` (defined on the
`ndh-Restriction` profile in the US National Directory IG) as
**deprecated** in the next NDH publication, and update the profile's
narrative/guidance pages to point readers at the DS4P security-label
extensions — specifically
`http://hl7.org/fhir/uv/security-label-ds4p/StructureDefinition/extension-sec-label-related-artifact`
— for partial inline labeling that needs to relate restricted data back
to a Consent/Permission instance. The element would remain in the
StructureDefinition for backward compatibility, carry a deprecation
extension and a clear note in `definition` / `comment`, and be removed
in a subsequent major NDH release.

#### Justification

This aligns NDH with the broader HL7 architectural pattern that
security/privacy labeling belongs in DS4P (a Security-WG-owned IG) and
that domain IGs should consume those extensions rather than redefine
them. It also addresses John Moehrke's concern that "data should not
point at policy" by replacing an NDH-local FHIRPath-restriction
mechanism with the standard DS4P approach in which Consent/Permission
artifacts carry the restriction semantics and the data carries a
generic label that points to those artifacts. Deprecating (rather than
removing immediately) gives existing NDH implementers a migration path
without an FMM-breaking jump.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep `restrictFhirPath` in NDH for now, but re-scope and re-document it
as an **NDH-specific complement** to DS4P rather than a replacement.
Concretely:

1. Add a "Security Labeling" guidance page to NDH that establishes DS4P
   inline labels (in particular `sec-label-related-artifact`) as the
   primary mechanism for relating restricted data to a Consent/Permission.
2. Constrain `restrictFhirPath` so it can only be used **alongside** a
   DS4P security label that already identifies the governing
   Consent/Permission — i.e., the FHIRPath expression scopes *which
   parts* of the directory entry the already-identified policy applies
   to, rather than encoding the policy itself.
3. Open a joint PA/Security tracker (or follow-up ticket) to confirm
   whether DS4P needs a new sub-extension to carry the FHIRPath scope,
   so that a future NDH release can fully delegate to DS4P.

#### Justification

This addresses Brian Postlethwaite's concern from the 29 Jan 2024 PA
WGM that DS4P, as it stands, doesn't carry the level of identity/detail
about the restriction that NDH directory entries need. It avoids a
deprecation that would leave current NDH implementers without a
mechanism to express path-level scoping, while still moving the
architecture toward the policy-points-at-data model Security
recommends. It also creates a clean joint work item with Security WG,
which is the right venue for evolving DS4P, rather than NDH unilaterally
designing security-labeling extensions.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Reject the request to deprecate `restrictFhirPath`. Resolve as **Not
Persuasive**, on the rationale that (a) NDH has a concrete need to
express FHIRPath-level scoping of restrictions on directory entries,
(b) PA's review of DS4P at the 29 Jan 2024 WGM concluded DS4P does not
currently carry that level of detail, and (c) deprecating the
extension would remove functionality from NDH with no equivalent
replacement in scope.

#### Justification

This reflects the literal outcome of the PA WGM discussion: until
Security WG confirms that DS4P (or a DS4P extension) can carry the
identity/detail NDH requires, removing `restrictFhirPath` would be a
regression for NDH implementers. Declining now does not preclude
revisiting the question later if DS4P evolves; it simply refuses to
deprecate a working extension before its successor exists.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The reporter and the Security WG comment are pointing at a real
architectural problem — domain IGs should not be defining their own
security-labeling extensions, and "data points at policy" is the wrong
direction. But Brian's WGM analysis is also clearly correct: today's
DS4P does not carry the FHIRPath-level scope that NDH actually uses,
so a clean deprecation (Disposition A) would strand current NDH
implementers, and a flat decline (Disposition C) entrenches an
NDH-local security extension that arguably shouldn't exist in NDH at
all. Disposition B captures the workgroup's actual stance from the 29
Jan 2024 minutes — re-document `restrictFhirPath` as a complement to
DS4P, defer the eventual deprecation until DS4P can carry the scope,
and formally hand the design question to Security WG. That gives PA a
defensible answer to Mohammad now, signals the right long-term
direction, and creates the joint work item that will let a future
ticket actually close this out.
