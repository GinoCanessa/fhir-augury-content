## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the "Development Roadmap" subsection of the Administration
module page (chapter 8.0.5) in the FHIR Core specification so that:

1. Items already delivered for the upcoming Normative milestone are
   re-described in the past tense and linked to the resources/profiles
   that satisfied them (e.g., entries that referred to Patient,
   Practitioner, PractitionerRole, Organization, Location,
   HealthcareService, Encounter, EpisodeOfCare, Account, Appointment,
   AppointmentResponse, Schedule, Slot, etc., should match each
   resource's current Maturity / Normative status).
2. Items that are no longer in scope or have been superseded are
   removed (or moved to a "Completed / Historical" sub-list) so the
   roadmap does not advertise stale plans.
3. A new "Upcoming Work" bullet is added that points to the
   **AdditionalResource Incubator** (Additional Resources Incubator)
   as the path through which further administration-adjacent resources
   may mature into the module in future releases, with a link to the
   Incubator landing page.
4. The page's last-reviewed date / editor's note is bumped so readers
   know the roadmap was reconciled at the Normative milestone.

The change is purely editorial against the module overview page; no
resource definitions, value sets, profiles, search parameters, or
operations are touched, so it carries no normative impact on
implementers.

#### Justification

The ticket's request is narrow, well-scoped, and editorial. A module
overview that still advertises a roadmap from an earlier development
cycle is actively misleading at the moment the module ships as
Normative — readers will use it to judge maturity and future
direction. Refreshing the roadmap and pointing forward to the
Additional Resources Incubator preserves the page's value as a
navigation aid without committing the work group to any new normative
content. This is consistent with how other FHIR modules (e.g.,
Clinical, Diagnostics) maintain their module intro pages alongside
ballot milestones.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than rewriting the existing "Development Roadmap" subsection
in place, restructure chapter 8.0.5 into two distinct subsections:

- **"Module History"** — a short, dated list of what the
  Administration module has delivered through each major release,
  ending with the Normative milestone.
- **"Future Direction"** — a forward-looking section that names the
  Additional Resources Incubator as the principal pipeline for
  candidate administration resources, links to the Incubator's index,
  and (optionally) lists currently-incubating candidates by name with
  a clear "non-normative, subject to change" caveat.

The current free-form roadmap bullets would be retired. The split
makes it explicit which content is a record of past decisions and
which is a forward-looking hint, which is the distinction that
actually matters once the module is Normative.

#### Justification

Once a module is Normative, mixing "what we shipped" and "what we
might do next" in a single roadmap is a frequent source of reader
confusion and Jira tickets — implementers read forward-looking bullets
as commitments. Splitting history from future direction (the pattern
already used informally on several other module intro pages) is more
durable than a rewritten single roadmap and reduces the chance that
the page will go stale again the next cycle. The cost is a slightly
larger editorial change than what the ticket literally asks for.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request as **not actionable in its current form** and ask
the reporter to either (a) submit a concrete proposed text for the
updated roadmap, or (b) re-file once the Administration module's
Normative content set is fully locked. The "Development Roadmap"
section would be left untouched for this ballot cycle, with at most a
one-line editor's note acknowledging that it predates the Normative
milestone and will be revisited after publication.

#### Justification

The ticket points at a real gap (a stale roadmap on a module that is
about to go Normative) but does not propose specific text, specific
removals, or a specific list of incubator items to mention. Without a
concrete proposal, the work group risks shipping a hastily-rewritten
roadmap that itself becomes stale within one cycle. Deferring the
edit and asking for a concrete proposal is defensible if the ballot
window is short and editorial bandwidth is limited, but it does leave
the misleading text in place at the Normative milestone — which is
the harm the reporter is trying to avert.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The change the reporter is asking for is editorial, low-risk, and
clearly the right thing to do at a Normative milestone — a module
overview page that advertises an outdated development roadmap will
mislead implementers and reviewers reading the Normative ballot.
Disposition A delivers exactly that fix and adds the requested pointer
to the Additional Resources Incubator without expanding scope.
Disposition B (split into "History" + "Future Direction") is
attractive and would produce a more durable page, but it is a larger
editorial restructure that should be raised as a follow-up ticket
rather than bundled into a fix the reporter framed as a quick
roadmap refresh. Disposition C (decline pending concrete text) is
hard to defend at the Normative milestone, since the cost of a
stale roadmap on a Normative page is greater than the cost of the
work group drafting the replacement text itself. The work group
should accept the request, draft the updated roadmap and incubator
pointer, and circulate it for a brief review before merging.
