## Summary

Eric Haas reports that the two invariants on `Encounter.participant` —
`enc-1` ("A type must be provided when no explicit actor is specified")
and `enc-2` ("A type cannot be provided for a patient or group
participant") — interact in ways that are confusing to implementers.
Together they imply three distinct cases: (1) actor is Patient/Group →
type is forbidden, (2) actor is omitted → type is required, and
(3) actor is anything else → type is optional. The intent behind these
rules — particularly the asymmetric treatment of Patient/Group — is not
spelled out in the element descriptions, only in the constraints
themselves. The reporter requests that the descriptions of the
participant elements be updated to convey the intent of these
invariants, but explicitly declines to propose specific wording because
he is unsure of the underlying rationale.

Context for the rationale is documented on the comment thread of the
related (already-published) ticket
[FHIR-34100](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-34100) ("Encounter
*cannot* have Group as a participant"), where Brian Postlethwaite
explains that `enc-2` was added precisely because
`Encounter.participant.type` is for *professional responsibilities*
(akin to `Appointment`) and is therefore not appropriate for the
patient side of the encounter. The PA/PC discussion captured on
FHIR-34100 also clarifies that Patient/Group are permitted in
`participant.actor` only because the workgroup chose to fold the
"subject" notion into participation (e.g., to model meetings *about* a
patient vs. *with* a patient), not because they are acting as
performers.

### [FHIR-34100: Encounter *cannot* have Group as a participant](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-34100)

Published change request (resolved *Not Persuasive with Modification*,
2022-01-21) that introduced the present shape of `Encounter.participant`
and is the direct origin of `enc-2`. The resolution explicitly added
the constraint that participants of type `group` or `patient` cannot
carry a `participant.type` (because `type` represents professional
responsibility, mirroring `Appointment`), and also added the
requirement that any Patient/Group appearing in `participant.actor`
must be the subject of the Encounter. This is the authoritative source
of the rationale that FHIR-54316 wants surfaced in the element
descriptions.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the description text of `Encounter.participant`,
`Encounter.participant.type`, and `Encounter.participant.actor` to
make the intent of `enc-1` and `enc-2` explicit, without changing the
invariants themselves. Specifically:

- On `Encounter.participant` (backbone): add a note explaining that
  participation in an Encounter covers two distinct uses — (a)
  recording professional responsibilities of practitioners,
  organizations, and similar actors (which is what `participant.type`
  conveys), and (b) recording the presence of the subject (Patient or
  Group) in the encounter, including their absence from a meeting
  *about* them. Cross-reference the parallel pattern in `Appointment`.
- On `Encounter.participant.type`: state that `type` describes the
  *professional responsibility* of the participant in the encounter
  and is therefore meaningful only for non-subject actors; it must not
  be supplied for `Patient`/`Group` actors (per `enc-2`), and it must
  be supplied when the actor is omitted entirely (per `enc-1`) so the
  participant row still carries a useful semantic.
- On `Encounter.participant.actor`: state that `Patient` or `Group` may
  appear here only to record the (potentially absent) subject's
  presence in the encounter (consistent with the resolution of
  [FHIR-34100](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-34100)).

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter asks for: a documentation-only
clarification of intent. The rationale already exists, formally
captured in the resolution of [FHIR-34100](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-34100)
and reiterated in Brian Postlethwaite's comment on this ticket;
surfacing it in the element descriptions removes the need for
implementers to reverse-engineer the constraints from the invariant
expressions. It is also a non-breaking change (no schema, cardinality,
or value-set effect) and keeps `Encounter.participant` aligned with
the analogous pattern in `Appointment.participant`, satisfying FHIR's
principle of documenting *why* a constraint exists alongside *what* it
enforces.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Instead of (or in addition to) editing prose descriptions, refine the
machine-readable layer so that the constraints are self-explanatory:

1. Rewrite the `human` text of `enc-1` and `enc-2` to embed the
   rationale (e.g., `enc-2`: "participant.type conveys professional
   responsibility and must be omitted when the actor is a Patient or
   Group, who participate as the subject rather than as a
   responsibility holder").
2. Optionally collapse the two invariants into a single, more
   discoverable constraint that names the three cases explicitly
   ("Patient/Group actors must not carry a type; non-subject actors
   may carry a type; participant rows with no actor must carry a
   type").
3. Add a small worked example to the `Encounter` notes page showing
   each of the three cases, including the "meeting about the patient
   without the patient present" use case from the FHIR-34100
   resolution.

#### Justification

Many implementers see invariants only via validators and
StructureDefinition tooling, not by reading the resource page top to
bottom; embedding the intent in the constraint's `human` text makes
the explanation travel with the rule into IG publishers, validator
errors, and downstream profiles. The trade-off versus Disposition A is
that constraint-text edits flow into every consumer of the
StructureDefinition (which is good for reach but does change a
machine-visible artifact, even if the FHIRPath expression is
unchanged), and collapsing two long-standing invariants into one is a
larger editorial change than the reporter strictly asked for.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve as *Not Persuasive*: the existing invariants `enc-1` and
`enc-2`, together with the resolution of
[FHIR-34100](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-34100), already define
the intended behaviour, and reading the constraint expressions
together with the element types is sufficient. No spec change.

#### Justification

Defensible only if the workgroup considers the current text adequate
and prefers not to expand element descriptions. The reporter has not
identified a concrete misuse — only the perception of asymmetry — and
the intent is documented (in the FHIR-34100 resolution) for any
implementer who follows the tracker history. However, this rationale
is weak in practice because implementers should not have to read
historical Jira resolutions to understand a constraint, which is the
core complaint raised by the ticket.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter is explicitly asking for a documentation clarification,
the rationale is already settled (FHIR-34100), and Disposition A is
zero-risk: it changes no invariants, no cardinalities, and no
machine-readable artifacts. It directly answers the complaint by
moving the intent from a tracker comment into the normative element
descriptions where implementers will actually find it. Disposition B
is a reasonable enhancement and could be merged into the same change
if the workgroup wants to invest a little more — sharpening the
`human` text of `enc-1`/`enc-2` is genuinely high-value — but should
not block the lower-risk description edits in A. Disposition C is not
recommended because it leaves the implementer-facing problem
unresolved and effectively asks readers to consult Jira to interpret
a normative constraint.
