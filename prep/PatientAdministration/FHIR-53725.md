## Summary

Corey Spears reports that section **8.1.8 "Patient vs. Person vs. Patient.Link vs. Linkage"** of the Patient resource page in FHIR R6 (`patient.html#linking`) still references the **Linkage** resource and contains an outbound link that now points at the PA Incubator IG (`build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/pc-incubator/StructureDefinition-Linkage.html`). Because the Linkage resource has been removed from FHIR Core R6 and moved to the PA incubator, the requester argues the section should be retitled and the Linkage discussion removed (or rewritten) — leaving guidance on `Patient.link` for linking patients, `Person` for linking individuals across resource types, and the new `Encounter.link` extension for linking encounters. The ticket carries the `AdditionalResources` label and was discussed at the **PA Jan 2026 WGM**, where Brian Postlethwaite logged a comment to verify the intent with FMG and pointed at a Zulip thread (`#implementers > Linking to Additional Resources`) that broadens the question to *all* core-spec references to incubated resources. The current resolution (set during PA review) is **"Not Persuasive with Modification"**, with the resolution note explaining the change is being deferred until the incubator exists and the linking mechanism for incubated content is operational. No related Jira tickets or GitHub items were surfaced by cross-references.

## Details

The section includes the following text (with broken link) to the Linkage resource, but does not apply anymore. It should be removed (and the section retitled).

> 8.1.8 Patient vs. Person vs. Patient.Link vs. Linkage

>
> > The [Linkage](https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/pc-incubator/StructureDefinition-Linkage.html) resource and the Patient.link property conceptually perform similar functions in FHIR, both provide an assertion of linkages between multiple resource instances that are referring to the same underlying individual.
> > When a Patient resource is linked/merged then it needs to have an internal indication that there is another patient resource that should be considered when referencing other records, which is achieved using the patient.link property.
> > Not detecting/checking for a potential linkage could mean that related clinical records are not discovered, potentially impacting patient safety. ## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the FHIR R6 Patient resource page (`patient.html`), edit section **8.1.8 "Patient vs. Person vs. Patient.Link vs. Linkage"** to:

1. **Retitle** the section to "Patient vs. Person vs. Patient.link" (drop "vs. Linkage").
2. **Remove** the entire blockquoted Linkage paragraph, including the broken/external link to `build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/pc-incubator/StructureDefinition-Linkage.html`.
3. **Reframe** the bulleted "should not be used in these cases" list as positive guidance:
   - Linking patient records → use `Patient.link`.
   - Linking individuals across resource types → use `Person`.
   - Linking encounters → use the new `Encounter.link` extension.
4. Repair the trailing fragment ("Additionally, THe ") — either delete it or complete the sentence the reporter began.

No mention of the Linkage resource (or PA incubator) would remain on the Patient page.

#### Justification

This is the most literal reading of the request and produces a page that is internally consistent with R6 — Linkage is no longer a core resource, so a core resource page arguably should not depend on its existence to explain `Patient.link`. It also eliminates the broken-link risk: incubator URLs are unstable and the current `pc-incubator` link may move. The bulleted positive guidance preserves the implementer-facing value of the section (telling readers *what to use*) without coupling it to an external IG.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach (Recommended by current resolution)

#### Proposal

**Keep** section 8.1.8, but rewrite it to align with the FMG/Grahame-Grieve guidance from the Zulip thread:

1. Retitle to something like "Patient vs. Person vs. Patient.link vs. linking to additional/incubated resources".
2. Keep a short comparison paragraph noting that Linkage (now incubated under the PA Incubator IG) and `Patient.link` cover overlapping but distinct use cases, and that the comparison/positioning text proper lives with the incubated resource.
3. Replace the current direct deep-link to a `StructureDefinition` page with the **standard "link to incubated content" mechanism** once it exists (per the resolution note) — a stable pointer to the PA Incubator entry for Linkage, marked as work-in-progress.
4. Retain the positive guidance bullets (`Patient.link` / `Person` / `Encounter.link` extension).
5. Defer the actual edit until the incubator-linking mechanism is in place, so the page is updated **once** rather than churning twice.

This matches the recorded resolution ("Not Persuasive with Modification — will be resolved once the incubator exists and the mechanism for linking to that content is operational").

#### Justification

Grahame's position in `#implementers > Linking to Additional Resources` is explicit: *core may link to incubated resources as work-in-progress, but the comparison text should live where the resource is incubated.* That means Disposition A goes too far — silently deleting the section severs a useful navigational/positioning aid that R5 readers (and implementers comparing R5→R6) will look for. John Moehrke also noted this is a class problem (Security ↔ Permission, etc.); solving it once with a stable cross-IG link mechanism is preferable to one-off deletions per page. The trade-off is that the page remains slightly out of date until the incubator-link mechanism lands, but the existing wording is not actively harmful — it simply needs the broken/raw `build.fhir.org` URL replaced.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Reject the change. Leave section 8.1.8 as-is for the R6 publication, with at most a minor edit to repair the trailing "Additionally, THe " fragment if it appears in the rendered build. Treat any future cleanup as part of the broader "core ↔ incubator linking" work that PA/FMG handle as a class.

#### Justification

The ticket conflates two issues: (a) one specific broken/awkward link, and (b) a structural question about how core references incubated resources. The structural question is being worked at the FMG level (per the Zulip thread and Brian's WGM comment), so making piecemeal Patient-page edits now risks re-work the moment the cross-IG mechanism is decided. The current text is editorially imperfect but not technically wrong, and the reporter's own description trails off mid-sentence — suggesting the request itself is not fully formed. Declining keeps the page stable until the broader policy is resolved.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **B**

Disposition B is what the workgroup has effectively already chosen — the ticket is sitting at "Not Persuasive with Modification" with a resolution note that says exactly this: defer until the incubator-linking mechanism is operational, then resolve. It honours Grahame's Zulip guidance (link to incubated resources is fine; move comparison text to the incubator), it solves the broken-`build.fhir.org`-URL problem by routing through a stable mechanism, and it generalises to the parallel cases John Moehrke flagged (Security/Permission and likely others). Disposition A is tempting because it is concrete and shippable today, but unilaterally deleting positioning text on the Patient page sets a precedent that other pages (Security, etc.) would have to follow, and removes a comparison that R5-aware readers will expect. Disposition C ignores the genuine defect (the broken-style external link); B captures the same "wait for the cross-IG mechanism" patience without abandoning the editorial fix. The workgroup should confirm with FMG (per Brian's comment) and then queue the section-8.1.8 rewrite against whichever incubator-link macro/template lands first.
