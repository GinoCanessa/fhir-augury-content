## Summary

The reporter (Lisa R. Nelson) asks Patient Administration to remove the
`EndpointReference` extension that the National Directory of Healthcare
Providers and Services (NDH) IG has applied to `Practitioner` (the
extension is bound on `NdhPractitioner` as `extension[endpoint] 0..*`,
referencing `NdhEndpoint`). Her position is that core FHIR deliberately
omits a direct `Practitioner → Endpoint` reference because endpoint
information is only meaningful in the context of the role a practitioner
plays for a particular organization — i.e., it must always be reached
via `PractitionerRole.endpoint`. Allowing a contextless
`Practitioner.endpoint` (even via extension) is, in her view, a
patient-safety / information-exchange-safety problem: a directory
client could obtain endpoints for a practitioner without knowing for
which organization or role the endpoint is appropriate, making it
impossible to judge whether sending or receiving information to/from
that endpoint is permissible.

This ticket is a follow-on to [FHIR-51997](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51997)
("Problem with All Resources Relationships 2 Diagram"), which the
reporter filed in August 2025 asking that the core diagram stop showing
a Practitioner→Endpoint relationship. That ticket was resolved
"Persuasive with Modification" on 2025-11-24 with the disposition
"make clear this is the extension: endpoint" — i.e., the diagram was
edited to surface the NDH extension rather than to remove the
relationship — and the change was carried out via
[HL7/fhir-us-ndh#9](https://github.com/HL7/fhir-us-ndh/pull/9). The
reporter argues this resolution missed her underlying point: the
relationship should not exist at all, even as an extension, because the
NDH IG should not provide a mechanism to bypass the safety design of
core FHIR. She requests reconsideration and removal of the extension
from the NDH Practitioner profile.

## Details

**Description:**

There are very important reasons why FHIR creators have purposely not included a
reference from Practitioner to Endpoint. In order for endpoint information to
accurately be related to a Practitioner, you must make the connection in the
context of the role that they are playing. That means you always should do the
query for a Practitioner's endpoint through the PractitionerRole Resource, never
directly through the Practitioner Resource.

Somehow NDH has skirted around this important consideration and allowed for an
extension to be created that circumvents this important issue. If this is
permitted to go forward, the NDH specification could be creating a major issue
for security and privacy, allowing a directory system to offer unsafe information
with respect to information exchange. And at a minimum it would not provide
clear guidance and warnings about why endpoint information should never be
provided in a directory without including a contextual basis for understanding
the identity of the practitioner and the role they are playing at the
organization they are working for when they send or receive information through
that endpoint.

If a system were to make it possible to look up endpoints directly through the
Practitioner Resource, the system response would provide endpoint information
without including the role and organizational context in which the Practitioner
was operating. The system would be dishing up endpoints without including the
necessary information to determine if it is permissible or appropriate to
exchange information with that endpoint. That would be a very dangerous design
for a directory system.

For example, what if an MD was in a directory with a Direct Address because
they worked for an innovative health-IT app developer responsible for a
medication regime assessment app. This organization may be operating as a BA
entity under contract with CE entities. It is totally appropriate for that
doctor to have a Direct address and to be findable in the DirectTrust Aggregated
Directory. The MD may also be a practicing physician treating patients at
Practice XYZ. In the first case, the doctor is fulfilling the role of a
consultant for a non-CE organization. In the second case, the doctor is
fulfilling the role of a physician for a CE organization.

If we permitted NDH to include this proposed endpoint extension, a directory
could publish only the Practitioner's name and MD qualification together with
endpoints — without connecting the endpoint through the PractitionerRole
resource. The endpoint information would be "contextless." A search on
Practitioner could return two endpoints with no information allowing the seeker
to know which endpoint was safe to send what kind of information to and under
what circumstance.

In August 2025, FHIR ticket [FHIR-51997](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51997)
reported: "The All Resources Relationships 2 diagram incorrectly shows
Practitioner Resource as having a reference to Endpoint. It does not.
PractitionerRole references Endpoint, but Practitioner does not. Fix the
Diagram to show and note that Practitioner Resource does not have a
relationship to Endpoint." The reporter notes she should have provided more
rationale; the resolution that was adopted ("keep the relationship and just
mark it as an extension") missed the underlying safety point.

This ticket is being logged to bring attention onto the core intention behind
the earlier change request: **fix the diagram to show and note that
Practitioner Resource does not have a relationship to Endpoint** —
because it never should. The endpoint extension added in the NDH IG
should be removed.

The reporter notes that on 2025-11-24 a persuasive-with-modification
disposition kept the relationship from Practitioner to Endpoint in the
diagram and corrected the diagram by showing the relationship as an
extension. She believes the safety implications were not weighed; the
extension capability of FHIR should not be used to bypass safety
considerations designed into a FHIR resource from its inception.

The NDH specification plays an important role in informing how
directories will be developed going forward. The reporter is concerned
none of us would want to recommend access to contextless endpoint
information, or to suggest that directory systems should be designed to
provide it.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Remove the `EndpointReference` extension from `NdhPractitioner` and
retire the extension definition itself in the NDH IG:

1. In `input/fsh/Base-Profiles.fsh`, delete the
   `EndpointReference named endpoint 0..*` slice from `NdhPractitioner.extension`
   and the corresponding `* extension[endpoint] ^short = "Endpoint Reference"` line.
2. In `input/fsh/Base-Extensions.fsh`, mark `Extension: EndpointReference`
   (`Id: base-ext-endpoint-reference`) as deprecated/retired (or delete it,
   if the WG accepts the small breaking-change risk for extensions that have
   a single intended consumer in this IG).
3. Update `input/pagecontent/resourceRelationship.md` and the
   "All Resources Relationships 2" treatment to remove the
   Practitioner→Endpoint line (extension or otherwise) and add explicit
   guidance: endpoints associated with a practitioner MUST be reached via
   `PractitionerRole.endpoint`.
4. Add a `change-log.md` entry citing FHIR-56338 and FHIR-51997, and a
   section in `conformance.md` warning implementers that
   `Practitioner.extension:endpoint` (`base-ext-endpoint-reference`)
   has been removed and how to migrate (use `PractitionerRole.endpoint`
   to expose the same endpoints in role/organization context).
5. Refresh `Example-Practitioner.fsh` to remove any use of the extension,
   and confirm `Example-PractitionerRole.fsh` continues to demonstrate
   the role-mediated endpoint pattern.

#### Justification

This directly addresses the reporter's safety argument and brings NDH
back into alignment with the deliberate design of the base FHIR
Practitioner resource. Core FHIR omits `Practitioner.endpoint` so that
endpoint discovery is always mediated by `PractitionerRole`, which
carries the organizational and role context an exchange decision
depends on (CE vs. BA, treating physician vs. consultant for a digital
health vendor, etc.). Allowing the extension reintroduces exactly the
contextless lookup pattern core FHIR was designed to prevent, and an
IG-level extension is not the right venue to override a base-spec
safety decision. The data path the reporter wants (`Practitioner →
PractitionerRole → Endpoint`) is already fully supported by
`NdhPractitionerRole` (which references `NdhEndpoint`), so removing the
extension does not lose functionality — it removes a duplicate, less
safe access path.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — Retain the extension but constrain and warn

#### Proposal

Keep `EndpointReference` on `NdhPractitioner` but tighten its
conformance and surrounding guidance so it cannot be used as a
contextless lookup target:

1. In `input/fsh/Base-Extensions.fsh`, add an invariant on
   `EndpointReference` (e.g., `ndh-prac-endpoint-context`) that *requires*
   the same `NdhEndpoint` instance to also be referenced from at least
   one `NdhPractitionerRole.endpoint` whose `practitioner` resolves to
   the containing Practitioner. Mark it as `severity = #error`. Document
   that conformant servers MUST reject `Practitioner` instances that
   carry the extension without a matching `PractitionerRole`.
2. In `NdhPractitioner` (in `input/fsh/Base-Profiles.fsh`), set the
   extension to `MustSupport = false` and add `^short` and `^comment`
   text strongly recommending PractitionerRole-mediated endpoint
   discovery; mark the extension itself as `^status = #deprecated` in the
   extension definition.
3. Update `input/pagecontent/conformance.md` and
   `input/pagecontent/resourceRelationship.md` to add an explicit
   "endpoints in NDH must be discovered through PractitionerRole"
   normative statement, and to position
   `Practitioner.extension:endpoint` only as a denormalised convenience
   pointer that mirrors data already reachable via PractitionerRole.
4. In `Example-Practitioner.fsh`, ensure every example that uses the
   extension also has a matching `Example-PractitionerRole` carrying the
   same endpoint, demonstrating the constraint.

#### Justification

This recognises the dispositioned PR-9 work and avoids a second
breaking change against an extension that some early NDH implementers
may already be using, while addressing the substantive safety concern:
the extension can no longer be the sole carrier of an endpoint, and any
endpoint exposed via the extension must also be reachable through a
PractitionerRole that carries the role/organization context. The
trade-off vs. Disposition A is residual confusion (two access paths to
the same data) and the cost of writing and validating a non-trivial
cross-resource invariant; the upside is backward compatibility and a
documented bridge to the role-mediated pattern Lisa is advocating.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (keep current behaviour)

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-56338 as "Not Persuasive" / "Already addressed by FHIR-51997"
and retain the `EndpointReference` extension on `NdhPractitioner`
unchanged. Optionally add a single sentence to
`input/intro-notes/StructureDefinition-ndh-Practitioner-notes.md`
noting that endpoint discovery is preferentially performed via
`PractitionerRole`.

#### Justification

The substantive change Lisa is asking for was reviewed under
FHIR-51997, dispositioned "Persuasive with Modification" on
2025-11-24 (Block-Vote-1), and applied via
[HL7/fhir-us-ndh#9](https://github.com/HL7/fhir-us-ndh/pull/9). The
WG's expressed view there was that the extension was acceptable as
long as the diagram surfaced it as an extension. Re-opening that
decision absent new technical evidence (e.g., an interoperability
incident, a security advisory, or a formal Security WG objection) sets
a precedent of re-litigating block-vote outcomes and would cost the
NDH IG either a breaking change (Disposition A) or a complex
cross-resource invariant (Disposition B) that current implementers
have not signalled they need.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A (Accept As Requested)

The reporter's argument is not a stylistic preference — it is the same
information-exchange-safety concern that drove core FHIR's deliberate
omission of `Practitioner.endpoint`. The NDH IG is an authoritative
directory specification, and IG-level extensions are not the right tool
to neutralise a safety decision made in the base resource. Disposition C
preserves a real risk (contextless endpoint discovery in directories
modelled on NDH) and leans on procedural finality rather than rebutting
the technical objection. Disposition B mitigates the risk but at the
cost of a bespoke cross-resource invariant that is hard to author in
FSH, hard to validate, and hard for implementers to enforce — and once
authored, it leaves two parallel access paths to the same data, which
is itself a directory-design smell. Disposition A is the cleanest
outcome: the role-mediated path (`Practitioner → PractitionerRole →
NdhEndpoint`) is already fully supported in NDH, so removing the
extension subtracts a hazardous duplicate path without losing
functionality. Patient Administration should be willing to absorb the
small extension-deprecation cost given that the alternative is a
documented safety hole in a normative directory IG.
