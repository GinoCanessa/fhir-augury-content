## Summary

The ticket asks the Patient Administration WG to revisit the Identity
Assurance Level (IAL) migration plan in the *Interoperable Digital
Identity and Patient Matching (IDI)* IG and align it with NIST SP
**800-63-4** if the new revision of the NIST publication is finalised
before STU2 ballot reconciliation closes. If 800-63-4 is not finalised
in time, the reporter is comfortable closing this ticket and tracking
the alignment work in a follow-up ticket against a future STU. The
request is therefore conditional and primarily a synchronisation /
versioning concern between the IG's IAL definitions (currently rooted
in 800-63-3 / 800-63A) and the next NIST revision.

Context from related tickets in the same ballot reconciliation tranche:
[FHIR-48026](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48026) (Persuasive,
Published) replaces colloquial "ID" wording with the NIST-defined term
"identity evidence" in IAL1.2, demonstrating that the WG is already
moving the IG toward NIST-precise vocabulary;
[FHIR-48043](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48043) (Retracted)
questioned whether IAL1.2 has any value distinct from IAL1 given the
"unspecified ID" wording. Together these show that the IAL section is
under active reconciliation in STU2, and FHIR-47825 is the umbrella
"keep-us-aligned-with-NIST" ticket that depends on external NIST
timing.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Wait for NIST SP 800-63-4 to be finalised, then update the
*Interoperable Digital Identity and Patient Matching* IG's IAL
migration narrative and the underlying IAL CodeSystem to reflect the
800-63-4 terminology and level structure. Concretely, this means
revising the IAL migration page in the IDI IG, re-mapping the IG's
local `identity-assurance-level` codes (currently aligned to
800-63-3 / 800-63A and cross-referenced with the SMART Health Cards
[`identity-assurance-level`](https://terminology.smarthealth.cards/CodeSystem-identity-assurance-level.html)
CodeSystem) to the 800-63-4 IAL definitions, and updating any
narrative that cites 800-63-3 to cite 800-63-4 (with a transition
note for implementers still on 800-63-3). Because 800-63-4 was not
finalised in time for STU2, this work is targeted at the next
publication (STU3 / a subsequent STU2 update), consistent with Lynn
Laakso's 2025-12-10 instruction to re-open post-STU2 publication.

#### Justification

The WG has already established a NIST-alignment trajectory for this
IG: [FHIR-48026](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48026) was accepted
as Persuasive specifically to use NIST-defined vocabulary
("identity evidence" from 800-63-3 / 800-63A). Continuing that
trajectory into 800-63-4 is the consistent, principled path and
avoids the IG diverging from the cited normative base. It also
addresses the SMART Health Cards maintainers' question raised by
Julie Maas about keeping the two IAL CodeSystems in sync — a single
800-63-4-aligned authoritative definition is easier to keep in sync
than ad-hoc divergence. The concern raised in
[FHIR-48043](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48043) about the
indistinguishability of IAL1.2 also benefits from the additional
precision 800-63-4 supplies.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than waiting on 800-63-4 publication and changing the IAL
codes themselves, decouple the IDI IG's IAL CodeSystem from any
specific NIST revision: publish the IDI IG's IAL codes as a stable
HL7-owned CodeSystem with explicit `concept.designation` /
mapping entries that point at the corresponding 800-63-3 (today)
*and* 800-63-4 (when finalised) levels via a `ConceptMap`. The IG's
narrative would then describe the mapping rather than embedding
NIST version semantics into the codes themselves. This also answers
the second open question in the SMART Health Cards Zulip thread by
giving HL7 a separately-versioned IAL CodeSystem distinct from the
SMART Health Cards CodeSystem.

#### Justification

NIST 800-63 has a long history of revisions (–1, –2, –3, and now
–4); binding the IG's IAL identifiers to a specific NIST revision
forces a breaking IG release every time NIST publishes. A
ConceptMap-based approach lets the IG track NIST asynchronously
without breaking implementers, which is consistent with FHIR
terminology design principles for evolving external code systems.
The trade-off vs. Disposition A is more terminology infrastructure
up front and a slightly less direct narrative, but materially
better lifecycle behaviour.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-47825 against STU2 with a resolution of *Considered for
Future Use* (restoring the 2025-04-15 4-0-1 vote outcome) and open
a fresh ticket scoped to "Align IDI IG IAL definitions with NIST SP
800-63-4" only after 800-63-4 is actually published, so the new
ticket can reference the final NIST text. No spec change in STU2
beyond what FHIR-48026 already delivered.

#### Justification

The reporter explicitly anticipated this outcome: *"If not [if
800-63-4 isn't finalised in time], we can close this ticket and
consider opening a further ticket to address in a future version."*
800-63-4 was not finalised before STU2 ballot reconciliation
completed. Acting now risks the IG diverging from a draft of
800-63-4 that may still change, which would force a second round of
breaking changes once NIST publishes. Julie Maas' "postpone until
STU3" comment supports this read.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The WG has already re-opened this ticket post-STU2 publication
(Lynn Laakso, 2025-12-10), explicitly reversing the earlier
"Considered for Future Use" decision, which signals that pure
deferral (Disposition C) no longer matches the WG's intent.
Disposition A directly satisfies the reporter's request, continues
the NIST-alignment trajectory the WG established with the
Persuasive resolution of [FHIR-48026](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48026),
and addresses the cross-IG synchronisation question raised by the
SMART Health Cards community on Zulip. Disposition B's
ConceptMap-based decoupling is attractive in the long run but is a
larger terminology refactor that should not block the immediate
800-63-4 alignment; it can be pursued as a follow-up ticket and
discussed alongside Disposition A in the workgroup. The
recommendation is therefore: accept the alignment work for the
next publication cycle, scoped to the IDI IG's IAL migration
narrative and `identity-assurance-level` CodeSystem, contingent on
the published text of NIST SP 800-63-4.
