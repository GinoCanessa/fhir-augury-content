## Summary

The Digital Identity team requests that the Interoperable Digital Identity and
Patient Matching (IDI / IDI-Patient) FHIR Implementation Guide add normative or
informative guidance covering "emergent" identity concepts — explicitly mobile
driver's licenses (mDLs, ISO/IEC 18013-5) and analogous mobile or
verifiable-credential-based identity artifacts — so that the IG remains usable
as these credentials become more widespread in U.S. patient identity flows.
The reporter frames the change as future-proofing: the IG already addresses
traditional identity evidence (physical IDs, demographics, knowledge-based
proofing) but does not yet describe how implementers should ingest, represent,
or weight an mDL (or other emerging digital credential) when constructing an
`IDI-Patient` profile or performing `$match` operations. The ticket was
originally resolved 2025-07-15 as "Considered for Future Use — Out of scope
for STU2, will be considered for STU3" by a 2-0-0 vote (Humberto Lee / Reuben
Daniels), then re-opened on 2025-12-10 by Lynn Laakso post-STU2 publication so
that it can be carried forward into STU3 planning. No cross-referenced Jira
tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were surfaced for this issue in the
ingested community data.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a new informative section to the IDI / IDI-Patient IG (target:
`HL7/fhir-identity-matching-ig`, in the IG's narrative `input/pagecontent/`
authoring area) titled approximately *"Emergent Identity Credentials (mDLs and
Verifiable Credentials)"*. The section would, at minimum:

1. Define the in-scope credential families: ISO/IEC 18013-5 mDLs (and the
   mDoc/ISO 23220 family), W3C Verifiable Credentials, and government-issued
   digital wallet credentials (e.g., REAL ID-aligned mobile IDs, eIDAS 2.0
   EUDI Wallet attributes).
2. Describe how attributes extracted from such a credential map onto
   `IDI-Patient` elements (`Patient.name`, `Patient.birthDate`,
   `Patient.address`, `Patient.identifier` with appropriate
   `identifier.type` / `identifier.system`), including a recommended
   `identifier.system` or `Identifier.type` coding for an mDL-sourced
   identifier and recommended use of the existing identity assurance / level
   extensions where present.
3. Specify the expected effect on identity assurance: a verified mDL with
   issuer-signed attributes SHOULD count as IAL2-equivalent evidence and
   therefore satisfy the higher-confidence `IDI-Patient-L1`/`L2` weighting
   without additional knowledge-based proofing.
4. Provide one or two worked examples (an `IDI-Patient` instance constructed
   from an mDL presentation, plus a `$match` request using it) under
   `input/examples/`.

#### Justification

The reporter is from the Digital Identity team itself and explicitly framed
this as "future-proofing" rather than as a gap that breaks current
implementations. Because the workgroup has already voted (2-0-0 on
2025-07-15) that the change is appropriate but out of scope for STU2, and
because the issue has been re-opened against STU3, accepting as requested in
the STU3 cycle is consistent with the documented direction. Treating the
content as **informative guidance** rather than new normative profiles keeps
the change additive and low-risk for existing STU2 implementers; the IDI IG
already establishes weighted-element and identity-assurance patterns into
which mDL evidence fits naturally, so the new section reuses existing
machinery rather than introducing a parallel one.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than authoring a single self-contained "mDL guidance" section directly
in the IDI IG, publish the guidance as a **companion appendix plus a small,
focused extension and ValueSet contribution**:

1. In `HL7/fhir-identity-matching-ig`, add only a short appendix that
   enumerates the credential families and points implementers at the
   external normative sources (ISO 18013-5, W3C VC Data Model, NIST SP
   800-63-3 rev. 4, EUDI Wallet ARF).
2. Contribute a new `IdentifierType` code (or reuse / extend
   `http://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/v2-0203`) for "mDL" and a small
   structured extension on `Patient.identifier` (e.g.,
   `idi-credential-source`) carrying credential-family code, issuer-trust
   list reference, and presentation timestamp. The terminology lives in the
   IG's `input/vocabulary/` area; the extension lives under
   `input/extensions/` (or wherever the IG's authoring layout puts
   StructureDefinitions).
3. Defer the deep narrative on assurance-level mapping to a Confluence /
   informative Digital Identity white paper that the IG references but does
   not embed, so the IG itself stays implementation-focused.

#### Justification

Emergent identity is a fast-moving area: ISO 18013-5 deployment patterns,
EUDI Wallet attribute schemas, and U.S. state-level mDL trust frameworks are
all still evolving. Embedding extensive narrative directly in the IG risks
shipping STU3 with guidance that ages out before STU4. Splitting the change
into (a) a small, stable structural contribution (one identifier code, one
extension) plus (b) a thin pointer to externally-maintained guidance keeps
the IG's ballotable surface small, lets the structural pieces be reused by
non-IDI profiles (e.g., US Core, IPS), and lets the narrative track
external standards on its own cadence. The trade-off versus Disposition A
is less prescriptive guidance for first-time implementers, but better
long-term maintenance properties.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as **Not Persuasive — out of scope for the IDI IG's mission**. The IDI
IG's stated scope is *patient matching* using FHIR `Patient` resources and
the `$match` operation; the choice of identity-evidence credential type
(physical ID vs. mDL vs. VC) is upstream of, and orthogonal to, the
matching mechanics the IG specifies. Implementers are already free to map
attributes from any credential — including mDLs — into `Patient` today, and
the IG's existing weighted-element and identity-assurance machinery is
credential-source-agnostic by design. The Digital Identity guidance the
reporter is asking for would more appropriately live in (i) the FHIR
Identity Management IG, (ii) an HL7 Digital Identity Implementation
Companion, or (iii) profile-level guidance from communities actually
issuing/accepting mDLs (e.g., AAMVA, NIST NCCoE, EUDI Wallet ARF), rather
than being absorbed into IDI.

#### Justification

Declining is defensible on scope grounds: the IDI IG already abstracts away
credential provenance through its identity-assurance model, so adding mDL-
specific narrative risks (a) implying that mDL is privileged over other
credentials, (b) committing the workgroup to track ISO 18013-5 / EUDI Wallet
revisions in lock-step with FHIR ballot cycles, and (c) duplicating guidance
that other HL7 work products (Identity Management IG, Digital Identity work)
are better positioned to author. The previous 2025-07-15 disposition
("Considered for Future Use") arrived at a similar conclusion; only the
re-open comment, not new evidence, has changed since.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The reporter's underlying need — that an implementer with an mDL in hand
knows how to land its attributes on `IDI-Patient` and how that evidence
affects assurance weighting — is real and worth addressing in STU3, which
rules out Disposition C. However, mDL / EUDI / VC ecosystems are moving
faster than HL7 ballot cycles, so embedding extensive prescriptive narrative
directly in the IG (Disposition A) is likely to ship guidance that ages
poorly. Disposition B captures the durable, FHIR-shaped pieces (an
identifier-type code, one focused extension on `Patient.identifier`, and a
short appendix) inside `HL7/fhir-identity-matching-ig`, and pushes the
fast-moving narrative to an external companion the IG can re-point at
without re-balloting. This also makes the structural pieces reusable beyond
IDI (US Core, IPS, payer/provider directories), which materially increases
the value of the work without expanding the IG's normative surface.
Workgroup should still confirm with the Digital Identity team that the
"mDL identifier code + extension" shape matches what their members are
actually presenting in production pilots before locking the structure for
STU3 ballot.
