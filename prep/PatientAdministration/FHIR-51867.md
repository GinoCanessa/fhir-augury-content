## Summary

While adding a new use-case diagram to the Identity Matching IG, the
authoring round inadvertently introduced regressions in adjacent diagrams
on the Use Cases page. Specifically, the reporter (Julie Maas) flags four
discrete defects in `input/pagecontent/use-cases.md` and the embedded
images:

1. The "Patient-Directed B2C (intrinsic OAuth service)" and
   "Patient-Directed B2C with Tiered OAuth" diagrams incorrectly depict
   the PHR acting as the FHIR server (it should not).
2. The §5.2.3 figure title still says "Patient" where it should say
   "Individual" (consistent with the IG's preferred terminology shift
   away from "Patient User" to "Individual User").
3. The label "Token response" is misspelled in the final diagram.
4. A broken (plain-text) link to the Artifacts page exists on the page.

No related Jira tickets were surfaced via cross-references. No Zulip
discussion of this ticket was found. A single GitHub commit
(`1e0500e0…` on `HL7/fhir-identity-matching-ig`, dated 2025-08-29 by
Aaron Nusstein) is cross-referenced and partially addresses the report —

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat FHIR-51867 as a multi-part technical correction against
`HL7/fhir-identity-matching-ig` and complete the four items in the
report:

1. In `input/images/Use Case Diagrams.drawio`, redraw the
   "Patient-Directed B2C (intrinsic OAuth service)" and
   "Patient-Directed B2C with Tiered OAuth" pages so the FHIR server
   role is held by the EHR/clinical system, not the PHR. Re-export to
   `patient-directed-b2c.png` and
   `patient-directed-b2c-tiered-oauth.png`.
2. In `input/pagecontent/use-cases.md` §5.2.3, change the figure
   `<figcaption>` (and any matching `alt`/`title` attributes on the
   `<img>`) from "Patient" to "Individual" so the §5.2.3 caption
   matches the IG's adopted "Individual User" terminology.
3. In the final diagram on `Use Case Diagrams.drawio` (and its exported
   PNG, e.g. `idp.png`), correct the misspelled "Token response" label.
4. Confirm the Artifacts hyperlink is rendered as real anchor markup
   (`<a href="artifacts.html">Artifacts</a>`) — already done in commit
   `1e0500e0`; verify it renders correctly under the current IG
   publisher build.
5. **Additionally**, fix the regression typo introduced by the
   2025-08-29 fix commit: change
   `"street_address": "1234 Hollywortod Blvd."` back to
   `"1234 Hollywood Blvd."` in the OIDC ID Token Example block of
   `use-cases.md`.

Bundle items 1–5 in a single follow-up PR referencing FHIR-51867 and
mark the Jira ticket Resolved once published.

#### Justification

This is explicitly classified as a **Technical Correction**, not a
substantive design change — the request is to repair authoring defects
on a single page and its supporting diagrams. The changes are
self-contained in `input/pagecontent/use-cases.md` and `input/images/*`,
require no FSH or terminology work, and have no cross-repo impact. The
2025-08-29 commit (`1e0500e0`) already addresses items 2 and 4 and
likely items 1 and 3 (binary diagram changes), so most of the work is
done — but the regression typo and a verification pass against the
published HTML still need to land. Editing the .drawio source first
and re-exporting respects the repo's authoring convention and avoids
losing fixes the next time the diagrams are regenerated.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Split FHIR-51867 into the parts that are already fixed vs. those that
are not, and close out the resolved parts immediately:

- Mark the §5.2.3 caption change ("Individual" wording) and the
  Artifacts hyperlink fix as **already addressed by commit `1e0500e0`**;
  link the commit on the Jira ticket and resolve those sub-items.
- Open a **new, narrowly-scoped technical-correction ticket** (or
  re-purpose this one) covering only what still needs work:
  (a) PHR-vs-FHIR-server role in the two B2C diagrams (require a
  re-review of the rendered PNGs against the narrative), (b) the "Token
  response" label spelling in the final diagram, and (c) the
  newly-introduced "Hollywortod" typo regression in the OIDC ID Token
  Example.
- Require the new ticket's PR to attach a screenshot of each updated
  diagram and a diff of `use-cases.md` so the Patient Administration
  workgroup can verify visually before merge.

#### Justification

The original ticket bundles four heterogeneous defects of differing
risk and verifiability. Two (caption text and Markdown link syntax) are
trivially diff-reviewable and already done. The other two (diagram
content correctness re: PHR/FHIR-server roles, and an in-image label
spelling) cannot be verified from a textual PR diff alone — they need
visual review of the regenerated PNGs. Splitting the ticket lets the
trivial items resolve now and forces the diagram items through a
review path that actually catches the regression pattern that produced
this ticket in the first place. It also makes the regression typo
visible as its own item rather than burying it inside a "diagram fix".

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the ticket and close as **Already Addressed** on the basis that
commit `1e0500e0` (merged 2025-08-29, present at HEAD) already touched
all four reported items: it switched the diagram reference, retitled
the §5.2.3 caption to "Individual", regenerated the PNGs from the
.drawio source (presumed to fix the PHR depiction and the "Token
response" label), and converted the broken Artifacts link to working
HTML.

#### Justification

A defensible "decline" rationale only if the workgroup is willing to
accept the binary diagram edits as fully addressing items 1 and 3
without independent visual re-review, and is willing to ignore the
regression typo (`Hollywortod`) as out of scope for this particular
ticket. If the workgroup takes that view, the cleanest action is to
mark FHIR-51867 Resolved/Already Addressed with a pointer to commit
`1e0500e0` and let the typo be picked up by a separate QA pass.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition C understates the risk: the very failure mode FHIR-51867
documents — a diagram-bundle change introducing collateral errors in
adjacent diagrams and narrative — has *already happened again* inside
the proposed fix (the `Hollywortod` regression). Closing the ticket
purely on the existence of `1e0500e0` would ship that regression and
would not give Patient Administration any confidence that the PHR-role
and "Token response"-label parts actually got fixed in the rendered
PNGs. Disposition A is the right shape of work but treats the four
items as one undifferentiated package, which is what produced the
original quality issue in the first place. Disposition B keeps the
already-resolved items resolved, isolates the diagram-correctness items
behind a visual-review gate, and surfaces the new typo as a first-class
item rather than burying it. It is the lowest-risk path to a clean
publication and matches the Patient Administration workgroup's
typical handling of IG technical corrections.
