## Summary

This Patient Administration ticket against the **Real Time Location Services
(RTLS) FHIR IG** asks whether the official URL for RTLS event content should
be anchored in **terminology.hl7.org (THO)** rather than under
**hl7.org/fhir**. The reporter (Ron G. Parker) is questioning the
canonicalization strategy for what is effectively a list of RTLS "events" —
content that behaves more like terminology than IG narrative, and arguably
belongs in the cross-version, cross-product home for shared terminology
(THO). The ticket has a single imported comment from the reporter and no
linked tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items in the cross-reference index.
A draft resolution is already in place ("Considered for Future Use") that
acknowledges the merit of moving the content to THO but defers it until
after the upcoming RTLS 2.0 ballot, citing instability in the
subscriptions-vs-messaging approach. The ticket carries the
`rtls-ready-for-block-vote` label, indicating it has been queued for a
work-group block-vote disposition.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Re-home the RTLS "events" artifact(s) under
`http://terminology.hl7.org/...` instead of `http://hl7.org/fhir/...`.
Concretely, for each RTLS event-list artifact (most likely a CodeSystem
and/or ValueSet enumerating RTLS events), change the canonical `url` to a
`http://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/<rtls-events>` /
`http://terminology.hl7.org/ValueSet/<rtls-events>` form, move the
authoring source into the THO repository, and remove the duplicate from
the RTLS IG. Update all references in the RTLS IG (profiles, examples,
narrative, search-parameter bindings, MessageDefinitions, and any
SubscriptionTopics) to point at the new THO canonicals. Coordinate
publication so the THO release containing the new artifacts is available
in the same window as RTLS 2.0 ballot, and add a redirect / deprecation
note for any pre-existing `hl7.org/fhir/...` canonicals so existing
implementers are not silently broken.

#### Justification

THO is the agreed cross-product home for shared, reusable terminology and
"event" lists; anchoring there is consistent with how PA and other work
groups have migrated similar enumerations (e.g., v2 / v3 tables, common
code systems). Aligning now — at the 2.0 ballot — is the cheapest moment
to do it, since canonical URLs are about to change anyway as part of the
ballot bump and implementers will already be re-pointing references. Doing
it later compounds the migration cost and risks the IG shipping with
canonicals that the work group already agrees should be elsewhere.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the canonical URLs under `hl7.org/fhir/...` for the RTLS 2.0 ballot,
but adopt the existing draft resolution's phased plan: (1) explicitly
mark the in-IG event CodeSystem/ValueSet as **trial-use, candidate for
THO migration**, with a `copyright` / `description` note pointing at the
intended THO target URL; (2) open a tracking ticket against THO to
provision the target canonicals; (3) after RTLS 2.0 publishes and the
subscriptions-vs-messaging direction settles, perform the migration in a
follow-on ballot, retiring the in-IG copies and replacing them with
references to THO. This is essentially the wording of the existing
resolution ("Considered for Future Use"), formalised into a concrete
two-step plan.

#### Justification

The reporter's underlying concern (THO is the right long-term home) is
acknowledged, but RTLS 2.0 is still resolving the broader architectural
question of whether events flow via Subscriptions (R5 SubscriptionTopic
machinery) or Messaging (MessageDefinition / MessageHeader). The shape
and even the membership of the "events" list could change materially
depending on which path is chosen, and minting THO canonicals before
that stabilises risks publishing terminology that has to be deprecated
or restructured in the next cycle. Phasing avoids both implementer churn
(one canonical change, not two) and premature commitment of a THO
namespace.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request and retain the RTLS event artifacts under
`hl7.org/fhir/...` permanently, on the basis that the events list is
intrinsic to the RTLS IG, governed by the RTLS project, and not intended
for reuse by other specifications. Document this decision in the IG so
the question is not re-raised.

#### Justification

A defensible "decline" rests on the argument that the RTLS event list is
IG-specific rather than cross-spec terminology, and that THO is reserved
for terminology with broad reuse value. If PA judges that the RTLS events
will only ever be consumed by RTLS-conformant implementations, keeping
them in-IG keeps governance, versioning, and release cadence aligned with
the IG that owns them and avoids splitting one logical artifact across
two publication pipelines.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The existing resolution on the ticket already endorses Disposition B in
substance ("makes sense to publish in THO… propose we move this content
into THO after the upcoming 2.0 ballot"), and that judgement is sound.
Accepting as requested (A) forces a THO mint and an IG re-canonicalisation
while the subscriptions-vs-messaging architecture is still in flux, which
risks shipping THO artifacts that have to be reshaped immediately after
RTLS 2.0 publishes — exactly the churn the work group is trying to avoid.
Declining (C) ignores a real governance signal: an "events" list is the
canonical example of content that belongs in THO, and the reporter's
question is well-founded. Disposition B captures the agreement in
principle, defers the actual migration to a moment when the event set is
stable, and gives implementers a single, well-signalled canonical change
in a future ballot rather than two. PA should accept the existing "Considered
for Future Use" resolution wording, add the tracking ticket against THO,
and confirm the block vote.
