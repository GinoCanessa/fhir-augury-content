## Summary

The ticket asks the NDH (National Directory of Healthcare Providers and Services) work stream to reconsider whether three ContactPoint-level extensions — `contactpoint-availabletime`, `via-intermediary`, and `language-speak` (telephone language spoken) — should remain in the current NDH release. The reporter (David Pyke) frames this as a scoping/maintainability question: the extensions are likely to be useful in the future, but it is not clear whether they are sufficiently mature, unambiguous, or implementer-ready to ship now. Subsequent comments push back, arguing that NDH inherits these from Plan-Net and that real directory use cases (per-phone-number hours, per-phone-number language support, distinguishing direct vs. delegated/intermediary numbers) cannot be expressed using only `Location.hoursOfOperation` or `HealthcareService.availableTime` (the two FHIR elements surfaced as the only cross-references for this ticket). One commenter (john_moehrke) signals openness to dropping `via-intermediary` specifically while keeping the other two. No related Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were surfaced by the cross-reference and search tooling.

  Signals willingness to drop `via-intermediary` only, while keeping `contactpoint-availabletime` and `language-speak`.

- **JIRAUSER20919 ("Ming")** — 2025-11-21 — extensive justification (paraphrased):
  - **`contactpoint-availabletime`**: different phone numbers at the same provider/service have different staffed hours (office line vs. after-hours triage line vs. new-patients-only line). `Location.hoursOfOperation` and `HealthcareService.availableTime` describe service hours, not when an individual phone number is staffed. The same concept exists in Plan-Net as `ContactPointAvailableTime`; NDH reuses it for ecosystem alignment, smarter call routing/fallback, and to satisfy CMS / No Surprises Act-driven directory accuracy requirements.
  - **`language-speak`** (per-ContactPoint language spoken): language support is not uniform across all numbers (front desk vs. call center, specialty nurse line vs. main line, payer vs. delegated administrator). Modeling language only at Organization or Location prevents expressing "call this number for Spanish M–F; this other number for Mandarin." Drives consumer language-filter searches, payer member portals, and language-aware call routing.
  - **`via-intermediary`**: needed to differentiate numbers that reach the provider directly from numbers that route through a delegated call center, hospital switchboard, third-party administrator, or behavioral-health triage center. Important for delegated credentialing, behavioral-health crisis lines, large health-system main numbers, referral management, care coordination, payer call routing, and delegated network administration.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Remove all three extensions — `contactpoint-availabletime`, `contactpoint-via-intermediary`, and the per-ContactPoint `language-speak` extension — from the current NDH release. Update the NDH IG to:

1. Drop the StructureDefinitions for the three extensions.
2. Remove their bindings from any NDH ContactPoint profiles (Practitioner, PractitionerRole, Organization, OrganizationAffiliation, Location, HealthcareService, etc.).
3. Update ContactPoint-related narrative pages to redirect implementers to `Location.hoursOfOperation` / `HealthcareService.availableTime` (and to Organization/Location-level `communication`/language metadata) for hours and language information at the service level rather than the per-number level.
4. Add a "Future Considerations" note explaining the deferral and the criteria under which they would be reintroduced (sufficient implementer demand, harmonization with DaVinci PDEX/Plan-Net, unambiguous semantics for `via-intermediary`).

#### Justification

This is the literal request: defer until the extensions are demonstrably maintainable, prioritized, and unambiguous. It reduces NDH's surface area and avoids shipping extensions that the WG itself flags as ambiguous (notably `via-intermediary`, where comment-thread sentiment is already wavering). It also avoids divergence risk if DaVinci/Plan-Net evolves their definitions in a different direction before NDH stabilizes.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — Targeted Removal + Harmonization

#### Proposal

Treat the three extensions individually rather than as a single bundle:

1. **`contactpoint-availabletime`** — **Retain**, but constrain its applicability to `ContactPoint.system = 'phone'` (per the 1/6/2025 FAST National Directory call action item). Add an invariant or `obligation`-style constraint on NDH ContactPoint profiles that limits where the extension may appear, and explicitly bind to / re-use the Plan-Net `ContactPointAvailableTime` definition rather than defining a parallel one. Document the relationship to `Location.hoursOfOperation` / `HealthcareService.availableTime` (service-level hours) versus per-ContactPoint hours.
2. **`language-speak` (per-ContactPoint language spoken)** — **Retain**, also constrained to `ContactPoint.system = 'phone'`. Re-use or align with the corresponding Plan-Net / DaVinci extension if one exists; otherwise propose harmonization back to DaVinci PDEX as a follow-up. Document the use-case gap vs. Organization/Location `communication`.
3. **`contactpoint-via-intermediary`** — **Remove** for this release. Comment-thread sentiment (john_moehrke, and the action item to confirm with DaVinci) is weakest here, and the semantics are the most ambiguous (what counts as "intermediary" — switchboard? answering service? triage center? — is currently under-specified). Add a "Future Consideration" note tying its return to a concrete DaVinci PDEX / Plan-Net alignment.

Before publishing, complete the 1/6/2025 action item: confirm with DaVinci whether `via-intermediary` is required under Plan-Net and what the inheritance story is.

#### Justification

The comments make it clear that the three extensions do not stand or fall together. `contactpoint-availabletime` and `language-speak` both have concrete, named use cases (per-number hours, per-number language access) that genuinely cannot be expressed using `Location.hoursOfOperation` or `HealthcareService.availableTime`, which are the only two cross-referenced spec elements for this ticket. Removing them would force implementers either to lose information or to invent local extensions, which directly undermines NDH's stated role as the "central place for accurate directory" data. Constraining both to `system = 'phone'` addresses the maintainability/ambiguity concern that motivated the ticket. Dropping only `via-intermediary` matches the actual rough consensus visible in the comments and preserves the option to reintroduce it later, properly harmonized with DaVinci.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (Keep All Three)

#### Proposal

Decline the removal request. Retain `contactpoint-availabletime`, `contactpoint-via-intermediary`, and `language-speak` in the current NDH release as currently defined. Optionally tighten usage notes to reduce ambiguity (e.g., examples showing per-number hours/language and a worked `via-intermediary` example) but do not remove or restructure the extensions.

#### Justification

NDH inherits these extensions from Plan-Net, which is already in production use by payers under regulatory pressure (CMS, No Surprises Act). Removing them now would create a regression for downstream Plan-Net implementers who expect NDH (positioned as the central directory) to be at least as expressive as Plan-Net, and would force vendors to maintain their own forks of the same extensions. The "Future Considerations" deferral pattern in Disposition A risks a permanent gap in directory expressivity for per-phone-number hours, language access, and intermediary routing — gaps the comment thread documents as actively used today.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the closest match to where the discussion has actually landed. The reporter's underlying concern — maintainability, priority, and ambiguity — is real, but it is not uniformly true across all three extensions. `contactpoint-availabletime` and `language-speak` have concrete use cases (per-number staffed hours, per-number language access) that the only two cross-referenced FHIR elements (`Location.hoursOfOperation`, `HealthcareService.availableTime`) explicitly cannot express, and they already have an inherited definition from Plan-Net to anchor maintenance. `via-intermediary` is the genuinely ambiguous one, and even sympathetic commenters (john_moehrke) signal openness to dropping it. Constraining the retained extensions to `ContactPoint.system = 'phone'` directly resolves the 1/6/2025 action item and meets the reporter's maintainability concern without losing the use cases that motivated NDH's directory-accuracy mission. The DaVinci/Plan-Net follow-up should be completed before the WG votes on the disposition so that any retained extensions are bound to (not duplicated from) the upstream definition.
