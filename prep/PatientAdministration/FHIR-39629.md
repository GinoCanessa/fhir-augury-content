## Summary

Julie Maas (reporter) raises a concern that, when a person's identity is
verified after they move, the only address held by the system from which a
match is requested may be a former address. In TEFCA-style queries and other
historical-record use cases this can defeat matching. The ticket asks the
Identity & Patient Matching IG to clarify two things in both the identity
verification guidance and the patient-matching guidance: (1) the identity
verification process should be able to capture and verify *all* home
addresses spanning the time frame a record search may cover (including
former addresses for patients who stopped receiving care while at that
former address), and (2) those multiple verified addresses — and other
attributes that change over time, such as name, telephone number, and
email — should be allowed in the match request when each was verified at
some point in time. A follow-up comment proposes that the §4.5 weighting
table also recognize a second verified address by allowing a max weight of
five when two or more attributes from that row are present *or* when more
than one street address is supplied. The ticket has been resolved
**Persuasive** with both of those edits planned (the verification rule
worded as: *"Historical demographics, such as a former address, are held
to the same verification requirements and guidance as current
demographics."*). No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or
GitHub items were returned by Augury for this ticket.

## Details

**Description:**

> In another industry group, a stakeholder mentioned a match concern when
> a person's identity is verified after they move but only their former
> address is in the system from which a match is requested. We discussed
> how the use of email address and mobile number, as well as the
> responder's use of private algorithms and identity management practices
> likely go some way to address this, but after considering this some
> more it occurs to me that we probably need a more specific call-out in
> the IG to clarify, particularly in TEFCA queries or other use cases
> where historical records may be needed that 1) the identity
> verification process should be able to capture and verify all home
> addresses during the time frame a record search may cover — being
> mindful of the case where a patient stopped receiving care while
> residing at a former address, such that that former address may be all
> the health system is aware of — and 2) those multiple verified
> addresses (or other attributes which may have changed like name,
> telephone number, and email address) are included in the match request
> if they were verified at some point in time. We likely need a short
> addition in the identity verification tab as well as in the match tab.

**Resolution Description (Persuasive):**

- Insert as a fourth-line-break sentence after the first sentence (in the
  relevant verification/match guidance):
  *"Historical demographics, such as a former address, are held to the
  same verification requirements and guidance as current demographics."*
- Add into the §4.5 weighting table:
  *"max weight of five for inclusion of two or more different attributes
  from this row OR more than one street address."*

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the resolution exactly as written in the Persuasive resolution and
the 2024-02-09 comment, in the **HL7/fhir-identity-matching-ig** IG
(authoring root `input/pagecontent/`):

1. In `input/pagecontent/digital-identity.md` (and/or
   `guidance-on-identity-assurance.md`, whichever the WG identifies as
   the canonical "identity verification tab"), insert as the fourth
   line break after the first sentence:
   *"Historical demographics, such as a former address, are held to the
   same verification requirements and guidance as current demographics."*
2. In `input/pagecontent/patient-matching.md`, insert the same sentence
   in the parallel position on the match tab. Reconcile with the
   existing line at `patient-matching.md:50` ("Historical address
   information is known to be useful to matching…") so the two
   statements do not contradict — preferred approach: replace the
   existing line with the resolution sentence, or extend it so the
   "same requirements and guidance" wording is explicit and the scope
   covers all historical demographics, not only address.
3. In the §4.5 *Patient Weighted Input Information* table in
   `patient-matching.md` (currently the row weighted **4**), change
   the parenthetical from *"(max weight of 5 for inclusion of 2 or
   more of these)"* to
   *"(max weight of 5 for inclusion of two or more different attributes
   from this row OR more than one street address)"*.
4. Per the 2024-04-08 comment, update an example IDI Patient instance
   under `input/fsh/` to include a second verified address of residence
   so the weighting behavior is demonstrated.

#### Justification

The Persuasive resolution and the implementing comment are concrete,
narrowly scoped, and aligned with the ticket text; the workgroup voted
to re-open the issue after STU1 specifically to land these edits. The
changes are low-risk (Markdown narrative + a one-line table edit + one
FSH example), they harmonize verification guidance with the realities of
TEFCA-style longitudinal queries, and they reinforce the IG's existing
posture (already noted in `patient-matching.md`) that historical
addresses contribute to high-quality matching. Recognizing a second
verified street address in the weight rubric also rewards systems that
do the harder identity-verification work, which is consistent with the
IG's IDIAL/AAL framing.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Land the verification-side narrative change exactly as proposed
(Disposition A items 1 and 2), but treat the §4.5 weighting change as
**normative** and gate it on (a) updating the corresponding **invariant
computation** on the *IDI Patient* / *IDI Patient L0* / *IDI Patient L1*
profiles (in `input/fsh/`) so that *"more than one street address"*
deterministically yields the additional weight, and (b) adding a
non-normative paragraph immediately under the table that defines what
"more than one street address" means in scope (e.g., two or more
distinct `Patient.address` entries each carrying a verified `period`
and not duplicates of each other). The example-patient update from the
2024-04-08 comment is included here too. Keep the verification-tab
sentence wording as-is.

#### Justification

The ticket's first ask — "historical demographics held to the same
verification requirements" — is unambiguous narrative guidance and can
land verbatim. The weighting table, however, drives the IDI Patient L0/L1
invariants the IG uses to gate match-request acceptability; changing the
table without updating the matching invariant risks an asymmetry where
implementers reading the table count two addresses toward the cap of 5
but the FHIRPath invariant on `IDI Patient L1` does not. Disposition B
keeps the spirit of Julie's 2024-02-09 proposal but ties the table edit
to the FSH/invariant work that the 2024-04-08 follow-up itself flagged
("the tables in sections 4.4 and 4.5 and related invariant
computation"). Adding a short definition of "more than one street
address" prevents implementers from claiming the bonus weight for two
addresses that are effectively the same (formatting variants, mailing
vs. physical of the same residence), which would inflate match
confidence falsely.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change on the grounds that the IG already states (in
`patient-matching.md`) that *"Historical address information is known
to be useful to matching; the same requirements and guidance for the
verification of current addresses applies"*, and that the weighting
table already captures address as a weight-4 attribute, so the
underlying need is addressed by existing mechanisms. Close the ticket
as *Not Persuasive — Already Addressed* without further IG edits.

#### Justification

Defensible only on the narrowest reading: the verification expectation
is at least implicitly stated, and `Patient.address` already supports
multiple address entries with `period`, so an implementer who chooses
to send historical addresses can already do so today. Declining avoids
re-opening the §4.5 weighting math and the IDI Patient L0/L1 invariants
during a maintenance cycle. However, this stance ignores (i) the
explicit workgroup vote to re-open this ticket post-STU1, (ii) the fact
that the existing IG sentence is *address-only* and does not generalize
to other historical demographics (name, phone, email), and (iii) the
absence of any current rubric reward for supplying a second verified
street address — which is precisely what Julie's 2024-02-09 comment
flagged. It is included for completeness but is the weakest of the
three options.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **B**

The ticket is already resolved Persuasive and the workgroup clearly
intends to land both edits, so Disposition C is off the table on
process grounds. Between A and B, Disposition A is the literal
resolution and is appealingly small, but Julie's own 2024-04-08 comment
explicitly identifies the "related invariant computation" on the IDI
Patient profiles as part of the work — meaning the table edit in
isolation creates a documentation-vs.-conformance gap that an
implementer could discover only by running the L1 invariant on a
two-address example. Disposition B preserves every concrete edit from
A, adds the FSH/invariant follow-through that the same comment thread
already anticipates, and adds a short definition that prevents the
"two formats of the same address" loophole. It is the cleanest way to
honor the Persuasive resolution without leaving a known soft spot for
STU2. A reviewer who wants a smaller-footprint vote can fall back to
Disposition A and file a separate STU2 ticket for the invariant work.
