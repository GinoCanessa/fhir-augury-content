## Summary

The reporter notes that the NDH IG hosts its own `NdhEndpointEnvironmentCS`
CodeSystem and `NdhEndpointEnvironmentVS` ValueSet, which are direct copies
of the R5 FHIR `endpoint-environment` CodeSystem/ValueSet. These are bound
through the `base-ext-endpoint-environment-type` extension that supplies
the R4-based NDH profile of `Endpoint` with an `environmentType` analog
(R4 `Endpoint` has no native `environmentType` element; that element
appears in R5). The request is to retire the locally duplicated NDH
artifacts and instead reference the canonical FHIR (R5) artifacts
directly, or to reference the THO-hosted versions once the migration
proposed by [FHIR-55292](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-55292) is
complete. The cross-referenced ticket
[FHIR-55292](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-55292) (also Patient
Administration, Highest priority) was triaged at the January 2026 PA
WGM as a "good candidate for moving to THO" â€” that decision is the
upstream dependency for option 2 in this ticket.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the NDH IG so that the `base-ext-endpoint-environment-type`
extension binds directly to the canonical FHIR R5 ValueSet
`http://hl7.org/fhir/ValueSet/endpoint-environment` (and the matching
CodeSystem `http://hl7.org/fhir/CodeSystem/endpoint-environment`),
removing the locally hosted `NdhEndpointEnvironmentCS` and
`NdhEndpointEnvironmentVS` artifacts. The extension's binding strength
and definition would be otherwise unchanged. Add an explicit dependency
declaration in the NDH IG package manifest on the FHIR R5 core package
so the canonical resolves at IG-build time, and refresh any narrative
that references the deprecated NDH-local artifact names.

#### Justification

This eliminates a verbatim copy of an upstream artifact, which is the
preferred FHIR practice â€” locally re-hosting another spec's terminology
forks the canonical and creates an obligation to maintain version
parity by hand. The extension's purpose (carrying an R5-style
`environmentType` value into an R4-based NDH `Endpoint`) is best served
by pointing at the actual definition. However, John Moehrke's comment
identifies a real obstacle: NDH is an R4 IG, and consuming an R5 core
ValueSet/CodeSystem from an R4 IG is not generally supported by R4
validators or downstream tooling that pin to a single FHIR version.
This disposition is only defensible if the PA WG is willing to accept
the cross-version dependency (e.g., by declaring the R5 core package as
an NDH dependency and documenting the cross-version pattern explicitly
in the IG).

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Defer the binding change until the `endpoint-environment` CodeSystem
and ValueSet have been migrated to THO under
[FHIR-55292](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-55292). At that point,
re-bind `base-ext-endpoint-environment-type` to the THO canonicals
(e.g., `http://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/endpoint-environment` and
`http://terminology.hl7.org/ValueSet/endpoint-environment`) and retire
the NDH-local copies. In the interim, keep the NDH-hosted artifacts but
add explicit narrative noting that they are temporary mirrors of R5
content pending the THO migration, and link this ticket and FHIR-55292
from the artifact pages so implementers understand the transition path.
Operationally, mark FHIR-55493 as blocked-on FHIR-55292 (and on the
not-yet-spawned THO ticket) so the substantive change is sequenced
correctly.

#### Justification

This directly answers John Moehrke's R4-vs-R5 concern: THO artifacts
are version-independent by design, so an R4 IG can legitimately bind to
them without inheriting an R5 core dependency. It also honours the PA
WGM Jan 2026 decision recorded on FHIR-55292 that the value set is a
good candidate for THO migration, and avoids the worst outcome â€” an
NDH-local fork that silently drifts from the eventual THO canonical.
The cost is calendar time: the change cannot land until the THO ticket
exists, is accepted, and the artifacts are published from
terminology.hl7.org. Given that NDH is still in active development and
the locally hosted copies are byte-for-byte equivalent to R5 today,
that delay is acceptable.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request and keep `NdhEndpointEnvironmentCS` /
`NdhEndpointEnvironmentVS` as locally hosted NDH artifacts bound to
`base-ext-endpoint-environment-type`. Rationale category: existing
mechanism is adequate and the cross-version dependency the request
would introduce causes more harm than the duplication it removes.

#### Justification

The locally hosted artifacts work today, validate cleanly inside the
R4 NDH IG, and impose no runtime burden on implementers. Pointing an
R4 IG at an R5 core ValueSet (Disposition A) creates a real cross-
version dependency that complicates validation and packaging, and
binding to a not-yet-existent THO artifact (Disposition B) blocks NDH
on work outside its control. A defensible "do nothing" path is to
treat the NDH copies as a deliberate, version-pinned snapshot and
revisit only if the upstream codes change. This is the conservative
choice but also the least progressive â€” it leaves NDH carrying
terminology that is duplicated elsewhere in the HL7 ecosystem.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the cleanest reconciliation of the reporter's intent
(stop hosting a fork of someone else's terminology), John Moehrke's
correctness concern (an R4 IG should not depend on R5 core), and the
PA WGM's own January 2026 direction on FHIR-55292 (move
`endpoint-environment` to THO). It accepts the request in substance
while sequencing it behind the THO migration that the workgroup has
already endorsed. Concretely, PA should: (1) confirm or open the THO
ticket required by FHIR-55292 and link it from both tickets so
progress is visible; (2) leave the NDH-local artifacts in place for
now but add narrative flagging them as temporary mirrors; and
(3) commit to re-binding `base-ext-endpoint-environment-type` to the
THO canonicals â€” and deleting the NDH-local copies â€” as soon as the
THO publication is available. Disposition A should be reserved for the
case where the THO migration stalls and the WG nonetheless wants to
remove the duplication; Disposition C should only be selected if PA
explicitly decides the duplication is preferable to any cross-spec
dependency.
