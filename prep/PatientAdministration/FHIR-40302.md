## Summary

The ticket "Additional identity text for V2" proposes editorial revisions to
the introductory "Identity is important to this IG because…" narrative in the
Interoperable Digital Identity and Patient Matching (IDI / Patient Matching)
implementation guide. The original change suggested replacing "Patient" with
"Person" (e.g., "Person-Directed Exchange") and replacing "patient" with
"individual" in several places, in order to clarify that authorized
representatives and other non-patient users are also in scope for the
workflows described by the IG. A subsequent ballot-cycle resolution
("Persuasive with Modification", 2024-03-07) accepted the spirit of the
change but kept the term "Patient-Directed Exchange" and added the
clarifying clause "as enumerated in this implementation guide". The
reporter's most recent comment (Julie Maas, 2025-04-15) argues this
follow-up edit is now non-persuasive, because earlier resolutions and the
existing use-case description already make clear that authorized users are
not excluded — implying the ticket should be re-dispositioned rather than
re-applied. No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub
items were surfaced for this issue.

## Details

**Description:**

Identity is important to this IG because:

When the identity of the person who is authenticated in a transaction is
known with high confidence, this information can be used in an access
decision, e.g., Patient-Directed Exchange. This allows implementers to rely
on user authentication in this workflow instead of probabilistic matching,
which becomes increasingly convenient as federated identity services
proliferate. This also reduces the number of credentials an individual
needs to maintain.

As a secondary effect, digital credentials with high confidence identity
assurance and a globally unique identifier associated with the individual,
even in Business to Business matching (where the patient is not
authenticated as being present in the transaction), emulate the perfect
matching described in the paragraph above. Attempting to match on such a
unique identifier is a preferred best practice over matching with a
combination of demographics.

When identity proofing has been completed for an individual, the process of
verifying that demographic attributes are consistent with a unique
individual in the real world makes the attributes more meaningful in match
requests and improves match quality when probabilistic matching must be
used.

**Resolution (recorded on the ticket):** *Persuasive with Modification* —
include the same three paragraphs but insert "as enumerated in this
implementation guide" after "high confidence" in paragraph 2, and replace
"the patient is not authenticated" with "the individual is not
authenticated" in paragraph 3. The literal token "Patient-Directed
Exchange" is retained.

## Proposed Dispositions

Note: because no GitHub cross-references exist for this ticket and no
repo briefing has been loaded, the proposals below name the IG narrative
rather than a specific repo path. If the workgroup chooses A or B, the
implementer will need to locate the corresponding intro / narrative page
in the IDI Patient Matching IG source (an HL7 IG repository under the
Patient Administration WG, not currently surfaced in this Jira item) and
apply the wording change there. A repo-analysis briefing should be loaded
before the change is actually executed.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested (apply the recorded "Persuasive with Modification" text)

#### Proposal

Update the "Identity is important to this IG because…" introductory
section of the Interoperable Digital Identity and Patient Matching IG so
that the narrative reads as the resolution dictates. Concretely:

1. In paragraph 2, after "is known with high confidence," insert the
   clause **"as enumerated in this implementation guide,"** so the
   sentence becomes: "When the identity of the person who is
   authenticated in a transaction is known with high confidence, *as
   enumerated in this implementation guide,* this information can be used
   in an access decision, e.g., Patient-Directed Exchange."
2. In paragraph 3, replace **"the patient is not authenticated"** with
   **"the individual is not authenticated"**, so the parenthetical
   becomes "(where the individual is not authenticated as being present
   in the transaction)".
3. Leave the term "Patient-Directed Exchange" unchanged elsewhere in the
   passage.

No structural, profile, value-set, or extension changes are involved —
this is a narrative-only edit to the IG's intro page.

#### Justification

This is the disposition the workgroup voted on and recorded as the
applied resolution. It threads the needle the discussion identified:
"Patient-Directed Exchange" is a recognized term of art and is preserved,
while "individual" replaces "patient" in the B2B matching paragraph where
no patient is actually a transaction participant, and the "as enumerated
in this implementation guide" clause scopes the high-confidence-identity
discussion to the use cases the IG actually defines (so authorized
representatives are not implicitly excluded). It is a low-risk editorial
change with no impact on conformance, profiles, or implementations.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — Apply the broader "Person-Directed" wording

#### Proposal

Apply the broader 2024-01-04 variant of the proposed text instead of the
narrower one. Specifically:

1. Replace **"Patient-Directed Exchange"** with **"Person-Directed
   Exchange"** in the relevant sentence of paragraph 2.
2. Still replace **"the patient is not authenticated"** with **"the
   individual is not authenticated"** in paragraph 3.
3. Optionally still add "as enumerated in this implementation guide" for
   the same scoping benefit as Disposition A.
4. Add a brief glossary / definitions entry (or a one-sentence footnote)
   noting that "Person-Directed Exchange" subsumes the previously-used
   "Patient-Directed Exchange" term, including authorized-representative
   workflows, to preserve discoverability for readers who know the older
   phrase.

#### Justification

This is closer to what the original ticket actually requested and to the
underlying concern raised in the 2023-12-21 comment ("commentary around
this guidance is cautious around the use of the word 'Patient' in
relation to our Authorized representative discussions"). "Person-Directed
Exchange" is unambiguously inclusive of authorized representatives,
guardians, and other non-patient end-users, whereas "Patient-Directed
Exchange" plus a scoping clause is still implicitly patient-centric. The
trade-off is terminological churn: implementers searching for
"Patient-Directed Exchange" against existing literature may not
immediately find this IG, which is why a definitions entry or footnote is
recommended to preserve the older term as a synonym.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (mark Not Persuasive / no change beyond what is already published)

#### Proposal

Reject the residual textual change and leave the IG as currently
published — i.e., do not apply either the "Person-Directed Exchange"
substitution or the "as enumerated in this implementation guide"
insertion. Re-disposition this ticket as **Not Persuasive** (or
**Persuasive — Already Applied**, depending on whether any of the wording
already shipped in STU1) and close it out.

#### Justification

This is essentially the position the reporter herself takes in the
2025-04-15 comment: "We previously resolved that it was not necessary to
change the naming to Person-Directed instead of Patient-Directed
exchange and the use case description updates that have already been
made clarify that authorized users are not being left out. This seems
non-persuasive." If the use-case descriptions elsewhere in the IG
already make clear that authorized representatives are in scope, then a
narrative-only edit to the introduction adds little reader value and
risks introducing terminological inconsistency with sibling IGs and
prior published material that all use "Patient-Directed Exchange". The
ticket is also labeled `block-vote`, so a clean "no change" disposition
keeps the block tidy.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The workgroup already deliberated and recorded "Persuasive with
Modification" as the resolution, and the modified text is the minimum
edit needed to address the underlying concern (that the introduction's
patient-centric phrasing implicitly excludes authorized representatives
from the high-confidence-identity workflows). Disposition A applies that
agreed text without re-litigating the broader "Patient-Directed" vs.
"Person-Directed" question, preserves the term of art that implementers
and prior literature already use, and is editorial-only with no
implementation impact. Disposition B more fully addresses the original
request but reopens a terminology debate the workgroup has effectively
closed, and Disposition C — while consistent with the reporter's most
recent comment — discards a resolution the workgroup already approved
and leaves the introduction's ambiguity unaddressed. If the workgroup
nevertheless agrees with the reporter's 2025-04-15 reassessment, the
clean path is to formally re-disposition to C rather than leaving the
ticket in its current "Triaged / Persuasive with Modification but not
applied" limbo.
