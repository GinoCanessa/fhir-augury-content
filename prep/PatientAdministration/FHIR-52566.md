## Summary

The reporter (Vincent McCauley) asserts that the **Real Time Location Services
(RTLS) FHIR Implementation Guide**, owned by Patient Administration, overlaps
substantially with FHIR functionality already defined by the **Devices working
group** — specifically the device *enrolment* and *notification* transactions —
and asks the Patient Administration WG to engage the Devices WG to harmonise
the two specifications before they diverge.

No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were surfaced
by the Augury index for this ticket. The single recorded comment, from
`mfaughn` on behalf of the Devices WG, states that DEV WG has already reviewed
the RTLS IG at recent WGMs and does **not** see a clear conflict with other
approaches (IHE, IEEE, or the IG itself); instead they see opportunities for
collaboration and Elliot Silver suggested that DEV WG consider joining the IG
as a co-sponsor in any future ballot cycle. The ticket has already been
resolved as **Not Persuasive** (per the snapshot's Resolution Description: no
spec changes required, but DEV WG co-sponsorship is being investigated). This
prep is therefore a confirmation/disposition-recording exercise rather than an
open design question.

## Proposed Dispositions

Because no GitHub repositories are implicated, the dispositions below are
process/spec-governance proposals rather than concrete authoring-root edits.
The ticket has already been resolved **Not Persuasive** with a stated
intention to pursue DEV WG co-sponsorship; the dispositions are framed to give
the workgroup explicit options to confirm, refine, or override that
resolution.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat the request as a directive to **harmonise the RTLS IG with the Devices
WG's enrolment and notification machinery before publication**. Concretely:

- Open a joint PA + DEV WG harmonisation track for the RTLS IG.
- Re-anchor the IG's enrolment flow on the existing Device-WG-owned patterns
  (Device + Provenance + Subscription/SubscriptionTopic for notifications)
  rather than introducing IG-local enrolment/notification interactions.
- Add a normative "Relationship to Devices WG specifications" section to the
  IG narrative documenting the alignment.
- Block republication of the IG until the harmonised content has been
  reviewed and signed off by DEV WG.

#### Justification

This is the literal reading of the submitter's request. It minimises the risk
of two HL7-published specs defining materially different enrolment /
notification mechanisms for overlapping device use cases, which is a real
implementer pain point and a frequent source of post-publication CRs. It also
gives DEV WG concrete review hooks rather than just a co-sponsorship label.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the *underlying* concern (cross-WG coordination on device enrolment /
notification) without re-architecting the IG. Specifically:

- Keep the RTLS IG content as balloted (no normative changes to enrolment or
  notification transactions).
- Add a non-normative **"Relationship to Devices WG artifacts"** appendix to
  the IG that explicitly maps RTLS interactions onto the analogous Device WG
  patterns (Device, DeviceMetric, Subscription / SubscriptionTopic, IEEE
  11073 mappings) and notes where the IG intentionally constrains them
  further for RTLS use cases.
- Formalise **Devices WG as a co-sponsor** of the IG (per Elliot Silver's
  suggestion in the comment) so future changes flow through both WGs.
- Track any concrete divergences as new, scoped CRs rather than blocking on
  a single harmonisation pass.

#### Justification

The DEV WG's own review (per `mfaughn`'s comment) explicitly concluded there
is **no clear conflict** with IHE, IEEE, or other device approaches. That
weakens the premise of "must harmonise before publication" while still
respecting the legitimate governance concern. Adding a relationship appendix
+ co-sponsorship is a low-cost, low-risk way to lock in cross-WG ownership
without re-opening normative content, and it matches the Resolution
Description already recorded on the ticket ("no changes to the specification
were needed, but are investigating the Devices WG as a co-sponsor"). It also
avoids the typical cost of a late-stage harmonisation track (rebalancing the
IG content, re-balloting).

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Confirm the existing resolution: **Not Persuasive**. No changes to the RTLS
IG, on the basis that:

- The Devices WG has already reviewed the IG and reported no clear conflict.
- The submitter's claim of overlap is not substantiated with specific
  artifact-level conflicts (no resources, profiles, operations, or
  SubscriptionTopics are identified in the ticket as duplicated).
- The request as written is a process directive ("engage the Devices
  workgroup"), not a spec change, and that engagement has already occurred.

Record the rationale on the ticket and close. Pursue DEV WG co-sponsorship
through normal WG-to-WG channels independently of this CR.

#### Justification

The CR does not name a single overlapping artifact; it asserts overlap at the
narrative level. The DEV WG's own on-record review contradicts the asserted
conflict. Under the standard "Not Persuasive" criteria (insufficient evidence
of a problem requiring a spec change), declining is defensible and is
consistent with the resolution already entered. Co-sponsorship discussions
do not require a CR to remain open.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **B**

Disposition B best reflects what has actually happened on the ticket and what
the workgroup is most likely to want to defend on the floor. The DEV WG has
already done the substantive review and found no concrete conflict (which
undercuts A's premise of mandatory harmonisation), but the submitter's
governance concern is real and a "decline with no follow-up" (C) leaves the
WG exposed to the same complaint at the next ballot. Adding a non-normative
"Relationship to Devices WG artifacts" appendix and formalising Devices WG
as a co-sponsor — exactly what `mfaughn`/Elliot Silver suggested in the
recorded comment — closes the loop without reopening balloted content and
matches the existing Resolution Description on the ticket. The workgroup can
fall back to C (pure Not Persuasive) if the floor agrees that the
co-sponsorship workstream does not need to be tied to this CR.
