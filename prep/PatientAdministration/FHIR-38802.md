## Summary

The Interoperable Digital Identity and Patient Matching (IDPM) IG, like
TEFCA, currently conflates the concepts of *patient* (the subject of the
data being requested) with *user* (the individual being authenticated to
initiate the request). The reporter, Cooper Thompson, asks the Patient
Administration work group to clearly separate these two concepts in the
IG so that pediatric, caregiver, and proxy use cases are properly
covered. Users are expected to hold OpenID tokens and undergo IAL-n
identity assurance, while patients (e.g., a 2-month-old infant) cannot
themselves perform identity proofing. The ticket suggests covering both
*user matching* (B2C / patient-mediated exchange) and *patient matching*
(B2B), adding a `RelatedPerson` profile suitable for weighted matching,
and auditing every place the IG references IAL-n to make clear whether
the requirement applies to the user or the patient. The ticket has been
resolved as **Persuasive**, and the work group has committed to
introducing explicit Patient / User / Authorized Representative actors,
relevant TEFCA and SMART App Launch alignment, and IAL guidance for
authorized representatives.

## Details

**Description:**

This IG seems to blur the lines between patient and user (TEFCA does
too).

To support pediatric use cases, adult care givers, etc., we should
clearly split the user and patient concepts. Users have OpenID tokens,
can do IAL, etc. Patients can't (in call cases, e.g., a 2 month old
baby).

We may want to cover both User matching and Patient matching.

- User matching for B2C (what is normally known as patient-mediated
  exchange).
- Patient matching for B2B

We might consider updating:

- Adding a RelatedPerson profile for weighed matching
- Identifying places where IAL-n is reference, and being clear that it
  is the user doing this, not necessarily the patient.

**Resolution (Persuasive, 2024-06-20):**

The work group accepted the request and plans to comb through the IG to
correct actor naming based on whether the actor is the *patient*
(subject) or the *user* (initiator) of a `$match` request. They will
also add normative narrative defining the two primary actors:

- **Patient** — the subject of the data query being sent in the `$match`
  request (or equivalent).
- **User** — the individual who is being authenticated to initiate a
  `$match` request (or equivalent).

The resolution introduces the concept of an **Authorized
Representative** — an individual authorized to access another
individual's health data — and gives examples:

- **B2B Use Cases** — A physician accessing a patient's health data
  through an HIE/QHIN query via their EMR.
- **B2C Proxy User Use Case** — An authorized representative accessing
  a patient's record (e.g., a parent accessing their child's records).

The resolution further specifies that an authorized representative's
identity SHALL be verified per IG guidance, sufficient demographics SHALL
be collected if the representative is to be matched, and matching that
authorizes data access SHALL be performed at IAL 1.8 or higher.
Establishing authorization itself is declared out of scope for this IG;
the B2C credentialed workflow is delegated to the HL7 SMART App Launch
IG.

## Proposed Dispositions

Note: this ticket has already been resolved as **Persuasive** with a
detailed plan; the dispositions below are framed for the work group's
follow-up "what does the IG actually need to change to deliver on the
resolution" discussion.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the IDPM IG to formally split the *Patient* and *User* concepts:

1. Add a normative *Actors* section that defines:
   - **Patient** — the subject of the `$match` request.
   - **User** — the individual being authenticated to initiate the
     `$match` request.
   - **Authorized Representative** — an individual authorized to access
     another individual's health data, with the B2B and B2C-proxy
     examples drawn directly from the resolution text.
2. Audit every reference to *IAL* / *IAL-n* in the IG and re-attribute
   each requirement to either the *User* or the *Patient* actor; in
   particular, state that authorized-representative identity SHALL be
   verified at IAL 1.8 or higher when the match grants data access.
3. Add a `RelatedPerson` profile suitable for weighted demographic
   matching of an authorized representative, with required demographic
   elements aligned to those used by the existing `Patient` matching
   profile.
4. Update the Use Cases page to add the TEFCA-aligned B2B and B2C-proxy
   workflows called out in the JIRAUSER24103 comment, and cross-link to
   the SMART App Launch IG for B2C credentialed flows.
5. Declare authorization determination explicitly out of scope, as
   stated in the resolution.

#### Justification

This is what the resolution already commits the work group to deliver,
and it directly answers Cooper's three asks (split actors, surface
`RelatedPerson` for weighted matching, disambiguate IAL-n requirements).
The pediatric / proxy use cases the reporter calls out are
implementation realities that the IG must support, and TEFCA's own
conflation of these terms makes it especially important for IDPM to
draw the line cleanly. The resolution text is already written in
near-spec language and can be adapted into IG narrative with relatively
little additional design work.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt the actor split and IAL clarifications from Disposition A, but
instead of introducing a new IDPM-specific `RelatedPerson` profile,
extend the existing IDPM `Patient` matching profile (and `$match`
parameter guidance) so that an authorized representative is conveyed by
a `RelatedPerson` resource referenced from the in-band `Patient`
contact / linkage, and constrain the *existing* base FHIR `RelatedPerson`
elements (name, telecom, address, identifier) for weighted matching via
`SearchParameter`-style guidance rather than a brand-new profile.
Align the demographic element set with the SMART App Launch and US Core
`RelatedPerson` profiles where they already exist, only adding IDPM
constraints (e.g., minimum demographic completeness, identity-evidence
extension) that are genuinely IDPM-specific.

#### Justification

A new profile is cheap to create but expensive to maintain across
ballots and downstream IGs. If the IDPM `RelatedPerson` ends up
duplicating most of what US Core or SMART already constrains, the IG
gains an extra surface area without much interoperability win. Reusing
existing `RelatedPerson` profiles and layering IDPM-specific guidance
keeps the actor model intact, keeps Disposition A's most important
clarifications (Patient vs. User vs. Authorized Representative, and
re-targeted IAL requirements), and reduces the number of conformance
artifacts implementers must consume. The trade-off is that weighted
matching guidance has to live in narrative + parameter guidance instead
of a profile, which is slightly less machine-checkable.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (Persuasive — Considered for Future Use)

#### Proposal

Acknowledge that the actor confusion is real, but defer the
Patient / User / Authorized Representative split, the new
`RelatedPerson` profile, and the IAL-n re-attribution to a future
version of the IDPM IG. In the current cycle, add only a short
*Terminology* note that flags the conflation, points readers to TEFCA
and SMART App Launch for the user-side identity flow, and warns
implementers not to assume the `$match` subject and the authenticated
caller are the same individual.

#### Justification

This ticket originally went through a
"Considered for Future Use" vote (Brown / Deroode, 7-0-1, 2023-03-15)
before being re-opened post-STU1. The change is broad — it touches the
actor model, multiple IAL requirements, and adds a new profile — and a
narrative-only nudge would reduce risk to the current ballot while
preserving optionality for a later, more thorough rework. Declining (in
the "defer" sense) would also let the work group coordinate the actor
split with whatever TEFCA finalizes, avoiding a second round of
churn in IDPM if TEFCA changes its own definitions.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The work group has already resolved this ticket as Persuasive with
resolution text that essentially *is* Disposition A, and the previous
attempt to defer it (the 2023-03-15 vote) was explicitly reverted by
lynn_laakso so that it could be addressed in the next cycle. Cooper's
request reflects a real interoperability problem — pediatric and
caregiver flows do not work if the IG assumes Patient ≡ User — and the
fix is largely editorial plus one new (or constrained) `RelatedPerson`
profile, both of which are well within the work group's scope.
Disposition B is a reasonable simplification if the work group wants to
reduce profile sprawl, and its `RelatedPerson` argument should be
weighed when the actual profile is drafted, but it should be treated as
an implementation refinement of A rather than a competing direction.
Disposition C is not recommended because it has effectively already
been tried and rejected by the work group.
