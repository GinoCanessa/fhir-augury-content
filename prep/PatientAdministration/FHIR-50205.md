## Summary

The ticket, raised against the *Interoperable Digital Identity and Patient
Matching (FHIR)* IG, asks the Patient Administration work group to look
into IANA registration of HL7-defined OAuth/OIDC parameters — initially
framed as registering `amr` and `acr` values, and subsequently refined in
the comments to focus on registering an "HL7 Person Identifier" claim with
IANA as a JSON Web Token Claim (per [RFC 7519 §10](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/pdfrfc/rfc7519.txt.pdf)).
Comment analysis concluded that, per OIDC Core §5.1.2, IANA registration of
the HL7 Person Identifier is **not required**, but the IG editors intend to
register it when feasible. The thread also surfaces two related editorial
suggestions: (a) renaming the claim to `HL7PersonIdentifier` to disambiguate
it from other HL7 identifiers that might be registered in the future
(per a HIMSS conversation with Dan Vreeman), and (b) populating Section 4.2
with concrete, externally-published URI lists (UDAP `idial1.5/1.8/2`, the
OIDC eKYC & IDA `ial2` URI, and the OIDC4VP / NIST NCCoE `acr` list) rather
than waiting on IANA. The 7/16/2025 PA public call deferred action until
after STU2 publication. No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or
GitHub items were surfaced for this ticket.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt a two-track action plan that mirrors what the comment thread has
already converged on, and treat this ticket as the umbrella for both
tracks against the *Interoperable Digital Identity and Patient Matching
(FHIR)* IG:

1. **HL7 Person Identifier claim — IANA registration intent.**
   - Add a note to the IG (in the section that defines the HL7 Person
     Identifier as a userinfo / ID-token claim) stating that, per OIDC
     Core §5.1.2, IANA registration is **not required**, but HL7 intends
     to register the claim with IANA's JWT Claims registry per
     [RFC 7519 §10](https://www.rfc-editor.org/rfc/pdfrfc/rfc7519.txt.pdf)
     as a future maintenance activity.
   - Rename the claim from "HL7 Identifier" to
     **`HL7PersonIdentifier`** throughout the IG (definition, examples,
     and any conformance language), reserving the more generic
     "HL7 Identifier" namespace for future, distinct registrations.
2. **`acr` / `amr` values — in-IG enumeration (no IANA registration).**
   - Update Section 4.2 to enumerate the concrete URIs already published
     elsewhere: `http://udap.org/code/id/idial1.5`,
     `http://udap.org/code/id/idial1.8`,
     `http://udap.org/code/id/idial2` (UDAP), and
     `https://openid.net/identity_assurnace_level/ial2` (eKYC & IDA) for
     systems referencing NIST 800-63-3 IAL2.
   - Add forward-looking references to
     [openid-connect-eap-acr-values-1_0](https://openid.net/specs/openid-connect-eap-acr-values-1_0.html)
     and [NIST IR 8480 ipd](https://nvlpubs.nist.gov/nistpubs/ir/2024/NIST.IR.8480.ipd.pdf)
     so implementers know where the canonical lists live. Explicitly
     state that HL7 will **not** pursue IANA registration of `amr`/`acr`
     values, since the original premise was based on a misunderstanding
     of OIDC Core requirements.

Defer the actual filing of the IANA JWT Claim registration until **after
STU2 publication**, consistent with the 7/16/2025 PA public call
disposition.

#### Justification

This proposal directly reflects the consensus that has already formed in
the comment thread and on the 7/16/2025 public call: the editors have
researched the OIDC and IANA requirements, have settled on a name change,
have identified the concrete URI lists to cite for assurance levels, and
have agreed that registration is desirable but not blocking for STU2.
Capturing the registration intent in normative text prevents the question
from being re-asked in the next ballot cycle, and enumerating concrete
URIs gives implementers something testable today rather than a pointer to
a registry that does not yet contain HL7-controlled entries. The rename
to `HL7PersonIdentifier` is consistent with general HL7 naming hygiene
and was recommended by Dan Vreeman, the HL7 terminology lead.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Split this ticket into two narrower tickets and resolve only the
editorial / in-IG portion now:

- **This ticket (FHIR-50205):** Close as documentation-only. Add a single
  paragraph to the IG noting that (a) per OIDC Core §5.1.2, IANA
  registration of the HL7 Person Identifier claim is not required, and
  (b) HL7 has determined that IANA registration of `amr`/`acr` values is
  similarly not required and will not be pursued. Update Section 4.2
  with the UDAP `idial*` URIs and the eKYC & IDA `ial2` URI as
  concrete examples.
- **New follow-up ticket (post-STU2):** Track the actual IANA JWT Claim
  registration as a separate work item, since registration is an
  out-of-band administrative process with its own cadence and reviewers
  (the IANA Designated Experts, not the PA work group). Also handle the
  `HL7PersonIdentifier` rename there, because a rename is a breaking
  change to any implementer that has wired the current claim name into
  code or test scripts and deserves its own balloted change with a
  migration note.

#### Justification

The current ticket conflates three separable concerns — (1) a
clarification that IANA registration is not required, (2) an
administrative commitment to register a JWT claim with IANA at some
future date, and (3) a rename of the claim — and treating them as a
single disposition risks blocking the easy editorial fixes on the slow
administrative work. A rename in particular is a breaking change for
implementers and conformance test tooling and should be balloted
visibly rather than slipped into an "IANA registration" ticket. Splitting
also lets PA close FHIR-50205 cleanly now and removes a stale
"Triaged / Unassigned" item from the backlog.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the ticket as **out of scope / no normative change required** and
close it as "Persuasive with Modification → no spec change". Document in
the resolution that:

- IANA registration of `amr`/`acr` values is not required per OIDC Core
  and is not an HL7 work item.
- IANA registration of the HL7 Person Identifier as a JWT claim is also
  not required per OIDC Core §5.1.2; whether and when HL7 chooses to
  register it is an administrative matter for the HL7 office, not a
  ballotable item against the IG.
- The Section 4.2 example URIs and the rename suggestion should be
  raised as their own tickets if the work group wants to act on them.

#### Justification

Strictly read, the ticket asks PA to "look into" IANA registration. The
research has been done (see Julie Maas's comments) and the answer is
"not required." There is therefore no normative IG change strictly
necessitated by this ticket; the editorial improvements that surfaced
during discussion are real but are arguably not what FHIR-50205 was
filed to deliver. Declining keeps the ticket scope honest, avoids
scope-creep into a rename and a Section 4.2 rewrite under a single
"IANA registration" label, and forces those follow-on changes to be
filed and reviewed on their own merits.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B best reflects how the comment thread actually evolved and
respects the 7/16/2025 public-call decision to revisit post-STU2. The
editorial fixes (clarifying paragraph + Section 4.2 example URIs) are
non-controversial, deliverable now, and clearly improve implementability;
folding them into FHIR-50205 closes a stale "Triaged / Unassigned"
ticket without overcommitting. The two items that genuinely need
post-STU2 attention — filing the IANA JWT Claim registration and
renaming the claim to `HL7PersonIdentifier` — both have characteristics
(out-of-band administrative process; breaking change for implementers)
that argue for their own visible tickets rather than being absorbed
silently here. Disposition A is a defensible alternative if the work
group prefers to keep the umbrella ticket open through STU2; Disposition
C is too narrow given that the discussion has already produced
actionable editorial guidance the IG should not lose.
