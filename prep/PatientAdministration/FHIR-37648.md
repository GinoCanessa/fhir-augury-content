## Summary

Patient Administration is asking how FHIR should handle Encounter instances whose `participant` list grows very large on long-stay admissions. For a patient admitted for a year, most clinicians recorded early in the stay remain on the resource with a `period.end` far in the past, so the "active" participant list is small but the historical list can be huge. The original ticket floated two directions: (1) allowing `CareTeam` as a `participant.actor` reference type so implementers can offload the list, or (2) moving historical participants onto `EncounterHistory` and redefining `Encounter.participant` as the currently-active set (possibly with recent/imminent). Since then the discussion has broadened: PA evaluated the FHIR-I [Operations for Large Resources](https://hl7.org/fhir/operations-for-large-resources.html) page (`$filter`, `$add`, `$remove` on Group/List) and found functional gaps, which were captured in the sibling FHIR-I tracker [FHIR-52912](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-52912) — specifically how a client detects that a list has been auto-subsetted, how to retrieve the complete list anyway, how to update the core properties of a resource whose large backbone has been omitted, and how the filter interacts with searches and subscriptions. The same pain applies to other long lists (`Encounter.location`, `Account.coverage`, and by analogy `CodeSystem.concept`, `Questionnaire.item`). The ticket carries the `MovingToGuide` label and the January 2024 PA WGM disposition marked it *Persuasive*: PA wants guidance published rather than a structural change to Encounter itself, pending FHIR-I completing the large-resource operations. The December 2025 Zulip exchange with Grahame Grieve and the "Dealing with Large resources" thread in `#implementers` both concluded that, at least for R6, this work belongs in the API Incubator and R6 will not ship a complete answer.

## Details

**Description:**

We've run into issues where the participant list of an Encounter can get very large for long stay admissions. Typically there are a reasonable number of *active* participants, but there maybe a large number of historical participants. For example, if a patient has been admitted for a year, the nurses they had in their first weeks of the stay may still be listed with a `period.end` of 11 months ago.

There are a few possible approaches we could consider:

1. Add `CareTeam` as an allowed type for the `participant.actor` reference. Then a system could move the participant list to the CareTeam. This kinda moves the problem.
2. Include participants on `EncounterHistory`, and have guidance that `Encounter.participants` is just currently active participants. Though maybe we also want recent and imminent as well?
3. Other options are welcome.

**Resolution description (PA WGM Jan 2024 – Persuasive):**
The [Operations for large resources](https://hl7.org/fhir/operations-for-large-resources.html) page looks like the right framework for Encounter, but three gaps remain: (1) how a client detects that a list has been auto-subsetted (a specific `issue-type` such as `too-long`/`too-costly`, an HTTP status, or a new meta tag); (2) how a client requests the complete list anyway; (3) how a client updates the core properties of the resource when the big backbone has been omitted on GET. Example candidate properties: `Encounter.participants`, `Encounter.location`, `Account.coverage`. Two detection options were described — (a) error/exception (with the note that this complicates search because a single too-large instance could abort a search) and (b) auto-subset via the `SUBSETTED` meta tag plus a second tag/extension that identifies *which* element was filtered. `$filter` should gain a `property` parameter (e.g. `Encounter.participant`, `CodeSystem.concept.concept.concept`, `Questionnaire.item.item.item`) and date range, and the extension on the returned resource should identify which backbones were filtered. Update of the core elements around the omitted list is the remaining hard problem; patch was considered but Brian Postlethwaite flagged that patch normally requires a versioned GET of the full resource.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Keep the ticket's current *Persuasive* posture and publish guidance (consistent with the `MovingToGuide` label) that directs Encounter implementers to the FHIR-I [Operations for Large Resources](https://hl7.org/fhir/operations-for-large-resources.html) mechanism, with explicit notes about its known limitations for Encounter:

- State that `Encounter.participant`, `Encounter.location`, and `Account.coverage` are the PA-identified candidates for large-list behaviour.
- Cross-reference FHIR-52912 as the open FHIR-I-side work item that must land before PA can commit to a single normative recommendation.
- Do **not** change the Encounter resource structurally for R6 (no new `participant.actor` type of `CareTeam`, no move of participants onto `EncounterHistory`).
- Add an Encounter page note that servers MAY auto-subset long participant/location lists and, when they do, SHOULD tag the response with `http://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/v3-ObservationValue#SUBSETTED` plus an extension identifying the subsetted element path, mirroring the approach FHIR-52912 is shaping.

Because the ticket is already resolved *Persuasive* and Grahame Grieve has stated on Zulip that "dealing with these issues is in the incubator space," accepting as requested means landing the *guidance*, not a spec change.

#### Justification

Matches the trail of WGM minutes from June 2022 → Sept 2022 → Sept 2023 → Jan 2024 → Oct 2025, all of which progressively narrowed the answer from "change Encounter" to "publish guidance and point at FHIR-I operations." It preserves R6 normative stability for Encounter, aligns with the Zulip consensus that the general large-resource problem belongs in the API Incubator, and keeps the door open for FHIR-52912 to backfill the infrastructure once it matures. It also honours Paul Church's Sept 2023 point that we should not scatter large-list machinery across every repeated element in the spec.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Do not wait for the infrastructure work. Instead, amend Encounter (and, symmetrically, Account and potentially AppointmentHistory/EncounterHistory where relevant) to give implementers a resource-level escape hatch without touching core cardinality:

1. Define a standard, normatively-registered extension such as `http://hl7.org/fhir/StructureDefinition/encounter-participant-source` whose value is a `Reference(CareTeam | List | Group)`. Encounter keeps `participant` as-is, but a server MAY populate only the *currently-active* participants inline and point to the referenced resource for the full list. This is equivalent to Option 1 from the original ticket (allow `CareTeam` as an `actor`) but avoids polluting the `actor` choice set and avoids an R6 breaking change.
2. Add a short page note on `Encounter.participant` and `Encounter.location` stating that when the extension is present, inline occurrences SHOULD be interpreted as the currently-active set, and that a client wanting the historical list MUST resolve the referenced CareTeam/List/Group.
3. Keep FHIR-52912 open; when/if the FHIR-I `$filter`/`$add`/`$remove` operations land, Encounter gets a second, more granular mechanism.

No change to EncounterHistory is required for R6; the extension is optional, backwards-compatible, and can be deprecated if FHIR-52912 ships something better.

#### Justification

Delivers value in R6 without waiting on the incubator. Aligns with Paul Church's position on the 2023 WGM thread ("solve it narrowly for List/Group/CareTeam and have Encounter reference out"), which was explicitly endorsed by Brian Postlethwaite in the same thread. It avoids Option 1's breakage (adding `CareTeam` to the `actor` choice set would ripple through conformance resources and implementer tooling). It also sidesteps Option 2's timing problem — moving historical participants onto EncounterHistory requires EncounterHistory to be mature enough and ubiquitous enough to hold that data, which is a bigger bet than R6 currently justifies. The trade-off versus Disposition A is that implementers see a concrete mechanism today, at the cost of one new extension and a small bit of Encounter page text.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-37648 as *Not Persuasive* for R6 core changes, on the grounds that (a) the problem is cross-resource, not Encounter-specific — `CodeSystem.concept`, `Questionnaire.item`, `Group.member`, `List.entry`, and `Account.coverage` all share it; (b) the infrastructure work has moved into the API Incubator per Gino Canessa and Grahame Grieve on 2025-10-13; and (c) no single Encounter-local change (allowing CareTeam as `actor`, moving to EncounterHistory, adding PA-specific query params) fully answers the detection/retrieval/update triad captured in FHIR-52912. Direct implementers to the existing `_elements`, `_summary`, PATCH, and `Operations for Large Resources` tooling, and track the real work under FHIR-52912 and the incubator.

#### Justification

Every Encounter-local fix considered since 2022 either "moves the problem" (CareTeam reference) or is a thin wrapper over functionality that FHIR-I has declared out-of-scope for R6 normative. Committing Encounter to a specific mechanism now risks conflicting with whatever the incubator produces. Declining here is defensible because the ticket has already captured its analysis in the resolution notes, the label `MovingToGuide` signals the work is non-normative, and FHIR-52912 exists precisely to carry the open infrastructure questions.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

PA has already voted this ticket *Persuasive* with a `MovingToGuide` label, and the 2025 Zulip exchanges confirm that the underlying infrastructure (FHIR-52912, operations-for-large-resources) will not be normative-ready for R6. Disposition A formalises what PA has been converging on for three years — publish guidance, point at the FHIR-I operations, and wait for the incubator — without committing Encounter to a specific mechanism that may conflict with whatever FHIR-I ultimately ships. Disposition B is tempting and would give implementers a concrete escape hatch today, but it creates a second way to express participant history that may be obsoleted by FHIR-52912, and Brian Postlethwaite's Oct 2025 observation that PATCH still needs a prior full GET means the extension alone does not solve the *update* problem either. Disposition C understates how much consensus already exists in the PA minutes and throws away useful guidance. The workgroup should ratify A, keep FHIR-52912 as the formal dependency, and revisit Encounter once the API Incubator produces a recommendation — at which point Disposition B can be re-opened as an R7 refinement if needed.
