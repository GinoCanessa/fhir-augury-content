## Summary

The ticket proposes restructuring the NDH `base-ext-rating` extension so
that the details of a rating (system, type, value, last update) are
carried directly on the top-level extension instead of being nested in a
separate `rating-details` sub-extension. It originated from the NDH
public call on 2024-09-16 and reflects feedback that the nested
extension-within-extension shape is harder to author than needed and
that the accompanying example is too thin to illustrate intent. During
ticket triage the working group pivoted: consensus emerged that the
nesting **is** useful (it cleanly separates the "headline" rating from
the constituent component ratings, e.g., CMS Care Compare overall stars
vs. safety / readmission / patient-experience sub-scores), but two
concrete changes are still warranted:

1. Provide a worked example that exercises the nested structure (the
   reporter ultimately supplied a CMS Care Compare hospital-overall
   example with nested component ratings in a 2026-02-23 comment).
2. In `ext-rating-detail`, combine `ratingSystem` and `ratingType` into
   a single `CodeableConcept` bound to an example `ValueSet`, rather
   than keeping them as two separate URL-typed sub-extensions.

No explicitly linked Jira tickets were surfaced in cross-references.
The only cross-reference is a Zulip agenda post for the 2026 January
WGM listing FHIR-48638 among tickets to discuss. No related GitHub

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Flatten `StructureDefinition/base-ext-rating` in the NDH IG so that the
rating's system, type, value, and last-update live directly on the
top-level extension (per Ming Dunajick's 2024-12-03 comment):

- `ratingSystem` — `valueUrl`, 1..1
- `ratingType` — `valueString`, 1..1
- `ratingValue` — `valueString | valueQuantity`, 0..1
- `ratingLastUpdate` — `valueString | valueDateTime`, 0..1

Remove the nested `rating-details` sub-extension. Refresh the NDH
`Organization` rating example to exercise the new flat shape.

#### Justification

This is what the original ticket literally requests ("clubbed into the
original extension instead of nested extensions"). Flat extensions are
simpler for implementers to author and validate, avoid
extension-within-extension traversal in tooling that handles it poorly,
and align with the common NDH pattern of one concept per extension URL.

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the nested structure of `base-ext-rating` (headline
`ratingType` + `ratingValue` on the parent, with 0..* nested
`rating-details` for component/sub-scores) and make two targeted
improvements:

1. In the nested `ext-rating-detail`, collapse the separate
   `ratingSystem` (URL) and `ratingType` (URL) sub-extensions into a
   single `ratingType` of type `CodeableConcept` bound (example
   strength) to a new NDH `ValueSet` seeded with CMS Care Compare
   component codes (e.g., `safety`, `readmission`,
   `patient-experience`). Retain `ratingValue` (string or Quantity) and
   add `ratingLastUpdate` (dateTime, 0..1) on the nested element.
2. Add a worked Organization example (the "Example Hospital" CMS Care
   Compare payload supplied by Ming Dunajick on 2026-02-23) as a
   concrete FSH/JSON instance in the IG so readers can see the nested
   form in use.

Document on the extension page that nesting is intended for composite
ratings where a single headline score is decomposed into component
scores published by the same rating system.

#### Justification

The Jira discussion explicitly converges on this outcome: John
Moehrke's 2026-01-26 resolution note states "nesting is useful" and
asks specifically to combine system+type into a `CodeableConcept` with
an example ValueSet, and Ming Dunajick's 2026-02-23 CMS Care Compare
example only makes sense with a parent + `rating-details` shape. A
fully flat extension (Disposition A) cannot represent a composite
rating and its components without repeating the parent extension once
per component and losing the "these are parts of the same headline
rating" grouping. Collapsing `ratingSystem`+`ratingType` into a
`CodeableConcept` also lets implementers use standard terminology
tooling (expansion, validation, mapping) instead of bespoke URL
handling. The resolution value on the ticket is already "Persuasive",
and this proposal matches the actual consensus captured in the
comments rather than the original text.

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the structural change. Leave `base-ext-rating` and
`ext-rating-detail` as currently published in the NDH IG, and address
the ticket solely by adding a richer Organization rating example to the
IG narrative — no StructureDefinition edits, no ValueSet additions.

#### Justification

The NDH rating extension is not known to be widely implemented against
published instances yet, but any shape change is a breaking change for
early adopters and test servers. The reporter's underlying concern is
readability, which can be fully addressed by a better example alone.
This is the least disruptive option if the WG decides the benefits of
Disposition B do not clear the bar for an in-flight IG change.

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The Jira thread reaches explicit agreement that the nested structure is
useful and that the concrete improvement needed is (1) a real example
and (2) collapsing `ratingSystem`+`ratingType` into a `CodeableConcept`
in `ext-rating-detail`. The reporter has already supplied a usable CMS
Care Compare example, and the reviewer has signaled the ticket is ready
to move forward. Disposition A would walk back that consensus and lose
the ability to express composite ratings (headline + components) in a
single coherent structure, which is the dominant real-world shape for
healthcare quality ratings. Disposition C leaves known design smells
(parallel URL-typed `ratingSystem` + `ratingType` sub-extensions) in
place and fails to capture the agreed-upon `CodeableConcept`
refactor. Disposition B is narrowly scoped, implements the
already-expressed consensus, and delivers the tangible artifacts
(example + ValueSet) that the reviewer asked for before closing.
