## Summary

The reporter (Craig Newman) flags an apparent copy/paste error in the
Terminology section (2.3.1) of the Real Time Location Services (RTLS) FHIR
Implementation Guide. As written, the **Subscriber** glossary entry is
identical to the **RTLS** entry ("the acronym for Real Time Location System,
which is a server/software that captures, processes, and stores information
about the location of tags"), which cannot be correct. The reporter further
suspects that what is currently labelled the **Subject** definition ("the
system that is 'subscribed' to … receive updates from the RTLS") was actually
intended to be the definition of **Subscriber**, which would also leave the
true definition of **Subject** missing or unclear. The ticket asks the work
group to correct the definitions so that RTLS, Subject, and Subscriber are
each defined accurately and unambiguously. No related Jira tickets, Zulip
discussions, or GitHub items were surfaced by the cross-reference index for
this ticket.

## Proposed Dispositions

> Note: with no GitHub repository cross-references, the dispositions below
> reference the RTLS IG narrative section (2.3.1 Terminology) directly
> rather than naming a specific authoring path. If/when this work is
> picked up, the editor should locate section 2.3.1 in the RTLS IG source
> (typically the IG's `input/pagecontent/` narrative pages) before
> applying the change.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Replace the obviously-duplicated **Subscriber** definition in RTLS IG
section 2.3.1 (Terminology) so that the three terms read consistently and
each describes a distinct concept. Concretely:

- **RTLS** — keep as-is: "The acronym for Real Time Location System, which
  is a server/software that captures, processes, and stores information
  about the location of tags."
- **Subject** — restate as the entity *being tracked* (i.e., the person,
  device, or asset that carries a tag and whose location is reported by
  the RTLS), removing the current "subscribed to" wording that conflates
  it with Subscriber.
- **Subscriber** — restate as "The system that is subscribed to, or
  wants to receive updates from, the RTLS about information pertaining
  to the location/status of tags." (i.e., the text that is currently
  mislabelled as the Subject definition).

#### Justification

This matches the reporter's diagnosis exactly: the current text appears to
be a copy/paste accident, and reassigning the existing prose to the right
glossary terms (and writing a clean definition for Subject) is the
minimum-risk fix. It preserves the IG's existing terminology surface so
that any downstream references to "Subscriber" in the rest of the IG
continue to resolve to the same concept, and it avoids deeper structural
changes to the IG narrative on what is fundamentally a documentation bug.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Restructure section 2.3.1 to drop the **Subscriber** term entirely and
re-anchor the IG's terminology on FHIR-native concepts (e.g., the
Subscription resource and its `subscriber`/notification model, plus a
clear definition of the tracked **Subject**). The glossary would then
list:

- **RTLS** — unchanged.
- **Subject** — the entity (person, device, or asset) being tracked by
  the RTLS, identified via a FHIR resource (typically `Patient`,
  `Device`, `Location`, or similar).
- *(remove "Subscriber" from the glossary)* — and instead, where the IG
  currently leans on a "Subscriber" actor, refer directly to the FHIR
  `Subscription` mechanism and its associated endpoints.

This is, in effect, what the recorded resolution ("We updated section
2.3.1 to no longer include the 'Subscriber' concept") already does.

#### Justification

The original "Subscriber" definition was effectively a custom IG-level
actor that overlaps with the well-defined FHIR Subscription framework.
Removing it avoids inventing parallel terminology, reduces the chance of
future drift between the IG glossary and FHIR core semantics, and makes
the Subject/Subscriber distinction unambiguous because there is no
longer a near-homophone pair in the glossary. The trade-off vs.
Disposition A is that any other RTLS IG narrative or artifact that
referenced "Subscriber" must be updated too, which is a wider edit but a
cleaner long-term result. This is consistent with what the work group
has already recorded as the "Persuasive with Modification" resolution.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline to make any change, on the basis that the IG is normative around
its resources and operations rather than its narrative glossary, and the
intent of the three terms can be inferred from surrounding text and from
FHIR's own Subscription mechanism.

#### Justification

This is the weakest of the three options: it is defensible only on a
"glossary text is non-normative" reading. The current text is
self-contradictory (two terms share an identical definition), which is
hard to defend as a deliberate editorial choice and will continue to
generate ballot comments and reader confusion. Declining would also
contradict the work group's existing Jira resolution
("Persuasive with Modification"), which already commits to changing
section 2.3.1.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The work group has already recorded the resolution as *Persuasive with
Modification* and noted that section 2.3.1 was updated to no longer
include the "Subscriber" concept. That outcome is essentially
Disposition B — a structural cleanup that drops the duplicated term
rather than just patching the prose in place (Disposition A). Disposition
B is also the more defensible long-term choice because it stops the IG
from defining a custom "Subscriber" actor that overlaps with FHIR's
Subscription framework, eliminating the very near-homophone pair
("Subject" / "Subscriber") that produced the original confusion.
Disposition A remains a viable fallback if, on review, the work group
discovers that other parts of the RTLS IG still rely on a distinct
"Subscriber" actor and removing the term would require more rewriting
than is practical for this ballot cycle.
