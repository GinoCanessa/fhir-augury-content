## Summary

The ticket asks the Interoperable Digital Identity and Patient Matching
(FHIR) IG to add explicit guidance on methods for **reestablishing account
access** when credentials are lost or forgotten. The reporter suggests two
candidate workflows to be explored: (1) repeating identity proofing to
verify consistency with previously verified demographics, and (2)
demonstrating control of the email address and mobile number previously
associated with the credential. The ticket was originally dispositioned
"Considered for Future Use" during STU2 processing (vote 8-0-0 on
2025-06-17) at the Identity chairs' recommendation, and then re-opened
post-STU2 publication on 2025-12-10 so the guidance can be carried into
the next version. A substantive comment from Julie Maas proposes concrete
replacement text for a new paragraph 2 in section 1.1 that situates
account-access reestablishment alongside credential assignment and
credential step-up, anchoring all three on the IG's requirement that
identity verification events always collect an email address and mobile
number. No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items
were returned for this ticket.

### juliemaas (2025-04-11)

These methods are well established in NIST Digital Identity Guidelines,
though we could help with some simply-worded guidance that serves as a
reminder, includes the workflows suggested above, and also provides some
background on the over-arching rationale for the IG's use of identity
assurance in a new paragraph 2 of section 1.1. Proposed text (summary):

- Implementers will encounter two main identity-management / patient-
  matching scenarios. The guide sets a minimum identity assurance bar
  (IDIAL1.5) for business-to-business matching that returns PII/PHI to
  HIPAA Covered Entities or Business Associates whose input data meets
  the same bar, and a higher minimum (IDIAL1.8) when data is returned
  to anyone else, particularly a patient or consumer.
- The B2B bar approximates NIST 800-63-2 Level 2; the consumer-facing
  bar originates in current NIST Digital Identity Guidelines and
  requires high confidence the unique individual is present in the
  transaction, reflecting health-data privacy law around authorization
  decisions.
- NIST Digital Identity Guidelines require CSPs to protect accounts
  with AAL ≥ IAL; the lowest level at which proofing occurs is IAL2,
  and disclosure of personal info from a proofed account requires at
  least AAL2. The IG therefore requires at least IDIAL1.8 + AAL2 for
  PII/PHI returned to a consumer. IDIAL1.8 allows some flexibility vs.
  NIST IAL2 in acceptable evidence but emphasizes notification as a
  compensating control; NIST's proposed IAL2 revisions in the second
  public draft of Digital Identity Guidelines v4 would largely meet
  IDIAL1.8 with some exceptions (e.g., more stringent notification).
- The well-defined identity-management concepts of (1) reestablishing
  account access after lost or forgotten credentials, (2) assigning
  credentials to the appropriate individual for portal or API access,
  and (3) stepping up credentials to a higher IAL/AAL all require
  similar practices. To support all three, the IG's identity
  verification events always collect an email address and mobile
  number. Consumer Match guidance recommends the minimum match input
  data and a minimum identity assurance for each data element used in
  these use cases.
- Implementers are encouraged to perform a risk analysis before
  implementing identity-management practices, as higher identity and
  authentication assurance than this guide establishes may be
  appropriate in some use cases or community contexts.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt Julie Maas's proposed text (as summarized above) as the new
paragraph 2 of section 1.1 of the Interoperable Digital Identity and
Patient Matching (FHIR) IG, **and** add a dedicated normative/guidance
subsection that spells out two concrete workflows for reestablishing
account access:

1. **Repeat proofing.** The user re-presents identity evidence
   sufficient to re-achieve their current IDIAL (at least IDIAL1.5 for
   B2B-managed identities, at least IDIAL1.8 for consumer-facing
   accounts), and the Consumer Match minimum-data rules are applied to
   verify consistency with the previously verified demographics held
   for that account.
2. **Control of recovery factors.** The user demonstrates control of
   **both** the email address and mobile number captured during the
   original identity verification event, using a time-bounded, single-
   use out-of-band code on each channel. This workflow SHALL be
   permitted only when the account already meets the IG's minimum
   IDIAL for its audience and SHALL be paired with an IG-required
   notification to the account holder.

Cross-link the new subsection to the existing credential-assignment and
credential step-up guidance and to the Consumer Match minimum-data
guidance so implementers see all three scenarios as variants of the
same identity-management pattern.

#### Justification

This is what the ticket literally asks for and what the substantive
comment from the IG's author concretely drafted. The workflows are
already well established in the NIST Digital Identity Guidelines, so
the IG is not inventing new security primitives; it is harmonizing its
own guidance so implementers do not have to infer account-recovery
behavior from the credential-assignment text. Framing the subsection
around the IG's existing IDIAL1.5 / IDIAL1.8 levels and its existing
requirement to collect email and mobile during proofing keeps the
addition internally consistent, and the pairing with the IG's
notification-as-compensating-control posture preserves the rationale
that distinguishes IDIAL1.8 from NIST IAL2.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than inscribing specific account-recovery workflows in the IG,
add a **shorter, non-normative** paragraph 2 to section 1.1 that:

- States that reestablishing account access is within the IG's scope
  as a variant of identity management alongside credential assignment
  and credential step-up;
- Requires that any recovery flow re-establish the account's original
  IDIAL (IDIAL1.5 or IDIAL1.8, as applicable) and satisfy the IG's
  notification requirement;
- Points implementers to NIST SP 800-63B §6 (Account Recovery) as the
  authoritative source for acceptable mechanisms, rather than
  restating them in the IG.

Leave the specific workflows (repeat proofing vs. email+mobile
possession) out of the IG normative text, but keep Julie Maas's
background paragraphs about IDIAL1.5/1.8 vs. NIST IAL2 and the
rationale for notification-as-compensating-control.

#### Justification

The IG's stated value-add is the IDIAL1.5 / IDIAL1.8 framework and
Consumer Match minimum-data rules, not a re-litigation of NIST account-
recovery mechanics. Prescribing specific recovery workflows risks (a)
drifting out of sync with NIST SP 800-63B Rev. 4 once finalized, (b)
creating subtle conflicts with issuer-specific recovery policies, and
(c) expanding the IG's testable surface area in ways the Identity
chairs flagged when they originally moved the ticket to "Considered
for Future Use." Anchoring the requirement on "re-establish the
original IDIAL" and on the existing notification requirement preserves
the IG's guarantees without pinning it to mechanisms that may evolve.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark FHIR-47702 as **Not Persuasive** / **Considered for Future Use
(Confirmed)**: do not add account-recovery guidance to the IG at this
time. The IG already (a) requires collection of email and mobile
number during identity proofing, (b) defines IDIAL1.5 and IDIAL1.8,
and (c) requires AAL ≥ IAL per NIST. Any conforming recovery flow is
already bounded by those constraints plus NIST SP 800-63B §6, which
is cited by reference. Revisit only if implementer feedback shows
concrete interoperability problems.

#### Justification

The ticket was originally dispositioned "Considered for Future Use"
(vote 8-0-0) at the Identity chairs' recommendation, and the re-open
is procedural (to carry it into the post-STU2 cycle) rather than new
substantive evidence that the gap is causing interoperability harm.
Account recovery is well covered by NIST SP 800-63B; duplicating that
material risks version drift and creates a testable-statement burden
without a corresponding use case from implementers. Declining is
defensible so long as the IG's existing text makes clear that NIST
account-recovery rules apply and that any recovery flow must
re-establish the account's original IDIAL.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the best starting point for workgroup discussion. The
reporter has identified a genuine documentation gap — implementers
reading the IG today have to infer account-recovery behavior from the
credential-assignment and step-up text — and Julie Maas's comment
shows the IG's own author believes a short explanatory addition to
section 1.1 is warranted. However, the Identity chairs' original
instinct (which led to the 8-0-0 "Considered for Future Use" vote)
was well founded: enumerating specific recovery workflows inside the
IG risks diverging from NIST SP 800-63B Rev. 4 as it finalizes, and
expands the IG's normative surface area beyond its IDIAL / Consumer
Match core. Disposition B captures the clarifying framing from Julie's
proposed paragraph 2, binds any recovery flow to the IG's existing
IDIAL + notification guarantees, and defers mechanism details to NIST
by reference — giving implementers the pointer they need without
committing the IG to mechanism-level text that may need to be revised
as NIST v4 lands. Disposition A remains a reasonable fallback if the
workgroup decides the IG should be prescriptive about recovery
workflows in STU3; Disposition C should be reserved for the case
where the workgroup concludes the original 2025-06-17 disposition
still stands on the merits.
