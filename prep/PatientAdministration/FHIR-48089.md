## Summary

This ticket, raised against the Interoperable Digital Identity and Patient
Matching (IDPM) FHIR IG during STU2 ballot, asks the Patient Administration
work group to review the TSC-approved
[Terminology Guidance for IG Authors](https://confluence.hl7.org/display/TSMG/Terminology+Expectations+for+IG+Developers)
and either anchor/move the IG-defined CodeSystem(s) to
[terminology.hl7.org](http://terminology.hl7.org/) (THO) or formally request
an exemption from TSMG when the CodeSystem is judged tightly bound to the IG.
Workgroup discussion (3/6/2025 and 4/3/2025 public calls) confirmed the WG
agrees with the policy and is actively working toward THO alignment for the
locally-maintained CodeSystem; an interim renaming to
"HL7-Identity-Person-Identifier" was floated. The ticket was previously
resolved as "Considered for Future Use" (Postlethwaite/Lee, 4-0-1) and then
re-opened post STU2 publication on 2025-12-10 so that the THO-anchoring or
exemption work can be tracked through to completion. There are no
cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items recorded in
FhirAugury for this issue.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Move the IG-defined CodeSystem(s) currently published under the IDPM IG
canonical (e.g., the candidate `HL7-Identity-Person-Identifier` CodeSystem
referenced in the 3/6/2025 discussion) into terminology.hl7.org. Concretely:

1. Submit the CodeSystem(s) to TSMG via the THO submission process,
   re-homing the canonical URL to a `http://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/...`
   namespace.
2. In the IG, replace the locally-defined CodeSystem resources with
   references to the THO-published canonicals (and remove the local
   `CodeSystem` resources from the IG sources once THO publishes).
3. Update any ValueSets, ConceptMaps, and bindings in the IG to point at
   the THO canonical URLs.
4. Coordinate with David Pyke (THO steward) on naming, content review, and
   the publication slot.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the TSC-approved Terminology Guidance for IG Authors
requires for HL7-published IGs, and it matches the WG's stated direction
in both the 3/6/2025 and 4/3/2025 calls ("we agree with this ticket … we
are working towards this goal this year"). Anchoring the CodeSystem in
THO gives it the cross-IG visibility, governance, and version stewardship
that TSC policy mandates and prevents the IDPM IG from carrying
terminology that other IGs (e.g., other identity / patient-matching
profiles) may also need to reuse. The 12/10/2025 re-open explicitly asks
the WG to keep this open until the move is completed rather than parking
it as Considered for Future Use.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Request a formal **exemption** from TSMG for the IG-defined CodeSystem(s)
on the basis that they are tightly bound to the IDPM IG semantics and
cannot be reused outside its context. Concretely:

1. Document the rationale (tight binding to IDPM-specific identity
   semantics, no expected cross-IG reuse) in a TSMG exemption request
   posted to the TSMG Zulip channel and confirmed at a TSMG meeting.
2. If the exemption is granted, leave the CodeSystem(s) in the IG's
   canonical namespace but add an explicit note in the IG's
   "Terminology" page citing the exemption, the date granted, and the
   TSMG decision reference.
3. Resolve this ticket as "Persuasive with Modification" describing the
   exemption path actually taken.

#### Justification

TSC policy explicitly allows for exemptions where a CodeSystem is
"tightly bound to the IG", and the policy referenced in the ticket
description documents the request channel. This option is preferable to
Disposition A only if the WG concludes — after reviewing THO — that the
CodeSystem's concepts are genuinely IG-specific and would not benefit
from THO governance. Trade-offs vs. Disposition A: avoids the THO
submission and re-homing work and preserves the existing IG canonical
URLs (no implementer churn), but it forecloses cross-IG reuse and leaves
the WG accountable for ongoing terminology stewardship the THO would
otherwise carry. The 3/6/2025 discussion did not signal that the WG sees
the CodeSystem as tightly bound; on the contrary, the WG indicated it
intends to move it.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket "Not Persuasive" on the basis that compliance with the
TSC Terminology Guidance is a publication-process obligation rather than
a balloted spec change and is therefore out of scope for a ballot
disposition. Track the THO-migration / exemption work in a separate
work-group action item rather than as a Jira change request.

#### Justification

The ticket does not propose a substantive change to the IDPM normative or
informative content — it points the WG at a TSC policy and asks for
process compliance. A purely process-oriented obligation could
reasonably be discharged outside the ballot reconciliation pipeline.
However, this rationale is weak given that (a) the ticket was a balloted
comment (label `ballot`, `block-vote-3`) and ballot comments are owed a
substantive disposition, and (b) the 12/10/2025 re-open was explicitly
intended to keep this visible *as a ticket* until completed. Decline is
therefore defensible only if the WG migrates the work to a tracked
non-Jira action item with a named owner and target publication.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The work group has already agreed in two public calls (3/6/2025 and
4/3/2025) that the CodeSystem should move to THO and that they are
actively working on it. The 12/10/2025 re-open by lynn_laakso, which
reverted the earlier "Considered for Future Use" resolution, signals the
WG wants this tracked through completion rather than deferred. There is
no recorded discussion suggesting the CodeSystem is tightly bound to
IDPM in a way that would justify a TSMG exemption (Disposition B), and
declining (Disposition C) would conflict with the explicit re-open
intent. The recommended path is therefore Accept As Requested: confirm
the candidate CodeSystem(s), engage David Pyke / TSMG on the THO
submission (using `HL7-Identity-Person-Identifier` or an equivalent
agreed name), and update the IG canonicals to the THO-published URLs in
the next IDPM publication. If, during THO review, the concepts turn out
to be genuinely IG-bound, the WG can pivot to Disposition B without
losing work.
