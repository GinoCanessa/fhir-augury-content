## Summary

The reporter (Jose Costa-Teixeira) observes that with the addition of the
`DeviceAssociation` resource, the legacy `implantStatus` concept that lives
in the FHIR Extensions Pack (the `device-implantStatus` extension and its
companion ValueSet at `http://hl7.org/fhir/ValueSet/implantStatus`) is no
longer used the way it was originally intended, and that
`DeviceAssociation.status` does not currently carry the right semantics to
replace it cleanly. The ticket asks the workgroup to review whether the
extension and its ValueSet should be deprecated outright, retained as-is,
or realigned with however `DeviceAssociation` ultimately models implant
state.

The issue is not standalone: a cluster of more recent O&O tickets has
been refining `DeviceAssociation` itself — most directly
[FHIR-48532](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48532) (the required VS
mixed association status with record status), [FHIR-46465](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-46465)
(the binding only fits implanted/attached devices), and
[FHIR-48713](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48713) (adding
`abandoned`), all of which have been Applied with "Persuasive with
Modification". Two open companion tickets,
[FHIR-54971](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54971) and
[FHIR-54463](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54463), are still working
out how `status`, `associationStatus`, `availabilityStatus`, and
`statusReason` should split on `DeviceAssociation`. Separately,
[FHIR-47270](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-47270) flags that the
extension-pack `ImplantStatus` ValueSet/CodeSystem are duplicated in THO
under different URLs. The most recent comments on FHIR-29776 itself
(2025-08, Marti Velezis and Lloyd McKenzie) explicitly say "needs
discussion alongside the other DeviceAssociation tickets" and "I think
DeviceAssociation now covers this", which is the disposition seam this
review needs to settle.

## Related Jira Tickets

### [FHIR-48532: Unclear purpose of DeviceAssociation.status](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48532)

Reports that the required VS for `DeviceAssociation.status` mixed
association status (`implanted`, `explanted`, `attached`) with record
status (`entered-in-error`), and is missing values needed for non-
implant devices (e.g., a bed or wheelchair) while not being extensible.
Resolved Persuasive with Modification (Applied) — directly motivates the
broader rework of `DeviceAssociation.status` that FHIR-29776 is now
asking us to align `implantStatus` against.

Reporter: Javier Espina; Assignee: Unassigned.

### [FHIR-46465: DeviceAssociation.status terminology binding does not reflect the scope of the resource](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-46465)

Argues that `DeviceAssociation.status` has a required binding that only
fits implanted/attached-to-patient devices, even though the resource's
scope covers all kinds of devices, and asks for a more generic VS or a
weaker binding strength. Resolved Persuasive with Modification
(Applied) — establishes that `DeviceAssociation.status` is being
relaxed/generalised, which is precisely the change that makes a
distinct `implantStatus` extension either redundant or, conversely,
still useful as a narrow implant-specific signal.

Reporter: Ardon Toonstra; Assignee: Unassigned.

### [FHIR-54971: DeviceAssociation updates to address association status and relationship types](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54971)

Active follow-on to FHIR-48532/FHIR-46465: needs `status`,
`associationStatus`, and `availabilityStatus` to be made distinct
(noting `availabilityStatus` may overlap with `associationStatus`),
and asks that CodeSystems/ValueSets be moved to THO if they are not
examples. Status Triaged. Most relevant peer to FHIR-29776 — the
disposition for `implantStatus` should be decided in the same pass as
this one because both turn on the same status-element split.

Reporter: Marti Velezis; Assignee: Unassigned.

### [FHIR-54463: Ensure DeviceAssociation relationship/status/statusReason/associationStatus element quality](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54463)

Calls out the overlap among `relationship`, `status`, `statusReason`,
and `associationStatus` and asks for clear, distinct definitions and
appropriately scoped bindings. Also asks how `relationship` (e.g.,
`implanted` ↔ `explanted`) interacts with `period`. Status Triaged.
Provides the conceptual framing the workgroup needs in order to decide
whether implant/explant lifecycle belongs on `relationship`,
`associationStatus`, or in an extension.

Reporter: Elliot Silver; Assignee: Unassigned.

### [FHIR-48713: Expand DeviceAssociation-status](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48713)

Adds `abandoned` to support CIEDs (e.g., leads or implantable loop
recorders left in the body but no longer in use). Resolved Persuasive
with Modification (Applied). Demonstrates that the working group has
already been adding implant-lifecycle codes directly to
`DeviceAssociation` rather than to the legacy extension-pack
`implantStatus` VS — i.e., precedent points toward consolidating on
`DeviceAssociation`.

Reporter: Andy Iverson; Assignee: Unassigned.

### [FHIR-47270: Duplication of code systems and value sets with THO (using different URLs)](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-47270)

Catalogues extension-pack VS/CS that are duplicated in THO at different
URLs, listing `ValueSet ImplantStatus` and `CodeSystem ImplantStatus`
explicitly (extension-pack URL `http://hl7.org/fhir/ValueSet/implantStatus`
vs THO URL `http://terminology.hl7.org/ValueSet/implantStatus`). Status
Triaged (Persuasive). Owned by Terminology Infrastructure but materially
constrains FHIR-29776: any "keep but realign" disposition has to
reconcile the two URLs, and any deprecation should also retire the
extension-pack copies.

Reporter: Bas van den Heuvel; Assignee: Unassigned.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Deprecate the `device-implantStatus` extension and its associated
ValueSet/CodeSystem in the FHIR Extensions Pack. Concretely:

1. On the `device-implantStatus` StructureDefinition, set
   `status = retired` and add a `standards-status` of `deprecated`,
   plus a `replaces`/comment pointing to `DeviceAssociation.status`
   (and, once FHIR-54971/FHIR-54463 land, to whichever of
   `associationStatus` / `relationship` carries the implant-lifecycle
   semantics).
2. Mark `ValueSet/implantStatus` and `CodeSystem/implantStatus` in the
   extension pack as `retired` and add a `replacedBy` note pointing to
   the THO copy referenced by FHIR-47270
   (`http://terminology.hl7.org/ValueSet/implantStatus` /
   `http://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/implantStatus`).
3. Add migration guidance in the extension's narrative explaining that
   implementers should record implant lifecycle on
   `DeviceAssociation`, with a worked example for `implanted` →
   `explanted` → `abandoned`.

#### Justification

Aligns with the explicit direction from Lloyd McKenzie ("Device
Association now covers this") and Marti Velezis on the ticket itself,
and with the trajectory of the Applied tickets FHIR-48532, FHIR-46465,
and especially FHIR-48713 (which added `abandoned` directly to
`DeviceAssociation` rather than to the extension-pack VS). It also
discharges the THO-duplication concern raised by FHIR-47270 in a clean
direction (retire the extension-pack copy, keep THO). Retirement (as
opposed to deletion) preserves the URL for existing R4/R4B
implementers flagged in the Zulip implementers thread.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — Retain and Realign Against the Reworked DeviceAssociation

#### Proposal

Do **not** retire the extension yet. Instead, treat FHIR-29776 as
blocked on the in-flight `DeviceAssociation` rework
(FHIR-54971, FHIR-54463) and resolve it by realigning rather than
removing:

1. Wait until `DeviceAssociation.status` /
   `.associationStatus` / `.availabilityStatus` /
   `.statusReason` are split per FHIR-54971's resolution.
2. Re-scope the `device-implantStatus` extension to a narrower,
   clearly-defined "implant lifecycle stage" semantic that complements
   (rather than overlaps with) `DeviceAssociation.associationStatus` —
   e.g., the distinction Hans Buitendijk drew on the ticket: a device
   can be implanted (association status) but not functional (implant
   status). Tighten the extension's definition and short text
   accordingly.
3. Re-bind the extension to the THO copy of the `implantStatus` VS
   (resolving the FHIR-47270 duplication in the same change), and
   review whether the value set itself needs `abandoned` and any other
   codes added by FHIR-48713.
4. Add an explicit "Use this extension only when …" usage note that
   directs implementers to `DeviceAssociation` for the common cases.

#### Justification

Honours Hans Buitendijk's reading that implant status and association
status are not the same concept and that the extension may still earn
its keep for the "implanted but not functional" case that
`DeviceAssociation` arguably under-models. Avoids a breaking-shaped
change for early adopters (especially R4B implementers per the Zulip
note) while still cleaning up the THO-URL split. The cost is the
disposition cannot be finalised independently — it is logically gated
on the resolutions of FHIR-54971 and FHIR-54463, and risks leaving an
extension whose niche shrinks to near-zero once
`DeviceAssociation.associationStatus` plus `availabilityStatus` (or
the equivalent) are in place.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (Not Persuasive — No Action Until DeviceAssociation Stabilises)

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-29776 as Not Persuasive at this time, on the grounds that
(a) the request is conditional ("…depending on how the element is
handled in DeviceAssociation"), (b) `DeviceAssociation` is still being
reworked under FHIR-54971 and FHIR-54463, and (c) any decision made
now would need to be revisited when those tickets resolve. Revisit
once those tickets are Applied.

#### Justification

The ticket itself frames the deprecation as conditional on a
DeviceAssociation outcome that does not yet exist, and acting in
either direction (A or B) without that outcome risks a churned
disposition. Defensible if the workgroup wants to keep the extension
pack stable and avoid coordination overhead with TI (FHIR-47270) until
the dependent O&O tickets land. The downside is that this ticket has
already been open since 2020-11 with the same "needs alignment with
DeviceAssociation" framing in 2021, 2025-05, 2025-08, and Triaged
status — declining without action effectively ratifies four more
years of drift between the extension pack and `DeviceAssociation`.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A (Accept As Requested — deprecate the
extension and its VS/CS, with a `replaces` pointer to
`DeviceAssociation`).

The most recent on-ticket signal (Lloyd McKenzie 2025-08, Marti Velezis
2025-08) is that `DeviceAssociation` is the right home for this
concept, and the workgroup has been demonstrating that view in code by
adding implant-lifecycle codes (`abandoned`, FHIR-48713) directly to
`DeviceAssociation` rather than to the extension-pack VS. Disposition
B's "narrow the extension to non-functional-implant" niche is real but
small, and FHIR-54971 explicitly intends to introduce
`availabilityStatus` on `DeviceAssociation` — which would absorb that
niche too. Disposition C is the safest procedurally but has been the
de-facto status for years and has not produced a resolution. A staged
deprecation (retire-with-replacedBy, not delete) discharges the
duplication concern from FHIR-47270 in the same motion, gives existing
R4/R4B implementers a stable URL, and removes the long-standing
ambiguity flagged by the ticket. The disposition should be coordinated
with — but not blocked by — FHIR-54971 and FHIR-54463 so that the
`replaces` pointer can name the correct successor element when those
are finalised.
