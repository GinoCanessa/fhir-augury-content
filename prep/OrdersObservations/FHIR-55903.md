## Summary

FHIR-55903 is a follow-on editorial / structural ticket to
[FHIR-55629](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-55629) ("Combine + Expand on Guidance for
Sharing Information"), which was already resolved *Persuasive with Modification* and
established a single consolidated "Sharing Content" page in the Clinical Order Workflow
(COW) FHIR IG. FHIR-55629 set up the page with sections covering Sharing Inputs,
Requesting Additional Information, Sharing Intermediate Outputs, Sharing Outputs, Effect
of Intermediaries, and Access Controls; FHIR-55903 now refines the **section headings**
on that page and adjusts the **prose on the Tracking Progress page** to make the
intent of each section more concrete and clinically grounded.

Specifically, the reporter asks the workgroup to:

1. Rename "Sharing Inputs" → **"Sharing inputs at time of ordering"**.
2. Replace the "Requesting Additional Information" heading with **"Sharing additional
   information about a clinical order"**, broken into two sub-sections:
   *Provided by placer unsolicited* and *Request for additional information by fulfiller*.
3. Rename "Sharing Intermediate Outputs" → **"Sharing outcomes of clinical orders"**.
4. Rename "Preliminary Results, Addenda, and Updates with FHIR Servers" →
   **"Preliminary or Intermediate Results, Addenda, and Updates with FHIR Servers"**.
5. On the Tracking Progress page, replace the current single-sentence statement that
   "progress is tracked via `Task.status`, `Task.businessStatus`, and `Task.statusReason`"
   with a fuller paragraph that (a) acknowledges that clinical orders have varying
   life-cycles, (b) recommends using the `.businessStatus` element on the Coordination
   Task to carry coded progress states (e.g., `collection-complete`,
   `mandatory-program-complete`), and (c) sets up subsequent guidance about when to
   persist/exchange progress vs. derive it from related resources, including a lead-in
   to the multi-step / multi-instance fulfillment patterns that follow.

The ticket is purely narrative/structural — no resource, profile, value set, or
constraint changes are requested. The only FHIR artifact mentions are read-only
references to `Task.status`, `Task.businessStatus`, and `Task.statusReason` in the
Tracking Progress prose.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the editorial changes verbatim in the **HL7/fhir-cow-ig** IG, in the
narrative pages under `input/pagecontent/` (the conventional authoring root for
IG Publisher narrative content):

1. **`sharing-content.md`** — rename the four sub-section headings exactly as the
   reporter listed:
   - "Sharing Inputs" → "Sharing inputs at time of ordering"
   - "Requesting Additional Information" → "Sharing additional information about a
     clinical order", and split the body into two new H#-level sub-sections:
     *Provided by placer unsolicited* and *Request for additional information by
     fulfiller*.
   - "Sharing Intermediate Outputs" → "Sharing outcomes of clinical orders"
   - "Preliminary Results, Addenda, and Updates with FHIR Servers" →
     "Preliminary or Intermediate Results, Addenda, and Updates with FHIR Servers"
2. **`tracking-progress.md`** — replace the current single-sentence opening of the
   "In general progress is tracked via …" passage with the three-paragraph block
   quoted in the ticket, including:
   - the life-cycle framing,
   - the explicit recommendation to use `Task.businessStatus` on the *Coordination
     Task* with coded values such as `collection-complete` and
     `mandatory-program-complete`,
   - the "persist-and-exchange vs. derive-from-resources" lead-in that sets up the
     two follow-on mechanisms.
3. Update any in-page anchor links and the IG ToC entries that reference the renamed
   sections (`#sharing-inputs`, `#requesting-additional-information`,
   `#sharing-intermediate-outputs`, `#preliminary-results-addenda-and-updates-with-fhir-servers`).
4. Where appropriate, add the example FHIR-55629 noted in its only comment (a nurse
   accessing an existing patient medication list) — though that example is technically
   FHIR-55629's scope, not FHIR-55903's.

#### Justification

The change is purely narrative and is the natural follow-up to the already-accepted
restructure under FHIR-55629. The new headings are more clinically meaningful
("Sharing outcomes of clinical orders" reads better than "Sharing Intermediate
Outputs"), and the "additional information about a clinical order" split correctly
distinguishes two genuinely different patterns (placer-unsolicited push vs.
fulfiller-initiated pull) that today live under one ambiguous heading. The Tracking
Progress prose change is consistent with existing FHIR Workflow guidance — `Task.status`
is a fixed state-machine, `Task.businessStatus` is the documented place for
domain-coded progress, and surfacing example codes such as `collection-complete` makes
the guidance actionable for implementers without binding the IG to a specific value
set. There is no impact on any normative element, no new dependency, and no cross-IG
ripple beyond updating internal anchors. The reporter is the assignee of the parent
ticket and is therefore well-placed to apply the wording.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the **intent** of all five changes but adjust the wording before applying:

- Keep the simpler, parallel "Sharing inputs" / "Sharing additional information" /
  "Sharing outcomes" headings, but **drop the qualifier** "of clinical orders" /
  "at time of ordering" from the headings themselves and move that scoping into the
  section's first sentence. Rationale: every section on this page is implicitly about
  clinical orders, so repeating the qualifier in each heading is redundant and inflates
  the ToC.
- For the "additional information" split, prefer the active-voice headings
  **"Placer-supplied additional information (unsolicited)"** and
  **"Fulfiller-requested additional information"**, which read as parallel patterns
  and match the existing IG vocabulary that already distinguishes Placer / Fulfiller
  roles.
- Adopt the new Tracking Progress prose, but **promote the `Task.businessStatus`
  recommendation into a short bullet list** instead of burying it in a paragraph, and
  cite an existing CodeSystem (e.g., the COW IG's own task-business-status
  CodeSystem if one exists, or a `ConceptMap`/CodeSystem reference for codes such as
  `collection-complete`) rather than presenting `collection-complete` as a free-form
  string. This avoids implementers picking arbitrary codes from the example.

#### Justification

The reporter's heading wording is verbose and the proposed Tracking Progress paragraph
risks suggesting that ad-hoc strings like `collection-complete` are normative codes.
Disposition B preserves the substantive improvement (better section names; explicit
guidance on `Task.businessStatus` on the Coordination Task) while keeping the IG's
heading style tight and pointing implementers at proper terminology rather than
inline string examples. It is also a smaller diff in the rendered ToC and avoids a
second round of editorial nits during ballot reconciliation. Trade-off: it slightly
delays acceptance because the workgroup must agree on the exact CodeSystem reference
before the Tracking Progress edit can be made.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline FHIR-55903 as a separate ticket and instead **fold the proposed wording into
the editorial cleanup pass that implements FHIR-55629**. Close FHIR-55903 as
*Not Persuasive – addressed elsewhere*, with a comment pointing back at the
FHIR-55629 implementation PR for the COW IG.

#### Justification

FHIR-55903 is purely editorial and has no normative content. Its scope sits entirely
within the page that FHIR-55629 already authorises the assignee to (re-)write. Tracking
two tickets for what will be a single PR creates extra review burden, splits the
ballot-comment record, and is at risk of producing inconsistent wording if the two
tickets are dispositioned at different cycles. Declining here is *not* a rejection of
the wording — the assignee should incorporate the new headings and the Tracking
Progress paragraph as part of the FHIR-55629 work — it is purely a process / housekeeping
disposition.

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The changes requested are small, internally consistent, and demonstrably improve the
clarity of the Sharing Content and Tracking Progress pages. The reporter is also the
assignee of the parent ticket (FHIR-55629), so accepting verbatim minimises round-trips
and keeps authorial voice consistent. Disposition B's editorial concerns (heading
verbosity; the risk of treating `collection-complete` as a normative code) are real
but better handled as inline review comments on the implementing PR than as a
workgroup-level alternative — they should not block acceptance of the substantive
improvements. Disposition C (fold into FHIR-55629) is administratively tidy but
sacrifices the explicit ballot-record paper trail that having a discrete ticket
provides; given that FHIR-55629 was resolved *Persuasive with Modification* nearly a
month before FHIR-55903 was filed, the two are now distinct units of work and should
be tracked as such.

If, before applying, the workgroup wants the dispositions sharpened with concrete
file paths, recommended change recipes, and gotchas drawn from a `repo-analysis` of
**HL7/fhir-cow-ig**, please run that skill first — the COW IG repo is not currently
in FhirAugury's GitHub source and no briefing exists.
