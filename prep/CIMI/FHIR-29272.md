## Summary

The reporter asks the vital-signs profile family to add support for
**percentile** and **z-score** results, with particular emphasis on the
**pediatric** use case where vital signs are routinely interpreted as
percentile rank against a CDC/WHO reference population matched on age and
sex. The request explicitly calls out that any added observations must use
**accurate LOINC codes** that identify the specific reference method
(e.g. CDC vs. WHO charts) used to derive the percentile.

The ticket has had a complicated lifecycle: in 2021-01 CIMI voted 19-0-8 to
defer it as "Considered for Future Use" on the grounds that the relevant
percentile profiles already live in **US Core** (e.g. the
`pediatric-bmi-for-age`, `pediatric-weight-for-height`, and
`head-occipital-frontal-circumference-percentile` profiles that Eric Haas
referenced in his 2021-01-26 comment). It was reopened post-STU1 (2022-09)
because that resolution did not address USCDI requirements, deferred again,
and then in 2024-02 Nathan Davis reverted the resolution again, noting it
had been "incorrectly deferred as part of a group of issues to be
deferred." It is currently Triaged with no assignee and an outstanding
request from rikim for CIMI to provide a disposition and vote.

There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets and no GitHub items in the
augury index. Two Zulip cross-references both belong to a single
**implementers > Percentile Observation** thread in which Matthew Wieler
(working on FHIR compatibility for the RCPCH growth-chart API) raised the
same gap from an implementer's perspective in late 2022 / early 2023.

## Related Zulip Discussions

### [implementers > Percentile Observation](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/implementers/topic/Percentile%20Observation/near/321448787)

The thread runs from 2022-09 to 2023-01 and discusses how to represent
percentile observations in FHIR. Kyle Thompson opened by asking where the
**reference group** used to compute a percentile (e.g. age- and
sex-matched cohort) should be recorded — `Observation.note` vs.
`Observation.component`. Bryn Rhodes suggested a continuous-variable
**MeasureReport** (CQF-Measures IG) as one alternative. Eric Haas pointed
implementers to **US Core's `pediatric-bmi-for-age`** profile and
concluded "notes for peds and I don't see a reason why it would be any
different for adults," signalling that today's practice is to carry the
chart/method identification in `Observation.note.text` keyed off a LOINC
code that identifies the percentile measurement. Matthew Wieler then
joined while integrating the **RCPCH digital growth-charts API** with
FHIR, listing the LOINC codes he could find (`8303-0` Body Height
[Percentile], `8336-0` Body weight [Percentile] per age, `77606-2`
Weight-for-length per age and sex) and noting **no LOINCs exist for
"Body Height Percentile per Age", "Body Weight Percentile per Age",
"Weight-for-Length Percentile per Age", or "BMI Percentile per Age"** in
the form he wanted. He also flagged that his investigation found
"a FHIR resource exists for pediatric head circumference percentile, but
not for percentiles for other pediatric growth chart measurements
(weight, height, weight-for-height, and BMI)" — directly linking to
FHIR-29272. His final UPDATE concludes that **percentile is already
covered in USCDI for weight-for-height, head circumference, and BMI**
(via separate resources/profiles), leaving weight and height as the
remaining gaps, and that for now the data can be carried in either
Observation or MeasureReport. No formal consensus was reached and no
HL7 disposition was committed in the thread.

Participants: Kyle Thompson, Lloyd McKenzie, Bryn Rhodes, Eric Haas,
Matthew Wieler, Ron Shapiro, Gino Canessa.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add **percentile** and **z-score** member observations to the **US Vital
Signs IG** as new profiles on `Observation` derived from the existing
`vitalsigns` profile (and reference them from the
`vitalsigns-panel`/relevant pediatric panels):

- `vitalsigns-body-height-percentile-per-age`
- `vitalsigns-body-weight-percentile-per-age`
- `vitalsigns-weight-for-length-percentile-per-age`
- `vitalsigns-bmi-percentile-per-age`
- `vitalsigns-head-circumference-percentile`
- A parallel set of `*-zscore` profiles (or, alternatively, an
  `Observation.component` slice on each percentile profile carrying the
  matching z-score `valueQuantity`).

Each profile would:

- Bind `Observation.code` to a value set of LOINC codes that name the
  specific reference method (CDC, WHO, Fenton, Olsen, etc.). Where a
  LOINC code does not yet exist (notably the explicit "per age" forms
  Matthew Wieler enumerated), file a LOINC submission and reference the
  pending code in the IG.
- Use a UCUM-bound `valueQuantity` (`%` for percentile, `1` /
  `{z-score}` for z-score).
- Add a required slice on `Observation.method` or a new
  `growth-chart-source` extension whose value set enumerates
  CDC-2000, WHO-2006, Fenton-2013, Olsen-2010, etc., so that the
  reference table is machine-readable rather than buried in
  `Observation.note.text`.
- Require `Observation.subject` and `Observation.effective[x]` (so the
  age used for the lookup is unambiguous).

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter asked for, fully addresses the USCDI
gap that lynn_laakso flagged in 2022 as the reason for reopening, and
brings the US Vital Signs IG into parity with the
`pediatric-bmi-for-age`, `pediatric-weight-for-height`, and
head-circumference percentile profiles that already exist in **US Core**
(cited by Eric Haas in 2021 and again in the implementers Zulip thread).
Making the chart source structured (rather than free-text in
`Observation.note`) directly answers the open question Kyle Thompson
raised and removes the ambiguity Matthew Wieler hit when trying to
encode RCPCH chart identity. Adding the LOINC-method binding satisfies
the reporter's explicit "special attention should be paid to using
accurate LOINC maps for the WHO/CDC method" requirement.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Do not add new percentile/z-score profiles to the US Vital Signs IG.
Instead:

1. Define a lightweight reusable **`growth-chart-percentile`**
   extension (URL under the US Vital Signs IG canonical) carrying a
   single complex extension with sub-extensions for `percentile`
   (`decimal`, 0–100), `z-score` (`decimal`), and `chart-source`
   (`Coding` bound to a CDC/WHO/Fenton/Olsen value set).
2. Allow that extension on the existing
   `vitalsigns` profile (and therefore on every member observation —
   height, weight, BMI, head circumference, blood pressure, etc.)
   instead of minting parallel "...-percentile-per-age" profiles.
3. Publish guidance in the IG narrative that, where a curated profile
   already exists in US Core (e.g. `pediatric-bmi-for-age`,
   `head-occipital-frontal-circumference-percentile`), implementers
   should continue to use it; the new extension is for the gaps
   (weight-percentile-per-age, height-percentile-per-age) and for
   z-scores, which US Core does not currently model.

#### Justification

This approach avoids duplicating profiles that already exist in US Core
(Eric Haas's 2021 objection that drove the original "Considered for
Future Use" disposition) while still closing the USCDI gap that caused
the reopen. A single extension is also closer to current
implementer practice in the Zulip thread — Eric Haas's "notes for peds
and... no reason why it would be any different for adults" pattern —
and is cheaper to evolve than a fan-out of percentile + z-score
profiles. Trade-off vs. A: implementers lose the validation strength
of dedicated profiles, and percentiles become a discoverable extension
rather than a first-class observation, which slightly weakens
queryability against
`Observation?code=<percentile-loinc>&patient=...`.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve as **Persuasive with Mod / "Not Persuasive – Already Addressed
Elsewhere"**: do not add percentile or z-score support to the US Vital
Signs IG. Document in the resolution that pediatric percentile
observations are within the scope of **US Core** (existing
`pediatric-bmi-for-age`, `pediatric-weight-for-height`, and
`head-occipital-frontal-circumference-percentile` profiles), that
implementers needing additional percentile codes should pursue them
through **LOINC** (per Matthew Wieler's gap analysis), and that
arbitrary percentile / z-score reporting is already supportable via
either `Observation` (with LOINC + `Observation.note`/`component`) or
`MeasureReport` (per Bryn Rhodes), so no normative US Vital Signs
change is required.

#### Justification

This is essentially the disposition CIMI voted 19-0-8 in 2021 (Susan
Matney / Nathan Davis), with the explicit rationale that "These are
part of US Core" (Eric Haas, 2021-01-26). The implementers' Zulip
thread reached a similar functional conclusion — current FHIR R4
mechanics (Observation + LOINC + note, or MeasureReport) are
sufficient — without anyone arguing for a US Vital Signs change.
Declining keeps the US Vital Signs IG narrowly scoped to the panels
already published, avoids overlap with US Core, and pushes the real
remaining gaps (missing "...-per-age" LOINCs and z-score codes) to
their proper home at LOINC.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The 2024-02 reverting comment from Nathan Davis explicitly records that
the prior "Considered for Future Use" resolution was an error, and the
2022-09 reopen note from Lynn Laakso is unambiguous that **without
percentile observations the IG does not meet USCDI requirements** —
which is the bar the US Vital Signs IG is expected to clear. Disposition
C effectively reinstates the resolution that has now been reverted twice
and leaves the USCDI gap unaddressed, so it should not be the workgroup
recommendation. Disposition B is attractive on cost grounds, but the
implementer evidence in the Zulip thread (Kyle Thompson's reference-group
question, Matthew Wieler's LOINC-mapping struggle) shows that the field
needs structured profile-level guidance, not just an extension; carrying
chart source in free-text notes is the status quo that implementers are
asking the workgroup to move past. Disposition A delivers that, aligns
US Vital Signs with the precedent already set by the US Core pediatric
percentile profiles, and gives CIMI a concrete deliverable to close the
ticket. The dependency on new LOINC codes (height-percentile-per-age,
weight-percentile-per-age, BMI-percentile-per-age, and the z-score
forms) should be tracked as a sub-task and pursued in parallel with the
profile work so the IG is not blocked on terminology turnaround.
