## Summary

The submitter (Greg Bloom) proposes adding a third use case to the Use Case
section of the Human Services Directory (FHIR) IG: **analysis of aggregated
services-directory information** (e.g., community asset mapping) to assess
how effectively resource allocation meets community needs and addresses
social determinants of health at a population level. The reporter notes
that the existing IG is scoped around service-discovery queries by
providers, payers, CBOs, and consumer apps, and does not explicitly cover
aggregate analytical use by researchers, analysts, advocates, or
policy-makers. The request is purely narrative — to acknowledge this
distinct user/use case in the IG even though the IG would not (yet) fully
specify mechanisms for it. No related Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or
GitHub items were surfaced via cross-references or search.

## Details

**Description:**

> Another, distinct use case for querying a human service directory database
> is for analysis of aggregate resource data to assess resource availability
> in a given community and/or domain.
>
> Such analysis might be performed by a different type of user — a researcher
> or analyst, for the purpose of intelligence gathering, advocacy,
> policy-making, etc — for the purpose of, for instance, community asset
> mapping, evaluation of population-level health and well-being, etc.
>
> This use case is not specified here. We may not have carefully considered
> it on its own terms, although it also may be served in a minimally viable
> capacity by the same functions established for service discovery.
>
> We suggest the following text that could be included as a third bullet in
> the use case section:
>
> - **Analysis of aggregated services directory information (for community
>   asset mapping, etc) to assess the effectiveness of resource allocation
>   for meeting communities' needs and addressing social determinants of
>   health at a population-level.**

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a third bullet to the Use Cases section of the Human Services
Directory (FHIR) IG narrative, using the submitter's wording (lightly
copy-edited):

- **Analysis of aggregated services directory information (e.g., for
  community asset mapping) to assess the effectiveness of resource
  allocation for meeting community needs and addressing social
  determinants of health at the population level.**

Frame the bullet as an acknowledged use case that the current STU does
**not** specify mechanisms for, but that may be partially served by the
existing read-only service-discovery queries. Add a one-line note
identifying the new persona ("researcher / analyst / policy-maker") and
flag the use case as a candidate for future-version specification.

#### Justification

This is a narrative-only change (use case section) with no impact on
profiles, search parameters, conformance, or operations. It honors the
2023-10-04 commitment to introduce the use case "during the ballot
reconciliation process and marked to future consideration in a
subsequent version." Acknowledging the use case helps implementers
understand why the directory's data model and read APIs may be exercised
beyond 1:1 service look-ups, and signals to the analytics community that
HSS sees this as in-scope long term. Cost is low; downside is
essentially nil.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the use case in spirit, but instead of adding it as a peer to the
two existing service-discovery use cases, introduce a clearly delimited
**"Out-of-Scope / Future Considerations"** subsection in the Use Cases
chapter and place the aggregate-analysis bullet there. Cross-reference
that subsection from the main use case list ("see Future Considerations
for additional use cases under study"). Optionally add a short paragraph
noting that the existing read-only `search` interactions on
HealthcareService/Location/Organization can be repurposed in a "minimally
viable" way for asset-mapping today, while a future version may add
dedicated analytical support (e.g., bulk export, summary statistics,
dedicated population-level profiles).

#### Justification

The submitter explicitly states "this use case is not specified here"
and that it is served only "in a minimally viable capacity" by the
discovery functions. Listing it as a peer bullet to the two specified
use cases risks misleading implementers into assuming the IG's
conformance requirements cover analytic workloads, which they do not.
Quarantining it under "Future Considerations" preserves the
acknowledgement, satisfies the 2023-10-04 commitment, and keeps the
normative-feeling part of the use case list aligned with what the IG
actually constrains. It also creates a natural home for any additional
deferred use cases that surface during reconciliation.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request as **out of scope for this STU** and reaffirm the
original 2023-03-06 disposition of "Considered for Future Use" without
adding the bullet to the published IG narrative. Document the use case in
the work group's backlog / project-scope statement for a subsequent
version rather than in the IG itself.

#### Justification

The IG's specified scope is read-only service discovery by providers,
payers, CBOs, and consumer apps. The aggregate-analysis use case
introduces a new actor (researcher/analyst), a different interaction
pattern (population-level aggregation), and likely different
privacy/governance considerations — none of which are specified or
constrained by this STU. Adding the bullet without specifying any
supporting conformance content could create unfounded implementer
expectations. The work group already voted 6-0-0 to defer this once;
absent new use-case material or implementer demand, the more conservative
path is to keep the deferral and revisit when concrete requirements
exist.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B best honors the 2023-10-04 commitment to surface this use
case during ballot reconciliation while protecting the integrity of the
specified Use Cases list. The submitter's own framing ("this use case is
not specified here") fits naturally in a "Future Considerations"
subsection, and that structure also gives the work group a stable place
to record any other deferred use cases that arise during reconciliation
without re-litigating each one. Disposition A is acceptable but blurs the
line between specified and aspirational use cases; Disposition C
contradicts the explicit re-open and the stated intent to introduce the
use case in the IG narrative. Recommend B with the bullet wording from A,
relocated under "Future Considerations" and cross-referenced from the
main use case list.
