## Summary

The reporter (Gay Dolin) requests a focused, governance-level review of
the **Required** and **Extensible** vocabulary bindings on elements in
the FHIR core Clinical Resources and clinically-adjacent resources
(Encounter, Patient, insurance-related resources, etc.). The request is
motivated by recurring profiling friction in **US Core** — for example,
the inherited extensible binding on `Location.type`
(`ServiceDeliveryLocationRoleType`), bindings in `Specimen`, and
Gender/Sex-related value sets — where the base-resource binding is
either too restrictive, conflicts with national/jurisdictional
vocabularies, or both. The reporter explicitly does **not** propose a
change set; an attached spreadsheet enumerates candidate bindings as
**input** to a coordinated review by FHIR-I, the relevant Resource
owners, and Terminology Infrastructure (TI). The ticket was moved to
**FMG** by Lloyd McKenzie because it is a governance / policy question
about how aggressively base resources should constrain vocabularies,
not an infrastructure change.

No explicit Jira links, cross-references, Zulip threads, GitHub items,
or Confluence pages were surfaced for this ticket by the FhirAugury
indexes. Several closely-related tickets were identified via keyword
similarity and are summarised below; they each illustrate the kind of
specific, per-element ticket Lloyd McKenzie's comments ask the reporter
to file:

- [FHIR-50937](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50937) — Location.type
  extensible-binding remediation in US Core (Persuasive, Applied). The
  archetypal worked example of the problem this tracker raises.
- [FHIR-55653](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-55653) —
  Provenance.entity.role: change Required → Extensible (Duplicate;
  pushed back on by John Moehrke in this ticket's comments because the
  binding encodes a W3C PROV state machine).
- [FHIR-45125](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-45125) —
  DiagnosticResult.status conflict between AU Core and base
  (Persuasive with Modification, Applied) — a parallel
  base-vs-national-IG conflict.
- [FHIR-43406](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43406) — Task.status as
  CodeableConcept w/ extensible binding (Not Persuasive) — establishes
  that some required bindings are intentional (administrative status
  semantics).

Together these illustrate both ends of the problem space the tracker
asks FMG to look at: bindings that are over-constrained and *should*
loosen (Location.type, AU Core diagnostic status), and bindings that
look loosenable but encode genuine standards / state-machine
semantics that should not change (Task.status, Provenance.entity.role).

## Details

**Description:**

> I identified a set of **Required** and **Extensible** vocabulary
> bindings in core **Clinical Resources** and clinically adjacent
> resources (e.g., Encounter, Patient, insurance-related resources)
> that warrant further evaluation by FHIR-I, in coordination with
> Resource owners and Terminology Infrastructure (TI). (See attached

>
> I have not performed a full determination of which bindings should
> change. Rather, based on challenges encountered while developing and
> maintaining **US Core** profiles, I believe there should be **few
> Required or Extensible bindings in base resources**, and that, if
> used, they should be **carefully considered for potential downstream
> use cases**.
>
> Some examples encountered in US Core to date include certain
> bindings in **Location**, **Specimen**, and **Gender/Sex-related
> value sets**.
>
> Some Required and Extensible bindings in base resources are valid
> and should remain unchanged, particularly where they reflect
> **workflow or metadata semantics** and long-standing HL7 Standards
> use (e.g., many `*.status` elements and Vital Signs ucum bindings).
>
> Looking ahead, as additional country-, jurisdiction-, and
> policy-specific profiles are developed — including **IPS-aligned
> national patient summaries** — similar challenges are anticipated.
> In some cases, **unresolved use-case conflicts** around
> base-resource Required and Extensible bindings could lead to
> undesirable profiling workarounds, reduced adherence to vocabulary
> guidance, or avoidance of specialized profiles in favor of overuse
> of generic resources (e.g., Observation).
>
> I request a **focused evaluation of the identified bindings** by
> FHIR-I, Resource owners and TI, with the attached spreadsheet
> serving as **input to that review**, not a proposed change set.
>
> **Offer of support:** I would be happy to assist in this evaluation
> effort, including helping assess downstream impacts and supporting
> cross-group coordination.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat FHIR-54613 as a **commission** to FHIR-I + Terminology
Infrastructure + the affected Resource owners to perform a focused,
time-boxed review of every Required and Extensible binding in the
core Clinical Resources and clinically-adjacent resources (Encounter,
Patient, Coverage, Account, etc.) using the reporter's attached
spreadsheet as the seed list. Concretely, FMG would:

1. Accept and stage the spreadsheet as a working document under the
   FMG Confluence space.
2. Direct each affected resource owner WG to review the rows for
   their resource, classifying each binding as
   **Keep-as-is / Reduce-strength / Replace-binding / Add-Additional-Binding**,
   with rationale captured per row.
3. Direct TI to review whether any base value sets need an
   `other`/`unknown` escape concept added (the existing auto-filed
   "missing escape valve" tickets that Lloyd referenced are the
   precedent).
4. Track the rollup as child tickets under FHIR-54613 so resolution is
   per-resource, not in this umbrella.
5. Accept the reporter's offered support on cross-group coordination.

#### Justification

The ticket is exactly the kind of cross-cutting, governance-level
question FMG exists to coordinate; resource owners cannot be expected
to spot a *systemic* over-constraint pattern from inside their own
resources. The Location.type case (FHIR-50937, Persuasive/Applied)
and the AU Core diagnostic-status case (FHIR-45125, Persuasive with
Modification/Applied) both demonstrate that when these reviews are
actually performed, the binding does turn out to need loosening. The
reporter has done substantial preparatory work and is offering
ongoing support, which lowers the marginal cost to FMG.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Decompose FHIR-54613 into two deliverables and **close the umbrella
tracker** once they are scheduled:

1. **WG Guidance on Vocabulary Binding Strength** (Eric Haas's
   suggestion in the 2026-02-11 comment): FMG, with TI, drafts and
   publishes guidance for Resource owners that (a) restates the
   existing rule "no Required or Extensible bindings on non-code
   elements" (Lloyd McKenzie's stipulation), (b) requires a recorded
   rationale when a Required or Extensible binding is introduced or
   retained (e.g., state-machine semantics, regulatory mapping,
   workflow status), (c) requires every Required binding to expose an
   `unknown`/`other` escape concept unless the rationale explicitly
   forbids it, and (d) recommends Additional Bindings as the default
   mechanism for jurisdictional vocabulary needs (per the
   Location.type pattern in FHIR-50937).
2. **Per-resource child tickets**: ask the reporter / Eric Haas to
   file specific change-request tickets per binding in the
   spreadsheet (which Lloyd McKenzie has consistently asked for), and
   link them as 'related' to this tracker. Each child ticket goes to
   the appropriate Resource owner WG for normal disposition.

The umbrella ticket itself is then resolved as **Persuasive with
Modification** with the action being "publish guidance + child
tickets filed."

#### Justification

Lloyd McKenzie's procedural objection is real: as filed, FHIR-54613
asks for no specific change and cannot be assigned to a single WG, so
under standard FHIR ticket hygiene it cannot be acted on by any
single resolver. The two-deliverable split converts the umbrella
request into work products that *can* be tracked: durable WG-facing
guidance (which scales beyond the spreadsheet) plus the per-element
tickets that are the actual mechanism FHIR uses to change bindings.
The Provenance.entity.role discussion (FHIR-55653 + Moehrke's
comment) and the Task.status outcome (FHIR-43406, Not Persuasive)
both show why a one-size-fits-all "loosen all required bindings"
sweep would be wrong; codifying *when* a strong binding is justified
is more defensible than reviewing every binding row-by-row in a
single cross-WG sprint.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve FHIR-54613 as **Not Persuasive (procedural)** on the grounds
that (a) the ticket asks for no specific change to the standard,
(b) FMG already has a standing rule that Required/Extensible
bindings should not appear on non-code elements, (c) automated
tickets have already been filed against required bindings missing an
escape valve, and (d) the existing per-resource ticket mechanism
(plus links such as FHIR-50937 and FHIR-45125) is the correct route
for any specific binding the reporter believes is over-constrained.
Direct the reporter to file specific tickets per element from the
spreadsheet, linked as 'related' to closed FHIR-54613, with the
appropriate Resource owner WG as target.

#### Justification

This is essentially the position Lloyd McKenzie has already taken
twice in the comment thread. FMG's normal disposition discipline is
that a tracker must request a concrete change a WG can act on; an
umbrella "please review a spreadsheet" item bypasses that discipline
and would create an open, unassignable ticket that FMG cannot close.
The Provenance counter-example (FHIR-55653) and the Task.status
counter-example (FHIR-43406) demonstrate that case-by-case review is
required anyway, so the umbrella adds little process value.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The underlying problem the reporter raises is real and well-evidenced
(FHIR-50937 and FHIR-45125 both ended Persuasive/Applied), and the
counter-examples in the comments (Provenance state-machine,
Task.status administrative semantics) make it equally clear that a
blanket "review and loosen" sweep would be wrong. Disposition B
captures both: it produces a durable, citable WG-facing guidance
artifact that future Resource owners (including IPS-aligned national
profilers) can apply *before* a binding becomes a US Core problem,
and it routes the actual change requests through the per-element
ticket mechanism Lloyd McKenzie correctly insists on. It also gives
the reporter a concrete, achievable closure path — which Disposition
C does not, and which Disposition A risks turning into an open-ended
cross-WG project. FMG's added value here is the guidance document;
the binding changes themselves belong to the Resource owners and TI.
