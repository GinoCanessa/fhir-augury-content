## Summary

The ticket asks the Structured Documents Work Group to expand the Social
History Section profile in the **US C-CDA on FHIR** Implementation Guide so
that it explicitly accommodates **Smoking History** entries, paralleling the
range of entry types defined for the Social History Section in C-CDA. The
reporter (Corey Spears) notes that the analogous DAF (Data Access Framework)
profile already defines a Smoking Status / Smoking History entry pattern that
could be reused, and observes that supporting the additional entry type may
require **sub-slicing** the existing `Observation` slice on the Social History
Section so that smoking-history observations can be distinguished from other
social-history observations. A 2024-03-12 comment from Lynn Laakso indicates
the Work Group's intent to **re-open this item post STU 1.2 publication**,
i.e., it is queued for the next ballot/STU cycle of C-CDA on FHIR. No
cross-referenced Jira tickets were found.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the next STU update of **US C-CDA on FHIR** (post STU 1.2, as Lynn Laakso's
2024-03-12 comment anticipates), update the Social History Section profile
(the FHIR `Composition.section` profile that corresponds to C-CDA's
Social History Section, templateId `2.16.840.1.113883.10.20.22.2.17`) so that
its `entry` slice on `Observation` is **sub-sliced** to call out a dedicated
**Smoking History / Smoking Status** entry. Concretely:

- Add a new `entry` slice (e.g., `entry:smokingStatus`) targeting an
  `Observation` profile constrained to:
  - `Observation.code` = LOINC `72166-2` *Tobacco smoking status* (and/or the
    existing C-CDA Smoking Status / Tobacco Use value sets), aligning with
    **US Core Smoking Status Observation** where appropriate.
  - `Observation.value[x]` bound to the SNOMED CT smoking-status value set
    used by C-CDA / US Core.
  - `Observation.subject` → `Patient`, `Observation.effective[x]` populated.
- Document the discriminator on the section's `entry` slicing (most naturally
  `pattern` on `resolve().code`) so smoking-status entries are unambiguously
  identifiable.
- Add or update narrative guidance and an example instance, and map the new
  slice in the C-CDA ↔ FHIR mapping tables for the Social History Section.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the ticket asks for and what C-CDA itself already
requires: the C-CDA Social History Section enumerates several distinct entry
templates (Smoking Status, Tobacco Use, Pregnancy Observation, Social History
Observation, etc.), and FHIR consumers/producers cannot reliably round-trip
those templates without a way to distinguish them at the section level. The
US Core Smoking Status profile and LOINC 72166-2 already give us a stable,
widely-implemented target, so the slice can reuse existing artifacts rather
than invent new ones — which the ticket itself anticipates ("DAF does have an
entry for"). Sub-slicing `Composition.section.entry` is the established FHIR
pattern for this kind of differentiation and is consistent with how other
US C-CDA on FHIR sections already constrain their entries.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than sub-slicing every C-CDA entry template into the Social History
Section profile one ticket at a time, define the Social History Section's
`entry` slicing **open** on `Observation`, and publish a **library of
referenceable Observation profiles** for the C-CDA social-history entry
templates (Smoking Status, Tobacco Use, Pregnancy Observation, Social History
Observation, etc.) as standalone profiles in US C-CDA on FHIR. The Social
History Section profile would then require/recommend (via guidance and
profile binding on the referenced `Observation`) that Smoking Status entries
conform to the new `Observation-SmokingStatus-CCDA` profile (which itself
derives from / aligns with US Core Smoking Status), without forcing every
implementer to navigate a long list of named slices on the section.

#### Justification

Sub-slicing the section's `entry` for each of ~6-8 C-CDA entry templates
makes the Social History Section profile heavy and brittle: every future
C-CDA template addition forces another slice and another change request like
this one. A profile-library approach concentrates the constraints on the
**resource** the data actually lives on (`Observation`), is easier for
implementers to reuse outside the C-CDA context, and aligns better with how
US Core already handles Smoking Status. The trade-off vs. Disposition A is
that conformance tooling cannot mechanically detect "this section is missing
a Smoking Status entry" purely from the section profile — that check shifts
into IG-level guidance or a separate constraint. Given that C-CDA itself does
not *require* a Smoking Status entry in every Social History Section, that
trade-off is acceptable.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline as **already addressed by existing mechanisms**. Note that
`Observation` resources representing smoking status (e.g., conforming to the
US Core Smoking Status profile, `Observation.code` = LOINC 72166-2) are
already legal contents of the Social History Section's `entry` slice today,
and that no profile change is required for an implementer to include a
Smoking History observation in the section. Document this in the Social
History Section's narrative guidance and close the ticket as Persuasive –
With Mod (guidance only).

#### Justification

The Social History Section profile in US C-CDA on FHIR already permits
`Observation` entries; nothing in the current profile prevents a Smoking
Status observation from appearing there. Adding a named slice does not
unlock new behaviour for implementers — it primarily aids conformance
checking and discoverability. If the Work Group judges that the existing
freedom plus narrative guidance is sufficient and prefers to keep the
section profile lean, declining (with a guidance-only update) is defensible.
The risk is that without an explicit slice, round-tripping with C-CDA loses
the template-level identity of Smoking Status entries, which is the very
problem the ticket raises — so this disposition is the weakest of the three.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A is the recommended starting point because it directly solves
the round-trip problem the ticket identifies (C-CDA's Social History Section
explicitly distinguishes Smoking Status / Tobacco Use entries via templateId,
and a FHIR profile that does not slice on this loses that distinction), it
matches Lynn Laakso's comment that the item is queued to re-open after
STU 1.2 publication (i.e., the Work Group already expects to act on it), and
it can be implemented cheaply by reusing US Core Smoking Status and LOINC
72166-2 rather than minting new artifacts. Disposition B is worth raising in
the same discussion as a structural alternative if the Work Group is
concerned about the section profile becoming bloated as more C-CDA entry
templates are added; the choice between A and B is largely an editorial
decision about where constraints should live (section vs. resource profile).
Disposition C should only be adopted if the Work Group concludes that
template-level fidelity to C-CDA is not a goal of the IG, which would
contradict the IG's stated purpose.
