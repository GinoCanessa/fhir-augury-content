## Summary

The ticket reports that the **US C-CDA on FHIR** implementation guide (Structured
Documents work group) does not address two of the more complex C-CDA structuredBody
constraints when mapping CDA documents (e.g., CCD / Referral Note) onto FHIR
`Composition.section`. Specifically, the IG silently drops:

- **CONF:1198-29102** (constraint xxiv): the document SHALL contain *either* an
  **Assessment and Plan Section (V2)** (template `2.16.840.1.113883.10.20.22.2.9:2014-06-09`),
  *or* both an **Assessment Section** (`2.16.840.1.113883.10.20.22.2.8`) and a
  **Plan of Treatment Section (V2)** (`2.16.840.1.113883.10.20.22.2.10:2014-06-09`); and
- **CONF:1198-29103** (constraint xxv): the document SHALL NOT contain the combined
  **Assessment and Plan Section** when either the standalone Assessment Section or
  Plan of Treatment Section is also present (mutual exclusion between the combined
  and the split forms).

These are XOR-style co-occurrence rules across `Composition.section` entries that
cannot be expressed via simple cardinality/slicing alone — they require an
invariant on `Composition`. The ticket was originally raised in 2016 against
US C-CDA on FHIR and was reopened by Lynn Laakso on 2024-03-12 to be addressed
"post STU1.2 publication". The related authoring page cited in the comments is
`http://build.fhir.org/ccda/ccda-referral-note-composition.html` (the C-CDA on FHIR
Referral Note Composition profile).

There are no linked Jira tickets, and the only cross-reference is an automated
GForge tracker post in Zulip from 2016 (no human discussion).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add two new FHIRPath invariants to the **C-CDA on FHIR Referral Note
Composition** (and the parallel **CCD Composition**) profiles in the
`HL7/ccda-on-fhir` IG so that the Composition-level XOR rule is enforced
on FHIR instances:

- `ccda-comp-1` (severity `error`, mapped to CONF:1198-29102):
  `section.where(code.memberOf('<AssessmentAndPlanSection-VS>')).exists() or (section.where(code.memberOf('<AssessmentSection-VS>')).exists() and section.where(code.memberOf('<PlanOfTreatmentSection-VS>')).exists())`
- `ccda-comp-2` (severity `error`, mapped to CONF:1198-29103):
  `section.where(code.memberOf('<AssessmentAndPlanSection-VS>')).exists() implies (section.where(code.memberOf('<AssessmentSection-VS>')).empty() and section.where(code.memberOf('<PlanOfTreatmentSection-VS>')).empty())`

Bind each `code.memberOf(...)` reference to a small ValueSet that contains the
LOINC `section.code` value(s) the IG already uses to identify each of the three
sections (so the invariant fires on FHIR semantics, not on the underlying CDA
templateId). Add narrative text to `ccda-referral-note-composition.html`
explicitly calling out the mapping from CONF:1198-29102 / CONF:1198-29103 and
the rationale, and add positive/negative examples that exercise both the
combined and the split forms.

#### Justification

The two CONF rules are normative SHALL/SHALL NOT statements in C-CDA. The whole
point of US C-CDA on FHIR is fidelity to the underlying C-CDA contract, and
silently dropping mandatory document-level constraints undermines the IG's
claim of equivalence. Composition-level FHIRPath invariants are the
idiomatic FHIR mechanism to express co-occurrence and mutual-exclusion rules
between sections, and they are validated by every conformant FHIR validator
without requiring custom tooling. Lynn Laakso's 2024-03-12 reopen confirms the
WG intends to address these post STU1.2.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Instead of (or in addition to) FHIRPath invariants on the Composition profile,
**slice `Composition.section` by `code`** in the Referral Note / CCD
Composition profiles to define three named slices — `assessmentAndPlan`,
`assessment`, and `planOfTreatment` — and express the XOR rule via
slice cardinalities plus a single, simpler invariant:

- `assessmentAndPlan` 0..1, `assessment` 0..1, `planOfTreatment` 0..1.
- One invariant: "exactly one of {`assessmentAndPlan`} or {both
  `assessment` and `planOfTreatment`} SHALL be present" — i.e.,
  `(section.where(...assessmentAndPlan...).exists() xor (section.where(...assessment...).exists() and section.where(...planOfTreatment...).exists()))`.

Pair this with a US C-CDA on FHIR-specific **conformance test bundle** in the
IG that exercises both legal shapes and the three illegal shapes (combined +
either split section, neither shape present).

#### Justification

Slicing makes the structural intent visible in the StructureDefinition itself
rather than burying it in a FHIRPath expression, which improves IDE / publisher
tooling support, generated narrative, and human readability of the profile.
A single combined XOR invariant is easier to maintain than two cross-referencing
invariants and is less prone to drift if the underlying section ValueSets evolve.
The trade-off vs. Disposition A is upfront authoring effort and a slightly more
opinionated profile shape; the upside is that downstream profiling work
(e.g., profiles derived from the CCD Composition) inherits the slices and
gets the rule "for free".

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as **Persuasive with Mod / Not Persuasive — Out of Scope for the FHIR
mapping**. Document in the IG that the C-CDA on FHIR profiles intentionally do
not re-express CDA templateId-level co-occurrence constraints (CONF:1198-29102
and CONF:1198-29103) at the FHIR Composition level, and instead direct
implementers to validate against the source CDA when those constraints matter.
Add a short note to `ccda-referral-note-composition.html` listing the unmapped
CONF rules so consumers are not surprised.

#### Justification

C-CDA on FHIR is a transformation/interoperability bridge, not a wholesale
re-publication of the C-CDA conformance contract. Round-tripping these
rules into FHIR adds maintenance burden (the invariants must track every
revision of the underlying C-CDA templates and any LOINC/section-code
changes), and a strict invariant could over-constrain real-world FHIR
documents that legitimately carry only one of the two assessment/plan shapes
for non-CCD use cases. If a consumer needs full C-CDA conformance, they
should validate against the CDA artifact, not its FHIR projection.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The substantive concern in the ticket is real — these are SHALL / SHALL NOT
rules in C-CDA and the IG currently drops them — so Disposition C
(decline) is hard to defend, especially given that Lynn Laakso explicitly
reopened the issue in 2024 to address it post STU1.2. Between A and B,
Disposition B is preferable because slicing `Composition.section` makes the
three section roles first-class in the StructureDefinition (visible in the
publisher-rendered profile, exercised by snapshot tooling, and inherited by
downstream profiles), while still letting a single XOR invariant carry the
mutual-exclusion semantics of CONF:1198-29103. Disposition A's two parallel
invariants are functionally equivalent but invisible in the structural view
and easier to drift out of sync. Both A and B should ship with a
conformance test bundle so the WG can verify the rule against representative
CCD and Referral Note instances; the WG should decide whether to apply the
slicing only to the Referral Note Composition or also to the broader CCD
Composition profile in the same IG.
