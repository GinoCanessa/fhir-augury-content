## Summary

The submitter (Corey Spears) reports that, in the **US C-CDA on FHIR**
specification, the **History of Past Illness Section** of the Referral
Note Composition profile is missing an `entry` slice/definition that
points at a Condition profile, even though the analogous **Problem
Section** of the same composition already carries such an entry that
references the (then-current) **DAF Condition** profile. The ticket
proposes adding a parallel `Composition.section.entry` definition to the
History of Past Illness section so that it MAY contain `0..*`
references to the same Condition profile that the Problem Section uses,
and includes a literal XML snippet of the desired StructureDefinition
element.

The ticket was filed in 2016 against the original C-CDA on FHIR draft
(when **DAF** was the canonical US profile family) and was re-opened by
Lynn Laakso in March 2024 with the note "re-open post STU1.2
publication", indicating Structured Documents wants to re-evaluate it
against the current STU1.2 baseline. In current C-CDA on FHIR (and the
broader US ecosystem), DAF has been retired and replaced by **US Core
Condition** (specifically `us-core-condition-problems-health-concerns`
in modern US Core), so any modern fix would target the US-Core profile
rather than the literal `daf-condition` URL the ticket cites. The
underlying gap — Problem Section has a typed Condition entry, History
of Past Illness Section does not — is what needs adjudication.

There are no other Jira tickets cross-referenced from FHIR-10324, and
the only Zulip cross-reference is the automated `[committers] >
tracker-item` announcement of this ticket; there is no substantive
community discussion in Zulip. There are no GitHub cross-references.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add an `entry` element definition to the **History of Past Illness
Section** in the Referral Note Composition profile of *US C-CDA on
FHIR*, mirroring the slice that already exists on the **Problem
Section**. Concretely, in the StructureDefinition that profiles
`Composition` for the Referral Note (the source of
`build.fhir.org/ccda/ccda-referral-note-composition.html`), add a
differential element on the History of Past Illness section's
`Composition.section.entry` with:

- `min = 0`, `max = *`
- `type.code = Reference`
- `type.targetProfile` pointing at the same Condition profile the
  Problem Section currently references

Because **DAF has been retired**, the modern equivalent is the **US
Core Condition** profile, specifically
`http://hl7.org/fhir/us/core/StructureDefinition/us-core-condition-problems-health-concerns`
(the same profile the Problem Section now points at in current
C-CDA-on-FHIR STU1.2). The literal `daf-condition` URL in the original
ticket should be silently replaced with the current US Core target so
the History of Past Illness Section is consistent with what the Problem
Section actually references today.

#### Justification

The Problem Section and the History of Past Illness Section both carry
clinically Condition-shaped content (active vs. historical problems);
profiling the latter to allow a typed `Reference(US Core Condition)`
entry while leaving the former untyped is an internal inconsistency of
the Referral Note Composition profile and was the original submitter's
explicit complaint. Closing this gap is a low-risk, additive change
(the slice is `0..*`) that improves discoverability and validation
parity across the two sections. Lynn Laakso's 2024 re-open comment
("re-open post STU1.2 publication") indicates Structured Documents
already considers this worth revisiting against the current baseline,
not just the 2016 draft.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than adding a typed `Reference(US Core Condition)` entry slice
to the History of Past Illness Section in the Referral Note
Composition only, **address the gap at the section-profile layer**:
either (a) define a single reusable `HistoryOfPastIllnessSection`
profile that constrains `Composition.section.entry` to
`Reference(us-core-condition-problems-health-concerns)` and reference
that profile from the Referral Note (and any other composition that
carries a History of Past Illness Section), or (b) extend the
**existing Problem Section profile** so its `entry` slice is reused
for both Problem and History-of-Past-Illness contexts, with a coded
discriminator (e.g., on `Condition.clinicalStatus` /
`verificationStatus` or `Condition.category`) to distinguish active
problems from historical ones.

#### Justification

The original ticket fixes a single section in a single composition,
but the structural problem is that "section that holds Conditions"
appears in multiple C-CDA-on-FHIR compositions and historically each
has been profiled point-by-point. A reusable section profile (option
a) keeps the constraint in one place and prevents the same bug from
recurring in the next composition that grows a History of Past Illness
Section. Option (b) is more invasive but acknowledges that the
clinical distinction between "active problem" and "history of past
illness" is really a state of the underlying `Condition` resource
(`clinicalStatus = inactive | resolved`), not a different resource
shape — which is also how US Core actually models it today. Either
variant is more durable than a one-off element addition, but both cost
more analyst time and at least (a) requires coordinating naming with
the rest of the C-CDA-on-FHIR section profile catalog.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline to add a typed Condition entry to the History of Past Illness
Section. Rationale category: **already addressed by the existing
generic `Composition.section.entry` cardinality**, combined with
**original target profile (DAF) is obsolete**, so the literal request
can no longer be implemented as written.

Action: close as "Persuasive with Mod / Not Persuasive" with a note
that (1) `Composition.section.entry` is already `0..*
Reference(Resource)` in the base spec so implementers may already
populate it with `Condition` instances conforming to US Core, and (2)
the specific `daf-condition` profile cited by the ticket has been
retired; implementers should follow general US Core guidance for
representing past illness as a `Condition` with appropriate
`clinicalStatus`.

#### Justification

Eight years have elapsed since the ticket was filed. The original
target profile (`http://hl7.org/fhir/StructureDefinition/daf-condition`)
no longer exists as a normative deliverable, and the broader US
ecosystem has moved on to US Core, which already models historical
problems as `Condition` instances distinguished by `clinicalStatus`
rather than by a separate profile. Adding a typed entry slice now
would imply that the History of Past Illness Section is meaningfully
different from the Problem Section in a way US Core does not actually
support, and risks setting an implementation precedent that other
"history of …" sections in C-CDA on FHIR will need parallel typed
slices. The ticket can be addressed by a documentation note on the
Referral Note Composition page rather than by a profile change.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A (with the URL substitution from DAF →
US Core Condition called out explicitly in the change history).

The submitter's underlying observation is correct and was not refuted
when the ticket was re-opened in 2024: the **Problem Section** of the
Referral Note Composition profile carries a typed Condition entry and
the **History of Past Illness Section** of the same composition does
not. That asymmetry is genuinely confusing for implementers reading
the two section profiles side by side, regardless of whether DAF or US
Core is the target. The fix is small, additive (`0..*`),
backward-compatible, and brings the section into alignment with how
the rest of the Referral Note Composition is profiled — exactly the
kind of cleanup the STU1.2 re-open comment was inviting. Disposition
B is structurally cleaner but is materially more work and would
re-litigate the C-CDA-on-FHIR section-profile design, which is out of
scope for a single tracker item this old. Disposition C is defensible
on "DAF is gone" grounds but throws away the still-valid consistency
argument and leaves the inconsistency in print. Take Disposition A,
retarget the reference to
`us-core-condition-problems-health-concerns`, and note the DAF →
US Core retargeting in the ticket's resolution comment so the change
history is auditable.
