## Summary

The ticket (originally GF#16662, "2018-May IPS #35") asks the IPS work
group to expand the **History of Pregnancy Section** of the
International Patient Summary so that it carries clinically useful
detail about a subject's pregnancy history rather than a minimal
indicator. The reporter (George Dixon) points out that a parallel
ballot, **CDAR2_IG_PREGNANCY_R1_D1_2018MAY** (the CDA Pregnancy IG),
was running concurrently with the IPS ballot in May 2018 and proposes
that the IPS adopt — or at least align with — concepts from that work,
specifically: Aborta, Date of First Prenatal Care Visit, Date of last
live birth, detailed Estimated Date of Delivery, Estimated Gestational
Age of Pregnancy, Gravida, Parity, and a general Pregnancy
Observation. The motivation given is that pregnancy is potentially
high-risk and involves more than one life, so summary data carried
between providers should be richer than a simple pregnant/not-pregnant
indicator.

The ticket originally received a 2018-08-02 Cologne IPS resolution of
**Considered for Future Use** (Hausam/Beebe, 18-0-2): the WG noted that
the main purpose of the IPS Pregnancy section is to highlight whether
the subject is pregnant and optionally give the EDD, and that detailed
prior-pregnancy information was deemed out of scope for IPS, with a
note that a future revision could revisit this once the CDA Pregnancy
IG matured. That resolution was later **reverted** by Lynn Laakso on
2022-11-22 ("re-open post STU update publication"), so the ticket is
back in Triaged status against the IPS FHIR specification and is
awaiting a fresh disposition. No cross-referenced Jira tickets were
found, and the only Zulip cross-reference is the automated tracker-bot
post that announces the original GForge item.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Expand the IPS **History of Pregnancy Section** in the FHIR IPS IG so
that, in addition to the existing pregnancy-status / EDD content, it
defines a recommended set of pregnancy-history observations modelled
on the CDA Pregnancy IG concepts called out in the ticket. Concretely:

- Add IPS-flavoured profiles (or extend the existing Pregnancy
  profiles) for `Observation` covering: Pregnancy status, Estimated
  Date of Delivery, Estimated Gestational Age, Gravida, Parity,
  Aborta, Date of First Prenatal Care Visit, and Date of Last Live
  Birth, each bound to the LOINC / SNOMED codes used by the CDA
  Pregnancy IG and (where they exist) the corresponding US Core /
  international pregnancy-observation profiles.
- Update the IPS **History of Pregnancy** section composition rule to
  permit (SHOULD, not SHALL) inclusion of the above observations in
  addition to the current pregnancy-status entry, so that consumers
  can carry richer history without breaking minimal IPS implementers.
- Add narrative guidance in the section's intro page and in the IG
  Composition rules describing how to populate / consume each new
  entry, with explicit alignment notes to CDAR2_IG_PREGNANCY.

#### Justification

This directly answers the ticket and aligns the FHIR IPS with the CDA
Pregnancy IG that the reporter flagged, producing cross-paradigm
parity for a clinically high-stakes domain. Most of the elements
requested already have stable LOINC codings and existing FHIR
Observation patterns (e.g., gestational age, EDD, gravida/parity), so
the IG additions are largely profile work rather than new resource
work. Keeping the new entries optional (SHOULD) preserves backward
compatibility for current minimal IPS implementations while letting
maternal-care senders carry richer summaries.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Do **not** extend the IPS History of Pregnancy section in the IPS
core. Instead, publish a small companion artifact — either an "IPS
Pregnancy Supplement" page in the IPS IG or a thin separate IG — that
defines the pregnancy-history observation set (Gravida, Parity,
Aborta, EDD detail, Gestational Age, First Prenatal Visit, Last Live
Birth, Pregnancy Observation) and references the CDA Pregnancy IG /
forthcoming FHIR pregnancy work as the normative source. The IPS
Composition would continue to require only the
pregnancy-status/EDD-style entry, but the History of Pregnancy section
intro would point implementers to the supplement when richer data is
in scope.

#### Justification

The original 2018 Cologne resolution argued that detailed
prior-pregnancy data is out of scope for an *international patient
summary* whose intended use is brief, cross-border continuity. That
argument has not gone away: pushing a richer pregnancy data set into
the IPS core risks inflating the summary, complicating Composition
slicing, and dragging IPS into terminology debates that are better
owned by a dedicated pregnancy/maternal-health IG. A supplement keeps
the IPS lean while still giving the reporter (and CDA IG users) a
sanctioned FHIR home for the same concepts. It also avoids cardinality
/ MS churn in the core IPS profiles, which is a known source of
implementer pain.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Persuasive: close the ticket with rationale **Out of Scope –
referred elsewhere**. Restate the 2018 Cologne position: the IPS
History of Pregnancy Section exists to communicate "is the patient
pregnant, and if so what is the EDD" for cross-border / cross-provider
summary use. Detailed pregnancy history (Gravida/Parity/Aborta,
prenatal-visit dates, last-live-birth dates, gestational-age
observations) belongs in pregnancy-specific IGs (the CDA Pregnancy IG
that triggered the ticket, and any successor FHIR pregnancy IG), not
in the IPS itself. Note that nothing in the IPS prevents a sender from
including additional Observations in the section — implementers who
need richer data already have an extensibility path.

#### Justification

The IPS has always been deliberately scoped as a minimum patient
summary; the work group has explicitly resolved this question once
already and the underlying scope argument has not changed. Adding a
half-dozen optional pregnancy observations expands the IPS surface
area, drives terminology and binding decisions that are better owned
by maternal-health stewards, and is unlikely to be implemented
consistently in the IPS's primary cross-border use case. The reporter
also did not supply implementer commitments or use cases beyond the
parallel CDA ballot. Declining is defensible because the
extensibility/profiling mechanisms in FHIR already let
maternity-focused implementers carry these observations in the
section without an IPS spec change.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the best balance of the two competing pressures
visible in the ticket history. The 2022 reversal of the original
"Considered for Future Use" resolution signals that the WG no longer
wants to simply close the door — there is real demand for a FHIR home
for the CDA Pregnancy IG concepts, and the reporter's clinical safety
argument (pregnancy is high-risk, two+ lives) is sound. At the same
time, the Cologne committee was right that bolting full pregnancy
history into the IPS core risks scope creep and would force
terminology decisions onto IPS that belong to maternal-health
stewards. A supplement or companion artifact lets IPS stay lean
(addressing the gotcha that drove Disposition C), while still giving
implementers a sanctioned FHIR pattern aligned with the CDA Pregnancy
IG (addressing what the reporter actually asked for in Disposition A).
If the WG decides the appetite is stronger than that, Disposition A is
the natural fallback; if the WG concludes there is still no
implementer pull, Disposition C remains defensible and consistent with
the prior vote.
