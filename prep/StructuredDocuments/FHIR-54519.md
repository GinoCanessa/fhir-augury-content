## Summary

The reporter asks Structured Documents (SDWG) to align the definition of
`Composition.text` with the general FHIR-i rule for `Resource.text` — namely,
that the narrative must contain "sufficient detail to make it clinically safe
for a human to just read the narrative" — or to explicitly carve out an
exception. Reading the current `Composition.text` description literally, the
reporter assumes that subject/header information *and* the content of every
`Composition.section.text` should be fully reflected in `Composition.text`. If
SDWG actually intends a different model (for example, that `section.text`
content is *not* duplicated into `Composition.text` and that `Composition.text`
is meant to carry only header / document-level information such as author,
custodian, subject), the reporter wants this stated explicitly in the
specification, in coordination with FHIR-I, so implementers do not have to
infer it.

The single cross-reference attached to this ticket is to the FHIR artifact
`Composition.text`, confirming the request is scoped to that element's
definition and accompanying narrative guidance. There are no formally linked
Jira issues or GitHub items, but several closely related Jira tickets exist
that have already shaped the area:
[FHIR-45975](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-45975) (Published — added
guidance recommending `Composition.text` be used for document-level
information and that `narrativeLink` IDs not repeat between `Composition.text`
and `section.text`),
[FHIR-50934](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50934) (Applied — same reporter,
asking that signature canonicalization make the treatment of `section.text`
explicit when `Resource.text` is excluded),
[FHIR-14167](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-14167) (Resolved – No Change —
older request for explicit guidance reconciling `Composition.section.text` and
referenced-resource `Narrative` for non-document Compositions),
[FHIR-43193](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43193) (Triaged — IPS lab
mapping confusion that hinges on the same ambiguity), and
[FHIR-41146](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-41146) (Published — narrative
text linking guidance in `Composition.section.text`). Together these show a
recurring implementer-level confusion that this ticket is asking SDWG and
FHIR-I to finally settle in normative-style wording on the `Composition.text`
element definition itself.

## Details

**Description:**

> I assume that Composition resource Narrative refers to the
> Composition.text field. The definition of this field states
>
> > "A human-readable narrative that contains a summary of the resource and
> > can be used to represent the content of the resource to a human. The
> > narrative need not encode all the structured data, but is required to
> > contain sufficient detail to make it 'clinically safe' for a human to
> > just read the narrative. Resource definitions may define what content
> > should be represented in the narrative to ensure clinical safety."
>
> This leads to the assumption that both subject and the section.text fields
> are included in the composition.text. If this is not the case, please state
> so explicitly and align with FHIRi to document this deviation of the rule
> for Composition resources.
>
> If section.text is excluded, the narrative should hold a description of the
> header information (including author, custodian, subject, ...).

## Related Jira Tickets

The tickets below are not formal cross-references; they were surfaced by
text search for the same `Composition.text` / `section.text` / narrative
concern and provide important precedent for the disposition discussion.

### [FHIR-45975: Add guidance for minimal expectations of Composition.text](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-45975)

Published, "Persuasive with Modification". Originated at the May 2024
Connectathon to address exactly the same confusion: what belongs in
`Composition.text` vs `Composition.section.text`. The accepted direction
was that `Composition.text` should carry document-level information
(title, patient name, document metadata), that since `section.text` is
required not all section content needs to be repeated in
`Composition.text`, and that `narrativeLink` IDs must not collide between
`Composition.text` and `section.text`. This is the most direct precedent
for FHIR-54519 and arguably already partially answers it; FHIR-54519 is
asking for the model to be made *normatively explicit* on the element
definition itself and aligned with FHIR-I.

Users involved: John D'Amore (reporter), SDWG triage/applier.

### [FHIR-50934: Signatures should allow exclusion of section.text in Compositions](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50934)

Applied, "Persuasive with Modification". Filed by the same reporter
(Bas van den Heuvel). Highlights that the canonicalization URI
`http://hl7.org/fhir/canonicalization/json#data` removes `Resource.text`
prior to signing but is silent about `Composition.section.text`. The
acceptance of this ticket already implies that SDWG/FHIR-I treat
`section.text` as a separate "narrative-bearing" element distinct from
`Composition.text`, which is exactly the deviation FHIR-54519 wants
documented. Strongly reinforces FHIR-54519's concern.

Users involved: Bas van den Heuvel (reporter).

### [FHIR-14167: Composition.section.text vs Narrative in resource for non-document compositions](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-14167)

Resolved – No Change, "Not Persuasive". Eric Haas asked for explicit
guidance reconciling `section.text` with referenced-resource `Narrative`
for non-document Compositions, enumerating four scenarios. SDWG declined
at the time. FHIR-54519 effectively re-opens that family of questions
from the FHIR-I-alignment angle and has the benefit of FHIR-45975 having
landed in the meantime.

Users involved: Eric Haas (reporter).

### [FHIR-43193: Representation of narrative](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43193)

Triaged, Unresolved. IPS / lab-report mapping question: should
`Result.narrative.report` and `Result.narrative.notes` be mapped to
`Composition.text`, `Composition.section[lab-no-subsections].text`, or
`Composition.section[lab-subsections].section.text`? This is exactly the
implementer-level fallout the reporter is trying to prevent — without a
clear rule about what `Composition.text` must contain relative to
`section.text`, IGs continue to have to invent answers per use case.

Users involved: Gabriel Leonhartsberger (reporter).

### [FHIR-41146: Add explicit guidance on use of Narrative Text Linking in FHIR Composition.section.text](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-41146)

Published, "Persuasive with Modification". Lisa Nelson (CDA Examples Task
Force) requested explicit guidance, drawn from CDA practice, on
narrative-to-entry linking in `Composition.section.text`. The accepted
guidance treats `section.text` as the primary narrative-bearing element
inside a section, which again presupposes the very `Composition.text` vs
`section.text` distinction that FHIR-54519 wants stated normatively.

Users involved: Lisa R. Nelson (reporter).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the element definition of `Composition.text` (the inherited
`DomainResource.text` description on the Composition resource page, and
the corresponding text on the Composition narrative / "Notes" section)
to **explicitly state the Composition-specific deviation from the
general `Resource.text` rule**, in coordination with FHIR-I. Concretely:

- Add a "Composition-specific narrative requirements" paragraph to the
  Composition resource page (and a matching note in the StructureDefinition
  short/comment for `Composition.text`) that says approximately: *"Unlike
  the general rule for `Resource.text`, the narrative in `Composition.text`
  is not required to be a complete clinically-safe summary of the
  document's section content. Per-section narrative is carried by
  `Composition.section.text`. `Composition.text` SHOULD contain
  document-header information (title, subject, author, custodian, date,
  type, encounter) sufficient to identify and contextualise the document
  when rendered standalone, and MAY summarise or omit per-section
  content. Renderers of a Composition SHOULD render `Composition.text`
  followed by each `Composition.section.text` (recursively), so that no
  clinically significant content is lost."*
- Cross-link to the existing FHIR-45975 guidance (document-level info,
  no `narrativeLink` ID collisions between `Composition.text` and
  `section.text`) so the rules sit in one place.
- File a coordinated FHIR-I tracker referencing FHIR-54519 so the
  exception is acknowledged in the `Resource.text` / Narrative datatype
  page rather than being a silent SDWG-only carve-out.

#### Justification

This is the smallest change that actually does what the reporter asks:
remove the ambiguity between the general `Resource.text` rule and the
behaviour SDWG already assumes for Composition. The accepted resolutions
on FHIR-45975 and FHIR-50934, the IPS Connectathon 37 consensus, and
Lloyd McKenzie's long-standing framing in the *implementers > SOAP
Notes* thread all already presuppose this model — FHIR-54519 simply
asks SDWG to write it down on the element definition. Coordinating with
FHIR-I (rather than carving it out unilaterally) is the explicit ask in
the ticket and avoids the same confusion re-emerging on the Narrative
datatype page.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than adding new narrative wording to the Composition resource
page, **consolidate and re-point**: produce a single "Narrative in
Composition" section in the Documents module / Composition Notes page
that comprehensively describes the relationship between
`Composition.text`, `Composition.section.text`, and any referenced
resources' `Resource.text` (covering rendering, signatures
[FHIR-50934], narrative linking [FHIR-41146], and minimal-content
expectations [FHIR-45975]). Update the `Composition.text` element
definition to a one-sentence pointer at this consolidated section, and
update the `Resource.text`/Narrative page on the FHIR-I side with a
single cross-reference noting that Composition has additional rules.
Optionally add an invariant on Composition (e.g.,
`cmp-narrative-coverage`) recommending — not requiring — that the union
of `Composition.text` and all `section.text` provide a clinically safe
summary of the document.

#### Justification

The recurring tickets in this area (FHIR-14167, FHIR-41146, FHIR-43193,
FHIR-44919, FHIR-45975, FHIR-50934, FHIR-53893, FHIR-54251, FHIR-54519)
show that the problem is fragmentation: the rules are spread across the
resource page, the Narrative datatype, the canonicalization page, and
several IGs. A single authoritative section is more durable than another
paragraph bolted onto the element definition, and it lets future
clarifications (signatures, narrative linking, IG-specific overrides)
land in one well-known place. The trade-off vs Disposition A is more
editing work and a longer review cycle, but materially less risk of the
same ticket recurring next ballot.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark FHIR-54519 as "Resolved – No Change", on the basis that
FHIR-45975 already added guidance that `Composition.text` carries
document-level information and that section content lives in
`Composition.section.text`, and that Composition does not in fact
deviate from the general `Resource.text` rule — it merely *delegates*
section narrative to `section.text`, which is itself a Narrative and is
covered by the same general rule. Close with a pointer to the
FHIR-45975 guidance and to FHIR-14167 (the previous "Not Persuasive"
disposition of the analogous question).

#### Justification

A defensible-but-weak rationale: the literal `Resource.text` definition
says the narrative must be sufficient for clinical safety — it does not
say a single `Resource.text` instance must carry *all* of it. A reader
who treats the rendered Composition (header narrative + each section's
narrative + recursively contained sections) as the human-facing
artefact can argue that nothing in the current spec is wrong and that
FHIR-45975 + FHIR-41146 already give implementers what they need. The
risk is that this interpretation is precisely what implementers
*cannot* derive from the current text without help, which is why this
ticket — and FHIR-43193, FHIR-54251 and the SOAP Notes / IPS Zulip
threads — keep being filed.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A is the right size and shape for the ask. The reporter is
not asking SDWG to invent a new model; they are asking SDWG to write
down, on the `Composition.text` element itself, the model that
FHIR-45975, FHIR-50934 and the IPS Connectathon 37 consensus already
assume. Doing that as element-definition text plus a coordinated FHIR-I
tracker is cheap, directly answers the ticket, and stops the ambiguity
from continuing to leak into related work (signatures in FHIR-50934,
search-coverage questions like the *implementers > Search the text of a
specific section of Composition* thread, IPS lab-mapping in FHIR-43193,
and the recurring SOAP Notes-style discussions). Disposition B is
strictly better in the long run but is materially more work and risks
slipping past the current ballot window; if SDWG has appetite for it,
Disposition A's text can be drafted in a way that is later folded into
the Disposition B consolidated section without rework. Disposition C is
not recommended — it relies on an interpretation that the field
demonstrably does not arrive at on its own and would predictably
produce another instance of this same ticket within one or two ballot
cycles.
