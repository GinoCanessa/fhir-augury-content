## Summary

FHIR-16003 reports that the IPS (International Patient Summary) profile of
`DeviceUseStatement` lost constraints that were intended to be carried by the
profile. Specifically, the reporter (Francois Macary) calls out that the
constraints on `whenUsed` (cardinality `1..1`) and on `device` (no
identifier) are not captured in the IPS IG. A follow-up comment by the same
reporter notes that constraints on `indication` and `bodySite` are also
missing. The ticket originally received a 2018-08-02 ballot vote of
"Considered for Future Use" (Rob Hausam / Gay Dolin: 16-0-3, deferred to a
later release) but was reverted and re-opened by Lynn Laakso post-STU update
publication on 2022-11-22, so it is presently in `Triaged` status and remains
unresolved.

The ticket sits in a small cluster of IPS-DeviceUseStatement issues that
shape its context:

- [FHIR-16204](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-16204) — *Wrong content shown
  (deviceusestatement-uv-ipss)* (Persuasive / Published) — Giorgio Cangioli
  observed that the rendered IPS DeviceUseStatement profile page was showing
  only the `subject` constraint, suggesting either the wrong instance was
  rendered or an IG-builder bug. This is essentially the same surface-level
  symptom FHIR-16003 documents and confirms the constraints loss was real,
  not just a rendering anomaly.
- [FHIR-16293](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-16293) — *Add
  data-absent-reason* (Persuasive / Published) — Adds `data-absent-reason`
  support to several IPS elements including `DeviceUseStatement.whenUsed`,
  reinforcing that `whenUsed` is a required-but-may-be-unknown element in
  IPS — directly relevant to whether the `1..1` constraint on `whenUsed`
  should be reinstated.
- [FHIR-47530](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-47530) — *Review of Device
  Use Statement (IPS) profile* (Not Persuasive / Resolved - No Change) — Notes
  that R4 `DeviceUseStatement` has maturity 0 and that R5 has moved to
  `DeviceUsage` with a richer `device` reference (`Device | DeviceDefinition`).
  Highlights forward-compatibility tension that any "fix" to the IPS profile
  must navigate.
- [FHIR-47602](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-47602) — *DeviceUseStatement
  is still provisional* (Not Persuasive / Resolved - No Change) — Argues IPS
  should not lean on a provisional resource and points at `DeviceAssociation`
  as the R5+ patient-link path. Same forward-compatibility concern.
- [FHIR-51239](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-51239) — *Device profile
  should not have constraints on patient element* (Persuasive / Published) —
  Confirms the working position that `DeviceUseStatement` (not `Device`)
  is the element that should carry the Patient link in IPS, which is
  consistent with leaving the `DeviceUseStatement` profile as the locus of
  patient/usage constraints.

Together, these establish that the IPS workgroup has accepted (in
FHIR-16204) that DeviceUseStatement constraints went missing, has actively
profiled `whenUsed` since (FHIR-16293), and has confirmed the resource's
profile remains the right place to express patient-linked Device usage in
IPS (FHIR-51239) — even while acknowledging the resource is provisional in
R4.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Reinstate the constraints on the IPS `DeviceUseStatement` profile
(`DeviceUseStatement-uv-ips`) that the ticket reports as lost:

- `DeviceUseStatement.whenUsed`: set min cardinality to `1` (must-support /
  required, consistent with the IPS pattern that `data-absent-reason` from
  FHIR-16293 is the unknown-value escape hatch).
- `DeviceUseStatement.device`: prohibit `Device.identifier` on the
  referenced Device (i.e., constrain the in-line referenced Device profile
  / slice to `identifier 0..0`, or whatever shape the original "no
  identifier" rule had — capture this as a structural constraint or a
  `constraint` invariant on the profile).
- `DeviceUseStatement.indication`: restore the original cardinality /
  binding constraint that the reporter cites as also missing (per the
  follow-up comment).
- `DeviceUseStatement.bodySite`: restore the original cardinality /
  binding constraint that the reporter cites as also missing.

The change lands in `HL7/fhir-ips` against the IPS profile differential for
`DeviceUseStatement-uv-ips`, with regenerated narrative/snapshots and an
IG-publisher build to confirm the rendered page (which was the symptom in
the related FHIR-16204) shows the restored constraints.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the ticket asks for and is internally consistent with
positions the workgroup has already taken: FHIR-16204 confirmed the
constraints were really missing from the rendered page; FHIR-16293 affirmed
`whenUsed` as a profiled (must-support) element by adding
`data-absent-reason` support to it; FHIR-51239 confirmed that
`DeviceUseStatement` (not `Device`) is the right home for IPS
patient-linked Device-usage constraints. Restoring the four constraints is
therefore the minimum action that aligns the published profile with the
profile the workgroup has consistently described.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Reinstate the `whenUsed` and `device.identifier` constraints (the two
explicitly described in the ticket body) and the `indication` /
`bodySite` constraints (from the follow-up comment) — but *as
guidance/invariants with `mustSupport=true` rather than hard cardinality
changes*, and pair them with `data-absent-reason` allowance on `whenUsed`
(per FHIR-16293).

Concretely:
- `whenUsed`: `mustSupport=true`, cardinality remains as the base resource
  permits, with a profile invariant requiring `whenUsed.exists() or
  extension('data-absent-reason').exists()`.
- `device`: keep the "no `Device.identifier`" expectation as a profile
  invariant (FHIRPath `device.resolve().identifier.empty()`) rather than a
  structural slice on the referenced Device.
- `indication` / `bodySite`: marked `mustSupport` with documented
  expectations, not necessarily `1..1`.

#### Justification

This honors the spirit of FHIR-16003 while accommodating the long-running
concerns raised in FHIR-47530 and FHIR-47602 — that R4
`DeviceUseStatement` is a maturity-0/provisional resource and that the IPS
profile may need to migrate to R5 `DeviceUsage` / `DeviceAssociation`.
Soft constraints (must-support + invariants + DAR) carry less migration
cost and don't make the IPS profile more brittle than the underlying
resource. It also avoids the awkward case where strict `1..1 whenUsed`
fights with real-world IPS source records that legitimately don't know
when the device was used.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-16003 as "Already Addressed" without further changes, on the
basis that:
- FHIR-16204 (Persuasive / Published) already covered the rendered-profile
  symptom and presumably triggered re-publication of the IPS
  DeviceUseStatement profile;
- FHIR-16293 (Persuasive / Published) already added `data-absent-reason`
  on `DeviceUseStatement.whenUsed`, which captures the working assumption
  that `whenUsed` is profiled;
- FHIR-47530 and FHIR-47602 (Not Persuasive) established that the
  workgroup is not, at this time, restructuring the IPS DeviceUseStatement
  profile around R5 DeviceUsage/DeviceAssociation;
- The ticket has been open since 2018, was once balloted as "Considered
  for Future Use", and has had no substantive activity since the 2022
  re-open.

#### Justification

If the published `DeviceUseStatement-uv-ips` profile *currently* carries
the constraints listed in FHIR-16003, then the ticket has been overtaken
by events and can be closed without change. Declining is defensible only
if the workgroup verifies on-page that `whenUsed`, `device.identifier`,
`indication`, and `bodySite` are constrained as described — otherwise
this disposition would close a real defect.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket reports a concrete, falsifiable defect (specific elements,
specific constraints) on a profile the workgroup has, in adjacent
Persuasive/Published tickets, repeatedly affirmed as the right home for
patient-linked Device usage in IPS. FHIR-16204 confirmed the rendering
problem was real, FHIR-16293 implicitly confirmed `whenUsed` as a
profiled element, and FHIR-51239 confirmed `DeviceUseStatement` carries
the IPS patient link. The Patient-Care-side R5/maturity concerns
(FHIR-47530, FHIR-47602) were both resolved as Not Persuasive, so the
workgroup has already declined to defer this kind of fix on
forward-compatibility grounds. Disposition B is a reasonable hedge if
the workgroup wants to soften `1..1 whenUsed` (and is consistent with
FHIR-16293's `data-absent-reason` allowance), so it should be the
fallback if anyone objects to strict cardinality. Disposition C should
only be selected if a current rendering of `DeviceUseStatement-uv-ips`
already shows all four constraints — in which case the workgroup
should close FHIR-16003 with a pointer to the page that demonstrates
it.
