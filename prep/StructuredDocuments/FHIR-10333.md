## Summary

The submitter reports that the C-CDA on FHIR guide's mapping for the History
and Physical (H&P) composition does not address four composition-level
C-CDA constraints (CONF:1198-30613 through CONF:1198-30616). These
constraints govern which sections a C-CDA H&P `structuredBody` must, and
must not, contain — specifically mutually-exclusive combinations of the
Chief Complaint / Reason for Visit / combined "Chief Complaint and Reason
for Visit" sections, and the Assessment / Plan of Treatment / combined
"Assessment and Plan" sections. The C-CDA on FHIR composition profile as
published does not encode these SHALL / SHALL NOT rules, so a conforming
FHIR Composition derived from it could validate cleanly while still
violating the source CDA conformance. The request is to close that gap in
the FHIR profile/guidance for the H&P composition so the C-CDA-on-FHIR
representation preserves the same structural requirements as the CDA
source. The ticket was originally filed in 2016 and re-opened by Lynn
Laakso in March 2024 for work post-STU 1.2 publication. There are no
linked or cross-referenced Jira tickets and no related GitHub items in
FhirAugury's index; the only cross-reference is an automated `FHIR Bot`
post to the Zulip `committers > tracker-item` stream announcing the
ticket's creation.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the C-CDA on FHIR profile for the *History and Physical
Composition* (`ccda-history-and-physical-composition`) so that the four
CDA constraints CONF:1198-30613 through CONF:1198-30616 are materially
enforced on the derived FHIR `Composition`:

1. **Slice `Composition.section` on `section.code`** using the LOINC
   codes already mapped to the relevant CDA templates:
   - Chief Complaint Section (1.3.6.1.4.1.19376.1.5.3.1.1.13.2.1) →
     LOINC 10154-3
   - Reason for Visit Section (…22.2.12) → LOINC 29299-5
   - Chief Complaint and Reason for Visit Section (…22.2.13) →
     LOINC 46239-0
   - Assessment Section (…22.2.8) → LOINC 51848-0
   - Plan of Treatment Section V2 (…22.2.10) → LOINC 18776-5
   - Assessment and Plan Section V2 (…22.2.9) → LOINC 51847-2
2. Add FHIRPath invariants on `Composition` expressing the SHALL /
   SHALL NOT rules:
   - **ccda-hp-1 (CONF:1198-30613):** exactly one of
     {Chief Complaint and Reason for Visit} OR
     {Chief Complaint and/or Reason for Visit} must be present:
     `section.where(code.coding.code='46239-0').exists() or
      section.where(code.coding.code='10154-3' or code.coding.code='29299-5').exists()`
   - **ccda-hp-2 (CONF:1198-30614):** exactly one of
     {Assessment and Plan} OR {Assessment + Plan of Treatment} must be
     present (analogous expression using LOINC 51847-2 vs.
     51848-0 / 18776-5).
   - **ccda-hp-3 (CONF:1198-30615):** Assessment and Plan (51847-2)
     MUST NOT co-occur with Assessment (51848-0) or Plan of Treatment
     (18776-5).
   - **ccda-hp-4 (CONF:1198-30616):** Chief Complaint and Reason for
     Visit (46239-0) MUST NOT co-occur with Chief Complaint (10154-3)
     or Reason for Visit (29299-5).
3. Update the composition's narrative / structure table on
   `ccda-history-and-physical-composition.html` to describe the new
   slices and invariants, and regenerate the composition example(s) so
   that they pass.

#### Justification

This disposition closes a real conformance gap: a C-CDA H&P serialized
as a FHIR `Composition` via the current profile can satisfy the profile
but still violate the CDA source's conformance. Because the CDA
constraints are purely structural (presence/absence of named sections
identified by template OID), they translate cleanly into
`Composition.section` slicing plus a small number of FHIRPath
invariants — mechanisms C-CDA on FHIR already uses elsewhere. Keeping
the FHIR representation faithful to the CDA source is the stated
purpose of C-CDA on FHIR, so missing constraints on the H&P document
type are in-scope to fix. The comment thread confirms the Work Group
explicitly wants this revisited post-STU 1.2.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than encoding all four constraints as hard `Composition`
invariants, address CONF:1198-30613 / 30614 with **slice cardinalities**
on `Composition.section` and express CONF:1198-30615 / 30616 (the
mutual-exclusion SHALL NOT rules) as **warning-level invariants** on
the Composition profile. Concretely:

- Define required-choice slice groups on `Composition.section`
  (min = 0/1 combined via `slicing.rules = open` plus a profile-level
  FHIRPath ensuring at least one variant is present) for CONF:1198-30613
  and CONF:1198-30614.
- Express the SHALL NOT mutual-exclusion rules as invariants with
  `severity = warning` and textual guidance on
  `ccda-history-and-physical-composition.html` explaining that
  round-tripping to CDA requires the exclusions to hold, even though
  they do not fail FHIR validation.
- Leave Section profiles themselves unchanged.

#### Justification

The underlying C-CDA source is conformant at the CDA level; when content
is authored natively as FHIR, authors may legitimately include both a
combined and a split section during migration, and a hard `error`
invariant on the FHIR side would block otherwise-useful content and
make it harder to accept partial C-CDA input during conversion.
Warning-level invariants still surface the problem in validators, still
block CDA round-trips, and keep the FHIR composition profile usable as
a FHIR-native authoring target. This approach also costs less
maintenance: the guidance page carries the narrative explanation, and
the profile only has to carry cardinality, not a per-section exclusion
matrix.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as "Not Persuasive" on the grounds that C-CDA on FHIR
is a **representation** guide, not a CDA-conformance enforcer: the
responsibility for producing a structurally conformant H&P document
rests with the CDA-side validator (or the conversion tool), and the
FHIR profile intentionally stays permissive to accommodate partial or
in-progress compositions. Document this position in the H&P composition
page with a short note pointing readers to the authoritative C-CDA H&P
companion guide for CONF:1198-30613 through CONF:1198-30616.

#### Justification

Each of the four constraints is a cross-section structural rule on the
C-CDA document type, not a statement about the contents of any
individual resource. Replicating them on the FHIR side duplicates logic
that already lives in the C-CDA companion guide and risks drift if the
CDA rules evolve. If adopters need machine-checkable enforcement, they
can use the C-CDA Schematron/companion validation on the produced CDA
artifact; adding redundant FHIR-side invariants increases maintenance
burden without adding new validation power for implementers who have
CDA tooling in place.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The request is narrow, correct, and testable: four clearly-scoped CDA
constraints that have direct, mechanical analogs in FHIR's `Composition`
slicing + invariant machinery. Declining (Disposition C) effectively
tells implementers that the C-CDA on FHIR profile is silent on a
structural rule that the CDA source makes mandatory, which undermines
the guide's core value proposition of a faithful FHIR representation of
C-CDA. The warning-only variant (Disposition B) is a reasonable
compromise if the Work Group has strong feedback from implementers that
hard failures would break in-flight migration tooling, but absent such
feedback the mutual-exclusion rules are genuinely SHALL NOT in CDA and
should be SHALL NOT in the FHIR profile so that a passing FHIR
validation implies (for these four rules) a passing CDA validation.
Disposition A also keeps the lift small — slicing plus four invariants
localized to one composition profile — and the re-open comment from
Lynn Laakso in 2024 signals the Work Group wants this revisited now
that STU 1.2 is out of the way.
