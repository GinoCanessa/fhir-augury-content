## Summary

The reporter asks that the current "trial use" note on the Composition
resource — which states that `$document` does not resolve how document
signatures are created, and that feedback is requested during the TU
period — be relocated onto the `$document` OperationDefinition itself,
and that the trial-use framing be dropped. The underlying position is
that signing a generated document *should* be possible via `$document`,
and that the open question should travel with the operation (where the
bundling/signing mechanics actually live) rather than sit on the
Composition resource narrative. The reporter explicitly scopes the
experimentation on "the best way to do so" to work that happens
**outside R6** (i.e., post-R6 exploration), so this ticket is primarily
an editorial/placement change plus removal of the TU comment — not a
proposal for a concrete signing mechanism in R6. No cross-referenced
Jira tickets were returned by the cross-reference index. A comment by
ehaas points at FHIR-54573, but that ticket is about the Variable Role
CodeSystem name/title swap and has no substantive connection to
`$document` signatures; the reference appears to be miskeyed.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Move the existing trial-use note off the Composition resource narrative
and onto the `$document` OperationDefinition (Composition's `$document`
operation) as a non-trial-use editorial note that records the open
question ("how document-level signatures are carried / produced by
`$document` is not yet specified"), and drop the "trial use / feedback
requested" framing. No change to the operation's parameters, the
generated Bundle shape, or signature mechanics in R6. The accompanying
exploration of *how* to actually sign `$document`-generated bundles is
explicitly deferred to post-R6 work (e.g., a connectathon track or a
follow-up change request against `$document`).

#### Justification

This matches the reporter's literal request and is a low-risk editorial
relocation: the note lives where the behaviour actually lives (the
operation), and the Structured Documents WG retains the ability to
evolve signing without re-opening a TU flag on Composition. It aligns
with normal FHIR practice of putting operation-scoped guidance on the
OperationDefinition's description, and it avoids claiming a signing
mechanism the WG has not yet agreed. Removing the TU comment also
reflects reality: Composition itself is not what is under trial here —
the signing approach for `$document` is.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Relocate the note to the `$document` OperationDefinition as in A, **and
additionally** replace the open-ended "feedback requested" text with a
short, concrete implementer guidance paragraph pointing at existing
mechanisms that can already carry a signature for a generated document
— specifically `Bundle.signature` on the returned document Bundle, and
`Provenance` resources referring to the Bundle — flagged as
non-normative guidance pending a dedicated post-R6 design. Keep the
content change out of R6 normative text (description-only), so no
conformance impact.

#### Justification

The request's underlying need is "make signatures possible from
`$document`". FHIR already provides `Bundle.signature` and `Provenance`,
so implementers do not need to wait for a new mechanism — they need to
know that these are the intended hooks. Pointing at them in the
operation description gives readers an answer today while preserving
the WG's freedom to specify something richer later. The trade-off
versus A is a small amount of additional editorial content and the
need to agree on the pointer language; the upside is that
`$document`'s documentation stops reading like an unresolved TU
question and starts reading like guidance.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Leave the note where it is on the Composition resource for R6 and
retain the trial-use framing, on the grounds that the open question
about how `$document` signatures are produced is still genuinely
unresolved and that moving the note without resolving it is cosmetic.
Re-evaluate in a future cycle once a signing approach has been
prototyped.

#### Justification

The TU note exists specifically to solicit feedback during the trial
period; removing or relocating it before the question is answered
risks silently dropping a call for input that the WG explicitly
wanted. If the WG believes the signing question is still live and
under-specified, keeping the note in its current, prominent place on
Composition maximises the chance of getting implementer feedback into
the next cycle.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter's change is a clean editorial move: the note concerns
behaviour of `$document`, so it belongs on `$document`, and the
"trial-use / feedback requested" framing has not produced a resolution
during the TU window — keeping it on Composition for another cycle is
unlikely to change that. Disposition A fully captures the ticket as
filed, avoids committing the WG to a signing mechanism that has not
been agreed, and leaves the door open for a follow-up ticket to
specify signing (the work the reporter explicitly wants to do outside
R6). Disposition B is attractive and the WG should consider folding
its guidance text in if `Bundle.signature` / `Provenance` can be
described without implying normative conformance; if that language
cannot be agreed quickly, fall back to A rather than delay. Disposition
C preserves status-quo visibility but at the cost of leaving a stale
TU note on a resource that is otherwise ready to exit TU, which is a
worse outcome than relocating the open question to where it actually
applies.
