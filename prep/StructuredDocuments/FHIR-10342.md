## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Remove the **Related Person** `Composition.section` slice from the
US C-CDA on FHIR Transfer Summary Composition profile (the artifact
behind `ccda-transfer-summary-composition.html`) and from any other
C-CDA on FHIR Composition profiles in the IG that currently slice in a
"Related Person" section. Concretely:

1. In the Transfer Summary Composition profile's differential, delete
   the `Composition.section` slice keyed to the Related Person LOINC
   section code (and any associated `mustSupport`, narrative, and
   invariant entries that exist solely to support that slice).
2. Add (or confirm the presence of) a header-level extension on
   `Composition` (or `Composition.subject`/`Composition.author`-adjacent
   placement, per the workgroup's prior decision) that carries the
   `RelatedPerson` reference(s).
3. Update the IG narrative pages and any examples that included a
   Related Person section to instead populate the header extension.
4. Sweep the other C-CDA on FHIR Composition profiles (e.g., CCD,
   Discharge Summary, Referral Note, Progress Note) for the same
   section slice and apply the same removal where present.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter asked for and exactly what the
workgroup determined per the description: Related Person belongs in the
**header as an extension** rather than as a body section. Keeping a
section that the workgroup has already decided to retire creates a
contradiction in the IG (two valid ways to convey the same data) and
sets implementers up for interop drift between senders that put it in
the section and senders that put it in the header extension. Because
the ticket has been re-opened explicitly "post-STU1.2 publication," the
balloting window is the appropriate place to take the breaking change.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

**Deprecate, don't delete.** Mark the Related Person `Composition.section`
slice as deprecated in the differential (e.g., add a `standards-status`
extension of `deprecated` and a `Composition.section` description
warning that the slice will be removed in the next major version), and
add the new header extension as the **preferred** mechanism. Schedule
hard removal for the version after this one. Update the IG narrative
to direct implementers to the header extension and to flag the section
as deprecated.

#### Justification

The IG has been published through STU 1.2 with the section in place,
and there are likely real-world C-CDA on FHIR documents already
constructed against that profile. A two-step deprecation gives those
implementers a release cycle to migrate without producing instances
that fail validation against the new IG. It also lets the workgroup
gather feedback on the header-extension shape before locking in the
removal. Trade-off vs. Disposition A: the IG carries a small amount of
"legacy" surface for one cycle, in exchange for a smoother upgrade
path.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Persuasive with Modification — No Change**, on
the grounds that the original 2016 decision predates the current
header-extension mechanism and should be re-evaluated by Structured
Documents before any structural change is made. Specifically, decline
to remove the section in this cycle and instead require a fresh
analysis (with implementer feedback) to confirm that the header
extension fully covers the use cases the section covered (multiple
related persons, role coding, narrative blocks, etc.).

#### Justification

Sections in C-CDA traditionally carry their own narrative block, which
is meaningful for human-readable documents. A header extension on
`Composition` does not naturally carry a narrative slot, so removing
the section may lose the ability to render a human-readable
"Related Persons" block in the document narrative. If implementers are
relying on that narrative behavior, removing the section is a
regression dressed up as a cleanup. Declining keeps the IG stable while
the workgroup confirms that the header-extension path is feature-equivalent.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The reporter's underlying intent (Disposition A) is sound — a single
canonical place for Related Person data is better than two — but the
ticket has been open since 2016 and was re-opened "post-STU1.2
publication," which means there is a deployed surface to consider.
Hard-removing a section slice between STU versions is a breaking change
to any implementer who built against the published Transfer Summary
Composition, and the workgroup gets very little in return for taking
that hit in one step. Disposition B preserves the workgroup's
direction (header extension is the future) while giving implementers
one publication cycle to migrate, and it lets Structured Documents
verify that the header extension covers the narrative-rendering case
that Disposition C raises before the section is finally removed.
Disposition C is too conservative given that the workgroup has already
made the design call; Disposition A is too aggressive given the eight-year
gap and the STU-published baseline. B is the path that gets the IG
to the right end-state with the least implementer pain.
