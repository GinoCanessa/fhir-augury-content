## Summary

The Documents page in the FHIR Core specification currently explains the
relationship between FHIR documents (a Bundle containing a Composition and
related resources) and the DocumentReference resource, but it is silent on
how DiagnosticReport fits into the same conceptual space. Bas van den
Heuvel asks SDWG to extend that text so the page also describes the role
DiagnosticReport plays alongside Composition and DocumentReference.

This ticket is the FHIR R6 follow-up that Structured Documents committed
to file when resolving [FHIR-46693](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-46693)
("Revise the text for 'Limitations on certain CDA document types'") in the
FHIR Clinical Documents IG. That earlier ballot resolution was
"Persuasive" and added guidance that a DiagnosticReport can be treated as
a FHIR Clinical Document by wrapping it with a Composition (becoming an
entry from the first Composition in the document Bundle). The resolution
explicitly noted that "the use of DiagnosticReport.composition can overlap
with the use of Composition in a FHIR Clinical Document, and SDWG will
raise a FHIR R6 issue to gain clarity here." FHIR-54513, against FHIR
Core, is the place where that clarification is intended to land.

## Details

**Description:**

> Note that FHIR defines both this document format (a document bundle
> containing a Composition resource and potentially other resources) and
> a [DocumentReference resource](https://build.fhir.org/documentreference.html).
> FHIR documents are used for documents that are authored and assembled
> in FHIR, while the DocumentReference resource is for general references
> to documents (which may include FHIR documents as well as PDFs, CDAs,
> etc.).

Please also mention DiagnosticReport and the role it plays.

### [FHIR-46693: Revise the text for 'Limitations on certain CDA document types'](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-46693)

A FHIR Clinical Documents IG ticket from Giorgio Cangioli arguing that
the IG's text implying highly structured CDA documents are "more
appropriately mapped to the FHIR DiagnosticReport" was misleading,
because in some jurisdictions even a single-section lab report has to be
exchanged as a legal document and therefore needs the document paradigm
even if a DiagnosticReport is also used. SDWG resolved the ticket as
**Persuasive** (Published, ballot block-vote-6) and added guidance that a
DiagnosticReport can be promoted to a FHIR Clinical Document by adding a
Composition that references it as an entry, while explicitly noting that
DiagnosticReport.composition overlaps with the Composition in a FHIR
Clinical Document and that SDWG would raise a FHIR R6 issue against
Core. FHIR-54513 is that R6 issue.

Reporter: Giorgio Cangioli; resolution applied by bob.dolin
([commit a275cf3](https://github.com/HL7/fhir-clinical-document/commit/a275cf3ba110e84d701532e504ede1f9281a641c)).

## Related Zulip Discussions

No Zulip thread mentions FHIR-54513 by key. The two threads below were
surfaced by content search on the same problem space (DiagnosticReport
vs. Composition vs. DocumentReference / FHIR documents) and are the most
useful community context for the disposition discussion.

### [implementers > ✔ Composition relationship to DocumentReference](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/implementers/topic/%E2%9C%94%20Composition%20relationship%20to%20DocumentReference)

Vadim Peretokin asked how to express that a scanned, wet-signed PDF
DocumentReference is the rendering/signature of a source Composition
(document Bundle), since DocumentReference.relatesTo only points to other
DocumentReferences. The thread surfaced multiple competing mechanisms:
DocumentReference.related (R6, unspecified semantics — Lloyd McKenzie
called it an anti-pattern and raised FHIR-53550), the derivation-reference
extension, repurposing DocumentReference.subject (rejected), Provenance
(Kari Heinonen, Rik Smithies), and the rewritten R6 Signature rules
(Eric Haas, Grahame Grieve). John Moehrke clarified that
DocumentReference.relatesTo is about lifecycle of DocumentReferences, not
about pointing at a source Bundle/Composition, and that the historical
IHE pattern uses DocumentReference.related as a last-resort bucket. The
thread closed with Vadim adopting the R6 Signature approach
(Composition Bundle + Provenance.signature). The discussion shows there
is no single agreed mapping today between DocumentReference,
Composition/document Bundle, and the underlying clinical content — which
is exactly the gap FHIR-54513 asks Core to address for DiagnosticReport.

Participants: Vadim Peretokin, Rik Smithies, Kari Heinonen, John Moehrke,
Lloyd McKenzie, Eric Haas, Grahame Grieve, Gino Canessa.

### [europe > EU Lab DiagnosticReport - mandatory composition](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/europe/topic/EU%20Lab%20DiagnosticReport%20-%20mandatory%20composition)

Kevin Mayfield hit the EU Laboratory Report IG's
DiagnosticReportCompositionR5 extension being 1..1 (forcing every
DiagnosticReport to carry a Composition) while his LIMS environment has
no Composition / no FHIR document workflow. Giorgio Cangioli explained
that the EU Lab IG deliberately requires every Laboratory Report to be
exchanged as a FHIR document (one DiagnosticReport per FHIR document),
whereas the in-progress UV Lab Report IG treats the FHIR-document
representation as one of three options. John Moehrke's view was simply
"don't claim conformance until you have a Composition." Kevin's pragmatic
resolution was to switch to the EU Base diagnosticReport profile (or the
UV Lab Report ballot) where the Composition extension is optional, and
build the document representation later. The thread illustrates that
implementers experience real friction at exactly the
DiagnosticReport ↔ Composition ↔ FHIR document seam, and that different
IGs (EU Lab, UV Lab Report, US Core) have already made divergent choices
because Core does not say enough about the relationship.

Participants: Kevin Mayfield, John Moehrke, Giorgio Cangioli, Ralf Herzog.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the FHIR Core "Documents" narrative page (the same page whose
existing two-paragraph text the reporter quotes, which today contrasts
"FHIR documents (Composition + Bundle)" with "DocumentReference") to add
a third paragraph (and one entry in any accompanying comparison
table/diagram) describing DiagnosticReport's role:

- DiagnosticReport is the resource for the *clinical content* of a
  diagnostic test/study (lab, imaging, pathology, genomics) as a set of
  observations, conclusions, and presented forms. It is not, by itself,
  a FHIR document.
- A DiagnosticReport can be wrapped in a FHIR document by including it
  as the first entry of a `Bundle.type = document` whose Composition
  references it (the pattern adopted by the FHIR Clinical Documents IG
  resolution to FHIR-46693 and by the HL7 Europe Laboratory Report IG).
- The same DiagnosticReport (or a rendering of it) can also be indexed
  via a DocumentReference for registry / XDS-style retrieval, in which
  case the DocumentReference points at the document Bundle (or at an
  attachment such as a PDF rendering).
- Note the partial overlap with `DiagnosticReport.composition` (R5+) and
  cross-link to that element so implementers understand when to use each
  approach.

The change is purely narrative on the Documents page and a one-line
cross-reference from `DiagnosticReport` and `DocumentReference` page
intros pointing back to the new paragraph.

#### Justification

This is the minimal change that closes the SDWG commitment recorded in
the FHIR-46693 resolution ("SDWG will raise a FHIR R6 issue to gain
clarity here"). The reporter is asking only that DiagnosticReport be
*mentioned* alongside Composition and DocumentReference, and the FHIR
Clinical Documents IG already publishes the substantive guidance that
needs to be summarised in Core. The Zulip "EU Lab DiagnosticReport"
thread shows implementers and IG authors actively making divergent
choices at this seam (EU Lab requires Composition, UV Lab Report and US
Core do not), which is direct evidence that Core's current silence is
costing the community time. Pure narrative additions on a single page
are low-risk for R6 and do not affect any conformance machinery.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than only patching the Documents page, treat FHIR-54513 as the
trigger to publish a small **"Document, DocumentReference, DiagnosticReport
and Composition — when to use which"** decision-guide section, anchored
on the Documents page but linked from the resource intro of each of
DocumentReference, Composition and DiagnosticReport. The guide would:

- Restate the existing Documents/DocumentReference contrast.
- Add a cross-resource decision matrix (axes: structured vs.
  rendered/scanned; legally a document vs. clinical content; needs
  registry/discovery vs. directly retrievable) with the recommended
  resource(s) per cell, including the Composition+DiagnosticReport
  hybrid pattern from the Clinical Documents IG resolution.
- Explicitly call out the overlap between `DiagnosticReport.composition`
  and the document Bundle's Composition, and recommend which to use when
  (e.g., use `DiagnosticReport.composition` when the consumer is
  expected to retrieve the report and only optionally drill into the
  document; use a full document Bundle when the legal/exchange unit is
  the document).
- Cross-reference the FHIR Clinical Documents IG, the EU Lab Report IG
  and US Core's diagnostic-report profiles as worked examples.

#### Justification

The Zulip evidence (both the "Composition relationship to
DocumentReference" thread and the EU Lab DiagnosticReport thread) shows
the underlying confusion is broader than "DiagnosticReport isn't
mentioned on this page" — implementers are repeatedly reaching for
extensions, Provenance, `DocumentReference.related`, and ad-hoc IG
constraints because Core does not provide a normative pattern. A short
decision guide is still narrative-only (no conformance impact) but
delivers far more long-term value than a single new paragraph. It also
positions Core to absorb related R6 work such as Lloyd McKenzie's
critique of `DocumentReference.related` (FHIR-53550) and Eric Haas's
rewritten R6 Signature rules without needing yet another R7 ticket.

The trade-off versus Disposition A is scope: B requires SDWG to align
with FMG and the relevant resource owners (DocumentReference is owned
elsewhere), and to write more text. The risk is mostly editorial.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark FHIR-54513 as "Not Persuasive" (or "Already Addressed") on the
grounds that the substantive guidance the reporter is asking for already
lives in the FHIR Clinical Documents IG (the FHIR-46693 Persuasive
resolution), that DiagnosticReport.composition exists in R5+ as the
explicit cross-link element, and that the Documents page in FHIR Core is
intentionally scoped to *what a FHIR document is* and how it differs
from a DocumentReference. Optionally add a single sentence to the
Documents page pointing at the FHIR Clinical Documents IG and at
`DiagnosticReport.composition`, and close the ticket.

#### Justification

A defensible argument for declining is that Core narrative pages should
not duplicate IG guidance, and the FHIR Clinical Documents IG is
already the canonical home for "how DiagnosticReport relates to
Composition." Inserting the same material into Core invites drift
between the two, and the existing `DiagnosticReport.composition` element
plus its definition already encode the formal relationship. Core is
also not the right place to adjudicate IG-level choices like the EU Lab
Report's mandatory Composition.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A is the smallest change that honours the explicit SDWG
commitment recorded in the FHIR-46693 resolution and that directly
satisfies the reporter, who has only asked for DiagnosticReport to be
"mentioned" on the Documents page alongside Composition and
DocumentReference. It is purely narrative, lives entirely on a single
Core page, and carries effectively zero conformance risk for R6.

Disposition B is genuinely better in the long run — the Zulip threads
show the underlying confusion is broad and persistent — but it expands
scope into territory that touches resource owners outside Structured
Documents (notably DocumentReference) and into related R6 work
(FHIR-53550, R6 Signature rewrite). Recommend logging B as a follow-up
ticket and pursuing it iteratively rather than blocking FHIR-54513 on
it.

Disposition C is hard to defend given that SDWG itself committed in the
FHIR-46693 resolution to raise this exact R6 ticket; declining would
read as walking back that commitment, and the reporter's request is
modest enough that the cost of accepting is well below the cost of
explaining a decline.
