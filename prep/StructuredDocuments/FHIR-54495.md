## Summary

The ticket asks the Structured Documents work group to add explanatory text
to the FHIR Core Documents page describing the relationship between
`Composition` (and the FHIR document Bundle it heads) and `DiagnosticReport`,
together with guidance on when an implementer should pick one resource over
the other. Bas van den Heuvel observes that the current Documents narrative
discusses `Composition` and `DocumentReference` as the two clinical-document
constructs but is silent on `DiagnosticReport`, which in practice is also used
to convey clinical-document-like content (especially for laboratory, imaging
and operative reports).

The companion ticket [FHIR-54513](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54513)
(filed by the same reporter on the same day) makes the narrower request that
the Documents page "also mention `DiagnosticReport` and the role it plays",
so 54495 and 54513 should almost certainly be resolved together. The earlier
ticket [FHIR-46693](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-46693) was applied
against the FHIR Clinical Documents IG (commit
[a275cf3](https://github.com/HL7/fhir-clinical-document/commit/a275cf3ba110e84d701532e504ede1f9281a641c))
and its persuasive resolution already states the SDWG position: a
`DiagnosticReport` may be carried inside a FHIR Clinical Document by adding a
`Composition` whose first section entry is the `DiagnosticReport`, and SDWG
agreed to "raise a FHIR R6 issue to gain clarity" about the overlap with
`DiagnosticReport.composition`. FHIR-54495 is, in effect, that R6 issue: it
asks for the equivalent guidance to be lifted into the FHIR Core Documents
page so non-clinical-document readers get the same picture. John Moehrke's
comment suggests deriving the new text/diagram from the analogous discussion
that already exists in US Core.

Filed by Giorgio Cangioli against the FHIR Clinical Documents IG and resolved
**Persuasive** in the 2025 ballot cycle. The agreed disposition added
guidance that where a diagnostic report needs to be treated as a FHIR
Clinical Document, a `Composition` is added whose first section entry is the

here." That R6 follow-up is the substance of FHIR-54495, so the resolution
text and the applied commit
([a275cf3](https://github.com/HL7/fhir-clinical-document/commit/a275cf3ba110e84d701532e504ede1f9281a641c))

## Related Zulip Discussions

### [Orders and Observation WG > DiagnosticReport vs. Composition](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/Orders%20and%20Observation%20WG/topic/DiagnosticReport%20vs.%20Composition)

Long-running OO thread (Nov 2022 – Sep 2023) that worked through how
`DiagnosticReport` and `Composition` should coexist for laboratory reports.
Rob Hausam drove a series of slide decks summarising x-eHealth, German HiMed
and Saudi XD-LAB approaches; the conclusion landed on `DiagnosticReport` 1..1
with `Composition` 1..1 and bidirectional references, accepting the unusual
circular reference because it lets the same Bundle satisfy both the OO
"`Composition` provides structure to the `DiagnosticReport`" pattern (the
`DiagnosticReport.composition` element added in R5) and a valid FHIR document
Bundle. Giorgio Cangioli briefly proposed dropping the
`Composition`→`DiagnosticReport` reference but Rob noted the IG-creation
discussion concluded the reference is needed for the Bundle to validate as a
FHIR document. Jay Lyle pushed back with "what does carrying both buy us
that `DocumentReference` doesn't?", which is a useful counterpoint to capture
in the Documents-page guidance. Andrea Pitkus and Elliot Silver layered on
the panel/grouping concerns (whether the panel `Observation` or the
`DiagnosticReport` should hold the structure).

Participants: Rob Hausam, David McKillop, Jose Costa Teixeira, Giorgio
Cangioli, Jay Lyle, Andrea Pitkus, Elliot Silver, Grahame Grieve.

### [europe > EU Lab DiagnosticReport - mandatory composition](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/europe/topic/EU%20Lab%20DiagnosticReport%20-%20mandatory%20composition)

Recent (Feb 2026) thread driven by Kevin Mayfield's SUSHI error that
`DiagnosticReport.extension:DiagnosticReportCompositionR5` is required by the
HL7 Europe Laboratory Report 0.1.1 profile even though his implementation
has no FHIR documents. Giorgio Cangioli explains the EU compromise: the EU
Laboratory Report is always one `DiagnosticReport` exchanged as a FHIR
Document, while the in-progress UV Lab Report IG keeps the FHIR-document
representation as one of three options. Ralf Herzog contrasts the two
philosophies — US-style "one `DiagnosticReport` per `ServiceRequest`,
reassemble via Bundle/Composition or by requisition identifier" versus the
EU "one holistic `DiagnosticReport` per report" — which is exactly the
selection guidance FHIR-54495 is asking for. John Moehrke takes the strict
line that an implementation should not call itself compliant with a profile
whose required elements it does not populate.

Participants: Kevin Mayfield, John Moehrke, Giorgio Cangioli, Ralf Herzog.

## Proposed Dispositions

Because there are no GitHub cross-references for FHIR-54495, the
dispositions below name the *likely* authoring location (FHIR Core's
`documents.html` page in `HL7/fhir`, with optional alignment edits in
`HL7/fhir-clinical-document`) but do not cite specific paths from a
`repo-analysis` briefing. If the work group accepts a disposition that
requires a code/spec change, run `repo-analysis HL7/fhir` (and, if needed,
`repo-analysis HL7/fhir-clinical-document`) before drafting the PR so the
exact authoring root and "do not edit" generated areas are confirmed.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a new sub-section to the FHIR Core Documents page (the same page that
already contrasts `Composition`-based FHIR documents with `DocumentReference`)
titled approximately *"Relationship to DiagnosticReport"*. The new text
should:

1. State that `DiagnosticReport`, `Composition` and `DocumentReference` are
   the three FHIR resources commonly used to convey clinical-document-shaped
   content, and explain the intent of each (`Composition` = the header of an
   attested, sectioned FHIR document Bundle; `DiagnosticReport` = a
   findings-oriented report that may itself be sectioned via
   `DiagnosticReport.composition`; `DocumentReference` = a pointer to a
   document — FHIR, CDA, PDF, … — that lives elsewhere).
2. Lift the resolution text from
   [FHIR-46693](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-46693): when a
   `DiagnosticReport` needs to be treated as a FHIR Clinical Document, add a
   `Composition` whose first section entry is the `DiagnosticReport`; the
   `Composition` sits at the head of the document Bundle and the
   `DiagnosticReport` becomes its first entry.
3. Acknowledge the overlap between `DiagnosticReport.composition` (added in
   R5 to give a `DiagnosticReport` document-like structure) and a
   `Composition` resource at the head of a document Bundle, and give a
   selection rule along the lines: use `Composition` at the Bundle head when
   the artefact is intended to be exchanged/persisted as an attested,
   sectioned, signable FHIR document; use `DiagnosticReport` (optionally with
   `DiagnosticReport.composition`) when the artefact is a findings report
   primarily exchanged via REST/messaging; use both together (the OO R5
   pattern) when the report must simultaneously be a valid FHIR document and
   a findings report.
4. Address `DocumentReference` once more as the pointer-to-an-external-blob
   case, closing the implicit gap that FHIR-54513 raises.
5. Adopt John Moehrke's suggestion to derive the explanatory diagram from
   the equivalent discussion in US Core, adapted to FHIR Core terms.

Resolve [FHIR-54513](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54513) at the same
time and reference both ticket keys in the changelog/commit.

#### Justification

This is the option that literally answers the reporter's request and finally
closes the R6 follow-up that SDWG already committed to in the resolution of
FHIR-46693. The Documents page is the canonical place an implementer goes to
choose between these resources, and the current omission of
`DiagnosticReport` is a real gap that has produced repeated confusion across
US (OO) and European (EU Lab Report) communities — both of which are
reflected in the Zulip threads above. The resolution text and applied commit
from FHIR-46693 give SDWG ready-made wording to start from, so the editorial
cost is bounded.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Resolve FHIR-54495 with a small "see also" addition on the Documents page
that names `DiagnosticReport` and the `DiagnosticReport.composition` element
and links out to:

- the *Limitations on certain CDA document types* section of the
  HL7 Clinical Documents IG (already updated by FHIR-46693), and
- the OO `DiagnosticReport` page (and its R5 `composition` element
  description).

Do **not** restate the full selection guidance in FHIR Core; instead, raise a
joint SDWG/OO project to publish a single shared "When to use Composition vs
DiagnosticReport vs DocumentReference" page (in the FHIR Clinical Documents
IG, which is jointly maintained), and link both FHIR Core's Documents page
and the OO `DiagnosticReport` page to it. Bundle the resolution of
FHIR-54513 into the same "see also" pointer.

#### Justification

The substantive guidance lives at the intersection of two work groups (SDWG
owns `Composition`, OO owns `DiagnosticReport`), and the Zulip threads show
real disagreement about details (bidirectional references, panel grouping,
EU vs US "one DR per ServiceRequest" philosophy). Putting the canonical text
in FHIR Core risks SDWG asserting wording that OO has not signed off on and
then drifting from the IG-level guidance produced by FHIR-46693. A
lightweight pointer in FHIR Core plus a jointly-owned page is more durable,
keeps the Documents page short, and avoids two sources of truth. The
trade-off vs A is that implementers don't get an immediate answer in the
Core spec — they have to follow a link — and the joint-project approach
slows the resolution.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark FHIR-54495 (and FHIR-54513) **Not Persuasive** on the grounds that the
guidance the reporter is asking for already exists in the FHIR Clinical
Documents IG (per the resolution of FHIR-46693) and that the FHIR Core
Documents page is intentionally scoped to the `Composition` /
`DocumentReference` distinction; cross-resource selection guidance belongs
in the IG, not the base spec. Optionally add a one-line "see the FHIR
Clinical Documents IG for guidance on `DiagnosticReport`" pointer, but
otherwise leave the Documents page unchanged.

#### Justification

The reporter's request can be characterised as "duplicate the IG guidance in
the base spec", and the resolution of FHIR-46693 was explicit that the IG is
the appropriate home for the *how-do-I-use-`DiagnosticReport`-as-a-document*
text. Keeping that guidance in one place avoids drift between the base spec
and the IG and respects the historical division of labour between SDWG (IG)
and core. The downside is real: the IG is not where most R5/R6 implementers
land first, so the gap that FHIR-54495 names will keep hurting newcomers,
and the open R6 follow-up SDWG promised in FHIR-46693 will remain unmet.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter, the commenters (gdolin, john_moehrke), and the resolution text
of FHIR-46693 all point in the same direction: SDWG already owes R6 a piece
of explanatory text about the `Composition`/`DiagnosticReport` overlap, and
the FHIR Core Documents page is the natural home for it. Disposition A
discharges that commitment, closes the sibling ticket FHIR-54513 in the same
edit, and gives implementers a single authoritative paragraph in the place
they actually look. Disposition B is more diplomatic but defers the work and
leaves the gap open across at least one more ballot cycle; Disposition C
ignores SDWG's own R6 commitment from FHIR-46693 and is hard to defend given
the volume of confusion already visible on chat.fhir.org. Editorially this
is a low-risk change because the wording can be lifted from the
already-balloted FHIR-46693 resolution and the US Core diagram John Moehrke
suggested. Before the PR is opened, run `repo-analysis HL7/fhir` to
confirm the authoring path for the Documents page and to surface any
"do-not-edit" generated areas around it.
