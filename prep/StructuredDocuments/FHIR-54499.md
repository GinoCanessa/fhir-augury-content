## Summary

The reporter flags an editorial defect in the Composition resource's notes
page (the "Compositions about multiple entities" subsection) where the word
"subject" is used three times in one sentence with two different meanings,
rendering the sentence unclear. The current text reads:

> "Typically, a composition is made about the subject — e.g. a patient, or
> group of patients, location, or device — and the distinction between the
> subject and the composition describes the subject."

The first and third uses of "subject" refer to the entity the composition is
about (e.g., the patient). The clause "the distinction between the subject
and the composition" is semantically opaque — it is not clear what
"distinction" is being referenced, nor what the second "subject" refers to.
The reporter asks the workgroup to clarify the wording, particularly the
final occurrence of "subject". This appears to be a documentation-only
correction confined to a single FHIR Core narrative file
(`source/composition/composition-notes.xml`) — there are no linked tickets,
no Zulip discussion, and no cross-referenced GitHub items in Augury for
FHIR-54499.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Edit the opening paragraph of the
`source/composition/composition-notes.xml` "Compositions about multiple
entities" section in `HL7/fhir` to remove the ambiguous "the distinction
between the subject and the composition describes the subject" clause and
replace it with a direct, single-meaning sentence. Suggested replacement
text (preserving the existing `<b>subject</b>` emphasis used elsewhere on
the page):

> "Typically, a composition is made about a single **subject** — e.g. a
> patient, a group of patients, a location, or a device — and the body of
> the composition describes that **subject**."

The next sentence ("Some kinds of documents contain data about other
parties or entities that are relevant to the subject of the document.")
already introduces the multi-entity case, so removing the confusing clause
does not break the flow. No other files require changes; the
`Composition.section.focus` and `composition-section-subject` links later
in the paragraph remain untouched.

#### Justification

The reporter's complaint is purely editorial — the sentence is genuinely
unparseable as written, and the intent ("the composition describes its
subject") can be expressed without the redundant "distinction between the
subject and the composition" clause. The Composition resource itself has a
clear definition of `Composition.subject` ("Who and/or what the composition
is about"), so the narrative should mirror that definition rather than
introduce a novel and undefined notion of a "distinction". The fix is
confined to a single XHTML paragraph in one file under `source/composition/`,
follows the FhirCore "Update narrative / prose" recipe exactly, and carries
no risk of breaking schemas, examples, or downstream IGs.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than make a minimal in-place rewrite, restructure the opening of the
"Compositions about multiple entities" section so that the relationship
between `Composition.subject`, `Composition.section.focus`, and the
`composition-section-subject` extension is introduced explicitly before the
multi-entity examples. A worked alternative for
`source/composition/composition-notes.xml`:

> "Most compositions are about a single **subject**, identified by
> `Composition.subject` — typically a patient, but it may also be a group of
> patients, a location, or a device. The narrative and entries of the
> composition describe that subject. Some compositions, however, contain
> sections whose data is primarily about a different entity that is
> relevant to the overall subject (for example, the mother in a neonatal
> discharge summary). In those cases, the differing entity is identified by
> `Composition.section.focus`; in the absence of a `focus`, the section is
> assumed to be about `Composition.subject`."

The remainder of the section (bulleted examples, the `focus` SHALL clause,
and the `composition-section-subject` extension paragraph) would be
retained essentially as-is, but moved to follow this clearer lead-in.

#### Justification

The reporter has identified the visible symptom (one ambiguous sentence),
but the underlying cause is that the section conflates three distinct
concepts (`Composition.subject`, `Composition.section.focus`, and the
section-level `subject` extension) without ever introducing them as a set.
A single-sentence patch (Disposition A) closes the immediate complaint but
leaves the surrounding paragraph as a likely source of future tickets in
the same area. Disposition B is still scoped to the same one file and the
same FhirCore "Update narrative / prose" recipe, but produces a passage
that is more durable. Trade-off: it is a larger editorial change requiring
slightly more workgroup eyes, and reviewers may reasonably prefer to keep
the change surface minimal during a ballot cycle.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline to make a change, on the basis that the existing paragraph,
although awkwardly worded, does not produce an interoperability defect: the
normative definition of `Composition.subject` lives in the Composition
StructureDefinition (and in the rendered table on `composition.html`), and
the surrounding bullets and the `Composition.section.focus` paragraph
already make the multi-entity story clear. The "Highest" priority is
disproportionate to a single confusing sentence in a notes section.

#### Justification

The clause is editorial only — there is no behaviour, cardinality, binding,
or invariant that depends on it, and no Zulip discussion, linked ticket, or
implementer report has surfaced an actual misimplementation traced back to
this wording. Workgroup time during the ballot window is finite, and the
defect can be addressed by a maintenance edit at any later cycle without
breaking conformance. This is the weakest of the three options because the
fix itself is genuinely cheap, but it is defensible if the workgroup is
prioritising substantive change requests.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter has flagged a real and easily reproducible readability defect
in a paragraph that introduces one of the more confusing parts of the
Composition resource (the interplay between `subject`, `section.focus`,
and the `composition-section-subject` extension). The fix is one paragraph
in one file under `source/composition/`, sits squarely on the FhirCore
"Update narrative / prose" recipe, has no cross-repo impact, and carries
zero conformance risk. Disposition B is attractive for long-term clarity
but inflates the change surface for what the reporter posed as a targeted
clarification — better lodged as a follow-up if the workgroup feels the
broader paragraph is also weak. Disposition C is hard to defend given how
small and self-contained the correction is, especially with the ticket
filed at "Highest" priority. Accepting as requested, with the suggested
single-sentence rewrite above, is the most proportionate response.
