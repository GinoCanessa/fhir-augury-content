## Summary

The submitter (Eric Haas) proposes that the US C-CDA on FHIR implementation
guide reuse the US Core profiles for the Plan of Treatment Section
(`us-core-careplan`) and the Device / Medical Equipment Section
(`us-core-device`) rather than defining or pointing to alternative
profiles. The submitter notes the proposal assumes that "medical
equipment" in the C-CDA context is intended to mean implantable devices,
which aligns with the scope US Core's Device profile was originally
written for (UDI-bearing implantable devices). The ticket is a
consistency / harmonization request: where US Core already defines a
profile for the same clinical concept, C-CDA on FHIR should defer to it
instead of binding to the unprofiled base resource or introducing a
parallel profile.

The ticket has already been triaged by Structured Documents and given a
resolution of **Persuasive with Modification**: the workgroup agreed to
bind the Plan of Treatment Section to the US Core CarePlan profile, but
declined to bind the Medical Equipment Section to `us-core-device`
because that profile is restricted to UDI devices, and the Medical
Equipment Section is broader than UDI-tagged implantables. The Medical
Equipment Section will instead point to the unprofiled `Device`
resource. No directly-linked Jira tickets were surfaced via
cross-references; the only cross-reference is an automated FHIR Bot
"tracker-item" Zulip post announcing the ticket on the `#committers`
stream, with no follow-on discussion.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Bind both the Plan of Treatment Section and the Medical Equipment
Section in the US C-CDA on FHIR IG to the corresponding US Core
profiles: bind Plan of Treatment to `us-core-careplan`, and bind the
Medical Equipment Section's Device entries to `us-core-device`. Update
the section-level profile constraints in the C-CDA on FHIR IG so that
conformant Compositions reference resources conforming to those US
Core profiles, and remove any parallel/local profiles or unprofiled
references that this would supersede.

#### Justification

This is the most direct path to the harmonization the submitter is
asking for and is consistent with HL7 US-realm guidance to reuse US
Core profiles wherever the same clinical concept is already covered.
It reduces the number of overlapping US-realm profiles implementers
must reconcile, and it inherits US Core's Must-Support and terminology
binding decisions for free.

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Bind the Plan of Treatment Section to `us-core-careplan` (per the
submitter), but point the Medical Equipment Section to the **base
`Device` resource** rather than `us-core-device`. The C-CDA Medical
Equipment Section is intended to cover medical equipment in general
(including non-implantable, non-UDI items such as wheelchairs,
nebulizers, glucose meters, prosthetics, etc.), whereas the US Core
Device profile is scoped to UDI-bearing implantable devices and would
exclude valid Medical Equipment Section content. Document this scope
mismatch in the section's IG narrative so implementers understand why
the section does not reuse `us-core-device`.

#### Justification

This addresses the underlying need — leverage US Core where it fits —
while honoring the broader semantics of the C-CDA Medical Equipment
Section. Forcing `us-core-device` onto the section would either
exclude legitimate non-UDI equipment from being represented or push
implementers to violate the profile's UDI requirements, both of which
are worse outcomes than a deliberate, explained use of the base
resource. This matches the workgroup's already-recorded reasoning on
the ticket (Resolution Description) and is therefore the lowest-risk
landing point.

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline to change the existing section bindings on the grounds that
the C-CDA on FHIR IG's Plan of Treatment and Medical Equipment
sections already cover their clinical concepts via the base FHIR
resources, and that introducing a hard dependency on US Core profiles
expands the IG's normative dependency surface without a demonstrated
implementer pain point in this ticket.

#### Justification

A decline is defensible only if the workgroup judges that adding a
US Core profile dependency to C-CDA on FHIR is itself a non-trivial
cost (versioning, conformance testing, cross-IG coordination) that is
not justified by a single submitter's harmonization request with no
supporting Zulip discussion or implementer reports. The ticket
contains no use-case evidence that current implementers are blocked
or producing inconsistent CarePlans / Devices because of the lack of
US Core profile binding.

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B matches both the submitter's intent (use US Core where
it fits) and the technical reality that `us-core-device` is scoped to
UDI-bearing implantables, which is narrower than what the C-CDA
Medical Equipment Section is meant to carry. Disposition A would
either over-constrain the Medical Equipment Section or invite
non-conformant use of `us-core-device`. Disposition C ignores a
legitimate harmonization win on the Plan of Treatment side. Notably,
this disposition is exactly what the workgroup has already recorded
on the ticket as **Persuasive with Modification**, so adopting B keeps
the IG aligned with the resolution that has already been agreed.
