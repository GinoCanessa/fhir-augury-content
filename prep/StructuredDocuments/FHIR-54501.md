## Summary

The ticket challenges a comment that currently lives on `Composition.title`
(quoted in the description) which says the title "is the same as the text or
a display name of `Composition.type` (e.g., a 'consultation' or 'progress
note')" and that — unlike CDA — title is "mandatory here during the trial
use period." The reporter asks what the comment is actually trying to say:
is it mandating that `title` be present, or mandating that `title` be
identical to a display rendering of `Composition.type`? They argue the
latter is not (and should not be) required by the spec — title and the
type's display ought to be similar but authors must be free to deviate.
They also note the appeal to "trial use period" is unclear in this
context.

The same wording is the subject of two sibling tickets that should be
disposed of together:

- [FHIR-54510](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54510) (same reporter,
  same day, Highest priority) reads the comment as ambiguous about
  whether `title` and the `type` display must be identical, and asks
  that any such alignment requirement be expressed as a real constraint
  rather than prose.
- [FHIR-54115](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54115) (Ryan Zoellner,
  Technical Correction) observes that `Composition.title` is now `0..1`
  (it was `1..1` in R4), so the comment's claim that title is
  "mandatory here" is factually stale and must be cleaned up.

Together these three tickets converge on the same outcome: the
`Composition.title` comment needs to be rewritten so it (a) no longer
asserts that title is mandatory, and (b) does not appear to require
`title` to equal a display of `Composition.type`.

## Details

**Description:**

> For many compositions, the title is the same as the text or a display
> name of `Composition.type` (e.g., a "consultation" or "progress note").
> Note that [CDA](http://www.hl7.org/implement/standards/product_brief.cfm?product_id=7)
> does not make title mandatory, but there are no known cases where it
> is useful for title to be omitted, so it is mandatory here during the
> trial use period.
>
> Is this about mandating title, mandating that they are the same? This
> is not required (nor should it be) in the current spec. They should be
> similar but the users should be able to deviate.
>
> ps. Trial use is unclear in this context.

## Proposed Dispositions

All three dispositions below treat FHIR-54501, FHIR-54510, and
FHIR-54115 as a single editorial change to the comment on
`Composition.title` in the FHIR Core specification. Any disposition
that edits the comment should also clear the matching wording in
FHIR-54510 and close FHIR-54115 as duplicate / resolved together.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Rewrite the `Composition.title` element comment in FHIR Core so that
it no longer:

1. Asserts that `title` is mandatory (it is `0..1` in the current
   build), and
2. Implies that `title` must be the same as a display of
   `Composition.type`.

A concrete replacement comment, suitable for the `Composition.title`
definition:

> Officially `title` is optional (`0..1`); however, it is strongly
> recommended for human readability of the composition. Authors
> commonly base the title on the display of `Composition.type` (e.g.,
> "Consultation Note", "Progress Note"), but they are not required to
> match — the title may add patient, encounter, or date context, or
> may be authored independently for clinical clarity.

Drop the "mandatory here during the trial use period" sentence and the
"feedback on this requirement is welcome" sentence entirely, since
both are stale relative to the current `0..1` cardinality. As an
editorial pass, also resolve the duplicates (FHIR-54510, FHIR-54115)
by pointing at this change.

#### Justification

This matches what the spec actually says today (`Composition.title` is
`0..1`) and removes the contradiction the three tickets independently
flagged. It explicitly reframes the "title ≈ type display" guidance as
non-normative advice rather than a hidden requirement, which is what
the reporter asked for ("they should be similar but the users should
be able to deviate"). It is a comment-only edit, so it does not change
conformance, validation, or instances in the field.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Take a slightly heavier editorial pass that keeps the spec's intent
about title quality, but moves it out of the comment and into more
appropriate machinery:

1. Comment on `Composition.title`: replace with one short sentence —
   "If supplied, `title` should be a human-readable label for the
   composition; it is often, but not required to be, derived from the
   display of `Composition.type`." Remove all references to
   "mandatory" and "trial use period."
2. Add a non-normative `usage note` (or a Best Practice obligation, if
   the WG prefers) on `Composition` recommending that systems supply a
   `title` whenever feasible and that the title typically incorporates
   the type display plus subject/encounter context.
3. Do **not** introduce an invariant that requires `title` to equal a
   display of `Composition.type`. The reporter explicitly argued such
   alignment must not be required, and FHIR-54510 asked that any real
   requirement be expressed as a constraint — by deciding not to add
   one, we make the answer to that question explicit.

#### Justification

Versus Disposition A, this preserves the original editorial intent
("title is almost always useful, and it usually mirrors the type
display") while separating *advice* (usage note) from *normative
definition* (the element comment). That is closer to how recent Core
edits have handled similar "almost always present" elements, and it
gives implementers a single, clearly non-normative place to look for
authoring guidance. The trade-off vs A is more spec churn (two edits,
not one) and a small risk of the new usage note drifting from the
comment over time.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (Persuasive — wording is editorial only)

#### Proposal

Decline the change as requested *as a normative change* and instead
reclassify it as a non-issue: the element comment is informative, the
normative cardinality (`0..1`) already governs whether `title` is
required, and no implementer can be conformant-or-not on the basis of
a comment. Close FHIR-54501 and FHIR-54510 as Persuasive — Not
Persuasive (no normative change), and roll any pure editorial cleanup
(removing the stale "mandatory here" sentence) into FHIR-54115 as a
Technical Correction.

#### Justification

Defensible on the grounds that nothing in the comment can constrain a
conformant instance: validators key off the cardinality and
invariants, not the prose. Treating FHIR-54501/54510 as Not Persuasive
keeps the workgroup's normative discussion focused on the cardinality
change already captured by FHIR-54115. The downside, and why this is
the weakest option, is that the comment is demonstrably misleading —
both reporters read it as imposing a requirement, which means leaving
it in place will keep generating duplicate tickets.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The three tickets agree on the underlying defect: the
`Composition.title` element comment was written when title was `1..1`
and now contradicts both the current cardinality and the WG's actual
intent (no requirement that title equal a `Composition.type` display).
A focused rewrite of the comment — Disposition A — fixes the defect
with the least amount of spec churn, directly answers the reporter's
question ("is this mandating title, or mandating they're the same?")
by removing both implications, and lets FHIR-54115 be closed as
resolved by the same edit. Disposition B is reasonable and the WG
should consider promoting the authoring advice into a usage note if
that is the prevailing editorial style, but the minimum viable fix
lives entirely in the element comment. Disposition C should be
avoided: even if the comment is technically informative, leaving
contradictory prose in a Highest-priority element will keep producing
duplicate tickets and erode trust in the spec text.
