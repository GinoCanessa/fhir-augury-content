## Summary

The submitter quotes the Composition guidance which says that, when a section's
resources are about something other than the document subject, either the
section MUST carry a `Composition.section.focus` (and the referenced resources
must agree on that focus, or omit `subject` entirely) or — for documents that
genuinely cover multiple subjects — `Composition.subject` is omitted and the
[`composition-section-subject`](https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/fhir-extensions/StructureDefinition-composition-section-subject.html)
extension is used per section. The submitter argues that an IPS Family History
section is a real-world case that does not fit either pattern cleanly: a single
section legitimately contains FamilyMemberHistory / Observation / Condition
resources whose `subject` is a different family member rather than the IPS
patient, and authoring one sub-section per family member is "overkill". The
ticket asks the work group to clarify the recommended pattern — for example,
whether such per-relative information should always be wrapped in
FamilyMemberHistory / Observation resources whose `subject` is still the IPS
patient (so that the section's subject rule is technically satisfied), or
whether the rule itself needs a carve-out for sections like family history that
inherently aggregate information about related persons.

## Details

**Description:**

> In all these cases, the subject of the document is a single patient, but
> some of the details are actually related to other persons or entities. When
> this happens, these other entities are detailed in the
> `Composition.section.focus` element. In the absence of a focus, it is assumed
> that the subject of the composition is the focus.
>
> If a focus is specified, then the resources referred to from the section SHALL
> either:
> - specify that their subject (however named e.g. patient) or focus element
>   (if present) is the focus indicated
> - not have a subject element
>
> A few compositions genuinely cover multiple subjects - different sections are
> about different subjects. In such case, `Composition.subject` is omitted, and
> the extension
> [section-subject](https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/fhir-extensions/StructureDefinition-composition-section-subject.html)
> is used on each section to indicate the subject.

In the case of a family history section, it might refer to resources that have
as a subject one of the family members. This section requires a sub-section for
each family member which is overkill. What is the recommendation for such
information? Wrap the information in Observations?

Please clarify.

## Proposed Dispositions

> **Caveat on repo grounding.** Because Augury surfaced no GitHub cross-refs
> for this ticket, none of the dispositions below are anchored to a specific
> `HL7/fhir` (or IPS IG) authoring path via a persisted briefing. The work
> group should confirm the actual edit targets — most likely the Composition
> resource narrative pages and the IPS Family History section profile — before
> taking action.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add explicit clarifying text to the Composition resource page (the narrative
that defines the `Composition.section.focus` rule and the
`composition-section-subject` extension pattern) stating that, for a section
whose entries inherently describe other persons related to the document
subject (canonical example: a Family History section in an IPS), the section
SHOULD NOT be split into per-relative sub-sections and SHOULD NOT use the
`composition-section-subject` extension to switch subjects per relative.
Instead, the section's resources SHOULD be ones whose modeled `subject` is the
document patient and which carry the relative as a structural property — i.e.
`FamilyMemberHistory.patient` is the document patient and the relative is
identified by `FamilyMemberHistory.relationship` / `name`, or, when using
Observation, `Observation.subject` is the document patient and the relative is
named in `Observation.focus` (typed as `RelatedPerson`/`Patient` for that
relative). Add a worked Family History section example to the Composition
narrative that demonstrates this pattern, and add a forward link from the
`composition-section-subject` extension page back to this guidance so authors
do not reach for the extension when the cleaner pattern applies.

#### Justification

This matches the existing FHIR design intent: `FamilyMemberHistory` is the
purpose-built resource for "facts about a relative, recorded as part of the
patient's record", and its `patient` element is explicitly the patient whose
history this is — not the relative. Treating the section as still being about
the IPS patient therefore satisfies the `Composition.section.focus` SHALL
without contortion, and avoids the section-per-relative explosion the
submitter calls out. It also keeps the `composition-section-subject` extension
reserved for the case it was actually designed for — documents whose sections
genuinely have different *primary* subjects (e.g. a family-care plan covering
multiple patients), not single-patient documents that happen to mention
relatives.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than only clarifying narrative, relax the `Composition.section.focus`
SHALL itself with an explicit carve-out: when a section's entries are
resources of types that describe a relative or related party as a structural
property (e.g. `FamilyMemberHistory`, `RelatedPerson`, observations whose
`focus` is a related person), the entry resources are permitted to have a
`subject` that differs from the section focus / document subject without
requiring an explicit `section.focus` or per-section `section-subject`
extension. Encode this as a normative exception in the Composition page and
update the IPS Family History section profile to demonstrate it.

#### Justification

This addresses the underlying validation problem (not just the documentation
problem) for implementations that mechanically enforce the current SHALL
against IPS Family History bundles. It is also more defensible for resources
like Observation where `Observation.subject` of a relative may be exactly what
the system has captured — forcing it to be rewritten to the document patient
purely to satisfy a Composition rule loses information. The trade-off is that
this is a real spec change with backwards-compatibility risk: validators that
have implemented the strict SHALL would need to be updated, and the carve-out
must be defined precisely enough that it does not accidentally re-open the
multi-subject ambiguity that motivated the original rule.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline as "no spec change required — already addressed by existing
mechanisms." Respond on the ticket that the existing pattern already supports
this case: a Family History section whose entries are `FamilyMemberHistory`
resources is, by `FamilyMemberHistory.patient`, still a section about the
document subject, so neither `Composition.section.focus` nor the
`composition-section-subject` extension is required, and no per-relative
sub-section is required. Where authors wish to additionally point at a
specific relative as the focus of a particular entry, they may use
`Observation.focus` on the entry without changing the section's subject.

#### Justification

The submitter's stated dilemma assumes the resources in the section have
relatives as their `subject`, but the FHIR resources purpose-built for this
information (`FamilyMemberHistory`, and `Observation` with `focus`) explicitly
do not require that. The current SHALL therefore is not actually being
violated by a well-formed IPS Family History section. Declining avoids
spending a normative slot on a clarification that is already discoverable by
reading `FamilyMemberHistory` and `Composition` together. The risk is that
real implementations are getting this wrong in practice, in which case
declining without at least a clarifying response on the ticket leaves
implementers with the same confusion the submitter raised.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The submitter's question is fundamentally a guidance gap, not a modelling gap.
FHIR already has the right mechanism (`FamilyMemberHistory.patient` plus
`relationship`, and `Observation.subject` + `Observation.focus`) to keep a
Family History section's resources legitimately "about" the IPS patient, so a
spec change of the kind in Disposition B is not needed and would carry
backwards-compatibility risk for validators that already enforce the current
SHALL. At the same time, the fact that an experienced implementer was driven
to ask this question — and was reaching for either sub-sections per relative
or the `composition-section-subject` extension — is direct evidence that
Disposition C ("nothing to do") under-serves implementers. Disposition A
splits the difference: keep the existing rule, but explicitly call out the
Family History case on the Composition page (and from the
`composition-section-subject` extension page) with a worked example, so the
next reader who hits this question does not have to file another ticket.
Structured Documents should coordinate with Patient Care on the wording of the
FamilyMemberHistory side of the example.
