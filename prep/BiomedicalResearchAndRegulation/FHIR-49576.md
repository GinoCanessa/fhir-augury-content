## Summary

The submitter requests that the US Pharmaceutical Quality / Chemistry, Manufacturing and Controls (PQ-CMC) FHIR Implementation Guide add a "Plain Language Summary about this Guide" section to its home page, in line with HL7's voluntary recommendation that IGs include such summaries to improve accessibility for non-technical readers (including patients and the general public). The MCC IG is cited as a reference example, with a note that FHIR leadership recommends omitting the boilerplate "About HL7" portion in new summaries. A community-maintained GitHub collection of draft PLS texts is offered as starter material that authors may adapt. The ticket also frames the request in the broader context of Plain Language Summaries becoming standard practice in scientific publishing (SAGE, Taylor & Francis, PLOS, BioMed Central). No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were surfaced by Augury for this issue.

## Details

**Description:**

HL7 encourages Implementation Guides (IGs) to voluntarily include a "Plain Language Summary about this Guide" on their home page. This addition can enhance accessibility and understanding for non-technical audiences, including patients and the general public.

For an example, see the "Plain Language Summary about HL7 and this Guide" section on the MCC IG homepage: <https://hl7.org/fhir/us/mcc/#plain-language-summary-about-hl7-and-this-guide>. Note that FHIR leaders recommend excluding the "About HL7" portion for new summaries.

To assist with this effort, IG authors can reference existing summaries available in this GitHub repository. You may edit as you see fit to use in your IG: <https://github.com/jmandel/chat.fhir.org-channels/tree/ae9bd11d491e3a62a0f2cd40a2ebff53dcf7afd8/summaries>.

This recommendation aligns with a growing trend among peer-reviewed journals to include Plain Language Summaries (PLS). These summaries simplify complex concepts, improving engagement, transparency, and understanding for diverse audiences. In fields like healthcare, PLS have become essential for fostering public involvement. Some journals, including those published by SAGE, Taylor & Francis, and PLOS, provide templates or require PLS as part of their submission process (BioMed Central, Science Editor, PLOS).

By adopting this approach, HL7 can promote inclusivity, better serve its stakeholders, and align with best practices in other domains.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a new section titled "Plain Language Summary about this Guide" to the PQ-CMC IG home page (the IG's `index` narrative, conventionally `input/pagecontent/index.md` in an HL7 IG-publisher project — exact path to be confirmed against the PQ-CMC IG sources). The section should:

- Describe, in plain English at roughly an 8th-grade reading level, what PQ-CMC is, who uses it, what problem it solves, and what the IG contains (no FHIR jargon, no resource names without a gloss).
- Follow the structural example in the MCC IG section linked in the ticket, but **omit the "About HL7" sub-section** per current FHIR-leadership guidance.
- Be authored by (or reviewed by) someone with regulatory/CMC subject-matter knowledge; the community-maintained drafts at `jmandel/chat.fhir.org-channels/summaries` may be used as a starting template but must be tailored to PQ-CMC's actual scope.
- Land in the next BR&R publication train after STU2 — consistent with the prior committee vote that this be done once CMC content is fully captured across the planned STUs.

#### Justification

The request is narrative-only — no resources, profiles, value sets, or conformance statements are affected — so the technical risk and implementation cost are minimal. It directly furthers an HL7-endorsed accessibility goal and aligns with broader scientific-publishing practice cited in the ticket. The earlier "Considered for Future Use" disposition (10-0-1) already established that the committee considers this a good idea; the 2025-07-08 reopen explicitly schedules it for "post STU2 publication," which matches accepting the request on a deferred timeline rather than declining it. The MCC IG provides a working template, lowering authoring burden further.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Instead of authoring a single bespoke summary directly into `index.md`, contribute a reusable Plain-Language-Summary fragment to the PQ-CMC IG (e.g., as a separate `input/pagecontent/plain-language-summary.md` page that is included from the home page via the IG-publisher `{% include %}` mechanism, and surfaced in the table-of-contents). Pair this with a short authoring checklist captured in the IG's contributor docs so that the PLS is refreshed as a deliberate step in each future STU release. Optionally, coordinate with the FHIR Management Group / HL7 publishing team to upstream a PLS template snippet that other IGs (not just PQ-CMC) can re-use, rather than having every IG re-derive the structure from MCC.

#### Justification

PQ-CMC is on a multi-STU trajectory (per the prior resolution, "after we have included all the content of CMC via the various STUs"). A standalone, included page is easier to revise across releases than prose embedded in `index.md`, and it keeps the home page's other governance/changelog content uncluttered. Promoting a shared template would also address the underlying HL7-wide gap that motivated this ticket (every IG re-doing the same work) and would be more durable than a single-IG fix. Trade-off vs. Disposition A: slightly more up-front coordination, but lower long-term maintenance cost and broader benefit.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as "Not Persuasive" / decline to add a Plain Language Summary to the PQ-CMC IG home page on the grounds that PLS authoring is voluntary HL7 guidance, the audience for an FDA-facing CMC IG is overwhelmingly regulatory and pharmaceutical-development professionals (not patients or the general public), and committee bandwidth is better spent completing the substantive STU content.

#### Justification

The HL7 recommendation is explicitly voluntary. PQ-CMC's primary consumers are pharmaceutical sponsors, CROs, and regulators submitting/reviewing drug substance and drug product information — a technical audience for whom a plain-language home-page section adds little. A poorly-written or under-reviewed PLS could even mislead non-specialist readers about what regulatory submissions actually require. If declined, the work group should record this rationale so the question is not re-litigated each ballot cycle.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A (with the deferred-timing already implicit in the 2025-07-08 reopen comment).

The committee has already gone on record (10-0-1) that this is a good suggestion, and the recent reopen explicitly schedules the work for "post STU2 publication" rather than rejecting it — declining now would contradict that prior consensus. The change is purely narrative, has no impact on conformance, profiles, or terminology, and leverages an existing exemplar (MCC) plus community-drafted starter text, so the authoring cost is small. Disposition B is attractive as a longer-term improvement and could be folded into A's implementation as an editorial choice (publish the section via an included page rather than inline) without changing the disposition vote. Disposition C is hard to defend given the existing committee record and the modest scope of the change. Recommend accepting, with the explicit note that the section omits the "About HL7" boilerplate per current FHIR-leadership guidance, and that delivery is targeted for the first PQ-CMC publication after STU2 ships.
