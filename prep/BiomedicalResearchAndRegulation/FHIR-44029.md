## Summary

The ticket asks the Biomedical Research & Regulation work group, in the
context of the Pharmaceutical Quality (Industry) FHIR IG, to reconsider how
the domain "model" diagram on the Domain Overview page partitions concepts
across its domains and subdomains. The reporter (Robert Hausam) singles out
the placement of "Microbiological attributes" as an example, but the request
is general: review whether the current grouping accurately reflects how
industry stakeholders think about Pharmaceutical Quality content. Comment
history makes the intended scope concrete — the work group has previously
discussed moving "reference standards" from **Composition** to
**Identification**, "microbiological attributes" from **Identification** to
**Composition**, and "excipients" from **Identification** to **Composition**.
The ticket was originally dispositioned "Considered for Future Use" (Wright /
Hausam, 15-0-0, 2024-02-21) on the basis that the work group would consult
industry stakeholders, then re-opened by Lynn Laakso post-STU1 publication so
that the reorganization can be progressed in a future cycle. No related Jira,
Zulip, or GitHub items were found via cross-reference or search.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Persistent / Substantive — update the Domain Overview page of the
Pharmaceutical Quality (Industry) IG to revise the domain "model" diagram
along the lines the work group has already discussed in the comment thread:

- Move **"reference standards"** from the **Composition** domain to the
  **Identification** domain.
- Move **"microbiological attributes"** from **Identification** to
  **Composition**.
- Move **"excipients"** from **Identification** to **Composition**.

In practice this means re-cutting the Domain Overview diagram (and the
matching list of subdomains in the page narrative) so that each item is
listed under its new parent domain, and ensuring any in-page anchor / cross
references that point at the moved subdomains continue to resolve. No
underlying resource definitions, profiles, value sets, or examples need to
change — this is a narrative / diagram realignment of how the IG explains
its scope.

#### Justification

The reporter's only concrete example ("Microbiological attributes") and the
work group's own follow-up comment converge on exactly these three moves, and
the previous "Considered for Future Use" disposition (15-0-0) signalled
agreement with the direction in principle, deferring only on timing. Re-opening
the ticket post-STU1 publication explicitly removes that timing constraint.
Aligning the diagram with how industry stakeholders conceptualise
Identification (what something *is*, including its reference standards) vs.
Composition (what something is *made of and characterised by*, including
excipients and microbiological attributes) makes the IG's domain model match
prevailing PQ-CMC terminology, which is the IG's primary audience.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Persistent / Substantive — instead of treating this as a one-shot edit driven
by three pre-identified moves, run a structured review of the full Domain
Overview taxonomy with industry stakeholders before changing the diagram.
Concretely:

1. Publish the current diagram plus the three proposed moves as a short
   discussion document on the BR&R / Pharmaceutical Quality call agenda and
   on the appropriate `#pharmaceutical quality` (or BR&R) Zulip stream.
2. Solicit input on (a) the three named moves and (b) any additional
   subdomain placements that stakeholders consider mis-categorised.
3. Capture the consolidated list of agreed moves as a follow-on disposition
   on this ticket, then update the Domain Overview page (diagram + narrative)
   in a single coherent edit for the next ballot cycle.

#### Justification

The original 15-0-0 vote and the May 2024 re-open both explicitly conditioned
the change on review "with industry stakeholders". Treating this ticket as
the trigger for that review — rather than as a self-contained edit — avoids
a likely follow-up ticket if other subdomain placements turn out to be
contested, and produces one ballot-cycle change instead of two. The cost is
a short delay relative to Disposition A; the benefit is a single durable
re-cut of the diagram informed by the very stakeholders the work group
committed to consulting.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Persistent — close as **Not Persuasive** on the grounds that the Domain
Overview diagram is an introductory orientation aid rather than a normative
artifact, no substantive specification content (resources, profiles, value
sets, conformance) depends on which subdomain a label is shown under, and no
implementer harm has been demonstrated. Leave the current diagram in place
and direct any future re-organisation to a broader IG navigation review
rather than a ticket-driven edit.

#### Justification

The ticket itself ("consider revising … e.g. the location for
'Microbiological attributes'") is exploratory; it does not assert that the
current placement causes confusion in implementation, only that it could be
improved. With no Jira, Zulip, or GitHub cross-references, there is no
external evidence base showing the current diagram is misleading users in
practice. Declining preserves stability for readers who have already
internalised the STU1 diagram and avoids spending ballot cycle effort on a
purely cosmetic change.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The work group has already debated this specific change, voted 15-0-0 in
favour of the direction, and re-opened the ticket precisely so the
reorganization could land after STU1. The three moves are well-bounded,
narrative-only, and carry no risk to conformance because they touch the
Domain Overview page rather than resource definitions or profiles. Disposition
B is defensible if the work group wants the broader stakeholder review on
record before editing, but it largely re-runs a consultation that the prior
vote already signalled consensus on; if the meeting confirms that consensus
still holds, the work group can proceed straight to A in the same session.
Disposition C undersells a change the work group itself has already endorsed
and would leave a known mis-categorisation in the IG's primary orientation
diagram.
