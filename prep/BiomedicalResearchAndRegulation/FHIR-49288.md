## Summary

The reporter has identified three typographical errors in the figure that
appears in the IG Overview, Section 1.3.1 "FHIR Resources and Profiles" of
the US Pharmaceutical Quality/Chemistry, Manufacturing and Controls
(PQ-CMC) (FHIR) implementation guide. Two of the typos are in orange
boxes that represent FHIR resource types ("Substance Defintion" and
"ManufacturedItemDefintion" — both missing the second "i" in
"Definition"), and the third is in two connector labels ("Test Ste"
should be "Test Site"). The request is purely editorial: the figure's
labels should match the actual FHIR resource names (`SubstanceDefinition`,
`ManufacturedItemDefinition`) and use the correct English spelling. No
cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were
surfaced for this ticket, which is consistent with a low-risk
documentation-only fix.

## Details

**Description:**

In the IG Overview, Section 1.3.1 FHIR Resources and Profiles, fix typos
in the the figure:

1. For orange box "Substance Defintion" change to "SubstanceDefinition"
2. For orange box "ManufacturedItemDefintion" change to
   "ManufacturedItemDefinition"
3. For connectors (2) "Test Ste" change to "Test Site"

## Proposed Dispositions

Because the request is editorial and the typos are objectively verifiable
against the published FHIR resource names, all three dispositions below
are framed around the same narrow figure in PQ-CMC §1.3.1.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the figure embedded in PQ-CMC IG Overview §1.3.1 "FHIR Resources
and Profiles" exactly as the reporter requests:

1. Rename the orange box "Substance Defintion" → "SubstanceDefinition"
   (correcting the spelling **and** matching the canonical FHIR resource
   name `SubstanceDefinition` with no internal space).
2. Rename the orange box "ManufacturedItemDefintion" →
   "ManufacturedItemDefinition" (same spelling correction; the resource
   name is `ManufacturedItemDefinition`).
3. Update the two connector labels currently reading "Test Ste" →
   "Test Site".

Concretely, this is a change to the source asset for the §1.3.1 figure
in the PQ-CMC IG (typically a diagram source such as a PlantUML / draw.io
/ SVG file under the IG's `input/images-source/` or `input/images/`
area, plus the rendered PNG/SVG that the page references) and a
republish of the IG. No FHIR conformance artifacts (StructureDefinitions,
ValueSets, profiles) are affected.

#### Justification

The change is purely cosmetic / editorial and removes objectively wrong
labels: "Substance Defintion" and "ManufacturedItemDefintion" do not
match any FHIR R5 resource, while `SubstanceDefinition` and
`ManufacturedItemDefinition` are the actual resource names the figure
intends to depict. Aligning the figure with the canonical resource
names eliminates a source of confusion for implementers comparing the
figure to the Resources page and to elsewhere in the IG. "Test Ste" is
self-evidently a typo of "Test Site". There is no normative impact, no
backward-compatibility concern, and no need for community discussion —
the request can be accepted on its face.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the substance of the request, but expand the scope of the fix to
a single sweep of the §1.3.1 figure rather than three isolated edits.
Specifically, while the figure is being edited:

- Apply the three corrections in Disposition A.
- Verify every other orange-box label against the canonical FHIR R5
  resource names (e.g., `Medication`, `MedicinalProductDefinition`,
  `Ingredient`, `RegulatedAuthorization`, etc.) and correct any further
  spelling, casing, or spacing drift discovered.
- Verify that all connector labels are spelled correctly and use
  consistent terminology with the surrounding narrative on the same
  page.
- If the figure has both an editable source file and a rendered raster,
  re-export the raster so the page actually shows the corrected text.

#### Justification

Editing a diagram is disproportionately expensive relative to typing the
fix — most of the cost is opening the source asset, re-exporting, and
re-publishing the IG. Doing a one-pass audit of the same figure while
it is already open costs almost nothing extra and reduces the chance
that another nearly identical ticket ("you also misspelled X in the
same figure") arrives in the next ballot cycle. The trade-off vs.
Disposition A is that the editor must spend a few extra minutes
checking labels that the reporter did not call out; the upside is a
single, clean figure rather than a drip of one-off corrections.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Reject the change request on the basis that the typos are confined to a
non-normative figure, do not affect any conformance artifact, and that
PQ-CMC editorial corrections of this size should be batched into a
larger errata pass rather than tracked as individual change requests.

#### Justification

This disposition is included for completeness but is hard to defend.
The errors are real, the fix is trivial, and "Substance Defintion" /
"ManufacturedItemDefintion" actively mislead readers because they look
like (but are not) FHIR resource names. There is no existing mechanism
that "already addresses" the mislabel — the figure will continue to be
wrong until it is edited. The only honest reason to decline would be
process-driven (e.g., the IG is frozen for a publication and editorial
fixes are deferred to the next cycle), and even then the right answer
is usually "defer", not "decline".

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition A is the minimum correct answer and would close the ticket
exactly as filed. However, because the marginal cost of opening the
§1.3.1 figure source, exporting it, and republishing the IG dominates
the cost of typing the fixes, the workgroup gets noticeably more value
by treating this ticket as the trigger for a one-pass audit of every
label in the same figure (Disposition B). That audit costs minutes,
keeps the figure consistent with the canonical resource names used
elsewhere in the IG, and reduces the likelihood of a near-duplicate
ticket arriving later. Disposition C should not be selected: the typos
are objectively wrong and visible to every reader of §1.3.1.
