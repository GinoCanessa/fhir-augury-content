## Summary

The reporter requests that the *Retrieval of Real World Data for Clinical
Research (FHIR)* (RWD) Implementation Guide be extended with a
**work-related use case** — i.e., a clinical-research scenario in which
information about a person's occupation, industry, work environment, or
work history is needed to answer the research question. The ticket is
explicitly tied to its companion ticket
[FHIR-40098](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-40098) ("Include ODH in
section 3"), which asks the RWD IG to incorporate the
[Occupational Data for Health (ODH) IG](http://hl7.org/fhir/us/odh/) as
the source of work-information data elements. The two tickets together
form a single proposal: FHIR-40126 supplies the *use case* (the "why")
that justifies the *content change* in FHIR-40098 (pulling ODH into
section 3 of the RWD IG). Both were originally resolved in March 2023
as *Considered for Future Use* on the basis that the current STU does
not include any work-related use case, then re-opened on 2023-05-29 by
Lynn Laakso "post STU publication" so the work group can revisit them
in the next iteration. As of the latest update (2023-12-20), the
reporter (Genny Luensman) has asked that **Amanda Deering** and **Lori
Fourquet** be included in any further communications.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Accept the request and add a **work-related use case** to *Section 3
(Use Cases)* of the RWD IG (`Retrieval of Real World Data for Clinical
Research (FHIR)`). Concretely:

1. Author a new use-case narrative in section 3 describing a clinical
   research scenario where occupational exposure / work history is the
   research variable (e.g., "Study of long-term cardiopulmonary
   outcomes in firefighters" or "Cohort identification by industry/
   occupation for an occupational-asthma registry"). The reporter
   (Genny Luensman) and the requested co-leads (Amanda Deering, Lori
   Fourquet) — all subject-matter experts in occupational health —
   should be tasked with supplying the narrative.
2. Bind that use case to ODH-defined data elements by accepting
   companion ticket [FHIR-40098](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-40098)
   in the same iteration: cite the
   [ODH IG](http://hl7.org/fhir/us/odh/) profiles
   (`odh-EmploymentStatus`, `odh-PastOrPresentJob`,
   `odh-UsualWork`, `odh-RetirementDate`, etc.) as the canonical
   source for the work data the new use case requires, and add the
   corresponding retrieval examples (search parameters, queries) to
   the same section.
3. Mark both FHIR-40126 and FHIR-40098 *Persuasive* / *Persuasive with
   Modifier* and resolve them together so the use case and the data
   binding land in the same publication.

#### Justification

The work group's original *Considered for Future Use* resolution was
explicitly conditional: "if a use case for having work-related studies
is considered and those studies require searching for or retrieving
work data, then that will be included." FHIR-40126 supplies that
missing precondition by offering to author such a use case, which
removes the only justification given for deferring it. Lynn Laakso
already re-opened the ticket "post STU publication" on 2023-05-29,
signalling that BR&R intends to revisit it in the next cycle. Pairing
this with FHIR-40098 follows standard IG practice (use cases drive
data-element selection) and reuses an existing, balloted HL7 IG (ODH)
rather than minting new occupational profiles inside RWD.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the *intent* of the ticket but scope it as a **non-normative
appendix and conformance-optional pattern** rather than a first-class
section-3 use case in the next publication:

1. Add a short **"Occupational / Work-Related Research" appendix** to
   the RWD IG that (a) describes one representative use case
   contributed by the reporter and (b) points implementers at the
   external [ODH IG](http://hl7.org/fhir/us/odh/) for the actual
   profiles and search parameters, without re-profiling those
   resources inside RWD.
2. Defer any normative requirement that RWD servers expose ODH
   profiles to a future ballot cycle, after the BR&R work group and
   the ODH co-leads (Amanda Deering, Lori Fourquet) have jointly
   reviewed which ODH elements are realistically retrievable from
   real-world data sources today.
3. Resolve FHIR-40126 *Persuasive with Modifier* (scope reduced to
   appendix) and resolve FHIR-40098 *Considered for Future Use* with
   a clear pointer to the appendix and to the future-cycle work item.

#### Justification

Lifting ODH into section 3 as a peer of the IG's existing use cases
implies retrieval-side conformance commitments (search parameters,
required elements, examples) that the work group has not yet
specified, and the original resolution noted exactly that risk. An
appendix lets BR&R acknowledge the legitimate research need *now*,
keep the publication on schedule, and avoid binding implementers to
an ODH retrieval surface that has not been validated against actual
RWD sources. It also separates the two tickets cleanly: the use-case
contribution (FHIR-40126) is cheap and low-risk, while the
data-binding work (FHIR-40098) is genuinely larger and benefits from
a scoped follow-up.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Re-affirm the original 2023-03-01 *Considered for Future Use*
resolution and decline to add a work-related use case in this
iteration of the RWD IG. Resolution text: "In this iteration of the
guide, our use cases do not include a work-related use case. Future
iterations will include new use cases, and if a use case for having
work-related studies is considered and those studies require searching
for or retrieving work data, then that will be included." Coordinate
with FHIR-40098 to apply the same disposition.

#### Justification

This was the work group's already-balloted position (vote 7-0-0,
Hosage-Norman / Gregory). The ticket as filed does not actually
contain the proposed use-case narrative — it offers to provide one —
so there is nothing concrete in the issue to incorporate today. Until
the reporter (or the requested co-leads, Amanda Deering and Lori
Fourquet) submit the actual narrative and the corresponding
retrieval requirements, the work group has no material to ballot.
Declining keeps the door open: the future-iteration commitment in the
original resolution remains valid, and the ticket can be re-filed
once the narrative is ready.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The original "no work-related use case exists, therefore no ODH" logic
was always conditional, and Lynn Laakso's 2023-05-29 reversion
explicitly re-opened both tickets *post STU publication* so BR&R could
revisit them in the next cycle — not so they could be declined a
second time. The reporter is an occupational-health subject-matter
expert and has asked to be joined by two named co-leads (Amanda
Deering, Lori Fourquet); that is exactly the contributor profile the
work group asked for in the original resolution. Accepting now, in
lockstep with the companion ticket FHIR-40098, lets the IG cite a
mature, externally-balloted HL7 IG (ODH) for the data layer rather
than inventing new occupational profiles internally, which keeps the
scope of work bounded. Disposition B (appendix-only) is a reasonable
fallback if the work group judges the next publication's calendar too
tight to absorb a full section-3 use case, but it should be chosen on
schedule grounds, not on principle. Disposition C is hard to defend
given that the work group itself already reversed it.
