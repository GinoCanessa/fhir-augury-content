## Summary

The reporter (Mitra Rocca) asks why the CDISC Mappings (FHIR) project team
chose to base its mappings on CDISC SDTMIG v3.2 rather than the newer
SDTMIG v3.3. The ticket was originally imported from a comment spreadsheet
(comment #13) by Lloyd McKenzie. The Biomedical Research & Regulation
work group's existing answer (recorded in the comments) is that the
project scope at initiation was CDASH v2.1 — which is itself based on
SDTMIG v3.2 — so SDTMIG v3.2 was the natural reference. The work group
voted 13-0-2 on 2021-02-23 to mark the request as "Considered for Future
Use." That resolution was briefly reverted on 2021-09-07 to re-open the
question post-STU1 publication, but the ticket has since settled back to
the original "Considered for Future Use" disposition (Resolved
2022-01-18), and is currently in **Triaged** status. There are no
cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items associated
with this ticket in the Augury index.

## Proposed Dispositions

The cross-reference index shows no GitHub items associated with this
ticket, so no repo-analysis briefing is in scope. The dispositions
below therefore frame the change in terms of the **CDISC Mappings
(FHIR)** specification itself rather than concrete repo paths.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Re-scope the CDISC Mappings (FHIR) IG to add an SDTMIG v3.3 mapping
track alongside (or replacing) the current SDTMIG v3.2 mappings. This
would entail: (a) auditing every mapping concept in the published IG
against SDTMIG v3.3 to identify additions, deprecations, and changes in
domain/variable semantics; (b) updating the ConceptMaps,
StructureDefinitions, and narrative pages that reference SDTMIG
identifiers; and (c) republishing the IG (with appropriate
versioning) as a multi-target mapping spec or as a successor STU
covering SDTMIG v3.3.

#### Justification

SDTMIG v3.3 is the more recent CDISC implementation guide and would
align the FHIR mappings with what new regulatory submissions are likely
to use. Accepting the request signals that BR&R intends to keep the
mapping IG current with downstream CDISC versions rather than freezing
it at the version originally chosen for project scoping.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the current STU mappings anchored on SDTMIG v3.2 (consistent with
the CDASH v2.1 scope under which the IG was authored), but open a
follow-on project — tracked as a separate Jira/IG proposal — to
produce SDTMIG v3.3 (and forward-version) mappings as an additive
deliverable. Document in the existing IG's "Scope" / "Background"
narrative the explicit rationale that the v3.2 anchor follows from the
CDASH v2.1 source and point readers to the follow-on project for
v3.3.

#### Justification

This preserves the integrity of the published STU (no breaking
re-scoping of an already-balloted artifact) while still acknowledging
the legitimate need for v3.3 mappings. It matches what the comment
thread effectively converged on — the v3.2 choice was deliberate given
the CDASH v2.1 source, but v3.3 is a reasonable future deliverable.
It also avoids the implementation burden and re-balloting that
Disposition A would impose mid-cycle.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Confirm the existing resolution: **Considered for Future Use**. The
question has been answered on the ticket (CDASH v2.1 → SDTMIG v3.2
chain) and the work group has already voted 13-0-2 to defer SDTMIG v3.3
work to a future project. Close the ticket with the original
disposition restored.

#### Justification

The ticket as filed is a *question*, not a change request with a
concrete deliverable. The substantive answer — that the v3.2 anchor
follows from the CDASH v2.1 scope chosen at project initiation — is
already on the ticket and was accepted by the work group. Upgrading
the IG to SDTMIG v3.3 is a substantial new scope of work that should
be planned as its own project rather than absorbed under this
question-style ticket.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** C

The ticket is, on its face, a question about why SDTMIG v3.2 was
chosen, and that question has already been answered in the comment
thread and ratified by a 13-0-2 BR&R vote. The 2021-09-07 re-open was
procedural (post-STU1 review) rather than the introduction of new
substantive arguments, and the resolution was subsequently reverted to
"Considered for Future Use." Disposition C closes the loop cleanly
without obligating BR&R to a re-scoping project mid-cycle. The
underlying need expressed by the reporter — current-version mappings —
is real and worth pursuing, but it belongs in a separately scoped
follow-on project (essentially Disposition B), which the work group
can authorize independently of this ticket. If the work group wants to
go further than simply closing, Disposition B is the next-best option;
Disposition A should only be entertained if there is an active
commitment of editor and SME bandwidth to re-baseline the IG against
SDTMIG v3.3 in the current cycle.
