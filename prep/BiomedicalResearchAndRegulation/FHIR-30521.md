## Summary

This Change Request, originally submitted by Daniel Vreeman against the
*CDISC Mappings (FHIR)* IG, asks the Biomedical Research & Regulation work
group to publish a **FHIR representation of CDISC ↔ LOINC terminology
mappings**, spanning multiple CDISC domains (lab, vital signs, PROs, etc.).
The reporter notes that elements in the CDISC structure could and should
align with LOINC term-name attributes and other LOINC properties, and that
because Regenstrief already publishes a detailed FHIR representation of
LOINC, those properties can be extracted in computable form to drive the
mapping.

The ticket was imported from a comment-thread spreadsheet (originally
"comment #6" by Lloyd McKenzie). It has had a non-trivial life cycle: the
BR&R call on 2021-03-09 voted 19-0-6 (Baker/Denney) to resolve it as
*Considered for Future Use*, citing the existing CDISC-published
[LOINC ↔ LB Mapping File](https://www.cdisc.org/node/7385). After STU1
publication the ticket was re-opened by Lynn Laakso in 2021-09-07 so the
work group could revisit it; it has since settled back into *Triaged /
Considered for Future Use* without a new disposition vote. There are no
cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, GitHub items, or related
work-group artifacts on file.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Within the *CDISC Mappings (FHIR)* IG, add a normative set of FHIR
`ConceptMap` resources expressing CDISC ↔ LOINC equivalences for the
domains the IG already scopes (starting with **LB / Laboratory**, where
CDISC has already published a curated mapping at
<https://www.cdisc.org/node/7385>, then expanding to **VS / Vital Signs**
and **QS / PRO** domains as source mappings become available). For each
domain, publish:

- A `ConceptMap` whose `sourceUri` is the CDISC code system /
  CDISC-Controlled-Terminology code list URL and `targetUri` is
  `http://loinc.org`, with `equivalence` populated per element.
- Companion narrative pages in the IG that explain coverage, gaps, and
  the import provenance (CDISC LOINC-to-LB file and Regenstrief LOINC
  FHIR distribution).
- Where a CDISC element corresponds to a LOINC *property* (e.g.,
  COMPONENT, METHOD, SCALE) rather than a whole code, also publish a
  `ConceptMap` whose `target.element.dependsOn` references the relevant
  LOINC property URI from the Regenstrief LOINC FHIR representation, so
  the alignment is computable rather than purely lexical.

#### Justification

This is the literal request: a *FHIR* representation of CDISC ↔ LOINC
mappings across domains. The infrastructure exists — `ConceptMap` is the
canonical FHIR mechanism for inter-code-system equivalence, and
Regenstrief already publishes LOINC properties in FHIR form, so the
"depends-on / property" alignment the reporter asks for is achievable
without inventing new data structures. Starting with the LB domain
piggy-backs on CDISC's already-completed mapping work and minimises the
risk of the IG publishing speculative equivalences. The request remains
recognisably "high priority" in the queue precisely because no
machine-readable FHIR artefact exists today; a published ConceptMap fills
that gap and is consistent with how other terminology IGs (e.g., HL7-to-
SNOMED CT, ICD-O ↔ SNOMED) handle multi-domain mapping problems.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than authoring the mappings inside the *CDISC Mappings (FHIR)* IG,
treat the CDISC ↔ LOINC alignment as **upstream content** and have the
IG instead:

1. Define a small set of profiles / extensions on `ConceptMap` (and the
   relevant `CodeSystem` slices) that prescribe *how* CDISC ↔ LOINC
   ConceptMaps must be structured (source / target URIs, required
   `equivalence` codings, mandatory `dependsOn` for LOINC parts, required
   provenance metadata pointing back to the CDISC source document).
2. Reference the CDISC-maintained LOINC ↔ LB mapping (and any future
   per-domain mappings) by canonical URL, expecting CDISC and/or
   Regenstrief to publish the actual `ConceptMap` instances.
3. Publish only *example* / *illustrative* ConceptMaps in the IG itself,
   plus a registry page that lists the canonical mapping URLs the IG
   conforms to.

#### Justification

CDISC controlled terminology is *governed* by CDISC (and LOINC by
Regenstrief). Hard-coding the equivalences inside the HL7 IG creates a
maintenance fork: every CDISC CT release would require an IG re-publish
to stay current, and any disagreement between the IG-published map and
the CDISC-published source would be a real interoperability hazard. By
specifying *structure and conformance* and leaving the *content* to the
upstream stewards, the BR&R work group avoids the fork, retains
authority over how the mapping is *expressed* in FHIR (which is the
HL7-shaped half of the problem), and gives implementers a stable
contract while letting CDISC continue to own the clinical correctness of
the mappings. This also addresses the practical concern that in 2021 the
work group already noted the LOINC ↔ LB mapping exists on cdisc.org —
the IG's value-add is making it FHIR-shaped, not re-deriving it.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Confirm the existing resolution: keep the ticket **Triaged / Considered
for Future Use**, with the explicit rationale that:

- The CDISC-published [LOINC ↔ LB Mapping File](https://www.cdisc.org/node/7385)
  already covers the laboratory domain, and no equivalent
  CDISC-curated source mappings exist for vital signs, PROs, or other
  domains the reporter mentions; publishing a FHIR-shaped mapping over
  unverified content would be premature.
- The Regenstrief LOINC FHIR representation already exposes LOINC
  properties to anyone who needs to do the alignment computationally, so
  no FHIR-side gap blocks implementers today.
- Re-opening the disposition (per Lynn Laakso's 2021-09-07 note) was
  intended to revisit the question post-STU1, but no new information,
  Zulip discussion, GitHub work, or linked tickets have surfaced in the
  intervening cycles to justify standing it up as scoped work for the
  BR&R work group.

Keep the `future` label and revisit only if a sponsor (CDISC, an
implementer, or another HL7 work group) brings concrete domain-by-domain
source mappings and a publication owner.

#### Justification

The ticket has effectively been re-affirmed-by-silence: the 2021-03-09
BR&R vote (19-0-6) resolved it as *Considered for Future Use* on the
strength of the existing CDISC LB mapping, the 2021-09-07 re-open hasn't
attracted new requirements or linked work, and no Zulip / GitHub /
related-Jira activity is on file. Declining now is honest about scope
(the BR&R work group is not staffed to author multi-domain CDISC ↔ LOINC
mappings as a parallel terminology project), avoids publishing
speculative non-LB mappings, and keeps the door open via the `future`
label if upstream content materialises.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B (Alternative Approach)

Disposition C is the path of least resistance and is *defensible*, but it
also leaves the original problem unsolved: implementers consuming CDISC
data still have to hand-roll `ConceptMap` content with no normative
guidance from the IG, and the work group will keep getting variants of
this ticket. Disposition A is appealing but commits BR&R to maintaining
clinical-content mappings that CDISC (and, for LOINC properties,
Regenstrief) are better positioned to own — a fork the work group should
avoid. Disposition B threads the needle: HL7 owns the *FHIR shape* of
the mapping (profiled `ConceptMap`, required `dependsOn` to LOINC
properties, provenance metadata, registry), CDISC keeps owning the
*content*, and the IG actually delivers a tangible, computable artefact
for the LB domain on day one without taking on a multi-year terminology
maintenance burden. It also explicitly responds to the reporter's
emphasis on "computable form" via the LOINC-property `dependsOn`
mechanism, which neither A's bare ConceptMaps nor C's status-quo
disposition guarantees.
