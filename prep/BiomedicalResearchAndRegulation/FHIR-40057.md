### [FHIR-36312: Bug fixes to support overall quality of the sIRB Questionnaires](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-36312)

A collection of quality-control fixes across the sIRB Questionnaires:
correcting stray all-caps text in group headings and questions, typos and
extraneous characters in question text / display items / linkIds / help
text, fixing questions with exactly two answer choices that were wrongly
typed as `open-choice` (which had been exposing an unwanted "Other"
option), enabling the Study Endpoints question (which had been greyed out),
removing drop-down item-control extensions from non-drop-down questions,
and correcting conditional enablement where it was not intended. Resolved
"Persuasive" on 2023-02-17; reopened only to attach the BRR-call change
spreadsheet. FHIR-40057 points at this ticket as one of three to be
clarified.

*Users involved:* Diane Rodden (reporter), JIRAUSER16657 (commenter).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat FHIR-40057 as an umbrella technical-correction ticket and formally
fold its content into FHIR-36310, FHIR-36311, and FHIR-36312 without
changing the published sIRB specification further. Concretely:

- Confirm that the "clarifications and additional details" FHIR-40057 is
  pointing at are the content of the attached spreadsheet
  `23515_changes shown at BRR call Mar15 2022 for 36310 36311 36312.xlsx`
  already linked from each of the three parent tickets.
- Add a short note to FHIR-40057 (and a mirrored comment on each of the
  three parents) explicitly stating that (a) the three parents have
  already been resolved "Persuasive" and Published, (b) the spreadsheet
  is the authoritative record of the changes that were made to the sIRB
  Questionnaires, and (c) FHIR-40057 requires no additional
  specification change.
- Resolve FHIR-40057 as **Persuasive – No Change Required** (or
  equivalent) and close it. No change is made to the sIRB IG or the
  Questionnaire artifacts themselves under this disposition.

#### Justification

The body of FHIR-40057 is purely a pointer to three other tickets; it
does not describe any new FHIR element, extension, constraint, or
behavior. Its three parents are already resolved Persuasive and their
supporting change spreadsheet has been attached. The request it makes —
"clarifications and additional details" — is satisfied by the artifacts
already on the parent tickets. Persuasive-with-no-spec-change is the
standard disposition for tickets whose substance has already been
addressed elsewhere, and doing so preserves the audit trail for
reviewers of the sIRB Questionnaires.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Instead of closing FHIR-40057 as a pass-through to the spreadsheet,
use it as the vehicle to land the spreadsheet content into the
narrative of the sIRB IG so that future implementers don't have to
chase a Jira attachment. Concretely:

- Author a short "Questionnaire change history (BRR, 15-Mar-2022)"
  section in the sIRB IG's narrative that enumerates, in prose / a
  small table, the populate-functionality addition (FHIR-36310), the
  `attachment`-datatype restructuring (FHIR-36311), and the
  quality/bug-fix list (FHIR-36312), each cross-linked to its Jira
  ticket.
- Where the spreadsheet identifies specific `Questionnaire.item`
  linkIds whose `type`, `item-control` extension, `enableWhen`, or
  `text` changed, include those linkIds in the narrative so the change
  record is machine-grep-able.
- Resolve FHIR-40057 as **Persuasive** with the narrative-change
  description above, and retain the three parent tickets as-is.

#### Justification

The three parent tickets capture the intent of the changes but the
concrete delta is only available as an Excel attachment on a Jira
issue — a form that is not rendered in the published IG and is
difficult for downstream implementers (and auditors at sIRB sites) to
consume. Promoting the spreadsheet's content into IG narrative both
preserves the Persuasive disposition on the parents and gives
FHIR-40057 an independent reason to exist beyond "see these three
other tickets." Because no sIRB **artifacts** (Questionnaires,
profiles, value sets) are themselves being changed under this
disposition, this is a pure-narrative / technical-correction-scope
change and does not require re-ballot.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve FHIR-40057 as **Not Persuasive / No Change** on the grounds
that it is effectively a duplicate pointer to three already-resolved
tickets and introduces no independently specifiable correction. Close
without any narrative edit, IG update, or artifact change; direct
future readers to FHIR-36310, FHIR-36311, and FHIR-36312 (and their
attached spreadsheet) for the actual change record.

#### Justification

FHIR-40057 does not state a concrete defect, requirement, or
correction that can stand on its own: its description is solely a
list of three other ticket keys, all three of which are already
Published with resolution Persuasive. Per standard HL7 practice,
umbrella / pointer tickets that contain no independently actionable
content are usually closed as Not Persuasive so the spec record stays
anchored on the tickets that actually drove the change. Declining
avoids the risk of implying, by accepting FHIR-40057, that there is
additional unaddressed work on the sIRB Questionnaires beyond what
the three parent tickets already delivered.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

FHIR-40057 is, on its face, an umbrella reference to three sIRB
Questionnaire tickets that are already resolved Persuasive and
Published. The substantive change record — the 15-Mar-2022 BRR-call
spreadsheet — is already attached to each of those parents, so there
is nothing left to "fix" in the specification, which rules out any
disposition that would require new spec work. Disposition B
(promoting the spreadsheet into IG narrative) is attractive for
implementer ergonomics but is better filed as a fresh narrative
ticket against the sIRB IG rather than retrofitted onto a ticket that
was never scoped that way. Disposition C (Not Persuasive) is
defensible but risks obscuring the audit trail: FHIR-40057 is the
ticket that points reviewers at the spreadsheet, and resolving it
Persuasive-with-no-change under Disposition A both preserves that
pointer and correctly signals that the underlying concerns have been
addressed. If the workgroup wants the change history surfaced in IG
narrative, that should be spun out as a follow-on ticket after
FHIR-40057 is closed under Disposition A.
