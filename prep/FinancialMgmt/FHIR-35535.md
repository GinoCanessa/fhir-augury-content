## Summary

The ticket asks the Da Vinci Patient Cost Transparency (PCT) IG to move
the canonical defining URL of `ValueSet-PCTGFETypeOfBillVS` from the
IG's own `hl7.org/fhir/us/davinci-pct` namespace into
`terminology.hl7.org` (THO), on the rationale that only ValueSets that
back required bindings to a `code` data type belong in
`hl7.org/fhir/...`. The request implies a companion UTG ticket to
register the moved ValueSet in THO. It is one of a coordinated cluster
of PCT terminology tickets (FHIR-35529, FHIR-35532, FHIR-35533,
FHIR-35534, FHIR-35536, FHIR-35537, FHIR-35540, FHIR-35545,
FHIR-35548, FHIR-35549, FHIR-35550 and the related sibling
FHIR-45812/45815/45816/45817/45818) all opened by Joan Harper / Ron
Parker against the same THO-anchoring policy. The ticket has been
voted "Considered for Future Use" twice (2022-11 and 2025-07, both
9-0-x block votes) and re-opened both times by lynn_laakso so the
broader coordinated FM/THO terminology effort can address it; the
2025-10 re-open is the most recent, scoping it to post-STU2 work.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat the ticket as **Persuasive** in the upcoming FM/THO terminology
batch. Concretely:

1. Open a UTG (UP-) ticket to register the value set under THO at
   `http://terminology.hl7.org/ValueSet/PCTGFETypeOfBillVS` (or the
   THO-naming equivalent agreed with the UTG steward).
2. Once the THO ValueSet is published, retire the IG-defined
   `https://hl7.org/fhir/us/davinci-pct/ValueSet/PCTGFETypeOfBillVS`,
   replace its definition in the PCT IG with the THO canonical
   reference, and re-point all bindings (PCT GFE Institutional
   profile's `Claim.subType` Type-of-Bill binding, plus any other
   PCT artifacts that bind to it) at the THO URL.
3. Note in the IG that the binding strength is preserved (currently
   `extensible` — Type of Bill is NUBC-sourced, not a `code` data
   type, so per the ticket's rule it does *not* belong in the
   `hl7.org/fhir` namespace).
4. Coordinate with the sibling cluster (FHIR-35529, …, FHIR-35536,
   FHIR-35537, FHIR-35540, FHIR-35545, FHIR-35548, FHIR-35549,
   FHIR-35550) so all PCT ValueSets/CodeSystems that fall under the
   same rule are migrated together.

#### Justification

This is exactly the "anchor terminology in THO unless it backs a
required `code` binding" policy that THO has been promulgating since
the Sept-2022 WGM (cited verbatim in lynn_laakso's 2023-03-30
comment) and is the policy the FM/THO larger effort cited in the
2025-10 re-open is built around. It also fulfils the original Comment
7 rationale Ron Parker imported. Doing all PCT siblings as one batch
keeps canonical-URL churn for PCT implementers to a single STU
boundary instead of dribbling across multiple publications.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

**Persuasive with Modification — defer the canonical move to the
co-ordinated FM/THO migration, but reaffirm intent now.** Specifically:

1. Keep the current IG-defined `PCTGFETypeOfBillVS` URL in place for
   PCT STU2 (no immediate canonical change, no breakage).
2. Mark the ValueSet in the IG with a non-normative deprecation /
   "will-move-to-THO" notice and a forward pointer to the planned
   THO canonical, so implementers are pre-warned.
3. Bundle the actual move into the larger cross-IG FM/THO terminology
   work item lynn_laakso referenced (the same effort already cited
   on the 2025-07 block vote), with a single co-ordinated UTG
   submission covering all PCT siblings (FHIR-35529, -35532, -35533,
   -35534, -35536, -35537, -35540, -35545, -35548, -35549, -35550)
   and a PCT publication plan that re-points bindings in lock-step.

#### Justification

The 2022 and 2025 block votes both landed on essentially this stance
("Considered for Future Use / Resolve across IGs continuing to add to
THO"), and the WG kept reverting purely so the work would not be
"closed and forgotten." Disposition B captures that intent
explicitly: it commits to the move, sets implementer expectations,
but avoids changing canonical URLs piecemeal — which is the most
disruptive thing that can be done to a ValueSet, especially one
already referenced by GFE submitters and payers in production. It
also de-risks the dependency on UTG (Corey Spears flagged in 2022
that the migration is materially easier once UP-316 has produced
all-codes ValueSets for the external code systems involved).

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

**Not Persuasive** — close the ticket and keep
`PCTGFETypeOfBillVS` defined in the PCT IG namespace. Rationale
category: *the requested change is a publishing-pipeline / governance
preference, not a defect, and changing the canonical URL is itself a
breaking change for downstream implementers without a corresponding
implementer benefit.*

#### Justification

`PCTGFETypeOfBillVS` is a PCT-specific selection of NUBC Type-of-Bill
codes used for GFE bill typing. The IG has shipped two STU
publications referencing the current canonical, payers and
clearinghouses have hardcoded those URLs, and the only justification
for moving it is editorial alignment with a THO-anchoring rule that
has itself been re-litigated several times. Declining preserves
implementer stability; if THO later wants to host an aligned copy
it can do so without forcing the IG to re-point bindings.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **B**

Disposition B is the honest reflection of where this ticket has
actually landed twice on the WG floor — the workgroup keeps voting
"defer to the cross-IG THO effort" and then keeps re-opening the
ticket to keep the work on the books. Codifying that as *Persuasive
with Modification* (commit to the move, schedule it inside the
broader FM/THO effort, warn implementers in STU2) gives the WG a
defensible, non-breaking path that still honours the THO governance
direction. Disposition A is the right *eventual* end state but doing
the canonical re-anchor in isolation, ahead of UP-316 completion and
ahead of the sibling cluster being ready, would create the very
implementer churn the prior 9-0 votes were trying to avoid.
Disposition C ignores the standing FM/THO policy and the WG's own
pattern of re-opening the ticket. B keeps the policy commitment, the
implementer stability, and the cross-IG coordination all intact, and
gives the larger FM/THO migration a clear list of PCT artifacts (this
one plus the 10+ siblings) to retire in one stroke.
