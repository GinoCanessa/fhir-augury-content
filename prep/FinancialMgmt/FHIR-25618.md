## Summary

The reporter (Lisa R. Nelson) raises that a contract's "subject" can be a
provider, or a provider at a particular location, and asserts that limiting
the subject to `Organization` would be too narrow — `PractitionerRole` should
be allowed. In the first comment, Peter Knapp points out that
`Contract.subject` is currently typed as `Reference(Any)`, so `PractitionerRole`
is in fact already permitted; he asks whether the issue is really about a
*sub-element under* `Contract.subject` (most likely the contract term-level
subject, `Contract.term.topicReference`, which in R5 is `Reference(Any)` as
well, or the older `Contract.topic` / role-specific references). A follow-up
comment a year later invites Lisa and Lenel James to a Thursday FM call to
clarify, but no resolution comment was recorded. The closely related historical
ticket [FHIR-9536](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-9536) (Persuasive,
Published) renamed the original `Contract.subject` to `Contract.topic` and
redefined a new `Contract.subject` as "the entity impacted by the contract or
of interest to the parties to the contract" — context that explains why the
current element is intentionally broad (`Reference(Any)`) rather than
constrained to `Organization`.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat the request literally: in
`HL7/fhir → source/contract/structuredefinition-Contract.xml`, modify the
`<differential>` for `Contract.subject` so that its allowed target profiles
explicitly enumerate the provider-style references the reporter has in mind
— at minimum `Organization`, `Practitioner`, `PractitionerRole`,
`HealthcareService`, and `Location` — instead of leaving the element as
`Reference(Any)`. Update the element's short and definition prose to call
out that the subject of a Contract may be a provider, a provider role, or a
provider at a particular location. Also revisit the resource intro
narrative under `source/contract/*.html` for consistency.

#### Justification

This directly addresses the use case Lisa Nelson described — provider, or
provider-at-a-location as the contracting party. Narrowing the type list
gives implementers stronger guidance and validators the ability to catch
clearly-wrong references. It is consistent with the broader effort
documented in the 2018 "Adding PractitionerRole to resource references"
work (David Hay) and "Close out for R4" sweep (Lloyd McKenzie), where many
`*.subject` / `*.recorder` / `*.author` fields had `PractitionerRole` added
to their target lists.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — Clarify, don't constrain

#### Proposal

Keep `Contract.subject` as `Reference(Any)` (the outcome of the published
[FHIR-9536](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-9536) redesign) and instead
update the prose in `source/contract/structuredefinition-Contract.xml`
(element `definition` / `comments`) and the resource intro page under
`source/contract/` to add an explicit "Usage Note" enumerating common
subject types — including `Practitioner`, `PractitionerRole`,
`HealthcareService`, `Location`, and `Organization` — with a brief example
showing `PractitionerRole` as the subject. If a tighter contract is needed
for specific business contexts, recommend that implementers publish a
profile (e.g., a "ProviderContract" profile) that constrains
`Contract.subject.reference` to the desired target set.

Before responding to the reporter, also confirm with Lisa Nelson and Lenel
James whether the actual concern is a *sub-element* below `Contract.subject`
(per Knapp's 2020-02-06 comment) — most plausibly
`Contract.term.topicReference` — and, if so, apply the same
clarify-or-profile pattern to that element instead.

#### Justification

`Contract.subject` is intentionally `Reference(Any)` as the result of
FHIR-9536, and real implementers already exploit that breadth (e.g., the
Verily consent-template pattern referencing `ResearchStudy` from
`Contract.subject`). Narrowing the type to a fixed provider-oriented list
(Disposition A) would be a breaking change that invalidates those existing
deployments without a strong justification. Documentation plus profile
guidance preserves the flexibility while still answering Lisa's underlying
need that "provider" and "provider at a location" be obviously legal
choices. It also explicitly addresses Knapp's open question — which has
been the reason this ticket has sat in Triaged for five years.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (Not Persuasive — already addressed)

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as Not Persuasive with the rationale: `Contract.subject`
is already typed as `Reference(Any)` (per the resolution of FHIR-9536), so
`PractitionerRole`, `Practitioner`, `HealthcareService`, and `Location` are
all permitted today and no spec change is required. If the reporter is
actually concerned about a different element (e.g.,
`Contract.term.topicReference`), invite them to open a new, scoped ticket
identifying that element specifically.

#### Justification

The ticket is a Question, not a Change Request. The factual answer is in
the first comment from Peter Knapp on 2020-02-06: the element is already
`Reference(Any)`. Knapp's clarifying question — whether the issue is really
a sub-element — was never answered by the reporter, and there has been no
substantive activity since the 2021-02-18 invitation to the FM call.
Without further input from the reporter narrowing the concern, the work
group has nothing actionable to change in the spec.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

`Contract.subject` is already `Reference(Any)`, which moots Disposition A's
literal type-narrowing (and indeed makes A a breaking change against
implementers like the Verily consent-template scenario found on Zulip).
Disposition C is technically correct but leaves the ergonomic problem
behind the ticket unresolved — five years of the question lingering
suggests the spec text is not making it obvious enough that "provider" and
"provider-at-a-location" are valid subjects. Disposition B is the smallest
defensible change that (a) preserves the FHIR-9536 design,
(b) actually answers Lisa Nelson's question in the spec itself by adding
explicit usage guidance and an example, (c) routes implementers who need a
tighter contract toward a profile rather than a core-spec breaking change,
and (d) creates the opening to confirm with the reporter and Lenel James
whether the real concern is `Contract.term.topicReference` and re-scope (or
spawn a child ticket) accordingly. The work group should put this on the
agenda with the reporter present — Lisa explicitly asked for Lenel James to
be looped in for any in-person discussion.
