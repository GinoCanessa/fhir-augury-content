## Proposed Dispositions

> Note: the ticket is already labeled `Pre-Applied` with resolution
> *Persuasive* ("Spelling has been corrected"). The dispositions

> outcome.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Replace the literal phrase **"In Patient Care Provider queries"** with
**"Inpatient Care Provider queries"** in the US Da Vinci PDex IG
narrative where it currently appears (the Care Provider / queries
section flagged by the reporter). This is a pure prose / spelling
change — no profile, structure definition, search parameter, example,
or operation needs to be modified. Confirm the change has landed in
the PDex IG source and persists through the next publication build.

#### Justification

The reporter's existing-vs-proposed wording makes the intent
unambiguous: the term of art in the surrounding context is
"inpatient" (a single word), and "In Patient" reads as a typographic
error. This is exactly the kind of change the `TechnicalCorrection`
label is intended for, and the ticket is already marked `Pre-Applied`
with a "Spelling has been corrected" resolution, so accepting as
requested simply ratifies the applied fix.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the spelling fix, but treat this as a prompt to do a small
editorial sweep of the same PDex narrative for adjacent typos and
inconsistent capitalization of clinical setting terms (e.g.,
"inpatient", "outpatient", "ambulatory") so that the IG uses each
term consistently in surrounding sentences. Bundle any additional
editorial fixes under this same ticket's resolution rather than
filing a separate trackers chain.

#### Justification

Single-word spelling tickets are cheap to apply but expensive to
ballot one at a time. If the publishing editor has the file open,
folding in nearby editorial cleanups reduces future ticket volume
without changing any normative content. The downside is mild scope
creep; mitigated here because the change set stays strictly
narrative/editorial and carries the same `TechnicalCorrection`
character.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline as out of scope / not actionable: assert that the existing
wording "In Patient Care Provider queries" is intentional (e.g., as
a literal reading of "in [the] Patient ... queries") and leave the
narrative unchanged.

#### Justification

This disposition is included for completeness only. The reporter has
provided a clear before/after, the surrounding clinical-setting
vocabulary in PDex uses "inpatient" as a single word, and the ticket
has already been marked `Pre-Applied` with a resolution stating the
spelling has been corrected. There is no defensible reason to leave
a typo in published normative-track guidance.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is a textbook `TechnicalCorrection`: a one-word spelling
fix in IG prose, already pre-applied with a "Spelling has been
corrected" resolution and no normative or schema impact. Accepting
as requested simply confirms the applied change; there is no
implementer burden, no backward-compatibility concern, and no
cross-reference traffic suggesting a broader discussion is needed.
Disposition B is a reasonable opportunistic upgrade if an editor is
already in the file, but should not block ratifying A.
