## Summary

The ticket, filed by Peter Bomberg in 2020, requests wording changes to the
FHIR resources that Health Canada (HC) reviewed in support of "regulated
authorizations" — that is, the licensing and inspection workflows that
regulators use when authorizing regulated products, providers, or
facilities. The specific changes are not stated inline; they are enumerated
in an attached Word document (`Invoice related wording changes.v1.docx`),
and the title of the attachment together with the reporter's note that
"While it would be nice to have the ability to leverage invoices in R4B,
it is not critical" indicates the proposed edits centre on the `Invoice`
resource (and likely surrounding billing/charge wording), with the
implicit ask that they be considered for a future release if not R4B.

There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub
items associated with this Jira issue. Financial Management has already
proposed a disposition of **Not Persuasive**, on the basis that
invoicing for products and services outside of direct healthcare
(such as provider license fees collected by regulators) is outside
HL7's — and therefore FM's — scope. The reporter has been invited via
comments (2021, 2024, 2025) to attend an FM call to discuss; no response
from the reporter is recorded after September 2021.

## Proposed Dispositions

The dispositions below take account of the fact that (a) the substance of
the requested changes lives in an external Word attachment that is not
itself in scope for the augury cache, (b) there are no cross-referenced
discussions or PRs to lean on, and (c) FM has already telegraphed a
**Not Persuasive** outcome on scope grounds. Because no GitHub
cross-references were surfaced, no repo briefings are available; any
disposition that touches the FHIR Core spec would land in the
`HL7/fhir` source tree (e.g., `source/invoice/` for `Invoice`) — but
that scope must be confirmed by a fresh `repo-analysis` of `HL7/fhir`
before code-level work begins.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Accept the wording-change list in `16950_Invoice+related+wording+changes.v1.docx`
in full and apply the edits to the `Invoice` resource (and any
neighbouring billing wording the document calls out) in FHIR Core.
Concretely: review each change marker in the attachment, map it to the
corresponding element in `Invoice` (likely candidates include
`Invoice.subject`, `Invoice.recipient`, `Invoice.lineItem`,
`Invoice.totalGross/totalNet`, and the resource and element
`definition`/`comment` fields), and update the narrative wording so
that the resource is explicitly broad enough to cover regulator-issued
charges associated with licensing and inspections (e.g., provider
licence fees, inspection fees). Defer any structural changes to the
next normative-eligible release; do not retrofit into R4B, as the
reporter has explicitly stated R4B uptake is "not critical."

#### Justification

This honours the reporter's literal request. `Invoice` is already
defined in fairly generic billing terms, so wording-only adjustments to
make the regulator-↔-licensee scenario an explicitly-supported example
would be low-risk, schema-stable, and would let HC and similar
regulators reference FHIR `Invoice` without having to argue the resource
covers their use case. It also keeps a door open for jurisdictions that
treat regulator fees as part of their broader healthcare administration
data exchange.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Do not change the base `Invoice` resource wording. Instead, point the
reporter (and Health Canada) at the existing extension and profiling
mechanisms: recommend that the regulator-licensing-and-inspection
invoicing pattern be expressed as a **profile of `Invoice`** (with
`Invoice.subject` constrained to the licensee organization/practitioner
and `Invoice.issuer` constrained to the regulator), accompanied by
example instances, and published in a regional/realm IG (Canadian or
Health Canada-led) rather than in FHIR Core. Optionally, capture the
clarifying examples as **non-normative narrative additions** to
`Invoice` ("Usage notes" / "Boundaries and Relationships") that
acknowledge regulator-issued fee invoices are within the resource's
expressive range, without prescribing them.

#### Justification

This addresses the underlying interoperability need (regulators and
licensees exchanging fee invoices in a FHIR-native way) without
expanding FM's stewardship boundary or committing the WG to maintain
non-healthcare invoicing semantics in the base resource. It also
respects FHIR's general design principle of pushing
realm/jurisdiction-specific patterns down into IGs and profiles rather
than baking them into Core. Trade-off versus Disposition A: the
reporter must either build (or sponsor) a profile/IG, which is more
work than a wording patch, but the cost lands on the constituency that
benefits from the change.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (Not Persuasive)

#### Proposal

Resolve the ticket as **Not Persuasive** with the disposition text FM
already has on file: "The Financial Management Working Group's
responsibility within HL7 extends to the borders of information exchange
for healthcare, and invoicing for other products and services, such as
provider licence fees, are best served outside of HL7." Close without
changes to `Invoice` or related wording; encourage the reporter to take
the use case to an external standards venue or to a jurisdictional IG.

#### Justification

This is the disposition FM has effectively already adopted on the
ticket (resolution = Not Persuasive, dated 2025-03-25). It is
defensible on three grounds: (1) **scope** — FM's charter is healthcare
financial information exchange, not regulator-↔-licensee fee
collection; (2) **engagement** — the reporter has not engaged with FM
since 2021 despite three documented invitations (2021, 2024, 2025), so
there is no live constituency pushing the change; and (3) **specificity
gap** — the actual proposed wording lives in a non-machine-readable
Word attachment from 2020 that has never been distilled into concrete
element-level proposals on the ticket itself, making any "accept"
disposition operationally hard to act on without further input.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** C

FM should ratify the **Not Persuasive** disposition that is already on
the ticket. The scope argument is sound and consistent with how FM has
historically drawn its boundary, the reporter has been unresponsive
across multiple invitations spanning four years, and the specific
wording changes have never been transcribed from the attachment into
the ticket itself, so accepting (Disposition A) would require FM to
reverse-engineer the request before doing the work. Disposition B is a
reasonable courtesy redirection and could be **added as a closing
comment** when applying Disposition C ("if you wish to pursue this,
consider a profile of `Invoice` published via a Canadian/HC IG"), but
it should not be the formal resolution unless a sponsoring
implementer or IG steward steps forward.
