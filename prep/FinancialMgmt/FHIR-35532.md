## Summary

The reporter (Joan Harper) requests that the ValueSet **`PCTGFEItemCptHcpcsVS`**
in the Da Vinci PCT IG be re-anchored so that its canonical URL lives under
`terminology.hl7.org` (THO) rather than `hl7.org/fhir/us/davinci-pct`. The
ticket asserts the original principle that only ValueSets bound `required`
to a `code` data type should remain in an IG's own canonical namespace; all
other ValueSets — and `PCTGFEItemCptHcpcsVS` is bound to
`(claim/eob).item.modifier` (a `CodeableConcept`) — belong in THO. The
ticket also asks that a UTG companion ticket be opened to actually register
the ValueSet in THO; that companion was created as
[UP-283: Create CPT/HCPCS VS in THO](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/UP-283),
with the proposed name **`USClaim-Professional-productOrService-Codes`** to
mirror the sibling ticket
[FHIR-35531](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-35531) (the same THO-anchoring
change for the Institutional VS, already published as *Not Persuasive with
Modification*).

The ticket has been carried across three reconciliation cycles. It was
pulled from the April 2022 Block Vote 2 at Pat Taylor's request so the VS
name could be aligned with FHIR-35531; resolved as *Considered for Future
Use* in November 2022 (revoted *Not Persuasive with Modification*-style
deferral); re-opened post-STU1; deferred again in the July 2025 Block-Vote
(`Block-2025-Jul`) on the basis that "this VS will require a greater level
of review across multiple IGs, and is planned in the coming months in
larger FM/THO effort that has already gotten underway"; and re-opened
again on 2025-10-08 post-STU2 publication. In April 2025 Corey Spears also
raised a scoping question on the ticket itself — whether the right move is
a single THO ValueSet covering *all* US procedure codes, and how that
would relate to the overlapping `PCTProcedureSurgicalCodes` (used in
`claim.procedure`) and `USClaimMedicalProductOrServiceCodes` (used in
`(claim/eob).item.productOrService`).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Migrate the canonical of `PCTGFEItemCptHcpcsVS` out of
`http://hl7.org/fhir/us/davinci-pct/ValueSet/PCTGFEItemCptHcpcsVS` and
into a THO-anchored canonical (e.g.
`http://terminology.hl7.org/ValueSet/USClaim-Professional-productOrService-Codes`),
delivered as part of the THO content via UP-283. In the next PCT IG
release: (a) delete or retire the in-IG `PCTGFEItemCptHcpcsVS` resource;
(b) update every binding that currently references the in-IG canonical
— most importantly the binding on `Claim.item.modifier` /
`ExplanationOfBenefit.item.modifier` in the PCT GFE profiles — to point
at the new THO canonical; (c) keep the binding strength as it stands
(it is a `CodeableConcept` binding, so `extensible`/`preferred`, not
`required`, which is what triggers the "must move to THO" rule in the
first place); (d) coordinate publication so the THO release containing
the new ValueSet is available before, or simultaneous with, the PCT
release that drops the local copy.

#### Justification

This is the resolution the ticket has literally asked for since 2022,
and the same pattern was already accepted for the Institutional sibling
in [FHIR-35531](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-35531) (Published, *Not
Persuasive with Modification*). The governance rule it invokes — that
non-`required` / non-`code` ValueSets belong in THO — is the standing
HL7 / Vocabulary guidance reaffirmed at the Sept 2022 WGM. The companion
UTG ticket [UP-283](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/UP-283) is already open
to do the THO-side work with the agreed name
`USClaim-Professional-productOrService-Codes`. Accepting now closes
the loop the original report opened, removes a long-standing
inconsistency between PCT and the rest of the FM IGs, and prevents
yet another ballot cycle from re-opening the same ticket.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Resolve as **Persuasive with Modification**, but explicitly fold this
ticket into the larger cross-IG FM/THO terminology rationalisation
effort that Corey Spears flagged on 2025-07-01. Concretely: (1) leave
`PCTGFEItemCptHcpcsVS` in place for STU2 with no canonical change in
the immediate next PCT errata release; (2) commit, in the resolution
text, to a single THO-hosted CPT+HCPCS ValueSet that supersedes
`PCTGFEItemCptHcpcsVS`, `PCTProcedureSurgicalCodes`, and any
overlapping use of `USClaimMedicalProductOrServiceCodes`, with the
scoping question raised by Corey Spears (2025-04-16) answered as part
of the disposition — i.e. decide whether one VS or multiple
context-specific VSes (modifier vs. procedure vs. productOrService)
should exist in THO; (3) drive the THO work through UP-283 and a
companion VS-rationalisation ticket, with the PCT IG re-bound in the
following major release once THO content is published; (4) tag the
ticket so it travels as a unit with FHIR-35531 and any sister tickets
in C4BB / CARIN / DEQM that reference the same code systems.

#### Justification

The narrow rename-and-relocate proposal in Disposition A is technically
correct but keeps stepping on a real, unresolved design question that
PCT has surfaced twice without answering: are these three VSes
genuinely distinct, or is the IG carrying redundant terminology that
should be consolidated before it's lifted into THO? Lifting
`PCTGFEItemCptHcpcsVS` into THO unchanged would either (a) entrench
that redundancy at the THO layer, or (b) force a second THO migration
later when the rationalisation does happen, with all the version
churn that implies. Disposition B respects the community signal from
the 2025-07-01 block vote (which approved the deferral 9-0-2) while
giving the requester a concrete commitment and a tracked path to
closure, instead of the indefinite "considered for future use" loop
this ticket has been stuck in since 2022.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve as **Not Persuasive** on the narrow ground that the canonical
URL of `PCTGFEItemCptHcpcsVS` is already part of the published STU1
and STU2 normative footprint of the PCT IG, and changing it now would
be a breaking change for downstream payers, providers, and reference
implementations that have already adopted the in-IG canonical. Direct
the requester to use the existing extensibility mechanism: external
implementations that prefer the THO canonical may bind their own
profiles to the THO ValueSet (once UP-283 is published) without
requiring PCT to retract its own.

#### Justification

The strict THO-anchoring rule is governance guidance, not a
conformance requirement. The PCT IG has shipped two STU releases with
the current canonical, and at least one previous deferral
(2022-11-29, 9-0-1) was specifically "we will get to this when THO
guidance is ready", which has now stretched to four years. A
canonical-URL change is, by FHIR's own rules, a breaking change to
every consumer that resolves it. With UP-283 already creating a THO VS
that consumers can opt into, the marginal benefit of also rewriting
PCT's bindings is small relative to the implementer disruption.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The history on this ticket — two prior *Considered for Future Use*
resolutions, both reverted; an explicit Zulip pull from Block Vote 2
in 2022 because the VS name was wrong; an explicit scoping question
from Corey Spears in 2025 about whether the three overlapping VSes
should even all exist; and a 9-0-2 community vote in July 2025 saying
"this belongs in the larger FM/THO effort" — makes Disposition A's
narrow rename-and-relocate feel like it is solving the symptom and
not the disease. Disposition C is also unattractive: the requester's
underlying point (non-`required` `CodeableConcept` bindings shouldn't
own their own canonical in an IG) is correct, the sister ticket
FHIR-35531 was already accepted on those grounds, and "decline because
it's hard to coordinate with THO" sets a poor precedent. Disposition B
gives the workgroup a way to honour the request, honour the community's
own block-vote rationale, and use this ticket to drive the answer to
Corey's scoping question via UP-283 — instead of reopening FHIR-35532
for a fourth time after STU3.
