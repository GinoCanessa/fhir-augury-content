## Summary

The reporter observes that the `PaymentReconciliation` resource — specifically
its `allocation` backbone — does not adequately represent **Provider Level
Adjustments (PLBs)** as they are used in U.S. payer/provider remittance
practice today. PLBs are payment line items that an insurer applies at the
provider/organization level (interest, bonus payments, capitation
withholding, refund recovery, late filing penalty, etc.) rather than
against a specific claim, claim line, or invoice. Because every entry in
`PaymentReconciliation.allocation` expects a `target` (the claim,
ClaimResponse, Invoice, etc. being paid), there is no clear modeling
pattern for an allocation that intentionally has no claim-level target.

The reporter raises two related gaps:

1. Whether it is acceptable to leave `allocation.target` (and
   `targetItem[x]`) unpopulated for an organization-level adjustment, and
   if so whether that should be sanctioned by the spec rather than being
   an undocumented workaround.
2. The lack of a structured "payment reason" element to convey the PLB
   reason — equivalent to the X12 835 `PLB03-1` adjustment-reason code
   set (e.g., `50` Late Charge, `72` Authorized Return, `WO` Overpayment
   Recovery, `CV` Capitation Interest, `L6` Interest Owed, `BD` Bad
   Debt Adjustment, etc.). The current allocation has no element whose
   semantics match "why this payer-level dollar amount is being moved."

The ticket is from a U.S. claims-processing perspective and is anchored
in X12 835 alignment, which is the dominant remittance format that
`PaymentReconciliation` is expected to interoperate with in the United
States.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update `PaymentReconciliation.allocation` in FHIR Core to accommodate
Provider Level Adjustments explicitly:

1. Relax / clarify `allocation.target` and `allocation.targetItem[x]` to
   make them explicitly optional with a new use-case note: "When the
   payer applies an amount to the payee organization as a whole rather
   than to a specific claim or invoice (e.g., a Provider Level
   Adjustment in an X12 835 PLB segment), `target` and `targetItem[x]`
   SHALL be omitted." Add an invariant or guidance that, when both are
   absent, `payee` (or the parent `PaymentReconciliation.recipient`) is
   the entity the adjustment applies to.
2. Add a new optional CodeableConcept element
   `PaymentReconciliation.allocation.reason` (or a more specific name
   such as `adjustmentReason`), bound `extensible` to a new value set
   `payment-adjustment-reason` whose initial concept set is sourced from
   X12 835 PLB03-1 (e.g., `50` Late Charge, `72` Authorized Return,
   `WO` Overpayment Recovery, `L6` Interest Owed, `CV` Capitation
   Interest, `BD` Bad Debt Adjustment, `AP` Accelerated Payment, `IR`
   Internal Revenue Service Withholding, `J1` Nonreimbursable, `FB`
   Forwarding Balance, etc.), with the X12 code-system OID identified
   externally and FHIR-curated synonyms where licensing permits.
3. Add explanatory paragraphs in the resource notes ("Provider Level
   Adjustments") and an example instance demonstrating a `PLB` mapping
   (e.g., a $250 interest payment with no `target` and
   `reason = L6 "Interest Owed"`).

#### Justification

The request reflects real, high-volume U.S. remittance traffic that
`PaymentReconciliation` is expected to interoperate with. Today there
is no sanctioned modeling pattern for organization-level payment
adjustments, leaving every implementer to choose between (a) leaving
`target` empty without spec backing, (b) inventing an extension, or
(c) misusing an unrelated `target` reference. Providing a documented
optional pattern plus a structured reason element (analogous to
`Adjudication.reason` on `ClaimResponse`) eliminates ambiguity, gives
US Realm IGs a stable hook, and aligns the resource with the `X12 835`
mapping the spec already aspires to.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Address the same need without changing the FHIR Core resource
structurally:

1. Add a non-normative **mapping note and worked example** to
   `PaymentReconciliation` that explicitly states an `allocation` MAY
   be recorded with no `target`/`targetItem[x]` to represent a payer
   adjustment that applies at the payee/organization level, and that
   the existing `allocation.type` (CodeableConcept) is the carrier for
   the adjustment reason. Re-bind or extend the value set behind
   `allocation.type` (currently
   `payment-type`) to include the PLB03-1 reason concepts, or layer a
   second value set in.
2. Define a US Realm IG (Da Vinci PDex Payer Network / US Drug
   Formulary / a new "Payer Remittance" companion guide, depending on
   FM's roadmap) that:
   - Profiles `PaymentReconciliation` with `target` cardinality
     constrained on a slice for "PLB allocations".
   - Adds a `payment-adjustment-reason` extension carrying the X12
     PLB03-1 code (until/unless core picks it up later).
   - Provides canonical examples for the most common PLB scenarios
     (interest, capitation withholding, recoupment).
3. Open a follow-up ticket against the IG once the core mapping note
   and value-set extension land.

#### Justification

This route avoids a normative resource-structure change in a Maturity
Model resource that already has implementer footprint, and pushes the
PLB-specific machinery into a US Realm IG where the X12 835 alignment
naturally lives. It also preserves backward compatibility for
implementers who already leave `target` unpopulated. The trade-off is
that international implementers gain only a clarification, not a
first-class element, and the "payment reason" concept lives on
`allocation.type` (slightly overloaded) plus an extension rather than
on a dedicated core element.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the ticket as **resolvable with the existing resource** and
close as "Not Persuasive". Rationale: `PaymentReconciliation.allocation`
already permits `target` to be absent (0..1 cardinality), and
`allocation.type` carries CodeableConcept semantics suitable for the
adjustment reason. PLB-specific concepts are best handled in a US
Realm guide rather than in international FHIR Core, and X12 835
alignment is already understood as US-realm guidance work for FM.

#### Justification

`PaymentReconciliation` is a Financial Management resource whose core
element set is intentionally minimal and locale-neutral; PLB and the
PLB03-1 reason code list are X12 835 constructs specific to the U.S.
payer ecosystem. Adopting them at the resource level risks pulling
American payer business rules into international FHIR Core, and the
existing `allocation.target` cardinality plus `allocation.type` already
provide enough surface to convey the data. Implementers who need the
full X12 PLB fidelity can do so in a US Realm IG without changing core.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter has identified a real and concrete modeling gap in a
resource whose explicit purpose is to represent payer-issued
remittances, and the gap exists on two distinct axes: (1) whether a
target-less allocation is sanctioned, and (2) how to convey the
adjustment reason. Disposition C papers over the gap by relying on
implicit cardinality and an overloaded `allocation.type`, which
guarantees divergent implementations and continued tickets in the same
area. Disposition B (US Realm IG only) is workable but pushes a
universally-recognised remittance concept (organization-level payment
adjustment with a structured reason) entirely into national guidance,
which is heavier and slower than fixing the model. Disposition A is the
most direct: a small, additive change — clarify the cardinality
intent, add an optional `reason` CodeableConcept with an extensible
binding, and document the PLB mapping — keeps backward compatibility,
unblocks U.S. implementers, and gives non-U.S. payers a generic hook
they can bind to their own reason vocabularies. The workgroup should
weigh whether `reason` belongs on `allocation` (per this proposal) or
on a new `PaymentReconciliation.adjustment` slice; that's a friendly
amendment rather than a reason to reject A.
