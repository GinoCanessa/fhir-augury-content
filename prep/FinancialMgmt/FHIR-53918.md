## Summary

The reporter asks for editorial clarifications to the narrative content of
the PaymentNotice resource page in FHIR Core, specifically section 16.8.1
(Scope and Usage). Three distinct wording changes are proposed:

1. Tighten the opening sentence so it reads as a single, clearer statement
   ("The PaymentNotice resource reports the current status information of
   the indicated payment.") rather than the current double-clause sentence
   that conflates "indicates the resource for which payment has been
   indicated" with "reports the current status".
2. Split the long second sentence (introduced in R5) into two sentences
   and fix a punctuation/spacing issue around the "for example…" clause.
   Also normalize the spelling of "canceled" to "cancelled" so that the
   16.8 section is internally consistent.
3. Consider removing the third paragraph of 16.8.1 because it largely
   restates content already in the first paragraph (payers/providers
   advising each other and regulators of payment status, employer/exchange
   premium-payment use case).

No code, schema, or constraint changes are proposed; this is a documentation
clarification ticket against the PaymentNotice resource intro narrative.

There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets and no related GitHub items.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply all three editorial changes to `paymentnotice.html` §16.8.1 (Scope
and Usage) of FHIR Core, exactly as proposed by the reporter:

1. Replace the first sentence with: "The PaymentNotice resource reports
   the current status information of the indicated payment."
2. Replace the long second sentence with two sentences and fix the
   missing space / introduce the period before "For example":
   "The payment notice may be used by providers and payers to advise the
   provider or regulatory bodies of the state of a payment (check in the
   mail/EFT sent, payment cashed, payment cancelled). For example, it may
   be used to provide payment status information or to document the
   forwarding or receipt of payment to support regulatory requirements."
   At the same time, normalize the spelling from "canceled" to
   "cancelled" to match the rest of §16.8.
3. Remove the third paragraph beginning "Payers and/or providers may have
   the need to advise providers and/or regulators…" on the grounds that
   it duplicates the first paragraph; if the employer / insurance-exchange
   / premium-payment use case is considered worth preserving, fold a
   single sentence about it into the first paragraph rather than keeping
   the standalone paragraph.

This is a narrative-only change to the PaymentNotice resource intro page;
no element definitions, cardinalities, value sets, search parameters,
operations, or constraints are affected, and no examples need to change.

#### Justification

The reporter's wording is clearly easier to parse and removes a real
defect (missing space between "canceled)" and "for example") that was
introduced in R5. The proposed first sentence preserves the original
meaning while eliminating the awkward "indicates the resource for which
the payment has been indicated" phrasing. Spelling normalization
("cancelled") matches the existing usage elsewhere in §16.8 and is a
trivial consistency fix. Editorial clarifications of intro narrative are
the cheapest, lowest-risk class of FM-WG change: they require no schema
or example regeneration, are fully backward compatible for implementers,
and improve the on-ramp for new readers of PaymentNotice.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept items 1 and 2 as written, but for item 3 keep the third paragraph
in place and only lightly edit it instead of deleting it. Specifically:

- Apply edits 1 and 2 verbatim as in Disposition A (including the
  spelling normalization to "cancelled").
- For item 3, retain the paragraph but rewrite it to add value rather
  than restate paragraph 1 — e.g., explicitly call out the
  employer/insurance-exchange/premium-payment scenario as a *distinct*
  use case ("In addition to provider–payer notification, PaymentNotice
  is also used by employers and insurance exchanges to notify payers
  that premium payments have been issued and settled.") and drop the
  "advise providers and/or regulators of the status of claim
  settlement" sentence that genuinely is duplicative of paragraph 1.

#### Justification

The premium-payment / insurance-exchange use case in the third paragraph
is the only place in §16.8.1 where that scope is mentioned. Outright
deletion (Disposition A item 3) risks losing the only signal to
implementers that PaymentNotice is intentionally scoped beyond
provider↔payer claim-payment messaging. Reframing the paragraph as a
*differentiating* example, rather than removing it, addresses the
reporter's redundancy concern while preserving the functional scope
information. This is also a smaller departure from the published R5 text,
which lowers the risk of an incidental scope-narrowing change being read
into a clarification ticket.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change as not warranting a normative-page edit at this time.
Rationale category: "Editorial — does not change conformance or
implementer behavior; current text is understandable in context."
Recommend the reporter raise the wording cleanups against the next major
version's editorial pass rather than as a per-paragraph change request.

#### Justification

PaymentNotice §16.8.1 is intro narrative; no implementer's conformance
or interoperability is affected by the current wording, and the
"canceled" vs "cancelled" inconsistency, while real, has no semantic
impact. If the FM WG batches editorial cleanups (typos, sentence
splitting, paragraph trimming) across all FM resource pages into a
single editorial sweep, that is typically lower-cost than processing
them as individual change requests. Declining-with-routing keeps the FM
ticket queue focused on substantive resource changes.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B captures all of the reporter's clearly-correct fixes
(item 1's tighter opening sentence, item 2's sentence split and the
"canceled → cancelled" normalization that fixes a real internal
inconsistency in §16.8) while preserving the only mention of the
employer / insurance-exchange premium-payment use case, which is genuine
scope information rather than pure restatement. Disposition A is close
behind, but outright deletion of paragraph 3 risks silently narrowing
the documented scope of PaymentNotice; reframing instead of removing
keeps that signal explicit. Disposition C undersells a low-risk,
high-clarity improvement — the missing space before "for example" and
the inconsistent spelling are defects, not stylistic preferences, and
addressing them now is cheaper than carrying them to a future editorial
sweep that may or may not happen on the next cycle.
