## Proposed Dispositions

The ticket is already labeled `Pre-Applied` with resolution
`Persuasive` and a resolution note stating the spelling error was
fixed. The dispositions below reflect that reality: confirming the
applied edit, broadening the edit, or rejecting the change.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Confirm the applied technical correction in the US Da Vinci PDex
narrative: change "This information iis added" to "This information is
added" in the IG page where the existing wording appears. No
metadata, conformance, or schema changes are required — this is a
pure narrative typo fix. Verify the edit is committed in the PDex IG
source and that the rendered IG no longer contains "iis".

#### Justification

The reporter quoted the exact existing text and the exact replacement
("iis" → "is"); there is no ambiguity about intent. Spelling
corrections in narrative IG text are non-substantive and have no
conformance impact, which is precisely the scope of a Technical
Correction. The workgroup has already marked the ticket `Persuasive`
and `Pre-Applied`, so this disposition simply ratifies what has
already been done.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Apply the requested fix and, in the same editorial pass, sweep the
PDex IG narrative for similar duplicated-letter typos (e.g.,
"iis", "thee", "ann", "thiis") and other obvious spelling errors,
correcting them under this ticket's resolution rather than waiting
for additional ballot comments. Document the broader sweep in the
resolution note so reviewers can audit the scope.

#### Justification

A single-letter typo fix is nearly free to apply, but the editorial
review and publication overhead per ticket is not. Bundling
trivially-verifiable typo corrections under one Technical Correction
reduces ticket noise in subsequent ballot cycles and improves the
narrative quality of the IG. The risk is small because no normative
content is touched. The trade-off vs. Disposition A is that the
applied change becomes broader than what was literally requested,
which can complicate diff review — mitigated by listing every
corrected location in the resolution note.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change on the grounds that the reported text could not be
located in the current PDex IG narrative, or that the typo has already
been addressed under another change, and close the ticket as
"Not Persuasive" or "Not Persuasive with Mod" rather than
"Persuasive".

#### Justification

This disposition is only defensible if reviewers cannot reproduce the
quoted "iis" string in the current IG source. Given the ticket is
already marked `Persuasive` and `Pre-Applied`, declining at this
stage would require evidence that the prior fix was misapplied or
that the typo never existed. Absent such evidence, declining a
zero-risk editorial correction is hard to justify and would be
inconsistent with how other PDex-Vote-1 Technical Corrections have
been handled.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is a textbook Technical Correction: a single-letter
spelling fix in narrative text, with the existing and proposed wording
quoted verbatim by the reporter. The workgroup has already resolved
it as `Persuasive` and tagged it `Pre-Applied`, and the resolution
note confirms the fix was made. The right action for the workgroup
review is to ratify Disposition A and verify the edit landed in the
PDex IG source. Disposition B is attractive in principle but expands
scope beyond what the ticket reports and should be pursued as a
separate editorial-sweep ticket if desired. Disposition C is not
supportable given the current ticket state and the absence of any
evidence contradicting the reporter.
