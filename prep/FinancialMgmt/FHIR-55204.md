## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Migrate all three flagged value sets and their backing code systems out of
the FHIR Core specification and into Terminology.HL7 (THO):

1. `CodeSystem/claim-decision` (ClaimAdjudicationDecisionCodes) and the
   matching `ValueSet/claim-decision` — bound at `ClaimResponse.decision`.
2. `CodeSystem/datestype` (DatesEventTypeCodes) and the matching
   `ValueSet/datestype` — bound at `ClaimResponse.event.type`.
3. `CodeSystem/claim-decision-reason` (ClaimAdjudicationDecisionReasonCodes)
   and the matching `ValueSet/claim-decision-reason` — bound at
   `ClaimResponse.item.reviewOutcome.reason`.

For each: republish the resource under the
`http://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/...` and
`http://terminology.hl7.org/ValueSet/...` canonical URLs, retire (or stub +
redirect) the existing FHIR-core-canonical artifact, and update the
`ClaimResponse` element bindings to point at the THO ValueSet canonicals.
Coordinate the publication windows with the THO release team and the next
FHIR Core ballot/publication so the bindings are not dangling.

#### Justification

These three terminologies describe adjudication-decision concepts and event
typing that are domain-stable Financial-Management vocabularies — exactly
the class of content THO is intended to steward. THO publication decouples
the value-set lifecycle from the FHIR Core release cadence, allowing FM to
add or refine codes without waiting for an R-version bump, and exposes the
codes for reuse by V2/CDA financial work and by IGs such as Da Vinci. The
ticket is explicit that the workgroup should *consider* migration when
either of those conditions holds, and `FM-Term` labelling indicates this is
the workgroup's terminology-stewardship queue.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Migrate selectively rather than wholesale: move only `claim-decision`
(ClaimAdjudicationDecisionCodes) and `claim-decision-reason`
(ClaimAdjudicationDecisionReasonCodes) to THO, and **retain `datestype` in
FHIR Core**. Reasoning by artifact:

- `claim-decision` and `claim-decision-reason` are general adjudication
  vocabularies likely to be referenced from V2/CDA financial transactions
  and from FHIR financial IGs, and are realistically going to gain codes
  over time — they meet both THO-migration triggers in the ticket.
- `datestype` (the codes used at `ClaimResponse.event.type` to label
  *which* date a given event represents) is narrowly scoped to the
  ClaimResponse event-date pattern, has limited cross-product reuse value,
  and the binding is `example`/`extensible` in nature; leaving it in core
  avoids gratuitous churn.

For the two migrated artifacts, follow the same mechanics as Disposition A
(republish under THO canonicals, retire the FHIR-core copies, retarget
`ClaimResponse.decision` and `ClaimResponse.item.reviewOutcome.reason`
bindings).

#### Justification

The ticket explicitly says migration is *not required* and should be driven
by (a) expected change rate and (b) cross-product utility. Applying that
test per artifact instead of as a batch produces a more defensible result:
the two adjudication-outcome vocabularies clearly satisfy both criteria,
while `datestype` is more of a structural-event-label list tied to the
ClaimResponse model and is a weaker THO candidate. This reduces
coordination overhead with the THO release team and avoids inflating THO
with content that has no realistic cross-spec consumer.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Take no migration action this cycle. Resolve the ticket as *Persuasive with
Mod / Not Persuasive — defer*: keep all three value sets and code systems
defined in FHIR Core under their current canonicals, and document in the
ClaimResponse resource notes that THO migration was considered but
deferred pending a concrete cross-product (V2/CDA/IG) reuse case or a
demonstrated need to publish out-of-cycle.

#### Justification

The ticket itself states migration is not required and the bar is "if it's
realistic for the value set definition to need to change" or for cross-spec
reuse. There are no Zulip threads, no linked Jira tickets, and no GitHub
PRs/issues backing an immediate need; the ticket appears to be a sweep-
generated candidate (Comment 170 - imported by Lloyd McKenzie) rather than
a request driven by a concrete consumer. Migration carries non-trivial
cost — coordinating THO and FHIR Core publication windows, updating every
binding reference, and managing canonical-URL redirection — and absent a
concrete pull from another specification family, the workgroup is entitled
to defer.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The ticket is correctly flagging real THO candidates, but it is also
explicit that migration is judgment-driven rather than mandatory, and the
three artifacts are not equivalent. `claim-decision` and
`claim-decision-reason` are the kind of adjudication-outcome vocabularies
that V2 and CDA financial work, and downstream IGs (e.g., Da Vinci PAS /
CRD), have a credible interest in reusing, and they are also the most
likely to accrue new codes between FHIR Core releases — both of the
ticket's own migration triggers. `datestype` is narrower, structurally
coupled to the ClaimResponse event pattern, and there is no surfaced
evidence of cross-product demand. Disposition A over-commits scope and
locks FM into coordinating three THO publications instead of two;
Disposition C ignores a real terminology-stewardship signal that the
`FM-Term` label exists to track. Disposition B captures the value with
proportionate effort and gives FM a defensible per-artifact rationale on
the ballot record.
