## Summary

The reporter asks the Da Vinci Payer-to-Payer (PDex) authors to clarify
whether multi-hop payer history needs to be standardized when a member
changes plans repeatedly within the 5-year P2P data window. The driving
scenario has a member on Plan A in 2022, Plan B in 2023, and Plan C in
2024: in 2024 Plan C requests data from Plan B, which holds both its
own records and records previously imported from Plan A. The ticket
poses two specific questions: (1) whether Plan C should be able to
retrieve the upstream Plan A records that now reside at Plan B, and
(2) how `Provenance` should be carried — whether the receiving plan
should preserve the original chain of `Provenance` resources, or whether
it is sufficient (and preferable) for Plan C to record only that the
data came from Plan B and treat the deeper history as opaque. The
reporter leans toward the simpler "my data vs. data imported from a
prior plan" model and questions the value of preserving the full
upstream provenance chain. There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets,
Zulip threads, or GitHub items associated with this issue. The ticket
has already been resolved by the work group as **Not Persuasive**, with
the disposition arguing that retaining original-source provenance is
valuable and that flattening it onto the most recent payer would create
unnecessary discovery work for that payer.

## Details

**Description:**

Asking for the clarification on the several payer-to-payer scenarios if
the behaviour for the scenario below requires standardization.

**Use case — multiple plan changes within 5 years**

Member Lara Mirren has plan A in 2022, plan B in 2023 and plan C in
2024. In this case it is expected that

a. Plan B acts as a client in 2023 and grabs Plan A records. Thus, in
2023, Lara Mirren is with plan B. Plan B has records from Plan A and
Plan B.

b. Plan C acts as a client in 2024 and grabs Plan B records.

**Questions and assumptions**

1. Should Plan C be able to grab Plans A and B records from plan B?
2. Should Plan C keep all original Provenance resources or should ditch
   them and create Plan C's Provenance resource that says "this data is
   from Plan B, I don't care much how and where plan B took it, for me
   it is just Plan B's data". I think this is enough for Plan C to
   indicate. An alternative is gathering the way to many Provenance
   resources with the indication that "This data is from Plan B but
   also actually it came from A but before that it even came from
   X...". I think the only information that the current plan should
   have is current plan resources vs imported from previous plans, i.e.
   my vs created by someone data. There is no need to keep the whole
   history.

**Comments:**

No comments are recorded on this ticket.

**Resolution (Not Persuasive):**

> Payer-to-Payer Exchange is limited to 5 years of data. This may limit
> the multi-payer history somewhat.
>
> I believe there is value in retaining the provenance that identifies
> the original source of data. If Plan C re-wrote provenance to point
> all data as coming from Payer B it could cause work for Payer B to
> discover the data being queried was not theirs, but rather belonged
> to Plan A.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the US Da Vinci PDex IG narrative on Payer-to-Payer Exchange
(member-match / `$member-match` and the bulk P2P data export pages) to
state explicitly that:

1. A receiving payer (Plan C) MAY request the full set of resources the
   sending payer (Plan B) holds for the member within the 5-year
   window, including resources that Plan B itself imported from earlier
   payers. There is no requirement for the sending payer to filter out
   "non-native" data.
2. The receiving payer (Plan C) is REQUIRED to record only a single
   `Provenance` instance per imported bundle attributing the data to
   the immediate sending payer (Plan B). The receiving payer is NOT
   required to preserve or forward the upstream `Provenance` chain
   (Plan A → Plan B). If upstream `Provenance` resources are returned
   by Plan B, the receiving payer MAY discard them.

Add normative SHALL/SHOULD language and at least one example bundle on
the IG showing the simplified single-hop `Provenance` Plan C would
write.

#### Justification

This matches the reporter's stated mental model — "my data vs.
imported-from-previous-plan data" — and minimizes the long-term growth
of `Provenance` chains as members change plans. It also lowers
implementation burden on receiving payers, who otherwise must reason
about and persist arbitrarily deep provenance graphs that they did not
generate and cannot independently verify. The 5-year window inherently
caps how far back the chain can stretch, which limits the practical
information loss from collapsing it.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the underlying behaviour the reporter describes (Plan C may
retrieve all in-window data from Plan B), but **preserve original-source
`Provenance`** rather than collapsing it. Specifically, add IG guidance
that:

1. Sending payers (Plan B) SHALL include any `Provenance` resources
   they hold that point at the original source payer for previously
   imported records, scoped to the 5-year window.
2. Receiving payers (Plan C) SHALL persist those upstream `Provenance`
   resources alongside their own newly-created `Provenance` that
   attributes the *transfer* to Plan B (i.e., add a hop, do not
   replace). The new `Provenance.agent` identifies Plan B as the
   transmitter; existing `Provenance.agent` entries identifying Plan A
   as the original author are retained.
3. Add an IG example showing a layered `Provenance` graph (Plan A
   author → Plan B transmitter → Plan C transmitter) and a short
   "implementer note" explaining how to query for original source.

#### Justification

This is essentially the position the work group took in the Not
Persuasive resolution: retaining original-source provenance is valuable
for downstream uses (audit, payer-of-record disputes, member-facing
"where did this come from?" queries, regulatory reporting) and
explicitly avoids putting Plan B in the awkward position of being
asked, after the fact, about data that was never theirs. The trade-off
versus Disposition A is more `Provenance` volume on the receiving side,
but the data is small relative to the clinical/financial payload, and
the 5-year cap bounds the worst case.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as **Not Persuasive** with no IG change, on the grounds that the
existing PDex guidance and the underlying FHIR `Provenance` semantics
already cover this scenario adequately, and that the work group has
already taken a position that original-source provenance should be
preserved. Reply to the reporter pointing at the resolution comment and
the existing PDex P2P narrative, and treat any remaining
implementer-side ambiguity as a question for the PDex implementer
forum / Zulip rather than a spec change.

#### Justification

Defensible because: (a) the current resolution already expresses a
clear work-group position; (b) the questions in the ticket are
clarification questions, not a concrete change proposal — there is no
specific element, constraint, or operation flagged for change; and (c)
no Zulip discussion, linked tickets, or implementer pull requests have
been brought forward to demonstrate that current implementations are
diverging or blocked. Declining keeps the IG stable and avoids spending
ballot cycles on text whose normative content would not change.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The work group has already landed on the substance of Disposition B in
its Not Persuasive resolution: original-source provenance has real
value, and re-pointing all imported data at the most recent payer would
create operational pain for that payer. However, simply closing as Not
Persuasive (Disposition C) leaves the IG in its current state, which is
exactly what prompted the reporter to ask the question — the IG does
not say clearly what a receiving payer must do with imported
`Provenance`. Disposition B preserves the work group's chosen semantics
*and* removes the ambiguity by adding explicit normative text and an
example to the PDex P2P pages. Disposition A is rejected because the
work group has already weighed and dismissed the "collapse provenance"
model, and adopting it now would be a substantive reversal without new
implementer evidence to justify it. Recommend re-opening the ticket as
"Persuasive with Modification" along the lines of B; if the work group
prefers to keep the existing close, fall back to C and add a short FAQ
entry on the IG page rather than changing the resolution.
