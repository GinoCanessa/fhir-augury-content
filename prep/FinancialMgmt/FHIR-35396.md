## Summary

The ticket asks the CARIN IG for Blue Button® work group to re-anchor the
canonical URL of `CodeSystem-C4BBPayeeType` so that it is published from
terminology.hl7.org (THO) rather than under the `hl7.org/fhir/us/carin-bb`
namespace. The reporter notes that a companion UTG ticket is required to
register the CodeSystem in THO. A previous block-vote disposition
("Considered for Future Use", 13-0-0 on 2022-07-26) was reverted post
STU2 publication, with the work group planning to move the CodeSystem
once the UTG ticket [UP-315](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/UP-315) ("Update
FHIR payee type Terminologies details (change from example)") is
completed and published. No other Jira tickets are explicitly cross-
referenced from FHIR Augury, but the ticket is part of the Block-Vote-6
batch (see Related Zulip Discussions) which groups many sibling tickets
of the same shape (`FHIR-35390`–`FHIR-35399`) requesting THO anchoring
for other CARIN BB code systems.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Move `CodeSystem-C4BBPayeeType` out of the CARIN BB IG namespace and
re-anchor its canonical URL in THO. Concretely:

1. Coordinate with Vocabulary/UTG to land
   [UP-315](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/UP-315) so that the payee-type
   codes are published at a THO canonical such as
   `http://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/payeetype` (final URL set by
   Vocab WG).
2. In the next CARIN BB publication, replace the IG-local
   `CodeSystem/C4BBPayeeType` resource with a stub/retired copy that
   redirects (via `replacedBy` / narrative) to the THO canonical, or
   remove it entirely once all in-IG references are updated.
3. Update every binding that currently points at
   `http://hl7.org/fhir/us/carin-bb/CodeSystem/C4BBPayeeType` (notably
   `ValueSet/C4BBPayeeType` and the `ExplanationOfBenefit.payee.type`
   bindings on the C4BB EOB profiles) to point at the new THO
   canonical.
4. Add migration guidance to the IG's change-history / "Changes since
   STU2" page covering the canonical change and the impact on
   conformance resources downstream.

#### Justification

This is what the ticket — and the broader Block-Vote-6 cohort — explicitly
asks for, and it aligns with HL7's standing terminology-governance
principle that reusable code systems live in THO rather than in IG
namespaces so that other IGs can depend on them without taking a
dependency on CARIN BB. The work group has already signalled its intent
to do exactly this (the original "Considered for Future Use" vote was
contingent on UP-315 landing); the November 2022 reversal kept the
ticket open precisely so it can be actioned when the THO publication is
ready.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the CodeSystem published from the CARIN BB IG namespace
(`http://hl7.org/fhir/us/carin-bb/CodeSystem/C4BBPayeeType`) as the
authoritative canonical, but mirror it into THO as a non-normative
copy, and update the THO mirror's metadata to point implementers back
at the IG canonical. Mark the ticket "Persuasive with Modification" —
adopt the move-to-THO intent, but only as a duplicate, not as a
relocation.

#### Justification

Re-canonicalising a CodeSystem after it has shipped in STU2 is a
breaking change for any implementer that hard-coded
`http://hl7.org/fhir/us/carin-bb/CodeSystem/C4BBPayeeType` into
configuration, validators, or terminology services. A mirror approach
preserves backwards compatibility for STU2 deployments while still
giving non-CARIN consumers a THO-anchored handle to bind against. The
trade-off is that it leaves CARIN BB as the source of truth for the
codes, which contradicts the long-term governance goal that motivated
the ticket; it is therefore a transitional, not a final, posture.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve as **Not Persuasive — Already Addressed by Process**, on the
grounds that the substantive work (creating the THO CodeSystem and
re-pointing CARIN BB bindings) is being tracked under
[UP-315](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/UP-315) in the UTG/Vocab process
and does not require an additional CARIN-side change request to
proceed. Close FHIR-35396 with a pointer to UP-315 and the next
CARIN BB publication's change log.

#### Justification

The ticket is essentially a duplicate of an already-tracked UTG work
item; once UP-315 publishes the CodeSystem in THO, the CARIN BB
publication will pick it up and the IG-local CodeSystem will be
retired in the normal course of work. Carrying FHIR-35396 as an open
change request adds bookkeeping overhead without changing the
delivered outcome. This disposition is defensible only if the work
group is confident UP-315 is funded and on a near-term THO
publication schedule; otherwise the ticket is the only forcing
function on the CARIN side.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket has already been block-voted once in favour of moving the
CodeSystem to THO (13-0-0 on 2022-07-26), and the only reason it was
reverted post-STU2 was timing — the underlying UTG ticket UP-315 had
not yet published. The terminology-governance argument for THO
anchoring is the same one the work group has accepted for every
sibling C4BB CodeSystem in Block-Vote-6, so re-affirming Disposition A
is consistent with the work group's standing position and with HL7's
broader policy on reusable code systems. Disposition B's "mirror"
posture is worth raising in discussion only if implementer feedback
surfaces a real backwards-compatibility risk for STU2 consumers; in
the absence of such feedback, accepting a clean canonical move
(coordinated with UP-315 and shipped with migration guidance in the
next publication) is the cleaner long-term outcome. Disposition C
should be rejected unless the work group has explicit assurance that
UP-315 is on a publication schedule that obviates the need for a
CARIN-side change request.
