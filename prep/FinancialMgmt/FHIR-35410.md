## Summary

The ticket asserts that the canonical defining URL for the CARIN Blue Button
ValueSet `C4BBAdjudication` should be anchored at `terminology.hl7.org` (THO)
rather than `hl7.org/fhir`. The reporter notes the broader principle that only
ValueSets bound `required` to a `code`-typed element should remain anchored at
`hl7.org/fhir`; everything else belongs in THO. The ticket also requests that a
companion UTG ticket be opened to register the ValueSet in THO. The issue was
originally bundled into Financial Management's Block-Vote-5 (2022-07-12) and
disposed as "Considered For Future Use" because it requires re-evaluating the
underlying `ExplanationOfBenefit` resource and its binding types; per Lynn
Laakso's comments the resolution was reverted and the ticket was re-opened
post-STU2 publication so that it can be reconsidered alongside other binding
work. No directly-linked Jira tickets were returned by the data layer, but the
single Zulip cross-reference (the Block-Vote-5 announcement) shows this ticket
was reviewed together with a sibling cluster of "anchor URL in THO" tickets
(e.g. FHIR-35389, FHIR-35394, FHIR-35397, FHIR-35400, FHIR-35408, FHIR-35412,
FHIR-35416, FHIR-35418–35423) all reported by Joan Harper against CARIN BB.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Move the canonical defining URL of `ValueSet/C4BBAdjudication` from the
CARIN BB IG namespace (under `hl7.org/fhir/...`) to a THO-anchored URL
(e.g. `http://terminology.hl7.org/ValueSet/C4BBAdjudication`). Concretely:
(1) open a UTG ticket to register the ValueSet — and any CARIN-defined
CodeSystem(s) it composes — in THO; (2) once published in THO, change the
ValueSet's `url` in the CARIN BB IG to the THO canonical and republish the
old IG canonical only as a deprecated alias; (3) update every binding in
CARIN BB profiles (notably `ExplanationOfBenefit` adjudication category
slices in the CARIN BB EOB profiles) to reference the new THO canonical;
(4) leave the binding strength unchanged; the request is purely about the
hosting location of the value set, not about its content or strength.

#### Justification

This matches HL7's stated terminology-governance pattern: shared value sets
that are not pinned to a `code`-typed element with a `required` binding
should live in THO so they can be reused across IGs and lifecycled
independently of any one IG's publication cadence. The ticket's own
rationale is consistent with that pattern, and the same author filed a
sibling cluster of identical asks against other CARIN BB ValueSets — moving
this one in isolation is small in scope but consistent with the larger
direction.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Do not relocate `C4BBAdjudication` in isolation. Instead, address it as part
of a single coordinated terminology-relocation work item that handles the
full Block-Vote-5 sibling cluster (FHIR-35389, -35394, -35397, -35400,
-35408, -35410, -35412, -35416, -35418, -35419, -35420, -35421, -35422,
-35423) together: (1) open one umbrella UTG submission that registers the
in-scope CARIN BB ValueSets (and supporting CodeSystems) in THO; (2)
publish those THO artifacts in a single THO release; (3) cut a CARIN BB IG
release that re-points all affected bindings in `ExplanationOfBenefit`
profiles in one pass, with deprecated-alias canonicals retained; (4) close
each individual Jira ticket against that umbrella resolution. Bindings
themselves and the underlying EOB profile design are not changed in this
scope.

#### Justification

The 2022-11-28 comment on this ticket explicitly states that the FM WG
re-opened the issue because *"some re-evaluation of the model of the
underlying resource (ExplanationOfBenefit) and the associated binding types
is necessary"*, and noted a longer-term effort in this area. Splitting the
URL-relocation work from any binding/model rework — but bundling all the
sibling tickets into one terminology release — gets the cleanly-scoped
governance benefit (THO hosting, reuse, alias canonicals) without waiting on
the EOB rework, and avoids the churn of re-pointing canonicals one ValueSet
at a time across multiple IG publications. It also avoids inconsistent
intermediate states where some C4BB* ValueSets are in THO and others remain
under `hl7.org/fhir`.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as "Considered For Future Use" (restoring the original
2022-07-12 block-vote disposition) and mark it explicitly dependent on the
broader Financial Management EOB / binding-type re-evaluation work. No
change to `C4BBAdjudication`'s defining URL is made in the next CARIN BB
release; the ValueSet continues to be published from the IG canonical, and
the THO submission is held until the EOB binding rework is scoped.

#### Justification

This is the position the FM WG actually voted in 2022-07-12 (7-0-2) and the
rationale captured in Lynn Laakso's reverted-resolution comment is still
valid: the underlying EOB resource and its binding types need re-evaluation
before relocating the value set is meaningful, and moving canonicals
prematurely creates churn for implementers who have already wired in the
current canonical. The "Considered For Future Use" framing keeps the
request on the backlog without committing to a partial fix.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The reporter's underlying request — host shared CARIN BB value sets in THO
rather than under `hl7.org/fhir` — is consistent with HL7 terminology
governance and is repeated almost verbatim across more than a dozen sibling
tickets in the same Block-Vote-5 cluster. Disposition A (act on this single
ticket) yields the right end-state for `C4BBAdjudication` but creates an
inconsistent intermediate state across the C4BB ValueSet family and forces
the IG to ship multiple canonical-relocation passes. Disposition C
(decline / defer) reasserts the original 2022 vote but does nothing for the
re-opened ticket and leaves the same governance gap in place. Disposition B
captures the value-add of the request, respects the FM WG's concern about
not entangling the URL move with EOB binding redesign, and groups the work
into one coordinated UTG submission and one IG re-pointing — which is the
lowest-cost path for both maintainers and downstream implementers. The work
group should use this ticket to ratify the umbrella approach and then apply
the same resolution across the sibling tickets.
