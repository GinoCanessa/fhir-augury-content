## Summary

The ticket asks the Da Vinci PCT IG to move the canonical (defining) URL for
`CodeSystem-PCTAdjudicationCategoryType` off the IG's own `hl7.org/fhir`
namespace and anchor it instead under `terminology.hl7.org` (THO), and to
create a corresponding UTG ticket to register the CodeSystem in THO so that it
becomes a shared terminology asset rather than an IG-local one. The change has
been deferred twice: in 2022-10 and again in 2025-07 PCT voted "Considered for
Future Use" on the grounds that the CodeSystem needs further definition and
cross-IG harmonization before being committed to THO. Lynn Laakso has now
re-opened the ticket post-STU2 publication (2025-10-08), so it is back on the
Triaged queue for FM/PCT to redispose. The cross-referenced Zulip thread (the
PCT Block-2025-Jul block vote) is not a separate request but the disposition
container for this ticket and ~22 sibling tickets, including the closely
related "anchor in THO" requests FHIR-35529 / 35532 / 35533 / 35534 / 35535 /
35536 / 35540 / 35545 / 35548 / 35549 / 35550 (all from Joan Harper / Ron
Parker, all on PCT CodeSystems and ValueSets).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Persuasive — move `PCTAdjudicationCategoryType` out of the PCT IG's own
canonical namespace and re-anchor it under THO. Concretely:

1. File a UTG (Unified Terminology Governance) Jira ticket against the
   `Vocabulary` work group to add `PCTAdjudicationCategoryType` (with its
   current concept set: `submitted`, `coinsurance`, `eligible`,
   `benefit`, `copay`, `deductible`, `noncovered`, etc., as currently
   defined in PCT STU2) as a CodeSystem under
   `http://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/adjudication-category-type` (or
   the name UTG agrees on after de-duplication review).
2. In the next PCT release, replace the IG-local CodeSystem definition
   with a stub/redirect that references the THO canonical, and update
   every binding in PCT profiles (notably the AEOB / EOB
   `adjudication.category` slices and the `PCTAdjudicationCategory`
   ValueSet) to compose from the new THO CodeSystem URL.
3. Coordinate the cut-over with Da Vinci PDex / CARIN BB / any other IG
   reusing the same concepts, and document the URL change in PCT's
   migration / change-log section.

#### Justification

This is the FHIR-wide pattern for terminology that is reusable beyond a
single IG: CodeSystems that classify domain concepts (here, the kind of
adjudication amount being reported) belong in THO so that other financial
IGs (PDex, CARIN BB, Davinci PAS) and downstream profiles can bind to a
single canonical without taking a hard dependency on the PCT package
version. The reporter's ask, the FHIR core terminology guidance, and the
group's own July-2025 vote rationale ("CodeSystem should eventually be
moved over to THO") all converge on this outcome — re-opening post-STU2
is the natural moment to do the work because consumers will expect URL
moves at the major-version boundary, not mid-cycle.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Persuasive with Modification — accept the principle of moving to THO,
but stage it via a deprecation window rather than an immediate cut-over.
Specifically:

1. Keep the existing PCT-anchored canonical
   (`http://hl7.org/fhir/us/davinci-pct/CodeSystem/PCTAdjudicationCategoryType`)
   in place for the next PCT release and mark it `status = retired` /
   `experimental = false` with a `replacedBy` extension pointing at the
   new THO canonical.
2. File the UTG ticket as in Disposition A and publish the new THO
   CodeSystem in parallel. Update PCT bindings to compose **both** URLs
   (THO authoritative, PCT URL retained for one cycle).
3. Schedule removal of the PCT-anchored copy for the cycle after THO
   adoption is in place across the affected Da Vinci IGs (PDex, CARIN
   BB, etc.), so implementers do not see a hard URL break between two
   consecutive PCT releases.

#### Justification

The group's repeated deferral (2022, 2025) has not been about disagreeing
with the destination — it's been about cross-IG harmonization risk and
implementer disruption. A deprecation-with-alias approach addresses
precisely that concern: it lets PCT publish the THO move now (clearing the
ballot comment) while giving sibling IGs and existing implementers a known
window in which both URLs resolve. It also respects the FHIR rule that
canonical URLs are immutable once published — by retiring rather than
silently re-pointing, PCT preserves traceability for existing AEOB
artifacts in the wild.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Considered for Future Use — keep the existing
`http://hl7.org/fhir/us/davinci-pct/CodeSystem/PCTAdjudicationCategoryType`
canonical in PCT and defer the THO move to a coordinated, multi-IG
terminology effort owned by Vocabulary / Financial Mgmt jointly, tracked
under a new umbrella ticket rather than against this single CodeSystem.
Resolution text: "Resolve across IGs continuing to add to THO" — i.e.,
the same disposition the group has voted twice before.

#### Justification

The 2022 and 2025 votes both flagged that the concept set in
`PCTAdjudicationCategoryType` overlaps with adjudication category
vocabulary used (and sometimes extended) by other financial IGs, and that
moving it to THO unilaterally risks locking in a definition before the
cross-IG reconciliation is done. Until that reconciliation happens, a
THO-anchored CodeSystem could end up needing breaking changes shortly
after publication, which is worse for implementers than leaving it in PCT.
Declining preserves the IG-local canonical that is already in production
use and avoids creating a THO artifact that other IGs would feel pressured
to bind to before the content is settled.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The substance of the request is uncontroversial — every prior vote agrees
the CodeSystem should ultimately live in THO — and Lynn Laakso's repeated
re-opens after each publication signal that the group cannot keep parking
the ticket without losing credibility on the "we'll do it later" rationale.
At the same time, the harmonization concern that drove the prior "Considered
for Future Use" votes is real: the vocabulary overlaps with PDex / CARIN BB
adjudication concepts, and FHIR canonical URLs are immutable, so a botched
move is hard to take back. Disposition B threads that needle: it commits
PCT to publishing the THO canonical (closing the ticket meaningfully), but
uses a `retired` + `replacedBy` deprecation window on the PCT-anchored
copy so existing AEOB implementations don't break and sibling IGs have a
known alignment target. Disposition A is acceptable if FM and Vocabulary
can confirm the concept set is stable enough today; Disposition C is the
weakest option because the same vote has been overturned twice already and
will likely be overturned a third time post-STU3 if nothing changes.
