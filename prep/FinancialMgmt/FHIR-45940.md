## Summary

The ticket targets a conformance sentence in the US Da Vinci Patient Cost
Transparency (PCT) IG that currently reads: *"The receiver SHOULD be capable
of displaying must support data elements for human use."* The reporter
(Celine Lefebvre) requests strengthening the conformance verb from **SHOULD**
to **SHALL**, making display of MustSupport data elements a hard receiver
requirement rather than a recommendation.

The ticket was originally batched into Da Vinci PCT **Block-Vote-1** for the
2024-12-10 Financial Management vote and resolved as *Considered for Future
Use* (vote 8-0-3, Spears/Scrimshire), with the proposed path being to revisit
this through **FHIR Obligations** in a future version. Per Lynn Laakso's
2025-10-08 comment, that resolution was reverted and the ticket re-opened
post-STU2 publication, returning it to Triaged for a fresh disposition.

No directly-linked or cross-referenced sibling Jira tickets were found
(beyond the block-vote announcement). The only cross-references are the
Zulip block-vote announcement and the 2024-11-20 PCT meeting minutes
(referenced from the corey_spears comment).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Change the offending PCT IG conformance sentence from:

> "The receiver SHOULD be capable of displaying must support data elements
> for human use."

to:

> "The receiver SHALL be capable of displaying must support data elements
> for human use."

The change is a narrative-only edit to the receiver-side conformance
section of the PCT IG (the FHIR-us-davinci-pct authoring source under
`input/pagecontent/`). No StructureDefinition, value set, CapabilityStatement,
or example artifact would change.

#### Justification

Strengthening to SHALL aligns receiver behavior with the FHIR base
definition of MustSupport for the consumer side: producers must populate
these elements when known, so requiring receivers to be capable of
surfacing them closes the loop and removes ambiguity for implementers and
testers. It is also the change the original reporter explicitly asked for,
and it gives certification programs a single, unambiguous bar to test
against.

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach (Scoped SHALL via Obligations / Role-Qualified Conformance)

#### Proposal

Do not promote the blanket sentence to SHALL. Instead, replace the single
sentence with role- and purpose-qualified language and (post-Obligations
adoption) attach explicit
[FHIR Obligations](http://hl7.org/fhir/extensions/StructureDefinition-obligation.html)
to the relevant elements/profiles in the PCT IG. Concretely:

1. Rewrite the narrative to say something like:
   *"Receivers acting in a role that surfaces information to a human (e.g.,
   patient-facing display of a GFE or AEOB) SHALL be capable of displaying
   must-support data elements intended for human consumption. Receivers in
   purely machine-to-machine roles (e.g., a Coordination Platform routing
   bundles, a system that aggregates or processes MS elements without
   display) are not required to display every MS element."*
2. As Obligations tooling matures, tag the human-display expectation with
   `will-display-ui` (or equivalent) obligations on the specific elements
   for which display is genuinely required, so the requirement becomes
   machine-checkable rather than narrative-only.

#### Justification

This approach honors the underlying intent of the ticket — receivers should
demonstrably handle MS data — without overreaching into MS elements that
were never meant for human consumption (search parameters, subscription
criteria, identifiers on infrastructural resources like Task,
DocumentReference, Bundle). It directly addresses Corey Spears'
2024-11-13 objection that a blanket SHALL is hard to justify across all
receiver roles, and it lines up with the originally-resolved path of using
Obligations once they are usable. Trade-off vs. A: it is more text to
maintain and partly defers the strongest enforcement to a future
Obligations pass, but it avoids creating known-impossible-to-meet
conformance for receivers in non-display roles.

### Disposition C: Decline (Restore Considered for Future Use)

#### Proposal

Decline the change for STU2.x and re-instate the original 2024-12-10
disposition: **Considered for Future Use** via FHIR Obligations, with no
narrative edit in this cycle. Add a brief note to the PCT IG (or the
ticket) recording that the SHOULD wording is intentional pending an
Obligations-based mechanism.

#### Justification

The block-vote disposition (8-0-3 in favor of "Considered for Future Use")
was reached with the reporter designee (Emma) explicitly accepting the
deferral on 2024-11-20. The technical objection — that SHALL is
indefensible across all receiver roles — has not been answered, and no
new evidence has been added since the ticket was re-opened on 2025-10-08
(only a procedural re-open by Lynn Laakso post-STU2 publication). Forcing
a SHALL now would create receiver conformance that machine-only
implementations cannot meet, exposing PCT to inspection findings that the
WG already judged unjustified.

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition: B (Alternative Approach — scoped SHALL with
Obligations).**

Disposition B is the best balance of the reporter's intent and the
substantive objection raised in the original 2024-11 discussion. The
reporter wants receivers to be reliably capable of displaying MS data;
Corey Spears' objection — uncontested in the thread and accepted by the
reporter's designee — is that the *blanket* form of SHALL captures
receivers (Coordination Platforms, aggregation pipelines, infrastructural
endpoints) for whom display is not the use case. Splitting the
conformance by receiver role gives the WG a defensible SHALL today,
while staging in Obligations gives a clean path to a machine-checkable,
element-level expression of the requirement as the Obligations
infrastructure stabilizes. Disposition A is too broad given the existing
on-record technical objection, and Disposition C does not respond to
Lynn's deliberate post-STU2 re-open, which signals the WG wants this
revisited rather than parked again.
