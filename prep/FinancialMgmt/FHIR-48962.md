## Summary

The submitter asks FHIR Core to add a first-class, uniform way to model
**patient payment plans** — the scheduled (automatic or manual) collection of
payments that healthcare providers (or third parties acting on their behalf)
arrange with patients/guarantors in pre- and post-visit contexts. At minimum
the ticket proposes capturing plan identifier, status, type, references to
account/invoice and guarantor/patient, creation and cancellation dates, total
invoice amount, first installment date, installment amount, and installment
frequency, with the ability to link out to the individual transactions that
satisfy the plan.

The ticket currently has resolution **Persuasive with Modification**. In a
Joint PA/FM discussion at the September 2025 WGM the groups concluded that
existing resources are sufficient and that the fix should be narrative
guidance in the Financial Module rather than a new structure: model the plan
itself as a **Contract**, record payments against it with
**PaymentReconciliation**, and use **Claim** to draw against the plan with
the plan manager acting as the "insurer" and `Contract.identifier` acting as
the Plan identifier. No cross-referenced Jira tickets were surfaced; the only
cross-reference is the originating Zulip topic `#financial > Patient
prepayment plans`, which is the source of the community use cases informing
this ticket.

## Details

**Description:**

> It is very common practice for patients to set up payment plans in a
> pre-visit and post-visit context. These payment plans essentially denote
> how a payment will be automatically (or manually) taken on a defined
> schedule.
>
> There should be a defined way that allows you to model the details of
> these payment plans and even link out to transactions. At minimum, I think
> the below fields would be valuable for a payment plan level:
>
> - identifier
> - status
> - type
> - reference to account / invoice
> - reference to guarantor / patient
> - created on date
> - canceled on date
> - total invoice amount
> - first installment date
> - installment amount
> - frequency of installment

**Resolution (Persuasive with Modification, drafted in Joint PA/FM at Sept
2025 WGM; FM may iterate before finalizing):**

> We think payment plans, and payments against them can be done with
> existing resources. A narrative section explaining which resources to use
> will be added to https://build.fhir.org/financial-module.html.
>
> Recommend creating instance of a plan using Contract and use payment
> reconciliation to record payments against the contract. Use Claim to claim
> against the plan where the plan manager "you" are the insurer and the
> `contract.identifier` is the Plan.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Introduce explicit payment-plan modelling in FHIR Core. The cleanest shape
is a **new `PaymentPlan` resource** under the Financial Module (maturity
0/draft) containing the fields the ticket lists:

- `identifier` (0..*)
- `status` (1..1, code; draft | active | suspended | completed | cancelled | entered-in-error)
- `type` (0..1, CodeableConcept; e.g. pre-visit, post-visit, third-party-administered, provider-administered)
- `subject` (1..1, Reference(Patient | RelatedPerson | Group)) — the guarantor
- `account` (0..*, Reference(Account))
- `invoice` (0..*, Reference(Invoice))
- `administrator` (0..1, Reference(Organization | PractitionerRole)) — the plan manager / third-party administrator
- `created` (1..1, dateTime)
- `cancelled` (0..1, dateTime)
- `totalAmount` (1..1, Money)
- `firstInstallmentDate` (0..1, date)
- `installmentAmount` (0..1, Money)
- `installmentFrequency` (0..1, Timing or code from a frequency value set)
- `payment` (0..*, Reference(PaymentReconciliation)) — realized payments against the plan
- `claim` (0..*, Reference(Claim)) — draws against the plan

As a near-term bridge, ship concrete examples and narrative in
`financial-module.html` showing how `PaymentPlan` integrates with
`PaymentReconciliation` (for received installments) and `Claim` (for draws
against the plan).

#### Justification

The Zulip record shows three independent implementers (Steve Atwood, Pearl
Lim, Maico Bernal) hitting the same gap over a 12-month window and all
falling back to custom, non-standard structures — exactly the signal FHIR
uses to justify a new resource. The ticket itself is filed at **Priority:
Highest**. The originating comment from JIRAUSER29021 explicitly calls out
that **Contract does not carry installment amount, frequency, or associated
invoices in a uniform manner**, which is the core reason existing resources
do not discharge the requirement. A dedicated resource gives implementers a
single, queryable, maturity-tracked shape for patient-administered and
third-party-administered plans alike, while still composing with
`PaymentReconciliation` and `Claim` as the resolution narrative recommends.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Ship the resolution as currently drafted but **operationalize it** so the
requested data actually has a home:

1. Add the narrative section to `financial-module.html` describing
   Contract + PaymentReconciliation + Claim as the standard pattern for
   patient payment plans, with a complete worked example (Contract instance
   + series of PaymentReconciliation + a Claim drawing on the plan).
2. Publish an **HL7-maintained Patient Payment Plan profile on Contract**
   (`Contract-paymentplan`) and a matching set of **standard extensions**
   on Contract to carry the structured plan details that base Contract does
   not: `payment-plan-installment-amount` (Money),
   `payment-plan-installment-frequency` (Timing/code),
   `payment-plan-first-installment-date` (date),
   `payment-plan-total-amount` (Money),
   `payment-plan-administrator` (Reference(Organization|PractitionerRole)),
   `payment-plan-linked-invoice` (Reference(Invoice|Account)).
3. Cross-link the profile from `PaymentReconciliation` and `Claim` guidance
   so implementers see the full pattern.
4. Add a tracker item to re-evaluate promoting this to a dedicated
   `PaymentPlan` resource after one ballot cycle if implementer uptake
   demonstrates the need.

#### Justification

This respects the Joint PA/FM recommendation (no new resource in this
cycle) while closing the specific gap JIRAUSER29021 raised — Contract
today lacks the uniform installment fields. A profile + standard extensions
pattern is FHIR's normal way to add domain structure without inflating core
resources and without the ballot overhead of a new resource. It is also
faster to deliver than Disposition A and backward-compatible with existing
Contract consumers. The trade-off versus Disposition A is that implementers
still reach for extensions to express the most important plan metadata,
which is less discoverable and less strongly typed than a first-class
resource.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket with a disposition that the Financial Module's existing
resources (Contract, PaymentReconciliation, Claim, Account, Invoice) are
sufficient, add *only* the narrative section to
`financial-module.html` exactly as drafted in the Sept 2025 WGM resolution,
and do **not** add a new resource, profile, or extensions. Implementers
needing additional structure are directed to use implementation-guide-level
extensions.

#### Justification

The Joint PA/FM WGM drafted the "Persuasive with Modification" resolution
on this basis, and FHIR generally avoids adding resources when an existing
pattern composes to cover the use case. Declining further scope keeps the
Financial Module's resource surface small and avoids pre-committing to a
model while real implementations still diverge (Steve Atwood's team,
Maico's team, and Pearl Lim's team are each solving it differently). The
risk is that the decline does not actually address the gap
JIRAUSER29021 raised — Contract lacks the structured installment fields —
and implementers will continue to ship custom non-standard structures,
which is the interoperability failure mode the ticket was filed to fix.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **B**

Disposition B is the best balance for the state the ticket is actually in.
The Joint PA/FM WGM has already agreed that a dedicated resource is not
warranted in this cycle, so Disposition A is unlikely to pass FM as-is
without the iteration FM themselves flagged. But Disposition C as currently
drafted is thin: adding narrative to `financial-module.html` without
publishing a profile and extensions leaves
JIRAUSER29021's objection unanswered — Contract genuinely does not carry
installment amount, frequency, or per-installment invoice links uniformly,
and three independent implementers are already on custom structures because
of it. A published `Contract-paymentplan` profile plus a small, named set of
HL7-maintained extensions gives the community a single canonical shape,
keeps the resolution's Contract/PaymentReconciliation/Claim pattern intact,
and produces something implementers can point at in the next ballot cycle.
It also keeps the door open to promote to a `PaymentPlan` resource later if
adoption data justifies it, which is the lowest-regret path between the
stronger (A) and weaker (C) options.
