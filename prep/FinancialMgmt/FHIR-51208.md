## Summary

The reporter raises two related concerns about the **CARIN IG for Blue Button®** EOB profiles, focused on the `noncovered` adjudication concept.

1. **Definition ambiguity.** The IG defines `noncovered` as *"the portion of the cost of this service that was deemed not eligible by the insurer because the service or member was not covered by the subscriber contract."* In real-world adjudication, however, the financial responsibility for a noncovered amount can fall on either the **provider** (e.g., when the denial reason is provider write-off / non-billable to patient) or the **member** (e.g., when the denial is a benefit-coverage exclusion). The current single-sentence definition does not reflect this branching and the reporter asks for an elaborated definition.

2. **Arithmetic inconsistency in the Notes on Fields.** On `ExplanationOfBenefit-Inpatient-Institutional`, the `total.amount` guidance states:
   - *Eligible amount = submitted − noncovered − discount*
   - *Member liability = deductible + coinsurance + copay + noncovered*
   - *Paid-to-provider / paid-to-patient = eligible − member liability*

   Substituting the first into the third yields `paid = submitted − noncovered − discount − (deductible + coinsurance + copay + noncovered)`, i.e. **noncovered is subtracted twice**. The reporter asks the WG to rectify the formula.

The ticket is currently **Triaged** (Medium). January 2026 comments from co-chair Corey Spears note the issue was discussed at the **January Virtual HL7 WGM (Wednesday Q3, FM)** and that the WG will refine the definition as the codes are migrated to THO and the IG is balloted as **STU 3**; Corey is also following up with adjudication SMEs (in/out-of-network framing, treatment of the difference between submitted and eligible/allowed).

The keyword-similarity neighbourhood (FHIR-51541, FHIR-51619, FHIR-34921, FHIR-26721, FHIR-26992, FHIR-30502, FHIR-32337) shows that the *same prose block* and the underlying adjudication-amount value set have been the subject of several prior tickets across **CARIN Blue Button**, **Da Vinci PCT (AEOB)** and **FHIR Core**, so any fix here should be aligned with the AEOB equivalents already published.

>
> That means noncovered amount was subtracted twice here to get the paidtoprovider/paidtopatient amount.
>
> Could you please rectify the calculation issue here?

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Issue a **Technical Correction** against the **CARIN Blue Button** IG that:

1. **Fixes the `total.amount` formula** in the *Notes on Fields* of `ExplanationOfBenefit-Inpatient-Institutional` (and the parallel C4BB Outpatient-Institutional, Professional/Non-Clinician, and Pharmacy profiles where the same prose appears) so that `noncovered` is **not double-counted**. Concretely, replace the bullet with:

   > `total.amount`:
   > - **Eligible amount = submitted amount − noncovered − discount** (`SUBMITTED = NONCOVERED + ELIGIBLE + DISCOUNT`).
   > - **Member liability = deductible + coinsurance + copay** *(plus any portion of `noncovered` that the denial code assigns to the member; do **not** add `noncovered` unconditionally)*.
   > - **Paid to provider / paid to patient = eligible − member liability − priorpayerpaid − paidbypatient** (jurisdiction-dependent).
   > - Note: a portion of the member liability may have already been paid to the provider as `paidbypatient`.

2. **Elaborates the `noncovered` code definition** in the C4BB adjudication value set (`AdjudicationCarinBBValueCodes`, FHIR-26992) — and mirrors it in the FHIR Core `adjudication` code system (FHIR-30502) — to clarify that:

   > *Noncovered* is the portion of the **submitted** amount that the insurer does **not include in the eligible amount**, either because the service/member is not covered under the contract, the service exceeds plan limits, or the charge is in excess of the contractually allowed amount. **Whether the noncovered portion is the financial responsibility of the member or the provider depends on the denial reason code** (e.g., contract exclusion vs. provider write-off / non-billable-to-patient). This element is calculated, not authoritative; the only authoritative payer obligation is `benefit`.

3. Aligns the published **PCT AEOB** "if applicable" qualifier (already present per FHIR-51619) with the C4BB wording.

#### Justification

The double-subtraction is a verifiable arithmetic error in the IG prose, independently surfaced by at least three Zulip threads spanning 2021–2024 (*Carin EOB Processing Field*, *C4BBEOB Inpatient 'adjdctn_amt'*, *Adjudication Calculations*) and by the related published ticket **FHIR-51541**. Paul Knapp's authoritative statement on Zulip — `SUBMITTED = NONCOVERED + ELIGIBLE` — directly contradicts the existing bullet, which adds `noncovered` to member liability. Aligning the C4BB prose with that identity is consistent with FM's stated model and with the qualifier already adopted on the PCT AEOB Summary profile (FHIR-51619). Clarifying `noncovered` to acknowledge the denial-reason branching directly answers the reporter's first concern and matches Corey's January-WGM follow-up plan.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Treat this as a **Persuasive (with Modification)** request and address it as part of the previously-announced **STU 3 / THO migration** of the CARIN BB adjudication terminology (per Corey Spears' January 2026 comments and FHIR-26992):

1. Defer the wording change to the STU 3 ballot cycle and bundle it with the THO move so the new definition lives on the canonical code system rather than as IG prose alone.
2. In the interim, publish a short **errata / known-issue** note on the C4BB IG page for `ExplanationOfBenefit-Inpatient-Institutional` stating that the `total.amount` bullet is under revision, that implementers should treat `noncovered` as satisfying `SUBMITTED = NONCOVERED + ELIGIBLE + DISCOUNT`, and that the inclusion of `noncovered` in `memberliability` is conditional on the denial reason.
3. During STU 3, replace the prose-only Notes on Fields with **invariants/guidance keyed off the adjudication slice** (e.g., a `total` slice pattern that names `submitted`, `eligible`, `noncovered`, `discount`, `memberliability`, `benefit`, `paidtoprovider`, `paidtopatient`) and document the relationships in a single canonical place, reusing the slice patterns established by FHIR-34921 on the AEOB.

#### Justification

The fix touches a value set that is already in motion (FHIR-26992 → THO) and a Notes-on-Fields block that has been amended in adjacent IGs (FHIR-51541, FHIR-51619). Making the CARIN-BB change in lockstep with the STU 3 ballot avoids two near-term breaking changes, gives implementers a single migration target, and lets the FM SMEs Corey is consulting feed in. The interim errata mitigates the double-count harm without forcing a new patch release. The downside vs. Disposition A is that implementers continue to consume the broken prose until STU 3 publishes; the errata is intended to bridge that.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request on the grounds that:

- The Notes-on-Fields paragraph is **non-normative implementation guidance**; nothing in the StructureDefinition constrains the arithmetic, so the IG is not technically incorrect.
- The CARIN-BB adjudication categories are scheduled to migrate to THO and be re-balloted as STU 3 (FHIR-26992, Corey's January-WGM comments); maintaining the current STU 2 prose is acceptable.
- Any clarification of `noncovered`'s allocation between member and provider is properly the job of the **denial / adjudication reason codes**, not the amount-category code definition.

#### Justification

A pure-decline is defensible only if the WG concludes that the prose's informative status removes the harm and that re-baselining will land soon enough that interim fixes are not warranted. It would, however, leave a documented arithmetic error in a published IG that has been independently surfaced by implementers since 2021 (Zulip *Carin EOB Processing Field*) and by another resolved ticket (FHIR-51541). The reporter's first concern — definition ambiguity — is also not actually addressed by the denial-codes argument because the value-set definition is what implementers cite when interpreting the field.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition: B (Alternative Approach).**

Disposition B aligns the fix with work the FM WG has already committed to (THO migration of the C4BB adjudication codes, STU 3 ballot) and with adjacent published changes on the PCT AEOB family (FHIR-51541, FHIR-51619), so it produces the cleanest single migration target for implementers. It also directly tracks Corey Spears' January-WGM commitment in the ticket comments. The interim errata addresses the substantive harm (the double-subtraction) immediately, which is what the reporter actually needs to act on, while preserving room for the SME consultation Corey has flagged on the in-network/out-of-network framing of `noncovered`. Disposition A is the right *content* but risks a one-off STU 2 patch that will be re-touched within months; Disposition C is hard to justify given the weight of community confusion already documented on Zulip and the existence of FHIR-51541 as precedent that the WG treats this prose block as in-scope for correction.
