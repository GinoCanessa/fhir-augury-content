## Summary

The ticket asks that the canonical URL of the CARIN BB `C4BBCompoundLiteral`
CodeSystem be anchored in `terminology.hl7.org` (THO) rather than under
`hl7.org/fhir/...`, and that a corresponding UTG ticket be opened to register
the CodeSystem in THO. The request is part of a broader tranche of similarly
worded CARIN BB tickets (FHIR-35389, FHIR-35397, FHIR-35400, FHIR-35408,
FHIR-35410, FHIR-35412, FHIR-35416, FHIR-35418, FHIR-35419, FHIR-35420,
FHIR-35421, FHIR-35422, FHIR-35423) reported by Joan Harper that all seek to
move locally-defined CodeSystems and ValueSets out of the IG's canonical URL
space and into THO. `C4BBCompoundLiteral` is a single-code CodeSystem that
the CARIN BB IG currently publishes to work around a gap in the base
ExplanationOfBenefit resource (`item.productOrService` being a required
element with no sentinel for "not applicable" prior to R5, where it becomes
optional). No Jira issue link fields were populated on this ticket, but the
Block-Vote-5 Zulip announcement on the `CARIN IG for Blue Button®` stream
groups it with its sibling tickets and records the STU2 disposition history
described in the comments.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Retire the IG-local CodeSystem `C4BBCompoundLiteral` (currently published at
`http://hl7.org/fhir/us/carin-bb/CodeSystem/C4BBCompoundLiteral` or
equivalent CARIN BB canonical root) and re-publish the same single-code
CodeSystem under THO at
`http://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/C4BBCompoundLiteral` (final slug to
be agreed with the UTG Vocabulary Facilitator). Open a corresponding UTG
ticket registering the CodeSystem in THO, update any CARIN BB ValueSets
that include it to reference the new THO URL, and retarget any profile
bindings (notably on the EoB `item.productOrService` slice(s) that use this
code) to the new URL. Retain the old canonical as an `oldCanonical` /
`replacedBy` note or keep a stub with a retirement notice per HL7
Terminology retirement guidance so existing instances continue to validate
on a transition release.

#### Justification

This is precisely what the reporter asked for and is consistent with the
broader HL7 direction — expressed across the entire Block-Vote-5 tranche
of Joan Harper tickets — that IGs should not mint canonical CodeSystems
under their own URL space when the content is reusable terminology. The
reverted STU2 resolution specifically noted the CodeSystem exists only
because of a base-spec shortcoming; keeping it housed inside CARIN BB (and
under a `hl7.org/fhir/us/carin-bb` canonical) bakes that work-around into
the IG indefinitely and makes it harder for other IGs to reuse or for
CARIN BB to drop once R5 alignment happens. Moving it to THO is the
low-risk, conventional fix.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Do **not** republish `C4BBCompoundLiteral` anywhere. Instead, in the next
substantive CARIN BB release, remove the CodeSystem and its bindings
entirely, relying on the R5-aligned cardinality change to
`ExplanationOfBenefit.item.productOrService` (minimum 0) and/or
`DataAbsentReason` to express the "not applicable / compound literal"
case. Where a positively-stated sentinel is still required for STU2
implementers, rebind to an existing standard CodeSystem (e.g., an
appropriate HL7 `NullFlavor`/`DataAbsentReason`-based pattern or an
existing THO CodeSystem that already conveys "not applicable") rather
than moving the locally-invented single-code CodeSystem to THO.

#### Justification

The prior WG vote (Corey Spears / Rachel Foerster, 7-0-2) explicitly
observed that the CodeSystem is a work-around for a base-spec shortcoming
that R5 already resolves. Promoting a single-code work-around CodeSystem
to HL7 Terminology entrenches it globally, where THO's intent is
broadly-reusable terminology, not resource-specific sentinels. Replacing
it with an existing standard absent-reason mechanism is cleaner for
consumers, avoids a net-new THO artifact, and reduces the number of
CodeSystems CARIN BB needs to maintain. Trade-off vs. Disposition A:
higher implementation burden for existing STU2 implementers (breaking
change to bindings) and a longer path to publication; mitigated by
scheduling the removal alongside a CARIN BB R5 migration.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Considered For Future Use** (or **Not Persuasive —
Considered For Future Use**), restating the original Block-Vote-5
rationale: `C4BBCompoundLiteral` is a single-code CodeSystem that exists
solely to address a shortcoming in base `ExplanationOfBenefit`, and that
shortcoming is already resolved in FHIR R5 via the relaxed cardinality on
`item.productOrService`. Defer any THO relocation and any UTG registration
until the CARIN BB R5 migration, at which point the CodeSystem is expected
to be removed rather than relocated.

#### Justification

Defensible because the cost/benefit of relocating the CodeSystem is poor:
implementers still need to switch bindings, THO gains a one-off
resource-specific sentinel whose future is to be deleted, and CARIN BB
incurs churn on artifacts that are slated to be removed in R5. The
rationale is on record from the 2022-07-12 vote. However, this ignores
the Nov 2022 reopen request from Lynn Laakso, which explicitly asked the
ticket be reconsidered post-STU2; declining again without new analysis is
likely to draw the same objection that triggered the reopen.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A is the pragmatic middle path and most directly honours the
Nov 2022 reopen. CARIN BB STU2 is already in the field using
`C4BBCompoundLiteral`; Disposition C simply re-affirms a stance that Lynn
Laakso has already rejected once, and Disposition B forces a breaking
change on existing STU2 implementers before CARIN BB has a clear R5
target. Moving the CodeSystem to THO via a UTG ticket is
low-implementation-cost, matches the pattern being applied across the
entire Block-Vote-5 tranche of reporter Joan Harper's tickets, improves
discoverability and governance of the CodeSystem, and does not foreclose
Disposition B's cleanup in a future CARIN BB R5 cycle (at which point the
THO CodeSystem can itself be retired). The Financial Management WG should
pair this disposition with a clear note in the CARIN BB publication
history that the CodeSystem's THO canonical supersedes the old
`hl7.org/fhir/us/carin-bb/...` URL, and explicitly flag removal as a
candidate for the next major release.
