## Summary

Jeff Rubitschun requests that the allowed `Reference` target types on
`PaymentReconciliation.allocation.target` be expanded so that capitation
payments can be allocated at the per-member-per-month (PMPM) level. The
current target choices (Claim, Invoice, Encounter, Contract, etc.) do not
let an implementer attribute a slice of an overall capitation check to a
specific patient and their coverage, because a capitation payment is not
tied to an individual service event. The reporter proposes adding
`Coverage` and `Patient` as additional permitted target reference types so
that each `allocation` entry can identify the member to whom that PMPM
component applies. The Financial Management work group has triaged the
change as **Persuasive with Modification**: rather than expanding
`.target`, the proposed resolution keeps `Contract` as the `.target` and
introduces a new `subject 0..1 Reference(Patient)` plus a
`characteristics` mechanism (either `Reference(Observation)` or an
inline `code`/`period` structure) on `PaymentReconciliation` (and on
`CoverageEligibilityResponse` for the Observation-style variant) to
carry the member-specific context. There are no cross-referenced Jira
tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items beyond the implicit FHIR-element
mention of `PaymentReconciliation.allocation.target`.

## Details

**Description:**

The possible options for the Reference type of the
`PaymentReconciliation.allocation.target` element does not account for
allocating capitation payments at the individual per member per month
(PMPM) level for each patient/coverage included in the overall payment
being represented by the PaymentReconciliation.

A capitation payment does not directly relate to a claim, invoice or
encounter as it is a payment provided regardless of if the patient
receives services in a month. While the Contract resource could be used
to represent the capitation contract being paid there isn't a way to
further break down capitation payments using the PaymentReconciliation
resource into the patient specific amounts included in the overall check.

To support relaying the member level of information, adding the Coverage
and Patient resources as potential target references would allow for
allocating individual PMPM capitation payment components to the
appropriate resources.

**Resolution Description (Persuasive with Modification):**

`.target` can refer to the Contract in the use case supplied, but the
following changes are required to support the remainder of the use case:

- add `subject 0..1 Reference(Patient)` — Subject of the payment
- and either
  - `characteristics 0..* Reference(Observation)`
  - and also add the characteristics to `CoverageEligibilityResponse`
- or
  - `characteristics 0..*`
    - `code 0..1 CodeableConcept` — Code for the characteristic; example characteristics value set
    - `period 0..1 Period` — Applicable time frame

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add `Patient` and `Coverage` to the list of permitted reference types on
`PaymentReconciliation.allocation.target`, so its type becomes
`Reference(Claim | Account | Invoice | ChargeItem | Encounter |
Contract | ... | Patient | Coverage)`. Update the element definition,
short description, comments, and examples to call out PMPM capitation
allocation as a supported use case, and regenerate the structure
definition / snapshot for `PaymentReconciliation`.

#### Justification

This is the minimal change that literally satisfies the reporter's ask:
each `allocation` entry can directly point at the `Patient` or
`Coverage` to which that PMPM slice applies, with no new elements
required. It is additive to the type set on a choice/reference element
and, by the FHIR R6 compatibility rules for adding allowed target types
to a `Reference()`, it does not invalidate existing instances. It also
keeps the data model flat: a single resource (`PaymentReconciliation`)
continues to carry both the cheque total and the per-member breakdown,
which mirrors how payers actually transmit capitation remittances.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach (the FM-proposed modification)

#### Proposal

Leave the `PaymentReconciliation.allocation.target` reference set
unchanged (continue to use `Contract` for capitation use cases) and
instead model the member context as first-class structure on the
allocation:

1. Add `PaymentReconciliation.allocation.subject 0..1 Reference(Patient)`
   — "Subject of the payment".
2. Add a `characteristics` structure on `PaymentReconciliation.allocation`,
   in **one** of the two shapes from the resolution:
   - **B1 (Observation-based):** `characteristics 0..* Reference(Observation)`,
     and add the same `characteristics 0..* Reference(Observation)` to
     `CoverageEligibilityResponse` for symmetry.
   - **B2 (Inline code/period):** `characteristics 0..*` BackboneElement
     with `code 0..1 CodeableConcept` (bound to an example characteristics
     value set) and `period 0..1 Period` ("Applicable time frame").

This is the disposition that aligns with the **Persuasive with
Modification** resolution already entered on the ticket.

#### Justification

The FM modification preserves the semantic meaning of `.target` as
"what is being paid against" (a `Contract`, `Claim`, `Invoice`, …) and
introduces dedicated slots for the member, time period, and any
additional characteristics of the PMPM line. That separation avoids
overloading `.target` with a `Patient`/`Coverage` reference whose
relationship to the payment is qualitatively different from the existing
target types. Adding `subject` is a small additive change and
non-breaking. The choice between B1 and B2 is the real trade-off:

- **B1 (Observation)** reuses an existing, extensible mechanism for
  arbitrary structured facts and pairs naturally with
  `CoverageEligibilityResponse` (where eligibility characteristics often
  live), but it forces consumers to traverse another resource for what
  may be very simple data (e.g., "January 2026").
- **B2 (Inline code/period)** is lightweight, keeps the most common
  capitation metadata (the covered period and a characteristic code)
  inside `PaymentReconciliation`, and avoids creating Observation
  instances solely to carry administrative facts.

Recommend wording B2 in the published resolution unless FM explicitly
wants alignment with an Observation-based eligibility model, in which
case B1 should also include the parallel change on
`CoverageEligibilityResponse`.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request as out of scope for `PaymentReconciliation` and
direct implementers to one of the existing mechanisms — specifically,
either (a) issue one `PaymentReconciliation` per member with `.target`
referring to the per-member `Contract`, or (b) carry the per-member
breakdown via an extension on `PaymentReconciliation.allocation`.

#### Justification

`PaymentReconciliation` is, in core FHIR, primarily a remittance-advice
analogue (one cheque, many adjudications). PMPM capitation allocation
is a domain-specific reporting need that some jurisdictions and payers
solve outside the resource. Keeping the core resource lean — and
pushing capitation specifics into an IG-level extension — avoids
encumbering every implementer of `PaymentReconciliation` with elements
they do not use. This option would, however, be defensible only if no
sufficiently prevalent use case is established; since FM has already
accepted the underlying need (per the "Persuasive with Modification"
resolution), Decline is the weakest option here.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B (specifically B2 — inline `code`/`period`
characteristics, plus the new `subject` element), matching the FM
"Persuasive with Modification" resolution already on the ticket.

The reporter's underlying need — attributing slices of a single
capitation cheque to individual members — is real and common enough in
US payer workflows that Decline (C) is hard to justify; FM has already
agreed it is persuasive. Between A and B, B better preserves the
semantic role of `.target` as "the obligation being paid": Contract is
the right answer for what is being paid against, while Patient and the
covered period describe to whom and for when that payment applies. A
dedicated `subject` element and a structured `characteristics` slot are
much easier for implementers to interpret than overloading
`.target` with a heterogeneous mix of obligation-like and subject-like
references. The inline B2 shape is preferred over the Observation-based
B1 because the most common per-member facts (covered period and a
small characteristic code) are simple enough that requiring a separate
`Observation` resource adds cost without proportional benefit; the
parallel change to `CoverageEligibilityResponse` (B1's main upside) can
be revisited as a follow-on if FM wants to align eligibility and
remittance modeling.
