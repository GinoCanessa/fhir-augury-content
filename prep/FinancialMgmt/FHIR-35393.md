## Summary

The CARIN IG for Blue Button (C4BB) defines a CodeSystem
`C4BBClaimProcedureType` whose canonical URL is anchored under
`hl7.org/fhir/us/carin-bb`. This ticket asserts that the CodeSystem is
not specific to CARIN BB and therefore its canonical URL should instead
be anchored under HL7 Terminology (`terminology.hl7.org`, "THO"). The
reporter (via comment 5 imported by Ron G. Parker) further requests that
a UTG ticket be created to register the CodeSystem in THO so that the
re-anchoring has a permanent home outside the IG.

The ticket was originally voted 13-0-0 on 2022-07-26 (Corey Spears /
Kathleen Conner) as "Considered for Future Use" because the work
depends on the THO-side ticket [UP-311 — Create Procedure Type Code
Systems that is not an example](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/UP-311),
which is still in `Proposal Draft`. On 2022-11-28 lynn_laakso reverted
that resolution and reopened the ticket post STU2 publication so it
will be re-considered once UP-311 lands. There are no other Jira
cross-references; the only surfaced Zulip thread is the announcement of
the original Block-Vote-6 batch in which this ticket was bundled.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Once UP-311 publishes a THO-hosted procedure-type CodeSystem, retire
`C4BBClaimProcedureType` from `hl7.org/fhir/us/carin-bb/CodeSystem/C4BBClaimProcedureType`
and re-anchor the canonical URL to the THO equivalent (e.g.,
`http://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/<thoCodeSystemId>`). Update every
ValueSet binding in CARIN BB that currently includes
`C4BBClaimProcedureType` (notably the binding on
`ExplanationOfBenefit.procedure.type` in the C4BB Inpatient/Outpatient
Institutional and Professional EOB profiles) to reference the THO
CodeSystem. Mark the old C4BB-anchored CodeSystem as deprecated /
retired with a `replacedBy` pointer to the THO canonical, and file the
companion UTG/THO PR. Coordinate with FHIR-35392, FHIR-35395, and
FHIR-35426 so the sibling C4BB code systems migrate as one batch.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter, the original Block-Vote-6 vote
(13-0-0), and the November 2022 reopen by lynn_laakso all anticipated.
HL7's terminology governance policy is that code systems not specific
to a single IG belong in THO so they can be reused across realms and
specifications. Procedure-type concepts are general clinical/financial
terminology and are not C4BB-specific. Deferring the change ad
infinitum keeps a non-IG-specific CodeSystem incorrectly nested under a
US-Realm IG canonical, which is precisely the anti-pattern UP-311 is
intended to eliminate.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than waiting on UP-311 to complete a brand-new THO CodeSystem,
publish the re-anchoring in two steps:

1. **In the next CARIN BB release**, mark
   `C4BBClaimProcedureType` as `status = retired` /
   `experimental = false` with explanatory text saying the codes are
   moving to THO, and keep the CARIN-anchored canonical functional for
   backward compatibility for one cycle.
2. **As soon as UP-311 publishes**, switch the active binding on
   `ExplanationOfBenefit.procedure.type` to the THO CodeSystem and add
   a `ConceptMap` from the legacy `C4BBClaimProcedureType` codes to
   the THO codes so existing implementer payloads remain validatable.

This avoids holding the C4BB ticket open indefinitely while UP-311
moves through the THO pipeline and gives implementers a documented
migration path rather than a flag-day swap.

#### Justification

UP-311 has been in `Proposal Draft` since April 2022 with no recent
movement; binding C4BB's resolution timeline strictly to UP-311 risks
the ticket sitting in `Triaged` indefinitely. Two-stage retirement is
the pattern HL7 has used elsewhere (e.g., for v3 → THO migrations)
because it both signals direction to implementers immediately and
avoids breaking existing instances. A `ConceptMap` is the standard
FHIR mechanism to bridge the legacy and THO code systems and is
materially less disruptive than a single hard cut-over once UP-311
finally publishes.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Not Persuasive — Out of Scope for CARIN BB**.
The C4BB-defined CodeSystem `C4BBClaimProcedureType` is the canonical
home for the procedure-type terminology used by CARIN BB, and
re-anchoring it under THO is properly the responsibility of the THO /
UTG project (UP-311). Recommend the requester pursue UP-311 to
completion; once a THO CodeSystem exists, a fresh CARIN BB ticket can
be opened to migrate the binding.

#### Justification

This is defensible only on a "separation of concerns" reading:
FHIR-35393 cannot be acted on inside the CARIN BB repository until a
THO CodeSystem actually exists. There is no benefit to keeping a CARIN
BB ticket in `Triaged` for what is effectively a THO-side dependency,
and the relevant work is already tracked under UP-311. The downside is
that closing without a tracked successor in CARIN BB risks the
re-anchoring being forgotten when UP-311 eventually publishes — so
this disposition is only acceptable if a follow-up issue is opened
against CARIN BB to apply the binding switch when UP-311 lands.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

UP-311 has shown no movement in the year-and-change since the original
13-0-0 "Considered for Future Use" vote, and lynn_laakso's November
2022 reopen explicitly signals the workgroup wants the question revived
post-STU2 rather than parked indefinitely. Disposition A is the right
end-state but, taken alone, ties FHIR-35393 to a THO ticket whose
schedule the FM workgroup does not control. Disposition C cleanly
delegates to THO but historically such "we'll reopen later" closures
get lost. Disposition B captures the intent of A — the CodeSystem
belongs in THO — while giving CARIN BB an action it can take in its
*next* publication cycle (mark retired, document migration intent,
plan a `ConceptMap`) regardless of UP-311's pace, and then completes
the swap as soon as UP-311 publishes. Because FHIR-35392, FHIR-35395,
and FHIR-35426 are structurally identical, the workgroup should adopt
the same disposition for the whole batch and execute them as one
coordinated migration.
