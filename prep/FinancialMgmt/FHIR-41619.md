## Summary

FHIR-41619 ("Appeals should be in scope") asks the Financial Management work
group to bring electronic prior-authorization (ePA) appeals into the Da Vinci
Burden Reduction family of guides — specifically the US Da Vinci Prior
Authorization Support (PAS) IG, where appeals are currently declared
out-of-scope. The reporter argues that without an automated appeals path,
payers may issue fast initial denials and rely on a manual, opaque appeal
process to defer the substantive review work — undercutting the burden-
reduction goals of PAS. The submitter cites NCPDP ePA (which already supports
drug-PA appeals) as precedent for medical-services PA, and points to the CMS
NPRM (and CMS-0057-F follow-on) requirement to report appeal metrics as a
strong industry signal that an automated appeals workflow is needed. The
ticket has been resolved as "Considered for Future Use" three times
(2023-08-29 by 9-0-0, 2024-07-30 by 7-0-6, 2025-10-21 by 10-0-0) and re-opened
each time after publication of STU2, STU2.1, and STU2.2; it now carries the
`Block-vote-22` label for the next disposition cycle. The closely related
ticket [FHIR-48816](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48816) ("Create a FHIR
to FHIR only IG") is referenced in the comments by Jean Duteau as the more
appropriate venue for an appeals workflow, since PAS itself is a 278-bridging
guide and appeals are not expressible through the X12 transactions PAS wraps.

## Details

**Description:**

Appeals are currently out of scope for the Burden Reduction guides. However it
would only strengthen the guides to have an automated appeals capability
included.

Given the current status quo, not to mention the increasing dependence on AI
generated algorithms, we are concerned that payers may issue initial denials
and defer the substantive work necessary for justifying a prior-authorization
request to the very manual appeals process. This would derail all the great
efforts that are being done here at Da Vinci.

Not to mention, NCPDP ePA includes ability to do appeals. Given that drug ePA
has this capability medical services PA should as well.

Reading the tea leaves, there could be a future emphasis on the need to
streamline the appeals process. For example, the CMS NPRM includes appeals in
the metrics reporting requirement. It would therefore be in the industry's
best interest to begin working on including this pretty critical piece of the
puzzle to the overall prior auth workflow.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add an in-scope, normative-track appeals workflow to the next major version
of the US Da Vinci PAS IG. Concretely:

- Extend the PAS Claim and ClaimResponse profiles with an explicit "appeal"
  use-case binding: re-use `Claim.related.relationship` (or add a PAS-specific
  extension) to mark a Claim as an appeal of a previously denied PAS Claim,
  carrying the original `ClaimResponse.identifier`.
- Define ClaimResponse outcomes for appeal phases (e.g., appeal-received,
  appeal-upheld, appeal-overturned, appeal-partially-overturned) via a new
  PAS appeal-status CodeSystem and ValueSet.
- Add operations / capability-statement entries describing how a provider
  submits an appeal Claim and polls (or subscribes for) the appeal
  ClaimResponse, mirroring the existing PAS submit/inquire pattern.
- Author a new IG narrative page ("Appeals workflow") describing the
  end-to-end interaction, including the metrics-reporting tie-in to
  CMS-0057-F's "percentage approved after appeal" metric.

#### Justification

This is what the ticket literally asks for and what the disagreeing
commenter (JIRAUSER16610) is pushing for. Drug ePA via NCPDP already
supports appeals, so there is precedent that medical-services PA should
match. CMS-0057-F mandates appeal-outcome metrics, so implementers will
need a structured FHIR representation of appeals regardless; centralising
that in PAS keeps providers from having to learn a second IG to close the
appeal loop. The 2024-07-30 vote (7-0-6) and the strong abstention count
suggest the WG is not opposed in principle, only on timing.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep PAS focused on its current 278-bridging mission, and address the appeals
need by chartering / advancing a separate **FHIR-to-FHIR Prior Authorization
IG** (the same vehicle proposed in
[FHIR-48816](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-48816)) that includes appeals
and decision letters as first-class workflows. Concretely, in PAS STU2.x:

- Add a short narrative section to the PAS IG explicitly stating that
  appeals are out of scope **for the X12-bridging PAS profile** and
  pointing implementers at the new FHIR-to-FHIR PA IG (when published) for
  appeal submission and tracking.
- Resolve FHIR-41619 as "Considered for Future Use — tracked in
  FHIR-48816" with a hard link to the new IG project once the project
  scope statement is approved by Financial Mgmt.
- In the new FHIR-to-FHIR PA IG, model appeals using PAS-aligned profile
  shapes (Claim/ClaimResponse with appeal-status codes as in Disposition A)
  but free of the X12 278 mapping constraints.

#### Justification

This is exactly the alignment Jean Duteau articulated on 2025-09-26 and
2026-03-26: "this guide is intended to facilitate going from practitioners
who will have FHIR data to payers who are accepting X12 transactions
today" — and X12 278 has no native appeal transaction, so jamming appeals
into PAS would create a FHIR-only sub-workflow inside an otherwise
bridge-shaped IG, confusing implementers. Splitting the work avoids
breaking the PAS↔X12 round-trip guarantee, lets the appeals workflow be
designed without 278 constraints (decision letters, attachments, narrative
rationale), and keeps the STU 2.x publication cadence on schedule.
Trade-off vs. Disposition A: implementers have to track two IGs, and the
new IG must be chartered, which is non-trivial WG work.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve FHIR-41619 once more as **Considered for Future Use** (or "Not
Persuasive") and remove the `Block-vote-22` label, on the basis that
appeals support requires a fundamentally different workflow than PAS
currently offers and is already represented by FHIR-48816, with no new
information in the four re-open cycles to justify a different outcome.

#### Justification

The ticket has now been disposed three times by clear majorities (9-0-0,
7-0-6, 10-0-0), each time as "Considered for Future Use". The substantive
WG position has been consistent: PAS bridges to X12 278, X12 278 has no
appeal transaction, and a robust appeal workflow needs decision letters,
attachments, and metrics that go beyond what the current PAS profiles
express. Industry adoption of PAS is still ramping; expanding scope before
the existing bridge is widely deployed risks delaying STU normative
maturity and forcing breaking changes when the appeals model is fleshed
out. The reporter's underlying need is acknowledged and is being tracked
under FHIR-48816, so declining FHIR-41619 specifically does not lose the
requirement.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the cleanest reconciliation of the WG's repeated voting
record (which has consistently said "yes, but not in PAS") and the
reporter / dissenting-commenter pressure (which has consistently said
"appeals are too important to keep deferring"). It does not require a
breaking expansion of PAS mid-lifecycle, it preserves the PAS↔X12
bridging contract that current implementers depend on, and it gives the
appeals workflow — which is genuinely substantial design work, including
decision letters and metrics tied to CMS-0057-F — a fit-for-purpose home
in a FHIR-to-FHIR IG. Because FHIR-48816 is already on the table for the
same WG and ballot block, the procedural cost is low: dispose FHIR-41619
in coordination with FHIR-48816, and explicitly carry "appeals" as a
named requirement of the new IG. Disposition A respects the reporter's
literal request but ignores the structural mismatch with X12 278;
Disposition C respects the precedent but, after four re-opens, no longer
gives the reporter a credible path forward.
