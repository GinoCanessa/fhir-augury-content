## Summary

The reporter argues that newly-introduced "self-pay" information should be
modeled on the **Account** resource rather than on **Coverage** in FHIR
Core. Three concerns are raised: (1) self-pay does not fit the granularity
of Coverage because patients without insurance are not normally described
as having a "self-pay coverage"; (2) self-pay decisions occur at the
*visit* level — even patients with insurance frequently self-pay for
specific uncovered services, which Account/Encounter can naturally
represent and Coverage cannot; and (3) putting self-pay on Coverage
introduces ambiguity about *when* a self-pay Coverage instance should be
sent (ever self-paid? no other coverage on file?) that risks real-world
billing errors when source-system data is incomplete.

Although the system reports no direct cross-references for this ticket,
the closest prior related work is
[FHIR-45855](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-45855) ("Create a
self-pay/uninsured Coverage profile and make Coverage required for the
information bundle"), resolved Persuasive-with-Modification in the PCT IG
and shipped under the *Block-2025-Jul* label. That ticket added a
self-pay *kind* extension and a self-pay Coverage profile in PCT;
FHIR-53993 is effectively pushback against propagating that pattern (or
an analogous one) into Coverage in FHIR Core, advocating Account as the
correct home instead.

## Details

**Description:**

> I recommend placing the new self-pay information fits better on the
> Account resource rather than the Coverage resource for these reasons:
>
> - It conflicts with the base granularity of the Coverage resource. I
>   don't think 'self-pay coverage' is a common way of referring to
>   patients that don't have insurance coverage.
> - Self-pay needs to be logged at the visit level. Patients with
>   insurance coverage still do self-pay for specific uncovered visits,
>   so self-pay status makes more sense as a visit-level element.
> - It introduces ambiguity around when to send a self-pay coverage.
>   - Should it be if the patient has ever self-paid for a service?
>   - Or if they don't currently have any other coverage on file in the
>     source system?
>   - This could cause a billing errors and headaches for patients if
>     their insurance wasn't documented in the source system and the
>     receiving system charges a visit as self-pay because that's all
>     they got in the response.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Move the new self-pay representation off **Coverage** entirely in FHIR
Core. Concretely:

- Do **not** add a self-pay `kind` (or equivalent) to FHIR Core's
  Coverage resource. If any such addition is currently in flight in the
  build for R6/Coverage, withdraw it.
- Document the canonical FHIR Core pattern for self-pay as an
  **Account** assertion, leveraging existing elements
  (`Account.coverage` becomes empty / absent, `Account.guarantor`
  identifies the patient or self-payer party, and the visit-level
  self-pay decision is recorded via `ChargeItem` /
  `Encounter.account` → Account, optionally with an Account.type code
  for self-pay).
- Add explicit guidance in the Coverage resource narrative that absence
  of a Coverage for an Account/Encounter is the modeled signal for
  self-pay, and add a worked self-pay example anchored on Account
  rather than Coverage.
- Coordinate with PCT (FHIR-45855 owners) so that the IG-level self-pay
  Coverage profile remains an *IG-scoped* construct and is not
  re-promoted into base.

#### Justification

The two Zulip messages from Brian Swinkels (Epic) and Lloyd McKenzie
explicitly identify Account, not Coverage, as the modeling home for
self-pay. The reporter's three concerns — granularity, visit-level
applicability, and "when to send" ambiguity — all map cleanly to
characteristics that Coverage was *not* designed for and that Account
already handles. This disposition keeps FHIR Core's Coverage semantics
("actual insurance coverage") clean and avoids implementer billing
errors of the form FHIR-53993 warns about, where a receiving system
infers self-pay from incomplete Coverage data.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the door open on Coverage **only** as a non-normative, narrow
mechanism, but move the primary self-pay assertion to Account:

- Define self-pay as **primarily an Account-level concept** (per
  Disposition A), and publish that as the recommended pattern.
- Additionally, add a code (e.g., `self-pay`) to the `Coverage.type`
  value set (or a sibling extension binding) so existing consumers that
  receive *only* Coverage in a payload can still recognize a declared
  self-pay scenario when the source system has no Account context to
  send.
- Tighten Coverage guidance so that emitting a self-pay Coverage is
  **only** appropriate when (a) the patient has explicitly declared
  self-pay for a billing context and (b) no other Coverage applies; the
  guidance should call out the FHIR-53993 anti-pattern (auto-emitting
  self-pay because no insurance is on file) as non-conformant.
- Cross-reference the PCT self-pay profile (FHIR-45855) as the
  IG-specific implementation, so PCT consumers continue to work.

#### Justification

This addresses the ambiguity concern (concern #3) head-on by making the
"when to send" rule explicit and normative, without forcing PCT and
similar IGs to abandon already-deployed self-pay-on-Coverage patterns.
It preserves backwards-compatibility for FHIR-45855's PCT work while
honoring Brian Swinkels' and Lloyd McKenzie's position that Account is
the conceptual home. The trade-off vs. Disposition A is a more complex
two-resource story for implementers; the upside is fewer breaking
changes for ecosystems that have already adopted self-pay-on-Coverage
patterns out of the PCT IG.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline to remove or relocate self-pay from Coverage. Rationale: the
PCT IG already established a self-pay Coverage profile via FHIR-45855
(resolved Persuasive-with-Modification, Block-2025-Jul) and at least
some implementers expect to find self-pay on Coverage. The "when to
send" ambiguity (concern #3) can be addressed with implementation
guidance rather than a structural change.

#### Justification

Defensible if the FM WG concludes that (a) the PCT pattern represents
sufficient real-world adoption, (b) Account-based modeling lacks an
equivalent normative path for the precise self-pay payload PCT
consumers expect, and (c) the change Window for FHIR Core
Coverage is closed for the relevant ballot cycle. The cost of this
disposition is the very ambiguity FHIR-53993 warns about — Coverage
gaining a meaning it was not designed for, and receivers risking
billing-context errors when source-system data is incomplete.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition A is the cleanest from a pure FHIR-Core modeling
perspective and is the position both senior community voices in the
captured Zulip thread (Lloyd McKenzie, Brian Swinkels) take. However,
PCT has *already* shipped a self-pay Coverage profile under FHIR-45855
in Block-2025-Jul; outright removing the self-pay-on-Coverage pathway
risks an uncoordinated breakage for that IG and any consumers
following it. Disposition B preserves Account as the canonical home —
honoring both the reporter's three concerns and the prior community
consensus — while adding a narrowly-scoped, well-bounded escape hatch
on Coverage and making the "when to send" rule explicit so the
billing-error scenario FHIR-53993 calls out becomes a non-conformance
rather than an undocumented edge case. It also gives PCT a forward
path that does not require an immediate IG-level revert. If the WG
determines coordination with PCT is unproblematic, falling back to
Disposition A is the next-best choice; Disposition C should be
rejected unless the WG explicitly accepts the FHIR-53993 risk.
