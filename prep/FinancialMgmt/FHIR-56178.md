## Summary

The reporter requests that the Da Vinci ATR (Member Attribution) IG add a new
atomic `$member-change` operation alongside the existing `$member-add` and
`$member-remove` operations. The motivating scenario is one where a consumer
organization agrees that a member belongs in a Group but disagrees with the
attributed provider the producer has assigned. Today, the only way for the
consumer to express this is to send a `$member-remove` followed by a
`$member-add` with the corrected provider — two non-atomic messages that may
double web traffic and, critically, leave the producer and consumer out of
sync if the second call fails or is refused. An atomic `$member-change`
operation would let a consumer express "the member stays, but their
attribution details change" in a single, all-or-nothing call.

The ticket is closely related to a previously-resolved (Persuasive with
Modification) ticket [FHIR-39667](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-39667),
which asked the same underlying question — how a consumer should modify an
existing member's attribution. That ticket was resolved by adding example
scenarios to the spec showing the documented `member-remove` + `member-add`
pattern as the recommended approach. FHIR-56178 effectively pushes back on
that prior disposition: implementer feedback (captured in the comments by
ekivemark, "Reviewed on PDex Community Call on 2027-03-27") confirms ongoing
concern that the Remove+Add pattern is not safely atomic in practice and that
producers can succeed on the delete and refuse the add, leaving the member
attribution in an inconsistent state.

## Details

**Description:**

> We have the Member Add and Member Remove operations, but we do not have a
> Member Change operation. This would be used in a similar but distinct use
> case.
>
> Sometimes, a consumer organization might agree that a member belongs in a
> given group but disagree with the specific attributed provider the producer
> lists. Today, the consumer could send both a Member Remove operation to
> request to remove that member from the group and a Member Add operation to
> add them back to the group with the desired provider. This is not ideal
> because 1) it requires two messages instead of one and 2) there is a chance
> that one of the messages fails to be received and processed by the
> producer. Since the pair of operations is not atomic, this could lead to
> miscommunication between the consumer and the producer.
>
> An atomic operation for Member Change would be better since it would reduce
> web traffic and potential miscommunications between producers and consumers
> in the ATR spec.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Define a new atomic operation **`$member-change`** in the Da Vinci ATR IG
(HL7/davinci-atr), peer to the existing `$member-add` and `$member-remove`
operations. Concretely:

- Add a new `OperationDefinition` resource (e.g.,
  `OperationDefinition/davinci-atr-member-change`) invoked at the Group
  level (`Group/[id]/$member-change`).
- IN parameters mirror `$member-add` so the consumer expresses the *new*
  desired state of the attribution: `member-id` (required, identifies the
  Patient), `provider-npi` (the desired attributed provider, possibly
  changed), `attribution-period` (the desired period), and any other
  attribution attributes currently expressible on `$member-add`. Optionally
  add an `expected-current-provider-npi` IN parameter so the producer can
  detect optimistic-concurrency conflicts and reject mid-air collisions.
- OUT parameters mirror `$member-add` (return the updated Group / member
  entry, or an `OperationOutcome` with diagnostics).
- Define semantics as **all-or-nothing**: either the member's attribution
  is updated to the new provider/period, or no change is made and an
  error is returned. Explicitly prohibit partial application.
- Update the IG narrative (use cases section, currently 2.5) and add an
  example bundle demonstrating the change-of-provider scenario described
  in the ticket. Note in the spec how `$member-change` differs from
  `$member-add` (which today is documented as both add and "delta update"
  via the Remove+Add pattern).
- Bump the IG version per Da Vinci ATR's normal release cadence; add a
  capability statement entry advertising support for the new operation.

#### Justification

This is the most direct response to the implementer pain captured in the
comments and on the PDex Community Call: the Remove+Add pattern is not
safely atomic, and the workaround proposed in [FHIR-39667](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-39667)'s
resolution has not removed the underlying risk. Defining a dedicated
operation with all-or-nothing semantics is consistent with how FHIR
typically handles "must succeed together" workflows (e.g., transaction
bundles, dedicated operations like `$everything`, `$apply`). Adding an
operation rather than overloading `$member-add` keeps the semantics of
the existing operations stable for current implementers (no breaking
change) while giving producers a clear contract for atomicity.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Instead of minting a new operation, **profile the existing FHIR transaction
Bundle pattern** for ATR member modification, and document it as the
normative way to perform an atomic provider change. Specifically:

- Add a new section to the ATR IG describing a "Member Change" interaction
  that the consumer expresses as a `Bundle` of type `transaction`
  containing the existing `$member-remove` and `$member-add` operation
  invocations as `Bundle.entry.request` / `entry.resource = Parameters`
  pairs.
- Profile that bundle (e.g., `StructureDefinition/davinci-atr-member-change-bundle`)
  with constraints requiring exactly one remove and one add for the same
  `member-id`.
- Update the conformance text to require ATR producers to honor the
  transaction's atomicity guarantees (FHIR transaction semantics: all
  succeed or none are applied).
- Update the IG narrative and examples accordingly; deprecate the
  "do remove then add" prose added under FHIR-39667 in favor of the
  transaction-bundle pattern.

#### Justification

This achieves the same atomicity guarantee the ticket asks for while
relying on a mechanism already in core FHIR rather than introducing a
new operation that producers and consumers must individually adopt. It
keeps the operation surface of the IG small and avoids a third operation
that overlaps semantically with `$member-add`. The trade-off is that it
puts more weight on producer support for `Bundle`-of-`Parameters`
transactions — a feature that is well-defined in core FHIR but not
universally implemented at the same maturity as plain operation
invocation, especially in claims/ATR ecosystems. It also leaves the
*intent* implicit: implementers must recognize that a remove+add inside a
transaction is "a member change," whereas a dedicated operation (A)
makes intent explicit on the wire.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline to add a new operation. Reaffirm the resolution of
[FHIR-39667](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-39667): the documented
pattern remains `$member-remove` followed by `$member-add`. As an
*editorial* clarification only, expand the existing spec text on
`$member-add` to acknowledge the atomicity concern and recommend that
producers and consumers coordinate retry / reconciliation procedures
out-of-band (e.g., periodic reconciliation against the full member list).

#### Justification

The ATR IG already has two well-understood operations and a recently
balloted resolution describing how to compose them for delta updates.
Adding a third operation expands the surface area implementers must
support, may force a republish of the IG and capability statements, and
risks fragmenting the "modify member" pattern (some implementers using
`$member-change`, others continuing with Remove+Add). If atomicity is
the concern, FHIR transaction bundles already provide it without an IG
change. Declining keeps scope tight and avoids re-litigating a question
the workgroup answered in 2023.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is being raised precisely because the FHIR-39667 resolution has
not held up in implementation — and the strongest piece of new evidence
since 2023 is the PDex Community Call review, where implementers explicitly
called out the producer-can-succeed-on-delete-and-refuse-add failure mode.
Disposition C therefore restates a position that practitioners are now
telling the workgroup is insufficient. Disposition B (transaction bundles)
is technically clean but assumes producer support for transactions that, in
the real ATR ecosystem, is not a safe assumption; it also leaves intent
implicit on the wire, which is exactly the property `$member-add` and
`$member-remove` were named explicitly to give. Disposition A (a dedicated
`$member-change` operation, peer to the existing two) is the smallest change
that directly resolves the reported problem, is consistent with the IG's
existing operation-per-intent style, is non-breaking (existing implementers
of `$member-add` / `$member-remove` are not affected), and gives producers a
clear contract — atomic, all-or-nothing — that they can advertise in their
capability statement. Recommend the workgroup approve as A and assign for a
draft `OperationDefinition` plus IG narrative update.
