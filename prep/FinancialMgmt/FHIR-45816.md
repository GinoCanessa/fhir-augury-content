## Summary

The reporter requests that the (unnamed in the title) PCT ValueSet — the
fourth in a numbered series of similar requests filed by Ron G. Parker
against the US Da Vinci Patient Cost Transparency (PCT) IG — be anchored
at terminology.hl7.org (THO) rather than published only inside the PCT IG.
The reporter notes that the underlying CodeSystems are already in THO and
argues the ValueSet itself is likely useful beyond the PCT IG. The ticket
is part of a broader cluster of sibling tickets covering the rest of the
PCT ValueSet inventory (e.g., [FHIR-45812](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-45812),
[FHIR-45815](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-45815),
[FHIR-45817](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-45817),
[FHIR-45818](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-45818)) and the older
"Defining URL should be anchored in THO" series ([FHIR-35529](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-35529),
[FHIR-35532](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-35532)–[FHIR-35550](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-35550)),
all asking the same structural change for PCT terminology. The ticket was
included in the FM Block-2025-Jul block vote and was originally resolved
"Considered for Future Use" with the disposition text *"Resolve across IGs
continuing to add to THO"* on 2025-07-08 (vote 9-0-2). Lynn Laakso then
**reverted that resolution** on 2025-10-08 ("re-open post STU2
publication"), which is why the ticket is back on the FM agenda. No FHIR
spec / GitHub cross-references were surfaced by FhirAugury; the only
external reference is the FM block-vote Zulip thread.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Persuasive: relocate the PCT ValueSet referenced by this ticket (the "4"
in the Ron G. Parker series — the FM/PCT editor will need to confirm
which specific VS this maps to; the sibling tickets explicitly name
`USClaimMedicalProductOrServiceCodes` (FHIR-45812) and `PCTAdjudication`
(FHIR-45818), so FHIR-45816 is one of the remaining four PCT ValueSets
not yet individually named) from the PCT IG canonical namespace
(`http://hl7.org/fhir/us/davinci-pct/ValueSet/...`) to a THO canonical
(`http://terminology.hl7.org/ValueSet/...`). Move the ValueSet definition
artifact into THO, deprecate the IG-local copy in the next PCT release
with a redirect/alias note, and update PCT profiles' bindings to point
at the new THO canonical. Coordinate the actual move through the
existing FM/THO migration effort already underway (referenced in Lynn
Laakso's 2025-10-08 comment).

#### Justification

Anchoring ValueSets in THO when the underlying CodeSystems are already
in THO is consistent with HL7's published terminology-stewardship
guidance and with how the sibling "Persuasive — Move to THO" tickets
(FHIR-35535, FHIR-35540, FHIR-35545, FHIR-35549) have already been
dispositioned for closely related PCT artifacts. It also aligns with
the reporter's stated rationale that the ValueSet has cross-IG utility,
which is the canonical trigger for THO hosting. The FM/THO migration
effort cited in the comment thread provides an already-resourced
delivery vehicle, so the marginal cost of including this VS is low.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Persuasive with Modification: keep the ticket open and re-disposition
it as part of a single **batch resolution** that covers FHIR-45812,
FHIR-45815, FHIR-45816, FHIR-45817, FHIR-45818, and the still-open
"Considered for Future Use" subset of the FHIR-355xx series
(FHIR-35529, FHIR-35532, FHIR-35533, FHIR-35534, FHIR-35535, FHIR-35536,
FHIR-35537, FHIR-35548, FHIR-35550). Adopt a single FM disposition that
(a) commits to migrating the named PCT ValueSets to THO on a stated
schedule, (b) defines naming/canonical-URL conventions for the THO
copies, and (c) records each child ticket's individual VS name as a
checklist item linked to the FM/THO tracker. Close the children
"Persuasive with Modification — see batch plan" rather than leaving
each one to argue the same point independently.

#### Justification

The October re-open comment is explicit that "this VS will require a
greater level of review across multiple IGs, and is planned in the
coming months in larger FM/THO effort." A batch disposition matches
that operational reality and prevents the workgroup from re-litigating
the same THO-anchoring argument 5+ times. It also avoids the failure
mode of the July block vote — where a generic "Considered for Future
Use" with no owner or date allowed the work to drift, prompting the
re-open. Naming the FM/THO tracker and a target publication makes the
commitment auditable.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Not Persuasive: keep the ValueSet anchored in the PCT IG namespace
(`http://hl7.org/fhir/us/davinci-pct/ValueSet/...`) and decline to move
it to THO. Justify the decline on the grounds that (a) the ValueSet's
composition is currently scoped to PCT use cases (especially Good Faith
Estimate / Advanced EOB workflows), (b) implementers can already reuse
the ValueSet by referencing its PCT canonical from any IG that needs it,
and (c) THO migration carries non-trivial governance and versioning
overhead that should only be incurred when concrete cross-IG reuse is
demonstrated, which the ticket does not document.

#### Justification

A bare assertion that a ValueSet "could be useful beyond this IG" is
not by itself a sufficient case for THO promotion under FM's normal
terminology-governance bar. THO hosting also locks the FM WG into a
shared change-control process with other WGs that consume the VS,
which has historically slowed PCT publication cadence. If no other IG
is actively asking to bind to this VS, deferring (or declining)
preserves PCT's release velocity at the cost of a minor inconvenience
for any future consumer, who can in any case bind to the PCT canonical.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The October 2025 re-open is unambiguous: the FM WG has already started a
larger FM/THO migration and intends to handle this class of ValueSet
through that effort. Disposition A would be technically correct in
isolation but, applied in isolation, repeats the July mistake of giving
the work no shared owner or schedule. Disposition C contradicts both the
existing "Persuasive — Move to THO" precedents in the FHIR-355xx series
and the WG's own stated direction. Disposition B captures the right
substantive answer (yes, anchor in THO) **and** the right operational
answer (do it once, as a batch, against the existing FM/THO program),
and gives the workgroup a defensible reason to close FHIR-45816 and its
siblings together rather than re-opening this debate for each VS in
turn.
