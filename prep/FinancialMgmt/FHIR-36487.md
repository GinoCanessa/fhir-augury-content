## Summary

The ticket requests that the CARIN IG for Blue Button (C4BB) stop publishing
the `C4BBRelatedClaimRelationshipCodes` CodeSystem (used for the
`ExplanationOfBenefit.related.relationship` binding) as an "example" CodeSystem
under the `hl7.org/fhir` IG namespace, and instead define it as a normative
(non-example) CodeSystem hosted in HL7 Terminology (THO).

This issue was originally dispositioned in the CARIN BB STU2 ballot block vote
(see Zulip: "Announcement - CARIN BB STU2 Block-Vote-6") with resolution
**Considered for Future Use**, with a note that the change would be made when
the corresponding THO content is published — tracked by the THO/UTG tickets
referenced in the comments: **UP-312** (Update FHIR Claim CareTeamRole
Terminologies) and **UP-313** (Create Related Claim Relationship CS/VS that is
not an example). Lynn Laakso re-opened the ticket in Nov 2022 ("re-open post
STU2 publication") so it could be picked up after STU2 publication once the
THO artifact lands. It has remained in the **Triaged** state since.

The request is closely related to two sibling tickets that target the same
artifact from a different angle: **FHIR-35398** (re-anchor the CodeSystem URL
in THO instead of `hl7.org/fhir`) and **FHIR-35431** (do the same for the
matching ValueSet `C4BBRelatedClaimRelationshipCodes`). Both received the same
"Considered for Future Use" disposition tied to UP-313 / UP-316. Together they
represent the same underlying remediation: stop shipping the codes as an
IG-local example and start consuming them from THO.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Replace the IG-local example `CodeSystem/C4BBRelatedClaimRelationshipCodes`
(currently published at
`http://hl7.org/fhir/us/carin-bb/CodeSystem/C4BBRelatedClaimRelationshipCodes`)
with a non-example HL7 Terminology (THO) CodeSystem. Concretely:

1. Coordinate with Vocabulary / UTG to land the THO CodeSystem tracked by
   **UP-313** ("Create Related Claim Relationship CS/VS that is not an
   example") at a stable canonical such as
   `http://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/related-claim-relationship` (exact
   id/URL to be confirmed by the Vocabulary WG).
2. In the next CARIN BB version, retire the IG-local CodeSystem (or mark it
   `experimental=false`, `status=retired`, and keep only as a deprecated
   alias) and re-anchor the matching ValueSet
   `C4BBRelatedClaimRelationshipCodes` so its `compose.include.system` points
   at the THO URL.
3. Update the binding on `ExplanationOfBenefit.related.relationship` (in the
   relevant C4BB EoB profiles) to bind to the re-anchored ValueSet.
4. Document the canonical change and provide a mapping note in the IG so that
   existing payer implementations can transition. Resolves FHIR-36487 jointly
   with the companion tickets FHIR-35398 (CodeSystem URL) and FHIR-35431
   (ValueSet URL).

#### Justification

This is exactly the remediation the original reporter requested and is
consistent with the workgroup's stated long-standing plan: the 2022 block-vote
disposition explicitly committed to making the change "when published in THO."
It also aligns with established FHIR/HL7 practice that non-example, reusable
terminology should live in THO rather than be embedded inside an IG, and it
unifies three Jira tickets (FHIR-36487 / FHIR-35398 / FHIR-35431) into one
coherent change. Implementers benefit from a single, canonical, governance-
backed terminology rather than an IG-local example they must "treat as
authoritative" in practice.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the artifact under the C4BB canonical namespace, but change its
classification: set `experimental = false` and explicitly mark the CodeSystem
as **normative within the C4BB IG** (not an "example"), while leaving the
canonical URL at `http://hl7.org/fhir/us/carin-bb/CodeSystem/...`. The
ValueSet binding strength on
`ExplanationOfBenefit.related.relationship` would be tightened (e.g.,
`extensible` or `required`) so that the codes carry real conformance weight.
Open a follow-on `Persuasive with Modification` action to re-host in THO only
if/when another IG adopts the same codes.

#### Justification

The reporter's underlying concern is that consumers should not treat the codes
as throw-away examples. That can be addressed without waiting on UTG by
removing the "example" framing inside the IG and tightening the binding. This
avoids a canonical-URL change (which is a breaking change for every
implementer that has already pinned to the C4BB URL) and avoids the
cross-organization dependency on UP-313 landing in THO before STU3 can ship.
The trade-off is that the codes remain owned by FM/CARIN BB rather than by the
Vocabulary WG, which goes against the harmonization rationale that drove the
original ticket.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark FHIR-36487 as **Not Persuasive** on the basis that the codes are narrow
in scope, fairly specific to the CARIN BB use case, do not warrant the
maintenance overhead of moving to THO, and are already adequately served by
the existing IG-local CodeSystem. Close FHIR-36487, FHIR-35398, and
FHIR-35431 together and retire UP-313 from the CARIN BB roadmap.

#### Justification

This mirrors the disposition CARIN BB has actually applied to several sibling
"move to THO" tickets in the same Block-Vote-6 (e.g., FHIR-35432, FHIR-35434,
FHIR-35436 for ValueSets the WG judged "not complex, fairly specific to this
IG"). If the WG concludes that
`C4BBRelatedClaimRelationshipCodes` is in the same category — small, IG-local,
not used outside CARIN BB — declining is internally consistent and avoids
ongoing coordination cost with UTG and the risk of canonical-URL churn for
implementers.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The workgroup has already publicly committed to this remediation (13-0-0 block
vote in 2022, with Lynn Laakso explicitly re-opening the ticket post-STU2 so
it can be executed). UP-313 was specifically created to deliver the THO
artifact, and FHIR-35398 / FHIR-35431 are the matching CodeSystem-URL and
ValueSet-URL tickets that only make sense if the underlying terminology
actually moves. Disposition B preserves the canonical URL but at the cost of
ignoring the WG's prior commitment and leaves a non-example CodeSystem
permanently owned outside the Vocabulary WG, undermining the harmonization
goal. Disposition C is defensible on a per-artifact basis but would
contradict the active UP-313 work item and reduce three coordinated tickets
to a confusing closed-without-action outcome. The workgroup should pick A,
gate the change on UP-313 publication, and execute FHIR-36487 / FHIR-35398 /
FHIR-35431 as a single coordinated change in the next CARIN BB version.
