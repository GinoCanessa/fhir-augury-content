## Summary

The ticket reports an internal contradiction in the US Da Vinci PDex IG
narrative around the single-member Payer-to-Payer Exchange flow. The
opening of the section (conformance statement `pdex-176`) explicitly
states that "Payers **MAY** choose to implement Payer-to-Payer Exchange
for a single member …", framing the entire single-member flow as
optional. However, a sentence buried under the diagram (conformance
statement `pdex-182`) reads "Health Plans **SHALL** support the
$member-match operation," which directly conflicts with the optionality
declared above and with how implementers have been understanding the
section. The reporter (Nathaniel Tomtishen) treats this as leftover text
from an earlier version and asks that the second (SHALL) sentence be
deleted so the single-member flow remains consistently optional.

The ticket has already been worked: it is labelled `Pre-Applied`, has
resolution **Persuasive**, and a commit titled
"FHIR-55928: Make single-member Payer-to-Payer Exchange a consistent
SHALL" (`8053e71e`) has landed on `HL7/davinci-epdx`. The applied
resolution went the **opposite direction** from the literal request:
rather than dropping the SHALL sentence to make the section uniformly
MAY, the IG was edited to revert to a consistent **SHALL** "as per
earlier version of IG" (per the resolution description). No related Jira
tickets are linked, but the originating Zulip thread (see below) shows
significant disagreement among PDex implementers about whether
single-member `$member-match` should be required at all, with several
major payers stating they only intend to implement
`$bulk-member-match`. Reviewers should weigh the literal request, the
applied "consistent SHALL" change, and the implementer feedback before
voting.

## Related Zulip Discussions

### [Da Vinci PDex > Payer to Payer Single Member - Scoping to one Patient](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/Da%20Vinci%20PDex/topic/Payer%20to%20Payer%20Single%20Member%20-%20Scoping%20to%20one%20Patient/near/575035107)

This is the originating thread that produced FHIR-55928. Artem Sopin
opens with an implementer question about how single-member
`$member-match` can work with SMART Backend Services (since
`client_credentials` cannot pass a launch/`patient` parameter). Michele
Mottini and Cooper Thompson (Epic) both state that they will only
implement **bulk** member match (`$bulk-member-match`) on both client
and server side, asserting that the single-member flow is incomplete
and not implementable at scale; Alexander Zautke adds that he advocated
deprecating single member match two years ago. Daniel Venton pushes
back, citing the "Health Plans SHALL support the $member-match
operation" sentence in
`payertopayerexchange.html#member-match-with-consent` and arguing that
without a mandatory single-member option you lose interoperability and
real-time UX (member-typing-time identity verification). Cooper
counters that CMS-0057 does not actually require `$member-match` (only
FHIR, US Core, and Bulk Export), so the SHALL is currently
non-binding but is "ripe for improvement". Nathaniel Tomtishen then
posts that he has filed FHIR-55928 to delete the conflicting SHALL
sentence.

The thread continues for several weeks, branching into related but
out-of-scope topics: Group export semantics, $everything's known
problems, the experimental flag on `BulkMemberMatch`, dedup/referential
integrity for imported bulk data, the relationship between
`$bulk-member-match`, `$multi-member-match` (the input bundle name)
and `$provider-member-match`, and a tension between the PDex
`$davinci-data-export` operation and CMS-0057's explicit naming of
`group-export` (FHIR-52063, currently proposed non-persuasive). No
single consensus emerges on whether the single-member flow should be
SHALL, MAY, or removed; the implementer-side weight is clearly toward
MAY/remove, while Daniel Venton remains the principal voice arguing for
mandatory support so interactive single-patient use cases stay viable.

Participants: Artem Sopin, Michele Mottini, Daniel Venton, Cooper
Thompson, Alexander Zautke, Nathaniel Tomtishen, Kelsey Hales, Craig
McClendon, Mark Scrimshire (mentioned), Lloyd McKenzie, Sameera
Gunarathne, Alec.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the literal change requested in the ticket: in
`HL7/davinci-epdx`'s `input/pagecontent/payertopayerexchange.md`,
**delete the "Health Plans SHALL support the $member-match operation"
sentence** that currently renders as conformance statement `pdex-182`.
Update the upstream conformance-language source
(`Requirements-fromNarrative.json` and/or `conformance_statements.csv`)
so that statement `pdex-182` is removed and `narrative-conformance.html`
no longer carries it. Re-run `process_conformance.py` and the
IG-publisher so the rendered conformance table matches. The opening
"Payers **MAY** choose to implement Payer-to-Payer Exchange for a
single member …" (`pdex-176`) remains and is the sole controlling
statement, making single-member exchange uniformly optional. Add a
change-history entry in `input/pagecontent/changehistory.md`. No
CapabilityStatement edit is required because no PDex CapabilityStatement
currently mandates `Patient/$member-match` for the payer-access /
payer-to-payer servers (the obligation lived only in the deleted
narrative sentence).

#### Justification

This matches what the reporter actually requested and aligns with the
strong implementer feedback on the Zulip thread: Epic, Michele
Mottini's organization, and Alexander Zautke have all explicitly stated
they will not implement single-member `$member-match` and consider it
not implementable at scale; the operation itself remains "experimental"
in the underlying definition (per Lloyd's note that this is incorrect
labelling but reflects current maturity). Removing the conflicting
SHALL eliminates the most obvious internal contradiction in the
section, removes a conformance trap for vendors who choose the
bulk-only path that CMS-0057 actually compels, and is the
lowest-surface-area edit available — single narrative paragraph plus a
single conformance-statement row.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — Resolve Inconsistency by Keeping a Consistent SHALL (the change actually applied)

#### Proposal

Keep the change that has already been pre-applied by commit
[`8053e71e`](https://github.com/HL7/davinci-epdx/commit/8053e71e17bb4bc965917ad7721e13d1a3d019ee):
in `input/pagecontent/payertopayerexchange.md`, rewrite the opening
paragraph so that single-member Payer-to-Payer Exchange is described
as a **SHALL** (Health Plans **SHALL** support `$member-match` and the
single-member flow), restoring the language that existed in the
earlier version of the IG. Update conformance statement `pdex-176` so
its modal verb is SHALL (matching `pdex-182`), regenerate the
`narrative-conformance.html` table via `process_conformance.py`, and
ensure the
`pdex-payer-access-server-capability-statement.fsh` /
`pdex-server-capability-statement.fsh` CapabilityStatements explicitly
list `Patient.$member-match` as a `mandatory` operation on the payer
server (or confirm they already do). Keep the change-history entry in
`input/pagecontent/changehistory.md` referencing FHIR-55928. This is
the disposition that the resolution text ("revert to SHALL as per
earlier version of IG") corresponds to and that already lives on the
publication branch.

#### Justification

This is the disposition the work group has already (provisionally)
acted on, and there is a coherent argument for it: a uniformly SHALL
single-member flow guarantees that any payer-to-payer interaction can
fall back to per-member match, which preserves the interactive
real-time identity-verification use case Daniel Venton is defending and
prevents the "33%/33%/33% no interoperability" outcome he warns about.
It also re-aligns the IG with its own historical position rather than
silently weakening conformance during a Technical Correction. The
gotcha that the conformance language is generated is addressed by the
pre-applied commit (it must update `Requirements-fromNarrative.json`
and re-run `process_conformance.py`, not just edit the markdown);
review should confirm that `pdex-176` and `pdex-182` now agree in the
generated `narrative-conformance.html`. The downside — that several
major payers have publicly said they will not implement it — is real
but is a market-conformance question better tackled via a separate
ticket (e.g., a deprecation proposal) than by silently weakening the
SHALL.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline FHIR-55928 as drafted (do not delete the SHALL sentence) and
also revert the pre-applied change in commit `8053e71e`, returning
both `pdex-176` (MAY) and `pdex-182` (SHALL) to a known prior published
state, with a comment that the broader question — whether
single-member `$member-match` should remain in PDex at all — is out of
scope for a Technical Correction and should be raised as a substantive
change (or a deprecation) ticket against the next ballot cycle.

#### Justification

The Zulip thread shows that this is not actually an editorial slip but
a substantive, contested design question (mandatory vs optional vs
removed) on which the IG's largest implementers and its conformance
champions are publicly disagreeing. A "Technical Correction" priority
is the wrong vehicle to settle that — whichever direction the WG picks
under this ticket, half the community will (legitimately) feel that a
substantive policy change was made through the back door. Declining
preserves the status quo, prevents a one-line edit from quietly
flipping a conformance obligation, and forces the disagreement into a
properly-scoped substantive proposal that can carry its own ballot
discussion. The cost is that the published IG continues to carry the
visible MAY/SHALL contradiction for one more cycle.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B (Persuasive — keep the pre-applied "consistent SHALL" change).

The resolution has already been pre-applied with that direction
(`Pre-Applied` label, resolution **Persuasive**, commit
`8053e71e`), and the resolution description explicitly says "revert
to SHALL as per earlier version of IG." Disposition B simply ratifies
what the WG has already done and what is already on the publication
branch, which is the cleanest outcome for the ballot. The literal
request (Disposition A) would silently weaken a long-standing
conformance obligation in a Technical Correction, which is exactly
the kind of change that a focused ballot ticket should *not* be used
for, even though the implementer signal in the Zulip thread is
sympathetic to it; that signal is better captured by a follow-up
substantive ticket proposing deprecation of single-member
`$member-match` outright (with engagement from Mark Scrimshire and the
implementers who have stated they will not support it). Reviewers
should additionally verify before the vote that (a) the conformance
table in `narrative-conformance.html` is regenerated so `pdex-176` and
`pdex-182` are now both SHALL, and (b) the
`pdex-payer-access-server-capability-statement.fsh` /
`pdex-server-capability-statement.fsh` CapabilityStatements reflect
`Patient/$member-match` as `mandatory` on the payer server — otherwise
the narrative SHALL would still not be enforceable through the
machine-readable conformance.
