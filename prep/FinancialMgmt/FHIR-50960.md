## Summary

FHIR-50960 asks the CARIN Blue Button (C4BB) IG to revert the HCPCS Level II
CodeSystem URI it references inside the
`C4BBEOBInstitutionalProcedureCodes` ValueSet (and sibling ValueSets that
reuse the same CLD) from `https://www.cms.gov/Medicare/Coding/HCPCSReleaseCodeSets`
back to the original `http://www.cms.gov/Medicare/Coding/HCPCSReleaseCodeSets`.
The `https` form was introduced when [FHIR-37334](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-37334)
aligned C4BB with the then-current THO definition, but THO 5.4.0 reverted to
`http` as errata under UP-472. The reporter (Joshua Kelly) also flagged a
parallel UTG proposal (UP-687) to revert the HIPPS CodeSystem URI for the
same reason — both CodeSystems used in this ValueSet drifted to `https` and
need to come back to `http` so terminology servers treat them as the same
canonical CodeSystem THO publishes. The resolved scope (per the issue's
Resolution Description) covers `AMACPTCMSHCPCSProcedureCodes`,
`C4BBEOBInstitutionalProcedureCodes`, and `AMACPTCMSHCPCSModifiers`, plus
the narrative and example instance. Comments from Corey Spears note that the
same drift exists in US Core (tracked by [FHIR-50807](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50807),
slated for US Core 9) and warn that this is a breaking change for downstream
implementers; he suggests timing the C4BB fix to the US Core release that
adopts the same URI so PDex/PAS/CRD do not have to support both URIs longer
than necessary.

## Details

**Description:**

In FHIR-37334 we updated the reference to a CodeSystem in the
`http://hl7.org/fhir/us/carin-bb/ValueSet/C4BBEOBInstitutionalProcedureCodes`
ValueSet. Specifically, from `http://www.cms.gov/Medicare/Coding/HCPCSReleaseCodeSets`
to `https://www.cms.gov/Medicare/Coding/HCPCSReleaseCodeSets`. This was correct
as of THO 5.3.0 but was updated in THO 5.4.0 as errata in UP-472
(`https://terminology.hl7.org/5.4.0/CodeSystem-hcpcs-Level-II.html`). This is
a change request to revert this change back to
`http://www.cms.gov/Medicare/Coding/HCPCSReleaseCodeSets`. We'll want to make
sure the narrative and `compose` all line up correctly in the ValueSet.

**Resolution Description:**

Will revert HCPCS Level II code system URL from
`https://www.cms.gov/Medicare/Coding/HCPCSReleaseCodeSets` to
`http://www.cms.gov/Medicare/Coding/HCPCSReleaseCodeSets` in ValueSet compose
sections (`AMACPTCMSHCPCSProcedureCodes`, `C4BBEOBInstitutionalProcedureCodes`,
`AMACPTCMSHCPCSModifiers`), narrative documentation, and example instance to
align with THO 5.4.0 (errata UP-472).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the change exactly as captured in the Resolution Description on
this ticket: in the next CARIN Blue Button publication, revert every
occurrence of the HCPCS Level II CodeSystem URI from
`https://www.cms.gov/Medicare/Coding/HCPCSReleaseCodeSets` to
`http://www.cms.gov/Medicare/Coding/HCPCSReleaseCodeSets` in the
`compose.include.system` of the three ValueSets
`AMACPTCMSHCPCSProcedureCodes`, `C4BBEOBInstitutionalProcedureCodes`, and
`AMACPTCMSHCPCSModifiers`; regenerate the human-readable narrative on
each; and update the example EOB instance that references the `https`
form. Coordinate the publication with the parallel UTG fix for HIPPS
([UP-687](https://jira.hl7.org/projects/UP/issues/UP-687)) so the same
publication can normalise both CodeSystem URIs against THO 5.4.0.

#### Justification

This is the direction THO took in 5.4.0 under errata UP-472, and the
authoritative HCPCS Level II CodeSystem at
`https://terminology.hl7.org/5.4.0/CodeSystem-hcpcs-Level-II.html` now
publishes the `http` URI. Terminology servers — as confirmed in the Da
Vinci PAS/PDex Zulip thread — already treat `http` and `https` variants
as distinct CodeSystems, so leaving C4BB on `https` actively breaks
expansion, validation, and translation against THO and against any IG
that has already realigned. FHIR-50960's resolution description is
already concrete and bounded; accepting it as written gives implementers
exactly one URI to support per CodeSystem in the next CARIN BB
publication and closes a known interoperability gap.

---

### Disposition B: Accept, but stage publication with US Core 9 and document a dual-URI transition

#### Proposal

Adopt the same content change as Disposition A (revert all three
ValueSets, narrative, and example to the `http` URI), but explicitly
schedule the CARIN BB publication so it ships *with or after* US Core 9
(which will close [FHIR-50807](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50807) by
making the matching revert in US Core's procedure-code ValueSet). At the
same time, add a short transition note to the C4BB ValueSet narratives
and to the IG's "Changes" section stating: (a) the canonical HCPCS Level
II URI is the THO `http://www.cms.gov/Medicare/Coding/HCPCSReleaseCodeSets`
form; (b) instances produced against prior CARIN BB versions may carry
the `https` form and SHOULD be treated as equivalent for validation and
expansion purposes during the migration window; and (c) servers SHOULD
accept either URI on input for one published cycle.

#### Justification

This is the disposition Corey Spears effectively recommends in his
2026-01-10 and 2026-01-20 comments, and it is corroborated by the Da
Vinci CRD Zulip thread where Cooper Thompson notes that CRD inherits a
US Core 7 ValueSet that cannot be re-published, so CRD will need to
accept both URIs for a release. CARIN BB is in the same boat: a clean
revert without coordination guarantees that PDex, PAS, CRD and CARIN BB
implementers will see a window where some payers emit the `http` URI and
others emit `https`. Pinning the publication to US Core 9 and documenting
the dual-URI window honours the request, prevents fragmentation across
the Da Vinci/CARIN family of IGs, and gives implementers a clear,
time-boxed migration story.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (defer to US Core / THO and do not change C4BB)

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-50960 as "Not Persuasive" (or "Persuasive — No Change", per
FM's preference) on the basis that the CARIN BB ValueSets should bind to
the upstream US Core procedure-code ValueSet rather than carry their own
inline `compose.include` of the HCPCS CodeSystem. Track the URI choice
upstream (in US Core via [FHIR-50807](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50807)
and in THO via UP-472), and instead open a follow-up ticket against C4BB
to refactor `C4BBEOBInstitutionalProcedureCodes` and its siblings to
include US Core's ValueSet by reference, removing the local CodeSystem
URI altogether so this class of drift cannot recur.

#### Justification

The defensible argument for declining is structural: the only reason
this ticket exists is that C4BB inlines a HCPCS CodeSystem URI inside
its own ValueSet `compose.include`, which forces FM to chase every
upstream THO/US Core URI revision with its own ticket. If C4BB instead
binds via US Core's ValueSet (which is the long-term direction
implementers expect, given EOB.item.productOrService is already
US-Core-aligned in the broader ecosystem), then UP-472 + FHIR-50807
deliver the fix without any C4BB change at all. That said, this
disposition leaves implementers running the *current* C4BB version
emitting an URI that THO no longer publishes — a real interoperability
problem confirmed in the Da Vinci PAS/PDex Zulip thread — and the
refactor to bind via US Core is itself a non-trivial breaking change.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the right call. The substantive change is small,
unambiguous, and well-scoped (the resolution description already
enumerates the exact ValueSets, narrative, and example to touch), and
both the THO errata (UP-472) and the cross-IG Zulip evidence make clear
that the `http` URI is the correct target. What FHIR-50960 cannot solve
on its own is the transition risk that Corey Spears and Cooper Thompson
both flagged: until US Core 9 ships the same revert under FHIR-50807,
C4BB-only changes will create a window where US Core-derived profiles
and CARIN BB-derived profiles disagree on the HCPCS canonical URL,
forcing PDex/PAS/CRD implementers to support both. Sequencing the C4BB
publication with US Core 9 and writing the dual-URI behaviour into the
ValueSet narratives turns a one-line URI change into a coordinated
ecosystem fix, which is what the broader Da Vinci/CARIN community has
been asking for in the Zulip discussions. Disposition A is acceptable if
schedule pressure makes co-publication with US Core 9 impossible, and
Disposition C is the cleanest long-term architecture but is too large a
change to bundle into this ticket and would leave the current break
unresolved in the interim.
