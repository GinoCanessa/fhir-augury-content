## Summary

The ticket reports an open editorial TODO in the InsurancePlan resource page
("TODO: Do we need to reference the InsuranceProduct here instead?") in the
Boundaries and Relationships section, and asks that the relationship between
Coverage, InsurancePlan, and InsuranceProduct be solidified and documented
consistently. The reporter requests that an InsuranceProduct row be added to
the relationship table on the InsurancePlan page, and that comparable
relationship tables be added to the Boundaries and Relationships section of
each of the three resources so that the `Coverage → InsurancePlan →
InsuranceProduct` chain is described uniformly across the resource pages.

There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items
recorded for FHIR-50479 in FHIR Augury. The ticket has been routed to a
joint Patient Administration / Financial Management discussion (per
Reuben Daniels' 2025-05-12 comment placing it on the PA-FM joint agenda),
and a PA WGM Madrid comment (Brian Postlethwaite, 2025-05-15) indicates the
group considers the underlying modelling question already resolved by the
recent split of InsurancePlan and InsuranceProduct, so the change is largely
documentation/cleanup.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the FHIR Core specification, update the **Boundaries and Relationships**
section of the `InsurancePlan` resource page:

1. Remove the TODO note ("TODO: Do we need to reference the InsuranceProduct
   here instead?").
2. Add an `InsuranceProduct` row to the relationship table, describing
   InsurancePlan as the consumer-facing offering that references an
   `InsuranceProduct` (the underlying insurance product/contract template).

Then add (or expand) parallel **Boundaries and Relationships** tables on the
`Coverage` and `InsuranceProduct` resource pages so that all three resources
present the same `Coverage → InsurancePlan → InsuranceProduct` chain, each
from its own perspective:

- `Coverage` page: rows for `InsurancePlan` (the plan being purchased / used)
  and `InsuranceProduct` (the underlying product that the plan instantiates).
- `InsuranceProduct` page: rows for `InsurancePlan` (offerings derived from
  the product) and `Coverage` (downstream coverage instances).
- `InsurancePlan` page: rows for `Coverage` (instances purchased) and
  `InsuranceProduct` (the product the plan is based on).

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter asked for and is consistent with the PA
WGM Madrid conclusion captured by Brian Postlethwaite (2025-05-15): the
plan/product split has resolved the open modelling question, leaving an
editorial cleanup. Stranded TODO notes in normative-track pages are a
quality-of-publication issue, and parallel relationship tables are an
established convention on related FHIR resource pages — applying the same
pattern across `Coverage`, `InsurancePlan`, and `InsuranceProduct` improves
discoverability of the chain for implementers crossing between
administrative and financial scenarios.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Address the editorial TODO in the same way as Disposition A (remove the
note, add the missing `InsuranceProduct` row on the `InsurancePlan` page),
but instead of duplicating relationship tables across three resource pages,
introduce a single shared narrative section — e.g., a short
"Coverage / InsurancePlan / InsuranceProduct relationship" subsection on
each page that cross-links to a canonical diagram or table maintained in
**one** place (for example, on the `InsurancePlan` page or on a
financial-resources overview page). Each resource page links to that
canonical description and only lists the directly-adjacent relationships in
its own Boundaries and Relationships table.

#### Justification

A single source of truth for the `Coverage → InsurancePlan →
InsuranceProduct` chain reduces the risk that the three tables drift out of
sync as the resources evolve in subsequent ballots — a very real risk given
that the resources are curated jointly by FM and PA. It also keeps each
resource's Boundaries and Relationships section focused on directly-adjacent
relationships, which is the dominant pattern elsewhere in the
specification. Trade-off vs. Disposition A: implementers landing on
`Coverage` or `InsuranceProduct` see slightly less standalone information
and must follow a link to get the full chain.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request as a documentation enhancement that does not require a
tracker-driven change: simply remove the stranded TODO comment as routine
editorial cleanup (per the PA WGM Madrid recommendation) and treat any
broader cross-resource relationship-table harmonisation as part of normal
editorial maintenance handled out-of-band by the FM and PA work groups,
rather than committing to add new tables on `Coverage` and `InsuranceProduct`
in response to this ticket.

#### Justification

The substantive modelling concern raised by the TODO has already been
resolved by the InsurancePlan / InsuranceProduct split, and PA has explicitly
advised that the note can just be removed. Mandating new parallel tables on
two additional resource pages expands scope beyond the original editorial
defect, and risks producing tables that read as boilerplate without adding
information that isn't already implicit in the element definitions and
references. Closing with a minimal TODO removal keeps the change
proportionate to the underlying defect.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter's request is well-targeted: they identified a specific stranded
TODO and a real inconsistency in how the `Coverage → InsurancePlan →
InsuranceProduct` chain is documented across the three resource pages. PA's
WGM Madrid feedback (Brian Postlethwaite) confirms there is no remaining
modelling debate, only an editorial fix, which makes the change low-risk and
easy to specify. Adding parallel relationship rows on all three resource
pages (Disposition A) gives implementers — particularly those who land on
`Coverage` or `InsuranceProduct` from a payer-side workflow — a complete
local picture of the chain without having to navigate to a sibling resource.
Disposition B's "single source of truth" idea is attractive in principle but
adds editorial overhead and a layer of indirection that the existing
Boundaries and Relationships convention does not use elsewhere; if drift
becomes a real problem in a later cycle, it can be revisited then.
Disposition C under-delivers given that the reporter has already done the
work of identifying the symmetry gap across the three pages.
