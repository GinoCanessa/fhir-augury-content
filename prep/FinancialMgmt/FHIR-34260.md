## Summary

FHIR-34260 flags that the CARIN IG for Blue Button `C4BBClaimCareTeamRole`
code system contains a concept, `purchasedService`, whose definition ("A
purchased service occurs when one provider purchases a service from
another provider and then provides it to the patient, e.g. a diagnostic
exam") describes a *transaction/relationship between providers* rather
than a *role played by a care team member*, making it inconsistent with
the other concepts in the code system. The reporter asks the Financial
Management work group to either remove the concept from the code system
or rewrite its definition so that it clearly represents a care team
role.

The ticket's trajectory was to be bundled into the CARIN BB STU2 ballot
block vote (Block-Vote-6, 2022-07-26) with a proposed resolution of
*Considered for Future Use*, pending harmonization work that moved the
`ClaimCareTeamRole` code system to THO. It was re-opened post-STU2
publication and eventually re-resolved *Persuasive with Modification*:
the concept is retained, but redefined to describe the *care team
member's* role — wording along the lines of "The provider from which a
service was purchased by another provider" — and the authoritative
CodeSystem moves to THO (`http://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/claimcareteamrole`).
Companion Unified Terminology tickets UP-312 (update the FHIR Claim
CareTeamRole terminologies with codes and details) and UP-313 (create a
non-example Related Claim Relationship CS/VS) are tracking the actual
terminology migration.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Retain `purchasedService` in the CARIN-BB `C4BBClaimCareTeamRole`
CodeSystem but rewrite its `definition` so it unambiguously describes a
*care team member's role* rather than a provider-to-provider
transaction. Concretely, change the concept's definition to something
like:

> **purchasedService** — "The provider from which a service was
> purchased by another provider. The care team member's role on this
> claim is that of the upstream provider whose service was purchased
> and then delivered to the patient by the billing provider."

Leave the code value (`purchasedService`) and its ValueSet membership
(`C4BBClaimCareTeamRole`, `C4BBClaimInstitutionalCareTeamRole`)
unchanged so no binding churn is introduced for implementers. Update
any narrative/example EoB fixtures that reference the concept to show
it bound to `ExplanationOfBenefit.careTeam.role` with the new wording.

#### Justification

This is faithful to the reporter's request ("define it to clarify why
it would represent a care team role") and is the minimum-impact change
for STU2 implementers: code, URL, and binding strengths are preserved.
It aligns with the explicit "Change to 'The provider from which a
service was purchased by another provider.'" wording lynn_laakso
recorded on 2022-11-28. For a small IG-local terminology fix, an
in-place definition update is consistent with normal FHIR terminology
maintenance practice and avoids forcing consumers to re-bind ValueSets.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — Move to THO (the actual applied resolution)

#### Proposal

Rather than editing the IG-local CodeSystem in place, move the
authoritative CodeSystem to THO as
`http://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/claimcareteamrole` and have
CARIN-BB reference it from there. The THO definition is already tuned
to describe `purchasedService` as a care team member role (see
[terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem-claimcareteamrole](https://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem-claimcareteamrole.html)),
so the substantive concern on FHIR-34260 is resolved by rebinding the
CARIN `C4BBClaimCareTeamRole` / `C4BBClaimInstitutionalCareTeamRole`
ValueSets to the THO CodeSystem URL and removing the in-IG CodeSystem
in the next major CARIN-BB version. Implementation of the terminology
migration is tracked in UP-312 (with UP-313 covering a parallel change
for related-claim-relationship codes).

#### Justification

This is what the work group actually chose to do. It addresses the
reporter's concern by using THO's curated definition, eliminates the
IG-local example CodeSystem (avoiding the anti-pattern called out in
the sibling tickets FHIR-35391, FHIR-33202, etc.), and centralizes
maintenance for other IGs that need claim care team roles. The
trade-off versus Disposition A is that consumers must rebind to the
THO URL, which is why it was deferred to a later CARIN-BB version
rather than patched into STU2; this lines up with the Block-Vote-6
thread's "Plan on moving when published in THO" disposition text.

---

### Disposition C: Decline — Remove `purchasedService`

#### Proposal

Decline the "rewrite the definition" option and instead take the other
option the reporter offered: *remove* the `purchasedService` concept
from `C4BBClaimCareTeamRole` and from any ValueSets that include it
(`C4BBClaimCareTeamRole`, `C4BBClaimInstitutionalCareTeamRole`), on
the grounds that "purchased service" is a claim/billing relationship
between providers, not a role a care team member plays on a specific
service.

#### Justification

A clean way to resolve the inconsistency the reporter identified is to
treat it as a scope error: the CARIN care-team-role CodeSystem should
only enumerate *roles on the care team*, and the relationship between
a purchasing and performing provider is already represented elsewhere
(e.g., `ExplanationOfBenefit.related` for related claims,
`Claim.provider` vs. referring/supervising provider roles, or
`CareTeam.participant.role` bound to broader role terminologies).
Declining to keep the concept avoids perpetuating a category error
even if the wording is improved.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

This is both the correct long-term answer and the one the Financial
Management work group in fact adopted: the resolution field on
FHIR-34260 is *Persuasive with Modification* and the resolution
description points at
`https://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem-claimcareteamrole.html`, with
UP-312 tracking the THO migration. Disposition A is a reasonable STU2
stop-gap, but it leaves CARIN-BB with an IG-local "example" CodeSystem
that the same ballot cycle (see Block-Vote-6 and FHIR-35391) flagged
as something to move out of `hl7.org/fhir/...` and into THO;
addressing FHIR-34260 in isolation by editing wording would create
terminology debt. Disposition C is too aggressive — `purchasedService`
is used by real CARIN-BB claims data to identify upstream providers,
and removing it would be a breaking change without a clean
replacement in another binding. Disposition B resolves the reporter's
concern (the THO definition is care-team-role-shaped), stays
consistent with the rest of the Block-Vote-6 cohort, and defers the
binding change to a planned major version, which is the path of least
disruption for implementers.
