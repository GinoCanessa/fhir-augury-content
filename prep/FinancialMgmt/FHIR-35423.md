## Summary

This CARIN IG for Blue Button (C4BB) ticket asks that the `C4BBPatientIdentifierType`
ValueSet's defining canonical URL be re-anchored under terminology.hl7.org (THO)
instead of `hl7.org/fhir`, and that a corresponding UTG ticket be filed to register
the ValueSet in THO. The reporter (Joan Harper) frames this as part of an effort
to align C4BB terminology artifacts with HL7's terminology-publishing policy: only
ValueSets bound `required` to a `code` data type should remain anchored under
`hl7.org/fhir`; everything else belongs in THO. The ticket carries the highest
priority and is currently in `Triaged` status with no assignee.

The ticket sits inside the C4BB STU2 "Block-Vote-5" tranche — a set of ~26
similarly-themed tickets that the Financial Management work group bundled for a
2022-07-12 block vote (announced on Zulip 2022-07-05). Sibling tickets in that
block include FHIR-35389, FHIR-35394, FHIR-35397, FHIR-35400, FHIR-35408,
FHIR-35410, FHIR-35412, FHIR-35416, FHIR-35418, FHIR-35419, FHIR-35420,
FHIR-35421, and FHIR-35422 — every one of them a "Defining URL should be
anchored in THO" change request from Joan Harper against a different C4BB
ValueSet/CodeSystem. In the block vote, FHIR-35423 was originally given the
disposition **"Considered For Future Use"** with the note "Currently in
development for a future version" (vote: Corey Spears / Rachel Foerster, 7-0-2,
2022-07-12). On 2022-11-28 lynn_laakso reverted that resolution and re-opened
the ticket post STU2 publication, which is why it is back in `Triaged` and
appears here for renewed disposition.

There are no explicitly-linked Jira tickets, no related GitHub items, and no
Zulip conversation other than the block-vote announcement itself — so the
substantive deliberation in this ticket's history is the block-vote disposition
and the post-publication re-open.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Move the `C4BBPatientIdentifierType` ValueSet's canonical URL from the C4BB IG
namespace under `http://hl7.org/fhir/us/carin-bb/ValueSet/...` to a THO
canonical (e.g., `http://terminology.hl7.org/ValueSet/C4BBPatientIdentifierType`).
Concretely:

1. File a UTG (Unified Terminology Governance) proposal against
   `HL7/UTG` to register `C4BBPatientIdentifierType` in THO with the new
   canonical, including its compose definition and any supporting
   CodeSystems that are not already in THO.
2. In the C4BB IG repository, retire the in-IG ValueSet definition and
   replace any internal reference to the old canonical with the new THO
   canonical (in profiles that bind to it — most notably the C4BB
   Patient profile's `identifier.type` slicing).
3. Add a deprecation/redirect note in the IG so existing implementers
   can find the new canonical, and bump the IG to STU2.x (or the next
   normative window) where the rebinding is published.

#### Justification

This is what the ticket literally requests and is consistent with HL7's
stated terminology-publishing policy: ValueSets that are not bound
`required` to a `code` data type should live in THO, not in an IG's
canonical namespace. The original 2022-07-12 vote (7-0-2) accepted the
substantive change but deferred it as "Considered for Future Use"
because UTG/THO registration was not yet in place; the 2022-11-28
re-open by lynn_laakso explicitly notes "Working on putting in THO for
future version", indicating the path is now considered viable. Doing it
now (post STU2) aligns C4BB with sibling FM IGs and reduces the long
tail of "in-IG ValueSets that should be in THO" debt.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt the THO re-anchoring **as a coordinated batch** across all
Block-Vote-5 sibling tickets (FHIR-35389, -35394, -35397, -35400, -35408,
-35410, -35412, -35416, -35418, -35419, -35420, -35421, -35422, -35423)
rather than per-ticket. File **one** umbrella UTG submission that
registers all in-scope C4BB ValueSets/CodeSystems in THO, then perform a
single C4BB IG rebinding pass that updates every profile reference in
one PR. Each individual ticket (including FHIR-35423) is dispositioned
**Persuasive with Modification**, pointing at the umbrella work item.

#### Justification

The ticket is one of ~14 effectively-identical "anchor in THO" tickets
that all share the same root cause, the same THO/UTG dependency, the
same authoring root in the C4BB IG, and the same reviewer set. Doing
them one at a time multiplies UTG round-trips, fragments the IG
rebinding into many PRs, and risks inconsistent canonical naming
conventions across the batch. A batched approach also lets the WG
review the **naming pattern** (e.g., `C4BB*` prefix retention, plural
vs. singular ValueSet IDs in THO) once, rather than re-arguing it on
every ticket. Trade-off vs. A: longer wall-clock to first delivery, and
the work is gated on the slowest sibling — but the total cost is
materially lower and the result is more coherent.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Disposition **"Not Persuasive"** — keep `C4BBPatientIdentifierType`
canonical anchored under the C4BB IG namespace
(`http://hl7.org/fhir/us/carin-bb/ValueSet/C4BBPatientIdentifierType`)
as published in STU2. Rationale category: *insufficient incremental
benefit; risk of breaking implementer tooling outweighs alignment
gain*.

#### Justification

C4BB STU2 has been published with the existing canonical in the field;
moving the canonical is a breaking change for any implementer who has
hard-coded the URL in profiles, validators, or reference data. The
ValueSet has limited re-use value outside C4BB (the WG used exactly that
rationale — "Simple ValueSet with little re-use value" — to mark several
sibling tickets in Block-Vote-5 as Not Persuasive). If the canonical is
not actually being reused by other IGs, the THO move is housekeeping
rather than functional improvement, and STU2 implementers pay the
migration cost for no behavioural gain.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The ticket is materially correct — `C4BBPatientIdentifierType` belongs
in THO under HL7's terminology-anchoring policy, and the 2022-11-28
re-open signals that the WG now intends to do the work rather than
defer it again. But FHIR-35423 is not a standalone request: it is one
slice of the Block-Vote-5 "anchor in THO" cluster, and the cost,
review, and risk profile is dominated by the cross-ticket coordination
(one UTG submission, one rebinding PR, one naming convention) rather
than by the per-ValueSet detail. Disposition B preserves the substance
of the original request (it does not decline the change), captures the
real unit of work the WG actually has to plan, and avoids the
fragmented-PR / inconsistent-naming failure mode that Disposition A
invites if applied per-ticket. Disposition C is hard to defend given
the WG explicitly reverted the earlier "future use" deferral; the
direction of travel is clearly *do it*, the only open question is *do
it together or one at a time*.
