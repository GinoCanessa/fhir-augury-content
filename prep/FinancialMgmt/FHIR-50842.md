## Summary

This Change Request asks the Financial Management work group to clarify the
intended scope and boundaries of the FHIR `Invoice` resource in the
specification's narrative ("front matter"). Based on a workgroup discussion
referenced by the reporter, `Invoice` is intended to represent charges for a
**single encounter**, and is **not** intended to be used for cumulative
"statements" (in the United States sense), nor for bills from a payer to an
employer or patient for insurance premiums. The reporter requests that those
boundaries be made explicit in the IG/spec text so implementers do not
overload `Invoice` with use cases better served by other resources. The
single comment on the ticket (from `ekivemark`, 2025-08-19) reinforces this,
suggesting the front matter explain how and why `Invoice` differs from
`Statement`-style aggregations, from `Claim`/`ClaimResponse`, and from
`ExplanationOfBenefit`, and proposing that the topic be carried into an
in-person discussion at the September WGM.

No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were
returned by the augury queries for this ticket, so the request stands on
its own as a documentation/scope-clarification item rather than as part of
a larger thread of work.

## Proposed Dispositions

> Note: No GitHub cross-references were surfaced by augury, so per the

> do not anchor to a specific authoring path. Reviewers may wish to

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a new "Scope and Usage" / "Boundaries and Relationships" subsection
(or expand the existing one) in the `Invoice` resource's narrative
("front matter") in FHIR Core. The text should explicitly state:

1. `Invoice` represents the charges associated with a **single billable
   event** (typically one encounter or one related set of services), not
   a periodic or cumulative aggregation of charges.
2. `Invoice` is **not** a US-style "statement" that rolls up multiple
   prior invoices, balances, payments and adjustments across a billing
   period — that aggregation is out of scope and should be modeled
   elsewhere (e.g., via a future `Statement` resource, an `Account` plus
   a query, or a profile/extension).
3. `Invoice` is **not** intended for **premium billing** between a payer
   and an employer or member, nor for any payer-to-subscriber billing
   for insurance coverage.
4. Cross-link to `Claim` / `ClaimResponse` / `ExplanationOfBenefit` and
   `Account` / `ChargeItem`, calling out the distinction: `Invoice`
   communicates what is owed for delivered services in a non-adjudicated
   context, while `Claim`/`EOB` carry the adjudication conversation with
   a payer and `Account` accumulates postings across time.

No structural (element, cardinality, binding) changes are proposed; this
is a narrative-only change.

#### Justification

The reporter and the commenter agree the gap is one of **specification
clarity**, not of resource structure. FHIR design guidance encourages
each resource to publish a clear scope/boundaries statement so that
implementers do not bend it to neighbouring use cases. The four
clarifications above directly mirror the misuses called out in the
ticket and comment, and stay within FM's purview without touching the
schema, profiles, or terminology — making this a low-risk, high-value
documentation change suitable for the next ballot/publication cycle.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the spirit of the request, but combine the narrative clarification
with a small structural / cross-resource hardening step:

1. Make the same front-matter additions as in Disposition A.
2. In addition, add (or tighten) a "Boundaries with other resources"
   comparison table on the `Invoice` page enumerating: `Invoice`,
   `Account`, `ChargeItem`, `Claim`/`ClaimResponse`,
   `ExplanationOfBenefit`, and a placeholder row for "periodic
   statement" (noting it as not currently modeled).
3. Add a short note to the `Account`, `ChargeItem`, `Claim`, and
   `ExplanationOfBenefit` resource pages cross-linking back to
   `Invoice`'s scope statement, so the boundary is discoverable from
   either side.
4. Open a follow-up tracker (separate Jira) to evaluate whether a
   future `Statement`-style resource or an `Invoice` profile is needed
   for cumulative US billing scenarios, so that this clarification
   doesn't simply close the door on a real implementer need.

#### Justification

Documentation-only fixes on a single resource page often go unnoticed
by implementers reading the related resources. Mirroring the boundary
language on the neighbouring resource pages and adding a comparison
table reduces the chance the same confusion resurfaces under a
different ticket. Spinning a follow-up tracker preserves the option to
serve the cumulative-statement use case later without conflating it
with this clarification. Trade-off vs. A: more pages touched and more
review surface, but a substantially more robust outcome and minimal
implementer burden (no schema changes).

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request on the grounds that the existing `Invoice` resource
narrative, taken together with the published `Account`, `ChargeItem`,
`Claim`, `ClaimResponse`, and `ExplanationOfBenefit` pages, already
implies the intended scope, and that the misuse described is best
addressed in implementation-guide content (e.g., a national IG) rather
than in FHIR Core. Close as "Not Persuasive — addressed by existing
material; out of scope for Core narrative."

#### Justification

This is the weakest of the three options. It would be defensible only if
FM concluded that Core-level narrative is already sufficient and that
adding more text risks over-prescribing US-centric billing semantics
into an international resource. Even so, the burden of proof is on this
disposition to show that implementers can already determine the
boundaries from the current text — and the existence of this ticket
plus the supporting comment is direct evidence that they cannot.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition A solves the immediate ask cleanly and at minimal cost, but
the same misunderstanding (Invoice vs. Statement vs. Claim/EOB vs.
Account/ChargeItem) tends to recur because implementers usually land on
the *neighbouring* resource pages first. Disposition B keeps the
narrative-only, low-risk character of A, adds a comparison table and
reciprocal cross-links so the boundary is discoverable from any of the
involved resources, and explicitly parks the "cumulative statement" use
case in a follow-up tracker so the work group does not implicitly close
the door on it. The commenter's suggestion to take this to an in-person
WGM session fits B well: that meeting can be used to ratify the table
contents and decide whether to spin the follow-up Statement-resource
ticket. Disposition C is not recommended; the ticket itself is evidence
that the current text is insufficient.
