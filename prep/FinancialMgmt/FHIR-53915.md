## Summary

NathanStrohm raises three related concerns about the definitions and
relationships between "benefits" and "costs" in the `InsurancePlan` and
`InsuranceProduct` resources, and argues that both resources likely
need more maturation time before being advanced to normative status.
Specifically, he flags (1) a short-display typo on
`InsurancePlan.generalCost.type` that reads "Classification of
**specific** cost" where it should almost certainly read "Classification
of **general** cost"; (2) confusion about the intended distinction
between `generalCost` and `specificCost` within `InsurancePlan`, noting
that "deductible" appears in both places which muddles the conceptual
split; and (3) confusion about the overall split between `InsurancePlan`
(positioned as carrying "costs") and `InsuranceProduct` (positioned as
carrying "benefits"), given that the "specific costs" on `InsurancePlan`
effectively describe copay/coinsurance behavior that is intrinsically
tied to benefits. No related Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub
items are linked through cross-references; the only cross-references
returned are mentions of the two FHIR element paths above.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat the ticket as three separate fixes against the FHIR Core
`InsurancePlan` and `InsuranceProduct` resources in a single disposition:

1. **Short-display correction:** Change
   `InsurancePlan.generalCost.type` element Short Display from
   "Classification of specific cost" to "Classification of general
   cost". This is a technical correction with no schema impact.
2. **Clarify general vs. specific cost:** Rewrite the element
   definitions for `InsurancePlan.generalCost`,
   `InsurancePlan.specificCost`, and
   `InsurancePlan.specificCost.benefit.cost` so that the intended
   distinction is explicit — e.g., make `generalCost` the
   plan/coverage-level aggregate amounts (deductible maximums, premium,
   out-of-pocket limits) and `specificCost` the per-benefit cost-share
   at point-of-service (copay, coinsurance percentage). Remove or
   re-bind "deductible" from the value set used by
   `InsurancePlan.specificCost.benefit.cost.type` so it no longer
   straddles both buckets. Add a Scope/Usage note with an example.
3. **Clarify InsurancePlan vs. InsuranceProduct split:** Update the
   Scope/Usage section of both resources to move away from the
   "InsurancePlan = costs, InsuranceProduct = benefits" framing and
   instead describe the split as "InsuranceProduct = the benefit
   design/catalog of covered services; InsurancePlan = a marketable
   offering of an InsuranceProduct with cost-share, premium, and
   network/territory bindings". Reword element-level definitions that
   currently echo the old framing.
4. **Maturity:** Do not advance either resource to Normative in the
   next release; keep at Trial Use until these definitional issues are
   settled and re-balloted.

#### Justification

Part 1 is an obvious typo that is demonstrably wrong (the short display
says "specific" on a `generalCost.*` path). Parts 2 and 3 reflect the
fact that the current resource model genuinely does blur costs and
benefits — "deductible" appearing in both `generalCost.type` and
`specificCost.benefit.cost.type` is internally inconsistent, which is
exactly the kind of issue that should block a normative advancement.
Pushing the resources to Normative while their Scope/Usage prose and
element bindings contradict each other would lock in that ambiguity and
be harder to repair later. This disposition also fits the recent
ownership transfer noted in the comments — FM is now the responsible
work group and has the context to resolve the split.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Split the ticket into three tracked items and dispose of them
separately:

- **B1 — Technical correction (accept immediately):** Apply only the
  Short Display fix on `InsurancePlan.generalCost.type`. This is a
  pure editorial change and does not need to wait for the larger
  modeling discussion.
- **B2 — Persuasive, follow-up ticket(s):** Treat the concerns about
  `generalCost` vs. `specificCost` overlap (including the "deductible"
  value-set overlap) and the `InsurancePlan`/`InsuranceProduct` split
  as design issues that require FM discussion and likely a balloted
  connectathon track. Spawn one or two follow-up tickets owned by FM to
  scope the redesign, rather than committing to specific edits from
  inside this ticket.
- **B3 — Maturity hold (persuasive-with-mod):** Explicitly mark both
  resources as "not proposed for Normative in this cycle" pending
  resolution of B2, without committing to any specific wording changes
  yet.

#### Justification

The ticket bundles an obvious editorial fix with two substantive
modeling concerns. Dispatching the typo immediately keeps the spec
honest without holding the fix hostage to a longer design debate.
Forking the modeling concerns into their own tickets gives FM a clean
surface to work on each one — particularly the InsurancePlan vs.
InsuranceProduct split, which was originally PA-owned and only recently
moved to FM (per the comments from corey_spears and egelkraut), so FM
may legitimately need discovery time before committing to a specific
rewrite. It also reduces the risk that a single "accept" on this ticket
implies consensus on changes that the work group has not actually
agreed to.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the ticket as "Not Persuasive — issue is already known and
being tracked through the normative-readiness review of InsurancePlan
and InsuranceProduct". Point the reporter at the existing maturity
review process for the two resources and close FHIR-53915 without
specific edits.

#### Justification

Each of the three sub-concerns in the description is either a
well-known pain point with the `InsurancePlan`/`InsuranceProduct`
pairing or a single editorial typo. A "Decline" disposition is
defensible on the grounds that (a) the typo alone is too small to
justify a full ballot disposition when FM is about to do a broader
review anyway, and (b) restructuring `generalCost` / `specificCost`
and the plan/product boundary is a non-trivial breaking change that
should not be prompted by a single external comment without broader
implementer input. Declining preserves stability until FM can publish a
coherent proposal.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B gives the work group the cleanest path forward. The
Short-Display typo on `InsurancePlan.generalCost.type` is clearly wrong
and should not be held up by the larger modeling debate — it can be
accepted and applied immediately. But the other two concerns
(general-vs-specific cost overlap, and the InsurancePlan /
InsuranceProduct split) are genuine design issues that touch
Scope/Usage, element definitions, and at least one value set; trying to
resolve them inside this single ticket (Disposition A) risks committing
to wording that the newly-owning Financial Management work group has
not fully deliberated. Disposition C understates the concerns — the
"deductible" overlap between `InsurancePlan.generalCost.type` and
`InsurancePlan.specificCost.benefit.cost.type` is an objective
inconsistency, not a stylistic preference, and declining outright would
leave an internally-contradictory spec on track for Normative.
Disposition B accepts the concrete, uncontested fix now and gives FM
structured follow-up tickets for the substantive redesign work, which
aligns with the reporter's closing note that the two resources "likely
need some more time before they are ready to be marked with a
normative status".
