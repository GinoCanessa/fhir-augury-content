## Summary

The reporter (PatShanahan) flagged three small editorial defects in the
PaymentNotice resource page (chapter 16.8) of the FHIR Core specification:

1. **§16.8.1** — "is a 'event' resource" should read "is an 'event' resource"
   (article agreement before a vowel sound).
2. **§16.8.2** — "occurences" is misspelled and should read "occurrences".
3. **§16.8.7** — In the *Requirements* note for `PaymentNotice.recipient`,
   "Need to identified the recipient of the notification." should read
   "Need to identify the recipient of the notification." (verb form).

The ticket is filed as a **Technical Correction** with priority **Medium** and
is currently **Triaged / Unresolved / Unassigned** under **Financial Mgmt** on
**FHIR Core (FHIR)**. There are no linked or cross-referenced Jira tickets, no
Zulip discussions, and no GitHub items. The only cross-reference Augury
surfaces is the implicit mention of the FHIR artifact `PaymentNotice.recipient`
inside the ticket body itself.

## Details

**Description:**

1. In 16.8.1 the last sentence should say "is an 'event' resource," not
   "is a 'event' resource."
   > The PaymentNotice resource is **a** "event" resource from a FHIR workflow
   > perspective - see Workflow Event.

2. In 16.8.2 the word *occurrences* is misspelled in the second sentence.
   > The resource is used commonly when tracking of payment **occurences** is
   > required for regulatory or business purposes, such as ensuring that
   > payment of claim adjudications occurs within a specific timeframe.

3. In 16.8.7, the Requirements for `PaymentNotice.recipient` should say
   "Need to identify…". It currently says "identified."
   > Need to **identified** the recipient of the notification.

No comments have been recorded on the ticket.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the three editorial corrections exactly as filed in the
PaymentNotice resource page:

1. **§16.8.1 (Scope and Usage)** — change
   *"The PaymentNotice resource is a 'event' resource from a FHIR workflow
   perspective"* to
   *"The PaymentNotice resource is an 'event' resource from a FHIR workflow
   perspective"*.
2. **§16.8.2 (Boundaries and Relationships)** — change
   *"tracking of payment occurences"* to
   *"tracking of payment occurrences"*.
3. **§16.8.7 (Element Requirements for `PaymentNotice.recipient`)** — change
   the *Requirements* text from
   *"Need to identified the recipient of the notification."* to
   *"Need to identify the recipient of the notification."*

The first two fixes are in the prose of the PaymentNotice resource intro
page (the chapter 16.8 narrative). The third fix is in the `requirements`
property on the `PaymentNotice.recipient` ElementDefinition (typically
carried in the StructureDefinition for PaymentNotice and rendered into
§16.8.7).

#### Justification

These are unambiguous editorial defects: a grammar slip ("a event"), a
common misspelling ("occurences"), and a verb-form error ("Need to
identified"). All three are surface-level English corrections that do
not change the normative meaning of the resource, do not affect cardinality
or types, and do not affect any conformance behavior. The reporter has
identified the exact section, the exact wording, and the exact desired
correction. Accepting as requested is the lowest-risk and most efficient
resolution and matches HL7's typical handling of *Technical Correction*
tickets.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the three corrections, and at the same time do a short editorial
sweep of the surrounding PaymentNotice prose (§16.8.1, §16.8.2, and the
`requirements` strings for the other PaymentNotice elements in §16.8.7)
to catch any sibling typos, article/agreement issues, or verb-form errors
of the same kind as the three reported here. Track the additional fixes
under this same ticket so the workgroup approves them as a batch
"Technical Correction" rather than spawning follow-up tickets.

#### Justification

The three issues filed sit in three different sections of the same
resource page and were each spotted by a single reader. Tickets of this
shape often indicate that the page has not had a recent editorial pass.
Bundling a quick proofreading sweep into the same change is cheap (the
file is already being touched), avoids the overhead of opening additional
single-typo tickets, and reduces the chance that a second reader will
file a near-duplicate ticket against the same page in the next ballot
window. The risk is that an "editorial sweep" can scope-creep into wording
changes that some reviewers may consider semantic; the scope must be
held to objective grammar/spelling fixes only, which is straightforward
to enforce in review.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket as *Not Persuasive* / *Decline* on the grounds that the
reported issues are minor surface-level typos that do not impact
implementer behavior, conformance, or interoperability, and defer them to
a future general editorial pass rather than processing them as individual
Technical Corrections.

#### Justification

This disposition is defensible only if the workgroup is operating under
a freeze that excludes editorial-only changes, or if the resource is
otherwise off-limits for non-substantive edits in the current cycle. In
those cases, declining and rolling the fixes into the next editorial
window avoids consuming review bandwidth on changes that have no
normative effect. Outside of such a freeze, declining is hard to justify:
the fixes are objectively correct, low-risk, and exactly the kind of
change that the *Technical Correction* type was created to handle.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

These are three clear, narrowly-scoped editorial corrections in the
PaymentNotice page — exactly the situation the *Technical Correction*
ticket type is designed for. The reporter has supplied the precise
wording on both sides of each change, the corrections do not alter any
normative content, and there is no community discussion (no Zulip, no
linked tickets) suggesting any disagreement with the underlying English.
Disposition B is appealing if an editor is willing to take the extra
fifteen minutes to proofread the rest of the page while the file is
open, but it should not block A: if the broader sweep is not feasible in
the current cycle, the three reported fixes should still go in. Disposition
C is not recommended unless an explicit editorial freeze is in force,
which is not indicated by anything on the ticket.
