### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Confirm the punctuation fix and **close FHIR-47894 as Duplicate of
FHIR-47899**, with no further IG change required. The narrative phrase
in the US Da Vinci PDex IG that contains "Member's Health History" is
edited to read "Member's Health History," (comma inserted before the
following clause), as already captured and applied under FHIR-47899
(Resolution: Persuasive, label `Pre-Applied`). Add a closing comment
on FHIR-47894 pointing to FHIR-47899 and confirming that the change is
already in the published / next-publication PDex IG narrative. No code
or profile artifact is touched; this is purely a narrative edit owned
by the PDex IG authoring team.

#### Justification

The ticket is unambiguously editorial — it asks for a single comma in
prose — and the duplicate FHIR-47899 has already been adjudicated
Persuasive by the same work group with a resolution note ("Punctuation
corrected.") that confirms the edit has been made. Closing FHIR-47894
as Duplicate keeps the ballot record consistent (the `PDex-Vote-1`
label stays attached for traceability) and avoids double-counting the
same change in the disposition log.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-47894 as **Not Persuasive – Duplicate** (rather than the
generic "Duplicate" resolution currently set) and additionally use it
as a hook to do a one-pass editorial sweep of the surrounding PDex
narrative paragraph. Concretely: while the comma fix from FHIR-47899
stands, the PDex IG editor takes the opportunity to re-read the
"Member's Health History" sentence in context and silently fix any
adjacent low-risk wording / punctuation issues in the same paragraph
under the existing technical-correction allowance. Document the wider
sweep in the FHIR-47894 close-out comment so reviewers can see the
scope.

#### Justification

The current `Resolution: Duplicate` value loses the editorial intent
of the ticket; classifying it as "Not Persuasive – Duplicate" with a
pointer to FHIR-47899 is more informative for future ballot
reconciliation. Bundling a small editorial sweep with a known-good
fix is cheap and reduces the chance of another `PDex-Vote-1`-style
ticket landing on the same paragraph later. The downside is scope
creep on what is otherwise a one-character change, so any sweep must
stay strictly within technical-correction bounds (no normative
content shifts).

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Reject FHIR-47894 outright as a duplicate that adds no information
beyond FHIR-47899 — i.e., close it without action and without a
"Duplicate of …" link, on the basis that the ballot disposition for
the underlying punctuation request is already recorded against
FHIR-47899.

#### Justification

The ticket carries zero unique content: same reporter, same body,
same target paragraph, filed minutes apart. Treating it as a no-op
keeps the work group's disposition log smaller. The (significant)
downside is loss of traceability for anyone who comes in via the
`PDex-Vote-1` label or the `FHIR-47894` URL and finds no explanation
of why the ticket was closed, which is why this is the weakest
option.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The change requested is a single comma in IG narrative, the duplicate
FHIR-47899 has already been adjudicated Persuasive by the same work
group, and the resolution note confirms the edit has been made. The
cleanest action is to formally close FHIR-47894 as **Duplicate of
FHIR-47899** (the resolution it already carries) with a brief
confirming comment, preserving the `PDex-Vote-1` ballot trail without
expanding scope. Disposition B's editorial sweep is reasonable but
introduces avoidable risk and ownership questions for what is supposed
to be a one-character fix; Disposition C saves a comment but at the
cost of traceability for future reviewers landing on this ticket from
the ballot record.
