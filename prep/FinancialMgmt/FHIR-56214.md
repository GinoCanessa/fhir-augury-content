## Summary

Hans Buitendijk reports that a normative sentence — *"The CRD Client **SHALL** also support 'update' functionality for all resources listed below where the client allows invoking hooks based on the resource."* — is missing from the published [CRD Client USCDI 3 CapabilityStatement (v2.2.1)](https://hl7.org/fhir/us/davinci-crd/2.2.1/en/CapabilityStatement-crd-client6.1.html). The submitter believes the omission was inadvertent and asks for a technical correction to restore the requirement, noting that the SHALL is appropriately scoped (it only applies where the client invokes hooks on the resource and therefore does not force support for unrelated updates). If the workgroup determines this is not a technical correction, the submitter expects the ticket to be reclassified as a Change Request.

The change is closely tied to the explicitly-referenced ticket [FHIR-53605](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53605) ("Update interaction SHALL be supported on supported request resource types"), which was the source of the wording change in the published IG. FHIR-53605's resolution explicitly removed/replaced the older sentence with new language ("CRD Clients SHALL support update requests received via supported system actions, but it is acceptable if clients opt to perform such updates via a mechanism other than their FHIR API.") and downgraded the row-level conformance from SHOULD to MAY. FHIR-56214 was originally closed as *Not Persuasive* on the basis that FHIR-53605 had intentionally introduced replacement language; the submitter pushed back, the resolution was reverted, and the ticket has been reopened for review (proposed for the April 9 Burden Reduction call).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat the omission as a technical correction and restore the original sentence verbatim to the CRD Client capability statement narrative — i.e., re-add *"The CRD Client SHALL also support 'update' functionality for all resources listed below where the client allows invoking hooks based on the resource."* — alongside the FHIR-53605 wording, on `CapabilityStatement-crd-client6.1.html` (and the equivalent non-USCDI CRD Client variants if they share the same omission). Leave the per-row SHOULD→MAY downgrade and the parenthetical from FHIR-53605 in place; the restored sentence is conditional ("where the client allows invoking hooks based on the resource") and therefore does not contradict the new MAY column or impose blanket update support.

#### Justification

The submitter's reading is reasonable: the dropped sentence is scoped to resources actually used by hooks, while FHIR-53605's replacement is scoped to "supported system actions" and explicitly allows non-FHIR mechanisms. They address overlapping but not identical concerns, and removing the hook-scoped SHALL weakens a normative requirement that implementers may have been relying on. Restoring it as a technical correction preserves backward intent without re-opening the design discussion that FHIR-53605 settled.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Reclassify FHIR-56214 as a Change Request (the submitter explicitly offers this fallback) and, instead of restoring the original sentence verbatim, **rewrite the narrative** in the CRD Client capability statement so that a single normative paragraph integrates both intents: "Where a CRD Client invokes hooks based on a given resource type, the client SHALL accept update requests for that resource type via supported system actions; clients MAY satisfy that requirement through a mechanism other than their FHIR API." Apply this in the same `crd-client` CapabilityStatement narrative section, and keep the per-row column at MAY with a footnote that points to the unified paragraph.

#### Justification

The original sentence and the FHIR-53605 replacement are subtly different (hook-scoped vs. system-action-scoped, FHIR-API vs. any mechanism). Restoring the original verbatim (Disposition A) leaves two near-duplicate SHALL statements that implementers will have to reconcile. A unified paragraph keeps FHIR-53605's deliberate broadening (non-FHIR mechanisms are acceptable) while restoring the hook-scoped SHALL the submitter wants. Treating it as a Change Request is also more defensible procedurally because the deletion was the documented outcome of a previously-balloted disposition, not a slip.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Maintain the current published text. Close the ticket as *Not Persuasive*, with the explanation that the removal was deliberate and the FHIR-53605 wording — *"CRD Clients SHALL support update requests received via supported system actions"* combined with the per-row MAY — already covers the requirement the submitter is concerned about, just at a different level of granularity.

#### Justification

The original *Not Persuasive* disposition rests on a defensible reading: FHIR-53605 was reviewed and approved on the Dec. 10 Burden Reduction call and explicitly chose to replace the sentence in question. Re-adding it now risks reintroducing the very ambiguity FHIR-53605 was raised to resolve. Declining is procedurally clean (the change was intentional) and avoids forcing implementers to re-ingest a normative paragraph for a v2.2.x patch.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The submitter has a legitimate concern — the FHIR-53605 replacement language is broader ("supported system actions") and looser ("mechanism other than their FHIR API") than the deleted sentence ("hooks-based" + implicitly the FHIR API), so the deletion does change the normative surface in a way the workgroup may not have fully intended. That said, simply re-pasting the old sentence (Disposition A) leaves two SHALL statements that overlap awkwardly, and Disposition C ignores the submitter's substantive point. A unified rewrite handled as a Change Request (Disposition B) preserves FHIR-53605's deliberate broadening, restores the hook-scoped SHALL the submitter wants, and respects the procedural reality that the deletion was the result of a balloted disposition rather than an editing error. The April 9 Burden Reduction call is the right venue to confirm the rewritten wording before assigning the CR.
