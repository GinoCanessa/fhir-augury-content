## Summary

The ticket reports that the FHIR validator raises errors when the Da Vinci
PDex Prior Authorization `ExplanationOfBenefit` profile is populated with
review-action and denial-reason adjudication extensions at the **header**
(top-level) `ExplanationOfBenefit.adjudication` slice. The reporter argues
that, because the header-level adjudication is optional and the extensions
are intended to convey review-action codes and denial reasons at the claim
level, the validator should not flag their presence as an error. The
submitter wants the spec / validator behavior aligned so that these
extensions are accepted at the header `adjudication` level without raising
validation errors.

The supporting Zulip thread (`#Da Vinci > EOB Header adjudication queries`)
records that Lloyd McKenzie confirmed the underlying issue is partly an R4
`ExplanationOfBenefit` modelling problem (the slicing and extension
contexts make header-level adjudication awkward) and suggested the
discussion move to `#Da Vinci PDex`. The ticket was filed to formalize the
request to suppress / fix the validation error. There are no other Jira
cross-references; the only related artifact recorded is this Zulip thread.

The ticket has since been **resolved as "Not Persuasive"**, with a
resolution note that the underlying need was addressed by a Da Vinci **PAS**
(Prior Authorization Support) update enabling use of these extensions at
the **line** (`item.adjudication`) level rather than at the header.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the Da Vinci PDex `pdex-priorauthorization` `ExplanationOfBenefit`
profile (and any companion guidance) so the review-action extension and
the denial-reason adjudication slice are explicitly permitted at the
header `ExplanationOfBenefit.adjudication` slot, with extension contexts
(or profile-level constraints) adjusted so the FHIR validator no longer
flags their presence at that level. This would include declaring the
extensions' contexts to include `ExplanationOfBenefit.adjudication`
in addition to `ExplanationOfBenefit.item.adjudication`, and amending the
profile's slicing of header-level `adjudication` to recognize a
`denialreason`-style slice analogous to the line-level slicing.

#### Justification

This is the literal ask in the ticket and matches the reporter's mental
model: the header `adjudication` is optional, and authorization decisions
(review action, denial reason) are conceptually claim-level outcomes, so
expressing them at the header is intuitive for implementers building
prior-authorization responses. Aligning extension contexts with that
usage avoids a class of validator errors that PDex implementers have
already hit in the wild (per the Zulip thread).

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — Resolve at the Line Level (the actual resolution)

#### Proposal

Do not change the header-level adjudication contract. Instead, document
in the PDex Prior Authorization profile that review-action and
denial-reason information must be conveyed at the **line level**
(`ExplanationOfBenefit.item.adjudication`), and ensure the companion
Da Vinci **PAS** profile permits those extensions at the line level so
implementers have a sanctioned, validator-clean place to put them.
Add narrative guidance to the PDex profile pointing implementers to the
line-level pattern and flagging that header-level use will continue to
generate validator errors by design. (This is what was actually done:
the resolution notes that PAS was updated to enable use of the
extensions at the line level.)

#### Justification

Lloyd McKenzie's comment in the Zulip thread acknowledges that the
header-level slicing/extension behavior is partly an R4 base-resource
constraint, and changing extension contexts on the header `adjudication`
ripples beyond PDex. Pinning the pattern at the line level keeps the
solution within Da Vinci's IG remit, avoids touching base R4 EOB
modelling, and gives implementers a single canonical location for
review-action/denial-reason data — improving cross-IG consistency
between PDex and PAS without trading one set of validator errors for
another.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as Not Persuasive: the request is to suppress validator errors
that correctly reflect the declared extension contexts and slicing of
the PDex profile and the underlying R4 EOB resource. Implementers
should populate the review-action and denial-reason information at the
slot where the extensions are defined (line-level `item.adjudication`),
not at the header. No spec change is required.

#### Justification

The validator behavior is consistent with the published profile and
extension definitions; the perceived bug is really a usage question.
Da Vinci already provides line-level mechanisms for the same data, so
there is no unmet need that justifies modifying header-level slicing or
extension contexts (a change with non-trivial backward-compatibility
implications across PDex, PAS, CARIN BB, and base R4 EOB consumers).

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The ticket has, in fact, already been resolved as "Not Persuasive" with
the rationale that PAS was updated to enable use of these extensions at
the line level — i.e., the workgroup has effectively executed
Disposition B (line-level pattern + cross-IG alignment), not a pure
decline. Disposition B captures that outcome more honestly than C: it
acknowledges that the reporter's underlying need (a validator-clean
place for review-action and denial-reason data on a Prior Authorization
EOB) was real and was addressed, just not at the header level the
ticket asked for. Disposition A is intuitive for implementers but would
have required changes to extension contexts and profile slicing that
reach further than the originating use case warrants, and Lloyd's
Zulip note that the header-level behavior is partly an R4 base-model
issue makes the blast radius of A genuinely larger than the line-level
fix already shipped via PAS.
