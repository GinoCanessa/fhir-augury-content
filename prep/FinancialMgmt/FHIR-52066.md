## Summary

The ticket flags an internal contradiction on the PDex IG's *Data
Mapping* page. The page first states that Health Plans **SHOULD** map
both claims and clinical information for a member to one of the
supported US Core baselines (3.1.1 / 6.1.0 / 7.0.0). It then says that
where US Core does not define a resource, Health Plans **SHALL** map
claims and clinical information to PDex or HRex profiles. The reporter
points out two consequences: (1) because claims data cannot fully be
represented as clinical US Core resources, the SHALL would mandate
falling back to PDex/HRex profiles for claims and effectively block the
CARIN BB IG mapping that the same page describes lower down; and (2)
because clinical data *can* be mapped to US Core, it is governed only
by the SHOULD in the first sentence, leaving conformance for clinical
data weaker than intended. The ticket asks the WG to spell out the
specific SHALL/SHOULD mappings separately for **claims**, **prior
authorizations**, and **clinical information**.

The ticket has no linked Jira tickets and no related Zulip
discussions surfaced via cross-references or search; the only
cross-references are to the PDex IG repo itself (change-history pages
plus the resolution commit).

## Details

**Description:**

> From the Data Mapping page:
>
> "Health Plans SHOULD map claims and clinical information for a member
> to US Core v3.1.1, US Core v6.1.0 or US Core v7.0.0 FHIR Resources
> based on R4."
>
> "Where a US Core 3.1.1. FHIR R4, US Core 6.1.0. FHIR R4 or US Core
> 7.0.0. FHIR R4 Resource is not defined Health Plans SHALL map claims
> and clinical information to FHIR Profiles defined in this IG, or the
> Da Vinci HRex IG."
>
> Since claims data can't (fully) be represented as clinical US Core
> FHIR resources, the SHALL seems to prevent usage of the CARIN BB IG
> described further down on the page.
>
> This also seems to allow not following US Core for clinical data.
> Since it can be mapped to US Core resources, it falls into the first
> sentence as only a SHOULD.
>
> Can this instead spell out the specific SHALL/SHOULD mappings for
> each of claims, prior authorizations and clinical information?

**Comments:** None on the ticket.

**Resolution (Persuasive, applied 2026-03-17):** Edits to
`input/pagecontent/datamapping.md`, splitting the omnibus
SHOULD/SHALL into per-data-type conformance statements:

| Conformance ID | Before | After |
|---|---|---|
| §pdex-69 | SHOULD map **claims and clinical** to US Core | SHALL map **clinical information** to US Core |
| §pdex-69a | *(new)* | SHALL map **claims/encounters** to CARIN BB non-financial EoB profiles |
| §pdex-69b | *(new)* | SHALL map **prior authorizations** to PDex Prior Authorization profile |
| §pdex-71 | fallback for "claims and clinical" | fallback for **clinical data** only |

The resolution notes this aligns with the existing CARIN BB content
already on the page and removes the ambiguity that previously allowed
treating clinical data as merely SHOULD while blocking CARIN BB usage
for claims.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the per-data-type conformance split exactly as captured in the
ticket's resolution, by editing
`input/pagecontent/datamapping.md` in `HL7/davinci-epdx`:

- **§pdex-69 (rewritten):** "Health Plans SHALL map *clinical
  information* for a member to US Core v3.1.1, US Core v6.1.0, **or**
  US Core v7.0.0 FHIR R4 Resources." (SHOULD → SHALL; scope narrowed
  to clinical only.)
- **§pdex-69a (new):** "Health Plans SHALL map *claims/encounters* for
  a member to the CARIN Consumer Directed Payer Data Exchange (CARIN
  BB) non-financial ExplanationOfBenefit profiles."
- **§pdex-69b (new):** "Health Plans SHALL map *prior authorizations*
  for a member to the PDex Prior Authorization profile."
- **§pdex-71 (rewritten):** restrict the existing PDex/HRex fallback
  to *clinical data* only — i.e., where a US Core resource is not
  defined for the clinical concept, SHALL map to a profile defined in
  this IG or in HRex. Claims and PA fallbacks are no longer covered
  here because §pdex-69a / §pdex-69b are now unconditional SHALLs.

Update `Requirements-fromNarrative.json` and
`conformance_statements.csv` (or re-run `process_conformance.py`) so
the new §pdex-69a / §pdex-69b appear in the generated conformance
table, and add a `changehistory.md` entry under the current
publication branch.

#### Justification

This is the literal change the reporter asked for and the resolution
already on file. It (1) eliminates the contradiction with the
existing CARIN BB content lower on the same Data Mapping page, (2)
strengthens clinical conformance from SHOULD to SHALL — matching the
intent of a USCDI-based exchange — and (3) makes the prior-auth
mapping, which has its own dedicated PDex profile, explicit rather
than implicit. The change is narrative-only in `input/pagecontent/`
and does not touch any FSH artifact, so the IG-publisher build risk
is minimal. The triple US Core pinning in `sushi-config.yaml` is
respected by listing all three baselines in §pdex-69, and the HRex
fallback called out in the briefing's cross-repo touch points is
preserved in the rewritten §pdex-71.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the per-data-type *split* from Disposition A, but tier the
strength of the new claims and prior-authorization clauses by data
availability rather than making them blanket SHALLs:

- **§pdex-69:** SHALL map *clinical information* to one of the pinned
  US Core baselines (as in A).
- **§pdex-69a:** "Health Plans **SHALL**, *where claims/encounter
  data is exchanged via PDex*, map that data to the CARIN BB
  non-financial ExplanationOfBenefit profiles; Health Plans **SHOULD**
  expose claims/encounter data through PDex when available." (Splits
  the *if you do it, do it this way* SHALL from the *should you do it
  at all* SHOULD.)
- **§pdex-69b:** Same conditional pattern for prior authorizations
  against the PDex Prior Authorization profile.
- **§pdex-71:** Clinical-data-only fallback as in A.

#### Justification

A blanket SHALL on claims and prior-auth mapping (Disposition A) reads
as a new mandate that every PDex-conformant Health Plan must surface
claims and PA data through CARIN BB / PDex PA profiles, even where
the use case (e.g. a clinical-data-only member-history exchange) does
not require those payloads. The conditional phrasing keeps the
*format* mandatory (which is the actual interoperability win — no
ad-hoc claims shapes) without inadvertently expanding the *scope* of
PDex obligations. This is also gentler on implementers running
older USCDI v1 / v3 deployments via `uscore3` / `uscore6` who may not
yet have CARIN BB or PDex PA pipelines stood up.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the rewrite. Keep the existing two sentences in
`datamapping.md` as-is (SHOULD for US Core mapping of "claims and
clinical", SHALL for falling back to PDex/HRex profiles where US Core
is not defined), and instead add a short clarifying paragraph to the
same page that says, in narrative, "where claims data is mapped, the
CARIN BB profiles described below are the expected target", relying
on the existing CARIN BB section further down the page.

#### Justification

Defensible only on minimum-disruption grounds: the original wording
has been ballot-tested across STU 1 / STU 2 publications and changing
SHOULD → SHALL for clinical data and introducing two new SHALLs is a
substantive conformance change that some Health Plans may treat as
breaking. A narrative-only clarification avoids minting new
conformance IDs (§pdex-69a / §pdex-69b) that downstream test suites
and the conformance table generated by `process_conformance.py` would
need to track.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket has already been adjudicated **Persuasive** and the
resolution commit (`93b59c8` on `HL7/davinci-epdx`) has been applied
in the pre-publish branch — Disposition A is, in effect, ratifying
what the WG has already decided and what the next PDex publication
will carry. The reporter's diagnosis is correct: the present wording
both blocks the CARIN BB pathway that the same page advertises and
weakens clinical conformance to a SHOULD, which contradicts the
USCDI-aligned intent of PDex. Disposition B's conditional phrasing is
worth a sanity check during ballot review — particularly the §pdex-69a
wording — but the underlying structural change (per-data-type
conformance with SHALLs anchored to specific profiles) is the right
shape and matches the briefing's cross-repo touch points (CARIN BB,
US Core triple-pin, HRex fallback). Disposition C preserves the
ambiguity the ticket was raised to fix and is not recommended.
