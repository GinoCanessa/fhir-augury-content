## Summary

The reporter (Celine Lefebvre) asks the Da Vinci PAS IG to spell out a way
for payers to communicate the reason for a prior-authorization decision
**in the form of a letter** — citing Michigan Medicaid as a state/payer
that requires this. Two concrete suggestions are made: (1) attach a PDF
via a `CommunicationReference` (likely intended to mean a
`DocumentReference`/`Communication` attachment) on the response, and/or
(2) carry a short free-text explanation in `ClaimResponse` "process
note". The submitter requests advance notice of when the topic is
discussed.

The ticket is currently labeled **Block-vote-22** and has been bounced
back into Triaged twice: first reverted from a 2024-08-13 "Considered
for Future Use" disposition (vote 8-0-5) so it could be re-opened post
STU2.1, and again reverted from a 2025-10-21 "Considered for Future
Use" disposition (vote 10-0-0) so it could be re-opened post STU2.2.
Both reverted dispositions used the same rationale: the X12 278
response does not natively support the capability (other than possibly
the PWK segment), so any change would only benefit payers using the
enforcement grant — and the WG suggested CDex or PDex as the more
natural home for this exchange in a future version.

There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets and no related GitHub items.
Two Zulip threads in `Da Vinci PAS` reference this ticket directly:
*Sending Auth Letters in ClaimResponse* (where the use case is restated
by an implementer and Lloyd McKenzie reiterates the X12 round-trip
constraint) and *Block Vote 22* (where Hans Buitendijk argues that
"Considered for Future Use" is more appropriate than "Not Persuasive"
because the venue question — PAS vs. CDex vs. PDex — is unresolved).
A related but non-linked CDex tracker, **FHIR-46630**, is mentioned in
the Zulip thread as covering similar ground and being on track for
closure due to no identified regulatory requirement.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Extend the Da Vinci PAS `ClaimResponse` profile to formally support
conveying a decision letter to the provider, by both:

1. Profiling `ClaimResponse.processNote.text` (and adding guidance on
   `processNote.type`/`processNote.language`) so payers may convey a
   short free-text explanation of the decision reason — explicitly
   permitted and described in the IG's narrative.
2. Slicing/profiling `ClaimResponse.communicationRequest` (or, more
   appropriately, adding an extension on `ClaimResponse` that
   references a `DocumentReference` whose `content.attachment` is a
   PDF of the decision letter), with guidance that the attachment
   carries the human-readable approval or denial letter required by
   states/payers such as Michigan Medicaid.

The IG narrative would spell out exactly which capability satisfies
which obligation, and the X12 mapping section would explicitly state
that the PDF letter has no native 278 round-trip (optionally referencing
the PWK segment as a partial carrier where applicable).

#### Justification

The reporter's underlying need is real and well-evidenced by the Zulip
thread: implementers are doing this today through custom integrations,
which is exactly the administrative-burden problem PAS exists to
reduce. `ClaimResponse.processNote` already exists in the base resource
for short payer narrative, so the free-text part is a near-zero-cost
addition. The PDF-attachment part is structurally feasible via a
`DocumentReference`-typed extension, mirroring patterns already used
elsewhere in Da Vinci IGs.

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — Accept the process-note guidance only; defer the PDF to CDex/PDex

#### Proposal

Within the PAS IG, accept only the lighter-weight half of the request:
add explicit IG guidance and (if needed) a profile constraint clarifying
that `ClaimResponse.processNote.text` may be used to carry a short
free-text decision reason, with examples and a note that this is not
intended to substitute for a full decision letter.

For the PDF/letter half, do **not** modify PAS. Instead, document in
the PAS IG that decision letters are out of scope for the X12-bridged
PAS exchange and direct implementers to use **CDex** (now used in the
payer→provider direction) or **PDex** to convey the document, and
coordinate with those IGs (and FHIR-46630's home WG) on a
`DocumentReference`-based pattern keyed by claim/auth identifier.

#### Justification

This is the position the WG has actually landed on twice (8-0-5 and
10-0-0 votes for "Considered for Future Use"), and it directly
addresses Lloyd McKenzie's hard constraint on the Zulip thread: PAS
content must be expressible over X12, and there is no agreed X12
carrier for a PDF letter. Splitting the request — accepting the
in-scope, X12-compatible piece (`processNote`) while deferring the
out-of-scope piece (PDF letter) to the IGs designed for general
payer↔provider document exchange — gives the reporter a partial,
implementable answer immediately and avoids inventing a PAS-only
mechanism that would diverge from X12. It is also consistent with Hans
Buitendijk's Block Vote 22 feedback that the venue question is not yet
settled and "Not Persuasive" overstates the WG's position.

### Disposition C: Decline (Considered for Future Use)

#### Proposal

Resolve as **Considered for Future Use**, with the disposition text
explicitly stating: (a) PAS content must be expressible over X12 and
the 278 response has no native carrier for a PDF decision letter
(beyond the limited PWK segment); (b) the use case is acknowledged but
the appropriate venue (PAS extension, CDex, PDex, or a future
FHIR-only PA guide) is unresolved; (c) implementers needing this today
should track FHIR-46630 (CDex) and the broader Burden Reduction
discussion. No spec changes in this publication cycle.

#### Justification

This is the disposition the WG has voted for twice (2024-08-13 and
2025-10-21), each time by a clear margin. The technical rationale —
no X12 round-trip — has not changed between the two votes and was
reaffirmed by Lloyd McKenzie on the Zulip thread in September 2025.
The only reason the ticket keeps getting reopened is post-publication
re-triage, not new technical or community evidence. CDex has already
investigated a parallel tracker (FHIR-46630) and could find no
regulatory driver. Declining (in the "future use" sense, not "not
persuasive") is defensible and preserves the option to revisit when
either an X12 carrier is agreed or a FHIR-only PA path is opened.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the right balance. The WG has correctly identified,
twice, that the *full* request cannot land in PAS today — the X12
round-trip constraint is real and unchanged — which makes a pure
"Accept" (Disposition A) infeasible without breaking the IG's
foundational invariant. But Hans Buitendijk's Block Vote 22 feedback is
also correct: simply re-resolving as Considered for Future Use a third
time gives the reporter and implementers (e.g., Alexander Longo's
team) nothing actionable, and the ticket will keep coming back. Carving
off the `processNote` free-text guidance — which *is* X12-expressible
and costs the IG almost nothing — gives an immediate, implementable
answer to part of Michigan Medicaid's stated need, while explicitly
routing the PDF/letter half to CDex/PDex alongside FHIR-46630 closes
the venue question instead of leaving it open. If the Burden Reduction
call disagrees and prefers C, the disposition text should at minimum
incorporate the CDex/PDex pointer so the next reopen has somewhere to
go.
