## Summary

FHIR-53311 proposes that the Da Vinci PDex Provider Access API stop relying on
pre-built attribution `Group` resources and instead drive member attribution
through a Bulk Member Match flow analogous to the Payer-to-Payer API. Large
plans report that maintaining attribution `Group` resources is operationally
costly because rosters are dynamic and very large. Under the proposal a
provider would attest to a treatment relationship via a `Consent`, the plan
would consult an Opt-Out `Group` and either a Member-Provider Treatment
Relationship `Group` (or an internal API equivalent) to verify scope, and the
Bulk Member Match would emit a short-lived (suggested 30-day) Matched Member
`Group` whose `id` is then used in `/Group/{id}/$davinci-data-export`. This
would also enable per-Practitioner / Location / Organization granularity if
the plan chooses.

The ticket is already resolved **Persuasive** and marked **Pre-Applied**; the
resolution note states "This was the original ticket that covered the
creation of Provider Access v2." There are no cross-referenced sibling Jira
tickets in the index, but the change has been recorded in the
`HL7/davinci-epdx` IG via commit
[`e3f1f06`](https://github.com/HL7/davinci-epdx/commit/e3f1f065e718787fc7ddc39fd9e5e88a183cf9ea)
("Add FHIR-53311 to changehistory as originating ticket for Provider Access
v2") and is referenced in the IG's changehistory pages. Two open implementer
concerns surfaced in comments and parallel Zulip threads: (1) whether the
plan's verification of the treatment relationship should be **MAY** vs.
**SHALL** given that CMS-0057-F is non-prescriptive about attribution
mechanisms, and (2) the exact output format of `$provider-member-match` and
how single-member ("Payer-to-Payer Single Member") flows interact with the
new bulk-match-based Provider Access v2.

## Details

**Description:**

> Maintaining Attribution Lists using the Group resources are problematic for
> Large health plans. The groups are dynamic and can be extremely large.
>
> The proposal is to switch to using the Bulk member match as a first step
> enabling the health plan to check for:
> - Provider being in-network
> - Has Member opted-out
> - Is there a treatment relationship between member and provider.
>
> Proposed changes:
> - Provider includes an "attestation to a Treatment Relationship with
>   Patient" consent resource. No requirement for member consent.
> - Plan can maintain an "Opt-Out" Group for members that have chosen to
>   opt-out of Provider Access data sharing.
> - Plan can maintain a Member-Provider Treatment Relationship Group, where
>   the member is the key and providers, Location, Organization are the
>   cohort. Alternatively a plan could choose to use an API to their internal
>   systems to confirm the Treatment Relationship.
> - By maintaining a Member-Provider Treatment Relationship group this would
>   enable plans to offer granular permission at the Practitioner, Location
>   or Organization level if they wished to do so.
>
> The Bulk Member match would instantiate a Matched Member Group in the FHIR
> API. It is suggested that this group resource is valid for 30 days. Upon
> receiving the reference to the Matched Member Group the Provider would
> initiate a `/Group/{id}/$davinci-data-export`.
>
> This process is closely aligned with the Payer-to-Payer API which performs
> a bulk member match before performing a `$davinci-data-export`.

**Resolution Description:** "This was the original ticket that covered the
creation of Provider Access v2."

**Comments:**

- **JIRAUSER17907 (2026-02-16)** — Thanks the WG for adding provider member
  match support. Notes that in the 2025-02-28 PDex workgroup call CMS-0057-F
  was characterized as **not prescriptive** about attribution mechanics, that
  Provider Access is restricted to HIPAA covered entities (no clearinghouses
  / vendors), and that HIPAA already permits PHI sharing for treatment,
  payment, and operations. Recommends that plan-side **confirmation** of the
  treatment relationship be **OPTIONAL** ("MAY"), with provider attestation /
  consent treated as sufficient for compliance. Cites HHS Privacy Rule
  (45 CFR Part 164) and the
  [2025-02-28 PDex ATR Implementer Support Meeting](https://confluence.hl7.org/spaces/DVP/pages/325453438/2025-02-28+Payer+Data+Exchange+ATR+Implementer+Support+Meeting)
  on Confluence.

- **michelemottini (2026-03-27)** — Counterpoint: the regulation **does
  require** payers to verify attribution. Cites the CMS-0057-F preamble
  (Federal Register 2024-00895), specifically paragraphs
  [#p-718](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2024/02/08/2024-00895/medicare-and-medicaid-programs-patient-protection-and-affordable-care-act-advancing-interoperability#p-718)
  and
  [#p-2220](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2024/02/08/2024-00895/medicare-and-medicaid-programs-patient-protection-and-affordable-care-act-advancing-interoperability#p-2220).

- **JIRAUSER17907 (2026-03-31)** — Quotes CMS-0057-F directly: payers "are
  certainly permitted to utilize a provider attestation as part of their
  attribution process", "there are multiple ways for impacted payers to
  verify a treatment relationship", and CMS's own DPC pilot for Medicare FFS
  uses per-patient attestation submitted with each roster-add request.
  Reinforces the position that attestation is an acceptable mechanism even if
  some form of plan-side check is still required.

## Proposed Dispositions

> Note for the workgroup: the ticket is already resolved **Persuasive** and
> labelled **Pre-Applied** — Provider Access v2 with bulk member match has
> landed in `HL7/davinci-epdx` (commit `e3f1f06`, IG version `2.2.0`). The
> three dispositions below frame the decision the WG still needs to ratify:
> the **normative strength** of the plan-side treatment-relationship check
> and the precise contract of `$provider-member-match` / Matched Member
> Group, both of which were raised in comments and Zulip after the change
> was applied.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Ratify the Pre-Applied Provider Access v2 design exactly as it sits today
in `HL7/davinci-epdx` (`input/fsh/Operation-provider-member-match.fsh`,
`input/fsh/pdex-provider-access-server-capability-statement.fsh`, the
Matched Member / Opt-Out / Treatment-Relationship `Group` profiles, and
`input/pagecontent/changehistory.md`). Keep the workflow as: provider
attests to the treatment relationship via a `Consent` resource (no member
consent required), the plan **SHALL** check Opt-Out and Member-Provider
Treatment Relationship `Group`s (or an internal-API equivalent), the plan
**SHALL** instantiate a Matched Member `Group` valid for 30 days, and the
provider invokes `/Group/{id}/$davinci-data-export` against that group.
Update only `input/pagecontent/changehistory.md` to confirm the ticket is
applied and (if the IG version is bumped) `FHIR-us-davinci-pdex.xml` plus
`package-list.yml`.

#### Justification

The change has already been implemented, exercised at the March
connectathon (CareEvolution server thread), and aligns the Provider
Access flow with the Payer-to-Payer pattern, materially reducing
maintenance burden for large plans (the original problem statement). The
SHALL-level plan check matches michelemottini's reading of CMS-0057-F
preamble paragraphs #p-718 and #p-2220 that payers are required to verify
attribution, and it preserves a single, deterministic interop contract
across implementers — important when only one reference server has been
shown to interoperate so far.

---

### Disposition B: Accept with the Treatment-Relationship Check as MAY

#### Proposal

Adopt the Pre-Applied Provider Access v2 design from Disposition A, but
relax the conformance language so that plan-side verification of the
treatment relationship via the Member-Provider Treatment Relationship
`Group` is **MAY**, with provider attestation (the `Consent` resource)
treated as sufficient for compliance. Concretely:

- In `input/pagecontent/` (Provider Access v2 page) and in
  `pdex-provider-access-server-capability-statement.fsh`, downgrade the
  conformance verb on the treatment-relationship check from SHALL to MAY,
  while keeping the Opt-Out check as SHALL.
- Tighten the `Consent`-based attestation profile to require provider
  identity, patient identity, the attested purpose-of-use, and an
  attestation timestamp, so the provider-side obligation is unambiguous.
- Also resolve the `$provider-member-match` output-format question
  (Zulip "Provider member match output format" thread) by explicitly
  documenting whether the operation returns a `Parameters` referencing
  the Matched Member Group, an async bulk-style response, or both — and
  reflect that in `Operation-provider-member-match.fsh`.

#### Justification

The 2025-02-28 PDex ATR Implementer Support Meeting and JIRAUSER17907's
direct CMS-0057-F quotations document that CMS describes attribution as
non-prescriptive ("multiple ways … to verify a treatment relationship"
and DPC's per-roster-add attestation as exemplar). Treating attestation
as sufficient lowers the implementation barrier for plans that don't
already maintain a member-provider relationship store, while keeping
HIPAA's covered-entity scope and the Opt-Out check intact. Bundling the
output-format clarification in the same disposition closes the most
visible open implementer question without re-opening the bulk-match
direction.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (Revert / Defer Provider Access v2)

#### Proposal

Decline the proposed switch and revert the Pre-Applied changes — restore
the prior Provider Access design that uses pre-coordinated attribution
`Group`s, and remove or defer the new Matched Member `Group` flow,
`$provider-member-match` operation, and associated Opt-Out / Treatment
Relationship `Group` profiles from
`HL7/davinci-epdx` (rolling back the relevant `input/fsh/` artifacts and
`input/pagecontent/` narrative until a future STU). Keep the historical
versions registered in `FHIR-us-davinci-pdex.xml` and `package-list.yml`
(versions cannot be removed once published per the README), but mark
2.2.0 as deprecated.

#### Justification

This option is included for completeness; it would be defensible only if
the workgroup concludes that the regulatory ambiguity around plan-side
attribution checks (michelemottini vs. JIRAUSER17907) cannot be resolved
in time for the STU 2.2 ballot, that single-member scoping behavior
(Zulip "Payer to Payer Single Member" thread) and `$provider-member-match`
output format (Zulip "Provider member match output format" thread) are
unresolved enough to risk non-interoperable implementations, and that
maintaining the existing attribution-Group flow — despite its operational
cost for large plans — is preferable to shipping an underspecified bulk
flow.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **B**

The bulk-member-match direction is sound, has running code in the IG and
at least one connectathon-tested server, and demonstrably solves the
operational problem the reporter raised — so reverting (Disposition C) is
not justified. The substantive open question is the normative strength of
the plan-side treatment-relationship check, and the explicit CMS-0057-F
language quoted by JIRAUSER17907 ("payers are certainly permitted to
utilize a provider attestation as part of their attribution process";
"there are multiple ways … to verify a treatment relationship") supports
treating that check as MAY rather than SHALL, especially given HIPAA's
existing TPO authorization for covered entities. Disposition B keeps the
SHALL-level Opt-Out check (the consumer-protection floor that *is*
clearly required), tightens the attestation `Consent` so the obligation
is enforceable on the provider side, and uses the same review cycle to
close the `$provider-member-match` output-format gap surfaced in Zulip —
which the Repo Context confirms is a small, localized edit
(`Operation-provider-member-match.fsh` plus the Provider Access v2
narrative). It is the lowest-risk path to a balloteable v2 that holds
both regulatory readings in tension without overconstraining plans.
