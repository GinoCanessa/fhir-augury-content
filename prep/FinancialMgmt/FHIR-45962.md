## Summary

Lloyd McKenzie reports that insurance payments are sometimes made directly to
the patient (e.g., to reimburse out-of-pocket spend) or, when the beneficiary
is a minor, to a relative such as a parent or guardian. Today the
`PaymentNotice` resource cannot represent these situations because its `payee`
and `recipient` references do not allow `Patient` or `RelatedPerson`. The
reporter notes that an implementer who only wants to enumerate payments that
have been made should be able to use `PaymentNotice` for that purpose without
having to construct a full `ExplanationOfBenefit`.

The Financial Management work group has triaged the request as **Persuasive
with Modification** and proposed a concrete set of changes on `PaymentNotice`:
add `Patient` and `RelatedPerson` to the allowed reference types on both
`payee` and `recipient`; add a new `patient 0..1 Reference(Patient)` element
to identify the subject of care; add a new `claimIdentifier 0..* Identifier`
element to carry the business identifier of the claim being paid; and add
matching search parameters (`payee`, `recipient`, `patient`,
`claimIdentifier`). The ticket is labelled `FM-R5+_ToBeApplied`, indicating
the change is queued for application in the next R5+ release. There are no
explicit Jira links and no cross-references in the augury index, but a number
of historical FM tickets (see *Related Jira Tickets*) cover the same theme of
broadening payee references on financial resources.

## Details

**Description:**

> Insurance payments may be made direct to the patient to reimburse for money
> already expended. In the situation where the beneficiary is a minor, payment
> may be made to a relative. If all I want to search for is a list of payments
> made, I shouldn't need a full-blown EOB.

**Comments:**

- **pknapp — 2025-01-30:** Pings Lloyd McKenzie to confirm that the proposed
  changes (adding `Patient`/`RelatedPerson` to `payee` and `recipient`, plus
  the new `patient` and `claimIdentifier` elements and search parameters) meet
  the original need.

**Resolution Description (from work group):**

To permit `PaymentNotice` to be used to document payments made to persons, the
following changes are required:

- `payee` — add `Patient` and `RelatedPerson`.
- `recipient` — add `Patient` and `RelatedPerson`.
- Add `patient 0..1 Reference(Patient)` — *Subject of care*.
- Add `claimIdentifier 0..* Identifier` — *Business identifier of the claim*.
- Search parameters: `payee`, `recipient`, `patient`, `claimIdentifier`.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the FM-proposed change set on `PaymentNotice` exactly as written in the
resolution:

- `PaymentNotice.payee`: extend the allowed reference types to include
  `Patient` and `RelatedPerson` (in addition to the existing
  `Practitioner|PractitionerRole|Organization`).
- `PaymentNotice.recipient`: same addition of `Patient` and `RelatedPerson`.
- Add `PaymentNotice.patient : 0..1 Reference(Patient)` — short
  "Subject of care".
- Add `PaymentNotice.claimIdentifier : 0..* Identifier` — short "Business
  identifier of the claim".
- Add SearchParameters `PaymentNotice-payee`, `PaymentNotice-recipient`,
  `PaymentNotice-patient`, `PaymentNotice-claimIdentifier` (token /
  reference as appropriate), so implementers can list payments by patient or
  by underlying claim identifier without dereferencing an `EOB`.

This is the change set the FM work group has already triaged as Persuasive
with Modification under the `FM-R5+_ToBeApplied` label.

#### Justification

The ticket identifies a concrete real-world workflow — direct-to-patient
reimbursement and payments to a related person on behalf of a minor — that
`PaymentNotice` cannot represent today. Disposition A keeps the change minimal
and consistent with how FM has handled the same gap on neighbouring resources:
FHIR-37740 and FHIR-31699 add `RelatedPerson` to `EOB`/`Claim.payee`, and
FHIR-26601 broadens `PaymentReconciliation` to first-class patient payments.
Adding `patient` and `claimIdentifier` plus matching search parameters
directly satisfies Lloyd's stated motivation ("if all I want to search for is
a list of payments made, I shouldn't need a full-blown EOB"). The R5+ label
shows the work group has already accepted the trade-offs and queued the
change for application.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt the same semantic change but trim the surface area:

- Still add `Patient` and `RelatedPerson` to `PaymentNotice.payee` and
  `PaymentNotice.recipient`.
- Replace the new `claimIdentifier 0..* Identifier` element with a documented
  use of `PaymentNotice.request.identifier` (the existing logical reference
  pattern: `Reference(Claim).identifier`) so the business identifier of the
  claim is carried without introducing a parallel element. Define a search
  parameter on `request.identifier` (e.g., `claim-identifier`) instead.
- Keep the new `patient 0..1 Reference(Patient)` element and its search
  parameter, since there is no existing element on `PaymentNotice` that
  unambiguously identifies the subject of care independent of who was paid.

#### Justification

This narrows the resource footprint while still solving the use case. Carrying
the claim's business identifier through the existing
`PaymentNotice.request` logical reference is consistent with how other FHIR
resources expose external identifiers via `Reference.identifier`, and avoids
having two places (`request.identifier` vs. `claimIdentifier`) where the same
information could live and disagree. It also preserves the most important
search ergonomics Lloyd asked for (search by patient and by claim identifier)
without committing to a brand-new repeating `Identifier` element that
implementers would have to populate alongside `request`. The trade-off versus
A is a slightly less direct authoring experience for senders that want to
emit an identifier without constructing a `Reference`, and a search parameter
expressed against a logical reference rather than a top-level element.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the requested broadening of `PaymentNotice.payee`/`recipient` and the
new `patient` / `claimIdentifier` elements. Rationale category: *already
addressed by an existing mechanism* — implementers who need to track
payments made to a patient or related person should use
`PaymentReconciliation` (now broadened by FHIR-26601 to cover patient-
originated and patient-directed payments) and `ExplanationOfBenefit` for
adjudicated reimbursements, both of which already carry richer payee and
patient context.

#### Justification

`PaymentNotice` is intentionally a lightweight notification resource. Adding
`Patient`/`RelatedPerson` to `payee` and `recipient`, plus `patient` and a
repeating `claimIdentifier`, expands its scope toward the territory that
`PaymentReconciliation` and `EOB` already occupy and creates two ways to
express the same information. The reporter's stated need ("a list of
payments made") can be met by searching `PaymentReconciliation` once
FHIR-26601 has landed, without changing `PaymentNotice` at all. Declining
preserves the resource boundary that FM has historically maintained.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The work group has already triaged FHIR-45962 as Persuasive with Modification
and labelled it `FM-R5+_ToBeApplied`, which is a strong signal that the
proposed change set has cleared FM's internal design review. The change is
consistent with the direction set by FHIR-37740 and FHIR-31699 (broaden payee
references to include `Patient`/`RelatedPerson` on financial resources) and
complements the broader patient-payment work in FHIR-26601 on
`PaymentReconciliation`. Disposition C under-serves the real workflow Lloyd
described — direct-to-patient reimbursement and payments to a relative on
behalf of a minor — and forces implementers into much heavier resources for a
genuinely lightweight notification use case. Disposition B is defensible and
worth a brief discussion at the call (the `claimIdentifier` vs.
`request.identifier` redundancy is a real concern), but unless the work group
sees concrete implementer pushback, applying the already-agreed change set as
written (Disposition A) is the cleanest path to clearing the
`FM-R5+_ToBeApplied` queue.
