## Summary

FHIR-47896 is a small technical-correction Change Request against the **US
Da Vinci PDex (FHIR)** implementation guide. The submitter (Jenny Couse,
with a noted import from Pradeep Podila — "Comment 5") reports that the
current narrative reads "In Patient Care Provider queries." and proposes
that it be replaced with "Inpatient." The change is a single-word
typographical / wording fix that joins "In Patient" into the standard
clinical term **"Inpatient"** in the context of Care Provider queries
within PDex. The ticket is already flagged `Pre-Applied` and tracked under
`PDex-Vote-1`, with a resolution of **Persuasive** ("correction
applied."), indicating the PDex authors have already accepted the wording
change pending workgroup confirmation. There are no cross-referenced
Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items associated with this
ticket — it is a self-contained editorial correction.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the wording change exactly as proposed: replace the phrase **"In
Patient Care Provider queries."** with **"Inpatient"** (joining "In
Patient" into the single word "Inpatient") wherever it appears in the
US Da Vinci PDex narrative covering Care Provider queries. This matches
the `Pre-Applied` label and the resolution note ("correction applied."),
and aligns the prose with standard clinical terminology where
**Inpatient** is the canonical adjective. No conformance content
(StructureDefinitions, ValueSets, CapabilityStatements, search
parameters) is affected; the change is purely narrative.

#### Justification

The existing wording mis-tokenizes "Inpatient" as two words ("In
Patient"), which is grammatically incorrect in the surrounding context
and inconsistent with how the term is used throughout US realm
encounter / healthcare terminology (e.g., `v3-ActCode#IMP`,
`encounter-class` codes, Da Vinci PDex Plan-Net usage). The author has
already vetted the change via PDex's `PDex-Vote-1` review batch, the
resolution is **Persuasive**, and the label `Pre-Applied` indicates the
correction has been incorporated in the working copy. Accepting matches
the FHIR maintenance practice of confirming pre-applied editorial fixes
without re-litigation.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the intent of the correction but, before closing, have the PDex
editors do a one-pass scan of the IG narrative for any other occurrences
of "In Patient" that should likewise read "Inpatient" (and conversely,
any legitimate uses of "in-patient" or "Patient" alone that should be
preserved). Roll any additional wording fixes uncovered by that pass
into this ticket's resolution rather than opening separate trackers, so
the editorial state of the IG is consistent in a single edit.

#### Justification

Tickets sourced from imported comments often surface a single instance
of a broader copy-edit issue. Bundling the sweep with the already
`Pre-Applied` fix avoids fragmenting the change history, keeps the
narrative internally consistent, and costs the editor only a quick
search across the IG sources. The trade-off versus Disposition A is a
slightly larger editorial diff in exchange for fewer follow-up
typo-fix tickets later in the ballot cycle.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change and leave the existing wording ("In Patient Care
Provider queries.") in place, on the grounds that it is purely
narrative and not material to interoperability or conformance.

#### Justification

This is the weakest of the three options because the existing wording
is in fact ungrammatical in context — "Inpatient" is a single English
word — and the workgroup has already accepted the correction
(`Pre-Applied`, resolution **Persuasive**). Declining would leave a
known editorial defect in a balloted IG without a defensible reason and
would contradict the workgroup's own pre-vote disposition. Listed only
for completeness.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is an unambiguous editorial typo fix that has already been
applied in the working copy, was triaged by the PDex authors at
`PDex-Vote-1`, and carries a **Persuasive** resolution with the note
"correction applied." There are no cross-references, no opposing Zulip
discussion, and no GitHub work items that would suggest controversy or
a wider scope. The workgroup should ratify the pre-applied change as
Disposition A. Disposition B is a reasonable enhancement only if an
editor is already planning a broader narrative pass; otherwise it adds
process overhead disproportionate to a single-word correction.
