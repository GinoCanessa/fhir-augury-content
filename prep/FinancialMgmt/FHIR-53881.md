## Summary

The reporter argues that `ExplanationOfBenefit.related.relationship` is
unnecessary on the EOB. Their reasoning: an ExplanationOfBenefit is, by
definition, a representation of how a particular claim was adjudicated, so
"adjudicates" is effectively the only relationship the EOB can have to the
referenced claim. They suggest the element is an inherited artifact from
`Claim.related.relationship`, where a claim can legitimately be related to a
prior claim (for example, replacing or canceling it), but argue that the same
expressiveness is not warranted on the EOB. The ticket has already been
triaged by Financial Mgmt with a resolution of **Not Persuasive**, on the
basis that `.related.relationship` is inherited from the underlying Claim and
exposed on the EOB so a downstream receiver can interpret the EOB without
having to fetch and process the original Claim — the same pattern used for
many other Claim/ClaimResponse fields surfaced on the EOB. There are no other
linked or cross-referenced Jira tickets for this change request.

## Details

**Description:**
ExplanationOfBenefit.related.relationship denotes the relationship to the
referenced claim. The EOB is a representation of how the given claim was
adjudicated, that is really only one way in which it could be related to the
referenced claim. This may be an artifact from the Claim resource, where a
claim could be related to a prior claim, such as replacing or canceling it.

**Resolution (already recorded on the ticket):**
The `.related.relationship` is inherited from the claim and expressed on the
EOB so that the receiver does not need to also obtain and process the claim,
just like many of the other EOB elements are inherited from the Claim or
ClaimResponse.

## Related Zulip Discussions

### [CARIN IG for Blue Button® > CARIN BB ExplanationOfBenefit - Pharmacy Profile M3P Impacts](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/CARIN%20IG%20for%20Blue%20Button%C2%AE/topic/CARIN%20BB%20ExplanationOfBenefit%20-%20Pharmacy%20Profile%20M3P%20Impacts/near/473835655)

A 2024 thread on the CARIN Blue Button stream raising the impact of the
Medicare Prescription Payment Plan (M3P) on pharmacy ExplanationOfBenefit
profiles. The original poster (Mona O) called out that
`ExplanationOfBenefit.related.relationship`'s binding is `required` and
exposes only `prior` and `replacedby` codes, which is too narrow to express
the relationship between an M3P secondary pharmacy claim and the underlying
primary claim. The CARIN authors (Corey Spears, Mark Roberts) acknowledged
they had not modeled M3P specifically and offered to bring the question back
to their core group; a follow-up meeting was scheduled but no consensus or
decision was captured in this thread. Notably, the community feedback in
this discussion points the *opposite* direction from FHIR-53881: real
implementers want `.related.relationship` to express *more* relationships,
not to be removed.

Participants: Mona O, Corey Spears, Mark Roberts, David Riddle, Pat Taylor.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Deprecate or remove `ExplanationOfBenefit.related.relationship` (and, by
extension, simplify the `ExplanationOfBenefit.related` BackboneElement to
just `claim` and `reference`). Update the EOB structure definition,
narrative, mapping table to Claim, and any examples that populate
`.related.relationship`. Retain `Claim.related.relationship` unchanged so
the relationship is still expressible on the source Claim.

#### Justification

Internally consistent with the reporter's argument that an EOB *is* the
adjudication of one claim, so the only meaningful relationship between an
EOB and another claim is "adjudicates" — already implicit in the EOB
itself. Removing the element would slightly simplify the resource and
remove an apparently underused field. It would also avoid implementer
confusion when the bound value set (`prior`, `replacedby`) does not match
the actual semantic relationship being represented.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep `ExplanationOfBenefit.related.relationship` in core, but address the
real implementer pain point surfaced on Zulip: relax the binding strength
from `required` to `extensible` (or expand the bound value set
`related-claim-relationship`) so EOBs can express relationships beyond
`prior` / `replacedby` — e.g., a coordination-of-benefits secondary EOB
related to the primary claim, or an M3P secondary pharmacy claim related
to the underlying primary pharmacy claim. Document in the element
description that `.related.relationship` carries forward the semantics of
`Claim.related.relationship` precisely so receivers can interpret the EOB
without having to fetch the source Claim.

#### Justification

Honors the actual community evidence we have: the only real-world
discussion that touches this element (CARIN Blue Button / M3P) asks for
*more* expressivity, not less. It also preserves the design rationale
recorded in the ticket's existing resolution — that EOB-side fields
intentionally mirror Claim fields so downstream consumers can work from
the EOB alone. This avoids a breaking change to existing EOB
implementations (US Core, CARIN BB, Da Vinci PDex, etc.) while delivering
tangible value.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Not Persuasive** (the disposition Financial Mgmt
has already recorded), with the published rationale: `.related.relationship`
is inherited from the underlying Claim and is intentionally surfaced on the
EOB so that a receiver can interpret the relationship without also
fetching and parsing the source Claim. This is the same pattern used for
many other EOB elements that mirror Claim / ClaimResponse fields.

#### Justification

(1) The change is breaking: removing a published element on a normative-track
resource invalidates existing instances and forces churn on every IG that
profiles EOB (US Core, CARIN BB, Da Vinci PDex, BB 2.0, etc.) without a
corresponding implementer ask to drive it. (2) The reporter's premise —
that EOB can only have one kind of relationship to the referenced claim —
is contradicted by real implementer feedback (CARIN BB / M3P thread) where
a richer set of relationships is needed. (3) The existing design rationale
(receivers should be able to act on the EOB alone) is well-established and
applies to many other EOB fields; carving out this single element would be
inconsistent. (4) No use case has been presented for which removing the
element solves an actual problem.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** C, with a strong suggestion to follow up via a
new ticket aligned with Disposition B.

The Financial Mgmt resolution already on the ticket (Not Persuasive) is
defensible: there is no demonstrated implementer pain caused by the
element's *presence*, and removing it would be a breaking change to a
widely-profiled resource. The only community evidence we have on this
element (the CARIN BB M3P Zulip thread) actually argues against the
reporter — implementers want `.related.relationship` to express more
relationships, not fewer. Closing as Not Persuasive is the right call for
this specific request. The work group should, however, capture the
M3P / coordination-of-benefits binding concern as a separate change
request so the real underlying issue (binding strength and value-set
coverage on `.related.relationship`) gets addressed on its own merits.
