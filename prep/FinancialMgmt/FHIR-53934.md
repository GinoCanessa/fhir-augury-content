## Summary

The submitter argues that provider-level adjustments (PLBs in the X12 835
world) are awkwardly represented inside the `ClaimResponse.payment`
backbone element. Because PLBs are *not* tied to the adjudication of a
specific claim (they are payee-level adjustments such as overpayment
recovery, interest, capitation withholds, etc.), embedding them on a
single `ClaimResponse.payment` obscures their meaning and makes them
hard for implementers to work with. The reporter asks the workgroup to
consider either (a) communicating PLBs entirely outside the
`ClaimResponse` transaction — most naturally on `PaymentReconciliation`
where actual remittance occurs — or (b) significantly expanding the
detail captured on the existing payment element so consumers can tell
*why* a given claim's payment was reduced and *which other resources*
the adjustment relates to (e.g., the prior overpaid claim being
recouped). No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip discussions, or
GitHub items were surfaced for this change request, so the analysis
below is based solely on the ticket text and the existing FHIR Financial
resource design.

## Details

**Description:**

> Provider-level adjustments (affectionately known as PLBs in the
> industry), are really challenging for groups to work with today. I
> appreciate that consideration was given in the spec! But they appear
> somewhat obfuscated within the payment backbone element. Since this
> spec is focused on the adjudication, I wonder if we wouldn't be better
> served communicating them outside of this transaction.
>
> If they must be included in this transaction, greater detail is
> needed. For instance, which other resources was an overpayment
> performed such that this current ClaimResource must have it's payment
> reduced?
>
> Would such adjustments be better handled at the time of payment as a
> part of the PaymentReconciliation resource?

## Proposed Dispositions

> resources most likely to be touched (`ClaimResponse`,
> `PaymentReconciliation`) but cannot cite specific authoring paths or

> `repo-analysis HL7/fhir` run is recommended before drafting the

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Deprecate (or constrain out, in a US-focused profile) the embedded
provider-level adjustment representation on
`ClaimResponse.payment.adjustment` / `adjustmentReason`, and instead
require provider-level adjustments to be conveyed exclusively on
`PaymentReconciliation` — most likely as additional
`PaymentReconciliation.detail` entries whose `type` identifies the entry
as a provider-level adjustment (e.g., a new code in
`payment-type` / a new `payment-detail-type` code) and whose `request`
reference is left absent (because the adjustment is not tied to a
specific claim) or points at the originating overpaid `ClaimResponse`
when known. Update `ClaimResponse.payment` documentation to note that
PLB-style adjustments belong on `PaymentReconciliation`, not on the
per-claim payment element.

#### Justification

This aligns the FHIR model with the underlying business reality and
with the X12 835 transaction it is most often mapped to: PLBs sit at
the payee/remittance level, not the individual claim level.
`PaymentReconciliation` already exists to describe the actual
movement of money for a remittance, and its `detail` backbone is
designed to enumerate per-line items that contribute to the payment
amount, which is the natural home for PLB entries. Removing the
duplicate path on `ClaimResponse.payment` eliminates the
"obfuscation" the reporter calls out and prevents the recurring
implementer question of which side of the model is authoritative.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep `ClaimResponse.payment.adjustment` for backward compatibility but
clarify and enrich it: (1) tighten the definition to scope it to
*claim-level* adjustments to the payment amount that are still
attributable to *this* claim's adjudication (e.g., a withhold applied
to this claim), and (2) add a structured way to point at the
originating event when the adjustment is a recoupment — e.g., a new
optional `ClaimResponse.payment.adjustment` sub-element (or extension
in the current cycle) `relatedResource : Reference(ClaimResponse |
PaymentReconciliation | Invoice)` plus a coded `relationship` (e.g.,
`recoupment-of`, `withhold`, `interest`). In parallel, publish
non-normative guidance that genuinely payee-level PLBs SHOULD be
communicated on `PaymentReconciliation.detail` rather than fanned out
across `ClaimResponse.payment.adjustment` on every claim in the
remittance.

#### Justification

This addresses the reporter's "if they must be included… greater
detail is needed" branch without breaking existing implementers who
already populate `ClaimResponse.payment.adjustment`. The added
`relatedResource` link directly answers the explicit question in the
ticket ("which other resources was an overpayment performed [on]")
and gives consumers a deterministic way to walk from a reduced
payment back to the offending prior claim or remittance. Pairing the
structural change with implementation guidance steers new work toward
`PaymentReconciliation`, achieving most of Disposition A's clarity
benefit at lower disruption.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change. Take the position that `ClaimResponse.payment`
already supports per-claim payment adjustments via
`adjustment` / `adjustmentReason`, and that payee-level PLBs are
already representable on `PaymentReconciliation.detail` today; no
normative change to `ClaimResponse` is required. Close the ticket as
"works as designed" with a pointer to existing guidance and to
`PaymentReconciliation` for payee-level adjustments.

#### Justification

The two relevant resources already exist and the spec does not
prohibit putting PLB-style entries on `PaymentReconciliation.detail`.
The reporter's concern is largely about *clarity* and *implementer
ergonomics* rather than a missing capability, and the workgroup has
historically been reluctant to deprecate or restructure long-standing
backbone elements (`ClaimResponse.payment.adjustment` has been in
the resource since early DSTU). With no Zulip discussion, no
companion tickets, and no implementer report of an inability to
exchange PLB data, the case for a normative change is thin in this
ballot cycle.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the most defensible middle path. The reporter's core
complaint — that PLBs are "obfuscated" inside the payment backbone and
that there is no way to express *which* prior resource an overpayment
recovery relates to — is real and is felt by implementers mapping to
X12 835. Disposition A solves it cleanly but is a breaking change to
`ClaimResponse.payment` and would require migration guidance for every
payer already producing `adjustment` arrays today; without supporting
ballot comments or Zulip consensus, that level of disruption is hard
to justify in a single cycle. Disposition C ignores the substantive
gap (no `relatedResource` link from a recoupment back to its
originating claim). Disposition B closes that gap with a small
additive change on `ClaimResponse.payment.adjustment`, and pairs it
with non-normative guidance steering payee-level PLBs to
`PaymentReconciliation.detail` — preserving today's implementations
while moving the ecosystem toward the cleaner model the reporter
advocates. If the workgroup wants to commit to the cleaner end-state,
B can be sequenced as the first step toward A in a future cycle.
