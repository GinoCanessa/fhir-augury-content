## Summary

[FHIR-54679](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54679) is a Change Request
filed by John Moehrke against the Scalable Consent Management (FHIR) IG
asking the work group to clarify the "Consent Network Relationship"
diagram — specifically, the meaning of the arrows that depict how
consent is exchanged between actors in the network. Cached ticket
content is limited (the captured description body is empty in the
augury snapshot), but the single comment and extracted keywords make
the intent clear: the diagram's arrows do not communicate *what* is
flowing between nodes, nor *who* is sharing with *whom*, and the
direction is ambiguous because consent exchange may be either a push
(the holder sends a registered consent to a recipient) or a pull (a
recipient subscribes to / fetches the consent). The disposition
recorded against the ticket is **Persuasive**, indicating the work
group accepted the request in principle.

A near-duplicate ticket, [FHIR-54678](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54678)
(same title, also from John Moehrke, labelled `Technical` rather than
`UseCases`), was resolved **Persuasive with Modification** with a
commitment to "accept suggested changes from John." That sibling ticket
appears to cover the *technical/textual* description of the network
relationship, while FHIR-54679 covers the *visual / use-case* rendering
of the same concept. Closely related work also exists in
[FHIR-54884](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54884) ("Add patient to
consent tree visual"), which proposes another change to the same family
of diagrams.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the Consent Network Relationship diagram in the Scalable Consent
Management (FHIR) IG so that every arrow between actors carries an
explicit "shared with" label identifying *what* is being shared (the
Consent resource, or a notification of a Consent), and add a legend /
narrative paragraph immediately under the diagram stating that the
relationship may be realised in either direction:

- **Push** — the Consent author / holder transmits the registered
  Consent to the recipient (e.g., via a direct exchange or a
  notification);
- **Pull** — the recipient subscribes to or retrieves the Consent (e.g.,
  via Subscription / SubscriptionTopic, search, or `$consent-fetch`
  style operations).

The companion narrative should explicitly say that arrow direction in
the diagram represents the *information-sharing relationship*, not the
transport direction, and call out that the same logical relationship
may be implemented as push or pull. Coordinate the labelling style with
the patient-on-tree change requested in
[FHIR-54884](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54884) and the textual
clarifications agreed in [FHIR-54678](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54678).

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter and the triage comment from
david.pyke ("Add 'shared with' labels to arrows. Note that it may be
push or pull.") asked for, and the ticket is already marked
**Persuasive**. The change is documentation-only — no resource
definition, value set, or invariant is altered — so the
implementation cost is low and the readability win is high. It is
consistent with the resolution pattern the work group has used on
analogous "push vs pull unclear" requests in HRex
([FHIR-23148](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-23148),
[FHIR-23156](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-23156)) and aligns with
the "Persuasive with Modification" track for the sibling ticket
[FHIR-54678](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54678).

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than annotating arrows on a single combined diagram, replace
the existing Consent Network Relationship figure with **two paired
diagrams** (or a single diagram with two clearly-labelled lanes): one
showing the **push** flow (Consent holder → recipient with a
"transmits Consent" arrow) and one showing the **pull** flow
(recipient → Consent holder with a "subscribes to / retrieves
Consent" arrow, and a return arrow carrying the Consent). Add a short
narrative table under each diagram naming the typical FHIR mechanism
(direct write/POST, `Subscription` / `SubscriptionTopic`, search, or a
fetch operation) that realises each direction.

#### Justification

The root cause of the confusion is that one diagram is being asked to
represent two structurally different exchanges. Splitting the figure
removes the ambiguity entirely instead of merely labelling around it,
and it gives implementers a direct mapping from the picture to the
FHIR mechanism they need to implement. Trade-off vs. Disposition A:
more authoring effort and a larger visual footprint in the IG, and it
diverges from the reporter's literal ask, which was simply to label
the arrows. Justified when reviewers feel the single-diagram approach
will remain confusing even with the "shared with" labels added.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket without changing the diagram, on the basis that the
narrative surrounding the figure already explains the push/pull
duality and that adding extra labels risks visual clutter without
materially improving comprehension.

#### Justification

This option is included for completeness only. It is hard to defend
given that (a) the work group has already marked the ticket
**Persuasive**, (b) the sibling ticket
[FHIR-54678](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54678) is being resolved
**Persuasive with Modification** for essentially the same complaint,
and (c) multiple independent reviewers (per the related-by-keyword
neighbours, including [FHIR-54739](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54739)
and [FHIR-54884](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54884)) have flagged
the same family of diagrams as unclear. Declining would leave a known,
acknowledged readability defect in the IG.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is already triaged Persuasive, the requested fix is small,
specific, and documentation-only, and the work group has a concrete
proposal on the table from david.pyke that the reporter is expected to
accept. Disposition A delivers exactly that fix and lets the work
group coordinate consistent "shared with" labelling across the family
of consent-network/consent-tree diagrams that
[FHIR-54678](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54678) and
[FHIR-54884](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54884) also touch.
Disposition B (split into push and pull diagrams) is the right
fallback if reviewers find that arrow labels alone still leave the
relationship ambiguous; it is a strictly larger change and should only
be taken if A is judged insufficient.
