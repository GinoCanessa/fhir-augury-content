## Summary

The ticket challenges the eLTSS guidance for the "Service Rate Per Unit" and
"Total Cost of Service" data elements, which originally proposed modeling
costing information by having `CarePlan.activity.reference` point at a
`ServiceRequest` and `CarePlan.supportingInfo` point at a `Claim`. The
reporter argues this is a poor fit because (a) `Claim` represents the result
of a completed service rather than a planned one, and (b) `ServiceRequest`
sits earlier in the workflow when no claim should yet exist. The reporter
suggests that unit price should live "at the service level" — ideally on
`HealthcareService` (linked from `ServiceRequest`) — and notes that
`HealthcareService` does not today carry rate/price information, so an
extension may be required.

The ticket was originally closed as "Considered for Future Use" in 2020 with
an Amber Patel / Kathleen Connor recommendation that `HealthcareService`
seemed the best fit and that an extension on it would be appropriate, with a
referral to FM (`Claim`), O&O (`ServiceRequest`), and Patient Administration
(`HealthcareService`) to determine the right home. Lynn Laakso re-opened it
post-STU2 publication in May 2024. Brett Heale then commented (April and
December 2024) that the existing FHIR `Claim` / `ClaimResponse` resources
already cover this need: `Claim.use` distinguishes claim / preauthorization /
predetermination, `Claim.referral` links back to the `ServiceRequest`,
`Claim.procedure.procedureReference` ties to a `Procedure`,
`Claim.supportingInfo` carries `Task` records, and `Claim.net` /
`ClaimResponse.net` provide total cost from which a per-unit rate can be
derived together with `ServiceRequest`/`Procedure` quantity and frequency.
He explicitly states an extension is "not necessary." No Jira links, Zulip
threads, or GitHub items are cross-referenced for this ticket; the only
incoming references in Augury are the FHIR element mentions enumerated above.

## Details

**Description:**

In reference to "Additional Mapping Details" for "Service Rate Per Unit" and
"Total Cost of Service" data elements:

1. Will use CarePlan -> activity -> reference to reference a ServiceRequest,
   and supportingInfo to reference a Claim.

   Comment: Claim is result of completion of service. Service Request is in
   earlier stage in workflow at the time of preparing plan when claim should
   not be created. There should be Unit price at the service level. Ideally
   ServiceRequest should link to HealthCareService where all service
   definition should be specified including unit, rate/price, etc. But rate
   is not currently included in HealthCareService and may require an
   extension.

**Comments:**

### bheale (2024-04-02)

FHIR `ClaimResponse` is what would be provided as acceptance to pay for
service. `Claim` can be used for predetermination and preauthorization. The
STU2 guidance includes a section "Requesting and Recording payment for Task,
Procedure or ServiceRequest" describing how a `Claim` can be used as a claim,
preauthorization, or predetermination (`Claim.use`); how it connects to a
`ServiceRequest` via `Claim.referral`; how `Procedure` can be referenced via
`Claim.procedure.procedureReference`; and how `Task` records can be carried
in `Claim.supportingInfo`. `ClaimResponse` provides the adjudication /
authorization result. `Claim.net` and `ClaimResponse.net` carry total cost,
from which a per-unit / hourly rate can be derived in combination with the
quantity and frequency on `ServiceRequest` or `Procedure` — potentially
"less ambiguous and more convenient than requesting entry of the cost per
unit."

### lynn_laakso (2024-05-27)

Reverted the previous "Considered for Future Use" resolution (made
2020-07-21 by vote Amber Patel / Kathleen Connor 12-0-1):
"HealthcareService seems to be the best fit – and suggesting add an
extension for the cost since it fits in the workflow. (Claim is after the
service has been provided, and Service Request is the request of the
service)." The reverter suggested talking with FM (`Claim`), O&O
(`ServiceRequest`), and Patient Administration (`HealthcareService`) to
determine where cost fits best for a service.

### lynn_laakso (2024-05-27)

Re-open post STU2 publication.

### bheale (2024-12-05)

An extension is not necessary and is not using the proper FHIR Resources of
`Claim` and `ClaimResponse`.

### bheale (2024-12-05)

Please read the `Claim` resource description (https://hl7.org/fhir/r4/claim.html#scope):
it can be used for "preauthorization — where the provision of goods and
services is proposed and authorization and/or the reservation of funds is
desired."

### bheale (2024-12-05)

A `ServiceRequest` is an active request, so the cost is "real" not
definitional. As part of the `ServiceRequest` a `Claim` would be sent and
`Claim.use` would have the proper value to indicate use.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the eLTSS STU2 "Service Information" mapping for the "Service Rate
Per Unit" and "Total Cost of Service" data elements to remove the
`CarePlan.supportingInfo → Claim` pattern as the planning-time vehicle for
unit price and total cost. Replace it with a `HealthcareService`-based model:
have `ServiceRequest.healthcareService` (or, where the request originates
from a plan, `CarePlan.activity.reference → ServiceRequest →
HealthcareService`) point at a `HealthcareService` instance that carries the
service's unit and price via a new eLTSS extension (e.g.,
`http://hl7.org/fhir/us/eltss/StructureDefinition/healthcareservice-rate`)
defining `unit` (CodeableConcept), `ratePerUnit` (Money), and optionally
`totalCost` (Money). Update the mapping table, the IG narrative, and
publish the extension `StructureDefinition` in the IG.

#### Justification

This matches the reporter's underlying concern — that price is a property of
the service definition, not of a completed financial transaction — and
matches the 2020 workgroup vote (Amber Patel / Kathleen Connor 12-0-1) that
identified `HealthcareService` plus an extension as the best fit. It keeps
the planning-time artifacts (`CarePlan`, `ServiceRequest`,
`HealthcareService`) free of `Claim`-shaped semantics, which can be
confusing to LTSS implementers who do not operate a claims workflow. An
extension is the standard FHIR mechanism for filling a gap in a base
resource, and `HealthcareService` is the natural home for definitional
properties of a service.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the planning-time linkage to a financial artifact, but explicitly model
it as a *preauthorization* `Claim` (`Claim.use = preauthorization` or
`predetermination`) rather than a post-service `Claim`. Update the eLTSS
narrative for "Service Rate Per Unit" and "Total Cost of Service" to:

1. State that `Claim.use` MUST be `preauthorization` or `predetermination`
   when a `Claim` is referenced from a planning artifact (`CarePlan` /
   `ServiceRequest`).
2. Clarify that the `Claim` is connected to the `ServiceRequest` via
   `Claim.referral` (not via `CarePlan.supportingInfo`), per the existing
   STU2 "Requesting and Recording payment for Task, Procedure or
   ServiceRequest" section.
3. Document that "Total Cost of Service" is conveyed by `Claim.net` (or
   `ClaimResponse.net` for the adjudicated value) and that "Service Rate Per
   Unit" is derived from `Claim.net` together with the quantity / frequency
   on the referenced `ServiceRequest` (or `Procedure`).
4. Optionally add a small profile on `Claim` named e.g. `ELTSSPlannedClaim`
   that constrains `use` to the planning-time codes and requires `referral`.

No new extension is published; all carriage relies on existing core
elements.

#### Justification

This is the path Brett Heale advocates in the 2024-04 and 2024-12 comments:
the FHIR `Claim` / `ClaimResponse` resources already cover the cost-carrying
need, the STU2 IG already includes guidance using them, and `Claim.use`
exists precisely to distinguish a claim from a preauthorization or
predetermination. It avoids publishing a new extension on
`HealthcareService`, which is itself a cross-WG change that would need
coordination with Patient Administration. The trade-off is conceptual load
on implementers (some find any use of `Claim` at planning time
counter-intuitive) and a derived rather than first-class "rate per unit"
value.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve the ticket as **Not Persuasive** on the grounds that the existing
STU2 guidance already covers the use case via core FHIR `Claim` /
`ClaimResponse` semantics — `Claim.use`, `Claim.referral`,
`Claim.procedure.procedureReference`, `Claim.supportingInfo`, `Claim.net`,
and `ClaimResponse.net` — without requiring any change to the IG, any
extension on `HealthcareService`, or any change to the Service Information
mapping table.

#### Justification

Brett Heale's December 2024 comments make this case directly: "An extension
is not necessary," and the published `Claim` resource description
explicitly admits planning-time use ("preauthorization — where the
provision of goods and services is proposed and authorization and/or the
reservation of funds is desired"). The STU2 IG already includes a
"Requesting and Recording payment for Task, Procedure or ServiceRequest"
section spelling out how to wire `ServiceRequest` to `Claim` for
preauthorization and how to derive a per-unit rate from `Claim.net` and
service quantity. Adding a parallel `HealthcareService` extension would
fragment the costing pattern across two mechanisms and create ambiguity for
implementers about which one to populate.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The 2020 workgroup vote landed on Disposition A's shape, but it predates
the STU2 narrative that now explicitly documents `Claim` /
`ClaimResponse` for planning-time costing — and the reverter (Lynn Laakso)
re-opened the ticket precisely because that 2020 resolution no longer
matched the published STU2 content. The most recent substantive comments
(Brett Heale, 2024) argue that the existing IG guidance is sufficient, but
the ticket exists because the mapping table for "Service Rate Per Unit" /
"Total Cost of Service" still reads as if a post-service `Claim` is being
hung off `CarePlan.supportingInfo`, which is what the reporter (and any
LTSS implementer reading the table) finds confusing. Disposition B keeps
the existing core-resource-only architecture that the workgroup has already
written into STU2, but pays off the reporter's concern by tightening the
narrative: making explicit that `Claim.use` MUST be a planning-time code,
that the linkage is via `Claim.referral` from `ServiceRequest`, and that
`Claim.net` / `ClaimResponse.net` is the canonical home for total cost. A
small invariant-only `ELTSSPlannedClaim` profile makes the constraint
testable. This avoids the cross-WG coordination cost of Disposition A
(Patient Administration ownership of `HealthcareService`, plus a new
published extension) while not simply declining the ticket as in
Disposition C, which leaves the originally-confusing mapping in place.
