## Summary

John Moehrke (reporter) raises a structural concern about the use-cases
page in the Scalable Consent Management (FHIR) IG: the narrative use
cases repeatedly refer to a "consent request" / "consent form" artifact
that flows between Administrator and Consenter, but no such artifact is
formally defined in the normative content. The ticket walks through
use-cases 1 through 8 and asks, for each, whether the implied artifact
is conceptual (and therefore a pre-condition out of scope) or normative
(and therefore needing a defined FHIR representation). Specific
sub-questions cover: whether use-case 1 has any normative content at
all; whether the partially-filled "form" in use-case 2 is the `Consent`
resource itself; what kind of digital signature use-case 3 requires and
where it lives; how a delegate (use-case 4) is represented on `Consent`
(e.g. `Consent.performer` vs. `grantee`) and `AuditEvent`; the apparent
incompleteness of use-case 6; the implication in use-case 7 that the
"consent form" must be a subscribable, identifiable normative artifact;
and whether the "provenance" mentioned in use-case 8 means a FHIR
`Provenance` resource or version history of the `Consent` resource.
Moehrke closes by recommending the page visually distinguish in-scope
artifacts/interactions (e.g. bold or link them to the normative
content). The work group has resolved the ticket as **Persuasive**, with
the resolution committing to (a) link each use-case diagram to the
relevant technical operation, (b) add a table showing which operations
each use-case requires, and (c) explicitly call out when a technical
artifact (`Consent`, `DocumentReference` to a consent form, etc.) is
involved in a use case.

The only cross-reference surfaced in FhirAugury is a mention of the
FHIR path `Consent.peformer` (sic — the reporter's typo for
`Consent.performer`) that appears in the description.

## Details

**Description:**

The use-cases seem to indicate that there is some Consent Request
artifact (e.g. consent form). For example in use-case 1 that is
communicated from Administrator to Consenter, and in use-case 2 which
may be initiated by the Consenter or requested by the Administrator. Yet
there does not seem to be an artifact like this. If this is a conceptual
artifact for which the details are not specified, then that should be
stated.

1. There seems to be nothing from use-case 1 that is normative. Is this
   intended? If so, then this should be defined as an important
   pre-condition for the normative use-cases.
2. use-case 2 seems to have a partially filled form. Is this form the
   Consent? Or is it still a pre-condition for which the details are not
   in-scope? You do have "shall" language in this use-case that requires
   the form to be preserved, so therefore there must be a form
   normatively defined.
3. use-case 3 seems to finally create a Consent. is this right?
4. use-case 3 mentions sign the consent form. I don't see any normative
   mention of this. What kind of signature? Where is this signature?
5. use-case 4 identifies delegate, but does not mention the impact of a
   delegate on the normative artifacts. As in how is the delegate
   identified as a delegate? How is the delegate identified in the
   Consent, and AuditEvent? (e.g. Consent.peformer or grantee?)
6. use-case 6 seems incomplete. Why is navigation mentioned? Why is
   there no post-condition?
7. use-case 7 indicates that a supported event is a consenter starting
   to review a consent form, this would require that the consent form is
   a normative artifact that can be identified and subscribed to.
8. use-case 8 is mentioning provenance. is this FHIR Provenance, or is
   this the provenance that is part of the Consent resource. If it is
   the Consent content, then is this use-case requiring the FHIR Consent
   Resource is maintained on a server that is supporting versioning?
   There is no mention of provenance elsewhere.

The use-case page has much that is in-scope, but also has much that is
not in-scope. There should be some distinction made. Possibly the
artifacts and interactions that are in-scope could be bold or linked to
the normative content.

**Resolution Description:**

We are going to include a link to the relevant technical operation in
each use case diagram and provide a table that shows what operations are
required for each use case. We will also indicate when a technical
artifact is included in a use case, such as the Consent resource, or a
DocumentReference to a consent form, etc.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Edit the Scalable Consent Management IG's use-cases narrative page so
that, for each of use-cases 1–8:

1. The use-case diagram is annotated with (or links to) the specific
   technical operation(s) it exercises (e.g. `Consent` create/update,
   `DocumentReference` create, `Subscription` notify, `AuditEvent`
   record, `Provenance` create).
2. A new "Operations & Artifacts" table is added at the top of the page
   listing each use case, the FHIR operations it requires, and the
   resources/artifacts it produces or consumes.
3. Every reference to a "consent form" or "consent request" is
   explicitly classified as either (a) a normative FHIR artifact
   (`Consent`, `DocumentReference` carrying a rendered form,
   `Provenance`, etc., with the resource name linked to its profile) or
   (b) an out-of-scope, conceptual pre-condition — and the latter are
   visually distinguished (italic / muted style or a "(out of scope)"
   marker).
4. Address each numbered sub-question explicitly: confirm use-case 1 is
   a pre-condition; clarify whether the use-case 2 form is the
   `Consent` resource or a `DocumentReference`; identify use-case 3 as
   the canonical `Consent`-creation flow and resolve the signature
   question (`Consent.sourceAttachment`, `Provenance.signature`, or
   neither); decide whether `Consent.performer` or `Consent.grantor`/
   `grantee` carries the delegate in use-case 4; complete use-case 6's
   post-condition; clarify use-case 7's subscription target; and disambiguate
   "provenance" in use-case 8 as either `Provenance` resource or
   `Consent` versioning.

This is the action the ticket explicitly requests, and it matches the
work group's already-recorded resolution ("Persuasive").

#### Justification

The reporter's complaint is fundamentally about traceability between
narrative use-cases and the normative artifacts. FHIR IGs are expected
to make in-scope artifacts identifiable and linked to their definitions;
mixing in-scope and out-of-scope concepts without distinction is a
common source of implementer confusion and ballot comments. The
proposed changes are documentation-only — no profile, value-set, or
resource changes are introduced — so the implementer impact is purely
positive (clearer guidance) with no risk of breaking existing
implementations. The work group has already accepted this approach in
the resolution text, so Disposition A simply formalizes the agreed
plan.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Instead of (or in addition to) restructuring the narrative page,
introduce a small, explicitly-scoped section titled **"In-Scope
Artifacts"** at the top of the IG that enumerates the FHIR resources
governed by the IG (`Consent`, `DocumentReference`, `Provenance`,
`AuditEvent`, `Subscription`, with profile links), and add a single
**"Out-of-Scope"** call-out describing the conceptual "consent
request"/"consent form" pre-condition pattern as informative only.
Then add cross-references *from* each use-case heading *back to* that
canonical list rather than annotating every use-case diagram
individually. As a separate, narrowly-scoped sub-task, also clarify the
delegate representation question (use-case 4) by adding a one-paragraph
"Delegation" section that fixes the IG's stance (e.g. delegate is
modeled in `Consent.performer` with a role code, and recorded in
`AuditEvent.agent.who` for the delegated action), since that question
has substantive modelling implications beyond mere documentation.

#### Justification

Disposition A risks producing a "wall of annotations" on each use-case
diagram, which is visually heavy and duplicates information across use
cases. A single canonical artifact catalog plus back-references is
easier to keep consistent across ballot cycles, lighter to maintain,
and gives implementers one place to look. The carve-out for the
delegate question recognizes that use-case 4 is the only sub-question
in the ticket whose answer affects modelling (which element holds the
delegate), not just documentation; failing to nail that down now will
likely generate a follow-up ticket. The trade-off vs. A is that
per-use-case readers have one extra hop to reach the artifact catalog.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the ticket on the basis that the use-cases page is an
informative narrative whose role is to motivate the normative content,
not to enumerate normative artifacts. Direct the reader to the
existing resource and profile pages for the authoritative artifact
definitions, and add at most a single sentence at the top of the
use-cases page reminding the reader that "consent forms," "consent
requests," and similar phrases are illustrative and that only resources
with a profile in this IG are normative.

#### Justification

If the IG's structure already separates normative content (profiles,
operations, capability statements) from informative content (use cases,
overviews), then the ticket can be read as conflating the two. A blanket
clarification at the top of the page is cheap and avoids the editorial
overhead of restructuring eight use cases. Declining would be defensible
on the grounds that the request, while well-intentioned, blurs the line
between a use-case page and a profile catalog.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The work group has already resolved this ticket as **Persuasive** with a
resolution that effectively *is* Disposition A (link each use-case
diagram to the relevant technical operation, add an operations/use-case
table, and explicitly call out technical artifacts such as `Consent` and
`DocumentReference`). That recorded position should be honored: A is
concrete, addresses every numbered sub-question the reporter raised,
and is documentation-only with no implementer breakage. Disposition B's
"central artifact catalog" idea is a useful refinement to layer on top
of A (and the work group may want to fold it in as an editorial
improvement), but standing alone B leaves several of the reporter's
specific sub-questions — particularly the delegate-vs-`Consent.performer`
question and the signature question in use-case 3 — only partially
answered. Disposition C would be defensible only if the IG already had a
clean normative/informative separation; the ticket itself is evidence
that it does not, so declining would simply re-surface as a ballot
comment. Recommend A, with the editorial team free to borrow B's
"In-Scope Artifacts" catalog as a presentation aid.
