## Summary

Hans Buitendijk asks the Community-Based Care and Privacy work group to tighten
how a computable policy is expressed on the `Consent` resource. Today
`Consent.policyBasis` is a backbone element that offers two sibling paths for
pointing at the underlying policy: `Consent.policyBasis.uri` (a URL that can
point at a computable expression in an agreed-upon library) and
`Consent.policyBasis.reference` (typed as `Reference(Any)`). The reporter
observes that `Reference(Any)` is so permissive it gives implementers no
guidance about what a "computable" policy reference actually looks like, and a
bare list of arbitrary resources with no enclosing logic is ambiguous. The
request is to drop `Consent.policyBasis.reference` and rely on
`Consent.policyBasis.uri` for the computable pointer, with the rhetorical
suggestion that the `Consent` resource itself is the natural FHIR way to
express privacy rules when one is needed inline. No cross-referenced Jira
tickets were returned for this ticket; the only cross-references are content
mentions of `Consent.policyBasis` / `Consent.policyBasis.uri` in the ticket
text itself.

## Details

**Description:**

> The Consent.policyBasis.uri provides a clear reference to a computable
> expression in an agreed to library locally or centrally. However, the
> .reference is Reference(Any) which seems wide open to express a computable
> version. In the absence of just using the Consent resource as a reference
> (it after all is expressing similar rules with enough similar metadata that
> can be profiled), having more consistency of how policies are expressed in
> computable form would be helpful. Since it is meant to be a computable
> expression and a list of resources without logic around it will be very
> ambiguous, suggest to just have the uri. What is the resource to express
> privacy rules if not the Consent resource?

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Remove `Consent.policyBasis.reference` from the `policyBasis` backbone
in the next FHIR Core ballot, leaving `Consent.policyBasis.uri` as the
single pointer to the computable policy. Make `policyBasis.uri` 0..1
(if not already) and update the element definition to state that the
target SHALL be a URL resolving to a computable policy artifact (e.g.,
a `Library`, `PlanDefinition`, `Questionnaire`, `DocumentReference`, or
an external, non-FHIR computable policy file). Update the narrative for
`Consent` (resource intro and `policyBasis` notes) to remove guidance
about `.reference` and to direct implementers who need multiple policy
inputs to either publish a composite artifact behind one URL or use
multiple `policyBasis` occurrences via `.uri`. Mark this as a breaking
change in the R6/next-ballot change log.

#### Justification

- The reporter's core observation is correct on the spec's face:
  `Reference(Any)` with no accompanying logical frame doesn't tell an
  implementer what a "computable" policy reference is. Zulip practice
  (`patient empowerment > US Interstate Interoperability`,
  `Consent Decision and Management Services > Consent for Research`,
  `ihe > ITI PCF`) consistently gravitates to `policyBasis.uri` as the
  computable hook; `.reference` does not appear to be carrying its
  weight in real modelling discussions.
- FHIR design principle: two sibling ways to say the same thing
  invite divergence. Collapsing to `.uri` aligns with the guidance the
  WG is already giving implementers informally.
- Keeps the door open for future typed-reference support via a formal
  extension if a concrete use case emerges, without baking
  `Reference(Any)` into normative Consent.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep `Consent.policyBasis.reference` but constrain it. Replace
`Reference(Any)` with a tight target list
(`Reference(Library | PlanDefinition | Questionnaire | DocumentReference)`)
and require (via an invariant) that at least one of
`Consent.policyBasis.uri` or `Consent.policyBasis.reference` be present,
with both allowed when the `.uri` resolves to a concept in an agreed
library and `.reference` points to the specific FHIR artifact embodying
that library locally. Update `policyBasis` narrative to describe when
each form is appropriate (external/canonical URL vs. on-server FHIR
artifact). Ship this as a non-breaking (or minimally-breaking)
clarification in the next R6 ballot.

#### Justification

- Addresses the reporter's real pain — "Reference(Any) seems wide
  open" — without removing a field that some existing implementations
  may already populate (the Zulip record shows `.policyBasis` discussed
  since R5 introduction, so there is some implementer footprint to
  consider).
- Tight target types give the Consent Decision Services community a
  FHIR-native way to embed computable policy artifacts on the same
  server as the Consent instance, which FHIR-43331's adjacent
  "regulatoryBasis" discussion shows is a live concern.
- Avoids the breaking-change classification that Disposition A would
  pick up, which is material for a ticket whose work group is marked
  `[deprecated]` and which may lack the bandwidth to shepherd a
  breaking normative change across ballot.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change. Keep `Consent.policyBasis.reference` as
`Reference(Any)`, with no structural change. Optionally add a clarifying
sentence to the `policyBasis` narrative that `.uri` is the preferred
computable pointer and `.reference` is for exceptional cases where the
policy is already represented as a FHIR resource on the same server.
Categorise as "Not Persuasive — existing mechanism is adequate".

#### Justification

- `Reference(Any)` in `policyBasis.reference` is intentionally
  permissive because the FHIR community has not converged on a single
  resource for "a computable policy"; `Library`, `PlanDefinition`,
  `Questionnaire`, and out-of-band artifacts are all in use. Removing
  the element forces every one of those use cases back through `.uri`
  even when the artifact is on the server.
- The ticket itself offers no concrete implementer report of breakage
  caused by `.reference`; it's a modelling-consistency concern.
  Triaged / Medium priority, no comments, and the work group is marked
  deprecated, suggesting this does not warrant a normative breaking
  change.
- Community discussion on Zulip (US Interstate Interoperability, IPS
  Pilgrimage) already treats `.uri` as the default in practice; a
  narrative nudge is likely sufficient.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B gives the reporter what they actually need — a
`policyBasis.reference` that is no longer "wide open" — without
forcing a breaking removal of an element that the Consent community has
already been reasoning about in multiple Zulip threads and adjacent
tickets (FHIR-43331). Disposition A is cleaner in principle but carries
real breakage risk for R5-era `Consent` implementations, and the
deprecated status of the Community-Based Care and Privacy work group
makes shepherding a normative breaking change awkward. Disposition C
leaves the exact ambiguity the reporter flagged in place and relies on
narrative-only guidance, which is the weakest form of constraint in
FHIR. Tightening the target list plus an "at least one of uri/reference"
invariant preserves both pointer modalities (the Zulip record shows
both are occasionally useful), aligns with the WG's ongoing
clarification of `regulatoryBasis` / `policyBasis` semantics, and is
the proportionate fix for a Medium-priority modelling concern.
