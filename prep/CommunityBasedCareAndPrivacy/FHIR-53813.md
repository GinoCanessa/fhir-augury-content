## Summary

The reporter identifies an internal inconsistency in the *Scalable Consent
Management (FHIR)* IG (FAST Consent). The FAST Consent `SubscriptionTopic`
declares support for emitting subscription notifications on the **delete**
of a `Consent` resource on the Admin Server, yet two other normative parts
of the IG say that delete of a `Consent` is **not** supported:

- The Technical Specification's *Revoke Consent Operation* section states
  flatly that "A revoked consent SHALL NOT be deleted from the consent
  management system."
- The `ConsentAdministrativeServerCapabilities` CapabilityStatement does
  **not** list `delete` among the interactions for `Consent`.

The reporter asks the Community-Based Care and Privacy work group to
resolve the contradiction by either (a) removing the `delete` trigger
from the FAST Consent `SubscriptionTopic`, or (b) updating the Technical
Specification to describe how `Consent` deletes are permitted and, if
allowed, adding the `delete` interaction to the
`ConsentAdministrativeServerCapabilities` CapabilityStatement.

No related Jira tickets were surfaced via cross-references, and no Zulip
or GitHub items were found for this ticket.

## Details

**Description:**

There is a mismatch in declared support for the FHIR delete interaction
on the `Consent` resource type.

The FAST Consent `SubscriptionTopic` declares support for a Subscription
notification on the delete of a `Consent` resource in the Admin Server.
However, there are references in the IG that indicate the delete of a
`Consent` resource in the Admin Server is **not** supported:

- See the [Technical Specification — Revoke Consent
  Operation](https://hl7.org/fhir/us/consent-management/2026Jan/technical.html#revoke-consent-operation)
  section that states:

  > A revoked consent SHALL NOT be deleted from the consent management
  > system.

- See the [Consent Administrative Server
  Capabilities](https://hl7.org/fhir/us/consent-management/2026Jan/CapabilityStatement-ConsentAdministrativeServerCapabilities.html)
  CapabilityStatement, where the `Consent` resource's `interaction` does
  not declare support for `delete`.

Clarification can be defined by either:

- Removing the `Consent` `delete` interaction declaration in the FAST
  Consent `SubscriptionTopic`, **or**
- Updating the Technical Specification with language describing how
  `Consent` deletes can be supported. If supported, also adding the
  `Consent` `delete` interaction to the
  `ConsentAdministrativeServerCapabilities` CapabilityStatement.

**Resolution (recorded on the ticket):**

> We will remove `delete` as a supported interaction on the Subscription
> Topic.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the resolution recorded on the ticket: **remove the `delete`
trigger from the FAST Consent `SubscriptionTopic`** in the Scalable
Consent Management IG. Concretely, edit the IG's `SubscriptionTopic`
artifact for FAST Consent so that `SubscriptionTopic.resourceTrigger`
for `Consent` no longer includes `delete` in `supportedInteraction`
(retain `create` / `update` as currently defined). Leave the
`ConsentAdministrativeServerCapabilities` CapabilityStatement unchanged
(it already omits `delete` for `Consent`), and leave the *Revoke Consent
Operation* normative text unchanged ("A revoked consent SHALL NOT be
deleted from the consent management system."). Add a brief implementer
note in the SubscriptionTopic's narrative explaining that delete is
intentionally not a trigger because revocation is performed via the
`$revoke-consent` operation rather than by deleting the `Consent`
instance.

#### Justification

This is the disposition the work group already recorded on the ticket
(Resolution: Persuasive — "We will remove 'delete' as a supported
interaction on the Subscription Topic"). It is also the
internally-consistent option: the Technical Specification's SHALL NOT
prohibition on deleting a revoked consent and the CapabilityStatement's
omission of `delete` for `Consent` jointly mean that conformant Admin
Servers cannot delete `Consent` resources, so a delete trigger on the
SubscriptionTopic could never legitimately fire. Subscribers reading the
topic today would otherwise be misled into building handlers for an
event the IG forbids servers from producing.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the `delete` trigger on the FAST Consent `SubscriptionTopic` and
**reconcile in the other direction**: update the Technical Specification
to explicitly permit `Consent` deletion under defined circumstances
(e.g., administrative purge after a defined retention period, or
correction of erroneously-created `Consent` instances that were never
acted upon), and add `delete` to the `Consent` `interaction` list in
`ConsentAdministrativeServerCapabilities`. Retain the SHALL NOT for
deleting *revoked* consents, but carve out the narrower cases where
delete is permitted, and tie the SubscriptionTopic's `delete` trigger to
those carved-out cases so subscribers can be notified.

#### Justification

The ticket itself offers this as an option, and it is the only way to
keep a `delete` trigger on the SubscriptionTopic without leaving a
contradiction in the IG. Some consent-management deployments do need to
support administrative deletion (data-correction workflows,
retention-policy purges, GDPR/CCPA-style erasure requests for records
that were never relied upon) and benefit from being able to subscribe to
those events. The trade-off versus Disposition A is materially larger
scope: it requires new normative text in the Technical Specification,
edits to the CapabilityStatement, and careful drafting to keep the
`$revoke-consent` model (which forbids deletion of revoked consents)
intact. That additional surface area is harder to land cleanly in a
clarification ticket.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket without changing the `SubscriptionTopic`,
CapabilityStatement, or Technical Specification. Treat the apparent
mismatch as a documentation question and add only an editorial note in
the SubscriptionTopic narrative cross-referencing the *Revoke Consent
Operation* section.

#### Justification

Declining is hard to defend here. The reporter has identified a concrete
internal contradiction — the SubscriptionTopic advertises a trigger that
the CapabilityStatement and the normative SHALL NOT in the Technical
Specification forbid Admin Servers from producing. That is precisely the
kind of clarification a published IG should resolve before the next
ballot/publication cycle, especially given the ticket's "Highest"
priority and "Persuasive" resolution. Leaving the artifacts as-is risks
implementer confusion and divergent server behaviour.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A matches the resolution the work group has already recorded
on the ticket and is the least-invasive way to remove the contradiction.
The IG's normative model treats consent revocation as an
operation-driven state change (`$revoke-consent`) explicitly forbidding
deletion of revoked consents, and the Admin Server CapabilityStatement
already excludes `delete` from `Consent`'s supported interactions —
together those make `delete` an event Admin Servers cannot legitimately
emit, so removing it from the `SubscriptionTopic` trigger list is a pure
correction rather than a behavioural change. Disposition B (carving out
permitted deletes) could be revisited as a separate enhancement ticket
if implementer demand for administrative-delete subscriptions emerges,
but should not block this clarification.
