## Summary

The reporter requests that section 4.2.1.1 of the Scalable Consent Management
(FHIR) guide be updated so that the **patient is explicitly depicted as a
tangential partner in the consent relationship tree visual**. The underlying
concern is that, as consent flows across systems and is changed, revoked, or
added to, the patient must remain informed and able to interpret the current
state of their consent. The reporter suggests that a business process
management (BPM) layer with access to consent language and status should
make consent activity (revoke/update/add) easily interpretable by the
patient. No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items
were found for this ticket.

## Details

**Description:**

> Under 4.2.1.1, add the patient as a tangential partner in the relationship
> tree. The patient needs to be informed as the consent management flows
> across systems, etc. Changes, revocations, etc should all be accessible by
> the patient. A business process management (BPM) with access to consent
> language and status should be easily interpretable (did they revoke,
> update, add, etc) by the patient.

**Resolution (already recorded on the ticket — Not Persuasive with Modification):**

> The consenter can be the patient or a caregiver or guardian. The use
> cases and operations will support the consenter as part of the process.
> But the explicit informing of a patient is out of scope of this guide.
> We will be defining our actors and will make it clear what the
> "consenter" actor entails.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the Scalable Consent Management (FHIR) IG, section 4.2.1.1, to add
the **Patient** as a tangential party in the consent relationship tree
diagram, alongside the existing parties (consenter, custodian, requester,
etc.). Add accompanying narrative immediately under the diagram describing
the patient's informational role: that the patient is to be kept apprised
of the current consent state and any changes (additions, updates,
revocations) as consent flows across systems, and that downstream BPM/UI
tooling is expected to surface the consent language and status to the
patient in interpretable form. Add a non-normative sub-section
("Patient awareness of consent state") referencing this requirement.

#### Justification

The request reflects a real privacy expectation: a patient whose data is
governed by a consent should be able to discover the current state of that
consent. Making the patient visible in the relationship-tree diagram
mirrors patient-centric framing used elsewhere in HL7 guidance and is a
low-cost narrative/diagram change that does not alter the conformance
surface of the IG. It also pre-empts criticism during ballot that the
guide's central diagram omits the data subject.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Do not add the patient as a separate node in the 4.2.1.1 relationship-tree
diagram. Instead, address the underlying concern by:

1. Defining an explicit **Consenter** actor in the actors section that
   makes clear the consenter may be the patient, a caregiver, or a
   guardian, and pointing to that definition from 4.2.1.1.
2. Adding a short non-normative paragraph in 4.2.1.1 noting that, where
   the consenter is not the patient, downstream systems are expected to
   make consent state available to the patient via their patient-facing
   channels (portal, BPM tooling, etc.), but that the mechanism for doing
   so is **out of scope** of this guide.
3. Optionally, add a "See also" pointer to existing patient-access
   guidance (e.g., patient access APIs or notification frameworks)
   instead of inventing new machinery here.

#### Justification

This is essentially the disposition currently recorded on the ticket
("Not Persuasive with Modification"). It honours the substantive concern
— that the patient must not be invisible in consent management — while
keeping the IG focused on the FHIR mechanics it actually defines. Adding
the patient as a tangential node in the tree without any operational or
conformance content attached risks confusing implementers about the
patient's role in the consent exchange itself. Re-using the
consenter/patient-access vocabulary that already exists across HL7 is
cheaper and more consistent than inventing a new "tangential partner"
concept.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline to modify section 4.2.1.1. Mark the ticket as "Not Persuasive"
with the rationale that explicit patient notification is out of scope of
the Scalable Consent Management guide; the patient's role is already
covered by the consenter abstraction (which can be the patient or their
representative), and patient-facing surfacing of consent state belongs to
patient-access / portal IGs rather than to consent management itself.

#### Justification

The IG is scoped to the mechanics of managing consents at scale across
systems. Patient notification, BPM dashboards, and interpretable
patient-facing language are downstream UX/integration concerns that the
guide cannot meaningfully constrain without exceeding its scope. Adding
the patient to the relationship tree without any associated conformance
or operations is documentation drift that risks implementer confusion.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The reporter's concern is valid: the patient must not be invisible in a
consent-management framework. But adding the patient as a "tangential
partner" node to the relationship-tree diagram, without any operations or
conformance attached, would confuse rather than clarify — readers would
reasonably expect that node to imply specific FHIR exchanges or
constraints that the guide does not actually define. Disposition B
matches the recorded resolution ("Not Persuasive with Modification") and
is the most defensible path: keep the diagram focused on the actors that
participate in consent exchange, but explicitly define the **Consenter**
actor (patient/caregiver/guardian) and add a short non-normative note
that patient awareness of consent state is expected, with the mechanism
deferred to patient-access guidance. This addresses the substance of the
ticket without diluting the diagram or expanding the IG's scope.
