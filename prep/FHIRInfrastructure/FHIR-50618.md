## Summary

The reporter has identified a small bundle of editorial defects in the
`SubscriptionTopic` narrative pages of the FHIR Core specification
(sections 2.14.6.3, 2.14.6.4, and 2.14.6.7.2 of the `subscriptiontopic.html`
chapter). The defects are: (1) a misspelling of "servers" as "severs" in the
discussion of trigger/filter separation in §2.14.6.3, with a related
suggestion to rephrase the closing sentence so it talks about combining
*triggers and filters* rather than the ambiguous word "tests"; (2)
inconsistent capitalization of "Resource Interactions" (sometimes title-case,
sometimes mixed) in §2.14.6.4, which describes Subscription Topics built
from `SubscriptionTopic.trigger.resource` and
`SubscriptionTopic.trigger.supportedInteraction`; and (3) a missing leading
capital on the word "with" at the start of a sentence in §2.14.6.7.2's
discussion of `filterDefinition`/`filterParameter` and externally-defined
search parameters that share a "short" name. The submitter has filed this as
a Technical Correction; no normative or behavioural change is being
requested. There are no linked Jira tickets, related Jira tickets, Zulip
threads, or GitHub items associated with this issue in the available data.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the three editorial fixes to the `SubscriptionTopic` narrative
chapter of FHIR Core, exactly as the reporter has scoped them:

1. **§2.14.6.3** — change "severs" → "servers", and rewrite the final
   sentence of the paragraph to:
   > "An implementation is free to separate triggers and filters or combine
   > them as desired."
   (replacing the ambiguous "combine the tests as desired", which uses a
   term — "tests" — that the surrounding text only applies to filters).
2. **§2.14.6.4** — make "Resource Interactions" consistently lower-case
   ("resource interactions") in the body prose of this section, matching
   how the term is used elsewhere in the same paragraph and in the element
   names `SubscriptionTopic.trigger.resource` /
   `SubscriptionTopic.trigger.supportedInteraction`. Leave any genuine
   section heading style untouched.
3. **§2.14.6.7.2** — capitalise the leading "with" → "With" in the
   sentence "**With** Search Parameters, it is possible that two
   externally-defined filters share the same 'short' name."

These are pure narrative edits: no element definitions, cardinalities,
search parameters, or invariants change.

#### Justification

The defects are unambiguous editorial errors in published narrative. The
"severs" misspelling and the leading lower-case "with" are objectively
wrong. The "combine the tests" phrasing is genuinely ambiguous because the
surrounding paragraph only uses "tests" in the context of filters, so the
clarified wording removes a real reader-comprehension hazard without
changing meaning. As a Technical Correction with no normative impact, the
change is low-risk and fits the standard handling for typos in the
`SubscriptionTopic` chapter (FHIR Infrastructure / Subscriptions).

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept items (1) "severs" → "servers", (1b) the rephrased final sentence of
§2.14.6.3, and (3) the leading "With" capitalisation in §2.14.6.7.2 as
described in Disposition A. **Defer the §2.14.6.4 "Resource Interactions"
capitalisation change** and instead open it as a discussion item: agree on a
single capitalisation rule for "resource interactions" / "Resource
Interactions" (and the parallel terms "Query-based Topics" / "named events")
across the entire `SubscriptionTopic` chapter, then apply that rule in one
sweep. Either accept Weiyu's lower-case form or pick title case, but apply
it to all occurrences of all three sibling terms, not just one paragraph.

#### Justification

Items (1), (1b) and (3) are non-controversial mechanical fixes and can land
immediately. The "Resource Interactions" item, however, is symptomatic of a
chapter-wide style inconsistency: the same chapter uses inconsistent
capitalisation for several "topic style" terms, not just this one. Patching
a single paragraph risks creating a new inconsistency between §2.14.6.4 and
its siblings, which would just generate the next ticket. Splitting the work
this way preserves the velocity of the obvious typo fix while making sure
the capitalisation question is resolved coherently for the whole chapter.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the ticket as a "no normative impact, content too small to action"
editorial complaint, on the grounds that none of the items affect element
definitions, conformance, instance validation, or interoperability, and
they can be picked up incidentally during the next normative-cycle
copy-edit pass on the `SubscriptionTopic` chapter rather than spending
ballot/CR cycles on them now.

#### Justification

This is the weakest of the three options and is included for completeness.
A "Decline" here is hard to defend on its own: the "severs" misspelling
in particular is objectively wrong in a published normative resource, and
the §2.14.6.7.2 sentence begins with a lower-case word, both of which are
the kind of issue Technical Corrections exist to clean up between
publications. The only colourable rationale for declining is workload
prioritisation, which is generally not considered a sufficient reason to
reject a Technical Correction whose fix is essentially zero-cost.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The three reported defects are textbook Technical Corrections to the
`SubscriptionTopic` narrative: a literal misspelling, an ambiguous wording
in the trigger/filter separation paragraph, and a sentence that begins with
a lower-case word. None of them touch element definitions, search
parameters, or conformance, so the risk surface is limited to the affected
paragraphs of `subscriptiontopic.html`. Disposition B is a defensible
refinement if the workgroup wants to pull on the capitalisation thread for
the whole chapter, but that work can be done as a follow-up; it should not
hold up the obvious typo fixes. Disposition C is included for completeness
only — declining a clean, scoped Technical Correction with a working
substitute text from the reporter would be hard to justify to the
submitter.
