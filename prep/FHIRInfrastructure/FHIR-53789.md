## Summary

The reporter has noticed that a sentence — "A list cannot be the subject of an intervention or a direct action" — was added to the Scope and Usage section of the `List` resource. They are asking FHIR Infrastructure to clarify what "subject of an intervention" is intended to mean, particularly in light of the established practice of using `List.mode = changes` to communicate reconciliation outcomes (for example, discharge medication lists that carry added and deleted items, where the list itself drives a downstream workflow). The reporter proposes that if the underlying intent is "Lists are not themselves clinical targets, but rather artifacts that communicate or support workflow actions," then the wording should be made explicit, since the current sentence sits in the opening paragraph and is easy to misread as forbidding any actionable use of `List`.

The cross-reference graph surfaces a single FHIR-element link, `List.mode`, which is the very mechanism the reporter cites as the case the new wording risks contradicting. No related Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were returned by the augury index for this issue.

## Details

**Description:**

> I see that the sentence "A list cannot be the subject of an intervention or a direct action" was added to the Scope and Usage section. Could you clarify what is meant by "subject of an intervention" in this context, particularly given the use of `List.mode = changes` to communicate reconciliation outcomes (e.g., discharge medication lists with added and deleted items)? If the intent is simply that Lists are not themselves clinical targets, but rather artifacts that communicate or support workflow actions, it would be helpful to state that more explicitly, especially since this sentence appears in the opening paragraph.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Revise the Scope and Usage opening paragraph of the `List` resource so that the new sentence — "A list cannot be the subject of an intervention or a direct action" — is replaced (or immediately followed) by a clarifying formulation along the lines of:

> "A `List` is an informational artifact that organizes or communicates other resources; it is not itself a clinical target. Clinical interventions and direct actions apply to the items the list references (e.g., the medications, conditions, or observations), not to the list resource. Lists may, however, be used to drive or document workflow — for example, `List.mode = changes` is the conformant way to convey reconciliation outcomes such as added/removed discharge medications."

The change is purely narrative: no element, cardinality, binding, invariant, or example is altered. Update the resource's Scope and Usage section in the FHIR Core spec only.

#### Justification

The reporter's reading is a reasonable one: in the current opening paragraph the bare sentence reads as a blanket prohibition, and `List.mode = changes` is a long-standing, explicitly-defined mechanism whose purpose is precisely to let a `List` carry actionable change information into a downstream workflow. Distinguishing "the list is not the clinical target" from "the list cannot participate in workflow" preserves the intent of the new sentence (don't model interventions on `List` itself) while removing the apparent contradiction with `List.mode`. The change is low-risk because it is a narrative clarification with no normative or schema impact.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the new sentence in place but split the clarification into two narrative moves rather than a single rewrite:

1. In the opening paragraph, soften the sentence to "A `List` is not itself the subject of clinical intervention or direct action — interventions and actions are recorded against the items the list references."
2. Add a short follow-up paragraph (or a sentence in the existing "Boundaries and Relationships" / "Background and Context" subsection, whichever the resource currently uses) that explicitly calls out workflow uses, naming `List.mode = working | snapshot | changes` and giving discharge medication reconciliation as the canonical `mode = changes` example, with a pointer to `List.entry.flag` and `List.entry.deleted`.

No element-level changes; the change is confined to the `List` resource's narrative subsections.

#### Justification

This preserves the editor's original intent (the new sentence was presumably added to push back against people modelling interventions on `List` itself) while giving the workflow story its own home rather than crowding the opening paragraph. Putting the workflow clarification in a dedicated subsection makes it discoverable from the resource's TOC and is more consistent with how other FHIR Infrastructure resources separate "what this resource is" from "how it is used in workflow." The trade-off versus Disposition A is slightly more editorial work and a denser opening paragraph, in exchange for a clearer long-term narrative structure.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as "Not Persuasive" / no spec change. Reply to the reporter that the existing sentence is intentional: "subject of an intervention or a direct action" refers to the `List` resource being the clinical target of an action (e.g., `Procedure.subject = List/...`), and is not intended to constrain the use of `List.mode = changes` for reconciliation, which remains conformant. No narrative change is made.

#### Justification

A pure-narrative ticket that turns on the reading of one sentence can sometimes be addressed by an explanatory comment rather than a spec edit, especially if the WG judges the wording to be unambiguous in context. Declining keeps the recently-added sentence stable and avoids the editorial churn of re-opening the opening paragraph. The cost is that the ticket has already surfaced a real ambiguity from at least one careful reader, which suggests the wording will trip up other implementers; a comment-only response leaves that latent confusion in place.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is recommended because the reporter has identified a genuine ambiguity that a spec comment alone will not fix for the next reader, and because the workflow story around `List.mode = changes` (discharge reconciliation, snapshot vs. changes vs. working) is substantive enough that it deserves its own narrative slot rather than a parenthetical inside the opening paragraph. Disposition A is acceptable and lower-effort, but folding the full clarification into the opening paragraph risks producing a long, qualified sentence that is itself hard to parse. Disposition C should be avoided: the very existence of this ticket, against a sentence that is only weeks old in the spec, is evidence that the current phrasing does not stand on its own. The Infrastructure WG should plan to (a) soften the opening sentence and (b) add an explicit workflow-uses note that names `List.mode` and references the discharge-reconciliation example the reporter raised.
