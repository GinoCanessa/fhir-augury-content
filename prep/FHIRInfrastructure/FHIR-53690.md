## Summary

Lloyd McKenzie requests that the `Requirements.actor.key` element be made
mandatory (cardinality `1..1` instead of the current `0..1`). The argument
is that `actor.key` is the identifier used by other elements (notably the
per-statement actor references introduced via FHIR-47737) to point at a
specific entry in the `Requirements.actor` BackboneElement, so an entry
without a `key` cannot be referenced and therefore serves no purpose.
There are no other Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items
cross-referenced to this ticket in Augury at the time of preparation;
the closest related ticket surfaced by content search is the already-
Applied FHIR-47737 ("Support actor links at the statement level"), which
introduced statement-level actor links keyed by `actor.key` and is the
practical motivation for tightening the cardinality here.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Change the cardinality of `Requirements.actor.key` from `0..1` to `1..1`
in the FHIR Core specification. Concretely, in the `Requirements`
StructureDefinition, set `min = 1` on the element
`Requirements.actor.key` and update the narrative cardinality column in
the resource page accordingly. No change to the element's type
(`id`), short description, or definition is required. Existing
`Requirements` instances generated under R5 (where `actor.key` is
already strongly recommended in practice and used by the
statement-level link added via FHIR-47737) will overwhelmingly already
populate this element; the change formalises current practice.

#### Justification

`Requirements.actor.key` is the identifier that downstream elements —
notably the statement-level actor link added by the Applied
FHIR-47737 — use to point at a specific entry in
`Requirements.actor`. An `actor` entry with no `key` is unreachable
from any such reference and therefore carries no useful linkage
information beyond what could be captured by listing the actor in
prose. Making `key` mandatory is consistent with FHIR's general
pattern that BackboneElement entries used as link targets carry a
required identifier (e.g., `Questionnaire.item.linkId`,
`PlanDefinition.action.id` when referenced,
`Bundle.entry.fullUrl` in transaction Bundles). It also closes a small
interoperability hazard where two implementers could legitimately
disagree on whether an unkeyed actor was a defect or a deliberate
choice.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Leave the cardinality at `0..1` and instead add an invariant on
`Requirements.actor` of the form:

- `key.exists() or %context.statement.actor.where($this = %this).empty()`

(or an equivalent FHIRPath expression) that requires `actor.key` to be
present whenever the resource contains any statement-level reference
that could target this actor entry, and update the element definition
of `Requirements.actor.key` to state that it is mandatory in practice
and may only be omitted in `Requirements` instances that contain no
keyed actor references. Optionally raise the element to "Must Support"
in the base resource.

#### Justification

This preserves backward compatibility with any R5 instances or
profiles that may currently omit `actor.key` (for example, simple
"informational" Requirements where the actor list is purely narrative
and statements are not linked to actors). It still prevents the only
problematic case — a dangling statement-level actor link — by tying
the requirement to its actual usage rather than to the structural
shape of the resource. The cost is additional invariant complexity
and a slightly subtler conformance story, which IG authors and
validators must implement correctly.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Leave `Requirements.actor.key` at its current `0..1` cardinality and
take no action. Document in the element definition (if not already
clear) that `key` SHALL be populated whenever any other element
references this actor, but do not change the structural cardinality.

#### Justification

The argument for declining rests on (a) the change being a breaking
constraint tightening on a recently published resource, which any
existing R5 instance omitting `actor.key` would fail to validate
against, and (b) the fact that an unkeyed actor entry, while
unreachable from statement-level links, is not strictly malformed —
the actor's `type`, `description`, and other attributes still convey
information. If no real-world implementations are known to be
producing unkeyed actors, the practical risk is low and a
specification clarification in the element definition may suffice.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The change is small, structurally clean, and aligns the resource with
how `Requirements.actor.key` is actually used after FHIR-47737 added
statement-level actor links. An `actor` entry without a `key` cannot
be the target of any structured reference and offers no expressive
power beyond what `actor.description` and the other sibling elements
already provide; making `key` mandatory eliminates an ambiguous corner
case rather than removing functionality. Disposition B's invariant
approach achieves the same safety guarantee at the cost of a more
complex conformance rule that implementers and validators are easily
prone to misimplement, and Disposition C leaves a known foot-gun in
place. The backward-compatibility concern is real but narrow: this is
a tightening of a single optional id-typed element on a resource that
is still maturing, and the FHIR Infrastructure work group is the
right body to make that call early rather than after wider adoption.
