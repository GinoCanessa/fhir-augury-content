## Summary

The ticket reports that the language in Bundle section 6.1.2.2.9, rule 5 — which
describes the special `Provenance.target` value `#` for self-signing — is
ambiguous. As written, it is unclear whether listing `#` as a target means the
Provenance signs *itself* in addition to the Bundle, whether the resulting
signature transitively covers the Provenance signature element, and how `#`
relates to the canonical "sign the bundle" target. The reporter asks for
clearer wording.

This sits inside a tranche of signature-related comments filed by Bas van den
Heuvel after a security expert review of the revised R6 signature page (see
the Zulip topic *Signature comments* in `fhir/infrastructure-wg`). The
companion ticket [FHIR-52810](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-52810)
("Signature on Bundle should not use id") was resolved *Persuasive with
Modification* with the workgroup agreeing to use a JSON-Pointer-style fragment
`#/` to refer to the containing Bundle (instead of `Bundle/[id]`) and to
update the page rules and worked examples — that resolution implemented in
[HL7/fhir#4028](https://github.com/HL7/fhir/pull/4028). FHIR-52811 is the
follow-up question of what the *bare* `#` target means in that same scheme
(i.e., does it mean "the Provenance also signs itself as a Bundle entry, but
without the digital-signature blob"), and how it composes with `#/`. The
sibling ticket [FHIR-52812](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-52812) asks the
related question of whether additional canonicalization rules are needed when
the whole Bundle (including the signing Provenance) is signed; the commenter
on FHIR-52811 explicitly links the two.

## Details

**Description:**

> Section 6.1.2.2.9 says:
>
> > 5. The Provenance resource is allowed to indicate that it signs additional
> > resources, including the special target value `#` which indicates that it
> > signs itself in addition to the Bundle (but not including any
> > `Provenance.signature` containing a digital signature).
>
> Does this mean that a target `#` signs the bundle including the Provenance?
> The sentence is a bit confusing. Please improve.

**Comments:**

- **JIRAUSER26199 — 2025-09-24:** Notes the wording is clearer than before
  but raises two scenarios (single-signature only, ignoring co-sign /
  countersign): (1) if the signing information is *not* itself signed, the
  Provenance has one target `#/` (per the resolution of FHIR-52810); (2) if
  the signing information *is* signed, the Provenance has two targets, `#/`
  and `#`. Argues case (1) is unlikely to be wanted in practice if (2) is
  already supported, and proposes that `#/` simply mean "signs both the
  Bundle and the current Provenance" so the design is unambiguous. If case
  (1) is genuinely needed, a dedicated canonical rule could cover it. Links
  the discussion to FHIR-52812.
- **ehaas — 2025-09-24:** Proposes alternative wording:
  > "The Provenance resource is allowed to indicate that it signs additional
  > resources, including the special target value `#` — which means that the
  > Provenance signs itself as one of the Bundle entries (but not any
  > digital signatures in the `Provenance.signature` element)."

## Proposed Dispositions

Note: there are no GitHub cross-references on this ticket, so no per-repo
briefing was loaded. The change scope is editorial wording inside the FHIR
Core Bundle page (section 6.1.2.2.9), the same area touched by the
FHIR-52810 resolution. Dispositions name that page directly.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the wording proposed by ehaas (with one small tightening). Replace the
current rule 5 in Bundle section 6.1.2.2.9 with:

> 5. The Provenance resource is allowed to indicate that it signs additional
> resources. The special `Provenance.target` value `#` means the Provenance
> signs **itself, as one of the Bundle entries**, in addition to whatever
> other targets are listed (including `#/` for the containing Bundle). The
> bytes covered by `#` are the Provenance entry's canonicalized form
> **excluding any `Provenance.signature` element that carries a digital
> signature**, so that the Provenance can be self-signed without the
> chicken-and-egg problem of signing the signature itself.

Add a one-paragraph worked example showing a Provenance whose
`target` list is `[#/, #]`, explaining that `#/` covers the Bundle (per the
FHIR-52810 resolution) and `#` separately covers the Provenance entry minus
its own digital-signature blob.

#### Justification

This is what the existing prose was trying to say; the issue is purely
editorial. The ehaas wording (a) names the unit being signed ("itself, as
one of the Bundle entries"), removing the ambiguity that prompted the
ticket, and (b) is explicit that the digital-signature element is excluded
from the bytes — which is the only coherent interpretation if `#` is to be
useful at all. Tying the example to `#/` keeps it consistent with the
resolution of [FHIR-52810](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-52810) /
[HL7/fhir#4028](https://github.com/HL7/fhir/pull/4028), which is already in
the same section.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — collapse `#` into `#/` semantics and define a canonical rule for the rare case

#### Proposal

Adopt JIRAUSER26199's suggestion. Redefine the meaning of `#/` so that it
*always* covers both the Bundle and the signing Provenance entry (excluding
the Provenance.signature digital-signature element), and **drop the bare
`#` target entirely** from rule 5. For the (rare) case where a caller
genuinely wants to sign only the Bundle without binding the signing
Provenance, define a new canonicalization rule —
`http://hl7.org/fhir/canonicalization/{fmt}#bundleWithoutSigningProvenance`
(name to be bikeshedded) — and reference it from rule 5 instead of
introducing a second target value.

This pulls the resolution of FHIR-52811 and
[FHIR-52812](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-52812) into one consistent
story: the *target* says *what* is signed; the *canonical rule* says *how*
the bytes are derived. It also avoids requiring implementers to construct
multi-element `provenance.target` arrays for the common "sign the whole
document" case.

#### Justification

The current design encodes "is the Provenance itself in scope?" as the
presence/absence of a second target value (`#`), while encoding "what bytes
of those resources?" as the canonicalization rule. Splitting one decision
across two mechanisms is what made the prose hard to write in the first
place. JIRAUSER26199 argues — credibly — that the bare-`#`-without-`#/`
case has no real use case, and the bare-`#/`-only case (Bundle without
signing Provenance) can be handled by a dedicated canonical rule, which is
already the established extension point for this kind of variation.
Trade-off vs. A: this is a *normative* change to the meaning of `#/` agreed
in [FHIR-52810](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-52810), not just an
editorial fix, so it costs another round of WG review and risks
re-litigating the recently-applied FHIR-52810 resolution; it also has to
land jointly with FHIR-52812.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket *Not Persuasive*. The current wording, read together with
the resolution of [FHIR-52810](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-52810) and
[HL7/fhir#4028](https://github.com/HL7/fhir/pull/4028) (which introduced
`#/` for the Bundle and updated the surrounding worked examples), is
already sufficient: `#` is the JSON-Pointer fragment for "this resource",
so `#` as a Provenance target unambiguously means "this Provenance resource
itself", excluding the digital-signature element by the rule that already
follows. No spec change is needed.

#### Justification

The rule is technically correct as written and is consistent with the
JSON-Pointer fragment convention already adopted for `#/`. Once a reader
has seen the FHIR-52810 wording, the meaning of `#` falls out by analogy.
Spending another R6 ballot cycle re-wording an already-correct sentence has
opportunity cost.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter's complaint is concrete and editorial: a sentence in section
6.1.2.2.9 reads ambiguously, and at least three reviewers (the reporter,
ehaas, JIRAUSER26199) interpreted it differently in writing. That is by
itself sufficient evidence that the current wording is not pulling its
weight, which makes Disposition C hard to defend even though the rule is
arguably correct. Disposition B is technically more elegant and would
unify the resolution with FHIR-52812, but it reopens the just-applied
FHIR-52810 design and changes the meaning of `#/`; that is a much larger
swing for a Triaged Medium ticket and should not be folded into an
editorial fix without explicit WG appetite. Disposition A — adopting the
ehaas wording, tightened to make the digital-signature exclusion explicit
and to cross-reference the `#/` convention from FHIR-52810 — resolves the
ambiguity at the smallest possible blast radius, keeps the door open to do
B later if the workgroup decides to act on FHIR-52812, and can be applied
as a small follow-up to PR #4028 in the same section.
