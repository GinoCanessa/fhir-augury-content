## Summary

Eric Haas requests a rewrite of the **Digital Signature Methods** section
on the FHIR Core `signatures.html` page. He raises two specific concerns
with the current text:

1. The paragraph that singles out **SMART Health Cards** (a W3C Verifiable
   Credentials *Data Model* secured by JWS) as an example of an
   internationally adopted signed-FHIR profile is extraneous to a section
   whose purpose is to document the use of the `Signature` data type and
   `Provenance` for CMS, JWS, and XML signature methods.
2. The advisory that "mixing formats may create challenges, and
   implementers SHOULD generally use JSON signatures with JSON
   canonicalization in JSON resources, and XML signatures with XML
   canonicalization in XML resources" is, in his view, conjecture: the
   common signature methods are content-format agnostic, and the choice
   of signature/canonicalization is generally a business / deployment
   decision rather than something dictated by the wire format of the
   resource.

He provides a concrete proposed rewrite (hosted in his personal fork,
`Healthedata1/fhir`, at `source/signatures.html`) that deletes both the
SMART Health Cards sentence and the "mixing formats" SHOULD, while
keeping (and slightly reinforcing) the statement that the listed signature
methods are agnostic to content format.

No Jira tickets cross-reference FHIR-53955 and no GitHub items
cross-reference it. The cross-reference index returned zero hits at the
time of preparation. A Zulip thread on related territory exists
(`IG creation > Signing Bundles`) where Grahame Grieve documents the new
IG-publisher Bundle-signing flow and Eric Haas himself notes that the
direction for R6 is to move signing onto `Provenance` and deprecate
`Bundle.signature` (decision pending). That discussion is adjacent to
this ticket's intent — modernising and tightening the normative guidance
on `signatures.html` — without overlapping it directly.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the reporter's proposed rewrite to the **Digital Signature
Methods** section in `source/signatures.html` (`HL7/fhir`), specifically:

- Delete the sentence beginning *"Note that SMART Health Cards is a W3C
  Verifiable Credentials Data Model…"* including the link to
  `hl7.org/fhir/uv/smart-health-cards-and-links/` and the COVID-19
  example.
- Delete the sentence *"However, mixing formats may create challenges,
  and implementers SHOULD generally use JSON signatures with the JSON
  canonicalization in JSON resources, and XML signatures with the XML
  canonicalization in XML resources."* (i.e. drop the
  format-coupling SHOULD entirely).
- Retain and lightly tighten the surrounding text so the section reads:
  the spec documents how to use `Signature` and `Provenance` for CMS,
  JWS, and XML signature methods; other methods can be used by
  extrapolation; CMS, JWS, XML, and other signature methods are agnostic
  to content format; and the choice of signature is independent of the
  resource's wire format and chosen canonicalization.

No changes to the `Signature` data type, `Provenance`, or any other
normative artifact are implied.

#### Justification

This is a tightly scoped, editorial change to a narrative page. Both
deletions are defensible on their own merits:

- The SMART Health Cards paragraph is a profile-specific aside on a
  page whose role is to give general guidance on `Signature` /
  `Provenance` use; pointing at one IG (and its COVID-era use case)
  reads as dated and gives the impression of endorsement that other
  IGs do not get.
- The "mixing formats" SHOULD is unsupported by the surrounding
  paragraph, which explicitly says signature methods are content-format
  agnostic. Grahame Grieve's own description of the IG-publisher
  signing flow in the Zulip *Signing Bundles* thread reinforces this:
  the publisher canonicalises to JSON regardless of resource wire
  format. Removing a SHOULD that contradicts current tooling avoids
  setting implementers up to fail conformance review against a rule
  the project itself does not follow.

Because the normative shape of `Signature` and `Provenance` is
unchanged, downstream IGs and existing implementations are unaffected.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the deletion of the SMART Health Cards paragraph, but **rephrase
rather than delete** the format-mixing guidance. Replace it with
non-normative text along the lines of:

> The CMS, JWS, and XML signature methods are agnostic to the wire
> format of the resource that carries the `Signature`. Implementations
> are free to combine any signature method with any resource format,
> using the canonicalization declared in `Signature.sigFormat` and
> `Signature.canonicalizationMethod`. In practice, choosing a
> canonicalization that matches an implementation's most common wire
> format (e.g. JSON canonicalization for predominantly-JSON pipelines)
> can simplify tooling and reduce round-tripping cost, but this is a
> deployment decision and not a conformance requirement.

Optionally, add a short forward-looking sentence (clearly marked as
non-normative) noting the in-progress R6 direction to prefer
`Provenance` over `Bundle.signature` for new work, mirroring Grahame's
suggested wording from the Zulip thread.

#### Justification

The reporter's underlying concern — that the SHOULD is conjecture and
not derivable from the FHIR content format — is correct and addressed by
this disposition. However, simply deleting the sentence loses a useful
implementer hint that explains *why* most deployments end up coupling
canonicalization to wire format. Reframing it as advisory ("can
simplify tooling … but is a deployment decision") preserves that
context, removes the unwarranted SHOULD, and aligns the page with
current IG-publisher behaviour (JSON canonicalization regardless of
resource format), which is exactly the situation Grahame describes in
*IG creation > Signing Bundles*. This costs little extra editorial
effort and pre-empts a follow-up "where did the canonicalization
guidance go?" ticket.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the rewrite. Leave `signatures.html` as-is for the current
publication cycle.

#### Justification

This would be defensible only on a strict scope/timing argument: with
larger R6 changes around `Bundle.signature` deprecation and Provenance-
based signing already being scoped (per the Zulip discussion), an
editorial-only patch to this section now risks being rewritten again
shortly. A reviewer arguing for Decline would say the page should be
rewritten **once**, when the R6 signing direction is settled, rather
than touched twice.

This is a weak rationale: the two sentences flagged in the ticket are
demonstrably inaccurate or unsupported today, and removing them now
makes the larger R6 rewrite easier, not harder. There is no harm to
implementers from removing a profile-specific aside and an unsupported
SHOULD.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B captures everything the reporter actually asks for — the
SMART Health Cards paragraph goes away, and the unsupported "JSON-with-
JSON, XML-with-XML" SHOULD is removed — while preserving the underlying
implementer hint as advisory text. That keeps the page useful to
readers who would otherwise wonder *why* canonicalization tends to track
wire format in practice, and it aligns the normative tone of the section
with the IG-publisher's actual behaviour (JSON canonicalization for any
resource format), which Grahame Grieve has already documented in the
*IG creation > Signing Bundles* thread. Disposition A is also
acceptable and is materially closer to the reporter's literal proposal;
the work group could safely fall back to A if it prefers a strictly
minimal editorial change. Disposition C should not be taken — leaving
demonstrably inaccurate guidance on the page until R6 lands costs
implementer credibility for no real benefit.
