## Summary

Corey Spears reports that the conformance sentence in the List resource's
Narrative Content section is internally inconsistent. The sentence reads:

> Each List.item should appear in the narrative for the resource; i.e. it
> SHALL NOT be necessary to retrieve the list items in order to have a
> human-readable rendering of the content.

The first clause uses a lower-case "should" (informative wording), while the
second clause — introduced by "i.e." (a paraphrase) — uses upper-case
"SHALL NOT" (RFC 2119 normative wording). The two clauses are presented as
restatements of each other, but they are not equivalent in conformance
weight: the first sounds optional, while the second is an absolute
prohibition. Implementers cannot tell whether they are permitted to omit
some `List.item` entries from the narrative (as the lower-case "should"
suggests) or whether doing so would violate the SHALL NOT (because the
narrative would then be insufficient on its own to render the list).

The ticket has no explicit Jira links and no Zulip or GitHub
cross-references in the Augury index. Keyword analysis surfaces several
adjacent tickets: [FHIR-3038](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-3038)
(historical re-wording of the same `list.html` descriptions, *Persuasive
with Modification*), [FHIR-44060](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-44060)
(US Core: lower-case "may" needs to be upper-case "MAY"), and
[FHIR-53892](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53892) (FHIR Infrastructure
clarification of a similarly ambiguous narrative-related sentence in the
digital signatures section). FHIR-53729 fits the same pattern as those:
small, surgical wording fixes to remove conformance-language ambiguity in
narrative-related guidance.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Edit the List resource's Narrative Content section in FHIR Core
(`list.html`) so that the offending sentence uses a single, consistent
RFC 2119 register. Specifically, replace:

> Each List.item should appear in the narrative for the resource; i.e. it
> SHALL NOT be necessary to retrieve the list items in order to have a
> human-readable rendering of the content.

with:

> Each `List.item` SHALL appear in the narrative for the resource; that
> is, it SHALL NOT be necessary to retrieve the list items in order to
> have a human-readable rendering of the content.

This promotes the lower-case "should" to "SHALL", removes the "i.e."
paraphrase mismatch, and makes both clauses say the same normative thing
in the same way.

#### Justification

This is the literal "fix the inconsistency" the ticket asks for and it
matches the precedent set by [FHIR-44060](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-44060),
where lower-case RFC 2119 keywords adjacent to upper-case ones were
promoted to upper case rather than rewritten. It is also the simplest,
lowest-risk reading of the existing intent: the SHALL NOT in the second
clause already implies that omitting items breaks the human-readable
rendering, so making the first clause SHALL is operationally equivalent
to what conformant implementations must already do today.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Restructure the sentence into two clearly distinct statements rather than
collapsing them into one normative restatement. In `list.html`, replace
the offending sentence with:

> The narrative for a List resource SHALL be sufficient to render the
> list to a human reader without retrieving the referenced `List.item`
> entries.
>
> Implementations SHOULD include a representation of every `List.item`
> in the narrative; alternative summarisations (for example, "12
> additional items not shown") are permitted provided the SHALL above is
> still met.

This separates the *outcome* requirement (the narrative must stand on its
own — SHALL) from the *recommended technique* for meeting it (include
every item — SHOULD), and explicitly carves out reasonable summarisation
patterns that implementers already use in long lists.

#### Justification

The current wording forces a single sentence to carry two different ideas
at two different conformance levels, which is the root cause of the
ambiguity Corey Spears identified — Disposition A dissolves the ambiguity
by deleting the SHOULD-flavoured idea, but that idea is itself useful
guidance. Splitting the statement preserves both, gives implementers an
explicit, conformant pattern for very long lists (which Disposition A
would arguably make non-conformant), and aligns with how
[FHIR-43783](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43783) was resolved in the
CQL IG: pick a register per statement and write each statement at that
register cleanly. The trade-off vs. Disposition A is a slightly larger
edit and a small risk of debate over the "summarisation is OK" carve-out;
that debate is worth having explicitly rather than leaving it implicit.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket *Not Persuasive* on the grounds that the operative
requirement (SHALL NOT need to retrieve items to render the narrative) is
already unambiguous, and the lower-case "should" in the lead-in clause is
informative scaffolding for that requirement, not an independent SHOULD.
No spec change; close with a clarifying comment on the ticket.

#### Justification

A defensible reading is that the "i.e." explicitly subordinates the
lead-in clause to the SHALL NOT, so a careful reader can already derive
the intended conformance semantics. Declining avoids touching widely-read
core resource text for what is arguably a stylistic concern. However,
this rationale is weak: the precedent in
[FHIR-44060](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-44060) and the existence of
the parallel ticket [FHIR-53892](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53892)
both indicate that the workgroup treats mixed-case RFC 2119 wording as a
real defect, not a stylistic preference, and that "a careful reader can
work it out" has not historically been an accepted answer.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition A is the smallest edit and is fully defensible under
FHIR-44060 precedent, but it quietly converts a SHOULD-flavoured
recommendation into a SHALL, and in doing so arguably outlaws the common
and reasonable practice of summarising very long lists in the narrative
("…and 47 more"). Disposition C ignores established workgroup behaviour
on this exact class of defect. Disposition B keeps the strong, unambiguous
SHALL that the section clearly intends (the narrative must stand on its
own), retains the SHOULD-level guidance to include each item, and
explicitly legitimises the long-list summarisation pattern — which is
exactly the kind of clarification Corey Spears is asking for. It also
sets a clean template that the workgroup can re-use when it gets to
[FHIR-53892](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53892), where the same
"informative-mixed-with-normative" pattern needs to be untangled. Before
committing the edit, the workgroup should confirm the exact authoring
path against the HL7/fhir repo briefing (this skill does not have that
briefing loaded because there are no GitHub cross-references on the
ticket).
