## Summary

The reporter, Vadim Peretokin, points out a discrepancy in the `Bundle`
resource invariant **bdl-3b**: the human-readable text and the FHIRPath
expression do not agree on the logical operator joining the `request` and
`response` clauses.

- The **human-readable text** says: *"For collections of type history, all
  entries must contain request **or** response elements, and resources if
  the method is POST, PUT or PATCH."*
- The **FHIRPath expression** says:
  `type = 'history' implies entry.all(request.exists() and response.exists() and ((request.method in ('POST' | 'PATCH' | 'PUT')) = resource.exists()))`

The text uses **OR** while the FHIRPath uses **AND**. Since invariants are
normatively defined by their FHIRPath, the human-readable description is
the side that is incorrect — every entry in a `history` Bundle must
contain **both** a `request` and a `response`, plus a `resource` whenever
the `request.method` is `POST`, `PUT`, or `PATCH`. This is a documentation
fidelity bug rather than a behavioural change. No cross-referenced Jira
tickets were returned for this issue.

## Details

**Description:**

> bdl-3b says For collections of type history, all entries must contain

> `type = 'history' implies entry.all(request.exists() and response.exists() and ((request.method in ('POST' | 'PATCH' | 'PUT')) = resource.exists()))`
>
> There is a discrepancy between the two.

## Proposed Dispositions

> Note: Because no GitHub cross-references were surfaced, no
> repo-analysis briefings were loaded for this ticket. The dispositions
> below name the affected artifact (`Bundle`) and invariant (`bdl-3b`),
> but do not cite specific authoring paths.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat this as a documentation-only technical correction in the FHIR Core
`Bundle` resource definition. Update the human-readable text of
invariant **bdl-3b** so that it matches the existing FHIRPath. Concretely,
change the human description from:

> *For collections of type history, all entries must contain request **or**
> response elements, and resources if the method is POST, PUT or PATCH.*

to (suggested wording):

> *For collections of type history, all entries must contain **both** a
> request **and** a response element, and must contain a resource if (and
> only if) the request method is POST, PUT or PATCH.*

Optionally also clarify the "if and only if" semantics that the FHIRPath
`= resource.exists()` already encodes (so that `GET`/`DELETE`/`HEAD` etc.
must **not** carry a `resource`). No change to the FHIRPath, no change to
the invariant key, no change to severity. Bump the resource maturity /
ballot status fields only as required by the editorial process for
documentation-only fixes.

#### Justification

Invariants are normatively defined by their FHIRPath, so the FHIRPath is
authoritative and the prose is wrong. Aligning prose with FHIRPath is a
non-breaking technical correction: implementers that already validate
against the FHIRPath are unaffected, and implementers that read only the
prose were already at risk of producing non-conformant `history` Bundles
(missing `response`, or carrying a `resource` on a `GET` entry). This is
exactly the class of change the "Technical Correction" type exists for.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than only patching the prose, treat FHIR-50118 as a trigger to do
a small audit and editorial pass on the **entire `bdl-3*` family** of
Bundle invariants (`bdl-3a`, `bdl-3b`, `bdl-3c`, `bdl-3d`) to ensure the
human text in each one accurately reflects its FHIRPath, including the
"if and only if" semantics implied by `=` (equality) constructs and the
specific Bundle types each one targets. Apply the prose fix from
Disposition A to bdl-3b, and apply equivalent prose fixes to any sibling
invariants found to drift. Optionally add a short normative reminder near
the Bundle invariants table that the FHIRPath is authoritative when text
and FHIRPath disagree.

#### Justification

The `bdl-3*` invariants are closely related and were authored together;
historically when one drifts, the others tend to drift too (the Zulip
thread for FHIR-24860 explicitly walks through this whole family while
auditing `ElementDefinition.condition`). Fixing only bdl-3b leaves a
known inconsistency pattern in place. The marginal cost of auditing four
sibling invariants in the same editorial pass is small, and it reduces
the chance of a follow-up "bdl-3c text doesn't align with fhirpath"
ticket. Trade-off vs. Disposition A: slightly larger editorial scope and
review surface, but identical risk profile (still documentation-only,
still non-breaking).

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as **Not Persuasive** on the grounds that the FHIRPath is the
normative definition of the invariant and the human text is explicitly
non-normative; therefore there is no conformance impact and no fix is
strictly required.

#### Justification

This is the weakest of the three options but is included for
completeness. The argument is that FHIR's invariant machinery already
documents that the FHIRPath is the source of truth and that the prose is
a courtesy summary; an implementer relying on the prose alone is already
out of spec. Declining avoids spending editorial cycles on
non-conformance text.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition C should be rejected: even if the FHIRPath is normatively
authoritative, FHIR is read by a very large audience that uses the prose
as their working definition (tooling vendors, profile authors,
implementers writing their first `history` Bundle), and a description
that uses **OR** where the constraint requires **AND** is actively
misleading and will produce non-conformant Bundles in the wild.
Disposition A correctly fixes the immediate bug at minimal cost.
Disposition B does the same fix and additionally hardens the surrounding
`bdl-3*` invariants in one editorial pass, which is the cheapest moment
to do that audit and prevents a likely follow-up ticket on a sibling
invariant. The incremental cost is small (a single editor-day at most)
and the risk is identical to A (text-only, non-breaking, no FHIRPath
change). The work group should adopt B unless there is editorial-cycle
pressure that argues for the strictly minimal A.
