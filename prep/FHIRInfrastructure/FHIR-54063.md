## Summary

Hayden Bader filed this ticket as a Comment on the R6 Signatures spec to flag a real-world implementation that depends on `Bundle.signature`: the Finnish Kanta national e-health platform. Finnish regulation requires FHIR data to be cryptographically signed, and Kanta's published "FHIR Specification for Electronic Signature" mandates the use of `Bundle.signature` to satisfy that requirement (see `https://www.kanta.fi/en/system-developers/fhir-specification-for-electronic-signature`). The reporter notes that the working R6 proposal — moving signing to embedded `Provenance` resources that are excluded from the canonicalized payload — should in principle be workable for Kanta if they migrate to R6, but argues that HL7 should engage the Finnish regulator directly before deprecating `Bundle.signature`. The reporter further suggests that if `Bundle.signature` is *not* deprecated in R6, the spec needs sharper canonicalization rules describing exactly which parts of `Bundle.signature` (if any) are included in the signed bytes. The single cross-reference resolved by the indexer is to the FHIR artifact `Bundle.signature` itself; there are no Jira links and no Zulip messages that mention this ticket key, so the broader context comes from related Zulip threads on the same subject (see below).

In reviewing the R6 Signatures spec, I figured I'd also write this up as a comment:

There's regulation in Finland that requires data be sent over FHIR, and part of that paradigm has involved making sure the data is signed - using Bundle.signature.

https://www.kanta.fi/en/system-developers/fhir-specification-for-electronic-signature

As far as I can tell, the new plan to have Provenances embedded in a Bundle (and not be a part of the data that is incorporated into the signature) should be able to work for them if they moved to R6, but it might be worth trying to start a dialogue with the relevant regulatory body directly.

If Bundle.signature were to not be deprecated, it would probably be worth adding some canonicalization updates to call out how much of the Bundle.signature (if any) were incoprated into the signature.

## Related Zulip Discussions

### [IG creation > Signing Bundles](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/IG%20creation/topic/Signing%20Bundles)

Grahame Grieve announced (Jun 2025) that the IG publisher will sign Bundles automatically when the source contains a `signature` with `sigFormat: application/jose` and no `data` element, using the `http://hl7.org/fhir/canonicalization/json` canonicalization, with key material supplied either by the validator (local builds), the `-go-publish` build chain, or `/input/signing-key.json`. In August 2025 Hynek Kružík asked for guidance on whether to sign at the Bundle level or via Provenance for FHIR Documents; Eric Haas and Grahame both confirmed the current direction is "proposal is to move to Provenance and deprecate `Bundle.signature` in FHIR R6, however a decision has not been made yet." Grahame indicated that IG-publisher signing of Provenance is planned but unscheduled, and warned that JSON canonicalization is straightforward while XML canonicalization remains hard. This thread is the most direct source of the R6 plan that FHIR-54063 is reacting to.

Participants: Grahame Grieve, Hynek Kružík, Eric Haas.

### [Security and Privacy > Bundle.signature](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/Security%20and%20Privacy/topic/Bundle.signature)

A long-running thread (2021 → 2025) where the workgroup has repeatedly debated whether `Bundle.signature` is fit for purpose. In 2021 Eric Haas, John Moehrke, Lloyd McKenzie and Josh Mandel argued out three competing patterns: (a) sign in place via `Bundle.signature` with payload-attached or detached JWS, (b) wrap signed payloads in a separate `DocumentReference` envelope, and (c) move signatures onto `Provenance`. Recurring concerns were ambiguous canonicalization, the risk of malicious insertion into unsigned parts of `Bundle.signature`, and lack of guidance/examples beyond the spec. In Jul–Aug 2025 Grahame restarted the discussion on behalf of FHIR-I with three R6 options — leave `Bundle.signature` at 0..1, change it to 0..*, or deprecate it in favour of `Provenance` — and the group leaned toward deprecation while explicitly tabling the decision pending broader input. Lloyd argued multiple signatures should live on `Provenance` (better tooling, avoids IGs fighting over the singleton slot), Eric pointed out 0..* extensions have the same problems, and John Moehrke pushed back that significant prototyping investment exists for `Bundle.signature` and almost none for `Provenance.signature`, and that "signature blocking" (which `.signature` element gets excluded from the hash for simple, co-, and counter-signatures) is still under-specified. No consensus was reached.

Participants: Eric Haas, John Moehrke, Lloyd McKenzie, Josh Mandel, Grahame Grieve.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat the ticket as new evidence that an active national-scale regulator-mandated deployment depends on `Bundle.signature`, and respond on two tracks:

1. **Process / outreach (FHIR-I action item, not a spec change):** open formal liaison contact with Kela / Kanta (the body that publishes the Finnish "FHIR Specification for Electronic Signature") before any decision to deprecate `Bundle.signature` in R6 is finalised. Capture the outcome of that conversation as a comment on this ticket and on the parent R6 Signatures discussion.
2. **Spec change (FHIR Core, in the Signatures / `Bundle.signature` material):** if the workgroup ultimately decides *not* to deprecate `Bundle.signature` in R6, augment the canonicalization rules in the FHIR Signatures page (`signatures.html`) and the `Bundle.signature` element definition to spell out exactly which fields inside `Bundle.signature` are included in the canonicalized bytes vs. blanked — covering the simple-signature, co-signature, and counter-signature cases that John Moehrke called out as still under-specified in the Security and Privacy Zulip thread.

#### Justification

The reporter is not asking for a redesign — they are asking the workgroup to (i) verify with a real regulator that the proposed migration path actually works for them and (ii) close a known gap in the existing canonicalization specification if the element is retained. Both are low-risk and consistent with how FHIR-I has handled prior national-implementer concerns. The Zulip record (`Security and Privacy > Bundle.signature` and `IG creation > Signing Bundles`) shows the deprecation decision is explicitly tabled pending broader input, so soliciting Kanta input is squarely in scope, and the canonicalization gap is a known issue that John Moehrke has flagged repeatedly and that already affects the IG publisher's signing implementation.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Use the Kanta evidence to support the deprecation path rather than to retain `Bundle.signature`, but lower migration risk for Kanta and similar deployments by:

1. Proceeding with the working R6 plan to deprecate `Bundle.signature` and move signing to embedded `Provenance` resources excluded from canonicalization.
2. Adding to the R6 Signatures page a normative migration recipe explicitly worked through against the Kanta use case: how an existing `Bundle.signature`-based signing profile maps 1:1 onto a `Provenance` with the equivalent signers, purposes-of-signature (`CommitmentTypeIndication`), and JOSE/JAdES canonicalization.
3. Prioritising IG-publisher support for signing `Provenance` resources (Grahame confirmed this is in the plan but unscheduled in the `IG creation > Signing Bundles` thread) so that the R6 path has tooling parity with the current `Bundle.signature` path before R6 normative.
4. Marking `Bundle.signature` as Deprecated (not Removed) in R6, so existing Kanta payloads remain valid FHIR R6 instances during a multi-year migration window.

#### Justification

The reporter themselves notes the R6 Provenance-embedded-in-Bundle approach "should be able to work for them if they moved to R6". The substantive Zulip consensus from Lloyd, Grahame, and (with reservations) Eric is that `Provenance` is the better long-term home for multi-signature semantics, and Grahame's stability argument against widening `Bundle.signature` to 0..* (it is the most cross-version-stable resource we have) cuts against keeping it as the primary mechanism. A well-documented migration recipe plus deprecation-not-removal addresses the regulatory deployment risk without entrenching a mechanism the workgroup has already identified as architecturally inferior. This also responds directly to the reporter's secondary ask — clearer canonicalization rules — by avoiding the "what's actually signed inside `Bundle.signature`" problem entirely.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket as "Persuasive with Mod" / "Not Persuasive" with the resolution that no spec change is required: the existing R6 Signatures direction (move to `Provenance`, deprecate `Bundle.signature`) already accommodates the Kanta use case as described, and outreach to a national regulator is operationally HL7-International / affiliate business, not an HL7 ballot resolution. The reporter's secondary canonicalization concern is moot if `Bundle.signature` is deprecated in R6.

#### Justification

The ticket type is `Comment` and it does not propose a concrete spec change; the reporter explicitly concedes that the R6 Provenance approach should work for Kanta. Treating "open a dialogue with the Finnish regulator" as a ballot disposition stretches the change-tracker process beyond what FHIR-I normally resolves through Jira. If the workgroup is confident in the deprecation direction (which the Zulip threads indicate is the leading position), the cleanest disposition is to acknowledge the input, point to the existing R6 plan, and close.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The reporter's own analysis — that R6's Provenance-based approach should work for Kanta — combined with the Zulip thread's prevailing direction (Lloyd, Grahame, and the FHIR-I call all leaning toward deprecation, with the decision tabled only to gather more input like exactly this comment) indicates that the workgroup should commit to the deprecation path rather than freeze it on Kanta's behalf. However, Disposition C under-uses the input: the reporter has handed FHIR-I a concrete, regulator-mandated deployment to validate the migration story against, and John Moehrke's "significant prototyping for `Bundle.signature`, almost none for `Provenance.signature`" warning is a real risk that a published migration recipe and prioritised IG-publisher tooling would directly mitigate. Disposition B captures both: it endorses the deprecation, but conditions the R6 normative milestone on having a worked Kanta-style migration recipe and Provenance-signing tooling in the IG publisher, and it preserves `Bundle.signature` as Deprecated-not-Removed so that Finnish implementers have a realistic transition window. Disposition A (engage Kanta directly + tighten canonicalization) is a reasonable fallback if the workgroup is not yet ready to commit to deprecation, and the regulator-engagement step from A can sensibly be folded into the execution of B.
