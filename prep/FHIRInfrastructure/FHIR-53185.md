## Summary

The ticket asks that the FHIR Core narrative section on tag/profile/security-label
updates (`resource.html#tag-updates`) be amended to mention the `$meta-add` and
`$meta-delete` operations alongside the already-mentioned `$meta` operation.
The reporter (John Moehrke) observes that this section currently discusses how
the meta tags behave under PUT/PATCH and references `$meta`, but omits the two
companion operations that exist precisely to add or remove individual
tag/profile/security-label entries. The change is purely editorial — a
documentation/cross-reference fix in the spec narrative — and was prompted by
an implementer-channel discussion on chat.fhir.org about HAPI's behaviour when
updating `meta.security` via PUT.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Edit the `resource.html#tag-updates` narrative in the FHIR Core spec to
mention `$meta-add` and `$meta-delete` alongside `$meta` wherever the latter
is referenced. Specifically, in the paragraph(s) that explain how clients
should manipulate `Resource.meta.tag`, `Resource.meta.security`, and
`Resource.meta.profile` outside of a full PUT, add a sentence (or extend the
existing one) along the lines of: "The `$meta`, `$meta-add`, and
`$meta-delete` operations (see [Resource Operations]) are the supported way
to add or remove individual tag, security-label, or profile entries without
rewriting the whole resource." Cross-link each operation name to its
operation definition page (`resource-operation-meta.html`,
`resource-operation-meta-add.html`,
`resource-operation-meta-delete.html`).

#### Justification

The change is exactly what the reporter — and the originating Zulip thread —
asked for. It is editorial: no normative semantics change, no breaking impact
on implementers or compliance. Mentioning the trio together resolves the very
implementer confusion that triggered the ticket (PUT-vs-append behaviour for
`meta.security`) by making the existing fix-paths discoverable from the
section readers actually reach when they look up "tag updates". It also
restores symmetry with how `$meta-add`/`$meta-delete` are already documented
on their own operation pages.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Same goal, broader fix: in addition to inserting the operation references,
add a short normative-style note (non-normative call-out box) in
`resource.html#tag-updates` that explicitly addresses the recurring
"PUT replaces vs appends meta entries" question. The note would (a) state
that PUT replaces `Resource.meta` like any other element, subject to the
server's preservation rules already described in this section, (b) point to
`$meta`/`$meta-add`/`$meta-delete` as the surgical alternatives, and (c)
cross-link to the equivalent guidance for PATCH. Optionally, file a parallel
ticket against the operation pages themselves so each `$meta*` page links
back to `resource.html#tag-updates`.

#### Justification

Per Lloyd McKenzie's comment in the Zulip thread, "we probably need to
provide guidance on how to explicitly remove tags." The literal request fixes
discoverability, but the underlying confusion is semantic — implementers
don't know which mechanism is the "right" one for partial meta updates.
Bundling the operation mentions with a short PUT/PATCH-vs-`$meta*` callout
addresses the root cause once, rather than leaving the next reader to
re-derive it. The added scope is still purely editorial and stays inside
FHIR Infrastructure's remit.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as "Not Persuasive — already addressed elsewhere." Rationale: the
`$meta`, `$meta-add`, and `$meta-delete` operations each have their own
dedicated specification pages and are listed under the Resource operations
index; the `resource.html#tag-updates` section is intentionally focused on
PUT/PATCH semantics, and additional cross-linking risks duplicating content
that lives on the operation pages.

#### Justification

This is the defensible "no-change" stance, but it is weak. The fact that the
operation pages exist did not prevent the originating Zulip confusion, and
the reporter explicitly recalls the references being present in this section
previously. The cost of a one- or two-sentence addition is negligible and the
benefit (discoverability for the precise audience that lands on
`#tag-updates`) is real, so declining mostly only makes sense if the
workgroup wants to defer the editorial pass to a larger spec-cleanup ticket.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the right call. The literal ask in Disposition A is
unobjectionable and should be accepted at minimum, but the originating Zulip
thread shows the underlying problem isn't just missing links — implementers
hit `#tag-updates` looking for an answer to "how do I replace vs add vs
remove a security label?" and walk away without one. A short call-out that
contrasts PUT/PATCH semantics with the `$meta*` operations resolves that in
one place and aligns with Lloyd McKenzie's expressed need for explicit
guidance on removing tags. The change is editorial, low-risk, and lives
entirely within FHIR Infrastructure; if scope feels like a concern, the
work can be scoped to "Disposition A plus a 2–3 sentence note" with the
parallel operation-page back-links spun off as a follow-up.
