## Summary

The ticket reports an internal contradiction in the FHIR Core specification page
`references.html` (FHIR R6 ballot 3). The introductory section
[`#2.1.3.0`](https://hl7.org/fhir/6.0.0-ballot3/references.html#2.1.3.0) tells
the reader that the subject context of a resource is **not** inherited across
references, and then states "**The only exception to this is the case of
contained resources** (see below)." However, the [`#contained`](https://hl7.org/fhir/6.0.0-ballot3/references.html#contained)
subsection it points at says the opposite: contained resources **do not**
"inherit" context from their parent resource (so if both parent and contained
resource have a `subject`, no implication is made that they are the same).

Because these two passages directly contradict each other, the introductory
"only exception" sentence is wrong (or, equivalently, the `#contained` section
is wrong). The reporter — Martin von Siebenthal — asks the work group to
resolve the contradiction.

The contradiction has a clear historical cause that is relevant to the
disposition: previously contained resources **did** conduct context, and
[FHIR-3691](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-3691) ("Context conduction in
contained is dangerous", *Persuasive with Modification*, Published) argued
this was unsafe and was applied. The introductory paragraph was apparently
not updated when the `#contained` section was rewritten, leaving the stale
"only exception" carve-out behind. Two earlier tickets on the same page,
[FHIR-25801](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-25801) ("Typo on references.html",
Persuasive) and [FHIR-37658](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-37658)
("Description of Resource References-possible typo?", Persuasive), corrected
adjacent wording in the same paragraph but did not touch the contradictory
sentence flagged here.

## Details

**Description:**

> The introduction https://hl7.org/fhir/6.0.0-ballot3/references.html#2.1.3.0
> explains that "there is no automatic rule or implication that [in this
> example] the condition has the same patient for its subject" and "… the
> context of the subject is not inherited".
>
> It then states "**The only exception to this is the case of contained
> resources** (see below)."
>
> But then the section on
> https://hl7.org/fhir/6.0.0-ballot3/references.html#contained explains
>
> "Resources that are contained inline **do not "inherit" context from
> their parent resource**. For instance, if the parent resource contains
> a 'subject', and the contained resource also has a 'subject' element
> defined, there is no implication that the contained resource has the
> same subject as the parent resource."
>
> So the contained resources are **not an exception**.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat the introduction in `source/references.html` (rendered at
`references.html#2.1.3.0`) as the wrong side of the contradiction and fix the
narrative there to match the `#contained` section, which already (correctly)
states that contained resources do not inherit context.

Concretely, in `HL7/fhir` edit `source/references.html` and remove the
sentence "The only exception to this is the case of contained resources (see
below)." from the introductory paragraph that currently reads "… the context
of the subject is not 'inherited', nor does it 'conduct' along the
relationship to condition. The only exception to this is the case of
contained resources (see below)." Replace it with a sentence that aligns
with the `#contained` subsection — for example: "The same is true for
contained resources: a contained resource does not inherit context (such as
`subject`) from the resource that contains it; see *Contained Resources*
below." Leave the `#contained` subsection unchanged. Run a repo-wide search
in `HL7/fhir` for the old "only exception" phrasing and for any inbound
fragment links that quoted it, and update them to the new wording.

#### Justification

The current `#contained` text reflects the resolved position from
[FHIR-3691](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-3691) (*Persuasive with
Modification*, Published), which explicitly rejected context conduction into
contained resources as unsafe. The introductory sentence is therefore stale
narrative left behind from before FHIR-3691 was applied; reverting it to
match the `#contained` section restores internal consistency and matches the
work group's already-published design intent. The change is a pure HTML
edit under `source/` (FhirCore authoring root from the briefing), no
generated artefacts are touched, and the repo-analysis briefing's only
caveat — checking for inbound fragment / wording references before merging —
is straightforward to satisfy.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Address the same contradiction, but rather than just deleting the "only
exception" sentence, restructure the `#2.1.3.0` introduction so contained
resources are explicitly called out as an *example* of the no-inheritance
rule (rather than as an exception). In `source/references.html`, replace the
two-sentence sequence ("… the context of the subject is not 'inherited' …
The only exception to this is the case of contained resources (see below).")
with a single paragraph that:

1. States the general rule (no inheritance / no conduction across
   references), then
2. Explicitly notes that this rule **also** applies to contained resources
   (with a forward link to the `#contained` anchor), and
3. Adds a one-sentence pointer that older versions of the spec previously
   allowed context conduction into contained resources but that this was
   removed (cite FHIR-3691 in the change history rather than in the page).

Optionally, add an `<a name="…"></a>` anchor on the new paragraph so future
tickets can deep-link to it.

#### Justification

Disposition A is the minimal fix; Disposition B is the same fix plus a small
clarifying restructure. The advantage is that readers who arrive at the
introduction expecting an exception (because that wording has been in the
spec for a long time and is referenced in older training material and
implementer notes) get an explicit "no, contained is not an exception"
statement instead of silent removal. The trade-off is more reviewer churn
and more places where the "Procedure / Condition" example wording (already
adjusted by FHIR-25801 and FHIR-37658) might need a follow-up edit, so this
costs a little more than A.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket *Not Persuasive* on the basis that the published narrative,
read in full, is unambiguous: the `#contained` subsection authoritatively
defines the contained-resource behaviour, and the introductory "exception"
sentence — while loose — does not change the normative content because
`references.html` is informative narrative, not normative material.

#### Justification

This is the defensible "do nothing" framing if the work group judges the
risk of editing well-trafficked narrative (and inadvertently breaking
inbound links or implementer expectations) to outweigh the value of removing
a contradictory sentence in informative prose. The argument is weak,
however: even informative narrative is expected to be internally consistent,
and the contradiction is exactly the kind of issue the *Technical
Correction* type exists for. Decline should only be chosen if the work
group has an explicit reason not to touch `references.html` in the current
ballot cycle.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The contradiction is real, narrowly scoped, and has a clear historical
cause: the introduction was not updated when
[FHIR-3691](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-3691) was applied to the
`#contained` section. Disposition A removes the stale carve-out with the
smallest possible change, aligns the introduction with the already-published
position on context conduction, and lands as a single-file HTML edit in the
FhirCore authoring root (`source/references.html`) — exactly the "narrative
fix" shape the cached `HL7/fhir` briefing identifies as low-risk. Disposition
B is a reasonable richer alternative if the work group wants to take the
opportunity to make the no-inheritance rule explicit for contained
resources, but the additional churn is not necessary to resolve the
ticket. Disposition C is hard to defend given that this is filed as a
*Technical Correction* against narrative the work group has already
revisited twice ([FHIR-25801](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-25801),
[FHIR-37658](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-37658)) for similar
clarity-only fixes.
