## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the editorial corrections to the FHIR Core "Signatures" page in
`HL7/fhir` at `source/signatures.html`:

- `avilable` → `available`
- `occurrances` → `occurrences`
- `seperate` → `separate`

In the same edit, perform a one-pass review of `source/signatures.html`
for any remaining abbreviations and spell them out (e.g., `e.g.` →
`for example`, `i.e.` → `that is`, expand any informal contractions),
honouring Lloyd's broader instruction that "abbreviations need to be
fully spelled out". Limit the edit to that single file; do not touch
StructureDefinition `<snapshot>` blocks, generated areas, or
`source/datatypes/*.xml` spreadsheets. Mark the ticket Resolved – Persuasive
with a short note enumerating the substitutions made.

#### Justification

The request is unambiguous, scoped to a single narrative page, and
trivially safe under the FhirCore "Update narrative / prose" recipe
documented in the repo briefing. Editorial corrections to misspellings
have no normative impact, no schema impact, no downstream IG impact, and
no terminology impact, so the change can be made in `source/signatures.html`
without any cross-repo coordination. Refusing or deferring obvious
spelling fixes — especially ones flagged by the work group's own
imported ballot comment — costs the spec credibility for no benefit.
The "expand abbreviations" sweep is bounded by the same single page, so
it does not expand scope in a way that would justify splitting the
ticket.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the three misspelling fixes exactly as listed
(`avilable`/`occurrances`/`seperate`) on `source/signatures.html`, but
explicitly defer the broader "abbreviations need to be fully spelled
out" instruction to a separate, lower-priority editorial ticket (or a
generic "spec-wide editorial sweep" tracker). Resolve FHIR-55343 with a
note that only the three named substitutions were made under this
ticket and that any other abbreviations on the page were left for a
follow-up.

#### Justification

The "fully spell out abbreviations" wording in the description is open-
ended and could reasonably extend beyond the signatures page, beyond
the three named misspellings, or to legitimate technical abbreviations
(e.g., FHIR, URI, JSON, XML, OID) where expansion would actually harm
readability. Keeping FHIR-55343 narrowly scoped to the three concrete
spellings keeps the change reviewable, audit-trail-friendly, and avoids
silently rolling editorial judgement calls into a ticket that the
reporter framed as a small correction. The briefing's "do not silently
roll out-of-scope concerns into a fix" pattern (called out for the
prior FML ticket) is good general hygiene to apply here as well.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline FHIR-55343 as out of scope for the current ballot cycle on the
grounds that purely editorial misspellings can be batched into a
post-ballot copy-edit pass and do not need an individually-tracked
work-group disposition. Close the ticket as "Not Persuasive — Editorial,
batch via copy-edit pass" and add the three substitutions to a running
editorial-fixes list maintained by the publisher team.

#### Justification

The work-group queue is finite and "Highest"-priority slots are better
spent on substantive normative or design questions; spelling
corrections do not change implementer behaviour and do not affect
conformance. A blanket policy of declining single-typo tickets in
favour of a batched editorial sweep is defensible administratively and
mirrors how some other HL7 projects handle minor copy-edits. (This
disposition is included for completeness; see the Recommendation
below.)

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The change is an obvious, low-risk editorial fix to a single
specification page (`source/signatures.html`) under the FhirCore
"Update narrative / prose" recipe, with no cross-repo, schema, or
terminology impact. The reporter is the work group's own incoming
review-comment importer, the misspellings are concretely named, and
the cost of fixing them is essentially zero. Disposition B's
deferral is unnecessary overhead — the abbreviation sweep is bounded
to the same single file and can be performed in the same commit
without scope drift, and the committer can use editorial judgement
to leave well-known technical acronyms (FHIR, URI, JSON, XML)
unchanged. Disposition C undervalues the credibility cost of leaving
visible misspellings on a published page when the fix is trivial; a
"Highest"-priority Technical Correction filed by Lloyd McKenzie should
not be punted to an unscheduled batch pass.
