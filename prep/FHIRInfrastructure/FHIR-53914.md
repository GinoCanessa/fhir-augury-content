## Summary

Eric Haas (FHIR-I) is asking for the **"Signing Bundles"** section on
`signatures.html` (FHIR R6 ballot) to be rewritten for clarity and technical
accuracy. The existing prose tells implementers they may "use enveloping
signatures (JSON, XML)" as an alternative to embedding a Provenance signature
in a Bundle entry. Eric flags two problems:

1. **The term "enveloping signatures" is wrong outside XMLDSig.** Enveloping is
   a structural relationship that only has meaning in XML signatures; JWS and
   CMS have fundamentally different structures, so the term does not generalise.
2. **The attached/detached distinction is irrelevant** in this context — either
   could be used regardless of whether the Provenance is in the Bundle or not.

The ticket supplies **proposed replacement text** (drafted in a fork at
`Healthedata1/fhir/source/signatures.html`) that:

- Introduces "Signing Bundles" by saying the **most common** use is to sign a
  Document Bundle and carry the signature as a Bundle entry via a Provenance
  resource, while explicitly allowing implementers to make the Provenance
  resources available **separately** rather than embedded.
- Replaces the "enveloping vs. detached" sentence with implementation-neutral
  guidance.
- Restates the embedded-signature rules as a clean numbered list:
  Provenance carries the signature; `Provenance.target.reference =
  "Bundle/[id]"` identifies which Provenance is the embedded signature for the
  Bundle; `Provenance.signature` SHALL be present; and the Provenance is
  either added/re-added after signing or signed itself using special target
  values (`#` for self, `fullUrl` for another Provenance signature entry, with
  the note that a target may live outside the Bundle).
- Keeps the standing notice that `Bundle.signature` is **deprecated** in
  favour of the Provenance-based approach and that applications SHOULD adopt
  it (and pre-adopt for earlier FHIR versions).

There are **no formally linked Jira tickets** and the cross-reference index
surfaces only artifact mentions (`Provenance.target.reference`,
`Provenance.signature`, `Provenance.signature.data`, `Bundle.signature`),
which is consistent with this being a self-contained editorial/clarification
change to one specification page. The supporting design context, however, is
substantial: a long Zulip thread between Eric Haas and Grahame Grieve in
**Security and Privacy > Provenance Signature** (July 2025) is the
conversation that produced these embedded-signature rules in the first place,
and the **IG creation > Signing Bundles** thread shows that the IG publisher
already implements an embedded JOSE signature on `Bundle.signature` and is on
a roadmap to move to Provenance — exactly what this ticket's prose
formalises.

## Details

**Description:**

> Note that it is not necessary to sign Bundles with embedded signatures like
> this — systems and/or ecosystems may choose to use enveloping signatures
> (JSON, XML), where the signed data is included within the signature itself,
> or make the Provenance resources available separately.

Update the Signing Bundles sections for clarity and readability.

Note:

1. The term "enveloping signatures" is incorrect. Enveloping signatures only
   have meaning for XMLDSig, because they describe structural relationships
   that are specific to XML's structure, but JWS and CMS have fundamentally
   different structures.
2. Whether the signature is attached or detached is irrelevant in this
   context. Either one could be used, whether the Provenance resource is in
   the Bundle or not.

Proposed text (source:
<https://github.com/Healthedata1/fhir/blob/master/source/signatures.html>):

> ### Signing Bundles
>
> The most common use of signatures in FHIR is to sign a Document Bundle and
> include the signature as a Bundle entry. In this approach, Provenance is
> used to carry a signature, which is added to the Bundle as an entry before
> or after the signature is created. Implementers may choose to make the
> Provenance resources available separately, rather than as a bundle entry.
> However, if the signature is being included in the Bundle, then the
> following rules SHALL be observed:
>
> 1. Provenance is used to carry a signature.
> 2. `Provenance.target.reference = "Bundle/[id]"` where id is the id of the
>    Bundle. In other words, the Bundle itself is being signed.
> 3. There may be other Provenance resources in the Bundle that carry a
>    signature. But if `Provenance.target.reference != "Bundle/[id]"` they
>    are not embedded signatures, and not subject to the rules documented
>    here.
> 4. The Provenance resource SHALL have a `Provenance.signature` element for
>    the Bundle signature.
> 5. The Provenance resource is either:
>    1. Added to the Bundle as a new entry after the signature is created.
>    2. Removed before the signature is created, and updated and added back
>       to the Bundle as a new entry after the signature is created.
>    3. Signed as described below.
> 6. `Provenance.target.reference` can reference other resources in addition
>    to its Bundle contents.
>    1. To sign itself minus the `Provenance.signature.data` which holds the
>       digital signature, the special target value "#" is used.
>    2. To sign another Provenance signature entry in the Bundle, the target
>       value `target = fullUrl` of the other Provenance entry is used. In
>       such cases, the `Provenance.signature.data` is also signed.
>    3. The target may not be in the Bundle; however, this specification does
>       not define how such references are resolved.
>
> The Bundle resource includes the `Bundle.signature` element. Because of
> several unresolved issues with its use, the element is deprecated.
> Applications SHOULD use the method described above to sign Bundles and
> pre-adopt it when working with earlier versions of FHIR.

## Related Zulip Discussions

### [Security and Privacy > Provenance Signature](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/Security%20and%20Privacy/topic/Provenance%20Signature)

This is the design conversation that produced the very rules this ticket is
trying to write down cleanly. John Moehrke opened it (June 2025) arguing that
any Signature-datatype changes must work for `Provenance.signature`, not just
`Bundle.signature`, and that for both XMLDSig and JWS the natural pattern is
per-resource hashes (so verifiers can validate a subset of resources). In
late July 2025, Grahame Grieve and Eric Haas ran a long back-and-forth that
landed the **embedded-Provenance-in-Bundle** model: a Provenance carrying an
embedded signature is a Bundle entry whose `target = Bundle/[id]`; if the
target is anything else it is **not** an embedded signature; signatures in
the Bundle must exclude the entire signature-carrying entry (not just
`signature.data`); a Provenance can also sign itself using `target = "#"`;
and ordering of multi-target arrays should follow `Provenance.target` order
(consistent with JCS, RFC 8785). Grahame also flagged that the "use an array
of things to be signed" pattern has a downside — it forces JSON for all
signed content. Open issues that remained at the end of the thread (and that
this ticket does *not* try to resolve) include whether `Provenance.signature`
should be a backbone with `sigFormat`/`targetFormat`/`data` plus invariants,
and how multi-signer / co-signature semantics interact with
`Provenance.agent.type`.

Participants: John Moehrke, Grahame Grieve, Eric Haas.

### [IG creation > Signing Bundles](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/IG%20creation/topic/Signing%20Bundles)

Grahame announced (June 2025) that the IG publisher will sign Bundles
automatically when an entry has `signature.sigFormat = "application/jose"`
and no `data` element, using a published canonicalization
(`http://hl7.org/fhir/canonicalization/json`) and either a built-in key (for
ci-build / local builds) or an author-supplied JWK in
`/input/signing-key.json`. In August 2025, Hynek Kružík asked which approach
implementers should use for Document Bundles; **Eric Haas** answered "the IG
builder is using `Bundle.signatures` today; the proposal is to move to
Provenance in FHIR R6 and deprecate `Bundle.signature`, however a decision
has not been made yet," and **Grahame** asked that the wording be softened
to "proposal is to move to Provenance and deprecate `Bundle.signature` in
FHIR R6, however a decision has not been made yet." Hynek welcomed the
direction; Grahame confirmed Provenance-signature support in the IG
publisher is "definitely in the plan" but unscheduled. This thread is the
direct evidence that Eric's proposed prose (which formalises the
embedded-Provenance approach **and** keeps the `Bundle.signature` deprecation
notice) reflects current FHIR-I/IG-publisher direction.

Participants: Grahame Grieve, Eric Haas, Hynek Kružík.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Replace the current "Signing Bundles" section of
`HL7/fhir/source/signatures.html` (anchor `<a name="SigningBundles">`) with
the verbatim text Eric supplied, namely:

1. Drop the existing leading sentence that uses the term **"enveloping
   signatures (JSON, XML)"** and replace it with the new opening paragraph:
   *"The most common use of signatures in FHIR is to sign a Document Bundle
   and include the signature as a Bundle entry. … Implementers may choose to
   make the Provenance resources available separately, rather than as a
   bundle entry. However, if the signature is being included in the Bundle,
   then the following rules SHALL be observed:"*
2. Replace the existing rule list with the seven-rule list as quoted in the
   **Details** section above (Provenance carries the signature;
   `target.reference = Bundle/[id]` defines what is/is not an embedded
   signature; `Provenance.signature` SHALL exist; the
   added/removed-and-readded/self-signed lifecycle; and the `#` and `fullUrl`
   special target values, including the note that the target may live outside
   the Bundle).
3. Keep the trailing block-quoted notice that `Bundle.signature` is
   deprecated and that applications SHOULD use the Provenance-based method,
   pre-adopting it for earlier FHIR versions.
4. Preserve the `<a name="SigningBundles"></a>` anchor so existing inbound
   links from elsewhere in the spec continue to resolve.
5. No StructureDefinition, ValueSet, code-system, or example-resource changes
   are implied; the edit is confined to `source/signatures.html`.

#### Justification

The change is **narrow, prose-only, and entirely consistent with the FhirCore
"update narrative / prose" recipe** for HL7/fhir — the page is hand-edited
HTML under `source/` and the publisher does not regenerate it. Eric's two
technical objections are correct and well-supported:

- **"Enveloping signature" is an XMLDSig-specific term** (RFC 3275 / W3C
  XML-DSig defines `enveloped`, `enveloping`, and `detached` as XML-tree
  structural relationships). JWS (RFC 7515) and CMS (RFC 5652) have
  fundamentally different structures — JWS has a JOSE header + payload +
  signature triple (compact or JSON serialisation), and CMS has a
  `SignedData` ASN.1 structure. Telling a JWS implementer to use an
  "enveloping signature" is a category error.
- **Attached vs. detached is orthogonal** to whether the Provenance lives in
  the Bundle. A detached JWS over a canonicalised Bundle works the same
  whether the Provenance is an entry in that Bundle or returned separately.

The new text also matches what the **Security and Privacy > Provenance
Signature** Zulip thread agreed to (the `target = Bundle/[id]` discriminator,
the rule that the entire signature-carrying entry is excluded from the
signed content, and the self-signing `#` target), and it preserves the
deprecation message that the **IG creation > Signing Bundles** thread
already states is the FHIR-I direction. There is no cross-repo impact and
no risk to generated artifacts.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the substance of Eric's rewrite, but stage the change in two separate
edits to `HL7/fhir/source/signatures.html` so each can be tracked
independently:

1. **Editorial-only fix (this ticket).** Limit the change to:
   - replacing the misuse of "enveloping signatures (JSON, XML)" with
     implementation-neutral wording (e.g., "alternative signature
     serialisations such as JWS over a canonicalised Bundle, XMLDSig, or
     CMS — none of which require the Provenance to be embedded in the
     Bundle"), and
   - moving the existing Provenance-target rules into a clean numbered list
     without otherwise altering normative content.
2. **Spin off a follow-up Change Request** for the additions that go beyond a
   pure editorial fix, specifically:
   - the explicit `target = "#"` self-signing pattern,
   - the `target = fullUrl` pattern for one Provenance signing another
     Provenance signature entry (with `Provenance.signature.data` being part
     of the signed content), and
   - the "target may live outside the Bundle, resolution undefined" note.
   These three rules introduce or formalise behaviour that is still under
   open discussion in the **Security and Privacy > Provenance Signature**
   thread (e.g., whether `Provenance.signature` should become a BackboneElement
   with `sigFormat`/`targetFormat`/`data`, whether multi-target arrays canonicalise
   in `Provenance.target` order per JCS, and how co-signers interact).

Track the editorial fix to closure here; let the follow-up ticket carry the
co-signature/self-signature semantics, which gives Security & Privacy and
FHIR-I a chance to weigh in jointly before the rules become normative prose
in R6.

#### Justification

Eric's proposed text mixes a **clearly correct editorial fix** (drop
"enveloping", drop attached/detached) with **new normative-flavoured rules**
(self-signing via `#`, signing another Provenance entry via `fullUrl`,
external-target semantics) that the source Zulip thread shows are still
being negotiated. Splitting the work limits the risk of locking in
under-discussed semantics in the FHIR R6 ballot prose, and it gives a clean
audit trail per ballot comment. The cost is one extra ticket; the benefit is
that Disposition A's broader set of rules is not gated on resolving the
larger Signature-datatype / `Provenance.signature` BackboneElement
discussion.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Do not adopt the rewrite as a `signatures.html` change in this ballot cycle.
Instead, file the substantive technical concerns (misuse of "enveloping
signatures", irrelevant attached/detached distinction, undocumented
self-/cross-Provenance signing patterns) as **input to the
Security-and-Privacy-led Provenance.signature redesign** that is already in
progress (see **Security and Privacy > Provenance Signature** thread). Leave
the current prose in place until that redesign concludes and produces a
single coherent update to both the Signature datatype, `Provenance.signature`,
and the Signing Bundles narrative.

#### Justification

The defensible reason to decline is **scope and timing**: the same Zulip
thread that motivated this ticket explicitly leaves open whether
`Provenance.signature` will become a BackboneElement, whether elements like
`Provenance.signature.who` / `.when` should be deprecated in favour of
`Provenance.agent` / `.recorded`, and how multi-signer/co-signature
semantics work. Patching the prose now risks documenting rules
(especially the `target = fullUrl` cross-Provenance signing pattern) that
the broader redesign may invalidate or reframe. Declining preserves a
single, coherent update path through Security and Privacy plus FHIR-I rather
than a piecemeal series of `signatures.html` edits across ballots.

This is the **weakest** of the three options because the two purely
editorial corrections (the "enveloping signatures" misuse and the
attached/detached red herring) are independently correct and harmless to
fix now, regardless of how the larger redesign lands.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B captures the unambiguous wins of Disposition A — the
"enveloping signatures" misuse and the attached/detached red herring **are**
technical errors in the current text and should not survive into the R6
ballot — while ring-fencing the parts of the rewrite that are still being
worked out. The **Security and Privacy > Provenance Signature** thread is
unresolved on questions that directly touch the new `target = "#"` and
`target = fullUrl` rules in Eric's proposal (whether `Provenance.signature`
becomes a backbone, what happens with co-signers, and how multi-target
arrays canonicalise), and adopting all of those as normative prose now
would either pre-empt that work or be silently overridden by it. Splitting
the change has a low cost (one extra ticket) and a real upside: the
editorial fix lands cleanly in this ballot, and the embedded-signature rules
land **once**, after Security and Privacy and FHIR-I have jointly signed
off — exactly the workflow Grahame already asked Eric to use in the IG
creation thread when he asked for "proposal … however a decision has not
been made yet" framing. If the workgroup judges the embedded-signature
rules to already have rough consensus, escalate to Disposition A; do not
fall back to Disposition C, since the editorial corrections are
independently sound.
