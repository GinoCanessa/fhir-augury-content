## Summary

The reporter flags a typographical error in narrative prose in the FHIR Core
specification. A sentence reads "However, the use of an extension such as
DataAbsentReason cannot be used to bypass a required binding requirement**s**."
The trailing "s" makes "requirement" plural where the surrounding grammar
requires the singular form. The reporter notes the same typo recurs in the
sentence immediately below. No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or
GitHub items were found for this issue — it is a self-contained, narrowly
scoped editorial correction classified as a Technical Correction at Low
priority.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply a verbatim editorial fix in the FHIR Core specification narrative:
change both occurrences of "required binding requirement**s**" to "required
binding requirement" (singular). Locate the offending sentences in the
spec page that introduces the DataAbsentReason extension in the context of
binding strength (most likely `terminologies.html` / `terminologies-systems.html`
or a closely related terminology-binding section in the `HL7/fhir` source).
Submit as a no-impact narrative-only change with no schema, definition, or
example updates required.

#### Justification

The reporter has identified an unambiguous grammatical error: the singular
"a required binding requirement" is the only construction that parses
correctly in the sentence. Fixing typos in narrative prose is the canonical
purpose of the "Technical Correction" issue type, requires no design
discussion, and carries zero risk to implementers (no normative content is
altered). The fix is local to two adjacent sentences and the second
occurrence is explicitly identified by the reporter, so the scope is fully
bounded.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the correction, but additionally rephrase the surrounding sentence(s)
for clarity while the editor is in the file. For example, replace:

> "the use of an extension such as DataAbsentReason cannot be used to bypass
> a required binding requirement"

with something like:

> "extensions such as DataAbsentReason cannot be used to bypass a required
> binding"

This drops the redundant "the use of … cannot be used" construction and the
tautological "binding requirement," producing prose that is shorter, more
direct, and less prone to a future plural/singular slip.

#### Justification

While the reported typo is the immediate problem, the underlying sentence is
awkwardly constructed — "the use of X cannot be used" is doubly passive and
"a required binding requirement" is tautological (the binding *is* the
requirement). A small rewrite removes the conditions that produced the typo
in the first place and improves readability of a frequently-cited section
about binding strength semantics. The trade-off vs. Disposition A is a
slightly larger diff and the need for an editor with enough context to
ensure the rewrite preserves the original normative meaning.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close as "Not Persuasive" / no change. Leave the existing wording in place.

#### Justification

There is essentially no defensible rationale for declining a clear
grammatical typo in spec narrative — the work group exists in part to keep
the published specification clean. Documenting Disposition C is included
only for completeness; it should not be selected unless the reviewers
determine that the cited text has already been corrected upstream and the
ticket is a duplicate of an already-resolved fix (which the cross-reference
search did not surface).

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Accept the fix as requested. This is a low-risk, narrative-only correction
of a clearly identified typo in two adjacent sentences, exactly the kind of
issue the "Technical Correction" type is meant to dispose of without
extended discussion. Disposition B (the broader rewrite) is reasonable but
expands the scope beyond what the reporter raised and risks bikeshedding the
prose; if a reviewer is already in the file and confident in the
surrounding context, B can be folded into the same PR, but it should not
block the simple `s` removal. Disposition C has no merit on the facts as
presented.
