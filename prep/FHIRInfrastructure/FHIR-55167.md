## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply a one-word editorial fix on the FHIR Core `implementationguide`
page: change the single occurrence of "relevent" to "relevant". No
normative content, structure definitions, examples, or constraints are
affected. The change is purely a spelling correction in narrative text
on the ImplementationGuide resource's main page.

#### Justification

This is a Technical Correction at the highest priority from the FHIR
Infrastructure work group's reporter. The defect is unambiguous (a
misspelling of an English word with a single, well-known correct
spelling) and the fix is the smallest possible editorial change. There
is no design judgement involved, no backward-compatibility risk, and no
implementer impact. Accepting as requested is the standard disposition
for typo-class Technical Corrections.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the spelling fix from Disposition A, and additionally do a
broader sweep of the same `implementationguide` page (and, optionally,
the rest of the ImplementationGuide resource's narrative pages) for
other spelling errors, abbreviation expansions, and grammatical issues
flagged by an automated proofreading pass. Bundle the corrections into
a single editorial commit referencing this ticket so the work group
reviews them together rather than in a series of one-line tickets.

#### Justification

The reporter's note ("The page contains one or more misspelled or
abbreviated words … Specifics: 'relevent'") suggests the page may have
additional issues beyond the one called out. Bundling related editorial
fixes reduces ticket churn and review overhead, and is consistent with
how the FHIR Infrastructure work group often handles narrative-only
corrections. The trade-off vs. Disposition A is a slightly larger diff
to review and a small risk of unintentionally re-wording text that
implementers have learned to recognise; this is mitigated by limiting
the sweep to true spelling/abbreviation issues and not stylistic
rewrites.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change on the grounds that a single-word spelling
correction does not warrant a tracked Jira ticket and a publication
cycle entry, and instead handle it as an out-of-band editorial fix
without a ballot disposition.

#### Justification

This is the weakest of the three options and is included only for
completeness. Declining is hard to defend: the report is correct, the
fix is trivial, and the work group's standing practice is to accept
typo corrections so the public record shows the fix was made
intentionally. The only scenario where this disposition makes sense is
if the page text in question has already been corrected upstream and
the ticket is duplicative — which there is no evidence of here.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

This is a Highest-priority Technical Correction reporting a single,
unambiguous misspelling on a narrative page. Disposition A applies the
exact fix the reporter asked for, with zero risk to implementers and
trivial review burden. Disposition B is reasonable if the editor is
already in the file and willing to do a broader proofreading sweep, but
should not block the one-word fix; if the work group prefers, B can be
adopted as "A plus an opportunistic sweep". Disposition C is not
defensible for a clear typo report from a known reporter and is listed
only for completeness.
