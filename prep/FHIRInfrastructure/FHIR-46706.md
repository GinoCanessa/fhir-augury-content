## Summary

The reporter has identified a small grammatical typo in the SMART on FHIR token
introspection section. The current sentence describing the `exp` claim reads
"…will be consistent the with `expires_in` interval…", with the words "the" and
"with" transposed. The requested change is to correct the wording to "…will be
consistent **with the** `expires_in` interval…". This is a non-substantive
editorial fix; no semantic change to the introspection contract is requested.
There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items
linked to this ticket.

## Details

**Description:**

The current text reads:

> *exp*. The integer timestamp indicates when the access token expires. This
> timestamp will be consistent the with `expires_in` interval provided in the
> original access token response.

The reporter requests it be corrected to:

> *exp*. The integer timestamp indicates when the access token expires. This
> timestamp will be consistent **with the** `expires_in` interval provided in
> the original access token response.

(The reporter highlighted the corrected words "with the" in bold red in the
original Jira description.)

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the editorial correction to the SMART on FHIR token introspection
section. In the bullet describing the `exp` claim, change:

> "This timestamp will be consistent the with `expires_in` interval provided
> in the original access token response."

to:

> "This timestamp will be consistent **with the** `expires_in` interval
> provided in the original access token response."

The edit lands in the SMART App Launch source (canonically authored in the
`HL7/smart-app-launch` repository, in the token-introspection narrative page).
No normative content, conformance statement, or example changes; this is a
prose-only fix.

#### Justification

The reporter's reading is correct: "consistent the with" is a transposition
of "consistent with the". The corrected wording preserves the intended meaning
(the `exp` timestamp aligns with the `expires_in` lifetime returned from the
token endpoint) and reads as natural English. The ticket is filed as a
"Technical Correction", which is the appropriate Jira type for non-normative
editorial fixes, and no implementer behaviour is affected. Accepting as
requested is the lowest-risk path and clears the ticket without further
review cycles.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Apply the typo fix and, in the same edit, lightly restructure the sentence to
remove the residual ambiguity around what "consistent with" means. For example:

> "*exp*. The integer timestamp indicates when the access token expires. This
> timestamp MUST be consistent with the `expires_in` interval that was
> returned in the original access token response (i.e., `exp` ≈ token-issuance
> time + `expires_in`)."

This keeps the editorial fix the reporter asked for, but also tightens the
prose and (optionally) promotes the relationship between `exp` and
`expires_in` to a SHOULD/MUST conformance statement consistent with the rest
of the introspection section.

#### Justification

While the reporter's request is purely editorial, token introspection is a
security-relevant area and inconsistencies between `exp` and `expires_in` are
a real source of implementation bugs. Folding a small clarification into the
same change avoids re-opening the section later for a separate clarifying
ticket. The trade-off versus Disposition A is that adding any normative
language ("MUST"/"SHOULD") or restructuring the sentence converts a
"Technical Correction" into a substantive change, which technically warrants
broader workgroup review and may require a different ticket type.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket as "Persuasive — Not Persuasive" / Won't Fix on the grounds
that the existing wording, while awkward, is unambiguous to readers and does
not affect the normative meaning of the token-introspection section. Defer
the editorial fix until the next bulk grammar/copy pass over the SMART App
Launch IG.

#### Justification

The only argument for declining is to avoid spending workgroup cycles
processing single-character editorial tickets one at a time. Even so, this
rationale is weak: the change is trivially safe, the diff is one line, and
the reporter has done the work of identifying the exact correction. There is
no implementer-facing risk to declining, but also no defensible spec-quality
reason to do so.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Accept the ticket as requested. The change is a literal one-line prose fix to
a clearly broken sentence, ticketed under the correct Jira type ("Technical
Correction"), with no normative impact and no cross-cutting implications.
Disposition B is tempting but conflates a clean editorial fix with a
substantive clarification that should travel through its own ticket and
review; bundling them risks the editorial fix getting blocked on debate over
the conformance language. Disposition C has no real upside. Apply the fix in
`HL7/smart-app-launch` and close the ticket as Persuasive.
