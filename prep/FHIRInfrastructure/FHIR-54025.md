## Summary

Eric Haas asks the FHIR Infrastructure WG to rewrite the **JWS Signatures**
subsection of `source/signatures.html` so that it constrains the **FHIR
`Signature` datatype** (what each `Signature.*` element should hold) rather
than the **JWS payload itself** (which JOSE headers are mandatory, whether
the JWS must be detached, etc.). His rationale is that the FHIR core spec
should remain a flexible base that does not pre-empt use-case-specific
profiles — particularly because (a) JWS-standard mandates such as "the JWS
protected header SHALL contain either `kid` or `x5c`" belong in JWS
profiles, not in FHIR core; (b) JAdES `srCms` recommendations already point
to `http://uri.etsi.org/01903/v1.2.2` commitment-type codes, so FHIR should
align its `Signature.type` guidance with that existing vocabulary rather
than re-inventing it; and (c) there is no technical reason to mandate
detached vs. embedded JWS — that is an implementation decision.

He attaches concrete proposed prose (sourced from his fork at
`Healthedata1/fhir:source/signatures.html`) that re-frames every bullet
around a `Signature.*` element (`type`, `when`, `who`, `onBehalfOf`,
`targetFormat`, `sigFormat`, `data`) and *recommends* (`SHOULD`) that each
element agree with the corresponding JWS / JAdES header (`srCms`, `iat`/
`sigT`, certificate Subject Name / SAN, `application/jose`, etc.) instead
of mandating anything about the JWS itself.

The ticket has **no formal Jira links and no FhirAugury cross-references**,
but it is clearly part of a larger, coordinated rewrite of the Signatures
page that Eric Haas has been driving alongside the FHIR-I / Security &
Privacy WGs ahead of the R6 ballot. The companion tickets are
[FHIR-53835](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53835) (rewrite the
introduction / "Digital Signatures" page),
[FHIR-54080](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54080) (apply the same
"FHIR-content-first" rewrite to the **XML** signatures section),
[FHIR-54035](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54035) (add a parallel
**CMS** signatures section using the same pattern) and
[FHIR-53891](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53891) (clean up the
**Canonicalization** section). Three Hayden Bader follow-ons —
[FHIR-54066](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54066),
[FHIR-54067](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54067), and
[FHIR-54068](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54068) — pick at the
**current** prescriptive JSON-signature text (mismatch with
`signature.targetFormat`, `srCms` clarifications, JAdES 1.2.1 instead of
1.1.1) and would be substantially reshaped, or partially absorbed, by
accepting FHIR-54025. The earlier
[FHIR-36158](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-36158) (closed Duplicate)
already raised the detached-JWS overreach.

## Details

**Description:**

> Switch focus to the FHIR content rather than the signature content.
>
> - To Keep the FHIR Standard as a flexible base standard that avoids
>   potential conflicts with use case specific profiles. Does not introduce
>   the JWS standard requirements like for example *"The JWS protected
>   header SHALL contain either a `kid` or an `x5c` property"*.
> - re `.type`: JAdES `srCms` header recommendations include the
>   `http://uri.etsi.org/01903/v1.2.2` codes — they are used in the
>   `srCms` header too (ETSI TS 119 182-1 §5.2.3 and ETSI TS 119 172-1
>   Annex B "Commitment types").
> - There is no technical reason that I know of to enforce only detached
>   vs. "non-detached" (standard JWS). That should be an implementation
>   decision.
>
> **Proposed text** (source at
> [Healthedata1/fhir `source/signatures.html`](https://github.com/Healthedata1/fhir/blob/master/source/signatures.html)):
>
> ### JWS
>
> When the digital signature is an [RFC 7515: JSON Web Signature
> (JWS)](https://tools.ietf.org/html/rfc7515) signature or a profile built
> upon it, such as [JAdES (JSON Advanced Electronic Signatures)](https://www.etsi.org/deliver/etsi_ts/119100_119199/11918201/01.02.01_60/ts_11918201v010201p.pdf),
> the following guidelines and rules apply:
>
> - **`Signature.type`**: SHOULD be populated with the codes from
>   [Signature Type Codes](https://hl7.org/fhir/6.0.0-ballot4/valueset-signature-type.html).
>   The Purpose can be the action being attested to or the role associated
>   with the signature. It SHOULD agree with any "purpose" headers in the
>   JWS signature (e.g., the `srCms` header defined in the JAdES profiles).
> - **`Signature.when`**: SHOULD be populated with the signature timestamp.
>   It SHOULD agree with any timestamp header in the JWS signature
>   (e.g., the `iat` or `sigT` headers defined in the JAdES profiles).
> - **`Signature.who`**: SHOULD be populated with a `Reference.reference`
>   or `Reference.identifier` of who signed the content. It SHOULD match
>   the signer's identity found in the digital certificate (e.g., Subject
>   Name (SN) or one of the Subject Alternative Names (SAN) in an X.509
>   certificate).
> - **`Signature.onBehalfOf`**: When applicable, SHOULD be populated with a
>   `Reference.reference` or `Reference.identifier` of the party
>   represented by the signer.
> - **`Signature.targetFormat`**: SHOULD be populated, and the value SHOULD
>   contain a canonicalization URL from one of those defined above or
>   defined by other specifications.
> - **`Signature.sigFormat`**: SHOULD be populated with the value
>   `application/jose` for JWS and JWS profiles.
> - **`Signature.data`**: The base64-encoded JWS.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In `HL7/fhir:source/signatures.html`, replace the body of the existing
**JWS** `<h3>` subsection (the block introduced by `<a name="JWS">`) with
the prose attached to FHIR-54025, **preserving the `<a name="JWS">` anchor
and the `<h3>JWS</h3>` heading text** so existing inbound links (e.g.,
from `Bundle` and Provenance docs) keep working. The new bullets are
keyed on `Signature.*` elements and use SHOULD-level guidance:

- `Signature.type` SHOULD be populated from the
  [Signature Type Codes](https://hl7.org/fhir/6.0.0-ballot4/valueset-signature-type.html)
  value set; it SHOULD agree with any `srCms` (or equivalent purpose)
  header in the JWS.
- `Signature.when` SHOULD carry the signature timestamp and SHOULD agree
  with `iat` / `sigT`.
- `Signature.who` SHOULD reference the signer and SHOULD match the X.509
  Subject Name / SAN.
- `Signature.onBehalfOf` SHOULD reference the represented party when
  applicable.
- `Signature.targetFormat` SHOULD contain a canonicalization URL.
- `Signature.sigFormat` SHOULD be `application/jose`.
- `Signature.data` is the base64-encoded JWS.

Do **not** restate JOSE-level mandates such as "JWS protected header
SHALL contain `kid` or `x5c`" or "the JWS SHALL be detached." Coordinate
the PR with Grahame Grieve's `2025-07-gg-digital-signatures1` branch so
the rewrite is layered on top of the already-merged FHIR-51143 work
rather than reverting it.

In the same change set, sweep the now-redundant tickets:

- Withdraw / mark Done by FHIR-54025: **FHIR-54068** (JAdES 1.2.1
  reference becomes a non-issue once the version-specific text is
  removed), **FHIR-54067** (the strict `srCms` SHALL is replaced by a
  SHOULD-level "agree with") and **FHIR-54066** (the `targetFormat`
  wording is rewritten in concert with the datatype page).
- Re-route or apply in parallel: **FHIR-54080** (XML), **FHIR-54035**
  (CMS), **FHIR-53891** (canonicalization), **FHIR-53835**
  (introduction) — all part of the same Eric-Haas-driven rewrite.

#### Justification

The proposal aligns with two long-standing FHIR design principles. First,
**"don't repeat other standards"**: the FHIR core spec's job is to say
how a JWS goes into a `Signature` instance, not to re-state RFC 7515 or
ETSI TS 119 182-1; profiles and IGs are the right place to mandate
specific JOSE headers. Second, **"prefer SHOULD in core, profile to
SHALL"**: the current prose mixes SHALL-level constraints derived from
JAdES into the FHIR base, which directly forces use-case profiles to
either accept JAdES or write a divergent profile, exactly the conflict
the reporter calls out. The Security & Privacy WG already converged on
this framing in 2025-09 ("this is all why the Signature datatype has a
mime-type and base64 element. To support standards use" — Moehrke), and
FHIR-36158 raised the same "detached-only is wrong" concern years ago.
The change is scoped to a single HTML page on `HL7/fhir`, the playbook
recipe ("Update narrative / prose") is well understood, there is no
generated content to regenerate, and the `#JWS` anchor — the only
brittle inbound dependency the briefing flags — is preserved.

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the *spirit* of FHIR-54025 but **fold its rewrite into Grahame
Grieve's in-flight Signatures-page restructuring** rather than landing
it as an isolated bullet-replacement. Concretely:

1. Treat the JWS subsection rewrite as one of the deliverables of the
   `2025-07-gg-digital-signatures1` branch and coordinate with the
   parent rewrite tickets (FHIR-53835 Intro, FHIR-54080 XML,
   FHIR-54035 CMS, FHIR-53891 Canonicalization). Land all four
   subsections together so JWS / XML / CMS look symmetric.
2. Keep **one** JWS-specific SHALL (or strong SHOULD): "the JWS SHALL be
   serialized using JWS Compact Serialization (a single string, not the
   JSON-serialized form)." This preserves the part of the current text
   that genuinely matters for FHIR interop — predictable
   `Signature.data` payloads — while dropping the over-prescription
   about `kid`/`x5c` and detached form.
3. Promote `Signature.sigFormat = application/jose` to **SHALL** (rather
   than SHOULD); without it, downstream validators cannot tell which
   parser to use, and the FHIR-54066 datatype-page alignment work
   becomes much easier when there is exactly one allowed value.
4. Move the JAdES-specific guidance (`srCms`, `sigT`, `iat`, JAdES
   profile names) into a non-normative "Recommendations for JAdES
   profilers" callout, so JAdES-specific tickets like FHIR-54067 /
   FHIR-54068 have an obvious home but do not pollute the JWS-base
   bullets.

#### Justification

The Zulip discussion shows that the WG's eventual direction is broader
than just the JWS subsection — they want JWS, XML, and CMS all
re-cast in the same FHIR-content-first style, *and* there is real demand
to keep `application/jose` as the unambiguous signal. Landing FHIR-54025
in isolation risks (a) churn, because FHIR-54080 and FHIR-54035 are
about to rewrite adjacent prose anyway, and (b) losing the one piece of
JWS-specific guidance — Compact Serialization and `application/jose` as
the marker — that *is* worth being firm about. This disposition keeps
Eric's editorial intent, gives Hayden Bader's three follow-ons
(FHIR-54066/67/68) a clean place to attach, and respects the briefing's
warning that the rewrite is happening on a build branch (avoid merging
conflicting prose into `master` mid-flight).

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark FHIR-54025 as **Persuasive with Mod / Not Persuasive** and keep the
existing JWS subsection essentially as written (after the
already-applied FHIR-51143 changes), with at most a one-line softening
that the JOSE-header mandates "MAY be relaxed by implementation guides."
The rationale: the current SHALL-level requirements on
`kid`/`x5c`/detached form are defensible interop minimums for the FHIR
base spec, the page is already going through one rewrite cycle for R6
(FHIR-51143), and a second back-to-back rewrite invites instability
right before ballot freeze.

#### Justification

This disposition is defensible only if the WG concludes that the
JWS-standard mandates currently in the page are doing real interop work
— i.e., that FHIR servers are failing to validate JWS signatures today
*because* of profiles that omit `kid`/`x5c` or use embedded JWS — and
that softening them would make the situation worse. The argument
weakens significantly given that (a) FHIR-36158 raised the same concern
and was closed only as Duplicate, not on merit; (b) the Zulip thread
shows WG members repeatedly reaching for "use JAdES / XAdES / CAdES and
get out of the JOSE-header business"; and (c) no implementer has spoken
up on the thread defending the current SHALLs. The honest version of
"decline" is "we don't have time before ballot," which is procedural
rather than technical.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition: B (alternative approach).**

FHIR-54025 is editorially right — the FHIR core spec has no business
restating JWS / JAdES mandates — and it should be accepted, but landing
it as a stand-alone bullet swap on `master` is the wrong vehicle. The
Signatures page is mid-rewrite on Grahame Grieve's
`2025-07-gg-digital-signatures1` branch, three sister tickets
(FHIR-54080 XML, FHIR-54035 CMS, FHIR-53835 Intro) are queued to land in
the same prose neighbourhood, and three Hayden Bader tickets
(FHIR-54066/67/68) are picking at exactly the SHALLs FHIR-54025 wants to
delete. The cleanest path is to fold all of those into one coordinated
PR against the build branch, which (i) preserves the few JWS-specific
anchors that *are* worth keeping firm — Compact Serialization and
`Signature.sigFormat = application/jose` — and (ii) gives the
JAdES-specific community a non-normative callout to attach further
clarifications to without re-introducing core-spec mandates. Disposition
A captures the right intent but, by treating FHIR-54025 in isolation,
will create a near-immediate merge conflict with FHIR-54080 / FHIR-54035
and force the WG to re-litigate the same decisions twice. Disposition C
is hard to defend on the merits and is effectively a "ballot timing"
veto.
