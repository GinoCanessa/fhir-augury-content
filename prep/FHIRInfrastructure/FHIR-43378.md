## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Accept the editorial correction exactly as proposed: replace the
duplicated "for for" with "for" in the affected SMART on FHIR
narrative page. Because PR
[HL7/smart-app-launch#413](https://github.com/HL7/smart-app-launch/pull/413)
has already been merged to `master` of `HL7/smart-app-launch`, the
substantive change is in place. The remaining work for this disposition
is purely Jira hygiene: mark FHIR-43378 as `Resolved` with resolution
`Persuasive` (or the equivalent technical-correction resolution used by
FHIR Infrastructure), link the merged PR as the resolving change, and
move the status out of `Triaged`.

#### Justification

The change is a one-word editorial fix with no semantic impact on the
SMART on FHIR specification, no normative consequences, and no
backward-compatibility risk. The community contribution path was
followed correctly (the reporter authored the PR themselves, an HL7
maintainer reviewed and merged it). Leaving the Jira ticket open after
the fix has shipped serves no review purpose and clutters the work
group's queue. Accepting as requested aligns with normal handling of
`Technical Correction` tickets where a maintainer-merged PR already
implements the request.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the spirit of the request but treat it as a prompt for a
slightly broader editorial sweep: in the same publication cycle that
absorbs PR #413, run a quick scan of the SMART on FHIR narrative
pages for other duplicated-word artifacts ("the the", "and and",
"of of", etc.) and fold any findings into a single follow-up editorial
PR against `HL7/smart-app-launch`. FHIR-43378 itself is then closed
against PR #413, with a note that any additional duplicated-word fixes
will be tracked under a new technical-correction ticket.

#### Justification

Duplicated-word typos tend to cluster in long narrative documents, and
the cost of a one-pass scan is low compared to the per-ticket overhead
of triaging individual single-word corrections through Jira. Bundling
follow-on fixes into one PR keeps the spec's commit history tidy and
amortizes reviewer effort. The trade-off versus Disposition A is a
small amount of additional editorial work for the maintainer; the
upside is a slightly cleaner spec and fewer future single-word
correction tickets.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline FHIR-43378 as a Jira action item on the grounds that the
underlying issue has already been addressed outside the Jira workflow
by merged PR #413. Close the ticket with resolution `Not Persuasive`
or `Already Addressed` and a comment pointing at the merged PR.

#### Justification

This option is defensible only as a procedural cleanup: there is
nothing left to decide and no editorial action remaining for the work
group. However, "Decline" is a poor label for a request whose substance
was actually accepted and merged; choosing this disposition would
mis-record the outcome in Jira's resolution metrics and could mislead
later searches. It is included here for completeness but is not a
genuinely attractive option.

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

This is a one-word editorial typo fix in the SMART on FHIR narrative

[HL7/smart-app-launch#413](https://github.com/HL7/smart-app-launch/pull/413),
