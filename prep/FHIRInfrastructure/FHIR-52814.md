## Summary

Reporter Bas van den Heuvel asks the FHIR Infrastructure work group to
sharpen the canonicalization rules for **document Bundles** at
`http://hl7.org/fhir/canonicalization/{fmt}#document`. The current text
does not, in his view, clearly state which parts of the Bundle and its
embedded `Provenance.signature` are stripped before the canonical form
is produced and signed/verified. He proposes a single explicit rule
along the lines of: "sign everything in the Bundle except `Bundle.id`,
`Bundle.meta.lastUpdated`, `Bundle.meta.versionId`, `Bundle.signature`,
and the Provenance resource that carries the current signature — except
that `signature.data` in the current `Provenance.signature` shall be
removed before signing/verifying."

The ticket sits inside a cluster of signature-related issues filed by
the same reporter on the same day (FHIR-52810…FHIR-52820, FHIR-52745;
see Zulip discussion below). It is most tightly coupled to
[FHIR-52813](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-52813) ("Countersigning
not completely clear"), which has already been resolved Persuasive by
the Security work group and applied via HL7/fhir PR #4028; the comment
trail in FHIR-52814 itself explicitly cross-references FHIR-52813 and
suggests the canonical-rule details belong on the canonicalization page
with a back-reference from `signatures.html#Bundles`. Eric Haas asked
in a comment whether there is in fact a remaining issue, since the
Documents section of `signatures.html#Bundles` already covers what is
omitted (root `Bundle.id`/`Bundle.meta`) and how co-sign vs.
counter-sign behave; the reporter's follow-up clarifies that the
**canonicalization page** is what needs to be tightened, not the
signatures narrative.

## Details

**Description:**
Please update the cardinality rules on
`http://hl7.org/fhir/canonicalization/{fmt}#document` and clearly state
how the signature part is removed. Currently it is not very clear
about that. Combining the comment #1b, one proposal would be: "The
method sign everything in the bundle exclude `bundle.id`,
`bundle.meta.lastupdate`, `bundle.meta.versionid`, `bundle.signature`
and the provenance resource which carries this current signature,
except that `signature.data` in the current `provenance.signature`
shall be removed before signing/verifying."

**Comments:**

- **JIRAUSER26199 (2025-09-24):** Acknowledges that the information may
  be present once everything is read together, but as a *canonical
  rule* it is incomplete in its current location. Readers may
  misunderstand and only remove `bundle.id` and `bundle.meta`. Suggests
  the canonicalization page should at least mention that the
  Provenance resource(s) may need to be removed depending on the
  signing choice described in `signatures.html#Bundles`. Argues the
  detail that currently lives on `signatures.html#Bundles` and in
  FHIR-52813 is canonical-rule territory and would fit better on the
  canonicalization page (with cross-references from the signatures
  page).
- **ehaas / Eric Haas (2025-09-24):** "Not clear what the issue is
  here." Quotes the existing prose that the Document `Bundle.id` and
  `Bundle.meta` on the root Bundle are omitted prior to signing (so the
  document can be copied server-to-server), notes there is only one
  root `Bundle.id` / `Bundle.meta`, and points out that which
  signatures to remove is already explained in
  `signatures.html#Bundles` and depends on co-sign vs. counter-sign
  semantics.

## Related Jira Tickets

### [FHIR-52813: Countersigning not completely clear](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-52813)

Filed by the same reporter on the same day as FHIR-52814 and
explicitly cited in FHIR-52814's first comment. FHIR-52813 targets
points 4–6 of the Bundle signature rules in `signatures.html#Bundles`
and the ambiguity around counter-signing — specifically whether
counter-signing is allowed to add an additional target to the referred
Provenance. It was resolved **Persuasive** by the Security work group
and applied via HL7/fhir PR #4028, with the agreed wording rephrasing
rule #4 to refer to entries whose Provenance has `Provenance.target`
including a reference to `#/`, and noting that counter-signing
signatures will include additional targets but always include `#/`
among them. FHIR-52814 effectively asks for the *canonicalization*
counterpart of that fix, so any disposition on FHIR-52814 should align
with the now-applied wording on FHIR-52813.

## Related Zulip Discussions

### [fhir/infrastructure-wg > Signature comments](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/fhir%2Finfrastructure-wg/topic/Signature%20comments/near/538442500)

Bas van den Heuvel opened the thread on 2025-09-09 to announce the
batch of signature-related Change Requests filed after a security
expert reviewed the revised signature page; FHIR-52814 is one of
twelve tickets in the list. He asked whether FHIR-I, Security, or both
should own the disposition, and when. **Grahame Grieve** noted that
the last ticket in the list was filed in error and singled out
FHIR-52810 as the most interesting (questioning whether
`#thisDocument` is even legal as a fragment). **John Moehrke**
confirmed that **FHIR-I has taken the lead on Signature updates in
R6** and thanked the reporter for the comments. **Essien Ge** added a
long, substantive position post arguing that signatures in FHIR are
being treated as a single mechanism while in practice they serve
distinct purposes — authentication, **data integrity**, and
**non-repudiation** — with very different requirements. He argues
non-repudiation signatures (where bundle.id / lastUpdated / versionId
must *not* be inside the signed payload because the document moves
between servers) and integrity signatures (where signing
server-managed metadata is fine, since the binding is to the server
and resigning on transit is acceptable) need different canonical
rules; he also makes a case for allowing XAdES / JAdES profiles, with
B-T as a sensible middle ground over B-LT. No formal consensus was
reached in the thread, but two points are clear: (1) FHIR-I owns the
R6 signature work, and (2) there is community support for being more
explicit and prescriptive about what is and is not in the canonical
form for documents, which directly supports FHIR-52814.

Participants: Bas van den Heuvel, Grahame Grieve, John Moehrke,
Essien Ge.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the **document** section of the canonicalization page
(`http://hl7.org/fhir/canonicalization/{fmt}#document`) so that it
states the canonical-form rules for a Document Bundle explicitly and
exhaustively, rather than relying on the reader to combine prose from
`signatures.html#Bundles`. Concretely, replace the current cardinality
prose with a normative rule of the shape:

> Before producing the canonical form of a Document Bundle for the
> purposes of signing or verifying a `Provenance.signature`, the
> following elements SHALL be removed:
> - `Bundle.id`
> - `Bundle.meta.lastUpdated`
> - `Bundle.meta.versionId`
> - `Bundle.signature`
> - any `Bundle.entry.resource` that is a `Provenance` whose
>   `Provenance.target` includes a reference to `#/` (i.e. the
>   embedded signing-Provenance(s) for this Bundle, per the rule
>   applied for FHIR-52813), **except** that for the *current*
>   signing Provenance, `Provenance.signature.data` SHALL be cleared
>   (set to absent) but the rest of the Provenance SHALL be retained
>   and signed.

Cross-link this rule from `signatures.html#Bundles` (and from the
co-sign / counter-sign sub-sections) so that the signatures page is
the *narrative* and the canonicalization page is the *normative
canonical rule*. Mirror the `#/`-target convention that has already
been adopted for FHIR-52813 so the two pages cannot drift.

#### Justification

This is exactly what the reporter and the first commenter (JIRAUSER26199)
ask for, and it matches the way the spec already partitions concerns —
canonicalization rules belong on the canonicalization page; signature
narrative belongs on `signatures.html`. It is also consistent with the
already-applied wording on FHIR-52813 (which uses `#/` as the
target-set criterion for "this is one of *my* signing Provenances")
and with John Moehrke's confirmation in Zulip that FHIR-I owns the R6
signature work, so the two pages can be revised together. Eric Haas's
"not clear what the issue is" objection is addressed by recognising
that the information *is* present overall but is not present *as a
canonical rule on the canonicalization page* — which is the only
place an implementer of canonicalization is required to look.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than restating the rule on the canonicalization page, **make
the canonicalization page authoritative by reference**: keep the
cardinality table as-is, but add a single normative sentence that
points at a new sub-section of `signatures.html#Bundles` titled
"Canonical form for document signatures", and consolidate *all*
strip-this/keep-this rules (including the FHIR-52813 wording about
`#/`-targeted Provenance and the `signature.data`-clearing rule) into
that one sub-section. Update `canonicalization/{fmt}#document` to
read approximately:

> For Document Bundles, the canonical form used for
> `Provenance.signature` SHALL be produced according to the rules in
> [Bundle Signatures](https://build.fhir.org/signatures.html#Bundles).
> No other element-level canonicalization rules apply.

Then on `signatures.html#Bundles`, add the explicit list of removed
elements (the same list as Disposition A) inside a single normative
block, distinguishing co-sign from counter-sign cases.

#### Justification

This avoids two pages stating overlapping (and potentially drifting)
normative rules. The Zulip discussion (Essien Ge) and the reporter
both note that signature semantics depend on use case (integrity vs.
non-repudiation, co-sign vs. counter-sign); concentrating the rule on
the signatures page keeps those distinctions next to the rule they
constrain. It also keeps the canonicalization page neutral for
non-document contexts where Provenance/Bundle-signature stripping is
not relevant. Trade-off vs. A: the *canonicalization* page becomes a
pointer rather than a self-contained rule, which is exactly the
structure the reporter is pushing back against — but it is still a
strict improvement on the current text because the signatures page
becomes unambiguously normative.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket **Not Persuasive** on the basis that the canonical
form for a signed Document Bundle is already adequately specified by
the combination of `canonicalization/{fmt}#document` (cardinality
rules) and `signatures.html#Bundles` (which root elements are omitted,
how co-sign vs. counter-sign behave, and — after FHIR-52813 was
applied — which embedded Provenance resources are excluded via the
`#/`-target rule). Direct implementers to read both pages together,
and add at most a one-sentence editorial pointer.

#### Justification

This mirrors Eric Haas's reading of the ticket: the information is
present, the Document Bundle root `Bundle.id` / `Bundle.meta` carve-out
is already stated, and the Provenance-stripping rule was clarified by
FHIR-52813's applied wording (PR #4028). Doing nothing avoids the
risk of two pages stating the same rule with subtly different wording
— a real maintenance hazard given that FHIR-52813 has already moved
the target-set definition to `#/` semantics. The cost is that the
reporter's specific complaint — that the canonicalization page is not
self-sufficient — is left unaddressed.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A best fits the actual gap: the reporter and the first
commenter both make the same point, which is that the
*canonicalization page* is the only document an implementer of
canonical form is normatively required to consult, and right now it
does not state the document-Bundle rule completely. Disposition C
under-weights that — Eric Haas is correct that the rule can be
reconstructed by reading two pages together, but canonicalization
rules that require reading two pages together are exactly the class of
rule that produces interop bugs. Disposition B is structurally clean
but leaves the canonicalization page in the same "look elsewhere"
state the reporter is objecting to.

Disposition A also has a near-zero risk of drift from FHIR-52813,
which has already been **Applied** via HL7/fhir PR #4028 and which
fixed the same family of wording on the *signatures* page; reusing
the `#/`-target criterion and the `signature.data`-clearing nuance
from that resolution keeps the two pages in lock-step. It is also
consistent with John Moehrke's Zulip confirmation that FHIR-I owns
R6 signature work, so this disposition can be carried out by FHIR-I
without a hand-off to Security. The workgroup may wish to consider
whether to also add a non-normative note about co-sign vs.
counter-sign canonical-form differences, picking up Essien Ge's
distinction between integrity and non-repudiation signing — but that
can ride along as an editorial improvement rather than block this
ticket.
