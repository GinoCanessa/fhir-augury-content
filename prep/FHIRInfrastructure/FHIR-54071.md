## Summary

The reporter notes an internal inconsistency in the `Range` datatype on FHIR
Core. The textual description for `Range` states that "the low and the high
values are inclusive and are assumed to have arbitrarily high precision;
e.g., the range 1.5 to 2.5 includes 1.50, and 2.50 but not 1.49 or 2.51."
However, the invariant `rng-2` is expressed in terms of FHIRPath's
`lowBoundary()` / `highBoundary()` functions, which derive their bounds from
the *displayed* precision of the supplied value rather than from
arbitrarily-high precision. As a consequence, a `Range` such as
`{ low: 2.4, high: 2 }` passes `rng-2` (because `high.highBoundary()` evaluates
to `2.5`, which is `>= 2.4`), even though under the "arbitrarily high
precision" reading the range would be empty / inverted. The reporter is
asking the workgroup to reconcile the prose definition with the constraint —
either by changing the constraint, by changing the description, or by
clarifying which interpretation governs.

No formal Jira/Zulip/GitHub cross-references have been recorded against this
ticket in Augury. Keyword-similar tickets surface a small cluster of prior
boundary/precision work — most notably the now-Applied FHIR-46280 and
FHIR-46281 (which clarified the meaning of `lowBoundary`/`highBoundary` itself)
and the still-open FHIR-45021 (which raises essentially the same "what does
inclusive mean here" concern for age ranges) — see *Related Jira Tickets*
below for context.

## Details

**Description:**

> Constraint rng-2 uses `lowBoundary()` and `highBoundary()`. This means, for
> example, the following Range passes this constraint:
>
> ```
> {
>   "low":  { "value": 2.4 },
>   "high": { "value": 2   }
> }
> ```
>
> (because `high.highBoundary()` is `2.5`)
>
> This could arguably be correct given how numeric precision works. However,
> the description for Range also says:
>
> > The low and the high values are inclusive and are assumed to have
> > arbitrarily high precision; e.g., the range 1.5 to 2.5 includes 1.50,
> > and 2.50 but not 1.49 or 2.51.
>
> These seem at odds. Constraint `rng-2` assumes a specific precision based
> off the value, but the description implies that low and high values should
> be taken as having infinite precision.

## Proposed Dispositions

The dispositions below are constrained by two facts already established in
related work: (1) FHIRPath's `lowBoundary()` / `highBoundary()` semantics
are settled as precision-aware (FHIR-46280 / FHIR-46281 Applied), so
re-defining them is off the table, and (2) `Range` is, by prior WG
decision (FHIR-22642), inclusive-only. The remaining lever is therefore
the `Range` element definition / `rng-2` invariant pair in FHIR Core.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat the inconsistency as a real defect in `Range` and align the prose to
match the constraint, since the constraint is what implementations actually
enforce. Specifically, in the FHIR Core R6 build, edit the `Range` datatype
description (the "low and high are inclusive and are assumed to have
arbitrarily high precision…" paragraph) to instead say that `low` and
`high` are inclusive and are interpreted at the precision implied by their
respective `value.value` (consistent with FHIRPath
`lowBoundary()`/`highBoundary()`). Add a worked example showing that
`{ low: { value: 1.5 }, high: { value: 2.5 } }` admits any value in
`[1.45, 2.55)` per `rng-2`, and explicitly note that comparing two
differently-precise endpoints is governed by the boundary functions, not
by infinite precision. Leave `rng-2` itself unchanged.

#### Justification

This is the lower-risk fix. `rng-2` has been published and is implemented;
relaxing it to enforce the "arbitrarily high precision" reading would be a
breaking change for validators that already accept Ranges that satisfy the
boundary form. It also keeps `Range` consistent with the
already-Applied FHIR-46280 / FHIR-46281 clarifications and avoids creating
a third interpretation of "the bounds of a decimal." The reporter's own
example (`low=2.4`, `high=2`) is, under this disposition, correctly
flagged as a *modelling* problem (the author should have written
`high=2.0` or `high=2.5`), not a constraint defect.

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the prose intent ("arbitrarily high precision") but tighten `rng-2`
so the constraint matches the prose. Replace
`low.value.empty() or high.value.empty() or (low <= high)`-style boundary
checks with a comparison that requires `high.value >= low.value` evaluated
at the *higher* of the two stated precisions, and add a second, advisory
constraint (`rng-3`) noting that endpoints SHOULD be expressed at the same
precision. In the description, clarify that "arbitrarily high precision"
means the *modelled* value (the digits the author wrote) are taken as
exact, not that trailing-digit padding is permitted.

#### Justification

This preserves the published, widely-cited "1.5–2.5 excludes 1.49 and 2.51"
example, which is pedagogically valuable and is the intuition many
implementers carry. The cost is real: it is a normative tightening of
`rng-2`, which will fail validation on instances that previously passed,
and it deepens the divergence from the precision-aware semantics that ITS
just *Applied* in FHIR-46280. It also leaves an awkward asymmetry between
how `Range` interprets decimal precision and how every other FHIRPath
boundary use does. Worth tabling primarily as a sanity check — if the WG
believes the prose example reflects the *real* intent of `Range`, this is
the only way to honor it.

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark **Not Persuasive — Already Addressed**: the boundary semantics are
defined by FHIRPath (per FHIR-46280 / FHIR-46281), `rng-2` correctly
applies them, and the surrounding prose, while imperfectly worded, is
illustrative rather than normative. No change is required.

#### Justification

Defensible only if the WG reads the description's example as informative.
However, the wording "*are assumed to have arbitrarily high precision*"
is a positive statement about how endpoints are to be interpreted, not a
hedge — implementers and validator authors *will* read it as normative,
and the contradiction with `rng-2` is already showing up in real
authoring (the reporter's example). Declining leaves the ambiguity in
place and effectively forces every implementer to rediscover this issue.
Listed for completeness; the bar to choose this disposition is high.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The boundary semantics underlying `rng-2` were settled, with WG consensus,
in FHIR-46280 and FHIR-46281 only recently, and they were intentionally
made precision-aware. Disposition A respects that decision, requires no
breaking change to a published constraint, and removes the contradiction
by fixing the *only* part of `Range` that is genuinely ambiguous — the
prose. Disposition B re-litigates ground that was just walked, at the
cost of validator breakage; Disposition C leaves a real defect in place.
The WG should also coordinate the resulting Range-description edit with
FHIR-45021 (still *Waiting for Input* in FHIR-I on the same paragraph),
and consider attaching the worked example proposed under A so that
implementers stop having to derive the precision behavior from first
principles.
