## Summary

The reporter (Bas van den Heuvel), advised by an internal security expert,
points out that the Signature narrative in FHIR Core uses the phrase
"electronic signature" in a way that is technically inaccurate and
confusing. The current text says a signature can be *either* an
"electronic signature (e.g. an image of an ink signature)" *or* a
"digital signature (cryptographic proof…)", treating the two as
disjoint categories. In information-security practice the two terms are
not parallel: a digital signature is, in fact, one *type* of electronic
signature (and the AdES family — XAdES/JAdES that FHIR already
recommends — literally stands for *Advanced Electronic Signature*). The
reporter therefore requests that the words "an electronic signature
(e.g." be struck so the sentence reads: "The signature can either be an
image of an ink signature, or a digital signature (cryptographic
proof…)". This is a narrative-only clarification on the Signature
datatype page; no element, cardinality, value-set, or invariant change
is requested. The single cross-reference is the Zulip thread
*fhir/infrastructure-wg > Signature comments*, where this ticket was
filed alongside FHIR-52810…FHIR-52820 and FHIR-52745 as a batch of
security-expert review comments on the revised Signature page; FHIR-I
(per John Moehrke) is the lead WG for R6 Signature updates.

## Details

**Description:**

> The signature can either be an electronic signature (e.g. an image of
> an ink signature), or a digital signature (cryptographic proof that
> the content has not changed), and in the case of a digital signature,
> either a JWS signature or an XML Digital Signature.

should be

> The signature can either be ~~an electronic signature (e.g.~~ an
> image of an ink signature), or a digital signature (cryptographic
> proof that the content has not changed), and in the case of a digital
> signature, either a JWS signature or an XML Digital Signature.

as the term electronic signature is confusing — it can also refer to
signing with public-key encryption.

**Comments:**

- **john_moehrke (2025-09-09):** Not against the change, but notes that
  "electronic signature" *is* a recognised term for a computer-controlled
  signature that is not a digital signature, citing
  <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electronic_signature>.
- **JIRAUSER26199 / Essien Ge (2025-09-10):** Argues the opposite reading
  — "electronic signature" is a *legal* concept (signature in digital
  form with the same legal standing as a handwritten one) that may or
  may not be implemented via a digital signature, quoting the same
  Wikipedia article: *"Electronic signatures are a legal concept
  distinct from digital signatures, a cryptographic mechanism often
  used to implement electronic signatures."* Highlights that FHIR
  already recommends XAdES/JAdES, where "AdES" expands to *Advanced
  Electronic Signature* (EU 910/2014), so simultaneously using
  "electronic signature" to mean "image of an ink signature" while
  recommending "Advanced Electronic Signatures" as the digital format
  is contradictory.
- **ehaas (2025-09-24):** "A digital signature is a type of electronic
  signature. So I support the change."

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Persuasive — accept the editorial fix exactly as proposed. In the
Signature datatype narrative on the FHIR Core spec (the paragraph that
currently reads *"The signature can either be an electronic signature
(e.g. an image of an ink signature), or a digital signature
(cryptographic proof that the content has not changed)…"*), strike the
words "an electronic signature (e.g." and the closing parenthesis so
the sentence becomes: *"The signature can either be an image of an ink
signature, or a digital signature (cryptographic proof that the content
has not changed), and in the case of a digital signature, either a JWS
signature or an XML Digital Signature."* No change to element
definitions, cardinalities, bindings, or invariants on the Signature
datatype; pure narrative correction on the Signature page (and any
sibling text on the signatures page that repeats the same dichotomy).

#### Justification

The current wording presents "electronic signature" and "digital
signature" as mutually exclusive categories, which is the opposite of
the standards meaning. Per ETSI/EU 910/2014 (the basis for the
XAdES/JAdES formats FHIR already recommends), a digital signature is
*one mechanism for implementing* an electronic signature; the very
"E" in *AdES* stands for *Electronic*. Reviewers Essien Ge and ehaas
both confirmed this on the ticket; John Moehrke explicitly stated he
is "not against this change". The minimal edit removes the misleading
contrast without losing the example "an image of an ink signature",
preserving reader intent. Risk is essentially zero (no implementer
behaviour depends on this sentence) and it directly answers the
security-expert review feedback.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Replace the offending sentence with a fuller, terminology-correct
explanation rather than a strike-through. Suggested replacement on the
Signature datatype page (and the signatures.html page where the same
dichotomy appears):

> A `Signature` may carry either a *non-cryptographic* representation
> of a signature (for example an image of an ink signature) or a
> *cryptographic* (digital) signature that proves the signed content
> has not been altered. Cryptographic signatures in FHIR are encoded
> as a JWS (JSON Web Signature) or as an XML Digital Signature, and
> may use the Advanced Electronic Signature (XAdES / JAdES) profiles
> when long-term validity or regulatory (e.g. EU 910/2014) compliance
> is required.

Optionally add a one-sentence glossary note: *"In this specification,
the term 'electronic signature' is avoided in favour of
'non-cryptographic signature' or 'digital signature' to prevent
confusion with the legal/regulatory meaning of 'electronic signature'
(of which a digital signature is one form)."*

#### Justification

Disposition A removes the confusing wording but leaves the reader with
no positive guidance on the ink-image-vs-cryptographic axis, and does
not capitalise on the opportunity to align FHIR's prose with the AdES
family it already recommends. A short rewrite (a) is still
narrative-only and low-risk, (b) eliminates the need for a future
ticket to clarify why XAdES/JAdES are called "Advanced *Electronic*
Signatures" if FHIR has just stopped using "electronic signature"
mid-sentence, and (c) gives WG members a single coherent paragraph to
agree on rather than a deletion that will inevitably be expanded later.
Trade-off vs. A: more text to bikeshed, slightly larger diff, but
addresses the underlying complaint (terminology confusion) more
durably. This approach is also consistent with the broader intent of
the FHIR-52810…FHIR-52820 batch, which is to tighten the Signature
narrative as a whole.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (Not Persuasive)

#### Proposal

Reject the change and keep the existing wording. Rationale category:
*existing wording is acceptable and the term carries an established
informal meaning*. Optionally add a parenthetical clarification (e.g.
"(non-cryptographic electronic signature)") rather than removing the
phrase, but do not adopt the requested deletion.

#### Justification

As John Moehrke noted, "electronic signature" is a recognised informal
term for a computer-controlled signature that is not a digital
signature; many readers — particularly those outside the EU 910/2014
context — will read the current sentence correctly. Editorial churn
on a stable narrative paragraph carries a small but non-zero risk of
breaking external references (anchors, cross-spec citations, training
materials). If the WG judges that the existing dichotomy is
"good-enough plain English", declining preserves stability and avoids
opening a wider terminology debate that the ticket does not require
the WG to resolve.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The complaint is real and well-grounded in standards usage (ETSI /
EU 910/2014; AdES literally expands to *Advanced Electronic
Signature*), and three of the four commenters on-ticket support
changing the text. Disposition C is hard to defend: even John Moehrke,
who supplied the strongest argument for the status quo, said he was
"not against this change", and the contradiction with FHIR's own
recommendation of XAdES/JAdES will not improve with age. Disposition A
fixes the immediate sentence cheaply, but leaves the surrounding prose
asymmetric (an example for the non-cryptographic case, none for the
cryptographic case) and does not help the next reader who notices that
"AdES" and "electronic signature" were both stripped of context.
Disposition B is only marginally more work — still a narrative-only
edit on a single page, no element or invariant impact, no
implementation burden — and it produces a paragraph that aligns the
FHIR Core text with the regulatory framework FHIR already references.
For an FHIR-I R6 Signature refresh that is taking on a batch of
security-expert comments at once, the small extra editorial investment
in B is the higher-value outcome.
