## Summary

This ticket asks the SMART App Launch specification to explicitly **permit
FHIRcast (and potentially other downstream IGs) to define top-level
launch-context parameters that travel alongside the access token in the
SMART token response** — specifically, the existing FHIRcast parameters
`hub.url` and `hub.topic`. Today, the SMART extensibility rules
(`scopes-and-launch-context.html#extensibility`) require non-SMART
launch-context fields to be carried inside an extension key (e.g.
prefixed with `__`) or, more recently, in the `fhirContext` object,
which conflicts with FHIRcast STU2 and with widely-deployed FHIRcast
implementations that have used `hub.url` / `hub.topic` at the root of
the token response since before `fhirContext` existed.

The ticket is the SMART-side companion to
[FHIR-37111](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-37111) ("FHIRcast fields
in SMART on FHIR"), which Bas van den Heuvel raised against FHIRcast.
That FHIRcast ticket was resolved as **Deferred / Considered for Future
Use** on the explicit basis that "we will log a Jira against the SMART
spec to carve out support for the top-level context paramters used by
FHIRcast and in production by implementers." FHIR-44863 is that promised
Jira. Isaac Vetter, in the related Zulip thread, also noted he expects
"the same outcomes as"
[FHIR-43419](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43419) — a related
SMART/FHIRcast ticket about token-response payload examples that closed
as **Not Persuasive with Modification**, signalling some prior reluctance
on the SMART side to formally bless additional top-level keys.

## Related Jira Tickets

### [FHIR-37111: FHIRcast fields in SMART on FHIR](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-37111)

Filed by Bas van den Heuvel against FHIRCast (Infrastructure &
Messaging) in May 2022. It proposed bringing FHIRcast into compliance
with SMART's extensibility rules — either by namespacing FHIRcast's
launch parameters under a single `fhircast` key in the token response,
or by using SMART extension parameters (`__`-prefixed). It was resolved
in March 2024 as **Deferred / Considered for Future Use** on the basis
that changing FHIRcast would break every deployed FHIRcast integration,
and that the right place to fix the discrepancy is on the SMART side by
"carv[ing] out support for the top-level context paramters used by
FHIRcast and in production by implementers." FHIR-44863 is the SMART-side
ticket that resolution explicitly promised, so the two should be
disposed of in a coordinated way.

Reporter: Bas van den Heuvel. Commenters / participants: Isaac Vetter,
Bas van den Heuvel. Assignee: Unassigned.

### [FHIR-43419: imagingstudy in fhircontext for EHR launch is a bad example, because imagingstudy is typically communicated at the top level in prod, currently](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43419)

Filed by Isaac Vetter against SMART on FHIR (FHIR Infrastructure) in
December 2023. It argued that the SMART STU example showing
`imagingstudy` inside `fhirContext` does not match production behaviour,
where additional keys are commonly placed alongside the access token at
the root of the token response (the same pattern FHIRcast uses).
Resolution: **Not Persuasive with Modification**. Josh Mandel pushed
back, suggesting that if there is real evidence of multiple production
EHRs emitting `imagingstudy` as a top-level parameter, it could be
"grandfathered" alongside `patient`/`encounter` in SMART — which is
precisely the framing FHIR-44863 is asking the SMART spec to formalise
for `hub.url`/`hub.topic`. The disposition history here is useful
prior art for how the SMART editors have historically reacted to the
"top-level params" pattern.

Reporter: Isaac Vetter. Commenters / participants: Isaac Vetter, Josh
Mandel. Assignee: Unassigned.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the SMART App Launch IG (the
`scopes-and-launch-context` page, "Launch context arrives with your
`access_token`" / "Extensibility" sections) to **explicitly
grandfather/reserve a set of top-level launch-context parameters defined
by other HL7 IGs**, starting with FHIRcast's `hub.url` and `hub.topic`.
Concretely:

- Add a normative statement that, in addition to SMART-defined
  parameters (`patient`, `encounter`, `fhirContext`, `need_patient_banner`,
  `smart_style_url`, `tenant`, `intent`, etc.), the token response MAY
  contain additional top-level launch-context parameters that are
  defined by another HL7-published IG, provided the IG names the
  parameters and prefixes them with the IG's reserved namespace token
  (e.g. `hub.` for FHIRcast).
- Add an entry in a new "Reserved IG-defined launch parameters" subsection
  that lists `hub.url` and `hub.topic` as defined by the FHIRcast IG.
- Relax the current "must use `__`-prefixed extension keys" rule so that
  it does not apply to keys that fall under such a registered IG
  namespace.
- Cross-reference FHIR-37111 / FHIR-44863 in a non-normative note so
  future readers understand why the carve-out exists.

#### Justification

This is the outcome that FHIR-37111 explicitly anticipated when it was
deferred: the FHIRcast WG closed that ticket on the assumption that
SMART would carve out room for the existing top-level parameters rather
than force a breaking change to every deployed FHIRcast hub and
subscriber. Accepting the request:

- Realigns the two specifications (SMART and FHIRcast STU2/STU3) which
  are otherwise contradictory.
- Avoids a breaking change to widely-deployed FHIRcast integrations,
  which Isaac Vetter and Bas van den Heuvel both flagged as
  unacceptable (FHIR-37111 comments, 2023-12-27 and 2024-02-23).
- Preserves the SMART extensibility model for the general case
  (`__`-prefix and `fhirContext`) while creating a small, explicitly
  enumerated escape hatch for HL7-published IGs.
- Establishes a reusable pattern for other IGs that have legitimate
  top-level launch-context needs, instead of a one-off for FHIRcast.

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than blessing arbitrary top-level keys, **define a single
SMART-level extension mechanism for IG-namespaced launch parameters**:

- Reserve a top-level `ig_context` (or similarly named) object in the
  SMART token response, structured as a map keyed by IG identifier
  (e.g. `"fhircast": { "hub.url": "...", "hub.topic": [ ... ] }`),
  mirroring the structure proposed in FHIR-37111's description.
- For backwards compatibility with deployed FHIRcast integrations,
  publish a normative "Compatibility" note saying servers MAY ALSO emit
  the same key/value pairs at the top level of the token response, and
  clients MUST tolerate either location for IG-defined parameters; the
  nested form is preferred for new implementations.
- Update FHIRcast (in a coordinated FHIRcast ticket) to document both
  the legacy top-level form and the new `ig_context.fhircast.*` form,
  with the nested form recommended for STU3+.

#### Justification

This keeps SMART's "the token response root is owned by SMART"
invariant intact for new fields, which preserves SMART's ability to add
its own top-level parameters in the future without colliding with
IG-defined ones — exactly the concern Josh Mandel raised on FHIR-43419.
It still solves FHIRcast's compliance problem, because FHIRcast's
parameters get a defined home in the SMART payload. The dual-emission
compatibility window means no deployed FHIRcast integration breaks,
which was the showstopper for FHIR-37111. Trade-off vs. Disposition A:
slightly higher implementation cost (servers emit two copies during the
compatibility window; clients learn a new lookup path), but a cleaner
long-term namespace story and a more reusable mechanism for future IGs
than a hand-curated list of grandfathered keys.

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Do not change the SMART App Launch spec. Maintain the existing
extensibility rules: IG-defined launch-context parameters must either
be carried inside `fhirContext`, or be emitted as `__`-prefixed
extension keys, as documented at
`scopes-and-launch-context.html#extensibility`. Direct FHIRcast (via a
new FHIRcast Jira) to migrate `hub.url` / `hub.topic` into one of those
two existing mechanisms in a future major version, with a deprecation
window.

#### Justification

A single, uniform extensibility rule keeps SMART's token-response shape
predictable for every implementer, server, and library author, and
prevents the spec from accumulating per-IG carve-outs. SMART already
provides two extensibility points (`fhirContext` for FHIR-resource
context, `__`-prefixed keys for arbitrary extensions), so the
underlying need is technically already addressed. The SMART editors
have previously taken a similar line on closely related requests
(FHIR-43419, "Not Persuasive with Modification", with Josh Mandel
explicitly conditioning any "grandfathering" on documented evidence of
broad multi-vendor production use). If FHIRcast wants a top-level
namespace, it should make that case with concrete production evidence,
not via a generic permission to all IGs to occupy the SMART root
namespace.

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition C is hard to defend in practice: FHIRcast STU2 is
published, deployed, and depends on `hub.url` / `hub.topic` being at
the top level of the token response. The FHIRcast WG has already
deferred FHIR-37111 specifically on the understanding that SMART would
not force that breaking change, so a flat decline here would leave both
specs permanently and knowingly contradictory, with FHIRcast
implementers caught in the middle.

Disposition A is the path of least resistance and most directly
matches what the ticket asks for, but it bakes a per-IG list of
"reserved keys" into SMART forever and gives every future IG an
incentive to lobby for its own top-level keys — exactly the
namespace-pollution risk Josh Mandel flagged on FHIR-43419.

Disposition B threads that needle: it solves FHIRcast's real
compliance problem, honours the FHIR-37111 commitment to FHIRcast
implementers (no breaking change in the compatibility window), and
gives SMART a single, principled extensibility point for IG-defined
launch context — closely matching Bas van den Heuvel's original
proposal in FHIR-37111 ("negotiate the key 'fhircast' for FHIRcast
specific fields") while generalising it for other IGs. It is the
disposition most likely to be accepted by both work groups
(FHIR Infrastructure / SMART editors and Infrastructure & Messaging /
FHIRcast) and the one that leaves the SMART token-response model in
the best long-term shape.
