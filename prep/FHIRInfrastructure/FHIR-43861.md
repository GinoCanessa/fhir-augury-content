## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Accept PR
[HL7/smart-app-launch#415](https://github.com/HL7/smart-app-launch/pull/415)
as merged. No further spec action is required: the editorial
corrections in `input/pages/brands.md` and
`input/pages/scopes-and-launch-context.md` are already on `master` and
will appear in the next published build of the SMART App Launch IG.
Mark FHIR-43861 as **Persuasive** with resolution **Already implemented
in PR #415 (merged 2024-02-13)** and close.

#### Justification

The PR is purely editorial: trailing-period consistency, a single typo
fix ("useres" → "users"), a missing space, three bullet-description
casing fixes, and two clarifying word additions ("patient portal").
None of these touch normative text, schema, examples that implementers
key off, or extension/profile definitions. The diff is symmetric (+16 /
−16, two narrative files only) and the PR was merged by an Infrastructure
WG editor (`jmandel`) without controversy. Per the FHIR Infrastructure
WG's standing practice for technical corrections that do not change
behavior, the appropriate disposition is to ratify the change that
shipped.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the bulk of the editorial fixes as in Disposition A, but **revisit
the two semantic clarifications in `brands.md`** before finalizing —
specifically the additions of the qualifier "patient" in:

- "...a hospital may offer more than one **patient** portal for legacy
  purposes"
- "...a tertiary cancer center may offer one **patient** portal for
  adults and another for pediatric patients"

Either:

1. confirm with the SMART App Launch editors (Mandel / Bender) that
   "patient portal" is the intended scope of the Patient Access Brands
   guidance and keep the wording, or
2. revert those two additions to plain "portal" so the Brands narrative
   continues to cover provider-facing and member-facing portals as
   well.

If option (2) is chosen, raise a small follow-up PR against
`input/pages/brands.md` and resolve FHIR-43861 as **Persuasive with
Modification**.

#### Justification

The Patient Access Brands work was originally framed for patient-facing
portals, but the abstract Brand → Portal → Endpoint model has been
discussed as a more general directory mechanism. Tightening "portal" to
"patient portal" in two examples is a small wording change in
isolation, but it nudges the reader toward a narrower interpretation of
what Brands publish. Confirming intent with the editors costs little
and avoids retroactively scoping a recently-balloted concept through an
"editorial" change.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline FHIR-43861 as **Not Persuasive**. Revert PR #415 from
`master` and ask the reporter to break the change set into two PRs: one
for purely typographical fixes (periods, "useres", missing space, bullet
casing) and one for the "portal" → "patient portal" wording changes,
so the latter can receive a substantive review separately.

#### Justification

This disposition is defensible only if the workgroup is concerned that
bundling the two semantic wording changes in `brands.md` with otherwise
trivial typography slipped a non-editorial change past review. Beyond
that narrow concern there is no substantive reason to decline; the
remaining changes are unambiguous improvements.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is a textbook editorial Technical Correction: small,
non-normative, narrative-only, already merged by an Infrastructure WG
editor, with no Jira links, no Zulip dissent, and no implementer-visible
behavioral impact. Disposition A (accept as merged, close as Persuasive
/ Already Implemented) is the lowest-friction outcome and matches what
the WG has already effectively done. Disposition B is worth keeping in
the back pocket only if a Brands editor objects on the call to
"portal" → "patient portal"; if so, the fix is a one-line follow-up and
does not need to block closing this ticket. Disposition C is hard to
justify for a +16 / −16 editorial diff and would create more
process overhead than the change is worth.
