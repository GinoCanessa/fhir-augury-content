## Summary

The reporter argues that the FHIR Mapping Language (FML) has seen very
limited adoption and is only partly supported by mainstream FHIR libraries,
and on that basis recommends spinning the `StructureMap` resource and the
Mapping Language out of the FHIR core specification and into a separate
"Additional Resource IG" so they can evolve on their own track. The ticket
is a single high-level proposal — it does not enumerate which capabilities
or text would move, what backward-compatibility commitment a separated IG
would carry, or how the existing core references to `StructureMap` (e.g.,
from `ConceptMap`, `$transform`, version-mapping work) would be rewired.

The Zulip thread "Move FML to a new spec?" in the *FHIR Mapping Language*
stream (the only cross-reference for this ticket) provides the operative
context: Brian Postlethwaite goes a step further than this ticket and
proposes pulling FML into its own free-standing specification (analogous
to FHIRPath/CQL) so that FML can advance faster than the FHIR release
cadence, while Grahame Grieve pushes back that the right move is to
stabilise the current FML/`StructureMap` definition as normative in R6
and continue to evolve it within the existing structure. Lloyd McKenzie
floats the option of moving `StructureMap` into an incubator while
balloting FML normatively, and Bas van den Heuvel (the reporter) reframes
the discussion around a concrete impact question: what concretely breaks
or is blocked if `StructureMap`/FML are not in core, particularly for
HL7's planned R4↔R6 mappings. The discussion is unresolved and is
expected to come back to the FML telco. There is no related GitHub PR or
issue surfaced in the cross-references for this ticket.

## Related Zulip Discussions

### [FHIR Mapping Language > Move FML to a new spec?](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/FHIR%20Mapping%20Language/topic/Move%20FML%20to%20a%20new%20spec%3F/near/572884189)

Brian Postlethwaite opens by citing this ticket and his own related
proposal (FHIR-54473) and arguing that FML should be carved out of FHIR
core and given its own specification — analogous to FHIRPath and CQL —
so that it can keep advancing without being gated on the FHIR core
release cycle, while existing FML can still be projected to/from older
and newer `StructureMap` representations. He collates 22 issues he has
logged against the current FML on a HackMD page and lists the known FML
engines/tooling (HAPI, Matchbox, his own .NET port, Healex, MaLac-HD,
Ignixa, MapBuilder, fml-processor, fhirpath-lab, etc.) to gauge
implementer impact.

The discussion then splits along two lines. Grahame Grieve argues that
nothing in the current arrangement is sufficiently broken to justify
removing `StructureMap` from core; the right move is to stabilise the
current FML/`StructureMap` as a normative R6 deliverable and continue to
evolve it (he highlights that the planned R4↔R6 version mappings are
written in FML, so removing FML from core leaves that work without a
formal basis). He pushes back specifically on the claim that FML can
evolve cleanly without `StructureMap` changes, citing FHIR-54474 as a
counter-example. Lloyd McKenzie raises the practical option of moving
`StructureMap` into an incubator while still balloting FML normatively,
but is concerned about how readers of the core spec would understand
they are not looking at the "current" version, and how the release
hierarchy would expose incubator releases. Oliver Egger (Matchbox) says
he would not mind FML having its own spec as long as it stays in the
FHIR ecosystem.

Bas van den Heuvel (the reporter) reframes the debate from "do we want
FML/StructureMap?" to "is the current specification mature enough for
normative?" and asks for a concrete impact statement — which production
activities are affected if FML/`StructureMap` are excluded from core, and
which stakeholders are affected — so the discussion is anchored to
requirements rather than positions. He proposes scheduling the topic for
the next Monday telco. The thread ends without consensus; there is broad
agreement that a stable definition of FML must be published for R6 and
that the current arrangement should not be allowed to block faster FML
evolution, but no agreement on whether the right vehicle is "stabilise
in core, evolve later" or "split out now."

Participants: Brian Postlethwaite, Grahame Grieve, Lloyd McKenzie, Bas
van den Heuvel, Oliver Egger, Reinhard Egelkraut.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Spin `StructureMap` and the FHIR Mapping Language out of FHIR Core into a
new HL7 "Additional Resource IG" (working title: *FHIR Mapping Language &
StructureMap IG*). Concretely:

- Remove the `StructureMap` resource and the FML grammar/processing pages
  from the R6 core ballot package and republish them as the sole content
  of a new IG with its own canonical, version axis, and ballot cycle.
- Keep the resource type name `StructureMap` and its canonical URL stable
  so existing instances and `ConceptMap.sourceCanonical`/`targetCanonical`,
  `$transform`, and version-mapping references continue to resolve.
- In core, leave a stub page that points at the IG, and continue to allow
  core-defined operations such as `$transform` to reference a
  `StructureMap` defined in the IG.
- Provide a one-time R6 baseline release of the IG that matches the
  content that would otherwise have shipped in core, so implementers
  have a stable normative anchor to point at.

#### Justification

This directly satisfies the ticket as written and matches the proposal
that the Zulip thread's instigator (Brian Postlethwaite) is also
advancing in FHIR-54473. The argument is symmetry with FHIRPath and CQL:
both are evaluated/tooled as living specs on their own cadence and have
benefited from not being bound to the FHIR release cycle. Multiple FML
implementers (Matchbox/Oliver Egger explicitly) have said they would
accept a separate spec as long as it stays in the HL7/FHIR ecosystem.

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Stabilise the **current** FML and `StructureMap` definition as the R6
normative deliverable inside FHIR Core, but explicitly designate FML as
an "evolvable specification" inside core (analogous to how FHIRPath is
referenced) and stand up a parallel incubator for FML+`StructureMap`
extensions that can publish out-of-band. Concretely:

- Keep `StructureMap` and the FML pages in R6 core and ballot them
  normative as currently planned, scoped to the syntax and semantics that
  current engines already implement interoperably.
- Add a clearly-labelled "FML evolution" page in core that points at an
  HL7 incubator project for new transforms, new functions, and any
  changes that would otherwise require waiting on R7.
- Bind `$transform` and the R4↔R6 version mappings to the R6-normative
  FML so they have a formal basis (Grahame's concrete blocker), while
  allowing the incubator stream to publish additive features that
  implementers can adopt early.
- Defer any decision to physically separate the IG until after at least
  one incubator publication cycle has demonstrated the workflow.

#### Justification

This addresses the underlying concern in the ticket — that FML adoption
and library coverage are uneven and that normative status risks freezing
features prematurely — without paying the structural cost of pulling a
core resource out mid-cycle. It directly answers the impact question
Bas raised on Zulip: the planned R4↔R6 version mappings still have a
formal, normative FML to bind to, and `$transform` keeps a stable home.
It also answers Lloyd's navigational concern (how a reader knows what
the "current" spec is) by keeping the normative anchor in one place and
treating the incubator output as additive rather than overriding. The
trade-off vs. Disposition A is that FML evolution stays nominally
HL7-process-bound, which Brian argued slows things down; the
counter-argument from Grahame is that the bottleneck is HL7 ballot
opportunities, not editorial energy, and splitting the IG would not
escape that.

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request to spin `StructureMap` and FML out of core. Resolve
as **Not Persuasive** with the rationale that (a) the request is
predicated on adoption / library-support claims that are not substantiated
in the ticket, (b) the immediate practical need — a stable, normative R6
definition of FML/`StructureMap` to underpin `$transform` and the
upcoming R4↔R6 version mappings — is best served by leaving the
artefacts in core, and (c) the change would be a breaking re-organisation
of a normative-track resource and there is no use case in the ticket
that an in-core normative ballot does not already cover. Recommend that
the reporter, Brian, and other interested parties take FML evolution
work to an existing channel (incubator, FML telco) rather than as a
core-spec restructure.

#### Justification

The ticket itself is a one-paragraph recommendation and does not present
the impact analysis that Bas himself called for on Zulip — i.e., it does
not enumerate which production activities are blocked, which
implementers are affected, or what backward-compatibility contract a
separated IG would offer. Grahame's point that "nobody is arguing the
current arrangement is so broken we cannot publish a stable version" is
on the table and has not been answered. Pulling a normative-track
resource out of core mid-cycle is a high-cost, externally-visible
change, and the FHIR design principle of keeping ConceptMap/StructureMap
co-located with the rest of the conformance stack still applies. If
later evidence shows the in-core arrangement is genuinely blocking FML
evolution, the request can be reopened with that evidence in hand.

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the disposition that takes both sides of the Zulip
discussion seriously without forcing an irreversible structural change
under time pressure. The thread shows two things at once: (1) there is
broad agreement — including from the reporter — that a *stable*
FML/`StructureMap` must exist for R6, and (2) there is also broad
agreement that FML should not be permanently bound to the FHIR release
cycle. Disposition A delivers (2) but at the cost of (1), because the
content that would otherwise be the R6 normative anchor has to be
republished as a brand-new IG inside the same ballot window — and as
Grahame noted, HL7 process and ballot opportunities are the actual
bottleneck, so an IG split does not in fact buy faster evolution.
Disposition C delivers (1) but ignores the legitimate evolution problem
the ticket raises and effectively re-litigates the same disagreement at
the next ballot. Disposition B is the only option that lets R6 ship a
stable normative FML/`StructureMap` for `$transform` and the R4↔R6
mappings while creating an explicit, sanctioned channel for the
faster-cadence FML work that Brian, Oliver, and others want to do — and
it preserves the option to physically separate the IG later, once that
incubator workflow has demonstrated value, which Disposition A
forecloses.
