## Summary

The reporter (Ignacio Jauregui) flags that the narrative describing logical
references on `Reference.identifier` in FHIR Core contains two sentences that
appear to contradict each other. The first asserts that there is "no
requirement that a `Reference.identifier` point to something that is actually
exposed or exists as a FHIR instance," while a later sentence says that the
"reference SHALL point to a business concept that would be expected to be
exposed as a FHIR instance." The parenthetical between them attempts to
reconcile the two by saying the reference will need to be resolved if any
information is required in a FHIR context, but the reporter finds that
bridging text insufficient: a reader is left unsure whether (a) a FHIR
instance must exist somewhere, (b) it merely needs to be conceptually
expressible as one of the allowed resource types, or (c) both, depending on
context. The ticket asks the FHIR Infrastructure work group to clean up the
language so the two scenarios (FHIR instance required vs. not required) are
clearly distinguished.

No cross-referenced Jira tickets, GitHub items, or in-person requesters are
attached to this issue, and the ticket has no Jira comments. A 2017
implementers Zulip thread captures the original design intent and is
referenced under "Related Zulip Discussions" below.

## Details

**Description:**

> The two sentences highlighted in bold in the quoted text below contradict
> each other. I understand that the text in parenthesis in the middle is the
> connection between both but is not entirely clearer as a reader how to
> make sense of both scenarios (FHIR instance required or not)
>
> *"**There is no requirement that a Reference.identifier point to something
> that is actually exposed or exists as a FHIR instance** (except, of course,
> that the reference will need to be resolved to a target resource if any
> information from it is required in a FHIR context). **The reference SHALL
> point to a business concept that would be expected to be exposed as a FHIR
> instance,** and that instance would need to be of a FHIR resource type
> allowed by the reference For example, it would not be legitimate to send
> the identifier for a drug prescription if the type were
> Reference(Observation|DiagnosticReport). One of the use-cases for
> `Reference.identifier` is the situation where no FHIR representation
> exists (where the type is Reference (Any)."*

## Related Zulip Discussions

### [implementers > Resource.identifier use for non FHIR identifiers](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/implementers/topic/Resource.identifier%20use%20for%20non%20FHIR%20identifiers/near/153869186)

This 2017 thread is the original community discussion that produced the
disputed paragraph. John Moehrke asked whether `Resource.identifier` /
`Reference.identifier` could carry *any* business identifier (e.g., a CDA
document ID, a DICOM object UID, a v2 message ID, a userID/processID) or
only identifiers that correspond to something that could be a FHIR resource
of the declared target type. Lloyd McKenzie articulated the position that
later became the spec text: the identifier need not point to an exposed FHIR
instance, but it must point to a business concept that *could* be exposed as
one of the allowed target resource types — so a paper lab report identifier
under `Reference(Observation|...)` is fine, but a patient MRN under that
same target list is not. Brian Postlethwaite pushed back, suggesting
`Reference.identifier` was originally meant for unrelated identifiers held
by the unit (e.g., an SSN on Patient) that don't point at anything. Grahame
Grieve sided with Lloyd: it is wrong to use `Reference.identifier` to refer
to something that is not a resource of the appropriate type, and a CDA
document is not an implicit Binary. Ewout Kramer agreed the documentation
was unclear and committed to filing a tracker (GF#12725) to clean it up,
explicitly noting he was "not entirely sure there's full agreement on
Lloyd's statement." The thread therefore confirms that (a) the SHALL on the
target type was intentional and consensus among the leads who responded,
and (b) the wording confusion the current ticket flags is long-standing.

*Participants:* Lloyd McKenzie, John Moehrke, Ewout Kramer, Brian
Postlethwaite, Grahame Grieve.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Rewrite the disputed paragraph in the FHIR Core narrative on logical
references so the two scenarios are separated and the apparent
contradiction is removed. Concretely:

1. Split the existing run-on sentence into two distinct rules:
   - **Rule 1 (existence is not required):** "A `Reference.identifier`
     does not require that a FHIR instance for the referenced concept
     actually exist or be exposed anywhere; the identifier may name a
     real-world business concept that has never been instantiated as a
     FHIR resource."
   - **Rule 2 (type compatibility is required):** "The identifier SHALL
     name a business concept of a kind that, *if it were ever
     represented in FHIR*, would be an instance of one of the resource
     types listed in `Reference.type` (or any resource type, when the
     declared target is `Reference(Any)`)."
2. Move the "reference will need to be resolved to a target resource if
   any information from it is required in a FHIR context" clause into a
   short third sentence that frames it as a *consumer-side*
   consideration, not an additional constraint on the sender.
3. Keep the existing "drug prescription under
   `Reference(Observation|DiagnosticReport)`" example as the
   counter-example for Rule 2, and add a positive example for Rule 1
   (e.g., "an MRN-equivalent identifier from a paper-only system for a
   patient under `Reference(Patient)`").
4. Edit lives in the FHIR Core spec narrative (most likely
   `source/references.html` in `HL7/fhir`); no schema, StructureDefinition,
   or invariant changes are needed. *Caveat:* the exact authoring path
   should be confirmed against the `HL7/fhir` repo-analysis briefing
   before a PR is opened — that briefing was not loaded for this prep run
   because no GitHub cross-references were attached to the ticket.

#### Justification

This is a Technical Correction ticket against narrative text that the
reporter explicitly identifies as confusing. The 2017 implementers Zulip
thread shows that the *intent* of the text is exactly what Disposition A
preserves (no instance required; target-type compatibility SHALL hold);
the problem is purely one of exposition. Splitting the rules and
demoting the resolution clause to a consumer concern preserves the
normative content (the SHALL stays a SHALL), avoids any breaking change
to validators or implementers, and addresses the reader confusion
directly. It is also consistent with how other FHIR Core narratives
state existence-vs-shape rules separately (e.g., contained resources,
canonical references).

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Restructure the section to lead with a small **table** that enumerates
the four cases the current paragraph conflates, then reduce the prose
to a short framing sentence plus the table. The table columns would be:

| Scenario | FHIR instance must exist? | Target-type rule |
|-|-|-|
| Concept is, or could be, instantiated as one of the allowed target types | No | Identifier SHALL be of a concept of one of the allowed target types |
| Concept could only be expressed as a resource *not* in the target list | — | Not allowed; widen `Reference.type` (e.g., to `Reference(Any)`) instead |
| Concept has no FHIR representation today, but conceptually fits an allowed type | No | Allowed; identifier travels alone |
| `Reference.type` is `Reference(Any)` | No | Any business identifier whose concept maps to *some* FHIR resource is allowed |

Place the table inline in `source/references.html` (or the equivalent
authoring page once confirmed against the `HL7/fhir` briefing), with the
existing prescription/Observation example as a footnote on row 2.
Also add a short cross-link from the data-type page for `Reference` to
this section so implementers landing on the type definition can find
the rule.

#### Justification

The reporter's complaint is that prose alone made the cases hard to
distinguish. A table makes the four cases explicit and removes the need
for the reader to reconcile two SHALL-adjacent sentences mentally. This
is consistent with how FHIR Core handles other multi-case rules (e.g.,
the cardinality/modifier rules table). The trade-off versus
Disposition A is that a table is a larger editorial change, slightly
harder to review as a diff, and may need to be mirrored in the printed
spec rendering — but it is more robust against future editors
re-introducing the same ambiguity. No normative content changes.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket as "Not Persuasive — text is correct as written" on the
basis that the two highlighted sentences are not actually contradictory
when read together with the parenthetical, and that the existing
counter-example (drug prescription under
`Reference(Observation|DiagnosticReport)`) already disambiguates them.
Optionally, add a one-line clarifying parenthetical
("…**SHALL** point to a business concept *of one of the target resource
types* that would be expected to be exposed as a FHIR instance…") and
otherwise leave the section unchanged.

#### Justification

The 2017 Zulip thread confirms that the substantive rule is settled and
the current text encodes it correctly. The ticket does not allege any
behavioral or normative defect; it is a readability complaint, and the
reporter themselves notes that the parenthetical *does* connect the two
sentences. A minimal in-place clarification preserves diff stability
across the spec, avoids the risk that a rewrite inadvertently shifts
the SHALL to a SHOULD, and keeps editorial churn off a long-quoted
paragraph. This option is defensible if the work group judges that the
editorial cost of a rewrite outweighs the modest readability benefit.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is explicitly filed as a Technical Correction against
*narrative* text, the underlying rule is well established in the 2017
implementers thread, and the reporter has identified a real reader-side
problem: two adjacent sentences each beginning with a normative-sounding
clause ("There is no requirement…" / "SHALL point to…") force the reader
to mentally reconcile them via a parenthetical. Disposition A fixes
that with the smallest faithful edit — splitting the existence rule
from the type-compatibility rule, demoting the resolution clause to a
consumer concern, and keeping the existing example — without touching
StructureDefinitions, invariants, or any downstream artifacts.
Disposition B is attractive if the work group wants a more durable fix
and is willing to absorb the larger editorial change, but it is best
treated as a follow-up if reader confusion persists after A lands.
Disposition C understates the reporter's concern and leaves a known
ambiguity in a frequently-cited paragraph.
