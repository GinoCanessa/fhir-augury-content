## Summary

FHIR-55141 is a Technical Correction against the FHIR Core specification page
for the `Basic` resource (`basic.html`). The reporter (Lloyd McKenzie, via an
imported HL7 ballot-style "Comment 107") flags that the page contains
contractions and a misspelling that should be cleaned up: specifically
`"doesn't"`, `"isn't"`, and `"managable"`. The reporter's stated rule is that
abbreviations (here, contractions) should be fully spelled out and that
misspellings should be corrected — i.e., `"doesn't"` → `"does not"`,
`"isn't"` → `"is not"`, and `"managable"` → `"manageable"`. The change is
purely editorial; there is no proposed change to resource structure,
cardinality, bindings, or invariants. No related Jira tickets, Zulip
threads, or GitHub items were surfaced by the cross-reference index for
this ticket key.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the editorial corrections requested on the `Basic` resource page
(`basic.html`). Concretely, in the narrative source for the `Basic`
resource page in the FHIR Core specification:

- Replace `doesn't` with `does not`.
- Replace `isn't` with `is not`.
- Replace `managable` with `manageable`.

Scope is limited to prose on the `Basic` page; no XML/JSON definitions,
StructureDefinitions, ValueSets, examples, or invariants are touched.
The change is editorial and should be marked as a Technical Correction
in the ticket disposition.

#### Justification

Contractions are routinely flagged by HL7 ballot reviewers as
inconsistent with the formal-publication tone of the FHIR
specification, and `managable` is an unambiguous misspelling of
`manageable`. The change has no semantic effect on conformance, no
backward-compatibility impact, and no implementer burden. Accepting
"as requested" is the lowest-risk, highest-value path for a
Technical Correction at "Highest" priority.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the spelling correction (`managable` → `manageable`) as
requested, but address the contraction concerns (`doesn't`, `isn't`)
as a broader editorial sweep rather than a one-off fix on the `Basic`
page. Specifically:

1. Apply the `managable` → `manageable` fix on `basic.html` immediately
   under this ticket.
2. Open (or roll into) a tracking ticket for a spec-wide pass that
   removes contractions from normative narrative across FHIR Core,
   driven by a `git grep` for common contraction patterns
   (`\b(doesn't|isn't|don't|won't|can't|aren't|wasn't|weren't|shouldn't|wouldn't|couldn't|hasn't|haven't|hadn't)\b`)
   over the spec's authoring sources, with results triaged page by
   page.

#### Justification

If contractions are stylistically inappropriate on `basic.html`, they
are equally inappropriate elsewhere in the spec. Fixing only the three
instances called out on a single page risks leaving the bulk of the
inconsistency in place and generating a long tail of near-identical
Technical Correction tickets. Splitting the work — a quick
page-scoped fix for the misspelling here, plus a coordinated sweep
for contractions — produces a cleaner end state with comparable
effort and avoids paperwork churn. Trade-off: the sweep is more work
than this single ticket and benefits from FHIR-I scheduling rather
than being absorbed silently.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the contraction portion of the request and accept only the
misspelling fix:

- Apply `managable` → `manageable` on `basic.html`.
- Reject the `doesn't` → `does not` and `isn't` → `is not` changes on
  the basis that contractions are within the FHIR specification's
  established narrative style and are not, in fact, "abbreviations"
  in the sense the FHIR style guide forbids (which targets undefined
  acronyms and shortened terms like "spec" or "info").

#### Justification

Contractions are widely used elsewhere in the FHIR Core narrative and
have not historically been treated as ballot-blocking style defects.
Treating them as such on a single page would either create
inconsistency with the rest of the spec or, if generalized,
manufacture a large editorial workload with no implementer benefit.
The misspelling is uncontroversial and should be corrected; the
contraction expansion is a stylistic preference best resolved by an
explicit FHIR-I style-guide decision rather than a per-page Technical
Correction.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

This is a "Highest" priority Technical Correction with three small,
unambiguous edits on a single page, raised by the FHIR-I co-chair.
The cost of accepting as requested is essentially zero, the risk to
implementers is zero, and the change is consistent with how the
imported "Comment 107" wave of ballot dispositions is being processed.
Disposition B is attractive in principle but should be tracked as a
separate work item rather than blocking this fix; if FHIR-I wants a
spec-wide contraction sweep, that decision can be recorded alongside
acceptance of A. Disposition C invites a style-guide debate that is
out of proportion to the size of this ticket and risks leaving
`managable` in the spec while the discussion runs.
