## Summary

The ticket asks the FHIR Infrastructure (FHIR-I) workgroup to consider migrating
several CodeSystem/ValueSet pairs that are bound from the `CapabilityStatement`
resource into THO (terminology.hl7.org), where they would be re-usable across
CDA, V2 and other HL7 specifications. The candidates are:

- `CodeSystem: simple-format-codes` (SimpleFormatCodes), referenced via
  ValueSet `supplemented-mimetypes`, bound to `CapabilityStatement.format`.
- `CodeSystem: restful-security-service` (RestfulSecurityService), referenced
  via ValueSet `restful-security-service`, bound to
  `CapabilityStatement.rest.security.service`.
- `CodeSystem: message-transport` (MessageTransport), referenced via ValueSet
  `message-transport`, bound to `CapabilityStatement.messaging.endpoint.protocol`.

The ticket has been resolved as *Persuasive with Modification*. The agreed
modification (per the Resolution Description and TSMG discussion captured in
the comments) is to migrate **only** the `restful-security-service` and
`message-transport` value sets/code systems — not `simple-format-codes` —
because those two had `terminology.hl7.org` URIs in R4/R4B and are reasonable
THO candidates. Because `CapabilityStatement` is normative, the migration is
contingent on TSMG confirming that the existing canonical CodeSystem URLs
(under `hl7.org/fhir`) can be retained even though the content is hosted in
THO. There are no explicitly linked Jira tickets and no related GitHub items
were surfaced in the cross-reference index.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Migrate all three CodeSystem/ValueSet pairs out of the FHIR Core specification
and into THO:

1. `simple-format-codes` (SimpleFormatCodes) + `supplemented-mimetypes`
   ValueSet bound to `CapabilityStatement.format`.
2. `restful-security-service` (RestfulSecurityService) +
   `restful-security-service` ValueSet bound to
   `CapabilityStatement.rest.security.service`.
3. `message-transport` (MessageTransport) + `message-transport` ValueSet bound
   to `CapabilityStatement.messaging.endpoint.protocol`.

For each, publish the CodeSystem and ValueSet under `terminology.hl7.org`,
deprecate the in-spec definition, and update the binding `valueSet` reference
on `CapabilityStatement` to the THO canonical URL. Coordinate with TSMG to
confirm that the existing `hl7.org/fhir` CodeSystem canonical URLs can be
preserved (the resource is normative, so the canonicals themselves cannot be
broken even after relocation).

#### Justification

This is the literal request and aligns with TSMG's stated policy that
re-usable terminology should live in THO so it is available to V2, CDA, and
other product families. It maximises cross-product re-use and centralises
maintenance. The in-spec ValueSet pages would become THO-published, which is
the steady-state TSMG would prefer.

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach (Recommended)

#### Proposal

Migrate **only** the two CodeSystem/ValueSet pairs that already had a
`terminology.hl7.org` URL in R4/R4B, exactly as captured in the Resolution
Description and confirmed by TSMG in the comments:

1. `restful-security-service` (CodeSystem + ValueSet) bound to
   `CapabilityStatement.rest.security.service` — relocate the authoring
   sources to THO/UTG and retain the existing FHIR canonical URL anchored at
   `terminology.hl7.org`.
2. `message-transport` (CodeSystem + ValueSet) bound to
   `CapabilityStatement.messaging.endpoint.protocol` — relocate the authoring
   sources to THO/UTG and retain the existing FHIR canonical URL anchored at
   `terminology.hl7.org`.

Do **not** migrate `simple-format-codes` / `supplemented-mimetypes`; leave it
in FHIR Core. Operationally:

- Update the FHIR Core CapabilityStatement bindings to point at the
  THO-published ValueSet canonicals.
- Move the CodeSystem and ValueSet authoring sources to THO/UTG, keeping the
  existing canonical URLs unchanged so no implementer-visible URL break occurs.
- Coordinate the change with TSMG and UTG so the THO publication takes effect
  at the same time the FHIR Core build stops emitting the relocated artifacts.

#### Justification

This is the disposition TSMG actually arrived at on the case-by-case review:
both of these CodeSystems were already published under
`terminology.hl7.org` in R4/R4B, so relocating their authoring while keeping
the canonical URL stable is non-breaking for R4 implementers (the larger
deployed base) and consistent with the normative-stability constraint on
`CapabilityStatement`. It declines to migrate `simple-format-codes`, whose R4
URL was *not* on `terminology.hl7.org` and would therefore require either a
URL break (forbidden — normative) or the unresolved R4-URL-retention question
that Josh Snell flagged. It addresses the underlying TSMG re-use rationale
without taking on the riskier third migration.

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Do not migrate any of the three CodeSystem/ValueSet pairs. Leave the
CapabilityStatement bindings unchanged in FHIR Core, on the basis that the
content is tightly coupled to the FHIR resource definitions, that
`CapabilityStatement` is normative (so any URL-changing migration is
prohibited), and that THO migration without canonical-URL changes adds
maintenance complexity (split authoring location vs. publication URL) without
clear cross-product demand.

#### Justification

A defensible rationale on grounds of (a) normative stability — the CodeSystem
URLs cannot change, (b) low evidence of cross-product demand for these
particular FHIR-CapabilityStatement-specific concept domains, especially
`simple-format-codes`, and (c) operational cost of maintaining content in THO
while keeping `hl7.org/fhir`-anchored canonicals. However, this disposition
would directly contradict the Persuasive-with-Modification resolution and
TSMG's case-by-case approval already documented in the comments.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B reflects the actual outcome of the TSMG case-by-case review
recorded in the comments and the Resolution Description: migrate the two
ValueSets that already had `terminology.hl7.org` canonicals in R4/R4B
(`restful-security-service` and `message-transport`) and leave
`simple-format-codes` in FHIR Core. It satisfies the original request's intent
(make THO-eligible terminology re-usable) while honouring the hard constraint
that the normative `CapabilityStatement` cannot have its CodeSystem URLs
broken, and it avoids opening the still-unresolved question Lloyd raised about
retaining `hl7.org/fhir` URLs after a THO move. Disposition A overreaches into
content TSMG explicitly did not endorse for migration; Disposition C
contradicts the resolution already on file. FHIR-I should ratify B and hand
the relocation work to UTG/TSMG with the explicit instruction that the two
existing canonical URLs must be preserved verbatim.
