## Summary

The reporter (Philip Wilford, ADHA) is asking the FHIR Infrastructure /
SMART on FHIR work group to formalise the use of the SMART App Launch
`fhirContext` parameter — and in particular the `role` slot on each
`fhirContext` entry — by promoting the current "experimental"
treatment of role values to a defined, normative-track set in the full
SMART App Launch specification. CSIRO and the Australian Digital Health
Agency (ADHA) are both implementing/POCing `fhirContext` in their
national specifications and have collaborated on a community-curated
"fhirContext Role Registry" on Confluence
(https://confluence.hl7.org/display/FHIRI/fhirContext+Role+Registry).
The ticket proposes that HL7 publish an `hl7`-stewarded ValueSet
(initial seed taken from the registry) so that adopters and
implementers can converge on a common, interoperable set of role codes
rather than each implementer minting their own URIs. No related Jira
tickets were surfaced by the cross-reference index. Two Zulip
references exist: one is the reporter's own announcement of this ticket
in `fhir/infrastructure-wg > Defining fhirContext` (no replies yet),
and the other is a tangential mention in `smart > SMART Web Messaging &
SDC` where the reporter asks the SMART chair for time on the weekly
SMART call to discuss this ticket.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the request as filed: in the SMART App Launch specification,
move the `fhirContext.role` element from its current
experimental/"open-ended URI" status to a fully-specified element
bound to an HL7-stewarded ValueSet whose initial content is seeded
from the community "fhirContext Role Registry" on Confluence
(https://confluence.hl7.org/display/FHIRI/fhirContext+Role+Registry).

Concretely:

1. Create a new HL7 CodeSystem (e.g.
   `http://hl7.org/fhir/smart-app-launch/CodeSystem/fhir-context-role`)
   containing the role identifiers currently in the registry, plus a
   short definition for each.
2. Create a companion ValueSet
   (`http://hl7.org/fhir/smart-app-launch/ValueSet/fhir-context-role`)
   that includes the new CodeSystem and is the canonical "starter
   set" for `fhirContext.role`.
3. Update the SMART App Launch IG description of `fhirContext` to
   bind `fhirContext.role` to that ValueSet with an `extensible`
   binding (still allowing implementer-coined URIs where the seed set
   does not yet cover a use case, but steering implementers to the
   common terms first).
4. Document the registry-driven extension process (how a new role gets
   nominated, reviewed, and added) so that the ValueSet remains the
   single point of growth, rather than continuing to grow on the
   Confluence page in parallel.

#### Justification

The reporter and the underlying registry work demonstrate that
`fhirContext.role` is already being used in the wild (CSIRO, ADHA,
plus the 2023 SMART thread "Non-local launch context"), and that the
key remaining barrier to interoperability is the lack of a shared
vocabulary. HL7 publishing the ValueSet is exactly the kind of
convergence step the registry was designed to enable — it codifies
de-facto practice rather than inventing new mechanism. Using an
`extensible` binding keeps backwards compatibility with existing
implementations that have already minted their own URIs, while still
providing a clear "land here first" vocabulary for new adopters. This
also aligns with the general FHIR practice of binding role-like
coordination codes to HL7-managed terminology (cf. Provenance, Consent,
etc.).

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Promote `fhirContext.role` to a defined element, but do **not** publish
an HL7-stewarded CodeSystem as part of this change. Instead:

1. In the SMART App Launch IG, define `fhirContext.role` formally as
   "a URI naming the role this context plays in the launch", with a
   `required`-format/`example`-strength binding pointing at an HL7
   ValueSet that simply *references the existing community registry*
   (a Confluence-anchored or registry-style URL) and a small set of
   "starter" example URIs lifted from the registry.
2. Encourage real-mint URIs in the implementer's own namespace
   (`http://example.org/fhir-context-role/...`) when no registered URI
   exists, and direct contributors to nominate new roles into the
   registry rather than into HL7's CodeSystem.
3. Optionally re-evaluate after one ballot cycle whether the registry
   has stabilised enough to be lifted into an HL7-managed CodeSystem
   (Disposition A) in a future release.

#### Justification

`fhirContext.role` was deliberately defined as a URI (not a `Coding`)
precisely so that implementers could mint identifiers in their own
namespaces without going through HL7. Standing up a normative HL7
CodeSystem for what is still effectively an emerging coordination
problem risks (a) ossifying definitions that the community is still
refining (CSIRO's and ADHA's POCs are active, not stable), (b) creating
maintenance pressure on HL7 to curate a registry it does not currently
have a process for, and (c) implicitly de-legitimising the URIs that
existing implementations have already coined. Binding to a registry
pointer rather than an HL7 CodeSystem preserves the URI-namespace
philosophy of the existing element while still giving implementers the
"common starter set" the reporter asks for. This is a lighter-weight
step that can be revisited once the registry has demonstrated
stability across more than two implementations.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request to add an HL7-stewarded ValueSet for
`fhirContext.role` in this revision of SMART App Launch. Keep the
existing element exactly as it is — a URI whose meaning is established
by community usage and the Confluence registry — and instead add a
non-normative IG note pointing readers at the existing
[fhirContext Role Registry](https://confluence.hl7.org/display/FHIRI/fhirContext+Role+Registry)
as the canonical place to look up and contribute role URIs.

Rationale category: *insufficient implementation evidence to justify
HL7-managed terminology at this time.*

#### Justification

Only two implementer organisations (CSIRO and ADHA) are explicitly
named in the ticket, and the public Zulip discussion specifically
about this proposal (`fhir/infrastructure-wg > Defining fhirContext`)
has so far drawn zero replies. The original SMART App Launch design of
`fhirContext.role` as a free-form URI was an explicit choice to avoid
HL7 having to curate launch-context vocabularies. Promoting role to an
HL7-managed ValueSet now would commit HL7 to ongoing terminology
governance for a relatively niche surface area, on the basis of
evidence that does not yet show widespread cross-implementer demand.
Pointing at the registry as a community resource preserves the
reporter's interoperability goal without taking on that governance
burden prematurely.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The reporter has identified a real interoperability gap — implementers
*are* using `fhirContext.role` and there *is* a registry — and simply
declining (Disposition C) leaves that convergence work entirely outside
the spec, where adopters reading the SMART App Launch IG won't even
discover the registry exists. At the same time, jumping straight to a
fully HL7-stewarded normative CodeSystem (Disposition A) is a heavier
governance commitment than the current evidence base (two named
implementers, a single-message Zulip topic) supports, and it works
against the deliberate URI-as-namespace design of `fhirContext`.

Disposition B threads the needle: it formalises that `fhirContext.role`
is meaningful and worth converging on, gives implementers an HL7-blessed
pointer at the existing community registry plus a small set of example
URIs in the spec itself, and explicitly leaves the door open to a
follow-on ticket that promotes the registry to a fully HL7-managed
CodeSystem once usage has stabilised across more implementations and a
ballot cycle has surfaced wider feedback. That sequencing matches how
other FHIR registries (e.g., the FHIRcast event catalog) have matured
from community-curated lists to first-class spec artifacts.
