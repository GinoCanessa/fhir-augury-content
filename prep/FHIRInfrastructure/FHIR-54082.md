## Summary

FHIR-54082 is an **umbrella ("parent") tracker** filed by Eric Haas requesting a
general rewrite of the FHIR Core **Signatures page** (`signatures.html`). The
rewrite has five overarching goals:

1. Reframe the page so that **FHIR content is the focus** and FHIR is treated
   as the "envelope" that carries signature content (rather than the page
   reading as a treatise on signature technology).
2. **De-emphasize signature-specific rules** and remove unnecessary constraints
   that the FHIR core spec currently imposes on signature methods (those
   constraints are seen as duplicating, and at times conflicting with, the
   underlying signature standards — JWS/JAdES, XMLDSig, CMS/CAdES — and with
   use-case-specific profiles).
3. **Add a CMS signatures section**, since CMS/CAdES is widely used in
   real-world signature infrastructure but is currently absent from the page.
4. **Add examples of non-digital electronic signatures** so the page covers the
   full breadth of what "Signature" means in FHIR, not just digital signatures.
5. **Edit the existing prose** for clarity and readability throughout.

The work has been broken into the following child trackers, each of which
addresses one slice of the rewrite. They are the actionable units; this ticket
exists to give the workgroup a single place to record the overall direction:

- [FHIR-53835](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53835) — Rewrite the
  introduction; rename the page from "Digital Signatures" to "Signatures";
  cover all kinds of electronic signatures and use the term "electronic
  signature" correctly.
- [FHIR-53891](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53891) — Edits to the
  Canonicalization section for clarity and consistency.
- [FHIR-53892](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53892) — Clarify or remove the
  "Uncertainty about what is signed" sentence about narrative-as-signed-form.
- [FHIR-53893](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53893) — Clarify and edit the
  Canonicalization Variants section (the `…#data`, `…#narrative`, etc. URIs).
- [FHIR-53913](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53913) — Update the SMART
  Health Cards link for "signature protocol for FHIR Bundles".
  *(Resolved – No Change / Retracted by reporter.)*
- [FHIR-53914](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53914) — Edit the "Signing
  Bundles" section, including correcting misuse of the term "enveloping
  signatures" outside XMLDSig.
- [FHIR-53955](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53955) — Rewrite the "Digital
  Signature Methods" section; remove conjecture about mixing formats with
  signature methods; treat that as a business decision.
- [FHIR-54025](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54025) — Rewrite the "JWS
  Signatures" section to focus on FHIR content rather than restating JWS/JAdES
  requirements (e.g., remove "JWS protected header SHALL contain either a
  `kid` or `x5c`").
- [FHIR-54035](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54035) — Add a new CMS
  signatures section (CMS / CAdES guidance, mirroring the JWS proposal).
- [FHIR-54080](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54080) — Rewrite the XML
  Digital Signature section; remove redundant statements and unnecessary
  XMLDSig requirements (e.g., "Signature SHALL contain an X.509 Certificate").
- [FHIR-54081](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54081) — Edit text and clarify
  the rules around using a single Provenance to carry multiple signatures.

The reporter has staged a working draft of the rewritten page in his personal
fork at `https://github.com/Healthedata1/fhir/blob/master/source/signatures.html`,
which the child trackers reference as the source for their proposed text.

## Linked Jira Tickets

### [FHIR-53835: Rewrite Introduction to Signature (aka Digital Signatures) Page](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53835)

Argues that the current page is mis-titled "Digital Signature" while being
referenced as "Signatures", uses the term "electronic signature" incorrectly,
omits non-digital electronic signatures, and over-focuses on JWS and XMLDSig
while ignoring CMS. Proposes renaming the page to "Signatures", writing a new
introduction that covers all electronic-signature types, and using the term
"electronic signature" correctly. This is the framing change that the rest of
the rewrite hangs off of.

Reporter: Eric Haas. Assignee: Unassigned.

### [FHIR-53891: edits to Canonicalization Sections](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53891)

Provides line-level rewrites of the Canonicalization section (the introductory
paragraph that explains why canonicalization exists for JSON and XML wire
formats and that the canonicalization method URI SHOULD be carried on the
signature). Aimed at clarity and consistency rather than changing semantics,
and is co-located with FHIR-53893 (the Canonicalization Variants subsection).

Reporter: Eric Haas. Assignee: Unassigned.

### [FHIR-53892: Clarify or remove sentence](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53892)

Targets §6.1.2.1.2 "Uncertainty about what is signed", specifically the
discussion of human-level non-repudiation and the suggestion that implementers
should pay attention to `Resource.text` (narrative) as the signed form. Eric
finds the text confusing and asks to either clarify it or remove it. Touches
on the broader question of what "is signed" when a FHIR resource is signed.

Reporter: Eric Haas. Assignee: Unassigned.

### [FHIR-53893: Clarify and edit Canonicalization Variants section](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53893)

Edits the Canonicalization Variants subsection that defines the
`http://hl7.org/fhir/canonicalization/{fmt}#data`, `…#narrative`, etc. URIs.
Clarifies what each variant omits (e.g., recursively omitting all
`Narrative` elements) and tightens the description so implementers can
choose a variant that matches the workflow the signature serves.

Reporter: Eric Haas. Assignee: Unassigned.

### [FHIR-53913: Update link for "signature protocol for FHIR Bundles"](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53913)

Originally proposed redirecting the SMART Health Cards link in the Signatures
page to
`https://hl7.org/fhir/uv/smart-health-cards-and-links/cards-specification.html#generating-and-resolving-cryptographic-keys`,
pending verification with Josh Mandel. **The reporter has since retracted this
ticket (Resolved – No Change, Retracted)**, so it is not in scope for the
parent rewrite. The struck-through link in the parent description reflects
this.

Reporter: Eric Haas. Assignee: Unassigned.

### [FHIR-53914: Edits to the Signing Bundles sections](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53914)

Rewrites the Signing Bundles section and corrects what the reporter sees as a
misuse of terminology: "enveloping signatures" is meaningful only in XMLDSig,
because it describes a structural relationship specific to XML; JWS and CMS
have fundamentally different structures. Also notes that signing Bundles with
embedded signatures is not the only option (enveloping JSON/XML signatures or
out-of-band Provenance can be used instead).

Reporter: Eric Haas. Assignee: Unassigned.

### [FHIR-53955: Rewrite Digital Signature Methods section](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-53955)

Rewrites the "Digital Signature Methods" section. Removes the SMART Health
Cards explanation (treated as extraneous, since SHC is a W3C VC Data Model
implementation that happens to use JWS) and removes statements about mixing
content formats with signature methods, calling those statements conjecture —
most signature methods are content-format-agnostic, and the choice is
typically a business/use-case decision rather than something the FHIR spec
should constrain.

Reporter: Eric Haas. Assignee: Unassigned.

### [FHIR-54025: rewrite "JWS Signatures" section to focus on FHIR content rather than signature content](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54025)

Rewrites the JWS section to describe what a FHIR implementer needs to know
about putting FHIR content into a JWS, while removing JWS/JAdES standards
language that belongs in the JWS specs themselves. Specifically calls out
removing requirements like "The JWS protected header SHALL contain either a
`kid` or an `x5c` property" and the JAdES `srCms` header recommendations
referencing `http://uri.etsi.org/01903/v1.2.2`. The aim is to keep the FHIR
core spec a flexible base standard that does not collide with use-case-
specific profiles.

Reporter: Eric Haas. Assignee: Unassigned.

### [FHIR-54035: Add CMS signatures section to support existing infrastructure](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54035)

Adds a brand new CMS section to the page, sitting before the JWS section,
covering CMS (RFC 5652) and CAdES (CMS Advanced Electronic Signature). Mirrors
the JWS approach (focus on FHIR content) and gives guidance on populating
`Signature.type` from the Signature Type Codes value set, on Purpose, and on
how CMS interoperates with the canonicalization variants. Brings parity with
JWS/XMLDSig coverage.

Reporter: Eric Haas. Assignee: Unassigned.

### [FHIR-54080: Rewrite XML Digital Signature Section](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54080)

Rewrites the XMLDSig section in the same spirit as FHIR-54025 and FHIR-54035:
focus on FHIR content, drop XMLDSig-standard requirements that the FHIR core
spec should not be re-stating. Concretely proposes removing redundant text
such as "When FHIR Resources are signed, the signature is across either the
Canonical XML form or Canonical JSON form of the resource(s) as indicated in
the targetFormat element" (already covered earlier on the page) and the
unjustified "The Signature SHALL contain an X.509 Certificate" requirement.

Reporter: Eric Haas. Assignee: Unassigned.

### [FHIR-54081: Edit text and Clarify using a single Provenence for multiple signatures](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54081)

Two edits to the Provenance-based signature subsection: (1) tighten the
opening sentence ("When a provenance resource is used to carry a signature,
the following rules apply" → "When Provenance is used to carry a signature,
the following rules apply"); and (2) clarify the third bullet so that it is
explicit that all digital signatures in the same Provenance share the same
signer/time/purpose, and that the primary reason to use multiple signatures
in one Provenance is to carry different media representations (text, image)
that should themselves be signed.

Reporter: Eric Haas. Assignee: Unassigned.

## Proposed Dispositions

**Note on grounding.** Because there are no GitHub cross-references for this
parent ticket, no `repo-analysis` briefing applies. The dispositions below are
framed at the *process* level — how the workgroup should treat the umbrella
itself — rather than at the per-edit level. The actual line-level changes
will be carried out (and grounded in `HL7/fhir` repo context) on the
individual child trackers.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Accept FHIR-54082 as the umbrella for the Signatures-page rewrite and triage
each of the ten still-open child trackers (FHIR-53835, -53891, -53892,
-53893, -53914, -53955, -54025, -54035, -54080, -54081) on its own merits.
FHIR-53913 is already retracted and need not be re-examined. Concretely:

- Adopt FHIR-53835's framing change — rename the page from "Digital
  Signatures" to "Signatures" and rewrite the introduction so it covers all
  electronic-signature types (digital and non-digital).
- Adopt the editorial-only children (FHIR-53891, FHIR-53892, FHIR-53893,
  FHIR-53914, FHIR-54081) as text edits to `source/signatures.html` /
  `source/signatures-introduction.md` in `HL7/fhir`.
- Adopt the FHIR-as-envelope rewrites of the per-method sections
  (FHIR-53955 Methods overview, FHIR-54025 JWS, FHIR-54080 XMLDSig) and the
  new CMS section (FHIR-54035), all of which **remove or relax** core-spec
  constraints that duplicate the underlying signature standards.
- Track the parent ticket as "in progress" until all children are resolved,
  then close it as Resolved – Persuasive with No Change of its own.

#### Justification

The reporter (Eric Haas) has done substantial up-front work: the rewrite is
already drafted in `Healthedata1/fhir/source/signatures.html`, decomposed into
focused child trackers, and individually justified. The framing change —
treating FHIR as an envelope and pushing signature-format details back into
the JWS/XMLDSig/CMS specs — is consistent with FHIR's broader principle of
not re-stating external standards in normative text and reduces the risk of
the FHIR Signatures page going stale relative to JWS/JAdES/CAdES updates.
Adding CMS gives parity with widely deployed real-world infrastructure and
closes an obvious gap.

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the umbrella in principle, but **bin the children into three
disposition tracks** and process each track as a unit rather than ticket-by-
ticket:

1. **Editorial track** (apply as Persuasive with Mod, batch PR): FHIR-53835
   intro/title change, FHIR-53891 Canonicalization edits, FHIR-53892 narrative
   sentence, FHIR-53893 Variants edits, FHIR-53914 Signing Bundles edits,
   FHIR-54081 Provenance edits.
2. **Constraint-removal track** (apply as Persuasive, batch PR, but flag for
   reviewer signoff because they relax existing SHALL/SHOULD statements):
   FHIR-53955 Methods, FHIR-54025 JWS, FHIR-54080 XMLDSig.
3. **New-content track** (apply as Persuasive, separate PR with a wider
   review window because it is net-new normative content): FHIR-54035 CMS
   section.

The umbrella ticket itself is then resolved Persuasive once all three tracks
land.

#### Justification

The children differ substantially in *risk*, even though the reporter has
filed them at the same priority. Editorial edits are safe; constraint
removals (e.g., dropping "SHALL contain an X.509 Certificate" from XMLDSig,
dropping the JWS `kid`/`x5c` requirement) change conformance and deserve a
deliberate sign-off because existing implementations may be relying on those
statements. New CMS content introduces a section that the workgroup has not
previously reviewed and warrants its own review window. Bundling by risk —
rather than processing eleven tickets in series — makes the workgroup's time
on this rewrite proportional to where the actual disagreement is likely to
arise.

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-54082 itself as **Not Persuasive – administrative**: the parent
adds no actionable change of its own beyond what the eleven child trackers
already carry. Direct the workgroup to dispose of each child on its own
merits and leave no separate parent decision on the books.

#### Justification

FHIR-54082 has no description content beyond a recap of the goals and a list
of links to child trackers. There is no edit to apply at the parent level
and no normative text the workgroup can adopt or reject from the parent
itself. Carrying it as an open item in addition to the eleven children
adds workgroup overhead (status to maintain, recommendation to vote on)
without producing a separate spec change. Declining the parent does **not**
decline the rewrite — every substantive edit lives on a child ticket and
will be triaged there.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The rewrite as a whole is well-motivated and the reporter has done the work
to make it triagable, so simply declining the parent (Disposition C) would
under-sell the effort even though it is procedurally clean. Disposition A
accepts that effort, but treating eleven children as eleven independent
votes risks the workgroup spending the same amount of time on a one-line
narrative-sentence edit (FHIR-53892) as on removing a SHALL from XMLDSig
(FHIR-54080). Disposition B preserves Eric's framing and gets the same end
state as A, but concentrates the workgroup's attention on the
constraint-removal track and the new CMS section, which are where genuine
implementer impact lives. The editorial track can be batched, and the
parent FHIR-54082 closes once all three tracks land.
