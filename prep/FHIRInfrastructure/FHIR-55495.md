## Summary

The ticket reports a casing inconsistency on the FHIR Core `fhirpath` page
(<https://build.fhir.org/fhirpath>): three occurrences of `System.string`
appear with a lowercase `s` and should instead use the canonical capitalised
form `System.String` that matches the FHIRPath / System type model used
throughout the rest of the specification. The change is purely editorial — no
semantics, conformance behaviour, or examples are altered, only the spelling
of the type name. The ticket has already been triaged with a resolution of
*Persuasive* and a stated resolution direction of "Update to System.String as
requested." It is grouped on the FHIR Infrastructure WG agenda with a number
of other small FHIRPath documentation fixes (see the Zulip thread below) that
the WG is considering as a block vote.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the editorial fix to the FHIRPath page in FHIR Core: change all three
occurrences of `System.string` to `System.String` on the page rendered at
<https://build.fhir.org/fhirpath>. The change is limited to the spelling of
the System primitive type name on that single narrative page; no XML/JSON
artifacts, examples, normative statements, or tooling outputs are altered.
Bundle this fix with the other small FHIRPath editorial tickets currently
queued for the FHIR-I block vote (FHIR-55637, FHIR-55417, FHIR-37861, etc.)
as Brian Postlethwaite proposed on Zulip.

#### Justification

The casing `System.String` (PascalCase) is the form used consistently
elsewhere in the FHIR spec and in the FHIRPath specification's System type
model; the lowercase variants on the page are demonstrably typos. The fix is
non-breaking, narrative-only, and matches the resolution direction
("Persuasive — Update to System.String as requested") already recorded on the
ticket. There is explicit Zulip support (Grahame Grieve proxy-voted in
favour) and no expressed opposition.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the fix in principle (same edit as Disposition A), but, while the
page is open, also sweep the rest of the FHIRPath narrative page and adjacent
FHIR Core pages for any other System-type casing inconsistencies (e.g.,
`System.boolean`, `System.integer`, `System.decimal`, `System.dateTime`) and
correct them in the same change set. Cross-link the sweep to the related
tickets in the FHIR-I block (notably FHIR-37861 "FHIRPath is and as samples
use improper case for System datatypes") so the work is not duplicated.

#### Justification

This ticket is one of several casing-related FHIRPath editorial reports
already on the FHIR-I agenda, which suggests there is more drift than the
three occurrences flagged here. A single coordinated sweep is cheaper to
review than handling each ticket in isolation and reduces the chance that
another reporter raises the next instance after publication. The risk is
minimal because all changes remain narrative-only.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket without making the requested change, on the grounds that
the FHIRPath narrative page mixes prose references to "System types" loosely
and the lowercase form does not impair reader comprehension or tooling
behaviour.

#### Justification

This is a defensible position only if the WG decides that further editorial
churn on the page should be deferred until a larger FHIRPath documentation
refactor (several such refactor-style tickets — e.g., FHIR-53958,
FHIR-53949 — are on the same block-vote slate). It is otherwise weak: the
existing resolution is "Persuasive", the fix is trivial, and there is no
implementation cost or risk of regression.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is a textbook editorial fix: three localised typos in the
narrative `fhirpath` page, with the canonical correct form (`System.String`)
already used elsewhere in the spec and in the FHIRPath standard. The
resolution direction recorded on the ticket is already *Persuasive — Update
to System.String as requested*, and the FHIR-I Zulip thread shows it being
queued for a block vote with explicit proxy support and no dissent. Holding
the change to exactly what was requested (Disposition A) is the lowest-risk
path and matches the WG's apparent intent. Disposition B (broader casing
sweep) is a reasonable enhancement and the WG may choose to fold it in if a
volunteer is willing to scan the surrounding pages, but it is not required
to dispose of FHIR-55495 itself. Disposition C is not supportable given the
existing resolution.
