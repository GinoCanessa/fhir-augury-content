## Summary

Brian Postlethwaite reports that the StructureMap resource (and the FHIR
Mapping Language grammar) does not cleanly represent the chaining of
properties in either source or target rules. The FML concrete syntax
already permits `context.element(.subElement)` where `.subElement` may
recurse to arbitrary depth on the **target** side, but this is not
defined for the **source** side, and the StructureMap resource itself
has no native way to capture the chained form — implementations are
left to either explode the chain into nested `then` rules or to invent
intermediate variable names. The ticket asks the working group to
clarify the intended round-trip behaviour and proposes a concrete
change: make the `element` property on both source and target repeat,
and document that source `context.element` may also chain into further
child elements. This ticket is one of a small cluster of FML / StructureMap
change requests Brian filed at the same time —
[FHIR-54473](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54473) (FML not ready for
normative), [FHIR-54477](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54477) (simple
identity transform missing in FML grammar), and
[FHIR-54480](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54480) (super-simple form
missing from both FML grammar and the StructureMap resource) — all of
which target gaps between the FML concrete syntax and the StructureMap
abstract resource. None of these tickets have explicit cross-references
in Augury yet; the relationship is via shared reporter, work group, and
keyword overlap.

## Details

**Description:**

> StructureMap doesn't happily represent the chaining of properties.
>
> The mapping language already defines:
> ***`context.element(.subElement):` where the subElement can recurse to any further depth***
>
> - is an implementation expected to just map it into the `then` syntax for the structure map?
> - would a round-tripper need to work that out and trim it down?
> - is there a specific variable name that should be used to make that work?
>
>   This however is not defined for the source side, making some maps larger than they need to be, in both the FML and StructureMap.
>
>   A sensible change for this would be to make the `element` property repeat in source and target.
>   And document that the source `context.element` can also repeat into further child elements too.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Make the `element` property repeat on both
`StructureMap.group.rule.source` and
`StructureMap.group.rule.target` (change cardinality from `0..1` to
`0..*`), with order significance — entry *n* is interpreted as a child
of entry *n−1*. Update the StructureMap resource definition,
constraints, and the rendered XML/JSON examples accordingly. In
parallel, update the FHIR Mapping Language page to (a) document that
`context.element(.subElement)` chained dotted access is permitted on
the **source** side as well as the target side, and (b) define the
canonical resource serialization of a chained source/target as the
ordered repeating `element` list above (so a round-tripper does **not**
need to invent intermediate `then` groups or synthetic variable
names). Add a worked example showing the same rule expressed in FML
chained form and in the resource form.

#### Justification

This is the change the reporter explicitly asks for and it directly
removes the FML↔StructureMap impedance mismatch driving the bug. It
also supports the broader normative-readiness push in
[FHIR-54473](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54473) and dovetails with
the simple-form gap in
[FHIR-54480](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54480), so all three can
be accepted as a coordinated change set. Repeating `element` keeps the
abstract model semantically equivalent to today's nested-`then`
expansion (no information is lost) while letting maps stay as compact
as the FML they came from. Round-tripping becomes mechanical: writers
collapse a `then`-only chain into the repeating element form, readers
re-expand if their engine prefers that shape.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Leave the StructureMap resource shape unchanged, and instead **define
the canonical lowering**: chained `context.element(.subElement…)` in
FML is normatively serialized as a sequence of nested `rule.then`
entries, with a documented, deterministic naming convention for the
intermediate `variable` (e.g., the source/target element name itself,
or a stable mangling rule). Document this lowering on the Mapping
Language page as a dedicated subsection ("Chained property access"),
add a round-trip conformance note that any FML→StructureMap converter
MUST produce, and any StructureMap→FML converter MUST collapse, this
canonical form. On the source side specifically, explicitly state
whether dotted chaining is permitted in `context.element` or whether
authors must use `where(...)` / `then` instead — answering one of the
ticket's open questions without changing the resource.

#### Justification

This addresses the underlying complaint (round-tripping is
under-specified, intermediate variable naming is ambiguous, source-side
behaviour is silent) without a backward-incompatible cardinality
change to a resource that the WG is actively trying to harden for
normative. It also has precedent: in
[FHIR-50272](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50272), the WG resolved
a similar source-side expressiveness gap by clarifying example
structure rather than mutating the grammar/resource, and in
[FHIR-23033](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-23033) it likewise
declined to broaden a StructureMap element type for the sake of a
concrete-syntax convenience. The cost is that maps stay verbose in
their resource form (the reporter's "larger than they need to be"
complaint persists); the gain is no risk to existing implementations
or to normative scoping.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline FHIR-54476 as **already addressed by the existing
`rule.then` mechanism plus deterministic variable naming**, and treat
the underlying concern as a documentation gap to be picked up under the
[FHIR-54473](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54473) normative-prep
work rather than as a discrete resource change. Recommend the reporter
fold the chaining concern into the FML incubator / separate-IG track
proposed in FHIR-54473.

#### Justification

The WG has previously declined adjacent expressiveness requests against
StructureMap and the mapping language in favour of clearer
documentation or restructured examples
([FHIR-50272](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50272),
[FHIR-23033](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-23033)). Accepting now
introduces a non-trivial cardinality change on a resource targeting
normative status, with attendant validator, generator, and
round-tripper churn, in service of a verbosity/aesthetic concern that
can be neutralized by tooling. If FML is split off per FHIR-54473, the
chaining shorthand becomes an FML-spec concern and never needs to
mutate the StructureMap resource at all.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the smallest defensible change that actually answers
the questions the reporter raised — round-trip behaviour, intermediate
variable naming, and whether chaining is legal on the source side —
without touching `StructureMap.group.rule.{source,target}.element`
cardinality on a resource the WG is trying to lock down for normative.
It is consistent with the WG's prior pattern in
[FHIR-50272](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-50272) and
[FHIR-23033](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-23033) of fixing
expressiveness/clarity issues in the syntax and prose layer rather
than mutating the StructureMap resource. Disposition A is the right
answer if and only if the WG concludes that compact resource
serialization is a hard requirement (e.g., if persisted-map size or
human readability of the StructureMap JSON is a real implementer
problem); in that case A should be coordinated with
[FHIR-54477](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54477) and
[FHIR-54480](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-54480) so the resource
shape changes once, not three times. Disposition C is too aggressive
on its own — the source-side silence the reporter calls out is a real
spec gap and shouldn't be deferred indefinitely to the FHIR-54473
umbrella.
