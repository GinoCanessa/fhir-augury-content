## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Edit `source/mapping-language.html` in `HL7/fhir` to expand every prose
occurrence of the contraction `"doesn't"` to `"does not"`. While performing
the edit, also scan the same page for the other common contractions the
workgroup style guide treats as informal (`won't`, `can't`, `isn't`, `it's`
when used as "it is", `we'll`, `you'll`, `there's`, etc.) and expand those as
well, on the basis that the ticket title is "Spelling/abbreviation issues"
plural and the reporter is consistently filing the same template across many
pages. Skip occurrences inside `<code>`, `<pre>`, attribute values, or quoted
grammar fragments where the contraction is part of an example. Do not change
section structure, anchors, or normative content.

#### Justification

This is the literal request and matches the disposition Lloyd's bot will
expect for the family of sibling tickets ([FHIR-55327](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-55327),
[FHIR-55167](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-55167), [FHIR-55164](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-55164),
[FHIR-55185](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-55185), and the rest of the
sweep). Expanding contractions in normative-adjacent specification prose is a
defensible style choice — it improves readability for non-native English
readers and removes a class of avoidable ambiguity (`"isn't"` vs `"is not"`)
without affecting normative content. The change is scoped to a single
authored HTML file under `source/`, requires no schema, terminology, or
StructureDefinition changes, has no cross-repo impact, and carries no risk of
breaking implementations.

---

### Disposition B: Accept In Principle, But Roll Into a Bulk Editorial Pass

#### Proposal

Acknowledge the report as valid editorial feedback but **do not** apply it
ticket-by-ticket. Instead, FHIR Infrastructure adopts a one-time bulk
editorial pass across all `source/*.html` and per-resource `source/<resource>/`
narrative pages in `HL7/fhir`, applying a workgroup-agreed style rule (e.g.,
"no informal contractions in normative or normative-adjacent prose; expand
`don't`, `doesn't`, `won't`, `can't`, `isn't`, `it's→it is` outside of
`<code>`/`<pre>`/quoted examples"). FHIR-55112 and every other ticket in the
sibling sweep that flags only a contraction (e.g., [FHIR-55327](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-55327))
are then resolved as **Persuasive with Modification — applied via bulk pass**,
referencing the umbrella commit. Tickets in the same sweep that flag genuine
misspellings (e.g., [FHIR-55167](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-55167)
`relevent`, [FHIR-55164](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-55164)
`exceptiont`, [FHIR-55185](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-55185)
`provice`) continue to be handled individually because they require a
per-page judgement.

#### Justification

The cached repo briefing confirms `mapping-language.html` is one of dozens of
authored prose pages under `source/`. Search results show this is the same
template ticket repeated for many Core pages, with the contraction `"doesn't"`
appearing as the specific item more than once. Triaging each as an individual
Highest-priority Technical Correction creates substantial workgroup overhead
for what is functionally a single style decision. A bulk pass also avoids the
inconsistent-state failure mode where some pages keep contractions and others
do not, depending on which sibling ticket happens to be reviewed first. The
trade-off vs. Disposition A is that it requires a one-time cross-page
coordination effort rather than a single-file edit, but the marginal cost per
ticket drops substantially.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (Not Persuasive)

#### Proposal

Resolve as **Not Persuasive**, on the basis that `"doesn't"` is a
well-formed English contraction, not a misspelling and not an abbreviation in
the sense the ticket template intends (which is acronym/initialism expansion
such as `"FMG"` → `"FHIR Management Group"` in [FHIR-55104](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-55104)).
Contractions are widely used throughout FHIR Core narrative prose and are
acceptable per existing convention. No change to `source/mapping-language.html`.

#### Justification

The ticket conflates two distinct categories that the sibling sweep treats
identically: (1) genuine misspellings (`relevent`, `exceptiont`, `provice`,
`occurence`, `desireable`, etc.) and (2) informal-but-grammatical contractions
(`doesn't`). FHIR Core specification prose has always permitted contractions;
silently changing this for one page without a workgroup-level style decision
risks an inconsistent style across the spec and sets a precedent for
mechanically expanding every contraction in the entire FHIR Core corpus —
which is a much larger change than a `Highest`-priority Technical Correction
warrants. If the workgroup wants a no-contractions style, it should be agreed
explicitly (see Disposition B) rather than driven page-by-page by an
automated reporter.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **B**

The ticket itself is genuinely trivial — expanding one contraction on one HTML
page is a five-minute change, which makes Disposition A tempting. But the
search results make clear that FHIR-55112 is one node in a large, mechanically
generated family of sibling tickets, several of which flag the *same*
contraction (`doesn't` also appears on `procedure` per
[FHIR-55327](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-55327)) while others flag real
spelling errors and others still flag regional spellings (`Organisation` on
`license`). Treating each as an isolated Technical Correction will produce
inconsistent prose across the spec depending on which tickets reach a reviewer
first, and burns workgroup cycles that would be better spent on a single
style decision. Disposition B captures the legitimate editorial signal Lloyd
is surfacing, applies it consistently across `source/` in one pass, and lets
the genuine-misspelling siblings be triaged on their own merits. Disposition A
remains a reasonable fallback if FHIR Infrastructure prefers per-ticket
traceability; Disposition C should only be chosen if the workgroup explicitly
endorses contractions as Core house style.
