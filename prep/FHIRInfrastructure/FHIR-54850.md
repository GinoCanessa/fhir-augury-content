## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the two literal text corrections to the FHIR Core specification's
Search page source:

- Replace `desireable` with `desirable`.
- Replace `explicity` with `explicitly`.

The change is editorial only — no normative content, examples,
constraints, or schema changes. As part of the fix, scan the rest of
the Search page (and ideally the broader spec) for the same two
misspellings to ensure consistency and to avoid leaving sibling
occurrences unfixed.

#### Justification

The reported strings are unambiguous English misspellings with no
plausible domain meaning. Correcting them improves the readability and
professionalism of a heavily-trafficked spec page (Search) at zero risk
to implementers. Technical Correction is exactly the issue type
intended for this kind of low-impact editorial fix, and the FHIR
Infrastructure work group routinely batches such corrections.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the two corrections, but bundle them with a broader spell-check
sweep of the FHIR Core narrative pages (or at minimum the
Infrastructure-owned pages: Search, RESTful API, Operations, Bundle
narrative, etc.). Optionally, add a CI step or scripted spell-check
pass that runs against the narrative HTML/Markdown sources so future
typos surface automatically before publication.

#### Justification

Drive-by typo tickets recur frequently and consume disproportionate
review cycles relative to their impact. A one-shot sweep plus a
lightweight automated check pays for itself quickly and reduces the
number of single-typo tickets the work group has to triage. The
trade-off is up-front effort and noise from false positives (FHIR
terminology, code names, and acronyms will need an allow-list).

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change on the grounds that the misspellings do not affect
the normative meaning of the specification and could be addressed in
the next routine editorial pass rather than as a tracked Jira ticket.

#### Justification

Strictly defensible only on workflow/process grounds — i.e., the work
group prefers not to track every individual typo as a Jira ticket and
would rather accumulate them. There is no substantive reason to leave
the misspellings in place; declining purely on cost grounds is hard to
justify given the trivial implementation effort.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

This is a textbook Technical Correction: two unambiguous English
misspellings on a high-visibility page, no normative impact, and no
community discussion suggesting any alternative meaning. Accept and
apply the literal fixes, and opportunistically grep the rest of the
Search page (and ideally the wider spec) for the same two typos so the
work isn't repeated in a future ticket. Disposition B is attractive in
principle but is out of scope for this single ticket and should be
raised separately if the work group wants a standing spell-check
process. Disposition C has no substantive merit here.
