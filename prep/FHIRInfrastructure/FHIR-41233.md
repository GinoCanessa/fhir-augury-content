## Summary

The ticket asks SDC to provide a standard mechanism for capturing the
"correct" set of answers for a Questionnaire — i.e., an answer key
against which a respondent's QuestionnaireResponse can be assessed.
The reporter (Julian Sass) suggests an extension of type
Reference on Questionnaire that points at a QuestionnaireResponse
holding the correct answers, but invites SDC to settle on a normative
shape. The use case is academic / quiz-style instruments (e.g., a
multiple-choice anatomy question with one objectively correct option),
and the underlying Zulip discussion broadens the scope to include
free-text answers, partial credit, scoring/weighting, and per-answer
feedback. There are no linked or cross-referenced Jira tickets and no
related GitHub items; the entire prior art lives in the SDC chat thread
and in committee notes captured on this ticket.

A previously-agreed (then reverted) resolution from the SDC work group
already specified the concrete shape: a `questionnaire-answerKey`
extension (Reference(QuestionnaireResponse), context Questionnaire) on
the Advanced Behavior Questionnaire profile, a companion
`questionnaire-item-answerAssessment` markdown extension on
QuestionnaireResponse.item, and a new "Assessing
QuestionnaireResponses" section in the SDC QR profile notes. That
disposition was withdrawn in March 2026 and the ticket re-opened
post-STU4 because the work group "will not have a chance to test this
before our next release." The ticket is therefore back on the table
with a well-developed proposal already on record.

## Related Zulip Discussions

### [questionnaire > Capturing right or wrong answers in a questionnaire](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/questionnaire/topic/Capturing%20right%20or%20wrong%20answers%20in%20a%20questionnaire)

Nina Haffer opened the thread (2023-03-21) asking whether right/wrong
answers for multiple-choice items belong on `answerOption` (e.g., via
extension) or outside the Questionnaire. Lloyd McKenzie immediately
flagged it as a reasonable candidate for a new standard extension and
invited a change request. Brian Postlethwaite raised an integrity
concern — embedding the correct answer in the Questionnaire makes
"cheating" easy if the definition is exposed — and proposed instead a
reference from the Questionnaire to a separate QuestionnaireResponse
containing the answer key. Paul Lynch, Lloyd McKenzie, and Ilya Beda
agreed the QR-as-answer-key approach is preferable because it works
across all answer types (not just choice), lets long-text answers
enumerate key points with marks, and lets normal access controls
protect the answer set. Brian later expanded the design space to
include per-answer feedback markdown, alternate-but-acceptable answers,
weighted scoring, and FHIRPath-based scoring/feedback expressions, and
suggested these likely live alongside the AnswerKey QR or in a derived
Questionnaire. Josh Mandel (relayed by Brian) noted the design has
not specified where an actual graded assessment result and its score
would land; Paul Lynch noted that piece was not in the original
request and would need explicit demand before being defined. No
dissent emerged on the core "reference a QR as the answer key" idea;
debate centered on how much surrounding scoring/feedback machinery
should ship at the same time.

Participants: Nina Haffer, Lloyd McKenzie, Brian Postlethwaite, Paul
Lynch, Ilya Beda, Julian Sass, Josh Mandel (relayed).

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the SDC work group's previously-agreed three-part resolution
(captured in the 2024-11-28 comment) as the normative response, in the
SDC IG:

1. Define a standard extension
   `http://hl7.org/fhir/uv/sdc/StructureDefinition/questionnaire-answerKey`
   — `0..1 Reference(QuestionnaireResponse)`, context `Questionnaire`
   — and apply it to the Advanced Behavior Questionnaire profile. The
   referenced QR SHALL set `QuestionnaireResponse.questionnaire` to
   the referencing Questionnaire's canonical (enforced via invariant
   on the extension). Include implementer guidance that the answer-key
   QR is typically access-restricted to protect instrument integrity.
2. Define a standard extension
   `http://hl7.org/fhir/uv/sdc/StructureDefinition/sdc-questionnaire-item-answerAssessment`
   — `0..1 markdown`, context `QuestionnaireResponse.item` — and add
   it (non-must-support) to the SDC QuestionnaireResponse profile, for
   free-text per-item analysis/feedback, including correct-vs-incorrect
   rationale.
3. Add a new "Assessing QuestionnaireResponses" section to the SDC
   QuestionnaireResponse profile notes covering scoring and weighting
   (including hiding weights/scores in derived forms), defining answer
   keys, use of `answerAssessment`, and automated assessment.

#### Justification

This is the design the work group has already converged on after
multi-year discussion (Zulip 2023 → SDC call 2024-10-03 → formal
proposal 2024-11-28), with a 4-0-0 vote in favor of the substance.
The QR-as-answer-key shape was endorsed by Postlethwaite, McKenzie,
Lynch, and Beda specifically because it (a) generalizes beyond choice
items to long text and other answer types, (b) lets ordinary FHIR
access controls protect instrument integrity rather than inventing a
new mechanism, and (c) reuses the existing Q/QR validation pairing
(`QuestionnaireResponse.questionnaire`) instead of bolting "correct"
markers onto `Questionnaire.item.answerOption`. The accompanying
`answerAssessment` markdown extension and the Assessing
QuestionnaireResponses notes section close the loop by giving
implementers a place to record per-answer rationale and a single
narrative on how scoring, weighting, and answer keys interact. The
only reason this was withdrawn was schedule ("will not have a chance
to test this before our next release"), which is explicitly resolved
by re-opening post-STU4 publication.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Ship a smaller, additive subset now and defer the broader assessment
machinery:

- Define `questionnaire-answerKey` exactly as in Disposition A
  (Reference(QuestionnaireResponse) on Questionnaire, with the QR
  pointing back via `QuestionnaireResponse.questionnaire`), and add it
  to the Advanced Behavior Questionnaire profile.
- **Do not** define `questionnaire-item-answerAssessment` in this
  cycle. Instead, document in the SDC QR profile notes that
  implementers may use existing `QuestionnaireResponse.item.answer`
  with text/coding plus standard `note`/`comment` patterns for
  rationale, and signal that a future cycle will revisit per-item
  assessment annotations once concrete implementer feedback exists.
- Add a short "Defining answer keys" subsection to the SDC QR profile
  notes (rather than the full "Assessing QuestionnaireResponses"
  section), covering only the `answerKey` extension, the back-pointer
  invariant, and the access-control caution.
- Track the broader scoring/weighting/feedback work
  (alternate-but-acceptable answers, FHIRPath scoring expressions,
  graded result location, per-answer markdown feedback) in a new
  follow-on ticket so it can be designed against actual assessment
  implementations rather than speculatively.

#### Justification

The Zulip thread shows the work group is confident about the
`answerKey` reference design but still actively brainstorming the
surrounding pieces — alternate answers, weighted scoring, FHIRPath
expressions, where the "graded result" lives (Josh Mandel's open
question, which Paul Lynch flagged as out of original scope). Brian
Postlethwaite's 2024-09-12 design sketch (alternateAnswerValue,
calculatedScore, additionalFeedback, etc.) is intentionally
exploratory. Shipping only the well-settled piece (the answer-key
reference) closes the original request, gets implementers something
testable, and avoids locking in `answerAssessment` semantics before
the broader assessment story is worked out. It is also the most
defensible response to the schedule concern that drove the original
withdrawal: a single small extension can be balloted and tested in one
cycle with low risk.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request as out of scope for SDC core, and instead direct
implementers to define their own project-specific extension(s) for
answer keys until a richer cross-jurisdictional use case for academic
/ exam-style questionnaires materializes. Close the ticket as
"Persuasive with Mod" pointing to existing extensibility (any
implementer can add a `Reference(QuestionnaireResponse)` extension on
Questionnaire today without IG changes) and reaffirm the SDC focus on
clinical data capture.

#### Justification

The reporter's underlying use case (multiple-choice educational
quizzes) is at the edge of SDC's clinical-data-capture remit, and the
demand visible in the discussion is concentrated among a small group
of contributors (Postlethwaite, McKenzie, Lynch, Beda, and the
reporter). FHIR already permits arbitrary reference extensions on
Questionnaire, so nothing in the platform blocks the reporter from
implementing the proposed pattern today. Standardizing the extension
also opens governance questions the work group has not fully resolved
(answer-key access control, where graded results live, how scoring
expressions interact with derived Questionnaires) and risks shipping
an under-designed extension that has to be revised. A "decline now,
revisit when there is an external IG that needs it" posture preserves
optionality.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The work group has already done the design work, ratified the shape
(4-0-0), and the only reason the previous resolution was reverted is
release-cycle timing — a constraint that has now passed (re-open
post-STU4). Disposition A simply enacts the agreed plan and adds it
to SDC where it belongs (Advanced Behavior Questionnaire profile is
the explicitly-named home). Disposition B is the obvious safety
valve, and is reasonable if the work group is uneasy about
`answerAssessment` markdown semantics or the Assessing-QR notes
content; the recommended path is to ship A but treat the
`answerAssessment` extension and the notes section as the
deliberately-scoped pieces that should attract the most ballot
review. Disposition C understates community interest — the Zulip
thread (24 messages over 18 months, multiple committers) and Brian
Postlethwaite's worked examples on forms-lab show real implementer
energy — and would force every adopter to roll their own incompatible
extension, exactly the situation standard extensions exist to
prevent.
