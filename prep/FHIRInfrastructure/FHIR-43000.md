## Summary

Eric Haas requests an editorial / terminology change in the SMART on FHIR
specification: replace the heading wording "Finer-grained resource constraints
using search parameters" with "Granular Scopes using search parameters". The
request applies to two locations in the SMART scopes documentation that the
reporter identified via screenshots in the ticket. No normative behaviour, scope
syntax, or conformance requirement is being changed — the goal is to align the
prose with the term "granular scopes" that the community has adopted in
practice (and that other parts of the spec already use). There are no cross-
referenced Jira tickets, GitHub items, or Zulip threads tied directly to this
ticket in Augury.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the editorial change exactly as requested in the SMART App Launch IG
authoring source: rename the section heading "Finer-grained resource
constraints using search parameters" to "Granular Scopes using search
parameters" at both occurrences identified in the screenshots
(`scopes-and-launch-context.md` in the SMART App Launch IG, and any anchor /
table-of-contents entry that mirrors the heading). Update internal anchor
links so existing deep links continue to resolve, either by adding an explicit
`{#finer-grained-resource-constraints-using-search-parameters}` legacy anchor
on the renamed heading or by adding redirects, so external implementer
documentation that points at the old anchor does not break.

#### Justification

The community already uses "granular scopes" as the term-of-art for the v2
scope mechanism, which is reflected in the long-running `#smart > Granular
scopes` topic and in other normative sections of the SMART App Launch IG.
Aligning the section headings with that term improves discoverability and
internal consistency without changing any normative requirement. The change is
purely editorial: scope syntax, semantics, and conformance language are
untouched, so risk to implementers is limited to anchor link breakage, which
the proposal mitigates.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt "Granular Scopes" as the headline term but keep "finer-grained" as
clarifying prose. Concretely, rename the heading to
"Granular Scopes using search parameters (finer-grained resource
constraints)" — or open the section with a one-sentence lead such as "Granular
scopes (also called finer-grained resource constraints) let clients narrow
access using search parameters." Preserve the original anchor IDs verbatim.
Optionally extend the change to the rest of the IG so every reference to
"finer-grained scopes" is reframed as "granular scopes" with a single
parenthetical explanation on first use.

#### Justification

This delivers the reporter's underlying goal — making "granular scopes" the
primary term — while protecting existing implementer bookmarks, training
material, and search-engine results that point at the "finer-grained" wording.
A short parenthetical also helps readers who learned the older term map it to
the new one, and avoids splitting the community vocabulary across spec
versions. The trade-off vs. Disposition A is a slightly longer heading and
modestly more editing surface area, in exchange for fewer downstream
disruptions.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline as a no-op editorial change: leave the heading "Finer-grained
resource constraints using search parameters" in place. Mark the ticket
"Persuasive with Mod" only if a different concrete improvement is identified;
otherwise resolve as "Not Persuasive" with a note that "finer-grained" is
descriptive (it explains *what* the mechanism does) while "granular" is the
informal nickname, and that the prose is acceptable as-is.

#### Justification

The change is purely cosmetic and does not affect interoperability,
conformance, or implementation. SMART App Launch is a widely-implemented IG,
and even editorial heading changes can ripple through training materials,
dashboards, and links. If the workgroup is conservative about editorial churn
in a stable IG, declining (or deferring to a larger terminology pass) is a
defensible position.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The reporter's intent — making "granular scopes" the primary term in the
section heading — is well-founded and matches community usage. However, simply
overwriting the heading (Disposition A) silently breaks deep links and
mismatches the rest of the IG's prose, and ignoring the request entirely
(Disposition C) leaves a real inconsistency between informal usage and the
spec. Disposition B captures both terms in the heading (or in an opening
sentence) and explicitly preserves the existing anchor, which gets the
discoverability win without the breakage. It also opens the door to a small
follow-up pass to make "granular scopes" the consistent term across the IG,
which is the change the community already acts as if has happened.
