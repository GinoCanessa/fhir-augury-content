## Summary

The reporter (John Moehrke) asks FHIR Infrastructure to clarify the
distinction between `CapabilityStatement.rest.resource.profile` and
`CapabilityStatement.rest.resource.supportedProfile`, including what each
element means in a *Requirements* CapabilityStatement vs. an
*Implementation/Instance* CapabilityStatement, and how those meanings flow
to clients vs. servers. The triggering Zulip thread on
`fhir/infrastructure-wg` made it clear (via Grahame Grieve) that `.profile`
is a strict, exclusionary statement — instances at that endpoint must
conform and non-conformant resources must not be returned — while
`.supportedProfile` is a non-exclusive "some resources conform to this
profile" statement. The reporter argues that this strict reading is not
obvious from the current normative wording (especially the verb
"applied"), that the client-vs-server applicability is not stated, and
that the rules for an Instance CS that claims conformance to a
Requirements CS (including the case of multiple, conflicting Requirements
CSs) are unstated. He notes that several IGs converted from STU3 (citing
IHE MHD as his own example) likely placed profiles in `.profile` without
realising the strict semantic. The ask is therefore documentation-level:
tighten the element definitions, the `profiling.html` "profile uses"
guidance, and the Requirements-vs-Instance/Server-vs-Client narrative so
implementers reach the same understanding without having to read the
chat.

## Related Zulip Discussions

### [fhir/infrastructure-wg > CapabilityStatement resource.profile meaning](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/fhir%2Finfrastructure-wg/topic/CapabilityStatement%20resource.profile%20meaning)

The thread (16 December 2025) is the trigger for this ticket. John
Moehrke opens by asking whether a *Requirements* CapabilityStatement that
lists a `resource.profile` means non-conforming resources must not be
returned, and whether that semantic also applies to an *Instance* CS or
to clients. Cooper Thompson pushes back on the operational realism of a
strict reading (real systems carry non-conformant historical data and
would need separate endpoints), while John keeps the discussion focused
on what the elements actually *mean*, especially across the
Requirements-vs-Instance and client-vs-server axes. Grahame Grieve
answers that `.profile` *is* exclusionary — "is applied" means resources
at that endpoint must conform — and that the Requirements CS author is
the developer/operator/purchaser who can make that commitment. John
accepts the clarification ("lesson learned") and confirms he will file a
ticket (which becomes FHIR-53641). On `.supportedProfile`, Grahame first
says it "is not a looser version of profile" and then walks that back,
pointing at `profiling.html#profile-uses`, the
`capabilitystatement-expectation`, `profile-mapping`, and
`capabilitystatement-declared-profile` extensions for finer-grained
statements. Lloyd McKenzie observes that IG requirements set a *floor*,
not a ceiling. Paul Church, like John, ends the thread still finding
`.supportedProfile`'s meaning ("some resources conform … but not
preferred, not mandatory") elusive. The thread concludes with John
restating his real ask — a Postel's-Law-style way to declare intended
conformance without exclusionary semantics — and Grahame's invitation to
"make a task". No consensus on wording was reached; only consensus that
the current wording leaves implementers (including the Infrastructure WG
itself) reading the same words differently.

Participants: John Moehrke (reporter), Grahame Grieve, Cooper Thompson,
Lloyd McKenzie, Paul Church.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Tighten the element definitions and accompanying narrative for
`CapabilityStatement.rest.resource.profile` and
`CapabilityStatement.rest.resource.supportedProfile` in the FHIR Core
spec (in `HL7/fhir`, on the CapabilityStatement source pages — i.e. the
StructureDefinition's element definitions, the per-element rendered
description, and `profiling.html` "Uses of Profiles" /
`#profile-uses`). Specifically:

1. Replace the verb "is applied" in `resource.profile` with explicit
   conformance language, e.g. "Resources of this type returned by this
   endpoint, or accepted by this endpoint, **SHALL** validate
   successfully against this profile. A server **SHALL NOT** return a
   resource of this type that does not conform; a client **SHOULD**
   reject a resource of this type that does not conform."
2. Add an explicit "Requirements vs. Instance CapabilityStatement"
   subsection that states, for each of `.profile` and
   `.supportedProfile`:
   - what the element asserts in a Requirements (capability) CS;
   - what an Instance CS that claims conformance to a Requirements CS
     inherits / may not weaken;
   - the client-side reading vs. the server-side reading.
3. Define `.supportedProfile` as "Some resources of this type returned
   or accepted at this endpoint conform to this profile; other
   resources of this type may exist that do not. This element is
   non-exclusionary." Cross-link
   `capabilitystatement-expectation`, `profile-mapping`, and
   `capabilitystatement-declared-profile` so implementers can find the
   stricter / finer-grained mechanisms.
4. Add a worked example contrasting a Requirements CS that pins a
   profile in `.profile` (strict, exclusionary) with one that lists the
   same profile in `.supportedProfile` (non-exclusionary, mixed
   population).

No structural change is made; this is a definition / narrative change
only.

#### Justification

The Zulip thread shows three Infrastructure-WG-active people (John
Moehrke, Cooper Thompson, Paul Church) reading the existing wording
differently from the design intent that Grahame Grieve articulated,
which is direct evidence that the spec text fails its own clarity
contract. The fix is small, additive, and backwards compatible: no
resource cardinality, type, binding, or invariant changes — just
definition text and an example. It also resolves the migration-pain
point John raised (STU3 → R4/R5 IGs that placed profiles under
`.profile` without realising the exclusionary semantic) by making the
distinction visible at element-definition reading time, not only via
chat archaeology.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt a layered approach that keeps the element definitions roughly as
they are but introduces an explicit, machine-readable "expectation"
hook rather than relying on prose:

1. Make the existing `capabilitystatement-expectation` extension a
   first-class, recommended companion to *both* `.profile` and
   `.supportedProfile`, and document the canonical pairings:
   - `.profile` + `expectation = SHALL` ⇒ exclusionary (the current
     intent of bare `.profile`);
   - `.supportedProfile` + `expectation = MAY` ⇒ "some resources
     conform";
   - `.supportedProfile` + `expectation = SHOULD` ⇒ "preferred but not
     enforced" (the Postel's-Law slot John was looking for).
2. In the CapabilityStatement narrative, state that bare `.profile`
   without the extension defaults to `SHALL` for backward compatibility
   and that bare `.supportedProfile` without the extension defaults to
   `MAY`.
3. Lightly reword the element definitions to point at the extension
   instead of trying to fully capture every Requirements/Instance and
   client/server permutation in element prose. Add the same worked
   example as in Disposition A but with the extension applied.
4. Add a section on multi-Requirements-CS conformance, stating that
   when an Instance CS claims conformance to multiple Requirements CSs
   the *most restrictive* expectation per element wins, with an
   explicit note that this is consistent with the existing
   "expectations on top of base FHIR" model.

#### Justification

This addresses the reporter's underlying need (a Postel's-Law-friendly
declaration mechanism) without re-litigating the meaning of `.profile`,
which Grahame considers settled. It also avoids the risk in
Disposition A of writing dense normative prose that future readers
again interpret unevenly. The trade-off is that it puts more weight on
an extension authors don't always reach for, and it requires IG
authors to act (apply the extension) to capture the looser semantic
they may already assume. It is still backwards compatible — defaults
preserve current behavior — and is consistent with how
`capabilitystatement-expectation` is already used at the
interaction/searchParam level in IGs such as US Core.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve the ticket as **Not Persuasive** with the rationale that the
existing element definitions, combined with the
`profiling.html#profile-uses` page, the
`capabilitystatement-expectation` extension, and the
`profile-mapping` / `capabilitystatement-declared-profile`
extensions, already cover the distinction; the ambiguity reported is
addressable by reading those pages together rather than by changing
the spec.

#### Justification

Defensible but weak. Grahame's position in the thread is that the
definition of `.profile` is "clear, btw", which would support a
Not-Persuasive close. However, the fact that the Infrastructure WG
itself disagreed *in real time* on the meaning, and that the reporter
is willing to attribute IHE-MHD non-conformance to the unclear wording,
is hard evidence against pure declination. Declining without at least
a small narrative tweak risks the same ticket being filed again from a
different IG.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is fundamentally a clarity bug, not a design bug. Grahame
already states the intended semantic and points at the supporting
extensions; the cheapest, most durable fix is to capture that
statement in the normative element definitions and in
`profiling.html` so future readers don't have to find a 16-Dec-2025
Zulip thread to know what `.profile` excludes. Disposition B is
attractive but it shifts work to IG authors and depends on adoption of
an extension that is not currently universal; it would be a reasonable
*follow-on* once the prose is clear, but not a substitute for it.
Disposition C is hard to defend given the on-channel disagreement
between three WG-active commenters and the design author. Recommend
Accept-As-Requested, scoped strictly to the CapabilityStatement
element definitions, the rendered `resource.profile` /
`resource.supportedProfile` description, and the
`profiling.html#profile-uses` section, with a worked example and
explicit Requirements-vs-Instance and client-vs-server statements.
