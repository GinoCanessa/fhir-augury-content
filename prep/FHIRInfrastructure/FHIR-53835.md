## Summary

Eric Haas requests a rewrite of the introductory section of the FHIR Core
`signatures.html` page (currently titled "Digital Signature"). The ticket
argues that the existing page (a) is mis-titled — it is referenced
elsewhere as "Signatures" but is scoped almost entirely to digital
signatures; (b) uses the term "electronic signatures" incorrectly; (c)
omits any documentation of non-digital electronic signatures (typed
names, scanned handwritten signatures, click-to-attest checkboxes); and
(d) covers only JWS and XMLDSig as digital-signature methods, ignoring
the widely deployed CMS (RFC 5652) family.

The ticket proposes a concrete replacement introduction. The rewritten
section would (1) re-title the page "Signatures"; (2) introduce the
`Signature` datatype and its uses in `Bundle`, `Contract`, `Provenance`
and the `questionnaireresponse-signature` extension; (3) define
electronic signatures as a superset that includes digital signatures,
calling out that the spec carries the data structures for non-digital
forms but leaves verification to local policy; (4) give worked examples
of non-digital signatures (typed name, scanned image, checkbox/audit
trail) showing which `Signature.*` elements are populated in each case;
(5) keep a Digital Signature subsection that names CMS, JWS and
XMLDSig together; and (6) summarise the canonicalisation / Bundle /
Provenance signing guidance that follows on the rest of the page. Full
proposed text is reproduced verbatim under **Details** below.

## Details

**Description:**

> 1. The Signature page is titled "Digital Signature" and focuses
>    almost entirely on digital signatures. However, the page is
>    referenced as "Signatures" and briefly documents other kinds of
>    electronic signatures.
> 2. The term "electronic signatures" is used incorrectly.
> 3. Documentation on non-digital electronic signatures is absent.
> 4. Focus on JWS and XML, ignoring the widely used CMS.
>
> To correct these issues, I propose rewriting the introductory
> section to:
>
> 1. Title the page "Signatures"
> 2. Create an introduction that covers all types of Electronic
>    signatures
> 3. Use the term "electronic signature" correctly.
> 4. Include a section on non-digital electronic signatures in FHIR
> 5. Include a section on digital signatures
> 6. Add CMS into the conversation
> 7. Summarize the sections that follow.
>
> Proposed text (diff to original is available at:
> https://github.com/Healthedata1/fhir/edit/master/source/signatures.html ):
>
> **# Signatures**
>
> FHIR Support signature functionality through the `Signature`
> datatype; the signature element is used in `Bundle`, `Contract`, and
> `Provenance`, and in the `QuestionnaireResponse Signature` extension.
>
> **## Electronic Signatures:**
>
> Electronic signatures range from simply typing a name or inserting an
> image of a handwritten signature to applying a digital signature that
> uses cryptographic proof to show the content has not changed. All
> digital signatures are electronic signatures, but not all electronic
> signatures are digital signatures. This specification provides the
> data structures for non-digital signatures but leaves implementation
> details and verification processes to local policy. The use of FHIR
> digital signatures is discussed in the following sections.
>
> Examples of FHIR non-digital signatures (omitting other Signature
> datatype fields for brevity):
>
> 1. Hand-typed name
>    - `Signature.data`: The base64-encoded hand-typed name
>    - `Signature.sigFormat`: the mime type for the image file (e.g, text/plain)
> 2. Scanned image of handwritten signature
>    - `Signature.data`: The base64-encoded JPG of the image.
>    - `Signature.sigFormat`: the mime type for the image file (e.g, image/jpg)
> 3. Checking a box: The "proof" is really the audit trail showing a
>    specific authenticated user clicked a specific checkbox at a
>    specific time. Therefore, the `Signature.type`, `Signature.who`,
>    and `Signature.when` are the critical elements, and
>    `Signature.data` and `Signature.sigFormat` are not used.
>
> **## Digital Signature**
>
> FHIR Resources are often parts of a Medical Record or are
> communicated as part of formal Medical Documentation. Digital
> signatures are sometimes needed to cryptographically bind a
> signature so that the receiving or consuming actor can verify
> authenticity, integrity, and rely on non-repudiation of the FHIR
> content.
>
> There are several digital signature methods, including
> [CMS](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc5652),
> [JWS](https://tools.ietf.org/html/rfc7515), and
> [XMLDSig](https://www.w3.org/TR/xmldsig-core/), each with many
> options. To provide implementations with the capabilities to support
> the set of local policies, implementation guides are required to
> specify how signatures should be created when authoring FHIR content
> and what actions should be taken to establish full verification when
> checking signatures. The goal of this section is to provide
> implementations with the capabilities needed to make the actual
> signature verification process interoperable.
>
> This specification defines FHIR canonicalization methods and how to
> identify them. It documents rules and guidelines for using the
> Signature data type for JWS, XML, and CMS signature methods and
> their profiles. It provides guidance for signing FHIR Bundles using
> the Provenance resource.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt Eric Haas's proposed text essentially verbatim into
`HL7/fhir : source/signatures.html`. Concretely:

1. Change the top-level `<h1>` from "Digital Signature" to
   "Signatures" (preserving or relocating the existing
   `<a name="DigitalSignature">` anchor inside the new "Digital
   Signature" `<h2>` so inbound links keep working).
2. Insert a new `<h2>Electronic Signatures:</h2>` subsection
   immediately after the page intro paragraph, with the three worked
   non-digital examples (typed name, scanned image, checkbox/audit
   trail) and the explicit statement that all digital signatures are
   electronic signatures but not vice-versa.
3. Rewrite the existing intro paragraph to list all three digital
   signature methods together — **CMS (RFC 5652)**, **JWS (RFC 7515)**
   and **XMLDSig** — and rewrap the existing "Digital Signature" `<h2>`
   subsection so it no longer reads as the whole page.
4. Add a short summary paragraph at the end of the introduction that
   names the major sections that follow on the page (canonicalisation,
   Signature datatype rules per method, Bundle/Provenance guidance).
5. Fix the broken `file+.vscode-resource.vscode-cdn.net/...` links in
   the proposed text to point at `datatypes.html#Signature`,
   `bundle.html`, `contract.html`, `provenance.html` on the spec
   itself (these are local-IDE artefacts in the reporter's draft).

#### Justification

The proposal is a pure documentation correction. The page is
demonstrably mis-titled (it is referenced as "Signatures" elsewhere)
and the omission of CMS is a real interoperability blind spot — CMS is
the dominant signature container in healthcare exchange (eHN, eIDAS,
PAdES, S/MIME, DICOM-signed objects). The non-digital examples (typed
name, scanned image, checkbox) reflect how `Signature` is actually used
in practice, where the description of the datatype already permits but
does not illustrate them. The repo briefing confirms the change is a
single-file prose edit in `source/`, with no generator coordination,
no cross-repo work, and no resource/profile change — i.e. a low-risk,
high-clarity edit. It also dovetails with Grahame Grieve's parallel
rewrite on the `2025-07-gg-digital-signatures1` branch (per the S&P
Zulip threads) and with the FHIR-I push toward Normative maturity for
Digital Signatures.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the *intent* but reconcile against Grahame Grieve's existing
rewrite branch
(`2025-07-gg-digital-signatures1/signatures.html`) before merging,
producing a single combined rewrite. Specifically:

1. Treat FHIR-53835 as the requirement spec for the introductory
   section and Grahame's branch as the implementation in flight.
2. Edit `HL7/fhir : source/signatures.html` to (a) take Eric's title
   change, (b) take Eric's "Electronic Signatures" subsection and
   non-digital examples, and (c) take Grahame's worked
   JWS/canonicalisation examples and embedded-signature framing for
   the "Digital Signature" subsection.
3. Coordinate with the open S&P Zulip threads ("Provenance Signature"
   and "Signature") so that the rewritten page is consistent with the
   in-flight Provenance-vs-Bundle.signature design (e.g. whether
   `Provenance.signature` becomes a backbone of `sigFormat` /
   `targetFormat` / `data`, whether `Bundle.signature` is deprecated
   in favour of Provenance) — at minimum, the introduction must not
   contradict whichever direction is chosen.
4. Sequence: defer the rewrite until the S&P thread reaches a working
   conclusion, then land both changes together so implementers see one
   coherent page rather than two competing rewrites in successive
   builds.

#### Justification

A documentation-only PR that lands while a structurally different
rewrite is in a parallel branch produces churn and risks contradicting
itself within one ballot cycle. The Augury Zulip evidence shows that
both work streams exist simultaneously and that key questions
(Provenance vs Bundle.signature, JCS canonicalisation rule for arrays,
backbone-ifying `Provenance.signature`) are still open with the
reporter (Eric Haas) and Grahame Grieve actively engaged. Folding the
two rewrites together preserves the narrative gains of FHIR-53835
(title, electronic-signature framing, CMS) while avoiding a
two-step land/relandcycle and keeping the introduction aligned with
whatever signing model the workgroup converges on.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline to make the proposed introductory rewrite as a standalone
change, on the grounds that (a) the substantive scope of
`source/signatures.html` is in active redesign in the
`2025-07-gg-digital-signatures1` branch and the open S&P threads, and
(b) once that redesign lands, much of the introduction will need to
be rewritten again in any case. Close as "Persuasive with Mod —
defer" and ask the reporter to re-file after the Provenance/Bundle
signing direction is settled, scoped to whatever introduction is
needed at that time.

#### Justification

This is a defensible position only if the workgroup believes the
substantive signing redesign is imminent and would obsolete this
introduction. It would avoid a ballot-cycle worth of churn. It is
weak on its merits, however: the page is mis-titled and omits CMS
*today*, those defects are independent of any future Provenance
redesign, and implementers consulting the page in the meantime are
actively misled. There is no breaking-change argument to make here
(no normative content is altered) and no "already addressed" argument
(Grahame's branch addresses worked examples, not the page title or
the electronic-vs-digital framing).

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The defects FHIR-53835 identifies are real and the proposed text is
substantively good — Disposition A would be acceptable on its own
terms. But the Augury evidence is unambiguous that a parallel rewrite
of the same file is already in flight on Grahame Grieve's
`2025-07-gg-digital-signatures1` branch, with the same workgroup, the
same reporter participating, and a related set of open S&P questions
about Provenance-vs-Bundle.signature. Landing FHIR-53835 in isolation
risks an immediate re-rewrite when the Provenance work merges, and
risks an introductory section that contradicts the worked examples
later on the same page. The pragmatic path is to take Eric's intent
(rename to "Signatures", add the electronic-signatures framing, add
CMS, add the section summary) as the requirements for the
introduction, integrate them into Grahame's branch, and land a single
coherent rewrite. This keeps the documentation gains, avoids churn,
and uses the open Zulip discussions to lock the framing before
publication. If the workgroup believes the Provenance redesign is far
enough off that waiting would leave the misleading current page in
place for another full ballot cycle, fall back to Disposition A and
accept the short-term churn.
