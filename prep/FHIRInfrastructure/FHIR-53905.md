## Summary

The reporter asks the FHIR Infrastructure work group to clarify the FHIR
Documents page's guidance on which referenced resources SHOULD be bundled
into a document. The current text says that referenced resources SHOULD be
included "to keep the integrity and wholeness of the document," which the
reporter reads as effectively requiring transitive inclusion of every
reference. They propose softening the guidance to apply only to references
that are *relevant* to the document — for example, references that are
needed to fully understand the parent resource (cited examples
`MedicationStatement.medication` and `DiagnosticReport.result`). The
ticket also explains why a constructor should not simply strip irrelevant
reference fields: doing so would diverge from the resource's normal REST
representation, can break Provenance / digital-signature integrity when
documents are assembled from multiple servers, and may violate
profile/resource conformance (e.g., mandatory elements). No prior Jira
tickets or Zulip threads were surfaced for this issue, and there are no
linked GitHub PRs/issues. A single comment from corey_spears notes the
ticket was reassigned to FHIR-I because the Documents paradigm is owned
by that work group.

## Details

**Description:**

> Currently the Documents page says:
>
> > When these resources reference other resources, the referenced
> > resources SHOULD also be included in the bundle. Omitting referenced
> > resources could compromise the integrity and wholeness of the
> > document, especially when considering that documents have legal
> > retention periods that may be longer than the life of the FHIR
> > server where the reference(s) point to.
>
> In my opinion, the first sentence should be clarified to say
>
> > When these resources reference other resources, the referenced
> > resources SHOULD also be included in the Bundle **whenever those
> > referenced resources are relevant to the document.**
>
> (or similar phrasing)
>
> Referenced resources definitely SHOULD be included in any situation
> where they're needed to fully understand the parent resource
> (`MedicationStatement.medication`, `DiagnosticReport.result`, etc.).
> However, document constructors certainly shouldn't include *all*
> referenced resources (especially not recursively!) if the referenced
> resources are not relevant to the FHIR document, to keep the document
> size reasonable.
>
> There are also various reasons why the document constructor shouldn't
> just *remove* the reference fields they don't intend to include, such
> as:
> - Keeping a consistent resource representation in the document vs.
>   other workflows (like REST)
> - Maintaining provenance and/or digital signature validity (if
>   constructing a document from multiple source FHIR servers)
> - Maintaining resource or profile conformance (e.g. mandatory fields,
>   even if not relevant to this specific document)

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the FHIR Core Documents page so that the sentence currently
reading

> "When these resources reference other resources, the referenced
> resources SHOULD also be included in the bundle."

is reworded along the lines of

> "When these resources reference other resources, the referenced
> resources SHOULD also be included in the Bundle whenever those
> referenced resources are relevant to the document."

Retain the existing rationale paragraph about integrity, wholeness, and
retention. Add a short normative-style clarification immediately after
that:

1. Constructors SHOULD include any referenced resource needed to fully
   understand a parent resource that is itself in the document (give
   `MedicationStatement.medication` and `DiagnosticReport.result` as
   examples).
2. Constructors SHOULD NOT recursively pull in every transitively
   referenced resource purely for completeness.
3. Constructors SHOULD NOT strip the underlying `Reference` element from
   the source resource even when the target is not bundled, citing
   (a) consistency with the same resource served via REST,
   (b) preservation of `Provenance` and any digital-signature integrity
   when the document is assembled from multiple source servers, and
   (c) maintenance of resource/profile conformance (mandatory or
   must-support elements).

#### Justification

This is a low-risk editorial clarification of existing SHOULD-level
guidance, not a normative rule change. It directly addresses a
misreading the reporter encountered in real authoring practice and
aligns the Documents narrative with widely-held implementer behaviour:
documents already in the wild routinely retain `Reference` values
without bundling every target. The added bullets capture the reporter's
three concrete reasons not to delete the references themselves, which
are sound FHIR design points (REST/document parity, Provenance/signature
integrity, profile conformance). Because the ticket is FHIR Core and the
Documents page is owned by FHIR-I (per corey_spears's comment), FHIR-I
can apply the change without cross-WG coordination.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Instead of editing the single sentence, restructure the relevant section
of the Documents page into a short "What to include in a document
Bundle" subsection with three explicit cases:

- **MUST include:** the focal `Composition` and any resource referenced
  from `Composition.section.entry` (existing rule, made explicit here).
- **SHOULD include:** referenced resources that are required to
  understand a contained resource's clinical/business meaning
  (`MedicationStatement.medication`, `DiagnosticReport.result`, similar
  "value-bearing" references).
- **MAY omit:** references that are purely contextual or
  administrative (e.g., `*.recorder`, `*.author` of an unrelated
  Practitioner, references to historical encounters not under
  discussion), provided the `Reference` element itself is preserved on
  the source resource.

Add a callout box repeating the three reasons not to delete the
`Reference` values themselves, and explicitly note that `Provenance`
resources covering bundled content SHOULD also be included when present.

#### Justification

The reporter's wording fix is correct as far as it goes, but the
underlying problem is that the Documents page does not give authors a
concrete decision framework, so different implementers will continue to
draw the line in different places. A short "MUST / SHOULD / MAY"
subsection turns one-line guidance into something testable and
review-able, and explicitly covers the Provenance / signature issue the
reporter raised — which Disposition A only addresses obliquely. The
trade-off is more editing work and a slightly larger footprint on the
Documents page; against that, the change still does not introduce any
new normative constraints on the wire format.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket "Persuasive with Modification – Editorial only" and
decline a substantive change, on the basis that the existing text
already qualifies the inclusion as SHOULD (not SHALL) and explicitly
ties the rationale to "integrity and wholeness of the document," which
is inherently a relevance test. At most, add a one-line non-normative
note pointing readers at the existing definition of "relevant" content
elsewhere in the Documents/Bundle pages.

#### Justification

A SHOULD already permits omission with justification, so a strict
reading of the current text does not require transitive closure of all
references. The reporter's concern is essentially that some readers
*interpret* SHOULD as a near-MUST, but rewording one sentence is
unlikely to fix a reader who already disregards SHOULD semantics, and
adding qualifiers ("whenever … relevant") risks weakening the guidance
for the cases where inclusion really is important (e.g.,
`DiagnosticReport.result`). Declining keeps the page short and avoids
the second-order question of how a constructor proves "relevance."

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition A correctly captures what the reporter asked for, but it
leaves the harder question — *how* a constructor decides which
references are "relevant" — entirely to the implementer, which is the
real source of the inconsistency the ticket is trying to fix.
Disposition C under-weights how often this confusion shows up in
practice; the reporter's three bullets (REST parity, Provenance and
signature integrity, profile conformance) are exactly the sort of
implementer-visible consequences a SHOULD clarification is meant to
head off. Disposition B costs FHIR-I a slightly larger edit to the
Documents page but produces a decision framework reviewers can actually
apply, explicitly preserves the `Reference` element even when the
target is omitted, and folds in the Provenance/signature concern that
the original ticket raised but Disposition A only nods at. It is also
the disposition that travels best into downstream IGs (US Core, IPS,
etc.) that re-state document-assembly rules in their own narrative.
