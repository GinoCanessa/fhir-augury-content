## Summary

The ticket reports that the published FHIR Core specification carries a
"To be developed" placeholder text in place of a working link to
"Conformance Service" documentation. The reporter (Brian Postlethwaite)
asks whether that placeholder is intended to point to the Feature
Capabilities IG, and by implication wants either a real link or the
placeholder removed/clarified. No related Jira tickets, Zulip threads,
or GitHub items were surfaced by the cross-reference index, so the
ticket stands alone — the request is purely an editorial / publication
correction in FHIR Core, not a normative change.

## Proposed Dispositions

> Note: with no GitHub cross-references and no persisted briefing
> loaded, the dispositions below describe the editorial change in
> generic terms. The likely target is the FHIR Core publication source
> (the "Conformance" section of the spec), but the specific authoring
> file should be confirmed against an `HL7/fhir` repo briefing before
> implementation.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat this as an editorial / publication fix in FHIR Core. Locate the
"To be developed" placeholder in the Conformance section of the
specification source, confirm the intended target is the Feature
Capabilities IG, and replace the placeholder with a working link to
that IG (text along the lines of "See the Feature Capabilities IG").
If the IG is not yet at a stable URL, link to its current ci-build /
HL7 publication URL and add a short sentence describing what readers
will find there.

#### Justification

The reporter explicitly identifies the placeholder and proposes the
Feature Capabilities IG as the likely intended target. "To be
developed" labels in a published normative spec are a publication
defect: they imply forthcoming content that never landed and erode
reader trust. Replacing them with a real link is the minimum viable
fix and is consistent with how other cross-references from FHIR Core
into companion IGs are handled.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than silently swapping in a link to the Feature Capabilities
IG, remove the "To be developed" placeholder altogether and replace it
with one of:

1. A short prose paragraph in the Conformance section that explains
   what a "Conformance Service" is intended to mean in FHIR Core, and
   then defers detail to "see also" links for both the Feature
   Capabilities IG and any other relevant IGs (e.g., CapabilityStatement
   tooling, registry IGs).
2. If no concrete target page exists yet, simply delete the dangling
   sentence/section heading so the published spec does not advertise
   missing content.

This requires the authoring WG to confirm scope before linking, rather
than assuming the Feature Capabilities IG is the intended destination.

#### Justification

The reporter asks a question ("Is this meant to be a link to the
feature capabilities IG?") rather than asserting that it is. Linking
to that IG without WG confirmation risks anchoring readers to the
wrong target and is itself hard to undo once it ships in a normative
build. Removing or rewording the placeholder is lower-risk and avoids
implying an editorial commitment that the WG has not actually made.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline as a normative change and reclassify as a documentation
backlog item: leave the placeholder text in place for the current
build, on the basis that "Conformance Service" content is genuinely
intended for a future cycle and the placeholder accurately signals
"not yet written".

#### Justification

If the original authors deliberately inserted "To be developed" to
hold space for future Conformance Service material, then the text is
working as designed and the right fix is to write the content (a
larger work item), not to retarget the link. Declining the editorial
fix in isolation forces the question of who owns the Conformance
Service content and prevents a misleading link to an IG that may not
in fact be the intended home.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The ticket is real (a placeholder in published FHIR Core is a defect),
but the reporter explicitly raises uncertainty about the intended
target. Disposition A acts on an unconfirmed assumption; Disposition C
leaves a known wart in the published spec. Disposition B both removes
the embarrassing "To be developed" string from the published build and
forces FHIR Infrastructure to make a deliberate WG-level decision
about whether the Feature Capabilities IG (or anything else) is the
right destination. Once that decision is made, the resolution can
either land as a "see also" link or as a clean removal — both of which
are small, low-risk publication edits to the Conformance section of
HL7/fhir.
