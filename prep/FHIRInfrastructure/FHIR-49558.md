## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the editorial correction in the `HL7/fhir` repository,
specifically in `source/overview-arch.html`, by replacing the string
"objected oriented" with "object oriented" in the sentence that
currently reads: "FHIR is neither objected oriented in its modeling
approach nor are the components that make up the FHIR specification
UML…". This is a single-character deletion (drop the trailing "ed"
from "objected") with no semantic, structural, or normative impact.
Mark the ticket as Persuasive / Applied once merged.

#### Justification

The ticket is a clear-cut Technical Correction of an unambiguous
typographical error in the FHIR Architecture overview page. The
correction is editorial, has no effect on conformance, validators,
StructureDefinitions, terminologies, or generated artifacts, and
restores the intended idiomatic phrase ("object oriented"). Accepting
exactly as requested is the lowest-risk, highest-clarity outcome and
aligns with how the workgroup routinely handles narrative typos in
the spec sources.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the spirit of the correction but, while the file is open,
perform a small editorial sweep of the same sentence and surrounding
paragraph in `source/overview-arch.html` to catch any adjacent
issues (e.g., consistent hyphenation — "object-oriented" vs.
"object oriented" — and parallel phrasing with "service oriented" if
present nearby). Apply the requested fix plus any minor consistency
adjustments in a single editorial commit, and note the broader sweep
in the disposition comment.

#### Justification

The typo sits in a high-visibility narrative section (Architecture
overview). It is common in editorial passes to find that a
mis-typed compound modifier coexists with inconsistent hyphenation
of the same term elsewhere on the page. Bundling a small consistency
pass into the same commit avoids a second editorial ticket later
and is cheap because the change is purely narrative. The trade-off
versus Disposition A is a slightly larger diff and a marginally
greater review burden; mitigated by scoping the sweep to the
immediate paragraph rather than the whole page.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as Not Persuasive / Not Persuasive with Mod, on the
grounds that the request is purely editorial and could be handled
via a direct PR to `HL7/fhir` without occupying a Jira ticket and
workgroup disposition slot.

#### Justification

This is a defensible position only on process grounds: workgroups
sometimes prefer that pure typo fixes be submitted as PRs against the
spec source rather than tracked as Jira tickets, to keep the ballot
queue focused on substantive issues. However, the ticket is already
filed and triaged, the fix is trivial, and declining would leave the
typo in the published narrative until someone independently
re-discovers it. There is no substantive (technical) reason to
decline.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

This is an unambiguous typographical error in published FHIR Core
narrative content (`source/overview-arch.html`). The correction is a
two-character deletion with zero impact on conformance, tooling, or
generated artifacts, and there is no community discussion or
cross-referenced work suggesting any other interpretation. Disposition
A applies the fix exactly as requested with minimal risk and minimal
reviewer burden. Disposition B is a reasonable optional enhancement if
the editor handling the change notices adjacent inconsistencies, but
should not block acceptance. Disposition C has no technical
justification and would leave a known typo in the spec.
