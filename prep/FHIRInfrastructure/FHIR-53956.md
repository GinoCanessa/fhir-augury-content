## Summary

The ticket asks for an editorial restructuring of the FHIR Core
specification's FHIRPath page (`fhirpath.html`). The reporter (Brian
Postlethwaite) observes that the page's heading hierarchy is uneven:
because the entire page is about FHIR's use of FHIRPath, several
top-level topics have been incorrectly tucked one level too deep under
the "Using FHIRPath with Resources" section. The specific request is to
promote four section headings — *Using FHIR types in expressions* (with
its child *Use of FHIR Quantity*), *FHIR Specific Variables*,
*Additional functions*, and *Environment variables* — so they sit at the
appropriate top level of the page rather than as sub-sections. The
ticket has been triaged as Persuasive with the resolution note
"Promote the heading level of the 4 heading sections as requested." No
related Jira tickets, Zulip discussions, or GitHub items were surfaced
by cross-reference or search.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Edit the FHIRPath page (`fhirpath.html` / its source in the FHIR Core
spec) so that the following four section headings are promoted from
their current sub-section depth (currently nested under *Using
FHIRPath with Resources*) to the top heading level of the page:

1. **Using FHIR types in expressions** — keep *Use of FHIR Quantity*
   as a child of this section.
2. **FHIR Specific Variables**.
3. **Additional functions**.
4. **Environment variables**.

Concretely: bump each of these four `<h3>` (or equivalent) headings
up one level to `<h2>`, leave any deeper children at one less level so
the relative hierarchy under each promoted heading is preserved, and
update the page's table of contents / anchors accordingly. No
normative content changes are required; this is a structural /
editorial change only. Confirm anchor IDs are stable (or add
back-compat anchors) so external links to the existing section URLs
do not break.

#### Justification

This matches the request literally and matches the resolution already
recorded on the ticket ("Promote the heading level of the 4 heading
sections as requested"). The page is wholly about FHIR's use of
FHIRPath, so demoting these topics under "Using FHIRPath with
Resources" is genuinely misleading: a reader scanning the table of
contents will under-weight content that is just as central as the
parent section. Because the change is editorial and produces no
normative differences, it is low-risk and consistent with FHIR's
general practice of fixing spec-page structure between ballots.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Promote the four headings as requested **and** take the opportunity to
do a single light-touch pass on the FHIRPath page's overall outline at
the same time, so the page does not need a second editorial ticket
soon after. Specifically:

- Promote the four headings called out in the ticket (as in
  Disposition A).
- While editing, audit the remaining sub-sections under *Using
  FHIRPath with Resources* and ensure any other section that is really
  a top-level FHIRPath topic (rather than a strict child of "using it
  with resources") is also lifted.
- Refresh the page's intro / table-of-contents so the new top-level
  structure is reflected, and add stable anchor aliases for any
  heading whose generated ID changes.

#### Justification

The reporter's underlying observation is that the page's outline
under-represents several top-level topics; the four named headings are
the most obvious offenders but probably not the only ones. Doing the
audit now avoids a "death by a thousand editorial tickets" pattern on
the same page and gives reviewers one coherent before/after to react
to. The risk is scope creep — the wider audit could surface
disagreements about what counts as a "top-level FHIRPath topic" — so
the audit must stay light-touch and any contentious moves should be
deferred to follow-up tickets rather than slipped into this change.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket without changing the FHIRPath page's heading
structure, on the grounds that the current nesting is intentional
(the page is organised around how FHIRPath is *used with FHIR
resources*, and the four sections in question are presented as facets
of that usage) and that editorial heading shuffles risk breaking
external bookmarks and table-of-contents references for a benefit that
is purely cosmetic.

#### Justification

This is the weakest of the three options. It would only be defensible
if the workgroup believed the existing nesting was deliberately chosen
and signalled something semantically important — for example, that the
four sections genuinely are sub-topics of "using FHIRPath with
resources" rather than independent FHIRPath-on-FHIR topics. The
reporter's argument that "the whole page is FHIR's use of FHIRPath"
makes that defence hard to sustain, and the resolution already on the
ticket marks the change Persuasive, so this disposition is included
for completeness rather than as a serious recommendation.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The request is narrow, editorial, and already recorded as Persuasive
on the ticket with a clear resolution note. The reporter's reasoning
— that the entire FHIRPath page is about FHIR's use of FHIRPath, so
nesting these four topics under "Using FHIRPath with Resources" buries
them — is straightforwardly correct, and the fix is mechanical
(promote four headings and update the table of contents / anchors).
Disposition B is appealing if an editor wants to pick up the page for
a broader pass, but the workgroup should not block this ticket on a
wider audit; if a broader cleanup is desired it can be filed as a
separate ticket. Disposition C is not defensible given the ticket's
existing Persuasive resolution. Implementation should land in the FHIR
Core spec source for the FHIRPath page (`fhirpath.html`), with
attention to preserving anchor stability so existing inbound links
continue to resolve.
