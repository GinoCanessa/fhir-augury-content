## Summary

The ticket reports that version `1.0.0` of the `hl7.fhir.uv.smart-app-launch`
package, as published on the FHIR package registry
(`https://registry.fhir.org/package/hl7.fhir.uv.smart-app-launch|1.0.0`), is
inconsistent with what the SMART-on-FHIR IG history page itself states. The
registry entry advertises the package as targeting **R4**, but the IG history
page (`http://hl7.org/fhir/smart-app-launch/history.html`) lists `1.0.0` as
**STU3 (R3)**, and the actual contents of the package are R3 artifacts. As a
secondary symptom, the package fails to load when consumed as a dependency
(e.g., `StructureDefinition-oauth-uris.json` is referenced but not present in
the published tarball), which surfaced in the linked Zulip thread when an
implementer attempted to depend on it from a downstream IG.

There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets. The single Zulip thread on the
`#implementers` stream is the source of the report; in that thread Grahame
Grieve confirms the package on the registry is broken and "different to the
one on the web site," and that no audit trail exists to explain how it
diverged. The conversation also notes that the SMART-on-FHIR specification
itself is intentionally version-agnostic and that the `oauth-uris` extensions
were deprecated years ago in favor of `.well-known/smart-configuration`.

## Related Zulip Discussions

### [implementers > SMART-on-FHIR package 1.0.0 is STU3 or R4?](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/implementers/topic/SMART-on-FHIR%20package%201.0.0%20is%20STU3%20or%20R4%3F)

Michael van der Zel opened the topic by contrasting the registry entry
(`registry.fhir.org/package/hl7.fhir.uv.smart-app-launch|1.0.0`, which
lists R4) with the SMART-on-FHIR `history.html` page (which lists `1.0.0`
as STU3), asking which is correct. Gino Canessa and Josh Mandel both noted
that the SMART-on-FHIR specification is deliberately version-agnostic
above a minimum FHIR version (DSTU2 and later) and that the `1.0.0`
package was published before R5 existed and contains very little — Josh
questioned whether there were any artifacts that downstream IGs actually
needed from it, and reminded the group that the legacy `oauth-uris`
extensions were deprecated years ago in favor of
`.well-known/smart-configuration`. Michael then tried to consume `1.0.0`
as a dependency in his IG and hit a hard failure
(`FileNotFoundException` for `StructureDefinition-oauth-uris.json` inside
the package tarball), confirming the package is not just mis-labelled but
also internally incomplete. Grahame Grieve then clarified the situation:
the registry copy of `1.0.0` is broken — it claims R4 but contains R3
content — and is "different to the one on the web site," with no audit
trail to explain how it diverged; he asked whether Michael truly needed
to depend on `1.0.0` specifically. Michael's resolution in the thread was
pragmatic — drop the IG dependency and just link to the SMART specs
narratively — but he also asked whether a Jira issue should be filed,
which is what produced FHIR-40721.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat this as a registry / republish problem and fix it at the source.
Concretely:

1. Locate the canonical `1.0.0` tarball for `hl7.fhir.uv.smart-app-launch`
   that matches the published IG at
   `http://hl7.org/fhir/smart-app-launch/1.0.0/` (the STU3 release).
2. Republish that tarball to `registry.fhir.org` under
   `hl7.fhir.uv.smart-app-launch|1.0.0`, ensuring the `package.json`
   manifest declares `"fhirVersions": ["3.0.x"]` (and/or
   `"fhir-version-list"` per the package spec) — i.e., **R3, not R4** —
   and that all referenced files (notably
   `StructureDefinition-oauth-uris.json`) are actually present in the
   tarball under `package/`.
3. Add a redirect or note on the registry entry linking back to the IG
   history page so future readers can verify the FHIR version of `1.0.0`
   in one place.

No change to the SMART-on-FHIR specification text itself; this is a
package-publication correction.

#### Justification

The ticket is filed as a **Technical Correction** and the Zulip thread
makes the diagnosis unambiguous: the registry copy diverges from the
published IG and from its own contents. The smallest, most defensible fix
is to make the published artifact match reality — the IG itself has
always declared `1.0.0` as STU3, the contents are R3, and there is no
R4 variant to preserve. Republishing also resolves the
`FileNotFoundException` Michael hit, because the corrected tarball would
include the file the manifest references. This is also consistent with
how the registry is treated for other historical IG versions, where the
`fhirVersions` field on `package.json` is the source of truth for tooling.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Mark `hl7.fhir.uv.smart-app-launch|1.0.0` as **deprecated/withdrawn** on
the registry rather than republishing it, and document `1.0.0` as
"historical, do not depend on" in the IG history page. Specifically:

1. Set the registry entry for `1.0.0` to a deprecated state (or remove
   it entirely if the registry supports withdrawal), with a pointer to
   the latest STU release and to the version-agnostic guidance in the
   current SMART-on-FHIR IG.
2. Update `http://hl7.org/fhir/smart-app-launch/history.html` to flag
   `1.0.0` as superseded and to note that it should not be used as an IG
   dependency; recommend depending on a current STU release (or no
   dependency at all, given SMART-on-FHIR's version-agnostic posture).
3. Add a short note in the current IG narrative explaining that
   downstream IGs generally do not need to take a hard dependency on the
   smart-app-launch package — they can reference the spec narratively
   and rely on `.well-known/smart-configuration` rather than the
   deprecated `oauth-uris` extensions.

#### Justification

The Zulip discussion repeatedly questions whether `1.0.0` is even useful
as an IG dependency: the package was published before R5 existed, the
spec is intentionally version-agnostic, the only artifacts of substance
are the long-deprecated `oauth-uris` extensions, and the implementer who
filed the ticket ultimately worked around the problem by dropping the
dependency and linking the specs instead. Republishing a six-year-old
broken package risks introducing fresh inconsistencies (hashes, cached
copies in `~/.fhir/packages` already in the wild) for very little real
benefit, whereas formally deprecating it captures the actual community
guidance ("don't depend on this") that came out of the thread.
Compared to Disposition A, this trades a one-time republish for a
permanent signal to tooling and authors that `1.0.0` is not a viable
dependency.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Not Persuasive / Won't Fix**, on the grounds that
`1.0.0` is a long-superseded historical release and that the SMART-on-FHIR
IG already publishes current, correctly-versioned packages that downstream
IGs should be using. Note in the resolution comment that the historical
registry entry is being left as-is and that implementers should depend on
a current STU release of `hl7.fhir.uv.smart-app-launch` instead.

#### Justification

A defensible reading of the thread is that there is no real harm here
that warrants spending Infrastructure / SMART WG cycles: the
SMART-on-FHIR IG is version-agnostic, the `oauth-uris` extensions are
already deprecated, and the implementer who filed the ticket has already
moved on by linking specs narratively. The package is six years old, the
audit trail to figure out how the registry copy diverged is gone (per
Grahame), and no one in the thread is requesting `1.0.0` as a forward
dependency for new work. Declining is consistent with how similarly old
broken artifacts are sometimes left untouched when no current consumer
needs them.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The Zulip thread converges, fairly clearly, on a single conclusion: the
registry copy of `1.0.0` is broken **and** depending on `1.0.0` from a
new IG is not actually a thing the community wants people to do. Pure
republish (A) fixes the cosmetic FHIR-version mismatch but leaves a
trapdoor — an implementer reading only the registry would still
reasonably take a dependency on `1.0.0`, hit the deprecated extensions,
and waste effort. Pure decline (C) ignores that the registry entry
actively misleads tooling about the FHIR version, which is the literal
defect FHIR-40721 raises. Disposition B addresses both the
mis-declaration (by marking the artifact deprecated/withdrawn rather
than letting it sit with the wrong `fhirVersions`) and the underlying
"don't depend on this" guidance that came out of the thread, and it
avoids the risk of introducing yet another `1.0.0` tarball variant into
caches that already hold the broken one. If the workgroup decides
deprecation is not supported by the current registry, fall back to
Disposition A as the minimum acceptable fix.
