## Summary

Isaac Vetter (Epic) requests that the `online_access` scope and the
corresponding `permission-online` SMART capability be removed from the SMART
App Launch specification. The reporter argues that the spec text — "as long
as the end user remains online" — is insufficiently defined and that many
plausible interpretations are architecturally impossible to implement.
He further notes that there is no documented evidence of implementers
actually supporting `online_access` (citing the
[#smart > Refresh token for online access](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/179170-smart/topic/Refresh.20token.20for.20online.20access)
Zulip thread), and that ONC's HTI-1 NPRM was, in his view, attempting to
regulate the capability without understanding its semantics. The single
comment on the ticket (Cooper Thompson) simply points to the same Zulip
thread for context.

A directly related ticket — **[FHIR-43559](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43559)**
("permission-online was excluded from HTI-1 & should be re-evaluated") —
was filed in January 2024 by Michael Briggs and resolved on 2024-01-26 as
**Persuasive with Modification**. Rather than remove the capability,
FHIR Infrastructure agreed to **limit `permission-online` / `online_access`
to EHR Launch contexts**, **mark it experimental**, and add cross-links to
the Scopes negotiation guidance. That resolution was applied on the
[2024-ballot-reconciliation-uab](https://github.com/HL7/smart-app-launch/tree/2024-ballot-reconciliation-uab)
branch of `HL7/smart-app-launch`. FHIR-41320 has not been formally
dispositioned and remains in **Triaged** status; reviewers should decide
whether the FHIR-43559 resolution adequately addresses FHIR-41320 (allowing
this ticket to be closed as a duplicate / "Already addressed") or whether
the WG still wants outright removal.

## Related Jira Tickets

### [FHIR-43559: permission-online was excluded from HTI-1 & should be re-evaluated](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43559)

Filed 2024-01-08 by Michael Briggs (FHIR Infrastructure, SMART on FHIR
spec). Notes that HTI-1 explicitly excluded the `permission-online`
capability and proposes either (a) removing the capability outright or
(b) limiting it to EHR-launch contexts where the EHR can actually
observe the user's session. Resolved 2024-01-26 as **Persuasive with
Modification**: the WG chose option (b) — keep the capability but scope
it to EHR Launch only, mark it experimental, and link it to the Scopes
negotiation guidance. The change was applied on the
`2024-ballot-reconciliation-uab` branch of `HL7/smart-app-launch`. This
is effectively the workgroup's response to the same concern FHIR-41320
raises, six months earlier and more aggressively.

Reporter: MichaelBriggs. Resolver / branch author: jmandel (Josh Mandel).

## Related Zulip Discussions

### [#smart > Refresh token for online access](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/179170-smart/topic/Refresh.20token.20for.20online.20access)

Long-running thread (Dec 2020 – Jan 2024) that the ticket itself cites as
evidence of non-adoption. Sagar Shah opened it asking what concrete
capability `online_access` provides if a client could just use the
authorization-code flow with an existing session cookie. Josh Mandel
clarified that the intent was to keep an app's access alive only while
the user-EHR session is alive (and acknowledged the capability is hard
to implement in highly-decoupled auth servers). Robert Scanlon (Inferno)
confirmed that ONC's Standardized API certification only tests
`offline_access` and treats `online_access` as optional. Jenni Syed
(then Cerner) reported that *all* of Cerner's production provider apps
used `online_access` because they don't need data after the user closes
the app, and warned that handing out long-lived refresh tokens to public
apps that don't need them is a security problem. Isaac Vetter (May 2023)
asked for any other evidence of adoption and got none; Cooper Thompson
echoed the spec-clarity concern ("which actor is the user 'online' with?").
Michele Mottini interpreted it as "online with the EHR" but admitted he
had never tried to implement it. By Jan 2024 Brendan Keeler summarised
the consensus bluntly: "Oh yeah it's a fake scope." No firm consensus on
*what to do* — opinions split between (a) remove it, (b) clarify it,
(c) keep it because Cerner's prior use case was real even if not
broadly adopted today.

Participants: Sagar Shah, Josh Mandel, Robert Scanlon, Jenni Syed, Isaac
Vetter, Hans Buitendijk, Cooper Thompson, Michele Mottini, Alexander
Zautke, Brendan Keeler.

### [#smart > online_access scope with SMART App Launch 2.0.0](https://chat.fhir.org/#narrow/stream/179170-smart/topic/online_access.20scope.20with.20SMART.20App.20Launch.202.2E0.2E0)

Companion thread (Aug 2023 – Feb 2024). Sagar Shah revisits whether
`online_access` adds anything when `offline_access` is also requested
(Josh Mandel: it does not, in practice). The conversation pivots in
Jan 2024 when Alexander Zautke describes a generic FHIR-server SMART
implementation with no notion of an EHR session, making `online_access`
unimplementable on his side. Josh Mandel asks the key design question:
"If we removed the scope, what mechanism would we replace it with?" and
floats clarifying that `online_access` MUST last *at least* as long as
the user session, with the EHR free to choose how long (potentially
treating it as equivalent to `offline_access`). Alexander likes that
direction. Isaac Vetter restates his position from FHIR-41320 — remove
it, and design something better if a real need is identified — but Josh
warns that without a "limited duration" option, apps will routinely
request `offline_access` even when they don't need persistent access,
which is worse for security. Josh also proposes adding an indicator in
the access-token response of how long the refresh token is expected to
remain valid (e.g., "until the user logs out"). Thread closes with the
note that this was discussed for ~1 hour in FHIR-I Q3 on 2024-02-01 and
the resolution is captured in **FHIR-43559**.

Participants: Sagar Shah, Josh Mandel, Isaac Vetter, Alexander Zautke,
Cooper Thompson.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Remove the `online_access` scope and the `permission-online` SMART
capability from the SMART App Launch specification (in `HL7/smart-app-launch`):

- Strike `online_access` from `input/pages/scopes-and-launch-context.md`
  (or current equivalent).
- Strike `permission-online` from the conformance / capabilities table in
  `input/pages/conformance.md`.
- Update the `CapabilityStatement` / `OperationDefinition` artifacts that
  enumerate launch capabilities to drop `permission-online`.
- Add a brief migration note: clients previously using `online_access`
  should request `offline_access` and rely on server policy for refresh
  token lifetime; servers MAY ignore `online_access` if still received.

This is the literal action FHIR-41320 requests.

#### Justification

No implementer in the public Zulip discussion (2020–2024) has provided
clear evidence of `online_access` being interoperably supported in the
form the spec describes; the one historic claim of broad use (Cerner /
Jenni Syed, 2021) predates SMART v2 and has not been re-confirmed.
The capability was excluded from ONC's HTI-1 final rule, and multiple
independent voices (Vetter, Thompson, Keeler, Zautke) describe the
semantics as ill-defined or unimplementable in decoupled auth-server
architectures. Removing the scope eliminates a footgun and a
regulatory-ambiguity surface.

However, **this proposal is in tension with the already-published
resolution of FHIR-43559** (Persuasive with Modification, 2024-01-26),
which kept the capability and merely scoped it to EHR Launch and marked
it experimental. Adopting Disposition A would require formally
revisiting FHIR-43559, which the WG should be aware is reopening an
already-balloted decision.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — Close as "Already addressed by FHIR-43559"

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-41320 as a **duplicate / already addressed**, pointing to the
**Persuasive with Modification** resolution of
[FHIR-43559](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43559). That resolution,
applied on `HL7/smart-app-launch` branch
`2024-ballot-reconciliation-uab`:

1. Restricts `online_access` / `permission-online` to **EHR Launch
   contexts only** (removing the standalone-launch ambiguity Cooper
   Thompson and Michael Briggs raised).
2. Marks the capability **experimental**, signalling its uncertain
   adoption status without breaking servers that do support it.
3. Adds cross-links to the Scopes negotiation guidance, making explicit
   that servers MAY downgrade or refuse the scope.

If the WG additionally wants to capture Josh Mandel's Feb-2024 idea of
returning an explicit refresh-token-lifetime hint in the token response
(e.g., a `refresh_token_expires_in` field, or "until the user logs out"
indicator), spin that off as a **new** Change Request rather than
re-opening FHIR-41320.

#### Justification

FHIR-43559 was explicitly framed as a re-evaluation of the same
capability, was discussed for an hour in FHIR-I Q3 on 2024-02-01 with
the same participants who appear on FHIR-41320's Zulip thread, and
reached a documented compromise. Reopening that decision based on
FHIR-41320's older, more aggressive ask would be procedurally costly and
risk re-litigating a balloted resolution. The "experimental + EHR-Launch
only" scoping responds to the substance of Vetter's complaint
(undefined semantics in standalone launch, low adoption) without the
backward-compatibility hit of removal. Disposition B is also the option
that requires the least new work in `HL7/smart-app-launch`.

---

### Disposition C: Decline (Not Persuasive)

#### Proposal

Decline the request. Retain `online_access` / `permission-online` in
their current (post-FHIR-43559) form: experimental, EHR-Launch-only,
with explicit Scopes-negotiation language. Record the rationale that:

- `online_access` provides a *requestable* short-duration alternative to
  `offline_access`, and removing it would push apps to request
  `offline_access` even when they only need session-bounded access (a
  net **security regression**, per Josh Mandel's Feb-2024 comment).
- At least one large historical implementer (Cerner, per Jenni Syed)
  reported relying on it; absence of more recent self-reports is not
  proof of zero deployment.
- ONC's exclusion from HTI-1 is a regulatory choice about *certification
  testing*, not a statement that the capability is harmful.

#### Justification

Removal is a breaking change for any server or app that currently
supports the scope — whether or not those deployments are vocal on
Zulip. The request as filed in 2023 has effectively been answered by
FHIR-43559's modification, and the remaining unresolved concern (better
guidance on refresh-token lifetimes) is a refinement that can be handled
without removing the scope. SMART has historically preferred to
deprecate-and-clarify rather than delete, especially for OAuth scope
strings that may be embedded in deployed clients.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition: B (Close as already addressed by FHIR-43559).**

FHIR-41320 was filed in May 2023 and has sat in **Triaged** for nearly
two years. In the meantime, FHIR-43559 — filed by a different reporter
but raising the *exact same concern* — went through full WG discussion
and was resolved Persuasive-with-Modification, with the change applied
to `HL7/smart-app-launch`. Closing FHIR-41320 against that prior
resolution is procedurally clean: it acknowledges the concern, points
the reporter to the substantive answer, and avoids re-balloting a
decision that has already shipped on the 2024 ballot reconciliation
branch.

Disposition A (full removal) would require the WG to formally reopen
FHIR-43559 and accept a breaking change to a published OAuth scope
string. The Zulip threads show genuine disagreement on whether removal
is the right call (Mandel argues against, citing the security
regression of pushing all apps to `offline_access`), so the WG should
not adopt A without re-running that debate from scratch — which
FHIR-43559 already did.

Disposition C is defensible but unsatisfying: it dismisses a
well-evidenced concern that the WG has, in fact, already partially
agreed with (hence the "experimental" downgrade in FHIR-43559).
Recording the resolution as B preserves the prior compromise while
giving the reporter a clear pointer.

If the WG wants to go further than FHIR-43559 did — for example,
adding the refresh-token-lifetime hint Josh Mandel proposed — that
should be filed as a **new** ticket against `HL7/smart-app-launch`
rather than carried on FHIR-41320.
