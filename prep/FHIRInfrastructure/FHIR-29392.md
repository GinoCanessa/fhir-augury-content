## Summary

The ticket reports that the SMART Web Messaging (SWM) IG, as balloted, did
not contain a section describing data provenance. The reporter (Theresa
Bessette) flagged this as an omission needing to be addressed in the IG.
During SWM ballot reconciliation on 2021-02-19 the WG voted 7-0-0 (Bas van
den Heuvel / Carl Anderson) to consider detailed provenance specification
for future use, with a near-term mitigation of adding an explicit mention
of provenance in the IG's *Limitations* section. Carl Anderson prepared
[HL7/smart-web-messaging PR #45](https://github.com/HL7/smart-web-messaging/pull/45)
to that effect. The ticket was originally resolved as *Considered for
Future Use*, then on 2022-05-06 lynn_laakso reverted the resolution and
re-opened the ticket with the note "re-open after publication of STU1",
returning it to a **Triaged / Unresolved** state. There are no
cross-referenced Jira tickets; the only external linkage is the
GitHub PR above and one Zulip thread on `#smart > SWM IG Provenance
Guidance` that announced the PR for community feedback.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a dedicated *Data Provenance* section to the SWM IG (a new narrative
page, or a sub-section of an existing page such as *Security &
Privacy*), in the `HL7/smart-web-messaging` repo under the IG's
narrative authoring root. The section would: (1) describe how a SWM
host should attribute messages exchanged via the web-messaging channel
back to an originating user / app / device, (2) recommend that hosts
record `Provenance` resources (or equivalent audit records) capturing
the SWM `messagingHandle`, the originating EHR session, the app
identity (`client_id`) and the responsible user, and (3) provide
non-normative examples mapping SWM message types to `Provenance.agent`
roles. Promote the existing Limitations-section paragraph into a
forward reference to the new section.

#### Justification

This is the literal request: the reporter said a provenance section
"could not be found." The 2022-05-06 re-open note explicitly says the
ticket should be reconsidered "after publication of STU1," which
implies the WG always intended the topic to be addressed, not
permanently deferred. SWM messages cross trust boundaries (EHR ↔ app),
which is exactly the territory `Provenance` was designed for, and
omitting any guidance leaves implementers to invent attribution
patterns that will not be interoperable. Aligning with the broader FHIR
pattern of pairing message/operation specs with provenance guidance
(e.g., `MessageHeader`/`Bundle` provenance discussion in core) keeps
SWM consistent with peer specifications.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the *Limitations* mention introduced by
[PR #45](https://github.com/HL7/smart-web-messaging/pull/45) but expand
it into a short, non-normative *Provenance Considerations* sub-section
of the existing Security & Privacy / Limitations page. The expanded
text would: (a) state explicitly that SWM defines no new resources and
therefore introduces no new provenance obligations beyond those of the
underlying FHIR resources exchanged, (b) point implementers at SMART
App Launch / SMART on FHIR audit guidance and at the core
`Provenance` resource for any application-specific auditing, and
(c) call out one or two SWM-specific edge cases (e.g., the
`messagingHandle` correlation identifier and scribe/voice scenarios
introduced by SWM) that implementers should consider when constructing
their own `Provenance` records. No normative `SHALL`/`SHOULD`
statements would be introduced.

#### Justification

This is closer to what the WG actually voted for in 2021 (defer
detailed specification, mention in Limitations) while addressing the
2022 re-open by giving implementers something more substantive than a
single sentence. It avoids the cost and risk of authoring full
normative provenance guidance for a small IG that has been published
without it, and it stays within the WG's stated comfort zone of
deferring detailed specification. It also honours Carl Anderson's
original observation that SWM "defines no new Resources," so a
narrative pointer to existing FHIR mechanisms is proportionate.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Resolve as *Not Persuasive* (or re-apply *Considered for Future Use*
without further changes), keeping the existing *Limitations* paragraph
from PR #45 as the IG's complete answer on provenance. Document that
SWM intentionally does not specify provenance because (i) it defines
no new resources, (ii) provenance for the FHIR resources exchanged
over SWM is fully covered by the underlying FHIR base specification
and SMART on FHIR audit guidance, and (iii) the 2021 WG vote (7-0-0)
already settled the question.

#### Justification

The substantive WG vote on the matter was unanimous (7-0-0) for
deferral, and the originally-applied resolution (*Considered for Future
Use*) was procedurally reverted in 2022 only to allow re-evaluation
post-STU1, not because new evidence of harm emerged. No implementers
have raised concrete interoperability problems on the linked Zulip
thread or in any other cross-referenced item. Re-doing work the WG
already voted on, in the absence of new evidence, sets a poor
precedent for ballot-reconciliation finality.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The 2021 vote was defer-with-mitigation, and the 2022 re-open note
("re-open after publication of STU1") signals that the WG wants *some*
forward motion rather than a permanent close. A full normative
provenance section (Disposition A) is disproportionate for an IG that
defines no new resources and whose only known stakeholder discussion
(the `#smart > SWM IG Provenance Guidance` Zulip thread) was a single
PR review with one participant. A flat decline (Disposition C) ignores
the intent of the 2022 re-open. Disposition B threads the needle:
it expands the Limitations paragraph into a properly-scoped
*Provenance Considerations* sub-section that points implementers at
existing FHIR/SMART mechanisms and flags SWM-specific edge cases
(e.g., `messagingHandle` correlation), without committing the WG to
authoring normative guidance the community has not asked for. Before
implementing, run `repo-analysis HL7/smart-web-messaging` so the
edit can be grounded in the actual IG authoring root and so any
template/page-list updates are caught.
