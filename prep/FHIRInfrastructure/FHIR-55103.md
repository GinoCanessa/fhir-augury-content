## Summary

Correction filed against the FHIR Core narrative page **`fhirpath`**

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Accept the change as Persuasive and edit the FHIR Core `fhirpath`
narrative page to expand the contraction **"isn't"** to **"is not"**
wherever it appears on the page. Concretely: locate the occurrence(s)
of `isn't` in the source for the `fhirpath` page in the `HL7/fhir`
repository (the page rendered as `fhirpath.html`) and replace each
with `is not`. While editing, scan the same page for any other informal
contractions (`don't`, `doesn't`, `won't`, `can't`, `it's`, `that's`,
`we'll`, etc.) and expand them in the same commit so the page is
internally consistent. No spec semantics, examples, conformance
resources, or FHIRPath expressions are altered — this is a
narrative-only editorial fix.

#### Justification

The ticket is exactly what it says on the tin: an editorial
correction. FHIR Core's house style favours formal prose without
contractions, and the page in question is normative-adjacent guidance
that is read by implementers across many jurisdictions, including
non-native-English readers for whom contractions add unnecessary
parsing cost. Expanding the contraction has zero impact on
machine-readable artifacts, on FHIRPath grammar, or on conformance,
and the resolution is already proposed as **Persuasive**. The change
is trivially safe, fully reversible, and consistent with how prior
similar "spelling/abbreviation" tickets in FHIR Infrastructure have
been dispatched.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the spirit of the request but broaden the scope: rather than
only patching the `fhirpath` page, run a one-pass editorial sweep
across the FHIR Core narrative pages owned by FHIR Infrastructure
(e.g., `fhirpath`, `fhirpath-changes`, and the surrounding "Foundation"
narrative pages) to expand contractions and obvious abbreviations,
gated on the same Persuasive resolution. File the resulting commit
against this ticket and reference any sibling tickets that report the
same class of issue on adjacent pages. Optionally, add a one-line
note to the FHIR Infrastructure editorial checklist that contractions
should be expanded in narrative pages, so future authors don't
reintroduce them.

#### Justification

"Spelling/abbreviation" tickets imported from the legacy comment
system tend to arrive in clusters because a single reviewer typically
flags multiple instances. Fixing only the literal word `isn't` on one
page leaves the workgroup likely to receive near-identical follow-up
tickets, each consuming agenda time. A broader sweep amortises the
review cost and produces a more uniform reading experience, at the
cost of a slightly larger diff and a marginally higher risk of an
unintended edit slipping in. The risk is low because the sweep is
mechanical (search-and-replace of a small, bounded set of
contractions) and the diff is reviewable on a per-line basis.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the request as **Not Persuasive — out of editorial scope**.
Document the rationale that contractions are an accepted stylistic
choice in FHIR's narrative pages and do not impair the normative
content, and close the ticket without changes.

#### Justification

The case for declining rests on cost-versus-benefit and on the
absence of an FHIR Infrastructure style rule that prohibits
contractions in narrative prose. The ticket reports a single
contraction on a single page; if the workgroup does not have a
standing rule against contractions, treating one reporter's
preference as Persuasive risks setting a precedent that invites a
long tail of similar editorial complaints with no normative payoff.
This disposition is defensible only if the workgroup explicitly
decides not to enforce expansion of contractions — otherwise it
contradicts the existing house style.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

This ticket is the textbook case for **Accept As Requested**. The
resolution is already proposed as Persuasive, the change is a
single-word narrative edit on the `fhirpath` page in `HL7/fhir`, it
touches no machine-readable artifacts and no FHIRPath grammar, and
it is consistent with FHIR Core's preference for formal prose.
Disposition B (broader sweep) is attractive in principle but expands
the scope of a single technical-correction ticket into an editorial
project that is better tracked separately if the workgroup wants it;
it should not block this ticket. Disposition C is hard to justify
given the Persuasive resolution and the trivially low cost of the
fix. Apply the change as a one-line edit, confirm the
`Fit-For-Teleconference` review, and close.
