## Summary

The reporter notes a documentation defect in the SMART App Launch
implementation guide page `brands.html`. Inside the **Brand Bundle Profile**
subsection, the second paragraph contains a hyperlink labelled "User Access
Brands Overview" whose target anchor (`#brand-bundle-profile`) is the same
heading the link sits under — the link effectively jumps three lines back to
itself. The reporter asks whether the link was intended to point somewhere
else (likely the top of the page or the "User Access Brands Overview"
section that exists earlier in the document) and is requesting the
self-referential link be corrected.

No related Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were surfaced via
cross-references or search, so the request stands on its own as a small
editorial fix to the SMART App Launch IG narrative.

## Details

**Description:**

> In the Brand Bundle Profile section, the second paragraph links to "User
> Access Brands Overview", which is the same section:
>
> #### *Brand Bundle Profile*
>
> *JSON FHIR Bundle (type: `collection`) of Organizations and Endpoints
> that is hosted at a stable, publicly available, publicly disclosed
> location.*
>
> *For background and context, see **[User Access Brands
> Overview](https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/smart-app-launch/brands.html#brand-bundle-profile)**.*
>
> It is odd to have a hyperlink to an anchor that is three lines above the
> link. In some sense, this is saying "for background and context, just
> keep reading this section".
>
> Was this meant to link to the top of the page?

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat this as a persuasive editorial correction. In the SMART App Launch
IG source for `brands.html`, locate the **Brand Bundle Profile**
subsection's second paragraph ("For background and context, see **User
Access Brands Overview**") and re-target the anchor so it points to the
intended overview section rather than back to itself. The most likely
intended target is the top-level **User Access Brands Overview** heading
(anchor such as `#user-access-brands-overview`) earlier on the same page,
not `#brand-bundle-profile`. Apply the change in the IG's authoring
markdown/HTML for the brands page and rebuild the IG so the published
anchor resolves correctly.

#### Justification

The reporter has correctly identified a self-referential link that adds no
navigational value and is confusing to readers ("for background and
context, just keep reading"). Repointing the link to the top-level
overview section is consistent with the surrounding narrative pattern in
SMART App Launch, where each profile subsection refers back to a
higher-level conceptual section for context. This is a low-risk technical
correction with no normative impact and no implementer burden.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than re-pointing the link, **remove the "For background and
context, see …" sentence entirely from the Brand Bundle Profile
subsection.** The Brand Bundle Profile subsection is already nested under
the User Access Brands Overview material on the same page, so a
back-pointer to the surrounding context is redundant. If additional
context is desirable, replace the link with a short inline phrase (e.g.,
"see the overview at the top of this page") or convert it to a
breadcrumb-style "↑ Back to overview" link styled consistently with other
intra-page navigation aids in the IG.

#### Justification

The underlying concern is reader confusion caused by a link that does not
move the reader anywhere meaningful. Removing the sentence eliminates the
problem at its root and avoids creating another anchor that future edits
could accidentally break. It also tightens the narrative — the
subsection's normative content (Bundle of Organizations and Endpoints
hosted at a stable URL) is what the reader needs there, not a recursive
pointer to context they have just read. Trade-off vs. Disposition A:
slightly more invasive editorially, but produces a cleaner result.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline as **Not Persuasive — editorial only / no normative impact**, on
the grounds that a self-referential anchor on a published page does not
mislead implementers about the conformance requirements of the Brand
Bundle Profile and the published HTML still renders correctly. Close
without change.

#### Justification

A defensible decline rationale is that the work group's bandwidth for the
SMART App Launch IG is finite and that purely cosmetic, non-normative
narrative defects can be deferred or batched. The link does not change
the meaning of the Brand Bundle Profile, does not affect validation, and
does not create an interoperability risk. If the IG is otherwise frozen
or in a maintenance posture, leaving the broken cross-reference in place
until a larger editorial pass is acceptable.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

This is a straightforward, persuasive editorial correction with effectively
zero risk and zero normative impact. The reporter has pinpointed the
exact paragraph and anchor, and the fix is a one-line change in the
SMART App Launch IG's `brands` page source. Disposition B (deleting the
sentence) is also reasonable and produces a slightly cleaner narrative,
but it changes editorial intent more than necessary; if the work group
prefers the tighter wording it can be folded into A as a small
refinement during the fix. Disposition C is hard to defend on the
merits — the cost of the fix is trivial and accepting small technical
corrections like this is exactly what the "Technical Correction" issue
type is for.
