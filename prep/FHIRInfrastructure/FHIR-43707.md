## Summary

Craig Newman is asking the FHIR Infrastructure work group to clarify the
"Notified Pull" narrative in the R5 Subscriptions Backport IG. He proposes
four narrative-only fixes: (1) add an explicit definition of "Notified
Pull" because the two sample use cases do not obviously share a common
shape; (2) reconsider whether "Time Shifted Services" is really a distinct
use case, since standard subscription notifications already imply that
follow-up queries can happen later; (3) cross-link from the
"Unstandardized Queries" passage to the "id-only" discussion on the
Payloads page, because the Payloads page comes after the Notifications
page in the table of contents and the id-only concept is introduced
there; and (4) reword the "Unstandardized Queries" paragraph so that the
roles of the *referring* facility and the *servicing* facility are
clearly distinguished, and so that the use of the word "queries" for
what is really an id-only/related-query payload is not misleading.

Although the cross-reference index did not surface any directly linked
items, the keyword-similarity index reveals a tight cluster of sibling
tickets against the same Notified Pull passages — most notably
[FHIR-43735](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43735) (Karl Naden, ask for
the diagrams/text to refer to subscription/notification/subsequent-query
explicitly), [FHIR-44107](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-44107) (Josh
Mandel, reframe "Unstandardized Queries" as "Enable Targeted Follow-ups"),
[FHIR-43607](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43607) (Cooper Thompson,
don't imply standardized queries are the norm) and
[FHIR-43612](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-43612) (Cooper Thompson,
relate id-only to related-queries). All four of those have been
resolved as Persuasive or Persuasive with Modification, and the broader
"notified pull" feature has been forward-ported to R6 via
[FHIR-46493](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-46493). FHIR-43707 itself
is still **Triaged / Unresolved** and unassigned, so the WG has not yet
formally voted on it.

## Details

**Description:**

A few changes to the Notified Pull section may make it easier to
understand.

(1) A definition for "Notified Pull" would be helpful, the two use cases
given don't seem to have much in common, so it's not clear to me what a
Notified Pull actually is.

(2) Is the Time Shifted Services really a distinct use case? Does the
standard notification workflow put time limits on when a notification
goes stale and can't be used by notification recipient as a trigger for
accessing data? I like the idea of clarifying that the follow up queries
might be delayed but is it really a distinct use case?

(3) The Unstandardized Queries references the id-only approach, but if
the reader is going through the IG in the order of the table of contents
(I know, who does that in the on-line world right?), then they haven't
been introduced to that concept yet because it's covered in the Payloads
page which comes after the notification page. A link to that
documentation from the Unstandardized Queries section would be helpful.

(4) For Unstandardized Queries, I initially had trouble understanding the
description because it referred to the "referring facility" not being
able to predict the necessary queries. This was confusing because it
seemed like it would be the Servicing Facility which would have to
formulate the unstandardized queries. But as I reread the id-only
approach, it seems like the referring facility is trying to predict what
information the servicing facility will need and including the links in
the notification to avoid the servicing facility having to do queries
(just following the links instead). The use of the word "queries" for an
id-only approach threw me. Some clarification or expanded description
might be helpful for (especially new) readers.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply all four narrative changes Craig requested in the Notified Pull
section of the Subscriptions Backport IG (the Notifications page):

1. **Add a definition.** Insert a one-paragraph normative-prose
   definition at the top of the "Notified Pull" section in the form
   "*Notified Pull is a pattern in which the server sends a
   notification that does not itself carry the clinically relevant
   payload; instead it carries enough information (an id, a reference,
   or a related-query) for the recipient to retrieve the payload
   itself.*" Frame the existing "Time Shifted Services" and
   "Unstandardized Queries" paragraphs as *examples* of this pattern,
   not as use cases that need to share business semantics.
2. **Soften "Time Shifted Services" framing.** Reword it from a
   distinct use case to a clarifying note: "Notifications are not
   time-bounded by the spec; the recipient may pull the data
   immediately or at a later time." Keep the example, drop the
   sub-heading "use case".
3. **Cross-link to the id-only payload section.** Add an explicit
   forward link from "Unstandardized Queries" to the id-only payload
   discussion on the Payloads page, and also (per FHIR-43612, already
   Persuasive with Modification) a back-link from the Payloads /
   id-only section to the related-query extension and the Notified
   Pull section.
4. **Reword the "referring/servicing facility" passage.** Make the
   roles explicit: the referring facility (the *sender* of the
   notification) provides links/related-queries because the servicing
   facility (the *recipient*) cannot in general construct a single
   standardized query that works everywhere. Replace the word
   "queries" in the id-only paragraph with "follow-up requests" or
   "related-query references" to remove the false implication of a
   FHIR search.

#### Justification

All four items are pure narrative / cross-link changes against the same
paragraphs that the WG has already agreed to rewrite via the resolved
sibling tickets. Specifically: item (1) is the same fix already accepted
in **FHIR-43735 (Persuasive)**; item (3) is the inverse of the cross-link
already accepted in **FHIR-43612 (Persuasive with Modification)**; item
(4) is the same wording fix already accepted (in spirit) in **FHIR-44107
(Persuasive)** and **FHIR-43607 (Not Persuasive with Modification)**.
Accepting FHIR-43707 as a single editorial change-set keeps the prose
internally consistent — otherwise the IG risks shipping with the
adjacent tickets' fixes applied while Craig's slightly different
phrasing of the same complaint sits open. The Zulip "Some minutes on
Notified Pull pattern" thread also confirms there is no shared, crisp
definition of Notified Pull in the community, which makes item (1)
particularly valuable to readers coming in cold from the Dutch TA or
from R6 core.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — Persuasive with Modification (consolidate as editorial)

#### Proposal

Resolve FHIR-43707 as **Persuasive with Modification**, with the
modification being that the change is rolled into the editorial pass
already authorised by FHIR-43735, FHIR-44107, FHIR-43612 and FHIR-43607
rather than tracked as four independent edits. Concretely:

- For items (1), (3) and (4): mark FHIR-43707 as duplicative-but-aligned
  with the resolved sibling tickets and have the editor confirm that
  the rewrite executed for those tickets also satisfies Craig's
  wording. No new normative content; only confirm that the new prose
  in the next ballot/publication snapshot answers Craig's specific
  questions.
- For item (2) (Time Shifted Services): take the **modification** —
  do *not* delete the sub-section, but downgrade it from "use case" to
  an explanatory note inside the new definition (per Disposition A
  item 2). The WG has not previously voted on this exact ask.

#### Justification

Most of FHIR-43707's content overlaps tickets that are already resolved
and (in the case of FHIR-46493) being forward-ported to R6. Tracking it
as an independent change risks either (a) a second, slightly different
rewrite that drifts from the agreed wording, or (b) the editor having
to reconcile two near-duplicate change requests. Persuasive with
Modification preserves Craig's specific wording concerns in the
disposition record (so a future reviewer can verify against item-by-item
acceptance) while keeping the IG's edit history coherent. It also avoids
declining a ticket that, on the merits, the WG has already agreed with —
an outright Decline would read as inconsistent with the FHIR-43735 /
FHIR-44107 / FHIR-43612 dispositions.

---

### Disposition C: Decline — Already Addressed

#### Proposal

Resolve FHIR-43707 as **Considered – Already Addressed (No Change)** on
the basis that FHIR-43735, FHIR-44107, FHIR-43607 and FHIR-43612
together cover items (1), (3) and (4), and that item (2) (Time Shifted
Services) does not warrant a structural change because the spec's
existing language already says notifications are not time-limited.
Direct Craig to the rewritten Notified Pull section in the next
publication snapshot.

#### Justification

The block-vote thread shows the WG has been actively resolving Notified
Pull narrative complaints in a coordinated batch. If the editor's
rewrite for those tickets in fact lands the four clarifications Craig
asks for, then opening a separate change for FHIR-43707 adds churn
without changing the deliverable. Time Shifted Services as a labelled
use case is also defensible on its own — it tells implementers
explicitly that "the recipient is allowed to defer", which is a
legitimate non-obvious affordance of the spec.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B (Persuasive with Modification —
consolidate as editorial alongside FHIR-43735 / FHIR-44107 / FHIR-43612 /
FHIR-43607).

Disposition A is technically correct but creates a second editorial
change-set against paragraphs that are already being rewritten, which
invites drift between Craig's wording and the wording the WG already
agreed to elsewhere. Disposition C is hard to defend against the
Zulip evidence: the "Some minutes on Notified Pull pattern" thread
shows there is genuinely no consensus definition of Notified Pull in
the community, so item (1) (an explicit definition) is real new value
even if items (3) and (4) are largely redundant with FHIR-43735 / -44107
/ -43612. Disposition B captures the work that *is* additive
(definition + downgrading "Time Shifted Services" from a use case to
an explanatory note) while avoiding double-tracking the parts that the
WG has already voted on. It also keeps FHIR-43707 visible in the
audit trail so a future editor can confirm Craig's specific wording
concerns were resolved by the consolidated rewrite — which a flat
Decline would not.
