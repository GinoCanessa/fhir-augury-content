## Summary

The reporter questions a sentence in the FHIR Shorthand (FSH) specification
that calls out trial-use support for the new R5 datatypes `integer64` and
`CodeableReference`. Their argument is that FSH as a language is largely
datatype-agnostic — values of new datatypes are still expressed as strings
in FSH source — so there is no FSH-level reason to single these out as
trial use. They suggest deleting the sentence. No related Jira tickets,
Zulip threads, or GitHub items were surfaced by the cross-reference index
or by a direct search for `FHIR-33552`, so the discussion captured below
is limited to the ticket's own comment trail.

## Details

**Description:**

> FSH supports new pre-release FHIR R5 datatypes integer64 and CodeableReference on a trial use basis.

As the language does not depend on these elements, why is this mentioned.
All new datatypes are supported are they not? They are just expressed as
strings. Suggest to remove this sentence.

**Comments:**

- **lynn_laakso (2022-02-17):** "re-open issue post publication of Normative
  Shorthand spec" — administrative re-opening after the Normative FSH
  publication.
- **lynn_laakso (2022-02-17):** Reverts a previous "Considered for Future
  Use" resolution from 2021-10-25 (Mark Kramer / Rick Geimer, 16 - 0 - 1).
  The prior reasoning was: FSH does have new *language constructs* for
  `CodeableReference` (see §3.6.3.7), so marking material related to
  `CodeableReference` as Trial Use is appropriate. For `integer64`, no new
  FSH language elements were introduced, so those sections could in principle
  go Normative — but removing the TU marking on `integer64` would be a
  substantive change and force another ballot cycle for the whole spec.
  The proposal was therefore to keep the TU/Normative markings unchanged
  and defer advancing TU sections to a later ballot.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Remove the sentence "FSH supports new pre-release FHIR R5 datatypes
integer64 and CodeableReference on a trial use basis." from the FSH
specification (the sentence currently appears in the introductory /
"What's New" framing for the R5 datatype additions). Treat both
`integer64` and `CodeableReference` as ordinary datatypes within the
Normative FSH grammar — they are already representable through FSH's
string-based value syntax — and rely on the underlying FHIR
specification (not FSH) to carry their TU status.

#### Justification

The reporter's core point is grammatically correct: FSH does not gate
on datatype identity. Values are written as strings (or numerics, etc.)
that the parser does not need to understand at the type level, so any
datatype FHIR introduces is automatically expressible in FSH without a
language change. Calling out only two datatypes as TU at the FSH level
is misleading because (a) the TU-ness lives in FHIR, not FSH, and
(b) it implies FSH would behave differently for those datatypes than
for `decimal`, `string`, etc., which it does not. Deleting the sentence
keeps FSH's normative surface clean and avoids future drift as new
FHIR datatypes are added.

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the substance of the sentence but split it into two narrower
statements that match the actual FSH-level impact:

1. **`CodeableReference`:** Retain the Trial Use marking on the FSH
   sections that introduce *new language constructs* tied to
   `CodeableReference` (per §3.6.3.7). State explicitly that the TU
   status applies to those FSH grammar additions, not to the underlying
   FHIR datatype's status.
2. **`integer64`:** Drop the FSH-level TU mention entirely, on the
   ground that FSH introduced no new language element for it; the
   datatype's TU status (in FHIR R5) is the authoritative source and
   does not need restating in the FSH spec.

#### Justification

This matches the reasoning recorded in the 2022-02-17 comment: there
*is* an FSH-language reason to mark `CodeableReference`-related text as
TU (new grammar at §3.6.3.7), but no such reason for `integer64`. The
alternative addresses the reporter's concern about over-marking
(`integer64` is a datatype-only concern, not a language concern) while
preserving the genuine FSH-language TU scope for `CodeableReference`.
It also avoids the practical risk flagged in the comment: stripping the
TU marking from `integer64`-adjacent sections wholesale could trigger
re-ballot if those sections were Normative-by-default, whereas a
targeted edit ("remove the FSH-level TU mention of integer64; clarify
that the CodeableReference TU scope is the §3.6.3.7 grammar") is a
clarification rather than a normative-status change.

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Persistent / Not Persuasive — keep the sentence as-is. Confirm the
2022-02-17 disposition that proposes deferring any TU-vs-Normative
re-marking to a later ballot, and close the ticket without an FSH spec
edit.

#### Justification

The 16-0-1 vote in 2021-10-25 already considered this and decided to
keep the TU markings; the 2022-02-17 comment re-confirms that touching
the markings would be a substantive change requiring another full
ballot cycle for the Normative FSH spec. Given (a) the change is purely
editorial in the reporter's framing, (b) the existing sentence is
literally true (FSH *does* support those datatypes, and they *are* TU
in the underlying FHIR spec), and (c) the ballot-cycle cost of touching
TU/Normative markings, declining is defensible on a cost/benefit basis
for FHIR Infrastructure to recommend on the Block-Vote-2 sheet.

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The reporter is correct that FSH-the-language does not depend on the
identity of the underlying FHIR datatype, so the blanket "FSH supports
… on a trial use basis" framing is misleading for `integer64`.
However, the 2022-02-17 comment establishes a real FSH-language reason
to keep TU scope around `CodeableReference`: §3.6.3.7 introduces new
grammar specifically for it, and that grammar is genuinely the part
that should carry FSH-level TU status. Disposition B captures both
truths — drop the misleading `integer64` framing, scope the
`CodeableReference` TU marking to the new grammar — without forcing
the broader normative re-marking work that the 2022-02-17 comment
explicitly warned would trigger another ballot cycle. Disposition A is
cleaner editorially but understates the §3.6.3.7 grammar TU concern;
Disposition C preserves the status quo at the cost of leaving an
inaccurate sentence on the page. Block-Vote-2 should consider B as the
default and fall back to C only if the editorial change is judged to
have non-trivial substantive impact on the Normative FSH text.
