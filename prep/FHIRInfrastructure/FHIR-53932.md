## Summary

The reporter (DanielSchuster) flags an apparent contradiction in the
"history" section of the FHIR HTTP / RESTful API page
(`http.html`) in the 6.0.0-ballot4 build. Two adjacent statements in
that section read, on the one hand, that "interactions
(`create`, `update`, `patch`, `delete`) **or operations** that
change/delete/add resources create history entries", and on the other
hand that "operations do not appear directly in the history log, but
side effects (e.g., creation of audit logs, stored binaries, etc.)
will appear where relevant." Taken together, the first sentence
implies operations themselves are recorded in history when they mutate
resources, while the second sentence asserts the opposite — that
operations are never recorded directly, and only their resource-level
side effects show up. The ticket asks the FHIR Infrastructure work
group to either reconcile the two statements or, if both are
intentionally true, add wording that explains how they fit together
(e.g., that the resource-mutating *side effects* of an operation are
what generate history entries, not the operation invocation itself).
The single comment on the ticket (corey_spears) notes a possible link
to [FHIR-40957](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-40957), an older
Persuasive-with-Modification ticket that tightened the
"interaction" vs "operation" terminology on the same RESTful API page;
that prior cleanup is plausibly the source of the wording mismatch
seen here.

## Proposed Dispositions

Note: because the cross-reference query returned no GitHub items, the
dispositions below cannot name a specific cached briefing or
authoring-root path. They do name the affected source page (`http.html`,
the HTTP / RESTful API page) which lives in the FHIR core
specification; the workgroup will know the corresponding source file.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Reconcile the two sentences in the "history" section of `http.html` so
they are consistent. Concretely, replace the first sentence

> "Interactions (create, update, patch, and delete) or operations that
> change/delete/add resources create history entries"

with wording that scopes history-entry creation to the four mutating
interactions, while explicitly acknowledging that an operation can
*indirectly* cause history entries via the interactions it performs
under the hood. Suggested replacement text (illustrative):

> "The mutating RESTful interactions — `create`, `update`, `patch`,
> and `delete` — create history entries on the affected resource(s).
> Operations (`$name`) do not themselves appear in the history log;
> however, when an operation's implementation performs one of those
> mutating interactions on a resource (or produces side effects such
> as audit logs or stored binaries), those resource-level changes
> appear in history where relevant."

Leave the second sentence ("Operations do not appear directly in the
history log…") essentially as-is, since it already reflects the
intended model.

#### Justification

This is the smallest change that removes the apparent contradiction
without altering normative behaviour. It is consistent with the
direction set by [FHIR-40957](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-40957),
which already tightened "interaction" vs "operation" usage on this
same page. It also matches what implementers actually do: history
entries are produced by resource-level mutations, and an operation is
just an RPC-style entry point that may or may not perform such
mutations. The fix is purely editorial, lives entirely within
`http.html`, has no compatibility impact, and addresses the reporter's
complaint directly.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Treat the contradiction as a symptom of a thinner underlying gap and
add a small dedicated sub-section ("Operations and history") to
`http.html` that spells out the model explicitly, rather than tweaking
two sentences in place. The new sub-section would state:

1. Only the four mutating interactions (`create`, `update`, `patch`,
   `delete`) directly produce version history entries on a resource.
2. An operation invocation (`$name`, at system / type / instance
   level) is *not itself* recorded in the resource version history.
3. An operation MAY cause history entries indirectly when its
   implementation performs `create` / `update` / `patch` / `delete`
   on FHIR resources, or when it produces persisted side effects
   (e.g., `AuditEvent`, `Binary`, `Provenance`) that are themselves
   resources with their own history.
4. Servers SHOULD document, per operation in their
   `CapabilityStatement`, what resource-level changes (and therefore
   what history entries) a given operation can produce.

Then trim the two flagged sentences down to a one-line pointer to the
new sub-section.

#### Justification

The reporter's confusion is unlikely to be unique — implementers
routinely ask whether `$everything`, `$apply`, `$submit-data`, etc.
"show up" in `_history`. A dedicated sub-section is more durable than
re-balancing two sentences and gives implementers a single citable
location. Trade-off vs. Disposition A: it is a larger editorial change
and slightly increases the page's surface area, but it costs nothing
normatively and avoids future tickets re-asking the same question. It
also dovetails with the spirit of FHIR-40957 (clearer
interaction/operation separation on this page).

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline as "Not Persuasive — already addressed by existing wording".
Argue that the two sentences, read in order, already describe the
intended model: the first sentence covers the four mutating
interactions and notes that operations *which themselves change/add/
delete resources* produce history entries (i.e., via those mutations),
and the second sentence clarifies that the operation *invocation* is
not separately logged. Close the ticket with a clarifying comment but
no spec change.

#### Justification

Defensible only if the workgroup judges the current wording to be a
defensible reading rather than a contradiction. The advantage is zero
spec churn at ballot stage. The disadvantage is real: a careful
reader (the reporter) saw a contradiction on first read, which is
itself evidence that the wording is failing its job. Declining sends
the reader back to the same text with no improvement and risks
recurring tickets at the next ballot.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A is the right starting point. It is a purely editorial,
non-normative fix to a single page (`http.html`) that the reporter
has correctly identified as ambiguous, it aligns with the direction
already set by [FHIR-40957](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-40957)
on the same page, and it can be implemented and re-balloted with
negligible risk. Disposition B is attractive and the workgroup may
prefer it if there is appetite for a slightly larger editorial pass
— it is essentially "A, plus a sub-section" — but it is not required
to resolve the immediate contradiction. Disposition C should be
avoided: the reporter's reading is reasonable, and leaving the two
sentences in their current form will keep generating the same
question.
