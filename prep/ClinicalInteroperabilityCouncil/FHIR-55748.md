## Summary

The ticket reports a wording problem in the Da Vinci HRex IG conformance
details page (`conformancedetails.html`, §sec-13) where the text states
that "There are certain constraints on this RFC that apply across all
Da Vinci IGs." The reporter, Karl Naden, points out that the phrase
"constraints on this RFC" reads as if the IG were constraining
RFC 8705 itself, which is not the intent. The intent is to layer
*additional* implementation requirements on top of RFC 8705 for any
Da Vinci IG implementation that supports mutual TLS. The reporter
proposes a clarifying rewrite: "When mutual TLS is used, it SHALL be
done in accordance with RFC 8705. The following additional constraints
apply to all implementations of Da Vinci IGs that support mutual TLS:".
No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were
found.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the Da Vinci HRex conformance details page (the source for
`conformancedetails.html` §sec-13) so the mutual TLS paragraph reads
substantially as the reporter proposed:

> "When mutual TLS is used, it **SHALL** be done in accordance with
> [RFC 8705](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc8705). The
> following additional constraints apply to all implementations of
> Da Vinci IGs that support mutual TLS:"

Concretely: in the HRex IG narrative source for §sec-13 (the "Mutual
TLS" subsection of the Conformance Details page in the
`HL7/davinci-ehrx` IG), replace the phrase "There are certain
constraints on this RFC that apply across all Da Vinci IGs:" with "The
following additional constraints apply to all implementations of
Da Vinci IGs that support mutual TLS:". No StructureDefinition,
ValueSet, CapabilityStatement, or other conformance-resource changes
are required — this is a narrative-only edit.

#### Justification

The reporter is correct that "constraints on this RFC" is ambiguous and
could be read as the IG attempting to modify RFC 8705 itself, which
HL7 IGs cannot do. The proposed wording is precise: it preserves the
SHALL conformance to RFC 8705 and frames the bulleted items that
follow as *additional* implementation constraints scoped to Da Vinci
IGs that elect to use mutual TLS. The change is narrative-only, has
no impact on conformance resources or downstream implementers, and the
ticket has already been reviewed and accepted on the Feb. 13 BR call
(per mckenzie's comment), with resolution "Persuasive". This aligns
with FHIR's convention that IGs add constraints on top of normative
external specifications rather than redefining them.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Adopt the reporter's intent but tighten the wording further to use
HL7 conformance-keyword style explicitly. For example:

> "Implementations that use mutual TLS **SHALL** conform to
> [RFC 8705](https://datatracker.ietf.org/doc/html/rfc8705). In
> addition, the following constraints **SHALL** apply to all Da Vinci
> IG implementations that use mutual TLS:"

Optionally, hoist the bulleted constraints into a labelled
"Additional Da Vinci mutual-TLS requirements" sub-section so each
constraint is independently citable from other IGs and from
test-tooling.

#### Justification

Functionally equivalent to Disposition A, but it (1) puts the SHALL
keyword on the implementation actor rather than on the act of
"using" mTLS, which is the conventional FHIR/HL7 conformance phrasing,
and (2) gives downstream IGs and test artifacts a stable anchor to
reference the additional constraints individually. Trade-off: it goes
slightly beyond what the reporter asked for, and a structural change
to the section may require re-checking inbound links from other Da
Vinci IGs that already point at `#ci-c-sec-13`.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as "Not Persuasive" on the basis that the existing
wording, while informal, is understood by the Da Vinci implementer
audience to mean "additional constraints" and has not been reported
as causing actual interoperability problems.

#### Justification

A defensible decline rationale would be that the surrounding paragraph
and the bulleted constraints make the intent obvious in context, and
that narrative churn in published-ballot IG content carries a small
risk of breaking external citations or translations. However, this
rationale is weak: the reporter is an experienced implementer who
*did* read the text as constraining the RFC, the fix is purely
editorial, and the BR call has already accepted the change. Decline
is included here only for completeness.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A is the right call. The change is a small, unambiguous
editorial clarification that removes a real ambiguity (the IG cannot
constrain RFC 8705 itself) without altering any normative behaviour or
conformance artifact. The reporter's proposed wording is already
fit-for-purpose, the workgroup has indicated agreement on the
Feb. 13 BR call, and Jira already records the resolution as
"Persuasive" with the `Ready-For-Vote` label. Disposition B's
phrasing improvements are nice-to-have but introduce scope creep
(actor rephrasing and possible sub-section restructuring) that would
require re-balloting effort disproportionate to the benefit;
Disposition C is not defensible given an implementer has explicitly
flagged the wording as misleading.
