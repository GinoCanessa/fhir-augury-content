## Summary

This ticket asks whether the Da Vinci HRex implementation guide should
include a "Use Cases" section, similar to the section that most other Da
Vinci IGs carry. The reporter (Lloyd McKenzie) raises this as an open
design question rather than a defect: HRex differs from peer Da Vinci
IGs in that it is **not intended to be implemented on its own** — its
profiles, operations, and patterns are designed to be inherited and
referenced by other Da Vinci guides (CDex, PDex, PAS, DEQM, etc.). That
makes it ambiguous whether a use-case narrative belongs in HRex
directly, or whether use cases should live only in the consuming IGs.

The ticket has already been **triaged with a "Persuasive" resolution**
that was reviewed and approved on the **June 11 CDex call**. The agreed
direction is to add a Use Cases section, but to frame it explicitly as a
description of **shared capabilities provided to other IGs** (with a
specific discussion of the inheritance process) rather than as direct
implementation guidance, and to call out which downstream guides
exercise which HRex features.

There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets for FHIR-48917; the only
inbound reference is from `HL7/davinci-ehrx`'s own
`input/ignoreWarnings.txt`, which already contains a comment marker
referencing this ticket. No related Zulip threads were found.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested (matches the already-approved Persuasive resolution)

#### Proposal

Add a new Use Cases section to the Da Vinci HRex IG, implemented as a
new markdown page in `HL7/davinci-ehrx` at
`input/pagecontent/usecases.md`, registered via a new entry in
`input/includes/menu.xml`. The page must:

1. Open with an explicit **scope statement** that HRex is a *substrate
   IG* — its profiles, operations, value sets, and patterns are
   intended for **reuse by other Da Vinci IGs**, not for direct
   implementation.
2. Describe the **inheritance process** in concrete terms: how a
   downstream Da Vinci IG declares dependency on HRex in
   `sushi-config.yaml`, how it derives profiles from HRex base
   profiles (e.g., `HRexCoverage`, `HRexPatientDemographics`,
   `HRexPractitionerRole`, `HRexProvenance`), how the
   `member-match` operation and Task-based data-request patterns are
   expected to be referenced, and what conformance obligations
   propagate to the consuming IG.
3. Enumerate the **shared capabilities** with a feature → consumer
   matrix (e.g., `member-match` → CDex / PDex; Task-based data
   requests → CDex / DTR; provenance pattern → most Da Vinci IGs;
   well-known endpoint pattern → PDex Plan-Net; reference / namespaced
   identifier patterns → all). The matrix should name actual consuming
   IGs, not be left abstract.
4. Once the page is in place, update `input/ignoreWarnings.txt` to
   remove or rescope the FHIR-48917-tagged comment block that today
   suppresses the "second menu item under Background" warning, since
   the menu structure will have changed.
5. Add a brief forward-pointer in `input/pagecontent/index.md` so
   first-time readers land on the framing before navigating into the
   profile detail.

#### Justification

This is exactly the resolution already reviewed and approved on the
June 11 CDex call ("Persuasive"), and it directly addresses the
reporter's concern: HRex's "different from other Da Vinci IGs"
character is precisely what the new section is asked to *explain*
rather than to elide. Implementing it as a narrative page (markdown +
menu entry) avoids touching any FSH, profile, or operation definition,
which means no conformance impact, no breaking change, and no
regeneration of derived artifacts. It also brings HRex into structural
alignment with the rest of the Da Vinci IG family, which lowers the
cognitive cost for implementers who navigate across multiple Da Vinci
guides. The cross-reference in `ignoreWarnings.txt` shows the team has
already been tracking this gap; closing it removes a long-standing
suppressed warning rather than just adding new content.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative — Add a "Scope & Inheritance" framing section instead of a "Use Cases" section

#### Proposal

Rather than literally calling the new page "Use Cases", add a section
at `input/pagecontent/scope-and-inheritance.md` (or a similarly named
file) and surface it under the same menu position. The page's content
overlaps substantially with Disposition A, but it is framed primarily
as **scope & inheritance documentation** — the feature → consumer
matrix becomes a supporting subsection rather than the headline. The
existing standardized "Use Cases" menu label across other Da Vinci IGs
would *not* be reused for HRex; instead, HRex would have a uniquely
named section that signals its substrate nature at the menu level.

#### Justification

The original ambiguity in the ticket comes from the fact that "use
cases" has a strong implied meaning of "stories for direct
implementers". For an IG that is *not* directly implementable, calling
the section "Use Cases" risks misleading first-time readers who arrive
from another Da Vinci IG and expect actor / scenario style content.
Renaming the section makes the substrate-IG distinction visible from
the navigation and eliminates the need for the page itself to
immediately walk back the term. The trade-off is loss of structural
parity with the other Da Vinci IGs — readers comparing menus side by
side will not see a matching entry — and that is the principal reason
this is offered as the alternative rather than the recommendation.

---

### Disposition C: Decline — do not add a Use Cases section

#### Proposal

Keep the HRex IG structure as it is today. Do not add a Use Cases (or
Scope & Inheritance) page. Instead, rely on each consuming Da Vinci IG
to document its own use cases, with HRex remaining a pure technical
substrate that documents only its profiles, operations, and patterns.
Optionally, expand the existing `index.md` with one or two paragraphs
clarifying that HRex is not directly implementable, but do not
introduce a new top-level section.

#### Justification

A defensible "decline" position rests on three points: (1) the
reporter's own observation that HRex is not directly implementable
makes a Use Cases section structurally awkward; (2) per-IG use cases
are best owned by the IG that *defines the actors*, which is always a
downstream guide, not HRex; (3) duplicating use-case narrative in HRex
risks drift as downstream IGs evolve, creating maintenance burden on a
guide whose value is supposed to be its stability. This disposition is
the weakest of the three because it ignores the already-approved
"Persuasive" resolution, leaves the long-standing
`ignoreWarnings.txt` suppression unresolved, and forces every new
reader of HRex to derive the substrate-IG framing on their own.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **A**

Disposition A should be adopted. The workgroup has already converged
on this direction (resolution = Persuasive, approved on the June 11
CDex call, ticket carries the `Ready-For-Vote` label), so the question
in front of the workgroup is execution, not principle. It also
maximally addresses the reporter's actual concern — the *ambiguity* of
whether HRex should look like other Da Vinci IGs — by adopting the
familiar section name *and* pinning down its meaning in the page's
opening paragraphs. The "Use Cases" label preserves cross-IG
navigational consistency for implementers who move between Da Vinci
guides, and the explicit "shared capabilities, not direct
implementation" framing prevents the misreading that Disposition B is
worried about. As a side benefit, the change retires a long-standing
`ignoreWarnings.txt` suppression that today exists *because* the menu
structure was incomplete. Disposition B should only be revisited if,
during authoring, the workgroup finds that the "Use Cases" framing
genuinely cannot be made to read clearly for a substrate IG.
