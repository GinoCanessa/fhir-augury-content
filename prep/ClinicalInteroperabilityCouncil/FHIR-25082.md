## Summary

Ballot comment "BC #67" against the US Breast Cancer Radiology Reporting
(FHIR) ballot. The reporter flags an incomplete sentence — "While nothing
being done here is specifically forbidden within FHIR, we feel that taken
together this strategy has." — which trails off without completing the
thought. The reporter also notes that, apart from a handful of typos, the
ballot otherwise appears to be in order, and emphasizes that the real
challenge is transitioning the Breast/Radiology community from an analog /
stand-alone reporting process to a digital, iteratively-improving process
that yields uniform data suitable for research and outcome improvement.
The ticket requests that the incomplete sentence be completed and that
the typos be corrected. No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads,
or GitHub items were returned by FhirAugury for this ticket.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Apply the requested editorial fixes directly to the US Breast Cancer
Radiology Reporting (FHIR) ballot narrative. Specifically:

1. Complete the truncated sentence currently reading "While nothing
   being done here is specifically forbidden within FHIR, we feel that
   taken together this strategy has." — rewrite it so the predicate is
   finished (e.g., "…has the potential to create interoperability
   risks that this IG is designed to mitigate.").
2. Sweep the surrounding narrative for the typos the reporter noted
   (e.g., "senteence", "appearrs", "organizaitons") and correct them.

No resource, profile, or conformance change is implied; this is a pure
prose/editorial update to the IG's narrative pages.

#### Justification

The ticket is a straightforward ballot editorial comment with no
normative content at stake. The reporter's request is narrowly scoped
(finish one sentence, fix typos) and the existing Jira resolution
("Persuasive" — "We agree. We intend for the next ballot to be much
improved in content and structure, and be more easily understood.")
already commits the work group to exactly this kind of cleanup. Accepting
as-requested is consistent with HL7 ballot-reconciliation practice for
non-substantive editorial comments and discharges the ticket at minimal
cost.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Treat BC #67 as one input to a broader narrative rewrite of the IG's
framing / rationale sections rather than a surgical one-sentence fix.
Rewrite the paragraph containing the truncated sentence (and adjacent
paragraphs) for clarity, explicitly articulating the interoperability
rationale the original author was reaching for, and run a full
copy-edit pass over the narrative to catch the other typos flagged by
the reporter plus any similar issues.

#### Justification

The reporter's broader observation — that the substantive challenge is
the analog-to-digital transition for breast/radiology reporting —
suggests readers are struggling with the IG's framing, not just one
sentence. A wider rewrite addresses the intent behind the comment
("content and structure … more easily understood", per the existing
resolution) rather than only its letter, and reduces the likelihood of
repeat editorial comments in the next ballot cycle. Trade-off: more
effort than Disposition A and a small risk of introducing new wording
that itself attracts ballot comments.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline on the grounds that the ticket has already been dispositioned
(Resolution: Persuasive, 2019-11-10) and that no further action is
required from the work group on this specific item — any remaining
editorial cleanup should be folded into the normal pre-ballot copy-edit
pass for the next publication of the IG, not tracked as a reopened
change request.

#### Justification

The ticket is from the October 2019 ballot cycle, was resolved Persuasive
at that time, and carries no open follow-up: there are no cross-
references in Jira, no Zulip threads, and no GitHub PRs/issues visible
to FhirAugury tying it to pending work. Re-opening or re-dispositioning
a six-year-old editorial comment adds process overhead without changing
the outcome, since the underlying prose-quality commitment is already
on record.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is already resolved Persuasive, and the only outstanding
question for the work group is whether to confirm that the specific
editorial fixes (complete the truncated sentence, correct the typos)
actually landed in the current ballot text. Disposition A operationalizes
the existing Persuasive commitment at minimal cost, keeps the scope
aligned with what the reporter asked for, and avoids the risk
Disposition B carries of expanding a narrow editorial comment into a
broader rewrite that was not requested. Disposition C is defensible on
process grounds but leaves a small, concrete fix unverified, which is
worse for implementers reading the IG than simply making the edit.
