## Summary

The ticket is a balloter comment (Breast Cancer ballot comment #77) on the
*US Breast Cancer Radiology Reporting (FHIR)* implementation guide,
recommending that **Biomedical Research and Regulation (BR&R)** be added as
a co-sponsoring HL7 work group alongside the Clinical Interoperability
Council. The request is purely organizational/process — it does not propose

ticket has already been triaged with a resolution of **Persuasive**, with
the resolution text indicating that the editors will contact BR&R to gauge
their interest. No related Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add **Biomedical Research and Regulation (BR&R)** as a co-sponsor work
group on the *US Breast Cancer Radiology Reporting (FHIR)* implementation
guide. Concretely:

- Update the IG's `package-list.json` / `sushi-config.yaml` (or equivalent
  publication metadata) to list BR&R as a co-sponsor alongside CIC.
- Update the IG's authoring/landing page (typically `input/pagecontent/index.md`
  or the IG's `ImplementationGuide` resource definition) to reflect the
  co-sponsorship in the rendered "Sponsoring Work Groups" section.
- File a request with HL7 TSC / publishing for the work-group attribution
  change to be reflected in the FHIR registry / IG publication listing.

This action is contingent on BR&R formally accepting co-sponsorship, per
the resolution text.

#### Justification

The resolution is already recorded as **Persuasive**, indicating
agreement in principle. Co-sponsorship by BR&R is consistent with HL7
practice when an IG's subject matter (here, oncology / radiology
reporting with relevance to clinical research and regulatory submission)
spans multiple work groups' scopes. Listing BR&R formally improves
discoverability and ensures the right reviewers are looped in for future
ballot cycles.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than adding BR&R as a formal **co-sponsor**, record the
relationship as an **interested / collaborating work group** on the IG's
landing page and in the project insight / NIB, while keeping CIC as the
sole sponsor of record. Publish a short collaboration note in the IG
narrative pointing readers (especially those from research/regulatory
contexts) at relevant BR&R artifacts and contacts.

#### Justification

Formal co-sponsorship carries ongoing process obligations (joint
quorum, joint approvals on changes and ballots, joint reconciliation).
If BR&R's interest is primarily review and liaison rather than active
authoring, a "collaborating WG" relationship captures the substance of
the request without imposing co-sponsorship overhead on either group.
This is also faster to implement — it requires no TSC / project insight
amendment — and is reversible if BR&R later wants to upgrade to formal
co-sponsorship.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the recommendation. Keep CIC as the sole sponsoring work group
of *US Breast Cancer Radiology Reporting (FHIR)* and close the ticket
as **Not Persuasive** (or **Not Persuasive with Mod**) on the grounds
that the IG's scope — radiology reporting for breast cancer screening
and diagnosis — is squarely clinical and that adding BR&R does not
materially improve the artifact for its target implementer audience.

#### Justification

Defensible if (a) BR&R declines or does not respond to the outreach
mandated by the current resolution, or (b) the working group concludes
that the IG's content and intended use cases are not in BR&R's scope.
Avoids an unfilled co-sponsor slot and the operational drag of a
co-sponsor that is not actively engaged. This disposition is the
appropriate fallback if outreach to BR&R fails to produce a commitment.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket has already been adjudicated as **Persuasive**, and the
resolution explicitly commits to contacting BR&R about co-sponsorship —
so the workgroup's existing direction is Disposition A. The right next
step is to execute on that commitment: confirm BR&R's acceptance and
then update the IG's publication metadata and landing page to reflect
co-sponsorship. Disposition B is a reasonable fallback if BR&R prefers a
lighter-weight liaison relationship, and Disposition C should only be
invoked if BR&R declines outright. Because the IG sits in the
breast-cancer radiology reporting space — which has clear regulatory and
research relevance — formal BR&R co-sponsorship strengthens the
artifact's credibility with that audience and is worth the modest
governance overhead.
