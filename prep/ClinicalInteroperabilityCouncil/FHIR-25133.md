## Summary

This ticket (ballot comment "BC #84") against the **US Breast Cancer Radiology
Reporting (FHIR)** IG reports that the IG references the "Breast Radiology
Reporting Guidelines" without a concrete version number and without a
resolvable link — the text literally reads "(version ?) [need link]". The
reporter asks the work group to supply the correct version and to add a
working hyperlink. The ticket has already been marked resolved as
**Persuasive**, with the recorded resolution stating that the version number
will be added in the next ballot. There are no cross-referenced Jira

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the US Breast Cancer Radiology Reporting (FHIR) IG narrative where it
currently reads "Breast Radiology Reporting Guidelines (version ?) [need
link]" to replace the placeholder with the specific published version of the
ACR BI-RADS / Breast Radiology Reporting Guidelines being referenced, and
replace `[need link]` with a working URL to the canonical guideline
publication (publisher page or DOI). This is a narrative/citation fix in the
IG's authoring source; no resource, profile, value set, or constraint
definitions are affected.

#### Justification

The ticket is an editorial defect: a placeholder citation shipped in a
balloted draft. Correcting citations to normative external references is
standard IG hygiene, and the existing resolution ("We agree. Version # will
be added in the next ballot.") already commits the WG to this direction.
Accepting as requested also preserves provenance between the IG and the
source guideline, which reviewers and downstream implementers need in order
to interpret the reporting constraints the IG carries.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than hard-coding a specific version and URL into the IG narrative,
add a neutrally-worded citation of the "Breast Radiology Reporting
Guidelines" that (a) cites the publisher and title, (b) references the
edition current at the time of publication, and (c) links to a stable
publisher landing page (e.g., the ACR BI-RADS overview page) rather than to
a version-specific PDF URL. Where the IG needs to pin behavior to a
specific edition, do so via a separate, clearly-marked "Referenced edition"
callout near the reference rather than embedding the version inside the
running prose.

#### Justification

External guideline documents are re-versioned on a publisher cadence that
does not align with FHIR IG ballots. Hard-coded version numbers and
deep-linked PDFs tend to rot between IG publications, producing the same
"need link" defect on a future cycle. Separating the conceptual citation
from the pinned edition keeps the narrative stable while still letting the
IG state exactly which edition its constraints were derived from, and
matches how other HL7 IGs handle references to external clinical guideline
documents.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close this ticket without an IG change, on the grounds that the affected
specification ("US Breast Cancer Radiology Reporting (FHIR)") is not being
progressed by the Clinical Interoperability Council and the placeholder
text can be removed entirely rather than corrected — i.e., drop the
sentence that cites "Breast Radiology Reporting Guidelines (version ?)
[need link]" instead of filling it in.

#### Justification

Declining is defensible only if the WG has decided not to carry this IG
forward, in which case adding effort to chase the correct edition and URL
is unjustified. A recorded rationale of "IG not being progressed; citation
removed to avoid shipping a known-broken reference" is cleaner than
shipping a half-maintained citation. This rationale does not apply if the
IG is still active, in which case Disposition A or B is preferable.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is an unambiguous editorial defect that the WG has already
agreed to fix ("Persuasive", resolution: "Version # will be added in the
next ballot"). Disposition A matches that committed intent and closes the
ballot comment cleanly. Disposition B is a reasonable style refinement,
but introducing a new citation convention through a ballot-comment fix is
out of scope for what BC #84 is asking. Disposition C only makes sense if
the IG itself is being retired, which is a separate decision and not
implied by the ticket. The WG should take Disposition A, confirming the
exact edition and canonical URL of the "Breast Radiology Reporting
Guidelines" to substitute in for `(version ?) [need link]`.
