## Summary

The ticket asks the Da Vinci HRex implementation guide to add an explicit
"Modifier Elements" section to its conformance page. Although the FHIR core
specification already defines expectations around modifier elements,
implementers do not consistently honor them. The reporter proposes that HRex
state, in plain conformance language, that implementations SHALL check for
the presence of modifier elements regardless of whether those elements are
flagged as `mustSupport`, and SHALL reject (or otherwise handle through an
exception process) any modifier element whose meaning they do not understand.
The accepted resolution is to make this addition in HRex while keeping the
wording brief and pointing to the core specification for the full normative
detail. No other Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items are
cross-referenced to FHIR-49869 in the indexed sources.

## Details

**Description:**

While the base core specification sets expectations around modifier
elements, these aren't always picked up by implementers. We should add a
section on "Modifier Elements" to the HRex conformance page that says:

> "Some FHIR elements are 'modifier' elements. These elements can change the
> meaning of elements descending from the element containing the modifier.
> As such, implementations SHALL check for the presence of modifier elements
> in instances, regardless of whether those modifier elements are marked as
> mustSupport. Implementations SHALL reject or engage in exception handling
> processes if they encounter a modifier element that they do not understand
> the meaning of."

**Resolution Description:**

Do this, but keep wording brief and point to the location in the core spec
for details.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Add a new "Modifier Elements" subsection to the HRex conformance page that
reproduces the reporter's proposed text verbatim:

> "Some FHIR elements are 'modifier' elements. These elements can change the
> meaning of elements descending from the element containing the modifier.
> As such, implementations SHALL check for the presence of modifier elements
> in instances, regardless of whether those modifier elements are marked as
> mustSupport. Implementations SHALL reject or engage in exception handling
> processes if they encounter a modifier element that they do not understand
> the meaning of."

The block becomes binding HRex conformance language and is cited from any
HRex profile or capability statement narrative that discusses element-level
support obligations.

#### Justification

The text accurately restates well-established FHIR core requirements and
makes them locally enforceable in HRex. It directly addresses the reported
implementer behavior of treating non-mustSupport elements as ignorable.
Inclusion as conformance language gives testers and certifiers a concrete
hook to write against. The reporter is the FHIR core specification editor,
and the wording is consistent with the existing core normative statements on
modifier elements.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Add a "Modifier Elements" subsection to the HRex conformance page, but keep
the HRex-specific text brief — one or two sentences asserting that the core
specification's modifier-element rules apply to all HRex exchanges
regardless of `mustSupport` flags — and link directly to the relevant core
specification section (e.g., the
[Modifier Elements](https://hl7.org/fhir/conformance-rules.html#isModifier)
discussion under "Conformance Rules" and the StructureDefinition guidance on
`isModifier`/`isModifierReason`). For example:

> "All HRex exchanges are subject to the FHIR core rules on
> [modifier elements](https://hl7.org/fhir/conformance-rules.html#isModifier).
> In particular, implementations SHALL inspect modifier elements in every
> received instance regardless of `mustSupport`, and SHALL reject or
> exception-handle any modifier element whose meaning they do not
> understand."

This matches the resolution comment ("keep wording brief and point to the
location in the core spec for details") rather than reproducing the full
proposed paragraph.

#### Justification

This approach is what the disposition comment from the June 11 CDex call
actually directs: persuasive, but with brief wording that defers to the core
spec rather than restating it. Pointing at the core normative location keeps
the rules in one canonical place and avoids the long-term drift risk of two
copies of the same SHALL text diverging across versions of FHIR and HRex.
It still gives HRex a discoverable conformance hook for implementers and
testers, while preserving the core spec as the single source of truth.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Do not add new HRex conformance text on modifier elements. Treat the FHIR
core specification's existing normative rules on `isModifier` and modifier
elements as sufficient, and instead note (if anywhere) in HRex's
implementer notes that the core rules apply.

#### Justification

The core specification already imposes SHALL-level requirements on modifier
elements. Restating them in HRex risks two things: (1) creating an
appearance that the rules are HRex-specific or somehow stronger than core,
which they are not, and (2) divergence over time as core wording evolves but
HRex copies are not refreshed. If the underlying problem is implementer
non-compliance with existing core rules, the right fix is core-level
clarification, validator improvements, or testing-program changes — not a
restatement in each downstream IG.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The June 11 CDex disposition explicitly endorsed the change ("Persuasive")
but with the qualifier to keep wording brief and point to the core spec.
Disposition B is the literal expression of that decision: it gives HRex the
explicit conformance hook the reporter (the core spec's editor) is asking
for, makes it actionable for testers and certifiers in a Da Vinci HRex
context, and avoids long-term drift by deferring substantive normative
detail to the core specification. Disposition A is acceptable but heavier
than the workgroup's stated intent; Disposition C ignores a documented and
recurring implementer gap that the workgroup has already agreed is worth
addressing in HRex.
