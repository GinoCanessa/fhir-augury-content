## Summary

FHIR-25116 is a ballot-style change request against the *US Breast Cancer
Radiology Reporting (FHIR)* implementation guide, filed by Mitra Rocca on
behalf of the Clinical Interoperability Council. The ticket recommends that
the IG authors review and incorporate the **CDISC Breast Cancer Therapeutic
Area User Guide (TAUG-BrCa)** — co-developed by CDISC with TransCelerate
Biopharma, C-Path, and FDA — as a source input when modeling concepts in the
breast-cancer radiology reporting profile set. The submission is identified
in the source comment as "BC #78", indicating it was one item in a numbered
batch of comments raised against the breast-cancer IG. The ticket is already
marked **Persuasive**, with the work group's resolution noting agreement and
mentioning ongoing conversations with CDISC about future collaboration. No
related Jira tickets were surfaced via cross-references; one historical Zulip
post from the legacy GForge tracker (GF#16663) describes the same underlying
TAUG-review request.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat TAUG-BrCa as an explicit input source for the *US Breast Cancer
Radiology Reporting (FHIR)* IG. Concretely: (1) add a "Source Materials"
or "References" subsection to the IG's authoring narrative listing the
CDISC Breast Cancer Therapeutic Area User Guide alongside the existing
clinical and radiology source documents; (2) walk the TAUG-BrCa data
elements (lesion-level findings, response-assessment timepoints, biomarker
status, staging) and, for each one already represented by a profile or
extension in the IG, add a mapping entry referencing the TAUG concept;
(3) for TAUG concepts that have no current representation, file follow-on
trackers (one per concept group) so they are dispositioned individually
rather than absorbed silently. This is the action the work group's own
resolution comment commits to ("We agree. … future collaboration with
CDISC").

#### Justification

The work group already marked the request **Persuasive**, so Disposition
A is the disposition that matches the recorded outcome. Adding an
explicit source list and concept mappings is the standard FHIR-IG
pattern for showing provenance of clinical content and is consistent
with how other CDISC-aligned guides (e.g., vital-signs, lab) cite their
source materials. Filing per-concept follow-on trackers keeps each
modeling decision auditable instead of bundling them into one vague
"reviewed TAUG" closure.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than embedding TAUG-BrCa concept-by-concept into the radiology IG,
acknowledge TAUG-BrCa as **non-normative reference material** in the IG
narrative and pursue the alignment work in a **separate, joint
HL7/CDISC artifact** — either a standalone CDISC↔FHIR mapping IG or a
ConceptMap resource set published alongside the IG. The radiology IG
itself would link out to that artifact rather than carrying TAUG mappings
on every profile. The follow-up CDISC collaboration mentioned in the
resolution comment becomes the home for the detailed mapping work.

#### Justification

The breast-cancer radiology IG has a focused scope (radiology reporting);
TAUG-BrCa covers the entire oncology trial lifecycle including
non-radiology data (treatment regimens, adverse events, survival
outcomes). Folding the full TAUG into a radiology IG risks scope creep
and creates maintenance pressure to track CDISC versioning inside an HL7
IG that has a different release cadence. A separate joint artifact keeps
each standard's governance intact and lets the mapping evolve as both
TAUG-BrCa and the IG version up. Trade-off vs. A: less immediate value
for IG implementers, but cleaner long-term ownership.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline on the grounds that TAUG-BrCa is a CDISC therapeutic-area
guide aimed at clinical-trial data submission, not at point-of-care
radiology exchange, and is therefore out of scope for an IG focused on
US breast-cancer radiology reporting. Recommend the submitter raise the
TAUG-alignment work against a research-oncology-focused IG (e.g., an
HL7 Vulcan / mCODE workstream artifact) instead.

#### Justification

Decline would be defensible only if the work group judged the audiences
to be disjoint enough that mixing them would confuse implementers. It
is the weakest disposition here because (a) the work group already
recorded agreement, and (b) breast-cancer radiology findings (lesion
measurements, response assessment) are exactly the overlap zone between
clinical care and trial reporting that TAUG-BrCa was written to
harmonise.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The work group's own resolution comment ("We agree. We have recently had
conversations with CDISC about future collaboration.") effectively
chose Disposition A in 2019, and the ticket was closed **Persuasive** on
that basis. Disposition A is also the lowest-friction way to honour the
ballot commenter's intent: it adds explicit provenance to the IG and
preserves the per-concept disposition trail through follow-on trackers,
rather than deferring everything to a hypothetical future joint
artifact (B) or walking back the agreement (C). If, in the years since
the original disposition, the CDISC collaboration has matured into a
dedicated joint deliverable, Disposition B becomes a reasonable
companion path — but it should supplement, not replace, the source
acknowledgement and concept mappings called for in A.
