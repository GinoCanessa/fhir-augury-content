## Summary

The reporter (Mark Kramer) flags that the narrative content of the
**US Breast Cancer Radiology Reporting (FHIR)** implementation guide
needs a thorough editorial pass. The submission cites numerous mistakes
and omissions in the prose, including at least one example of an
incomplete sentence ("While nothing being done here is specifically
forbidden within FHIR, we feel that taken together this strategy has."
— missing the predicate). The request is a quality / editorial change
rather than a substantive content or modeling change. The ticket has
already been triaged with a `Persuasive` resolution and the project
lead committed to a more thoroughly edited next ballot. No
cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip discussions, or GitHub items were
surfaced for this ticket.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Perform a thorough editorial / copy-editing pass over the entire
narrative of the **US Breast Cancer Radiology Reporting (FHIR)** IG
prior to the next ballot. The pass should cover: incomplete sentences
(such as the cited "...this strategy has." fragment), typos, grammar,
inconsistent terminology, broken or dangling references between
paragraphs, and clarification of any sentences whose intent is not
obvious to a non-author reader. Schedule the edit so it lands and is
re-reviewed before the IG is locked for ballot.

#### Justification

The reporter's complaint is concrete and verifiable (an actual broken
sentence is quoted), and the workgroup has already agreed in the
resolution comment that the next ballot will be more thoroughly
edited. Editorial quality directly affects ballot pass rates and
implementer confidence in the IG; HL7 ballot reviewers routinely
return technically-sound IGs with negative votes when the prose is
sloppy. Accepting as requested matches both the spirit of the ticket
and the existing `Persuasive` resolution.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the editorial intent, but rather than scheduling a one-shot
pre-ballot pass, institute a lightweight standing process for the IG:
(1) require any author touching narrative to run their changes through
a basic spelling/grammar check, (2) appoint a single editorial owner
on the project who reads the rendered IG end-to-end before each
publication (ballot or otherwise) and files specific fix tickets, and
(3) add an "editorial review" gate to the IG's publication checklist.
The original ticket would be marked as the trigger for this process
change.

#### Justification

A single editorial pass clears the immediate complaint but does not
prevent the same problem from recurring as the IG continues to
evolve. Standing editorial ownership and a publication-time checklist
distribute the cost across iterations and keep narrative quality high
between major ballots. The trade-off vs. Disposition A is that this
requires a small ongoing time commitment from a named editor rather
than a single bounded effort.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark the ticket as Not Persuasive on the grounds that editorial quality
issues are normal pre-ballot housekeeping rather than ticketable change
requests, and close it with a note that authors should fix typos and
incomplete sentences in the ordinary course of authoring without a
dedicated change request.

#### Justification

This would be defensible only as a process-philosophy stance — that
the Jira tracker should be reserved for substantive content and
modeling changes, not prose cleanup. In practice this disposition is
weak here because the reporter has cited a concrete defect and the
workgroup has already agreed the IG needs editing; declining would
contradict the existing `Persuasive` resolution and would likely be
re-filed.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket has already been triaged `Persuasive` and the resolution
comment commits the project to a thorough edit before the next ballot,
so accepting as requested simply formalizes what the workgroup has
already agreed to do. Disposition B is attractive as a long-term
process improvement and the workgroup may want to layer it on top, but
it should not be a substitute for the bounded editorial pass the
reporter is asking for. Disposition C is not defensible given the
existing resolution and the concrete defect cited in the ticket.
