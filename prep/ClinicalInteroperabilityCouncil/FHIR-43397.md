## Summary

The reporter requests that the mCODE implementation guide explicitly incorporate
the HL7 Occupational Data for Health (ODH) `PastOrPresentJob` and `UsualWork`
profiles into the Cancer Patient profile. The stated rationale is that job and
work-history data are important for cancer care and reporting: the `PastOrPresentJob`
profile captures current and historical employment so that workplace exposures
can be explored as potential contributors to disease, while `UsualWork` covers
a person's predominant lifetime occupation — important in cancers with long
latency and/or long exposure windows, and explicitly required when reporting to
cancer registries.

This ticket is the re-filed successor to [FHIR-40863](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-40863)
(same authors, same request), which was previously closed as "Resolved - No
Change / Not Persuasive" with the rationale that mCODE does not exclude such
data but, being a *minimal* oncology data set, does not explicitly require
occupational/industry data. The reporter (on behalf of genny luensman) opened
FHIR-43397 as a replacement so the issue could be revisited, and a commenter
(JIRAUSER25378) clarified a preference that any adoption take the form of a
profile (i.e., the ODH profiles themselves) rather than loose elements. Lynn
Laakso re-opened this ticket post-STU4.0.0 publication (2025-02-17), reverting
an earlier "Considered for Future Use" resolution, and flagged it for the
STU4 reconciliation cycle to gather community feedback on how "minimal" mCODE
should be.

## Proposed Dispositions

Because no repo-analysis briefing was surfaced for this ticket, the
dispositions below are intentionally framed at the profile / IG level
(Cancer Patient profile, ODH profiles) rather than naming concrete source
file paths. If the WG pursues Disposition A or B, a `repo-analysis` run on
`HL7/fhir-mCODE-ig` should precede implementation so that concrete
authoring paths, generated-area exclusions, and any cross-repo touch points
with the ODH IG are captured before edits begin.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Update the mCODE Cancer Patient profile to explicitly reference the ODH
`PastOrPresentJob` and `UsualWork` profiles as recognised ways of conveying
occupational information about the cancer patient. Concretely, in the mCODE
IG:

1. Add a "Supported Profiles" / "Related Profiles" entry on the Cancer Patient
   profile page that lists
   `http://hl7.org/fhir/us/odh/StructureDefinition/odh-PastOrPresentJob` and
   `http://hl7.org/fhir/us/odh/StructureDefinition/odh-UsualWork`, with
   narrative describing when each applies (current/historical exposures vs.
   long-latency / usual-work registry reporting).
2. Mark the inclusion as **SHOULD** (recommended when known) rather than
   **SHALL**, preserving mCODE's "minimal" stance while signalling that
   ODH-conformant occupation observations are the expected representation
   when such data are shared.
3. Add example instances (one `PastOrPresentJob`, one `UsualWork`) keyed to
   the existing mCODE cancer patient example(s), and cross-link them from
   the Cancer Patient profile page.
4. Add a dependency on the ODH IG in the mCODE IG's `package.json` /
   `sushi-config.yaml` at the version family currently published by CIC WG.

#### Justification

The ticket's use case — linking workplace exposures to cancer and meeting
cancer-registry reporting requirements for Usual Work — is concrete,
well-scoped, and directly motivated by registry submission rules. FHIR-40863's
prior resolution already acknowledged that "when shared, it would be shared
with the ODH profile"; making that guidance explicit in mCODE (as opposed to
implicit) improves interoperability without forcing the data to be present.
Referencing rather than redefining the ODH profiles also respects the
`preSTU4-block-vote` label's intent (coordinated STU4 reconciliation) and
avoids duplicating Public Health WG work.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than embedding the ODH profiles as recommended companions on the
Cancer Patient profile, add a dedicated narrative page in mCODE —
"Representing Occupational Data" — that:

1. Identifies cancer-relevant occupational use cases (environmental/industrial
   exposure, registry reporting where Usual Work is required, survivorship
   contexts).
2. Points implementers at the ODH `PastOrPresentJob` and `UsualWork` profiles
   as the normative representation, without binding them to the Cancer
   Patient profile itself.
3. Leaves the Cancer Patient profile's `StructureDefinition` unchanged and
   does not introduce a hard IG dependency on ODH; instead, ODH is listed as
   a "related IG" in the mCODE home page / dependencies section.
4. Optionally ships a companion `SearchParameter` / example bundle showing how
   a cancer patient record can be returned alongside ODH job observations.

#### Justification

This keeps mCODE faithful to its "minimal" charter (the core rationale of
FHIR-40863's original close) while giving implementers explicit, citable
guidance when occupational data *are* available — which is the real gap the
reporter is pointing at. Avoiding a hard ODH dependency keeps mCODE's
dependency graph lean and reduces the risk of ODH-version churn forcing
mCODE republications. It also sidesteps the ambiguity of "SHOULD on a
profile referencing an external profile," which has historically been
awkward in US Realm IGs.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-43397 as **Not Persuasive**, reaffirming the FHIR-40863
resolution. Record in the resolution that (a) mCODE is a minimal oncology
data set by design, (b) the ODH profiles already provide the FHIR-conformant
way to represent job/usual-work observations about any patient, including
cancer patients, and (c) implementers who need to exchange such data with
an mCODE payload can do so today without any mCODE change by including ODH
observations in the same Bundle / on the same Patient. Optionally add a
non-normative "See also: US ODH IG" cross-reference on the Cancer Patient
profile narrative, but make no structural changes.

#### Justification

The 2023 resolution already addressed the technical question: mCODE does not
block this data, and the ODH profiles are the correct tool. The case for
elevating ODH to a recommended companion on every Cancer Patient instance
rests on cancer-registry reporting — but registry reporting is itself
typically governed by a registry-reporting IG rather than mCODE, and
coupling mCODE tightly to ODH sets a precedent (every "cancer-relevant" IG
becomes a recommended companion) that conflicts with mCODE's minimality
principle. The reopening is procedural (reporter was unavailable during the
original vote) rather than evidentiary — no new use cases, data, or Zulip
discussion have surfaced since FHIR-40863.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B best matches both the substantive concern (implementers need
explicit guidance on where ODH plugs into a cancer-patient exchange) and the
stated WG posture in Lynn Laakso's 2025 comment — i.e., that the STU4
reconciliation cycle is specifically about "information that may be important
for cancer, but may not be considered minimal." A narrative
"Representing Occupational Data" page with pointers to ODH captures the
reporter's intent, meets cancer-registry-reporting implementers' needs by
directing them at `UsualWork`, and preserves mCODE's minimality charter so
that accepting this ticket doesn't implicitly open the door to every
"cancer-adjacent" IG asking for the same treatment. Disposition A is a
reasonable fallback if the WG decides the ODH relationship is important
enough to bind at the profile level; Disposition C is defensible on
procedural grounds but wastes the reopening — if nothing is going to
change, the STU4-reconciliation label on the ticket is misleading.
