## Summary

The ticket (a ballot comment against the US Breast Cancer Radiology
Reporting FHIR specification) raises the concern that the proposed
profiles do not establish an explicit link between the radiology report
and the underlying ImagingStudy. The reporter asks how the actual
imaging objects (defined in FHIR as `ImagingStudy`) relate to the
profiled resources — specifically whether that relation is intended to
flow through `DiagnosticReport.media`, and whether the lack of a clear
link weakens the embedding of the proposal in the broader FHIR schema.
The same comment also reopens a recurring meta-question on the IG:
whether the design is too narrowly scoped to breast cancer and would be
better positioned as a more general oncology / radiology reporting
artifact. There are no cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or
GitHub items; the only system cross-reference is the mention of
`DiagnosticReport.media` itself.

## Details

**Description:**

> Existing Wording: "In the context of FHIR we are currently utilizing
> the FHIR DiagnosticReport anchor resource as since it provides a

> current proposal more embedding in the larger schema. This also
> brings me back to the previous comment, how specific is this proposal
> to breast cancer, and would it be better to repurpose it for oncology
> radiology reporting?

## Proposed Dispositions

The ticket already carries a recorded resolution of *Persuasive with
Modification* deferring substantive action to "the next several ballot
cycles". The dispositions below are framed for a fresh workgroup pass
that revisits whether the deferred action has been (or should be)
taken.

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

In the US Breast Cancer Radiology Reporting IG, add an explicit, typed
link from the report-bearing artifact to `ImagingStudy`. Concretely:
either (1) slice/constrain `DiagnosticReport.imagingStudy` (the
purpose-built reference to `ImagingStudy`) on the IG's
`DiagnosticReport`-derived profile to require at least one reference to
a profiled `ImagingStudy`, or (2) if the IG anchors on `Composition`,
add an IG-defined extension on the relevant section/header (or
introduce a `Composition.event.detail` reference pattern) pointing to
`ImagingStudy`. Update the IG narrative to state that
`DiagnosticReport.media` is *not* the canonical link to the underlying
study (it is for inline media artifacts) and that
`DiagnosticReport.imagingStudy` (or the IG's chosen extension) is the
required mechanism. Provide a worked example bundle that includes a
profiled `ImagingStudy` and the corresponding report.

#### Justification

This directly resolves the reporter's stated gap: the report is
currently disconnected from the imaging objects it describes, and the
reporter's guess (`DiagnosticReport.media`) is the wrong element —
`media` is for inline images, while `DiagnosticReport.imagingStudy` is
the standards-aligned link to the study. Mandating the link strengthens
the IG's embedding in the broader FHIR ecosystem (consumers can
navigate report → study → series → instance) and aligns with how DICOM-
and Imaging-IG-aware implementers already model radiology workflows.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than mandating a link in the breast-specific IG, address the
need at a higher level of abstraction by (a) publishing a
*radiology-reporting* companion guide (or extending the existing
Imaging IG) that defines the canonical
report-`Composition`/`DiagnosticReport` ↔ `ImagingStudy` linking
pattern once, and (b) having the breast IG reference and conform to
that pattern via `must-support` on the linking element rather than
redefining it. In the breast IG itself, document the link as a
SHOULD/Must-Support constraint pointing at the shared profile, and add
a note clarifying that `DiagnosticReport.media` is not the intended
mechanism. This approach also responds to the second half of the
ticket — the reporter's question about whether the proposal should be
repurposed for oncology radiology reporting more broadly.

#### Justification

The recorded resolution explicitly flags that "many consider the Breast
Radiology Reporting effort to be a prototype of a broad collection of
similar clinical use cases", and the deferred action was about
balancing *generalization vs precision*. Solving the imaging-link gap
in a shared, reusable artifact avoids re-deriving the same pattern in
every disease-specific reporting IG (oncology, cardiology, pathology
imaging, etc.) and keeps the breast IG focused on what is genuinely
breast-specific (terminology, BI-RADS, lesion measurements). The
trade-off vs Disposition A is timeline: it requires coordination with
the imaging community and is heavier-weight, but produces less
duplication.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the comment as already addressed by the recorded *Persuasive with
Modification* resolution and the existing base-resource element
`DiagnosticReport.imagingStudy`, taking no further IG-level action at
this time. Document in the resolution that implementers MAY use
`DiagnosticReport.imagingStudy` to link to an `ImagingStudy` and that
`DiagnosticReport.media` is intentionally not the linking mechanism;
defer any normative tightening (Disposition A) or shared-pattern work
(Disposition B) to a future ballot cycle, consistent with the original
deferral language.

#### Justification

The mechanism the reporter was looking for already exists in the base
specification (`DiagnosticReport.imagingStudy`); the gap is one of IG
guidance, not of FHIR capability. The original disposition explicitly
parked the broader generalization question for "the next several ballot
cycles", and re-opening it without committed scope risks reopening the
larger "is this breast-specific?" debate without resolving it.
Declining preserves the existing resolution while clarifying the
correct base element.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The reporter's substantive issue is concrete and inexpensive to fix
inside the breast IG: the report needs a typed, navigable link to
`ImagingStudy`, and the base resource already carries the right
element (`DiagnosticReport.imagingStudy`). Mandating its use (or the
equivalent extension if the IG anchors on `Composition`) closes the
gap, corrects the reporter's misconception that
`DiagnosticReport.media` is the link, and produces an example bundle
that downstream implementers can copy. Disposition B is the more
strategically attractive answer to the second half of the ticket
(scope and reuse), but it depends on cross-WG coordination that is out
of scope for a single ballot pass; it is a strong candidate for a
follow-up ticket once Disposition A is in. Disposition C under-serves
the reporter — the original deferral has now had several ballot cycles
to mature, and leaving the IG silent on imaging linkage continues to
push the workaround onto every implementer.
