### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat the ticket as still actionable: locate the sentence "While nothing
being done here is specifically forbidden within FHIR, we feel that taken
together this strategy has." in the **US Breast Cancer Radiology
Reporting (FHIR)** IG narrative (the section that motivates the chosen
modeling strategy — typically a "Design Considerations", "Approach", or
"Background" page), and rewrite it into a complete, well-formed sentence
that conveys the original intent. A concrete replacement, drawing on the
surrounding paragraph's apparent meaning, would be: "While nothing in the
approach taken here is specifically forbidden by FHIR, the combined
effect of these design choices warrants explicit discussion." The fix
should be made on the IG's authoring branch, included in the next
publishable build, and the ticket should then be closed as
Resolved/Persuasive with a link to the commit.

#### Justification

This is a pure editorial defect — an obviously truncated sentence
("…this strategy has.") that contains no completed predicate. There is
no FHIR semantics, no resource model, and no profile to weigh; the cost
of the change is one sentence of prose, and the benefit is removing a
visible defect from a balloted IG. The original triage already concluded
that the ask is reasonable ("We agree"), so executing on Disposition A
just discharges that promise.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than spot-fix a single sentence in isolation, fold FHIR-25131
into a broader **editorial sweep of the IG's design-rationale narrative**
in the next ballot cycle. Open (or attach to an existing) "narrative
cleanup" tracking ticket for the US Breast Cancer Radiology Reporting
IG, sweep the surrounding paragraphs for similar truncated/awkward
sentences (this ticket's wording suggests the section may have been
edited mid-thought elsewhere too), and address them as a single
coordinated PR. Mark FHIR-25131 itself as a duplicate-of / rolled-into
the sweep ticket.

#### Justification

When a single sentence in an IG is obviously truncated, neighbouring
sentences from the same editing pass are often suspect. Doing the fix as
part of a sweep avoids a churn of one-line edit PRs, gives reviewers a
single coherent narrative diff to review, and lets the work group
publish a "we cleaned up the rationale section" ballot note rather than
N micro-fixes. The trade-off vs. Disposition A is latency: the sentence
stays broken until the sweep lands, which is a small reader-experience
cost for a ballot-quality IG.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close FHIR-25131 as **Not Persuasive / No Change** on the grounds that
the ticket is non-actionable as written: it provides no suggested
replacement text, the original sentence may have been already corrected
in a later publication of the IG, and the original 2019 triage's promise
("improve in the next ballot") has had ample time to be discharged
silently. If a defect still exists in the current published IG, request
that a fresh ticket be opened against the current ballot cycle with a
proposed replacement sentence.

#### Justification

This disposition is defensible only if the work group can confirm that
the offending sentence was in fact rephrased in a subsequent ballot of
the IG (in which case the ticket is stale and should not be re-litigated)
or if the IG itself has been retired/superseded. It is the weakest
option on its merits — declining a "please fix obviously broken prose"
ticket sends a poor signal to balloters — but it becomes the right
answer if the underlying text no longer exists to fix.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket is editorial, the original triage already agreed with it, the
ask is unambiguous ("the sentence is broken, fix it"), and the cost of
the fix is trivial. Disposition A simply finishes the work the work
group committed to in 2019. Disposition B is a reasonable wrapper if the
section turns out to need more than one sentence's worth of cleanup —
the executor of Disposition A should keep an eye out for adjacent
defects and escalate to B if they find them — but B should not be used
as a reason to defer the fix again. Disposition C is only appropriate as
a paper-trail close if the sentence has already been corrected in a
later publication of the US Breast Cancer Radiology Reporting IG; if
that is the case, close the ticket with a citation of the publication
that contains the corrected wording rather than a bare "Not Persuasive".
