## Summary

Submitted on behalf of The Clinician team based on a proof-of-concept PROMS
collection project for breast cancer patients on the ZEDOC platform, this
ticket asks the **ICHOM Patient Centered Outcomes Measure Set for Breast
Cancer (FHIR)** IG to expand its representation in five concrete areas so
the ICHOM standard set can be implemented end-to-end:

1. A schedule of administration for milestones / "touchpoints" (and their
   states such as scheduled / completed) — likely modeled with a
   `PlanDefinition` that, when executed, instantiates a `CarePlan` whose
   activities trigger Questionnaires via
   `CarePlan.activity.detail.instantiatesCanonical`.
2. Discrete (un-combined) Questionnaires assigned to each milestone.
3. A designation per Questionnaire indicating whether it is patient- or
   provider-facing.
4. A way to capture variables that should not appear inside a Questionnaire
   (e.g. "does a date exist or not").
5. Extensions on Questionnaire for displaying copyright information and
   controlling its display behaviour (e.g. on the first page only vs. on
   every page).

The discrete-questionnaire request (item 2) is the same modelling concern
raised separately in [FHIR-40321](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-40321)
(Modelling of questionnaires) by the same submitter and should be resolved
together. The IG team's only triage comment (Vadim Peretokin, 2023-02-22)
agreed in principle on item 1, deferred item 2 to FHIR-40321, asked for
clarification on items 3 and 5, and pushed back on item 4 as something the
ICHOM Breast Cancer Workgroup (not the FHIR IG) needs to change in the
underlying measure set.

## Details

**Description:**

Feedback is based on a proof-of-concept PROMS collection project for breast
cancer patients on ZEDOC.

To implement the ICHOM standard set fully, there are several additional
areas that will need to be implemented in the IG.

- The schedule of administration of milestones/activities (i.e.
  "touchpoints") in ZEDOC and the states of these activities (e.g.
  scheduled, completed). A potential option is to use a
  [PlanDefinition](https://hl7.org/fhir/plandefinition.html) resource which
  corresponds to the ZEDOC project concept. When these milestones are
  executed, it can instantiate a CarePlan which [can trigger/instantiate
  Questionnaire resources](https://hl7.org/fhir/careplan-definitions.html#CarePlan.activity.detail.instantiatesCanonical).
- The discrete Questionnaires (not combined) to be assigned to a milestone
  (i.e. "touchpoint").
- Designation for each Questionnaire (patient | provider).
- Certain variables which should not be part of a Questionnaire (e.g. if a
  date exists or not, refer to point 1 under "Individual Questions").
- Questionnaires should also have extensions for displaying Copyright
  information and their behaviour (e.g. on first page vs on all pages).

Submitted on behalf of The Clinician team.

**Comments:**

- **vadim_peretokin (2023-02-22):**
  - Schedule of administration of milestones/activities — agrees this is a
    good idea and will consider it for a future update of the IG.
  - Discrete questionnaires — see [FHIR-40321](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-40321).
  - Designation of each questionnaire — asks the submitter to clarify; the
    request can be interpreted in several ways.
  - Variables that should not be part of a Questionnaire — those variables
    are part of the published ICHOM measure set; recommends raising the
    concern with the ICHOM Breast Cancer Workgroup so the set itself is
    updated.
  - Questionnaire copyright extensions — asks the submitter to clarify the
    use case (e.g. all copyright aggregated in `Questionnaire.copyright`
    vs. per-question copyright).

## Related Jira Tickets

### [FHIR-40321: Modelling of questionnaires](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-40321)

Companion ticket from the same The Clinician / ZEDOC submission. It argues
that the ICHOM IG's composite Questionnaires (e.g. `PatientReportedBaseline`,
which bundles SACQ, EORTC QLQ-BR45, EORTC QLQ-C30, BREAST-Q, and a patient
education question) should instead be represented as **discrete**
Questionnaire resources, optionally grouped via a FHIR Bundle. It also
flags downstream `QuestionnaireResponse` modelling issues that arise from
composite Questionnaires (single `authored` date, single `language`, single
`source`) when the constituent instruments are administered separately,
have separate licences, or are completed at different times. This is
directly the issue called out in bullet 2 of FHIR-40324 and the IG team
has explicitly redirected that bullet to this ticket.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Treat the ticket as a roadmap of five additive changes to the ICHOM Breast
Cancer FHIR IG and accept all five for a future publication of the IG:

1. **Touchpoint scheduling.** Introduce a `PlanDefinition` per ICHOM
   project / measure-set instance that lists each touchpoint as an
   `action`, with timing on `PlanDefinition.action.timing[x]` and the
   target Questionnaire(s) referenced via
   `PlanDefinition.action.definitionCanonical`. Add a profiled `CarePlan`
   that is `instantiatesCanonical`-linked back to the PlanDefinition; its
   `CarePlan.activity` instances carry the runtime status (`scheduled |
   in-progress | completed | …` via `CarePlan.activity.detail.status`)
   and link to the Questionnaire(s) via
   `CarePlan.activity.detail.instantiatesCanonical`, exactly the
   mechanism the submitter cites.
2. **Discrete Questionnaires.** Adopt the disposition agreed under
   [FHIR-40321](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-40321): split each
   composite Questionnaire (e.g. `PatientReportedBaseline`) into one
   Questionnaire per underlying instrument, and reference the set from a
   PlanDefinition action (or, where transmission as a unit is required,
   from a Bundle).
3. **Patient | provider designation.** Use the standard
   `Questionnaire.subjectType` and, where the responder differs from the
   subject, constrain `QuestionnaireResponse.source` (and/or apply the
   `questionnaire-target-structure-map` / `questionnaire-usage` style
   extensions only if the standard elements prove insufficient after the
   submitter's clarification).
4. **Out-of-Questionnaire variables.** Define a small companion profile
   set (e.g. on `Observation` / `Condition` / `Procedure`, depending on
   the variable) for the ICHOM data points the submitter says do not
   belong inside a Questionnaire, and reference them from the
   PlanDefinition alongside the Questionnaires.
5. **Copyright display.** Add (or reuse, if already standard) extensions
   on `Questionnaire` and `Questionnaire.item` to (a) carry
   per-instrument copyright text where `Questionnaire.copyright` is too
   coarse, and (b) declare display behaviour (e.g. `first-page-only` vs
   `every-page`) for renderers, after first confirming via the
   submitter's clarification that this is the intended use case.

#### Justification

Each of the five items is a well-formed, internally consistent FHIR
modelling request. The PlanDefinition/CarePlan/Questionnaire chain is the
canonical FHIR pattern for exactly the touchpoint workflow the submitter
describes, and the IG team has already publicly agreed (item 1 in the
triage comment) that it is a good direction. Bundling the discrete-
questionnaire change with FHIR-40321 keeps two halves of the same
modelling decision in lock-step, and using `Questionnaire.subjectType` /
profiled siblings keeps items 3–4 within standard FHIR machinery rather
than inventing IG-only constructs.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — Split, Clarify, then Adopt the Achievable Subset

#### Proposal

Do not accept FHIR-40324 as a single five-headed change request. Instead:

- **Close FHIR-40324 as "Split"**, replacing it with five focused tickets
  (one per bullet) so each can be tracked, balloted, and
  implementation-tested independently.
- **Resolve item 2 by linkage** to [FHIR-40321](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-40321)
  and dispose of it under that ticket only.
- **Park items 3 and 5 pending submitter clarification** — Vadim
  explicitly asked what "designation" means in this IG's context and
  what the desired copyright-display behaviour is. Dispositioning either
  without that clarification risks shipping the wrong extension.
- **Refer item 4 upstream to ICHOM**, as the triage comment proposes:
  the variables in question are part of the published ICHOM measure set,
  so the durable fix is for ICHOM to update the set, after which the IG
  can re-profile against the new set.
- **Accept item 1 (PlanDefinition + CarePlan touchpoints) on its own
  merits** in a near-term IG release, since it is uncontested and is the
  highest-leverage change for end-to-end ZEDOC-style implementations.

#### Justification

A single ticket that bundles a roadmap-level change (touchpoints), a
modelling debate already tracked elsewhere (discrete Questionnaires), two
under-specified extension requests (designation, copyright display), and
an upstream-standard-set issue (variables that should not live in a
Questionnaire) is hard to ballot. The IG team already triaged each
bullet with a different recommended path (accept / cross-link / clarify /
defer to ICHOM), which is exactly the shape that calls for splitting.
Splitting also avoids the procedural risk of a no-vote on item 4
blocking adoption of the agreed-on item 1.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline FHIR-40324 in its current form on the grounds that (a) it is a
multi-issue change request rather than a discrete change, (b) one of its
five items is already tracked under [FHIR-40321](https://jira.hl7.org/browse/FHIR-40321),
(c) two further items (designation, copyright display) are blocked on
clarification the submitter has not provided since 2023-02-22, and (d)
one item (variables that should not be in a Questionnaire) is an upstream
ICHOM measure-set concern, not an IG concern. Recommend that the
submitter resubmit any still-relevant items as separate, scoped tickets
with the requested clarifications.

#### Justification

The ticket has been Triaged but Unresolved since February 2023 with no
follow-up from the submitter answering the IG team's two clarification
questions, which suggests either reduced engagement or that some items
have already been addressed in the submitter's own deployment. Declining
in this shape — while making clear the door is open for resubmission of
each scoped item — keeps the IG backlog free of perpetually open
multi-issue tickets without losing the underlying intent.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the most honest reading of where the ticket actually
stands: the IG team's own triage comment effectively split the five
bullets into four different dispositions, and it has sat Unresolved for
almost three years because the ticket as filed cannot be dispositioned
atomically. Splitting captures the parts that are already agreed (item
1) and not yet contested (item 4 → ICHOM), preserves cross-linkage on
the part that genuinely belongs to FHIR-40321 (item 2), and prevents the
ballot from getting stuck on the parts that need clarification (items 3
and 5). It is more constructive than an outright decline (Disposition C),
which would discard the agreed-in-principle touchpoint work, and more
realistic than Disposition A, which would commit the IG to two extension
designs the submitter has not yet specified.
