## Summary

The reporter requests that the acronym "BIRAD" be spelled out as
"Breast Imaging Reporting and Data System" within the US Breast Cancer
Radiology Reporting (FHIR) implementation guide (ballot comment #108).
This is an editorial readability request rather than a technical change
to any resource, profile, or value set. The ticket carries a "Persuasive"
resolution that was applied on 2019-11-06, indicating the workgroup
already agreed to make the change. No Jira, Zulip, or GitHub
cross-references were located, and no follow-up discussion appears to
have occurred.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Within the US Breast Cancer Radiology Reporting (FHIR) implementation
guide, edit every occurrence of the bare acronym "BIRAD" (and its
variants such as "BIRADS" / "BI-RAD") so that the first occurrence in
each narrative page, profile description, value set description, and
example commentary expands the acronym to "Breast Imaging Reporting and
Data System (BI-RADS)". Subsequent uses on the same page may keep the
short form "BI-RADS". Update the IG glossary (if present) to define the
term once. No structural, profile, or value-set changes are required;
this is a narrative/markdown edit only.

#### Justification

The request is a low-risk editorial improvement that increases
accessibility of the IG to readers (clinicians, implementers, reviewers)
who are not familiar with breast-imaging jargon. Expanding acronyms on
first use is consistent with HL7 publishing conventions and the
broader FHIR style of being explicit about domain terminology. The
ticket already carries a "Persuasive" resolution noting the workgroup
agreed ("We agree. We will spell out acronym BIRAD…"), so Disposition A
simply formalises the work that the resolution committed to.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than only expanding the acronym inline, add a dedicated
"Abbreviations and Acronyms" / glossary section to the US Breast Cancer
Radiology Reporting IG that defines BI-RADS (Breast Imaging Reporting
and Data System) along with any other domain abbreviations used (e.g.,
DCIS, IDC, MRI). Replace inline expansions with a single first-use
expansion plus a hyperlink to the glossary entry. Optionally cite the
American College of Radiology as the authoritative source for BI-RADS.

#### Justification

A glossary scales better than per-page inline expansion: it gives a
single canonical definition, supports future additions, and avoids
repetition across narrative and profile descriptions. It also surfaces
the fact that BI-RADS is a trademarked ACR product, which is helpful
context for implementers selecting terminology bindings. The trade-off
versus Disposition A is a slightly larger editorial change and the need
to maintain a new section, but it is still a pure narrative change with
no impact on conformance artifacts.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket without making the requested edit, on the basis that
"BI-RADS" is a widely recognised term of art within the breast-imaging
community that this IG targets, and that the IG's audience (radiology
informaticists and implementers of breast cancer reporting systems)
would already be familiar with the abbreviation.

#### Justification

Declining is defensible only if the workgroup judges the audience to be
sufficiently specialised that acronym expansion adds noise rather than
clarity, and if editorial bandwidth is constrained. However, this
position is weak given the ticket's existing "Persuasive" resolution and
the negligible cost of an editorial change; declining would also be
inconsistent with general HL7 style guidance to spell out acronyms on
first use.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

The ticket has already been resolved as Persuasive with an explicit
agreement to spell the acronym out, so Disposition A is essentially the
codification of work the workgroup has committed to. It is a pure
narrative edit confined to the US Breast Cancer Radiology Reporting
(FHIR) IG, carries no risk to conformance artifacts, and requires no
upstream coordination. Disposition B (a glossary) is a reasonable
enhancement but exceeds the scope of ballot comment #108 and can be
tracked as a separate improvement if the workgroup wants a more
systematic treatment of breast-imaging abbreviations. Disposition C is
not recommended because it contradicts the existing resolution and
HL7's general editorial conventions.
