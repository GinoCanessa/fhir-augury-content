## Summary

The ticket flags a constraint problem in the Da Vinci HRex `Coverage` profile:
`Coverage.payor` is currently restricted to a `Reference(Organization)` only,
which makes it impossible to represent a self-pay individual (where the payor
would naturally be a `Patient` or `RelatedPerson`). The reporter notes that an
alternative approach was floated — represent self-pay through `Account` and its
`guarantor` rather than expanding `Coverage.payor` — but no decision has been
recorded. The known burden-reduction use cases driving HRex do not require
self-pay support, while Da Vinci Member Attribution may. Because HRex is a
shared dependency for multiple Da Vinci IGs, the reporter asks the HRex team to
coordinate with Financial Management (FM) to settle the constraint before any
further IG layering on top of HRex hardens the current restriction. No related
Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were surfaced for this issue by
the cross-reference index; the only structured cross-reference is the FHIR
element `Coverage.payor` itself.

## Details

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Relax the HRex `Coverage` profile so that `Coverage.payor` accepts the same
target types the base FHIR R4 `Coverage.payor` allows — `Reference(Patient |
RelatedPerson | Organization)` — instead of being narrowed to
`Reference(Organization)` only. Concretely, in the HRex Coverage profile
update the `payor` element's `type.targetProfile` to add the HRex Patient
Demographics profile (or base `Patient`) and `RelatedPerson` alongside the
existing `Organization` constraint. Update the HRex narrative for `Coverage`
to describe the self-pay pattern (payor = the patient or a related person),
add at least one self-pay example instance, and revisit the search-parameter
expression `Coverage.payor` to ensure it still resolves correctly across the
broader target set. Coordinate with Da Vinci FM so that any downstream IGs
(PDex, ePDx, Member Attribution, PDex Plan-Net consumers, etc.) that re-bind
or further constrain `Coverage.payor` are reviewed for compatibility.

#### Justification

This restores parity with the base FHIR R4 `Coverage.payor` cardinality and
target set, which already models self-pay by allowing `Patient` /
`RelatedPerson`. Narrowing the target in HRex is a non-trivial constraint that
forces every downstream Da Vinci IG to inherit an inability to represent
self-pay coverage, even when the IG's own use cases (e.g., Member Attribution)
may need it. Loosening rather than tightening also avoids breaking any
existing implementations that already populate `Coverage.payor` with
`Organization` — they remain conformant — while unblocking the self-pay
scenario the reporter raised.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep `Coverage.payor` constrained to `Reference(Organization)` in HRex, and
instead document a normative pattern for self-pay using `Account` and
`Account.guarantor` (the alternative the reporter floated). Add a section to
the HRex Coverage page that explicitly states: "HRex `Coverage` represents
third-party / organizational coverage only. Self-pay is represented out of
band via `Account` with `Account.guarantor` referencing the responsible
`Patient` or `RelatedPerson`, not via `Coverage`." Provide a self-pay
`Account` example instance in HRex, and add a cross-reference from the
Coverage page to the Account guidance. Co-ordinate with Da Vinci Member
Attribution so that, if MA needs a self-pay signal, it consumes
`Account.guarantor` (or defines its own profile/extension) rather than
expanding HRex Coverage.

#### Justification

This preserves HRex's current modelling decision — that `Coverage` in the
burden-reduction context is always organizational — and avoids loosening a
constraint that other Da Vinci IGs may already rely on for validation and
implementer guidance. It also matches a real-world separation many payer
systems use, where self-pay isn't tracked as "coverage" at all but as a
patient-responsibility/guarantor relationship on an account. The trade-off vs.
Disposition A is that consumers now have to look in two places (Coverage and
Account) to know how a service is being paid for, and downstream IGs must add
explicit guidance whenever they care about self-pay.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as "Persuasive with Mod / Not Persuasive — defer pending use
case." Decline to change `Coverage.payor` (and decline to publish new Account
guidance) unless and until a Da Vinci IG — specifically Member Attribution —
brings forward a documented, balloted use case that actually needs self-pay to
flow through HRex. Per the existing comment from `mckenzie`, the first step
should be for MA to confirm whether and why self-pay matters; absent that, no
HRex change is justified.

#### Justification

The ticket itself states the existing burden-reduction use cases do not
require self-pay, and is explicit that Member Attribution "may or may not"
need it. Triaged for nearly two years with no follow-up Zulip discussion, no
linked tickets, and no GitHub activity, the request lacks the use-case
evidence needed to justify either loosening the HRex constraint (Disposition
A) or publishing a normative Account-based pattern (Disposition B). Changing a
shared, cross-IG profile without a concrete consumer carries non-trivial risk
of churn and rework if the eventual MA design takes a different direction.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** A

Disposition A is the cleanest fix because the constraint on `Coverage.payor`
is the actual defect: HRex narrows a base R4 element in a way that makes a
common, real-world scenario (self-pay) unrepresentable, and that narrowing
propagates to every downstream Da Vinci IG that builds on HRex Coverage.
Restoring the base R4 target set is a loosening, not a breaking change — every
existing organizational `Coverage.payor` instance remains conformant — so it
carries low implementation risk while immediately unblocking Member
Attribution and any other IG that needs self-pay. Disposition B preserves the
status quo at the cost of forcing every downstream IG to bolt on a parallel
Account-based pattern, which is more work and more fragmentation than simply
mirroring base FHIR. Disposition C (decline) is defensible given the
two-year-old, low-evidence state of the ticket, but the underlying constraint
is genuinely a modelling bug in HRex and should be fixed pre-emptively rather
than waiting for MA to discover it during ballot. The CIC/HRex team should
take this to Financial Management for sign-off before publishing the relaxed
constraint, per the reporter's original request.
