## Summary

Submitted on behalf of The Clinician (ZEDOC) team based on a proof-of-concept
PROMs collection project for breast cancer patients, this ticket challenges the
ICHOM Patient Centered Outcomes Measure Set for Breast Cancer (FHIR) IG's
practice of bundling several distinct PRO instruments into a single
`Questionnaire` resource. The reporter argues that each composite IG
`Questionnaire` (e.g., `PatientReportedBaseline`, which combines SACQ,
EORTC QLQ-BR45, EORTC QLQ-C30, BREAST-Q, plus an extra patient-education
question) should instead be modelled as one `Questionnaire` per discrete
instrument, since instruments are versioned and licensed independently and
are often administered separately in real-world workflows. The ticket
proposes either (a) representing the group as a FHIR `Bundle` of
`Questionnaire` resources, or (b) — if composite `Questionnaire` resources
are retained — clarifying how downstream `QuestionnaireResponse` fields such
as `authored`, `language`, and `source` should be populated when the
component instruments are completed at different times, in different
languages, or sourced from different licensors. No cross-referenced Jira
tickets, Zulip discussions, or GitHub items were found by the FhirAugury
data layer for this issue.

## Details

**Description:**

Feedback is based on a proof-of-concept PROMS collection project for breast
cancer patients on ZEDOC.

- In our system (ZEDOC): Each `Questionnaire` resource (and therefore
  `QuestionnaireResponse`) represents a single instrument by a single
  instrument author. This allows us to increment and semantically version
  each instrument according to changes made by the different authors /
  licence holders.
- In our system (ZEDOC): when we create custom questionnaires we group
  questions into discrete and semantically coherent questionnaires to
  optimise for re-use across various touchpoints. In a way, questionnaires
  become the minimal unit of administration with a set of related and
  complete questions to measure or assess a particular subjective aspect.
- In the FHIR IG: Many of the defined `Questionnaire` resources in the ICHOM
  set are a composite of various questionnaires. For example,
  `PatientReportedBaseline` consists of 4 separate questionnaires (SACQ,
  EORTC QLQ-BR45, EORTC QLQ-C30, BREAST-Q) and 1 additional question about
  patient education. In practice, these individual questionnaires are often
  administered separately and are not combined to make 1 questionnaire.
  There are also licensing considerations that may preclude the combination
  of questionnaires.
- Instead of combining multiple questionnaires into a single `Questionnaire`
  resource, a potential solution is to create a FHIR `Bundle` for the group
  of relevant questionnaires. We understand this was most likely done for
  simplicity of first implementation.
- If the `Questionnaire` resources remain a composite of various
  questionnaires, there will need to be a way to define various fields in
  the resulting `QuestionnaireResponse` in light of this.
  - E.g. `authored` normally refers to the date the answers were gathered.
    If different questionnaires were completed on different dates, which
    date should be taken for the final `QuestionnaireResponse`?
  - E.g. `language` or `source`. Each questionnaire may be in a different
    language or come from a different source.

Submitted on behalf of The Clinician team.

**Comments:**

### vadim_peretokin (2023-02-08)

Appreciates the real-world feedback. Explains that the measure-set guidance
groups the discrete questionnaires together because, per the measure set,
they are intended to be presented as a single unit at the point of care
(e.g., at baseline a patient should answer SACQ, EORTC QLQ-BR45, EORTC
QLQ-C30, and BREAST-Q at the same time point). Acknowledges the value of
separating questionnaires for re-use, updates, and licensing during
development, and asks whether
[SDC's Modular Forms](https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/sdc/modular.html)
feature would meet the need by letting questionnaires be defined separately
yet presented as a single unit to the responder.

### JIRAUSER24649 — The Clinician team (2023-03-01)

Responds that even though SDC's modular-forms mechanism technically works,
there are feasibility concerns. In a scenario with two source
questionnaires of five questions each combined into one administered
questionnaire, **five** definitions would have to be maintained: the two
original instruments, two modules (one per instrument), and one composite
that assembles the modules. Also notes that while the questionnaires are
intended to be completed together, in practice patients may complete them
at different times within a "baseline" window of 3–5 days, undermining the
"single point-of-care administration" assumption.

### vadim_peretokin (2023-03-01)

Clarifies that only **three** definitions would need to be maintained, not
five: the two original instruments and one composite for the combination of
the two modular questionnaires — separate per-instrument modules are not
required. Suggests that the multi-day completion concern sounds like an
ability to save a questionnaire as a draft and resume it later, which
should not be tied to how the questionnaires are structured.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Refactor the ICHOM Breast Cancer FHIR IG so that each clinical instrument
is published as its own `Questionnaire` resource, and replace each existing
composite `Questionnaire` (e.g., `PatientReportedBaseline`) with a
**FHIR `Bundle`** profile that aggregates the constituent `Questionnaire`
resources together with any additional measure-set questions (such as the
patient-education item in `PatientReportedBaseline`). Update the IG's
profiling, examples, and conformance text so that:

- Each instrument (SACQ, EORTC QLQ-BR45, EORTC QLQ-C30, BREAST-Q, etc.) has
  its own canonical URL and version, allowing independent revision aligned
  with its licence holder.
- The IG defines a `Bundle` (likely `type = collection`) profile per
  measure-set time point that lists the required `Questionnaire`
  references.
- Responses are captured as one `QuestionnaireResponse` per source
  instrument, optionally aggregated by a `Bundle` profile that mirrors the
  instrument bundle, eliminating the multi-source `authored` / `language` /
  `source` ambiguity.

#### Justification

This matches how the source instruments are actually authored, versioned,
and licensed in the real world, and how the ZEDOC implementation already
operates. It also resolves the metadata ambiguity raised in the ticket:
because each `QuestionnaireResponse` would correspond to exactly one
instrument, `authored`, `language`, and `source` retain their normal,
unambiguous semantics. The grouping intent (presenting multiple
instruments at the same baseline visit) is preserved by the `Bundle`
profile, and patient draft-and-resume behaviour across the 3–5 day
baseline window — explicitly raised by The Clinician team — falls out
naturally because each instrument's response can complete on its own
timeline.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the current composite `Questionnaire` resources but normalise their
construction on **SDC's Modular Forms** pattern, exactly as suggested by
the work-group response in the comment thread:

- Publish each licensed instrument as its own canonical `Questionnaire`.
- Replace each composite `Questionnaire` (e.g., `PatientReportedBaseline`)
  with a parent SDC Modular Form `Questionnaire` that references the
  per-instrument `Questionnaire`s via the modular-forms extension, plus
  any additional measure-set items inline.
- Add IG-level guidance on `QuestionnaireResponse` population for modular
  forms, explicitly addressing the ticket's `authored` / `language` /
  `source` questions: e.g., `authored` on the parent response is the
  latest sub-response `authored`; `language` and `source` belong on the
  sub-responses corresponding to each module.
- Add a normative statement that conforming systems MAY persist sub-form
  responses independently within the baseline window and assemble the
  parent `QuestionnaireResponse` once the last module is complete.

#### Justification

This addresses the underlying need — independent versioning, licensing,
and re-use of source instruments — without abandoning the IG's original
"one administration unit per time point" model, which preserves
backward compatibility for current implementers and keeps the measure set
consistent with ICHOM's intended administration semantics. It directly
answers the work-group's modular-forms suggestion in the comment thread
while also addressing The Clinician team's complexity concern by writing
explicit IG guidance and committing to only three artifacts per composite
(two instrument `Questionnaire`s + one parent), as Vadim clarified. The
draft-and-save concern is handled by IG guidance rather than by changing
the resource topology.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Decline the change as **out of scope for the current edition of the
ICHOM Patient Centered Outcomes Measure Set for Breast Cancer (FHIR)**
on the grounds that (1) the composite-`Questionnaire` modelling is a
deliberate choice driven by ICHOM's measure-set administration semantics,
(2) FHIR already provides multiple mechanisms — SDC Modular Forms,
`Bundle` aggregation, and `Questionnaire.derivedFrom` — that
implementations may use to manage instrument re-use without an IG change,
and (3) the licensing / versioning concerns raised are the responsibility
of the source instrument publishers, not the measure-set IG. Capture the
modelling guidance as non-normative implementation notes and close the
ticket as "Persuasive with Mod" only to the extent of adding those notes.

#### Justification

The IG is a measure set, not a general PROMs framework; its primary
contract with implementers is what to capture at each ICHOM-defined time
point, not how to internally compose the instruments. Both alternative
modelling approaches (Bundle-of-Questionnaires and SDC Modular Forms) are
already available to implementers without any change to the IG's
published artifacts, so the change request can be honoured pragmatically
through guidance instead of a structural break. A structural refactor at
this stage would be a breaking change for any early adopters of the IG
without a corresponding new implementer benefit that cannot be obtained
today.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B threads the needle between the reporter's real-world
concerns and the work-group's measure-set semantics. It explicitly
adopts the answer Vadim already proposed in the Zulip-equivalent comment
thread (SDC Modular Forms), which keeps the ICHOM "single point-of-care
administration" intent intact and preserves backward compatibility for
existing implementers, while still delivering the reporter's core
requirement that each licensed instrument live in its own
independently-versioned `Questionnaire`. It also directly answers the
two open questions in the ticket — what to do with `authored`, `language`,
and `source` when component instruments are completed at different
times or in different languages — by writing IG-level guidance for
modular `QuestionnaireResponse` population, which Disposition A sidesteps
by changing the topology and Disposition C leaves entirely to
implementers. Disposition A is a defensible second choice if the
work-group concludes that the measure-set's "single administration unit"
framing is itself the wrong abstraction; Disposition C should only be
chosen if the WG decides this is purely an implementer-education problem.
