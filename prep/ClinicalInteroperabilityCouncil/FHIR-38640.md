## Summary

The reporter notes that several Questionnaires in the ICHOM Breast Cancer
FHIR IG (most prominently
[ClinicalResponseAnnualUpdate](https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/fhir-ichom-breast-cancer-ig/Questionnaire-ClinicalResponseAnnualUpdate.html))
include items that capture the patient's name and Medical Record Number
(MRN) as questionnaire data. Because `QuestionnaireResponse.subject` can
already point to a `Patient` resource carrying that demographic and
identifier information, the request asks the IG editors whether the
in-questionnaire capture of name/MRN should be removed to avoid
duplicating data that is normally available via the referenced `Patient`.
No formally linked Jira tickets were surfaced by cross-references; the
related Zulip activity is the ICHOM Connectathon 31 thread where the
ticket was first announced (FHIR-38641 and FHIR-38642 were filed by the
same reporter against the same IG in the same chat post but are unrelated
in scope to this duplication question).

## Details

**Description:**

> The Questionnaire [HL7.FHIR.UV.ICHOM.BREASTCANCER\\ClinicalResponseAnnualUpdate - FHIR v4.0.1](https://build.fhir.org/ig/HL7/fhir-ichom-breast-cancer-ig/Questionnaire-ClinicalResponseAnnualUpdate.html)
> and other questionnaires are capturing Patient name and Medical Resource Number.
>
> However, QuestionnaireResponse also has an (optional) reference to a Patient via subject, which could include this information. Do we want to deduplicate this?

No comments are recorded on the ticket itself.

**Resolution Description (already applied by the workgroup, 2023-02-08, "Not Persuasive"):**

> QuestionnaireResponses in case the Patient resource is not available,
> so we'll keep it as-is for now.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Edit the affected ICHOM Breast Cancer IG Questionnaires (starting with
`Questionnaire/ClinicalResponseAnnualUpdate` and any sibling forms that
include the same items) to remove the questionnaire items capturing
patient name and MRN. Update the IG narrative / form-filler guidance to
state that consumers MUST populate `QuestionnaireResponse.subject` with
a reference to the corresponding `Patient`, and that name/MRN are to be
sourced from `Patient.name` and `Patient.identifier` (with the MRN
identified by the appropriate type code, e.g. `MR` from
`http://terminology.hl7.org/CodeSystem/v2-0203`). Provide a worked
example QuestionnaireResponse + Patient bundle in the IG so implementers
have a clear pattern.

#### Justification

Removing the duplicated capture aligns with FHIR's general design
principle of avoiding redundant representation of the same data in two
places: the canonical home for patient demographics is the `Patient`
resource, and `QuestionnaireResponse.subject` exists precisely to link
the response back to it. Deduplication eliminates the risk of the
in-form copy and the linked `Patient` drifting out of sync, simplifies
forms (fewer items, less burden on the form filler), and makes the IG a
better example of idiomatic FHIR for downstream profilers.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Keep the name/MRN items in the Questionnaires but make their capture
behaviour explicit and machine-driven rather than free-typed. Specifically:

- Mark each name/MRN item as `readOnly = true` and add a
  `questionnaire-initialExpression` (SDC) FHIRPath expression that
  populates the field from `%subject.name.given.first()`,
  `%subject.name.family`, and the MRN identifier on `%subject` at
  render time.
- Document in the IG that the items exist for human readability of a
  standalone `QuestionnaireResponse` (e.g., printout, exchange without
  the Patient resource) but are not an authoritative source of truth —
  the `Patient` reference remains canonical.
- Optionally add a `questionnaireresponse-population` invariant or IG
  conformance rule that the values in those items, when present, must
  match the referenced `Patient`.

#### Justification

This approach respects the resolver's stated rationale — the IG editors
want the MRN to travel with the QuestionnaireResponse so it can be
shared in environments where the `Patient` resource is not available —
while still removing the burden of manual entry and the drift risk that
motivated the ticket. SDC's pre-population pattern is exactly this use
case, and the result is "deduplicated" from the form-filler's
perspective without sacrificing the standalone-readability property the
IG cares about. The trade-off versus Disposition A is added IG
complexity (SDC dependency, expression authoring) in exchange for
preserving the standalone-exchange use case.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Not Persuasive** (the disposition the IG editors
in fact applied on 2023-02-08), on the basis that intentional
denormalization is acceptable in this IG: the MRN is included on the
Questionnaires so that a `QuestionnaireResponse` can be exchanged,
read, and reconciled in workflows where the linked `Patient` resource
is not co-transmitted (e.g., document-style exports, registry
submissions, paper-equivalent printouts). Make no spec change.

#### Justification

The IG's primary deployment context is multi-organisation reporting of
breast-cancer outcome measures, where a `QuestionnaireResponse` is
frequently the unit of exchange and the receiver may not have the
sender's `Patient` resource (or may not yet have linked it). In that
context the in-form MRN is a deliberate identifier-of-record that lets
the receiver match the response to their own patient. FHIR's
non-duplication principle is a guideline, not a hard rule, and IGs are
allowed to denormalize for documented exchange reasons. Declining keeps
the IG aligned with how it is actually being used at the connectathon
and avoids destabilising existing implementers.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

The IG editors' stated reason for keeping MRN in the form (resilience
when the `Patient` resource is not co-transmitted) is legitimate and
should be honoured — that argues against pure Disposition A. But the
ticket's underlying concern (duplication / drift between the form copy
and the `Patient`) is also legitimate, and was simply not addressed by
the "Not Persuasive" close (Disposition C). Disposition B resolves both
sides: SDC pre-population from `QuestionnaireResponse.subject` removes
the manual-entry burden and drift risk, while the resulting
`QuestionnaireResponse` still carries the MRN/name on the wire for
standalone exchange — exactly the property the resolver wanted to
preserve. The cost is modest IG plumbing (SDC initialExpression items
and a short narrative section) and is the kind of change implementers
filing this ticket would expect to see. If the workgroup decides the
SDC dependency is too heavy for this IG, fall back to Disposition C and
record the standalone-exchange rationale prominently in the IG narrative
so the next reviewer doesn't refile the same ticket.
