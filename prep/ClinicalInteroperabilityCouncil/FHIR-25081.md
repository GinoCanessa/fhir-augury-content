## Summary

FHIR-25081 ("IG Clarification - BC #58") is a ballot-style change request
filed against the *US Breast Cancer Radiology Reporting (FHIR)* Implementation
Guide. The reporter (Claude Nanjo) asserts that "this IG still requires some
work to be more accessible to the audience" and asks the authors to revisit
the IG so that its content and structure are clearer for readers. The ticket
provides no specific section, artifact, or wording target — it is a
high-level editorial concern about overall accessibility of the IG. The
ticket has already been triaged with a resolution of **Persuasive**, with the
authors agreeing that the next ballot iteration should be substantially
improved in both content and structure.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Adopt the request as a generic editorial directive: commit to a full
editorial pass on the *US Breast Cancer Radiology Reporting (FHIR)* IG
prior to the next ballot. Concretely, the authoring team would (1) rewrite
the IG home page and use-case narrative so that the clinical audience can
identify scope, actors, and conformance expectations without reading
profile sources; (2) add introductory prose to each profile/extension page
describing intent and example usage; (3) ensure example instances are
present and rendered for every profile; and (4) add a "How to read this
IG" section. Track the rework as ballot reconciliation actions on this
ticket.

#### Justification

The reporter's concern is editorial / accessibility-oriented rather than
normative, and the ticket has already been triaged as **Persuasive** with
the authors expressly committing to "be much improved in content and
structure, and be more easily understood." Accepting the request as
written codifies that commitment as concrete editorial deliverables for
the next ballot cycle, which is consistent with how IG ballot comments of
this shape are typically dispositioned in HL7.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Accept the underlying intent but narrow the action to specific, testable
improvements rather than a free-form rewrite. Require the IG editors to:
(a) add a structured "Audience and Scope" section identifying intended
readers (radiologists, IG implementers, registry submitters) and their
respective entry points; (b) add a glossary of breast-cancer radiology
terms and acronyms used in the IG; (c) ensure each profile has a
human-readable "Purpose" paragraph before the differential table; and (d)
add at least one worked example per profile. Close this ticket once those
four deliverables are landed and link follow-up tickets for any further
editorial work surfaced during the rewrite.

#### Justification

The original ticket is intentionally vague ("more accessible to the
audience"), which makes "done" hard to define and risks the ticket being
left open indefinitely. Converting the ask into a small, enumerable
checklist preserves the reporter's intent while giving the workgroup an
objective bar for closure. It also matches typical HL7 practice of
splitting broad editorial ballot comments into targeted follow-on items
so that progress is visible across ballot cycles.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Close the ticket as **Not Persuasive / Out of Scope** on the grounds
that it does not identify a specific defect, page, profile, element, or
constraint to change. Direct the reporter to refile concrete,
section-level ballot comments against the next ballot of the IG that
identify the specific passages or artifacts that are unclear, along with
proposed wording.

#### Justification

HL7 ballot tracking generally expects each ticket to identify a specific
artifact and proposed change so that disposition is auditable. A
free-form "make the IG clearer" comment is not actionable in that frame
and risks being interpreted differently by each editor. Declining with
guidance to refile would produce a sharper, reviewable backlog.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B preserves the reporter's clearly legitimate concern — and
honors the workgroup's existing **Persuasive** stance — while giving the
editors concrete, closable deliverables. Disposition A accepts the
ticket but leaves "accessible" undefined, which is what allowed this
ticket to sit in *Triaged* from 2019 to 2021 without visible movement.
Disposition C is defensible procedurally but is unlikely to be accepted
by the workgroup given that the authors have already agreed in writing
that the IG needs editorial improvement; declining now would contradict
the recorded resolution. Disposition B threads that needle: it converts
a vague ballot comment into a small, enumerated editorial work item that
can be tracked to closure in the next ballot cycle.
