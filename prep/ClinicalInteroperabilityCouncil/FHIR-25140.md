## Summary

The ticket (titled "Add sections - BC #90") is a ballot-style comment against the US Breast Cancer Radiology Reporting FHIR IG asking the authors to broaden the composition/section model used for breast-imaging reports. The reporter proposes several new or expanded sections — a history section (symptoms, hormones, family history), an observations section that covers clinical signs (scar, implant, birthmark, etc.), explicit structure to handle screening vs. diagnostic reporting, an intervention section (e.g., biopsy), and consideration of dose reporting even though FHIR has no dedicated dose resource. The reporter also asks about support for double-reading workflows and argues that the modeling approach should reference ACR BI-RADS (in whole or in relevant parts) and should not ignore the DICOM Breast Imaging Report Templates, which have been in production use for roughly 15 years. No cross-referenced Jira tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub items were found for this key, so the context is limited to the ticket itself and its resolution note ("DiagnosticReport appears sufficient.").

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Expand the US Breast Cancer Radiology Reporting IG's report composition to add and/or profile the following top-level sections, each bound to an appropriate code (LOINC and/or BI-RADS where a match exists): (1) History (symptoms, hormonal status, family history); (2) Clinical Signs observations (scar, implant, birthmark, skin findings) added to the observation value sets / profile; (3) Screening vs. Diagnostic indication — either as two context-bound Composition profiles or as a required context element on the report; (4) Intervention (biopsy and related procedures) captured as a Procedure-backed section; (5) Dose information captured as Observations (dose-area-product, mean glandular dose) pending a dedicated FHIR dose resource; and (6) a documented alignment table between the IG's section model and both ACR BI-RADS section structure and the DICOM Breast Imaging Report Templates, with an explicit note on whether/how double-reading workflows are represented (e.g., a second Composition referencing the first, or a Provenance/author chain).

#### Justification

The reporter's requests are all legitimate content gaps for breast imaging reporting, and FHIR Composition/DiagnosticReport plus Observation/Procedure/Provenance are the natural carriers. Explicitly aligning with ACR BI-RADS and DICOM Breast Imaging Report Templates would materially improve interoperability with the implementers already producing those reports and would answer the reporter's core concern about modeling approach. Adding clinical signs and history sections parallels existing radiology IGs that model history and technique as distinct sections.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach

#### Proposal

Rather than mandating new sections in the base IG, publish a layered update: (1) keep DiagnosticReport as the envelope but define a companion Composition profile for breast imaging that enumerates optional sections (history, clinical signs, intervention, dose) with LOINC/BI-RADS-bound codes, leaving them optional so existing implementers are not broken; (2) add a required context element (screening | diagnostic) on the report profile; (3) express double-reading via Provenance / Composition.author chaining rather than a new section; (4) add an IG narrative page that maps the profile to ACR BI-RADS and DICOM Breast Imaging Report Template sections without binding to them normatively. Dose reporting is covered by Observation profiles only until a dedicated FHIR dose resource exists.

#### Justification

This preserves backward compatibility with implementers who have already built against the current IG, addresses every concrete concern raised by the reporter, and cleanly separates the "what codes/sections exist" question (solvable now) from "how do we model dose" (blocked on base-spec work). Using Provenance for double-reading matches how FHIR already represents multi-author/multi-reader workflows elsewhere and avoids inventing a section that would duplicate existing machinery.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Mark as "Not Persuasive — Considered for Future Use," consistent with the resolution note on the ticket that "DiagnosticReport appears sufficient." Close without spec change, with a documentation note that implementers may use DiagnosticReport plus referenced Observation/Procedure resources to represent history, clinical signs, intervention, and dose, and that BI-RADS/DICOM template alignment is out of scope for this IG revision.

#### Justification

The ticket is a broad, exploratory set of "consider" items rather than a single concrete defect, there are no linked tickets, Zulip threads, or GitHub artifacts driving urgency, and the IG has stood since 2019–2021 without follow-up cross-references. The existing resolution note already takes the position that DiagnosticReport is sufficient as the envelope. Declining keeps the change surface small and avoids committing to BI-RADS/DICOM alignment work without a sponsor actively willing to do it.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** B

Disposition B is the best starting point for the workgroup. It takes the reporter's concerns seriously — every bullet (history, clinical signs, screening vs. diagnostic, intervention, dose, BI-RADS/DICOM alignment, double-reading) gets a concrete answer — without forcing a big-bang rewrite of the IG or committing to normative alignment with external templates the IG team may not have the bandwidth to maintain. It also reconciles with the existing resolution note ("DiagnosticReport appears sufficient") by keeping DiagnosticReport as the envelope and layering an optional Composition profile on top, which is the least disruptive path for existing implementers. Disposition A is the right long-term target but is too much scope for a single ticket with no linked discussion or sponsor; Disposition C discards legitimate modeling gaps (clinical signs, dose, screening vs. diagnostic context) that implementers will hit in practice.
