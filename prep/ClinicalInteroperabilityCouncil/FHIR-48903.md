## Summary

Lloyd McKenzie requests that the **HRex `ClaimResponse` profile be dropped**
from the Da Vinci HRex IG. The rationale is that the two consumers that the
profile was originally intended to support no longer use it: CRD (Coverage
Requirements Discovery) no longer exposes prior‑authorization information via
`ClaimResponse`, and PAS (Prior Authorization Support) does not consume the
HRex profile. The profile is therefore stale and unmaintained. The
ticket's resolution ("Persuasive with Modification") additionally extends
the cleanup to remove the prior‑authorization‑related extensions
(`AuthorizedDate`, `AuthorizedProvider`, `ItemTraceNumber`,
`PreAuthIssueDate`) that were associated with that profile and are likewise
unused by PAS or any other downstream IG. The disposition was reviewed
and approved on the May 14, 2025 CDex call, and a commit
(`b61a86c3` / short `7cb585b` in `HL7/davinci-ehrx`) has already been
authored that removes the profile, the four extensions, an example, and
updates `ignoreWarnings.txt` accordingly.

No related Jira tickets were surfaced via cross‑references, so the request
is self‑contained.

## Proposed Dispositions

### Disposition A: Accept As Requested

#### Proposal

Drop **only** the `HRexClaimResponse` profile from the HRex IG, exactly
as the description requests. Concretely, in `HL7/davinci-ehrx`:

- Delete `input/fsh/profiles/HRexClaimResponse.fsh`.
- Remove any narrative references to the profile from
  `input/pagecontent/fhir.md` and `input/pagecontent/index.md`.
- Add a `history.md` entry noting the profile has been retired in this
  release, with a redirect note pointing implementers to base
  `ClaimResponse` (and PAS for prior-authorization workflows).
- Prune `input/ignoreWarnings.txt` of any messages tied to the deleted
  profile.

Leave the four prior-authorization extensions
(`AuthorizedDate`, `AuthorizedProvider`, `ItemTraceNumber`,
`PreAuthIssueDate`) **in place**, since the original ticket asks only
for the profile to be dropped.

#### Justification

This option matches the ticket text literally and minimizes the
breaking-change surface. If any party is using the four extensions
outside the now-removed profile context, leaving them defined is the
conservative move. It is consistent with FHIR's general guidance to
prefer extension stability over premature pruning when usage data is
incomplete.

---

### Disposition B: Alternative Approach — Remove the profile *and* the orphaned PA extensions (matches landed commit)

#### Proposal

Adopt the broader cleanup that has already been implemented in commit
`b61a86c3` of `HL7/davinci-ehrx`:

- Delete `input/fsh/profiles/HRexClaimResponse.fsh` (the profile).
- Delete the four prior-authorization extensions:
  - `input/fsh/extensions/AuthorizedDate.fsh`
  - `input/fsh/extensions/AuthorizedProvider.fsh`
  - `input/fsh/extensions/ItemTraceNumber.fsh`
  - `input/fsh/extensions/PreAuthIssueDate.fsh`
- Delete the
  `UnsolicitedReferralAuthorizationResponseExample.fsh` example.
- Update `input/ignoreWarnings.txt` to remove the now-unnecessary
  suppressions.
- Update narrative pages (`fhir.md`, `index.md`, `credits.md`,
  `downloads.md`) and add a `history.md` entry stating the profile and
  the four extensions have been retired because they are unused by
  CRD, PAS, or any other consumer.
- Cross-check `HL7/davinci-pas` and `HL7/davinci-crd` to confirm no
  remaining references to these URLs before publication; if any are
  found, restore the corresponding extension or coordinate a parallel
  change in that IG.

This is the "Persuasive with Modification" disposition that the CDex
workgroup approved on the May 14, 2025 call.

#### Justification

The four extensions were authored to support the very profile that is
being removed, so leaving them as orphans grows the IG's maintenance
footprint with no consumer. The reporter (HRex's primary author) and
the CDex workgroup have explicitly affirmed that PAS and CRD do not
bind to either the profile or the extensions. Removing them now,
during the same release, presents a single coherent breaking-change
event to implementers rather than a slow attrition of HRex artifacts.
The commit shows the change is mechanically straightforward and the
narrative updates have already been drafted. The dominant risk
(undiscovered downstream binders) is mitigated by a deliberate
cross-repo check before publication, which the missing repo briefing
makes especially important to perform manually.

---

### Disposition C: Decline

#### Proposal

Retain `HRexClaimResponse` and the four PA extensions in HRex, marking
the profile as **deprecated** (Maturity 0, narrative banner on the
profile page noting it is no longer actively maintained and pointing
implementers to PAS for prior-authorization workflows) rather than
removing it.

#### Justification

Removing a previously published profile and four extensions is a
breaking change for any external implementer that has relied on those
canonical URLs, and the IG has no mechanical way to detect such
out‑of‑tree use. A deprecation-then-remove cycle (deprecate this
release, remove next release) gives the community a notice window and
preserves the canonical URLs for backwards compatibility. This is the
defensible choice if there is residual doubt about downstream
consumers.

---

### Recommendation

**Recommended disposition:** **B (Alternative Approach — remove the
profile and the orphaned PA extensions).**

This is the disposition the CDex workgroup has already approved on the
May 14, 2025 call, and the implementing commit (`b61a86c3` in
`HL7/davinci-ehrx`) is already in place. The reporter (Lloyd McKenzie),
who is closest to HRex, asserts that neither CRD nor PAS uses the
profile or its extensions, and the four extensions exist solely to
support the now‑defunct profile, so leaving them as orphans creates
maintenance debt with no benefit. Disposition A under‑delivers by
leaving stranded extensions; Disposition C delays inevitable cleanup
and entrenches stale artifacts. The principal residual risk —
undetected downstream consumers — is best addressed not by retaining
the artifacts but by a deliberate sweep of `HL7/davinci-pas`,
`HL7/davinci-crd`, and any other Da Vinci IGs for references to the
five canonical URLs prior to publication. Because the
`HL7/davinci-ehrx` repo-analysis briefing is missing, that cross-repo
sweep should be performed (or re-confirmed) before this disposition is
finalized at ballot time.
